Finally solved the DomainConflict issue, and the solution was through https://pic.lync.com.

Looked in old DNS zone files for the domains that had conflict and found the Access Edge Service FQDN used setting up the Skype connectivity/federation long time ago.

1. Log in to https://pic.lync.com with Microsoft Volume Licensing Agreement Number + FQDN and Stop the service/(update service)

2. When the service is discontinued ==> verify the DomainPicStatus (should now show Disabled):

  • Open Windows Power shell as Administrator
  • Set-ExecutionPolicy RemoteSigned
  • Import-Module LyncOnlineConnector
  • $credential = Get-Credential  ( Enter Global Admin Credentials)
  • $session = New-CsOnlineSession -Credential $credential
  • Import-PSSession $session
  • (Get-CsTenantPublicProvider -Tenant <TenantID>).DomainPICStatus

3. Log in to SfB Admin Center ==> Organization ==> External Communications - unmark Let people use Skype for Business to communicate with Skype users outside your organization ==> Wait up to 24h and enable it again.

4. Verify DomainPicStatus after 24h - Should now show enabled.

shiftydrunk say:

Well that sucks.. I have never used a onHub but I have had similar issues before. Here is what I would suggest to do.

Step 1: Unplug your modems power cord. Then unplug your onHub power cord. Leave them unplugged for 30 seconds minimum. I would suggest that you leave them unplugged for 10 full minutes. This will cause your modem to drop your ISP and ARP tables to truly refresh them.

Step 2: Unplug your xbox power cord.

Step 3: Plug your modem back in. Wait for all the lights to come back on then plug your onHub back in.

Step 4: Plug your xbox back in.

Now if none of that works the last thing I would suggest to do is go in and factory reset your onHub. When I was having issues this is what I ended up having to do. Some random setting I had changed messed things up. After the factory reset things were solid again.

I hope this helps you out some.
