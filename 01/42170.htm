                    I have old VHS tapes (movies) that I don't want to loose but I want to get rid of the tapes. Is this possible and if so how?
                



            



            



            
            


 a. user  say:

            
            
            
            

Yes, it's very possible and you should definitely do it because those VHS tapes don't age well.




VHS tapes are analog technology and computers are digital. So the first thing you need to do is to convert the images on the tape to a digital format. The technical term for this is 'capturing' the video. You'll need to buy a capture device to convert the
 video and audio to digital. An example of this is the 
Dazzle DVD Recorder.




You'll also have to decide what digital format you will use to capture the video and audio.




Capturing video is a processor-intensive activity, so you'll need a fairly powerful computer to do it. Many recent computers should have what it takes.




Once you've got the video and audio in digital format you can edit it, if you wish. You'll need video editing software for that, like

Pinnacle Studio.




A few years back I used older versions of both the above products to capture my own VHS tapes. You absolutely can do this but there will be a learning curve, and you'll want to get a good quality capture device.
