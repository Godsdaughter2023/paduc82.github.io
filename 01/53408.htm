Intel Wireless Bluetooth driver stopped working after installing a - Microsoft Community

johnp lee say:

Intel Wireless Bluetooth driver stopped working after installing a recent Windows Update

After installing a recent Windows Update, Bluetooth seems to have stopped working:


  1. I’m unable to switch the Bluetooth toggle on in Settings, nor can I switch it on from the Action Center.
  2. The Bluetooth icon is missing from the taskbar.
  3. The Device Manager displays an error (a yellow exclamation mark) for the Intel® Wireless Bluetooth® device.
tiero chi say:

Microsoft is aware of an issue in which an Intel Wi-Fi driver update may cause a Bluetooth® driver error due to incompatible Wi-Fi and Bluetooth® driver versions. The Wi-Fi update may have been installed with your latest Windows critical update. All devices using a combination of an Intel Wi-Fi driver newer than 19.80 but older than 20.50 along with a Bluetooth driver newer than 19.0 but older than 19.50 are affected by this issue. The current solution is to install the latest Intel Wi-Fi driver (version 20.50+) available for your system and then perform a cold reboot of your device.

Part 1:  Check the Bluetooth driver version to confirm whether this issue applies to your device

  1. Open Device Manager and ensure the “View” tab is set to “By Type”.
  2. Expand Bluetooth® and locate your Bluetooth radio which will most likely appear as “Intel(R) Wireless Bluetooth(R)”.  Confirm that a yellow exclamation mark appears for this device.
  3. Right-click “Intel(R) Wireless Bluetooth(R)” and select Properties.
  4. Click the Driver tab and check the Bluetooth driver version.  (See Figure 1.)  If your driver version is 19.50 or greater, you are not experiencing this issue. If your Bluetooth driver version is newer than 19.0 but older than 19.50 continue to the next section.

Part 2: Update your Intel Wi-Fi driver version to 20.50+

  1. In Device Manager, expand Network adapters and locate your wireless controller.
    • If you’re using having trouble identifying the name of your Wi-Fi controller you can also check its name using this tutorial.
  2. Right-click your Wi-Fi controller (see Figure 2) and select Properties.

3. Click the Driver tab to check your Bluetooth driver version. (See Figure 3.)

4.  If your driver version is newer than 19.80 but older than 20.50, click Update Driver and select Search Automatically. Windows will automatically install the latest driver version for your device.

5.  If Windows installs Wi-Fi driver version 20.50 or later in the previous step, proceed to Part 3 (Cold Boot).

6.  (Optional) If Windows does not automatically find and install driver version 20.50 or later, visit the Intel Download Center to download the latest driver for your device.  Click here  to learn how to manually install your downloaded driver.  

Part 3: Execute a Cold Reboot

After you have confirmed that you have successfully installed an Intel Bluetooth driver version of 19.50 or newer or an Intel Wi-Fi driver with version 20.50 or newer, a cold reboot is mandatory to completely resolve the issue. 

1.   Save all open documents and close all applications on your device. 
2.   Press and hold the power button on your device until your device powers down.
3.   Disconnect any power supplies to your device for 10-20 seconds.

      a.   Laptops/Tablets should disconnect their charger.

      b.  Desktops should be unplugged from their wall sockets.

4. Reconnect the power supply.
5. Power the device on and you’re all set. 

Once your device has been powered on, open Device Manager and check to ensure that Bluetooth no longer has an error. If Bluetooth still has an error, try the Alternative Resolution.

Alternative Resolution:  Update the Bluetooth® driver to latest available driver. 

  1. Open Device Manager and ensure the “View” tab is set to “By Type”.
  2. Expand Bluetooth®, and locate your Bluetooth radio which will most likely appear as “Intel(R) Wireless Bluetooth(R)”.  Confirm that a yellow exclamation mark appears for this device.
  3. Right-click “Intel(R) Wireless Bluetooth(R)” and select Properties.
  4. Click the Driver tab and check the Bluetooth driver version.  (See Figure 1.) 
  5. If your driver version is older than 19.50, click Update Driver and select Search Automatically. Windows will automatically install the latest driver version for available for your Bluetooth radio.
  6. If Windows installs Bluetooth driver version 19.50 or newer in the previous step, perform a cold boot as described in Part 3, and you’re all set. 
  7. (Optional) If Windows does not automatically find a new driver, visit the Intel Download Center to download the latest driver for your device and click here  to learn how to manually install your downloaded driver.  Once the new driver is installed, perform a cold boot as described in Part 3.

    If none of these solutions work, it is likely you are experiencing a different issue. Try running the Bluetooth troubleshooter, which can be found at Settings > Update & Security > Troubleshoot > Bluetooth Troubleshooter. You can also visit our FAQ page and/or let us know about your issue via Windows Feedback Hub (Category: Devices & Drivers).

More Info
In most Bluetooth-capable Windows devices, Bluetooth and Wi-Fi exist on the same chip and share the same antennas for receiving and transmitting data.  In this case, an incompatibility issue between a Wi-Fi driver and a Bluetooth driver caused an issue.  All Intel Wi-Fi driver versions of 20.50 or later include a resolution for this issue.  We recommend updating your drivers periodically to ensure you have the best and most secure experience with your peripherals.

milagrosm2020 say: Hi,

Solution 1: Restart Windows Image Acquisition (WIA) Service


1. Type services in the search box, and then you will see services application is on the best match position. Open the services desktop application.

2. Find the Windows Image Acquisition (WIA) in the services.

3. Right-click Windows Image Acquisition and select Properties from the context menu.

4. In WIA services properties, select the startup type as Automatic. And then click Start button in service status.


Tips: if the services status is running, try to stop it and then restart it again.

After you click the start button, windows 10 is attempting to start the WIA service on local computer. The process takes several seconds.

5. After the services control finish, click OK button.

6. Follow above steps to start other two services: Shell Hardware Detection and Remote Procedure Call (RPC).

After that, you can use the printer and scanner to test if it needs WIA driver when scanning.


Solution 2: Uninstall Scanner Driver



1. Open device manager and expand the Imaging devices.

2. Find the scanner and right-click it to select Uninstall.
Then the scanner driver will be uninstalled in your Windows 10.

3. Restart your computer, system will scan all the devices and install Windows 10 WIA driver for your scanner. And now, when you scan the documents, it can copy files properly.


Solution 3: Update Scanner WIA Driver

If the computer cannot help to install the WIA drivers correctly, you should do this thing by yourself. And sometimes, after you upgrade system to Windows 10, the incompatible or corrupted scanner driver may cause this problem. And there are two ways you can do to update WIA drivers for your scanner.

As the error remind, there is a CD containing WIA drivers for your Scanner, so reboot the CD with the CD/DVD drive to install the WIA driver step by step. But if you cannot find the CD media, you should go to the manufacturer’s website such as HP, Dell, Epson, Fujitsu.



1. Enter the scanner manufacturer’s website to find the scanner drivers and firmware, download them manually.

2. Install the scanner driver and update firmware step by step.



Solution 4: Troubleshoot for your Scanner


If the Scanner appears the error of “You need a WIA driver to use this device”, you can troubleshoot to fix the WIA driver missing problem. It is an automatic tool which can help to fix all the scanners whatever it is, Canon scanner, HP scanner or Dell scanner.

1. Go here: Windows > Settings > Update & Security > Troubleshoot.

2. Find Hardware and Device, and then click Run the Troubleshooter.


It will start scanning all your Windows system devices including Scanner and find the Scanner error and then repair it automatically.

Tips: If you are using a printer which contains the scanning function, you can also choose to troubleshoot the printer.

I think these three solutions will help to solve WIA driver disappearing problem. And if the scanner cannot scan, you can do a system restore to solve it if you want.
