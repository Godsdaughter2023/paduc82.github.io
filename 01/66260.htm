don cuthbert say:

When you boot to Repair mode, it creates a RAM disk (all in RAM memory) with a small version of Windows called Windows RE on it. That is drive X:

Often in this mode, drive letters are not what you expect, so your usual C: drive could become D: or E:, etc.

Try running fsutil fsinfo drives to see what drive letters are detected.  You can then check what letter your usual C: has by running dir D: or dir E: to see if the Users and Windows folders appear on it.  In this mode the drive letters used do not matter.

If you find your Windows drive, you can run chkdsk D: /X or use whatever letter it has to check it for any errors.

Also, your laptop may have some built-in diagnostics you can run. Check with HP as to how to launch that. They may show if the disk has had any SMART errors or is indicating any failures or errors that will lead to a hard drive failure soon.

Trinidad & Tobago is in the GMT-4 time zone (4 hours earlier than the time in Greenwich, England).  This is also called Atlantic Standard Time (AST).  Trinidad & Tobabo does not use daylight saving time. 

So, for your computer, pick any of the GMT-4 entries (for example, "Georgetown, LaPaz, Manaus, San Juan") and do NOT check the box to "Automatically adjust clock for daylight savings changes."
