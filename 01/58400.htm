How does my hard drive get fragmented?

how does my hard drive get fragmented

graham mayor say:

This forum is for feedback on the answers forums and not for technical questions - however discs become fragmented because of the way the filing system utilises free space on the drive. When a file is deleted the space it occupied is marked as available for re-use. When another file is saved, part of the file may be saved in that free space. Hence with time the files become scattered over the surface of the drive, which may impair the speed at which a drive can access the parts of the file. In practice the effects of fragmentation may not be noticeable.
