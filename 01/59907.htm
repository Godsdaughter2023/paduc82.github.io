mark ole say:

Hi,


Shutting down your Surface RT usually resolves the issue with wireless adapter for typing cover not working. Follow the steps below on how to force shutdown your Surface.

  1. Press and hold the power button for a full 10 seconds, until the screen turns off, then release the power button.

    Note: On some Surface models, the screen may immediately turn off when pressing the power button. If this happens, continue to hold for the full 10 seconds and then release.
  2. Press and release the power button to turn your Surface back on. You should see the Surface logo.

However, if still the wireless adapter for typing cover does not work, you can try pair the the wireless adapter again with the typing cover. To do that, follow these steps:

  1. Make sure that the wireless adapter has been charged and has power.
  2. Attach the Wireless Adapter to a Surface Typing Cover.
  3. On the Start screen, swipe in from the right edge of the screen, and tap Settings.  (If you're using a mouse, point to the upper-right corner of the screen, move the mouse pointer down, and click Settings.)
  4. Tap or click Change PC settings, and tap or click PC and devices.
  5. Tap or click Bluetooth. Make sure Bluetooth is On.
  6. Press and hold the power button on the adapter. When the status light flashes, it’s ready to pair.
  7. Tap or click Wireless Adapter for Typing Covers, and tap or click Pair.

Reinstall the Surface cover driver if still the keyboard is not working after trying to pair it.

  1. Select the search box in the taskbar, enter device manager, and select Device Manager in the results.
  2. Expand Keyboards.
  3. Right-click Surface Type Cover Filter Device.
  4. Select Uninstall >.
  5. Restart your Surface. It'll automatically download and install the Surface Cover driver the next time you connect to the Internet.

Let us know how it goes after performing the recommended troubleshooting steps.

The song length should be in the file's metadata.  If the problem is that WMP is not showing the length of one or more tracks and thus won't let you burn them, one of the following methods should work:

Drag and drop the track

yoan schinck say:

Hi David :)

Applications that you install via the Windows Store aren't standard programs, so they won't show up in the "Control Panel", "Uninstall a program" like all the others. In order to uninstall an application under Windows 8/8.1, you have to right-click on its tile on the Metro Menu, and select "Uninstall". If you are using a touchscreen (like a tablet), simply tap and hold the tile of the app and you'll have a "Uninstall" button in the option bar at the bottom of the screen.

Let me know how it goes.
