This occurs when one computer uses network mapping to connect to another computer to share files.

To correct this issue, perform one of more of the following methods.

 

Method 1: Check equipment

a)      Ensure that the computer with the file share is powered on.

b)      Ensure that any router or network hub is powered on.

c)      Ensure that the cables are securely connected.

If this does not correct the problem, go to Method 2: Ensure the network resources are shared properly.

Method 2: Ensure the network resources are shared properly

Perform the following steps to share a folder on a drive, and connect to the shared drive.

Step 1:

To make a folder that is located on the hard drive accessible to another computer, follow the steps below.

a)      Right-clickStart , and then click Explore .

b)      Right-click the folder that you want to share, clickSharing , and then click Share this folder .

Step 2:

To connect to the shared folder from another computer, follow the steps below.

a)      ClickStart , then click Run .

b)      In theOpen box, type \\computer\share (where computer is the name of the computer where the network resource is located and share is the share name of the shared folder on that computer).

c)      ClickOK .

Hi,

 

A guest account allows people to have temporary access to your computer. People using the guest account can't install software or hardware, change settings, or create a password.

Once the guest account is enabled, you will see the guest folder in the Users folder and you will not be able to delete it. And when you disable it also the guest folder will be present, but it will not take enough space.

¡firedog say:

It's not immediately apparent from your post, but I'm assuming that the two Gmail accounts are configured to use IMAP - the default in WLMail 2011. Click on a Gmail account name in the folder pane and then click on the Accounts tab on the ribbon. Under IMAP folders, you'll see the Gmail folders available and, on the Visible tab, the ones you've elected to show. You can Reset the list to see an up-to-date listing of the folders in your Gmail account. If one of them is All mail, click on it to select it and then click Hide. The All mail folder is a virtual folder showing you all mail in all folders, so you'll see every incoming message twice - once in the Inbox and once in All mail. There's only one message, but you see it twice.

In IMAP settings in Gmail, you can select which labels to make available to WLMail.
