canadian tech say:

I advise all my clients to NOT use camera software to transfer.

Instead, buy yourself an SD card reader.  They are usually quite small -- like a USB thumb drive, and quite inexpensive.  The reader has a slot in it to accept the SD card.  

You remove the SD card from your camera, insert it in the reader, then the reader into any USB slot of ANY computer and simply drag and drop the files to where you want them.

Many computers come with built-in SD card readers too, but those devices commonly fail.

Once the SD card is on the computer, it is far easier and faster to delete pictures from the SD card itself.

If you are asking about photo editing, that is a different story.  There are lots of those kinds of applications on the web -- many of them free.

You only needed to hold the Shift key until your selection was complete.  You also could have clicked on the first item you wished to delete, scrolled to find the last item you wished to delete and then, holding Shift, clicked on that list item.  This would select everything between the first click and the second click.  This is the standard way to select multiple items contiguously (i.e., no gaps).  If you want to select items that are separate from each other, click the fist one to select it, then hold Ctrl as you click more.  Each one you click will be added to the selection.  When you're done selecting, press Delete (with no other buttons held).
