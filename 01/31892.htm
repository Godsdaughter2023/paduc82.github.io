All Windows 10 editions include fonts that provide broad language support, and the Windows platform includes font fallback mechanisms designed to ensure that text in any language always displays with legible glyphs rather than boxes. But some apps may take direct dependencies on particular fonts for displaying certain Unicode characters and do not utilize the font fallback mechanisms provided by Windows. In some cases, these apps have taken direct dependencies on fonts that are not present by default on all Windows 10 systems.

 

Because, the font that the app is trying to use is not present on the system, some other font gets used to display the text instead, and that font may not support all of the characters being displayed.
