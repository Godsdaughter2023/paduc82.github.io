winston m say:

Hi,

 

If your connected drives change or go away or you just don’t want them connected anymore, you may have to go ahead and unmap or delete the connected locations.

 

Windows 8 has a very simple utility that helps you with removing mapped drives from your system. There are two easy ways to access this tool.

 

Method 1:

First, you can search for "Computer" from the Start screen. Right-click or long-press Computer when it appears in the search results and then click or tap "Disconnect network drive." This will take you to the Desktop and launch the tool.

 

Method 2:

 

Follow the steps from Windows Explorer.


a. If you’re already on the Desktop, you can open File Explorer by clicking the folder icon on your taskbar. Click or tap Computer from the left pane and click or tap "Map network drive" on the ribbon. Next, click or tap "Disconnect network drive."
 
b. The resulting window displays all networked shares your have mapped to your local computer. To remove one, select it from the list and click or tap OK.
 
c. If you’d rather manually disconnect the mapped drives, open File Explorer and click or tap Computer from the left pane. Right-click or long-press the mapped drive in question and click or tap Disconnect.

  6. This will run a set of diagnostics on the hardware of your system - it takes maybe 5 minutes to complete
