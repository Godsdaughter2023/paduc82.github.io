    Thank you!

    tryx3 say:

    There are two possible approaches to this, I recommend the first [paras 1.1, 1.2] rather than the second [para 2].

    1.1  Leave the existing user account as it is.  It is an Admin-level account & is a "local" account.  If it did not already exist then I would be strongly recommending creating it so you have a fallback in case your intended main account ever suffered from corruption & refused to log you in.  Make sure you put a decent password on it and write the password down somewhere secure - you might have heard of Password reset disks for local accounts but they achieve no more than you achieve by writing the password down.  If you rushed the initial setup and put in a silly password such as 12345 then go to Settings, Accounts, Sign-in options and change it to something better.

    1.2  Create an additional account for your day-to-day use and link that to your MSAccount. 

    1. Go to Settings, Accounts, Family & other animals, Other  people - Add someone else to this PC and follow through that procedure.  Make the username something that will immediately remind you that it is the one linked to your MSAccount - I use DenisOC as my reminder.  Set it up in the first instance as another local account so you can log into it and test it before linking it online.
    2. When you have finished, you will see the new account under Other people - Add someone else to this PC. 
    3. Click on the new account and click on Change account type to see how easy it is to change it from Administrator to Standard user - you will probably want to leave it as an Admin-level account at first to ease all the installations & setting up you are likely to be doing.
    4. Now log into the new account and go to Settings, Accounts, Your info where you will see the entry Sign in with a MS account instead.  This will handle the conversion of the new account into an MSAccount-linked one.

    2  Convert the account you have already created into an MSAccount-linked one by going to Settings, Accounts, Your info where you will see the entry Sign in with a MS account instead.  This will handle the conversion of the new account into an MSAccount-linked one.

    I have tried to include everything above so might have made it look very complicated.  The whole procedure should take you only a couple of minutes.

    Denis
