icestorm iii say:

[quote user="alexduskthorn"]will my stats from games be on the harddrive and onto the other xbox?[/quote]Let's back up.

 

Your profile is what contains your Achivements and activity history. That can be downloaded to any console and any storage device. It's also not locked to a specific console. It can be moved from one storage device to another if you so choose.

 

Your game save data is stored on one or more storage devices. Most game data and saves can be Copied between storage devices. There are some exceptions that can only be Moved, and supposedly a small subset that won't work if they're moved (or work, but provide reduced functionality).

 

If you pop a 250GB HDD into a 4GB console, then go to Settings, System, Storage, you'll see both a 4GB internal memory device and a 250GB HDD. From there you can highlight the 4GB device, drill down through the folders, and Copy individual game folders from the 4GB internal memory device to the 250GB HDD. Some things will not Copy, but will Move. A few won't do either. You can do the same with other subfolders.

  1. Insert the recovery media into a USB port or a DVD drive, depending on the format you used to create the recovery media.
  2. Power the computer off.
  3. Power the computer on and, at the Dell logo screen, tap the F12 key several times until you see Preparing one time boot menu in the top right corner of the screen.

Here is how to remove your saved password in Thunderbird:

1. Click on Tools > Options

2. In the window, click the Security tab

3. Click Saved Passwords. If you've set one up, you may need to enter a master password.

4. Select your email account from the list and click Remove.

The next time you open Thunderbird, it will ask you to enter your password.
