jimwynne say:

The first indication that something was seriously wrong with the flash drive was the warning that something was wrong and a scan was needed.  The fact that this repeatedly happened, with the scan reporting no issues, was an indication that something was wrong beyond the ability of the scan to deal with it.  This is the point when you should have moved files off of the drive. 

You are running both McAfee and Norton, which is not a good thing and conceivably could be the root of your problem.  Both are known to cause  more trouble than they are worth, and in combination you are bound to have problems.  I would get rid of both and use the built-in Windows Defender and firewall.

The fact that you've used Recuva to no avail doesn't bode well for being able to recover your files.  There are many free recovery programs available, and some will succeed where others fail.  Keep trying.

Finally, it's generally not a good idea to boot or reboot with an external drive connected, especially a flash drive.  It's also not a good idea to store important files on flash media as a matter of habit.  They are far more likely to fail than hard drives.
