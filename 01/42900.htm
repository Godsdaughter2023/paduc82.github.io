I think you're making things really complicated and 'over-organized'. Your Gmail, for example: at mail.google.com, you probably have arrangements to apply labels to some of your incoming mail. This incoming mail to Gmail is also being forwarded to your Hotmail account, where again rules may be applied to move messages around the Hotmail folders. Then when the Hotmail account is synchronized with Windows Live Mail, all of this incoming Gmail is again delivered, but now in folders under the Hotmail account. I think you could really get tied up trying to manage all that and avoid a lot of duplication.

If your aim is to have all your mail available in one place, whether you're at home at your desk using Windows Live Mail or out and about using outlook.com through a browser, then you can achieve this by using outlook.com's advanced message rules to organize all your incoming mail, whether it's sent to your Hotmail address or forwarded by your ISP and Gmail accounts. On the IMAP tab, select Store special folders on IMAP server,  then enter the server's names for the special folders in the boxes. If the folder for sent messages in webmail is called Sent Mail, then enter Sent Mail into the box alongside Sent Items path and so on. It's important to be clinically accurate with folder names - Sent Mail is not the same as Sent mail.
folders work the same way for all accounts:
