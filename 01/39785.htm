spider91610 say:

If you want your console to have priority over your other devices, I have you covered. Jump on your computer, and pull up the command prompt. If you're using windows XP, Vista, or 7, just pull up the start menu and type "Command Prompt" into the search bar. When it comes up, type in "IPconfig", all one word. You are looking for the Default Gateway. When you find the default gateway, remember those numbers, go back to your console, and then type in that gateway, adding one number to the last string of numbers. Say you have a default gateway of 192.168.2.1. That is your router's IP address. Make your console's IP address 192.168.2.2, which makes it the priority device on the network. Bear in mind, if you have another device on the network with that same address, you're going to have problems, so reboot all your devices afterwards to ensure everything gets a smooth message. Feel free to ask any additional questions, I am flagging this thread so we can work it out if you have any more trouble.

Step 1: Region and Language settings.

To use Cortana, your region and language settings have to be aligned. Check the following list of regions where Cortana is available, and the corresponding language for each of those regions.

  • Cortana is available in these regions for these languages:
    • Australia: English
    • Canada: English
    • China: Chinese (Simplified)
    • France: French
    • Germany: German
    • India: English
    • Italy: Italian
    • Japan: Japanese
    • Spain: Spanish
    • United Kingdom: English
    • United States: English
  • Note: If you change your region, you might not be able to shop at the Store, or use things that you’ve purchased, such as memberships and subscriptions, games, films, TV and music.

Step 2: Setup

  • To use Cortana, all these settings have to be set to the same language:
    • Languages (this is your device language)
    • Speech language
    • Country or region
  • To confirm that your settings are correct or to make a change:
    1. Open Settings.  Select Time & language, then Region & language.
    2. Check the Language (set as default) setting for your Windows display language. To get a new language, select Add a Language, then choose one from the list. It may take a few minutes for the language pack to load. If you remove a language, you can always add it back later.
    3. Check the Country or region setting. Make sure that the country selected corresponds with the Windows Display language set in the Language setting.
    4. Go back to Settings > Time & language > Speech and make sure that the Speech language setting is aligned with the previous settings. If the language you want isn’t available, follow the steps on the next tab to add it.
    5. Sign out and then sign back in for the new settings to take effect.

Step 3: Language packs.

For some languages, you might need to manually download a speech pack.

  1. Go to Start and open Settings.
  2. Select Time & language > Region & language.
  3. Select the language that you want to add speech to, then select the Options button.
  4. Under Speech, select Download. It may take a minute or two for the speech pack to load.
  5. Sign out and then sign back in for the new speech pack to be added to speech options.
  6. Go back to Time & language, select your new language, then Set as Default.
  7. On Settings > Time & language > Speech, make sure that the Speech language setting is aligned with the previous settings.
  8. Sign out and then sign back in for the new settings to take effect.

I request you to try booting to Safe Mode and immediately booting back to normal mode.

Method 1: Follow the steps to enter Safe mode:

  1. Open the Settings menu and go to Update & security > Recovery > Advanced startup. Clicking Advanced startup will restart your PC in recovery mode.
  2. Click Troubleshoot to see your advanced options.
  3. In the Troubleshoot menu, click Advanced options. Click Startup Settings to change Windows' startup behavior.
  4. In the Startup Settings menu, click the Restart button to restart your PC. Your PC will now restart.
  5. To start up in Safe Mode with networking, press 5 or F5.

Method 2: If you are running a 3rd-party antivirus software, we recommend uninstalling and then reinstalling the antivirus software, as this may also provide a workaround for this problem.  The easiest way to uninstall an app is to find it in the Start menu’s app list, right-click the app, and select Uninstall.

  1. Go to Start menu and then click on Settings.
  2. Choose Accounts and then select Other User Accounts towards the left.
  3. Select Add an Account.

  5. Click on Finish.
  6. Sign out from the current Account and Log into the new account.
