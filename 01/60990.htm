waynerupp say:

Hey Cheryl,

If you are the only person with access to your computer device, you can save time by eliminating the requirement to sign in with a password every time you boot up your device.  Be forewarned though, that others who have access to your device, will  have free reign with it because you have rendered it so!  Follow the instructions below.

Right click Start Menu> right click Run> type NETPLWIZ left click OK> Uncheck the box "Users must enter a user name"> Left click Apply> Enter your FULL Username> Enter your Password> Confirm your password>left click OK>left click OK

You will need to restart your device to determine if changes have been implemented.

Now, one more thing, when your computer enters sleep mode after a period of disuse, when it wakes up, it will require a password to get back to the desktop.  To change this password requirement after sleep mode, do the following.

Left click Start Menu> Left click Settings> Accounts> Sign In Options left panel> Under "Require Sign In" select  Left click Start Menu>Left click Settings>Accounts>Sign In Options> Select  NEVER in the drop down menu.

That's how you do it.  Good luck in your endeavors.
