Hi Shannon,

I'm sorry to hear about the current issue. This is currently a known issue that is going around that has been affecting majority of our customers.

Check out the full details on our website: https://support.xbox.com/en-US/xbox-live-status

For this we have seen that there's one solution that has been helping out. 

The process that has been helping out is to perform a power cycle on the Xbox and reset/restart the networking hardware. 

To perform a power cycle on the Xbox: 

  1. Turn off the Xbox One console by pressing the Xbox button  on the front of the console for about 10 seconds until it shuts down completely.
  2. Unplug the console's power cable. Wait 30 seconds.

    Important Be sure to wait 30 seconds. This step resets the power supply.

  3. Plug the console power cable back in.
  4. Don't turn on the Xbox yet.
  5. Next you perform the restart/reset network: 
  • Unplug the Router's power cable. Wait 30 seconds.
  • Plug the cable back in and wait for the network to be back up and running.

Give it a shot and let us know if it helps.

michelle vil say:

We appreciate your update. You may consider performing the following steps on how to roll back your video camera driver:

  1. Select the Start  button, type Device Manager, and then select Device Manager from the search results. 
  2. Find your camera driver under Imaging devices or Sound, video and game controllers. 
  3. Press and hold (or right-click) the name of your video camera, and then select Properties.
  4. Select the Driver tab, select the Driver Details button, and look for a file name that includes stream.sys. If it’s there, your video camera was designed before Windows 7 and you'll have to replace it with a newer video camera.
  5. If you don't find a file name that includes stream.sys, try rolling back your video camera driver.

Let us know if that worked out for you.
