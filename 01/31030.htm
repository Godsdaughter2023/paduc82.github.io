i=i+1

lemp say:

Your system is behaving normally. 

Once upon a time, when you bought a computer from major companies like HP, Dell, Compaq, and so on, the computer would come with a number of "extras," including a set of speakers.  As price competition increased, "accessories" such as speakers were removed from the "bundled" price and offered as extra-cost items.  Moreover, for "business" model computers (that's what the "B" in your "hp Compaq 500BMT" means), speakers were definitely not included by default -- can you imagine the cacophony if each computer in a 100-cubicle open-plan office had speakers?

Thus, either your computer didn't come from the store with speakers or if it did, you no longer have them.

You can, of course, buy an inexpensive set of speakers for your computer.  This is what you should do if you want sound, including a "ding," when your mail arrives.  In the old days, just after computers replaced pencils, computers used to play a recording of a man saying "You've got mail" to announce an incoming email message. This became such a cliche that Nora Ephron made a movie with that title starring Tom Hanks and Meg Ryan.

Unless you play a lot of intense computer games or want to turn your computer into a home theater, you don't really need the expensive high-output speakers with sub-woofer and surround sound.  An inexpensive set of speakers ought to be just fine.  You can get these just about anywhere, either in at your local office supply store, electronics big box store, or on-line.  This is just one example out of, literally, hundreds:

http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16836121013&cm_re=speakers-_-36-121-013-_-Product

Just don't get USB-connected speakers.  There is no need for this for your computer and you'll just use up one of your USB connectors.

The audio connector from the speaker plugs into the "headphone" jack on your computer.  On your computer, I believe you have two of these:  one on the front panel and one in the back.  Either one should work.
