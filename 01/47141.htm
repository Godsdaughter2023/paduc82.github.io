cmdrkeene say:

F7 or F8 by themselves won't change volume, you would need to use a special FN key (usually color-coded) to make that happen.  Or, if your laptop has a FN-Lock (function lock) so that it always sends the volume commands rather than the F-number commands instead.

Although really rare these days, it's possible that you may need a special app running in the background to 'listen' for these volume keys.  Every computer manufacturer calls this something different.  Dell QuickSet, Asus HotKeyManager, etc.  They all have their own pet names for those types of utilities. But more than likely, you just need to turn back on the FN-Lock so it works without pressing both Fn+F7 but rather just with F7 by itself.

Although Windows can take some time to detect a new USB keyboard the Computer BIOS should recognise it straight away. ruwim.b say:

The new Microsoft Skype for Desktop (the current version 8.25.0.5 or 8.27.0.85) does not have the option to delete the entire chat history. However, you can delete a single chat conversation.

 

Right click on the conversation (chat) name on the left pan and select Delete conversation. This will delete all chats within this conversation both on your computer and on the Cloud. However, the chat messages will not be deleted from your conversation partner's Skype.

 

If you want the Skype window to be open when you click on the icon in the Taskbar, then unselect the option Launch Skype in the background.

 

Click on your Profile image icon and select Application settings. Set the option "Launch Skype in the background" to OFF.
