markfinch1 say:

I got my computer fixed.

Storport.sys is a Microsoft driver, so the crash was unrelated to my cpu/gpu drivers. I read somewhere to try resetting power plans with "powercfg -restoredefaultschemes" in cmd.

That fixed it. Running on high performance messes with sleep and hibernate and further issues down the line. So I have the balanced power plan and then tweaked sleep settings.

I don't know how common this solution would be to others but it's something to try out first before messing with drivers.
