hellhog say:

As a PC and Console gamer, I can flat out tell you, any monitor for PC gaming is fine for console gaming. As far as I am concerned, console gaming is PC gaming but locked down. I use my monitor connect via DVI for my laptop and VGA for my 360 (since it is an older one without HDMI out). I've also connect via HDMI for it from another laptop. Between two laptops and one console, picture is always great. When it comes down to picture quality, the higher gamut displays are technically better. When I bought my laptop, I had the option of either a 72% or 95% Gamut display, many people went with 95% (which was an expensive upgrade option) but I chose the lesser. Personally, I think it is one of the best displays I've ever used and that's on a laptop. If you want to know good quality, just look for monitors on Newegg and read the reviews on what experienced users have to say. Now, 1ms vs 5ms is non-existent. You will not and never notice a difference at all. I don't even know of any 1ms. Just 2ms at lowest. As for refresh. That's when your monitor refreshes the screen. No, all you need is 60Hz. Whether on a PC or gaming console, you only need more than 60Hz if you are going 3D, that's when 120Hz come into play. Technically a game can run at higher than 60fps on monitor, but if a monitor is 60fps, then you will only see 60 fps, this is where v-sync comes into play on computer games to prevent possible screen tearing. Such as when I run SW The Old Republic, I can get as high as 100fps but I get screen tearing and technically only 60 frames flash by my eye anyway, turn on V-sync and the game becomes capped at 60fps. 60Hz is the bare minimum on LCD monitors. And its all anyone ever needs if you don't need 3D. TVs are sold at 120Hz only because movies are 24p as in 24fps. 60 is not divisible by 24 but 120 is. This helps with frames being cut or jumped in movies. For gaming, 60Hz is all you need. On the X1, games are going to be frame capped at either 60fps or 30fps. So no need to waste money on monitors that are 120 Hz. And you definitely don't want something in between 60 and 120 because if a game is frame capped at 60, you could have screen tearing unless the games frame capping (v-sync) is triple buffered which would allow 90 Hz I believe. Not sure. Either way, 60Hz is fine. Like I said, there is no monitor that is best for console gaming rather than just PC. They are one in the same. So search for any monitor that is 1920x1080 at the size you want, 60Hz, 2 HDMI in, and at least 5ms response time. From there, it comes down to specifics that you want and the reviews. Do you want a Matte or Glossy screen? Do you want one that has a brighter backlight? Personally, just look for something from Dell, Samsung, or Asus as they are best for monitors.   1. In File Explorer, right click on the documents folder.
  2. Click Properties.

  4. Click on Edit to change the permissions.
  5. Click the box to allow Home Users full control.
  6. Click OK and make sure the change is applied to all subfolders and files.

  • Right-click the folder and go to Properties
  • In Properties, go to Security (tab) > Advanced (button at the bottom) > Owner (tab)

After you are the current owner, you should be able to change permissions as you need.
