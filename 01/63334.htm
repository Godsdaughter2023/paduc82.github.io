    a. user say:

    Firefight and Campaign handle the connection differently.

    Lag is applied [therefore experienced] differently between Matchmaking and Firefight/Campaign.

    The AI needs to be synchronised to all players, and lag is experienced equally by everyone. An American, I presume, connecting with an Australian will have a very laggy game, as will the Australian, but it doesn't need to be anywhere near that far away to be a significant problem.

    I can't say how common it is for you to get a good connection in those gametypes, but they're easily the worst because of how lag is applied and the AI needs to be synchronised.

    Matchmaking:

    Button.

    Action.

    Lag.

    AI Gametypes:

    Button.

    Lag.

    Action.

    It's annoying and far worse the way AI gametypes currently implement lag.

    __________________________________________________________

    If it's a rarity for you I wouldn't worry about it though, plenty of people have it worse or more often -- or both. It also has barely anything to do with the Host, it's more due to distance but if someone has an extremely bad connection I presume that would be very bad in Firefight/Campaign.

    Hi Mehdi. I'm Greg, an installation specialist and 8 year Windows MVP, here to help you.

    If these are switchable graphics, then make sure you have the latest display drivers and that they are configured to switch when under load. You may need to contact your PC maker's Support for how your model handles this, but it's normally in the driver software which I would go over closely after updating.


    Update the Display drivers from the PC's Support Downloads web page. Switchable graphics will normally have drivers customized by the PC maker. Use the exact make and model number, Serial Number, or Dell Service tag from sticker on PC.

    While there check also for newer chipset, BIOS, network, sound, USB3 and other drivers, comparing to the ones presently installed in Device Manager reached by right clicking the Start Menu.

    if this doesn't give you the latest or ideal driver for what you need, compare it to the driver offered by the Intel driver update Utility here: http://www.intel.com/content/www/us/en/support/...
    or the Nvidia Update utility here: http://www.nvidia.com/Download/Scan.aspx?lang=e...
    or the AMD autodect utility here: http://support.amd.com/us/gpudownload/windows/P...

    You can also try older drivers in Device Manager > Display device > Driver tab > Update Driver > Browse > Let Me Pick.

    Then you will know you've tried everything in addition to Windows Update drivers.

    If nothing can be found to resolve this then I'd roll back Version 1803 in Settings>Update & Security>Recovery and then hide it until it matures using the Hide Updates Tool downloaded from here: http://download.microsoft.com/download/f/2/2/f2...

    I hope this helps. Feel free to ask back any questions and let us know how it goes. I will keep working with you until it's resolved.
