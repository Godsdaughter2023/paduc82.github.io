    Location of Restore Point? - Microsoft Community

    boblink say:

    Location of Restore Point?

    Now that I have created a Restore Point (Restore Point - Oct 2 2017) on my W10 system, I would to know where on my computer I can find it (where it's located) so that I can keep track of it and/or delete it when I create a newer Restore Point(s)?

    Thank you 

    Bob


    a. user say:

    Restore Points are stored inside a hidden folder called System Volume Information. Windows keeps track of your restore points - you cannot do this separately - and you don't delete them when you make a new one.

    It seems you may be confusing a restore point with a backup. They are two different things. A backup is stored wherever you choose for it to be stored - the best place is anywhere that's not inside your computer - and you can manually delete them as you make new ones, although most people let their backup software do this automatically.

    A restore point, by contrast, is a copy of certain key system files are registry keys at a point in time, that are chosen to give you back access to the UI as well as the basic functionality of Windows, in case you run into trouble. The original idea was that if you have the use of the Windows GUI, you can use Windows' own tools to (hopefully) work your way out of the problem.

    Restore points can still be useful, but with the wider availability of image backups, it's usually easier to simply restore a known-good image rather than try to figure out what's wrong and fix it.
