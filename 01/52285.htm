srimadhwa b say:

Hi Steve,

I realize that you want to stop Windows 10 from automatically downloading updates. I will try to help you with this issue.

I would like to let you know that Windows won’t download updates on connections you mark as “metered”. This ensures Windows won’t waste valuable tethering data or other mobile data on updates that can wait until it reaches a solid, unrestricted Wi-Fi network.

Open the Wi-Fi settings panel and select “Network settings”, or open the Settings app and select “Network & Internet”. Scroll down in the list of Wi-Fi networks and select “Advanced options”. Activate the “Set as metered connection” option here.

Further, I would also like to let you know that there two more other options in the Windows Update screen which you can explore. They are “Change active hours” and “Restart options”.

You can now set the time in which you are most active on your device by adjusting active hours. Active hours lets Windows know when you usually use this device. When a restart is necessary to finish installing an update, Windows won't automatically restart your device during active hours.

When a restart is scheduled, you can use a custom restart time to temporarily override active hours and schedule a custom time to finish installing the current update(s). When your computer restarts on the scheduled custom time, custom restart time will automatically be turned off, and Windows Update will only restart outside of active hours again. You can also manually turn off custom restart time at any time if you change your mind.

Keep us informed to help you further.
