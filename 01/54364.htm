grandpa jim say:

I know numerous ways to open Task Manager. I could even pin it to the Taskbar. I specifically asked how to get it to appear automatically in the Notification Area.

As it happens, I discovered the answer myself and pass it along to others who might find it interesting.

When the Task Manager button is ON, the active icon appears in the notification area when the app is running, displaying the CPU percentage, etc., and remains there even if Task Manager is diminished. However, it disappears when the app is closed.

When the Task Manager button is OFF, the icon does not appear in the Notification Area.

Everytime I turn my computer off the clock stops and resumes from where it left off the next itme I turn my computer back on.  Why is this happening and how do I fix it?


hi, when you turn the power off at your computer, a small round battery supplies power to keep the computer clock running, when this runs out the lack of power stops your computer clock.

 

to maintain clock/time, either replace the battery inside your computer, or do not switch power completely off at wall switch or on/off at back of computer, just close computer, leaving it supplied with power, or even standby, this will keep the computer clock/time running and up to date.

It`s very difficult to tell just from photographs. Ideally you would have to compare it side by side with what can be confirmed as a genuine wired controller.

Neither of my genuine controllers have a slice in the `o` on the holographic stickers, so I wouldn`t worry too much about that.

Oh, and as for the in-line connector/separator, I have had genuine controllers both with and without.
