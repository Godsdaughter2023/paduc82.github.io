jagermeis7er say:

Thought I would type a problem summary for anyone Googling, which is picking up significantly with Destiny connection errors.

When running two Xbox One consoles on one router, achieving two Open NAT's will be very difficult. The list of routers supporting multiple consoles is not confirmed, though it is generally considered that the Netgear Nighthawk N8000 is a good choice. Personally, I tried this very router and was left with Open on one console and Moderate on another.

Open and Moderate will generally be "good enough" for most games but not sufficient for Destiny.

Mythbusting:

  • Port forwarding is NOT a solution as it can only forward ports to one address.
    • If you only have one console, then yes, you can port forward.
    • Port 88 (UDP)
    • Port 3074 (UDP and TCP)
    • Port 53 (UDP and TCP)
    • Port 80 (TCP) forwarding not needed for Port 80!
    • Port 500 (UDP)
    • UDP Port 3544 (UDP)
    • UDP Port 4500 (UDP)
  • Similarly, port triggering will not provide any support either as this is not the purpose of triggering.
  • Placing one console in DMZ and the other on port forwarding will cause conflicts.
  • The primary issue is UPnP which MUST be used.
  • There is potential for static IPs to fix the issue (limited success).
    • Assign static IPs to your consoles within the router, but leave the Xbox One consoles IP settings to "Automatic".
    • Do not manually assign the static IP in the console itself.

An ideal solution is to use two IPs if you can; ask your ISP about this. You may be able to install a switch just after your modem (so it would go outside-->your house-->modem-->switch) From the switch, hook up your router to one port (and one xboxv to the router) and the other xbox on another port. If you have two IPs from your provider, then this will work for you.

TL;DR: UPnP is mandatory for two consoles on one network. Obtaining Open NATs on both consoles is dependent on how well UPnP is implemented on the router. The Netgear Nighthawk has a good track record but your mileage may vary. Using two IPs is the surefire solution if possible.
