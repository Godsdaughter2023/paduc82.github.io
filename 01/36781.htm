a. user say:

Hi Everyone,

 

The narration feature is now available in Movie Maker 2012. You can download the latest version by visiting Windows Essentials 2012.


Once you have downloaded Windows Movie Maker 2012 version, please follow the steps below to record narration.

  1. From the Home tab click on record narration.
  2. Click The Record button to begin recording.

Please review waveforms to make sure that the narration is recording.  If the microphone is not picking up audio, Movie Maker will continue to record. However, the track will be silent. When the narration is stopped, you must save the file to add it to the storyboard. Canceling it will not save the file. 


Note:  Aside from recording narration you can also add prerecorded narration, music or sound file.

You can drag files within the same track. For example, an audio file can be dragged to a new location within the audio track. However, an audio or video asset may not be dragged from one track to another.

 

To know more on what's new in Movie Maker 2012 please  check What's New in Movie Maker 2012?

 


Thanks!

a. user say:

To add narration to your project you can use a sound recorder to record your voice. Below are the steps on how to do so:

1. Click on Start button,
2. Type in Sound Recorder.
3. Record your voice and Save the file.
4. Open your movie on Movie Maker.
5. Under Home tab, click on Add music, then locate your vocals "audio file format".
6. Then click on Open.

You can wear headphones instead of muting your speakers to keep unwanted audio out of your narration. By using headphones, you can also hear any other audio on your video clip. This gives you a better idea of what your audience will hear when they play your published movie.

Note: If there is an existing audio in your video and you want to lower the volume you can adjust it by clicking on "Edit" under "Video Tools", Click "Video Volume" at the left side and adjust it to your preferred setting, to adjust the settings of your Audio (Voice) click "Music Tools", click "Music Volume" and adjust it.
If Sound Recorder is not installed on your computer, you can use a webcam to record your voice then just convert it as an mp3 file.
I understand that narration option will be a great and convenient feature in Windows Live Movie Maker. systems over-charging is not an issue.

Here are some tips to help and troubleshoot battery related issues.

Old battery? Unplug power to computer - remove battery and clean the contacts with a pencil
eraser (do not use this if your battery has thin slots - just clean the knife edges that fit into
them and be careful). Batteries do wear out if old, or it could be a problem with the computer.
Check with the support of the system maker and many of them have on line forums.

Newer type Litium-Ion batteries usually last longer if you do not discharge then below 30%
however on a laptop that will not extend their life that much. Best is to use A/C adapter
whenever possible.
