a. user say:

As you've discovered, Epson doesn't support that model on Windows 10. The most recent driver for that model was issued on 2/7/2013.

When a hardware manufacturer stops supporting their product on Windows, it's usually just a matter of time before it stops working properly, or it may stop working altogether.

Lack of support means that Epson hasn't tested your device on Windows 10, doesn't know for sure whether it will work properly, and isn't updating its driver to keep up with the changes in Windows 10. Only Epson can do these things - they're out of Microsoft's hands.

What's confusing to most people is that an unsupported device can run well for a very long time as Windows changes, until Windows changes enough that the manufacturer's lack of support starts to matter. That usually happens after an update to Windows, which is why you see posts along the lines of "update X wrecked my computer..." only it's not the update that did the damage; it's the hardware manufacturer's lack of support.

That doesn't necessarily mean that your RX680 is done for. If you haven't already, try installing the most recent driver in compatibility mode. That may be enough to get the 4-in-1 going for a while longer, at least.

Failing that, post a question in a community forum that deals with Epson printers. I don't think Epson sponsors their own forum, but you might try this Epson Printer Forum or this iFixit Forum or something else you might find with a web search. FWIW here is Epson Support but don't pin a lot of hope on that.

This is a major problem for me as I use the laptop  for presenting.
aleksandr 10 say:

I did find this solution on another forum:

Here is the solution that worked out for me:

1. Click on the windows icon
2. Type regedit
3. Right-click on regedit icon, click Run as administrator
4. Go to HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Control\Power\PowerSettings\238C9FA8-0AAD-41ED-83F4-97BE242C8F20\7bc4a2f9-d8fc-4469-b07b-33eb785aaca0
5. Double click on Attributes
6. Enter number 2.
7. Go to Advanced power settings (click on Windows button, write power options, click on Power Options, in the selected plan click on the Change plan settings, click on the Change advanced power settings).
8. Click on the Change settings that are currently unavailable
9. Click Sleep, then System unattended sleep timeout, then change these settings from 2 Minutes to 20 for example.
That's it! At least it works for me... 

janelliott_1969 say:

Check your screen saver!  

I had this problem too.  I trouble-shot and changed every power setting I could find.  However the problem was that the SCREEN SAVER in my new Windows 10 computer had come set to 1 minute!  To find the screen saver (which is NOT with the power settings), open your control panel.  Select Personalization.  Now select Screen Saver, which may be one of the icons along the bottom of the window.  Now you can set your screen saver to come on after some more reasonable amount of time.

It's kind of amazing and annoying how hidden the screen saver choices were.

igwegabriel say:

Dell Laptop Battery

canadian tech say:

Chances are, the transformer in the power cord has failed.  Could also be the female port that you plug into the computer is broken.  Most often it is the battery, charger cord combination.

Batteries last about 3 years.  You should replace the charger cord at the same time.  Not terribly expensive Google your  make/model battery cord.  

Batteries and chargers are very specific to your make/model.  Do not substitute.

Hi,

 

'Storage folders' contains archived emails in draft, sent and deleted items from Windows Live Mail’s user created folders. If it is functioning properly, the draft, sent and the deleted messages are not present in the email account's storage folder or not archived elsewhere in Windows Live Mail or the user's hard drive they are most likely gone.

 

If you are using Windows Live Mail 2012, you can view the emails in your storage folder via this path: C:\Users\<user>\AppData\Local\Microsoft\Windows Live Mail\Storage Folders
