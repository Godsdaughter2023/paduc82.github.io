How do you set up Auto-response? - Microsoft Community

margaretyo say:

How do you set up Auto-response?

I can't find any way in Windows Live mail  of setting up an auto-response or out of office response.. Please help. 

a. user say:

Hi,

 

Please be informed that the Out of office auto-reply is not yet available in Windows Live Mail. However you can use rules to set up an auto-response to people who send you emails while you're out of the office. Here are the steps to create an automatic out of office message in Windows Live Mail:

1. Open Notepad.
2. Type the message you want to appear in the body of your auto-response.
3. Select "File" | "Save" from the menu.
4. Type "Vacation Auto-Reply" under File name.
5. Make sure "Text Documents (*.txt)" is selected under Save as type.
6. Click "Save".
7. Close Notepad.
8. Open Windows live mail.
9. Select "Tools" | "Message Rules" | "Mail" from the menu (WLM 2009). Click on "Folders" menu then "Message Rules" for WLM 2011.
10. Go to the "Mail Rules" tab.
11. Click "New".

Make sure "Where the To or CC line contains people" is checked under Select the Conditions for your rule. Alternatively, you can check “For all messages”. It usually makes sense to reply automatically only to messages sent directly to your email address, however.

1. Under Select the “Actions for your rule”, check "Reply with message".
2. Click on the "contains people" link under Rule Description.
3. Type your "email address".
4. Click "Add".
Note: If you have more than one address, repeat the previous two steps. 
5. Click "OK".
6. Now click the "message" link, also under Rule Description.
7. Select "Text (*.txt)" from the Files of type: drop-down menu.
8. Highlight the "Vacation Auto-Reply" file.
9. Click "Open".
10. Under “Name of the rule”, type something like "Vacation Auto-Reply" as well.
11. Click "OK".
12. Click "OK" again.

 

Note: Your computer will have to be left on for the whole time you were out but using this message rule it will automatically reply to all messages with the txt document.

 

I hope this helps.

 

Thanks.

Note When you perform a clean boot, you may temporarily lose some functionality. When you start the computer as usual, the functionality returns. However, you may receive the original error message, or you may experience the original behavior.
