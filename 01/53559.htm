There are three ways of accessing an email account in Windows Live Mail, called protocols: POP3, IMAP and DeltaSync. You can often choose between the first two for any particular email account - provided for example by your ISP or by Gmail or Yahoo - but the third is exclusively for Outlook.com accounts. It's DeltaSync that will eventually stop working, so you'll have to select one of the other two. IMAP works in a very similar way to DeltaSync, so it's the obvious choice for most people.

Second point: you should only have one account actively synchronizing at any one time. Identify the IMAP account that looks best - with the most messages in its folders, for example. Then right-click on the names of all the other accounts and ensure that Include this account when receiving mail or synchronizing is not selected on the General tab.

The superfluous 'special' folders you're seeing will have arisen because synchronize works in both directions. If there's a folder in Windows Live Mail that isn't there at Outlook.com, synchronizing will create it at Outlook.com. You should have avoided this by 'mapping' the IMAP folders before synchronizing, but it looks as if the program got there first.

To get rid of the superfluous ones, sign into the account at Outlook.com. Expand all the folders in the left-hand pane. The top five should be these:

When they're all gone, return to Windows Live Mail.

After a few minutes, right-click on the active account's name and select Show or hide folders.

In the Show/Hide IMAP Folders dialogue, make sure you have the right account selected in the left-hand pane, then click Reset List.
