You could have drive issues as the CD/DVD is actually 4 drives in 1 case ( CD & DVD burn and
CD and DVD read). So it is not unusual for 1 or 2 parts to not function while the others do so

Burning at slower speeds or using the Master Format might help. A CD/DVD cleaner might help.

Brand of CD or DVD disk can also be the issue. Low quality (cheap brands) are always problematic.

CD/DVD's have a +- tolerance and yours might read/write on the edge outside of those discs

gil deg say:

Hi,

The error message Microsoft Visual c++ Runtime Library error that you are getting is sometimes caused by your graphics card. To resolve your concern, we suggest that you uninstall and reinstall your graphics card on your computer.

Kindly follow the steps below:

1. In the Search bar enter device manager. Select Device Manager from the list of results

2. Expand the Display adapters section and right click your graphics card. Select Uninstall device from the menu.

3. Confirmation menu will now appear. Check Delete the driver software for this device and click on Uninstall.

4. Close the Device Manger window as well as the other windows you have opened.

5. Reboot your Windows 10 PC.
