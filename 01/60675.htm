Be sure to unplug the system from the wall outlet before attempting to change the motherboard battery. 

Since you have a Dell computer - tell us the Service Tag number so we can find out more about your system and look for manuals from the Dell WWW site.  The service tag number will be usually be found on the back of the computer case, but it may be located on the top or side of the system.

Tell us the exact error message you see about NTLDR (no paraphrasing).

Prior to this beeping and no boot incident, what do you think happened to the system, when was the last time it worked, did you have a power failure, pull the plug, use the power button, etc. to try to solve some problem?

Do you have a genuine bootable XP installation CD that is the same Service Pack as your unspecified Service Pack (this is not the same as any recovery type CDs that may have come with your system).

When your system reboots the BIOS performs a Power On Self Test (POST) and if there is a problem, reports the result of the POST with beeps.

Beep codes are giving you a message and you need to interpret the beep codes.

Usually a single short beep when restarting is a good sign.  If you are hearing something else you need to figure out what the beep code mean by listening to the pattern of beeps.  Maybe something like:

3 short-2 long-3 short.

Then you can use this Advanced Troubleshooting page where you can find more information about what the beep codes are telling you along with the diagnostic lights on the back of the unit (since I can't find it on Dell's page):

http://www.l3jane.net/doc//2400/advtshoo.htm#1105830

If the beep pattern you hear is not in that list, describe it and tell us what the pattern you hear is using the same short/long example above.

To reset your system to the as shipped from the factory condition (the second worst possible troubleshooting idea), you press Ctrl+F11 at just the right moment (assuming your restore partition is intact) but you might not get very far until you fix the issue reported by the beep codes.

Here is a copy/paste from the Dimension 2400 User Manual about how to do that:

Restore restores your hard drive to the operating state it was in when you purchased the computer.
Any programs or files added since you received your computer permanently deleted from the hard drive. Data files include documents, spreadsheets, e-mail messages, digital photos, music files, and so on. If possible, back up all data before using PC Restore.
NOTICE:
Using PC Restore permanently deletes all data on the hard drive and removes any applications or drivers installed after you received your computer. If possible, back up the data before using PC Restore.
To use PC Restore:
1 Turn on the computer.
During the boot process, a blue bar with www.dell.com appears at the top of the screen.
2 Immediately upon seeing the blue bar, press <Ctrl><F11>.
If you do not press <Ctrl><F11> in time, let the computer finish restarting, and then
restart the computer again.
NOTICE:
If you do not want to proceed with PC Restore, click Reboot in the following step.
3 On the next screen that appears, click Restore.
4 On the next screen, click Confirm.
The restore process takes approximately 6–10 minutes to complete.
5 When prompted, click Finish to reboot the computer.
NOTE:
Do not manually shut down the computer. Click Finish and let the computer completely reboot.
6 When prompted, click Yes.
The computer restarts. Because the computer is restored to its original operating state, the
screens that appear, such as the End User License Agreement, are the same ones that
appeared the first time the computer was turned on.
7 Click Next.
The System Restore screen appears and the computer restarts.
8 After the computer restarts, click OK
