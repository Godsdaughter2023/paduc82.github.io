Hello Gavin,

We appreciate that you posted your concern on our Community forum page. 

There are several possible causes as to why your Surface Go is showing the message that the USB device might need more power. Below is the list of possible causes for getting this message. 

  • Your Surface Go can't power the connected device because it's not supported.

  • Your Surface Go has temporarily limited the amount of power it can provide to the connected device because your PC (or phone) is running on battery power.
  • Your Surface Go has temporarily limited the power it can provide to the connected device because it’s providing power to other connected devices instead.
  • The connected device requires more power than the Surface Go can provide.

On the contrary, there are several solutions you may try and they are:

  • If the USB device can be powered externally, plug it into an external power source.
  • Make sure your Surface Go is plugged into an external power source and not running on battery power.
  • If you have any other USB devices connected to your PC but aren't using them, disconnect them from your PC.

You will create a bootable CD with the file you download. If you are using an older operating system you will need third-party burning software to do this such as Roxio, Nero, or the free ImgBurn. Boot with the CD you made and do a thorough test of the drive. If it fails any physical tests, replace it.

If the hard drive tests good:

Start>Search>type: cmd
When cmd appears in Results above, right-click it and choose "Run as administrator". Supply the password/OK to UAC and an elevated command prompt will open. Type:

fsutil dirty query c: (or the relevant drive letter) [enter]

This will tell you whether the drive is dirty or not. Then still in cmd:

chkntfs /x c: (or the relevant drive letter) [enter]

This tells Windows not to check that drive on bootup. Reboot and it should go to the Desktop. Once Windows has loaded, run cmd again and:

chkdsk /f /r c: (or the relevant drive letter) [enter]

Chkdsk will run through all 5 stages. After it is finished, still in cmd:

fsutil dirty query c: (or the relevant drive letter) [enter]

to see that the dirty bit is not set on that drive.
