Clash in the Clouds - Blue Ribbon Challenges

There are a number of excellent guides for the challenges themselves, such as this one at True Achievements. This is tough achievement to get, so expect to put in a few hours and get to know the maps well by the end of it.

This post is just a primer for anyone who hasn't tried to tackle the achievement for this and wants to.

  1. First and foremost, do not concern yourself with the challenges the first time you try to clear each of the four arenas. You do not have to complete the challenge to complete one of the 15 waves in an arena. For every wave, minus the final wave, that you clear you will receive either an Infusion or a piece of Gear. Some of the challenges will all but demand that you have all of the Infusions and some of the Gear from the four arenas in order to complete it. The better equipped you are, the better your chance of succeeding the challenge.
  2. If you fail a wave, you will have the choice of forfeiting your score and continuing, restarting from wave 1, or going back to the challenge hub. Don't be afraid to forfeit your score and continue, especially until you have all the Infusions. You are rewarded with the Infusions and Gear for completing the wave, not completing the challenge, and it doesn't matter if you continue in the middle of the wave. So long as you complete the wave, you will get your upgrade.
  3. You will also have the option to purchase a single resurrection prior to the start of the wave or during the wave if you make your way to "Booker Office Door" on the map. You can essentially have two of these in a wave, however, if you purchase one prior to the wave and then purchasing it again mid-wave if you happen to die and thus use your first one. Resurrections will save both your score and your challenge progress, so on the particularly hard challenges, it is worth it to purchase them. However, you can only purchase three total resurrections throughout the course of any 15-wave go, so do not purchase them until the wave you expect to use them. If you are trying to get the ribbon for wave 13 of 15, don't purchase a resurrection until wave 13. Just forfeit your scores on waves 1-12 if you happen to die before wave 13.
  4. I recommend upgrading Shield and Salts before Health. Some challenges will be Vigor-dependent, so it helps to have as much Salt as possible. But be sure to take a few Shield upgrades first, as this will definitely help you survive the wave so you can get the Infusions and Gears.
  5. When you have enough Silver Eagles, upgrade all of your Vigors and Founder Weapons before upgrading Vox Weapons. No challenge is going to call for a Vox Weapon. I haven't verified if the Vox counterpart to a Founder weapon will count for a challenge, such as the Heater for a Shotgun, but unless you prefer the Vox Weapons, I would say save them to last. You will eventually have enough Silver Eagles in the DLC to upgrade everything.
  6. Some challenges will ask you to defeat an enemy using a certain Vigor or Weapon, and others will ask you to defeat an enemy using only a certain Vigor or Weapon. The key difference is this: if it has the word "only" in it, you cannot damage or kill an enemy using anything else. An example might be "Defeat all enemies using only the Sniper Rifle." For that challenge, you cannot use damage from anything except the Sniper Rifle. This would mean not using support from Tears, using Possession, or splash damage from enemy rpgs or volley guns. Be very careful, as I had a few I had to repeat because I accidentally shot a rpg out of the air and killed the rpg gunner with the splash damage from the explosion, thus negating my shotgun challenge, for example. However, if the word "only" doesn't appear, you are allowed to use other means to damage to the enemy, but you must kill the enemy using the challenge requirement. An example of that could be "Defeat each enemy using a different Vigor or Weapon." So long as the killing damage is from a Vigor or Weapon that has not killed an enemy on that wave, it doesn't matter how you whittled them down.
  7. If you have not already learned to use it, master the use of the Return to Sender Vigor, especially its quick shield use. Some challenges are going to be very tricky, such as "Kill Two Enemies Using a Fireman's Suicide Attack." Having something to negate the damage while you're setting up the enemies for this is very, very helpful. Using it to collect ammo can also be a life-saver, but it will drain your Salt quickly and you will need to be careful using it in this manner for any challenge that would prevent you from damaging or killing an enemy with it.
  8. Some challenges will be timed. While everyone has their own style, I found it helpful to have a fully upgraded Sniper Rifle, RPG, and Undertow for these. RPGs are good for taking out several enemies at once, Sniper Rifle for quick kills at a distance, and Undertow for knocking enemies off Columbia. Possession is also helpful if you use it early.
  9. Gear can be critical, both as a benefit or a detriment. Swap out your gear as needed, and remember to do this. Anything that increases Vigor or splash damage can completely negate a challenge on accident if you didn't intend to damage or kill an additional enemy, so use things like the Storm hat carefully.
