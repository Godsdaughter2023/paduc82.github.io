Hi Joe,

 

SIP/Skype for Business conversations are endpoint-to-endpoint. If you’re on multiple devices, you must accept the conversation on one of them, and all communication for that conversation will be between that one device and the remote user’s device. If you start a new conversation from a different device to the same user, you will replace existing older conversation, and then all subsequent messages will be between that new device remote user’s device. That’s how Skype for Business works.

Hi,

 

Welcome to Microsoft Community. There might be chances that icon is hidden in notifications area. When this happens, you may perform the following steps:

 

Step 1: Make sure the Icon is not hidden.

  1. Right-click an empty area on the taskbar, and then click Properties.
  2. Under Notification area, click Customize.
  3. For each icon, select one of the following options in the list:
    • Show icon and notifications. The icon always remains visible on the taskbar in the notification area and any notifications are displayed.
    • Hide icon and notifications. The icon is hidden and notifications aren't displayed.
    • Only show notifications. The icon is hidden, but if the program triggers a       notification balloon, it's displayed on the taskbar.
  4. Click OK.

 

You may also follow step 2:

 

Step 2:  The "Safely Remove Hardware" icon can sometimes disappear from the notification area. Even though there are devices installed that require it - it's nowhere to be found.

 

When this happens, you may perform the following steps:

 

1. Open the Run command by pressing the Windows logo key +R.

2. Enter the following command and hit Enter:

 

    RunDll32.exe shell32.dll,Control_RunDLL hotplug.dll

 

This will bring up the "Safely Remove Hardware" dialog box:

 

 

From this dialog box you can click on the device you want to remove, and press Stop. Once Windows is done with it, you can then remove the device.

 

If it happens often enough, you can create a desktop shortcut for the “safely remove hardware” command.

 

a.  Right click on your desktop, click New, and then click Shortcut.

b.  In the resulting dialog box enter the following command:

 

             RunDll32.exe shell32.dll,Control_RunDLL hotplug.dll

 

c.  Click Next and change the shortcut name to Safely Remove Hardware.

d.  Click Finish.

 


 

Now, whenever you need to remove hardware, and the icon isn't present on the taskbar notification area, you can simply double click on this new shortcut and get the job done.

Method 3: If this does not help you might have to update the motherboard drivers and chipset drivers from the manufacturer’s website.

Download and install drivers
