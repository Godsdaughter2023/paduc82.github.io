a. user say:

Two licenses exist, a console license and an account license.

 

Now the console license belongs to the 360 the DLC was first downloaded to. What this means is that any account on the console can access that content, online or off.

 

The account license of course belongs to the gamertag that was used to purchase the DLC. With this license you can play the content on any console but ONLY when connected to Live.

 

So download first on the other console, play on your 360 with the account license.

 

It is worth noting that while there is nothing technically wrong with sharing DLC in this manner it is fraught with problems. The biggest being that if the 360 with the console license ever needs to be replaced, then that license is gone. The only way to get it back would be to perform a license transfer but that would give the console license for ALL your DLC to that new console.
