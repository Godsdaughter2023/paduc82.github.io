techfreak_ say:

Skype for Windows Desktop cannot wake your computer from Sleep.  Only the Skype App can do that for low power processors that support it.  Various versions of the Skype App come preloaded with Windows 10.  Depending on the build of Windows 10 you may be prompted to update it.  If you install Skype for Windows Desktop it will also suggest you update it.

As far as I can tell, not a bug or a phantom.  More than likely this is what occurred:

You have Windows 10 with Skype installed.  When you logged into Windows 10 with a Microsoft account it auto-logged you into many of your Windows Apps including Skype.  This means by simply signing into your computer, you were signed into Skype.  Assuming you have a lower power device, someone called you and that woke up your computer.  Whatever version of Skype you launched to view the call, prompted you to update it.

When Skype for Windows Desktop is running the process is called Skype.exe.  When the Skype App is running the process is called SkypeApp.exe.  When the Skype App is or is not on screen an additional background process called SkypeHost.exe remains to receive calls and IMs.  If you do not want the Skype App to receive calls and IMs you can either set the status to "Do not disturb" or log out of the App.  If you do not want ANY Windows Apps to auto log you into their services when you sign in, simply change your Windows 10 to sign in using a local account instead of your Microsoft account.  That is how I have all my Windows 10 machines configured.  If you do not want the SkypApp at all, simply right click on the Skype icon and select "uninstall".  This is different from Skype for Windows Desktop that needs to be uninstalled from "Programs and Features" in the Control Panel.  Be aware that every major update of Windows 10 will reinstall the Skype App as it is included with the OS.
