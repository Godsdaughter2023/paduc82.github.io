a. user say:

You are correct. In Microsoft's way of looking at things, the upgraded Windows is a continuation of the original Windows, like adding a floor onto an existing building.

If you upgrade to Windows 10 from Windows 8.1, you lose the right to use your original license for Windows 8.1.

Even so, Microsoft has not mastered the science of tracking licenses, so people have sometimes discovered that they can still use their original license even after they upgrade. The older the original license, the better the chances of succeeding. But it's a risk (and, let's face it, illegal.)

For the purposes of activating Windows 10, a virtual machine is considered a unique "device." If you have an activated copy of Windows 10 running in a virtual machine, you can't move it to a physical computer. Not legally, at least.

In the good old (pre-Windows 10) days, you could install Windows into a virtual machine and then migrate V2P if you had the right license. Now, with the new activation system, Windows 10 is permanently bound to the device on which it was first activated.
