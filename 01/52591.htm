stefan steiner say:

Perhaps this will help some of you that have this issue. 

I had this same problem after I removed a Windows 10 machine from my company's AD Domain and enrolled the machine in the Windows Insider Program to get the pre-release Win10 Anniversary Update. After enrolling in the fast ring and waiting for a few hours, the update downloaded and installed. After rebooting, I went into Windows Update and saw My company has a WSUS server to manage Windows Update. It appears the registry entry wasn't reset when the machine was removed from the domain and the GPO that controlled the WSUS settings was removed.

Here are steps that I took to fix this:

1. In regedit, go to: "HKLM\Software\Policies\Microsoft\Windows\WindowsUpdate". In my case, I had WUServer and WUStatusServer reg values which pointed to my company's WSUS server. Also under the AU key, the UseWUServer value was set to 1.

2. Delete "HKLM\Software\Policies\Microsoft\Windows\WindowsUpdate". Export it first just in case this doesn't fix your problem.

3. In the Services.msc MMC snapin, stop the "Windows Update" and "Background Intelligent Transfer Service". Then restart both of them. (I'm not sure if BITS really needed to be restarted, I just do it out of habit).

4. Go back to Windows Update in Settings and press the Retry button. In my case, Windows connected to Windows Update and pulled down a Windows Defender signature file. Success! 


YMMV. Best of fortune to you.

volunteer j say: No, VLC does not have that option.

The freeware Format Factory has a Video Joiner
option that will join the files very quickly and may be
worth a try.

(FWIW...it's always a good idea to create a System
Restore point before installing software or updates)

Format Factory
http://www.videohelp.com/tools/Format_Factory
(the 'direct link' is fastest)
(the file you want to download is : >FFSetup280.zip<)
(FWIW...when installing...you may want to uncheck
'all' the boxes on the final screen)

First you will need to unzip the file or just open the
folder and drag FFSetup280.exe out of the folder
and drop it on your desktop. Left click it to install.

Next...after downloading and installing Format
Factory...you may want to open the program and
left click the 'Option' button on the toolbar and
select an 'Output Folder' / Apply / OK.
(this is where you will find your files after they
are converted)

Once installed...open Format Factory...in the left
pane...choose...Advanced / Video Joiner...
Left click the Add File button and import your video
clips. Choose an output format and left click OK.

Left click the Start button on the toolbar and your
video files will be combined.

Good luck...
