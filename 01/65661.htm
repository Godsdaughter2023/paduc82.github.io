It is good practice to never install more than one antivirus product that runs constantly in the background. They can cause conflicts and affect how well your computer behaves. AV programs that only run on-demand are generally safe to install alongside your main AV, but for emergencies an AV that can be run independently from bootable media is a better backup than AV which is installed in Windows and can potentially be disabled if a virus infects the system.

Windows Defender is not super sophisticated and feature loaded but it is completely free. Its virus definitions are also free and updated quite regularly. It doesn't slow down your computer, doesn't interfere with networking or (in my own experience anyway) any other installed software.... unlike some other security products. Defender doesn't have ads or nag-ware. Defender requires little user interaction to configure and is quite basic to use.

Which AV product is ranked best seems to come down to personal preferences. None give perfect protection and Defender seems to have at least average performance. 

This web page is probably as good a source for AV evaluations as any:  

https://www.av-test.org/en/antivirus/home-windows/

Personal perspective, over the years I've tried and used many different AV products, some of them for years and some even retail. Every single one except Defender has been the root cause of some annoying issue or other... even if it was just incessant begging for money or in-your-face advertising other products.
