Hi MD.FAKHRUDDIN GAZZALI FAHIM,

The boot logo is actually determined by the firmware interface used during the installation. Computer auto turns on at same time daily - Microsoft Community

shashikavde say:

Computer auto turns on at same time daily

My computer turns on itself automatically at 10.00 AM.  This problem stated about 6 months back and I have no idea what brought this on.  I would appreciate help in getting this auto tunr on stopped.  Thanks. 

a. user say:

Well, none of those Microsoft engaged Support Engineer "expert" ideas will help you if you are running Windows XP.

It could be a Scheduled Task that is causing your system to wake up at 10AM every day but I think that is unlikely, but you can check.

Click Start, Run and in the box enter:

%windir%\tasks

Click OK and the Scheduled Tasks folder will open and you can expand the window and drag the column headings around to see if there is a ST configured to run at 10AM every day.  If you find one and don't want it running every day, disable it or change the Properties of the Task.

It is more likely that your system of unspecified make and model with unspecified operating system and Service Pack is getting a "magic packet" from your Internet Service provider (ISP).

That means that even if the system is turned off, some systems have a feature that will wake them up when there is some activity on their Internet connection - even if the machine is powered off (that is by design and can be useful).  Sometimes this can be referred to as receiving a "magic packet" that is just what the system needs to see on the Internet connection to wake itself up.  Often these things seem to happen shortly after midnight when your Internet Service Provide (ISP) might be sending out these magic packets.

To test the WOL theory, the next time you turn off your computer, physically unplug the Internet connection from the computer and then wait (as long as you think it takes to convince you) and see if the system turns itself on or not when the cable is unplugged at the time you suspect - after waiting longer than it usually takes for it to turn on by itself.  That means you may have to wait a day or two to test this idea.

You can test it today if you turn your system off and  unplug your network cable before 10AM your time.  Then wait a while and see what happens with the cable unplugged.

There could be a setting in your system BIOS to enable/disable this feature or in your network card, but since we don't know anything about your system, that is just a guess.  You can look around in your system BIOS and see if you can spot this feature and disable it, save the setting and see how it goes.

You might visit the manufacturers web site for your system of unspecified make and model to see how to disable this feature.

There could also be a setting to disable in the Properties of your network interface card to not allow the system to be started or come out of hibernation or standby on a signal from the device or router.  You can look in Device Manager and the Properties of your device for those settings.

On my motherboard in the system BIOS settings under Power Management is a section called "Wake Up Setup Event" and under that are the various ways that one could configure the system to wake up from some event.  Under that section are things like keyboard, mouse and one is "Resume by Onboard LAN" which if I enable that, and the correct "magic packet" comes into the built in Ethernet connection, my system would startup by itself.

Try not to think of it as an XP thing you need to change - it is more of a hardware thing that has to do with your motherboard and/or network interface and the way you connect to the Internet.

Wake on LAN (WoL) support is sometimes implemented in the motherboard of the computer. Most modern motherboards with an embedded Ethernet controller support WoL so you need to find it and disable it.

This can be done in Windows from the Properties of the network adapter in the Device Manager, on the "Advanced" or "Power Management" tab.  You may have to look around for it.

Look for things like "Allow this device to bring the computer out of standby" or "Only allow management stations to bring the computer out of standby" and make adjustments to disable and turn off these features.

Then you may have to wait a day to see how things work.
