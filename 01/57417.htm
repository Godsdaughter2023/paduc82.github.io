lemp say:


The eHome Infrared Transceiver is a piece of PC hardware built directly into a computer. It detects infrared signals from remote controls and translates these signals into control information that the computer can use.

The eHome Infrared Transceiver is used with a remote control to control Windows Media Player and other media applications and devices on your PC. You can use the remote to change the settings on these media players from across the room. The remote is packaged with your PC

You can uninstall the software for the transceiver by right-clicking your desktop, selecting "Manage," selecting "Device Manager," clicking the plus sign next to "Human interface devices," right-clicking "eHome Infrared Transceiver and clicking "Disable" or "Uninstall." The device will still be in your computer but will be inoperable.

"Lead3", you got me thinking... even though my drivers were registered as up-to-date, it still seemed like a driver issue.  So I tried a couple of things and was able to identify and solve the issue as follows.

SOLUTION:  Windows 7 incorrectly recognized my Nvidia graphics card as a Quadro FX 360.  In fact, it is a Quadro FX (Notebook).  That subtle difference meant that even the most up-to-date drivers were the wrong ones.  For those of you who are experiencing the same issue, the graphics card indicated by your operating system may not be correct.  By forcing the download of alternate drivers (for a different graphics card) the system will operate as expected.

You can cancel your Xbox subscription either immediately or by stopping automatic renewal. If you cancel immediately, you’ll also lose access to the subscription service at the same time. If you stop automatically renewing your subscription, you can continue to use the service until it expires.

To cancel your Xbox subscription immediately, go to Cancel or turn off recurring billing for a Microsoft subscription and follow the steps under Cancel.

To stop automatically renewing your subscription, go to Cancel or turn off recurring billing for a Microsoft subscription and follow the steps under Turn off recurring billing.

Important

  • You can’t cancel an Xbox subscription if it’s past due or suspended. You must pay the past-due balance before you can cancel or turn off auto renewal. For more info, see Update payment info to continue your Xbox subscription or How to pay a past due balance on an Xbox subscription
  • If you have a prepaid subscription, you won’t see the Cancel or Remove option next to your Xbox subscription because prepaid subscriptions automatically expire and you aren’t billed.
