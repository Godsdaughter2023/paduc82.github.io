Hi,

There is a program called Parted Magic that comes as an ISO that you burn to a CD that's bootable..

Is has an ERASE feature that uses the SSD's internal mechanisms to RESET it...

The newest free version is 2013-08-01.

While you're at it, you might check to see if there are any Firmware updates for your SSD.

I noticed a couple of things you mentioned that are not quite right... whether a typo, or other.

The AHCI setting in BIOS is to set the way the Computer uses the drive... not Windows...

Your choices should be something like AHCI, AHCI\RAID, or IDE... You want AHCI...

The MBR and GPT are entirely different, and have to do with how the disk is FORMATED and the Formatting method... MBR or GPT... MBR is the more common, and GPT is required to use UEFI...

But this has nothing to do with AHCI... two entirely different functions that are only related because they both effect the SATA or IDE drives.

You didn't mention what type of media you are using to Install Win 8, but my understanding is that to install in UEFI mode, Win 8 must be on a bootable USB Flash drive...

Otherwise, you might set your BIOS to Legacy or UEFI\Legacy or Legacy... whichever options you have.

You also mention installing an "Upgrade". What are you upgrading, Win 8, or Win 7 to Win 8, or other ?

BTW... unhook all of your other hard drives during the install... 

Having them attached during install may cause nothing but headaches later.
