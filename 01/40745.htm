Hi DB,

You've probably already got an answer to your problem but I did fix this problem for a client of mine.

Basically, the answer is probably going to be that you probably have a Realtek "sound card" on your motherboard and this is the problem.  It probably has to do with the driver, since it usually works in Windows 7.  Here's a cheap way to get Dragon working.

If you have an ANALOG speaker/microphone combo (such as a headset or speaker/mic, etc., follow the directions below for the Sabrent USB sound card below and then go to Step 1:

Go to Microcenter.com's website and order the Sabrent USB audio sound card for $10(search for it with the SKU # 550509).

If you have a digital USB solution, like a headset with a USB connector with a built-in sound card and then go to Step 6:

You probably can use this directly, it depends on the device and Dragon.

1. When it arrives (you can also order it from Amazon or anywhere else), turn on your computer and run the "Device Manager" as an administrator.

2. Scroll to "Sound, video, and game controllers" on the list and expand it.  If you seek something like "Realtek High Definition Audio" listed, right-click it and choose "Disable"

3. Connect your ANALOG device to the ports on the Sabrent USB audio card

4. Now, insert the Sabrent USB audio sound card (I've confirmed this works on multiple PCs running Windows 10).  Should be plug and play for Windows 10, drivers-wise.

5. Now open Dragon and make sure that this new sound card and headset are the default input/output and you should be good to go.

6. Turn on your computer and run the "Device Manager" as an administrator.

7. Scroll to "Sound, video, and game controllers" on the list and expand it.  If you seek something like "Realtek High Definition Audio" listed, right-click it and choose "Disable"

8. Connect your USB device to the computer

9. Now open Dragon and make sure that this new sound card and headset are the default input/output and you should be good to go.

A couple of caveats:

A. In case your USB input/output device doesn't work, I've tested a couple of Logitech and Creative devices that have worked fine.

B. I think the C-Media "sound card" in the Sabrent device will work every time, whether it's in a Sabrent USB sound card or another manufacturers', but at least Sabrent is a recognizable name brand (maybe not Tier 1, but not Tier Z)

rey bel1 say:

Hi,

This issue may occur if changes were made on the display settings or the display driver is outdated or damaged. Let's tune your monitor display first and check if this would help resolve your concern. To do this on a Windows 10 device, right-click on the desktop screen and select/click Display settings>Advanced display settings>ClearType Text to open the ClearType Text Tuner window, and then proceed with tuning the text that you see on the screen. After done, check if the issue persists.

If the issue still persist after doing the steps above, we recommend that you update your graphics driver by doing the following:

  1. Select the Start button, type Device Manager, and select it from the list of results.
  2. Expand one of the categories to find the name of your device, then right-click (or tap and hold) it, and select Update Driver. For graphics cards, expand the Display adapters category, right click your graphics card and select Update Driver.
  3. Select Search automatically for updated driver software.
  4. If Windows doesn't find a new driver, you can try looking for one on the device manufacturer's website and follow their instructions.

If these steps don't work, try reinstalling the driver: Open Device Manager, right-click (or tap and hold) the name of the device, and select Uninstall. Restart your PC and Windows will attempt to reinstall the driver.
