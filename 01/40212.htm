cmdrkeene say:

The sounds you hear are likely the hard drive.  All hard drives fail, typically there's a 20% failure rate of drive that are 4 years old (meaning 1 out of 5 drives will break and die within 4 years).  That's why it's always critical that you have a backup of your important files somewhere else, so that when that day comes you can get a new hard drive without losing all your important stuff.  Drives that spin or are inside laptops that get moved tend to die younger because they spin at 7200 RPMs and are subject to a lot of torque and G-forces when you move the computer.

If you don't have a backup, I'd do that first, before trying anything else.  Even if the problem isn't severe now, running a virus scan or any other drive scan that makes the drive work hard for a long time could put it over the edge.

I will say that it is normal to see programs use multiple processes like that.  Google Chrome (for just one example) does this so that even if one tab of a website crashes, it won't crash all your other tabs because they are running separately.

It's not usually normal for a new computer to make those sounds, so that's why I'm worried the drive could be impending a soon failure.  I just don't want you to lose your important files if it really does die.

There is no available command in the command prompt yet that lets users connect to a wireless network they haven't connected before. For now, you can only connect to the wireless network via command prompt if the network profile is already registered in the computer.
