Recovery partition is provided by the company manufacturer. It will not affect your OS after deleting that partition since Windows 10 is installed in C: drive.

 

To delete a hard disk partition, follow the steps below:

 

Step 1: Open Run command by pressing Windows Key + R.

Step 2: Type diskmgmt.msc and press Enter.

Step 3: Right click on the disk partition you want to delete and select Delete option.

Step 4: Select OS(C:) disk partition, right click and select Extend Volume.

myronh say: I believe this is a UAC issue. Now the first and second issue are somewhat related. When you install a program the installer needs to make changes to system files, specifically the registry, so that it will run properly (or at all). Because you have a password on your account anytime a program that doesn't have UAC support needs to make a change to your system files it needs administrator controls and you will have to input your password. If you didn't have your account password protected you would just receive a prompt that you would click Yes to continue.

Therefore, lets take care of the second issue first, the account password. There are two options here. 1) you can go into your user account and change the password to blank. You will have to supply the old password (I believe) but that would be it. You may still have to go to the login screen to select your user upon boot up but no more passwords. You probably want to do this anyway to avoid the password check for the UAC prompt regardless of if you decide to go with auto-login...which is 2) Auto-login: Here is a quick walkthrough on how to log in straight to your desktop. For some reason I never had to do that, I've always had auto-login except for a brief period before my last computer blew up when I was running Ultimate so, if you only have one account and no password, you may not have to do this either.

Now, as to the first problem, if you don't even want to be bothered with the UAC prompts you can turn those off as well. It's not recommended so do it at your own risk. Here is a quick walkthrough on the UAC and how to use the slider bar to chose the level of protection you're comfortable with.

You certainly should have all the permissions you need. The only way you wouldn't is if you were a regular user and someone else was the administrator (even then you would most likely have the permissions to change your password to blank). Since this is your computer and you are the only, or at least initial, user you are part of the administrator group. That's why you can install software with your account password for administrator permissions.

If this doesn't work, post back with what you tried and exactly what is happening and I'll try to find a better solution.

  2. Select Accounts and click on Your e-mail and accounts from the left side panel of the window.

  4. Open the file/folder where you have saved pictures on your PC and select a picture of your choice.
