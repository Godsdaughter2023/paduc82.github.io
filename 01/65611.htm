Why Multiple Layers of Sign-In Procedures? - Microsoft Community

tracykay1 say:

Why Multiple Layers of Sign-In Procedures?

Why do I have to go through multiple layers of security to sign in to my Skype?  In order to sign in, I have to enter user name and password, of course, but then Skype send a code to another one of my devices, then I have to enter that code before I can use Skype?  In years past, it was just user name and password, and my Skype was signed in all the time.  How can I get rid of the extra procedures?
jomel_g say:

Hi

This happens when "Two Step Verification" is activated on your account. If you turn on two-step verification, you’ll get a security code to your email, phone, or authenticator app every time you sign in. When it’s turned off, you will only have to verify your identity with security codes periodically, when there might be a risk to your account security.

Below are the steps on how you can turn this feature off:

  1. Go to the Security basics page, and sign in with your Skype account.

  2. Select more security options.

  3. Under Two-step verification, choose Turn off two-step verification to turn it off.

I hope this helps.
