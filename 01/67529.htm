You may also reduce the touchpad sensitivity. Follow these steps:

c.       Examine the left panel of the "Properties" window and expand the option labeled "Sensitivity." Click on the option labeled "Touch Sensitivity."

d.      Reduce the touch sensitivity setting by moving the slider to the right, towards the "Heavy Touch" label. Experiment with different levels of sensitivity until you find one suited to your needs.

e.      Click on the button labeled "OK" to close the "Properties" window; repeat with the "Mouse" control panel. Your new touchpad sensitivity settings are now saved.

Method 1:

Check if you need to update drivers for your Bluetooth adapter from Device Manager.

a.   Click on Start, type Device Manager and enter.

b.   Click on Other Devices and find your Bluetooth device and see if there are any warning symbols. If yes update the drivers.

c.   Right click and click on Properties, and go to Driver tab and click on Update driver.

See Update a driver for hardware that isn't working properly for more information.

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows7/Update-a-driver-for-hardware-that-isnt-working-properly

After making sure that Bluetooth is enabled and working properly in your system, turn on your device and make it discoverable, sometimes called pairing mode. 

To make it discoverable, check the manual that came with your device or visit the manufacturer's website.

Once you've verified that a Bluetooth adapter is installed on your computer, and the device you want to connect with is discoverable, you can add most Bluetooth enabled devices to your computer. Here's how:

a. Open Devices and Printers by clicking the Start button, and then, on the Start menu, clicking Devices and Printers.

b. Click Add a device, and then follow the instructions.

c.   Click the Bluetooth enabled device you want to add to your computer, and then click Bluetooth devices.

Note: - Windows might ask for pairing code or PIN number for authorizing the wireless device.

Method 2:

Check your Bluetooth device does not have any yellow warning symbol in Devices and Printers in Control Panel.

If you find a warning symbol, it may be because your blackberry might have a service that allows it to stream audio to a Bluetooth headset. This service isn't needed for the device to work with your computer, but if Windows can't find a driver for that service, it might report that the device failed.

You can disable the unsupported Bluetooth service by right-clicking the device in Devices and Printers, clicking Properties, and then clicking the Services tab. Clear the check box next to each unsupported service, and then click OK. This will make the yellow warning icon disappear.
