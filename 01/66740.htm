andrew france say: Killing processes from task manager can get you out of trouble, especially if one particular process is grinding your machine to a halt, or something like IE has stopped responding. But I would only advise killing a process that you actually know what it is, and like you said this is usually only temporary fix until you restart your machine. So doesn't really help for trying to speed up your machine during startup.

Also, background programs can restart processes that have been stopped, this is usually the case with Anti-Virus Software which is protecting itself from viruses that might be trying to shut it down.

When trying to lighten the load on a machine at startup, I always look first at the notification area down by the time. How many background apps have you got running down there? Things like MSN messenger, Skype, Adobe updater, Quicktime updater, they'll all congregate down there taking up resources. Sometimes you'll find in the settings of each program they'll be an option to stop it from running at startup. Or at least it gives you an idea of the apps you no longer need, and therefore can uninstall.

Next take a look at your Startup folder under All Programs. Here you'll find other programs that will start running as soon as they get the chance. you can normally safely remove things from here

Open MSConfig and look under the Startup tab, you'll find a whole list of things here, and you normally get a good description of each item. You can select the items you do not want to run at startup and disable them.

There is also a very useful tool freely available called HijackThis from Trend Micro, which allows you to deep scan your registry and file settings showing up things like startup processes and apps. With this you can remove these settings completely, but be warned it does not seperate what's safe and not safe to remove.

Lastly, I guess you could check the Registry manually with regedit, and really there only two places I would look at:
HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Run
HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\RunOnce
This will show you apps that run at startup. Just make sure you backup the registry key before you make any changes. Mistakes in the Registry can cause serious problems in Windows, so always tread with care, and always make a backup.
