palmettobling say:

Ok, tried everything.  Well, let's start here: Teredo Tunneling

Teredo is basically translating IPv6 traffic packets onto an IPv4 network, when there is no native IPv6 connection.  One thing you can try is to avoid that altogether and allow traffic to an IPv6 network.  That might require settings on Windows to be changed, as well as the router, IN ADDITION to possibly contacting your ISP about enabling IPv6 traffic.

In Windows it can be as simple as "Changing Adapter Settings" for your Network Adapter and checking the IPv6 option, but for your Router and ISP, that availability can vary, and is different for each device.

Take a look into that, and if you have some details on your equipment, we can try to guide a bit on enabling that, if available.
