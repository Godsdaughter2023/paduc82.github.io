considerate_guy say:

For the best results, crop your photos in Photo Gallery to be horizontal and in the exact same aspect ratio as your project in Movie Maker.  So for example, if you plan to make a widescreen (16 x 9) project, crop your photos in Photo Gallery to 16 x 9.  And if you plan to make a standard (4 x 3) project, crop to that ratio in Photo Gallery.


To set the aspect ratio in Movie Maker, go to the Project tab.  To crop photos in Photo Gallery, double-click on the photo to bring up the photo editor (big view of the photo).  Under Adjustments, find Crop.  But click under the icon (where the word Crop and a black triangle are) and then choose Proportion.  Choose widescreen (16 x 9) or 4 x 3.  And crop the photos so they are horizontal (landscape view) not vertical (portrait view).


It seems like what is happening is that you are cropping some photos to close to a 3 x 2 aspect ratio.  Many DSLRs use this ratio by default.  In that aspect ratio, Movie Maker tries to be helpful and will crop automatically to make the photo fit the full width or height, but that means you might lose part of the photo.  To avoid this, do the cropping yourself to the exact aspect ratio, as I noted above.

I'm not sure I can explain in a way that makes any sense but will give it a try.

Basically, you are asking about aspect ratio...Width in relation to Height.

Most digital cameras use the 4:3 aspect ratio. For example, a best quality
unedited photo from a 16 megapixel camera is 4608x3456 pixel dimensions
or an aspect ratio of 4:3.

So...if you crop a photo and do not maintain the aspect ratio...the overall
shape of the photo (Height in relation to Width) will be altered. In many
editing programs you can choose 4:3 or 16:9 crop frames.  These can
insure that your cropped photo is the correct aspect ratio.

Sometimes when the top and bottom of a photo is cut off...it is because
most of your files are in Landscape Orientation (Horizontal) and the affected
photo is in Portrait Orientation (Vertical). If you pre-crop the photo yourself...
you can include the most important content instead allow auto-crop to cut
off the top of someone's head.

davewillett say:

Hello VxShady,

I was experiencing the same problem and your last post gave me some ideas, leading to the following fix that worked for me.

  1. Open a command prompt with right-click and use the "Run as Administrator" option.
  2. Use sfc to restore the correct version of spoolsv.exe, "sfc /scannow".
  3. Start the Spooler service to confirm it works and set it to start automatically.
  4. Open a Windows Explorer with right-click and use the "Run as Administrator" option.
  5. Navigate to the Windows\System32 folder, right-click on spoolsv.exe and choose the properties option.
  6. Tick the Read Only checkbox and click OK.
  7. Copy the spoolsv.exe file to the desktop for good measure.
  8. Reboot.

The program or service that was replacing your spoolsv.exe file with a corrupt version should now be unable to overwrite it. This solved the problem for me and spooler service started automatically after the reboot.

ronin0ni say:

Battlemages perk trees can get thin.

You need damage mitigation. Get this through Alteration OR Conjuring. Alteration beefs your armor, Conjuration gives the enemy alternate targets. I tend to think Conjuration is better since the summons will also cause damage and you are wearing armor, not relying on robes.

Destruction is an obvious tree to drop points in. Pick 2 elements to pump or you'll find yourself in trouble facing enemies resistant to your primary, and these 2 perks are the ONLY way to buff destruction damage. You can skimp on the finishers to save points (near dead enemies are near dead... just finish them). I recommend Fire and Lightning, or Ice and Lightning. Lightning drains Magicka which makes it primo for fighting dragons and magi... I like fire better then ice because of the increased damage and # of frost resistant enemies...

One Hand is your main combat tree. You can go for 2 hand (or dual wield) if you plan to swap back and forth between dual dest and dual combat.

Block... can be ignored IMO but others say it's a must. You probably want to skill it up, but I don't think it's worth points since mostly you'll be using spells and weapons.

Restoration has a few must haves. Regeneration, Recovery, and Respite. Regen improves HP recovered for all spells, Respite makes it so you heal HP and stamina (meaning you can convert Magicka to Stamina) and Recovery improves your mana regen (a must sicne you won't have +100% regen robes). You should also pick up Avoid death... amazing 1 pt perk that you'll have the prereqs for already. You will want to go down to at least Apprentice level perk, maybe adept, so you can get faster mid-combat heals. Fast Healing with Regen heals 75 though, at half cost (35mp) so that is probably sufficient and only an apprentice level spell

I think Enchanting benefits Battlemages quite nicely, but your crafting perks are up to what you want. It is thematic with the build however and ties in nicely to pump your weapons and armor. This way you can also Frost enchant weapons to get the stamina/slow effect going (If you Fire/Lightning your destro). Alternately you can go with Smithing to craft and improve your weapons and armor.

You'll need to choose between heavy or light armor. Both have benefits. If you take smithing, even if you go with light armor, you might want to go down the heavy armor side of smithing. Daedric weapons are the best, and chances are you'll want Dragon Scale for your light armor anyways (more accessible/available and better looking, IMO, than Glass which is only moderately better)

Don't even think about archery perks... you're a Battlemage, use spells for ranged. Archery is for warriors and thieves.

Personally I'm going even a bit more hybrid and it may bite me in the @@@.... I'm adding stealth for 6x sword crits and disappearing in combat (for more crits) relying on my summons to help keep mobs busy while I work my way. I'm also planning on taking the dual weapon perks instead of going down for the paralyze strike and +10% sword crit. The idea is that I'll sneak in, instagib (or seriously injure) an enemy, call forth summons, and then enter a melee frenzy of swinging dbl enchanted swords as fast as I can (+35% swing speed on dual wield & +10% crit chance should pair nicely) and using dual cast destro magic for range.
