frederik long say:

Sorry, you can't. Microsoft designed Windows in such a way that the operating system, the applications and the user data all dwell on the same partition. This makes sense as far as the apps are concerned because they are tightly linked to the operating system. As far as user data is concerned - well, Microsoft pretty well forces you to store it in one of the profile folders under the folder C:\Users.

There are two methods that deliver more or less what you want:

  • Create an image of the system drive when the machine is brand new.
  • Use the hidden partition to perform a destructive Factory Restore.

arielmt say: OpenDNS is a DNS name resolution provider.  If neither you nor anyone you know set it up on your PC, router, or modem, then it's almost certainly your ISP partnering with OpenDNS to give you name resolution.

DNS is the component of the modern Internet that allows your computer to know how to reach any domain name on the Internet, such as "answers.microsoft.com".

OpenDNS is not harmful.  I've found it to be occasionally annoying, but it's not harmful.

Most often when OpenDNS blocks access to a site, especially if it was one provided in an email message, it's because the site engages in a harmful activity such as silently installing malware on your PC, or tricking you into installing malware by promising that it's something you need or will help your PC instead of harm it.  OpenDNS also blocks access to a site that pretends to be a site you trust, in order to keep you from being tricked into giving sensitive information like your passwords to a total stranger.
