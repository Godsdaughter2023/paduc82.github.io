Hello Rob,

Both are Anti-Spyware, two or more Anti-Spyware in one PC could cause issues and conflict. Whichever of the two given alternatives is the case, if you have installed another antivirus app, Windows Defender will turn off automatically.

Here's how to disable Windows Defender Antivirus via Group Policy:

Note: Local Group Policy Editor is only available in Windows 10 Professional, Enterprise, and Education.

1. Open the Local Group Policy Editor. (Type gpedit.msc in the Cortana search box. Click on the app to launch.)

2. In the left pane of the Local Group Policy Editor window, follow this path: Windows Defender Antivirus > Turn off Windows Defender Antivirus (double-click).

3. Tick the radio button for Enabled. Then click on the OK button.

Important Note: Antivirus software can help protect your computer against viruses and other security threats. In most cases, you shouldn't disable your antivirus software. If you have to temporarily disable it to install other software, you should re-enable it as soon as you're done. If you're connected to the Internet or a network while your antivirus software is disabled, your computer is vulnerable to attacks.

Hope this information helps.
