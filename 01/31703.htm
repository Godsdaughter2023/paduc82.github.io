If your username is now Administrator then you are indeed logged into the Built-In Administrator account. 

The Built-In Administrator account suppresses UAC protection so you, or an internet hacker who has got through to your system, can install anything or change anything without any Admin warning being shown onscreen. 

The intended use of the Built-In Administrator account is during automatic system recovery rather than for normal operations. 

The safest thing to do with the Built-In Administrator account is to use it to create additional password-protected Admin-level local user accounts and then to disable the Built-In Administrator account so that it cannot be corrupted.  Admin-level accounts created this way give you the ability to get in the system to fix things if, for example, your day-to-day user account suffers from user profile corruption.
