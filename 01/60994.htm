spiritx say:

Hi,

Note do not remove any Ghosted Devices for this issue.

There are two type of Ghosted Devices.

1. Devices that were once connected and no longer are. They will not have any drivers loaded.
If you ever re-connect the device then the driver will be found and loaded without the usual

search for a driver. Though these can be removed there is rarely any reason to do so.

2. Devices which are part of another device driver such as, but not limited to, sound, video and
game controllers, or system non-plug and play devices. Those will be activated when they are
called for and should never be de-activated. Also these will updated when the parent driver is
updated using the normal methods.

So since Ghosted Device either do not have any drivers loaded or are part of a parent driver
setup there is no reason to consider them in this case. And many reasons to just leave them
alone.
