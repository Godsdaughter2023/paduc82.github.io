considerate_guy say:

In order to be able to decode video, the necessary codec must be loaded on your system. MPEG-2 is a codec that requires a licensing fee to use and someone must pay for it.

The MPEG-2 codecs for decoding and encoding were provided along with most versions of Windows 7. But after that, support for them was dropped. Apart from the cost which would need to be passed along to every licensed copy of Windows, my guess is the MPEG-2 video codec is seen as a legacy codec. MPEG-2 is used on video DVDs and can't handle HD video directly. It has a maximum resolution of 480p where HD requires 720p or 1080i/p.

While you could possibly pay for and install your own MPEG-2 codec, the cost is quite expensive and the results aren't guaranteed to let you edit those files anyway. The best workaround I can suggest is to find a conversion program that can turn the .mpg2 files into a compatible format like .wmv. That's the format the Movie Maker prefers.
