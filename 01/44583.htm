Hi Andy,

 

Hibernation puts your open documents and programs on your hard disk, and then turns off your computer. Of all the power-saving states in Windows, hibernation uses the least amount of power. The computer uses the Hiberfil.sys file to store a copy of the system memory on the hard disk when the hybrid sleep setting is turned on. If this file is not present, the computer cannot hibernate. A hardware device might be keeping your computer awake. USB flash drives and USB mice are common devices that can keep your computer awake.
