Another thing to check is if you have the option to select the SSD in the caddy as the first boot device in your UEFI firmware/BIOS settings.

The SSD has to show up as a UEFI boot device and be visible as that from BIOS.  That means it needs to be a GPT disk with the right partitioning scheme (System files on a small FAT32 partition, etc.).  Windows setup would normally take care of setting that up properly.

I figured it out! I did it! 

Before you start research your motherboards "select boot device key". For MSI motherboards, BIOS is del key and F11 or F10 is the other. 

If you have 2 hard drives, disconnect the one that does not have the operating system on it. AKA your HDD you probably have all your files in, just in case the system deletes the files. 

Here is how I did it:

  1. Use another computer and download windows 10 onto a 8gb flash drive. (8gb because it is large enough to store windows 10. Also make sure the flash drive has nothing on it)
  2. Go to this site https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/software-download/windows10ISO and follow the instructions to download the file. PLEASE MAKE SURE IT IS A .ISO OTHERWISE IT WILL NOT BOOT.
  3. Once that is done, safely remove flash drive and plug into a usb port of the poop computer. 
  4. When you start up again, quickly and repeatedly hit F11 or F10 to get on a "select boot device screen."
  5. You should she 2 options, 1 is your original hard drive with the operating system and the USB drive. SELECT THE USB DRIVE.
  6. From then on, follow the instructions to download windows 10 onto the busted hard drive. Will take a while
  7. Once completed, shut off machine, unplug USB, replug in your HDD (or additional SSD if you have one). 
  8. Repeat step 4, but now pick the hard drive which has the newly installed operating system. 

It's a pain to go through but it will save your computer.
