- If you are getting a DX error what game is it for, and what brand and model graphics processor does

your machine use ?

- The following is a list of the Direct X .dll's you will have when the files are up to date.
Go to the Windows / System 32 folder, (and SysWOW64 if you have 64bit).  
They are in alphabetical order and will start with d3dx9 - 24 > 43 . Then d3dx10 - 33 > 43 & finally d3dx11 - 42 > 43.
There should also be - d3d9, d3d10 and d3d11 (among others).

These come before the D3Dcompilers - 33>47.
- Win10 currently has only two d3d 12 files. One in System 32,and SysWOW64 .
There are more DX files, but these are most of the graphics related Direct X  .dll's

- Unfortunately the Technology Update 'audio talk ' linked by Billion is not relevant, and useless for updating DX,

[ From Billions link  "...This talk brings you up to speed with the API, offers tips on how to get your renderer

up and running, presents key feature...  " ]

The DirectX Web Installer  below will install any 'missing' DX files, which may help if one of the DX11 files is missing -

Download DirectX End-User Runtime Web Installer from Official Microsoft Download Center

bossdweeb say: I can't answer that without benchmarks for GTA4. I have no personal experience with laptop configurations.
It's difficult to extrapolate from  the games reviewed, but as I said earlier I expect,  you would be looking and low and medium settings even with a small monitor. You may get 30-40fps average.
Games don't in themselves have fps requirements, but when frame rate drops
below 20 things start getting seriously choppy and crashing is more likely.

People have different opinions on what are playable fps. I'm quite happy with quite low minimum fps with some games. I used to play Crysis with minimum of 20fps.
I consider 35+ fps average as smooth in most games. Some games seem to look smoother with lower fps than others.
Some games, like Oblivion have a very variable frame rate. Oblivion on my
current machine (AMD Phenom 965 @3.4GHz / GTX560ti / resolution 1920x1080) will still drop as low as 20fps at times (when riding a fast horse) but averages closer to 50fps.
Other games, like Crysis 2 have a very steady fps with only a 10fps variation.
However,  many would insist that anything below 50fps is unplayable. I guess they have Superman eyes or a lot of money for  high end machines.

Personally - I wouldn't bother with GTA4 on your laptop because I like to see
all the eye candy a game can offer.  Older games and games  with lower requirements would be better suited to your laptop - imo.
I found GTA Vice City and San Andreas were more fun than GTA4.

The issue you're describing sounds like you are possibly installing the incorrect driver for your sound card. 

Have you verified that the driver is for your specific device?

If you look in your system device manager, is there a yellow exclamation mark next to the sound controller?
