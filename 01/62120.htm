a. user say:

I have very fond memories of Windows 98 SE.

In that era, CDRW was an uncertain technology. A CDRW could be reliably read with the same software (and software version) that burned it, but there was no guarantee that any other software would read it. People often discovered this when they switched from Roxio to Nero or vice versa.

Eventually, CDRW fell out of popularity because the much higher capacity of DVDs and later Blu-ray made the re-writable disk unnecessary.

I have two solutions for your wife:

1- Can't miss: Upload all her files to cloud storage, like OneDrive or Dropbox.  The ancient Internet Explorer in W98 can do that. Then download them to the new computer.

2- Not guaranteed: Burn the files to a regular CD (i.e., not a RW.) Whether or not the files are recognized depends on how the software formats the CD.
