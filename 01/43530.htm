Windows 10 - WiFi DHCP not assigning address - Microsoft Community

kpx1 say:

Windows 10 - WiFi DHCP not assigning address

Just upgraded to Windows 10 on a HP2000 Notebook.  The upgrade worked flawlessly with one exception.  I performed the upgrade while wired to the network.  When I switched to WiFi, the adapter showed "Limited Connectivity".  Using Regedit, I looked at HKLM/SYSTEM/CurrentControlSet/Services/Tcpip/Parameters/Interfaces and found that my WiFi adapter was receiving a DHCP assigned address. 

In my home, I use DHCP statically assigned addresses and typically I assign the same IP to both wired and WiFi adapters on a given machine.  What I didn't realize is that because the wired adapter had already been assigned the same DHCP address, the WiFi adapter somehow couldn't re-use the address it was given by the DHCP.

After giving the Wifi adapter a different DHCP-assigned address than the wired adapter, it worked perfectly.
