dshadowgrim say: Hello

That is the 3.5mm jack.It is not soldered directly to the board, is more like press fitted.
Locate the 3.5mm position then in order to align it make sure that the contact pin are touched the pads on the PCB.Close back the shell and you should be good to go.

dazza 433 say:

hi,

I have personally had a lot of experience replacing the thumbstick sensors and other damaged parts inside controllers.

That part that fell out is not actually physically soldered to the circuit board.

That is the audio jack, What you need to do is match it up with where the audio jack output is on the case (should sit next to the larger older styled chat/audio output port to the left of it if you have the thumbsticks facing upwards towards you)  It simply sits on top of the contacts on the circuit board (a little tricky) Then when you put the case back on it forces it down on to the main boards contact points.

Make sure the side with the 7 gold pins is the side that presses down onto the main board.

Hope this helps.
