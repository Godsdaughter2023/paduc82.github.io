It sounds like the laptop may be overheating.  Are you sure there is adequate airflow for the notebook?  Make sure it's setting on a flat surface, with nothing clogging the vents.  Ensure that any vents and fans and heat sinks are free of dust - consider blowing them off with compressed air (power the system down first, of course).  Depending on the model laptop, it can be relatively straightforward to blow out the dust with compressed air, or otherwise clean things up a bit.   Fans should be held in place though - not allowed to spin when being blown off.  My Dell laptop has a removable fan (two small screws need to be removed) that can be blown out easily.  This also provides access to the heat sink fins.

Some laptops also have a removable filter that trap dust, and should be periodically checked and cleaned.
