Is your Surface device a RT or PRO? When was the last time you went through your windows updates?

You can check for Windows updates by:

1. Choosing settings from your charm bar

3. in PC Settings, scroll to the bottom and tap on "Windows Updates"

4. Tap on "check for updates" on the right side and follow the steps on the screen.

 

If you have disabled the “Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power" power management setting for the Marvell wireless adapter in Windows device manager, their Surface Pro may not wake from sleep when the power button, Windows button, keyboard, track pad or mouse is pressed.

To resolve the issue and allow Surface to properly wake from sleep, You will need to re-enable the “Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power" power management setting on the Marvell wireless adapter (see instructions below). 

 

For Surface Pro, the “Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power" power management setting for the Marvell wireless adapter is enabled by default.

 

Note Surface Pro will not maintain network connectivity during sleep, even with this setting disabled to keep the network adapter “on” during sleep.  This is by design.

 

1.  Turn your Surface on:   Press and release the power button. 

    If within a few seconds the Surface logo does not appear on the touchscreen, turn your Surface off: Press and hold the power button for at least five seconds, and then release it.
    Any unsaved work will be lost.
    Then press and release the power button to turn Surface on.

2.  Sign in to Surface.

3.  Open Device Manager:  Swipe in from the right edge of the screen and tap or click Search (or if you're using a mouse, point to the upper-right corner of the screen, move the mouse pointer down, and then click Search), and enter Device Manager in the search box.

4. Tap or click Settings, and then tap or click Device Manager.

Note You might be asked for an admin password or to verify your choice.

5. Double-tap or double-click the Network adapters group to expand that category, and then double-tap or double-click Marvell AVASTAR 350N Wireless Network Controller.

6. Tap or click the Power Management tab, then tap or click the checkbox to enable Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power.

7. Tap or click OK to save the change and close the Properties window.

rayellis say: I, too have been searching for hours and hours to find a fix.
Try this, it worked for me on my Windows7 machine where the microsoft games kept crashing with a directx error.
1 - Create a new folder on your desktop and name it "games" or whatever.
2 - Navigate to c:/program files and double click the microsoft games folder
3 - There are folders for all the installed games. Open each folder, one at a time and copy the .exe file and paste it into the new "games" folder. DO NOT use the media center shortcut.
Some of the games require that you also copy their .dll file with them into the new "games" folder. For me, it was Chess, Mahjong, and MineSweeper. The rest of them worked without copying the .dll file.
Open the game by clicking it in the new "games" folder, or make new shortcuts using the files in the new folder.
This somehow bypasses using the Media Center which seems to be the problem with the directx crash.
Hope this works for you!
