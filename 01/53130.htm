frederik long say:

I moved my " User" folder from my "C" drive 100G almost used up. To my "F" drive 1TB LOTS OF room.

Moving the folder C:\Users to F: is not a good idea. It is unsupported by Microsoft and is likely to cause numerous problems in future. I suggest you do this:

  1. Move it back to C:\.
  2. To free up space, move the various user libraries to drive F:. This will free up almost as much space and is fully supported.
  3. To free up even more space, manually move your .pst files (Outlook), your temp folder and your Temporary Internet Files to drive F:.

Post again if you require more detailed instructions.

I sense there are a few serious problems on your machine, e.g.

  • Using unapproved software such as Driver Detective - see Visitor.
  • Keeping your family fotos in just one place. Lots of people ignore the rule that irreplaceable files must be stored on at least two independent media until they suffer a major catastrophe.

I suspect that your previous move of C:\Users to F:\ has caused some changes in the registry which might be hard to reverse. If this was my machine then I would do this:

  1. Save all data files to an external disk, including your E-Mail files (if you use a product such as Outlook).
  2. Connect this disk to some other PC and run a few spot checks on the saved files.
  3. Reload Windows from scratch.
  4. Move specific user libraries to drive F:.
  5. Leave the Guest account disabled.
  6. Refrain from installing registry boosters and other optimising software such as Driver Detective.

avatarrrr say:

The short answer is no.  Because the game changes the resolution, the icon sizes are changed and Windows places them as you have specified.  Possibly, if you unchecked the "align to grid" option they *might* stay put.  They may change size during game operation but, after that, they should remain where they were.

Regarding your “grouping” icons idea, maybe a video card that supports multiple desktops might be a workaround.  Alternatively, you could try the Desktops utility from Sysinternals dot com (http://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/sysinternals/cc817881.aspx) to set up multiple desktops.  That’s the best I can offer.
