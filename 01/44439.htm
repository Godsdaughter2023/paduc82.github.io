Navigate to:

HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Classes\Local Settings\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion \TrayNotify

From the main window, delete both IconStreams and PastedIconsStream by right click and delete

I'm no gaming expert but I think there is little to choose between ATI/AMD (AMD now owns ATI) and NV cards, as long as they are matched spec wise (see the charts).

Pick what best meets your needs for the best cost, rather than the make.


Quickly looking at the specs for the games you mention, and looking at the recommended spec NOT the minimum spec (min spec will usually get a game running at a playable rate (just) with everything turned to low), the highest card required was a NV 8800GT, so anything level with or above that should do what you require.


Again from the charts, something like an ATI HD5670 or HD5770 should suffice I would say for around £60-£70 ish or £80-£100 ish respectively. It depends on what you want to spend.

I'm not sure about tweaking windows to allocate more memory, but even if you did I doubt it would make that much difference with the GPU you currently have,

1)  Secondary-click (right-click) your start menu button and select "file explorer".

2)  Scroll down and find your primary disk where Windows is installed (we will assume "drive C" here).  Double click it.

3)  If it is the correct drive, it should have a folder named "Users", open it and then find your username.  Open it.

5)  Secondary-click the folder you want to move, then choose "Properties".

6)  Select the "Location" tab.

7)  In the folder location box, where you have something like "C:\Users\LittleRedBrush\Downloads", simply change the drive from "C" to the letter of the drive you desire, then click the "Apply" button in the lower right corner of the box.
