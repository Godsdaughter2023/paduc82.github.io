abdul s say:

Hi,

 

 

·         Which application are you using to type the word?

·         Have you made any changes prior to this issue?

·         Is it a laptop or desktop?

 

 

Method 1:

If it is a laptop, then I would suggest you to decrease touch pad sensitivity and check if it helps.

Refer the below steps:

a. Click on "Start" menu and click on the option labeled "Control Panel."
b. Change the view by option from category to large icon, Double-click on the icon labeled "Mouse.". The "Mouse Properties" window will open. Navigate to the tab labeled "Device Settings." Click on the button labeled "Settings." A window labeled "Properties" will appear.
c. Examine the left panel of the "Properties" window and expand the option labeled "Sensitivity." Click on the option labeled "Touch Sensitivity."
d. Reduce the touch sensitivity setting by moving the slider to the right, towards the "Heavy Touch" label. Experiment with different levels of sensitivity until you find one suited to your needs.
e. Click on the button labeled "OK" to close the "Properties" window; repeat with the "Mouse" control panel. Your new touchpad sensitivity settings are now saved.

 

Method 2:

If it is a desktop, then try to enable ‘hide pointer while typing’ from the mouse properties and check if it helps.

Refer the below steps.

a.       Click on ‘start’.

b.      Click on ‘control panel’.

c.       Click on ‘large icons’, and select large icons (right side at the top of the window).

d.      Click on ‘mouse’.

e.      Go to ‘pointer options tab’.

f.        Check the option, ‘hide pointer while typing’.

g.       Click on ‘apply’.

h.      Click on ‘OK’.
