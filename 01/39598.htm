hairyfool say:

The main component that is at risk is the hard disk due to the rotating mass and when it suffers this type of damage it is normally obvious fairly quickly, read errors, system crashes etc. Drives have a G rating for its limits, normally two, one for storage and the other for in operation with the latter being much smaller. Some laptops have a free fall sensor that parks the disk in a safe zone when it detects being dropped. Drives are much less delicate than they used to be and your description would indicate relatively low G forces being generated.

Fans can sometimes get noisy in such cases but only if at end of life anyway.

Otherwise if everything is properly fixed in place a small incident like you describe should have no lasting effects.

Thank you for your response and sorry for delay in response.

I suggest you to follow the below steps to update the touchpad driver and check if it helps.

  1. Press “Windows+X”, go to “Device Manager”.
  2. Locate for “Human Interface Devices” and expand it.
  3. Right click on the “Device” and click on “Uninstall”.
  4. After uninstallation close the “Device Manager”.
  5. Again open the “Device Manager” and click on “Scan for Hardware Changes”, this will scan the drivers for your device and install it on your computer.
