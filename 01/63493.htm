¡firedog say:

... documents from an Apple computer in Pages. 

A file with the .pages extension is a zipped collection of files, one of which is a PDF of the original document.

  1. In File Explorer, right-click on the .pages file and select Rename. Change the extension to .zip
  2. Open the file and Extract all.
  3. Find preview.pdf and copy it to your documents folder.
  4. Open preview.pdf in MS Word, or in Adobe Reader and convert to Word.

You may lose some formatting if the PDF file is complicated with many graphics. For files consisting largely of text, the resulting Word doc should be quite adequate.

cmdrkeene say:

Does WMP have the ability to remember what was played so it will not repeat a selection until all songs on the list have played at least once, assuming it was not stopped and restarted?  

It doesn't have this ability, because it doesn't do it this way.  When you choose to shuffle, the entire playlist queue is sorted randomly, then playback is started.  So no song could ever play twice until they all play, unless the song was already listed more than once in the play queue (whatever list was selected before you clicked "play", which could be a playlist, or the entire library).

Example:  The song titled "Summertime" has been performed by several different artists, and it also might show up  on more than one album by a given artist if there are "original" albums and "greatest hits" albums by a given artist.

Every media file, even if identical in every way (even same file name if stored in different folders) is a totally different and unique item and will not be skipped just because another item with the same name had played. Because again, the play order is entirely determined before playback ever begins. It randomizes the list first, then plays from that newly ordered queue.  It never has to track what has played, it just goes down the queue list in order.

    a. user say:

    Hi,

    Thank you posting your query on Microsoft Community.

    I appreciate you for providing details about the issue and we are happy to help you.

    Please be informed that, you cannot change the date/time which appears on the photo in the Windows Photo Gallery. But you can change the data of the photo taken.

    Follow the below steps to change the data of the photo taken:

    1.  Open the Windows Photo Gallery.

    2.  Open the picture in the full screen mode.

    3.  Locate Date taken: option under the Information from the bottom right corner.

    4.  Click on the date and time to make the necessary changes.

    angelabrown3 say: I can't get Outlook open to allow me to backup the emails. Terrified I will loose then as used for work!

    andre da costa say:
    Press Windows key + R
    Type: C:\Users\%username%\Documents\Outlook Files\
    Hit Enter

    You should find your Outlook data files there.

    Press Windows key + R
    Type: outlook.exe /safe

    Hit Enter

    See if Outlook starts. Here are some additional troubleshooting tips you can try:

    https://www.howto-outlook.com/faq/outlookdoesnt...

    angelabrown3 say: The first part worked so I have the files but the safe opening is still getting the same message. Any other tips appreciated. 

    andre da costa say:
    Open Start > Settings > Apps > Apps & features
    Scroll down to Microsoft Office
    Select it
    Click Change

    Now, wait while the repair wizard starts up.

    Choose the type of repair you would like to perform. Depending on the severity or availability of your Internet connection, you could try the first option, Quick Repair, which is much faster and tries to fix the problem without a data connection.

    If that doesn’t resolve the problem, then go back and try the Online Repair option. For my particular issue, I ultimately had to use the Online Repair.

    The repair process is straight-forward following the wizard. Note that you’ll need to save your work and close all Office programs first, and then click Repair.

    Then wait while the repair process runs.

    You will notice the repair experience is similar to a fresh installation. The amount of time it takes will depend on your connection speed and what the issue is.

    You will see your Office apps light up in their respective colors while the repair completes.

    Once the installation is complete, restart your computer just to be sure and launch your apps and verify if the problem you were having is resolved.
