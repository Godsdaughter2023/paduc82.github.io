bill sanderson say:

If the machine is truly off or hibernated, and not in standby, then the issue is a setting in the bios.
 
Many Dell machines, for example, can be set to turn on or off at a set time, in addition to having Wake On Lan enabled or disabled.
 
If you cannot find such a setting in the bios, there's an additional possibility:  the mechanism may be there, but not exposed in your bios version, and there might be corrupt data causing this action.
 
What I would try, if you rule out an explicit bios setting, is to get a new bios version (or the same bios version) and update the bios code--even if it is to the same version.
 
After doing so, go through all the settings and arrange them to your satisfaction, then see if that solves the problem--but if the machine is in fact off or hibernated--the OS is not involved--so the trigger has to be in the BIOS.
