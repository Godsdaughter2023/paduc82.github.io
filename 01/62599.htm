a. user say:

Hi, LeahGXX,

I'm an Independent Advisor and will assist you.

Does your mom use the same laptop or one of her own?
You can explicitly turn off automatic connection for individual network profiles.

On your laptop do the following:

Tap the Action Center icon and choose All Settings.
Select Network & Internet.
Choose Wi-Fi.
Select Change Adapter Options on the right panel under Related Settings to open the Network Connections dialog.
Double-click on the relevant Wi-Fi connection to open the Wi-Fi Status dialog.
Click the Wireless Properties button below the General tab to open the Wireless Network Properties dialog.
Uncheck the entry Connect Automatically When This Network is in Range under the Connection tab. - Source Lifewire

In Advanced Settings of the adapter properties have you enabled Single Sign-On?
Disable Single Sign-on for This Network."
This option has enabled Windows to authenticate and establish the wireless connection prior to loading Sign-In screen.

Click the name of your preferred wireless network (if more than one appears in the list), and then enable the "Connect Automatically" option. Click the "Connect" button.
Type the security key for the router in the "Enter the Network Security Key" field, and then click "Next."
Click the "Yes, Turn on Sharing and Connect to Devices" option. Wait a few seconds for Windows to establish a wireless connection with the Wi-Fi router and obtain an IP address automatically.
