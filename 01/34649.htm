thomasmoyland say:

I have had an ongoing issue with my Lenovo Miix 8 tablet. The problems focused primarily around losing touch capability at seemingly random times (sometimes immediately upon boot up - I couldn't log in, during regular use, etc. al.).

After reading some of the issues here on Microsoft Community, I went rooting around the I2C HID Devices in Device Manager. There is a Property page for the I2C HID Device labeled "Power Management". On the Miix 8, the default setting for this property is to have it checked, that is, the default setting is to "Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power." I unchecked that option for all I2C HID Devices on the Miix 8 (there are two) and, for the past two days, I have not had a single loss of touch or issues with the Miix 8 at all!

Note that preventing Win8.1 from powering this device down when it thinks it needs to may lead to excessive power draw and may reduce the duration your computer may last on a single battery charge. However, I have not noticed any reduction in battery life. As a matter of fact, I have noticed that my Miix 8 is now more consistent in its power consumption when it is on battery power (which is 8-10 hours a day) after I changed these settings on the I2C HID Devices.

I think that Win8.1 on a Lenovo Miix 8 is an amazing piece of tech. However, I think that Microsoft and Intel have to work a LOT harder to improve the overall system behavior when it comes to Win8.1 and gear like the Lenovo Miix 8. I shouldn't have to dig around in Device Manager to make the tablet useful and easy to use.
