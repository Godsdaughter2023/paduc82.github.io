If your computer came with a previous qualifying license such as Windows Vista or Windows XP and the Certificate of Authencity is still attached to the machine in good condition, then the COA is reason enough for representing compliance to the software license agreement. How to do a clean install with the upgrade disk:

Boot from the Windows 7 DVD

Click Install Now

Accept License Agreement

When the option is displayed to select an installation type, click (Custom Advanced)

Click Drive Options

Select the drive/s click Delete

Click New

Click Apply

Click OK

Click Format and click Next to proceed with the installation

Skip entering your Windows 7 product key and complete the installation.

Once you reach the desktop, click Start, right click Computer

Click Properties

Scroll down to Windows Activation

Click the link x days until activation.

Click the link that says 'Show me other ways to activate'

Enter product key

Click Next

Select Phone Activation

Click Next when Enter your key page displayed

Select your country

Click Next, call the number listed

Provide the generated installation ID when requested by the call agent

15. UPGRADES. To use upgrade software, you must first be licensed for the software that is eligible for the upgrade. Upon upgrade, this agreement takes the place of the agreement for the software you upgraded from. After you upgrade, you may no longer use the software you upgraded from.

canadian tech say:

"extremely high temp" will destroy your computer.  It is possible that that has happened.  All electronic devices produce a lot of heat and are engineered to provide air flow and heat dissipation devices to get rid of that heat.

I strenuously object to the name "laptop" computer because it should never be used on a laptop.  A notebook computer is a very confined space and it needs a lot of flowing air to keep it from over-heating.  There is likely a fan with a vent on the side of your computer.  There are also a whole bunch of vents on the bottom of the computer.  Those vents on the bottom must be able to bring air in.  If you place your computer on a soft surface that blocks those vents, it will overheat. 

Bottom line, a notebook computer should always be placed on a hard smooth surface.

If your computer shut it self down abruptly to protect itself, it could have caused corruption on the hard drive.

You need a System Repair disk.  Find another working Windows 7 computer of the same bitness (32 or 64) as yours.  You will need a blank recordable CD.  Start, All programs, maintenance.

Boot your computer using this CD.  Be certain that you have in fact started the computer on that CD.  It will look different if you have.    If your system will boot on that CD, it will offer alternatives. 

You could try a factory restore.  But understand that it wipes the hard drive and all data on the computer is lost.  To do a factory restore on your Dell, press F11 repeatedly on startup to begin the process.  Factory Restore over-writes the hard drive with a precise copy of what was on the drive when it left the factory those 5 years ago.
