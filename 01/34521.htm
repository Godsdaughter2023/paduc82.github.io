considerate_guy say:

The speed of a scrolling title is inversely proportional to its duration.

In other words, if you have a certain amount of text to scroll and it has a duration of 10 seconds (for example), then it will take 10 seconds to scroll from bottom to top.

If you want it to go *twice* as fast (faster), you would make the duration *half* as long (e.g. 5 seconds)

If you want it to go *half* as fast (slower), you would make the duration *twice* as long (e.g. 20 seconds)

Steps:

  1. Click on the pink rectangle that contains your caption (in your project area to the right.)
  2. Go to the pink tab labeled Text Tools | Format.
  3. Find the box labeled Duration and change the value accordingly. Remember, a longer duration makes the scrolling animation take longer (slower) and a shorter duration makes the scrolling take less time (faster).
  4. Press Enter to save your value.
  5. Test the results.

Note: You can type values into the box especially if you want decimals like (12.25 seconds)

considerate_guy say:

When you have a text caption or a photo, the only option is for duration. The speed option is only for video clips. That's why the option is grayed out to show that it is unavailable in this context.

If you think about it, the two are inversely related. If you want to speed something up, you reduce the duration. If you want to slow something down, you increase the duration. The steps for doing this were explained in my prior response and this is the method you should use.

Do you have a link to the tutorials that mention you should use the Speed setting to adjust a text caption? That's misinformation if that's truly what they were saying. Or perhaps those were steps for changing the speed of a video clip and you assumed it should work for text captions too?
