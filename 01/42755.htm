cobalt jacket say:

Fatou, I think I should clarify ABCDTim and Vlad's concern. First, it is important to know the difference between "Connected Standby" (Sleep 0) and "Suspend to Ram" (Sleep 3). If you want to skip my abridged explanation, visit the MSDN page linked below. It goes into a lot more depth than my brief summary.

What is connected standby? (MSDN link)

"Suspend to Ram" (Sleep 3)

Older devices like the Surface Pro 2 work like regular computers. When they sleep, they pause what's going on the system completely. Only minimal power is used to keep the data paused in RAM. This means they can fairly accurately estimate exactly how much "on" time the battery can provide. These show "time remaining" in the battery icon.

"Connected Standby" (Sleep 0) AKA InstantGo

New computers with special hardware requirements (CPU, motherboard, RAM, SSD, Power) support a new, special low-power mode where the computer is "sleeping" and consuming much less power, but is still connected to WiFi and working in the background. This is called "Connected Standby" and the Surface Pro 3 supports it.

Depending on WHAT it is doing in the background while sleeping, power draw and battery life can vary

To avoid confusion, Microsoft decided instead to only show "Battery % remaining" on devices that have Connected Standby enabled. (Typing "powercfg -a" in Command Prompt should confirm this.)

It is possible to DISABLE Connected Standby if you are having problems. This will give you back the "Time left" amount in your battery bar. However, given that Connected Standby is a really neat feature that you paid extra to have in your laptop, you shouldn't turn it off.

If you absolutely want to see how much "time" you typically get out of your laptop battery, or see the historical statistics, you can do this by typing "powercfg /sleepstudy" or "powercfg /batteryreport". That will give a bunch of data showing about how much life your computer gets out of the battery for a full charge. Then keep that in mind when you use your computer on its own.

If you wish to see a "Time Remaining" number:

There is an app called "Battery Bar Pro" which will restore the "time remaining" number even on computers with Connected Standby support. I have no experience with it though.
