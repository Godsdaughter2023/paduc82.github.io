arminatorx say:

First of all, you need to understand that you are always getting a Stereo signal from the two speakers in your headphones, as there are only two speakers. 

Since you only have two ears, your brain will get the spatial information from where the sound is coming from by comparing the sound difference, echos and reverb between your two ears.

The Dolby Atmos App simply adds echo and reverb effects to the sound to fool your two ears to think that something to the right and above you is making a sound by applying a small delay to the sound reaching your right ear, and then playing the sound a bit later in your left ear. 

From my experience, if a game does not support Dolby Atmos, you do get "flat" surround sound, meaning you should at least hear sounds appearing to come from "behind" you, however on the same height level as your ears. Just as with a normal standard 5.1 setup. Just be aware, that this also is just simulated surround through your two stereo headphone speakers. 

As for the upmixing of non-Atmos sound to spatial Atmos sound: This is in testing on the Alpha and Alpha Skip Ahead channels of the Insider Preview. However, unless you also want to have issues with soundbars not working or Atmos receivers crackling and popping, I recommend to NOT join the Insider Preview just yet for that feature, if you value sound quality. If however you wish to test stuff, and file feedback for things that are broken, feel free to join the Insider Preview. Just be aware, that you have to "work" your way up to the Alpha rings by being active and filing good feedback. If you just join the Insider Preview, Beta is the highest Ring you can get to at first.

Hello Ina,

Thank you for posting your query in Microsoft Community Forum.

  1. What is the make and model of the USB device you are trying to connect?
  2. Are you able to connect other USB devices to your computer successfully?

Basically, code 43 error occurs when one of the drivers controlling the device notified the operating system that the device failed in some manner. Refer to the following KB article:

Code 43: Error codes in Device Manager in Windows

So, I would suggest you to try the following steps and check if the issue persists.

Method 1:

Please try the fixit(s) provided below which will help you resolve common issues related to USB ports and devices.

  • Hardware devices are not working or are not detected in Windows
  • Diagnose and fix Windows USB problems automatically

If Method 1 does not help, try method 2.

Method 2:

Remove and reinstall all USB controllers.

  1. Open Device Manager by clicking the Start button, clicking Control Panel, clicking System and Security, and then, under System,clicking Device Manager. If you're prompted for an administrator password or confirmation, type the password or provide confirmation.
  2. In the list of hardware categories, find and expand Universal Serial Bus controllers.
  3. Right-click every device under the Universal Serial Bus controllers node, and then click Uninstall to remove them one at a time.
  4. Restart the computer, and let the USB controllers get reinstalled.
  5. Plug in the removable USB device, and then test to make sure that the issue is resolved.

If the issue still exists, proceed to Method 3.

Method 3:

This method will install the latest device drivers for your USB device.

  1. Click the Start button, type Windows Update in the Search box, then click Windows Update in the results pane.
  2. Click Check for Updates. After the scan is complete, click Review optional updates.
  3. Click the check box next to the updates, then click Install updates.
  4. If prompted, review the license agreement, then click I Accept.
  5. Follow the onscreen instructions to download and install the updates.
  6. If prompted, reboot your computer.

Additional Information: Update a driver for hardware that isn't working properly
