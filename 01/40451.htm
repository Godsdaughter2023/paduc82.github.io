regile reb say:

Hello,

Some of the possible reasons why you're having display issues is due to corrupted drivers or hardware failure. We recommend that you follow the steps below to troubleshoot your concern:

  1. Go to Control Panel > Type Troubleshooting in the search box located on the upper-right corner.
  2. Click Troubleshooting > Select Hardware and Sound.
  3. Hit Hardware and Devices.
  4. Follow the on-screen instructions and check if the issue still occurs.

If the issue still occurs, uninstall and reinstall your display driver:

  1. Press Windows key + X > Select Device Manager.
  2. Expand Display adapters > Right-click on your driver.
  3. Select Properties > Go to Driver tab.
  4. Hit Uninstall > Visit your hardware manufacturer's website, download and install the latest display driver.

Note: Uninstalling your display driver will run minimal display settings for your computer.

Let us know what happens after.

a. user say:

That works with an auto playlist, which does not provide the function of a regular or sync playlist.  So I haven't found a way to limit the size of a sync list. 

 However, and a "doh!" to me:

The key for me was setting the USB Mode of the player to MTP "media transfer protocol" (which plays nicely in the sandbox with Windows Media Player),  rather than MSC (Mass Storage Class) where upon it acts like a hard drive: drag.drop


With MTP, once I set up the sync with my mp3 player, the sync panel shows the capacity of the player and the amount of free space left.  The latter is dynamic: decreasing/increasing as one adds/removes sync list items. So you can at least see if your sync list will fit.  The only wrinkle I've found is that you need to leave enough space on the player for the WMP database
