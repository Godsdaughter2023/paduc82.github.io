lemp say:
Yes, the repair shop would know what they did, but the fact that you gave them a Dell Reinstallation CD, along with the fact that you got the computer back without your files, suggests that what the shop did was a "clean install."

The facts that the files appear in green and have the encryption box checked are bad for you.  These both indicate that the files are in fact encrypted using the Windows Encrypting File System (EFS). 

I have never heard of files becoming encrypted without any user action, but how the files became encrypted is now beside the point.

AFAIK, your only hope is to download the trial version of the Elcomsoft application linked in my earlier post and see if it can find any trace of the encryption key on your hard drive that was not overwritten by the reinstallation.  I don't know, but I suspect, that the free trial version will tell you whether it can or can not decrypt your files ... but will only actually decrypt them if you pay for it.

Elcomsoft does claim some success in situations like yours.  The less you use the computer before running the Data Recovery software, the better your chance of recovery.  I think you'll have to go with the more expensive "Professional" version.

Here is what I did for my model router MI424-WR.

1 Log into your router and go to My Network

2 On left hand side, click on Network Connections

3 Click the edit button for the Broadband Connection (COAX)

4 Scroll down to Settings

5 Scroll down to DHCP Lease: and click on Release

6 Wait about 15 minutes

7 Click on Renew, if you you get a new IP address the problem should be resolved.  If you get the same IP again, release and try again.
