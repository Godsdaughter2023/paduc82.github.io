crizzr.8202 say:

How to change the name displayed on the sign-in screen in Windows 10?

how do I change the name on the start up screen?

Original subject/title: windows 10

ramon imp say:

Hi,

Changing the username displayed on the sign-in screen depends on what account you're using to sign in. If you're using a Microsoft account, you may need to change your Microsoft account name online. If you're using a local account, you can change your account name from the Control Panel. To do so, follow the these steps:

  • For Microsoft account:

  1. Click the Windows logo, and click on the gear icon to open Settings.
  2. Go to Accounts.
  3. Select Your account.
  4. Click the Manage my Microsoft account link.
  5. In your Microsoft account main page, click Edit name below your account picture.
  6. Enter your preferred name, and click Save.
  7. Restart your computer.

Note: Please keep in mind that it may take some time for the changes to take effect. Also, the name you entered on your Microsoft account will also be displayed on other Microsoft products and services that you're using.

  • For local account:

  1. In the search file on your taskbar, type Control Panel, and press the Enter key.
  2. Under User Account, click the Change account type link.
  3. Choose your local account.
  4. Click the Change the account name link.
  5. Enter your preferred name, and click Change name to confirm.
  6. Reboot your computer.

Let us know if you need further assistance.

oldhobo say: If it's maxing out at 115 degrees Fahrenheit your cpu is not overheating. The problems you are experiencing are originating from something else such as a software program. From your explanation it does not sound like a hardware problem. 

Try opening msconfig and unchecking the software that is being automatically started when Win7 starts up. Run the computer for a while and see if the problem has disappeared. If so, reopen msconfig and reintroduce the startup programs one at a time. When the problems begin again, then the last program you added to the startup sequence is the culprit.
