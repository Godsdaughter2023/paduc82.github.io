Installing Windows 10 after reformatting my HDD - Microsoft Community

ben_611 say:

Installing Windows 10 after reformatting my HDD

a. user say:

The installer for Windows 10 will always be free and available from Microsoft's website.

Your right to use Windows 10 now and in the future is based on your activation code, not your license.

Your activation code is calculated the first time you install Windows 10. It's a hash (a type of formula) based on the hardware in your computer. Your activation code is stored in the cloud; in this case, on a Microsoft server.

At any time, Windows 10 can and does re-calculate the hash and compares it to the stored value. That's how Windows 10 knows it hasn't been moved to a different computer.

Going forward, you can re-install or clean install Windows 10 on the same computer as often as you like, any time you want, without needing to enter a license, ever. Microsoft 'remembers' your right to use Windows 10 from the activation code.

If you change your motherboard and/or CPU, Microsoft considers that to be a new computer and your original activation code no longer applies.

A license for Windows 10 merely gives you the right to activate. The 'deal' with free Windows 10 is the ability to activate without a license. You can do that if you are upgrading a computer that is currently running Windows 7 SP1 or Windows 8.1 or - since the Fall Update - if you are clean installing and you have a valid license for Windows 7 or Windows 8. Otherwise, you'll have to pay for a license.
