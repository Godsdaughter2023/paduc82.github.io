That sounds like you are in Device manager and letting Windows search for drivers for your device (right?).

It is never a good idea to let Windows suggest hardware drivers for your system (video, audio, network, Bluetooth) because Microsoft does not know what the best drivers are for your system.  By accepting and installing what MS suggests will often break hardware devices.  You should only get device drivers from your manufacturers WWW site.

Many is the system that has somehow become afflicted after accepting device drivers from Microsoft and/or Windows Update.  Many are the topics of discussion where the subject is "Windows update broke my computer".

If the system used to work and you think a driver update from Microsoft might have slipped in you might be able to roll back the driver to the previously installed version.

Here is an example of rolling back a video driver to the previously installed version that worked before an update - substitute your Bluetooth device and follow the same process:

If you inadvertently allowed a video driver offered by Windows Update to get installed on your system you can roll back that driver to the one that was previous from Device Manager.

Open Device Manager from the Start button:

Then expand the Display Adapter section, right click your display adapter and make a note of the currently installed version since that is probably the version that does not work (good to know).

If there has been a driver update since you brought the new computer home you can Roll Back the video driver to the previous version.  If there is no previous version that button will not be clickable:

If you rolled back the driver restart your system and check the video driver version again to see what version it is now and make a note of that.

Windows has a way to disable hardware driver updates from being offered from Windows Update so you might not have another incident with hardware drivers in the future.

I'm not sure it works and have no way to really test it but why not apply it anyway - read about it here:

cmdrkeene say:

If you have two USB plugs to put them into, you can copy them directly:

  1. Plug them both in.
  2. Press +E to open the Windows Explorer and go to "Computer", and open the 1st drive.
  3. Press +E again to open another folder, and open the 2nd drive.
  4. Drag the files from one drive to the other.

If you only have one USB plug, you will have to drag the files from the 1st one to your desktop, them switch drives and drag them from the desktop to the new drive.
