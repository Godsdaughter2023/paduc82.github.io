neilpzz say:

If the device came pre-installed with Windows 10 then the licence is OEM and not transferrable to a new device. A motherboard change counts as a new device. (with the possible exception being you are fitting the same make/model of MB)

Putting the hard drive into another PC will just complicate things further. An OEM licence is not transferrable and whether linked to an MS account or not will not make a difference.

andre da costa say:
It depends on how Windows 10 was originally configured on your computer.

If its a full retail store bought license online or offline, it is transferable to a new computer or motherboard.

If its the free upgrade from a retail store bought Windows 7 or Windows 8 license, it is transferable to a new computer or motherboard.

If its the free upgrade from a OEM preinstalled Windows 7 or Windows 8 license, it is NOT transferable to a new computer or motherboard.

http://www.groovypost.com/howto/transfer-windows-10-license-new-pc/

Basically you can define a shortcut consisting of any characters which the OS will expand to a phrase you defined.  It is similar to word completion but it can replace the patterns you enetred with a phrase you define. e.g. if one defines

 

        supo = *** Email address is removed for privacy ***

 

and anytime the system see the pattern "supo" in an edit control, it will offer to repalce it with *** Email address is removed for privacy ***

 

This feature is very handy for entering email adddress as currently many sites (inclusing this site) now use email address as user id.
