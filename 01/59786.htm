don cuthbert say:

Have you moved any standard folders, such as C:\Users\ or C:\Program Files (x86)\ off of your SSD?  That could be behind the unsupported directory message.

Using the USB does not equate to losing any data.  You just run setup.exe, from the USB flash drive, within your activated Windows 7, choose to keep files and settings and do not enter any Product Key at any time when asked.

However, you will have to resolve the unsupported directory error first.  Possibly clean install and activate Windows 7 on your SSD to upgrade it properly.  You could even disconnect other hard disks during the upgrade if you are concerned about their data.
