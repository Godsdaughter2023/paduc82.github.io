If your computer constantly restarts while you are using it or if you are trying to shut down the computer after it has stopped unexpectedly, disabling Automatically Restart option will give you receive an error message when the computer stops responding. roshanlal say:

Yay, I fixed it all by my lonesome. After more than 2 weeks of extensive googling and trying all kinds of fixes that never fixed anything. Well there were 2 things I did and since I did them simultaneously, I wasn't sure which did the trick. Here's my recap:

1. Right click properties on Network adapters in Device Manager. Click the Power Management tab. Scroll down to Wake on LAN and uncheck Wake on Magic Packet and Wake on Pattern Match.

2. Click the Start button and hover over the arrow next to Shut Down (for me the button says Log off) and click Hibernate.

The screen went blank and I had to press the power button to resume Windows. I then restarted and set my screensaver to 1 minute to test it (with logon required on resume). Presto, it worked!

I then went back to Wake on LAN and checked the 2 things I had unchecked earlier and restarted, and it still worked. So it must be Hibernate that did it. I think it was some update that messed it up and all it required was a sort of reset. Hope this helps someone with a similar problem.
