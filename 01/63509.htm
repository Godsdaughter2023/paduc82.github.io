There are 3 possible scenerios that you can experience when attempting to Extend your Desktop
1. One Monitor can be active and the other blank
2. Both Screens are Reverse
3. Monitor 2 is a clone of Monitor 1

I am under the impression that you are experiencing Scenerio 3.  Is this correct?  Well I am going to give you 3 possible solutions, I recommend that you try them all in sequential order....

To Fix number 1, I recommend that you uninstalled the both monitors from Device Manager and ensure that they both have the appropiate drivers. During this process.  Only reinstall you primary monitor.  Please be sure to turn off and disconnect the 2nd monitor.  Once the first monitor is installed

Fix 2:  I doubt in your case the monitors are switch.  I will skip this one for now.

Fix 3:  To get out of clone mode: Open display settings and check the box on the 2nd monitor for "Extend the desktop onto this monitor". The 1st monitor should be checked for "This is my primary monitor

If the issue is still not taken care of, Click Advance Settings and Start the Nvidia Control Panel and select Multiple Display then the option Extend Desktop.
