Configure Port

What were you trying to do that caused the error to appear?

How is your printer connected to your computer (e.g., USB, wifi, connected to another computer on your local network, connected to a USB port on your router, something else)?

This error occurs when an attempt is made to configure a printer port while a print job is currently assigned to that port.

It may be sufficient to simply clear the print queue, but your responses to the above questions would potentially help avoid the error in the future.

To clear the print queue:

  • Open an Elevated Command Prompt (click Start Orb, type cmd.exe then right-click on the link for cmd.exe and select "Run as administrator")
  • Type the following in the black Command Prompt window and press Enter after each line

                           net stop spooler

                           del /Q "%windir%\system32\spool\PRINTERS\*.*"

                           net start spooler

                           exit

Is there a way to verify that the OS and hard drive are indeed connecting via USB 3.0 connection and not just a 2.0 connection?


Thank you all!

james vinyard say:

Your SSD card has nothing to do with the buss speed on devices connected to the USB port.  The device being attached is 'pinged' by the device controller (USB) to derive the optimum speed for a reliable connection.


In your case, it may be the cable to the WD hard disk.  It could also be something else interfering on the tablet causing the USB controller to downgrade the external hard disk to 2.0.


There is no way to look at the cable or the plug to tell the difference.  The only thing that you can do to verify that the hard disk is working properly is attach it to other computers with a USB 3.0 port and see if you are getting full buss speed of the drive.


A lot of other people are reporting the speed of the port not coming out as 3.0 and it could be the driver or something else on the tablet.


You can check to see if there are any Windows Updates or go to Hardware & Sound and go to the Device Manager and right-click on the USB controller and tell it to update drivers.  If that is okay, and the USB cable to the drive is removable, see if you can find a different cable and try it.  Most of them are permanently mounted and you can't change them.


That is about the best you can do on that.  Even though it does drop down to 2.0, you will get a reliable transfer to/from the tablet.  Just not at full speed.
