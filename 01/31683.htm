thahaseena m say:

Hi Beener1976,

Welcome to Microsoft Answers.

Check if Quick Launch folder is missing

The Quick Launch bar is represented in Windows Explorer (or My Computer) as a standard yellow folder called Quick Launch, contained within a parent folder called Internet Explorer (C:\Users\(your username)\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Internet Explorer\Quick Launch). If that 'Quick Launch' folder is somehow missing from your own user account, or from a new account you have just created for yourself, there are two ways to fix this. 

1.      Either by creating a brand new Quick Launch folder.

2.      By copying a Quick Launch folder from a different, or the former user account, or from the administrator account, provided a Quick Launch folder exists in those other accounts. 

Method 1:

Create a brand new Quick Launch folder

1.      Open the Taskbar's right-click context menu and ensure there is no tick against either 'Lock the Taskbar' nor 'Quick Launch'. 

2.      Now return to the search window which has your own IE folder (C:\Users\(your username)\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Internet Explorer) opened in it and right-click on the window > New > Folder > change the name of the folder from New Folder to Quick Launch and click anywhere in the window to make the new name take. 

3.      Double-click the new folder to open it as an empty window and go back to the search results' window and look for any shortcut to Internet Explorer use the right-hand mouse button to drag any IE shortcut and drop it on the window of the new Quick Launch folder > at the menu which pops up, choose Copy Here. 

4.      After Copy Here, close all windows which are open > right-click on the taskbar > on the Taskbar's menu, click on Quick Launch to put a tick against it.  You should now find you have a Quick Launch folder back in place on the taskbar with at least one icon on there.

Hi,
 
 
After several months I actually found a solution. Not Microsoft.
 
 
I was using mini display port to HDMI on my monitors.
 
 
I replaced like for like cables, ms sent me a new docking station and even a new surface! The were trying but I STILL had issues. Even with everything swapped out... Apart from the monitors.
 
 
Tested with borrowed monitors and all worked ok. Odd. One thing I did change in testing the monitors was to use mini display port to DVI.
 
 
The DVI cables worked even on my old monitors.
 
 
Once I explained this to Microsoft they actually said "We may suggest using/switching an Active mDPto HDMI adapter, one that supports 4K @ 60Hz."
 
A few months of fault finding and then after I fix it they tell me a solution!?!? After a bit more research some monitors have few extra features to deal with sleep. It you don't have the features you are in for a world of hirt. They are features not easily found or listed on monitors.even if they do support them.
 
Anyway. Back to your issue. He's what what recommend:
 
Swap your cables out. Try a different type.
 
Update to the latest build. 1809 I think. With all the patches.
 
Reset the OS. This will reset back to a factory image with the updated OS. Especially relevant since your having BSoD
 
If that doesn't work, do the following:
Download the most up to date graphics drivers for Intel... Not Microsoft.
 
Since you won't be able to upgrade the driver on the surface as it's "proprietary" uninstall the driver and make sure it's  removed. 
 
This is important. Disconnect from the internet and reboot. This flushes out the driver in memory and stops the OS from downloading a fresh driver from Microsoft on boot.
 
Update the driver from Intel.
 
Fingers crossed you'll be good to go.
 
Failing that go to Google a look up
 "not fit for purpose" and info about small clams court. You are entitled to a full refund if something doesn't work.
 
Good luck!
