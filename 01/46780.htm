No problem ... it's easy when you know how. You don't have to remove the OneDrive app, although you can if you want to.

For any program where you save files - Microsoft Office, for example - just go to the program options and change the default save location. That's exactly what I did. Here are the instructions for Word 365:

File -- Options -- Save -- Save to computer by default

Many Microsoft programs will save to OneDrive by default, which should come as no surprise, but you can always change the default save location to your own computer.

In general, I do not like to remove default apps or cripple my operating system. Instead, I disable whatever Windows lets me disable and ignore it. Let me assure you, there's a lot I disable and ignore! You can effectively disable the OneDrive app by making sure not to store anything in your OneDrive folder. That's all you have to do. And this won't prevent you from uploading files to OneDrive if and when you want to.

Three reasons why I don't rip things out of Windows like some people here like to do:

1- I might make a mistake and create problems for the operating system, which may not show up for months.

2- If I disable/ignore an app I can forget it exists with no harm done.

3- At some time in the future I may wish I had that app.

Hello,

An unstable connection between your iOS device and your Windows 10 computer is one of the possible reasons why you cannot see the photos from your iPhone 6s when connected to your PC. To troubleshoot the issue, we suggest reinstalling the Apple Mobile Device USB driver by following the steps below:

  1. Disconnect your device from the computer.
  2. Reconnect your device. If iTunes opens, close it.
  3. Press the Windows and R key on your keyboard to open the Run command.
  4. In the Run window, enter:
    %ProgramFiles%\Common Files\Apple\Mobile Device Support\Drivers
  5. Click OK.
  6. Right-click on the usbaapl64.inf or usbaapl.inf file and select Install.
