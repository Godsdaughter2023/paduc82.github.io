Windows Movie Maker max output/export resolution is 1920 x 1080; this is to meet the current HDTV standards. 
To export video at Windows Movie Maker's highest setting, just choose the MENU option, Choose the option to SAVE MOVIE and then For High Definition Display.

If the source video [MOV] is in a higher resolution than the exported video,  the video playback will be degraded.  As a suggestion, you may want to look at the source video resolution information. To verify this, you'll need to have some video software that can examine the source video and verify the resolution.

If you use VLC Player, that'll work. VLC plays everything [just about], but it can also tell you the codec that's being used; for both audio and video, it's under tools and Codec information [or CTRL-J].

I'm not understanding your question however.  You are saying that you took poor resolution videos using your HD (high definition) camera and you want to reduce the quality even further by outputting the movie in SD (standard definition) format?

Yes, obviously Movie Maker can save your movie with less resolution than the source videos.  I'm not sure if that will help your situation of poor source video, but to do it, you use File > Save movie.  In the choices for file type, pick a lower resolution format.  In fact, you can go to the bottom of the list and choose Create custom format.  Then you can pick the resolution that matches DVD (e.g. 480p).

By the way, if you have the Burn to DVD option, it will automatically be down-rezzed to the DVD video standard which is 720 x 480p maximum.  That's because the DVD standard came before the era of digital HDTVs and HD content.
