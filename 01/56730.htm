I have discovered the origin of the problem -- which continues to this day -- and how to fix it, though not on a permanent basis. The issue is that when certain Metro media apps are closed (examples: Slacker, Netflix, many more) their processes keep running. Killing the process always fixes the problem. james vinyard say:

Now, I do have the i3 CPU and not the i5.  The i5 has a lot more "under the hood" plus it also has Turbo Boost which kicks in automatically when needed (no control for that).  The i5 is the only one in the "Haswell" family that has that feature.

The i3 is much like a Celeron compared to a Pentium.  The tablet does clock in almost as fast as the i5 in most situation.  I am surprised in how much speed I am actually getting from it.

Now, also remember that you streamed videos.  When I ran mine on battery, I only watched one video and that was the latest episode of "Falling Skies" in Xbox Video.  When you stream videos on the Internet, that cuts your battery life down quite a bit because of the demand made on the WiFi adapter to maintain bandwidth.

So, in your case, it is not the screen brightness that is cutting you down.  Constant web browsing can cut you down as well.  Again, you are tasking the WiFi adapter.  Networking adapters use more power than other devices because that is how they work.  No way around it.  Bluetooth is part of the Marvell adapter but eats little as long as it is only controlling a keyboard or mouse.  You use a headset or sending output to a Bluetooth printer and then it eats power as well.  There are some things you cannot trim on or the device would not work.

I have worked with i5 laptops quite often and 4.5 hours would usually be the maximum they would run on battery.

Now then, you are probably going by the 'supposed' 9 hour battery life potential that was advertised.  That was most likely an i3 model with no keyboard and the machine was only turned on and not used.  That is usually the method of battery life tests with computers.  Don't go by that.  Nobody can ever guarantee the recommended life limit because another factor is involved as well - the batteries themselves.  No two batteries are exactly alike.  Lithium-Ion batteries are also unpredictable.
