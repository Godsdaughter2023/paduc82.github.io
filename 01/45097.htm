Nothing will show up in the horizontal Favorites BAR until you put something there

There are two steps to make this work:

  • Enable the Favorites BAR (which it appears you have already done)
  • Move some favorites into the Favorites BAR

Most people use the horizontal Favorites BAR for just a few of their frequently used favorites... and do not attempt to put their entire collection of favorites there.  Instead, the "big" list of favorites remains in the vertical Favorites PANE.

So, open the vertical Favorites PANE the normal way (via the "hub" icon... the 3 little lines), expand the FOLDER called "Favorites Bar", and move a few favorites there via a "drag-n-drop" operation.  This is a special folder that mirrors the horizontal Favorites BAR.  So, anything you put there will show up both in the vertical Favorites PANE and in the horizontal Favorites BAR.

Note: Make sure that you don't accidentally have two folders called "Favorites Bar" in the vertical Favorites PANE.  If you have more than one, then you should be using the one at the top of the list.

Resetting the entire Windows Update Component manually

 

Perform the following steps to reset the Windows Update component manually...

 

Step 1:
Stopping the Background Intelligent Transfer Service and the Automatic Updates service....

Renaming the Catroot2 folder...
