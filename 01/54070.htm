Sometimes it is difficult to determine if a problem is caused by the hardware failing to function according to manufacturer specifications. If a battery is not charging/charging, it is either the cable is faulty or the charger component on the motherboard that is not working properly.

Please try these basic steps and check.

  • I would suggest you to remove the battery and reinsert it, to make sure it’s connected firmly.
  • Make sure to use the original adapter that came with your Windows 7 Pc.
  • Check for any damage or loose connection on the charging port of the tablet.
  • Connect the charger to the wall socket directly instead of a surge protector while charging.

Follow the methods if above steps doesn't help.

Method 1: Use the hardware troubleshooter.

The Hardware Troubleshooter is an automated tool which will check the hardware connected to the computer for any known issues with it and provides the details and on how to fix them. Follow these steps and run the troubleshooter.

  1. Connect the charger to the computer.
  2. Click on Start.
  3. Go to Control Panel.
  4. Type troubleshooting in the search tab and press Enter.
  5. Under Hardware and Sound.
  6. Click Configure a device.
  7. Follow the on screen instructions.

If the issue persist, follow method 2.

Method 2: Disable USB selective suspend setting.

This method prevents your USB device from powering down.

  1. Click the Start button, type power plan in the Search box, then click Choose a power plan.
  2. Next to your currently selected plan, click Change Plan Settings.
  3. Click Change advanced power settings.
  4. Click the box to expand USB Settings, then expand USB selective suspend settings.
  5. Click plugged in, then click the drop down menu and select disabled.
  6. If you're using a laptop, click Battery, then click the drop down menu and select disabled. Hope this information is helpful. If the issue still persists, please post back for further assistance, we'll be glad to assist you.

    mdavej say:

    Asus laptop touchpad cursor / mouse pointer jumps - SOLUTION!

    There are many, many threads about this issue, all of which give the wrong answers. The problem is that on Asus x401u and probably many other similar makes and models, the touchpad works properly when the laptop is running on battery power alone, but jumps wildly when plugged into the power supply.

    Other threads just say to add/remove/uninstall/reinstall/update drivers, even reformat the computer and re-install the OS. None of these solutions do anything because the cause is simply that cheap, RF noisy, 3rd party replacement power supplies affect the very sensitive touchpad. It's an electrical problem, NOT as software problem. All the software/settings changes in the world cannot fix it.

    The solution is to throw the bad power supply in the garbage and get a higher quality one. The original part from Asus is an option but is quite expensive. However, I have found at least one Dell power supply that's cheap and plentiful and works fine. Mouse pointer is rock steady with it. Here's the exact model I have which I pulled from an old Dell thin client box. It has the same specs (19 VDC, 3.42 A) and plug as the original Asus adapter and exhibits none of the jumpy pointer issues other 3rd party power supplies do.

    Dell P/N 59826 OEM power supply made by APD for the Wsye 7010 thin client box

    Here's a link to one on Amazon for $25: https://www.amazon.com/APD-Wyse-7010-Power-Adapter/dp/B07JFNBKWT

    Or search ebay for "APD 59826" and find several for $8-$12 shipped, which is as cheap as the lower quality ones that don't work.
