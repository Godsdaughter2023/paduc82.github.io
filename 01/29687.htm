chad tpruitt say:

  Well, the first thing is that you have to have a Gold subscription...which you do.  The next is that you have to determine the online capabilities of any games you wish to play.  You can usually do this by reading the manual (if you game has one!) or looking at the box it came in (if you bought a new one at a retail store) and there is normally two lists in the lower-left hand corner:

(1) The offline capabilties:  for example, Gears Of War 3 (in green lettering) says "player 1-2", "co-op", etc.  These are aspects of the game that can be played without a Live connection.

(2) The online capabilties:  GOW3 states (in red lettering) "online multipler 2-10", "co-op 2-5", etc.  These aspects require a Live connection and a Gold sub. 

Specifically, the "online multiplayer" is what you are looking for...not all Xbox Live enabled games support this.  You need to make this distinction or you will buy a game just because it says "Xbox Live" in the assumption that you can play competevily online against players, only to find out you can't.  I made this mistake myself a few years ago when I purchased the first "Monopoly" game for the 360 console.

Once all this is determined, you will have to locate the menus that will allow you to play online in whatever aspect you wish.  This differ from one game to the next.

The Factory image partition on the D: drive is used to restore the system to its original configuration if something goes wrong with the operating system. This partition should not be used to store files and other folders.

 

Check to make sure if system restore is disabled on this drive by following the steps below:

 

  1. Press Windows icon key on the keyboard, type Control Panel and select the top most search result.
  2. Click on View by located to the top right of the window and select Large option.
  3. Click on System and select the System Protection option from the left side panel of the screen.
  4. From the window, select D: drive and click the Configure button.
  5. Under Restore Settings, check the Disable system protection option.
  6. Click on Apply and OK.
  7. Close all the open applications on the PC and reboot the PC to check if the changes are effective.

 

When you are performing a backup of your data you will prompted with the list of drives you want to backup. You can choose to back up all the drives at the same time.

 

Follow the steps below to backup your drives:

 

  1. Press Windows icon key, type File history and select the top most search result.
  2. Click on Add a drive and add the drives you want to backup.
  3. Once the drives are added you will be notified with a message on the screen saying your drives are now backed up.
  4. You can also choose to back up the drives according to your concern by going to More options and enabling the options as per your interest.
