  4. Click on view all and select Printer.

  1. Press Windows +R keys.
  2. Type services.msc in the box and press enter.
  3. Now locate services called Print Spooler, make sure the services are Started and set to Automatic.
  4. To set the service to automatic, right click on the service and click Automatic.
  5. Click Apply and then click ok.

madeni k n say:

Hello,

 

I apologize for the delayed turn-around on this one. I believe I have some information that could help you understand this behavior in the computer.

 

A computer usually has a graphics card that controls the attributes like Screen Resolution. But, when the computer is booting up, the graphics card is not active. During the boot phase, it is usually the BIOS that controls the display and this display is usually much smaller than what your Screen Resolution is set to. This is pretty normal and it is basically because BIOS is a basic routine that does not have to worry much about a high resolution display.

 

When you are at the screen that you have referred to, the video is still controlled by the BIOS. The behavior you have described in the computer is because your BIOS does not support a display equivalent to the resolution that your graphics card does. The resolution supported by your BIOS is much smaller than the resolution you have set for Windows. This is why the screen you are referring to appears to be compact.

 

So, you do not have to worry about this and it is not a fault in your monitors. It is just that the BIOS in your computer does not support a higher resolution than what you see in that screen while booting. I believe this helps you understand the behavior.

 

Do let us know if you need any further help with this issue, we will be glad to be of help!
