Hi Jerace

If you are dragging the document window with the mouse, this is probably the Windows Snap feature that is resizing the window to full screen.

Most Windows computers do not have an F: drive.  A standard, run-of-the-mill Windows installation has a C: drive and -- at least until fairly recently -- a D: drive that is an optical disk (CD/DVD) drive (because most new software is downloaded from the Internet rather than purchased as a physical CD or DVD, computer manufacturers save a bit of money, space, and weight by eliminating the CD/DVD drive).

As Solid State Drives (SSDs) were starting to be introduced, some computers came with a relatively small SSD where Windows was installed and a larger (and slower) "standard" hard disk drive where most of the user's data was stored.  This might or might not be your situation.

1.  In general, it is safe to let Disk Cleanup to remove "Windows Update" files.  The effect of deleting these files is that you will not be able to reverse previously installed Windows Updates.  If your system has been running satisfactorily for a while, there's no reason that you should not delete them.

2.  TreeSize Free shows that your Windows folder is using about 33 GB.  That's fairly normal.

3.  TreeSize Free shows that the main use of the F: drive is two old system backups.  First of all, it's never a good idea to have backup files on the same physical drive as the information that's being backed up.  If the drive fails, you'll lose everything.  Second, those are very old (2011 and 2012).  You can keep them if you think you'll need them for historical reasons or delete them.  Your choice.  On this score, I noticed a backup from 2018 on your C: drive.  You might want to look at that to see what it is and if you still want to keep it.

4.  What I suggest you do is move your personal user data folders (My Documents, My Photos, etc.) from C: to F:.  If you were to move all of these folders, you'll free up about 62 GB on C:.  See the directions for moving these folders here:  https://www.howtogeek.com/howto/windows-vista/moving-your-personal-data-folders-in-windows-vista-the-easy-way/
