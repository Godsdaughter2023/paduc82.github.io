Hi Annie,

It is possible that your PC's current Battery driver is not well optimized. It is also possible that your laptop is not well calibrated, giving wrong battery percentage measurements. To troubleshoot this, we recommend reinstalling your Battery controller driver. To do so, follow these steps:

  1. In the search box, search and open the Device Manager.
  2. Expand Batteries.
  3. Right-click Microsoft ACPI-Compliant Control Method Battery and click Uninstall.
  4. Restart your PC and Windows will automatically reinstall your Battery controller driver.

allan mej say:

Try calibrating your laptop's battery. Calibration every two or three months will ensure accurate battery readings. To do so, follow these steps:

  1. Charge your laptop fully (100%) and leave it plugged in for 2 hours after a full charge.
  2. Go to Control Panel > Hardware and Sound > Power Options > Change plan settings > Change advanced power  settings and set your laptop to automatically hibernate at 5%.
  3. Remove the plug and use or let your laptop run until it automatically hibernates.
  4. Let your laptop rest for 5 hours or more after it automatically hibernates..
  5. Charge your laptop again until the battery reaches its full power (100%).

Your laptop should now report the accurate values of its remaining battery life.
