Hello

The act of Windows automatically reducing the volume of certain applications is actually the operating system. When a user is making or receiving a Skype call, it will automatically lower or mute the volume of other apps while the call was in progress.

To stop Windows from automatically reducing your apps’ volume:

  • Go to Control Panel and select Hardware and Sound.
  • Select "Sounds" from the context menu and then choose the "Communications" tab.
  • Select the "Do nothing" radio button to prevent other sounds on your computer from being lowered during Skype calls.
  • Select "Apply" and then choose "OK" to commit to the changes.

Your follow-up comment [quoted below] regarding re-adding the primary account to the Admins group pointed me in the right direction.  I found that my primary user after being removed from the Admins group subsequently had no group memberships at all...  I added it to the Users group and low and behold, one restart later and my primary account reappeared at the sign in page together with my secondary admin account.  I'm unsure why Windows 10 wouldn't add the default user account to the systems Users group as a matter of good practice, but the issue is that simple to correct.  I hope this helps you and other security minded Windows 10 users.

"It was AFTER I removed my user from the Administrators account that the user vanished. It hadn't occurred to me before, but when I re-added the account to Administrators, the account turned up again and started working properly."
