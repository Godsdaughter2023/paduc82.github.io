Hi Joshua,

When your laptop battery isn’t charging, there’s a chance that the battery itself or your motherboard has a static charge. In order to make it work normally again, you will need to discharge it. To do this, follow these workarounds:

  1. Shutdown your computer.
  2. Remove your laptop batter temporarily.
  3. While it was removed, press the power button for 15 seconds to drain it.
  4. Re-attach the battery again and start your computer.
  5. Once started, press Windows + X and click Device Manager.
  6. Expand the Batteries category.
  7. Uninstall the Microsoft ACPI Compliant Control Method Battery.
  8. Go to the menu and click Action > Scan for hardware changes.
  9. Wait for Windows to scan your computer for available resources for this hardware.
  10. Restart your computer.
  11. Check if your battery is now charging.

In addition, we recommend updating your BIOS (Basic input/output system) from your manufacturer’s driver download website.

If the above resolution didn’t work, you will need to contact your computer manufacturer for hardware service warranty or motherboard replacement.

What happened to Microsoft?

I like the overall engagement with the community from the MS side, but given the insane amounts of problems raised with this Skype Beta and Skype Preview ad-hoc releases, I think it's worth asking:

  • What background / expertise does the development team have in developing applications for Linux? Ditching powerful native tools the likes of QT in favour of Electron speaks for itself, but I'm still curious about the causes. As a well established software giant, I doubt it's lack of talent ...
  • What is the QA staff doing? Why is the community being used as beta testers?
  • What is the UX staff doing? The new SfL UI is abominable to say the least, and the SfL Preview release is even worse in terms of UI than the Beta release (despite being more functional).
  • What about transparency? Changelogs are important if you want sensible feedback from your users. We have yet to see what actually changes from one preview release to the next except minor cosmetic changes and alleged bug fixes as reported by MS reps here. Skype was moved to a "cloud" architecture yet we have little details on how cloud data is handled when it comes to privacy and encryption (e.g. photos and files sent out so that they can be received even if the sender is offline).

Feedback is important, and MS should engage w/ it's customers/consumers as it does, but it would appear Linux users presumably because of their traditionally techy-er background are subtly being asked to do the job for an entire department at their own expense.

MS, if you value feedback, please get a grip! It's not a good direction that you're headed in ...

arminatorx say:

The Xbox's online sign in system relies on an accurate clock. Therefore there is no way to manually set the clock on the Xbox One. If it's online, it will automatically set it's clock to an internet time server with the correct time and date. 

If the console is offline, it will not be able to check the correct time, and since there is no battery or manual time setting, you're stuck with an incorrect date.
