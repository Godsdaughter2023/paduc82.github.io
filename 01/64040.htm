Your 20-digit product ID (PID) describes what product you have.  This is not evidence of your license; for that you need the 25-digit "product key."

I don't know to decode all of the information from Microsoft PIDs, but the first five digits indicate the "edition" of Windows (e.g., XP Home, 7 Professional, etc.) and the 3-digit second group shows the "distribution channel (e.g., OEM, Volume License, Retail, bundle/Not For Resale).

For example, this page --> http://wiki.lunarsoft.net/wiki/Product_IDs attempts to decode some Windows XP-related Product IDs.

Your original PID, which begins with 00359-OEM, means that you had Windows 7 Home Premium pre-installed by the manufacturer of your computer.

Your current PID, which begins with 00359-112, means that you still have Windows 7 Home Premium; I believe that the "112" means "Retail" channel.

At any rate, the key that you need to enter in order to activate your computer is the 25-digit key that is on the sticker on the case of your computer.  If you have a laptop, it may be inside the battery compartment.  Do NOT post this key here.

III. Starting from the most recent dates, find an update for Windows that has failed to install, and then double-click the update to view more information.

IV. Updates that have failed to install will display Failed under the Status column.
