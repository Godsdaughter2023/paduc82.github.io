You might of tried some of these 'fixes' but here are some of the things I did, they should also work with Windows 7, except for 'BCDEDIT /SET DISABLEDYNAMICTICK YES' fix.


1. BIOS: Go into your computers' BIOS settings (usually you hit F2 or Delete when booting-up) and turn off any 'power saving' options as CPU throttling seems to be a problem.

2. POWER PLANS: I had a problem on my Windows 8, Samsung NP350V5C-A06, Intel i7 CPU, laptop which would reset the power plan. Turns out the problem wasn't Windows 8 - it was the Samsung settings running in the background. To fix the 'problem' I opened up the Samsung configuration settings and went to: General tab > Silent Mode > and selected 'Off'. The AUTO (default) fan option was controlling the fan speed noise, but, it was also resetting my preferred Windows power plan back to the 'Power saver' plan every time I booted-up! It seems other manufacturers have this annoying hidden 'feature' as well, so check your computer settings.

3. CPU MANAGEMENT: Check the CPU 'Processor power management' under your preferred power plan. Go to Control Panel > Hardware and Sound > Power Options > Edit Plan Settings > Change advanced power settings. Make sure you have these settings: Minimum processor state > Plugged in: = %100 and Maximum processor state > Plugged in: = %100 and System cooling policy > Plugged in: = Active. Check anything else that could throttle back or slow down your computer, such as the hard disk settings or any other power saving options.

Hi Paul,

 

The following steps would enable you to transfer files to the new user account and delete the old user account. However, to perform these steps, you need to be signed in as an administrator on your computer. If you haven’t set the new user account as the administrator, you can perform the following steps:

 

1. Press Windows+I.

2. Click on Accounts.

3. Click on Family and other users.

4. Under Other People, click on the account that you want to set as administrator.

5. Click on Change account type.

6. Click on Administrator, then OK.

 

To transfer files from one user to another in Windows 10, you can follow the steps below:

 

1. Open the File Explorer by pressing Windows+E.

2. On the left pane, click on This PC.

3. Click on Local Disk (C:), then go to the Users folder.

4. Click on the account that you want to copy files from.

5. Right-click on the files/folders/programs and select Copy.

6. Go back and access the Users folder again.

7. Click on the name of the new user account folder you want to copy files to.

8. Right-click on it and select Paste.

9. Repeat the steps as needed.

 

If you are looking to deleting the old user profile because it is corrupted, you can follow the steps below:

 

1. Press Windows+I.

2. Click on Accounts.

3. Click on Family and other users.

4. Under Other People, click on the account that you want to delete and click on Remove.
