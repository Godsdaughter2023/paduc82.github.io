ottersgonnaott say:

I have a Surface Book and have had no such issues, ever.  The trackpad is perhaps one of the best I've ever used.  You can hold your finger down and just roll on the tip for per-pixel changes.  As an artist and a photographer, this is a godsend even compared to great trackpads like the ones on MacBook Pros.

There is however one issue of note: capacitive conductance.  If you clean your hands and dry them thoroughly, then additionally let them air-dry for a moment, you'll have a hard time activating the trackpad.  After about 20 minutes your fingers collect natural oils again and all is fine...  usually.  The weather, what you just ate, if you use hand sanitizer...  many things contribute to the delay or deadzone before the device recognizes your finger.  When this happens to me in the very rare case, I can always stop it by just wetting my fingers (side of a cold soda can works great) and trying again.  The issue will be solved and won't crop up for weeks, as again the trackpad is great in general and it comes down to personal usage cases.

I know this is a very 'your mileage may vary' sort of reply, but the issue is entirely circumstantial.  That is, of course, unless you have a defective unit or have modified the software powering it.  Your first course of action should have been to call support and lead down whatever pathway ends in a replacement unit or repairs.  Your store may do on-spot replacements as well, but again that's circumstantial.  If that fails, it's likely not a hardware or software issue and is a personal usage issue.  You might have your air conditioning set to 'polar icecaps' and are sapping all stray humidity from the air, which dries out your fingers more.  You may have too much grease on the touchpad itself, creating a conductive layer that stops the trackpad from determining what is your finger versus grime.  Whatever the case, it's not due to all Precision Multi-touch Trackpad units being poorly designed.  Do some debugging and really keep the mindset of potential bad conductance in your head and you'll probably find a solution like I did.

I hope that helps even if a little, and I wish you luck.
