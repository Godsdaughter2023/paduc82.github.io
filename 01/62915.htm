john_barnett say:

Hello judyhelpel,

If a webpage has video running then you are using data. I'm not a gamer, but I assume that gaming site run video on their webpages, this then could quickly increase your data usage. While Windows does keep a browsing history it is only of where you have been, not necessarily how much data has passed between you and the webpage. You may, on this occasion, just have to accept that you used 1.5GB of data.

However, for future usage you could download a small utility called Network Activity Indicator. This put a small icon in the notification area (it displays two monitor's which blink when you are exchanging data with the web.) 

As well as letting you know that your internet connection is working, by simply hovering your mouse over the two monitors it will also tell you how much data has been sent and also how much data has been received. This then will give you a rough idea of your daily data usage.

Download the Network Activity Indicator from: http://www.softpedia.com/get/Network-Tools/Network-Information/Network-Activity-Indicator.shtml  Once downloaded, click on the Network Activity Monitor. exe file and the icon will appear in the notification area. Once in the notification area, right click on the icon and select Settings. In the Setting box make sure you place a check mark (tick) next to the Load at Windows Startup option.

No doubt there are many other network activity application and I'm sure other people will suggest alternatives. The Network Activity Indicator, however, is the one I use, even though I am on an unlimited download package.
