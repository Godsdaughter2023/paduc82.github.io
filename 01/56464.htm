roohi_taj say:

Hi DerrickMiller2,

Thank you for writing to Microsoft Community Forums.

We at Microsoft prioritize our customer’s privacy, your personal information or browser history is not visible to organization or Microsoft. When it says “gives my work a level of access” that means the access provided to the applications of your organization. Those policies are set by the organization.

What access does this give my work to my personal computer?

The access you get on your personal computer with the work account is dependent on the IT management.

Can they see what I have saved throughout my PC? Can they see my browser history?

No organization cannot see anything which you do or save on the computer.

Can they monitor my PC in general as if it was theirs?

No, they cannot monitor your PC, as it is not a Domain computer.

Note: A Domain PC provides single user login from any computer connected to that network. Users are allowed (or restricted) to access resources depending upon the permissions they have given from the organization. Everything we do on the pc is restricted and recorded from the organization IT department.

If so is there a way to access my OneNote and not allow them this privilege?

You can access OneNote by adding the work account in the Access work or school account without worrying about the privacy of your data on the computer.

Where can I see what level of access they have to my personal pc? 

The level of access is set by the organization. For specific information about this, I would suggest you to contact the IT admin of your organization and check.

For more information, I would suggest you to check the article on Enroll your Windows 10 device in Intune.

Hope it helps.
