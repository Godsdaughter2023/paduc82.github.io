Where is the PrtSc key - Microsoft Community

marie melancon say:

Where is the PrtSc key

I need to know what this is so that I can post a screen shot to find our how to merge 2 files.
tryx3 say:

It is most often in the upper right hand area of the keyboard but your PC manual should show you exactly where it is.  It can be labelled PrtSc, PrntScrn or anything similar really.

When you find it you might see it is in blue [particularly if you have a laptop] to indicate that you need to press the blue Fn key at the same time [again, your PC manual should tell you].  The blue Fn key is often in the lower left area close to the Windows key.

When you try it and have five minutes to spare you might want to experiment - PrntScrn "captures" the whole screen area to the "Clipboard" & you then paste into a graphics application such as MS Paint [part of the standard Win7 system] then save this as a png / jpg / other graphics format file to suit your preferences.  [jpg is probably the most common format so that makes it convenient for sharing with other people].

You will also find that the Alt key with PrntScrn will capture just the active application window or a dialog box if you have one on the screen at the time.  You can also try combinations such as Ctrl key with PrntScrn to see how they behave on your PC [as some people report differences between their machines].

On my laptop, for example, I use

PrntScrn - capture the whole screen [this is a standard action]

Alt-PrntScrn - capture the active window [this is a standard action]

Ctrl-Fn-PrntScrn - capture the whole screen including any right-click context menu that is onscreen at the time  [I understand that this is not a standard action]
