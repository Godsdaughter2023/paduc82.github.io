This is normally a result of the "Print Spooler" not being started...


Make sure the Print Spooler in "SERVICES" is started, and Bidirectional Support is Enabled... If this doesn't help, you will probably need to uninstall\reinstall.


The "Print Spooler" can be found in Control Panel... Administrative Tools... Services.


To find Bidirectional Support, right click on the printer in Devices and Printers, select Printer Properties, PORTS tab, and it will be near the bottom...


If you attempt to reinstall, turn off the Print Spooler, REMOVE your printer from Devices and Printers, and unhook it from your computer, and uninstall the software\drivers.


When reinstalling, make sure the Print Spooler is turned off... The reinstallation should turn it back on... It is supposed to start automatically.


HP may have newer drivers for it as well.

Installing new SSD, HOW do I do it? - Microsoft Community

ian7a.7macmillan say:

Installing new SSD, HOW do I do it?

Computer is working great.  Dell XPS 9100 Studio.  Would like to install a 250 Gb SSD to hold the operating system and make faster.  Already have the SSD. How do I do this?
techie bear say:

Hello Ian,

Hopefully the drive came with a set of instructions to physically install the drive. Follow those very carefully. Installing hardware is beyond the scope of this website, though the manufacturer of the SSD probably has a support website similar to this one. Go there and learn what you need to!

 

Once the hard drive is in place, you have two choices, you can migrate the operating system from your existing HDD or install it anew from a System Disk or Hidden Partition. Either way is acceptable.

 

To migrate the OS from the HDD to the SSD, I recommend a freeware program AOMEI Partition Assistant, download from here:  http://download.cnet.com/AOMEI-Partition-Assistant-Standard-Edition/3000-18512_4-75118871.html .

 

Follow the programs instructions very carefully, and as an added safety precaution I would back up my HDD prior to transferring the OS or even installing a fresh OS - just in case. Depending on the drive you purchased, there may be a driver disk with it, if so this would be the time to add it in.

 

Lastly, you'll have to go into the BIOS to change the boot order so your new SSD drive will become the C Drive, and your current HDD will become the "D:\" drive.

 

Let me know if this helps you out.

 

Regards,

BearPup
