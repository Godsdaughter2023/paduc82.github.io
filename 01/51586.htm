lemp say:

I assume that you're referring to Windows Live Photo Gallery (if that's not correct, then please clarify).

Windows Live Photo Gallery is only an image organizer, photo editor, and photo sharing app.  By default, your images (photos) are saved locally on your computer in your "Pictures" library.

Although it's relatively easy to copy photos from your computer back to the SD card for your camera, what happens when you attempt to look at those photos with your camera may not be what you expect. 

What are you trying to accomplish by putting the photos back on the SD card?

If you want to do it, however, here are the steps.

Put the SD card in the card reader on your laptop.  If the "Autoplay" window pops up, select the option at the bottom to "Open folder to view files using Windows Explorer."

If the Autoplay window doesn't automatically open, click the Start Orb and then "Computer."  Look in the category "Devices with removable storage."  In the example below, a 4-slot card reader is connected, which why there are 4 "removable drives" and an SD card from a Canon camera is in one of the slots (CANON_DC), which in this example happens to be drive I:

If you double-click on the entry for your SD card or expand it in the left pane as done in the example above, you'll see the folder structure on the SD card and eventually find where the images are stored. 

Now that you've determined where on the SD card you want to copy your pictures, leave the folder structure on the left pane expanded (as shown) and now click "Pictures" under "Libraries" and navigate through your Pictures folder until you find the images you want to copy.

Now select the image(s) you want to copy and either just drag it (them) to the correct folder on the SD card or right-click and select "Copy" then right-click on the desired folder on the SD card and select "Paste."

What happens next will depend on whether the software in the camera is smart enough to recognize that image files have been added.  If you modified the image files on your computer in anyway, especially if you've changed them from jpg to another image file format, the camera software may not be able to deal with them properly.
