sam___ say:
I can confirm with windows explorer that I'm (somehow) only using 43.9 MB of the available 9.87 GB of the storage space drive, so why does this screen show 3.5 GB in use, and the drive stats show somewhere between 18 and 25% usage?

You are seeing this because your drives are really low capacity (5GB each). The space is consumed by the Storage Spaces meta data to provide drive resiliency. The space is consumed by having multiple copies of the file allocation table, the block allocator and the file system itself. This small overhead is inherent in all software RAID solutions.

Also why do the 5 GB drives provide 4.01 GB pool capacity, but the total pool capacity is 12.7 GB?

Because you selected parity protection. Parity will need around 1/3rd of the space reserved for parity data, therefore you lose that data from your pool. This is shown in the Total Pool Capacity of 12.7GB and Available Pool Capacity of 7.75GB. So basically 7.75GB is the usable part.

I can understand 10 GB including resiliency being 15 GB, but I don't understand why my space fills up @ roughly half total drive space; 

Try not to think of it like RAID. Basically Storage Spaces works at the data block level. So if you have 100GB of data that you want to store, Storage Spaces will eat 150GB from the pool to store that data in a parity configuration. In order to get a proper feel for Storage Spaces make sure the drives are 25GB minimum.

In terms of NAS vs Storage Spaces ... if you're not planning on sharing your storage then stick with Storage Spaces. If you need to have a storage solution for multiple PCs than NAS is the way to go.

-Sam
