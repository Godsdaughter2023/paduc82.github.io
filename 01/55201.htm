bossdweeb say: As I said previously - Win7 has All versions of Direct X 9, 10 and 11. Including 'older' files.

The reason for using the updater is twofold.
One - The updater will install any 'missing' DX files. Win7 does not come with all the DX 9 files ( a mystery known only to those who developed Win 7 ), and it will install those.
Two - There have been a couple of new DX 10 and 11 files introduced since Win7 was released.

Graphics cards that support DX 10 also support DX 9, but the majority of games still  use DX 9.
Some games use DX 10, and even fewer use DX 11.

If you are still unclear on what has been said do some research into the topic.

The following is a list of the Direct X .dll's you will have when the files are up to date.
Go to the Windows / System 32 folder, (and SysWOW64 if you have 64bit). 
They are in alphabetical order and will start with d3dx9 - 24 > 43 . Then d3dx10 - 33 > 43 & finally d3dx11 - 42 > 43.
