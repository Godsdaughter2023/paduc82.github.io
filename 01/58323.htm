cmdrkeene say:

When you mentioned ratios, you were close.  It really comes down to the pixel density, or DPI (dots per inch).  If a TV and a monitor both have the same resolution (same number of pixels), but the TV is larger, then obviously those pixels are spaced further apart, making images appear larger.

Computers have methods of scaling images to try to adjust for this.  Windows 8 is the first that included some new ways (as well as compensating for different DPI on each screen).  Previously you just had to "live with it" as it was, and the differences in sizes could be very stark.
