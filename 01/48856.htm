Missing and corrupt operating system files (like many DLL files) are arguably the biggest cause of major Windows issues. Considering that, plus the fact that SFC /scannnow is completely automatic and very easy to use, the tool should usually be one of your first troubleshooting steps.

robert aldwinckle on forums say:

When I press the start button at the top nothing happens and the Surface doesn't respond at all. 

What happens if you press the Surface Logo button instead?  Do you have anything running?  E.g. is there a Taskbar visible with a Taskbar icon which looks like it is not just the icon?  Depending on what you are seeing in the Taskbar this symptom can have an explanation regarding the Display state.  E.g. it happens when Windows thinks the Tablet is not primary but doesn't understand that it is writing to a phantom primary.

Otherwise when this (no Start menu) happens to me what I do is open Task View, e.g. press Win+Tab and then press Ctrl+Esc (instead of anything else) to raise the Start menu.  When the Tablet is not primary this at least should let you see the Task View for the secondary and then switch back to whatever was showing before.

Also, it is a good idea to start Narrator when you can't see anything changing because it may be happening on the other monitor (even though one is not attached).   So, then you could let Narrator assist you aurally through the "darkness".   Press Win+Ctrl+Enter to start Narrator.  Or presumably on your "Surface 1" you could still use the Logo+Volume Up shortcut for that too.
