lemp say:
The ipconfig command is for diagnostic purposes.  There is nothing to enable or disable.

The fact that the ad hoc network is shown when you "View Available Wireless Networks" on the laptop strongly suggests that you have configured things properly -- which is what it looked like in your original question.

If you can't find the ad hoc network in other devices, your laptop wifi radio is probably turned off.  Most laptops can turn the wifi radio on/off with a key press combination (Fn+some other F key).  Sometimes, there is an actual physical switch.

The user guide for your make and model laptop should explain this.

You can also take a look in Device Manager as follows, but I think the key combination is more likely.

Open Device Manager (Start > Run > devmgmt.msc > OK)
Expand the "Network adapters" section by clicking the + to its left
You should NOT see any warning icons (exclamation point)
Double-click the entry for your wireless adapter
Under "Device status" you should see "This device is working properly."
Under "Device usage" you should see "Use this device (enable)"

If you see something different than the above, please provide that information.
