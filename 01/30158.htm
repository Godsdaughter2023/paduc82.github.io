job sam varkey say:

Hi,

 

We cannot directly pin recycle bin as it is by design of Windows 7.

 

I would suggest you to follow the below steps and check if it helps.

 

A.   Make sure the Recycle Bin is on the desktop.

B.   Right click on the desktop and select New and Folder, then rename it anything you like. (i.e. Trash    Bin)

C.   Drag & drop the Recycle Bin on the new Folder to create a shortcut.

D.   Save the new Folder (containing Recycle Bin shortcut) to the directory of your choice (i.e. C:\Trash Bin) by dragging      and dropping.

 

E.   Unlock the taskbar.

       Unlock and move the taskbar

F.   Right-click on the taskbar, select Toolbar and New toolbar.

G.   Navigate to the location you saved the New Folder (Ex: Trash Bin), select it and click on the Select Folder button.

 

H.   This places the new Toolbar and a Separator (dotted lines) on the taskbar.

I.   Right click on the Separator and uncheck Show Text and Show Title.

J.   Right click on the Separator and select View and Large Icons.

K.   You now have the Recycle Bin pinned to the taskbar.

L.   To Keep the Recycle Bin on Right Side of Taskbar

        I.   Left click on the Separator to the left of the folder and hold, then drag it to the left and right to make any adjustments to how you want the Recycle Bin positioned on the right side of the taskbar.

 

        II. Release the left click when done.

        III. Go to step N.

 

M.   To Have the Recycle Bin on Left Side of Taskbar

 

        I.   Left click on the Separator of the pinned taskbar programs on the far left side and hold, then drag it to the right past the Recycle Bin and release.

        II.   Left click on the Separator of the pinned taskbar programs on the now right side and hold, then drag it to the left next to the Recycle Bin and release.

 

N.   Lock the taskbar.

        Unlock and move the taskbar

 

 

Hope this helps.
