I'm sorry to say that I don't really have a solution for you, only guesses.

You can try posting in the Apple forum, but although I've seen other posts describing your problem, they don't usually have a solution:  https://discussions.apple.com/community/itunes/itunes_for_windows

You do have the current version of iTunes, so that isn't likely the problem.

You said that you used to be able to access the iTunes store and hadn't made any changes.  This is a long shot, but check the following.  First, however, if your computer is able to connect to your router by either wifi or Ethernet, connect it using the method that you haven't been using.  Assuming that made no difference or that you only have one network adapter, do the following:

  1. Open Device Manager (click Start Orb, type Device Manager, press Enter)
  2. Double-click the entry for your network adapter
  3. Select the "Driver" tab
  4. Is the "Rollback driver" button greyed-out or is it active (if it is clickable, don't click on it just yet)?
  5. Make a note of the Driver Provider, Driver Date, and Driver version
  6. Go to the web site of your computer's manufacturer, enter your specific model identification (e.g., a Dell Service Tag) and determine the date and version of the network adapter driver on offer.
  7. If the driver installed on your computer is older than the driver offered by your computer manufacturer, download and install the newer driver.  If the driver installed on your computer is newer than the driver offered by the driver offered by your computer manufacturer and the answer to question 4 is "Yes," then use the "Rollback driver" button to reinstall the older driver.

As far as Bonjour is concerned, you probably can safely uninstall it.  This may or may not have any effect on your connection problem.  See these:

http://apple.stackexchange.com/questions/45765/do-i-really-need-bonjour-on-windows

https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/windows_7-performance/do-i-need-bonjour-on-win-7/266c36f9-8239-46cc-bd5d-7dd2715a7622

Have you checked the time and date on your computer? See these:

http://www.appledystopia.com/how-to/fix-connect-itunes-store-page-1/

http://www.idownloadblog.com/2016/03/16/fix-cannot-connect-to-itunes-store-2/

Assuming that you don't have problems accessing other Internet sites, the symptom you describe sounds (to me, at least) like a security block between your computer and the iTunes store.  You said that you have no LAN or proxy server set-ups; I assume that you don't use a VPN, either.  If the problem remained even with Norton 360 completely uninstalled, the only other thing that I can think of would be some restriction imposed by your ISP.  I find it hard to believe, however, that if this was the case you wouldn't have heard about such an issue from general chatter in your country (I assume from your use of Windows 7 Pro N that you're in the UK or Europe).

Some of the suggestions in the two articles above focus on problems connecting to the iTunes store from an iOS device and suggest trying from a MAC or PC on the same network.  You should try the opposite:  assuming that you have a wireless router, can your iPhone or iPad access the iTunes store over your Internet connection?  If you can, then you can be fairly certain that the problem is NOT your ISP or Internet connection.

The following is just grasping at straws.  It won't hurt and might possibly help.  Do this after you uninstall Bonjour (reboot after uninstalling Bonjour).

Open an Elevated Command Prompt (click Start Orb, type cmd.exe then right-click on the link to cmd.exe and select "Run as administrator")

Type the following commands in the black Command Prompt window and press Enter after each line

ipconfig /flushdns

ipconfig /registerdns

netsh int ip reset c:\resetlog.txt

netsh winsock reset

Turn off your computer (click Start Orb then click "Shut down")

Turn off your router or disconnect it from mains power. 

Wait a minute or two and then restart the router.

Give the router several minutes to reconnect and then restart your computer.

Test iTunes store connectivity.

a. user say:

Hi cardeler,

The functionality scan to email is only for printers and scanners all in one. If Windows Live Mail is not include on the option. The only option available is to scan and send it manually in Windows Live Mail.

Please follow this workaround.

1. Turn on your scanner.
2. Place your document face down on the scanner glass. Close the lid.
3. Open your scanner/printer software on your computer. Most printer software will give you the option to print, copy or scan. Choose scan. You probably will be asked how you want to scan your document. Choose to scan it to a PDF.
4. Hit the scan button on your computer. Once the scan is complete, you will name and save your PDF. Save the PDF on your desktop.
5. Open your Windows Mail.
6. Click "File" in the main menu. Scroll down and click "New Mail Message."
7. Click "Insert" in the main menu. Scroll down and click "Attach." You also can click the paper-clip icon at the top of the message.
8. Browse for the PDF you named and saved in Step 3. Click the "Attach" button. The PDF will appear in the header of the message.
9. Compose your message and add recipients. Click the "Send" button.
