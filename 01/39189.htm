    gokul t say:

    Hi Ashok Bishnoi,

     

    After you convert a basic disk to a dynamic disk, you cannot change the dynamic volumes back to partitions. Instead, you must delete all dynamic volumes on the disk and then use the “Convert To Basic Disk” command. If you want to keep your data, you must first back it up or move it to another volume.

     

    Important Once you change the basic disk to dynamic disk, it cannot be changed back to basic disk without deleting the partitions and rebuilding the disk and subsequently resulting in data loss. Hence before proceeding, please backup all your data.

     

    Once you’ve backed up the data, please follow these steps to convert the dynamic disk to basic disk:

     

    a.    Click “Start” and click “Control Panel”.

    b.    Select “Administrative Tools”.

    c.    Click “Computer Management” in Administrative Tools.

    d.    If prompted by User Account Control (UAC), click “Yes”.

    e.    In the left pane, click “Disk management” under “Storage”.

    f.     In the dynamic disk (e.g. Disk 1) that you want to convert to a basic disk, right click on each volume on the disk and click “Delete Volume” until the whole disk is unallocated space.

    g.    Right click on the disk (e.g. Disk 1) that you want to convert to a basic disk, and click on “Convert to Basic Disk” if available.

     

    When dynamic disk is converted to basic disk, you may create partitions if required.

     

    For more information on converting dynamic disk to basic disk, see Change a dynamic disk back to a basic disk

     

    Note The steps mentioned in the above article are applicable to Windows 7, too.
