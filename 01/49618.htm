Simply increase "repeat delay" rate to "long" in the system settings.

Open the Windows Start Menu and click on the gear icon

Select “Devices” item in Windows Settings

Click on “Speed” tab in the Keyboard Properties window

You can adjust the repeat delay (the length of time the computer waits before it starts repeating a key). I moved this all the way to "Long".

Click "Apply".

Click on “OK” .

sarafrazier say:

This is an ongoing problem or bug with Microsoft Live Mail.  I encountered it a year ago.  Here's how I solved the problem.  When I want to send a new email to one of my "groups" I click Email Message.  A New Message window appears.  I click the "To".  A Send An Email window appears.  I type the name of the group in the Search Contacts space.  The group name appears in the To, CC or BCC line which I have chosen.  A + sign also appears.  I click the + sign and all names appear on the To, CC or BCC line.  Then I type my message and click Send and the email is sent to all of the names in my group.  Unless you follow this routine the email will not be sent to all of the people in your group.

Once you get used to this routine is't no problem by this is definitely a Microsoft Live Mail programming problem that Microsoft is unable to solve or is not interested in solving.
