If the file is missing or removed, then the Windows installer will present you with a menu of all versions, asking you which to install. If you use this option, you MUST have a corresponding product key for that version. Windows knows!

3. The salient point here is that you must have a valid product key to activate and validate your installation. File-sharing updates

• This feature is turned on by default, but you can turn it off by going to Settings > Update & security > Advanced options > Choose how updates are delivered.

Annoying notifications

• Get your notifications under control by going to Settings > System > Notifications & actions and turning off things like Show me tips about Windows and individual app notifications.

Start menu ads

• Turn off these pesky ads by going to Settings > Personalization > Start > Occasionally show suggestions in Start.

Targeted ads from third-party apps

• You can turn this off by going to Settings > Privacy > General > Let my apps use my advertising ID for experiences across apps (turning this off will reset your ID)

Getting to know you

• You can stop Cortana from getting to know you, and clear your information on your device, by going to Settings > Privacy > Speech, inking, & typing and clicking Stop getting to know me.

Apps running in the background

• To do this, go to Settings > Privacy > Background apps and toggle off each app individually.

The lock screen

• You can disable the lock screen and go straight to the log-in screen, but you'll need to head into the Windows Registry.

All the syncing

• To turn off settings syncing (including themes and passwords), go to Settings > Accounts > Sync your settings. You can turn off all settings syncing, or you can selectively turn off specific settings.

• To turn off search history syncing, open Cortana and go to Settings > My device history and My search history.

The pretty visual interface

•  You can turn off most of Windows 10's visual effects by right-clicking the Start button and going to System > Advanced system settings. Under the Advanced tab, go to Performance and click Settings, then uncheck all the visual effects you'd prefer not to see.
