I'm sorry, that should have been 'Shell:Personal' to open the Documents folder. But that area where you see the path to the folder you are viewing and can select dropdowns to navigate to other locations is known as the Address Bar: Parts of the File Explorer Window

I imagine that when you tried redirecting the first time, you selected the parent folder where you wanted  the folder & instead inadvertently designated the parent as Documents or Music or whatever (there's no shortage of "My D:\ Drive is Now a Music Folder" posts out there!)

Even though it's not your core concern, I would recommend pointing the Shell folder entries to your folders on the D:\ drive. It will allow you delete the folders you don't use on your C:\ drive. Also, many programs/apps query these values to determine default Open/Save locations when displaying a dialog, so less typing/navigation if they open to your preferred locations. These are found in the registry under:

HKCU\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\User Shell Folders

You would, for example, under the Personal value, replace:

%UserProfile%\Documents

with

D:\Users\Klaus Rossler\MyDocuments

Most of the names are obvious, with the exception of Downloads, which is labeled as:

{374DE290-123F-4565-9164-39C4925E467B}

Other user folders that can be redirected but don't appear here until redirected (or you create the entries)can be found here:

http://www.winhelponline.com/blog/windows-10-shell-folders-paths-defaults-restore/
