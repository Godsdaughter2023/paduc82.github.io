sam___ say:

Pivor,

I dont understand that part, how can storage spaces simultaneously mirror and stripe data with only 3 drives? And how does this even protect from drive failures, unless its a parity (RAID5) but its write performance is insanely poor.

You are thinking about Storage Spaces as a RAID. It's not like RAID where it works on device level, Storage Spaces abstracts the physical storage so it's more flexible than traditional RAID. Because resiliency is performed on the block level it can store the blocks on any device in pool. In order to stripe on 3 disks it would have to make sure that the blocks are spread across drives in such a way that 2 copies of the blocks exist on 2 different physical devices. That way if 1 of the drives fails the blocks will be available on the remaining 2 drives in the pool. Write performance is that of a single drive and quicker for sequential writes. Are you tuning for IOPS or pure write performance?

What is COW and ReFS? And how it can fail due to file system?

COW is copy-on-write. It's the method of writing data blocks to disk to ensure atomic consistency. When coupled with Microsoft's Resilient File System (ReFS) the data blocks are additionally check summed (CRC-64) and the data scrubber will ensure data consistency. In other words it will automatically protect your data against bit-rot, etc. Because you have further abstracted your storage you have forgone the benefits of these features.

So 1GB of data on a mirrored volume consumes 2GB of space. That 1GB on parity consumes 1.5GB.

Pre-orders of the Limited Edition of the game grant access to a DLC pack called "Back to Karkand", a reference to the "Strike at Karkand" map (one of BF2's most popular maps, featuring some of the game's most intense battles), to include four maps and several weapons and vehicles brought over from Battlefield 2. The offer will end when BF3 is released, but leaks from EA indicate that the pack will become available for general purchase after one month.

Beta Information:

On 30 July 2010, EA announced that purchasers of the Limited Edition and Tier 1 Edition of Medal of Honor for the PC, Xbox 360 and PlayStation 3 will receive an invitation to the beta contingent on Beta availability within 1 year of Medal of Honor's release on 12 October.
