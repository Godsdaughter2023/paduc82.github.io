lemp say:

If the person who fixed your laptop charged you for the service, you should go back to him and ask him to install all of the drivers necessary to get Windows working properly. 

Actually, you may well want to ask him either to reinstall the same edition of Windows 7 that you originally had (e.g., Home Premium or Professional), or provide you with a new Certificate of Authenticity for Windows 7 Ultimate.  I've seen many people in a situation similar to yours post here wondering why -- from several months to several years after their computer was "upgraded" --  they were experiencing popup messages reporting that their installation of Windows was "not authentic" any more.

If you're unable or unwilling to get help from this person, you need to either use the "Detect Drivers" button (if you select "Guide me") or enter your Dell Service Tag (if you select "Find it myself") when you go to the Dell support site.  The Service Tag is supposed to enable Dell to determine exactly what hardware is installed in your particular computer and then offer you the specific drivers that you need.  If you can't find your service tag on your computer (e.g., the label has worn off), use the "Detect Product" button.
