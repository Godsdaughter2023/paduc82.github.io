considerate_guy say:

The project files from earlier versions of Movie Maker (2010 to 2012) should open fine on the newest version of Movie Maker 2012.

Do you get a message saying it can't open the project file? Or does it open the project and not show any video?

Remember, a project file is not a movie file. The project file contains the current editing state of your project. That means it remembers which clips you added, where you trimmed those clips, what transitions and effects you applied, etc. But the project file doesn't actually contain any video, photos or music.

A project file is more like a blueprint or a recipe telling how to put things together. But it relies on the source files (videos, photos and music) being in the specified location on your computer. So if you take a project file from one computer to another (without the accompanying source files) you'll end up with a project that shows the timeline but with a yellow triangle with an exclamation for each media item.

If that is what you are seeing, you need to copy the original source files from the Windows 7 PC to the Windows 10 PC. You may also need to double-click on one of the problem clips and navigate to the location on your computer so it can relink them with the project.

To prevent this problem in the future, don't forget to finish your project as a movie. That movie can be passed from computer to computer without needing any of the source files. And if you do need to transfer a project file, you'll have manually transfer copies of all the videos, photos and music too, or else the project won't open fully.

mark ole say:

Hi Brian,


We understand that music does not play though the external speakers when connected to your Surface device.

Here are some troubleshooting steps that may help you resolve the issue.

Adjust the volume

Make sure that you've adjusted volume levels in the right places. You can control the volume in several places:

Volume button: Press the volume button on your Surface to adjust the volume up or down.
Keyboard: If your Surface Typing Cover or keyboard has volume keys, press the volume-down, volume-up, or mute key.
Desktop taskbar: In the desktop taskbar, select Speakers Speakers icon, and adjust the volume control up or down.

Audio accessories: Adjust the volume on the headset, speakers, phone, or other audio accessory or adjust it in the software included with the accessory. To learn more about adjusting volume levels, check the info that came with your accessory or go to the manufacturer's website.

Apps: Some apps have a volume control within the app.

Try another device with your external speakers.

Plugging your external speakers to another device will help you further isolate the issue.

Get the latest updates


In many cases, downloading and installing the latest updates for Windows and your Surface will fix the issue. To see how, go to Install Surface and Windows updates.

Restart your Surface

  1. Go to Start, and select Power > Restart.
  2. After your Surface has restarted and you've signed back into Windows, try listening to something (play music or a video) on the built-in speakers and try recording something using the built-in mic.

Check your default audio device settings


Check and, if needed, change your default audio device settings:

  1. Type in sound in the search box. Select Sound.
  2. Do either or both of the following:

    Select the Playback tab, select the device you want to use for playback (for example, the built-in speakers on your Surface, external speakers, or a headset), and select Set Default.

    Select the Recording tab, select the device you want to use for recording (for example, the built-in mic or an external mic), and select Set Default.
  3. Select OK.

Run troubleshooters

Try running automated troubleshooters. These tools can find and fix problems with sound:

Download the Sound playback troubleshooter, and in the File Download dialog box, select Open, and follow the on-screen instructions.

Force shutdown

  1. Press and hold the power button on your Surface for 30 seconds and then release it.
  2. Press and hold the volume-up button and the power button at the same time for at least 15 seconds and then release both.
    The screen may flash the Surface logo, but continue holding the buttons down for at least 15 seconds.
  3. After you release the buttons, wait 10 seconds.
  4. Press and release the power button to turn your Surface back on. You should see the Surface logo.

Restore your Surface


Restoring your Surface is a way to undo recent system changes that may be causing problems. Restoring your Surface doesn’t change your personal files, but it might remove recently installed apps and drivers. To perform System Restore, follow these steps:

  1. Select the Start button, type control panel in the search box and then choose it from the list of results.
  2. Search Control Panel for Recovery.
  3. Select Recovery > Open System Restore > Next.
  4. Choose a restore restore point before the issue started and then select Next > Finish.
