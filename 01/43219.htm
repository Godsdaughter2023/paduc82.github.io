rcwhite say:

Hi, amzvt.

"What we have here is a failure to communicate."  (Cool Hand Luke)  ;^}

Despite the common usage, we never assign a "drive" letter to a physical drive.  A "drive" letter actually applies to a partition (or volume) on a physical drive.

For example, your Drive C: is not the same as your Disk 0.  It is a partition - perhaps the only partition - on Disk 0.  You could shrink that partition and create a second partition on Disk 0 and assign it any available letter - maybe Drive S: (for "Second"?).  Disk 0 would still be Disk 0, holding "drives" C: and S:.

Removable media devices cause some confusion with this scheme.  CD/DVD disks almost always have only a single partition - but they are quite capable of being partitioned into several "drives".  So while your Disk Management might say that Disk 1 is removable "E", it could just has easily hold both "E" and "G".  In addition to optical drives, we also can have SD readers, cameras and other devices which can be partitioned into multiple "drives". And USB flash drives can also hold multiple "drives".

In Disk Management, my USB flash drives are shown in the far-left column of the Graphical Display as "Disk 3" and "Disk 4", but my DVD burners are shown as "CD-ROM 0" and "CD-ROM 1".  And, despite the rule to assign "drive" letters only to partitions (volumes), DM will allow me to "Change Drive Letter and Paths..." on the flash drives and optical drives, with or without media in the physical devices.  (I don't have a partitioned media at hand to see what would show up if I put that into one of the devices.)  And when I move a DVD from CD-ROM 0 to CD-ROM 1, its name (label) stays the same, but it is now in Drive W:, no longer in Drive V:.

Have you tried inserting a disk into that physical drive and then using Disk Management to assign a letter to the partition on that disk, rather than to the physical drive or the physical disk itself?

In your Volume List, at the top of the Disk Management window by default, do you have a drive (D:)?  Is it your DVD drive?  How is that physical drive shown in the Graphical View?

Win7 added some new wrinkles to disk management, and OEMs have different ways of working with the new features.  Some new computers come with two unlettered partitions on Disk 0.  One is the System Partition; the other is a Recovery partition added by the computer maker.  My Acer Netbook, for example, has two such partitions BEFORE Drive C: on Disk 0.  The first is a 12 GB "Recovery Partition".  The second is 102 MB (not GB) labeled "SYSTEM RESERVED"; this contains the boot-up files (bootmgr and the \Boot folder containing the BCD) that Win7 used to start Windows.  (If you insist, you can assign a letter to this partition, then use DIR /a in a Command Prompt window to see these contents.)

I'm not sure this fully explains why you can't change the letter on your DVD drive, but maybe it will help you find the answer.  Please post back so that we can learn from your experience.
