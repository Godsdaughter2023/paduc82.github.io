cmdrkeene say:

There's a few ways to go about this.  But since you do not actually wish to use the Pictures folder that is located in your OneDrive, one easy option is to make sure the "non-OneDrive" pictures folder is the default pictures folder.

The only real warning I'd give you:  storing items outside of OneDrive means they aren't backed up. Make sure you backup your files some other way, or else it could be permanently lost if your computer or hard drive ever has a problem. And even if it doesn't have a problem, most hard drives fail after about 4-5 years (particularly spinning drives). That's just average: some fail sooner of course.

But if you're sure you don't want to keep your files backed up in OneDrive, here's how to make the change you want:

1)  Go to Insert Picture and let the "wrong" picture folder get highlighted.

2)  Right-click this Picture folder and go to Properties.

3)  On the Location tab, click Move and go choose the actual Pictures folder you wish to use instead.

4)  Click OK and click Yes on the warnings.

Now when you go to Insert Picture, it will use the picture folder you selected in step #3.

marilyn o say: Hello jsrober,

This is the information I have found on the 0xC0000185 error:

0xC0000185, or STATUS_IO_DEVICE_ERROR: improper termination or defective cabling of SCSI-based devices, or two devices attempting to use the same IRQ.

If the first and second parameters are 0, then the stack signature was not found in the kernel stack. The cause of this issue is defective hardware. If the I/O status is C0000185 and the paging file is on a SCSI-based hard disk, you should verify the disk cabling and SCSI termination.

Method 1:

For example, to update a graphics card driver, expand the Display adapter’s category, right click your graphics card, and select Update Driver.
