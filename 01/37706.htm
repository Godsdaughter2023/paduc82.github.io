You cannot start in Safe Mode "with everything loaded".  That
would defeat the primary purpose of Safe Mode, which is to
facilitate troubleshooting.

That is also why Safe Mode only loads a basic set of drivers,
essential for the operation of your computer.  For instance,
Safe Mode loads Windows video drivers, instead of fully
featured drivers for your video card.  And you even need to
choose between Safe Mode with networking and Safe Mode
without networking.

I am not aware of any way of enabling printer drivers, after you
have booted into Safe Mode.  You would almost certainly need to
start the print spooler service - which you cannot do in Safe
Mode.

Furthermore, I'm not sure why you would need to boot into Safe Mode.  To set permissions, you must sign into a Windows account with administrator privileges.  (Incidentally, if your operating system is Win XP Professional, you can operate the built-in Administrator account without booting into Safe Mode.)

The following pages may be relevant
