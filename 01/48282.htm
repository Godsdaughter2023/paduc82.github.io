Finally - here’s what worked for me. First a little background. My PC is a Dell All-In-One that originally shipped with Windows 8 on a 1TB mechanical drive that later was upgraded to Window 8.1. Last year, I swapped out the mechanical drive to a smaller (240GB) Intel SSD. The software used to copy the partitions from the old drive to the new SSD proportionally shrank all the partitions so that they would fit on the new SSD. My guess is that one of these partitions is being used by the Windows 10 upgrade but was now too small. I carefully researched the purpose of each partition and deleted 3 small partitions that I deemed/hoped were unnecessary. I just left the space that was freed up as unallocated and did not change the size of any remaining partition. Before doing this the upgrade failed at 24% every time. Now the upgrade made it all the way to "your PC will restart..." but still failed (the PC did not restart). I then rebooted, ran the upgrade again and it worked.

I kept these 3 partitions and deleted the rest: EFI System Partition, OS(C:), Recover Partition.

Of course, if you are going to try this – BE VERY CAREFUL. Deleting the wrong partition will definitely leave you with an unbootable PC.
