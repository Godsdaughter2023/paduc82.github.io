Just some thoughts you might find usefu to narrow things down..  Most of these are kind of a mix of advanced and what if and hail merry.

If your console is in the DMZ,  the issue could be various things on the Internet (including the ISP) probing the WAN address, which essentially is the 360 when it's in the DMZ..   You could try taking it out of the DMZ and using port triggering instead, or just letting UPnP handle it all.   (Use one though, don't mix them all.)       There's also using the firewall/a firewall to block everything but the  traffic to the 360, assuming that the firewall isn't causing an issue.  You could also have  network loops on your end if there are multiple DHCP servers, or multiple DNS caches internally, or of you've got switch or routing loops somehow.   Also if something like port forwarding/triggering become active suddenly when something else does.

You can also turn off the ability to be pinged if it's on and able to be (turn off IDENT and NAT forwarding etc).   Or you can leave respond to ICMP on, and run an external tests, such as the line quality monitor and smokeping located at the dslreports.com  website.    

More advanced, if you can use a hub or port mirroring, you could use wireshark or the like to capture the traffic going to/from the 360 to try and see exactly what traffic is on the network.

rich_alcala say:

I posted this in another thread, this solved the problem for me.  I can't take credit, I got this from another user Nikkeya:

This is what Finally helped me:

1. Go Windows+R or regedit.exe on start menu

2. Go to HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\Setup\Status\Auditboot and make sure its set to zero.

3. Go to HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\Setup\Status\SysprepStatus\Cleanupstate and change value to 7 as well as GeneralizationState to 7
4. Reboot.

Make sure you backup your registry key because i haven't seen this solution thus far anywhere and kid f just stumbled across it clicking on things today. It worked and I was finally able to upgrade to Windows 10.  And everything seems to be working fine.

I implemented this solution and was able to install no problem.  Good luck!

loraine mon say:

Hi,

There is a way to play a game from one device to another. However, on your case, you can only play your friend's game on your computer if he will sign in to the Xbox app or Windows Store using his account.

When you a purchase content, you will receive a license to use that content. The license has two parts: the system license and the local license. The system license applies to the gamertag that purchases the content and the local license applies to the device where you first downloaded the content.


Once the account who purchased the game is signed in, you can download the games from the purchase history list of the said account. This process only applies to the games that were purchased digitally.

If you are trying to play a game with a physical disc copy, all you have to do is insert the disc on the computer.
