In order to HIDE the partitions you will need to use something like the Partition Wizard program I mentioned, and boot from it.

The Recovery Partition you have on the SSD may or may not be useful....

The reason is that the size of the partitions has been changed, and as a result, so has their physical location, or address.... That can sometimes be an issue...

The problem is that you won't actually know until you try and use it...

A lot of times, even with the DVDs needed to RECOVER to Factory from the Image, it won't work on a SMALLER drive, and your SSD is smaller than the HDD.

Movies and things, I would just unhook the HDD... both Power and SATA cable, until you have a method to make an image of it... If you decide to make a backup image of the entire HDD, you could then delete ALL of the partitions and reformat it.

It would be a good idea to test it first, before doing anything extreme to the HDD...

Make an Image of the HDD and then try to RECOVER the image to the SSD...

Part of the USED space is actually empty space when the computer is shutdown or restarted...

A Hibernation File, and a Page File... even though they may show taking up 20GB... that is reserved space that is actually empty... at least as far as an image from a bootable program is concerned.

Empty space is compressed so that it takes up almost 0MB in the actual image.
