The system boots,
The splash screen of the manufacturer or the motherboard shows
This is the point where you can select a boot device.
After this point is where Windows starts to load

Your Power Brick has a status LED - Microsoft Community

deerlover1 say:

Your Power Brick has a status LED

for seasoned Xbox users, this may be common knowledge, but i fell victim to RRoD as a newbie to my first Xbox not aware of what caused it until shortly after my original was dead as in no lights, red or otherwise shortly after a three-RRoD moment, and noticing the little LED in my power brick being red, thinking it was the norm. until i turned it over to reveal a tiny fine print chart. i will of course revise it in this thread

The PSU has a LED on it and many online articles do not tell the real story on it. when your console is on, it should be green. when off, it normally is green as well, but can change to an orange (usually only if you have it plugged into a surge supressor shared with other devices, my current white original which replaced my first is green all the time and it does not matter if the console is off or on)

if your console experiences freezing, or reboots itself, or artifacts show on your screen, immediately check your power brick LED. many times it can be orange or even red even if the console is on. this indicates a weak or failing power supply and yes, a RRoD, mostly the three-red ring one, is power supply related. if your console has issues not turning on at all, and suddenly works after multiple attempts, same issue. your LED is likely red or green.

the power supply LED should be green under normal circumstances. orange is considered 'standby mode' on the power brick label, but it can also mean that it is weak and not supplying enough power to your Xbox even when it is on. your power brick's LED should never  be orange when your console is on. and a red LED indicates fault, and your console may or may not function or may function erratically if this is the case. if your power supply shows an orange or red LED when your Xbox is on, it is advisable you find a replacement before your end up paying for a new Xbox. when mine died completely, it must have also fried a component inside it as well as a different power supply could not even revive it. so i lost the entire setup.
