Partial answer here: http://4sysops.com/archives/how-to-setup-remote-desktop-with-windows-7/ (read the comments too)
But it does not cover the "remote" part, when you have a dynamic IP from your ISP, when you are behind a router, etc.

If you have a router, you need to configure it to forward port 3389 requests (Remote Desktop) to your desktop PC. Usually just type in the IP address of your router in your browser address bar, it should open the router's configuration menu. You can find that IP somewhere typing "ipconfig /all" from the Command Prompt. See also:http://portforward.com/ . You may also want to set up your desktop PC with a static IP address within your home network, between your router and your desktop PC.

Now that you have established the Remote Desktop connection between your router and your desktop PC, you need to work on the connection between your laptop and your router. Your router will most likely get dynamic IP addresses from your ISP, so you can't really use this IP information to connect home, you need a "fixed" address. You may be able to get a static IP address from your reluctant ISP at a cost, but here's a better way:

- Go to http://www.no-ip.com/newUser.php and Create a fixed hostname such as TheMighty-ric4444.no-ip.org for free.
- Download and run ducsetup.exe to automatically assign you IP address to that hostname. It should run at all times in case your IP changes.
- In Remote Desktop Connection, type in that hostname instead of your IP address.
