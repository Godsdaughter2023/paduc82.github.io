chris cupler say:

Hi Robbie.

No you didn't fall thru the cracks.  The forum just seems to go thru the occasional lull period, is all.  And, we are (mostly - exception being those people who are shown as MS employees) all volunteers here. 
And, nope - not Redmond here.  I'm from MI.  :)

Back on point....  one of the links that MSeng posted earlier has instructions on how to change the Recorded TV location:
http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows7/Set-recording-options-in-Windows-Media-Center

Your desire to use something other than the System drive for Recorded TV, though, is actually  A Good Thing - aside from space, it helps quite a bit for performance, particularly on multiple-tuner systems, and/or systems with multiple Extenders connected to it.
I've set mine up just like that for quite some time.

 

Some points to note:
- changing the Recorded TV location will only set that as the location for future recordings (and your Live TV buffer.) It will not move your existing recordings to the new folder.
- IIRC, MC will not automagically use both folders as 'watched folders' for Recorded TV - only the new one.  If you want to maintain the old folder as a watched folder, you'll need to add that (via the Libraries selection in Tasks\Settings.)
- If you use a USB hard drive as your Recorded TV drive, and it (for whatever reason) goes missing when MC needs it, MC will, well, barf, and all TV functionality will fail to work.  It will not fail back to the default location.  For this reason, USB drives are, well, highly discouraged.  Internal drives are preferable (and, far more reliable.)
