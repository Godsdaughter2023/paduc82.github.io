obsessive power say:

[quote user="Kanaye x"]

I'd suggest a power converter instead of buying a new power supply for the machine - the power components inside your console are manufactured to the power standard of the place it's meant to be sold, so buying a power supply that's friendly with the outlets of where you want to go could damage the console.....[/quote]

Not true.  The internal components inside the Xbox 360 console are almost exactly the same the world over.  The reason for the 'almost exactly' is the inclusion of a hardware region lock out. Not all Xbox 360 games are coded to read this lock out however and are therefor region free.  There is a list here http://gaming.wikia.com/wiki/Region_Free_Xbox_360_Games.  The only part of the console which is manufactured to accept region specific power sources is the PSU brick.  The brick is available in either 110V or 240V.  So if you have a U.S console, it is possible to change its PSU from a 110V unit  to a 240V.  Once you have the correct power supply, the console will function normally, no matter what part of the world it is located in. 

[quote user="FearedNightmare"]

The thing is PAL is 25 fps while NTSC is something about 29,98fps so you won't be able to play NTSC games on your PAL xbox[/quote]

Such a comparison doesn't apply to video games systems.  Hi definition games consoles have done away with difference in frame rates and refresh rates between PAL and NTSC. HDMI and Component HD refresh at 60Hz in both PAL and NTSC.  NTSC games no longer run faster than PAL software.

The region lock outs are coded into the games. Many Xbox 360 games are region free and will run fine on any 360 console.  You can play North American games on a PAL console if the game is region free.  There are no technical limitations. 

fearednightmare say:

Alright, just tought PAL xbox 360's were unable to read/play NTSC games and vice versa but you made a valid point about how a lot of games are not region locked which I forgot about on the 360

obsessive power say:

The region locks technically aren't even NTSC/PAL locks because such a differential does not exist within the console.  The games are just coded with a region lock.  When a region locked game is loaded on a console from another region, it will display an error message.  If a game is region free, it does not look for what region the console comes from and continues to run.  The region lock outs for Xbox 360 games these days are down to the publishers, nothing more.

Xbox One on the other hand has no regional lock outs at all for disc games.  You can play any Xbox One game on any Xbox One console.  There are certain region locks for downloads, but that has nothing to do with NTSC and PAL.
