Hi,

There's a possibility that your PC is managing the power for Bluetooth devices and, in the process, dropping the Bluetooth connection when it goes to sleep or when the screensaver comes on.

We suggest that you go to the Advanced tab for the Properties of your Bluetooth radio, check the HCI and LMP versions.

  • If the HCI and LMP versions are 5.x or lower, uncheck the checkbox for “Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power”.

Here's how:

1. Click the Start button and type Device Manager in the Search box. 
2. Click on Device Manager in the Results window.
3. In Device Manager, expand Bluetooth and select the Bluetooth radio. 
4. Right click on the Bluetooth radio and select Properties.
5. On the Power Management tab, uncheck the checkbox for “Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power”.

  • If these versions are 6.0 or higher, then update to the latest radio driver available by checking Windows Update. If there is no update available, check with the manufacturer of your PC using their support site. You can also uncheck the box next to the Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power option in the Device Manager, as listed above.

How do I keep my touchpad from reactivating every time computer restarts?

I set my touchpad to disabled when using a mouse,  but every time I restart my computer, the setting for the touchpad reactivates and I have to go back in and disable.  Is there a way to keep the touchpad disabled UNLESS I want to use it (not often)

a. user say:

Hi Sandi,

If you're comfortable using the mouse over the touch pad and wish to disable it when working on your laptop, then our initial step is to Disable the device through the Device Manger. To do this, you can follow the steps below:

  1. Press the Windows + X key, then select Device Manager.
  2. Locate and click the drop-down arrow for the touch pad adapter.
  3. Right-click on the touch pad driver and select Properties.
  4. Under the Driver tab, select Disable Device.
  5. Restart the device if prompted, and then check if the issue persists.

In the event that you encounter the same concern, our next step is to disable it permanently to prevent it from running upon startup. Typically, the touch pad option is often found in BIOS. For a detailed guide on how to modify/configure BIOS, we suggest that you contact the device manufacturer.

Note: Modifying BIOS/ complementary metal oxide semiconductor (CMOS) settings incorrectly can cause serious problems that may prevent your computer from booting properly. Microsoft cannot guarantee that any problems resulting from the configuring of BIOS/CMOS settings can be solved. Modifications of the settings are at your own risk. 

Let us know should you need further assistance.
