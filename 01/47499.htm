mlg63 say:

You are out of luck retrieving anything on the ext drive. A Recovery drive is supposed to be a boot device--dvd or thumb drive only, not to be used for backup. It is to access the backups and system image on an ext hard drive when the hard drive has failed. It is not clear in the name of the utility and also not in the directions for various new backup methods in Windows 10. Your hard drive is completely wiped out and will have to be formatted and setup as a new backup drive. Many of us--even experienced users-- have fallen into this trap. The warning that all will be lost on the drive goes by very fast and can be easily missed or misunderstood. Since it happened to me I keep several external drives with multiple copies of important files--not backups since no backup program is needed for retrieving copies.

Contacting the drive manufacturer will not help. You can use Disk Management to reformat the ext drive so you can use it again. Reboot the computer and reformat. 

Creating a Recovery Drive should be updated and named Create a Boot Disk and external hard drive should not be in the list of available drives.
