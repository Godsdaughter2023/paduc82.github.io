photonimus say:

That's because if you put something downloaded on a new console, after it was already downloaded on a different one, it requires you to do a license transfer. This is to ensure that people don't simply take their gamertag and put it on a friend's console and download the DLC or other item for them to use for free. It prevents illegal copying.

Once you connect and do a legal transfer, all would be well again.

As for the Arcade games though, most of them do require being connected to play. I am not sure why.

Now, Skyrim's DLC in particular... If you brought your whole console to your friends house and downloaded it with your own gamertag and console, there should not be a problem, so if that's the case, I don't know what's wrong. If you only took your storage device, such as your HD or a USB, then the license reads to your friends console and you wont be able to play offline.
