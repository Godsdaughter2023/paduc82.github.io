davem121 say:

Hi Michael, my answer above was to show the user how to set active hours, in other words the hours the user does not want their system to reboot and spend hours installing the never ending list of Automatic updates . . .

Personally, I have disabled Automatic Updates and when I have some free time I download and install the latest updates from the Microsoft Catalog Page

To disable Automatic Updates:

  1. Click your Start Button, type services.msc and hit Enter
  2. Scroll down the list of services to find 'Windows Update"
  3. Double click that service, stop the service if it is running and set startup to 'Disabled'
  4. Restart your System

Then when you have some down time, you can download the latest cumulative update for your version of Windows 10:

Click your Start Button, type winver and hit enter

Take note of the version of Windows 10 you are running (1511, 1607, 1703 . . . etc.)

Then go to the Microsoft Catalog Page - Click HERE

Type Windows 10 + your version No. in the search box (e.g. Windows 10 1703)

Download the latest cumulative update and security update

When the download is complete, double click the msu file(s) to install the update(s)

Well that is how I manage Windows Updates so they do not interfere with my work !

steven kehoe say:

InstantGo Waking Surface Pro 3 While In Bag Causing Overheating

The InstantGo (formerly Connected Standby) functionality of the Surface Pro 3 is a differentiating feature of the Surface Pro 3.  There are many positives to it, particularly while carrying the Surface Pro 3 around during the workday.  However as many Surface Pro 3 users have noticed, this came at the expense of a total disablement of the hibernation feature common to almost all Windows 8 PCs.  While users on many forums have been discussing this, I found nothing on the Microsoft answers forum so I wanted to bring the discussion here where it belongs.  The common issue is as follows:

I close the lid on the Surface Pro 3 or hit the power button to initiate Sleep/InstantGo mode.

The Surface Pro 3 is placed in a Nixon Sleeve or some other equivalent carrying case.

While on the go either just by general movement or perhaps by the surface pen getting touched, the device will sometime awake out of connected standby. 

Because the device is in the pouch, there is nowhere for the heat to vent and the device gets extremely hot (to be expected)


On a normal mobile device or tablet (i.e. typical android and ios devices) this kind of connected standby feature has been around for quite some time, however you usually have to press the power button to wake it.  The Surface Pro 3 can be woken by the slightest graze of the touch pad, touch of the pen buttons, or touch of the keyboard as well as the power button.  It's simply too sensitive on it's own.

What would be really nice, and in line with the Surface Pro 3 vision of being a full laptop experience as well as a tablet experience, would be to have the hibernate option available in power settings.  I could definitely see my self using InstangGo as my power option for 3 minutes of idle time or for when the lid closes, but I'd love to make it so that hitting the power button caused a full hibernation.  This option should certainly be available for users to configure.  Right now everyone has to use the command line hack 

"shutdown /h" 

via a batch file to get the machine to hibernate.  It would also be nice to be able to configure the amount of time between when the Surface Pro 3 goes into Sleep/InstangGo mode, and when it then transitions into Hibernate mode.  I believe the current speculation is that this is currently hard coded to happen at around 4 hours.

I'm interested in what other community members think on this, as for me the potential for overheating basically makes InstantGo a non-option for the Surface Pro 3 while in the case.  The danger is I'm on the move so much occasionally I hit the power button and forget to do a full shutdown, leaving me always open to the risk of overheating.
