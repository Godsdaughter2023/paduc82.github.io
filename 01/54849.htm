keijo kortelainen say:

Ok folks. It seems I've managed to fix the situation. The machine has been now up and running for 13 hours without a single crash :)  

I was pretty much on the right track when I made a wild guess it might be related to power saving on some device.. 

For starters I did stumble upon this KB Support article for Windows 8: https://support.microsoft.com/es-bo/help/2792463/window-8-may-stop-responding-hang-when-multiple-abort-commands-are-sen . It basically covers a situation I was having, except this was supposedly fixed Windows 8.1 and Win10 should not have it all. But based on various forum writings, out there are still several Win10 users suffering of this).

The most essential phrase in this article is that situation affects only systems with "embedded storage device" (eMMC),. And that it is caused when the eMMC does not respond fast enough.  There seems to be an "experimental" hotfix for Win8 and Server2012, but since Microsoft does not seem to recommend it, I decided to try something else first.  It occurred to me that if the eMMC drive was in "sleep", then the response might be too slow. 

So I headed out to advanced options of Windows Power Plan tool. Under the "Hard Disk" section there is "Turn off hard disks after" option. The defaults are 10 minutes on battery, and 20 minutes when plugged on power.   

So I opted for insanely high values of 99999 for both and rebooted the system.  And it's been crash free ever since :) 

Hopefully this will help others experiencing the same.
