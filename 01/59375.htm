XBox One Full Audio Thru Headsets - Microsoft Community

haonthegreat say:

XBox One Full Audio Thru Headsets

So I've had this idea in the back of my head for a while now.  The XB1 is going to have two hugely underrated features:

1) Wi-Fi Direct (250+ Mbps), capable of directly connecting to any compatible peripheral; and

2) The proprietary sound port on the controller, allegedly capable of high quality full stereo sound. 

Combined, what does this mean?  It means that the potential for direct sound out to headsets has finally arrived.  Think about it: in addition to piping sound out through the HDMI cable or through the optical out, MS could "simply" (as a non-engineer, I use that term very loosely) pipe all sound out through Wi-Fi Direct.  From there, it could go to a wireless headset or to the controller, then through the port up to a headset.  The kicker is whether the console hardware and firmware is set up like this; can you even send sound straight out through WFD without crossing wires or rewriting the base code? 

Now, I poked Support about this theory, and here's what I've got IRL:

                 haonthegreat We don't have specific info to share on that one, unfortunately. ^PC

 

When I told them to poke the Tech/PR guys for me, they told me to post it on the forums, so here I am.

http://xbx.lv/10lopl3  ^PC

 

So, Tech guys, if you could make this work you'd have another selling point and a hugely valuable use for WFD.  PR guys, if its already there, it'd be nice to hear about it.  And community, I'm guessing that if enough noise got made, someone up top would strongly consider it.

haonthegreat say:

@ Aexra  Here's my basic thoughts on the difference, though it'd be nice to have someone from the field corroborate or reject these:

1) Higher quality sound.  250 Mbps trumps Bluetooth by a longshot, and I believe traditional radio wireless headsets (ie, the kind that plug into your TV's optical out) as well.  

2) Full audio straight from the XB1.  My whole premise is based on the fact that all the sound from the XB1 could go out now straight from the XB1, not just voice communications.   This is important because you are piping it straight from the XB1 via WFD (the "direct" part), you remove some of the latency from bouncing the signal from the box to the TV to the base to the headset.  It's also a convenience issue in that you no longer need a base, adapter, or additional wires.  

3) Cost effective.  By piping sound through the controller, you could opt for a cheaper wired headset (relative to a comparable wireless).  I for one never purchased a full audio headset last gen because I found the cost to prohibitive.  I really wish I could have bought a wired one and plugged it straight into the XB360 controller as opposed to into the console.
