Todd, you have to access the BIOS directly from the machine, not from Windows 10 recovery. 

Turn off the computer completely, wait 60 seconds then press the power on button.  Now, during the POST (Power On Self Test) when the machine is first starting up and doing it's memory test, press the F2 key repeatedly until the BIOS screen appears. 

Once the BIOS screen is displayed, you can use your arrow keys and esc key to navigate the BIOS.  Be careful as setting something wrong may cause your machine to not boot.

I'm not sure if you will find the HPET timer here as each manufacturer sets their BIOS up differently as to what to display and what is allowed to be changed.  But from here you can at least see what the settings are.
