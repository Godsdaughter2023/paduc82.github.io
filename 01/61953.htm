Hi,

Thanks for posting your query to Microsoft forum. I will certainly help you with this.

As for the error code 0X8000CCC0F, this can be broken down into 2 causes, namely:

  • Incorrect POP settings (usually the SSL option for SMTP) - check with your provider if it is required or not.
  • Firewall blocking ports (usually port 25) - could be 3rd party since Windows Firewall generally allows this port through, unless specifically told not to do so.
  • This could also be due to incorrect pop-smtp port combination.

Method 1:

Please remove then re-add your account in Windows Live Mail. Please click on this link for instructions:

How do I add an account to Windows Live Mail and make sure to check the "The server requires a secure connection box (SSL)", on both the pop and smtp for your accounts.
