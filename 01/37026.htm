This method didn't work for me, as I said in my reply earlier, but I tried the other alternative method in the discussion thread...


"I used the following solution:

1) I run the following command to know the GUID of my desired power plan.
>> powercfg list

2) Then I used this info to create the following bat file.

ENERGY-PLAN.BAT
REM I introduce a delay of 60 seconds
ping -n 60 127.0.0.1

REM
powercfg -SETACTIVE 368ed7a5-fd1c-4b86-b9f7-26d94355eb01

- I have to put the delay because Windows restores its "default plan" a couple of seconds after the login. So, with this delay I ensure that the command sets my power plan after Windows has restored its default.
- Where "368ed7a5-fd1c-4b86-b9f7-26d94355eb01" is the GUID that I got in step 1.

3) Finally I put this bat file in the startup folder of my user "pbocan". "C:\Users\pbocan\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Windows\Start Menu\Programs\Startup""


I tweaked the delay timing a little to match the timing of my machine's startup actions timing. Now, at almost exactly the time the default "Balanced" power plan scheme is initiated by something in the hardware or somewhere else in the OS I don't know of, my two line powerplan.bat batch file:


"REM I introduce a delay of 20 seconds
ping -n 20 127.0.0.1

REM Use the Power Scheme GUID for your desired custom power scheme
powercfg -SETACTIVE a4ec9cbf-9bba-4a52-b6e6-f58eecff374a"


in the startup folder of my user account, "C:\Users\Dr. Ray E. Osejo\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Windows\Start Menu\Programs\Startup" changes the power plan scheme back to my desired custom Power Scheme!


Problem solved for me!  :-)
