    Bought an Xbox Elite controller and soon enough had stick drift. Sat as a paper weight for a while as I was too nervous to pull it apart. Watched a few vids on the net and here is my result..... Yes I fixed it....

    So the green housing that holds the sensor on the joystick modules needs to have a certain amount of pressure when connected to the metal housing. Replacing the sensor certainly works if there is damage to the sensor, however on mine, the sensor was fine.

    I removed the grey pressure clip as told in the vids, and this worked temporarily, then the drift came back. Trying to reposition the grey clip was in short unsuccessful and resulted in a wobbly grey clip and stick drift...

    Sweet!!! Just thought I had completely ruined the controller and started to contemplate buying the hot iron for micro welding when it clicked...... Perhaps the if grey clip maintains the pressure then the green housing must do the same and need consistent pressure to work with such slight variable electrical resistance (IE joystick movement)??

    Solution....

    Wording - as you hold the controller as normal, and look at the joystick module, front is at the top, with no housings, or pressure clips. Left is the grey clip, right and back are the sensor housings.

    Grab some medum/fine wire (like fuse wire) that can be twisted with hand pressure and can maintain the integrity of the twist. I suppose a zip lock tie would also suffice however I have not needed to try that.

    Loop the wire around twice on the outside of the joystick module, and begin twisting on the front side on the module. As the pressure grows to the correct pressure, begin to move the wire up on down the module to where the pressure is required.

    For me, I had forward drift, so I needed the pressure lower to the board on the right/front side of the module as you look at it.

    Don't be concerned about the protruding plastic parts when the joystick is moved, as the pressure will most likely be needed near the top of the module or at the base from what I can tell.

    Twist, test, snip the wire so you can reassemble.

    Also to note - don't over tension the wire. I noticed that as I tensioned the wire, the ability to press down on the joystick (IE to run in some games) became increasingly hard. Not to worry as I can map a paddle for that if needed, however if using a normal Xbox controller, this will need to be tested as you increase the tension.

    Let me know how you go
