irfan h say:

Hello panther1968,

I suggest that you try to Sync your Memory chip manually with the Media Player.

1. Start the Player, connect your device to your computer, and then click the Sync tab.

2. If more than one device is connected, in the Navigation pane, click the device.

3. Drag files, albums, and/or playlists to the List pane to add them to the list of items to sync.

To select multiple adjacent items, press and hold the SHIFT key while selecting. To select nonadjacent items, press and hold the CTRL key while selecting.

4. To remove items, click the item in the List pane, and then click Remove from List.

5. In the List pane, verify that the list of items to sync contains the items you want and that they all fit on the device.

6. Click Start Sync.

Here is a link that will give you more information about this:

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows-vista/Sync-manually-in-Windows-Media-Player

You can also refer the below link that will give you information on ‘How to setup a device to sync’:

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows-vista/Set-up-a-device-to-sync-in-Windows-Media-Player

You can also try to copy the files from the Windows Explorer to your Memory Chip.

Determine the location where you have the Music files saved on your computer:

1.      Open Media Player right click on the file that you want to transfer and click on ‘Open File Location’

 

2.      You will be taken to the folder where the Music file is saved. Right click on the File and copy, open the Memory chip from the Windows Explorer and paste the files to the location where you want to copy the file on the memory chip.

If you are trying to transfer music from your computer to the Motorola Phone, you can use the Motorola Phone Tools that will have an Utility to transfer music from your computer to the phone.

You can visit the Motorola website to download the Latest PC suite that will give you options to transfer music from your computer to the phone.
