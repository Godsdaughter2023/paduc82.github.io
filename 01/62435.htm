"How to Refresh Windows 8 and Windows 8.1"

  • The PC boots into Windows RE (Recovery Environment).
  • Windows RE scans the hard drive for your data, settings, and apps, and puts them aside (on the same drive).
  • Windows RE installs a fresh copy of Windows.
  • Windows RE restores the data, settings, and apps it has set aside into the newly installed copy of Windows.
  • The PC restarts into the newly installed copy of Windows.

"When you reset your PC, the drive will be completely reset, and all your apps and files will be removed from it. Hi Gleasonton1,

If your PC came with Windows pre-installed then you should either have a set of 'recovery discs' or else a recovery partition on your hard drive. To restore back to factory conditions you will need to check your PC user manual/guide. This will tell you how to proceed. One word of warning, though. Restoring completely wipes your hard drive and you will loose everything. Your recovery system will then reinstall Windows from scratch. Any programs you installed after you purchased your PC will need to be re-installed, so hopefully you have the original CD/DVD's and product keys. It is also a good idea to backup all of your important files to a DVD so that you don't loose them also.
