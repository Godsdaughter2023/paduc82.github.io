¡firedog say:

I have a brand new Windows 10 PC and this thing came with Skype installed ...

So do I, and it didn't have Skype installed. That's beside the point, though; the presence of echo123 in your contacts list indicates that you're signed in in Windows Live Mail with a Microsoft Account. Whether you have Skype installed or not has no bearing on it.

Every Microsoft Account is also a Skype account as well as an email account, so that you have a choice of methods of connecting with your contacts - email, instant messaging or voice call with video if you have the equipment for it. If you are signed in, you are making use of the ability to synchronize your contacts list with the one at people.live.com and thus with any other that uses the same Microsoft Account, such as another computer or a mobile phone. If you want to have this synchronization, you're stuck with the echo123 contact. If you don't want to avail yourself of the facility, just click on the right-most button on the Home tab of the ribbon and select Sign out of Windows Live Mail.

I can't see that the presence of an extra contact is anything to get angry about, or that it makes life so difficult, or that it is any sort of a bug.
