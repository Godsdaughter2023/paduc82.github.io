ken blake say:

See Bill Smithers's reply and let me add/reinforce a couple of points:

1. Yes Vista is no longer supported, but that does not mean you have  no security. You can still use the security software that came with it, and you can still use many of the third-party security programs (some for sale, free) that are available.

2. Upgrading to a more modern version of Windows may not be possible; you may need drivers for that new version and they may not be available for a computer as old as yours. Check with its manufacturer to find out what drivers you would need and can get.

3. Even if you *can* upgrade, you may find that performance is very slow with a modern version of Windows running on such old hardware.

4. If it were me, rather than upgrading, I would probably buy a new computer running Windows 10. It will give you better performance and keep you up to date with support, with better security, and new versions of the programs you use.
