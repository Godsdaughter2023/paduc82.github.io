gts-nj say: Connecting a wireless router to a modem/router is a bit involved.  You normally do not want to chain routers.  There are two approaches to this.

1,  Configure your wireless router to perform as a wireless access point.  (or purchase a WAP)  Some wireless routers have a built in option you can set for this.  If not, it can be configured manually. 
http://www.speedguide.net/articles/how-to-set-a-wireless-router-as-an-access-point-2556

2.  It is also possible to reconfigure the DSL modem to turn off it's routing function and set it to bridge mode.  In that case your wireless router may also need to be configured to log into the DSL connections (if using PPPOE).  You will likely need help from your ISP's support staff to do this.
