arminatorx say:

[quote user="Storm Fz"]... I restarted the router and restored the internet. Every other device worked perfectly fine with no lag or disconnecting, but my when I tried to play Xbox, the game would run smoothly for a minute, then it would suddenly lag out ... I was wondering if this was part of the DDoS attack or if it's something else? And if it is a result of the DDoS, how should I fix it or how long will it take for the connection to be repaired? Or if it is not a part of the DDoS, any ideas what it could be? Thanks![/quote]

First a little bit of background information about a DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service). In your particular case, another player used tools on his PC to find out your IP Address of your internet connection. Think of that as a street address.

If they found out how to contact your internet connection, they will start another tool that will instruct "an army" of infected PCs to send bogus data to your home address. In case of your home internet connection, data from a few hundred infected PCs is enough to "clog up" your internet connection with enough garbage data, that your game data can't be processed anymore.

If you restart your router, it depends on your Internet provider how they handle reconnects. Some providers will assign you the same IP address again, which means that all the garbage data from the DDoS is still being sent to your router, which is then again "clogged up" with bogus data.

After you restarted the router again, it might be that you got a new and different IP address as before, so the DDoS garbage data is now being sent to the wrong (your old) address still, and you should be able to connect normally.

However, depending on the infected PCs being used for the DDoS attack, it might still be, that the amount of garbage DDoS data "clogs up" resources of your Internet Provider in that area, and while you are able to access websites, it won't appear to lag or hang like a real-time multiplayer game.

In either case: DDoS Attacks are illegal in most countries. You should definitely report that offending player with the reporting feature of your console (bring up the offending players gamertag, and then use the report option). Also it might be a good idea to inform your ISP and the police that you have been DDoSed, so they can monitor your connection, and if another DDoS happens, they have logs about the attack and can work with law enforcement to find the person responsible for the attack, and punish him accordingly.

And when you report that attack to the police, it'd be a good idea to NOT mention that you were unable to play your game. Tell them that your whole Internet connection went out, and your parents were unable to do important work stuff, possibly losing money, because a kid DDoSed your internet connection.
