internet expert say:

Hard drives normally make little clicking and whirring noises while they're reading or writing data. If it's an abnormally loud "knocking" noise that recently began occurring, and you're encountering system freezes, data corruption, or other errors, then I'd suggest scheduling a repair.

If your internal hard drive is still booting and running fine, I might suggest you get an external hard drive (that's at least 256GB and USB 3.0) and move all of your games and media there. It will at least decrease further wear on the internal drive.

a. user say:

Hi,

Thank you for posting your query in Microsoft Community. Appreciate your interest in using Windows 10.

I would like to inform that Windows 10 will have DirectX version 11 installed in it which will meet the requirements of DirectX version 9. So, the applications which require DirectX 9 should open with the version of DirectX 11. To check the version of DirectX installed in the system, follow the steps below:

  1. Press Windows key + R, type dxdiag and press Enter.
  2. In the System Tab under System Information, the version of DirectX will be displayed.

carlo fer say:

Hi Satya,

Based on the error message, it is possible that the issue is caused by a printing job stuck in the printer queue. We suggest that you clear and restart the Print Spooler manually. Please follow the steps below:

  1. Click the Start button> type services > then press Enter.
  2. Look for the Print Spooler > Right-click on it and select Properties from the context menu.
  3. Under the General tab, click the Stop button.
  4. Open File Explorer > navigate to C:\Windows\System32\spool > delete the contents of the folder.
  5. Return to the Print Spooler properties window > click the Start button to restart the service.

After performing the above steps, try to install the printer drivers.

Update us with results for further assistance.
