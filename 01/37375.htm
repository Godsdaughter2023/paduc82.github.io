ron sommer mvp 2009-2017 say:
These directions give you a way to use the Notes field to sort contacts.

If you don't sign in, there's no way unless you include category affiliation in a contact data field, like Notes or some other unused field. I give my categories names starting with numbers, and put the number at the beginning of the Notes field for each member. This has several advantages:
  • They are neatly arranged in the left-hand pane on the contacts window
  • They appear at the top of contact picker lists (that you see when you start typing in an address box in a message compose window, or click the To... button)
  • Sorting the contacts list by Notes groups all members of categories and sub-categories together. This makes it easy to re-assign categories in the restore scenario you describe.
Here's an example of some category names:
3 Church
31 Parish Council
311 Clergy
312 Churchwardens
32 Employees
321 Vergers
322 Cleaning staff
323 Gardeners
33 Choir
331 Organists
332 Sopranos
333 Altos
334 Tenors
335 Basses
336 Choristers
...
If I put the one, two or three-digit code into the Notes field, sorting by Notes will show contacts in their groups as they appear in the left-hand pane. If I type 3 into the To... box of a message compose window, I will see all those categories listed first, followed by all of their members in alphabetical order. If I type another 3, I'm left with only the choir categories and their members. Type another 3 and only the Altos sub-category and its members are left.

To restore from a CSV backup, simply sort by Notes, select the (contiguous) members of a category, right-click and select Copy contact to > category.

Or sign in.
