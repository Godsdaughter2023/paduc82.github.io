considerate_guy say:

A few things:

1) In order to fade in/out a music clip, you need to choose the music clip, not the video clip.  You'll know you did it correctly if you are on the Music Tools tab (not the Video Tools tab).

2) A fade in goes at the beginning of a clip and a fade out goes at the end of a clip.  If you want to have it in the middle of a clip, you need to first split the Music at the desired point.  You can use the Split icon or just press 'M' on the keyboard.  The result will be that the song gets split into two.  Now either apply the fade out to the first part, or the fade in to the second part (or do both).

 

While I'm here, let me mention one other feature of Movie Maker 2012 that might do this for you automatically.  It is called "audio ducking" which was added in Movie Maker 2012.  In that version, you can go to the Project tab and choose Emphasize video.  That will automatically lower the volume of the music when there is sound coming from the video.

webwizard65 say:

If you're still interested, try looking here: http://oocities.com/sms043/ups-2000.html

 

or try with this pinout (PC Side):

1 (DCD) - Ground signal from UPS for Battery Low (initiates PC shutdown or hybernate)

8 (CTS) - Ground signal from UPS for Power Fail (generates a Power Alert on the PC)

4 (DTR) - Positive signal from PC - shuts down the UPS (if UPS accepts such command)

Pin 8 (CTS) and 1 (DCD) need to be tied with a 1k8 pull up resistor to pin 7 (RTS). Pin 5 is Ground
