a|p say:

FWIW: Microsoft may have already fixed this problem, I don't know.  I recently had the same problem but my env is a laptop (dell xps15, win8.1) & microsoft arc touch surface edition mouse (all purchased July 2014).

Problem: When I bring up the "arc touch mouse surface edition" settings app (to try to disable the "clicking" noise during scrolling) it says "no device" found & does not recognise the mouse.  The mouse however works just fine.

Solution (for my env): I was logged in w/ my local admin a/c.  I deinstalled the settings app, went to MS Store app, search "arch touch surface edition", the settings app shows up, click install.  It prompted me to switch to my Microsoft a/c - which I did - it then installed the settings app.  The app came up normally & I could disable the "clicking" sound (just move the slider to "off" & no more clicking sound).  I then switched back to my local admin a/c, verified that the settings app still came up fine & that my mouse worked fine & was as quite as a mouse when scrolling !

[IMO - the key for me was that the settings app had to be  installed w/ my microsoft a/c & not w/ my local admin a/c.  I had previously tried to install the app w/ my local admin a/c & that did not work & showed the same above problem]

Love the mouse & the settings app - just wish there was a built-in switch that would auto-power the mouse off (I don't always remember to flatten it & turn it off) after some time of inactivity, to conserve battery power; even better would be to allow me to set the auto-power off time due to inactivity in the settings app.
