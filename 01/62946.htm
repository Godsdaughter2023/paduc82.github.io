a. user say:

Send the bill to yourself. It only takes a few minutes to lookup your laptop on Acer's website and see that Acer isn't supporting it on Windows 10.

Here's the annoying thing about being 'not supported': It doesn't necessarily mean that your laptop (or any unsupported hardware) will immediately stop working as soon as you install Windows 10. If only that were true. That way, you would find out instantly that the hardware isn't supported, and save yourself a lot of trouble.

But often, what happens is that the hardware works fine when you first install Windows 10. But then Windows 10 gets updated. And because Acer isn't providing support, it isn't sending out updates to your laptop to keep up with the changes in Windows. Time goes by ... Windows continues to be updated ... and finally an update modifies Windows in a way that your laptop as is can no longer accommodate. Then, wham! All of a sudden your laptop is toast. And you tell yourself - and this community forum - that the update broke your laptop. But that's not what happened.

Microsoft can't do anything about your laptop. In the first place, Microsoft doesn't have the proprietary knowledge of your laptop to know what to do. And even if Microsoft somehow acquired that proprietary knowledge (industrial espionage?) that's Acer's intellectual property that Microsoft has no legal right to alter.

And of course, nobody said anything about this before you upgraded (or were forced into upgrading) your laptop. Because that doesn't make for a very attractive commercial, does it? Maybe technology companies should be required, like drug companies, to include possible side effects in their advertising.
