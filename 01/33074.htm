ben millard say: Thinking laterally, you could move the taskbar to run across the top of your screen. Then your favourite applications are the closest to the Start button. (Specifically,  'pinned' applications are immediately below the Start button, with your 'recent' applications just below that, ordered with the most frequently used at the top.)

This also means your Start button is nearer to the menus and toolbars which you are probably clicking a lot during your normal Windows use.

To move the taskbar around:
  1. Right-click an empty part of the Taskbar.
  2. You can right-click on the time if the rest of your Taskbar is full.
  3. Click "Lock the taskbar" if it is ticked. If it is not ticked, skip this step.
  4. Hold down the left mouse button on an empty part of the taskbar.
  5. (The clock won't work for that step. You can use the small gap between it and the volume control, though.)
  6. Move the mouse from the bottom of the screen all the way to the top.
  7. You might have to move it towards the centre at the top, top stop the Taskbar sticking to the right of the screen.
  8. Release the left mouse button.
You can now lock the taskbar in place, to avoid accidentally moving it:
  1. Right-click an empty part of the Taskbar.
  2. Click "Lock the taskbar" so it is now ticked.
This is the setup I use - maybe we are both fans of Fitts's Law?

a. user say:

Hello,

The Windows 7 product key is a 25-character code that's used to activate Windows and where you find it depends on how you got your copy of Windows: 

  • From an authorized retailer. The product key is found on a label or card inside the box that Windows came in.
  • A new PC running Windows. The product key is pre-installed on your PC, included with the packaging the PC came in, or included on the Certificate of Authenticity (COA) attached to the PC.
  • A digital copy from a Microsoft website. The product key is in the confirmation email you received after purchasing itor it may be distributed as a digital license if you bought Windows 10, in which case it doesn't include a product key. Learn more about activation in Windows 10.
  • Free upgrade to Windows 10. If you upgraded to Windows 10 for free from Windows 7, then you should have a digital license instead of a product key.

Please visit this helpful article by Andre Da Costa on how to purchase a Windows 7 product key for additional information. For more info about digital licenses in Windows 10, see Activation in Windows 10.

Hope this helps and let us know if you have any questions.
