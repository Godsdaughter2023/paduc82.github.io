lemp say:

Most laptops can switch off various peripheral devices, including webcams and BlueTooth, to save power.  Most often, this is accomplished by pressing a combination of the Fn key with one of the numbered F keys.  Try to interpret the pictograms on the F keys (or, you know, read your User Manual*).

With respect to BlueTooth, the same key combination often is used to turn on/off the "normal" wifi radio, BlueTooth, and cellular broadband (if that option exists).  They may all turn on/off at the same time, or it may "rotate" -- press once to turn off BlueTooth, press again to turn off wifi, press again to turn both back on, etc. -- or an on-screen menu may pop up from which you can select which devices are on or off.

You can look in Device Manager (Start > Device Manager > Enter) to see if there's a driver issue, but if the devices are powered off either by a key combination or an actual physical switch, they won't appear in Device Manager.  BlueTooth should be near the top and webcam should be in the "Imaging Devices" category but also look for Bluetooth under "Network adapters."  Don't forget to look in  "Other devices" and in "Universal Serial Bus Controllers" for devices with warning icons.

__________________________________

*  The Asus "Notebook PC Manual" (http://dlcdnet.asus.com/pub/ASUS/nb/S500CA/E_eManual_S550CA_S550CM_VER7814.pdf), which may be for your model, says Fn+F2 turns airplane mode on/off (this should control both wifi and BlueTooth at the same time).  The webcam appears to be enabled through the Asus "LifeFrame" app, which is activated using the Fn+V keys (the V key should have a pictogram of a camera on it).
