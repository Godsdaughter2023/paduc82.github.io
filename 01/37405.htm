Issue With Avatar related Game Saves [Solution] - Microsoft Community

bettiepagegames say:

Issue With Avatar related Game Saves [Solution]

An avatar award is something that you earn in a game that you can apply to your avatar to wear outside of that game and that others can see. For example, a hat or shirt or outfit of some kind. They are not to be confused with achievements or items that can only be worn within a certain game. Famestar awards are similar and can be worn only within certain Famestar games like Wreckateer, Full House Poker & World of Keflings. You will find all of these in the Awards section of the Avatar Editor.

If you are receiving error messages similar to these....

"The progress, settings,or achievement's for [gamertag] can't be saved at this time." or "Can't save to gamer profile. [Game] is unable to save your avatar award at this time."

Then this is what you need to do....

First, check and see if you are able to update your avatar awards.

     •On your console, sign in using your Xbox LIVE gamertag.
     •Go to Social, and select your gamertag.
     •Select Customize Avatar.
     •Select Awards & follow the on-screen instructions to update your avatar items.
     Note: If you are getting the error message(s) above you will not be able to update your awards.

Next, go to your account online at https://live.xbox.com/en-US/Account?xr=mebarnav and log in. Select Edit Avatar and on that page choose My Style. In the left pane you will see the selections of items to outfit your avatar. Move the left arrow all the way over in each category until you reach the first item on each row. If you see a little orange or red present, then those are your awards. Now we just have to get your console to recognize them again.

Next, on your Xbox console go into Settings>System>Storage and select Avatar Items. In that folder you should see all the items that you have earned and they should match up with what you see in your account online. If you are seeing less of them on your console, that is part of the problem why you are getting those error messages.

To get your console to receognize them again you will need to delete those files in that folder. Be aware that other profiles share that same folder so when you delete them you will be deleteing them from all profiles on your console. Don't worry they will still be able to be re-downloaded.

Use the A button and select each file, then select delete.

Now we are going to put them back, but first you are going to clear your console's cache three consecutive times. To do this go here http://support.xbox.com/en-US/xbox-360/settings-and-initial-setup/clear-system-cache and follow the instructions and repeat three times.

Re-boot your console by powering it off, wait 30 seconds and turn it back on.

Before you sign-in re-download your gamer profile. On the main dasboard screen scroll over to the right and select Download Profile. You will be prompted to enter your Windows Live ID & Password. Remember to select Remember Password so that you don't have to sign in everytime you log in to Xbox Live.

Now we will update your awards and get your console to recognize them again.
     •Go to Social, and select your gamertag.
     •Select Customize Avatar.
     •Select Awards & follow the on-screen instructions to update your avatar items.

You should then have a screen showing the transfer of your awards to your avatar awards folder. Once they are finished transferring you can select each one to see if it needs updating. If needed, follow the on-screen instructions to update your avatar items. Not all awards will need updating. Repeat this step for the other profiles on your console.

You are done!


Now you should be able to play the game(s) that you were receiving an error message for without issue.

Happy Gaming!

ron sommer mvp 2009-2017 say:
WLMail can be used to compose a signature.

1. Open your WLMail program.
2. Create a new Email message composing using Rich text (HTML).
3. Enter the preferred information that you want to see as your signature.
4. Set the font setting by selecting the color, font size, and the format.
5. Save the document as an HTML file, F12.

After saving, select the file as a signature by referring to the steps below:

1. Open Windows Live Mail.
2. Click Alt, F, O, M.
3. Click the "Signature" tab.
4. Click New.
5. Click Rename and name the signature.
6. Click the File radio button and click Browse.
7. Select the HTML file as your signature.

8. Checkmark the Auto-insert signature if you want.
9. Click Apply.

The game plays a bit like "Broken Sword".  It's a bit of a point and click adventure with some combat thrown in to mix things up a bit.  You walk about the area and sparkles indicate things you can interact with.  Sometimes there will be conflict and when that happens you have fights.

The combat is very basic but suits the show well enough.  In these fights as you perform better you build up a double team power with Jake as well as powering up your weapon.  You can activate the double team power for massive damage and you can activate the magic properties of your weapon as well.  At the end of the battle you proclaim that you did amazing... Jake says "look at all the money we made" (because apparently this is how you make money) but there is nothing to really give you any idea how well or poorly you performed.

The point and click nature of the game works pretty well until you have to do a certain something to progress.  It's not too hard to figure out... but sometimes it's pretty hard to figure out.

This figuring out can sap the flow of gameplay as you attempt to try different combinations can really sap the flow of the gameplay as you bang your head against the wall a little.

The game plays very simply.  With almost a child like sense of simplicity.  Also the game is like Broken Sword or the orginal Resident Evil with sort of a fixed or limited camera view.  You have the freedom to move about but you are not in an openworld with a 360 degree view.

Early impressions are this is a simply, playfully silly "Adventure Time" game which captures the spirit of the show perfectly... but hinders itself by being too simple in places to be a game adults who enjoy the humour of the show to enjoy... while being filled with some tasks and requirements that put it beyond the grasp of of a young kids.

As an "Adventure Time" product it's spot on.

As a video game... it's alright but i wouldn't call it 'great'.
