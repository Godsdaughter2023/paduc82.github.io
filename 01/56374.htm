Is (are) your device(s) one or two independent devices?  Many modems have built in routers.  You cannot have TWO routers in a local area network system.  If the router in your modem device is not disabled, you cannot use a separate router.

Modems are usually provided by your internet provider and they should be the ones to ask about any updating.  "updating" modems in not a commonplace thing.

Step 1:

  Check whether you are able to connect to the Internet.  If not, you need to configure your Internet Connection Sharing.

  If your a using one modem to switch between your Xbox console and computer then do the following:

Step 3: To ping your console from your PC:
 
   1. If it's off, turn on the Windows Media Center PC.
   2. Click Start, Run, then type CMD and press Enter.
   3. At the command prompt, type PING and the IP address your wrote down (for example, PING 192.168.1.9), then press Enter.
   4. If you see a series of replies, then your PC "sees" your console. If you see a series of "Request timed out" responses, then it can't find your console.
 
Again, make sure that both your PC and your console are connected to the same network.

Your PC may be blocking your console from connecting by using a firewall to filter traffic by blocking ports. You'll need to open certain ports in order to connect to your PC.
