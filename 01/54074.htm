cmdrkeene say:

There's several ways to do this, depending on what picture program and what email program you like to use.  I prefer using the desktop so that it works just like Windows 7 and earlier.

On that note, Because computer manufacturers bundle extra apps, many users are unaware that after Windows XP, Windows Vista was the last OS to come with a full email program. Windows 7 and 8 do not come with any successors to Windows Mail. Instead, you can get it as an optional download from the Windows Essentials page. Windows 8 did later come with a bare-bones basic email app that you’ve probably discovered, but it’s designed for tablets/phones and light finger-friendly use. If you want something that compares to the Mail program and Outlook Express before it, you’re going to want to get the free Essentials download at that link.

For now, I'll assume you're just using Windows 8.1 "as it comes", and you haven't decided to use any other picture or email programs.  In that case, here's how to do it:

  1. Open the picture you want to send
  2. Swipe in from the right (touch screen) or point to the right corner (mouse), and choose "Share" and then "Email someone"
  3. A new email message will appear with the photo already attached. Just name who you want to send it to, and click Send.
