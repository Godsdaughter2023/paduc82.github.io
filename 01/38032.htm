considerate_guy say:

There have been two different approaches on what to do when you change the speed of your video.

One approach is to change the speed of the playback and not worry about the pitch. This results in low deep voices when you slow the video down and high chipmunk sounds when you speed it up.

The other approach is to apply algorithms to the audio to try to maintain the same pitch. This will try to keep the same pitch but just at a slower or faster rate.

In Windows XP, Movie Maker 2.1 used the first approach - changes pitch

In Windows Vista, Movie Maker 6.0 used the second approach - maintains pitch

In Windows 7/8/10, Movie Maker 2012 again uses the first approach - changes pitch

I'm not sure why the change back to the first approach, but that is just how it works. There are no settings to switch. About all you could do is to perhaps edit the video clip in Windows Movie Maker 6.0 first with the desired speed-up/slow-down. Then you can do further editing in the latest Movie Maker 2012.

But if you are going to use just Movie Maker 2012, there isn't anything you can do. The pitch changes as the video is slowed down or sped up.
