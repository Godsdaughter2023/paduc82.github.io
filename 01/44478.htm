diana d say: Hi Rockymom092259,

You can copy the files from one user account to another and delete the one that is not required. You must have at least three user accounts on the computer to complete these steps, including the new account you just created.

1. Log on as a user other than the new user you just created or the user that you want to copy files from.
2. Open Documents by clicking the Start button , and then clicking Documents.
3. Click the Tools menu, and then click Folder Options.
If you don't see the Tools menu, press ALT.
4. Click the View tab, click Show hidden files and folders, clear the Hide protected operating system files check box, and then click OK.
5. Locate the C:\Users\Old_Username folder, where C is the drive that Windows is installed, and Old_Username is the name of the profile you want to copy files from.
6.Select all of the files and folders in this folder, except the following files:
•Ntuser.dat
•Ntuser.dat.log
•Ntuser.ini
7. Click the Edit menu, and then click Copy.
If you don't see the Edit menu, press ALT.
8. Locate the C:\Users\New_Username folder, where C is the drive that Windows is installed, and New_Username is the name of the new user profile you created.
9. Click the Edit menu, and then click Paste. If you don't see the Edit menu, press ALT.
10.Log off, and then log back on as the new user.

If you have e-mail messages in an e-mail program, such as Windows Mail, you must import your e-mail messages and addresses to the new user profile before you delete the old profile. If everything is working properly, you can delete the old profile.

kieran_rosevear say:

First what make and model is your router/modem and is it up to date with it's firmware and also check to see if your local area is experiencing network connectivity issues.

Things for you to try;

Okay can I have you to first reset your router to factory settings by holding the little button on the back of the router, you may require a pin to press the button. And hold it down for 15 seconds and then release and wait 3-4 minutes for your connection to come back online.

Next open your ports also called port forwarding I will provide a link below telling you more about this, open a new tab on your browser and browse to http://192.168.0.1 where 000.000.0.0 is your default gateway. your default gateway is usually labeled on the bottom of your router/modem along with the default username and password to access its settings. Once in the router settings you will have to check with your device manufacturer on instructions on where to open your ports on your device as other modems have there own different way on doing this. Ohh and you must have a static IP address set up or else port forwarding will not work. Once you have done that restart your router for the changes to take effect. and also restart the console so you will get the new static IP assigned to the console.

When opening your ports it is recommended that you disable UPnP as with port forwarding enabled UPnP is useless.

Test to see if all is working if not look for if your router supports it which most do a DMZ (Demilitarized Zone) this is basically if you don't think you have opened your ports properly this will open ALL the ports allowing all the connections through to the IP you have set in DMZ there are risks when doing this so please take that into account first.

The rule of thumb to go by is when you are port forwarding you disable UPnP leaving both on will cause problems, if your using DMZ remove the port forwarding and also disable UPnP as DMZ is basically doing both of them and leaving them open.

Check to see if your problem exists if it still does move on to the next troubleshooting step.

Going back to the router settings look for a MTU Size setting most will have this setting on 1500 on Ethernet and 1492 for PPPoE you can check for the optimum size simply by doing a ping test but usually 1492 or 1500 left along will be fine.

If these do not work for you i suggest you to give you manufacturer a call and have them tinker around with your router to further access the problem.

Information on port forwarding and the ports used:

http://support.xbox.com/en-GB/xbox-360/networking/network-ports-used-xbox-live
