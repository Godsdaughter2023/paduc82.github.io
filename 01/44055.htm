Can't uninstall drivers in Windows 10 - Microsoft Community

abanathieb say:

Can't uninstall drivers in Windows 10

After installing Windows 10, I was having problems with my Wi-Fi adapter.  It would lose the ability to see networks after a few minutes of operation.  I attempted to uninstall the drivers and software with the intent of reinstalling the drivers afterward as part of the troubleshooting process.  However, uninstalling hangs during the initial screen and will remain hung for at least six hours (overnight).  Out of desperation, I did attempt to install drivers over the old driver.  This also hangs the system.

How do I get rid of these old drivers?  Is anyone else having issues with Wi-Fi adapters with Windows 10?

abanathieb say:

All right, it's probably bad form to answer one's own question, but I did come up with a solution. 

First, I had to disable the device, the Wi-Fi adapter in this case.  Then, I had to disconnect it from the system and shut down the computer.  There is a chance that the existing driver, even though it is disabled, will interfere with the shut down process.  It did for me; I had to manually, press the off switch, shut down the computer.  After shutting down, I restarted and uninstalled the drivers.  In my case, I reinstalled the drivers, and my adapter now works without any issues.

From what I know, the driver is causing issues.  During an uninstall, the driver is still somehow active and hanging the uninstall program.  Disable, disconnect and shutdown eliminates the driver and keeps it from loading into memory on a restart.  Without the "bad" driver in memory, Windows can manipulate the files, whether it is an uninstall or reinstall of files.
