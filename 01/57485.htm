If you use a splitter, you will get the same thing on both monitors. Unless that's what you want to happen, using a splitter is far and away the wrong thing to do.

Most people with two monitors don't want that. Most people with two monitors want some applications on one monitor and other applications on the other (that's what I do with my two monitors).  If you do that, in effect having two monitors is equivalent to having one bigger monitor.

So assuming that you want to do what I do, a splitter should be avoided.
