If you keep the automatic sync option, you can either choose which files to sync or you can have the entire Player Library copied to the device.

Every time you connect your device to your PC while Windows Media Player is running, the Player automatically updates the contents of the device to mirror the contents of the Player Library.

For example, if you rip a CD to your Player Library, that album will automatically be copied to your device the next time you connect it to your PC. Likewise, if you delete items from your Player Library, those items will also be deleted from your device the next time you connect it to your PC.

Even if your device is set up to sync automatically, you can still choose which items are automatically synced.

Information about manual syncing:

If the storage capacity of your device is less than 4 gigabytes (GB), or if your entire Player Library can't fit on the device, Windows Media Player will manually sync. You'll need to follow a few steps every time you want to add or remove items from the device.

mike hall... always say:

In my experience, clean installing an operating system should result in a problem free computer, and if it doesn't, then there is a hardware issue somewhere.

 

Starting with memory..

Regardless of the quoted minimum spec for Windows 7, 2gb is low end in reality, and as Gerry has suggested, you would benefit from more.

 

I see from HP specs that your power supply is only 300w. According to the CoolerMaster PS calculator, your machine is should have no less than 275w. While the HP power supply is within range, HP should know that a computer needs at least 50w as a buffer, and preferebaly more.

This could be an issue for you if the computer is running a little warm.

 

Not sure if HP have hardware diags within the recovery partition, but if they do, press F11 directly after powering up the unit to access them.

 

Run Memtest86+ anyway.. http://www.memtest.org/

 

While Windows has more than its fair share of capacity to create errors, generally they can be fixed using tried and tested means, al of which are well documented. Some of the errors you have are not well documented as Gerry has already made you aware.

Faulty hardware can cause Windows to produce exactly the same errors and more, but the well documented software fixes don't work because the problems are being generated from outside of Windows..   

 

I note that the model was released in August of 2011 which gives you time to return the computer under warranty and get it checked out and fxed without putting you out of pocket.
