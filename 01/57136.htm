2. I CANNOT perform a clean install of winXP Pro (SP3 integrated) because the winxp setup recognizes the 500GB HD as 131 GB HD. Therefore the remaining capacity is not recognized.

4. Strangely enough, if I use Knoppix, Debian, Ubuntu, or Mint linux boot CD and boot the laptop with any one of those linux distros, then the linux installer recognizes the HD correctly during hardware recognition phase, and later during HD partitioning phase, the linux system reports full 500 GB capacity available for partitioning. Additionally, when there are pre-existing partitions, then the linux partitioner lists those partitions correctly as well!

Considering the 4 points and especially MS WinXP PRO (integrated with SP3, which should recognize HD with sizes larger than 137 GB limit --since MS says you need at least XP SP1 to overcome 137 GB size limit-- and that the various linux distros can see the full 500 GB HD size without any problems, what exactly is the problem with Win XP? Why doesn't it recognize the full capacity? And how come win xp recognizes 500 GB capacity if the new HD is attached to the laptop via USB as an external disk when running XP PRO (SP3) from the internal 80 GB HD?

Sooo.... Can anyone shed a light to the issue with Win XP Pro installation on 500 GB IDE HD?

Kindly,

LO

lozon say:

Hi Palcouk and Doc,

I believe this is the final answers!

Although, there are a mix of simple to complex answers to the problem at hand, what it all boils down is that there's a significant difference between WinXP  installer and Linux installer. XP installer needs help from BIOS to deal with the HD, and if the BIOS doesn't support it, XP has no facility to deal with the situation. OTOH, most of the new Linux kernels rely on the BIOS to a limited extended, but then directly communicate with the hardware, in this instance, with the HD itself, and therefore manages the hardware accordingly.

It seems among the dual-boot users (win/linux) who use old PCs, this is a pretty well-know issue. What they end up doing is using the first 137 GB that is seen by the BIOS without problems for XP (either as a single partition or divided into several partitions), and the rest of the space is used for Linux, since linux has no problems.

I guess this really nails down the problem with my laptop to a NON-48-bit-lba BIOS!

1 - I uninstalled the printer from the device manager

2 - I then uninstalled the drivers and mf toolbox from the control panel programs area (anything that says Canon on it)

3 - Unplug the printer from the computer

4 - Restart the computer

5 - Download the latest DRIVERS from Canon (not MF Toolbox yet) and install those first.

6 - Download and install MF Toolbox....voila....I'm printing again

Of course, your mileage may vary as all computers are different.
