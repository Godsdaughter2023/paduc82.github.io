aileen alf say:

Hi Raymond,

If the BIOS does not detect the external hard drive, it's possible that the drive is not enabled or turned off in the BIOS. To enable the drive in the BIOS, you will have to get in touch with the manufacturer for assistance. The issue can also be caused by faulty or unplugged data cable. Inspect the motherboard and hard disk connections for bent or misaligned pins. Folding or creasing data cables can cause the wires to break inside the insulation. Try to use another data cable and check if the issue will persist.
