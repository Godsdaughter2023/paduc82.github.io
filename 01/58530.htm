david m. connell say:

Do you use Onedrive? Onedrive can take up a lot of space on your local storage if you let it save local copies.

One way you might reclaim some space is to get a microSD card and move your stuff to it. The basic steps: Buy a card and reformat it as "NTFS". Create folders for "Documents", "Music", "Video", "Downloads" "Onedrive". Then, in Windows Explorer go to Libraries, look for the "My Documents" My Music etc folders and right-click on them. Click Properties and go to the Location tab. Click move, and in the window that pops up, go to the SD card and select the folder you just made. Windows will as if you want to move the contents of the original folder to the new location. Click yes. You can do this for the main folder of each library, and for Onedrive. This should get you some space back, and slow down your C drive refilling, since your stuff will be saved to the SD card.

If you want to try this and need more detail feel free to ask.

Another way to reclaim space is to Disk Cleanup. Go into Explorer and right click on your C drive. Click Properties. On the window that pops up you'll see a pie chart representing your C drive space usage. Next to it is the Disk Cleanup button. It will scan your C drive and come up with a selection of unneded items you can delete to reclaim space. It should be safe to select everything. Click OK and Windows will delete those files, hopefully getting a good amount of your space back.

Method 1

  • Write Troubleshooting in the search box and hit enter.
  • Click on Troubleshooting tab.
  • Click on view all option on the upper left corner.
  • Select the Windows Update option from the list.
  • Click Next to run theTroubleshooter.
