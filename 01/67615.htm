    considerate_guy say:

    The ability to burn a video DVD is accomplished by having Windows DVD Maker on your computer. Microsoft included this program in Windows Vista and Windows 7, but dropped support for it in later versions of Windows. It's similar to how Apple dropped iDVD from their iLife suite in 2011.

    If you are using Movie Maker on those versions and Windows DVD Maker is installed, you'll see the "Burn to DVD" option in the menus, otherwise you will not. You appear to be in this second group.

    The video DVD format is a legacy format from the era of analog TVs and cannot handle HD video. It is limited to 480p (NTSC). It also uses an older licensed MPEG-2 codec. For all these reasons, I think Microsoft decided it wasn't worth supporting.

    You have a few options:

    1. If you still want a video DVD, then you can look into 3rd party options like DVD Flick, DVD Styler and others. Those are free or you can look for paid solutions from Roxio, Nero, etc.
    2. If you are looking toward the future, you can burn the .mp4 from Movie Maker directly onto a DVD disc (as a data file) and play it back in a Blu-ray player. This format is an HD format and is understood by most standalone Blu-ray players.
    3. If you just want to project the movie, you can just directly play the video on your laptop connected to an LCD projector.
    4. You can share the movie on a video sharing site like Vimeo, YouTube, etc.
    5. For use in the home, you can copy the file onto a USB stick and play it back on many Smart TVs. Or if your TV is network connected you might be able to use DLNA streaming from your home PC.

    pircy23 say: So I know nothing about your computer..and I don't know if you are still having problems.  I had the same problem, my computer would dim every time I would go to a darker image.  I was getting so frustrated with no solutions anywhere.  

    Well anyways, I found the answer for me was to to into the Intel Graphics and Media Settings. Under Power there is a setting for on battery.  That was set to Display Power Saving Technology.  I just unclicked that and now my computer doesn't dim anymore. 

    It's crazy all the places on Windows that controls a power and brightness setting.  I found this after days and days of searching. 

    lordfate say: Your Intel graphics chip is probably the culprit, a setting called "Adaptive Contrast Enhancement"

    1. Search for and launch "Intel Graphics and Media Control Panel"
    2. Click Advanced and click OK
    3. Media -> Image Enhancement
    4. Uncheck "Adaptive Contrast Enhancement" and "Film Mode Detection" then click apply

    That should fix your problem.

    If your problem is with the brightness on your laptop changing randomly when "Adaptive brightness" is disabled, here's the solution: 

    1. Launch "Power Options"
    2. Cover your light sensor (small lens somewhere near the keyboard)
    3. Wait for your screen to dim
    4. Drag the "Screen brightness" slider all the way to the right while still covering the sensor
    5. Uncover the sensor

    That seems to reset your brightness to max regardless of if the sensor is covered.
