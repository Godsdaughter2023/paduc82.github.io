bossdweeb say:

That's not screen tearing. Screen is caused by running a game at frame rates that are higher than the monitors refresh rate.

eg - a game running at 120 fps on a monitor with a 60Hz refresh rate may tear. The tear shows as a line across the screen

(usually not as distinct as in the image below, or as far offset), or in vertical objects in the middle part of the screen when it's only mild.

When there is tearing, using V-sync in the games settings should (but not always) help the problem.

- Your images are showing  artefacts, and there are several causes, and different types of artefacts, but because you don't have these

artefacts when running with the Intel HD620 graphics you can eliminate an issue with the monitor.

- We should also rule out corrupted graphics drivers, so if you have not already, you may want to download and reinstall the

most suitable driver for your machine, which is the Nvidia drivers provided by Lenovo for your model laptop.

pcsupport.lenovo.com/us/en/products/laptops-and-netbooks/300-series/320-15ikb/downloads

- Because the artefacts are also showing when you are not gaming your machine may not be running too hot, but you should

monitor the temps under load to verify it's running at safe temps.

GPUz has sensors to monitor functions on your Nvidia gpu, and also has a temp sensor for the cpu.

GPU-Z Video card GPU Information Utility

- If the temps are high your machine may be clogged with dust, and cleaning the machine may help.

- If the machine is not running too hot your Nvidia graphics processor is likely faulting.

In order to delete a homegroup you have to leave it first on all machines but the one that created the homegroup.
Keep the machine running where the homegroup resides and shut down the rest of the machines.
Go to C:\windows\ServicesProfiles\LocalService\AppData\Roaming\PeerNetworking
Look for the files that are named idstore and delete them all.
If you can't delete them cause they are locked then right click your start button and go to Run and type services.msc.
When that opens go to the Homegroup services and stop and disable them and then try and delete the idstore files again.
Once done reboot your machine and then you will see that the homegroup is gone.

ken blake say:

Press the PrtScrn key.

Back in the days of DOS, the PrtScn key used to print the screen. But in all versions of Windows, this works differently, and the name of the key is now an anachronism. The key does *not* print the screen. PrtScrn captures the whole screen, and Alt-PrtScrn captures the active window.

Either one captures the image to the Windows clipboard. Once it's in the clipboard you can paste (Ctrl-V) it into any application that supports graphics (Windows Paint, other graphics programs, even your favorite word processor). You can edit or add to the image as you wish, then print it.

This ability to manipulate the image in a program before printing it is an improvement over the original DOS method of just printing it. But if you'd like that old facility back, there are several third-party freeware/shareware programs that can do this, such as PrintKey2000 at http://www.sharewarejunkies.com/00zwd2/printkey2000.htm
