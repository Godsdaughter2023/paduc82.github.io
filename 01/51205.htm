How does refreshing your PC work for Windows 10? - Microsoft Community

matrixlocked say:

How does refreshing your PC work for Windows 10?

Will my files and programs be saved or will I lose them?  How long does refreshing take?

a. user say:

A refresh and a reset are both clean installs. In both, your hard drive is erased and Windows 10 is reinstalled. In a reset, that's where it ends.

In a refresh, your computer's user accounts are backed up to the cloud (a Microsoft server). After Windows 10 has been reinstalled, your user accounts are restored, which means that when your computer restarts, all the same user accounts will be waiting for you. All that extra work means that a refresh takes longer than a reset. You should allow about an hour for a refresh.

There are certain caveats about a refresh:

If you store your personal data anywhere outside your user accounts, like the Public folders or the Guest account, or anywhere else outside user accounts, that data is going to be lost.

With the exception of apps that you got through the Windows Store (whether free or paid), all your software programs will be gone. You're going to have to reinstall them on your own.

  • Went into both PCs and went through "device manager", "programs and features", and "turn windows features on and off". Just removing things all willy nilly. Including deleting all my PC's profiles, from previous installations, out of the other's "device manager" section (I think they were all hidden and stacked up in "software devices").
  • Restarted both PCs.
  • Went into my PC and went to "c:\windows\Service Profiles\Local Services\APPDATA\Roaming\peernetworking" and deleted "idstore.sst.net"
  • Went into services and stopped "Homegroup Provider". Then restarted it.
  • Went into Windows Explorer and could create a homegroup. So did so, setting all files to not share.
  • Joined the other PC to the homegroup. Not sharing any files on it. Then restarted it.
  • Let it fully get to desktop. Then restarted my PC the same way.
  • Shared select folders on both computers with the homegroup.
  • Rinsed and repeated the full restarts in different orders.
  • Finally ended with both PCs fully shutting down and restarting.
