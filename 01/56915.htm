Hi basilorjwan,

I would like to clarify some information to better assist you with this concern. Allow me to ask the following:

  • Are you trying to put Speech Recognition to sleep or wake it up from sleep mode?

Basically, Speech Recognition goes to sleep mode when not in use. You can wake it up by using the Start listening voice command. If you use the Stop listening voice command, it will turn the Speech Recognition off. You'll have to click the microphone button or use the CTRL + Windows key shortcut to turn it back on.

For more information about commands in Speech Recognition, please check out Windows Speech Recognition commands.

In this case, you may need to re-train your computer to better understand you so it can respond accurately to your commands. To do this, follow these steps:

  1. Type in Control Panel in the Cortana search, and select it from the search results. 
  2. Click Ease of Access > Speech Recognition.
  3. Select Train your computer to better understand you and follow the on-screen instructions.

Once done, test again your speech recognition and see if it responds accurately.
