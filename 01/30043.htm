This was a scam, and a common scam at that.

The next time you come across such a web page, simply open Task Manager [  CTRL+SHIFT+ESC  ] and 'End Task' on your web browser. Your web browser will close and you'll lose everything you were looking at or working on, but at least you won't be scammed or worse ... attacked with malware.

As soon as you can, change all your passwords, not only for your computer but for all websites where you have to sign in.

Pay careful attention to your banking, credit card and investment accounts for signs someone is trying to remove money. In general, be wary to the possibility that your identity may be stolen.

Hopefully, no damage was done. But don't let your guard down, not now and not in the future.

pkazza say: I'm pretty sure your problem is related to the remote computer not having a password. The sequence of events is that Remote Desktop makes the connection, the remote user signs out, the remote user sign in again because no password is required, the remote desktop session is closed. A password on the remote system will prevent the user logging in again. Given this the actual message makes some sense.
