bigbenx88 say:

Power issues can be a little tricky to fix.  Usually the fix itself is always simple, but we need to find out where the actual issue stems from.  Is the system itself overheating/shorting out and turning off?  Is the power supply unit itself overheating/shorting out?  Is it a bad outlet you have it plugged into?  You have to take all these things into consideration when looking at the problem.

First thing I would ask is where do you have it plugged in?  A power strip, a surge protector, directly into the wall?  The first two options there are No-No's and we would need to fix that if that's the case by trying to plug it directly into the wall.  If the latter is the case then we need to try another outlet.  Either way you go I would reset the PSU before doing anything.  Unplug the PSU from the outlet and from the Xbox, plug into a wall outlet (preferably one with as little plugged into it as possible), and then plug it into the system.  Now when you plug it into the system, one of three things is going to happen.  Either the amber light is going to go out, which indicates a bad PSU and needs to be replaced.  The light will flash several times over a 5-10 second period between white and amber, which again means a bad PSU.  Or the light will turn white for 5-10 secs and then turn back to amber, which is good.  Try turning the system back on at this point and see if you have anymore issues.  If you do, then try the same thing in another outlet.  If it does it again, replace the console.

Let us know how it goes.
