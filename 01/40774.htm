freddrick pal say:

Hi Richard,

 

 

To configure the Sleep settings on your computer, just follow the steps below:

 

1. Press Windows+C.

2. Once the Charms bar is up, go to Settings.

3. Click on Change PC settings.

4. Select PC and devices.

5. Click on Power and Sleep and you will see options on how you would like your computer to go to Sleep mode.

 

There is also a possibility that your computer’s capability to go to Sleep mode has been removed when you reverted your computer back to default settings. To check if your computer still has this option, see the steps below:

 

1. Press Windows+R.

2. Type cmd and press Enter.

3. Type powercfg /a and press Enter.

 

After performing these steps, it should list down the sleep states or options, as well as their descriptions. If you don’t see the Sleep state options, it only means that it’s neither supported nor available as an option from your computer. If this is the case, it would be best to consult your computer’s manufacturer to know which steps to take to get this setting back.
