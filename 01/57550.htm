There are several approaches to converting a WordPerfect file to Word:
 
1. Open the .wpd file in WordPerfect, then use "Save as" to save it as
a .doc file. Then open the .doc file in Word to see what it looks
like.
 
2. Open the .wpd file in Word to have Word convert it, and see what it
looks like.
 
3. Open the .wpd file in WordPerfect, then use "Save as" to save it as
an .rtf file. Then open the .rtf file in Word to see what it looks
like.
 
4. Open the .wpd file in WordPerfect, then use "Save as" to save it as
a text file. Then open the text file in Word to see what it looks
like.
 
Which of these did you try? Depending on how much formatting your
document has, you may find that one of the last two choices works very
well for you. And they are both almost guaranteed to work without a
problem.

canadian tech say:

Yes. 

My suspicion is that the thermal paste between the CPU and the heat synch was not applied correctly or has dried out.  CPUs produce a huge amount of heat.  That is why that heat synch is there with the fan on top.  I can tell you how to fix this.  It is not actually very difficult.  First, open the covers and check to see the fan on the case and the one over the CPU is working OK and clean.

  1. Buy a small tube of thermal paste at the computer store.  Should  not be more than about $10
  2. Unplug the power cord.  Hold the on button in for at least 7 seconds. Remove the cover of the PC
  3. Using a long Phillips screw driver slowly loosen the 4 screws on the corners holding down the heat synch to the main board.  They are spring loaded.  Loosen the screws a few turns at a time on each corner and keep rotating till you remove them or at least you can tell they are loose. 
  4. Remove the small plug from the fan to the main board -- note how and where it fits
  5. Pick up the heat synch/fan assembly.  Examine the surfaces between the bottom of the synch and the top of the CPU. 
  6. Using rubbing alcohol very, very sparingly and a small soft cloth patiently remove the paste that is on both surfaces.  Make sure the surfaces are now clean and dry.
  7. Place small dots of the paste on the 4 corners (not at the edge) of the underside of the heat synch.
  8. Press the heat synch in place (do not attach yet), pull it back off.  You should see an even coating of paste on both surfaces.  NO paste over flow.  If you see blank spots, apply more paste there.
  9. Test again
  10. Re-attach the heat synch and plug.

Power up, boot up.
