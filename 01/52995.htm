Windows 10 is bothering to ask you every single day if you finally want to try the new Skype version. Once you are finally annoyed enough to accept the challenge, the new Skype will be installed in a different directory. This is because the "new Skype version" is installed as SkypeRT and seems to account in strange ways as an WindowsApp and will be installed in C:\Users\username\AppData\Roaming\Skype\SkypeRT. After the sucessful instaltion Skype is opening automatically an account using your Windows 10 account-email. Of course, as this is a new account, there are no contacts or chat history.

Now, your "old" Skype installation is still existent.

There are two ways to get your Skype back:

  1. Klick on your Desktop shortcut and the old Skype will be back, minimize it and pin it then to your taskbar.
  2. Go to the installation folder on C:\Program Files (x86)\Skype\Phone\Skype.exe and double klick on it. You can proceed as before said or create a new desktop shortcut.

How to get rid of the new SkypeApp?

  1. Don't try that, just live with it. I have not tried it and my concerns are that by uninstalling the App our real Skype is getting punished somehow by the uninstallation routine.
  2. If you want to take the risk, go to: Settings>>Apps>>Apps&Features. Scroll down to Skype, where you will find 2 Skype installations. Currrent desktop version is 7.40.0.104 (by 11/11/2017) and you want to keep this version. The other listed Skype installtion must be the "bad one."
