tcp422 say:

How do I set my time zone to Lord Howe Island?

Lord Howe Island (373 miles north-east of Sydney) and has its own time zone, UTC+10:30 standard time, and UTC+11:00 when on daylight saving time. 

See http://www.timeanddate.com/time/australia/lord-howe-island.html

In Windows 7, there is no matching time zone in the drop down. Nor is there a match in the Registry (HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows NT\CurrentVersion\Time Zones). Nor is it listed in .NET/Powershell ([TimeZoneInfo]::GetSystemTimeZones()). 

Is there a hotfix or a setting somewhere to enable this to show up?

Hi,

 

I would suggest you to reset the icon cache database and then if that does not help then rebuild icon cache.

To Reset Icon cache follow these steps:

a. Right click on empty area of the desktop and click on Personalize.
b. Click on the Display Properties link.
c. Under Colors, select Medium (16-bit) and click on Apply. (See screenshot below)
NOTE: If you already have Medium (16-bit) selected, then select Highest (32-bit) instead. Sometimes you will need to do a restart first, then proceed to step d. Try both ways.
            i) Click yes to confirm the new settings.

            i) Click on Yes to confirm the new settings.

a. Open Folder Optionsin Windows Explorer and under View tab, check Show hidden files and folders.
b. Open a Windows Explorer window.
c. Go to C:\Users\(User Name)\AppData\Local.
d. Right click on IconCache.db and click on Delete.
e. Click on Yes to confirm the deletion.
NOTE: This deletes the file to the Recycle Bin. It is safe to empty the Recycle Bin when finished.
f. Close the window.
g. Empty the Recycle Bin.
h. Restart the computer.
i. When you go back, you will notice the Size of the IconCache.db file is smaller, and the Date Modified is now the current date.

NOTE: If for some reason IconCache.db is not there or the size has not changed much,then restart the computer again. You may need to restart a couple of times in some cases.

gerald vit say:

Hi Damon_203,

It is normal to have lower CPU Core Frequency when your computer is idle for it is not working as hard as when it is active. This can help your computer save energy since less resources are used during this period. If you do not wish Windows to throttle your CPU when idle, you can either set your Power Options to Performance or change the minimum processor state from the default 5% to your desired setting in Advance power settings. To do this, please follow the steps below:

  1. Press the Windows and R keys simultaneously.
  2. Type-in powercfg.cpl and press Enter.
  3. From here you choose your desired power plan.
  4. You can change power plan's settings by clicking Change plan settings.
  5. Click on Change advanced power settings.
  6. Look for Processor power management and click on the plus sign (+) to expand the list.
  7. You can set the Minimum and Maximum processor states to 100%.
  8. Click OK.
