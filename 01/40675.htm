"i used drivereasy, driver reviver and pc health advisor to update all the drivers and clean registry..."

All a bad idea -
Always do driver update manually. Uninstall via Programs and Features and
install the drivers you download from the mfg's web site. In your case ATI/AMD
for graphics drivers and the motherboard mfg for chipset drivers.

Registry 'cleaners'  and driver locating software are  largely hocus pocus  and not  necessary.
There may be occasions when a registry scan can be handy for finding entries for a specific software, but doing a general  clean is not really beneficial. The concept of keeping the registry entries limited goes back to the early 'slow' cpu's.  Registry entries, contrary to the adds on the web pages that sell the software, rarely become corrupt. Usually reinstalling the related software is the best fix when they do.
If you are going to have a registry cleaner at least disable it in msconfig \ Starup.
