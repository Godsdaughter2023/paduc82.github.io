Try deleting the root folders responsible for the apps.

  • Now navigate to the location C > Program files.

Note: To access the WindowsApps folder you need to take ownership of the folder.

  • Right click on WindowsApps folder.

  • Click on Properties at the bottom of the context menu.

  • Click the name of the person you want to give ownership to.

  • If you want that person to be the owner of files and subfolders in this folder, select the Replace owner on sub-containersand objects check box.

  • Click Apply and then click OK.

After taking ownership of the WindowsApps folder open it, and then look for the folder of the apps you want to remove i.e. Netflix, Candy Crush Friends Saga and delete them.

For the location of installed apps like Microsoft Edge, Mail & Calendar and other apps you need to navigate to the below location.

  1. Go to Settings, alternatively you can press Windows key + I key together.

  2. Click on Apps > Apps and features.

  3. Search for the apps you want to see.
