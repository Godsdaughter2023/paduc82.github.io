srimadhwa b say:

Hi,

 

Dongle is a hardware device attached to a computer without which a particular software program or a particular hardware device will not run.

It is also called as a wireless adapter. It is a device that can be plugged into a USB port to enable wireless access from a computer to an external Wi-Fi device, as a mobile phone, or to the Internet, via high-speed broadband, or to enable wireless connectivity in a printer or other peripherals like a keyboard or mouse.

 

A wireless keyboard allows its user to operate free from the restraints and inconveniences of wires, as well as minimizing the clutter caused by an excessive amount of loose wires.

1. Insert the wireless keyboard drivers CD or DVD into your disk drive and follow all the instructions stated in order to install the drivers. This will allow your computer to recognize the usb transceiver and keyboard as they are plugged in.

2. Purchase batteries for the keyboard. Check the accompanying manual for whether AAA or AA batteries are needed.

3. Find the battery cover in your wireless keyboard, open it and install the batteries. Make sure the plus (+) sign of the battery matches the plus sign in the battery socket. Likewise for the minus (-) sign of the battery.

4. Turn off your computer.

5. Connect the wireless transceiver into an available PS2 or USB port. The PS2 keyboard port is characterized by a round socket with slots for pins, while the USB keyboard port is characterized by a flat, square socket. Observe the plug of your wireless transceiver to see which category it belongs to, or consult the manual.

6. Turn on your computer.

Note: Remember to refer to the keyboard manual for the maximum range of your wireless keyboard from its transceiver. The standard range is somewhere between 1 meter to 1.5 meters, but varying models have varying specifications. Check the manual for the ideal range wherein your keyboard can be used to optimum effect. You will also find the details regarding the Fn switch in the manual.
