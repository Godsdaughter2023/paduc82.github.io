mick of the dart say:

John

Thanks for your response.  Since I posted my query I've been advised of 2 further solutions which may help someone else in the same pickle.  I used Solution 2. and it worked but I had to go back a couple of backups to find everything.

Solution 1.

If your old hard drive is still available, you can remove it and fit it into a hard Drive "enclosure" or "case" which you can find on Amazon or Maplins.  Then connect by USB to your new PC and copy your old files  over - treating it as an external hard drive. 

They are usually called a 3.5 inch USB HDD enclosure. You will just need to find out if your hard drive is IDE or SATA, so that you buy the right type.
IDE = lots of pins in two rows
SATA = 2x plastic L shaped sockets.

Solution 2.

It may be possible to get to the files in your backup with the following program...

http://www.7-zip.org/
- Select the 'Download msi 64-bit x64 1 MB' option.
- It should prompt you to save the install file automatically after a few seconds, or you can click 'direct link' if nothing happens.
  
- Run the 7zip install file and follow the instructions.
- Once it's installed, access your DVD so you can see the files on it.
- I'm not sure exactly what you'll have, but hopefully it should be something like a load of zip files within 'backup' folder(s).
- Select the zip files, one or more at a time, and right click so that a menu pops up.
- Then select '7Zip' and 'extract files' from the pop up menus.
- A window should pop up allowing you to choose where you extract the files to (click '...' to browse your pc).
- Then click 'ok' to start extracting the files.
- A progress bar should pop up to show you what it's doing/how long it will take.

If it won't let you extract files direct from the DVD, you may have to copy the contents of the DVD onto your new PC first, and then try from there.  That's what I found.
