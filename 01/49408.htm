Recover System Image to a Surface Pro 3

Screen damaged. Totally out of order.

Microsoft replaced it by a charge.

The process worked like a charm.

I now understand the role of insurance.

With new machine in hand, restoring the system image would be a piece of cake, right ?

No, it wasn't that easy as I imagined.

Some tricks for you, that may be in the same situations as mine:

What I have done ?

1. Before sending my damaged machine to microsoft, I managed to save a system image to my NAS.

2. After receiving my new machine

  • Created a USB Repair Disk with a normal USB Key;
  • Transferred the system image to USB Hard Drive; I tried to recover straight from my NAS, but it did not work.
  • Connected ONLY the USB Recovery disk; Leave the USB Hard Drive disconnected. 
  • Restarted the new machine holding the left shift key (It tells the machine to start from the USB Repair disk)
  • It will bring you to the Recovery screen.
    • Choose Troubleshoot
    • Attach your USB Hard drive (Critical step)
    • Detach your USB Repair disk (Critical step)
    • Choose System Image Recovery
    • It will find the image on your USB Hard Drive
    • Click to continue
  • It may take you 1 hour to see your system image back

Hope it helps you

mark ole say:

Hi Dave,

It's always good to keep copies of your files on another drive in case something happens to your computer's hard drive. Good thing, Windows 10 has made it easy for users to create a system image. To create a system image backup, follow these steps:

  1. Open Control Panel and go to File History.
  2. At the bottom of the left pane, look for the link to System Image Backup, under "See also."
  3. The System Image Backup utility will open. Select the device where you want to save your system image backup and click Next. Confirm your settings and click Start backup.

In case you'll need use your system image to restore your PC in the future, open the Windows 10 Settings menu and go to Update & recovery. Under Recovery, go to the Advanced startup option, and click Restart now. When your PC restarts, go to Troubleshoot, Advanced Options, and then choose System image recovery.
