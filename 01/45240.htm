1) As long as you don't have some form of Whole Disk Encryption installed on your computer and someone has physical access to your computer, it is almost trivial to reset any password on your computer.  I've done it myself on many occasions when coworkers forget passwords at work or when we dig out an old computer that hasn't been used in years and I'm hardly the expert that many here are.  The rules of this [Microsoft] forum do not permit more details but it's not too hard to Google it out.

3) I cannot even guess at what Microsoft will or will not do.  I don't believe anyone in this forum can speak for Microsoft.

If you want to protect your disk content in event of theft, you would need to use an encryption product.  Windows 7 Pro has "Bitlocker", commercially there is PGP Whole Disk Encryption among others, or you can go with a freeware product such as Truecrypt.  For me, at home I have created a Truecrypt container that I use for all my personal, financial, and private data and I leave the rest of the hard drive unencrypted.  Should my computer be stolen, I would be comfortable that my personal information is safe.
