viktor_s say:

Hi,

Sorry to hear about your problems.  Must admit I went through the spell of rather "interesting replacements" myself in our corporate devices pool, however the latest bunch of devices seem to be OK. 

My observations when MS Surfaces get "re-certified" is that they go through a rather quick health check such as - physical damage, basic operation (turn on/off)  and then put a new image.  I prey that I am wrong!

In your case.  Industry "recommendations" through major computer manufactures is 3 strike rule, i.e. if the same issue is not resolved through 3 repairs consistently, then you should be reimbursed either financially for the full sum or via replacement with a brand NEW device.

Surface Pro are obviously sealed units, so no repairs as such can be done (only at vendor level).  Whether this can be classed as repair is another matter.

What I would recommend is to be put through to the complaints / escalation department when you report technical issues with your device (don't worry if it's out of warranty) and politely explain your issue, that you received a number of replacement products, but with each replacement you discover various other issues.  At this rate you only used the original device for less than 6 months, as for the others you were inadvertently been used as field tester.

Good luck!
