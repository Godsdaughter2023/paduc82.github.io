The mistake of the new Skype for Desktop: it should not have - Microsoft Community

bornival say:

The mistake of the new Skype for Desktop: it should not have copied the cell phone model...

Hi,

I guess the big mistake made by Microsoft is to consider that Skype for Desktop should be the same as the one for cell phones...

Cell phones have a small screen and require the application to fill it entirely for the text to be legible (or the buttons to be easily acted upon).  Also, the settings for the cell phone version can be less than for the desktop version because cell phones are often just for conversations and/or light work. 

Conversely, desktops (and most laptops) have rather larger screens and the user does not wish to have all this area eaten up by Skype (as it is now).  Also, the Desktop version is (most often) operated by a mouse and a keyboard, which means that the buttons do not need to be as big and the text will remain legible even with a small font.  The Desktop version is also often used for more intense and mission-critical work, which means that many settings are important and must allow fine and even granular configuration of the program.  This is where the old Skype (version 7) was good at, as it could be configured in many respects.  Thus is largely lost with the new version 8...

Lastly, the settings that could be accessed from the top of the window (by clicking  "Skype", "contacts",  "calls", "View", "Tools" and "Help") have now disappeared.  While again, this could be logical for a cell phone version (where space is limited) and with no or little headrow, it becomes a nuisance for the desktop version as (i) it moves all those settings (if available) to sometimes bizarre, unexpected and often non-intuitive places; (ii) it goes away from the general layout of many (if not most) other programs (if every program would hide its key settings in different places, it would create a large loss of time and a lot of frustration to users).  

Thus, a Desktop program is for Desktops, not for cell phones.  Its layout, window size, and number of configuration settings should be distinct from those of cell phone apps...

Actually, reverting to the "old" Skype is fairly easy.  I did it and here is how you may do it for your-self:

1. Go to https://www.skype.com/en/get-skype/

2. There, hover your mouse over the big blue button "Get Skype for Windows" BUT DO NOT click on the text but rather on the little vertical down arrow ( like a v )...

3. This will open a menu where you see, in the last position, "Get classic Skype".  Click on that...

4. It will let you to download a 56 MB file  "SkypeSetupFull.exe", which is the last incarnation of the older version...  Save it in a folder that you name "Skype-classic" (to make sure you do not confuse it with the new version... which would be named Skype-8.12.0.14.exe)...

5. Make sure you exit completely from the new (hated) version 8 of Skype ...

5. Double click the "SkypeSetupFull.exe"... and let the installation proceed (while it does, make sure to uncheck the options you do not want such as making Bing your search engine and so on....). It will overwrite the version 8 (which is why version 8 must be have been fully closed)

6. At the end of the installation process, you will have the full version 7.40.0.104 with all its nice and useful setting options...  and all your contacts (including your groups !).  One option you may like to set is to disallow automatic updates (Tools > Options > Advanced > Automatic updates > Turn off automatic updates  [you need to have administrative rights]).  But beware,

(i) this may not prevent Skype to suggest to upgrade... When this occurs, you need to click "No" and it may (I have not checked) prevent further invitations for upgrading to appear... (!! this may put your Skype in a "do not disturb" status; if such is the case, turn it back to "on line")...

(i) this will keep you out of potentially important and useful security updates... So, we will need to see how long we can keep this version...  Hopefully, the criticisms about the new version will trigger Microsoft to get back to the old and trustful layout and setting options...  (Coca-Cola did it after the disastrous launch of the so-called "new [but hated] Coke"...).
