It’s been our pleasure assisting you. We appreciate you giving us an update. While waiting for the replacement battery, you can try to reseat the AAAA battery in the pen. Please be careful opening and closing the pen as doing so incorrectly could lead to damaging the pen. After you reseat or replace the battery, kindly pair the pen with Surface again. Make sure that your Surface Pro 4 is up-to-date. The Surface and Windows both release updates may help resolve the issue. For more information about Windows updates, see Windows Update: FAQ. You can also visit Surface Pro 4 update history to check the recent update.

To open the pen correctly, follow the below steps:

  1. Using two hands, hold the pen horizontally, with the flat side towards you. The pen cap should be in your right hand, and the pen tip side should be in your left hand.
  2. Gently, twist the pen cap towards you. The pen will turn slightly, and stop twisting in less than a quarter turn. Do not force the pen cap to twist past this point.
  3. Gently pull the pen cap away from the pen.
  4. Slowly pull the pen cap out until the white label or battery pin is clear of the pen cap.

To reseat or replace the Pen’s AAAA battery, follow the below steps:

  1. Holding the pen in your left hand, tip the pen towards the ground slowly to allow the AAAA battery to slide out of the pen. Only a very slight angle is required for the battery to come out.
  2. Check the pen shaft for damage or debris. Please note that if there is damage or debris, continue to troubleshoot your customer’s symptom, but note this in your case notes.
  3. Place the same (reseat) or a new (replace) battery back in the pen shaft. The AAAA battery should be placed flat side (-) down, towards the pen tip.

To close the pen, follow the below steps:

  1. Hold the pen tip side of the pen in your left hand horizontally, with the flat edge facing you. Hold the pen cap side of the pen in your right hand with the flat side of the pen facing you, and the pen clip away from you.
  2. Carefully insert the white label on the pen cap into the pen shaft. Please note that some pens may not have a white label.
  3. As you do so, align the silver/metal pin of the pen cap to the bottom corner of the flat side of the pen shaft. This should mirror where the pen stopped twisting when you first opened the pen. Note not to push the pen cap into the pen shaft unless the metal pin is aligned to the corner edge of the flat side.
  4. With the metal pin on the pen cap and the corner of the flat side of the pen shaft aligned, push the pen cap and pen shaft together.
  5. Twist the pen away from you slightly so that the flat edges align. You will hear a click when the pen is closed. Please note not to force the pen closed beyond this point or force the pen to close.

If the issue remains the same, once you’ve got the replacement battery, kindly replace it, then follow the steps on how to replace the battery mentioned above.
