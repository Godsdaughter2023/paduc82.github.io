burrwalnut say:

It may be due to the way you are ‘installing it on the screen’ (as you said) that is the problem.

 

1. Open a Run window (Windows Logo key+R), type Shell:Common Desktop and press Enter. This will display the items that are common to all users.

 

2. Change the command to Shell:Desktop and you will see a list that is relative to you. Let your wife log on and run Shell:Desktop to see her desktop icons.

 

You can now decide which shortcuts to make common (by updating the Common folder in [1] above) and make the user unique items in (2) above. Don’t delete or move anything that is not a shortcut – there are other ways to update these, e.g. Recycle Bin.
