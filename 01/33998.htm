It appears that you changed the active partition on your disk, thus preventing it from booting. By selecting the Boot Repair process while in Repair Mode, you can fix this problem. A less intrusive method would consist of using a partition manager (e.g. Acronis DiskDirector 11) to mark your boot partition as active. If you follow this path, make sure that the partition manager is certified for Windows 7. Most aren't.

I doubt that you will be able to boot from your hibernation file. Doing so involves an initial normal boot, followed by a changeover to the hibernation file which is triggered by some unknown flag. On the other hand I do not think that this will be necessary. When encrypting files with TrueCrypt then the usual process to is to copy them to the TrueCrypt volume, then to delete the originals. If your TrueCrypt volume is incomplete or inaccessible then you still have your original files. If not then (and I hate to say it) it's time to review your backup policy.
