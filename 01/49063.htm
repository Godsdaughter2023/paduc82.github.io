Does the number of programs installed affect performance? The answer is: No. The number of programs running affects a computer's performance.

Does the number of open processes affect a computer's performance? The answer is: No. What matters is what those processes are doing, if anything. You could have 100 open processes that are just sitting around doing nothing until they are called upon to do something. Or you could have 1 processes that is using a disproportionate amount of CPU capacity and RAM.

On the other hand, if you have so many programs installed that your hard drive is completely filled, your computer's performance will definitely suffer. Windows needs unoccupied disk space to do its work. But in that case, you would know long before you have a problem because Windows will tell you about it.

If you have questions about Android Studio you need to ask its developers.

For any software (including Windows) the minimum requirements are what you need to load the program and get it running. Its performance may be lousy, but at least it works. If you want the program to work with acceptable performance you should adhere to the recommended requirements. 8 GB are recommended for your program and you have 4.
