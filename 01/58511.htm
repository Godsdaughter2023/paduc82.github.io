a. user say:

Forever? Ain't gonna happen. Still, there's much that you can do.

Switch to an email provider with a built-in spam filter, like Outlook.com or GMail. All the major email providers have one.

If you're using an installed email program, like Office Outlook or Thunderbird, most have a junk mail filter built in. You just have to activate it. You can also install a third-party spam filter.

Any email service or application will let you create filters, to block email from sources you deem to be spammers.

Never open a spam email, even if you desperately want to tell the sender to f-off. Opening an email sometimes sends a signal back to the sender, which tells them that your address is valid, and that only brings more junk mail.

rohn007 say:
As well, make a habit of clicking on the "Spam" button when ever you identify any. That way the automated spam filter in your email system will learn your specific spam.

Routing your email through more than one service exposes it to more automated spam filters. ie, you give people your gmail address, but you setup Outlook.com to automatically pick up the email. You just have to remember to log in and check the intermediate spam folders periodically so you don't lose accidentally filtered content.
