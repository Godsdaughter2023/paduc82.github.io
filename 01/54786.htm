Microsoft's policy with respect to OEM licensing for Windows hasn't changed: the license is tied to the computer on which Windows was first installed. Generally, Microsoft considers a new motherboard to be a new computer and thus won't permit the transfer of the OEM license. However, there has always been the following exception to that:

If your replacement motherboard is identical to the original motherboard (or the manufacturer's replacement for the original mobo), then you <should> be able to re-authenticate. You may have to talk to a live human being. See https://www.groovypost.com/howto/activate-windo...

arminatorx say:

When calibrating the Microphone on the Kinect Sensor, make sure that you turn up the sound "really" loud. After the test you can lower the volume again, but the louder you turn up the speakers, the better the result of the calibration. 

If you do the calibration at a normal room level volume, Kinect can't properly detect the size of the room, as it also takes account of the audio reflections from walls and such. 

So blast the volume for that test, and it should work.

Ideally, imagine having an argument with a stubborn person that's covering their ears going "Lalalalalalala", and having to shout to get through. 

Set your TV or speakers to a similar volume to get a good test result.
