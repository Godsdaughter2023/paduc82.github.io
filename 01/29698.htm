If the System Restore doesn't work, do a Startup Repair by booting to the genuine Windows Vista Installation Disk (or one you can borrow from ANYONE) or from a Recovery Disk.  Here's the procedure: http://www.bleepingcomputer.com/tutorials/tutorial148.html.  To boot to the CD you may need to change the BIOS to make the CD-drive first in the boot sequence.  To do that, wait for the screen that tells you the F key to push to access the boot menu or boot setup.  Push it quickly. There are two types of wireless mouse (mice?):  Bluetooth and wifi.

If you have a wifi wireless mouse, it will have come with a small "dongle" that you need to plug into an available USB port.  After you've done that, turn on the wireless mouse and it will install automatically.

If you have a Bluetooth wireless mouse, you need to ensure (1) that your computer has Bluetooth support and (2) that Bluetooth is turned on in your computer.  The procedure from there depends on the particular product that you have.  Follow the manufacturer's directions.
