You can change the time limit set for your account by following the steps below. If you want to remove the time limit set to your account, you’ve to login with an admin account which is password protected and you can turn off the parental controls.

1.    To password protect your Administrative accounts, go to Start, Control Panel and then select Users, Accounts and Family.

2.    From there, click on the option to Change Windows Password and fill in the appropriate password in the field provided.

3.    In order to change a time restriction on a user account, the procedure is actually quite simple.

4.    Go to Start, Control Panel, Users and Family.

5.    Under the heading of Parental Control, click on the link labeled “Set up PC for any user.”

6.    Click OK on the Windows pop up warning

You should now be at the Parental Controls window, which has a number of settings. The first one is an option to turn on Parental Controls which is already on for your user account. Choose "On to enforce current settings ” and to enable the other settings.

After you do this, go down toward the bottom of the window and choose Time Limits from the list

This will take you to a screen with a table that has days down the left side and 24 hours across the top. This table, in case you didn’t guess, represents a week and all the cells within the table represent the hours within that week.

In order to change time limits for a user, all you have to do is figure out what the hours should be that you would like to allow access to the PC and select the hours that correspond on the table. In order to highlight the cells in the table, take your mouse pointer and hover over the squares you want. Then click and drag the mouse pointer over the cells you want. This will turn the cells from white to blue, indicating that the hour is restricted and off limits to this user.

You can also check the box Allow if you want to access the system without any sessions getting expired.
