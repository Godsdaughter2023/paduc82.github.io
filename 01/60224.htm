Preparatory Step: Download the Norton Removal Tool and the McAfee Consumer Products Removal Tool (since you don't know which, if any, free-trial came preinstalled on the computer) and save them to your desktop (or the desktop of another computer), then transfer the two executables (i.e., Norton_Removal_Tool.exe; MCPR.exe) to a brand-new, never-been-used flash drive.

1. Back up your personal data (e.g., My Documents; IE Favorites; any Mail Client data) to the same, brand-new, never-been-used flash drive.

2. Format the HDD & do a clean install of Windows.

3. Uninstall any Norton or McAfee free-trial & reboot.

4. Transfer ONLY the appropriate removal tool from the flash drive to the desktop of the computer then right-click on the executable & select Run as Administrator to run the utility. Don't TOUCH your computer until the run has completed, then reboot again.

5. After making certain that the Windows Firewall is enabled, you can now connect the computer to the internet.

6. Download/install/update Microsoft Security Essentials (preferably) & reboot.

7. Run a manual check for Windows Updates; follow all prompts.

How can I tell if my computer is up to date?
http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows-vista/How-can-I-tell-if-my-computer-is-up-to-date

8. After you've gotten the computer fully-patched (or at least Service Pack 1 installed), download/install KB971029 manually: http://support.microsoft.com/kb/971029 

9. Now it should be safe to insert any flash drive or SDCard.

10. With the flash drive holding your backed-up data inserted into a USB port, have Microsoft Security Essentials run a FULL SCAN of the flash drive. If all is well, you can then transfer your back-up to the appropriate location(s) on your computer.
