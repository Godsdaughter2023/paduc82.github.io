Check the Print Spooler service:
1.  Go to Control Panel, Administrative Tools, Services.
2.  Scroll down to "Print Spooler" service, the Status should be "Started" and Startup type should be  "Automatic". If not click on the "Restart the service" on the left pane.
3.  If the service won't start as you are getting error 1068 (dependencies). Double click on the Print Spooler service to open its Properties.
4.  Click on the Dependencies tab under "This service depends on the following system components: Remote Procedure Call (RPC) and then expand RPC (click on the + sign) you'll see DCOM Server Process Launcher (you may have to close and open the services console if it does not appear). Those two are also services, check in the list if their Status is "Started", start them as necessary

The 3 services should have Status of "Started" and Startup Type of "Automatic".

If the above would not help you may want to do a System Restore to the time the printer is working.
