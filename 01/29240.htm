burrwalnut say:

This little program will allow you to analyse the index.dat files, showing the activity of users, e.g. who looked at which website and when. It doesn’t specifically show when the logon took place. Download the free version of Index.Dat Analyzer, here http://www.systenance.com/indexdat.php 

 

To run it, click the drop down box alongside Location and then select a specific index.dat file to interrogate. Now select a relevant heading, e.g. click ‘Accessed’ to sort the entries by date/time. Also, there is a filter to narrow down your search.

 

To include all index.dat files, click the green plus sign and click the Tab ‘Search for index.dat files’. Click Select Folder, click on the C:\ drive, click OK and now click Search then Add. These now appear in the drop-down box alongside Location.

 

Embarrassing website visits can be deleted by putting a tick in the box alongside them and clicking the red X above.

I think there may be some confusion here. It is true that the Xbox One cannot currently output 1080i over its HDMI output, only 720p or 1080p. However, this does NOT necessarily mean that the Xbox One cannot accept a 1080i signal from the cable box connected to its HDMI input then downscale/upscale that to 720p/1080p (depending on your Xbox One's settings) for output to the TV. It appears to me that it can and does--at least with my DCH-3416, because it is set to 1080i and the 1080i light is illuminated on its front display whether watching through (A) my Xbox One to AVR to TV or (B) directly to my AVR to TV. In my setup, both A and B result in an upscaled 1080p signal being ouput to my TV. I attribute any difference in picture quality there may be to the fact that with A the Xbox One is doing the upscaling to 1080p and with B my AVR is (and its dedicated upscaler likely gives better results than the Xbox One, which scales using its GPU as I understand it).
