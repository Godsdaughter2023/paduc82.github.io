How do I reset Windows Update components

Suggest you download and save the Fixit. Then configure the system to Clean boot prior to running it:

How to troubleshoot a problem by performing a clean boot in Windows Vista/Windows 7

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/929135

a. user say:

Dave gave you good advice which you can take even farther if you wish.

There are two things that are important to password-protect: your router and your network.

When I talk about password-protecting your router, I'm actually referring to the router's 'administration console,' which is a fancy name for your router's settings.

Dave's instructions will password-protect your router's administration console, so no one will be able to change your router's settings. That's very important!

Password-protecting your network is also very important. When you password-protect your network, only devices that have the password can join your network. If your neighbor doesn't know the password, they can't join your network. You can password-protect your network by entering the router's administration console and finding the page where you can create a password. Choose a complex, impossible-to-guess password - the more characters the better - and you will be very safe.

One last thing to do, and it's easier to do than to explain. Your wireless devices speak to your router with radio waves, and you want to encrypt the information flowing through those radio waves. You can also find the settings for this in your router's administration console. You'll be given a choice of encryption methods; the one you want is called WPA2 (which is sometimes called WPA2-PSK.) You'll also be offered a choice of encryption algorithms - the mathematical formula that does the encrypting - and you want AES.

When you protect your router's administration console, your network and the radio transmission, that's a security troika that can't be beat. A great place to learn more about network security is the SmallNetBuilder website. It's a place for network gearheads but they also offer a really good 'Basics' section that even I can understand.
