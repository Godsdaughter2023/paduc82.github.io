You can move your files (pictures, documents, videos, music, etc.), but not programs.

Or at least, not most programs.  There is such a thing as a "portable" version of some programs, but it's incredibly rare, less than 0.00001% of programs in Microsoft's compatible list of Windows software has such a version.  Still, some that come to mind include WinSCP, Notepad++, and others.  None of these are made by Microsoft, they are just some programs I know that have "portable" versions of them.

Transferring your files is super easy: just plugin a flash drive or hard drive, drag-drop the files, and then carry the stick/drive to your new computer and plug it in and drag-drop once more to put the files wherever you like them to go.

Installing things like Office, you'll need to either get that CD or go to the website you purchased it from (if it was a downloadable version).
