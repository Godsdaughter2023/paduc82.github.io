bossdweeb say: +1 to Nirmal's suggestion - contact HP support, but -

All very hit and miss. Miss-matching RAM is not a good idea, and miss-matching the quantity  (2x512MB - 2x1GB) often  fails. You may get lucky and the machine will boot, but the RAM's  performance will suffer regardless.
There do seem to be a lot of people who are able to run 3GB RAM (laptops mostly), but it's an odd setup.
Better to have two 2GB sticks and (assuming Win7 32bit) only be able to access around 3.25GB of that as you still get the performance benefit of dual channel.
I have an old PC ( built it in '05) with an AMD 939 socket cpu  and have experimented with  two 512MB  and  two 1GB sticks ( I already had 2x512MB sticks from earlier on), an it will not boot into windows with this configuration.

AMD made a number of cpu's with the Athlon64 x2 designation. Several are socket 939, others may be AM2. You have to know exactly which cpu and motherboard to know what is what.
If the RAM is DDR2 then there is a chance the cpu/MOBO  are AM2.

Upgrading an HP or other OEM PC can be a minefield as they often use a 'special' motherboard design (if you have to use chipset drivers from HP it is probably one of their 'specials'), and with 1GB RAM and only an 80GB HDD in a small form factor case with a cheap power supply, you get what you pay for.
Personally - you would have done better to buy the parts from someone like New Egg (if you are in the USA), and  assembled the machine yourself. Basically, you are trying to make a silk purse out of a.... )
