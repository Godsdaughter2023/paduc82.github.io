    The issue is a set of 3 small (very small) contacts on the rotation analyzer. 

    With time one of them lose contact and it make the wheel going sometime in the direction opposite to expected direction.

    BTW Very strange that Msoft didn't use a optical system rather than this very old fashion method that limit lifespan of mouse.

    Anyway it can be cured if you are VERY METICULOUS!

    - Open the 3500 (you can start like a 4000 (many YouTube video deal with it)

    - Dismount the WHOLE electronics to allow simple access to the the rotating wheel holder.

    The wires between the 2 PCB are so strong that you must remove the top PCB to ease.

    Not a big deal, just another 3 screws to remove

    - Now you have easier access to the small rotating switch encase in a metal bracket with 4 plied pieces of metal.

    This is the thing the axis of the wheel go trough.

    - Very carefully disassemble this module. exposing 1 flat spring (with a notch that create the click while rotating), and 1 small wheel with 3 contact at 120 degree each.

    - Level up the 3 contacts to increase pressure on the fix (soldered) part of this module

    - Clean the fix part (better now than never)

    - Reassemble.

    Be careful not to press too strongly the 4 pieces of metal, they decide what is the pressure on the module hence the fluidity of the wheel.

    Try the wheel before reassembling to adjust the fluidity that you want.

    Not very critical to press them, their is no mechanical effort on this pieces. So it better be properly set than too firm and being  unable to rotate the wheel with fluidity.

    - Reassemble.

    lemp say:

    The answer for both Soul_Seeker and jay_834 -- but especially for Soul_Seeker (given that the headset works with an iPhone) -- is very likely to be that you have a new Bluetooth device that uses Bluetooth Low Energy (Bluetooth LE), also known as Bluetooth version 4.x or later.

    If you have a Bluetooth LE device and are relying in the Bluetooth capability built into your Windows 7 computer, you'll have a problem because Windows 7 does not natively support Bluetooth version 4.x.

    1. Open Device Manager on your computer (click Start, type Device Manager in the Search box, and press Enter)
    2. Double-click the Bluetooth category to expand it
    3. Right-click your Bluetooth adapter (receiver, transceiver, etc.) and select Properties
    4. Select the "Advanced" tab
    5. Check the LMP version

    • LMP 0.x - Bluetooth 1.0b
    • LMP 1.x - Bluetooth 1.1
    • LMP 2.x - Bluetooth 1.2
    • LMP 3.x - Bluetooth 2.0 + EDR
    • LMP 4.x - Bluetooth 2.1 + EDR
    • LMP 5.x - Bluetooth 3.0 + HS
    • LMP 6.x - Bluetooth 4.0
    • LMP 7.x - Bluetooth 4.1
    • LMP ​8.x - ​Bluetooth 4.2
    • LMP 9.x - Bluetooth 5.0

    If you don't have LMP 6.x or better and the headset uses Bluetooth 4, that's your problem.

    Can you pair the headset with your smartphone and hear sound through the earphones?  Most smartphones do support Bluetooth 4.

    If this is, in fact, your issue, you can buy an inexpensive Bluetooth USB transceiver that will work with Windows 7.  For example: https://www.newegg.com/Product/ProductList.aspx?Submit=ENE&N=100158097%20600315449&IsNodeId=1&Description=bluetooth&bop=And&PageSize=96&order=RATING

    If you do get a Bluetooth transceiver, disable the onboard Bluetooth in your computer.
