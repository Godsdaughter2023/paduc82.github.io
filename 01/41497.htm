a. user say:

Hi Chris,

Thank you for posting your query on Microsoft Community forums.

It is interesting to note that the screenshot can be taken by pressing only PrtScn button. The screenshot is temporarily saved on the clipboard and you can then paste this screenshot anywhere you like. However, as you mentioned correctly, in some laptops, two functions are designated to one button and are distinguished by the Fn button. But, you need not to press Alt+Fn+PrtScn together, you can just press Fn+PrtScn on a laptop to get the screenshot.

For more information in taking screenshots, refer to the following article:

Take a screen capture (print your screen)

Alternatively, there is another awesome utility from Microsoft that enables you to take screenshots of the parts of the screen important to you with various other features. This utility is called Snipping tool. Have a look on the following article:

Use Snipping Tool to capture screen shots

A screenshot, as the name suggests, is a shot or photograph of the current display on the screen. It does not matter what is on the screen, or if the screen is displayed inverted, sideways or with different colors; a screenshot will always record the current display irrespective of the contents of the display. It is then copied to another utility called Clipboard, temporarily. A clipboard is a temporary storage area for information that you have copied or moved from one place and plan to use somewhere else. It will be stored until you use the Paste command to insert it elsewhere. This is why you get the same screen with inverted colors, when pasted after turning the inversion off.

Hope this information is helpful. Do let us know if you need any further assistance, we'll be glad to assist you.
