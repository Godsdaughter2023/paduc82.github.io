hairyfool say:

This article has been recently updated so have you seen it?


http://support.microsoft.com/kb/2860880


Key issue really is the RAM installed. I agree don't disable it altogether, it can cause odd errors if you do and the article gives a couple of examples. However with sufficient RAM the primary role will be for crash dumps and we all hope these never happen. Therefore with 16GB it is not going to affect performance wherever you put it.


So the other issue is  SSD life. With wear levelling and TRIM most disk manufacturers do not consider paging files compromise disk life to any degree. I did read an article that claimed that those processes would be less effective if the drive was excessively full but the maths blew me away. It boiled down to the idea that less space free made it harder for levelling to work. Similar in principle I suppose to the point that defrag (no, not on an SSD) needs 15% free space to work.


Every Microsoft document I read states to let Windows manage the paging file and several have been updated since the advent of SSDs and they haven't changed that general advice.
