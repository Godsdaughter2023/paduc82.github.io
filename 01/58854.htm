barrysmithlittlebaz say:

It may be to do with your power option settings, even plugged in it can be set to Hibernate when plugged in.

To check your settings, right click the battery icon in the taskbar and select Power options -- the box should show you which power option is ticked -- click on Change plan settings -- click Change advanced power settings -- in the Power options box, scroll to the bottom -- click the + sign by Battery and do the same on Critical Battery Level. See if it is set to Hibernate. You can also check what the critical Battery level is set at. It may be that these settings got changed with the update.

The other thing you can try is, when this happens, wait a couple of minutes after it shuts down and you have plugged it in. If it starts up without the black screen and needing turning on and off, that will indicate that it is your settings as a couple of minutes charge will raise the battery level above where it will trigger it to hibernate.

Hope this helps.
