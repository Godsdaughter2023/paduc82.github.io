patricia yu say:

Hi Gracie,

After the recent release of the Windows 10, version 1709, some users have reported an issue that they are prompted to log in twice upon start up. We found that turning off a certain settings in Privacy settings resolves the issue.

Here's how:

  1. Go to Settings.
  2. Click on Accounts.
  3. Click on Sign-in options.
  4. Scroll down to Privacy, and then click the toggle switch to turn off Use my sign-in info to automatically finish setting up my device after an update or restart.
  5. Restart your PC.

Note that the said feature is only available on PCs that are not managed by enterprises. This includes home PCs that are synced to an enterprise (work, school, etc.,) email.

Win7 is the first OS  to use DX11, so I imagine your still unnamed graphics card is only capable of DX10.

- It's a common misconception that Win7 has only DX 11, or that Win10 has only DX12.

Win7 has three versions of DX - 9,10 and 11.

- The following is a list of the Direct X .dll's you will have if the files are up to date.
Go to the Windows\System 32 folder, (and SysWOW64 if you have 64bit).  
They are in alphabetical order and will start with d3dx9 - 24 > 43 . Then d3dx10 - 33 > 43 & finally d3dx11 - 42 > 43.
There should also be - d3d9, d3d10 and d3d11 these come before the D3Dcompilers - 33>43.

- There are more DX files, but these are most of the graphics related Direct X  .dll's
