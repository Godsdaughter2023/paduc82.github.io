arminatorx say:

Since you already checked your router configuration, and made them configure the modem for bridge mode, try checking if your provider is using Carrier Grade NAT (CG-NAT) and DS-Lite. 

Most new internet providers didn't get enough free IPv4 adresses for all their customers, and thus put multiple users behind a big router in their data center. 

You can try to check for that this way: Sign into your own router and take a note of the WAN IP address your router got from the modem and your ISP. 

Then go to a website that shows you your IP the website receives the request from. If the WAN IP and the IP from the website are different, then your Internet Provider is hiding you (along with a lot of other customers) behind a NAT router. 

You need to contact your ISP again and ask them to give you a public IPv4 address and to NOT put you behind a CG-NAT. 

Some providers will simply do that, other providers will do that for a monthly fee, and some providers are unable to give you a public IPv4. In that case your only option is to change internet providers again.
