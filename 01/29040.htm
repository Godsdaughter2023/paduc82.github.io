Windows 10 SVCHOST.EXE, high cpu utilisation, high memory consumption, - Microsoft Community

gavp_996 say:

Windows 10 SVCHOST.EXE, high cpu utilisation, high memory consumption, high network utilisation, windows update

Hi all,

Just thought I would share a recent experience in case it helps someone else out.

I have two Windows 10 Version 10.0.10586 PC's at home on my local network (one is my wife's, the other mine). Both had been running fine since day 1.

A few days ago my wife mentioned the fan on her PC was very noisy and it was performing poorly. I had a look and discovered that svchost.exe was consuming every last CPU cycle it could (pinned at 99%), its memory usage was at 3GB (its an 8GB machine) and that it was consuming 100Mb/s of network bandwidth. The PC itself was extremely sluggish (its an i3, 8GB RAM, 128GB SSD) - it was difficult to launch and use applications because of it. I came to the conclusion that it was performing windows updates (although I had never seen anything like this before) and essentially decided to let it run its course.

The next day it was the same thing (I had left it on over night and it had rebooted to complete the install of some updates) but even worse, now my PC had started to exhibit exactly the same behaviour. My PC is an older core i7, but with 12GB of RAM and a 256GB SSD - on my PC svchost was consuming 6GB of RAM. I had to leave both PC's as they were because I didn't have enough time to troubleshoot but tonight (3 days after this began on the original PC, and 2 days after the second - for the whole period they exhibited this behaviour) I managed to have a good look at it and ultimately resolve the issue.

Short version, both PC's were set to get "Updates from more than one place", with both set to get/send updates to "PC's on my local network". I turned off "Updates from more than one place", rebooted and the problem is now gone on both PC's. Updates are still downloading and installing, but svchost CPU, memory and network utilisation are now normal and PC performance is back to how it was before.

Prior to turning this off I was unable to find anything of use (in terms of troubleshooting) in the event logs or the windows update log. The other interesting thing was that the high network utilisation appeared to be between the PC's and a Microsoft IP, not each other (from what I could tell from the resource monitor).

Not sure what set this off or why, but this seems to have solved the problem. To access the setting for "Updates for more than one place", open the action centre, click on "all settings", click on "updates & security", click on "windows update", click on the "advanced options" link on the updates page, click on the "choose how updates are delivered" link.

Cheers,
