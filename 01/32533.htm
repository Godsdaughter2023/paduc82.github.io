Yes, with a legal Windows key, you sure can install W10 and activate it (before the year expiry), but not without somewhat of a process because a licensed W7 basically allows the one year free install of W10 onto your computer... Only as an upgrade first, then as a clean install.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

The information that I had gathered is that you have two options:

1) Perform the upgrade onto W7, then fish out the 'new' generated key with 'productkey.vbs'. With that new generated key, you can go ahead and do a clean install with your Windows 10 disc.

2) Perform the upgrade onto W7, then Windows will allegedly recognize your computer specific hardware (motherboard, etc.), so you can reformat and do a clean install with your Windows 10 disc.
