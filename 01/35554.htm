jerrybr say: Overheating can be caused by several factors, obviously you already mentioned the vent problem.  I'm running a similar processor (Turion x2 TL-66) and it gets fairly warm in my HP Pavilion at times.  Windows 7 and AMD's heat sensors seem to work well in my laptop, so I don't think that would be the problem.  You might check to see if you're running balanced or high power.  Balanced will usually keep the processor using the lower load range.  It could also be that you have a heat sink/fan problem, which could be the thermal compound has failed, or it could also be that your graphics chip is over heating.  Being my HP is out of warranty, I opened it up, and replaced the thermal compound and replaced the thermal pad on the gpu with a copper shim+thermal compound (I use Arctic Silver 5).  After it cured, my temps are considerably better.  You can download a good 'freeware' program called System Information for Windows which gives a detailed readout of under it's "Sensors" listing.  That might give you a clue as to what is happening.  The only other thing you might want to check right up front is whether or not you're using the latest drivers for your systems ACPI.  One last thing to check is to make sure you've got the most recent Bios, because that has been known to be an issue that can cause the fan/cpu temperature monitoring to not work as it should.

This may be 'overload' but hope something helps...it's frustrating when a system is running hotter than it should, plus it can really do some damage to the cpu and gpu.  In addition, a good 'laptop cooling board' does a lot to reduce the heat.
