Additionally, turning the wheel up or down while plugged into the desktop still adjusts the volume of the headphone's background white noise, which leads me to strongly believe that this issue is directly related to Windows 10.

The headphones are Turtle Beach PX21s. They connect via both a USB cord and a green audio jack. They function if only the USB cord is plugged in, but not if only the jack is plugged in, and to my memory the purpose of having both is so that the headphones can draw power from the usb port while sending the audio data via the audio jack.

a. user say:

Hi,

xorph say:

Looking around I've found both the source of the problem and a fix: I'll post them here in case anyone in the future runs into a similar issue.

Basically, the headphones are read by Windows as two separate audio devices. If you right-click Volume Control and select Playback Devices, you should have two entries for Speakers. One listed as "Turtle Beach USB Headset" and the other listed as whatever your audio drivers are (mine was Realtek High Definition Audio).

If your default playback device is set to Turtle Beach, Windows bypasses the adjusting wheels on the headset. Setting your default playback device to Realtek or whatever your standard speakers are should fix the issue.
