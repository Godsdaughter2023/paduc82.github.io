jeremy m - support engineer say: Miss Tila,

The speed range of USB  1.1 is between  1.5Mbps & 12Mbps

Though USB 2.0 is capable of speeds up to 480Mbps not all devices themselves are capable of running at those speeds, such as, in the case of your MP3 player. It should be noted that most devices do not actually reach the 480Mbps rate.

 

If you are concerned about getting back to the driver that came with your MB, you should find the driver CD that came with your MB. On it, you will find the chipset drivers which will also restore your USBs to any previous state. You could also go to the manufacturer of the MB for any available updates for your 64bit system.

 

The reason that you may have noticed the name of the driver suddenly change through an update, is because it was the driver/controller that was updated. These controllers have different names according to the manufacturers that make them. Here are a few controllers that are all USB 2.0:

·         Intel Enhanced

·         SiS Enhanced

·         VIA Enhanced

·         ALi Enhanced

·         Standard Enhanced

·         NEC Enhanced

2.      Start tapping the F8 key. The Windows Advanced Boot Options Menu appears.

3.      Ensure that the Safe mode option is selected.

4.      Press Enter. The computer then begins to start in Safe mode.

5.      Install the downloaded update.
