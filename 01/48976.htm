Hi,

 

This is fairly common with Win 8. I have had no problems, but have seen a lot of issues on the forum. The problem usually happens with Toshiba and Hitachi drives though.

 

One thing that may be a problem is that both the Seagate 2TB and WD 3TB may have a program or modification to allow non x64 machines, or older versions of Windows to access the drive.

 

I had a Seagate 3TB that came with three partitions on it which were made to look like one in COMPUTER. This was something that was built into the USB adapter.

 

I ended up removing the drive from the enclosure and putting it in a 3rd party enclosure, reformatted it as a single 3TB partition, and was good to go.

 

It will no longer be readable by a 32 bit OS however.

 

If you were to reformat them in Win 8, they would probably work fine, but you may end up with the same issue if you then tried attaching them to a Win 7 machine, for example.
