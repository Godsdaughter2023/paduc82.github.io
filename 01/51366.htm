In about 15 years ago, malicious people had managed to social engineer support personnel and gotten access to other people's account. 

Since then the security has been improved, and they will make you jump to a lot of hoops to get your account back. 

I understand that you feel annoyed by that. But imagine how you feel, if I could (for example) look at your Facebook page, and see people congratulating you to your Xth birthday, or getting your home address because you snapped a picture of your cat at home with your phone adding the GPS location to the photo's EXIF data, and then calling support and telling them I need access to your account, giving them your actual address and personal info like birthdate and the likes. 

You can try to recover your account by following these instructions on recovering a compromises Microsoft Account. 

After you gained control over your own account again, make sure you enable Two Step Verification on https://account.live.com/Proofs/Manage and setting up the free Authenticator App on your smartphone. 

With Two Step Verification, whenever a hacker gained access to your password and login information, the Live Service will ask for a secret code that only your phone app can provide. So they would also have to physically steal your phone to get into your account. 

Then, with that set up, make sure your Security Info (i.e. phone number and secondary email) are up to date and that the hacker didn't add their own phone number or email, so they could recover your account with their own email again. 

If you see foreign phone numbers and emails, remove them. 

And finally, after you got your account back, and hardened the security, do NOT give your account to anyone else. For example, a lot of kids engage in "game sharing", where they let friends or strangers download their own profile to the console to let them play their games and vice versa. 

But they forget to remove their account from the other person's console or at least lock it with a code after that, and so the other person has full access to the account. 

So just make sure that Two Step Verification is enabled, and that you do not leave your account unattended on someone else's device.
