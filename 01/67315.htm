If still no joy, you may need to do a clean install of Win8 Final.

 

Note: If a Norton appliction and/or or a McAfee application was EVER installed on your Win7 computer - including a preinstalled free-trial, even if you never used it, the Norton and/or McAfee "leftovers" may be your troublemaker.

Hi Bhupesh,

To enable audio conferencing from Skype for Business clients, you don’t need any add-ons. Based on your description, you want to join in the audio conferencing via mobiles, which called Dial-in conferencing, then you need the PSTN conferencing add-on. People can call into Skype for Business meetings using a phone, instead of using the Skype for Business app on a mobile device or PC.

I notice that you’re a prepaid user. For prepaid user, it’s not feasible to directly purchase the PSTN conferencing add-on from add-on subscriptions in Office 365 admin portal. You need to contact the partner where you prepay the E3 plan and seek advice from them about the price and how to purchase it.

Note: It's important to remember, when people call into a meeting using a phone, they will only get audio, no video. If they want to get instant messages (IM)s during the meeting, see shared desktops, or get files that are being shared in the meeting, they will need to join the meeting using a mobile device or PC that has the Skype for Business app installed on it.
