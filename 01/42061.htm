322756

lemp say:

Although I agree in general with the advice from Chaos_Edit, there are some additional points you should consider:

  • WordPad may not be able to deal with certain formatting that has been used in documents created in Word 2003.  Notepad almost certainly will not work well (if at all).
  • If you installed ALL of the Office Professional 2003 components (Access 2003, Excel 2003, Outlook 2003 with Business Contact Manager, PowerPoint 2003, Publisher 2003, and Word 2003), you may not need all of them.  For example, if all you need is Word, you can remove the other components, which should get you some additional disk space.  For directions seeInstall or remove individual components in Office
  • Although uninstalling programs such as Office will free up a certain amount of disk space (as will running the Disk Cleanup tool andproperly adjusting the System Restore settings ), odds are that you will soon be back in the same situation.  The only long-term solution is to get a larger hard drive.  Drives are not expensive and are relatively easy to install.  If you have a desktop/tower, you probably have space to install a second hard drive and use that to store data (documents, pictures, songs, etc.) or you can replace the primary drive.  Generally, laptops are limited to a single drive, although some laptops can use the space usually occupied by a CD/DVD drive for a secondary hard drive.  Alternatively, you can get an external drive, but external drives are best used for backup and data storage because read/write access is generally slower than for an internal drive.
