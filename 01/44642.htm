How to Change Windows 7 Taskbar and Adjust Button Length or Width Size
 
   1. At first Run Registry Editor

   2. Then navigate “HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Control Panel\Desktop\WindowMetrics” registry key.

   3. Then right click on a blank space on the right pane, select New, then String Value. Name the new string value as MinWidth. If Minimum Width is already exist then skip the step.

   5. Then restart the computer

   1. At first right click on Taskbar, and select Properties.

Hi Carl,

Start your PC, just as Windows attempts to load (spinning dots), press and hold Power Button for 5 - 10 seconds to perform a Hard Shut Down

Do this twice

On the third start Windows will boot into the Recovery Environment and from there you can access System Repair, Safe Mode, Command Prompt . . . etc.

Please try each option one at a time, then see of Windows will boot . . .

Go to Troubleshoot - Advanced Option - Revert to previous build of Windows
This would be the preferred option, if available . ..

Go to Troubleshoot - Advanced Option - Startup Repair
See if Windows startup can repair itself

Go to Troubleshoot - Advanced Option - System Restore
Check to see if you have a restore point you can go back to

If you do not have a Restore Point, go to Troubleshoot - Advanced Option - Startup Settings and click Restart
Upon restart, press 4 to enter Safe Mode
Once in Safe Mode, backup your data if you do not already have a backup
Open Device Manager and check to make sure all your Drivers are up to date
Reboot to see if Windows will start Normally

If the above fails, go to Troubleshoot - Advanced Options - Reset this PC
Choose to keep your files and apps, just your files or to keep nothing
Then start the Reset

victor-b say:

Hi.  Pressing Win + P Gived you a couple of options, the two most relevant in this case is "Duplicate" and "Extend".

Duplicate will as it says, duplicate the same image on both screens, but that's not what you want.

 

Extend will extend your screensize, which will make your mouse be able to move out of the first screen and over to the next one. The screens will still be "seperate" meaning that if you maximize a windows on one of the screens, it will only maximize within that screen.

 

You are mentioning that when you select "Extend", you get a picture on the laptop, and the monitor is black with a cursor on it? In that case, it should be properly configured, and you can drag windows over to the extended screen. If you wish to set the screen resolution etc. on the external screen, right click on the background while the mouse is at the extended screen, and choose your display settings.
