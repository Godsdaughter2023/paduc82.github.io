lemp say:

Microsoft did not provide a "Windows Easy Transfer" solution for moving to Windows 10.  Instead, Microsoft made an arrangement with Laplink to provide a free version of Laplink's PC Mover program.  When Windows 10 was first released, PC Mover Express was free, but that offer expired on August 31, 2016.  You can now buy PC Mover Express for $14.95, which is half off the retail price:  https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/transfer-your-data

PC Mover Express, like Windows Easy Transfer and all similar programs, will only move files and folders (these transfer programs also are supposed to move "settings").  None of these programs move programs.  Traditionally, the only way to move programs from one computer to another was to reinstall the programs using original installation media (CD/DVD/downloaded installer file) and the original license key.

Laplink, however, sells a "Professional" version of PC Mover that is supposed to transfer programs as well as data.  I have no personal experience with this, but I do know that Laplink is a reputable company.  The current "Microsoft discount" price for PC Mover Professional is $44.95:  http://pcmover-10.laplink.com/index.html#pro

With regard to your cable -- if your current Easy Transfer cable is a USB to USB cable, it may or may not work with Windows 10.  I don't have any information about this.  On the other hand, it's really not necessary these days to use a special USB to USB cable to transfer data from one computer to another.  An Ethernet cable will work just fine -- and in fact the cables that come with PC Mover ($4.95 with "Express" and free with the boxed version of "Pro") are Ethernet cables. 

If you have a router to which your computers are (or will be) connected, you already have a network connection over which you can transfer data.  Otherwise, most modern computers have "autosensing" Ethernet ports that will allow you to connect two computers with an ordinary Ethernet cable; older computers require a special "cross-over" Ethernet cable.

Personally, I prefer not to use any of these transfer programs, but rather just copy the data manually.  If you have a backup system in place (you do back up your data regularly, right?), you can just copy the data from your backup copy to your new computer.
