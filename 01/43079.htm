dominicp say: If the Mac monitor will only accept digital input the adapter is useless.

The adapter will convert the plug-out from a DVI-I signal, which includes an analog signal, but cannot convert a VGA (analog) signal to a DVI-D (digital only) signal.

scottturner_938 say:

Solved:

1. Avoid the problem in the first place by not allowing Microsoft to hijack your login. Make your login account local. Set up a backup local user just in case they somehow manage to hijack your login despite your diligence. Make your local login passwords simple and easy to remember. Store backups of those passwords somewhere.

2. Pick an easy to remember password for your Microsoft Account, not a complex random secure password. It is unfortunate that you have to be less secure in order to reduce the odds of trouble. For every other site , in my experience, you can safely choose the maximum length/complexity/randomness and never have to worry because they will never hijack your login and make that password your login password.

3. Despite not knowing about 1. and 2. above, I would have been able to avoid trouble via resetting my Microsoft Account password if I had known how to keep Bitdefender from blocking internet access after upgrading to Windows 10 (see Bitdefender FAQ for instructions). Thanks for putting the last several months to good use toward avoiding this mess Bitdefender...

4. If you somehow end up in this situation anyway, my solution was to remove the boot drive from my Windows 10 computer, plug it in as a non-boot drive in another computer and extract my password manager file. I was then able to open that file with the password manager on that computer and access my VeryLongComplexPassword2 and then I put the boot drive back in the W10 computer and used the old password to get past the "Your device is offline. Please sign in with the last password used on this device." blockage.

5. After getting back in I fixed Bitdefender and created two local login accounts with one of those being the default login account.

Fun times.

Sorry to hear that you're experiencing difficulty with attempting to launch this game.

In this situation, I would first suggest trying the steps here on our support site entitled "My Xbox One game won't start or freezes during gameplay" and see if that helps resolve the issue.

If that does not work, then the next best course of action in my opinion would be for you to attempt what we call a soft reset of your console. It's a reset of your console back to factory settings but one that does not uninstall any of your games or apps and is a really good troubleshooting step for most issues.  

Just make sure that before you go ahead with it, that you do know all of the sign-in information for any/all of your accounts(asthey will be removed) and have easy access to your internet network.  

Also make sure that you select the option denoted reset and keep my games and apps.
