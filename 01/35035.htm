Hit Windows key + X and click Device Manager.

Expand the tree to Universal Serial Bus controllers and then right click each device which contains the description "Hub" and then click Properties.

Click the "Power management" tab and remove the checkmark where it says "Allow computer to turn off this device to save power".

xircal say:

By the looks of it, it's a common problem with Toshiba USB 3.0 drives. I did a quick search of the Toshiba forums and it appears from the results that there's an issue with their USB 3.0 drives needing more power: Toshiba external HDD disconnects using USB 3.0

You can try doing the following.

  1. Hit Windows key + X and go to "Power Options".
  2. Click the link on the right of whichever power plan you use called "Change plan settings".
  3. Click the link to "Change advanced power settings" and then scroll down to "USB Settings" ---> "USB selective suspend" and change "Plugged in" to Disabled.

The will prevent the computer from disabling the USB hub driver.

More on that particular subject here: https://msdn.microsoft.com/en-gb/library/windows/hardware/ff540144%28v=vs.85%29.aspx?f=255&MSPPError=-2147217396

lemp say:

Generally, a printer will show as "offline" if it's powered off (or Windows does not detect that it's powered on).

With the printer connected to your computer's USB port, turn the printer power off, wait about 10 seconds, and turn the printer power back on.  Do you hear the USB device connect "bong" sound?

If you don't hear any sound, make sure that the USB cable is firmly connected to the computer.  If the cable is meant to be disconnected at the printer end, check that connection as well.  Finally, with the printer powered on, connect the USB cable to another USB port on the computer and check for the USB connect sound.

Go to Devices and Printers and double-click on the icon for your printer.

Click on the printer status link to open the Printer status window

Click on "Printer" to open the menu and if there is a check next to "Use Printer Offline" click on the check to clear it.
