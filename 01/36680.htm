I finally figured out what the problem was. Their was a driver for the Synaptics Touchpad and Windows was download and installing the wrong driver. Here is how I removed that driver and installed the correct one manually as well as preventing Windows Update from installing the incorrect driver again. For reference, the driver from Windows Update that is causing problems has a update name of Synaptics driver update for Lenovo Pointing Device.

1. Go to the control Panel, Click on Programs and Features, and uninstall the following program. It may have a different name, but find the most similar to it. The name of the program that needs to be uninstalled is called:

Synaptics Pointing Device Driver

Once you have uninstalled this program, do not restart the machine. We have a couple more things to do first.

2. Right click on the Windows Start Button, and then click on Device Manager. Under Mice and other pointing devices, Select Lenovo Trackpad Mouse. I uninstalled all of mine, Just to be on the safe side. We will install them later.

3. Now proceed to go to keyboards, and uninstall your keyboard driver. Just to be on the safe side, once again, I uninstalled all of the keyboards here.

4. Now close out of the Programs list and the device manager.

5. Go to the settings ( Not Control Panel) and select Network and Internet. Then on the left side of the screen, Click on Wi-Fi. The in the main setting page for Wi-Fi click on Manage Known Networks.

From here, select the network that you are currently connected to. Click on it, Then click on Properties. Where it says Metered Connection, turn this On. We need to prevent Windows from installing the update on boot up, so enabling this prevent that from happening.

6. You will need to download the following Troubleshooter from the Official Microsoft Website:

http://download.microsoft.com/download/f/2/2/f22d5fdb-59cd-4275-8c95-1be17bf70b21/wushowhide.diagcab

Once you have downloaded the file, locate the file in the File Explorer and Double click on it, or in your browser click Run at the bottom of the screen. Once the Trumoboshooter opens, click on next at the bottom of the screen. Wait for it to search for updates.

On this next screen, click on Hide Updates. Select the Touchpad driver (Check the box next to it) and then click okay. Once the Troubleshooter shows the results click on close at the bottom or just close out of it.

Now restart the machine.

When it boots up, Click on the start button, followed by Settings (Not Control Panel) and then click on Network and Internet. Then click on Wi-Fi on the side panel. Click on Manage Known Networks and then find the Wi-Fi network you are connected to. Click on it, then click on Properties and where it says Metered Network turn that off.

Now Check for updates and the touchpad driver should hopefully not come up. Then go to your Maufactions Website and download the correct driver for your system. I found out that the 64 bit drivers for Windows 8.1 work fine for my Windows 10 Machine. Download the EXE and follow the instructions in the installer to install the driver. Then restart the computer and test everything out. Hopefully it works now! Let me know if their are any other problems or if it did not work and I will help you out.
