karen_[01] say:

Unfortunately, it's not possible to consolidate your existing Skype account and the other account that was created using your Microsoft credential. When you sign in to Skype using your email address or Microsoft account, it will generate a new account with pre-defined username, and since this follows your MS credential, it's considered linked to the MS account which is why you cannot link it to another account. You can only link one account to either MS or Skype account.

If you will delete the second account, note that it will also close the Skype and MS accounts associated with it. This is due to the full migration of Skype to Microsoft and one credentials to all Microsoft products.

For this case, I can only advise to sign in using the correct log in details and avoid accessing the second account as much as possible. In a few years of inactivity, that account will be closed automatically.

As for the other issue, I highly suggest to check your correct Skype name here: CLICK HERE then sign in to it in the Skype website. If you get the same error "Signing into Skype ... please wait" you may try using a different browser or clear your browser's cache and cookies and then try it again.

Once you were able to sign in, proceed with cancelling your subscription. For the steps, please refer to this FAQ: How do I cancel or change my Skype subscription?

Let me know if you have further questions.
