irixion say:

It could be a disc issue, or your 360's laser may be dirty. Disc density is different, and the way in which the DVD drive reads is different. So make sure that the disc is clean (wipe from the inner circle, outwards, in a straight line, never in a circular motion), and then make sure the center XBOX holograms in the middle are also clean and free of scratches/cracks. If that's all clean, clean your laser. You can get kits for $5 bucks at most electronics retailers.

I would also try it in a different video mode. Set your video settings to 480p, and try it then. The Xbox emulator is very broken. (Seriously, it's kind of pathetic) Some games freak out in certain situations and throw out random errors at you.

I haven't tried SH4 (Silent Hill tho <3), but if everything above checks out, then likely though, it's your disc.

    First, to register with Microsoft, go to http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows-vista/Registration-frequently-asked-questions and follow the instructions under what is registration.  I contacted Microsoft chat and they told me it would work even though it had been done before.  There's no other way to do it even if it doesn't work.  But this won't help you with your problem.

    You need to create an administrator account for yourself (as well as a standard account).  Login as an administrator. If his is the only available Administrator Profile (you need to be an administrator to fix this), enable the Hidden Administrator Account (HAA) using http://www.microsoft.com/communities/newsgroups/en-us/default.aspx?&lang=en&cr=US&guid=&sloc=en-us&dg=microsoft.public.windows.vista.administration_accounts_passwords&p=1&tid=d20f9db4-7b2c-48be-a087-7835dc2a9055&mid=d20f9db4-7b2c-48be-a087-7835dc2a9055. or get the password from him and create another administrator account for yourself.   If you can't remember the password of the HAA, try nothing as that's probably what he (or the vendor) did during installation.  Once done, be sure to deactivate the HAA to save it in case it's needed again and for security purposes (since people often try to hack into systems using this account).  Do NOT use the HAA as your administrator account because if you lose the only administrator account on the system or it becomes corrupt then you are hosed.

    You need to take ownership of his profile and then give yourself full rights permission to everything there.

    To see your permissions, right click the folder/file, click on properties, and check out the security tab.  Check to see what permissions you have by clicking on your username (or user group).  Here are the types of permissions you can have: http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows-vista/What-are-permissions.  You need to be an administrator or the owner to change permissions (and sometimes being an administrator or even an owner isn't enough - there are ways to block even them from access (though a smart administrator knows these ways and can get around them - but usually shouldn't because they were denied access usually for a very good reason).  Here's how to change folder permissions in Vista: http://www.online-tech-tips.com/windows-vista/set-file-folder-permissions-vista/.
     
    To fix this problem with the folders, take ownership of the folders or the drive (as an administrator) and give yourself full rights.  Right click the folder/drive, click on properties, go to the security tab and click on Advanced and then click on the Owner tab.  Click edit and then click the name of the person you want to give ownership to (you may need to add it if it isn't there - or it may be yourself). If you want it to apply to sub-folders and files within that folder/drive, then check the box for replace owner on sub-containers and objects and click OK.  Back out and now there's a new owner for the folders/drive and files who can change permissions as needed.  You can  now give yourself full rights to the folders and files, then you can delete them since you are the owner with full rights.  Here's more information on taking ownership of a file or folder:     http://www.vistax64.com/tutorials/67717-take-ownership-file.html.  If you moved pictures or music to his folders, be sure to move them to yours before deleting them and then removing him as a user.

    I hope this helps.
