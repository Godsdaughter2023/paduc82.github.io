exlegalalien say:

OK, so I searched the registry for my old email address, and found that it is stored in HKEY_CURRENT_USER\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\ActiveSync\Partners\{GUID} key.  There were two string values, Email and Email Address.  The former just had my old email address, while the latter had both, in the "old;new" format.  I changed Email value to my new address, and removed the old address from Email Address.  After a restart Microsoft account showed correct email, and Windows stopped nagging me to "Fix" the account.

ETA: Turns out there was no need to change Email Address value. Changing Email to the correct address is sufficient.

A word of caution (using Microsoft's own words): Serious problems might occur if you modify the registry incorrectly by using Registry Editor or by using another method.
