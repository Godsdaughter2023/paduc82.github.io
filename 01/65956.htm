It's confusing I know,  but the recovery partitions read as 100% free because there's no drive letter assigned to them. The operating system can only read data on drives which have been assigned a drive letter.

But they do contain the necessary files to ensure the operating system starts (handled by the EFI partition) together with the one containing all the programs the machine was supplied with commonly known as 'bloatware' as well as the files necessary to restore the OS should you decide to reset.

arminatorx say:

Sharing on PC is a bit more complex and complicated. 

Your friend would have to add your Microsoft Account to their Family in https://account.microsoft.com/family.

Then they would have to go to your PC, and sign in with your account, start the Windows Store, and sign you out of the store. 

Then they sign in with their own account in the store, download and install ARK from the Windows Store. After the game is installed, make sure you sign your friend out of the store again, otherwise you could (un-)intentionally buy stuff in the store in your friends name. 

After that procedure, you should be able to play your friend's ARK game on your PC. 

Your friend however should know that there is a limit of devices on which he can install PC apps from his account!
