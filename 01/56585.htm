This was a problem with some of the early PAL 360 titles.  Normally it only effected VGA connections, but it can also affect HDMI connections too.  I think Feeding Frenzy came out before the Xbox 360 included HDMI as standard.

Anyway I digress. There is a way to fix it, but you'll need the original SD composite cables your console came with, and in your case, access to a TV with SD composite inputs (the yellow, white and red connectors).


Assuming you have a TV, here is what to do: 

1.  Disconnect your console from the monitor, remove the HDMI cable and insert the Xbox 360 composite cable.  Connect these cables to the TV and turn on your console.
2.  Go to the Display settings screen and change the display to 60Hz.
3.  Turn it off, disconnect the cables, and set your console back to the PC monitor again with HDMI.  If all goes well, your game should now work, and it should continue to work from now on.
