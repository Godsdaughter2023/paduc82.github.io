a.c. hawley say:

This happened to me as well. I went online, found powercfg /requestsoverride DRIVER "\FileSystem\srvnet" SYSTEM, entered that in my command prompt, and nothing happened.

I ended up digging really, really deep into my computer and figured it out, at least for me. This solution might work for you as well.

I noticed in someone's comment on one of these posts that they mentioned print spooling. I blew by it because I don't have a printer, but because nothing else worked, I went and checked into it.

I opened services.msc and looked at print spooling. Lo and behold, it was running. I stopped the service. Note: I did not say disable. I just stopped it. Given that srvnet is a server concern (it is in the name), I looked at the server service. It was for sharing, which is something I do not do from my machine. I looked further, it queries srvnet. So, I stopped it as well. Didn't disable, just stopped.

When I entered powercfg /requests in my command prompt, imagine my pleasure to see no requests.

tl;dr: if the override doesn't work, open services.msc and check if print spooling and server are running. If they are, stop both and enter powercfg /requests to see if the srvnet request has stopped.
