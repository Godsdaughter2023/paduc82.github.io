Memory? Are you asking about memory or disk space?

The term "memory" is used to refer to the place where the computer
holds the program and data it is working with at the moment. It's
normally random access memory (RAM), but that can be extended with the
page or swap file on the hard disk. The word "memory" is never used to
refer to the space on a drive (whether that's a hard drive, diskette,
CD, DVD, or thumb drive). Space on a disk drive is normally called
"storage" or simply "disk space."
Calling disk space "memory" isn't so much wrong as just
unconventional. In a sense, disk space is, of course, memory, but the
important thing to realize is that the word is never used that way,
and if you do so, you do it at the risk of seriously confusing your
audience.
So please tell us how much disk space your computer has, and how much
is used. And tell us where you see that your files take up 1.4 of your
disk space, and what that number is.

Which files will be affected?

When you reset your PC, the drive will be completely reset, and all your apps and files will be removed from it.
