Recording Device on Xbox One - Microsoft Community

epicticket say:

Recording Device on Xbox One

I have a Roxio Gamecaptue HD Pro and i wont work on the xbox one. I don't know if I can or not, But if I can i need to know how to do it. please help.

gimli1357 say:

I have a Roxio Game Capture HD Pro too and I had a little trouble getting it to record at first, but finally figured it out. Try turning off the Xbox One by holding in the power button on the console until the power brick light turns orange (if you can't see your brick just hold it in for about 10 seconds). It doesn't matter if the console is on or off when you hold it in, it will shut down completely. Turn it back on (you'll have to use the console button or controller, voice won't work when it's completely off). It will take longer than normal to turn back on and will show the Xbox logo on a green background for about 30 seconds or so. Once it is back on, connect your Roxio Game Capture HD Pro and it should now work (make sure you select "HDMI" for the input for recording).

The reason you have to do a complete shutdown is because of the encrypted HDMI signal to keep people from recording TV or movies (which is good). The encryption turns on once you run a video app: TV, Netflix, Amazon Video, etc. And once it is on it will stay on even after you shut down the console, unless you do a complete shutdown. Once you do a complete shutdown it removes the encryption and allows you to record all you want. Any time you run a video app you'll need to do this again.

Hope this helps. :D
