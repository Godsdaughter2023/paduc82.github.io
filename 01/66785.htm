tomsed say:

I also have the same issue with a Bose QC30 headset but I believe this is a generic problem with Bluetooth headsets which do provide separate profiles for hands-free and stereo audio sink. In Windows (Version 10 Creators Update) there are two playback devices (hands-free and stereo) and one microphone (hands-free). The standard device is the stereo one to allow proper audio playback for music and other things. The standard communication device is the hands-free to have audio playback and microphone.

The notifications obviously are played over the standard device (stereo) which is fine until  you are on a call using applications like Skype, Skype for business or Webex. In this case the hands-free device is connected which works fine until a notification sounds is played. For this Windows does switch the channel to the stereo device , plays the sound and then switches back to hands-free. Of course this does interrupt the calls audio.

I believe that the behavior of windows shouldn't be like this. As soon as the standard communication device is in use Windows should play all sounds over this device until it is no longer used by applications and then returning to the stereo audio. The result would be similar to what you get on a USB headset. There you get the notification sound overlaid into the calls audio. There is even a setting to control the volume (mute, 80%, 50% or no change) of the notification in those cases.

All solutions like turning of notifications as described in the Bose community forum or switch the hands-free to be both standard device and standard communication device are only bad workarounds to me.
