    Solution 2: Test your connection

    Test the Xbox LIVE connection on your Xbox 360 console. Here’s how:

    1. On your console, go to Settings, My Xbox and then select System.
    2. Select Network Settings.
    3. Select Wired Network or the name of your wireless network if you are prompted to do so.
    4. Select Test Xbox LIVE Connection.
    5. Do one of the following depending on your results:
    • If the test fails and you can't connect to Xbox LIVE, see Can't connect to Xbox LIVE?
    • If the Xbox LIVE service is available, try the next solution.
    • If there is an Xbox LIVE service alert, try again later when the Xbox LIVE service is available.

    If you have a firewall or are connected to a network through your workplace or school, you might need to open network ports in order for your Xbox 360 console to communicate with Xbox LIVE. See Network ports used by Xbox LIVE for help with opening network ports

    Finally I FOUND A GUARANTEED  SOLUTION TO Error Code 8007002

    1- All you have to do is to download a program by Microsoft called Autoruns

           E - Now go to the Drivers Tab, You have to be EXTRA CAREFUL in this tab, Some of the entries in this tab are required by the system to boot & unchecking them will definitely damage your system, DO NOT Randomly untick the entries of this tab, look at the Description & Publisher columns in the program to determine which entries to untick, The entries that should Be unticked are the:

    Like (RealTek HD Audio) or any other audio driver

    Untick any 3d party driver, & by third party drivers i mean the drivers added by programs you installed like (Internet Download Manger WFP Driver) & (Samsung USB Driver) etc..

           F - Now go to the Codecs Tab, untick all the entries that could be unticked (some will remain ticked no matter what), Alternatively you can uninstall the codec software installed in your computer Like (K-lite Codec) or (Shark007 Codec )

    jaymo1978 say:

    Hi Sween! Let me just ask a couple of clarifying questions - is Destiny the only game in which you're noticing a framerate drop? Also, when you use your Xbox, how many different things are you doing with it before shutting down (for example, just a game, then shutting down again, or play a game, watch some videos, watch TV, play another game, etc.?)

    The reason I ask that last question is, in Instant On mode, one of the issues people tend to see is that when they use their system over time, as they use games and apps, things start responding more slowly, and in most cases the reason is because the system is keeping a list of all of these recent programs in its memory, for purposes of quick-resume. If you press the Home button on the controller to back out of a game and return to the dashboard, and then immediately start another game, that other program is technically still on standby in the background (kind of like minimizing a program on your computer vs. clicking the X to close it.)

    If you notice that you might be doing that, try an experiment. Put your system back in Instant On mode, and then play a game. But THIS time, when you back out to the home screen, highlight the large window (current game) and press Menu --> Quit. This should free up the memory being used by that game/app. THEN, shut down, start back up, and try a game and see if it plays any more smoothly.

    The fact that a full shut down (hard boot) fixes this, and that you don't encounter it when the system is not in Instant On mode makes me think this might be the issue, because when the system goes into Standby or Hard Boots, it basically clears the memory every time, as opposed to Instant On, which keeps that memory in use.

    If that DOESN'T apply to you or doesn't seem to work, then that's a puzzle! Let me know, and we'll see if we can troubleshoot some more! :-)

    capnphoton say:

    I would just like to add that a hard reset can help, but it is not a full shutdown and restart. Look for a moment at Windows. If you were just to press the power button every time until it shuts off, eventually Windows would have major issues and have to be reinstalled. This is because it does not go through a full shutdown unloading files, and putting things in their place so to speak. The same is true for your Xbox. Mine goes through the normal shutdown and restart (not hard reset) each day and I very rarely have issues.
