¡firedog say:

It seemed a little scary to me to deselect that security setting,

The setting on the Security tab of Windows Live Mail's Safety Options dialogue Do not allow attachments that could potentially be a virus to be saved or opened dates from the early years of Outlook Express when virus delivery methods were as primitive as the measures taken to avoid them. Now that both the bad guys and virus protection have moved on in the intervening 15 years or so, the setting should be regarded as an anachronism. If you're careful about not opening attachments you're not expecting, your real-time anti-virus will protect you from any of the dangers that this setting would protect you from. In other words, this option need not be selected on any OS later than Windows XP SP1. It's wise to run Windows Live Mail in the Restricted security zone, but running it in the Internet zone will not normally be problematical for careful users.
