cmdrkeene say:

You seem to be confusing two different things. 

Microsoft Accounts are online usernames that you can use to connect to a website like this, or Xbox, or Hotmail.  They can be either primary or alias, and both work the same way.  If you login to a website using an alias, you get the full abilities you would have if you had used the primary. There's no difference at all, because they're all just separate names for the same account.

Windows Accounts are on your computer, just like always (since Windows 95 anyway).  They can be either standard or administrator, and as you know, administrators get full acces to install programs and make system changes (such as changing networking settings).

That all being said, it sounds to me like you want to just change which email account your computer is connected to.  In that case, just go to PC Settings > Account.  You'll see the old email address at the top of the screen.  You can disconnect it and then connect to your new address.
