tonyupchurch say: I have come up with a way to estimate the usable available space.  I used the PowerShell command:

Set-StoragePool -FriendlyName "Pool Name" -ThinProvisioningAlertThresholds 85.1

I changed the value of the Threshold until I found the exact percentage that the warning in the Manage Storage Spaces window stops being display.  Then I calculate the useable space by dividing the amount of data on the drive by the percentage I found in the first step.  In my case I had 833GB of data on my drive and my alert turned off at 94.0 so 833/0.940 = 886GB.  I then filled up the drive and ran out of space at 888GB.  This method will work to find the useable space within less than 0.1 percent.  You can tune this in by using decimal percentages such as 85.1, 85.2.  I tested the threshold and it worked up to 1 decimal place in my case, but could be dependent on the size of the pool.  Hopefully, someone will have a better answer in the future.
