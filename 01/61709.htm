randyau93 say:

The current Microsoft Store Terms of Sale are rather clear in stating that refunds are not granted on digital purchases. It is true that Microsoft is reviewing changing the terms to permit refunds on purchases made within the previous 14 days and that have been played for no more than two (2) hours, but that change has NOT yet gone into effect. Only a subset of Xbox Insider Program participants have been granted the privilege.

I, along with others, do have that privilege, and presuming that the proposed rules do not change in the final release, here is a subset of what you can and cannot refund:

CAN REFUND
-Xbox One games (both AAA and indie games downloaded to your Microsoft account) that have been played for less than two hours and that have been purchased in the past 14 days

MAY REFUND POTENTIALLY
-Xbox One durable DLC (season passes, expansion packs)


CANNOT REFUND
-Xbox 360 games (both AAA and XBLA games) and other content
-Consumable DLC (things like premium currency - VBucks in Fortnite, for instance)

In practice, refundable content will have a link adjacent to it in your Microsoft account purchase history that says "request a refund". If the link is not present, the content is not (or no longer) refundable.

Again, the key point here is that the rules have NOT changed - when you buy a game, you are agreeing there are no refunds. If one is granted, it is in extreme circumstances (such as how "Fortnite" was released before they made Battle Royale free).

Thinking about the interaction between a margin, a word, and a particular zoom I feel that this must simply be an artifact of WordPad trying to keep a word in the margin at an "odd" zoom %. To prevent artifacts due to zoom I imagine WordPad actually scales down the font size of text as you zoom out, and scales it up as you zoom in.
