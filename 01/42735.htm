cmdrkeene say:

There is a gesture of swiping in from the left edge of the screen will switch to the previously used app.  I bet that's what's happening and it's not really specific only to the store/tips apps.

Actually Microsoft doesn't make the touchpad follow those same gestures (it's only supposed to work on touch screens) but many laptop makers try to helpfully add the same gesture to the touchpad.  We see it all the time here.

Unfortunately every manufacturer gets to decide how, where (and even IF) to provide options for the touchpad gestures, so I can't specifically tell you were to go to turn it off.  But most companies put it here:

1 - Press +R to show the Run box, type MAIN.CPL and press OK.

2 - Look for touchpad settings. If you don't see any, go to the Hardware tab, choose the touchpad from the list and click settings.

If you still don't see it, you're going to have to ask the company that built the laptop where the settings are.  Toshiba, for example, makes you download the settings program separately yourself.

cmdrkeene say:

"Sometimes it decides to open an app (not always windows store),"

That means you swiped in from the left edge, which causes you to go back to a previously-used app (like flicking through a magazine).  You can turn that option off in the options if you want.
