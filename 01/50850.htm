earl of grey say:

After having done extensive reading on this subject, I believe I have 'solved' my problem. Actually, it's not so much a solution as it is a greater understanding of how the Administrators group has been hardened in Windows 7 (and possibly in Vista as well.)

In Windows XP, for example, a member of the Administrators group had unchallenged Full Control. Even if a user ran as Limited (known as Standard in Windows 7), there are attack vectors that allow the malware to elevate itself to Administrator rights, allowing the malware unchallenged control of the machine.

In Windows 7 (and possibly Vista), there are some permissions and rights where Administrators do not have unchallenged Full Control, but instead they have permission to take Full Control. This requires additional steps on the part of the Administrator that malware cannot perform...yet.

A similar protection is afforded with the introduction of the TrustedInstaller security principal. This prevents malware from installing software that modifies the Windows kernel, even if the malware is running as Administrator.
