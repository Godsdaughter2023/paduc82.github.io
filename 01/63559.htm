rob koch say:

I typically recommend that non-technical users stay out of such highly technical areas of the operating system unless they plan to take courses in Windows management to understand what these truly mean.

You're more likely to cause yourself problems than find or fix anything if you don't know exactly what you're doing.  At least you had the sense to ask before doing anything.

As for your specific questions, you dropped the 's' from the first item, which is the Local Administrators group associated with the PC.  All Administrator users are a member of this group and this is how they gain their permissions to access any file or folder.  The DESKTOP-random digits is an arbitrary name provided to the PC when it was initially setup.

administrators (DESKTOP-1l9tr80/administrators)

Interactive User is associated with the currently logged in user who launched the program, as explained more technically in the following Microsoft Developer Network (MSDN) document.

Interactive User (COM)

As you can see, there's often reasonable explanations for most items in Windows that may seem alien to someone without lots of technical background, so your own assumption that you should "do nothing" is typically best.  Even if there's a problem it's not likely to be obvious to anyone without many years to background using and managing Windows systems.

How to organize files and folders

Hello,

 

In 'My Documents' I have about 400 items - mostly saved from the internet and some 'word' documents. I need to arrange them by putting selected items in different folders (new folders).  I know how to create new folders but I don't know how to include selected items in the new folders I create. Please tell me how to go about this. It would be most helpful if I can have step by step instructions. My OS is Windows 7 Home Premium.

Thank you.

tauheedul ali say:

There are loads of ways of doing this...

 

Method 1 - Select A Range of Random Individual Files and move to New Folder

  • First create your folder and give it a name
  • Then to select individual items from the list, click on one of the items and hold onto CTRL on your keyboard while you select the other items you want individually.
  • Then once you've selected the ones you need, all of the files you clicked will be highlighted.
  • Then, Right click on any of the highlighted items and click CUT (or press CTRL and X together)
  • Then, open up your new folder and right click on the empty area and click PASTE (or press CTRL and V together on your keyboard).

 

Method 2 - Select Range of Files By Type and move to New  Folder

  • Right click on an empty white area of the folder, and click Sort By > Select Type this will automatically rearrange all the items by the file type.
  • Then using your mouse, click an empty area near the range of files you want, and drag your mouse to highlight the list of files you want.
  • Then press CTRLand X together
  • Now open the new folder you want to move it to, and press CTRL and V together.

 

Method 3 - Drag and Drop Range of Files in to New Folder

You can highlight the range of files with your mouse,

Then on one of the highlighted files but don't release the left-click.

Note: Keep your finger pressed down on the left click, and drag and drop the collection of files into the new folder.

 

That's it. See Full Step By Step Guide With Images Here...
