THE ISSUE IS "WHAT IS IT"!

Hi Michelle,

I am a real person, and I will answer this for you!

InstallAgentUserBroker.exe is used / called by the Windows Store app to download / update a program or app.

This program checks and communicates with the local machine registry certificates and networking settings.

Another called InstallAgent.exe talks between the file and registry part of the system and creates checkpoints.

This does run sort of high at times but depending on the ENTIRE environment conditions (hardware/software) this could vary.

If you don't already have a solid state drive, now is a good time to start looking, you will barely (if at all) notice any of this anymore!

Hi Damian,

Set your data connection to a metered connection:

To change your wifi connection,

Open the Settings app>

Network & Internet > Wi-FI, and click “Manage Known Networks”.

Choose your Wi-Fi network and click “Properties”.

From there, enable the “Set as metered connection” option. 

========================================================

Not Recommended: Disable Automatic Updates and then update the system only when you can use wifi elsewhere, or at the end of your internet billing cycle you can and do the updates if you have data left. 

If you have a phone billing cycle that is metered, you could connect your system to your phone hotspot at the end of your billing cycle and use up any data leftover to update then.

I do know that there are often updates small as they may be that happen with other software each time you connect to the internet, depending on your settings, which can affect your data usage.

To configure Windows Updates, use the following:

I DO NOT RECOMMEND THIS IN CASE OF AN ISSUE LIKE THIS, DEFINITELY NEED UPDATES

Navigate to Computer Configuration\Administrative Templates\Windows Components\Windows Update. Locate the “Configure Automatic Updates” setting in the right pane and double-click it.

Set it to your preferred setting
