Double Nat 2 xboxs -1 wired 1 wireless - Microsoft Community

asunderf say:

Double Nat 2 xboxs -1 wired 1 wireless

So I have a Archer c50 ( https://www.tp-link.com/us/home-networking/wifi-router/archer-c50/) model in brackets as cannot find from drop down selection.  that is running one xbox one s through ethernet and one via wifi and I have the double nat issue and strict nat type.  Please help 
arminatorx say:

A double NAT happens, when two NAT devices (i.e. routers) are used to connect your home to the Internet. 

Is your Archer C50 connected with some kind of Broadband Modem you got from your Internet Provider (a cable modem or DSL modem)?

Check if that Modem is actually a router itself (i.e. a gateway device), allowing multiple devices to connect to the internet. If so, contact your ISP on how to set up the gateway to "pass through" mode or "direct mode". 

If you connect the Archer C50 directly to the Internet (for example via Glass Fibre), it also might be, that your Internet Provider has more customers than free IPv4 adresses, and therefore uses Carrier Grade NAT (CG-NAT) and IPv6 tunneling techniques like DS-Lite to connect your home to the internet. 

You could imagine the ISP having a huge Archer C50 on their premises, and connecting your whole neighborhood or city to one of their own Internet lines. 

If your ISP does do CG-NAT through DS-Lite, please contact your Internet Provider and ask them for a public IPv4 Address. 

Some Providers will give you that for free, others want a monthly fee for a public IPv4 address, and some are unable to give you a public IPv4 address. 

If they can't give you a public IPv4 address, there is no way other than changing your Internet Provider. 

Should you be able to get a public IPv4, or set your modem to pass through mode, you need to go to the Network Settings of each of your consoles, and choose a custom port, different for each console.

After setting a different port for your consoles, set up UPnP or manual Port Forward rules for these ports to the specific console. 

You can find instructions on how to forward ports on https://www.portforward.com (just be aware, that they advertise their software to "help" you. But simply follow the manual instructions for your router, and you're good to go).
