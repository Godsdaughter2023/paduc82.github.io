harry stevens say:

Hi,

Windows 10 Activation works by registering your hardware specifications on Microsoft's Activation Center, after you upgraded for free. If you replaced your computer hardware (Especially Motherboard), the Activation Center won't recognize your system anymore, and thus, no longer activated.

It would be best to contact Microsoft Support for this issue and explain the replacement of your hardware. Prepare proof of purchase or product key of your previous OS when you upgraded your system to Windows 10.

And to answer your question, no your Windows will not be inoperative. It will still continue receiving Windows Updates, but there will be limitations like you won't be able to personalize your computer, and there is a watermark on your desktop that your Windows' not activated.
