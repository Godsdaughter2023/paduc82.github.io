slance310 say:

Hi,

 

To start, Win 7+ 8 x64 can in fact read a 3TB HDD, although booting from it may be an issue.....

 

The only requirement  to use a 3TB disk in Win 8 x64 is that it must be GPT partition type instead of MBR.

 

If you have nothing important on the disk yet, plug it in via USB, delete the partition(s), change it to GPT, and reformat....or better yet...just do it internally.

 

Incidently...if this drive was in a external enclosure to start with, Seagate includes some trickery in the chipsets in the enclosure to make the drives compatible with 32bit OS's....

 

When taken out of the enclosure, the partitioning is entirely different than what it appears while in the enclosure...

 

The reason I mention this is that I and many other people have done just that...buy an external drive and then take it apart for the drive inside...it's cheaper that way a lot of times.
