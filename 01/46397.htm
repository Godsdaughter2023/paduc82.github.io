    ron sommer mvp 2009-2017 say:

    t-brall say:

    I know this is an old question, but in case other people have the same question:

    Yes, just make/edit a Rule in Outlook to display alerts for messages you are sending to that folder.


    Presuming you've already made some rule that is sorting mail to whatever custom folder you have.

    1. Click "Rules" in the ribbon menu, which will drop down the rules menu

    2. Then click "Manage Rules & Alerts"

    3. Then find your rule in the list of rules shown, and left-click it so it is highlighted

    4. Then click "Change Rule", then "Edit Rule Settings"

    5. Click Next to pass the "Select conditions" page for that rule, to get to the "Select actions" page

    6. Then just select "Display a Desktop Alert" as one of the actions to take on the messages that match the rule.  Notice you can also make it play a sound, and lots of other options

    And if you don't have a rule or folder already, you can do all of this through the Advanced Options when you Create Rule.

    You can even turn off alerts for Inbox (File->Options->Mail->Message Arrival), and leave the other alerts on.

    Disclaimer:  I'm answering this as a fellow user, and I am on Outlook Desktop Client 2013 on Windows--potentially this functionality is new since this question was asked, or not available in every, but I'll bet it works for most people
