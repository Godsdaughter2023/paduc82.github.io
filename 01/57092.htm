Hi,

What is the operating system installed on the computer?

 

I would like to let you know that when you buy a new Microsoft-supported computer, chances are that it will be packed with dozens of preinstalled programs. Some of these programs are truly free and quite useful, but many of the programs are simply trial versions that are preinstalled with the hope that you will purchase the full versions.

The applications /programs like Microsoft Visual C++, Microsoft SQL Server are required to run other applications/programs. Many other applications depend on these applications to run properly. Uninstalling Microsoft applications or older versions may result in some programs not working.

 

Try these steps.

1-Click the "Start" button in Windows and select "Control Panel" from the Start Menu.

2- Double-click the "Add or Remove Programs" or "Programs and Features" icon. This will open a programs list, but it may take a minute or two for Windows to populate the entire list.

3-You need to browse the list from top to bottom. Whenever you see a preinstalled program that you don't want, left-click it to highlight it, then click the "Remove" or "Uninstall" button that appears next to the entry. After you click this button, you will likely need to confirm the decision to uninstall the program and you may need to click a "Finish" button at the end of the process. The specific steps involved vary from one program to the next, but this process is largely automated for every piece of software. Continue this process until you've uninstalled all unwanted software using the "Add or Remove Programs" or "Programs and Features" window.

4- Browse the program icons on the desktop. In most cases, when you uninstall a program through the "Add or Remove Programs" or “Programs and Features" window, the desktop icon will be automatically deleted. Some programs, however, do not do this. If you see any icons for programs you've deleted, drag and drop them to the Recycle Bin.

5- Click the "Start" button in Windows and move your cursor to the "Programs" heading. Browse the list of programs on the program menu. Just as some uninstallation processes fail to remove desktop icons, some will also fail to remove program list entries. If you see an entry for a program you've deleted, right-click on the main program folder and select "Delete" from the pop-up menu.

Your Windows Product ID: 00359-233-2266705-86545

Lately there have been several reports of instances of blocked MSDN keys packaged with legitimate looking media. I can't stress enough to save your packaging, media and reciepts, if you purchase software such as this seek recourse with the store you purchased it from and report it to www.microsoft.com/piracy

If you have purchased a key, with or without DVD's or packaging from EBAY or any other person or vendor and believe it may be an MSDN key because your key has been blocked you can check a few things yourself, if you received your key with the physical product go to www.microsoft.com/howtotelland verify your product however some counterfeit software is so convincing you may not be able to spot it as such.

To check your PID press the win key and pause at the same time, or open system properties through the control panel, scroll down to activation section and look at the PID It will look something like this 12345-067-1234567-54321, the portion you are interested in is the three digit section, if it matches or is within one or two digits of the examples below you probably have an MSDN key.

065, 066, 067, 068, 069 for Ultimate, 220, 221, 222 area for Professional and the 230, 231, 221, 232,

About MSDN or TechNet Product Keys

They are genuine Microsoft product keys, they are actually retail licenses, but it is intended for a particular product channel either the Microsoft Software Developer Network (MSDN) or TechNet for IT Professionals who pay a subscription fee. The main purpose is for evaluation purposes. The great thing about them, unlike trial Microsoft software, MSDN or TechNet keys don't expire. Because the agreement under which the subscription is provided is a single license, none of the software should be distributed outside of it. Even though its $50,000 worth of licenses, it is for one person only to use and no one else. Unfortunately, regardless of the licensing terms, persons still abuse the program, either giveaway product keys or resell it on auction sites. Microsoft licenses it in good faith that customers won't do so, but I guess human nature wins out.

In your case, what probably happened is, you bought an MSDN licensed key, which carries up to 10 activations unlike full packaged retail licenses which only carry 1 activation. The person who sold it to you probably sold it to 10 other persons. Somewhere along the way, one of those persons might have installed it on a second system, activated it, because it went past 10 activation threshold, Microsoft detected it that it was being abused and blocked the key from further use.
