Hey Peter,

I'm seeing a few things here...

 

  1. In most cases, the IP address to access your router is 192.168.1.1 as opposed to your 168.192.1.1. It's still possible that it's different for your specific router, but it's worth a shot.
  2. The IP address that your router is handing out to your is a public IP address, not a private IP address. To properly understand this I have to go into a little bit of basic home networking. The idea behind having your router is to build a private network with IP addresses similar to 192.168.1.100 and have them all share a single public IP address that your ISP (Internet Service Provider) gives you. In your case, your pubic IP address starts with 70.188. (I'm leaving the rest out of the post and have edited your post above to remove it, it's considered personal information).
So to me, the issue is that your router isn't creating a private network for you. If you had hit the reset button on the router, it's possible that you erased all your personal settings on the router. If that's the case, you should really contact your ISP (Cox) and have them help you reconfigure the router.
