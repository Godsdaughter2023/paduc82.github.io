How to print a label and case cover for DVD - Microsoft Community

bake50 say:

How to print a label and case cover for DVD

Print a Label and Cover for a DVD

How do you print a label for a dvd and a cover for the case using Windows 7?

azeez n say: Hi Bake50,

Thank you for using Microsoft Windows forum.

LightScribe is a technology developed by Hewlett-Packard that allows you to print directly on your discs. First, you burn the data side of the disc and then you flip it over and burn the label. Not only it is easier, it also gives your discs a more professional appearance. You can use your own pictures as well as add text.
What’s required?

The requirements aren't lengthy, but they are pretty important. LightScribe is actually a system that depends on interaction between your hardware, software, and media.


Hardware

For starters, your optical disc drive must be LightScribe enabled. Many new computers come with optical drives that are LightScribe enabled. You can tell whether it is LightScribe enabled by looking at the front of the disc tray. If the LightScribe logo appears there, then you’re all set to go.

If you don't see the LightScribe logo, don't worry. You can buy a LightScribe external drive, or even replace your internal drive with one that's LightScribe enabled. For more information about upgrading your computer's optical drive, check with your computer manufacturer. You can also find more information about LightScribe drives at the LightScribe website .


 Software

All of the software that you need should be included with the drive. If you have an internal drive, it’s likely that the LightScribe system software (the software that controls the drive itself) is already installed. If not, you can download a generic version from the LightScribe website .

You’ll also need disc-labeling software. Again, in most cases, disc-labeling software comes with your hardware. Additionally, you might find many disc-burning programs (such as Nero and Roxio) come with label designers that support LightScribe. For more information about disc-labeling software that supports LightScribe, visit the LightScribe website .


 Media

The final piece is the disc itself. You must use LightScribe discs that are specially coated to allow the laser in the drive to etch the surface of the disc. You can find LightScribe media at most places that carry blank CD's and DVD's.

Printing labels

This is where the fun begins. Find that great shot of the Eiffel tower and use your label-designing software to add it to your discs. You can also add your own text. Check the documentation that came with your disc-labeling software for information about how to create and print LightScribe labels.

If you're looking for design ideas, check out the LightScribe website which has some great ideas and templates that can help get you started.


For more information see : http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows-vista/Forget-the-Sharpie-Create-your-own-disc-labels ( also applicable for Windows 7)

Hey dude,

Just because you both have the same NAT's, doesn't mean you'll be able to play together. What type of NAT do you both have?

Here is a description of each of the NAT types:

Open NAT: With an OPEN NAT type, you’re able to chat with other people, as well as join and host multiplayer games with people who have any NAT type on their network.

Moderate NAT: With a MODERATE NAT type, you’re able to chat and play multiplayer games with some people; however, you might not be able to hear or play with others, and normally you won’t be chosen as the host of a match.

Strict NAT: With a STRICT NAT type, you’re only able to chat and play multiplayer games with people who have an OPEN NAT type. You can’t be chosen as the host of a match.

So if you are either moderate or strict, that could be the reason. Some further tips on how you can solve the problem can be found here: support.xbox.com/.../nat-error-solution
