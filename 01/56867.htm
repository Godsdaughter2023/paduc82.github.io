Sam,

Hit Windows key + R to launch the "Run" command and then copy/paste the following command and click OK:
Reg add HKLM\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Services\BITS\Parameters /v ServiceDll /t REG_EXPAND_SZ /d %windir%\System32\qmgr.dll

Reboot the machine before you do anything else.

If you get the same error again, run this command next and then reboot again:
Reg add HKLM\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Control\BackupRestore\FilesNotToBackup

Don't skip rebooting because you're making changes to the Windows Registry. They have no effect until you reboot.

Hello PCUserGrouper,


Here are the steps necessary to take pictures or video with the Surface RT Camera.


Shoot videos or take photos

 

The following info is for using the built-in Camera app. If you’re using another camera app from the Windows Store, the steps might vary.

 

Shoot a video

 

  1. From Start, tap or click Camera.
  2. Tap or click Video mode.
  3. The icon toggles between video and photo modes.
  4. Tap or click Change camera to switch between the front and rear cameras.
  5. A small privacy LED light displays near the active camera.
  6. Tap the screen to start recording video.
  7. A timer appears in the lower-right corner of the screen.
  8. When you’re done, tap the screen to stop recording.

Tips

 

  • Tap or click Camera options to change settings like video stabilization, brightness, and contrast.
  • Tap or click Timer if you want a 3-second delay after you tap the screen to take a video.
  • Videos you shoot with Surface are saved in the Camera Roll album in the Pictures library, accessible in the Photos app. For info on how to play videos, see Play videos and import video clips.

 

Take a photo

 

  1. From Start, tap or click Camera.
  2. Make sure Video mode is not selected.
  3. Video mode toggles between video and photo modes.
  4. Tap or click Change camera to switch between the front and back cameras.
  5. Tap the screen to take a photo.


Tips

 

  • Tap or click Camera options to change settings like resolution, brightness, or contrast.
  • Tap or click Timer if you want a 3-second delay after you tap the screen to take a photo.
  • Not hearing the camera shutter sound? Check your volume settings.
  • The pictures that you take with Surface are saved in the Camera Roll album in the Pictures library, accessible in the Photos app. For info on how to view and share your photos
To view the photos and videos you've taken, please visit this link.

james.burgess say: You can also make a USB stick install from the ISO, which I find much faster

First mount the ISO, using a virtual drive software (alcohol 54%, PowerISO, Virtual Clone drive)

On Win7
insert USB
open command prompt (as administrator)
each of these commands should be run in order

type diskpart
list disk
select disk (number of the USB stick)
clean
create partition primary
format fs=ntfs QUICK
active
assign

From the mounted ISO, goto D:\boot (or whatever the DVD drive letter is)
at the D:\boot\ prompt
type bootsect.exe /nt60 X: (where x: is the drive letter of the usb)
this makes the usb stick bootable.
then just copy the entire contents of the DVD onto the USB stick.
job done!

now you never have to worry about scratches or anything on your DVD, and can enjoy a lightning fast install.

Also make sure you download the correct Win 7 media, there are a few different types. You probably need an OEM Disk, not Retail (RTM), or Volume Licence (VLK)
