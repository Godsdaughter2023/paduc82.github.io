The bubbles sound like phantom Touch taps being detected by the screen.  That is frequently accepted as an explanation for this symptom.  If it is too much of a disruption you can try disabling the Touch screen or, if possible, just its Multi-Touch feature.  Doing the latter would allow you to use a digitizing stylus (but not a capacitive one).  It would probably affect some of your Touchpad functionality as well.  This is a problem which could conceivably be helped by recalibrating your Touch Screen (e.g. spurious interrupts which would make no sense when seen with normal expected interrupts might be learned to be ignored that way.)  FWIW this happened to me with my TouchSmart which also was based on Ntrig device support.

                   confirmation is desired.
