tzon say:

Hi,

First of all, all storage media (from DVDs to flash sticks) have smaller real capacities than the ones advertised. This is due to the counting method. In science, Giga means 1,000,000,000 or 10^9. In information technology one Gigabyte equals to 1,024^3 bytes, not 1,000^3 bytes. But companies consider Giga as in science, so when they offer 1 GB they actually offer 0.931 GB. In your case 128 * 0.931 = 119 GB.

Another reason why the capacity appears reduced can be hidden partitions that your system has reserved to work properly. Hidden partitions can also include a recovery image of your system, in case you need to restore your PC to factory defaults. A recovery image may require a large amount of space, that's why your first drive's capacity appears significantly reduced. You can see hidden partitions through Disk Management, following Gordon B-P's instructions.

Finally, there is a feature called over-provisioning that SSDs use to improve performance and expand their life. This feature reserves though at least another 10% of the drive's capacity. It is not enabled in all models, but Samsung for sure uses it a lot.

If you want to gain some free space on your disks, try using the Disk Cleanup tool following the instructions here http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/delete-files-using-disk-cleanup#delete-files-using-disk-cleanup=windows-8 . You could also delete the hidden recovery partition and expand the C:\ partition via Disk Management, but this is not recommended.
