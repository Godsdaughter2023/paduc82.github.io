Yes, there is. To do so, we suggest that you boot in safe mode through the Windows Recovery Environment option. To do this, follow these steps:

  1. Turn on your computer. When the Windows logo appears, unplug and replug the device. Perform these steps three times.
  2. On the fourth instance, turn on your device without unplugging it. The Windows Recovery Environment window will appear.
  3. On the Choose an option screen, click Troubleshoot.
  4. Click Advance options.
  5. Click Startup Settings.
  6. Click Restart.
  7. Once your computer restarts, you will see a list of options. Press the F4 key to start your computer in safe mode.

Once done, we suggest that you perform a system restore after booting in safe mode. System Restore will be able to revert your PC or laptop to their previous state, which can be used to repair and recover system malfunctions or issues. Restoring won’t affect your personal files, but it will remove apps, drivers, and updates installed after the restore point was made. To do this, follow the steps below:

1. Right-click the Start button, and then select Control Panel.

2. Search Control Panel for Recovery.

3. Select Recovery > Open System Restore > Next.

4. Choose the restore point related to the problematic app, driver, or update, and then select Next > Finish.

Unfortunately, as you have seen, the higher the resolution, the smaller objects appear. This will only get worse as monitors achieve increasingly higher native resolutions. Running the monitor at a lower resolution will make objects appear bigger, but images and text will look fuzzy.

One solution is to buy a bigger monitor. That's the 'throw money it' solution but it works. Another solution is to use Windows' own Ease of Access controls to magnify portions of the screen. You can also do this with third-party screen reader software, which may have more or more convenient features.
