domcarter say:

Sometimes the simplest answers are the best.

One that I have started seeing recently is that you may need to clean the contacts of your USBC port. the best medium to use is Rubbing alcohol or Isopropyl Alcohol. USBC ports have been around long enough now to start showing this kind of issue with dust and dirt.

Turn off your computer, do not hibernate, turn it fully off and unplug from the  ACDC mains.

Use a toothbrush to gently dust the USB port, do the HDMI port also.

Use a  fully wet cotton bud (Isopropyl not water) and clean all the contacts USBC and HDMI at all points of the connection.

Leave for 5-10 minutes to dry.

Plug in and try again.
