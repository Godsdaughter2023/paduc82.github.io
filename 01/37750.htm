That happens to me from time to time and it's a simple fix. Power off your router and your modem, or your gateway if that's what you have. That's where the problem is, not Windows.

Cable companies call this 'power cycling' and it's usually the first thing they ask you to do when you call them with internet problems.

    frederik long say:

    Here you go:

    1. Log on with your fingerprint reader.
    2. Click the Start globe.
    3. Type  cmd.exe  in the Search box.
    4. Press Ctrl+Shift+Enter
    5. Click "Run as Administrator".
    6. Type the following command and press Enter:
      net user "%UserName%"  123456
      (This will set your password to 123456)
    7. Plan ahead a little and create, test and document a spare admin account, same as you have a spare house key. Having a single admin account is high-risk stuff.
