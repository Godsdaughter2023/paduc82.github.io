    I know this is old but i still wanted to answer because on this thema there still is too few information available in Internet and way too much ambiguity and lack of transparency.
    First of all, the BTD500 does NOT require any additional software to work, it is completely independent.
    It ALWAYS automatically works in Aptx when you connect an Aptx device, and in SBC when you connect a non Aptx device (SBC is the other Codec used by the A2DP profile. Aptx also works under the A2DP profile, so, when you say "I suppose I am using A2DP" it is not correct. You always use A2DP, also with APTX).
    So, if your LED does not turn purple but stays blue, then your device (Headphones) does not support Aptx or you have to enable Aptx on it (like for the Sony Mdr-1RBTMK2, or MDR-10RBT, or MDR-XB950BT, they all have three modes, High Quality, Standard, and another one. And only with High Quality you have Aptx, but they come with the Standard as default, so you must enable High Quality with a combination of buttons at startup as the Manual explains).

    Which Headphone are you using?

    Now, the Sennheiser BTD500 or Creative BT-D1 or the coming soon new dongle of Avantree are all independent from the system and working without additional drivers or software.

    The other solutions are a 3.5 adapter like the Avantree Priva (a new version coming soon with improvements).

    And then a usb dongle like the Azio BTD-V401, which needs the CSR Harmony BT Stack to be installed. Which means, you could have problems depending on which version of Windows you use, and other things.

    The only Notebook listed on the official Aptx website, a Samsung, which natively supports Aptx, uses an Intel Stack.

    As far as I know, you cannot get Aptx by just installing one of these Stacks on a normal PC with a normal BT chip. The Chip must be a particular one.

    All this is copyrighted so, not really free.

    When you use the CSR Harmony Stack, if you connect an Aptx device you will get a Popup with the Aptx logo informing you that you connected an Aptx device.

    If there is no popup, there is no Aptx connection.

    In some mobile phones it is the same, you get a popup when you connect an Aptx device. No popup = no aptx connection.

    But some smarphones do not give a popup (I find that unpractical). I think some samsungs do not give a popup. Xperia and HTC give it.

    Hope this helped you, or anybody else who may land in this thread like I did, searching for answers about this obscure theme.
