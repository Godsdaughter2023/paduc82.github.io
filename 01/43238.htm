hounddog999 say:

 I resolved this problem.

In Windows' Device Manager (devmgmt.msc), the SXUPTP driver was showing as disabled. Windows gave a warning that the device was known to have stability issues. I found out that SXUPTP is a driver from a company called Silex. Silex makes network USB hubs, so I assume that the Belkin hub is a rebranded Silex hub.

In order to update the Silex driver follow these steps:
1) Close the Belkin network hub's tray application.
2) Download the latest version of SX Virtual Link (sx-virtual_link_win_3120.zip).
3) Extract all of the files.
4) In Windows Explorer, navigate to sx-virtual_link_win_3120\s-33a3120\Files\Bin\Sxcsapi\WinVistaX64 if you are running a 64 bit OS, or sx-virtual_link_win_3120\s-33a3120\Files\Bin\Sxcsapi\WinVista if you are running a 32 bit OS.
5) Right-click on "Sxuptp.inf" and select "Install" from the menu.

The driver will be installed and the warning icon in the device manager should disappear. You may need to reboot for the changes to take effect.

Hope this helps.

charliews say:

The problem is most likely interference.

 

From your laptop, search "cmd", then pick the searched result "cmd.exe"

 

A DOS window will open, in the DOS window type this

 

          netsh wlan show all

 

All the WiFi's around your laptop will now be listed individually with it's channel number and signal strength.

 

Examine the list, and pick a channel that is least used (or not used) and has the weakest signal strength.

 

Remember, you only have channel 1 to 11 to choose from. The closer they are to each other the more interference.

 

Now go to your router, and change the channel to the one you just picked. DO NOT worry about what your router says about the picked channel.

 

What your router sees does not matter, what matters is what your laptop sees.

 

You may have to move around with your laptop in your house to find a "good" receiving location. Different location in your house may give different results. Also, you may have to repeat doing this depending on how your neighbour's WiFi's behave.
