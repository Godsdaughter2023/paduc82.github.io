Three points:

1. Defragging does not save any space. It just rearranges the used space to make access to files more efficient.

2. Deleting files doesn't save any space. It just moves the files to the recycle bin. To regain the space used by the deleted files, you need to empty the recycle bin.

3. How big is the hard drive? There are various things you can do to save some space, but they are all temporary, and the problem will recur quickly. I suspect that if you are very short, it's primarily because the drive is too small. The only real solution to the problem is replacing the drive with a bigger one. I recommend nothing smaller than 500GB, and 1TB  or even more is even better  for most people.
