    gza say:

    This (ICS-Internet Connection Sharing) is essentially using your laptop as a NAT router. I would think you could accomplish the same thing using a dedicated router. What problems were you having? I could see how ICS would offer a workaround to get the Xbox One onto a network that requires the user to authenticate via a web site before being granted full Internet access (captive portal).  Other than that, I do not see how ICS would get you Open NAT where previously you did not have it without ICS.  If your Xbox One's connection to the Internet goes through a campus- or dorm- wide NAT router that is directly connected to the Internet, then getting open NAT will require DMZ or port forwards on that router, which you presumably do not control and are unlikely to convince your IT folks to open ports through. Even if they do, that would just help you, as your Xbox would be the only one on that particular NAT network with Open NAT-- the necessary ports can only be forwarded to one Xbox at a time. That said, if on your particular campus each dorm room's ethernet port provides a direct Internet connection (with a public IP), then ICS might help beyond just the web authentication issue. I would not expect this to be the case with many/most campuses, so folks should not get their hopes up too high.

    While copying the picture files, if you are getting any error message saying that you do not have enough permission or an error message similar to “Access Denied”, then it can be resolved by taking the ownership of these files and changing the permission to “Full Control” for your user account.

    To take the ownership and to change the permission of the picture files, you may follow these steps:

    a) Login as an administrator on the computer.

    b) Press “Windows Logo” + “Q” keys on the keyboard and type “cmd” in the search box.

    c) Right click on the “Command Prompt” icon from the search results and select “Run as administrator”

    d) On the command prompt window, type the following commands, and then press “Enter”

    takeown /F “Enter the complete path of the folder where you have saved the picture files” /a /r /d y

    icacls “Enter the complete path of the folder where you have saved the picture files” /grant administrators:F /t

    e) Wait for the process to complete and at the end, it should show the message “Successfully processed “n” files …..”

    f) By this you will have the ownership of the folder where you have saved the picture files and provides “Full Control” permission to all the administrators on the computer.

    You may now try copying and pasting the pictures to another location and check if that resolves the issue.

    For more information about talking the ownership and to grant permission to a file, folder or drive, you may refer to these TechNet articles:

    Takeown

    Icacls

    For posting / sharing the pictures to your favorite social networking websites (like facebook) or photo sharing websites, you may search for an appropriate app in the Windows Store which can perform this activity. Then, you may download it from Windows Store and install it on the computer.
