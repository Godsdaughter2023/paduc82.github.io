kid neru say:

Hi Harry,

I will have a go.

Your devices will be assigned IP address by your DHCP server. For most home networks this will be on your gateway router (i.e. the device connecting your Internet and all your devices)

I expect if you log on to your router you will see that it has an IP in the 192.168.x.x. range and any device connecting to it and using it as the DHCP server will be assigned a 192.168.x.x address. As such I expect your PC is also displaying an IP address in the 192.168.x.x range.

I am guessing you are trying somehow to use your PC as a bridge for your XBOX to your Internet connection - I have never tried to connect my XBOX to the Internet this way so would need to look in to it to advise how to do this correctly.

As for why your XBOX is getting the 169.254.x.x address it would appear this is because it is unable to locate the DHCP server and is therefore not being assigned an IP address from your routers IP pool.

Google states the following;

The 169.254.x.x range of IP addresses is reserved by Microsoft for private network addressing. If you have a pc set to automatically obtain an IP and you receive one of these addresses, windows has assigned this because it cannot find a DHCP server within the network subnet.

I expect if you carry your XBOX over to your router an plug it in directly it will receive a 192.168.x.x address as expected.

As such the problem looks to be in how you have interconnected devices. The router does not see the XBOX, the XBOX does not see the router - perhaps it is trying to obtain IP address from the PC, which is not acting as a DHCP server.

Without looking in to using a PC as an intermediary so not sure if it will work or not in daisy chained scenario;

You could try assigning static IP to the XBOX within the same network subnet. For example if your router is on 192.168.0.1 and it assigns your PC 192.168.0.2, you could statically assign your XBOX 192.168.0.3 for example. If you regularly connect multiple devices to your wireless you may want to leave a bit of a gap to avoid any IP conflicts with those devices being dynamically assigned IP's.

For example if you have your PC and perhaps phone and a tablet connected to the wireless then 3 IPs from the routers IP pool will be assigned - for example PC 192.168.0.2, Phone 192.168.0.3, Tablet 192.168.0.4. In this scenario may better assigning a static of 192.168.0.10 for the XBOX as an example.

I am not sure it is going to be quite as simple as that though (pause)

Ok, gave in and had a look -  a quick check and you probably need to check your Internet sharing settings on your PC - managed to find this guide, may help;

XBOX connection sharing

The best option though is to direct connect - if you have a lot of distance between your XBOX and router and don't want to string CAT-5 through the house then can invest in units that leverage your houses electricity cabling for network connectivity. Plug in one module into power socket in the room you have your XBOX, plug XBOX in here with network cable. Plug another module into power socket near your router, connect network cable from here to spare LAN port on router. Job done. Generally a faster (network speed) solution than using wireless, let alone sharing wireless. Also the router will see the XBOX directly and vice versa so much easier for DHCP to work correctly.

Check here for example units;

Amazon - Netgear Power Adapters

I use Netgear as example as have used their products (routers and power adapters) for years without any issues - very reliable, but obviously take the time to look and purchase what makes you happy.

Hope this helps a bit. Let us know how you get on.
