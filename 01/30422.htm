marc cai say:

Hello,


It seemed that your PC has enabled the optimized resolution for your PC, supported by your Graphic Processor, but not by the screen. Try to boot your PC with low resolution.

In order to boot in low resolution, you will need to access into Diagnostic Mode/Automatic repair. Turn your computer on and off three times. You will need to work with your senses here, since you have to be sure that you will turn off the PC when it's on the welcoming Windows logo without seeing it on the screen. After the third time, Windows will boot into diagnostics mode. Click Advanced options when the Automatic Repair screen appears.

From Advanced options, click Startup Settings and click Restart. Now the Startup Settings screen will be revealed. Press the designated key for Enable low-resolution. Once it successfully booted to your desktop, you may now set the screen resolution just as it was before.
