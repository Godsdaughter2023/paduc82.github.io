Hi CochraneTimeLord,

Yes, it can be done, but it is a registry hack, you have to fool the registry into thinking that external drive is in fact a fixed drive . . .

The reason Windows is set up to not run from an external drive is simply a licensing issue!

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

Opening a network port has nothing to do with increasing your connection speed.  Think of a large house with a row of doors on the front wall.  The house is your network connection.  Each door is a port.  Your web browser always uses one (port 80) or two (port 443) of those doors.  Your mail client uses a couple as well (port 110 and port 25 or ports 995 and 465).  Each of the different network protocols can use only specific doors.  Opening more doors does not increase the rate at which informaiton can leave or enter your PC because the information of a specific type can go only through specific doors and it doesn't matter which door the traffic uses.  It always goes full speed through whichever door it must use.

Your network attachment point (usually a router) is a determining factor in what speed your network connection can achieve.  While it's true there are some settings you can adjust on your PC (like the trasnmission packet size), it's rare that adjusting any of them will have pronounced effect on the speed at which your traffic flows.

Port forwarding allows the network traffic coming into your network through specific ports outside your router to be sent through different ports on the inside of the router, which is your internal LAN.  The router performs a process called Network Address Translation.  This protects your systems inside your network so that their ports and IP addresses are hidden from the outside network and makes them more difficult to access from the unsafe external network.
