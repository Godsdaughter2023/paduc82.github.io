val_h_574 say:

I have the same issue as well this is what worked for me: (I have a dell xps 13 9380)

1. Plug in headphone and restart your computer. Keep your headphones plugged in. For me my headphones worked right away (so the jack wasn't messed up). BUT when I removed my headphones and tried to plug it back it still wasn't detecting. 

P.S. I am no means a computer expert, this is just how I got my headphones to detect again.

2. Uninstall Realtek(R) Audio and manually downloaded the driver from Dell - the system creates a restore point automatically if it doesn't you can create one. 

3. Do a system restore to that specific restore point (when you installed the Realtek(R) Audio driver from Dell.)

4. After its done, open device manager (enable view hidden devices) and check to see if any devices have issues (Eg. are your speakers running an error?)

5. Under system devices find a device with the name Intel(R) SmartSound Technology something. It shouldn't be hidden. I rolled back the driver - it reverted to "High Definition Audio controller". After this I was able to plug in my headphones and the computer could detect it. 

I don't know if the issue will return but is there an easier way to fix it? My way is very tedious and I only managed this through sheer luck (I think).
