Here’s what you can do:

  • Visit this link: Update drivers in Windows 10 to know how to perform a driver update for your devices.
  • Make sure that your device/peripheral is inserted properly (plugged tightly into their port).
  • Your BIOS (Basic input/output system) should be the latest version (you can download this update from your computer manufacturer website).
  • Use the Device Manager to detect your computer for hardware changes. To do this, follow the steps below:
    1. Make sure that you are connected to the Internet.
    2. Press Windows + R to open  Run window.
    3. Type devmgmt.msc, then press Enter to launch Device Manager.
    4. In the Device Manager, click your computer so it will be highlighted.
    5. Click Action, then click Scan for hardware changes.
    6. Wait for your computer to scan the hardware that were attached to it.

This tool has the latest version of Windows 10 (Version 1709 Fall Creators update) that will give you fresh copy of files for your computer.
