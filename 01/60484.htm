szz743 say:

Hope I can help to someone.

I had the same issue with the fresh Windows 8.1 Pro.

Couldn't find answer so had to debug Windows to find a solution.

 

"Microsoft Link-Layer Discovery Protocol" binary is \Windows\system32\DRIVERS\mslldp.sys

Its config registry key is HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Services\MsLldp

 

During backup a VSS process running under NETWORK_SERVICE account calls cryptcatsvc!CSystemWriter::AddLegacyDriverFiles(), which enumerates all the drivers records in Service Control Manager database and tries opening each one of them. , The function fails on MSLLDP record with "Access Denied" error.

 

Turned out it fails because MSLLDP driver's security permissions do not allow NETWORK_SERVICE to access the driver record.

 

The binary security descriptor for the record is located here:

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Services\MsLldp\Security

 

It should be modified, I used SC.EXE and Sysinternals' ACCESSCHK.EXE to fix it.

 

The original security descriptor looked like below:

 

>accesschk.exe -c mslldp

mslldp
  RW NT AUTHORITY\SYSTEM
  RW BUILTIN\Administrators
  RW S-1-5-32-549       <- these are server operators
  R  NT SERVICE\NlaSvc

 

No service account is allowed to access MSLLDP driver

 

The security descriptor for the drivers that were processed successfully looked this way:

 

>accesschk.exe -c mup

mup
  RW NT AUTHORITY\SYSTEM
  RW BUILTIN\Administrators
  R  NT AUTHORITY\INTERACTIVE
  R  NT AUTHORITY\SERVICE  <- this gives access to services

 

How to add access rights for NT AUTHORITY\SERVICE to MSLLDP service:

 

1. Run: SC sdshow MSLLDP

You'll get something like below (SDDL language is documented on MSDN):

 

D:(D;;CCDCLCSWRPWPDTLOCRSDRCWDWO;;;BG)(A;;CCDCLCSWRPWPDTLOCRSDRCWDWO;;;SY)(A;;CCDCLCSWRPDTLOCRSDRCWDWO;;;BA)(A;;CCDCLCSWRPWPDTLOCRSDRCWDWO;;;SO)(A;;LCRPWP;;;S-1-5-80-3141615172-2057878085-1754447212-2405740020-3916490453)S:(AU;FA;CCDCLCSWRPWPDTLOCRSDRCWDWO;;;WD)

 

2. Run: SC sdshow MUP

You'll get:

 

D:(A;;CCLCSWRPWPDTLOCRRC;;;SY)(A;;CCDCLCSWRPWPDTLOCRSDRCWDWO;;;BA)(A;;CCLCSWLOCRRC;;;IU)(A;;CCLCSWLOCRRC;;;SU)S:(AU;FA;CCDCLCSWRPWPDTLOCRSDRCWDWO;;;WD)

 

3. Take NT AUTHORITY\ SERVICE entry, which is (A;;CCLCSWLOCRRC;;;SU) and add it to the original MSLLDP security descriptor properly, right before the last S:(AU... group.

 

4. Apply the new security descriptor to MSLLDP service :

 

sc sdset MSLLDP D:(D;;CCDCLCSWRPWPDTLOCRSDRCWDWO;;;BG)(A;;CCDCLCSWRPWPDTLOCRSDRCWDWO;;;SY)(A;;CCDCLCSWRPDTLOCRSDRCWDWO;;;BA)(A;;CCDCLCSWRPWPDTLOCRSDRCWDWO;;;SO)(A;;LCRPWP;;;S-1-5-80-3141615172-2057878085-1754447212-2405740020-3916490453)(A;;CCLCSWLOCRRC;;;SU)S:(AU;FA;CCDCLCSWRPWPDTLOCRSDRCWDWO;;;WD)


5. Check the result:

 

>accesschk.exe -c mslldp

mslldp
  RW NT AUTHORITY\SYSTEM
  RW BUILTIN\Administrators
  RW S-1-5-32-549
  R  NT SERVICE\NlaSvc
  R  NT AUTHORITY\SERVICE

 

6. Run you backup app, the error is gone for my Home Server backup.

!!! Do not forget to use your security descriptor for MSLLDP driver since I guess there can be some rare cases when its different for your machine. Do not copy my SDDL descriptions, just in case. And backup the old descriptor just in case !!!

 

I don't know what reason MS had behind all this, probably some security concerns or probably this is just a bug. Definitely not a security problem in my environment.

 

Good luck!

CONNECTING TO XBOX LIVE AT U OF M TWIN CITIES CAMPUS

Hi I'm a freshman living in Yudof Hall on the University of Minnesota-Twin Cities Campus. I'm having trouble connecting to Xbox LIVE like most people that I've talked to about it.  There's a ton of people that live on campus at the U, and I'm assuming also a good amount of gamers who are experiencing this problem too, so if anyone finds anything that works, you should prob post it here.  

I've been reading about it, and here's the main problem: When trying to connect an Xbox to the ethernet jack, it won't connect because "Resnet" doesn't recognize your Xbox's MAC Address as one that has connected through an x.500 username (your @umn.edu email address). A tech support guy in my building e-mailed me a solution that in theory should work, but for whatever reason just doesn't work on mine.... Here's his step by step process, it's worth a try!

:::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::::

Free-Yet-Temporary Way

On the Computer:
1. Plug your computer into the one of your room's Ethernet ports 
2. Get on the internet and authenticate computer with Resnet by using your school username and password (X.500) 
3. Look up your computer's MAC address (see below if you're not sure how to do this) 
4. Turn on your Xbox 360 
5. Unplug the Ethernet cord from the computer and plug it into the Xbox 360 

On the Xbox:
6. Under the "My Xbox" tab, go to "System Settings" 
7. Go to "Network Settings" 
8. Select "Wired Network" 
9. Go to "Configure Network" 
10. Go to the "Additional Settings" tab 
11. Select the option that says "Alternate MAC Address" 
12. Select "Alternate MAC Address" 
13. Enter the MAC address you found on your computer into the blank field 
14. Go back to the page where you originally chose to "Configure Network" and now select "Test Xbox LIVE Connection"

Tada! You should now be connected to Xbox LIVE. Just sign into your profile, and you'll be good to go for a while. However, if you leave your Xbox dormant for too long, you will be logged out of Resnet's central authentication and will have to repeat this process. On the bright side, you won't have to re-enter the MAC address or anything like that -- it will stay the same! 

Finding the MAC address

Mac
1. Open "About This Mac" from the Apple menu in the top left
2. Click "More Info..."
3. Click the "Network" tab on the left side
4. Scroll down until you see where it says "MAC Address:" which should read something like this:

g8:2i:14:7b:d8:hb

Windows
1. Open "Network and Sharing Center"
2. Go to "Local Area Connection"
3. Click on "Details"
4. On this page, the MAC address will be listed next as the "Physical Address", and will look something like this:

00-5H-3B-G9-42-5B
