Get-AppxPackage -allusers *WindowsStore* | Remove-AppxPackage

visageofanangel say:

As Favious M stated this is due to poor or non-existent anti-aliasing in certain games.  Anti-aliasing is what smooth's out jagged edges on non-straight lines.  The games you mentioned use a lot of system resources (high graphic fidelity with many objects on the screen, especially due to the audiences) on an aging system and the developers probably turned down AA to 2X or completely off to maintain a smoother frame rate.

 

It might also be more visible if you are playing a game that is natively 720p in a 1080p resolution.
