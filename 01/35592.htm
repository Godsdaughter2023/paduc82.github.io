xca ghosttown say:

Games that are no longer available

Sometimes games will be pulled down from the marketplace for various reasons. When this happens, some users who have the trials for these games on their hard drives will finally decide to pull the trigger and make that purchase. In this scenario, those users will be presented with a game price of: 4294967295MSP. Well, nobody has a balance quite that high on their account and the sad truth is that the game is simply no longer available. They can keep the trial if they would like, but the chances of the full game becoming available again are very slim.

However, if the customer purchased a game before it became unavailable, they can always access their download history which will allow them to download their games again. In very rare situations, some games will no longer be available to download through the download history though – Ultimate MK3 is the most prominent example of this.

It is sad when great games are taken down from the marketplace, and the majority of the time that means those games are gone for good. But in same rare cases, unavailable XBLA games can still be attained through legitimate retailers. A great place to look for these games would be the Microsoft Online Store. Here are a couple of the more desired games that are still available at online retailers:

TMNT 1989 Arcade

Marble Blast Ultra

Games stuck in trial mode

Sometimes even after a proper license transfer, a few games might remain stuck in trial mode (meaing they have failed to recognize that users have in fact purchased the full versions of a particular game). This can be frustrating for customers but there is a workaround that seems to work the majority of the time. Here are the suggested steps to take when this occurs:

   Launch the trial for the game experiencing the issues

   From within the game, select the “Unlock Full Game” option from the main menu.

   Select the full game from the list of items available to download and click on “Download Again”.

   As opposed to selecting “Play Now” or “Continue” when the download completes, press the “B” button until you are back at the main menu for the game.

   Try moving the joystick up or down to cycle through the available options which should make the “Unlock Full Game” option disappears.

If this doesn’t seem to work, have them try repeating these steps a few times and even have them try choosing the “Continue” option after they redownload the game to see if they get better results with that option. The “Play Now” option rarely works, so never start with that option. Instead, leave that for more of a last resort if the other two options are not successful after multiple attempts.

The most common games you will see this occur with will be Sonic the Hedgehog, Sonic the Hedgehog II, Golden Axe and Streets of Rage II. Keep in mind though that this can occur with other games, too.
