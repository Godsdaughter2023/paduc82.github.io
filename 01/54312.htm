If you have access to your son's account, simply go to https://commerce.microsoft.comand check his order history. 

Every order is listed there with timestamps. 

If your son claims, his account was hacked and someone else did it, you can go to https://account.live.com/Activity to check his account's sign in activity with a small map next to it. 

Be aware, that the map does NOT show your actual residential address, but the location where your Internet Provider connects you (and several other customers from your area) to the Internet. 

But if a hacker used a different Internet Provider, or is from a vastly different area, you will see the location of the sign in differ. 

That way you can check if your son is lying or if his account was actually hacked. 

If this page proves your son was lying, take the spent amount from his allowance or something like that. 

If the account was hacked, go to https://account.live.com/Proofs/Manage and check the security info on your son's account. Make sure the hacker didn't add their own email or phone number, and remove any unknown phone numbers and emails. Otherwise the hacker is able to recover the account again. 

Also, make sure your son's phone number and secondary email are correct and up-to-date. 

If your son used the same password on multiple accounts (e.g. the same password for Xbox and his own email), change both to be different. Otherwise the hacker could use your son's hacked email to recover the account again. 

After you made sure the security info is correct, change the password on your son's account if not already done so. 

And MOST IMPORTANTLY: Enable Two Step Verification (TSV) on your son's account (and your own account as well). 

After enabling TSV, you both set up the free Authenticator App on your smartphones (if your son owns a smartphone himself). 

With TSV and the Authenticator App enabled, a hacker would have to also physically steal your phone to access your account(s), as Microsoft will ask for a secret code whenever someone (e.g. a hacker) tries to sign into your accounts from a foreign device.
