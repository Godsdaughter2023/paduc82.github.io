When opening an attachment directly from within Outlook you could get an error message saying that it can’t create the file and to that you need check the permissions on the folder you want to save it in. Outlook Secure Temp folder

Unfortunately this is easier said than done. The subfolder name Outlook creates (on installation of Outlook) in the Temporary Internet Files folder is quite random. In Outlook 2003 and previous, the name starts with OLK and is followed by up to 4 random numbers or letters. In Outlook 2007 and Outlook 2010, this folder is called Content. Outlook and then has a subfolder which is named with  8 random numbers and letters. In addition, by default, you cannot simply browse to the folder to clean it out. Getting to the Temporary Outlook Folder can still be accomplished in 2 easy steps though.

 Step 1: Locate the folder

The folder location is stored in the registry in the following key;

 Outlook 97 HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Office\8.0\Outlook\Security

Outlook 98 HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Office\8.5\Outlook\Security

Outlook 2000 HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Office\9.0\Outlook\Security

Outlook 2002/XP HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Office\10.0\Outlook\Security

Outlook 2003 HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Office\11.0\Outlook\Security

Outlook 2007 HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Office\12.0\Outlook\Security

Outlook 2010 HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Office\14.0\Outlook\Security

Step 2: Get to the folder

 A.     Open the OutlookSecureTempFolder registry key from the location provided in Step 1.

B.     Copy the path from the key.

C.    Open Explorer

D.    Paste the address in the Address Bar and press Enter

bossdweeb say: No work. I'm here for the fun, and education (retired 66yr old mad gamer).

First - For the most part you have to read Newegg reviews with a grain of salt, and general
knowledge. I've seen reviews from people who give a gpu or case one or two eggs because
they didn't find the case long enough or card too long after buying the product. Who's fault is this ?
GTX690,  lol - monster in every way....

The V3 case is very similar to the Commandar (which I have had experience with), and effectively the
same length.
The V3 should fit a long card, but the fit will be tight. The problem I have with the tight fit is getting
air to the cpu.
With a long card air from the front case fan is partly  blocked by the card, even with the HDD mounted
in the bottom. The side fan option on the commander is too low for the cpu as well. It would be
better for the side fan to be at cpu level as air from the front fan will reach the gpu.
-If the window on the V3 can be removed to allow for a fan it would be better, and while it may draw
more dust (you have to clean dust regularly regardless), if you remove the window the cpu fan will still get some fresh air from outside the case.
- Both of these cases come with only a rear exhaust fan. Not good enough. You do want good air flow even if not OC'ing, so you will need to add a front and side fan at least. An extra exhaust in the top
rear position would be good to.
- It does have a good size hole in the motherboard tray for future cooler fixing.
- cable management is also important to help with good air-flow. One advantage with the Commander
is the motherboard side of the case has a bulge in the side panel to make room for cables.
- Don't put the case in one of those tiny PC desk compartments = poor air flow and exhaust.

Coolermaster HAF   Ugly ? Maybe, but functional - Oh yeah! Sometimes you gotta let form follow function. What's special ?
- Side fitting  HDD cage (the top one comes out making more room for gpu ) = good air flow
and cable management.
- A bit longer than the V3
- USB 3 in front panel (in case you get a USB 3 something one day).
- Option for side fan is still only for a low one, but you can put two in front, and the case comes with
a front and rear fan. Later you could move the rear exhaust to the front an put a whopping great
200mm fan in the top for exhaust.

- My fav (have two) . Over budget, but what I like is the external HDD bay. Great for backups.
 CM -690 II     best value case on the market - imo.
(Wow - only $69 with rebate. I payed $109 here in Oz. That version of the HAF costs $82- here , and you

It stands for Windows Update Service. So, yes, it is the same thing.

Before carrying out this procedure, disconnect from the Internet. That means turning off Wi-Fi and disconnecting from a wired connection. When complete, hit Enter, then restart, then try updating again.
