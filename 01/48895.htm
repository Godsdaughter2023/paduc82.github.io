Computer manufacturers would declare if there is a camera embedded in the device. Some people worry about unknown apps, organizations, or malware using their camera. Whenever your camera is used, you should be in charge. That’s why you’re always told when the camera is turned on:

  3. Under Website permissions, select Manage.
  4. Select the website you want to manage.

a. user say:

Here's something that (I hope) will help you through this and other similar situations.

Merely having a hardware device installed is not sufficient to make it work. Indeed, many times you'll see that your computer has detected the presence of a hardware device, but Windows can't use it.

In order for Windows, or any operating system, to use hardware, it needs to have a device driver for the hardware, which is simply called a driver for short. The driver is software that makes it possible for Windows to work with a hardware device.

It's an apt description: You can sit in a car all day but you won't get anywhere until someone with the ability to operate the car - the driver - gets inside. Your Inspiron needs a driver for your camera.

You can be sure that Dell included a driver when it shipped your computer, and that driver worked well at the time. But Windows is constantly being updated, and if a driver isn't updated to keep up with all the new technology in Windows, eventually the driver no longer has the ability to operate your camera properly - or at all. So what probably happened in your case is that the camera's driver has fallen behind the times so Windows can no longer use it. You need to remove the existing driver and install a newer, updated driver.

But ... there's always a but ... after a time, a hardware manufacturer may decide that they are no longer going to update the driver for one of their devices. That can happen for several reasons: it may be too difficult, technologically, to make an older camera work with newer versions of Windows; or it may become impossible; or the hardware manufacturer wants people to buy their newer stuff. When that happens, you may be out of luck.

So your job #1 is to find a Windows 10 driver for your camera and then follow the manufacturer's installation instructions carefully. If the manufacturer decided to stop supporting your camera, which means they're not going to update its driver any more, you might find that the Windows 8.1 driver still works in Windows 10, or maybe even the Windows 7 driver. Otherwise, it's time for a new camera.
