a. user say:

A Microsoft Account is simply Microsoft's version of a cloud account.

The idea behind a cloud account is that you can sign in from any device, or with any application connected to the account. That makes it very convenient. It also makes it a pain in the a** when you want to completely sign out of a cloud account, because there's no master 'off' switch.

On your own PC, you sign out of your Microsoft Account the same way you would with any Windows user account. That will sign you out of your Microsoft Account on your own computer but you may still be signed in to your Microsoft Account on a different computer or on a phone or a tablet.

And even if you sign out on every device you can think of, you might still be signed in to one or more Microsoft Account services like OneDrive or Outlook.com.

If you setup your Microsoft Account to sync your settings, they'll be synced on every device and every service connected to your Microsoft Account, automatically, the next time you sign in with a Microsoft Account on any device. Cloud accounts are the STDs of the internet age.

Of course, you can also make this work for you: If you stop syncing, partially or completely, on any device, your wishes will propagate out to every device when you sign in to that device with your Microsoft Account.
