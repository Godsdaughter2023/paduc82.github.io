johann eva say:

Hi Tenshi,

When your laptop battery isn’t charging, there’s a chance that the battery itself has a static charge. In order to make it properly again, you will need to discharge it. To do this, follow these workarounds:

  1. Shutdown your computer.
  2. Remove your laptop batter temporarily.
  3. While it was removed, press the power button for 15 seconds to drain it.
  4. Re-attach the battery again and start your computer.
  5. Once started, press Windows + X and click Device Manager.
  6. Expand the Batteries category.
  7. Uninstall the Microsoft ACPI Compliant Control Method Battery.
  8. Go to the menu and click Action > Scan for hardware changes.
  9. Wait for Windows to scan your computer for available resources for this hardware.
  10. Restart your computer.
  11. Check if your battery is now charging.

Let us know if this works for you.
