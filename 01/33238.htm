Sign into the present install with your MS Account until it links the Digital license to it in Settings > Update & Security > Activation at the top under Status.

mike hall... always say:

There are no official Windows 7 drivers for your machine, in fact there is nothing official beyond XP drivers.

 

However, you may find a fix here..

http://windows7forums.com/windows-7-support/2861-soundmax-integrated-driver.html

Try it out, and if it doesn't work, run Windows Update. That may find an optional update for your sound card

 

Please note:

The minimum hardware specs quoted for Windows 7 encompass many older XP machines, and the premis is that Windows 7 will run ok. In reality, the minimum specs are way below reasonable performance and there is no account taken in regard to Windows 7 driver support for all internal hardware.

As a result, upgrading an older XP machine is not a particularly good idea. Sound and video are often casualties of 'zero' support. While video will always work, albeit as basic VGA , sound will not, and you may find yourself having to buy 3rd party sound and video cards supported by Windows 7. In my opinion, putting money into an aging machine is not the best way to go..
