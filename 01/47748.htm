How can I save a project and come back to add photos to finish in - Microsoft Community

gigitd say:

How can I save a project and come back to add photos to finish in movie maker?

I'm new to Movie Maker, when I have added several pictures and then needed to get out of the program, it required to save.  Upon saving the movie it did NOT allow me to edit later.  I need to be able to work on a project over a period of days and weeks.  How do I save and then add media later?

considerate_guy say:

There are two things you can save.  The first option is to save the project.  Look in the blue File menu and you should see an option to Save project.  When you save your project, Movie Maker is remembering all the instructions for assembling your items.  It remembers the individual photos, what transitions are applied, what captions are added, etc.  If you want to come back and make further edits to your project, this is the option you should always choose.  It creates a "Windows Live MovieMaker Project" file (.wlmp)

 

And then, when you are done with your editing and want to create a video file that you can play in Windows Media Player, share with others, upload to sharing sites, etc., you use a different option to save the movie.  Look lower down in the blue File menu and you should see an option to Save movie.  Movie Maker will incorporate all the instructions in your project and will assemble a single combined movie (audio + video) as desired.  Movie Maker will save this as a playable movie in either .mp4 or .wmv format, depending on your version and your chosen output type.

 

I like to use the analogy of baking a cake.  Your ingredients are all the photos, videos and music from your hard disk.  The recipe is the project file that tells how to put these ingredients together.  And finally you bake your cake by saving as a movie.

 

If you have only saved the final movie but failed to keep your project, it is like throwing away your recipe.  You'll have to go back and figure out how to reassemble your ingredients to make another cake.  So, if you want to edit further, always save your project. 

 

If you have the source files and the project you can always recreate the movie.  But if you only have the movie, you can't go back and create the project without starting over.

 

Incidentally, Movie Maker keeps a list of your recent projects on the blue File menu.  Look there to see what projects you recently saved.  Then click on one of the names to reopen them.  You can also reopen a project by finding where you saved the .wlmp file and clicking it directly to reopen it in Movie Maker.
