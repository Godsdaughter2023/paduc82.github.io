A: Yes, but this is not a recommended or supported scenario. Microsoft recommends persons running the Release Candidate released in April 2009, reinstall Windows Vista and do upgrades from that release to Windows 7 RTM.

Q: Didn’t previous development versions of Windows support build to build upgrades?
A: True, but Microsoft has changed this policy for the Windows 7 release. They want users to test against real world scenarios.     Looks like your numbers are good. What do you mean by ping/jitter creeps up to 30/10ms range? A jitter of 3ms is not bad at all.

    Have you tried switching between Best and Normal in preferences when searching for a game? It helps me a lot.

    Try speedtest.net for a speedtest and pingtest. Select a server that is at least 500 miles from you.

    Also, you can try www.dslreports.net. They have a smoke ping test that pings you over a period of 24 hours from three different servers. That will tell you how good your connection is over a period of time. Look for anomalies.

    Check all the processes and services in your computer. Shutdown processes that are not required.

    ping -t www.google.com

    ping -t 192.168.1.1 or 192.168.1.254 or whatever IP your router is assigned.
