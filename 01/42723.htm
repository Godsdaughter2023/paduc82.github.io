Normally, Windows disk partitions are limited to a single physical drive. Windows does support "dynamic disks" which can span across multiple physical drives, but Windows itself can't be installed on a dynamic disk.

First, download and run (as administrator) TreeSize Free (https://www.jam-software.com/treesize_free/). This will let you know what is occupying space on your hard drive. Depending on what is taking up space, you may be able to move it from C: to your larger D: partition.

You CAN'T "move free space to your C drive."

You can either delete or move to the D drive files that are now on your C drive (which will thus create more space on the C drive) or get a larger C drive.

One obvious thing to do is to run the built-in Disk Cleanup tool if you haven't done so already. See https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/help/402661...

If you already have moved most of your data on the D drive and there is nothing remaining on the C drive that is obvious to move or delete, your only option is to install a larger C drive.
