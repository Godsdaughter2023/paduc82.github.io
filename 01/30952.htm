I would say that this would a perfect reason to use the Restore Point option, assuming you have Automatic Restore Points.

Rolling back to a previous Restore Point should allow you to go back a week or two to the last update, or even the last time you installed an app. It won't affect any files or doc.s as this is just dealing with the OS. If the nearest Restore Point is right at the time you started getting problems, check the Choose a different restore point option. You should have at least two and possibly more like six to choose from. This is much less drastic than re-installing Windows and just brings the system back before you had problems.

You can type in Restore Point in the Cortana Search box to take you there. It will default to Create a Restore Point, but on the same panel is the System Restore button. Click that and choose the most appropriate date; ideally at least 3-5 days before you had any malware issues.

BTW: The suggestions you got* for solving this honestly should not be needed and may make things worse. A Restore Point will take care of that though, so don't bother undoing it manually. Either way, those things are probably not why you are having issues; it's clearly related to the malware. I don't think Avast itself damaged the system, but it may not have been able to properly fix what happened.

Hope that helps!
