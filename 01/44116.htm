marcusaquinas say:

I will add one other tidbit that might be helpful to others with similar issues. For upgrade installations, it is possible to do a clean install, minus the partitioning capability.

First, make a backup of your data files, run the normal upgrade and let it do whatever you want to let it do.

Second, after the upgrade has got you back to a desktop, go into "Update and Security" in Settings and select "Recovery" from the left panel. Select "Reset this PC" in the right panel and follow the prompts from there.

It will take a while, depending on how big your drives are, but you can reformat them and lay down the OS only. Be sure to have current drivers for all of your hardware and peripherals on hand. You might not need them as Win10 was pretty solid about having compatible drivers for everything except my graphics card (it's a gamer thing), but better safe than sorry. You will need to reinstall your apps and restore your data files, but that's about as clean as you can get.

devlin sco say:

Hi Cindy,

Active hours only pause necessary restarts when an update installation is complete. Windows 10 will still download and install updates in the background. If you really need to pause Windows Update downloads and installation to maximize the performance of your PC, you can simply enable Pause Updates. With Pause Updates, updates will be paused temporarily for a maximum of 7 days.
