5. Tap or Click OK.

6. Restart the computer.

7. Install the feature, and then restart the computer.

8. Repeat step 1 to step 3, and then change the startup type for the “Nalpeiron Licensing Service or NLS” to “Automatic”.

loud fan noise - Microsoft Community

rodavlascuba say:

loud fan noise

My Dell desktop w/Windows XP Home Edition has become very fan loud whem is not being used. What is the cause and can I fix it?
tricky300 say:

Follow Gerry's advice, it is something that should be done at intervals anyway. I am not an electrical engineer, but I suspect that attaching an antistatic wrist strap to a PC case after taking out the mains lead would be unlikely ground you.

If the offending fan continues to be noisy, which experience tells me it most likley will, you must first identify which fan (or fans) is (are) causing the problem.

Inside your PC there could be fans in any or all of the following places...

  1. On top of the CPU heatsink
  2. On the rear of the case
  3. On the front of the case
  4. On the top of the case
  5. On the graphics card
  6. Inside the power supply unit

I have even (once) seen a fan on a motherboard chip, though never on a Dell (I assumed this was a graphics chip but didn't check if this assumption was correct).

Inside many Dell PCs the rear case fan doubles up as a CPU heatsink fan.

Identifying the fan will almost certainly involve running the PC with the side/cover removed. If you are not comfortable doing this then take the PC to a repair shop. The cost should not be more than around £15-20 (i.e. UK pounds Sterling), maybe a bit more if it is the CPU heatsink fan. If it is more than that get them to justify it. If it isn't immediately obvious visually (sometimes the fan can be seen to be stuttering/vibrating), then a rubber/plastic tube, with one end to the ear and the other end pointed toward the various fans in turn should assist diagnosis.

Once you have identified the fan, you need to source an exact replacement and fit it. Fans are typically measured in two dimensions (two of the three dimensions are the same). It is important to make sure that both dimensions are the same when purchasing the replacement. Fitting should be relatively straightforward for a case fan, less so for other fans. Replacement of a CPU heatsink fan may involve removal and/or replacement of the heatsink too, which would also mean replacing thermal conductivity compound. Replacement of a power supply unit fan involves disconnecting all power leads, removing the power supply unit, disassembling the PSU, replacement of the fan and the reverse processes. Sourcing a replacement graphics card fan may prove difficult and may only be achievable by replacement of the whole unit rather than just its fan (more expensive).

If you feel replacement is beyond you my advice is take the PC to a repair shop and get a quote before authorising repair.

When replacing a fan it is vital that you direct the airflow in the correct direction, i.e. the blade orientation must be the same as the way Dell did it during their manufacture.
