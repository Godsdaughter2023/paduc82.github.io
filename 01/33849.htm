Because you did not properly stop the drive before removing it, you've toasted its FAT (the File Allocation Table that every drive has). Your files are still there, but they can't be read by "normal" software ... think of a hard drive as a housing development ... formatting a drive (any drive) is like grading all the areas that will be roads and determining where housing structures (folders) can be placed.
