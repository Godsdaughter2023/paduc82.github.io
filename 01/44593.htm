Hi,

Could you do the following for each account to ensure that these accounts have Family Safety running?

1. Log in to the windows account(s) that adult sites were not blocked.

2. Go to the Desktop.

3. In the taskbar at the bottom right corner near the date/time, there is an up arrow icon, click on it and you should see the Family Safety icon (see below). If you don't see the Family Safety icon, then this account is not being monitored by Family Safety. See instructions below to turn on Family Safety.



4. If the Family Safety icon exists, click on it and the User Settings panel will appear. Under this user profile on the right side of the panel, you will see "Web filtering" setting below it. Make sure that the status for this setting is not "Off", and the settings is set to one of the following: "Allow list only", "Design for children", 'General interest", and "Online communication (basic)". You can change Web Filtering setting for this user by logging in to https://familysafety.microsoft.com using your email account.


If the Family Safety icon does not exist, then Family Safety is not running for this account. Family Safety will be turned on when a Windows Account is a "Child" account type. You should verify that this account is a Windows "Child" account type and not a Windows Standard account type. Here are steps to check this:

a. Log into your windows administrator account using your email address.

b. Search for "Manage other accounts".

c. Select "Add, delete, and manage other user accounts" from the search result.

d. Select the account in question.

e. Click on Edit.

f. Verify that the Account Type is set to Child. If not, select "Child" from the down arrow and click on Ok.

e. Hit the Windows key to go back to the Start page and search for "Family Safety" and select Family Safety from the search results.

d. In the Family Safety panel, click on "Manage settings on the Family Safety website" link.

f. If prompt to sign in, use your email to sign in to the Family Safety website.


Now log into your child account(s) and see if the adult sites are still unblocked.
