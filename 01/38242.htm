There are two things happening here.

  1. The source video is much wider than a 16:9 aspect ratio. To my eye it looks closer to 21:9. How is this captured? Can you provide more details on the source? In any case, that's why Movie Maker is adding black bars on top and bottom, to fill it out to match a 16:9 ratio.
  2. You are then playing this back full screen again on a much wider aspect ratio monitor. So it now fills the left and right with black. You should see these same black bars on your computer with *any* 16:9 video source unless you do stretching or cropping, so it isn't Movie Maker.

The fixes are:

- Use 16:9 video sources so that Movie Maker doesn't add black bars on top and bottom.

- Play back the video on a true 16:9 monitor so you don't see the added black bars on left and right.

Ideally, you could create a movie that matched your monitor's resolution (21:9?) matching the original video, but Movie Maker doesn't allow that. It can only create projects in a 4:3 or 16:9 ratio.
