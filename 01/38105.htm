What an unhappy story. It's not helped by the fact that it took the Microsoft moderator three weeks to move your question to the right forum.

When an account is removed from Windows Live Mail, a warning is shown:

The warning is incomplete: it should point out that all the account's folders and their contents will be permanently deleted. I don't know why your tech didn't realize this.

Windows 10 has a splendid function called File History which is capable of storing copies of folders as their contents change. If you had configured this to back up your store folder, all your old messages would have been saved many times over. I take it you didn't do this.

So, to answer your questions: if you didn't back up these messages one way or another, they're most probably gone for good. There is no fairy who saves them for you, and Microsoft is certainly not in the business of storing users' email (except for that in Microsoft email accounts like Hotmail, of course).

I'm afraid your only possibility is to use a file undelete utility like Recuva to search your drives for deleted files with the extension .eml. Sadly, so much time has passed since the vandalism happened that the chances of success are not high; the more you use the computer, the greater the probability that a deleted file is overwritten with new data and thus rendered irretrievable. 

Sorry not to have better news for you.
