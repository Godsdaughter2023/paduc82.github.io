hairyfool say:

Each ATI image is a complete standalone image so the status of any other image is completely irrelevant. The backed up image is of an activated Windows 10 installation so the restore is activated by default, it does not have to reactivate with one caveat.

The license for the Windows 10 upgrade is valid for the lifetime of the device. A restore to a new motherboard in the future would be classified as a new computer and invalidates the license. If your original 8 license is transferable you could reinstall it and upgrade back through to Win10 before July for free.

After July the license remains valid for the original device so a clean install can be done on the same registered device at any time even after the free period has expired. It would be recommended to get the latest ISO for any rebuild but the license is for Windows 10, it does not at this stage restrict to particular builds.

The build number is also irrelevant, any restore of an old image will just appear that the computer has been turned off since the backup. The next time it connects it will then update itself to the latest build.

Electronic distribution allows Microsoft to refresh the master image published for download, whether they do or not for any particular release is down to them. One of the problems with the Windows 7 distribution was that once SP1 had been integrated no further builds were published, the last install I did from an ISO download required 128 updates.

However backups are only half the process, it does not matter how many you do if you are not sure they are recoverable. The only solution is a test restore. In fact Microsoft have moved away from complete image backups, instead recommending clean installs, reinstalling software and connecting into the Microsoft Account to reinstate OneDrive and the File History function.

Transfer of music from Windows 8 operating system to Surface RT - Microsoft Community

josephohnstad say:

Transfer of music from Windows 8 operating system to Surface RT

I am finding that Surface RT does not have an App to run just about everything. I now find that I can't transfer music from a flashdrive that was on Window Media Player.  This major flaw is never told to prospective buyers. I would not have bought.
james vinyard say: Windows Media Player is a program on Windows 8.  It primarily gets its music from the stored files in the music folder.

If you opened up the File Manager on your computer that has the music, click on the MUSIC folder you see in the left tree.  If that folder is blank, then your music has been stored somewhere else.  Check the other folders including the Downloads folder.

Windows Media Player scans the entire computer for files and catalogs them but won't display where they are stored.  You have to manually browse the folder via the File Manager to find them.

Best to stick a pendrive into the USB port and when you are looking around and find the files, copy them over to the pendrive.

If it is music you have been downloading, most of the time they will go to the Downloads folder or to the last location where you had downloaded a file from Internet Explorer.

When I have backed up people's data to work on their computers, I have noticed that a lot of them stored pictures, videos and music in the Documents folder for some weird reason.  However the program in windows scan the computer for the media type they are designed for and will organize them in their own built in file manager.

Always best to stay organized.  Chances are, your music files are scattered all over the place.

You can organize at the same time you are copying them.  Just tell the program to "Cut" and then "Paste" to the pendrive.  That way, it will move the files to the pendrive and then when you are done, and you want the music to stay on your main computer, just copy the music back onto the main computer into the MUSIC folder.

That way you will always know where they are and the music is not only organized on your computer but also copied onto the pendrive so that you can copy them onto the RT.

I kept mine pretty simple.  I put all of my files onto a micro-SD card and inserted it into the RT of mine.  I opened up the XBox Music app and waited about two minutes and bingo, my music was being cataloged and ready to play.  Most people screw that up and think the computer can't find their music.  They forget that they have to WAIT and let the computer scan the drives for the media.  That RT can be slow but sure.

james vinyard say: All of your files are going to be on you main computer, not the RT.  You are trying to get your files from the main computer "TO" the tablet.

Right now, we are trying to FIND your music so that you can copy onto a pendrive so that you can then copy it onto the RT.  You can park the RT, you don't need it right now.  Get on your Touchsmart and on the desktop, open the file manager and your folder are listed on the upper left corner.  Search each of those folders for your music.  They are supposed to be in the MUSIC folder and since you have iTunes, iTunes creates a sub-folder in MUSIC called "iTunes".  Any media you bought through the iTunes Store will go into that sub-folder.

Once you can find the music files themselves (you don't need the media players running, you can close those), insert the pendrive and copy the files over to the pendrive.

Now then for playback of those files, especially iTunes, there are a few programs in the Store that will.  In fact, the RT may already have the codecs some of the formats already.  I haven't tried it.

For your main MP3 files, playback will be through the XBox Music App and it does a fairly good job.  It doesn't have an equalizer though.

Now also, you are mistaking the media players as the file catalog of your music files.  The media players list the music you have on the computer.  The files are located (supposedly) in the MUSIC folder.  That is where the media players are getting those files at.

As for the iPad, I have an iPad 4 myself and bought it to replace the RT with since it had an issue with connecting with WiFi.  In fact, I have it already configured with the programs and files I need to replace the RT now.  The iPad plays back everything; all formats.  The only problem with it though, no USB port or SD/micro-SD slot for expansion and file transfers.

A note about the iPad for productivity though; Pages, Numbers, and Keynote are about the only productivity programs that you can do anything with.  The other programs they have can compete with a vacuum cleaner (they suck).  Pages, Numbers, and Keynote make Microsoft Works look good if that tells you anything.  BUT, the iPad works, gets online instantly, and just plain works.
