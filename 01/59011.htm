robert aldwinckle on forums say:

lost the mouse cursor

That's possible.  Your touchpad may be appearing to provide only touch functionality.  If you have enabled Mouse Sonar for your Mouse Properties (unfortunately not a default) you may be able to detect the location of the mouse pointer by pressing a Ctrl- key.  So then you can move the mouse a bit in a desired direction and then check with the Ctrl- key if it is where you would want to use a mouse button (for example).  If you have a real mouse you could use it to move the pointer and then that would show the mouse pointer normally.  A related possibility is activating Mousekeys and moving the pointer with one of its pointer move commands.  You need access to a full function Numeric keypad to use Mousekeys.

Another possibility I discovered recently is using Narrator to turn on Mouse mode.  Press Win+Ctrl+Enter to turn on Narrator and then its Mouse mode command (Caps Lock + NumLock  assuming you do not use its Locked Narrator key mode)  will toggle the Mouse mode state.  Narrator will tell you what your press of NumLock accomplished.
