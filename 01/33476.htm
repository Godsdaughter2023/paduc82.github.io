Hello Mocha,

Thank you for contacting Microsoft Community.

Regret the inconvenience you have experienced.

Did you tried force shutdown?

Are you able to see Manufacturer logo during Startup?

After Windows logo, will it goes to black screen?

Hold down the power button on your Windows laptop for 5 to 10 seconds until your laptops powers down completely. Wait for about 30 seconds, then power up your laptop to see if it starts correctly.

If in case problem still appears, then you need a Windows 10 bootable or recovery disk to fix this issue. Using them boot your PC into the Safe Mode, then try to restart your PC to see if it works fine.

To start your PC in Safe Mode using a recovery drive:

  1. First, make sure your PC is shut down. Connect the recovery drive to your PC, and then turn on your PC.

  2. On the Choose an option screen, select Troubleshoot > Advanced Options > Startup Settings > Restart.

  3. After your PC restarts, select 5 or F5 to restart your PC in Safe Mode with Networking.

To start your PC in Safe Mode using installation media:

  1. On a working PC, go to the Windows 10 installation media.

  2. Insert the installation media you created, and then restart your PC.

  3. On the Install Windows screen, select Repair your computer. (If you’re not seeing this screen, your PC might not be set up to boot from a drive. Check your PC manufacturer's website for info on how to boot from media, and then try again.)

  4. On the Choose an option screen, select Troubleshoot. From there, you can use Safe Mode. Select Advanced Options > Startup Settings > Restart.

  5. After your PC restarts, select 5 or F5 to restart you PC in Safe Mode with Networking.

On successful login to User Profile through Safe Mode, then reboot your PC and see if it fix the issue.

Keep us posted if you require further assistance.
