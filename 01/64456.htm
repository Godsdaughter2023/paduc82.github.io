if the restart was caused by a windows error (or another piece of software interfering with windows) and unless it is turned off in startup and recovery, an automatic memory dump will have been written and an event will have been written to the event logs before the restart.

These will both have been deleted with a clean install.

if, as you originally suspected, it was a power issue, then of course they may not have been written. 

as the problem is resolved, there is little point in trying to collect evidence of the issue such as turning of the auto restart so that you can see the BSOD message and ensuring that the creation of dump files is not turned off.

without the logs there is little that anybody will be able to do to investigate the issue.

its not that Microsoft doesn't care about problems faced by only a few people but, it makes sense that a major issue, experienced by millions would have a higher priority.
