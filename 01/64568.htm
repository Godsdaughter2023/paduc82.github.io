arminatorx say:

Double NAT happens, when (at least) two devices are doing "Network Address Translation". 

Your Router has only one internet connection, but "hides" multiple devices in your home (like your PC, Laptop, the console, a tablet and your smartphones) behind that one internet address. 

So when data comes in from the outside internet, you need to tell the router to which device the incoming data has to be sent to. 


The trouble is, that a lot of internet providers are running out of IPv4 addresses, and start to hide multiple customers behind a Carrier Grade NAT (CG-NAT) or tunnel network traffic through IPv6 via DS-Lite. 

If you are stuck behind such a Carrier Grade NAT, incoming multiplayer data into your ISP wouldn't know if it has to go to your house, or to the Millers next door, or to Mr. Smith further down the street, similarly to the router not knowing if the incoming data should be sent to your Xbox console, your PC or your sister's smartph
