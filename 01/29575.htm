To add a little more info, as some suggestions I've seen showed some lack of understanding of the problem domain, Windows maintains two separate profiles that are applied to firewall, network discovery, and homegroup settings:  "public" and "private". Where public should normally be locked down for added security and "private" would be your home network. The "Find Devices and Content" option toggles between "public" and "private" profiles. You can also see the current status in Network and Sharing Center -> Advanced Sharing Options. Either public or private will show "(current)" next to it. You cannot switch between the two profiles from that window, but you can change the rules for each of the profiles if you really wanted to there also.
