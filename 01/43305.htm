considerate_guy say:

In the latest versions of Movie Maker, Microsoft has done away with a traditional timeline and has replaced it with a hybrid storyboard/timeline.

 

In order for it to behave more like a traditional timeline, use the zoom slider at the bottom right of your window. When the slider is all the way to the left, it behaves more like a storyboard (with one rectangle per added item).  Move the slider to the right and each item will then expand in size relative to its duration. In your case, you'll see the video expand out.

 

Now begin playing the video clip and you'll see the playback marker moving along the "timeline".  At the point where you want to split your clip, pause.  You can either use the 'Split' button (which should now be enabled) or you can press the 'M' key.  The reason you weren't seeing it enabled before is you were probably at the beginning or end of a clip. Obviously you can only split if you are in the middle of a clip.

The easiest way to make a project into parts is to make two copies of the project (.wlmp) file.  That's much easier than trying to manually copy half to a new project.

 

You would open up the first copy of the project file and delete everything after the middle.  Save that project and then save the movie.  Now open up the second copy and delete everything before the middle.  Save that project and its movie.
