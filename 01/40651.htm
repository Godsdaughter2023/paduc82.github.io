Hi Ashwin,

We have checked along the information you provided where you have mentioned that your Samsung Galaxy S8 isn't charging.  There are several possible reasons we have discovered.

The first thing we need to know is how much power is needed to charge the S8.  Most mobile phones, smartphones, or Android phones would require a certain charging voltage.  The USB port of the Surface Pro 6 has 5 volts * 1 amps=5 watt.  Based on several tech blogs, the S8 would often switch the charger to 9v and draw 1.5A or almost 14 watts if it were fast charging where your phone gets a good 25% battery charge in just 20 minutes.  On an instance when it is plugged in a 5V charger, it draws about 1A or 5 watt of power.  The screen may indicate that it is currently Fast Charging but in reality, you would notice that it is not charging as fast as the original charger that provides you a 9v charge.

Now, based on the information, we can say that when you plugged in your S8, it may not seem to be charging, however, it is too slow for it to be noticeable on our end.  Further, the actual charging time would be longer as opposed to the original charger of the phone.
