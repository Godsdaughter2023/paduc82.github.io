This issue can be caused if your pen drive is in FAT32 format. Basic reason is that FAT32 cannot hold files larger than 4GB. To put files larger than 4 GB on a USB stick, it must be formatted in NTFS format. Almost all USB sticks are formatted in FAT32 by default.

If you have any data in the pen drive, first take a backup of it. Now you need to convert your USB drive from FAT32 to NTFS. This is the easiest and safest way of converting.

  1. Right click on USB drive
  2. Click format
  3. Change the type to NTFS.

I have an ACER E 15 and the laptop keyboard was not working. Hence could not try connecting to another device. 
I also could not complete the prescribed steps from the Acer support website since they don't have any specific drivers for Keyboards. 

I tried the following solution assuming it was a registry issue in the system (since the driver update option had failed earlier) 
1. Opened registry editor by typing 'regedit' 
2. Go to HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE --> SYSTEM --> CurrentControlSet --> Services --> i8042prt
3. Changed the Start parameter to '1' within the port driver system file. It was previously set to "3"

It has worked and I am currently able to use my laptop keyboard again.
