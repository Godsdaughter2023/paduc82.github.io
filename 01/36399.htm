4. Reboot system and try using command prompt searching it in windows search bar

1. Press WINDOWS + I

3. Click on "Restore"

6. Click on "Command prompt"
7. Digit "sfc /scannow" without quotes and press ENTER
8. Digit "DISM /Online /Cleanup-Image /CheckHealth" without quote and press ENTER
9. Digit "DISM /Online /Cleanup-Image /ScanHealth" without quote and press ENTER
10. Digit "DISM /Online /Cleanup-Image /RestoreHealth" without quote and press ENTER

Hi,
Look at your DVD packaging. The DVD region is noted on the back side of the case. This is the region you need to change your DVD player to. To do that follow the steps below:
a. Click "Start," followed by "Computer" or "My Computer," then right-click the DVD icon and select "Properties."
b. Choose "Hardware" and select the "DVD Region" tab. Click the region you want to change your computer DVD player to, then select "OK."
c. Insert the DVD into your DVD drive and open Windows Media Player. The computer now can play your foreign DVD movie.

Your DVD player can change regions only five times. The fifth change becomes your permanent DVD region.

frederik long say:

This is where a little advance planning comes in handy. Everyone insists on having a spare house key but few people bother to have a spare Windows logon account. When something bad happens to their one and only admin account they are in deep trouble, as you are right now.

There is a 2-phase process that ***might*** get you out of trouble. It is complex and requires a lot of patience. Here is Phase 1:

  1. Set your BIOS so that it uses the DVD drive as its primary boot device.
  2. Boot the machine with your System Repair CD. If you do not have one, ask a friend to burn one for you via the Control Panel / Backup and Restore. It takes less than five minutes.
  3. Press a key when prompted to boot from the CD.
  4. Select "Repair PC" from the menu.
  5. Select the Command Prompt from the menu.
  6. Identify the correct drive letter for your Windows installation. It could be a drive other than C: but it won't be X:. You do it by typing the commands
    dir C:\Win*
    dir D:\Win*
    dir E:\Win*
    until you find the Windows folder.

I'll post the instructions for Phase 2 when you report the correct drive letter.
