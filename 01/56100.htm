jarem say:

Yep. This was a known DPI scaling issue that exists well before Windows 10 that Microsoft has yet to solve. It involves connecting a monitor of a lower or higher dpi and is not hardware related.

Basically what happens is that the explorer shell as well as non-dpi-scaling-aware win32 applications (such as Photoshop, for example) aren't "refreshed" when you connect your monitor. Your icons and apps continue to draw at the higher DPI making things huge when your monitor is connected; and when you restart or sign out/in, they are correctly sized on your monitor until you disconnect it, causing things to be super tiny (since they're now drawing at the lower dpi on a much higher dpi display).

UWP (and modern applications), like Microsoft Edge, are unaffected because they're adaptive to DPI changes by nature of its UI rendering.

While it is quite annoying, you can somewhat resolve it without rebooting. Instead, try signing out and back into Windows, and things will be normal.. that is until you undock and all your icons/(some Win32 apps) are the wrong size again - just wash and repeat.

Until Microsoft fixes this (very common) scenario, the only other way around it is to use a monitor with a higher resolution (like a 4k monitor) which you can comfortably match the DPI of your Surface. This way the scaling is not adjusted when you attach your monitor.

Mod Edit: Please view Windows scaling issues for high-DPI devices for more information and current solutions.
