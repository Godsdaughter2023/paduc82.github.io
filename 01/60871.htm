Many of Directly's so-called 'independent advisors' can do little more than copy and paste generic troubleshooting from their notes, or from something they saw on the internet that sort of sounds like your issue. To their credit, they are certainly polite, but they don't have much of a knowledge base.

The resolution to your question turns on this: When you press the physical 'power on' button on your computer, what happens?

To be excruciatingly specific, the physical 'power on' button is like the light switch that you flip when you enter a room in order to turn on the lights. You press this button with your finger, not with a mouse or a keyboard.

If nothing at all happens, or if something starts to happen and then stops and your computer looks like you never pressed the physical 'power on' button in the first place, then you have a problem with your hardware. By 'hardware' I mean the physical components of your computer (to be excruciatingly specific,) not with Windows. So your computer dies or sputters to a sad death before Windows even gets a chance to load.

If that is what's happening, this community forum can't help you because your problem isn't with Windows or with any other software. You have to show your computer to a technician who will tell you what part of your hardware is broken and needs replacing. It might be your power supply (you'll be eating sandwhiches for lunch for a few days) or your motherboard (for a few weeks) or, if you're lucky, the physical power button itself may need to be replaced.

a. user say:

About the quality of the help you get in online forums ... best to not get me started.

When describing a problem, it's most helpful if you can use the correct terminology. That's difficult for many people and who can blame them? It's no easy thing to come up with the correct technical terms for what you are trying to describe. But it's really helpful if you can do it.

On the other hand, I have gone to great lengths to describe a problem in detail, as well as providing a complete, technically correct description of what I am experiencing and what I have done to troubleshoot the problem so far. And I still get the same cr*p answers from people who basically have no idea how to help me.

What you are seeing, when you see anything at all, is called a BIOS screen (even though computers haven't used a BIOS in years.) OEMs sometimes replace the usual BIOS screens with their own logo. There's reasons for that. The term 'BIOS screen' is also used to describe the configuration choices you can make with the BIOS or the EFI (for modern computers.)

A 'splash' screen is eye candy that you'll see while a program is loading. There's reasons for that too. A lock screen is what you see after you lock your computer.

You've got a hardware problem, since no software has loaded at the time your problem occurs. The only thing close to software that's running at the time is the BIOS itself, and that's actually firmware. I might be inclined to think that the BIOS code is somehow corrupted - which is really hard to do - but that wouldn't account for when you press the power button and zippo happens.
