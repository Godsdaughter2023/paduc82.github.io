It emulates this
 
To find 10 % of 20
 
20 x (10/100) = 2
 
Twenty (the whole) multiplied by ten (the percentage required), then divided into 100 parts (the percentage) equals a part size of two
 
A slightly harder example
 
To find 8% of 17
 
17 x (8/100)
 
Seventeen multiplied by eight, then divided into 100 parts gives a part size of 1.36 (to two decimal places)
 
On Windows Calculator, this equates to
 
+ 17 *8 /100 (the percentage now shows in the window)
