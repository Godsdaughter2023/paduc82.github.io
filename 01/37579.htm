teethterror say:

Hello! I feel your pain, as I've been having the same problem. Just built a new PC specifically for drawing (in Sai) only to have Windows pee the bed. Luckily, I've found a solution that has worked for me, so hopefully it will work for you.

OKAY so first off, you need to disable Windows Ink Workspace. Which you said you already know how to do, but just in case someone reading this doesn't:

  1. Press the Windows Key + R. 
  2. In Open:, copy and paste this: regedit
  3. Press Okay. Windows may ask you to give permission, just say yes.
  4. In the new window, navigate to: Computer\HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Policies\Microsoft
  5. Right click on the Microsoft file and select New > Key.
  6. Name the new key WindowsInkWorkspace
  7. While in WindowsInkWorkspace, right click anywhere on empty space. Select New > DWORD (32-bit) Value
  8. Name it AllowWindowsInkWorkspace and leave the value at 0.
  9. Close everything and restart your computer. Poof! Ink is gone. You will notice the icon is missing.

Now that Windows Ink has been shut off, time to handle the Wacom side of things. Again, you said you've disabled Windows Ink in there, but now it's making you be more specific about it.

  1. Make sure all Sai windows have been closed.
  2. Open Wacom Tablet Properties.
  3. At the top of the window, there is a section called Application. In it, you will see "All Other" and then probably "Photoshop", if you have photoshop. Click on the plus sign to the right of this menu.
  4. You will get a new window named "Add Application for Custom Settings". Click on the "Browse" button and find your Sai's exe. It's wherever you saved your Sai file. With your exe located, click 'Okay'.
  5. Now that you've added it, select Sai in the Applications menu at the top of the window.
  6. Go to Mapping and uncheck the box labeled "Use Windows Ink"
  7. Close the Wacom Tablet Properties and your settings should save. Open Sai to check if it worked!

I don't know why you have to both disable Ink and make specific settings for Sai, but whatever gets the job done, I guess!

Hope that helped someone!
