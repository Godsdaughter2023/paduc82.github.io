Nope, there's no setting for this.  That's just simply the way it has always been since Remote Desktop was invented (previously known in the 1990's as Microsoft Terminal Services Client or MSTSC).

At the heart of the issue is that the programs running on the desktop have no way of knowing that the display size has changed, and even if they did, they also cannot know what their previous size was.  It's simply not stored.

rgharper911 say:

There is no "maximum" as such, the performance hit depends on a great many factors.  If you have a slow processor, slow hard drive, or a graphic card that doesn't perform well in Windows then folders will always render more slowly with large numbers of files in them.  They'll render more quickly with a faster hard drive, faster processor, or better graphic card - but more files will always render more slowly than less files will just because of the overhead involved in displaying that many files.

a. user say:

Hi Tom,

It's odd that the update causes an issue with the graphic cards on your computer. The compatibility of your driver could be the culprit here. We suggest that you update your graphic cards driver in Device manager. Here are the steps on how to do so:

  1. Click the Start menu, then search for Device Manager.
  2. Expand the cate3gory for your graphic card/s, then right-click on it and choose Properties.
  3. Go to the Driver tab, then click on Update Driver.
  4. Select Search automatically for updated driver software.

Note: If Windows doesn't find a new driver, you can try looking for one on the manufacturer's website of your graphic card.
