euler13 say:

@YoyoThroopy: Sadly this a long term issue which Microsoft have not managed to fix yet. If you scan through the pages of this forum you will see a large number of threads with the same problem. I had exactly the same issue with Fallout 4 and the first time it took 4 hours 10 minutes, but for other people it has taken over night or even days.

Whatever you do, DO NOT follow Vorpal1986's advice. If you remove your account from your console it will remove all you local game saves. Every game you try to play after that will need to sync with the MS cloud and download your game save data again, and the source of the problem is the MS cloud. The extent of the problem for other games probably won't be quite as great as with Fallout 4, because other games will generally have smaller game save files. My original Fallout 4 file was a couple of GBs in size, compared with, say, Rise of the Tomb Raider, which was around 30MB. However, even that took an hour to sync!

When it does finally resync, and it will if you're patient, then when you get the game up and running I suggest that you delete non-critical game save points in case it happens again. In other words, keep your game save file as small as possible rather than having different saves every time you, say, enter a building. I tend to keep saves from critical decision junctions in the game and have a rotation of a few saves I use for my current mission.
