mann_manohar say:

Hi Ridge

 

  1. What is the make and model number of your Printer?

 

Follow the below steps to set print spooler service to restart automatically when computer boots.
 
1. Go to Start / Run and type in "services.msc" without quotation marks, then press Enter or click OK.
 
2. In the window that opens (Services) look for "Print Spooler" on the right
 
3. Right-click "Print Spooler" and select "Properties".
 
4. Click on the "Recovery" tab and change all 3 drop-down boxes to "Restart the Service".
 
5. Make sure the 2 text boxes underneath say "1" in them.
 
6. Click Ok until you are back at the Services window, close it and you're good to go.
 
7. Go back into the properties of the "Print Spooler" tab and be sure to click Start to restart the Print Spooler service and it will restart the service immediately.
 
8. The Print spooler depends on the Remote Procedure Call (RPC) service. Is this service disabled? Go to services.msc look for RPC. Check state: Automatic

peteredwards_702 and peteredwardsur say:

group policy

how do I access group policy?

tybar say:

If you are a systems administrator, install the RSAT tools on your Windows computer. After installation, open the Group Policy Management mmc and search the list of group policies in your domain. RSAT = Remote Server Administration Tools

If the group policy is local to your computer click the Windows Start icon and type gpedit. When the Local Group Policy Editor opens, expand the Computer or User configuration to view settings.

You can also run a Resultant Set of Policy on your computer to determine which GPOs are applying to your user/computer accounts. Open a command line in elevated mode and navigate to your desktop or some other directory/folder. Type the following gpresult /r > gpo.txt. The output will be in a text file in the current directory. That's why I suggest navigating to your desktop. The output will display GPOs applied to your comptuer/user account and GPOs which were filtered out. You can also see computer/user security group memberships.
