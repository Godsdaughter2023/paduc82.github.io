Manually set the network adapter of the computer that has connectivity problems to half-duplex and a lower speed. With full-duplex, transmit and receive paths are separate. Therefore, you can transmit and receive at the same time, and collisions are prevented. Because of the increased throughput and lack of collisions, full-duplex is more susceptible to bad cable terminations or to cable attenuation that exceeds recommended limits. This can generate data retransmissions that become sufficient to degrade performance.

Connect the system to a switch that is configured to half-duplex and 10-Mbps, or use a 10-Mbps hub, to see whether connection can be established at a lower transmission speed.

Follow the steps to change the duplex mode for Ethernet card:

  • Press Windows+X keys and select Device Manager.
  • Locate the network adapters. Expand the driver icon.
  • Right Click on the Ethernet card and select Properties.
  • Click on Advanced tab. Locate Link speed and duplex mode or search for Speed and Duplex option from the drop down menu.
