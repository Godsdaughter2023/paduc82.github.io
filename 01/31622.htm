n4aof say:

I'm using WLM2011, not 2009, so I can't be sure, but the behavior he describes matches the default behavior of WLM2011.

 

IF he is experiencing what I think he is describing, his messages may not be gone at all.  When using WLM with a IMAP account, WLM fails to ever move "deleted" messages to the Trash folder. Instead, the messages remain in the INBOX just flagged as "deleted" - by default WLM (at least WLM2011) hides the "deleted" messages in the IMAP INBOX so they look like they are gone forever while they are actually just sitting in your INBOX.

 

In WLM2011, under ACCOUNTS, there is an IMAP Setting on the ribbon bar to "Show deleted messages" -- once you select this, the "deleted" messages are no longer hidden and you can see them all still in your inbox (they are displayed in a strikethrough font in the message list).

 

So far as I have been able to find, WLM2001 apparently does not include the functions you mentioned to automatically Purge deleted messages when leaving IMAP folders -- and much more seriously, it also doesn't provide any way to purge the deleted messages manually without more tweaking of its default settings.

 

Under OPTIONS - MAIL - ADVANCED, you need to put a check in the box for "Use the 'Deleted Items' folder for IMAP accounts."   Once you do this, WLM2011 will move "deleted" IMAP messages into the Trash folder, then you can use the function to empty the trash folder to purge the messages.
