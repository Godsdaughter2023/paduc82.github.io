SOLVED: Check your audio receiver, Audio error 0x80bd0009

Ok after months of having to deal with uncompressed stereo sound coming from my xbox one S, I couldn't select any other option besides "uncompressed stereo" without getting an error saying my receiver couldnt do it. Finally after searching countless forums with no particular answer I finally got it. In my particular case, using the Sony STR-DH770. Go to [Amp Menu]-->[HDMI]-->[HDMI Audio Out]-->AMP. I didnt change any settings in the xbox itself, but my current settings on the xbox were, Display 4k UHD, everything my tv would allow me to check in "Video Modes", and "Video fidelity & overscan" was set to "Auto-detect.(recommended)". The Documents link on the start menu is a shortcut to the Documents library, which by default shows contents of both public documents and your documents.  It sounds to me like the library is no longer gathering your true documents folder.  Here's how to verify and restore that relationship:

1 - Click Start, then click Documents
2 - At the top you should see Document Library in green, and a blue link underneath that says "Includes 1 Location".  Click that blue link.
3 - Is your documents folder is in that list?  If it already is, then this suggestion doesn't fit this circumstance (reply back to us).

4 - If it is not listed, here's an easy way to fix it:  cancel that screen and close the folder you opened in step 1.  Then copy/paste this into the start search box and hit enter:

%userprofile%

In the folder that opens, right-click Documents and choose "Include in Library: Documents"
