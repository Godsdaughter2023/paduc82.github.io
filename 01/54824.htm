Also, the calibration procedure will tell you to "please remain quiet" during certain points in calibration. This means that YOU should remain quiet... many people have said that it's actually better to have 'typical' background noise. Furnace, A/C, fans, anything that might cause background noise, if it's possible, have one of (preferably the loudest of) those things running. Why? Because when it asks you to be quiet, it is setting its sensitivity to you, so that it can differentiate between you and typical background noise... not complete silence.

During the calibration process, the sensor actually uses the satellite speakers for a baseline measurement. If it can't 'hear' a speaker that is normally there during the calibration, how can it determine if a sound is coming from it (and not you or something else) when it is in use? I have a 5.1 SS system and I always calibrate with it set up just like it is when I'm gaming.

Anyway, these pointers are what I follow when I calibrate (which some say you should do multiple times and actually continue doing it regularly).
