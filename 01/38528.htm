cmdrkeene say:

The short answer is: that depends.  Microsoft doesn't make touchpads, and every computer builder who does supplies their own settings software for it.  Most manufacturers add an extra tab into the standard Mouse Control Panel for their touchpad settings, so I would check there first.

You can get to the Mouse Control Panel by going here:

1) Right-click Start button, choose Control Panel

2) Go to Hardware and Sound

3) Choose "Mouse"

4) Look for a touchpad tab (may be named similar depending on your computer company)

If you don't see it, try the Hardware tab and see if you can select your touchpad in the list and then click settings.

If still not, you'll have to ask Toshiba where they put the options for the model computer you have.
