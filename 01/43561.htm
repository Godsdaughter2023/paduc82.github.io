how do I create a file association for an xlsx file to open with an older version of excel?

I have Office XP Pro installed on my Windows 10 computer, which does include excel...but when I try to open an "xlsx" file, I get an error that tells me there is no file associated to open this with -- argh!  how do I associate my older version of office (which I love) with this file type?  I do NOT want to have to buy another program...

Thanks in advance for your help.

a. user say:

Files are associated with executables, so .xslx is associated with excel.exe, which has been the same executable for every version of Excel.

The open XML formats (.docx, .xslx, ,pptx) began with Office 2010 or maybe Office 2007, but they definitely aren't supported by Office XP, so Office XP won't open them. Here are some choices:

Microsoft used to have an Office file viewer that would open (but not edit) Office formats even if Office wasn't installed. Unfortunately, the file viewer was retired in April (2018) and isn't available from Microsoft any longer.

If you can find a newer version of Office, use it to 'Save As' your file in the older .xsl format.

Open the file in the free Office Online and 'Save As' in the older .xsl format.

You can probably open the file in Google Sheets with minimal loss of formatting, if any at all. And, of course, virtually any of the free Office alternatives will open and edit your file.

Office XP isn't supported on Windows 10, so you should consider yourself fortunate that it works for you.

Note that when you save an Open XML format in the older version, you may lose some formatting, but this won't be a problem unless you're really into fancy-looking spreadsheets.

a. user say:

Hi,

To assist you better, I would like to know what the content inside the .ISO format disk is.

An ISO file contains the image of a disk. That means it contains all the files and folders that were on that disk, much like a .zip or .cab file contains a collection of files and folders. The real difference is that an ISO is a byte-for-byte copy of the low-level data actually stored on a disk.

The best option to open the files inside the .ISO is to burn the file to a DVD or CD and once done with that, you may be able to open the contents inside the files.

jat_fl say:

So with HDMI connecting the Apple TV and your Television Set. 

Verify the following::

  1. iTunes is running on your computer, parental controls blocking library sharing or restricting content are not enabled within iTunes, AND you are signed in with your Apple ID in the Home Sharing section (library sharing is a different feature).   Both are required for Apple TV to access the content on your computer. 
    1. If there was an error displayed on your PC, confirm that your antivirus software or firewall is not denying an inbound request from Apple TV to your computer. reboot before proceeding.
  2. Confirm that you can play one of the video or audio files on your computer with it's current configuration.   Once verified stop the content but leave iTunes running.
    1. Playback issues at this point would indicate issues with PC, Firewall, or Content access.
  3. On the Apple TV you need to be connected to the same network as your computer (wired or wireless)  AND you are signed in with the same Apple ID in Step 1.
    1. Go to Apple TV settings -> Computers and sign in at this location.  *
    2. After you sign in with your Apple ID you should see your computer device name displayed. 
      1. If you do not see your device name displayed recheck your computer for error and the firewall for denied access.  If none are found then a network block exists somewhere within your home network which is not permitting the communication between Apple TV & your computer.
    3. Navigate to the main Apple TV menu when successful.
  4. Select Computers (Green Icon) on Apple TV menu. 
    1. Content is displayed (divided by type)
      1. If content is not displayed again check your computer for errors, firewall denial of access AND that you are still signed in with the same Apple ID in Home Sharing.
    2. Attempt playback of video content any shuddering is an indication of insufficient bandwidth to receive the video file. 
      1. Normally an indication of wireless isn't able to handle the traffic required to send video.  Consider making the apple TV and/or the computer a wired connection for more consistent bandwidth.
      2. If just certain video files will not play then the format of that content is not compatible with your Apple TV.  (see info for your particular version to see what file types are supported)

      Hope that helps!
