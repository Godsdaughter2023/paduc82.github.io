hosch250 say:

Go to the Charms and search Trackpad.  Go to the Settings tab and open the Settings link.  Uncheck the box that says Disable Edge Swipe and hit OK.  The left, right, and top edge swipes will be disabled.  The left swipe cycles through open programs, the right swipe opens the charms, and the top swipe brings up the All Apps button on when you are on the start screen.

ellieritchie say: What's the Charms?

hosch250 say:

It is the bar on the right side of the screen that pops up when you do a swipe form the right side of the track-pad, go Win-C on the keyboard, or put your cursor in either of the corners on the right side of the screen.  You can also search in it by just typing on the start screen, it will automatically pop up and start searching.

Yes, you can use that retail Windows 7 Home Premium DVD as it was Home Premium you had before.  If you had 64 bit Windows 7 before then you will want the 64 bit DVD.  If you had 32 bit Windows 7 before then you will want the 32 bit DVD.  If you had 64 bit Windows 7 before then you can use the 32 bit DVD if you have no choice and you might never notice the difference.

When you speak to them be sure to explain that the motherboard failed and was replaced by the same type of motherboard as anything that sounds like an upgraded motherboard would not be eligible for activation.
