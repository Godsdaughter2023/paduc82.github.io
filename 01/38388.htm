You do not replace the thermal paste unless the machine was taken apart and the cooling system was removed from the CPU.

Who told you it had to be replaced?

Delidding is done by those who buy performance desktop PCs that are used for gaming to replace the paste with a high grade paste.  Rarely done with a laptop.

Other than that, the paste should remain good for about 30 years of normal use (that is, if paste is even used on its particular design).  Paste is not used on all machines.

A lot of paste that comes with low cost PCs is real cheap grade and is the reason why some people replace it with liquid metal based paste.  Expensive but you do see results.

Some of these newer laptops and tablets with Intel Core i5 CPUs no longer need CPU fans and the i7 models really don't need that much either.  For those, they normally look to using a heat sink with off-set cooling fans.  Those setups usually use a thermal tape which is a paste on a thin layer of mylar.

If you do replace the paste, you will have to spend for the high cost version for best results.

e.      Right-click on the printer and select “Use Printer Online”.

f.        The display should change to ready when the printer is set to online.
