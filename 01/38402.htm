Surface Pro 2 Battery Only Holding 2 Hours Of Charge (Or Less) - Microsoft Community

jdaddy4c say:

Surface Pro 2 Battery Only Holding 2 Hours Of Charge (Or Less)

I've had my Surface Pro 2 for quite a while and never had any batter life issues. One of the main reasons I got it was not to have to worry about charging often. All of a sudden it only has two hours or less of battery life. Is there a fix? Thx!

paulo reichert say:

Can you open a command prompt (assuming Windows 10: right click start button and select Command Prompt) and once in the command prompt, type the following commands:

C:\Users\<your user>>cd Desktop

C:\Users\<your user>\Desktop>powercfg /batteryreport /output battery_report.html

The commands above will generate a battery_report.html file on your desktop. In that file look for a section called "Installed Batteries" and an entry called "Cycle Count" in there. Also look for a section called "Battery Capacity History" and check for the most recent values in "Full Charge Capacity" and "Design Capacity".

Lithium-Ion batteries lose capacity over time. That happens as they complete full charge cycles (full discharge -> recharge) and a full charge cycle doesn't have to be in one session (use device to zero battery, fully charge) but two sessions where you use 50% of the battery should account for a full charge cycle. As these cycles happen, they take away a little bit of the maximum capacity the battery can hold.

If the current charge capacity in your battery is significantly lower than the design capacity then you don't have many options other than either accepting that your device will last less time on a single charge or contacting Microsoft support to check whether they can replace the battery for you for a new one, but that will incur a cost.

However, if the full charge capacity is close to the design capacity then the next step would be to look at software you have on your machine that can explain unusual battery drain.

Feel free to post the battery report here if you want us to have a look.

electricmal say:

volunteer j say:
When trying to import video files to Movie Maker I get the message; M2U00017.MPG could not be imported. An interface has too many methods to fire events from

What does this mean and how do I solve it so I can import video files to edit?
=======================================
Movie Maker has issues with .mpg files...best bet would be
to convert the .mpg files to the .wmv format before importing.

Many formats are reportedly compatible with
Movie Maker but the most reliable choices are:

Photos - bmp
Video -   wmv or dv-avi
Music -   wma, wav, .wmv

See the following article:

Movie Maker 2 - Importing MPEG Files
http://www.papajohn.org/MM2-Importing-Video-MPEG2.html

The following freeware can do the conversion:

(FWIW...it's always a good idea to create a System
Restore point before installing software or updates)

Format Factory
http://www.pcfreetime.com/
(FWIW...you may want to uncheck
all the boxes on the final screen)

After downloading and installing Format Factory...
open the program and choose an Output Folder...
(this is where you will find your files after they are
converted)

Drag/drop your video clips onto the main screen...

Select 'All to ???' / OK...
(the ??? represents your choice of format)

Click...Start...on the toolbar...

That should do it...

Good luck...
