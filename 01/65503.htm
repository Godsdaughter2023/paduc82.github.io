Currently, I have it set up like this:

Modem > router > desktop PC (via WiFi) > Xbox 360 (via Ethernet plugged into my mobo Ethernet port)

When I try to connect to Xbox Live, I get the "IP Address Error" notification.  I have followed, to the best of my ability, the instructions on these three pages:

http://support.xbox.com/en-US/xbox-360/networking/win-computer

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/using-internet-connection-sharing#1TC=windows-7

markherrington say:

I was wrong.  I figured everything out.  I had not selected my Local Area Connection because I enabled it after enabling ICS.  Neither ICS nor bridging require anything more than an Ethernet cable.

markherrington say:

I figured it out.  I had enabled ICS prior to enabling my LAN.  Neither a switch nor a router are required for ICS and bridging.
