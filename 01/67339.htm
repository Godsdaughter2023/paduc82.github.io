rrhain say:

How to stop Windows from entering phantom spaces when copying/pasting words

Is there any way to convince Windows that when I am selecting text, I don't want the space on the end of the word?  One of the most annoying things about Windows is the logic it uses with regard to selection.  I am very grateful that you can select an entire word by double-clicking on it, but it is maddening that when you do, it includes the space on the end.  That means when I paste it wherever it needs to go, I have to delete the space.  Does nobody at Redmond understand that words do not include spaces?

And now it seems that Outlook will add a space at the beginning of words that are pasted in.  It's a specific circumstance:  If you are composing a message in Outlook (I'm using 2010) and have started a quotation, then if you paste in text after the quotation mark, Outlook will insert a space *before* the text you pasted in.  I now have to go back and delete the space between the opening quotation mark and the pasted text.  As an example, suppose I have the following in my message:

And I quote, "

If I then copy some plain text from somewhere and paste it in after that quote, Outlook puts in a space between the quotation mark and my pasted text:

And I quote, " Here is my pasted text.

What is that space doing there?  It wasn't in that I copied.  Why did Outlook insert it?

The logic behind selection and pasting should be very simple:

1)  Do not add anything that wasn't in the original selection.  You paste what is on the clipboard and nothing else.

2)  Auto-selection should follow natural breaks in text layout.  That means no extra spaces unless I deliberately slide my mouse over them to include them.  Thus, double-clicking on a word selects the word and only the word.  No spaces in front of or behind it.  This also means no punctuation or symbols.  Numbers should count as letters (so that when you double-click on a piece of hex code, you get the whole thing), but things like hyphens, punctuation marks, and other symbols are to be excluded from a simple double-click.  If you do a double-click-and-hold to start dragging over text, then follow those natural breaks:  Grab the space at the end as a jump, then the next word, then the punctuation mark at the end, then the space after the punctuation mark, etc.  This way, I am the one telling the computer what I want to select, rather than being forced to take characters I don't want.

This needs to apply even if you start doing a single-click-and-drag when it starts selecting words:  No spaces unless I deliberately drag over them.

rrhain say: Please try again and don't use Word 2010 (since it seems to have some logic in it about how to handle pasted content with a space on the end) but something else like Notepad.  For example, go to the following URL in Internet Explorer:

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows/buy?ocid=GA8_O_WOL_Hero_Home_FPP_Null

Double-click on the word "Clicking" in the first paragraph under "Upgrading is easy."  Notice that the space on the end of the word has been chosen and you don't have any way to not choose it.

If you then double-click-and-hold on the word "Clicking" and then start dragging to the right, you'll notice that every word you move over includes the space on the end.  At this point, copy the text you have selected.

Now indeed, if you were to go into Word at this point, put your insertion point on an empty line, and then paste in the text you just copied, there wouldn't be a space.  And, if you were to put your insertion point right in front of a word that is already in the document and paste in the text, you'd get a space between the text you put in and the word that was in there.  As I mentioned, Word seems to have some logic regarding this and that's fine.

But go into Notepad and paste in the text you selected.  There's that space on the end.  That's because it was selected when you copied it and you didn't have any way not to select it.

Now, here in this forum (answers.microsoft.com), there seems to be some control over this, but it is a bit weird:  If you double-click on a word, the space on the end of it is included.  If you double-click-and-hold, the space is included but as soon as you move the mouse, the space is taken off.  It then starts behaving exactly as I would like it to as you drag over more words:  The word is highlighted, but the space on the end is not included until you actually drag over the space.

There is a way to fake this on the URL mentioned above:  If you double-click-and-hold, you get the space on the end.  Drag to the right to include words beyond the ending point of what you would like to select.  Then, drag backwards.  The system suddenly realizes that spaces on the ends of words aren't necessarily wanted and will allow you to remove them from the selection.

Note, however, if you only want one word, you have to select at least two words beyond that one in order for the drag-forward/drag-backward trick to work:  If you double-click-and-hold on just one word and then drag-forward on only one word, dragging backward drops the selection of the first word and instead reverts to if you had only done a single-click-and-hold, selecting only from the spot you had originally clicked rather than the entire word.

All this logic and back-filling for what should really be a simple process:

1)  Single-click-and-hold:  Start selecting from the position you clicked.
2)  Double-click-and-hold:  Start with the entire word you clicked on, no space.
3)  When dragging, do not include the space unless it is actually selected by the drag.
