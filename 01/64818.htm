E. USB and Hardware issues:

Removing hardware by just pulling it off the USB plug is the worst way of handling your PC, always use the XP, Vista and W7 Safety Remove Hardware and Eject Media tool. Refresh your USB drivers as follows:

First you needaccess to see all hidden files inside your PC. Right click Computer > Properties > Advanced System Settings at the left side > in the Advance tab select Environment Variables > in System Variables click New and enter: "devmgr_show_nonpresent_devices" without the parenthesis and enter 1 for the Value. The above instruction you can safely use in XP, Vista, and W7, and is intended to be used only on single non network PC ( never ever on a LAN, where some PCs connect their underside to a docking station and use another keyboard and monitor to interact or the USBs are assigned to a specific fixed driver). To have a better understanding of the conflict that is caused by removing the hardware from the USB, by only pulling the plug and not using the Safety Remove Hardware and Eject Media tool, please do the following: (W7 added the words Eject Media)

Plug the first same device and now in XP and Vista right click it and select Safely Remove and unplug. No transparent icon is shown.

Sometimes even using the Safely Remove feature, the Windows driver is left assigned to a specific USB port, this is because the hardware came with exclusive, non-Windows assigned driver, and the USB was disabled, in this case Windows will reserve that same USB port position and expects the same hardware to be plugged again, this USB icons have a black arrow pointing down. Is important to check and uninstall the transparent USB icons. When in doubt Uninstall, don’t Disable. If you have not done the maintenance, don't blame any of the Windows OS for the USB conflicts when installing your hardware devices. After you finish, check for transparency on all devices in the Device Manager and see how many belong to things that you no longer use. How many months or years have passed since you used and removed some of the devices shown? Do the maintenance and you will have perfectly working USB ports. Again, when in doubt Uninstall, don’t Disable.
