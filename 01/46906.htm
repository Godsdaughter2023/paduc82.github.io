considerate_guy say:

One way is to use the automatic "ducking" feature in Movie Maker 2012. On the Project tab, there are several buttons for which track you want to emphasize. For example, if you choose "Emphasize video" then whenever there is significant sound happening in your video (say someone is talking) then the background music will automatically get quieter. If there is no talking, then the music volume will automatically go up.

The more time-consuming method is to split your music at the various points where you want different volumes. Splits are made by selecting the rectangle representing the song (in the storyboard/project area to the right). Then play until you get to the point where you want to change the volume and make a split in the song (shortcut is M on the keyboard). Then make another split at the end of the section.

Now double-click on that portion of the song (which should bring up the Music Tools tab) and change its volume and set it lower. Repeat manually for other sections where you want to make it softer. But again, try the automatic method first (assuming you've updated to the latest Movie Maker 2012).
