4. If Required enable USB Boot

6. Save BIOS changes, restart your System and it should boot from the Installation Media

fahimulla k say:

Hi semofo,

While most new broadcasts are in HD programming, a significant amount of TV broadcasts are still sent in the conventional 4:3 ratio. It's the difference in shape between those two ratios that can result in a "pillar boxed" picture — one with black columns standing to the left and right of the image — when you watch a conventional 4:3 program on your widescreen TV.

Contents provided by Netflix come in both 4:3 and 16:9 format.

If the 4:3 ratio movies are stretched to fill the 16:9 screen, then the movie will flatten as it is happening with you. It is very much normal as it depends upon the aspect ratio of the source file.

I guess I found a resolution that the TV does not support, because now, the TV no longer recognizes the HDMI input.

I have tried all the project modes, but neither duplicate, extend or second monitor only works. What happens when I connect is that the computer finds the HDMI and the screen goes blank, only to resume some 10 seconds later, then nothing happens for 3 seconds and then it searches again. I have tried different laptop resolutions and reinstalling the graphics drivers, but nothing works.

I guess what I want to do is to reset the external display settings to default, but how do I do this?

Thanks!
trippma say:

Assuming the Vaio display is still active when you're connected to the TV:

  • Set the displays to duplicate.
  • Open Control Panel> Display
  • Click Change display settings from the list on left side of the window
  • From the pull down menu labeled Display select monitor number 2.
  • From the pull down menu labeled Resolution select the desired resolution. 1920x1080 is most likely the correct choice
  • Click Apply

If you can't change any settings while connected to the TV and you're comfortable with editing the Registry:

  • Start regedit
  • Go to HKLM\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Control\GraphicsDrivers\Configuration
  • Delete the keys that reference your TV. For example my 2nd display is a Gateway and the associated key begins with GWY
  • Close the registry editor, restart (just for the heck of it), and reconnect the TV

Hope this helps

bill smithers say:

Hi,

I am sorry but you will have to completely reinstall the Operating System after you replace the Motherboard with a different Motherboard.

That "Startup Repair" is just that: A Startup Repair NOT a System Repair

 

What has happened is that the existing operating system on your hard drive contains the drivers for the old and failed Motherboard and you can not change drivers by doing what you are doing.

The only way to fix this is to reinstall Vista.

You wipe the hard drive clean and start again and make sure the Repair Shop gave you the disk to install the new Motherboard Drivers from after you reinstall vista or download them from the Motherboard maker's website.

 

And here is a question for you to consider, too.

If you bought a retail copy of Vista yourself you may use the above method of a clean installation then install the new Motherboard Drivers from disk.

But, if your Computer is a bought Manufacturers brand, HP, Toshiba etc, you can not change the Motherboard as the OEM Vista Licence is locked to the original Manufacturer's Motherboard that it was installed on and you have now voided your Vista Licence.
