balaji kundalam say:

OEM, acronym for Original Equipment Manufacturer, is used to refer manufacturers of devices as a whole unit. For example, HP, Dell, Acer, Lenovo are OEMs. OEM products, thus, comes with fully connected hardware and software (depends on configuration). On the other hand, there are manufacturers of individual components, referred as retail manufacturers. Examples include Asus, Gigabyte, Biostar, Seagate, Transcend, etc. They manufacture individual components only that make up a complete PC, like hard disk drives, RAM, motherboard, etc. Windows shipped with computers preinstalled is referred as OEM licence and Windows purchased from stores is referred as retail license. 

1. Hence, as a PC, your's is an OEM PC. However, legitimacy of Windows and being OEM are not related. Windows shipped with the PC preinstalled are legitimate copies, however.

2. In your scenario, you can reinstall Windows 8 that came preinstalled in your PC and go for free upgrade to Windows 10. Or if you're planning to purchase a new Windows license, you can purchase Windows 10 as Windows 7 is no more available for sales.

3. As said earlier, Windows 10 upgrade is available for legitimate copies of Windows only and need not be OEM. It can also be retail.

4. Purchasing a new product key is equivalent to purchasing a new Windows copy, and this is the recommended step suggested by Microsoft.
