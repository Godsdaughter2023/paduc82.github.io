Okay I was able to resolve this issue by the "hunt and peck" method and I will post this in the event that someone else might have the same "dilemma".

  1. Go to your task bar and select the folder "file explorer" > left click
  2. Than with a DVD or CD already in your PC,  click on "this PC" (left column)
  3. Go to BD Rom drive and "right click" > select "pin to quick access"
  4. Your drive than shows up in "frequent folders"
  5. You  than are  finally able to "right click" the BD-ROM drive and scroll down to "create shortcut"

There it is the "new and improved" way to "Create a shortcut"?? Thanks Windows 10 Geeks!

Click the 'Power Management' tab

Uncheck 'Allow the computer to turn off this device....'

Click 'OK'

Although technically 50GB will be sufficient because it exceeds the hardware requirements in practice it is going to be very small and will potentially run out very quickly.

If you have other storage then making a few changes as soon as you have installed Windows can move some of the folders onto another drive and reduce their impact on the C: drive.
