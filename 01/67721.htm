considerate_guy say:

Let me preface my answer by saying I'm just a member of the Movie Maker community and don't have any direct ties to the Movie Maker team.  Thus I can't answer any questions about the design of the product, why it behaves a certain way, whether it could do something, etc.

 

I see your dilemma of wanting to merge two projects into one big project.  The usual response to such a request is the one you already mentioned of creating each as movies and then putting them together as two .wmv files in a new project.  You mentioned the problems of having a caption and a transition at this point.  My response to that would be to apply the caption and the transition in the final project when you join the two movies.  In that way the caption isn't part of the transition and you hopefully get what you want.

 

Regarding your concern about loss of resolution, that is true.  You are suffering one generation of lossy > lossy, but in my experience it isn't that noticeable.  If you are concerned, you could try increasing the bitrate in a custom save profile to reduce the amount of information that is lost on each generation.

 

The only other method I can suggest is to open the second project, copy one part of it, then close it, paste it into the first project, then save.  Return to the second project and copy the next item, etc.  That's pretty painful and tedious however.  Your suggestion of trying to edit the XML in the .wlmp project is a good one but there is a lot of dependency between the different parts and I don't think it would be easy to figure out the new offsets, renumber items, etc.  There isn't a published spec on the XML contents, so you would be flying blind.  I wouldn't even try that myself, even after having played a lot with editing those files to see what they do.
