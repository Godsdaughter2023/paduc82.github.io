BTW if you have a real numeric keyboard a possible substitute for a "real mouse" would be Mousekeys.   Press and hold LeftAlt+LeftShift+NumLock to activate Mousekeys.   Choose the state of NumLock that you want to have Mousekeys active with.  Also, I find it helpful to have its icon in the Notification area showing permanently as an indicator of the NumLock state.   Then to make your pointer show you would just do something with the Numeric keypad which would make the pointer show, such as press any of the eight keys designated for moving the pointer.

Another handy thing to have available is the Lens Magnifier.   E.g. press Win-+ (Ctrl-Alt-L if necessary to switch to the Lens) and then see where your pointer is that way.   Note that you can further enhance your awareness of the Magnifier's presence by using its Reverse video option.   Press Win-Esc to turn off the Magnifier if you can't spot its on-screen control.   <eg>
