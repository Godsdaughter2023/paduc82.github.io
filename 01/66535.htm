considerate_guy say:

Movie Maker can generate videos in one of two aspect ratios (Widescreen 16:9 and Standard 4:3). This is done from the Project tab and you click on the desired aspect ratio for your project.

But this only manages the final output. It has no effect on your source files. It sounds like you have 4:3 source files or files that otherwise are not 16:9 (say with vertical video). There is no capability within Movie Maker to stretch the video horizontally to fit into widescreen. Instead you'll see your video with black bars on the side.

Movie Maker is aiming to keep  your original aspect ratio so that you don't end up stretching things horizontally making things appear squat and fat. I understand some people may be okay with that, but Movie Maker doesn't let you do it.

So the only thing you can do is film your original footage in the desired aspect ratio (16:9) to begin with. Set your project to match and you should have no back bars.

A window to select the printer port appears. We needed the USB port and it's all the way down the list beside "Use an existing port". You choose your appropriate port.

A manual search "Install the printer driver" window appears. Ignore device list for now and select "Windows Update" button under the device list.
