This reminds me of the original X Box D.V.D. remote...

Was working a lot hours, and didn't have a lot of hours for gaming and watching movies. After voicing my dissatisfaction (In a polite manner.) to the X Box rep, he gave me something to try...Pull the batteries out, press each button twice starting with the top left of the controller, put the batteries back in and try it. It worked! He explained that static electricity builds up in remotes when not in use, and that pulling the batteries and pressing the buttons discharges it. Found one of the other remotes and tried it, and that one started working also.

So it is "possible" that something "breaks for no reason". Lol. Now in the case of my headset recently breaking, that can be attributed to letting it hang from the controller to "unwind".

On a side note, the little trick I learned about the static buildup scored me a 360 media remote for a dollar! Was in a little game store where the owner just got in a remote that didn't work; offered a $1, took it home and "fixed" it. Just recently got rid of it.
