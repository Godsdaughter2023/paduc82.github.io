canadian tech say:

I would expect that this means you did not apply thermal paste correctly between the heat synch and the CPU.

My suspicion is that the thermal paste between the CPU and the heat synch was not applied correctly or has dried out.  CPUs produce a huge amount of heat.  That is why that heat synch is there with the fan on top.  I can tell you how to fix this.  It is not actually very difficult.  First, open the covers and check to see the fan on the case and the one over the CPU is working OK.

  1. Buy a small tube of thermal paste at the computer store.  Should  not be more than about $10
  2. Unplug the power cord.  Hold the on button in for at least 7 seconds. Remove the cover of the PC
  3. Using a long Phillips screw driver slowly loosen the 4 screws on the corners holding down the heat synch to the main board.  They are spring loaded.  Loosen the screws a few turns at a time on each corner and keep rotating till you remove them or at least you can tell they are loose. 
  4. Remove the small plug from the fan to the main board -- note how it fits
  5. Pick up the heat synch/fan assembly.  Examine the surfaces between the bottom of the synch and the top of the CPU. 
  6. Using rubbing alcohol very, very sparingly and a small soft cloth patiently remove the paste that is on both surfaces.  Make sure the surfaces are now clean and dry.
  7. Place small dots of the paste on the 4 corners (not at the edge) of the underside of the heat synch.
  8. Press the heat synch in place (do not attach yet), pull it back off.  You should see an even coating of paste on both surfaces.  NO paste over flow.  If you see blank spots, apply more paste there.
  9. Test again
  10. Re-attach the heat synch and plug.

Power up, boot up.

mouneshwar r say:

Hi custodian4,

 

Code 43 means ‘Windows has stopped this device because it has reported problems.

 

Windows 7 contains several troubleshooting programs that can automatically fix some common problems with your computer. One among them is hardware and devices troubleshooter.

 

Step 1: Open the Hardware and Devices troubleshooter

It checks for common issues and makes sure that any new device or hardware attached to your computer was installed correctly.

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows7/Open-the-Hardware-and-Devices-troubleshooter

 

Step 2: Re-install your chipset drivers

This error is also caused by miscommunication between the motherboard chipset and the graphics card. I would suggest you to please visit the chipset manufacturer's website to download and install the updated chipset drivers for Windows 7.

 

Alternatively, you can resolve this issue by updating the chipset drivers from the Device Manager.

a.    Click on Start; in the start search type ‘devmgmt.msc’ and hit Enter.

b.    In the Device Manager, under the "System Devices" section, find the graphics controller (typically AGP to CPU controller or PCI Express Root Port), right click and choose update driver.

 

Hope this information is helpful and let me know if you need any further assistance.
