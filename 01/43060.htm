How to defragment your disk drive after upgrading to Windows 10 - Microsoft Community

j w stuart say:

How to defragment your disk drive after upgrading to Windows 10

Technical Level : Basic

Summary

After upgrading to Windows 10 your Hard Disk Drive may be highly fragmented. You can improve your systems performance by using Windows 10 built-in defragmentation utility.

Note: Solid State Drives do not need to be defragmented.


Details

1) Click on the Start menu on the bottom-left side of the toolbar, then click on the search box and type: defrag.

2) In the list that pops up, click on 'Defragment and optimize your drives'.

3) A "Optimize Drives" window will be displayed with a list of partitions and associated drive letters on your disk drive (or drives if you have more than one drive),


4) Click on the C: (Windows partition) to highlight it, then click on the 'Analyze' button. When the analysis is finished you will see how fragmented the partition is and if it needs "Optimization".

5) If you decide to defragment the drive or partition you selected, click on the 'Optimize' button.

Note that this process can take from only a few minutes to several hours depending on the percent of fragmentation and the speed of your disk drive.

6) After optimization has completed you can change when the next time defragmentation is performed by changing the settings located in the "Scheduled optimization" box by clicking on the 'Change settings' button.

7) You also have the option not to optimize specific drives or partitions.

a. user say:

Hello,

 

Error 0x800CCC0E happens when there is a problem with Microsoft Outlook, Outlook Express or Windows Mail. Error 0x800CCC0E is usually caused by a conflict with Simple Mail Transfer Protocol (SMTP) servers while using your E-mail program. The exact error messages may look like:

 

  • SMTP, Port: 25, Secure(SSL): No, Socket Error: 10051, Error Number: 0x800CCC0E
  • SMTP, Port: 25, Secure(SSL): No, Socket Error: 10060, Error Number: 0x800CCC0E
  • SMTP, Port: 25, Secure(SSL): No, Socket Error: 10061, Error Number: 0x800CCC0

 

In order to fix error number 0x800CCC0E , you will need to make sure that your e-mail account settings are correct and that PORT 25 is the port used in the SMTP area - unless specifically told to use another port by your Internet Service Provider (ISP).

 

It's possible your ISP has started to block the standard Outgoing Mail Port (25). You may need to consult your ISP's support pages to get the SMTP e-mail settings.

You can also get error 0x800CCC0E messages when you have anti-virus or firewall software enabled on your pc. Turn the anti-virus or firewall off for a moment and try to send your email again. If it works then the security software is the problem and you will need to contact your ISP for help.
