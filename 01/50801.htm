ellerem say:

Hello,

I assume the lights on the controller come on when you press the Guide button and the batteries are fresh or charged well.

I will also assume you know how to sync the controller and console.  Some hints here : support.xbox.com/.../connecting-game-controllers

It is obvious the controller works fine as it has been used on two other consoles.  You are taking all the right steps.  You can try the steps below:  

1. Make sure the line of sight from the controller to console is clear - remove any obstacles

2. Ensure there is no interference - turn off other consoles, microwaves and remove any cordless phones (land line style)

3. Try to sync as per xbox instructions

The best method for forcing a sync for a controller is to use the USB charge cable.  These are quite cheap and can be used with a controller even if it has no batteries in it.   The idea is to make the wireless controller temporarily a wired controller.  It forces the console to recognise it.  

If the console cannot recognise it as a wired controller then there is an issue.  This may be fixed by clearing the system cache but its not a guarantee.

1. Remove profile - SETTINGS page (far right from home page), select STORAGE, then find your profile and remove.

2. While in you hard drive under GAMES & APPS look for any red or yellow icons with incomplete or unknown.  Delete them.

3.Clear System Cache - SETTINGS page, select STORAGE and highlight a drive but don't select it.  Press Y and select Clear System Cache.  Agree when asked.

4. Be Patient - turn your console off for a full minute.  This assists in resetting the console.

5. Turn on the console and download your profile - it is the icon next to 'create a profile'.  Try again

Good luck & happy gaming
