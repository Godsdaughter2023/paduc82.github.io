Audio Connections

If you are using VGA or DVI to connect your computer to a TV, you’ll have to find another way to get your audio going.  You basically have two choices: you can use external speakers that you hook up to your computer (or use the speakers built into your laptop), or you can use a separate audio cable to output the sound from your computer to the TV.

DVI to HDMI

You’ll probably never see a laptop or television with a DVI port on it.  However, it’s pretty common to find them on desktop computers.  DVI uses digital signals to send video output, so it can be easily adapted to HDMI while still maintaining great quality.  The biggest drawback to using DVI instead of HDMI is that it doesn’t carry audio.

scottcallori say: I spent some time chatting with Microsoft support about this issue today and here is how they resolved it:

Initially, both the wireless and type cover were not working. I also had the unknown device in device manager with hardware id MSHW0027.

Swipe from the right and choose Settings - Change PC Settings. Update and Recovery - Recovery. Under "Refresh your PC without affecting your files" choose Get Started.
NOTE: This will reset the Surface back to factory defaults except for your saved files. Any programs that have been installed will need to be re-installed.

Continue through the wizard to restore your PC. The Surface will reboot and the process took about ten minutes.

After starting back up, I still had the unknown device, but wireless and type cover were working. Download the Surface Pro 2 firmware/driver pack from here: http://www.microsoft.com/en-us/download/details.aspx?id=40856
Unzip it to a location on the hard drive.

Open Device Manager. Expand Firmware. Right-click on "Surface Pro System Aggregator Firmware" and choose Update Driver. Browse for the driver and point it to the unzipped folder. You should get a message that the driver was updated.

Now right-click on the Unknown device and choose Update Driver. Browse to the same driver folder. You should get a message that the driver was updated and it should change from Unknown Device to "Surface Cover Telemetry".

After, I performed Windows Updates (including the firmware update). After restart the wireless stopped working again. However, another restart fixed that issue.

My Surface is now up to date with firmware and Windows Updates and all devices are working.

Hope this helps others!

The reason why it would not go to sleep was that because of your device's power consumption. To fix this, we suggest that you run a command to figure out which drivers and applications that have been eating up its power:
