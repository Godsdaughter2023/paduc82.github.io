Hi Tom,

 

Thank you for posting the issue on the Microsoft Community. Thanks for giving me an opportunity to help you.

 

1)      What is the model of the printer?

2)      Are you on domain network?

 

I suggest you to follow the steps.

 

a)      First make sure that the printer on the XP machine is shared. You can do this by right-clicking on the printer and choosing Sharing.

b)      Click the Share this printer radio button and give your printer a share name. Make sure is less than 8 characters and does not contain any symbols.

c)       Make sure you can see the printer share from the network browsing area in Windows 7. You can do this by going to Control Panel and clicking on Network and Internet.

d)      Then click on View network computers and devices under Network and Sharing Center.

e)      At this point, you should see the name of your XP computer in the list of computers. Mine XP machine is called Tom.

f)       Double-click on the computer name and you should see your shared printer in the list. Here you can try to add the printer by right-clicking on it and choosing Connect.

g)      Click on Start and then click on Devices and Printers. At the top, click on the Add a printer link.

h)      Next choose Add a local printer. Yes, that sounds counter-intuitive, but this is what you have to do!

i)        Next, click Create a new port and choose Local port from the list of options.

j)        Click Next and in the Port name box, type in the path to the shared printer. It should be something like \\tom\HPXP, where Tom is the name of your XP machine and HPXP is the shared name of the printer.

k)      Now choose the printer driver from the list or download the latest driver for the printer and choose Have Disk. Note that if you printer is a little older; it’s a good idea to download the Windows 7 driver for the printer and click Have Disk.

    Under the General tab

    Choose Automatic
    Click Start

    Click Device Manager
    Expand Bluetooth
    Right click each entry then click Properties
    Select the driver tab, click Rollback if its not grayed out.

    Restart your computer then check if its working again.
