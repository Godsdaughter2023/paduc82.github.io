and look for an entry that's labeled 'Vertical Sync'. Set it to 'Force Off' and click 'Apply' or 'OK'
 
Roll Back Driver to Previous Version:
 
There is a possibility the newest driver for that particular card may hinder its OpenGL capabilities. I would suggest, if turning off VSYNC does not fix the issue, that you try and rollback to the original driver that was shipped with the machine and wait for a newer release from nVidia.
 
You can do this via these steps:
 
1. Click START
2. Right click 'Computer'
3. Select 'Properties'
4. Click 'Device Manager' at the top left
5. Expand the 'Display Adapter' category
6. Right click your nVidia display adapter and select 'Properties'
7. Click on the 'Driver' tab and click on the 'Roll Back Driver' button and follow the prompts.
 
If these steps do not resolve your issue, please let us know and we can see what other troubleshooting steps can be taken,
