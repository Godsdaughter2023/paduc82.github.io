Understanding and getting rid of device listing of "Generic volume - Microsoft Community

normjay2001 say:

Understanding and getting rid of device listing of "Generic volume shadow copy"

Under devices I have a listing of 40 Generic volume shadow copy - How do I get rid of them and what is this all about?
old giza say: These relate to the Windows "Restore" capability. If you have Shadow copies and you run into difficulties with Windows Ststem or Drivers or Program Installations you can Restore your system to a previeously known good state.

If you go to Control Panel then System, then System Protection you will see a System Restore button. This will show a list of saved restore points.
The storage volumn Shadow copies relate to these although there is not a one to one correspondence. You may have fewer restore points then SVSCs. 

On my computer I also have 40 listed. But 32 are semi grey, 8 are solid. Right clicking on the 8 shows "This device is working properly".
Right clicking on the 32 says "This device is not connected". I have 8 saved Shadow copies of my C Drive listed in system restore. These are the 8 solid ones. Your number of copies will vary depending on the space you have allocated for system restore. The default is 15% of your hard drive.

15% of my hard drive would be about 36 GB. I have restricted my resore space to 10GB so I have space for a maximum of 8 copies.
I think windows creates a maximum of 40 restore points and as new system restore points are created the old ones are deleted in sequence.

You do not want to get rid of the "solid" ones and I would leave the "semi greys" alone too. If you eliminate them one of two things will probably happen...you may not be able to create additional restore points (possibly bad news) or windows will recreate them as needed with additional restore points up to a maximum of 40 and you will be back where you started.

In any event deleting Hidden Devices can be as potentialy disasterous as deleting Hidden System Files. This is why Windows hides them by default. If you like to Hyper-tweek your system you are probably aware that sometimes you can get away with that sort of thing and "improve" things in the bargiain. However in this case I don't think any gain will come from it.

On the Device Manager menu there is a "View" tab. If you unselect the "Show Hidden Devices" the Shadow copies will go away.
Out of sight out of mind.
