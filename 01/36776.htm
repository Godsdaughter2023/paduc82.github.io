  • TCP 53
  • TCP 80
  • UDP 88
  • UDP 3074
  • TCP 3074

kmagnuss say:

Do you happen to know the ideal size for a photo or slide in Movie Maker? For example, I want to create a slide so that I can place the image on the left and text on the right - what size should the slide be to appear the best?

considerate_guy say:

Movie Maker will reduce the height and width of the photo, but keep its original aspect ratio (ratio to height and width).  In other words, it won't change the look of the photo by stretching/squashing the photo.

 

The ideal aspect ratio is one that matches the aspect ratio of your project.  For example, if you are working on a 4:3 project, you want your photos to also have a ratio of 4 units wide by 3 units high.  Similarly, if you are working on a 16:9 project, then the photo should be 16 units wide by 9 units high.

 

In your case, you are asking about having room off to the right for text.  Movie Maker doesn't have the ability to change the horizontal alignment of the photo.  It will be in the middle.  To do what you want, you would have to add blank space to the side of the photo to fill it out to the resolution of the project, but you'd have to do this in an image editing program, not in Movie Maker.

In this case, my guess is that "soon" means "at GamesCom." Also, don't be surprised if/when you see Crytek's name attached to the project. The company has expressed interest in continuing the franchise. If the rumors are true, it's a good time to be a Timesplitters fan!
