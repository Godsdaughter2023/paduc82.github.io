arminatorx say:

Thanks for the novel idea. Does the full code give any more clues?

Well, in the past there had been posts about older consoles failing the update, because they were already updated, but the storage drive got corrupted, and the system software therefore seemed "different" from the actual update. 

So the console tried to look for newer software. 

The first set of numbers is the current version of the console software, and the second set is the version the console is trying to download. If the console had a corrupted system on it's storage, you would see the most up to date version code in the beginning (4476), and all zeroes as the second code (0000) followed by the other numbers. 

Unfortunately for my limited knowledge, your console has an intact system software, and is really trying to download a newer system software. 

However as for the C000-0099 code, I also only have what the Xbox support website offers, meaning that the update sees the console as being manipulated or that there is an issue with your Live account. 

If it's about the Live account, you could try the following experiment (be aware, that if that doesn't work, there is a chance you won't have access to your Xbox 360 savegames unless you find a way to restore your profile to the console).

First of all, prepare an USB drive as storage, and move your profile off the console's drive to your USB drive. Turn the console off, and wait for about 30 seconds, as the console still writes to the storage drives after it's turned "off", and if you remove the USB drive too early, it'll become corrupted and all data will be lost. 

With your profile safely stored on the USB drive, the console shouldn't see your profile anymore. Do your previous troubleshooting steps again (clearing the cache and removing the updates with the button combination). Disconnect the console from the network and try to update the console offline with another USB drive (not the one with your profile). 

Cross your fingers that the console can now be updated. 

If you still get the error message, I'm afraid something inside the console is broken, so the update mistakenly sees the console as being modified, or you bought the console used, and the previous owner modified it, or you gave it away for repairs at a non-Microsoft repair center, and they "repaired" it wrong, and now the console is considered modified. 

In all these cases there is nothing you can do than to get a different Xbox 360, transfer your data over to that other (working) console. 

As for the theory of Microsoft killing off older consoles: I still have an older "fat" Xbox 360 console, which works perfectly fine. So I don't think that Microsoft is deliberately killing off old consoles. They'd shoot themselves in the foot if they did, as people wouldn't be able to upload their old savegames to the cloud for the Xbox One to use it through it's backwards compatibility, should they want to upgrade. 

And if people can't continue their games on the new Xbox One anyways, there'd be no reason to stay within the Xbox family, and they could just as well get a Switch, PS4 or PC as their next platform. Even more so, if it became known that Microsoft is actively "breaking" other people's stuff for whatever reason.

Reinstall latest iTunes then please try:

1. Open iTunes.

2. Click Edit then Preferences then Advanced.

3. Click on the Change button next to the box for iTunes Media folder location box.

4. Navigate to the folder on the external drive where the media files are.

5. Click on Select folder.

6. On the two tick boxes below select what you want (I usually have mine both ticked).

7. Click OK.

If the 454.98 GB free Logical space is where your data partition was, I have no idea why it would be deleted during the Version Update to Creators Version. 

But since apparently it's only been deleted and not formatted over, you can try undeleting it following one of the methods in this tutorial which also applies to Windows 10:  Partition - Recover Deleted Partitions in Windows.

This is because until a deleted partition is again formatted, it still has all of its data intact but only the Partition Table hidden.  The tools in the link will recover the Partition Table and hence the data should reappear - unless there's something else that we don't know that's been done. 

In case the tools offered in Shawn's tutorial are all now the paid Pro versions, the last version where this feature was free in Partition Wizard appears to still have the download intact at this link:   partition wizard 4.2 free.zip - Windows Live.
