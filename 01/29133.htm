Hi Lawrence,

To change which apps run at startup, go to Task Manager, and then select the Startup tab. Choose an app, then select Enable or Disable.

To add or remove an app from the Startup tab, press the Windows key+ R, type shell:startup, and then select OK. This opens the Startup folder. Select Start button. Find the app you're looking to add or remove, and press and hold (or right-click) it. Select More > Open File Location. In the file location folder, press and hold (or right-click) the app and select Copy. Paste the shortcut to your app into the Startup folder to have it run at startup.

minimooo say:

Hi there - it may be that you haven't transferred your licenses for this downloaded content. Basically, when you download something you are downloading two bits: the content itself, and the license that gives you the right to play it. As you've got a new Xbox, it's best to think of it like this: you've moved the content, but the license cannot simply be moved with it, as it's essentially tied to your original console, as well as your gamertag. What you'll need to do is a license transfer, so your new Xbox recognises that you are the rightful owner of the content on your hard drive. What you'll need to do is this: Settings --> Account ---> Your Billing Options ----> License Transfer. Then just follow the instructions, and you should have access to your DLC in no time!
