cmdrkeene say:

Yes you'd want to set it in advance, but I'm sorry my memory failed me.  The option is under Storage (you can see I have several drives as well:  internal, SD, and a large external USB drive).

This applies to apps from the app store, but traditional programs that provide their own installer/setup routines, well that's going to be a mix.  Some programs during install ask "where to install", and some just don't (as I'm sure you're aware since it's been that way for 30 years). 

cmdrkeene say:

They will run just as-in.  Even though the app's files and data are on the external drive, a reference to them in the registry on the C: drive will be there (plus a shortcut on your Start menu for example).  So "technically" they will still be using some space on the C: drive (nearly infinitely small, just a couple letters worth of text).  Of course if you unplugged the external drive and tried to run the app it would say it can't launch it.

Now that other question about installing Windows itself on a portable drive, the How-To-Geek has a great guide on that.  Normally this is an enterprise feature (it's designed mostly for big businesses, they can give an employee a portable drive that they can plugin to any computer and boot it from the external drive), but he has a description of how to do it with any version.  It's similar to those "live Linux" CDs that work nearly the same way (except a bootable CD can't store new data so when you shutdown everything you did in that session is gone).

Hi,

The Microsoft account email address will automatically create an Xbox Live gamertag once you use it to sign in on Xbox.com or on the Xbox App. If you want to remove your Xbox Live account, you can sign out of the Xbox App and change the email address attached to the gamertag by following the steps on this link.

Note If you choose to remove an alias that uses a Microsoft domain such as @hotmail.com, @outlook.com, or @live.com, that alias will be completely deleted and will not be available to sign in to any Microsoft services.

To remove the email address you no longer use to sign in to your account, select Remove next to that alias on the Manage your account aliases page, and then follow the instructions provided.

Important Do not remove this email address if you still want to use it to send and receive email messages.
