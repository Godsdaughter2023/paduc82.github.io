It's not a mistake. You have just zoomed into your storyboard. In the zoomed in mode, each rectangle gets bigger. Then it gets filled with repeats of the original thumbnail, but slightly grayed. It's still just one picture or video.

If this view bothers you, go to the lower right to the Zoom slider and drag it all the way to the left. That will return you to a fully zoomed out mode where a single small rectangle is used for each item regardless of length.

However, while you are there, try dragging it left and right just so you get an idea of what it does. It can be helpful, especially when you have a video clip or two to use the zoomed in mode to get a better idea of where the playback marker is and the relative lengths of the clips. But when you are done playing with the slider, you'll probably want to put it back to the far left.

The Windows 10 Home license is OEM, so, it cannot be transferred. Its the same case if the Windows 10 Pro license came preinstalled on the other computer or was upgraded from an OEM Windows 7 or Windows 8 license.

If you want Windows 10 Pro on the new laptop, you have one of two options:

andre for directly say:
Yup, just as I suspected.

Whoever installed it, might have installed a volume license (organization key) on your computer.

Windows 10 Pro/Enterprise volume license clients are used by large organizations that deploy Windows 10 in bulk to hundreds or thousands of computers. Sometimes, users will copy these organization keys off the Internet and use them to install Windows 10 Pro/Enterprise, but they are blocked from activating because it can't reach appropriate KMS host (Server computer) which handles the activation request.

So, the best thing for you to do is install Windows 10 Home license you bought or if your intention is to install Windows 10 Pro, return the Home license and purchase the right edition.

Option 2, install Home, then purchase a $99 upgrade to Pro through the Store.

Open Start > Settings > Update & security > Activation
Click the link ‘Go to the store to upgrade or enter a product key’
Click the $99 button to make your purchase (the price might vary by region or depending on the edition you are upgrading from or upgrading to).
Follow the on screen instructions to make your purchase.
Within 5 to 10 minutes, your system will be upgraded to Windows 10 Pro, your personal files, apps and settings preserved.
You won’t get a product key, you get a digital license, which is attached to your Microsoft Account used to make the purchase.
