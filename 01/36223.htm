annix say:


The entire reason that replacing your internal hard drive is a bad idea is situations exactly like this - just because a component has a PC connector does not mean it can or should be modified or swapped out like a PC. There are custom software and hardware considerations built in to the Xbox operating system, and if swapping your internal drive worked reliably with this, it would be officially supported. 


To anyone considering replacing their internal HDD with an SSD, don’t do it. You will run a massively increased risk of bricking your console and you won’t get the performance boost you’re looking for.


On my Xbox One X I have a 4TB external HDD and an external 512GB SSD. Any games that I’m currently playing I just move on to the SSD, and I see massive improvements in load times and general performance. For example, Witcher 3 would take somewhere around a minute and twenty seconds to load in from the menu with the game on my HDD, vs 18-22 seconds on my SSD. 


Additionally, even if there wasn’t a massive risk of bricking your console, and voiding your warranty by replacing the internal drive, the internal drive uses a SATA interface, the data throughput of which is not going to take any better advantage of your SSD than the USB 3.0 ports on your Xbox are. You literally won’t get better load times on an internal SSD on an Xbox One vs having it external, so why risk it? If you need repairs done, contact Xbox Support, they do a fantastic job and are great to deal with. Save yourself the headache of going through situations like this.
