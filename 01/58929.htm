great white north say:

Hi kn1000a:

Go to Control Panel | Hardware and Sound and see if there is a section for a NVIDIA Control Panel.  If this software is installed you should be able to confirm your GeForce 8 Series graphics card is a GeForce 8800 GTX (e.g., at Help | System Information) and check for an update for your graphics drivers (e.g., at Help | Update).

I'm not an expert at reading hardware diagnostics but it looks like you're using a generic Windows VGA driver (6.0.6001.18000 is the build number for Vista SP1) for your GeForce 8 Series graphics card that dates back to 2006.  If you can confirm that you have a model GeForce 8800 GTX, the official GeForce site at https://www.geforce.com/Drivers indicates that there is a v342.01 graphics driver (released 14-Dec-2016) that is compatible with 32-bit Vista SP2.

I've published a recent Speccy snapshot of my own 32-bit Vista machine at http://speccy.piriform.com/results/zghFKJFHYgAnFECgKXl2BfV for comparison and the Graphics section shows that I currently have driver version 9.18.13.783 (a.k.a. v137.83) for my NVIDIA GeForce 8400M GS graphics card.  It might be helpful if you could publish your entire Speccy profile - go to File | Publish Snapshot and then copy and paste the URL this generates (e.g., http: // speccy.piriform.com/results/xxx... ) in your next post as instructed in the support article Publishing a Speccy Profile to the Web so we can see all your system specs.

What is the model of your Dell desktop computer?  When your computer begins losing its time/date settings that often means that the small CMOS battery on your motherboard needs replacing, which ties in with your low system battery voltage warning. Dell might have instructions for your computer model on their support site, but for generic instructions see the Computer Hope support article How to Replace the CMOS Battery.  If you want to replace the CMOS battery yourself note the warnings in that article about electrostatic discharge (ESD) that can damage components on your motherboard if you aren't properly grounded.  I use an inexpensive ESD wrist strap when repairing computers but there are other simple suggestion in the article ElectroStatic Discharge or ESD on how to prevent ESD.
