syriel say:

To use a US 360 in the UK you will need to be aware of the following:

 

  1. BUY A NEW UK COMPATIBLE POWER BRICK FROM XBOX CUSTOMER SERVICE! - Sorry for the caps, but this is the most important thing. The US power brick will not work in the UK and will be damaged if you plug it in.
  2. If you are connecting via HD cables (component or HDMI) you shouldn't have an issue. 720p and above are standard both in the US and the UK. Lower video resolutions do differ, but most newer UK TVs are multi-standard and should work.
  3. DVD Movie playback on the US Xbox will be Region 1, so you will not be able to play UK DVDs. Any US Region 1 DVDs (or region free DVDs) will continue to work fine.
  4. Game discs are a little trickier. Microsoft leaves region coding up to individual publishers and DOES NOT advertise region status on the box. As a general rule, most first party games are region free as are many third party games where one publisher distributes the game in every territory. A region free "PAL" labeled game will play just fine in your "NTSC" US Xbox 360. A region locked game will fail to boot. You'll probably want to rent (or check compatibility lists online) before buying.
  5. Your US based Xbox Live account/Gamertag will continue to work just fine (and you will be able to connect to the US version of Xbox Live from within the UK). If you wish to keep using it, you can, but you will need to purchase Gold time and MSP from US sellers, such as Amazon.com. Older Gold codes are generally region free, but there have been reports of newer Gold codes being region locked. MSP codes are ALWAYS region locked. 
  6. Digital items purchased on your US account and downloaded to your Xbox 360 will continue to work. Digital items purchased on your US account while you are in the UK will probably download just fine (as the two territories have a lot of licensing crossover), but there is always the possibility that a specific download will be blocked in the UK if the US publisher of it does not have distribution rights there. Really, this is the same thing that US and UK players with Japanese accounts (for purchasing items released only in Japan) have to deal with today.
  7. You have the option of creating a new UK account (and thus having two Gamertags) or converting your US account to a UK account. If you convert, current points, time and items will transfer over, though there is a disclaimer on the Microsoft site that some digital items will not make the transfer due to licening restrictions.
  8. So long as a UK game boots up in your US Xbox 360, you can use your US Gamertag to log in, play online and earn achievements. Achievements are based on game ID and are not impacted by region.
  9. No matter what you do with your account, you will not have access to region restricted US apps. On the flipside, you WILL have access to region restricted UK apps, even if you keep your US account.

 

I think that covers most of it. Hopefully that gives you an idea of what to expect.

a. user say:

Is there a video editing program that I could use.

considerate_guy say:

Same answer.  Those other video editors don't have support for importing a AVS4YOU project file.  The only thing you could export is the final movie from there and then treat it as a video file in another video editing app.  But the constituent parts would not be accessible, just the combined video and audio.


If you want to be able to work on a project and continue working on another PC, your best bet is to have exactly the same software on both computers.  You would also need to have identical folder layouts in order to transfer the source files (videos, photos, audio) and make them available in the same locations on the other PC.

considerate_guy say:

I did add more detail to your prior thread if you would like to further diagnose your issue with Movie Maker.


As for your current question about AVS4YOU, my guess would be that the VPS is a project file that is unique to that program.  In other words, the other person that would continue editing your project would have to have the same editing program, the project file and all the source files in the same locations on their PC.


All video editing programs are probably very similar in this respect.  For example, in Movie Maker, when you are adding items to your project, adding transitions, etc. they are getting recorded in your project file.  In the case of Movie Maker, this is a .wlmp file.  A project file doesn't contain any actual video, photos or music, just the current editing state of your project.  It will contain a list of all your source files (the folder and file name of the relevant media file) along with details on the edits made, transitions/effects/titles/captions added, etc.  But it is not a file that can be understood by other video editing programs.


In order to share the results of your edits, you would have to save the resulting movie into a video file.  There are numerous formats to choose from.  For example, the latest version of Movie Maker can save in MP4 or WMV format.  That can be used in other programs.  AVS4YOU lists a number of formats too.


However, that would result in a single video file with all your edits incorporated.  It wouldn't have the split points, individual videos/photos, transitions, etc.  So basically, once you start editing in one video editor, you can't really transfer that session to another video editor except as a completed movie.


There are some exceptions, for example Adobe makes it so their other products understand Photoshop and Illustrator files, or that part of a project in Premiere can be linked to an After Effects project.  Another example is that a Movie Maker project can be imported into Nero Video 2014 for further processing. But it has to be designed for and isn't common when going to another video editing program.
