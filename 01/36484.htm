j w stuart say:

Mount a Backup and Restore 'System Image' backup VHD file

1) Click 'Start' then right click on 'Computer' and click 'Manage'.

2) Click on 'Disk Management'.

3) In the Disk Management menu click the 'Action' menu item and select 'Attach VHD'.

4) Use the 'Browse' option to locate the 'WindowsImageBackup' folder on the external hard drive.

5) Double click on the 'WindowsImageBackup' folder, double click on the folder which is now displayed (bears the same name as your computer name).

6) Of the 3 folders displayed choose the one that is named 'Backup YYYY-MM-DD XXXXXX and double click.

7) You will now see two .vhd files, one is only about 40KB in size, the other and larger (XXGB) .vhd file is the one you need to select (single click) and then click the 'Open' button in the lower right.

8) In the 'Attach Virtual Hard Disk' window click 'OK'. The VHD file is now mounted and assigned a drive letter and a Volume Name of 'Local Disk'.

9) Open Windows Explorer and click on the newly created drive letter (local Disk).

10) Now open the 'Users' folder, double click on your 'User Name' folder and then the My Documents folder. Pick a document and use Copy and past to a folder on your hard drive to recover the document.

When your done the safest thing to do to unmount the .vhd is to reboot.

Around a week ago my games began to minimize to desktop at random times. I have noticed that when I am not in game that something pops up on my screen for less than a second and disappears again . Ive scanned for viruses and corrupted files and nothing has been found.

***Post moved by the moderator to the appropriate forum category.***

I had the same issue while playing games. As someone who streams their games and have it minimize during a game was getting out of hand. However, after some searching around figured out that the little window that pops up for a split second was not a virus or malware running, but has to do with Microsoft Office.

The window that pops up is called "officebackgroundtaskhandler.exe"

The one that runs every hour is called "OfficeBackgroundTaskHandlerRegistration"

You could disable it by following these steps to see if it helps you. Just make sure to check that Microsoft Office is running smoothly afterwards.


Windows > (type in) Task Scheduler

Task Scheduler > Task Scheduler Library > Microsoft > Office
Find "OfficeBackgroundTaskHandlerRegistration"
Right-click > Disable

If you do not want to disable, but instead want to keep the window from popping up:

Windows > (type in) Task Scheduler

Task Scheduler > Task Scheduler Library > Microsoft > Office
Find "OfficeBackgroundTaskHandlerRegistration"

Right-click > Properties > "General" > Click "Change User or Group"

Type "system" > Click "OK"

Let me know if this solved your issue at all.
