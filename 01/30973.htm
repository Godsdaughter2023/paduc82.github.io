The PC has three USB 3.0 and two USB 2.0 ports at the back, with two USB 3.0 at the front. It all worked without any problems since it was new about a year ago. The PC antivirus and firewall is Windows Defender included with the OS. It has never run any other antivirus or firewall.

Yesterday I tried to recharge my tablet from the monitor and found the monitor's USB ports were all dead. The Hardware and Devices troubleshooter is broken. I have several times carried out the following actions in various combinations:

1. Restarted the PC, or powered off completely and restarted.

2. Unplugged some or all USB devices, including the USB cable to the monitor USB hub, and plugged them in again, sometimes before and sometimes after restarting.

3. Opened Device Manager. Everything looks right. I have checked all drivers. They are up to date. From Device Manager I have uninstalled all USB hubs and USB controllers and reinstalled. I have confirmed all drivers for USB and storage controllers and devices are up to date.

4. Run the Hardware and Devices troubleshooter. It always fails:

"An error has occurred while troubleshooting". The details reference path C:\WINDOWS\Diagnostics\Index\DeviceDiagnostic.xml and error code 0x8000FFFF.  

5. Run services.msc to confirm services beginning "Diagnostic ..." are running. The first time I did this one of them was not running and the option to start was greyed out so I could do nothing. I checked later and Policy Service, Service Host and System Host were all running but still the troubleshooter error occurred. I checked again and Policy Service, Service Host and System Host were all running but not Execution Service. I started it manually. It made no difference. I did all that again and restarted. It made no difference.

6. Restarted the PC with no USB devices at all, installed the bluetooth USB dongle (essential so I could use my Bluetooth keyboard and mouse), and ran the troubleshooter. It made no difference.

7. Plugged in and unplugged other USB devices in different ports. Nothing works in the monitor USB ports, they are completely dead. The PC ports appear to be working normally, except I cannot understand why my tablet does not charge when plugged in to any of the PC ports. Data and media transfer is available through USB in the PC, but not charging. (I had to recharge the tablet from a mains power charger plugged in elsewhere.)

8. Opened an elevated Command Prompt and ran 'SFC / scannow' as well as 'DISM'. No problems were found, both commands completed smoothly.

9. Used a restore point to roll back the PC to its state before the last Critical Update. The restore operation failed several times until I used [shift & restart] to open the option to restore during the restart before Windows loads. This worked smoothly. It made no difference to the USB ports and the troubleshooter still fails to run. The most recent Windows Critical Update then installed again. It made no difference. Windows is fully up to date again. I again ran 'SFC / scannow' as well as 'DISM'. No problems were found, both commands completed smoothly. It made no difference.

10. Restarted the PC in Safe Mode. It made no difference to the USB ports and the troubleshooter would still not run.

I found one web site that among other advice suggested some registry changes to fix the troubleshooter. This was no good because the registry key it suggested modifying does not exist in my PC registry.

There is one other thing I should mention. For most of its life the PC has operated with two external HDDs attached and a third which is only connected occasionally. Recently I decided to rationalise my data storage. I attached all the HDDs as well as a new HDD of 6TB that includes a USB hub with two USB 3.0 ports. I transferred a large amount of data between the HDDs. I then removed the older three HDDs as archives and left just the new HDD attached.

The monitor USB ports are dead, and the troubleshooter fails, regardless of whether the new HDD is attached or not. So perhaps swapping external HDDs has nothing to do with it. But perhaps it does. So after taking all the above actions I powered off the PC and reinstalled all the other HDDs. I restarted the PC. The HDDs all look fine in Explorer and Device Manager shows everything as it should be. It made no difference. I repeated all the previous actions except 9 and 10. It made no difference. I removed the HDDs one at a time, using the safe removal utility to ensure no data loss, and tried the troubleshooter after each one was removed. It made no difference. I restarted with no external HDDs. It made no difference. I reattached the new HDD. It made no difference.

So, my question - How do I find the fault and fix it?

andre da costa say:
Power on and off your computer three times
On the third time, your computer will boot into the Advanced Recovery environment
Click Advanced Options
Click Troubleshoot
Click Advanced Options

Click Startup Settings
Click Restart
Press (4) to Enable Safe Mode
Wait while Windows 10 starts in safe mode.
Log in

Press Windows key + R
Type: appwiz.cpl
Hit Enter
Click View installed updates

If the following KBs are are installed, right click KB4093112 then click uninstall

restart then see if USB functionality is restored.
