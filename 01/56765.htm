You may or may not need to change the BIOS settings.  Assuming that you have a bootable CD with this tool, put it in your CD drive and reboot the computer.  One of 3 things will happen:

1.  The computer will boot from the CD and you can follow whatever instructions you have from Symantec.

2.  Watch very closely as the computer is booting.  You may see a message that says something like "Press any key to boot from CD".  If you see such a message, press any key (e.g., the spacebar) and the computer will boot from the CD.

3.  Watch very closely as the computer is booting.  You will see a message that says something like, "To change settings, press F1."  The key that you have to press will be different depending on your particular computer.  It might be F1 or F10 or the DEL key.  Whatever it is, press that key and you will enter the BIOS configuration utility.  From there you can change the boot order to have the CD first.  Directions for navigating in the BIOS configuration will appear on the screen, but they will involve using the Tab key, the Enter key, and the arrow keys, because your mouse will not work in this utility.
