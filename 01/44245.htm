lemp say:

This issue ought to be taken to your network administrator or IT support staff.

Job separator pages can be initiated from more than one location.

The first place to look is in the printer driver settings, which you see by right-clicking on the printer's icon in Devices and Printers, selecting "Printer properties," and then (usually) the "Advanced" tab (you should look on the "Device Settings" tab as well).  I assume that this is what you checked when you say that "Page Separator" is disabled.

Separator pages can also be turned in within applications.  Given that it's happening across several applications, that's not likely to be the case.  Also, these generally are not blank pages but contain information about the print job and/or file being printed.

Presumably your computers are on a network.  In this case, the extra page could be configured in the network print server.

If I had to guess, I'd suspect that a new printer driver was pushed out to the individual computers or, if the printers are shared using a print server, either a new printer driver was installed on the server or some configuration change was made.
