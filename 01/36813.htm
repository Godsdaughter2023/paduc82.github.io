Windows 10 Home/Pro used on standard desktops is coded to run on Intel x86 processors and most Android tablets use the ARM processor which has a completely different instruction set making them incompatible.

Here are some of the main causes for “Your PC ran into a problem and needs to restart” error:

• Conflicting drivers - It’s possible that certain drivers may not be compatible with one another. The first thing you have to do is to open the Device Manager utility, located in the Control Panel of your Windows operating system. Select the System tab from the top of the list. Click the Properties button. Windows will try to show you all of your devices, along with the resources that those devices are using. Now you will be able to see in the list whether two of your devices are using the same resource by any chance. Now, all you have to do is change the settings on one or more of the devices that are presently trying to access the same resource. Consult the manual of the corresponding device in case of any doubts.

• Faulty hardware - It may be a result of certain hardware not being installed properly, or perhaps if this is an external component that is being plugged in. To resolve it, you may try to update the OS and some software of your PC.

• Overheating - Systematic overheating of your computer or any one of its components is bad for it. When this occurs often, your computer will crash sort of as a self-defense mechanism, to prevent reaching the temperature point of no return. We recommend downloading a temperature monitoring program. This will help you monitor the temperature of your PC, while you’re working.

• Registry error - Registry errors may occur due to corrupted device drivers or corruption in the registry itself due to some recent software changes (installing or uninstalling a given program). A number of other instances of corruption could be the root of the issue, as well, like RAM corruption, system files corruption as the result of virus infections. A damaged hard disk can also be the cause of a registry error.

If you are able to access your files and the computer has yet to crash into an unusable state, back up all your personal files and work files as well as any applications and purchases you made on your laptop. The easiest way to clean up your system is to use System Restore to set it to a less consumed disk configuration when you had fewer files, as well as to use System Restore to manage restore points and remove a few of them. A restore point is about 4 GB and using defrag to compact that and the process defrag runs to move all the files around is not integrated into the System Restore program. There is a serious risk if the system restore table has an error with a critical file location like kernel**.dll or something in the driver cache like your video driver.
