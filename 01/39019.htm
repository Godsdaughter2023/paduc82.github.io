jonathanburgess1 say: The update happened, the usual screens changed, the files disapeared. The computer has now put me on a temporary user.How can I change to how it was before?

a. user say:

Windows signs users in to a temporary account when their regular user account becomes unusable, which can happen for any number of reasons. The purpose of the temporary account is to give you the opportunity to create a new user account, and then to move your personal files from the old user account to the new user account. The whole procedure takes a few minutes plus whatever length of time is needed to move your files.

In earlier versions of Windows, if your user account became unusable you needed to perform advanced surgery to recover your personal files. The temporary account is a much better solution. Here's what you do:

1- Sign in to an account with Administrator permissions and create a new Standard user account - that's your new Windows user account. It can be a Microsoft account or a local account, your choice.

2- While you're still signed in to the Administrator account, use File Explorer to copy (not 'move') your personal files (documents, pictures, etc.) from your old user account to the Public user account (to which all users have access.) Windows will ask you if you want to 'take ownership' of the folders - just say 'yes.' That allows the Administrator account to access your old personal folders.

3- Sign out of the Administrator account and sign in to your new user account. Use File Explorer to navigate to the Public user and move (yes) your files to the corresponding folders in your new user account, e.g., your documents go to the Documents folder, etc.

4- Take your new user account on a test drive to make sure it works properly. You can take this opportunity to setup your new user account the way you like, e.g., desktop background, screen saver, etc.

5- Once you're sure that you've transferred everything you want from the old user account to the new - don't forget anything - you can delete the old user account.

Going forward, you really should make a daily backup of your personal files. It's easy to do with third-party backup software.
