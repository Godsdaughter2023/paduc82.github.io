lemp say:

Your computer is not communicating with your router.

You never did clarify what it was that you "got back from IT guy."  Was it your router or your computer?  What was supposed to have been done?

The cause of your problem is likely to be either a bad Ethernet cable or a hardware problem with your router.

Your router has 4 Ethernet ports.  The 3 ports closest to the power connection are designated LAN1, LAN2, and LAN3.  The fourth port is designated LAN4/WAN.  See the diagram:

1.  Is there a cable connected to LAN4/WAN?  If so, what is the other end of that cable connected to?

2.  The cable connected to your computer should be connected to LAN1, LAN2, or LAN3.  The following steps should be done with the computer booted up and working normally.

3.  If there normally is a cable connected to LAN4/WAN, temporarily disconnect it.  With the cable connected to your computer connected to LAN1, is the light designated by number 4 in the diagram below on, blinking, or off?

4.  If light number 4 is off, connect the cable from your computer to port LAN2.  Is the light on, blinking, or off?

5.  If light number 4 is off, connect the cable from your computer to port LAN3.  Is the light on, blinking, or off?

6.  If light number 4 is off whether the cable from your computer is connected to LAN1, LAN2, or LAN3, you probably have a bad Ethernet cable.  Try steps 3, 4, and 5 with a different cable. 

If you don't have a different Ethernet cable, or a second cable also does not result in light number 4 turning on, disconnect the power from the router, wait 30 seconds, then power it back on and try steps 3, 4, and 5 again.

If still no joy, you can see if resetting the router to its factory settings fixes things.  This may or may not require you to reconfigure the router in order to connect to the Internet.  To reset the router, with the router powered on, use a ball point pen, an unfolded paper clip, or similar device to press the small button to the left of the power input (#10 in the diagram).  Keep the button pressed for about 3 seconds until light #2 starts blinking.  Release the button and the router will reset and restart automatically.

If still no joy at this point, take the router back to your "IT guy."

Microsoft released the first beta back in June 2010 followed by a Release Candidate late October 2010 and the final RTM availability (February 16 2011 MSDN/TechNet subscribers) and public Availability (February 22 2011). Unlike previous Service Packs, Windows 7 SP1 does not contain any major improvements; it’s basically a rollup of updates that have been released for the operating system since it went to manufacturing July 22 2009. mr_awesome say:

Not all games are free to use on any system. Need for Speed: Hot Pursuit is region locked and can only be played within the coding that it was orginally bought in. For example, a game such as Gears of War can be played on an Xbox 360 from any region because the producer of the game made it without region-coding. Need for Speed Hot Pursuit however, is produced where it can only be played on an Xbox 360 where the coding matches the disc. Your Xbox 360 and your copy of Need for Speed: Hot Pursuit are coded differently, hence the error you're receiving. The game that didn't give you the issue was free of region coding and is playable on any console, regardless of the country it was produced in.
