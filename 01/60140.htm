Hi Mudpukket,

 

Since you confirmed that the problem is happening after recent Windows update, performing System restore to the date prior to Windows Update would be a good idea. Step 1: Perform system restore

 

For help with System restore:

 

System Restore: frequently asked questions

What is System Restore?

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows7/What-is-System-Restore

 

Step 2: Turn off Automatic Updates

 

1.     Go to start type Windows Update and press Enter.

2.     In the left pane, click Change settings.

3.     Select “Check for updates but let me choose whether to download and install them”.

4.     Click OK.

 

 

Step 3: Check for Updates.

 

1.    Go to Control Panel\System and Security\Windows Update

2.     Click on Check for Updates.

3.     From the list of updates, install one update at a time. Note down the KB article number so that you know which update you’re performing.

4.     Continue installing the recommended updates till the problem recurs.

5.     Post back with the KB article number so that further action can be taken.

mister ohnaka say:

Hi Weevil! I am not exactly sure what you are asking, but that error code you provided is in regards to region settings.

Below I have listed two possible solutions in regards to that error code.

Solution 1: Make sure that your computer’s regional settings match your Zune account’s regional settings

First, check your Zune account settings:

  1. Go to Billing Contact Information on Xbox.com to review your Zune account information. Sign in using your Microsoft account (formerly called Windows Live ID).
  2. Check the location that’s listed under Country/Region.

Next, check the regional settings on your computer:

  1. Click Start. In the Start search box, type Region and Language.

    On Windows 8: From the Start screen, start typing region and make sure that Settings is selected in the search bar at right. When region appears on the screen, tap or click the icon. Your region appears in the window.

  2. Click Region and Language, and you’ll see your region listed at the top of the window.

If the region and language listed for your Zune account doesn't match the one listed for your computer, correct it. Then, try to use your Zune software again.

Note Your current location will vary.

Solution 2: Create a new Zune account and Microsoft account (formerly called Windows Live ID)

If the location settings on your computer are correct, but the settings on your Zune account are incorrect, you will have to create a new Zune and Microsoft account. This means you’ll lose access to content you downloaded or purchased from your previous or existing account.

Note Changing the region of your existing Microsoft account won't fix the problem.

  1. To determine the location settings on your computer and Zune account, see Solution 1.
  2. To create a new Microsoft account, go to Microsoft account sign up and fill in all the required information to create a new account. Make sure that you set Country/Regionto your correct location.

    Note If this link takes you to an existing account, sign out of your page, and then click the link again to create a new account.

  3. To create a new Zune account, open the Zune software, and then sign in to your current account.
  4. Right-click your Zune tag, click Switch user, and then click Create a new account.
  5. Follow the steps to create your new Zune account. 



Let me know if you have any more questions. Cheers!
