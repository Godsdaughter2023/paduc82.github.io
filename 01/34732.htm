The password eye (aka Password Reveal Button) is a new feature in Internet Explorer 10 (IE10). If you click on the password eye, what you have typed will show up to enable you to check the typing of your password. The password will only show up while the mouse button is pressed - as soon as you release the button your typing will disappear.

 

Some security considerations when using the password eye:

  • Be wary of anyone looking over your shoulder.
  • Avoid using it if your computer is being accessed remotely (by for example a tech support person) - even if it’s a trustworthy company. In fact, I would avoid typing any password into a IE10 box that has a password eye during a remote access session.
  • Do not leave your computer unattended with the password box filled in (but not submitted).

in the sticks say:

I do not think this problem is associated with your RAM insertion/removal. Did you remove the CMOS battery too? Or did you change BIOS settings?

Stop 7b is normally associated with the disk access mode being incorrect. XP has no native support for AHCI/RAID operation.

Go into BIOS setup and switch the SATA operation to ATA/IDE (as opposed to RAID/AHCI). The Dell manual to do this is here

ftp://ftp.dell.com/Manuals/all-products/esuprt_desktop/esuprt_dimension_desktops/dimension-5150x_Owner%27s%20Manual_en-us.pdf

go to the Appendix-System Setup-Entering System Setup to see instructions.

If it is already set to IDE/ATA switch to ACHI/RAID.

joey chewy say: It's set to RAID ON and will setting it back to ACHI AUTO affect the drive performance? I never removed the CMOS battery.

in the sticks say:

Set it to AHCI/Auto. Your PC should really only be set to RAID if you have more than one disk drive installed and you want to combine them as one drive (as opposed to utilise them as separate drives), which is not the case for the vast majority of PCs.

At the moment, only you know whether or not you have two or more drives.

Performance of AHCI is better than IDE/ATA, but that is academic if your Windows installation does not have AHCI drivers installed - it will not boot in AHCI mode (or RAID mode) if those drivers are not installed.

joey chewy say: My only other option from RAID On, which is where it's at now, is to switch to RAID Autodetect/ATA. Is this what I want?

in the sticks say:

Two posts ago you referred to it as "setting it back to ACHI AUTO", now you refer to it as "RAID Autodetect/ATA". Regardless, if you only have two options and the current one is not working, you have nothing to lose by switching it away from RAID ON to the other option and then see what happens. Whenever I have never seen stop 7b, it was caused by this setting being wrong (but that doesn't mean you will not be the first, in my experience, to find another cause).

If it doesn't work out then you can always set it back to RAID ON. Though, as I said before, RAID is really only an option that is needed if you have two or more disk drives (or intend to use RAID by adding a second disk drive). At the risk of complicating things, maybe you should read this

http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/RAID

and decide if it applies to your PC. I really doubt that it does but, at least for the time being, only you know what disk drives are inside your PC.

julie ram say:

Hi,

To help you in transferring the files from your PC to your SD card, follow the steps below:

  1. Connect the micro SD card reader to your computer.
  2. Plug the micro SD card into the card reader.
  3. Open the folder that contains files you want to transfer to the SD card.
  4. Select the files, press and hold "Ctrl" and then, click the files that you want to transfer. Or press "Ctrl + A" to select all the files in the folder.
  5. Press "Ctrl + C" to copy the files.
  6. Choose a drive on your SD card and open the folder where you want to store the files.
  7. Press "Ctrl + V" to paste the files.
  8. Wait until all files are transferred then, remove the micro SD card from the card reader.
