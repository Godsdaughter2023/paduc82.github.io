brianlynn say:

I finally figured it out!

I had an old Bluetooth adapter that I was not using any more.

Windows was stuck on the devices attached to that adapter.

Unfortunately all the "broken" devices were talking to the Microsoft Bluetooth Enumerator so there was no way to tell that they were actually attempting to be a device connected to a non-existent radio. The Enumerator seems to have caused some of the problems.

I had to dig out the old adapter, reinstall it, and then UnPair/UnInstall all the devices attached to that adapter.

Then I re-uninstalled the adapter, and re-installed my new adapter.

All my stuck headsets are gone. All headsets now add/remove in all ways expected.

Thanks for the help!
