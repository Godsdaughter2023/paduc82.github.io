When light 1 is on and green, the machine is warming up.

When light 1 is blinking and green, the machine is receiving data.

When light 1 is on and red, there is an error, including open cover, paper empty, installer error, or invalid error.

When light 1 is on and orange, there is a paper jam.

When light 2 is blinking and red, the toner is low.  You should have a new toner cartridge ready.  You can remove the toner cartridge and gently shake it from side to side about 5 or 6 times to get a bit more useful printing out of it.

When light 2 is blinking rapidly and red, the toner is very close to empty.

A reset erases your hard disk. There is no such thing as erasing a hard disk "the Windows way." I have to admit, that was amusing. A reset is, for all intents and purposes, a clean install.

How do you re-download pictures? - Microsoft Community

bill-nyc say:

How do you re-download pictures?

I downloaded my pictures from the camera to the hard drive.  Then, I advertently deleted some.  I had not erased them from the camera, but when I went to re-download them I got a message that there were no un-dowloaded pictures on the camera.  How can i download these pictures again? 
tad cook say: It sounds like in the process of making this feature "smart", Microsoft lost some flexibility.

Create a new subdirectory for the photos, then point the download toward that subdirectory.

Right click on the Start button, select "Open Windows Explorer".

Click on Pictures, then at the top click on New Folder.

Now you have a new place to put them, and I **assume** the photo downloading feature will work properly.

An alternative would be to use Windows Explorer to transfer the photos.

With the camera attached, look for it as a drive or device under Computer, then highlight the photos you want, hit ctrl-c (hold down the Control key and hit C), then click on your new folder and click ctrl-v.  This will paste them in the new location.

To make it a bit easier, in Windows Explorer first click on "Change your view" (a weird little icon in the upper right), and select Details from the drop-down.  Now you can click on Date Modified to organize them in order by date/time (or you can right-click the bar to the right of Date Modified, and select the Date created feature, and sort by the date/time you took the photo)

Now click on the first photo (oldest, or newest) that you want to copy, hold down the shift key and use the down arrow on your keyboard to select/include all the images files you want, THEN hit ctrl-c to copy, and use ctrl-v to paste in your new folder.

There are no doubt other ways to do this.
