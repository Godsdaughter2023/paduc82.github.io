Hi,

How to get a Desktop Shortcut for it and also re-pin it to Taskbar.

 

Left click on far left Icon in the Taskbar > then in the "Start" Window click on down Arrow at bottom left to go to "Apps by Category" Window > locate the App under Windows System > right click on it and select "Open file location" > in the next Window that presents itself you right click on App from the list > run Mouse Cursor over "Send to" > select "Desktop (create shortcut)".

And while you are at the "Apps by Category" Window with it > right click on the particular App > select "Pin to Taskbar"

palmettobling say:

Content appearing on the store is up to the developer and/or publisher of that game.  It takes additional time and effort of the developers, and thus money, to program the game for each platform.  Because it does require work to make changes for each platform, these developers and publishers will weigh how much it will cost them to make those changes for each platform and weigh if what they project to make from that work will be worth the effort.  And that's just the practical aspect, there are other instances where there are business deals that would make titles exclusive, design philosophies that might prevent them from releasing on other platforms, or legal issues.  Most of the time, it's the first issue though. 

For example, I reached out to Super Giant Games to let them know that I love their work and if they had any plans to release their more current titles on Xbox One, and they responded that they would love to, but they made a conscious decision to focus development for the PS5 and PC, as those were platforms they are familiar with and with current company plans and projections could not dedicate the resources developing for a third platform.  I'm very sad that it's the one platform I prefer to play, but after watching the No Clip documentaries following them through development with Hades

, I can FURTHER FULLY understand why they are strapped for resources.  I just hope they are able to expand next generation, because I love their games and almost even more so the music in those games.
