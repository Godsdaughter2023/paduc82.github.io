a. user say:

The problem is that the programming to allow the accounts to re-open is included in the shutdown program and if it is not done correctly (as occurs with a crash from overheating) then the process doesn't work.  There's no patch or correction for this.  If it shutsdown properly, it works so the program/feature is operating correctly.  In a crash, it simply has no opportunity to save the information as it does with a normal shutdown so it is lost.

There are small fans that can be attached to processors that sometimes help with overheating.  Also, you should completely clear out the inside using an air can to remove dust and other debris that may be restricting air flow and make certain that the intake and exhaust vents are completely clear and that the fan is clear and running properly.  If the problem persists, it may be wise to take it to a computer repair shop where they may be able to do something to identify and correct the cause of the overheating or add something more than just the fan I mentioned to provide better circulation.  In time, this overheating problem will damage your computer (it it already hasn't) and should be addressed before things get any worse.

I hope this helps or at least explains the situation.

Good luck!
