al jarvi say:

Gaming across the VPN may or may not be a pleasurable experience. There is quite a bit of overhead with a VPN and the game may be slow. As always YMMV...

Can you not simply run the game between your computers using the appropriate port forwarding scheme for the particular game?

http://portforward.com/cports.htm

The From and To addresses are a range of addresses. The first address, ie. the From address is the IP assigned to the VPN server as seen by the VPN client. The To address is the last address in the range, ie. it could be the From address plus one or plus 5 or plus n. Whatever. The point being the native PPTP VPN function can only have one incoming connection at a time so I arbitrarily assigned it the next address when I ran a PPTP VPN server. The To address will be assigned to the remote client by the VPN server software when you make the connection.

Just make sure the range of addresses match your local LAN but they should be outside the range of any DHCP assigned IP address from your router. So if your router DHCP server assigns addresses starting at .50 for example create a From/To range of .30 to .35 for example. Also make sure the addresses don't conflict with any static IP addresses you may have setup.

The other issue is the addressing you use on your LAN and the addressing your friend uses on his LAN. If they use the same range, ie. 192.168.1.X for example, then one of you needs to change their LAN IP range to something else, ie. 192.168.10.X for example. Not doing so will cause issues with the remote client trying to find computers on your LAN.
