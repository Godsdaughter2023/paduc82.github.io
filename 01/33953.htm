    jetta48 say:

    Your camera is set to use Canon's prorietary RAW image format known as CR2. Without getting too technical, RAW images have the advantage of being "more true" to the image taken in by your cameras' sensor. This is great for professional photographers/enthusiasts, but for the average camera user it is unnecessary and RAW files have the disadvantage of being larger than JPEGs and not supported without additional software by Windows and many programs.

    If you are an average home user by far the best solution would be to set your camera to capture photos in JPEG/JPG format instead - check your cameras' manual on how to do it (it should be simple). That way all your photos will be in JPEG/JPG format and you won't have any comaptibility problems on Windows (or probably anywhere else).

    If you want to use CR2 files, see if your camera is supported on this Microsoft Camera Codec Pack:

    http://www.microsoft.com/download/en/details.aspx?id=26829 .
