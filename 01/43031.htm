patrick barker say:

Hi,

The attached DMP file is of the VIDEO_SCHEDULER_INTERNAL_ERROR (119) bug check.

This indicates that the video scheduler has detected a fatal violation.

If we take a look at the call stack:

 # Child-SP          RetAddr           Call Site
00 fffff880`0bad7488 fffff880`06a2622f nt!KeBugCheckEx
01 fffff880`0bad7490 fffff880`08118ec5 watchdog!WdLogEvent5+0x11b
02 fffff880`0bad74e0 fffff880`08119131 dxgmms1!VidSchiVerifyDriverReportedFenceId+0xad
03 fffff880`0bad7510 fffff880`08118f82 dxgmms1!VidSchDdiNotifyInterruptWorker+0x19d
04 fffff880`0bad7560 fffff880`0802013f dxgmms1!VidSchDdiNotifyInterrupt+0x9e
05 fffff880`0bad7590 fffff880`06ed525f dxgkrnl!DxgNotifyInterruptCB+0x83
06 fffff880`0bad75c0 00000000`00000000 atikmpag+0x625f

From the above, it appears that the crash occurred in the DirectX routine that reports the out of order fence returns (dxgmms1!VidSchiVerifyDriverReportedFenceId).

Essentially, what happens is the fence ID's are being returned in a different order than expected, and/or out-of-order, therefore the bugcheck. DirectX says 'Woah, this is not right' and likely goes ahead and calls the bugcheck.

The likelihood of an 0x119 being hardware is very small, and usually when it's hardware we'll see 0x116, etc. However, that does not mean it's not possible and it does in fact happen at times. In general, most of this bug check is caused by bugs in drivers causing good fence ID's to be overwritten with incorrect addresses.

-----------------

For more info, let's take a look at the parameters:

BugCheck 119, {1, 19d21e, 19d220, 19d21f}

The 1st parameter indicates the driver has reported an invalid fence ID.

The 2nd through 4th parameters are the actual fence ID's themselves. The 2nd parameter specifically is the fence ID that caused the crash as it was not the expected ID.
