a. user say:

The H&S Service does not need to be set to Automatic - setting it to Manual is fine and it will start itself when it needs to start.  Setting it to Manual also keeps the potentially unnecessary Service from starting and running until you need it, then it will keep running until you restart.  I would just set it to Manual but you can set it to Automatic if you are compelled to do so.

If the H&S Service is Disabled, you will get a message like this:

Windows cannot open Help and Support because a system service is not running.
To fix this problem, start the service named 'Help and Support'.

If you are seeing nothing when you click it, you may need to reinstall the H&S Service (you don't have to reinstall XP - just the H&S part).  If you truly formatted and reinstalled XP that doesn't make a whole lot of sense though unless it got broken post reinstall.

The Help and Support Center sometimes gets broken with overzealous cleaning and there are lots of things to "try" that sometimes work and sometimes they don't. Sometimes people just give up and never use Help and Support Center again, but it can always be repaired.

  1. Right-click on the driver's name.
