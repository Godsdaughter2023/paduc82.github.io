mike hall... always say:

Boot, re-boot.. The process where a computer system is initialized, started up.

Hard boot is where the computer is started from a powered OFF state..

Soft boot is where the computer is restarted while still powered up..

Install, re-install.. The process where folders, files and executables are transferred from removable media, (diskette, CD, DVD, Flash drive) or a remote server to a permanent location on the local hard drive.

Re-install.. where a failed program/application installation is re-run with a view to correcting errors et al.

OK?

Generally, if a total re-install does not cure problems, the hard drive is the culprit. They do not last forever, some failing early in their lives and some not.

The symptoms of a failing drive show as excessive boot times, the time taken from a hard or soft boot, to get to the login screen, and then excessive times to get from the login to a usable desktop environment, as in the cursor has stopped spinning. Tis is often followed by programs being slow to start and as an example, word processor files taking their time appearing.
