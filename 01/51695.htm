    andre da costa say:

    The interesting thing is, new computers that come with a trial version of Microsoft Office (which is actually a fully working version, but just for a limited time), will include Office 365 Home Premium, not Professional Plus, which is normally a volume license distribution sold to big businesses that do large deployments of Office.

    The Office 365 trial, which is normally what is included with the purchase of a new PC is for 30 days, requiring that you purchase a subscription to continue using it once it has ended. Its possible the computer was not bought new out of the box and was preloaded with a Professional Plus trial by the retailer. Its possible this was also the 180 day trial from TechNet.

    In either case, you will need to purchase a new license whether a subscription or perpetual license:
