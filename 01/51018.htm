davem121 say:
Hi Zafatta, it is not easy to remove a file association in Windows 10,

Open Windows File Explorer

On the view Ribbon (tab) check the box marked 'File Name Extensions'

Close File Explorer

Right click your Desktop and choose New - Text File

Change the file extension on that new file from .txt to .exe

Confirm the change

Right click a .7z file, choose open with - Choose another App

Check the box marked 'Always use this app to open .7z files' - IMPORTANT!

Scroll to the bottom and click 'More Apps'

Again scroll to the bottom and choose 'Find another app on this PC'

Navigate to the dummy file exe on your Desktop and click OK

The .7z file will not open

Delete the dummy exe file on your Desktop and restart your PC

The file association will then be broken!

robert aldwinckle on forums say:

only like 1 out of 10 times and those other 9 times are very frustrating since i have to turn off my computer and every time i have to restart every programe and so on...

  

Unfortunately this is the way it now works for some hardware.  However, you can almost certainly improve your workaround by substituting some keyboard shortcuts.

  

The first thing to check is "Where is the Display going?"  E.g. it sounds as if you are looking at a system that has (inexplicably?) put your Project state to Second Screen Only.  So, assuming that is the case try pressing Win+P, CursorDown, Enter.  The main point is that regardless of what state the video system was in that change will cause a refresh.  If our assumption is correct it will also change it to PC Screen Only which depending on resolution of the primary screen may be exactly what you want to get going again.

  

You can use Narrator to check on what is happening even though you can't see it (provided you have your speakers turned on and the default audio port is not associated with a failing video one.)  Press Win+Ctrl+Enter to start Narrator.

  

Another method of refreshing the video system via the keyboard is supposedly Win+Ctrl+Shift+B.   Unfortunately it is not very reliable, although you may at least hear a beep from it.  Often it does something more on a subsequent try.  YMMV.

  

Good luck
