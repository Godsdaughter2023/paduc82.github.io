Mr. Murphy answered your question about how to recover your data that wasn't backed up.  You had two other questions:

To get into safe mode, click repeatedly on F8 while booting until the recovery menu appears.  Then select safe mode with networking and enter.  You won't have full functionality, but it will give you quite a bit of access to the system (enough to do the backup you wanted to do without needing to make that disk if you can get into safe mode).  If F8 does not bring up the recovery screen or if trying to get into safe mode doesn't work, then you have more serious problems.

You've already tried Startup Repair and System Restore with no success.  That was good thinking - it would have been my first two choices for troubleshooting.

Do you have a genuine Windows Vista Installation DVD or a Recovery Disk provided by the manufacturer or made by you from the recovery partition on your hard drive?

To boot to the CD you may need to change the BIOS to make the CD-drive first in the boot sequence.  To do that, wait for the screen that tells you the F key to push to access the boot menu or boot setup.  Push it quickly. While in Command Prompt, type chkdsk /f /r and enter and let it run.  It will scan and try to fix any corruption or bad sectors on your hard drive and mostly remove that as a cause.

Hopefully one of these procedures will resolve your problem.  If not, post back and we'll try something else (probably a clean boot since that's all that will be left as an option).  Post back in any case so we'll know how it turned out.

Good luck!

In-order to re-install the audio drivers, you need to completely uninstall the existing audio drivers including any Realtek sound software. Before you uninstall the current Realtek drivers on your computer, download the latest audio drivers from your sound card manufacturer or your computer manufacturer.

Once you completely uninstall all the audio components on your computer, double click on the downloaded audio driver file and follow the installation instructions.
