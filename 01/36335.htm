anannya podder say:

First, try uninstalling and reinstalling drivers using the Device Manager by following these steps:

1.       Click the Start button, and then click Control Panel.

2.       Click Hardware and Sound, and under Devices and Printers, select  Device Manager.

3.       In Device Manager, expand DVD/CD-ROM drives, right-click the CD and DVD devices, and then click Uninstall.

4.       When you are prompted to confirm that you want to remove the device, click OK.

5.       Restart the computer.

6.       After the computer restarts, the drivers will be automatically installed.

If this fails to work or if the drive does not appear in the Device Manager at all, the Hardware and Devices Troubleshooter may be able to resolve the issue.  To run the troubleshooter:

1.       Click the Start button, type troubleshooting in the Search box, and then press ENTER.

2.       Click Hardware and Sound.

3.       Click the Hardware and Devices troubleshooter, and then press Next.

 

If you are still unable to use the CD/DVD drive, see Your CD drive or DVD drive is missing or is not recognized by Windows or other programs, and follow the same steps as provided for Vista.  (http://support.microsoft.com/kb/314060/)

Start by creating a new identity, File | Identities | Add New Identity. Create a new one and import your messages and Address Book from the old identity and delete it. This will have the same effect as having a brand new OE and should speed things up.

General precautions for Outlook Express:
 
Do not archive mail in the Inbox or Sent Items. Create your own user defined folders and move the messages you wish to save to them. Empty Deleted Items folder daily. Although dbx files have a theoretical capacity of 2GB, I recommend about a 300MB max for less chance of corruption.
 
Information about the maximum file size of the .dbx files that are used by Outlook Express:
http://support.microsoft.com/?kbid=903095
 
After you are done, follow up by compacting your folders manually while working *offline* and do it often.
