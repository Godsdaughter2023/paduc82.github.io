6. Problem Solved

thedyzaster say:

Disconnect all devices from the network, on the Xbox, Reset Alt Mac Addr once more, then when it turns on, delete the cache by holding the power button on the front of the console for ten seconds. Keep the console off. At the point the only network device that should be on is the router, so if you look at the lights on the router, all should be solid. If one is flashing, then go play Hide and Seek with your wireless devices because something is still connected. After the lights are completely solid, pull the cable out of the back of the router, keep it off for 5 minutes. After that turn the Router back on, wait 2 minutes, all lights should be back on and solid (If not, find the device that's connected) After that turn the Xbox on, and test the multiplayer connection once more.

The update itself will not cause your speeds to drop, however, an update will cause you to get a new DHCP lease, which means it acts like the Xbox is a newly connected device. Some routers are set to give a "priority" device the majority of the speed, while giving secondary devices a throttled amount to share equally. With routers like those, that's when you get this error. The Xbox was most likely a priority device, and when you did the update, it removed it from priority.

If these steps don't work, make sure you're wired and not wireless (As you will always get better speeds from a wired connection and will never ever get your fastest possible speeds using wireless because there is not a direct connection, its spread through the room.

Hence for black screen users a procedure that I had been suggesting (before getting the Second Screen idea) was this loop procedure:   <loop>  Shift-Tab  Shift-Enter  Wait for signs of something happening  If signs of disk activity wait some more  </loop>   The idea is that this would get you into the Shutdown menu, positioned at the Restart command on which you would then be able to press Shift-Enter  (or Shift-Space.)   I was actually hoping that the loop would also be useful in case the user did not notice any disk activity from that and overshot to enter the next menu, the Accessibility menu but It appears that Shift-Enter would not work there, only Shift-Space but then you would hear some comforting words from Narrator, offering to guide you through the darkness.   I can't remember why I chose Shift-Enter for my loop.   I think there may be a case in some of the preceding waypoints where only Shift-Enter would work (not Shift-Space) and at the time I was not aware of the problem with Shift-Enter at the Accessibility menu.   

 

That loop goes backwards to the Restart command by design.   You could also try going forwards to the Accessibility menu.   But then you would have to press just Tab and not know how many to press, depending on where you were starting from.   And you could not just press your Spacebar indiscriminately.   E.g. if you only pressed enough Tabs to land you in the Password field your space would not be ignored there.   Pressing Shift- for the Backtab  conveniently skips the lockscreen issue (assuming the lockscreen isn't really locked and requires a Ctrl-Alt-Delete; then you are on your own.  I have no ideas for getting around in that case, especially if there is more than one account set up which seemed likely to me.)
