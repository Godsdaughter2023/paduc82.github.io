Yes, that's what portforwarding is. Guess you have it configured then.

DMZ is the demilitarized zone which describes a part in your network between the Internet (or better your public IP) and your firewall. So if putting a device in the DMZ you put in outside of your firewall protected home network. This is useful for servers which need to be accessible from out of the Internet. And for your gaming console as it needs to be accessible from there, too. By portforwarding you make certain pinholes in your firewall pointing to a certain device, by putting the device in the DMZ you place it outside the firewall so it will get all ports without forwarding them.

So it's easier to setup the one in the DMZ as you do not need to configure portforwarding then and especially you will never have to add certain ports if a new released game needs ports you have not forwarded yet.
