arminatorx say:

If you already played on your Xbox 360 and want to keep that progress on your "soon to be Live" account, skip the following paragraph. 

If you are just starting with Xbox and didn't have any previous gaming done on your console, simply go to https://www.xbox.com and create your account there. You can then download it later to your console. DO NOT DO THIS IF YOU INTEND TO BRING YOUR EXISTING CONSOLE PROFILE ONLINE!!!

Now to get your console online. 

Usually, such "captive WiFi Portals" store the MAC Address of the device that signed in. So use your regular PC/Laptop to sign into your WiFi, then find the MAC Address of your network interface. 

To find that, open up a command prompt on Windows by pressing Windowskey + R and type "cmd", then press OK.

In that black command window, type:

IPCONFIG /all

This will list some information about your network adapter(s). Find the network adapter that is used to connect your PC online, and find the line about "Physical Address". It should have a MAC address that looks like this: 

1A-2B-C3-45-DE-F6 

(Six pairs of numbers and letters from A-F and 0-9)

Write down that MAC address, and go to the network settings of your Xbox 360. On the Xbox 360 you can go to the advanced network settings and set a Custom MAC address. Copy the MAC address you wrote down from your PC to the console. 

Make sure your PC/Laptop is now turned off, and is not accessing the internet at the same time. 

With the cloned MAC address, the Xbox 360 should now be able to connect to Live. Do a connection test to Xbox Live in the network settings to be sure. 

If necessary, have the console update itself after it connected to Xbox Live. 

If the console still can't connect to Xbox Live, contact your Network provider for a solution. 

If the console was able to connect, it now depends on if you created a new account above, or if you want to use the account from your console. 

If you want to start new, press the guide button and choose to "Download Profile". Provide the Email address and password you used on the Xbox.com website, and it'll download your profile to use. 

If you want to bring your existing Gamertag Profile online, sign into that profile now, and choose "Join Xbox Live" in the Guide menu. 

Provide a Microsoft Account email, and follow the on screen instructions to bring your console profile online.

This problem may be a sign of hard-disk damage. When a file resides on a damaged area of the hard disk, Windows cannot access the file. When this occurs, the program crashes.

Let us try the following steps, and check if it helps.

Step 1: End the Windows Explorer process and create the new one.

  1. Press Ctrl+Shift+Esc, under Process tab, search for Windows Explorer.
  2. Right click on it and click on End task.
  3. Click on File in the Task Manager at top left corner of the window and select Run new task.
  4. Type explorer.exe and click on OK. 

Step 2: Run the Chkdsk.exe program to try to recover files from any damaged areas of the disk. To do this, follow these steps:

  1. Press Windows + X keys.
  2. Click on Command Prompt (Admin) to bring up an elevated command prompt.
  3. In the command prompt type chkdsk /r and then press ENTER.
  4. Press Y, and then press ENTER.
  5. Restart your computer. During startup, Windows will scan your disk for errors, and Windows will try to recover any files that are located on damaged areas on the disk.

Disclaimer: While performing chkdsk on the hard drive if any bad sectors are found on the hard drive, while the bad sectors might be recovered however there might be pertinent data loss.

Hope it helps.
