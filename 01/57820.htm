ken blake say:

OK, I understand better now.

Two points:

First, part of the reason your question was confusing was that you are treating XP and XP Professional as two different operating systems. Note that there is no such thing as  "Windows XP operating system" in the sense you meant it. There is the operating system called Windows XP, and it comes in two different editions: Home and Professional. When you say "Windows XP operating system," you presumably mean Windows XP Home. Please understand that I'm not telling you this to give you a hard time. But correct terminology is very important, especially when asking questions, lest you get misunderstood and get wrong answers.

What you were told is correct: you can not *downgrade* from XP Professional to XP Home.  However, you *can* install XP Home on a computer running XP Professional. But it has to be a *clean* installation, from scratch, not a downgrade. A clean installation begins by formatting the drive and that means you will lose everything that was on the drive. After installing Home, you will have to reinstall all your programs from their original media, and reload all your data files from the backups you've made of them on external media (you do do regular backups to external media, I hope).

But let me ask why would you want to change from Professional to Home? It's a lot of work and there is no advantage to doing that. Any program that will run on Home will also run on Professional.
