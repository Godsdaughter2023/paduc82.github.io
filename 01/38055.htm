bravoman21 say:
Hello Berdako,

Based on the information that you provided, it seems like the file inherited some sort of permission that denies you the ability to delete the file. In Windows, even if you have the ability to perform a function (such as deleting a file), any Deny permissions on a file, folder, or group will supersede any Allow permissions. This can explain why you are unable to delete the file, but you're the only user on that computer.

To see if you are the Owner or have the ability to manage this particular file, we'll need to check on its Security settings.

Right click on the file with the issue and select the Properties option. A new window with several tabs at the top should open, click on the Security tab. Click on your username and check to see if there is any checkmarks on the right side under the Deny column.

https://i.imgur.com/9dXwKhG.png

If you see any checkmarks in the Deny column, click on the "Edit..." button and uncheck any box that is checked under Deny. Once all the checkmarks have been unchecked and hit OK.

To check and see if your account is the owner of the file, click on the Advanced button at the bottom of the Security window. A new window should appear showing you the Advanced Security Settings for the file. At the top of this new window should be an Owner field that should have your username filled in.

If your username is not filled in, click on the Change link to the right of this field. A new window should appear where you can type in your username.

Let us know if this helped.

Issue 2: Network

Method 1:
Remove and re-create the wireless network connection.
To remove and re-create the wireless network connection, follow these steps:
1. Click Start, type ncpa.cpl, and then hit Enter. 
2. Right-click your wireless network connection, and then click Properties.
3. Click the Wireless Networks tab.
4. Under Preferred networks, click your wireless network, and then click Remove.
5. Click View Wireless Networks.
6. Under Network Tasks, click Refresh Network List.
7. Under Choose a wireless network, click the wireless network to which you want to connect, and then click Connect.

Method 2: update the Network Drivers from Device Manager.
1. Type Windows key + X.

3. Expand Sound Video and Game controller.
