Microsoft intentionally releases new feature updates gradually over time. As Microsoft explains:

"We will be taking a measured and throttled approach, allowing us to study device health data as we increase availability via Windows Update."

" ... device health data ..." is Microsoft-speak for 'how many computers have been harmed.' People who rushed to be among the first to receive the previous feature update are very familiar with this.

The smart way to receive a new feature update has always been to wait for Windows Update to send it to you. It can take weeks or months for a new feature update to reach all 800+ million devices running Windows 10. Unless there is a new feature in an update that you really must have now, or you are affected by a specifically identified bug that an update fixes, you would be wise to follow the majority and wait for the update to come to you, however long it takes.
