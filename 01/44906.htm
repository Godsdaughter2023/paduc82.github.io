dratomic say:

Ranking in the multiplayer wait screen is bugged; it can only display positions, wins and losses from 1-256.

Say that your actual ranking is 102421 place with 12 wins and 61 losses then the ranking displayed in the wait screen is 21 (you are 21st in the range of 102400-102656). Then you win and climb to 102388, next time the multiplayer wait screen displays 244 since you are place 244 in the range 102144 to 102399.

Wins and losses is a bit different, they simply reset to 0 in the multiplayer waitscreen once you reach 256 and start counting again. However if you look up your ranking on the leaderboard it still shows a total of 256 and new wins or losses will be added on top of it.

The bug is that the code on the multiplayer screen cant handle outputs bigger then 256 and basicly cycles untill it reaches a valid value within the 0-256 range.

This can give funny setups where you have 4 people playing each other who are all in first place.

[based on observation]Now the actual multiplayer ranking seems to work on a formulae that takes into account points gained, games played, wins and losses. The win loss ratio weighs heavy but is also heavily offset against the quit ratio, another factor allthough it seems to factor in lightly is recent activity. For instance I seldomly quit and am currently ranked 41st (however I was ranked 23rd overall with my current w/l, I didnt play for a couple of months and slowly dropped.

[based on observation]I know for sure from observing notorious quitters that quitting heavily influences the overall ranking, it may be that you are awarded 0 points or 2 points for a game you quit and you are awarded a loss, the loss however doesnot display in your ranking. Known quitters have often skewed win / loss ratios of something like 800 wins and 200 losses but are not ranked where you would expect them. The very best players reach a w/l  ratio of about 50/50. No matter how good you are, you are bound to loose some games be it through bad luck or through the sheer stupidity of others.

Trueskill is a different story alltogether, trueskill is a ranking system made by Microsoft; it's basicly meant for match making. What it does is as follows; you gain points based on the points of players you beat, if you loose you loose points based on their strenght (ie loosing from a low ranked player hurts bad).

The best trueskill rank I've been is 15 or 14, normally averaging around 12 when I'm in the flow so to speak (currently 9, coming back after a long period of absence and having lost a fair number of games getting back into it).

I.e. if I loose to a player ranked 50000 my trueskill will drop dramatically, while if I win from say No Cursing (5) or Texas Ham (4) it will climb a little depending on their current trueskill ratings. No Cursing has a trueskill of 11 at the moment, I dont know about Texas Ham because he isnt on my friendslist.

Trueskill doesnt seem to be effected by quitting.

Now this ranking seems to be bugged as well or maybe they changed the algorithm in a patch or something because the number one has a trueskill of 35 and a worse ratio then I do.

Read the trueskill as follows:

12+ top ten quality player, manages the game, will win given the slightest chance

11 top 100 player, can beat anyone, needs one chance to win it

10 good player

9 solid player  may make a mistake here and there

7-8 decent player bound not to see all dangers lurking, likely to not use left trigger

6 average will make mistakes/not trade/costly trades, wont use left trigger

1-5 starting player prepare to scream and be frustrated
