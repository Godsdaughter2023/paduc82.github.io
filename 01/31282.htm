If the console you bought came new with a hard drive installed, then the console has an "original Xbox 360 hard drive". 

Be aware however, that if you bought the console used, that the previous owner might have installed a "third party" hard drive, which works for Xbox 360 games, but does not have the necessary Xbox emulator for running backwards compatible titles. 

Some Xbox 360 models (like the original Xbox 360 Arcade, or later the 4GB slim models) didn't come with a hard drive, and you could buy a hard drive accessory. However, since Microsoft stopped making the Xbox 360, you will have difficulty finding "original" drives. And as I said above, only the "original Xbox 360 hard drives" that you could buy from Microsoft will work with original Xbox titles. 

Examples for original hard drive accessories would be this hard drive for the Original Xbox 360, and here an original hard drive for the Xbox 360 S and E models.

While this hard drive fits into the Slim and E models of the 360, and will probably work with Xbox 360 games, it will probably not work with original Xbox backwards compatible titles.
