1. First, go to the 'Settings' menu of your desktop or laptop. To do so, click on the Windows logo that you will find in the lower left corner of your screen and select 'Settings' from the list of programs.

2. In 'Windows Settings', locate the 'Update and security' icon and click on it.

3. In the menu on the left, click on 'Recovery', followed by the gray 'Start' button that you will see under the 'Reset this PC' section.

4. Another window will automatically appear in which you must choose an option: either reset your PC keeping the files or restore it without saving any files or configuration. In our case, select the second option, which will also reinstall Windows permanently deleting personal files as well as applications and installed programs. In addition, it will change the configuration to its default values.

5. By clicking on 'Remove all', followed by 'Next' and 'Restore', your PC will start to reset, which in English is known as hard reset or factory reset. Your computer will reboot automatically when the reset is complete.

How to restore the factory settings on a Windows 7 or 8 PC with the installation disk.

1. On Windows 7 computers, open the 'Control Panel' and select the first option, called 'System and security'.

On Windows 8 computers, you must go to 'PC Settings' by clicking on the gear icon in the quick access menu.


2. In Windows 7, click on 'Backup and restore', followed by 'Recover system or computer configuration' and 'Advanced recovery methods'.

In contrast, in Windows 8, click on 'Update and recover' and select 'Recovery' from the menu on the left.


3. On your Windows 7 device, click on 'Reinstall Windows', always remembering that you will need the installation disc. In this sense, hit 'Yes' when they ask you if you have it.

It will give you the option to make a backup of your files. If you have already done it or do not need it, click 'Skip'. Finally, click on 'Restart' to restart your computer.


In Windows 8, click the 'Start' button that you will see under the 'Remove all and reinstall Windows' section. Finally, you must insert the installation disc and follow the instructions that you will see on the screen.


4. For Windows 7, there is another step. After restarting the computer, the 'System recovery options' will appear on the screen. Click 'Next' until the list of recovery tools appears.


Click on 'Restore Management' to restore the initial configuration and again on 'Restore the operating system to factory defaults'. Click on 'Next' until the restoration process begins.

Finally, follow the steps of the Windows 7 installation process

How to restore the factory settings on a Windows 7 or 8 PC without installation disk:
1. Start or restart your computer.


2. During the boot process, press the corresponding function key.

This can change from manufacturer to manufacturer, so we list below the possible combinations according to the model of your computer.
Acer = Alt + F10
Asus = F9
Dell / Alienware = F8
HP = F11
Lenovo = F11
MSI = F3
Samsung = F4
Sony = F10
Toshiba = 0 (no numeric keypad, stop pressing when the brand logo appears)

We recommend pressing the keys repeatedly since there is very little time to do so. You may have to repeat steps 1 and 2 more than once.


If it still does not work, look on the screen for a message indicating how to start the recovery process or contact the manufacturer directly.

3. A good indication that the key combination has worked is the appearance of the system recovery program, which may vary between one brand and another.


Finally, follow the instructions to restore factory settings, as we did in the last step of the previous tutorial.


In total, the process will take about 30 minutes if it is formatted and the system is reinstalled at the same time. There are some recovery programs that install device drivers and separate applications.

The process to configure a Tablet with Windows 10 with the initial factory settings is exactly the same as that to be done for computers and laptops with this same version of the Microsoft operating system.

For Windows 8 or 8.1 tablets, the steps to follow are very similar, but so that there is no doubt, we describe them one by one below.
