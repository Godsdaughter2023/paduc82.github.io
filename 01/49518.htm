To Turn Off Windows Defender (PERMANENTLY)

Look for gpedit.msc (type directly after opening the start menu) and run it as admin (secondary button). Then, enter system passsword and press Enter.

Go to Computer Configuration/Administrative Templates/Windows Components/Endpoint Protection.

In the right pane of Endpoint Protection in Local Group Policy Editor, double click/tap on the Turn off Endpoint Protection policy to edit it.

In the new window that will be opened, select (dot) Enabled, Apply and then click/tap on OK.

When finished, close the Local Group Policy Editor and Restart the computer.

Deactivating "Windows search" service for that device/pendrive (or any other external unit) is also highly recommended.

And, of course, remember to install another antivirus by your own (remember you have just permanently removed the by default protection of Windows Defender).

I finally solved my issue, and not just with the corruption of the Windows Store but with the other Modern Apps that got corrupted for still-unknown reasons.

It's basically this: I reinstalled Windows 10 on my machine.

Nuances worth noting:

1. I intentionally didn't use the Refresh PC or Reset PC options because this would entail losing my apps and settings or everything, respectively.  I wanted to be sure I still kept everything as -is.

2. I initially tried reinstalling from an ISO I created using the Media Creation Tool.  However upon running the tool, I was being asked for my license key -- something I didn't have.  I checked with my laptop's manufacturer and was told the license key was embedded in the motherboard, so there was no way to find that out.  In other words, my Windows 10 license key was granted via Digital Entitlement.

3. Fortunately the Media Creation Tool was smart enough to recognize the license key.  Using the tool, I downloaded the binaries of Windows 10 and performed an "upgrade" of my system.  It took 2 hours and 15 minutes to complete everything from download to install.  I suspect that I might have spent the same amount of time if I used either the Refresh PC or Reset PC options anyway. 

It's just an inconvenience to perform an in-place "upgrade" / reinstall just to fix Modern Apps not working while keeping existing apps and settings -- but it worked.  I checked and all my profile accounts have working Windows Stores and / or Modern Apps where previously such got corrupted.

As far as I'm concerned this issue is closed... for now.

acid xix say:

those rumors about Epic working on a Gears of War Kinect game? Well they were all rubbish, apparently.

Speaking to Eurogamer, Gears of War 3's lead gameplay designer Lee Perry said that his team are not currently working with Microsoft's peripheral-free evil eye machine.

"There's definitely interest in terms of looking at [motion controls] in terms of what is a fit for us," said Perry.

"Personally I don't think I've seen any one thing that's been like 'that's what we need to make an amazing Gears IP'. I think there's a lot of potential in a lot of different systems. There's a lot of stuff to explore but we haven't nailed anything down.

"I don't think as a studio we'd ever force anything out there that we didn't feel actually did the world true, but that's not to say that any of the things out there might not make for a great project, it's just not what we're doing right now."

Speaking in a separate interview, design director Cliff Bleszinski echoed the sentiment, saying, "I've been asked about integration into Gears. If we had put it into Gears 3, considering the development time table, it would have been a tacked-on feature.

"If we were to get around to doing something Kinect-based, we would want to dedicate something to it and really knock it out the park and make it a Kinect game."

So there you go, no Kinect Gears game. Not just yet. Genuine question: Would you want one anyway?

Gears of War 3 Showcased on Late Night with Jimmy Fallon

Written Wednesday, June 15, 2011 By Richard Walker

View author's profile

 

Having hosted a demo of Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 3 on Monday's show, last night saw Late Night with Jimmy Fallon's game week continue with a short burst of gameplay footage from Gears of War 3, showing some of the action from the E3 2011 demo in which a massive Leviathan boss chomps its way through the floor while Marcus Fenix pilots a Silverback mech.

There's a lot of Fallon struggling with the controls and shouting before he hands the controller back to Epic's Cliff Bleszinski who then struggles to finish off the boss after an extended period of filling its gooey yellow eyeball full of lead. "Die, you ***!" he even shouts at one point, as the Leviathan refuses to yield during the E3 demo.

Once the demo's finished, Bleszinski confirms that Gears 3 will be the trilogy closer like Lord of the Rings, but says that there "might be a Hobbit", which either means there might be a Hobbit in the game, or more likely Epic could potentially explore prequel stories for subsequent Gears of War games. Whatever the case, we can't imagine that Gears of War 3 will be the final Gears title when it launches on September 20th, 2011.
