xbf diego m say:

Hi there,

I took a listen to the video you posted and the sound relates more to a slight grinding sound due to some kind of friction with the motor for the fan. Most mechanical devices with rotating components, like a fan motor, will have bearings which are small balls inside of the wheel allowing smooth movement in one direction. These balls also act as the medium between the outer components with the inner components of the wheel so that there is no friction when it is spinning. These balls stay in place while they rotate, but it's possible that one of these bearings may have come loose from it's original position causing some friction while it rotates which then causes this sound.

I wouldn't worry about it too much until it becomes reasonably louder to where it's disturbing or if the fan loses efficiency and causes the Xbox to over-heat and shut down. If that ever happens then it would be time for a repair.

If you have any other questions just let us know!

Hi Robert,

We can check the display settings and may need to try several actions to correct this probem. First, while the external display is connected although upside down, open Settings (Windows key + I) > System > Display. Under select and rearrange displays, you will see the monitors/displays. Click on Identify to determine which is the Surface display and which is the projected display. Select the projected display (it should be highlighted in blue), then go to Orientation and check the current selection. Choose Landscape to correct the orientation of the external display.

If the steps are unable to resolve the issue, go to Device Manager by pressing Windows key + X > Device Manager. Once there, expand Monitors. You will see the Surface's own monitor and the external display. You can try to unplug the external display to be certain which one it is. It should reappear when you reconnect the cable. Right-click on the external display's adapter and choose Uninstall device. When asked if you would like to remove the driver software, leave the box unticked then Uninstall. Then, restart the Surface with the external display connected and powered on. The Surface should be able to recognize the external display and reinitialize the driver installation.
