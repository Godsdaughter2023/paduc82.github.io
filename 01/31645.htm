Seems to me that this is a common post-XP scenario. The problem seems to be that the player volume controls aren't directly linked to the master volume control for the output device in the mixer. IOW, the speaker volume is attenuated separately from the output levels of the sound sources.

A workaround you can use in the Mixer is to crank up the speaker volume as you have and then pull down the volume levels on system sounds and whatever other sound sources that might play over your video sound track. Or you can Mute the other sound sources completely by clicking on the speaker icons below their volume sliders.

If the video volume is still low despite that then other factors are involved. The audio track could in fact be at a lower level, or there might be settings that need to be adjusted in the audio codec/renderer to raise the sound output level (eg, down-mixing multi-track audio to stereo, equalization, volume boost for certain audio formats etc.).
