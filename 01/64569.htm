hairyfool say:

That was only a suggestion, in digital terms there are no limits in the data stream. 20KHz is just a set of numbers as is 30 or 40. The actual limit is the sampling rate (nominally 44/48KHz) as the frequency can be no higher that and that in itself would be an incomplete waveform. The Digital to Analogue converter will have a limit as will the small amps in the chip and that will be lower than the sampling rate. Top end is probably 18-22KHz and the bottom 20-25Hz as a linear response. Top will shut off like cliff, bottom could go much lower (I had a class A that could do 0.2Hz) 

My point was that if Windows generated a frequency higher than the amplifier can cope with it would not know, there is no feedback. There are plenty of PC based signal generators using the sound card as the output but when you can hear nothing you don't know why, your ears, the speakers, the Amplifiers, the D to A converter all have their limits.
