lemp say:
The message "The network password needs to be 40bits or 104bits depending on your network configuration. This can be enteres as 5 or 13 ascii characters or 10 or 26 hexadecimal character" means that the encryption system being used is WEP (rather than WPA or WPA2).  Although this is the weakest wireless security available, now that I think about it, I'm a bit surprised that the mifi comes with ANY wireless security enabled.

There are at least 3 different "passwords" that are potentially used by your device.

1.  There may be a password that permits the device to connect to the cellular service.  As near as I can tell from the manual, this is a "PIN" code which may or may not be required by your cellular carrier (i.e., Verizon).  See page 15.

2.  There is a password that gets you access to the mifi's configuration utility.  This is the password that is supposed to be printed on the back of the mifi AND displayed in the LCD screen when the mifi is powered on.

3.  There may be a wireless encryption password (or "network key") depending on whether the wireless link between the mifi and your computer is configured to use WEP, WPA, or WPA2.

The more I read the manual for this device, the more I am convinced that the default setting for wireless encryption is "None" and that the password printed on the back of the device is #2 above.  On the other hand, I can't imagine how it could have configured itself to have WEP encryption.

Here's my last suggestions.

1.  Reset the mifi to factory defaults.  Next to the jack where the power cord is connected is a small button.  With the device powered on, press and hold this button. The manual just says "press and hold."  I'd keep the button pressed for at least 20 seconds or until there is some indication on the screen that a reset has occurred. You'll need an unbent paperclip to depress the button. See http://support.verizonwireless.com/clc/devices/knowledge_base.html?id=50011#alt

2.  Make sure that the battery is fully charged.

3.  Turn it on and confirm that the Service (e.g., 4G LTE), connection ("up" and "down" arrows), and signal strength (bars) are lit:



4.  Wait about 10 seconds and then go to "View available wireless networks," find the one that corresponds to your mifi (double-check to make certain that you don't have a neighbor with a mifi with a similar SSID), and click Connect.  You should NOT be requested for a password.

5.  Open a web browser and enter 192.168.1.1 OR vz.hotspot in the address box and click GO.  You should see the following:



6.  Enter the password from the sticker on the back of the mifi in the box and click the "Login" button.

From here you can follow the manual to configure the wifi security settings.  Good luck.
