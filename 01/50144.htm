and as Dave mentioned, those few other programs that will only install in C:\ .

- A lot of software installs data folders in one of the three C:\Program folders by default as well, even when the main program is

installed on another Partition or HDD.

Make a new folder on your D:\ drive for your program (a precaution in case the program does not make one automatically).

that is related to audio, video, Photo, and 3D modelling.

personal data to D:\ anyway. Things like Video, music and hi-res photos can us a lot of drive space.

As with my Steam experience, I don't trust changing location of any of my User folders, so I just make a new folder on the other

drive and move my data there manually. I use a keyboard hot key or Start Menu shortcut for quick access to those 'custom' folders.
