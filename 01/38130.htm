What kind of failure are we talking about? Is it a physical problem (read/write arms broke off, flames appeared, mushroom cloud) or is it a logical problem (the drive works fine but you can't do anything with it?)

In general, once a computer has been activated on Windows 10, it remains activated for life. (Changing the motherboard is an exception.) That being the case, you don't have to re-install Windows 7 and then upgrade to Windows 10. You can install Windows 10 directly. Just get yourself a new hard drive and do a clean install of Windows 10.

I asked about what kind of failure because as long as the drive physically operates it can usually be fixed, and if it can't be fixed you can usually get your personal files back even so.

elbab say: Hi there!

If you're using Windows 10 and 8.1, there are ways to block Skype from using your Camera.
On Windows 10:
Go to Start, then select Settings > Privacy > Camera.
Choose your preferred setting for Let apps use my camera hardware.
Under Choose apps that can use your camera, turn off for Skype.
On Windows 8.1:
Swipe in from the right edge of the screen, tap Settings, and then tap Change PC settings.
(If you're using a mouse, point to the lower-right corner of the screen, move the mouse pointer up, click Settings, and then click Change PC settings.)
Select Privacy > Webcam.
Set Let apps use my webcam to Off or turn it off for Skype.
If you just need to stop Skype from using your camera, you can just quit the application by stopping processes from Task Manager.
