f.      Click Change connection settings.

g.      Click theSecurity tab, and then, underSecurity type, clickShared.

h.      ClickOK, and then clickClose.

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows-vista/Set-up-a-security-key-for-a-wireless-network

c.     Change the startup type to Automatic

d.      Click onStart button

e.     Click on Apply,Ok.

f.      Restart the computer and verify if it works.

guji say:

Ok I think i found a solution to this by using NVSBIND.

1. Using nvsbind.exe /n i got the list of network cards on my laptop, found the one I wanted at the top of my binding and made a note of the name

2. Using nvsbind.exe /++ "Adapter Name" ms_tcpip command - this placed the adapter at the very top of the binding list.

3. Ipconfig /release and /renew

4. Everything working fine as I wanted.
