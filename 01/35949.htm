malkeleah say: Most antispyware programs and antivirus programs (separate things) work with each other. You don't want too many security programs installed or you will have a problem.

On Vista, you want:

1. A good antivirus. I recommend NOD32 (commercial) or if you want a free program, Avast, Avira, or Microsoft Security Essentials. I don't care for "security suites".

2. You already have a real-time antispyware program that runs resident - Windows Defender. Along with that, I like to install the free version of MalwareBytes' Anti-Malware and update/run it manually once a week or so.

3. The built-in Windows Firewall is fine. In my experience with clients' machines, third-party firewalls cause more trouble than they are worth. You only want to have one firewall running.
