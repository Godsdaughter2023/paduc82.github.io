john sieber say:

I was having the same problem and I tried all the steps suggested, but nothing worked.  But, then I thought of a setting I had made before and it fixed my problem.

On the Homegroup page, there is a link to "Change advanced sharing settings".  If you click that link, there is an option called "HomeGroup Connections".  I have it set on "Use user accounts and passwords to connect to other computers".  When this option is selected, then the "install printer" button doesn't work.  As soon as I selected the other option "Allow Windows to manage homegroup connections" and I performed the required log out and then log back in operation, the "install printer" button worked flawlessly.

Of course, after I verified the printer now worked correctly, I changed back to my preferred option to "Use user accounts and passwords to connect to other computers" and then after a reboot the printer shows offline.  So, I guess you can't use a "Homegroup" shared printer unless you use the "Allow Windows to manage homegroup connections" setting.

Hope this helped.
