[quote]Made by Miss Portia

I am trying to watch Netflix and I get errors R8157-4001 or R8157-6034. What does this mean? 
These specific error messages refer to an HDCP issue that exists between your console and your TV or Monitor.



What exactly is HDCP? 
HDCP stands for High-bandwidth Digital Content Protection, which is a secure link that allows your console to display copyrighted content such as movies in high definition. When used with the Xbox 360 console, this protocol requires 2 things: 

  • A TV or Monitor that supports HDCP
  • A cable that supports the HDCP protocol, such as an HDMI cable.

Okay, I'm getting one of those errors. How should I fix it?
HDCP errors such as R8157-4001 or R8157-6034 mean that the secure link between the console and your TV or Monitor has been lost or was not established. Anything that can disrupt this secure link can be at fault. To resolve this error, try following these steps in order: 
  1. Try a direct connection between your console and your TV or Monitor. Many users utilize an entertainment system or surround sound receiver along with their consoles. Some of these devices have HDMI inputs and outputs, simplifying  setup. However, multiple connections in between your console and TV or Monitor may interrupt the HDCP communications link. Try just connecting your console directly to your display device to see if the issue resolves. 
  2. Refrain from switching your TV's inputs when the Netflix app is running. Switching inputs while the Netflix app is running may disrupt the HDCP connection. Try to avoid switching your TV or Monitor inputs in order to prevent any issues with HDCP.
  3. Try another HDMI Cable. Some cables may not be functioning correctly, even though they appear to have no visible display issues. Trying another cable may resolve this issue.
  4. Try another input on your TV or Monitor.  In certain cases, input ports that are failing may show HDCP errors before completely becoming unusable. 
  5. Check to see if your TV or Monitor is HDCP compatible. While most HDTVs and some computer Monitors are HDCP compliant, many do not explicitly support this technology. Check with the manufacturer of your display device to see if it specifically supports HDCP. If it does not, you would need to acquire another HDTV or Monitor to view protected content. 
  6. Try using a Component cable instead of HDMI. Component cables do not use HDCP protection, so this should allow you to play back content on your TV or Monitor. However, not all display devices have these inputs. Please check your particular display device before purchasing another cable.[/quote]
