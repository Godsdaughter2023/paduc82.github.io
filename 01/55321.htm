Typically, this means that the pen or the pen cap is not receiving any power from the battery. We suggest that you follow the troubleshooting steps provided below to get it resolved:

  1. Hold the pen cap down for one second, then release.
    • Clicking the pen cap once wakes the pen from sleep.
  2. Verify that the pen’s LED light is coming on.
    • Press and hold the pen cap for 3-7 seconds.
  3. Reseat the AAAA battery in the pen.
    • Follow the instructions for how to open and close the pen.
  4. Test inking in a known good app
    • Use OneNote or Fresh Paint.
  5. Replace the AAAA battery.
    1. Holding the pen in your left hand, tip the pen towards the ground slowly to allow the AAAA battery to slide out of the pen. Only a very slight angle is required for the battery to come out.
    2. Check the pen shaft for damage or debris.
    3. Place the same (reseat) or a new (replace) battery back in the pen shaft. The AAAA battery should be placed flat side (-) down, towards the pen tip.

Should you need further assistance, feel free to get back to us.
