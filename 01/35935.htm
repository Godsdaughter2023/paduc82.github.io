lemp say:

If Windows was pre-installed on the computer by the computer's manufacturer, your product key is on the COA sticker.  On laptops, that will be on the bottom of the laptop or perhaps inside the battery compartment / under the battery).  On desktop/tower computers, it will probably be on one side, but look at all sides of the case.  The COA sticker looks like:

If you bought a retail copy of Windows and installed it on the computer, the product key is in the packaging that came with the DVD.  If you bought Windows from a legitimate online source, the product key may be in an email.  For retail and OEM System Builder installations only (e.g., not for pre-installed versions, MSDN versions, Volume Licensed versions), you can retrieve the product key if Windows is working by using a utility such as Nirsoft's ProduKey (http://nirsoft.net/utils/product_cd_key_viewer.html) or Belarc Advisor (https://www.belarc.com/products_belarc_advisor).
