maurice n say:

You need the retail-boxed Windows 8 DVD if you are going from a 32-bit to 64-bit.

The online upgrade will not result in getting from the systems you have (32-bit) to where you want to get (64-bit).

ALSO, you will need to do a Custom install followed by re-installing all your applications.

If you want to move from a 32-bit version of Windows to a 64-bit version of Windows  or vice versa, you'll need to back up your files and choose the Custom option during Windows  installation. Then, you'll need to restore your files and reinstall your programs

 

IF you are building a new system with no previous installed Windows, you need to get the Windows8 System Builder version.  That's typically at a large local computer store and at Newegg, TigerDirect, etc.

maurice n say:

Have you done backups to offline media ?  {external drive, DVD, CD, or even cloud-based backup service}

Have you done mirror-image backups ?

Backups are intended & used to restore a system in case of disaster.

 

btw, on 1 of the issues you mentioned before.....  IF you are buying the WIN8 Upgrade, you must do & buy for each of your systems separately.

The bottom line is 1 buy = 1 license = 1 activation on one system.

You cannot buy multiple licenses in one single pass.

And you need a separate license/product key for each of your systems.

{One assumes you are not a commercial Volume license customer}.

maurice n say:

BTW, you need to insure that each of your systems meet (or exceed) the minimum hardware requirements for Windows 8:

  • Processor: 1 gigahertz (GHz) or faster with support for PAE, NX, and SSE2 (more info)
  • RAM: 1 gigabyte (GB) (32-bit) or 2 GB (64-bit)
  • Hard disk space: 16 GB (32-bit) or 20 GB (64-bit)
  • Graphics card: Microsoft DirectX 9 graphics device with WDDM driver

     

    You can & should run the Upgrade Assistant on each system to see if it qualifies PLUS as well what it shows for non-compatible programs & drivers.

    Depending on the age of your XP hardware, you could be looking at lots of incompatibilities.
