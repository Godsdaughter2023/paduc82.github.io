palcouk say:

3 Q: What operating system supports SpeedStep™ technology?

A: Windows 95, Windows 98, Windows ME, Windows NT4, Windows 2000 and Windows XP all support it. With the exception of Windows XP, you will need to load a driver in order to use SpeedStep.

To determine if the driver is loaded, first look in the notification area in Windows taskbar. You should see icon of a flag. If not, go into the power applet of the Control Panel and see if there is a tab for Intel SpeedStep technology. If the icons of the flag do not show in either place, the driver is not running and it is probably not loaded.Note: Since Windows XP has the Intel SpeedStep technology driver built into Windows, you will not see an icon on the taskbar and you will not see a tab for Intel SpeedStep technology using the power applet located in the Control Panel.

You should be able to adjust the CPU speed by changing the Processor Power Management setting in the active Power Plan.

You should only need one playthrough of the main story if you follow this guide, although some achievements may require you to return to some of the levels if you were unable to complete them.

You must hide from the T-Rex on rooftops and in buildings and try to shoot the lasers mounted on his head - all while dealing with a small army of Bertrams - because if he sees you, he will activate those lasers. If he catches you on the ground, you may quickly be trampled. BEWARE - if you die here, the T-Rex's damage meter refills. This phase can be frustrating.

Phase 2: Destroy the rocket launchers on the T-Rex. This phase seems much easier than the first, especially when using the Rail Gun from a distance. The rockets should be fairly easy to avoid.

Phase 3: Destroy the cars that the T-Rex picks up in his mouth before he throws them at you. This seems to be the easiest phase.  Make sure to die once in Phase 3 to unlock:

Ugly Tastes Terrible

Multiplayer


This is merely a suggestion on how to set up the multiplayer matches for the various achievements. You can use any character you want to in any mode and if boosting these alone, you can just kill yourself to switch characters - except in Multiverse Madness - and get more achievements in a single game mode. Of course, if you have others to play with, just screw around and you're bound to get most of these along the way. With that said I will write this out assuming it will be just you and an extra controller and setting the match options to the minimum for time.


Deathmatch

This is for 2-4 players in teams or a free-for-all. 


Choose Meg for both players. When the match starts you get:

Mr. Unpopular


Melee kill Player 2 and unlock:

Shut up Meg


Melee kill player 2 two more times for:

Roadhouse


Continue to kill player 2 and you will eventually get:

The Cleaner

Kings of Quahog


Capture the Greased-Up Deaf Guy

This mode is basically capture the flag, with a twist. The flag is, obviously, the greased-up deaf guy. You have to defeat him to pick up a flag, and then return the flag to your vortex base. If you play this as Quagmire, you can go kill player 2, then taunt him to unlock:

Teabag a D-Bag

Once you capture the rest of the flags to finish the match you will unlock:

Grease Can't Save You Now

Lockdown


Multiverse Madness

This mode plays like Firefight in Halo or Horde in Gears of War. You must defeat waves of enemies from across the multiverse. The only catch? You just get one life. If you die, it's game over and you must start from wave one again. This can be done in co-op, but only 2 can play this mode. You must survive all waves required to unlock the associated achievement if playing in co-op mode; even if your partner survives, if you die, you won't get the achievement.

Multiverse Madness mode is similar to Firefight mode in Halo or Horde mode in Gears of War. What worked for me was to play on the Space Station map and stick to the starting area (in the room near the Joe special item with the health pack special item in it). this way I could force the enemy waves to funnel themselves to me through the room's only entrance. I played as Quagmire and once I had enough cash I purchased the Acid Sprayer and watched my enemies melt away. I also purchased Land Mines and placed them between waves. If you have not purchased Quagmire, or prefer not to use him, you can play as Stewie and use his Magma Gun for a similar effect. It has been suggested that by quickly entering and exiting the store portal (Press X then B), you can reload much faster. After 15 waves, if you don't already have it, you will get:

Bunch of Chumps

And once you have completed all 30 waves:

Cartoon Apocalypse

Teabaggin'

Challenge Mode


Challenge Mode features seven different challenge objectives, each based on one of the game's levels. Each challenge allows you to earn up to three stars. How you earn these stars will vary from challenge to challenge, some require you to choose a difficulty, while others simply require you to survive a certain period of time. There are also some bonus challenges that were pre-order bonus DLC content. I do not know if they will eventually become available to all, but have included what I know just for information sake as they do not count towards achievements as of this writing.

The first three challenges are immediately available for play.


Money Shots - Gather 

Remember collecting the raunchy sorority photos in "It's All Greek To Me"? Now it's your turn to take them. You can choose between Meg and Quagmire to do this. It may take you a couple of tries, but the best tip I can give you is to try and just work around either clockwise or counterclockwise. Don't skip houses or jump back and forth across the street. You must get to your car to complete the challenge. 

* Take 6 photos of girls before time is up 

** Take 8 photos of girls before time is up  

*** Take 10 photos of girls before time is up 


Amish Amuck - Elimination 

This is the easiest challenge, hands down. There is no time limit; you just have to kill the Amish without killing more than the specified amount of civilians. You can choose between Brian and Lois. I found that by using Brian and the shotgun, it made short work of the Amish, and reduced the amount of civilians you would shoot. 

* Kill 30 Amish with less than 6 tourists killed 

** Kill 40 Amish with less than 5 tourists killed 

*** Kill 50 Amish with less than 4 tourists killed 


The Crips and Ables - Rescue

Remember the pods you activated to rescue the Quahogians from in "Chickens in Space"? Well, the "Crips" have trapped several "Able" people in them in the hospital area from "Handicapable" and it's your job to rescue them within a set time limit. You can choose between Quagmire and Cleavland. All of the objectives are marked here, so do your best to get them all by working your way to each one. There is a fairly linear pattern you can follow from your first to your last, starting on the top floor from where you start and working your way down and across to the other side of the hospital. I found that doing this made things easier. 

* Rescue 4 people before time runs out 

** Rescue 5 people before time runs out 

*** Rescue 6 people before time runs out


Once you have earned at least 7 stars (you'll have up to 9 once you've done the above challenges), you will be able to try these next two:


Town Hall Beating - Wave Defense 

Based in the small "****" level, you must survives waves of enemies, much like in Multiverse Madness. You can play as Adam West, or Evil Adam West. Most people advise that you stay at the top of a flight of stairs and/or inside the ruined building where the ammo crate and portal store are located. In fact, the game seems to advise this as well. If you are good, you can run about outside, but you cannot find ammo or power up out in the field. Just stay in the three story building, where you have access to ammo and bonus items, and use the rifle to pick off enemies in the distance and the six shooters (or whatever weapon you're best with) for those that come close; the rifle seems to be very effective against the mechs. 

* Survive many enemies per wave 

** Survive many more enemies per wave 

*** Survive a (bleep) ton of enemies per wave 


Sea Men Attack - Wave Defense 

If you hated the cannon sequence in "Legend of Long John Peter", I'm sorry. This challenge, in which you play as either Peter or Death, requires you to survive for an unspecified amount of time in order to earn up to 3 stars. The longer you live, the more stars you earn. Death has a great melee attack, but you can use whomever you're best with.


Once you have earned at least 13 stars (you'll have up to 15 once you've done all of the challenges above), you will have access to the final two challenges.


Last Minute Shopper - Escape 

After picking up the first bomb, go back to your spawn and jump off the railing, just like when you had to collect the Rupert in "Santa's Sweatshop". This makes the going easier. Then just keep going down and run out the front when you have the last bomb. Don't worry about the enemies too much, especially the ones that explode, and try to focus on running and picking up the bombs as quickly as possible. 

* Collect all of the bombs with a 30 second time bonus per bomb 

** Collect all of the bombs with a 20 second time bonus per bomb 

*** Collect all of the bombs with a 15 second time bonus per bomb 7. 


Someone to Overwatch Me - Overwatch

This challenge is co-op required. You can do it by yourself with two controllers if you don't have someone to play with. It should not be too difficult to plant the bombs and defend yourself with Lois's needle gun. Watch out for the snipers but, you can take them out pretty easily. The tricky part is making the jump from the crane to the second building, this is what makes this challenge a pain in the ass. The jump is very far and hard to pin down (just like the above mentioned Rupert). It has been suggested that to make the jump, you must stand at the left corner of the crane and jump, making sure to time it carefully, and you should make it. Plant the last four bombs and jump into the helicopter. You may not have to touch the second controller at all ("To the Peter-Copter!") except, perhaps, to take out some snipers. 

* Set 4 charges without either character dying

** Set 6 charges without either character dying 

*** Set 8 charges without either character dying


After getting your first 7 stars, you will unlock

Little Dipper


When you complete all 7 challenges and earn all 21 stars, you will unlock:

Big Dipper


Bonus Challenge


Remember that the bonus challenge does not seem to increase your star total, and thus does not count towards the above achievements. I am including the bonus challenge merely for fun. 


The Great Eggscape - Escape 

(Pre-Order bonus available in the US via GameStop and UK/Ireland via GAME and Xtravision)

In this challenge, set in the "Chickens in Space" level, you must go from room to room in a linear fashion, clearing each room by killing all chickens and robot chickens in the room before moving on to the next. You finish by getting to Quagmire after clearing the final room. You have a time limit and must avoid killing too many of the civilians, who foolishly run into your line of fire at times, especially on the 3 star difficulty. You can destroy eggs from a distance to prevent them from hatching and can perform "mercy killings" by shooting the people strung up, which prevents chickens from bursting from their chests. You choose between Stewie and Chris. Personally, I like Chris and his primary weapon, the pulse rifle. Don't rush and try to get headshots on the robot chickens and you should do fine.

* Clear all 6 rooms in 6:00 and kill less than 8 civilians 

** Clear all 6 rooms in 5:35 and kill less than 6 civilians

*** Clear all 6 rooms in 5:05 and kill less than 4 civilians
