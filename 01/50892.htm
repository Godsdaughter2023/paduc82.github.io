a. user say:

Hello Chris,

Welcome to Microsoft Community. I will certainly help you with the issue that you are facing. This usually occurs then the drives are not formatted or partitioned.

If you connect a new hard drive to your PC, it shows the hard drive space as unallocated space. You need to partition and/or format the drive. Once the drive is formatted in NTFS disk format, it will be listed in the This PC window. To do so, you may follow the steps below:

  1. Press Windows + R on keyboard when you are on the desktop screen.
  2. Type "diskmgmt.msc" and click on Ok.
  3. Right-click an unallocated region on your hard disk, and then click New Simple Volume.
  4. In the New Simple Volume Wizard, click Next.
  5. Type the size of the volume you want to create in megabytes (MB) or accept the maximum default size, and then click Next.
  6. Accept the default drive letter or choose a different drive letter to identify the partition, and then click Next.
  7. Follow the prompts and click Finish.

Note: When you create new partitions on a basic disk, the first three will be formatted as primary partitions. Beginning with the fourth, each one will be configured as a logical drive within an extended partition.

Note: While formatting the partition, all the data will be deleted and is unrecoverable. Make sure there is no data on the hard drive or you have the backup for the data.

Hope this information was helpful. Let us know the results once you have tried these steps.

found the hack:
