    Windows 7 USB Drive with RAW partition. - Microsoft Community

    trey noe say:

    Windows 7 USB Drive with RAW partition.

     I have a 1.5TB drive that has several partitions on it. How do I stop Windows from asking me every time I attach the drive whether I want to format the RAW partition? That partition holds a "copy" of a damaged disk that I am running a recovery tool on and I do not want it to be lost.
    sudarshan bhat m say:

    Hi,

    You can stop those prompts from coming up by removing the drive letter from the RAW partition that is in question. But, before doing that, you will have to make sure if the RAW partition needs a drive letter in order for you to carry on with the recovery process you are running on it. If the recovery process needs a drive letter associated with the RAW partition, I would suggest you to just cancel those prompts until the recovery is completed.

    If removing the drive letter for the RAW partition does not affect the recovery process you are running, then here are the steps to do that :


    1. Open Computer Management by clicking the Start button, clicking Control Panel, clicking System and Maintenance, clicking Administrative Tools, and then double-clicking Computer Management. If you are prompted for an administrator password or confirmation, type the password or provide confirmation.

    2. In the Navigation pane, under Storage, click Disk Management.

    3. Select the RAW partition you are referring to, right click on it, and choose the Change drive letter and Paths item.

    4. Select the drive letter and click Remove.

    Let us know if it helps.
