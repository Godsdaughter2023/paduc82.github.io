    matt h - say:

    Hi 2_tone,

    I think Abhe has you on the right path.

    Cleaning out unwanted or unnecessary programs from msconfig is always a good start to help with boot speed.

    Far too many programs “think” they ought to be loaded on startup, thus clogging the computer.

    Also check your startup folder for unwanted entries there.

    I would also clean out your temp folders and prefetch folder, especially if you have been uninstalling programs.

    Defrag may help.

    Also, run chkdsk. Start->Search bar->type cmd, enter and chkdsk.

    Note: This only checks for bad data you’ll have to run a chkdsk /F and reboot to fix any problems found.
