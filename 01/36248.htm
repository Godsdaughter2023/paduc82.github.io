¡firedog say:

When a mail client downloads new messages using POP3, there is a fail-safe mechanism that will re-start the download if it doesn't complete for some reason. There is most probably a corrupt message that can't be downloaded from the server, so the download never completes.

Log in to your account at Charter webmail. Clear out the junk mail and deleted items folders, then create a new folder and move the newest half of the messages in the inbox that haven't yet been downloaded into the new folder. Back in WLMail, use send/receive again. If it succeeds, move the oldest half of the messages in the temporary folder back into the inbox and send/receive in WLMail again. Repeat this process, halving the number of messages moved each time, until you can identify the message(s) causing the download to stall. Deal with them in webmail.

naiver say:

Library Files Archive Bit reset by wmpshare.exe

I've been trying to figure out why my differential backup size reverts to nearly full backup size without updating or running a full backup on the affected files. I found that this only affects library files and seems to be due to wmpshare.exe resetting the archive bit on these files when windows media center or windows media player has been running. My media libraries are very large and this is causing me problems with maintaining a reasonable backup routine. Can anything be done to prevent wmpshare from resetting the archive bit?

praseetha k say:

Hi,

You may try the following steps and check if it helps.

Step 1:

 You may also refer the following link and try the update and check if it helps:

Cumulative update for Windows Media Player 11 in Windows Vista

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/941651

 

Step 2:

You may try running the command from a command prompt at the root of the volume type.

"attrib -a /s"

a. Click the Start button. In the Search box, type command prompt.

b. In the list of results, right-click Command Prompt, and then click Run as administrator. If you are prompted for an administrator password or confirmation, type the password or provide confirmation.

c. At the Command Prompt window, type "attrib -a /s" drive:\<path>\ and Press Enter.

This will remove the Archive file attribute from all files and sub files.

Hope this information is helpful.

Disney-Pixar Brave: The Video Game achievement walkthrough - Microsoft Community

hyrulebalverine say:

Disney-Pixar Brave: The Video Game achievement walkthrough

Disney-Pixar Brave

The game is rather linear and while maybe not technically on-rails, your route is predetermined, except for a few short side roads to coins and chest, so I will only point out where you can/will get achievements and where the collectible items are, as well as tips on defeating the guardians. Keep in mind that the collectibles are essentially cosmetic. While most of them offer upgrades, you can choose to use whichever you want as the upgrades are to the characters not the specific items. So, for instance, regardless which sword you use, you’ll have the highest upgraded sword ability earned.

Be mindful of your surroundings, you can walk off of ledges and platforms floating in water often sink under your weight. You can also find in a number of fights, a higher ground that your enemies can’t get to because they can’t jump, or other areas where the enemies won’t or can’t go. Use this to your advantage and kill them with your bow / charged shot.

There are several achievements that require you to kill 100 and 250 enemies with the different Charms. The same is required of kills with Power Shot and Charged Shot. These two require you to purchase their respective abilities from the Upgrade shop. Depending on your play style, these could come naturally as you play through the game, or you might want to consider switching up your chosen Charm as you play, so that you don’t have to grind these out later. But, as you’ll be grinding out coins for upgrades later, it’s not a huge issue.

Some other achievement will come naturally as you play as well: Minimum Wage, Affluent, and Wealthy will all pop as you play through the levels and grind you way to purchasing all of the upgrades. “Mum’s the word” will also pop as you progress through the game and grind coins. Depending on your play style and upgrade choices, “All bunched up” should come naturally as you progress as well (on both my normal playthrough and again when working on the walkthough, I got this in the second level).

For your upgrades, I recommend focusing on the “Charged shot: earth” upgrades and the “Charged shot” upgrade that decreases the time it takes to charge charged shots. You will probably also want to upgrade your “Health potions”. You will want to purchase your “Charged shot: fire” and upgrade it at least once before you get to the 6###sup/sup### level, as you’ll need it there. You will want to purchase the dodge ability by the time you start the final level as there is a chest you cannot get without it on that level. You will eventually want to purchase and upgrade the “Power attack” but that just depends on when you want to work on this. Do not waste coins on upgrading the Wisp until you’ve fully upgraded Merida, as the Wisp is only a coop character and these upgrades do not help Merida if you’re playing single player.

You really have to options on how to play this: 1) Play on whatever difficulty you want, breezing through to get a bunch of the achievements really quickly, but then playing again to get “Gaelic hero” by playing the whole game on Brave difficulty, or 2) just play on Brave difficulty and switch the difficulty afterwards for grinding; for the most part, it’s not that difficult of a game, especially when using Charged Shot - Earth and upgrading the minions released along with the time it takes to charge it. If, at any time, you choose to quit a level and go back to the ring of stones, you will have to start that level over, so be aware of this when choosing upgrades and deciding stopping points.

Prologue

This is just the tutorial; it will walk you through movement, jumping, weapons, targets, pressure plates, and other game mechanics. You’ll also choose which one of the two starting outfits (1/5 and 2/5) you’re going to wear. Follow along the path and you will get

·         Swordful

·         Bowsome

Once the prologue ends, you’ll be taken to Ring of Stones (which is the level select hub). Follow the Will-o-the-wisps to the witch’s hut (which will later become a “Merchant” where you can purchase upgrades) and after the cut scene you will get

·         Digging in the dirt

Go back to the Ring of Stones and defeat the wolves to open the first level and get

·         Dirty work

1.       Forest Grove

This will be your first chance to get “Ranged Only” though I found level 3 to be a good spot to work on this. Of course, you can always get this later by replaying any level. If you’re playing the “Brave” difficulty, you’ll probably want to use your sword to collect as many coins as possible and then replay a level once you’ve upgraded a little bit. I did use Elinor’s paw swipe once during the bear fight, so this attack may not count against the achievement.

If you manage to not get hit for a long stretch in this level, you will also get “Impressive” and “Untouchable” here. If not, don’t worry, you’ll easily get these later.

Shortly after the level begins, just after the platform that you create by hitting the Earth Charm symbol, you’ll see your first chest. This contains your first Tapestry piece (1/18) getting you

·         Carpet runner

Soon you will come to an area covered in a purple miasma (or “Blight” as the game will call it). When you get close enough, this triggers an enemy spawn fight. You’ll often encounter these blight sections throughout the game. Once you’ve defeated all of the enemies, the blight will clear and flowers will grow around the Waystone, signifying that the battle is over.

You can access the merchant here (as you’ll be able to do for each of these during the game). Access the merchant and purchase your first upgrade to get

·         Ante Up

The other two will come as you earn/grind coins throughout the game and level replays.

·         Full of power

·         Gotta snag ‘em all

Once you try to pass through the opening, the portcullis will close and after the cut scene, you’ll begin your first “Triplet Puzzle”, which is rather easy (in fact most are not that complex).

When you get to the swamp area, make sure to use your bow to shoot the pods as they will explode if you get to close to them. After the first lip line in the swamp, jump up the small rise to the right, making sure to shoot the pod. Next to the pod is a chest containing Tapestry piece 2/18.

During the bear sequence is your first chance to get “Get out of my way,” but if you don’t get it here, you’ll have plenty of chances to get it later.

In the castle, you’ll find the Fire Charm

·         Toasty

2.       Coastal Cliffs

Just follow the path. Ignore the first chest you see; you can’t get it for now, but will come back later in the level. When you get to the fight at the Waystone under the blight, you’ll want to run back to the bridge as you fight, as the enemies won’t cross it. They’ll actually give up and go back once you reach a certain point. Rinse and repeat until you’ve defeated them all, and then return to defeat the rock golem and clear the blight.

When you go inside the castle/keep, you’ll cross a draw bridge into a blight. Immediately, once the fighting starts, back onto the drawbridge. If you didn’t take too long or walk too far, it will still be there and the enemies won’t go onto it. You may still have to jump over projectiles from the Rock golem, but you won’t have to for long.

When you get to the part where the rock golem is across the water from you (you’ll have to shoot a Wind Charm emblem to cross over to him), you’ll notice that there are targets behind him (you can simply shoot him from a distance with the Charged Shot earth), after killing him, shoot those targets for a chest with Tapestry piece 5/18.

Once you reach the area with the blight, there will be a lot exploding minions appearing from stone rings that spawn them. If you don’t get it by the end of your play through, this is probably the best place in the game to come back for it later when you’ve upgraded.

3.       Chilled Creek

After the first blight / waystone, you’ll enter a cave. Shortly after you begin through the cave, you’ll see some rocks with purple spots on them. Attack these to break them and reveal a chest with Tapestry 7/18. Shortly after that, you’ll encounter another blighted area. After defeating the enemies, you’ll get the final charm, The Ice Charm, unlocking

·         Ice to see you

This is a good level to work on “Ranged only” if you didn’t do so already, because all of the destructible items can be destroyed with the bow. There is nothing that requires the sword here.

You’ll reach an area where you have to shoot moving ice in the water with the Ice Charm equipped to continue. As you do so, keep heading up (toward the top of the screen) and you’ll reach an area with a sigil on the ground and a sealed cave in front of you. Walk over the sigils in a clockwise fashion starting with the one in the 9 o’clock position (you’ll need to climb the platform/rocks) and you’ll open the cave, revealing a chest with Sword 3/7.

When you see the next Waystone, you’ll notice another, short, path leading to it from the right of the path you are on. You can turn back onto this path to reach the chest at the end, which has Tapestry piece 8/18. You probably already saw this chest as you took the path leading to the draw bridge and the Waystone.

After you “solve” the “puzzle” requiring you to simply stand on two pressure plates, you’ll enter a small keep so that you can obtain a keystone which will allow you to progress through the level. During the Elinor section on this level, I simply hit “B” as soon as I gained control and hit 5 enemies for “Get out of my way”.

When you reach the next waystone, you’ll see the distinctive glow of a chest above it on the screen. You’ll need to follow the Ice Charm platforms to the little rock island about it and shoot the Wind Charm emblem to reach it and Bow 3/7.

Then, use the Launchpad to get to one of the most difficult boss battles of the game. When you get to the next Launchpad, which takes you right to the giant rock golem (The rock golem guardian), you’ll want to have your Earth Charm selected, so that you can attack with your little minions. Do your best to avoid the boars and focus on the floating rings so that they stop spawning boars. Don’t try to dodge the rocks that the troll throws; instead jump up when they land to avoid the blast radius. Don’t get too close to the troll, either, as he’ll stomp you. ·         Brilliant

After the triplet puzzle, you’ll be in an area with a gate in front of you and two targets above it. When you get to the gate, you’ll see 4 sigils on the wall to the right; the third sigil will be lit up. Shoot the left target to activate the first sigil, the shoot the right target to move the “cursor” to the second sigil and shoot the left target again. Shoot the right target twice to select the final sigil, then shoot the left target to complete this puzzle and open the gate. Open the chest to get Bow 7/7.

·         Bowlicious

After this is a waypoint; if you haven’t purchased it already, purchase the dodge ability. You’ll need it for the final chest. Or, you can go after it later when you replay this level to grind coins. After you turn the next corner, you’ll see another gate in the distance. There will be a pressure plate which will lower a stone that opens the gate. However, boulders will roll down the stairs right at you (double jump over them) and the gate will lower quickly once you get off of the pressure plate. Once it’s up all of the way, repeatedly dodge to get into the gate before it lowers too far to let you in.  Open the final chest in the game for Outfit 5/5.

·         Clothes horse

You’ll face off against four Guardians in order to defeat Mor’du. Luckily, after you’ve defeated a guardian, the game gives you a checkpoint, so even if you die facing one, you won’t have to fight them all over again; you’ll just face off against the guardian that killed you. You can use the charged shots for the elements that the guardians are weak against, but I had an easier time just using Charged Shot Earth against them all. Each one will take you a few tries as you fight and try to avoid the minions that they spawn as you attempt to destroy the guardians themselves. They should be the real focus of your attacks; only kill the other enemies when necessary to keep yourself alive. After you defeat a guardian, a pillar will appear in front of you. Destroy it to proceed to the next guardian.

The first guardian is the Ice Wolf guardian. He is weak against the Fire Charm. You can use this, if you’ve upgraded the Charged shot: fire enough, or use the fully upgraded Charged Shot: earth.

Next is the Fire Boar guardian, who is weak against ice. It’s been suggested that, when fighting the Fire Boar guardian, you keep near the edge of the platform and just walk off before he can hit you with a charging run. I personally never had success with that tactic, despite the fall doing less damage than the Fire Boar guardian (mostly because I just seemed to respawn right where he was and got attacked by him anyways), but if this works for you, use it. This is probably the most difficult of the four guardians.

The third guardian is the Harpy guardian, which we faced earlier. Just use the same tactics that you used the first time you fought her, and be careful because the ground has been split apart.

The final guardian is our favorite pain: the Rock Golem guardian. The broken ground has now been separated; work your way up to the Rock Golem guardian as quickly as you can and finish him off.

Once you’ve defeated him and destroyed the pillar, Mor’du will be defeated.

Now, it’s time to grind coins and kills. Go into the difficulty and change the game to Easy. Then replay the last level up to the check point after you play as Elinor. You’ll have to replay this level several times, but it won’t take too long.
