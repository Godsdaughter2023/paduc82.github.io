how do you recover compacted emails in outlook express?

steve cochran say:

All that is supposed to do is to remove wasted space, but it notoriously wipes out messages in folders sometimes.  The first thing to do is to check your Recycle bin and message store for bak files that are backups of the dbx files that you can restore.  See www.oehelp.com/OETips.aspx#2 and www.oehelp.com/backup.aspx#wm .  If there are no bak files, or if restoring them does not recover the lost messages, then your only shot is DBXpress (www.oehelp.com/DBXpress/) which can extract the messages from the files or, when the files are empty of messages, can recover them from the hard drive directly, if they are still on it.

In the future, its important to backup your messages frequently, as this problem tends to happen a lot.

wulfgerdts say:

Ok, so do it the following way:

 First took out anything from the USB port. Later, it’s easier to find the right mass storage. Then  load down the drivers from the site (Surface Pro 3-Site!)

https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/download/confirmation.aspx?id=38826

When you click Download, you can choose which files. You only need
SurfacePro3_Win10_151026_2.zip
Download it, it is 211,3 MB.
Unzip the file. Under drivers / System, you find the folder CardReader.
Don’t forget where to find the unzipped folder CardReader.

Now open Device Manager by right-clicking the Start Button and left clicking Device Manager.
The culprit is the USB Controller. Double click on it, and it shows you some things and the item mass storage. That is the misunderstand Cardreader. Right-click on it, click on Actualize drivers (I have the German version. Terms might differ a little bit), Click on Search on the PC, in the next window, put in the right path to card reader, check Subfolders included, click continue – and you should hear a pling, and the card reader is recognized as a card reader, and the micro sdxc as an sdxc-card.

I confess, I did it a little different way: from my own Surface Pro 3, I copied the folder
rtsuerxdisablemmc.inf_amd64_6e6fa2460297ed78
to my new Surface Pro 4, then opened the device manager and used the path to this folder. It worked at once.
Only difference between CardReader folder and rtsuerx… folder is that the latter contains the file rtsuerxdisablemmc.PNF. I hope it will work for you without – or you or a friend of yours own a Surface Pro 3, and you can get it from there.
I’m a little bit short of time, so I could not test the card reader folder on my own. But I think it should work. If not, just tell me.

Good luck and good success from Germany!
