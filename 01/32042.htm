silviut. say:

It took me forever to figure out what was wrong.

Before I installed Win 10 on the current SSD, I had another 180 GB SSD on which I had Windows 8.1

My Orochi was paired on that installation and apparently it has some sort of memory that keeps track of what machine is paired with.

So what happened was, because my Bluetooth MAC was the same, the Orochi was trying to pair with settings that were applicable to my previous installation of Windows 8.1 . Which in turn confused my new Windows 10 installation and made the pairing impossible.

So I replaced my SSD with the old one, booted into Windows, unpaired my Orochi there.

Put my new SSD back in, attempted to pair and what do you know... this time it worked.

Crazy if you ask me. Scroll is working fine now btw.
