lemp say:
Get an external drive enclosure. 

Take the drive out of the old computer and put it in the external enclosure. After you copy the files, you can format the old drive and use it for backups.

When choosing an external enclosure, the two specs you need to keep in mind are:
  • internal interface -- SATA in your situation
  • drive size -- 2 1/2 inch if the old computer is a laptop and 3 1/2 inch if the old computer is a desktop/tower
Examples:

3 1/2 inch SATA enclosures --> http://www.newegg.com/Product/ProductList.aspx?Submit=ENE&N=100007705%20600006254%20600006269&IsNodeId=1&name=SATA


2 1/2 inch SATA enclosures --> http://www.newegg.com/Product/ProductList.aspx?Submit=ENE&N=100007705%20600006269%20600006255&IsNodeId=1&name=2.5%22


There are, of course, many variations.  Some enclosures will handle both 2 1/2 and 3 1/2 drives; some will have both SATA and IDE (that's the flat grey cable type) connectors; some will have external power supplies (generally a good idea) and some won't; etc.  


Generally, these external enclosures connect to your new computer via USB.  If your new computer has USB 3.0 ports, look for an enclosure with that capability in order to get the higher transfer speed.  If your new computer has an eSATA connection, you can look for an external enclosure with that sort of connection as well.


Newegg is a good place to look for user reviews, even if you decide to buy elsewhere.
