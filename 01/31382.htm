After trying just about every recommended solution out there to try and resolve the 0x80072EE7 error, I finally fixed the issue I was having by making changes to the registry permissions.

In particular, "HKLM\CurrentControlSet\Services\Tcpip\Parameters" seems to have had the incorrect permissions applied somehow. I just navigated one key up, to HKLM\CurrentControlSet\Services\Tcpip and had its permissions apply down to its children.

Steps:

  1. Open RegEdit.
  2. Navigate to Computer\HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Services\Tcpip
  3. Right click the key "Tcpip" and select "Permissions..."
  4. In the dialog, click "Advanced"
  5. Check the box "Replace all child object permission entries with inheritable permission entries from this object"
  6. Click OK, then Close the permissions dialog
  7. Run Windows Store, Edge, etc. to make sure the fix has succeeded.
