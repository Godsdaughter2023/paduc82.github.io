rur say:

Success! I had used a response to the "Keyset is not defined" solution to this problem and it worked. Basically, I booted the system in Safe Mode with Command Prompt. I seached Windows\System32 for either rsabase.dll or rsaenh.dll - I found rsaenh.dll and applied regsvr32.exe to that file, rebooted and it cleared the problem.

Exact text from the Microsoft entry is: "Use of Regsvr32.exe on CSP DLLs (Rsabase.dll or Rsaenh.dll) may fix the problem."
