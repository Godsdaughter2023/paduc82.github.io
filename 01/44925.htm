andre da costa say:
1. Reset Facial Recognition and/or Fingerprint Options

Press Windows key + I to open Settings.
Click Accounts and select Sign-In options.
Locate Fingerprint or Facial Recognition options and click Remove under them.
After that, click Get Started and follow the on-screen instructions to reset Fingerprint and Facial Recognition.
Reboot your PC if needed.

2. Modify Group Policy Settings

Creators Update might have modified some of the crucial settings of Group Policy. Therefore, we would recommend to launch the Group Policy Editor and check/edit the settings related to Windows Hello functionality.

Pres Windows key, type gpedit, and press Enter.
Click on Computer Configuration and open Administrative Templates.
Select Windows Components and open Biometrics.
Click on Facial Features and right-click on Configure enhanced anti-spoofing option.
Select Edit and disable the Configure enhanced anti-spoofing feature.
Reboot your PC.

3. Update drivers

If some of the system drivers, most probably webcam and fingerprint reader drivers, were corrupted during the upgrade process, Windows Hello may fail to perform. To fix that, try to update the corresponding drivers:

Right-click on Windows key and select Device Manager.
Locate the Hello, webcam, and fingerprint drivers individually and right-click on each of them.
Select Remove driver software and wait for the removal to be finished.
Repeat the removal with all Windows Hello related drivers and then reboot your PC.
Once done, open Device Manager, click Action -> Scan for hardware changes.
The system should automatically detect and reinstall the drivers.

lolmaniac621 say:

Hello,

Generally, this type of thing happens when a program conflict makes one or the other stop working, maybe even for just a few seconds. During those few seconds, Windows will pick it up and give you that popup. Here's a possible solution:

  1. Click the Start or Windows Orb/Button.
  2. In the search bar, type "system" (without the quotes)
  3. At the top of the search results, there will be an option for "System Configuration"
  4. A window willl pop up, with several tabs and options. Click on the "General" Tab
  5. Under that tab, there will be a selection for "selective startup". Click this.
  6. Then, two options will become clickable under that, de-select "Load Startup Items"
  7.  Reboot your computer. ! WARNING: Don't access the internet during this time as you will be vulnerable to viruses, spyware, and malware!
  8. Once it boots back up, launch Carbonite Backup, as no programs will be running.

The problem should be solved. If it is, turn on your antivirus program right away and enjoy your working computer. If not,

  1. Follow steps 1-5 above to back to System Configuration.
  2. Under selective startup, also de-select Load Services.
  3. Reboot the computer.
  4. Once it starts up, Launch Carbonite.

It should now be solved. If not, there is definitely a problem with either Carbonite, or with how Carbonite and Windows are working together, and you should contact them further.

 

For further assitance, if this does solve the problem, then slowly launch your normal programs one-by-one until the issue ocurrs again. You have now isolated the program conflict! Congratulations!
