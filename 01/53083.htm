mike hall... always say:

With 6gb of actual RAM, and probably only using half or less of it, you will see no improvement from ReadyBoost.

Press CTR + Shift + ESC to invoke Task Manager. Now click on the Performance tab. It will show total RAM installed, used RAM and cached RAM. What is the difference in numbers between total installed and total cached?

ReadyBoost was developed to help under-specified computers cope with Vista. All it does is give space to a fast cache, faster than a mechanical drive. It is no replacement for RAM, but as said, you have 6Gb already and it is doubtful that you ever get close to using half of it at any one time.

Why ReadyBoost was extended to 8 flash drives and 256Gb is beyond me. Most PCs only have six USB ports, two of which are used for mouse and keyboard, one for occasional use with digital cameras and flash drives used for saving and moving files around.

If I was you, I would forget about ReadyBoost.. :-)
