david vinod say:

Hi,

Welcome to Windows Vista answers community.

In Windows XP you can add the “Line-In” to the mixer. Windows Vista will try to detect the hardware installed on your computer and will not show additional options that your computer won’t take advantage of and therefore not showing “Line-In” as an option. You were right about Windows XP being able to add the “Line-In” control to the mixer, but if you if there is no “Line-In” port then the “Line-In” control in the mixer will not be usable as it doesn’t see the signal.

Note : A microphone level is a lot lower then a line level signal therefore your computer is using an amplifier to amplify the microphone signal to a line level signal. By trying to plug in a line level signal in to a microphone signal the amplifier will take the line level signal and will amplify it the same amount that it would amplify a microphone signal hence the distortion.

Here would be just some options that you have to accomplish recording Line level to a microphone input.

1. Contact Manufacture

  a. You may be able to contact your manufacture to see if they have developed a driver  that will convert the “MIC-In” to “Line-In”


2. Try to turn the source volume WAY down.

  a. Turning the source (mp3 player, stereo, CD player) of the signal all the way down.

  b. Turn off the microphone boost in the recording settings.

  c. Slowly turn up the volume of the source until you start to notice distortion, then turn  it down just enough to where the distortion is gone.

  d. If you hear the distortion as soon as you turn up the volume from the source then this method will not be possible.

3. Use an attenuating cable.

  a. This cable will turn a line level signal in to a microphone signal. (So when it is amplifier it is amplified to line level again)

  b. This cable will replace the cable that you are using now. (Connects from the source  “Line-Out” to recording device “MIC-In”)

  c. You can find this cable in most electronic stores.

4. Using a different sound card.

  a. You can buy a sound card for your laptop if you want more functionality then your laptop provides.

  b. You will want a sound card that has a Microphone Level Input as well as a Microphone Level Input.

  c. There are various different ways you can install sound cards on your laptop. Most common being through USB connection or through an Express Card.

  d. Once you install a sound card that has a Line Level Input the option for “Line-In” will become available in Windows Vista.

  E.This devices are usually sold in major electronic and computer stores.


Let us know if this helps
