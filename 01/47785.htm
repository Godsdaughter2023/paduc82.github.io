aj3 say:

Turned out to be icon confusion. (Not sure why a network doesn't work for all users on the PC after it's setup by an administrator. Not sure why each user must take the following steps. But glad it's working now.)

In the systray, the wireless connection icon and the wireless network adapter's icon look almost identical. (The latter is slightly larger, but both look like a bar chart, with bars of increasing height. Kind of like those AT&T commercials.)

Well, I'd only been checking the wireless connection (and all its options) because that's all that was visible. But, after seeing the previous reply, I looked further, and saw the wireless network adapter's icon in the "hidden icons" part of the systray.

There, I clearly saw that it wasn't connected.

Details of resolution: I right-clicked it, then choose Connection Wizard. That brought me to my wireless network adapter's proprietary wizard. (In my case, it's a D-Link adapter, so it's the D-Link software that I saw at this point.) I chose "manually connect", then scanned for my wireless network, found it, selected it, entered password, and now all's well.

Thanks very much.
