Windows can also download device software and info. This might include an app that the device manufacturer created to go with your device or info like the product name, manufacturer, and model number, to help you distinguish between similar devices.

Drivers are updated occasionally. Windows can update them automatically, or you can install the updated drivers yourself. Windows notifies you if it detects a suspicious or unsafe driver you shouldn't install.

Share apps between different users on the same laptop - Microsoft Community

originalgreekmags say:

Share apps between different users on the same laptop

How do I do this on Windows 8.1?

Not all users have (or - to my mind - need) microsoft accounts. How can I share a Windows Store app between a user with a microsoft account and one with a local account?
cwrightco say: I am equally frustrated with this issue.  I have spent the last week trying to get some of the apps into my children's local accounts.  I have gone through every step by step explanation and NOTHING works.  I have even tried temporarily switching my child's account to an administrator account, signing out of my microsoft account on my log in and signing into it on his account.  I hit a brick wall every time.  

Bingo!  I just figured it out!  It is a royal pain in the neck, but at least my kids will be able to use the apps.  

When I hit the store screen that tells me to log into my microsoft account, there was an option at the bottom of the page to install apps individually.  I hit that and am able to install the apps, one at a time, on my child's account.  

I also turned off all the family safety options on this child's account and changed his settings to standard user instead of child.  

Once I was able to install the already purchased apps on his account, I switched to another child's account.  On the second child, I kept his family safety checks on and kept his account at child, and it is all working.  (Troubles downloading the Kindle app, but I think it is a setting in the family safety.  I am just trying to figure out which one.)

I think it is ridiculous to have to go through each app and manually install it on every single account.  There should be an option to share the apps, just like sharing folders or pictures.  

From the store, if you go to the categories and click on "see all", it will open a box across the top.  Click on "your apps," and choose "apps not installed on this PC" to get the list of the apps that are on the pc, just under a different user. Still a pain, but at least you have a list and can install one at a time.

Hope this helps someone else.  I was at the point of trying to figure out how to ditch the Windows 8 for a good old 7.  (Hubby is a programmer and two adult children are computer guys and also were unable to figure it out.)
