After hours of being annoyed........   I find no other way. 

1. Homegroup Icon:
open my computer, control panel, system and security, administrative tools, services, then scroll down to: homeGroup listener and homeGroup provider, disable both. close out, log off. the icon should be gone when you log back on.

¡firedog say:

When you cut a slice from a web page, you are capturing only part of the HTML that makes it look the way it does. Font definitions, line heights and other text attributes may be somewhere else on the page, or even on a different page completely. You will rarely be able to copy and paste successfully like this.

To solve the problem you illustrated with your screenshot, click the Plain text button, then click it again. The first click will strip all of the HTML markup that's not helping, and the next click will apply your favourite font attributes. If there are images involved, copy and paste them separately after the second click on Plain text.

In Internet Explorer, you should also have the Send > Page by email (Alt-F E P) and Send > Link by email (Alt-F E L) options on the File menu. The last one is the most user- and world-friendly, but it maight work unexpectedly if the page you're sharing changes dynamically, like most news pages.
