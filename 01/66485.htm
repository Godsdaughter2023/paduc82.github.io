If you bought the game used, chances are the store you purchased it from most likely has buffered he disc to rid it of any minor scratches. When you buffer a disc you are pretty much taking away a small layer of the disc itself. Meaning if the disc was buffered many times before, it may seem to be brand new in appearance, but in actuality, it has been buffered so much that most of the important data needed for it to work has been buffered off.

Now if you have purchased Black Ops 2 brand new then i would say try to clear your system cache  System>system settings>storage>memory unit> press the Y button and go down and choose clear system cache. Repeat this 3 times. After this, if the game still does not seem to read i would recommend trying a different disc copy. Hope this Helps!
