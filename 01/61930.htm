tryx3 say:

The easiest way would be to put its HDD into an enclosure that connects to the new computer by USB.

Here is just one example - http://www.amazon.co.uk/Hard-Drive-Caddy-Case-Enclosure/dp/B003MVW2SM

You probably have a 2.5" HDD because all laptops I know of do.  So you need to buy an enclosure for a 2.5" drive [not a 3.5" drive which would be from a desktop computer].

Almost all enclosures derive the power to drive the HDD from the USB connection itself [rather than from an external power supply].  If you see one that needs an external power supply then you are probably looking at the wrong thing.

You need to find out if the HDD is IDE or SATA.  That depends on the age of the computer.  Your computer handbook should tell you or you can just whip the HDD out to have a look at it.

  • Here is an illustration of an IDE connection - http://www.amazon.co.uk/Generic-Toshiba-Fujitsu-Samsung-Hitachi/dp/B0042WZZD4/ref=pd_sim_147_4?ie=UTF8&dpID=21bYEH0CONL&dpSrc=sims&preST=_AC_UL160_SR160%2C160_&refRID=125C3XKTAFS54XACCCZ8 - if you look at the diagrams you will see that there are long rows of pins sticking out from the HDD - that is IDE.
  • Here are illustrations of a SATA connection - http://www.amazon.co.uk/Screw-drive-Enclosure-laptop-Laptop/dp/B003RIL0EM/ref=pd_sim_147_2?ie=UTF8&dpID=419CrJiBX9L&dpSrc=sims&preST=_AC_UL160_SR160%2C160_&refRID=125C3XKTAFS54XACCCZ8 and http://www.amazon.co.uk/drive-Enclosure-laptop-Caddy-Laptop/dp/B00926NM8K/ref=pd_sim_147_9?ie=UTF8&dpID=31sO4aOkykL&dpSrc=sims&preST=_AC_UL160_SR160%2C160_&refRID=125C3XKTAFS54XACCCZ8  - the diagrams show the connection inside the enclosure that the HDD connects into [so it's the reverse image of what sticks out from the HDD] - you can see that the connection is a long slot in two sections so the HDD has a long protrusion in two sections - that's SATA.

How do I burn and play a MP4 Movie Maker file produced on Windows 10 - Microsoft Community

bobhornby say:

How do I burn and play a MP4 Movie Maker file produced on Windows 10 on a standard video player and TV?

How do I burn and play an MP4 Movie Maker video produced on Windows 10 on a standard video player and TV.

Bob Hornby

considerate_guy say:

If you have a Blu-ray player, you can just burn the .mp4 directly onto an optical disc (e.g. DVD+R or DVD-R) and then insert it in your Blu-ray player connected to your TV.

If you are looking to use an older DVD player, then you would need to find a program to author a *video* DVD. Note: This is an older, legacy format that can't handle HD video. It uses an older MPEG-2 codec with a maximum resolution of 480p (NTSC).

Microsoft used to include a program (Windows DVD Maker) in Windows Vista and Windows 7 for creating this disc format, but dropped support in 2011 (much like Apple did in dropping their iDVD program).

If you absolutely want to down-rez your video to standard definition so you can play it in a standard DVD player, look for software that will author a video DVD.

But if you already have a Blu-ray player for HD content, I'd use that instead. Windows 10 will let you easily burn the .mp4 file directly onto a disc just by dragging and dropping onto your DVD writer drive. That disc is then immediately playable in HD.

Alternatively, you can also put the .mp4 file onto a USB drive and many modern TVs have the ability to play from that.
