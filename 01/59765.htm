Can anybody tell me how to use the zoom tool in window 7 live movie maker , to zoom in on a video

Can anybody tell me how to use the zoom tool in window 7  live movie maker , to zoom in  on a section of a video
considerate_guy say:

There is no "Zoom tool" in Movie Maker.  There is a zoom slider at the bottom right, but that is just used to expand/contract your storyboard/timeline.  That doesn't actually change anything about the video being viewed.

 

There are effects you can apply to pan and zoom, but those are generally reserved for photos.  They provide a dynamic zoom in on a region of the picture.  It's not a static zoom where one part of the photo (or video) is zoomed in.  Also, the effect is very slight and even less noticeable if used on video (because it takes the full duration of the video to zoom in and because it's hard to notice a zooming action compared to the action in the video).

 

It sounds like you are asking for a zoom/crop feature which might allow you to focus in on only one portion of your video and that is not presently a feature of Movie Maker.  You can always go to feedback.live.com and propose such a feature for Movie Maker.

considerate_guy say:

Let me add a little to the discussion because there appears to be some confusion about what a project file is compared to what a movie file is.

 

A project file is most akin to a recipe. It contains the instructions for assembling a movie.  A project file is usually very small, on the order of KBs (kilobytes) because all it contains are the file names, the list of edit points, the text for captions, etc.  It contains no actual video, photos or music which will be on the order of MBs (megabytes) or even GBs (gigabytes).

 

As you start a project, it probably contains just the initial instructions for loading the videos (e.g. just the file names and their order). Then as you start editing, you are adding further instructions on where to split, what to delete, etc.  This actually adds to the project file size because you are adding more instructions on what to do.  But I would not worry about this at all.

 

What you want to be concerned with is the final movie size.  This is where Movie Maker follows all the instructions in your project file and makes a final movie.  So you might have several MBs or even GBs of video, photos and music and Movie Maker will pull together only the parts it needs to make your final movie.  You should see that a movie created from your parts is smaller in total size compared to the sum of all the sizes of your source files.  The only exception would be if you had perhaps increased the final output dimensions, chosen a really high-bitrate etc., were dealing with lots of photos with lots of animations and effects, etc.

 

But in your case, with just some trimming out of footage, the final movie (not project) should be smaller than your original source video files.

 

So just be sure you are comparing apples (source video files) to apples (final movie).  Looking at the size of your project file over time has no correlation to the size of your movie in the end.

 

It would be nearly impossible for your original video to only be 4 KB.  That would only be around 4,096 bytes and you aren't going to get any amount of video into that file size.  But I can believe that your original project to be 4 KB.  And as you add edit instructions, it might grow slightly.  Looking at how many parts you now have in your project file, a size of 43 KB does not seem unreasonable either.

 

Now go back, look at your original .wmv file.  And compare that to the generated movie.  I think you'll see that from the original file that you converted from YouTube to the final edited movie out of Movie Maker, there is a large reduction in size.  But again, remember that video files are MBs or GBs, not KBs.
