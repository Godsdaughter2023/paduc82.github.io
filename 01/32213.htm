Hi Seismicdude,

Thank you for the detailed description of your problem and for sharing the steps that you've already tried. Getting the error Windows could not automatically detect this network's proxy settings may have something to do with the Internet Settings, the TCP/IP settings, or could be caused by the antivirus installed on your computer.

Disable or uninstall antivirus software

  • Some antivirus software causes issues with proxy in Windows 10. If you have other antivirus software installed on your computer other than Windows Defender that comes with Windows 10, I suggest that you temporarily disable or uninstall it for us to isolate the problem. If removing it resolves the issue, then it definitely is causing the problem. You can utilize Windows Defender to protect your computer.

Change proxy settings

  1. Navigate to Settings > Network & Internet.
  2. In the left pane, click Proxy.
  3. Under Automatic proxy setup, toggle off Automatically detect settings.

Reset TCP/IP

  1. Right-click Windows key and select Command Prompt (Admin).
  2. In the command prompt window, type in netsh int ip reset resetlog.txt and press Enter.
  3. Restart your computer and see if it works.

Reset Internet Settings

  1. In Cortana search, type in Internet Options, and select it from the search results.
  2. Click the Advanced tab, then click Reset. (Deleting personal settings is optional.)
  3. Click Apply, then OK.

If you have additional questions, or if the issue persists after the troubleshooting steps above, please let me know by replying to this thread. This way, I'll get notified and respond back to you as soon as I can.
