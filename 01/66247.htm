chris - support engineer say:

Hi 0per,

"What U Hear" is the Creative Labs version of the Windows "Stereo Mix" function, which is the output from the "out" on the sound card if I'm not mistaken.

Here's some info on that:

http://www.sevenforums.com/tutorials/20595-what-u-hear-recording-enable.html

"I can see that the microphone's "listen to this device" box is NOT checked, yet I hear the mic through my speakers."

That's because whatever your output (mics, music, etc.) is not muted in the Speaker (output) Properties window:

If you click on the speaker inside the red circle, you will not hear the playback of that source through the headphones/speakers unless the "listen to this device" box is checked:

The Stereo Mix combines whatever sounds are routed through the speakers, and uses them as a single input.

For example, this computer has a mic input on the front panel and a rear input on the back of the PC.

It also has the Stereo Mix that it can record from:

 

While using the Stereo Mix, I can record from the Microphone input and the Rear Input at the same time.

I can also select the Microphone as the default device and record just from that input, or just from the Rear Input by selecting that as the default device.

My advice would be to use the Stereo Mix to record from by selecting it as the default device.

You can leave the input levels un-muted in the Speaker Properties in the Playback window to have the signal to record and listen to.

Since you are recording what is being played through the mixer, you will not be able to mute the signal coming from the inputs on the sound card.

That should give you the output you can pass to your effects and recording apps.

At least that how it is on my setup.

I see that the jacks are labeled as output on the sound card you're using, so you might not be able to use all of them as inputs.
