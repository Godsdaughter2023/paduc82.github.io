arminatorx say:

You might also want to contact the maker of your graphics card for that issue. 

The way that background recording works, is that a game renders the graphics to a special window area (which is full screen in your situation). 

However, when you change to a different window, and the full screen app "disappears", the graphics card has no window plane to render to, and therefore the Xbox capturing app doesn't have anything to capture anymore, which is why there is a black screen and no sound.

Unless the game window changes to a still visible smaller window among other windows, there is nothing you can do, as the graphics card isn't rendering anything in it's memory, as long as the game window is not visible.
