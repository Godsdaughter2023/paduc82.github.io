how do i get windows media player to let songs fade into each other on - Microsoft Community

garylagueux say:

how do i get windows media player to let songs fade into each other on playlist

how do i get windows media player to let songs fade into each other on playlist
elrumbo say:

Gary,

I found this info:

 

With Windows Media Player, you can create gradual transitions between songs in your playlists by turning on crossfading. When you do this, the volume at the end of one song fades out and gradually decreases while the volume of the next song fades in and gradually increases. Crossfading is available only when you play Windows Media Audio (WMA) or MP3 files that are either in your library or on a data or HighMAT disc. Crossfading is not available with audio CDs.

  1. Click the Start button , click All Programs, and then click Windows Media Player.

  2. Click the Switch to Now Playing button in the lower-right corner of the Player.

  3. Right-click an open space in the Player, point to Enhancements, and then click Crossfading and auto volume leveling.

  4. Click the Turn on Crossfading link, if visible.

  5. Move the slider to select the amount of time that you want the items to overlap.

  6. To hide the settings, click the Close button in the Crossfading and auto volume leveling dialog box.

    Note

    • In order for crossfading to work with copy-protected files, all of the files that you're playing together must be copy protected.

 

Good Luck.
