Hello John:
I am just a computer techie reading these posts, seeing where I can chime in,
I did some research and it appears to be a SMART - error,
Some articles I read, pointed to it being controlled in the BIOS. Others mentioned that it was part of DELL. Further, there is a "SMART" application in Windows, but it is different that the HDD Smart.

Find the Windows app in: control panel\administrative tools\computer management\services and applications\services

Find "Smart Card" in the services list and open it.
You can then enable, or disable smart software. In Windows.

Now, the Smart Card service in Windows has nothing to do with SMART on the HDD.
The Windows "smart" relates to reading smart cards. It is usually switched off by default anyway. If you don't see SMART in BIOS then you are stuck with the error until you replace your drive. Read the SMART data from your HDD and decide whether the failing attribute, there may be more than one, is likely to cost you important data loss. If you can't interpret the data then you'd better replace your drive. Take a system image first.

Summary:
If you do not see a SMART technology feature within the BIOS Setup Screen, you may not be able to do anything about it. Some users mentioned 3rd party applications, such as fan-tune etc. I would not "stray" and go into the 3rd party apps for a solution. Do some thorough digging under the hard drive management section in the BIOS setup. Please let me know if this was helpful in any form or manner.
I hope it did.
