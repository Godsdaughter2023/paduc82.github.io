The last 30 days of your conversation history are stored in the cloud, so you can access it when you’re signed in to Skype on any device.

History older than 30 days is saved on your hard drive, according to your history settings. 

You can transfer your history from one computer to another – for example, from a Mac to another Mac, or a Mac to a Windows machine – by copying your Skype Name folder from one computer to another. 

To transfer your conversation history:

  1. Open Finder and navigate to ~/Library/Application Support/Skype/.
    • The “~” sign means your home folder. To find your home folder, open Finder and in the main menu, select Go > Home or press Command (Apple) + Shift + H keys.

  4. Replace the Skype Name folder with the one that you saved in step 2.
  5. Sign in to Skype. Your chat history will be there when you browse through your conversations.

Hi,

 Code 10 error is generated in Device Manager in one of the following situations:

  • Device Manager can't start the device.
  • One of the drivers that the device needs does not start.
  • Device Manager has insufficient information to recognize the error that is bubbled up by the device driver.

Note In this situation, the Code 10 error can be a very generic message that indicates some kind of unspecified driver or device problem.

You can either try installing the native drivers in compatibility mode or you can contact the hardware device vendor's technical support for further assistance.

You can skip around in any Fallout game, but easily doing so is supposed to require prior knowledge of the game (that is, if you're replaying the game and know where everything is and how everything plays out already) to reduce the chances of skipping around in the story by simply stumbling around.  Not that this still can't happen (and it's an open world game, so it can certainly stem from random exploration), but the developers do try to hide it.  Of course, if you do accidentally skip ahead, your character gains all this knowledge they should've have (as you said, which does ruin the story a bit), but it's not a big problem and some people actually like that kind of openness in a game

2. Fallout has always been glitchy (as have RPGs and other games of this scale in general), so saving often and saving in multiple slots is always a good rule of thumb to live by.  But glitches are variable (and Bethesda has patched the game up pretty well), so I wouldn't advise putting much weight into them.  Doesn't mean it doesn't stink when it happens to you, but following that rule helps avoid this, and it shouldn't ruin the experience regardless.  Also, no one is going to be stealing things from a house in the middle of a minefield

¡firedog say:

... two accounts ... identical in every way.

What is the reason for setting up two identical accounts? This is bound to cause problems as they vie for attention. It may also be the source of the difficulties you're facing.

Every IMAP server has its idiosyncrasies, so there can be no universal set-up instructions. Since you didn't tell us who the email service provider is, we can't say where the fault lies. However, here are some general guidelines that might help. These are suggestions only - you may find on experimentation that different settings work better for you: 

  1. Reset the list of IMAP folders:
    Click on the account name in the folder pane and then press Alt-S F to open the Show/hide IMAP folders dialogue. Click Reset List to refresh the folder list, then use the buttons to show only the folders you want to see. Note the exact names of the 'special' folders.
  2. Map the local special folders to the IMAP folders on the server:
    Right-click on the account name in the folder pane and select Properties.
    On the IMAP tab, select Store special folders on IMAP server,  then enter the server's names for the special folders in the boxes. If the folder for sent messages at the server is called Sent, then enter Sent into the box alongside Sent Items path and so on. You may have to provide a Root folder path, but we can't tell what this should be without knowing the name of the IMAP server.
  3. Make Deleted items folders work the same way for all accounts:
    When viewing your message list, press Ctrl-Shift-O for Options.
    On the Advanced tab, select Use the 'Deleted Items' folder for IMAP accounts.
