mark ole say:

Hi Charles,

Surface Pen may stop working if the battery is running low. Replacing the battery usually resolves the issue.

If replacing the battery does not resolve the issue, here are some solutions you can try.

Put Surface to sleep, then wake it up

  1. Press and release the power button on your Surface.
    Your Surface goes to sleep.
  2. Wait a minute, and then press and release the power button to wake your Surface up.

You may also try to pair your Surface Pen again.

  1. Go to Start, and select Settings > Devices > Bluetooth.
  2. If Surface Pen appears in the list of discovered devices, it may not be properly paired yet. So, select it from the list and remove the device.
  3. Surface will then re-discover the Surface Pen when you hold down the top button of the Surface Pen for about seven seconds–until the light in the middle of the pen clip starts to flash (on the original Surface Pen) or the light on the flat side of the pen glows white (on the new Surface Pen).
  4. When the pen now appears in the list of Bluetooth devices, select it and then choose Pair.

Install the latest Surface and Windows updates

  1. Go to Start , and select Settings > Update & security > Windows Update.
  2. Select Check for updates.
  3. If updates are available, they will install automatically.
    You may need to restart your Surface after the updates have installed.

Two-button shutdown

  1. Go to Start , and select Power  > Shut down
    If you can’t shut down your Surface normally, press and hold the power button for 30 seconds.
  2. After your Surface turns off, press and hold the volume-up and the power button at the same time for at least 15 seconds, and then release both.
  3. The screen may flash the Surface logo, but continue holding the buttons down for at least 15 seconds.
  4. After you release the buttons, wait 10 seconds.

Run Hardware and Devices Troubleshooter

  1. Type Troubleshooting in the search box, click Troubleshooting.
  2. Under Hardware and Sound, choose Configure a device.
  3. The Hardware Troubleshooter will open.
  4. Click on Next to run the Hardware and Devices Troubleshooter.
  5. Choose the one you want to fix and click on Next. The Hardware and Devices Troubleshooter will try to fix the problem with the pen.

You may also watch this video

for more on troubleshooting Surface Pen issues.

Let us know if you need further assistance.

3. Choose “Export to a file” and then click Next
