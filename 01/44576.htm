Welcome to Microsoft Community. I appreciate your efforts towards resolving the issue. I will certainly help you with the issue that you are facing.

Since the headphones work fine on a different device and other headphones work fine with your computer, this may be an issue with the drivers itself. Usually headphones are plug-n-play devices. However, it seems that Windows might not be able to determine the right driver for the headphone.

Lets try to uninstall the driver and reinstall and check if it works fine. To do so, follow the steps below:

  1. Plug the headphones and go to the desktop screen.
  2. Press Windows + X and select Device manager.
  3. Expand the audio category and right click on the headphone driver and select Uninstall.
  4. Follow the on-screen instructions to uninstall the driver. (make sure to keep a check on the option which says delete the driver)
  5. Once the driver is uninstalled, unplug the headphones and restart the computer.
  6. Plug back the headphones and check if it works fine.

Let us know the results once you have tried these steps. Hope this information was helpful.

andre da costa say:
Do a roll back to Windows Vista

1. Disable any security software before attempting to upgrade or do a clean install.
2. Make sure your computer is updated (devices and applications)
3. Disconnect any external devices before installing.
4. Check your hard disk for any errors:
Click Start
Type: CMD, from the results, right click CMD
Click 'Run as Administrator'
At the Command Prompt, type: chkdsk /r
When you restart your system, your computer will be scanned for errors and attempts will be made to correct them.

Also, run the Windows 7 Upgrade Advisor, it will scan your system and provide a report telling you if your PC can run Windows 7 and if there are any known compatibility issues with your programs or hardware.

Windows 7 Upgrade Advisor:
http://www.microsoft.com/downloads/details.aspx?FamilyID=1b544e90-7659-4bd9-9e51-2497c146af15&displayLang=en

1. Click Start, type msconfig in the Start Search box, and then press ENTER.
       User Account Control permission
       If you are prompted for an administrator password or for a confirmation, type the password, or click Continue.
   2. On the General tab, click Selective Startup.
   3. Under Selective Startup, click to clear the Load Startup Items check box.
   4. Click the Services tab, click to select the Hide All Microsoft Services check box, and then click Disable All.
   5. Click OK.
   6. When you are prompted, click Restart.
   7. Attempt the upgrade again
