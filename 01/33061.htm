The BIOS is separate from Windows. When you start your computer, the BIOS runs first, then it hands control of the computer to the first bootable operating system it finds.

The BIOS comes from the motherboard manufacturer. Since most computer manufacturer's customize the motherboards they use, the computer manufacturer decides how you access the BIOS in your computer. For example, in my Dell desktop I access the BIOS by tapping the F2 key while the computer is restarting.

Your computer manufacturer can tell you how to access the BIOS in your computer. It's got nothing to do with Windows.
