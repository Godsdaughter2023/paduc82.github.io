the larch say:

We don't have a perfect way, but there is a way to get closer to ideal.  Unfortunately it requires special syntax symbols ~= that, when used with the content property, only work when searching in folders that are not indexed by Windows 7 Search, and even then, ~= permits characters attached to the start and end of the phrase.

 

For example, content:~="distributed transactions" would include items whose content text had “bdistributed transactionsq”.  If only content with “distributed transactions” as a distinct phrase was desired, then to get results closer to that desire (but not completely successfully), enter content:(~="distributed transactions" "distributed transactions").  That search would, however, include items that did not have the distinct phrase but instead “bdistributed transactionsq” if the content text also had separate instances of both “distributed” and “transactions”.

 

In brief, try:

content:(~="distributed transactions" "distributed transactions")

A sequential power cycle is the first step in clearing connection problems, and the order and timing of the steps is important to success:

 

1) turn off the Xbox and unplug the power supply cord from the back of the Xbox then

2) unplug power from the router

3) unplug power from the modem.

4) Wait at least 5 minutes then replug the modem power.

5) Wait 'til the light on the front of the modem which says BROADBAND, ONLINE, READY, CABLE or DSL is on solid (not flashing), then plug in the router.

6) Wait until the WiFi light on the router is on; you may not use WiFi, but it's still a good indicator your router is finished booting up.

7) Plug in and turn on the Xbox.  For more background on Ports, see whatismyipaddress.com/port

Solution 2: Contact your bank or financial institution

The Xbox LIVE purchase might have failed because:

  • The credit card was declined due to insufficient funds.
  • The credit card is not authorized for online purchases or automatic billing.
  • The credit card company is blocking the transaction.


Contact your credit card company or bank and make sure that the card that you’re using was activated, that it has available funds, and that the transaction is not being blocked for some reason.

The Xbox LIVE purchase might be blocked because:

Wait and try the transaction again in 24 to 48 hours. If lots of purchase failures occur, that can prevent transactions from being processed until no purchase attempts have been made for a while (usually 24 to 48 hours).
