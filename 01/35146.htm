You Need a WIA driver to use this device. No scanners were detected. Epson Scan cannot be started. Solved

I have an Epson Perfection 4490 Photo scanner.

I had it set up on my Windows 10 desktop for the longest time then mothballed it. 

Dug it out to use it with a laptop and it wouldn't work. Went through all the driver reinstall etc. Figured maybe it was too old for my current Windows 10 (1803 at the time of writing this) Then I tested it back on my desktop... same windows version.... it was working under WIndows 10 on my desktop. 

The symptoms on the non working laptop were as follows:

Launch Windows fax and Scan, click on New Scan and get the message "No scanners were detected."

Launched Epson Scan (version 3.24 at the time of writing) and got the message "Epson Scan cannot be started."

I used Control Panel and ran Devices and Printers. In the Devices section there was Epson Scanner, but if I double click on it I get the message "You need a WIA driver for this device."

In Windows 10 Settings I select Devices then Printer and Scanners and I can see the Epson Scanner. I click on it and select Manage. This brings up the Epson Scanner Manage your device window and I select Scanner Properties and I see no devices... Tried adding a device but it is not found. 

In Device Manager I see it under Imaging Devices and it has the exact same driver as my working system and it says it is working properly.

I did a LOT of troubleshooting, driver reinstalling, Epson removal utilities, checked USB cables, tried another USB port, restarts etc... then I stumbled upon a web page somewhere that said this was an old device, plug it into a USB 2,0 port. 

Yup. Simple as that. As soon as I plug the scanner into a USB 2.0 port it all worked. Plugged it back into a USB 3 port, no joy. 

Now my laptop (Dell latitude E6530) has 4 USB ports, and it is NOT obvious which are USB 2 and which are USB 3... Some new systems colour the USB 3.0 ports blue. On my laptop the ports have a tiny SS beside the USB logo which stands for Super Speed. In my case the two USB 2 ports are located at the back left corner (on the angle) and the right side (eSATA USB combo port) and if I plug into those all is well. Check your system carefully, newer systems often have a mixture of USB 2 and USB 3 ports and various ways of indicating... 

Hope this helps others.

autocheck autochk /k:C

autocheck autochk /k:C /k:D *

autocheck autochk *
