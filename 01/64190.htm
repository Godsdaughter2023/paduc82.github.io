arminatorx say:

[quote user="Sxunds"]Status Code: 3F4A-0000-0080-0200-8015-1802[/quote]

According to the Update Error code Website, this code (8015-XXXX) means that your console couldn't download the update for whatever reason.

From what I experience in the Ambassador chat, this mainly happens when people connect the console wirelessly, and/or have a flaky internet connection, or the console is just "impatient" when an update package doesn't arrive as quickly as the console expected.

Therefore I suggest doing an offline update, by downloading the update on your PC (PCs are more fault tolerant during slow/flaky downloads) and using an USB Stick to install the update on your console.

To do that, first click on this link here.

When the download is finished, double click on the ZIP File that was downloaded.

You should see a folder called "$Systemupdate"

Drag and drop THIS folder onto the USB drive.

Do NOT copy the ZIP File over! If you open the USB Stick, you should ONLY see the "$Systemupdate" Folder and NOTHING ELSE! Also do NOT put the "$Systemupdate" folder inside another folder!

Now safely eject the USB Drive from your computer and go to your console.

To be on the safe side, you should force the console to remove the partially failed update.

Turn the console off, and press and hold the Sync button (it's the small button next to the memory unit slots on the old 360, and the small button next to the USB flap on the front of the Xbox 360S).

Hold down the Sync button and start the console with a push on the console's power button. Hold Sync until the dashboard is started.

Now go to the settings hub, go to the System tile in the upper left, and go to storage.

There press "Left Bumper, Right Bumper, X, Left Bumper, Right Bumper, X"

After that is done, insert your USB Drive with the update.

Cross your fingers and let the console update.Biting your fingernails during the process is also allowed.

After that, the update is finished, and you're done!
