a. user say:

It's just sad that this whole problem is caused by a little piece of plastic preventing them from running at the same time. Any reason for this besides trying to sell an otherwise unneeded accessory?

zacabeb say:

There is indeed a reason.

Since the Xbox 360 cannot output both analog and digital video at the same time, it must decide on one over the other when booting up. If it finds the HDMI connection is terminated and can shake hands with the sink in the receiving device, it switches to HDMI. Otherwise it switches to analog. The analog AV connector in turn has pins to identify the presence of a cable and what type it is.

If both were connected at the same time (as some people have tried doing by jury rigging a regular AV cable), it confuses the Xbox 360. Losing the handshake with the receiving HDMI device is interpreted the same way as if the HDMI cable was pulled out, leaving the Xbox 360 not knowing what to output. If it then finds an analog AV cable connected, it reboots to set that up instead. If only HDMI is connected, or HDMI plus a properly wired audio adapter, it will simply wait for the HDMI connection to re-establish.

So by blocking off the HDMI output when using an AV cable they're pointing out that the Xbox 360 can't output over both connections at the same time. The audio adapter was an afterthought, and when they released the Xbox 360 S, they stopped making the official audio adapter altogether. The third party ones are to the best of my knowledge unlicensed.
