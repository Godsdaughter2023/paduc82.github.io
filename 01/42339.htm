Hi Bigdave55,

Media Center will read sub directories. You need not transfer all the videos to the local har drive from your external raid hard drive. You can add any number of folders to your Media Center Libraries. Even if you have videos or movies stored in a variety of locations, including other Windows 7 computers on your network, they can be added to your Media Center Movie Library.

Follow these steps:

1. Open Windows Media center and then click on tasks and then click on settings.
2. Click on Media Libraries.
3. Select Movies and click next.
4. Select Add Folders to the Library and click Next.
5. Now you’ll need to select the folder location of your movie files.
6. Choose “On this computer” if you are adding a folder from a local hard drive, a folder on mapped network drive, or a folder from USB attached hard drives.
7. Browse the file directory for the folders of your choice. Click on the the plus (+) symbol to view sub-folders. You won’t be able to “see” the video files themselves, only the folders,
    so you’ll need to know where your videos are located ahead of time. Click to the check box to the left to select each folder.
8. Click “Next” when you’re finished.
9. Media Center will confirm your changes. Only the first five media directories are shown on the screen. Scroll to see any additional folders by clicking the up or down arrows.
    If you want to add more folders, click “No, I want to make more changes” and repeat the process to add your additional folders.
10.  When you are done, select “Yes, use these locations” and then click “Finish.”
11.  Your video files should now appear in your Movie Library. Depending on the amount of files you are adding, it may take a few minutes for Media Center to add and display all of them.
12.  Then simply browse for any of your selections and click to play them.

Hope this helps!

Right click on the external drive, select Properties then the Security tab and give your user name Full Control over the drive. Make sure the permissions are propagated through the folder structure.

For Outlook 2007 and earlier the pst files should be transferred from C:\Documents and Settings\<username>\Application Data\Microsoft\Outlook to C:\Users\<username>\AppData\Local\Microsoft\Outlook.

eraser (do not use this if your battery has thin slots - just clean the knife edges that fit into

however on a laptop that will not extend their life that much. Best is to use A/C adapter
whenever possible.
