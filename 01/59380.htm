This is a search index problem. Files you have deleted earlier still appear in search results because the Indexing Service needs some time to update its records.

To resolve the issue, you can rebuild the search index.Follow these steps to rebuild the Search Index in Windows Vista:
a. Click Start, type Indexing Options. Press Enter.
b. In the Indexing Options window, click Advanced
c. When the User Account Control prompt appears, click Continue.
d. To rebuild the Search index, click the Rebuild button.

NOTE: Rebuilding the index can take a long time to complete, depending upon the number of files and available resources in the system.

e. Click OK when you see the following message:
This operation will completely re-build the indexed locations on your computer and might resolve problems if your search results often appear to be out of date. This operation might
take a long time.
f. Click Ok.

Follow the below steps to put your computer in clean boot state:
1. Log on to the computer by using an account that has administrator rights.

5. Click OK, and then click Restart.

The above steps would put the computer in clean boot state.
