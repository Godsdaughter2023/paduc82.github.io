Can I add a photo in the middle of my video without losing the - Microsoft Community

michaeloberlin say:

Can I add a photo in the middle of my video without losing the sound?

Original title: Microsoft Live Movie Maker - Photos in video

 

Can I add a photo in the middle of my video without loosing the sound?

considerate_guy say:

Yes and no.

 

The audio associated with your video is tied to the video.  So if you clip out a portion of video and replace it with a photo (or just make a split and insert a photo), the audio won't be there.  Movie Maker doesn't have the option to overlay a different visual element but to keep the audio continuing.  In essence it has one video track and can't handle more.

 

If you are using the latest version of Movie Maker 2012 though, you can treat the audio separately.  You would add your video, and then mute it (Video Tools | Edit, Video Volume = 0).  Now you would add this same file as the "music" (or the sound file).  Normally the Add music button will show you audio-only files, but you can change the selection in the lower right of the file dialog to "Video files".  Choose the video file you already added.

 

If you did it correctly, you'll have the silent video, and then audio playing below as the "music".  You now can treat them separately.  So if you want to add a photo, make splits and delete the equivalent amount of video (say 7 seconds) and then add a photo with a duration of 7 seconds in its place.  The result will be you'll see video, then the photo, then video continuing.  All the while, the audio will be playing along as expected.

 

So, the feature to overlay a photo isn't there in Movie Maker, but you can workaround it by treating video and the audio (from the video file) as separate entities.  The only hard part is then keeping the two in sync.

d) Click OK, and then click Restart.
