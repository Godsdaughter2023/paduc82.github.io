troz1 say:

Answering my own question to report a solution.


Had good support from a Dell technician who spent a couple of hours remotely logged in to my machine, but couldn't isolate the problem.

Tried various other things including a complete re-image, no success.

Then I noticed that on the original install disk for the Fuji Xerox printer there were both Postscript (PS) and PCL6 drivers. If I let Windows choose the driver, it always chose the PS one. So I manually installed the PCL6 driver on the desktop machine to which the printer was connected.

Then I uninstalled and re-installed the printer on the Dell laptop. This time, when I selected "connect to a shared printer on another computer" it gave me the choice of "Fuji Xerox P255 PS" or "Fuji Xerox P255 PCL6". I chose the latter, and suddenly MS Office and Adobe Reader were able to talk to the printer, and I could open the printing preferences dialogue.

Why one computer refused to talk to a printer with a Postscript driver when two other near-identical computers on the same network were happy to do so remains a mystery to me, but at least I have a workaround.
