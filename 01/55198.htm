[Original title: Windows Live Mail Issue]

¡firedog say:
... locked by another session. 

Your phone is accessing the mailbox via POP3, with push mail enabled. There can only be one connection to a POP3 mailbox at a time, so because your phone is always connected to it, Windows Live Mail can't get a look in. Turn off push mail in the phone and set the polling frequency (how often to check for new messages) to an odd number like 7 or 11 minutes. Set Windows Live Mail to check at a different frequency - 13 or 17 minutes. This way there won't often be collisions as both devices try to connect at once. To change the frequency in Windows Live Mail, press Ctrl-Shift-O for Options. On the General tab, set the number n under Check for new messages every n minutes.

***Post moved by the moderator to the appropriate forum category.***

markinkent say: What is it you are trying to achieve by doing this?  Most common route of getting 2 laptops to talk to each other is using some kind of networking.

a. user say:

USB connections are supposed to have a single "host" - the computer end of the connection is 'special', and responsible for managing the data from one or many devices plugged into the port and supplying them with power. Directly connecting two computers would break that spec and produce a conflict because both of them would expect to be the host.

Seconding MarkInKent, you're better off with connecting using an Ethernet cable (if both laptop has an Ethernet Port). Networking is the way computers talk to each other most naturally, and you'll get better transfer speeds.

davem121 say:

Your best option for connecting two laptops is either through your household WIFI or by using an Ethernet crossover cable - a Male/Male USB will not work 

However there is a USB bridged cable available with specific software that will do the job - just Google USB Bridged cable . . .
