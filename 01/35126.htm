Installing from a CD is much different from what is implied regarding the files/folders you want to keep from your current operating system. During the installation of software from a CD, you should indeed be prompted but that's not necessarily so either. That would just depend on whether or not you have turned off your "AutoRun" default setting. I doubt you have so no worry there. Copying files or folders and saving them to a CD or other removable media is different. In that case as well, you might be prompted...or not. It would still depend on the autorun feature. Most folks just leave that alone so again, there should be no worry there...but, in the case of files or folders, perhaps other documents that you save to a CD, the only prompting you may receive might be something like:
"Open folder to view files" or something similar. In effect, what Windows would be asking you there is what it is you want to do with the CD you placed in the drive. Open it, write data to it, play music from it, create a music CD...etc. You would just open it, copy the files/folders you saved on it, and paste them to your desktop...from there you can move the files/folders wherever you want them.
