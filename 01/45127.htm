mr. chris d. say:

(You know those times when you type a lot and you lose everything you typed? Yeah, that just happened.)

Since I have no more patience anymore, I'll be a little more concise and less detailed.


I have a Lenovo X220 Tablet with a Wacom multitouch + stylus tablet.

Firstly, download Wacom's most recent drivers. I've always been able to use my stylus without worrying about touch because it disables touch when the stylus is in the screen's proximity. But still, the proximity is not too far of a distance, so it is very easy for me to make stray marks with my hand while using OneNote.


To remedy my problem - that is, to disable multitouch (when I use OneNote) - I created a shortcut to disable (and another to enable) multitouch using devcon.

You can download devcon (x64) and the shortcuts here: http://uppit.com/wduku6xjgqot/Enable_Disable_Multitouch_Windows_8_x64.zip


-Move devcon.exe to Windows\System32.

-Move the two shortcuts to C:\ProgramData\Microsoft\Windows\Start Menu (to pin to Start)

-Disable your Human Interface Devices one by one in Device Manager to find your multitouch device. Then open the properties of the appropriate device, go to Events and copy the (long and obscure) device id/name

-Edit the target of the shortcuts; replace "@[my device name]" with "@[your device name]"

There you go. When you use the shortcut to disable/enable, the Command Prompt will open (you'll have to give permission since it opens it as admininstrator) and it will confirm that the device is disabled/enabled.

While inside the Reliability and Performance Monitor:

 

1.     Expand the ‘Data Collection Sets’ folder;

2.     Right click on ‘User Defined’;

a.     Select ‘New’,

b.     Select ‘Data Collector Set’,

c.     Provide a name for the new data collector set,

                                          i.    Ensure that ‘Create from a template’ is selected,

                                         ii.    Click ‘Next’,

                                        iii.    Choose ‘System Diagnostic’ or ‘System Performance’ (Membership in Local Administrators group, or equivalent, is the minimum required to run this Data Collector Set.),

                                        iv.    Click ‘Finish’.

d.     Right click the new collection set,

e.     Click ‘Start’.

 

To view the data graphically or in the report format:

 

1.     Expand the ‘Reports’ folder;

2.     Expand the ‘User Defined’ folder;

3.     Expand the name of the newly created collection set;

a.     There should be two reports inside the folder displaying year/month/date and serial # of report,

                                          i.    The first is the graphical display,

                                         ii.    The second will be in the report format.

b.     Click on the second report to view.
