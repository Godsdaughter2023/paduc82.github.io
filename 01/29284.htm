frederik long say:

You can use Remote Desktop to access your PC, provided that:

  • Your desktop PC runs Windows 7 Professional or higher.
  • Remote Desktop is enabled.
  • Your account is authorised to use Remote Desktop.
  • Your network administrator has created a tunnel for port 3389 on his firewall.
  • The Chinese do not block such traffic.

I would do this in preparation:

  1. Set up Remote Desktop on your machine.
  2. Test it from some machine on the internal network, using this command:
    mstsc /v:192.168.1.55 (you must use your PC's internal IP address)
  3. Test it from your own home, using this command:
    mstsc /v:www.mycompany.com (your network admin can tell you the correct name)
  4. Ask a friend in China to perform Test 3.

In regards to problem #2 with the on/off commands being out of sync...it might be that some of your devices use an on/off toggle command (ie, it sends the same signal for both on and off). All it's doing is blasting the IR signal for all of your configured devices, so if any of your devices use the same IR command for on and off (and a lot do), there's no way around this.  The XBone/Kinect ccan only send the IR command that it knows...it has no knowledge of the current on/off state of the connected devices.

Here's my setup:

- DirecTV receiver connected to Xbox One

- Xbox One connected to Pioneer A/V receiver

- A/V receiver connected to my Vizio TV.

- All connected via HDMI (obviously).

The only device in the chain that has separate IR commands for on/off is the DirecTV receiver (I know this because there are separate buttons for on/off on the DirecTV remote).

Here's  a common situation:

My son wants to play the Wii U, so he turns on the TV, the A/V receiver, and the Wii U, then changes the A/V receiver input to the Wii U and starts playing. He never touches the XBone. An hour later, I want to play some Forza, so I give him some B.S. chore to do so I can take over the TV. I turn on the Xbox One and the "instant on" feature turns on the DirecTV receiver, but it turns off the A/V Receiver and the TV. Since the Xbox was turned on, it sent the "on" command to the DirecTV receiver. However, it sent the on/off toggle command to the TV and A/V receiver (again, that's all it knows).  Because they were already on, they get toggled off and I have to turn them on individually.  

I think you just have to teach everyone in your house to always turn the TV on by turning on the Xbox One, even if they aren't using it specifically. That way, they're always kept in sync.
