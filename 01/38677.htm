You'll have to go into the Libraries 'the back way', but here's how you can fix it up.

  1. Press +R to show the Run box, and run this command:

    %appdata%\Roaming\Microsoft\Windows\Libraries


  2. In the folder that opens, you'll see the libraries listed. These are the actual library files.  If you're feeling nerdy, you can open a Notepad window and drag one of these into it to see how the library is built (the settings that control where it searches and how it displays the results).

    But to fix it, right-click and go to properties on these.  This time, you'll see the normal "General" tab at the top, so you an access and unset the Read-Only flag.

obsessive power say:

Hi,

There a number of things that need to be taken in to account when talking about BC.

1.  While the BC games are sold on the store, most users will either already own the game on disc, or it will be part of their digital library.  Microsoft are mostly doing this a kind of courtesy, there is very little profit for them as a company or their partners in this feature.  I doubt whether the BC team is big enough for the amount of games you're after.

2.  Each game has to be effectively reverse engineered and repackaged for the Xbox One platform.  When you insert a backwards compatible 360 disc into your Xbox One for the first time, you'll be asked to download an update.  But these aren't updates, they are the actual game, reprogrammed and tested to run on the Xbox One platform.  This isn't a quick process. Also, this process is only done by the BC team from Microsoft, they don't get any assistance from the developers.

3. Developers/Publishers have to agree to make their old games BC.  Some games can't be made BC due to expired licenses.
