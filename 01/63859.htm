Which game or games are you wanting to play using your Intel HD4400 integrated graphics ?

Your Intel HD4400 graphics is a decent performer as far as integrated graphics goes, but

updating a graphics driver does not make your graphics processor more suitable for newer games.

Newer drivers sometimes fix issues with a new release game, but if the processor does not meet the

system requirements of the game there is nothing you can do.

- Nvidia and AMD drivers are suitable only for their respective graphics processors.

If you wish to install an Intel driver manually rather than allow Win10 to select and install the driver via

Windows updates, the best driver for your Intel HD4400 would be the one offered by HP.

The driver offered by Intel may suit, but is not optimised for HP system.

You can try the driver from the Intel site, but if you have issues go back to whatever HP has to offer on their site,

or allow Windows update to reinstall it's choice.

(Allowing Windows to install drivers does not extend to dedicated Nvidia or AMD cards though.

With these it is still best to manually install the driver of your choice.)

after I did the 1511 update (because msinfo32 and Device manager use a different numbering system to

on Win7 .

Intel graphics do not get updated as frequently as Nvidia or AMD, and they don't need to because the

processor does not have as many functions (high end visual effects such as ambient occlusion)

as Nvidia or AMD dedicated  graphics processors .

Nvidia and AMD also release a new series graphics card far more frequently, and new series processors are

more likely to need 'tweaks' in the drivers than an older series processor.
