How to get WPA2 for Windows 10

 am  unable to change  security from None to WPA2 for wireless connection onWindow 10

a. user say:

Wireless encryption (like WPA2-PSK) is a function of your router, not of Windows.

To change the wireless encryption on your WiFi network, log in to your router's administration console. That's where you'll find the encryption settings for your network.

The manual you received with the router will have complete instructions for doing this. If you can't find the manual, just go online to the manufacturer's website and for the instructions for your model number.

Very generally speaking - this may not apply in your case - you open a web browser and type the URL for your router's administration console, which might be 192.168.1.1 or something similar, into the address bar. Press Enter and you'll be at the sign in screen for the admin console. Sign in and look for the encryption settings.

General troubleshooting steps
A. Reboot your computer. Generally, rebooting solves many problems.
B. Try a different USB port
C. In case of wireless mouse, pair it again with the computer
D. In case of wireless mouse, check if batteries are not weak.

Please try these options one by one and check after each option.

1 - Use the Hardware & Devices Troubleshooter
Type Troubleshoot in Windows Start Search Box > Click Troubleshoot > Click Hardware and Devices > Click Run the troubleshooter and follow onscreen directions

2 - Take actions related to mouse (Check after each step A, B and C)
Windows Key+X (Or right click on Start) > Click Device Manager > Expand Mice and Other Pointing Devices > Right click on the mouse listed there >

A. Roll back - Click Properties > Roll Back - Under Drive tab, if Rollback Driver is enabled click it > Reboot

B. Uninstall - Uninstall > Reboot and the driver will be automatically installed

C. Update - Update Driver > Click Update Driver Software > Click Browse my computer for driver software > Click Let my pick from a list of device drivers on my computer > Choose the driver > Click Next to update > Reboot
If this does not work, select Search automatically for the updated driver in place of Browse my computer for driver software > Reboot

I had this same issue.  I found that the issue was being caused by my Biometric device that came with the computer.  I have an HP.  The Biometric software that came with the computer was conflicting with the Windows Driver Foundation User-Mod Driver process that was running.

I would suggest going into task manager and attempting to shut this process down multiple times (It took me three consecutive times for it to shut down).

Unfortunately if you have a biometric device you can't disable this process as it needs to run to run the biometric device.  What I ended up doing was uninstalling the software that came from HP and I not longer have an issue.

colonelron say:

I need to cut sections out of a video to remove some mistakes made during filming. Can I do multiple cuts and splices with this program?

<Original title:How to cut and splice?>

I need to cut sections out of a video to remove some mistakes made during filming. Can I do multiple cuts and splices with this program? I can not figure out how to cut and splice on this. I have a 10 minute video and there are a couple of sections where an unwanted person walked into the field of view. Can this be done? OR do I need to reshoot the entire film?

considerate_guy say:

Use the Split feature.

Let's say you have a clip that is:

GGGGBBBGGGG

The part with BBB is the bad part you want to remove.

Play until you get to the start of that part. Then go to the Video Tools | Edit tab and click on Split. You can also just type M on the keyboard as a shortcut. Make another split at the end of the bad part.

The result will be that your clip is split into three.

GGGG|BBB|GGGG

Now select the bad part and press the Delete key to remove it from your timeline. The other two parts will squeeze together so when you save it as a movie, it will play without the bad part between.
