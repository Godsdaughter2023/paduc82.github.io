Windows XP Home Edition is your operating system (OS) on top of which you will likely install and use all sorts of other applications/programs.

Pentium CPU 2.53GHz means you have an Intel (name brand) processor running at 2.53GHz "clock rate" <- click for more.

0.99gb of ram means you have 1 gigabyte of system memory or RAM (random access memory) to use.  It's the 'active' part of the storage of your system - usually what you have open/are doing on the computer is stored in memory (as well as other things that are running in the background like the operating system.)

You could get more memory - but it won't help too much.

You may be able to get a faster processor - but it would probably entail upgrading many more parts.

Windows XP was released at the end of 2001/beginning of 2002.  There have been three operating system releases from Microsoft since then (technically Windows 8 is out - just not public yet.)  You wouldn't want to upgrade that on the hardware you have.

Your best bet - buy a whole new system.

tricky300 say:

Hi,

Thinking of comparing a PC with a human working in a totally manual office...

The CPU (central processing unit) is the human. In general the more GHz the faster is works.

The hard disk is the filing cabinets, files have to be taken out of the filing cabinets and placed on the desk for the human to work with. Upgrading to bigger or more disk drives is like buying a bigger filing cabinet or more filing cabinets.

RAM (random access memory) is equivalent of the office desk that the human works at. More RAM is like having a bigger desk so that you can have more files in view at any one point in time. Upgrading to more RAM allows your PC to work more efficiently because it increases the amount of information that can be worked on concurrently.

GB is just a measurement of storage capacity either for the RAM or hard disk. A single GB is approximately 1,000,000,000 characters (letters/digits/symbols) of information.

Your RAM is probably upgradable but it depends on the capabilities of your PC. For more specific information we will need your PC brand, range and model number. You cannot just purchase any RAM, it has to be the correct type for your PC. Many online RAM shops will guide you through the process of selecting the correct RAM for your PC.

If you are using XP it is doubtful that your PC can have a CPU upgrade. This is due to the fast pace of hardware development which almost certainly precludes the purchase of a compatible brand new CPU. Second hand CPUs may be usable as upgrades, but you would have to check compatibility with your PC. Again, we will need details of your PC to give definitive advice.

rooooog say:

I've finally been able to get this working (so far). Here's what I did:

Download latest Windows 7 64-bit Intel Bluetooth drivers:

https://downloadcenter.intel.com/download/25319/Intel-PROSet-Wireless-Software-for-Bluetooth-technology-for-Windows-7-64-bit

Installed, and seems to be fixed. But somehow the Microsoft drivers are being used, and my Bluetooth LE keyboard and mouse don't work anymore. Went to Device Manager -> Microsoft Generic Bluetooth Device -> Properties -> Driver -> Update Driver -> Browse My Computer -> Let Me Pick -> Uncheck "Show Compatible Hardware" -> Intel Corporation -> Intel Wireless Bluetooth. The updated 2015 Intel driver is now used instead and LE devices work.

Now BOTH "Listen to Music" and "Forward VoIP Calls to Handsfree" are selected in the device services. From here it was just a matter of selecting "Bluetooth Headphones" as the default devices in the "Playback Devices" window.

I've noticed there is a slightly longer delay between initial connection with the headphones and having audio available, but it's on the order of ~5 seconds which is much better than what I was doing before.

For me it appears that deselecting the VoIP calls option has no effect on a reconnection, both services still get selected.
