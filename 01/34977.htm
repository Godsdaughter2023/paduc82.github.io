datoneguy say:

Laptop Screen doesn't work properly without pressure on LCD Cable?

I've recently installed a new laptop screen on a computer I bought off a friend with a broken screen. The laptop screen lights up when turned on, however it receives no video data. This can be fixed by applying upward pressure on the LCD cable into the screen, which allows the screen to work normally.  However, when I try attaching the screen to the laptop and stop applying pressure, if the cable moves at all it stops working. Sometimes it tints red/green/yellow, sometimes color spaz out completely, and sometimes it just turns off. I've tried re-connecting the cable multiple times, but I get the same result each time. The tape on the cable meant to keep it attached to the screen does not fix the issue, regardless of how tight it is pulled. My assumption is because this provides a slight upward force instead of a force directly into the connector. It isn't the laptop screen because it's the second brand-new laptop screen I've tried connecting. Should I buy a new LCD cable, or is there a well-known solution to this? I doubt the cable is supposed to be unseated so easily, but I just don't know.

Hi,

I went through the entire scenario which you have described here and it appears that you are experiencing issues with VGA Monitor Port. This is mostly due to lose connections between the VGA Monitor Port coming from your CPU.

We can get this resolve by ensuring that VGA Cable has no broken pins & by replacing your Cable with the new one. Usually this is not that expensive and you can buy it for less than $5 .
