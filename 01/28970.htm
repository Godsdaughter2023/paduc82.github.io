mac_ha say:

I had exacxtly same problem, took me two days to look around util found this thread.  And the solution was some somehow burried within all the posts - yes, it was the wrong permission on some files inside Users\Default folder, but looks like in each case it's different. And you can't just look at the permission of the Users\Default folder itslef, as it is laways correct. What I've done, is forcibly propagated the permission of the Users\Default folder down to all folders/files within it:

1. Right click Users\Default folder, select Properties and go to the Security tab;

2. Check that all permissionas are correct:

- Everyone: Read & execute; List folder content; Read

- SYSTEM: Full control

- Administrators: Full control

- Users: Read & execute; List folder content; Read

2. Click Advanced, then Change Permission (it'll ask for Admin's password if you have UAC enabled)

3. Check "Replace all child object permissions with inheritable permissions from this object" box

4. Click Apply and/or OK

Now you should be able to logon to your new profiles/users....

Hope this helps...

If this email account has already been deleted or removed, this account cannot be recovered anymore. If you are unable to access your primary email account, you can reset your password by following these steps provided below:

 

·         In the sign in page, please click on “Can’t access your account?”

·         Choose either “I forgot my password” or “I know my password and Microsoft account, but can’t sign in”.

·         Enter your Microsoft account that you would like to recover and type in the characters that you see in the image below (this is case sensitive), then click “Next”

·         You will be provided with different options to recover your account.

-          Email me a reset link (if available). This will send a password reset link to your alternate email address.

-          Secret Question (if available). This allows you to reset your password immediately after you have provided your Secret Answer.

-          Send a code to my phone (if available). This will give you the option to send a text or call to you for a verification code to your mobile phone. After you enter the code, you would be able to sign in.

1.       Click on this link. https://account.live.com/ResetPassword.aspx

2.       Enter the Microsoft account you are trying to recover.

3.       Enter the characters you see on the picture and then tick on Next.

4.       Click on I can’t use any of these options.

5.       Under Where should we contact you?, provide an active alternate email address.

6.       Click on Next.

7.       Fill out the form and provide as much information.

8.       Click on Submit.

 

When asked to enter an email address in "Where should we contact you" field, please enter the email where you want to receive the code. Please make sure to provide as many information as you can to have a better chance of recovering the account.
