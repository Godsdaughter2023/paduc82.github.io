jan wijninckx say:

Issue of device manager refreshing emerged after fresh install of Windows 10 onto previously working Windows 7 machine.

One place where you can see this problem of the refresh is in the Settings>Devices (WinKey+I, click Devices); it refreshes there too and "USB to serial" kept appearing / disappearing

Tried the power settings as other post recommend, nothing.

Tried the registry fixes of other site's posts, nothing.

In the end I had 2 issues:

1. USB port on motherboard is faulty under Win10 (worked under 7).

2. Microsoft 900 wireless keyboard / mouse receiver has an issue in Win10 not in Win7

Happened to be the port the Microsoft 900 wireless keyboard / mouse receiver was plugged into.

Resolution for me:

a.  Used an old mouse to disable one by one the usb ports in the device manager (control panel, right-mouse drag a copy of the link onto your desktop, to make your life easier). Without that youmay end up losing your keyboard/mouse and you only have the power button to rescue you.

b.  Tricky to deactivate the "USB Root Hub" (because of refreshing) but one by one deactivated till I hit the offending port, and the refresh stopped.

c.  Put MS900 receiver in another port, same issue is back.

d.  Removed USB root where MS900 was plugged into, used the mouse with wire to Action>rescan and problem sorted.

e.  Put MS900 back into another port, issue is back.

f.   Put another device into motherboard initial offending USB, refresh issue appears.

g.  Deactivated that "USB Root Hub" and issue is gone.

Wow. What a fiddle. Just wanted to share this in case it helps others.
