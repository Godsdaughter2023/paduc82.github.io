Welcome to Microsoft Windows community Answers Forum!

If you've deleted the shortcuts from the desktop, click on Recycle Bin >> retrieve them from there.

'Access is denied'

This problem crops up when you either upgrade your Operating System from a previous Operating System or copy your "My Documents" folder from an earlier to certain folders on a computer.

What happens when you upgrade your computer?

It sets the Read permissions of the "My Documents", "My Music", "My Pictures", and "My Videos" folders to Deny and causes the problem you are seeing when you try to access these shortcuts.

It's a bit of a convoluted situation but fortunately Microsoft goes into great detail about the cause and the solution in : http://support.microsoft.com/default.aspx/kb/930128

Once you take a look and follow the instructions you should be able to access your My Documents folder again without a problem.
