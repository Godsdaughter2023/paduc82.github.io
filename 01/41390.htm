williamsteinhoff say:

My computer states I have low disc space. How do I fix this problem?

sammysupper say:

Hi William,

Lots of things spring to mind after reading your question...

Have you had your computer for many years and it probably is full of pictures and video and music etc?

If so, you can purchase a hard drive to transfer all of your pictures and music etc over to and free up huge amounts of space again on your computer.

To check just how full your hard drive is, you can do this...

In the bottom left corner of your screen, left click the start menu, (or round windows icon).

In the list that pops up, can you see 'Computer'?  If so, left click that word.

Now, in this new window that's just opened, you can see how full your computer disk drive is.

What you need to look at will more than likely be called 'Local Disk (C:)'.

If the image there doesn't show a graph, or any picture representation of how full your drive is, you need to right click on the picture of the drive, and in the menu that appears, click Properties.

You will now definitely be able to see a pie chart type image of how full your drive is.

On a side note, have you ever performed any maintenance on your hard drive?  What I mean is, have you ever ran a programme called Disk Defragmenter, and have you ever deleted your browsing history?

If not, it's worth doing.

First of all, I would say, if you haven't done either of those tasks before, doing them now is likely to take quite some time... not for you.. I mean for your computer to do them.  Not so much for the deleting browsing history, but for the defragmentation, that will take a while depending on the ability of your computer.

What the defragmenter program does, to put it simply, is move things around on your hard drive to a more efficient place.

To make it easier for your computer to do the disk defragment, it's much better to not use your computer during that time.  Allow a couple of hours or so... it might not take that long, I can't say without knowing anything about your machine.

If you want to use your computer while running the defrag program, it can be done, but it will take longer, ok.

To delete your browsing history, and you may find this clears out huge amounts of space...

Open a web page, and at the top of your screen, where it says File Edit View Favourites and Tools, click Tools.

Now in the list of Tools that are there, look in there for one that says 'Delete Browsing History', with any luck it'll be the top one in the list.  If it's there click it.

If it's not in the list, choose the last tool that says, 'Internet Options', and in the new window that appears, about three quarters of the way down, you'll see Browsing History, and a button that says Delete.  Click the delete button.

Once you've clicked to delete your browsing history, another window will open, that asks for you to tick the boxes of what you want to delete.

The ones I'm recommending deleting are your Temporary Internet Files and Website Files, History, and Download History.  You can of course select any of them.  Personally I have a tick in all of them except the top one, but I remember my passwords.  The more boxes you choose, the more stuff will get deleted and the more space you'll free up on your drive.

Please bare in mind, this does not delete your pictures of films or music or documents or anything like that... it only deletes website information.  If you've never deleted it before, remnants of the very first web you visited on the first day you used your computer could still be stored somewhere there, and I'm sure you don't need that do you.

Ok, the other program I mentioned was the disk defragmenter.  To run that...

There are several ways to get to the program, but the easiest is this...

Click the start menu again (bottom left corner) and where your curser is flashing now, start typing defragmenter.  Before you've even finished, a result at the top should say, Disk Defragmenter.  Click on it.

A second or two, or three, may pass, and then the program window will appear.

In the window, you will see a list of the drives your computer has.  You need to highlight, (left click), the 'C' Drive.

Once you've done that, click the Defragment disk button.  You can click Analyze (sic) first if you want, but that will take a long time too and so will lengthen this whole procedure.

Now go make a cup of tea and put your feet up.

It'll tell you when it's finished.

If either of those don't free up much space and your drive is nearly full, just do like I said at the start and buy a separate hard drive and transfer what you want over to it.  You can then delete that content from your computer because it'll be on the separate hard drive.  Once you've deleted it off your computer drive, run the defragmentation program again.  It's a good idea to run that program every couple of months anyway, to keep your computer in tip top shape.

About the separate hard drive... if you want to transfer pictures and music and movies, buy one that is mains electric powered... not one that is powered by USB.  The reason is, the mains powered one will be much quicker to transfer everything. - It'll take only minutes, whereas a USB powered one with literally take hours and hours (if you've a lot to do)

Hope that helps.
