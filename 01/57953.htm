hassanb say:

Hi,

I'll be happy to explain how it works. You're definitely on the right track here.

As long as you have 1 gamertag on the Xbox One console that has a gold membership, all profiles that sign into that Xbox One console will have a gold membership as well.

You are correct in that you can only have 1 gamertag per Microsoft Account (e-mail). If you wish for others to have a gamertag, you will need to make them their own e-mail address and then their own Xbox LIVE gamertag. As long as the account being created is under the age of 18, you will be asked to enter in a parental e-mail. This will allow you to control certain features of your child's account as well as access to the things they can and can't do on Xbox LIVE.

There's no need to sign up for another gold subscription. As long as at least 1 account on the console has a gold membership, that membership is shared with everyone on that console. This would mean that if your son goes to a friends house, he can also share his friends gold membership for the time that he is at his house.

The only issue with this is that your son would not be able to game on the Xbox 360 without a gold membership as this Xbox One Home Gold is not backwards compatible. Your son will also not be able to game online if he goes to a friends house and his friend does not have a gold subscription. This is meant for families that want their accounts separate but also don't wish to pay $60 for each family member to play online.

Hope that explains it a bit better.

macallan say:

Security Centre Pop-up

Why does the pop-up notify me that the security centre states that my computer is at risk. When I go into the centre it states that all is normal and the computer is running normally.
john_barnett say:

Hi macallan,

Does the message appear and then, after a short while, disappear and everything goes back to normal?

The security center covers areas such as anti-virus, firewall, etc. I have seen this happen if the PC has Microsoft Security Essentials installed. Upon booting a flag appears in the notification area informing the user that the machine is at risk. Then, after 10 or 15 seconds (or so) the security action center notification indicates that 'no current issues detected.'

In the case of Microsoft security essentials the firewall is usually the cause of the problem. For some reason it doesn't turn the firewall on as fast as the security center would like, thus initiating an error message. After a few second the firwalls initiates and everything runs as it should do.

If you are not using Microsoft security essentials then it maybe that your anti-virus or something similar is a little slow to initiate and the security center immediatelty flags the issue. So long as the error corrects itself within a few seconds then everything should be fine. You do say that when you click on the security center icon it say's that your PC is running normally. It is simply a 'time delay' that is causing the problem.
