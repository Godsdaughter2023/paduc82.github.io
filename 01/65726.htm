a. user say:

Hi,

If you haven't tried checking if there's an available driver update of your Bluetooth device, please do so. Hardware components or software drivers of some devices may be updated during a device's lifecycle which may impact Skype performance.

  1. Select the Start Windows logo Start button, type Device Manager, and select it from the list of results.
  2. Expand one of the categories to find the name of your device, then right-click (or tap and hold) it, and select Update Driver. For graphics cards, expand the Display adapters category, right click your graphics card and select Update Driver.
  3. Select Search automatically for updated driver software.
  4. If Windows doesn't find a new driver, you can try looking for one on the device manufacturer's website and follow their instructions.

Close any applications that might have been running in the background that could interfere with your Skype call. I also recommend to uninstall and reinstall Skype app (Windows Desktop). Go here then locate "Completely uninstall and reinstall Skype" and follow the instructions carefully. 

In addition, a quick audio compatibility check might be helpful too. Compare your Windows Sound settings with your Skype Audio settings. To locate your Windows Sound settings, choose Start → Control Panel → Sound or Sounds and Audio Devices. (This path may differ on some computers with different operating systems and configuration setups, but your aim is to locate the Sounds and Audio Devices control panel.) Make sure that the sound devices match.

I hope this helps!
