This issue occurs when all the following conditions are all true:

 

a)       You have a removable drive, such as an Iomega Zip drive, configured as drive letter C.

b)       You do not have a removable disk in the removable disk drive.

 

Method 1:

 

Change the drive letter assignment for the removable drive to a letter other than C:\.

 

a.     Log on to the computer as Administrator.

b.     Insert a disk in the removable drive.

c.     Type computer management in the start search box and hit Enter.

d.     Click Disk Management.

e.     Right-click the partition, logical drive, or volume that you want to change, and then click Change Drive Letter and Paths.

f.     Click the removable drive, click it, click Change, click the drive letter that you want to use, and then click OK.

 

 

Method 2:

 

I would also suggest you to boot the computer in Clean Boot and check which non Microsoft program is causing the issue.

 

How to troubleshoot a problem by performing a clean boot in Windows Vista and Windows 7

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/929135

 

Disclaimer: After you used the clean boot to resolve your problem follow Step 7 to reset the computer to start as usual.

 

 

Method 3:

 

You can go into device manager and see if you have any yellow bang! for the Mouse or any devices.

 

a)      Click on Start and then Control Panel.

b)      Click on the System and Maintenance link.

c)       In the System and Maintenance window, click on the Device Manager link near the bottom of the window.

 

Now, in Device Manager, go to Mice or other pointing devices.

Do you see any yellow Bangs!? This would mean you have an error with that device. Try to remove it and reinstall.

After writing this post, I found Release notes: Important issues in Windows 10 post at Microsoft TechNet.

As the Geolocation Service can no longer be started, Geolocation will remain disabled on the device and prevent location based functionality from working. To work around this issue, manually delete the following regkey using regedit: HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Services\lfsvc\TriggerInfo\3.

Type Printer in Cortana Search box > Printers & Scanners > Select your printer > Remove device
