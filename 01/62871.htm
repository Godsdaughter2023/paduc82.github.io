langware say: What is make/model of your router?

Does the problem occur when you are in the same room as your router ... or when you are several rooms away?

Problem could be caused by wireless interference from your neighbor's wireless networks.

If you are comfortable setting your router's wireless configuration, sign on to your router and change channel. Use either channel 1, 6, or 11.

If you search for this word:   inssider
you will find a site that offers a free utility (inSSIDer for Home) that can be installed on your Windows 7 laptop (there's also a version for Android ... if you have an Android cell phone). With inSSIDer, you can see a graphical representation of all wireless network signals in your location (i.e., in the location of the device that is running inSSIDer) and what channel they are using. You will see your wireless network (its strength and channel), and whether there are other nearby wireless networks on the same channel (and what their strengths are).

inSSIDer can help you pick a channel that has the least number of other wireless networks. but if you do not want to use it, you can just configure your router to use another channel (different from the one it currently uses) and see if that helps. You do not need to do anything to your laptop .... just change the wireless network channel number in your router.

If you do not feel comfortable changing your router's wireless channel, then first sit in the same room as your router (as close to it as possible) and see if the problem occurs. Even if you are having some interference from your neighbor's wireless networks, when you are sitting next to your router ... its signal should be strong enough to overcome any interference. So, if the problem still happens when sitting next to the router, then changing channels will most likely not help (but if the problem goes away when sitting near the router, then changing channels might help).

Another thing to try is using your laptop on some other wireless network (i.e., a friend's network, a public network, etc). If the problem happens when using some other network (and sitting as close to the router as you can), then changing channel will not likely help (but if problem goes away, changing channel might help).

There are a few other settings you could try on your router, but first try the above suggestions to see if they help.
