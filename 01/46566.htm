Over-clocking can easily be the root cause as it can damage hardware and even drivers can
have a negative impact on an over-clocked system. 1st step I would take is to remove ALL the
over-clocking which should help to determine the cause which is likely to be heat and/or hardware.

Over-clocking, especially since it increases the levels of heat on the components, can have the
same effect as running low quality oil in an engine. It will not immediately fail yet the damage done
can show up well before the normal lifespan is reached or even in-sight.

Also keep in mind that heat related does not always mean excessive heat as it could be that a
component has become too sensitive to even the normal levels of heat.

The most common heat related cause is the heat sink, the connection of the heat sink and
processor, and the compound between the heat sink and processor. The latter is particularly
suspect when a system has been over-clocked.
