gokul t say:

Hi Saleh2506,

 

You may try and remove the disk, remove the write protection (by flipping a tab on the disk), then try again.

 

If you do not have the flip on the USB drive, try the following steps to resolve the issue:

 

1. Click Start, type regedit.exe and hit ENTER.

2. Navigate to the following location:

 

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrrentControlSet\Control

 

3. Right-click Control and select New and Key.

4. Name it as StorageDevicePolicies.

5. Click StorageDevicePolicies and on the right pan right-click and select New > DWORD and name it as WriteProtect.

6. Double click on it and set its value to 0.

 

This would disable write protect on USB/flash drives.

 

Restart your computer and check if that fixes the issue.

How to back up and restore the registry in Windows
