About MSDN or TechNet Product Keys
They are genuine Microsoft product keys, they are actually retail licenses, but it is intended for a particular product channel either the Microsoft Software Developer Network (MSDN) or TechNet for IT Professionals who pay a subscription fee. The main purpose is for evaluation purposes.

The great thing about them, unlike trial Microsoft software, MSDN or TechNet keys don't expire. Because the agreement under which the subscription is provided is a single license, none of the software should be distributed outside of it. Even though its $50,000 worth of licenses, it is for one person only to use and no one else. Unfortunately, regardless of the licensing terms, persons still abuse the program, either giveaway product keys or resell it on auction sites. Microsoft licenses it in good faith that customers won't do so, but I guess human nature wins out.

In your case, what probably happened is, you bought an MSDN licensed key, which carries up to 10 activations unlike full packaged retail licenses which only carry 1 activation. The person who sold it to you probably sold it to 10 other persons. Somewhere along the way, one of those persons might have installed it on a second system, activated it, because it went past 10 activation threshold, Microsoft detected it that it was being abused and blocked the key from further use.

hairyfool say:

The authentication servers will block keys once the activation count reaches an unacceptable level, Microsoft do not publish their precise parameters as to at what point that is. Therefore the point at which it becomes blocked is somewhat variable. It could even be that the activation count has not been exceeded but the MSDN subscription has expired.

The counterfeit production of the media is very convincing, in fact on more than one occasion Chinese factories producing media for legitimate channels have been the source of illegal media through the back door. In fact of course very few legitimate MSDN license holders will even use physical media. Disks are restricted to the product type by the EI.cfg file, incidentally going back to your point about labelling the [Channel] section only allows "Retail" and "OEM", no other options are valid.

The fact remains -068- in the product ID is an MSDN Windows 7 Ultimate license and no WGA will not change the key. If you want to prove the point run a VM install using the key and check the System dialogue for the product ID without enabling the network interface. To the best of my knowledge there is no tool to enter the key and report the embedded Product ID.

As long as the key's checksum is right and the product ID decoded from the key matches the media it will allow setup to continue, setup does not check a key on line as it has no internet connection at that point. Once setup completes the grace period starts and runs until 30 days is up or activation is successful. It doesn't matter why activation fails the grace period carries on until it expires, that way it allows you to resolve the problem causing a failure within the 30 days.
