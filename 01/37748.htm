shishira d say:

 

Hello Nadim,

 

Deleting files without leaving the entry in search box is not possible, but you may rebuild your search keywords index stored by Windows Desktop Search in Registry. This will also delete the present files entry from search index.

 

If you would like to do so, then follow the steps below.

1.     Click on Start, Run, and type regedit. Press Enter.

2.     Double click on HKEY_CURRENT_USER

3.     Scroll down below and double click on Software, Microsoft, Windows, Current Version, Explorer

4.     In Explorer, click on WordWheelQuery and at the right hand side delete MRUListEx, this contains the current search keywords entered. Upon deleting it, it will be rebuilt with the latest search keywords entered Windows Desktop Search.

 

Note: - Editing registry values incorrectly may cause serious problems that may prevent your computer from booting properly. Microsoft cannot guarantee that any problems resulting from the configuring of REGISTRY settings can be solved. Modifications of these settings are at your own risk.
