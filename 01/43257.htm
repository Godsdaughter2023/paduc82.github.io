Multiple Monitor Support- Notification Area/Clock on all displays - Microsoft Community

matthew u. say:

Multiple Monitor Support- Notification Area/Clock on all displays

There is no clear way to provide constructive feedback to Microsoft about their products, so it's my hope that this discussion will receive enough attention to fulfill that need.  The lack of feedback resources is truly a shame, as Windows 8.1 has a lot of potential to be something great, and I fear that the majority of customer feedback Microsoft sees is from angry customers uncomfortable with change, and unwilling to spend 10 minutes familiarizing themselves with a Help/Tips page.
That said, I may just include the above statement in all my future posts, if an issue/product feature motivates me enough to post again.

The built-in multiple monitor support in 8.1 is a vast improvement to Windows 7, props on that.  However, the inability to display the notification center and clock on all displays, or at least the display of your choice, is more than a little inconvenient.  And frankly, a little alarming.  With all the options for the built-in support, it seems like the developers would have had to deliberately work towards not including that feature.
Anybody using dual monitors is doing so for one reason:  Multitasking.  In most scenarios, the main display is typically the better of the two (or three), and is used for full-screen applications, such as movies, games, and anything else that may hide the taskbar.  The secondary monitor is showing everything else, and is the more logical choice for the notification center placement.

Yes, I am aware that I can change a lot of settings to make full screen programs run on the secondary monitor, and make the better monitor the secondary display.  But it seems a bit backwards to have to do all that just for the convenience of seeing the clock and having access to the notification center.  Would it not make more sense, and be much simpler, to completely duplicate the primary taskbar on both displays?

In the past, I had to use third party programs, such as Display Fusion, to even have multiple taskbars.  And while Display Fusion does work on Windows 8.1, and cosmetically clones the primary taskbar, it severely limits the functionality of the second Start button.  The Windows 8 built-in second taskbar has all the same funtionality, minus the all-too-important features on the far right.

I don't know why the decision was made to not include that feature (and yes, a decision had to be made, it wasn't just an oversight), but I do really hope it arrives soon in a future update.
