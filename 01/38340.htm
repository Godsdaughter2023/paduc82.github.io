a. user say:

Nobody knows what all your Windows problems are, or what files are corrupted - or if they really are corrupted - so it's impossible to make an informed decision about whether a factory reset will help.

A factory reset returns your computer to the way it was when it left the factory. If your copy of Windows was working properly when you first brought it home, it will work properly again after a factory reset.

But you will lose all the changes you made to your computer after you first brought it home. All updates to Windows will have to be redone, all software you installed will need to be re-installed and updated and you'll have to setup your computer all over again.

Before you start you'll have to backup all your personal files (documents, music, etc.) because they will be erased during a factory reset. After the reset is complete you'll have to restore your backup.

Clearly, a factory reset is not something done lightly. A factory reset should only be considered if your computer is hopelessly messed up. It's a last resort. Only you can decide if that's case in your situation.

A factory reset won't help at all if your problem doesn't involve Windows. For example, if your problem is caused by hardware, a factory reset won't make any difference at all.
