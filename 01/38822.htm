Seems you are referring to Windows Explorer, not Windows Photo Gallery.

In Windows Explorer, right click in the white area (not in the actual photo file). This will bring up a context menu. On that context menu, click on "Sort by" -> "More...". In the "Choose Details" dialog, click on "Date Taken" so that it appears in the options.

Most Laptops have a physical switch for the Wireless card. Read the computer's manual and make sure that the Wireless card is On.

When working turn On the Wireless security.

Note* People sometimes make mistakes in writing and retyping the security passphrase.

Alternative way is to copy it from the Router's menu, and then paste when asked by the Wireless card.

it kept coming back as "undeliverable"!


The Delivery Status Notification (DSN) that you received will tell you why the message couldn't be delivered. Different servers use different methods to provide a Non-Delivery Report (NDR); it may be in the displayed part of the DSN or sent as an attachment (often with a *.dat extension), or you may have to examine the message source to find it. You're looking for a line starting Diagnostic-Code.

If the NDR is an attachment, open it with Notepad to read it. If you can't see an NDR anywhere, select the DSN message in the message list and press Ctrl-F3 to reveal the message source. Look carefully for the Diagnostic-Code in a section headed something like Content-Type: message/delivery-status. If you can't understand it, post it here so someone can help you interpret it.
