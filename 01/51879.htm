cmdrkeene say:

The SecureBoot message on the screen is not a cause for concern and will not inhibit the operation of your system, so you are safe and sound there.


This indication on the screen simply means that your computer's BIOS is currently unlocked, which allows you to install any operating system you want (you could pop in a CD-ROM and install something right now, if you wanted to).


If you want to re-lock it, you can go into your BIOS settings and change the option back.  The Asus folks should have told you that, because the computer manufacturer is the one is builds the BIOS.  Since they built it, I can't tell you precisely where to go to set that option, but it's relatively easy:  I've done it on several computers so far, including Dell and HP.  Each one was different, but if you just open the BIOS settings and look for the word "Secure Boot" it's not hard to recognize.


If you don't feel comfortable setting the option yourself, I would give Asus a call back and ask them.  Like I said, they built their own BIOS and they know where that option is, it's literally just a few clicks to change it and you'll get rid of that indicator on your desktop screen.
