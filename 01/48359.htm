bill smithers say:

Hi,

Do you have a pre-installed OEM Windows License (Dell, HP, etc) or a Retail License that you bought yourself?

If you bought your own Retail or OEM System Builders Windows 7 License and installed it yourself, yes, Product Key Finder Programs will obtain the Product Key for you.

__________________________________________

But if you mean the Sticker on the case from the preinstalled Windows 7 OEM Operating System License from Dell, HP, Acer, Toshiba, etc, Product Key Finder Programs will not find a useable Product Key suitable for reinstallation purposes.

All they will find is the Manufacturer's Product Key which was used on multiple machines at the Factory Production of your machine.

These are then the options:

 

You can contact your Computer Manufacturer and ask them to send you a set of Recovery Disks.

They should do that for a small cost.

 

And if you never received a Recovery Disk when you purchased your Computer there should be a Recovery Partition on the Hard Drive to reinstall Windows 7 back to how you bought your Computer.

The Recovery process can be started by pressing a particular Key or Keys combination at Boot. (Power On / Startup)

It may be F10, F11, Alt + F10, etc depending on the Manufacturer.

Ask them or us for the correct Key sequence.

 

And if you do have a Manufacturer's Recovery Partition on your Hard Drive, you should be able to make your own Recovery Disks from it to reinstall the Operating System.

Look under Programs > your Computer Manufacturer's name > then their System Tools or Recovery Software Headings for them, depending how it is worded.

If you can not find any reference to it, contact the Manufacturer for advice on how to make those Recovery Disks.

 

The above reinstallation methods do not normally require you to supply a Product Key during the Installation Process as the Recovery Process uses the Factory Installation Product Key to automatically Activate during the reinstallation process.

 

Cheers.
