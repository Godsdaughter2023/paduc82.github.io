Unable to extend C drive - Microsoft Community

croxford say:

Unable to extend C drive

I am trying to extend my C drive as I am running out of storage space.  I have 15gb of unallocated space available but I have 2 Healthy Partition Sections between my c dive and the unallocated memory.

Can someone please let me know how I can extend my c drive as when I use the Disk Management routine the extend option is not available

Many thanks

robertjwin say:

As mentioned above you can use any third-party partition manager. I have used EaseUS Partition Master Free successfully many times over the years.

Currently your partition layout looks something like this according to your description:

C | D | E | Unallocated Space

Using a partition manager simply shift D and E to the right so you end up with the following instead:

C | Unallocated Space | D | E

Finally extend C so it takes up all the unallocated space.

Points to note:

1) A backup is always recommended before partition editing, especially when so much data shifting is involved.

2) Make sure power supply isn't interrupted during the process.

3) Shifting D and E will definitely take time, possibly even hours, depending on your drive's speed and how much data the partitions contain that needs to be shifted.

4) To shift D and E to the right you'll have to use the left and right handles (those 2 tiny circles) shown in the Move partition dialog below:

Basically you'll have to first extend E by dragging the right handle to the right so it takes up the unallocated space, then drag the left handle to the right so E is reduced back to its original size and the unallocated space appears on its left. Now you'll have C | D | Unallocated Space | E. Next you'll have to do the exact same move operation with D to get C | Unallocated Space | D | E. Finally you can extend C to end up with C | D | E.

I hope I managed to explain it properly. There are tutorials on the site and if you have any doubts then ask before you do anything.

gary vandermolen say:
You have submitted both a send error and a receive error.
Regarding the first (send) error:
Magicmachining.com is not your ISP. As such, your home ISP will block your use of any port 25 SMTP server other than their own. For an explanation of this anti-spamming policy, see
http://www.postcastserver.com/help/Port_25_Blocking.aspx
 
The available workarounds for your issue are as follows:
 
1. Use webmail for sending (via your browser).
2. Ask the host for magicmachining.com if they have a port other than '25' for SMTP.
3. You can substitute the SMTP server belonging to your ISP. This is a bit
tricky, but if you want to go that route, I can provide more details.
 
Regarding your second (receive) error:
You are making the mistake of allowing AVG antivirus to interfere with your email. Your AVG antivirus was probably installed with its default install option, which installs an email scanning module. Just disabling its email scanning is not a sufficient remedy. Uninstall AVG, then reinstall, but select 'custom' install, which then allows you to unselect installing its troublesome email
scanning module. Even better, replace it with the free Microsoft security program:
http://www.microsoft.com/security_essentials
