a. Right-click on sound icon in the taskbar and select “Recording Devices”.
b. Highlight the microphone and select “Properties”.
c. Go to Levels tab and drag the slider to the extreme left.
d. This mutes the microphone.
By dragging the slider towards right, you may turn on the microphone volume and adjust accordingly.
Step 3:
Test the sound from Control Panel.
a. Here’s how: Right-click on sound icon in the taskbar and select “Playback Devices”.
b. Highlight on the Speaker and select “Properties”.
c. Go to “Advanced” Tab and click on Test. Is there any echo?
Step 4:
You may try disabling audio enhancements if that improves anything.
Here’s how:
a. Open Sound by clicking the Start button, and then clicking Control Panel. In the search box, type sound, and then click Sound.
b. On the Playback tab, click Speakers, and then click Properties.
c. Click the Enhancements tab, and then select the Disable all enhancements check box.
If you don't see an Enhancements tab in the Speakers Properties dialog box, those features are not available for your speakers and this procedure is not applicable to your computer.
Step 5:
If disabling audio enhancements solves the audio problem you're experiencing, this typically indicates that your audio driver needs to be updated. Check to see if an updated driver is available for your computer.
Do one of these:
• Visit the Web site of the computer manufacturer if the device came with the computer.
