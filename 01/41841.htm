An unlocked desktop for ARM based Windows devices is very much desired.

Devices like the Surface RT are designed to be convertible devices, designed for touch and keyboard mouse input, and it works great. I'm hesitant to bring it up, but the so-called "jailbreak" for Windows RT has show us that Windows ARM PCs are perfectly capable for running much more than just the bundled desktop apps. The ARM processor itself, while lacking in binary compatibility with x86, is perfectly capable of running many .NET 4 apps (I understand the default compile option is to target any cpu, but specific architectures), as well as any Win32 app recompiled for it. Why are Atom-based devices allowed an unlocked Desktop, but not ARM-based devices?? (They are very similar performance-wise.)

One of the biggest strengths of Windows has always been its flexibility, but artificial restrictions like this are a huge disappointment, and a disturbing step in the wrong direction in my opinion.
