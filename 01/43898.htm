[Moved from feedback]

considerate_guy say:

Movie Maker does not stretch or otherwise alter the original aspect ratio of your photo.  Nor is there currently a feature to have Movie Maker zoom your photo with the resulting crop to fit in the project rectangle.  You can propose that feature by going to feedback.live.com and choosing Movie Maker.

 

In the meantime, the workarounds are:

  1. Set the aspect ratio of your Project to match the aspect ratio of the majority of your photos. That usually means a 4:3 setting for your project.  Try to avoid using vertical (portrait) photos.
  2. Manually crop your photos to the same aspect ratio as your movie.  This can be done in Photo Gallery.  Double-click the photo you want to adjust which will bring up the photo edit tools.  Click the arrow below the Crop icon, then choose Proportion and finally either Widescreen (16x9) or 4 x 3.  After saving your changes, repeat with the next photos as needed.

bossdweeb say: You would likely have your monitor or graphics settings different.
I can only use Nvidia as a reference for forcing full screen, but you should be able to extrapolate to whatever graphics you are using - You use the graphics drivers Control Panel
Go to -  (Nvidia Control Panel)
Select the option - 'Adjust desktop size and position'  .
Here there are three options -

There is also  an option to 'Perform scaling on: monitor - or gpu'  .
Here you would use  'gpu'  if you want to use the options above.
Use 'monitor' if you want your monitor settings to override the Nvidia Control Panel settings.
Using 'monitor' will make everything full screen unless your monitor has a specific setting to  allow 4:3 aspect ratio.

More on aspect ratios:
These games were not designed for wide screen monitors. They use a 4:3 format.
While you can force wide screen the results may not be desirable.
Two different things can happen when forcing wide screen depending on the
game.
1) The image gets stretched sideways which distorts the image
2) The top and bottom of the image gets chopped off to make the image fit the screen without stretching.
This also has the effect of foreshortening the depth of field .
This option is not so bad for some games. Personally I prefer to stick with the 4:3 aspect ratio for many older games, but also use (on a 24" monitor) 1600x1024   which has smaller black bars than the 4:3 aspect ratio but also keeps the perspective close to original.

Aspect ratios  relate to the shape/size of the monitor and it's native resolution. 
eg - 4:3= 1024x768  /  5:4 = 1280xx1024 / 16x10 = 1920x1200  / 16:9 = 1920x1080
Again - This is only a sample and relates to a 24" monitor. Your laptop monitor
would use  some different resolutions.

16:9 is an unfortunate aspect ratio for a lot of older games as you are restricted to a max of 1280x1024  as these games often have an option of 1600x1200 which cannot be done on a monitor with a max vertical resolution of 1080 (24" monitor) so with these games 1600x1024 is a good option if the game allows it.
1680x1050 can be good  with some games as well.
btw - I use 1280x1024 with Diablo 2 and have black bars top and sides.

If you don't already have it, the mod in the link below is very handy for Diablo 2 -

Glide3-to-OpenGL-Wrapper
