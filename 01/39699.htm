hairyfool say:

Li-Ion batteries do not suffer the same memory effect as traditional NiCad so many of the issues they used to suffer from no longer apply.

 

They do however have a slightly more complex charging requirement which seems detrimental but in fact what it results in is proper charge management electronics being designed into the system instead of just applying a voltage to charge them. The net result is they are less likely to suffer overcharging in a device than used to be the case.

 

In fact whereas with original NiCad which the recommendation was to almost fully discharge and recharge at intervals this is not required with Li-Ion and is potentially detrimental to the life of the battery. The primary thing is as with all rechargeable battery technologies they should not be left in a discharged state.

b.      You should get a success message back.

c.       Close the elevated command prompt.

d.      Restart the computer to apply.

 

Note: Once you are done with Step 1 and Step 2, try to add the program to Data Execution Prevention and check if it helps to open the game or not.
