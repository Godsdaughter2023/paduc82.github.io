      Uhm... I think the issue here is a misunderstanding of what you are looking at.

      If you want access to your files from the old hard drive - you need to look in the correct place.  If the old system was "Windows 7", "Documents and Settings" is the *wrong* place to look and "Users" would be the place to go.

      You *will* be taking ownership and changing the permissions on EVERYTHING (you will want to propogate those to all subfolders/files when doing it - to save time) in order to get to them properly.

      Again --> Windows Vista/7 would have your files located in the "Users" directory from the root of the drive - then under your username.

      • \USERS\<yourOLDusername>\Desktop\
      • \USERS\<yourOLDusername>\Downloads\
      • \USERS\<yourOLDusername>\Favorites\
      • \USERS\<yourOLDusername>\Documents\
      • \USERS\<yourOLDusername>\Music\
      • \USERS\<yourOLDusername>\Pictures\
      • \USERS\<yourOLDusername>\Videos\

      ... etc ...

      Documents and settings on WIndows Vista/Windows 7 is a symbolic link and would result in what you are seeing if you attempted to use it.
