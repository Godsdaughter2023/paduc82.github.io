Change your gamertag through a web browser

Here’s how:

  1. Go to the Change gamertag page at account.xbox.com.
  2. Sign in using the Microsoft account email address and password associated with your gamertag.
  3. Enter your new gamertag, and then select Check Availability.
  4. Once you find an available gamertag, click Claim it.
    Note If your gamertag was created for you when you initially signed up for Xbox (meaning you did not choose it yourself), you can change it one time for free. Additional Xbox gamertag changes cost extra.
    If you created your own gamertag at signup and want to change it, you will be charged (fee varies by region and currency).
Change your gamertag on Xbox One

Note Xbox One may not be available in all locales.

  1. Press the Xbox button  to open the guide.
  2. Select your gamerpic in the upper-left corner of the screen, and then press the A button  on your controller
  3. Select My profile, and then Customize profile. Select your gamertag to edit it.
  4. You can either select one of the suggested available gamertags, or select Make my own to create your own:
    • If you’re making your own, type in the new gamertag, and then select Enter. If you receive a “Sorry, this gamertag is now reserved. Try another one” message, that means the gamertag is not available and you need to create another one.

  5. When you're finished, select Claim it, and then select Sounds good.
  6. Once you see your gamertag, select Close.

After you confirm your new gamertag, the change is reflected across Xbox, and if there is an additional cost, the payment option associated with your Microsoft account is charged. The method of changing the file name extension of your file requires you to disable the Hide extensions for known file types in the Folder Options settings on a Windows operating system computer. Once you have disabled the said setting, you just need to rename the file extension.

  1. Launch the File Explorer.
  2. Click on the View tab.

  7. Click on the OK button.
