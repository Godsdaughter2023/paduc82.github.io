ferdz amp say:

Hello TeckyWilson,

Windows firewall helps keep your computer more secure. It restricts information that comes to your computer from other computers and gives you more control over the data on your computer. For this to work, you need to properly set up the forwarding rules on your router to your system. Then follow the steps below to allow incoming Port 80 connections from 3 IP addresses:

  1. In the run dialog box, type in wf.msc and press Enter to open the Windows Firewall with Advanced Security interface.
  2. In the left pane, click on the Inbound Rules.
  3. Click on the New Rule...
  4. Under the New Inbound  Rule Wizard, click Custom.
  5. Choose the appropriate options for your case. All programs or This program path.
  6. In this step, fill up the correct protocol type and number.
  7. Under Scope, you can input the IP address of the machines that are allowed to connect.
  8. Under Action, choose Allow the connection option.
  9. Next, check the options on which profile this rule with take effect.
  10. In this step, you need to provide the name for this rule, then click Finish.

After setting up, this setting will apply on new incoming connections.

lewishilary say:

To the person who also wanted to know how to change the Full Backup drive destination, I have stumbled upon the solution.

On the Backup and Restore window (Control Panel>System and Security>Backup and Restore) the last item in the Backup section is called 'Schedule', and under 'Schedule' is 'Change Settings'. This does not refer to scheduling but is, in fact, where the Full Backup destination drive is changed. When clicking 'Change Settings', do not be alarmed that the system tells you that it is starting a backup, after a few seconds, the window that allows the user to change the destination drive appears.
