a. user say:

With a name like 'System Restore,' who wouldn't think it's a backup?

Actually, a restore point is a collection of crucial system files, drivers and registry hives that can restore the essential functions of Windows, to give you the ability to use Windows' own tools or a third-party program to work yourself out of a jam. Sometimes, simply restoring a restore point is enough to bring a damaged PC back to life.

But that's not a backup. In the first place, it only restores the essential functioning of Windows, not the entire operating system, and it doesn't restore your applications or your personal files at all. When System Restore was introduced, in Windows Me, backing up was something for geeks, so System Restore was a lifesaver.

Today backing up is simple and there are lots of great backup programs, both free and paid. You can backup two ways:

1- backup individual files and folders, like your personal folder (Documents, Music, etc.); and

2-backup an entire hard drive partition, like C:, in one shot. That's called an image backup.

Backing up is a big topic - there's too much to cover for a post in a community forum. But there's lots of great information on the internet, which is how I learned about it.
