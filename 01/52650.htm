dobuya say:

The difference between opening ports and forwarding ports is an advanced question but I will try to explain.

First off, most consumer level gateway/router/firewall devices will link the two, so that when a port is forwarded it is also opened, or vice versa.

Forwarding the port is related to routing network traffic, whereas opening the port is related to firewall rules.

In more advanced firewalls and gateway routers you can do one without doing the other.  

When forwarding a port, you are telling your gateway device that when it receives traffic on a certain port (eg. 3074/TCP) to route that traffic on to your XBox.

Opening the port is telling the firewall component of your gateway device to allow the traffic that matches the firewall rule you created to pass from the internet to your XBox.

The same can be said for outbound traffic.  Typically your devices inside your network (your PC, your XBox, etc) are configured with a "Default Gateway" also known as the "default route" to send traffic that is destined for locations not on your network.  The default gateway is typically the next hop out towards the internet, usually your gateway router/firewall device.

You don't need to set up port forwards for outbound traffic, because by default the devices and routers know which way is "out" and can already do that.  You do however need to make sure that if there is a firewall built into your gateway router that it is configured to allow traffic to port 80/TCP on remote servers.  Note that 80/TCP is the standard port for web pages (HTTP) and therefore is likely already "open" outbound.

My question to you would be, when you log into your WDR3600, if you see the WAN and LAN IPs, do either or both start with anything like "10." or "192.168." or "172.16." (could be anything from 172.16.X.X to 172.31.X.X)?

If both WAN and LAN IPs are in the above ranges then you might be behind a double NAT.  If the WAN IP is NOT one of those then you are unlikely to be behind a double NAT.
