The capacity is fine - lasts for ages - but the charge rate is ridiculously slow! It's probably about 5% per hour, so yes, it takes many hours to fully charge it from empty. My old 6-cell used to charge at nearly 1% per minute by comparison.

The other day, I clean installed Windows 7 (was running the RC before this, which is where I was first having the slow charge problem), and I noticed that my battery indicator said "plugged in, not charging". Apparently this is a known problem with some Dell models however after a bit of searching around I managed to get it to charge again, only this time it was as fast as it *should* be, at nearly 1% per minute! I have since installed Battery Bary, but unfortunately, at that stage I didn't have Battery Bar installed so I couldn't tell what the actual charge rate in mW was, but my problem now is that it has gone back to the slow charge rate. Right now it is indicating about 36,500 mW. The capacity is about 18,500 out of 62,130mWh (~30% charged) and the battery wear is 5.9% of 66,000mWh.

This is the series of events over the last couple of days that caused it to revert back to the slow charge rate:

- After finding that my battery would not charge at all (clean install of Windows 7, released ed.), I tried going into Device Manager and uninstalling the "Microsoft ACPI-Compliant Control Method Battery", then ran a "Scan for hardware changes". It immediately picked it up and installed the drivers. This did not make the battery charge.

- Turned off the laptop, took out the battery, turned it back on and it registered that there was no battery present.

- Turned off the laptop, reinserted the battery, turned it back on, and now it had registered that there was a battery there, and from about 70% capacity, it charged to 100% in around 30 mins (correct charge rate of approx. 1%/minute). I left it plugged in overnight.

- Used the laptop the next day, drained it to probably around 60%. At work, I put it on a different (smaller, 60W) adapter, still Dell branded, but not 90W like my one at home. I've noticed that if I turn on the laptop with this adapter, it will say that the battery may charge more slowly, however with my old battery, with the laptop off, this didn't seem to affect the charge rate, so I presume it should be the same with the "fake" 9-cell. The laptop was not turned on all day, but I did notice that the charge light took a few hours to turn off, which means that at that point, the charge rate was back to being slow.

- Used the laptop again, down to about 60%. Got it home, used it again down to about 20% battery. Turned off the laptop.

- Turned on the laptop once more, plugged in with the original 90W adapter, and it is still charging slowly. It has taken a good 2 hrs to charge from 20% to 30%, so it's definitely charging slowly.

What could be causing this? I was thinking that perhaps it's because I used the 60W adapter which may have trigged something in the battery (?) to revert back to a slow charge, but AFAIK the charge rate is controlled by Windows? As mentioned above, the laptop was never turned on while using the 60W adapter so it might be something else?

What can I do to get around this problem? Should I uninstall/reinstall the drivers for the "Microsoft ACPI-Compliant Control Method Battery" once more? I'm a bit reluctant to do this just in case it doesn't work for some reason and I'm stuck again not being able to recharge my battery.

I'm also fairly certain that it was this process that caused it to charge quickly again, as I had previously tried removing and replacing the battery many times with no difference in charge times (under Windows 7 RC). I should also mention that it was while Windows 7 RC was installed that I swapped from the 6-cell to the 9-cell battery. I am now running a totally clean Windows 7 final ed. installation.

Any ideas are much appreciated!

melchizedek qui say:

Hello Alex,

In line with your concern, there is no option in the File Explorer settings to disable the automatic sorting/arrange of files and folders. Though, there is a workaround that will require you to edit the Registry to disable auto sorting:

1. Press Windows key + R, then type in regedit and press Enter.

2. In the Registry window, navigate to this location:

HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Classes\Local Settings\Software\Microsoft\Windows\Shell

3. Right-click on the Bags subkey and select Delete.

4. Navigate to this key:

HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Windows\Shell

5. Right-click on the Bags subkey and select Delete.

6. Lastly, navigate to this key:

HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Windows\ShellNoRoam

7. Right-click on the Bags subkey and select Delete.

8. Reboot your computer. It should now allow you to arrange your files and folders freely.

Note: The method to fix the issue will have Registry modification. Before you modify the Registry, make sure to back it up and understand how to restore the registry if a problem occurs. Please refer to this link: How to back up and restore the registry in Windows.

Looking forward to your response.
