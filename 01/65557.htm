Hello 1Startdancer,

 

My apologies. I assumed (wrongly) that the photos had been stored in the Wallpaper folder. It looks as if the repairer has set the photos  'as desktop background.'  To do this the photos must be stored in the 'photo' folder. If you already have other copies of these photos stored elsewhere the best option is to delete all of these photographs from the 'photo' folder. Once you have done this go to display properties (right click on desktop and select properties, then in the properties window, select desktop). Once you see the list of desktop wallpaper, select one that naturally belongs to Windows (do not select any of the pictures you want to get rid of) and then click Apply. Once you have selected a windows desktop and the desktop has refreshed, close the display properties and then open the display properties again. The pictures that you have deleted should now no longer be there.

Change screen resolution

 

Screen resolution refers to the clarity of the text and images on your screen. At higher resolutions, items appear sharper. They also appear smaller, so more items fit on the screen. At lower resolutions, fewer items fit on the screen, but they are larger and easier to see. At very low resolutions, however, images might have jagged edges.
