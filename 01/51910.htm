Let me correct/clarify a few responses since this old thread has been reopened.

First, Windows Live Movie Maker (at least in the latest release) now does have the ability to speed up/slow down the movie.  You simply select a video clip and then go to the Video Tool | Edit tab.  There's a choice for Speed going from 0.125 (1/8 = 8 times slower) up to 64x (64 times faster).

As for the prior versions of Movie Maker (including the most recent version WMM 6.0) you can add up to 6 effects.  In the case of "Slow Down, Half" or "Speed Up, Double" you could apply those 6 times which results in a factor of 64.  There are also custom speed effects you can install to get speed changes other than powers of 2 (e.g. 3x, 4x, 6x)

The problem you describe is cause by a bus reset generated as a direct result of enabling (or plugging-in or powering on) another FireWire™ device.  After a reset, all devices are re-enumerated (as you have observed).  There are a number of Microsoft Knowledge Base articles available.  Go to http://support.microsoft.com and searching for 1394.  Here are some of the most interesting:

KB307736 - Description of 1394 Connection in Windows XP
