davem121 say:

Please Note: I do understand why you want to turn off Automatic Updates, but I have to warn you if you do turn off automatic updates, the following are some of the consequences:

  1. Windows will be more vulnerable to attacks from malware, ransom-ware like WannaCrypter . . . etc.
  2. Your drivers will not be updated
  3. Defender will not be updated
  4. New features and fixes released for windows will not be applied

If you really want to turn off Automatic Updates, here is how you do it:

  1. Press your Start Button, type services.msc and hit enter
  2. Scroll down the list of services to find Windows Update
  3. Double click the service to open its properties
  4. Click 'Stop' if the service has already started
  5. Set Startup to Disabled - it must be disabled, manual is not the same as disabled
  6. Restart your system

On a more personal note, the positives to turning off Automatic updates include:

  1. Less Data usage if like me you are on a Pay-as-You-Go Mobile Plan
  2. I can download whichever updates I need from the Microsoft Catalog Page
  3. I never get BSOD's or black screens
  4. My device drivers are the ones I choose to install rather than ones Windows 10 Updates installs that don'r work
  5. My system is totally stable and reliable . . .

frederik long say:

When I click C:/Users it brings up 'Guest' & 'Public' icons but not my administrator user account icon.

However, when I go into Control Panel/User Accounts, I only get my administrator user account icon & not Guest or Public.

How can I get my administrator icon back into everyday use?

The items you see under C:\Users are called "Profile Folders".

The items you see in the Control Panel\User Accounts are called "Accounts".

In most cases each account has a profile folder. It is often spelt the same way but does not have to be.

It is possible to have a profile folder without an associated user account.

It is also possible to have a user account without an associated profile folder.

In your case I recommend you report what your actual problem is, e.g.

  • What account name do you log on under?
  • Are you missing any files or folders?
  • Did you change anything recently?
