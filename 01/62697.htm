Yep.

Open the Xbox Accessories app, click your Elite controller, and head to button mapping.  Choose the "Left Stick Click" or "Right Stick Click" as the button to map, and map it to "Unmapped." - it's the last option in the list.  That will make it so the click does nothing.  You might want to also map that function to one of the paddles in the back if it's a command you need in game.

internet expert say:

Microsoft has always recommended that you plug the Xbox directly into the wall outlet, since the power supplies in the consoles always had built-in surge protection. 


On the other hand, many people plug it into surge protectors or power strips anyway, and mostly have no problem with this. Those who did have a problem usually observed that their Xbox would randomly turn itself on, or at the worst, the console would shut itself off while playing.


If you ever encounter a problem, you can first try returning it to the store for an exchange. Otherwise, you can always set up a repair with Microsoft. Any additional assistance, you can contact Xbox Support by choosing phone or chat.

sc tom say:

Not that I'm aware of; services doesn't list anything that's depending on it, not even Apple.

You could set it to Disabled instead of Manual, and the next time you start a program that's dependent on it, you'll get an error that the Bonjour service is not running. The only reason I have it set to Manual instead of Disabled is that I occasionally connect my iPod to load new songs. If not for that, I'd have it Disabled, just to see what else uses it.

 SC Tom
