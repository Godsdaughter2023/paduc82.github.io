a. user say:

Hi James,

I will be glad to help you with the issue you have with your computer.

The Automatic Maintenance normally runs at a time when the computer is idle. When it finds that the computer is in use at a time scheduled for the automatic maintenance, then it does not start automatically and instead it waits for the next time when the computer is idle.

 

It is apparently not possible to disable it completely. You could at least disable it from waking your computer.

Steps:

a. Click on the Action center flag on the right hand bottom corner to enter Action center.

b. Expand Maintenance and click on Change maintenance settings.

c. Uncheck the option which says allow computer to wake up my computer at the scheduled time

d. Set the Automatic maintenance Schedule time to a time when the computer is powered off / not in use

Note: The task will remain enabled, but with triggers disabled, it won't actually start.

 

If the computer is running slow, I would suggest you to follow the steps given in the Microsoft KB article:

Ways to improve your PC's performance

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows-8/improve-pc-performance

¡firedog say:
... the messages are deleting from the server...   I can't get the messages deleted from the windows live mail laptop gmail, all mail, imap folder

All mail is just that - all messages, including ones marked Deleted. They will be visible in All mail until they're purged. You don't need to see All mail in IMAP, because it's just a repeat of what you see in the other folders.

It might be an idea to review all your Gmail account settings. These are suggestions only; you may find on experimentation that different settings work better for you: 

In Gmail,
  • Adjust the account settings
    Go to https://mail.google.com and log in. Click the settings cog  and select Settings.
    1. Under Forwarding and POP/IMAP, select Auto-Expunge Off
    2. Under Labels, select the labels you want to see in Windows Live Mail. These will treated as if they were separate folders, so fewer is usually better to avoid duplication. In particular, you don't want All mail (which is just that: all messages in all folders). I have opted to hide Starred and Important also.
    3. Make a note of the names of what will become special folders. I see Sent mail, Drafts, Spam and Bin.
In Windows Live Mail,
  1. Reset the list of IMAP folders
    Click on the account name in the folder pane and then press Alt-S F to open the Show/hide IMAP folders dialogue. Click Reset List to refresh the folder list, then use the buttons to show only the folders you want to see.
  2. Map the local special folders to the IMAP labels on the server
    Right-click on the account name in the folder pane and select Properties. On the IMAP tab, select Store special folders on IMAP server,  then enter the server's names for the special folders in the boxes. If sent messages in webmail is called Sent Mail, then enter Sent Mail into the box alongside Sent Items path and so on. It's important to be clinically accurate with folder names - Sent Mail is not the same as Sent mail.
  3. Make Deleted items folders work the same way for all accounts
    When viewing your message list, press Ctrl-Shift-O for Options. On the Advanced tab, select Use the 'Deleted Items' folder for IMAP accounts.

Windows 10 very slow, with lenovo laptop, and with antivirus McAfee. - Microsoft Community

matiasklein say:

Windows 10 very slow, with lenovo laptop, and with antivirus McAfee. (Problem solved!!!)

Dear all,

I wish to share a possible solution when the Lenovo laptop is running slow (for non laptop user I recommend to read this anyway because  perhaps it could apply to other devices).

Within the first week of purchase of Lenovo laptop, my first experience was not good, but it improved when problem was solved.

Problem: The thing is, that when starting, I read a recommendation of saving battery use to avoiding battery deterioration and the eventual need of battery hardware replacement, which I had to do with a netbook not long ago. Because of that recommendation I hit the button  "BATTERY SAVER". By doing this, the computer slowed down almost 80%. I didn't realize this had happened, because I was simultaneously downloading software I needed, Microsoft office included amongst the software installed.

However, the slow down was not obvious at all times and with all software. The slow down was intermittent (for moments the Lenovo laptop ran fast specially without using internet, then slow, and then extremely slow specially when using internet).

Throughout the course of my attempts of solving the problems, I signed into Lenovo, then I signed into Intel, also I had signed into Microsoft. McAfee recommended me to update drivers. When searching for support on internet, I received very fast windows links that sent me to prepaid support, which said "Microsoft partners" and strongly recommended prepaid drivers updates, which I declined, because I considered that a new laptop should have online free advice of the manufacturer. Eventually, I found and received free support from  two Microsoft technicians , one guided me on my laptop to run antimalware search running mrt.exe after pressing the windows button+r, and running the full scan. Also, another support agent from Microsoft ran something named "windows imaging" or something like that and asked me to restart the laptop. All this improved the performance of the Lenovo laptop, but the slow speed problem performance still continued. I ran Lenovo quick optimizer, but it didn't solve the problem, apparently it didn't consider the power plan usage within the check system. I checked possible updates, there were no new updates according to my system info. Also I ran  troubleshooting software, but the problem wasn't entirely solved.

Finally: After continuing to check the Laptop on different days (because it was still unacceptably slow despite the help and solutions I had happily received), I discovered the "battery saver" was affecting not only when running the battery, but also when laptop was plugged to the power source and charging, because that setting called "battery saver" tries to avoid the wearing out of the laptop battery, at all times: for example it charges battery only up to 50% though having the laptop plugged permanently to power source and also keeps the brightness low, and also slows the software running, all this with the laptop plugged to power source, and when unplugging it got even worst. So the SOLUTION WAS: GOING INTO SETTINGS, THEN SYSTEM, THEN POWER AND SLEEP, ADITIONAL POWER SETTINGS> THEN CLIKING UPON THE OPTION OF POWER PLAN: "BALANCED (RECOMENDED)".  So my recommendation is in the case of laptop running slow (also non laptop devices): check the power and sleep options and consider not using power saver recommendations, and it is always recommended to check for malware.

Hope this will help, its the sole reason of shearing this experience.

ByeBye!!!
