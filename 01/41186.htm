Hi,

If you encounter issues connecting an Xbox One Wireless Controller on your Windows 10 PC via Bluetooth, it may be because of an incorrect device configuration. Another factor that we have to take into consideration is an outdated Bluetooth driver. We suggest running the Bluetooth troubleshooter on your Windows 10 PC. Here are the steps:

  1. Select the Start button, then select Settings > Update & security > Troubleshoot.
  2. Under Find and fix other problems, select Bluetooth > Run the troubleshooter.
  3. Restart your device so that the changes will take effect.
  4. Disconnect then reconnect the controller and check if the same issue occurs.

We also recommend verifying if the Bluetooth device shows on the Device Manager. To do so, kindly perform the following steps:

  1. In the search box on the taskbar, type Device Manager, and select it from the list of results.
  2. In Device Manager, select View > Show hidden devices.
  3. In Device Manager, select View > Devices by connection.
  4. Find your Bluetooth hardware.

For additional troubleshooting steps, refer to How to connect and troubleshoot Bluetooth on your Xbox One Wireless Controller. The details on that page will guide you how to connect the controller via Bluetooth as well as troubleshooting methods you may perform if you encounter issues with the Bluetooth connection.

sc tom say:

First thing you need to do is uninstall the printer and any software that was installed with it.
*Unplug the printer from the PC. If it's connected wirelessly, turn the printer off.
*From the Charm bar, go to Settings > Control Panel > Programs and Features and uninstall anything that is listed as "HP OfficeJet J4680" or similar.
*Reboot

 

After you're back up and running, download
A) The "HP Printer Install Wizard for Windows" <http://h10025.www1.hp.com/ewfrf/wc/softwareDownloadIndex?softwareitem=mp-115766-2&cc=us&dlc=&lc=en&os=4132&product=3692888&sw_lang=> and run it, following its instructions; or,

 

B) The Full Feature Driver from here <http://h10025.www1.hp.com/ewfrf/wc/softwareDownloadIndex?softwareitem=mp-109973-1&cc=us&dlc=&lc=en&os=4132&product=3692888&sw_lang=>
Run the installation. Somewhere during the install, you will be prompted to either plug the printer in and turn it on, or just turn it on for a wireless discovery. Do NOT plug it in or turn it on until prompted or the installation may not complete correctly.
Once it's done, reboot whether it says to or not (just for good measure). Now you should be able to print to your heart's content :-)

 

I would try A) first, since it'll give you step-by-step instruction on what to do.


If you have any problems, post back.

 SC Tom
