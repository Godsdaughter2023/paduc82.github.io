trevor j lawrence say:

As I see it you are being really over the top about worrying about sharing stuff.  As Entegy says, nothing gets shared unless you say so.  Your comment "W8 apps might share things like android apps, location, name, etc." is not a correct interpretation of the situation.  Modern apps are actually much more strictly controlled about what they are allowed to do than old style desktop apps that can basically do anything they like.  A modern app must declare in its manifest what resources and services it intends to use.  For the more sensitive of these, when you install the app you are asked to confirm that the app is allowed to use these resources (e.g. location).  Using a resource like location is not the same thing as sharing your location.  An app can use your current location to provide relevant information to you without ever sharing your location with anyone.


Also, deleting all the apps possible will save only a tiny bit of disk space, and save absolutely NO CPU.  The apps don't have to be on your start screen, so all you'll achieve is an annoyance some time in the future when you change your mind and have to re-install an app.


So my advice is, unless you work for NSA :), chill out about sharing, let the machine take care of your security, and enjoy your computer.
