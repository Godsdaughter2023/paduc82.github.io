What you don't say is whether your current driver is Microsoft Windows "Approved" / "Certified". As far as my own personal opinion goes, I'm a firm believer in "If it ain't broke, don't fix it.". I've seen too many otherwise good products (video cards, sound cards, etc.) get trashed because someone downloaded and installed a new driver over one that was working just fine.

 

As long as your machine is working well, leave well enough alone would be my advice.

 

Let me know what you decide.
