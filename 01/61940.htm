cmdrkeene say:

There's a complex set of several algorithms that determine what gets shown there.  It's essentially a "high score" system, where every time you open a program from a shortcut, it gets a point.

  • Once enough points are reached, it shows on the menu. But points fall off over time, so if you stop using a program, eventually it will fall off the list.  But since customers generally don't like the program list changing very often, the points fall off slower than they build up for something new, so once an icon makes it on the list, it can take a long time to get de-prioritized enough to fall away.

  • In addition, there is no sorting among the list. Something listed in 4th place will never move up to 1st place, even if you use it more often. This is because again of the "users don't want to keep guessing where the icon will be today", so once it shows on the list: it stays in that position until the user decides change it or it loses enough points to disappear completely.

How can you take control over the process?  Easy: right-click.

  1. To remove something (remove all its points), right-click and "remove from this list"
  2. To pin something and keep it easily accessible regardless of points, right-click and "Pin to start"
