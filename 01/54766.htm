Thanks for your Patience.

Following are the points determined by the escalation team:

  1. The Windows 10 DVD Player app does not natively support media streaming by design.
  2. The only way the DVD can be "streamed" wirelessly to a TV is through connecting the TV as a wireless display. 
  3. The only way this is possible is if the TV supports Miracast. If the TV is not Miracast-enabled, the user will not be able to stream DVD's.

Mira cast is a streaming protocol that lets users mirror content from their Windows PCs/Mobiles to their TV screens.

If your TV is Miracast enabled, Please follow the below steps to cast a DVD to a Miracast-compatible TV:

 1. Open the Action Center by clicking on the notification icon next to the date and time in the lower right hand corner of the screen.

 3. The computer will now search for Miracast-enabled displays.

 4. If a Miracast-enabled device is in range, it will show up on the list, click to connect.

 5. The computer should now be casting the screen to TV.

 6. Open the Windows DVD Player app and load a DVD into the disk drive.

 7. The app should now auto-recognize the DVD and it should begin playing.

Try to enter Safe Mode to repair Windows.
Interrupt the normal boot process of your Windows 10 PC
If Windows 10 fails to boot normally three times over, the fourth time it enters by default in an Automatic Repair mode. Using this mode, you can boot into Safe Mode. To trigger the Automatic Repair mode, you must interrupt the normal boot process three consecutive times: use the reset or the power button on your Windows 10 PC to stop it during boot, before it finishes loading Windows 10. If you use the power button, you might have to keep it pressed for at least 4 seconds to force the power off

With a reset, all of your Installed applications are lost, but you get an option to save your files if you want. If a system image from the OEM exists-called a recovery partition, then it also gives you an option to restore it. It would make your PC load to default factory settings.
