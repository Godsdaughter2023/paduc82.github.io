a. user say:

With all due respect, it's only cosmetic. Upper case and lower case, or however else your name is represented or misrepresented, doesn't affect the operation of your computer at all.

If this is something that's really going to bother you, the easiest way to fix it is to create a new user account that looks how you want it to look. It's certainly possible to change your name, but it's complicated and messy, because your user name is spread out in dozens of different places.

Once you have a new user account setup, move all your data from the old account to the new account (like this: old account ---> Public account ---> new account) but don't delete the old account yet. You may still come across your old username, and you may need your old account to deal with that. After a few months of regular computer use you can probably delete the old account.

The system generates a fault, which normally indicates that the system looks for data in the paging file. In this circumstance, however, the missing data is identified as being located within an area of memory that cannot be paged out to disk. The system faults, but cannot find, the data and is unable to recover. Faulty hardware, a buggy system service, antivirus software, and a corrupted NTFS volume can all generate this type of error.
