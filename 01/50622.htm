I'm really sorry to say it does sound like that was the event that took place.  You were logged into a temporary profile, likely because of some benign thing like your antivirus was still scanning your profile data file when you logged in and it couldn't access the data.  If you had simply rebooted at that point, you'd likely have been back to normal without an issue.

Unfortunately, it sounds like you unintentionally dragged your files from their safe home (such as C:\Users\Kim\Documents) into the temporary profile's folders instead, thus losing them immediately when you logged out.

A side note about the libraries:  I won't get into the weeds, but your Documents Library, is your Documents Folder. They are (by default) one and the same.  That is, if you drag something to the Documents library, the file is actually stored in the Documents folder. And it works vice-versa: anything in your Documents folder will show up in the Documents library. The library is just a handy shortcut, the files area actually in the Documents folder.

But at this point, you could try a data recovery tool like Recuva or take the computer to a specialist in data recovery.

My advice to you is to always make sure you have a backup. This could be manual (making a copy every week on a flash drive or another hard drive), or automatic (the backup program built-in or another).  Or the easy way: install a sync service like OneDrive, Drop Box, Google Drive, or Apple iCloud and keep all your files in that folder.  Then if your computer crashes or burns in a fire, all your files are still safely backed up all the time, without you having to have remembered to do it ahead of time.
