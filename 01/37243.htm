The "busy cursor" shadow is occasionally corrupted after - Microsoft Community

emkay1001 say:

The "busy cursor" shadow is occasionally corrupted after waking up computer from sleep

Hello,

Nothing big but for the sake of bug reporting:

The "busy cursor" shadow is occasonally corrupted after waking up the computer from sleep in Windows 8.1. This also happened on Windows 8, as far as I remember. I've noticed this on two laptops - one Sony VAIO (nearly 1 year old laptop) with ATI Radeon graphic card (latest Windows Update driver) and one 5 year old laptop with NVIDIA GeForce 8600M GT (latest manufacturer driver). Changing graphic card driver has no effect.

The corruption looks as follows: while the busy cursor is animating ("spinning") the cursor's shadow appears to be thicker every 1,2 second or so.

The corruption is not visible when taking screenshots.

To resolve: go to Control Panel -> Mouse -> Pointers -> uncheck "Enable pointer shadows" -> Click "Apply" and then check "Enable pointer shadows" again and click Apply. It just gets midly annoying to do on long running laptops (that are put to hibernation/sleep rather than shutdown)

The problem appears on two completely different hardware platforms that I have so this could be Microsoft Windows issue
