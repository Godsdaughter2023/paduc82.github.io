stanimal21 say:

Copying data from one location to another, whether it's another drive or another location on the same drive, can take varying amounts of time depending on the file sizes and the quantity of files. For example 1GB of data in a single file will copy much faster than 1GB of data spread between 1,000 files (the same concept applies to transferring files over the internet). With that in mind, the files in the OS partition could be smaller on average compared to the files on the data partition, which is why you see such slower throughput on the OS partition. This isn't necessarily attributed to a slow computer, it's just the fundamentals of file systems.
