"55 degree's Celsius before playing BF-3"

You really need to know what the temps  reach while playing the game.

- I'm not familiar with Sigma psu's but it looks like (at Newegg) a cheap low quality power supply.
A cheap 'high wattage' psu is not a bargain, and gaming machines in particular, need a good
quality power supply.
BF3 and similar games stress a machine much more than normal desktop computing and internet.
This stress requires a stable supply of power.

Your Sigma 700W (800W peak) has 20 amps on each of the two +12V rails. This equals 480W .
Most good quality power supplies will have very near the Watts advertised on the label on
the +12V rail/s alone.
But equally important, is the components (capacitors, coils, etc) in cheap psu's don't last.
This can result in unstable current production and overheating of the psu.
Overheating and unstable current can lead to random reboots, BSOD and shutdowns, and
potential for damage to other components (that cost more than a good psu).
Again, this is more likely when gaming than doing non system intensive computing.

Now, I'm not saying  that you should rush out an buy a new psu, but it is a serious contender.
With some things you do get what you pay for. PSU's are one of those.
I tend to be a bit of a cheap psu bigot. Particularly if I can't find a proper review for a brand.
Personally, there is no way I would risk loosing other expensive components to a cheap psu.

AMD recommends a minimum 500W quality power supply for the 7950. Most gamers will add
another 50+W additional overhead (but good 550W psu's are a bit scarce so 600W is good too).

Some examples of quality psu's -
Corsair  600W w/ 46Amps (approx 550W) on a single +12V rail

OCZ 600W    w/ 25Amps (approx 600W) on 2 +12V rails

Seasonic 550W   w/ 45Amps on a single +12V rail

mike hall... always say:

Browsing and download speeds are tied to the speed of the server on which the pages and files are situated, and some servers are better than others.
