When your router is able to do UPnP, do NOT use manual portforwarding, unless UPnP is implemented badly.

Additionally, port forwarding the same port to two different IP adresses should normally not be possible. It makes no sense to port forward anything to your Gateway's IP address 192.168.1.1, as the router gets the packets "from the outside" anyways. If the router now hits the rule that tells it to forward incoming packets for example on port 3074 to 192.168.1.1, the packet will be sent to the router's internal network adaptor.

Imagine your router being the mail department for a large office building. You are working in the Xbox department, and are awaiting packets labeled with "Xbox Live". So your current rules tell the post office in your building:

Every packet with Xbox Live traffic should be directed to Floor 21 to the hands of "Skrbism" (forward to 192.168.1.23). WAIT! NO! Scratch that. Just leave the Xbox Live packets right at the mail department at the front desk to the hands of Mr. Smith (192.168.1.1).

To resolve your problem, it is important to know if the Xbox One is your ONLY console, and if UPnP properly works on your router. It seems, that the Xbox One console does NOT request to open the UPnP ports, if it is NOT in power saving mode (orange LED on power brick). If you have the console in the power state so it downloads updates and can be turned on by voice, it might be that UPnP isn't properly called to open the proper ports.

If UPnP doesn't work even if the console shuts down, make sure that the console is correctly set up to have a static IP at 192.168.1.23. If the IP address changes for whatever reason, the router will forward the Live packets to the wrong IP. And remove the port forwarding rules to the 192.168.1.1 address.
