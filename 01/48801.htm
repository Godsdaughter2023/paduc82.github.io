chaznet say: I just got one and experienced the same questions.  It's a very slick, innovative little gizmo -- but it's very overpriced considering what's 'under the hood'.

It takes 4-5 hours to charge from a fully discharged state.  You can tell when it's complete as it goes from a blinking white light to a steady-white.  Would be nice if the color of the LED was different for power management.  It does go red when it's nearly out, and blinking red when completely discharged.  Solid-white has the dual role of also meaning the power is on; should turn green to indicate charging is compete -- for $60, they could've sprung for a 3rd LED color.

Microsoft makes no mention of battery life on the product page, or the product documentation page.  My experience has been that it runs about 6 hours of continuous use.  A standard Bluetooth keyboard (which is what this accessory turns your Surface keyboard into) get's much longer battery life -- understandable, there's a lot more room for battery size in a keyboard as opposed to our little black magic wand ;-)

Microsoft opted to use the antiquated NiMH battery technology in this device, a huge disappointment.  NiMH batteries take longer to charge, and lose their charge at a high rate even when the devices they power are shut off.  A NiMH battery will quickly discharge even if it's taken out of a device.  NiMH doesn't do 'stand-by' well.  A step up from the now obsolete NiCd battery technology, but not expected in a modern-day accessory like the Microsoft Wireless Adapter for Typing Covers -- especially at that price.  Lithium-ion is the obvious choice, and what I'd assumed would be in it.  Quicker to charge, lasts a lot longer on a charge, and holds onto it's charge when not in use.  The only reason to mass produce something with NiMH is cost -- Microsoft saved a lot of money by using NiMH instead of lithium-ion :-(

Regardless, I like this accessory enough to accept it's got old battery tech inside.  In this case, functionality trumped taking advantage of the 30-day return policy.
