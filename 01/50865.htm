tom2miliardy say:

JBL Charge 4 pairing with pc

Hello!

Do you guys know how to pair JBL Charge 4 speaker with a laptop (my laptop is Dell latitude e7450).

Computer finds the device using bluetooth but then it searches for drivers and communicates that it fails to find a driver.. JBL does not have any drivers for their speaker. I tried all different ways to deal with it but from what I found many people had the same problem and usually they fail.

My windows version is windows 7 64 bit.

Thank you in advance for your help.

Tom

lemp say:

The JBL Charge 4 speaker uses Bluetooth 4.2 (spec).  Windows 7 does not natively support Bluetooth 4.x.

  1. Open Device Manager on your computer (click Start, type Device Manager in the Search box, and press Enter)

  2. Double-click the Bluetooth category to expand it

  3. Right-click your Bluetooth adapter (receiver, transceiver, etc.) and select Properties

  4. Select the "Advanced" tab

  5. Check the LMP version

    • LMP 0.x - Bluetooth 1.0b
    • LMP 1.x - Bluetooth 1.1
    • LMP 2.x - Bluetooth 1.2
    • LMP 3.x - Bluetooth 2.0 + EDR
    • LMP 4.x - Bluetooth 2.1 + EDR
    • LMP 5.x - Bluetooth 3.0 + HS
    • LMP 6.x - Bluetooth 4.0
    • LMP 7.x - Bluetooth 4.1
    • LMP ​8.x - ​Bluetooth 4.2
    • LMP 9.x - Bluetooth 5.0

If you don't have LMP 6.x or better, that's your problem.

Can you pair the speaker with your smartphone and hear sound?  Most smartphones do support Bluetooth 4.

Some laptop manufacturers have updated Bluetooth drivers that add Bluetooth 4.x capability.  I looked at the downloads for generic Dell Latitude e7450 and although there are Bluetooth drivers, the descriptions don't indicate what level of BT is supported. 

I suggest that you go to https://www.dell.com/support/home/us/en/19, enter your Dell Service tag, and then download and install the most recent Bluetooth driver for you laptop.

Then check the LMP version again and/or just try to pair your speaker.  If still no joy, you can buy an inexpensive Bluetooth USB transceiver that will work with Windows 7.  For example:

If you do get a Bluetooth transceiver, disable the onboard Bluetooth in your computer.
