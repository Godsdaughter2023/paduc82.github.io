I just wanted to provide an update, since at least a few people appeared to have a similar problem.  I have fixed the problem.

Under the Windows 10 settings, you can click the System button, and one of the choices is Apps & Features.  Click on it, and then scroll down to Mail & Calendar.  Click on Advanced Options, and there will then be a button for Reset.  When you click on it, it warns you that it may cause loss of data and/or preferences (or something like that), so I had refrained from choosing that approach beforehand (since I was trying to find a fix without loss of old emails).  I decided to go forward with this approach since I had not gotten any response to my inquiry (and, by the way, a subsequent System Restore to a point before the problem arose didn't work either).  The Reset of the Mail & Calendar app worked, and I didn't lose any of my emails (past ones or the ones since the problem arose).  Your mileage may vary, but I thought it was worthwhile to mention what worked for me at least for my emails (my Calendar still doesn't work, but I'll play around with that further).

I think what happens is the screens turn off a split second difference between them causing this issue.

In that case try using Hibernate.  Then it is up to the OS to preserve the machine state before quiescing.   However, then the problem will be making the physical state of the machine come back before you do a Resume.  In the case of a monitor which was acquired wirelessly that may be impossible.  So if you are using a multi-monitor configuration I think you need to get used to occasionally throwing windows back to the general location of where you want them, e.g. using Win+Shift+CursorLeft.  At least then they seem to remember the original size and shape.  For example, I have exactly the same problem with my HDMI monitor (Second Screen Only) that gets lost somehow by Sleep.  Then all the windows get placed on the (Portrait oriented) tablet and may or may not need to be thrown back.  However, switching back to Second Screen Only again (e.g. using Win+P, End, Enter) remarkably often does restore the windows exactly where they were.

So, what happens in your case if you temporarily switch your display configuration to Second Screen Only?  Is the third monitor part of that description?  Then maybe you would be able to preserve the position of more of your windows.  Doing Extend from Second Screen Only may or may not make things appear again on the primary monitor.  But I imagine it can depend on the application involved.  Notepad windows seem to stay put for example.  But that can cause confusion if you forget about a previous state with a different display configuration.  E.g. if you don't have the monitor to use again you would need to use keyboard tricks to get off-screen windows back to somewhere visible.

What I would like to see is an enhancement to the Task view UI (Win-Tab or swipe from the left) which would allow us to throw windows to other monitors, e.g. not just limit its throwing to different Desktops on the same monitor.  Or how about being allowed to drag a window thumbnail to a different monitor?  Have you tried that and been disappointed by the result too?   ; }

http://www.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/windows-10-faq

This update is not required to get Windows 10, you can still get it even though you don't get the notification. Just download it on July 29th 2015 when it becomes available. You will have one year to do so. The Reserve app is just a reminder/notification for its availability.

How can I cancel my reservation? I don't want to upgrade to Windows 10 at all.

Open the Reservation app in the notification area (double click it)

Click the Hamburger menu in the upper left corner, then select View Confirmation. 

In the lower left, click Cancel reservation and then confirm (twice). 

Can I change the email address used to reserve the free upgrade?

You have to cancel the reservation and reserve Windows 10 again if you want it to a different email address.

To cancel reservation follow the below steps:
•Click/tap on the Get Windows 10 app icon on the taskbar notification area.
•Click/tap on the "hamburger" style menu button at the top left corner in the Get Windows 10 app.
•Click/tap on View confirmation under Getting the upgrade.
•Click/tap on the Cancel reservation link.
•Click/tap on Cancel reservation to confirm.
•Click/tap on Close.

This will cancel the reservation, now go ahead and reserve it with the new email.

Can I uninstall the Reservation completely from my computer?

Click Start (or press Windows key + S for Windows 8.1 users)

Type: view installed updates

Hit Enter on your keyboard

Look through the list for the KB3035583 and KB2976978 update.

Right click each update and click Uninstall.

Why don’t I have the Get Windows 10 app?

If you don’t see the Get Windows 10  app on your PC, it might be because:

  1. Your device isn’t up-to-date with at least Windows 7 SP1 or Windows 8.1 Update.
  2. Windows Update is turned off or is not set to received updates automatically.
  3. You’ve blocked or uninstalled the necessary Windows Update functionality.
  4. Your device is not running genuine Windows.
  5. Your Windows License is from a MSDN or TechNet subscription. If it is, you will be able to get Windows 10 through your subscription if it is still active.
  6. Your computer is part of a Windows Domain
  7. Microsoft has not determined your device capable of running Windows 10 even though it meets the minimum system requirements.
  8. You are running a volume license client: Windows 7 Professional, Windows 7 Enterprise, Windows 8.1 Pro, Windows 8.1 Enterprise (only retail store bought or preinstalled OEM licenses) get the upgrade offer.

Running Windows Update and installing any available updates will fix the first three issues. Need more help? Go to our Windows Community Forum.

PCs that we determine cannot run Windows 10 will not see the Get Windows 10  app before July 29, 2015. After July 29, 2015, we’ll enable the icon in the system tray. This is to help ensure that you can easily check your PC’s compatibility if you choose.

If your device is managed as part of a school or business network, please check with your IT administrator about upgrading to Windows 10.

Hi Jan, I think he was trying to sell you a new Drive . . .

File History is a safe way of holding previous versions of your files.

If the drive that file history is failing, you will get a warning from Windows, not file History.

Windows does this automatically with all drives, including the new drive he was trying to sell you.

As for buying a new drive every 2 years - nowadays you get a lot longer than that from a drive.

All modern drives contain a function called SMART and if the drive is failing, you will be warned that SMART reports an imminent drive failure

wmic diskdrive get status

All healthy drives will return OK if they are healthy . . .
