jimkaze say:

icloud email on Wndows 8.1

I am not able to add my icoud.com email address in my mail app on my Windows 8.1 machine. Does anyone have any solution for adding an icloud email account to my Windows 8.1 mail app??

a. user say:

Hi Jim,

I understand that you need add your iCloud account to Mail app. To choose the correct SSL-settings is very important to get iCloud email in the Windows 8.1 mail app running.

The set up steps are the following:

  1. In the Mail App, go to ‘Settings’ -> ‘Account’ -> ‘Add an account’ -> ‘Other account’ and choose ‘IMAP’ for setting up iCloud email and click on ‘Connect’.
  2. Fill in your iCloud email address and your iCloud email password and click on ‘Show more details’.
  3. The following settings should now be additionally filled in:
  • Username = *** Email address is removed for privacy *** (your iCloud email address)
  • imap.mail.me.com, SSL = on, Port = 993
  • smtp.mail.me.com, SSL = off, Port = 587
  • Outgoing server requires authentication = yes
  • Use the same username and password to send and receive email = yes

It is very important to switch on SSL for IMAP and off SSL for SMTP! After clicking on ‘Connect’ iCloud email on Windows 8.1 email app should work now.

Hope this helps.

Please post back if the issue persists and we will be glad to assist you further.

uninstalled your current driver. This can happen quite quickly (almost a blink and you'll miss it scenario),

and does not prevent you from proceeding with installing your new driver, which can lead to conflicts

due to the one driver being installed over the other.

- Note that after a complete uninstall of the Nvidia drivers Window may reinstall it's generic VGA drivers,

as it has always done. This driver allows for display of the Desktop when no dedicated drivers are installed,

and should not conflict with Nvidia/AMD, or Intel or AMD integrated graphics.

How to temporarily prevent a Windows or driver update from reinstalling in Windows 10

Note that while it says temporarily. hiding the Nvidia driver seems more or less permanent.

- While most updates and software installs that require a reboot will do so automatically, if you deferred the reboot

(there is often an option to wait)  and did a lot of installing/updating without rebooting after each install/update,
