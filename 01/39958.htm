considerate_guy say:

It sounds like you might have high-definition video footage if it is 7.5GB and just over an hour long.  In any case, as Skier-Hughes mentioned, you should have no problems fitting that onto a standard DVD.  The key is that you must use a program to create a video DVD.  You can't just burn your existing movie file onto a disc and expect it to play in a DVD player.  In fact, as you already noted, it is too big to fit by doing this anyway.

Assuming you are on a version of Windows that supports it, you should already have Windows DVD Maker.  Just launch that program, give it your movie file, give your DVD a title, set the options, choose your menu style, preview the results, etc.  At the end, tell it to make a DVD and the program will do all the work of transcoding your big file into the final format to fit on the disc, with the menus, etc.

So how does your 7.5GB file magically fit onto a 4.7GB DVD disc?  I expect the reason is that the resolution will be reduced.  Assuming the current video is HD, DVD players can only handle SD (standard-definition).  People tend to forget that DVDs came from the era of SD video.  So your 1920 x 1080 video (or perhaps 1280 x 720) will get converted to 640 x 480 (SD) and will fit easily. Or even if your current footage is 640 x 480 (SD), the DVD might be using a lower bitrate and MPEG-2 compression resulting in a smaller file.

If on the other hand, people want this for their computers and can handle the larger file, you can just transfer it to them however it makes sense.  If someone has an 8GB (or larger) USB thumb drive or a memory card, for example, that would work. But remember, you are giving them a movie that is playable on a PC then, not one that is directly playable on a TV.

One final option you might want to consider is uploading the movie to a video sharing site like YouTube or Vimeo and just emailing them a link.  You can even set restricted access so only people with the correct link (or even a password, on Vimeo) could see the movie.  In the case of Vimeo, there's even an option to download the movie.  Those that are tech-savvy could then download the file and make their own DVD if required.

In summary, your movie should fit on a standard DVD if you create it as a video DVD.  You can also save yourself the work of putting it on to physical media by uploading it to a video sharing site.  And finally, you can transfer the file on a flash drive assuming people have one that is large enough and that they intend to play this on their PC.

Edit: Oops, just realized that YouTube and Vimeo have length restrictions that would probably preclude sharing a video of that length. I suppose you could break it into 10 minute chunks, but that's probably more work than it is worth, so forget I mentioned that. :)

Q. If my customer asks me to upgrade a PC with new hardware components, when is a new operating system needed? When would the PC be considered "new"?

A. Generally, an end user can upgrade or replace all of the hardware components on a computer—except the motherboard—and still retain the licence for the original Microsoft OEM operating system software. If the motherboard is upgraded or replaced for reasons other than a defect, then a new computer has been created. Microsoft OEM operating system software cannot be transferred to the new computer, and the licence of new operating system software is required.

If the motherboard is replaced because it is defective, you do not need to acquire a new operating system license for the PC as long as the replacement motherboard is the same make/model or the same manufacturer's replacement/equivalent, as defined by the manufacturer's warranty.

Hi Bob

 

You can customize that Toolbar.

 

Example:

 

Go to C:\ProgramData\Microsoft\Windows\Start Menu\Programs.

 

Create a New Folder in Programs named Utilities, Multimedia, or Communications or whatever name you wish.

 

Place Shortcuts to whichever programs or applications you wish in those folders.

 

These new folders with the shortcuts will appear on that same Toolbar. From the Desktop, you can expand that Toolbar and drag and drop the folders/shortcuts to rearrange into any order that makes sense to the way you work.

 

As you work with the tasks that you use most often, you can move things around in the toolbar to make your tasks easier to complete. You will soon be able to work much faster.
