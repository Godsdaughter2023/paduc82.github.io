TawfikKh, it has been a while, and your problem has probably already been solved one way or another.

But for people having the specific problem of stale entries for deleted files showing up despite re-indexing, consider that you might have a corrupted disk.

To diagnose and fix the corrupted disk:
1.  Go to My Computer

2.  Right click on the suspect disk drive and select Properties.

3.  In Properties, tick the Tools tab.

4.  In the Tools tab, under Error Checking, click Check.

5.  When prompted that you don't need to scan the drive, tell it to scan the drive anyways.
6.  If it is your C: drive, you may need to reboot so the scan can run.
7.  Reboot the computer again.

8.  Rebuild the index.

This should fix the problem.

  • By searching for Recovery Drive you can create a recovery drive on a USB drive that typically requires ~6 GB.

erdeslawe say:

They are essentially the same thing, so no you don't need both.  

The USB option is probably best for most people, as USB sticks are much less likely to get damaged than a DVD and they also transfer data much faster than a DVD Reader/Writer, so are quicker to use if/when the need arises. Also, newer PCs/Laptops are much less likely to have internal DVD Drives.

Hi Mickey,

The Windows 10 Recovery Drive is what you would use if the system will not boot up to Windows, it can also be used for other reasons. Which media you create depends on the drives available on your device and/or the boot order setting in the system BIOS as you will be booting from the Recovery drive. When you create the drive if you choose the "backup system files to the recovery drive" option you will have the full toolset available.
