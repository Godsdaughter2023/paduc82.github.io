a. user say:

How to increase size of fonts in latest 2017 version of Win 10

I was able to change the font size in to older version of WIN 10 using the slide bar after going to Systems then Display, but the slide bar was removed from the LATEST UPGRADE of WIN 10  I downloaded 7/1/17.  It changed a lots of my settings and was back to super small fonts for my Icon names, ribbon, taskbar etc...   I searched and searched on how to correct this, but kept getting either obsolete methods or statements saying that Microsoft have remove this option, which had made many posters very angry...me included.  But I continued to search for a way and today I FOUND A SOLUTION.  Here's how I got there and what I did:

Click SETTINGS

Click SYSTEMS, this will open up to DISPLAY.

Where it says SCALE & LAYOUT, I changed to percent to `150%, I l also tried 175%, but that's was a bit too large for me.

I did not click on the Custom Scaling

I DID NOT change the Resolution!!!!

When I checked to see what happened, I found that my ALL of the following had nice large test/icons: taskbar , my menus/ribbon, desktop Icons etc.  It was heavenly; I could see everything easily.  NOTE: Awhile ago,  I had changed my desktop default Icons to custom Icons, so when I did the change to enlarge the font size, it also made those Icons even larger...too large, so I fixed this by holding down the Control button and then using the scroll wheel on my mouse, moving it in and out to get the right size.  This scrolling only changed the Icon size, but not the font size.  So I am all set and very happy.  I hope this helps many of you with small fonts that are hard to read.
