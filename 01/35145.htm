eileen gal say:

Hi Russel,

Utilizing a HomeGroup makes things easier to share. Since you're not using the HomeGroup feature, we suggest that you disable it instead to have your Microsoft Account be the default sign-in on Startup. Follow the steps listed below to disable HomeGroup:

Step 1: Leave the HomeGroup. (This only applies if your PC is currently part of a HomeGroup. If your PC is not part of any group then proceed to Step 2.)

  1. Type HomeGroup in the Search bar and press Enter.
  2. Under Other homegroup actions click on the Leave the homegroup option.
  3. On the Leave the Homegroup window, confirm that you want to leave by clicking Leave the homegroup.
  4. Click the Finish button when the wizard finishes removing you from the HomeGroup.

Step 2: Disable HomeGroup Services
You'll need to disable two HomeGroup services to disable the HomeGroup feature in Windows.

  1. Type Services in the Search bar and press Enter.
  2. Scroll down on the list and look for HomeGroup Listener and HomeGroup Provider services. Double-click the HomeGroup Listener service to open its properties window.
  3. In the Properties window, select Disabled from the Startup type drop-down menu and then click OK.
  4. Do the same procedure for the HomeGroup Provider services.

If you need further assistance, just don't hesitate to let us know.
