Folks, what finally fixed it for me was this:

- Take the plug out. No point trying to do this with a blank/dark screen. :-P
- Click the battery symbol in lower right corner.
- Click Power & Sleep settings.

- 'System' pop-up screen will appear, you will be on the 'Power & Sleep' tab. Scroll down to Related Settings and click Additional Power Settings.

- 'Power Options' pop-up screen will appear. Click Choose when to turn off the display or Change when the computer sleeps on the left hand side. It doesn't matter which as both take you to the 'Edit Plan Settings' screen.
- Now here is the crazy part. On the Adjust plan brightness option, adjust the slider for 'Plugged in' to the far left. YES, that's correct, I said the far left with the circle symbol (as opposed to the far right with the circle symbol with rays around it). Yep, doesn't make sense but it seems the Windows 10 update has it reversed, the far left (dim bulb) is brightest and far right (bright bulb) is darkest. It did on my system at least and may be the same issue for yours.

- Click Save Changes and try plugging in now. If it worked, your screen should no longer go dark/blank when plugged in.

I hope this helps. For anyone curious, my laptop is an Acer Aspire E 15.

lizette ags say:

Hi Jonas,

If you live in a region that does not observe daylight saving time (DST), but the Windows setting for that time zone does adjust for daylight saving time, we recommend that you do not clear the Automatically adjust clock for Daylight Saving Time check box in the Time Zone Settings dialog box. Instead, we suggest that you select a time zone that does not observe daylight saving time and that has the same Coordinated Universal Time (UTC) offset as the current time zone. 

For example, assume that your current time zone is (UTC-06:00) Central Time (US and Canada). There are two time zones that do not observe daylight saving time and that have the same UTC offset (-06:00):

  • (UTC-06:00) Central America
  • (UTC-06:00) Saskatchewan

To change your time zone setting, follow these steps:

  1. In Control Panel, click Clock, Language, and Region.
  2. Click Change the time zone.
  3. In the Time Zone area of the Date and Time dialog box, click Change time zone.
  4. In the Time zone list, select a time zone that does not observe daylight saving time and has the same UTC offset as your current time zone.
  5. Click OK.
  6. Click Apply, and then click OK.

Let us know how it goes.
