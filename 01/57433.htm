gareththomas1138 say:

I just want to say: I have solved the problem on my own.

I came across the solution quite by coincidence when I wanted to find out how to modify the Recent Places bar.

It's really quite simple:

The instructions were to modify the registry for the comdlg32 entries. Problem was, I did not have this key AT ALL - it appears as though it was wiped out. So after following instructions on restoring these keys, I had a little look at what other keys were supposed to be there. There were three keys (mentioned in the link below) and were absent. So I re-entered them all.

The registry key and all its entries that were missing are as follows:

HKEY_CURRENT_USER
   Software
      Microsoft
         Windows
            CurrentVersion
               Policies
                  Comdlg32

Lo and behold my dialog boxes started working again, with absolutely NO ill effects.

Trust me, I will be very careful about using a registry cleaner in the future.

How to re-enter these missing keys can be found here:

http://msdn.microsoft.com/en-us/library/cc750930.aspx
