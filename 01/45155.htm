I don't know where Microsoft "Support Engineers" get their information.

Write caching definitely does "apply to external hard drives" but it is usually disabled to prevent to sort of issue that you are experiencing.

I have seen several posts that report the same issue that you do.  All of those posts were with SATA drives.  Is your Seagate an eSATA drive?

Is the drive recognized as an external drive in Device Manager?  In Device Manager, go to the drive's Properties dialog and click on the Policies tab.  An external drive should have two options:  "Optimize for quick removal" and "Optimize for performance."  An internal drive shows those options as greyed-out (with "performance" selected) but there should also be an "Enable write caching on disk" check box below the second option. 

What shows on your Policies tab?

As I understand it, "delayed write" or "write behind" is implemented by the disk drive hardware and/or its driver.  If the option is not available on the drive's Properties > Policies tab, I'd suggest getting in touch with Seagate support.
