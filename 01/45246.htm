Yes, moving your library folders should clear up space on your C Drive, but DO NOT drag your Documents library onto your other drive, you can brick your system doing this!

Moderator Edit: it is supported and recommended to drag special folders to reassign their location and will not cause any harm.

If you want to move your library folders (Documents, Music, Pictures . . . etc.), create the appropriate destination folder on your D Drive first, then right click a library folder, select Properties. Then on the resulting dialog, click the 'Location' tab and click 'Move'  and redirect to the new folder you created on your D drive . . .

cmdrkeene say:
Dragging a shell folder to another location will automatically update the Location tab's target folder without hassle.  Either way works, both do the same job.

wim_ruiterkamp say: Thanks, the system is now executing the update.

cmdrkeene say:

@Dave - dragging a special folder ('shell folder') to reassign it's location is not hazardous and won't hurt a system.  It's documented, supported, and even the actual recommended way of moving a folder.  Sure you can use the properties "Location" tab of the folder, but dragging the folder to a new place is what Microsoft recommends.

For example, the folder that is by default under C:\Use\name\videos\captures, captures is a special shell folder and has a location tab (just like Videos, Documents, Pictures and many others).   But if you look up how to move a special folder, such as OneDrive or that Captures folder, you'll see the official instructions are to "just drag it wherever you want".  The location tab in the properties will update itself automatically, it is not a dangerous thing to do and certainly won't "brick" a system.
