andre da costa say:

All you really need to do is boot normally. There's one thing to remember when installing an existing Windows 7 on a hard disk in new hardware – don't choose a repair install. It won't give you any problems, and will probably fail, but it does waste time. Booting normally, Windows will revert to default drivers where necessary, and will either go online to download what you need (or in the case of the new network drivers and USB 3.0 drivers needed for the motherboard, it will prompt you for the appropriate driver disk, which should be available on the motherboard disk that came with your new computer). The whole process should take less than 15 minutes and a couple reboots, including downloading and installing all the Windows and application updates that had accumulated since last September.

Finally you will have to reactivate some of the applications on your PC – including Windows itself.

If its a full retail version, then all you need to do is reactivate by telephone if prompted.
