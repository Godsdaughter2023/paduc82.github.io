greg [msft] say:

Hi Dmitry,

To help save power, Surface Laptop uses Adaptive Hibernate. I think this may be what you are noticing.

Instead of waiting a fixed amount of time to move from sleep to hibernation, Surface Laptop uses a predicted user presence service. If the prediction service indicates that you are away, the device will hibernate without waiting a fixed amount of time. When the prediction service indicates that you are expected to use the unit, it will wake the unit a few minutes prior. In normal usage, you should get the best of both worlds; an instant-on experience and great battery life.

Surface Laptop will also move from sleep to hibernate if it detects a 5% or greater battery drain over a 6-hour period.

Due to these variables, there can be scenarios when you will need to manually wake the device, for example if you decided to use the device during a time that you normally don’t.

This setting can be changed at Settings > Power & Sleep > Save me power when my device knows I’m away. If this setting is Off, Surface Laptop will only move from sleep to hibernation after a cumulative 6 hour period sleep session has elapsed (or) if a 5% battery drain occurred while in the sleep state – whichever happens first.

Note: This setting is removed in Windows 10 Fall Creators Update, so if you have installed this update, there is now a more consistent experience in waking the device.

Hope this helps,

Greg
