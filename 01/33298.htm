To answer your questions, here is a step by step case description;

1 -- First account is a member of Administrators group.

2 -- Open Windows 10 Disk Management with this account.

3 -- Create a new Virtual Drive (VHDX) from menu "Action / Create VHD" and save new VHDX file to any location on the PC.

4 -- Right click on new virtual disk on the same Disk Management window and "Initialize Disk".

5 -- Then create a new NTFS Partition on new virtual disk

6 -- Close Disk Management window.

7 -- Open Windows Explorer "This PC" window

8 -- Right click to new virtual disk and "Turn On Bitlocker" and create a new BitLocker password.

9 -- Copy some files into new virtual disk.

10 -- From Windows Start Menu, switch account to second account, without signing out current admin account.

11 -- Second account is not member of the Administrators group.

12 -- Check Windows Explorer "This PC" window with non-admin account

13 -- You should see the second account can browse the virtual disk.

evie20s say: Thank you for your reply. My husband actually figured out a solution that seems to be working so far. He went to the control panel/device manager/system devices. There were six or seven instances of "12C Controller". Uninstalled all of them being sure to check the box that says "Delete the driver software for this device" (it didn't work to just uninstall them). Reboot. On reboot it appears that a different driver wase installed as system devices then showed that the "12C Controller" instances were replaced with "Intel(R) Atom(TM) Processor 12C Controller". That's what worked for us. Good luck.
