    tyree jordan say:

    I was mistaken about shockwave. I was able to fix my video by using a Windows 8.1 video driver, rather than the Windows 10 driver that auto installs with the upgrade.  

     

    Most computers use Nvidia or AMD video cards. After downloading windows 10. Windows 10 will install the newest AMD driver for AMD video card users. The problem is that AMDs download page will say your video card (especially older ones) are compatible with the new driver, but they are not. if you have an AMD video card and have the video issue. Uninstall the windows 10 video driver. Then install one compatible with your previous version of windows.

     

    In example,

    I have an AMD 7000 series video card. Windows 10 auto installed AMD driver 15.7 for windows 10. The driver would not completely install and says it works with my card. I found a forum at AMD with other people having the same problem. The AMD  tech rep told us about the incompatibility. AMD is aware but is not informing anyone who is not asking. He asserted we uninstall the windows 10 diver and let windows update figure out what driver to use after uninstalling. The original poster did so only to have windows update reinstall the same problematic driver.  Another poster decided to use an earlier driver and it worked. I did the same and it worked. I uninstalled driver 15.7 for windows 10 and installed driver 15.6 for windows 8.1.

     

    Version numbers aside. Windows 10 replaces your computers previous video driver on installation. Your video card may not work with the new driver. Uninstall the new windows 10 driver and find one that works for your; 1) video card, 2) previous version of windows the computer was using.
