dub dublin say: Actually, Microsoft Garage has a really elegant (if "officially unsupported", it's Garage, after all...) way to do (almost) this, and it doesn't even require a video cable or input - just both computers connected to the same network:  It's called Mouse Without Borders, and it lets you use the keyboard and mouse to control Windows desktops across PCs.  You can get it here: https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/download/details.aspx?id=35460  (Note that programs on each connected computer are still running locally on that computer, not the other, but this makes it way easier to work with multiple PCs.)

This is amazing if you work with both a laptop and a desktop, for instance, or want to easily remotely control a PC connected to a projector in a Conference Room, for example.  Basically, once you've installed MWB on the PCs, it allows you to tell it the physical arrangement of multiple screens, but across *different* PCs - Yes, you can move your mouse cursor straight from one computer to another (even if one or both have multiple monitors), and then type from the keyboard.  It just works.  (Actually, you can use the mouse/keyboard on either PC to control both.  IIRC, it supports more than two PCs, but I've never used it that way.  It's not a software KVM, because each PC does need at least one monitor physically attached...)

Better yet, MWB lets copy and paste work seamlessly across the PCs, and drag-and-drop , while not perfectly transparent, works well enough - there's a special "MouseWithoutBorders" folder on the desktop where files can be dragged to the other PC (You'll have to move them from there in a bit of a two-step, if you want them elsewhere, but this is still way easier than setting up local file sharing for most people.  (Why does MS make that so bloody hard to do?  Local filesystems are half the benefit of a real OS!))

I used this for a few years with my old laptop (with an external monitor), connecting both screens of that desktop to a second PC, a 27" HP All-in-One desktop.  MWB connects automatically when you connect to the network, so I never did anything but plug in power, HDMI, and Ethernet to the laptop (didn't have a docking station), and I was magically controlling both the HP All-In-One and my laptop from the HP's nicer keyboard and mouse.  For what it lets you do, MWB works exceptionally well, and while there are occasional bugs, it's as solid as most "real" MS software.

I notice there's also an App version now, to allow Windows Tablets or Phones to control a PC desktop as well - I haven't tried that version, but the original desktop version is truly one of Microsoft's great hidden gems.  If you have more than one PC in your work area, you'll wonder how you ever got along without it - and also wonder why MS didn't just make this part of Windows years ago.
