I don't see where you said you tried changing the resolution.  You changed the DPI, but that is different than changing the resolution (although can be connected to ALSO changing the resolution.)

The resolution is not user independent - meaning when you change the resolution for the screen, it is set to that size for all users of the computer.  Your original problem was:

all objects and pictures on screen appear to be "stretched" horizontally with all apps and web sites.  A good example is a picture of someone... normal picture is tall and thin person.. on my screen they appear to be shorter and wider..lol

Which means you are utilizing a non-widescreen resolution on a widescreen monitor and the monitor is allowing it to fill in the entire screen.

You say you have the AOC 2219V1 monitor (<-- linked to there) and according to the specifications on that web page, the resolution you want to set the monitor at is: 1680 x 1050 @ 60Hz.

So, in your case, if you have the correct driver for your video card installed *and* said video card supports widescreen resolutions (is this a new monitor?) then you should...

1.

Open Display in Control Panel.

2.

On the Settings tab, under Screen resolution, drag the slider to the right until it shows the selection settings at 1680 x 1050, and then click Apply.

3.

When prompted to apply the settings, click OK. Your screen will turn black for a moment.

4.

Once your screen resolution changes, you have 15 seconds to confirm the change. Click Yes to confirm the change; click No or do nothing to revert to your previous setting.

 

Now - if you don't have the choice of 1680 x 1050 and this is a new monitor and you do have the latest driver for your video card installed - then likely your video card wasn't made to support the widescreen monitor resolutions.

theplectrum say:

If I read your message correctly, it seems like you have 2 active sound devices - the X-Fi and the SoundMax. You have therefore a conflict.

I would suggest just using one of them - the X-Fi is probably the best quality. Turn the other off in the BIOS (before boot up, press Del (or read the manual) then disactivate the Sondmax.

Once booted up, un-install any mention of the Sound Max. Clean up the registry (back it up first) using Ccleaner (its safe to use and is recommended by many PC mags).

Download (if you haven't done it) the latest W7 specific drivers/software for the X-FI. Un-install any mention of that device and its drivers/softwxare. Re-boot, and then re-install the X-Fi drivers. You may have to re-boot again once the installation has finished. You should then have sound through your X-Fi.
