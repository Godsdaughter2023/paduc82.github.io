Hi SuprithaShanbog,

 

We may need to reset the computer to its factory defaults by doing a physical Windows Reset. To do this, you can perform the following steps:

  • Turn off your computer by doing a force shutdown or simply pressing and holding down the Power button until the screen goes black.
  • Turn it on again by pressing the power button. While it is attempting to boot up, immediately turn it off as soon as you see the spinning dots.
  • Repeat the force shutdown during the boot up process two or three times until you see the message “Preparing Automatic Repair” during the boot up. Wait for the automatic repair to complete.
  • After the Automatic Repair runs, you should get a prompt whether you want to “Restart” or access the “Advanced options.” Click Advanced options.
  • On the “Choose an option” screen, click Troubleshoot, then click Reset this PC, and click Remove everything. The PC may restart and you should see the prompt to “Reset this PC,” click Just remove my files.
  • Wait for the Reset process to complete. Afterwards, it would require you to set up your account just like how you had the PC out of the box.
