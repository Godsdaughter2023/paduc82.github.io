Can't connect to Homegroup Windows 7 between Modem to PC and Modem - Microsoft Community

jorchking say:

Can't connect to Homegroup Windows 7 between Modem to PC and Modem to Router to PC users.

I am talking about WIRED computers, not talking about wireless.

I have 3 PCs:

-2 are connected through Modem directly to PC.

-1 is connected through router and that router is connected to the modem.

The problem is this last one, this can't detect the Homegroup that I have created on the computer that is directly connected through modem.

The 2 PC  through modem are perfectly detected and connected to the Homegroup, so I want to know what I should do to the other PC (That is connected from router through modem) detects the Homegroup I had already created?

Please help.

hairyfool say:

A Modem can only have two ports, one to connect to the internet (Wide Area Network) and the second to a SINGLE local device. In most cases the Internet Service Provider will only provide one IP address hence cannot itself connect to more than one device at a time. In current solutions it is very rare for a Modem to be used. If as you state you have more than one device connected to that unit then it is itself a router albeit possibly with the Modem built in if you have ADSL from your ISP.

If your second unit is also a router then this explains why you cannot connect all the devices into the same HomeGroup.

The 1st router has created a Local Area Network consisting of the two PCs and the WAN port of the 2nd router. The 2nd router has created another independent LAN with the 3rd PC. It is most likely the two LANs will be IPv4 based and traffic cannot be routed between them properly in both directions depending on the subnet ranges of the two LANs.

Even if they are configured correctly to allow traffic HomeGroup requires IPv6 which few domestic routers fully support and each member of the HomeGroup must be on the same LAN anyway, it will not support a routed connection.

Remove the 3rd PC from the 2nd router and plug it into the 1st router direct, remove the 2nd router from the 1st to free up the port. All 3 PCs are now on the same LAN and should communicate at both IPv4 and IPv6 and the HomeGroup should work.

If you need the additional ports provided by the 2nd router for other devices not a part of this problem then consider replacing it with a switch. If you are using the 2nd router for other non relevant Wi-Fi devices then you need a Wireless Access Point.
