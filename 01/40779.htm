Overheating laptop could be due to performance issues. It might also happen if the power plan settings are not appropriate for the system’s processor.

Method 1:

Please follow the steps to run the system maintenance troubleshooter:

a) Press Windows and X key together and select Control Panel.

b) Click on Troubleshooting.

c) Click on View all on the left side panel.

c) Click on System Maintenance and follow the onscreen instructions.

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows-8/improve-pc-performance  

 

If the issue still persists, move to the next method.

 

Method 2:

Let’s change the power plan settings to use minimum processor state to prevent overheating.

Reducing the maximum processor state for your laptop (both when it is on battery or when the power cable is plugged in), reduces the processor’s performance a notch (depending on your settings) and prevents it from being used at optimum potential by an application or game, which will reduce thermal heating. For example, if you are playing a game that is consuming 100% of your processor’s capacity, then it may also result in heating up your system, whereas reducing the battery power state to, say 80%, can resolve this problem, and also result in battery power conservation.

 

Follow the steps to do so:

a) Press Windows and X key together and select Power options.

b) Click on Change plan settings for the power plan that you have set on the laptop.

c) Go to Processor power management. Expand the icon and expand maximum processor state.

d) Reduce the processor state (for both Plugged-in as well as On battery) to a certain level to verify if it makes any difference.

e) Click on Apply and then OK.

 

For reference, you can refer to these link to optimize the computer’s performance:

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows-8/improve-performance-optimizing-hard-drive

Important:  Data available on bad sectors might be lost when chkdsk tries to repair your hard disc.

mayank verma_048 say:

Hi,

The good news is that your laptop is not overheating. The bad news is that some programs are unnecessarily using your GPU, resulting in "overheating". As natural with any GPU, your notebook will be warmer than usual. Usually, NVidia Optimus Technology should detect the applications that actually need the power of GPU. Unfortunately, some poorly designed applications request for more power when they don't need that at all. Based on my research on this issue for over 3 months I've found one temporary and one permanent solution.

Temporary solution:

1. Open Device Manager and Disable your NVidia GPU. The screenshot will help you to find that option.

2. Then restart your notebook.

3. Open Device Manager and Enable your NVidia GPU. Your notebook will not "overheat" anymore.

What it does: Disabling and restarting your computer prevents annoying programs from utilising GPU as GPU drivers are disabled. However, GPU would still run as there are no drivers active to stop it from running unnecessarily. The solution is to re-enable the drivers after restart. Once this happens, NVidia will only know that no programs currently need GPU so, it will shut it off.

What it doesn't: You must disable your GPU before restarting every time and then re-enable after restarting. That's because the moment start-up programs detect availability of GPU they'll start using it.

Permanent solution:

Summary: You must identify the programs that use your GPU by default. Then, you may decide to either uninstall them or somehow make them use integrated graphics by default.

For NVidia GPUs only:

  1. Open NVidia Control Panel.
  2. Click on Desktop menu item.
  3. Check Display Notification Icon in Notification Area.
  4. In System Tray, look for GPU-like icon. Click on it. This will display programs that are currently using your GPU.
  5. Open Task Manager and close all programs that are using your GPU.
  6. Now, we must prevent these poorly written programs from utilising GPU the next time you start your computer. To do this click on Manage 3D Settings on the left pane.
  7. On the right pane, click on Add.
  8. Find the troubling program(s).
  9. In Select the preferred graphics processor for this program drop down list, click on Integrated Graphics.
  10. Click on Apply.
  11. You're done. Next time you start your PC you won't find any "overheating" issues.
  12. Note: Both NVidia GPU and Intel HD 4000 GPU drivers must be installed properly for this to work. If issues still persist then uninstall both GPU drivers and re-install Intel HD 4000 Graphics Driver first and then only NVidia drivers for Optimus Technology to work properly.

For other GPUs: Please read the summary and try identify the programs yourselves as I've got no knowledge about the technologies used by other GPUs for switching GPUs. In worst case scenario, you can always follow Temporary solution which should hopefully work in all cases.
