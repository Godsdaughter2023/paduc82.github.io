Hi Sachin,

The error message “Please Insert a Disk into Drive \Device\harddisk\Dr1” might show up on the startup when you have a removable drive such as zip drive or a virtual drive (mounted drive) which has the same drive letter as that of the operating system drive. Another reason that could be causing the issue is you do not have a removable disk in the removable disk drive.

When exactly do you receive the error message? Is it on startup?

First of all, connect an external drive such as a Flash drive before you start the computer and then check if you still receive the error message on startup. If not, Press Windows+E and check if there is a drive letter assigned for the flash drive.

If that doesn’t fix the issue, change the drive letter of the external drive to a letter other than the operating system drive. Follow the methods:

Method 1:

Follow the steps to change the drive letter of the external drive under Disk Management:

  • Press Windows+X keys and select Disk Management.
  • Right-click the partition, logical drive, or volume that you want to change, and then click Change Drive Letter and Paths.
  • Click the removable drive, click it, click Change, click the drive letter that you want to use, and then click OK.

Restart the computer and check if the error message still shows up.

If yes, proceed to the next method.

Method 2:

This issue might also occur due to virus or malware present on the computer.

Run the Microsoft Safety scanner and check if there are infected files on the computer that are causing the issue. Refer to the link to download and run:

http://www.microsoft.com/security/scanner/en-us/default.aspx    

Note: Any data files that are infected may only be cleaned by deleting the file entirely, which means there is a potential for data loss. This Scanner expires 10 days after being downloaded. Hi,

Thank you for posting your question in the Microsoft Community Forums.

A simple way to check the integrity of the battery is to remove it entirely and try plugging in the laptop. If the laptop powers on properly, the problem is likely with the battery.

 

A non-charging battery can sometimes be caused by an overheating laptop. If the laptop is charging for a very long time then the battery becomes quite hot. As the temperature rises, the battery sensor misfires, telling the system that the battery is either fully charged or missing completely, causing the charging problems. This problem becomes far more likely when dealing with older laptops which don't have the quality of cooling technology used today, or when using the laptop on the couch or in bed, with a blanket or pillow covering the cooling vents. Let the system cool down and take the time to make sure that the air vents are clean and unobstructed.
