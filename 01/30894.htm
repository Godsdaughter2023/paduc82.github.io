¡firedog say:

Is it possible in Windows Live 2011 on a PC to view more than one calendar? 

There are a couple of ways of doing this.

The calendar (and contacts list) you see is determined by the Microsoft Account you sign in with. This is probably the easiest way to maintain separation between business and private business; it has no effect on sending, receiving or reading email. To see the calendar for private‍‍hotmail.com, sign in with that address, and to see the one for business‍‍hotmail.com, sign in with that address.

Otherwise, you could use the Share function at calendar.live.com to share the calendar for private‍‍hotmail.com with business‍‍hotmail.com or vice versa.‍

PS: Which mail accounts you may have added in Windows Live Mail has no bearing at all on which calendar you see. It's only the sign-in that matters.

¡firedog say:

I can only view the calendar of the accoun that I log into in Windows Live on my PC but I can share may calendars in calendar.live.com and can see multiple calendars there.  Is this correct?

Yes, but ...

In Windows Live Mail, it only takes a moment to Sign in with a different email address (one of the options on the right-most button on the Home tab of the ribbon when you're signed in). So, if you're conducting private affairs and need to see your business calendar, just sign in with your business address.

If you share each of your calendars with the other address, you'll see both calendars in Windows Live Mail whichever address you sign in with. You only have to visit calendar.live.com to perform the share; that can't be done within Windows Live Mail.

One caveat: with the changes currently underway for Hotmail accounts, the way calendars synchronize is changing. They haven't quite got it right yet, but they will eventually. Let's not cross that bridge until we get to it.
