tryx3 say:

Mike,

1  Firstly, check your power settings -

Settings,

System,

Power & sleep,

Additional power settings, 

{for the active power plan},

Change plan settings,

Change advanced power settings,

Battery

 - if your battery is crashing through the Critical battery level because it is getting worn out then you can restore the correct behaviour by increasing the % level.  To decide just what level to set it either requires trial & error or watching the battery charge level continually to see what % it is at just before it crashes.

2  Secondly, check how well charged the battery is when Windows says it is at 100%. 

2.1  Note that "100%" means 100% of what is capable of not 100% of what it was capable of when it was new. 

2.2  To find out how well it is actually charging, enter a command in an Admin command window by going to Start menu, Windows system, right-clicking on Command prompt, More, Run as Admin. 

2.3 Enter this [but use a convenient folder of your own]

powercfg /batteryreport /output D:\Desktop\battery_report.html /Duration 1

2.4  Look in the section Battery capacity history.

2.5  Compare Full charge capacity [how much it is actually charging] to Design capacity [how much charge it was supposed to be able to take when it was brand new].

2.6  The lower the ratio of Full charge capacity to Design capacity, the more worn out the battery is.

2.7  The more worn out the battery is the greater the chances that it discharges irregularly e.g. it discharges from, say, 15% to 5% in the blink of an eye.  Such erratic behaviour can cause the crashing to which you refer.
