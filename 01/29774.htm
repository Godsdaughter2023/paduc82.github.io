My videos on Windows Live Movie Maker won't save to a flash drive - Microsoft Community

sdrocks1 say:

My videos on Windows Live Movie Maker won't save to a flash drive or burn to a dvd?

i made a video slide show with music on windows live movie maker,  i thouht i saved it to the flash drive and burn to a dvd but when i try to play it back on another laptop they come up blank?

considerate_guy say:

Did you save the project or the final movie onto the flash drive?

 

Making a movie in Movie Maker is a lot like baking a cake:

  1. You start with your ingredients which are the photos, videos and music from your computer.
  2. Next you are creating a recipe in the form of a project file.  When you are working in Movie Maker you are creating a list of ingredients to use, setting their order, adding effects, transitions, captions, etc.
  3. Finally when you are done, you bake your cake to create the final movie that is shared with others.

It sounds like you may have just copied the project file (.wlmp) to another computer and that won't work.  First you don't have any of the same ingredients also on the flash drive or they are in different locations.  Second, the project file isn't "playable" since it isn't a media file.  It won't open in Windows Media Player.  It might open in Movie Maker (assuming the same version is on the other computer), but because the ingredients are missing, you'll see them all as blank.

 

What you want to do is to actually create your final movie using File > Save movie to create a .wmv or .mp4 file. That file can be transferred to another computer and played (assuming it has the right codecs for playing a .wmv or .mp4 file).

 

Finally if you want this movie to play in a standard DVD player, you can't just copy the file onto a DVD disc.  Most players expect the video to be in a specific format with menus, .vob files with MPEG2 video, etc.  You need to create a video DVD, if that is what you are trying to accomplish.

Netapi32.dll error messages might appear while using or installing certain programs, when Windows starts or shuts down, or maybe even during a Windows installation.

The context of the netapi32.dll error is an important piece of information that will be helpful while solving the problem.


Cause of Netapi32.dll Errors
Netapi32.dll errors are caused by situations that lead to the removal or corruption of the netapi32 DLL file.

In some cases, netapi32.dll errors could indicate a registry problem, a virus or malware issue or even a hardware failure.
