Hi,

As you do not get anything on your Screen at all, it would appear to be a Hardware problem.

This is the order of things from Monitor to Motherboard:

Monitor > Cable > Port on Computer > which is either connected to a Graphics Card in a slot on the Motherboard or to Onboard Graphics Hardware which is part of the Motherboard.

You could try swapping the Monitor, Cable, then Graphics over for good ones.

 

You do not say if you receive any error "beeps" at Boot, which would give an indication of what has gone wrong.

One short "beep" is what is supposed to happen.

 

If that is not the problem:

If not under Warranty, contact a good, local Repair Shop.

 

Cheers.

Hi,

Have you disconnected the monitor and reconnected it recently? If so, have you reconnected to the same graphics output? Some PCs have a connector (or connectors) for graphics on the mainboard and another connector (or connectors) on a discrete graphics card. The discrete graphics card is likely to take precedence over mainboard graphics.

If the problem persists, you need to cross test hardware as advised by the previous contributor.
