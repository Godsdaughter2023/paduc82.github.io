c.w.flink say:

Lessons learned:

1) easiest way to get the latest updated graphics and audio drives for Intel ATOM based boards such as in the ASUS T100TA proved to be:   https://downloadcenter.intel.com/   DUH!

Utility installed scans your system and downloads updated graphics and audio drivers for ASUS T100TA....

  ....and presumably many other platforms using Intel chips.

2) the "camera problem" with the ASUS T100TA after a clean Win10 install is NOT the camera driver, but rather the lack of a driver for the "Camera Sensor MT9M114" which appears as an "unknown device" in the Device Manager list after a clean Win10 install.  (Upgrade from Win8.1 "inherits" the old driver.)

3) the driver for MT9M114 was found in response to scanning the C: drive for an update to the driver for the "unknown device" from Device Manager AFTER various Intel drivers were downloaded in my search.

The one that worked: is dated 6/27/2014 version: 14.31.53.405

Note to Microsoft developers:   This device driver should have been found when Win10 install scanned for and installed drivers.  Check your database of divers and update.

Thanks to a half dozen people who posted bits and pieces of information that led to the solution.  It appears that a lot of people had similar problems with clean installs of Win10.

To Take a Screenshot Without Saving

Press the “PrtScn” key. A screenshot of your display is now copied to the clipboard. Open your favorite image editor, word processor, or other program you want to use the image in. Choose Edit > Paste to paste the screenshot wherever you like. The dimensions of the image will be the same as your desktop resolution. Note: On some laptops and other devices, you may need to press the “Alt + Fn + PrtScn” keys instead. Check your laptop’s manual for more information.

The print screen button may be labeled as “PrtScn,” “PrntScrn,” “Print Scr,” or something similar. On most keyboards, the button is usually found between F12 and Scroll Lock. On laptop keyboards, you may have to press the “Function” or “Fn” key to access the Print Screen feature.

To Take a Screenshot of Part of Your Screen

Press “Windows + Shift + S”. Your screen will appear grayed out and your mouse cursor will change. Click and drag on your screen to select the part of your screen you want to capture. A screenshot of the screen region you selected will be copied to your clipboard. You can paste it in any application by selecting Edit > Paste or pressing Ctrl + V, just as you’d paste a full-screen shortcut taken with the Print Screen key.
