cmdrkeene say:

This would do it:

rd /s W:\$Recycle.bin

But you'd want to replace W with whatever your windows drive is.  But as you noticed, the drive may not show the same when you've booted from recovery media.  That is, C: may not be the letter of the installed system.  One way to tell is to just type C: {enter} and then {dir} and see if that holds your user files or other things you expect to see.  If not, type D: {enter} and repeat to find the drive letter that actually holds the files you want to delete, then use that letter in the above command to erase (remove directory/RD) the recycle bin folder and everything inside it.

    The first thing to try is turning off the console and unplugging it for 30 seconds. Next would be to remove and redownload your account http://support.xbox.com/my-account/manage-gamertag-and-profile/add-remove-account-xbox-one and if that doesn't work please reformat your console keeping games and apps: Settings > System > Console Info & Updates > Reset console > "keep games and apps". (requires account redownload) The reformat usually solves a lot of problems if your problem isn't fixed by unplugging the console.
