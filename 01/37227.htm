andre for directly say:
Hi Ryan

My name is Andre Da Costa; an Independent Consultant, Windows Insider MVP and Windows & Devices for IT MVP. I'm here to help you with your problem.

Based on research, this is what I found about this error:

The problem in this case is that the service for Windows SecurityCenter (wscsvc) have a delayed autostart while "things" like the WscBrokerManager and WscCloudBackupProvider tries to initiate very early in the startup process. It will fail with error event 10016. wscsvc will initiate after ~2 minutes and then everything will be instantiated as normal so you will have no side effects of these errors in the event log but it's annoying.

You solve this by changing the service wscsvc from delayed autostart to normal autostart. You don't (normally) have permission to do it from the services app so you must do it in the registry.

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Services\wscsvc

Change the DWORD DelayedAutoStart from 1 to 0.

Source: https://www.tenforums.com/general-support/10907...

cmdrkeene say:
LCD panels only have 1 resolution they're capable of showing, because there is an exact number of pixels on the hardware display itself.  Changing the resolution on these types of displays does one of two things:

1 - it shows the resolution in absolute terms (uses exactly as many pixels as you tell it to, and black space on the extra non-used pixels).
or
2 - doubles or stretches pixels as needed to fill the display, resulting in blocky or blurry images, skewed and unreadable fonts, and can even damage the display itself, resulting in a need to buy a new monitor entirely.

The preference of most hardware makers is #1.  Sometimes (and rarely), you can change this option in the computer BIOS settings.

-----------------

Now you might say, But I want to change the size of elements on the screen!?  You can, still do this, without resorting to damaging your display or causing bad quality.  To do this, simply change the DPI used by the operating system:

Step 1:  Type dpi into the Start menu, then choose the option Make text and other items larger or smaller
Step 2:  Choose a size of your liking, then click Apply
Step 3:  Repeat if you want to choose another size.  You can also choose custom sizes by choosing "set custom size" on the left.
