a. user say:

You may be getting an 'early warning' message from the S.M.A.R.T. utility that is included with hard drives by their manufacturers. Go to the technical support website of the hard drive's manufacturer and run their own drive health checking utility. (The manufacturer has complete directions.) If it reports problems, you should probably replace the drive, but check with the manufacturer to be sure.

Hard drives are so reliable now that it's rare to get a S.M.A.R.T. warning, but it happens.

(S.M.A.R.T. stands for Self-Monitoring, Analysis and Reporting Technology)
