Delete unused files on hard drive. - Microsoft Community

mstallworth say:

Delete unused files on hard drive.

I want to permanently delete files from my hard drive. Is it better to defragment or use the CCleaner? Why?
cmdrkeene say:
Defragmenter doesn't remove anything, it just re-arranges files around on the drive.  Most modern fast drives don't see a huge performance gain from this.  But consider you download a large file (say, a large movie file) but there's no single empty spot on the drive to fit the whole thing.  It'd be spread out across little bits of empty space.  When you went to open the file, the hard drive would have to spend time moving the reader arm to each spot to read the data.  Defragmenter puts all those bits into one chunk again so that the drive doesn't have to move so much to find all the bits.

Cleaning the drive, on the other hand... I don't recommend CClearner or other tools like it.  They can remove some more files that the built-in tools, but they can also cause problems or remove too much.  The registry cleaner especially can cause significant trouble.

My recommendation: use the built-in Disk Cleanup program.  Just type the word clean into the Start menu's search box to find it.  It will help you remove any unnecessary files.

But just because a file isn't needed, doesn't mean you should remove it.  Consider this: when you open your pictures folder, Windows probably shows you little thumbnail previews of each picture.  Those previews can optionally be removed with the cleanup program, but that means those previews will have to be rebuilt next time you open your Pictures folder.  It's not the end of the world, but it'd cause you to wait for something that's already been done.  For this reason, in most cases I recommend that you don't worry about trying to do too much cleaning.  The system takes care of itself these days.

Final thought:  Extra files won't usually slow down your computer, but extra running programs do.  So instead of just removing some files, try cleaning out your browser toolbars and add-ons that you don't need.  For example, in Internet Explorer you can go to Tools > Manage add-ons.  Disable any you don't use so they don't waste your time loading every time you open a browser tab.
