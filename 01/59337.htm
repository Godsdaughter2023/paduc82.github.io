    a. user say:

    Hi Shaanlone,​

     

    Do you have a video card or an onboard integrated video?

     

    I would like to tell you that assigning more memory to a specific hardware might lead to overclocking. The system can become unstable if the equipment is overclocked too much, and the risk of damage due to excessive overvoltage or heat generation.

     

    A dedicated graphics card has a fixed amount of RAM. An integrated graphics card usually has a minimal amount of its own, and the ability to share from the system memory when you start your computer.

     

    It depends on the type of motherboard and whether your bios allows you to change the allocated memory. So I would suggest you to check your motherboard manual or contact the computer manufacturer for further assistance.

    ken blake - microsoft mvp say:
    Hi 

    Was wondering if it's possible to assign more ram to the video memory 

    Laptop  running windows 8. Sony vaio sve1511q1esi


    There are two kinds of video memory:

     

    1. The RAM on a Videocard

     

    2. A portion of the RAM on the motherboard.

     

    If you have a spearate Video Card, you can't change it. All you could do is buy  a new video card with more RAM and replace the old one.

     

    But if you don't have a video card and instead use some of the motherboard RAM (since you have a laptop) that's probably the case), you may be able to do this. Look in the manual that came with the computer or contact Sony and ask them.

     

    But let me point out that doing this means that Windows gets less RAM, and that will very likely decrease your performance. Why do you want to do this?
