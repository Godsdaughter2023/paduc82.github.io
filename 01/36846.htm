You can do the Panning...with Photo Story 3. 

(FWIW...it's always a good idea to create a System
Restore point before installing software or updates)

PhotoStory 3 For Windows
http://www.microsoft.com/download/en/details.aspx?id=11132
(runs on XP, Vista and ?Win7?)

This is not a complete tutorial but should be enough to get you started...

First be sure the photo is less than 7200 pixels x 7200 pixels.

Import a panoramic photo into Photo Story 3 and advance to
the 'Narrate Your Pictures and Customize Motion' screen.
(do not choose to remove black borders)

Left click the Customize Motion button...

On the Customize Motion screen...check the box...'Specify
Start And End Position Of Motion'. Resize and reposition the
Start Position and End Position frames as you prefer and
left click the Preview button to see the result.

Also you could import more than one instance of a photo and
Pan from point A to point B on the first instance,...then point B
to point C on the second instance and so forth.

When the motion effects are complete you can add music if
you wish and advance to the 'Save Your Story' screen.

You can left click the 'Settings' button for several choices of
resolution...the 1024x768 profile may be a good choice.

Also...choose a Save location and a name for your video.

To Save the project as a .WMV video file...click Next one
last time.

Then you can import this video along with other photos and
video clips into DVD Maker to create your DVD. If you have
problems with the MP4 video clips you may need to convert
them to the WMV format before importing into DVD Maker.

To prevent these errors message, change the Server and Computer Browser services to Manual Startup:

  1. Press Windows key + R and type sevices.msc
  2. In the list of installed services, click Computer Browser.
  3. Click Startup.
  4. Change the Startup Type to Manual, and then click OK.
  5. In the list of installed services, click Server, and then click Startup.
  6. Change the Startup Type to Manual, and then click OK.
  7. Click Close.
  8. Restart your computer.
