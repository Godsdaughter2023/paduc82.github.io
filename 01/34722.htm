a. user say:

Hi nerveracking,

To confirm the change of alternate email address, you will need to confirm it using your current alternate email address itself. Since you do not have access to it, you cannot verify the change. There is a workaround for this, however, there will a removal period of 30 days to 6 months depending on the number of strong proof (password reset options) associated with your account. To change your alternate email address, you must remove the inaccessible account first. Please follow these steps for removing the alternate email address:

1. Sign in here using your Windows Live ID and password.
2. Locate the “Account Security” section, and then click “Manage” beside “Security Info”.
3. Click “Delete” after “Alternate email address”.
4. Click “Mark them all for removal” to continue.
5. Click “Mark for removal”.

Note: The alternate email address will be removed after 30 days. If you have more than 1 strong proof (Secret Question and Answer, alternate e-mail address, mobile number and trusted PC) in your account, the removal period would be 6 months

Once the alternate email address has been removed, you may now proceed in adding a new email address. To do this, refer to these steps:

1. Sign in here using your Windows Live ID and password.
2. Locate the "Password reset information" section, and then click "Add" next to "Email."
3. Provide the email address and re-type e-mail address that you want to use, and then click "Add."
4. If you have previously added an alternate e-mail address, you will need to confirm the changes you have made. Go to your alternate e-mail address mailbox and open the e-mail message that has the subject "Windows Live Account Security Removal”.

This is my generic how to for proper driver updates :

This utility makes it easy to see which versions are loaded :

DriverView - Free - utility displays the list of all device drivers currently loaded on your system. For
each driver in the list, additional useful information is displayed: load address of the driver, description,
version, product name, company that created the driver, and more.
http://www.nirsoft.net/utils/driverview.html

For Drivers check System Maker as fallbacks and Device Maker's which are the most current.
Control Panel - Device Manager - Display Adapter - write down the make and complete model of your
video adapter - double click - Driver's tab - write down the version info. Now click UPdate Driver (this
may not do anything as MS is far behind certifying drivers) - then Right Click - Uninstall - REBOOT

Repeat that for Network - Network Card (NIC), Wifi, Sound, Mouse and Keyboard if 3rd party with their
own software and drivers and any other major device drivers you have.

Now go to System Maker's site (Dell, HP, Toshiba as examples) (as rollback) and then Device Maker's site
(Realtek, Intel, Nvidia, ATI as examples) and get their latest versions. (Look for BIOS, Chipset and software

Download - SAVE - go to where you put them - Right Click - RUN AD ADMIN - REBOOT after each installation.

Always check in Device Manager - Drivers tab to be sure the version you are installing actually shows up. This

driver - reboot - check to be sure it is installed and repeat as needed.

Repeat at Device Makers - BTW at Device Makers DO NOT RUN THEIR SCANNER - check manually by model.
