peterlc say:

Logitech MX5000 keyboard problem with Windows 7 64 bit

I have an issue with the numeric keypad not working under Windows 7 Ultimate 64 bit with my Logitech MX5000 bluetooth keyboard. It worked fine under XP Pro 32 bit. I have tried it both with and without the Logitech Setpoint software (yes 64 bit version) to no avail.

It's like the numlock key is stuck in the OFF position except that this keyboard does not have a numlock key as it is a dedicated numeric keypad.

The "/" "-" and "+" keys all work as expected but pressing the numbers just behaves like navigation keys.

It seems ridiculous to have to buy a new keyboard.

---------------------------------------------

 

For anyone else having the same issue I worked this out a week or so ago.

Firstly it makes no difference which way the numlock is set in your computer's BIOS. Because this a software control issue, it is simply toggled back off again when Windows starts.

 

To get the numlock to toggle just for the current session, I opened the onscreen keyboard from the "Ease of Use Center" in control panel. Toggled the numlock key and all worked fine.

 

Of course this was going to be a nuisance to have to do this every time the computer or Windows was restarted. To fix the problem permanently required a registry edit.

Start the registry editor by clicking on the start button and typing "regedit" into the "search programs and files" field. Save a backup of the registry to be sure.

Go to HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Control Panel\Keyboard

Select the "InitialKeyboardIndicators" vale and change it from a "0" to a "2"

Exit from regedit. From now on the numlock will always be on when Windows is started, by default. Change it back to "0" to reverse the status or for a temporary change just use the OSK numlock again.

 

Another way is to create a "numlockon.reg" file from the registry editor which has it toggled on and one called "numlockoff.reg" to toggle it off again. Simply double-click on one of the other depending on how you want it set at the time, though I can't think of a reason to have it turned off.

The console licenses are associated with the console, not the hard drive. So you wont have to do anything but transfer the content to the new hard drive or download it again.

hassanb say:

Q. How does the process work with just changing the hard drive though?

A. Just swap the hard drives and that's really it.

Q. Do I need to do anything besides transferring my content from my current hard drive to the new one via transfer kit?

A. Nothing else is needed besides using the transfer kit or a USB Flash Drive to move content over.

Q. Any there any licenses I need to transfer?

A. No, nothing in terms of licenses are needed.

Q. What happens to the licenses of the downloadable content I have deleted off of my current hard drive?

Since licenses are tied to the Console ID of the XBOX 360 it wouldn't matter if you do delete anything off of your hard drive as you can instantly download anything that you've purchased again by visiting this link here.

Q. Do they simply stay associated with my gamertag and console?

A. Content that's purchased on the console are connected to the console and the gamertag. If you were to go to a friends house and download your profile on his XBOX 360 and login with your account that's connected to the internet you will be able to download any play any content you've purchased. When playing on consoles that are not your own the only check the XBOX 360 does is an authentication with XBOX Live. As long as the gamertag was the same that purchased it and is connected to the internet the content will download and display properly.

Q. Will I still be able to play my downloadable content offline after I have downloaded/transferred it to my new hard drive?

A. Yes, you should not have any issues with that what so ever.

If you have anymore questions feel free to ask them, we'll do our best to answer them for you.
