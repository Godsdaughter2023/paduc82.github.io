elrumbo say: Duk,

Let me explain the basics about a wireless network security. In order for any wireless device to connect to a wireless network you need to know the following:
1.- SSID or the name of your wireless network.
2.- The security type: WPE, WPA, WPA2.
3.- The encryption type: AES, TKIP (sometimes your security type will determine this encryption so no need to find it).
4.- The wireless key or password.

I will assume that you already know or Win7 got the first three and that somehow the fourth setting was not entered correctly or you never had a chance to enter it. I'm also assuming that you are using the defaults of your gateway (according to the 2wire documentation the default of "[t]he network name is the word “2WIRE” (in all capital letters), followed by the last three digits of the gateway serial number (for example, 2WIRE110). The encryption key is a 64-bit hex value, located beneath the bar code on the bottom of the 2Wire gateway." The key is at the bottom of your gateway.) To enter your wireless key or passcode manually, Follow the next steps:

- Click on the wireless networks icon on the notification area (5 bars).
- Find your wireless network on the list and right click (2WIRE### if you're using the defaults).
- Click Properties.
- Make sure the Security tab is selected. To see your wireless key in plain text, click the Show characters check box.
- Type your passcode or Network security key in the appropriate text box (The hex number beneath the bar code on the bottom of your gateway.)
- Click OK button.

If after this, you still cannot connect, it means that the Security and encryption settings are not correct. Unfortunately, there's no straight way to get this info. The method to get the info, varies even when using same brand gateways. In this case, I recommend to talk to the tech support team of your ISP company. I'm sure they will guide you through the process better than me.

Do not forget to mark my posting as answered and/or helpful.
