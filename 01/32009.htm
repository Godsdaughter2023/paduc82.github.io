I suggest that you perform the methods mentioned below and check.

Method 1: Install the latest updates

Installing the latest updates can help fix many common connection issues.

1. Open Settings page, click on Update and Recovery.

2. Open Windows Updates and check if there are any latest updates available.

3. If yes, unplug all external devices other than mouse and keyboard and perform the update. 

If installing updates doesn’t help, try Method 2.

Method 2: Check the HDMI and USB Connection

Make sure both the HDMI end and USB end of the adapter are connected correctly.

  • Make sure the HDMI end of the adapter is connected to the HDMI port on your HDTV, monitor, or projector. Use the included HDMI extension cable if needed.
  • Make sure the USB end of the adapter is plugged into a USB power source.

If you’re still having trouble after checking your connections, try Method 3.

Method 3: Update the adapter’s firmware

If you can connect your device to the adapter, but what you can see on your device still isn’t visible on your second screen, the adapter’s firmware may be out of date.

The Microsoft Wireless Display Adapter app provides settings and firmware updates for the adapter. If you haven’t already done so, install the app from the Windows Store. To use the app to update the adapter’s firmware:

1.  Tap or click Microsoft Wireless Display Adapter to open the app.    

2.  If a firmware update is available, tap or click Install, and follow the on-screen instructions.     

Note:

The Microsoft Wireless Display Adapter app must be in the foreground on your device while installing firmware updates. Suspending the app will also suspend the update or may cause it to fail.  

  

If your adapter’s firmware is up to date and there’s still a problem, try Method 4.

Method 4: Reset the adapter

Try resetting the adapter. This is also helpful if you’ve forgotten your password.

1. On the adapter next to the wired connection, press and hold the reset button for 10 seconds.
The LED light on the adapter will blink.   

2. When the message, “Ready to connect,” appears on your second screen, swipe in from the right edge of the screen on Surface, and tap or click Devices.   

3. Tap or click Project, and in the list of displays, tap or click the Microsoft Wireless Display Adapter.  

4. Tap or click Tap to fix connection, and in the list of displays, tap or click the Microsoft Wireless Display Adapter.
