I had the same problem and was able to solve it with help from Microsoft support.  On my HP laptop the disc was constantly being accessed, memory and CPU usage was very high, making the computer almost impossible to use.  The problem turned out to be a 'virtual disc' app that is loaded standard on the HP, and executes at startup.

Solution: (1) at bootup press the 'windows' and 'r' keys simultaneously.  At the 'Open' prompt type 'msconfig' and click on the 'OK' tab.   (2) Select the 'Service" tab at the top of the window and check the 'Hide all Microsoft services' box at the bottom. (3) Select the 'Startup' tab at the top of the window and click on 'Open task manager'.  (4) systematically disable each startup application and restarting the computer until you have identified the problem app.  Right clicking on each app lets you choose to enable or disable it.  All non-offending startup apps can then be re-enabled.
