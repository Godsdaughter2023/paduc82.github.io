¡firedog say:

... this properties window only has a General tab ...

This indicates that the account is configured to use POP3. There is no synchronization with POP3 accounts; it's a one-way download of messages from the account inbox at the server. You can only change synchronization settings for an IMAP account.

... it keeps downloading thousands of his old emails that he's deleted. 

First thing to check are the settings under Delivery at the bottom of the Advanced tab of the account's properties dialogue. Right-click on the account name in the folder pane and select Properties to open it.

Next, do some housekeeping at the server. Sign into the account in webmail and start by emptying the folders for spam and deleted items. Then weed out the inbox; move important messages that he wants to keep online to user-created archive folders, leaving only the latest ones in the inbox.

Repeated downloads of the same messages suggests that there's at least one message in the webmail inbox that Windows Live Mail can't or won't download. When this happens, the POP3 protocol goes into fail-safe mode and assumes that the download session has failed and starts it again, either immediately or next time there's a poll for new messages. Try and identify the rogue message - it may be very big, or very spammy or just damaged - and deal with it there in webmail.

vicky acs say:

Hi,

Thank you for supporting our products, especially the recently released Surface Book 2.

To answer your question, Surface Book 2 has USB 3.1 Gen 1 at 5Gbps and USB 2.0 at 480MBps are available by default. In addition, when 4 lanes of Display Port are requested as an alternate mode, USB 3.1 is repurposed for 2 of the lanes and USB 2.0 data speeds are all that remains.
