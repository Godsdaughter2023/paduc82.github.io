shenan stanley say:

Put one on the one network, one on the other and disable the one you don't want to use at that time via the Network Connections control panel.

The "Bonus" sounds like you actually want to be connected to both at the same time *all the time*.  Multi-homed personal machines (multi-homing in general) is almost always a nightmare and should be avoided except where ACTUALLY needed IMHO.

My suggestion for the printer - purchase a network printer-sharing device.  A simple box that allows you to connect what is obviously not a network capable printer (I assume - since you are not connecting it directly to the network) to the network.  This way your computer doesn't even have to exist to have that printer shared on that network.
