bitlocker recovery key - Microsoft Community

jorge h.riveros say:

bitlocker recovery key

Hello everybody, I lost my bitlocker recovery key number from my lenovo tablet, during a update of windows bios. I try find in my onedrive windows and isn´t here. How can recovery my tablet?

juliuspiv say:
Hi Jorge
Sorry to hear about your troubles after the BIOS update. I have seen situations where certain BIOS changes resulted in a BitLocker recovery prompt but on a subsequent restart, it no longer prompted for recovery. That said, your mileage may vary but it's worth restarting the machine, or shutting it down and powering it back on a few times to be sure.

Places to look for your BitLocker key - https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/help/402618...

1) On a printout you saved. Look in places you keep important papers.

2) Saved on a USB flash drive. Plug the USB flash drive in to your locked PC and follow the instructions. If you saved the key as a text file on the flash drive, use a different computer to read the text file.

3) In your Microsoft account. To get your recovery key, go to BitLocker Recovery Keys here: https://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/p/?LinkId=237614

4) In your Azure Active Directory account. For work PCs where you sign in with an Azure Active Directory account, to get your recovery key, see the device info for your Microsoft Azure account here: https://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?linkid=857635


If your computer is associated with an educational facility or corporation, and/or is connected to a domain, ask a system administrator for your recovery key.

If it's a personal machine and you can't find a copy of the recovery key and you can't do anything to revert the BIOS changes that triggered the recovery prompt, then I'm afraid you'll need to reset your PC and you can find instructions for that here: https://support.microsoft.com/help/12415/

jorge h.riveros say: Really thanks you. I´ll do.

juliuspiv say:
Happy to help Jorge. I really hope you can get back into your system.
If you can't find your recovery key but you are able to get back into Windows, you can back up your recovery key by going to the Control Panel and opening 'BitLocker Drive Encryption' and clicking the 'Back up your recovery key' option. Alternatively you can open an elevated command prompt and issuing the following command to get your recovery key:

manage-bde -protectors -get C:

Note: I'm assuming your Windows installation is on the C drive but if it's on the D or E drive you'll need to adjust the command line. (e.g.: replace C: with D: or E:). Similarly, if you have other protected drives, you'll have to issue the same command for each drive you need to get the recovery key for

There is more information and other examples on how to use manage-bde on the Microsoft BitLocker recovery guide: https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/securi...

Keep me in the loop on how this goes.
