Many things are better in 7 than in XP...

• Aerosnap (can drag a window to the far left or right of the screen and it fills half a screen) - very useful!

I had a similar problem.  I was getting an activation code error after I purchased the Media Center upgrade.  I was no able to activate in the normal fashion so I was forced as noted in this thread to call for technical support.  It seems that that is the only way to fix this problem.  Here is what you must do in order to get to the proper activation screen which also has the phone number to call for help.

1) Go to the Metro Screen or Desktop and press and hold the 'Windows Key + 'R' Key' to bring up the Run screen in the lower left hand corner.

2) Type 'slui 4' then click 'OK' to open the activation screen

3)  Select your country and click 'Next'.

4) Follow prompts on screen to appropriate phone number to call for reactivation.

5) The tech support tech will guide you through reading the current activation code series and issue you a new activation code.  Be sure to tell them that you have a Media Center update.

I hope this helps people.   It only took a few minutes (15) to get through all of this and I was up and running.  In the past you used to be able to reactivate automatically in Windows 8 but that is no longer the case I guess.  Anyway, this ought to be very helpful to people who purchase the Media Center and then have an activation problem.  Or even those who may have some other type of activation problem once they've purchased an activation code.
