davem121 say:
Hi Judy

Typically with processors these days, the speed of the processor is managed by software. Your processor will idle at a low speed when your PC is not performing intensive tasks and when required will boost to about 110% of its top speed when required. This is all done on the Balanced setting, and it is best for the performance and longevity of your system

If you set your Power Plan to Performance, that software is bypassed and your Processor will run at 100% all the time and as a result your system will heat up and the fans will be running all the time.

You can if you wish apply the Performance or Ultimate performance power plans, however I doubt you will see an improvement in the performance of your system because the software that manages your processor is clever and would produce the best result ...
