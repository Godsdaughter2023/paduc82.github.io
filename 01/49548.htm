Hi,

This error could indicate that your hard drive may be damaged. Let's get to the Windows Recovery. Interrupt your device during the boot process by powering it off. Do this for about three times and it should trigger the Windows Recovery screen. From the options, select Troubleshoot > Advanced options > Troubleshoot > Command Prompt. Run these commands:

  • Bootrec /fixmbr then press Enter.
  • Bootrec /fixboot then press Enter.
  • Bootrec /scanos then press Enter.
  • Bootrec /rebuildbcd then press Enter.

If the issue persists, here are your options:

  • Reset/refresh your device. If you haven't deleted the recovery partition yet, select this option from the Windows Recovery environment.
  • Change your boot priority. You can do this in the BIOS settings. Contact your device manufacturer for proper instructions.
  • Reinstall Windows. Windows 8 has reached its end of support which is why it is no longer available for download. Contact your device manufacturer to obtain the installation media to reinstall your operating system.
