¡firedog say:

... use the Gmail contacts on WLM instead of the Outlook ones.

There are probably many ways of getting close to what you want, but only one that I know of that achieves the end result you're looking for.

Google contacts can only be edited at Google (e.g. in Gmail), so you can't ever edit, delete or add a contact in Windows Live Mail and then have the change propagated to Google contacts.

You can do it the other way around: connect your Google account to an Outlook.com account, then sign in in Windows Live Mail with that Outlook.com account. You would then see the same contacts list in Windows Live Mail as you see in Gmail, but any editing has to take place at Google.

Another, probably better, solution is to use the excellent Outlook for Android app for all your mail and contacts business on your Android devices. You would then use Outlook.com People as your main contacts list, and you'd be able to manage these contacts at People, on your devices and in Windows Live Mail and keep the whole lot in sync. You can add your Gmail account to the app instead of using the built-in mail app. You would export your Google contacts and import them on the People page, again taking care to avoid duplicates.

Both of these methods might run into difficulties in Windows Live Mail when the Outlook.com account involved is upgraded to Outlook Mail. Until and unless an update is released for Windows Live Mail 2012, contacts will no longer synchronize.
