davem121 say:
Hi Andrew, your Display Driver will need rolling back . . . Windows Update has lately been installing incorrect display drivers . . .

Open Device Manager (accessible by right clicking your Start Button)
Expand the Display Adapters Section
Right click your graphics card, choose properties
On the Driver Tab, click 'Roll back Driver'

Close Device Manager and restart your system



If the rollback option is greyed out:

Right click on your display adapter, click 'Uninstall device'
Choose 'Delete the driver software for this device' then click 'Uninstall'
Restart the computer (myst be a restart, not a shut down)
Windows will then download the correct driver


If the above does not work, you probably have an AMD ATI graphics card

Open Device Manager (accessible by right clicking your Start Button)

Expand the Display Adapters Section
Right click your graphics card, choose properties

Right click on your display adapter, click 'Uninstall device'
Choose 'Delete the driver software for this device' then click 'Uninstall'

Restart your PC

Open Device Manager (accessible by right clicking your Start Button)

Select any device, and then click Action - Scan for hardware changes.

Windows should load a good driver for your ATI graphics card

Then, go to Start - Settings -> Update & Security,

Click Search for Updates, and install any available updates
