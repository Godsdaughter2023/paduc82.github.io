Perhaps I've lead you astray.  My scanner doesn't use Windows Fax and Scan, but I do use WF&S for faxes and I was assuming that the scan part of things worked similarly to the fax part.  Instead, WF&S appears to save scans on a per-user basis rather than on a system basis like the fax.

A commonly-suggested method for changing the default location for saved scans is to create a symbolic link pointing to the new location.  However, if this is what you had done, looking in "My Documents\Scanned Documents" should have opened that new location.

First of all I will remind you that it is normal for Disc usage to be high when first booting-up due to the fact that everything that is scheduled to startup at boot has to load up, including the OS. With a HDD this usually takes anywhere from 2 to 5 minutes under ideal conditions before the high disc usage calms down to a low-ish percentage of use. When a SSD is used as opposed to a HDD the amount of time that the disc usage remains high is greatly reduced as a SSD has no moving parts, and so then also, any fragmentation of the data on the disc that may occur in due course therefore becomes a non-issue in that the data search and retrieval, for the most part, is instantaneous.

That being said, it is difficult for me to ascertain what is happening with your disc usage from the snapshots you provided due to the fact that there were no times specified as to how long after boot up the snapshots were taken, coupled with the fact that there seem to be programs opened that would/should not be opening from the start menu at boot (Google Chrome for example).

If/as you are using any 3rd party anti-virus program(s) I would recommend that you first completely uninstall it/them and then enable and use Windows Defender instead as it is a top shelf AV protection solution, then see if anything changes performance wise for the better.

When that has completed then click on the Tools tab, in the Tools tab click on Optimize, In the resulting window choose the disc you want to work with and then click on Analyze to check how fragmented the drive is and if you wish to perform a defragmentation click on Optimize.

A fragmented HDD will/can cause a noticeable increase of time in the searching for and accessing data on the disc.
