If you're unable to modify the configuration for requiring sign-in when your PC wakes up from sleep via Settings, you can change it from Command Prompt instead. You will need administrative access to complete this procedure.

phugor say:

The Xbox One does NOT support 1024x768 and it does NOT support 4K and it does NOT support 1080i.  The only resolutions the Xbox One supports are 1080p, 720p and 640x480.  It is severely limited in the resolutions it supports, especially in comparison to the Xbox 360.

There are a bunch of requests on the new Xbox feedback site (http://feedback.xbox.com) requesting that additional resolutions be added in an update.  If you want 1024x768 or other resolutions to be added you can vote on some of them.

phugor say:

Not sure what's so hard to understand.  The HDMI cable is the only thing that supports 4K.  Just because it was included with the Xbox One that does not make the console itself capable of 4K.  

You don't need to wait for another poster to come along and tell you what they think because it won't necessarily be true.  You can find out for yourself by going into the Xbox One display settings and changing it from Auto-detect to HDMI and it will list the only resolutions the Xbox One is capable of - 1080p, 720p and 640x480.  You will see that 4K is not listed as one of the resolutions.

The Xbox One didn't support 3D at launch either.  It was just added within the last month.  So either that article is wrong or Microsoft was lying.
