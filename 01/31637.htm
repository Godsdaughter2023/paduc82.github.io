Hi eigbobloblaw,

 

Audio distortion can occur for many different reasons.Audio distortion refers to how much the audio signal is being degraded while passing through your speakers. Since you’ve tried with different audio devices and sound card and also you’ve restored the computer back to the factory settings, the issue seems to be that the output power isn’t sufficient to drive your speakers.

As of now, I would suggest you to try the steps mentioned below.

 

Step 1: Test your speaker configuration

 

To test your speaker configuration and make sure that you're getting the best sound from your computer, follow these steps:

 

a.    Open Sound by clicking the Start button, and then clicking Control Panel. In the search box, type sound, and then click Sound.

b.    Click the Playback tab, select your speakers, and then click Configure.

c.    Select your speaker configuration, and then click Test. You should hear a tone coming through each speaker.

 

To perform more speaker tests, click next, and then follow the steps in the wizard.

 

Step 2: Disable optional speaker enhancements

 

a.    Right click on the Sound icon and click on Playback devices

b.    On the Playback tab, click Speakers, and then click Properties.

c.    Click the Enhancements tab, and then select the Disable all enhancements check box.

 

If you don't see an Enhancements tab in the Speakers Properties dialog box, those features are not available for your speakers and this procedure is not applicable to your computer.

 

Additional Information:
