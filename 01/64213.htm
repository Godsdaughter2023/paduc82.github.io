    rr1165 say: In a world dominated by the "Blind Leading the Blind" and "Forum Gadflies", the answer I found here was correct plus some tweaks that worked for me.

    Manually downloaded KB954430 & KB973688  (Both appeared in the "Add or Remove Programs" but were not recognized as installed from a previous attempt to download and install them using Microsoft Update)

    Using Add or Remove, I removed KB954430.

    Renamed installed MSXML4.DLL "OLD" and ran KB954430 - Msxml4 Version 4.20.9870.0 was installed.

    Using Add or Remove, I removed KB973788.

    Renamed MSXML4.DLL version 4.20.9870.0 "OLD2" and ran KB973788 - Msxml4 Version 4.20.9876.0 was installed.

    Deleted the "OLD" versions - Microsoft Update no longer showed these two "Fixes" as needing to be installed.

    I am back computing and waiting for the next irritation from MS...

    ...hope this helps - again - it worked for me.  OS: XP Pro, IE 8 and Office 2007.

    Skype Classic VS. Skype 8 - Microsoft Community

    stefenhudson say:

    Skype Classic VS. Skype 8

    Hello there!

    I've been reading many threads about Skype for Desktop and how Skype Classic is being retired in favor of Skype 8. Many users of Skype are obviously very unhappy with the fact that Skype classic is being retired. There are many reasons for this. Some simply aren't happy with the layout of Skype 8. Some need or want compact view, which Skype 8 doesn't have. Other features are either missing or broken.

    Time and time again, I see the same responses when users express their dislike for the new Skype. "We'll forward your feedback to the developer team." "We're aware that this feature hasn't been added. We hope to have it added soon." "Your feedback helps." Even after users have been expressing their disgust and concerns about this so-called best version of Skype for months, the Skype team are bent on forcing everyone to upgrade to it anyway, pushing something on users when they've made it clear they don't want it. There was even a response a few months ago that said that most people prefer the classic version over the new one.

    I think part of the problem is a serious disconnect when it comes to users and developers. Developers obviously can't read every single bit of feedback that comes in through the moderators or the feedback app, and I'm sure a lot gets lost when it's full of rather destructive criticism. I also imagine the team probably has this huge plan that they have outlined, and no matter how loud users get, it's going through because the developers are already in too deep to go back now. Writing software like this isn't cheap. Users are probably extremely frustrated because they don't understand why things are changing so drastically when they were working fine before, and being forced to use something you don't want to use is pretty irritating. So here's what I suggest:

    First, I think we, the users, need more information about why certain things are being done. Ignorance breeds fear and hatred, and none of that helps anyone. For one thing, we need to know why it's necessary to entirely overhaul Skype's interface to the point where it looks and feels nothing like the Skype we've known and loved. We need to know why it needs to be an Electron app instead of a normal desktop app that we've used for years. We need to know why something that's obviously feature incomplete and inferior is being pushed, and why something that works fine is being dropped.

    Second, developers should maybe stop pushing this mobile/tablet experience on desktop users. I get that the Skype team wants everyone to have the same experience, or at least as close to the same experience as possible on every device without having to maintain separate large bits of code. There's also the whole cloud based thing. Electron apps seem to be the answer to that issue. However, not everyone who uses a desktop wants the same experience people get on a tablet or phone. It's probably safe to assume that in most cases, people who use a desktop or even a laptop want a normal PC experience. If they want a tablet or phone experience, they'll use a tablet or phone. One size doesn't really fit all in this instance.

    Third, the team seriously should cease the false advertizing. Skype 8 and even Skype for Windows 10 are advertized as having the same look and feel and having all the features we love. Anyone with half a brain can plainly see that this isn't the case. It's not the same, and anyone who says it's the same is likely lying to you to make you feel better about upgrading. That's marketing for you, I suppose. It just isn't smart marketing, because in the end you just end up insulting the intelligence of your user base. Not all of us are sheep who blindly install whatever we're told to use and just deal with whatever happens.

    Lastly, users should really think about the language they use when submitting feedback. I've seem some pretty harsh criticism here. A lot of it is directed at the moderators. I realize that it's frustrating to get the same responses over and over, but I'm pretty certain that this is what the moderators are trained to say. Someone can correct me if i'm wrong. Having skype turned inside out is frustrating, but that doesn't give anyone the right to be a jerk. Being a jerk is probably just going to result in feedback not being read. We also have to remember that whether we're speaking to developers or moderators, they're people too.

    All that said, let's discuss it. I'd love to hear from users, moderators, and even developers if at all possible. Let's fix this so we can all help make Skype be the best it can be.
