Thanks for reaching out to us! We understand it can be frustrating since the console shut off, and it keeps having network issues when no other device has issues. I would recommend the following:

change the console to Energy-Saving Mode and remove the profile:

  1. Press the Xbox button  to open the guide (Don't hold the Xbox Button on the controller)   
  2. Select System > Settings   
  3. Select Power & startup >Power mode & startup.    
  4. Select Power mode to change the setting to Energy-Saving

Note: It is very important to make sure you know the primary email/password to the account before removing it from the console.

Random hangs are often due to damage to the Windows configuration, faulty hardware, or malware. However, because this behavior is random, it can be especially difficult to identify the proper cause. Please review the steps below to help identify the cause and determine the next course of action:

-Do you see black screen with cursor ?
-Did you perform any changes to the computer prior to the issue ?

If you have external devices connected to the PC, try starting the PC with those devices disconnected. Device connection and driver issues can contribute to hangs. If the PC starts successfully without external devices connected, try repeating with more devices connected to identify the specific device or combination of devices that are contributing to the hang.

Method 1:
 Let's run CHKDSK from command prompt.
Suggest you go to windows recovery mode and perform check disk and see if issue occurs
a) Boot from the Windows installation disk.
b) Click on Repair your computer.
c) Click on Troubleshoot.
d) Click on Advanced options and click Command prompt.
e) Type “CHKDSK C: /F” in the command prompt without quotes.

Method 2:

1. While at boot on the Choose an operating system screen, click/tap on Change defaults or choose other options at the bottom.
2. Click/tap on Choose other options.

(Or)

1. Press and hold the power button on your PC until it powers off.
2.  Press the power button on your PC to turn it on.
3.  Repeat steps 1 and 2 above until you see Please wait during boot like below. Usually it may take repeating up to 3 times.
4.  When displayed, click/tap on See advanced repair options.
5.  Click on system restore and follow on screen instruction.
