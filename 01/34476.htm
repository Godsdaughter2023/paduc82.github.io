Netgear WN311B PCI Wireless adapter working with Windows 10

I have found a driver that works with the Netgear WN311B PCI Wireless adapter. It from Lenovo and I am getting great bandwidth and stability for the old adaptor, 7.7 to 11Mbps constant.

Link

https://download.lenovo.com/ibmdl/pub/pc/pccbbs/mobiles/guw503ww.exe

You will need to do a manual install.

1. Download the file

2. Run the file so it extract the files. DO NOT let it install the driver. it will copy the files to C:\SWSetup\SP58848

3. Go to device manager and open Network Adapters

4. Double click the Broadcom 802.11n Network adaptor (It may be different name depending on driver installed)

5. Click the driver TAB and Click Update Driver

6. Click Browse My Computer For Driver Software

7. Click Let Me Pick From A List Of Device Drivers On My Computer

6. Click Have disk

7. Click Browse

8. Go to the file location C:\SWSetup\SP58848 select file bcmwl63.inf and click open

9. select Broadcom 43225 802.11b/g/n. Click Next

10. Follow prompts until the driver is installed.

Hope it helps

(One manufacturer must have had updated the driver to work with win 10)

Hi,

 

After looking at the Image on your previous post I see you have a bit of a problem...

 

Because your Lonerin Files partition is between your OS (C:\) and the unallocated space, you can't just EXPAND like you would normally be able to do...

 

The only way I can see to Expand your OS would be to first EXPAND Lonerin Files into the unallocated space, and then SHRINK Lonerin Files leaving the unallocated space next to OS(C:\)

 

I don't think this is possible in Win 8... Win 8 doesn't appear to be able to put the unallocated space in the needed position...

 

Here is a link to a free program called Parted Magic...it is an ISO image that you burn to a CD that will be bootable... I'm pretty sure it will do what you need to do...

http://sourceforge.net/projects/partedmagic/

 

There is another program called Partition Wizard that is smaller in size...

Easeus may also have what you need... and I'm sure there there are more.

 

Also,  A lot of your missing Disk space is probably in two hidden system files on C:\...

pagefile.sys and hiberfil.sys.  They can each take up about the number of GB as you have in actual memory...  8GB of memory could equal 16 GB for the two of them...

 

Select Show Hidden Files and Folders at the top of your window to see them.

 

ALSO... You should still be able to burn the disks you need to restore your computer to it's factory condition. Asus will have a program on your computer to do that... look for something about RECOVERY...

 

The problem with using them would be that you would lose your Lonerin Files partition....

 

You might try using Win 8 to make an image just of Windows, and make a Repair disk to boot with, but you will need to research that a little bit...

Hi Lonerin

 

The problem is that the Unallocated Space is at the End of Drive D:.

 

The Unallocated Space must be at the Beginning of the D: drive before you can Extend the C: Dive using that space.

 

I recommend that you download the free Easus Partition Manager to fix this problem. It is an easy to use partitionong program and you can use it to move the unallocated space to the beginning of Drive D: without affecting your files.

 

You can then extend the C: Drive.

http://www.partition-tool.com/personal.htm

 


The other item is the way you are determining the Free Space on the C: Drive. You cannot simply add all of the space used by the folders and files on Drive C:. This will not give you an accurate result of the used disk space.

 

The only accurate way to determine how much space is being used is to Right Click the C: Drive and select Properties. This will show a dialog with the accurate amount of Used and Free space on the drive.
