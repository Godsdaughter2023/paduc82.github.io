cmdrkeene say:

Excel 2010 doesn't really support even the taskbar.  It sort of fakes it to provide indivual taskbar buttons per document, even when all documents are really running inside one excel.exe process in memory.  That's why you can have several Excel files "floating" inside a master "Excel window frame".  This is called an MDU (Multi Document User Interface), and it's a relic of the past.  Most Office programs other than Excel dumped this design a decade ago, but Excel kept it around because it's exceedingly difficult to separate from that model and still support the past 30 years of VBA Macros and other functions that depend upon that type of window relationship.

The only real "fix" is to use Office 2013 or 2016 instead, where Excel finally sheds it's 1980's legacy and starts working like a modern program.
