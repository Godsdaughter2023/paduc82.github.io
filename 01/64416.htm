Hi,

 

I can imagine the inconvenience you have experienced.

 

To turn on Family Safety, you or at least one designated parent needs an administrator account; the kids you choose to monitor each need a standard user account.

 

Method 1:

You may check for the UAC settings (User Account Control) if that is enabled then try disabling the same and check if the issue persists. Follow these steps for the same.


a) Press the Windows key and from the Start screen type UAC and on the right,

b) Click on Settings and select Change User Account Control settings and verify here.

Note: User Account Control (UAC) can help you prevent unauthorized changes to your computer. It works by prompting you for permission when a task requires administrative rights, such as installing software or changing settings that affect other users. We don't recommend turning User Account Control off. If you do turn it off, you should turn it back on as soon as possible.

 

I would recommend you to reconfigure the Family Safety settings and check if it works.

 

Method 2:

To fix this, you need to delete any corrupted files, and re-download your settings from the Family Safety website. Here’s how:

1) Make sure your children are signed out of any monitored accounts.

 

2) Sign in with an administrator account and find the folder that contains the corrupted files.

There are two ways to do this. The easiest way is to enter the complete file path:

On the Start screen, type C:\ProgramData\Microsoft\Windows\Parental Controls and press Enter.

 

If you’d rather open each folder individually, follow these steps:

a. Swipe in from the right edge of the screen, and then tapSearch.
(If you're using a mouse, point to the upper-right corner of the screen, move the mouse pointer down, and then clickSearch.)

b. Make sure thatApps is selected, and then enter Computer in the Search box.

c. Tap or click to open theComputer app. Open C:, open the ProgramData folder, open theMicrosoft folder, open the Windows folder, and open the Parental Controls folder.

 

3) Delete all contents of theParental Controls folder.

This will delete any corrupt files. Your settings will be downloaded again from familysafety.microsoft.com the next time you sign in.

  • Windows RE restores the data, settings, and apps it has set aside into the newly installed copy of Windows.
  • The PC restarts into the newly installed copy of Windows.

"When you reset your PC, the drive will be completely reset, and all your apps and files will be removed from it.
