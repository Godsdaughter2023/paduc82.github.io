You can see a video of what happens here: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JKYcg53YHMo

There is also a similar issue someone posted in the Arduino forum.

  • The board works(enumerates) just fine in a USB 2.0 port on the same PC.
  • The board also works(enumerates) just fine in Ubuntu 12.04(same pc) even in a USB 3.0 port.
  • I have also tested it on a Windows 7 machine(USB 2.0) and it works.

The board seems to enumerate once, then it seems the system resets the device, and on the second time it blue screens. You can see some of the WinDbg output of one of the minidumps here: http://pastebin.com/QX8rGstW

The FT232R chip has a VID:0403h and PID:6001h just in case you want to look up driver stuff (.inf, .sys, and registry).

I am running on a ASUS K55N laptop. The USB 3.0 controller is a xHCI: AMD USB 3.0 eXtensible Host Controller (according to the Device Manager). I do not have AI Suite installed so I believe I do not have the USB filter driver (see issues with that driver).
