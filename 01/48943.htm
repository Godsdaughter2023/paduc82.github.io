The problem is that your computer thinks that you are in Dual Monitor mode, it is a pretty easy fix actually.

Here is some steps that I took to fix this:

1. Once the black screen shows up, press enter, put in your password, then press enter again.

2. In a few moments, you should be to the secondary desktop, most things you try to launch will launch on the non-existent monitor. Go to the start menu then launch settings.

3. Once it is launched and you can see it on your taskbar, press Alt+Space+M, your cursor should disappear.

4. Press the left and right keys to bring the window onto the secondary monitor.

5. At this point, go to the display setting and change them where it says Multiple Displays to Only Show 1.

This should solve the issue it sounds like you are having!
