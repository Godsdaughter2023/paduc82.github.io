The problem is Windows 10 requires PAE, NX, and SSE2 to be supported on your processor

Old P4's (Willamette cores from about 2000 and Northwood cores from 2002) do not have the NX bit, more recent P4's (Presscott cores from 2004 and later) do.

NX stands for No Execute page protection. NX processor bit allows the processor to help and safeguard PC from malicious attacks from hackers and crackers. For NX bit to be set, PAE needs to be enabled first.

What you could try is to go into your BIOS and check if you have the option to set the NX or XD flag in the BIOS.

Sometimes this is disabled - you never know, you might have this setting . . .
