Did you move between Windows 10 RTM and Windows 10 November Update?

If all drives showed up as Corrupt, with an option to "Reset Drive" from the storage spaces control panel application, this is usually a sign that your storage pool is at a version number that is higher than what your current OS is. In this case, you should not have reset the drive because that wipes the metadata and makes the drive clean.

I am afraid that by resetting all the drives in your pool, you have removed all the metadata that described the pool. Your data on the disk will still be intact, but recovering it is not easy.
