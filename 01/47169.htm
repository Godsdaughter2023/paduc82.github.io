Neverwinter - Bait and Switch - Unacceptable Behavior - Microsoft Community

sundance909 say:

Neverwinter - Bait and Switch - Unacceptable Behavior

In the Winter of 2015, and January of 2016, specifically, Arc Games released information regarding an update the Xbox version of the game was to receive, that update being the recently released Module 8: Underdark. In the build up to the release of this DLC Arc Games presented a series of articles highlighting the changes that would be coming with the release; the PC version of the game, which is one module ahead of the Xbox, received the same exact information prior to their receipt of the same module. 

Based on the information released by Arc Games players spent actual money to acquire several in-game digital items and services, namely VIP and Enchanted Keys, so that players could amass an in-game currency called "Tarmalune Bars." According to the documents released by Arc Games, these "Tarmalune Bars" could be used to purchase a wide variety of items in the new Trade Bar Store, which would accompany the release of Underdark. Some of these advertised items were high-value items, such as Coalescent Wards and Blood Rubies. With particular regard to the former, Coalescent Wards are required to advance certain items within the game and are a key component of character play and development. Aside from a low chance to randomly receive a Coalescent Ward, players must purchase them, either with in-game currency or real money. The cost for a Coalescent Ward is $10.00USD--each. 

The reveal of the new Market  was met generally with praise, as the new price of Coalescent Wards  (75 Tarmalune Trade Bars) represented a significant price reduction. The PC received the same developer's notes and the same marketplace when Underdark launched on the PC in November, 2015. Because of the large price reduction, and the volume of Tarmalune Bars in circulation, the Blood Rubies and Coalescent Wards were easily the most popular and most anticipated items to be included in the new store.

The week prior to the release of Underdark, Arc Games and Neverwinter held an Enchanted Key sale; during this sale players purchased keys, which enabled players to gather Tarmalune Bars in advance and anticipation of the new Marketplace. 

On the day of release, February, 9, 2016, Underdark was released to the Xbox community; the new marketplace was in place, but missing three items: Preservation Wards, Blood Rubies, and Coalescent Wards. In response to the community's outrage--or perhaps in anticipation of it (we cannot know)--Arc Games deliberately and intentionally went back to their original developer blogs and altered them to include new language indicating that the expectation XBOX players had of parity between the PC and Xbox version of the game was inaccurate, even though no such statement was evident when it was first presented to us. In fact, the copy of the article cached by Google demonstrates the original blog post of January 18, 2016 was indeed intended for an Xbox audience. 

The tone from Arc Games, since the release of the Tiamat Module in summer of 2015, was that the intended vision of the game was to seek parity between both versions. As such, Xbox players who stay in the loop with Neverwinter were aware--as early as October of 2015--that the Tarmalune Trade Bar re-work was coming to the Xbox at a point in the future. At no point, between October until Release Day, did anyone from Arc Games represent that the Xbox community was to receive a substantially different experience than what was advertised. In fact, they represented the categorical opposite: parity. Going so far as to include a detailed inventory with prices, which--in light of the dubious and sudden inclusion of a disclaimer--is all the more fraudulent. 

Those players--myself included--who paid real, actual money during various sales between October and now, did so with the full understanding and expectation that the Xbox players would be receiving the same content. That understanding was confirmed by official release and patch notes--corroborated by parity with the PC version--and then unceremoniously removed without explanation. 

The period since has been marked a noticeable lack of any communication or assurance on behalf of the Developer. Instead we are told "that they are investigating the situation." They claim their decisions were intentional. That the Trade Bar Store--with all of their hype, detailed inventory and price sheets, alleging goals of parity with the PC version of the game, and a lack of any disclaimer prior to release--was all purely hypothetical and not meant to be construed as a statement or promise of what was to be delivered to the player.

Arc Games has defrauded Xbox players across the world. Our version of the game is not as advertised. Perfect World and Arc Games and Cryptic took advantage of Xbox Players. Few things in the world of Neverwinter are actually required. You can get by without a lot of things they sell for real money. Coalescent Wards are not optional. When it comes to creating certain items these are required by the game, and the items you create, by extension, are also required in order to meaningfully participate later in the game.

They advertised it; they sold us on it.  They got their money in the run up to the release, and then released a different version than advertised, all the while surreptitiously altering their blogs. 

I am deeply disappointed in the developer's behavior, and it does not compare with any of the experiences I've had in 10 years of Xbox Live. This is classic bait-and-switch. Advertise one product, make that product unavailable, and offer an alternative product which, in the purchase thereof, enriches the seller directly because of the false advertisement. 


Please stand by your players; they are feeling overwhelmed by a bully at the moment. Thank you.

wunders say: I stated the path in my first reply.  In gpedit.msc, the path is:

Local Computer Policy\Computer Configuration\Windows Settings\Security Settings\Local Policies\Audit Policy

...and inside there are a list of things you can audit.  You can enable them on Success, Failure, both, or no auditing at all.  Turn all the auditing off and you won't record anything in your log file.

As the saying goes...  "Size doesn't matter..."  ;-)  You can set the size of your log files independently to anything you want to set them to.  In the Event Viewer, simply right-click on the "System", "Applicaiton", or "Security" entry in the left column and select "Properties".  Then tell it how big you want your log file to be before it starts overwriting entries.

Yes, Windows can open an  .evt  file.  That's what the Event Viewer does.  When the Event Viewer is open, simply right-click on "Application", "Security", or "System" in the left-hand column depending on what type of .evt file you want to open and then select "Open Log File".  It will then give you the opportunity to navigate to that log (.evt) file.

I don't know what else I can tell you other than to point you to this:
"How to audit user access of files, folders, and printers in Windows XP"
  < http://support.microsoft.com/kb/310399 >
The path they give in step 1 doesn't exist on my machine.  I get to it by:
  Start -> Programs -> Administrative Tools -> Local Security Policy.
or
  Start -> Run -> "secpol.msc"
