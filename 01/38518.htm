Combining 2 video of the same event from different angle into 1.

Is there not away to run the clips side by side to make this job much easier?

I;m running Movie Maker on Windows 8.1 

 

Thank you Bill

considerate_guy say:

Unfortunately not... Movie Maker has always had one single video track to there is no easy way to have two videos loaded and then switch between them. You could do it manually, but it would be pretty involved.

If you do choose to do this, add both video clips to the project area. Play the first clip and add split points (using M on keyboard) to break it into smaller clips. You want to break the clip each time you want to change camera angles.

So basically, you'd have splits before and after each time that person A was speaking. You would then remove all the clips where person A is *not* speaking.

You'd repeat that process for the second clip, splitting and choosing all the clips where person B was speaking.

As the final step, you'd have to intersperse the clips so that they alternate. You'd have person A speaking then person B, then A, then B, etc.

The other option is to look for video editing software that lets you have two separate video tracks and a multicam feature. Each track goes on a separate track and you can then choose which camera is displayed at certain points.

andre for directly say:
Hi Rich

My name is Andre Da Costa; an Independent Consultant, Windows Insider MVP and Windows & Devices for IT MVP. I'm here to help you with your problem.

If you are prompted for a product key during setup, I recommend you skip entering the key.

After arriving at the desktop, you will need to contact support to use your Windows 7 or Windows 8 product key to reactivate.

You have a retail license, so all you need to do is contact chat support and explain the issue and they will reactivate it for you:

https://partner.support.services.microsoft.com/...

You can also call support:
https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/help/13948/...

As for Office 365, if the subscriptions were previously in use on the VM's that were discarded, you will need to deactivate them.

From any computer, go to your Microsoft account, Installs page and sign in using your Microsoft account, if prompted.

https://account.microsoft.com/services/office/i...

Under PC/Mac installs used, locate the computer where you want to deactivate Office 365.

Select Deactivate to deactivate Office 365 on the PC or Mac.

You can proceed to reinstall and reactivate Office on your VM's.
