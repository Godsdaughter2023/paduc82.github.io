knightoffaith say:

Let me clarify a couple things quickly.

'Computer' is really just a way to view all of the top level in one place, which is your access to physical storage devices, ie hard drives, cds, flash drives, etc. (all of which are given a letter, ie C is your main drive which includes your OS, E is an external hard drive, F is the DVD drive, K is a flash drive, etc). These devices are your real top level.

On the next level, everything but C (the drive containing the OS) is pretty much defined by whatever you as the user put in it, aside from any files the device actually needs to run, which you generally won't see, and anything pre-installed, which you can usually remove.

The second level for the C drive, however, is a bit different. While you can add your own folders generally however you want, by default, it includes a number of folders that handle operations for your OS and your manufacturer, performance logs, fonts, etc, and the folder you're specifically looking at, Users. This contains folders for each 'user' of your machine (user accounts, publicly accessible files, as well as folders for some programs which operate outside of any user account and not your system). In this folder, you have, in addition to your own folders and files you decide to put there, a number of 'default' folders, like Documents, Desktop, Favorites, etc. These are commonly used file paths, and some have uses within your system (ie desktop contains everything on your desktop, favorites contains your Internet Explorer bookmarks, etc).

To my knowledge, Windows handles the movement of these types of folders, however I haven't dealt with this before, so I'd leave someone else to respond whether it is ok to do that, and what the potential consequences might be.
