Hello,

Thank you for posting the query on Microsoft Community. I am glad to assist you on this.

Kindly try the below and check if it helps.

  • Open PowerShell with admin rights. To do so, type Windows PowerShell in the search box to see PowerShell in results, right-click on PowerShell, and then click Run as administrator option.
  • In the PowerShell prompt, type the following command and press Enter key.

Get-Appxpackage –Allusers

  • Scroll down and locate the entry of the app that you want to reinstall and copy the package name. For instance, if you’re looking to reinstall Store app, locate Windows Store entry, and then copy the package name of Windows Store.

Tip: After selecting the PackageFullName, use Ctrl + C to copy. You won’t be able to right-click and copy.

  • Finally, execute the following command:

Add-AppxPackage -register “C:\Program Files\WindowsApps\<PackageFullName>” –DisableDevelopmentMode

In the above command, replace PackageFullName with the package name of the Windows Store or any other app that you copied in Step 3 and replace “C” with the drive letter of the driver where Windows 10 is installed.

a. user say:

Okay then.

If there is still an interest.

Please search, in this forum, for a posting related to "Windows 10 mail app not syncing sent folder" dated February 11, 2017 from MiguelCarrero.

Here's what I did.

And now my sent folder updates perfectly.


I have both a GMail and a Road Runner (via Time Warner cable) email account.
This issue (sent emails not updating) never affected the Road Runner account.

Open the MS Mail app
L click on the GMail account to select it.
Under "Folders", R click on "Sent Mail".
L click on "Remove from favorites".
End the MS Mail app

Start GMail and get logged in.
On the left side of the GMail user interface, I had a folder (Gmail calls them labels) named "[Gmail]".
Under that , I had a sub folder (label) titled "[Imap]/Sent Mail".
Because I have almost 200 email messages in the "[Imap]/Sent Mail" folder, I chose to rename it instead of deleting it. (This is where I deviated a bit from your post.)
R click on the down arrow all the way to the right of the "[Imap]/Sent Mail" folder, and L click on "Edit".
You will see a dialog that allows you to modify the the "Label name:".
I changed mine to "[Imap]/Rays Sent Mail".
L click on the "save" button.

At this point I ended GMail and restarted the MS Mail app.

Under "Folders" I L clicked on "More".
I then scrolled down to find the "Sent Mail" folder (Not any of that "[Imap]" business).
R click on the "Sent Mail" folder, and L click on the "Add to favorites" option.

I then L clicked on the "Sent Mail" folder, now appearing on the left, to select it.
Then chose to "sync" it by clicking on the "syncing" icon in the upper right corner of the mail app.

That was it.
At that point, I could once again "see" all of my sent emails.

I tested things pretty well after the above sending myself emails and sure enough my "Sent Mail" folder does once again update as it should.

I can't explain why I had an "[Imap]/Sent Mail" folder to begin with.
I'm certainly not a GMail expert.
