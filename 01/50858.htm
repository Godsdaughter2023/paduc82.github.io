cmdrkeene say:

I had assumed that logging in with a second Microsoft account would give me an entirely separate "Documents" folder from my first Microsoft account login on the same PC.

Nope on this one.  Your account is the same on the PC and everything. Linking a Microsoft Account to it is just a relationship, not much unlike logging into some website by using Facebook to login.  You can unlink and re-link to a different Microsoft Account at any time under PC Settings > Accounts (just click "Disconnect" from the Microsoft Account).  Although that's not something you'd want to do often, it requires a logoff/logon, and linking again will cause a full OneDrive re-sync with the new account you link.

But no, your local Documents folder and everything stay right where they are regardless of which Microsoft Account you link to, or none at all.

If you don't link to any MS Account, you can still login to other apps using a Microsoft Account (login to each app separately).  OneDrive is the only exception to this: it will only work if you login to the PC using a Microsoft Account, and it will only sync with that account. Unlike other apps, you can't login to OneDrive separately when you don't have an MS Account linked to your local profile.

PS. A lot of this catches people new, but Windows 7 lets you link to your Microsoft Account also. It didn't provide as many awesome abilities like backups and stuff, but it did let you stream your music collection from anywhere, and sync your passwords.  The Windows 8 "link your MS Account" is just the evolution from this:

ken blake say:

On Thu, 29 Jul 2010 00:06:59 +0000, alainplateau wrote:

I have a LaserJet 6L from HP and I am not able to print...  I have windows 7.  I never had this problem with other windows version before...

What I have to do?????

Whenever you change to a new version of Windows, you need to install
drivers for printers, scanners, video cards, etc., that have been
written for that new version of Windows.  And also note that you need
different drivers for a 32-bit version of Windows and a 64-bit
version.

It's up to the manufacturer of the hardware to write and make
available for download those drivers. Especially if your hardware is
older, it may  well be that the manufacturer has decided that it's not
worth his time and effort to develop drivers for hardware that he is
no longer selling.
Check with the manufacturer or on their web site to see if a driver
exists. If it doesn't, you're out of luck.

So in the future, before you do such an upgrade, be sure that you've
done the appropriate research to find out what drivers don't exist,
and therefore what hardware you will have to replace if you upgrade.

Ken Blake

no.compromise say:

A noisy fan can be caused by a few things - a faulty fan, a poorly made fan, or a fan that is clogged with dust.

Consider seeing if you are able to clean the fan with compressed air.  Ensure there is no obstruction in the fan.  Your laptop service manual, or even the owner's manual, may include details on how to go about doing this.  Else, the other option would seem to be replacing the fan.  If the laptop is new / under warranty, you may be able to contact the manufacturer about the problem.
