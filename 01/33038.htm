Freezing and crashing are two different things.  

     
  • Freeze means that the program interface becomes unresponsive; when you click on something, nothing happens and you may hear a sound indicating that your clicks have filled the Windows buffer to overflowing so you might as well stop clicking until the interface becomes responsive again. If it doesn't, the program has 'hung', so a freeze is known as an 'application hang'
     
  • Crash means that the program stops working and closes down, always showing an error message to that effect before doing so. A crash is known as an 'application error.'

slance310 say:

Hi,

This is usually caused by a feature in Win 8, 8.1, and 10 called Fast Startup....

The machine shuts down and restarts because a driver of some type doesn't work well with Fast Startup...

The easiest way to tell if this is the problem is to turn it off and see what happens.

This feature only works when you do a SHUTDOWN and then Boot.

It doesn't effect a RESTART.

To turn it off or on...

Go to Control panel...Power Options, and select Choose What the Power Buttons Do on the left.

Then select Change Settings That Are Currently Unavailable near the top center of screen...

Lower down on the window, uncheck Fast Startup.
