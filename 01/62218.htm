darleen pen say:

Hi,

Firewall rules allow traffic through the Firewall that can be configured to specify the program, service, protocol, or port. For further information about how the rule works and how it was added to your Windows Firewall, it will be best to contact the support team of the program. They will be the best ones that can discuss the features and information that you need to know about the rule that they developed.

An administrator can delete any rule in Windows Firewall, but before deleting a rule, it will be best to review the security settings that are included in it.

Here are the steps on how to delete a rule in Windows Firewall:

  1. Open the Control Panel and then click on System and Security.
  2. Choose Windows Defender Firewall and then click on Advanced settings.
  3. On the left panel, click on Inbound Rules.
  4. Look for the rule that you want to delete, click on it and then on the right panel, select Delete.
  5. On the left panel, click on Outbound Rules.
  6. Repeat step 5.
  7. Restart your computer.

If further assistance is needed, we are here to help.
