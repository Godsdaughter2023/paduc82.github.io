jaymo1978 say:

Hi Danny! It sounds to me like the best option for you here is to set your audio out to Bitstream either Dolby Digital or DTS (depending on which sounds/works best for you.) The way you describe it, it sounds like the HDMI cable is going directly from your One to your TV, without going through an HDMI pass-through on your SS system. The 5.1 through HDMI wouldn't work entirely, because I'm assuming your TV by itself is not a Surround Sound TV, hence needing the SS system besides.

So, by selecting 5.1 over HDMI, your One is sending the surround signal to the TV which is then trying to play a surround sound signal through its built-in stereo speakers, which won't work.

Bitstream is the audio format which sends surround sound signal via optical cable on the One. By setting this setting to a Surround, the One sends audio through the Optical cable directly to your SS system, which takes care of decoding the multi-channel audio.

The other option would be to run your HDMI out from your ONE into the SS system, then out from the SS system/Blu Ray into the TV. This would allow the Surround Signal to pass Via HDMI to the surround system, and the video would pass through to the TV (IF your SS system supports HDMI passthrough, which some do not.)

Hope this helps!

therealmagaman say:

The issue is your tv is unable to decode the 5.1 over HDMI. There are 5 channels of audio plus 1 channel for subwoofer they go in this order (Left, right, center, Surround Left, Surround Right). Since your TV is only stereo it's only playing channels 1 and 2, left and right, and commentary is obviously on channel 3 center. Some TV can Downmix, where they take all 5 channels and mix them into 2 stereo channels.

What I would do is set the HDMI back to Stereo so you get the correct 2 channels of audio(the Xbox does the downmix) and then since you are using optical to your surround sound system, set optical to Bitstream and DD 5.1 or DTS is your system supports it.

koop10010 say:

Set your audio over HDMI to stereo since that is going directly to your TV, no need for anything fancy.  

Set your audio over optical to Bitstream either Dolby or DTS, as I just explained in another thread, I recommend Dolby.

SOLVED.
Follow these steps to get the flashing to stop.

Step 1
1. Press Ctrl + Alt + Delete 
2. If the screen to select task manager does not display then it is most likely a driver issue. If you can select task manager then skip step 1 and go to steps 2 or 3. One of them will fix it.
3. To update your driver restart your computer and at the login screen hold down shift and click on the power symbol and click restart. This will give you the option of starting in safe mode. Select safe mode with network and then download driver and update. 

Step 2
1. Press Ctrl + Alt + Delete and then select task manager.
2. Select File in the top left corner and then select create new task.
3. Click create this task with admin privileges.
4. You need to type appwiz.cpl in the open box. Because of the flashing click in the box then type one letter, click again then type another, if it doesnt take a letter, click again and type the letter again. It can be frustrating but with a little practice you can time the blinking and type it in. Again its appwiz.cpl
5. Scroll down to icloud and click on it. Press yes to uninstall. After icloud is uninstalled, restart your computer.
6. If your desktop is still flashing go to Step 3.

Step 3 
1. If you are here then you probably have Norton installed. The ONLY way to remove all of Norton is to use their uninstall tool. 
2. You will need another computer at this point, unless rebooting in safe mode with networking will allow you to download and execute the file. 
3. Go to https://support.norton.com/sp/en/us/home/current/solutions/kb20080710133834EN_EndUserProfile_en_us and click download.
4. Save the file to a thumb drive. (open downloads and transfer the removal tool to a removable drive)
5. Pray
6. Put the thumb drive in one of your usb slots.
7. 1. Press Ctrl + Alt + Delete and then select task manager. Click File Open New Task. Then click browse.
8. Be patient because this could be running very slow because your system resources are probably at 99%. Either press the up arrow to the left or the search & nav box or scroll through organize until you see This PC. Click on it and look for you thumb drive. Its probably k, J, etc.
9. Click on your drive, click on the file, and follow the directions. 
10. Your screen will probably stop flashing before the restart your pc box comes up. Click restart and the flashing screen is fixed.
*In step 7 you can type the location of the file if you know the drive letter of your thumb drive. For example j:/Norton_Removal_Tool.exe
I would rename the file to a.exe since typing will be difficult. 
Hope one of the steps works. Please reply back if one of these steps do not work.
