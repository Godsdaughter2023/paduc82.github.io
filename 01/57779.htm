No Sound

1) If you have external speakers - are they connected properly to the computer? If they need power are they plugged in? Are they turned on?

3) Have you set the correct sound device? Click Start, type: sound. Then click Sound under Control Panel. Then set the default sound device.


More options:

4) Is the sound device disabled?

A. Right-click the speaker icon on your taskbar

B. Select "Playback Devices"

C. In the white empty space right click and select both Show Disconnected/Disabled Devices

D. Do you see icons for your sound device now?  

E. If you now see it you may need to right click on the correct sound device and select "Set as default device"


5) You can also try the troubleshooter below to discover any connection issues that may exist, instructions are below:

A. Click 'Start' > Select 'Control Panel'

B. Type troubleshooting in the top-right search field > Select 'Troubleshooting'

C. Click 'Hardware and Sound' > Select 'Playing Audio'.

Low Volume (or no volume):

arminatorx say:

...the thing is i see this is a common problem people are having and It seems that its a design flaw. why has xbox not not made a recall/repair offer not available? or is there?

The reason why you might think it's a "common problem" is, because if you search for an issue on the internet, you will ONLY see posts regarding that issue. 

No person in their right mind will ever go to an internet forum and tell you "Hey guys, I just put in the disc in my Xbox One and it worked! It simply detected the disc and it worked flawlessly. I'll post again tomorrow, if the disc is read properly again!"

You will ALWAYS read posts like "My console won't read my disc. Help!"

And since you only read posts about discs failing to read, you might get the impression it's a "common" problem. 

But let's try to help you with that issue. Sometimes, there might be a program that is stuck accessing the drive, that blocks the operating system to properly detect a disc, as the program that last accessed the drive might have crashed, and won't "let go" of the drive. Also, even though I take good care of my discs, sometimes "invisible" dirt (or as I call them "phantom fingerprints") give the console trouble reading a disc. 

So let's kill two birds with one stone, and do the following to resolve both:

Eject the disc that is not detected, and then hold the power button on the console for about 10 seconds. 

This will end all programs and completely shut down the console ending the stuck disc accessing process, compared to having it in "instant on" mode, where the console is constantly running in the background, downloading and updating your games and it's system. 

Turn the console again, and it should show a green bootup screen. 

Since that boot process takes a few minutes, take your disc to the kitchen, and apply a small drop of mild dishwasher soap to the silver surface of the disc. 

Hold the disc with one finger in the center hole, and the other fingers only on the edge of the disc. With wet fingers, "massage" the soap around the silver side with spiraling motions. After the complete silver surface is covered in soap, rinse the disc under running water. Shake the disc several times and wipe the remaining drops with a soft, lint-free cloth. 

When you return to the console, it should have finished booting, and you can insert the disc. 

The console should now detect the disc properly.
