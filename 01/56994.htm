Forced gamerscore reset

Why a forced gamerscore reset is imposed. A gamerscore reset is imposed on a player's Xbox LIVE account when he or she gains achievements in a game through external tools and by avoiding gameplay. Xbox LIVE is committed to keeping the service free of this type of behavior, as it violates the Xbox LIVE Terms of Use. When abnormal achievement or gamerscore activity is found, Xbox LIVE modifies the gamerscore of players who meet specific and automated criteria.

An Xbox LIVE player who obtains his or her gamerscore in a fraudulent way will:

◦Find his or her gamerscore reset to zero.

◦Be unable to regain all previously earned achievements.

◦Be labeled as a “cheater” for the community to view.

Although players who are identified by these criteria will start from a zero gamerscore, they will still be able to gain achievements for future games. In other words, Xbox LIVE gives these players another chance to earn future achievements by earning them as other “fair” players do.

The original gamerscore reset is permanent; there is no appeal.

Hi Ryan,

If the display on your computer still dims even if the adaptive brightness has been disabled, then there's a possibility that the Display Power Saving Technology or the Adaptive Contrast Enhancement has been enabled. To disable both options, follow these steps:

  1. Search for and launch Intel Graphics and Media Control Panel.
  2. Click Power > Battery.
  3. Uncheck Display Power Saving Technology, then click Apply.
  4. Go back to the Intel Graphics and Media Control Panel main menu.
  5. Click Advanced > Media > Image Enhancement.
  6. Uncheck Adaptive Contrast Enhancement and include as well the Film Mode Detection, then click Apply.

Please update this thread with the result so I can assist you further if needed.

a. user say:

To fix this

  • Go to Control Panel then to Program and Features
  • On the Top left hand side corner click the third item =>Turn Windows Features on or  off 

  • Click the Internet Information Services check box and let Windows turn on the feature.
  • Once the Feature is installed go BACK to ALL Control Panel Iteams.
  • Click the third iteam on the first line => Administrative Tools
  • Once you click that a new Window will open showing you the administrative tools
  • Click the sixth iteam on the list => Internet Information Services(IIS) Manger
  • Click the seventh iteam on the first line=> HTTP Response Headers
  • On the Right Side there will be column called actions 
  • Click Set Comman Header from that list.

  • Click on the item check boxEnable HTTP keep-alive
  • Press OK 
  • I would recommend to restart the computer but if you want you can start to download...( No harm done )
  • Then start downloading

Hope this was helpful ! Enjoy!
