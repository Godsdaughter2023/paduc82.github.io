Xbox One Reputation System Guide V1.00

Xbox One Reputation System Guide

Rusted Ace2013

Version 1.00(7-12-2015)

Introduction:

Ever wonder why you have an Avoid Me reputation or are you just curious how the reputation system works on the Xbox One? If you are reading this, you came to the right place! This FAQ will discuss, to the best of my knowledge and current information, the Xbox One's reputation system, how it works, and why it matters to you. It will also discuss how to raise your reputation and some of the most common behaviors to avoid while playing online.

1. Basic Overview:

The Xbox One's reputation system is designed to encourage good behavior online and to sort the good people from the troublemakers. The reputation system consists of these grades:
 

Good(Green): This reputation level is where most people will fall into and is the default reputation level. As long as you exhibit good behavior online, your reputation shouldn't drop below this level.

Needs Work(Yellow Green): This reputation level kicks in once a gamertag has a lot of confirmed reports filed against it. Consider working on your behavior before your reputation continues to drop.

Needs Work(Orange): This reputation level serves as a final warning to a gamertag before it loses access to certain features and being matched only with other low reputation players due to a continued pattern of bad behavior. Improve your behavior right now.

Avoid Me(Red): This reputation level is reserved for those that behave poorly on Xbox Live and have made no effort to improve their behavior to a reasonable standard. If you find yourself in this zone, you will only be matched with other Avoid Me players and will find that you cannot broadcast gameplay. Communication features may be restricted. Improve your behavior to avoid being reported and your reputation should start rising which will lift those restrictions.

Taken from: https://enforcement.xbox.com/Home/HowTo#sub-topic0-0

There is a fifth reputation level above Good:
 

Superstar(Light Blue): This reputation level is reserved for people that showcase excellent behavior over an extended period of time and have no confirmed reports against him or her. If your reputation is at this level, congratulations and keep up the excellent work! Currently, only Zunam has achieved this rare reputation level.

 

2. Checking Your Reputation Status:

To check your reputation status, you can go to the Friends App to view your own profile, https://account.xbox.com/en-US/Profile, or the Reputation page on the Xbox Enforcement website: https://enforcement.xbox.com/ At the enforcement site, you can see how many negative feedback reports were made against you and what type they are within the past 6 months which can show you what aspects of your behavior you need to work on.

 

3. Types of Undesirable Behavior:

Here is the list of examples of behavior that can lead to a player having a low reputation level:

  • Team killing: The act of killing your teammates in shooter games.
  • Quitting matches early
  • Cheating: Abusing glitches or running modified game code.
  • Trash talking
  • Excessive profanity
  • Complaining about teammates
  • Screaming/shouting
  • Harassment of any kind
  • Spam messages
  • Boosting
  • Driving the wrong way to intentionally cause other players to crash in racing games
  • Playing music over your headset
  • Trolling/griefing: Gameplay behavior that causes your team to lose or have a hard time
  • Offensive user created content
  • Racism
  • Homophobic comments

This list may not contain every example of bad behavior while gaming, but it serves to create an idea of actions that players should consider avoiding.

 

4. How to Improve Your Reputation:

To improve your reputation on Xbox Live, play online while not getting negative feedback. Check https://enforcement.xbox.com/Home/Reputation to see what aspects of your behavior are causing others to report you for. If your reputation is in either of the Needs Work zones, you won't have trouble matchmaking into sessions. If your reputation is Avoid Me, you may have trouble using matchmaking to find sessions with other Avoid Me players due to the low population of people in the Avoid Me zone. In that situation, you can play Co-op games and modes such as Exo Zombies from Call of Duty: Advanced Warfare, Evolve, Assassin's Creed: Unity, and Minecraft with those on your friends and followers lists.

 

5. Frequently Asked Questions:

1. How is my reputation calculated? Everyone's reputation on Xbox Live is calculated using a secret formula to obtain a reputation score. Each reputation level covers a certain score range. Verified reports of bad behavior will cause that score to be lowered while anything other than a negative report will raise your score. This post on the Halo Waypoint website hints at a points system: https://www.halowaypoint.com/en-us/community/blog-posts/halo-community-update-5-1-15

2. Who can report me? Anyone you play with as well as certain games such as Halo: The Master Chief Collection that have an automated system in place that tracks certain actions such as quits and teamkilling.

3. If multiple people reported me during the same session, how many reports does it count as? If they reported you for the same action within a short time span, the reports will be lumped into one report. If they reported you for multiple things, it only counts as one report per category. If the same person files multiple reports in a short time span, it is also lumped into one report.

4. If I rack up many reports in a short period of time, will my reputation automatically go down? No, your reputation will not go down until someone from the Xbox Live Policy Enforcement Team reviews the reports. If the reports are false, they will have no impact on your reputation. If the reports are confirmed, your reputation score will lower which could cause you to move to a lower zone.

5. Will I be notified if my reputation changes? An automated Xbox Live service message will be sent to you to inform you if your reputation drops or returns to Good.

 To sum it up: You are responsible for any thing that your account does, even if another person used it to commit any wrongdoing.
