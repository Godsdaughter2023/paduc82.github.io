    lemp say:

    You're overthinking this.  You don't need to bridge or share.

    Please clarify what you mean by this:

    The laptop and all LAN connections go through a switch that up-links to the router.

    Is this another piece of hardware or are you referring to the 4 Ethernet jacks on the back of the Netgear 6300?  If the former, it should be connected to one of the 4 Ethernet jacks on the Netgear 6300.

    You've also omitted from your description the device that actually connects to your outside line (either DSL or cable, I presume).  Given that the DSL or cable "modems" provided by ISPs often include their own routers, home network setups can often be complicated by connecting the devices incorrectly.  Is your modem connected to the "Internet" jack on the Netgear 6300 or to one of the Ethernet jacks?


    You have your phone configured as a "wifi hotspot."  Thus, you have two wireless routers available for connection.   One with a gateway address of 10.0.0.1 (the Netgear 6300) and the other with a gateway address of 192.168.43.1 (the phone).  I assume that what you mean by the following

    With both connections active I can successfully connect to both the LAN and WiFi. 

    is that you have the laptop connected by an Ethernet cable to your router AND have it connected by wifi to your phone.

    The reason you can't "access the android app from another machine" -- which is presumably connected to your Netgear router either by using an Ethernet cable -- is because the phone isn't connected to your router. 

    Turn off the "hotspot" feature on your phone and connect the phone, using wifi, to the Netgear router.  Your phone should now be assigned an IP address of the form 10.0.0.x by the DHCP server in the Netgear router and you should be able to access it from any other computer connected to the router -- regardless of whether the computer is connected to the router by Ethernet or wifi.
