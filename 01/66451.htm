rick rogers say: Hi,

Close the Documents dialog when it appears. Then, hit ctrl+shift+escape to bring up Task Manager. On the applications tab, click "new task" and type in explorer.exe, then hit <enter> to start the Windows shell.

If the above works, you probable had a bad entry in the registry under:

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows NT\CurrentVersion\Winlogon

Click start, type regedit and hit <enter> to open the registry editor. Expand the branches (keys) to reach the above on and click on it. Look in the right pane for the "shell" string. Double click it, replace the contents with just:

explorer.exe

Click ok, close the registry editor and reboot.

This is NOT you issue however.  When you remove the account (using it on a different console for example) then recover it, there is no recovering the achievements earned offline.  This is because there is no record either on your hard drive, Memory Unit, or USB stick that, the achievement was unlocked on that particular account.  The system sees it as unlocking the achievement on an offline profile that happens to have the same name as your gamertag. 

 

I learnt this the hard way after losing about 400gs in Dragon's Age: Origins.  You have to play it again.  Moral of the story is, use the "Recover Gamertag" feature SPARINGLY.   Move data and gamertags between consoles using a USB stick.  That way the gamertag is kept together with it's saved games.
