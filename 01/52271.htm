    hal hostetler - mvp-outlook say:
    1. This happens when the Delivery Location is set to Personal Folders instead of "Mailbox - (your name)'.  Reset the delivery location to be your Exchange Mailbox, then drag everything from your Personal Folders back into it.

    2. All email from both accounts will be delivered to the "Default Delivery Location", either your Personal Folders o your Exchange Mailbox.  I don't exactly follow your "it then refused to download anything", it should download your GMail to whichever is your default delivery location. If this is set to "Mailbox", it should NOT download anything from Exchange (since everything remains on the server).

    3. If you want to keep email separate, either make a Message Rule to move your personal email into your Personal Folders after being downloaded, or create a second Outlook profile with only one account in each.  This way, personal mail downloads to Personal  Folders (you won't see any Exchange mail) and School (Exchange) mail goes to your Exchange Mailbox (no personal mail shows).  The only disadvantage to this is you must close and re-open Outlook to swap profiles.
