kevin-o say:

Those suggestions did not work, let alone get it fixed.  However....

I did indeed get it fixed.  The problem was that there was no partition marked "active" in the msdos partition table.

I dual-boot with Linux, and use the GRUB (GRand Unified Bootloader) that comes with it.  GRUB, unlike Windows' loader, is happy with GPT partitions and does not care about "active" status, so I never paid it much attention.

It was enough to mark the small loader partition as active.  Then the Windows 10 utilities could figure out what was what and got all the updates loaded.
