tedfay say:

I think I've finally found an answers. I've tried lots of workarounds, and none were successful until now.

 

Here's what seems to work. 

For XBOX Music to reliably display album art that it doesn't grab from the cloud, the album art MUST be exactly the way XBOX Music expects it when you import the album. If the album does not come into XBOX music with album art, or XBOX Music assigns it the wrong album art, there appear to be only two ways to fix this: 1.) UNINSTALL Xbox Music, modify the album art, then reinstall XBox Music, OR 2) Delete the all tracks in the album from Windows Media Player (from the library, NOT from you hard disk), the MOVE them to another directory, delete them from XBOX Music, then move them back to their original directory and add them back to WMP with the correct album art. 

 

WMA files seem to work MUCH better than MP3 files, and both must have their album art embedded for them to work properly.  You can embed the album art by using the "Paste Album Art" function in WMP.

Seems to work pretty consistenly for me now with WMA files. MP3 files seem to be hit or miss.

tedfay say:

Your iTunes M4A will have to be converted to MP3 or WMA files in order to successfully use custom album artwork in XBOX Music. I believe this is because XBOX Music MUST have the album artwork embeded in each indivisual track, and it must be 200x200. Larger artwork will fail.

 

Here is the process that has worked for me.

-Only use WMA files. No MP3, WAV or M4A.

-Makes sure that the album artwork is 200x200 and is embedded in each idividual track.

    - The "Past Album Art" function in Windows Media Player should achieve this.

    - However, if you have existing MP3 you may need to use a tag editor to delete exisitng artwork, particulary if it is larger than 200x200.

    - To be safe, remove all other .jpgs, including hidden jpgs when you use "Paste Album Art" in WMP. This will help avoid conflicting artwork.

- Once your have your album art embeded with WMP, and everything looks good in the Windows Media Player libraries, then proceed with the following steps:

 

Option 1:

1) Use only WMA files, with any custom art being 200x200 album art pasted into WMP.

2) Delete the all tracks in the album from Windows Media Player (from the library, NOT from you hard disk)

3) MOVE them to another directory

4) delete them from XBOX Music

5) Then move them back to their original directory

6) Add the tracks back into WMP

7) Watch them show up into XBOX Music with their correct album art.

 

Option#2

If you have a LOT of track missing artwork, this option may save you some time.

1.)  Use only WMA files, with any custom art being 200x200 album art pasted into WMP. (Transcode the files if you have to, stripping out any extraneous or larger artwork than 200x200.

2.)  UNISTALL XBOX MUSIC

3.) Go to the App Store - swipe from the bottom of the screeen (or right click at the bottom of the screen) and select "Your Apps"

4.) REINSTALL XBOX MUSIC

5.) Go back to the START Screen. Watch for when XBOX Music moves from "Pending" to "Installing" and then completes.

6.) As soon as it completes, open XBOX Music and then open the charms bar, selecte preferences and turn off "Automatically Retrieve and Updata Album Art and Metadata."

 

The above combination of steps seems to work very well, and I now have custom album art in XBOX Music. 

WARNING: I have a large collection and it took me the better part of two days to get this all sorted out.  Hopefully the above steps can shorten the amount of time it takes for you and for others.

HOWEVER: I would LOVE to see Microsoft update XBOX Music so it is not so finicky. If the album art work in WMP, MCE and Zune, then it should work in XBOX Music. That we have to jump through all these hoops seems excesive and wasteful for their customers.

Turn your console off, wait a minute, and then turn it on again.

If you have an Xbox 360 Rechargeable Battery Pack:

Plug the charge cable into each USB port on your console, one by one. There are two USB ports on the front of the console and one on the back.

If the controller works in one USB port but not another, try another accessory in the USB port that did not work with the controller.

Turn off vibration feedback (rumbling) on the controller. If you play games with feedback while charging, it takes longer for the controller to charge and might drain the battery pack faster than it can recharge. Here’s how to turn vibration off:

To use the Windows CleanUp utility, follow these steps:

1.     Click Start, point to Programs, and then click Windows Install Clean Up.

2.     Select the program that you want to remove, and then click Remove.

3.     Click OK, and then click Exit.

 

For more information about how to download, install, and use the Windows Installer CleanUp utility, click the following article number to view the article in the Microsoft Knowledge Base:

 

Description of the Windows Installer CleanUp Utility

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/290301

 

Step 2: Hide the icon from the notification area if it still appears.

3.     Select Hide icon and notifications.

4.     Click OK to save the changes.
