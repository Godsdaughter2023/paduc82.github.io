arminatorx say:

I have watched the video, and I'd say that this is normal behavior. 

You see, when you are on the main Home Screen, the console doesn't have much to do, and can run very quiet, as there is not much computing going on that produces heat. 

However, when you start a Xbox One X enhanced game, the graphics hardware and processing power are running at peak performance, and generate much more heat than running a "normal" Xbox One game, or simply the Home Screen. 

The console has an automated fan control, that can dynamically speed the fan up, when the console gets warmer. Be it because of the higher processing power for Xbox One X enhanced games, or because of the summertime, and maybe higher temperatures in your home.

As far as the video goes, the console sounds healthy, and there is nothing you need to worry about.

If the Surface Book is closed, or if the Surface Pro cover is closed, the power button remains active. Since the power button is external, this leads to accidental power-ons when stored in a bag, leading to the "hot bag" syndrome (which kills the battery).
