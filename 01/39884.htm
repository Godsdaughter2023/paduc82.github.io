Are you using ATT DSL, or ATT 3G/4G cellular data? What is the make and model of your wireless router?

Some NAT problems are subtle and don't appear in the Test Xbox Live Connection test. Most likely there's a port blocked... ports can be blocked in your router, modem, or at your ISP. Since the Xbox 360 uses different ports than PCs & Macs use, you can't see the effect of a port blockage except on the Xbox. A sequential power cycle is the first step in clearing a port blockage:

1) turn off the Xbox then

2) unplug the power from the router

3) Wait at least 30 secs then replug the router power.

4) Wait 'til the light on the front of the router which says ONLINE, READY, or DSL is on solid (not flashing), then plug in the router. If you have a gateway instead of a modem and router, then when that light says it's ready, skip step 6.

5) Wait until the WiFi light on the router is on; you may not use WiFi, but it's still a good indicator your router is finished booting up.

6) Turn on the Xbox.

uTorrent is notorious for spreading viruses and other malware because it opens holes in your firewall to let in intruders AND because you really don't know what you're downloading (it could be the real software or it could have been loaded with any number of malware programs).  You should NEVER get anything from uTorrent and I suggest you delete it form your system immediately.  The program itself is probably OK, its what you download with it that is deadly.  Trend Micro is a pretty good product (not as good as some others but not bad) but it's no match for what can come from uTorrent - none of the AV programs are.

You are almost certainly infected with malware (and forget the almost part) - quite likely a number of different kinds. To fix this problem (if it is a problem - and I'd bet the farm on it) download,  install, and run the following two programs: http://www.malwarebytes.org/mbam.php and http://www.safer-networking.org/en/index.html.  You should also try a free trial of Pandasoft (www.pandasoft.com).  Before downloading pandasoft, uninstall whatever anti-virus program you are currently using - Trend Micro (because installing and running two at the same time can cause conflicts, freezes, and all sorts of problems).  Once you've uninstalled your current AV program, download, install, update, and scan using Pandasoft.  I think you might be amazed at how much it finds that the other software missed.  Once complete, uninstall pandasoft and re-install your current AV program (unless you decide to switch which I did when I first tried it about 4 years ago).  You sshould also try the new, free Microsoft Security Essentials at: http://www.microsoft.com/security_essentials/default.aspx (with the same caveat that you can have only ONE anti-virus program installed and running on your system at any one time).  They say that MSE has the lowest impact on PC performance while i've found that Pandasoft finds the most malware.  You can decide if you want to keep your current AV program or switch to one of these others.  You should also run UnHackMe and Security Exploded which specifically detect rootkits and services hacks better than most other products http://www.greatis.com/unhackme/ and http://securityxploded.com/winservicemanager.php.- you could have been infected by one of those as well.  I realize this may seem like overkill, but you''ve exposed yourself to one of the worst sites on the whole net for spreading malware and have evidence of infection already - who knows what more is to come with some that have delays built into them.  One of the reasons you are having troubles with System Restore is that the malware is trying to protect itself by removing the means by which it can be eliminated.  This is NOT overkill in your situation.  If I didn't think using all of these products would take care of the problem (and I think that's likely since they're all very good) then I might even suggest that you do a clean install as the only sure way to clear the system (and even that might not work because some malware can infect the BIOS).  PLEASE don't think that doing just one or two will be sufficient - do them ALL or you risk problems down the road that may be so deeply rooted that only a clean install will be possible (maybe even a reinstallation of the BIOS as well).

Next you should do some basic PC maintenance and cleaning.  Delete files using Disk Cleanup http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows-vista/Delete-files-using-Disk-Cleanup.  Reset your passwords for all your user accounts (they may be compromised).  Optimize Windows for Better Performance http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows-vista/Optimize-Windows-Vista-for-better-performance.  Run a free safety scan from Microsoft http://onecare.live.com/site/en-us/default.htm.  Check Event Viewer for errors http://www.bleepingcomputer.com/forums/topic40108.html (particularly those that repeat themselves).

If this doesn't work (and please do try to do the scans with each of them), then let's check some of your system files:

Go to Start / All Programs / Accessories / Command Prompt and right click on command prompt and click run as Administrator.

Type sfc /scannow and enter and let it run.  It will scan and try to fix some of your system files.  Hopefully it will complete with no errors it could not resolve (if it does have such errors, post back here or you can try to analyze them using http://support.microsoft.com/kb/928228).

While there run chkdsk /f /r and enter and let it run.  It will scan and try to fix bad sectors on your hard drive.  Though this is not a typical symptom of a virus, it's a good maintenance task and it can't hurt to do it to make sure your hard drive is in good shape.

If you have a genuine Windows Vista Installation Disk (or can borrow one from ANYONE) I would then do a system repair/upgrade using these procedures: http://www.vistax64.com/tutorials/88236-repair-install-vista.html along with the section on upgrading from a prior version of Windows from http://support.microsoft.com/kb/918884.  This will put your system back to its original condition.  You may need to uninstall some SPs first but you can get them back by going through Windows Update (which will find probably over 100 updates for you to re-installl - including the service packs).

That should do it for now.  If you do all these things you can probably recover with minimal harm done - perhaps even entirely.  I know it's a lot of work, but it's necessary to get your system back in shape after who knows what happened to it.

Good luck and feel free to post back with any questions or concerns.  We'll be happy to help you in any way we can.  Good luck!
