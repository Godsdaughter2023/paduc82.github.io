Bitlocker is enabled by default in the Surface Pro 3. It is on out of the box. It is on after you refresh your PC as well. I own a Surface Pro 3. You can do a simple Google search and find that other users are talking about this also (e.g. see the relevant thread on reddit). This is actually not a bad thing from a security point of view, given that the Surface Pro 3 is an extremely portable and attractive device for thieves. I am not sure if MS support is aware of this; the department you contacted might have been working with outdated information (its true that Bitlocker is normally not enabled by default, but we are talking about the Surface Pro 3 here).

That means if you attempt to go into recovery mode, the Bitlocker security policies will kick in and prompt you for the recovery password. Obviously, this is not caused by any malware. It is perfectly expected behavior, to prevent others from messing with your machine if they come to possess it. To avoid this, e.g. during important UEFI updates, or during a refresh, MS will recommend that you temporarily disable Bitlocker while the upgrade or refresh is happening (note that this prompt only happens when you trigger the upgrade or refresh while you are signed-in to your account).

*MS should inform its users about this.*

For most customers, this is not an issue because if you sign-in to your device with a Microsoft account, the key will be automatically backed up to the cloud. However, the automatic backup does not work all the time (for some reason, it does not work for me).  ---- @Kevin99x 

So, to perform a manual key backup, you have to right click on the C:/ drive, go to Manage Bitlocker, and back it up somewhere, e.g. as a txt file in a separate drive. Keep the recovery key separate from your machine, because if the key and the machine gets stolen, then the adversary can unlock it.

In other words, MS does not keep the keys. You have the responsibility of getting them out from the Manage Bitlocker tool.

In the recovery options, you should have the option to continue and start Windows 8.1. I personally tried going into Safe Mode, and they prompted me for the recovery key, but I could exit that screen and choose to resume Windows 8.1 as per normal. You would need MS support on how to reformat your PC under these conditions. --- Kevin99x

aakkam22 say:

Here are two possible scenarios:

The WiFi switch was flipped or toggled on the laptop itself

Many laptops have a switch on the side of the laptop or below the screen to toggle the WiFi radio on or off. This switch could have been flipped. To turn WiFi back on, flip the switch to "On".

The WiFi adapter was disabled in Device Manager

In addition to using a switch to turn off the adapter, you can also enable and disable it from the Device Manager in Windows. To turn it back on, first open the Start menu, type devmgmt.msc and hit Enter. Then, under the "Network Adapters" category, find your WiFi device, and check to see if it's disabled. To enable it, right-click it and select "Enable".

Take a screen capture (print screen)

You can take a picture of what is on your screen by pressing PRINT SCREEN (PRTSC or PRTSCN on some keyboards). This is called a screen capture. You can then paste the screen capture into a document, e‑mail message, or other file. There are two types of screen captures you can take: the entire screen, or just the active window. For example, if you have three programs open at the same time, you might want a screen capture of only the active window.

Note

Some keyboards or mobile PCs that don't have the PRINT SCREEN button might use other keyboard combinations, such as FN+INSERT, to take a screen capture. Check the information that came with your computer or the manufacturer's website for more information.

To copy the entire screen

  • Press PRINT SCREEN.

To copy only an active window

  • Press ALT+PRINT SCREEN.

Tip

You can paste your screen capture into Paint, which is a drawing program you can use to crop, rotate, and resize pictures.

 To paste a screen capture into a Paint file

  1. Open Paint by clicking the Start button , clicking All Programs, clicking Accessories, and then clicking Paint.

  2. Click Edit, and then click Paste.
