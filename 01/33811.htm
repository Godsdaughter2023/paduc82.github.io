Compacting messages in Windows mail - Microsoft Community

xambicula say:

Compacting messages in Windows mail

A window pops up every two weeks or so when I close the Windows mail program saying that Windows will compact the mail messages.
I tried reading an article on this and just do not understand.  I delete e-mails daily.  What is it compacting if the messages are deleted?
What does compacting do and why is it necessary so often? 

I have Vista Home Premium, 64 bit if that makes a difference in your answer.

steve winograd say: When you delete messages, they go to the Deleted Items folder.  When you empty the Deleted Items folder, entries for those messages are removed from the index of available messages, but the messages themselves aren't removed.

Compacting the mail folders frees the disk space that the deleted messages occupied.  It's a good idea to compact the mail folders regularly to minimize the size of the mail folders.
