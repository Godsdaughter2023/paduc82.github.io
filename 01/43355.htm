a. user say:

No such list. Microsoft can't possibly be aware of every printer out there. Generally speaking, a printer that works properly with Windows 7 or 8 works with Windows 10, but there's no guarantee, and the older the printer, the less likely that it works properly (or at all) with Windows 10.

If you have your eye on on a particular printer or printers, go to the manufacturers' websites and check whether the printer is compatible with Windows 10.

"... can I uninstall Windows 10 and go back to a previous version?"

Yes, at any time. How you do this depends on the circumstances:

Easiest - restore an image of your disk made before you upgraded.

Harder - if you are still within the first 30 days after upgrading and you have not made any changes to your user accounts, use Windows 10's own restore capability.

Hardest - reinstall the original factory image.
