a. user say:

You're confusing two different speeds: the speed of your internet connection and the speed of your local network.

The speed of your internet connection - downstream and upstream - is determined solely by the internet service provider who provides you with your connection to the internet. Microsoft has nothing to do with that and Windows has no control over it. If you're having problems with your internet connection, your internet service provider is the only organization that can help you.

The speed of your local network - in this case, a WiFi network - is also affected by circumstances that have nothing to do with Windows. These include:

  1. whether your WiFi adapter is supported on Windows 10;
  2. interference from physical objects that are reflecting the signal;
  3. interference from other devices on the same frequency band; and
  4. interference from adjacent WiFi networks that are using the same channel as your network

among other circumstances, including your graphics driver, which may not be optimal for the current version of your game.

There's no easy or obvious solution. You'll have to go through troubleshooting until you finally hit on  the solution. Searching on the internet for how to diagnose a slow WiFi connection will guide you.

The most sensible thing you can do is to work your way through the troubleshooting, one step at a time. By contrast, the worst thing you can do is to run a wild goose chase through whatever idea sort of sounds like it might be good. That's how well-meaning users make a mess of their computer.
