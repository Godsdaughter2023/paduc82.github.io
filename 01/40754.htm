  1. Right-click the Start icon, then select Control Panel.
  2. Choose the Large icons option from the View by drop down list found on the upper-right part of the Control Panel window.
  3. Locate then select Troubleshooting.
  4. Click View all from the left pane.
  5. Locate and click Power to run the troubleshooter then follow the next steps.

Method 3 - Run the Hardware and Devices Troubleshooter.
This will help you in finding and repairing problems with your devices and hardware.

  1. Follow the step numbers 1 and 2 from Method 4.
  2. Locate and click Hardware and Devices to run the troubleshooter then follow the next steps.

Method 4 - Boot into Safe Mode and see if the issue persists.
Starting your PC in Safe Mode can help you troubleshoot problems on your PC while using a limited set of files and drivers. Here's how to set your PC in Safe Mode:

  1. Press the Windows + I key from the keyboard to launch Settings.
  2. Select Update & Security, then Recovery.
  3. Click the Restart now button under Advanced startup.
  4. Select Troubleshoot when you get into the Choose an option screen.
  5. Select Advanced options, then Startup Settings.
  6. Click Restart.
  7. Select 4 or press the F4 key from the keyboard after your PC restarts to start your PC now in Safe Mode.

Method 5 - Perform a Clean Boot and observe if any application is causing the issue.
