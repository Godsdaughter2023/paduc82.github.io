Surface Pro 4 Pen not writing but the buttons and the eraser work. - Microsoft Community

brant paquette say:

Surface Pro 4 Pen not writing but the buttons and the eraser work.

I have had an issue with the Surface Pro 4 Pen not writing a couple times. I have seen YouTube videos and posts in this community how to fix this issue multiple ways. Nothing I came across fixed the issue for me, but other people they found the solution in those posts. I wanted to share my fix, but the current posts did not let me reply. Hence this discussion:

I have had issues with the Surface Pro 4 pen not writing but the buttons and the eraser still work.  I also realized the zoom in and out with my fingers on the screen did not always work.  This is probably key to why my fix worked and the others did not. 

Background on the Pen.

The pen works with two different technologies. You have the back of the pen which is Bluetooth with buttons and you have the writing tip that produces a field that is detectable by the screen. One may work and not the other. 

Different Fixes for the Surface Pens:

  1. Restart your computer? I have found this acts up sometimes when the Surface goes to sleep. 
  2. Check the batteries. The Pen takes a AAAA battery (Quad A) try replacing the battery.
  3. Try removing the battery and keeping the pen open for 5 minutes. Then put the batteries back in and cap back on. Watch the guide bump so you push the pen back together the correct way.
  4. When you remove the battery check to see if there is any debris over the connectors located in the pen barrel and on the pen end cap. Three little dots. They are under the clip area of the pen. 

  5. Remove the Pen from your Bluetooth devices. Restart and then add then set the pen back up. 
    1. Go to Settings. (Cog icon when you click the windows icon/key)
    2. Open Bluetooth and Other Devices
    3. Click on the Surface Pen
    4. Then Click Remove Device
    5. If Prompted Click Yes
    6. I recommend Restarting but not needed
    7. Follow steps a. through b.
    8. Click add Bluetooth or other device.
    9. Click Bluetooth
    10. Hold Button on back of Pen to repair.  You should have flashing white light on pen.
    11. On the search screen you should see Surface Pen listed click that.
    12. Click Done. You should be all set back up.
  6.  I uninstalled the Display/Monitor and deleted the current drivers. Then I shut down my PC and restarted. On restart the Surface went to update drivers but I also had it check for updates. It reinstalled the display drivers and my Pen worked again.  
    1. Go to Device Manager, I usually click Cortona or search on the bottom tool bar and type device manager.


    2. Expand Monitors to see Surface Display 
    3. Right click on Surface Display and click uninstall device
    4. Check Delete the driver software for this device and click uninstall
    5.  Restart the computer
    6. Windows update may run and update the driver for you. However, you can also run it yourself.
    7. Go to Settings. (Cog icon when you click the windows icon/key)
    8. Open Update and Security
    9. Windows update tab
    10. Check for updates and let them install.
    11. Once installed it should work however, restarts never hurt.

I ended up doing all these steps and it worked. Step 6 is the step I added when I realized I had touch screen issues.  I did have this problem surface again briefly after my Surface was unplugged and went into sleep. It only took a restart to fix this issue.

I hope this complied list helps others with this issue.

Brant Paquette, MCP

Step 1:

Using Windows Sound Recorder to test the microphone.

You can use this section to find out if the computer can record a signal from the Microphone and play it back. Use Windows sound recorder to create an audio file using the following steps:

1. Plug the microphone into the computer.

2. Click Start, All Programs, Accessories and select Sound Recorder.

3. Click Start recording and speak into the microphone.

4. When finished recording, click Stop Recording.

5. A save screen automatically opens.

6. Type a filename and click Save. A file of type WAV or WMA is created in the Documents folder.

If you can record the Audio using Microphone using the above method then the Microphone is working fine.

Enabling the Microphone.

2. Click Start, All Programs, Accessories, and select Sound Recorder.

3. Click Start Recording and speak into the microphone.

4. Recording tab showing a detected signal for the Microphone device.

If you see the Green check mark on Microphone the Microphone definitely works.

Step 3:

Increasing Microphone Sensitivity and Un-muting the Microphone.

1. Select Microphone and click on Properties button.

2. Adjust the slider until to 100 % of its range.

3. If Mute is on, click the mute button to un-mute the device.

4. If Microphone Boost is available, boost the volume.

5. Click OK to make the changes.

6. Now the Microphone should work.
