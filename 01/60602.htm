I now have a workaround solution rather than a fix and I'll give you some background to how I got there.

My PC is a Dell Inspiron One 2330 originally running Windows 8 upgraded to Windows 10 Home on the MS option.

Various online searches showed quite a few owners of these Dell machines suffering similar problems after the April 2018 W10 update.

One poor guy after failing to solve the problems was reluctantly selling his for £100 on Ebay. His PC problems were the same as mine.

Another posting described failed repair attempts carried out by PC World, who eventually told the owner, the PC was recording excessively high temperature readings that were not correct and suggested a motherboard replacement. It was declined, outcome unknown.

I then found a posting with a number of possible resolutions that gave me a simple workaround suggestion. i.e. Hit the sleep button then wake up the PC - Problem solved, no fan surging.

Hello,

 

The basic different between private and public network sharing is level of permission to share files with other computers.

 

When you have Private Network Sharing turned on you can share files only with all the computers and devices on a home group. Computers and devices on other HomeGroup network can access and share files with your computer only when you have HomeGroup connection settings enabled.

 

However, when you have Public Network Sharing turned on, any computer or device can access or share files with your computer.
