frederik long say: OK, this looks exactly the way it should. Now let's have a look at the Chrome issues.

He goes into his Win 7 account and he's logged into my mail and google docs!
I suspect that you made a mistake some time ago and used your own webmail while logged on under your son's account. You probably also got Chrome to remember your password. The result is that your son now automatically logs into your webmail account, using a remembered password.

The fix is easy:
  1. Log on to Windows as paulandmo.
  2. Launch Chrome.
  3. Open your webmail account.
  4. Change the password. Keep a written note of it!
  5. Log on to Windows as "anyone".
  6. Launch Chrome.
  7. Open your own webmail account.
You should be unable to open this account unless you entered the new password from Step 4. Do not enter the new password!

I suspect that something similar is going on with your cookies but I would need to know in greater detail how you arrived at this conclusion.

To avoid future confusion about logon accounts I would select two different desktop wallpapers so that each of you knows at all times which account is currently active.

windows 10 lisence key - Microsoft Community

stevenreynolds1 say:

windows 10 lisence key

i have a key from a windows 7 pc i upgraded to windows 10 and i no longer use that computer, can i use that key for my new computer that i built?

andre da costa say:
It depends on how Windows 10 was originally configured on your computer.

If its a full retail store bought license online or offline, it is transferable to a new computer or motherboard.

If its the free upgrade from a retail store bought Windows 7 or Windows 8 license, it is transferable to a new computer or motherboard.

If its the free upgrade from a OEM preinstalled Windows 7 or Windows 8 license, it is NOT transferable to a new computer or motherboard.

Learn more about transferring your license:
http://www.groovypost.com/howto/transfer-window...

If its a retail license, learn how to reactivate:
http://www.groovypost.com/howto/activate-window...

sumit (volunteer moderator) say:
Hello,
Activation depends on what type of license you have of Windows 7/8/10.

If Windows 7/8/10 came preinstalled with the computer, that is called an OEM license. For an OEM license, the license dies as soon as Motherboard dies. So essentially it is non-transferable. If that is the case you need to get a new license.

However, this is not same for retail licenses i.e. you purchased the license through Microsoft store. That does carry transfer rights. If that is the case, please see the below steps:
(Not applicable to you)
What you need to do is to grab the key using any Software like Showkey Plus, uninstall that key from older PC and use it on another computer.

Download Showkey Plus:
www.thewindowsclub.com/showkeyplus-product-key-finder


Uninstall the key
https://winaero.com/blog/how-to-deactivate-wind...

Use the key by Showkey Plus on the newer computer.

Alternatively, there are two more options available other than this:
(Not applicable to you ends)
Contact Microsoft at http://www.support.microsoft.com/contactus through chat support. They would take control of your PC and activate(might need a proof of purchase)
Use the Activation troubleshooter on the new PC signed in with same MS account.
Go to Settings>Update and security>Activation and run the troubleshooter.
Select I recently changed hardware on this device.
