How to read e-books on my Surface Pro 2? - Microsoft Community

al1213 say:

How to read e-books on my Surface Pro 2?

How do I purchase and download e-books on my Surface Pro 2?  Where do I go to shop?

roberteb1 say:

Go to the Store in your "apps" section of the start screen.  From there perform a search; two good choices are Kindle and Nook.  Kindle is Amazon.com's reader and Nook is Barnes and Noble's reader.

I downloaded the Nook.  When you download, you will get a magazine, a newspaper and a few books as samples to play with (read and flip thru) so you can test and adjust the Nook reader (fonts, layout, etc).

You can tie the Nook reader account to your Microsoft account (this is what I did) and you can buy books directly from the app...... but there's a better choice.

I also opened a Nook account online at "bn.com"  Set up an account here as well using the same login or setup as you did with the Nook app.  Here you have much more choices when you search.  You can even search for free books.  When you buy a book here, you will have to go to your Nook app and look in your "library."  If the item does not appear here (probably because you used another computer to buy) just "sync" it or "import" it to bring the list of books you purchased online to your Nook app library.  From this point you can download the book, magazine or newspaper to your surface pro for reading anytime without an internet connection.  When you are thru reading, you "archive" the book..... which means you remove it from your hard drive and store it in the nook "cloud" or wherever it goes so it does not take up space on your hard drive.  You still own the book and can always download it again later or you can download it to another device (up to 4 others) in case your account is shared by family.  You can also loan the book one time to another person (this service is limited).

Do yourself a favor and go to the BN.COM website and under the "Nook Books" tab, look for the "Quick Links" link on the left toward the bottom and read the Nook Book FAQ.

The difference between ordering books using the app and using the online account is the choices (more for online) and payment methods.  You can use gift cards only in the online account.  I buy gift cards (or get them as gifts) and then put them into my account (the online account) and all purchases are first subtracted from the gift card.  You can put 3 cards in at a time so you don't have to link this to your Microsoft account or to credit card if you don't want to.

Be patient, the Nook book site and app usage is complicated and can be frustrating.  Don't hesitate to call their customer support if needed.

I have not tested the Kindle app because I was satisfied with the Nook.  If you download it and decide it is not for you, simply uninstall it and try something else.

The Description of Error code 39 is Windows cannot load the device driver for this hardware. The driver may be corrupted or missing.

 

There are several causes of Code 39 errors:

  • A required device driver is missing.
  • A required binary file is corrupted.
  • There is a problem with the file I/O process.
  • A driver that is referencing an entry point in another binary file is unable to load.

b: Expand Network Adapters.

d: Restart the computer.

e: Open Device Manager, click Action, and then click Scan for hardware changes. Follow the instructions.
