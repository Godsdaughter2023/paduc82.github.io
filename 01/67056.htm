jim sather say:

I think it is pretty fundamental. :-) 

 

If you don't have a local machine account and if your cached credentials get blown away or expire, then you must have internet connectivity to re-validate your Microsoft Account credentials.  For wifi only devices this is a serious problem, because if your last/current connection doesn't allow internet access until some secondary authorization operation occurs, you don't have the connectivity you need re-validate your credentials.  You have a chicken and egg scenario.

 

In my case, either Windows Update blew away the cached credentials or they coincidentally expired on the same day as the update.  The update then forced a restart, and I was stuck. 

 

I'm unsure of this, but I imagine that if you are logging on with Microsoft Account (MSN, Hotmail, Live, etc.) cached credentials, they do periodically expire, forcing the occasional real handshake with Microsoft Servers at sign in.  If you are always on an open connection, or your own LAN, this probably isn't a problem.  But if you are on say a hotel network, or a campus WiFi network, the secondary auth, will leave you stuck.

 

In the end, I was forced to purchase a USB wired Ethernet adapter to break the chicken and egg problem.  $30 later, I was able to unblock myself.  Fortunately, the tablet recognized adapter, plug and play kicked in, and I had wired internet connection.  Credentials were re-validated and I was good to go.

 

Many others have had this problem.  I've read dozens of posts on different forums of users running into the exact same problem.  Wifi only and cached credentials are no longer recognized.  Last password doesn't work, and in many cases they have never changed a password or there is no last password. 

 

One clever person managed to find a way to break the Wifi chicken and egg problem by turning on Internet Sharing (tethering) on their phone.  They set the SSID to have the identical name to their last network.  Wifi  on the PC/Tablet made the connection, and because it was an open connection with no secondary auth, the machine was able to connect to the internet and re-validate credentials.

 

Lessons learned for anyone that reads this:

1.) ALWAYS Create a local machine account with admin privs.

2.) ALWAYS Create a Windows 8 Password Reset Disk
