Hello,
Activation depends on what type of license you have of Windows 7/8/10.

If Windows 7/8/10 came preinstalled with the computer, that is called an OEM license. For an OEM license, the license dies as soon as Motherboard dies. So essentially it is non-transferable. If that is the case you need to get a new license.

However, this is not same for retail licenses i.e. you purchased the license through Microsoft store. That does carry transfer rights. If that is the case, please see the below steps:
(Not applicable to you)
What you need to do is to grab the key using any Software like Showkey Plus, uninstall that key from older PC and use it on another computer.

Download Showkey Plus:
www.thewindowsclub.com/showkeyplus-product-key-finder


Uninstall the key
https://winaero.com/blog/how-to-deactivate-wind...

Use the key by Showkey Plus on the newer computer.

Alternatively, there are two more options available other than this:
(Not applicable to you ends)
Contact Microsoft at http://www.support.microsoft.com/contactus through chat support. They would take control of your PC and activate(might need a proof of purchase)
Use the Activation troubleshooter on the new PC signed in with same MS account.

lemp say:
Interestingly enough, the website that I linked to earlier does not include any driver for a wireless adapter.

Although it's usually a better idea to get drivers from the computer manufacturer (especially for laptops) than from other sources, if you can't get a driver from the computer manufacturer, the next best source is the device manufacturer or at least the manufacturer of the chip used in the device.

Go back to Device Manager or msinfo32 and get the make and model of your wireless adapter.  For more precise identification, get the "Device Instance ID" (msinfo32 calls this the "PNP Device ID").  This will be a string of letters and numbers starting with the letters PCI.  You only need the first part of this string, for example:

          PCI\VEN_8086&DEV_2449

You can then look up the information here --> http://www.pcidatabase.com/

I found one UK website that offers to sell a collection of drivers for the Packard Bell iGO 2000 series --> http://www.user-guides.co.uk/cddownload/Packard/PackardBell/Packard-Bell-iGo-2000-XP-Driver-CD-Recovery-Restore-Disk-419868.html

You could purchase that collection (which may include other drivers you need) or you can confirm the information that is on that web site that says that your computer has an "Intel Calexico2 (PRO Wireless 2200BG)" wireless adapter with PCI Device ID beginning PCI\VEN_8086&DEV_4220  (the website shows the complete PCI Device ID if you want to compare it to what shows on your computer).

If that identification is correct for your computer, you can download the driver for that device (for free) directly from Intel --> http://www.intel.com/p/en_US/support/highlights/wireless/pro2200bg/  (you might consider running one of the Intel device/driver identification tools also on that same web page).
