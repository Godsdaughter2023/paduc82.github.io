peterjchandler say:

Frequently see video freezes here, the issue is that the other ends am calling to have very weak wifi or cellphone connections, although usually good FTTC here.  In v4.3, the video quality periodically updated to reflect the available connection strength, with a black moment for the process in the early versions of the updating process.  With v4.3 the video becomes more and more pixelated as the connection strength drops, with a freeze as the connection becomes too weak for sustained video, or a series of still pictures, with the camera then dropping for sustained weak connections.

With the skeleton version 5.3, the call is dropped at levels way above v4.3 offering sustained but pixelated video.  Stable version 4.3 is massively better at sustaining video for weak connections than skeleton version 5.3.

When one video feed freezes or drops, it is the sending video that shows the weak link, for most connections the upload rates are far lower than the download rates, hence it is the video sender's connection, even if only transiently, that is the source of the freezes.  The transients could be that there is a large load from one of the other users at the shared point of your connection absorbing the available bandwidth, with gamers often guilty of this, trying Skyping to a cybercafe with many gamers to get a clear illustration of that issue!  

The only solution is for everyone to be on FTTP or FTTD connections with the great capacity that those connections offer! ;)  Buffering calls to cope with these transients would lead to significant video lag, and poor audio / video syncing, an odd experience

you have.

installing actually shows up. This is because some drivers rollback before the
