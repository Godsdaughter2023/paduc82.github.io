¡firedog say:

When composing a message, the email address the message will be sent from is shown in the From box:

Your two accounts both have the same email address, so it won't be obvious which one is selected, but the message once sent should be stored in the appropriate account's folder for sent items. If your Email Service Provider allows 'plus' addresses*, you should be able to remedy this by a simple fix: right-click on the POP3 account name in the folder pane and select Properties. On the General tab, amend the Email address entry to read, say, amateur+pop @ total.com. You could do the same with the other account so it reads amateur+imap @ total.com. This is what you'll then see in the From dropdown.

* Some do, some don't. Hotmail (i.e. Outlook.com) supports addresses with an extra word after a plus sign before the '@' symbol. The addition is simply ignored on sending. GMX on the other hand won't allow this and throws an error if you try.  

I use plus addresses a lot with my workhorse Hotmail account. Whenever I'm required to give an email address, I stick a plus bit in to indicate where I gave it. Email from Ebay to me is addressed to jnb+ebay @  hotmail.com; from Amazon, it's addressed to jnb+amazon @ hotmail.com. This makes the messages easy to spot in the message list and easy to manipulate (e.g. divert to specific folders) using message rules that look for the +-word in the recipient's address.

ralf_g say:

 "I'm not sure what you mean by graphics card model but this is was I see below the keyboard of my laptop: Radeon Graphics HD8530M 1GB."

That would be it. :)  There are alternate versions of drivers for different graphics adapters depending on the adapter series (HD8000) and the type (M-mobility), whether it is a discrete adapter (plug in card) or integrated into the motherboard. 

The driver you are using is already a few versions old. There is a driver identification tool at AMD which you can use to identify an appropriate update for your system. Before you actually install the suggested driver check at your computer manufacturer's website to see what Windows 10 driver updates they have for your system and use those instead. Also check if there are any known special issues that are relevant to running Windows 10 on your model.

http://support.amd.com/en-us/download/auto-detect-tool

Being in a laptop also affects the driver selection because there may be other hardware customisations in the computer that require using only the drivers provided by the computer maker rather than drivers from other sources.  This is especially true for laptops with switchable graphics as there can be customisations in the motherboard chipset drivers that are needed for the graphics card switching to work properly. A lot of those laptops simply will not ever work properly or at all with Windows 10.

"How do I know if it is a model that has switchable graphics?"

That's listed in the product specifications for your computer. If it has switching you would also see that there are 2 different graphics adapters listed in your Device Manager. It is meant to be a battery saving feature, with one adapter being low performance - low power consumption and the other (as your HD8530M) being a higher performance adapter. The graphics adapters can be entirely different makes, such as AMD paired with Intel graphics. Switching is meant to be automatic.
