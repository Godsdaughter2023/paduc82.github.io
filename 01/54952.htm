b.) net user administrator /active:yes

5. Reboot computer and sign in to administrator (You can see the profiles on the lower left corner of the sign in screen)

6. Click on the following, 'Start' and then 'Settings' and then 'Accounts' and then 'Family & other users' and then look for the 'test' account click on it and then change account type to administrator

7. Reboot computer and sign in to 'test' account.

To disable the built in administrator account that we also activated you can follow the steps indicated above to activate it and just replaced the command from 'net user administrator /active:yes' to 'net user administrator /active:no'

santosh y say:

Hi phy49,

It is not possible to move Windows from one partition to another without re-installing Windows. However, you can shrink the other partition to create free unallocated space and later extend the Windows drive with the free space created. Or else, you can also create a system image and then format the hard drive and restore it to the drive that has more space.

Method 1:

Follow the steps below to shrink and then extend a partition.

1. Click Start, type “diskmgmt.msc” (without quotes), to open Disk Management.

2. Right click on the partition that you want to shrink to create unallocated space and select “Shrink volume”.

3. Follow the instructions on your screen and enter the amount you want to shrink in MB (megabytes).

4. You will see unallocated space after shrinking the partition.

5. Now, right click on the partition you want to extend and select “Extend volume”.

6. Follow the instructions on your screen and enter the amount in MB by which you want to extend the partition.

Note: You can extend the volume with focus into next contiguous unallocated space. For basic volumes, the unallocated space must be on the same disk as, and must follow (be of higher sector offset than) the partition with focus. In other words, unallocated space should be on the immediate right of the volume that you want to extend. A dynamic simple or spanned volume can be extended to any empty space on any dynamic disk. Using this command, you can extend an existing volume into newly created space.

Create a system image and then restore it to the partition that has more space and format the other drive.

ymtakeda say:

Found the issue: A recent change in security permissions screw things up (Recorded TV folder)

Resolved my issue -- 

Thank you for making me aware that a change in security permissions for the Windows Media Center storage location “Recorded TV” will prevent the Windows Media Center Receiver Service and Windows Media Center Scheduler Service from starting and restarting as it should.

I have two Windows 7 computers on which I use Windows Media Center. I had a look at the Security Permissions under Properties for the “Recorded TV” folder on both computers and confirmed that the security permission had been changed on the computer on which I was having issues.

It seems that giving “Network Service”  read/write permission as jk sdkfj did is not the only group/user name for which you have to change security permissions. Rather than add the ehSched and ehRecvr group/user name and whatever else may be missing from the list of group or user names under the Security tab and allow the correct permissions manually, there is a much simpler solution.

Go to Windows Media Center Settings-->TV-->Recorder-->Recorder Storage and change the TV recording drive from D: to C: and briefly recorded TV on drive C:. Windows Media Center creates C:\Users\Public\Recorded TV with the security settings that it needs to work properly. Go back to Windows Media Center Settings-->TV-->Recorder-->Recorder Storage and change the TV recording drive back to drive D: from C: and briefly record TV on drive D:.
