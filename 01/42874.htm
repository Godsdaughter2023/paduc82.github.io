a. user say:

The most important thing that I need for you to understand is that Windows 10 cannot be your backup program. Microsoft has demonstrated in several ways that it is exiting the backup business, probably because there is so much excellent backup software in the third-party market. In fact, Microsoft now recommends that users instead use "commercial software" (as Microsoft calls it) for backing up.

At present, Windows 10 has just one supported utility for backing up - File History. But File History was designed for backing up individual files and folders. It was not designed to backup all your personal data at once or on a regular, recurring basis, which is why it is so awkward to use when compared to real backup software.

To backup the way you know you should, you really need to use a third-party backup program. Fortunately, there is no shortage of excellent backup software.

A community forum like this is the wrong place to learn about backing up; there's just too much information to fit into posts in a community forum. You can find everything you need to know on the internet. I can only give an introduction here.

There are two types of backup: a file-and-folder backup and an image backup. File-and-folder backup is what you'll use if you only want to backup your personal files, i.e., your documents, music, pictures etc. Backup4All is a good example of a file-and-folder backup program.

If you want to backup your entire hard drive (which, for most people, also includes their personal files,) you'll use an image backup program like Macrium Reflect, which has a paid and a free version.

You can find backup programs by reading professional reviews on the internet. Take advantage of the free trials and choose one (or more) backup programs that appeal to you.

One thing I can guarantee: Once you see how capable, convenient and speedy real backup software is, and you become adept at using it, you will never again fear losing your data or needing to re-install anything.

Don't spend your time trying to make Windows 10 do what it wasn't intended to do. You deserve the real thing.

One last point and then I'll shutup (finally.) The ultimate test of any backup method is how easy it is to restore what you backed up. With image backup software, I have restored my entire C: drive (typically between 32 and 35 GB) in under 7 minutes and there's nothing to repair, recover, re-install or re-configure. It just works.
