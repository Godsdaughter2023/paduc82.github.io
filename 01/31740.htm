does full virus scan wear out hard drive? - Microsoft Community

popsla say:

does full virus scan wear out hard drive?

  Can running a full scan with MSE  cause wear and tear on the hard drive? I've read conflicting opinions on this.
spiritx say:

Hi,

Any time the drive is used there is wear and tear however malware scans would add on a very tiny amount
of wear. Compared to its otherwise normal use that added tiny amount would be totally insignificant. Compare
a tiny amount of wear compared to damage than can be done by malware and its a total no contest that you
should scan the drive on a regular schedule.

One issue that confuses many is that when a hard drive is very near failure it is the next heavy action or use
which triggers that failure. Those actions might be updates, program installations, uninstalls, malware scans,
and others uses when the drive is accessed. That the drive failed does not mean those functions actually
caused the failure. Rather the drive failed due to use, age, or possibly was defective, and was about to fail
anyway and just needed to be used by something to trigger the failure. Of course some drives just stop
working when the computer is on but not being used and others fail on shutdown and will not restart.

You will find a lot of myth and folk tales out there about hard drive failures, cause and effects, and most are
totally bogus. Check with the hard drive maker and they can provide information on life span and expected
reads/writes and other data.
