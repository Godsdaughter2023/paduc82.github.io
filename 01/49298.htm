Hi Pete,

Service Host (svchost.exe) is a system process that is essential for running multiple Windows services. It's separated into multiple processes in Task Manager because having a failure in one service could potentially make your system crash, Having multiple svchost.exe processes running is quite normal.

RuntimeBroker.exe is another core process in Window 10. This checks app permissions when apps are launched--like your browser and cloud service program, and is background service that has a minimal effect on your computer's performance. Conhost.exe and crss.exe are both executables that are vital for the modern interface of Windows, like scroll bars in File Explorer. WmiPrvSE.exe is also a background service that manages your operating system. It's a child process of svchost.exe. These processes have no effect on your computer's boot time.

A slower boot time may have been a result of third-party apps (the other processes you listed above that we didn't mention) or other settings on your computer. We'd like to learn more about your concern so that we could provide the proper resolution. We just have a few questions for you:
