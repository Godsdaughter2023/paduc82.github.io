FSX can be very finicky.   You could try the following as there isn't any one particular fix for this problem, but do it one at a time and restart FSX to see if the problem rectifies after each change:

a.   go to your FSX Settings and make sure that your screen resolution matches your monitor's settings in Win10.

b.   check or uncheck DX10 Preview in your FSX Settings.  If you leave it checked then you may need Steve's DX10 fixer to correct graphic anomalies.

c.   go to your Logbook.BIN file (located in your Libraries > Documents) and rename it to:  e.g.  Logbook.BIN.old.   Then restart FSX and see if there is any change.   Delete the .old to revert back to your original logbook.

d.   do the same with your fsx.CFG file.   You can find that file here:  

Your Drive Letter:\Users\Your Name\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\FSX.   

Go to fsx.CFG (not the text file) and rename it fsx.CFG.old and restart FSX.   FSX will create a new .cfg file but you will lose some settings.   Again, you can go back to the original (after deleting .old).

e.   does hitting Alt + Enter make any difference to your screen?

Hope you can make some headway with this.
