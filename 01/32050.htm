nashetq say:

Usually, laptops come with these 'stop' mechanisms where if the CPU's temperature rises too much, the computer will shut itself down or to go sleep/hibernate etc. You can technically turn off that mechanism but its highly not recommended since you can do some permanent damage to your CPU and possibly other hardware. 

If your computer is overheating, the best option is to clean your laptop from the inside and its vents. Since over time your computer has accumulated dust from the environment around it, your cooling fans may perform poorly and the dust could lead to heat being trapped inside. You can do it yourself, but I recommend going to a computer repair shop and getting it done. 

If that's not an option, you can simply buy a laptop cooling pad but it is less effective than the above solution.

For the time being, you can go to your desired power mode, go to advanced options and limit your max CPU usage to 90% or lower and your minimum CPU usage to 10%. This will allow your CPU to run at lower frequencies when not needed and will disable Turboboost (Intel's overclocking of the processor) which will limit the frequency your CPU runs at, thus keeping it cooler and preventing your sleep/shutdowns. Works pretty well for me. 

You can use GPUTemp to monitor your GPUs temperature as well. The mods may cause the GPU to heat up.
