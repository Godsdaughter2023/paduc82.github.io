davem121 say:
Hi snci112400, this will require a registry fix . . .

Click your Start Button, type regedit and hit Enter to open the Registry Editor

Click View and make sure 'Address Bar' is turned on

Paste this into the Address Bar at the top and hit Enter

HKEY_CURRENT_USER\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Explorer\User Shell Folders

In the right hand pane, double click this value (make sure it is exactly this value)

{374DE290-123F-4565-9164-39C4925E467B}

Set its value to: %USERPROFILE%\Downloads

Clock OK and close the Registry Editor

You must Restart your PC at this point

Upon restart, your downloads folder will now be back in its original location

Open Windows File Explorer

In the right hand pane, right click Downloads and choose Properties

Click the location Tab

In the location box type D:\Downloads

Click Apply and OK

That's It!
