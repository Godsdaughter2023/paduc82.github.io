When you upgraded from a previous version of Windows or receive a new computer preinstalled with Windows 10, what happened is the hardware (your PC) will get a digital entitlement, where a unique signature of the computer will be stored on Microsoft Activation Servers. From how you describe these disconnects (meaning you get kicked out of the server AND your profile), this sounds a lot like WiFi interference causing the disconnection.

This can happen when one or more WiFi networks overlap or broadcast on the same channel. Similar to walkie-talkies, if one device broadcasts a message, all other devices can't broadcast themselves. 

So if multiple different WiFi networks are "talking" on the same broadcast channel, this can cause interruptions on other WiFi networks. 

Also, other wireless devices that broadcast in the 2.4GHz frequency range of most WiFi networks, this can also cause disconnects. For example wireless baby monitors, garage door openers, cordless phones and even microwave ovens can disturb a WiFi signal.
