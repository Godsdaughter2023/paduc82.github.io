Hi Keyvan,

Are you receiving Kernel Power 41 error? This usually happens because of hardware or BIOS issues. Here are some causes and workaround for Kernel Power 41:

  1. Overclocking
    Overclocking is the process of increasing the processor's computing speed, increasing its power. Overclocking is not recommended if you do not have a high-quality CPU cooler as it increases your CPU temperature. Disable overclocking to see whether the issue occurs when the system is run at the correct speed. If you have no idea what Overclocking is, you probably are not overclocking.
  2. Check the memory
    Faulty memories could cause unexpected shut downs. Make sure that each memory chip is the same speed and that it is configured correctly in the system. You could also use the Memory Diagnostics in order to diagnose the possible problems with your RAM.
  3. Power supply
    Make sure that the power supply has enough wattage to appropriately handle the installed devices. If you are using a desktop, make sure to use a power supply with an 80 plus rating.
  4. Overheating
    Overheating could cause unexpected shutdowns. Kindly check the temperature of your PC using temperature monitoring applications or using the BIOS. If the temperature goes up to more than 70 degrees Celsius, you may need to add more fans. Also, maintaining a proper air ventilation could reduce the temperature of your PC.

Method 1:

You may use the steps below to scan for more channels and check -
On the Windows Media Center start screen, scroll to Tasks, click Settings, click TV, click TV Signal, and then do one of the following:

- Click Scan for More Channels, and then follow the instructions. Media Center will look for additional channels that might be available for your TV tuner.

- Click Digital TV Antenna Signal Strength, and then follow the instructions. Media Center will scan each channel individually and display its signal strength. On the Adjust Digital TV Antenna and Choose Channels screen, you can clear the check box next to channels with little or no signal so they won't show up in the Guide.

Also you may use the TV Tuner troubleshooter and check if it lists any issues -
1. Open the Set up TV tuner troubleshooter by clicking the Start button, and then clicking Control Panel.

Method 2:

To add a missing broadcast TV channel in Windows Media Center, you may follow these steps.

a. On the start screen, scroll to Tasks, click settings, click TV, click Guide, and then click Add Missing Channels.

b. Click Add Channel.

c. Type the name of the missing channel, and then click Next.

d. Type the number of the missing channel, and then click Next.

e. If prompted, type the physical number of the missing channel, and then click Add.

f. Click Done.
