The context menu (the thing that pops up when you right click) is different if you right click on an empty spot on your Desktop compared to right clicking on some icon on your desktop.

If you right click on an icon, you get the "Send to" choice with several sub-choices including "Compressed (zipped) folder."

If you right click on an empty spot on the desktop, you get the "New" choice with several sub-choices, including "Compressed (zipped) folder."

If you right click on some -- but not all -- icons, you will get a "New" choice that does not have any sub-choices.  Selecting the "New" will open whatever program opens the item specified by the icon.  In other words, if you right-click on a Word document (or a shortcut to a Word document), selecting "New" will open Word.  Items that are associated with programs that can't create a new file (such as Adobe Reader for pdf files) won't even have the "New" choice.

So ... if you right-click on a blank spot on your Desktop, is the "New" item there?  And if it is, is "Compressed (zipped) folder" missing from the list of sub-choices?  If so, see Number 3 here (and read the directions lower down on that page) --> http://www.sevenforums.com/tutorials/28677-new-context-menu-remove-restore-default-menu-items.html
