Here I leave possible solutions

If the remote is frequently disconnected from the Xbox Wireless Adapter for Windows, it is usually due to environmental problems, for example, if there is no line of sight between the remote and the adapter or there is a lot of interference from metal objects, for example. Try these suggestions to improve the connection between the remote and the adapter:

Make sure you are within the optimal coverage of the adapter (6 meters or less).
Use the USB extender device to bring the adapter closer to the control.
Make sure there is a good line of sight between the adapter and the remote. Use the USB extender device if necessary. For these two devices to communicate without problems, it is necessary that there be as few obstacles as possible between them.
Move the adapter away from the metal areas and do not place it directly on a metal surface.

    kimdmason say:

    For the 'System and Compressed Memory' high CPU usage, here's the solution that worked for me:

    1. Open Control Panel (i.e. hit the Windows key, and type 'Control Panel').

    2. In the search box in Control Panel (on the top right), enter 'edit power plan'.

    3. Select the 'Edit power plan' result that appears. This will take you to the settings for your current power plan.

    4. Select the 'Change advanced power settings' link (near the bottom of the page).

    5. In the tree that appears, open Sleep -> 'Hibernate After', and turn the options underneath it (in my case, 'On battery' and 'Plugged in') to 0 minutes. This has the effect of disabling the hibernation function during sleep.

    6. Press 'OK' on that dialog box, and then press the 'Save Changes' button on the editing page for your plan.

    Basically, the 'hibernate during sleep' functionality seems to be causing problems for some people.

    Others have also reported that turning off 'Fast start-up' (in the power settings under 'Choose what the power buttons do') also helps. I have them both turned off because I don't value either of them, and turning them both off stops 'System and Compressed Memory' from eating my battery.
