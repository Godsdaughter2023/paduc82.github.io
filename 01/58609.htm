====================================
Your Photo Gallery database may be corrupted.

Maybe the following article will offer some ideas:

(944221) Windows Live Photo Gallery stops
responding or will not start
http://support.microsoft.com/kb/944221/en-us

(In the later versions of Windows Live Photo Gallery...
the file name is Pictures.pd5.)

The file can also be found with an Advanced
Search for .pd4 or .pd5.

Go to...Start / Search / Advanced Search.

Check the box..."Include non-indexed, hidden,
and system files" Enter pd4 or pd5 in the Name
field and left click the Search button.

After renaming Pictures.pd4 you will have to re-catalog
your pictures and some tags may be lost. If renaming
Pictures.pd4 does not solve the problem...you can delete
the new Pictures.pd4 file and  change the name of the
original file back to Pictures.pd4 and the catalog should
be restored.

One more possibility.....

No guarantees...but the following Microsoft 'Fix it' may be worth a try:

(FWIW...it's always a good idea to create a System
Restore point before installing software or updates)

Windows Photo and Slideshow Problems
http://support.microsoft.com/gp/windows_photo_and_slideshow_diag

Good luck.....

jeeped say:
There could be a number of causes for erratic mouse movement. Here are some examples.
  • If you have an ancient roller-ball mouse, the ball may be dirty or the mechanics for picking up the ball movement may be dirty and/or worn.
  • If you have an optical or laser mouse, you could have a dirty lens on the underside of the mouse. Optical mice are also more particular about the material they are moved on. Wood grain surfaces (e.g. wood finish table) or glass are two notable trouble-makes. Lasers are better than optical mice on diverse surfaces but are still susceptible to erratic movement signals.
  • The driver may be conflicting with other software. Update the driver with the latest available from Dell Support.
You may have installed some silly add-on that was reputed to 'help' and 'augment' using the mouse. Get rid of it and then download/install/run MalwareBytes to ensure you haven't picked up some undesirable side program.
