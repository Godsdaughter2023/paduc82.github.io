Printing to local port (virtual USB001 etc.) crashes the - Microsoft Community

marc-from-ch say:

Printing to local port (virtual USB001 etc.) crashes the spooler.

Hello everyone.

I've found a peculiar/unexpected behaviour to crash the print spooler repeatedly.

 

It started in Spring 2012 and prompted me to uninstall SP1 but has persisted since then. I've got it resolved now (read on).

 

My PC is a HP notebook with Windows 7 Pro 64-bit installed (ca. mid-2011, model XX971EA#UUZ).

I use it with a HP Laserjet 6P (universal printer driver 5.5.0 by HP) connected to my notebook with a USB to parallel (Centronics) converter (Prolific model 2305, driver provided by Win 7). Prior to Spring '12 I didn't observe the problem.

 

Symptom: whenever I wanted to print to the USB-attached printer, the job gets stalled/blocked mid-way (a few KB go through) in the spooler. Sometimes, disconnecting/re-connecting the converter restarts the job & gets it finished, sometimes even clearing all print jobs doesn't recover the printing function.

Caveat: a second installed printer (networked, queue provided by WLAN router) continues to work perfectly (different make/model).

Event viewer reports an error 87 (0x57) repeatedly, as well as, occasionally, a warning (ID:3, source BTHUSB).

After much searching the forums (at answers.microsoft.com & sevenforums.com), I discovered that other users experienced this when there were problems with the Bluetooh service (there are printers that connect through bluetooth, I know).

...this let me recall that I had disabled Bluetooth service on my HP at around the time that I first experienced the problem described above.

 

So, I re-enabled bluetooth on my laptop and was rewarded with a stably connected USB-centronics converter. BINGO!

It appears that my HP (as many other models today) contain a number of hardware features connected to the system via USB (e.g. the biometric fingerprint reader/validator or the bluetooth module). If you choose to disable one of them, you may experience nasty side-effects in many other USB-related services (such as my printing via a virtual USB printer port). I assume this happens through software dependencies (eg. the RPC function, shared by many devices, too). But the latter is just my guess.

 

OK. I posted this just to draw your attention towards (accidentally or intentionally) disabled USB connected devices of your system(s) as a potential source of trouble with other USB attached devices. Better keep them around than to disable them (my experience).

 

Regards,

Marc.

P.S. My system's bluetooth device is made by Atheros.

That there has to be a USB-centronics converter is due to the fact that newer NB don't feature this port, any longer (my last NB from 2004 still did).

 

Once you re-enable bluetooth service, OTHER USB-based devices (such as the centronics converter/dongle) revert to initial behaviour - spooling print jobs the ususal way.

If these people had a NB with bluetooth and also had theirs disabled, there is a chance for them to run into this issue, as well.
