malkeleah say: This is caused by either:

1. A faulty A/C adapter; or
2. A fault on the motherboard with the charger component.

1. For the first case, swap out the adapter with a known-good one that is specific to your laptop. If all is well, discard the original adapter. If all is not well...
2. Then the problem is on the motherboard and you can't fix this yourself. Contact Acer tech support. BTW, this is very common in laptops - especially those used by people in school who are constantly plugging the charger in and out.
