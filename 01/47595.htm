ken blake say:

Several points:

1. You say "I have not downloaded any new programs that would eat up memory," but you are confusing "memory" with "disk space." The term "memory" is used to refer to the place where the computer holds the program and data it is working with at the moment. It's normally random access memory (RAM), but that can be extended with the page or swap file on the hard disk. The word "memory" is never used to refer to the space on a drive (whether that's a hard drive, SSD, diskette, CD, DVD, or thumb drive). Space on a disk drive is normally called "storage" or simply "disk space."

Calling disk space "memory" isn't so much wrong as just unconventional. In a sense, disk space is, of course, memory, but the important thing to realize is that the word is never used that way, and if you do so, you do it at the risk of seriously confusing your audience, and perhaps therefore getting erroneous answers to your questions.

2. Your D: drive is very probably a recovery partition. It is there in lieu of an installation DVD. Do not write anything there, delete anything there, or do anything else to it.

But you should follow your manufacturers instructions to burn its contents to a DVD so  you are not up the creek without a paddle if your drive dies (especially do it first if you follow my suggestion below and get a bigger drive)..

3. Your C: drive--220 GB--is very small these days. You can do several things to reduce the used space there (see Bill Smithers;s reply) but in all probability whatever you do will be temporary and you will soon find yourself back in the same situation. The only real solution yo your problem is replacing it with a bigger drive; I wouldn't recommend getting anything smaller than 1TB.
