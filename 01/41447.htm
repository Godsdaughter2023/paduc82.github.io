hazmat radio say:

NAT Type is what the Xbox sees when its trying to communicate through the router


You can try and clear the alternate MAC address on the Xbox and see if the NAT Type opens after the Xbox reboots


If that doesn't work then you'll want to log into your router and look for the feature usually called UPnP.


Upnp is typically on by default in your router. Choose the option to turn it off, save changes and reboot the router.


After the router reboots. Go back in and enable that Upnp feature, save changes and reboot the router again.


Go back to the Xbox and check the NAT Type and see what shows up there and try a power cycle on the Xbox one if needed if the NAT Type is still unavailable.


Also, the NAT Type can be unavailable if your router can't connect online either, it can't open ports if it can't go online so you may want to power cycle your modem too

If you have a Retail Windows License and the Operating System is running and Activated, use Key Finder Programs to locate the Product Key.

http://www.belarc.com/free_download.html

http://www.magicaljellybean.com/keyfinder/

 

@@@@@@@@@@@@@@@@@@@@@@@@@@@@@@@@@

 

If you have a preinstalled OEM Windows License (Dell, HP, etc) contact your Computer Manufacturer and ask them if they have Recovery Disks for your Model Computer.

They normally send them for a small cost.

Also ask them if you have a Recovery Partition on your Hard Drive to reinstall the Operating System from.

You press F10, F11, etc at Boot to start the Recovery Process.

 

The above OEM Licensed reinstallation methods do not normally require you to supply a Product Key during the Installation Process as the Recovery Process uses the Factory Installation Product Key to automatically Activate during the reinstallation process.
