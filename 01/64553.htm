This solution worked for me after calling the xbox support team:

When xbox is on, hold down the power button for 10 seconds until xbox shuts off. Remove power plug and hold down the power button again for 10 seconds. This removes any excess power from the console. After plugging it back in follow these next instructions carefully: Hold down the find controller button, the eject button and the power button(all on the front of the xbox) until xbox light turns on. After the setup your xbox should start up normally. I made sure to do a xbox update under settings, system, updates and downloads. After the update your xbox will restart and the screen may blink 3 or more times. This is normal to correct any updates or display issues. vanessa yar say:

Hi Edokie,

The error 0xc000000f appears when an error occurred while attempting to read the boot configuration data. The BCD (Boot Configuration Data) File might be corrupted. To address this error, make sure to disconnect all external devices connected to your computer except for the mouse and keyboard before trying the installation again.

If the installation still fail, boot into the WinRE and follow below steps to regenerate the BCD from the WinRE commad prompt:

bcdedit /export D:\BCD_Backup
ren c:\boot\bcd bcd.old
bootrec /RebuildBcd

You may get the below error message after regerating the BCD:

windows\system32\ntoskrnl.exe is missing

Boot into the WinRE and run the command x:\sources\recovery\StartRep.exe tool to repair.

You may also look into the suggestions provided by RizwanPasha on this link.

Please let us know how it goes so we can further assist you.
