Are there some best practices concerned to the partitioning of the - Microsoft Community

pedroproiettile say:

Are there some best practices concerned to the partitioning of the disks using windows 7?

Are there some best practices concerned to the partitioning of the disks using windows 7? In particular what is the right disk space dimension for the os?
oldhobo say:

Hard drive storage space is relatively inexpensive these days and there really is no good reason not to provide ample room for for the OS and your programs. You need to think ahead (which you apparently are) and set it up to meet your requirements and way of operating.

100 Gigs for the primary partition where the OS is going to be installed is not unreasonable. This will provide lots of room for future software installations, the pagefile, restore points, etc. If you only provide the minimum required, then chances are that in a year or two, with added software, you're going to get the 'not enough hard drive space' message which will cause problems since it is often difficult to expand the size of the partition when there is no room next to it to do so. The remaining drive space could be left as one partition or divided into two partitions for data storage with, for example, photos and videos on one and music on the other, or whatever.

Like I said above, pretty much everyone has their own idea of how to set up the drives on their system to meet their needs.
