techie bear say:

Hello,

FYI, when Windows is installed and a previous installation is detected, Windows saves the previous installed version in a folder called "Windows.old". If the people who fixed your computer reinstalled Windows more than once, then multiple copies of the installed OS are residing on your disk. Windows does this so you can recover personal files should you need to; however, it also eats up hard drive space...this maybe where your 91 GB is coming from.

 

You clear out the Windows.old files through Disk Cleanup. As part of that process, it will ask you if you want to get rid of previous installations of Windows; saying "yes" is the only way to get rid of those files. I'd suggest running Disk Cleanup again, this time looking for previous versions that Windows can get rid of. I'd also suggest you try TreeSize and see what it gets for disk totals.

 

Lastly, there is the possibility that Windows Disk Management is at fault. I had the experience where WDM gave me a total drive size that was larger than the sum of my partitions. I literally had to create some blank "unallocated space" in order for the numbers to add up correctly. I'd try the first two things and see what the total size of your drive is at that point, then see if the sum of your folders equals or not equals the total (reported) size of the drive.

 

Let me know how you fare.

 

Regards,

BearPup
