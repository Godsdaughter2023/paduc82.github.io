When I upgraded to Windows 10 it really messed my Adobe applications up.

I did a little research online to try and combat the problem per application which unfortunately didn't help as much as I'd hoped. So, I did the last resort which is to use the cleaner tool provided by Adobe.

So, to try and help you circumnavigate the mass of text on the help page in the below link (I found it a little intimidating the first time I read through it - not sure if you will as well - but still it made me apprehensive reading through the steps)...

1. Uninstall all of your Adobe applications

2. When everything is uninstalled - restart your computer

3. Click this link and follow the instructions Adobe Cleaner for Creative Cloud and Creative Suite

Once you've followed the steps for the cleaner and fully removed the application(s) from your system you'll have to restart again and then you can reinstall your Adobe applications.

(the steps you'll need to really pay attention to are steps 6 through to 9 ... 10 and 11 are fine if you are super thorough but the fact of the matter is, is that if the clean was successful it will say so inside the cleaner application. So, those steps are like an extra layer of verication and of course good to pass on to Adobe IF something goes wrong, which it shouldn't as long as you stick to the instructions inside the application- which are basically

--- hitting 'E' then 'enter' for English

--- then 'Y' and 'enter' for the end user agreement.  

--- then hitting a number corresponding to what you want to delete completely

--- and then 'Y' and 'enter' for yes - proceed 

:::: IMPORTANT ::::

Do not run the cleaner first and then uninstall applications - it will cause big problems down the line for you. I did this on a mac a few years back and wow did it break it for a while. So uninstall the normal way you uninstall applications and then run the cleaner.

If you have creative cloud application installed you will have to uninstall this last as it won't let you uninstall it whilst there are adobe programs present on your system. just so you know :)

:::: Note for After You Install (If you use Creative Cloud Libraries in any of your Adobe applications):::::

One thing that happened to me after all the above was completed, was that my Creative Cloud libraries would no longer sync on my computer. To remedy this simply Ctrl, Alt, Delete > Task Manager and then from the 'Processes' list select 'CCLibraries (32 bit)' and hit the 'End Task' button at the bottom right of the screen. This will switch off Adobe Libraries and then automatically through no intervention of your own the CCLibraries will magically start again and this time they will sync to your system every time from that point forward.
