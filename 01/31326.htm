    1. Run troubleshooter provided by Windows OS
    Type Troubleshoot in Windows Start Search box > Click Troubleshoot > On the right side, Click Printer > Run the troubleshooter

    2. Update Printer Driver
    Download printer driver from printer manufacturer's website and reinstall.

    3. Uninstall and reinstall the Printer (Credit to Andre Da Costa, MVP for providing steps)
    A. Uninstall Printer
    Click on Windows Key+X > Apps and Features > Scroll down and click Programs and Features > Select your printer > Select Uninstall
    Type Printer in Windows Search box > Printers & Scanners > Select your printer > Remove device
    Type Printer in Windows Search box > Click Printers & Scanners > Scroll down and click Print server properties under Related settings > Under Driver tab, look for your printer > Select it and click Remove button at the bottom

    B. Install Printer
    Go to the Printer manufacturer’s website and search for the latest Windows 10 drivers > Reinstall the Printer driver > Reboot the computer
    Type Printer in Windows Start Search box > Click Printers & Scanners > On Right side, Click Add a printer or scanner > If Windows detects your printer, it will be listed > Select the printer and follow on screen directions to set it up (In case of Wifi printer, your computer should also be logged into Wifi network)
    If Windows doesn't detect your printer, then you will get a blue message - Click The printer that I want isn't listed
    If you are using Bluetooth / Wirelss printer > Select Add a Bluetooth, wireless or network discoverable printer > Choose the printer > Select your Printer and follow on screen directions
    If you are using wired printer > Select Add a local printer or network printer with manual settings > Select Use an existing port > Select your Printer and follow on screen directions

    An unusual and rare BSOD story, this one with a happy/lucky ending - c0000021a 0x00000000 0xc00000001 0x0001048C

    I'm posting this just on the chance it helps someone else, although I'm willing to bet this is a very rare situation (for example, there were absolutely no matches on c0000021a and 0x0001048c). I'm also willing to bet that the odds of this matching another person's problem to be quite low, but you never know.

    I have a 5 year old Acer with Vista x64 and an AMD chip. I just replaced the battery, and ran a little maintenance, including CCleaner, then I wanted to test out the battery during it's return period so I let it run almost down (until the system hibernated, the hibernation part wasn't really intentional), then I ran ScanDisk. Everything appeared to work fine, there were no signs of trouble, until after the reboot after scandisk. At that point, the computer gave BSODs no matter what I did, restart normally, safe mode, safe mode with/without networking, recovery, etc. Also, for a period while I wasn't watching it was restarting, reaching the BSOD screen, restarting again, over and over too.

    I tried everything, and like most people, spent much of that time kicking myself for not having created a repair CD, for not having a Vista DVD handy, and for not having ran backups as often as I should. Anyway, for the **** of it, after trying everything I could (other than having a rescue CD or Vista DVD for repair), I unplugged the battery and tried a restart without being plugged in, and the computer started up and ran fine.

    In conclusion:

    • I realize it's unlikely that this will solve many people's problems, but if your errors seem partly similar, and it's a laptop, what's the worst that can happen from giving it a try? (Actually, if you're not positive your battery has enough charge, don't try this). If you are in that state of desparation, and if your battery is good, and if your error codes are somewhat in the same ballpark as mine, trying this is no more than 60 seconds out of your life, so why not try.
    • And even if you aren't having problems, drop whatever else you are doing, go make a system repair CD, run a backup, write down your Windows product key, and find your OS DVD!
