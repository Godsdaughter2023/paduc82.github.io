¡firedog say:

If the email account you were using was provided by your former Internet Service Provider (ISP), then it depends. You should have been told whether you will be able to continue using the same account, and if so for how long. It's unlikely that you will be able to go on using it indefinitely, so ask them. Your new ISP should make a new account available to you.

Most ISPs will allow a period of grace and even make it possible for mail sent to the account to be forwarded to a different one for some time, to give you chance to inform your correspondents that you have a new address.

This problem with changing ISPs is the reason why many users open an Outlook account, like the one you're using to sign in to this forum. This account is independent of ISPs, so there's no need to change anything if you change ISP. See your account by visiting http://mail.live.com and signing in with the same address and password that you use here at this site.

You can arrange for Outlook to collect your mail from the old ISP account as long as it's active. See Add a Yahoo! Mail or other POP account to Outlook.com on this page: Set up your Gmail, Yahoo!, POP, or Microsoft email accounts in Outlook.com

You can set up your Outlook account in Windows Live Mail in the same way as any other. All you need to do is to press Ctrl-Shift-T and then enter is your email address (the one you used to sign in here), password and display name to show on outgoing mail. The program will do the rest.
