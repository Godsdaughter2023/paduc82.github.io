"Limited connection" in the Wireless icon usually means that your Wireless card sees the signal and attempts to connect but cannot obtain a valid IP address.

Assuming that the signal is strong at your computerâ€™s location, there is No electrical noise around (like 2.4GHz telephone), the Wireless Router Broadcast its SSID, and the DHCP has enough IPs to assign to all computers.

Make sure that No Firewall is preventing/blocking the Wireless components to get to the Network.

throwawayhelper say:

I made an account just to post this, as I was struggling to figure it out myself as I was trying to re-enable Data Execution Prevention.

Instead of just typing bcdedit, you must tell the computer where to look.

Run cmd.exe as administrator.

Then type:

bcdedit /store c:\Boot\BCD

This will give you a list of options.  

Say for example you want to enable DEP after disabling it.

You would type the following:

bcdedit /store c:\Boot\BCD /set nx AlwaysOn
