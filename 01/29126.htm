  3. Click on the file type or protocol that you want to change and click on Change program.

  4. Follow the on-screen instructions.

  5. Close the window after setting the programs defaults and check if the changes are effective.

Riot Shieldin&#39;: A Riot Shield Guide by a Riot - Microsoft Community

rscnyx say:

Riot Shieldin&#39;: A Riot Shield Guide by a Riot Shield Vet.

I made a quick guide on Riot Shielding during Modern Warfare 2, and I feel it's time to put up a quick update.

NOTE: Reading this guide will not instantly make you successful at Riot Shielding. Most of the learning will be done in-game and after countless, countless deaths. It's about mind games and knowing how your opponents will try to take you down. Play it right, you're near-invunerable. Play it wrong, you're dead in seconds.

However, nothing is more satisifying than a Riot Shielder who both protects his/her teammates and holds his/her own in combat. I will tell you what I know about my preferred build, and offer some hints and tips on how to survive, and, more importantly, prosper.

1: Riot Shield Basics

The Riot Shield is a Primary weapon of it's own class. You cannot carry a Primary weapon of any kind unless you equip the Overkill perk. In game, the only hint the game gives you as to what you'll be getting is, "Bullet Resistant". In-game, the following things will happen:

-Your movement speed will drop to LMG-level speed. Note that if you pick up a Riot Shield when you don't start with one, your movement speed is unchanged.
-You will have a unique character model equipped with heavy armor, but this is a merely cosmetic difference that has no effect on gameplay.
-While equipped, any bullets of any kind will be deflected by the shield and reflected upwards. This includes Sentry Guns and Air Support Killstreaks.
-While unequipped, the Riot Shield will still partially protect your back from any incoming fire.

-When opponents attack your Shield, you gain points based on how much damage you absorb. This will slowly diminish to avoid abuse.
-When you deflect an opponent's attack, and they are killed by a teammate, you receive a special "Croud Control" Assist, giving you extra points.

-Sprinting will lower your shield slightly to the side, leaving half of your body exposed.

-In whatever direction your Shield is facing, any explosive damage will be reduced dramatically as long as the Shield is facing the impact point of the explosion.


When standing with the Riot Shield, anything ankle-up directly in your line of vision will be deflected. However, your feet will be 100% vunerable and players WILL aim for you this way.

When crouching, the Riot Shield covers almost all of your body. Your feet are BARELY targetable, but otherwise, any frontal fire will be ineffective.

You CANNOT go prone with the Riot Shield equipped. You can with a Secondary weapon equipped, however.

A Semtex stuck to the Shield WILL be a guaranteed* kill.
*With one special exception...

With the Riot Shield equipped, your regular Knife slash will be replaced with a Shield Bash. This will attempt to charge at a close-range opponent and hit them with the Shield. It takes two Shield Bashes to take down a full HP opponent, or three if they have a Ballistic Vest. The Shield will move slightly to the side after a Shield Bash attempt, leaving you partially vunerable. It also tends to do crazy, glitchy nonsense.

In addition, Shield Bashing an opponent will stop them from being able to fire for slightly under half a second.

 

This is most of the basic info of Riot Shields. In short, your offensive capabilities seem to be butchered in favor of very picky defense. Why would anyone want to do something that doesn't even protect the body right? Hell, a SEMTEX can kill you still, flat out! And who's gonna miss a target in plain sight?

Keep reading...there are solutions to all the problems.

2: Weaknesses to the Riot Shield

To understand how to properly use something, you must first be aware of how it can be beaten. Allow me to list the ways:

-CQC Strafing: Getting up close to a Riot Shielder and running around them, hip-firing all the while, will make it very difficult for even a skilled Shielder to defend all of it. One wrong turn means they're instantly dead at this range. Jumping will also force them to adjust their shield suddenly, or, again, they're very quickly dead. The only option they really have is Shield Bashing the attacker, and even then, it's a stretch. They can try to backpedal to attempt to block the bullets, but with the reduced movement speed, it will be difficult without allies.

However, if their sensitivity is high enough and they're skilled enough, an experienced Riot Shielder will be able to fend off even a close range attack like this with good frequency.

-Semtex Stick: As mentioned, a stick means instant death...with one exception.

-Knifing: In the two slow, awkward bashes that a Shielder can land on an opponent, it only takes one swift knife attack to kill them. In close ranges, if they're not careful, they're vunerable from the head and the sides. In addition, after a Shield Bash, their body and side are slightly vunerable to a quick knife counterattack. It's never a guarantee, because the knife CAN be blocked by the Shield, but it requires proper positioning by the Shielder.

-Rockets/Grenade Launchers: While all explosive damage is drastically reduced in whatever direction the Shield is facing, anything to the side or behind the Shielder is fair game. Launching a Grenade or Rocket right behind the Shielder will put a quick end to their efforts.

-Waiting for them to try to do anything: Throwing Equipment, switching weapons, sprinting, Shield Bashing...all of these move the Shield to more favorable positions. A Riot Shielder will NEVER be able to pull out a weapon fast enough to shoot anything! Absolute madness...

-Flanking to their backs: A Shield can only cover on side at a time, and even if it's on their back, their body is mostly exposed still. Flanking around to their sides or their backs will make for an easy kill.

-Stun/Flash Grenades: Related to above, the Riot Shield offers ZERO protection against either of those grenades. A Riot Shielder who can't turn or move while Stunned is as good as dead, and a Flash right in their face means they have to guess where they'll be attacked. Again, good as dead.

Of course, Killstreaks that use non-bullet-based means of punishment will kill Shielders just as easy as anything else.

So with all of these weaknesses, how could one even HOPE to stand a chance against anything? You might as well be weilding a piece of paper in front of the enemy!

Read on...

 

3: Riot Shield Strategies.

First, some general tips:

-NEVER SPRINT IN UNKNOWN AREAS!: Sprinting around a corner right into an enemy means, 90% of the time, you're dead. You'll either be knifed, or shot down.

-Stay crouched AND LOOKING DOWN SLIGHTLY!: Looking down slightly while crouching with the Shield will prevent your feet from being hit. At all. This effectively means your front is mostly indestructable.

-Stand in CQC situations!: You move too slowly while crouching to defend against a CQC spray/knife fest. Stand so then you can move with your faster attacker, and be in a better position to Shield Bash.

-Don't rush headlong into cross-fire situations!: You can only block fire in one direction at a time. Rushing into a situation where you can be shot in two directions means they get an extra kill, and your team has one man/woman down. Funnel enemies to face you in one possible direction.

Now, this is when you are alone. When your teammates are involved, you're going to be there to distract opponents and cause them to misfire. So, a few extra tips when you're with allies:

-Stay crouched and advance!: Stay in front of incoming fire, crouched, and attempt to absorb as many shots as you can. This will buy your teammates some time and put pressure on the attackers.

-Shield Bash aggressively when in range!: If they decide to ignore you and let you advance, make them punish their decision by Bashing the closest attacker, and force them to divert their attention to the lone guy with the Shield---away from the guys with guns.

-Don't do anything fancy: With your teammates doing all the firing, there's no reason for you to be aggressive: Just approach and put pressure on your opponents. Doing anything else will expose you and leave you dead in moments.

So, those are some general tips with Shielding. You'll learn with experience that there are some situations you can run into without fear, and some where you should hang back and see how the enemy reacts. As I stated, Riot Shielding is all about mind games: If your opponent reacts a certain way, react in a way that counters them.

"BUT I CAN'T ATTACK WITH ONLY A RIOT SHIELD I NEED A GUN AND A MILLION KILLS. PLUS, SEMTEX STICKS SUCK."

Well, allow me to impart with more...specific information.

4: Riot Shield Perks/Equipment

First, Perk preferences which will increase your effectiveness, albeit in different ways.

Extreme Conditioning: Let's face it: You move SLOW. With Extreme Conditioning, you can move according to the battlefield, making sure you're in the right place. You can also close the distance to Shield Bash someone vunerable. This is pretty much for those who wish to go "Up Close and Personal" with their Riot Shields.

Slight of Hand Pro: This allows you to switch weapons quickly, as well as reload quicker. With a Riot Shield, this means that in less than half a second, you can have a weapon pulled out, and instead of being a helpless masochist, you're now unloading shots on an unsuspecting enemy. Even pisols can kill in a few hits in close range situations. For those who wish to throw in a very useful offensive package with their Shielding.

Blast Shield Pro:  Know that whole problem with explosives? The whole "Semtex Instagib" thing? Flash/Stun grenades? Blast Shield Pro solves ALL of these. You're taking drastically reduced explosive damage (It's near-nullified if the Shield also absorbs the blast), you'll survive direct Semtex sticks TO THE SHIELD (Semtex stuck to the body will still be a guaranteed kill, and if stuck on the Shield, the damage will leave you in critical, a-toothpick-will-kill-you health), and Flash/Stun effects will be nullified to managable levels. This is almost a required perk for any Riot Shielder, as it nullifies almost all of the big weaknesses, and being able to survive explosions more reliably is always a huge boost. Very recommended.

Hardline Pro: Hardline alone lets you access your Killstreaks one kill earlier. Hardline Pro has a more useful effect in terms of Shielding: Two Assists will give you one "Kill" for your Killstreak. Considering you have dedicated teammates killing things you're distracting, you'll be racking up plenty of Assists. I don't recommend taking this over Blast Shield Pro, but if you have teammates that you KNOW will back you up, it could be worth switching out for this.

Steady Aim Pro: This is basically the only real perk I can recommend. All of the others have such limited effects for Shielders. If you happen to pull out guns (With Slight of Hand Pro, preferably), this will increase your aim. You'll, 9.9 times out of 10, will be in CQC if you have a gun out, due to your limited selection of various close-range weapons. However, even if you don't use guns, your Shield will be back in a defensive position more quickly with Steady Aim Pro than without. Given the other options, I think this is the only one that stands out.

 

Equipment wise:

Lethal: Throwing Knife: The Shield, much like anything else, can cancel whatever else it's doing, and instantly throw the Throwing Knife straight ahead. The speed that a Shielder can do this can EASILY catch someone off-guard...and leave them dead. The fact that you can retract it and use it again makes it a worth-while addition to a Shielder's offensive arsenal.

Bouncing Betty: Most of the time, when enemies see a Riot Shielder, they will chase them, wanting that satisifaction. Bouncing Betties have proven to be a very effective way to punish those who seek to close the distance and try to corner you.

Claymores: Same as the Bouncing Betties, but work in a different way. They both share the same thing: They're quick to set up and maintain a defensive presense while luring enemies to their unexpected doom.

C4: Works the same way as Bouncing Betties, except you make it explode yourself. Seeing how, unlike the Betties, the explosion is unavoidable, it may be worth swapping it for the manual detonation, but there are times the manual detonation could be a bit of a burden. It's up to personal preference, this one.

 

Tactical: Trophy System: I love this damn thing. Predator Missiles raining from the sky? Pop out a Trophy System before it lands, make an enemy upset. Someone spamming grenades? Pop a Trophy System, make an enemy upset. Course, a weakness to this is that it takes a few seconds to place, meaning you shouldn't ever do it in a heated firefight, but the utility a Trophy System can give is outstanding. You can spare your team from suffering Multi-Kills with a quickly-placed Trophy.

Portable Radar: Situational awareness. It's best to know someone is coming to flank your side or ambush your team than to wait and see it happen when it's too late to react to it. Keep in mind, Assassin perk users will be immune to the Radar effect.

Now, there's a selection of weapons you can use, and they can all work well, but my personal preference is Akimbo FMG9s. Pull them out once quickly when you spawn, pull the Shield back out, and you can pull these things out in extremely fast time with Slight of Hand Pro on. It's VERY strong firepower for such a defensive class, and is suitable for all Close-Mid range combat, especially with Steady Aim Pro.

So, my preferred Shielding class is:

Riot Shield (Melee Perk)
Akimbo FMG9s
Bouncing Betty
Trophy System
Slight of Hand Pro
Blast Shield Pro
Steady Aim Pro

So, with this loadout, I can offer a few new strategies:

-Wait for your opponent to act: If you see your opponent holding a grenade, or moving in to circle-strafe you, pull out your Secondaries and unload on them. While sprinting or holding a Grenade, they cannot fire back! Take this time to take them out before they can react!

-Shield Bash without mercy!: With enough practice, you can get the timing of Shield Bashing down, and with the Melee Perk, it is a very valid offensive tactic. Block your opponent's knife attack, and then viciously pound them before they can launch another one. If someone's spraying at you in a straight line, bash them, and then bash them again. With the Melee perk, you can keep them locked out from firing!

-Don't be afraid to take the fight to the enemy!: If there are several targets in close range that aren't aware of you, don't be afraid to switch to your secondaries and unload on them before they can be aware that you're even a threat. If you feel under pressure, try to find cover and switch back to the Riot Shield for protection!

-DON'T FORGET YOUR GRENADES!: A Bouncing Betty placed in a way that enemies cannot safely advance, as well as a Trophy System placed right before a Missile kills you, and your teammates, can change the pitch of any battle. Don't be under direct fire when you use these, but don't forget about their use!

-Don't even let CQC classes get close!: If you see someone with Akimbo weapons or a Shotgun, don't let them get into optimal range! Life becomes a lot more dangerous that way. Switch  to your weapons early and gun them down before they even expect a Shielder to attack them!

Many opponents are unaware that there are ways to counter the Stun/Zerg strategy that normally work on Riot Shielders. You'll have ample chances to gun down unaware opponents.

And you'll be hated. Trust me, you will be hated, but more importantly, you will be a well-rounded addition to your team with enough practice. Positioning, attacking...all of these will be more clear the more time you spend practicing.

Good luck, and hope these tips are of good use.
