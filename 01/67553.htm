judyyunkers say:

How to set no password on Microsoft Surface?

Original Title:password settings

 

How do I set my surface so that it doesn't require a password?

cmdrkeene say:

You can unlink it from your Microsoft Account and only use a local PC user account, but that means you can't sync your settings, your files with SkyDrive, and you'll have to login separately for all other apps (store, mail, games, smartglass, music, etc).

 

I would suggest instead that you have a password, but only enter it when doing a full reboot. Since a full reboot of a tablet is so rare, you will almost never have to enter the password, but you get to keep all your benefits of having one.

 

To do this, just set it to no longer require a password when waking up.  You can do this from the desktop by going to power settings (right-click the battery icon near the clock on the taskbar).  Look to the left of that page for "require a password on wake up".

 

Note: if you get company email on your tablet, your company can force you to have a password after a certain timeframe.  If this is the case, you cannot remove the password because of company policy without removing the email account from your tablet.

steven. s say:

Hello Nordlicht71,

 

To add to Chris Tucker’s response, here is the information regarding using a Briefcase with a removable storage media:

  1. Copy the Briefcase to any type of removable media.
  2. Remove the media from the first computer and insert it into the second computer.
  3. Make any changes you want to the files in the Briefcase on the removable media. Do not copy the Briefcase from the removable media onto the second computer.
  4. To sync your changes with the first computer, remove the media from the second computer and insert it into the first computer.
  5. From the first computer, open the removable media folder, right-click the Briefcase, and then click Update All to sync any changes you have made.

Notes 

  • When you use Briefcase with removable media, you never sync files in the Briefcase with the second computer. You simply gain the ability to work on these files using the second computer and then sync those changes with the first computer.
  • Briefcase doesn't work with Sync Center.

So really this is used to make sure that your first computer doesn’t have a different version of a file than a second computer. For your purpose, which sounds like you are trying to transfer the file over to the 2 computer, it would be best to just copy the files over in a folder on the removable storage device.

Hope that helps, look forward to hearing back from you.
