laptop plugged in, not charging. yet power monitor says "plugged - Microsoft Community

joseph ficaro say:

laptop plugged in, not charging. yet power monitor says "plugged in, not charging." what are some options?

my laptop is plugged in, yet power monitor says "plugged in, not charging." what are some solutions?
kosh vorlon say:

Hi Joseph,

 

Something similar just happened to me. The first time they serviced it, they replaced the adapter and that resolved the problem. 5 months later it started happening again and this time they replaced the battery, the motherboard, and the platen (thank heaven's it is still under warranty). Luckily, with the new adapters, the battery began charging and the experience did not seem to damage it in any way (but you should keep your eye on it anyway just in case).

 

The way they were able to tell on my laptop - a Dell E1705 (besides just experience and common sense) was to go into the BIOS (at boot there should be a brief flash on the screen telling you how to enter BIOS setup or the BIOS menu (usually F2) and check on the information concerning the battery. It showed that the AC adapter was "unidentified" instead of showing "90W" which it should when working properly and that the battery was not full and not charging although plugged in. They changed the motherboard and platen the second time because it too could cause the adapter not to be recognized by the battery (or the battery not to recognize the AC adapter).

 

The beeping is being caused by power settings and sound settings which are set to notify you of a low and/or critical battery condition. In a normal situation, the computer will run just off the AC adapter and does not need the battery (that's one way to help maintain battery life is to let it periodically run down and recharge if you ordinarily leave it plugged in most of the time). If you remove the batttery entirely, will the laptop power up? If not, it is most definitely at least the AC adapter and/or the motherboard. Eventually the battery will get so low it will either go into sleep or hibernation or the system will shutdown (you should turn it off normally before allowing it to cold stop like that). If so, then I would expect the beeping to go away as it will no longer detect a low battery charge as there is no battery - but then you'll want to be sure to use either a battery backup UPS or at least a surge protector if not already in place because if you lose battery the laptop will crash hard and that's not good for it.

 

I'm using 32-bit Vista Business SP2 and in Control Panel / Sounds / Sounds there is an option for Low Battery Alert. The default it to have this on. In this situation you may want to turn it off to avoid the beeping. Also, you may want to go into Control Panel / Power Options, click on plan settings, and click on Battery. It will provide you with levels for low battery and critical battery and provide you with actions to take when that occurs. If you cannot run the system without a battery and it is so low that the alerts have started you're probably down to 11-5% remaining. You may want to adjust the actions to nothing for low and shutdown for critical as 5% is about as close as you want to get to the point where you may crash. Then you just need to wait until it is repaired and/or the appropriate parts are replaced.

 

While you still have some life in it, it's best to contact the manufacturer (they's certain have the correct parts) or a reputable computer repair shop to get a proper diagnosis (perhaps over the phone at first), a determination of what needs to be fixed, the applicable costs and timing, if it's still under any type of warranty, and what needs to be done in what order. They may find the AC adapter is OK and it's just the battery (or vice-versa or both or more). But that's essentially where you need to start.

 

I hope this helps.

 

Good luck!

The attached DMP file is of the UNEXPECTED_KERNEL_MODE_TRAP (7f) bug check.

This bug check indicates that the Intel CPU generated a trap and the kernel failed to catch this trap.

BugCheck 7F, {8, 80050033, 406f8, fffff80002ec4f24}

0x00000008, or Double Fault, indicates that an exception occurs during a call to the handler for a prior exception. Typically, the two exceptions are handled serially. However, there are several exceptions that cannot be handled serially, and in this situation the processor signals a double fault. There are two common causes of a double fault:
  • A kernel stack overflow. This overflow occurs when a guard page is hit, and the kernel tries to push a trap frame. Because there is no stack left, a stack overflow results, causing the double fault.
  • A hardware problem (likely the second hard disk)
