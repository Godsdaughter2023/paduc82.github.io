run (as administrator) cmd (command prompt AKA Windows Command Processor) program.

make sure any open file handles are closed on partition you want to fix, if possible (f you only have c: this is not possible, but see below).

type chkdsk x:/f  (where 'x' is the partition you want to fix) and press Enter.

if 'x' is your only partition (i.e. c:), then select 'schedule for next restart' when prompted and then close out any running apps and restart windows.

the chkdsk utility will clean up things rather easily and lost space will be magically reclaimed.

this is a common problem when there are a lot of large sized files on the partition.

in good disk management you should partition your drive and move any documents & data (including large non-program files) to other than the primary partition (usually c:), then you can backup the entire c: as an image (which should be shrunk to its operating system size, with allowance for future usage due to program upgrades etc.).  this makes partitions easier to manage.

I had this exact problem and these are the steps I took to get the issue resolved:

  1. Navigate to family safety settings and remove the child from the family having issues.
  2. Login to the childs account and launch Settings (windows key + i)
  3. Click the Accounts icon and select Sign in with local account instead
  4. Follow the steps to change to a local account.  When completed, Login back to your account (admin account)
  5. Go back to the family safety settings and add your child back to the family group.
  6. Access your childs email address to accept the family invitation
  7. Verify the child now shows properly in the family settings and that the screen times are still correct.
  8. Login to the childs account and launch Settings (windows key + i)
  9. Click the Accounts icon and select Sign in with microsoft account instead
  10. Your family settings should now take affect.

I know this is pretty tedious, but like I said, it worked for me.  If you are still having trouble, I suggest contacting support and they can help walk you through the steps to get it resolved.

martmcd say:
borderless seems to b a common misnomer (e.g. borderless TVs do have a thin border).

in windows 10 there are two things you may be seeing
1) the border which is a one pixel line around the window, when the window is active it will show the accent colour
you can change the Accent colour if you wish - settings-> personalization -> colours and choose a colour (you can see in the sample the border and titlebar change)

2) the shadow around the window.
you can turn off the shadow click start, type advanced system settings
under performance, click the settings button.
untick "show shadows under windows" click apply.
see if that improves it.

personally I like the thin border and the shadow, but I might be the minority, you can use the feedback app to make your suggestion to the developers.

do let us know how you get on. if there is no improvement please post a screenshot.
