Hi Larry,

This problem with your cursor may be caused or triggered by having an outdated mouse driver, or could be that some settings are enabled that cause your mouse pointer or cursor to behave that way.

For us to isolate this problem, I suggest that you try these steps:

  1. Navigate to Settings > Devices > Mouse.
  2. In the right-pane, click Additional mouse settings.
  3. Click the Point Options tab.
  4. Uncheck the Hide pointer while typing, and the Enhance pointer precision options.
  5. Click Apply, then OK.

  1. Right-click the Windows key and select Device Manager.
  2. Expand the Mice and other pointing devices.
  3. Right-click the HID-compliant mouse, then click Update Driver Software.
  4. Select Search automatically for updated driver software.
  5. Follow the on-screen instructions.
