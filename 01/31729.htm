System Refresh or Restore: Some files are missing or Windows isn't booting.

Technical Level : Intermediate

Summary

This Wiki explains how you can refresh your system or restore it when you get the error “Some files are missing. Your Windows Installation or recovery media will provide these files.” or when Windows simply does not boot anymore and startup repair isn’t working.


Details

Important Notice

  • You need a Windows Installation Disc
    • If you do not have a Windows Installation Disc but you do have a retail Windows key then you can download a Windows installation disc using this wiki: Download a bootable Windows 8(.1) Disc.
    • If you have ever created a USB Recovery Drive then you can use this to refresh your system. If you have not and can still boot read here on how to make this: Advanced Repair Tools: Create a USB Recovery Drive
  • If your system doesn´t boot anymore then you must be able to boot from a disc or USB, if your system does not support this or does not have these inputs, then this Wiki can´t help you further. 

Situation:

You are receiving the following error when trying to restore or refresh your Windows installation.


or


Your system isn't booting anymore and automatic startup repair is not working, due to damaged Windows files. Skip method 1 in this case.

Method 1: Use the Windows Installation Disc to restore your system


In most cases if you have the disc or USB drive in your system and then choose to refresh your system it will automatically detect and use the disc or USB stick. But in some cases it does not, or you simply can't enter your system anymore and still want to try System Refresh / Restore, in that case method 2 is what you need.

In case you don't know how to access the recovery options within Windows read below.

  1. Open the right charm bar (by dragging your mouse to the right top corner of the screen)
  2. Click on Settings
  3. Click on Change PC Settings
  4. Click on Update and Recovery
  5. Click on Recovery
  6. Choose the option you wish to use, read below what the difference is between reset and refresh.

Method 2: Boot from a USB or Installation Disc.

If your able to enter Startup Repair (see 3th image at Situation) options then you can skip the boot from USB or DVD part of this, go directly to step 6. First click on See Advanced Repair Options, and be sure the installation disc or usb is in your computer. 

  1. Plug the USB Stick in your system or put the Installation disc in your disc drive and reboot your system
  2. When the message `Press any key to boot from...` appears press any key on your keyboard, if this message does not appear then read this article on how to set a device to boot: http://www.wikihow.com/Set-Bios-to-Boot-from-a-CD-ROM


                
  3. Wait for Windows Installer to load, depending on your system speed this could take a couple of seconds to minutes.
  4. Select your Installation Language (does not have to match), Keyboard Interface and Timzone. Then click on Next
  5. Click on Repair your Computer


  6. Click on Troubleshoot


  7. Click on Refresh your PC or Reset your PC, read below for more information.


  8. Follow the instructions on the screen.

Refresh or Reset?

Refresh is the best option to go when your system is instable after a software or hardware installation, It will remove all programs from your system but all User Data will be retained, no personal files should be lost after this procedure.

Reset is the best option if your system became unstable after a virus infection or any other infection, it formats your hard drive and you begin at 0 again. Everything, even your personal files are lost.

As long as you did not have an infection it’s recommended that you try refresh first, then if that does not work, use the reset your PC function.

I hope that this Wiki helped you solve your system issues.
Yuri.

There is only so much you can do with a bugcheck like this until it comes down to a faulty processor that will need to be replaced. Start from 1 and work downward:

1. Ensure your temperatures are within standard and nothing's overheating. You can use a program such as Speccy if you'd like to monitor temps - http://www.piriform.com/speccy

2. Clear your CMOS (or load optimized BIOS defaults) to ensure there's no improper BIOS setting - http://pcsupport.about.com/od/fixtheproblem/tp/clearcmos.htm

3. Ensure your BIOS is up to date.

4. The only software conflict that can usually cause *124 bugchecks are OS to BIOS utilities from manufacturer's like Asus' AI Suite. If you have something like this software-wise, remove it ASAP.

5. If all of the above fail, the only left to do is replace your processor as it is faulty.
