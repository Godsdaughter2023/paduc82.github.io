There is nothing wrong with your computer specifications. There are two hardware components critical to WSR (Windows Speech Recognition) you did not mention. These are the sound card and the microphone. In a nutshell, the sound card must not only digitize analog speech and must do this without introducing electronic noise into the audio stream. The microphone must not only transmit your voice it must do so without picking up background noise. The problem may be that the ordeal is distorted and the software is struggling to figure out what you were saying and it locks up.

 

The sound card can pick up electronic noise if you use the front connectors that have on shielded cables going back to the main sound chip at the rear of the computer. This is why you should always use the rear inputs for an analog microphone. Another way around this problem and to assure yourself of the best quality of input in terms of the sound card, is to use a USB converter designed specifically for the frequency range of speech recognition. This includes the Andrea USB-SA and VXI USB sound pod.

 

Microphones built into a computer or on the web cam typically pick up lots of background noise. This will distort the audio signal and make it difficult for the speech engine software to decode what you were saying. A good noise canceling microphone designed for speech recognition will assure you the best accuracy. You can check out a speech recognition specialist web site like Speech Recognition Solutions.

 

And easy way to test if your sound card and microphone OK is to create a Windows Sound Recorder recording. The playback should be free of any electronic noise will background noise.
