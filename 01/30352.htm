aeneas32 say:

USB port power-only configuration in Windows OS

Often, when trying to charge smart devices like a cell phone or GPS device, it is necessary to connect the device to a USB port on a PC/Laptop which is not going to communicate through the USB port to the device (e.g. USB flash file system or ActiveSync).

Is there any way on the PC/Laptop in Windows to turn off the USB enumeration and communication, preferably on a per port basis ?

Most such external devices do not come with a AC charger, only a car cigarette lighter charger.

Thus, to get around this problem customers are often forced to bring a car battery starter with its cigarette lighter socket into the house to use this alternate 'dumb' charging mechanism.

It should be possible to configure maybe in  Device Manager/USB_Controllers  to remove a specific port from OS enumeration and communication, but continue to power that port.

Let’s try to reset WINSOCK, IPv4 TCP/IP and IPv6 TCP/IP stack.
