techie bear say:

Hello Leo,

That's both a generic warning and a specific alert. In other words, sometimes a program following the right process - say a self-extracting archive - gets triggered in an .exe file; this is unexpected but 'legal' so Windows allows it, but pops up a generic warning. Other times something doesn't happen according to a script, but its rated as a non-fatal error, so the install proceeds and you get a warning.

 

Its up to you to test out the newly installed software, see whether its functioning properly or not, and if not, take 'appropriate' action, whatever you determine that to be. The warning won't lead to an uninstall, at most it'll re-run the install software and let you try again. Bottom line:  if you get the warning, and you're at all concerned, uninstall the software and re-run the installation routine.

 

Does this assuage your concerns? Let me know how you fare.
