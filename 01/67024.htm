a. user say:

Hi, I  recently got HP Probook 4440s laptop with windows 7. I am trying use the
"Windows Speech Recognition" for dictation into word file.  I am using the inbuilt microphone in the laptop.
The words recognised by the application are not what I am speaking. I am wondering if  there
is some help in this regard.

There are 2 basic requirements for WSR, Windows Speech Recognition, to achieve high accuracy:

  1. A high quality microphone. One that transmits your voice with clarity and does not pickup extraneous noises in the background or keyboard clicks. Some microphones built into the notebook computer may work okay in a quiet environment. However, they will never yield the best accuracy like a good close talk microphone because the distance between the microphone and one's mouth requires a high pickup level that will pickup all noise.
  2.  As you speak, words should be enunciated clearly like a good TV newscaster. You do not necessarily need to speak slow, just feel your mouth form the words. It helps to speak in phrases as the software is not only listening for the sounds of words, but comparing words before and after. This is how homophones like to, too, and two are differentiated as in, "Two boys went to see a doctor because they ate too much food." Accents do not usually matter as long as spoken words are clearly enunciated.

 On our website <removed> is a link to, "Getting Started with Windows Speech Recognition."
