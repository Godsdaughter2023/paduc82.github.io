Having tried the following solution, I have managed to connect two tablets successfully.

As we know, the Xbox app is a default Windows program and therefore cannot be uninstalled simply or have its preferences reset by normal means. However, by using the 'powershell' on Windows 10, we are able to purge the app.

• First take the tablet (if using one) out of tablet mode to enable the keyboard for powershell. This can be done by selecting the extra settings at the bottom right of the desktop window.
• Next use Cortana/search for 'powershell'
• Right click or hold your finger down on the tablet until a box appears, this enables right click and Run As Administrator. 
• Enter the following into powershell and press enter afterwards:

Get-AppxPackage *xboxapp* | Remove-AppxPackage

• The Xbox app is now uninstalled from the device 
• We now need to reinstall the app 
• Using Admin rights again, enter the following into the powershell:

Get-AppxPackage -AllUsers| Foreach {Add-AppxPackage -DisableDevelopmentMode -Register “$($_.InstallLocation)\AppXManifest.xml”}

• This will complain about some apps, such as Cortana UI and others you have open, causing problems while the installation is working it's magic, this is fine.
• Restart the system, search for the Xbox app and launch it.


You should now be able to connect to and stream from your Xbox to your Windows 10 tablet/PC.
