Hi EricTabet,

Thanks for your posting. According to your description, you are repeatedly prompted for Exchange credentials after you alternately use two Skype for Business accounts. This problem occurs for multiple reasons, and in certain scenarios, it is the expected behavior. Skype for Business generally prompts you for credentials only after you're signed in and when it must connect to an external service such as the Microsoft Exchange Free/Busy service or the Exchange Calendar service. As you alternately use two Skype for Business accounts on your computer, the Skype for Business might re-connect to the service for different credentials. In this way, it’s recommended for you to use one Skype for Business account in the Skype for Business client of one computer to get a better experience. Otherwise, if you want to switch the accounts to use Skype for Business, we suggest you try to sign out of the Skype for Business (Click the triangle icon next to the gear icon > Select File > Choose Sign Out). And then, click Delete my sign-in info before you type another Skype for Business account credentials information. Besides, we suggest you clear your cached passwords by following steps (you may need admin right to do this):

1. Close all Office clients.
2. Open Control Panel > Click View by in the upper right of window> choose Large Icons.
3. Select User Accounts > Click Manage your credentials in the left column.
4. Select credentials for Skype for Business and choose Remove.
5. Close and reboot your computer.
