    Troubleshooting Video Card Problems (BSODs, Instable Driver and - Microsoft Community

    yuri pustjens say:

    Troubleshooting Video Card Problems (BSODs, Instable Driver and Brightness)

    Technical Level : Intermediate

    Summary

    Apart from memory and damaged system files. Video cards are the biggest reason for blue screens. But not only blue screens (BSOD) cause problems there been many people having brightness issues, instable driver issues and much more. This wiki shows you how to identify and solve these issues.


    Details

    Situation

    If one or more situations listed below match your situation, then this Wiki is what you might need to solve your issue.

    • You’re having Blue Screens of Death with the following drivers.
      • Atikmdag.sys, atikmpag.sys or any other ATI Video Card Driver
      • nvlddmkm.sys, or any other NVidia driver
    • You are given the warning: Display driver stopped responding and has recovered.
      (This example is for NVidia, it’s the same for ATI or Intel chipsets)
    • Your system’s brightness level cannot be adjusted.
    (This example is for NVidia, it’s the same for ATI or Intel chipsets)
  • Your system’s brightness level cannot be adjusted.

Important

  • You need to have Administrator permission to follow this guide
  • You need to be able to boot into Windows
  • You must have a working network connection to find newer drivers.
  • Windows must be fully updated.

Finding out which video card you have

Before you can start troubleshooting anything at all you first need to find out which video card(s) you have in your system, to do this you need to open Device Manager:

  1. Press the Windows Icon + W on your keyboard
  2. Type “Device Manager”
  3. Click on Device manager

Or

  1. Open the right charm bar
  2. Choose settings
  3. Click on Control Panel
  4. Click on Hardware and Sound
  5. Under Devices and Printers click on Device Manager

You should now have reached Device Manager, unfold the group Display Adapters. You should now see one or multiple video cards, write these down for further steps.

Basic Video Card driver version troubleshooting.

Sometimes solving a video card problem can be very easy, just roll back to your old driver before you updated and wait for a new driver to be released which works for you. If you did not update your video card, then skip this part and continue to the new chapter, if you have brightness issues then do not skip this part! 

From within Device Manager right click on your video card and choose “Update Driver Software…” now click on “Browse my computer for driver software” then click on “Let me pick from a list of device drivers on my computer”

You should now see the screen shown above, try to find a recent but not the newest driver in that list, select it (the bar will turn blue) and click on Next. After your driver is installed, reboot your device and test if the problems reappear.

If you are having brightness issues, then revert your Intel onboard video card to an older driver, this does solve the problem in 80% of the cases.

Always update all video cards you have on your system, higher-end laptops and desktops have integrated video cards, be sure to update both of them!

Video Card Reinstallation

If the above steps did not result in your issue being solved, then the steps below will show you how to reinstall the entire driver. The installation technique we use is clean, installation, therefor follow the steps of this guide as precise as possible.

There are 3 guides below, use the guide for your graphics card. If you haven’t already done so, read Finding out which video card you have on how to find out which one you have.

  • The colors in the steps represent the colors in the screenshots.
  • Please do not install BETA drivers. Always use Stable drivers as normal computer user.

Nvidia Card Reinstallation.

  1. Go to http://www.nvidia.com/Download/index.aspx?lang=en-us
  2. Choose your graphic card in the dropdown menu or use the automatic detection tool.
  3. Download the correct installer.
  4. Start the downloaded exe file and let it extract the files to any location of your choice, then wait for the installer to start.
  5. Once the installer has opened read and agree on the license agreement
  6. Select the Installation Option:  Custom (Advanced)
  7. Tick the “Perform a clean installation” box.
  8. Let the driver install and follow the instructions on the screen
  9. Reboot your system and see if the problems are gone (which is safe to say after 48 hours of use)

ATI Card Reinstallation.

  1. Download the latest removal tool, to ensure we first remove all drivers from your system
    http://www2.ati.com/drivers/amdcleanuputility.exe
  2. Open the tool and click on OK after reading the warning
  3. The process will complete in the background, as soon as it’s done, you will get a message
  4. Reboot your system.
  5. Download the latest drivers using the auto discover tool or by manually downloading the drivers
    Autodetect: http://support.amd.com/en-us/download/auto-detect-tool
    Manual: http://support.amd.com/en-us/download
  6. Follow the installer its instruction, there are no special user inputs.
  7. Reboot your system and see if the problems are gone (which is safe to say after 48 hours of use)

Intel Video Card / Chip Reinstallation.

  1. Manually or automatically download the latest drivers for your video card using the following links:
    Manually: https://downloadcenter.intel.com/default.aspx (skip to step 3)
    Automatic: http://www.intel.com/p/en_US/support/detect?iid=dc_iduu
  2. Once the Auto Detect tool is done, download the latest Graphics Driver and run the installer
  3. Follow the instructions in the installation program, there is no special user input required.
  4. Reboot your system after its done installing.

I’ve updated all my graphics drivers, but my problems are still not gone…

In most cases the graphics drivers are the reason for the errors described under Situations but in some cases other outdated drivers can cause this issue as well, it’s a good thing to always have these things updated: BIOS, Chipset, Raid Controllers, and Host Controllers since these are the most common sources of trouble.

You can download the latest drivers on your computer support site or at Intel.com / Amd.com.

If updating these drivers doesn’t work, or you don’t know how to do this then always look for help on Microsoft Answers.

I hope your problem is solved using the steps above and you don’t need further help.

In the System Information applet, you should see something like the following at the bottom of System Summary:



As long as "Installed Physical Memory" is the same as what you think you have (in your case, 4.00 GB), your system is almost certainly working as it should and you shouldn't start tweaking things.

The readings on the Task Manager "Performance" tab are neither intuitive nor particularly helpful.  Instead, click the "Resource Monitor" button and select the "Memory" tab.  You should see something like this:


Although the numbers here will continually change depending on what processes are running, you can get a good idea of how your RAM is being used.  The above figure shows:
  • 8 GB of physical RAM installed
  • Hardware is using 300 MB of address space (that is, the hardware isn't actually using the physical RAM, but the address that point to 300 MB of RAM are being used to point to hardware devices, including any RAM that might be installed on a video card).  Thus, the "Total" shown in the figure is the 8 GB (8192 MB) of physical RAM less the 300 MB that can't be addressed because those addresses are being used for hardware resources (8192-300=7892)
  • The processes that are currently running (including Firefox, which I'm using to view this forum and post this message) are using about 2 GB (2041 MB).
  • About 5.7 GB (5770 MB) of memory used by running but inactive processes has been paged out to the pagefile because the operating system has decided that it's not needed at the moment
  • About 5.8 GB (5808 MB) is available.  This includes 5727 MB of "standby" memory and 81 MB that's not being used at all.
