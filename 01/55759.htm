Here is another take on the CBS.LOG file problem, if that is what is happening to you.

CBS.LOG FILES FILLING DRIVE 100% DISK FULL
https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/for...

https://www.computerworld.com/article/3112358/m...

It is just a file logging error messages. If you have having no problems, you can safely delete it.

This seems to be a problem that comes and goes in Windows. A few other people have reported the same thing recently. In theory, as the log hits a limt, something like 20-30 MB (not GB) it is supposed to be rolled over into a CBSPersist*****.LOG. Then those files are supposed to be compressed into .CAB files. I checked my system and couldn't identify any CAB files that look like they are part of the CBS system. Part of the process seems to have been broken.

Note: these log files can also “overflow” into the C:\Windows\Temp folder as “Cab_xxxx_X” files

One suggested fix is:

* In start menu find services.msc

* Right click on it and select “Run as Admin”

* In the displayed list, find the Windows Modules Installer service, “TrustedInstaller”

* Right click on it and select “Stop” (Do NOT close the services dialog yet)

* Set the startup type to “Manual”

* In File Explorer, go to C:\Windows\Logs\CBS\

* Delete or move or Rename any ALL of the CBS.LOG or Cbspersist_XX.log files

* In file Explorer, go to C:\Windows\Temp

* Delete all CAB_xxxx_x files

* Go back to the Services dialog

* Right click on the TrustedInstaller service again, Select “Start”

* Close the services dialog.

* Restart the computer.

This should stop the broken process that was generating all of these files to fill you drive.

flying cattle say:
  • Humanity can be earned by playing as a Phantom, and helping to kill an end-level boss.
  • Humanity can be earned by killing bosses.
  • Humanity can be earned by killing Evil Spirits (invading players), or by invading and killing a player in another world.
  • Humanity can be earned by having your hint sign up-voted by another player. ( however this humanity will go straight to your held humanity, thus upon dying it will be lost.
  • Humanity can be earned by killing a large amount of monsters in one area if the boss is alive (up to 10 Humanity).
    • Dying, using the bonfire, or leaving the area does not reset your monster kill count for that area.
  • Humanity can be bought from Undead Merchant (Female) and Patches the Hyena.
  • Humanity can be found, as a dropped consumable item, from some killed enemies.
    • These consumables can be stored, just like other items, and will not be lost if the player dies.
    • Rats, Octopus-Headed Guards and Skeleton Babies drop humanity items.
  • The three consumable humanity items are:
    • Humanity (Acquire one humanity)
    • Twin Humanities (Acquire two humanities), not dropped by regular enemies.
    • Firekeeper Soul (Acquire five humanities), not dropped by regular enemies. ( This will void it from the ability to upgrade your estus flask.
    • Consuming a humanity item will also restore the player's health.

    From the Dark Souls Wiki

r3d p01nt say:

Being human also allows you to kindle bonfires and gain more estus flask drinks.  An easy way to tell if you are human is to look at your head without a helmet.  If you look like a zombie, then you're hollow.  If you look like a person, you are human.

.

You can also check the circle in the upper left of the screen next to your health/stamina.  That tells you how much humanity you are holding.  That humanity can be used to turn human and to kindle bonfires.  When you use a humanity item, it will increase the number in the upper left.  If the number is greyed out a bit, then you are hollow.  If it is white and bright, it means you are in human form.
