To be safe, i wish to have 2 partitions on my Vista PC (one for current Vista) and another one for Windows 8 Professional. How can i achieve this?

Lose your licenses?  As long as you still have the serial numbers/product keys and installation media (or can download the installer, etc.) - you won't *lose* any licenses.
 
As for an application or piece of hardware (including, but not limited to, those inside your computer) being compatibile with Windows 8 - that is checked on an individual basis (component by component) with the manufacturer of each component.  For example, your printer, your scanner, your digital camera, your webcam, etc.  Internal examples might be your video card, your sound card, your network card(s) and even your motherboard (chipset.)  The former list - you check with the manufacturer of each product... so those whose brand name is stamped on the printer, the scanner, the digital camera or the webcam.  For the latter - you could, if it is a big name brand computer, just check with the manufacturer first - so Dell, HP, Gateway, etc. - see if they support your particular model PC under Windows 8 (chances are high they do not.)  After that - you could check the individual components (video card manufacturer will likely be Intel, nVidia or ATI/AMD; for example...) to see if those individuals have WIndows 8 drivers and support for your devices.
 
The software - that's the same deal.  You check with the software manufacturer to see if they support the software you have (down to the version) under Windows 8.
 
Technically - you can dual boot multiple Windows OSes - as long as you have standalone licenses for both (in other words - you legitimately could not purchase the Windows 8 UPGRADE and run it in parallel with your current Windows Vista OS - since that is the product that qualified you for the *upgrade* and if it is running on the same system - you technically did not *upgrade* it) or that is the way it has been in the past.  It may be TECHNICALLY possible, but that doesn't mean you are in the clear to do it.
 
There are many articles that explain how to dual boot Windows 7 and Windows 8 - and the steps would not be much different (if at all) for your Vista.  I suggest googling and checking some of those out.
 
As for being forced to go through Windows 7 first - I think that goes back to your original question and what I believe you REALLY meant to ask.  What I read into your original question is that you don't want to (or can't) install your applications again - you want them to come along for the ride when you upgrade to Windows 8.  At least that was my first impression - it somewhat changed when you asked about dual-booting because then the applications CERTAINLY do not come along for the ride.  However - to continue along that assumption - the only way the applications you have installed could follow you all the way to Windows 8 would be to first upgrade to Windows 7.  Then you could upgrade to Windows 8 and keep the applications that were installed on Windows 7.
 
Hope that makes sense.

I don't know whether this solution applies in your situation.  I suggest you take the time to read the thread thoroughly.  However, this solution works.  I used it and a lot of others have with success:

Ken Morely is the author of this:

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/windows_7-update/windows-update-remains-at-checking-for-updates/ff3c442e-79a6-40d3-ac35-29e130cebef1

The following procedure was successful for me and I repeated it again just to be sure:

1) Download KB3083710 and KB3102810 in advance.

2) Disconnect the computer from the Internet so Windows Update cannot automatically update itself to 7.6.7600.320.

3) Perform a fresh reload from Recovery Media or Windows 7/64 with SP1 RTM.

4) Manually install KB3083710 and restart - WU components will now be version 7.6.7601.19016 dated 9/25/2015.

5) Manually install KB3102810 and restart - WU components will now be version 7.6.7601.19046 dated 10/20/2015.

6) Restore the Internet connection and launch Windows Update as normal.

7) After a few minutes, you should have plenty of updates to download and install.

I am not sure that both steps 4 and 5 are required, but I didn't want to mess with success.  I hope this saves someone else some time and effort.
