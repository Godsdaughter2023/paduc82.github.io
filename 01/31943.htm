jaimemw say:

Hello,

Thank you for reaching out to the community - there are a couple of methods you can use to personalize your Search view on the taskbar to minimize or hide.

To Minimize (Option 1):

  • Right-click on an empty space in your taskbar
  • Choose Search or Cortana (depends on your set-up)
  • Select "Show Search Icon" instead of "Show Search Box"

To Minimize (Option 2):

You can change the view on the taskbar to "Use small taskbar buttons", which will reduce the search box to a magnifying glass or Cortana icon (depending on your set-up).

  • Right-click on an empty space in your taskbar
  • Choose Properties
  • Select the checkbox for "Use small taskbar buttons" in the Taskbar pane
  • Select OK

To Hide:

  • Right-click on an empty space in your taskbar
  • Choose Search or Cortana (depends on your set-up)
  • Select "Hidden"

    The location platform provides the location information to apps (such as Cortana or Maps) based on whatever location info they could gather (Ethernet, Wi-Fi signal, cell towers, GPS) most phones have GPS integrated, which provides the most accurate location positioning and usually avoids this issue.

    The location platform team is aware of the problem and working to improve location accuracy in the future. For now there's not much we can tell users about this issue.     2. Start a new feedback item and select Problem

    3. Select the Hardware, Devices and Drivers/Location Services area

    4. Click on the Reproduce button

    5. Switch to the Maps app and click on the Locate me icon (Concentric circles)

    6. Send your feedback

    This will send additional logging of your issue to help us investigate further.

    There's no way to override the wrong location for the Maps app or the system currently. We can suggest users to submit or vote for that suggestion on the Windows Feedback app or through UserVoice (https://windows.uservoice.com/forums/265757-windows-feature-suggestions)
