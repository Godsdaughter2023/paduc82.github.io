gokul t say:

Hi vj_internet,

- Did you make a system image backup, by chance?
- Which application did you use to create the backup?

VHD is Virtual Hard Disk format.

The reason why you have a VHD file is because you’ve created a system image backup. A system image includes Windows and your system settings, programs, and files. You can use a system image to restore the contents of your computer if your hard drive or computer ever stops working. When you restore your computer from a system image, it is a complete restoration; you can't choose individual items to restore, and all of your current programs, system settings, and files are replaced.

You cannot restore VHD files using the “backup and restore” center in Windows.

However, you can mount the image (.VHD file) using the steps listed below:

a. Right-click “Start” and click “Manage”

Note: You must have administrative privileges to do this.  

b. Click “Disk management” on the left-pane.
c. Click Action and Attach VHD.
d. Specify the location of external hard disk on your computer.

For more information refer to the "Extracting files from a system image (advanced)" section in the following article:

Recovering your files in Windows 7

Also see Restore your computer from a system image backup
