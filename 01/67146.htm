To change or restart the Windows Update service

You must be logged on as an administrator to perform these steps.

  1. Open Administrative Tools by clicking the Start button , and then clicking Control Panel. In the search box, type administrative tools, and then click Administrative Tools.

  2. Double-click Services. If you're prompted for an administrator password or confirmation, type the password or provide confirmation.

  3. Click the Name column heading to sort the names in reverse order. Find the Windows Update service, right-click it, and then click Properties.

  4. On the General tab, next to Startup type, make sure that Automatic is selected.

  5. Next to Service status, check to see if the service is started. If it is not, click Start. If you're prompted for an administrator password or confirmation, type the password or provide confirmation.

oraclea say:

How have achievements changed the way you play video games?

I've asked a couple questions in here about achievements, and I've been pleasantly surprised to learn that we have a very diverse set of attitudes toward achievements!  Let's keep the discussion train rolling!

Most of us have probably played games that do feature achievements/trophies/etc. and games that don't at some points in our lives.  Does the presence of achievements in a video game change how you play a game, or even what you choose to play?

For me, I still avoid playing games with a guide the first time through, but the biggest way achievements have affected how I play is that they add replay value and make me somewhat more likely to try to replay the game (or certain parts of it) in order to get the achievements, particularly if I really like the game.  Whether it's performing a simple task I may have missed, or trying to get a certain level of mastery over something in the game, or doing additional exploring or collecting or (minor) grinding, I'm inclined to at least give these a try.  (Whether or not I actually complete them all is another story; usually, no.)  I wouldn't say that achievements affect what I choose to play, though I certainly know people who may be more or less inclined to play a game given what they see on the achievements list, or possibly even play on a specific platform because of the prospect of getting achievements/trophies/etc. for that platform.

How about you?  Have achievements changed the way you approach playing video games and/or your process in choosing what games to play?

xircal say:

David,

Windows 8/8.1 has a tool called USB selective suspend which might be the culprit.

To check, do the following.

  1. Right click the Start button and go to Power Options.
  2. In the menu which opens, click the link far right called "Change plan settings".
  3. In the following menu, click the link called "Change advanced power settings".
  4. Next, scroll down and expand: USB Settings | USB selective suspend and disable both.

This will prevent the USB driver from sending a message to Windows to idle the device which can happen if the operating system thinks it's no longer in use.

I have it permanently disabled on my own laptop because I use a wired mouse which gives me much more flexibility than the Touchpad.
