jake nelson say:

Hi Brad,

When an IP Address starts with 169 it indicates that you are not receiving an IP address from a DHCP server; your computer is actually choosing an APIPA address so that it can still communicate with other computers on the same network that are also not getting an IP Address. This can be both good and bad news. It basically means that your computer is detecting a valid link (at a hardware level) between you and whatever is on the other end of the cable, this is great news, your hardware is probably not faulty, on the other hand it's not receiving the information it needs from your Router and that could mean either the router is not sending it out or your PC is not understanding it correctly.

I found your network topology a little confusing. Most modern networks go something like " Wall socket (either adsl or cable) >  Modem / Router in one unit > PC (or possibly access points). Is this similar to your setup?

You should try restarting all of your devices. Modem / Router should be restarted first, it will take a few minutes before it reconnects to the internet.

Please let me know how you go?
