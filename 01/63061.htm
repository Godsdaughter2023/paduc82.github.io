Windows Mail - what format should a video be for sending? - Microsoft Community

valerie67 say:

Windows Mail - what format should a video be for sending?

My video format is MP4.  What should I convert it to in order to attach it to email?

 

mister wizard say:

Hello Valerie,

You can attach any type of file to an e-mail, as long it is small enough to be handled by your ISP and by the recipient's ISP.  A common limit is 10 megabytes, but you should verify that with your ISP and your recipient.

The real question is "can my recipient play this format?".  That depends on what players they have installed and (sometimes) on what operating system that they use.  You should ask before sending.

Mp4 is actually a container, not a video format.  Mp4 files can contain video, audio, and other things in a variety of formats.  Once you know which players on your PC can play the file, then you can advise your recipient what works for you.

If they say that they have Windows 7, then Windows Media Player 12 can handle most Mp4's.  On Vista or earlier, WMP 11 usually cannot play Mp4's unless special codecs have been installed.  Otherwise, they would need a third-party player like VLC or Quicktime.  If they have only WMP on Vista or on XP, you could convert the file to WMV format before sending it.  If they have a MAC, then Quicktime will be available to play Mp4's.

Converting videos reduces the quality, so it's best to avoid that when possible.

Doug M. in NJ
