naveen_m say:

Hi,

Thank you for writing to Microsoft Community Forums.

Windows 10 has a feature namely 'Active hours' to prevent Windows 10 from automatically restarting your PC to install updates when you're using it. It is designed to specify a period when you're likely to be working on the system. If an update is pending, restart will happen outside your active hours.

To setup active hours:

1. Press Windows key + I together to open Settings.

2. Click on Update & Security.

3. Click on Change active hours option.

4. Set the Start time and the End time, you're usually working on the computer.

5. Click on Save.

Once it is done, even if there are pending updates, the system won't restart during the time range that you have specified.

Note: The maximum amount of time that you can set is 18 hours.

Hope it helps.

Hi Emily

Click your Start button, type services and hit Enter

When the Services applet opens, click on the Name column to sort the services by name, and then scroll until you locate the “Touch Keyboard and Handwriting Panel Service“.

Save that and restart your PC
