considerate_guy say:

This is a common symptom of a corrupted project file. If you make a copy of your project file (.wlmp) and open that copy in Notepad, you'll probably notice many of the video clip sections with an outTime="0", but where the inTime is some number bigger than 0.  That isn't valid and causes Movie Maker to get confused the next time you reload your project.

To "uncorrupt" the project, you can try editing the copy and changing all the outTime values so they are at least 1 bigger than the inTIme. You should then be able to see items on your storyboard again and you can hopefully set the durations correctly inside of Movie Maker.
