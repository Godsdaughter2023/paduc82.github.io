I removed everything manually (except from register) and malwarebyte, Ccleaner, panda etc only find riconman.exe suspicious.

The registry has no self-awareness.  It is nothing more than a sheet of paper (a database) where entries are put in and read out.  If something is left in - it is not because the registry requested it or held onto it - it is because whatever wrote it did not get rid of it when removed. 
 
In the case of malware - that is expected, you could say.  Unfortunately malware gets changed as it spreads (variants) - these variants are many - it is almost like the malware gets infested by other malware and the trouble of getting rid of it without a backup is enormous.  So...
 
Do you have a backup of your system state from before you had this issue?  A system restore point that dates to prior to the issue?  Something you can restore to?
 
If not - the chances of finding everything and being 100% positive you did: slim at best.
 
Now are the entries you are worried about hurting anything?  Unless they are being referred to by something running and/or are in a location where they are read and cause things to start running (and those things exist on your system): No - they are just scribbles on that piece of paper (entries in that database.)
 
Will they slow down your system?  Yes.  Will that millisecond over the next 5 years be noticeable?  Don't know how sensitive you are to time fluctuations, would you call yourself a "Time Lord"?  *grin*
 
Could they cause future issues...? While there is a possibility - it's remote.  You'd have to get hit by something that not only looks for the entries - but uses them in some way to its advantage.
 
So - what can you do?
  • Restore your registry/system state to some known good state (a backup you made before the incident.)
  • Restore the entire system from your last known good/clean backup.  A little more drastic - but about the same as the above.
  • Perform a system Refresh and install all of your non - 'Windows Store' applications back and patch, etc.  Then restore your data from a known good backup.
  • Clean install the system, put back all of your programs, etc.  Restore your data from a known good backup.
  •  Take ownership and change permissions on all of the registry values you want to get rid of and get rid of them.
  • Continue on as-is.
