a. user say:

What does "reset my Windows 7" mean?

In most situations of home users your system will obtain the IP address it uses by "asking" your Internet Service Provider (ISP) for an IP address and that IP address is granted to your system through a "lease" that expires after a certain period of time.

The IP address is not assigned to your specific computer, it is assigned to your connection.

This happens through DHCP that you can read about here:

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dynamic_Host_Configuration_Protocol

This is usually done automatically and if you were to look at the network adapter properties on your system you might see something like this:

In a case like that, you don't have to configure anything.  When your system wants to connect to the network it asks for an IP address, one is granted and that is what your system will use for the duration of the connection.

If you disconnect from the network and connect again later you might get the same IP address and you might not.

You can't see what the IP address assigned to your connection is from those properties but you can by using the ipconfig command from a Command Prompt windows:

If you connect wirelessly at another location your system will still most likely request an IP address from wherever you are connecting and you will be supplied with a different IP address but there should be nothing for you to do or worry about since it is all automatic.

Now that you know how to check the IP address assigned to your connection (not your computer) check what you have now and it you connect wirelessly someplace else, check it again and see what you find.

Most of the time you don't have to worry about it since it happens automatically and there is no need to change your IP address since you don't really control it anyway.  Your IP address is assigned to your connection when you ask to be connected.

To see even more information about your connection from the Command Prompt enter:

ipconfig   /all

Then you can see more information about the lease your system has on that assigned IP address and a bunch of other stuff. 

In this example, I turned on this wireless computer about 08:28:52, it automatically asked AT&T for an IP address and got one so that is what we are using at this moment:

If there is some reason you think you want to change your IP address you should explain to us why you think you need to do that and who your Internet Service Provider is and maybe you can get some ideas... or you can talk to your ISP and they can tell you exactly how their stuff works and what you need to do.
