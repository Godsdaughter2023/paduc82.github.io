95 Processes Running. - Microsoft Community

robertrasul gorman say:

95 Processes Running.

How do I end extra processes to speed up my computer?
mike hall... always say:

I have 89 processes running which is about normal for me. Many of the processes use hardly any resources at all, and it is not them which slow down a computer.

 

Open Task Manager and click on the Processes tab. Now click on the CPU tab until the processes using the most resources are grouped at the top.

 

System Idle should be in the 90's, and there maybe one or two processes registering around three to five below it. If this is what you see, processes are not what is slowing anything down. Don't think that just because a lot of processes are running, shutting some down would automatically speed everthing up. That is not how it works. 

 

If there is a process which is forcing System Idle down to the 60's and 70's, make a note of the process and do a search on it. The effects of this condition may not slow your computer down either, but it will force the HDD activity light into hyper mode, and it is not a condition that is beneficial.

 

If the process is tied to a program or utility that you want, try uninstalling and re-installing the program/utility. It could be that there is a glitch in it.

 

It could also be malware. It is not unknown for malware authors to 'borrow' file names from valid Windows processes. You will want to run something like Malwarebytes if you suspect malware is the cause..

 

Badly written utilities can cause issues to as can sidebar gadgets which are monitoring something, e.g. cpu meters, clocks, anything which records events in real time..

How many Network Adapters should i have showing in device manager? - Microsoft Community

jennylc say:

How many Network Adapters should i have showing in device manager?

I have a wireless conection to another pc in home,   Network adapters (2) listed in Device manager Belkin USB Wireless Adapter is one, Why do I have ordo I need the other is NVDIA nforce 10/100 Mbps Ethernet?

 

langware say: It appears that your computer has both a wired (10/100 Mbps) card (adapter), and a Wireless adapter.

You do not need to have both of them enabled (you only need to enable one of them).

Your wired connection will most likely be faster (100 Mbps) than your wireless connection. It will also not be subject to interference from your neighbors.

Wireless connections are convenient for portability, but can suffer interference from your neighbor's wireless connections, and/or have degraded performance depending upon the distance and obstructions that esist between your computer and your wireless router.

If your computer is a desktop, you may want to consider using the wired adapter and disabling the wireless adapter.

If your computer is portable (such as a laptop), then you may want to use (enable) the wireless adapter and disable the wired (Ethernet) adapter.
