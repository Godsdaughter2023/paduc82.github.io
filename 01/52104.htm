You can safely MOVE your Documents, Pictures, Videos, and Music folders to the other drive.  Navigate to the Users folder, Your user folder.  Right-click on each and choose move.

Copy all your data off Drive D to an external storage device.

obsessive power say:

Hi,


Sometimes this happens with hard drives.  My 1TB drive didn't work one day, and none of the support troubleshooters worked.  This does not mean the drive is dead though.  As long as the drive itself is in working order, it should be possible to get it working again, but you'll need to fix the issue in Windows, and it will format the drive, so you'll need to Install/download your content again.

Connect your hard drive to a PC/Laptop.  If you're running Windows 10, right click on the Windows Start button and select 'Disk Management'.  If you're running a different version of Windows, you may need to search for Disk Management instead.   If your drive is in working order, it should be detected here.  You can then format it on the PC.  Once this is done, reconnect it to your console and follow the on screen instructions.

Let us know how you get on please.

Thanks.
