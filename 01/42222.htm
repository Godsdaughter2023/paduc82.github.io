Typically - unless otherwise specified - programs create shortcuts on the desktop when they install. (Apps from Microsoft Store don't do that.) Since this is your work laptop, I'm going to guess that you don't install your own software. It's probably installed by your IT department, because you don't have Administrators permissions.

In corporate settings, IT departments usually opt for a default install rather than a custom install. In a custom install you have a certain amount of control over the installation. For example, you can decide to not put a shortcut on the desktop. IT departments don't spend the time to do a custom install, so they just do a default install.

You can't get rid of the shortcut - at least, not permanently - because you need Administrators permissions to do that. And you probably don't have those Administrators permissions.

So accept them or ask your IT department to remove the shortcuts.
