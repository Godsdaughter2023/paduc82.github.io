Why are my pictures blurry when burned to a DVD but appear sharp on the project

considerate_guy say:

The preview is actually showing you a real-time rendering using the hardware (GPU) in your computer.  There's a lot of processing power available in your GPU to handle high-resolution photos and videos.

However, realize that when you are saving to a DVD, you are greatly reducing the resolution of your photos.  For example, let's imagine you have 12 megapixel pictures coming from your camera.  The DVD standard is much, much lower resolution (on the order of 1/3 of a megapixel).

You can see that demonstrated in this resolution comparison diagram that I created.  A 12 megapixel picture is represented by the purple rectangle.  The DVD resolution is shown by the orange rectangle.

But that doesn't explain everything.  In going from images that are pretty static and encoding those using a video codec, you are doing a lot of compression.  A video codec needs to be able to handle both static (still) and dynamic (moving) action in a movie.  In order to optimize the playback of moving material (which is the more common case in a movie), still photos can get compressed more than you expect.

Finally, the DVD player and the TV that you are using can also affect how clear the DVD looks.

andre da costa say:
1. Reset Facial Recognition and/or Fingerprint Options

Press Windows key + I to open Settings.
Click Accounts and select Sign-In options.
Locate Fingerprint or Facial Recognition options and click Remove under them.
After that, click Get Started and follow the on-screen instructions to reset Fingerprint and Facial Recognition.
Reboot your PC if needed.

2. Modify Group Policy Settings

Creators Update might have modified some of the crucial settings of Group Policy. Therefore, we would recommend to launch the Group Policy Editor and check/edit the settings related to Windows Hello functionality.

Pres Windows key, type gpedit, and press Enter.
Click on Computer Configuration and open Administrative Templates.
Select Windows Components and open Biometrics.
Click on Facial Features and right-click on Configure enhanced anti-spoofing option.
Select Edit and disable the Configure enhanced anti-spoofing feature.
Reboot your PC.

3. Update drivers

If some of the system drivers, most probably webcam and fingerprint reader drivers, were corrupted during the upgrade process, Windows Hello may fail to perform. To fix that, try to update the corresponding drivers:

Right-click on Windows key and select Device Manager.
Locate the Hello, webcam, and fingerprint drivers individually and right-click on each of them.
Select Remove driver software and wait for the removal to be finished.
Repeat the removal with all Windows Hello related drivers and then reboot your PC.
Once done, open Device Manager, click Action -> Scan for hardware changes.
The system should automatically detect and reinstall the drivers.

a. user say:

Yep, it works. So, efbolton, you CAN remotely assist your mother. Vindication never felt so good.

This is how I allowed a remote connection to my Surface.

Assistance recipient steps :

1. Search in Settings for "remote".

2. Select "Invite someone to connect to your PC and help you, or offer to help someone else".

3. Select "Invite someone you trust to help you".

4. Select "Save this invitation as a file".

5. At this point a window will open showing you a 12-digit password.

6. Open mail app, and send an email to your helper with the password, include the invitation file as an attachment. This is where your assistant takes over the process.

Assistance giver steps:

1. Open the email, copy the password, and save the invitation file.

2. Search in Settings for "remote".

3. Select "Invite someone to connect to your PC and help you, or offer to help someone else".

4. Select "Help someone who has invited you".

5. Select "Use an invitation file".

6. Open the invitation file.

7. Enter the password.

8. The connection is made! The assistant now clicks "Request control" at the top left of the window showing the recipients desktop.

 

It isn't as simple as it could be, but it DOES work.

OK, we will go for the Registry fix to recreate the Captures folder . . .

Click your Start Button, type regedit and hit Enter to open the Registry Editor

In the pane, navigate to this Key:
HKEY_CURRENT_USER\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Explorer\User Shell Folders

In the right hand pane, do you see this exact item:

{EDC0FE71-98D8-4F4A-B920-C8DC133CB165}

If so double click that and set its value to:

%USERPROFILE%\Videos\Captures



If you do not see that in the right hand pane:

Right click in an empty part of the right hand pane and choose New - 'Expandable String Value'

Paste this as its name:

{EDC0FE71-98D8-4F4A-B920-C8DC133CB165}

Then double click that new string and set its value as:

%USERPROFILE%\Videos\Captures



Close the Registry Editor and restart your PC
