Hi,

If you are using the Microsoft account then, there is no option to disable the password from the lock screen. It is mandatory to enter the Microsoft account credentials to login to Windows 10. As some of the services and settings requires the Microsoft account access.

I suggest you to create a local user account to disable the password from the lock screen. You can also switch to local account and remove the sign in option. Refer to this link:

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-my/windows-10/create-a-local-user-account-in-windows-10

Note: If you want to use any of the Microsoft apps/services then, you will be prompted with the Microsoft/Administrator password when you have logged in through local user account.

After creating Local user account follow these steps:

a.     Open Run Command by pressing Windows key + R.

b.     Type control userpasswords2 and press Enter.

c.      Select the User under the option Users for this computer.

d.     Un-check the option Users must enter a username and password to use this computer.

e.     Click on Apply and then OK.

Hope this information helps!

a. user say:

Hi Karlos,

You can change the background color of the Movies & TV app through its settings. To do so, kindly follow these steps:

  1. Type movies on the search bar.
  2. Click on Movies & TV.
  3. On the lower left corner, click on Settings.
  4. Under Mode, select Dark.
  5. Close the window, then relaunch the app.

Regarding the background color of the online videos that you are watching, it is most likely that it is the default background color that was set by the makers of that website. We recommend that you contact them to know if there's an option to change it.

Let us know if you need further assistance with your concern.

a. user say:

Since the steps that were previously given to you did not resolve your concern, we recommend that you change the closed caption settings on your computer. To do so, kindly do the following steps:

  1. Type settings on the search bar.
  2. Click on Settings.
  3. Click on Ease of Access.
  4. On the left panel, click on Closed captions.
  5. Under Background and window, click on the drop-down menu of Background color, then select the color of your liking.
  6. Close the window.

Relaunch the Movies & TV app, and check if the issue persists.

Don't hesitate to reach out to us if you need further assistance.
