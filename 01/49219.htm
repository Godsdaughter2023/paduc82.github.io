c chuck cole say: Hi, 
Similar problem FIXED.  I found that I had this problem after a recent upgrade to a new mobo.  If I had my external sata HD turned on before boot, there was no problem, but if I turned it on AFTER boot nothing would happen.  

I went into my bios and there was a setting in the bios where you could choose to enable or disable SATA hot swapping.  I had 6 Sata connections on my MSI mobo, my esata HD was on number 3 so I enabled that one and now when i turn on my external HD is shows up fine.

a. Is the eSATA hard disk getting detected in the BIOS?

eSATA does not provide bus power; therefore, an eSATA hard drive will require a power source in order to function. Just to make sure, unplug and re-plug the power cable to the eSATA hard disk.

I’d suggest you to check if it gets detected in BIOS or not.

If it does get detected in BIOS, check if the same gets detected in Device Manager.

To check in Device Manager,

1. Click ‘Start’, type ‘Device Manager’ and hit Enter.
2. Locate ‘Disk Drives’.
3. Expand the same and check if eSATA hard disk gets detected or not.

You may also check for the same in Disk Management.

Follow the steps mentioned below to open Disk Management:
1. Open Computer Management by clicking the Start button, clicking Control Panel, clicking System and Security, clicking Administrative Tools, and then double-clicking Computer Management. If you're prompted for an administrator password or confirmation, type the password or provide confirmation.
2. On the left, under Storage, click Disk Management.

Check if the eSATA hard disk gets detected under Disk Management.

Method 1: Increasing the Screen Refresh Rate.

You can reduce or eliminate flicker by increasing the screen refresh rate. A refresh rate of at least 75 hertz generally produces less flicker.

You might need to change your screen resolution before changing the refresh rate because not every screen resolution is compatible with every refresh rate. The higher the resolution, the higher your refresh rate should be.

2. Tap or click “PC & devices”, then tap or click “Display”.
3. Click “Advanced Settings”.
