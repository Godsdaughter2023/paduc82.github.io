Go into power options and disable sleep and do not let the system turn of your display.  The Surface Book has major issues with power management and the new Intel skylake chips.  Until Microsoft releases a firmware update to fix all of the issues, the best course of action is to keep your computer from going to sleep or turning your monitors off.  Sleep has also been causing issues where it drains the batter more than when it's on.

john_barnett say: Hi matterb,

You need space to the right of the C: drive to extend into. Say you have partitions C; D; E; F; G; H the easiest way is to move all the data (by manually coping) from your D: drive (assuming this partition is right next to your C: drive, if not you will need to use the relevant drive letter that is to the right of the C: drive) to another drive/partition (I assume you are only running one windows operating system?). Once you have copied the data to the new drive/partition then 'delete' your D: drive/partition (or whatever drive letter is next to the C: drive). You will then have 'unallocated space'. You can now 'extend' the C drive into the unallocated space, thus making your C: drive/partition bigger.

If your D: drive happens to be your system recovery partition then you will need to reassign the D: drive letter to whichever drive you move the data to.
