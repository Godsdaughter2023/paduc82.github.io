lemp say:
You're not very good at following instructions, i.e., "report any error message completely, without paraphrasing."

I assume that you are getting something like, "The operation has been cancelled due to restrictions in effect on this computer.  Please contact your system administrator."

This is most likely caused by malware -- and removing the malware may well not restore the disabled functionality.

I assume that "yes like in picture" means that your computer has a link to Control Panel like in the picture.

Frequent contributor Jose Ibarra has a "reg file" that will restore many functions disabled by malware, but unfortunately, restoring access to the Control Panel is not included.  Although I can tell you a registry value that definitely will disable Control Panel, my experiments seem to indicate that merely removing this value (or setting the value to 0) may
First see if you have XP Pro. 
  • Click Start, then click Run, then type the following in the "Open" box and click OK
               gpedit.msc
  • If the Group Policy editor window opens, click the + next to "Administrative Templates" under "User Configuration."  Then click "Control Panel."
  • In the right pane, right-click "Prohibit access to the Control Panel" and select Properties
  • Click the radio button for "Disabled" and then click OK
  • Close the Group Policy editor and your access to Control Panel should be restored.

If the Group Policy editor didn't open, you probably have Windows XP Home (although I suppose there could be more malware at work).  In this case,

  • Click Start, then click Run, then type the following in the "Open" box and click OK
                   regedit
  • In the left pane, navigate to HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Policies\Explorer
  • In the right pane look for three registry values
         NoControlPanel
         RestrictCpl
         NoSetFolders
  • If any of those values are present, right-click on each, select "Modify" and make the Value Data 0
  • Close regedit and you may have restored access to Control Panel (or maybe not).

Or see http://www.kellys-korner-xp.com/xp_tweaks.htm Line 256, left side (but this also may not be sufficient).
