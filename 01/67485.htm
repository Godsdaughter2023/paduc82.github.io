chris cupler say:

Most likely, Media Center did find the QAM channels.  The issue lies (this affects all cable systems; severity varies by the specific headend) with the lack of PSIP (in-band) data that MC relies on to map a digital (ATSC or QAM) channel to a Guide listing.  For a cable-only channel, missing PSIP data is pretty much a given.  For cable rebroadcasts of OTA channels, some headends actually strip the PSIP data from the stream.
In other words, it's not broken - rather, that's just how it works.

Here's a howto on setting up Win7 MC for QAM (includes instructions on mapping channels to Guide listings):
http://www.hdtvtunerinfo.com/win7mcqamsetup.html
Also helpful is the SiliconDust channel lineup server.  It'll help you with figuring out what physical channels relate to their actual (Guide) channels.  Just enter your ZIP, and select <your_cableco, digital>:
http://www.silicondust.com/support/channels/

HTH,
Chris
