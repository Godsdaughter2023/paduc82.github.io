a. user say:

Hi Phil,

You might be experiencing this issue because adaptive brightness is enabled on your desktop. This feature checks the lighting conditions of your computer’s surroundings and adjusts the brightness and contrast level automatically. Additionally, your desktop and graphics card might have a similar feature that automatically adjusts the screen brightness based on what is displayed on the screen. For example, Dell has Dell Intelligent Display, and Intel graphics has Display Power Saving Technology.

Let's try disabling adaptive brightness on your desktop. Here's how:

  1. Open Power Options by clicking Start, clicking Control Panel, clicking System and Security, and then clicking Power Options.
  2. Under any plan, click Change plan settings.
  3. Click Change advanced power settings.
  4. In the list, expand Display, and then expand Enable adaptive brightness.
  5. To turn adaptive brightness off when your computer is running on battery power, click On battery, and then, in the list, click Off.
  6. To turn adaptive brightness off when your computer is plugged into an outlet, click Plugged in, and then, in the list, click Off.
  7. Click Apply, and then click OK.

We also suggest that you update your device drivers especially the graphics card driver. While it's likely you already have the most recent driver, if you're having trouble with a device, you can try fixing it by updating the driver or reinstalling it.

To troubleshoot hardware-related issues and any configuration settings related to your graphics card and desktop, we highly recommend that you reach out to your desktop manufacturer for further assistance. For more information on how to contact your desktop manufacturer, you may refer to the following article: Computer manufacturers' contact information.

Keep us updated on the status of the issue, and we will be glad to assist you further.
