ken blake - microsoft mvp say:

On Wed, 6 Mar 2013 15:02:32 +0000, DanielOrtner wrote:

why the system writes: installed 4 GB ram but usable only 3.6 GB ram?

3.6GB is a very good number--much higher than most people get.

All 32-bit client versions of Windows (not just XP/Vista/7/8) have a
4GB address space (64-bit versions can use much more). That's the
theoretical upper limit beyond which you can not go.

But you can't use the entire  address space. Even though you have a
4GB address space, you can only use around 3.1GB of RAM (and as I
said, you are doing much better than that). That's because some of
that space is used by hardware and is not available to the operating
system and applications. The amount you can
use varies, depending on what hardware you have installed, but can
range from as little as 2GB to as much as 3.5GB. It's usually around
3.1GB.

If you need to select other webcam/audio devices, you need to go to the webcam options in Movie Maker. For instructions on how to go to webcam options, please refer to the following steps:

1. Click on the blue file menu button located at the upper left of Windows Live Movie Maker or as an alternative, press Alt+F on your keyboard.

lemp say:

I haven't been able to figure out how Apple decides what to make visible to Windows.

Assuming that you're using "Import pictures and videos using Windows" (you may not see the AutoPlay window if you checked the "Always do this for pictures" box), it may be that the pictures are not being correctly marked as not having been already imported ("Windows detects your newest pictures and videos and doesn't import duplicate versions of older pictures or videos that you've already copied to your computer").

Or it could be that Apple is just hiding things. 

Use Windows Explorer to manually import pictures as Hairy Fool suggested or ask here: https://discussions.apple.com/community/iphone/using_iphone

It's messy and time consuming, but your best option is probably to configure your phone to backup all of your photos to iCloud, then install iCloud for Windows and download the photos to your computer.
