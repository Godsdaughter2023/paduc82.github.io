When you say you need the clip in .avi format, what do you mean?  AVI stands for Audio Video Interleave and is a container format.  As such, it may contain video encoded with any of a number of codecs.  So you really need to provide the specific codec you need in your final file.  Most often I've seen .avi files containing h.264 encoded video.  And Movie Maker 2012 will create video encoded to the h.264 standard.  The difference is it is within an .mp4 file instead of an .avi container.

 

As you are on Windows Live Movie Maker 2011 (rather than Movie Maker 2012), the only option you have is .wmv format.  But there are converters that will transcode the movie from .wmv to your desired codec.

spiritx say:

Hi,

Control Panel - Mouse - look for TouchPad Settings - mine is under Device Settings tab and yours
maybe different (it is NOT the Scrollings tab which is for the mouse). On some you can enable
and disable the scroll and/or set the size of the area and the sensitivity of the touchpad.

Some will have a TouchPad control progam/icon in the Notification Area near the clock - lower
right. 

Check with System Maker's Support, on-line documentation, and their forums (if any).

==========================================

If needed : (make sure you have rebooted at least once if the above did not work)

Control Panel - Device Manager - Mice and Pointing Devices - Double Click on Touch Pad - Driver's Tab -
Click Update Driver (this is may not do anything as MS is far behind certifying drivers), now RIGHT CLICK
on TouchPad and UNINSTALL.

Then go to USB Controllers and UNINSTALL EVERYTHING except the category itself (all in it). REBOOT

This will refresh the TouchPad Driver and the USB Stack.

Here is the similar procedure in XP, Vista's is the same except we need to clear the specific device if present.
http://support.microsoft.com/kb/310575

Then go to System maker's site and get the latest touchpad drivers - reinstall the current ones if no newer
are available.

Download - SAVE - go to where you put it - Right Click on - RUN AS ADMIN.

alliance united say:

Hello,

At this moment the Xbox One does not yet have that feature. I am sorry for any problems this may cause. The only way you can get your Microsoft account to link is if you login at: http://account.live.com/ There you can add your account as aliases. Please click Add or remove Account aliases. Then you can start signing using the new Email.  Or you may completely switch the account over using a Xbox 360. If your friend or family member has one. However if this is the case your account will need to be downloaded on that Xbox for 30 days before you can switch it over. Please take in note that any Microsoft Currency you have on your account will not transfer. Please use the Currency on your account before switching over.
