greg carmack - windows mvp 2010-2020 say:
David -

Chrome is a browser like any other browser which will install itself to C. It will place it's downloads wherever you tell it to do so in Settings.

There is no reason to change the partition where you're installing an app like that, in fact it's inadvisable and makes no sense.

If you're wanting to separate your girlfriend's downloads and browsing from yours then what you can do that's practical is:

1) Create a new Local Account for her on the same Windows 10, following http://www.howtogeek.com/226540/how-to-create-a.... She can later change it to a MS Account if she wants. This will keep her stuff separate from yours with a separate sign-in.

2) Install Google Chrome browser and just ignore it and let her use it. If you don't want her downloads going to your account then change their location to download in Chrome Settings: https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/95759?...

Having more than one browser is what most people do. I would have several browsers on your task bar so there's always one that's best for the task. I use Firefox, Edge and Chrome in that order of preference. It's easy to share bookmarks between all of them in their settings under Import Favorites from another Browser. Set the preferred one as Default in Settings > Apps > Default Apps. If she wants to use Chrome then just ignore it yourself.

Hope this helps.
