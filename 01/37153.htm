    How do I queue videos in Media Center? - Microsoft Community

    seven007 say:

    How do I queue videos in Media Center?

    I was under the impression that there was going to be a "Queue" Feature in this newer release of Media Center. How do I go about creating a queue or playlist for videos?
    vijay b say:

    Hi Seven007 ,

    Thank you for Visiting Microsoft answers Forum.

    To add music to the queue

    The queue is a temporary list of songs you want to play. You can queue up music to listen to so that you don't have to keep selecting songs to play.

    1.       On the start screen, scroll to Music, and then click music library.

    2.       Click album artists, albums, artists, genres, songs, playlists, composers, or years, and then navigate to the music you want to add to the queue.

    3.       Click either a title or a name, and then click Add to Queue.

    To view and edit the queue

    1.       On the start screen, click Now Playing + Queue, and then click View Queue. The queue displays.

    2.       Click Edit Queue, and then do one of the following:

    ·         To move a song up or down in the list, click the up arrow or down arrow buttons.

    ·         To remove a song from the queue, click the Delete button.

    3.       Click Done.

    To save the queue as a playlist

    1.       On the start screen, click Now Playing + Queue, and then click View Queue. The queue displays.

    2.       Click Save As Playlist.

    3.       Use the numeric keypad to enter a name for the playlist, and then click Save. When you save the queue as a playlist, it is added to your permanent set of playlists. When created, you can access your permanent playlists in either Windows Media Center or Windows Media Player under Playlists.

    The queue is discarded if you do any of the following:
