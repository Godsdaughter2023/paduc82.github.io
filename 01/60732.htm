  4. In the list of options, click on Hardware and devices.

  5. Click Next and follow the on-screen instructions.

Method 2:

Let’s check Bluetooth service, start if it’s disabled.

  1. Go to the Start screen.

  2. Type “Services.msc” without quotes and press Enter.

  3. Right click on Bluetooth support service and click on Properties.

  4. Set the Startup type as Automatic and then click Start under Service status.

  5. Click on OK.

Method 3:

If the problem persists even after running Windows hardware and devices troubleshooter and starting Bluetooth support service, follow the suggestions by Angela T.  on February 7, 2013 in the thread below to remove ghost devices from Device Manager.
