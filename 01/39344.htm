iWar, give these things a try first

  1. Make sure your Xbox power supply is plugged into the wall, and not into another surge protector (your Xbox PSU is a surge protector, and you can do a quick Bing search to learn about all the reasons you shouldn't chain surge protectors)
  2. On your console, go into Settings -> All Settings -> Disc and Blu-ray -> Blu-ray -> Persistent Storage -> Clear (these are temporary files saved each time you put in a disc)
  3. Power drain your console by turning it off, unplugging the power supply from the back, then plugging it back in and turning your console on.  If you use Instant On power mode, your console normally 'sleeps' instead of turning off.  This makes sure it actually turns off and clears the storage files.
  4. Make your Xbox do some work.  Eject your disc, and this time when you put it in, instead of letting your Xbox lazily accept it, hold on to it just a smidge, so that the Xbox has to wake up and actually take the disc.  This helps make sure everything inside aligns to the size of the disc (did you know game discs can actually be different thicknesses?  This can affect how well the console reads them!)

Usually doing these four things clear up disc read errors, as long as it's not a completely broken disc drive.  There's a couple more solutions, as well as information on how to set up a repair, at this link, in case the steps above don't help: https://support.xbox.com/en-US/xbox-one/console/disc-error   I'm pretty sure from your post you already checked it, though.
