mouneshwar r say:

Hi Peter,

 

·         Could you provide the complete error message?

·         Do you also receive this error message while playing the DVD in Windows media player?

 

To prevent the illegal creation of perfect digital copies of DVDs, the information on most DVDs is scrambled. Your DVD decoder and Windows Media Player/Windows Media Center have copy protection software that decodes the information on the DVD just before displaying it. If this software doesn't start correctly, you might receive this error.

 

Method 1: Troubleshooting DVD playback

1.    Remove the DVD from the DVD drive and then reinsert it. This will force the copy protection software to reset.

 

2.    While the DVD is out of the drive, make sure that your DVD is clean. If your DVD is scratched or dirty, the DVD drive may not be able to read it correctly, and you might receive this error.

 

3.    If you have been able to play DVDs on your computer before, and you receive this error message, you might be able to solve the problem by restarting Windows Media Player/Windows media center on your computer.

 

4.    If you receive this error message the first time you try to play a DVD, you might not have a DVD decoder installed, or your decoder software might need to be updated. In addition to your DVD-ROM drive and Player software, you must have either a software or hardware decoder to play DVDs. Hardware decoders and their drivers are available from third-party manufacturers. For more information about software decoders, see

Plug-ins for Windows Media Player
http://www.microsoft.com/windows/windowsmedia/player/plugins.aspx#DVDDecoder

 

Method 2: Update your Video driver

Visit the link below to know how to update a device driver that isn’t working correctly.

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows-vista/Update-a-driver-for-hardware-that-isnt-working-properly

 

Method 3: Troubleshoot problems with DVDs and movies in Windows Media Center
