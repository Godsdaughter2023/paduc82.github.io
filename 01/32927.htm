Hi Bonnie,

Thank you for posting your query in Microsoft Community.

I understand that you are facing issues recording sound and I appreciate the effort made towards trying to fix it.

Many sound problems are caused by hardware that isn't set up properly. This step covers checking your sound card, plugging cables into the correct locations, making sure the hardware has power, and checking the volume.

In some cases, you can work around the problem by disabling optional speaker effects that are collectively known as enhancements. If disabling speaker effects solves the problem, you might need to update your audio driver.

If disabling audio enhancements solves the audio problem you're experiencing, check to see if an updated driver is available for your computer.

a. Go to Windows Update and install any available audio driver updates.

b. If no updates are available or if installing the update doesn't solve your problem, go to your computer manufacturer's website and search for audio driver updates for your specific computer model (updates are usually available in the "Support" or "Software Downloads" section of the company's website).

I would also suggest you to:

a. Unplug the USB audio device and restart your audio app. Test for sound using the internal audio device. If you hear sound, there might be problems with the USB audio driver or with Windows not using the USB audio device as the default audio device.

b. Close all audio apps, unplug the USB audio device, wait for the USB driver to be uninstalled (this should happen fairly quickly), plug the USB audio device back in to the USB port, wait for the driver to load, and then start the audio app and check for sound.

c. Check that you have the correct audio device set as the default in Windows and in the app.

d. Check the audio device manufacturer’s website for updated drivers.

frederik long say:

I originally thought that Mirroring would keep me safe should a drive fail and I wouldn't lose any data.

Most data loss occurs because of user error, file system corruption, external interference (malware, viruses), external influences (fire, water damage, theft, physical shock). Loss due to failed disks is rare. You would get far better protection for your data at a fraction of the price and without the complexity of a RAID array by taking these measures:
  • Back up your files regularly to an external medium.
  • Have two such media: One connected to the PC, the other one a long way away from it.
  • Create and maintain an image of your installation.
RAID solutions have something in common with all other backup solutions: Unless they are fully tested at install time (e.g. by starting the system with just one disk) they are close to useless.
