josephv_a say:

Hello LeTrollz,

 

 

Welcome to the Skype Community forum!

 

We recently had an emerging issue wherein customers are unable to send / receive messages or make calls. However, it should be resolved by now.

 

You may try the following to troubleshoot the issue:

 

  1. Delete shared.xml and config.xml file

> Make sure that Skype is no longer running.

> Press the Windows and R keys on your keyboard at the same time.

> Type "%appdata%\skype" into the Run window and click OK.

> Find and delete the shared.xml file. If file extensions are not displayed on your computer, the file will just be called shared.

> Open the folder that has your Skype name.

> Locate and delete the config or config.xml file.

2. Rename Skype folder

3. Un-install and re-install Skype

4. Install MSI version

5. /msnp24 and restart Skype

     > Sign in to the Skype application

     > Click on your Contacts Tab. Pick any contact. On a chat window any contact will do type /showplaces and click enter. This will show the devices that you are signed in. This needs to have a 1 end point

     > If you are sign in to 2 or more devices please type /remotelogout on your chat box with any contact and click enter.

     > Change the status of your account to offline or invisible.

     > Sign out and Sign back in to your account.

     > Ask a friend or a contact to monitor the status of your account

 

Should you have further queries, please do not hesitate to reply to this post.

 

Thank you.
