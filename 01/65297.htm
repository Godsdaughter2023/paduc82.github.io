Moving contents of old PC to new PC - Microsoft Community

bigbass22 say:

Moving contents of old PC to new PC

What is easiest and cleanest way to migrate all contents from my old PC ( XP ) to my new PC with Windows 8.1. Microsoft says it is making a download available from Laplink ( PCMover Express ). Will this downloaded software do the job if I connect the new PC to my router / FIOS network via a Ethernet cable and do the transfer from PC to PC via the network, or would it be more effective to use the Laplink USB cable by buying their software package?
lemp say:

It depends on what you mean by "all contents."

In general, it's easy to move data files (e.g., documents, pictures, music, etc.) between machines, and if the machines are all connected to the same network, copying over the network is the best way to accomplish the move (it's always prudent to  copy, check the new computer to see if all went well, and then delete from the old computer rather than to move, which automatically deletes the original files).

Some applications, such as Microsoft's Windows Easy Transfer, move "settings" as well as data.  Mostly, this includes things like desktop customizations and similar personalizations; it may also include browser "favorites," music playlists, and so forth.  These "settings" are really more data files, but they are located in more obscure places and the average user probably wouldn't know which files to move or where they should go.

In general, you can't move programs (applications).  These need to be reinstalled (assuming that they are compatible with the new version of Windows) using original installation media (CD, DVD, downloaded installer file).  Laplink's PCMover, however, claims to be able to move programs.  I've never used this product and I've seen mixed reviews.  So, if you either don't have installation media for your programs or you don't want to go through the reinstallation process, read the reviews and decide if you want to take a chance on this program.

As to whether you should use the proprietary USB cable or your network, apparently PCMover will work with both.  The main difference will be the speed of transfer, and that will depend on the type of network you have and the type of USB connection available.  Typical home networks today have a theoretical max data rate of 100 Mbps.  USB 2.0 has a theoretical max of 480 Mbps.  Gigabit networks are faster and so are USB 3.0 connections (although my guess would be that the Laplink USB cable doesn't support USB 3.0).  Here's what one 2010 review had to say on this topic:

With a USB2 external hard disk, we found it took around four hours to move 30GB of data, and over 100Mbits/sec Ethernet the same load took more than a day of solid copying. Your best bet is either a Gigabit LAN (if you have one) or the Laplink USB cable, either of which should in theory work out around twice as fast as an external drive.
