geoffc1 say: @HalleMax -

The "NO" symbol will show up on the photos while the photos are being dragged through the thumbnail pane. But if you drag them out of that pane into the Folder pane (on the left), then the symbol disappears. You will then see, as you drag the photos up and down the Folder hierarchy, a message similar to "Move to xyz", where xyz is the name of the Folder over which the photos are currently positioned. Releasing the mouse button to drop the photos will complete the operation of moving the photos from the source folder to the target folder ("xyz").

geoffc1 say: @HalleMax -

It sounds, from what you wrote, that you have used Windows Live Photo Gallery to import your photos (possibly from an attached external drive?).

If that is the case, then yes, WLPG will use rules to create folders and organise the imported photos into them.

You set those rules up in the Options panel of WLPG, under the "Import" tab. Take a look at that, and see if you can set rules that matches the way in which you want to have your photos organised.

If you can't, then you can always use Windows Explorer to set up the structure that you want on your PC, and then manually copy across the photos from the external drive. Once the photos are on your PC, then just tell WLPG where to find them (use the "include Folder" to give the starting point), and it will browse the photos and show them in WLPG.
