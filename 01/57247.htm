  3. Right click on it and select initialize.

Method 2

Try taking the ownership of the Drive and see. Follow the steps below:

  1. Go to Explorer and right click on your drive, then click Properties.
  2. Click the Security tab, click Advanced, then click the Owner tab.
  3. Click Edit. If you are prompted for an administrator password or confirmation, type the password or provide confirmation.
  4. Click on ‘other users or groups’.
  5. Type ‘Everyone’ without quotes in the box and click ok.
  6. Select the Replace owner on sub containers and objects check box.
  7. Click OK.

Now after changing the ownership of the file, you also need to change the permission on the file to allow full access.

Follow the steps below:

  1. Go to Explorer and right click on your drive, then click Properties.
  2. Click the Security tab, click Advanced, then click the Permissions tab.
  3. Click on change Permission and click on Add.
  4. Type ‘Everyone’ without quotes in the box and click ok.
  5. Select the Include inheritable permissions from this object’s parent check box.

Method 3

Change storage policies for the disk. Follow the steps below:

  1. Click on start and type ‘regedit’ in the search bar without quotes.

  5. Change the value to 0, that is set “Default value: DWORD=0”.
