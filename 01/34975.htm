You are not wrong.  In the world of email contacts, if you press Delete, it is deleted. However, in WLM you have the opportunity to right click on an entry while in the Category list and remove it only from the Category, not from your Master Contact List.  So you have to be careful what you are doing.  Look carefully at your Right Click options.  "Remove Contact from Category" versus "Delete Contact" have different meanings.  Some people still like to retain the contact in their address book after "removing it from the category".

Unfortunately for WLM they do not allow us to ADD Fields, which would be the solution to most of the design problems of WLM.  Having a FIELD option in the creation of these contacts which would allow us export our whole Contact list, sort it by the CATEGORY, identify all our CLUB in that Category.

This is one of the worst things about WLM.  Lack of options for sorting contacts.

That said, you could use "Notes" for this purpose.  In your Notes section of the Contact form, you type your CLUB name.  Then export your entire contact list to a CSV spreadsheet.  Make sure that when you export, you click NEXT and choose all the fields you want to appear in that spreadsheet.  Make sure "Notes" and all the fields are checked off.  Save the Contact list to a folder in My Documents labeled Address Lists or something that handy to find.  Open it with Excel Spreadsheet or Open Office Database, and SORT it by the NOTES column.  If all your Club Contacts have the right designation in the Notes, it will be a piece of cake to then delete all the contacts that don't have the word "CLUB" in the Notes column.

Then, and only then might I go back to Windows Live Mail > Contact List > Open the Club Category and either permanently delete them or remove them from the Category.  From here on in, I would manipulate my CLUB contacts using the Excel or Open Office Database exclusively, making regular backups I might add!

When it comes time to mail to your Club, you open your spreadsheet, copy the column of 100 contacts email addresses at a time, drop them into your BCC section of your email, and repeat the process till you have mailed your email to your whole club.  There is a limit of 100 contacts per email message in WLM.  *I add that you might have to manipulate how the email address appears in the spreadsheet column.  If they are not "hyperlinks" they may not work in WLM.  I have discovered that GMAIL handles a spreadsheet column of email addresses nicely, but WLM seems confused.  *

If you are the Club Secretary, this is what I would recommend.  It will save you a lot of hassles.  And, when it comes time to pass along your files to the next Secretary, you just give them the spreadsheet and explain how it's done, and your own address book is not a complete mess.   This prevent conflicts between various email programs, leaving each person to sort out how to copy the contacts into the TO, CC or BCC box.

Having used WLM for quite some time now, I would exercise extreme real caution when integrating a CLUB list with a personal email Contact List, as it will likely cause some confusion in the long term. 

No one should integrate names from their CLUB database enmasse into their CONTACT list.  If you allow this (Not to say that you can prevent it), the list can become out of date quickly.  Someone has to be in charge of the lists updating on a regular basis.  I have seen gmail documents and spreadsheets used fairly effectively for this if you have multiple people sharing a document.  Maybe even One Drive works if more than one of you have to access that information.  But, I am a bit sticky about who updates my emailing list as some people are not as careful as others, and you might find things accidentally deleted by less careful persons.  You can allow people to READ ONLY.

SO, the Categories is just a "Shortcut" back to your "Contacts"; if you delete the Contact (Master File), your shortcuts, in this category list, will also be deleted.

WLM is not intended as business application, nor is it completely developed in all areas.  It is more for home use, which would generally have more simple applications.  

If you are considering purchasing MS Outlook email client, please keep in mind that you will have to study how to convert things to that, as the file extensions are different.  I believe you can export your WLM contacts into a CSV file and then import them into another email program contact list.

You might like to look into MS Outlook email client which has a more sophisticated system of categories, or if your list and club is extremely large, investigate other (not free) mailing programs that organizations use.   They have better query capabilities (e.g. Tapestry).

If you are handy with Access Database, I would recommend putting your contacts in a DATABASE, not a SPREADSHEET.   A database allows you to query, sort, extract particular information. 

MS Works has a fantastic Database (MS works 9 - works with Win7), but Microsoft doesn't know how to support their products and have abandoned selling it.  Shame on them. 

You might consider learning the Open Office Database program if you don't have Access as part of your cheap home/student package.   I don't understand Access, never have.
