Hi,

Could be your computer just does not have the complete specs to run those games. Best to check with the game makers' support and forums.

ID41 with no Bug_Check - The main cause of this issue as you have described is hardware related :                

https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/help/2028504/windows-kernel-event-id-41-error-the-system-has-rebooted-without-clean  

1. Over-Clocking -> best to reset ALL to the defaults especially while troubleshooting

2. Memory (check thoroughly as indicated below).

3. Power Supply (see below to ensure you have enough power).

4. Over-heating - even if the overall temps are with-in specs a single component can be too sensitive to even the normal levels of heat and fault at a lower temp. Try adding a fan blowing into the case to reduce the effects of heat even more than the cooler usually does. Of course ensure all built-in fans are running.

====================================================


After checking determining the wattage of the power supply you will then need to check the requirements of the motherboard and devices. Usually, the video card is one of the major power users.

How do I determine how big my power supply is?

How Do I Test the Power Supply in My Computer?

behind slower memory. So it is best to also swap sticks in and out to check for

aerdian say:

My initial thought considering you were playing games would be that hardware on your device overheated. When different components get higher than the recommended maximum temperature, they will crash your computer to prevent damage. When gaming, that is the normal cause of a crash. You can check your temperatures with a program called NZXT CAM. https://camwebapp.com/download. If you have higher temperatures than you should, ensure you have plenty of fans, don't have your computer on carpeting, and make sure your case has plenty of airflow. (If you have a laptop, it should be made to be safe temperatures).

If your temperatures look fine when playing games, I would recommend checking your memory.

  • Press Windows Key. Type mdsched.exe. Press Enter.
  • Choose Run the tool immediately.
  • Your computer will restart automatically and after login, you'll be able to view test results.
