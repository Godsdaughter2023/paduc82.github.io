How to disable Automatic Windows Updates and/or Automatic Driver - Microsoft Community

pashap say:

How to disable Automatic Windows Updates and/or Automatic Driver Installation on Windows 10.

1. Start the Group Policy editor: windowkey + R and type gpedit.msc, press enter.

2. To disable the driver installations.
-In Policy editor go to Computer Configuration > Administrative Templates > System > Device Installation > Device Installation.
-On the right pane click on Prevent Installation of Devices not described by other policy settings.
-Set it to Enabled. Click Apply/OK.
-Windows Update will now fail all driver updates because of the group policy denial.
(WARNING: this will disable ALL driver installations, even the manual ones-- you may want to investigate other policy options that are not so indiscriminate.

In order to prevent a specific device from being updated use the Prevent Installation of Devices that match any of these device IDs group policy:
- Find your device ID: https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/cc731134%28v=ws.10%29.aspx (it would look something like this PCI\VEN_10DE&DEV_05EA&SUBSYS_075310DE&REV_A1)
-In Policy editor go to Computer Configuration > Administrative Templates > System > Device Installation > Device Installation.
-On the right pane click on Prevent Installation of Devices that match any of these device IDs

-Set it to Enabled. Click on the "Show..." button and add your Device ID. Click OK. Do NOT click the checkbox that says "Also apply to matching devices that are already installed"(or else your device will be uninstalled. Click Apply/OK.).

3. To disable automatic Windows updates:
-In Policy editor go to Computer Configuration > Windows Components > Windows Update .
-On the right pane click on Configure Automatic Updates.
-Set it to Enabled, and in the "Configure automatic updating:" select "3 - Auto download and notify for install" OR "2 - Notify for download and notify for install". Click Apply/OK.
Now windows will tell you "You need some updates - Click this message to install" in Action Center whenever there are updates.
If you have disabled the driver installs in previous step, the driver updates will not install.

This worked on Windows 10 Pro.
Regards,

(It looks like this will not work for Windows 10 Home since the Home edition doesn't have Group Policy Management)

System restore: This option takes your PC back to an earlier point in time, called a system restore point. Restore points are generated when you install a new app, driver, or Windows update, and when you create a restore point manually. Restoring won’t affect your personal files, but it will remove apps, drivers, and updates installed after the restore point was made.

System Restore

  1. Click the Start button, then click on Settings

  2. Then click on Update and Security, locate and select Recovery and click on Advance Startup.

  3. You will now enter the Windows Recovery Environment, once there click on Troubleshoot and then Advance Options.

  4. Click on "System Restore". Enter any administrator passwords (if prompted).

  5. Follow the steps in the Wizard to choose a restore point.
