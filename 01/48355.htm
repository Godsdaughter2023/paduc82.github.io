Who knows, you might get lucky, but more than likely you'll only be wasting your time and can even compound your problem by performing a technical procedure incorrectly, or by drawing an incorrect conclusion, as you have done.

A clean install is never a solution: It's a do-over. It doesn't help you to understand what went wrong or how to avoid a repeat of the same thing. Moreover, a clean install is no walk in the park. If you want to get it right on your first try you'll need to do lots of preparation. And once your clean install is over, you have to reinstall all your software and drivers, update everything that needs updating, setup your computer the way you like it, restore your backed up personal data and then test everything to see that it's working right. Nice way to kill off a summer Saturday.

On top of all that, if your problem is actually caused by hardware, reinstalling software is not going to accomplish anything.

This is why I always recommend that if someone is unable to accurately identify the cause of an error on their own or with help, they should not hesitate to bring their computer to a professional. Nothing beats watching someone who really knows their sh*t work on your computer and hearing them explain what they're doing, why they're doing it, what happened to your computer and what you can do so it won't happen again. That, in my view, is money well spent.

In my opinion, there's a huge difference between masking a problem so it seems to go away, or treating its symptoms, and actually solving a problem so it doesn't affect you now and won't affect you in the future.

So how did I finally fix the problem? Well, apparently it’s a super simple solution! No need to edit the registry, uninstall drivers, or anything of that sort.

Simply UNPLUG YOUR COMPUTER FROM THE POWER SUPPLY . Yes that’s it! Note that this does not mean just turning off your computer because modern computers don’t really turn off when you press the power button as the motherboard still gets power.

First to explain, the motherboard is where all of hardware of the computer is connected to, including the USB ports. Sometimes the motherboard needs to be “rebooted” also because something can go wrong, i.e. all your USB ports suddenly stop working. The small microprocessor on the motherboard will reload the drivers and your USB ports should be back to recognizing all devices!

So first turn off your computer using Shut Down or pressing the power button and then UNPLUG it from the wall outlet. Let it sit for about a minute and then plug it back in.

Hope this helps someone fix that highly irritating “USB device not recognized” Windows error!
