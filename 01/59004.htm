1. Turn off your laptop and inspect the HDMI port with a flashlight. If you notice a visible crack somewhere in the port, then you are dealing with a hardware problem. You'll need to take your laptop to a trained professional for further inspection and repair.

2. If no visible crack found, try to dip a cotton swab in isopropyl alcohol and gently swab the edges of the HDMI port. Do not press the cotton swab deep into the HDMI port, as tiny cotton fibers can get stuck inside it. Cleaning the edges of the port helps the cable fit more snugly and allows a better connection.

3. Blow compressed air into the port. Compressed air flushes out internal debris without the risk of leaving behind cotton fiber residue.

4. Reconnect your second monitor with the HDMI port and turn on your laptop, press Win + P again to see if this works.

Good luck!
