n01d3a say: I have noticed that with windows 8, when the touchpad is disabled and no external mouse is plugged in, the cursor will disappear.

First and foremost, I always recommend restarting windows. Sometimes updates are installed that may affect your system. Assuming you have already restarted the computer, I recommend the following.

Most laptops will have a function key along the top row that has an icon which is supposed to resemble a touchpad with a line going through it.

Once you find this key, press the "fn" key and this function key at the same time. The "fn" key is normally located near the bottom left of the keyboard. If the touchpad was accidentally disabled, this should re-enable the touchpad. 

Some laptops don't have a function key for this, but a specific button. This button may be above the touchpad or elsewhere.

At this point, if you are still having issues, I recommend plugging in a USB mouse because it will make things much easier. 

If this does not work, you mouse may still be disabled using other software. If you press the windows key, which will take you to the start screen, then type the word "mouse." Off to the right hand side, select settings. You should now see a list, select the option that has a picture of a mouse and says mouse.

Now, select the tab on the top that says hardware. On this screen, about midway down, you should see an option that says disable this device or something like that. Make sure this option is not selected. If it is not selected, your mouse may be defective or there might be other issues causing your problem.

Hope this helps.

I'm Daniela, an independent advisor and I'm here to help you. Your display name on Skype is different from your Skype username. You can change the display name at any time, and that's what other Skype users see when they communicate with you. Your Skype username is the email address used to create your Microsoft account.

You can change the display name of Skype simply by opening the app and selecting your name.

Here's how to change your Skype name:

1) Start the Skype app.
2) Click on your Skype profile image or the display name, both in the upper left corner of the screen.
3) Click on your name displayed on Skype.
4) Type a new name.
5) Press Enter / Enter or click the checkmark in the right part of the text box.

Alternatively, you can also change display name to Skype on the Skype website:

1) Open your web browser and go to Skype.com.
2) Click on your name in the upper right corner of the screen.
3) From the drop-down menu that appears, click on My account.
4) Click Edit Profile, located at the bottom left and bottom right of the screen.
5) Click Edit profile again.
6) Change your name, then click Save.

Printing documents or files on a paper doesn't literally mean that you need to have all of the colors in your ink cartridge. However, the print quality may be reduced if one is missing. If Windows was preinstalled on the computer:

Reinstall Windows Preinstalled On Computer

OEM computer manufacturers are required to provide at least one way for you to reinstall Windows if you buy a computer with Windows preinstalled - your computer should have included either a restore partition on your hard drive, or Windows install/restore disks. ﻿Most computer manufacturers also have a feature to make your own install disks.

If the restore partition is still there and was not removed, it will reinstall Windows 7. There is usually a prompt when you first start the computer telling you what key to press to start the restore﻿ - check your manual if you're not sure how to. Or if you have install disks - insert them, restart and boot from them to start the reinstall process.

If you restore it to factory condition, it will be like it was when you first bought it - all preinstalled programs will be installed. Anything you installed after buying it must be reinstalled. This will remove any personal files on the computer, back them up first.


Contact the OEM computer manufacturer and ask them to send you an install disk for your computer - they may or may not do it. If they agree to send you one they may charge a small fee. Contact information for the manufacturer of your computer can be found here:

http://support.microsoft.com/gp/oemphone/
