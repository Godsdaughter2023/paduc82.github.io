gza say:

A cone NAT is a good thing. Per Microsoft, cone NAT works best with and is recommended for Xbox Live.

download.microsoft.com/.../Xbox-Live.doc

So, that is not your problem.  Getting a "port not open" etc. message with port check tools is normal because the Xbox isn't actually listening on the ports you likely checked. Despite what many here recommend under the mistaken belief it is needed (largely due to misleading information on the MS support site), these are the only ports you need to forward:

88 UDP

3074 TCP and UDP

and if you have COD Ghosts, also:

3075 TCP and UDP

3076 TCP and UDP

With your router, you should not even have to forward ports, just enable UPnP (pretty sure that router has a good UPnP implementation even with stock firmware).  Just delete all port forwards and DMZ (it is rarely a good idea for home users to use DMZ) and enable UPnP.  If you are somewhat adventurous, you can flash your particular model router with superior third-party firmware. I use Tomato, but you may want to start with RMerlin, which is just an improved version of the stock Asus firmware (Asus even incorporates some of his changes into the official firmware, which is open source).  Even if you stick with the official Asus firmware, do make sure it has been updated to the latest version (via the web interface).

External Hard Drive Issues - Microsoft Community

the3ggman say:

External Hard Drive Issues

I've been using a WD MyBook 4TB hard drive with my Xbox One for almost a year now.  Just today I thought my xbox was broke because it was stuck on the green loading screen.  I starting unplugging cables to see what caused the problem and as soon as I unplugged the HDD, the xbox would load properly.  As soon as I plug the HDD back in, the xbox goes black(Actually, it's the default picture from my AV Receiver on the screen that shows up, but for arguments sake the screen is black.) .  Then I unplug it, the xbox will reboot and come to it's main menu without a problem....

...so something isn't working with my HDD. Whether it's the hard drive itself or the Xbox I don't know.  Any thoughts on how to fix this?

maxen say:

Disconnect the hard drive and perform a power cycle (support.xbox.com/.../power-cycle-console) with the console. Then reconnect the hard drive and see if it begins behaving.

If that's not working, you may wish to try a factory reset on the console with the external drive disconnected. This will delete the contents of the internal drive and you will need to redownload them, but it's probably less content than would need to be redownloaded onto the external hence why I suggest this first. Game saves on the internal will be deleted, but they are backed up to the cloud if you play while connected to Xbox Live, so they will be resynced to your internal drive next time you play each game.

If even that is not working, can you reformat the hard drive either with the console or a PC? This will delete all its contents and you will need to redownload games.

Let us know how you get on or if you have further questions about anything.
