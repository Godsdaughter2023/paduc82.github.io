bossdweeb say:

- You may want to drop by the ASUS forums as they are more likely to have someone that has experience with

your particular model who can offer some ideas on how to prevent your fan speed cycling issue.

It may not be helpful, but I'll babble on a bit more anyway...

"After that moment, when GPU reaches 80C, it trottles"

Oops. I had looked at a review for your laptop  and they mentioned 91C, but looking again I see that I

overlooked that this was due to their unconventional method of measuring the temp from the outside

of the case using a probe (must have been awfully hot inside though).

- Running the Nvidia gpu over 80C is not desirable, but it should be able to handle up to 90C

without becoming unstable.

However, laptops have little space for air flow, which would mean the fans have to run faster for longer

to bring temps back in control, so I am not surprised if the gpu begins to throttle at 80C.

I would be even less surprised if the cpu began to throttle at 75C or less.

On a desktop the throttling temp for gpu has been 100C +/- ,  but once a gpu hits 90C instability is more likely

(with a few exceptions. The old Nvidia GTX480 were hot running gpu's and 90C was not uncommon).

I may be going on old information as well. The 900 series gpu uses less power than previous models, so it may

have a lower throttling temp ?


Obviously ambient air temps also affect cooling, so if the air temp is cool and your machine struggles to

keep cool, and the fans still go from 0-100% all the time there is more likelihood of faulting components.

- If the ambient temps are say 28C + you may find a cooling pad helpful.

When you use SpeedFan have you set up a profile that so the minimum fan(s) speed @40C,  is as fast as

possible without being too loud (often between 50-60%), and then ramping the speed up to something

like 70% @ 60C  - 80% @ 70C (or whatever other increments that keep the heat under control without

too much noise )  ?

"...trouble with Intel video card"

The Intel HD graphics is actually part of the cpu.  Cpu temps should be lower than the Nvidia gpu temp.

They don't have as high a heat tolerance as gpu's. I would be a bit concerned if my Intel cpu went over 75C

(my older AMD cpu has a max temp of 65C ).

3D and video rendering work the cpu pretty hard, so I wouldn't be surprised if a laptop cpu struggled to keep cool

doing this sort of work over extended periods. Laptops are notorious for high temps when pushed.
