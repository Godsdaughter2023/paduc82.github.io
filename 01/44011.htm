I have been going over some of the very good responses and your comments below.  So why not throw one more into the ring right, LOL.  

I have been running the Windows 10 Preview versions on an old Windows Vista machine for months and have never worried about overheating my processor.  A processor is usually only doing work when you first start a program or are changing to a different web page or doing something like playing a game. MOST of the time your processor is just sitting idle not doing much of anything when you are doing something like reading a web page.  You can actually see how busy your processor is by doing a Ctrl, Alt and Delete and then by starting the task manager.  In the task manager you can look at the graphs of the work your processor is doing along with your memory and hard disk activity.    

But I certainly understand your concerns so here is what I recommend.  I would create what is known as a DUAL BOOT SYSTEM.  

What is dual boot or a dual booting system?  Well it takes a little work to set it up but basically it allows you to pick WHICH operating system you would like to RUN when you first start your computer.  In short what you will be doing is creating a new partition or section on your computer's hard drive and then installing Windows 10 Preview edition on that new partition.  If your current hard drive is called C:\ and if you have a CDROM drive then your second partition will most likely be named E:\.  The size of the new partition you want to creating should be about 30-50 GB if you have that much empty space on your hard drive.  

Anyway when you create this new partition you can name it something easily understood like "Windows_10".   To make a long story short, this is where you will be installing the Windows 10 Preview edition.

When you install Windows 10 it will give you several choices.  One of those choices will be to SPECIFY where you want to INSTALL Windows 10.  That is why you named that new partition on your hard drive Windows 10 so there is no misunderstanding of where to install it.  Once Windows 10 installs itself in about an hour or so, you will be asked to reboot your computer.  When your computer reboots you will be given the choice of which operating system you want to run.     

Just make sure that when you install Windows 10 Preview edition to your computer you install it to your newly created partition.   If you don't, Windows 10 will overwrite your current operating system.  

Now all of this might sound really complicated but it really isn't.  Just take you time and learn as you go. www.howtogeek.com/187789/dual-booting-explained-how-you-can-have-multiple-operating-systems-on-your-computer/

I believe creating a dual boot system on your computer might be the answer to the concerns you have since you can always go back to what you had by just rebooting your computer.
