cmdrkeene say:
That means two things:
  1. First:  The folder you created (with the lock) is private and only you can see it.  No one else, on your network or other users of the same computer, can open that folder.  It's for your eyes only.

  2. And Next:  The folder it is in (the parent of the folder with the lock) is not private.  If it were (if both folders were private), there would be no lock because it would be redundant.  For example, if you went inside the locked folder and made another folder, it would be private too, but it would not show the lock.
You can right-click the folder with the lock and choose to "Share With" someone to remove the lock by giving someone else access to the folder.

techfreak_ say:

While the local SQLite databases are unencrypted (main.db, skype.db, etc.), they are not accessible unless you provide physical and access-level permissions to a user or user account to view them or make a copy. Those two forms of security would have to be broken, in order for the data to be accessible to everyone. This would apply to any unencrypted data (cached or otherwise) on a computer. Even if data is encrypted, it is only as strong as its encryption. Once access is provided to it, it may be accessible to everyone with enough resources. A padlock does little for an item in your house when the front door is unlocked and left wide open.
