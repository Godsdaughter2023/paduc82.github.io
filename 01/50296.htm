Iwork on a 3 year old laptop running Vista Home Premium 32 bit.  This PC has 1 GB installed memory and I am considering adding more.  I can add another 1 or 2 GB and was wondering how much the performance might improve. 

How much RAM you need for good performance depends on what apps you
run. However for almost everyone running Vista who does more than just
play solitaire, 1GB is insufficient for good performance. I can't tell
you exactly how much your performance would improve by going to 2GB,
but I would expect the improvement to be substantial.

The question should you go to more than 2GB is harder. You get good
performance if the amount of RAM you have keeps you from using the
page file significantly, and that depends on what apps you run. Most
people running a typical range of business applications under Vista
find that somewhere around 2GB works well, others need more. Some
people, particularly those doing things like editing large
photographic images, can see a performance boost by adding even more.

If you are currently using the page file significantly, more memory
will decrease or eliminate that usage, and improve your performance.
If you are not using the page file significantly, more memory will do
next to nothing for you. Go to
http://billsway.com/notes%5Fpublic/winxp%5Ftweaks/ and download
WinXP-2K_Pagefile.zip and monitor your page file usage. That should
give you a good idea of how much more memory can help.

Skype US Numbers have never officially supported incoming SMS texts until recently. Even so, only incoming texts from actual phone numbers is supported. Due to this, this could not be used as a form of multi-factor authentication. Only a mobile number would work. If the authentication comes from a Microsoft/Skype short code, there is always a possibility such short codes might be received. However, I am unaware of a method to send a reply, even for a test, as the Skype client has no capability to dial short codes from the Skype dialer.

Any services provided greater than what is officially offered is not guaranteed, as it could stop functioning at any time. I know of one user claiming to get transcribed incoming Skype SMS texts to his email. That was certainly abnormal and if true, likely due to a local provider misconfiguration. If your Skype Number supported incoming SMS texts from short codes or from phone numbers for a long time, consider yourself lucky and  in the minority as the Skype Number I have had for over a decade only recently started receiving incoming texts from actual phone numbers.

If your issue was simply having issues receiving SMS verification codes from a Microsoft/Skype source for multi-factor authentication or some other purpose, it is best to text 'Help' (if possible) to the last known number used to send the verification.
