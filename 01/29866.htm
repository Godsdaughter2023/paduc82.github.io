ralf_g say:

What can I do to destroy these DOS and DDOS attacks?

 

btw, I just kicked out of the internet a few minutes ago :S


Not a lot you can do to stop it while your IP address remains the same. If your internet provider uses dynamic IP addressing you'll often get a different IP each time you log on. When you change IP the attacks stop, at least until they find you again. If the attacker is monitoring your chat group or IRC channel and you go back in right away it won't take them long to start up again. Same thing if they are watching the connections on P2P apps, especially the ones that identify users with nicknames. They don't know or care who you are but the nickname tells them it's the same computer coming online again.

It's hard to identify the true source of an attack, especially for DDOS because those guys can be using multiple trojan infected computers to mount their attacks with. Even a DOS attacker can be spoofing his IP with a fake address so it makes them hard to find or block. Router's with firewalls can have the ability to block specific IPs or whole IP ranges. The router activity logs might indicate attack type activity and which IPs they originate from so you could block those IPs yourself using the router firewall... How much info you get in the logs depends on the router model. Some routers are better than others at dealing with this sort of thing. Some can even block attack packets automatically. A bit about Stateful Packet Inspection in routers:

 http://www.homenethelp.com/router-guide/features-firewall.asp

Some good tips here for configuring your router:

http://blogs.technet.com/steriley/archive/2006/07/10/Configure-your-router-to-block-DOS-attempts.aspx

  The other thing you can do is change your online habits. Try to identify which of your activities is attracting the attackers and stop using it if you can't get the attacks blocked. Some IRC channels police themselves and have methods to boot offenders if they can be identified but I don't know how common that is.
