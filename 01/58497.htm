waseemulla shariff say:
Hi,
 
1. How did she delete the recovery drive?
2. Are you referring the recovery drive to another partition on the hard disk?
 
Sometimes the drives will be missing from Windows Explorer if the drive letters are misplaced. Try the following steps to check if the drive is still present.
1. Click Start, type diskmgmt.msc and hit enter.
2. Check if the recovery drive is present in the drives list.
3. If there is a drive without a letter assigned to it, assign a new drive letter.
4. To assign a drive letter if one has not already been assigned, right-click the partition or drive, click Add, click the letter that you want to use, and then click OK.
5. Close the disk management tool and restart the computer.
 
For more information on "Change, add, or remove a drive letter" check this link:
http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows-vista/Change-add-or-remove-a-drive-letter
 
Hope this helps.
