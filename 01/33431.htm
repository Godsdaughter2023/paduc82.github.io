Thanks for following up. While waiting for your response, we tried to reproduce this issue on our end and was able to find the necessary paths and keys to the Registry that needs to be edited for a workaround. Here are the steps:

  1. Press Windows + R (run), type regedit, and hit OK.
  2. On the Registry Editor window, click the address bar and type Computer\HKEY_CLASSES_ROOT\.smod then hit Enter.
  3. Right-click on the .smod Registry Key, and click Delete. Select Yes on the prompt to permanently delete the key and its subkeys.
  4. Do the same steps from Step 2 and Step 3, but this time for the following Registry Key: Computer\HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Explorer\FileExts\.smod
  5. Restart your computer for the changes to take effect.

Before doing the above steps, make sure that you perform a back up of the registry. In case something goes wrong, you can restore the registry to get it back to the state prior to editing.

Please also note that doing these steps may remove the file extension .smod from the "list of default apps by file type" of your Settings. However, this should not affect the Stonehearth's capability of accessing your .smod files.
