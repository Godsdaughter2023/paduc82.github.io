We'll need more details.

What is the original resolution / pixel dimensions of the source picture? Is from a camera? What brand?

Where do you see this pixelation?

Is it in the preview window during previewing of the project in Movie Maker?

Is it if you preview in a fullscreen window in Movie Maker?

One common way that you can see "pixelation" added to your pictures is if you are expecting your multi-megapixel pictures from a camera to look exactly the same with the full pixel resolution when put into a video. Remember even an HD resolution is 1920 x 1080 or about 2 megapixels. So by definition you might be taking your 16 megapixel photo and losing resolution when put into a movie. Additionally the compression on a static picture into a video format designed for moving pictures can cause issue. You may not have specified enough of a bitrate for the video to be able to load a full resolution HD picture quick enough and so it initially appears pixelated and then perhaps sharpens as the video goes along.

Without knowing more of the symptoms it's hard to diagnose what you mean by "my pictures look pixelated".

  3. Drag-select the command below and press [Ctrl]+[C] to copy it:
    • regsvr32 /i C:\Windows\system32\shell32.dll
  4. Switch back to the command window, right-click in the blackness, click [Paste], and press [Enter] to execute the command.  A message should appear indicating that the command was successful.
  5. If you're running a 32-bit edition of Windows, skip to step 8.
  6. Drag-select the command below and press [Ctrl]+[C] to copy it:
    • regsvr32 /i C:\Windows\syswow64\shell32.dll
  7. Switch back to the command window, right-click in the blackness, click [Paste], and press [Enter] to execute the command.  A message should appear indicating that the command was successful.
