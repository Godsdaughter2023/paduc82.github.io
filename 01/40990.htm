Xbox One uses different behaviors to benefit and to make peer to peer (P2P) more simple than the Xbox 360. The main benefits is IPv6 with IPsec and Teredo (which is a transition mechanism that gives full IPv6 connectivity for IPv4 connectivity that do not have a native connection to an IPv6 network).

Xbox One will mainly use Teredo implementation (which is sensitive to NAT behavior) and it prefer port 3074 for P2P traffic which is the same port for Xbox 360 traffic. Does this mean that your current setup will break UDP PortMappings (Port Forwarding, QoS) for Xbox One! No it doesn't!. If there is a port competition problems, Xbox One has a fall-back-mechanism to use other ports as well if 3074 is not available.
