Hi,

 

Thank you so much for getting back to us with the updates. We are sorry to hear that the above suggestions did not resolve the issue.

 

This issue happens when the syncing between the Skydrive and the computer is broken. Thus, it will try to resync again and hangs on that process when the computer starts up. Some users were able to resolve this issue by converting the effected Microsoft Account to a Local Account and then reconverting it back to the Microsoft Account. This will again rebuild the connection with the SkyDrive.

 

I would suggest you to try that as well and check if that resolves the issue. To convert you Microsoft Account to a local account, you may follow these steps:

 

a) Sign in to the effected Microsoft account on the computer.

b) Press “Windows Logo” + “C” keys on the keyboard and then click on “Settings” from the Charms bar.

c) Now, click on “Change PC settings”.

d) Click on “Account” and then click “Your account” on the left hand side of the "Accounts" screen, click on “Disconnect” button under your Microsoft account display name on the right hand side of the screen.

e) Enter your Microsoft account's password, and click on “Next”.

g) Click on “Sign out and finish” button.

 

NOTE: This will sign you out of your Microsoft account, and take you to the lock screen to sign in to your new local account.

 

h) Sign in to your new local user account and we are done.

 

Now, you need to reconvert this local account to the same Microsoft Account. Follow the steps given below:

 

a) Sign in to the new local account on the computer.

b) Press “Windows Logo” + “C” keys on the keyboard and then click on “Settings” from the Charms bar.

c) Now, click on “Change PC settings”.

d) Click on “Account” and then click “Your account” on the left hand side of the "Accounts" screen, click on “Connect to a Microsoft Account” button. You might be asked to verify your current Local Account password.

e) Enter you Microsoft Account email ID and click “Next”.

f) Now, you may restart the computer and check if the issue still persists.

a thameem say:

It sometimes depends on the security software you have on the computer. If the security software you have has a firewall in it or if you have installed a third party firewall software then you don’t need to have windows firewall turned on. As having two firewall softwares will conflict each other, so to avoid conflicts Windows Firewall will generally turn itself OFF.

But if you are sure that you don’t have any other firewall on the computer then try the following steps:

Method 1:

Check if the windows firewall service is set to automatic

Start -> Run -> type SERVICES.MSC then hit Enter key -> In the Services windows scroll down to findWindows Firewall -> Right Click and click Properties  

Set he “Start up type ” to Automatic and not manual

Method 2:

Start -> Run -> type NETSH FIREWALL RESETand hit Enter

Restart the computer

Also Check: http://support.microsoft.com/kb/283673
