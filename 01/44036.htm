Hi nchap2,

 

Comparison between PATA drive and SATA drive

 

·         The connect pin height for SATA are designed so that ground pins connects first while connecting the cable and when disconnecting, the ground pins are removed last. As such SATA hard disk can be removed even in running condition without any damage to the disks.

·         SATA is capable of data burst rate of up to 150 MB/s whereas PATA can deliver only up to 133 MB/s speed. The latest SATA II technology can have burst rate up to 300 MB/s. SATA has sustained throughput of around 55 MB/s while it is 52 MB/s data transfer rate for PATA drives. So, there is about 5% increase in efficiency in sustained throughput.

·         SATA cables have only four wires two for sending and two for receiving data. On the other hand, PATA cables have 40 pins with 20 pins at a row. PATA cables come in 40-wire or 80-wire designs. Most modern storage devices require the use of the more capable 80-wire PATA cable to meet certain speed requirements. PATA cables are bulky, hard to install and impede air-flow in the chassis. SATA cables are easier to install & uninstall, consume minimal space and do not impede the air-flow thereby allowing better circulation.

·         SATA use low voltage differential signaling method that allows higher transmission speeds on longer cables. So the cable length may be upto 1 mtr long. PATA cables can be up to a maximum of 45cm.

·         The connect pins are more robust and hence durable in PATA. There is a risk of bending the pins in the motherboard connector for SATA.

·         The power connectors for SATA and PATA are also different. It is simpler (and less painful on the hand) to connect SATA power cables.

·         SATA drives do not need to use jumper settings for master or slave configurations. In fact SATA drive has eliminated the concept of master-slave configurations. You simply use a different connection for each drive. The simplicity means ease of installation too.

·         SATA enables lower cost but higher density hard drives unlike PATA. Today we have terabyte SATA drives available at commodity prices, something we couldn't even imagine for PATA drives.

Overall SATA drives are significantly superior to PATA drives. If your Motherboard supports SATA as well as PATA drives then always go for SATA drives. The only reason you should buy PATA drives today is for legacy system and then you should stock them now because very soon they may not even be available.

To convert PATA to SATA you needs to change the Motherboard. Generally, it's not possible to change PATA to SATA HDD. So you can check your laptop model at the gateway website if it supports SATA before buying one.

http://support.gateway.com/support/drivers/dlcenter.asp?cmpid=topnav
