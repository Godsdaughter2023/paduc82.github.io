    Hi Mika, to remove the need for a password in Windows 10, you must first switch to a Local Account, rather than a Microsoft account for login . . .

    Open the Settings App
    Go to Accounts - Sign-in Options
    Choose to sign in with a local account
    (in newer versions of Windows 10, this might be on the Your Info Settings Page)
    Enter your old password and enter a new password or leave it blank as appropriate
    Close the Settings App

    Click your Start Button, type netplwiz and hit Enter
    In the resulting dialog, uncheck the option 'Users must enter a user name and password to use this computer box'
    Click OK

    Once that is done, log out and log back in

    Hi PDrummond,

    Welcome to Microsoft Answers.

    Is it an upgrade installation or a clean installation?

    It could be possible that the icon cache is corrupted. You may consider reloading the icon images into the icon cache to fix this issue.

    Before following this, make sure that you have configured to show hidden files, folders and drives.

    Go to Organize -> Folder and Search Options -> View tab, and select "Show hidden files, folders and drives", and uncheck "Hide protected operating system files (Recommended)".

    To refresh and reset the icons cache, follow either of these two methods.

    Method 1:

    Go to the following folder:
    C:\Users\<user name>\AppData\Local\
    Once inside the folder, delete IconCache.db. Create a new file named IconCache.db, and set it to Read Only attribute in Properties (if the file is not read only, the cache won’t be reset).     2. In the command prompt, type each command line below one at a time followed by pressing enter after each single line.
    CD /d %userprofile%\AppData\Local
    DEL IconCache.db /a
    EXIT
    3. Log off and log on, or restart the computer to rebuild the icon cache.

    andre da costa say:
    Why are my Skype contacts or credit missing?

    If you're signed into Skype but don't see your contacts or Skype Credit, you probably just signed in to a different account than you were expecting to see.


    How did this happen?

    You might have multiple Microsoft accounts and not even know it.
    Have you ever used Outlook.com, Hotmail, Office 365, OneDrive, Xbox, or Windows? The email address you use for those services can also be used to sign in to Skype. If you have more than one account, you can be signed in to Skype but not with the correct account where your contacts and Skype Credit are stored.

    Someone else may have used your device to sign in to their Microsoft account.
    Someone like a friend, family member, child, or spouse may have used your device to sign in to their account and forgotten to sign back out.


    You can contact chat support to help you find out whats the status of your account.

    I recommend you contact chat support and choose Technical problem so an agent can help diagnose whats the problem with your account:

    https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/skype?langu...


    Select Account & sign-in
    Select Can not access paid services OR Account is blocked
    Select Technical Problems
    Select Download & install
    Click Next

    You should now have access to chat support
