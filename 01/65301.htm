Local Area Connection Status - Media State: Enabled/Disabled - Microsoft Community

jpdesnews say:

Local Area Connection Status - Media State: Enabled/Disabled

by JPD of JPD.Enterprises

______________________________________________________________________

The Bing Search:

("Local Area Connection Status") AND ("Media State:") AND (Enabled OR Disabled)

The Question:

What does "Media State: {Enabled or Disabled} on the Microsoft Windows 7 "Local Area Connection Status" {applet/dialog/property-sheet} indicate?--What does it mean (e.g., about our computer hardware and software, and about our {intranet or internet} connectivity)? 

The Simple Answer:

If your Local Area Connection Status - Media State is 'Enabled' then your computer has an unbroken physical (wired) connection to your network provider [e.g., to your intranet {Local Area Network (LAN)}, or to the Internet {World-Wide-Web (WWW)}]; if it indicates 'Disabled' then your computer's Network Interface Card (NIC) has no connection to any network.  [I don't know if this applies to 'Wireless' Networking!]

The Complex Answers (Referenced):

(1) "This [the Microsoft Windows "Local Area Connection Status" {applet/dialog/property-sheet}] shows you whether you have connection to the IPv4 Internet and/or the IPv6 Internet, and whether the media is enabled (network carrier sense is active).  It also shows how long your connection has been active, and the connection speed (e.g. 1.0 Gbps).  It also shows the number of bytes sent and received on this interface."--(See: "Network Configuration in Windows 7" by Lawrence Hughes--Sixscape Communications @<http://www.sixscape.com/joomla/sixscape/index.php/ipv6-training-certification/ipv6-forum-official-certification/ipv6-forum-network-engineer-silver/network-engineer-silver-manual-network-configuration-on-various-platforms/network-configuration-in-windows-7>.)
