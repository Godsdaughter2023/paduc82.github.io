cbarnhorst say:

Just install it.  No product key required.  The requirement to run Windows 7 or 8.1 only applies to the initial upgrade (the entitlement upgrade) which verifies your eligibilty for the free copy.  But you aren't upgradeing.  You are clean installing.

After the entitlement upgrade the computer is a registered Windows 10 device and your Windows 10 Pro activation is permanent (unless you replace the motherboard).  The activation is stored in the cloud, not on the hard drive, hence no need for a product key.

The business about upgrades to Windows 7 and 8 only applies to getting the Get Windows 10 icon to show up on the taskbar, since the GWX program and its icon are at the end of the upgrade chain.  The GWX program pertains to the entitlement upgrade only.  It no longer applies to you because you have done the entitlement upgrade.

You are free to clean install as much as you want and no product key is needed.
