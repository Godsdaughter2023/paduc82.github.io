frederik long say: I can't quite follow your train of thought but here are a few thoughts of my own:
  • I see no point in burning system images to DVDs. There are three reasons:
    - It takes too many disks.
    - You cannot re-use the disks.
    - Burnt DVDs are far less reliable than hard disks.
  • Of every machine I look after I maintain three images on an external hard disk:
    - The "Factory" image (which allows me to go back to square one)
    - The most recent image (which I renew once every few months)
    - The image before the most recent image (which lets me go back in case the most recent image turns out to be flawed.
  • In view of the somewhat unreliable performance of the native image creation facility I use a third party product. It's functionality is far superior and it compresses its image files by around 50%. The product lets me burn a "Rescue CD" which allows me to restore an image to a blank disk.
The above measures let me recover quickly from any disaster, regardless what it is.
