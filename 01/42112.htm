The 'burn to DVD' option only appears if your computer has Windows DVD Maker loaded.  That extra program was present on versions of Windows Vista and Windows 7, but has been discontinued.  I'm assuming you are on Windows 8 or 8.1 and that would explain why you don't see the option.

The video DVD standard (maximum resolution is 720 x 480, SD) is rather outdated at this point, if you ask me, which may explain why Microsoft dropped support for it.  If you still need a shiny disc to hold your movie, you might want to look into 3rd-party applications for burning video DVDs (DVD Flick, DVD Styler, Freemake Video Converter, etc.).  

Or you may want to consider solutions that will save your movie so it is playable on a modern Blu-ray player instead.  In fact, you can directly burn an .mp4 file onto a DVD disc and it should play in most Blu-ray players, and if the original source is in HD, it will playback in HD. The only thing you won't have are any menus.
