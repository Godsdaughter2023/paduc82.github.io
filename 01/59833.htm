windows should remember the location of the app that last time it was closed and open it in the same place, are you saying this is not happening?

some other questions that may help to identify the problem
are you running in tablet mode?
are the apps opened in full screen?

you haven't said how your monitors are connected, I would guess that the M422i-B1 (TV screen) is connected to HDMI and that the Acer LCD Monitor P201W is connected to DVI.
the reason I ask is that windows assigns the identification numbers by port rank, HDMI outranks DVI so a monitor connected to HDMI will be 1 and monitor connected to DVI will get a 2, if you unplug the HDMI the monitor connected to DVI becomes 1, regardless of which is marked as the primary monitor.
there are applications that will label them the other way round (my intel HD graphics control panel does) but it has no impact on the primary monitor or where applications are opened, However windows does get it wrong in the projection flyout (winkey+p) where the primary monitor is listed as second screen (?!?!) and pc screen is monitor 1 which is not the primary monitor.

if you want an app to open on the primary monitor, reduce it to window (ie not full screen) move it to the primary monitor, then close it. next time you open it, it should open on the primary monitor.
the easy way to move a lot of open windows is to temporarily stop extending the desktop - winkey+p choose second monitor only (so that it displays on monitor 2, the primary monitor) when you extend the desktop again all the currently open apps will stay on the primary monitor

hope that helps, or at least doesn't make it more confusing, please let me know if I have misunderstood.
