a. user say:

If you have the Professional edition of Windows 10, you can sign in to an Administrator account and choose which update branch to join and how long to defer feature updates (i.e., new versions of Windows 10) and quality updates (bug fixes and security updates.)

While you are deferring updates, they will not download to your computer. After the deferral period is over, an update can download at any time and install itself. This is done 'in the background' so as not to interfere with your work.

Updates do often require a restart, however your computer won't restart during the Active Hours that you choose.

If Windows 10 notices that you're still using your computer when Active Hours have expired, and it wants to re-start your computer, you'll be prompted and you can tell Windows 10 to either go right ahead or you can delay the restart to a time when you won't be actively using the computer.

This system has worked for me since the very beginning of Windows 10.

On the other hand, if you have the Home edition of Windows 10 you are unable to defer any updates.

  1. Open the Task manager.  Here's a tip:  Press Ctrl + Shift + Esc.

  3. Make sure you have a check mark beside "Create this task with administrative privileges".
