Having 2 display ports doesn't automatically mean your adapter can support 2 monitors simultaneously. All it means for sure is that you can connect a single monitor using either the VGA or the DVI connector. Both ports will initialize during power-up so that doesn't mean anything. The DVI port should work if it is the only one with a monitor connected to it. 


Check you computer specifications to confirm that the on-board video adapter supports dual monitors. Usually they don't. Most add-in video cards do support dual monitors these days. Graphics performance is usually much better with add-in cards as well.

a. user say:

Hi,

 

Thank you for the update.

 

The transfer speed of the Kingston V300 SSD can be measure in two modes, as mentioned below.

  •  Compressible Data Transfer for 240 GB SSD is 450MB/s Read and 450MB/s Write
  • Incompressible Data Transfer for 240GB SSD is 191MB/s Read and 142MB/s Write.

Note:

Compressible data is nothing but your music, video, picture, etc. files.

Incompressible data is nothing but boot drive, programs, executable files.

 

The HD tune program which shows you the average transfer rate of 200 might be Incompressible Data Transfer speed, also which is not accurate. The MSI 970 gaming mother board has only SATA 3 port and if you connect SATA 3 cable from the SSD to the mother board it should show SATA 3 but it show SATA 2, which might be the issue with HD tune software.

 

I suggest you to install any other program to measure the transfer rate & SATA drive status, or you may contact HD tune support.
