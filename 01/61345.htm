Hi there. Just dropping in and putting in my two cents on a couple of suggestions for the Xbox One, as suggested to do so by the Xbox Support twitter account (https://twitter.com/XboxSupport1/status/406155740831703040)

 

Firstly - as we currently have no way to play music off a USB stick or drive, it would be great if it were possible to have some way to play music in the background whilst playing games. At the moment, all I've managed to find is that we can snap Xbox Music, but this means we lose a chunk of screen real estate and the games run in a letterbox. I was amazed at the catalogue of music on the Xbox Music app (it appears to have a LOT of the obscure artists I listen to that a lot of other streaming services don't, so well done there), but to be honest, gameplay is probably the only time I'm going to use it - so without being able to minimize it to the background I honestly can't warrant the subscription, and I imagine that a lot of other people would be in a similar boat. This is of course all a moot point if you are intending on allowing us to play music in the background from USB devices once USB support is added, but even still, for people who like streaming music and so on, it seems like it would be a good idea to implement.

 

Second - similar suggestion, but this time surrounding the Upload Studio app. From what I can tell, clips stop uploading if you don't have the app snapped (mine certainly have failed to successfully upload when I've not had the app either open fullscreen, or snapped to the side). Again - it's great that we can do this in the first place, but I have a pretty bad upload speed (about 1.0 mbit/sec at the most), and mine is actually a LOT better than most of my friends here (Australia), so clips can take sometimes up to an hour to upload! At this stage, I basically walk away from my Xbox One and go use my pc... same deal - it would be a great improvement, in my opinion, if we could minimize and upload in the background whilst playing. Obviously it could cause issues with multiplayer gaming, but given that people can still do it in a snapped mode, I assume this has either been deemed acceptable or dealt with (I've not actually tried to play multiplayer whilst uploading).

 

Third - Give us a way to switch off in-game voice commands, without disabling the system ones. I ---LOVE--- being able to say Xbox go to Skype, Xbox Snap Friends, etc. But so far, of the four games I have played, three have voice commands, and two of them (Dead Rising 3 and Need for Speed: Rivals) are VERY touchy. In DR3, the game interprets anything from me laughing to random conversation as me saying "Over Here", and Need for Speed is, quite frankly, awfully incorrect. I think DR3 has a voice commands off option, but so far I have not been able to find one in Need for Speed. I often talk to friends via party chat, and now Skype, whilst playing... and it's incredibly annoying to have the commands go off when I say "windows live" in conversation, and the game interprets it as me saying "leave" instead of live, quitting the race I am in. Even if we just had to say "game" or something before the command, much like the requisite of "Xbox" before system commands, that would fix it a lot.

 

Fourth - More control over notifications!!! I want to know when my friends come online. Xbox 360 had this, so why doesn't the Xbox One? I want to know when people talk to me on Skype - but I don't need a notification for every single message they send - ideally I would just get one for the first message, and none for any subsequent messages until after I read the first one. Even if we could just disable skype notifications, whilst keeping the others, or just have it tell us when our friends come online on skype, but not message notifications, that would be good. Or even an option to make the notification smaller, or change the location. Any of these options would be great. As it is, the notification system is a little annoying - I often stop playing a game to see 99+ notifications, and I only have a small group of Xbox Live and Skype friends... so I can't even imagine what it would be like for people with larger groups.

 

Finally - A way to get the game clips off our Xbox without uploading, then downloading. The Game DVR feature is spectacular. I run a gaming website (http://shoddypixels.com for anyone interested) and I am already starting to use clips for some video reviews and so on. The footage is spectacular and it all works great. But it's very irritating that, in orderto access it, I need to save the footage, then upload it to SkyDrive, then download it on my PC, then edit it and upload it again. Again - this might all change once we have access to USB drives, but even if there were a way to upload it to our pc's via our home networks or whatever, it would be amazing.

 

Anyway, overall, the console is amazing. Loving it so far, and it's a huge leap from the last generation of consoles. I was not expecting the voice commands to be such a big deal, but they are truly something special. Hopefully some, or all, of these suggestions were already on their way to being done, but if not... please give me free games to say thankyou XD

chocoheretohelp say: I had this problem, but my dad taught me how to fix it. (HP Envy 15X2)

 

1. Go to Command Prompt and type 'devmgmt.msc'

 

2. When you are on device manager,  and click 'Network Adapters'

 two times

 

3.Right click your adapters and then it should say "Uninstall, Disable, Update Driver Software"

click on Unistall.

 

4.Repeat to all other adapters, (adapters only)

 

5.On the top bar, click the 'Action' button and you will be given choices; now click "Scan for hardware changes."

 

6. Look at your taskbar, and Voila!
