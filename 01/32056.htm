a. user say:

A simple an easy-to-manage way of adding Startup items is by putting shortcuts to your programs into the Start Menu's "Start-up" folder. This has been around since Windows 98, or maybe even earlier, but due to the recent changes in the way Start works it's a bit more difficult to find. You can add a start-up item as follows:

  1. Press Win+R on your keyboard, or right-click Start and choose "Run". This will bring up the "Run" dialogue.
  2. Type in, or paste, the following: shell:startup. This will open your start-up directory in File Explorer.
  3. Right-click an empty space, and choose "New".
  4. From the drop-down menu, choose "Shortcut".
  5. Click "Browse", and locate your program's .exe file.
  6. Click "Next".
  7. Type in the name of the shortcut (optional, but you may want to do this if you plan to add many items for better management).
  8. Click "Finish".
  9. Verify that the program has been added to start-up from the Task Manager's Start-up tab.
