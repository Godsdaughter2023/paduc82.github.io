Method 1: Perform a clean boot to check if any third party application is causing this issue.

 

Steps to perform a clean boot on the computer that is running Windows Vista:

 

1. Click Start , type msconfig in the Start Search box , and then press ENTER .

  If you are prompted for an administrator password or for a confirmation , type the password, or click Continue .

2. On the General tab, click Selective Startup .

3. Under Selective Startup , click to clear the Load Startup Items check box.

4. Click the Services tab , click to select the Hide All Microsoft Services check box, and then click Disable All .

5. Click OK .

6. When you are prompted, click Restart .

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/929135

Method 2: Perform an SFC scan and try if this can resolve this issue

This method takes a while (between 20-30 minutes or less depending on the number of files you have on your computer) but is a lot more thorough and will most probably fix most of the issues you are having with WMP11 as it not only re-registers the components but it also replaces any corrupt or missing files on your computer from your VISTA INSTALL DVD.

 

Steps to perform sfc scan:

 

1. Click Start, click All Programs, click Accessories, right-click Command Prompt, and select Run as Administrator.

2. Click Continue or supply Administrator credentials if prompted.

3. In the Command Prompt window type the following, and press Enter: sfc /scannow

4. You should see the following on-screen messages:

 

Beginning the system scan. This process will take some time.

Beginning verification phase of system scan

Verification % complete

 

5. If any files require replacing SFC will replace them. You may be asked to insert your Vista DVD for this process to continue

6. Once the scan has completed you will receive an onscreen message resembling one of the following:

… found no integrity violations

… found corruption but repaired it

… found corruption that it could not repair

7. After the scan has completed, Close the command prompt window restart the computer and check.

Is your console enrolled in the Xbox Insider Preview Program? Since they are fiddling around with Atmos sound issues currently, something might also interfere with the WiFi software, when you use Atmos. 

If your console is on the Insider Preview, please file feedback about that issue through the Feedback function when you hold the Xbox button on the controller (in the menu as if you were turning off the console).

If the console is on the standard system software, the only thing I could think of, would be that you are getting a ground loop, where your soundbar is connected to a different wall outlet than your console, which causes a potential difference on the ground (on analogue speaker systems you would then get a 50/60 Hz humming). If the console and sound bar are on different wall outlets, try to connect both on a power strip on the same outlet.
