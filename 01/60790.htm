1.

Open Display in Control Panel.

2.

On the Settings tab, under Screen resolution, drag the slider, and then click Apply.

3.

4.

Note

•

To open Display, click Start, click Control Panel, click Appearance and Themes, and then click Display.

•

A higher screen resolution reduces the size of items on your screen and increases the relative space on your desktop

•

To switch, right-click the game or program (on the desktop or on the Start menu), and then click Properties. Your monitor and video adapter determine how much you can change your screen resolution. You may not be able to increase the resolution above a certain level.

Changes to screen resolution affect all users that log on to the computer.

considerate_guy say:

Movie Maker doesn't restrict what you put in your movie.  While it won't accept files protected by DRM (digital rights management), it can't prevent you from using items that you don't own the copyrights too.  That is your responsibility.

 

It is a violation of copyrights to use items that you do not own the rights to.  For items like photos, videos and music, you need to verify that they were all created by you (you own the copyrights), or that you have rights to use them (say by paying rebroadcast rights), or that someone has granted you rights and you have their express (written) permission.

 

While it may seem "fair use" to use a song that you might have purchased on CD or via download, you don't have those rights.  You have the right to use it personally, but once you put it on a site like Facebook, you need to have rights to broadcast that song and that hasn't been granted to you.  So you are violating the artist's copyrights and it is within Facebook's rights to remove the offending video.

 

If you have further questions about the removal, you should contact Facebook.  But basically, you need to NOT use music belonging to someone else unless you have their permission.  You might want to look into sites like Free Music Archive where those specific artists grant you the ability to use their songs if you give them credit.
