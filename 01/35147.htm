petercon [msft] say:

Hi, M. Amadeo.

The DaunPenh font is available for Windows 10 as an optional feature. The font was designed primarily for the Khmer language, though it also has Latin letters and can be used for English or other languages. Many international fonts that were always installed on every Windows system in the past have been moved into optional features in Windows 10 so that the fonts are not taking up resources for users that don't actually require the fonts.

If you want to create or edit documents in Cambodian, then the best solution for you would be to add Khmer into your language settings. This will automatically install the optional font feature. But if you don't actually use Khmer and don't want to change your language settings, you can also install the optional feature manually.

To add the Khmer language to your language profile:

  • In Settings, click Time & language.
  • Click Region & language.
  • If Khmer is not included in your languages, click the "+" icon next to Add a language.
  • Scroll to find Khmer , then click on it to add it to your language list.

Once you have added Khmer to your language list, then the optional Khmer font feature and other optional features for Khmer language support will be installed, provided you are online. This should only take a few minutes.

To manually add the optional Khmer fonts feature:

  • In Settings, click System.
  • Click Apps & features.
  • Click on the link, Manage optional features.
  • If "Khmer Supplemental Fonts" is not listed among the installed features, click on the "+" icon next to Add a feature.
  • Scroll to find "Khmer Supplemental Fonts". Click on that item, then click on Install.
  • Click on the back arrow in the upper corner of the window.

You should see the Khmer feature in the list as installed or in the process of being installed.
