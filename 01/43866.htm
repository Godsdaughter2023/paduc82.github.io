If the issue persists and is in reference to some other program then you may try the below provided steps and check if these help -

Method 1: Remove any in-progress installations and check.

REGISTRY EDIT DISCLAIMER:
Important:  This section, method, or task contains steps that tell you how to modify the registry. However, serious problems might occur if you modify the registry incorrectly. How to back up and restore the registry in Windows: http://support.microsoft.com/kb/322756

1. Choose Start and then click Run, and type regedit in the Open text box; click OK.
2. Open the key
HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Installer\Inprogress.

Method 2: Unregister and then reregister the Windows Installer Service.

1. Choose Start > Run, and type msiexec /unreg in the Open text box; click OK. 
2. Choose Start > Run, and type msiexec /regserver in the Open text box; click OK

    sandm1tch say:

    If its an app like netflix, that you're talking about, then those apps have a dynamic quality mechanism. This works in tangent with the higher quality stream detection if your network speed is fast enough, but this has to do with what you set the Aspect Ratio in a streaming app to. If you chose "Native" then when the movie/show starts out just as you opened it up, while the app is detecting your network speed, it won't scale the image to the edges of your display until it reach's the native quality of the movie/show. Starts out at about 240p->360p->480p(SD)->720p(HD).

    -

    So in summary, choose any other aspect ratio in the App if you don't want that automatic aspect ratio. But as for the Xbox 360's system settings, there are no options that attempt automatic aspect ratio to your display and I haven't seen a TV that does this on the fly either. But if it isn't something I have explained, maybe its on your TV?
