Theory of Escalating Commitment - Microsoft Community

eagle22us say:

Theory of Escalating Commitment

Well this one is going to be a little highbrow (and a wall of text) for some of the younger folk but has anyone ever heard of the "Theory of Escalating Commitment" it was started by Barry M. Staw in I believe 1976. The XBox One is quickly becoming a great case study on this theory gone wrong. 

In a nutshell the Theory of Escalating Commitment is based around the idea that an individual or company will keep putting/committing resources to an idea that in the face of everything is the wrong/immoral course of action

As an example, several years ago Boeing was approached by the military and asked if they would be able to make a break for an aircraft thin enough to lessen the weight of the aircraft yet still have enough stopping power to safely land the vehicle. Boeing of course said they could and placed their top man on the job. This individual worked for the company 20+ years and drafted a break that exceeded any expectations set by the military. At the time a new employee, a recent graduate from MIT no less, reviews the specs and brought it up to management that there was no possible way for the break to function correctly without overheating and failing. The manger that had placed the head engineer on the job ignored the new worker believing that he made the right decision to move development further on the project.

Then the Prototypes started to be produced and tested, and sure enough just as the new employee stated upon lab tests the break would overheat and fail. Now one would think that at this time they would realize the mistake and start over but at the time the company and manager had too much invested and decided to begin modifying the tests to get the break to pass. For example they began air-conditioning the testing room etc. to allow for cooler than normal temperatures. Eventually they “passed” the break and shipped it out to the military. Sure enough the breaks started to fail, Boeing started to hold meetings to come up with reasons/excuses to explain what has happened etc.

The rest well as they say is history. This is a bit of an extreme example of the theory but kind of applies here. Microsoft had to have done market testing before revealing the new console (I hope to God they did) and if anyone had half a brain they had the reaction to the DRM/Used Game issue that a lot of people, including myself, have had to about the machine

My best guess they ignored this for whatever reason and pressed forward thus alienating a large amount of their built in fan base. This commitment to an obviously poor choice of action/policy is not only a clear example of the above mentioned theory but more than likely the future of the Xboxs undoing if not corrected in a timely manner.  

I am a huge Xbox360 fan and have had  Xbox Live since launch but if things stay pat it’s more than likely going to be the Playstation 4 route for me.
