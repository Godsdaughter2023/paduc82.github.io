a. user say:

In a hard drive partitioned with a master boot record there can be a maximum of 4 primary partitions, because there's only enough space in the MBR code to describe 4 primary partitions.

The way to overcome this is to create 3 primary partitions and an extended partition. The extended partition can be divided into logical drives. This isn't the greatest way to partition a hard drive, but it works.

You said that you only wanted to image the C: drive, but it looks like you imaged your entire hard drive. Had you only imaged the C: drive, that's all you would have on your new, larger hard drive.

The easiest way to take advantage of the extra 300 GB of unallocated space is to add it to your C: drive or your D: drive, and the safest way to do that is with third-party partitioning software.

Do not move or change the 200 MB system drive or the OEM partition.

If you still have the original hard drive there are several ways to make use of it. You could store your data there (documents, pictures, etc.) to free up even more space on the new, larger hard drive, or you can put it inside an enclosure and make it into an external hard drive and a great destination for your backups.
