Downloaded iphone won't fill screen - Microsoft Community

tmball2 say:

Downloaded iphone won't fill screen

I've downloaded video from my iphone and it it will fill the entire computer screen when played but it was rotated wrong.  I used movie maker to rotate the video but now when I play the video it only takes up the middle 1/3 of the screen.  How do I get it to fill the entire screen?
considerate_guy say:

You can't because of the way aspect ratios work.  In general terms, you have a horizontal rectangle that represents your project.  The size of the rectangle matches that of TVs, monitors and other playback devices.  The two choices for output are 16:9 (widescreen) and 4:3 (fullscreen).

 

When you took video on your iPhone, you took it in a vertical orientation (taller than it is wide).  To make it fit into a horizontal rectangle, after the video is rotated, it ends up with blank space on either side.  While I suppose Movie Maker could stretch your video to make it a wide rectangle, I don't think you want everyone in your video to look short and fat.  Hence, Movie Maker does the "right thing" and keeps it in the vertical aspect ratio and the empty space is blank.

 

There is no option in Movie Maker to save in a vertical aspect ratio.  That's probably because all TVs and monitors are horizontal, online sources (YouTube and Vimeo) have horizontal video, etc.  The obvious workaround is to film using your iPhone turned horizontally since that is what most online and TV sources will expect.  Either that or live with the black space on either side.  The other option would be to look for software that can save in a vertical orientation.

Attempting to pin an installer to the taskbar may fail and leave a broken pinned item that doesn't show up on the taskbar. After this happens you may notice that some items pinned to the taskbar don't rearrange in the way they expect. When moving an item from left to right, the item will end up one or more positions to the left of where you intended to move it. You can work around this by moving items from right to left to achieve the desired order. You can also fix the issue by removing any broken pinned items with the steps under using the following steps:

 

1.       1.       Browse to the following folder in your user folder under C:\Users

AppData\Microsoft\Internet Explorer\Quick Launch\User Pinned\Taskbar. Note that "User Pinned" is a hidden folder.

Note: You may need to go to AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Internet Explorer\Quick Launch\User Pinned\Taskbar

2.       2.       Delete any shortcuts in this directory that aren't represented on the taskbar.

3.       3.       Click Start

4.       4.       In the Search programs and files box, type regedit

5.       5.       In the Programs list, click regedit.exe

 If you are prompted for an administrator password or confirmation, type the password or click Continue.

6.       6.       Browse to and select the following registry key

HKEY_CURRENT_USER \Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Explorer\

7.       7.       Right-click Taskband!FavoritesRemovedChanges and click Modify

8.       8.       Change the Value Data to 0

9.       9.       Browse to and select the following registry key

HKLM\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Explorer\StartPage\

10.   10.   Right-click FavoritesRemovedChanges and click Modify

11.   11.   Change the Value Data make sure the value is greater than 0

12.   12.   Log off and log back in, pinned items should be rearrange normally again.
