a. user say:

Hi craigreason,            

Welcome to the Skype Community Forum.

If you’re not receiving calls to your Skype Number, or if your Skype Number is ringing busy, you probably need to adjust your privacy settings to allow incoming calls. Please follow these steps:

Skype for Windows

  1. In Skype select Tools > Options
  2. Click the Privacy tab, then under Allow calls to my Skype Number(s) from… select anyone, and click Save.

If the privacy settings were already configured like this, complete the two steps below to refresh the settings. 

  1. Go back to the Privacy tab by selecting Tools > Options and change the privacy settings to allow calls to Skype Number(s) from phone numbers in my Contact list only and click Save.
  2. Wait approximately 5 minutes then open the privacy settings once more and change the setting back to anyone and Save.

Skype for Mac

  1. In Skype, go to Skype > Preferences > Privacy
  2. Under Receive calls to my Skype Number from, select Anyone.

If the privacy settings were already configured like this, complete the two steps below to refresh the settings.

  1. Go back to the Privacy tab by selecting Skype > Preferences > Privacy and change the privacy settings to allow calls to your Skype Number from Only people in my contact list
  2. Wait approximately 5 minutes then open the privacy settings once more and change the setting back to Anyone.

These steps are not applicable in mobile devices. Also, you must also ensure that you are using the latest version of Skype. Please let us know if you have questions.

Hello Peltka,

Thank you for the update on the issue and sorry for delayed response.

If your ASUS DRW is showing in Windows Explorer and device manager, it means it is connected to your computer. You have also mentioned that it is showing as removable device. If you want it be shown like other HDD then you may need to assign a drive letter to that device and see if it helps you to fix the issue. For assigning a drive letter you may refer to the steps provided below.

Ideally, if the device is detected in Devices & Printers Window, it will automatically be displayed in the MY PC page. If it is not showing there or you are unable to access it then there might be a chance that the drive letter is not assigned to it. To check if drive letter is assigned for the device, please open Disk Management. Also confirm if the device is detected in Disk Management Window.

If it is not assigned please assign any drive letter to it, otherwise try to change the drive letter and it can be changed by going to Disk Management and requesting the change manually. For this, please follow the instructions below:

  1. While on the Desktop, move the mouse pointer to the bottom-left of the screen, and right-click on top of the Start icon.

  2. From the menu, click on Disk Management.

  3. Disk Management will open up and display all hard drives that are connected to the system. Focus on the bottom-half of the screen, and locate the desired drive to change the drive letter to.

  4. Once the drive is found, right click on top of it. From the menu, click on Change Drive Letter and Paths....

  5. Select the current drive letter and then click on Change.

  6. From the new window, click on the drive letter to the right side to display a drop-down that shows the available drive letters. Select the desired drive letter and click on OK.

  7. Disk Management will display a warning indicating that some programs may not run correctly if they are reading information from that hard drive. If that's not the case, then click on Yes to continue.

  8. When done, Disk Management will display the drive with the new drive letter attached to it.

Hope this would help. Do keep us updated about the issue and we will be happy to assist you further if required.
