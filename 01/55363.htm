However, you can disable USB ports by through Settings in Windows 10. Kindly check with the steps listed below and see if it works:

Method 1: Enable power saving.

We can turn on power saving on USB ports which intact turns of the device for power management. To do that,

  • Right click on the Start menu and choose Device Manager.

  • Navigate to Universal Serial Bus controllers and expand it.

  • Right click on the USB Root Hub driver and click on Properties.

  • In the Properties tab, choose Power Management tab from the top menu and put a check mark on Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power.

  • Repeat the above step for all the USB Root Hubs on the computer.

Method 2: Update Chipset drivers.

I would also recommend you to update your Chipset drivers in Device Manager. To do that,

  • Go to Device Manger from the Start menu and click on it.

  • Navigate to Chipset drivers and right click on it to choose Update driver.

  • Select Search automatically for updated driver software and proceed with the on-screen prompts.

You may also refer to an article on how to Update drivers in Windows 10.  

If the above suggestions doesn't work, you may follow method 3.

Method 3: Registry Settings.

We can also disable USB ports from registry settings. To do that,

  • Press Windows + R keys and type regedit.exe and put a check mark on Run as administrator option.

  • Navigate to the following key:

    HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Services\USBSTOR

  • On the right pane, double click on Start key and change the Value data to 3.

  • Once after making the necessary changes, click on OK and close the registry.
