yoan schinck say:

Hi YT :)

You're right, this issue is caused by the fact that your hard drive is partitionned using the MBR partition table style, which only allows you to create partitions of a size of 2TB maximum each. In order to "break" that limit, you have to convert your hard drive to a GPT partition table, which doesn't have that limit (well, the limit it at 128TB so here it doesn't matter a lot). You can convert your hard drive from MBR to GPT without any data loss. Follow the instructions in the tutorial below:

http://www.thewindowsclub.com/convert-mbr-to-gpt-disk

Method 2 and 3 explains how to do the conversion without data loss, Method 3 being the easiest.

Let me know if you have any questions.
