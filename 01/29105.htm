vladimir lashchev say:

I found the solution myself :-)

In short: stop insider builds, fix feedback setting and start getting insider builds again...

Here are the steps:

  1. Settings > Update & Security > Windows Update > Advanced options > Stop Insider builds
  2. Settings > Privacy > Feedback & diagnostics > Diagnostics and usage data > Send your device data to Microsoft = change to "Full (Recommended)"
  3. Settings > Update & Security > Windows Update > Advanced options > Get Insider builds > Get started...

My setup is still activated after these steps. It was my biggest worry with this approach with all the activation changes done in preview build 10240.

Now you should be able to start Feedback App and share your pain and/or cheers!

anil kumar b say:

Hi,

 

Thank you for posting your query in Microsoft Community Forums.

 

Based on the issue description I understand that you are unable to delete backup data from the external hardware on Windows XP computer.

 

I would really appreciate if you can help me with the following information.

 

1.      Did you make any changes on the computer prior to this issue?

2.      What happens when you try to open disk management? Please mention the exact error message/code?

 

I would suggest you to try the steps and check if you are able to open disk manager.

 

Step 1

Try following and revert back if the problem still persists:

 

a)      Remove the external hard disk from the machine.

b)      Restart the machine

c)      Click on Start and click on Run.

d)      Type compmgmt.msc and press enter

e)      In the left pane select storage->disk management.

f)       If it works well, try connecting the external hard drive and follow the steps.

 

Step 2

You can try these steps to delete data from external hard drive.

 

a)      Connect your external hard drive to your computer or laptop.

b)       Open the drive on your computer by double clicking on the appropriate drive in the "My Computer" folder or selecting the "Open Folder to View Files" option from the Autorun utility.

c)      Browse through the files to find the ones you want to remove. To delete the files, right click the file or folder and click "Delete" from the drop-down menu. Click "Yes" to confirm the removal.

d)      You can also click on the file or folder icon and drag it to your desktop's Recycle Bin. Continue deleting files until you have removed all the unnecessary files from your external hard drive.

e)      Open the "My Computer" folder to locate the icon for your external hard drive. Right click on the icon and select "Format" from the drop-down menu to reformat the drive. This will erase all the data on the drive. This is a quicker option to delete files from your hard drive if you want to delete everything the drive contains.

 

Perform these steps and let us know the status. If you need further assistance, we are here to help you and guide you on the right direction.
