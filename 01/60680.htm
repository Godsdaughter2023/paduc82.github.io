frederik long say:

In a network you have a certain hierarchy of techniques. Here are the various levels in simplified form:

1. At the bottom there are the physical network connections. Your PCs must be connected either by CAT5 network cables or wirelessly, or a mixture of the two. So far we do not know what it is in your case.

2. At the next level is the IP addressing scheme. All members in a network must use addresses that are compatible with each other. The command
ipconfig.exe /all
tells you what addresses you have.

3. At the next level come he concepts of "client", "workgroup" or "homegroup", "shares".

Your report suggests that you have a problem at the first two levels. A good starting point would be to post details on Item 1 above, then go through the following steps:
1. Click Start, then type the three letters cmd into the Search box and press Enter.
2. Type this command:
   ipconfig /all > "%UserProfile%\Desktop\ip.txt" {Enter}
3. Open the file IP.txt on your desktop
4. Copy and paste the text into your response. Do this for two different PCs. Use flash disks to take the data from one PC to another.

If this puts you outside your comfort level then you should ask a computer-savvy friend to assist you.

lemp say:

Run TreeSize (as administrator) for the entire drive, not just for C:\Windows or C:\Users.

You're missing some potentially large folders:  C:\Program Files, C:\Program Files (x86), C:\System Volume Information, c:\pagefile.sys, and c:\hiberfil.sys

I suspect that you'll find that C:\System Volume Information is very large.  The primary user of space in SVI is System Restore.  I forget what the default is for Windows 7, but you shouldn't need more than about 10 GB (if that).

After you see how large the SVI folder is, do the following:

  1. Click the Start Orb, type System Restore and press Enter.
  2. On the "Restore system files and settings" window, click Next
  3. Click the box to "Show more restore points" and take a look at how many RPs you have and how far back in time they go.  As a practical matter, you don't want to restore to a point more than a few days ago.  The older the RP you try to use, the more likely that some intervening change will cause you trouble.

  1. Now close out of System Restore, click the Start Orb, right-click "Computer" and select "Properties.
  2. Click the link on the left for "System Protection"
  3. Once the "Protection settings" window has populated, select your C:\ drive and click "Configure"
  4. You can adjust the allocation for RPs by moving the slider.  As the allocated space fills up, SR deletes the oldest RPs to make room for newer ones.

If things are running well at the moment, you can use Disk Cleanup to remove all but the most recent RP and then see if the SVI folder starts to fill up rapidly. (Step 7 here: http://www.sevenforums.com/tutorials/818-disk-cleanup-open-use.html)

I haven't seen it lately, but from time to time I've seen reports of the SVI folder growing very large in a way that isn't caused by System Restore.  IIRC, the cause is usually some backup software or security software writing log files to the SVI folder.
