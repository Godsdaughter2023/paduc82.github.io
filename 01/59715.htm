But you do have 2 disk drives. One contains the C:,M:\ and the unlettered 25GB Partition (which I am dubious about). The other contains the remaining D:,P: and E:. Don't be confused by the fact you are seeing them through the C partition properties, the hardware tab always shows the complete drive set in the computer. If you right click on the unlettered drive in Disk Managers lower pane you could assign it a letter which would then allow you to see its contents. An unlettered drive is actually common in Windows 7, what I suspect is this was intended to hold the recovery image many manufacturers include on the disk to restore the computer back to factory state but it would appear to be empty although a very small file set may not register in terms of used space.

 

Personally I would back up any contents of the second physical disks partitions and delete them. Reassign the M: partition as D:\ and shift any optical drives up high such as X or Y. The unlettered drive is a bit of a pain but in the grand scheme 25GB could be ignored.

 

You would however need to be 100% sure there are no system files in any of the drives you change.
