reid_williams say:

Something tells me you don't understand the question. The difference between UHS-1 and UHS-2 or -3 is not the same thing at all as the difference between U1 & U3. A UHS-1 card reader only has one row of 8 connector pins and has a maximum transfer rate of 104 mb/s. A UHS-2 or -3 card reader has 2 rows of 8 pins per row and has a maximum transfer rate of 312 mb/s and 624 mb/s respectively. 

I don't know this for sure but I believe the Surface Pro 4 Micro SD card reader only has one row of pins which would imply that it is only capable of 104 mb/s maximum transfer speed. It will use the UHS-2 or -3 cards but you will not gain anything by spending the extra money for these cards because the bus for the card reader will be the limiting factor.

I say I don't know this for sure because I have asked Microsoft support team directly on the phone and I also corresponded with the support team directly online. On both occasions I actually got one tech. that said it did have a bus that utilized the UHS-3 interface and in the same conversation another tech. said that it did not have this capability. I also ask the same question at the local Microsoft store where I bought my Surface Pro 4 and no one there even knew the difference between the two interfaces. 

So there is no question that the Surface Pro 4 will use the UHS-2 and -3 card but rather is the card reader designed to take advantage of these cards? Or to cut to the chase does the card reader have 1 row of pins or 2? If you actually know the answer to this question please share that information, but please be sure you are correct because the difference in price for these cards is substantial.
