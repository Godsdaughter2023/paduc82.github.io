davem121 say:

Press and hold the power button to shut your laptop down . . .

Start your Laptop, just as the manufacturers logo disappears and Windows attempts to load (Spinning Dots), press and hold Power Button for 5 - 10 seconds to perform a Hard Shut Down

Do this twice

On the third start Windows will boot into the Recovery Environment and from there you can access System Repair, Safe Mode, Command Prompt . . . etc.

Go to Troubleshoot - Advanced Option - System Restore
Check to see if you have a restore point you can go back to

If you do not have a Restore Point, go to Troubleshoot - Advanced Option - Startup Settings and click Restart
Upon restart, press 4 to enter Safe Mode
Once in Safe Mode, backup your data if you do not already have a backup
Open Device Manager and check to make sure all your Drivers are up to date
Reboot to see if Windows will start Normally

If the above fails, go to Troubleshoot - Advanced Options - Reset this PC
Choose to keep your files and apps, just your files or to keep nothing
Then start the Reset

Windows 10 Taskbar - Bug? - Microsoft Community

rvastronomer say:

Windows 10 Taskbar - Bug?

There is a bug in how Windows 10 and applications deal with the Task Bar if the Task Bar is moved to the top of the screen.  Some (but not all) programs "hide" the (_ square (S) X) symbols under the Task Bar if the program is full screen (square) when the Task Bar is on the top.  

This happens on all of my Windows 10 devices (SP3, multiple standard desktops, etc.).  Quicken is one very negative example - the entire top of Quicken is hidden under the Task Bar.

I suspect the various developers never tested their products using the Task Bar in the top (or side) modes.  Most people use the Task Bar on the bottom of the screen - so it never interferes with the _ S X symbols.  The only work around appears to be to move the Task Bar back to the bottom so I can see the _ S X symbols.

So to reduce any troll comments, there are very good reasons to move the Task Bar to the top - especially when you use vertically stacked monitors, and the bottom of the bottom screen is masked by the keyboard, etc.
