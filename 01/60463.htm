How to post spoilers - Microsoft Community

aka scratch say:

How to post spoilers

There might come a time when you want to talk about something in a game that will contain spoilers.  You can do this on the forums and we ask that you indicate in your post a warning for those who might not want to see it.

 
Here's how you can do that and avoid being tarred and feathered by the rest of the community -

 
 If you create a new post first you should put the word Spoiler in it along with a brief description

  • for example  SPOILER - need help on Chapter 3  This way people who are on chapter 4 and above can check it out. You see the idea here.  You don't want to ruin anything but you don't want people to be afraid to click.

 

If you are creating a post within a discussion and your post might contain spoilers you can add a spoiler tag to avoid any unnecessary spoiling.

  • To do that you'll need to be in the Rich Formatting screen of creating your post.
  • Type out the body of you post as you normally would and then identify the part that contains the spoiler.
  • Add these tags before and after the spoiler and put them in bold 
  • Spoiler the spoiler starts here and contains anything considered to be a spoiler  /Spoiler - Highlight to read
  • Then you highlight the spoiler (minus the tags) and change the font color to white
  • The spoiler will look like this Spoiler the spoiler starts here and contains anything considered to be a spoiler  /Spoiler - Highlight to read

 

That's it and you're done!  The font color can be change at the top in the formatting menu.  Locate the  symbol and click it to be presented with a palatte of colors to choose from.

 

What is considered a Spoiler?

  We're not too worried about the device on a car or tractor trailer used to deflect wind and improve aerodynamics.  We are more concerned with anything that reveals a plot point or story line event that gives away the result or outcome of the game or a major point of the storyline.

 

When has a game been out long enough that I don't have to worry about doing all this?

Some would say that after a game has been out for a year or after the game has a sequel or when major points of a story have been talked about and covered by the media in a public manner.  Certainly, after these things have taken place the likely hood of being ran out of town for posting them goes down but it's a good practice to always use the spoiler tags since there could be people who still haven't played the game.

 

Thanks!

ramayon ningshen say: To convert an existing FAT or FAT32 volume to NTFS, follow these steps:
  1. Click Start, point to All Programs, point to Accessories, and then click Command Prompt.
  2. At the command prompt, type the following, where drive letter is the drive that you want to convert:
    convert drive letter: /fs:ntfs
    For example, type the following command to convert drive E to NTFS:
    convert e: /fs:ntfs
    Note If the operating system is on the drive that you are converting, you will be prompted to schedule the task when you restart the computer because the conversion cannot be completed while the operating system is running. When you are prompted, click YES.
  3. When you receive the following message at the command prompt, type the volume label of the drive that you are converting, and then press ENTER:
    The type of the file system is FAT.
    Enter the current volume label for drive drive letter
  4. When the conversion to NTFS is complete, you receive the following message at the command prompt:
    Conversion complete
  5. Quit the command prompt.

Make sure there is no interuption during the covertion like power outage and so on.

Once the convertion is done restart the computer and start the Backup
