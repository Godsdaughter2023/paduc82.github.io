My suggestion would be to enable Admin rights on the local account as follows.

robert aldwinckle on forums say:

I get the "black screen" problem. E.g. the screen turns black but I can see the mouse moving and do CTRL-ALT-DEL.

I call that the "blank screen problem".   And often it means that the video system has inexplicably decided to turn a non-existent second monitor into the primary of an Extended display mode.  Then a solution which has worked for some is to use keyboard commands and act accordingly, even though they cannot see the result of those commands.  E.g. your Ctrl-Alt-Delete could be an example; you may have just put that prompt up and just need to press End and Enter to start Task Manager.  So then you could continue using it blindly or press Alt-Space, M, CursorRight  and move it to the monitor where you could see it.  Similarly if you thought you were on the Lock Screen you could do something for it to get into your Login screen, do something there, and so on.  In fact, early on when this symptom was first seen users were getting very creative about driving through there blindly.  Some took a detour into the Accessibility menu and activated Narrator, so they could at least "hear" what they were doing.  For a long time my suggestion was to use this simple loop:  Shift+Tab, Shift+Space, wait (watching and listening for any changes from the Shift+Space); Repeat.  The desired goal of such a procedure was to end up at the Shut down menu, open it up, select the Restart item and action it while holding down a Shift- key.  Thus getting into the Advanced Options boot menu where hopefully things would "look" brighter.  <eg>   Fortunately, I have since discovered a much simpler procedure that is also available:  Win-P, CursorUp, Enter.  In the hypothesized scenario that would have the effect of changing from Extended to Duplicate which presumably would put your Login screen onto your real monitor.  A related workaround is attaching a real second monitor so you can see what is on it.

Good luck

Robert Aldwinckle
