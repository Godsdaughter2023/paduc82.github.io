I just bought a laptop with 128GB SSD and 1TB HDD. The OS is install on 128GB SSD and i had extend partition to it maximum. So can i extend my C partition to 1 TB HDD? (something like RAID 0, but my laptop lenovo ideapad 320s had no RAID in BIOS). 

So any solution can extend my partition to another hard drive in windows 10?

obsessive power say:

You can't extend these two drives to one partition.

As for Raid-0, even if you could do it, I wouldn't recommend it for the following reasons:

1.  The read/write speed of a RAID array would be limited to the maximum speed of the slowest drive in the array.  This would be your mechanical 1TB HDD, massively slowing down your laptops performance.

2. The total capacity of a RAID-0 array is based upon the drive in the array with the smallest capacity. So if you RAID-0 a 128GB drive and a 1TB drive, then your RAID array's total capacity (before formatting) will be 256GB (128 x 2). 

Just keep the 120GB for the O.S and a few small apps that you think might benefit from being on the SSD.  Install everything else on the 1TB drive.
