AAE files are not images (or videos).  See https://fileinfo.com/extension/aae

curioustechie say:

You have two options with monitors that do not support DisplayPort daisy-chaining.

1) Simply connect the second monitor to the DisplayPort connector on the Surface itself. One monitor connected to the Dock and the other connected to the Surface. Yep, not ideal and a bit clunky but it works. This is what I do.

2) Purchase one of many DisplayPort hubs available. This allows you to connect the hub to the Dock DisplayPort connector and then the two monitors connect to the hub. I tried one of these and was not happy (the monitors were not always recognized at bootup), your mileage may differ.
