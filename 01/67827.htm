Here are a couple of other settings that you can try:

First of all, some systems have a control for this in the system BIOS, (it's worth checking). Here's an example of the setting:

1. Restart the computer

3. Go to Power Management
4. Select Wireless Radio Control
5. Untick Control WLAN radio and WWAN radio

Secondly, a setting change in the Ethernet adaptor properties could/should work:

1. Go to network and sharing center in control panel.
2. Select change adapter settings
3. Right click on your Ethernet device and select properties on popup menu.
4. In properties menu, click configure button.
5. When configure menu, select advanced tab.
6. In advanced menu, under property list, look for "Priority & Vlan" and select it.
7. Look in value list to the right and select disable toggle.
8. Click Ok, on your way back out of menus and wait 15 seconds until system updates new value.

You have just disabled your Ethernet LAN priority so it should now function together with your WIFI.

Good luck!
