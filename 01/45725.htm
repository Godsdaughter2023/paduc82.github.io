vincent wansink say:

You do not need both a local and a Microsoft account.  You choose which you prefer.

If you use a local account, you will not be automatically logged into those services you mention.  You need to use a Microsoft account for that.

Not sure why you ran into the problems you did, but you definitely don't want to delete your Microsoft account, except maybe remove it from your PC.  So in that sense you're not deleting the actual account, just telling your PC to stop using it.

To do that you have to first switch to using a local account, then remove your Microsoft account.  Reboot, make sure all your Microsoft account data is gone (email, etc).  Then switch back to using your Microsoft account.  All this is done on the Accounts page of the Settings app.

'Workgroup' refers to a collection of computers, but it's only a name. You might use it for internal administration, but a workgroup is not something that's separately accessible as a unit, which is why you don't see your workgroup name in the networks list. That list is for networks

With that said, networks are divisible into subnetworks - subnets, for short - so if you re-made your workgroup into a subnet then it would show up in the list of networks.

Setting up a subnet is not that difficult but you'll have to spend some time learning how to do it, which means learning the basics of networking and the basics of IP addressing. It's beyond the scope of this forum, which is for Windows.

However, none of that is necessary if all you want to do is share files. That's easily enough accomplished with Windows' own built-in file sharing capability.
