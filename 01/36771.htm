Hi Mom of teen,

There's a few things that could be wrong here:

1. If your child has an Administrator account on their machine, then they can bypass all the parental controls.  Take a look at the accounts on that PC and make sure they are a Standard User, not an Administrator, and that they have not created a different account for themselves to use without restrictions.

3. There is a discrepancy between the time used to enforce time limits, and the time that gets reported.  We enforce time limits based on how long their user profile is signed in to Windows, but the time reporting is based on how much time they spend interacting with the device.  So, if they log in and sit there doing nothing for 3 hours, it will say "1 min" but still lock them out.  Unfortunately, this just means that the time reported is almost always way too low, which can be confusing.
