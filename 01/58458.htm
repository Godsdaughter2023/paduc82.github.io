    Hi TomHathaway1,  

    Here's how to change the battery in Surface Pen. 

    Surface Pen with no clip

    • Pull the top end straight out from the bottom.
    • Replace the AAAA battery with the positive (+) end of the battery pointing toward the tail end of the Pen.
    • Align the flat sides of the Pen and push the two parts back together. 

    Surface Pen with single button on flat edge 

    • Turn the cap (the eraser end) counter-clockwise until you hear and feel it click into place (about 30 degrees). Gently pull the cap straight out from the body. 
    • Replace the AAAA battery with the positive (+) end of the battery pointing toward the tail end of the Pen. 
    • Line up the metal guide bump on the cap with the half-circle cutaway on the Pen body, and slide the cap back into place. 
    • Turn the cap a little bit to align the flat edges. 

    Surface Pen with two side buttons 

    • Unscrew the top of the Pen from the bottom.
    • Replace the AAAA battery with the positive (+) end of the battery pointing toward the writing tip. 
    • Screw the top back on.

    Here's how to check the battery life of Surface Pen using a Surface app. 

    • Tap the Windows logo. 
    • Type "Surface app"
    • Tap the Battery icon under Battery level for Microsoft accessories. You can see all connected Bluetooth accessories and it's battery life.

    Note: Only Microsoft Bluetooth accessories will be listed here. (Make sure that the Surface Pen is connected to a Surface device and the Surface device is up to date).

    Windows uses TLS (Transport Security Layer) to ensure a secure connection.  On some machines, we have found that these errors were caused by TLS being disabled.  Here is how to check if your TLS setting is disabled and causing these errors: 

    1. Click the Start button and click Settings.
    2. Click on Network and Internet.
    3. Select Wi-Fi in the left navigation pane, if you are not already on that page
    4. Scroll down and select on Internet Options.
    5. Click on the Advanced tab.
    6. Scroll down to the bottom of the list of options, to the Security section
    7. Ensure there is a check mark next to Use TLS 1.2.
    8. Click Apply and then OK.
    9. Re-open Windows Store and Cortana and check if the issue is resolved. A restart should not be required.
