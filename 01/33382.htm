If Only the Xbox fails to connect, then your Router, Modem or ISP are accidentally blocking Port 3074 traffic. 'Ports' are numbers encoded in each Internet data packet which tell the router what to do with your data. 1) turn off the Xbox and unplug the power supply cord from the back of the Xbox then

5) Wait 'til the light on the front of the modem/gateway which says BROADBAND, ONLINE, READY, CABLE or DSL is on solid (not flashing), then plug in the router. If you have a gateway instead of a modem and router or a modem with no router, then when that light says it's ready, skip to step 7.

I have no DVI input - Microsoft Community

elbertamaudchristensen say:

I have no DVI input

I have windows 7 .  When I turn computer on it says No DVI input.  What does this mean?

imran chand say:

Hi,

a. How long did you have this issue?

b. Did you make any changes on your computer?

The Digital Visual Interface (DVI) is a video interface standard designed to provide very high visual quality on digital display devices such as flat panel LCD computer displays and digital projectors.

If your monitor is telling you that there is no DVI input then either your DVI cable is unplugged or loosened from either the monitor or Video Card or you are using a different kind of interface and you need to tell your monitor from which interface to expect the signal.

1. Check your monitor cord, if it is loose, just plug it in tighter.

2. You may try removing the video card and see if you can boot using the original onboard graphics. If so we have just diagnosed a bad video card.

Since the PC is connected to the monitor, we can assume that it has two input ports.

The first is a standard VGA port (which you might have connected to) and the second is a DVI port.

If you are using VGA port, there will be a Menu button on the monitor and you'll need to look for the setting that defaults the input to VGA. (If you have the manual that will make it a bit easier). Then each time you turn the monitor on it should be in that mode.

If your graphics card is high quality and the monitor is too, then it may be worth buying a DVI cable (or finding the one that came with the monitor). Because DVI is digital and VGA is analogue and generally digital is better.

I hope the above information helps you.
