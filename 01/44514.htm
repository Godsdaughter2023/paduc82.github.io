gauss1 say:

Does DirectX utilize Cuda software?

I am trying to purchase a new laptop but I have a program on my old one which utilizes Cuda software in the video card.  I got the specs on this new laptop I might get, the HP Probook 6570b, and it lists Video as 512 MB ram Direct X 1, anyone out there help me decipher this jargon??  I was told don't get a new laptops unless it has Cuda software in the video card.  Thanks

bossdweeb say: Direct X does not utilise Cuda, it's the other way around.

"Cuda" is a term for the architecture of the Nvidia graphics processor, and it's cores.
There may be actual Cuda software (As the program you have), but the Nvidia
graphics driver would be a "Cuda" software of major importance, and this is what a laptop
with an Nvidia graphics card would use.
-Nvidia drivers are quite capable of using Direct X, as are ATI graphics drivers, and Intel Integrated graphics.

For what function do you intend to use the laptop ?
With graphics desgin - 3D modeling, CAD the Nvidia cards do have a small advantage over
ATI.  Obviously, if your software specifies "Cuda", then it will run more efficiently on an
Nvidia graphics card than on an ATI.

 You should verify the laptop does indeed have a dedicated graphics card with 1GB or more (512MB if you think it is enough for your purposes) of genuine dedicated VRAM  rather than a small amount of VRAM  (as little as 64MB), and the rest of the video memory "shared"  with slower system RAM, as is often the case.
Be aware laptop mfg's are often vague at best, and misleading at worst, regarding dedicated VRAM. 
If the system specifications only mention something like "1GB video memory" don't trust it.
The specifications should specify 1GB VRAM, or 1GB dedicated video memory.

Visit the Nvidia developer forums for more information regarding Cuda -

DevZone Forums

Browse here too

Another important thing here is which model ? 630m - 640m, etc. It all makes a difference
in price and performance, so you need to know the exact model graphics card.
