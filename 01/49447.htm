Selling Digital content with Xbox One. - Microsoft Community

ejmm5 say:

Selling Digital content with Xbox One.

I'm planning on saving for a car and in order I'm selling my Xbox one, I got the Assassin's Creed bundle and downloaded the titles with my Account on the machine. If I sell it, does the next owner has the rights to play those games if he makes that his home xbox? How does Digital content works in these cases.

maxen say:

For the purchasing account's (let's call this profile A) Home Xbox, every other profile on that console can indeed play games bought by Profile A. Profile B, C, D and E can all play games on Profile A's Home Xbox that Profile A bought even when Profile A is not signed in.

However, if Profile A then changes their Home Xbox to another Xbox One, the other profiles will not be able to play Profile A's game on the previous console anymore.

Now, to your original point - you want to sell the console. It is advisable that you delete your account off the console before you sell it. Now, the new buyer will still be able to play your digital games, but if you were to ever change your Home Xbox in future then they would lose access to the games - and so to avoid an angry buyer you probably would not want to do this. And from an account security standpoint, it is incredibly inadvisable to leave your account on the console as this compromises your account and personal information, while from the Xbox Live Code of Conduct's and Terms of Use's standpoint you would be violating those agreements and risking enforcement action (suspensions or bans) for both the account and console.

In short, the new buyer would be able to play the games, at least initially -- but you probably should not advertise that as those console licences could be revoked the next time you use an Xbox One, and you risk enforcement actions and potentially compromise the security of your account and personal information.

Hope this helps clarify things.
