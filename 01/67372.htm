Hello,

If you've the only administrator account on your system and it is Microsoft account, you need to create another admin account to delete the account. Also note that you cannot remove or delete an account you're currently signed in. You need to login from another admin account to remove your account. 

Additionally, when you're on Microsoft account you can go to Settings > Accounts > Your info > click Sign in with a local account instead to switch to local account. 

If you still can't remove Microsoft account, try these steps after creating a System Restore point:

1. Login to another administrator account.

2. Open Registry Editor by running regedit command.

3. Go to HKEY_USERS\.DEFAULT\Software\Microsoft\IdentityCRL\StoredIdentities.

4. Under StoredIdentities registry key, look for registry sub-key with your Microsoft account name. Right click on it and select Delete.

5. Select Yes in confirmation.

6. Reboot and check, the Microsoft account must be removed.

"How to Refresh Windows 8 and Windows 8.1"

  • The PC boots into Windows RE (Recovery Environment).
  • Windows RE scans the hard drive for your data, settings, and apps, and puts them aside (on the same drive).
  • Windows RE installs a fresh copy of Windows.
  • Windows RE restores the data, settings, and apps it has set aside into the newly installed copy of Windows.
  • The PC restarts into the newly installed copy of Windows.

"How to Reset Windows 8 and Windows 8.1"

"When you reset your PC, the drive will be completely reset, and all your apps and files will be removed from it.
