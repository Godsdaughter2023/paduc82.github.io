The newnext.me\nenengine.dll is not something that you want to reintroduce onto your system.

davem121 say:
Hi Jenyjen

I am sorry a Windows 10 Reset is a one way operation . . .

Did you not choose to keep your files, when you initiated that Rest operation?

Try this, but honestly, if you chose not to keep your files, there will be little hope of recovering those files . . .

Open Windows File Explorer
Navigate to C:\Users
Do you see a user folder that look like yours?
Open that folder and check inside Pictures, Documents . . . etc. and see if your files are there

If you still have not found your files, look for a Windows.old folder on your C Drive, expand that to open the users folder contained inside - check there for your files, icons . . .etc.

If you do find your files . . . etc. there, copy them out as soon as possible as Windows will remove the Windows.old folder in time
