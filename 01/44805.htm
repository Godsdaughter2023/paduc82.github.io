I am pretty sure that the problem lies with the Windows 7 laptop (laptop 2). When you switch laptop 1 to WEP you must also switch laptop 2 to WEP and provide the WEP key that you set up with laptop 1.

It's getting late here now, but I will try to respond tomorrow after I test it out on my two Windows 7 laptops. In the meantime maybe I can point you in the right direction...

  • In Network and Sharing Center, click Manage wireless networks (close to the top on the left)

  • Click the security tab
  • Change it to WEP and provide the encryption key
  • Click OK

Portable Devices become Hidden devices when they are disconnected and appear normally when connected. This means you will mean that you need to select View, Show Hidden Devices if {8e77e02a-11e6-a5e6-806e6f6e6963} is not connected when you attempt to identify the device.

  4. Restart your computer.  

  5. At this point, nothing should happen if you try to launch Microsoft Edge.

To disable Cortana:

  • Click the Cortana search box > Notebook icon > Settings > Turn off Cortana can give you suggestions, ideas, reminders, alerts and more option.
