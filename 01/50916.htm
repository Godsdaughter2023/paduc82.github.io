daniel moisesmagulado say: Hi CAL59

As mentioned above, my usual fix for this is to turn off "Windows Search" via "Turn on or off windows feature" but as RajithR said.. That feature is no longer available for Windows 8.1.

If you're that kind of person that wants his/her event viewer clean and no errors every start up(Like me).
I suggest to turn off your windows search via "services.msc".

Here's how to do so.
1. Press "Windows Logo Key + X".
2. Press "r".
3. Type "Services.msc". Without the quotation.
4. Once the services windows open, scroll to the bottom part of the services and look for "Windows Search".
5. Right click on that service and click Properties.
6. Under General Tab, click the "Start Up" and select disable.
7. Click apply then click ok.
8. Restart your PC.

Note:
Here's some things to take note of by doing so.
- Possible slow down in searches for files on your computer.
- Won't affect overall computer performance.
- Will affect computer files searching performance.
- Can prolong the life of your Hard Drive.
- No more 3006, 3007, and 10021.

Now, if you have an SSD as your main storage drive. There's nothing to worry about then.
