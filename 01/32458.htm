    Note: This response contains a reference to a third party World Wide Web site. Microsoft is providing this information as a convenience to you.     Information about the Multimedia files types that Windows Media Player supports

    http://support.microsoft.com/kb/316992

     

    Method 2:

    To convert the songs to the format that the Windows Media Player can understand, Open iTunes and do the following steps:
    a. Click Edit, Preferences and go to the General tab
    b. Click on the "Import Settings" button
    c. Change the "Import Using" to "MP3 Encoder"
    d. Click OK and go back to your music library
    e. Change to list view and select the first song in your library
    f. Scroll all the way down to the last song in your library
    g. Press the Shift key and then click on the last song to select all of the songs in your library
    h. Click "Advanced" and then "Create MP3 Version"
    i. Your songs should start to convert and depending on how many songs you have in your library, this could take quite some time
    j. Open Windows Media Player and press F3 or click File, Add to Library
    k. Click OK- Windows Media Player should start to search for songs on your computer and after it has finished the search, you should find the songs from your iTunes Library in your Windows Media Player Library.

     

    NOTE: This method will only work if you have bought iTunes plus songs or if you have converted the songs to iTunes Plus for 30c each
