ungertc say:

You have a couple of options, depending on the type of AMP you have.

If your AMP can handle HDMI input AND HDMI outputs, then run the xbox to the appropriate HDMI input and connect the TV to the HDMI output.  Select the appropriate input on the AMP, and you should be set.

If your AMP cannot handle that, then just run the HDMI from the XBOX to the TV and the optical from the XBOX to the AMP.  Set the AMP on the appropriate input on the AMP, and you should be set.

aquadslguy say:

I have an old xbox without HDMI, but this may work depending on what type of AMP inputs you have. I purchased a VGA input cable so the video is direct to TV existing PC port. On the VGA adaptor there is also a port for a fiber optic sound cable. The fiber optic goes directly into my AMP receiver. This option will cost you a few bucks to get teh right cables, but may be easier depending on input port options available.

Also with most AMPs or receivers, as stated above, it is best to plug everything into the AMP and have one cable back to your TV. THere is a much more powerful audio processer in the AMP over your TV and all audio signals will sound much better.

Cheers!
