Not really.  Your Windows 7 product key, is exactly that a Windows 7 product key, it is not a Windows 10 product key.

In theory by the license if you have a standalone (bought as separate retail DVD) copy of Windows 7, your upgrade to Windows 10 should be stand alone too, and you should be able to do the install and get it activated.  If you have an OEM (came with the machine, Windows 7 license) it can't used on another device, and a new motherboard is considered a new device.

Now for the "in practice part".  The answers you are going to get here are going to be the "safe route", as in tell you that you would have install Windows 7 and then do the update again.  That certainly will work.

The only way that can be bypassed is if Microsoft Support will activate it for you.  And by Microsoft Support I mean something like using the Contact Support app or running this command line, command to bring up a telephone number to contact them:

slui 4

No one posting here is going to do it for you.

And then you have to convince them that you have the right to do it.

Note this is from the Windows 10 license:

b.    Stand-alone software. If you acquired the software as stand-alone software (and also if you upgraded from software you acquired as stand-alone software), you may transfer the software to another device that belongs to you.

For Dell, press CTRL + F11 directly after powering up the unit

For HP, press F11 directly after powering up the unit

For LG, press F11 directly after powering up the unit

For Toshiba, press and hold '0' BEFORE and during the power up

For Acer, press and hold ALT + F10 as soon as you see the logo

For Asus, press F9 as soon as you see the Asus logo.

For Samsung, press F4 at power up..

For Fujitsu, press the F8 key repeatedly directly after powering up

For Advent, restart your computer. Then Press F10 repeatedly until the message "Starting System Recovery" is displayed

For Sony VAIO, restart and press "F8" or "F10" repeatedly until the "Advanced Boot Options" screen appears.

Is It Fair To Charge For DLC That’s Already On Disc? - Microsoft Community

a. user say:

Is It Fair To Charge For DLC That’s Already On Disc?

Earlier this week, Epic released The Horde Command Pack DLC for Gears of War 3, but it turns out that much of that content was already on the disc, and players just needed to download an unlocked. Is that fair? Epic thinks so.

 

"We're not saying that everything on the disc is the product," Gears 3 executive producer Rod Fergusson told Kotaku in a recent interview. "The disc is another delivery mechanism."

 

According to Fergusson, in an attempt to unify a game’s online community, game developers want all players to have access to new DLC content whether they pay for it or not. This makes DLC a trick proposition. The solution is that many developers provide new DLC to all players, but only allow those who paid for it to use the DLC weapon skins or host a new DLC map.

 

"One of the concerns was we would have to have people download compatibility packs,” explains Fergusson. However, since Gears of War was delayed from its original released date, earlier this Spring, Epic felt it had the option to include it’s first DLC pack on the disc, so that everyone wouldn’t have to download the new content. "We felt it was a win/win, because no one is going to have to download anything huge when the DLC comes out."

 

Fergusson also said that he thought that there was so much on the original Gears of War 3 disc that he didn’t feel like Epic was shorting players who didn’t want to buy the DLC.
