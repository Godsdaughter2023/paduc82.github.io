Yes because updates are intended to fix bugs and strengthen security. If any are not installed the system gets progressively more out of date. Failures are also often indicative of file corruption which can lead to system failure.

? Why are some failures not recorded in 'WindowsUpdate.log'?

This does not commonly occur. It could be the result of damaged or corrupt files.

? Why didn't the Windows Update application inform me of the failures?

These notifications are not always conspicuous and they can arise when users are intent on moving on to a non-computer related activity.

? Where is 'View update history' getting its information?

The system maintains a record of what is installed in the component store which is checked to see whether new updates are required as further updates are released.
