Notes:

  1. Make sure you have Bluetooth built in.  Really.  I bought my laptop because I THOUGHT it came with BT (Bluetooth).  That’s how it was advertised!   I ended up doing the live chat with an ASUS rep on their website, and they were helpful.  I gave them the serial number, and they looked up the model.  ASUS said that this model came with a Bluetooth OPTION! Huh!  Turns out, that means I have to get my own BT adaptor.  Buyer beware!  As an IT person, I checked everything else first. UGH!  So save time and check this out first. 

    For newbies:  it may be difficult to tell if you do or do not have BT built in.  To see if you have BT, go to the Control Panel --> Device Manager.  When you click on Device Manager, you will see a list of devices.  If Bluetooth is NOT listed there, you do not have Bluetooth in your laptop, and you will need a BT adaptor.  You might even have BT software installed already, like I did, which can fool you.  However, if BT is not in the Device Manager list, you probably don’t have BT built in.
  2. Purchase a Bluetooth adaptor if you need it.  I bought the Cirago Bluetooth 4.0 Mini USB Adapter. 
  3. Very important before sticking that BT into your usb port:  uninstall all BT drivers that may have been installed at the factory level.  Look in Programs to uninstall all BT drivers.Note:  If you skip this step, your BT will not work as expected.   My sys admin friend who worked hours on this for me did many installs/uninstalls in a variety of combinations to get my BT working,  Turns out, the best thing to do is to start fresh with no BT drivers installed at all. 

    Forget about updating your BT drivers that came with the ASUS, even the one that came with the USB adapter you just bought.  Make sure you are online, and let microsoft install all of your drivers for you, and use their Generic Bluetooth Radio.  Note:  The drivers that are on the ASUS site did not work, and the driver that came with the new Cirago BT did not work either!  My theory is that Windows 8.1 is so new all the existing hardware has not caught up with the needed changes.

 Recommended procedures:

1. Uninstall all bluetooth drivers (very important, see above!).

2. Restart machine.

3. Log in, then insert the bluetooth adaptor into the usb port.  I used the Cirago Bluetooth 4.0 Mini USB Adapter. 

3. Your system will download needed drivers from Microsoft automatically. 

4.  Now you can add the device(in this case, my keyboard) by going to the Charms menu, Settings --> Change PC settings --> PC and devices --> Bluetooth.    Follow the instructions to pair the keyboard.

5.  You are set!  When you check the Device Manager, you will see “Bluetooth”.  If you open up the Bluetooth section, you will see a variety of things, like the Generic Bluetooth Radio, and Goldtouch Bluetooth Keyboard among other things.

Last note:  I’m not sure from where or at what point the Microsoft Generic Bluetooth Radio installed – was it when I paired the Goldtouch keyboard, or was it when I inserted the BT adaptor?   Regardless,  the keyboard only works when using the Microsoft BT drivers.  Thank god it works finally!
