Method 2: I suggest you to perform the Network Adapter troubleshooter.


1. In the search bar, Type “Troubleshooting” without the quotes and click on Troubleshooting
2. Then click on View All on the left panel of the screen.
3. Click on Network Adapter.
4. Click on Next and follow the prompt to run the troubleshooter.

If the issue still persist, refer to the below method.

Method 3: I would suggest you to update the network adapter and check if it helps. 

1. Press Windows Key + R Key.
2. Then type 'devmgmt.msc' without the quotes and hit on Enter Key.
3. Expand Network Adapter by pressing the right arrow key.
4. Right click on the driver and click on Update driver software.
