which is better, pentium 3.2 GHz or Core 2 Duo - Microsoft Community

antj say:

which is better, pentium 3.2 GHz or Core 2 Duo

Hello. I want to play a game that requires a pentium 4 3.2GHz or better. I have an Intel(R) Core(TM)2 Duo CPU T5550 @ 1.83GHz 1.83GHz. Don't know alot about computers and want to know can I play it before I open it. Thanks.
shawn eary say:

Please get/run CPU-Z and post the exact model of your older Pentium Processor

http://www.cpuid.com/softwares/cpu-z.html

In general, Core 2 Duo's will outperform the older Pentium processors even at really low clock speeds.  Passmark provides a very basic charts to show you the overall performance of various processors:

http://www.cpubenchmark.net/common_cpus.html

as you can see in the chart, Passmark implies that even a Intel Core2 Duo T5500 @ 1.66GHz outperforms an Intel Pentium D @ 3.40GHz.  That's pretty radical that a Core 2 Duo is that good; unfortunately, you need to take in the account the fact that Passmark is probably accounting a certain portion of the results to multi-threaded performance.   Many people don't get to use all cores of their CPU's so multi-threaded performance is not always indicative of real world performance.  A much more realistic (but harder to read) benchmark is provided by SPEC [1]:

  1. Goto http://www.spec.org/cgi-bin/osgresults?conf=cpu2006
  2. Change DropDown to Processor 
  3. Type "Pentium D" in the Matches TextBox 
  4. Click the "Execute Simple Fetch" Button 
  5. Record an average of the CFP2006 Baseline results for your processor speed (assuming you have and older Pentium D)
  6. Repeat 1-5 for Core 2

 

When I do this, I get a Single Threaded Floating Point Rate of about 11 for a Pentium D @ 3.2 and also 11 for a Core 2 Duo @ 1.86.  So if I read the SPEC benchmarks correctly (this can be tricky), the single threaded performance of a Pentium D @ 3.2 is about the same as a Core 2 Duo @ 1.86.  So I guess its a toss one way or the other, but many games these days are becoming multi-threaded, so another factor might be the CFP2006 Rates measurement which is (multi-threaded).   When I do this, I get a MultiThreaded Floting Point Rate of about 18.9 for a Pentium D @ 3.2 and 19.6 for a Core 2 Duo @ 1.86.  So the Core 2 duo wins by a small margin.  

To make a long story short, I am guessing that a Pentium D @3.2 and Core 2 Duo @ 1.86 are about the same but I am assuming your old Pentium is a Pentium D.  It could be an even older Pentium like a single core Pentium or the old hyper threaded Pentium (hack), so the Core 2 Duo might give you even more performance gains.  Also, Core 2 Duos will should run cooler than old school Pentiums so if you are a serious gaming head, you might want to consider getting a Core 2 Duo just so you won't have to worry about your thermal alarm going off so much. 

Finally, you may get better responses posting this type of question at Tom's Hardware:

http://www.tomshardware.com

Those guys' really take benchmarking seriously!!!...

[1] - For games, you are often concerned with Floating Point results unless you have a really killer graphics/physics processor and you only use your main CPU for Artificial Intelligence.
