Right click the folders that you want to relocate and select Cut. Navigate into the folder you want them to appear in, right click again and select Paste.


If the source and destination are different drives you will see the move process as the "flying files" animation which is the files being copied then a pause at the end which is the source being cleaned up

Cut and Paste therefore does not require the original folders and files to be deleted afterwards.

There is one thing you might try... it won't "Fix" the underlying problem, but might make your life a bit easier...

Win 8 and 8.1 have a new feature called Fast Startup. It can sometimes cause problems like you mention as well as others.  Drivers sometimes don't load properly when it is turned on...

Lower down on the window, uncheck Fast Startup.
