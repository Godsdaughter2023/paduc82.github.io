Why do desktop icons auto-arrange to the left side after every windows - Microsoft Community

sean09 say:

Why do desktop icons auto-arrange to the left side after every windows update?

For about the last 3 updates my desktop icons, which are placed in specific locations of my choosing across my desktop, are auto-arranged to the left side of the screen when I log into my account after my PC has restarted after a windows update installation. This is very annoying, and I would like to know why it keeps happening now, as it did not used to happen with Windows 10 updates.

a. user say:

Desktop icons move to the left side of the monitor when the graphics driver is replaced.

If you've been using Windows for a while, then you know that the default location for new icons is the next available space on the left side of the monitor. Keeping that in mind, when a graphics driver is replaced, for the instant in time after the former driver is removed but before the new driver is installed, Windows is using its built-in default graphics driver. Windows is also using its default graphics driver when you enter Safe Mode, which is why Safe Mode looks like that.

In any case, the default driver doesn't know how you previously arranged your icons, so they all get moved to the default position on the left side of the monitor.

Then the new graphics driver is installed and, not knowing any different, leaves the icons where it found them. So you have to re-arrange them.

But if you didn't replace your graphics driver ... then who did? Our friends at Microsoft, in their continuing desire to be helpful whether we want them to or not, when updating a computer will replace whatever graphics driver we installed with a driver that Microsoft thinks is better suited. How nice.

Leaving Windows Update aside for the moment, a purist will tell you that we're not supposed to clutter our desktop with icons, even though almost everyone does anyway, including yours truly. Instead, we're supposed to rely on the Start menu and its tiles; or the Taskbar and its jump lists; or the Recent items list. Oookay ...

To maintain your desktop icons in the arrangement you like, you've got to find a way to 'remember' their positions. Fortunately, Stardock and its Fences app has you covered.

If you would like to go out from safe mode look at recommendation below:

1/ Open System Configuration. Press the Windows logo key + R. Type “msconfig” and press Enter. In System Configuration, click the Boot tab, and then uncheck Safe boot. After that, click OK. In the dialog popping up, click Restart.
