zacabeb say:

The video output stage in the Xbox 360's GPU has a dedicated hardware scaler unit that doesn't use up any other of the GPU capacity. It's inherited from the Xilleon architecture ATI developed. The GPU also has a hardware overlay pipeline that's used for notifications. The hardware scaler, the original ANA TV encoder, and HANA were designed to handle 1080p from the outset.

As for Dolby Digital, the AC-3 encoding is done by the CPU but uses capacity already reserved for system use. The 5.1-channel LPCM output by the game is sent through two pipelines; one does the AC-3 encoding, the other does stereo downmixing of the main five channels. The latter is also done by already reserved capacity. Those two streams are sent off to the south bridge and from there, passed on to the audio DAC as well as HANA (video goes to HANA straight from the GPU).

For those reasons, I don't think the Xbox 360 is to blame. There may be latency compensation issues between audio and video though in the setup depending on whether you pick LPCM or Dolby Digital.
