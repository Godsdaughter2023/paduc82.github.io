Hi

Those numbers show that the battery still has over 95% of the original charging capacity.

The situation that you are describing can usually be fixed by performing a Power Cycle on the laptop.

Perform the following.


1. Shut down the computer.


2. Unplug everything from the computer. This includes the AC Adapter, Modems and Routers, and external devices, such as printers, drives, USB devices, etc. Nothing should be left plugged into the computer.

3. Remove the battery.

4. Wait 30 Seconds. Press and hold the Power Button on the laptop for 15 seconds. This will drain all of the excess electrical energy out of the laptop.

5. Plug everything back into the computer, including the AC Adapter and battery.

6. Start the computer and check the results to see if the problem is resolved.

If this does not fix the problem, the problem is likely in the hardware charging circuit on the laptop.
