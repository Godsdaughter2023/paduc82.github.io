ldoron say:

First, rather than a reset or clean install of Windows, you can try removing the remnants of McAfee by running the McAfee Consumer Product Removal tool (MCPR). See this page for directions and download:

https://service.mcafee.com/webcenter/portal/cp/home/articleview?articleId=TS101331

For other readers: Always try, if possible, to uninstall McAfee the "usual" way (Windows Settings -> Apps OR Control Panel -> Programs and Features) first, and restart when done. Then run a newly-downloaded copy of MCPR (avoid old versions), and restart when done.

Regarding the original problem, I found that my crashes (all tagged StackHash_2264) were caused by the McAfee Firewall's Intrusion Detection feature. One can disable just that feature, and still have all the other protections (though Windows Defender has gotten very good). I had this feature enabled because it used to be the default. I uninstalled McAfee and reinstalled it, and Intrusion Detection was now turned off by default, possibly because of this problem. McAfee has fixed it in the Business software, but not the Consumer software (see https://kc.mcafee.com/corporate/index?page=content&id=KB89023).

To disable it, open the Firewall settings, scroll down and select Intrusion Detection, and then uncheck "Use Intrusion Detection". Click Apply. The StackHash_2264 crashes should immediately stop. Hope this helps!
