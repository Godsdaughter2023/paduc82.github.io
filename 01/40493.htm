obsessive power say:

The term 'laser burn' is a misnomer.  The laser that is projected from the inside of the DVD drive to read discs isn't powerful enough to 'burn' a disc.  And despite what another poster has stated, it has nothing to do with dust build up either.

What actually happens is that at some point when the game is being read, the disc comes into physical contact with the laser diode. It is the diode that causes the circle scratch, not the laser per-say.  There are only two ways in which this scenario can occur.  Either the console has been disturbed in some fashion or nocked over.  This will cause the game disc to crash into the diode, causing a rather nasty scratch.  The other is a faulty DVD drive.

To prevent this happening again, make sure you place your console on a stable surface where it can not be shaken or nocked over.  Do not pick up the console while a game is inside being read.  As stated above, if you install your games they won't be read from the DVD during play, and therefore won't be scratched, even if the console is moved.

As for your disc, it may yet be salvageable.  I would suggest taking it to a game store to see if they can 'clean' the disc for you.  They should be able to do this if they sell pre-owned games.  The disc cleaning process removes a very thin layer of the disc, just enough to remove the scratch and enable the disc to be read once again. This can only be done a finite amount of times however.
