electroflying say:

If your 360 has a Hard Drive. One possible solution is to remove the Hard Drive door, lay it aside, and thereby allow cool air to circulate around the Hard Drive.

Also make sure you have plenty of open space for the fan to exhaust heat away from the system.

Having a Hard Drive in an unventilated, closed plastic enclosure I have always seen as a poor design flaw.

One other thing: If you have in home Air Conditioning, set it at around 70 degrees. This will also help to keep the system running cooler as well.

If none of that helps, then I can only suggest calling support, and possible sending it in for cleaning, and or repair.

electroflying say:

Its that 'not much height' answer which is concerning me. The fan (Top of console) should have plenty of space with which to allow the expulsion of heat AWAY from the unit. So when laid flat, I would give it more than a foot of space above the unit. And believe me. Removing thr Harddrive door (Located on the right side on the console when laid flat) does help to draw in cool air around the harddrive. Which can and will get hot enough to cause the system to shut down.

irixion say:

Removing the HDD 'door' gives negligible air flow. If your console is overheating with the door on, it's not magically going to stay cool with the door off. There's nowhere for the air to go in, as there is no intake of air designed to go in that way. All removing the door would do is let air circulate around the millimeters around the HDD. Just make sure your console has room to breathe. 1-2 inches in every direction. An inch is about 3 fingers together. 2 inches is a bit of space. Also make sure that there's room between your furniture and the wall so air can move around. Really, if it keeps overheating your best bet is sending it in for repair, or, getting an air conditioner.
