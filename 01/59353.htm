Hi,

 

I know nothing about Virtual Drives, but if your external 2TB is not  showing up in Bios or Disk management, it is probably the one suffering the "Disk Failure"

 

Windows 7 and 8 play a little game when they are installed with other drives attached...

They create a hidden (or hard to find) boot file...

 

If you then try to boot with one of these drives removed, reformatted, or whatever...windows won't boot. I don't remember if there is an error, or it just hangs....

 

If your external drive was attached when windows was installed, it probably is why the boot failed.

 

A malfunctioning drive would also cause a problem reinstalling windows....

I had a similar problem due to a bad sata cable on one of my machines.

 

If you were to attach only your old OS drive and boot with a Win 7 or 8 Disk, you could probably do an automatic repair or a boot repair and get it to work...

 

ALSO ...it may be that removing drives has screwed up the boot drive order in your bios...

Check to make sure the Original boot drive is still listed properly with the other drives removed
