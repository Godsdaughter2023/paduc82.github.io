Why I am getting tired of [current] video games - Microsoft Community

gameshoes3003 say:

Why I am getting tired of [current] video games

From my blog:
http://gameshoes.wordpress.com/2012/04/07/why-i-am-getting-tired-of-current-video-games/

I didn't write it in my original post, but I am talking about multiplayer titles. There are single player titles out there but those were not what I was interested on while writing this. Enjoy.

.

Oh, video games. Us gamers have sunk so many hours into them to the point where others may believe that we’re very unproductive. But what entertainment it has brought us. For many, video games are providing endless hours of entertainment but some of us it’s just getting old. I am one of those gamers who is suffering from boredom when it comes to video games now and every year, and the big AAA titles just aren’t bringing what I want to the table. But I think I’ve found the reason why I am just not feeling it for these video games anymore and I’d like to discuss about it. Perhaps any of you reading may agree with my sentiments.

The Exhaustion

Yes, I am getting exhausted by video games. Now this sounds very strange as video games generally excite players when engaged. This also applies to me as well – I too get excited when playing. However it’s after playing that I really feel that exhaustion. Now it’s not exhaustion from playing too many hours (though I’m sure that contributes to my end result), but it’s exhaustion from being too competitive. What’s even worse is that this exhaustion from competition can happen after only one short session.

The Competition

Look at the big titles as of late. We’ve got Call of Duty: Modern Warfare, Battlefield 3, StarCraft 2, League of Legends, etc. What do all of these games have in common? They are Player vs. Player (PvP) centric games that absolutely thrive on competitive play. In these games, the name on the top of the scoreboard is herald as the winner and the objective is to make sure your name is in that spot. Yes, you can argue that these games require some teamwork which is true in most cases but at the end of the day it’s all about your own performance. This is where I find the exhaustion building up.
If I perform well in one game I feel obligated to do well in the next game. I cannot have a large drop in personal score. If I do end up performing badly I feel that I have somewhat failed myself. You may think that it’s silly to feel bad over a video game but because I find video games to be simple I should be able to play well; it’s not like I’m doing a difficult task. That’s how I feel: simple task, good performance. If I fail at that simple task it just feel bad.
It’s rather silly that I’m just competing against some numbers on the screen, yeah, but there are also many other video gamers out there who share this competitive drive. That’s why we see these big tournaments such as the Intel Extreme Masters (IEM), and Major League Gaming (MLG). There is a big drive for competitive play and people are continuously buying into it so that they can see themselves at the top whether at a tournament or locally in a small session.
But this competitive drive does drain me a bit but that’s easily solved by taking a break.

Enough is Enough

I just explained myself as a player who is competitive and won’t accept losses so why am I having issues with these competitive games? The reason is that there are too many of them. Year after year the PvP scene is growing especially with the rapid expansion of the internet. Just like how people get tired of eating the same cereal every morning I am getting tired of playing the same thing every year. There are very few non-competitive titles that can match the quality of development of the AAA titles – we all want the best so we buy the best. Then in those games, we join a short session and compete, and then join another one shortly after. However every time I play I get exhausted from competing, then I take a break, then I compete again an hour later, then I take a longer break for a day, then I compete again, then I take an even longer break that can go on for weeks.
The lack of variety on the market is alarming to me as I can’t seem to buy two different products. Eh, that’s a bit of an exaggeration but it’s not that far off. The end product that we seem to be paying for is the ability to compete against others through some medium whether it be in first person shooters or via Tetris. So if I want to play video games, I must play against somebody with the small assistance of ‘teammates’. Can’t there just be a game where I can directly cooperate with others?

No Demand for Co-op

The best cooperative I have ever played is Resident Evil 5. I’ve seriously poured in many hours into this game; perhaps around 200 hours. Now that’s a lot of game time. So why can’t I just go back to those games? The reason why is rather unfortunate as it’s because there is just no demands for them. The masses are buying into competitive video games such as Call of Duty and developers/publishers are just following the herd. With fleeting player base numbers these cooperative games just don’t seem to last that long which further encourages developers to make more PvP based games to get that bite into the market. While you will find people still playing Resident Evil 5 it’s hard to come across a game that you want to join (I prefer joining games on Professional difficulty which others hardly play now).
One can ask about why the cooperative gaming market is so small. I believe the reason is that cooperative play generally takes a lengthy amount of time to complete. Cooperative games tend to be games where a party must really sit down and ensure that they have enough time to play before beginning their session. These players construct their game plan together then execute and if they fail, they make up another plan until they get one that works. Now compare that to being able to spare 10 minutes, jump into a game, and compete against people for one round. This fits the palette of our modern gamer who generally has a hectic schedule. But you cannot just apply the same logic to cooperative play by shortening the length of time; that directly degrades the quality of the game. In game AI can only do so much so making short sessions will exacerbate their incompetence thereby making the game not challenging. One of the best examples of this would be: “Operation Flashpoint: Red River” where its short sessions made for some really easy game play. On the other hand real players though can quickly change their plans on the fly in a PvP session so there is always a challenge to be had.
But there are more expensive options for those really wanting cooperative play. They’re known as Massive Multiplayer Online Role Playing Games (MMORPGs) with the most notable title being: “World of Warcraft”. However these options generally carry the heavy price of monthly fees which a lot of people just can’t afford. Might I just mention then that this is why I am looking forward to Guild Wars 2 which is a future MMORPG that won’t have the bane of monthly fees. But if you can afford it, go for it; MMORPGs tend to have a lot of content that should entertain you for a long time.
All in all though, the mass market is not seeking for the cooperative play that I so desire and that’s a shame.

In Conclusion…

My generation of gamers are dwindling as the developers and publishers follow the masses for gaming. But there are a few studios looking out for us cooperative gamers. Ubisoft is coming out with Ghost Recon: Future Soldier which in comparison to other shooters has a lot of cooperative play content, there’s also CAPCOM’s Resident Evil 6, and then there’s Guild Wars 2 which many PC gamers are pinning their hopes on as their flagship game.
It may only be a few titles, but I really hope that they are quality games that will make gamers like me happy.
