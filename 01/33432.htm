How Device Manager displays a particular camera varies depending on the camera model and whether or not it is setup as PTP/MTP (Picture Transfer Protocol or Media Transfer Protocol) or MSC (Mass Storage Class). MTP/PTP ones will appear in Device Manager as either Portable Devices or Imaging Devices while MSC ones will be listed under Disk Drives. I suggest you look in all 3 of these places, but my Nikon Coolpix S8000 appears as a Portable Devices in Windows 7 because its PTP.

Also look in:

Start->Control Panel->Autoplay

and see if you camera is listed at the very bottom under Devices. The dropdown menu next to your camera should be set to Open device stage.

Laptop USB ports do not always supply enough power for some devices to be recognised.

Having said all that I transfer all of my photos/videos by taking the memory card out of the camera and plugging it into a card reader on my PC - I find it simpler than tracking down/untangling all the different cables for my 3 cameras.
