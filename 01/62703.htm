I would suggest doing a very -long- power cycle on your network. Follow these steps and the issue should be cleared up.

1. Go to MY XBOX, click all the way to the right to SYSTEM SETTINGS, and from there select NETWORK SETTINGS. Select your network whether WIRED or WIRELESS.

2. Once in the NETWORK SETTINGS menu, select the CONFIGURE NETWORK option and then click to the right which is ADDITIONAL SETTINGS.

3. Select the RESTORE TO FACTORY DEFAULTS (Note: This will only reset your network settings and nothing more) and say YES to the prompt.

4. Shut the Xbox 360 Console off, and then unplug the power to your router (If applicable) and then the power to your modem (If applicable, the idea is to shutdown your network).

5. Wait 5 full minutes. Just let everything sit offline for around five minutes and yes this is important.

6. Turn the modem on first (If applicable) followed by the router afterwards (If applicable). Let them both come all the way back online.

7. Turn the Xbox 360 Console back on and navigate once more to MY XBOX, click to the right to SYSTEM SETTINGS, and then select NETWORK SETTINGS.

8. Select your network again, whether WIRED or WIRELESS (Note: If you have a WIRELESS network, you may be required to enter your wireless passphrase [WEP/WPA/WPA2] once more.

9. Lastly, run the 'Xbox LIVE Connection Test' and see what happens.

Hello Kerry,

TMP files are temporary files that are created to temporarily hold data on your computer while you are using a specific software application. Since it is just a temporary files, it is safe to say that there are no consequences when you delete.

I suggest that you use the Disk cleanup tool to delete the temporary files. Here's how:

  1. Press the Windows key on your keyboard and type Disk cleanup in the search box and hit Enter.
  2. Under Files to delete, select the file types to get rid of. To get a description of the file type, select it.
  3. Select OK.

If you want to temporarily disable the Background Intelligent Transfer Service to manually delete the said file, just follow these steps.

  1. Press the Windows key on your keyboard and type Services in the search box and hit Enter.
  2. In the Services window, look for and right-click Background Intelligent Transfer Service, and choose Properties.
  3. If you want to stop it from running, just hit the Stop button under Service Status, but if you want to disable it, change the option in Startup type to Disabled, hit Apply, then OK.
