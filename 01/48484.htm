usasma say:
I noted several problems in the memory dump file.

1)  scmndisp.sys dates from 2007.  It's a component of your wireless networking device (most likely a wireless USB device from Netgear).  Win7 does not like wireless USB devices - and when coupled with Vista-era drivers will spit out BSOD's like there's no tomorrow. 
Please update this driver immediately.  If no update is available, uninstall the software/drivers and physically remove the device from your system.  More info on this driver here:  http://www.carrona.org/drivers/driver.php?id=scmndisp.sys

Here's my rant about these devices:
[QUOTE]
I do not recommend using wireless USB devices.  Especially in Win7 systems. 
These wireless USB devices have many issues with Win7 - and using Vista drivers with them is almost sure to cause a BSOD. 
Should you want to keep using these devices, be sure to have Win7 drivers - DO NOT use Vista drivers!!!
An installable wireless PCI/PCIe card that's plugged into your motherboard is much more robust, reliable, and powerful.
[/QUOTE]

2)  GVTDrv64.sys dates from 2006  It is a component of your Gigabyte EasyTune6 driver (and it has known issues w/Win7).  Please uninstall this program from your system.  More info on this driver here:  http://www.carrona.org/drivers/driver.php?id=GVTDrv64.sys

3)  Recently AppleCharger.sys from Gigabyte has been showing up as a problem with Win7 systems.  I'd suggest uninstalling it to see if that stops your problems.  If not, feel free to install the latest available, Win7 compatible version - and monitor for further problems.  More info on this driver here:  http://www.carrona.org/drivers/driver.php?id=AppleCharger.sys

4)  AODDriver.sys is a component of AMD Overdrive - but is also present in Gigabyte EasyTune.  Please uninstall it as it's known to cause BSOD issues in Win7 systems.  More info on this driver here:  http://www.carrona.org/drivers/driver.php?id=AODDriver.sys

5)  gdrv.sys dates from 2009.  It's a component of Gigabyte Easy Saver - a mobo power utility driver.  It's been associated with BSOD's - but is mostly found alongside the EasyTune6 program.  Please uninstall it to see if it stops the BSOD's.  If you must reinstall it, please download a fresh copy that's Win7 compatible.  More info on this driver here:  http://www.carrona.org/drivers/driver.php?id=gdrv.sys

6)  Some of your USB 3.0 drivers date from 2009 - please obtain an updated version of these drivers from the mobo manufacturer's website.  If no updates are available, please post back and we'll see if we can point you to a source for more current drivers.  More info on these drivers here: 
http://www.carrona.org/drivers/driver.php?id=nusb3xhc.sys
http://www.carrona.org/drivers/driver.php?id=nusb3hub.sys

In the event that none of the above steps fix the problem, please run Driver Verifier according to these directions:  http://www.carrona.org/verifier.html

2. Use the removable memory card. If your phone is equipped with a removable memory card you will find it very easy to just remove the card and use it to move the photos from your cell phone to your computer. To use this option you may need to purchase a memory card reader. Just make sure to purchase the correct type of reader for your memory card.

3. Remove the memory card from your phone as per its specific instructions. Insert the memory card into the reader/adapter. Plug the reader into an available port on your computer. Open the memory card's drive and transfer the photos from your phone to your computer.

 

Alternatively, if your computer is Bluetooth enabled then follow these steps:

1. Turn on your computer's Bluetooth. Most new computers will have a Bluetooth icon in the toolbar near the clock. Click on the icon to make sure Bluetooth is on or active.

2. Make your phone "discoverable”. Scroll to the Bluetooth setting in your phone's menu. It's usually found in the "Settings" menu and will be listed under something like "Bluetooth Menu." There will be a setting that says "Make Discoverable" or similar.

 

3. Place the phone near your computer. This will activate the Bluetooth on your computer, and the two devices will "search" for each other. When they find each other, both devices will display a "passkey”.

4. Verify the passkeys are the same. Look at both devices, make sure the numbers match and press "OK" on both devices. You're now paired. Your phone should appear in the Bluetooth menu on your computer.

 

5. Choose your phone from the computer's menu. This will open Bluetooth File Exchange or a similar program (depending on your computer). Your phone will prompt you to accept the request. Click "OK" or "Accept" on your phone. This will open File Exchange, and you should see your phone's files listed.

6. Open the "My Photos" folder in Bluetooth File Exchange to download your pictures. Some phones and computers will have different names for the program and folders, but once the devices are paired you should be able to navigate the files easily.
