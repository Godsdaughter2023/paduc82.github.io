    sdonaldsonjr say:

    Here is the answer to this: 

    I upgraded to a newer version of Windows 10 on a Lenovo Helix tablet/laptop., and my device started cycling through the colors... first white then black, then red-green-blue, and back thru the cycle again. The device was totally unusable, or so I thought. This color-cycling came even before the BIOS splash screen would come up... before Windows loaded, so that told me that this Win10 upgrade that was pushed somehow updated the UEFI preboot settings... or so I thought. 

    I was able to gain access to the device by going through an external monitor, luckily Lenovo put a mini-display port on the device so I could connect it to my monitor with an adapter. I tried multiple things like rolling back the BIOS (make sure that you close the lid of your laptop at startup and it will make the external port the default graphics port.) None of the software changes I did made any difference. This basically confirmed my suspicions that this was a hardware issue. 

    I tried calling Lenovo support, they told me my warranty expired in April of this year... so I called their warranty extension sales number, and they said there were no post-warranties that I could purchase. Basically this device was out of support after only 3 years. 


    **** THE FIX ****

    So I took apart the tablet screen of the Helix "laptop" and found a ribbon labeled LCD. I figured this supplied at the very least the data to the LCD screen and that this would be necessary to take out to see if I could somehow force a reset of whatever controller or replace the ribbon was built on to the screen. 

    I thought the connector of this ribbon had a lever to it to secure the ribbon into the connector. It appeared to have an edge on the connector so I lightly pried on the connector with a pair of tweezers i was using to take apart the tablet. Well... I broke the corner of the ribbon connector. :-( 

    Apparently this ribbon connector to the motherboard was not a lever type at all, the ribbon just slid in and out of this connector! (stupid design IMHO) With this ribbon out of the connector, I found to no surprise that the screen went black. So after breaking the corner of the connector, I wanted to see if I could at least get the screen to turn back on and cycle. I plugged the ribbon back into the connector (mind you the device was still turned on at this time) and BOOM the screen lit up and was showing the Windows Log-in screen. 

    I quickly checked the ribbon again to make sure it was securely fastened into my now "broken" connector (be careful when you pry on things!) which it was, but I took a small piece of black electrical tape to actually tape the ribbon to the connector so it wouldnt come loose over time. 

    I am positive that I just needed to pull the ribbon from the connector and it would reset whatever the controller is on the LCD screen thus ending the color-cycle that I was experiencing. 

    I hope this post helps someone out. I know I would have benefited from it had I seen it.
