What happens when you right-click the Desktop > Arrange icons > Show Desktop icons?

If Show Desktop icons is missing you need to change a registry key, so create a System Restore point first.

Now open a Run window (Windows key+R), type regedit, press Enter and in the left pane navigate to HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\ Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Policies\Explorer

In the right pane, double-click NoActiveDesktopChanges and set it to 0 (zero) then double-click NoActiveDesktop and set it to 0 (zero). If neither of the keys exist, it’s a different issue.

Close Registry Editor and restart the computer.

disgruntleduser1 say:

Create Recovery Drive takes too long

a. user say:

The better question is: Why make a recovery drive in the first place?

Recovery drives are old technology. Years ago they were a good idea, even a life saver. That was then. Today there's a much better way to recover from computer disasters: image backups.

An image is an identical copy of your entire hard drive, compressed into a single file. When you restore an image, your computer goes back in time to exactly the way it was when the image was made. There's nothing to re-install, recover, fix, update or reconfigure. Just step back in time.

If that sounds too good to be true, consider this: everything you have on your computer - all your software, settings, personal files, Windows itself - is nothing more than one gigantic series of 0's and 1's. How hard is it for a software program to make a copy of that?

You can see why making images has been standard practice in large organizations for years, and why the backup software industry is so large. What do you suppose Microsoft's 'reset' procedure is - it restores an image of Windows 10 (only.) Or how about your computer manufacturer's factory reset procedure - that also restores an image (of all the software installed at the factory.)

To make and restore images you'll need a third-party image application. Fortunately, there are many great choices. We can make suggestions or (even better) you can search for image backup applications on the internet and look for professional reviews to help you make a choice.

Want convenience? It takes just minutes to make an image, and minutes to restore an image. Making an image is so fast and easy - once you learn how to do it - that you can make an image every day (and you should.) The software can even make images for you automatically.

Here's the bottom line: Why would anybody want to spend hours trying to fix problems with their computer or, if that doesn't work, to re-install, re-configure, update their software and then restore their backed up data, and test to make sure that everything works properly ... when in literally minutes you can simply send your computer back in time to when everything was working fine?
