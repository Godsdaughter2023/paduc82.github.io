    Feature update to Windows 10, version 1803 - Generic External Sound - Microsoft Community

    john 1072 say:

    Feature update to Windows 10, version 1803 - Generic External Sound Cards show in Device Manager - but no longer work

    Good day! 

    I am using Windows 10 Pro and always keep it updated with the latest patches.  I connect an external, generic USB sound card device (Tigertronics Signalink USB) to my computer, which then interfaces with amateur radio equipment.  Similar external sound card devices are manufactured by several sources and, when attached to a USB port on the computer, a USB audio CODEC is generated by the OS.  Once the CODEC is in place, an application is able to "see" the external card as a sound device, allowing selection of the device or CODEC. The sound is then ported to the application for use. Sounds generated by the application are able to be sent to the external sound card, which sounds can then be transmitted over the radio.

    After installing the Windows 10, version 1803 update, the attached external sound cards are still visible in device manager. However, no application is able to select them for use. Various error messages result, with most indicating that the sound card is "in use" and not available.  I am able to select them as a "default" windows sound device and send regular Windows sounds to them, but that is not acceptable for this purpose. A typical entry under "Audio inputs and outputs" is identified as an "Audio Endpoint" and looks like this:

    Microphone (5- USB Audio CODEC)

    Speakers (5- USB Audio CODEC)

    The entry under "Sound, video and game controllers" is:

    USB Audio CODEC

    The identified device driver is dated 4/11/2018. No updates are available, and the driver cannot be "rolled back," as the selection is "grayed out."

    Looking for driver "updates" reveals that there are no updates available. 

    The manufacturers of these relatively straightforward devices do not provide drivers because the generic USB drivers previously installed and available under Windows worked just fine, prior to this recent update. For comparison, connecting one of these external sound cards to a Windows 7 computer allows them to work just fine, just like they used to in Windows 10.

    Other suggested solutions I have read here, with similar "no sound" complaints, are of no value. The suggestions for repair mostly deal with internal sound cards, or suggest contacting the manufacturer for drivers.  There seems to have been a fundamental change in the capacity of the USB Audio driver, and it needs to be repaired - or a suitable replacement provided.  This capability is used by thousands of amateur radio operators, world-wide.  Thank you for your time.

    john 1072 say:

    Here is an update to my original issue, and hopefully this will help someone else. The first part of this solution, which addressed my primary issue dealing with non-working external sound cards, was posted by another community member, so I cannot take credit for this. It is repeated here because it is easy to overlook some replies.

    I spent most of my time trying to find an answer in the device manager, in the sound settings, etc., everywhere except where I needed to be. The solution was found in the "Privacy" settings! 

    1. Go to Privacy Settings

    2. Turn on "microphone"

    3. Disable any applications on that screen that you do not want to access the now turned on microphone (I disabled all of them for my personal preference)

    The three steps above were all that was needed! Access to the external sound cards was immediately restored!

    There was one other thing. I could hear audio from my external sound card coming through the computer speakers. This was something I had not experienced before, and was not desirable for my application.  To stop this audio, follow the following steps.

    1. Settings

    2. Sound

    3. Under "Input" select "Device Properties"

    4. Select the "Listen" tab

    5. Uncheck "Listen to this device." 

    6. "Apply"

    Hope these steps help anyone still dealing with the repercussions of this MS update 1803.
