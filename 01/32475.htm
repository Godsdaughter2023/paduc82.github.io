brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

Printer manufacturers don't make much money on their printers.  They make their money selling you ink.  Printer manufacturers put circuits in their cartridges that tell the printer when they're empty.  Often, those circuits cannot be reset even if you refill the cartridge.  The circuit still tells the printer that the cartridge is empty.  The printer manufacturers don't want you to buy cheap ink and refill the cartridges.

Some printer manufacturers also have circuits in the cartridges that identify who made the cartridge and if the printer doesn't see a identity that matches the printer, the printer will report a problem with the disk.  With these two ploys, the printer manufacturers try to ensure not only do you buy expensive ink, but you buy the exact brand of ink they want you to buy.

I don't know if this is what's happening with your printer, but it wouldn't surprise me.
