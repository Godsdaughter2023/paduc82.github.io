I'd start by clearing the system cache (it's just updates plus other temporary files, nothing that's at all important to anyone) by:

1) Tap the silver Xbox Guide button on the controller, go right 2 clicks to Settings, pick System Settings.

2) Select Storage (or Memory).

3) Move over any hard drive or memory unit (not the Cloud) & press (Y) on your controller & pick Clear System Cache.

4) When asked to confirm storage device maintenance, choose Yes.

 

Now, some NAT problems are subtle and don't appear in the Test Xbox Live Connection test. Most likely there's a port blocked... ports can be blocked in your router, modem, or

 

A sequential power cycle is the first step in clearing a port blockage:

 

1) turn off the Xbox then

2) unplug power from the router (Some folks have a combination modem-router AKA 'gateway'. If so, skip step 3).

Anyway, this is far less than ideal but it is a good solution for me until MSFT decides to fix their broken software.

2. Copy the text below and paste it into the blank document (I realize this requires an amount of trust that I am not going to delete your hard drive or something nasty like that, but it really is legit):

@echo off
start %SystemRoot%\System32\rundll32.exe "%ProgramFiles%\Windows Photo Viewer\PhotoViewer.dll", ImageView_Fullscreen %1

3. Save the file wherever you want (I recommend Desktop) and name it "Windows Photo Viewer.bat" (or whatever you want) . Make sure to choose "All Files" under the "Save as Type" drop down box.  This will make sure you get a batch file that Windows can load and not just a text file.

From within the Photos App, you can use the "Open With" command to get the photo into something else you can work with. Sadly, you can't use the Photo App's "Open With" command to do this method automatically even though you can select a batch file as one of the things to open a picture with.
