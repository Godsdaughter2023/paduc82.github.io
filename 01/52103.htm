janeane cru say:

Hi Vince,

On-Screen Keyboard might show up due to a misconfigured settings after an update. For us to isolate this issue, please follow these methods:

Method 1: Disable the On-Screen Keyboard in Ease of Access.

  1. Click on Start.
  2. Type Ease of Access Center.
  3. Press the Enter button.
  4. Click on Use the computer without a mouse or keyboard link.
  5. Uncheck the Use On-Screen Keyboard checkbox. (If it’s already unchecked, click it to put a check mark on it. Click on Apply button, then uncheck it again.)
  6. Click on Apply button to save changes.

Method 2: Disable the Touch Keyboard and Handwriting Panel Service.

  1. Click on Start.
  2. Type Services.
  3. Press the Enter button.
  4. Look for Touch Keyboard and Handwriting Panel Service, and then double-click on it.
  5. Make sure that you are on the General tab.
  6. Locate the Startup Type drop down. Change it to Disabled.
  7. Click on Apply button to save changes.
  8. Click on OK button to close the Touch Keyboard and Handwriting Panel Service Properties window.

Let us know if the methods given above helped you to resolve the issue.
