    pr2459 say:

    I have the same problem with CPU usage after hibernation. Today I figured out a "solution" based on infor I found online but it is not related to nvidia driver directly. Maybe it is just my computer. What I did was to enable the "power saver" plan (as opposed to Balanced). If you don't have it, you can find online instructions to unlock the hidden options via regedit. Once in the Power Saver mode, the "system and compressed memory" will not using high amount of CPU. But what is the difference between Power Saver and Balanced? After playing with all the options I determined it is just the PCI express Link state power management. If you set it to Maximum power savings while plugged in, the high CPU usage by System and compressed memory will stop. This also explains why it didn't happen on battery because the link state was in the power saving mode already.

    If you don't want to enable the Power Saver plan, you may be able to add just the PCI policy to existing "balanced" plan although I haven't tried myself to see if this works, You can search for it (link state eightforums) online.
