  1. Uninstall any security software before attempting to upgrade.
  2. Disable any encryption software you might have installed.

  5. Disconnect any external devices before installing.

    - Click Start

    At the Command Prompt, type: chkdsk /r /f
    Exit the command prompt.
    When you restart your system, your computer will be scanned for errors and attempts will be made to correct them.

The feature you want is called a Split. It's found on the same tab (Video Tools | Edit) as the trim tools.  The keyboard shortcut is 'M'.

The workflow I would suggest is the following:

1) Add your 12 minute long video to the storyboard/timeline.

2) Play through your video and make splits at the relevant points.  Alternate between good and bad footage.

For example, let's imagine you had video like the following (G = good, B = bad)

BBBGGGGGBBBBGGGGGBBBBBGGGGBBGG

BBB|GGGGG|BBBB|GGGGG|BBBBB|GGGG|BB|GG

3) Select each of the bad clips and delete them:

GGGGG|GGGGG|GGGG|GG

4) Finish the rest of your editing (adding titles, captions, transitions, etc.)
