sigmathedj say:

Instant on mode basically just means that the console goes into standby mode, rather than turning off. It's kinda like putting a computer into sleep mode instead of shutting it down. Instant on mode gives you several advantages over power saving mode: -

You can turn the console on with your voice

The console boots up much quicker as it's just waking up, rather than booting up from scratch

You can quick resume the last game you were playing

Updates will be downloaded while the console is turned off

You can buy/queue up content via SmartGlass or Xbox.com and it will automatically download even if the console is off

So ideally if you use instant on mode, you would turn automatic updating on as well. Even if you decide to leave the preview program, it's worth doing that, as then if a new update for a game comes out while the console is turned off, it will be downloaded automatically. With automatic updates turned off, that game update wouldn't be downloaded automatically, so when you turn your console on and launch the game, it will then force you to download the update for it.

In a minority of cases, an update will be rolled out for a game while you're actually playing it and unfortunately, the console currently forces you to update at that exact point, so it would kick you out of the game. Being in or out of the preview program has no effect on that.
