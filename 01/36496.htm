Hi Cortland10,

 

Thanks for coming back. For your concern about your Fit to Music you have done on your project, please be informed that after removing a photo, you need to click again Fit to Music option so that the pictures' duration will adjust depending on the music that is included and configured. If you do not click again that option, you will see that the music you added will be shortened.

 

Note : It would be best if you save your video as a project file first and make a back up of it so that if you want to revert to any prior changes you made you can still go back.

 

As for the saving, if you click Burn a DVD option, you will be asked to save the movie first in wmv format on your local disk before it will be burned using Windows DVD Maker. After burning, you just have to open again the project file of the movie you created and you can now save it again on the desired setting.

 

In case you have other concerns, feel free to post back.

 

Regards,

Richard

considerate_guy say: As mentioned by Richard, you can save your movie as many times as you like, each time in a different format if desired.  Just be sure to give the movie a different file name so you can tell them apart.  For example, you might want a high quality version for viewing on your PC, but a lower resolution (hence smaller file size) for sending via email, and you already mentioned wanting to save to DVD, so that is another option.  And don't forget that you also have the option to upload to various sharing sites like YouTube, Vimeo, SkyDrive, etc. where your friends and family could then stream the video to their PCs.
