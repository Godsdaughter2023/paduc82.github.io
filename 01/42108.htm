Hello Barath,

Thank you for posting your question on Microsoft community.

I appreciate you for providing details about the issue.

This issue may occur if CD/DVD drive may be incompatible with the Microsoft AHCI driver (msahci.sys) or CD/DVD media may be damaged or dirty.

If your CD/DVD drive is not compatible with the Microsoft AHCI driver (msahci.sys), your disk controller may be set to AHCI in the system BIOS. You may be able to resolve the issue by disabling AHCI mode for your disk controller in the BIOS. There are multiple BIOS manufacturers and BIOS versions, you should check your system documentation or visit your PC manufacturers website for instructions about how to change this setting.

Remove the DVD media and check for scratches, cracks or smudges that may make the DVD difficult to read, if the media is scratched or cracked, you must get replacement media. If the DVD is dirty or smudged, clean the DVD with warm water and a soft cloth. Make sure that the DVD is completely clean and dry before re-inserting into the CD/DVD drive. Next, restart the computer and press any key when you are prompted and start Setup.

Important notes:

I highly recommend backing up all of your important files and folders onto an external storage device before attempting any type of OS repair or upgrade.

Modifying BIOS/ complementary metal oxide semiconductor (CMOS) settings incorrectly can cause serious problems that may prevent your computer from booting properly.
