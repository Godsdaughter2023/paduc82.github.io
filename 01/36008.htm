I upgraded to Windows 10 and find that some documents no longer open - Microsoft Community

j w stuart say:

I upgraded to Windows 10 and find that some documents no longer open with the same application I used in Windows 7 or 8.1

Technical Level : Intermediate

Summary

You may find that Windows 10 does not always open a file with your preferred application. Here is how you can change which application opens a file.


Details

I will be using an Adobe .PDF document file for this example. As you may or may not have discovered after upgrading to Windows 10, when you click on a .pdf file expecting Adobe Reader to open and display the contents; instead you find that Microsoft’s Edge browser has opened the file.

Or you may be prompted (if no file association /app has been assigned) to choose what app you want to open .pdf documents and you selected the Edge Browser.

After trying the Edge browser you decide you want to use Adobe Reader when you open a .pdf file.

Right click on the .pdf file you want to view and select the ‘Open with’ option and in the list of apps that can open a .pdf file choose Adobe Reader.

After the .pdf file opens, in a few seconds you should be prompted to “Make Adobe Reader my default PDF application”, click the ‘OK’ and click ‘Yes’ when prompted’ to make Adobe the default application.

Now your .pdf documents will always open with Adobe Reader.

Want to have even more control over “Default apps”:

Click on Start / Settings / System and in the list of options, click on ‘Default apps’

Scroll down to the bottom of the ‘Default apps’ list and click on the “Choose default apps by file type” option.

A long list of file types (3 letter file extensions) and the default app that will open that file will be displayed. Scroll down until you reach the .pdf extension.

Located to the right of the .pdf file extension you will see the Microsoft Edge browser icon. Click on the icon and you should see a list of apps that can open an Adobe .pdf file. To use Adobe Reader to open your .pdf documents single click on the Adobe Reader icon.

Click on the Edge browser icon and you should see a list of apps that can open an Adobe .pdf file. To use Adobe Reader to open your .pdf documents single click on the Adobe Reader icon.

If you have a Desktop shortcut to a .pdf document you will immediately see the shortcut symbol change from the Edge browser logo to the Adobe Reader icon.

To change the default app back to the Edge browser, single click on the Adobe Reader icon and you will see a list of apps that can open a .pdf file.

Click on the Microsoft Edge icon to change the default.

If you have a Desktop shortcut to a .pdf document you will immediately see the shortcut symbol change from Adobe Reader to the Edge browser logo.

Once you have set the default app to what you like best, close out the ‘CHOOSE DEFAULT APPS BY FILE TYPE’ window.

The “Choose default apps by protocol” option is a great way of checking the Microsoft Store for an alternative app to open file, global program or networked devices like Xboxes, and email clients.

Just click on the icon and choose an app already on your device or check the Microsoft Store.

“Set defaults by app” is the last option in the list.

Similar to the Windows 7 Control Panel’s ‘Set Default Programs’ option, this is an extensive means of managing which files are handled by the apps installed on your computer. This option contains as shown in the screen shot below, a list of programs that may be available on your computer now or installed at a later date.

Click on the “Choose defaults for this program” link and select / place a check mark in the box for the file extensions you want the “Edge browser” as shown in this example to open. If you have made any changes click the ‘Save’ button in the lower right corner, otherwise click on ‘Cancel’ to exit.
