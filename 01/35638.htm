arminatorx say:

What exactly do you mean when you are "connected to your laptop over LAN wire"?

Are you connecting the Xbox to the laptop, and then the laptop to the internet? Then this is where the double NAT comes from. 

Your Router is a NAT device since it has to do "Network Address Translation" for the devices directly connected to the router that access the internet through one IP address, and if you connect your Xbox through your Laptop, then the Laptop is the second NAT device, as it has to translate it's own "internet-facing" IP address to the multiple devices (in theory you could connect more than just the Xbox to the Laptop through a network switch or a hub) connected to the laptop.

To resolve your double NAT issue with Laptop, you'll have to open the network interface settings by pressing Windowskey + R and then typing "ncpa.cpl" and pressing OK. 

In the network interface window, select both interfaces (the network Interface that connects the Laptop to the internet, and the interface that connects to the Xbox). If necessary, hold the CTRL key and click on each of the two interfaces to select both. 

Then right click on one of the selected interfaces, and choose to bridge these connections. 

This will then create a network bridge instead of a internet connection sharing that uses NAT.
