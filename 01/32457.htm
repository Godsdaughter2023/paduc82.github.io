Hi Johnny,

 

When an attachment arrives in an e-mail through the Windows 8 Mail app, you'll recognize it: It's a large rectangle at the top of your e-mail. The rectangle lists the file's name with the link “Download” listed directly beneath it.

 

To change the default location where the Mail App saves the attachments, you may try the following steps and check if that resolves the issue.

 

a)       Click the link “Download” next to the attached file. This tells the Mail app to actually download the file. Until you click the rectangle, the Mail app tells you only the attached file's name and file size. When the download completes, the rectangle turns into an icon representing the newly downloaded file.

b)       When the file downloads to the Mail app, click the attached file's icon and choose “Save”. Now, the Mail app will prepare to copy the file from your e-mail and save it to a folder in your computer.

c)        Choose a folder to receive the saved file. The Windows 8 File Picker appears now, letting you navigate to a folder of your choice.

d)       Click on “Files” in the top-left corner of the File Picker and then choose which library to receive the incoming file: Documents, Pictures, Music, or Videos.

e)       Click the “Save” button in the bottom-right corner of the File Picker.
