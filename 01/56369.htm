bossdweeb say: Your motherboard has only two SATA connections. One is already in use for the DVD drive, so
there would be no place to connect a second HDD. You would need a new HDD. A 1TB would
be the most cost effective at only about $15 - $20-  more than 500GB.

- Your machine supports 3GB/sec,  this is a SATA 2 connection. A SATA 3 drive will work but
at the slower rate of SATA 2.
- The more megabytes in the cache the better, but 32Mb fine.
- Green HDD's (of all brands ) are slower - 5400 rpm vs 7200 rpm, so they are best left to storage.

- Platter type HDD's  have always had variable life expectancy, as there can be occasional mechanical faults. 
There is a possibility some of the recent HDD's were not up to scratch as all the HDD factory's went
under water in the big Bangkok floods a while back, so maybe some of their 'seconds' were hitting
the market.  They have built new factories in the north and things should be back to normal regarding distribution and quality.
- With the upsurge in laptop use there would be more failures due to laptops being moved around
a lot. I've never had a HDD failure myself, and that includes one 80GB HDD that was in operation for
about eight years (starting to show signs of problems recently so is now used as a secondary external backup).
- SSD's would be the most reliable drive in a laptop that does not get handled with a lot of care.

- Customer feedback on on any forum (Newegg included)  will very often be imbalanced by negative feedback, imo. Happy customers are far less likely to do a review or ask questions at a forum.
One good example is forums for Nvidia and ATI graphics cards. If you were to read enough of these
you would be afraid to buy either.
An example of Newegg feedback is I've read comments from people who give two eggs for a
graphics card because they didn't find out it was too long for their machines case before they bought
it, or because they found their power supply was to weak to run it. Zero eggs for such reviewers....

- Hitachi came out on top for speed (and overall) in a recent HDD comparison in APC (Australian PC mag).
Samsung have been popular here for some time now.  I've used eight Western Digital HDD's over
the past eight years, both the 'Black' and 'Blue' models (black has longer warranty).
I also have a second hand Samsung 1TB that I use as my main external backup drive, but for the
most part the different brands are all equal regarding reliability. It changes year to year.
Some years Seagate is regarded a noisy (as was Hitachi a few years ago), and one brand may be slightly faster in one or two aspects, etc.
