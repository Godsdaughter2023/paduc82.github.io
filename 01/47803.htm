As for lip syncing I would say turn off any post processing features that are meant to clean up images on your TV MPEG, Noise, Color/Contrast enhancement turn it all off. Turn on a game mode if it has one. These are normally fine if your TV is putting out the sound since it will know how long it's taken to clean up an image so it can sync the audio correctly.

Using a surround sound speaker setup instead of built in TV speakers means sounds are played when they are received by the receiver. The image can then be cleaned up by the TV which can take a noticeable amount of time so the sound is always ahead of the TV image and so it's out of sync. Can also make gaming annoying since what your seeing on screen versus inputs from your controller can be out of sync as well.

As for not getting any sound from your surround sound speakers using LPCM uncompressed is this when playing a game, or just certain TV and Movies? Most games should provide full surround sound and a lot of them have in game audio settings to make the most out of a surround sound setup. If it's just certain TV, Streaming, Movies it could be they simply are stereo audio sources and your getting a one to one reproduction. I checked out the specs on your receiver and it does have modes to expand stereo sound sources into simulated surround.

It also lists in it's specs LPCM (2CH/5.1CH/7.1CH) thru HDMI : Yes (2CH / 5.1CH / 7.1CH) decoding. Which is what I posted you should use in your other thread.
