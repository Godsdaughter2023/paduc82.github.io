Hi

The advertised length of time that a battery can last on a full charge will vary depending on the load that is placed on the battery. Manufacturers will test the battery usage time based on a minimum load, such as surfing the internet. The manufacturer will usually state that "you can browse the internet for up to 12 hours". This is because just browsing will place a minimum load on the battery.

As soon as you place a heavy load on the system, such as palying a video DVD, Gaming, multitasking with many different pregrams, etc, etc, the time before the battery runs down will be substantially less than the advertised length of time.

This is why most battery meters do not display the 'time left' before the batteries charge is depleted. The time could show 10 hours left and then you place a heavy load on the system and that time might immediately drop down to 3 or 4 hours, or less.

The best way to keep an eye on the battery is to use the percent of charge remaining.

Most modern portable devices use Li-ion batteries. If you frequently drain a lithium-ion battery, and then recharge it, it can quickly lose its ability to hold a charge, which affects the accuracy of the battery meter. The practice of completely draining the battery, and then fully charging it, is advice that applied to nickel cadmium (NiCd) batteries and, to a lesser extent, nickel metal hydride (NiMH) batteries. Lithium-ion batteries last longer if you charge them often, a little at a time, to maintain a minimum charge of about 40 percent capacity.

Li-ion batteries are built to last appx. 1 to 3 years. These batteries have an extraneous circuit that will shut down the portable device when the charge reaches a minimum level, usually somewhere bet 40% down to 20%. This prevents damage that can occur to the charging circuit which could render the batteries charging circuit inoperable.

Recommended resolution

This error means that Windows is in the process of removing the device. However, the device has not yet been completely removed. Here are some things that you can try to resolve this problem.

  • Wait several seconds, and then press the F5 key to update the Device Manager view.

OEM versions of Windows 7 are identical to Full License Retail versions except for the following:

- OEM versions do not offer any free Microsoft direct support from Microsoft support personnel

- OEM licenses are tied to the very first computer you install and activate it on

- OEM versions allow all hardware upgrades except for an upgrade to a different model motherboard

OEM licenses are to be installed by professional system manufacturers only. Under Microsoft's OEM License Agreement, they are not to be sold to end-users under any circumstance, and are to be preinstalled on a computer using the OEM Preinstallation Kit (OPK) before shipment to the customer, and must include at the very least the manufacturer's support contact information.
