    I would suggest download the Windows 10 media creation tool and burn an install dvd. You may have to use another computer to do this.
    

    

    On Disk Management, check your hard drive for errors and let Windows fix the errors. (you can also right click the hard drive,properties, select check drive for errors.)
    

    

    In bios, I would also check your memory for errors.
    

    

    Once you have the Windows 10 install DVD (make sure it is the correct one..64 bit or 32), you can then do a clean install. Save all the files you want to save to a usb or dvd.
    

    It will automatically activate since you already installed it as an upgrade.
    

    

    Then go to the Laptop mfg website and download update Win 10 drivers (note 32 or 64 bit).
    

    Go to your WIFI modem mfg and download windows 10 drivers.
    

    

    You need to verify your cpu temperature to eliminate it as a cause. Consider Hardware Monitor (free-open source) to provide realtime temps. Also look at your voltages in bios,,they should be within 5% of spec for the computer to work properly.
