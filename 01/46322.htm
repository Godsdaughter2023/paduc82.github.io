jmh3143 say:
Re a BCCode: 116

"It's not a true crash, in the sense that the Blue Screen was initiated only because the combination of video driver and video hardware was being unresponsive, and not because of any synchronous processing exception".

Since Vista, the "Timeout Detection and Recovery" (TDR) components of the OS video subsystem have been capable of doing some truly impressive things to try to recover from issues which would have caused earlier OS's like XP to crash.

As a last resort, the TDR subsystem sends the video driver a "please restart yourself now!" command and waits a few seconds.
If there's no response, the OS concludes that the video driver/hardware combo has truly collapsed in a heap, and it fires off that stop 0x116 BSOD.

If playing with video driver versions hasn't helped, make sure the box is not overheating.
 Try removing a side panel and aiming a big mains fan straight at the motherboard and GPU.
 Run it like that for a few hours or days - long enough to ascertain whether cooler temperatures make a difference.

If so, it might be as simple as dust buildup and subsequently inadequate cooling.

I would download cpu-z and gpu-z (both free) and keep an eye on the video temps.

Maximum microSD size

barb bowman say:


Thank you for posting your query on Microsoft Community. For your concern, the current maximum capacity of microSD card that the device can support is up to 128GB and any brand will do.
     

Where is the 128GB limit documented?

From https://www.microsoft.com/surface/en-us/support/storage-files-and-folders/surface-storage-options?os=windows-10&=undefined

"Removable storage options

You can use USB storage ora microSD card of any size with your Surface to store your documents, music, videos, and pictures. Surface works with standard microSD card formats—microSD, microSDHC, and microSDXC. If you have a Surface Book, you can also use a full-size SD card.

"

SDHC support is limited to 32GB, while SDXC is up to 2TB (2048 GB).

and support is quoting even a specific model of a Sandisk 200GB microSD card http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/surface/forum/surfpro4-surfstorage/how-to-get-the-most-out-of-the-microsd-card-reader/a9807a91-4887-4206-9e9e-60e6534342a0

"

I spent a lot of time searching for this. I'm hoping this will help others.

I just called Tech Support for Microsoft and confirmed that the MicroSD slot on the Surface Pro 4 i7 is only UHS-I compatible. They quoted that the 200GB Sandisk (probably this one: http://www.amazon.ca/SanDisk-Ultra-200GB-Micro-SDSDQUAN-200G-G4A/dp/B00V62XBQQ) can get read speeds of 80MB/s and write speeds of 20MB/s.

Bottom line: don't waste money on a UHS-II card because you will get only UHS-I speeds."

vijay a. verma say:
A. Try to boot into safe mode

Power ON your computer and as Windows / manufacturer's Logo appears, Power OFF - Repeat 3 times
Now your computer will go into Advanced Recovery Environment
Advanced Options > Troubleshoot > Advanced Options > Startup Settings > Restart
Press 4 or F4 to enable Safe Mode (5 or F5 to enable Safe Mode with Networking)
Log in once Windows starts in safe mode

B. Another option when you are in Advanced Options in previous step is to choose (Note - You won't lose any files in this step)
1. System Restore - This will restore your system to a point when things were working fine.
OR
2. Startup Repair - Startup Repair upgrade fixes all Windows errors and retains all files, applications and settings.

C. Final option could be Reset this PC.
In Step A > Troubleshoot > Reset this PC - Keep my files and Remove everything. Choose this carefully as most likely you would like to keep your files.
All apps will be removed and windows will be reinstalled.
