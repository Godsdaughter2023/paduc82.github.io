ken blake - microsoft mvp say:

On Sat, 10 Mar 2012 17:30:31 +0000, Linda-AnneRoss wrote:

Whenever I try to send my resume as an attachment - the receiver can't open the attachment - I'm not quite sure what to do?

When you send someone a file, whether it's a resume or any other type
of file, and whether it's as an e-mail attachment or any other way,
you need to be sure that it's a type of file for which the recipient
has an appropriate program installed to open it.

A wps file is a Microsoft Works file, and not everyone has Microsoft
Works installed. Especially if you are dealing with a company to whom
you are sending a resume, it's very likely that they don't have Works.

Works should let you save your resume in rtf format instead of wps. Do
a "save as" instead of a "save," and choose rtf. If you then send the
rtf file, almost certainly the recipient will be able to open it;
almost everyone can read rtf files.

Ken Blake, Microsoft MV

ken blake say:

Yes, but two points:

1. You don't need to reformat the drive before installing Windows. Just do a clean installation, which will begin by formatting the drive.

2. If you are backing up the entire drive, you are backing up more than you need to. After reinstalling Windows, you can copy all your data files back to the internal drive, but if it was your intent to also restore your programs, with rare exceptions, you can *not* do this. That's because the great majority of programs have many references and components within the Registry and else within \Windows. All of those will be lost when you reinstall Windows, and most restored  programs won't work. Programs need to be reinstalled from their original media, so all you really needed to backup was your data files..
