AFAIK, you don't "deactivate" it.  A retail Windows XP package (CD with product key) can be transferred to anyone.   After it's installed, the new owner needs to "activate" the installation.  If the new owner's attempt at activation is more than 120 days after the last time you activated an installation with this product key, the automatic on-line activation system should work OK.  If it is less than 120, the new owner will have to activate by phone and explain that he or she purchased the product from you and that you removed it from your computer.

Transfer to Third Party. The initial user of the Software may make a one-time permanent transfer of this EULA and Software to another end user, the end user receiving the Software must agree to all the EULA terms.

Transfer to Third Party. The initial user of the Product may make a one-time transfer of the Product to another end user. The transfer may not be an indirect transfer, such as a consignment. Prior to the transfer, the end user receiving the transferred Product must agree to all the EULA terms. No Rental. You may not rent, lease, lend or provide commercial hosting services to third parties with the Product.

If you need to bring up the Xbox Startup Troubleshooter again, follow these steps:

  1. Power off your console, and then unplug the power cord to ensure that the console is completely powered off.
  2. Wait 30 seconds, then plug the power cord back in.
  3. Press and hold the BIND button (located on the left side of the console) and the EJECT button (located on the front of the console), and then press the Xbox button on the console.

  6. The console should power up and take you directly to the Xbox Startup Troubleshooter.
