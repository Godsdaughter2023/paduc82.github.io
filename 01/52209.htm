mozzeralla firefox say:

I'm assuming you're using a laptop.

I'm not 100% certain but I think it's due to the "Fast startup" option. I have Windows 10 and I checked my Event Viewer and noticed the same thing. After turning off "Fast startup" the system didn't log a sleep at all. Shutdown felt noticeably faster, but the boot time suffered a lot.

From what I recall from other threads, about fast startup, is that upon shutdown the system saves a boot-able image of itself. The image is used upon startup which dramatically increases the boot time, I believe it acts as a "resume" function although this is an oversimplification of what actually happens.

To enable/disable feature you need to go to: Control Panel\Hardware and Sound\Power Options\System Settings and click "Choose what the power button does", then click "change settings that are currently unavailable-admin". Scroll down and check/uncheck.

Or you could just right click on the battery icon in your taskbar and click power options and you know the rest.

billion debbarma say:

Hi,

Thank you for coming back to us.

To switch the email user account at first you need to convert the current Microsoft account to local account and try log-in with Office 365 ID. Follow the below steps.

  1. Go to settings and click on Accounts.
  2. Click on Your email and accounts and click on Sign-in with a local account instead.
  3. Enter the password, click on Next and follow the onscreen instruction to convert into local account.

Once the user account is converted to the local account, you can convert the local account to Microsoft account using Office 365.

Hope this help. Keep posting us with the status we will be glad to help you.
