HP LaserJet 2200d Printer Driver for Windows 7 - Answer

FYI - Got a new HP laptop with Windows 7 Home Premium. I was having trouble with the HP LaserJet 2200d driver that came with Windows 7. The driver that comes with Windows 7 is for the HP LaserJet 2200 PCL5 (which is an older driver, and doesn't let you tell the Printer Config in Windows that you have duplex, and that you have multiple input paper trays). After some troubleshooting and exploring, I installed the HP LaserJet UPD PCL6, from the HP website. Bingo! The PCL6 driver lets you configure the printer as duplex and configure the printer with Tray2, Tray3, etc.

jack mvp say:

Hi

Computer's ports are always open, what block (“close”) the ports are the Software Firewall and Router.

If you disable the computer's Security Center, all the ports are opened on the specific computer.

If the computer is on a Network, the ports are opened toward the Network.

If the Network is supported by a Router, the ports are opened toward the LAN, but closed toward the Internet.

If the computer is by itself with No Router and no firewall connected directed to a regular bridged modem, it probably will not last a day without being corrupted from the Internet in.

*Note. If the auto-update is On, at times it might affect the Firewall settings  as part of the update.

On Port forwarding

Ports are forwarded as needed by a specific application (usually the application support will inform which ports need to be use)

Ports have to be forwarded toward the specific computer's IP that is running the application.

A specific port can only be forwarded toward one computer.

Computer's ports are always open, what block (“close”) the ports are the Software Firewall and Router.

Forwarding the needed ports through the Firewall/Router means Unblocking them from being “Closed”.

The ports numbers have to be forwarded in the Router, and be allowed through the software Firewall.

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows-vista/Open-a-port-in-Windows-Firewall

If you use a 3rd, party software firewall read its manual.

As for Routers, the instructions depends on the specific Router that you have (the manual might be helpful).

This site is the champion of port forwarding for Routers, http://portforward.com/
