olipo186 say: That is not true.

When using either an eSATA or USB 3.0 link between the PC and an external drive, the bottleneck is most certainly the hard drive itself. Not the link. USB 3.0 supports a transfer rate of 5 Gbps, which corresponds to 625 MB/s. The fastest SSDs on the market today delivers ~550 MB/s read and write speed. And those are SSDs! Regular drives are way slower.

bornunderpunches say:

Recording device not found after Windows 10 update

Hello,

Last week (on March 14th), my version of Windows 10 got upgraded. Everything seemed fine at first, but then I noticed something was missing - the recording device that I had used before the update (Stereo Mix) was not listed at all under Recording devices, and the three devices that were listed under there (two different "Microphone"s and "Line In") were both disconnected. Because of this, I can't make calls on Skype (I screenshare often in my calls, and I had to use the Stereo Mix to play audio directly). 

What can I do to fix this? I've searched as much as I can, but this seems to have happened to nobody else.

a. user say:

Hi,

With regard to your query, we suggest that you install the most recent driver for your audio and sound controllers. This issue may happen if haven't installed appropriate driver for your device. To do this, please follow the steps below:

  1. Press Windows + X keys.
  2. Select Device Manager.
  3. Expand the Sound, video and game controllers category.
  4. Right-click the device driver and select Update Driver Software.
  5. Select Search automatically for updated driver software.
  6. If Windows doesn't find a new driver, you can try looking for one on the device manufacturer's website and follow their instructions.

If these steps don't work, try reinstalling the driver:

  1. Open Device Manager.
  2. Right-click the name of the device, and select Uninstall.
  3. Restart your PC and Windows will attempt to reinstall the driver.

Update us with the result.
