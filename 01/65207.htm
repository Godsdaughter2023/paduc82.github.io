aj don say:

Hello Niraj,

The newest Surface Pro running on Intel® Core™ 7th-generation m3 have a 128 GB SSD storage capacity. However, the storage capacity shows less than what was advertised due to several components. First is how the manufacturers advertise the hard disk capacity. They count 1 gigabyte as 1,000,000,000 bytes and Windows would read 1 gigabyte as 1,024,000,000 bytes. Basically, an advertised solid state drive having a capacity of 128 GB would be read around 125 GB minus the recovery partition and other shadow files, giving the 117 GB storage capacity.  The 98 GB we see on the link you provided would be after Windows 10 is installed, which is at least 16 GB for 32-bit OS, 20 GB for 64-bit OS and drivers, which still doesn't include the updates for Surface Pro, leaving you with 80 GB storage capacity for your music, photos, videos, and other documents.

Hope this helps.
