lemp say:
If you don't see "Safe Mode" in the four corners of the screen (along with the Windows version information at the top of the screen), you're not in safe mode.

Your screen resolution has been changed.

Right-click on an empty part of the desktop and select Properties.  Click the Settings tab.  In the box labeled "Screen Resolution," click on the slider and move it to the right until you get something more appropriate for you monitor (you can try 1024x768).  Click Apply.   If the setting is appropriate for your monitor, you'll get a box asking if you want to keep the new settings.  If you don't respond (or if you get an "out of scan range" message), the screen will go back to its previous setting in several seconds.

If you can't do the above, it's possible that your video driver may have become corrupted.  You'll have to identify your video card and get the current driver.  If you have a brand name computer (Dell, HP, etc), go to the manufacturer's web site and enter your computer information and search for the video driver.  Otherwise, open Device Manager (Start > Run > devmgmt.msc > OK), identify the video adapter, and then go to the web site of the adapter's manufacturer for the driver.
