Hi nseibel14,

Your system has a 128GB SSD Drive and a 1TB hard drive

Since they are physically separate drives, you cannot extend C into D, it is just not possible . . .

To recover your D Drive:

Open Disc Management

Right click the unallocated space (marked in black)

Choose New - Simple Volume

Accept the default size

When asked to assign a drive letter, accept the default

Once that is done, your D Drive should be back in use . . .

    Why is this happening?

    Thanks,

    keith a. miller say:

    Saved Pictures is a defined System folder (just like Documents, Pictures, etc). Normally it's created as a subfolder of Pictures, but it sounds like yours has been redirected to the Desktop.

    The next time it appears, don't delete it, but instead open it's Properties. select the Location tab, and either type a path similar to:

    C:\Users\keith\Pictures\Saved Pictures

    or use the Move button to navigate to Pictures and create a New Folder named Saved Pictures.

    If you don't want it as a separate folder, just select your existing Pictures folder.
