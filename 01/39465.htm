slance310 say:

Hi,


There is nothing special about CD\DVD drive drivers, they are pretty much standard and are included with all versions of windows... there is no need to find another one.....


One exception might be if the CD\DVD drive is a LightScribe drive... If so, try uninstalling the LightScribe software and see if that helps...


It is possible that you have a program on your machine that is confusing the installer... Burning software of some type possibly... or iTunes...


You by chance didn't some how change your bios to some kind of RAID setup, or switch from AHCI to IDE or vice versa ?


I've also included a registry fix that's used for Win 8\8.1 machines that lose their drive after installation...

I suppose that the same type of filter may be interfering with your installation...


You can look in the registry for UPPER and LOWER FILTERS at this key...


[HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Control\Class\{4d36e965-e325-11ce-bfc1-08002be10318}


The filters will be listed on the right side after clinking on the key...


Anything listed there is a potential problem, but all filters don't cause a problem...only some...


Deleting the filter will usually fix the problem, but may cause one of your programs to not work properly... You can usually do a Bing or Google search for the file in the filter, and identify which program it goes with...


Then try updating the program... As a last resort, delete it...
