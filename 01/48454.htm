What’s changing?

·         Game clips that are 90 days or older, and have 0 total views will be deleted from Xbox Live starting today

·         Going forward, clips uploaded to Xbox Live that are 30 days old with 0 total views will be automatically deleted (all-time views, not a rolling window)

·         GameDVR storage capacity will be doubled for all Xbox Live users

Why the change?

There are currently 700M user generated game clips stored on Xbox Live that are 90 days or older and have zero total views. Storing these clips not only costs a significant amount of money each month, but it also can affect performance for various services. To effectively scale our services heading into the holiday season, we will be purging these game clips over the coming months. This process will take time and we’re targeting January 2019 for completion.

The new retention policy of 30 days & 0 total views is in place to prevent this buildup of non-engaged content in the future. It also means we’re able to increase the allotted storage capacity for all users as we will cut down on overall storage costs while increasing the amount of engaging content stored on Xbox Live.

What do users need to do?

If your uploaded game clip has 1 total view (including views by you), no action is required. Game clips that are uploaded to Xbox Live and have not received at least 1 view within 30 days of uploading will be at risk of deletion going forward. Either watch the clip or follow these steps to back-up your captures to OneDrive, or PC. Note: A view is counted after watching 60% of a clip.
