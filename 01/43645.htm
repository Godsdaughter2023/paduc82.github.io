Alright, let me explain a bit so clear up any confusion.

M rating might coincide to 17 years old, but for Microsoft any account under the age of 18 is considered a "minor" and can't download game demo's that have that "M" rating (since they don't have a 17 age limit on their adult status). I don't think any game that's a demo can be allowed regardrless of parental permission, I believe that Microsoft automatically defaults this to M (sort of like the situation with Resident Evil 6 public release demo).

This is the blurb from EA's own website for Warfighter:

What is the ESRB rating for Medal of Honor Warfighter?

We do not have an official ESRB rating yet for Medal of Honor Warfighter, but keeping with the authenticity of Medal of Honor 2010, we anticipate a Mature rating for Blood, Intense Language, and Violence. 

-

Anyone can technically purchased an M rated game, a parent can purchase a disc copy of the game for their child regardless of age. This age limitation is put forth by EA on their own servers, so anyone that buys the disc copy of the game and has an account that is over the age of 13 will be able to connect to EA's online server (though they technically shouldn't be playing the game due to the rating). All EA games have a online rating of 13+ to be able to connect to their online servers (Battlefield, Medal of Honor, Madden, FIFA, NHL, etc).

However, there are companies, for example Activision that don't follow those same rules where any child can get a game (again purchased by whomever, a parent, and adult, etc) and be able to play their game if their 12 or 11 or what have you. Age to them isn't an issue in terms of being able to play the game.

2. Do one of the following:

• If your computer has a single operating system installed, press and hold the F8 key as your computer restarts. You need to press F8 before the Windows logo appears.
