divya r say:

Hi Kit Y, 

These are the possible causes for this issue to occur: 

  • Media Center has been unable to download the new listings.
  • Data is not available for your location from the Microsoft TV Listings service provider.
  • Media Center determined the existing data on your computer to be expired or corrupt. 

The Guide information is downloaded periodically over the Internet from your Guide listing service provider. You are notified that "No data is available" in the Guide if either of the following things happen:

  • Windows Media Center cannot connect to the Internet and download the new Guide information for an extended period of time, and the Guide information that was previously downloaded is corrupt or has expired.
  • Guide information is not available for your location from the Guide listings service provider.

I suggest you to setup the Guide Listings. Follow these steps to do the same:

Step 1: To set up Guide listings 

To find your favorite programs more easily, you can configure your Guide listings to match your local service provider's guide listings.

1.  On the start screen, scroll to Tasks, click settings, click TV, click Guide, and then click Set Up Guide Listings.

2.  Follow the directions on the screen to set up Guide listings for your area.

If you are asked to select a TV service provider, and you're not sure which one to select, check a recent bill or statement from your TV service provider to find the appropriate information.

The providers listed in Windows Media Center vary by ZIP or Postal Code. If the provider you subscribe to is not listed for the ZIP or Postal Code you entered, try entering codes for nearby areas to find the appropriate Guide listings.

Follow this step to download the latest Guide Listing:

Step 2: To download the latest Guide listings 

You need to connect to the Internet and initiate a download to receive Guide updates. For the most up-to-date information, it is recommended that you connect to the Internet and get Guide listings at least once per day.

1. On the start screen, scroll to Tasks, click settings, click TV, click Guide, and then click Get Latest Guide Listings.

2. Click Yes to confirm that you want to download the latest Guide listings.

Refer this link for additional assistance:

Use the Guide in Windows Media Center

Hey gonedog,

 

Here are certain things you may try to fix the burning problem. 

 

1.     Run troubleshooter to check for common issues such as what kind of disc you've inserted in your disc drive and whether the drive supports playing or burning content to a disc.

 

Refer:

 

Open the Playing and Burning CDs, DVDs, and Blu‑ray Discs troubleshooter

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows7/Open-the-playing-and-burning-cds-dvds-and-bluray-discs-troubleshooter

 

2.     Try creating a CD or DVD using a blank CD/DVD from a different manufacturer. Sometimes certain CD-R and CD-RW drives have issues with writable CD/DVD  media from a particular manufacturer.

 

3.     Turn off any programs that might activate themselves while burning is in progress such as your anti-virus programs.

 

4.     Make sure the CD/DVD drive has all the updated drivers from the hardware manufacturer.

 

5.     If you have any other burning software such as iTunes, Nero, Roxio and Zune, etc, please uninstall each and every one of them and test again.

 

6.     The CD/DVD drive may be encountering buffering errors because of the write speed of the burn. To resolve this issue, select a slower write speed.

 

To reduce the write speed for the burner

 

a.     In Windows Media Player, on the  Tools menu, click Options.

b.     In the Options dialog box, on the  Devices tab, click the name of the CD/DVD  drive you are using, and then click the Properties button.

c.     In theProperties dialog box, on the Recording tab, select a slower write speed.

 

If you continue to encounter this error after reducing the write speed to the smallest (slowest) value, do one or more of the following.

 

7.     Try uninstalling and reinstalling the drive.

 

Go to device manager to do so:

 

a.      Go to Start, typedevmgmt.msc  and press Enter.

b.     Right-Click on the CD/DVD drive and select “Uninstall”.

c.     Restart the computer and test on those blank CDs/DVDs.

 

8.     Get the latest firmware for your burner. Find the updates from the Manufacturer’s support section. For help, refer:

 

Update drivers: recommended links

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows7/Update-drivers-recommended-links

 

Note: Running the computer without firewall or the anti-virus software is a potential threat to the computer; make sure to enable the security software after completing the troubleshooting steps and after identifying the issue.

Regards,

Shinmila H - Microsoft Support
