The problem could be in Real Player or in the video files you downloaded.

Real will download several types of video files, but YouTube ones usually are flash .flv video files. I am using 64-bit Vista and the most recent Real player, so details may be a bit different on other OSs. Go to start > your personal folder name at upper right of menu that comes up > videos > Real Player Downloads. Then right click the icon for a video that gives problems and select properties. You likely will find Flash Video (flv). Real will play isolated .flv files, but some people have another player installed that will play .flv. If you do, right click a video icon and select open with to bring up a menu. If another player you have that will play flv comes up, select that. If your other flv player is not listed, select "choose default program" and search for your other flv player. Before you select this player, make sure you uncheck the box labeled " always use the selected program ---". If your second flv player works properly, you know the problem is with the Real Player. If the second flv player also has problems, then the problem is most likely with the flv. It could be corrupted in some way by either Real or YouTube.

If you still have problems and have no second flv player, you might try removing the Real Player at add/remove and re-downloading and installing it. This sometimes will remove a corrupted program. I just checked the videos I have downloaded with Real and they are all working properly even though I have the most recent version of Real.

Ok, please do the following.

  2. In the left hand menu, expand Windows Logs and then click System.
  3. Wait for it to populate and then right click System and go to "Filter Current Log".

cmdrkeene say:

The new mail client is only a reader that reads an online email account like Gmail, Yahoo, MSN, Hotmail, Outlook, or work/school/university accounts like Exchange.  You can't import offline-stored messages into it directly.

One easy solution would be to create the same folders in your Outlook/Hotmail (whatever, it's all the same service by different names).  Then in your WML program, copy the emails back into your online account there.  Then the Windows 10 mail app will see and download it from the online server.

cmdrkeene say:

I'd just create the folders, then go into one of your storage folders, ctrl-a to "select all", then drag it to the online folder so it will sync over to your Windows 10 app via the online email service.

And even without doing this, you can always continue seeing those messages in your WLM program.  The program won't download new mail from the Hotmail server, but everything you dragged into Storage Folders is local in the app stored on the hard drive.  You could just keep that for your older archive, which may be increasingly rare to use over time goes on.
