In Windows 7, an administrator is more akin to what used to be referred to as a power user rather than an actual administrator of the computer. The rights you require are now referred to as elevated privileges and are granted by right clicking your menu item or desktop shortcut and selecting Run as administrator.

This new approach to what entails an administrator is probably due to a large number of end-users assigning themselves administrator rights not because they ever actually needed them, but simply because they could have them. This by itself is not an issue, but when the same end-users get onto malicious web sites and click everything that says Free Download, any malware or viruses that result from a poor choice have the administrative rights of the end-user who initiated the action.

So Microsoft decided to put certain actions into an elevation privileges category. The biggest problem is that they had to reuse the term Administrator which leads one to ask "Why do I have to Run as administrator if I am an administrator?" It starts to sound like something written by Joseph Heller.
