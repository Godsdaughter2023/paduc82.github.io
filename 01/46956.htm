horizons1 say:

I am going to share my solution as I came across this thread when upgrading Windows 10 to version 1903. Suddenly, my happy system was throwing the BSOD and the DRIVER IRQL NOT LESS OR EQUAL jraid.sys error. Here's my particular situation - it might help others:

I have a Gigabyte GA-P55M-UD2 motherboard circa 2010. The board has six SATA ports, four controlled by the Intel P55 chipset and two (gray colored) GSATA ports controlled by - you guessed it - jraid.

After a day of messing with BIOS it turned out that simply moving my drives off the GSATA ports solved my boot issue. With no drives connected to the GSATA ports, jraid.sys is not invoked, and I can go eat lunch.

smwutches say:

1. No. You can only download the games via the console. You can redeem the codes via the PC, but that will queue the games to download to your console.

2. & 3. Anything you purchase is tied to your account so you can redownload it for free at any time. There is no legit way to backup the download by say saving it to a disc to keep elsewhere.

a. user say:

actually you can use a USB storage device to download games, and then you can remove the storage device, which would sort of be a way to "backup" the games.  once you remove the USB device you should be able to download the games again to your internal hard drive.  i'm not 100% sure it works like this, but i believe so.  however, there is really no need to do this because, as mentioned above, you can always redownload the games again to any console using your microsoft account connected to xbox live.

the only reason to backup games to another storage device would be if they announced xbox live servers for xbox one were closing down, or you don't plan to have access to an internet connection anymore, and you didn't have enough room to store all your games on the internal drive.

The files listed in the Movie Maker MRU list are .mswmm
project files...it just makes them easier to find when you
wish to re-edit them.

OK...you say the offending file has been deleted from
your system.

In Movie Maker...if you go to File and left click the unwanted
entry in the MRU list you should see a dialog that says it
cannot find the file. When you close the dialog the entry
should disappear from the list.
