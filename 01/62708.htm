The process would work, but it would be tedious and boring.

The best way to backup is with third-party backup software, which makes it easy to:

  • define and save several different backup jobs that you can run with one click or tap
  • run backup jobs automatically, every day, on your schedule (even overnight while you sleep)
  • keep a rotating history of backups, e.g., the last week's backups, the last 10 backups, etc.
  • verify the accuracy of a backup, at any time
  • send you email (if you wish) to let you know that a backup has succeeded or failed

This makes backing up practically effortless.

Microsoft announced that System Image Backup is deprecated, which means it won't be developed any further and will likely be removed. Its replacement is third-party backup software.

File History backs up individual files, but it was not intended to replace third-party backup software.

Backup and Restore (Windows 7) is intended for restoring backups made on Windows 7. It's not supported for making new backups.

System Restore is not a backup.

Yes, it would work. The restore and transfer process is identical to the backup process--drag and drop or copy and paste.

Is this a good way to do backups? Maybe. It has advantages and disadvantages.

Advantages

    . No special software is required.

    . There's no learning curve

     . If there's a second operating system involved, you don't have

     to worry about compatibility with its operating system.

Disadvantages

       . Normally no file compression, so it may take up more disk space

       . No option to do incremental backups

     . If there's a second operating system involved, you *do* have

     to worry about compatibility with its operating system.

So it might or might not be a good choice for you. You need to evaluate the points above and decide for yourself.
