a. user say:

Well, methinks I have been lucky enough to find a solution on the community.

I performed the following and ever since, that particular error no longer appears.

The following is NOT my solution but one i found by reading and searching.

In the registry search for F72671A9-012C-4725-9D2F-2A4D32D65169 (HKEY_CLASSES_ROOT\APPID)
When you find it - right click and take the permissions option.  
On the screen that then appears, take the advanced option.
Change the owner to "Administrators" (assuming you are part of that group)
Then check names and OK - so that the owner value appears as "Administrators".
On the main permissions screen, add "Administrators" if its not there and make sure that you have Full Control and Read.

OK this
Now go and change the owner back to to "NT SERVICE\trustedinstaller" and check the name and accept, so its back to normal
Then in Component Services (dcomcnfg), go to DCOM and scroll down to the bottom of the list to find appid {F72671A9-012C-4725-9D2F-2A4D32D65169}.

Add "system" to the local activation, tick all the boxes.

And the error seems to go away forever more.

       2. Select Network Settings, Configure Network.

       1. If it's off, turn on the Windows Media Center PC.
       2. Click Start, Run, then type CMD and press Enter.
       3. At the command prompt, type PING and the IP address your wrote down (for example, PING 192.168.1.9), then press Enter.
       4. If you see a series of replies, then your PC "sees" your console. If you see a series of "Request timed out" responses, then it can't find your console.

        * TCP ports 3390, 3932, and 5555.
