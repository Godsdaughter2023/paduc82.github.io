a. user say:

The message "Your connection is not private" usually comes from your web browser. It means that the website you want to reach is not setup to communicate with your computer securely, so any exchange between you and the website can be observed and intercepted by a third party. It's up to the website in question to setup a secure server; there's nothing you can do about it besides complaining to the website's owner.

davidcoop3r say:

When trying to access a work required website, if I shut down computer and restart, it will not allow access.  Message is NOT SECURE - Your connection is not private.  I regularly use this site for business.  Sometimes if I can get access, if I shut down, I am blocked again.  I a\have allowed popups and flash exceptions for this site (and a couple others that I also have this issue with).

Getting "Your connection is not private" in Google Chrome and "Your connection is not secure" in Mozilla Firefox are the same. This error is often caused by some following things:

  • Invalid cookies and cached files on your browser: In this case, you need to clean up all those things. Reset browser if you can. Make sure to backup your browser profiles to save bookmarks and saved passwords.
  • Wrong date and time on your device: Make sure the date and time on your computer (or smartphone) are correct. If this value is incorrect, your browser can't verify the SSL certificate and lead to this error.
  • Antivirus or firewall block SSL connections: Your SSL connection can't be blocked by antivirus software or firewall and lead to "Your connection is not private" error. In this case, you can test it by turning off those apps temporarily. Besides, there is a feature on your antivirus / internet security application can cause this issue, called SSL scanning or SSL filtering, depending on the application. You can turn this feature off to get rid of this issue.
  • SSL certificate is expired or configured in a wrong way: The SSL certificate of the website you are accessing could be expired or set up in a wrong way. In this case, you can't do anything to get rid of it, except bypass. Use the "--ignore-certificate-errors" tag and add to Google Chrome shortcut to bypass this error.

Also, you can bypass this error temporarily by clicking on the Advanced link and then choose the "Proceed to the website..." option to access the website normally.
