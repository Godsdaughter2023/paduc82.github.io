Computers, which are built by large manufactures that come with Windows Pre-Installed, come with two (2) Product Keys:

 

A)   OEM SLP: This key comes pre-installed in Windows, when it comes from the Factory. This key is geared to work with the special instructions found only on that Manufacturer's computer hardware. B)    COA SLP: This is the Product key that you see on the sticker on the side (or bottom,[ or inside the battery compartment]) of your computer. It is a valid product key, but should only be used in limited situations (such as if the OEM SLP key stops self-activating for whatever reason). The key must be activated by Phone. considerate_guy say:

The preview mode is just that, a preview.  If Movie Maker has to lower the resolution to keep up with displaying your photos in near real-time or to reduce memory used, it may.  On the other hand, when you save your project as a final movie (File > Save movie) it will then use the full power of your computer (CPU and GPU) to generate the movie in the specified resolution and bitrate.

 

If you want to look at your movie on the full screen, finish your edits, save the movie and play it full screen in your media player (e.g. Windows Media Player).

considerate_guy say:

I see what you mean.  It appears that when a caption is applied, then Movie Maker renders differently in full screen mode.  It seems to take whatever is shown in the smaller preview window and do a plain scaling.  But yes, the first time it just showed the photo in its original resolution.

 

However, when I did save the final movie, it had the full resolution again... so yes, you have identified there is an issue with the way the full screen preview is rendered when text is applied.  But fortunately the final movie is unaffected.

 

One workaround I found was to resize the preview window as much as possible before switching to full screen.  That at least then uses that scale as the initial resolution before scaling to full screen.  To do that, just drag the vertical dividing line further to the right.

 

Maybe one of the moderators can escalate this to the Movie Maker developers and get a better answer as to why the full screen preview behaves that way.
