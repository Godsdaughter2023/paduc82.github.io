cmdrkeene say:

You can remove your password from the account in the PC Settings app (just use blank or no password for your "new password).

 

One note though, this is only possible if you do not sign-into your computer using your online Microsoft Account.  If needed, you can unlink your account (convert it to a local account) from the same PC Settings app.  Keep in mind that a local account can't take advantage of PC-to-PC setting synchronization and a few other features.  If you only have one PC this might not matter to you much, and of course you can still setup your email, skydrive, and whatever else individually in those apps.

 

And I would be remiss if I did not point out a security consideration.  Your PC may never leave your house normally, but what if someone was to steal it?  If you ever visited your bank website, or other confidential/provide site and used the 'remember my password' feature, the thief could get into your bank account.  And even if you didn't use the "remember my password" feature, they could always use the forgotten-password link on your bank website and have it email you a new password. Since they have your PC, they would get that email and be into all your stuff.  Just a strong argument to always use a password.  But of course, you are not forced to.

1. Click start-Type Folder options->Open Folder Options. Click on the view tab. Select  Show hidden files and folders. Click Ok.

   Note

If SFC could not fix something, then run the command again to see if it may be able to the next time. Sometimes it may take running the sfc /scannow command 3 times restarting the PC after each time to completely fix everything that it's able to.

If still not, then you could do a refresh of Windows 8, or run a System Restore using a restore point dated before the bad file occured to fix it. You may need to repeat doing a System Restore until you find a older restore point that may work.

"How to Refresh Windows 8 and Windows 8.1"

  • The PC boots into Windows RE (Recovery Environment).
  • Windows RE scans the hard drive for your data, settings, and apps, and puts them aside (on the same drive).
  • Windows RE installs a fresh copy of Windows.
  • Windows RE restores the data, settings, and apps it has set aside into the newly installed copy of Windows.
  • The PC restarts into the newly installed copy of Windows.

"How to Reset Windows 8 and Windows 8.1"

"When you reset your PC, the drive will be completely reset, and all your apps and files will be removed from it. Windows 8 will be reinstalled, and you'll need to change any settings and reinstall any apps that you want to get back after resetting. If you know if any files that you don't want to lose, then you should back those files up before starting the reset"
