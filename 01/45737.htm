obsessive power say:

Hi,

There is a misconception of what 4K content actually means here on these forums.  I can understand that, most of you are console only gamers and are not technically minded.

A 4K update doesn't always mean you get new graphics.  In most games, these updates simply instruct the console to run an updated game in a higher resolution when run on the One X.  Skyrim does this, there are no new graphics in the Xbox One X version. 

This is especially true for Enhanced Xbox 360 software. Take the game Ninja Gaiden II - recently updated for the X - for example.  When it ran on the Xbox 360 console, its resolution was a rather low 1120x585 and it still runs at this resolution on the original Xbox One and the One S.  But play this game enhanced on the Xbox One X and the game now renders at 3360x1755, a 9x increase over the original version.  Digital Foundry Ninja Gaiden II

Also, 4K updates only apply to older software released before the Xbox One X was released, and then later 'Enhanced'.  All new games, be they on disc or digital download will already have the necessary files to run 'Enhanced' on the Xbox One X hardware.  They don't get 4K updates.

I would also like to point out, as a technical person, this isn't my opinion on the matter, it's fact.

It's up to developers in what state their games are released in, not Microsoft.
