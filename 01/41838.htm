naman r say:

Hello Vivek,

Thank you for reaching out to us at Microsoft Community, we are glad to assist you.

Ideally, changing the CMOS battery should fix this issue but in this case, it appears that the new CMOS battery is not working as expected.

When a CMOS Checksum error occurs during system startup, it could mean that the system has been restored to default factory settings. The CMOS contains data related to BIOS and may have been reinitialized. It may also be possible to fix the problem by reinitializing BIOS. The problem could also occur due to the depletion of the CMOS battery.

To fix the issue, it is recommended to update the BIOS firmware, based on the make and model of the computer, I would request you to get in touch with the computer manufacturer to get the latest BIOS updates.

Note:

Modifying BIOS/ complementary metal oxide semiconductor (CMOS) settings incorrectly can cause serious problems that may prevent your computer from booting properly. Microsoft cannot guarantee that any problems resulting from the configuring of BIOS/CMOS settings can be solved. Modifications of the settings are at your own risk. 

If updating BIOS does not help, this may be due to the fact that the battery powering the CMOS (when the computer is shut down it allows the CMOS to store information), is depleted. You might want to check the hardware manufacturer or local tech shop for information on whether the new CMOS battery is working or not.

Hope the information helps. Do feel free to post on Microsoft Community in case you have any issues while working on Windows.

Open the Windows Store icon on your taskbar
Click the ellipsis in the top right corner of the screen (...)
Click My Library
Scroll through the list, if the apps are listed there, click the download button.

If you are referring to apps not opening, try re-registering them:
In Cortana, type PowerShell. In the search results, right-click Windows PowerShell and select Run as administrator.
In the Powershell window type the following commands. These steps may take a few minutes to complete.

reg delete “HKCU\Software\Microsoft\Windows NT\CurrentVersion\TileDataModel\Migration\TileStore” /va /f

get-appxpackage -packageType bundle |% {add-appxpackage -register -disabledevelopmentmode ($_.installlocation + "\appxmetadata\appxbundlemanifest.xml")}

$bundlefamilies = (get-appxpackage -packagetype Bundle).packagefamilyname

get-appxpackage -packagetype main |? {-not ($bundlefamilies -contains $_.packagefamilyname)} |% {add-appxpackage -register -disabledevelopmentmode ($_.installlocation + "\appxmanifest.xml")}
