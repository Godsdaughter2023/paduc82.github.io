You don't contact a department. 

You can stop that person yourself. Bring up the profile that is bullying you on your console, and then Block and Mute that profile. That will prevent the person from messaging you and to be heard by you in online chats. 

However, be aware that as soon as you message that person yourself (e.g. you feel the need to tell him you blocked him now), you will automatically un-block the person again!!! 

So do NOT reply to the person bullying you, and just mute and block him. 

If the person bullying you is using multiple accounts to get around your blocks, go to https://account.xbox.com/settings and temporarily set your online and privacy settings to "Friends only". That way, only people that are on your friend list can message and talk to you. 

If you already received bullying and threatening messages, use the reporting feature on your console to report these messages. 

This will then send a report with the message attached as proof to the Xbox Live Policy Enforcement Team, who will then review your report. 

If the attached message violates the Xbox Live Code of Conduct, they can then issue suspensions or bans for this person.
