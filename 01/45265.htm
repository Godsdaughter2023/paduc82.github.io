riley lynn nairn say:
You're welcome, I appreciate your working through my suggestions. Your PC may not have a digital license, which could cause the activation error you are getting now. Even if the PC does have a digital license, the Windows 10 installer will still accept your Windows 8.1 product key when you boot from the USB flash drive. After the PC connects to the internet for the first time, Windows should activate automatically.

riley lynn nairn say:
You do not need to reinstall Windows 8.1 then upgrade to 10.

Regarding the command in command prompt, you do not need parentheses or "<>". Only the product key numbers, without dashes. For example: slmgr.vbs /ipk 0000000000000000000000000

Replace the 0s with your Windows 8.1 product key.

If that still does not work, you may need to reinstall Windows 10 again. Go to this website: https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/software-downlo...
Click "Download tool now" then install the Windows 10 media creation tool. You will need a blank USB flash drive with at least 8 GB of storage space. After booting your PC from the USB flash drive, the Windows 10 installer will ask you for a product key. Enter your Windows 8.1 product key.
