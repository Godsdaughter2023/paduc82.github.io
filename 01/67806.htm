Hi Cymbal Man. I'm Bulldog. I don't have any titles after my name and I'm as dumb as a doorknob. But I know this: If you get into the habit of making daily backups of your C: drive - known as image backups - you will never again be in the situation you are now.

Making and restoring backups with third-party imaging software takes just minutes, and you can schedule your backups to run automatically, overnight. So easy even a dog can do it. The only thing easier than making a backup, once you've learned how, is restoring a backup. When you restore an image backup your computer travels back in time to the moment when the image was made.

But enough speeches. Job #1 for you is to make sure your personal files are secure (documents, pictures ... everything you want to keep) so if you can find them inside windows.old, copy them to an external location like an external hard drive or a USB flash drive. Once you know your files are safe, you can try extracting yourself from your current situation.

You didn't mention how you made your backup, but if it's recent you may as well try to restore it. You can also do a Windows 10 reset, which effectively erases your hard drive and installs a fresh copy of Windows, or you can do a factory restore, which returns your computer to the way it was when it left the factory (ergo the name.)

It's certainly possible that the update was the root cause of your present troubles, but I'm not so quick to blame everything on Microsoft. There could have been a problem or two with your computer, something you might not have even been aware of. The installer expects to find a supported configuration, and when it doesn't, it can choke. If it left behind an error message (sometimes in Event Viewer) you can often find out after the fact what happened.

In the bad old days this could leave your computer useless, but Setup (the colloquial name for the Windows installer) has gotten a lot smarter. Among other things, it will try to undo what it's done and return a computer to the way it was, or it can continue and switch you to a temporary profile, and it creates windows.old where you can (hopefully) find the files you want to preserve.
