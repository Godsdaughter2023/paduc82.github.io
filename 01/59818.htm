Personally, I wouldn't even bother with an HTiB. Unless it's a pretty high end system, it'll end up a throw-away. Even a lot of the higher end systems by better manufacturers aren't easily upgradable, as they often use custom connectors & have no real inputs. The only HTiBs that are worth anything (IMHO) are the ones that simply package a real receiver & speakers into one box. While I know speakers are the key component, and should be at least half of any home theater budget, I personally would start out with a good receiver (that the OP would intend on keeping for a while) and a cheaper set of speakers that can be upgraded when the option becomes realistic. I say this because good receivers can be had pretty inexpensively, while great speakers can be very expensive, especially for someone on an HTiB budget (great speakers will probably have to wait). A good receiver & some okay (but replaceable) speakers are better than a complete throw-away HTiB. At least they'd be a starting point rather than a dead end.
