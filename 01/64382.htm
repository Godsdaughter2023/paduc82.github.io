jamescapalmer say:

BBC iPlayer Full Screen Taskbar Problem & Solution

I had an issue where the taskbar was sitting on top of full screen videos in BBC iPlayer Desktop on my Surface Pro.

 

Here's the solution...

  1. Force quit the background BBC iPlayer Desktop process in Task Manager if it's open.
  2. Navigate to C:\Windows\Program Files (x86)\BBC iPlayer Desktop\
  3. Right click the 'BBC iPlayer Desktop' application file and select 'Properties'
  4. Go to the 'Compatibility' tab and select the 'Disable display scaling on high DPI settings' option. Click 'OK'.

The application will now be tiny, but it will importantly play full screen videos properly and quit in the taskbar when you ask it to :-)

 

Hope this helps someone!

a. user say:

Recovery discs are old technology ... very old. They have been replaced by disc imaging.

A recovery disc - if it works - provides you with tools that let you attempt to repair whatever issues you are experiencing. The first problem is: recovery discs don't always work. The second problem: even when the recovery disc works, that's just the beginning. Now you have to figure out which of the tools it provides will help fix your computer's issue(s) and then hope that one of those tools will do the trick.

In the olden days, when there were no better choices, a recovery disc was a lifeline. Now it's a waste of time, quite frankly.

An image is an exact copy of your hard drive compressed into a single file. It's important to understand: Inside that single file is an exact copy of your computer at the time the image was made. The image software lets you restore that image any time you want. When you restore an image, everything that is currently on your hard drive is replaced by what's in the image. That means your messed up, impossible to fix computer is replaced by the correctly functioning computer in the image. This is the only form of time travel that really works.

Unfortunately, Windows 10 does not have a properly functioning image tool, and it's not likely to get one because there are so many excellent image applications made by organizations that specialize in this technology, such as Macrium Reflect or Acronis True Image or EaseUS ToDo, among many others.

Here comes the best part: Third-party image software makes it easy and convenient to make an image of your hard drive every day, automatically. If you do this, then even if your computer is a hot mess ... even if it's completely unbootable ... you can easily, and in a matter of minutes, replace the mess with a recent copy of your computer when it was working well. Why would someone want to start fiddling with recovery discs and recovery utilities when you can simply step back in time?

BluetoothLogView is a small utility that monitors the activity of Bluetooth devices around
you,  and displays a log of Bluetooth devices on the main window. Every time that a new
Bluetooth device arrives to your area and when the device leaves your area, a new log line
is added with the following information: Device Name, Device Address, Event Time, Event
Type ('Device Arrival' or 'Device Left'), Device Type, and the company that created the device.
BluetoothLogView also allows you to specify a description for every device (according to its
 MAC address) that will appear under the 'Description' column. - FREE
http://www.nirsoft.net/utils/bluetooth_log_view.html
