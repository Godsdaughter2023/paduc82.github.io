This problem may appear when mail app fails to sync with the email server. If a license for an app is out of sync with the license installed on your PC, the app might stop working. 

Change the Sync Settings in the Mail App, follow the steps below to change mail app sync settings.

  1. On Search, click on “Mail” to open the Mail App.
  2. Click on “Settings”.
  3. Click on “Accounts”.
  4. Click on your email account from the list of accounts.
  5. Select the option “Email” under the section “Change mail box Sync settings”.

I would also suggest you to refer the article: “To sync a folder” given under the section “Why aren't I seeing messages I expect to see?” from this link: 

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows-8/mail-app-faq

1. Press WinKEY + X or put the mouse in the lower left corner

2. Open the Command Prompt (Run as Administrator)

3. Type in WSReset.exe and hit enter.

4. Type the following in a Command Window (CMD) to re-register the Store App:

powershell -ExecutionPolicy Unrestricted Add-AppxPackage -DisableDevelopmentMode -Register $Env:SystemRoot\WinStore\AppxManifest.XML

babs [skype] say:

Moving a linked Skype account to a different Microsoft account

THIS FACILITY HAS BEEN DISCONTINUED.

Previously, users that merge their Skype account with a Microsoft account find that they were not able to move their Skype name to another Microsoft account. 

We now have a solution! Microsoft will now enable linked Skype accounts to do a onetime move to a different Microsoft account starting on Nov 20, 2017.

What happens when you move your Skype account to another Microsoft account?

The Microsoft account you move from:

  • Is no longer associated with your Skype account.
  • Retains your contacts.

The Microsoft account you move to:

  • Is now merged with your Skype account.
  • Only keeps your Skype contacts but you can easily transfer existing contacts to your Skype account before you merge anything.
  • Skype Credit, Skype Numbers, subscription balances, and chat history are imported.
  • Payment information is not saved and will need to be re-entered after your account is moved.

To learn more about how to do a one-time move of your Skype account to a different Microsoft account, please see our FAQ.

Microsoft Wireless G adapter

This adapter was designed specifically for the Xbox 360 by Microsoft. It is the first wireless adapter for the 360, it operates on "B" and "G" modes at 2.4Ghz frequency and "A" at 5Ghz.
