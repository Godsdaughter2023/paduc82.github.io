To disable automatic disk checking at Windows startup through registry, go to the following registry key:

Important: This section, method, or task contains steps that tell you how to modify the registry. However, serious problems might occur if you modify the registry incorrectly. HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Control\Session Manager

In the right hand pane, double click BootExecute.

The default value of the key is autocheck autochk *

* means every drive is checked for consistency. Just add /K:C at the end before *. /K switch will disable autocheck on C: drive at Windows startup. So the final value should look like this:

autocheck autochk /k:C *

If you want to add more drives, the key should look like this: (disabling C and D drives)

autocheck autochk /k:C /k:D *

If you want to restore everything to default, just replace the key with the default one i.e.

autocheck autochk *

elquietone say:

I have never really heard of anyone price matching digital content with that of a retail physical copy. This comes from the issues that, the digital content is hosted online and is not technically stocked at any one location, so the price you see is the price everyone else also sees.  You would have to talk to support for sales of MS products, because the only way to give you and you alone a sale, would be to have them push the content to your console, while also subtracting the deal total cost from your account.


The second problem comes from your current location, all sales are based on US prices, while retail sales are based on State/Province/Territory.  So while you might have a sale at your current location, some person 25km/miles away will not. Granted you are trying to price match Amazon, and they really don't have a "location" as they are an online retailer, but they also mostly deal in physical copies.


I would say off hand the answer is no, you cannot price match a retail sale for an MS store price.  However, given time I'm sure that the digital copy will someday be that price, but if your truly adamant about getting that price, just buy the disk and install it to your HDD. It will sort of become a digital copy, but you'll still need the disk to load it, the rest will run off your HDD.


Try support, but just know they will probably not honor a match as it might be quite difficult to remove currency from your account without you doing it on your own.  Legality's and so on.
