Sorry if I explained it confusingly.

Listing him as an "avoided" player is NOT the same as "blocking" him. When you choose to "avoid" him, Xbox Live will TRY to not match you with that player, but if no better alternatives are available will still do so. And even if he's not in a match with you, simply having him set as "avoid" will still allow him to view your online presence and what you are doing (unless you turned that off in the profile and privacy settings of yours).

If you really block him, he won't see your online status and what you are doing. So he can't track you down, join a match lobby you're in, can't then find the IP and therefore can't DDoS you.

As long as you "allow" him to find you ("allow" in the sense of "not actively blocking" him, not in the meaning of actively inviting him), he can detect you online, find your IP and DDoS you. This also includes other means of contact outside of Xbox Live, so if you for example chat with him via Instant Messengers or anything else that could provide him with your public IP address, he can (and probably will try) to DDoS you.

Additionally, DDoS attacks are illegal in most countries. So also contact your local police department and your Internet Service Provider, so they can set up logging for your Internet connection, to collect evidence.

Together with your ISP and the Police you can then press charges against that person. Depending on the severity of the attacks (as recorded by your ISP), your ISP can then demand compensation from the attacker, and depending on the age, might even lead to an arrest.

However, only you, your ISP and the police can do that. Microsoft and this forum here can't help you with that issue. See also my next post in response to Gweedos.

net stop wuauserv

net stop bits

net stop msiserver

3. Now rename the SoftwareDistribution and Catroot2 folder. You can do this by typing the following commands in the Command Prompt. Press the “ENTER” key after you type each command.

ren C:\Windows\SoftwareDistribution SoftwareDistribution.old

ren C:\Windows\System32\catroot2 Catroot2.old

4. Now, let’s restart the BITS, Cryptographic, MSI Installer and the Windows Update Services. Type the following commands in the Command Prompt for this. Press the ENTER key after you type each command.

net start wuauserv

net start cryptSvc

net start bits

net start msiserver

5. Type Exit in the Command Prompt to close it.
