alexander bertnikov say:
Hello Rekhmir, my name is Alexander. I'm an Independent Advisor and I'm happy to assist you!

I recommend that you try the following methods and check if the issue persists.

Method 1: Try to run the hardware troubleshooter on your computer.
- Go to the Settings and click on ‘Update and Security’.
- Tap on ‘Troubleshoot’ and choose ‘Hardware & Devices’.
- Run the Troubleshooter and follow the on-screen instructions.
- Once done, restart the computer.

Method 2: Try to install the Bluetooth driver in compatibility mode.
- Right-click on the driver setup file and choose Properties.
- Click on Compatibility tab.
- Check the box next to “Run this program in Compatibility mode” and find the Windows version from the drop down list.
- Once done, install the driver.
- Check if the issue persists.

Method 3: Remove the check mark from the option ‘Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power’.
- Go to the Search to type ‘device manager’.
- Search for the ‘Bluetooth Adapter’.
- Right-click on the driver and click on ‘Properties’.
- In the Generic Bluetooth Adapter Properties window, remove the check mark from “Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power”.
- Click on OK.

Best regards,
Alexander B.

_elaine_ say:

Hi, TjeerdKorse

There are two initial diagnostic steps you can take, which Skype Customer Service would also perform, plus a handful of other ideas:

  1. Ensure your app is one of the more recent versions.  An old Skype app is a common reason for a call to Skype Number to fail.
  2. Check your preference settings, which may look a bit different on the app versus the desktop software.  If you can not locate the settings, sign on to your account using a desktop version of the Skype software. 
  3. Remember to receive calls to the Skype Number, the account on which the subscription was started needs to be signed on, and the status set to on-line.
  4. Last but not least, you may need to uninstall the app, re-start the phone, and re-install the app.  When you test again, remember that we can not call the Skype Number from our own computer or handheld device where we are signed on, placing the test call from Skype, as Skype sees us as calling ourselves.

If none of these steps works, please contact Skype Customer Service directly to request your number be tested as we here in the community do not have the tools to do this.  Let us know how it goes.

Kind regards,

Elaine

Although by default the task bar appears on the bottom of the screen,
it can be on any of the sides you prefer it on. To move it from any
side to another, simply click on an unused part of it and drag it
where you want it. Be sure to click *within* it, not on the edge;
clicking on the edge and dragging will resize it, not move it.
 
If it won't move, it may be locked. In that case, right click on it
and uncheck "Lock the taskbar," then try again.
 
By the way, with today's wide-screen monitors, I prefer the task bar
on the left or right side of the screen. I think that makes a better
use of screen real estate than having it at the bottom.

Most of the time, computers only use a small fraction of their CPU power. In fact, many computers use less than 5% of their CPU the majority of the time. Once the CPU intensive process completes, the CPU usage should once again drop down to lower level. Most applications will not use up more than 50% of your CPU for an extended period of time. However, some programs and system processes can use up a large amount of CPU without you even knowing it. This may happen if a program gets stuck in an infinite loop or encounters an unexpected error.

• Out-of-date drivers (Run Driver Hub to automatically update or manually update them).

Two methods.

1. Right-click on the wireless icon in your systray, choose Open Network and Sharing Centre.

Click on Manage Wireless Networks in the L/H pane

2. Log on to your router, (type http://192.168.0.1 or whatever your gateway IP address is) in your Browser. Go to Wireless and you can see the passkey there.
