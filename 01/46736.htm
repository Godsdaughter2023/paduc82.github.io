Device Manager displays a list of all devices that are installed on a Windows based computer. When you view device information in Device Manager, you may see a device listed as Unknown Device. It may be difficult to determine the cause of this unknown device, because there are few indications of what generates it.

A black exclamation point (!) on a yellow field (also referred to as yellow exclamation point) indicates the device is in a problem state. Note that a device that is in a problem state can be functioning.

A yellow exclamation point next to a device in Device Manager means that Windows has identified a problem of some kind with that device.

The yellow exclamation mark provides an indication of a device's current status and could mean that there is a system resource conflict, a driver issue, or any number of other things.

The yellow question mark appears as the full sized device icon and will appear when a device is detected but not installed. You can solve this problem by updating the drivers for the device.

Update the driver, if needed, and check if that fixes the issue.

The yellow mark itself doesn't give you any valuable information but what it does do is confirm that a Device Manager error code has been generated. To fix whatever problem is going on, you'll need to view this code and troubleshoot accordingly.

To view the Device Manager error code that was generated for this piece of hardware, you'll need to view the device's status in Device Manager.

Viewing the status of a hardware device in Windows 7 is necessary if it's not working properly or if you were warned about an issue by a yellow exclamation point in Device Manager.

The current status of each individual piece of hardware in your Windows 7 computer is available from inside Device Manager. If there is a hardware problem, a detailed Device Manager error code will be listed to help you troubleshoot the issue.

To View a Device's Status in Device Manager in Windows 7, right click the “unknown Device” and select ‘Properties’.

How to edit an xbox live profile! - Microsoft Community

hacks xc say:

How to edit an xbox live profile!

Hi all,

If you are looking to edit your Motto, Bio, Name or Location on your Xbox Live profile then you'll need one of the following devices to do so:

  • Xbox 360
  • Xbox One
  • Windows Phone 7 or Windows Phone 8 device
  • A laptop, tablet or PC running Windows 8 that meets the requirements posted here
  • Android device that meets the requirements posted here
  • iOS device that meets the requirements posted here

If you do have one of those devices, then just follow the steps below and you'll have your profile updated in no time!

Xbox 360

1). Sign in to a profile on an Xbox 360 console.
2). Press the guide button on your controller.
3). Select the "Settings" tab.
4). Select "Profile".
5). Select "Edit Profile".
6). Select the desired profile section you would like to change.
7). Press the Start button when done editing to save your changes and you're good to go!

Xbox One

1). Sign in to a profile on an Xbox One console.
2). Move over to the "Friends" hub.
3). Select the "Friends" tile.
4). Select "My Profile".
5). Select "Customize profile".
6). Select the desired profile section you would like to change.
7). Press the Menu button when done editing to save you're changes and you're all set!

Windows Phone 7 device

1). Launch the Games app that is preinstalled with the phone's OS. No need to download this app from the app marketplace.
2). Swipe to the right until you are where your avatar stands and then click on the "profile" option.
3). Click on the little pencil icon at the bottom-middle section of the screen to edit your profile.
4). When finished with the edit, click on the little floppy disc icon to save your changes and you're good to go!

Windows Phone 8 device

1). Launch the Games app that is preinstalled with the phone's OS. No need to download this app from the app marketplace.
2). Swipe to the right until you are where your avatar stands and then click on the little trophy icon that shows your achievements and games played list.
3). Swipe to the right until you are on the profile page and click on the "bio" option.
4). Click on the little pencil icon at the bottom-middle of the screen to edit your profile.
5). When finished with the edit, click on the little floopy disc icon to save your changes and you're good to go!

Windows 8 device (laptop, tablet, PC)

1). Launch the Games app that is preinstalled with the Windows 8 OS. No need to download this app from the app marketplace.
2). Scroll to the left where your avatar stands and select the "Edit Profile" option.
3). Make the desired changes to your profile, click "Save" and you're good to go!
(While viewing Xbox.com on any laptop or PC, you can also still update your profile at this page right here)

Android devices

1). Download the Xbox 360 SmartGlass app and then launch the app.
2). Sign in to your Xbox Live account if prompted.
3). Swipe to the right until you are where your avatar stands and then click on the little pencil and paper icon at the bottom-middle of the screen.
4). Make the desired changes to your profile, then click the floppy disc icon to save your changes and you're good to go!

iOS devices

1). Download the Xbox 360 SmartGlass app and then launch the app.
2). Sign in to your Xbox Live account if prompted.
3). Swipe to the right until you are on the "Social" tab.
4). Click on the little pencil and paper icon.
5). Click on the "Edit Profile" option.
6). Make the desired changes to your profile, click "Save" and you're good to go!

That should have ya covered from all angles but always feel free to reach out to us here in the support forums if you are seeing any issues when updating your Xbox Live profile. Thanks!

Instructions: What to do if your system is automatically upgraded to Windows 10?

=============================================================

If Windows 7 or 8.1 suddenly decides to install Win10, all on its own — you still have one fallback point. The very last step in the new installation sequence involves clicking “Accept” on the EULA, the End User License Agreement. If you click “Decline,” the whole update unwinds and your system should be restored to its original Windows 7 or 8.1 state. (Note the operative term “should.”)

If you or someone you know made a mistake and clicked “Accept” on the EULA, they’re running Windows 10 now. I strongly recommend that you immediately go back to Windows 7 or 8.1 using the built-in tools. Detailed instructions in my InfoWorld feature “How to roll back your Windows 10 upgrade.”

¡firedog say:

Server Response: -ERR [IN-USE] account is locked by another session or for maintenance, try again.

Although a server may occasionally be taken out of service for maintenance, this will not normally last for more than an hour or two. The usual reason for this error is that the same account is being accessed from a mobile device - a smart phone or a tablet - that's configured to use push mail. Because there can only be one POP3 connection to the account at once, mail clients like Windows Live Mail can't get a look in.

This might also happen if the polling interval (Check for new messages every n minutes) is too short. Some POP3 servers will lock the account for, say, fifteen minutes at each poll, so if the client tries to connect before those fifteen minutes are up, it won't get through.

Is either of these possible causes likely in your situation?
