rodrigo queiroz say:

I have already figured out a solution.

I managed to run "msconfig" and restart my PC on safe mode.

On safe mode, start menu worked just fine.

Then I restarted the PC back to normal mode, deselecting a lot of apps (just to start faster).

Right when PC starts, before clicking anywhere, run powershell with administrative privileges and use the command:

Get-AppXPackage -AllUsers | Foreach {Add-AppxPackage -DisableDevelopmentMode -ForceTargetApplicationShutdown -Register "$($_.InstallLocation)\AppXManifest.xml"}

Note that I added the "-ForceTargetApplicationShutdown".

I had to restart the PC several times in order to complete this.

Finally, I figured out that the store app was outdated. Then "magically" after this command had completed, it was updated and all apps reinstalled. Now everything is working just fine!

Use the safe mode and check if you can use the startup menu. It worked for me!

marc cai say:

Hi,


Thanks for bringing this to the community. We hope we can provide an accurate advise to your concern about the blue screen errors.

The following that were mentioned: ndis.sys and wdiwifi.sys, are network drivers. These are failing and could be due to file corruptions.

To resolve this, you will need to uninstall and reinstall your network driver. But since you're unable to boot to Windows properly, you can try the Startup repair. This method is known in fixing many system startup problems in Windows. This can also include the linkage of the drivers to the system, so it worth a try. To perform it:


  1. Select the Startbutton, then choose Settings.
  2. Select Update & security> Recovery.
  3. Under Advanced startupselect Restart now.
  4. After your PC restarts to the Choose an optionscreen, select Troubleshoot> Advanced options > Startup Repair.
  5. Allow it to finish and restart the PC.

If this did not work, we will be needing to manually delete the corrupted drivers:

  1. Follow the steps provided above. Stop at step 4.
  2. After your PC restarts to the Choose an optionscreen, select Troubleshoot> Advanced options > Command Prompt.
  3. Type this command:

    DEL /F /S /Q /A "C:\Windows\System32\drivers\<driver name>"


Note: <driver name> is equal to the one that fails. For example, if the ndis.sys is failing: DEL /F /S /Q /A "C:\Windows\System32\drivers\ndis.sys"   

There are 2 drivers that were failing, so there is the need for both files to be removed. Once remove, you should be able to reboot the PC as normal.

Next, if you will need to reinstall the network drivers for the internet connection to work properly, you will need the details of your device, and download it from the manufacturer's support site (Acer).

andre da costa say:
Here are some general troubleshooting tips you can try:

Check Your Surface Type

If you're having intermittent trouble with your mouse, make sure you're using it on a smooth, flat surface that is not shiny or reflective. We recommend using a mousepad to create a consistent work surface.
Resync Your Devices with the USB Receiver

Your keyboard and mouse work by sending wireless signals to a USB receiver that came with your setup. This might be a small hub that plugs into a USB port with a wire, a small dongle similar in size and shape to a thumb drive, or a very tiny unit that is barely visible once you slide it into a USB port. Sometimes the receiver gets out of sync with the wireless devices, causing them to stop working.

Resyncing the setup is fairly easy. There is usually a Connect button somewhere on the USB receiver. Press that, and a light on the receiver should start flashing. Then press the Connect button on the keyboard and/or mouse and the flashing light on the USB receiver should stop. Your receiver is now synced with the keyboard and/or mouse.

If your receiver does not have a Connect button, refer to the directions that came with your mouse or keyboard to find more specific instructions for reconnecting your devices.

If you continue to have trouble with your devices, you may want to try connecting the receiver to another USB port on your computer. It might take a minute or two for your computer to recognize a device plugged into a USB port.
Test Your Devices on Another Computer

It's possible there's an issue with one of your devices. To find out if this is the case, connect your devices to a different computer to see if you encounter the same issues.
Update Drivers

If you are working on a Windows machine, make sure you are using the latest drivers and software for your device. Device drivers and software should be available from the manufacturer's website.
