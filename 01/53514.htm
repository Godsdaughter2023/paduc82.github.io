The message is coming from your monitor, not your computer, and means that the monitor can't handle the output of the video card. Is this a new monitor? Old monitor? Did you make any changes to your display resolution from Windows?

Troubleshooting, assuming that you have made no changes and this is the same monitor you've always been using:

1. Attach the computer to a different monitor. If all is well, discard the original monitor - it has died.

2. If all is not well, then the issue is with the computer's video card. Swap it out for a known-working one to test. If all is well with a different video card, replace the original one since it has died.

Testing hardware failures often involves swapping out suspected parts with known-good parts. If you can't do the testing yourself and/or are uncomfortable opening your computer, take the machine to a professional computer repair shop (not your local equivalent of BigComputerStore/GeekSquad).
