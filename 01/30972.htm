zack - say:

Hello BatoutaAustin22,

 

Thank you for posting.  Even though this is a hardware issue I will try to help you to the best of my ability.  The way to get these speakers to work with Vista will depend on what version of the speakers that you have.  There are two versions of the speakers: one is digital and the other is analog.  I will provide both methods to connect them, but you will have to check with the manufacturer of either your PC or the speakers to see if you have the proper hardware to use them and how to connect them if my steps don't help you.

 

First method of connecting Analog.

First of all, locate a 1/8 to 1/8 male stereo connecting wire (or go out and purchase one). Now remove the digital cable from the back of the computer and from the subwoofer. Take the new 1/8 to 1/8 stereo male connector, plug one end into the analog input of the subwoofer and the other end into your computer. Then take the speaker wire that you removed from the analog input of the subwoofer and plug it into the other satellite speaker.

 

Digital method of connecting. There are three major issues here:

1) The speakers are digital.
2) The audio port on your computer is probably analog.
3) The audio settings are set to analog.


Plug the speakers into your computer's audio port. We're not sure yet if it really is just analog, or if it can support both, or what.

After plugging in the speakers and booting up your computer, open up your Volume settings. You can find this under Start-->Control Panel-->Sounds and Audio Devices. Once you have the settings open, go under Options and click "Advanced Controls". This will give you a button on the bottom left of the controls, right beneath "Mute All."
These settings depend on your sound card
Click on Advanced. Once here, you will notice Tone Controls to adjust the Bass and Treble, and below these, you will see "Digital Output Only." Click on the little white box next to "Digital Output Only" to put a check mark in there. Hit close. Make sure that your Main and Wave audio levels in the volume settings are high enough to hear something (I suggest at least half volume, or highest volume for testing purposes).

Test out the sound again.

If it doesn't work, then you know the problem lies in the sound card. What you need to do now is to install a digital sound card into your computer.  If you've never done it before, I suggest consulting a computer specialist friend of yours, or to look on the internet for PCI Installation guides.

SECOND_BOOT, MIGRATE_DATA, 8007002C-4000D ***FIX***

So upon trying to upgrade to Windows 10, some poeple would eventually get a black screen with no cursor, and eventually their computer would restart and restore their previous version, and give an error saying something about the SECOND_BOOT and failing to MIGRATE_DATA. Error code 8007002C-4000D (Or similar)

This is my solution. I was able to upgrade from Windows 7 Home Premium (SP1, 64 bit), and didn't come across any errors or problems

TL;DR

1. Go to your BIOS and use your motherboard's integrated graphics

2. Instead of keeping your data and programs when going through setup, select the option to delete it all and perform a clean installation.

Longer Version

First, I went to my BIOS and chaged the settings to use my motherboard's integrated graphics, and not my graphics card. I also used an old monitor and a VGA cable for this, because I was also having bad experiences with DVI getting picky with Analog and Digital signals. This might not be necessary, but it's just what I did.

Second, I downloaded the Windows 10 ISO using the Media Creation Tool. I used WinRAR to open it and run setup.exe, went through it and chose the option to keep nothing. Get rid of all my data and programs. Because I've done this, I can reassure you that NONE of the data on any other connected drives will be affected. To be on the safe side though, I'd disconnect any other drives, if you're willing.

After waiting for it install, I was finally presented with the Out-of-box interface for finalising the installation, which my computer had been failing to give me every other time I tried to upgrade.

Hope I've helped some people out with this. Best of luck with Windows 10 :)

Finally I FOUND A GUARANTEED  SOLUTION TO Error Code 8007002

1- All you have to do is to download a program by Microsoft called Autoruns

Autoruns Download Link

2- in this program (Autoruns) you will find literally every program, service, drive, etc.. that starts during & after system boot (startup).

       A - In the program options, Make sure that (Hide Microsoft Entries) & (Hide Windows Entries) are ticked, This insures that you don't accidentally disable any of the files required by the system to function correctly

       B - Now got the Logon Tab & untick (uncheck) all the entries, DO NOT DELETE the entries just untick them

       C - Then do the same thing in the Scheduled Tasks Tab

       D - Also untick (uncheck) all the entries in the Service Tab

       E - Now go to the Drivers Tab, You have to be EXTRA CAREFUL in this tab, Some of the entries in this tab are required by the system to boot & unchecking them will definitely damage your system, DO NOT Randomly untick the entries of this tab, look at the Description & Publisher columns in the program to determine which entries to untick, The entries that should Be unticked are the:

                             - (Graphic Drivers):

in my case the graphic drive is NVIDIA so i unticked all the entries from NVIDIA

                             - (Network Drivers):

Untick all the network drivers found, For me there was many (Media Tek Wireless, Huawei USB Modem, AnchorFree For Hotspot Sheild VPN, etc..) as i said you have to look at the [Publisher column] to know what the entry represent

                              - (Audio Drivers):

Like (RealTek HD Audio) or any other audio driver

                               - (3rd Party Drivers):

Untick any 3d party driver, & by third party drivers i mean the drivers added by programs you installed like (Internet Download Manger WFP Driver) & (Samsung USB Driver) etc..

       F - Now go to the Codecs Tab, untick all the entries that could be unticked (some will remain ticked no matter what), Alternatively you can uninstall the codec software installed in your computer Like (K-lite Codec) or (Shark007 Codec )

3- Now you can close the program & proceed with the Windows 10 upgrade

4- After the end of the Windows 10 upgrade process SURPRISE Windows Successfully upgraded to Windows 10, no more Restore Previous System, no more Error Code 8007002

5- After The Upgrade, run the (Autoruns) program & tick all the entries you unticked previously

Code 41

Windows successfully loaded the device driver for this hardware but cannot find the hardware device. (Code 41)

Recommended resolution

This problem occurs if you install a driver for a non-Plug and Play device, but Windows cannot find the device. Here are some things that you can try to resolve this problem.

If the Troubleshooting Wizard does not resolve the problem, do one of the following:

  • If the device was removed, uninstall the driver, connect the device, and then click Scan for hardware changes to reinstall the driver.
  • If the hardware was not removed, the driver files might be obsolete. In this case, update the driver from Device Manager. In the device Properties dialog box, click the Driver tab, and then click Update Driver to start the Hardware Update Wizard. Follow the instructions to update the driver.
