wendy_jones say:
Hello nico89s
Thank you for participating in the Microsoft Community, my name is Wendy Jones, I am an Independent Advisor.
Excellent that you have verified that you have a Retail license.
But before transferring the license you must unlink the license of the machine that you will not use at this time, for this: open a Command Prompt as administrator and execute these two commands:

slmgr / upk

slgmr / cpky
The first uninstalls the license, and the second deletes it from the registry. After restarting the computer, Windows will not have a product key and will be in an inactive and unlicensed state.

Then on the new computer once you have installed Windows, to activate it:
Open a system symbol and execute: slmgr / ipk [product key]

and then: slmgr / ato
Finally, link your new license with your Microsoft account.
Try these steps and let me know if they worked.

valeriesnetzinger say:

I had the same problem. My touchpad settings would not save. After trying update after update I called HP support. Here's what we did and it works!!

Hold the windows key and the R key at the same time to bring up the run box. Type in regedit and the click OK

When you have the registery editor open choose: HKEY-LOCAL-MACHINE. Then choose SOFTWARE, then SYNAPTICS, then INSTAL. At this point go to the right hand box and choose DELEATE USER SETTINGS ON UPGRADE. A new box will open and you need to change the value from 1 to 0. Reboot your machine and set up your touchpad the way you want it and your good to go
