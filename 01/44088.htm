daov say: Hi Igor B.,

I just wanted to let you know that I tested what was on the link I sent prior and it works flawlessly in regards to turning off the screen but leaving apps, such as music running and playing audio.

Here are my steps (modify as needed in regards to location or anything else):
  1. Download nircmd from here
  2. It will be a zip file. Browse to it once it downloads and copy/extract all contents to a location of your choosing that you can easily access.
  3. I chose c:\users\%username%\nircmd\. %username% would be whatever your username is in your profiles directory. You can type/copy that path in the Charm search box as well to get there. If you have other users on your SP who may need access to it, it is best to put the nircmd files somewhere else, such as C:\Program Files\. Press Continue or OK for any warnings you receive
  4. Once copied, go to the desktop
  5. Right click->New->Shortcut
  6. Type: "c:\users\%username%\nircmd\nircmd.exe" cmdwait 1000 monitor off (include the quotes or modify the path to the path you chose) You can modify that time as well. I left it at 1000
  7. Press Next and give it a name. I chose Power Off Screen but name it anything you like and press Finish
  8. Once the shortcut appears on your desktop, right click it again and choose Properties
  9. Click the icon button and say OK to the warning.
  10. It will open a host of icons to choose from. Double click one and then press OK
  11. Start some music playing. Test the shortcut by pressing/double tapping/clicking it
  12. If you screen turned off but the music continued, then it worked!
  13. You will obviously need to modify your battery/plugged in sleep times from Power Options to offset it going to sleep while the screen is off

These are optional steps:

  1. I wanted this shortcut to appear on the Metro/Start screen
  2. Right click the shortcut and choose Copy
  3. Browse to: c:\programdata\microsoft\windows\start menu\programs
  4. Paste the shortcut there and press Continue if prompted

Now go to your Metro/Start screen and you should have a shortcut to the ... shortcut
