chris de herrera say:

Hi,
A quick preface, the opinions of what is occuring are my own.  I do not work for Microsoft.

There are a couple of items to check prior to drawing a conclusion. 

1. Are the date and time on the PC and the device the same?  

2. How about the timezone? 

3. Are the files fully synchronzied to the PC or the device?

The biggest problem is around the full synchronization of files.  Right now by default ActiveSync leaves behind partial files that are synchronized with the current date.  Thus when the user synchronizes again the partial files are now synchronized the other way and destroy both copies of the data.

If the time is out of sync on the devices, then they can have the problem you described as well.  One can be ahead of the other and that can cause problems when you sync the next time and ActiveSync thinks that the file is newer now.

Overall, while these issues have existing, I have discussed them with Microsoft on multiple occasions.   At this point I can say that you are using the current version of ActiveSync and there is no announced plans to update it.

I highly recommend considering 3rd party solutions (MightySync if I recall right is one) or copying the files to an SD card via a SD card reader as an alternative.
