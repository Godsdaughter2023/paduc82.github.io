Well, it's not really Word, it's copy/paste -- which is an OS utility not intended for graphic quality... Unfortunately, we sometimes get it so we've come to expect it :-)

Maintaining graphic fidelity is a very challenging task, though, & there are literally dozens of other variables that easily can come into play.

The problem seems to come from the fact that some sync error messages are locally cached and kept for quite a while, although it has only been a short "hiccup" (e.g. because of the device going to sleep before the sync was complete). The error itself is long gone but still displayed. In this case, it should be safe to ignore the error, wait for it to disappear or try to remove it by starting a manual sync. At least this is what a OneNote engineer explained. Although he is referring to the error code 0x000007, your case seems similar as you could confirm that the syncing actually took place.

This seems similar to the error memory of modern cars. One single spark plug misfire and you get a permanent engine error displayed which just has to get reset.

The OneNote team is looking for ways to improve the error handling and lower the confusion created by it.

Again, I am just passing what a OneNote developer explained about it. Here is his original statement on Reddit: https://www.reddit.com/r/OneNote/comments/71jpn...
