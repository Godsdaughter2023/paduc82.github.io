a. Insert the installation DVD or USB and boot Windows 10 from it.

SOLVED: Classpnp.sys Safe mode loop on Windows 7 64bit. - Microsoft Community

dagaa say:

SOLVED: Classpnp.sys Safe mode loop on Windows 7 64bit.

I have solved this problem for good on my machine.

Description of problem: Windows hangs on the logo screen, startup repair wont work from OEM/factory preinstall or from recovery cd or from new copy of windows 7. Safe mode locks on the "CLASSPNP.SYS" file. Data seems fine every time it randomly lets me log in. Letting it cooldown seems to increase the problem.

Potential cause of problems: Updated windows, and power went out. I proceeded to knock my computer to the ground. while stumbling in the dark.

Specs:

Emachines ET1331g-03w (modified)
 6GB ram, 1 TB Seagate Hardrive, AMD Athlon II X2 235e Processor 2.70GHz, Windows 7 64 bit Service pack 1. Nvidia Quadro GFX

Solution ******WARNING**** If you dont know how to disassemble and reassemble your pc, you will damage your hardware.

You need to know how to remove RAM/HDD/GFX/PROCESSOR/POWER SUPPLY;
Tools I used: Phillips and Flat head screwdrivers

1) Discharged extra static electricity from hands and body

2)I turned my PC off, REMOVED THE POWER CABLE, and opened the side of my case up.

3) Take canned duster and clean your pc, gently. Follow the instructions on the can/bottle.

4) Take ALL RAM out of your computer, place on a STATIC FREE area(do this for ALL hardware.)

5) Remove screw that holds GFX is place, remove card and place on a STATIC FREE area.

6) Remove HDD (Hardrive) and place on a STATIC FREE area

7) GENTLY Remove Processer ONLY IF YOU CAN. if it looks like its flat against your motherboard, chances are you cant remove it.

8) dust processor fan off. Place on STATIC FREE area.

9) Remove power supply, and cables. Place on area near you. Dust it off.

10) Re-Dust your computer.

11) Discharge your hands and body once more.

12) Re-assmble in this order AND MAKE SURE THE HARDWARE IS IN PLACE AND SECURE : Power supply and cables; HDD, RAM, Processor, GFX card.

13. Double check your hardware securely in place. make sure any screws or bolts that are supposed to be there are there. and tight.

14) close case, re connect cords and power cable.

15) Start PC up.

This has worked for me on 2 dells and this above emachines. The loop is caused by a piece of hardware not responding to the CLASSPNP.sys  driver correctly.

Fall out 3 (all disks) world of warcraft, skyrim and AutoCAD ( all respective trademarks are owned) now run wonderfully again. I have no boot up problems, my PC starts right up now.

Hope this helps at least some of you.
