ThinkPad driver trick for Win 10 (tested with Tablet 10 64Bit - Microsoft Community

eirk whiteway say:

ThinkPad driver trick for Win 10 (tested with Tablet 10 64Bit

For those looking to, or have upgraded a Lenovo device to Win 10 and are having driver issues - I want to point out the SCCM driver package.  This is a package of the raw drivers need to run a machine, in a format the will auto install.

1. go to support.lenovo.com and driver section for your laptop/desktop

2. Change component to 'enterprise management'

3. D/L the Sccm pack for windows 8.1 (64 or 32 bit based on your needs)

4. run the package - should unzip to c:\drivers

5. for each 'unknown' device in device manager, right click, update driver, browse, then go to the c:\drivers folder (stay at that higher level)

6. windows will find the correct driver and install, just keep doing that till no more unknown (you make have to skip some and go back to them).

On my Thinkpad Tablet 10 - I now have 0 unknown, and only NFC shows an error.  Dock, wifi, LTE Touch - all seem to work.

Hope that help a lot of you - saved me a ton.

If anyone know how to make windows rescan that folder and auto-apply the drivers, please share!

julie fka profjulie say:

The room chat feature uses the practically discontinued windows live messenger service. I just spent a lot of time working this out on my account and see what appears to be the full history of my family room chat messages. I hope it works for you too.

From a computer, go log into outlook.com, using the same email address and password that you used when you created the Microsoft account on your phone. Your email inbox will be shown.

Select the gear icon, found in the blue navigational bar that runs across the top of the screen (the icon is on the right side of the screen). Select "options" > "group by conversation & preload messages" (found under the "Read email" heading > select the radio button next to "Group messages by conversation" > Save.

Return back to the Inbox & select the gear icon again > "Manage categories." Place a checkmark in both columns for "Contacts" (Show in folder pane & filter columns)

Return to the Inbox scroll down to the bottom of the screen. Look in the left-most light gray "folders" column for Contacts (found under the Categories heading), select Contacts

Click the "view" drop down arrow at the top of the next column & select "Everything else"

Click the "Arrange by" drop down arrow at the top of the same column & select "Conversation"

Review the messages to see if you recognize your room chat messages.

If you follow these directions and find your chat messages, you can print them out by selecting the message you want to print, then tap the three dots at the top of the screen in the blue navigational bar > select "Print"
