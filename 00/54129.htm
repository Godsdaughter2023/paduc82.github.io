This error indicates that a "bad block" was found on your hard drive, one the first partition. In order to fix that error (basically, make Windows mark that "block" as being bad, and not use it anymore), you need to a check disk on your drive. Right-click on the Windows Start menu and select Command Prompt (Administrator). In the command prompt, enter the command below and press on Enter:

chkdsk /r

A message telling you that the disk is currently in use, and chkdsk cannot be ran right now will appear, but it'll asks you if you want to schedule that operation on the next reboot, to which you'll enter y and press on Enter. Once done, restart your computer, and let the chkdsk operation go through.

IMPORTANT: Please do not shut down your computer during the chkdsk operation, since this can damage your hard drive and render your Windows installation unusable (and even lead to data loss). This operation can take a few hours to complete, depending on your hard drive and system specs.

BCCode: F4    0x000000F4 

Check and reseat all cards, memory and cables (on both ends when possible) actually remove and
replace - do not just snug. On a laptop about all you can do is reseat the memory and possibly check
the contacts/connection to the hard drive. Memory could be the problem so if you have more than 1
stick swap them in and out to see if the problem follows one stick or try new memory. Antivirus/anti-
spyware/security programs have been known to cause or have a role in this issue.

Most general fix is updating drivers, BIOS, and low level chipset drivers and other control software.
Also check cabling to hard disk.
