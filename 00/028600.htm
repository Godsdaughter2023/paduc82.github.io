ken sheridan say:

OBJECTS also contains many fields and among those are OBJECTS_ID (Primary Key), Type, SerialNumber, OwnersSurname, OwnersFirstName and so on.

The referencing table, Objects, should not have OwnersSurname, OwnersFirstName or any other columns  which represent attributes of the Person entity type.  Other than columns representing it's own attributes, it should only contain a Persons_ID foreign key column referencing the primary key of the Persons table.  To do otherwise would mean that the Objects table is not normalized to Third Normal Form (3NF) which requires all non-key columns to be determined solely by the table's primary key.

The process of Normalization is fundamental to a good relational database design.  If you are unfamiliar with its principles you might like to take a look at Normalization.zip in my public databases folder at the link I gave you earlier.

This little demo file explains the principles of Normalization as simply as possible, but no more so.  You should be sure that you have a good understanding of Normalization to at least Third Normal Form, and preferably to Boyce Codd Normal Form (BCNF), which is a replacement for the first three normal forms and overcomes some of the inadequacies which were identified with them.  Do not pay too much attention to the higher normal forms  beyond BCNF initially.  You can come back to those when you have a sound understanding of the basics.
