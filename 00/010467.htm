kosh vorlon say:

Hi Hein,

 

Go to Start / Run and type MSCONFIG and enter.  Go to the startup tab and uncheck anything you absolutely don't need running at startup.  Leave your AV program and Defender if it is enabled, but everything else is pretyy much fair game.  That will cut down a lot of what is running in the background and taking up resources.  When I did this the first time myself, I ended up unchecking about 90% of the items.  Remember, just because it refers to a program you use doesn't mean it needs to run a startup - if you uncheck that item and open the program, it will run just fine.  Just do this for startup programs.  Do NOT do this for any of the other tabs (like Services or Boot) unless specifically instructed as unchecking some of those other items can seriously hose your system if you choose the wrong ones.

 

Now do a disk cleanup - being especially sure to clear up the temp files (and anything else you feel safe including): http://support.microsoft.com/kb/310312.

 

Defragment your hard drive (though not likely to be the problem in this case, it can't hurt and it may help and is a good thing to do from time to time): http://support.microsoft.com/kb/305781.

 

Now reboot your system and without opening anything else, start by opening Excel and that spreadsheet and see if it works.  If so, the problem is that when you were trying before you had too much other stuff running or cluttering up the system and your computer just isn't powerful enough to do all that at the same time.  You'll need to experiment to see just how much you can do simultaneously without causing that problem to occur - and if necessary, close down some other things that are running to clear up the additional resources you need.

 

If your computer can handle it, probably the best thing to do in the long run (assuming you intend to keep this computer for a while longer) would be to add more RAM - which may mean replacing RAM that exists with larger capacity sticks if all the slots are already full (making certain you get the right type for your specific computer - I'd check with the manufacturer to see what exactly you should get and how much the computer can handle).  While it won't solve all your problems, it will increase your resources and may allow you to do more at the same time than is currently possible (even after cleaning things up).

 

I hope this helps.

 

Good luck!
