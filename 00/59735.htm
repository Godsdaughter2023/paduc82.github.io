You need to understand: Windows 10 doesn't care whether your hardware is relatively up to date. It's either supported or it's not supported.

Here's what I did to find out whether my hardware was supported:

  • I made a list of all the hardware installed in and on my computer, including model numbers.
  • I went to the website of each hardware manufacturer and found out whether they are supporting that model on Windows 10.
  • If you discover that one or more of your hardware components is not supported, and can't be easily replaced, all is not lost. Hit the manufacturer's community forum and find out from other users if they had success with that model on Windows 10.

It took more than a few hours to do all that, but in the end I was happy to find out that all my hardware would work on Windows 10. And it does. If you can find an easier way to be absolutely sure that hardware is supported, I'm all ears.
