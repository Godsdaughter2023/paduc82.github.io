The advice to remove old versions is just a fall back to the "simplest" (is the advice simple or the advisor ... ?) "best practice" defined by MS. They always assume that everyone will immediately buy and install the newest version. That everyone would only ever want to run one version at a time.

jllatham say:

Sorry, you're pretty much stuck with two solutions:

#1 - unlock all cells that might be included in a sort.

or

#2 (said in hushed voice) a macro.

If you have only a few ways that the data might be sorted, you can prepare canned macros for each method.  What has to happen within each canned process is:

unprotect the sheet
perform the sort
protect the sheet

Now, another option is to prepare just 2 simple macros:  an UNPROTECT macro and a PROTECT macro.  You could put buttons (one for each) on the sheet to allow them to take the indicated action, as:
Click [MAKE SORTING AVAILABLE]

then they sort as they please, and presumably they would remember to then

Click the [Protect from Accidental Change] button (puts the sheet back into protected mode).

Each of the macros is a simple one liner.  But as you say, some people are paranoid about using workbooks with macros in them, so maybe even this is unacceptable.
