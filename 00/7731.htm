Now I am trying to determine the best way to share the quick parts with other users. I discovered I could link my quick parts to my template, but then realized it removed those quick parts from all of my other templates so I would have to go in and reestablish them in order to add them to each template. Then I discovered that I could make them globally accessible. I did a test and it seems to work, but I have a few questions:

(1) How do I edit them once they are global (i.e. if I just need to edit the phone number or something)?

(2) Do I have to get on every computer to do the add-on or is there a way to do it without direct access?

(3) If we have a change in staff and I create a new building block or delete an old one, will it automatically update on everyone else's computer or do I have to do something to update the global list?

(4) Will the process need to be redone if my agency performs updates or upgrades our license?

(5) Is there a better way to do what I am trying to do? My main goal is to ensure that everyone is pulling from the master templates when drafting motions, so I want all the signatures to be accessible from that document (otherwise staff will save local copies with each signature so there are countless copies that are hard to keep track of).

Thanks!

jay freedman say:

The generally recommended setup is something like this:

  • There is one master template of quick parts stored on the firm's server, in a folder to which only the administrator (or delegate) has write permission and everyone else has just read permission. The administrator can make updates to the template at any time.
  • Each other user's profile is given a network login script that copies the master template to that user's %appdata%\Microsoft\Word\STARTUP folder, overwriting any existing copy of the template in that folder. During most logins that copy will be identical to the previous copy, but this procedure will propagate any updates that have been made to the master template since the last login.

That answers your questions 1 through 3. For question 4, updates or upgrades should not be affected (unless Microsoft does something stupid like changing the location of the folder that Word reads for global templates; so far that hasn't happened). You would, though, have to make sure that each new user's profile has the login script.

The situation that this setup avoids is what can happen if there's just a single copy of the master template on the server, which is accessed by all users. Windows would lock that copy against updating whenever any user's copy of Word had it loaded, so it would be difficult for the administrator to update it -- you'd have to find a time when no one else is using the template, or (ugh!) kick everybody off the system.

jay freedman say:

If there is any text on the same line as the equation object, the equation will be considered to be "in line" with the text and the fraction will be made small. If you then click the down arrow on the right side of the equation box and click "Change to Display", the equation will be placed in its own paragraph -- separated from the text -- and the fraction will be large.

There are very few ways to put a large fraction in line with text and keep it large. Probably the best way is to create the large fraction, use the Windows snipping tool to take a screen shot of it, and save that as a graphic file (.png or .jpg format). Insert the graphic (Insert > Picture), set its text wrapping to Square or Tight, and drag it to the required position in the text.

Please be informed that these are the possible reasons why your emails were missing or deleted from your mailbox:

  1. Messages were manually deleted either due to accidental deletion or were moved or deleted due to your account filter settings.
  2. Messages were deleted due to account compromise issues where a hacker might have gained access to your account and deleted the emails.
  3. You are using an e-mail client or a third party spam filtering software to access your account. This often happens when using the POP3 protocol to retrieve your mail and the option “Always leave a copy of the message on the server” is not selected. There are also security programs that include e-mail filtering systems. There are instances when the software configuration causes false positives causing valid messages to be either sent to the junk folder or deleted altogether.

    Please note that if you have configured your emails on your phones using POP3, you might lose your emails too if you haven't chosen to leave a copy on the server. We suggest that you use Exchange ActiveSync (EAS    ) to access your emails on your phone instead of POP3. EAS keeps everything in sync between your phone and Hotmail, so whatever you do on your phone, like delete and email, add an appointment, or update one of your contacts with a new number, will also be reflected on the web, and vice-versa.

  4. The account was marked as “Inactive." Microsoft accounts become inactive if you don’t sign in for more than 365 days or within the first 10 days after signing up for an account. After an account becomes inactive, all messages, folders, and contacts are deleted. Incoming messages will be sent back to the sender as undeliverable. Your account name is still reserved.
