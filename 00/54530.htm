kegler73 say:

Any time you turn on electronics the heat from the electricity makes components expand. Likewise when you turn it off it cools down and contracts.

Most likely the joints around the plastic case are just getting a little loose with age due to repeated expansion and contraction.

Now since it heats up really fast it's likely to expand fast enough that you hear it, but you may not hear creaking when it cools down because it cools down and contracts much slower than it heated up.

Now, it heating up so fast now that it makes the creak sound may mean that it's really dirty inside and your heat sink and fan are having a hard time dealing with the heat.

I'd suggest getting a can of compressed computer cleaner gas, like Dust Off or 3M Dust Remover, and blow it out real good. Blow it in all the vents.

Be sure to do it somewhere easy to clean up, because if you have never done this before dust is going to be going everywhere, including all over you.

Also, be sure to use a compressed gas especially made for cleaning computers. DO NOT use compressed air.

Let us know if blowing it out real good helps.
