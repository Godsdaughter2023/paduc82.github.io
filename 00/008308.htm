The formula would show 31 because the sum of B:B  for all instances of A:A being in the month of August equal 1 for each day.

joeu2004 say: emoboy wrote:
Column A is Dates (Daily several months)
Column B is Room Nights
Column C is $$$

I'm having trouble creating a formula to SUM all of B:B (and also C:C) when A:A is within a certain month.

That depends on a lot of details that you have omitted.  Perhaps the following will work for you:

Sum of room nights in July 2011:
=SUMIFS(B:B,A:A,">="&DATE(2011,7,1),A:A,"<="&DATE(2011,7,31))

Sum of costs in July 2011:
=SUMIFS(C:C,A:A,">="&DATE(2011,7,1),A:A,"<="&DATE(2011,7,31))

More generally, you might want to construct a table with month name in column D (for example) and the sum of room nights in column E.

Put actual dates into column D, starting D2 for example.  To wit:

1/1/2011
2/1/2011
...etc...

Format column D as Custom mmm if you want to see only the name of the month.  (Note:  I write dates in the form m/d/yyyy.)

Then put the following formula into E2 and copy down:

=SUMIFS($B:$B,$A:$A,">="&D2,$A:$A,"<="&EOMONTH(D2,0))
