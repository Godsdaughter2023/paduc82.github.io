lion wayne say:

How to stop "Mail Delivery System (failure) Notifications" for message I have not sent

In my Microsoft Office Outlook, for the past several weeks, I've been receiving countless "Mail Delivery System Notifications" from "Mail Administrator [*** Email address is removed for privacy ***]" regarding email I've supposedly sent to various foreign addresses (Germany, etc.)  relating to an email address I no longer have access to:: *** Email address is removed for privacy ***. I delete them, run my Malware and Spyware program, and each time I open Outlook there appears some more notifications. How can I stop this?  Thank you.

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

Sounds like someone has hijacked your address.  Receiving non-delivery reports for messages you did not send is pretty much a fact of life in today's world.  Spammers harvest addresses from all over the Internet and then send their junk using some of the addresses they've harvested.  Apparently, your address is in that lot and is being used as the sending address for junk mail.  Since those harvested addresses contain a lot of false addresses, the mail router on the domain to which the junk is sent respond to the bogus address with an NDR like the ones you've received.  Since that router thinks you are the sender, even though you're not, you get the bounce.  This is often called "backscatter"  (see http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Backscatter_(email)).  Some people are campaigning to get mail providers to stop sending non-delivery reports.

You can try to formulate one or more rules that will delete the majority of the NDRs.  Look for commonality in the messages.
