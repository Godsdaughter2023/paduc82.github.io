how do I stop password signin on windows 10 and interruption if - Microsoft Community

charles s.titone say:

how do I stop password signin on windows 10 and interruption if inactive for a few minutes ?

how can I start without password or pin and also prevent requests for pin if inactive for a few minutes??

Moved from: (Windows / Windows 10 / Desktop, Start, & personalization / Start Menu)

argel obm say:

Hi Charles,

When you use either your Microsoft account or a local account as your login to the computer, you will always need to enter the password if there is one.

If you want to prevent Windows 10 from asking for your password whenever you wake it up from sleep and if you're using your Microsoft account as your login to the computer, you can follow these steps:

  1. Click the Windows logo (Start button).
  2. Click the gear icon (Settings).
  3. Select Accounts.
  4. On the left pane, choose Sign-in options.
  5. Click the drop-down menu under Require sign-in, and then choose Never.

Please take note that following the steps above will only remove the sign-in part of Windows 10 if you will wake up your computer from sleep. You will still need to enter your password if you will restart,

turn on, or lock your computer.

If you do not want to enter any password at all, you will need to switch to a local account so that you can remove the password. To switch from a Microsoft account to a local account, do the following:

  1. Click the Windows logo (Start button).
  2. Click the gear icon (Settings).
  3. Select Accounts.
  4. On the left pane, choose Your info. (Under Your info, you will see your name and your email address. Please make sure that you will see the word "Administrator" under your email address before following the succeeding steps.)
  5. Click Sign in with a local account instead.
  6. On the pop-up window, type in your current password and click Next.
  7. Type the User name that you want. If you do not want to have any passwords, click Next.

After following the steps above, your account will now change to a local account that doesn't need a password when signing in to the computer.

If you're already using a local account and you want to delete the password and pin, follow these instructions:

  1. Click the Windows logo (Start button).
  2. Click the gear icon (Settings).
  3. Select Accounts.
  4. On the left pane, choose Sign-in options.
  5. Under Password, click Change.
  6. Type in your password, and then click Next.
  7. Click Next again.
  8. Click Finish.
  9. Under Pin, click Remove.
  10. Click Remove again.

Should you need further assistance, kindly reply to this thread.
