doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

The headers and footers are an attribute of a Section in a document.  Therefore, if you want to copy both text and the headers and footers, you will need to included in what you are copying, the "element" in the document that contains the header and footer information.  In a document with only one Section, that information is stored in the final paragraph mark in the document.  If the document contains multiple Sections, it is stored in the Section break at the end of the Section. 

If you want to copy pages 62-65 of a 70 page document, the easiest thing to do is probably copy pages 62 - 70 and after pasting into the other document, delete pages 66-70, retaining what was the final paragraph mark of the original document.

If you have other pages in the target document and you do not want the header and footer to appear on those pages, you will need to insert a Section Break before the location into which you are pasting the text from the other document.  You may then need to access the headers and footers in the pasted part of the document and unlink them from the heads and footers in the previous Section of the document so that you can delete the headers and footers from that Section of the document if they appear there.

    • Click-to-Run installation type:

    Note The 1x placeholder represents your version of Office (16 = Office 2016, 15 = Office 2013)
