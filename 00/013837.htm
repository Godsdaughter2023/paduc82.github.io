counting the occurence of a specific word in excel - Microsoft Community

jennifer a davis say:

counting the occurence of a specific word in excel

I want to count how many times the word "and" occurs in the range F2:G28.

The cells are populated with text in a sentence format.

I have tried COUNT, SEARCH and FIND but I must have some error in the formulae.

Thank you.

rajesh c. say:

Try the following array formula:

(Note: You would definitely be aware that an array formula needs to end with a CTRL+SHIFT+ENTER)

=SUM((LEN(A2:A100)-LEN(SUBSTITUTE(A2:A100," and ","")))/LEN(" and "))   CTRL+SHIFT+ENTER

Pls have a careful look at the formula the word 'and' is padded with a space - both before and after. If we do not put these leading and trailing space, then other similar words which contain 'and' as it's part will also get counted. For example   'andy' ,   'sand' ,   'brandy'  'sandra'

Hope this helps.

ashish mathur say:

amit tandon say:

Presuming "and" is the first or last word, and all other "and" are separated by space (not comma like "and,") and provided 2 of them don't appear together (like "today and and tomorrow") in which case they are counted as one, then below formula should work:

=(SUMPRODUCT(LEN(" "&F2:G28&" "))-SUMPRODUCT(LEN(SUBSTITUTE(" "&F2:G28&" "," and ",""))))/LEN(" and ")

There could be multiple "and" in a cell ex. "today and tomorrow and dayafter" and these will be counted as 2. Note: the formula is case-sensitive, and it will exclude "And". It can be made case-insensitive also.

Windows expects a running program to "interact" with Windows, and respond to Windows' requests in a timely fashion. If you type a key and the application doesn't take it because it's too busy doing something else, which could be a problem. Similarly, if you click on the Close Program "X" on a programs’ window, and the program doesn't acknowledge that, then that too is a potential problem.

When Windows asks a program to do something, like take a keystroke or close itself, and the program fails to acknowledge that request within a certain amount of time, the program is "Not Responding". If the program never comes out of that state, we might also call it "hung", as in "hung up" on something.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

There are three ways to do this:

  1. You select the URL and copy it, then press Ctrl+K or use Insert | Links | Hyperlink to open the Hyperlink dialog, where you can paste the URL into the Address box.
  2. If your AutoFormat As You Type settings have not been changed, it will suffice to just type a space at the end of the URL. This functionality is provided by the "Replace as you type: Internet and network paths with hyperlinks" feature at Office Button | Word Options | Proofing | AutoCorrect Options | AutoFormat As You Type.
  3. If this same option is also enabled on the AutoFormat tab, and if you have added the AutoFormatNow button to your Quick Access Toolbar, you can just select the URL and click the button.
