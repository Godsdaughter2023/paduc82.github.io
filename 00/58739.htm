New retail computers from OEMs like Dell are pre-activated, so there is no need to activate them on your own. Either your computer is not new, or you don't have a retail copy of Windows. david scammell say:
Hi ChoonKek,

Microsoft Surface supports POP, IMAP, and Exchange ActiveSync (EAS). 

  • When you are in the Mail app, swipe from the right to show the Charms and tap Settings.
  • Tap Accounts
  • Tap add an account
  • If you're not adding a Hotmail, Outlook, Google, AOL or Yahoo!, then simply tap Other account.
  • The next screen that appears give you a choice of Exchange ActiveSync, IMAP or POP.
So, no need to return your Surface RT tablet, it has handled POP since day 1 and does it quite nicely. I should know as I'm accessing 11 different accounts of all 3 types, including POP (which includes POP3).

EDIT: I apologize, although POP is listed, it is not currently available. However, almost all if not most POP-based email systems also provide IMAP access. I would suggest checking with your e-mail provider to see if they can provide IMAP settings.

I hope this helps!
David
