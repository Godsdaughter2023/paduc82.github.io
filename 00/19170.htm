danscoop say: Howdy, I don't see what the issue with listing the fixes would be. If nothing else, it gives good accountability. There should be a list of known issues that the engineers who work the firmware are attempting to repair. Listing the fixes gives a record of what was changed, and when. It is standard practice for all software engineering to use some sort of Current Version System (CVS) just so that anyone working the system knows what specific version they're using, and what the differences between versions are. This way, if someone detects a new bug, they can determine if it was always present and undetected, or related to a change in the firmware. If it is the latter, they have an idea where specifically to look for the repair.

I would be pretty surprised if, at the engineering level, Nokia didn't use such a system. Obviously we wouldn't need information at the resolution that the engineers are using it, but a simple change log to the effect of:
Updated SMS transmission.
Corrected Bluetooth Implementation.

It doesn't need to be specific about what change was made, but it could easily list why the change was made. As far as users being hypochondrical about their phone problems, that shouldn't be an issue. If I am suffering a bug, and the firmware claims to fix it, no problem. If I am not suffering a bug, but I trick myself into thinking I am suffering a bug that a firmware upgrade will fix, no problem.

The only possible drawback to listing what a firmware corrects is that it involves being honest about recognizing that flaws do in fact exist. For instance, I currently suffer the same malady that many users complain of, where my N91 bluetooth is incompatible with my car kit. Had I known that from the start, I'd never have purchased the phone. Nokia would have lost a customer for its honesty.

The flip side is, listing corrected bugs will help sell phones. Many phones don't work with car kits. If I knew for certain that the N91 had a new firmware that finally fixed the Bluetooth flaw, I'd post it to every BMW site I visit, and there would be a lot of new business generated. People don't often buy new cars to work with a phone, but they'll very often buy a new phone to work with a car...

If there was a list of bugs corrected, and a list of bugs identified, I'd at least be hopeful that a bug that affected me personally might one day be addressed. Instead, I'm constantly trolling message boards looking for a fix for my phone, or for a new phone that I like that will actually work as advertised with respect to my car. If I end up selling this phone because Nokia hasn't addressed the bluetooth issue before I find a phone that works properly, then they can be pretty certain that they'll never have my business again, and that I'll share my experiences with others.

Full disclosure is a good thing. I'm really impressed that Nokia has closed the loop to enable faster firmware upgrades. I am hopeful that they'll make the bug submittal and repair a bit more transparent. I think they'll find the users they have are happier, and that we'll encourage others to try a product we're clearly pleased with.

In the meantime, I'd like it if they just listed the most current firmware version and date for every phone, so I didn't have to download the whole package to determine if a newer version was available... Any ideas where I could find such a list?

Dan
