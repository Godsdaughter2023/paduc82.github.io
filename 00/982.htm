If it is a pilcrow (paragraph mark) enclosed in a dotted-line rectangle, then it is probably a style separator. These are used to provide "lead-in emphasis" or create run-in headings. To reproduce:

  1. Type a paragraph in one of Word's built-in heading styles (or any other style with an outline level other than Body Text). Press Enter.
  2. Type another paragraph in Normal style or another style with an outline level of Body Text.

I had the same issue and non of the methods on the link above worked for me, but by using the MFCMAPI tool I was able to change the folder from IPF.Appointment to IPF.Note and it resolved the issue

1. open MFCMAPI tool

7. expend Top of Information Store

10. In the Ansi TextBox change the IPF.Appointment to IPF.Note

11. Click ok

12. Wait couple of seconds and open Outlook click on Outbox and it will open as Outbox should and not as a Calendar, (if the  icon still show as a calendar, just close Outlook and open again)

Note: there is no need to start Outlook with /resetfolders or /resetfoldernames.
