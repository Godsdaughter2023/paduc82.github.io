butch pedorcik say:
Table.field1 NOT IN("1","2","3") - Where is the difference to my statement?
It just looks better, right?


No, your statement WHERE (((Table.field1)<>"1" Or (Table.field1)<>"2" Or (Table.field1)<>"3")) doesn't exclude any of these values because you are using Or.  It won't matter what the value of field1 is, this statement ends up not filtering anything out.  In other words, if field1 contains the value "2", it satisfies the condition field1 <> "1" so it would be included in your report because of your use of Or the same way that field1 containing "1" would satisfy the condition field1<>"2".

 

If your statement were WHERE (((Table.field1)<>"1" And (Table.field1)<>"2" And (Table.field1)<>"3")) it would not include records where field1 has any of those three specific values.  The statement Table.field1 NOT IN("1","2","3") would do this as well.
