considerate_guy say:

There are several factors that determine how much will fit on a DVD.  First, realize that your HD 720p footage will be down-rezzed to the DVD standard of 480p.  Second, DVD capacity is measured in GBs (about 4.7GB for single layer DVDs, 8.5GBs for dual layer DVDs).  Until the video is encoded to the DVD standard MPEG2 format and menus, etc. are added, it's not easy to tell whether a certain duration movie will fit.  It actually depends on the content, the chosen bitrate encoding, etc.

The DVD disc manufacturers say about 120 minutes should fit on a DVD, but your mileage may vary.  It's always better to underestimate (e.g. assume you can fit less on a disc) than to assume you can get every last minute of that estimate.

This is probably because the screen resolution was changed when connecting via Remote Desktop.  If you right-click your desktop and select "Screen Resolution" and change it to something small (or different) like 800 x 600, you will probably see the same effect as Windows scrambles to rearrange icons so that they all fit on the desktop.
