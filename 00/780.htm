You may notice that messages you send using your Gmail address will be sent "on behalf of" your Gmail account. This means that Outlook is actually sending the email, but setting the "From:" address to be your Gmail address. The From: header in most email clients will look something like this:

 

From: *** Email address is removed for privacy *** on behalf of Dick Craddock (*** Email address is removed for privacy ***)"

hansv mvp say:

I'm sorry, but I don't think your wishes can be fulfilled.

The ColumnHistory function, introduced in Access 2007, has the following syntax:

 

ColumnHistory(TableName, ColumnName, queryString)

 

where:

TableName is the name of a table

ColumnName is the name of a memo field in that table whose AppendOnly property has been set to Yes

queryString is an expression that specifies which record to retrieve from the table.

 

As you see, the syntax doesn't leave any room to specify additional information.

 

If you cleared the New Comment box, a line with the date and time plus an empty comment would eventually be added to the History box; I don't think you'd want that.

 

So if you really need this, you'd have to forego using Append Only and the ColumnHistory function and roll your own...
