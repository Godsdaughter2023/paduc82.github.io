Taffy's right about the common names, though in reality it's much more simple in that they try all such names since at least one will have used this on every large email service.

In my own case, when I created my first account many years ago, that combination was already taken, so I simply used one additional character/numeral in a non-traditional position, which was enough to make it both unique and not easy to guess.  However, with most major domains today there are literally dozens or hundreds with that same name, so combinations like "FirstnameSurname78 AT" aren't uncommon, for example.
