MS Word for Mac 2011 - Problem w/ Footer & Footnotes - Microsoft Community

walter keats say:

MS Word for Mac 2011 - Problem w/ Footer & Footnotes

I didn't see anything in the previous discussions about this.  I have set all 4 margins in an A5 size document to 0.6" and the footer to 0.3".

I inserted page numbers in the lower right of the footer.  Later I added some footnotes.

Unfortunately this seems to have made the bottom margin about an inch or more.  It is not clear if the footnote is actually in the footer or why it should casue the footer to increase in size, contrary to the settings I made for the whole document.

Thanks.
Walter
bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
Footnotes are not -- and cannot be -- inserted into Footers. Footers cannot overprint footnote/body text, nor can Footnotes/body content overprint Footer content. It may help to think in terms of layers, with the Header/Footer being a fixed opaque bottom layer on which everything else is stacked. Body content is a separate 'transparent' layer & Footnotes are contained in yet a third. This can be more clearly visualize by going to Word> Preferences> View to check the box for Text Boundaries. You'll see that as footnotes are added their boundary height increases & the height of the body content boundary decreases proportionately. The height of the Footer boundary, though, isn't affected in any way.Neither does the position of the margin markers change.

Footnotes [usually] are appended to the bottom of the page below the body text. As footnotes are added to any given page the body text reflows to accommodate the space they require. The basic effect is that the more space occupied by footnotes the fewer lines of body text the page can contain.

The footnotes along with the volume of body text that will fit is constrained by the margin settings. The insertion of footnote, though, does not change the position of Footer content or alter margin settings. IOW, margins determine how far content can extend toward the edges of the sheet. I.e., margins are set boundaries which control the text flow, the text doesn't control the margins.

Not knowing exactly what steps you took to create the Footer or to insert the footnotes it's difficult to say what might be giving a different impression. My first guess is that you may have used one of the built-in Footers from the Document Elements tab. If so, many of them are based on Table structures which require more height than what your .3" margin allows for. That could force the Footer height to infringe on the content above.

joanne li msft say:

Hi IComputerSaysNo,

Based on your description, I understand you want to transfer information that meet the condition to another spreadsheet.

However, it is not feasible to achieve this using formula, formula is used to perform calculations or other actions on the data in your worksheet.

There is a  simple formula that can achieve this, I’d like to use the table you provided to explain this formula.

As you assumed, the original table is on “Members” spreadsheet, we want to transfer the attendee’s information in June 2017 to “June 2017” spreadsheet.

First, in “June 2017” sheet, input column names. In A2, input “=IF(Members!D2=1,Members!A2,"")” and press enter, it should return “Mr” in A2.

Similarly, in B2, input “=IF(Members!D2=1,Members!B2,"")”, it should return “ Joe”. In C2, input “=IF(Members!D2=1,Members!C2,"")”, it should return “Bloggs”.

Next, select cells from A2 to C2, put your cursor on the bottom-right of the selected box to let the plug sign(+) appear, drag it down to let the cells below have the same format. We should get a table like the following picture.

 

Though the formula can achieve your requirement, it needs you to take several actions manually, which is inconvenient.
