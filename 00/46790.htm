robyn-- say:  

Hi Ben,

Thank you for taking the time to post on the Microsoft Answers Forums.

The problem with a laptop when it comes to gaming is the lack of upgrade possibilities. Unlike desktops were you can buy a new graphics card and replace it fairly easily, you have top end graphics, the laptop graphics are built into the main board (motherboard) and is not upgradable.

Some manufacturers did design a replaceable graphics chip in laptops called MXM: Mobile PCI Express Module, basically this was a gpu (graphics processing unit) that could be replaced with a better and more powerful chip when you wanted to. The actual idea was good, however manufacturers were put off with the high cost added on to the end product, they predicted it would put people off the laptops when they would probably by costly.

However there’s a few software based tweaks you can do to enhance and improve your laptops gaming graphics:

First go to your laptops manufacturers website and download the latest video drivers, you may need to go to the Nvidia and download the appropriate drivers.

Then make sure your DirectX drivers are all current and up to date with the latest drivers.

Finally just to be safe go to system properties>advanced>performance>advanced and then increase the windows page file, this may help your memory wise (system ram)with your games.

Also if you go into set up (press delete at start up), you can go into either advanced chipset features or integrated peripherals or change the video memory from there, this will help as well.

Remember; keep your driver up to date, as every graphic card driver update will improve your games because of fixes, new dll’s and other additions to the driver.

  • Type Troubleshooting in search bar and click it
  • Click System & Security
  • Click Power and run the troubleshooter

Method 2: If the power troubleshooter doesn’t detect any incorrect settings, then it is possible that the issue is caused by an incompatible or corrupt driver.
