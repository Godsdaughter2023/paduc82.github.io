le boule say:
How do I turn on defender when trial security expires

To prevent software conflicts, performance issues and reduced efficiency of the affected antimalware programs, Windows Defender is disabled on Win 8 when third party antimalware programs are installed on Win 8. 

 

If there is a trial version of McAfee or Norton installed you need to remove it through Control Panel, run the McAfee or Norton Removal Tool and then you can enable Windows Defender (it may enable without action by you once the reboot as occurred).  You may need to restart the Security Service after removing the third party antimalware program.

 

McAfee Removal Tool: http://service.mcafee.com/FAQDocument.aspx?id=TS101331

 

Norton Removal Tool: https://www-secure.symantec.com/norton-support/jsp/help-solutions.jsp?lg=english&ct=united+states&docid=20080710133834EN&product=home&version=1&pvid=f-home


If you are using a different third party antimalware program (other than McAfee or Norton) the same guidelines would apply - you can find the appropriate removal tool from this list:  

List of anti-malware product removal tools

 

Recommend you thoroughly review  

Windows Defender on Windows 8 - Introduction and Frequently Asked Questions

Hi Geepster,

 

What are the steps you have tried?

 

I have come across similar issue wherein, giving full control to the C:\Users\<Username>\AppData\Local\Packages folder, fixed the issue. I would suggest you to follow the steps provided below:

 

Above folder is hidden, we need to unhide it first. Refer to the below steps:

 

Step 1:

a) Press Windows and x together and select Windows Explorer.

b) Click on View tab and Click on Options at the top right corner.

c) Click the View tab, and then click Show hidden files, folders, and drives.

d) Uncheck the box which states Hide protected operating system files.

e) Click Yes to confirm, and then click OK.

 

Now browse to the folowing location:

C:\Users\<Username>\AppData\Local\

 

To give full control to the Packages folder, refer to this step.

 

Step 2:

a)      Right click on the folder and click Properties.

b)      Click Security Tab, click Edit under Group or User Names and click Add.

c)       Type “Everyone” and click check names.

d)      Click Ok.

e)      Click Full Control under Permissions for everyone.

f)       Click Apply and click Ok.

g)      Click Ok.

 

Note: After you complete the troubleshooting, follow the above method, hide the files that we have unhide.

 

Hope the information provided is useful. Do reply if you need any further help.
