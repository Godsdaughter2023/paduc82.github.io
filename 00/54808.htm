First thing I would do is to remove the HP support assistant.  Contrary to what it does, updating drivers routinely is a bad idea.  Drivers should only be updated to solve a problem, if you have one, and then only to solve that problem.  Just the affected drivers. 

Johnny P's advice to reinstall the driver is good.  If that does not work, you need to roll back the latest driver install.

  1. Start globe (bottom left)
  2. type device into the text box that pops up
  3. Click on Device manager
  4. In the Device Manager window click on the tiny yellow arrow next to DVD drives
  5. Right click on your DVD drive listing, choose Properties
  6. In the window that pops up, click on the Driver tab
  7. Now click on the button that says Roll back driver  (if it is gray, than the problem is not an updated driver)

If this is not resolved, go to the HP support web site and find your model and download the driver for your DVD.  Install that.

If that does not solve the problem, then you likely have a hardware problem that may require the replacement of the DVD drive, which is not inexpensive.
