jllatham say:

In Column B on Tab 1 use a VLOOKUP() formula.  It will look something like this (assumes your data on each tab starts at row 2) :

=VLOOKUP(A2,Sheet2!A:B,2,FALSE)

Now, that formula will return a #N/A! error if the entry on tab 1 doesn't exist on tab 2, you can prevent that from showing up by testing for the NA error this way:

=IF(ISNA(VLOOKUP(A2,Sheet2!A:B,2,FALSE)),"",VLOOKUP(A2,Sheet2!A:B,2,FALSE))

Fill the formula down the sheet next to all of your entries in column A on tab 1.

  • The document will be converted to a normal Word document.
