bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

A bit more to Bob G's reply:

The marching ants serve to indicate content on the clipboard available to be pasted.

When you cut, the ants go away as soon as you paste because cut content can be pasted only once.

When you copy, the ants persist because the content continues to be available for repeated pasting.

Pressing esc does dismiss the ants, but that's because it clears the clipboard content.

graham mayor say:

Word relies on the limits set by the printer driver and unless that driver allows printing to the edge of the page (most don't), there will be an unprintable area all around, the width of which is set by the driver.

To explore the limits set the margins to zero and click OK. The application will report an error and provide the option to Fix it. Click Fix and the margins will be reset to the driver settings.

PDF drivers do not have such limits and can 'print' to the edge of the page when viewed in a PDF viewer such as Adobe Reader. It doesn't follow that the PDF document will print to the edge of the paper when printed on a home/office printer.

Importing Word templates from a previous version - Microsoft Community

gwnatl say:

Importing Word templates from a previous version

I have a Normal.dot template from Word 2003 that contains some macros that I want to use in the Word 2010 version I have at work. How do I import them.
graham mayor say:
There are at least three ways to do this.

One is to open the 2003 template in Word and then copy the macros to your local template via the vba editor.

Another is to rename your normal.dotm template with Word closed. Put the 2003 template in the same folder and restart Word. Word will build a new template based on normal.dot. You can then save the newly created template and remove normal.dot from the folder.

The latter is only of use if there is nothing you wish to retain in the normal.dotm template you have been using.

The last method, and probably the best for an Office machine, I will suggest is to open the 2003 normal.dot template in Word 2010 then save it as a macro enabled template e.g. macros.dotm in the Word Startup folder.

You can in fact use the 2003 template as it stands in the Startup folder, but having two templates called normal can only lead to confusion.

If you have not changed the preferred startup folder it can be located (in English language versions of Windows) by typing
%appdata%\Microsoft\Word\Startup
in the Windows Explorer Address bar and pressing Enter. It will then load with Word and make its macros available to Word.

If this is a company PC, get permission to make these changes.
