doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

Select a footnote and then go to the Paragraph dialog and change the Text direction from Left to Right to Right to Left.

Then with the footnote selected Press CTRL+Shift+s and you should then see Footnote Text as the Style in the Apply Styles dialog.  Click on the Modify button in that dialog and then in the Modify Style dialog, check the Add to Quick Style list.  The Footnote Text style should then appear in the Styles gallery on the Home tab of the ribbon.  With then now Right to Left footnote selected, right click on the Footnote Text style in the Gallery and then left click on  "Update Footnote Text to Match Selection"

The reason for this round-about approach is because you cannot change the Text Direction when accessing the Format Paragraph dialog from the Modify Style dialog.

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

If the Footnote Text Style does not appear in the styles gallery access the Styles Pane and then click on the Options link to display the Style Pane Options dialog and from the Select styles to show drop down, select All styles

Then in the Styles pane, you can click on the drop down for the Footnote Text style and then on Update Footnote Text to Match Selection.

Note that unless the footnote is typed in a Right to Left language, the words will still appear in a Left to Right direction

To get the footnote separator to appear in the right to left direction, you will need to switch to Draft View and the click on Show Notes in the Footnotes section of the References tab of the ribbon and then from the dropdown in the footnotes page, select Footnote Separator and format the paragraph as right to left.  

You may want to do the same thing for the Footnote Continuation Separatore and Footnote Continuation Notice.

Now Word adopts Right-to-Left (RTL) editing features, such as setting the footnotes to the right. Edit and save the document.

You can restore the previous Left-to-right (LTR) editing features if you want to (maybe you want to compose a text with footnotes in English). Even when you restore them, the saved document when Urdu was 'set as default' should retain its RTL editing features. 

If you've already started composing, Doug's method should be used.

macropod say:

Hi Stef,

OK, that suggests you're using a 3of9 barcode and the data you're using isn't the complete barcode string, including the asterisk delimiters.

You could probably get around this by simply inserting the asterisks into the document either side of the REF field and simply formatting them in the barcode font (at the same size as the font for the REF field, of course). Alternatively, you could use a compound field, coded as:
{QUOTE "*{REF Text1}*" \* Charformat}, where 'Text1' is the name of your formfield and the barcode font is applied to the 'Q'.

Note: The field brace pairs (ie '{ }') for the above example are created via Ctrl-F9 - you can't simply type them or copy & paste them from this message.
