a_user_here say:

There seems to be quite a bit of confusion on how copy and paste works when copying files.

What you are copying to the clipboard is the list of files, not the contents of the files.

File Explorer goes through that list opening and copying them.

Now I will mention that xcopy tends to be faster than File Explorer, but that is mostly because File Explorer has to reassure the user.  As in it has to draw a nice progress bar.  To do that it needs to know exactly how many files it is transferring, and their sizes.  As it turns out that is is a "costly" operation with NTFS.  In fact if you are talking about a small file, it can take just about the same amount of time to copy it, as it does to get its size.

Anyways, I would venture to say that if you are having copy problems, it is most likely not copy and paste that is the problem.

Notice the mention that is works one way and not the other.  This really is a red flag to me that the problem has nothing to do with copy and paste.
