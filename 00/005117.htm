    To access People from Outlook.com, hover the cursor over the Outlook logo in the top left and click on the down-arrow that appears to the right of the Outlook logo.
    Select People. 

    Returning to your Inbox from People:
    Hover the cursor over the People logo and click on the down-arrow that appears on the right of the People logo. Select Mail.  
    You can also get to Calendar or Skydrive in the same manner.

    Filtering your list:
    Above the search box at the top of the Contact list on the left, is a filter option which says "All ". 
    If you click on this you should see the groups you created in the past.  
    Click on any of these to filter your list to just the contacts in that group.

    Selecting one or many Contacts:
    If you click on a contact name in the list, the details for that contact will appear on the right.  
    Alternatively, if you click on the check box to the left of the contact picture, you can build up a list of selected contacts on the right by clicking on the check box to the left of multiple contacts.

    Adding/Removing from Group membership:
    Whether you select one or many contacts, you will see a top menu bar option for Groups.  
    If you click on this, then it will display the list of groups with a check box checked for each group that the contact(s) is/are a member of. 

    You can uncheck to remove or check to add, the selection of contact(s) to the chosen groups, and then click on Apply to apply the changes.  
    Your selected contacts will then be updated to be added or removed from the groups you chose as appropriate.

    Delete
    When you select one or many contacts, you will see a top menu bar option for Delete.  
    If you click on Delete and click Delete in the confirmation box, the selected contacts will be deleted.

    Edit:
    You can choose edit if you have clicked on a single contact (name or picture) in the list on the left to see their details on the right.  
    When viewing their details, click on Edit in the top menu bar to edit or add details.

    Browsers:
    We recommend that you keep your web browser up to date to get the most out of the People experience.
    If you’re not using an updated browser, some parts of People might not work and it might not display properly.

     To update your browser, click here:
    http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows/outlook/supported-browsers
    If you are unable to upgrade your browser then you can view your contacts using the mobile link: 
    http://profile.live.com/contacts?bfv=um

    Work vs Duration - Microsoft Community

    christopherporter1 say:

    Work vs Duration

    I understand the basic equation Duration/Units = Work. Is there a way to schedule and divest duration from work?

    For instance, I will have a task lasting 3 months with an unknown actual work quantity (A specific hiring process). We would like to show this on the schedule as a certain 90 day time frame and assign a resource for that duration, but not have the work total display the total 90* 8 = 270 hrs. We want to assign 60 hours thru that 3 month period as an estimate of work. 

    Suggestions?

    Thanks in advance for any assistance!

    dale howard [mvp] say:

    ChristopherPorter1 --

    Really good question, my friend.  Before you enter either Duration or Work for the task in question, double-click the task and then click the Advanced tab of the Task Information dialog. Click the Task Type pick list and select the Fixed Duration option, then click the OK button. This action "locks" or fixes the Duration of the task so that it will not change when you adjust the Work value.  For the task in question, enter a Duration value of 90 days and then press the Enter key on your computer keyboard.

    Next, right-click anywhere in the Gantt Chart pane and select the Show Split item on the shortcut menu. In the Gantt Chart pane, select the task in question.  In the Task Form pane, select the name of the resource you want to assign to the task and then enter 60h of Work.  Click the OK button in the Task Form pane to make the resource assignment.

    The result is that you have a task with a Duration of 90 days and a resource assigned to perform approximately 60 hours of Work on the task.  Hope this helps.

    trevor rabey say: Also, after you do what Dale says, switch to the task usage view and you should see that MSP has distributed the 60 hours uniformly across the 90 days duration. You can fine tune the work profile.

      The Barcode command on the Ribbon or the Quick Access Toolbar is supported only if you're connected to a SharePoint document library in SharePoint 2010 (that support was removed beginning with SharePoint 2013, according to this article). For your purposes, the button is useless.

      Office 2013 and later added the DISPLAYBARCODE field and the MERGEBARCODE field for displaying text or numbers in any of 10 barcode formats. The syntax for the DISPLAYBARCODE field is given in https://msdn.microsoft.com/en-us/library/hh745901(v=office.12).aspx, and the syntax for the MERGEBARCODE field is in https://msdn.microsoft.com/en-us/library/hh745903(v=office.12).aspx. Unfortunately, these fields have not been added to the list in the Insert > Quick Parts > Field dialog box or even to the Help topic about field codes, so they have to be built manually (using Ctrl+F9 to insert the field braces).
