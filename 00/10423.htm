OK, general observation first :-) You're probably having difficulty in Excel because:

  • Each table differs not just in column widths but also number of columns
  • You're trying to 'stack' all the tables one below the other as you would in Word

You're right -- that's virtually impossible to do in Excel without merging cells -- but merging cells is something I wouldn't recommend to my worst enemy. However, if you put each table on a separate worksheet you can do whatever you want with each one. Alternatively, they could be arranged side-by-side on the same sheet which would allow for variation of column widths but would then impose the consideration for variation of row heights.

At any rate -- although I respect my friend Jimmy's ongoing campaign to promote a flat file data storage program as a suitable substitute for a relational data management system :-) -- I truly believe it's more than you need or want to get involved with. If you're just looking to have individual cells in the Word table linked to specific cells in the Excel document;

  1. In Excel copy the source cell
  2. In Word, click in the destination cell
  3. Go to Edit> Paste Special
  4. Click Paste Link
  5. Select Unformatted Text
  6. Click OK
  7. Repeat as required for each additional cell.

malin d say:

In order to achiieve this, divide your document into sections. This means that instead of placing a page break between chapter 1 and 2 etc. - Place a section break there instead.

Section breaks will allow Word to change information that normally applys to the entire document (like header/footer, margins, orientation etc.).

You will find section breaks in the Page Layout tab and the Breaks button.
If you do not have special neeeds the Section Break Next Page option is usually the easiest.

After dividing the document into sections then enter the header footer area and jump between the sections with the "next" and "previous" buttons.

 

The important thing to notice is that if of want a section to be unique you will have to unselect the "Link to previous" button in the header/footer tab.

 

Jump through all your sections and unselect the link to previous.

 

When this is done, add the desired information in the header/footer and each section may now have it's unique content.

 

Good luck.

Malin D
