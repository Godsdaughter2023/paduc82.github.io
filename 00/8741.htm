suzanne s. barnhill say:
Word does not normally AutoCorrect punctuation at the beginning of lines except when the beginning of the line is the beginning of a paragraph. Word (reasonably enough) assumes that the beginning of a paragraph is the beginning of a sentence. Display nonprinting characters using Ctrl+* or the Show/Hide ¶ button in the Paragraph group on the Home tab. Do you see a ¶ symbol at the end of each line? If so, this means that you have pressed Enter and thus started a new paragraph. If you need to end a line before it wraps naturally, you can use a line break (Shift+Enter) to start a new line without starting a new paragraph.
