pa bear - ms mvp say:

Yes, it really matters.

If you were migrating from XP to Vista or Win7, you could use Windows Easy Transfer to transfer the data to the new computer...but you cannot use Easy Transfer when migrating from Vista to WinXP.

Similarly, if you were migrating from XP to XP, you could use Files and Settings Transfer (FAST) wizrd to transfer the data to the new computer...but you cannot use FAST when migrating from Vista to WinXP.

This leaves you with one option: Backing up your IE Favorites (bookmarks) and then importing them into IE on the WinXP computer. Unfortunately, I don't know of any way to back up your passwords, profiles, history, etc.

References:

• Import or export favorites in Internet Explorer (applies to WinXP & IE6 despite what the page says) 
http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows-vista/Import-or-export-favorites-in-Internet-Explorer
