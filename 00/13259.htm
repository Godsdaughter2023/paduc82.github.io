Method 1:
In your second sheet (e.g. the one that has dates in the even numbered columns), put this formula into A1,
=INDIRECT("Sheet1!"&CHAR(INT(COLUMNS($A:A)/2)+65)&ROW())
and then press Ctrl+C (e.g. Copy). Press F5 (e.g. GoTo) and type the following into the Reference text box,
C1,E1,G1,I1,K1,M1,O1,Q1,S1,U1,W1    (<-you cannot copy and paste this because you have A1 copied)
... and click OK in the lower right. Press Ctrl+V (e.g. Paste). The formula will paste into alternating cells from C1 to W1. You can then Fill Down as necessary.

Method 2: (more straight-forward but a bit more work)
In your second sheet (e.g. the one that is going to get dates in the even numbered columns but does not already have them), put this formula into A1,
=IF(ISODD(COLUMN()),INDIRECT("Sheet1!"&CHAR(INT(COLUMNS($A:A)/2)+65)&ROW()),"")
... then fill right to column W and then fill down A1:W1 as far as necessary. You can erase the formulas in the even columns in preparation for putting in the dates.

    jay freedman say:

    Sorry, that's not correct. You can use AutoCorrect to insert images.

    Insert the image, such as a jpg or gif, into any document. While that image is still selected, go to Office button > Word Options > Proofing > AutoCorrect Options. The "Formatted text" option button should automatically be selected, but click it if it isn't selected yet. Enter a "name" for the entry in the Replace box and click the Add button.
