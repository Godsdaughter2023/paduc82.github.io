    tricky300 say:

    Firstly you could just pull the Ethernet lead if it is a wired adapter. Or delete the wireless network details if it is a wireless adapter.

    Some adapters might be disabled via BIOS setup, in which case they will not even be visible to Windows as available hardware. These adapters would be "on-board" adapters, i.e. the Ethernet cable plugs into a socket on the motherboard's I/O panel not the socket on a separate add-in card.

    Add-in cards can be safely removed after powering down, disconnecting the mains lead and taking antistatic precautions.

    Alternatively you can...

    • Click Start
    • Click Run
    • Type ncpa.cpl and hit Enter

    From the resultant window you can right click Ethernet adapters (wired or wireless) and then click disable.

    Your computer's display consists of two pieces of hardware: a video card (also known as a graphics card or display adapter), which is installed inside your computer, and the video monitor, where you see all the text and graphics your programs display. Windows uses drivers to work with these hardware devices. The majority of display problems are caused by incorrect, corrupted, or missing video drivers. Hardware failure or incorrect installation make up another set of problems, and external factors can also cause video problems.

    If you have just installed Windows, or have changed your video card and are experiencing problems, the most likely cause is a missing or incorrect video driver. Most video cards will come with a disk that contains drivers and other software. Before you install it, check the information that came with the video card to be sure it supports your current version of Windows. If not, go to the manufacturer's website and download the most current driver for your version of Windows.
