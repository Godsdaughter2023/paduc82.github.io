bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

By default, the Equation engine in Word 2016 for Mac uses the Cambria Math font which is "Times-like"... assuming you mean a serif font. If you mean something other than that you'll have to be more specific - there are several members of the Times font family, each with its own distinct attributes.

Appropriate math fonts can be designated in place of Cambria Math by using the Format> Equation Options dialog as shown in the video. However, what I find here is that the program still will use Cambria Math despite the specified default. Each equation has to be reformatted directly.

FWIW, I'd stay with Cambria Math for exactly the reason you cite as #1. My guess is that if an unavailable font were used Word would substitute Cambria Math but I have no idea how well it would display. I'm pretty sure it would have an impact on layout, though, because the font you mention seems to be a good bit narrower than Cambria Math.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

How are you creating the new document? If you use Office Button | New | Blank Document, you should get a document based on your Normal.dotm, which should incorporate your changes providing they have actually been saved. Sometimes an add-in (PDF Complete is one notorious culprit) prevents Normal.dotm from actually being saved, and it is necessary either to get rid of the add-in or, as a temporary stopgap, to open Normal.dotm directly for editing. If you think that's the issue, we can provide more help on that.

Alternatively, if you are using a New Office Document shortcut on the Start menu or Desktop in Windows, then there is a known "issue" that documents created that way are based on the default (out-of-the-box) Normal.dotm.

Hi Vasil,

I now understand your requirement. The answer is yes. You can follow the steps below to achieve it:

1. Login to your Office 365 admin center
