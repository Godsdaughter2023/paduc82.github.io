tryx3 say:

Sara,

I also see the same behaviour as you.  It is not a fault.

  • Clicking on the ToDo bar's calendar entry [the "Calendar Peek"] only controls the list of appointments shown beneath it.  It does not control the date shown in the Calendar view itself
  • Double-clicking on a date in current month** displayed in the Calendar Peek the main Calendar view opens to show that date [and it sometimes also closes the ToDo bar but I have not figured out what the pattern of behaviour is in this regard]. 
  • If your last Calendar view was single day then the date double-clicked on will be shown in the main Calendar view.   
  • If your last Calendar view was of multiple days then the date double-clicked on will be shown in the main Calendar view as one of the days shown, the number of days in view does not change. 

** If you double-click on one of the dates of the preceding month or following month that you can see in the Calendar peek then the date that appears in the main Calendar view is anybody's guess.  Outlook just throws a wobbly on this one. 

The equivalent of the ToDo bar "Date picker" / "Date navigator" that you were used to in Outlook appears in the left-hand pane [the "Folder pane", which can be turned on or off on the main View tab]. 

  • When you select the Calendar view, the date picker appears and it shunts down your folder list / shortcuts list. 
  • You can use it to select the date or range of dates that appear in the Calendar view. 
  • You can drag it down at the bottom to expand the number of months shown but that does shunt your own folder list / shortcuts list even further down the window. 
  • Unlike Outlook 2007, the Date picker in Outlook 2016 cannot be shown on the right-hand side instead.  There does not seem to be any solution to this.
