bernie deitrick say:

Taking an action on a worksheet using VBA clears the undo stack.  So, every time you enter a value in that sheet, your code is taking an action - putting in the date / time stamp in column B.

You could keep track of all the old values on another sheet - a bit of a pain, and it can be buggy, but it can be done:   John Walkenbach has example code and a power utility that offers it, IIRC.  You just need to decide if having the date/time stamp in column B is worth not having undo available, or if adding in some level of undo is worth the added complexity.

Open letter to Microsoft regarding Windows 8 and Windows Phone 8 - Microsoft Community

millerwdjr say:

Open letter to Microsoft regarding Windows 8 and Windows Phone 8

Hello all,

 

I've included a letter I just sent to Steve Ballmer and Joe Belfiore highlighting some key points I think could greatly improve some of our problems with Windows 8 and Windows Phone 8. I would greatly appreciate it if everyone would raise the status of this comment. I feel that if this letter gets enough attention, there could certainly be some improvements to our situation. I realize the forum may not be perfect for this, but I figured it was worth a shot.

 

Dear Mr. Ballmer and Mr. Belfiore,

First off, I'd like to take the opportunity to thank you for reading this e-mail. I realize that you are quite busy and I'm sure that you get thousands of e-mails each day, so thank you for taking the time to read mine.

If I may, I'd like to give you a little background on myself. My name is Bill and I'm currently a graduate student. My area of study is in a field called Human Factors, which encompasses areas of user experience, interface design, design intuitiveness, which I'm sure you would agree are key points that Microsoft has been focusing on with the introduction of Windows 8 and Windows Phone 8. While I don't consider myself a UX professional, at least not yet, I do consider myself as someone who is well-informed when it comes to technology, and personally, I've been a big champion of Microsoft and the recent changes Microsoft has made to head in a brand new direction. In fact, I'm so much of a champion that I've ditched my iPhone for a Lumia 920, upgraded my laptop to Windows 8 the day it came out, and ditched Gmail for the new Outlook.com.

If I may, I'd like to offer you 4 key things you could do that would greatly improve the experience on Windows 8 and Windows Phone 8. Make no mistake about it, I think the software is a great start, but I think they need work, and I feel that these points would certainly address some of the issues.

1. Please unite Windows 8 and Windows Phone 8. Currently, the 2 devices are almost totally segregated, with no real means to sync the data on a computer to a phone. The Windows Phone app, while fairly attractive, is just not as functional as it needs to be. I hate to name-drop other products, but what will convert people to Windows Phone is an experience similar to or better than that of Apple, where changes made on the PC sync to the phone, and changes to the phone sync to the PC. Currently, this doesn't exist, and it's really frustrating the Windows Phone faithful, particularly those that previously used Windows Phone 7 or the Zune Software.

2. Empower Windows 8 apps, don't cripple them. It seems clear to me that the Xbox Music app on W8 is designed to be the absolute media hub on Windows. But an absolute media hub requires that the software be full-functioned. While I understand that it is very early in the app's life, and that more changes are coming, to be unable to add/remove or change song information, as well as album artwork makes the software feel less like something I want to use, and more like something I'm forced to accept. Similarly, echoing my first point, translate the beautiful look and feel of Xbox Music to WP8. Why is it that the song info and album artwork that I have set up in Xbox Music on my computer don't translate to my phone? When Xbox Music has the properly specified album artwork and artist information, it is the most beautiful music app out there. But that app must be powerful and consistent, not just beautiful. The same goes for the Calendar app. I should be able to manage my calendars from the app, not from the Windows Live website. I understand that again, a transition process is in play here, but you want people to want to use W8 apps for their beauty and their features. Right now, Microsoft's focus comes off as being more about beauty and less about features.

3. Teach Windows 8 and Windows Phone 8 what the word “sync” really means. Too many times I've noticed throughout using WP8 and W8 that the software doesn't pick up on changes the user makes. For example, when I delete or move pictures in the Pictures app on W8, for days, those thumbnails still persist, with the app failing to show the appropriate contents of the photo library. The same thing occurs with Xbox Music, where songs just don’t show up, regardless of being located in the library. These apps should always poll their library’s contents every time the app is opened and closed, constantly updating the index with the most recent information. In essence, there should always be a refresh command that is executed every time an app is opened or closed. This same “refresh” behavior should be in apps like the Mail app in WP8. It shouldn’t be the case that when I read an e-mail, I need to press the “Sync” charm before I delete it in order for the e-mail to not be marked as “unread” when it’s in the trash. Similarly, when I delete an e-mail, I shouldn’t have to press the “Sync” charm in order for that e-mail to be placed in the trash in Windows 8’s Mail app. Every action in mail, be it reading, deleting, moving, or marking, should instantly transmit a “Sync” command so that the server and all the clients have up-to-date information, and that when new actions are performed, the clients know to refresh themselves and reflect the most current information. Even the lock-screen background on Windows Phone 8 could use this. When you update the Bing photo of the day, if my lock photo is a photo of Bing, the user should have the option to have that background change every day, or stay the same. These are small touches that I know would greatly improve the experience of both Windows 8 and Windows Phone 8. Leverage the speed that both of these software programs have, don’t slow things down my making users press and “Sync” charm and wait for things to update.

4. Focus on the little things even more. So many things about W8 and WP8 that make it great are the little touches, like customizing the menus in apps and eliminating some of the fiddly parts of apps in lieu of cleaner designs. Personally, I don't think anyone can argue about abolishing the right-click menu in W8 apps. The solution for allowing the camera app to pull other apps into the app and use their specific functions using Lenses is downright elegant. And coming from an iPhone, I can't tell you how much I appreciate a keyboard that offers appropriate suggestions and lets you teach it words, instead of constantly just changing things around. But there are so many little touches that are missing, as well, and in most cases, people notice the have-nots more than the haves. Why is there no support for a picture password on WP8, particularly when that's such an emphasized feature in Windows 8 advertising? Why, when entering advanced information, into an e-mail account, is there no option to specify a port? (Yes, I realize that adding ":587" etc. at the end of a server solves that issue, but most people don't.) Why is there no shortcut to go to the top of a webpage, like tapping at the top of the screen? Why does the menu bar at the top disappear when certain apps are opened, or why don't I have the option to keep that status information present at all times? Why doesn't the Mail app allow me to select what address to use when replying when I have a linked account, or at least understand where an e-mail was sent to and pick the appropriate address automatically? Why is there no week view for Calendar? Why is there no global search on the phone? Why can't I highlight specific characters in a word, or cut words? Why isn't there undo or redo functionality when typing? Why does highlighting text seem so finicky in general? These are relatively simple changes, but ones that would improve usability to such a degree that competitors like Apple and Android would have no advantage over you.

Make no mistake about it, I'm thrilled with the direction Microsoft is heading in, and I'm happy with my purchases, but as the 3rd phone ecosystem, Windows should find its niche, and then capitalize on it. You currently have an ease of use that's close to Apple, with a customizability that's slightly less than Android. You can absolutely thrive in that space. I don't need 1800 widgets and be susceptible to viruses to have a good phone, and I don't need a phone that is boring, restrictive, and who's yearly improvement centers around a better camera and a loyalty card holder. I love what WP8 does. I love being able to use a true version of Office on my phone (which I used to write this e-mail). I love living in a Microsoft ecosystem with Skydrive, W8, and all the Xbox extensions that are currently developing. But the major selling point to consumers will be if the user experience from WP8 is truly a no-compromise solution (excluding the app selection). If it is, then people will be attracted to the hardware, speed, and ecosystem that WP8 offers, which is something that Android can't offer, and up to this point, Apple hasn't been able to offer.

I really hope that this e-mail will not fall on deaf ears. I hope that my feedback is constructive and that it will help improve the overall experience of the new direction Microsoft is taking. Please, if you have any questions or would care to talk, feel free to contact me. I look forward to seeing Windows 8 and Windows Phone 8 improve.
