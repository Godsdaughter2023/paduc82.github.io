Apparently it is not a software issue, but a hardware one. Found a solution (temporary) on another forum as follows:

The problem has hardware origin. There is a flat cable behind the battery, leading from the upper circuit board, to the bottom small circuit, which contains two microphones, and the loudspeaker on it. The problem is with the contact on the upper circuit board - it's the weak point. Dropping the Phone, or sitting on it may cause contact fail, and get stuck in flight mode, or deaf microphone, or faulty loudspeaker, etc.. You may try pressing the phone's back cover, between the camera and the lower volume button - if it is the contact issue, it may help. Or disassemble the plastic cover above battery, and try to disconnect-reconnect the cable. It has very tiny precise contacts, so be careful, not to deform them! I had to put some layers of sticky tape on the connector, to have a constant pressure on it when i screwed the plastic cover back. It solved my deaf microphone and flight Mode issue.

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

No disrespect to my friend Rich, but for what you're referring to there's no need to go quite that far. The setting you need to change is on the TOC page of the Index & Tables dialog.

Word can include all 9 Outline Levels in the TOC but by default it only uses the first 3 (Heading 1-3). Further, it doesn't automatically adjust to include others even if you use Heading4-9. If you want to include more increase the Show Levels value accordingly.
