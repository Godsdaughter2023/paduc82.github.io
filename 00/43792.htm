mnmama say:

Satellite Toshiba laptop changing wallpaper results in a blurred and enlarged background. ____

Originally I used one of the VISTA supplied backgrounds, and it was fine (1920x1200).  I wanted to change to a christmas-themed wallpaper and went to http://www.christmas-corner.com/wallpaper.php   I saved a bunch of the thumbnails(.jpg), and then picked one to use as my background (110x68).  I was successful at changing the background to the desired wallpaper, but it is enlarged and blurry.   Is there a way to modify the image's dimensions to use it as wallpaper (widescreen) to maintain the clarity and focus of the picture?  Thanks in advance!
mohammad _ imran say:

Hi Mnmama,

Welcome to Microsoft Answers!

You may download high resolution pictures and set it as wall paper.

The primary reason for this distortion is caused by the difference in size between the image and your systems display ‘resolution’ or screen size. If, for example, the size of the image that you chose as your wallpaper is 300 x 200 and your screen resolution

is set at 800 x 600, than Windows is going to ‘blow up’ the image by a factor of 2.67 for the width x 3 for the height. This will cause two changes to the image. First, when you increase the size of an image, it becomes very ‘pixilated’ or blurry because there are not enough ‘dots’ or ‘pixels’ to fill the larger space.

The easiest thing to do is to allow the image to be displayed in its original size, which will not allow it to cover the entire background, but the image will be clean.

To do this, right-click anywhere on the background, then select Properties. This will open a ‘Display Properties’ dialog box with several tabs across the top.

Select the ‘Desktop’ tab, then look towards the lower right side of the box for the ‘Position:’ setting. It should currently say ‘Stretch’ which can be changed to either ‘Center’ or ‘Tile.’

‘Center’ will place the image in the center of the screen at its original size and ‘Tile’ will repeat the image to cover the entire background.

If the image appears very small when you select ‘Center,’ then it’s true resolution is very low and may not be a good candidate as a Wallpaper image.

If you want to know what your current screen resolution is, click on the ‘Settings’ tab and look at the ‘Screen Resolution’ towards the bottom left.

Hope this was helpful.
