john korchok say:

The parts of a PowerPoint presentation are related to one another. The master slide (View>Master>Slide Master on the OS X menu, View>Slide Master on the Ribbon, look for the larger slide at the top) is parent to the slide layouts (the layouts are the smaller slides below the master). In turn, the layouts are parents to the slide. If one of the parents is damaged, it's children no longer have a reliable source of information.

On your slide master, you deleted all the placeholders. The text placeholder on the master would normally tell the content or text placeholders what formatting to follow. Since it's missing, PowerPoint gets easily confused. If you add the placeholders to the master again, PowerPoint may be able to reconnect the master to the layouts. If it can't, you'll have to delete the text/content placeholders on the layouts and replace them with new ones, which should then correctly receive the style information.

On the slide master text placeholder, create levels that have as much common styling as possible. Perhaps level 1 text one one layout will be 16 point bold with a bullet, while on another layout it is 16 point bold without a bullet. On the slide master, make level 1 16 point bold with a bullet, then remove the bullet on the layout that doesn't need it.

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

You might need to use a macro with code such as

Dim rng As Range

Dim i As Long, n As Long

With Selection.Tables(1).Columns(n)

    For i = 2 To .Cells.Count 'assumes first row is a header row

        Set rng = .Cells(i).Range

        rng.End = rng.End - 1 'eliminate the end of cell marker from the range

        rng.Font = "Free 3 of 9"

    Next i

End With
