Starter is not actually installed on your machine in the "traditional" way. It is run inside of a "Virtual Computer" that is also known as "click-to-run" or CTR. It requires a connection to the internet.

Since it is not actually installed on your machine the things we can do to fix it are very limited:

HP instructions on Office Starter Repair, Program Manager, Uninstall Starter and Re-install Starter via Program Manager in Pictures

The best instructions I've seen for "fixing" Office Starter are this HP page:

http://h10025.www1.hp.com/ewfrf/wc/document?cc=uk&lc=en&dlc=en&docname=c02981221#N272

Starter is not actually installed on your computer. It runs inside of a “virtual computer” located in the “Q:\” drive and the program code is downloaded over the internet every time the program is started. Therefore there are limited things we as users can do to "fix" starter:

1.       Run an Office Repair on Starter
