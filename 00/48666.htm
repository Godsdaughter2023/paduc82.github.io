Well, there may actually be a problem with the power supply, it is not all that uncommon for a power supply to develop a problem on a single circuit as opposed to the entire unit to fail all at once. The code may very well be a code generated by the OEM firmware initiated during post or boot. If a new power supply is needed I would recommend going to a higher rated wattage power supply than the OE PSU, there is no such thing as over powering a PC, only spending more money than one needs to (as in going overboard with the purchase). The PSU will only deliver the amount of current that the device is calling for, not more than.

c. If the Speakers sound option is Disabled, right-click Speakers, and then select the Enable option.
 
The sound should now be restored. If the Speakers sound device is still not working properly, then verify whether the sound device is enabled in Device Manager.

If there is no sound from the speakers, follow the steps below to determine the state of the sound hardware within the Device Manager.

b. Click on Sound, video and game controllers to expand the list of sound devices installed on the computer.
c. Determine if the sound device in question is enabled or disabled.
 
·         If a down arrow displays on the volume icon, the sound device is disabled. Go to Updating sound driver.
·         If a down arrow does not display on the volume icon, the sound device is enabled. Go to Verify that the sound device is enabled in Device Manager.
 
d. Right-click the name of the sound device, and then click Enable. If problems persist, then follow step e.
e. Right-click the name of the device, select Properties, and then view the message in the Device status field. The Device status field contains either:
