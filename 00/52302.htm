paul a. smith say: Hello Robbielock.

There's two things it could be, either the fans in your computer, in which case they could need cleaning, or replacing.  Or worse, it could be your hard disk failing, if its more like a grinding/clicking sound its probably the hard disk.

I'd suggest opening up the side of the case and see if you can locate where the noise is coming from.  If it is a hard disk, backup your data immediately and replace the drive.

Usually its the little fan on the motherboard that tends to go to the grave early, so I'd suspect its that.  But try and rule out the hard disk. :)

Benjamin Spain's post on November 1, 2015:

This approach worked for me as well.

My Surface Book state was:

- Clipboard clipped into Keyboard Dock

- Both batteries were visible by the OS, and charging normally

- Ports on the Keyboard Dock were functional (USB and DisplayPort)

- Hovering over Detach icon showed "Surface Detach: Ready to Detach"

- Keyboard and Trackpad WERE NOT FUNCTIONING

- Pressing the Detach button on the Keyboard would only cause it to blink green for a few seconds, CLIPBOARD WOULD NOT DETACH

Failed solutions:

- Shutdown Surface Book using Start -> Shutdown

- Power on Surface Book using Power button + Volume Up to enter BIOS

    The Surface Book entered into the BIOS, but the Keyboard could not be used to navigate within the BIOS.

Successful Solution:

- Shutdown Surface Book using Power Button + Volume Up

   The OS acted as through CTRL+ALT+Delete had just been pressed, but I proceeded to hold Power Button + Volume Up until the Surface logo appeared following the reboot cycle.

   The Surface Book did restart, and I saw the Detach button light blink Red momentarily (something that hadn't happened under my failed solutions)

  The Surface Book entered the BIOS screen, AND THE KEYBOARD WORKED within the BIOS

As such, the key for me was using "Power Button + Volume Up" as a means of shutting down the Surface Book, and holding that keyboard combo through the reboot cycle until the "Surface" logo posted on restart.

ramesh.kumar say:

Hi,

Thank you for posting your query on Microsoft Community.

I understand that the USB port stopped working on your computer. The issue may arise if the currently loaded USB driver has become unstable or corrupt or if your PC requires an update for issues that may conflict with a USB device and Windows. This may also occur if your USB controllers may have become unstable or corrupt. There might be a chance that USB port get corrupted or damaged.

First, I would suggest you to check the issue in different ports. If issue persists, I would suggest you to try the following steps and check if the issue persists.

Method 1:

Please try the fixit(s) provided below which will help you resolve common issues related to USB ports and devices.

  • Hardware devices are not working or are not detected in Windows

If Method 1 does not help, try method 2.

Method 2:

Remove and reinstall all USB controllers.

  1. Open Device Manager by pressing Windows key + X and select device manager from the list. If you're prompted for an administrator password or confirmation, type the password or provide confirmation.
  2. In the list of hardware categories, find and expand Universal Serial Bus controllers.
  3. Right-click every device under the Universal Serial Bus controllers node, and then click Uninstall to remove them one at a time.
  4. Restart the computer, and let the USB controllers get reinstalled.
  5. Plug in the removable USB device, and then test to make sure that the issue is resolved.

If the issue still exists, proceed to Method 3.

Method 3:

Disable USB selective suspend setting. This method prevents your USB device from powering down.

  1. Press Windows key + Q, type power plan in the Search box, then click Choose a power plan.
  2. Next to your currently selected plan, click Change Plan Settings.
  3. Click Change advanced power settings.
  4. Click the box to expand USB Settings, then expand USB selective suspend settings.
  5. Click Plugged in, then click the drop down menu and select disabled.
  6. If you're using a laptop, click Battery, then click the drop down menu and select disabled.
  7. Click Apply, then click OK.   

In addition to that I would also suggest you to update the USB drivers from the link provided below and see if it helps you to update the drivers or not.
