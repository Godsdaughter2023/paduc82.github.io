steve rindsberg say:

4) I am trying to create a slide master template that has one Title Master, and at least four different Slide Masters. 

 

You need to think abut this slightly differently.  As John's explained, a presentation can have many Slide Masters.  Each Slide Master may or may not have an associated Title Master.

If you choose Slide Master A for your new slide and give it a Title layout, one of two things will happen:

1) If Slide Master A has an associated Title Master, PowerPoint will use that to format the new title slide.

2) If there's no Title Master, PowerPoint will apply its own default formatting for the new title slide.

 

>> I have created all five of the slides in the Master View, but when I close the Master View and insert a new slide, only the first of the four

 

PowerPoint never shows you the Title Masters.  It simply applies them automatically(if they exist) when you choose to add a title slide.

 

> Is there a way to set this up so I can choose which of the four slide masters to use?  (The layout of the four slide masters is the same--the only difference is a different customer logo on each of the four).

 

You either insert a new slide and then choose the slide master you want it to use ( Format | Slide Design ) or duplicate an existing slide that has the master you want to use, or insert a new slide; PowerPoint will use the same master for the newly inserted slide as the slide you're currently viewing.
