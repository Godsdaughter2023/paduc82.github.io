jay freedman say:

The wildcard syntax to find one or two digits enclosed in parentheses is this:

\([0-9]{1,2}\)

The \( and \) at the beginning and end will match the opening and closing parentheses. The expression [0-9]{1,2} matches one or two of the digits specified by [0-9].

al edlund say:

I haven't found a limit to the number of row/fields, but. Be aware that when you link a row to a shape all of the fields in the row are added to the shape, not just the ones you want to work with. As an example if the row has thirty fields and you only want the user to see two of them, all thirty are added to the properties section. Just because you can add the data does not mean that the performance will be acceptable.

The shape data window has a fixed capacity and can't be changed.
