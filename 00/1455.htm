jay freedman say:
In Word 2013, click File and then click Options. On the General page of the Options dialog, click the Office Theme dropdown and change it from White to Dark Gray. (Of course, Microsoft's idea of dark gray is considerably lighter than anyone else's, but that's the best you can get in this version.) Don't bother with the Office Background setting, which just puts some cartoony things in the right side of the title bar.

This setting affects all of the Office 2013 programs at once.

A bit of info about that Personalization: In Windows 7, the dialog that allows independently setting the colors of the window components is available only if you choose one of the six themes in the Basic and High Contrast Themes. After choosing one of those themes, click the Window Color link at the bottom.

In Windows 8, the two "basic" themes, Windows 7 Basic and Windows Classic, have been removed and only the High Contrast themes remain. You can customize the colors of those themes, although not as many of the window components are listed and they don't have the same effect as in XP. For example, choosing the White high-contrast theme and setting its Window Background to a dark gray changes the color of Word's background and the "paper" area and parts of the ribbon and Quick Access Toolbar. That's no better than the original all-white.
