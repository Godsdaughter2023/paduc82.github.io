jaeson cardaño say: Hi,

Assuming that your table ranging A1:B100 then use this:

=MEDIAN(IF(A1:A100>=DATE(2013,2,1),IF(A1:A100<=DATE(2013,2,4),B1:B100)))

You may use to change manually those in bold for the dates.

=MEDIAN(IF(A1:A90>=D1,IF(A1:A90<=E1,B1:B90)))

As both suggested formula are Array formula, press Ctrl+Shift+Enter upon putting it in a cell so that the brackets may appear.

subhass say:

Hi Adrian,

 

The ShapeSheet is located on the Developer tab, which is not displayed by default. To show the Developer tab, do the following:

  1. Click the File tab.
  2. Under Help, click Options.
  3. Click Customize Ribbon.
  4. Under Customize the Ribbon, select the Developer check box.

To open the ShapeSheet, do the following:

  • On the Developer tab, in the Shape Design group, click the arrow below Show ShapeSheet and then do one of the following:
    • To open the ShapeSheet for a selected shape, click Shape.
    • To open the ShapeSheet for the active page, click Page.
    • To open the ShapeSheet for the entire drawing, click Document.

 

Hope this helps!
