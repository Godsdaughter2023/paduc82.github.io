this is setup exactly the way my host tells me to. except right now, the boxes for secure connection aren't checked.


You have to follow the provider's instructions precisely, unless you can establish by testing that some other set of settings works more reliably. For a start, hosted mail accounts will require you to authenticate yourself to the SMTP server - otherwise, how do they know it's one of their customers using their service? And if you don't authenticate yourself, your ISP will almost certainly prevent you from sending mail over port 25. If the server requires a secure connection, you're not likely to get through over an unsecured one.

Secure connections usually use different ports from unsecured ones. The standard for unsecured, not authenticated SMTP is 25; for authenticated SMTP, it's 587 and for authenticated, secure SMTP it's 465, but there are plenty of variations on this. Hostgator used to support port 26 for authenticated SMTP, but I can't find out whether they still do. That's why it's important (a) to apply the host's recommended settings, and (b) to examine the error message to see why it's not working properly.

Incidentally, it's not usually necessary to remove and re-add an account just to change the settings for it. And uninstalling and re-installing the program won't make any difference at all to account settings.
