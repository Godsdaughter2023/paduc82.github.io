jay freedman say:
The answer depends on what you know about the acronyms in your document. In general, what you want to use is a "wildcard" search (http://www.gmayor.com/replace_using_wildcards.htm).

For example, if you want to find all words that consist of two to four uppercase letters, use the search expression

    [A-Z]{2,4}

If some of the acronyms and abbreviations include lowercase letters, numbers, or punctuation, the job of creating a search expression becomes more complicated. Sometimes you'll just have to search (without wildcards) for a specific item that you know exists, but that you can't find in a wildcard search without also getting things that aren't acronyms.

stop synch calendar and contacts - Microsoft Community

alainl say:

stop synch calendar and contacts

Hello, 

I am new to windows phone, my previous phone was an android phone, and I just bought myself a Lumia 900, OS version: 7.10.8773.98

So far, I am still getting used to the differences, but one of the things that bugs me at the moment is that my phone seems to insist on synchronizing my contacts and calendar with my hotmail account, which is something I definitely do not desire. I want to keep those informations (contacts and calendar) on my phone, and not see them replicated on a web site.

Thank you
toddallcock say:

As Entegy said, you can't really stop this by design.  However, you can workaround it, at your peril (meaning, unless the data is backed up to an online account, there's no way to retrieve it from the phone if you lose it, erase it, or have to "hard-reset" the device.)

 

First create a throwaway Hotmail or Google account; something you won't use after this.

 

Now , export the existing contacts and calendar from Hotmail (where they synced) and import them into the throwaway account.  Now add that account to the phone, and let the contacts and calendar sync down to the device.  You'll probably have duplicates of all the data on the phone at this point, one set associated with your Hotmail account, and one associated with the new throwaway account- don't worry, we'll get to that in a moment.

 

Now, turn OFF automatic sync on the new account by setting the sync interval to "manual".  Now delete all of the contacts and calendar info on the throwaway account's webpage.  You can erase them all from Live/Hotmail too at this point (and the next Hotmail sync will remove those duplicate copies from the phone).

 

Now, you have a bunch of contacts and calendar data on the phone, and have removed it from the two websites (Hotmail and whatever throwaway account you created.)  Here's the important part- in the future, when you create a new contact or calendar item on the phone, the device will ask which account to create it in.  ALWAYS pick the throwaway account, otherwise it will sync to Hotmail.  Equally important, NEVER sync with the throwaway account, otherwise all the deletions you made on the web will sync to the phone, wiping your data, and your new data will sync up to the web site.  (You might go into the account settings on the phone and change the account password to something incorrect so even if you hit sync by accident, the sync will fail.)

 

Lastly, I'm answering your question because you asked- not because I think you should do this.  I think it's crazy to have important data on a phone with no backup, but it's your phone and your data.  Good luck!
