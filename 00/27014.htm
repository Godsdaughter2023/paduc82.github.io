If you are getting the “Pending DNS” error when you access your Windows Live Admin Center account, it is possible that the MX Record of your domain is not properly configured. Please follow the instructions below to fix this.

 

I.  Get the MX record from your Windows Live Admin Center

 

    a. Go to http://domains.live.com and sign in with your administrator account.

 

    b. Click on the domain you would like to manage.

 

    c. Click on the “Domain Settings” link at the left side of the page.

 

    d. Under MX Record, copy the MX Server values.

 

II. Change the MX Record configured in your domain

 

Once you have copied the MX Record, you then need to edit the MX Record configured in your domain.

 

    a. Go to the registrar where you have purchased your domain.

 

    b. You may contact the technical support of the registrar where you have purchased your domain for assistance in changing the MX Record.

 

If you’ve already configured your domain settings but suddenly encountered an error, please refresh your Windows Live Admin Center account by following the instructions below:

 

    a. Go to http://domains.live.com and sign in to your account.    

 

    b. Click on the domain you would like to manage.

 

    c. Click on the “Domain Settings” link at the left side of the page.   

 

    d. Click on the “Refresh” button.

stefan blom say:

By default, each heading number is followed by a tab character. This is reflected in the table of contents as well, so that you have the paragraph number, a tab character, the text of the paragraph, a tab character and finally the page number at the right margin.

What you have to do is adjust the positions for tab stops within the TOC to accommodate the numbers. Specifically, try moving the tab stop for the tab after the number farther to the right.

This task will be easier if you first display nonprinting marks, for example by clicking the ¶ icon on the Home tab.

Click in the relevant TOC entry and drag the tab stops on the ruler. (That works because the TOC styles are set to update automatically by default.)
