Let me re-cap my issue just in case this isn't what others are experiencing:

I've got WEP and MAC address filtering on my Linksys access point (AP).  (It's one of the older, c.2003, Linksys models.)  My 8525 can see the AP and is assigned an IP address from the LAN's router.  However, the 8525 does not get out onto the Internet over the AP for any application.  (Yes, my other WiFi devices have no issue with this AP.)

For the record, I also have a newer (c.2006) Netgear access point configured with the same settings as the Linksys, but the AP names are different and my 8525 sees both APs.  When I choose and connect to the Netgear AP, I have no trouble whatsoever to get beyond the AP and out onto the Internet.

I've addressed the Linksys issue by cycling through the "Connects to" setting and back again.

Start > Settings > Connections TAB > Wi-Fi icon > (choose your access point) > Menu > Edit (Puts you on the "Configue Wireless Network" Page) > "Connects to" >

Your choices are:
"The Internet"
or
"Work"

Typically it's already on "The Internet".  By changing it from "The Internet" to "Work" then tapping "Next" until you "Finish", then going back through and changing it back from "Work" to "The Internet" then tapping "Next" until you "Finish".... I've been able to get from the AP to the Internet.

This seems to work for a little while then it reverts to it's original lazy self.  So I go through the Settings routine again and I'm back on.  I believe it reverts whenever the connection sits idly for "too long".  I have no idea what constitutes "too long".

In a pinch, it's a work-around.  A more complete solution would be greatly appreciated.

jason (office engineer) say:

Hello Graeme,

You cannot do exactly what you are asking for, but there may be a way to get closer to what you want.  The only way to have style/layout set to a particular value upon creating a new Sway is by duplicating an existing Sway or by creating a new one from a template.  You could create a bare bones Sway that has the style/layout you want and then either save it as a template (which can only be done on a per-user basis, not across an org) or open the link and then duplicate it.  In your new Sway, instead of starting from a document, you can insert the document by choosing the upload option on the + menu.  Uploading a document in this manner is very similar to starting a Sway from a document except that it adds all the content of the Office document at the point in the Sway where you choose to upload - this means that it won't populate the Sway title card, but, aside from this, it should be very much the same.

Let me know if this works for you or if you have any other questions.
