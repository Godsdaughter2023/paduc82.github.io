amit tandon say:

In cell D5 if you enter as below:

=SUM($B$1:$B$4)

and then move as you have mentioned, then this formula after moving will also remain the same ie. =SUM($B$1:$B$4) and return the SUM of v=cells B1 to B4.

You may use the $ sign to keep cell reference constant. 

If you enter the formula as "=SUM($B1:$B4)" and move it to the same row but a different column, the formula will remain the same because the $ sign will keep the column ("B") constant.

If you enter the formula "=SUM(B$1:B$4)" in cell D5 and in the same column you move it to D10 (ie. different row), the formula remains the same as "=SUM(B$1:B$4)" because the $ sign keeps the rows constant.

Entering the formula as "=SUM($B$1:$B$4)" and moving it across columns & rows will keep the formula same as the $ signs keep both columns & rows constant.

Reset only if you want to change the email address from which you want to send email.

Honestly, you did nothing wrong. All your aliases are valid for your Microsoft account. No matter what alias you signed in with, you're going to get the same emails, contacts, calendars and your app purchases, Xbox Live, etc. will all be the same. Literally the only thing this affects is sending email.

The reason why the primary Microsoft account only has the option for email is because you CAN'T turn off contact and calendar sync for the primary account.
