valerieheffernan say: { ={ MERGEFILED MYFIELDNAME Amount} * 100\#"0% " }

a. user say:

You need

{ ={ MERGEFIELD thefieldname } * 100 \#"0% " }

e.g. taking the name from your original post, you would need

{ ={ MERGEFIELD Spring_11_ratio_to_all_students_1_4 } * 100 \#"0% " }

All the {} have to be the special field code braces that you can enter using ctrl-F9 - you can't just type them on the keyboard.

If the name has spaces in it (I don't think they do, once a name from Excel arrives in Word), you will need to quote the name, e.g.

{ ={ MERGEFIELD "Spring11 ratio to all students 1 4" } * 100 \#"0% " }

I am sure you have spent quite a bit of time thinking about this, but IMO it is impossible to get this kind of thing "right" every time. At best, it may be possible to improve  the statistics, e.g. the proportion of people who don't make that mistake after reading your page, and that in itself would I think be hard, without being able to measure the impact of spelling out that particular message in different ways.

Any web page either has too much information, too little information, or just the information that the reader happens to need. When they start reading, they may have no idea about what's likely to be important and what isn't. I would guess that many people see the red block, and some may even think "I must remember that", but by the time they've found the section they really need, it may have slipped to the back of their mind. Nor is it obvious that you could put a reminder at the "end", because the "end" is where the reader happened to finish reading, and constant reminders would doubtless result in other problems.

I've never attempted the stats., but it seems to me that responses to threads with some suggested field coding have a relatively high risk of failing first time around if the message does not spell out the "you have to use ctrl-F9" mantra.
