¡firedog say:

Let's do this by stages:

  1. On your Surface, add a new event for next week to the principal calendar.
  2. Open Internet Explorer and go to http://calendar.live.com. If you're not already signed in, sign in with the same Microsoft Account as you're using in Calendar.
    Can you see the new event that you just entered in the Calendar app?
  3. On your PC, start Windows Live Mail.
  4. When everything has settled down and there is no status message next to Working offline at the bottom of the window, look at the right-most button on the Home tab of the ribbon
    •   If it reads Sign in, go to (5).
    •   If it shows anything else, click it and select Sign out of Windows Live Mail.
  5. Switch to the calendar window (click on the second of the five icons at the foot of the folder pane or press Ctrl-2). Check that you have no entry for the time you used at (1).
  6. Click the right-most button on the ribbon and sign in with the same account as you were using at (1) and (2).
    Did the new event show up for next week?
  7. Reverse the process: double-click on the new event to edit it. Add some text in the body of the event window, then click Save and close.
  8. Press F5 and watch for a message at the right-hand end of the status bar.
  9. Open your browser and go to http://calendar.live.com. Sign in with the same account again.
    Can you see the newly-edited event?
  10. On your Surface, check the Calendar app again.
    Has the event you entered at (1) changed?
