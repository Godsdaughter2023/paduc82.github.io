I need help saving a 30GB HD movie on a DVD, how do I compress the - Microsoft Community

mmush say:

I need help saving a 30GB HD movie on a DVD, how do I compress the movie?

 

Using Windows 7 - Live Movie Maker 2011.  I have tried to get a smaller file through custom settings with 426 x 240 at 1550Mbps, the quality is really bad and I can only play the DVD on the computer, not on a regular DVD player. How can I save that extremely large 30GB movie onto one DVD and be able to play it from any DVD player? I'm not an expert,I have read many threads on this forum and I'm really confused. Can you suggest an easy solution? Thanks.

considerate_guy say:

First, it sounds like you want a video DVD to play on a standard DVD player.   In that case, the fact that you have HD footage is irrelevant; it is going to be transcoded down to the DVD standard of 720 x 480 (SD).  I would think that it should fit fine on a DVD.

Assuming you are running a version of Windows 7 that supports Windows DVD Maker, it should do the work for you.  It will transcode the video to MPEG-2 video in the SD standard.  It can create menus for you, if you like, or you can just put the video on there.  So just start up Windows DVD Maker, pick your movie file and take the steps from there.

What you did was to try to create a *data* DVD and that won't play on most standard DVD players. Again, let the program do the work for you.

If are on a version (e.g. Windows 7 Basic) that does provides Windows DVD Maker, you can try some freeware alternatives like DVD Flick or DVD Styler.

Remember, DVD players were created to handle SD footage. They came before the advent of HD video was popular and they don't playback HD movies. For that you would need something capable of playing Blu-ray disc or a way to stream directly to your HDTV from your computer (through a media device like an XBox, PS3, Roku, etc.).
