sharonmary say: Hi again Mamo49,

I've just had one more thought after reading your posts again. Could it be that there is a table at the end of your document - and that the table reaches the very end of the page?

If that is the case, you cannot delete the final paragraph marker after a table. If it just so happens that the paragraph marker slips on to the next page, then there is a little trick that I learned eons ago.

You'll need the Show/Hide characters switched on to do this:

1. Select the final paragraph marker

2. Right click on it and choose Paragraph... from the pop-up menu

3. Under the Spacing category, make sure you have 0 in Before: and After:

4. Click OK if you made any changes to the Spacing

5. With the paragraph marker still selected: click on the little arrow in the Font Size button in the Font category and type 1 (you won't find it to select in the list)

    >>> This will ensure that the font is the smallest size possible. With a bit of luck it will make the rogue paragraph marker jump on to the preceding page.


If the above doesn’t work ... you could try looking for other paragraph markers on the last page and making them smaller using the above method. Or you could make the bottom margin on that page only a bit smaller.

Well ... I hope this or the previous suggestion works! It would be great if you could let us know as I am sure at one point or another someone else will coming looking for an answer.   :-)

sharonmary say: Hi Mamo49,

To follow on Gordon's responses, I'm guessing that you you don't have the Show/Hide characters switched on? This could explain your description of the problem i.e. you have characters in the document that you cannot see; it could either be a a series of paragraph markers (¶) as a result of a repeated pressing on the Enter key, or perhaps even a hard page break at the end of your document.

On that assumption, try the following:


1. Click the Show/Hide button in the toolbar on the Home tab (it's the one with the ¶ symbol in it) to display your document formatting.

2. Go to the last page of your document and look for either a series of ¶ or  ---Page Break ---

3. Click at the beginning of these unwanted characters and press Delete until they have all gone.


If you want to switch off the Show/Hide characters just click on the button again to hide them. Personally, I leave them switch on all the time, it takes away all the little "surprises" such as the one you are experiencing.

Hi Jmiller.olatheschools.org,

 

For your current situation, we have to remove the current license and then add the full license for users you want add the Teams license.

The error you met is the command lacking an action before “licenseoptions”.

 

To remove license, you can refer to this command:

Get-MsolUser -All | where {$_.isLicensed -eq $true -and $_.Licenses.AccountSkuId -eq "$233fac"} | foreach {Set-MsolUserLicense -UserPrincipalName $_.UserPrincipalName -RemoveLicenses "$233fac "}

 

To add license, you could refer to this command:

Get-MsolUser -All | where {$_.isLicensed -eq $false} | foreach {Set-MsolUserLicense -UserPrincipalName $_.UserPrincipalName -AddLicenses "$233fac "}

suzanne s. barnhill say:

It sounds as if you had an empty paragraph between paragraphs and were changing the font size of that paragraph. This is not recommended. Instead, in the Paragraph dialog for a given paragraph, add the desired amount of Spacing Before or Spacing After.

Note that, by default, these will not be additive. Unless you enable the Layout Option "Don't use HTML paragraph auto spacing," the larger of the two measurements will be used. That is, is you have 12 points Spacing After on one paragraph and 6 points Spacing Before on the following paragraph, you will get 12 points rather than the 18 you might expect.
