cesar2000 say:

I found the cause of the issue and the solution. The problem is Groove Music is automatically streaming all your music that you have on your OneDrive "Music" folder (and this cannot be avoided, unless you move your music out of the "Music" folder), and at the same time your local OneDrive "Music" folder has been added as one of the local folders Groove is "watching". The solution is to remove your local OneDrive "Music" folder from this list. To do this, follow these steps:

1. Open Groove Music.

2. Click on the gear icon (settings) towards the bottom left of the window.

3. Under "Music On This PC" click on "Choose where we look for music".

4. Remove your C:\OneDrive\Music folder, and click done.

Now, you should be seeing all your songs only once, which are being streamed from your OneDrive folder online.

The Groove Music app was created specifically to be integrated with OneDrive, so if you are logged into Windows with an online Microsoft account, you cannot tell Groove Music to not stream your OneDrive music. Big flaw in my opinion, but what are you going to do, it's Microsoft!
