canadian tech say:

If your drive has 100G capacity and you have 6G left available, your drive is in serious trouble.  You will not solve that by removing programs.   You will only solve this by removing data -- your data.   Your computer will perform very poorly and may be even not at all when you get well above 75%.  75% is the practical usable capacity of a hard drive.

There are a lot of things that Windows will not be able to do with only that much space left.  Clean out about 25 Gigabytes of data, then run Disk cleanup, defrag, and chkdsk.

If you really want a good checkup, again after cleaning out first, run your hard drive manufacturer's hard drive tester program to be sure your hard drive is not failing.

If you need much more space, buy a new rotating 7200rpm hard drive with 500G.  They are available as OEM products for around $60.  Of course that means you will need to re-install your system.

canadian tech say:

There is no point to not using the entire physical space for C:.

If your library pointers point to Documents, etc to D: drive, you are going to need to change that.  First remove the data to your backup drive.  Then move the library pointers to C:  Extend C: to the full 500.  Then copy your data back to C: in the right places.

The concept of logical drives had its birth back when Windows saw 2G as the biggest possible drive.  It, of course has no relevance today.  Some purists extend that idea to the idea that by keeping your OS and programs on a separate drive you increase your security.  I believe that is an unjustified myth. 

I have seen so many people get into trouble because of this drive partitioning.  It is just not worth it.
