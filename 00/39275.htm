In Windows 10, there is an option where your Windows will notify you before installing updates automatically. To access this settings follow the steps below:

You'll be asked to schedule a restart to finish installing updates. Updates won't download over a metered connection (where charges may apply).

  • Click on the Advanced system settings from the left pane.
  • Click on Hardware tab and click on Device Installation Settings.
  • Check mark on the No, Let me choose what to do option.
  •  Check mark on the Never install driver software from Windows update option.
  • Check on Save Changes.

graye say:

Last night my edge browser deleted my favorites and bookmarks.  I have no idea why - it was not an instruction I gave.  Does anyone have an idea  how to recover these items?

Yeah, this problem is well documented on this forum... favorites just tend to magically disappear (typically associated with a windows update).  For some, it happens more than once... and for countless thousands of others, it never happens at all.

For most people, there is no recovery of  your favorites... however, you can re-import them from Internet Explorer.

We haven't heard from Microsoft what the root cause is... or how to prevent it from happening again.  Instead, they merely suggest that you ignore the problem and reload your favorites from Internet Explorer.

However, "Reload your favorites, and hope it doesn't happen again"... isn't very comforting.

I think the only good news that I've found is that the folks on the Windows Insider program that are testing the newer versions of Edge aren't complaining about this issue.  That could be: a) it's been fixed, or b) Insiders don't get many Windows Updates so a conclusion is not statistically valid

So, about the only thing that we can offer is a way to perform a backup and restore of your favorites for the next time.  (Yeah, I know...)

There are two approaches... a simple one that only works if you're using the Edge "Sync your content" feature, and a more turn-key version where you install a 3rd-party application

1) If you are using Sync your content, you can copy the contents of the %USERPROFILE%\AppData\Local\Packages\Microsoft.MicrosoftEdge_8wekyb3d8bbwe\RoamingState for safe keeping.  And then just copy it back after a crash/reset

2) You can use the free 3rd-party EdgeManage application to perform a backup/restore (plus many more features).  This technique works without regard to the "Sync your content" settings.

Windows Live Mail doesn't have a time setting. It uses the system time zone to adjust the timestamps on mail messages to local time.
