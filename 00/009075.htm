Consecutive numbers in the text. - Microsoft Community

robin clay say:

Consecutive numbers in the text.

I am to give a presentation with slides.

My text will be along these lines:-

Here is a picture of my grandfather (1)... and my grandmother (2).... and the house they lived in (3)...

The numbers indicate the slide number, and are the cues to press the Advance button.

How can I put these into the text so that they automatically re-number when I insert more text & slides in the middle?

suzanne s. barnhill say:

What you want is a SEQ field. Here's the easiest approach:

  1. Press Ctrl+F9 to insert a field, which will have field delimiters (which look like ordinary braces but can't be typed from the keyboard) with two spaces between. The insertion point will be between the two spaces: { | }
  2. In the field, type "SEQ slide" (without the quotation marks): { SEQ slide }
  3. Type parentheses around the field.
  4. Select the expression you've just created (both parentheses and field) and press Alt+F3.
  5. In the Create New Building Block dialog, type a name for the AutoText entry. Make the name something that is at least four letters long and something you won't be typing except when you want to insert a slide number. Leave the rest of the default settings and click OK.
  6. When you want to insert a slide number, type the AutoText entry name and, when you get the AutoComplete tip, press Enter.

The advantage to this approach as opposed to just inserting the SEQ field and copy/pasting it is that the number will update with each insertion (if you paste it, it will keep displaying the same number until you manually update it).

SEQ fields update "automatically" but not "dynamically." This means that if you insert a new slide number between two existing ones, the following ones won't update immediately; you will have to Ctrl+A, F9 to update all the fields.
