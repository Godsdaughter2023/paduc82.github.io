macropod say:

You don't need to know the table width to do that. For example:

Sub ToggleTableWidth()
Application.ScreenUpdating = False
Dim oPageWidth As Single, oPrintWidth As Single, oTmpWidth As Single, Rng As Range
With ActiveDocument.Tables(1)
  With .Range.Sections.First.PageSetup
    oPageWidth = .PageWidth
    oPrintWidth = .PageWidth - .LeftMargin - .RightMargin - .Gutter
  End With
  .Rows.LeftIndent = 0
  .Rows.Alignment = wdAlignRowCenter
  .PreferredWidthType = wdPreferredWidthPoints
  Set Rng = .Range.Characters.Last
  With Rng
    oTmpWidth = .Information(wdHorizontalPositionRelativeToPage)
  End With
  .PreferredWidth = oPrintWidth
  If Rng.Information(wdHorizontalPositionRelativeToPage) = oTmpWidth Then
    .PreferredWidth = oPageWidth
  Else
    .PreferredWidth = oPrintWidth
  End If
End With
Application.ScreenUpdating = True
End Sub

a. user say:

Hello Chad:

Thank you for posting to the forum regarding the questions you have about your Music collection, your Zune player and the Xbox Music Pass.  I will do my best to clarify all the points you have brought up.  If anything is missed or if you have additional questions, please let us know.

The first thing I want to discuss is access to your Music Pass content.  When you download content with the Music Pass a physical file is downloaded to your computer.  This file has a license attached to it that has an expiration date.  As long as you have a Music Pass this license can be updated and renewed.  With this said you would not have restart your collection or rebuild your playlists.

Next, we are going to discuss the content on your Zune player.  This content can be copied to your computer where you would then be able to have the licenses updated via the Zune software.  You would need to install the Zune software on your computer and then sync your collection to the computer via the software.  As long as you are signed into an account with an active Music Pass the syncing of your content to the computer should update the licenses.  The content on the player should be updated with new licenses as well.  If you notice that the content on the player does not play, delete all the content from your player and then sync everything back to the player.  Follow the steps below to accomplish this.

  • Connect Player to computer with Zune software.
  • Within the Zune software go to settings > device > sync options > erase all content.
  • Once all the content has been erased, press the forget this player button.
  • After this is all done disconnect the player from the computer and then reconnect the player.
  • You should be prompted in the software to set your sync options.  I would suggest you set this to "items I choose" or manual.
  • Finally, sync your collection to the player.

If you have any issues restoring the music on your Zune player you can restore all the content you downloaded with your Music Pass via the Zune software.  You can do this by launching the software and then signing into your account.  Once signed in, click on settings > account > subscription history > restore all.  This should lead to all the Music Pass content you downloaded in the past to be restored to your computer.

tom_bpc say:

Hi Michael,

In agreement with Jan, yes, check the Total Slack field for all tasks in the project.  Also know that Project sets the "Critical" flag for the task based on its Total Slack compared to the threshold you set in the advanced options.  If you set the threshold at 3d, then Project will flag as "Critical" any task with Total Slack <= 3d. (The default is 0d).

As Jan implies, yes - any early constraint (SNET, FNET) will raise the total slack of tasks before the constraint and "break" your critical path.  This seems most likely for the case you describe - assuming of course that you have followed all the other good practices of logically linking all of your tasks into a closed network.  Insert a column in your table called "Constraint Type" and inspect the values.  Any values that are not ASAP ("As Soon as Possible") need to be justified by reality or changed back to ASAP.  

Of course, it IS possible that a constraint in your project is quite justified - for example, delivery of customer-furnished equipment or software which is contractually determined to occur no sooner than a certain date - and has zero total slack.  In that case, I would argue, the critical path "is what it is," correctly truncated by the "external" constraint - all preceding tasks have some slack.       

Aside from this discussion there are other situations where total slack becomes unreliable as an indicator of critical path:
1. (Possible in your case.) If you use variable task calendars, then total slack can have a step-change along a single logic path, so the true "critical path" actually includes several different total slack values.  If you see this happening, then you can raise the "Critical" threshold in the advanced options to try to capture the whole path.

2. (Possible but not indicated for your case.) If you impose any task deadlines or late constraints, these can reduce the total slack of some tasks and totally confuse "the" critical path.
3. (Also possible but not indicated for your case.)  If you have automatically leveled resources, total slack does not reflect the logical sequence of the leveling process.  

"Other functionality" to address these limitations are available through MSP's "Task Path" bar chart tool (only 2013+) or some add-ins.
