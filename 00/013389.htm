Normal.dotm really should be left blank, as it forms the basis of all documents not explicitly based on a template.

If you do want to make changes to it, then your second method (right-clicking on it and choosing Open) is the correct way to go about it.

So if it is blank, what should you be using it for?

Firstly, basic things like page layout (page size, margins, etc.).

Secondly, and perhaps most importantly, style settings. These have been discussed elsewhere (e.g., http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/office/forum/office_2013_release-word/help-with-formatting-styles/98ca7dc7-9c01-4810-a788-c5729efe03e1), so I won't go into detail here.

There are some other bits that also end up in the Normal.dotm template (as one example, if you click on the "New documents based on this template" option in the Modify Style dialog when Normal is the base template).

The minute you want to start adding text, you should consider rather adding that to a different template which you create for that purpose (e.g., a letterhead template, etc.).

As a side note, if you ever totally mess up this template, Word can regenerate it for you--Close Word, delete the Normal.dotm template (delete to the recycle bin, just in case...), and re-open Word, and upon not finding Normal.dotm, it will regenerate a pristine (i.e., as shipped by Microsoft) version for you.

What happens to Sway links/projects after account deletion? - Microsoft Community

kyle minnie say:

What happens to Sway links/projects after account deletion?

After an account with shared Sways gets deleted, do those links to the Sways get deactivated as well? Or can people still access/edit them. If they get deleted with the account, then I have a follow-up question:

Can you create Sways under a company domain, so if one employee's account is deleted, their Sways will stay?

jason (office engineer) say:

Hi Kyle,

If the Sway belongs to an Office 365 account (like from a school or business), once the account deletion propagates through the system, the Sway is deleted and retained with nobody being able to access them for 30 days.  During that time period, the organization admin can move them to an active account to reinstate them; if not, when the time is up, they are permanently deleted.

If the Sway belongs to a public account, the Sway remains in the same state is was as when the account was active.

Currently, Sways must belong to an active user account.  There is no way for them to belong to a company domain.  If a company wants to do this, they should create a persisted, shared user account to store them in.

What to do if Outlook or OneDrive Android apps won't let you sign - Microsoft Community

leah96xxx say:

What to do if Outlook or OneDrive Android apps won't let you sign in

If you're having problems signing in to some Microsoft apps like Outlook or OneDrive on Android simply follow this guide. It worked for me when the web-based password screen kept crashing on each of these apps.

First, reset the Office account on your device: 

  1. Ensure no Microsoft accounts are stored on your device. (Office, Outlook, OneDrive or MS Exchange)
  2. Back-up any Office documents to an SD card or PC. They should be safe but it's always best to take precautions.
  3. Re-install all of your Microsoft apps, this will force them to do a first time set-up. (If they're factory installed then uninstall updates and clear data for each app then update them again.)

Once you've done that open Word and log in to it using the Microsoft account you are trying to add. This will save your email and password as a new "Office" account on your device. (The web-based sign-in screen shouldn't crash on Word as long as it asks you for an email first. It may also work on Excel, PowerPoint, OneNote etc. but I only tried it on Word.) If you don't have Word then just download it from the Play Store for free then uninstall it afterwards if you don't need it.

To add the account to Outlook:

  1. Open Outlook and go to the Add Account page.
  2. Outlook will search your device for accounts. Select the account you want to add and tap "Add Account"

To add the account to OneDrive:

  1. Open OneDrive
  2. Enter your email and tap "Done" or the "next" arrow.

If done correctly, Outlook and OneDrive will bypass the password screen by taking the password directly from the stored Office account.

To add multiple accounts simply follow the steps above then sign out of Word and then sign back in again with your other account and follow the steps for the relevant app again. Repeat this for each account you want to add. (Only one personal account can be added to OneDrive but you should still be able to add a business account with this method. I don't have one to test it with.)

To add the account to To-Do (only one account can be connected):

  1. Add the account to Outlook or OneDrive.
  2. Re-install To-Do if you haven't done so already.
  3. Open it and select your account. It will automatically find your password and sign in.

Once you've added all your accounts remember to go back to Word and sign back in to your preferred Office/OneDrive account. Do the same for other Office apps too if you need to. (You'll definitely have to do this if you have a paid Office subscription.) 

Unfortunately this method will not work for the Edge app, even if you try to enter your email manually. If you have added the account to Office, Outlook or OneDrive you will still be able to store favourites and history on your device but you cannot sync with other devices without password verification. If anyone can find a work-around or fix for this then please post it below.
