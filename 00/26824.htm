jeff griffin say: I had the same issue, the problem for me turned out to be that the Root Certificate was installed but not trusted. I had to go into my keychain and find the organization's root cert. Once I found it I had to right click it and select "Get Information". From there I was able to expand the trust section and change "When using this certificate" from "Use System Defaults" to "Always Trust". After that Communicator worked like a champ.

neha singh msft say:
Hi Boris,

Microsoft account is created by user for personal use. The Microsoft account is the combination of an email address and a password that a user uses to sign in to all consumer-oriented Microsoft products and cloud services such as Outlook (Hotmail), Messenger, OneDrive, MSN, Windows Phone or Xbox LIVE. Whereas Office 365 log in id might be (yourname)@(business).onmicrosoft.com or your primary email address. The Office 365 account is used to log into your mailbox (either in Outlook or in webmail) and other Office 365 services.

Microsoft has two different databases that have credentials for you – your email address and a password. They’re separate. They’re used for different things. They can be the same email address; you can set the password to be the same, if you like; but they are separate accounts and Microsoft’s servers look in different databases to authenticate you for various services.

Given the situation, you can vote for the requirement in our UserVoice, where Microsoft merges both the accounts i.e. Microsoft account and Office 365 account that use the same email address.

sky pei msft say:

Hi Bdrajer,

 

Microsoft account is not supported to sign up for PowerApps preview. To use this feature, Work or school email address is needed.

 

If user’s work or school email address is associated with valid Office 365 subscriptions, they can add PowerApps service to their Office 365 tenant with this email address directly. It will not create additional accounts.

 

If user’s work or school email address is not associated with valid Office 365 subscriptions, when they sign up for PowerApps preview using this kind of email address, a tenant including an Office 365 for Business admin account will be created automatically. This admin account is the work or school email address you mentioned “Ironically, it's the second one that isn't connected to any company that is designated as my "work" (or school) account”.

Note:

1.This Office 365 for Business account can’t be deleted on end users’ side. The expired state of this account is 30 days and the disabled date of it is 90 days. After that, if you don’t reactive this subscription,  this account will in a deprovisioned status and then be deleted.

2.Once this account is deleted, you are not able to use this service.
