How to set up My Family in order to download apps and games in your - Microsoft Community

adultage say:

How to set up My Family in order to download apps and games in your Windows Phone.

Do you have a child and you want to to control what he/she can buy; which content can download with his/her Windows Phone 8? In this case you need to set up My Family. Setting up My Family for non adult people is mandatory.

Remember, the system calculate the age based on the date of birth inserted in Basic Info in http://account.live.com. If you are an adult the system won't ask you to set up My Family. 

In order to set up My Family you need two email addresses; one for the adult person and the other for the child (but you can put under your control more children, in this case one email address for every child). Both accounts must be completed in every part. Here below there are three links where is explained how to do it:

How to complete an email account:

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/winphone/forum/wp8-wpaccount/insert-all-the-necessary-information-into-the/2341f001-ffc6-4f58-9be6-42cbc5fa47da?tm=1400196156211

How to make compatible an email account which doesn't belong to Microsoft; in other words it is not an hotmail, live, outlook email account (ex.: yahoo, gmail, sky, tim, alice, etc); and complete it:

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/winphone/forum/wp8-wpaccount/do-you-have-an-email-account-as-yahoo-gmail-sky/d3a564a8-8b1f-4ddd-a6ef-b4c7f8d30b17?tm=1400192533546

How to create a complete Microsoft Account for your Windows Phone from scratch:

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/winphone/forum/wp8-wpapps/how-to-create-a-microsoft-account-or-a-primary/60ae2b6f-00ad-400c-9318-c8235ff3a094?tm=1399746845078

Once you have the two emails accounts (adult and kid), but you can have more kids (and related kid email accounts under your parental control), you are ready to set up My Family.

  1. Go to http://www.windowsphone.com and sign in using the adult account.

  2. Click on My Family.

  3. Click on Get Started.

  4. Click on Go.

  5. It will appear a new web page where you have to insert the email account of your son/daughter and related password.

  6. Click on Sign In.

  7. If you want to add another son/daughter email account under your control you will need to click onAdd a Child in My Family section (once you sing in to www.windowsphone.com using the adult account).

  8. Sign out from www.windowsphone.com

  9. Set up the son/daughter email account as Microsoft Account in the phone. If the son/daughter email account is already set up as Microsoft Account in the phone you will need to reset it and insert the same email account as Microsoft Account again. During this phase the phone need to be connected to internet by WiFi or a mobile operator data connection.

  10. Sign in to www.windowsphone.com using the adult email account again.

  11. Click on My Family.

  12. Click on the yellow link FixIt at the side of the kid account.

  13. Mark as you are read and accept the terms of use.

  14. Click on OK.

  15. Turn off and turn on the phone

  16. The phone is ready for being used.

If you found this discussion useful please suggest is as useful.

Bye.

tj_909 say:

Remove space between headings in Word 2013

I have Word 2013 on Windows 10.

In my document I have about 50 "Heading 1" numbered headings. After each heading, I have some "normal" content for the paragraph underneath the Heading. Everything was working great yesterday. Then, today, for whatever reason, my document wants to push every heading to the next page! That is, I put some content into the paragraph following a given heading (no problem there), but after that paragraph is a huge space, because the next heading has been pushed to the next page. This has only started at about heading 27 (where I resumed work today).

I have tried highlighting ALL headings and formatting the paragraph by checking "keep with next" but it only works for the FIRST heading of all the selected headings.

Why is it doing this, and what can I do to format all of it to "keep with next" instead of having to format it every time I start a new heading?

suzanne s. barnhill say:

Word's built-in heading styles all have "Keep with next" as part of the style definition. If you create a document that contains nothing but headings, they will all be stuck together in a clump. As soon as you insert a paragraph that does not have the "Keep with next" property, it and the heading above it will move to the next page. As soon as you have content beneath every heading, the text will flow properly. If you will have several headings in sequence without following content, you may need to remove KWN from some of the headings. If the document will consist entirely of headings (an outline, for example), you should remove KWN from the style of all but the top level or two, allowing pages to break naturally. (A better approach for an outline is to use the List Number series of styles instead of heading styles for the multilevel list levels.)

If a heading is insisting on moving to a new page despite having normal content above it, check to make sure that the heading has not been formatted as "Page break before."
