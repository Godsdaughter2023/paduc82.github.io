What is SPF Record?

 

Sender Policy Framework (SPF) is an email validation system designed to prevent email spam by detecting email spoofing, a common vulnerability, by verifying sender IP addresses. It is a security standard that has been implemented to stop forgery of sender address.

 

SPF allows administrators to specify which hosts are allowed to send mail from a given domain by creating a specific SPF record (or TXT record) in the Domain Name System (DNS).

 

To resolve this, the sender’s domain administrator should add the SPF record in the domain registrar.

Identifier in Access 2010 - Microsoft Community

spq28 say:

Identifier in Access 2010

I am reading the "Guide to expression syntax" in Access 2010 help, trying to understand how to use identifiers in expressions.

This page includes a section "Identifiers".

This section says there are three operators that may be used in an identifier: '!', '.' and "[]"

The section then goes on to describe the '!' and "[]" operators but says nothing further about the '.' operator.

My question is: where are the syntax and semantics of the '.' operator of identifiers defined or described? How is this operator used?

marshall barton say:

Technically, ! is used to refer to a member of a collection (e.g. Forms!myform) and dot is used to refer to an object's properties/methods (e.g. Forms.Count) or a in query, a field in a table (e.g. mytable.myfield).

Because many people don't have a good grasp on which is what, there are many places in Access that allow abbreviated references (e.g. Forms!myform.Controls!mytextbox can be shortened to Forms!myform.mytextbox or Forms!myform!mytextbox).  To make matters even more cofusing, the fine print on the previous two references are a little different and as far as I know, there is no one place that describes all the nuances.

I think it is best to stay with the basic technical definition until you have gained the experience to understand what to use when and why.

  1. Open Outlook 
  2. Go to Tools > Rules > Outgoing
