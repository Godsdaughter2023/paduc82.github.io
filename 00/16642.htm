kellyhranac say:

hard drive died and cannot uninstall office 2007 student to reinstall on new hard drive

rohn007 say:

Not a problem, there is no absolute requirement in the License or the activation systems that you uninstall. The license simply requires that you make sure the "old" copy is no longer usable before transferring the installation. A dead HD that has been replaced fills that requirement.

 

The thing is, the activation system does not actually track uninstalls, just activated installations. It is a logical defect in the system design.

Replacing a HD qualifies within the allowed limited hardware upgrades that the activation system tracks. You will be 're-using" the same activation allowed on your license because you are re-installing on the same hardware.

hansv mvp say:

Select Q2:R100 or as far down as needed.

On the Home tab of the ribbon, select Conditional Formatting > New Rule...

Select 'Use a formula to determine which cells to format'.

Enter the formula

=OR($P2="no show",$P2="fail",$P2="no response")

It's essential that there is a $ before the column letter P, but not before the row number 2 in the formula.

Click Format...

Activate the Fill tab.

Select grey.

Click OK, then click OK again.
