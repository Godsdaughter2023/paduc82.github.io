[ pete ] say:

You'll need to do this through Outlook.com (sign in with your Microsoft account)

 

Once there, click the "down" arrow next to where it says "Outlook" in the top left of the screen and select the Calendar icon.

 

Once there, you should be able to remove the US holiday calendar (just click on the US Holidays item and then hit the "Delete" button at the bottom of the page that appears).

 

Look at

 

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-GB/hotmail/holiday-alternative-calendars-ui

 

For instructions and a link to install UK holidays. Where it says "Step 2 - Locate the calendar you want to add", you'll just need to click the hyperlink for United Kingdom in the list of countries.

 

Hope that helps.

samwang_msft say:

Hi Nick,

Thanks for your updates.

Theoretically, it will work. When you set up your Office 365 account in the Outlook client via Exchange, the duplicated calendar events in OWA (outlook.office365.com) can be synced to the Outlook calendar. Then you can use the corresponding tool to delete the duplicates and the results can be synced back to OWA. After that, these items no longer exist on the server side. The detailed steps for your reference:
1. Disconnect your Office 365 account on your iPhone and iPad.
2. Set up your Office 365 account in the Outlook 2013 client on a Windows desktop and check if the duplicated items can be synced.
3. If yes, remove all instances of the calendar event. (using MFCMapi tool or CalCheck tool.)
4. Check if the duplicated events persist in OWA.

Let us know if anything is unclear.
