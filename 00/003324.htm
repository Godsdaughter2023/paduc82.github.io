    jay freedman say:
    On the References ribbon, click the Table of Contents button and choose Insert Table of Contents at the bottom. In the dialog, click the Options button. Here you can determine which items in the text will be captured by this TOC: Use
    There usually isn't any difference between Styles (Heading 1, etc.) and Outline Levels, because the heading styles are assigned outline levels equal to their heading levels (in the Paragraph properties of the style definitions). However, it is possible to change the outline levels of the styles, or to assign outline levels to styles that aren't headings; in that case the TOC would differ depending on which boxes you checked in the options.

    You can insert two separate tables of contents, one set one way and the other set the other way. One will contain only the headings, and the other will contain only the field entries.

    If you're comfortable with editing field codes, you can change a "headings and outline levels" TOC field to a "fields" TOC by removing the \o and \u switches and inserting a \f switch instead. See http://www.word.mvps.org/FAQs/Formatting/TOCSwitches.htm.

    Click one of the scatter points to highlight the series.

    XY Scatter should be highlighted in orange on the left hand side.
