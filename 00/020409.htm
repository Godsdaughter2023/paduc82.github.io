hansv mvp say:

Select the picture.

On the Insert tab of the ribbon, click Bookmark (I originally wrote Hyperlink by mistake).

Assign a name, e.g. MyPic, then click OK.

Click elsewhere in the document.

Press Ctrl+F9 to insert field brackets { } (don't type { and } yourself).

Type the name of the bookmark between the brackets, e.g. { MyPic }

Press F9 to hide the field code.

After modifying the first picture, you'll have to update the copies (select them and press F9) or print/print preview the document.

philipradler say:

You're right about using AutoCorrect. Probably the easiest way is to first find and copy (Ctl+c) the symbol you want so it's readily available. Then go to File>Options>Proofing>AutoCorrect (make sure you're on the AutoCorrect tab, and that the "Replace text as you type" box is checked).

 

Click in the "Replace" box and type your "trigger phrase" (==>). Then tab or click into the "With": box and press Ctl+v to paste in the desired symbol. Click the "ADD" button below, click OK twice, and you should be in business.

 

In case you don't already have a particular arrow in mind, there are several options available in the Symbol, Wingdings, and Wingdings 3 fonts.

As a workaround, we recommend that you use Windows Live Mail for it has the said feature.

If you don't have Live Mail, you can download it from the below link: 

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows-live/essentials-other#essentials=overviewother

Once you download Windows Live Mail, Please refer the below link to set up your live mail:

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows-live/mail-add-email-account-faq

Once you add your Outlook account to Live Mail, everything will be synchronize between your mail client and Outlook. Therefore, whatever you do on your mail client, like delete an email, add an appointment, update one of your contacts with a new number, send messages, or create a folder will also be reflected on the web, and vice-versa.

Once done, please follow the steps below on how to enable read receipt feature:

  1. Sign in to your Live Mail account.
  2. Press Alt + F.
  3. Click Options.
  4. Click Mail.
  5. Select Receipts tab.
  6. Under Returning Read Receipts, tick on Notify me for each read receive request.
  7. Click Apply and then OK.

 To receive a notification that your recipient read your email, your recipient should have a mail client read receipt enabled.

Please refer to the below link to know more about delivery failure in Outlook.

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/outlook/delivery-failed
