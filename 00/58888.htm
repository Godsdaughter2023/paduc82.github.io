Xbox One outputs all audio as 7.1 channels even when source material - Microsoft Community

concretellama say:

Xbox One outputs all audio as 7.1 channels even when source material is not 7.1 resulting in silent rear speakers

I have an Xbox One X and an Atmos capable 7.1 receiver with a 7.1 speaker setup. When I set the Xbox One to 7.1 uncompressed, or if I set it to bitstream atmos, the Xbox always sends out a full 7.1 channels even if the source material does not have 7.1 audio channels.

You may wonder why this is an issue - well, normally receivers are sent the number of channels that the actual source material contains and the receiver can then do some processing on that if the user desires. So, for example, if a 5.1 signal is sent to the receiver, the receiver can take that signal and interpolate it to a 7.1 output - sure, it's not quite as good as having a 7.1 source, but at least all the speakers are being used and you're more enveloped in the sound.

The problem is that if the Xbox is sending out a 7.1 signal - even for 5.1 sources - the receiver sees it as a 7.1 input, which contains 2 silent channels. This means that for games that do not support 7.1 or Atmos - such as, surprisingly, Red Dead Redemption 2 - sound will only come out of 5 speakers and the sub rather than 7 and the sub.

My current workaround for this is a little awkward but does work. Basically if I'm launching a game that only supports 5.1, I go into settings and change it to 5.1 uncompressed. If it supports 7.1, I go into settings and change it to 7.1 uncompressed. It's not ideal, but it works... shame it's so fiddly though.

Have MS ever directly addressed this issue? It seems there are a few solutions to this that only MS could implement:

1) Really the best solution would be for the Xbox to only output the number of channels that the source material contains - assuming it knows.

2) Have the option to always send the source audio directly out to the receiver. This would of course mean that the Xbox would be unable to mix multiple sound sources and would mean the OS can't make any sound - at least while a game is running, and I imagine it would take some reworking of pretty much everything to implement - not exactly ideal

3) If MS is really that intent on sending out all channels even if unused, have the Xbox interpolate 5.1 sources to 7.1 itself
