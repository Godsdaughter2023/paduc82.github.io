I found a youtube video that explained how to fix it. It is fairly simple once you know how to do it.

1. type control panel in the search windows field at the bottom of your screen. This will bring up the control panel app. Open it. You will be relieved to see a control panel like the one that you are used to from previous versions of windows instead of windows settings.

YOU CAN NOT FIX THIS PROBLEM THROUGH WINDOWS SETTINGS YOU MUST USE THE CONTROL PANEL APP

2. when you open the app, click on "view devices and printers" under the "Hardware and Sound" heading

3. You should see a familiar screen with your printers on it. The one you are having trouble with should be there but grayed out "offline" even though it is not. If your printer is not there, you have a different problem.

4. right click the problem printer. a menu will come up.

5. Select "Printer Properties"

6. click the tab labeled "Ports"

7. at the bottom of the "Ports" page there is a button that says "configure port". Click it.

8. a port configuration screen should come up. at the bottom of the port configuration page there is a check box with SNTP protocol stuff next to it. Uncheck this box and your printer will come back online.

As best I can figure out Windows added a new layer of printer to PC communication that isn't supported by most printers yet. Unfortunately Windows 10 defaults to using this new communication and when your printer doesn't know how to reply to the signal the computer assumes it is offline. by turning off this function your computer will go back to talking to the printer as it used to. HP seems to have figured this out now as their most recent driver install seems to turn this function off automatically.
