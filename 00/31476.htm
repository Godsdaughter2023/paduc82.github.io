andrewclockmaker say:

Hi all,

After sleeping on it and going through a few more online forums, stumbled on the fix for my problem, although possibly not others.

Turns out I had to enable SMB1.

To do that, the simplest way I saw was Control Panel->Programs->Turn Windows Features On or Off; then navigate to SMB 1.0/CFS Client and click on the + box and make sure all the three boxes are ticked.

Specifically, the box "SMB1.0/CIFS File Sharing Support" needed to be ticked.

Thanks.
ganesh achar b say:

Hi,

 

Thank you for the post.

 

"/X - Perform free space consolidation on the specified volumes."

 

/X will tell defrag to also defrag the free spaces, moving the files together at the start of the drive, so the free space is all in one chunk. This makes it easier to store new large files in one piece, and can let you shrink a volume more in some cases, assuming there are no unmovable files towards the end of the partition.
