Repair the disk
The following procedure is the equivalent of booting from your system DVD and doing a disk repair, minus the hassle of finding the plastic disc. The important steps are underlined:

  1. Restart your Mac
  2. As soon as you hear the Boing! sound, press Command S and keep holding until…
  3. You see a black screen with white text. That's Unix booting in "Single user mode." When the scrolling stops, you'll see a Unix prompt like 12-345-678-910: ~ YourName $.
  4. Now type fsck -fy and hit Return. In Unix speak, that stands for "file system check, space, minus sign, force yes."
  5. This repair operation will take about 5 minutes, depending on your disk's file population. You will see about ten message lines like "Checking this" and "Verifying that." If the disk was repaired, the last line will say "File system was modified." Else, you will be informed "The disk appears to be OK." Either of these indicate the end of the repair operation.
  6. After those messages stop, you will again see the Unix dollar-sign prompt again. Type reboot and hit Return.
  7. The Mac will reboot.
  8. Test.
