a. user say:

Found solution to problem. You have add the email domain as an alternate UPN suffix in Active Directory Domain and Trusts.

The issue is that we have non routeable domain name for Active Directory and a 2nd internet routeable domain for Exchange. So our computers and user accounts that are used authenticate use our non-routeable domain name as part of its sign in name. So when the Office 365 attempts to proxy back to our internal server it tries to use the email address to gain entry. This doesn't match any known account in Active Directory. Adding the second suffix adds an alternate sign in name which seems to have fixed the issue.

If you have enabled the backup then they will be restored on your new device.

 

Text messages

You can automatically save your SMS and MMS messages to the cloud. That way, the next time you get a new Windows Phone, you can restore them all when you set up the phone. When you delete your messages from your phone, they'll be deleted from the cloud as well, so you're always in sync.

Within the next couple of months there will be an update to your phone, Windows Phone 8.1.  This will let you install or move apps to the SD card and install map data to the SD card. This will be a great improvement to the phones like the 520 with low storage space on the phone.

Now the SD card should store you pictures, videos and music. You should verify the card is working correctly by going to settings, phone storage, You should see phone and SD card storage there and the selection on what to store on the card.  When you tap the phone or SD card you see what is stored on each.  Verify the card is being used for media storage. Are you referring to a Navigator app and maps specifically for the app? For street maps and directions most users use Nokia Here Maps and Here Drive. In 8.1 the 'phone storage' in settings is replaced by 'storage sense'.
