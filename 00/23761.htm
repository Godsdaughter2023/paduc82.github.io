unrequited still say:

There is no guarantee that the password was actually compromised, unless the employee gave it out. Tell them NEVER to do that.

Change the password as recommended, and note that it's not necessary to hack anybody's account to do this.

A really good and secure rule is to never CC to large groups of recipients. If someone's account IS compromised that's basically a ticket to send malicious attachments en masse.

Send in batches no larger than 5 recipients and do not make blanket posts with separate instructions for individuals. If you have instructions for a specific person, send an email to that person with that message.

Encourage your employees and people that regularly receive messages from your company/organization to change their passwords regularly and to make them unique. Some organizations also change the formatting of their email addresses from time to time.

The point is to avoid giving email harvesters a shot at causing harm by mixing it up.

Also ALWAYS scan attachments before opening them. Make sure you have a malware/antivirus scanner active for each account. This would be easiest at the email server but also on each PC/device in your users' paths.

One malicious attachment opened by accident could filter down the entire chain of users. Avoid messages addressed to everyone in general. Do not pass emails on until they are determined to be safe. It does not matter what type of attachment is indicated, they can all be unsafe. Always assume they are and have them scanned.

It's human nature to open emails that seem friendly or anecdotal even, train your employees to always scan first. There may not be any obvious way to tell otherwise. Also instruct them to keep personal email away from the business account and to get that from their personal accounts and devices, not on your computers. Personal emails are not business.

Security breaches occur primarily because someone does not follow protocol and relaxes security. Passwords and codes are company property. Change them when an employee leaves and close/transfer that account immediately.

It sounds like you are thinking it's just a PDF file. Nobody here can tell you if that file is safe in general, they have no knowledge of it. You have to do the security work.
