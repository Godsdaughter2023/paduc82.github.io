Unsaved files are copies of files that you elected not to save, which are stored temporarily in the Unsaved Files folder.  These files are not automatically opened when Word is started\re-started.

The files that are saved in the AutoRecover file location are files created when Word crashes, which are automatically loaded when Word is re-started.

hal hostetler - mvp-outlook say:

Your email account data - email addresses, server settings, passwords, etc. are NOT stored in a .PST file.   These comprise your Outlook Profile which is kept in the Windows System Registry.  All your old emails, folders, contacts, calendars, to-dos, rules, etc. are in your .PST files, but not the email account settings, themselves.  This is why you have to re-create them on the new computer.  Once the accounts, themselves, are setup, the data files (.PST files) supply everything else.   Having said that, it is possible for you to export your account settings from the old computer and import them on the new one.  It involves editing the Registry and is not for the faint of heart:

How to Backup your Outlook Account Settings

is my costa rica subscription for calls originating or to Costa Rica - Microsoft Community

g.j.rancourt say:

is my costa rica subscription for calls originating or to Costa Rica or both?

is my costa rica subscription for calls originating or to Costa Rica or both?
techfreak_ say:

As already explained, calling subscriptions are outgoing. You purchase a subscription based on where you are calling to, not where you are calling from. For example if a subscription existed to call the Moon ("Moon" subscription), calls to the Moon would be covered. Calls from the Moon to anywhere else on the planet Earth would not be covered.

If you plan on calling solely Costa Rica, you would purchase a Cost Rica subscription. If you plan on calling USA and Canada, you would purchase a singular subscription that covers those areas (North America subscription) or two separate subscriptions: a US subscription and a Canada subscription. If you plan on calling Costa Rica numbers and North America numbers, you would purchase multiple subscriptions to cover those areas or use credit for the countries that would not be worth buying a subscription for.

If you are concerned with getting return calls from USA/Canada residents, you might consider a Skype Number. While you cannot get a Costa Rica or Canada Skype Number, you can get a US Skype Number in the state of your choosing. People could then return your call if they do not mind paying the cost of dialing your US Skype number, which is probably cheaper than calling a Costa Rica phone number.
