zhang_yi say:

Hi Bujar,

You’re right. If there is no Exchange Online in your environment, the user photo will be uploaded to the SharePoint Online user profile directly.

Generally, if you are using DirSync to upload your user profiles from local AD to online AD, the users’ pictures will first enter Exchange Online, and then sync from Exchange Online to SharePoint Online.

In this situation, Exchange Online is required for this to work. Photos in SharePoint Online are only synced from Exchange Online. In another word, if there is no Exchange Online in your environment, the only way is to upload photos manually to SharePoint Online. Thanks for your understanding.

Once you have it pinned to your Taskbar, make sure that Outlook is closed. Then hold the SHIFT button and choose: Send To-> Desktop (create shortcut)

When Outlook isn't closed, you can still achieve the same by first right clicking on the icon and from the list that pops up, right click on Outlook 2010 while holding the SHIFT button and choose: Send To-> Desktop (create shortcut)

This will also work for every other application of course and you don't actually have to pin it to your Taskbar first if you have the application running already.

There are two types of additional storage you can purchase for Office 365 Business Premium:

How did you purchase the additional storage? Was it an add-on under your Business Premium plan? Or did you buy a OneDrive for Business plan?
