Hi Lilly,

 

Is your router a wireless router?  If it is not properly-secured they may be connecting to your network through the signal it broadcasts (I can see the signals from 4 of my neighbors on my machine - two are secure and two are unsecure and if I were so inclined, I could piggyback my internet from their connection - but I would never do that since I could get infected through their network and because my own is a stronger and better connection and i have no need and of course because it is stealing). 

 

As Mike said, they are not really stealing your "IP address" (but they may be piggybacking off the signal being delivered to the router by the ISP through the router or they may have actually connected to the landline itself (the cable or the telephone line) and be connecting to the signal through that.  In either case, I do not see how a record of their site visits would be appearing on your PC (unless by penetrating your router, they have also gained access to your local network and thus to the computers connected to it through it).

 

If your router is wireless see the following:

 

************************************************************************

 

Typically, access to the router is by typing http://192.168.1.1 (or whatever your gateway address is) into your browser address bar and you'll bring up the router's configuration settings. It will probably want a username and password. Sorry, I can't help you there because I have no idea what it might be. If by 'IP' you mean your IP address, you can't do that...  Your PC settings are off (possible but less than likely) or your router is having issues- most likely settings.  Your PC IP will be a public address (meaning anyone can use it on a private network...  these numbers get used over and over again and are not addressable over the internet.)  Your router has a private IP address that is internet routable, and cannot be used by more than one user at a time.  It may be static or dynamic, but that depends on your ISP.  Your router does NAT (network address translation) to 'route' traffic that comes to it's address to your PC's IP address.

hairyfool say:

There can be two reasons for this.

 

1st, load the image or disk for Windows 8 into a drive

 

Then extend the DISM command

 

DISM /Online /Enable-Feature /FeatureName:NetFx3 /All /LimitAccess /Source:imagedrive:\sources\sxs

 

This forces the Enable Feature function to use the local installation files from your disk image instead of try to download them. Make sure the version of image matches your version of Windows.

 

Then if you still have a problem you need to repair the Windows Image (thanks to Kapil-Arya for this and the WindowsClub)

 

net stop wuauserv

cd %systemroot%\SoftwareDistribution

ren Download Download.old

net start wuauserv

net stop bits

net start bits

net stop cryptsvc

cd %systemroot%\system32

ren catroot2 catroot2old

net start cryptsvc

 

Reboot the computer and try the DISM command again.

 

If it is working the command is noticeably longer to run. Did this last night and once I was using a 8.1 ISO for my test PC it worked.
