macropod say:

Your description suggests your Excel data contain a mix of dates, text and/or numbers in the same column. You should ensure only one data type is used - in this case dates. Note that text that looks like a date isn't necessarily treated by Excel as a date - it could just as easily be text.

A simple test for text masquerading as dates is to select the affected column in Excel and widen it to, say 25% more than normal, then click on the right-alignment tab twice. If any of the dates change their alignment to left-aligned on either click, they're probably text, not dates. That's because dates are normally right-aligned and clicking on the right-alignment tab won't change their alignment.
