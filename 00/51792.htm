As Shawn mentioned there is no method for disabling command and control in WSR (Windows Speech Recognition). However, the jumping cursor may be related to audio/microphone issues.

The audio input may be too high and is sensitive to breath inhales/exhales or background noise being interpreted as commands. Run Setup microphone utility from Control Panel - Speech Recognition.

If you are using a microphone built into the computer, sounds from outside your sphere of talking may be entering the audio stream during periods you are not dictating. WSR works best with a close talk microphone such as a good noise canceling headset. If you are using a headset, make sure the microphone element is about a half inch off the corner of your mouth, not in front. This eliminates breath inhales/exhales being interpreted as words. Good entertainer microphones work best in front of one's mouth about 1/2 to 1 inch away.

lemp say:
When you say "it has disappeared from the printer list" do you mean that Printers and Faxes is blank or that there are other printers listed but not the Dell 942?

If the Print Spooler service is stopped, Printers and Faxes will be empty and you will not be able to add a printer.

If that is your situation, and given that it's a Dell, which is often a rebranded Lexmark, have you looked at the dependencies of the Print Spooler service (Start > Run > services.msc > OK)?  Lexmark printers make the Print Spooler dependent on the LexBce service, and if that file gets corrupted, the Print Spooler won't run.

The fix for the above situation is to restore the default dependency of the Print Spooler as follows:

Open a Command Prompt window (Start > Run > cmd > OK) and enter the following command:

            sc config spooler depend= RPCSS

There is no space before the = but there is a space after it.

If the above gets the Print Spooler started, then remove and reinstall the driver for the Dell 942 following the directions here --> Article One.  Do not skip step 4.

For more thorough printer driver cleaning instructions, see --> http://members.shaw.ca/bsanders/CleanPrinterDrivers.htm
