ossiemac say:

A relatively simple method is to use COUNTIF at the bottom of the data on each worksheet and for each column and then sum the values in a table on the last worksheet (or a separate worksheet just for the results.)

You can reduce the amount of work involved by selecting all of the worksheets that require the formulas and they can be inserted on all pages simultaneously.

Assume you have 20 questions in rows 2 to 21 (with first row as column headers.)

Select all sheets (To do this select first sheet and hold the shift key and click last sheet.)

In row 22 to 31 in the first column (A22:A31under your row titles) insert the numbers 1 to 10 (These will be used in the formula). Now in cell B22 enter the following formula:

=COUNTIF(B$2:B$21,$A22)

The reason for the partial absolute addressing ($ signs) is so you can now drag the forumla across and down (AutoFill) for the remaining columns and rows. The 1 to 10 in the first column is so you can use that value to find in the COUNTIF formula.

The forumals should now be in all worksheets.

Now either below the formulas on the last sheet or on another sheet enter the following forumula to sum the values on each worksheet.

=SUM(Sheet1:Sheet10!B22)

Replace Sheet1 in the formula with your first sheet name and Sheet10 with your last sheet name. (It does not matter what the sheet names are in between).

You can now drag that formula across and down.
