a. user say:

Create and edit my contacts

When you set up accounts (like Google or Outlook) on your Windows Phone, all the contacts or friends from those accounts will show up as contacts in your People Hub. You can create new contacts, edit existing ones, or delete the ones you don't need anymore.

Creating contacts

To create a new contact in Windows Phone 8.1

  1. On Start , tap People .

  2. Swipe over to Contacts, and then tap New .

  3. If you have more than one account on your phone, you'll have a chance to choose which one to save the contact in. Do one of the following:

    • If this is the first time you've created a contact, tap the account you want to use. This will be your default account—where your new contacts will be saved from now on.
    • If this isn't your first new contact, you can change the default account and save the contact to a different account. Just tap Save to, then choose the account you want. This will be your new default account, until you change it again.

  4. To create a quick contact with just the basics, do one or more of the following:

    • Tap the Name box and type your contact's first and last name, or just their first name—you can always add a last name later.
    • Tap the Mobile phone box and type the phone number.
    • Tap the Personal email box and type the email address.

  5. If you want to add more details, do any of the following:

    Note

    You can also add any of these details later, by editing the contact.

    • To add a company, middle name, nickname, title, suffix, or last name (if you didn't type it already), tap the plus sign  in the Name text box. Add the information you want, and then tap Done .
    • To add another phone number, tap Phone, tap the type of number you want to add, and then type the number. To add more numbers, tap Phone again and repeat the steps.
    • To add another email address, tap Email, tap the type of email you want to add, and then type the address. To add more email addresses, tap Email again and repeat the steps.
    • To change the ringtone or text tone you hear preview a tone, and tap the name of the tone to associate it with this contact.
    • To add other information, such as a street address, birthday, significant other, and more, tap Other.
    • To add a profile picture to a contact, tap Add photo.

  6. Tap Save .

To delete a contact in Windows Phone 8.1 or Windows Phone 8

  1. On Start , tap People .

  2. Tap the contact you want to delete.

  3. Tap More , and then tap Delete.

  4. If the contact is a linked contact—one contact card with multiple accounts associated with it—you'll need to tap the account from which you want to delete the contact.

  5. Tap Delete.

Notes
  • You can't delete contacts from social networks like Facebook or Twitter using your phone, so you won't see those accounts as choices when deleting a linked contact.
  • For a quicker way to delete a contact, tap and hold it in your contact list, then tapDelete. 

For more Follow the source:http://www.windowsphone.com/en-us/how-to/wp8/people/create-and-edit-my-contacts

Hi Peter,

 

Thanks for your query in this community. Unfortunately, it's not feasible to set two OneDrive sync folders with one Office 365 account, but you are able to move your current C drive OneDrive sync folders to another drive.

Change the location of your OneDrive folder

 

And here is my opinion on your concern:

The OneDrive sync client will only sync the files inside of it, it's also feasible to sync the Known folder redirection.

But in your E drive, the folders are created by yourself, thus, they can’t be redirected to OneDrive sync client.

 

In your scenario, I would suggest you move the E drive files in to OneDrive folder located in your C drive, and enable the OneDrive sync client feature

With this feature, the files won't took any of your local hard drive storage. And you could always access them and sync the changes at the real time when the network is available.
