                Hello all,

For the life of me I am unable to figure out or look up how to subtract time. HH:MM

I need a formula for D when I am subtracting B and C from each other it's giving me #VALUE in D.

I also need a formula for F when I am comparing what's saved from E and C.

Any help would make my job a lot easier

Thank you

A B C D E F
1 Route OT Asked OT Negotiated Saved Projected Projected VS Negotiated
2 1 1:00 00:45 00:15 00:40 -00:05
3 2 00:30 00:25 00:05 1:00 00:45

joeu2004 say:

We cannot tell if the actual data is text, simply by its appearance in

The formula in column D should be of the form:

=B2-C2

formatted as Custom [h]:mm .

If that returns a #VALUE error, the problem is not because B2 or C2 is text per se, but because Excel does not recognize the text as a numeric form.

For example, the formula ="1:00"-"0:45" works just fine for most regions, displaying the numeric time 0:15 when formatted as Custom [h]:mm .

Use a formula of the form =ISTEXT(B2) to determine if any cells in columns B, C and E contain text.  If so:

1. Be sure that colon (":") is a valid time separator for your regional configuration.  Enter 12:34 into A1 in a new worksheet, and use a formula of the form =ISNUMBER(A1) to determine if Excel recognizes that as numeric time.  If so....

2. Use a formula of the form =--SUBSTITUTE(SUBSTITUTE(B2,CHAR(160),""),CHAR(32),"") to remove any leading spaces and any non-breaking spaces, as well as to convert the result to numeric time, when formatted as Custom [h]:mm .

The formula also removes trailing spaces and spaces around the colon, even though they are okay.

If necessary, copy-and-paste-value the result of #2 into B2, formatted as Custom [h]:mm .

-----

Ostensibly, the formula in column F should be of the form:

=E2-C2

formatted as Custom [h]:mm .  (The result in F3 will be 0:35, not 0:45.)

However, Excel does not display negative time, normally.  Instead, it displays "####".

I deprecate setting the 1904 Date System option on Windows platforms, just for the purpose of displaying negative time.  That might cause problems when doing copy-and-paste-value between Excel workbooks that do not set that option.

Instead, use a formula of the following form in column F:

=IF(E2-C2>=0, E2-C2, TEXT(C2-E2,"\-h:mm"))

formatted as Custom [h]:mm and explicitly right-aligned.  Do not rely on the default horizontal alignment.

The comparison of that form allows for "text time" as well as numeric time in columns C and E.

Note that "negative time" will be text, not a numeric value.  In such cases, the value in column F cannot be referenced directly in time arithmetic or comparison in other cells.

Instead, use an expression of the following form to reference column F in other cells:

IF(ISNUMBER(F2), F2, -MID(F2,2,255))

Arguably, 255 is overkill; but it is okay, because MID returns the minimum string.  18 is sufficient; but that is difficult to remember, IMHO.

[EDIT] For -MID(...), MID(F2,2,255) must represent 9999:59 or less.
