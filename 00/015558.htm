By default, each new section uses the same headers and footers as the previous section.

 

Go into the header of the second section, and click to unhighlight the "Linked to previous" button on the toolbar (2003) / ribbon (2010).

You can now specify a different header/footer for the second section.

You can add more sections if needed, and treat them the same way.

jay freedman say:
The standard shortcut Ctrl+Alt+E should work, but it might have been assigned to something else -- either by you or by an add-in.

To find out, click the Insert tab, click the Symbol button near the right end, and click More Symbols in the dropdown. In the Symbols dialog, set the Font dropdown at the top left to "(normal text)"; set the From dropdown at the bottom right to "Unicode (hex)"; and set the Subset dropdown at the top right to "Currency Symbols". Click on the euro symbol to select it. Then look at the bottom center, where it says "Shortcut key". It might say Alt+Ctrl+E or Alt+0128 or something else.

If it doesn't say Alt+Ctrl+E, click the Shortcut Key button. In the next dialog, in the "Press new shortcut key" box, press the Ctrl, Alt, and E keys together, then click the Assign button at the bottom, and click Close. That will reassign the standard shortcut to the symbol.

For some other ways to insert the symbol in Word, see http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/word-help/display-and-enter-the-euro-and-other-currency-symbols-HP005275875.aspx. It's for Word 2003 but it should all work in Word 2010.
