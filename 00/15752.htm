Do you have any Application.OnTime events?  Do you have any VBA event macros (e.g. Worksheet_Change)?

What makes you think the problem is a delayed update; that is, incomplete recalculation between two VBA statements?

Note:  I am not trying to be provocative.  These are simply the types of questions I ask myself and my colleagues when we are debugging "inexplicable" issues with parallel processes and threads.  Rarely is the behavior and root cause what we perceived.  Instead, usually our perception is distorted, in part by our expectations and theories.

Surely, on rare occassion, our perception was correct and asynchronous events did not occur in the sequence we intended.  So I am not dismissing your perception altogether.  I just prefer to assume my perception is incorrect and look for a simpler explanation before I finally accept the "unlikely" answer.  Occam's Razor. ;-)

Maybe, the extractFromProjectTab procedure is doing something to change the Calculation setting?

Maybe, Excel insists on a recalculate when you delete a worksheet?  As long as extractFromProjectTab is non-destructive (i.e., it leaves the source sheet unchanged), you may want to skip the copying of sheets(2) and then the deletion of the copy.

You may also want to move the Application.Calculation = xlCalculationManual to before the Open method.  But, Excel may still insist on recalculating the workbook on open -- it will under certain conditions, one of which is that the workbook was last modified in an older version of Excel.  If the data you are extracting are in a table, you may want to look at using SQL to retrieve the data w/o opening the workbook.

julie fka profjulie say:

You won't be able to transfer information directly from your HD2 to a new HD7.  You can use the Hotmail/ActiveSync Access service to sync the Contact and Calendar data that is on your HD2 to the Windows Live account you plan to use with the HD7.  Once this data is in your Windows Live account it is easy enough to sync it to your HD7:

 

  1. Click on âActiveSyncâ under Start menu or under Programs.
  2. Click on the link above to configure the ActiveSync profile for Hotmail access. You may need to delete the existing profile if it is already in use.
  3. Disable automatic detection of Exchange server settings.
  4. Enter your full Hotmail address in User name field. Enter password. Leave Domain blank.
  5. Enter m.hotmail.com in the Server address field.
  6. Select the data to sync. Hotmail supports all 4 data types via ActiveSync.
  7. The device will connect to Hotmail ActiveSync and perform an initial sync for the selected data.

There is no way to transfer phone history or text messages from an old phone to a windows phone 7.  You can upload any Office-related files or PDF files to SkyDrive, Dropbox or any other cloud-based file site you use and then it will be there to download to a new windows phone 7.

Applications you've purchased and/or installed to a windows mobile phone (like the HD2) are not compatible with a windows phone 7, so you won't be able to install any of those applications to an HD7.
