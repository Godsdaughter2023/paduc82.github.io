graham mayor say:
As Steve suggests, there are two mail service providers involved here. One at the office and one at home. Your home service provider will use port 25 for its outgoing SMTP port and that will almost certainly be reserved for the home service provider's e-mail account.

Your company account will also be configured to port 25 for its SMTP service and thus be blocked when you are at home. The answer is to check what alternative port the company mail provider will allow for outgoing mail and if available to change port 25 to that port for your company e-mail.

If there is no alternative port then you could work around it by using (say) GMail as a proxy mail function - see http://www.gmayor.com/use_google_gmail_to_remove_spam.htm Note that some of GMail's dialogs have changed a little from those shown on my web page, but the principle is the same.

From your description I doubt that there is a company Microsoft Exchange server involved, but if there is you need to consult the company IT support to establish how to connect to that remotely as Gordon suggests.

john w. vinson say:

Light mining, yes... but if you're talking about hundreds of text files you may be getting into the sixty-ton earthmover open pit mine realm <g>.

 

Karl's suggestion isn't really necessary: the LIKE operator will let you seacrh a memo field. Where it gets complicated is that

 

WHERE memofield LIKE '*" & [Enter first term:] & "* AND memofield LIKE "*" & [Enter second term:] & "*"

 

will work but it is likely to be VERY slow for large memo fields and/'or large numbers of records (since you cannot index memo fields and indexes won't work searching inside fields in any case), and it's inflexible - you would need to rewrite the query when you want to use THREE strings.

 

Access can be made to work, but it's very likely that there are better tools!

Is there a way to create a custom Cover page?

james yan msft say: Hi Becca,

In Word 2016 for Mac, we can't add a custom cover page into the cover page gallery, but here is a workaround, we can use a Word template which contains the custom cover page:

1. Open the cover page which you want to use.
2. Go to "Format" > "Document".
3. Click on "Layout" tab, select checkbox for "Different first page" > OK.
4. Go to "File" > "Save as Template...".
5. Give this template a name and save.

Next time when you want to use this custom cover page, you can click "File" > "New from template..." > choose the template you just created.
