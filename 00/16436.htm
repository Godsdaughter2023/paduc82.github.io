Resolution
When you send an SMS text message from Skype, the recipient will see the first 11 characters of your Skype Name and they won’t be able to reply to your SMS text message.

david barkley say:

Sharing OneDrive documents with someone outside the organization

Is there any way to share documents stored in one drive with people that are outside your organization or those that do not have a Microsoft account?

Is there anyway to create an account in office 365 for just giving access to one drive documents.

I would really like to start using one drive since it is part of the package but if I can not let people outside the organization gain access then I am thinking I better stick with dropbox as I can share with whoever I like.

jerryduan_msft8021 say:

Hi David,

1. To let anyone view or edit the file you shared, you can consider posting an anonymous guest link by clicking Get a link when sharing, then clicking CREATE LINK under View Only or Edit. So it will not require users to sign in with a Microsoft account or an Office 365 Work or School account.

2. A tenant administrator is able to create new users in Office 365. In this way, the user will be taken as an internal user of the organization, which enables you to share document with him. 

Regards,
Jerry

rangao_msft4227 say:

Hi David, 

An anonymous guest link is only for the document you selected. They will not have access to other documents you haven’t shared.

You can create a new site or a document library, store all the files you want to share then share the whole site or library with people outside your organization. For more information, you can refer to the following article.
