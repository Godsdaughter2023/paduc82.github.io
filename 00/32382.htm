bill.p. say: Hi Garth, I'm Graeme, an independent adviser here to help.

Storage Sense is a feature of Windows 10 that monitors your temporary files used by Windows in its normal daily functions, and removes ones that aren't required anymore to keep your drive clean.

You don't need to worry about Storage Sense removing your important files, as it only has access to remove temporary system files and clear your recycle bin periodically.

If you'd like to see what Storage Sense manages, you can search Start for "Storage" and choose Storage, then click "Change how we free up space".

You'll see options to select how Storage Sense functions. Rest assured that this is normal behavior and you won't lose any of your personal files. You can always disable Storage Sense if you decide.

Please let me know if you need further support! Thanks!

hiltonoishi say: Hi, Garth

You can not find what was deleted. The only precautious way is having this disabled.

Windows 10 should have Storage sense disabled by default, but you can check and turn off the feature as necessary using these steps:

1.Open Settings.
2.Click on System.
3.Click on Storage.
4.Turn off the Storage sense toggle switch.

Once you've completed the steps, the feature will stop running automatically on your device.

In the event that your device starts running out of space to perform an upgrade or store files, you can re-enable the feature and use these tips to free up space.
