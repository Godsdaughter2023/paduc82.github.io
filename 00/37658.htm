zakhh say:

Eddie,

Skype doesn't "decide" what app your phone uses to make phone calls. That's a setting defined in Android, the operating system of your Moto G7.

When you install Skype, Android recognizes it as an app that is capable of making phone calls. When you dial a number and no default app is defined for making phone calls, Android will ask you which app to use for the call. If you then select Skype and click on "Always" instead of "Just once", Skype will be set as your default app for making all calls.

You should be able to change that setting in your system settings.

Similarly, in the Contacts app of your phone, you can select which contacts should be displayed there. You can store contacts locally or on external servers linked to an app account, like Skype or Exchange.

Furthermore, you can allow apps like Skype to access or even manage your contacts, but you can also select to block Skype from accessing your contacts. But again, these privileges are managed by Android, not by Skype.
