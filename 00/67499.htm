monica cam say:
Hi EdFelker,

You can use the Local Group Policy Editor to prevent updates from installing automatically. This is an advanced troubleshooting so I suggest that you create a System Restore point so you can go back to this state in case that you will encounter any issues along the process. To create a system restore point, refer to this article.

Once you're done creating a system restore point, you may then proceed to defer the updates in Local Group Policy. Here's how:

  1. Press the Windows key + R on your keyboard.
  2. On the run command box type, gpedit.msc, and then hit Enter.
  3. Navigate to Computer Configuration\Administrative Templates\Windows Components\Windows Update.
  4. Double-click the Configure Automatic Updates policy.
  5. On the left pane, check the Enabled option.
  6. Under Options, you may choose to the following choices on how to configure automatic updates:
          •2 - Notify for download and notify for install.
          •3 - Auto download and notify for install.
          •4 - Auto download and schedule the install.
          •5 - Allow local admin to choose the settings.
  7. Click Apply, and then hit Enter.
  8. Restart your computer to apply the changes.

Post us back the results once you've tried the above steps.

shenan stanley say:

You probably *don't* have a URL.  You have an IP, you might have an internal/private one and an external/public one you share with other devices on your private LAN.

http://www.whatismyip.com/

That'll give you the External IP.

If you press "Windows Logo Key" + R on your keyboard and in the "Open:" blank type:

   cmd /k ipconfig

... and click OK.  A command prompt will open and there may be a part called "IPv4 Address" and after that will be your private IP address.

As far as your email address, you should know that because you get that from the Email Service Provider you choose (and usually your Internet Service Provider coughs up a few too.)  If you don't know it...
