stanimal21 say:

You've explained things just fine and I know what you want, but I haven't been clear enough because it does do what you've requested. To set the record straight, SharePoint does NOT require check-in/check-out on any file; that's totally configurable and has been for years. Second, SharePoint is the backbone for OneDrive for Business; no matter what, you're using SharePoint document libraries to host your content. Think of it like this: SharePoint hosts the information whereas OneDrive for Business facilitates the syncing process. In fact, you can sync any pre-existing SharePoint Online or SharePoint 2016 library to your computer with OneDrive for Business just by clicking the Sync button on the SharePoint site/OneDrive for Business Group webpage:

If you have experience with traditional SharePoint (SP2010 or SP2013), the Group I mentioned in my previous post is another SharePoint site (sorry if I misspoke before), however you can create multiple document libraries for a single Group just like you could with SharePoint sites. If you've already created a group, you can add more Libraries to it:

  1. Browse to your company's OneDrive for Business website and then click on the Group on the left-hand side
  2. Click on the "Go to site" link located on the top-right of the page (it's small text)
  3. Click the "New" button located below the main page title
  4. Choose "Document library"

Now you can have different libraries to hold different information within a single group. OneDrive for Business syncs those libraries to separate folders on your local machine.

If you're worried about how it syncs any edits, it's exactly what you'd expect. In fact, OneDrive for Business/SharePoint Online seamlessly handles simultaneous edits with Microsoft documents. Have you used Google Drive and edited a spreadsheet or document with someone else and saw their cursor moving around the screen? It works just like that in OneDrive for Business/SharePoint Online, except that functionality also occurs in Microsoft Office 2016. If they don't have internet access when they edit the file, the change is saved locally and then uploaded later; if there are any conflicts then it creates another file with the computer name in it to prevent information loss. Non-Microsoft Office files can't be synced live though, so the change is synced after the change is saved the local computer, and then it's uploaded and synced to others.

There's a learning curve here because it's corporate-grade software so it'll take some time to familiarize yourself (and teach your co-workers) how to use the product.
