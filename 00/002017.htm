cody c. say:
The computer I am trying to connect to is at a different location , I know it is open to accept RDC as I can access it from my other computer at home which is not a MAC.

It may work at home, but when you try to RDP into a machine from another location, the whole game changes. When you're connecting, are you using your computer name (i.e. Gabrielle-PC) or your public IP address (i.e. 123.232.555.5). If you're not using the latter, that's likely the problem.

There's a pretty complex article that explains the entire process of connecting to a computer outside of your local network here:
http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows7/allow-remote-desktop-connections-from-outside-your-home-network

If you want to use Remote Desktop to connect to your PC away from home, you'll need to follow the steps there. There are a few other third party services that make this much easier. The following thread's answer offers a few that are worth looking into:
http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/windows_7-networking/remote-desktop-over-internet/686c85c3-b314-4ec3-bff2-cd8b6b3b0433

Unfortunately, none of this is going to help in the short term and you'll need to have access to your home machine to perform any of these fixes.
