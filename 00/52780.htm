To clear the duplicate entries, click another feature tab in the Player (such as Now Playing), and then click Library.

If this doesn't clear the duplicate entries from your library, you can use the Add to Library by Searching Computer dialog box to scan a folder on your computer that the invalid duplicate entries point to. The Player will remove the invalid entries in your library that point to files that no longer exist in the folder.

Do the above step for the files that are stored in Hard drives.


Method 1: To use the Add to Library by Searching Computer dialog box


a) Start Windows Media Player.

b) Press F3 on your keyboard to open the Add to Library by Searching Computer dialog box.

c) Click the Browse button to locate the folder on your computer for which your library contains invalid entries. Specify the folder location in the Look in box.

If you are not sure which duplicate entry in your library is invalid, you can add a File Path column to your library display. Specify the folder on your computer that matches the invalid file path displayed in your library.

d) Click the Search button.

The Player will search for digital media files and playlists in the folder that you specify and will remove the invalid entries in your library that point to files that no longer exist in the folder. If invalid duplicate entries point to other folders on your computer, repeat this procedure, specifying a different folder each time.

If only a small number of duplicate entries exist, you can delete the invalid ones manually by right-clicking the invalid entry, and then clicking Delete.

For large number of duplicate entries in your library (or if your entire library is duplicated), it might be best to create a new library and delete the files.

Doing a factory reset does not lose all your progress. Your game progress is saved to the cloud and when you add your profile back to your Xbox One and start to play a game, it syncs back to the console.

I would strongly suggest doing a factory reset keeping all games and apps.

  1. Press and hold the console power button till it fully shuts down.
  2. Press and hold both bind and eject buttons (keep held)

arminatorx say:

When the console takes that long to start, it's usually because of an inconsistency of the file system, that the console has to check and tries to repair (similarly to the Chkdsk program your Windows PC runs, if it was turned off unexpectedly by a power outage or something). 

If the console is unable to repair the inconsistency, your only option IS to use the factory reset, or if that doesn't do it, reinstall the console OS via an USB thumb drive. 

Don't worry! As long as you have the email and password for the accounts on your console, you can simply sign into these accounts again, and all save game progress is primarily stored on the Xbox Cloud online.

The console only keeps a copy of these locally on the hard drive. So if you start any game with your profile after the factory reset or USB recovery, the console will simply download your game progress, and you'll continue where you left off.

So as long as your console was online before that behavior happened, your progress and everything are safely backed up online. Your console can burn down to the ground, and you can get another one, sign in with your account, and you can download your games for free, and your progress will simply download to the new console. 

So don't be afraid of resetting your console.

Hi,

 

I know how frustrating it is to be unable to receive important emails. We have to consider several causes why you were not able to receive emails such as:

 

A. Filter Settings of the account.

 

To ensure that your account filter settings are not causing incoming email not to be delivered to your account, I suggest that you check your settings by following the steps below:

 

Check your Junk folder

 

1. Sign in here.

2. Click on Junkon the left pane.

3. Check to see if the missing mail is in this folder.

4. If you see any messages that should have been delivered to the Inbox , click the box on the left of each message to put a check mark and then click the Not Junk button.

 

Check your Blocked sender list

 

1. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the gear icon, and then click More mail settings. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.

2. Click Blocked senders.

3. In the Blocked senders list, select the name or domain (the part of an e-mail address that follows the @ sign, such as hotmail.com) that you now want to receive mail from, and then click << Remove from list.

 

Add the email address to your Safe senders list

 

1. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the gear icon, and then click More mail settings. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.

2. Click Safe Senders.

3. In the text box, type in the email address or domain you’d like to always receive email from, and then click Add to list >>.

 

Check your Email Rules

 

1. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the gear icon, and then click More mail settings. Under Customizing Outlook, click Rules for sorting new messages.

2. Perform one of the actions as per your requirement:

Click Edit next to the rule that you want to edit.

Or

Choose the rule that you want to delete and click Delete.

Note: Not all international characters are supported as filters.

 

Check your Junk Mail Settings

 

1. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the gear icon, and then click More mail settings.

2. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.

3. Under Choose a junk e-mail filter, choose the level of protection that you want, and then click Save. If the Exclusive level was set, messages from senders not on your Contacts or Safe sender’s lists will have been sent to the Junk e-mail folder.

 

Check your Reply-to address

 

1. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the gear icon, and then click More mail settings. Under Writing email, click Reply-to address.

2. Select Current e-mail address radio button.

3. Click Save.

 

B. Mail Client Settings.

 

You might not also send or receive emails due to incorrect configuration when accessing account using a Mail Client. For this reason, please reconfigure your account by following the settings of the link below:

 

Send and receive Hotmail from an email app

 

C. Your inbox is full

 

You need to delete your emails and leave the ones that are important to save space.

 

D. 3rd Party Email account.

 

If you are using University email account such as (.edu) or other 3rd party account, it is possible that there is an issue with your network/server. You might need to contact your domain administrator for further assistance. They may have changed some settings in your domain's system that's why you are having problems receiving emails.

 

If the above steps provided did not resolve your issue, I would like to ask additional details regarding your concern:

> Have you tried to send a test mail to your own email address? did you receive the test email?
