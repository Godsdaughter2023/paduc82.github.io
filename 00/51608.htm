    Well - the '14 disks' that you made when you bought the system new - that's probably the clue/method.  How you start that - that's not a Microsoft thing.  Sounds like whomever manufactured the computer has come up with their own method of restoring it to the 'factory default' state.You should follow those instructions (what is the computer make & model?)

    Beyond that (and before that) however - you should get an external hard drive and backup your stuff.  Internet Favorites and bookmarks.  Documents.  Spreadsheets.  Music.  Pictures.  Drawings.  Databases.  Installation files.  Emails.  Contacts.  Product Keys/Serial Numbers.  Etc. and so on...

    I'd also suggest downloading, installing, running and printing the results of Belarc Advisor (that's a link) so you know what hardware you have in the system.  You may (likely will) need to obtain drivers for various hardware components either from the computer manufacturer or the component manufacturers (or both.)  This will help you do that as well as record other things - like product keys and serial numbers for some of the more common applications.

    After you restore the system, you'll need to likely update the operating system first with patches.  Possibly - given the '14 disk' thing - all service packs.  Here's what you'll do there:

    Get SP2 (manually) and save it to your desktop. (There's a link there.) [You are an IT Professional for now.]

    Install it.

    Reboot afterwards at least a couple of times with a few minutes between each reboot.

    Get SP3 (manually) and save it to your desktop. (There's a link there.) [You are an IT Professional for now.]

    Install it.

    Reboot afterwards at least a couple of times with a few minutes between each reboot.

    Download at least Internet Explorer 7 (if not Internet Explorer 8.)

    Install it.

    Reboot.

    Now try to get updates through the normal web page (http://windowsupdate.microsoft.com/) and see how many more you can get (100+) and keep checking until you don't have anymore.

    Then you can install your other applications and update those and restore your data backups.
