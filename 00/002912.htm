Protect cells in Excel - Microsoft Community

sasavona say:

Protect cells in Excel

Hi!

I do not want users to editing information on some cells in  Excel, how do I lock only those cells without protecting the whole sheet?

hansv mvp say:

By default, ALL cells in an Excel worksheet are locked, but this only becomes effective when you protect the worksheet.

To do what you want takes two steps:

  1. Unlock the cells that users should be able to edit:
    - Select those cells.
    - Press Ctrl+1 to activate the Format Cells dialog.
    - Activate the Protection tab.
    - Clear the Locked check box.
    - Click OK.
  2. Protect the worksheet:
    - Activate the Review tab of the ribbon.
    - Click the Protect Sheet button.
    - Use the check boxes to specify what users are allowed to do.
    - At the very least, leave 'Select unlocked cells' ticked.
    - Click OK.
    - If you entered a password, you'll have to enter it again.

One real concern here: WHY do you want to store the Employee type redundantly in a second table? That's very rarely a good design! Just store the employee type once, and once only, in the Employee table, and look it up (using a combo box, or a query, or a DLookUp function) whenever it's needed.

SELECT tblTECHS.TECH, tblTECHS.EmployeeType FROM tblTECHS ORDER BY [TECH];

 

as the rowsource; set the Column Count property of the combo to 2 (to include both fields) and the ColumnWidths to 1.5";0" to display the name but conceal the type; and put an unbound textbox on the form with a control source

 

=comboboxname.Column(1)

 

to display (but not store) the second column from the combo box. (The Column property is zero based).
