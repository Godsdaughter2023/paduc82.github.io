vijay a. verma say:

Use below for total compounded amount assuming that you have deposited 100000 and then no further deposits -

=FV(15%,33/12,0,-100000)

If you want to know, only interest portion

=FV(15%,33/12,0,-100000)-100000

But if you are paying 100000 every year, then compounded amount =

=FV(15%,33/12,-100000)

And Interest would be 

=FV(15%,33/12,-100000)-100000*33/12

suzanne s. barnhill say:

Displaying a document in Final view just hides the markup. In order to remove the markup and make sure that the document is as you see it in Final view, click the bottom of the Accept button in the Changes group on the Review tab and select Accept All Changes in Document. Then turn off Track Changes and Save.

 

This has nothing to do with protection, of which there are various kinds. If this is a protected form, then you can't make or accept changes unless you unprotect it. Mark as Final is a different kind of protection, which makes the document Read Only but does not remove your markup. If you have marked it as final, then you can't make any further changes (including accepting the changes you've already made). You'll have to remove the Read Only status, which you can do in Windows Explorer by right-clicking, choosing Properties, and removing the Read Only flag.

Go to Settings/Update and Security/Activation to check the activation of your system.  It should tell you that Windows is activated with a digital license. 

Please understand that Windows 10 does not use a product key for activation.  It uses a digital license file on Microsoft's licensing servers that has the unique hardware IDs of your device and the edition of Windows 10 installed on it.  This means that the device is registered with Microsoft as a Registered Windows 10 Device.  This registration is what activates Windows 10.  

This is all that is needed to activate Windows 10 should you need to re-install the same edition of Windows 10 or install additional copies in new partitions.  This is also why when you use the Go to Store button on the Activation page to upgrade from Home to Pro you do not get an email with a product key in it.  Instead, the Store records a new digital license on Microsoft's servers.  

This is an anti-piracy measure.  MSFT got tired of playing whackamole with hackers who kept finding ways to exploit the activation files.  So MSFT got rid of the activation files entirely and started relying on the unique hardware IDs of the device, such as the motherboard's serial number and perhaps that of the cpu.  No more activation files, nothing to hack anymore.  Now all a product key is used for is the initial registration of a new Windows 10 device so that MSFT knows what edition is installed.

Windows 10 Insider preview builds are nothing more than new versions of Windows 10 that are not released to the public.  No new registration of the device is needed.  All preview builds are activated by the device's digital license, as the Activation page indicates.
