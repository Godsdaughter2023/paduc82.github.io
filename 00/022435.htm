Over the air synchronisation failure

About 4 weeks ago all my WM5 users experienced a failure of over-the-air synchronisation and push email. I have been unable to get it back, and am about to switch to Blackberry to get them back online.

 

The devices are various smartphones with Windows Mobile 5, the mail server is Exchange Server 2003 running or Windows Server 2003 R2.

 

The problem is that in general no email is downloaded to the smartphone. When the users do a manual send/receive the status bar shows the device connecting, identifying, say, 0/56 emails, then 56/56 and disconnecting. No email is downloaded to the device. All connection and synch settings are correct and the devices worked until 4 weeks ago and then all failed together. Outlook Web Access works fine. A consultant has checked out the Exchange setup and the firewall and can't find a fault. The mobile service provider checked the device settings and also can't find a fault.

 

The really weird thing is that if the someone logs on to the user's PC and opens their mailbox in Outlook, even if the smartphone is hundreds of miles away in the middle of nowhere it will automatically sync completely and stays synced until he logs off again. Unfortunately the obvious workaround of buying each user a spare PC to leave logged in in a locked room somewhere isn't attractive!

 

If someone else, say a secretary, opens the user's mailbox in their Outlook nothing happens, but if they then modify an email just that modified email is downloaded to the smartphone.

 

The single exception is that when I recently set up a brand new mobile (after the user broke one), it synced correctly once after installation, but from then on behaved like all the others.

 

Any ideas anyone? Microsoft are about to lose a little bit of business to RIM!
