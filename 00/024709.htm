The first screen shot shows that you have added a tab to the Ribbon. That won't work in Office for Mac. The Ribbon is not programmable. Instead, make a toolbar using the Toolbars collection. Menus and Toolbars in Office for the Mac work the same as Office 2003 and earlier on Windows. 

You can also customize contextual menus on the Mac. How to do this is explained in Excel MVP Ron DeBruin's wonderful page of Excel 2011 tips for the Mac. Visit this site for more suggestions.

Your screen shot of the tabbed userform has pictures on the userform. Pictures on userforms have special requirements.


edwin tam say: In VB Editor, when you program on a PC, use the Form controls which originally come with Excel. Do not add references to anything ActiveX components.

You MUST test your macro on a Mac to ensure they work on the Mac. Mac Excel 2011 have a lot of funny undocumented incompatibilities with Excel for Windows.

I highly recommend to re-do all forms on the Mac in order to make them look nice on the Mac.

But overall, don't be scared by the testing. Windows and Mac Excel basically have VERY GOOD cross-platform compatibility. Microsoft has done a very good job to make this happen.

Concerning Win-Mac cross platform add-ins, if you want to see a real-life demonstration of how complex such compatibility can go, you can have a look at my Excel Power Expander. I think it is the biggest cross-platform add-in shareware projects.

I have also opened a Discussion about Mac Excel 2011 bugs in the Discussion section. You may refer to my posts for some information on some of those points to need to bear in mind.
