kosh vorlon say:

Hi Shopper,

 

In Settings, you can set default levels for the various types of Alerts.  For more about responding to different levels of malicious software (including a link to the various types of Alerts, see http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/what-should-i-do-if-microsoft-security-essentials-detects-malicious-software-on-my-pc.

 

In the MSE Settings Tab, there's an link for Default Actions.  There you can choose how MSE responds to each type of Alert.  You can also choose check a box where the system will always do the default action (which may include some prompts) or not check that box and the system will usually prompt for the action to take without doing it automatically (though it will offer the established default as the first choice).

 

Personally, I choose to set everything at Quarantine and check the do it automatically box.  I then go to the Advanced section of the Settings Tab and increase Quarantine time to the maximum (3 months at present).  This gives me the most options.  Anything put in Quarantine can be restored or removed at your choice.  If you choose Remove and discover it was a false positive and you really needed that file, it will be difficult and perhaps impossible to recover.  If you choose Allow, then you're opening the system to a potential threat.  If you choose System Default then if could do anything depending on the threat - including removal or allow.  That's why I think Quarantine is the best choice for all of them - you can always change you mind later (and your system is just as safe with it in Quarantine as it is with it removed, but in Quarantine you'll have access to it if you want to submit it for testing or double-check complete removal with another tool that might be more specific for that particular infection. 

 

The chances of the scan completing and not being stalled at a prompt is reduced if you choose the automatically used default option button.

 

The History log is just of log of what was detected and what action was taken (depending on what you chose to do or how you set the MSE to respond).  It does not determine anything and cannot be used to change anything - it's just a log.  If I recall, a detection lasts 15 days in this log (it was once 30, but I think it changed since the last detection I had was 15 - but it's been so long since the last I'm not certain).  In any case, whatever it is, you can't change it.

 

I hope this helps.

 

Good luck!

 

Kosh

MSE is new to me and Kosh confused with his long explanation so bear with me.

 

Under 'Settings, Default Actions,' what option should be chosen for each alert level and should 'Apply Recommended Actions' be checked?

 

Under the 'History' tab, will a threat be displayed under 'Quarantined Items, Allowed Items, and All Detected Items' depending on the option that I chose in response to an alert 'and' after a scan has detected a threat or do I have to choose one of these three options in advance?

kosh vorlon say:

In Settings, go to Default Settings.  For each of the four alert options use the dropmenu to choose Quarantine.  Then check the box at the bottom to do this automatically to minimize being prompted.  Save the changes.

 

Next, in Settings, go to Advanced and check the box for "Remove Quarantine files after" and then use the dropbox to choose the longest period available.  Save the changes.

 

The History tab will work then as follows for you:

 

1.  After the scan is done, anything detected during scans or in real-time will show in the All Detected Items area along with information about it and what was done.  It's just a list.  You can't do anything but look at it.  It will only show there for 15 days and you can't change that.

 

2.  If something was Quarantined, it will show when you go to that area.  You can do nothing (and then it will remain for the time you set in Advanced and then be removed automatically), or you can restore it if you're absolutely certain it was a mistake, or you can remove it (which I'd recommend you do not need to do as knowing what was put in quarantine can come in handy later and your system is just as safe as if it were removed).

 

3.  If you restore something that was quarantined by accident (be REALLY sure that's the case), then you should manually add it to the approved list so it doesn't continue to happen every time you run a scan.  Those stay indefinitely but will not occur with scans as you're setting them up.  This area will only have items you add manually yourself if ever needed.  I've never needed to do so myself.

 

I hope this helps explain better.  Sorry for being too detailed earlier.  Ask if you still remain confused.

 

Best wishes,

 

Kosh
