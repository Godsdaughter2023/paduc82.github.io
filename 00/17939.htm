youssef.e say: Hi all, after struggling for ages I finally found a way to go around this issue.

First of all shame on you Microsoft. You try and become more consumer friendly, you so far demonstrate your complete failure. This issue is a very good example. I bought a Surface after 10 years on Mac OSX, and I stumble on a problem for which google spits out zillions of complains stacking up for so many years... and still no official fix.. Seriously: do you even care about customer feedback?

Now to the solution: There is a virtual vertical line on oneNote pages that if you click to the left of, the new text box will not go further to the left. Let me say it in different words: If you type a title when you start a new page. Look at where the first letter starts, and try and click (or touch) further down the page to the left of that letter, this will bring the pointer at that level of the page, but perfectly lined up with the first letter of the title. Try it if not clear and see what this does.

I hope this works for you too. Good luck, and hopefully Microsoft technocrats will understand customer feedback counts more than their quirky sense of good design.
