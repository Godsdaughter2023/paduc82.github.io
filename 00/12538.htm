From the above posts what I understand is even if you send email from your Outlook account (Hotmail) to the recipients, they reply to your Office 365 Account. Also you set up email forward option from Office 365 to Outlook.com (Hotmail) account.

 

There are two possibilities for this issue.

  • Your Office 365 account should not be set as “Default email address” while sending email from Outlook.com.
  • Your Office 365 account should not be set as “Reply to address” when you receive email from recipients to your Outlook.com (Hotmail) account.

You need to change two settings on your Outlook.com email account.

 

Set your Outlook.com (Hotmail) account as default "From" address & default “Reply-to address” in your Outlook.com (Hotmail) account setting.

 

Please follow the below method to set your Default “From” address.

  1. Click the Options icon in the Right top corner of the browser page, then click Options.
  2. Click “Your email accounts” under “Managing your account” category.
  3. Under “Default "From" address”, select the address you'd like to send from by default.
  4. To return to your inbox, click the Outlook.com logo in the upper left.

Please follow the below method to set your Default “Reply-to address”.

  1. Click the Options icon in the Right top corner of the browser page, then click Options.
  2. Click “Reply-to address” under “Writing email” category.
  3. Make sure “Your "From" address” option is checked and click on “Save”.

stefan blom say:

After I signed out through Word 2013, the MS OneDrive still synchs. It means signing out Word doesn't mean signing out the MS OneDrive.

Which version of Windows are you using? Recent versions won't let you disable OneDrive completely, but you can turn off the synchronizing in OneDrive and run Office without being signed in.

In Word, do the following: Click File | Options | Save. Clear the option to "Show additional places for saving, even if sign-in may be required" and select "Save to Computer by default."

You may also want to select "Don't show the Backstage when opening or saving files"; once you have done that, you can quickly access your local folders by using keyboard shortcuts: Ctrl+O opens the Open dialog box and F12 opens the Save As dialog box.
