katrina men say:

Hi,

This message will show if your junk email filter is set to Exclusive. We suggest that you turn this off by following these steps:

  1. Log in to Outlook.com with your account.
  2. Ensure that you are not in Beta version. Check this by looking at the toggle located near the top right side of your window. It should show Try the beta.
  3. Click on the gear icon at the top right side of your screen.
  4. Click on Options.
  5. Under the Junk email settings, click on Filters and reporting.
  6. Set the Choose a junk email filer setting to Standard.
  7. Click Save.

This should allow emails to be routed to your Inbox and not your Junk folder.

In short, when moving certain messages from one gmail imap 'folder' to another from Outlook 2016, I wind up with two messages in my gmail account. I say certain messages because it really only seems to be a problem with HTML messages with images, not sure why this would be. when I examine the headers from gmail, it seems that Outlook has modified the headers during the move to the other folder. Because of this, gmail sees them as two different messages and keeps both.

Basic steps to reproduce:

Connect outlook 2016 to a gmail account.

Find an HTML message in your inbox (non-spam ad messages from corporations usually fit the bill)

Click to delete the message

Switch back to gmail.com, the message is in 'Trash' and also still in 'All Mail'

For each message in gmail, expand the menu beside the reply button and click 'Show Original'

The one that Outlook copied to Trash has different headers than the original which is still in All Mail. I'm not gonna post the whole headers here now, but the most obvious sign is that the moved message displays "Using Microsoft Outlook 16" beside the from address. 

The rest of the actual headers are all rearranged and even the content of the message seems to be different, like Outlook reformatted the HTML. Comparing the two 'Original Messages' with a compare tool shows them to be very different. 

SO, why is Outlook 2016 changing messages when moving them to another folder? Because of this I wind up with duplicated messages on my gmail account thus making Outlook completely useless as an email client to me. I pay for Office 365, yet I have to resort to the win 10 mail app or gmail.com to manage my gmail.
