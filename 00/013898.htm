ronrosenfeld say:

I assume from your wording that you want to count cells that contain zero, but not cells that are blank:

This formula must be **array-entered**

=AVERAGE(IF((LEN(myRange)>0)*(MOD(ROW(myRange),2)=0),myRange))

Replace myRange with the obvious.

----------------------------------------
To **array-enter** a formula, after entering
the formula into the cell or formula bar, hold down
<ctrl><shift> while hitting <enter>.  If you did this
correctly, Excel will place braces {...} around the formula.

amit tandon say:

You have already got replies to calculate the average of even rows, which are not blank (but which may contain zero) which is an array formula (CTRL+SHIFT+ENTER) as below:

=AVERAGE(IF(MOD(ROW($A$1:$A$30),2)=0,IF($A$1:$A$30<>"",$A$1:$A$30)))

However, if you prefer a non-array formula, you may try below:

=SUMPRODUCT((MOD(ROW($A$1:$A$100),2)=0)*($A$1:$A$100))/SUMPRODUCT(($A$1:$A$100<>"")*(MOD(ROW($A$1:$A$100),2)=0))

Because they are just masks and not actual replacements, they only affect you. The other person will still see cos(x) unless they too are on Windows Phone 7.
