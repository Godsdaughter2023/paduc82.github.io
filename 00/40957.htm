    M-SEARCH * HTTP/1.1
    Host: 239.255.255.250:1900
    ST: urn:schemas-canon-com:service:ICPO-WFTEOSSystemService:1
    Man: "ssdp:discover"
    MX: 3

    Disclaimer: I'm not a PC administrator (but I am command line savvy on other platforms).  I tried the recommendation given in http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/windows_10-networking/windows-10-ssdp-discovery-issue/d228e91f-ff5d-4786-80d1-52ebaf429eb0 but to no avail.

    FWIW, the packets don't happen when the machine is rebooted, they only start after logging in.  I know SSDP is part of the UPnP discovery system, but could not find anything useful in the online help system.

    So: how do I discover what app or service is generating these packets?  And -- even more crucially -- how do I make them stop?

    robertpoor say:

    Found the culprit with the help of Task Manager: EOSUPNPSV (part of the Canon EOS suite) was eating up 35% of the CPU and emitting all the packets.  I don't know what caused EOSUPNPSV to go haywire in the first place, but I unceremoniously uninstalled the app and everything is happy.
