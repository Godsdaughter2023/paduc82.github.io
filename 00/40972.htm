a. user say:

Is it normal for access to my camera to be requested every time that I try to go certain websites?

Yes, that's very normal, unfortunately.

All the major web browsers give you the choice of 1) allowing all websites to have access to your webcam; or 2) allowing no websites to have access to your webcam (which is recommended); and 3) to select specific websites that you allow and don't allow to have access to your webcam.

I personally allow no websites to have access to my webcam, except for specific websites for which I make exceptions.

In addition, to deal with malicious websites that don't play by the rules, I have a webcam cover. That's something everyone with a webcam should have.

davem121 say:
Hi Paou

Windows 10 contains a Telemetry service that reports back to Microsoft on hardware usage/errors etc.

Device Census is part of that Telemetry Framework and the process is only gathering data on your usage of the webcam

If you want restrict this access to your webcam, go to Settings - Privacy - Camera and uncheck the option 'Let apps use my camera hardware'

Or to specifically restrict this process scroll down the "Choose apps that can use my camera' list and uncheck "Feedback' hub', this will limit the data being sent back to MS Community and should free up your Webcam . . .

joegrodriguez9614 say:

I wish I could say this helped but it did nothing to resolve the issue.

For any Future Installers of SSD on a Home PC with Windows 10. When you install the SSD with SATA Cable to the Mother Board your current Windows 10 Operating system should be on SATA3_0.

When you Migrate you Windows 10 it should be on "C" Drive. After the Migration the Windows 10 Operating System will be on another Drive "D, E, F, G, ...L, M, P" and SATA3_1 or SATA3_2 or higher, but most important the SATA Port is not the boot drive port.

When I did the Migration my Windows 10 on SSD Drive was "M" Drive and SATA3_3.

You can not change or delete "C" Drive, to rename the New Operating System.

All I had to do was change the SATA Cables and Move the SSD Drive to SATA3_0 and move my old Operating HHD Drive SATA Cable to SATA3_3 and Everything is working and the Operating system Windows 10 is now in "C" Drive.
