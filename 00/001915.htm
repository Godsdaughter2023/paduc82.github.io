bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
Not "slow" but maybe 'uninformed' :-)

The term leading [pronounced 'ledding' as opposed to 'leeding'] is a typesetting term which refers to what has been dumbed-down for typewriting (and carried forward in word processing) as "line spacing'. It defines the spacing of lines of text based on a percentage of the font size rather than as a number of lines.

For most purposes, what many people refer to as "single spaced" is based on the standard leading spec of 120% of the font size. That's why there's more space between lines of 18 pt type than there is between lines of 12 pt type even though both paragraphs are set to "single"... 120% of 18 is greater than 120% of 12 to prevent conflict between the ascenders & descenders of adjacent lines of type.
