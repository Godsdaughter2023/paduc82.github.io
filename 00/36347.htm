Hi Selena,

If you run your own email server or rent one from another independent provider, setting up a POP3 email account can be a bit more complicated than a standard configuration. However, we're here to assist you to do it easily.

First, you need to access your web server (in your case, it's Dodo) to find out what their server addresses are. These settings are usually under "server settings" in your email management site.

  • For incoming email - they usually look something like "pop.emailserver.net"
  • For outgoing email - take down the address of anything that looks like it has the acronym “SMTP” in it. (Example: “smtpout.secureserver.net”)

To configure your account in the Windows 10 Mail app:

1. Launch the Windows Mail app.

2. Click on the Settings icon.

3. From the menu that will pop up, choose Accounts.

4. Select the option to “Add account” with the plus sign attached.

5. For POP3 base account, choose “Advanced Setup”.

6. Click on “Internet Email”.

7. After selecting Advanced Internet email, you will need to enter all the information you have acquired from your third-party email service.

8. Once you’ve chosen POP3 from the drop-down menu, enter the incoming POP server information and the outgoing SMTP address.

9. Once all are filled up, click Sign-in, and you’re good to go.

If the account was added successfully, you should see it appear on the right-hand pop up bar which lists all accounts tied to your Windows Mail ecosystem.

10. Then, verify if the account works by sending a test email.
