rick rothstein, mvp - excel say:

Here is an array-entered formula** that will work...

=AVERAGE(IF(B2:B30<>0,B2:B30))

** Commit the above formula using CTRL+SHIFT+ENTER and not just Enter by itself.

If you think you might edit that formula in the future and would forget about using CTRL+SHIFT+ENTER to re-enter it, then here is a little longer formula that works by just entering it normally (just use the ENTER key by itself to commit the formula)...

=SUMIF(B2:B30,"<>0")/SUMPRODUCT(--(B2:B30<>0))

NOTE: I do not have XL2010, so I developed these formulas in XL2003 where they work fine.

Both Mail and Outlook will send out rich text/html e-mails.
You can compose the e-mail in any HTML editor with a preview mode (including Word): put the whole thing together and copy from the preview. Then paste in Outlook and that's it. Downloading the images automatically is a different issue. It's security related.

The difference you are seeing with Mail is not about HTML/rich text messages. The e-mail is probably sent with the image as attachment, and not referenced to a server as it typically is for HTML e-mails. It clearly is a limitation in Outlook.

I believe that Microsoft may have removed this feature in the Skype for Windows 10 app.  But there are still ways to appear offline. 

Clicking on your account profile will bring up several settings where you have the option to toggle "Do not disturb" and "Do not share my presence" on or off.  I find these really useful when someone wants to call me when I'm working.  Using "Do not disturb" will make Skype not alert you about incoming calls, just remember to turn it off when you have finished.  "Do not share my presence" will make you appear offline to other contacts.

Another way to silence incoming calls in Windows 10 is to bring up the "Action Center" and use the "Quiet Hours" option at the bottom to make your computer not alert you about any notifications.

Or if you just want the old way back you could just open up the old Skype and use it like normal.
