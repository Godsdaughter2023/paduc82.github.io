qa guy commelec say:
Checkout the Timer Function --

This example uses the Timer function to pause the application. The example also uses DoEvents to yield to other processes during the pause.

Dim PauseTime, Start, Finish, TotalTime
If (MsgBox("Press Yes to pause for 5 seconds", _
        4)) = vbYes Then
    PauseTime = 5    ' Set duration.
    Start = Timer    ' Set start time.
    Do While Timer < Start + PauseTime
        DoEvents    ' Yield to other processes.
    Loop
    Finish = Timer    ' Set end time.
    TotalTime = Finish - Start  ' Calculate total time.
    MsgBox "Paused for " & TotalTime & " seconds"
Else
    End
End If

    I have a complicated formula I need to calculate and I'm not sure - Microsoft Community

    debrac28 say:

    I have a complicated formula I need to calculate and I'm not sure how to do it.

    This is what I need calculated:  I have a "manager" cell and 8 "partner" cells.  The 8 "partners" have an initial investment of $X.  I have a profit amount starting at year 1...the manager and partners split the profit 50/50 (50% goes to Manager, the other 50% gets split between the 8 partners) UNTIL the partners meet the break even point - their initial $X investment.  This can take multiple years.  But after the partners reach the break even point, the profit is split 80/20 - 80% to the manager and the remaining 20% gets split between the 8 partners.  How can I create a formula that will automatically calculate the 50/50 or 80/20 based on their accrued amounts and profit for the year?  If the break even point occurs in the middle of the year, I need it to split the profit for the year - the amount that gets each partner to the break even point is 50/50 but then the remainder is at 80/20.  Does this make sense to anyone?  Can it be done?  I know formulas pretty well but not nearly this well.

    Thanks.
    bernie deitrick, excel mvp 2000-2010 say:

    In row 2, starting in column A, put these headers (you should end up in column M):

    Year Profit 50/50 split 20/80 split Partner1 Partner2 Partner3 Partner4 Partner5 Partner6 Partner7 Partner8 Manager

    Then in row 1, above each partner, put in the amount of their initial investment (I have written these formulas to allow even or uneven investment amounts among the partners).

    Then in cell C3, enter the formula:

    =IF(SUM($B$3:$B3)<2*SUM($E$1:$L$1),0.5*$B3,IF(SUM($B$2:$B2)>2*SUM($E$1:$L$1),0,MIN(0.5*(2*SUM($E$1:$L$1)-SUM($B$2:$B2)),SUM($E$1:$L$1)-SUM($C$2:C2))))

    In cell D3, enter the formula

    =(B3-2*C3)*0.2

    In cell E3, enter the formula

    =E$1/SUM($E$1:$L$1)*($C3+$D3)

    and copy to F3:L3.

    Then in M3, enter the formula:

    =B3-SUM(E3:L3)

    Starting in row 3, in column A enter the year, column B enter the profit, and continue down as many rows as you need. Then copy all the formulas in row 3 down for as many years as you entered.

     

    Bernie

     

    jeeped say:
    This can be accomplished with a complication of basic functions nested together.

              

    I've disregarded the Manager profits to begin with since that is simply whatever is left over from the Partner Share × 8.
    The formula in E6 builds the Partner Share until that share aggregates to the Break Even Point in A3. It uses the profits in B6:B14, and the percentage found in D3. E6 contains,
    =IF(SUM($B$5:$B5)*$D$3>$A$3,0,MIN(SUM($B$6:$B6)*$D$3,$A$3)-SUM($E$5:$E5))
    The formula in F6 collects that portion of the profits based on the percentage in E3 where the initial investment in A3 has been paid off. The formula in F6 is,
    =IF(SUM($B$5:$B5)*$D$3>$A$3,$B6*$E$3,MAX(0,SUM($B$5:$B6)-($A$3/$D$3))*$E$3)
    Put together in D6 is,
    =IF(SUM($B$5:$B5)*$D$3>$A$3,0,MIN(SUM($B$6:$B6)*$D$3,$A$3)-SUM($E$5:$E5))+IF(SUM($B$5:$B5)*$D$3>$A$3,$B6*$E$3,MAX(0,SUM($B$5:$B6)-($A$3/$D$3))*$E$3)
    ... which can be filled down as required.

    Note that these formula rely upon ranges starting within their column labels so that the first row can start with a references zero value for previous entries.
