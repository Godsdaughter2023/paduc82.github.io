Hi,

 

Sorry for the late reply. We are currently experiencing high volume of inquiries. By the way, let's find out what are the possible options that we can do to resolve your issue.

 

You only got two options in deleting downloaded messages in Outlook.com. When you delete an email using other mail clients (not in Outlook.com website) you have an option to delete it permanently. You also have an option to move the emails to POP folder if you delete the emails using other mail clients.

 

For instance, if you use your Outlook.com email account in your mail client and you deleted some emails, you have an option to delete in permanently. You have to:

 

1. Go to www.outlook.com and sign in with your Microsoft account

2. Click on the Gear icon and choose More mail settings

3. Click on POP and deleting downloaded messages under Managing your account category

4. Select Do what my other program says — if it says to delete messages, then delete them

5. Click Save to confirm changes

 

Based on your description, it seems that you already configured your Outlook.com account to delete the messages permanently when using other mail clients.

 

The 5 day rule in storing emails in Deleted folder is only applicable when you delete your emails through Outlook.com website. When you delete your emails using your mail client, and you set your Outlook.com account to Do what my other program says — if it says to delete messages, then delete them, the deleted emails will not be directed to your deleted folder in Outlook.com. It will be deleted from the server permanently.

 

Let us know if you have any questions.
