rena yu msft say:

Hi Jacob,

There’s no such feature to automatically save all slide show record to video. The media files in the zip file > ppt > media, showing media 1,2,3… is in m4a format. You can change the format to mp3. They should be the sound only file. I don’t find video files in the zip files.

Refer to Record a slide show with narration and slide timings, when you finish recording your narration, a sound icon appears in the lower-right corner of each slide that has narration. This should be the sound file in zip folder. And the recorded slide show timings are automatically saved. Timings are shown in Slide Sorter view just beneath each slide. In this process, what you record is embedded in each slide, and the recording can be played back in Slide Show. A video file is not created by this recording process. However, if you want a video file, you can save your (whole) presentation as a video with a few extra steps.

There’s no such video settings to change to 1920x1080 or the position of the slide.

The export PPT as video only supports to save your whole original presentation as a video, no custom show. However, you can go to slide show > set up slide show > show slides > custom show to set the custom show, then go to recording > save as show to save it as a .ppsx file. This file will play the custom slide show automatically when you open it. But it is not the video format file.

Each slide show is always replaced when you re-record the same slide. If you re-record your narration (including audio and ink), PowerPoint erases your previously recorded narration (including audio and ink) before you start recording again on the same slide. The way you use to export multiple times is the workaround for that.
