trekdozer say:
I bought a Toshiba NB505 to replace my old NB205 and have trouble with the limited features of windows 7 starter; can I use the XP license I bought with my old netbook to install XP on my new one?


Sorry, no - the XP license that came with your old computer is OEM and can not be moved to another computer.


Can You Move Windows 7 To A New Computer 

If it's a retail Full or Upgrade license - yes. You can move it to a different computer as long as it's only installed on one computer at a time (and if it's a Windows 7 Upgrade version the new computer must have it's own qualifying XP/Vista/7 license). The previous Windows 7 installation on the old computer must be formatted/deleted. You might have to call Microsoft and explain what happened to complete the activation. Activating it on the second computer will automatically in effect "deactivate" the license for the first computer. The key will work with both 32 and 64 bit, but only one can be installed at a time.

If it's an OEM license - no. OEM licenses, including Windows preinstalled on a computer before purchase and Windows bought separately, are tied to the first computer they are installed on and can not be transferred to a different computer. To install Windows on a different computer you will need to buy another copy.



Difference Between a Full/﻿Upgrade/Retail License and an OEM/System Builder License.

OEM licenses of Windows:

1) Are bound to the first computer installed on and can't be moved to a different computer.

2) Can not do an in place upgrade from older versions of Windows, only a clean/fresh install is possible.

3) Offer no free support from Microsoft.





If you want more features you can buy an Anytime Upgrade to a higher version of Windows 7:

Buying Anytime Upgrade

Type Anytime Upgrade in the Search programs and files box in the Start menu and click on the Windows Anytime Upgrade icon. From there, you can buy an Anytime Upgrade to Windows 7 Home Premium/Professional/Ultimate. Then you can enter your Anytime Upgrade product key and perform a simple upgrade to Windows 7 Home Premium/Professional/Ultimate. Online Anytime Upgrade is not available in all countries. All programs and files will stay on the computer, no disk is needed and it takes about 10 minutes.

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows7/products/windows-anytime-upgrade

Basically you can think of the different resolutions like this.

720p - 1080p - 4K

So 4K is better than 1080p and 1080p is better than 720p.  But, at the moment there isn't any console on the market that displays a true 4k screen, all that happens is that the TV will "upscale" the image to 4K (but you don't have to worry about that).

At the moment, the Xbox outputs at a maximum of 1080p, which still looks good but by trying different cables you wont get better images or anything, any bogstandered HDMI cable will work just fine.

The best thing you can do with your TV is actually set the display up properly, IE change the brightness levels etc.  Usually TV manufacturers just set them up to the highest levels and that's it, but with a little tweaking you can get the picture to look much better.

To do this you can either just try and set it yourself (a bit of trial and error) or you can google your TV model and calibration settings (IE if your TV model is UE55JU6800, google "UE55JU6800 calibration settings" and you get a site called AVFORUMS, they always know whats best)

Also regarding on if the Xbox One is good or not, both the PS4 and Xbox One are fairly similar, the PS4 is slightly more powerful, but the only way you notice is if you actually pause the game, and study the screen in fine detail (like Digital Foundry do)  Personally I prefer the Xbox One, but that's down to me not liking the PS4 controller.

I installed Windows 7 HP 64 on a system with an motherboard and Opteron 180 processor on a second HD; XP is installed on the original HD and I can dual-boot. Win 7 boots fine, however, the sound card I have installed in a PCI slot and the video card installed in the AGP slot are not recognized by Win 7.

It's not a driver issue; neither device shows up as being in existence at all in the Win 7 device manager. Both devices are operating fine when XP is booted on the same system.

I read elsewhere that the A8V-X southbridge (VIA VT8251) driver for the Win 7 RC had similar problems but that it should be fixed in the final release of Win 7, which is what I purchased and installed. Obviously this has not been the case.

I have installed all available Windows updates and latest available BIOS update. I can supply screen shots of the XP and Win 7 device managers if there is a way to do that.

steve-isns say: OK, after digging around some more, I found on the VIA forum a workaround for the PCI problem.

http://www.viaarena.com/forums/archive/index.php?t-33197.html 

The link lays out the whole story on the errant southbridge and ASUS' previous attempts to fix the problem. The fix is to open a command prompt with administrator privileges and enter:

bcdedit /set CONFIGACCESSPOLICY DISALLOWMMCONFIG

This actually worked! After reboot, my sound card showed up in the device manager and I was able to install drivers and get it working.

The AGP video card remained a problem. Further digging revealed, if I am reading correctly, that Win 7 doesn't support AGP at all. I found ATI's latest AGP hotfix package, which at least has the card showing up in the device manager; however, the drivers are not working properly just yet. This I can deal with, at least for now. The hotfix is found at:

http://support.amd.com/us/kbarticles/Pages/CatalystAGPHotfix.aspx

Thanks again for your help, MadGoat, and I hope this helps someone else in the same situation.

Your screen capture idea is the answer.

Player to make your screen caps and then import
them into Movie maker.

VLC Media Player
http://www.videolan.org/

To setup for your screen captures...open VLC and
go to...Tools / Preferences / Video button / Video
Snapshots section.

In the directory field...Browse to and select a folder
to save your captures in. (for example...you could
create a new folder on your desktop and select it)

At...Prefix...check the box...Sequential numbering.

At...Format...choose JPEG or PNG.

Finally.....left click the 'Save' button.

Now...Play the video and pause by pressing your
Spacebar when you see a screen you wish to capture.

To capture the screen...type...  Shift+S

Press spacebar to advance to the next photo...
ect...etc...
