daniel g. grau say:
You'll see how to do it better if you enable, in the menu bar, Table > Gridlines to put the table cells in evidence (it's a toggle control).

Notice that you have a wide left column, a thin separator (no text) column and a short right column. But you are typing most of the text on the leftmost cell, and it's growing downwards and now spans two pages. You need to use a new table row, with only one cell.

  1. Place your cursor in the table
  2. Select Table > Insert > Rows below (and 3-cell empty row will be added)
  3. Click and drag across the three empty cells to select them
  4. Select Table > Merge Cells
  5. Cut the excess text from the first cell that is spilling over into page 2
  6. Paste it into the new row
If you end up with a blank line at the top of page 2, it's caused by an extra Carriage Return. Place your cursor there and type Backspace to get rid of it.

daniel g. grau say:
I see the problem. Click on the first title after that (the one that originally said "Overview"), select Format > Paragraph, click on the second tab (Line and Page Breaks) and cancel Keep with next.

It's curious that those paragraph attributes don't work at all within cells, except for Keep with next and Page break before when applied to the first paragraph of the leftmost cell in a row.

Why did it happen? When the cell got too long, Word tried to keep the "Overview" title on the same page as the last paragraph in the document (which, by the way, cannot be deleted).
