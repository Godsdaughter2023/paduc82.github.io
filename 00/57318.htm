doug30087 say:

This issue has been resolved.

Kaspersky Small Office Suite had been blocking the adapter from showing up in the Windows 10 Update.

Here is the FIX

Reason:
This problem is caused by Self-Defense function of Kaspersky which is enabled by default. It forbids you to use the wireless adapter normally and this occurs not only on our TP-LINK adapters but also on other brands’ adapters.
 
Solution:
Step 1
Double click the Kaspersky icon on the system tray to open its UI (User Interface). Then click Settings on the top right corner.
 
Step 2

Click Options on the left side, uncheck Enable Self-Defense, and click OK.

Step 3
Reinstall /Update driver of your wireless adapter.
For how to install/update driver manually, please refer to:
Your software OS help file
After you installed the adapter successfully, you can try to find your wireless network and enjoy it.
 
Step 4

Go back to the Kaspersky settings and enable Self-Defense again, it will not block your adapter any more.
