alex t jr say: No you dont have to reinstall Windows to free up more space.  No you cant do it without losing the programs you mentioned.

No you dont necessarily lose all information when changing partitions.  You do when you format them.  You can use a 3rd party tool, such as GParted that is built in with Ubuntu, and be able to shrink your partition without losing anything.

Yes SSD's do have a limited lifetime.  That will never change as all flash based media is this way. Even your flash drive and SD Cards you own will all one day succumb to the same fate as your SSD.  That is the flaw of flash based media over a mechanical drive.

dominicp say: You may be able to shrink your SSD if you defrag it, so that there are no files in the last portion. Otherwise you will need a third party partition manager.

Your Linux distribution could be installed on a VHD on the SSD. For some, there are specific tools to install on a VHD within Windows.

You would get best performance if you left frequently accessed documents and email files on the SSD. There are limited writes to SSD data cells but the firmware manages the data cells so you should not worry about it. Just accept that eventually the SSD will wear out.

Use the HDD for high volume data - music, videos, photos and the like.

=============================================
If you are using Movie Maker 6 and not Live Movie Maker, the following
tutorial originally composed for XP Movie Maker should get you started

In timeline view...drag a second copy of the video to the Audio/Music
track and drag it left or right to align it to the Audio track.
 
Then right click/mute the Audio track.

Now you can split the video anywhere you wish...Ctrl+L...and remove
a section...then insert a Photo and give it the same duration as the
segment you removed.

Here's another method:

Split your video clip on each side of the area where the stills will be.
Now...drag the cut portion to the Audio/Music track. BTW...If you wish
to add a 5 second still...remove 5 seconds of the video clip. (info below
on splitting if you need it)

Drag your still shots to the time line and drop them in the location where
you removed the video.

The Audio clip on the Audio/Music track can be dragged right or left to
adjust it's position.

More info on splitting....

"Splitting A Clip In MM2.1"

Drag the video/audio clip to the Timeline.

Play the video/audio by clicking the "Play" button under the view screen.

You will see the Playback Indicator (vertical line) advancing across the
timeline.

Click the "Pause" button at the point where you wish to trim and type...
Ctrl+L or click the "Split" button at the bottom of the view screen.
(you can also make adjustments by dragging the Playback Indicator)

Make as many splits as you want and remove the unwanted clips by right
click / delete.

    We are aware of an issue where activity reports will stop being sent, and settings changes may not take effect, if the child's account becomes unverified on their device. This can happen after a few months of usage, if they change their password online, or when the terms of service are updated.

     

    If you notice activity reports are not coming from your child's device, have them verify their account in the Settings app, under Accounts.

     

    We are actively working on ways to improve this situation.

     

    Thanks,

    Daniel
