Hi Helaina,

For software related issue, the first thing you need to perform is a soft reset. To perform a soft reset, press and hold the volume down and Power button until your phone vibrates and restarts. Note that you should not touch the device screen during this process (not even when the 'slide down to power off' prompt appears).

The soft reset is the same thing as pulling your battery (on devices where that is possible). This method will not remove anything at all from your phone and is completely safe to attempt in any problem situation.

If a soft reset didn't work, you will need to perform a hard reset. You can reset your phone under Settings: System > about > reset your phone. Make sure to back up all of your files before performing a hard reset. To back your files, go to System> Update and security > Backup >More options > Back up now.

The hard reset will bring your phone back to factory settings, the same state as when the device was first turned on. All apps, messages, media, personal settings, etc will be removed from the phone. If backup is turned on under Settings, you can restore your apps and settings after performing a hard reset (as long as you initialize the phone with the same Microsoft Account). Note that resetting your device will not remove any OS or firmware updates. You will remain on the same version of Windows Phone after the reset.

If performing a soft reset and hard reset didn't work, you will need to use the Windows Device Recovery Tool. Start by downloading and installing Windows Device Recovery Tool (for Windows 10, 8.x and 7).

When the installation has finished, start the application and the main screen will appear:

The procedure to restore your phone using Windows Device Recovery Tool is as follows:

  1. Connect the phone to your PC (using the USB cable that came with the phone).
  2. After a few seconds, the PC should find the phone and you'll see the Phone Selection screen. Click your phone in this screen to select it.
  3. After a few seconds more, the latest available firmware version for your device will be shown. Click the 'Install software'/'Reinstall software' button to flash your phone

Windows Device Recovery Tool will then download and install the latest firmware + OS available for your phone

Note! Your phone will be reset to factory status when using this tool.
