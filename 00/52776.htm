Some warning messages in the Event Viewer are excusable when the system starts up... as other services depend on each other to be running.  Continuous messages (even warnings) in the Event Viewer are annoying, so you would hope that you would only see the white "i" informational type messages when things are running smoothly.   No event should ever defy reasonable explanation.

A question about Recovery D drive - Microsoft Community

maws_paws say:

A question about Recovery D drive

Is D drive a different circuit board? Or is C and D the same board? My daughter had her hard drive replaced and she now doesnt have a D drive.

a. user say:


The D: Recovery Drive/Partition is Imaged by the Computer Manufacturer onto the original Hard Drive during the manufacture of your Computer. 

The Recovery Partition was put there to put your Computer back to Factory Settings(how you bought it), in case of System crash.

Once the original Hard Drive is replaced, you can not re-image that D: Recovery Partition back onto the new Hard Drive.

Your original Hard Drive was divided into 2 Partitions by the Manufacturer: One for the Vista Operating System, and the D: for the System Recovery Files.

You were also supposed to make Recovery Disk/s from that D: Recovery Partition.

If you did not make Recovery disks from it, when it was up and running:

Contact your Computer Manufacturer and ask them to send you a set of Vista Recovery disk/s, in case of System crash or another Hard Drive failure..

 

They normally do that for a small $ cost.
