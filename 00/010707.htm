The three buttons: Back, Start, and Search - Microsoft Community

faq-en2 say:

The three buttons: Back, Start, and Search

What do the three buttons on my phone do? What do the three buttons on my phone do?
*kent* say:

All Windows Phones have three buttons across the bottom: Back, Start, and Search.

 

Back

The Back button, as you might expect, takes you back one screen from where you are. Your phone remembers all the apps and websites you've visited since the last time your screen was locked, and will take you back one page each time you press Back, until you get to the Start screen.

 

The one exception is when you're in Internet Explorer Mobile. If you're browsing the web and press Back, you'll return to the previous webpage you visited, rather than to the previous app. But once you're out of it, Back goes back to taking you, well, back again.

 

Start and Speech

With the Start button, you get a two-for-one deal. Any time you press Start, you go back to Start, no matter where you are or what you're doing on your phone (as long as it isn't locked).

 

If you press and hold the Start button, though, you'll activate Speech. To learn more about using Speech, see Use Speech on my phone. And to learn more about what you can do on Start, see Everything goes back to Start.

 

Search

You can use the Search button to find what you need, anywhere on the phone. Need to find something on the web? Press Search from Start, or in Internet Explorer. Looking for a contact? Press Search from the People hub. Trying to find a particular app? Press Search in Marketplace.
