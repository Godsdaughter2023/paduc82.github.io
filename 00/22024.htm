  It is broadcast by Bluetooth, not WiFi.  Other PC's that have not previously provided the password can not see this when your internet sharing is turned off. With internet sharing on the phone turned off and your laptop has previously connected to the phones internet sharing they will see this WiFi name. If you select this source on the laptop the phone automatically turns on internet sharing to allow the connection.

Because you changed the internet sharing password on the phone it was seen as a new connection by the PC that had not been setup with the new password so the WiFi name was not visible.  Turn on internet sharing and connect the laptop with the new password, then disconnect the laptop. In a few minutes the phone internet sharing will turn off. Then you will see the WiFi from the phone as available again on the PC.

This feature was added to the OS with the OS update that came with Cyan.

This is a Windows 8.1 feature, it syncs your devices with your Microsoft Account, and if they ever pair on bluetooth, it will appear as a wireless network, for seamless internet sharing instead of going through setting up a hotspot.

When you insert a mergefield that begins with a numeral into a mail merge main document, Word adds M_ at the beginning of that field.   That being just one of many changes that are made on the fly my Merge Tools utilities cannot use such fields.  In your case, to be able to make use of my Merge Tools utilities, the easiest thing to do would be to add the M_ to the field name in the data source.

As for your original issue of Word crashing (it's probably not actually crashing, but just says that it is not responding) that is probably due to the size of the task as you are asking it to create a document of nearly 2,000 pages and then, the data source being a 22MB Excel workbook doesn't help either.
