WARNING! Don't let N85 battery run down without Nokia official charger handy!

I purchased the N85 2 weeks ago, and came across a problem related to USB charging that I would like to share.

Smart huh? Only one cable, and 3 possible power sources. Car, Mains or Laptop. And I can charge my other USB and Mini-USB devices with the same setup.

WRONG!

Credit where it's due, the battery lasted for maybe 8 hours of continuous use over 2 days, but after 1.5 hours on the motorway with Nokia Maps, GPS, and streaming MP3's to the car stereo (works great btw!) the battery died.

Then it was nearly IMPOSSIBLE to charge. I've looked into this and found out that the sync cable will only provide a slow/trickle charge: this is part of the USB specification. To make the sync cable provide a fast charge, you need to short a couple of pins together. This is how a mains or car charger works.

But even if this is the case, there appears to be a firmware related issue that means you are really screwed if you end up with only a sync cable and a dead battery.

The N85 would start to charge from empty (Battery on one bar, no charging animation) until there was enough power for the phone to boot up, then once the phone had successfully booted, there was not enough power left to continue the charging cycle, and it would shut down again. This loop appeared to continue indefinitely when connected via the mains or car adapters (and yes they DO have the capability to supply a high enough fast charge current).

Finally after dozens of "trickle charge --> boot --> drain --> trickle charge" cycles connected to a laptop, there was enough power to complete the boot cycle, and the battery icon changed from 'one bar solid' to the charging animation. Once this had happened, the phone successfully slow-charged over 5 or 6 hours.

So be warned, don't travel without your official Nokia car or Mains charger, or if you do, make sure you never run the battery out completely.

Of course it would be a great firmware improvement to wait a bit longer before running a boot cycle after the battery has discharged completely, if anyone from Nokia is reading :-)

suzanne s. barnhill say:

See http://wordfaqs.mvps.org/FrontMatterRibbon.htm.

If your Introduction starts on page iii and the Foreword starts on page iv, you cannot have a blank page between them. Nor can you have page iii on an even page and iv on an odd page. Word is very literal-minded about the meaning of "even" and "odd."

Assuming that your title page is i and page ii is blank, those two pages should be Section 1, showing no page numbers. Section 2 should start with an Odd Page break so that the Introduction is page iii and the Foreword is iv. The header or footer (wherever the page number is) must be unlinked from Section 1. Personally, I would start both Introduction and Foreword on an odd page, but that's up to you. You will have another section break (Odd Page) at the end of the front matter so that you can start the main document body on page 1.

FWIW, the KB article "Operating parameter limitations and specifications in Word" makes no mention of a limit to the number of merge fields. There is a limit of 10,000 names displayed in the recipients list, but the limit for fields generally is 2,147,483,647.

Right now, when mail routes to mymts.net, the most likely situation is that domain name records direct that mail to the Outlook.com servers.  If MTS remains in service, they probably won't cancel that routing, since it would affect all of their customers.  This would mean your mail would still get routed to your Outlook.com mailbox.

AT&T uses a third party mail service (Yahoo) for their mail, but if you stop subscribing to AT&T Internet service, your AT&T address will remain active and you can retain the att.net mail address.  In fact, you can sign up for a free att.net email address without using AT&T Internet services.  Perhaps MTS uses the same approach.  Why not call them and ask?

 

Another approach would be to create an independent email account on one of the free mail services and keep that as yor main mailbox.  Forward your mymts.net there.  Then if the address goes away, you will still have your data and it would be independent of any ISP you choose.
