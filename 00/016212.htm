Hello Brian:

Thank you for posting to the forum regarding the issues you are having with your music collection management.  I can see your concern with the duplicates you are finding in your collection.  I will do my best to answer the questions you have posed and to give you some suggestions on how to clean this up.  

You will find information related to your four questions below.

  1. The folders you found are intended to manage your collection based on various types of content added to the computer.   Music/Subscription - this folder is where content downloaded with a Music Pass through the Zune software would be stored.  Music/Xbox Music/Purchases - this is the folder where Music purchased via the Xbox Music application would be stored.  Music/Xbox Music/Subscription Cache - would be the folder that contains content you have downloaded with your Xbox Music Pass through the Xbox Music application.  The Xbox Music/Music Pass folder is one that I have not been able to pinpoint the significance of.  This should be where Music Pass downloads are stored but from my work with the application and the Pass, all downloads are stored in the subscription cache folder.

  3. When it comes to correcting the behavior you have described here there are a couple of options.  The safest but most time consuming way to do this would be to locate the duplicate files and delete the duplicates via file explorer.   Another option you can pursue is a complete rebuild of your collection.  If you have access to a USB flash drive, a smartphone or a MP3 player you can copy your MP3 collection here.  This will ensure you have a back up of your files so they will not be lost.  Once you do this, you can delete all the contents of the four folders you see in your Music library folder.  Once this is done you can restore the content you downloaded via the Zune software by going to the software and clicking on settings > account > SUBSCRIPTION history > restore all.  Any content added via the Xbox Music application would still be part of your cloud collection and would still be showing in your collection.  From here you can simply download the tracks back to the computer.  Finally, you can copy the contents you moved to your device back to your computer so that they are seen by Xbox Music.

The following sums the range from the cell above the SUM function to cell A2. (I used A2 because row 1 usually contains column headers but you could use A1 if you want)

=SUM(INDIRECT(ADDRESS(ROW()-1,COLUMN())):A2)
