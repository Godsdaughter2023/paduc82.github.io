That's good - that indicates you are booting in Safe Mode, right?  I was not getting the picture until you posted up that stuff.  Now it makes a little more sense.

It would take me a long time to relate what I have read about this.

The Kmxstart.sys is part of several files that get loaded by the Computer Associates software package.  You have that - I am guessing...

There is a lot of talk about this problem in their message forum - it is not new.  

The problem may have been fixed in some later version of the CA software, but it appears (conveniently) that the version with the problem may not be supported any more.  Delightful!

It could also be a conflict with some recent updates to XP or your CA software.  Does any of that sound familiar?

Your system is probably not hanging on Kmxstart.sys as it appears, but the file that comes after it (there are several more CA oriented files).

I think you may have a Computer Associates software problem, not an XP problem.

If your Internet Service Provider is Road Runner (is it?), they have free support and have some knowledge of this problem and may be able to help you.  They know about it and maybe have a fix over the phone or something.  It is not a new problem.

If you bought the CA stuff yourself, you can try their technical support ,but I could not find the magic bullet instant solution in all their try this, try that, maybe, might be, could be, stuff.  Here is the WWW to their support page:

http://www.ca.com/us/support.aspx

You can call them or open a thread in their forum.  They know about this problem.

Here is something else I will share about about chkdsk /r and Safe Mode...

If you are running normally and schedule a chkdsk /r for some disk problem and answer Y to the question to run on the next reboot and then boot your system in Safe Mode, it will appear to "hang" on mup.sys until the chkdsk /r completes.  This could take a long, long, long time (several hours) depending on how big your HDD is, what it has on it and what chkdsk /r finds to do.  Keep an eye on your HDD light if you have one and you can see it is doing "something" - it is not hung.  You need to be sure you wait "long enough" however long that is.  Eventually, it will complete and put the results in the Event Viewer log like it is supposed to.  

When you do that, you might think your system is broken or stuck on mup.sys (or in your case Kxmsart.sys), but it really is running chkdsk /r because you told it to do that the next time the system boots (it is just doing what you said).  If you don't let it complete (press the reset button), it will just do it again.

If I had your problem, I would boot into the XP Recovery Console (make a CD if you don't have one) and run chkdsk /r from there.  Then you can at least see what it is doing and watch it complete (or fail), and you can at least you can watch it run and see what it is doing.  Maybe your HDD is bad, but I would not think that right away unless I saw it myself.

Using Recovery Console, you can also disable/prevent the CA stuff from loading at all by disabling the services (I think there are 6 or 7 parts) using the "Disable" command and that may get you going long enough to uninstall or update your CA if you are compelled to keep it.

There would be a way to also, using Recovery Console to stop the scheduled chkdsk /r completely and that may let you get past the stuck point in order to fix your CA issue (I would just dump CA all together).  When the reboot comes to the chkdsk /r part, it will fail and hopefully just move on past it. 

I don't know what the exact command to disable the CA stuff is (the Road Runner people might know), and I am not going to install some old version of the CA stuff to figure it out (sorry).  I hate CA software and many shops that used to use it all the time no longer put it on their systems going out the door (there is better stuff nowadays).

If I had your system, I think I would be able to get it running, but since I do not have the problem, cannot recreate it to figure it out and I have never had to fix it so I have no copy/paste ideas for this problem.  It would be a fun project though.

Sorry for rambling, most of my stuff is copy/paste but I have not seen this one before and maybe what I found out will inspire you to figure it out - I think you can fix it.
