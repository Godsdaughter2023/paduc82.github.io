slance310 say:

Hi,

Just a couple guesses as to why some do it and some don't...

Some machines are most likely using the newer UEFI Bios which uses Windows Boot Manager on a separate EFI Partition to boot... It can get screwed up much more easily than the Legacy Bios machines...

My experience with Startup Repair and the EFI Partition is that it doesn't work well...

For one thing, the Startup Repair usually ends up looking at the wrong partition...

Another possible issue is a feature in Win 8 and above called Fast Startup...

This is kind of a hybrid Hibernation\ Power Off feature that does not necessarily "Close" HDDs properly before shutting down... This speeds things up a lot when powered back on...

The problem is that if you remove the drive under this circumstance, it can possibly become corrupted... especially if attached to another machine....

This may not be functioning on some of the machines, so no problem... With others it is active and there are issues...

The ideal solution would be to turn it off before removing the HDD...

This feature only works when you do a SHUTDOWN and then Boot.

It doesn't effect a RESTART.

To turn it off or on...

Go to Control panel...Power Options, and select Choose What the Power Buttons Do on the left.

Then select Change Settings That Are Currently Unavailable near the top center of screen...

Lower down on the window, uncheck Fast Startup.

If certain errors occur that cause a reboot, the result can be that Startup Repair will automatically run to find and fix the problem that caused the reboot. Since you were not there to see what happened, it could be anything, such as a power outage or other malfunction.

What is required to connect to Xbox LIVE using a wireless connection?

A wireless network connection lets you connect your Xbox 360 console to Xbox LIVE without using network cables. This allows you the freedom to move your console further away from your network hardware (your router, gateway, or modem).

Here is what you need:

    • A high-speed Internet connection such as cable or DSL

      Note Satellite and 3G or 4G mobile connections are not recommended because you are likely to experience slow performance or lag.

  • A router, gateway, or wireless access point
  • An Xbox 360 Wireless Networking Adapter (original Xbox 360 console only)
Connect a wireless adapter (original Xbox 360 console only)

If you have an original Xbox 360 console, you need to add an Xbox 360 Wireless Networking Adapter to your console.

Note If you have an Xbox 360 S console (which has built-in Wi-Fi), skip to the next section.

Connect the wireless networking adapter:

1. Snap the plastic tabs on the adapter into the slots on the back of your console.
2. Plug the USB cable into the USB port.
3. Flip up the antennae.

4. If a network cable is plugged into the back of your console, unplug it.

5. Check the light on the wireless adapter. If the light is green, you are ready for the next step. 

No green light? See Troubleshoot wireless networking adapter.

Have a Kinect sensor? See Extension cables and USB hubs with the Kinect sensor.

Configure and test your Xbox 360 wireless Internet connection

Next, you need to configure your Xbox wireless settings and test your Xbox LIVE connection.

Configure your wireless settings
  1. Press the Guide button on your controller, go to Settings, and then select System Settings.
  2. Select Network Settings.
  3. Select the name of your wireless network.

    Note If you do not see your wireless network, select Advanced Options. Then, select Specify Unlisted Network, and enter your wireless network name. Next, you are prompted to choose the security type.

  4. Enter your wireless network password. How to find your wireless network password.
  5. Select Yes if you are prompted to update your console software.
Can’t connect?

If you get a connection error, try using our Xbox LIVE Connection Troubleshooting Assistant to fix the problem. If your wireless network is not listed in Step 3 above, or if your network has a low signal,see Improve Xbox LIVE speed
