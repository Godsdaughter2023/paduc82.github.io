No, but you can disable it with a /q at the commandline.

1. Create a new shortcut on the desktop for Word directly from
    "C:\Program Files\Microsoft Office\Office14\WINWORD.EXE"
2. Modify the target in the shortcut, add a " /q" (without the ""):
    "C:\Program Files\Microsoft Office\Office14\WINWORD.EXE" /q

This disables the splash screen if word is opened from the desktop.

If you want to disabled it too if you/she double click on .DOC/.DOCX/.DOCM/... files you have to modify the open command in the filetype section in the windows explorer in the same way as in the shortcut.

The open command should now look like this
    "C:\Program Files\Microsoft Office\Office14\WINWORD.EXE" /n "%1"

change it to
    "C:\Program Files\Microsoft Office\Office14\WINWORD.EXE" /q /n "%1"

Take a closer look at the ruler - the ruler goes to zero at the left and top margins of the page, and then goes negative to the left and above that point.  To the left of that, the ruler goes -1, -2, etc.  Above that, the ruler goes -1, etc.  Those represent your margins.  So you might be seeing:

7.25 to the right of the margin

total = 8.5 inches.
