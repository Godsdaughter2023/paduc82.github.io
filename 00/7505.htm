I'm very sorry to hear that your important email draft is gone and I understand that feeling. Unfortunately, "Discard" is the term used by Outlook for delete. Once a draft is discarded, it is permanently deleted and cannot be recovered.

Your Drafts folder is your Unsent folder. Once you use the "Save Draft" feature once, the email will remain in the Draft folder until it is Sent or is Discarded. Changes you make to a draft message are frequently auto-saved, but it is always best that you click on Save Draft to ensure that no work is lost.

If you are making changes to an email that you aren't sure you want to keep, I would recommend copying and pasting the content of your email into a second draft email so you can discard it and retain the original if you dislike your changes, as there is not a way to revert to a previously saved version of your draft. You can use ctrl + z or command + z to undo your most recent text changes, but once a draft is saved or discarded, you cannot undo any changes.

The image files, themselves, do not contain 'colors' - They contain color values. Those values are interpreted by each of the devices involved when the image is displayed or printed.
