arminatorx say:

If you just changed your phone number to a new number, the security code is being sent to the OLD number. 

This is, so that hackers can't simply add their own phone number, and take control over your account. 

If you don't have access to your old number anymore, you'll have to wait until next month, when your new number becomes an active security info. 

While you're at it, in order to prevent such long waiting times in the future, make sure you keep your security info up to date, and have multiple security measures as a fallback. 

I recommend to have a secondary email, a mobile phone, and if applicable also a landline phone number added to your account. 

And most importantly: Enable Two Step Verification for your account, and set up the free Authenticator App on your phone. With that, a hacker can't get into your account, even if they get hold of your password, as the Live service needs a code confirmation that only your phone can provide.
