bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

You seem to have the impression that you'll have to do 118 print jobs - That's not how it works :-). You're simply merging to a document rather than merging directly to a printer. The Edit Individual Documents option creates only 1 document. That document is comprised of 1 page for each of the 59 envelopes or a single document containing 1 copy of the letter for each of the 59 recipients. You'll need to print each of those 2 documents once... IOW, you will not have to do 59x2 print jobs, just 1 print job to print 59 envelopes & 1 print job to print 59 copies of the letter.

The name of the feature may be a bit deceptive: Among other uses it merely gives the opportunity to personalize the individual letters or envelopes or labels that are contained the finished document, but that single document comprises copies for all recipients.

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

This is reminiscent of something reported by a few users several releases back... Make certain that your installation of Office 2016 is fully updated. It should be at least version 15.30 as of earlier this month.

Additionally, confirm that your installation of OS X is fully updated.

Equally important, visit the support site for your printer to determine whether you have the most current driver software for your model. It may be necessary to remove the printer, then install it anew, especially if you're running Sierra.

As an interim alternative, use the Finish & Merge option to Edit Individual Records. That merely generates a new document which you then should be able to print. That document need not be saved unless you want an archival copy of the finished merge.

Windows 10 is “more” aggressive in power management, and it has a tendency to “turn off” the network card when it not being used, or it sees no activity.

 

Right click on the network connection in the lower right tray. Choose “Open Network and Internet settings”.

 

From that choose change adaptor settings.

 

Right click on the network adaptor you are using and choose properties. Then from that dialog choose “configure”

 

From that, choose power management.

 

Un-check the box that says:

 

Allow the computer to turn off the device to save power.

 

So windows 10 tends to do “a lot” to try and save power on a laptop.

 

Turing off the above option should help things a lot.
