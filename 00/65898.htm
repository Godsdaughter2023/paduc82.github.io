CAB files are like ZIP files which means they are compressed so they take up less room on the
hard drive. CAB files are known as Cabinet files. Vista and Windows 7 has internal support for
CAB files. To Open a CAB files double click on it and it will open in Windows Explorer similar to

Unticking a service under msconfig causes the respective registry entry to be moved into a holding area, thus "blowing" the service away while keeping it visible for msconfig. Restoring the tick mark will move the registry entry back into the area where all other services dwell, thus causing it to be loaded at the next reboot.
