The WhereCondition syntax should be more like:

  . . . , "ViewCurrentStock.BookedInDate < Date() - 90"

or

  . . . , "ViewCurrentStock.BookedInDate < " & Format(Date - 90, "\#yyyy-m-d\#")

If you already tag your contact as a safe sender and still his email is going to junk mail, I suggest that you set your Junk email filtering level to Exclusive.

 

If you set your filter to this, only messages from your contacts and safe sender’s lists will be allowed to go to your Inbox, the rest will go to your Junk Folder. Here are the steps to setup your junk e-mail filter in Hotmail:

 

1. Sign in to https://mail.live.com using your Hotmail account.
2. In the upper-right corner of your Inbox, click Options, and then click More options.
3. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.
4. Under Filters and reporting, select the options that you want, and then click Save.

 

Note:

  • You should occasionally check your junk email folder to make sure that good messages are not sent there by mistake.
  • Your Junk folder is regularly cleaned up. So you need not to delete those emails manually.
