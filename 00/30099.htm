And this reminds me also that trying to find a description of my other monitor always seems problematic, e.g. msinfo32 is no help with that at all.  Control Panel's Hardware and Devices at least shows it as "Generic PnP Monitor".  FWIW the closest I have come so far for better details is with this PowerShell pipeline

Get-WMIObject -Class Win32_PnPEntity | where DeviceID -match "Monitor|Display" | ft Caption, DeviceID

E.g. using that shows me 3 matches, one of which could plausibly be my external monitor, but my guess is that you will only get matches for your laptop's screen and that will be with DeviceID which match "Display" only.

Also, I just remembered that one of the things that I did for my mother's machine is update the BIOS.  Checking your BIOS version is something that msinfo32 could help with or if you want extra detail use WMIC, e.g.

wmic.exe BIOS get /format:list

Note that that is also a way to find your machine SerialNumber.   ; )
