Burned DVD's won't play in home DVD players - Microsoft Community

jojobear2002 say:

Burned DVD's won't play in home DVD players

Why won't the DVD's that I burn play in any home DVD player? Do I need to format it first before I burn it and if I do need to format it, how do I do that?
cari [former ms-mvp] say:

Normally they do play but here are some tips for you...

Make sure the standalone DVD player has the right kind of media, some will ONLY play DVD-R and some will ONLY play DVD+R.  There's a website at www.videohelp.com which will tell you exactly what your standalone is compatible with.

Make sure you burn a VIDEO DVD and not a DATA DVD.  Standalone players do not cope well with data DVDs unless it's specified that they will play DIVX files. 

Change manufacturer of the media you are using, some players prefer one to another.  At that VideoHelp site, folks have written reviews of which disk manufacturers they have had succes with.

You only need to format DVD-RW and DVD+RW, and then only if you wish to erase the data already on the disk.  Many standalone DVDs do NOT like rewritable disks, whether they are + or -.  They prefer the write once type, ie without the W at the end.

You might want to try a third party DVD writer program such as Roxio, Nero, PowerDirector etc etc.

Make sure you are burning in the correct TV format for your country,  Most US DVD standalone players will NOT handle a PAL disk.  They will read ONLY NTSC.
