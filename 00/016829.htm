One Drive for Business is a App similar to Dropbox. We cal this a stand-alone product.

Microsoft Office365 products fall under 2 families, "Business" and "Enterprise"

If I have to change the dates to just years to make it easier I can. I have about 3k lines and do not want to have to go in manually to highlight any "trash" files so any help would be appreciated!

bernie deitrick say:

Select your data and sort by the Date Closed field, ascending. The oldest files will be at the top, and you can select and "trash" those files.

If you truly want to 'trash' the files, you could use a macro to find the files and delete them (if they are actually files stored somewhere and the logic to finding them is clear.)

If you want to just highlight the dates with CF. select from D2 to the end of column D, choose CF, select "New Rule", then choose the option "Use a formula to determine which cells to format" and use the formula

=D2<DATE(YEAR(NOW())-5,MONTH(NOW()),DAY(NOW()))
