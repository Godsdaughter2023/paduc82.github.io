judith_c. say:

Hi Kenny,

 

Welcome to Microsoft Community. I apologize for the delay in response. From what I understand, the emails that were moved from your archive folder to your Inbox folder in your AOL accounts doesn't sync to your Microsoft account. Am I correct?

 

If yes, you can use the Upload your email to Outlook.com feature we have in Outlook.com to move your emails from your AOL account. Live Mail then add your account using POP protocol. For correct POP and SMTP settings, I suggest that you contact your email service provider.

 

Once done, follow these steps below on how to upload emails to Outlook.com:

Reminder: If emails stored in Outlook.com is still in your AOL Inbox folder, it may duplicate the emails already stored in your Microsoft account when you perform this action.

 

1. Log in to https://outlook.com.

2. Click the cog icon .

3. Select More mail settings.

4. Under Managing your account, click Your email accounts.

5. Click Add send and receive account.

6. Under Upload an email account, click Upload your email to Outlook.com.

7. You will be prompted to install the needed add-on to perform the process.

8. Select the account that you want to upload the emails from.

9. Click Next.

10. If Windows Live Mail is still open, you will be prompted to close it before uploading the emails. Once done, click OK.

11. Click Upload.

12. You will be asked if you want to receive new emails from the POP account or if you only want to upload old emails from your POP account.

13. Click OK.

 

You will be notified if the emails were uploaded successfully or if the process failed. Once the upload process succeeded, you will receive an email confirmation that emails were uploaded to your Microsoft account.

 

To learn more about this feature please click the link below:

 

I can't move my email account to the cloud

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

I'm not really sure what your desired result is... It seems to me that if the 2 text boxes are linked they're linked for a reason - usually because the 2nd contains a continuation of what's in the 1st. Why you would want to break the link just because the distance between the text boxes changes seems to me to be counterproductive. If that's what you're trying to do, though, the simplest way I know of is to;

  1. Select & cut the text in the 2nd text box
  2. Paste the cut material into a new text box in the preferred location
  3. Delete the remaining empty text box (formerly the 2nd)

There is no provision for retaining the content in separate text boxes when breaking a link.
