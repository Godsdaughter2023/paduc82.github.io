How do I set up remote access on my system? - Microsoft Community

charlesg4tv say:

How do I set up remote access on my system?

how do iget a friend or security to remote access my cpu
kosh vorlon say:

Hi Charles,

 

First, I believe you want them to be able to use Remote Desktop (not really Remote Access which is a little different) to connect to your computer and then work on yours remotely on their own computer as if yours were in front of them (with some minor limitations).

 

To allow your system to receive remote Desktop connections (not all versions of Windows will - but most allow it), see: http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/windows_vista-networking/cant-remote-desktop-from-vista-home-premium/f6d87400-58a2-423c-86f3-5650a4e0b153.  This thread tells you how to do it in XP and Vista.  It's pretty much the same as Vista for W7.

 

Then the person who wants to connect to you will need to send you some things to install to work with the program they are using for remote desktop.  It's usually a matter of logging into a web page and following the instructions you get on the phone (they need to give you a code usually) and then they have access. 

 

Be VERY careful who you give this to.  They can do pretty much anything once they are in control of your system.  While you generally can chat with them and usually can see what they are doing, it's possible for them to do quick downloads and such that you really don't want on your computer.  You can cut them off with a click, but there's no telling what they can do in the meantime (depending on how they do it and how aware you are of what is happening).  I've let my ISP and Dell use Remote Desktop with me, but nobody else ever and only after I called them (and not if they called me) to make sure I dealing with the right people and not scammers.  If it's a friend, be certain it's a really good one who knows what he or she is doing.  With remote Desktop, they can help you fix problems or completely hose you.  You've allowed them past almost all your security protection when they are connected so it isn't going to save you. 

 

I hope this helps.

 

Good luck!

Finally, and with respect to the problem that seems to be common to all three of you, if the numeric keyboard doesn't work even if the NumLock key is pressed and the NumLock light is lit, put your mouse cursor somewhere in the middle of your screen and then press and hold any of the number keys on the numeric keypad other than 5 or 0.  Does the mouse cursor move? 

If so, then the reason that your keypad "doesn't work" is because "Mouse Keys" has been turned on.   And this need not have been done deliberately:  Mouse Keys can be turned on if the ALT, LEFT SHIFT, and NUMLOCK keys were pressed simultaneously (admittedly, a difficult combination to happen accidentally, but stranger things have happened).

For Windows 10, see https://www.isunshare.com/windows-10/2-ways-to-turn-on-mouse-keys-in-windows-10.html
