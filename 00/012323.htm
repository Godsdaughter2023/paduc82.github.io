tom ogilvy say:

You can do a conditional sum on a range with hidden rows to just sum the visible rows with

 

=SUMPRODUCT(SUBTOTAL(103,OFFSET($D$2,ROW($D$2:$D$18)-ROW($D$2),))*$C$2:$C$18)

You really don't need the condition of >0 unless you have negative values.  If you need that

 

=SUMPRODUCT(($C$2:$C$18>0)*SUBTOTAL(103,OFFSET($D$2,ROW($D$2:$D$18)-ROW($D$2),))*$C$2:$C$18)

or greater than 1 (only visible rows)

=SUMPRODUCT(($C$2:$C$18>1)*SUBTOTAL(103,OFFSET($D$2,ROW($D$2:$D$18)-ROW($D$2),))*$C$2:$C$18)

Data is in A1:D18 with headers in row 1, numerical values in column C.

i understand that you want to create a customized calendar including holiday lists of your organization and then publish it to all users in your organization. generally, if there are too many users in your company, we suggest you put them into a security group in eac(exchange admin center) and send the sharing calendar invitation to the security group instead of sending it to each user individually.

here are the steps to share the calendar:

1. log into owa(outlook web app).
2. navigate to calendar, click + icon to add a customized calendar and then fill the holiday lists of your organization into the customized calendar.


3. right-click the customized calendar and select share calendar.
4. type in security group email address you want to share with, choose the full details permission and select the customized calendar, then send the invitation email.


5. then users in this security group will receive an invitation email, they can add the shared customized calendar into their calendar list by clicking accept.
