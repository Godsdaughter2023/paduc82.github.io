Tina -

 

Thank you for advice and guidance.  Apologies to all for delays in posting my workaround.

 

I tried opening the files with Excel online and then tried and experienced the following:

 

1. Copy from Excel Online to New Local Excel Workbook - Result:

  • Doesn't bring over formats
  • Doesn't copy over formulas and references
  • Doesn't allow for "Paste Special" to copy formats and / or formulas.
  • Whether pasting as Unicode Text or Text, one cannot then format the data using Number, Currency, and Accounting formatting.
  • Additionally, one cannot use Autosum or other formulas on the copied and reformatted text.

2. Copy from Excel Online to Another Excel Online Workbook - Result:

  • Doesn't bring over formats
  • Doesn't copy over formulas and references
  • Doesn't allow for "Paste Special" to copy formats and / or formulas
  • Well you get the idea…

I then saved the Excel Online copy of the Workbook to a Local Drive (actually a Dropbox folder) on my MacBook Pro and then proceeded to:

 

3. Copy from Excel Online to a Local Drive; Then copied now Local Excel Online Workbook to New Local Excel Workbook (essentially Local to Local) – Steps & Results as follows:

 

  • Was able to and did Copy over all Relevant Cells (not entire sheets nor all worksheet cells) to New Local Excel Workbook using the following steps:
    • "Paste Special" to copy “Formula and number formats.”
    • Then, with same highlighted cells, followed with "Paste Special" to copy “Column widths.”
  • These steps were repeated for each of the 8 sheets that were in my Workbook.
  • The above resulted in New Local Workbook with formulas referencing the worksheets and cells within the Excel Online Workbook.
  • To reset all formulas so they referenced worksheets (tabs) within the New Local Workbook, I then conducted a Find and Replace Search (Edit/Find/Replace) and replaced the reference to the Excel Online Workbook (you will see the reference within one of your reference formulas) with a blank (“ “; without the quotes).
  • After making some 264 replacements, all formula refences to worksheets (tabs) were within the New Local Workbook.
  • Importantly, all formulas worked (yes, even the circular references) and I was able to run several single variable data tables.

 

I have no idea what code, references, and / or corruption issues were corrected.  I do know this work around worked and saved a lot of time recreating entire spreadsheets and recreating complex formulas and references, etc.

 

As mentioned elsewhere on a similar thread by Patti, as a matter of good practice, I would hit “esc” after copying and pasting so as to “Unselect” the previously heighted cells as this may also be why error messages have indicated problems with "Multiple Selections."

 

I do hope the above helps.  It worked for me.

john korchok say:

There's more than one way to do this. My preferred technique is to make each chapter a separate section (Insert>Break>Section Break (Next Page)).

  1. Choose Format>Document and choose the Layout tab.
  2. Check Different first page and OK out.
  3. On the first page of the document, double-click on the top margin to open the header and footer layer.
  4. Choose Insert>Text Box>Text Box and draw a text box the width of your text. The top edge should be just above the top margin and the bottom edge just above where you want your chapter text to start.
  5. With the text box still selected, choose Shape Format>Shape Outline>No Outline.
  6. Still on the Shape Format tab, choose Wrap Text>Top and Bottom. You should see the main text area shift down. Adjust the text box height to get the exact look you want.
  7. Double-click on the main text area to exit the header and footer.

By default, this text box with automatically be repeated on the first page after every section break.

charleskenyon (ia) say:

No problem Ron,

John's procedure will not be needed if you have a section break at the beginning of each chapter in that the space-before formatting will be applied at the beginning of a section.

While I do not recommend using a section break (unless you need one for some other reason), that will handle your immediate problem.

I try to discourage section breaks where they are not needed. In my opinion, section breaks are recommended and used far too frequently; multiple section breaks make for confusing headers/footers and page numbering.

Good reasons for section breaks (IMHO):

  • Change from portrait to landscape orientation
  • Change in newspaper-style columns
  • Change in margins (that can't be handled using indentsor other methods - rare)
  • To completely change headers or footers in ways not handled by the StyleRef Field
  • Restarting page numbering

Not good reasons for section breaks (IMHO):

  • Changing headers/footers so they reflect the content of the current chapter or page (See StyleRef Field )
  • Moving text down on the page at the beginning of a Chapter (when it can be handled with a frame)
  • Change in margins that should be handled using indents or other methods., ala Wordperfect

using System;

namespace Wallpaper

Center, Stretch

public const int SendWinIniChange = 0x02;

SystemParametersInfo( SetDesktopWallpaper, 0, path, UpdateIniFile | SendWinIniChange );

}

There is only one reason why two phones - identical in every other way - will have some features missing. The mobile phone operator has disabled that feature, for some reason.

You haven't mentioned if the two phones - old and new - are from the same operator. If they are from the same operator, then, only they can tell you why this is the case.

On a side note, a little more information about backups on regular issue 928 phones. When text backup works as advertised, two phones will begin to sync text messages on the fly. That means, if you receive a text message on phone 1, in a few minutes, phone 2 will have a mirror image of that text pushed to that device as well, even if they have different numbers.
