First, you can (in the sense of there being no adverse effects) delete *.tmp files. However, if you don't figure out where the files are coming from, they'll just come back.

Second, these particular tmp files appear to be related to Kaspersky antivirus, and particularly to those installations where Kaspersky is configured to scan Outlook email files. See Large PRxx.tmp Files .

Note that you can safely disable scanning both incoming and outgoing email .  A virus attached to an incoming email will (for the most part) do you no harm until you click on it -- and at that point, your antivirus app. should detect the problem and stop the virus from executing.
