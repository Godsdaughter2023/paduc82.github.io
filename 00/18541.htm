stefan blom say:

Place the insertion point in the first Heading 1 paragraph, and then click Home tab | Multilevel List | Define New Multilevel List. Verify that the numbering is correct.

In particular, make sure that level 2 is set to restart after level 1 and that the format includes level 1. You may have to recreate the number formats (in the "Enter formatting for number" box). Use the "Number style for this level" box to add the current number, and use "Include level number from" to add any previous level(s). See also http://www.shaunakelly.com/word/numbering/numbering20072010.html.

When you are done, update the TOC. Right-click it, select Update Field, and, when prompted, choose to update the whole table.

Tools>Internet Options>Security tab, Trusted sites icon, Sites button,

remove any public websites from your trusted sites list. eg. facebook.com, Instagram.com, twits.com or even google.com or Microsoft.com

Save changes.

IE has a security zone setting "Prevent navigation into a zone of lower integrity"...

some public websites like Instagram, facebook, google, Microsoft etc use a validation service to validate your user credentials when you revisit websites...

eg. google.com uses myaccount.google.com.

If you have google.com in your IE's trusted sites list, but not myaccount.google.com then IE will spit the dummy and won't allow navigation to myaccount.google.com.

The trusted sites zone has a default security level of Medium, while the Internet zone has a security level of Medium High... viz: the Trusted sites zone is ACTUALLY  less secure than the internet zone....

The https protocol works in any IE security zone, so you can safely remove any public websites from your IE Trusted sites list, without loosing https security.... you should however make sure that the Lock icon appears in the IE address bar and that there are no certificate errors.

If you are using a work computer, you should in the first instance contact your IT department or Help desk.... Some companies may have strict rules on the personal use of their computers. Use your smart phone instead to access your personal websites at work.

Your company IT department, may also have implemented security features in IE that is preventing sites like Instagram or facebook from being accessed from their computers.
