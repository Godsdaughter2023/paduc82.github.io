janerowland say:

How do I stop hyperlink from printing out?

I am doing a Manual - in the electronic copy I want users to be able to click the hyperlink I will insert at the top of every page (via header/footer function) but I don't want the hyperlink to be seen when I print out a hard copy?  How can I do this?

 

Cheers

Jane.

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

You could put the hyperlink in a bookmark and then use a macro containing code to format the font of the range of the bookmark as hidden.

 

ActiveDocument.Bookmarks("bookmarkname").Range.Font.Hidden=True

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

It is really better not to put the hyperlink in the header of the document as if that is where it is, the user as to first double click to open the header pane and then click again (or twice) to get the hyperlink to fire.

On the other hand, putting them at the top of each page requires you to do just that and it needs to be done when the document is otherwise complete as otherwise edits and the resulting re-pagination may move them out of position.  And of course, when you hide the hyperlinks, the pagination will almost certainly change, unless each page is ended with a hard page break.

You are really better off to just insert a bookmark at the beginning of the table of contents (say named "contents") and then create a macro that you assign to the keyboard that contains the following code:

With ActiveDocument
    If .Bookmarks.Exists("contents") Then
        .Bookmarks("contents").Range.Select
    End If
End With
