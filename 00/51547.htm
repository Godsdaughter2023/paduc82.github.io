"How to Refresh Windows 8 and Windows 8.1"

  • The PC boots into Windows RE (Recovery Environment).
  • Windows RE scans the hard drive for your data, settings, and apps, and puts them aside (on the same drive).
  • Windows RE installs a fresh copy of Windows.
  • Windows RE restores the data, settings, and apps it has set aside into the newly installed copy of Windows.
  • The PC restarts into the newly installed copy of Windows.

"When you reset your PC, the drive will be completely reset, and all your apps and files will be removed from it. a. user say:

If your only or most important reason for changing to 64-bit Windows is "to get better performance" you can save your time. Although you may observe better performance in some applications on benchmarks, you are unlikely to notice it in real life.

The advantage of 64-bit computing is the ability to install more RAM. A 32-bit computer limits you to 4 GB of RAM while a 64-bit bit computer can address huge amounts of RAM. However, unless your applications or use case routinely exceed 4 GB of RAM in use (not reserved) you are unlikely to see an improvement in performance.

Nevertheless, 64-bit computing is the future (until it's overtaken by 128-bit computing) because technology always moves forward, regardless.

If you move to 64-bit computing you will need to replace your 32-bit drivers with 64-bit drivers and some of your software will need to be replaced as well, particularly your anti-malware applications.
