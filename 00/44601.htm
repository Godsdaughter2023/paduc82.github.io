    If the computer’s screen turns off after few minutes of inactivity and turns on when you press any key or touch the touchpad, then it could due to either sleep/hibernation option or the turn off display option. You have mentioned that you have already checked all the power options and they set to never.     a)      Press Windows key + X, select ‘Power options’.

    b)      Click on ‘Change plan settings’ against the current selected power plan.

    c)      Click on ‘Change advanced power settings’.

    d)     Expand the sleep section and check if the options ‘sleep after’ and ‘Hibernate after’ are set to never; both while on battery and plugged in.

    e)      If yes, expand the ‘Display’ section and check if the option ‘Turn off display after’ option is set to never.

    1. Press Windows key +F to bring up the search Charms bar.
    2. Type Troubleshooter and press enter; click on the Settings tab below the search box.
    3. Click on the Find and Fix problems tile and then click on the view all button the left pane.
    4. Click on the Power troubleshooter and run it.

    You say "I know that I have a 32-bit x64 based processor," but that makes no sense .32-bit and X64 contradict each other.

    So I'll assume you mean you have a 32-bit processor, not a 64-bit one. To go to a 64-bit one, you need to replace both the motherboard and the processor (CPU). Alternatively it may be less expensive to sell the computer you have and buy a new 64-bit one.

    Why do you want to do this? It won't be an inexpensive change and it may or may not provide any significant  value for you.

    mlg63 say:

    Post your complete info--model computer and operating system when asking a question. What you might mean is you have a 32 bit operating system with a 64 bit processor. You don't have 32 bit and 64 bit processor. The only way to change to 64 bit operating system is to reinstall the 64 bit version of windows and do a clean install. Clean install will remove your 32 bit version of windows and everything else on your hard drive. Depending on the hardware in your computer, it might not be worth the expense or the trouble. You need to have a 64 bit processor and more than 4 GB of memory to take advantage of a 64 bit version of windows.
