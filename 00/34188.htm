luciwright say:

picture shrinks in Movie Maker

I have Windows 7 version of MovieMaker.  My pictures download fine from my camera into MovieMaker and I'm working in 16:9 format with JPEG files.  The size is 5184x 3456 in MovieMaker.  However when I save the completed movie under the recommended HD setting, the top and bottom edges of the pictures appear to be cut off or the whole picture is zoomed in.  I have carefully composed my shot, but in the saved movie version now mountaintops and steeples are cut off.  I had a lovely picture of stained glass windows with the colorful reflections on the floor and in MovieMaker the reflections were cut off---picture was ruined!

How can I keep this from happening?  I've been saving my movies onto a flashdrive and then playing on the TV, which has worked well except for this problem.

Another issue:    I did recently also notice that some pictures have a "flutter" in the middle---not sure if that's the flashdrive or how they were saved by MovieMaker.  Any ideas?

Bewildered & Frustrated

considerate_guy say:

The aspect ratio of that photo is 3:2.  If you do the math you'll see it:

5184 = 3 x 1728

3456 = 2 x 1728

In other words, it is about 1.5 times as wide as it is tall.

Movie Maker has an undocumented feature that if you have a photo in this aspect ratio (3:2), it will try to fit it to match your project's aspect ratio so you don't have black around the photo.

If you are working within a 16:9 project, Movie Maker will crop a little from the top and bottom of all 3:2 photos to make them fit into a 16:9 aspect ratio.

In your case, that means:

5184 = 16 x 324

2916 = 9 x 324

So it will trim off 240 pixels (about 7%) equally from the top and bottom of the picture.

On the other hand, if you are working in a 4:3 project, it will crop a bit from the sides of your photo:

In your case:

4608 = 4 x 1156

3456 = 3 x 1156

That means you will lose 288 pixels (about 6%) from the left and 288 pixels from the right.

Unfortunately, this behavior automatically happens if the photo is in a 3:2 format, just like what your camera creates.  About the only thing I can suggest is that you try to compose your pictures leaving more space at the edges so the auto-cropping doesn't cause you to lose anything important, or you do your own  cropping to a 16:9 ratio. 

For example, if you don't want to lose the mountaintops or the steeples, crop the photo higher up and take more from the bottom.  You can use Photo Gallery's crop feature to crop to a 16:9 proportion.

It would be really nice if Movie Maker actually let you turn this auto-cropping on or off.  In fact, I've seen other people complaining that their 4:3 photos don't get auto-cropped and leave black on the edges and asking for it to be a choice.  Yours is the first request I've seen to not automatically crop a 3:2 photo, but I can understand why you'd want to keep the photo as it was originally composed.

Maybe this can be a suggestion for a future version of Movie Maker...

If Windows 10 fails to boot normally three times over, the fourth time it enters by default in an Automatic Repair mode. Using this mode, you can boot into Safe Mode.
