Here ya go, hawkeye!  ;-)


Sub ChangeFontColour()

 

Dim oSh As Shape

Set oSh = ActivePresentation.Slides(1).Shapes("ShapeName")


With oSh

If .TextFrame.TextRange.Font.Color.RGB = RGB(0, 0, 0) Then

    ChangeToBlack

End If

End With

 

End Sub

 

Sub ChangeToBlack()

 

 Dim osld As Slide

 Dim oshp As Shape

 Dim x As Long


For Each osld In ActivePresentation.Slides

  For Each oshp In osld.Shapes

   If oshp.HasTextFrame Then

      If oshp.TextFrame.HasText Then

        With oshp.TextFrame.TextRange

            For x = .Runs.Count To 1 Step -1

            ' 255,255,255 is WHITE, not BLACK

            .Runs(x).Font.Color.RGB = RGB(0, 0, 0)

 

            Next x

        End With

        End If 'has text

    End If 'has textframe

    Next oshp

 Next osld

 

End Sub

VAT and UK Charities - Microsoft Community

brianhead- [o365] say:

VAT and UK Charities

Hi,

As  a small UK charity we have been advised that even without VAT registration we should not have VAT on our invoices from Ireland. Can you confirm your agreement to this, and how we can action that.

Thanks,

Brian

Here is the expert advice:

In terms of your query below, Microsoft are wrong.   As you have business activities and as the supply is of a software licence the supply is ‘B2B general rule’. So the place of supply is the UK and the UK VAT rules apply, not the Irish rules. Microsoft should treat this as a reverse charge supply and not charge Irish VAT. From your perspective the supply value counts towards your UK registration threshold, but unless that triggers UK registration, the net effect is you get the software VAT free.

Here is the HMRC guidance to support this position:

https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/vat-notice-741a-place-of-supply-of-services/vat-notice-741a-place-of-supply-of-services#reverse-charge

18.10 B2B general rule services

If you belong in the UK and receive supplies of any B2B general rule services (see section 5) where your supplier belongs outside the UK the reverse charge applies. If you are unsure whether you belong in the UK, see section 3.

18.10.3 What if I am not already UK VAT registered and receive these services?

If you are not already UK VAT registered and you receive supplies of B2B general rule services which meet the conditions in paragraph 18.10 and are treated as supplied where the recipient belongs, the value of those supplies must be added to the value of your own taxable supplies in determining whether you should be registered for UK VAT. Even if you make no taxable supplies yourself you will still be a taxable person if the value of your imported services exceeds the registration limits.

We see this fairly regularly with US multinationals – they seem to assume that if the customer does not have a VAT number that inevitably means the supply is B2C – but of course you can have exempt business activities. You need to make clear to Microsoft that you are a business but unregistered and as such it is the UK VAT rules that apply, not the Irish VAT rules.

Floating images move floating images! - Microsoft Community

donna0804 say:

Floating images move floating images!

When adding floating pictures, I have added multiple images to a document using Wrap Text because I want to be able to freely adjust the location. Now, when I adjust one image others on the same page move!  Why? I can't find how they are in any way tied to each other!
suzanne s. barnhill say:

All wrapped (floating) images are anchored to a text paragraph. If you display "object anchors," then, when you select a graphic, you can see what paragraph it is anchored to. The behavior of images may vary depending on whether they're anchored to the same or different paragraphs. If the latter, note that their "order" will be based on the order of the paragraphs regardless of where the actual images are placed on the page.

That said, there are settings in the Advanced Layout Options that seem to pertain to how images interact with each other ("Allow overlap" is one of them); I have not worked with graphics enough to have figured these out, but you might want to experiment to see what effect they have.
