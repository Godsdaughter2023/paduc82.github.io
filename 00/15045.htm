jeeped say:
You haven't mentioned which row your data starts in, but I will assume there are headings in row 1 and the data starts in row 2 for the example below. If your data starts in another row, substitute that row number.

Select A2:R369 and click Conditional Formatting, New Rule the Home tab's Styles group. Select Use a formula to determine which cells to format from the Select a Rule Type list and put this formula into the Format values where this formula is true text box:

=AND(LEN($D2),LEN($E2),LEN($G2),LEN($H2),LEN($R2))

Note that 2 is the top row in your selection. Next click Format and select a Fill. Click OK to close the Format Cells dialog and OK again to save your new Conditional Formatting rule.

                     

The use of LEN() on the various columns interprets a zero as False and anything other than zero as True. The rows are left as relative references while the columns are absolute.


Addendum: I just reread your original post and realized you will likely have more data at a future date. Instead of selecting A2:R369, tap F5 for the GoTo dialog and paste A2:XFD1048576 into the Reference text box then click OK. ray gao msft say:

Hi Farhan,

 

There is no SharePoint icon in the app launcher for OneDrive for Business Plan 1. The SharePoint icon will only appear when you have the SharePoint Online license which is not included in OneDrive for Business Plan 1. For more information about the feature availability in OneDrive for Business Plan 1, please refer to OneDrive for Business Service Description.

 

About adding the custom tiles, it is not doable in OneDrive for Business Plan 1. As the support article noted, you need an Exchange Online mailbox assigned to you and you've successfully signed into your mailbox before you can use this feature.
