Hi Amtri,

 

Since you have configured your email account as IMAP protocol, Outlook will show the mirror image of the webmail. If the email is read on other device or on the webmail, then Outlook will show the email as Read.

There is no option/Rule within Outlook to control this feature.

If you want the emails on the desktop to be shown as Read, then you may setup the email account with different protocol like POP3.

Cannot sort rows with merged cells - Microsoft Community

honest abe say:

Cannot sort rows with merged cells

Why doesn't Excel let the user sort rows with horizontally merged cells?  FIRST, all rows in the sort range have identical formatting, including the same width as surrounding cells and other merged cell configurations.  SECOND, there are no vertical merges that link cells across rows. As such, all row formatting is identical, but the range still cannot be sorted.  Why?

It appears that ranges with merged cells simply cannot be sorted.  The limitation does not seem reasonable given that certain forms of cell merging are compatible with the type of sort the user is attempting to perform.  It also casts a long shadow on the functionality and desirability of the merge feature.  Merged cells also pose problems when creating a table, presumably for the same reasons.

hansv mvp say:
I agree that this limitation appears to be unnecessary, but that's the way it has been in older versions, and still is in Excel 2010. It's one of the reasons for avoiding merged cells. (VBA doesn't work well with merged cells either)

jllatham say:

I concur with your comments and HansV's.

One way around this, and around the whole merging cells mess, is that when you want to make something appear as if it were in a group of merged cells, try this:

Enter the text/number/formula into the leftmost cell of the group of cells you might normally merge.  Then, go back and select that cell and the following cells on the row that you might have merged and choose

Format --> Format Cells and on the [Alignment] tab of the dialog, under horizontal alignment, choose "Center Across Selection".

rich007a say:

We're all in agreement that the behaivour of merged is not perfect.  But in your case ("all rows in the sort range have identical formatting, including the merged cell configurations" and "there are no vertical merges that link cells across rows") isn't achieving what you want to do as simple as:

  • Select the range to be sorted.
  • Unmerge the cells.
  • Sort as required.
  • Re-merge using the Merge Across function.

 

Or are there multiple horizontal merged groups in each row?

If so, may I suggest an alternative work-around?

  • Make a copy of the worksheet (drag and drop sheet tab while holding Ctrl).
  • Go to the original sheet, select all cells and unmerge.
  • Sort as required.
  • Go to the copy of the sheet, select all cells and click the Format Painter.
  • Go to the original sheet and apply the format painter by clicking on the "select all" square between A and 1.
  • Delete the duplicate (unsorted) sheet.

Does that help?

I see this was a post from years ago, but someone may still be looking for this answer for sorting with merged cells.

As a workaround I found that the original spreadsheet sent me that some of them had the first four (A,B,C,D) columns had been merged so that they align centered (vertically) in each column and the fifth column (E) that some had two phone numbers (1 in row E 21 and another in row E 22 for example). I then edited that cell by adding the appropriate amount of spaces (with the space bar) between the two phone numbers so they aligned one above the other (as if they were in two different rows). After the wrap text is applied there may need some adjustment for cells a.b.c & d to align centered centered left with the alignment tool under the home tab. 

It works fine in Excel 2010 to sort uneven row heights with No Merged Cells.
