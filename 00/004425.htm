nsilmike say:

You can drag and drop to it...  It works this way though-  you drag from the collection view in the zune software to the device.  You can do this in either of two sync modes- manual, and 'choose' mode.  In manual, the software does nothing to manage your sync'd content on the device.  You add and you remove.

In 'choose' mode you get automatic sync'ing of content you choose.  The difference is you can choose songs, or albums, or playlists, etc. and when a change is made in your collection (you buy a new album for a 'chosen' artist for example) it is sync'd automatically.

As for creating a sync folder... I have no idea where you got the idea that is required.  Zune software uses the same library concept that win7 uses.  You just have to tell it where your stuff is, so it can display, play and sync it.  You can create a specific folder if you like, but whatever your current folders are works just as well.  And, if you have multiple folders, you can also drag and drop from windows to the zune software to add them to the collection as monitored content.  There is no additional storage space required... you are just telling the software that the music it should use is over here, in that folder...

ken sheridan say: I notice that you say the query is designed to 'sort by rep', which implies that it has an ORDER BY clause.  A report will not respect this clause, and must be sorted/grouped using its internal sorting and grouping mechanism.  It may be that the fact that the query's result table is correctly sorted is disguising the reason for the apparent duplication of rows in the report.

If the same query is used in another context, e.g. as the RecordSource property of a form, in which it is appropriate to order the query's result table, then there is no need to remove the ORDER BY clause as the report's internal sort order will override the query's.  Ensure that the report's group and sort levels correctly represent what you want by way of output of the data in the report.

erind903 say:

Making Business Cards in Publisher

I'm trying to make business cards in Publisher and am wanting only 1 card to show up on the screen.

So ... I want to edit 1 card and have the changes show up on all 10 when printed.

I hope I'm making sense. Please help!

davidf2 say:

If you go to File > New > Installed templates > Business Cards templates, you can pick any of the templates including the blank cards and when you create the business card you will get a single card. It may look huge because Publisher scaled it to fit/fill your screen but it is probably the standard size 3.5 X 2.  Go to View > check Rulers to get a perspective or just hit F9 to get 100% scaling. Then when you go to File > Print it should show you 10 copies of the card on the sheet.

If you have an old business card template file from the past, it too should open as a single card.
