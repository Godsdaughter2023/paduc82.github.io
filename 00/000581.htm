jay freedman say:

As you may have read in your research, Word 2007 and 2010 treat AutoText as just one part of a larger feature called Building Blocks (and, confusingly, also called Quick Parts on the Insert ribbon). Here's the procedure for transferring the old AutoText entries, all in one step (probably).

- On the old computer, open the My Computer application. In the address bar, type or paste this:

   %appdata%\Microsoft\Templates

   Then press Enter. This shows you the folder where your templates are stored.

- Make a copy of the file Normal.dot, and name the copy AutoText.dot.

- Transfer AutoText.dot to the new computer -- through a remote connection or a disk or CD or USB drive -- and store it in the folder

   %appdata%\Microsoft\Document Building Blocks

- Start Word or, if Word is already open, shut it down and restart it.

- Click Insert > Quick Parts > Building Blocks Organizer. You should see the transferred AutoText entries listed in the AutoText gallery (along with a bunch of other galleries).

techie psychdoc say:

can't get Windows Mobile 6 to retrieve contacts from Outlook 2003.

Specs: I have Outlook 2003 running on a computer with Windows XP operating system. I have a Palm TX and just bought an HP iPAQ 110 Classic handheld.

Problem: I was able to transfer all of my info from the Palm into Outlook, including Contacts, Calendar, Notes, Memos, etc.  I then set up Microsoft ActiveSync from the disc provided with my new iPAQ PDA, followed all the on screen instructions and synced the PDA. Some of the information transferred from Outlook into the iPAQ, namely the notes and memos. But the Contacts and Calender info did not transfer, despite the fact that the ActiveSync window showed sycronization with these two databases. So ActiveSync thinks it properly syncronized with Contacts and Calendar, but it did not actually pick up the information. I have tried numerous remedies, including removing and re-installing all the software, hard resetting the PDA, syncing with all sorts of configurations, etc.  Why can't I get my Contacts and Calendar to load onto my new iPAQ??????

julie fka profjulie say:

Try this:

On the IPAQ, tap Start > Settings > Connections > USB to PC > remove the checkmark in the box next to "Use Enhanced Network Functionality"

Then, delete your existing partnership with the IPAQ and set it up again.  This time:

On first sync, select only one data type to sync (Contacts), after the sync is completed, check the IPAQ to make sure all your contacts synced over to it

Then on second sync, select another data type to sync (Calendar), after the sync is completed, check to make sure all calendar items are synced over to it

Then continue in this manner until you've selected all the data types you want to sync with your IPAQ.
