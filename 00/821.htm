daniel g. grau say:
Instead of [a-z], you can use [0-9A-Za-z] or even combine these as [0-z]. You get the idea. These ranges will definitely work if paragraphs are separated by blank ¶ marks. Try it.

If your PDF text uses ¶¶ to separate paragraphs, then you need to delete all single ¶s. For this, there is an easier way that does not require wildcards. You do two replacements in a row:

  • First, change ^p^p into, say, Red color.
  • Then look for ^p in Auto color and replace by a space.

Advanced Find function within Word does not always highlight found - Microsoft Community

bamahoop say:

Advanced Find function within Word does not always highlight found text

Using MS Word (2013 Office Pro Plus 32-bit version) when searching on text using the 'Find and Replace' or 'Advanced Find' feature (the one where the "Find and Replace" box pops up), found text does not always highlight in gray.  So as you keep clicking to find the next occurrence, it will jump to a new page, but sometimes no text is highlighted.  Click again, and it will jump to the next occurrence, which then is highlighted.  Does not seem to be a pattern, but more often than not it's the first occurrence on a page that doesn't get highlighted.


So if you're not careful you will miss found occurrences, or you will have to visually search the entire page yourself to find it.


The other find feature, using Ctrl-F or clicking the 'Find' button to open the 'Navigation' pane/sidebar, does seem to work reliably.  It not only highlights all occurrences in yellow simultaneously, it also highlights each subsequent occurrence in gray as you click through and focus shifts, forward or back.


So if you are just trying to find text, not replace it, it's best to not use the 'Advanced Find' feature.
