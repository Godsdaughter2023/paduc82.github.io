a. user say:

It's not a question of which is better. They are two different types of account. Choose the one that you prefer. Windows 10 works exactly the same no matter which account you choose.

The only real difference between a local account and an online account - whether it's a Microsoft account, a Google account, a Yahoo account, etc. is that an online account lets you store files and system settings in the cloud. With a local account, all your files and system settings are stored on the local machine.

Online service providers like Microsoft, Apple, Adobe, Google, etc. want you to store your data in the cloud, because it benefits them, so they each have their own tricks and gotchas to entice you. For example, you can only get apps from the Windows Store if you have a Microsoft account. But that only means you have to sign up for a Microsoft account, which you can do online. You don't actually have to use your Microsoft account on Windows 10 if you don't want to.

andre for directly say:
Did you upgrade from a Windows 7 Professional retail store bought license or a preinstalled license?

It depends on how Windows 10 was originally configured on your computer.

If its a full retail store bought license online or offline, it is transferable to a new computer or motherboard.

If its the free upgrade from a retail store bought Windows 7 or Windows 8 license, it is transferable to a new computer or motherboard.

If its the free upgrade from a OEM preinstalled Windows 7 or Windows 8 license, it is NOT transferable to a new computer or motherboard.

Learn more about transferring your license:
http://www.groovypost.com/howto/transfer-window...

If its a retail license, learn how to reactivate:
http://www.groovypost.com/howto/activate-window...
