Welcome to Microsoft Community! Let me assist you regarding your concern not being able to receive emails.

 

Please try to check the following settings if you are having issues receiving emails.

 

A. Email Forwarding

  1. On your inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.
  1. Under Managing your account, click Email forwarding.

Note: If it's set to Forward your mail to another email account, tick on Don't forward then click Save.

 

 

B.  Blocked Sender's List

  1. On your inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.
  1. under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.
  1. Click Blocked senders.
  2. In the Blocked senders list, select the name or domain (the part of an e-mail address that follows the @ sign, such as hotmail.com) that you now want to receive mail from, and then click<< Remove from list.

 

C. Add the email address to your Safe senders list

  1. On your inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.
  1. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.
  1. Click Safe Senders.
  1. In the text box, type in the email address or domain you’d like to always receive email from, and then clickAdd to list >>.

D. Check your Email Rules

  1. On your inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.
  2. Under Customizing Outlook, click Rules for sorting new messages.
  1. Perform one of the actions as per your requirement:
    Click     Edit next to the rule that you want to edit.
    Or
    Choose the rule that you want to delete and click     Delete.
    Note: Not all international characters are supported as filters.

 

E. Check your Junk Mail Settings

  1. On your inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.
  1. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.
  2. Under Choose a junk e-mail filter, choose the level of protection that you want, and then click Save. If the Exclusive level was set, messages from senders not on your Contacts or Safe senders lists will have been sent to the Junk e-mail folder.

 

F. Check your Reply-to address

  1. On your inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.
  1. Under Writing email, click Reply-to address.
  2. Select Current e-mail address radio button.
  1. Click Save.

 

Hope this resolved your concern. Please do not hesitate to contact us again if you have other concern.

 

Thanks,

Take a look at the option to 'drill down through a hierarchy'.  If you look at the RowSource properties of each of the three unbound combo boxes in the form header you'll see that those for district and parish each reference the combo box above in the hierarchy as a parameter.  You need to do the same.

The code in the AfterUpdate event procedures of the county and district combo boxes simply sets those below to Null and requeries them so that they show the restricted lists.  As you can see below the code is quite straightforward, but if code is completely ruled out in your case you just need to reproduce the same for each with macros rather than code.

In the AfterUpdate event procedures of all three the form is requeried to progressively restrict it to those rows which match the selections in the combo boxes.  You may want to do something different, however.

Private Sub cboGotoCounty_AfterUpdate()

    ' set district and parish combo boxes to Null
    ' and requery controls to show districts in
    ' selected county
     Me.cboGotoDistrict = Null
     Me.cboGotoDistrict.Requery
     Me.cboGotoParish = Null
     Me.cboGotoParish.Requery
     
     ' requery form to show locations
     ' in selected county
     Me.Requery
     
End Sub

Private Sub cboGotoDistrict_AfterUpdate()

    ' set parish combo boxwes to Null
    ' and requery control to show parishes in
    ' selected district
     Me.cboGotoParish = Null
     Me.cboGotoParish.Requery
     
     ' requery form to show locations
     ' in selected district
     Me.Requery

End Sub

Private Sub cboGotoParish_AfterUpdate()
     
     ' requery form to show locations
     ' in selected parish
     Me.Requery

End Sub

graham mayor say:

File paths are not case sensitive, so the fact that

Dim strTemplateLocation As String
strTemplateLocation = Options.DefaultFilePath(wdUserTemplatesPath)

produces a result in lower case will not prevent Word 2010 from finding that location. and if you were to use

Dim strTemplateLocation As String
strTemplateLocation = Options.DefaultFilePath(wdUserTemplatesPath)
Documents.Add strTemplateLocation & "\templatename.dotm"

provided the template templatename.dotm is actually in the folder in question, then Word 2010 will base a new document upon it.
This works equally well with dotx and dot templates.

What will not work is a command to use a dotx or dotm template in Word 2003. Word 2003 has no ability to work with XML document templates.

If you want to use the same macro in either version and you wish to use both dot and dotx/m templates as appropriate, you can test for the application version

Dim strTemplateLocation As String
strTemplateLocation = Options.DefaultFilePath(wdUserTemplatesPath)
If Application.Version > 11 Then
    Documents.Add strTemplateLocation & "\TemplateName.dotm"
Else
    Documents.Add strTemplateLocation & "\TemplateName.dot"
End If

The important part is that the template must be in the folder you call it from. The actual template locations for the two versions may not be the same.
