martin crosbie say:

This is by design.

When you take pictures, the phone records which way up they are supposed to be, and they will be displayed that way up on all viewers, unless it is adjusted by an application which can rotate them.

Windows Phone will automatically rotate images, so that the bottom edge of the photo will be displayed on the bottom edge of the phone, whichever way up it is.

There is no control for this function.

joeu2004 say: JR wrote:
How do I fix excell to do the math the right way?

The answer is 5.49 and NOT 5.5 or 5.50.  I have NOT told excel to roundup or to use only two decimal points and I the math has to be correct, not incorrect.  How can this be fixed?

It is unclear to me exactly what math you consider incorrect.

In one case, you are adding 1.83 three times (D3+D3+D3) or multiplying 1.83*3, and you get 5.49.

In the other case, you are adding 22/12 three times (H5+H5+H5 [sic]) or multplying (22/12)*3, and you get 5.5.

Obviously, 22/12 (1.83333333333333) is not the same as 1.83.  So why would expect that multiplying both by 3 should have the same result?

I suspect that you want to compute =ROUND(22/12,2).  If that is in H5 and you have 1.83 in D3, then =H5*3 will be the same as =D3*3.

Does that answer your question?

----

Note that normally, formatting does not affect arithmetic.  That is, it does not matter whether you format with 2, 4 or 8 decimal places.

The exception:  if you have the "Precision as displayed" (PAD) calculation option set.  However, it is apparent that you do not.  And I do not normally recommend it because PAD is error-prone to use.

So normally, if you write =22/12 and display the result to 4 decimal places, it will actually about 1.83333333333333.

If you then write =1.8333*3, the result (about 5.4999) will be different from =(22/12)*3 (about 5.5) because you truncated the actual value.

Does that help you understand all of the differences that you see?
