Keep with next and Keep lines together pagination options - Microsoft Community

p j mccollum say:

Keep with next and Keep lines together pagination options

After inserting a page break, when we select the keep with next and keep lines together pagination options it does not force text to move with the title.
charles kenyon say:
First. Don't insert a page break.
Instead select the paragraph you want to begin your page and format it to have a page break before. Even better, use a style with this formatting.

Keep with next tells the paragraph with this formatting to keep with the next paragraph. Word defines a paragraph as that stuff between paragraph marks. If you are using your Enter key to put space between paragraphs then you are entering extra paragraphs. Your keep with next may be staying with an empty following paragraph.

Note that in Word's definition of a paragraph, a page break that you insert will be inside a paragraph. This sometimes leads to very confusing formatting. Thus the advice to not insert a page break.

Keep lines together tells the paragraph with this formatting to not split across a page division unless you format too much text this way to keep it all on the same page.

Do your formatting using Styles, not formatting icons on the rRbbon, and you'll be much happier in the long run.

Tips for Understanding Styles in Word by Shauna Kelly, MVP

 Style Basics in Word 2007 (& 2010) - Microsoft
