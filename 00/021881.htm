bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

Without seeing the document it's hard to be certain, but I agree with Charles that the brackets indicate Bookmarks. However, the display of Bookmarks is not controlled by the display of non-printing characters (¶) - at least not in Mac Word. The display of Bookmarks is a separate Preference in Word> Preferences> View - Show in Document. With that Preference checked Bookmark brackets will display in documents regardless of whether non-printing characters is toggled on/off. To hide the brackets, clear that check in the Preferences.

By doing as you are the brackets are being removed because you are removing the Bookmarks, themselves. Bookmarks are created for a purpose, such as to serve as the target for Cross-References, Hyperlinks, & various types of Fields. By replacing the Bookmarks with un-Bookmarked text those links are being broken, so they no longer will serve their intended function.
