john - project say:

Ignacio,

Display the Total Slack field. You will see that tasks with edays will have a positive total slack. Here's why. The total slack is based on the Project calendar workdays which by default means Saturday and Sunday are non-working days. Those extra two days of a task that is scheduled with elapsed days automatically provides positive slack (i.e. 2 days beyond the normal 5 day workweek).

Hope this helps.

tom_bpc say:

Ignacio,

The short answer to your direct question: You would need to set your threshold to 3 days to catch a task with TS = 1 ed.  Total Slack of 1 eday = 24 hours = 3 "days" (if you've left the default hours per day = 8.)

I have a few clarifications to add to John’s response:

  1. The Total Slack of each task is a Duration variable, and like all Duration variables, it is computed in minutes of working time according to the task’s effective calendar.
  2. For scheduling purposes, using an elapsed duration is essentially the same as applying a 24-hour task calendar.  That is, the task sees all 24 hours each day as potential working hours. 
  3. The “Critical” flag threshold value is also a Duration variable, so increasing the value from 0 to 1 day actually means that any task whose Total Slack is less than or equal to 8 hours (when hours/day = 8) shall be marked as “Critical.”
  4. As you have found, this is not enough to flag many elapsed-duration activities.  For example, with the default hours per day = 8:
    • An elapsed-duration task finishing at 17:00 on Wednesday – and driving a regular task with TS=0 that starts at 08:00 the following morning – possesses 15 hours of Total Slack.  Setting the “Critical” threshold to 2 days (16h) is needed to flag this task as Critical.
    • An elapsed-duration task finishing at 17:00 on Friday – and driving a regular task with TS-0 that starts at 08:00 the following Monday – possesses 63 hours of Total Slack.  The threshold would need to be set at 8 days (64h) to flag this task as Critical.
