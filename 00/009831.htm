Well, it's not all THAT hard. To insert a single doublequote in a doublequote delimited string use a double doublequote (now how's THAT for doubletalk!):

 

=[LocLatD] & "° " & [LocLatM] & "' " & [LocLatS] & """"

 

That's four consecutive " characters at the end of the string.

lbarbera say:

SOLVED Upgrade to 9860 fails with "Cannot update the system reserved partition" error C1900200

I want to share my experience with upgrading from Win10 TP build 9841 to 9860. The system in subject is an HP Pavilion laptop with 500GB HDD that has received Win10 TP by upgrading directly from Windows 8.1 Update 1, and that process went smooth as silk.

I started the upgrade to 9860 as usual from PC Settings / Preview Builds, patiently waiting for the download to complete, and then started the upgrade process; which, to my utter disappointment, failed with the message Cannot update the system reserved partition: error C1900200 .

I tried again several times assuming something went wrong with the download, to no avail. Any attempt failed with the same message, albeit I've seen different error codes.

Then, doing a routine check on my system, I have found that the system reserved partition (which I never bothered about) had 5MB (five) free out of a size of 350MB: there simply was no more space to do whatever the 9860 installer needed!! So I installed MiniTool Partition Wizard (it's free and doesn't seem to contain any crapware), shrunk the main partition by some 150MB, extended the System Reserved partition to 500MB and applied the change.

One reboot later I started the preview build download again, triggered the installation, and voila - PROBLEM SOLVED.

I hope this can help some of you out there. It's a trivial thing but often overlooked.

Oh, and I have also seen on the field issues with upgrading to 9860 caused by

  • not enough space on the main disk partition (apparently 22+GB are required to install 9860! but I haven't seen this specified clearly anywhere)
  • USB devices connected to the computer at upgrade time (solution in this case apparently is to REMOVE all USB devices and then try installing)
