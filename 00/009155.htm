Close any applications that could be interfering with your call. File sharing applications, streaming audio or video, or even just having a web browser open are all things that can eat up bandwidth.

As far as scanning goes, open MSE and go to the Settings Tab and click on Scheduled Scan.  Do you have the box checked to "Start the scheduled scan only when my computer is on but not in use"?  If so, it's not starting because you just said you are using the computer.  If you uncheck that box, then it should start around that time (it may vary by as much as an hour in either direction especially if you have asked it to update before scanning).  The box is there because scanning uses a lot of resources and can slow you down noticeably depending on how much else you have going on at the same time (and your CPU and RAM) - so some people prefer it not interrupt them if they're busy.  I personally leave it unchecked because I want it to start even if I'm using the computer (as I do not like leaving it on overnight and don't want to miss the scan).  That appears to be what you too want (or at  least what you expected to be happening).

 

By the way, you can tell if it it scanning quickly by looking at the MSE icon in the notification area and if it is scanning or updating, what is normally just a checkbox in the center of the icon will become a moving indicator to show it is happening.  You can double-click on it to open MSE and see which is actually occurring (but if you know the scheduled time, it's likely to mean that it is scanning so it's up to you if you want to confirm that or trust it to be the case).

 

Are you doing quick scans or full scans?  Remember it needs to stay on until the scan completes - you can't turn it off or put it to sleep or hibernation during the scan and expect it to pick up where it left off when it is restarted.  It will not complete that scan.  This is fine if you plan on leaving the computer on and connected to the internet the entire time even if you go to bed yourself.  My full scans take 8-10 hours, so I generally bypass the scheduled event and start them manually when I awaken on that day (as when that occurs can vary by as much as 5-6 hours - LOL!).  Quick scans are much faster, but you do want to do full scans periodically because quick scans alone are fine every 2-3 days or even daily just to catch threats in common places - but you want to run full scans (I do so weekly) to make sure everything is checked since many threats know that there's a difference and avoid areas included in quick scans and hide instead in places that can only be found with a full scan.

 

Good luck!

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

This is a limitation of Outlook.com's SMTP server.  You can send from other send/receive or send-only accounts when using the web interface, but the sender address will always get changed to your primary alias when sending from a mail client that specifies one of those other accounts as the sending address.

The way to deal with this is to use the mail providers' own SMTP servers when configuring the accounts in Outlook.  Configure those accounts so they do not receive mail (i.e., do not include them in the send/receive group or uncheck the "Receive mail items" check box in the send/receive group properties for the accounts.

I have yet to see the value in funneling all mail through a single mail service as opposed to accessing the individual mailboxes in the Outlook client.
