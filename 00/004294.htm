jllatham say:

Just as you wrote it, with an = symbol in front of it all:

=A1-B1-C1-D1

If you need to do more complicated math than that, you'll need to be aware of the way different operations (add, subtract, multiply, divide, raise-to-power) are dealt with.  There is a hierarchy.

=A1-B1-C1-D1 / 2  will not give the same result as =(A1-B1-C1-D1) / 2 will give.

 

All formulas in cells begin with the = symbol.  That's the basic starting point.

I debugged this with Ashley and the issue occurred because her organization migrated all email accounts to a new Office 365 tenant.  When that happens, behind the scenes, new accounts are created for the users even though the email accounts don't change.  Therefore, the Sways she had previously created were located in an account scheduled to be deleted that she was no longer technically the owner of.  She is now opening up the Sways and duplicating them into her 'new' account.  The Sway team is also exploring ways of supporting organizations that do this type of migration so that Sways can be moved behind the scenes by the tenant administrators.
