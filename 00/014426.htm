tina zhou msft say:

Hi Robert,

Based on your description, you currently use your Microsoft account to sign in Windows 10.

Please perform the following steps to delete your Microsoft account from this computer:

  1. Go to Settings > Accounts > Email & app accounts > your Microsoft account > Manage.
  2. In your Microsoft account web page, click your Device name for this computer under Devices > Remove PC > check I’m ready to remove this device: your computer name > Remove. And here comes the below message.
  3. Go to Settings > Accounts > Your info > Sign in with a local account instead, follow the instructions to create a local account, sign out and then sign in with your newly created local account.
  4. Go to Settings > Accounts > Email & app accounts > your Microsoft account > Remove > Yes.

And then go to Settings > Accounts > Access work or school to connect to work or school account if your work account is not listed there.

Moreover, to get contacts stored in your Microsoft account, please perform the following steps:

Go to People app > Add accounts > add your Microsoft account, in the Sign in to this device using your Microsoft account? dialog box, choose Just sign in to this app > Done.

swischner say:

Yes, I am sharing your opinion regarding "storing on OneDrive means there is no need for a backup". Of course there is. It's not just about malware attacks or technical failures. A wrong sync, any user error when editing or deleting, problems with your MS account ... all potential dangers for your data and having some kind of backup just lets me sleep better.

So...here are the options:

1. Install OneNote 2016 (or use existing OneNote 2010 / 2013) for Windows. It has a backup function which lets you save local copies of your notebooks even if they are stored on OneDrive (you just have to check the option "also backup notebooks shared on OneDrive"). Of course you  would have to open the notebooks you want to backup first from OneDrive which can be done with OneNote 2016 also; you may continue to work with the WIndows 10 app of course.

2. There is a trick to download notebooks from OneDrive just using a browser. Don't try to download the visible notebook link; there is no download option at all as you already noticed. If your notebooks are stored in a folder download that complete folder instead. All notebook files (.ONE files and the ONETOC2) will be included in the created zip file. You may also create a new folder on OneDrive (still using the browser, NOT the Windows explorer) and copy (not move!) all notebooks you want to backup into that new folder. You may just copy the visible link file; all invisible content files will follow. Then download that new folder to your PC. You may delete the created "backup folder" again afterwards or just delete the content. Of course for the next backup you have to copy your actual notebooks to that folder again first to get the most recent version.

Please note: These are methods to create backups. To restore them to OneNote again you absolutely need a desktop version of OneNote for Windows (NOT the Windows 10 app). Ideally one that comes with MS Office instead of the free OneNote 2016, especially if you used the method Nr. 2 as those are local OneNote files which cannot be handled by the free version.

But apart from possible inconveniences when it comes to restoring it is good to have a local backup at all, right? I am mostly using method #1 but for my most important notebooks I do addidtional backups using method #2

Use

Dim i As Long, j As Long, k As Long, m As Long
With ActiveDocument
    For i = 1 To .Tables.count
        With .Tables(i)
            For j = 1 To .Rows.count
                For k = 1 To .Columns.count
                    With .Cell(j, k).Range
                        For m = 1 To .Paragraphs.count
                            With .Paragraphs(m).Range.ParagraphFormat
                                .Borders(wdBorderLeft).LineStyle = wdLineStyleNone
                                .Borders(wdBorderRight).LineStyle = wdLineStyleNone
                                .Borders(wdBorderTop).LineStyle = wdLineStyleNone
                                .Borders(wdBorderBottom).LineStyle = wdLineStyleNone
                                .Borders(wdBorderHorizontal).LineStyle = wdLineStyleNone
                            End With
                        Next m
                    End With
                Next k
            Next j
        End With
    Next i
End With

greg maxey say: You get a type mismatch error because Selection.Range.Paragraphs is a collection and not a range object:

Sub ScratchMacro()
'A basic Word macro coded by Greg Maxey
Dim oBorder As Border
Dim oPar As Paragraph
Dim oRng As Range

  Set oRng = Selection.Range
  For Each oPar In oRng.Paragraphs
    For Each oBorder In oPar.Borders
      oBorder.LineStyle = wdLineStyleNone
    Next oBorder
  Next oPar
lbl_Exit:
  Exit Sub
End Sub
