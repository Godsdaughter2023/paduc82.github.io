joeu2004 say:

It returns FALSE because you are failing to handle the case when F2 does not match any. In other words:

=IF(F2="yes",G2*0.02+G2, IF(F2="late",G2*0.025+G2, IF(F2="no",H2, IF(F2="N/A",F2,missingValueIfNone))))

But if those are the only 4 possibilities, the formula should be:

=IF(F2="yes",G2*0.02+G2, IF(F2="late",G2*0.025+G2, IF(F2="no",H2,F2)))

Nevertheless, that does not explain why FALSE is returned when you say that F2 is "yes".

I suspect it is not. I suspect there are spaces before or after. For example, F2 might be "yes " (space after y-e-s).

Confirm with the formula =LEN(F2). It probably returns more than 3.

It is difficult to say how you should fix that, since we do not know the origin of "yes" etc.

And the "space" might be a non-breaking space (HTML nbsp; ASCII 160).

The "quick fix" might be to enter the following formula into a parallel column and copy down for the length of the data in column F:

=TRIM(SUBSTITUTE(F2,CHAR(160),""))

Then copy the parallel column and paste-special into column F.
