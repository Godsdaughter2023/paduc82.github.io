keith a. miller say:

My 2 Cents:

You can get a quick PDF by opening your JPEG for viewing, then selecting the print option & selecting "Microsoft Print to PDF" in the Printer selection box.

You said you like to lean.  Check your Scanner documentation to see if it came with OCR (Optical Character Recognition).  Rescanning your documents using OCR would give you editable text that could be formatted like any other document and then, as above, printed to a PDF file.

If you scanner didn't come with OCR software, at least you've got a good, targeted search term to look for freeware.

    When you say you "formatted" the disc?  What exactly do you mean?  When using a DVD-R or DVD+R disc, it is already formatted and you just simply burn the files to the disc and then if finalized it cannot write to that disc anymore.  If not finalized you can add files later but you may not be able to access the first files depending on how you have done it.
