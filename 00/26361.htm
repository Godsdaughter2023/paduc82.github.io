ken sheridan say: I'm having a little difficulty understanding your problem, and can only assume that you are using the term 'data entry' in a wider sense than that which is meant in Access by the DataEntry property of a form.  The property is badly named, and often gives rise to confusion.

1.  If you set the DataEntry property of a bound form to True (Yes) in form design view, whenever you open the form it will open at an empty new record as Scott says.  Any subforms which are linked to the parent form will also be empty of course by virtue of the relationships.

2.  If you wish to open a bound form which is also used for editing existing data at an empty new record, then this is done with a macro by setting the Data Mode argument of the OpenForm action to Add.  The form will then open at an empty new record.

3.  If you have a bound form open at an existing record, but wish to move its record pointer to an empty new record by means of a macro, then set the Record argument of the GotoRecord action to New.
