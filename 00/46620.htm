cmdrkeene say:

Consider this:

Every web page you open, the entire contents of it are not just shown to you on your screen.  The entire website contents are downloaded and saved to your hard drive.  All those pictures, videos, files, clips, text.  everything.  These are saved so that if you ever go back to the same site, the pictures and content will load up again from the cache (temporary files) immediately instead of having to be re-sent down the wire from the server.

This is just one example.  Caching also occurs when you open a folder and the icons have thumbnails, or you open your media library and the album art graphics show.  Or hundreds upon hundreds of other cases.

Windows cleans up these files periodically on set schedules, or when the cache reaches a certain age (expires) or the server it came from invalidates the data and chooses to send new data.

So yes it's very normal, and in most cases you can just ignore it and use your PC normally.
