suzanne s. barnhill say:

The "Style for following paragraph" for all the heading styles is set to Normal. This means that when you press Enter at the end of a heading in one of the built-in heading styles, even when it is part of a multilevel list, you will get a Normal paragraph rather than a paragraph in the same style.

When you want to create an outline based on headings, you have two options:

  1. If you are creating an outline that will later be fleshed out with body text paragraphs, you can work in Outline view. In this view, when you press Enter at the end of a heading paragraph, you get another paragraph in the same style. You can then switch back to Print Layout view to add text.
  2. If you're just creating an outline and will have nothing but heading levels, it's really better to use styles other than the heading styles, but since you've started with headings, you'll need to modify each heading style so that the "Style for following paragraph" is the same style (or the next lower-level heading, if preferred). You should also disable "Keep with next" for all but the top two or three heading levels, as this setting will cause problems with pagination.

In your case, you have a mixture of heading and non-heading styles, which is why you observe different behavior at different levels.

hansv mvp say:

In the Replace dialog, click More >>, then tick the check box 'Use wildcards'.

In the 'Find what' box, enter

 

([0-9]{3,3})-([0-9]{3,3})-([0-9]{4,4})

 

In the 'Replace with' box, enter

 

\1.\2.\3
