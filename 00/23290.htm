I ran into this problem when opening links in Outlook 2003 on Windows 7 64-bit. The links by default would open the 64-bit Internet Explorer 8 and therefore required third-party plug-ins would not be loaded.

I found a few places in the registry where the default IE is identified by its path and they need to be changed.

Copy the text below the dotted line to notepad (Windows Registry... should be first line copied) and save the file as “IEdefaults.reg” (without quotes) to your desktop. Double-click the file on your desktop to merge the registry information. Be sure to backup the registry beforehand, but all that’s being modified are the paths to Internet Explorer, where “ (x86)” is added next to Program Files. The locations can also be updated manually if you don’t want to create the registry file. Just find the locations in the registry and add “ (x86)” (without quotes).

Dear John,

I am an independent advisor, and I am trying to help other users in Community with my experience in Microsoft products.

In order to close Microsoft Account please do the following:
1. Go to Close your account http://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?LinkId=523898
2. When you're prompted to sign in to your account, double-check that it's the account you want to delete. If not, select Sign in with a different Microsoft account.
3. Check that the page shows the correct Microsoft account, and then click Next.
4. Read the list, and select the checkboxes to acknowledge you’ve read each item.
5. In the Select a reason drop-down list, choose the reason you're closing the account.
6. Select Mark account for closure.

When you tap or click the Mark account for closure button, Microsoft will wait 60 days before permanently deleting your Microsoft account in case you change your mind or need to access something on the account before it’s gone forever. After 60 days there is no way to retrieve any information.

For more information and other tips please check this support article https://support.microsoft.com/en-gb/help/12412/... Please make sure that you read it carefully, especially part about Before closing the account.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

Even in Word 97 you may not be able to open files from a CD. You'll need to copy them to the hard drive as Stefan suggests, but even then they will be marked as Read Only. Insert footnotes at end of chapter instead of end of page - Microsoft Community

wild child say:

Insert footnotes at end of chapter instead of end of page

I have Office Word 2007 and I use it to help me create epub books.  Since there's no way to tell where a page will end in the various epub readers out there I want the footnote (when needed) to insert at the end of the chapter instead of the bottom of the page.  I don't want them to insert at the end of the document either.  Is there any way to do this? 

Window 7 Home Premium

Office 2007

jay freedman say:

Page breaks are OK, as all pages between two section breaks are part of the same section.

What you cannot do is insert, say, a Continuous section break to change the number of columns or the orientation for part of a chapter, because that will create a new section and drop the endnotes there for the part before the section break.
