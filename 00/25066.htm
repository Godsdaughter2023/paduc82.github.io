faysal farooqui say:

- First, You have mentioned 5% (0.05 in number) as your annual rate, then use =0.05/365.

- Second, for IPMT function is used to calculate the Interest Amount only against the Loan Payment.

=IPMT(0.05,1,1,-B1) here 1 = PER value which means which days or which terms calculation it is. If you calculate the second days interest rate, then it would be 2, third days interest rate 3 and so on. For example if you wish to calculate the interest amount for the 73th days, then you have to use =IPMT(0.05/365,73,365,500000) if you again try to calculate the interest amount of 93th days then =IPMT(0.05/365,93,365,500000).

Now note that, the calculation of 73th day's amount is greater than 93th days. As because day after day, after your pay to bank per term (days), your loan amount becomes smaller. And that is why the interest amount for the first day is higher and day after day it becomes lower.
