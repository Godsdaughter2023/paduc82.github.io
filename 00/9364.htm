aaron zhu msft say:

Hi Jason,

 

If litigation hold wasn’t turned on for the mailboxes and the deleted items weren’t put under In-Place hold, the emails won’t be able to be recovered after users deleted them from the Recoverable Items folder at either user or administrator level. If litigation hold was turned on for the mailboxes or the deleted items were put under In-Place hold, administrators can use In-Place eDiscovery to recover the emails even if they were deleted from the Recoverable Items folder by users.

 

Since you’re using Office 365 Enterprise E1 plan, I’d like explain In-Place Hold and Litigation Hold features are not included in E1 plan. Office 365 Enterprise E3, E5, .etc plans include the features.

In-Place Hold and Litigation Hold.

Exchange Online Service Description.

 

Additionally, it’s not feasible to prevent user from deleting emails from the Recoverable Items folder. And given the situation, I suggest you upgrade your subscription from E1 to E3/E5 to get the In-Place Hold and Litigation Hold features.
