charles kenyon say:

I do not have a Mac and am writing from my experiences with the Windows version. That version is similar, but the two are not the same. While I used Word on a Mac, that was a long time ago. Nevertheless, I hope that I can be of some help.

1. There is no Word 2017. There is Word 365 which currently incorporates Word 2016.

2. The Mac version, to my understanding, does not currently support saving building blocks. I could be wrong on that.

The formatting of a TOC is generally controlled by the TOC styles.

See TOC Tips and Tricks by Suzanne Barnhill, MVP.

If you save those styles in your template, the formatting controlled by them will also be preserved.

I believe, again may be mistaken, that you can save AutoText in the Mac version. You could save your TOC as an AutoText entry. It would not be available in the TOC gallery, but it would be available.

OTH, if you have access to Word 2007 or later in Windows, you can save as a building block. Again, though, the styles are the key to most formatting of a TOC. If edited and saved in a template as a TOC building block, it should be available through the TOC gallery (assuming that gallery is already available) on Mac versions. Many Word features can be created/stored on the Windows version and used in Mac versions. I know of no list of these features.
