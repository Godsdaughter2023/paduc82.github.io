Which XBox should I buy? - Microsoft Community

wench2 say:

Which XBox should I buy?

HI guys, I want to buy XBox recently. I know that there are xbox one and xbox x. I prefer xbox kinect games, I also knew that microsoft have no longer maintain kinect. Anyone has good suggestion? Thanks.

arminatorx say:

...I know that there are xbox one and xbox x. I prefer xbox kinect games, ...

I'm afraid you're out of luck for Kinect and Kinect games.

If you buy a Xbox One console new nowadays, the consoles don't have a dedicated Kinect port. 

So you not only need the Kinect sensor, but also an Adapter. Both devices aren't produced anymore, and are also sold out in basically all stores. 

The only way to get Kinect and an Adapter would be used devices from eBay and the likes, and usually for horrendous prices. 

Also, there aren't a lot of Kinect games for the Xbox One. While Kinect was mandatory in 2013 when the Xbox One originally launched, there are some Kinect only games like Kinect Sports Rivals, or Disney Fantasia. And some games use Kinect for additional function, like taunting Zombies in Dead Rising 3. 

And the Fitness app that could use the pulse sensing functions and body tracking for a fitness workout has been discontinued. 

Then, after Microsoft made Kinect optional, there are only a handful of Indie games that use Kinect, and another Disney game for kids.

So as much as I love Kinect, due to the lack of support I can only recommend to not waste your money on Kinect nowadays. 

Basically, you now have to decide between getting a Xbox One S or Xbox One X.

If you do have a 4K TV and want to have 4K graphics, then there is no way around the Xbox One X. 

Even if you only have a "standard" 1080P TV, the Xbox One X can downsample 4K video, to make Xbox One X enhanced games look "smoother", by rendering a 4K image, and then compressing it to 1080p, which will have less "jaggy" lines compared to a natively rendered 1080p image like on the Xbox One S. 

However, the Xbox One X is of course more expensive than the Xbox One S. 

If you don't care about 4K resolution, you can still get HDR enhanced game graphics on the Xbox One S. Everything that works on the Xbox One X will also work on the Xbox One S, but "only" in 1080p.

As for BluRay, both consoles will be able to play 4K BluRays in 4K. They will of course downsample to 1080p, if you don't own a 4K TV yet. 

So, to sum it up:

If you REALLY have money to burn, get a Xbox One X new, and try to find your Kinect sensor and Kinect Adapter used. 

If you have enough money for 4K hardware, or plan to upgrade to 4K in the forseeable future get the Xbox One X. 

And if you want to be more sensible about spending money, and don't have a 4K display, simply get the Xbox One S.

zigzag3143 (ms -mvp) say:

Unless you have a printer that needs Teredo you probably do not need it But.....

This is a known problem if Zone alarm is installed.  If you have it installed remove it at least to check

To turn off Teredo tunneling interface 6to4 open an elevated command prompt and type

netsh interface terredo set state disabled.


To Re-install it
  1. Click on Start.
  2. Type Device Manager in search box and open it.
  3. Click the Action tab at the top and click add legacy hardware
    then click next and next again it will then scan and find nothing click next on the screen after that.
    Wait a minute and you will see a list of hardware appear, scroll down and choose network adapters then click next, then from the left column choose Microsoft then in the right hand column scroll down and choose Microsoft Teredo Tunneling Adapter, then click next and this will install it.
    To check just make sure it is set to show hidden devices, right click on device manager on the right and click view then show hidden devices.
If there is no "add legacy hardware" option, you need to plug in the device that uses tunneling.
