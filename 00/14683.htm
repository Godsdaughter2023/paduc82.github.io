jeeped say:
You could use the largely undocumented DATEDIF() function, parsing the yyyymm dates and comparing then to the start and end of the fiscal year.


D1:M1 contain the years (e.g. 2003, 2004, etc) with a custom number format of "F"0.

The formula in C2 is,

=DATEDIF(DATE(LEFT(A2,4),RIGHT(A2,2),1),DATE(LEFT(B2,4),RIGHT(B2,2)+1,1),"m")

... which can be filled down. The formula in D2 is,

=IFERROR(DATEDIF(MAX(DATE(D$1,9,1),DATE(LEFT($A2,4),RIGHT($A2,2),1)),MIN(EDATE(DATE(D$1,9,1),12),DATE(LEFT($B2,4),RIGHT($B2,2),1)),"m"),0)

... which can be filled both right and down.

Please note that some unreliable results may be returned with DATEDIF(), particularly with counting days around leap years (which you are not doing). If the number of dates is absolutely mission critical, you should at least know what you are up against. See the following,

The DATEDIF Worksheet Function

I have assumed the start date and end date are properly formatted dates.

 

I have assumed your earliest start date is 2004 and the start date and end date are in D1 and E1 respectively. This formula does months in fiscal year Sep 2004 - Aug 2005

 

 

=SUMPRODUCT(--(DAY(ROW(INDIRECT($D1&":"&$E1)))=1)*(MONTH(ROW(INDIRECT($D1&":"&$E1)))>=9)*(YEAR(ROW(INDIRECT($D1&":"&$E1)))=2004))+SUMPRODUCT(--(DAY(ROW(INDIRECT($D1&":"&$E1)))=1)*(MONTH(ROW(INDIRECT($D1&":"&$E1)))<=8)*(YEAR(ROW(INDIRECT($D1&":"&$E1)))=2005))

FAQ: Using notebooks from SkyDrive

 

1.      Can I open multiple notebooks?

OneNote mobile for Windows Phone supports opening multiple notebooks. There aretwo ways to open notebooks:

 

Send yourself an invite from your PC. In OneNote 2010 on your PC click on theShare Tab->Share This Notebook, then click onE-mail others about this notebook. Enter an email address reachable from your phone. On the phone, open the email, then open the first link.

 

Alternatively, on the phone, ensure that the Internet Explorer settings are for "mobile browser", then navigate tooffice.live.com. Find your notebook, then open it. When the IE browser is set to mobile browser, the notebook will be opened in the OneNote mobile client.

 

2.      Can I sync Section Groups with my phone?

OneNote mobile for WP7 does not support section groups natively, but there is a workaround. You need to open a link to each section group you want to use on your phone as if they were notebooks. Similarly to opening a notebook by sending yourself a link (see above), you can send yourself a link to the section group. On your PC, right-click on the section group tab and click Copy link to Section Group. Then paste this link in a new email and send it to yourself. On the phone, open that link.

 

3.      Can I use multiple accounts/live-id?

You shouldn't do this. There's partial support on the PC, but I wouldn't recommend it on the PC or on your phone. The good news is that you shouldn't need to. If you have notebooks that you want to access that are not owned by the primary account you can simply share them from that other account to the primary one. You will then be able to open them using the methods above. The sharing currently needs to be done on the web - ideally using your PC. Login to office.live.com using your other account. Once you find the notebook you want to share, move the mouse over the link More-> Share->View Permissions. From there you should be able to access the permissions to the folder and share it to your other account.

 

Just be mindful that if you change the permissions you are changing the permissions of the entire folder. Assuming yourPersonal (Web) notebook is under yourMy Documents folder, changing the permissions will change the permissions to the entireMy Documents folder. If you own both account that is fine, but if you intend to share only that notebook with someone else you might want to create a separate folder to share it in.

barry xie msft say:

Hi KRBS Petar Koychev,

 

The contacts under Directory are contacts in your organization’s global address list. It’s not feasible to directly export them as a CSV file in Outlook Web App.

 

If the number is not large, you can peform the below steps to add them as your contacts and then export them:

1. Select the contacts you want to export under Directory.

2. Choose List > Add to a custom contact group.

3. Select Your contacts folder > find the contact group.

4. Choose the contact group, select the contacts in the contact group and choose Add to contact.

5. After that, choose these contacts and export them to a CSV file.
