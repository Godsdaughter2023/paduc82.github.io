john_barnett say:

Hello Stepheneverton,

 

You have allowed them access to your PC so, basically, they could have installed anything without you knowing. Sadly, there is only one piece of advice and that is to re-install Windows and all of your programs. I know it is drastic, but it is the only way to ensure that your system is completely clean.

 

If your PC came with Windows pre-installed you either have a recovery disk or else the PC manufacturer included a recovery partition on your hard drive. Check your PC manufacturer's website (or the PC user guide) for information on reverting back to factory conditions.

 

Alternatively, if you have previously used backup software to image your hard drive (recommended) then you could just as easily re-image your hard drive from that previous backup image. obviously any programs that you installed after creating an image will need to be installed onto your system.

 

Also, as Palcouk has pointed out, make sure you change all of your passwords. If you do online banking it is also wise to notify your bank that a security breach on your PC has occurred. Make sure you also change your online banking password too.
