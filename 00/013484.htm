To have an idea on what is the purpose of To, Cc and Bcc field. Refer to the details below.

To: This is where you put the email address of the person whom you want to receive your message.

Cc: stands for carbon copy. Anyone listed in the Cc: field of a message receives a copy of that message when you send it. All other recipients of that message can see that the person you designated as a Cc: recipient received a copy of the message.

Bcc: stands for blind carbon copy. This is similar to the Cc: feature, except that Bcc: recipients are invisible to all the other recipients of the message (including other Bcc: recipients). For example, if you send a message To: *** Email address is removed for privacy *** and Bcc: *** Email address is removed for privacy ***, then johndoe sees himself as the message's only recipient. Unclesam, on the other hand, is "in the know" he can see that you sent the message To: johndoe, and that you blind-copied him. mihael_8532 say:

Hi Mihael_8532,

Thank you for posting in the forum.

I understand that you have updated your Nokia Lumia 820 to 8.1 version and since the update you are not able to connect your phone with your home Wi-Fi connection.

First of all, I would suggest to turn off Wi-Fi connection on your phone and reset your home Wi-Fi (turn off router for longer than 30 seconds).

Later reboot your phone (press and hold power button + volume down) and try to connect phone again.

If the issue persists, I would perform hard reset of your phone, to make sure that system works without any problems, and then set it up again with your Microsoft account. You can as well do an extra copy on your PC hard drive. Of course, please make sure that you backup your data. More info you can find here:

http://www.windowsphone.com/en-us/how-to/wp8/basics/back-up-my-stuff

http://www.windowsphone.com/en-us/how-to/wp8/basics/reset-my-phone

Then please set up your phone with your Microsoft account.

Thanks for your contribution to the forums!

Kind Regards,

Michal TR

I got it to work, finally. I had to change the Security Mode and Encryption Mode of my wireless modem.

What I did was to change the security mode from "WPA2" to "WPA-WPA2" and Encryption Mode from "AES" to "TKIP-AES".

Btw, I didn't have to reset the phone or anything.
