james vinyard say: You can go to IFIXIT.COM and do a search for "Microsoft Surface Pro" and they have a tear down/repair guide that lists all the components and from there, you can get the information for the battery packs to search with Samsung for the details on them.

They can be replaced but only by somebody who knows what they are doing and the tablet is classed almost non-repairable.  You will see what I mean when you look at the guide.

Number of cycles "unknown" but for the number of years, compared to similar battery packs, they last for 4 to 6 years from what I can find but some of these types of batteries can retain a full charge one day and then go dead the following day.

On paper, rechargeable batteries are forecasted to last 10 years but that is not a guaranteed life expectancy.  I have worked on a 14 year old laptop which had the original battery in it and it maintained a full charge and the laptop was used every day.

Chances are, your tablet will give out before the batteries do.
