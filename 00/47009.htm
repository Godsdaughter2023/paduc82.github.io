Xbox Music Primer - how it works

As background, I have been a loyal user and fan of Zune since V1. I have 10,000 meticulously maintained music tracks in my library, hosted on a server. I listen to about 2000 in shuffle on a regular basis. It is my opinion that the current version of Zune offers the best music listening and management experience around. This is because 1) it offers complete control and easy access to locally stored files; 2) has a beautiful now playing screen; and 3) streams music without fault.

I have been using Xbox Music ("XBM") since its release. I wanted to use it, I want it to succeed. It has fallen fantastically short of my expectations. In this post I want to point out where it falls short, offer my methods on how I am using it and pose questions about areas I am unclear about.

Falls Short:
First, there is no control over locally stored files. to edit ID3 data, add album art, etc. you must use File Manager and a third party ID3 tag editor (I use MP3Tag).

Second, the now playing screen only *rarely* and I mean VERY rarely will display artist images, album art, etc. in the now playing screen. More often than not I will see only geometric shapes and a muted color changing palette.

Third, streaming music frequently just stops playing and returns me to the start screen.

These can be easily over come by allowing users to select which files XBM should prefer: Local or streaming. We should also be able to link a streaming file and local file if they are shown as duplicates, and XBM shoudl dedupe the My Muisc list.

How I Use:
The first problem I had was figuring out what the Streaming icon ((*)), the cloud icon and no icon meant. As far as I can tell, its the following;

NO ICON = locally stored music

((*)) STREAMING ICON = Music is avaialble for you to stream. This may consist of music that you have, in the past, downloaded with a Zune Pass, or used the "Add to My Music" functionality in XBM to listen. The music is not stored on your machine and will only play with a network connection.

CLOUD ICON = Music that is available to you becasue it is sync'd with the cloud.

When I first booted my Surface and then my Laptop into Windows8 and XBM I had no idea why I was seeing albums and tracks that I had not listened to for years. The reason is that as a Zune user XBM pulled my streaming/download (not purchased) history and put those in my library as streaming files. Presently, there is not method to filter by ownership type as there is in Zune. So I literally started from scratch. These steps are tedious and would be entirely unnecessary if XBM included a method to filter My Music
1) removed the My Music location from my library
2) Disabled XBox Music Cloud in Preferences
3) Disabled Music Pass Downloads
4) Manually deleted all Streaming Tracks that remained in My Music (Ctrl+A - then Delete). This merely removes the reference to your straming access from My Music - no files are actually deleted (as far as I can tell)

At this point your My Music should be empty. To begin repopulating I added my music folder back into my windows library. If you want, you can start by adding the streaming or XBox Music. I have many obscure tracks that I listen to and I wanted those available off-line.

After that I let XBM add metadata. That's right. I did this to try and enjoy the now playing screen and decided to just live with the results. The problem is that XBM often incorrectly retags files. This is usually with independent or less well known artists/tracks. Microsoft needs to add the funcitonlity included with Zune which allows a user to over write this information.

So at this point, in My Music I have all my local music and things looks good.

I then sync'd with the Xbox Music Cloud. I have no idea what happens here. Some of my local tracks got a cloud icon, some got the streaming icon, some did not change. There was no pattern as some music avialale in the store did not get an icon and others did.

The main issue is that XBM defaults by playing the streaming version. There is also no way to remove the link to the streaming version. so if that track fails to play, but you have it stored locally you are hosed.

When you search the store for a song or album and "Add to My Music" this merely puts a reference in the cloud and the track/album appears in My Music with a streaming icon. the file is not saved to your PC **UNLESS** you enabled the "Music Pass Downloads" in Settings. The benefit of this is that when you add to My Music it is automatically available on all your devices. Unlike Zune where it was only available on the device you downloaded it to. to remove it from any of your devices just swipe it and select delete.

A simpler and more intuitive function would be to ask at time of download/adding if you want this avialable on all devices or just the one you are currently accessing from and make it so you can add it to all later.

Similarly, when you buy a track it downloads to the device you bought it on, but only the streaming version is available on other devices.

PLAYLISTS - playlists are very different from the 20th century playlist. In XBM they are stored in the cloud so only music that is avialable in the store is avaialble to put on a playlist. Similarly, they are only playable on XBM devices. so I cannot put a playlist I created on my Surface on my Zune HD. I can however, put a playlist I created on my Zune on my Surface. For those using Playlists this is going to suck and be a hard pill to swallow. That said, my experience with them has been super good. I created a playlist on my Xbox and was pleased to see it on my phone when I was driving to work.

This seems long and complicated becasue it is. I think the short story is that any track you listen to anywhere using Xbox Music is available on any device you use. It is a bold step for Micorsoft and super risky. Zune was and is a great piece of software. I am unaware why they had to change make a new system rather than just rename Zune and add the cloud features on top of what they already had.

In other news, I hope this is helpful in some way to someone.
