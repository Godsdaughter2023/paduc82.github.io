abi99 (2) say:

3 UK ship their SIM cards (UICC) just with a USIM application installed. The legacy SIM application (software) is not installed on their UICC (hardware). That is a speciality of 3 UK, because not even all 3 subsidiaries worldwide do it this way. Your Nokia does not know about USIM and expects a SIM, which cannot be selected/started on a UICC from 3 UK. Therefore, hat SIM is rejected. Newer Nokia Mobile Phones – even those without WCDWA – support the USIM.

As recommended by 93tid already, the solution is to go for another network operator. More about this and the accompanying specifications…
