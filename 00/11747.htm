Typing text outside Table cells. - Microsoft Community

davidcotterell say:

Typing text outside Table cells.

How do I type text either above, at side or below a Table. After inserting Table cursor is " locked " inside
suzanne s. barnhill say:

Note that dragging the table by its handle (even just nudging it a short distance) makes it wrapped; that is, it accomplishes the same thing as changing the "Text wrapping" (on the Table tab of Table Properties) from None to Around. This is important to know because many problems with tables arise from their being wrapped when you think they are inline.

Other important things to know:

  1. If the table is at the very top of the document, with no text paragraph preceding it, you can generate a text paragraph above it by pressing Enter at the very beginning of the first table cell. To make sure you are at the very beginning of the document (since Enter anywhere else will just insert a paragraph break inside the table), press Ctrl+Home.
  2. You can get out of the table by pressing the right arrow at the end of the last cell, but this should not be necessary, as there is always a text paragraph (even if empty) below the table, and you can click in it. If you have nonprinting characters (or at least paragraph marks) displayed, this is easier to see.
  3. You must make the table wrapped if you want to wrap text around it (above, below, and to the side), but if you just want to type text beside a table, the easiest way is to insert another column, remove any borders, merge cells if there are more than one, and use the added cell to type text that will appear to be outside the table.

marysully say:

Make sure Show/Hide is on. Select the Table by clicking on the 4-headed arrow at top left of table. With Table selected maneuver it to any new location. To type above the table move table down and then click in area above table. To type on side or below table, move table and then click and type.

timothy d_606 say:

as far as the printer goes, drivers you get from Windows itself, or windows update, usually only support printing. If your printer also has a scanner and/or fax capability, you'll probably need the manufacturer's drivers for that.

for chipsets, I've only found drivers to be a performance enhancer. Your computer will run fine without them, but games, video editing, high-end audio production, won't work as well as they could.

Chipsets are fairly standardized and all the essential functions are identical from chipset to chipset, therefore standardized drivers from Microsoft that are packaged with windows are sufficient for installing the OS and getting it up and running. For other non recognized hardware, just leave it be for the moment, and keep checking back with the manufacturer.

marc_sp say: In reply to deleted message

I found another workaround...

When attempting to connect two walls together, first bring them as close to each other as possible without causing them to "auto-join."  (If they auto-join then click on the 'undo' icon and try again.)  (Note: Like others, I cannot figure out how to disable this 'auto-join' function.  I think it has something to do with the "wall" shape/object more than something to do with the 'drawing settings'/'visual aids' on the page.)

Once that is accomplished center your screen on where the joint needs to be and then zoom in 800%.  (Sometimes it helps to zoom to 200%, center the screen, then zoom to 400%, center the screen again, and then zoom to 800%.)

Now grab the wall and bring its corner together with the corner of the other wall.  (I am using Visio 2013, so it draws a little green box on the connection point when the two walls meet.  It is critical that this green box is perfectly centered (over the connection point) before you let go of the mouse button.  (Note: Other versions of Visio may have some other visual component for lining things up.  Sadly, I can't testify to what visual clues are present because I only have Visio 2013 on my PC.)   Let go of the mouse button and your walls should be joined.

Validation: Check the length of the two walls that you joined to make sure they didn't change length.  If they did, click on the 'undo' icon and try again.  It may take a few attempts.  Once you've done this a few times it goes much faster.
