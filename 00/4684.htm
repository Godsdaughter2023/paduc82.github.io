Let's say you have birth dates in D2, D3, ...

The formula for the date the employee in row 2 turns 65 is =EDATE(D2,65*12)

The year of this event is =YEAR(D2)+65

These formulas can be filled/copied down to the rows below.

Sync times will depend on how many mail messages you have, the size of each message and, of course, your network connection speed.

First, are you storing more than a few hundred messages in your Exchange Inbox? If so, this can cause speed issues. Your Inbox is synchronized more frequently than most other folders because this is where new mail is arriving. Use Outlook Web Access (OWA) or Outlook for Windows to move most of your messages out of the Inbox into other mail folders outside of your Inbox. Do not create sub-folders within the Inbox. Do you receive a lot of attachments and are they sometimes sizable? You might try enabling Outlook to receive headers only. This means you'll only download a list of messages but not the actual messages themselves. You'll download the messages only when you preview them or view them.
