To remove the blank line: (1) click the ¶ button on the Home tab to turn on nonprinting characters so you can see the paragraph mark there, (2) select the paragraph mark and press the Delete key.

After the tables are combined into one table, you can split them apart: (1) click in the cell that will become the first cell of the second table, (2) on the Table Tools > Layout tab, in the Merge group, click Split Table. The blank line will appear above the cell containing the cursor.

The character in your post is a unicode line separator. If they are all the same, a Replace All of^u8232 with nothing should do the trick. Whenever text goes through HTML (or even plain-text email), any spaces beyond a single one will be converted to nonbreaking spaces. In most fonts, a nonbreaking space is represented by a small circle (identical to the degree symbol), but it could be that in a given font it would be represented by a square instead.
