john w. vinson say:

Your lines

NewStartDate = DateAdd("d", "1", "FiscalYearEndDate")
   NewFiscalYearEndDate = DateAdd("yyyy", 1, "FiscalYearEndDate")
are pretty clearly wrong: the second and third arguments to DateAdd should be a Number and a Date respectively, and you're passing two strings!

The "1" will (probably) be correctly converted, but the text string "FiscalYearEndDate" is just that - it's not a date, and it's not a reference to a field in the recordset. You've defined a recordset but you're not using it. Do you mean

DateAdd("d", 1, rstMyCompanyInfo.FiscalYearEndDate)

perhaps?

 

john w. vinson say:

aha... You need to wrap the date value in # date delimiters; it seems that Access is interpreting 4/30/11 as a division expression (4 divided by 30 divided by 11, giving a time a few seconds after midnight December 30 1899).

Try

  strSQL = "UPDATE tblMyCompanyInfo SET StartDate = #" & Formst(DateAdd("d", 1, [FiscalYearEndDate]), "yyyy-mm-dd") & ",  FiscalYearEndDate = #" & Format(DateAdd("yyyy", 1, [FiscalYearEndDate]), "yyyy-mm-dd") & "#;"

The format may not be essential but it will prevent dd/mm vs. mm/dd ambiguities; Access expects mm/dd.

ActiveDocument.Bookmarks("A").Range.Tables(1).Delete
