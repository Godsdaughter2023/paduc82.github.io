Lumia 920 (and other WP8 phones?) - stringing multiple - Microsoft Community

grschinon say:

Lumia 920 (and other WP8 phones?) - stringing multiple albums together in a single playlist

Technical Level : Intermediate

Summary

Details

It took me a while and some research to find out how to do this so I thought I'd share.

 

The idea is to string multiple albums together (eg. all those of the same band in the order of their release) into a single playlist so that you can enjoy hours of continuous music without having to grab hold of the phone and start the next album when one finishes playing. OK, I'm lazy, I admit it, but technology is supposed to do what we want, right?

 

In order to get this working you'll need an app called "Stop the Music!" from the marketplace. It does all sorts of things that you might want to consider buying the full version for, but if this is all you'll be using it for then the free (ad supported) version will do fine. Get it here.

 

Edit 06-Feb-13: I would suggest not pinning the live "Stop" tile of Stop The Music! to your homescreen. I've done this a couple of times and each time it has resulted in other live tiles no longer being updated (when your phone has been on charge all night and Battery Level's live tile and lock screen notification both still say 52%. you begin to smell a rat) and sometimes the phone freezes up. More often than usual, that is.

 

The way the music player works in Windows Phone 8, as far as I can see, is with the "now playing" queue. Whenever you select an album to play, its contents is copied to this queue and playback commences. You can have anything played back simply by adding it to this queue. This includes albums, individual titles, entire collections from an artist... When you stop playback, the queue is kept in memory, which explains why the latest song you were listening to when you stopped playback still appears in the bar at the top of the screen when you press the Vol+ or Vol- button on the side of the phone.

 

The only way to clear this "now playing" queue under normal circumstances is to power the phone off. Another, more practical way is to use a third-party app to clear it for you, and that is one of the things that "Stop the Music!" will do. We need to do this so that we can start with a clean slate when we're building the playlist up.

 

So, with no further ado:

 

1) Use "Stop the Music!" to cease playback and clear the "now playing" queue.

 

1b) optional - use the volume rocker to bring the volume down to zero. When you add the first album to the "now playing" queue, playback of that album will start immediately. If that's what you want anyway then that's fine, you can leave things as they are. If you only want to start listening once you've built the finished playlist or if there are people around you that you don't want to disturb, then you'll want to silence it.

 

2) Go into the "Music + Videos" app, flip to the "collection" page if you're not already there, tap on "music", flip to the "artists" page, locate the artist whose albums you want to add to the playlist and then tap on the artist's name. Don't tap on the "play" symbol to the left of the artist's name as that will just place all that artist's songs in alphabetical order into the "now playing" queue, and you'd have to start again from step 1. Flip to the "albums" page if you're not there already:

 

 

3) Long press on the album that you first want to add to the playlist, wait for the menu to appear and select "add to now playing":

 

 

This will start playing that album and show you the music player controls:

 

 

4) Press the "←" back button to go back to the list of the artist's albums.

 

5) For each additional album that you want to add to the playlist, long press on it and select "add to now playing".

 

6) Press the "←" back button twice to get back to the "collection" page of the "Music + Videos" app, then tap on the album art of the song currently playing. This will take you back to the music player control screen. Tap on the titles of the tracks that appear below the album art. This will show you the "now playing" queue with a "save" icon at the bottom:

 

 

7) Tap on the save icon and enter the name you want to give to the playlist:

 

 

8) Tap the "→" button. Your playlist is saved!

 

From now on, when you go into the "Music + Videos" app and tap on "music", you can flip to the "playlists" page and the playlist that you just saved will appear in there:

It would help to see the code in the Next button, but what I would suggest is the following at the top of the Next button code module:

Me.Dirty = False   'commit record

strSQL = "INPUT INTO tblTrailerHistory (RecordNumber, TrailerID, EmployeeID, etc)" & _

               "SELECT  RecordNumber, TrailerID, EmployeeID, etc. " & _

               "FROM [Trailer Data] " & _

               "WHERE RecordNumber = " & Me.Recordnumber & ";"

CurrentDB.Execute strSQL, dbFailOnError

I would also add two fields to tblTrailerHistory

TrailerHistoryID (PK Autonumber)

HistoryWhen Date/Time (set default value to =Now())

BOL

FB

LoadNumber

Comments

So, each time you need to add an event you would add it as a record int he Events table.
