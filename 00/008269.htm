boyshanks say: After days of searching and investigation I have answered my own question!!!!  This answer will put to rest EVERY question of this nature for which there has been no answer until now.  Sorry in advance for the length. 

I am using desktop Outlook 2011 on a MacBook Pro update 10.7.5 Lion.

If you have a GoDaddy hosted domain ( abcxyz.net) and have created an email for that domain (*** Email address is removed for privacy ***) the chances are very great that you have a POP account.  

Why does this matter?  

Well, in Outlook for Mac 2011, the POP emails for some reason are grouped together under the ON MY COMPUTER folder that you will see in the pane on the left.  (Reference: http://bit.ly/hnvMDU )

(If you can't see the On My Computer folder go to Outlook > Preferences> General.  There UNCLICK --> "Hide On My Computer folders."  The On My Computer folder will now appear.) 

If you have both IMAP and POP accounts, coming into your desktop Outlook 2011 for Mac, the left pane will look like this -- 

INBOX 
  • *** Email address is removed for privacy *** (IMAP)
  • *** Email address is removed for privacy ***   (IMAP)
  • On My Computer (all POPs) 
So when setting up my GoDaddy email I was expecting another email box to appear and when it didn't, I thought the set up had not occurred.  The setup had been successful but since it was POP, my Godaddy emails are landing in the On My Computer folder.  The problem this creates is SENDING emails from my GoDaddy email via Outlook is not possible.  

I don't understand why all POP email addresses are grouped together under On My Computer.  But you don't like that and neither do I.  To change that you will need an IMAP email address from GoDaddy.  

To get an IMAP email at GoDaddy, at this time, you need to purchase an unlimited storage email plan which at the present is $4.99 / month billed on an annual basis at $59.88. So if you have multiple (≤10) GoDaddy email addresses that you want in Outlook 2011 desktop, then that could be the plan for you.  

If you don't want to do this, just know that your POP emails will arrive under On My Computer but to send from your GoDaddy email address you will need to access your GoDaddy email at the server at:   email.secureserver.net.  There you can compose and send your emails.  

Whew!!! I am so glad to have figured this out.  It was driving me crazy!!  I hope this helps you too!

I am not seeing this kind of behavior myself.  Could you review your Modeling Preferences to see what options are selected - this is on the Database ribbon, in the Manage group.  When you select Modelin Preferences, a dialog will open.
