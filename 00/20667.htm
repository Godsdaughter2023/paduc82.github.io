ken sheridan say: I strongly concur with what Scott has said.  In the example you have given all non-key columns bar Name are transitively dependant on the key.  The table is consequently not normalized to Third Normal Form (3NF) which requires that all non-key attributes are determined solely by the whole of the primary key.  The table is therefore wide open to the risk of update anomalies, and should be decomposed into at least two, and in all probability more, related tables, each of which are fully normalized.

Without knowing what is being modelled more than that cannot be said, and we'd need to know more of the underlying logical model to advise further.

First, no you DON'T want to use keywords as field names. That is not proper design. A survey requires a specific structure, it doesn't appear you are using a proper structure for a questionnaire. scott a. helmers say:

Your issue with comments is related to the version of Visio. In Visio 2010, comments are actually attached to the page, even if you select a shape and then add a comment to it. It's very misleading.

Fortunately, in Visio 2013 and 2016, when you add a comment to a shape it is actually attached to the shape and moves with the shape. You can also add a comment to a page, by the way, and a comment balloon will appear in the upper left corner of the page. 

But the bottom line is that in order to get a visible comment balloon and actually have it attached to a shape, you need to upgrade at least to Visio 2013.
