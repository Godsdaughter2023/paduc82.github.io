lemp say:
The HP Photosmart C3100 series printers do not have a parallel port.  Your description of what appears in the "Printer" window, however, shows that your computer is directing the print job to the parallel port (LPT1).  That's why you don't see any output.

Assuming that this printer was correctly set up and printing at some point, do the following:

  • Open Printers and Faxes
  • Right-click on the icon for your C3100 and select Properties (BTW, do you have more than one icon for this printer?  If so, try selecting each of the icons in turn; one of them may be correctly configured)
  • Click on the Ports tab
  • For now, do not click anywhere in the list of ports other than the scroll bar on the right side of the list.  Use the scroll bar to find the line that is checked. 
  • If the line that is checked says LPT1 in the first column, continue scrolling until you see a line that has "DOT4_001" in the first column.  The last digit may be something other than 1. 
  • Assuming that you (a) find a DOT4 entry and (b) you don't have any other USB-connected printers, put a check in this box and then clear the box for the LPT1 line
  • OK your way out
  • Reboot the computer
  • Try printing a test page.

Post back if you don't have a DOT4 port or if there is anything else that doesn't fit the above directions.

Step 1: Check to see if the “Internet Connection Sharing” service is running. To do so, follow these steps.

d.      Click the  Sharing tab, and then select the “Allow other network users to connect through this computer’s Internet connection” check box.
