hansv mvp say:

Let's say the dates are in B1:AZS1, and the values in B2:AZS2.

Enter the start date in a cell, say in B4, and the end date in another cell, say in B5.

On the Formulas tab of the ribbon, click Define Name.

Enter XValues as name, and enter the following formula in Refers to:

=OFFSET($B$1,0,MATCH($B$4,$B$1:$AZS$1,0)-1,1,$B$5-$B$4+1)

Click OK. Excel will automatically add the sheet name to the formula.

Repeat the above steps, but with the name YValues and the formula

=OFFSET($B$2,0,MATCH($B$4,$B$1:$AZS$1,0)-1,1,$B$5-$B$4+1)

Create a chart based on - say - B1:E2. We are going to modify it to be dynamic.

With the chart selected, click Select Data in the Data group of the Design tab of the ribbon.

For all chart types except XY Scatter chart:

Select the series, then click Edit under Legend Entries (Series).

Clear the Series Values box, and enter the formula ='Sheet1'!YValues where Sheet1 is the sheet name.

Click OK.

Click Edit under Horizontal (Category) Axis Labels.

Clear the Axis label range box, and enter the formula ='Sheet1'!XValues where Sheet1 is the sheet name.

Click OK.

Remark: Excel will replace the sheet name with the workbook name.

For an XY Scatter chart:

Select the series, then click Edit under Legend Entries (Series).

Clear the Series X Values box, and enter the formula ='Sheet1'!XValues where Sheet1 is the sheet name.

Clear the Series Y Values box, and enter the formula ='Sheet1'!YValues where Sheet1 is the sheet name.

Click OK.

Remark: Excel will replace the sheet name with the workbook name.

Finally click OK.

Now change the dates in B4 and/or B5 to see the chart change automatically.
