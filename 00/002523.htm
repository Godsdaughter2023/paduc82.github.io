time/date = total hours calculation - Microsoft Community

tammy0419 say:

time/date = total hours calculation

I am trying to calculate how many hours it takes to complete a multi-day task - example...  Task is handed off to first department on 05/09/12 at 1550pm, and is completed by another department on 05/12/12 at 1350pm.  I need a formula which will calculate how many hours this took taking into account the dates of start and completion.

 

Can anyone help me with a complete formula for this?    Thank you.

joeu2004 say: Tammy wrote:

I am trying to calculate how many hours it takes to complete a multi-day task - example...  Task is handed off to first department on 05/09/12 at 1550pm, and is completed by another department on 05/12/12 at 1350pm.  I need a formula which will calculate how many hours this took taking into account the dates of start and completion.   Can anyone help me with a complete formula for this?


First, I assume that start and end date/time are in one cell each.  For example, A1 is 5/9/12 15:50, and A2 is 5/12/12 13:50.  (Note, with 13:50 and 15:50, "PM" is both superfluous and incorrect.)  If that is not the case, we need to make adjustments before proceeding.

Second, there are two common ways (among others) to do the computation, depending on what you mean by "calculate how many hours".

The simplest method:

=A2-A1

formatted as Custom [h]:mm.  The square brackets permit the number of hours to exceed 24.  With your example, the result will display 70:00.

Note that that stores the number of hours as an Excel time serial number; that is, as a fraction of 1 day.

Alternatively, if you want decimal hours, you can use this method:

=(A2-A1)*24

formatted as General or as Number with 2 decimal places perhaps.  Your example will display as 70.00.

However, note that neither takes weekends (perhaps just Sunday in your case) and holidays into account.  You might want to look at the NETWORKDAYS function (read as "net workdays", not "network days" ;->).
