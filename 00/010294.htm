Major Paste Special (Convert Text to Numbers) Bug in Office - Microsoft Community

purebalonium say:

Major Paste Special (Convert Text to Numbers) Bug in Office 365 Excel (Version 1805)

Hello,

I'm currently tracking down a pretty severe bug in Excel that I've been diagnosing today. It appears that using Paste Special as a quick method to convert numbers to text can cause the loss of all non-numeric text in the paste range (at best) or corrupt the workbook (at worst). Here's what I've discovered so far:

Product: Microsoft Office 365 ProPlus Excel (Version 1805 Build 9330.2087 Click-to-Run).

Reproduction Steps:

  1. Open Excel and create a new, blank workbook.
  2. Type differing text into cells A1:A3 ('A', 'B', 'C')
  3. Type the number 1 into A4.
  4. Copy cell A4 via Control-C.
  5. Highlight A1:A3
  6. In the Ribbon use Home->Paste down arrow ->Paste Special...
  7. Select Paste:Values and Operation:Multiply
  8. Select OK
  9. Save the workbook and then close it.
  10. Reopen the workbook.

At this point the workbook is corrupted and all text in A1:A3 is permanently gone. Here's what I think is happening:

  • Multiplying non-numeric text with a number is an invalid operation and should produce a #VALUE! error.
  • Because the operation occurs via UI rather than a straight calculation, the values displayed in the cell are not replaced with #VALUE! but retain their original data. However, the data storage mechanism behind Excel has the cells equal to #VALUE!. e.g. Display='My Text', Value=#VALUE!.
  • When Excel goes to save the document, things go sideways. What I've seen so far is that Excel thinks all of the cells have the same string value because #VALUE!==#VALUE! when, in fact, they don't ('A'!='B').
  • Because of the above, ALL cells with the hidden #VALUE! error are considered to be text AND equal to each other. This flags them to be saved in 'sharedStrings.xml' and is why data is lost. ALL of the cells are then pointed to the index in sharedStrings.xml with the value of the first cell. When I've been able to recover the document, what happens is all of the cells in the paste range suddenly become the same value ('A' repeated over-and-over again).
  • However, due to the inconsistent state of the cells, Excel never makes it to the above bullet point and just errors out. At this point the data becomes irrecoverable.

I've told my office to avoid Paste Special when trying to convert data from numbers stored as text to numeric values but wanted to ask if this was a known issue and if there was a known ETA for resolution and it's pretty damaging when we stumble across it.

Thanks in advance.

(2) You will have to create separate conditional formatting for Time formatted cells and for other normal number formatted cells - If you try to apply the conditional formatting from a time formatted cell to a normal number formatted cell then the formatting will change to time formatted.

Assuming the sample data which you have shared is starting from cell A1   up till  cell F2

You can select cell B2 and then do the conditional formatting as follows:

Click as follows -

"HOME" - "CONDITIONAL FORMATTING" - "Use a formula to determine which cells to format"

key-in the following formula in the box having header as "Format values where this formula is true: "

"B2<B1"

Click "Format"  and choose RED color and click OK

Click OK again

Now click "HOME" - "CONDITIONAL FORMATTING" - "MANAGE RULES" - "NEW RULE"

Again select "Use a formula to determine which cells to format"  and

key-in the following formula in the box having header as "Format values where this formula is true: "

"B2>=B1"

Click "Format"  and choose GREEN color and click OK

Click OK again

Similarly apply conditional formatting to other cells

Hope this helps.

Transfer of contacts is still possible, let me show you some steps below.

To back up your contact list and transfer it to your new Skype account:

  • Sign in to Skype with your old account details.

  • Click Contacts, then click Advanced > Backup Contacts to File.

  • Navigate to where you want to save the file on your computer, then click Save.

  • Click Skype > Sign out to sign out of your old account.

  • Sign in to Skype with your new account details.

  • Click Contacts, then click Advanced > Restore Contacts from File.

  • Find the file that you saved on your computer, then click it and click Open. Your contacts will appear in your new account.

  • Make sure that both accounts appear in each other’s contacts lists, then sign in to Skype with your old account details.

  • Send the contacts you want to transfer to the new account.

  • Click Skype > Sign out to sign out of your old account.

  • Sign in to Skype with your new account details and click Recent. You'll see the contact requests in your recent list.

  • Send a contact request to each of the contacts.
