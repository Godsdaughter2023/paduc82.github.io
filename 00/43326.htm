ar rom say:
Hi Dustin,


When the "Open with" option appears, you need to choose the program you want to associate the particular file type with. Set defaults for the programs to avoid the "Open with" option from popping up. Here are the steps:

1. Click the Start button and select Control Panel.
2. Click Programs, choose Default Programs.
3. Select Set Default Programs.
4. On the left of the screen is a list of all programs installed on your computer.
5. Click the program you want to associate with a particular file type.
6. Click Choose Defaults for this program. Place check marks next to each of the file types you want to associate with the selected program.
7. Click the Save button.


Get back to us if you need further assistance.

ken blake say:

If you are getting the "open with" popup, you are not trying to open the program by clicking on the program. Rather, you are clicking on a *file* that you want the program to open.

Instead of doing that, find the program itself, or a shortcut to it, and double-click that. That should open the program, and then once you are in it, you can open whatever file you want it to work on.

What happens when you try that?

The maximum current that can be supplied by a USB port is determined by the chip that drives it. It is not determined by the amount of power that the battery or power supply could deliver. The USB standard specifies 500 mA and this is what the makers comply with. Maybe future USB generations will raise this limit.

annonymous_user say: Maybe we can upgrade that on our favourite computer somehow? But where would the chip be located?

frederik long say:

Maybe we can upgrade that on our favourite computer somehow? But where would the chip be located?

The chip is mounted on the motherboard. Motherboards consist of up to 8 layers of circuit boards with tracks running in between the boards all over the place. The chips are soldered on in a liquid solder bath. Your chances of replacing a chip successfully (if you could find one) are as great as replacing a defective pixel on your TV screen. In other words, nil.

Much easier to find a charging cable with three plugs: One plug for your phone, two for your PC. This would double its output current capacity.
