monomeeth say:

Is this possible: Calculating due dates based on cell values AND the year in which the value was entered into a cell

I've been asked to design a spreadsheet to allow an area to manage the accreditation status of certain employees. Employees are trained in one of four rounds per year. Once accredited, their accreditation only remains in force for two years from a set date commencing in the year they were accredited.

 

Here is a bit more info to explain what I mean:

 

  • Round 1 means that the accreditation date falls between 1 March and 31 August inclusive, and that their accreditation expires on 31 May + 2yrs.
  • Round 2 means that the accreditation date falls between 1 September and 28 February inclusive, and that their accreditation expires on 30 November + 2yrs.
  • Round 3 means that the accreditation date falls between 1 January and 30 June inclusive, and that their accreditation expires on 31 March + 2yrs.
  • Round 4 means that the accreditation date falls between 1 July and 31 December inclusive, and that their accreditation expires on 30 September + 2yrs.

 

So, if John Doe is accredited in Round 1 on 15 April 2011, his accreditation would expire on 31 May 2013. However, if he was accredited in Round 3 on the same date (i.e. 15 April 2011), his accreditation would expire on 31 March 2013.

 

My approach

What I was going to do was create a spreadsheet with a number of columns. Column A for User ID, Column B for Surname, Column C for Given Name, Column D for Round, Column E for Description and Column F for Accreditation Status.

 

Column D would have validation set via a list of options, with only Round 1, Round 2, etc acceptable. Column E would have the following formula in its cells: 

=IF((D1="Round 1"), "COE 1",IF((D1="Round 2"), "BSL1",IF((D1="Round 3"), "COE 2",IF((D1="Round 4"), "BSL2","")))).

 

What I wanted to then do is to use formulas to dictate what value, if any, appears in Column F. The idea was to somehow have the spreadsheet compare todays date using =Today() in a cell somewhere (say in Z1) and then working out whether it was before or after the accreditation expiration date. If today's date was after, then the word "INACTIVE" would appear in Column F against that employee. However, I'm not sure how to achieve this "automatically". I wanted Excel to work out where to count the two years from for each individual based on some sort of date stamp it generated somewhere (within the same row but in another column hidden from view).

 

Here is an example of how I thought this may work:

 

Assume today is 1 July 2011. I'm in row 50 and I enter the details in Columns A, B, C and D. Column E is then automatically populated based on the value in Column D (using my IF formula above). Excel, unbeknownst to the user, has entered in Column AA at row 50 today's date. This date remains static - that is, if the user opens the spreadsheet up on 2 July 2011 then cell AA50 still contains a value of 1 July 2011. Based on this value, Excel has calculated in cell AB50 the Accreditation Expiration date. To do this it must: (1) work out that 1 July 2011 is in 2011, (2) checking to see what "Round" is selected in Column D, and (3) adding two years to the relevant "due date", but from 2011. So, if Round 1 is selected in D50, then the expiration date would be two years from 31 May 2011, whereas if Round 4 was selected at D50 the expiration date would be two years from 30 September 2011. This expiration date appears in AB50 and Excel compares this to today's date in cell Z1 using the formula =Today(). If today's date is after the expiration date in AB50, then cell F50 would display "INACTIVE" (based on a formula in Column F which would also do the comparison between the dates).


Complicating factor: If Round 2 is selected, and the date on which data was entered into a particular row was somewhere between 1 January and 28 February, then Excel would have to determine that the two years being added to 30 November for the expiration date is in fact added to 30 November the year before, not 30 November in the current year.

 

So, how do I do this? Can it be done just using formulas, or do I need to use a macro? I am guessing it may be a combination - use a macro to capture the current date in Column AA for the relevant Row data was entered into, and to store it - and maybe formulas could do the rest.

 

My preference is NOT to have another column for the user to enter in the actual accreditation date for the employee. I know this would “probably” simplify things, but unfortunately prior experience has shown that human error gets in the way, not to mention having to worry about date formats etc. 

  

Or, do I forget this approach and just include the extra column for actual accreditation dates and use formulas (either IF or SUMPRODUCTS) to do all the background calculations and hope that the date formats remain correct and consistent.

 

Any help would be most appreciated!

macropod say:

If you had a table in Word or Excel such as:

Fred Smith CEO
Jo-Ann Brown CFO
Anna Bligh CMO
John Doe  
you could use a mailmerge to generate a list like:


Fred Smith, Chief Executive Officer

Jo-Ann Brown, Chief Financial Officer

Anna Bligh, Chief Medical Officer


by using IF fields in a Directory mailmerge main document. Such a series of IF fields might be coded as:
{IF{MERGEFIELD Position}= "CEO" "Chief Executive Officer"}

{IF{MERGEFIELD Position}= "CFO" "Chief Financial Officer"}

{IF{MERGEFIELD Position}= "CMO" "Chief Medical Officer"}

{IF{MERGEFIELD Position}= "" "grunt"}

or:

{IF«Position»= "CEO" "Chief Executive Officer"}

{IF«Position»= "CFO" "Chief Financial Officer"}

{IF«Position»= "CMO" "Chief Medical Officer"}

{IF«Position»= "" "grunt"}

though you'd put the fields all end-to-end without line breaks.

Note: The field brace pairs (i.e. '{ }') for the above example are all created in the document itself, via Ctrl-F9 (Cmd-F9 on a Mac); you can't simply type them or copy & paste them from this message. Nor is it practical to add them via any of the standard Word dialogues. Likewise, you can't type or copy & paste the chevrons (i.e. '« »') - they're part of the actual mergefields, which you can insert from the 'Insert Merge Field' dropdown. The spaces represented in the field construction are all required.

stephanusdurus say:

I have been been working on this problem for 2 days. I have tried all the solutions proposes here and several proposed elsewhere and nothing worked. In the process I uncovered the fundamental problem which, fortunately, has a very simple solution. 

"Cannot resolve recipient" apparently indicates that Outlook 2010 is having trouble recognizing one or more of the intended recipients. The reason is that Outlook 2010 saves the information slightly differently than earlier versions. The trick is to open and resave any contact you are having trouble emailing. The reason why so many of the other solutions worked (for others and not for me) is that resaving the contact was part of the process. It did not work for me because some of my contacts were already in the suggested revised formats. It was only when I opened and saved (without any changes) all 100 or so of the contacts in my group that the I was able to send the email.
