The tag is the container for the link bar.
for example:
<div id="nav">
<!-- link bar goes here -->
</div>

In a FrontPage nav bar the "link" for the current page will be plain text, the other links will be hyperlinks.
If you add to the CSS:

#nav {font-weight:bold;color: pink;}

the current page will be bold and pink, the other links will be bold and white.

spike zhang msft say: Hi MikBai,

Thanks for your updates.

As Tim mentioned above, once you set the tenant sync client restriction, users can only sync files when they are logged in to the domain-joined computer. For the issue which you can’t sync OneDrive files in the domain-joined computer, it’s not an excepted behavior.

To troubleshoot this issue, please check the domain sync allowed list via the steps below:

1. Go to Office 365 admin Portal > OneDrive Admin Center, check if the "Allow sync only with PC's associated with specific domains” is enabled for your domain guid.

2. Open SharePoint Online PowerShell, run the command:  Get-SPOTenantSyncClientRestriction, check if the AllowedDomainList matches the domain GUID in the OneDrive Admin Center permissions.

If not, please try re-setting the allowed domain list via the steps below and see if the issue can be fixed.

1. Run the command: Remove-SPOTenantSyncClientRestriction to disables the sync client restriction feature for the tenancy.

2. Run the command: Get-SPOTenantSyncClientRestriction to verify the restriction is removed.

3. Run the command: Set-SPOTenantSyncClientRestriction -Enable -DomainGuids "your domain guid" to re-set the sync client restriction.

In addition, as our forum focus on Office 365 for business online services, for your queries about domain joined system, we suggest you post your question to our TechNet forum for expert help.

We appreciate your understanding.

Sync music, pictures, and videos

There are several ways to copy or sync files with your phone, depending on the type of file you're trying to sync, and where it's stored. Here's how to sync multimedia files (music, videos, pictures, and podcasts) between your phone and your PC.

To learn how to do this on a Mac, see Sync and get updates with my Mac.

To sync music, videos, pictures, and podcasts with your phone

To sync multimedia files between your phone and your PC, you'll need to install a free program called Zune software on your computer. The Zune software helps you organize your multimedia collection, choose what to sync to your phone, and even download new stuff from Zune Marketplace.

  1. Connect your phone to your PC using the USB cable that came with your phone.

    If the Zune software is installed on your computer, the program will start automatically. If it isn't installed on your computer, see Install the Zune software.

    If this is the first time you've connected your phone to your PC, follow the instructions in the Zune software to name your phone and perform other setup tasks.

  2. In the Zune software, click Collection.

  3. Find the item you want to sync by doing one of the following:

    • Click a category (Music, Videos, Pictures, or Podcasts), and browse until you find the item you want to sync with your phone (such as a song, album, artist, playlist, genre, and so on).
    • In the Search box, enter what you're looking for (for example, a song title or artist name). In the search results, click the desired item.

  4. Drag the item to the phone icon in the lower-left corner of the Zune software.

Tips
  • If you don't have music, videos, or podcasts on your PC yet, see Get music, videos, and podcasts from Marketplace. You can also use Zune software to rip your own CDs. For more information, see Play, add, and delete media in your Zune collection.
  • To see information about what you're currently syncing, click the phone icon in the lower-left corner of the Zune software. To see the specific media files that are on your phone, click one of the categories (Music, Videos, Pictures, or Podcasts) near the top of the Zune software.
To change sync options

If you want more control over what gets synced between your phone and PC, try experimenting with the Zune software sync options. For example, if you don't want to bother dragging individual songs, artists, or albums to the phone icon, you can configure the Zune software to automatically sync all of the music on your PC with your phone (space permitting).

  1. In the Zune software, click Settings > Phone > Sync Options.

  2. Click the items that you want to change (for example, in the Music section, click All), and then click OK.

To find pictures and videos synced from your phone to your PC

If you take a picture or record a video on your phone, that picture or video will automatically be copied to your computer the next time you sync with the Zune software.

  1. Connect your phone to your PC using the USB cable that came with your phone.

  2. In the Zune software, click Collection > Pictures.

  3. Under My Pictures, click the album that matches the name of your phone (for example, From David's Phone).

    Tip

    To see where on your computer the picture is stored, right-click the picture, and then click Properties.

To check free storage space on your phone

  1. Connect your phone to your PC using the USB cable that came with your phone.

  2. In the Zune software, click Phone > Summary.

  3. At the bottom of the screen, look at the right-end of the storage indicator (for example, "FREE 3.24 GB" means that your phone has 3.24 gigabytes of free storage space).

Tip

If you've changed the Zune software sync options to sync all your music, videos, pictures, and podcasts to your phone, you might find that you don't have enough free storage space on your phone to do other things (such as take pictures, record videos, or download email and apps). To solve this problem, increase the amount of space on your phone reserved for doing these tasks. With your phone connected to your computer, in the Zune software, click Settings > Phone > Reserved Space, and then drag the handle to the right.

To set up wireless sync

If you don't want to physically connect your phone to your computer every time you need to sync, you can set up your phone to automatically sync with your computer over your home Wi-Fi network.

  1. Connect your phone to your PC using the USB cable that came with your phone.

  2. In the Zune software, click Settings > Phone > Wireless Sync.

  3. Follow the on-screen instructions in the Zune software.

After you've completed the steps above, files will automatically sync with your phone wirelessly when the following conditions are met:

  • Your phone and PC are both connected to your home Wi-Fi network. Note that wireless sync is not supported on workplace Wi-Fi networks.
  • Your phone battery has at least a medium-level charge ( ) and your phone is connected to an AC power adapter (that is, your phone must be connected to the wall charger and not connected to your computer).
  • You're not interacting with your phone. For example, you're not touching things on the screen, the backlight is off, and music and videos aren't playing (it's OK if a song or video is paused).

It might take up to 15 minutes for wireless sync to start, and there is no way to manually force it to occur. For more information about wireless sync, see Syncing wirelessly from your Zune software.

Notes
  • See How do I get contacts onto my phone? for information about syncing contacts.
  • For information about syncing notes between your phone and SkyDrive, see Use Microsoft OneNote Mobile.
  • The Zune software won't work on Windows RT. But you can get music on your phone using an Xbox Music Pass, and you can use SkyDrive to sync your documents. You can also get the SkyDrive app from the Windows Phone Store.

Hey OopsIbrokeit,

 

Check to see if System Tools was accidentally dragged to another location or into another folder. This can easily happen when navigating through the Start Menu with almost no chance of noticing it until what you’re looking for is missing.

 

Try following this method instead of using Internet Explorer (No add-ons):

·        Open Internet Explorer

·        Click Tools

·        Click Manage Add-ons

·        Click Toolbars and Extensions

·        Under Show: click All add-ons

·        Click any add-on

·        Press CTRL + A on your keyboard (this should select all add-ons)

·        Click Disable all

·        Click Close

·        Close Internet Explorer (all windows of Internet Explorer)

·        Open Internet Explorer

 

Now all add-ons are disabled in Internet Explorer, test to see if the same problem occurs without add-ons. If the problem is fixed then the next step is to enable the add-ons one by one to find which is causing the problem. (an alternative method is provided below these instructions)

·        Click Tools

·        Click Manage Add-ons

·        Click Toolbars and Extensions

·        Under Show: click All add-ons

·        Click the first disabled add-on

·        Click Enable

·        Click Close

·        Surf a few pages

·        Close Internet Explorer

·        Open Internet Explorer

 

If Internet Explorer’s RSS feeds still opens/works correctly, the add-on you enabled is good to stay. Repeat the enabling process for the next disabled add-on and continue this process until the problem returns. Then the last enabled add-on will need to be disabled/uninstalled/updated to avoid further problems.

 

Alternative method:

Instead of enabling add-ons one at a time, you could enable the first half of the add-ons and then test. If the problem is still gone, the add-ons you enabled are good to stay.
