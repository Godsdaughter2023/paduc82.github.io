james vinyard say:

So far, seems that the only "cure" was to check with the router makers web site to see if there is a newer firmware for it.

The Marvel Avastar adapter is choosy with routers and doesn't like a lot of models out there.  The problem "IS" the Marvel''s driver and actually not the router itself.

The only other fix would be to get a USB WiFi adapter and use it in locations where you get limited or no connection.  If you do that, you steer away from modifying the router and possibly making it non-functional.  There is a warning issued by the router makers in regards to installing the firmware and heed that warning and follow the instructions to the letter (that is, if an update is available).

Some of the newer routers do have issues even with other computers.  I have a HP Stream 14 laptop with a Broadcom adapter that is pulling the same stunt as the Marvel adapter.  Broadcom is working to catchup and getting the newer drivers up to speed in regards to it.

You can try the router upgrade route or get a USB WiFi adapter and use it temporarily.  Most models should work and RealTek and a few others make a real good one and they are cheap.
