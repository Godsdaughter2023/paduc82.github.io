jeeped say:

... Does ANYONE have any idea what is going on?


Seeing the formulas in the cells instead of the formula's results can come from several circumstances. Here are two distinct scenarios:

Try tapping Ctrl+<accent grave>. The <accent grave> is the backwards apostrophe typically found above the <Tab> key and below <Esc>. This has the effect of toggling between Show Formulas and Show Formula Results display modes.

Another possibility is that the cells were formatted as Text before pasting the formulas into them. This can often lead Excel to believe you intend to paste the formula in as a textual representation of a formula rather than an actual formula. A quick fix for this is to perform a Find and Replace by searching for an <equals> sign and replacing it with an <equals> sign. This forces Excel to re-evaluate the cells' contents and the text string formulas become active formulas. You can accomplish the same thing by tapping Ctrl+A (i.e. Select All) and running Text to Columns and clicking Finish as soon as the first page shows up.

One of those two solutions should fix your worksheet.
