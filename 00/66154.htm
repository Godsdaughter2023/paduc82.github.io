2.  Ensure it is not maximized.  It is considered maximized if the one application takes up the entire screen and you cannot see any part of the desktop.  If it IS maximized, click on the center (squarish, blue highlight) button in the upper-right corner of the application's window.  It will grow smaller and you'll be able to see the desktop around it.

3.  Now move your mouse to the title bar at the top of the window, click the mouse and drag that window to the left edge of the screen.  Keep dragging until the mouse reaches the edge and you see the outline of the window jump to the center of the screen.  Let up the mouse button.  The application will now be sized to fill only half the screen.

4.  Repeat steps 1 and 2 with the second application.

5.  Drag it, as in step 3, but this time to the RIGHT side of the screen.

Voila, two windows tiled side by side on your screen.  Drag them off the edge again and they will return to their original size and shape.

FYI, the Aero Snap setting needs to be enabled (which is the default) in order for the above to work:

http://www.winhelponline.com/blog/enable-and-disable-aero-snap-docking-windows-7/
