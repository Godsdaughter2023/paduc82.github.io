charles kenyon say:

Hi,

How to layout the watermark with the following effect?

  • Watermark is filled to the entire document
  • Watermark is brought to the front of all contents

***

Brief answer is you cannot do the second part, you may be able to do the first to a limited extent.

In Word, a Watermark is an image file (even if text) inserted into the header. When it is inserted using the Watermark button in Word it adds some additional criteria. Here, you could create your text and then scan it in as a png file. Use that picture for your watermark using the Watermark button. You may have to play around with the intensity.

That will give you the Watermark. However, because it is in the Header, it will always be behind text.

When I qualified as "to a limited extent" above, I meant that not all printers will print edge to edge. Most will not.

Watermarks in Ribbon Versions of Word

For a single page, making sure the background in your png is transparent, you could instead of inserting a Watermark, insert your image file and set wrapping on it to be in front of text.
