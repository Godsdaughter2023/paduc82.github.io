e151280


Assuming that this is peer-to-peer Network with Client OS', I am not sure why you are using a Managed switch and how it is configured.

Speaking in functional terms Giga "speed" (x10 form 100Mb/sec.) is not the functional "Speed" of the Network's transfer, it is the Speed of the NIC’s chipset.

Windows Client OS' on a peer-to-peer topology max out at a functional sustained "Speed" of about 70-80 MB/sec. (B=Byte). That means around 640Mb/sec. (If you use a bench mark that transfers in short burst of small files, higher reading can be obtained)

This can be achieved with good Giga switch and Giga hardware that is Jumbo Frames capable (MTU=9000), doubling the RCwin seems to contribute to it too, provided that the basic Internet bandwidth is Not disturbed.

This an example to an optimized Giga transfer, http://www.ezlan.net/vista/Giga-Optimized.jpg . I.e., 200Gb should take about 50-60 Minutes.

 Before carrying out this procedure, disconnect from the Internet. That means turning off Wi-Fi and disconnecting from a wired connection.                            net stop cryptSvc

                           net stop bits

                           net stop msiserver
