You may also troubleshoot or determine what conflict is causing the problem by performing a clean boot. You must log on to the computer as an administrator to be able to perform a clean boot. Your computer may temporarily lose some functionality when you perform a clean boot. When you start the computer normally, the functionality returns. However, you may receive the original error message, or experience the original behavior if the problem still exists. If the computer is connected to a network, network policy settings may prevent you from following these steps.

Follow the steps mentioned in the following article.https://support.microsoft.com/kb/929135 

Note: Once you perform clean boot do refer to the section “How to reset the computer to start normally after clean boot troubleshooting” to boot the computer in normal mode.

gary zhu msft support say:
Hi Johnep71，

Generally, it will take up to 2 weeks of consistent training for Clutter to take assumed actions on similar items.

However, when the Clutter feature is first enabled, it's not very accurate yet. It needs to process 5,000 email messages before it has a sufficient training model to take action on items that are delivered to the inbox. (There must be 5,000 email messages that are delivered to the inbox and not sorted anywhere else.)

For example, a user received 5,000 email messages, in which 2,000 of these messages were sorted to different subfolders and 3,000 of them were delivered to the inbox. In this scenario, the model has not yet been established. The user needs to wait for an additional 2,000 email messages to be delivered to the inbox before Clutter starts to take action.

So please check if there are 5000 email messages in the inbox folder.

Gary
