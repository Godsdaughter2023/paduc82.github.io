avej say:

Copy a custom theme to another drawing

Suppose you have two drawings: Drawing1 and Drawing2
Drawing1 has custom theme that you want to copy in Drawing2

Follow steps below:

1. Open the Drawing1 that has the custom theme you want to copy.

2. Select a shape with the custom theme applied.

3. Click on "Copy" button in Home Ribbon

4. Open the Drawing2 that you want to copy the custom theme to.

5. Click on "Paste" button in Home Ribbon.


Check in DESIGN -> Variant -> Colors. The custom theme is added to the Custom section.

 

6. You can now delete the shape that you pasted into your drawing.

guessed say:

Bree

Helvetica Neue is not a standard typeface and may not be installed on your work computer. Have you checked to see that it is installed there?

Using non-standard typefaces is always a risk if you are expecting the document to display the same on other computers. Whilst you can embed some typefaces (ie store a copy of the font inside the document), this is often problematic and best avoided.

Fonts get onto a computer either as part of the Operating System or by being included when installing various applications or by specifically loading fonts from various sources. paul herber say: Dates are controlled by what Windows considers to be a valid data, thus the earliest date you can use id 1899.
However, use the Drawing Explorer, find the timeline among the shapes on the page, expand the timeline shape to show the shapes within the group, work down the list of shapes until you get to one of the date shapes, note its name (Sheet.xx), click the TextTool button and edit the shape's text to enter the required date, when finished click the PointerTool button. Now go back to the list of shapes (this is where you need the Sheet.xx above because otherwise you'd lose track. Repeat for all required dates. All other automation within the timeline can never be used as it will overwrite your dates.
