First, you may not need or want macros for this. For many symbols, you can assign keyboard shortcuts directly without any macro as an intermediary.

I would save the keyboard shortcuts in a separate Global Template, not in normal.dotm, and place that template in the Word Startup Folder. That way they would be accessible in all documents and very portable/sharable.

I would store macros in the same template, not in normal.dotm.

Getting back to your question, what happens, when with the vba editor open, you tell one of the macros you've recorded to run? (Click inside the macro listing and press F5.)

charles kenyon say:

You are welcome.

You can use that same dialog to reassign keyboard shortcuts to macros. I don't know what would have happened to break your shortcuts. Since they run from the macro menu, they are still active and working.

I would recommend deleting the macros you don't need and then moving those you want to keep to the same .dotm template you are using to hold your keyboard shortcuts for the symbols. You can do this in the vba editor (Alt+F11).
