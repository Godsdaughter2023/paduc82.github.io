How to create outlook.com spam filter rules that actually work? - Microsoft Community

keithd32623523 say:

How to create outlook.com spam filter rules that actually work?

I am wondering how to create outlook.com rules that actually filter out spam.  I get these spammers spamming paypal scams, but I have legitimate emails from paypal itself as well as a business that includes the term "paypal" in its emails.  How would I create a rule that would automatically delete all emails containing the term "paypal" except those from two specific email addresses?

stanimal21 say:

That's tough because I know exactly what you're talking about; I had over 300 spam messages just on Tuesday and some were let through the regular spam filter and some weren't. I checked my own rules and tried to create another one with specific exclusion criteria but you can't do it (if you can, it's tricky and I couldn't figure it out). An effective method to remove spam though is to turn on Exclusive Junk Mail filtering, which labels all mail as JUNK unless it's from a verified sender or a sender you've deemed as trustworthy in the Safe Senders list.

To turn on Exclusive Junk Mail filtering, do this:

  1. Browse to Outlook.com and then click on the Settings button (the gear cog on the top-right), then click Options
  2. Click "Filters and reporting" under the Preventing junk mail section
  3. Choose "Exclusive" under the Choose a junk email filter option

I think Paypal is a verified sender and their address should be allowed through the Safe Senders list, but if not then you can manually add the email address. Follow step 1 above, but then click on Safe and Blocked senders instead for step 2. You can add entire domains to the safe senders list, which is nifty since Paypal and other businesses will use different email addresses to send you different information; allowing an entire domain through will allow all of them through.

Note: monitor your spam mail folder every day for important mail. The exclusive filter setting is very strict, so there's higher chance of good email being caught in that. If you see something you need, add them to the Safe senders list.
