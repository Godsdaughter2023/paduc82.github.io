potfamily say:

Office 365 on older iMac

I have Word for Mac 2011 on my 2009 iMac (latest version of MacOS supported is El Capitan). I want to puchase Office 365 for my MacBook Air and want to know whether I would be able to use it on both devices. I am worried that if I install it on the older iMac and it didn't work, I might end up without Word on that device at all. Thanks
sheen valentine ali msft say:

Hi PotFamily,

Office 365 for Mac is supported on the three most recent versions of macOS. Right now, the latest version is 10.14, which means that Office 365 supports 10.12, 10.13, and 10.14.

El Capitan on your 2009 iMac is not supported. If your Macbook Air is on any of the three latest versions of MacOS, you will be able to use Office 365 on it.

For your reference:

System requirements for Office 

In this case, you can continue to use Word for Mac 2011 on your 2009 iMac, and Office 365 on your Macbook Air.

Let us know if you have any additional questions.

chuck norris say:

WORD 2013 PROTECTED FILLABLE FORM CAN STILL HAVE LINES ADDED BY HITTING ENTER KEY

I have created a form using Microsoft Word 2013, inserted Legacy controls, restricted editing so that only fill-in forms may be populated, and set the password to protect the form.

When I test the document to and hit the 'enter' key within the cell/field, the action creates a new line in the document.

I've read that I can set the 'enter' key as a 'tab' key, but that's not going to work for other users who don't & won't reset their 'enter' key.

How do I protect the document so that no new lines can be added by hitting the 'enter' key?

stefan blom say:
As a workaround, you can put your text form fields inside table cells whose dimensions are fixed (add or remove cell borders as desired). That won't prevent anyone from adding excessive content, but most people would stop pressing Enter when they notice that text disappears into the bottom margin of the cell.
