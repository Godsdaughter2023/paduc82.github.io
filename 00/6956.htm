petrib say: Only devices without a keypad (such as Bluetooth headsets) have a default Bluetooth passcode (or pairing code).

The phone has no defaults, but when pairing two Bluetooth devices, you enter the same code on both devices (and if the other one has a fixed passcode, then on your phone you enter whatever the fixed code is).

When it is a fixed code, it can be 0000, 1234 or whatever the device manufacturer decided (it can also vary for each individual device manufactured, too, in which case the code is usually printed on a separate sticker or paper).

The passcode is only used once to establish the Bluetooth encryption/decryption keys for the two devices to communicate. The code is not used thereafter by the devices when they talk to each other (and is not saved/stored, either).

daniel g. grau say:
You can't.

A document will open in whatever view it was last saved in. That is, if the document was in Draft View at 200% when you last saved it, it will be in Draft View at 200% the next time you open it. Likewise with Print Layout View. The only way to change the view permanently is to force a re-save. For this, you have to enter a dummy change (type something, then backspace), so Word will allow you to save it.

New documents will open in the view of the last-viewed document, at the Zoom percentage defined by its template.

Hi MMJII,

Regarding “Add Account  button is not on the File link”, please provide us a screenshot. You can refer to this article on how to create an outlook account via Control Panel.

To setup a POP3 mail account on your Outlook 2016, first you need to know the Outgoing and Incoming mail servers and Port for setup. You can find this in OWA > Settings (gear icon) > Mail  > Accounts > POP and IMAP settings.

Now that you know the server settings, you can follow these steps:

1. Go to Control Panel > Email Account > E-mail tab > New > Select Manual setup or additional server types, hit Next.

2. Enter their respective outgoing and incoming mail servers, select POP3 as the Account Type. Enter the correct Email address/Username and password.


 3. Click More Settings.. , then under Advanced, Configure the Ports as shown below.

4. Back to POP and IMAP account settings, click Test Account Settings to test and then Click Next > Finish.

You can refer to this article for POP and IMAP settings on Outlook Office 365 and to setup Outlook email.

Hi,

You might try turning off a feature called fast Startup...

This feature only works when you do a SHUTDOWN and then Boot.

It doesn't effect a RESTART.

To turn it off or on...

Go to Control panel...Power Options, and select Choose What the Power Buttons Do on the left.

Then select Change Settings That Are Currently Unavailable near the top center of screen...

Lower down on the window, uncheck Fast Startup.

If this helps, then the source of the problem is most likely a driver not loading properly...
