Do you have Zoho's two-factor authentication enabled? Make sure you have your AV program configured to not scan mail.

0x800ccc90 is a POP error that means "client response invalid". Your POP server didn't get a valid response from Outlook when exchanging POP data. I can see the POP command "+OK" in the text of the sample message. This indicates a broken protocol exchange. Try turning on diagnostic logging to capture the entire exchange between Outlook and the server. See https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/kb/2862843

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

I didn't say you should switch off the AV, I said it shouldn't scan mail. Leave it on, but disable mail scanning. It's pointless and can interfere with mail reception. In order for anything to infect your machine, something first has to be written to your hard drive. The on-access scanner portion of your AV will scan the file at that point. It's doubly pointless to scan outgoing mail because if your AV could detect something on your PC you were about to send, it would have detected it already, long before you send a message.

Try this one:

=HLOOKUP(K5,$D$1:$H$28,MATCH(K3,A1:A28,0)+MATCH(K4,INDIRECT("B"&MATCH(K3,A1:A28,0) &":B"&MATCH(K3,A1:A28,0)+COUNTIF(A1:A28,K3)-1),1)-1,FALSE)

MATCH(K3,A1:A28,0)+MATCH(K4,INDIRECT("B"&MATCH(K3,A1:A28,0) &":B"&MATCH(K3,A1:A28,0)+COUNTIF(A1:A28,K3)-1),1)-1

,INDIRECT("B"&MATCH(K3,A1:A28,0) &":B"&MATCH(K3,A1:A28,0)+COUNTIF(A1:A28,K3)-1) is creating the range where the value is equal to K3.
