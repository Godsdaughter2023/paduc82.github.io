jay freedman say:

All versions of Word from 2007 onward can work with .docx files, so you won't have any trouble with that. (Some even older versions can open .docx files with the help of a compatibility pack.)

Strictly speaking, .docx files are "document" files, not what Microsoft calls "templates" -- that term is reserved for a different kind of file, with a .dotx or .dotm extension, that is used as a basis for new documents. The files offered by officeresumes.com are templates in a looser sense -- they contain the necessary tables, styles, and places to insert text and pictures to make your resume.

I haven't downloaded any of their files, so I can't say how easy they are to edit. I'll suggest (if they don't) that you make at least one copy of the files you receive and store them somewhere safe, so you can start over if you make a serious mistake while editing.
