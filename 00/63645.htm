eeeehaw say:

Okay, for the benefit of others having the same problem, here's how I fixed mine...

But first, some background: I tried the Microsoft support chat service, but the support rep told me that because Windows 10 Education edition is licensed under a commercial volume license agreement that he couldn't help, that the Microsoft professional support service department had to deal with it, and gave me their phone number.  When I finally spoke to a pro support rep, she said the same boilerplate response above "do a clean install and reinstall your apps afterward", but if I wanted to learn about any work-around to do an in-place upgrade or how to first backup my apps/registry/etc for later restore if I had to do a clean install, it would cost me $400 before I could learn about that and if I had to to the backup/restore route than I would have to acquire the iso image for Windows 10 Education.  Human ransomware.  I wasn't about to do that yet, so...

I called OnTheHub support, who are the third party service that many universities and schools use for free and discounted software from various manufacturers, including Microsoft.  My university uses them, which is where I received the Windows 10 Education product key to use to upgrade my Windows 10 Home laptop.  The support rep there was very helpful, having seen this before.  Using a remote control app, he verified my problem, then downloaded an iso image of Windows 10 Education.  I then took over and used a freeware app "ISO to USB" to burn that iso image to a USB drive (it must be at least 5 GB in size, I used a 8GB stick), also checking the option to make it bootable.  Altho I did not have to reboot from that USB stick drive I just made, I then ran the setup.exe program installed on it, which launched the Windows 10 setup program.  Sure enough, there eventually was a screen where I could set "keep apps and data", which results in an in-place upgrade.  It proceeded to install, download updates, etc, which took quite awhile, but it finished with my other apps and files still installed.  I had to manually restart the computer after it was "finished", as it didn't allow me to select some Start menu items, notably "Settings", but once I rebooted that worked.  Finally, I went into the Start menu, clicked Settings, Update & Security, Activate, Change Product Key, and entered the Windows 10 Education key that I acquired originally from OnTheHub.  It then activated properly (and registered that with my Microsoft account, which is an advisable practice for others).  I still needed to do some Microsoft updates to bring the system current, but this problem is now solved.

Yes, OnTheHub needs to change their student offering, as just getting the product key alone does not good at all with OEM installed Windows 10 Home on a laptop (apparently only with a full retail edition, according to the Microsoft support rep I spoke to earlier).  Doing a clean install without having the new license key does not work, either.  It takes both components, and as I discovered an in-place upgrade can indeed be done thus saving in my case weeks of prior work installing and configuring special apps.

You can tell the computer not to ask for a password when it comes back from screensaver or sleep mode:
  1. Right-click Desktop, choose Personalize, then click Screen Saver at the bottom right.
  2. Choose not to display the logon screen (checkbox).
  3. Click the power settings link in the same box, and then choose 'require a password on wakeup' at the left to turn that option off as well.

If you want to boot up your computer and not have to enter a password, take these steps (note: this can make your computer less secure if someone was to steal your computer, they'd have access to all your files, plus likely any website you have been too, such as your bank).
  1. While holding the Windows logo key, type R to open the Run box.  Run this command:
    control userpasswords2

  2. Uncheck the 'users must enter a username and password to use this computer' option, then click OK.  You'll be asked which account you want to use instead (which one should stay signed in).
