When you connect a compatible external storage drive, Xbox One will prompt you to format the drive and walk you through setting it up to store games and apps.

The basic requirements for storing games and apps on an external drive are:

hellhog say:

Buy a monitor such as the one in my sig, use a VGA cable for your laptop and HDMI cable for your Xbox, problem solved. I do the reverse. I have my laptop 'docked' sitting on a laptop cooler 24/7 in my dorm, a keyboard and mouse plugged in, and use the HDMI out from my laptop to my monitor. I could technically use the VGA out but I have a VGA cable for my Xbox since my Xbox doesn't have HDMI out (it is one of the original ones). You don't need any converters and should never convert HDMI and VGA signals. The only conversion that is okay is HDMI and DVI or VGA and DVI but not VGA and HDMI (note: there are two different types of DVI signals which only one of each will work with HDMI or VGA). The plus side is I still have a free input available on my monitor (DVI). I've thought about buying a DVI to VGA converter to use the VGA out on my laptop so that my HDMI port is free instead for something like a Bluray player. Another note: the video output port on the Xbox 360 is a multifunction propriety port. There are Component, Composite, S-Video, and VGA cable connections available for use with that port. Of course, like I said for your scenarios, use an HDMI cable for your Xbox and if you do buy a monitor, they usually include a VGA cable.

As for the under $100 price range. Lookup on Newegg. I bought my monitor during Christmas and it was originally $210 with $25 off, plus another $25 off coupon code so it cost me $160. It is a 23" 1920x1080 LED monitor with DVI, HDMI, and VGA inputs (bigger screen than my old LCD TV but better resolution, quality, thinner, and lighter not to mention the box is even smaller). No speakers but that is ok, I use a headset. If I want TV, I'll just buy a USB TV Tuner for my laptop and use Windows Media Center to play shows. For brands, just stick to either ASUS or Samsung. I don't recomend a resoultion other than 1920x1080 as that is 1080p which obviously is the standard right now for Full HD. Oh, for refresh rate, 5ms or less and you'll be fine.

Here is what I recomend for you. Same series as mine but slightly smaller (21.5" screen instead of 23"). 5ms response time. DVI, HDMI, and VGA inputs. LED backlighting. 1920x1080 resolution. Basically you'll be set for all of college and even longer. It cost $144 but will look amazing for your laptop, your xbox, or if you get a desktop then you don't need to worry about a monitor. And when it comes to 1080p quality videos, gaming, etc. VGA = DVI = HDMI, there is no noticable difference. Really the only differences are that VGA is an old format, DVI is the newer standard as seen on pretty much all modern graphics cards, and HDMI has become a home theater standard for transmitting all sorts of audio/video/networking data. http://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16824236176
