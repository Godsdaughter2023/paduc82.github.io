How to stop overwriting of cells across worksheets in Excel 2007? - Microsoft Community

jb2333 say:

How to stop overwriting of cells across worksheets in Excel 2007?

In Excel 2007, when I enter information into Cell B12 of Sheet 1, the information overwrites B12 of Sheet 2 automatically.  This is a simplified example of what happens often and randomly across worksheets within a workbook.  It is ruining my work.  Is this a bug in program?  How to fix?  Thank you.
mike h.. say:
In Excel 2007, when I enter information into Cell B12 of Sheet 1, the information overwrites B12 of Sheet 2 automatically.  This is a simplified example of what happens often and randomly across worksheets within a workbook.  It is ruining my work.  Is this a bug in program?  How to fix?  Thank you.


Hi,

 

Unless you have macros set to do this; and it doesn't sound like you have, then this is most likely caused by having sheets 'grouped'.

 

When sheets are grouped then entering data in a cell in the visible sheets is copied into all cells in the group.

 

Sheets are grouped by holding down the CTRL and click on other sheet tabs. You can also hold down the shift key to group a range of consecutive sheets and could be inadvertantly doing either of the above.

 

When grouped the word GROUP appears in the banner at the top of the workbook next to where it says 'Microsoft Excel'.

 

To ungroup sheets click on any sheet tab that's not part of the group.

hansv mvp say:

You may have selected multiple sheets by accident. If you see [Group] after the name of the workbook in the title bar, more than one sheet has been selected, and anything you enter will be entered in all sheets.

Right-click a sheet tab and select 'Ungroup Sheets' from the context menu.

 

(Other possibilities are that you have formulas or VBA code that copy the contents of a cell from Sheet1 to Sheet2)

paulreed(1) say:

I had this exact same issue - and it was the HID Touch Screen device which was causing the issue.

Do you have a Touch Screen running ?

Try closing Excel.

Going into Device Manager => Human Interface Devices

Right click the Touch Screen device and select Disable.

Accept the prompt and try Excel again. Was this your issue ??

To Re-Enable the Touch Screen, you can simply Enable the device.

note: This process can be scripted to allow for a desktop shortcut to make this change, but its complicated by security. You may need to hoop jump to be able to run this.

However, here is the script:

--

DISABLE_TOUCHSCREEN.ps1

--

$f = Get-WmiObject Win32_PnPEntity | Where-Object {$_.Description -Match "touch screen"}
Invoke-WmiMethod -name "Disable" -InputObject $f

--

ENABLE_TOUCHSCREEN.ps1

--

$f = Get-WmiObject Win32_PnPEntity | Where-Object {$_.Description -Match "touch screen"}
Invoke-WmiMethod -name "Enable" -InputObject $f
