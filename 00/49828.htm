davem121 say:
Hi Adam

Windows 10 is different to previous versions of Windows, you will not see a Windows COA sticker on new devices

Your Windows product key is stored on a chip on your Motherboard

When you initially turn on that device and it is connected to the Internet, that device will contact the Microsoft Activation servers and automatically activate. At the same time a digital entitlement is stored on the Microsoft servers for your device using a number generated from your hardware

Should you ever need to re-install Windows, you will not need a Licence key and activation will be automatic . .


If you do want to keep a copy of your Licence Key:

Click the link below to download a small utility which will find your Windows 10 Product Key

Hi adamm123,
Actual Windows 10 licence aren't written down on stickers or on paperwork, but linked to the hardware. However you can obtain your product key doing that:

1. Digit "cmd" in windows search bar
2. Right click on "Command prompt"
3. Execute as admin
4 Digiit "wmic path SoftwareLicensingService get OA3xOriginalProductKey" without quote
5. Read and copy wherever you want your Product Key.
