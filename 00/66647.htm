FIX : Windows 10 100 Percent Disk Usage Problem

1. Press W8K + R and type devmgmt.msc in the Run, hit Enter key to open Device Manager snap-in.

2. In the Device Manager window, expand IDE ATA/ATAPI controllers and right click on Standard SATA AHCI Controller, select Properties.



3. In the property sheet, switch to Driver tab and click Driver Details.



4. If the Driver File Details window shows storahci.sys driver listed there, it means you’re running inbox driver and hence it is possible to disable MSI mode. Click OK.



5. Back on the property sheet, switch to Details tab. Against Property, select Device instance path from drop-down and note down the Value mentioned there.



Registry Disclaimer: The further steps will involve registry manipulation. Making mistakes while manipulating registry could affect your system adversely. So be careful while editing registry entries and create a System Restore point first.

6. Press W8K + R and put regedit in Run dialog box to open Registry Editor (if you’re not familiar with Registry Editor, then click here). Click OK.

7. In the Registry Editor window, navigate to following registry key:

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Enum\PCI\<AHCI Controller Value>\Device Parameters\Interrupt Management\MessageSignaledInterruptProperties

*Substitute <AHCI Controller Value> we got in step 5.



8. In the right pane of MessageSignaledInterruptProperties key, look for the registry DWORD (REG_DWORD) named MSISupported which should be corresponding to Value 1 as its Data. Double click on the DWORD to modify its value:



9. Finally, set the Value data as 0 and click OK. Close Registry Editor and reboot. After restarting your machine, the 100% disk usage problem should disappear.

Managing Payment Methods - Microsoft Community

mister cerb say:

Managing Payment Methods

Heyo!

Are you having issues managing, adding, or removing payment information? Or maybe you just want to know more about managing the payment information on your Xbox One account? Well, you've come to the right place! Click on the links below to be taken to a specific section of this sticky, or just scroll down at your leisure until you find the information you're looking for.

Table of Contents:

  1. General Payment Issues and Questions
  2. Credit Card Issues and Questions
  3. PayPal Issues and Question
  4. What if none of this helps? 


___________________________________________________________
What happened to Microsoft Points?
They're gone, but not forgotten! Check out our Microsoft Account Transition to Local Currency FAQ for more information. 


What are valid payment methods?
Currently you can use credit and debit cards with major labels (Visa, Mastercard, and American Express) as well as PayPal. These should be current accounts, and the billing information should match that of your Xbox Live account. Also, prepaid credit/debit cards may not work on Xbox Live, as the service requires the card be able to accept online and recurring charges, something prepaid cards sometimes cannot do.

I get "Can't Authorize" errors for multiple payment methods.
If you are getting this same error with multiple payment methods from multiple providers (PayPal, different banks or credit cards, etc), you should contact phone support in your region for more information and possible resolution. This could indicate some kind of issue with your account that only phone support will be able to assist you with.

I'm getting charged $1 when adding a payment method!
These are just test charges that are sent to verify that your payment method is currently accepting charges. They will be reversed within a few days. If you don't see them leave your account, first verify you didn't make any $1 purchases, and then contact either your bank or Xbox Phone Support.

Is there another way I can purchase my content?
In addition to purchasing content directly on Live, you could purchase a pre-paid card from a local retailer or online. These cards can be redeemed for funds in your Microsoft Account, games, or add-on content. 

I can't remove my payment method.
Before taking any steps, you need to know if your account is currently on a auto-renewing subscription to Live. If you purchased Live through the console with a payment method, possibly some kind of promotion, a valid payment method must be attached to the account for the duration. You can switch the payment method by adding a new one and then removing the original method, but you cannot remove all payment methods.

If you wish to cancel your auto-renewal so you can remove your payment method, start by calling up phone support in your region and asking them to turn it off. Now you just sit back and let your Live time run its course. When the subscription expires, you are free to remove any and all payment methods from your account. 

IMPORTANT: Please note that you can call phone support and have them manually cancel your subscription as this is the only way to prematurely remove all your payment methods. Please make sure you and the phone agent are clear on this before continuing. Once your Live time is canceled, it cannot be returned to you. Refunds are generally not given when you manually cancel your subscription.  


How do I add a payment method?

You can add payment methods on the console or on commerce.microsoft.com. For the console, just follow the steps below:

  1. Navigate to the Home Hub and select Settings.
  2. On the left hand side, select Payments and Billing.
  3. On the left hand side, select Add Credit Card.
  4. Fill out the required info and select Next.

 

I can't add a payment method!

First, verify that you are entering all the information correctly and that it matches your Xbox Live account. This includes your name, address, card info, etc. For instance, if the name on your card is "John Smith" and the name on your Live account is "Master Chief" then there could be some issues when authorizing or using the card. Also, the billing region on the payment method must match that of the account.

If you are sure that all the information on the account and payment method are correct, you could try to remove the payment method entirely and re-add it back to your account. You could also try using a different payment method. 

I’m getting a specific error code or message. 
There are several different messages you could be getting. These errors might look like:

Can't authorize the selected payment option. Please select a different payment option or add a new one.

Can't retrieve information from Xbox Live. Status code ######## 

Here are some specific things to double check before posting about this issue:

Your account billing address does not match your billing address for the credit card. 
You can check your billing address on Xbox.com under the Account section. 

Your credit card billing address and the account region do not match. 
You can check the region of your account by visiting account.live.com and clicking on “Personal Information”. 

Your card has not been activated, or 
Your card was declined due to insufficient funds, or 
Your card cannot accept online purchases or automatic billing, or
Your credit card company is intentionally preventing the card from being used. 
You will need to get in touch with your payment method provider to check on these issues. Two things that a lot of users don’t realize is that not all credit cards are created equal. Your card must allow for online purchasing and recurring fees (like auto-renewing Livetime). Also, some prepaid credit or debit cards do not work with Live as they cannot be counted on for automatic billing. 

You have too many unsuccessful purchase attempts, and will need to wait 24 to 48 hours. 
Pretty self-explanatory, but if you have been trying to authorize a card too many times, the fraud protection on the card might kick in, preventing any further attempts. You may need to also call your provider to see if you need to personally authorize the use of this card for this purpose. 

I'm unable to add PayPal to my account
If you are having issues with adding PayPal first check to make sure that your account meets all the requirements for using PayPal. Please ensure that all of the following criteria are met before continuing to other troubleshooting steps. 


PayPal Regions
PayPal is currently available to users in the following regions. Please ensure your Account Region is set to the correct region. You can verify this by visiting account.live.com and clicking on "Personal Information".  
 

  • Australia
  • Austria
  • Belgium
  • Canada
  • Denmark

  • Hong Kong SAR

  • Italy
  • Mexico
  • Netherlands
  • New Zealand

  • Sweden
  • Switzerland
  • United Kingdom
  • United States


PayPal Age
PayPal is currently only available to users 18 years of age and older. This is checked based on the birthday set when creating the account.

PayPal Funds
In order to properly use your PayPal account, it must either have a valid credit card attached to it or a positive money balance. If you have an attached bank account and are still having trouble adding/using your PayPal method, try transferring some money from the bank account to PayPal so the PayPal account actually has a positive balance. 

PayPal Account Information
Please ensure that the account information on both your LIVE account and PayPal account is current and that it matches. For instance, if your PayPal account is under "John Smith" but your Xbox Live account is registered to "Master Chief", you may have issues linking the two accounts. This also goes for contact information, billing address, etc.

Protecting File History from Ransomware - Microsoft Community

rockfoxx say:

Protecting File History from Ransomware

One of my clients was recently infected with the Cerber Ransomware and all data files were encrypted.  She had a USB external hard drive on which she kept a Windows System Image plus File History.  Fortunately the System Image was not touched but the configuration folder in File History was encrypted thus preventing restore from File History.   But the data files in File History were all okay.

Restoring the System Image got her system back to 2016-10-17.  I then wrote a   Rexx   program   to restore all her history files from that date forward so her system was completely restored. 

The point of this post is that it is not safe to have your File History device connected all the time.  As ransomware becomes more sophisticated it will eventually encrypt ALL files on ALL connected USB devices and even on network drives.

Even if your File History device is disconnected File History will continue to back up your files to:

C:\Users\%username%\AppData\Local\Microsoft\Windows\FileHistory

When the File History device is reconnected these files will be copied to it.

I typically leave my File History device disconnected all week until some time when I will not be using the PC.  I plug in the external device, go to Control Panel => File History => Run now.  (You may see a message that the device is disconnected but when you click Run now the red X will be removed.  When File History is finished it will display a message "Files last copied on mm/dd/yyyy hh:mm"  I then use Safely Remove Hardware to disconnect the history device.

File History will continue to run even if you Sign out of your account.

bill smithers say:

Hi,

No; you can not use it on another PC.

 

If the Computer came with the Windows Operating System preinstalled (Dell, HP, Acer, etc), that Product Key that came with the Laptop is for the preinstalled OEM License and is non-transferable.

 

The OEM License/Product Key (preinstalled Operating Systems on Dell, HP, etc machines) is tied to the first machine it is installed on and dies when that machine dies.

 

Only Retail Operating System Licenses, that you buy and install yourself, are allowd to be transferred to another machine.

And Retail Licenses can also only be installed on one machine at any particular time.

 

XP has not been legally sold for a few years.

Most of what you find for sale on the Net is pirated or malware-ridden.
