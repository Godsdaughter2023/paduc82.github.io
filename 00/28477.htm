julie fka profjulie say:

Sorry, you'd have to activate your phone through Adobe's servers before you can read DRMed ePUB books on your phone and Adobe does not provide this kind of service nor does it support DRMed ePUB books on windows phones.  You also can't read ePub books in Adobe PDF reader - you'd need to install a different reader that is compatible with ePub books, like freda (available in the Marketplace in the Books & Reference category), but "freda" can only be used to read ePub books that are not locked down with DRM.

To make a long story short, you will not be able to read any DRMed books on your windows phone until Adobe, Overdrive (which is commonly used by libraries when checking out electronic books), and any other sellers and developers of these DRM protocals decide to support and provide software that is compatible with windows phones.  There is, however, a Kindle application in the works, so sometime in the future, you will be able to purchase DRMed books from Amazon.com and read them on your windows phone.

a. user say:

Hi DublinLisa,

 

Thank you for reaching out to Microsoft Community for your question today. I understand that you've deleted your VIP folders by mistake. Let me assist you with your concern.

 

You can still recover your deleted emails. However, it depends on the settings of the mail client on your phone and Outlook.com web mail.

 

First, I'd like you to check the settings of the mail client on your phone. The option to leave a copy to a server must be ticked.

 

Next, kindly check POP and deleting downloaded messages option on your Outlook.com account web mail. Follow the steps below:

  1. Sign in to your account at Outlook.com via web.
  2. Click on the Gear icon then select More mail settings.
  3. Under Managing your account, choose POP and deleting downloaded messages.
  4. The Don't let another program delete messages from Outlook must be selected.

If the above options is already set to that settings before you accidentally deleted your folders, it will automatically create a POP folder on your Outlook.com account and all deleted emails from your phone will automatically go in there.

 

On the other hand, emails will not be recovered if the options stated above was not set to that settings before you deleted your folders by mistake.
