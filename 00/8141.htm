xbin---01 say:

Seems your provider has no clue! SSL (secure socket layer) provides a way of encrypting communications between your PC and your providers server, each of which has part of a key and without that the data cannot be understood at either end. So if your provider uses SSL for email comms then this will have to be established before you can set up an email account or communicate using it.

 

It depends what software you use on the phone to create and connect an email account - on mine all these types of settings are done automatically (nokia messaging for instance). If the settings on your phone are set to use SSL for an email account and it works then I would be very surprised if your email provider does not use SSL because your phone will require what is called an SSL handshake to verify the server it is communicating with.

 

Hope that makes sense!

xbin---01 say:

Forgot to say, TLS (transport layer security) is a newer version of SSL.
