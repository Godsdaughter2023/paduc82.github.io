Sub AutoOpen()
Dim oRng As Range
If ActiveDocument.Type <> wdTypeTemplate Then
    If Len(ActiveDocument.Path) <> 0 Then
        ActiveDocument.Range.InsertParagraphAfter

    Set oRng = ActiveDocument.Range

suzanne s. barnhill say:

Apparently by "references" you mean "endnotes." The line is the Endnote Separator; see http://wordfaqs.ssbarnhill.com/NoteSeparators.htm

As for the last page problem, the issue here is that you can't (by default) put anything after your endnotes. In order to have a new page after the notes, you'll need to create it not after the notes but after the main body of the document. Sometimes it's easiest to see what you're doing in Draft view.

When you've created the new section, select the References tab and click the dialog launcher (small arrow in the bottom right corner) in the Footnotes group. Change the location from "End of document" to "End of section." Then, assuming your document has several sections before the notes, open the Page Setup dialog (the easiest way is to double-click at the top of the horizontal ruler) and select the Layout tab. For each section before your last content section, check the box for "Suppress endnotes." This will force the notes to follow your last content section, followed by the blank one-page section at the end, from which you can remove the header.

If anyone has a Quantum (or other phone) that is getting the 80004005 error, flash your phone.
