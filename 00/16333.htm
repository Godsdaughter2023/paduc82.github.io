cbarnhorst say:

6 am local time.  That means NZ goes first.  Which devices get it tomorrow or some other time in the next couple of months depends on the device's update history with regards to hardware and software configurations.  Devices known to have problematic hardware or software are not in the first wave.  MSFT evaluates telemetry from each wave, works with OEMs and third-party devs to resolve the issues that might cause a poor update experience and then goes on the next wave.  The first wave will mostly go to Surface devices and Hyper-V virtual machines as well newly released devices because all of these are well known as up to date by MSFT.  Older devices with outdated software and hardware no longer supported by an OEM are more likely to be in a much later wave.  MSFT continues to try to resolve known issues with problematical configurations until about half-way through the six-month development cycle for the next version of Windows and then releases to all still unserviced devices. 

You can "cut the line" by downloaded an ISO file when the Fall Creators Update ISO is online (in a few days) but if you suspect a delay is likely due to the device having a problematical configuration then I suggest you just wait and let MSFT continue to try to resolve issues.  Not being in the first wave indicates that something is not ready.
