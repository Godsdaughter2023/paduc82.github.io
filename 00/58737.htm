Did you build the system yourself?

 

Other than a single long beep indicating a successful POST (Power-On Self Test), any other beeps usually indicate a hardware problem.

 

I downloaded a copy of your motherboard's user manual from the MSI site:

 

MSI
760GM-E51 (FX) v1.6
 http://www.msi.com/product/mb/760GM-E51--FX-.html

 

I had to guess at the version as there was a v1.1 and a v1.6, since you said it was new I went with the v1.6. The v1.6 has an American Megatrends, Inc. BIOS, v02.61. You should be able to check the beep codes at the site below:

 

AMI BIOS Beep Codes
 http://www.bioscentral.com/beepcodes/amibeep.htm

 

Depending on what you find out from the beep codes, if you built the system yourself or you're comfortable working inside the case, I would recommend re-seating all memory sticks and the graphics card; also unplug and reconnect all connectors including power connectors and hard drives - who knows what may have worked loose. Also, visually inspect as much as you can, especially on the motherboard; look for bulging or leaking capacitors near the CPU/heatsink assembly, also look for discoloration that may be caused by heat (this is not so much of a problem as it once was but still worth a look if you're inside the case). 

 

You might also try a barebones startup, disconnect all un-necessary devices and hardware and booting with just the bare minimum. Try using just a single stick of RAM.

 

If the beep codes indicate memory, I would download Memtest86+ and burn it to disc and run it as long as possible or until the RAM starts indicating errors. If you have more than one stick, you can check each stick individually.

 

Memtest86+
 http://www.memtest.org/

 

If you had the system custom build recently, I would suggest getting in touch with the builder.

Based on your description, it seems like you just added your phone number to your profile but this does not make it your sender ID when sending out SMS.

To set up your sender ID, we need to set up your account's caller identification. Please follow the steps below for more information on how to set it up:

  1. Sign in to your account.
  2. In the Manage features section, click Caller ID.
  3. Select your country from the drop-down list.
  4. Enter your mobile number in the text box and click Verify number.
  5.  You will receive an SMS text message on your phone containing a confirmation code. Enter this code in the field displayed.
  6. If you entered the code correctly, you will see a confirmation message, and your Caller ID will be activated in 24 hours.
