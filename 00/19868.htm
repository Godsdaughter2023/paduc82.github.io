megadodo---01 say: Depending on the year the first 8 digits specify the type of phone, e.g. Nokia 6230i for O2. If these digits are not recognized/registered with a network the phone won't get service! Up until recently the last 2 digits of the first 8 specificed the final assembly country. The next 6 digits are a unique indentifier and the last digit is a checksum for the previous 6. The unique indentifier along with the first 8 digits is used to "blacklist" a phone on a network. There is no date coding associated with the IMEI (except at the factory perhaps).

1.    Open Windows Registry Editor: press Windows Key-R on your keyboard, type regedit in the “Open” box, and press Enter.
2.    Use the folder hierarchy on the left to navigate to the following location and find the one for OneDrive for Business: hkey_current_user\software\microsoft\windows\currentversion\explorer\desktop\namespace.

4.    Double-click the DWORD entry labeled System.IsPinnedToNameSpaceTree and set its value to 0 (zero).

5.    Close all of your programs and reboot your PC.
