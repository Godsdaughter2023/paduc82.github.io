If you applied that same formula to all the input cells in the column you will find that the formula adjusts because the addresses you used were relative not absolute.

So the formula that you wrote for E12: =SUM(E12:E25)+(E27:E40)<=171

mccbleue say:

Your post begs a few questions, but I know a thing or two about warranties in the EU so I will try to clarify matters.

 

Strictly speaking, the warranty is valid for the original purchaser who buys the phone from an authorised dealer, and authorised dealers would always a proof or purchase that includes the buyer's name, address, phone number, the IMEI number and the date of purchase as these are officially required to be on the document for warranty claims. The manufacturer's limited warranty is not valid if the phone is not purchased from an authorised dealer and also not transferrable to second owners.

 

However, nowadays it is often the case, especially in Europe, that the Nokia Care network can usually check the warranty status in the computer system without the need to see the proof of purchase. Often, the proof of purchase is often only used to validate where there is a discrepancy in the dates in which the warranty is valid - this is because the warranty in the company's system is recorded as starting a month after manufacture, so if your phone sat on a shelf in the shop for six months before you bought it, you might have to prove the date of purchase because whatever the system says, your warranty would be honoured from the date of purchase, not the date of manufacture.

 

This might not give you a solid answer to your specific case, but I would simply advise you not to worry too much and to discuss it with the authorised repair network in your area. The support pages of the Microsoft web site for your country will tell you where and how you can get the phone fixed.

me252 say: To insert headers and footers in Excel 2016: Step 1) Click "File." Step 2) Click "Print." Step 3) At the bottom of the printer settings, under "No Scaling," click "Page Setup."  Step 4) Click "Header/Footer" tab. Step 5) Click "Custom Header" or "Custom Footer." Type in your header or footer, and click "OK."
