karl timmermans [outlook mvp 2012-2018] say:

#1 - Whenever you click on the Send button - the email goes into the Outbox. How long it takes before it actually gets "sent" depends on your Outlook settings. If you want something to be <sent> immediately, ensure that you have the "Send immediately when connected" option checked

 

From O'2010 menu

File --> Options --> Advanced --> Send/Receive

 

#2 - When one or more settings are incorrect AND you have the "Send immediate" option properly set, Outlook would show a Send/Receive error in the status bar - something that is immediately evident (you'll see a triangle with an exclamation point beside the words "Send/Receive Error"

 

If something is "so critical" involving a 60 second timeframe - perhaps the best approach is to simply monitor the process after clicking the Send button and ensure that no error is reported.

 

Lastly, going to assume your "sending" issues are resulting because you're connecting using a different ISP from the one that was used when your email account was originally set up otherwise, an incorrect port number would result in a failed test message.

don lotter say: Thank you, but this does not answer my question.  Please read my question again.  I ALWAYS send immediately, that is my default setting.  Outlook does NOT notify me when the immediate send does not occur and the mail sits in the Outbox.  This occurs fairly often for different reasons, reasons that I cannot plan for.  I cannot be expected to look at the Outbox for every email.  Can you please give me a solution.

palcouk say:

karl timmermans [outlook mvp 2012-2018] say:
I need a pop-up type warning for this situation, if emails fail to send within one minute.

Your requirement was as stated above. 

Answer was: Everything goes to the Outbox before actually being <sent> and Outlook provides notification that an error has occurred under normal circumstances - all that is required is to check the Outlook status bar. Additionally, also stated that there is no option for a "pop-up" message in addition to or to replace the Status Bar notification. 

If your situation is such that emails do not get sent "fairly often for different reasons" - then would further suggest that what is required is to address the "cause(s)" to avoid the problem instead of looking for a "work-around" solution to the <symptom(s)>. What you're looking for is a different way for Outlook to notify you of something it already does (showing that an error has occurred in the status bar) versus a pop-up message using a standard Outlook setting (not an option). Don't know of any email client that includes your requested requirement as an option.

On the other hand, <IF> you are "constantly" encountering the scenario where items remain in the Outbox and actually are not being sent at all with no error being indicated in the status bar (as would be the case if a wrong port number was in effect) - then what really needs to be identified is why those situations occur and correct the problem at its source.

Your only other option would be to write your own custom code to monitor the Outbox and take whatever action desired based on whatever rules are set up for it. Beyond that, will have to leave it to others for any other suggestions/answers.
