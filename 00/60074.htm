a dumb toaster say:

Re5 Mini FAQ &amp; Tips

Basically, there are a lot of repetitive solutions to problems and some tips in the game that might seem commonly know, but aren't. I'm mostly a mercenaries player with control setup Type B, so most of these relate to that and not so much the technical problems like disc reading errors, but hopefully this will help anyone who reads it.

Activating taunts:
Pressing both the Left and right sticks at the same time will activate a taunt unique to Chris, Sheva, Jill and Wesker.
Chris and Wesker make a gesture with Chris saying "Come on, come on" and Wesker laughing.
Jill and Sheva both have a type of whistle.
*Spamming the sticks may activate Wesker's dash, to prevent this and just do the taunt; Hold aim with LT as Wesker won't activate his dash while aiming.


Wesker's Dash & Functions:
Wesker's dash has three variable options to move, a cancel and will drain his health by ~1/16 of full on activation.
RS=Activate[-1/16HP], Left/Right/Down LS + A=Variable_Movement[CAP3] (within 2S of other variable movement or activation), RT=Knee_Cannon_Cancel, Wall barrier/Maxed distance=Cancel.


Basically, Pressing the Right stick will activate the dash. Much like doing the quick turn normally, moving the Left stick to either left, down, or right and pressing A will swap Wesker's movement to the corresponding direction. It is important to note that only three directions can be moved and can be repeated like this for example: Down, Down, Down would do multiple 180 degree turns. Also, each variable movement must be activated within two seconds of another variable movement or from activation. Lastly, pressing RT will cancel the dash and do Wesker's special Knee Cannon halting Wesker and dealing lethal damage to any non-boss enemy.

*Hitting a wall or sprinting a very, very far distance will cause Wesker to cancel the dash.
*The dash will act as a melee move, generating a +5 second bonus if it kills an enemy in mercenaries.
*If an enemy susceptible to consistent dash hits is hit multiple times, it will "stack" melee's allowing for finishing moves like the Haymaker by Chris.


Duplicating Exchangeable Campaign Items:
Some items (most commonly rotten eggs) can be doubled up to a maximum of a full inventory by utilizing how the game saves. In the case of rotten eggs, PLAYER_A will start a level with no held items. PLAYER_B will be holding the desired item to be duplicated (rotten eggs, max is 5 per 9 slots[45]). Once the game initiates, PLAYER_B will transfer all the rotten eggs to PLAYER_A. After all the rotten eggs have been transferred, exit the game session and PLAYER_A will save their data while PLAYER_B won't.

The process gives PLAYER_A the inventory of PLAYER_B, but PLAYER_B didn't save making them keep their inventory successfully duplicating the inventory.
*If this doesn't work, restart your Xbox 360 after exiting the game as it may glitch out.


Infinite Ammo Issues:
Infinite ammo is available after completing the entire game. For each chapter or other missions like mercenaries, points are awarded based on how well the player did. In the "Bonus features" menu, guns that are fully upgraded through campaign will have the option to buy infinite ammo for them. Once the desired weapon(s) are purchased, toggle infinite ammo set to ON in the "special settings" menu. Please note that the option when the host starts a campaign game for infinite ammo will also disable infinite ammo if set to NO.

*SPOILER: The rocket launcher must be earned by beating the game in less than 5 hours on any difficulty.
*Some items like the grenade launcher and proximity mines have no legitimate way to get unlimited ammunition.
*Versus characters are also bought through the "bonus features" menu

Getting Every Weapon in the Game:
This is mainly regarding the achievements like stockpile or people who are wondering why some weapons aren't available after collecting everything and beating the entire game. Specific weapons require the first obtained weapon to be fully upgraded to be able to purchase newer ones.

VZ61 Upgraded = Gatling Gun (Chris exclusive)

Various Mini-Tips:
Items inside the inventory on the Up, Down, Left and Right correspond to the Xbox 360's Dpad. For example: pressing up on the Dpad will equip the weapon/item in the top slot in the inventory. If nothing is there, it won't do anything.

While the inventory is open (Y for Xbox 360), weapons can quickly be reloaded by pressing X on the ammunition and X on the weapon. This can be used for combining herbs as well. If the items don't match up, they swap places. Additionally, another shortcut is to press LT to equip the desired item.

On the sell treasures screen in item management or alternatively after a death, chapter, etc, the player can press RB to sell all the treasures at once instead of individually. Also, on a side note, selling treasures will not affect the "they belong in a museum" achievement. It counts the treasures as you find them, not if they are of 1+ quantity.

In single player, the MOST valuable tip I can give for getting S ranks for every chapter on professional is to utilize the restart option. I had the fun of not having Xbox Live when I decided to go through professional and the A.I. can ruin everything. Pausing the game in single player will freeze the in game actions as well, unlike in multiplayer. Knowing this, whenever either player goes into the "dying" mode, you can restart from the last checkpoint by pausing and selecting restart. This will not add any deaths or time for the level from restarts, making it easier to get the S rank.

When searching for a custom match for campaign, a triangle shaped "red icon"or "X icon" will appear beside the connection to the game. This stands for attack reaction which determines if friendly fire with certain items will affect the other player. Explosives like rockets still have affect regardless if this is set to ON or OFF.


Discarding Weapons & Ammo IMPORTANCE (Mercenaries):
Ever wanted to only get shotgun shells for the Hydra? Is the other player desperately out of ammo and you have it all? Well the solution is to discard it.

In particular, mercenaries (but still applies to campaign), only spawns ammo for the weapons the player has (with the exception of campaign spawning handgun ammo regardless). Using Wesker STARS as an example, if I were to discard his magnum and handgun; the new enemies would only drop herbs, grenades, etc like usual, but the only ammunition received would be shotgun. This is useful if you don't plan on using certain weapons.

Discarding ammo has a similar effect. If one player has 100 Handgun rounds and the other none, the chances for the enemies to drop handgun ammo are slim for both players. The drop chances for ammo are calculated as a total of how much both players need them. In this example, a player with 100 rounds is ruining the other player's chances at ammo. Also, if you notice, another example is the drop rate of green herbs. When the player is of orange health or low green, green herbs are spawned in very often. If a teammate has health items, this will actually slightly lower, but still be very high since the default drop rate is high.
*Yes, you can manually give the player ammo as well, but for large maps like Ship Deck or Missile Area where there is lots of ground to cover, this is more ideal.

Killing the Chicken in Mercenaries:
If you're planning to go for a high score in mercenaries, the chicken is an absolute must. Considering an average Majini is 300-400 points depending on the stage, having a chicken worth 2,000 is easy points. Those easy points are just the start though; once the chicken is dead, it allows for another enemy to spawn in for the rest of the game in its place upping the amount of kills/score within a certain time. Lastly, the chicken counts as one of the 150 kills required to "beat" a mercenaries level, with the exceptions of "The Mines" having 2 chickens instead of 1 like the rest of the maps. If 150 enemies are managed to be killed, each second left on the timer is counted as an additional 1,000 points. For example: 3 minutes leftover would yield 180,000 extra points to the score.

Don't Grab Combo Chests After a 60 Combo:
Ever wanted to risk a high combo thinking that a combo chest could earn you a couple extra thousand points? Well, now you don't have to. It is a very simple "rule" to memorize, but I'll explain the reasoning behind it for those who want to know why. In theory, picking up a combo chest shows 1000 points multiplied by the amount of enemies killed, but it is actually 1000 points multiplied by the amount of enemies killed minus the current combo chain bonus. You see, if I were to kill 2 enemies with a combo chest active, 1980 points would only be obtained from the combo chest, not 2000. This works because a 2 combo yields 20 points for the combo chain, and a combo chest only adds this bonus up to a maximum of 1000 per a kill making it 2000 minus the combo chain bonus of 20 equal to 1980. If we continue the pattern for this all the way to a 50-60 combo, regular enemies already get almost 1000 extra points per a kill so the combo time earns miniscule points, or at a 70+ range, nothing as enemies earn more than 1000. Besides, at a 50 combo the time it takes to pick up the combo chest is worth more points than taking the combo chest.

regardless of time leftover just from the bonus. Score from the combo generates a lot more rapidly per kill, so without a definitive answer, around the 40 combo mark is when combo chests aren't necessarily effective. Basically, grabbing the combo chests very early on still gives a lot of score, but isn't worth getting later on in the game.
