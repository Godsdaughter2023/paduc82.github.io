Hello,

johnlv say: Joe-

First, it needs to be a multi-select listbox control.  Find the Multi Select property and set it to Simple or Extended.  (I recommend extended for the most flexibility.)  Then you need some VBA code to go through the ItemsSelected collection of the listbox and pick of the values to use in a filter.

Your code might look like:

Dim varItem As Variant, strWhere As String

    For Each varItem in Me.ListBox.ItemsSelected
        strWhere = strWhere & Me.ListBox.ItemData(varItem) & ", "
    Next varItem
    ' Strip off the ending comma
    strWhere = Left(strWhere, Len(strWhere) - 2)
    ' Turn it into an IN clause
    strWhere = "MyID IN (" & strWhere & ")"
    ' Now open a report or form filtered
    DoCmd.OpenReport  "MyForm", WhereCondition:=strWhere

What is the Calorie Adjustment in my Exercise Diary?

MyFitnessPal pulls your calorie burn directly from our total daily calorie App Gallery partners. Since partner devices track minute to minute, and our program views the entire day as a whole, we then project forward to the end of the day based on your calorie burn so far. When viewing your adjustment (either by tapping the “i” next to the adjustment in your Exercise Diary on our main website at www.MyFitnessPal.com, or by tapping the adjustment itself in the app), you should see a number directly under the partner, which represents the instant sync, and a number to the right of the partner which represents our projection for the day based on your current sync.

Because the adjustment value is recalculated every time your device uploads new data, you should expect this value to change over the course of the day. It is also necessary to make sure your partner device and MyFitnessPal are set to the same time zones (time zones can be updated in the main settings). At midnight, the projected sync and your actual sync should then match exactly. Depending on when you last sync, there may be a slight variation between one day and the next as the two values catch up to each other.

On the calculation page, under the partner totals, you should then see your MyFitnessPal total. The MyFitnessPal total includes not only your daily goals, but also the calories needed to reach the goals you have set within the program, such as lose 1 pound, (we originally deduct these calories when providing your daily goals on MyFitnessPal, they then must be added back in when comparing against your actual total burn from your partner device). Since our program instantly awards calories to your day when exercises are added into the cardiovascular section, to avoid double credit, we also add your cardiovascular totals in with your MyFitnessPal total.

When your device partner projected total is Greater than your MyFitnessPal total, you will then see a positive adjustment of the difference added to your MyFitnessPal account. If your device partner projected total is Less than your MyFitnessPal total, you will then either see a 0, or if you have opted to turn on Negative adjustments, you will see a deduction of calories. If the adjustment is negative this indicates the partner's reported calorie burn is lower than what MyFitnessPal’s has already provided for you.

Because the adjustment value is recalculated every time your device uploads new data, you should expect this value to change over the course of the day.

The default template doesn't have double spacing. It's 1.15 with a 10pt space at the end of a paragraph. If you really have double spacing, your normal template was changed and you might try deleting/renaming normal.dotm.

Open xml is the new format used starting with Office 2007. It's basically a zip archive containing xml files. The Word files in this format have the extensions docx, dotx, dotm, and docm. By default Word saves documents in those formats. If you still want to use the older formats (as 2007 templates don't run in 2003 for example) you should make sure you changed the file type when saving the document!

Check the details of the error message you get when you try to open the Word document. Do you see something similar to

/word/document.xml, Line: 2, Column: a huge number 

If so, then something went wrong while saving the document. Index entries and equation naming are the best suspects. The document is most likely recoverable, but it requires some xml knowledge. The process involves opening the file as a zip archive, and fix the issue in the document.xml file. If you have no xml knowledge, and the document isn't confidential, then me or someone else might be able to help you if you upload the document somewhere.

If your error message has different details then above, you might want to try the "Open and Repair" option in the "Open" dialog, or as an extreme solution, you might want to try using OpenOffice (http://www.openoffice.org), a free Office alternative, which reads the same formats but is a bit better at recovering from errors.

julie fka profjulie say: There is some loss in functionality between WMDC and ActiveSync, but not what you describe.

When you create a new syncing relationship between your computer and your pocket pc, you may be asked how you wish to handle the very first sync between the two devices since both devices may have data on them.  But after the first sync, Windows Mobile Device Center should handle things in much the way way it handled things in ActiveSync:
  • If you change specific files on the pocket pc and not on the computer, the changes on the pocket pc should get transferred to your computer;
  • If you change specific files on the computer, but not pocket pc, the changes on the computer get transferred to your pocket pc.
  • You will set an option in WMDC that tells it how to handle potential conflicts that deal with changes made to the same files on both the pocket pc and the computer under the "Manage a partnership" screen of WMDC. 
If your experience is different than this, then there is an issue with the partnership between your Pocket PC and your computer.
