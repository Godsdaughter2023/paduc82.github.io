Outlook for Mac does not support CalDAV or CardDAV. These protocols are needed to sync to Google, iCloud, Yahoo, AOL and other services that require these protocols.

Outlook supports Exchange that does sync contacts and calendars. You get get Exchange online for $4/month.

Exchange Online

http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/exchange/microsoft-exchange-online-email-for-business-FX103739072.aspx

Sync Basics

CalDAV & CardDAV are protocols used by Apple, Google, Yahoo and others to sync contacts and calendars. Neither Entourage 2008 nor Outlook 2011 support CalDAV or CardDAV. 

Apple removed sync in Mavericks with iTunes.

You can no longer sync with an iPhone or iPad via iTunes. You can only sync with an iPhone or iPad via a cloud-based service like iCloud or Google.
You can no longer sync local calendars with OS X Calendar (formerly iCal). You can, however, continue to sync calendars hosted on a cloud-based service like iCloud or Google with Apple's Calendar.
You can no longer sync with any third-party apps running on your Mac that use Sync Services, like Entourage or Outlook.

If this is a feature that you consider useful, be sure to let Microsoft know by sending them feedback (via the Send Feedback command in Outlook’s Help menu)

The more reports they get the higher it gets on the list.

Outlook 2016 Limits Search Which is a Problem - Microsoft Community

joseph_rsf say:

Outlook 2016 Limits Search Which is a Problem

I am using Outlook 2016 which is part of Office 365 with Win 7 on my desktop and Win 10 on my Surface Pro 4.  I am having a problem with Search.  My email address uses outlook.com.  I have been using Outlook.com for the past 2 years and never had this Search problem.  I am told that Microsoft has made changes to the outlook.com servers.

I recreated my profile to make sure I am using the correct Exchange server on outlook.com.  After opening Outlook, I try to search on my current folder which is the Inbox and only 75 returns are provided when I know there are many more emails that should be listed.  Under Options, I have unchecked the "improve search speeds by limiting the number of searches."  I was told by the Microsoft store that I should be getting at least 250 results.  How do I correct this Search problem and if possible I would like unlimited search results which is what I used to receive?

Thank you for your assistance.

roady [mvp] say:

When you scroll down your search results and there are more results available, up to 75 additional results are being returned until there are no more. This is a feature of Exchange-level search to provide the results more quickly and consistently across all your devices.

For additional information and options to return it to Windows-level search, see this post discussing a related search question to which the same solution applies:

joseph_rsf say:

Thank you for your assistance.  I appreciate your time and efforts.

I installed the "DisableServerAssisted Search" into my registry.  Now when I search on a word I get many emails, e.g. 1,500, but they only go back 1 year which is now Feb 2016.  I then scroll to the bottom of the list and click on "More".  What is now returned are only 250 of the most recent emails.  I was hoping to find emails that go past the 1 year timeframe.  Any suggestions?

Thank you.

roady [mvp] say:

You probably have the Sync Slider set to 1 year only so only 1 year is locally cached and searchable.

You can adjust the Sync Slider by;
File-> Account Settings-> Account Settings...-> double click on your email account

joseph_rsf say:

I moved the Sync Slider over to "all" for keeping all my mail offline.  My 1st search goes back 1 year with all emails.  I scroll to bottom of the list and click "More".  What is now returned is 250 emails that only go back a few months.  Is there a way to search past the 1 year mark?  The Synch Slider option to "all" did not solve.

Thank you again for your assistance.

roady [mvp] say:
Wait for the sync to complete and then wait for the indexing to complete. Depending on the amount of emails that you have, this could take several hours. This is also one of the reasons why they switched it to Exchange-level search.
