dawn b say:

Recommended/"best" image file formats to insert into PowerPoint 2010 presentations

We are currently moving from PowerPoint 2002/XP to 2010. Many of these can now be output to JPG or TIF if requested or required by the managers but change is slow.

steve rindsberg say:

For simple graphics, EMFs will often be smaller than the equivalent bitmap format, and will be scaleable -- you can enlarge them to any degree w/o their going jaggy on you.  This assumes that the EMF contains vector (ie, drawing) graphics and not bitmap graphics.  EMFs and WMFs can contain both.  Since you refer to them as "image files" I'm wondering if that's the problem right there:  images are stored in EMFs/WMFs in uncompressed BMP format ... meaning they're going to be BIG.  And while the vector portion of the graphics will be scaleable, the bitmaps won't.

If the graphics are complex, if they contain lots of curves, fancy gradients and such, and ESPECIALLY if they include bitmap images, an appropriately sized PNG or JPG may well be much smaller than the equivalent EMF.

It's fairly simple to test.  Take one of your existing files, make a copy of it.  Then for each EMF graphic, select it, press CTRL+C to copy it, delete it, then use Paste Special, As PNG, to paste a reasonably sized PNG back onto the slide.  Save the presentation.  Compare size and quality.

If you need a bit more quality out of the PNGs, enlarge the EMF before copy/pasting it.
