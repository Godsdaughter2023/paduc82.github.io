How do you carry down conditional formatting from cell to cell AND - Microsoft Community

nmgco say:

How do you carry down conditional formatting from cell to cell AND have it pick up the values of the row and not the original cell

In previous versions, you would create your conditional formatting and then when you copied and pasted the cells down to the next row, it would pick up the value of the current row and NOT the original row.  How do I do this so I do not have to recreate the same condition for every row?

Example:

Row 1 Cell A1 -

Conditional format1 (A1>Today()) Red w/ white bold font

Conditional format2 (A1<Today()+5) Yellow w/ auto font

I copy/paste this now into Row 2 and get the reference to A1 and NOT A2...so it does not carry down.  The contidional format should be for A2:

Conditional format1 (A2>Today()) Red w/ white bold font

Conditional format2 (A2<Today()+5) Yellow w/ auto font

jeeped say:

With Excel 2007's Conditional Formatting, I  always Use a formula to determine which cells to format. In your case, the first rule would be:

=IF(A1>TODAY(), TRUE, FALSE)  (Red w/ white bold font)

...and the second rule would be:

=IF(A1<TODAY()+5, TRUE, FALSE)  (Yellow w/ auto font)

I had some similar difficulties getting the Conditional Formatting acting the way I wanted, and after I started to exclusively use formulas to discern the criteria my problems went away. I just tried those two supplied conditions on a test worksheetsheet (e.g. copy & paste to another location and/or filling down) and everything went as expected.

jim thomlinson say:

Be careful with absolute vs relative references. If you CF has $ signs in the cell addresses then the references are absolute and the behaviour you describe will happen. Without the $ signs then you have relative referencing and all should be well.
