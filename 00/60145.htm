Hi Gerald,

Recovery Partition has created automatically, to hold the Windows Recovery Environment, which will allow you to use Windows Recovery options. The recovery partition on Windows 10 consumes about 450MB, Windows 8/8.1 200MB, and Windows 7 100MB.

Some computer manufacturer like Dell and HP has made their computer come with an OEM recovery partition, which contains OEM partition Windows Recovery options and their factory installation files. It is usually located in D: drive.

We are not allowed to delete the recovery partition. Once you upgrade your Operating system to Windows 10, Recovery Partition will be automatically created.

You may reinstall your Operating System, so that, you may allot enough volume to your D: drive to store the Recovery Partition, then allot all the remaining space to your C: Drive.

Let us know if you have any other concerns.
