Instructions

  1. Open your gamer profile from the Xbox Dashboard or Guide and select "Edit Gamer Profile." We'll use "Player1" as a gamertag.

  2. Select "Profile Name" and enter a new gamer profile name, like "Player2," then sign out of the Xbox Dashboard.

  6. Sign back into the Dashboard using your original profile (renamed "Player2,") then quickly change the profile name back to your original profile name ("Player1.") Your games and achievements will now be associated with your original gamertag.

You'll need to set up another Microsoft Passport Network account during signup, since your original account will stay linked to the "Player3" gamertag. nickj5206 say:

If the above suggestions don't solve your lip-sync problems you might want to do a search for "lip-sync correction".  You will find there are "many" things that can cause audio video sync error other than your computer.  The classic problem is caused by video processing taking much longer than audio processing which causes the audio to come first.

Of course hearing sound "before" lips move is impossible in the real world so our reaction to this un-natural - actually "impossible" - phenomenon is to avoid it, possibly by looking away from the characters' lips. Research at Stanford found we have feelings about the characters as if they aren't making eye contact with us when lip-sync is off. We feel they are less truthful, more agitated, less successful, etc. That study can be found via a search for: "Effects of Audio-Video Asynchrony on Viewer's ". What's ironic is that "we" are the ones not looking the characters in the eye as we try to avoid the impossibility of sound "before" lip-movement. Video processing delays within your computer, within your display, during editing, during broadcast or encoding and disc mastering are cumulative and if not precisely cancelled by adding equivalent audio delays create lip-sync error.  Since it isn't fixed and changes between programs and DVD and BluRay's the only way to have "perfect lip-sync" is to adjust a variable delay while watching the content at the last stage - the final display. Companies like ALchemy2, Felston, Primare, etc. make external delay boxes that intercept s/pdif audio and allow adjustment via remote control which will correct lip-sync error no matter where along the broadcast chain it occurred. Unless, of course, somewhere along the chain in an effort to cancel video delay too much audio delay has already been added.  Luckily most displays nowadays add 50 to 100 ms (some 4K displays add over 200 ms) so with a variable delay you can actually correct that much delayed audio by using your display's inherent video delay.

The system is telling you to do a disk check. It can't do it because you are still running the computer. You will need to restart the computer in order for it to do disk check.

Here is how to do diskcheck. I am using my own words instead of the tutorial which I feel is liable to get people confused.

Start button > in the search box, type cmd > look to top left, right click at cmd.exe > click Run As Administrator > press Enter key.
You will now see a black and white window. That is your cmd window.
At the flashing prompt, type this .......   chkdsk /f/r
It is best if you copy/paste that above command line to avoid typo. There is a space between "chkdsk" and "/".

press Enter key.
The window will say something like...( I'm paraphrasing).... it can't do it now, but will do it at restart. Do you want to do so, y/n.
Type  " y " as in yes  > press Enter.

Exit cmd window.
Restart your computer > the system will start doing disk check.
It will take quite some time.... a long time. Just sit back and wait. DON'T turn it off.
The system will restart when disk check is completed.

 

Now, go to Safe Mode and do your system restore. Is it successful ?
