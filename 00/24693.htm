twinnedsum12728 say:

Windows 10 refuses to connect your device, This advice may or may not help.

A lot of people are having problems connecting Ipod/Ipad/Iphone and other devices to their Windows 10 PC/Laptop. This could be due to no drivers installed or an outdated driver. When Troubleshooter fails to fix it, Or searching forums and other other discussions just simply won't fix. This may or may not help. It has helped me connect my wife's Samsung Tab, and her Ipod. So here it goes.

1. Open Start Menu, click on Settings.

2. Open Devices.

3. Scroll down on the page that opens, and search for "Related Settings". Click on "Devices and Printers"

4. This will bring up another screen, It will have all devices connected to your PC. Search for Unknown Device, or I-pod (Unknown), or Samsung Unknown Device. Anything along these lines... Now Right click this icon, and select "Properties".

5. At the top of this screen, choose "Hardware". In the list on Hardware screen, Double click the device.  This will bring up another screen.

6. Now Under the "General" Tab, click "Change Settings", Click on the "Driver" Tab. Click "Update Driver".

7. Now 2 options, Search Internet or computer for drivers. I would choose Search Internet option here. This will take a moment or so depending on your internet speed. IT should start downloading, then installing this driver on its own.

8. Once Driver is installed, click close, Go to "This PC", On the left hand column you should now see your device here.

Good luck, hope this helps, its helped me connect all kind of stubborn devices that refuse to install or show up.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

Are you just looking for the degree symbol (°)? If so, you can insert it in Word by pressing Ctrl+Shift+@, Spacebar (or of course you can insert it from the Symbol dialog, where it is U00B0). You can insert it in any Windows application by pressing the Alt key and typing 0176 on the numeric keypad (with NumLock enabled).

If you are looking for a symbol that combines that symbol with the letter F (℉), then it is Unicode character 2109. You will not find this character in most fonts (though you can look in the Symbol dialog), but in Word if you type 2109 and then immediately press Alt+X, you will get the symbol in some font or other. Here it is Cambria Math in Word 2013.
