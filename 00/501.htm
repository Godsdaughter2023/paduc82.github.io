brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

Your Exchange admins can tell you how to set up your Exchange account.  In general, you will need to know 1) your Exchange authentication domain, your Exchange username and password (what you use to authenticate to OWA), and your Exchange server name.  In my version of OWA, I can't find an "About" option under Options and I'm using Exchange 2003.

What you get in answer to 2) varies depending on your configuration.  If OWA contains Bob and Chuck then the Contacts folder in Outlook that is contained int he Exchange mailbox will, of course, contain Bob and Chuck.  if you have a Personal Folders file with a different Contacts folder containing Adam and Bob, then, of course, that Contacts folder will contain Adam and Bob.  If you elect to move Adam to the Exchange Contacts folder, then you'll have Adam, Bob, and Chuck in that folder.  Your Exchange mailbox and your PST won't be merged automatically for a normal configuration.  If you elect to specify the PST as delivery location, however, everything will be sucked from the Exchange mailbox (Contacts, Calender, Email, and so on) and put in your PST.  This is not what you want.  Instead, you'll want to leave the Exchnage mailox as the delivery location and copy (or move) the contents of your PST to the Exchange mailbox.

Thanks for your reply Juan.  Yes, we went through all this but it was unclear as to where the problem was.  So I got help with the online chat and I've posted the following info for others who may find the issue confusing. 

Though our situation has been remedied, for those who have followed the instructions but are are still having an issue connecting, there were a number of things that seemed unclear to us that we thought might be helpful to others to know about.  

  1. You do not have to enter your billing information on the "Skype My Account" page.
  2. Once activated, the Skype minutes are stated--and will be updated--on the "Skype My Account" page rather than within the Skype program.
  3. Within the Skype program (under Calls > Call Phones on a PC), Skype indicates that there are 0 minutes available and there is 1 subscription.  There is no indication of subscription's minutes or balance left on this page (see 2 above). 
  4. Though there are 60 countries to which you can call, there are only 8 countries to which you can call a mobile number ( see https://www.skype.com/en/offers/office365 ).  When only a landline connection is permitted and you call a mobile number, Skype simply cuts-off with no indication as to why it has done so.  We were calling England, so it would have been helpful for us to know that we were actually calling a mobile number rather than a landline.
