ogerriz say:

How do I set-up a dynamic filter in my macro

I do weekly data dumps from our project database.  This dump goes from col A to col KA and can be from 150 rows to over 2000 rows.  No telling how many rows of data will come up.

 

I have recorded a macro doing all the formatting and hiding of non-useful information but I need to send this spreadsheet which now has 10 cols of data to other sales team members for comment so I recorded a macro to filter for project amounts not equal to zero in the column AK which is always the column I need.  The macro lines relating to it are:

    Columns("KC:KC").ColumnWidth = 12.29
    Range("AK1").Select

I tried using the macro for more than 154 rows and it seems to work but am worried that at some stage it will encounter errors if I don't fix it or make it dynamic.

 

Thanks

ossiemac say:

I don't believe that  Criteria1:=Array("<>0"), Operator:=xlFilterValues will work. The Operator xlFilterValues is for when you select several options (more than 2) from the AutoFilter Dropdown.

Also Selection.AutoFilter toggles AutoFilter On and Off. If AutoFilter just happens to be on because you had run the code once then the next time it will fail because the code turns AutoFilter Off. This is a problem when using AutoFilter.Range as per the example below and hense the line to ensure AutoFilter is Off before turning On.

ActiveSheet.AutoFilter.Range can be used as the dynamic range for the AutoFiltered data. No need to find the last row.

 

Try the following code.

Range("AK1").Select
Rows("1:1").RowHeight = 43.5
Columns("KC:KC").ColumnWidth = 12.29
ActiveSheet.AutoFilterMode = False      'Ensures AutoFilter is Off before turning On.
Range("AK1").Select
Selection.AutoFilter
ActiveSheet.AutoFilter.Range.AutoFilter Field:=37, Criteria1:="<>0"

 

Note: No operator is required with the Criteria1 in the above code.

Hotmail has several features to stop receiving spam messages. In order to filter it, I suggest that you follow one of the options below:

A. Block a sender or a domain name

1. Sign in to your account. Go to Inbox.
2. Click Options located at the upper-right side of the page.
3. Select More Options.
4. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.
5. Click Blocked senders.
6. Enter the email address or domain on the box labeled Blocked email address or domain: and click Add to list >>.

B. Sweep function - This will allow all received or even future emails coming from the specific sender be deleted. Here are the steps to activate the Sweep option:

1. Sign in to your Hotmail account. Go to Inbox.
2. Check the specific sender you would like to filter.
3. On the upper part, click Sweep
4. Choose Delete all from
5. On the pop-up box, just click Delete all.

Note: You can also block future messages from that sender by checking the box beside Also block future messages.

C. Rules - we can create a rule that can block a specific word from the subject of the spam email.

1. Sign in to your Hotmail account with your Microsoft account password.
2. Click on Options then More options... in the upper right corner of the page.
3. Under the Customizing Hotmail section, click on Rules for sorting new messages.
4. Click on the New button.
5. Under Step 1: Which messages do you want this rule to apply to, 
    a. First drop-down menu - choose Subject
    b. Second drop-down menu - choose contains
    c. Third field - input the word you would like to block
6. Under Step 2: What action do you want to apply, click on Delete these messages
7. Click Save button.
