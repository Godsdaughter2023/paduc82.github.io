suzanne s. barnhill say:

Earlier versions of Word had several "presets," one of which was Formal (others were something like Colloquial or Casual and so on). Now Word provides a menu of individual items for you to choose. Essentially, "formal" grammar requires that you check all of the boxes under Grammar and probably most of them under Style. Find these check boxes via File | Options | Proofing | Settings (under "When correcting spelling and grammar in Word"). You'll also need to change the setting at the top from "Grammar" to "Grammar & Style." If you are following a style manual that proscribes certain uses (passive voice, use of first person), then you'll want to be sure to check those boxes.
