You could try your luck with the following wild card Search/Replace operation, which assumes the following:
1. You want to replace Unicode RIGHT SINGLE QUOTATION MARK (HTML entity &#8217;) with Unicode APOSTROPHE (HTML entity &#39;) when the former is used to replace elided vowels in English contractions.
2. Use copy/paste from a word document to enter the character for RIGHT SINGLE QUOTATION MARK (“smart” or “curly” apostrophe) into the Find What box. Or use "Alt+8217".
3. Most English contractions are made up of “Apostrophe” + “d, or l, or m, or r, or s, or t, or v" (see English auxiliaries and contractions).

So you could try using the following wild card Search/Replace:
Find What:
(')([dlmrstv])

Replace With:
^39\2

HTH.
