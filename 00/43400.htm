arminatorx say:

...But someone said it could be condensation that built up to about a cup of water inside my xbox which sounds as likely as someone cursing me with a demon that with my xbox every 6 months.

Condensation only happens if warm room air hits a cold surface. Unless your Xbox was moved outside into the cold winter air, and brought back into a humid, warm room, condensation will not happen. 

However, if your console is next to an old window, it could happen that during the night, the humid air that you breathe out while you sleep, is at your body temperature. 

If the air is humid enough, and hits the windowpane, which is usually colder than the air in the room, it could lead to condensation. That condensation can then run down the window, and collect on the windowsill. 

If your console was sitting there, the water from the window might collect under the console. 

Other than that, I doubt that condensation happens in the console, as long as the console is at least at the same temperature as the surrounding air. Condensation can only happen, if an object is significantly cooler than the air around it.

And as the others said: There usually is no liquid inside the console that could leak. While the Xbox One X has a vapor chamber for "quasi"-liquid cooling, the original Xbox One is bone dry on the insides. 

So the liquid under your console is 100% coming from an outside source.
