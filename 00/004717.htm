Getting rid of left tab "corner brackets" on ruler line of MS - Microsoft Community

bookwormkw say:

Getting rid of left tab "corner brackets" on ruler line of MS Word (03-07 version)

I am putting paragraph indentions into a World manuscript and numerous left tab "corner brackets" are appearing on the ruler line and even extending past the manuscript page.  Can anyone tell me how to get rid of them?

Thanks for any help.

jay freedman say:

If this is just for specific paragraphs in the particular document:

  • Select some or all of the paragraphs.
  • Double-click the ruler to open the Tabs dialog box. Click the Clear All button and click OK.
  • Repeat as necessary.

If it's for all the paragraphs in one or more particular styles, or for all your documents, you need to remove the tab stops from the styles in the template underlying the documents.

  • Right-click the name of the style in the Home ribbon or in the Styles pane, and click Modify.
  • At the bottom of the Modify dialog, click the option button "New documents based on this template".
  • Click the Format button and click Tabs in the menu. This opens the same Tabs dialog as shown above, but this one applies to the tab stops defined in the style.
  • Clear all the tabs, and OK both dialog boxes.

Redefining the tabs in the template's styles won't fix the styles in existing documents. For that, you can use the Organizer.
