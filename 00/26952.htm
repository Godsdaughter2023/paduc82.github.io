andrewnz74 say:

Sending email via smtp.office365.com - User could not be authenticated.

We came up against an issue where we could not send emails via smtp.office365.com from some servers, these servers were SAP workloads running on Suse Linux.

After testing using telnet (telnet smtp.office365.com 25) from these machines to confirm the correct ports had been opened they connected but still when an SMTP message was sent it failed all the time.

After checking on various other machines, my laptop some other servers, they would all work and deliver mail as expected.

It turns out that it was the TLS version being used on the SAP machines, make sure it's the newest possible, in our case the SAP team had to do a kernel upgrade which took the TLS version to 1.2.  

After testing it worked, I thought I'd post this in case anyone else has had this issue or might like to comment.

lars-Åke aspelin say:

I've created an appointment log to help project future appointment dates based on last seen date.  I want the projected date to be formatted with pink fill if the date calculated returns a date on a Saturday or Sunday.  The future appointment date is a formula (=last appt date + 7 days for weekly appts, last appt date +14 for everyother week, and last appt date + 30 days for monthly visits).  It would help catch those falling on Saturday and Sunday if the conditional formatting could be applied.  I can't figure it out.

Assuming your date is in cell E2.

Select cell E2 and click on "Conditional Formatting" in the "Styles" section of the "Home" toolbar.

Click  on "New Rule..."

Click on "Use a formula to determine which cells to format" in the "Select a Rule Type" section

Type =WEEKDAY($E$2,2)>5 in the "Format values where this formula is true:" box in the "Edit the Rule Description" section

Click on the "Format..." button

Click on the  "Background Color" you prefer in the "Fill" tab of the "Format cells" popup window

Click on OK

Click on OK

If you want the same format to be applied to other cells, use the "Format Painter" in the "Clipboard" section of the "Home" toolbar.
