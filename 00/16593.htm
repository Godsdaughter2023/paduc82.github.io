How to identify the product key

Microsoft product keys contain 25 characters that are typically divided into groups of 5.

Where to locate the product key

The product key is typically located on an orange or yellow sticker on the back of the CD case or the DVD case. If the Windows operating system that you are using was preinstalled by your computer manufacturer, you may find the Windows product key sticker on the computer. To find the product key for a full version, check the following locations:

·Sticker on the CD case or other packaging.

·Certificate of Authenticity.

·Confirmation e-mail message that was received from an online purchase.

·Retail perpetual product key, located inside the new Product Key Card available at retail stores.

·Retail perpetual product key, purchased online from Office Online (http://office.microsoft.com) with the full download of Office.

If you still cannot find the product key for your full version,

Make sure that you store the product key together with the product CD or DVD in a safe location in case you have to reinstall the product in the future. The product cannot be reinstalled without both the product key and the CD or DVD.

Product keys are specific to their software products. For example, you cannot use a Windows product key to unlock a Microsoft Office product.

cbarnhorst say:

Please give the Insider build you are using.

The telemetry that goes back to Microsoft concerns your build and your device, not you.  However, you can limit the level of detail that MSFT collects. 

You should not ever use an Insider preview build on your primary or production device and especially not on one that you use for financial and personal data.  Some kinds of bugs can open your system to a hacker exploit.  All preview builds have at least some bugs and finding them is the primary purpose of the Insider program.  Another important purpose is to allow Insiders to test the usability of new features.

If you are using a preview on your main computer opt out of the program immediately.  Not only are you risking your data to a buggy copy of Windows, Microsoft does not offer any kind of support to any Insider device, meaning if you do have a crash you are on your own to save what you can. 

Of course, all users should make system backup regularly regardless of how a device is being used and if you haven't been doing this, please start.

cbarnhorst say:

You should opt out of Insider previews on your primary computer.  If you don't have another device to use for evaluating or testing Insider previews then the Insider program is not for you.  I'm glad you are getting a backup drive.  There are a number of free backup programs that are highly recommended by others in the forum and you can read reviews of free backup programs online by searching on "Best free backup programs for Windows 10."  I use a paid program, Acronis True Image.

The simplest way to opt out is to go to the Windows Insider Program page (type that in Search/Cortana) and click on the button to Stop Receiving Insider Previews at the top.  You will see the following options.  Choose the fourth.  Once our current Redstone 4 build releases to the public, you will get that and then your settings will be automatically changed so that you do not get any more Insider software.  You don't have to change any of the other options or wait and opt out manually as suggested elsewhere, although you can do it that way if you want.
