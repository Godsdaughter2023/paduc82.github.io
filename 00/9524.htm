jeff plc say: Backing up your files is a good practice whether or not you're installing a new application.  Hardware/software failures are an inevitable part of computing.  Scheduling a regular backup of your files (say, weekly) would prevent the loss of important files should anything ever happen.  Also, backing up your important files to an external hard drive would give you something small and quick to grab should there ever be a disaster (a fire, etc.).

Concerning your upgrade, you don't need to backup your files before moving to Office 2010.  Your files will not be deleted in the process of upgrading to Office 2010.  And yes, you should probably uninstall Office 2007 before installing Office 2010 with a restart between the uninstall and the new install.  Note that once you've uninstalled Office 2007, the icons for your files will change as Windows will temporarily not have a default application with which to open those files.  This will be resolved once you install Office 2010.

Outlook vs. Outlook Connector - Microsoft Community

Outlook vs. Outlook Connector

I have Outlook Connector 2013. I asked MSFT about switching to Outlook 2016, and I was told that to install Outlook 2016, I'd have to delete the Connector. That means deleting many, many e-mails - some of them useful along with contacts. Is there a way to install Outlook 2016 without loss of the files from Connector? (Or is it not worth while doing anyway?)

I have Windows 10, Office 365/2016 (other than Outlook).

roady [mvp] say:

Outlook 2016 connects to Outlook.com/Hotmail via the Exchange ActiveSync protocol and therefor no longer needs the Outlook Hotmail Connector.

Upon upgrading to Office 2016 and starting Outlook 2016 for the first time, Outlook will detect your Outlook Hotmail Connector account and will replace this with an EAS account. You can then safely uninstall the Outlook Hotmail Connector from your computer.

Note that the reconfiguration will cause all your Mail, Calendar and Contact items to be synched again. Also, if you kept any data in the Tasks, Notes or Journal folders of the Outlook Hotmail Connector account (or other folders marked with "This computer only"), you'll need to export that content to a pst-file first as that data will be lost since it didn't sync with the Outlook.com servers.
