Vegas style Klondike Solitaire in Solitaire Collection is defective. - Microsoft Community

tony_stone say:

Vegas style Klondike Solitaire in Solitaire Collection is defective. The latest fix made it worse.

Vegas style Solitaire in the Solitaire Collection has two problems.  The bigger one is that it does not keep score correctly.  The less noticeable one is that it thinks that all moves have been made when there are still moves to make.

Correct scoring is in dollars.  It cost $52 to play a hand.  This includes the first hand, so when the game starts, it should start at -$52 (negative fifty-two dollars) unless the player is using cumulative scoring.  In which case, the starting score is the last score minus $52.  Fifty-two dollars is your bet.  For each card moved to the Aces stack, the player is paid $5.  A winning hand is one where the player ends up with more money than he starts.  Eleven cards is a winning hand because you won $55 - $52 (original bet) = $3, a big $3 win.  If you get all cards up to the Aces stacks, then your win is $208, (52 cards X $5) - $52.  There is no bonus in Vegas Style Solitaire.  A casino is not going to give a bonus of more than $3000 each time a player wins $208.  Changes needed.  Subtract $52 from the first hand as you do for the other hands.  Stop adding a bonus to the score, especially when playing cumulative scoring.  I repeat, KILL the BONUS.

When all the cards are played and all the stacks look correct, the game thinks that all moves have been made.  Usually, it is correct.  Sometimes not.  Sometimes you might have a card such as a seven of clubs as the top card of a stack.  You might be able to play a seven of spades, which is in another stack covered by a six, five, four and three.  The game thinks all moves are done and you can agree or undo the last move.  The last move was to bring down the 7 of clubs.  If you undo it, you will not be able to move the 6, 5, 4 & 3 from the seven of spades so that you can play it.  In addition to the Undo last move button, add a Continue button which will allow you to continue playing until you think you are done.

andre da costa say:

All you really need to do is boot normally. There's one thing to remember when installing an existing Windows 7 on a hard disk in new hardware – don't choose a repair install. It won't give you any problems, and will probably fail, but it does waste time. Booting normally, Windows will revert to default drivers where necessary, and will either go online to download what you need (or in the case of the new network drivers and USB 3.0 drivers needed for the motherboard, it will prompt you for the appropriate driver disk, which should be available on the motherboard disk that came with your new computer). The whole process should take less than 15 minutes and a couple reboots, including downloading and installing all the Windows and application updates that had accumulated since last September.

Finally you will have to reactivate some of the applications on your PC – including Windows itself.
