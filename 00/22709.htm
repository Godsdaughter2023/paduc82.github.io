In the Registry editor, browse to:


HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\


Right click on the Mail key and Export a copy for backup.

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\

papaerns say:

In a more techinical sense, FM transmission and FM reception (receiver) are 2 quite different things. they work in tandem with your phone. basically your phone uses two signals to make a call. one to receive FM signals, this is the calls you listen to. and another frequency to send your voice when you talk.

 

In the context of flight mode or offline mode. both of these are turned off since there are flight laws that mandate all radio communication to be turned of so that they don't interfere with the plane's radio transmission and reception. between you and me, Discovery's Mythbusters debunked that and proved that modern planes are shielded from outside interference with regards to radio waves.

 

other areas of precaution would be hospitals where some equipment work via radio waves and mining sites (where they use a certain frequency (very dissimilar to cell frequencies) to detonate charges. But it's best to be safe than sorry.

 

In the context of Radio reception as in music, I very much doubt if this will be enabled during offline mode. haven't tried or tested it personally, though. Since WIFI is still enabled during offline mode, you can enjoy internet radio which functions on a very different signal.

 

Offline mode saves battery for the s60 devices such as your phone because it doesn't have to use power to transmit or receive signals. as opposed to turning off your phone, offline mode uses less power than having to power up your device.

 

cheers.
