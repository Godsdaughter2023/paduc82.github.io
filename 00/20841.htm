jay freedman say:

I think the root of your problem is that you're working with Windows' libraries, possibly the most confusing thing Microsoft has ever invented. The best solution is to close the Libraries icon and never touch it again.

A "library" is not a real folder, it's a sort of shortcut to a collection of folders that do exist in other places on your drive. That's why you think you're seeing dozens of places -- you can see the real folders as well as the shortcuts to them in various libraries. For some reason, the My Documents icon under Libraries > Documents drags in thousands of files that aren't really in the My Documents folder at all.

If you want to bother to learn about libraries, here are a few links:

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows7/products/features/libraries

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows7/working-with-libraries

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/libraries-faq#1TC=windows-7

The "proper" place for Word documents is the "My Documents" folder that you're already using, or subfolders that you create within it. One thing to check out is in Word, click File > Options > Save and make sure the path to "My Documents" is shown in the "Default file location" box.

Another thing that will help is to use Windows Explorer to open the folder C:\Users\Jeff, grab the My Documents folder's icon on the right side, and drag-and-drop it to the Favorites icon on the left side. It will stay there, and also appear in the Office dialogs for Open and Save As. Clicking the My Documents icon under Favorites will go directly to that real folder, avoiding the Libraries nonsense.

jay freedman say:

If you can see the index marking (XE fields), you must turn off the display of hidden text before you update the Index field. The problem is that when the XE field codes are displayed, they take up extra space and change the locations of page breaks. The XE fields are marked as Hidden, and you have to hide them, either by clicking the ¶ button on the Home tab or by opening the Options dialog to the Display page and unchecking the "Hidden text" box.

john korchok say:

The Recent Files list uses a list from OneDrive, if you are signed into your Microsoft account and store files on OneDrive. To eliminate those, sign out of your Microsoft account.

The .plist shows files that you have edited locally on your computer. Here's how to find and delete it:

  1. Close Word.
  2. Hold down the Option key while clicking on the Go menu and choosing Library. Your hidden user Library folder opens.
  3. Open Library/Containers/com.Microsoft.Word/Data/Library/Preferences.
  4. Drag com.microsoft.Word.securebookmarks.plist to your desktop (or the trash).
  5. Reopen Word. The Recent Files list should be empty (actually, it just says More...)

If you do this frequently, you probably want to create an alias on your desktop to the Preferences folder. That .plist doesn't hold any other significant information, so Word is otherwise unaffected.
