cathydennis29 say:

hypothesis testing

I am trying to test the null hypothesis to find the p value in excel and I am having some trouble.

the hypothesis is ho:p = 0.79 versus h1=0.79 n=500 x=390,a=0.01. which formula should I use and how do I set up the formula so that I quit getting an error message. Please help my Bachelor's degree depends on this.

virfranz man say:

Hi Cathy,

We understand that you want to know the formula for the hypothesis testing. To better assist you with your concern, what is the exact error message that you are getting?

mike middleton say:

The hypothesized population proportion is 0.79. 

For the large sample size n = 500 the distribution of sample proportions is approximately normal with standard error SQRT(0.79*(1-0.79)/500) = 0.018215.

The observed sample proportion is 390/500 = 0.78. 

The difference between the observed sample proportion and the hypothesized population proportion is 0.78 - 0.79 = -0.01.

The z test statistic is the difference expressed in standard error units, i.e., z = -0.01/0.018215 = -0.549.

The one-tail p-value is P(z<=-0.549) = NORMSDIST(-0.549) = 0.2915.

One-Tail Interpretation: For a random sample of 500 from a population with proportion 0.79, the probability of obtaining a sample with proportion 0.78 or less is approximately 0.29.

Two-Tail Interpretation: For a random sample of 500 from a population with proportion 0.79, the probability of obtaining a sample that differs from 0.79 by 0.01 or more in either direction is approximately 2*0.29 = 0.58.

Using the typical alpha values of 0.01, 0.05, or 0.10, there is not sufficient evidence to reject the null hypothesis that the population proportion is 0.79.

srinivasu guntimadugu say:

AOL account is usually configured as an IMAP account in Outlook. Sent items for IMAP accounts in Outlook by default will get saved inside the Sent items folder under Personal Folders. 

Changes can be made to save the sent items in AOL folder; follow the steps below:

1.       Click on Tools> Account settings

2.       Double click on your AOL account.

3.       Click on More settings

4.       In the Internet email page, click on Folders tab

5.       Select the second option, chose an existing folder or create a new folder to save the sent items.

6.       Below is the AOL folder, you can choose any existing folder or create a new folder and name it SENT ITEMS, highlight that folder and click on OK.

After which all the sent items will be saved in the folder you selected or created.

 

For additional resources on how to modify the location for sent e-mail messages in Outlook, check the following link:
http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/outlook/HA101642161033.aspx
