The version of Office supplied with a PC (unless you specifically paid extra for the Office application) will be a trial version. With a trial key that application would have lasted for 60 days. ronaldvan ackooij say:

All,

I've talked to Microsoft Support and the answer was that the mailboxproperties where gone after the license change. Meaning we had to set the Public Folders settings to each user mailbox:

Get-Mailbox -resultsize unlimited | where {$_.recipienttype -eq "Usermailbox"} | set-mailbox -defaulpublicfoldermailbox "name of PF hierarchy MB"

That has solved the issue.
