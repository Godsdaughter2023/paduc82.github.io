jay freedman say:
If you display the document in Print Layout view, and each page is squished into the upper left three-quarters of the page, then what you're seeing is the Track Changes and/or Comments features reserving room for balloons.

We've seen reports that this can happen even when Track Changes is turned off and there are no changes or comments to display -- it's not common, but it happens.

On the Review tab of the ribbon, make sure Track Changes is turned off. Click the Show Markup button, click Balloons, and set the selection to "Show All Revisions Inline". If there are changes, you probably want to accept or reject each of them to get a clean document.

More info at http://www.shaunakelly.com/word/sharing/howtrackchangesworks.html.

Let me simplify this for you.

 (to simplify, this comparison not 100% detailed, just the main items)

Office365 is the name of a family of products. (Think McD restaurant menu)

For McD these would be Burger, Cheeseburger, Big Mac, Choc Sundae, Coke...etc

For Office365 these are Exchange, SharePoint, Skype for Business, One Drive for Business, Yammer, Teams, Planner...etc

Office365  products are available as Standalone subscriptions. (Think Chesseburger)

Each Standalone product comes in different sizes.  (think Single Cheeseburger, Double Cheeseburger..etc)

In Office365 they call it plans. Like Kiosk Plan, Plan 1, Plan 2

For mail products, we have 

- Exchange Online Kiosk (2GB Mailbox, Webmail only)

- Exchange Online Plan 1 (50GB Mailbox, 50GB Online Archive, Outlook-Web, MS-Outlook compatible)

- Exchange Online Plan 2 (50GB Mailbox, Unlimited Online Archive, Outlook-Web, MS-Outlook compatible)

There are similar plans for other products in the Office365 family like SharePoint Plan 1, Skype for Business Plan 2 etc

Office365 products are available in bundled plans (Think Cheeseburger Meal with fries and coke)

Bundled plans come in different names (Think Cheeseburger Meal, Double Cheese Meal, Big Mac Meal..etc)

In the Office365 context, the bundles that include Exchange Online as a component include

Business Family (SMB plan, cheaper but max 300 licenses per tenant)

- Business Essential (Exchange Online Plan1, SharePoint, Skype for Business, Yammer, Teams, Planner)

- Business Premium  (Business Essential + MS Office Business + Forms + Bookings + Invoice + Staff Hub...etc)

Enterprise Family - no 300 lic limit

- E1 (Exchange Online Plan1, SharePoint, Skype for Business, Yammer, Teams, Planner)

- E3 (Exchange Online Plan 2, SharePoint, Skype for Business, Yammer. Teams, Planner, MS Office Pro Plus, DLP, AIP...etc)

- E5 (E3 + Enterprise Voice + etc)

Exchange Online Kiosk (USD2) does NOT work with PC-Outlook
Exchange Online Plan 1 Will work with PC-Outlook but min version Outlook 2010
ALL of the above mentioned support AAD Connect. We have deployed them so we are confident.
ALL Exchange Online plans work with shared Mailboxes

E1 does NOT COME with MS-Office (hence outlook) . It does not mean it does not work with MS-Office.

E1 is generally used by customers who already own a copy of MS-Office.

If you require a new copy of MS-Office, either its obsolete, or its a new PC, then you can consider Business Premium bundle or the E3 bundle.

bobgreenblatt say:

I did a bunch of "playing around" with this. Apparently the Mac wants the ranges to be unambiguously qualified. In your running code you may have had errors turned off. And, if it broke with an error, then enable events is false and must be turned back on.

So, srcPg.range(... does not work and throws an error. Thisworkbook.sheets("srcpg").range(... is fine. Me.range(... is also fine. Value is not necessary and is implied. So, instead of:

srcPg.Range("F11").Value = srcPg.Range("F6").Value

use

me.range("f11")=me.range("f6")

Works fine for me (no pun intended) in both Excel 2011 and 2016.
