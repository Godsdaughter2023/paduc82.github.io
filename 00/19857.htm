ruchi bisht say:

Send document for translation over the Internet in unencrypted HTML format is basically used for online search. Once you click on the green arrow below that, an Internet browser window will open to the World Lingo website with your text translated into the language you have selected. The text sent will be unencrypted and may be viewed by a third party website.

 

You can follow the below  steps for translation:

 

 1. Highlight the text to be translated, then go to the Tools menu and select the Language menu option and then the Translate submenu option. The Research Task Pane will appear, with the highlighted text displayed in the "Search For:" field.

2. In the Translation section of the Research Task Pane, you will see the fields From and To. In the From field, select the language that you want to translate from; the default is probably English, but you can change that if the text you are starting with is of a different language. In the To field, select the language that you want to translate the selected text into.

3. Once the languages in the From and To fields are selected, click on the green arrow below the To field to translate the text.

4.  If the language you selected to translate the text into is available in the Word translation dictionary, the translated text will appear in the "World Lingo" section of the Research Task Pane.

If the language you selected to translate the text into is NOT available in the Word translation dictionary, you may see the message "The required translation dictionary is unavailable. Check Translation Options to see if the dictionary is disabled, or connect to the Internet for additional online dictionaries

5.       If you receive this message, you can have Word search and use the World Lingo online dictionary to perform the translation. To enable the online translation, follow these steps:

1. In the Research Task Pane, click on the "Translation Options" link.

2. In the Machine Translation section, check the box that reads "Use online machine translation", and then click the OK button.

 

6.       Click on the Green arrow for “Send document for translation over the Internet in unencrypted HTML format”.

It is possible to have application addins that do not show in the AddIn dialog.

Running this code may find more.

Sub chex()
Dim oadd As AddIn
Dim ocom As COMAddIn
Dim strrep As String
Dim strloaded As String
strrep = "AddIns" & vbCrLf & "--------" & vbCrLf
For Each oadd In Application.AddIns
If oadd.Loaded = 0 Then strloaded = " /Not Loaded" Else strloaded = " /Loaded"
strrep = strrep & oadd.Name & strloaded & vbCrLf
Next
strrep = strrep & vbCrLf & "Com AddIns" & vbCrLf & "--------------" & vbCrLf
For Each ocom In Application.COMAddIns
If ocom.Connect = False Then strloaded = " /Not Connected" Else strloaded = " /Connected"
strrep = strrep & ocom.Description & strloaded & vbCrLf
Next ocom
MsgBox strrep
End Sub

For users that have access to Office 2016 in 365 this won't be a problem, as installing these proofing tools will just upgrade your Office to 2016.

But for users who still only have access to Office 2013, this causes an error when they attempt to install the language pack.

I have checked what the link they get sent to before they end up on the 2016 proofing tools is, and the link is the following: o15.officeredir.microsoft.com/r/rlidFreeProofingTools?ver=15&app=winword.exe&clid=1044&p1=2060&p2=x86&lidhelp=0414&liduser=0414&lidui=0414

Now as you can see, this link clearly states that it is Office version 15 (2013) that it is comming from, and so it should redirect to the 2013 proofing tools, however someone at Microsoft has decided to change this to redirect to 2016 proofing tools, despite the fact that the entire Education and Enterprise 365 market won't have access to Office 2016 for another 4 months.

And i know you can go straight to the Office 2013 Language Options page (https://products.office.com/en-us/language-packs/microsoft-office-language-options-multilingual-support) and navigate from there, but this is not a good solution when you have a few thousand users who expect to be able to download the correct language packs through their Office application.
