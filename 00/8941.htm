Outlook doesn't have user interface access to the Infobar.  According to several books on Outlook programming, the Infobar is a container for receiving data to be displayed on the message form, not for being the source of data.  In other words, Outlook writes the Sent date into the Infobar, displaying it as "You replied to this message on...".  So, as Elemental says, you would use the Sent date in the Sent Items folder to obtain the date on which you replied to the message.  That's the same date you'll see in the Infobar.

Hi MKK4855,

 

According to your description, you want to unprotect the worksheet and remove the password.

It seems you do not remember(do not know) the password used when the worksheet is protected.

 

Unfortunately there is no out of box feature to unprotect a worksheet without knowing the current password. We appreciate your understanding.

 

As a workaround, you may manually copy all content from the protected worksheet and paste it in another/new worksheet.

2.If the password-protected worksheet has external links to other sheets or workbooks, you may need to re-create those links manually.
