rowena cam say:

Hi, Steve1921.

Thank you for posting your query on Microsoft Community.

About your concern, screenshots are considered camera photos. To upload photos to OneDrive please follow the steps provided below:

For Windows 10: Set your phone to upload photos automatically to OneDrive

  1. In the OneDrive app, tap Menu  > Settings  > Camera upload.

  2. Under Camera upload, turn the switch On.

    To also automatically upload videos you take with your phone, turn On the switch at Include videos.

    TIP: You can upload videos automatically only if Camera upload is also turned on.

  3. Under Camera backup account, tap open the list and select the OneDrive account that you want pictures uploading to.

TIP: If you are using a metered internet connection, such as a cellular data connection, turn the switch Off at Upload on metered connections. If your connection is not metered - most wi-fi network connections are not metered - you can turn this switch on.

For Windows Phone 8 or 8.1: Save photos and videos to OneDrive Upload photos and videos

  1. In OneDrive, in the All Files view, tap Add at the bottom of the screen.

  2. At Choose an app, pick the source of the photo or video you want to upload, such as your phone's Photos app.

  3. Find the photo or video you want to upload, select it, and then tap done.

Set your phone to upload photos automatically to OneDrive

  1. Go to your phone's Settings and tap backup > photos + videos.

  2. Under Photos or Videos, select either Good quality or Best quality.

    If you don't want to automatically upload photos or videos, make sure Don't upload is selected.

TIP: To learn how to move photos and videos on your phone to the OneDrive folder on your computer, see Sync photos and videos using my computer on the Windows Phone website.
