suzanne s. barnhill say:

You will have to set both top and bottom margins and header and footer margins to zero. I'm sure you realize that you will not be able to print the result, as every desktop printer has an irreducible nonprintable area (usually larger at the bottom) required for paper handling.

If your intent is just to view your text in a continuous block, without the distraction of the header/footer area, then you can achieve that by hiding the "white space between pages." This is discussed in http://wordfaqs.mvps.org/WhiteSpace.htm, and the toggle is in the Word Options, but it is most easily toggled by just double-clicking between pages.

Alternatively, if you just want to see a large block of uninterrupted text and are not concerned about layout, you could view the document in Web Layout or Draft view.

https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/news/security/malvertising-group-spreading-kovter-malware-via-fake-browser-updates/

 

https://www.proofpoint.com/us/threat-insight/post/kovter-group-malvertising-campaign-exposes-millions-potential-malware-and-fraud

Settings and more > Extensions >

https://github.com/gorhill/uBlock
