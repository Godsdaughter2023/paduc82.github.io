How do I take a Company's logo from their site and put it on a - Microsoft Community

tl001 say:

How do I take a Company's logo from their site and put it on a PowerPoint presentation?

I have a MacBook Pro running Lion and Office:mac 2011.

Thank you!
steve rindsberg say:

Do consider the possible ramifications (legal, that is) of using a company's logo without permission.

Also consider that they may have graphic standards that govern the use of their logo; you might inadvertently run afoul of these and annoy someone rather than impress them.

That said, here's how I'd go about it:

IF you can find a decent logo image on their site, use the browser's Save As feature to save it as a picture on your own computer and insert it into PPT from there.

Look for a "Press" section on the site; sometimes that will have better quality images than the regular pages.

If you can't find anything decent on the site, look for a downloadable PDF of the company's annual report.  There, the logo is likely to be in much higher quality vector format; view the PDF in Reader, zoom in and pop a screen capture at MUCH higher resolution than the web images would give you.

 

david marcovitz say: I am not a lawyer, but I have some experience with copyright. It would seem to me that this would be fair use, and you would be fine to use it. I would think that you would be more likely to impress them by personalizing the presentation than to annoy them by using their logo in a way someone might not approve. But someone else might have another opinion on this. Here are things you can try:

(1) In your browser, right click on the logo and choose Save Picture As (or similar wording) from the pop-up menu. You can then click on Insert > Picture within PowerPoint.
(2) if the logo isn't a separate image on the Web, you can use Grab to select a portion of the screen and save the resulting image. Grab is likely to be in the Utilities folder of your Applications folder (at least is in mine, except I keep it conveniently on the Dock).
