mo7 say:
Uninstall all instances of  KB2553290 update 

I  was also suddenly having this problem. I have many customized templates but can no longer set them as a default template for any sections.  I tried deleting my cache and settings (via /safeboot)  and no change. I also ran the office repair. I'm  on Win7 64-bit as well. 

I solved this by uninstalling all updates for KB2553290. They will be marked as "OneNote 64-bit edition". Do this under control panel - programs and features - installed updates. Double click the item to uninstall. Make sure both onenote and the onenotem.exe processes are stopped in task manager first.

 Although this patch was released in mid November,  it might not have gotten installed until you manually ran an update or your company pushed it out through their update server.   I'm not sure if the patch itself is the issue, or the order in which it installed. On mine, I noticed it had 3-4 entries at different points that day and that there were a whole bunch of other office patches pushed at the same time. So it might be that the order of install was problematic.

kevin-r-a say: Thank you for the quick reply. It looks like I'll have to learn VBA.

gord dibben say:

The Function and the two macros go into a General Module.

To create a General Module, hit ALT + F11 to open the Visual Basic Editor.

Hit CRTL + R to open Project Explorer.

Find your workbook/project and select it.

Right-click and Insert>Module.

Paste the function and macros in there.

Save the workbook.

Hit ALT + q to return to your worksheet window.

Hit Alt + F8 to choose which of the two macros to run.

Gord

TRUE OR FALSE ? - Microsoft Community

navy pilot say:

TRUE OR FALSE ?

I am trying to do what I believe should be very simple, but I cannot find the answer.  I am in Excel 2007, with three columns of data.  Column A is questions.   Column B is either a "T" or an "F" for true or false.  In Column C, I am trying to build a formula that will take each T and convert it to a "1", each F and convert it to a "0".  Can't figure it out.  Thanks for your help.
jeeped say:
... Column B is either a "T" or an "F" for true or false.  In Column C, I am trying to build a formula that will take each T and convert it to a "1", each F and convert it to a "0" ...

There is an inherent flaw in your logic and depending on what you plan to do with the 1'a and 0's it could have detrimental effects.
The problem as I see it will be totaling the 1'a and 0's since you haven't provided for the eventuality of a blank cell which will likely read a 0 . In any event, here are a couple of possibilities,
=IF(B1="T", 1, IF(B1="F", 0, ""))
  ... or,
=IF(B1="T",TRUE, IF(B1="F",FALSE, ""))
  ... or,
=B1="T"

Remember that a boolean value of TRUE evaluates to a 1 and a FALSE evaluates to a 0.
