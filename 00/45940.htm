johann eva say:

Hi snowman666,

Each brand and model of display monitors are completely different from each other. Manufacturers do not create a hardware or device that is exactly identical to their competitor brands. In your case, the situation mentioned above is applicable since you're using AOC 2460 connected with DVI-D and an Acer G246HL connected with HDMI. They also have two different cable types.

If you connect multiple display monitors with different brand and model, your computer will have a conflict with its drivers and will generate communication problem within these monitors. Having a different output of the two (2) or more monitors will make confusion on your one and only driver which is the graphics card.

A single difference when using multiple displays will make an impact to its display in terms of screen resolution, color, saturation, hue, brightness, contrast, etc.

Since your secondary monitor is not the same as your existing primary, we suggest that you only attach or connect display monitors that are completely identical with each other (having the exact brand and model number) only. When you met this, it will avoid hardware and output conflicts in the future.

Disclaimer: Overclocking your hardware like CPU and GPU will put them on a high risk of getting fried due to high temperature exposure. Most manufacturers do not cover this warranty its to it’s a user’s intervention to modify its recommended performance.
