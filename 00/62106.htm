lemp say:

A blue insert usually means a USB 3.0 port.

However, a USB 3.0 device should work if connected to a USB 2.0 port. although it will only run at the lower USB 2.0 speed.

Your laptop should have 1 blue USB 3.0 port and 3 black USB 2.0 ports.  What is the make and model of your external hard drive?

kenny22091 say:

WD My Passport Ultra 1TB

Product Number WDBMWV002BT-05

lemp say:

The My Passport Ultra is a USB 3.0 device, but should work when connected to a USB 2.0 port. 

If it works when connected to your blue USB port, my advice would be to not worry about it and move on.  You should want to connect the drive to a USB 3.0 port in order to get the data transfer rate you paid for.

If the problem was happening only on one of your black USB ports, I'd suspect that something was wrong with that port or the USB 2.0 drivers on that computer, but you said that the drive also did not work on another computer.

If you still want to dig further into this, I suggest first determining if the drive works when connected to any of the 3 USB 2.0 (black) ports on your laptop.  If the drive fails to work in all of those ports, confirm that all of the ports are functional by connecting another USB device, such as a flash drive (thumb drive) to each port.  Also open Device Manager (click the Start Orb, type devmgmt.msc in the Search box, and press Enter) and confirm that there are no warning icons in the "Universal Serial Bus Controllers" section.

Assuming other devices work in the black USB ports and that there are no warning icons in Device Manager, the only thing I can think of suggesting is to open a support case with Western Digital --> http://support.wdc.com/support/case.aspx?lang=en&fid=wdsfMP_UltraFD   Be sure to tell them that you have Windows 7 and that (a) the drive works in a blue USB 3.0 port, (b) does not work in any of your black USB 2.0 ports, (c) other USB devices work properly in the black USB 2.0 ports, and (d) there are no warning icons in Device Manager.

On the other hand, if nothing works in the black USB 2.0 ports and/or there are warning icons, post back with the details.

Skype calling - Microsoft Community

angel672 say:

Skype calling

This may be a dumb question, but what the heck does p stand for? On Skype's rate page is says a call within the US etc costs 1.7 P per minute. What does p stand for? Is this a new abbreviation for cents?

techfreak_ say:
It stands for pence.  A pence would be the British equivalent of cents. For example, if you are viewing prices based off the UK pound, prices below a pound would be denoted by pence instead of cents.  The Skype rates page provides you with the option to list prices based off your currency.  If it is not in the currency of your choice, you can change it.  If you do not see the option you might have to sign out of the Skype site to get the option (it generally defaults to either your current location or the default currency defined in your account).
