Aside from agreeing with you that the adoption of a different "field separator" character between Outlook and the rest of the office apps is confusing (not to mention doesn't conform to what people expect in a "comma separated values" file....some points of clarification


#1 - Contacts have and are never imported into the AddressBook. Contact items are imported into a "contact folder" which would appear in the AB if the option was selected "Show this folder as an e-mail address book". The AddressBook is a specialized "view" of the contact folders that comprise it.


#2 - The only changes in the import function between O'2013 and previous versions of Outlook is that the number of supported file formats has been reduced down to CSV and PST. The other major "noticeable difference" is that the import function uses EAS for Outlook.com accounts which may have existed in previous versions but EAS was not a protocol used by 99.9% of email accounts. (i.e. Outlook.com accounts in '2010 and earlier used the Outlook Hotmail Connector which configured the account as MAPI). To that end, have no idea what you're referring to in terms of "quick import option from the persons addressbook into Outlook" - leastwise not on a "generic" level. I suspect you're answer is going to be related to something specific to the OS is use and the associated options available - i.e. Outlook Express in 'XP, Windows Live Mail etc.

(The original import/export code for Outlook was written by and licensed from a 3rd party company - the name of which escapes me at the moment and as far as I know has remained unchanged since it's original release either in O'97 or O'2000)


#3 - The Win8 <Mail> metro app is completely different and separate from Outlook (regardless of version) and have nothing to do with each other.


Let me stress - the word AddressBook in Outlook has a very specific meaning. The word cannot be used to generically describe what you are importing/exporting between different things.
