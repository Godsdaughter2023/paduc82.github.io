julie fka profjulie say:
Once the message appears on the phone to update to the new password, the user will not be able to access any mail or any of the services related to that Hotmail account.

aaron pascual say: What about for my iPod? I also have my Hotmail account logged in to my iPod also using the mail app. Will the same process happen if I change my password? My iPod is not lost but I just want to be more educated. 

entegy say:

aaron pascual say:

I did an experiment and changing your password does NOT guarantee all devices being logged out of Hotmail.

1. If a "device" has a constant and working internet connection, then there is no need for the "user" to put in the new password.

2. If a "device" has a working internet connection but the connection to the internet breaks off (no Wifi/Internet), and the "device" connects back, then the "device" will send its old info with the old password. Then the server will send back a message to the "device" saying that the "user" must use the new password to continue syncing mail to the "device"

Summary:

1. Device has constant working internet: No need to put new password.

2. Device's internet stops working and soon, reconnects back to the internet: Device will not sync with Hotmail and is required to put in new password. 

Device only needs a constant internet connection to still use the email account. That means if a thief steals the phone and the user of the email changes the password, the thief can still read and use the emails because there is still a constant working internet connection. The thief can also continue to get new mail, contacts, etc.. This is a BIG flaw.

For Google, when you change your password, it will automatically stop syncing to all devices which means that the thief won't see new mail, contacts, etc.. It also shows you who and what devices are and were logged into the Google account from present to 28 days in the past. Google also gives you the option to revoke devices (phone, tablet, iDevices) that are connected to your account for an extra layer of security. This is what Microsoft needs.
