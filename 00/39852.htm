There is an issue with dead keys being cancelled whenever the shift key is depressed or released if the On-Screen Keyboard is running.  Here are instructions to reproduce the problem:

  1. Add a keyboard layout that includes dead keys, such as the US-International keyboard.
  2. Use Notepad to ensure that you can use dead keys properly, such as the following sentence in Spanish:  Él sabe que los pengüinos no pueden volar.
  3. Open the On-Screen keyboard, and type on the physical keyboard again.  You should get mistakes whenever the shift key is used around accents:  El sabe que los penguinos no pueden volar.  (Note the missing accents over "Él" and "pengüinos".)
  4. Try again, but without changing the shift state between the dead key and the following key:  él sabe que los pengÜinos no pueden volar.  (Note that accents are now correct, but obviously the capitalization is off) (Also note that if the shift key is released and depressed between the diaereses and the U, the mark will not appear, similar to #3)
  5. Use the On-Screen keyboard to type the sentence correctly:  Él sabe que los pengüinos no pueden volar.  (Note that all keys work properly on the software keyboard.)
  6. Now, exit out of the On-Screen Keyboard and try the dead keys one last time: Él sabe que los pengüinos no pueden volar.  (Note that all keys on the physical keyboard work now that the On-Screen Keyboard has been closed.)

I have seen this bug on two computers.  Both are running Windows 7 Home Premium in US English, and both have Saitek keyboards.  One computer uses the Saitek keyboard drivers, and the other uses the standard keyboard drivers.  I have not had sufficient access to additional computers to test the bug on other computer setups.

frathos say:

I don't think YouTube allows a new channel to stream live so your son would have to go through the process of uploading previously recorded and saved videos.

There are different ways this can be done depending on what is available and vary in cost from cheapest to more expensive:

  • stream to Mixer using console native broadcast feature. Setup your Mixer channel to save your streams as a VOD. Download the VOD to your PC or OneDrive and then upload to YouTube.
  • connect an external USB 3.0 NTFS formatted HDD to console and set it as the location for your saved DVR clips. Record a video clip up to 60 minutes long. Connect HDD to PC and upload video to YouTube.
  • Invest in a PC with an internal or external capture card. Use that to capture gameplay from the console (natively if supported or via Mixer VOD as suggested first) and then upload that content to YouTube.

The first option may not be able to give you the same level of quality as the second two but it is the cheapest way to begin. Quality will depend on your ISP speed and quality of VOD file.

The second and third options costs more and is dependent on the size of HDD, PC or laptop purchased. Additionally one could invest in a video editing application to edit the video before uploading to YouTube. If you are only interested in basiv video editing then the latter can be skipped for now and use YouTube editor for simple edits.

If your son wants to record commentary type videos then I recommend he learn how to write a script of what he wants to say. Having that available as you record the video may help prevent having to do multiple takes because of mistakes made in saying something wrong when adlibbing off the top of your head.

Good luck!
