Creating two A5 pages per sheet of A4 in Word 2007 - Microsoft Community

talullah62 say:

Creating two A5 pages per sheet of A4 in Word 2007

I have been asked to make up an A5 leaflet, with two leaflets per page for printing.  I tried various ways which all worked to a certain extent, but then found this article by Microsoft MVP, Suzanne S Barnhill: http://wordfaqs.mvps.org/2PagesPerSheet.htm which seemed like a really simple way of doing it so I thought I would give it a go.  I've set it up exactly as advised and, in terms of typing in data etc., it seems to work ok, but when I print it out I just get one copy on a sheet of landscape as opposed to two.

I have another problem in the left/right margins were quite big so I tried to change them, but they don't seem to change within the document.

Has anyone else tried using Suzanne's method and could perhaps help me resolve this?

suzanne s. barnhill say:

When you enable "2 pages per sheet" or "Book fold," the Left and Right margins change to Inside and Outside because Word automatically enables "Mirror margins," assuming that (especially in a folded booklet) you might want to have larger margins in the gutter/fold. That doesn't stop you from having the same margins on both sides, but it does make it difficult to have different margins Left and Right.

If you want to change the margins for the entire document, instead of selecting anything, you should go to the Margins tab of Page Setup and set the margins, making sure that "Whole document" is selected in the "Apply to" dropdown.

As the article tries to make clear, which "2 pages per sheet" setting you choose (the one in the Print dialog or the one in the Page Setup dialog) depends on whether or not you have already created a full-size document. If you've created the document as A4 and want to print it twice on A4 (two A5 copies), then you need to use the one in the Print dialog; unless you've duplicated the page (pages 1 and 2), you'll need to print pages 1, 1. If you are starting from scratch, you'll probably find it simpler to use the setting in the Page Setup dialog so that you are starting with an A5 document to begin with. This makes it easier to set margins, choose the appropriate font size, etc. But you still have to print pages 1, 1 if you have only one page.

If the intent is to create more than a single page (one side of one sheet), the end result being a "folded booklet" (even if it's just the front and back of a single sheet), then you're better off using the "Book fold" setting, which will allow you to create the pages in chronological order but print them in the correct order for being folded into a booklet.

1. Re  Inactivity

To keep your Outlook.com account active you must sign in to the account at least once every 365 days.

After 365 days of inactivity, your email will be deleted and cannot be recovered.

After 2 years of inactivity, your Microsoft account will be deleted and cannot be recovered.

2. Have you a Back up of all your important email data that you can access?

If your data is important it is prudent to back it up, especially if it is in the cloud and not under your control.

When you signed up for the email account there was (in the fine print) the Terms of use which clearly said that the user is responsible for the data and not Microsoft.
