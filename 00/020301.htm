This should do it (make sure you have a reference to Microsoft DAO Object Library 3.6 set:

 

Private Sub member_number_Exit(Cancel As Integer)

Dim rst As DAO.Recordset

Dim lngID As Long

lngID = Me!ID


 Forms!frm_fix_member_time.Requery

Set rst = Me.RecordsetClone

 rst.FindFirst "[ID]=" & lngID

If rst.NoMatch Then

   Me.Recordset.AddNew

Else

   Me.Bookmark = rst.Bookmark

End If

 

rst.Close

Set rst = Nothing

End Sub

    Outlook question - auto bcc incoming messages - Microsoft Community

    m in lajolla say:

    Outlook question - auto bcc incoming messages

    I'm told certain regulatory requirements make it necessary that incoming messages to a specific email address be auto bcc'd to the regulatory required compliance site (email address).  How can I best do this?

     

    Thank you.

    jeeped say:
    As the receiver of the incoming mail,you cannot BCC anyone as this is a function of sending the mail. You can forward the mail automatically however with a Rule on incoming messages.

    1. Select Tools, Rules and Alerts from the pull-down menus. In the Rules and Alerts dialog, click New Rule.
    2. On the first page of the Rules Wizard dialog, select Check messages when they arrive from the Start from a blank rule category. Click Next.
    3. Check through the specified account from the available options available within Step 1: Select condition(s) then click on specified in the Step 2: Edit the rule description (click an underlines value) and supply an account. Click Next.
    4. Check forward it to people or distribution list from the Step 1: Select action(s) then click people or distribution list from the Step 2: Edit the rule description (click an underlines value) and supply a destination email address. Click Next.
    5. You may want to check this next screen for any exceptions to your rule. When you are satisfied, click Next.
    6. Supply a name to display for your new rule, ensure that Turn on this rule is checked and review the rules steps and actions, modifying if necessary. Click Finish.
    7. Back at the Rules and Alerts dialog, if you have more rules move the rule up or down in the hierarchy. This probably should be at the top. Click OK to save your changes.

    The new rule should be in place.

    If you want a similar action for outgoing mail from the same account create another rule using select Check messages after sending from the Start from a blank rule category on the first page of the Rules Wizard dialog and CC the message to people of distribution list from the Step 1: Select action(s).

    I think you may have some terminology confused.

    Under Office365 SKUs, there is

    1. Exchange Online Plan 1 or Exchange Online Plan 2 - These are standalone plans.

    2. Enterprise bundle E1, E2, E3, E4 - These are bundled plans.

    3. SMB bundle Business Essential, Business and Business Premium - These are bundled plans for SMBs

    Exchange Online Plan 1 has 50GB mailbox and 50GB Archive. As long as your existing archive usage does not exceed 50GB then I think you are ok.
