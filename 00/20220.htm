henry huang- [o365] say:

Hi April,

Yes, from the user side, the difference is easy to imagine.

The mailbox items in the Recoverable Items Folder cannot be sorted, viewed or searched directly when a user access their mailbox. But user are able to manage their archive folders and check the archived items at any time.
Litigation hold is more like a protection to prevent items from being lost. Online archiving is a feature to manage aged items which are still useful. Litigation hold is a protector of the Delete Items Folder, while online archiving is a extension of the Inbox folder.

In addition, since the online archiving services are included in the licenses, it means that you are able to provide users with the online archive folder experience, and hold the emails at the same time. When litigation hold is turned on for a mailbox, the archive folder will be put on hold as well. It can enrich the user experience for Office 365.
