If you have a Win 8.1 Machine with built-in Bluetooth from Qualcomm Atheros Communications, the Bluetooth may no longer work after you've upgraded to 8.1; this happened to me.

If you go to Device Manager, there is a Bluetooth section and in it there is a thing called "Bluetooth USB Module."  Actually, the Qualcomm Atheros Communications Bluetooth is built into the Wifi Adaptor, but that does not really matter.

Acer (but probably other manufacturers as well) have not updated the drivers for this device to Win 8.1 yet on their sites.  The Atheros website is also useless.

I found the Win 8.1 Driver (64 bit) here:  http://drivers.softpedia.com/progDownload/Acer-Aspire-V5-573P-Atheros-Bluetooth-Driver-801305-for-Windows-81-64-bit-Download-306204.html

I have an Acer Aspire Desktop, so I can verify that it works for Acer.  Might also work for other PC Manufacturers, but not sure.

It's a big download (over 300 mb), but it fixed the issue for me:

Download the zip file; extract all
Open "Install CD" folder 
Find "setup" and run it
Restart machine after it's done

  1. On your Xbox 360 console, sign in to Xbox LIVE using the account that you want to change.
