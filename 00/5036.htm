Even when you choose to delete your out-of-date security info, MS don't fully remove it for 30 days.

Hi Vincent,

 

To answer your questions:

 

1） The storage base per tenant is 10 GB + 500 MB per subscribed user + additional storage purchased. This includes the team site storage and public site storage in your tenant.

For example, if you have 10,000 users, the base storage allocation is approximately 5 TB (10 GB + 500 MB * 10,000 users).

Site collection storage limit is up to 1TB per site collection.

Remember that although SharePoint Online imposes a limit of 1 TB per site collection, your particular tenant might not have enough storage available to contain a site collection of 1 TB.

 

About whether it is possible to change the storage limit, that depends on the Office 365 plan we are using:

If it is Office 365 Business Essentials , Business Premium, Office 365 Enterprise, Education, or Government plan, we can change the storage limit of a site collection in SharePoint admin center > site collections > select the URL of a site collection > storage quota . (Note: we should set the Site Collection Storage Management to Manual first.)

If it is Office 365 Small Business or Small Business Premium plan, the storage  limit of our team site is calculated like this: the total storage limit of tenant as mentioned in point 1 subtract the public site storage limit.

To increase the storage of team site, we need to purchase additional licenses and assign them to new users.

 

About OneDrive for Business storage, it is counted in Personal site storage (Up to 1 TB per user, as soon as provisioned).

Note: Personal site storage applies to a user’s OneDrive for Business library and personal newsfeed. This amount is counted separately, and does not add to or subtract the overall storage allocation for a tenant. Personal site storage applies to a user’s OneDrive for Business library and personal newsfeed.
