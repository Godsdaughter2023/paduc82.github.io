hula_baloo say:

 Check out the Works 9 Solution Center at

http://support.microsoft.com/ph/12025 .  They talk about going back as far as Works 6, but it sounds like you may be going back further than that.  I know current versions of Works have the capability to store things in .RTF format, but don't remember if the old version you have can.  If you can do that, Works 9 will open them, as will any word processor.  Worst case you could open the old files in the old version, then copy and paste them into a modern version. 

 

 I went back through my old versions of Works.  I have version 4.5 and 2000.  4.5 has no .RTF capability, Works 2000 does.  With the 98 version, I believe you're in between those two, so hopefully you do have .RTF capability.  Even the oldest version I have (2.0 for DOS!) will store in .TXT format for whatever that's worth.  You'd lose your formatting with .TXT but at least would save the text of the document. 

 

 If you decide to buy Works 9, you may have a hard time finding it.  The last time I looked it wasn't in stores, Amazon, or the Microsoft store.  MS wants you to spend $140 on the Office Home and Student version.  :-( 

 

 I hope this helps.  Good luck.
