balaji kundalam say:
Whenever you connect your speaker to your PC and you can see it in device manager and find that it is working properly, you need to configure your PC to deliver audio through that speaker. For this, you need to right click on the speaker icon at the system tray->click Playback devices. You can see a dialog like this (the following is just an illustration and may not reflect the same settings and devices your PC has):


You can see the Bluetooth speakers as Bluetooth Handsfree Audio. If not, right click anywhere in the dialog and click Show Disabled Devices and Show Disconnected Devices, like the image below shows:


Once you do that, you see the Bluetooth device. Right click it and click Set as Default Device. Once you do this, the audio from the PC will be delivered to the Bluetooth speakers.


If you are playing any audio in Windows Media Player during this setting change, it may stop during the transition and report an error. But once configured, you can play the file again it will play through the wireless speakers. If not, close Windows Media Player and open it again and it will play.
