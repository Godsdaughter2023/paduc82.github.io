bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

It isn't a Section Break, it's a Paragraph Border. Starting a paragraph with 3 consecutive characters followed by pressing return triggers specific styles of Border:

  1.   =  Double Line
  2.   -   Thin Single Line
  3.   _   Medium Single Line
  4.   #   Oxford Rule (Triple Line, Thin-Thick-Thin)

Click on the bordered paragraph, go to Format> Borders & Shading then select None to remove the Border. (Or you can use the Borders tool on the Home tab to select No Border.)

To avoid it create a blank paragraph then go back to that paragraph to type the symbols - but don't press return after doing so.

The feature can be disabled by going to Tools> AutoCorrect Options - AutoFormat As You Type to clear the check on Border Lines. If you restore the check when you're done, what you've typed will not be affected.

Hi Bradley,

The behavior you are encountering is the Office 365 Single Sign-On (SSO) feature. With ADFS deployed in your on-premises, the authentication request to Office 365 is redirected to the on-premises ADFS server (https://adfs.[company-name].com/adfs/ls/?client-request-id=[...]).

The SSO configuration is deployed by your local AD and Office 365 administrator(s). If you are an end user, you cannot disable it. If you are the Office 365 administrator, you can change your UPN to another standard domain. If an email was sent to a non existing email address, you will receive a return email stating that the delivery failed.
