arnold931 say:

Separate email accounts

I recently upgraded from a Windows 7, Outlook 2010 PC to a Windows 10, Outlook 2016 PC.  My wife and I have separate email accounts.  On the old PC, we had separate POP email accounts and when you started up Outlook, it would have a drop down to have you choose which email account you wanted to go into.  The way I have it setup on the new PC (which is probably wrong), my email is set up as a IMAP/SMTP and hers is a POP/SMTP but just as separate folders in the same instance of Outlook.  So when you start up Outlook, it opens up both email accounts.  How do I set up the way my old PC was?
hal hostetler - mvp-outlook say:

What you probably had was separate Outlook profiles for each account.  This keeps both accounts completely separated from each other.  With Outlook closed, go to "Control Panel > Mail > Show Profiles", create a new profile for your wife and add her email account to it, and make sure you select "Prompt for a profile to be used".  When you open Outlook the next time, you should have your choice back.  The last step is to remove her email account from your profile, be sure you make a backup of her .PST file first, so she doesn't lose anything.  She can re-use this file if she stays on POP, or add it as a storage file, if she uses IMAP on the new profile.
