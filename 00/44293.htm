considerate_guy say:

The way to reduce the file size of a video is via video conversion and choosing:

1) Lower quality / bitrate

2) Lower resolution (e.g. smaller size)

3) Higher compression codecs

Movie Maker generates a .wmv file which is designed to retain as much of the original quality as possible, so it may not be the best tool for compressing your video.  However, you can explore the options when you save your movie.  Rather than picking from the default choices, scroll down to the bottom of the list of output options and choose 'Custom'.  There you can pick various settings and it will give you an estimate of how many MBs will be needed for each second of video.

Alternatively, you can just go for a video conversion application like Freemake Video Converter or others you might find.  You add your video, choose the output settings and click 'Convert'.

Oh, and another way to reduce size is to actually remove unwanted video.  So if there are parts you don't like, make a split before and after and delete that portion.
