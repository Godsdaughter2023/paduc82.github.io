Multi String Zero means a lot of string zeroes can be had as a parameter to such an entry of a registry key.

 

Thus, one would expect to have a /a parameter of REG ADD which would mean append a string of data to a given entry of a given key. However, REG ADD deletes any previously stored strings of data and overwrites the contents of a given entry of a given key and does not append such.

pa bear - ms mvp say:

McAfee (and Norton) applications are notorious for not uninstalling (or upgrading) cleanly.

1. Download the McAfee Consumer Products Removal Tool, saving it to your desktop:  http://download.mcafee.com/products/licensed/cust_support_patches/MCPR.exe

2. Close all open applications (i.e., anything with an icon on the taskbar).

3. Right-click on the file you saved in #1 above and select Run as Administrator to run the utility. Don't TOUCH your keyboard until the run completes, then reboot.

Note: You should have run this removal tool BEFORE you installed Avast AND BEFORE you installed any updates (e.g., KB2530548, KB2544893, KB2476490, KB2393802, KB976932 and/or IE9).

4. Reset IE Advanced settings per http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows7/Reset-Internet-Explorer-settings

5. Open Internet Explorer (32-bit only!) to http://support.microsoft.com/kb/971058 [ignore any DOES NOT APPLY warnings] & run the Fix It in DEFAULT and then AGGRESSIVE modes. [1]

6. Reboot one last time then test by running a manual check for updates per http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows7/How-can-I-tell-if-my-computer-is-up-to-date and seeing if KB2532531, KB2555917& KB2507938 are offered & successfully install.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
[1] Full Disclosure: Running the Fix It in AGGRESSIVE mode will delete your Update History but not the list of Installed Updates.

moltar1138 say:

First: Yes, anything you buy is licensed to two things: your gamertag and the console you use to buy it. So, if you ever change consoles, your account needs to be logged in to use whatever content you downloaded. (There is a license transfer tool available if you do have to change consoles. http://www.xbox.com/drm)

But yes, you can re-download (just about) anything you paid for. There are some very limited exceptions to that rule. Just access your "purchase history" (either via the website here, or on your console).

 

Second: Never share your account. First, it's against the terms of use, and second, if you did let someone use your account, and let's say, they go play "World at War" and start using "infections" or put some message in your profile that says "I will give you _____ in exchange for ______" (i.e. selling services or mods) then you'll lose the account.  Plenty of people have been banned, quite recently, because "oh, I let a friend use it while I made lunch" or "oh, my kids played on it while I was at work" - and now those people have no more account.  It's up to you, and you alone, to protect the account!

 

FYI, if your account is ever banned, you LOSE anything you paid for. Just, you know, if you still want to risk it.
