julie fka profjulie say:

To expound a little on LCRT's explanation....

  • The Windows Phone 7.8 OS is NOT the same thing as the Windows OS. Even if you managed to download a virus that was designed to run on a Windows computer, the virus would not be much of a threat to a Windows Phone since it runs a different OS.
  • Windows Phone is ranked number 3 in the mobile OS universe, with Android falling into 1st place and iOS falling into 2nd place. People who write viruses tend to focus on areas that have the most bang for the buck. Given Android's open OS and large customer base, it comes as no surprise that viruses have cropped up on Android devices, but not on iOS (large customer base, but the OS is too locked down) or Windows Phone (fairly small customer base, and the OS is too locked down).

In regards to your concerns about the message you received to verify your AOL password, I've seen that message too on my various email accounts. I think the message pops up when, for numerous reasons, the phone is not able to achieve a successful connection to the email server it is attempting to contact. The reason for this message could be that the password has been changed on the server so needs to be changed on the phone, but other reasons could be that the email server was not available exactly at the time the phone attempted the connection, or the internet connection on the phone was disrupted momentarily on the phone (this happens all the time on my phones when I am connected on my home WiFi network). In the past when I've experienced the message, I just wait a few moments and try again.

In Access, how do you add between 1 and 6 Years to a date?

I am working on trying to build a database that contains an in service date and a replacement date. The in Service Date is manually entered by me, and then I want to the year to be 6 years. I am testing to try to get it to work right with 1 year (see below) but it is not working. Any Assistance would be GREATLY appreciated.

 

Design View

Date in Service - Date / Time - Short Date

Replacement Date - Calculated:

  • [Date in Service] + Year(1)
  • Date / Time

When I do this, this is what I get:

Date in Service - 6/5/2011

Replacement Date - 8/16/2016

 

Am I doing something wrong? Is there a way to calculate these easily?

 

 

john w. vinson say:

Yes. The DateAdd() function will do just what you want.

The Year() function returns the year number of the Date/Time value in its argument - e.g. Year(#5/29/2012#) will return an integer number 2012. Year(1) will convert the number 1 to a date, which - because of the way Access stores date data - will be #12/31/1899#, so your expression will add 1899 days to the DateInService - not what you want!

Try a calculated field in your Query (NOT IN YOUR TABLE!!) of

DateAdd("yyyy", 1, [Date in Service])

You can press Ctrl-G to open the VBA editor (which will give you access to the VBA Help file), press F1 to open Help, and search for help on "DateAdd". Take a look at DateDiff and DateSerial while you're there, they can also be very useful.
