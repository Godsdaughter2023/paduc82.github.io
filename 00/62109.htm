arminatorx say:

Family sharing on the Windows 10 PC is possible, but a bit more awkward than on the Xbox console where you just set the console as your Home Console. 

While your brother can't download your Game Pass games himself, you can install them for him like this:

Make sure your brother's account is added to your Family at https://account.microsoft.com/family

Then let him sign in on his Windows 10 Account on his PC. 

Start the Windows 10 Store app, and sign him out of the store. 

Sign in with your own account in the store app, and install the game(s). 

After the installation is done, sign out of the store and let your brother sign in on the store again. 

Be aware that you ONLY sign in on the store app. You do not need a complete own separate account on your brother's PC. 

If you sign in on a separate account on his PC, your brother won't be able to access the games you install in your user area on his PC.

lemp say:
If you right-click on the printer icon in the Notification Area (near the clock), you should bring up the Print window for that old printer and you should be able to delete the print jobs there.

The print jobs themselves are stored in %windir%\system32\spool\PRINTERS\ but unless the new printer is identical to the old printer, you won't be able to use them:  the files in the spooler are the output from the driver for the old printer, and have control codes specific to that printer. 

If you are really desperate, you could try to print them as follows:

In the old days, when printers were connected to the serial or parallel port, you could just copy the file to the printer port.  With USB, it's a little harder.  You can share the printer and then copy to the share:

COPY OUTPUT.PRN /B \\Computer_Name\Printer_Share_Name

Where Output.prn is the file in the spooler (where it does not have the PRN extension).  Or you can use Fastprint.

No guarantees.  I believe that the files in the spooler are the same as *.prn files, but I've never tried to use them.

Finally, you probably won't be able to access the files unless you stop the print spooler as in the usual commands for clearing out print spool files:
  • Open a Command Prompt window (Start > Run > cmd > OK)
  • Type the following in the black Command Prompt window and press Enter after each line
                           net stop spooler
                           del /Q "%windir%\system32\spool\PRINTERS\*.*"
                           net start spooler
                           exit
