mike hall... always say:

If you have a limited connection, your computer Wi-Fi adapter is communicating with the modem/router, so driver updates will NOT change anything.

The problem is with the OTHER side of the modem/router, the side which is BEYOND the control of Microsoft and Windows.

  1. Remove the power connector from the modem/router and leave it for 30 seconds.
  2. Also, power down the computer.
  3. Reconnect the modem/router to mains power and let it set itself up. 
  4. Check that the lights on the modem/router are showing as they should. You will need to confirm this with your Internet Service provider.
  5. Now power up the PC.

If it was just a blockage in the modem/router, your Internet should be back. If it doesn't, contact the ISP to confirm that a signal is being sent to your modem/router.

Your ISP may give you the run around, getting you to check connections, cables etc because they don't like admitting to errors, BUT if you have not moved anything, this is unlikely to rectify the problem.

What I do in these instances is first disconnect the ISP supplied modem/router and then take it back to them for replacement for one that WORKS. :-)

ISP supplied equipment is often quite shoddy stuff which has a limited useful life..
