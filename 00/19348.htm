steve rindsberg say:

You don't really need to worry about pixel width so much as aspect ratio.  If your slide proportions match your computer's video setup and the projector's capable of  projecting same, your'e good to go.

For example, if you know you'll be projecting to a 16:9 aspect ratio screen, you'd want to start a presentation by changing the slide setup (Design tab | Page Setup group | Page Setup) to 16:9 ... it's a built-in size.  

If you've already created the presentation, you could try changing to the new proportions on a COPY of your original presentation.  Graphics and a few other bits are liable to get stretched when you make the change, but if it's not too awful, you could make adjustments for that manually.

Or you can make a copy of your original pressie, switch to the new aspect ratio, then open both presentations side by side.  On each slide:

Delete everything from the 16:9 slide

Select and copy everything on the original slide

Paste to the 16:9 slide and adjust for centering.
