v.sutharsan say:
Hello Inglés,

Thank you for your question.

In the most simplest terms:

1. Outlook.com is an email service provider powered by Microsoft
2. Microsoft Outlook (also Office 365 Outlook) is an email client targeted for enterprises
3. Outlook Mobile is an email client for Android/iOS designed mainly with Microsoft accounts/services in mind

Don't be misled by the name, they are completely different applications! They work exactly like third-party email clients—they cannot sync what is beyond the email protocol allows: IMAP, POP3, Exchange ActiveSync. What makes them special is the functionality each of those applications can offer, unparalleled to third-party email clients.

Though, Outlook.com and Microsoft Outlook are getting more integrated. Some of their functionality are getting more consistent and supporting more features between the 2 platforms.

Find out more: https://support.office.com/en-us/article/compar...

I hope my answer is helpful to you.

Regards,
Sutharsan

rohn007 say:
As I understand it, the Mac and PC apps are written separately, so there no easy ways of translating features from one to the other.

I don't know of any easy way of changing the symbols color. It is a system controlled attribute. I suspect they are defined to have the font color so you can't separate them.

However, you can set the grammar checker to look specifically for spaces between sentences:

File tab > Options command > Proofing option > "Settings ..." button.  You can specifiy checking for one or two spaces after period.  As well several other options. 

It's not ideal, but it is something.

Also, there is a free (for personal use) addon that may come closer to what you are looking for. It is called "Crosseyes", http://www.levitjames.com/Products/CrossEyes.aspx

It provides a pane with a WordPerfect like "Reveal Codes" translation of the document. It shows formatting as color coded html-like codes.
