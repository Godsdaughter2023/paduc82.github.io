john john __ say:

Hi,

The issue seems to be related to the design of the processor.

In terms of raw speed, the Pentium D 945 is 3.4 GHz (Giga Hertz) processor, the ES5500 C2D is a 2.8 GHz (Giga Hertz) Processor and this is the reason that the Windows Experience Index has dropped.


The pipeline on the E5500 is one instruction set wider but about half as long than the one on the D945.

What does this mean?  The D945 can execute 3 instruction sets per clock cycle, the Core 2 Duo can execute 4 with its wider pipeline.  Not only does the E5500 deliver more instruction sets per clock cycle but it also has a better branch prediction success rate of about 93% vs 90% for the 945D, even at its slower clock speed the E5500 with its much shorter pipeline can flush the pipeline much faster than the D945 when these branch prediction errors occur and it can generally deliver the instruction sets much faster than the D945.  You can't compare and rate newer processors with older ones by clock speed only, no more than you could rate and compare older Intel and AMD processors by clock speed only.  Clock speed is only one factor which Intel successfully exploited for marketing purposes until the 30+ stage pipeline required to sustain 3.4 Ghz processors put an end to the ever increasing clock speed.

a. user say:

Hi Wael, 

Thank you for contacting Microsoft Community.

As per the issue description, the issue seems to be with your Dell XPS 8700 laptop. The issue could be caused if the power plan settings are incorrect. To get a better understanding of the issue, I want to know:

Did you make any changes on your computer prior to this issue?

To fix the issue, please try the methods below:

Method 1: Run Power Troubleshooter.

The Power troubleshooter checks things like your computer's timeout settings, which determine how long the computer waits before turning off the monitor display or entering sleep mode. Adjusting these settings can help you conserve power and extend your computer's battery life.

  1. Press Windows Logo + "W", type in "Troubleshooting" and press enter.
  2. On left Pane, click on "View all".
  3. In the list click on "Power" to run the troubleshooter and follow the instruction.

After trying method 1, if the issue still persists try method 2.

Method 2: Restore Power Settings to Default.

  1. Press Windows Logo + "X" and on left click on Power Options.
  2. Click on "Change Plan Settings".
  3. Under "Plan Settings", click on "Restore Default Settings for this Plan".
  4. If prompted, click on "Yes".

Note: Once finished, restart your computer and check if your computer is working fine, if not proceed with Method 3.

Method 3: Change the Power Management settings.

  1. Press Windows key + R key.
  2. Type devmgmt.msc and click Ok.
  3. Double-click on Network Adapter to expand it.
  4. Right click the Network Adapter and then click Properties.
  5. Click on Power Management tab, uncheck the box to “Allow this device to wake the computer”.
  6. Check the box “Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power.” for network adapter.
  7. Click OK.

 

Please try the methods provided above and let us know the status of the issue.
