jay freedman say:
Presumably the macros are saved in .doc or .dot files from Word 2003. Just transfer those files to the computer with Word 2013.

If the macros were saved in the 2003 Normal.dot template, it would be best to rename it as something else like 2003Normal.dot on the new computer. If you want the macros to be in the Normal template of 2013, you could then use File > Open to open the old template, open the VBA editor, and copy/paste the code from the old template's project into the new Normal project; or you could use the Organizer (press Alt+F8 to open the Macros dialog, and click the Organizer button there) to copy the modules from the old template to the new one.

Although many macros from 2003 will run as-is in 2013, there are some things that won't work and will need to be revised or rewritten. A big area that changed is anything to do with the CommandBars collection or CommandBar objects, some of which no longer exist in any version of Word that uses the ribbon. Also, macros written to intercept certain commands (notably Print and SaveAs) don't intercept actions on the Backstage view (the stuff you see when you click File).

mel77084 say:

Hi, Sundowner,

That new, erratic behavior isn't just on your machine ... it belongs to this build.

When you click on it, if the icon for it moves to the Taskbar from the Hidden Icon Box, or if the particular USB drive has a light, and it goes out, the drive has apparently been rejected from the system, and can be safely removed. Then, if the notification for this action hasn't been turned off, it may pop out with an ok to remove message a few seconds after (or sometimes, before, if you wait long enough) the drive has been removed.

In earlier builds, it would commonly pop up an erroneous warning that the drive was in use, so couldn't be removed.
