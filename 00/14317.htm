Turn on your PC, do not do anything else than FileCopy & Kill with your macro, turn it off and on again. If CHKDSK starts, then it is highly likely that your code is the reason.

The NAME method in VBA can also move files, but I recommend to use the FileSystemObject, it is extensive but comfortable.
To simplify the development set a reference to "Microsoft Scripting Runtime", that enables the intellisense if you type "fso." in the sub below.

Read also the help to FileSystemObject in VBA.

Andreas.

Sub Test()
  Dim fso As FileSystemObject
  Dim ts As TextStream
 
  Set fso = CreateObject("Scripting.FileSystemObject")
 
  Set ts = fso.CreateTextFile("C:\test.txt")
  ts.Write "Hello World"
  ts.Close
 
  fso.MoveFile "C:\test.txt", fso.BuildPath(fso.GetSpecialFolder(TemporaryFolder), "test.txt")
End Sub

erik dupuis say:
Hello All,

This problem was quite frustrating for me, and I have seen numerous other posts on here for individuals experiencing the same issue with Gmail. I have determined the cause of the issue and would like to share my solution with the community.

During the initial setup, your Gmail account may have blocked application access to Outlook.com as it thought that the site was attempting to maliciously access your account. This is a Google server issue, and not one that can be reversed by Microsoft. This issue must be addressed by the account owner on an individual basis.

When attempting to setup the mail receipt for your Gmail account on your first try, if your Gmail account page was open, you may have seen an obscure warning message in your browser: "Warning: We prevented a recent suspicious login attempt"
This message means that Google blocked Outlook.com from accessing your account.

To reverse the block, please follow this process:
  1. Open Outlook.com.
  2. Click on the "gear" in the top right-hand corner of the page.
  3. Click on "More mail settings".
  4. Click on "Sending/receiving email from other accounts".
  5. Under "You can receive mail from these accounts", click on "Add an email account".
  6. Input all of your account settings, but do not click the "next" button yet.
  7. Open a seperate tab in your browser and navigate to "https://accounts.google.com/DisplayUnlockCaptcha". You may need to sign in.
  8. Read the instructions at the top of the page carefully, then click the "Continue" button.
  9. Switch back to your Outlook.com tab.
  10. Click "Next".

You should now be able to proceed with adding your Gmail account to receive mail at this point. This process unblocks Outlook.com from your Gmail account on the Google servers.

I hope this solution works for other, as well as it did for me.

Cheers,
Erik

jim gordon mvp say:

CSV is a text format, meaning all that is ever saved in CSV file format is text. Formatting, formulas, pictures, anything else you may have added to the file along the way will be discarded. That is why you received the message warning you to change from CSV to a different format, such as Excel Workbook, that supports the features you introduced to the file.

You have to use File > Save As and change the file format (preferably to Excel Workbook) after you add unsupported features to a CSV file. CSV does not support cell formulas. Period.

File formats are the same in 2011 and 2016. Please see this article for details:

http://www.dummies.com/software/microsoft-office-for-mac/excel-for-mac/choosing-excel-file-formats-in-office-2011-for-mac/
