First. Do not do anything with any material you value on a flash drive (or any other removable media)  from within Word.

Second, copy the document(s) you want to work with onto your hard drive from the flash drive. Remember where you put them.

Fifth, save your completed documents to your hard drive from Word.

Sixth, if you need them back on the flash drive, copy them there from Windows.

Is there a way to use Page color ONLY on the first page - Microsoft Community

darla_916 say:

Is there a way to use Page color ONLY on the first page

Is there a way that I can use the Page color on my title page, but not have it carry over to all my pages?
jay freedman say:
The Page Color button on the ribbon can't do that -- it always applies to the entire document. You can't even limit it to a single section.

This the workaround:
  • Click File, click Options, click Advanced on the left side, and make sure the box in the "Editing options" section for "Automatically create drawing canvas when inserting AutoShapes" is NOT checked. Click OK.
  • Double-click in the header area to open it, which displays the Header & Footer Tools tab on the ribbon.
  • On that tab, check the box for Different First Page.
  • Click the Insert tab of the ribbon, click Shapes, and choose the rectangle tool.
  • Click-and-drag to draw a rectangle as large as the page. If necessary, drag the little circles at the corners and edges to make it large enough.
  • On the Drawing Tools tab of the ribbon, click Shape Fill and choose the color you want; and click Shape Outline and choose No Outline.
  • Double-click in the main text to close the header area.


You should be aware that this uses up a lot of very expensive ink. If you're printing more than one or two copies, buy colored paper and use it just to print page 1 (or 1 and 2). For electronic copies, though, it's perfect.
