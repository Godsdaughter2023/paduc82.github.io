https://support.office.com/en-us/article/what-a...
POP
POP works by contacting your email service and downloading all of your new messages from it. Once they are downloaded onto your PC or Mac, they are deleted from the email service. This means that after the email is downloaded, it can only be accessed using the same computer. If you try to access your email from a different device, the messages that have been previously downloaded won't be available to you.

Sent mail is stored locally on your PC or Mac, not on the email server.

A lot of Internet Service Providers (ISPs) give you email accounts that use POP.

swischner say:

Those are the initials of the person who last edited or added that content. This may even be yourself if you edited the note on another device. Usually they represent the initials of the real name used in the Microsoft account. If someone uses a "fantasy name" or some other word as an account name, the initial will show accordingly. In addition in OneNote for Windows (desktop version) you may change those initials to anything you want, independant from your MS account name. So, yes, U and MS are your own mystery :)

Some versions of OneNote allow to switch the display of those markers off, e.g. Windows and Mac versions, some other app versions won't let you do that (yet?).
