It's best to leave that recovery partition out of view and left alone as far as deleting it goes, it's indeed something that appeared since the 1803 update, it's essentially the Windows recovery environment that shows up if your installation has been corrupt or something, and it has an array of recovery tools. Common causes:

- A faulty USB cable.

- Try another USB cable from the enclosure to the computer USB port. E.g. a printer may use the same type.

- A physically faulty USB port, but normally that would apply to only one at a time - bent out of shape, the plastic plug is dislodged or missing -try other ones.

- Your USB enclosure circuits or it's power adapter or the hard drive in it may be faulty. Remove the drive from the case and try it directly connected to your computer internally. If it's a 2.5" drive inexpensive adapters are available.
If the hard drive works fine, either there is something wrong with your USB, or the enclosure's circuits are faulty, or the power adapter is faulty.

Hi,

The Surface Book keyboard requires minimal care to function well. To clean the keyboard, wipe it with a lint-free cloth dampened in mild soap and water. We don't recommend removing the keys from the keyboard as this may cause more damage. There are some who use electrical contact cleaner spray to clean the keyboard. However, we also don't recommend applying liquids directly to your Surface. You can use a rubber air blower instead.
