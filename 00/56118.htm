considerate_guy say:

Let me go over some terminology, so we are on the same page.

There are two things that you can save from Movie Maker. 

  1. You can save your project in a .wlmp file.  This saves the complete state of your editing session with all the individual elements ready for further editing (rearranging, trimming, adding transitions, etc.)
  2. You can save your movie in a .wmv or .mp4 file.  This gathers all your editing instructions together and creates a single video/audio file for playing in Windows Media Player.

If your intent is to come back to make further edits, you need to do that with your File menu (Alt+F) and look under Recent projects.  That will show a list of .wlmp files (projects) to let you reopen and edit them further.

If your intent is to view or share the results of your editing session (usually the last step when you no longer plan to make further edits) then you save your movie.  This is done by going to the File menu and choosing Save movie.  The resulting file is a single video/audio stream.  This is good for playback in Windows Media Player.

I think what you tried to do was to open up this .wmv file as if it were a project.  But it isn't.  Instead, use the method I mentioned (File > Recent projects) to open the .wlmp file instead.

Another analogy, to make it clearer is to think of baking a cake.

  • The videos, photos and songs are the ingredients.
  • As you add items to your storyboard, you are creating a recipe.  You can come back to your saved recipe (project) to make further changes later.
  • When you save your movie, it is like baking the cake.  Movie Maker follows all your instructions about which clips go where and it makes a final creation that can be shared with others.

If you try to instead go back to your cake and make further changes, it won't let you easily.  That would be like trying to extract eggs from a completed cake.  But if you instead want to modify the recipe and say use 2 eggs instead of 3, you just do that in your recipe (project).

Hi Gabriela,

This is a recovery partition brought into view in 1803. You cannot remove, but only you must remove its mapping of its respective drive letter, which is D. Once you do this, it brings it back to its intended visibility, being hidden and out of sight. Once you do this, you won't be pestered with low disk space notifications anymore. You will need to remove the drive letter and mount point in DiskPart in order to do this.

Hit Windows key + R together to go into Run. Type in the following: diskpart

Enter the following in the new DiskPart window:

select volume D
remove letter=D

Does not work after a reboot? Try entering this instead in an administrator session of Command Prompt: mountvol D: /D

pedro v flores say:
Hello AmyLu361
Please Follow these methods
Method 1
-Open Settings app by simultaneously pressing Windows logo and I keys on the keyboard.

-In the Settings app, navigate to Accounts > Family and other accounts. If you are on Enterprise edition of Windows 10, please navigate to Accounts > Other accounts.
- Here, you can see all admin as well as standard user accounts. As said before, the account which you have currently signed in doesn’t appear in the list here. For instance, if you have two admin accounts “A” and “B” on your PC, you need to sign in to account “A” to delete admin account “B.”

-Click on the admin account which you want to delete or remove. Click the Remove button.

-When you see the following confirmation dialog, click Delete account and data button to delete the account and all data from the account.

Method 2
-Open Control Panel. Change View by to Small icons. Click User Accounts.

-Click Manage another account link to see all user accounts on the PC.

- Click on the admin account which you want to delete or remove.
- Click Delete the account link.

-When you see the following confirmation dialog, either click Delete Files or Keep Files button. If you click the Keep Files button, Windows automatically saves the data of the account in a folder on your current desktop before deleting the account.

I hope it helps you
