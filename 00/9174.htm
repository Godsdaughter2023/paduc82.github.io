MS Publisher mailmerge loses transparency when exported to a pdf - Microsoft Community

amandaaes say:

MS Publisher mailmerge loses transparency when exported to a pdf

MS Publisher loses transparency and functional hyperlinks when I export a mailmerge to pdf format. But will export and retain these features when exported to xps format. I would rather have a pdf format, how can I make this work? Thanks.

davidf2 say: Some PDF formats do not support transparencies. When you export your PDF > Optimize for: options button > under PDF options uncheck ISO 19005-1 compliant (PDF/A).

If you produced your 'transparencies' using Publisher tools then consider using a third party image editor to produce your transparent GIF or PNG.

You might also either compress all your pictures before you produce your PDF to keep the file size down, and/or under the Publish options choose the minimum size which will produce a PDF at 96 ppi which is the best choice for viewing on screen.

It appears you are using Win10 which I understand has a new PDF printer driver installed. You can try printing to it instead of the Publisher export to PDF approach.

If none of these suggestions lead to a solution then change your page layout and design until you get the results you desire. Sometimes Publisher just can't produce the results you want and you have to tweak the layout and formatting until it is something Publisher can work with. For example if you layer a picture over a text box with a hyperlink and convert the publication to a HTML formatted web page, Publisher will choke and convert the picture and text to a single combined picture and at the same time kill the hyperlink. The only fix is to not overlap the two elements. Rebuild your page one element at a time, testing as you go to learn what will work and what won't.

Hi Palcouk

I am not quite sure why this has been split off from my previous thread but it may account for your questions here.

If you care to look at the previous thread, which was started by me, you will see that I detailed exactly which versions/builds are affected and what steps I and, I think, Trogladites have taken to resolve the problem.  The fact that your system is unaffected does not, sadly, mean that there is not a problem for others.

Very simply, the problem appears to be due to the way that the New Ribbon has been coded.  And from what I can see it is a problem that appears to manifest itself when there are a large number of Outlook folders.

As I explained in the previous thread the problem arose after upgrading to Office 365 Business Premium - Version 1809 (Build 10827.20181 Click to Run).  Immediately the shortcuts Ctrl+Shift+V and Ctrl+Y became unusable with the folder dialog taking 20-30 seconds to open. 

The first clue to the problem came after it was suggested that we should try Safe Mode (which made absolutely no difference).  However, when Outlook was restarted in Normal Mode it opened, for reasons that have not been explained (presumably another coding error), with the Old Style Ribbon and immediately the shortcuts started working again as they always had.  Unfortunately, after opening and closing Outlook a few more times it reverted back to the New Ribbon and the shortcuts again became unusable.

Like Trogladites, I and my client have wasted days, following all, the no doubt, well meaning but scripted suggestions, including the MS Diagnostics, online repair, recreating the profile, re-downloading the data all to absolutely no effect.

So finally, today, I decided to revert back to the version of Office 365 immediately prior to the introduction of the New Ribbon (Version 1808 (Build 10730.20102)) and instantly the problem was resolved.  Once again the shortcuts all worked instantaneously.

I realise this doesn't "prove" that the problem is with the New Ribbon but as they say "if it looks like a duck and quacks like a duck it probably is a duck".

Nothing else on the system was changed, the operating system is the same, the Outlook .ost file is the same, the Profile is the same and the number of folders are unchanged.

So it would be really good if instead of pointing the finger at the users and wasting their time with a string of speculative suggestions someone could actually have a look at the way this New Ribbon has been coded and what it is doing to the program resources.  My guess, for what it is worth, is that the problem lies with the way that the scalable graphics icons have been used in the folder dialog and what happens when there are a large number of folders.
