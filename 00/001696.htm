bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

I didn't expect updating to change anything in this regard :-) I simply was commenting on the fact that your installation was behind.

The first point is that Quick Look is a basic utility provided by the OS as a simple means of viewing the general content of any sort of document. It does not have the tools to render anything other than a reasonable impression of what the document contains. It is not at all uncommon for that content to display somewhat differently than the document displays when it is opened in a program which supports its structural layout & certain more sophisticated features. For example, the flow of the document will differ because there is no pagination, Headers & Footers don't repeat, page numbering may be off or may not display at all. In short, Quick View is just that, it can't be relied on for anything more.

The key consideration is: Does the document display properly in the Quick Preview when you go to File> Print? Also, does the document actually print to paper or PDF as expected?

If so, my guess is that what you're seeing in Quick Look is content in the document which has been formatted as Hidden text. Hidden is one of the font attributes which is not supported by the Quick Look utility, so even though printing it is at the discretion of the user Quick Look will display it regardless of whether it is showing in the document, itself. That can be determined quite readily by opening the document in Word then displaying the non-printing characters [¶]. If my hunch is correct the Hidden text will be visible when you do.

If my hunch is incorrect I'm not sure what else might be the cause without having a first-hand look at the document. john w. vinson say:

The format of the field is irrelevant. A Date/Time value is stored as a number, a count of days and fractions of a day since an arbitrary start time (#12/30/1899 00:00:00# if you're interested).

To calculate elapsed time you can use the DateDiff function: e.g. in a Query you can type

Elapsed: DateDiff("h", [ReferralReceived], [WhenSeen])

using your actual fieldnames of course.

Note that this returns integer hours, the number of hour boundaries crossed between the times. If you need hours and fractions of an hour, use "n" (miNutes, "m" is Months) in the DateDiff expression and divide by 60.
