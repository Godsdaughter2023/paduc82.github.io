Hi Shashank,

Modern UI apps including Windows Store will not work due to the proxy settings. You may have to disable and check with the issue.

Method 1: Use the Modern UI apps as well as Windows Store without proxy settings. Follow the steps to disable the proxy connection:
a. Open Internet Explorer.
b. Click on tools and select Internet Options.
c. Click on Connections.
d. Click on LAN Settings.
e. Remove check mark next to proxy server.
f. Click on OK.
g. Restart the browser.

Method 2: If the issue still persists, you may also try resetting the store cache and check:

a. From the desktop press Windows + R keys.

b. Type "wsreset.exe" on run window and press enter key.

Method 3: Make sure that the Date and Time settings are correct.
Some secure sites require that the date and time match the date and time of the secure site. Make sure that the Date and Time settings are correct on the computer. To do this, follow these steps:
a. Double click on the time and date on the right hand side bottom of your screen.
b. Click on change date and time settings.
c. Click on Internet time and click on change settings.
d. Place a check mark at “Synchronize time with internet server”
e. Click on Apply and Ok.

The screen Resolution should be at least 1024x768.

Write to us if you are still unable to connect to Windows Store. We will be happy to assist you.

Thank you Palcouk. I have learned some more since first posing my query, and I will now fill in some details about how to set up a new account without email option. The key is getting back to the starting point where excluding email is an option (the MS Outlook Setup wizard). Close Outlook if it is open. In Win7Pro go to Control Panel. Then, Mail > Show Profiles button > General tab. Add a new profile. Delete the old profile. Click on the radio button for "Always use this profile" (when starting Microsoft Outlook), and be sure that the new profile is selected in the drop down box immediately below. Now, open Outlook, and follow the Account Setup wizard selecting the option to setup without an email account, but be prepared for the warning that an email account is required for "full functionality" of Outlook.
