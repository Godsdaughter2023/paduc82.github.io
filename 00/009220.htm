Hi mike hishan,

 

We understand your frustration regarding your lost emails. Below are the possible reasons why emails got lost/deleted from a Outlook account:

1. Messages were manually deleted either due to accidental deletion or were moved or deleted due to your account filter settings.

2. Messages were deleted due to account compromise issues where a hacker might have gained access to your account and deleted the emails.

3. If you are using an e-mail client or a third party spam filtering software to access your account. This often happens when using the Pop3 protocol to retrieve your mail and the option “Always leave a copy of the message on the server” is not selected.

4. The account was marked as “Inactive." Accounts become inactive if you don’t sign in for more than 365 days or within the first 10 days after signing up for an account.

8. If you were accessing your emails on a mail client such as Windows Live Mail or through your mobile device, but didn’t log into your web account for over 365 days, you may lose emails due to inactivity in web account.

carashanelaris say:

MS Word 2013 inserts 2 spaces after a Period. How can I change this behavior to insert only 1 space after a period?

  • With some versions of MS Word the standard is that two spaces are automatically added after a period (full stop).
  • With some versions of MS Word the standard is that one space is inserted after a period (full stop).

  • In the 'olden days', 2 spaces were used as a means of working with typesetting machines (think Gutenberg press). 
  • In the modern day, with the advent of computers, the "typesetting" need for two spaces is no longer an issue - so, we only need one space after a period. 

I have a version of MS Word (MS Word 2013) that inserts two spaces after a period (full stop). [I know many of you don't believe it - but it's true. This is not due to Add-ins or anything else.]

My question:

How do I stop MS Word 2013 from inserting two spaces? I only need one space after a period.

NOTE: I am not asking about spaces after a paragraph. I'm asking about the period that you see at the end of sentences.

suzanne s. barnhill say:
There is no version of Word that automatically inserts two spaces after a period. This must be done manually or perhaps with a macro. If you are receiving documents that have two spaces following a period, it is because the creator typed them that way. The quickest way to clean up such documents is to search for two spaces and replace with one. If the creator has also used spaces for indents, centering, etc. (not uncommon among older users who learned to type on a typewriter), then of course you have worse problems.

I'm replying to my own question because I have found a solution to it.  Apparently what happened was that the scammers somehow managed to force  Edge to open with their own page.  Once opened, it could not be closed by ordinary means.  I opened Edge 'indirectly' by opening an external link.  Edge then opened with my link as the 2nd tab - the first tab being 'their' page.  Hitting the red X on their tab then closed it leaving only my page open.  Next I went to the browser settings and cleared all the browsing history.  Problem solved.
