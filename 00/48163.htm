considerate_guy say:

To apply any effect to just a *portion* of a video clip, you must split out that portion. Set the playback marker at the beginning of the part you want to affect and split the clip (Video Tools | Edit > Split). The keyboard shortcut for this is M which is much faster. Then set the playback marker to the endpoint and make another split.

Once you have split the clip into 3 parts, select the part in the middle and apply the Speed Up to just that.

This same splitting method works for any effect that you want to apply to just a part of a clip.

logan lyon say:

Recording in Windows media center in sleep mode

Original title: Recording in hibernate or sleep mode

Will windows media center record from an HDHomeRun Prime in sleep mode or hibernate?
kevin d souza say: Hey Logan,

Windows Media Center will record the programs from HD home run prime or any television tuner installed. However, you may have to follow some rules.

- If you turn off your computer, Windows Media Center does not record a scheduled program. The computer must be turned on or be in sleep mode to record a TV program. The computer does not automatically go into sleep mode if Windows Media Center is open.

- Make sure you turn off the monitor and turn down or mute the volume if you have a scheduled a recording in the middle of the night and do not want to be disturbed.

- Your scheduled recording might not record in the following situation: You schedule a recording in Windows Media Center to start in less than ten minutes, close Windows Media Center, and put your computer in standby or hibernation immediately. Use either of the following to record your scheduled recording:

a. Do not close Windows Media Center before you put your computer into standby or hibernation.
b. After you schedule a recording, wait one minute before you put the computer into standby or hibernation.
