I’ve researched and tried several find and replace methods, including caret codes, with no luck. I cannot seem to get it to find the string of left square bracket, 4 characters (numbers), right square bracket. There are other strings in the document that have data between square brackets, but they all have more than 4 characters between them. Any ideas?

jay freedman say:
You can use either a regular search or a wildcard search.

For the regular search, the Find What expression should be

[^#^#^#^#]

For a wildcard search, the equivalent Find What expression is

\[[0-9]{4}\]

Because the brackets have a special meaning in a wildcard expression, you need to use a backslash to say that you want to find an actual bracket.

tom ogilvy say:

NH,

when you use currentregion with special cells, you get a contiguous range. 

Contiguous means all the cells touch each other - there are no missing rows.

Assuming you data (for example purposes) is in the range A1:Z100

So Range("A1:Z100")  is a contiguous range and

ArrDAta = .Range("A1").CurrentRegion.Value

would expand to

ArrData = .Range("A1:Z100).value

would hold all your data and the it would be an array of size ArrData(1 to 100, 1 to 26)

But if you applied a filter to your data and say  the filter had rows

1:10 visible and rows 21:30 visible then your rows.count would be 20 rows

But your range would be   Range("A1:Z10,A21:Z30")

this is not contiguous because it includes two separate areas.  When  you did

ArrData = .Range("A1").CurrentRegion.specialCells(xlVisible).Value

then ArrData will be   ArrData(1 to 10, 1 to 26)

then when you loop through your data 1 to 20, you will get the subscript out of range when you first dimension for the array goes above 10. 

As a work around, you could pick up all your data

ArrDAta = .Range("A1").CurrentRegion.Value

then when you have your code calculate j, instead of j + 1 you woud set the value of j to the next visible row. 

I haven't gone all through your code to discover where all the places are where you might have problems with hidden rows - I am just addressing the error you posted.

And I fully support Dana's suggestion of doing   Range("A1").CurrentRegion.Specialcells(xlvisible)

because for me  Range("A1").SpecialCells(xlvisible) picked up every cell in the worksheet that was visible.  (I am testing in Excel 2007).  This is different from versions previous to that I believe in that they did seem to stick to the current region when only one cell was used as a base - but I could be misremembering.

I guess from your description you had ONE table in mind, with ResourceID, WeekEndingDate, RfiNumber, etc.

That would be a mistake.

Let's step back and understand the data: Each resource works on many RFIs. Each RFI is worked on by many resources (or the same one over time).

That sets up a classic many-to-many relation, which requires THREE tables to resolve:
tblResources
ResourceID autonumber PK
ResourceName text(50) required uniqueindex

tblRfi

RfiID autonumber PK
RfiNumber text(50) required uniqueindex

tblWorkEntry

ResourceID long int PK

RfiID long int PK

WeekEndung date required duplicateindex

Billable yesno required default=yes

Then open the Relationships form, put the tables on the design surface, draw the lines between related fields and in the dialog that comes up check the box to enforce the relation.

One more thing you should do is replace the RfiNumber textbox in the subform by a combobox on tlbRfi.

Questions? Just ask, but this is the relational approach. It may seem foreign right now, but it will make sense over time, and the benefits vastly outweigh the extra complexity.

scottgem say:

First I see a couple of problems, Leaving your autonumber PKs with the name ID, is a mistake IMHO. I always name my PKs tablenameID and the corresponding FKs the same. This make using the relations easier. Another issue is using spaces in object names. This can come back to haunt you.

So, for example The ID field in the RFIReason should be RFIReasonID and the corresponding foreign key would also be RFIReasonID. Also, in your WorkTable, if RFINumber is unique and you are going to use it as a key, then make it the key and forget the ID field. Another, similar issue is in tblGFSResources. names are not unique and should not be a PK. Use the EmployeeNumber instead and use that as your FK in in tblGFSData and tblLinkResources1. Also you don't need EmployeeName, EmployeeNumber and LegacyEmployeeNumber repeated in two tables. I'm not even sure why tblGFSResources even exists. Also person names should be broken out into their components. So your data structure does need work.

Now to your question. In the subform, set the default values for the EmployeeNumber and Weekending controls (those controls will be hidden) to reference the controls on the main form:

=Forms!mainformname!cboEmployee

=Forms!mainformname!txtWeekEnd

So when a new record is entered, it will pick up the values from the mainform.

In fact, you don't really need a subform. Us a form in Continuous form mode instead of a datasheet. And put the two unbound controls for the employee and data in the form header.
