Hi.  Just two cents.  I believe with the large (~200000) value in the equation, the slope for the inverse is "kind of steep" near the expected value, and is nearly flat a little ways away.  The 'Inverse' has a narrow range, so when there is a derivative approaching zero just a little ways away from the answer, the normal "Newton" method will take the next guess outside the valid range of the inverse function.   I believe that's why one needs to help Solver by placing the start valve away from values that are nearly flat and closer to the solution where there is a slope.

Another technique is to recognize the machine precision issues of testing if two values are equal.

(ie Target = 1000)

The technique here is to change the target equation to a zero function

=200000 * (1- NORMDIST(A4, 0.1666, 0.00265, TRUE))-1000

The objective now is to minimize (not equal), with the added constraint that Target >= 0.

Then, switch to the Evolutionary method, and bump up the constraint precisions.

This will take longer as Solver tries harder to find a valid point to work with, and then it should home in on a solution.

The NORM.S.INV  is an alternative to NORMINV that seems to be pretty accurate for this problem using Excel 2013. 

=0.1666-0.00265*NORM.S.INV(1000/200000)

If you add the account with IMAP/Exchange, you may follow the steps to achieve your goal:

  1. Create a folder under “on my computer” list.
  2. Right click the email folder from this account>choose “copy”
  3. Copy the folder to another folder under “on my computer” list.
  4. Repeat the steps until you copy all the email folders from this account to “on my computer” folder.
  5. Remove the account.
