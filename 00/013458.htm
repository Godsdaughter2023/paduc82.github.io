Typically this is cause by...
- Not having the latest updates or not running windows essentials
- Being behind an access point or proxy that does extra filtering
- Not running the WebClient service, which is necessary for OneNote to operate (it is on by default on client OSes)
- Having stale credentials or multiple credentials saved in the credentials cache.

You can see which passwords are cached by running "cmdkey /list" or "Manage Network Passwords". I have seen people clearing this state by stopping the webclient service, using the Credential Manager (Manage network passwords) to delete all hotmail and live-related passwords, restarting the WebClient service and restarting OneNote. Disclaimer: You better know all your passwords if you try the workaround above. If this paragraph doesn't sound straightforward you probably shouldn't attempt these steps - deleting credentials could block your from sites you are using daily (but obviously if you know all your passwords that will not be a problem).

Hi,

Here are reasons why you might get Junk Mail:

  • Senders of junk mail (spammers) have applications that guess common email addresses, such as addresses that use only a first name, a first initial and last name, or a popular nickname.
  • When you sign up for a service, the service could sell your email address to other services, and those services might send you unsolicited email.
  • When you post your email address on a public web page, your address could be copied and used for unsolicited email.
  • Spammers who have your email address may provide it to other spammers.
