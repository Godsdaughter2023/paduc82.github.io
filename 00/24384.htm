Heads up: The Windows Insider Program page in Settings changed - Microsoft Community

cbarnhorst say:

Heads up: The Windows Insider Program page in Settings changed recently. Check it out if you haven't already.

If you are like me you made your WIP settings choices months or more ago and have not had occasion to revisit the WIP page in Settings and make new choices.  You might find some surprises when you look again.  The recent update to the WIP page that enabled Skip Ahead also changed the way you choose Release Preview, Slow, and Fast rings.  You no longer just choose one of the three.  You now choose the kind of content you want and the app tells you what ring you are on and sometimes a choice of branch.

Release Preview:  You no longer choose Release Preview directly.  Instead, you choose as in the illustration below:

The bottom line is wrong.  You will not get a build on Release Preview until the end of the cycle when you will be upgraded to the new version of Windows just before the public roll out.  Release Preview Insiders do not receive development builds.  They stay on the current (public) release and receive quality and security roll ups in advance of Patch Tuesdays in order to give feedback on problems with the updates so MSFT can fix them before Patch Tuesday.

Slow Ring:  You choose the content and the pace.  Choose "Active development of Windows" and "Slow" for the pace.

Fast Ring:  You choose the content and the app chooses the pace.  Choose either "Active development of Windows" or "Skip ahead to the next Windows Release" and "Fast" will appear for the pace."  You are not choosing the Ring.  You are choosing the content and the app chooses the ring automatically.  Note:  Skip ahead is a limited-seat offering and is currently closed for new Insiders because all seats are filled.

In the above three screens, you will see "Only one level is available for your selected branch" in red when your content choice only makes sense in one Ring.  Currently, only screen two has a content possibility that makes sense in two different rings (Slow or Fast).
