    Quit Skype or use Windows Task Manager to kill any Skype.exe process. Go to Windows Start and in the Search/Run box type %appdata%\microsoft and then press Enter or click the OK button. The Windows File Explorer will pop up. There locate a folder named “Skype for Desktop”. Rename this folder to something different, e.g. Skype for Desktop_old.

    Reboot your computer and try again to make a Skype call.

     

    roberta a.. say: Hi Ashlysion, I got it, Go to Skype> Preferences> Audio / Video and click on Clear audio preferences.
    Check your sound and the microphone if they work properly on Skype, you can make a test call to our assistant Echo.

    1 Search for the Echo / Sound Test Service contact.
    2 Call Echo and follow the instructions.

    If you heard Echo's voice and listened to your recorded message, your sound and your microphone are working fine.

    But if you can't hear your voice Echo checks the speaker or the headphones. If your device is integrated, you don't need to connect anything.

    However, the problem persists, completely uninstall and reinstall the Skype app https://support.skype.com/en/faq/FA10634/.
    Reboot your PC after that.

    Try also to:
    1 Update the firewall settings:    https://support.skype.com/en/faq/FA1017/can-i-c...
    2 Configure the ports used by Skype: https://support.skype.com/en/faq/FA1017/can-i-c...
    3 Restore Internet Explorer

    Hope this helps!
