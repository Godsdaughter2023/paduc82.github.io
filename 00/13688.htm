a. user say:

Excel stores dates as the number of days since Dec 31, 1899, including portions of dates (1 AM is +.0146666 for example).  It doesn't actually store dates in the format it uses, it just uses that format to talk to humans.  Granted, it has a few limited means to take certain human date formats and immediately convert them to the number of days since 1900, but your format isn't one of them.  All is not lost though.

(all formulas below use A1 as the cell with your date)

If you have the date you mention in a cell, you can type in =datevalue(right(A1,9)) and you will get the number of days for that date.  The one problem is that you have a variable length date, but there is a way around that...

=datevalue(right(A1,len(A1)-find(" ",A1,1))) will do the same thing for any length date

Now we just need the time.  =LEFT(A1,FIND(":",A1,1)-1) gives you the hour, and =right(left(A1,find(":",A1,1)+2),2) gives you the minute.  =right(left(A1,find(":",A1+4)),2) gives AM/PM

So here is my proposed formula:

=DATEVALUE(RIGHT(A1,LEN(A1)-FIND(" ",A1,1)))+LEFT(A1,FIND(":",A1,1)-1)/24+RIGHT(LEFT(A1,FIND(":",A1,1)+2),2)/1440+IF(RIGHT(LEFT(A1,FIND(":",A1,1)+4),2)="PM",0.5,0)

I played around with a few inputs and they all worked.  You will need to then format that result as a date/time custom format, but excel will know what date it is too.

Edit: Ignore everything below unless you like seeing someone overthinking a problem...

What you need to do is look at your format as a group of numbers and calculate the excel date number using those numbers.  That said, this is not a trivial problem, I started to take a shot at it and quickly realized that I would need a table for "days per month" to figure out the days since January 1st.  I started with:

=(RIGHT(I12,4)-1900)*365 which should have given me the number of days since 1900 (I was 1 off intentionally, have to add the day of the month anyway, why add 1 here and subtract one there).  Guess who forgot leap years???

OK, so *365.25, minus 1 for 2000 not being a leap year, that works:

=(RIGHT(I12,4)-1900)*365.25-1

Now, the fun part, you can either put an array in an ifs, or put it in separate cells, for the ifs you get:

=if(mod(right(i12,4),4),ifs(left(right(i12,10),2)=12,335,left(right(i12,10),2)=11,305,left(right(i12,10),2)=10,274,left(right(i12,9),2)=9,305,244...

Note that the number for the month finder (right(i12,X) changes by one when you go from two digit months to 1 digit months, this could be simplified if you can use the date format 02:10PM 11/01/2016 (same number of characters for any date).

The mod if in the front is to select which of your two ifs arrays you need based on leap year or normal year.

then you figure the day of the month, add that, then divide the hour by 24, add .5 if PM, and add the minutes/1440.  Look at that is date/time format and you will get your input, but excel will also know what the date and time are.

How much work are you willing to put into this though?  You can see that the formula would not be trivial.
