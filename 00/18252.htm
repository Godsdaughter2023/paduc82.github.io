steve rindsberg say:

When PPT creates a presentation or template, it includes "prompt" text (the Click here text you're seeing here) in the language specific to the version of PPT.  In this case, the Taiwanese version of Chinese. 

While you can change all of the defaults of the presentation to English, the original Chinese text remains in the slide master and layouts (including the names of the layouts themselves). 

However, if you do View | Slide Master, you can edit the "Click here" text on the master and each of the layouts and replace the Chinese with whatever English text you want.  As you do this, check the Status bar to make sure that it changes from Chinese-Taiwan to English-US (assuming you're in the US) as you type the new text.  It should, but if not, you may need to make a change to your options.

Then right-click each of the layouts, choose Rename and type a new English name for the layout.

I think that should get you going w/o a huge amount of effort.

erica blair say:

Hi Peter,

To ensure this formula works with vertical dragging, make the change below:

=$B4*C2

Removing the '$' before the 4 will "unlock" it, and allow it to automatically change as you drag down. You can add a '$' before the letter in a reference to lock the column, and before the number to lock the row.

Ensure that your C2 reference is also locked properly. i.e. if it should always be C2 regardless of where the formula is, lock both values: $C$2. If the row should not change, lock just the number: C$2, etc.
