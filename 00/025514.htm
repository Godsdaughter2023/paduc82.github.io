If you are referring to adding a POP account in your Microsoft Hotmail account to send and receive emails, there is an advanced options where in you can configure the POP and SMTP server of the other account. To send an email for other account, here's how: 

  1. Sign in here.
  2. Click gear icon on the upper right side of the screen.
  3. Select More mail settings from the dropdown box.
  4. Under “Managing your account”, click Sending/receiving email from other accounts.
  5. Under "You can send mail from these accounts", click Add another account to send mail from.
  6. Enter the other email address in the space provided.
  7. Click Send verification email.
  8. Sign in to the other email address and look for the email notification to verify the process.
  9. Sign back in to your Outlook.com account.

Once done, you can compose an email and you will noticed that in your main account above in the compose page, a dropdown arrow is available. Click that arrow then select the other email address that you just set up in your account to send emails from.

 

To receive an email for other account, you may perform these steps:

  1. Sign in here.
  2. Click gear icon on the upper right side of the screen.
  3. Select More mail settings from the dropdown box.
  4. Under “Managing your account”, click Sending/receiving email from other accounts.
  5. Under "You can receive mail from these accounts", click Add an email account.
  6. Type your email address and password.
  7. Under the textbox for password, click Advanced options.
  8. For POP server information, type the correct POP server for your email service provider.
  9. Enter the appropriate port to use in "Port".
  10. Key in your username for that account. 
  11. Click Next.
  12. Tick on A separate folder.
  13. Click Save.

For more details, please read this article.

evan risk say:

I had this problem too at work. This is how you can change the default file type while doing a save as.

In Word click on the office logo upper left cornor --> click options then click the "Save" section on the left there should be a drop down menu labled "Save files in this format:" and choose Word 97-2003 file format (*.doc) select "Ok"

 

By doing this though you will loose some formatting ability when you save, but as long as you stick to Word 2003 formatting limits you shouldn't have an issue.
