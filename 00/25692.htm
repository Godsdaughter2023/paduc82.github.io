Check for these in Nokia PC Sync before you synchronize - Microsoft Community

cricfancy say:

Check for these in Nokia PC Sync before you synchronize

Technical Level : Intermediate

Summary

Details

We are seeing too many issues related to Nokia PC Sync.  These are the few things what you can do before raising the issue in discussion forum

 

Check the System requirements and your device compatibility with PC Suite as well as the PIM you are using is compatible with Nokia PC Suite.

 

Make sure that to synchronise your phone with Nokia PC Suite, you have to login with Administration rights 

 

Take backup of your PIM Data on a periodic basis using Nokia Content Copier.

 

Make sure that your Phone's firmware and Nokia PC Suite has got latest versions.

 

If you are synchronizing your device's PIM data with Mails for Exchange, OVI.com etc, please don't synchronize your device with Nokia PC Suite, as it result in loss of your valuable data.

 

Make sure that you have define the settings in correct way(Automatic Sync, Two way Sync), because wrong sync settings will result in loss of your valuable data.

 

Whenever new update available for Nokia PC Suite, after updating PC Suite, you can create new sync settings before you synchronize your device with Nokia PC Suite.

 

Make Sure that your phone is in idle state(No applications should kept open on the phone) while sychronising your phone and PC.

 

Contacts :-

 

Make sure that your contacts does not have any special characters in their First Name & Last Name.

 

Calendar :-

 

Make sure that your calendar entry's Subject, Location field does not have too many characters.

 

Make sure that your calendar entry's does not have indefinite End time.

 

Make sure that your calendar entry does not have too big attachment file.

 

If everything above is clear and if still you are facing issue in synchronizing your data, On your system tray, right click on Nokia PC Sync icon(similar to Yin Yang symbol), exit and then disconnect your phone, connect again and then try to synchronize your device with Nokia PC Suite.

 

I expect our experts to add more details to this, so that it could help our forum members.

 

Hope this helps

    Since Outlook 2016 relies exclusively on Autodiscover to connect with Exchange, it will fail if there are problems with Autodiscover. I'm using an email account with country specific live.com domain (live.com.pk) and had to face a similar problem. I tried adding my email account in Outlook 2016 but it would not find the server. When I tried to add it manually by selecting "Outlook.com or Exchange ActiveSync compatible service", it would not fetch emails. Instead, I would get only a single email saying "Reconnect Outlook 2016 or Outlook 2013 to Outlook.com for uninterrupted email access". Contacting and spending many hours with MS support didn't prove to be useful. 

    What I found was that there was no CNAME setup for autodiscover.live.com.pk due to which Outlook 2016 could not connect to the server using Autodiscover. Other country specific live.com domains (such as autodiscover.live.ca, autodiscover.live.co.uk) had their CNAME pointing to eas-autodiscover.glbdns2.microsoft.com 

    Microsoft needs to fix the broken Autodiscover for live.com.pk domain. As a workaround, I added an entry to the PC's hosts file to point autodiscover.live.com.pk to 157.55.6.73 (the IP address of autodiscover.glbdns2.microsoft.com; had to use the IP address as windows host file does not support adding alias/CNAME) after which I was able to add my email account without any problem.

    • Domain Name: autodiscover.yourdomain.com

    • IP Address: 65.54.188.109

    Amortization table using interest accrued on a 360/365 day basis. - Microsoft Community

    noyazo say:

    Amortization table using interest accrued on a 360/365 day basis.

    Need guidance on what formula to use in order to calculate interest accrued for a loan using 360/365 day basis.

    joeu2004 say: NOYAZO wrote:
    Need guidance on what formula to use in order to calculate interest accrued for a loan using 360/365 day basis.

    That day-count method does not make sense to me.  I wonder if you really mean 365/360, more commonly written as actual/360.  See http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Day_count_convention.

    For actual/360, the annual rate is converted to a daily rate based on 360 days per year.  In the US, that is annualRate/360.  In the EU, I believe that is (1+annualRate)^(1/360)-1.

    Then the periodic interest between Date1 and Date2 is (Date2-Date1)*dailyRate.  The accrued interest is simply the sum of the periodic interest.

    Note:  I prefer not to round interest calculations.

    If you truly mean 360/365, the annual rate would be converted to a daily rate based on 365 days per year:  either annualRate/365, or (1+annualRate)^(1/365)-1.

    And perhaps the periodic interest would be (360/frequency)*dailyRate, where "frequency" is the number of periods per year.  For example, for monthly periods, the periodic interest would be 30*dailyRate (30 = 360/12).

    (But 30/360 is more common for that purpose.  That is, the annual rate is based on 360 days per year, and each month is presumed to have 30 days.)

    For a specific formula, you will need to provide more information, namely what columns and rows contain what values in the amortization table.
