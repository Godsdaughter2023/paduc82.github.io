doktor jones say:

I might suggest restarting your computer as well as your network equipment (cable/dsl modem, router, etc).

My computer hung on the "Checking for updates" for about 15 minutes, but did eventually transition to "Downloading Windows 10", with a percentage progress indicator (and since it started 5 minutes ago, I'm already at 26% downloaded of 2,712MB, which attests to my connection speed and quality). Nachappa obviously isn't paying too much attention to your question because even if a slow/poor quality connection were making your download very slow (up to 24 hours) you still should get "Downloading Windows 10", and not just sit indefinitely at "Checking for updates" for days at a time.

Restarting your computer will help in case the update process is hung, and restarting your network equipment will help if something isn't resolving quite right and it's not able to find the update server (or return responses from the update server to your computer correctly). Specifically, I would recommend the following procedure:

1. Power off your computer

2. Power off any routers/switches you may have (if separate from your cable/DSL modem)

3. Power off your cable/DSL modem

4. Wait 30 seconds

5. Power on the cable/DSL modem and wait for its lights to return to "normal" status (note that if your computer or router are connected via ethernet, one of the lights may remain off, as this would be the ethernet status light, and the connected equipment is currently turned off)

6. Power on your routers/switches (if separate). Wait for their lights to return to "normal" status

7. Power on your computer, and check the update status

8. Wait up to 15 minutes (go make yourself a snack, take the dog for a walk, etc) to see if "Checking for updates" transitions to "Downloading Windows 10"

*Note: If you are not familiar with your cable/DSL modem's lights, and/or router's lights, the best course of action may be to wait 5 minutes after powering on the equipment before proceeding to the next step. 99% of the time the equipment will be online/ready much sooner, but sometimes (especially with advanced cable connections, and some fiber connections) it can take quite a while for them to fully sync up with your ISP and be ready for service.

Good luck!

Have you had a look in the Device Manager for any errors with the USB devices?

Use Device Manager and uninstall all the USB root hubs and devices that show on your machine now (Right-click on them for the uninstall option).  Reboot and let Windows rediscover them and see if that solves your problem.

If nothing helps then the drive may be u/s.  If you ever get a machine to recognise it then you could try Formatting it as FAT32 by Right-clicking on the drive in Explorer and select Format.
