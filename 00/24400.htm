What do you mean by a "backup copy of the program"?

If you want to backup Office, what you need to backup is the installation file/cd and the Product Key number. You can burn the installation file to a CD/DVD. Simply backing up the files that have been installed on the HD is not adequate because that misses all of the registry entries you need to make it work.

However, you should be making periodic backups of all of the files on your HD. That way, WHEN (not if) the crash comes you will be able to recover without losing too much. Here is some introductory information about one type of backup.

Image Copy Backup

Basically a image backup is a "photocopy" of your HD, one that you can "paste" back to the drive to restore it to current condition in case something you do makes you "unhappy".

I haven't played with the Win7 backup tool, but it sounds like is finally adequate. You can also get freeware backup programs to do the same thing. I use Macrium Backup and DriveImage XML. Backup to DVDs or an external USB Drive.
