rohn007 say:
Yes they do.

The 2013 Retail / Boxed CD license allows install on 1-computer and allows you to transfer that installation to new devices as required. This is the type of license you are looking for.  Initially when 2013 was released the Retail license terms had been "improved" to be equivalent to the PKC terms. About a month later, due to bad media coverage, MS retroactively re-instated the right to transfer, but not the second concurrent installation.

Yes both the Product Key Card and OEM licenses are permanently tied to the first hardware they are installed on. The license terms are essentially identical, so go with the cheaper one. There is one additional difference. The OEM license is cheaper because the license terms between MS and the OEM require the OEM to provide "Help" type support for the OEM license.

For the online download and media free card  I am not familiar with their license terms. You have to read the fine print of the specific page. Often it is not clear, so you have to assume the worst.  Office 2007 was offered as a MLK, Medialess Kit, license that was equivalent to the PKC/OEM license terms.

    nayana s m say:

    Hello,

    Welcome to Microsoft community and thanks for posting the question.

     

    As I understand, you would need assistance on how to turn off the ‘Auto Correct’ entries in Outlook.

     

    Which version of Outlook is installed in the computer?

     

    To turn off the auto correct entries in Outlook, take a look at the below steps specified for different Outlook versions:

     

    For Outlook 2003: http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/outlook-help/turn-on-or-off-autocorrect-options-HP003085392.aspx

     

    For Outlook 2007: Open Outlook > click on Tools > Options > Mail Format > Click on ‘Editor Options’ > Under ‘Proofing’, click on ‘Auto Correct Options’ > under ‘AutoCorrect’, uncheck the option ‘Replace text as you type’.

     

    For Outlook 2010: Open Outlook > click on File > Options > Mail > Click on ‘Spelling and AutoCorrect’ > Under ‘Proofing’, click on ‘Auto Correct Options’ > under ‘AutoCorrect’, uncheck the option ‘Replace text as you type’.

     

     

    Hope the information provided is helpful.
