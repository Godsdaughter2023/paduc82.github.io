andre da costa say:
There are some compatibility issues with Windows 10 version 1709/1803 and certain hardware or software. There is no explanation why a lot of these problems are happening in Windows 10 1709/1803, but the release is significantly flawed. Software and Hardware that once worked in previous versions, just suddenly stops working in Windows 10 1709/1803 after an upgrade and sometimes loses functionality or stability.

Just to touch back on why I personally think it might be broken. When Microsoft starts preparing for a new release, it goes through a process called refactoring. It seems this is introducing some significant code changes that breaks certain protocols and API's required by older apps and drivers. Considering the Windows Driver Model has not changed much in its near 30 year history not mention the application programming interfaces such as Win32, this might be the culprit.

My recommendation, focus on the apps and less on having the latest version of Windows. If your apps are working fine and you are getting your work done, minimize your need to jump on the latest release.

As recommended, keep a good Antivirus utility installed (I have Avira installed on a system that can't go any further), use a standard user account for daily usage.

If your system originally came with Windows 8.1 or Windows 7, I would recommend even downgrading to that release and stick with it.

Linux itself is also an option you can consider. I personally like Ubuntu, especially when you go with an LTS release, but make sure your system is supported.
