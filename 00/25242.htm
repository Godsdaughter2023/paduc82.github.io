Within two months at that rate of use you will more than have recovered any time you spend learning them. If you use Word more than that the savings are much more dramatic. See Understanding Styles in Microsoft Word, Tips for Understanding Styles in Word  and Yet Another "Use Styles" Verbal Beating!

This difference was due entirely to the document being directly formatted - a much higher level of complexity. Each paragraph mark in a directly-formatted paragraph carries with it up to fifty different formatting commands. john korchok say:

Many users have issues with text boxes "mysteriously" moving around, but Word's actions are predictable and repeatable. 

The key to positioning text boxes is found by selecting a text box, then choosing Format>Text Box...>Layout>Advanced and selecting the Picture Position tab. This is a complex dialog, since there are first several position types for Horizontal and Vertical measurements: Alignment, Absolute position and Relative position. For most purposes, Absolute position is the best choice.

Then, there is the measurement, and finally the object that the text box will be positioned relative to. For this last parameter, very often I find I choose Page for both horizontal and vertical. This keeps a text box at the same position on a page regardless of where the paragraph is to which it is anchored. The exception is when the paragraph moves to a different page, then the text box moves with it.

The second most common positioning occurs when the text box content is directly relevant to a particular paragraph. In this case, the text box is usually close to the paragraph. Then you want to change at least the Vertical option to Paragraph instead of Page. When you do this, the text box will closely follow the vertical position of the paragraph on the page.

It will be worth a few minutes of your time to play with these options to see how they affect the position of text boxes relative to their anchoring paragraphs. Though it is complex, it is also very customizable to exactly the behavior you require.

In almost every circumstance, there is nothing random about text box positioning. The exception is when you anchor a text box to a paragraph, but then also apply text wrapping that is meant to apply to the anchoring paragraph. In this case, the text box is changing the formatting of the paragraph to which it is anchored and you can end up with unpredictable and weird results.
