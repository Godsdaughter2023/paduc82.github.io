Can I just have one voice recording for the whole presentation, not - Microsoft Community

mmmx22 say:

Can I just have one voice recording for the whole presentation, not separated ones?

I'm working on a project and own a really bad microphone which is why the default recording settings don't help much. Aside from that, cutting off the sound at the beginning and end of a slide completely kills the flow.

Is there any way to get around this?

steve rindsberg say:

The audio quality may be due to your microphone, but it might also be PowerPoint itself. It used to give you quality options so you could choose the best balance between audio quality and file size.  That feature's been removed, and the new "improved" default to create smaller but low quality recordings.

Have you tried recording the narration using an audio recording program?  Audacity is very capable and is free.  It's possible that you'll get better audio quality that way, and if so, there are ways you can chop the audio up into per-slide chunks and insert them into PPT in place of the ones PPT itself records.  That won't help with the stop/start audio on each slide but with a little luck, the audio itself will be better.

It's also possible to insert externally recorded audio as a single file and have it play across multiple slides, which would eliminate the audio start/stop problem but PPT has never been especially accurate at maintaining exact timings, so the slide changes will rarely synch against the audio reliably and will change from PC to PC.

john korchok say:

You can vote for this User Voice suggestion: No audio ducking between slides Microsoft uses these suggestions to prioritize their programming.

I wouldn't hold out a lot of hope for the one-big-narration-file idea, as you would have to re-record the entire narration every time you made an edit to the deck that affected the timing of any one slide. But perhaps a sample of the end of the audio could be looped until the next slide narration begins, so there isn't a silent gap.
