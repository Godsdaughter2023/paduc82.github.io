Using today's date (5/12/15), an employee was last trained on a specific procedure on 4/15/14 it would be over one year past the last training date, I would like that to turn red 365 days after 4/15/14 to show that the employee needs training on that procedure. If an employee were trained on 6/15/14 they would still have a month before they need training on that procedure, I would like that to turn yellow 335 days after 6/15/14 to show that the employee needs to be trained soon! Is conditional formatting the best solution and what formula should I use to get the red and yellow dates.
ashish mathur say:

Hi,

1. Suppose the training dates are in B2:B10

2. Click on cell B2 and go to Home > Conditional formatting > New Rule > Use a formula to determine which cells to format

3. In the formula bar there, enter this formula

=TODAY()-B2>=365

4. Click on Format > Fill > Red > OK/Apply

5. Copy cell B2, select B3:B10, right click > Paste Special > Formats > OK

6. Repeat steps above for the other condition,  The formula will be

=AND(TODAY()-B2>=335,TODAY()-B2<=365)

Hope this helps.

Whether or not you can install your existing Office on the new laptop will depend upon the type of installation that you have.  If the software came already installed on that computer, it is quite likely and OEM version that is only licensed for use on that particular computer.  On the other hand, it you purchased it as a separate item, even if it was at the same time as you purchased that computer, and you have the installation media and the Product Key, then you are free to install it on the new computer, but you may have to use the telephone method of activation.  If you no longer have the Product Key, you may be able to find it by downloading and using the free BelArc advisor to make an inventory of your old computer, which will include the Product Keys for the applications installed on it.
