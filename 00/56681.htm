If the metadata of the drive or the identifier does gets updated, it would notify a drive name such as this. Even when you assign a new drive letter to an unassigned drive on the computer, it would be taken up by this drive. This usually won’t happen as the drive would be deleted or disabled when you remove drive from the computer. As the metadata is not updated, it might create such scenarios.

I would suggest you to check for any unassigned drive under Disk Management. Follow the steps:

  • Press Windows+R to open Run. Type “diskmgmt.msc” and hitEnter.
  • Check if there is an unassigned drive. If yes, right click on it and selectProperties.
  • Navigate to Security tab and check if theObject name matches to the name of the drive\?\Volume.. under Disk Defragmenter.

Apart from this, I would suggest you to update the chipset driver from the manufacturer website of the computer to troubleshoot the issue.
