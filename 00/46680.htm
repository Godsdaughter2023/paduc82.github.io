techfreak_ say:

Skype charges nothing for incoming calls to your Skype Number. The cost of the Skype Number subscription covers any costs involved to reach the number. If you are incurring any charges they would be from your Internet provider for your data usage. If you have set up your Skype Number to forward calls to another phone number (an outgoing call), you will be charged for such calls. This is equivalent to you dialing the forwarded number yourself from your account, every time. If you do not have a subscription that covers the cost of the calls to the number you are forwarding to, you will be charged Pay as You Go rates. Pay as You Go rates include a connection fee per call plus the per minute charge. All calls are rounded up to the next whole minute and charged to the next whole cent.

Be aware that no Skype subscription covers the cost of calls to premium, special (like shared cost), or non geographic numbers (like business numbers). These types of calls incur higher rates than traditional numbers. Any calls or forwards not covered will adhere to Pay as You Go rates. The current rates are on the Skype rates page under the credit section under See rates. 

I can only assume you are forwarding your inbound Skype Number calls to a higher rate phone number that is a more expensive landline/mobile, shared-cost, premium or business number.

anil kumar b say:

Hi Cdubach,

 

A trusted PC is a Microsoft account security feature. Only trusted PCs are allowed to synchronize between computers. One of the great benefits of running Windows 8 on multiple PC’s you own, you can easily sync common settings across computers such as passwords for apps, websites, and networks in addition to other benefits such as themes, backgrounds and color schemes. To do this, you need to trust your Windows PC.

 

a)      To trust your PC after signing into Windows 8 for the first time, open the Action Center and click the Trust this PC link. You can also mouse over the bottom or top right corners of your screen, click the Settings charm, and select Change PC Settings.

 

b)      Click the Trust this PC link on the Users pane.

 

c)      After logging in with the Microsoft account you’re using with Windows 8, you’ll be able to enter the security code Microsoft sent you. This code may be sent to your email address or your cell phone, depending on the recovery information you provided while setting up your Microsoft account. You can click I didn’t get the code to have Microsoft send you the security code at an email or phone number of your choice.

 

d)      If you were emailed a security confirmation, you can also click the Confirm button in the email you received from the Microsoft account team.

 

e)      Click the button or enter the code and your computer will be confirmed as trusted for your Microsoft account.
