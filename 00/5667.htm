In Hotmail (and it appears to still be true in outlook.com) there was a hierarchy to email addresses: Personal, then Work, then Other.

 

If you send an email to a contact that has multiple email addresses without specifying which email address to use, then outlook.com will use the Personal email addresses first. If there are no Personal email addresses then Work email addresses will be used. And lastly Other email addresses will be used if no Personal or Work email addresses exist.

 

If you send email to a group of contacts, then the hierarchy discussed above will be applied to each contact in the group to determine the address to use.

 

If you want to override this hierarchy, then you could create multiple contact entries for those contacts you want to be able to send to a specific address and include in the group the appropriate contact entry.

 

There may be other solutions, but that is what I have done in the past.

 

I hope this helps or answers your question.
