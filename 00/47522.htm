    a. user say:

    Yes I know what a headset is, I even have one. The Plantronics BT300 Bluetooth is not one of those.

    I know what a desktop microphone is, even though I don't have one. The Plantronics BT300 Bluetooth is not one of those; unless I lay my head on my desk while wearing it I suppose.

    In your first message, you implied that the BT 300  was a headset microphone.  In a nutshell, a microphone isand analog device. This means  it captures your speech  via sound waves  created  by your voice  causing  the microphone element to vibrate  at different frequencies.. A computer is obviously a digital  data device.  in order for a computer to read your analog  sound waves, these sound waves must be converted to a digital format. That is why we have soundcards.  The original sound card was usually built into the computer.  Now we can have a sound card connect  to a computer through a USB connection.  This enables us to  use various microphones and bypass the noise within a computer.

    The truth be told, it really does not matter which  microphone you select in the microphone set up. The most important thing for you to do is designate  the soundcard input . In your case this is the Bluetooth USB  BT 300.
