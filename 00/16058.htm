bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

First, a clarification :-) Page Break Preview simply indicates what content will be included on each page... It does not indicate where on the page it will be situated. Unless specified otherwise, Excel prints content starting in the upper left corner based on the Margin settings.

That brings up the first reason why the content is being composed as you see it in Page Layout View & in the Print dialog's Quick Preview: The Margins are set to 0" on all but the right side which, for some reason, is set at .888888888888889". The first suggestion I'd offer is that you specify Margins that are more reasonable. Generally, at least .35" on each side provides a more professional presentation. (Also, it's quite possible that the current settings may even cause content to be cut off on the top & left.) [File> Page Setup - Margins]

Another adjustment you might consider is to use the options in Page Setup to Center the content Horizontally and/or Vertically. Keep in mind, though that the centering is based on the Margins, not the edges of the paper. IOW, If the Top/Bottom margins are not equal and the Left/Right Margins are not equal the content still will not be centered on the page.

Finally, if you want the content to fill more of the page you can tamper with the Scale to Fit features. However, due to the height/width ratio & graphic content it may be better to avoid possible distortion.

Accounts of life[edit]

One of the most prominent Nayanars, Sundarar (8th century) is the first to name Nandanar (called Tirunalaipovar) in literature, however Tirunalaipovar ('he who will go tomorrow') relates to the tale of Nandanar longing to visit Chidambaram; no details of his life are revealed. In the eleventh century, Nambiyandar Nambi devotes a stanza to Nandanar in his Tiruttondar Tiruvandhadhi while recalling the lives of the Nayanars. Tirunalaipovar is described as a Pulayar (Pulaiya, Pulai) who lived in Adanur. He is said to have visited Thillai Nataraja Temple, Chidambaram of his patron god Shiva "by God's grace" and "three thousand Brahmins (priests) of Chidambaram saluted him."[2]

The earliest full (and primary religious) account of Nandanar's life is found in the Tamil Periya Puranam by Sekkizhar (12th century), which is a hagiography of the 63 Nayanars, but it was the Nandanar Charitram by the Tamil poet Gopalakrishna Bharati (1810–1896) brought Nandanar's tale to public attention. The Nandanar Charitam (printed in 1861),[2] the magnum opus of Bharati, added new elements to Sekkizhar's tale.[1][3] Though it is unknown when he lived exactly, generally he is dated to 7th or 8th century CE.[1]

The Periya Puranam[edit]

The Periya Puranam narrates that Nandanar belonged to Adanur (Adanoor) in the Chola kingdom. Presently, Adanur is located in Thanjavur district, in the Indian state of Tamil Nadu. He was born in the Pulaiya caste, who were regarded "untouchables" (see Dalits). They were agricultural labourers and singers. Another description considers Nandanar from the Dalit caste of Paraiyar, who served as labourers and were drummers as per the caste code.[4]

Sub RemoveBrackets()
    Dim lngStart As Long
    lngStart = Selection.Start
    With Selection.Find
        .ClearFormatting
        .Text = "["
        .MatchWholeWord = False
        .MatchWildcards = False
        .Wrap = wdFindStop
        .Forward = False
        Do While .Execute
            If Selection.Start < lngStart Then Exit Do
            Selection.Extend Character:="]"
            Selection.Delete
        Loop
    End With
End Sub
