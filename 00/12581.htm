I think I found a way to do it by adding a table referencing the dates from the Pivot Table, and then using that table in each formula. Here goes:

This is the PivotTable that I created based on your data. It matches what you show:

This is the dashboard that I created to match yours. I am assuming you only want to show from Mon-Wed:

Now here is the table I added below the dashboard to break out the dates of the Pivot Table:

And here are the formulas that I used in that table to get the date parts in each cell:

Now, here are the formulas that I used in the cells of the dashboard:

In cell B2 I entered this and copied down to B5. (It's a modification of the one you posted above):

=INDEX(Sheet1!$B$5:$F$8,MATCH($A2,Sheet1!$A$5:$A$8),IF(LOOKUP("Mon",$A$8:$E$8)="Mon",MATCH(DATEVALUE(HLOOKUP("Mon",$A$8:$E$11,2)&"-"&HLOOKUP("Mon",$A$8:$E$11,3)&"-"&HLOOKUP("Mon",$A$8:$E$11,4)),Sheet1!$B$4:$F$4)))

Then I copied B2 to C2, changed the references from "Mon" to "Tue" and added the +B2 at the end, to look like this, and copied down to C5:

=INDEX(Sheet1!$B$5:$F$8,MATCH($A2,Sheet1!$A$5:$A$8),IF(LOOKUP("Tue",$A$8:$E$8)="Tue",MATCH(DATEVALUE(HLOOKUP("Tue",$A$8:$E$11,2)&"-"&HLOOKUP("Tue",$A$8:$E$11,3)&"-"&HLOOKUP("Tue",$A$8:$E$11,4)),Sheet1!$B$4:$F$4)))+B2

Finally, I copied C2 to D2, changed the references to "Wed", and copied down to D5. Like so:

=INDEX(Sheet1!$B$5:$F$8,MATCH($A2,Sheet1!$A$5:$A$8),IF(LOOKUP("Wed",$A$8:$E$8)="Wed",MATCH(DATEVALUE(HLOOKUP("Wed",$A$8:$E$11,2)&"-"&HLOOKUP("Wed",$A$8:$E$11,3)&"-"&HLOOKUP("Wed",$A$8:$E$11,4)),Sheet1!$B$4:$F$4)))+C2

I am assuming that you didn't want Thursday and Friday in your dashboard, but if you do, you just need to repeat above.

Now, even if there is only 1 day of data, or more, the formulas will account for that and automatically show the correct amount(s).
