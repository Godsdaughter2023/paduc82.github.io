Hint...sometimes you have to make a cutout of people
or objects that are in the background photo and just
leave them in place so you can "slide" a cutout of
another person behind it to make it look realistic. That
is the way the Dad was added to the example ...his
cutout had to be layered "under" the edge of the other
two subjects in the photo. Also I had to make a
cutout of his right arm and reverse it so I could use
it as his left arm.

Also the "Clone Stamp" is important because it can
aid in blending the different layers by copy/pasting
portions of the photo in areas that do not fit as expected.
In my example photo the clone stamp was very useful
for blending the grassy areas.

***Post moved by the moderator to the appropriate forum category.***

joseph ban say:

Hi,

The Telemetry logs is a temporary file created by Microsoft Customer Experience Improvement Program in providing automatic feedback to improve the performance of the operating system. To disable it, you may follow the steps below:

  1. Click Start, type task scheduler and press Enter.
  2. On the Task Scheduler window, go to this path: Task Scheduler Library\Microsoft\Windows\Application Experience.
  3. On the Application Experience folder, right-click on the Microsoft Compatibility Appraiser and select Disable.
