A quick way to see whether there are such section breaks is to click View > Draft, where they'll appear as double dotted lines with "Section Break (Next Page)" in the middle (or maybe Odd Page).

If there aren't section breaks there, then the document is probably corrupted. To try to fix that, copy everything in the document except the final paragraph mark on the last page, and paste into a new blank document. If there are section breaks but not at those locations, then the corruption might be in the first section break that occurs after the second page 11; in that case, copy/paste in two pieces, from the beginning up to but excluding that section break, and from just after the section break to the end of the document.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

Apparently the document contains section breaks and you have restarted numbering at some point?

But I'm not sure what you mean by "Navigation view." The Navigation pane does not show page numbers. Are you referring to the page numbers shown on the status bar? If so, note that there are two different kinds of page numbers that can be displayed here:

The one that shows "Page: 1" is the Formatted Page Number. That shows the number that will print on the page. If you have restarted numbering somewhere, this may be different from the "whole" page number (the number of the page in the total sequence).

The one that shows "Page 1 of 75" is what I call the "whole" page number. It shows the actual sequence of the page in the document.

So if you had a section break between pages 4 and 5 of a 75-page document and restarted numbering in section 2, on the fifth page of your document (assuming you were also displaying the section number on the status bar), the status bar would say:

Page: 1   Section: 2   Page: 5 of 75
