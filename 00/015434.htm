From this link: http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows/outlook/alias-rename-link-FAQ?woldogcb=0

 

How can I join together two or more Microsoft accounts (Outlook.com, Hotmail.com, or Live.com)?

If you want to read and send email from two Microsoft accounts in a single inbox, you can make one of the accounts an alias for the other.

The first step is to choose which account you want to be the primary alias. Typically you would choose the account with the contact list you most want to use.

Next, add the second account as an alias to the account that will be the primary alias.

  1. Sign in to the primary alias account, and then click your user name in the upper-right corner.

  2. Click Account settings. You might have to sign in again.

  3. Click Overview.

  4. Click Account aliases, and then click Add alias.

  5. Select Add an existing email address as a Microsoft account alias, enter the email address for the second Microsoft account, and then click Add alias.

(At this point it give an error about Hotmail.com being a reserved domain.)

 

Finally, verify the alias.

  1. Under the new alias, click Verify. You might have to sign in again.

  2. Click Send email.

  3. Sign out of your primary alias account, and then sign in to your second account.

  4. Open the confirmation email from the Microsoft account team.

    The mail contains two links, one to verify the alias, one to cancel it.

  5. Click the link to verify the alias.

    You'll be taken back to the primary alias Account aliases page with the message Your alias has been verified.
