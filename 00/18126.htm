swischner say:
Unfortunately there is no way to access OneNote files (like ONE or ONEPKG) with any other OneNote version than OneNote 2010/2013 or 2016 for Windows as part of MS Office for Windows.

You absolutely need access to a Windows PC with MS Office to open the OneNote files from there. Then you could transfer them to OneDrive using OneNote's internal "Share" function. After that you can access the notes from any OneNote including OneNote for MacOS.

Don't try to simply copy the files to OneDrive without assistance of OneNote for Windows. You would not be able to open them on your Mac, because they need to be saved in a special, hidden format that (for now) only OneNote for Windows is able to create.

mccbleue say:

The software recovery tool needs the phone to be booted up and working to operate normally. In your case, you need to select the options to treat the phone as if it were dead, then the software will be force-flashed onto the phone regardless of operational condition. Since plugging in to the computer via data cable initiates charging and therefore switches the phone on, you might have to allow the phone to fuly discharge first. If it doesn't work, then you may have to get it professionally repaired.

 

About disconnecting the battery for ten minutes:

(1) Ten minutes will acieve nothing, if the battery being cut was going to help then a few seconds would do the trick - I think the soft reset procedure normally takes about ten seconds.

(2) When you say that you disconnected for ten mins, it sounds like you have been rooting about inside the phone itself. If you have opened it in any way, then you will have kissed a sweet goodbye to your warranty and will have to throw yourself on the mercy of the repair network, you might have to pay for a non-warranty repair if this is the case.
