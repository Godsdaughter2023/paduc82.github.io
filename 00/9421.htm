OK, this will take few minutes to clear up your misunderstandings.

You have learned that Windows and Office require separate product keys. This is because they are separate products that are sold and installed separately. In other words, Office is not a "free" part of Windows.

Office requires a 25 character product key to "activate".  This key is specific to the year and Office bundle, ie you CANNOT use the key from Office 2007 Home and Business to activate Office 2016 Home and Business or any Office 365 bundle.

Next, after you do a system restore you normally have to at the very least re-activate Office. And you probably have to re-install it too.

Many/most computers come from the factory with a "free trial" of Office installed.  That trial may or may not be the version that was activated by your Office license.

So, first step, do you know the 25 character Office 2007 Product key (No, do not type it here, it is of no use to use and could be stolen by a "bad guy")?

Next step, do you have the installation disk/file for your specific Office 2007 bundle?

Next step on your computer, go into Control Panel > Programs and Features.  Look through the list for any Office installations.  I am guessing you have 2, one Office 2007 factory trial and the Office 365 you installed.  Uninstall the Office 365.  Look closely at the Office 2007 bundle name, Is it the same name as the bundle for your Office 2007 product key?  If not you will have uninstall.  If the bundle name is correct, do an Office Repair of the Office 2007 installation.  Then you can activate the Office 2007.
