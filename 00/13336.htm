here is the revision: 

Sub CreateUniqueList()
 Dim r As Range, lastrow As Long
 Sheets("DATA").Activate
 lastrow = Cells(Rows.Count, "A").End(xlUp).Row
     ActiveSheet.Range("A1:C" & lastrow).AdvancedFilter _
     Action:=xlFilterCopy, _
     CopyToRange:=Sheets("REPORT").Range("X1"), _
     UNIQUE:=True
 With Sheets("Report")
  Set r = .Range("X1", .Cells(Rows.Count, "X").End(xlUp)).Resize(, 3)
  r.Sort Key1:=r(1), Order1:=xlAscending, Header:=xlYes
 End With
 End Sub

Tested and worked for me.   Just be aware that when Advanced filter determines what rows are unique, it considers all three columns in making that determination.    ex:   A,B,C  will be different from A,B,D.  Hopefully that is what you want. 

Given that, if you want to sort on all three columns from left to right in precedence, then: 

Sub CreateUniqueList()
 Dim r As Range, lastrow As Long
 Sheets("DATA").Activate
 Sheets("Report").Range("X1").Resize(, 3).EntireColumn.ClearContents
 lastrow = Cells(Rows.Count, "A").End(xlUp).Row
     ActiveSheet.Range("A1:C" & lastrow).AdvancedFilter _
     Action:=xlFilterCopy, _
     CopyToRange:=Sheets("REPORT").Range("X1"), _
     UNIQUE:=True
 With Sheets("Report")
  Set r = .Range("X1", .Cells(Rows.Count, "X").End(xlUp)).Resize(, 3)
  r.Sort Key1:=r(1), Order1:=xlAscending, _
  Key2:=r(1).Offset(0, 1), Order2:=xlAscending, _
  Key3:=r(1).Offset(0, 2), Order3:=xlAscending, _
  Header:=xlYes
 End With
 End Sub
