ken sheridan say:

As Mimmo has pointed out, your design is fundamentally flawed.  By having multiple columns, one for each disability, you are doing what is known as 'encoding data as column headings'.  A fundamental principle of the database relational model is the Information Principle (Codd's Rule #1).  This requires that all data be stored as values at column positions in rows in tables, and in no other way.

You have a many-to-many relationship type between students and disabilities.  This should be modelled by a table which resolves the relationship type into two one-to-many relationship types:

StudentDisabilities
….StudentID  (FK)
….DisabilityID  (FK)

....Primary (Yes/No column)

The primary key of this table is a composite one of the two foreign keys, each of which reference the primary key of Students and Disabilities tables respectively.

For an example of a basic many-to-many relationship type like this take a look at StudentCourses.zip in my public databases folder at:

https://onedrive.live.com/?cid=44CC60D7FEA42912&id=44CC60D7FEA42912!169

If you have difficulty opening the link copy its text (NB, not the link location) and paste it into your browser's address bar.

This little demo file illustrates a number of possible interfaces, but that of a conventional form/subform is code free, and strongly recommended.

With a correctly structured model like this counting the total instances of each disability is a matter of a simple 'totals' query, grouped by disability and returning the count of rows per group.

raju s das say:

Hello,

Welcome to Microsoft Community and thank you for posting your query.

Let me assist you with Office repair.

Usually the repair process does not take too long to complete, it depends on the type of internet connection you have. 

Print spooler service may also cause setup\repair process to hang. You can temporarily stop the service, wait for the repair process to complete and then restart the service to fix the issue.

1. On a Windows 8 PC, type services.msc from the Start screen and tap or click services in the list of results.
On a Windows 7 PC, click Start > Run, type services.msc, and then click OK.

2. Look through the list of services, find Print Spooler, and open it.

3. Tap or click Stop, and then tap or click OK.

4. Try installing Office again.

5. Once Office is installed on your PC, go back to the list of services and open Print Spooler.

6. Tap or click Start, and then tap or click OK.

Note This issue may occur if you have an HP Office jet 4500 printer.

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/office/forum/office_2013_release-office_install/how-long-does-office-2013-online-repair-take/832bf3dd-d760-48f0-937e-539158c7d086
