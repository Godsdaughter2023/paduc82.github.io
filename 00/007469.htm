Question 1: Let's say i have 10 E3 users on 1 January and we pay annual by credit card. On 8 February one of the users leaves the company and we change the amount of licenses to 9. Will the annual bill and price say 9 or 10 E3 licenses?

The billing is calculated based on how many licenses you have and how many days you hold. So the answer is “9”.

Let me provide an example. If you purchase 10 E3 licenses on Jan 1, 2015, the billing will be created on Jan 2, 2015. If you didn’t change any licenses, the next billing will be created on Jan 2, 2016. If you reduce one license (from 10 to 9) on Feb 8, 2015, users should receive a new bill for the changed licenses on March 2, 2015. Next year, they will receive a new bill about total licenses on Jan 2, 2016.


Question 2:  And what about monthly payments? Is it the same?

The answer is yes. It is similar. However, for monthly billing, it will be created every month.

For example, if you purchase 10 E3 licenses on Jan 1, 2015, the billing will be created on Jan 2, 2015. If you didn’t change any licenses, the next billing will be created on Feb 2, 2015. If you reduce one license (from 10 to 9) on Feb 8, 2015, users will see the changes reflected on the next bill that is generated after the license change on Mar 2, 2015.


Question 3: if we have 10 licenses but only use 9, will we still be billed for 10 licenses?


The answer is yes. As I mentioned above, the billing is calculated based on how many licenses you have and how many days you hold.  It means you have 10 licenses and you hold them.

michaelmalloy- [o365] say:

Hello Craigar, I am assuming that you are working inside one single site collection. If this is the case then please consider the following:

  1. Any group you create either at the top level or any sub sites within the collection will ultimately live at the top level site.
  2. If you have sub sites which are inheriting permissions from the top level site then they are in fact inheriting all of the groups you created. Therefore, even though you created a security group within the "Technology" department...the group gets rolled up to the top level and then dispersed out to all sites of the top level which are inheriting permissions from that top level site.
  3. They way to alleviate this is to create all of your groups that you need like "Technology", "Facilities" and "HR" at the top level
  4. Now make sure you go to the permissions for each sub site and delete any unique permissions you may have created in your attempt at following Sky Pei's instructions. You can do this by going to each sub site and clicking on the Gear icon in the upper right portion of the screen >>>Shared with>>>Delete unique permissions. This will re-inherit things so that all of the groups you created will be inherited across all sub sites.
  5. Now all groups will have access to all sub sites. I know this isn't what you want but we are basically getting you zeroed out and re-calibrated for lack of a better term.
Now go to the "Technology" site. I assume you only want folks in the "Technology" group to be able to see the "Technology" sight. You can make this happen by using the following steps:
  1. Open the "Technology" site and access permissions by clicking the gear icon in the upper right corner and then choosing "Shared With"
  2. Now you should see that ALL of the groups you created have access by virtue of inheritance
  3. In the upper left click on "Stop Inheriting..."
  4. Now you will notice that all of the groups now have empty check boxes in front of them.
  5. Place check marks in the boxes for the security groups that SHOULD NOT have access and delete them
  • Now you can use the navigation settings to show sub sites and folks who don't have access won't see the other links to sites where they don't have access
  • You may want to get rid of the "Everyone" group when you delete security groups in step #5. If the everyone group still has access then everyone will still see the links to sites where they shouldn't have access.

> How can I see only the revisions I make (but all the revisions I make)?

On the Review tab, click the Show Markup button to drop down the menu, and click Specific People. Click the All Reviewers item in the secondary menu, which will turn off all the check marks in the menu (this temporarily hides all the revisions in the document, and also closes the menu). I believe a delete query can only delete records from one table, even if multiple tables are joined.  Unless the relationship has the "Cascade Deletes" attribute set, you'll need to use two queries in succession, deleting first from the child table and then from the parent table:

    DELETE FROM Reviews WHERE ProdID = "sq10123"

    DELETE FROM Products WHERE ProdID = "sq10123"

If the relatinship *does* have Cascade Deletes set, then you can just delete from the parent table -- Products, I assume -- and all the related child records will be deleted, too.
