dennisepley say: I have a DELL Latitude D830, that I have upgraded with an SSD, maxed out the memory, installed Windows Pro 7 and now have installed Windows 10 pro. My Intel 4965agn wireless adapter worked fine under Windows 7, not so much with Windows 10. Every time I started the machine from an off position (whether from sleep or a hard off) the network adapter would fail to start. I could get it to connect by running the troubleshooter (which said the adapter was not working properly). I spent 4 days of searching, trying to get any answers that would help from DELL. Microsoft, and Intel, to either resolve the problem or find a suitable replacement card. Somewhere in all that research I ran across a hint about denying Windows from shutting down the card to save power. Guess what. It works. Go to Control Panel, Device Manager, Network Adapters. Click on the Intel 4865AGN. Click on the Power Management Tab. UNCHECK the “Allow the computer to shut down…..”. Problem solved. Too bad neither Intel, DELL nor Microsoft will share this simple fix to a major headache.
