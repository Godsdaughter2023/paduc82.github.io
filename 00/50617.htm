To identify what loads when you boot use Autoruns (freeware from Microsoft).

http://www.microsoft.com/technet/sysinternals/ProcessesAndThreads/Autoruns.mspx

With Autoruns you can uncheck an item, which disables it from
starting,or you can right click an item and then delete it. If you
uncheck you can recheck to re-enable the item. It is a much safer
approach than editing the Registry and better than using msconfig..
Another useful feature of the programme is that you can right click an
item and select Search Online to get information about the item
selected.

Reinstalling Windows 10 wiped out my BIOS, what to do? Can I claim - Microsoft Community

ivicanesic say:

Reinstalling Windows 10 wiped out my BIOS, what to do? Can I claim my license?

Greetings community,

A little history about licensing: Before I gave it away, I had a computer which came with OEM Windows Vista Home Premium. When Windows 8 came out I bought upgrade disk, and leveled it up to Windows 8 Pro. Few years later they offered upgrade to Windows 10 for free, and being naïve I upgraded it. That was actually first time when I called local Microsoft support service, because I wanted a clean install. They told me that, considering that it was OEM OS, I had to upgrade it first, then download ISO and reinstall it again. When I asked which product number I had to use, they said I was to skip that part because product number would be saved in BIOS on motherboard during initial upgrade process.

Now, don’t get me wrong it worked pretty fine while it was with me, but one year ago I decided to cheer up myself with new machine, so I clean-reinstalled my old machine, with USB Windows 10 stick, that came with my new machine,  and gave it away to my neighbor’s kids.

Problem raised a month ago when my neighbor called me to reinstall unstable machine, because kids were being installing and uninstalling all sorts of games (probably pirated), and went to all sorts of websites (you know, boys will be boys).

So, I came and started the process. Everything went fine until the restart. After restart machine became unresponsive, with nothing on the screen, only coolers humming inside. Not having a better choice, I carried machine to the service.

Few days later man from service called me, and told me that he was puzzled. He couldn’t believe at first so he tested every component separately: memory, graphic card, hard disks, and he even pulled out the processor and slotted it in one old machine that was also here for service. He said he have never seen it in his life, it was about BIOS. Not that it was deleted, but what was inside was not a BIOS.

He introduced me to my options: he may try (no guaranties for success) to find BIOS for that old motherboard and push it inside using some tricks, but I will lose my license that way, if Microsoft doesn’t have it saved somewhere else. Other costlier option is to buy new motherboard (probably processor too, because it’s hard to find motherboard with LGA775), and of course new Windows. He said if I buy parts at their place, service is for free.

So here I am with perfectly good computer except for BIOS, and what I want to ask is this: is there any way that I can claim my license, if service people can implant new bios (or the old one from cd that came with it), or I have to buy new license anyway?

andre da costa say:
As noted, the BIOS and the Operating System are two separate things. Updating your BIOS should not be done on a whim unless required by the operating system. Updating the BIOS should not invalidate the operating system, but changing your motherboard can. In your case, you can refresh the BIOS without invalidating your license.
