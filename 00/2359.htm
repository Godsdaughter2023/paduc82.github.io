jay freedman say:

Are you asking about how to make new paragraphs while typing inside a content control, or how to repeat the clauses, or both?

For the paragraphs issue, notice that there are two kinds of "text" content controls: Rich Text and Plain. In a Rich Text control, the Enter key will start a new paragraph, just as in regular text; and almost all formatting (font changes, bold & italic, etc.) also work as in regular text.

To enable multiple paragraphs in a Plain Text control, you must click in the control and click the Properties button (to the right of the content controls in the Developer ribbon), and check the box at the bottom of the dialog:

If the number of distinct clauses that need to be repeated is less than about a half-dozen, then the method in Section 7 "Content Controls" of https://gregmaxey.com/word_tip_pages/repeating_data.html for repurposing the built-in document property content controls will work. (Note that of the 15 built-in controls, some are not text-entry controls and others are automatically filled by Word, so the number of usable controls is small.)

If you need more clauses, or if you don't want to risk users trying to use the built-ins under their original names and messing up the document, then download Greg's add-in from https://gregmaxey.com/word_tip_pages/cc_var_bm_doc_prop_tools_addin.html and use the tab labeled "Create/Manage Mapped Content Controls" to make as many text content controls as needed. (The add-in only has to be installed on the one PC where the template is being created.)

Current recommendations for minimizing junk mail in Outlook.com.
1. Sign in to Outlook.com.
2. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the Cog icon, and then click on
More mail settings.
3. Under Preventing junk mail, click Safe and blocked senders.
4. Under the Safe and blocked senders, click on Blocked senders.
5. In the Blocked senders list, add the name or domain (the part of an e-mail address that follows the @ sign, such as outlook.com) that you don't want to receive mail from, and then click Add to list >>.

You can create rules to delete incoming junk mails.
1. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the Cog icon, and then click on More mail settings.
2. Under Customizing Outlook, click Rules for sorting new messages.
3. Click New and then under Step 1, click the drop down arrow on the first box and choose Sender's address.
4. Under Step 1, type in on the third box the email address.
5. Under Step 2, choose Delete these messages—don't even put them in my Deleted folder.
6. Click Save at the bottom of the page.

You may also set your Junk email filter to Exclusive.
If the Exclusive level was set, messages from senders not on your Contacts or Safe senders lists will be sent to the Junk folder.
By default, the Junk folder will automatically delete messages after 10 days.
This is more convenient as we don't have any control if senders are randomly changing their address.

charles kenyon say:
Elaborating on the excellent responses you have received.

"Normal.dot" is not a system file for Word 2007. It is not activated, on its own, when in the user templates folder. If there is a normal.dot file in the user templates folder but no normal.dotm, Word 2007 will use the existing normal.dot as the basis for a new normal.dotm. (This may put Word 2007 into "compatibility mode.")

Any template containing macros (whether .dot or .dotm) placed in Word's Startup Folder will share the macros with all open documents and templates. When placed there it will be listed among the Active Add-Ins. Global Templates

This is a good temporary solution. It may be a perfect solution as far as macros that do not need frequent tweaking. However, normal.dot carries a lot of excess baggage that can slow your system down when added as an Add-In. People have complained that it slows Word's startup down.

As with other Add-In templates, macros in normal,dot loaded as an Add-In are not available in the Visual Basic Editor (VBE) unless the template is actually open, not just "loaded as an Add-In."

You can copy macros individually using the VBE from normal.dot to normal.dotm (assuming you have normal.dot open as a document). You can also copy them using the Organizer, but this only copies entire modules, not individual macros.

If you have customized Styles in your normal.dot, you can copy those to normal.dotm using the Organizer. Styles in Global templates are not available in other documents without macro support. A Global StyleSheet in Microsoft Word?

In Word 2007, use the File > Open dialog to open the Normal.dot (which I assume is still in the Startup folder). Yes, it will appear as a blank page, but it isn't the same blank page you get when you just start Word and get Document1. The title bar should say Normal.dot [Compatibility Mode].

Now go to the VBE. You should see two icons named "Normal" in the Project pane. One of them (it should be the first one) is the Normal.dotm template that belongs to Word 2007, and the other one is the Normal.dot template that you just opened.

Now you can open a module under the project for Normal.dot, copy the macros from it, and paste them into a module in the project for Normal.dotm. After each paste, press Ctrl+S or click the Save button on the VBE's toolbar, which will save the Normal.dotm template.

All of this will be easier if you rename the Normal.dot in the Startup folder to something else, such as MyMacros.dot. (Word has to be closed when you start to do this.) That way there will be less confusion about which project is which, and where you are in the copy/paste cycle.
