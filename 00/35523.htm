    1slyfox say: I posted this fix in another thread.  It may not be the fix for everyone, but it is so weird that most people wouldn't think of it.  Try this...

    1.  Open up msconfig.exe (you can do this by typing it in search programs and files).  It will make you enter the Admin password to open it.

    2.  Click on the boot tab.

    3.  Click on the Advanced Options button.

    4.  Ensure the number of processors listed matches what your machine actually has.


    I have an AMD 3 core processor, but the number of processors was somehow changed to 0.  After changing it back to 3 and rebooting, I haven’t had any mouse stutter problems.
