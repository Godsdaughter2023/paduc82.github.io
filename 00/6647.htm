stephen-s say:

Hi PD,

I'm no expert but this solution worked for me.  The problem with configuring an Outlook.com email account on an iOS7 device (eg iPhone) is that iOS7 will assume you want it configured as an IMAP account & will not give you the option to configure it as POP.  To achieve this you need to configure a POP email account & then change the details to the POP details for the Outlook.com account.

I was able to set up an ISP email account (but it might work for any non-web based email account, that is NOT Outlook, Google, Yahoo, AOL, etc).  iOS7 gave me the option to select IMAP or POP, so I selected POP.  I then changed all the details (including the Outlook.com email address & password) to:

Incoming mail server                                        pop3.live.com

Incoming mail server port                                995 (SSL required)

Outgoing (SMTP) mail server                          smtp.live.com

Outgoing (SMTP) mail server port                   587 (SSL/TLS required)

Outgoing server (SMTP) authentication         yes, same settings as my incoming mail server

Just make sure your Outlook.com account is configured to allow POP & not delete downloaded messages off the server.  This can be found under the Options section under the Options icon (little cog) on the right hand side of the screen.  Mine were set correctly to begin with, but it would pay to check.

I hope this works for you as it did the trick for me.

The idea that you should not use POP3 is not a well-informed one and this advice should be disregarded.  Let me explain a thing or two about email:

POP3 accounts are typically allotted very, very cheaply by service providers like GoDaddy, for example whereby you are give 100 with many of their hosting plans which is a great deal if you understand email and the cost implications over using IMAP.

Most people have both mobile device and a computer.  As long as you set up your laptop to physically download email from the server, you will not normally exceed the small allocation that you would normally allot to each of these POP3 email accounts, you can save massive amounts of money over IMAP which keeps a copy on the mail server unless you specifically request that download the email.

POP 3 is just as good as IMAP and much, much more economical in the vast, vast majority of the cases.

Furthermore:

Additional Notes: - POP3 Mail Service (Strongly Recommended)

(FYI: POP3 is the method automatically used by your email service provider when automatically setting up your email account as it is the most economical.

 

The POP3 incoming server email method also has other advantages over IMAP in that it automatically downloads a physical copy of each email to your machine when you check your email, and does not require a connection to the Internet to review those emails that you have received should you need to review them in the future).  For your Laptop/Desktop (As opposed to your phone!), find the setting to “Download Messages from Server” and set it to “ON/YES” so that space is freed up on the server when you check your messages from your laptop or desktop, whichever the case may be!

 

IMAP service does NOT automatically download physical copies of your received email to your local computer, tablet, laptop, etc.  Instead it downloads ONLY a summary header.  Consequently, Unless you lose access to your received email if you someday lose access to the email account through which you received the email.

 

IMAP service also requires much more space on your email server than POP3 as the mail is kept on the server instead of being downloaded to your machine; hence, IMAP service tends to be more costly than POP3 service.

Please get your facts straight before dispensing device to beginners as this is a way of dispensing nonsense to novice users who go around believing (and spreading) the same nonsense.
