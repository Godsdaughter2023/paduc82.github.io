vincentchoy say:

To directly answer your question - 

My suggestion is you subscribe to a tool like Dmarcian, that can help you check if your SPF, DKIM and DMARC are set properly. If offers a 30 day trial so you can start going immediately.

For a start, I would suggest your dmarc policy be set as p=0 and rua be sent to Dmarcian, so you can analyze if legitimate mails are being rejected. 

Additional advice -

For SPF, you need to specify all legitimate senders of your domains, not just Office365. If you don't - any unspecified senders will be considered impersonation and may be marked as spoof. eg Mailchimp sending mail on your behalf. You may also want to find out how to set SPF with your smarthost vendor when it is used together with Office365

For DKIM, Office365 has its own way of implementing dkim, via CNAME. The links provided should get you on your way. A word of caution, is DKIM checks if mails are modified after leaving Office365. If you have additional services that modify email after it leaves Office365 (such as a disclaimer service), then DKIM that is set at Office365 will fail. You mentioned a smarthost providing you a DKIM value - that needs looking into. You may want to check with your smarthost vendor how to implement DKIM when your smarthost is used together with Office365. 

For DMARC, use dmarcian to help you phase your DMARC. Set p=0 for monitor mode, and have aggregate reports (rua) send to dmarcian. By setting p=0, at least your mails wont be rejected as spoof by recipient anti-spam servers.

jay freedman say:
The widow/orphan control option is on or off in each individual paragraph style, not in Word as a whole. Also, the same-named style in two different documents can be set differently, and one style can override the other when you paste between documents or insert one document into another. There are lots of permutations in there.

The only certain solution is to make sure that the option is turned on in the style applied to the paragraph in each of the small source files and to the paragraph containing the bookmark in the template. (Ideally, the styles should be exactly the same in all of the documents and the template, to avoid any other gotchas.)

vitallyr say:

I had the same problem on my E66, and read reports about it on E71 after upgrading to 400.21.013.

 

The problem is caused by restoring Settings from a backup. None of the data connections (GPRS and WLAN) work at all after restoring settings. You get odd error messages like "WLAN Connection already in use, close it and try again" or just "Connection not available".

If you don't restore the Settings from a backup, no problems!

 

To solve you problem now, just reload the same firmware (400.21.013) and do not restore Settings from backup.
