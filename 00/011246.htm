Below are the possible reasons on why your emails are deleted/missing in your account: 

  1. Messages were manually deleted either due to accidental deletion or were moved or deleted due to your account filter settings.

This often happens when using the POP3 protocol to retrieve your mail and the option “Always leave a copy of the message on the server” is not selected. There are instances when the software configuration causes false positives causing valid messages to be either sent to the junk folder or deleted altogether.

  5. The account was marked as “Inactive". Outlook.com accounts become inactive if you don’t sign in for more than 365 days or within 10 days after signing up for an account. Mail and contacts in Outlook.com accounts will be purged after 365 days of inactivity.  Any existing mail cannot be retrieved from the accounts.

paulcordts say: You need to have a target of some kind established in order to do a countdown.  Either a start time or projected completion.

When you enter 5 hrs 20 minutes, is that 5 hrs 20 minutes from the moment you enter the data or from some start time.  When you use a egg timer the start time is when you start it.  

In Excel the NOW() function will give you a time stamp, but when left as a function the values changes.
That is to say if you entered the formula =NOW() in A1 at 2:15 the value would be 2:15, but if you check in 5 minutes =NOW() would be 2:20.

If you establish a set start time (either by entering it or using=NOW() and copy pasting as a value) you can then add your expected duration to get an expected completed time.

Then the expected completion time-NOW() could be your egg timer

ryukurai say:

The Nokia 5130C can use any size card,up to 16GB, but 8GB and 16GB cards will take longer to load the music files. Not because of the capacity itself, but the number of file names that need to be loaded into the Music Player memory (the 30MB of RAM the phone is equipped with). It can take between 1 to 2 Seconds per song, so 1400 songs will take up to 14 Minutes to load.
