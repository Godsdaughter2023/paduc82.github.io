gord dibben say:

Sub TRIM_EXTRA_SPACES()
Dim Cell As Range
    For Each Cell In ActiveSheet.UsedRange    'Selection

                If (Not IsEmpty(Cell)) And _
                Not Application.IsNumber(Cell.Value) And _
                InStr(Cell.Formula, "=") = 0 _
                Then Cell.Value = Application.Trim(Cell.Value)
    Next
End Sub

hal hostetler - mvp-outlook say:
Are you using an Anti Virus/Spam application that includes an email scanner component?  By the fact that a new email reaches the Sent folder, Outlook believes it has successfully completed the send operation with your outgoing email server.  One item that could allow a successful send transaction, but still not allow the email to be sent is an outgoing email scanner. These sit in between your email client and your ISP's SMTP server.  They first pretend to be your ISP's server so they can intercept your outgoing email to scan it, then they pretend to be Outlook (or whatever your email client is) to complete the send process with the SMTP server.  By nature of the way they must work, they are trouble prone.

Of all the email scanners, Norton's is arguably THE most trouble prone of them all.  No Norton settings changed, but you added a "failed account" then removed it, which, it very much sounds like, caused Norton to break.  You do NOT need an email scanner over and above the resident file system scanner that all A/V applications provide.  They are totally redundant, waste time, and cause issues like this.  The best solution, if the Norton installer allows it, is to uninstall the whole suite, then do a custom re-install WITHOUT the email scanning components.  If the installer no longer allows custom installs, then the scanner components should be disabled including any Outlook Add-In.  Here's an older FAQ from Norton, themselves:
