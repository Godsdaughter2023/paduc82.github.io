    When you tried "Detect & Repair", did it indicate that it found & fixed *any* problems?

    I think that a better solution at this point would be to delete the partnership on both devices and start again.   This will delete the various items on your phone that originated from your PC, and delete the various items on the PC that originated from your phone.   The original items on the original device will not be harmed.

    You can accomplish this by:
    1.) Go into ActiveSync on the device Menu > Options, highlight PC name and click Delete button.
    2.) Open ActiveSync on the PC, click on File > Delete Mobile Device.
    3.) Reboot your computer, and perform a soft-reset for your device.

    Since you didn't mention what your device is, then you will need to consult your device documentation on how to do a "Soft-Reset".   Alternately, you can remove your device's battery for 15 seconds, then replace.  This will force a Soft Reset.

    4.) After both systems are back up and working, then go ahead and reconnect the USB cable and start a new "Partnership".
    5.) When selecting what items to sync (calendar, contacts, etc) don't select all at once.  Place a checkmark next to one and then sync.  After a succesful sync, open the options and select the next item to sync and place a checkmark next to it, then sync.  Rinse and repeat until all items are synchronized...

    FYI: PIM is short for Personal Information Manager.   VOL is the extension of the filename, and is short for Volume, like a volume from an encyclopedia set.
