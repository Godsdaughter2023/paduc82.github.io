shane devenshire say:

I've just run a test in 2010 and you still can't link the smartart to the sheet.  And my initial test suggests you can't do it via VBA.

However, you can link the contests of cells to Shapes (not smart art) and you can apply a lot of the nifty formatting to those shapes, and you can organize them to look like smart art.

Choose Insert, Shape, and pack a shape. Drag in the spreadsheet to make the shape.  Click the border of the shape, then click on the Formula Bar and type = finally click on the cell you want to link to the shape.

This works in all versions of Excel.

Since these are absolute cell references and your pivot table may change, you can write a GETPIVOTDATA formula outside the pivot table which adjusts as the pivot table changes, and you link to this cell rather than directly into the pivot table.
