Page Formatting Breaks - Microsoft Community

strategisgroup say:

Page Formatting Breaks

In the draft view mode in Word 2007 there appears single dotted lines that affect the page layout. How do I get rid of these? And how are they introduced into the document?

sharonmary say:

Hi StrategisGroup,

The single dotted line indicated an automatic page break (i.e. one that you didn't add manually) and these are introduced into the document when your text reaches the bottom margin.

You cannot get rid of automatic page breaks (although they do disappear if you add a manual one of course).

If you print the document a new page will start immediately after the dotted line. If that doesn't suit you, and that is not where you want a new printed page to start, then you can place your insertion point where you want a new page to start and then press <CTRL>+<ENTER>. The indicator will change slightly from a single dotted line to:

-----------Page Break ---------

When you see the above, that indicated a forced page break which you *can* delete.

I hope that answers your question? Just come back if you have more.  :-)

All the best,

Sharon

I added a loop thru the workbook worksheets and an error handler.

Sub Button4_Click()
'revised 08/17/2016
On Error GoTo Shultz
   
Const strFolder = "E:\INSPECTION\ABC Charts\"
Dim wbk As Workbook
Dim wks As Excel.Worksheet
Dim strFile As String

Application.ScreenUpdating = False
Application.DisplayAlerts = False
 
strFile = Dir(strFolder & "*.xls*")
Do While strFile <> ""
  Set wbk = Workbooks.Open(Filename:=strFolder & strFile)
  For Each wks In wbk.Worksheets
    wks.Cells.Replace What:="X405/*", Replacement:="X405/H", LookAt:=xlPart, _
        SearchOrder:=xlByRows, MatchCase:=False, SearchFormat:=False, ReplaceFormat:=False
    wks.Cells.Replace What:="X3009*", Replacement:="X3009 REV W", LookAt:=xlPart, _
        SearchOrder:=xlByRows, MatchCase:=False, SearchFormat:=False, ReplaceFormat:=False
  Next 'wks
 
  wbk.Close savechanges:=True
  strFile = Dir
Loop

End Sub

Current recommendations for minimizing junk mail in Outlook.com.
1. Sign in to Outlook.com.
2. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the Cog icon, and then click on
More mail settings.
3. Under Preventing junk mail, click Safe and blocked senders.
4. Under the Safe and blocked senders, click on Blocked senders.
5. In the Blocked senders list, add the name or domain (the part of an e-mail address that follows the @ sign, such as outlook.com) that you don't want to receive mail from, and then click Add to list >>.

You can create rules to delete incoming junk mails.
1. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the Cog icon, and then click on More mail settings.
2. Under Customizing Outlook, click Rules for sorting new messages.
3. Click New and then under Step 1, click the drop down arrow on the first box and choose Sender's address.
4. Under Step 1, type in on the third box the email address.
5. Under Step 2, choose Delete these messages—don't even put them in my Deleted folder.
6. Click Save at the bottom of the page.

You may also set your Junk email filter to Exclusive.
If the Exclusive level was set, messages from senders not on your Contacts or Safe senders lists will be sent to the Junk folder.
By default, the Junk folder will automatically delete messages after 10 days.
This is more convenient as we don't have any control if senders are randomly changing their address.
