I have a worksheet with rows of data, the last one being a drop-down box with three options. So, 1 mail worksheet with everything, then 3 additional spreadsheets, 1 for each of the three reasons. How do I do this?
max, singapore say:

Here's a simple non-array formulas play which should deliver the automation you desire
Assume your source data is in cols A to C in Sheet1, data running in row 2 down, 
where the key col is say, col B

In another sheet,
Criteria
Assume your DV containing the selectable options (for the source key col B) is in A2
Put in B2: =IF($A$2="","",IF(Sheet1!B2=$A$2,ROW(),""))
Copy down to cover max expected extent of source data, eg down to row 500? Minimize col B

Extraction
In C2:
=IF(ROWS($1:1)>COUNT($B:$B),"",INDEX(Sheet1!A:A,SMALL($B:$B,ROWS($1:1))))
Copy B2 across to D2, fill down by the smallest range large enough to cover the max extent of results, eg down to row 100 will do? Cols B to D will return the required results, ie only the source lines satisfying the specified criteria (ie the DV selection in A2), with all lines neatly packed at the top. You could then dress up this sheet to taste and make as many copies of it as required to cater for extracts based on the other DV selections
