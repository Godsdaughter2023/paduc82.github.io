Excel stores the formulas always in English inside the files, regardless of the locale language.

You can show the formulas in English and your locale language very easy, the Range object properties for that:

Sub Test()
  MsgBox ActiveCell.Formula, , "English"
  MsgBox ActiveCell.FormulaLocal, , "Local Language"
End Sub

But it is not possible to translate the formulas into a different language, e.g. if you only have English and you want Czech.

For that you have to convert the formula by yourself (with VBA) before you show it anywhere using a "translation table and code". A short look shows that such tables can be found on the web, e.g.

To write a "translation code" is not hard, a simple VBA.Replace will do it. Sort the table by the length of the name descending, means the longest name is at the top.
