What you want is not possible using the shape tool.

  1. When I open an epub file on my e-reader, the first thing I do is adapt the font size to my likings. It is unlikely that your bounding box will fit my choosen font size.
  2. Depending on the properties of the e-reader documents are displayed differently (reflown). Although things will depend on resolution and font sizes, you can easily imagine that a 10 inch reader will have more words on one line than a 6 inch one. As you have no anchor to link your box to a specific word, your box could end up anywhere on your text and not necessarely around the word you had in mind.

What you want is not possible using tables. An epub is nothing more than an xhtml document with some fancy stuff around it. Positioning a one cell table in the middle of a text is a nightmare. And even if you would succeed to get that layout working, a document reflow would once again make that layout disappear.

What you want is theoretically possible using the CSS3 text-outline property. Unfortunately, there are not yet any e-readers which support CSS3 as far as I know.

Have you considered using the highlight tool? Highlights can be stored in xhtml using a span tag with a CSS background-color property. Of course then the whole word will have a colored background rather than just a box around it. But this should act nicely with all e-readers which support CSS1.
