el_loco---01 say: Current drawbacks of OLED vs. TFT

* Life time. OLED will last fewer hours than TFT so in theory they don't last as much. Of course this will improve with each generation of OLED screens, but this is the fact.

* Visibility in sunlight or bright environments. As mentioned above the OLED screen is not as good when outside in the sun or very bright environment (like in a car on a sunny day)

* Price. It's more expensive than TFT of the same size and rez.

Pros

* Lower power. The main reason for this is that OLED can reproduce Black color by shutting off pixels thus dark or black parts of the screen consume no power. A dark theme consumes less power in an OLED than TFT as TFT still has to power the pixels when black.

* Better color saturation. Colors look more vibrant and saturated.

* Better contrast and black levels.

Yes, that's the way it works.  You are proceeding correctly in not using the product key for the temporary mobo.  You won't be able to do a few things with an un-activated copy but that is normal.  What will happen when you install your permanent mobo is that you will go to the Windows Update page in Settings and click Activation in the left side.  Follow the instructions to enter your retail product key and the licensing software will register the mobo's hardware identifiers with Microsoft and your computer will become a registered Windows 10 device (permanent) and activate Windows.  

If you need to reinstall Windows at some future time do not enter a product key in the request page.  Just do as you have done once already and click the "I do not have a product key" link.  Since the hardware identifiers will already be registered with Microsoft, Windows will simply activate the first time the computer connects to the internet following the re-installation.  Just make sure it is the same edition as before.
