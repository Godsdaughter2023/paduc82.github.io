To restart the numbering you need to insert at the end of page 2 a Next Page Section Break. Then with your cursor in the footer area of Page 3 go to the Header and Footer tab and uncheck the box labeled Unlink to Previous, this separates the footers and you can now setup section two's footer to be distinct from section one.

Delete the page number field in section two's footer and use the Insert > Page Number from the Menu at the top of your screen and not the one on the Ribbon to insert a new page number. Before clicking Ok, go to the Format button on the Page Numbers dialog and click the button to restart numbering.

hpholm say:

Thanks for the feedback.

Others reported similar issues, so the Microsoft developers must be aware now.

Based on the experience in the post by Adam.E earlier in this subject, I suggest backup any important messages to local storage. There is a Windows 10 PC app Windows Phone Message Backup that may be useful for this purpose.

Regarding the contacts, all I can suggest is clear everything from people.live.com, then sync all devices empty, switch off all devices, restore the contacts list to the people.live.com website, and carefully inspect all entries for formatting errors before syncing the devices again.
Extra space in front of names and numbers like " Microsoft" instead of "Microsoft", brackets and dashes in phone numbers (the preferred format is like +1212345678 in one string), and strange characters anywhere, have previously caused other issues.

Contacts can be exported from people.live.com from 'Manage' in the top bar, and the .CSV file can be edited in e.g. Notepad or Excel. This should make even thousands of contacts relatively manageable. The charset is Unicode (UTF).
It is advisable to secure a local copy of the .CSV file from people.live.com before syncing the devices again.

All this should of course simply work out of the box. The retired quality assurance person inside me cannot understand why nobody takes charge.

Hans
