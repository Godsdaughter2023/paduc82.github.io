a. user say:

Hello Don,

I understand the inconvenience you have experienced.

Occasionally, Windows might not read your user profile correctly, such as if your antivirus software is scanning your computer while you try to log on or might be some other cause. In that case, restarting your computer and logging on with your user account again will mostly resolve the issue.

If you restart your computer and it does not resolve this issue, then you can only fix the issue by doing those registry fix. Modifying those duplicate profile list registry entries will take you back to User Profile correctly. Hence I have suggested you to try that.
