Sync contacts and Calendars between a Mac and Windows Phone - Microsoft Community

kosh---01 say:

Sync contacts and Calendars between a Mac and Windows Phone

Technical Level : Intermediate

Summary

Details

This post covers the procedure to sync your Mac calendar (iCal) and contacts (Address Book) with a Lumia family Windows Phone.

 

To sync your iCal calendar  and Mac address book with your Lumia you will have to create a link to any EAS(Exchange Active Sync) service like gmail or Hotmail. If you use just one calendar the easiest would be to sync to a Google calendar. If you use multiple calendars you will have to subscribe to them from your Microsoft account in order for all of them to sync to and from your Lumia.

 

Here is what to do:

  • Sign on to Google Calendar with your Google/gmail account. Create one if required.
  • Click the down arrow next to the calendar you want to share on the left side of the browser window in the 'My Calendars' box and select 'Share this calendar'.
  • Click the green 'iCal' button next to 'Private Address' on the Google Calendar Details page.
  • Copy the URL (web address) on the pop-up window. This is your private calendar address and shouldn't be shared with anyone else since it shows all your events on Google Calendar.
  • Launch iCal in Mac OSX from your Dock, select 'Calendar' from the top menu and click on the 'Subscribe' option. Paste (control-V) the URL you copied into the 'Subscribe to:' field on the window that pops up and hit the 'Subscribe' button.
  • Type in the name of the calendar in the 'Name' field of the information window that pops up after you subscribed to your Google Calendar. You can also type in a description just below the 'Name' field. Click the check boxes next to 'Alarms,' 'Attachments,' and 'To Do items' if you don't want to import those things from Google Calendar. Also select a time interval from the 'Auto-refresh' drop-down box. This will tell iCal to refresh Google Calendar according to the time interval you selected.
  • Hit the 'OK' button. Your Google Calendar is now listed underneath 'Subscriptions' in the left pane of iCal's window.

 

For Mac addressbook:

  • Launch Address Book
  • Click on the “Address Book” menu and select “Preferences”
  • Click on the “Accounts” tab
  • Select the “On My Mac” under the left accounts
  • Check the checkbox accompanying “Synchronize with Google”
  • Click on “Configure…”
  • Enter your Google Account login, click “OK”
  • Then click “Sync Now” from the menubar

 

Now create a Google account on your Lumia in Settings>email+accounts>add account 'Google' and you are all set.

 

 

If you have additional calendars on your Mac you'd like to sync you need to make Google calendars your 'main' calendar app and move all your calendars to Google Calendar;

 

  • Export each calendar to an .ics file on your Mac. Import this file in Google calendar as a new calendar.
  • Flag each calendar under the dav preferences and cancel them on your Mac.
  • Subscribe to each calendar in http://calendar.live.com. Login with your Microsoft account
  • Use the private iCal URL from Google Calendar to subscribe.
  • When you sync your Microsoft account next on the Lumia all your calendars will be available on the phone.

If you choose to subscribe to all Google calendars on your Microsoft account make sure you deselect the option to sync calendars in the Google account settings on the Lumia.

 

 

If you have any further questions, feel free to ask them in the forum.

Kosh

kenneth l mantey say:

Are Full Scan Reports archived?

I just ran a full scan.  It took over 6 hrs and I wanted to keep a log of what it found, how many files were scanned and what was done, but I couldn't see where to do that.  Are previous scan reports archived, or Is there a log kept somewhere?
greginmich say:

The MpCmdRun.log file is a very readable resource for reviewing scheduled scans, updates and other events where the Command Line Utility is invoked by MSE itself.

 

C:\Windows\ServiceProfiles\NetworkService\AppData\Local\Temp\MpCmdRun.log

 

However, this is an “internal events” log, which doesn’t include the results of manual scans (“Scan now” scans), context-menu scans, or command-line scans, and the MpCmdRun.log file doesn’t contain the wealth of information that’s available in the System Event Log:

 

1.) Open Event Viewer by typing “eventvwr” (or just “event”) in the search box and hitting the “Enter” key.

 

2.) Expand “Windows Logs” and click on the “System” folder.

 

3.) Click on the “Filter Current Log…” option in the “Actions” pane or “Action” menu.

 

4.) Select “Microsoft Antimalware” as the Event source and enter the relevant Event IDs from the list below, separated by a comma.

 

5.) Click on “Save Filter to Custom View” and name the Custom View.

 

The next time you open Event Viewer, simply expand “Custom Views” and click on the desired Custom View.

 

Partial list of MSE System Events:

 

1000 – Scan started

1001 – Scan completed

1002 – Scan stopped (canceled)

1005 – Scan terminated due to error

1011 – Item deleted from quarantine

1013 – History removed

1116 – Malware detection

1117 – Malware remediation

1118 – Malware remediation error (non-critical) [not confirmed]

1119 – Malware remediation error (critical)

2000 – Successful update

2001 – Failed update

2002 – Engine update

2010 – Dynamic Signature Service retrieved additional signatures

2011 – Dynamic Signature Service discarded obsolete signatures

3002 – Real-time protection failure: behavior monitoring

5000 – Real-time protection enabled

5001 – Real-time protection disabled

5004 – Real-time protection configuration changed

5007 – Configuration changed

 

Windows 7 also gives us the option to save a filtered log as a text file (“Save Filtered Log File As…” or “Save All Events in Cust…”).

tom van stiphout say:

This is too big of a topic for this forum, which works best for precise questions about a specific narrow topic.

A few points:

* You cannot store CC information in an Access database. It is not PCI compliant and you may get on the CC companies' bad side. This will not end well for you.

* If you study the documentation for PayPal (and I'm sure other payment gateways), you will learn that you can setup monthly recurring payments. However while this can be initiated from Access (using XMLHTTP library), it cannot be managed by it, since the payment gateways will "call you back" on an endpoint to report status. This requires a web application.

If I had to implement this, my first instinct would be to create a shopping cart website. Maybe it only has one product: monthly subscription to the XYZ magazine. Then have the site handle the interactions with the payment gateway. Nopcommerce is one of my favorites, and it has this built-in. Woocommerce (a popular plugin for WordPress) is another strong contender.

Microsoft Word 2016 Freezes Randomly - Microsoft Community

a. user say:

Microsoft Word 2016 Freezes Randomly

I have Microsoft Word 2016 through Office 365, on a Windows 10 Pro (Latest Release Build) 64-bit

It will occur either when I'm press Ctrl+S to save a document, or randomly when I am trying to navigate elsewhere to type (by clicking on a word to place the typing line). I have restarted my computer, and used the office repair tool. I have to use Task Manager in order to end the task, but when I go to the document I closed, generally it will be saved just fine, so it is more of an annoyance than a devastating blow to productivity.

Edit:

I seemed to have resolved the issue.

Because freezing usually ocurred whenever I tried to save the document, I figured it has something to do with the network (either up or down and the apparatuses that enable them). I created two separate documents, put them under the same load, and originated them separately to either my local PC (non-backup) or to my OneDrive (backup, where I save everything because I have 1 TB). The document consistently slowed or froze only when saved to OneDrive (despite my internet testing upload and download far exceeding the mere couple dozen kilobytes my average Word document is). A quick search of OneDrive troubleshooting pointed to a sync conflict between Office trying to save it and OneDrive, and by going to the OneDrive settings for Office and unchecking "Use Office 2016 to sync files..." has had an immediate turn around positively.
