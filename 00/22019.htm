“sam” say:
Hi Jordan,

Hold the CTRL Key on your keyboard down and Press A this will highlight all the emails that are for example in your Junk folder. Then while holding the Shift Key down and press the Delete key on your keyboard. There will be a pop up say yes to permanently delete emails. Do not leave the folder you have just deleted emails from until the emails have been deleted. Give it a couple of minutes. Then do the same to the other folders. If you do not use shift then all emails deleted will go into your recover deleted Items folder.
I also like to filter my inbox by size to make sure there are not emails with large attachments that I can save for later viewing. Click filter All Checked then More Sort by size. Save attachments if you want to keep them before deleting emails. Again hold the Shift Key then press the Delete key.
Once completed the Deleted Items folder look in that folder you'll find Recover Items Deleted from this folder check and delete those if not needed. You can shift a or Ctrl and click on individual emails as long as your holding the Ctrl key when clicking the will highlight. I would then do the Shift Delete to remove these emails.
Clear your browser cookies CTRL SHIFT AND DELETE will bring up the box to delete cache and cookies. A restart of your computer never hurt. If still having a problem try deleting in a Private window.
Chrome and Opera Browser -Open a browser window hold the CTRL & SHIFT Keys and press the N key to open a private (Incognito) Window
Edge Internet Explorer and Firefox-Open a browser window hold the CTRL & SHIFT Keys and press the N key to open a private window

Do the delete from there after clearing your Cache.
Please let me know how it goes the biggest problem is when you change folders before the deletion has completed. Give it time!

Regards,
Sam
