cmdrkeene say:

Did you perhaps create a Microsoft Account using the roadrunner email address as the username?  For example, are you able to login to this answers community website with your roadrunner email address?  Another way you can tell is if you open PC Settings app, under Accounts section, and see if it lists your roadrunner address there.  If so, that's a Microsoft Account.

You can disconnect that and re-link that to your new address, but that would be creating another brand new Microsoft Account, using your gmail address as the username.  However I don't recommend that.  For one thing, it means you lose your entire Microsoft Account history, which is important if you want the benefits it provides:

  • Backups you already have from your Windows 8's automatic profile and settings backups
  • Files stored online in OneDrive
  • Encryption Recovery Key backups stored in your account
  • Purchases made under your account, such as music, movies, apps, or services like Office
  • Purchase history and verification of Office, Windows purchase receipt, etc.

Instead of creating a brand new account, I would instead recommend changing your current one to use the Gmail address instead of the RoadRunner one.  Here's how to do this:

  1. Login to https://account.live.com/names/Manage

  2. To confirm you're making changes to the right Microsoft Account, make sure the page lists your roadrunner account (it will probably be marked as the "primary alias" of the account).

  3. Click the Add email alias link.


  4. Choose the second option: "Add an existing email address as a Microsoft account alias" and type your gmail email address.

Once this is done, you can login to Microsoft systems and services using your gmail email address as the username (your Microsoft Account password won't change, and all your services remain working just like they always have).  If you want you can even change the primary alias and then delete the roadrunner one entirely without actually impacting the account.

Things are never simple are they.
