chriso747 say:

Re:  your other matters with FSX.    I don't know your computer specs (CPU, Graphics, RAM, etc.), but if you're satisfied that they are adequate for running FSX, then I would look elsewhere.   

First, defragment your drive, particularly FSX.   Make sure that unnecessary services are not running or not scheduled to run while in FSX.   How many add ons do you use, particularly with detailed aircraft, scenery, and scenery enhancements?   Set your FPS limit to max of 30.   Setting to 24 for starters could be useful.   Running at Unlimited requires a lot from your CPU, cycles which the CPU will need to use to pre-render.   I use Unlimited only when landing at high detailed airports to help with the sudden frame loss on Short Final.

Go to your FSX settings and set most of them back to lower levels (maybe even default).   Fly a default aircraft with the new settings (the graphics may not look good but it may be helpful to find out why FSX is taking a long time to load and for the pauses in-flight).   If it does run better, then slowly increase setting's levels, mainly the 'must haves' you require.   Remember, the FPS killers are Traffic, Clouds, Autogen (the lower you can go with these, the better).

Even those simmers with high end machines need to lower their settings to run FSX 'acceptably', so compromises have to be made.   Anyway, you may be well versed in the above, but just pass it on to you to double check through them.

And, the driver update mentioned earlier applied to my GTX 580 - don't know what the update is for your Card.
