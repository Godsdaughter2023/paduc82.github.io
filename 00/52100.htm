klokkwork say:

If you go on your console to Settings --> Account --> Xbox Live Gold, the item line "Member Since" indicates the date your Gold cycle actually started, regardless of when you paid for it.  Bear in mind that payment for an automatically renewed Gold membership doesn't necessarily happen on the same day it renews, but on the billing date of the account, which will always be somewhere within 30 days of the renewal.  This billing date is based on the date the account was created.

Thus, if you created your account on March 3rd 2011, and you bought a 1-month Gold membership on November 17th 2013, you'd pay the $9.99 on November 17th 2013, and next month, on December 17th, your Gold would renew, but you wouldn't be charged for that until January 3rd 2014, because the 3rd of the month is the account's billing date (thanks to creating the account on March 3rd).  Every month thereafter, the account would renew on the 17th of the month, and bill on the 3rd of the next month.  Now, down the road, say July of 2014, let's say you've forgotten the date you actually bought your Gold, you're just looking at your bank statement saying you're billed on the 3rd of every month, and on July 8th, you cancel your auto-renewal.  Your Gold then runs out on July 17th, because that's the end of the cycle, but if you're only looking at your bank statement, it gives the appearance that you were cut off early.

This difference between the billing date and the cycle date can catch one off guard sometimes, and make one think that something is happening ahead of or behind schedule, when in reality, it's not.  I'm not certain that the 12-Month Gold mentioned by the OP is experiencing this very issue, but it's a possibility.  I've never known Gold to cut out early, unless there was a problem with the billing, but if that's the case, it happens within a couple months of the renewal, not just prior to the end of a 12-Month cycle.  So, if this doesn't help clear up any confusion on the issue, then I recommend actually calling Xbox Customer Support, as they can look into the billing details and see what's actually happening in the account, without relying on speculation.  You can also view this activity at commerce.microsoft.com.  But nobody here in the forums has the capability of viewing billing details for your account.

@Dakrkplayer2:   The thing about ending hours before it should... Xbox's systems run on the UTC time zone, so midnight for their system is about 7:00pm EST here in the USA.  If your Gold is scheduled to expire on April 10th, it will actually run out on April 9th at around 7:00pm EST, give or take a while for the system to actually process the change.  You can take advantage of this by redeeming prepaid membership codes, or buying memberships outright, after 7:00pm EST, as that will count as your cycle beginning the next day, scoring you a bit of extra time.

Thought someone else having this problem might like to know how I fixed it.

FINAL SOLUTION:  

Because this seemed to be happening especially when I streamed videos from Hulu, I decided to check the video card. Opened the computer case, checked to see if the video card was seated properly; it was.  So, that wasn't the problem. 

Then, I removed the video card from the motherboard and cleaned the dust/dirt from where it attached to the motherboard with a vacuum cleaner and cleaned the fan on the card.  I also removed the old thermal grease from the video processor on the card and replaced it with new thermal grease (Arctic Silver) so it wouldn't overheat. Reinstalled the video card on the motherboard and closed everything up.

Now, my computer works perfectly.  No blue screens or any problems since.  Apparently, the problem was that plain old dust was causing the video processor to overheat.  Simple enough to do it yourself.  (If you want to change the thermal grease, which will do wonders for preventing overheating of your computer processors, look at one of the many videos on YouTube that show how to do it.)
