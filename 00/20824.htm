=index(Master!B:B,match(A2,Master!A:A,0))

=B2-D2

jllatham say:

Are the columns in your sample, D and E your "master list?  And it is the entries in columns A and B you need to compare?

If that is the case, you could put a formula like this into column C (cell C2 formula shown)

=B2-(VLOOKUP(A2,D:E,2,FALSE))

If the result is negative it means that column B price is lower than column E price.  I used simple "Format only cells that contain..." type conditional formatting, with less than zero showing one color (I chose Blue), and with those greater than zero showing another color (Red?).
