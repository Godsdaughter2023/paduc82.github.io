arminatorx say:

You can't "transfer games". 

But your buddy can share his games to your console, if that's what you mean. The game ownership will still be on your buddy's account, and your buddy can un-share his games anytime. 

But your buddy can visit you, sign in with his own account on your console, and go to the console settings. 

Under "Personalization", he can then set your console as his "Home Console". 

Be aware, that this change can only be made five times for his account within 365 days! So if your buddy changes his Home Console to another console, to share his games there, this will reduce his allowed 5 Home Console changes per year!

After he set your console as his Home Console, he can delete his account from your console for security reasons (or at least protect it with a passcode), and you'll be able to play his games for as long as he keeps your console his Home Console.

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

Always state your Windows version when asking a Windows question. I moved your post to the Windows forum because it was off-topic in the Community Participation Center forum.

Every folder contains this file. It controls what icon appears for the folder when viewed in its parent and what type of files the folder contains. If you see this file, it means that you have enabled viewing hidden and system files in that folder.

For example, the desktop.ini file contained in the "My Documents" folder contains this:

[.ShellClassInfo]
LocalizedResourceName=@%SystemRoot%\system32\shell32.dll,-21770
IconResource=%SystemRoot%\system32\imageres.dll,-112
IconFile=%SystemRoot%\system32\shell32.dll
IconIndex=-235

You have two on your display because you're looking at a library folder that points to two separate folders. Each of those folders contains its own desktop.ini file.

For more information on this file, see https://msdn.microsoft.com/en-us/library/windows/desktop/cc144102
