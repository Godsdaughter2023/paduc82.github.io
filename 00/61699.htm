    chris cupler say:

    Question:  you are running the Digital Cable Advisor with MC in 'windowed' mode, yes?  If MC is full-screen, you won't see the UAC prompt that comes up after you click the 'change system settings' button, it'll time out, and the changes (the Registry key for Digital Cable support - in other words, the activation key) will never be written.

    Other thing I've seen that causes the DCA to fail in this way: 
    Many Asus motherboards have a pre-populated OCUR table in their BIOS.  Unfortunately, this is the *first* thing that the DCA looks for - if it finds an OCUR table, it assumes that the system is already digital cable-ready, and simply does not write the Reg key (because "it's ready.")
    Workaround is to use the 'public' digital cable key (just Google for that.)
