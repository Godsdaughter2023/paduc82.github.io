What is the CRCDISK? - Microsoft Community

hansoloagain say:

What is the CRCDISK?

I was looking at my driver list and found this disabled. I did not do this so I am wondering what it controls

a. user say:

CRCDISK.SYS identifies itself as a "Disk Block Verification Filter Driver" and was written by Microsoft. Presumably, it does some kind of CRC checking on data being read off the disk.

Crcdisk.sys is not essential for the Windows OS and causes relatively few problems. The crcdisk.sys file is located in the C:\Windows\System32\drivers folder. Known file sizes on Windows 8/7/XP are 22,632 bytes (72% of all occurrences), 24,632 bytes, 21,984 bytes, 17,920 bytes or 22,584 bytes. 

The driver can be started or stopped from Services in the Control Panel or by other programs. The program is not visible. Crcdisk.sys is a Microsoft signed file. The file is certified by a trustworthy company. There is no detailed description of this service. It is not a Windows core file. crcdisk.sys appears to be a compressed file. Therefore the technical security rating is 9% dangerous.

Frontier Defense - A guide for beginners - Microsoft Community

sigmathedj say:

Frontier Defense - A guide for beginners

I've played this mode a lot, so here's some tips and suggested weapons for people who are new to it.

1. Use the Smart Pistol. Whatever you think about this gun, it's perfect for slaying grunts and spectres. Every time you kill a grunt/spectre, the build time for your titan goes down, so you might as well use the best gun for the job. There is some logic in using the Spitfire LMG with the Slammer attachment, but only if you're going to do a lot of titan rodeoing, which I don't think is a particularly worthwhile thing to do given that you can easily drop a titan in round 1, or at worst, during the beginning of round 2. The Smart Pistol is also great if the harvester gets mobbed by grunts/titans and you have to dash back to take care of a bunch of them, as you can jump around and avoid their shots while taking them down super fast.

2. Use the Triple Threat and the Atlas. The Triple Threat is a great choice for this mode. It slays titans really quickly when you're up close. It slays groups of grunts/spectres quickly too. It's the only weapon that can be fired over the top of buildings, so you can often kill a drop pod of grunts or do a decent amount of damage to a titan before they're even in your line of sight. It has decent range too. I'd recommend the Atlas simply because it has a good combination of speed/dash and strength, plus damage core is really useful when you have a bunch of titans that need taking out quickly.

3. Use Nuclear Eject. There's 2 reasons to use this. If you kill 3 titans at once with it, the build time for your next titan reduces to zero, so literally as you're nuke ejecting out of one titan you can call the next one in. This is great for the later rounds where you might have dozens of nuke titans spawning. The second reason to use it is that it's great for "last resort" kills, for instance, if the harvester is really close to death and a nuke titan is approaching and you haven't got time to kill it with your weapons, you can nuke eject and kill it to save the team from losing the round.

4. Kill the Mortar Titans quickly. I play so many games with randoms where people seem to allow the Mortar Titans to batter the harvester. You want to take them out as quickly as possible. I use Electric Smoke, which can be handy if you have 2 or 3 Mortar Titans in a cluster as it'll not only deal damage, it'll stop them from firing as they have to get up to move out of the way of the smoke.

5. Activate the turrets. If you're on a map with turrents, make sure you activate them. Not only will they kill grunts/spectres and damage titans, when they shoot titans, the titans will often stop and attack the turrent, rather than continuing to approach the harvester. If any turrets go down, re-activate them at the end of a round when it's safe to do so, or quickly at the beginning of the next round. I've played lots of games with randoms where the other players just stand around the harvester at the beginning of round 1, rather than going off to activate the turrets - don't be one of those guys! :P

6. Use your mini-map. It sounds obvious, but a lot of players don't make good use of the mini-map. For instance, if there's a single Mortar Titan that's off in the distance and one of your team mates is dealing with it in their own titan, most of the time there's no point in you going out there as well. Be aware of what the other players are doing. For example, I've played games where I've gone over to the other side of the map because I know Mortar Titans will be dropping in, then next minute, my 3 team mates are there too, leaving the harvester to get battered by grunts and spectres, lol.

7. Make good use of your own portable turrets. There are certain "golden" spots to place them where you'll rack up a ton of kills. You'll learn them through trial and error, but have a look to see how many kills a turret is getting when it's in a certain spot. Also, look to see what routes the grunts and spectres take on their way to the harvester and try and cover those routes with turrets. If you get rodeoed by a spectre and there's a turret nearby, put your back to the turret and it'll shoot the spectre to save you having to deal with it using other means.

8. If you die, remember that if you have a titan ready, you can drop straight back in without having to wait for a countdown. Also, remember that you can fire your guns and throw grenades/mines/satchels from the dropship, so you can be damaging titans and killing grunts before you're even properly back in the game. You can even shoot through the floor of your own dropship, even though the titan you're shooting at is obscured from view. You'll see your aiming reticule turn red, despite the fact that your line of sight is blocked.

9. Be aggressive. I always take the fight to the enemy, rather than waiting for them to come to me. I can always dash back to the harvester if it's in danger. Also, remember that grunts are more powerful in this mode. They'll sometimes lob grenades at you, so be wary of them as nobody likes being killed by a grunt!

10. If you like using the Arc Cannon instead of the Triple Threat - the Arc Cannon is my favourite titan weapon for all other modes except this one - remember that when you kill an Arc Titan it will drop an amped Arc Cannon that you can pick up to replace your standard one.

11. IMO, the 2 best burn cards for this mode are Wi-Fi Hack and Double Agent. With Wi-Fi Hack, if a spectre tries to rodeo your titan it'll instantly get hacked, then it'll jump off your titan and fight on your side. Double Agent is great because grunts and spectres ignore you. An amped anti-titan weapon can be worthwhile too.

12. I like to use a combination of Electric Smoke and Big Punch. When you trigger the smoke, a titan will often continue following its path towards the harvester, so it will walk through the smoke taking damage, then as it comes out of it, you can big punch it back in again.


That's about all I can think of for now. Hopefully someone finds these tips useful. Obviously, advanced players will have their own way of playing, but beginners should find something that helps them.
