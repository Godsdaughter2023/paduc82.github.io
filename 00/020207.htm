Note that my suggestion was not made to be a "solution" but a simple diagnostic.  Diagnostics are intended to refine a problem's symptom description.   So telling me it doesn't help, doesn't help the diagnosis.  ; }

BTW notice the host name used for the favicon I pointed out?  It's not the host name that you are trying to use; that one is an alias and could easily explain your symptom if you do not have connectivity to the first name, aka the canonical name.  So if you are having trouble getting a lookup done for that one you could try switching to a cmd window and enter the following commands:

   ipconfig  /flushdns
and
   ping  -n 1  seekingalpha.com
and
   ping  -n 1  www.seekingalpha.com
and
   ipconfig  /displaydns  >displaydns.txt
and
    notepad  displaydns.txt

Then in the notepad window press  F3 to search for  seekingalpha 

E.g. after doing that I find this:

<extract displaydns.txt>
    seekingalpha.com
    ----------------------------------------
    Record Name . . . . . : seekingalpha.com
    Record Type . . . . . : 1
    Time To Live  . . . . : 268
    Data Length . . . . . : 4
    Section . . . . . . . : Answer
    A (Host) Record . . . : 65.74.170.164

</extract>

Without that  A(Host) Record  IE would have to do a lookup each time and thus with a marginal DNS fail to have connectivity with your host site on any of its requests using the alias name.

BTW I just used that IP address to open the site's home page and what results in the Address bar after that is

http://seekingalpha.com/?source=refreshed

So I suggest trying either of those possibilities instead of the alias name that you are trying to use.

And again, you should regard these suggestions more as diagnostics than as "solutions".  ; )

I want to make a user as administrator to both SharePoint and OneDrive for Business services in the O365 tenant. About Office 365 admin roles

OneDrive for Business - Admin help

rena yu msft say:

Hi Vamsi,

 

Here’s some information related to your questions:

  • The user who is assigned “SharePoint administrator” role will have access to the SharePoint Admin Center and OneDrive Admin Center.

 


  • The SharePoint administrator can access and manage SharePoint site collections except OneDrive for Business site collections by default.
  • Typically, the user who the OneDrive site is being created for is the only OneDrive site admin. However, you can set “My Site secondary admin” so this admin will be able to access and manage all the new created OneDrive sites. For more information about this, please refer to How to enable My Site secondary administrator for OneDrive for Business.
  • For the existing OneDrive sites, the SharePoint admin only can set “access delegation for secondary My Site owner”. This admin’s access and manage permission will only be granted when he gets the email that alerts you “the user’s My Site will be deleted in 14 days”.
  • To set “My Site secondary admin” or “secondary My Site owner”, go to SharePoint admin center>User Profiles>My site settings>setup my sites
