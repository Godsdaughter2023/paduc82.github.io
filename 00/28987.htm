lz. say:

Hi vikas,


Taskhost.exe is a generic host process for Windows services that are DLL (not EXE) based. You can therefore find several occurrences of Taskhost.exe running at the same time and that's OK.


The problem is you can't identify which real process "sits" behind a Taskhost.exe instance in Task Manager and therefore can't immediately know - in your case - which real process seems to consume the +/- whole internet bandwidth (assuming I understood correctly what you mean by "taskhost.exe uses internet connection and therefore i m unable to connect to other websites").


Fortunately enough there are some good guys on this planet and a tool like Process Explorer will assist you in identifying the real service(s) sitting behind your Taskhost.exe instance(s). From there things will be easier...

It's just the nature of converting a still to video:
