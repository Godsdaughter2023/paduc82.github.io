What does the notation *.~ mean to Windows? I'm - Microsoft Community

maggietoo9 say:

What does the notation *.~ mean to Windows? I'm familiar with *.* but not *.~

What does the notation  *.~  mean to Windows?  I'm familiar with *.* and *.?, but not *.~  which I encountered in file/folder exclusions in Acronis True Image.  Searches for this term have turned up nothing.  Thanks.

a. user say:

The asterisk (*) and the question mark (?) are both wildcards, which can be replaced by other characters.

The asterisk can be replaced by any number of characters. For example, if you want to search for all the Word documents on your computer, you would search for *.docx

The question mark can be replaced only by a single character.

If you want an asterisk or a question mark to not be a wildcard, you place a tilde (`) just before it.

In your case:   *.` could represent (among other things) all files with a tilde in the file extension.
