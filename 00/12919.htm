Successfully Downloading Applications over 20MB in size to Windows - Microsoft Community

j woods - msft say:

Successfully Downloading Applications over 20MB in size to Windows Phone 7; using Wi-Fi

For applications or games greater than 20MB that are purchased on your phone, you complete the download over a Wi‐Fi connection, even if using Zune software to purchase.

 

Examples of applications that are greater than 20MB* include: The Harvest™, Monopoly®, The Sims™ 3, and Earthworm Jim™ HD.

 

You may receive one or moreerror messages when attempting to download large applications:

 

Error message under download bar in “download/s” on your phone: “Attention required. Tap here”.

 

Error message when tapping an individual app or game Tile in “download/s”: “Connect to a Wi‐Fi network or connect your phone to your PC to complete the download.”

 

Here are 7 steps to complete your download:

 

1. Connect to Wi‐Fi from your phone.

 

2. Tap the Marketplace Tile on the Start screen on your phone.

 

3. Tap on “downloads” at the bottom of the screen (for example: “1 download”).

 

4. Tap and hold the app or game of your choice until you see “retry”.

 

5. Tap “retry” to initiate download over Wi‐Fi connection.

 

6. Confirm that the download progress bar is active (note: if your screen locks during the download process, you may need to return to step 2).

 

7. Once the download is complete your apps can be found in the App list or games in the Games Hub.

 

*Note: You can check the size of the application at the bottom of the app details page in Marketplace on your Windows Phone.

 

 

For other, unexpected issues with a trial version of an application, please try uninstalling/reinstalling the application from Marketplace.

 

 

Using Zune software on your PC

If you attempt to download an application or game larger than 20MB from the Zune software on your PC, you will need to complete the installation using a Wi‐Fi connection.

 

We recommend that you connect your phone to the PC when prompted, and then use Wi‐Fi on the phone to complete the installation of the application or game.

 

Using the Zune software sync to download applications or games larger 20MB is not possible at this time the plan is to provide an update enabling this functionality soon.   In the interim, these downloads can be completed over a Wi‐Fi connection from your phone.

 

Simple steps to install any 20MB+ application or game download or purchase from the Zune software on the PC:

 

1. Connect your phone to the PC following purchase of paid or free app or game (if not already connected).

 

2. Confirm that the phone screen displays “Connected”.

 

3. Confirm that the phone screen displays “Sync complete – to view content, disconnect your phone from the computer”.

 

4. Disconnect the phone from your PC.

 

5. Follow the same steps 7 steps to download using Wi‐Fi from your phone, also included above:

 

a. Connect to Wi‐Fi from your phone.

b. Tap the Marketplace Tile on the Start screen on your phone.

c. Tap on “downloads” at the bottom of the screen, example “1 download”.

d. Tap and hold the app or game of your choice until you see “retry”.

e. Tap “retry” to initiate download over Wi‐Fi connection.

f. Confirm that the download progress bar is active (note: if your screen locks during the download process, you may need to return to step 2).

g. Once the download is complete your apps can be found in the App list or games in the Games Hub.

  

Zune software note

If you have purchased paid or free applications or games from the Zune software and would likeimmediate access to these on Windows Phone 7, please connect the phone to your computer and follow steps 1‐4 above. Remember, for any apps or games larger than 20MB, please follow the steps outlined above to complete installation.

 
nsilmike say:

Hiding your SSID is not standards compliant... although many folks do it.  So, if you support it you get nailed for not following standards, and if you don't you get nailed by users.   Kind of a tough spot...

Also, many Network admins do this for improved security, and unfortunately it actually make things worse.  If you stop broadcasting your SSID, then nodes trying to connect have to broadcast it back and forth (Beacon and Probe messages.)  And since they don't know when they are in range, they broadcast it over a much wider radius than the access point would...
