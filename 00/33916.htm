david vinod say:

Hi,

 

Have you made any changes to the computer prior to the issue?

 

The werfault.exe is used for Windows Error Reporting. It is a feature that allows Microsoft to track and address errors relating to the operating system, Windows features, and applications. It gives you the option to send data about errors to Microsoft and to receive information about solutions.

Method 1: Try to stop and restart the service and check if that helps.

 

Step 1: Disable the ‘Windows error reporting device’.

a)   Click on ‘Start’ and type ‘services.msc’ in the search box and press Enter.

b)   In the service list navigate to the ‘Windows error reporting service’.

c)   Right-click on the service and click on ‘properties’.

d)   Click on the ‘Stop’ to stop the service.

e)   Next to ‘Startup type’, click on the drop down menu and select ‘Disable’.

f)    Click on the ‘Apply’ and click ‘Ok’.

Now try to open the programs and check if you receive the error message.

 

Step 2: If you do not get the error when you open the open then, restart the service and check if that helps.

a)   Click on ‘Start’ and type ‘services.msc’ in the search box and press Enter.

b)   In the service list navigate to the ‘Windows error reporting service’.

c)   Right-click on the service and click on ‘properties’.

d)   Next to ‘Startup type’, click on the drop down menu and select ‘Automatic’

e)   Click on the ‘Start’ button and click on ‘Apply’ and click ‘Ok’.

NOTE: If the Start option is grayed out and will not start, then you will need to restart the computer to start it after you set it to Automatic and clicked OK.

 

Method 2: If the issue persists then, start the computer in clean boot and then try to run the programs to check if any third party program is causing the issue. To configure your computer to clean boot refer the following link.

 

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/929135

 

Note: After you have finished troubleshooting, follow step 7 in the article to boot to normal startup.
