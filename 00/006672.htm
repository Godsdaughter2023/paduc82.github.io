To achieve that, you will need to do the following:

  1. In the Define new Multilevel list dialog, for the level 1 numbering, that you link to the Heading 1 style, set the "Follow number with:" option to "Nothing"
  2. Modify the formatting of the Heading 1 style by removing the hanging indent and setting the required centre aligned tab stop
  3. In File>Options>Proofing>AutoCorrect Options>AutoFormat As You Type, remove the check mark from the "Set left- and first-indent with tabs and backspaces" under the "Automatically as you type" section of the dialog

scottgem say:

OK, this is the problem. Your combobox is returning the ID, not the firstname, even though the first name is displayed. The Bound column indicates which column of the RowSource is stored in the control.

So your query needs to include the ID column and your criteria should be in THAT column, not the First name column. Since you are filtering the query by the selection in the combo, then you don't need a WHERE clause. However, I would recommend changing the process by removing the  criteria from the query and adding a WHERE clause to your macro:

[ID] = [Forms]![Contact Information Dashboard]![comboboxauxiliary]

Frankly, I would do this in VBA. Its one line of code:

DoCmd.OpenForm "GGTC Auxiliary",,,"[ID] = " & [Forms]![Contact Information Dashboard]![comboboxauxiliary]

As an aside, its not a good idea to use spaces in object names. Use Camel notation (ContactInformationDashboard) or underscores (Contact_Information_Dashboard).
