Hi Louis,
Were the calendar and contacts in folders labeled 'this computer only' ? Did you delete the profile or make a new profile?

When you use an IMAP account and have calendar & contacts in 'this computer only' folders and anything happens to the data file, they are gone. Removing accounts from the profile or deleting the profile will delete the data file.

A normal upgrade or repair shouldn't affect it, unless you have the windows store version - if you do have the store version, i recommend switching to click to run now, before you create new events and contacts.

bluebloodnypd say:

How can I prevent my account from receiving junk emails?

Original title: junk mail

a. user say:

Hi bluebloodnypd,

 

Welcome to the Microsoft Community! I'm pleased to assist you with your concern on your account.

 

Let me see if I have this right. You are receiving more junk emails after your account's upgrade to Outlook. If so, it is possible that you have been a victim of a spam attack. To prevent unknown or unwanted contacts from sending you unwanted messages, I would advise you to add their email address/domain to your Blocked senders list. Please be informed that messages from blocked senders are automatically deleted. Here are the steps:

 

1. From your Inbox, click the gear icon on the upper right corner beside your name.

2. Select More mail settings.

3. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.

4. Click Blocked senders.

5. Enter the email address or domain in the box labeled Blocked email address or domain: and click Add to list >>. Make sure that you enter the correct email address/domain.

 

In addition to that, you can also stop receiving junk mails by setting your junk email filtering level to Exclusive. If you set your filter to this, only messages from your contacts and Safe senders lists will be allowed to go to your Inbox, the rest will go to your Junk Folder. Below are the steps on how to do this:

 

1. From your Inbox, click the gear icon on the upper right corner beside your name.

2. Go to More mail settings.

3. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.

4. Choose Exclusive  where everything is sent to the junk email folder except messages from your contacts and safe senders, Outlook service announcements, and alerts that you signed up for.

5. Click Save.

 

Note: You should occasionally check your junk email folder to make sure that good messages don't get put there by mistake. Also, your Junk folder is regularly cleaned up. So you need not to delete those emails manually.

 

You can also create an email rule that will either automatically delete the junk message or move it to another folder.Here are the steps:

 

1. From your Inbox, click the gear icon on the upper right corner beside your name.

2. Go to More mail settings.

3.

 Under Customizing Outlook, click Rules for sorting new messages.

4. Click New.

5. Under Step 1, select the email attribute from the drop-down list and type the email address that you want to apply the rule to.

6. Under Step 2, determine where you want the messages to be moved or what you want to do to that email (e.g. if it would be auto Deleted):

     a. To move the messages to an existing folder, choose Move to Inbox, and then click the folder name

     b. To move the messages to a new folder, choose Move to a new folder and type the name for the folder you want to create.

     c. To delete the messages, choose Delete these messages.

     d. To forward messages, select forward messages and type the email address where you wish to forward the said emails.

7. Click Save.

 

Note: On instances where the spammer just changes the domain (sending email address), it would be better if you would create an email rule that will specify the subject instead of the sender's email address. (e.g. If the email contains the word "Viagra", delete the email).

 

Hope this helps. Feel free to post back if you need further assistance.

 

Thank you.
