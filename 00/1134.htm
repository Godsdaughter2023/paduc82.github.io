suzanne s. barnhill say:
The trick is to use IF fields and include the line break in the result. Something like this:
 
{ IF { MERGEFIELD Child2 } <> "" "<linebreak>{ MERGEFIELD Child2}<tab>{ MERGEFIELD Teacher2} {MERGEFIELD Grade2}" }
 
where <tab> and <line break> represent the appropriate characters. This will mean that you'll actually end up with something like this:
 
{ MERGEFIELD Child1}<tab>{ MERGEFIELD Teacher1} {MERGEFIELD Grade1}{ IF { MERGEFIELD Child2 } <> "" "
{ MERGEFIELD Child2}<tab>{ MERGEFIELD Teacher2} {MERGEFIELD Grade2}" }{ IF { MERGEFIELD Child3 } <> "" "
{ MERGEFIELD Child3}<tab>{ MERGEFIELD Teacher3} {MERGEFIELD Grade3}" }{ IF { MERGEFIELD Child4 } <> "" "
{ MERGEFIELD Child4}<tab>{ MERGEFIELD Teacher4} {MERGEFIELD Grade4}" }<paragraph break>
 
So you don't get any line breaks unless the following mergefield is not empty. The line can be a bottom border on the paragraph.

C:\Users\<login name>\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\UProof

a. user say:

Hi,

 

Welcome to Microsoft Community! I am glad to pay attention to your concern regarding the emails that is missing on your Inbox.

 

Yes, there's auto cleanup for emails. Just follow the steps provided below to do the Clean up.

 

The new Sweep option Schedule cleanup automatically deletes email from a specific sender after 30, 60, or 90 days. If you subscribe to a daily deal site (like LivingSocial or Groupon) and never want to see anything but the most recent offer, you can use a scheduled cleanup to do the tidying for you.

 

To schedule a cleanup, select a message, click Sweep, and then click Schedule cleanup. Choose what you want to do with the sender's messages, then click OK. Outlook will automatically create a rules.

To delete that rules, just follow the steps below.

 

1. When you are in your Outlook. Click the Gear icon at the upper right.

2. Click More Mail Settings -> under "Customizing Outlook".

3. Click Rules for sorting new messages.

4. And then delete the rules that you've created. tick the check box on the left side and then click Delete.

 

Also, the possible reasons why e-mails suddenly disappear are as follows:

  • Messages were manually deleted either due to accidental deletion or were moved or deleted due to your account filter settings.
  • Messages were deleted due to account compromise issues where a hacker might have gained access to your account and deleted the emails.
  • You are using an e-mail client or a third party spam filtering software to access your account. This often happens when using the Pop3 protocol to retrieve your mail and the option “Always leave a copy of the message on the server” is not selected.
  • The account was marked as “Inactive." Windows Live Hotmail accounts become inactive if you don’t sign in for more than 270 days or within the first 10 days after signing up for an account. After an account becomes inactive, all messages, folders, and contacts are deleted.

To check if you can still recover the emails that is missing on your account after the upgrade, please check the steps that is provided in the link.
