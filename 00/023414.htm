Please confirm if you have Office 2013 Professional Plus bought on the "Home Use Program" or one of the Office 365 bundles? I'm guessing you have Pro Plus.  365 has slightly different "issues"

Now that we have the full error message text (in the other reply <grin> ) we have something to work with.

The problem has nothing to do with having a "legal copy" or "registration/activation".  The problem is with Windows Update or your internet connection.

First, go into Control Panel > Windows Update > View Update History.  Look for recent updates with status other than "successful".  That is almost certainly the cause of the error message.

Double click on the row to expand it.  There should be a bit more of an explanation, plus a link to more information about the error.  If you don't understand it, report the error code and message text back here for more help.

The second part of the message is hinting there is a problem with your internet connection. 

It is NOT what you think, it is NOT saying you don't have ANY internet connection, it is just saying that "Office" does not "like" something about your internet connection.  Sometimes Office 2013/365 gives this sort of "connection" message when you are trying to download from a corporate network that has a "Proxy" server.  Office 365 just doesn't "like" proxies.   Try going to another place to do the downloads, ie home or a friends place.

Another reported cause for the connection message is using a wireless / wifi / "Verizon Jetpack" wireless connection.  Simply changing to a different internet connection, such as a wired connection has been reported to fix the problem.

vijay a. verma say:
Hi StumfSlo,
I am Vijay, an Independent Advisor. I am here to work with you on this problem.

Your OneDrive account will be frozen if you exceed your OneDrive storage limit for more than three months. So you have a grace period of 90 days.

AFTER 90 DAYS - You have two options to bring your account back into good standing:
a. Choose Get 1 TB (1024 GB) of OneDrive storage with Office 365 and buy an Office 365 subscription - https://onedrive.live.com/about/en-US/plans/
b. Choose Unfreeze your account to temporarily access your files. You'll have 30 days to remove enough files to be within your storage limit. Your files will be read-only, but you can download or delete files to get back under your storage limit. If you don't remove enough files within 30 days, your account will be frozen once more and you won't be able to unfreeze it again.

When unfreezing your account, most accounts are restored within 24 hours.
