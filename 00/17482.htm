Private Sub Document_ContentControlOnExit(ByVal CCtrl As ContentControl, Cancel As Boolean)
Dim i As Long, StrTxt As String
With CCtrl
  If .Title = "Company" Then
    For i = 1 To .DropdownListEntries.Count
      If .DropdownListEntries(i).Text = .Range.Text Then
        StrTxt = .DropdownListEntries(i).Value
        Exit For
      End If

      .SelectContentControlsByTitle("CRN")(1).Range.Text = Split(StrTxt, "|")(0)

      .SelectContentControlsByTitle("Phone")(1).Range.Text = Split(StrTxt, "|")(2)

Absolutely impossible for anyone to answer that question without knowing any details about the email and what, if anything, it was asking you to do. Just like there are endless phishing emails supposedly from Paypal, iTunes, any financial organization, there are also phishing scam emails supposedly coming from Microsoft.

Step #1 - check the sender's email address. If it's not from a Microsoft domain, it's a phony message. I have rec'd countless such msg's that only highlight's the stupidity of the sender. HOWEVER, even if the sender's email address appears to come from MS, that doesn't mean it's legit since the email address in a msg can be easily spoofed which requires moving to step #2

Step #2 - Check any link in the message to see if it's coming from a valid MS domain. That can be done by simply hovering over the link. It's not enough to just pay attention to text of the link - you absolutely need to ensure that the underlying link is valid. The other way to check is to right-click on the link --> select <Copy Hyperlink> and then paste it in any text editor (i.e. Notepad).

On a general level, MS isn't going to ask you to validate your credentials anymore then your bank is going to ask you for your account info etc etc.

john korchok say:

Office 2010 includes an optional Org Chart module in its installer. You can access it this way:

  1. Close all Office programs.
  2. In Windows, click on the Start button, then on Control Panel.
  3. Select Programs, then Programs and Features.
  4. Right-click on Microsoft Office 2010 and choose Change.
  5. Select Add or Remove Features, then click on Continue.
  6. Expand the Microsoft PowerPoint section, then click on Organizational Chart Add-in for Microsoft Office programs and choose Run from the computer.
  7. Click on Continue. Allow the process to complete, then restart your computer.

You can also create better-looking org charts using SmartArt.

In PowerPoint, if you click on File>New, then enter org chart in the Search Office.com for templates field, you'll see a dozen templates from MS. Googling org chart template also reveals many more.
