john w. vinson say:

Your question isn't quite clear. What is the meaning of [Table#], and what do you mean by "based on table#"? Could you post a sample of the data with the desired result? A running sum implies a sort order: how is your query sorted?

ASSUMING - and it's a guess in the dark! - that your query is sorted by date, and you want to show a running sum of the Lbs Produce field for all records where Table# is not blank, the easiest way is to add a calculated field in the form's Query. In a vacant Field cell in the query grid type

RunSum: DSum("IIF(Len([Table#] & """") > 0, [Lbs Produce], 0)", "YourQueryName", "[Date] <=# " & [Date] & "#")

Set the Control Source of the running sum textbox to [RunSum].

A couple of design suggestions: Use some fieldname other than Date, that's a reserved word for the builtin function that returns today's date; avoid using blanks and special characters like # in fieldnames - use FirstName or TableNo; and consider normalizing your table structure. If you have multiple sales for each person, the FirstName and LastName fields should be in a Customer table and this table should have a CustomerID link to that table.
