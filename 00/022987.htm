jay freedman say:

In the Find dialog, click the More button and check the box for "Use wildcards". Then put the following expression into the Find What box:

^13[a-z]

Then use the Find Next or Find In or Reading Highlight button, depending on what you want to do.

If you want to use the Replace dialog to remove the paragraph marks from all the instances at once, after checking "Use wildcards", put this in the Find What box:

^13([a-z])

and put this in the Replace With box:

\1

(include a space character before the slash if the line before the paragraph mark doesn't end with a space). Then click the Replace All button.
