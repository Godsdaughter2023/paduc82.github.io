    considerate_guy say:

    Movie Maker doesn't create video DVDs (suitable for playback in a standalone DVD player). Instead, this duty is handed off to Windows DVD Maker if present.

    Windows DVD Maker was included as part of Windows Vista and Windows 7, but has been dropped since. It's much like the way Apple dropped iDVD support back in 2011 when they stopped including it in their iLife Suite.

    So the short answer is, video DVD *creation* is not provided in Windows 10 (even if you were to pay to install the DVD *player* as noted by Juzar Ali).

    There are a few options:

    1. You can look for 3rd-party options for creating video DVDs. Some shareware choices are DVD Flick or DVD Styler.
    2. You can choose other ways of saving your movie. The video DVD standard comes from the era of analog TVs and can't handle resolution higher than 480p (NTSC) and also uses the older MPEG-2 compression codec. If you still want a physical disc copy, burn the resulting .mp4 file from Movie Maker onto a DVD disc (as a data DVD) and play it in a Blu-ray player or modern game console with Blu-ray support (XBox, PS3/4, etc.). You can also consider just streaming the movie over your home network using DLNA, or copying the movie to a USB drive and connecting that to your TV.
