Customer Retention Rate Formula - Microsoft Community

jwike say:

Customer Retention Rate Formula

Hello,

 

I have a spreadsheet with a list of 34,712 email addresses and order dates next to each. There are duplicates because many have ordered more than once. Example:

*** Email address is removed for privacy *** 6/15/2011

*** Email address is removed for privacy *** 6/14/2011

*** Email address is removed for privacy *** 3/12/2010

 

I'm trying to determine the customer retention rate from year to year - or how many customers come back each year and then the next. The data goes all the way back to 2006. I'd like to know what percentage of customers returned from 2006 to 2007 then to 2008 and so forth. How should I do this?

 

Thanks for the help!

Joe

 

bernie deitrick, excel mvp 2000-2010 say:

You can do this using extra columns of formulas.

Let's say that your data is in Columns A and B. Insert a new row 1, and then in D2, put 2006, E2 - 2007, and so on to 2011 in I2.

In C3, put this formula, to capture only the first instance of an address

=IF(COUNTIF($A$3:A3,A3)=1,A3,"")

In D3, put the formula

=IF($C3<>"",IF(SUMPRODUCT(($A3:$A30001=$C3)*(YEAR($B3:$B30001)=D$2))=0,"No","Yes"),"")

and copy over to E3:I3, then copy C3:I3 down to match your list.

Then to calculate your retention rate for 2006 to 2007 use this - entered into cell E1 and formatted as percentage.

=SUMPRODUCT((D3:D35000="Yes")*(E3:E35000="Yes"))/COUNTIF(D3:D35000,"Yes")

Then copy that to the right, and the retention rate will be calculated for each year to year period.

23hitman say:

If the cell = nothing in there, display this color. If the cell = a specific number, display a different color

Excel 2010

Both rules in the same cell.
And then I want to apply this to every cell in a range, just changing the specific number.


1. What would be the exact steps, starting from clicking the Conditional Formatting button,
2. How would I copy the formula to a range of cells?
3. Would would be the steps to modify the conditional formatting rule for each of the new cells?

Thanks in advance!
ashish mathur say:

Hi,

 

Try this

 

1. Click on the first cell (say B4) in the Range and go to Home > Conditional formatting > Highlight Cell Rules > Equal to.

2. Enter the specific number and select any colour from the drop down.  Click on OK

3. Click on the first cell (say B4) in the Range and go to Home > Conditional formatting > New Rule > Use a formula to determine which cells to format.  In the formula bar there, enter =ISNUMBER($B4)

4. Click on Format and select any colour

5. Click on OK and Apply

6. Copy cell B4, select the range and press Alt+E+S+T.  This is basically Edit > Paste Special > Formats

 

To modify the rule, click on the first cell and go to Home > Conditional formatting > Manage Rules > Edit Rule.  Select the specific rule and click on Edit.  To then replicate the revised rule to other cells in the range, repeat step 6.

 

Hope this helps.
