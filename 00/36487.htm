chet814 say:

OK, when you power-on the machine, besides the beeps, what else happens?

 

Do any of the fans spin-up (power supply, cpu, video card, auxilary/case fans)?

Do the activity lights on the CD/DVD and hard drive show activity?

If you have a power light on the front of the case, does it light?

 

You mentioned earlier that you removed the video card and tried the on-board video connector with no change. Can you remember if the BIOS is set to automatically default to the on-board video if no video card (PCI, PCIe, AGP) is detected? If it is set to only start from the video card, then trying the on-board video may be useless.

 

Since you sound comfortable inside the case, I'd suggest disconnecting and re-connecting all cables for everything; power supply cables to motherboard, drives and option video (if applicable), cables from motherboard to drives, etc. Be careful inside the case, if you don't have an anti-static strap, ground yourself to a metal part of the case before touching anything inside.

 

Inspect all fans for resistance. Visually inspect motherboard and video card for signs of discoloration and overheating, look for bulging or leaking capacitors - especially around the cpu socket on the motherboard.

 

Should you have more than a single stick of RAM, try using only one at a time. Depending on your motherboard, you might try single sticks in different slots also.

 

Since the machine when powered-on beeps, it's an indication the power supply is working, but if you have access to a power supply tester or multimeter, it's still worth checking if the proper voltages are present.

 

The link below includes four flowcharts for troubleshooting the power supply, video, motherboard/cpu and hard drive which may be helpful. (I'm not referring to the 17 flowchart package for $$)
