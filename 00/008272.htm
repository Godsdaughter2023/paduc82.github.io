spacing after last bullet, a solution. - Microsoft Community

algebrat say:

spacing after last bullet, a solution.

I think this applies to earlier versions of Word as well (outside of location of various options).

 

Long story short, when you are at the last bullet in your list, hit return once, then click on the style button to switch back to the formatting of your main document. The natural reaction of most users may be to just to hit return then delete a couple times to get rid of the last bullet. Unfortunately, this leaves a change in formatting for the remainder for your document, likely not in line with earlier parts of your document.

 

In the following image, the first set of bullets is ended by hitting return, then switching styles (either by using the ribbon, or simply hitting CTRL+SHIFT+N to switch to normal style (can use CTRL+SHIFT+L for bullet environment as well)).

 

The second set of bullets was ended by hitting return then delete a couple times, thus some of the formatting of the bullet environment has carried over. We are no longer in the "Normal" style environment.

 

I've seen several other posts regarding the issue related to the second set of bullets, and thought this solution would be a good addition to the possible methods.

 

Part of the offending issue is a formatting option called "Don't add space between paragraphs of the same style". You can get to this option in the in line spacing or paragraph options, both in the Paragraph group. It is also available and visible if you have the Reveal Formatting pane open.

 

In general, when wrestling with the formatting of Word, my current approach to understanding what's going on is to turn on "Show paragraph marks and other hidden formatting", and "Reveal Formatting". The latter is not in the Ribbon by default, so I've added both to the Quick Access Toolbar; there are also the keyboard shortcut's CTRL+* and SHIFT+F1.

Another thing that should be kept in mind, is that, as I currently understand it, formatting is stored in the paragraph markers, so besides font and section environments (or scopes?) formatting depends on which two paragraph markers you are in between. This is more or less in-line with the structure found in the Reveal Formatting pane.
