davidf2 say:

"text is in a non-web ready form" means you are using a non-web friendly font and it is likely that it will be converted to an image instead of text or another font may be substituted in. Reference: http://www.w3schools.com/cssref/css_websafe_fonts.asp

Bottom line is Publisher cannot always convert a print formatted publication into a HTML or web formatted publication. Sometimes there simply is no HTML equivalent for the print formatting.

If you want to continue sending the newsletter as the HTML formatted message body in Outlook you will have to deconstruct your newsletter to the most basic and then add formatting and testing as you go. If the conversion to HTML breaks it, then you will have to do something else. Then once you get your newsletter to work correctly and render correctly in Outlook you need to test it in all the other email clients out there to see if the HTML that is specific to Outlook renders correctly. Then remember that if the viewer has his/her email client to read only plain text and no pictures for security reasons that all that work will be for naught.

To avoid all that work and potential frustration, then simply convert your newsletter to a PDF file and attach it to each email message. IMHO the only way to get consistent results is to convert to a PDF and attach it.  If you write a simple but effective introduction in the body of your email message and your message is of interest and wanted by the recipient they will open the PDF. If it is spam, then it won't matter whether it is in the message body or a PDF...it will be deleted.

If you must send HTML formatted messages then consider using specialty software such as:

www.constantcontact.com or www.group-mail.com and a free version: http://www.group-mail.com/asp/common/downloads.asp or another popular one is http://mailchimp.com/ - some free services - Store up to 2,000 subscribers & send up to 12,000 emails per month.

DavidF
