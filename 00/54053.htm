hellhog say:

The HD pack is for textures. Has nothing to do with resolution of the screen and aspect ratio. Some Xbox 360 games support both 4:3 and 16:9 aspect ratios. However, some games only support 16:9 and on a 4:3 device it will letterbox to adjust to 16:9. The downside is you have less pixels to work with but still get the widescreen advatage (larger peripherals basically). It is up to the developers. Splinter Cell Double Agent did this as I can remember. At least it doesn't stretch and distort the image. I bought my TV 3+ years ago thinking it was 16:9 720p. Turns out it is 16:10 so every game stretched from 16:9 to 16:10 and distorted the image. After buying a VGA cable and setting it to 1440x900 (a 16:10 ratio), it now sets up a letterbox for me so it runs 16:9.

carolina calling say:

Only if it was designed for one.  The brand new (as still in the original box but not really NEW)  Mitsumi keyboards I've seen are designed with DIN (aka AT) and mini-DIN (aka PS/2) plugs and do not come with an adapter.  They preceed  the widespread use of USB.  Later keyboards use lower signal levels and are designed with hot-plugging and passive adapters in mind.

As above, most adapters are passive and only connect the PS/2 wires to the equivalent USB contacts.  What you really need is a PS/2 to USB converter which has an integrated circuit (IC) that does active signal conversion,  I know the IOGear GUC10KM ($19.95 USD MSRP) is one such device but I've never used it.  TigerDirect/CompUSA sells them for $15.99.
