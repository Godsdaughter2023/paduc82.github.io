2. Before that we need to know whether you are using a USB mouse on your computer?

You can take assistance of your computer manufacturer or someone who is well equipped in BIOS troubleshooting.
 
Method 1:

Ensure that to enable the option of  Allow this device to wake the computer for the Mouse and check if it helps, to do that follow the below provided steps.
 
1. Click on start.
 
2. Now type devmgmt.msc in the search files and programs field.
 
3. At the device manager screen, right click on the Mice and other pointing devices.
 
4. Select properties.
 
5. Now you have to click on power management tab.
 
6. Now check mark Allow this device to wake the computer.
 
Now restart your computer and check for the issue.
 
Important:
Modifying BIOS/ complementary metal oxide semiconductor (CMOS) settings incorrectly can Cause serious problems that may prevent your computer from booting properly. Microsoft cannot guarantee that any problems resulting from the configuring of BIOS/CMOS settings can be solved. Modifications of the settings are at your own risk. 
 

Method 2:

Disable hybrid sleep on your system and then check for the issue.
 
To "Disable Hybrid Sleep Mode" follow the steps below.
 
1. Open up the power options setting from the control panel and select the "Change plan settings" link below the selected power plan.
 
2. Once in the resulting dialog, choose "Change advanced power settings".  Now you need to scroll through the miles and miles of options to find the "Sleep" node.  Expand this, and then the "Allow hybrid sleep" node below it.  You should see two options, "On battery" and "Plugged in".  Set both of these to "Off".
 
3. Once this is done, click "OK" and you should be all set!  From now on, whenever your laptop would normally go into "Sleep" - usually when the lid is closed or when inactive for a certain time - it will enter full sleep mode, rather than hybrid sleep.  And when you go to awaken it from its slumbers, it should be fully responsive within a second or two of reactivation - as opposed to the 10-15 seconds after hybrid sleep.
 
Method 3:

Update the graphics drivers and see if it makes any difference. You must be logged on as an administrator to perform these steps.
 
a. Open Device Manager by clicking the Start button, clicking Control Panel, clicking System and Maintenance, and then clicking Device Manager. If you are prompted for an administrator password or confirmation, type the password or provide confirmation.
 
b. In Device Manager, locate the Display adapter, and then double-click the device name.
 
c. Click the Driver tab, and then click Update Driver and follow the instructions.
 
Now try to follow the below provided link to Update a driver for hardware that isn't working properly and check for the issue.
 
http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows7/Update-a-driver-for-hardware-that-isnt-working-properly
 
You need to contact your computer manufacturer to get the latest drivers.
 


Reference: http://support.microsoft.com/kb/266283/en-us
 

 


Hope this information helps. Please let us know for further assistance.

a form or integrated graphics. In this case it it part of the cpu.  Older integrated graphics were
in the form of a chip on the motherboard
A dedicated graphics card (as separate card that attaches to the moterboard) uses what is called
VRAM which is a true dedicated  graphics memory.

Apparently Acer has has allocated a fixed amount of system RAM to graphics (dedicated) while allowing up  to 1GB to be accessed 'dynamically' when required by a game or other software that uses the HD4000.

As long as you are not multi-tasking while playing games the HD4000 should be able to access a
full 1GB RAM.

Laptops are set up differently to PC's and they have different methods between mfg's.
Some have software that allows you to alter the amount of RAM allocated to the integrated graphics,
and some may have an option in the BIOS,  as does my PC, to allocated different amounts of RAM to
the HD4000 graphics.

Regardless, as the others have mentioned, the HD4000 has limited capabilities vs  a mid-high end
graphics card.  It is one of the best integrated graphics at this time (recently having been superseded
with Intels newer cpu's ), but it is still  limited.
