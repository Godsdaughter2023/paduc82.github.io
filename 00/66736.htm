david. f say: Hello,Trikster66

You will need to connect the sound output of your computer to the TV.

Check your TV for what type of audio connections it accepts, it is likely that it has standard RCA red/white inputs. If there are multiple audio inputs, ensure that you locate the correct one to use with S-Video, it should be labeled or have a border around the audio and video inputs. If it doesn’t, you can just try each input to test as needed.

Next, check your computer for what type of audio output it has, in most cases, you will just have 3.5mm audio jack. If the audio connector is different than what’s on the TV, you will need a converter cable at one end.

Personally, I have a male to female 3.5mm cable running from my laptop to the TV, then a 3.5mm male to RCA male connector at the TV.

They do make long 3.5mm to RCA cables that may be more desirable than my setup since it’s just the one part.

In your Network and Sharing Center, under Advanced Sharing options, you can disable media streaming from your computer by clicking "Block All".  It will no longer advertise the availability of media streaming on your network.

 

This should also stop the "Windows Media Player Network Streaming Service", but you can run services.msc and disable this yourself if you want.
