darth continent say: It sounds like your monitor's speakers may be plugged directly into your sound card via a different cable than your headphones.

One option would be to get a separate pair of speakers w/amplifier that has a headphone jack, then unplug the monitor's speaker cable and use the separate speakers instead; that way the sound from the speakers should automatically cut off when you plug the headphones and turn on when you unplug them. I'd consider this, particularly if your monitor speakers' sound quality isn't that great.

dena b say:

Windows 7 Starter and Home Basic Editions do not come with the ability to change backgrounds or use desktop themes (Personailsation). This is not a fault with your system, it is the way it was designed to work by Microsoft. There are workarounds, see for example:

http://www.howtogeek.com/howto/16041/change-the-wallpaper-and-customization-settings-in-windows-7-starter-edition/ .

amrita m say:

Hi Detaill,

 

Welcome to Microsoft Answers!

 

 

By default, when ‘autoruns’ utility is downloaded and started, windows disc diagnostic user resolver is unchecked. The symbol for it is a red cross. You may put a check mark against it and keep it.

Scheduled task will start the Windows Disk Diagnostic User Resolver Wizard (Dfdwiz.exe) when a problem with a hard disk is detected.
