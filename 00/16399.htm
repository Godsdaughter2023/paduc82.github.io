bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

Regardless of where they may be stored, keyboard shortcuts are not transferable from one operating system to another. Due to OS differences as well as software differences there's too much potential for damage to be done. You'll have to recreate any that are important to you.

To help in that endeavor you can open your Normal.dotm template, then in the Print dialog;

  1. Open the Copies & Pages dropdown
  2. Select Microsoft Word
  3. In the Print What: dropdown select Key Assignments
  4. Print a hard copy &/or a PDF

Please understand that I'm not being critical, but am offering a suggestion based on experience. I've found over the years that it really isn't a good idea to use a WinWord Normal template on a Mac or vice-versa. It can be quite problematic, often more sooner than later :-)

Instead, allow the Mac version to work from its own native Normal.dotm template. Use Organizer to import;

  • Styles
  • AutoText
  • Macros

from the foreign Normal template to the native template. [Note that there also is a page in the Organizer for Toolbars, but toolbars aren't supported in O2016.]

Additionally, it sounds like you may have pointed Word 2016 to look to a location other than the Default for its Normal.dotm template. Just my opinion, but I'd be quite reluctant to do so... This release is installed in a completely different manner than previous versions based on Apple's sandboxing methodology. Both that & the current flaky nature of O2016, itself make me quite leery.

Welcome to the community forum. As a new participant, one of the things to know is to search the forum's postings to see if your question has already been asked and answered. In your case, it has. Here is an outline of the answer:

The first thing to do is to understand what the Service Host (svchost) is and what it does. This is explained in detail on the internet.

The next thing to do is to install a tool like Microsoft's own Process Explorer to get a detailed list of the processes that are being hosted.

The last step is to research each hosted process on the internet, in order to find out what they do. Then you can make an informed decision about whether or not your computer should be hosting that process.
