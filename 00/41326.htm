How To-Set the least restrictive permissions:

This is written to elevate your permissions on your secondary storage drive, substitute D: with your storage drive letter if it is not D:.

NOTE: This is not recommended for use on your main drive as serious damage to the OS could then occur very easily.

If you choose to use this information to change the permission settings on your main system drive you will be accepting any and all liability(s) and/or responsibility(s) for any and all changes you make including those that result in any causation of any and/or all damage(s) and/or loss of functionality and/or service of the device and/or damage to the system itself that does occur or that may occur due to the result of the change(s) made by you and/or any agents thereof. Any changes made to the device or system using the instruction(s) within this document will be at your own risk and discretion and heretofore you and you alone shall and will be responsible for any and all damages, remediation(s), and/or recovery(s) that may or do become necessary as a direct or indirect result thereof.

Open File Explorer.
Right-click your D: drive and click Properties.
Go to the Security tab and click [Advanced].
At the top, next to Owner, click Change.
Type your Windows username and click [OK].
Tick Replace owner on sub-containers and objects.
In the list of permission entries below, remove all the entries using the [Remove] button.
Click [Add] and click Select a principal.
Type Everyone and click [OK].
Tick Full Control and click [OK].  You should now see one permission entry granting Full Control to Everyone in the list of permissions.

Tick Replace all child object permission entries with inheritable permission entries from this object and click [OK].  This will propagate the new permissions to all the files and folders on your D: drive, which should take a couple seconds.  Any inaccessible files should be accessible when it is done.
