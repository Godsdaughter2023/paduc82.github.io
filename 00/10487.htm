dan at it associates say:

Are you getting the "invalid product key" message when you try to enter the product key into the activation wizard, or, are you getting the error messag when you try to do the automatic online activation?

 

If upon entry of the product key, then it could be a case of misreading product key characters (it's common to mix up the letter B with the numeral 8 for example), or using the wrong "flavor" of product key, meaning that you may be trying to activate the same product from one sales channel using a product key that will only work with the same product from another sales channel.  For example, an OEM license Office H&S 2007 product key will not work to activate a retail license Office H&S 2007 installation.

 

If the error comes when trying online activation, then the product key has been flagged by Microsoft's activation servers.  The two most common error messages are "this product has been activated too many times" and "invalid product key."  Too many times means the product key is valid but there have been too many activation attempts over too short of a time period, so a Telephone Activation will be needed.  Invalid product key upon activation attempt usually means that the product key is not genuine, meaning its construction follows the Microsoft algorithm but that it was created by software pirates with a "KeyGen" program and was never created by Microsoft, therefore it is not genuine.

 

You wrote, "I can't activate by telephone , is there any solution easier to use?"

 

Please explain why you cannot do a Telephone Activation.
