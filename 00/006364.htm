How to tether your phone to your laptop for Internet access - Microsoft Community

elizabeth at ms say:

How to tether your phone to your laptop for Internet access

This is a cool little feature that allows you to use your phone's data connection to access the Internet from your laptop. It's particularly useful if you're traveling and don't have easy access to a wireless connection for your laptop.

 Called Internet Sharing on your phone, note that not all Windows(r) phones have this available.

If you have a touch screen phone, tap Start > Programs > Internet Sharing.

If you have a non-touch screen phone, click Start > All Programs > Internet Sharing.


Under PC Connection, choose either USB or Bluetooth depending on how you want to connect your phone to your computer. (Note that if you’re phone doesn’t have Bluetooth capabilities, you’ll only see the option to connect through a USB cable.)

 
Make sure your phone is plugged into your laptop with a USB cable or that Bluetooth is enabled on both your phone and your computer, and then click Connect.

After several seconds, your laptop should be connected to the Internet.

To make sure the connection is working, try opening a web site on your laptop.

If this is the first time you’ve used Internet Sharing with your phone, you may need to install some drivers to your phone, which will happen automatically. 
When you use the Internet Sharing feature, your laptop is using your phone’s data connection to access email and the Internet. Depending on your data connection plan, you may incur extra charges. Contact your mobile operator if you have any questions.
While Bluetooth is useful for quick, simple wireless access if you don’t have your cable with you, be aware that you’ll probably use up your phone’s battery pretty quickly.
