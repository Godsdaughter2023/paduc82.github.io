erins. say:

I use this

 

=YEAR(TODAY())-YEAR(A2)-IF(DATE(YEAR(TODAY()),MONTH(A2),DAY(A2))<=TODAY(),0,1)

 

where A2 contains the birth date.  It subtract the birth year from today's year and then subtracts 1 if the birthday this year is greater than the current date (i.e., the birthday hasn't occured this year).

chakraview say:
I think you can use the below solution reliably.

Here is the algorithm:  Your final age should be NO_OF_YEARS and NO_OF_DAYS days. NO_OF_DAYS represents exact days after the previous year birthday instead of decimal values:

NO_OF_YEARS = difference of YEAR_PART(DOB) and YEAR_PART(CURRENT_DATE)

NO_OF_DAYS = difference of days in DOB_CURRENT_YEAR and CURRENT_DATE. Thus if this difference is negative, your DOB in the current year is still to arrive in the remaining days of the year, else the difference will be positive. If POSITIVE, then the NO_OF_YEARS have to be reduced by 1.

Here is the formula for obtaining NO_OF_DAYS:
=IF(DATEVALUE(TEXT(TODAY(),"mm/dd/yyyy"))-DATEVALUE(TEXT(CONCATENATE(TEXT(A1,"mm/dd/"),TEXT(TODAY(),"yyyy")),"mm/dd/yyyy"))<0,(DATEVALUE(TEXT(TODAY(),"mm/dd/yyyy"))-DATEVALUE(TEXT(CONCATENATE(TEXT(A1,"mm/dd/"),TEXT(TODAY(),"yyyy")-1),"mm/dd/yyyy"))),(DATEVALUE(TEXT(TODAY(),"mm/dd/yyyy"))-DATEVALUE(TEXT(CONCATENATE(TEXT(A1,"mm/dd/"),TEXT(TODAY(),"yyyy")),"mm/dd/yyyy"))))

Here is the formula for obtaining NO_OF_YEARS:
IF(DATEVALUE(TEXT(TODAY(),"mm/dd/yyyy"))-DATEVALUE(TEXT(CONCATENATE(TEXT(A1,"mm/dd/"),TEXT(TODAY(),"yyyy")),"mm/dd/yyyy"))<0,YEAR(A2)-YEAR(A1)-1,YEAR(A2)-YEAR(A1))


I have tested with few values and it returns correct values.

markinthebox say:

Deleting an email account is accomplished by going to Settings > email & accounts.  Tap and hold on the account you want to delete, and select "delete".

HOWEVER, note that the Windows Live account that you used to set-up your phone can not be deleted from the phone.  To remove that one, you'd have to reset your phone (which brings it back to the factory state).

lynnwymer say:

tracking employee time in Excel

I am using Excel to track employee hours and billable hours.  I present Time In, Time Out and calculate decimal hours. What I would like to do is be able to round off to quarter-hours. Can someone help me structure a formula that will do that?
hansv mvp say:

Let's say Time In is in column C and Time Out in column D, and Hours Worked in column E, starting in row 2.

Enter the following formula in E2:

=MROUND(24*MOD(D2-C2,1),1/4)

Format E2 as General, or as Number with 2 decimal places.

This formula can be filled down.
