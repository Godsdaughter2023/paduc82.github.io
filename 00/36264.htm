rohn007 say:

Why are computers complicated?

Technical Level : Basic

Summary
A desktop type computer is an unspecialized machine.  It allows you to do a virtually unlimited range of functions. Add to that, the simple fact that as a concept, computers are relatively new. There has not been enough time to "polish the rough edges".

Consider a very common, simple household machine, the toaster. All it does is burn bread. The machine is simple today, but it has had literally thousands of years to develop and polish the technology behind the idea to reach that level of apparent simplicity.

Details

You want simple, buy a toaster. 




Sorry, that is rather snide, but, really.

What computers can do for you is incredibly complex and computers are
still relatively "young" as a concept. What a toaster does is simple,
it burns bread. But the design and technology
that went into them is literally thousands of years old.  Ignore
everything that went into creating bread, making metal, electricity
(especially since the last 2 are also used in computers) just think
about all of the thought and effort that has gone into
the simple process of making toast:


  • Bread sitting next to a fire,
  • bread on a wooden stick,
  • bread on a metal stick,
  • bread on a metal grill over an electric element
  • bread inside a manual electric toaster that you had to time "in your head"
  • FINALLY bread in a pop up toaster in your kitchen


    (but you still have to manually select the darkness and it doesn‚Äôt work
    when you switch bread types, ie white bread slice vs a bagel vs muffins
  • In the near(?) future a toaster that will recognize the type of bread and adjust heat and baking time to meet your preference

You want a "simple" computer, they are out there. Buy a calculator,
buy a digital watch, buy a game console.  A game console is a computer
just as complex as your desktop computer but its design has been
specialized to do ONE complex
function with apparent simplicity. It allows you to play massively
complex games with a very simple user interface.



A desktop computer is like the "ultimate Swiss army knife" I saw on TV
(and found on Amazon).


http://www.amazon.com/dp/B001DZTJRQ/?tag=059-20




The "knife" can do 141 functions! The BODY of that knife is 9" long with

Your desktop/laptop computer is a general tool, like a Swiss Army knife.
It can allow you to do MANY very complex things. To name just a few:


  • play games (just like a game console)
  • word process
  • spreadsheets
  • statistics 
  • "surf" the internet
  • "VoIP" audio AND video phone calls
  • edit static pictures
  • edit audio
  • edit video

Your desktop computer is part of an "open" environment:


  • It can interchange parts from many manufacturers.
  • It can run programs from many companies.
  • It can even run many different "operating systems", such as various Windows and Linux versions.



That sort of functional flexibility, and relatively short design time
frame means that the design of the tool is still going to be incomplete.
The tool is going to be complex to use. There has not been enough time
to develop the apparent design simplicity that
is demonstrated in other tools.



There are comparatively "simple" computers like the Apple line. But,
they are a closed environment. They only take "official" apple parts and
can only run officially approved apple programs.  And since the
environment is "closed", Apple
can charge more money and make a much higher profit margin for the
company.
