Hi AnupamGoswami,

First of all has your father a valid Windows 10 license?
Is possible to transfer a Windows 10 license only if it is a RETAIL one.
To check which version is your PRODUCT KEY, you have to:

1. Digit "cmd" in windows search bar
2. Right click on "Command Prompt"
3. Select "Run as Administrator"
4. Digit "slmgr /dli" without quote and press ENTER
5. Read the MAIUSC string after "Description", if is RETAIL, you can transfer your license.

how do i export photos from my photo gallery to a memory card - Microsoft Community

ronevans0404 say:

how do i export photos from my photo gallery to a memory card

nephew downloaded pictures from his phone to Windows7 Photo Gallery. Would like to copy some of them to a memory card or flash drive to take them in for developing. Can't figure out how to pick and choose photos and to get them to the card or flashdrive.
volunteer j say:
nephew downloaded pictures from his phone to Windows7 Photo Gallery. Would like to copy some of them to a memory card or flash drive to take them in for developing. Can't figure out how to pick and choose photos and to get them to the card or flashdrive.
========================================
In Windows Live Photo Gallery...right click a thumbnail
or a selected group of thumbnails or even a folder and
from the menu...choose..."Copy".

You can multiple select the photos of your choice by
holding your Ctrl key while left clicking the thumbnails.

The flash drive or memory card should be recognized at...
Start / Computer / Devices With Removable Storage......
with a drive letter.

Right click the drive letter that represents your flash drive
or memory card and from the menu...choose..."Paste".

--

If you go directly to your Pictures folder to transfer the
files...when you right click the selected (highlighted)
thumbs or a complete folder...the menu will have an
option to 'Send To' and you can choose the drive letter
of the SD Card.

If you are receiving an error that states:

"Cannot copy XXXX: The directory or file cannot be created"

This occurs when too many files are added to the root
directory of the SD Card...the solution is to create folders
on the SD Card to copy the photos into.

(FWIW...you may need to remove one or more photos from
the SD Card to make room for the folders)
