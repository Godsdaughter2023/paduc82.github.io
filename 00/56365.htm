On what evidence are you basing your conclusion that your problem is not related to temperature?

From your description of your symptoms -- "There is no blue screen, no messages, nothing" when the computer shuts down -- you have a hardware problem of some sort.  The most obvious one is a heat related issue.

If you are confident that your computer is not actually overheating, then the likely cause is that a thermal sensor has failed and is incorrectly reporting an over-temperature condition. 

Running a tool such as Speccy -- as ElderN suggested -- might give you some insight about what is going on.

It's also possible that some other hardware component is failing.  For example, the power supply circuitry internal to the computer (i.e., not the "brick" that is plugged into the wall outlet) might have an intermittent (likely also heat-related) short circuit condition that would abruptly remove power from the computer.  This doesn't seem particularly likely, especially not with the repeatability you have described.

Based on your description, it seems much more likely that you have a hardware rather than software problem.  Hardware problems -- especially on laptops -- are not easily diagnosed without professional help. You probably should take your laptop to a good independent computer repair shop.  I wouldn't expect that a big chain store, a consumer electronics store, or an office supply store would be able to help much with the kind of symptoms you have described.
