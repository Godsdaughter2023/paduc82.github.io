john mcghie say:
  1. On the View menu, choose "Header and Footer".
  2. A "Header and Footer" tab will appear in the ribbon.  Click it to open it.
  3. In the Options section, ensure that "Different odd and even" is checked in each of your 5 sections.  That's what creates the left header and the right header.
  4. It is normal with a double-sided document to also check "Different first page" because you don't normally want either the left or the right header on the first page of each chapter.

You need to understand the following:

  • In a word document, all of the section-level formatting such as headers and footers and page size is contained within the section break.
  • The section break that controls your section is the one that FOLLOWS the text it controls.
  • Each section has up to six headers and footers: First, Left, and Right header, and First, Left, and Right footer.
  • When you cluck "Link to previous" you are linking to the previous header or footer OF THIS KIND.  So if you enable Same as Previous on a Left header, it will attempt to replicate the LEFT header from the section before the one you are in.

Once you have set this up, you can then choose to have left headers running through all of the document, while the right headers may be different in each section.

If you want to copy text out of the body into the header, you can use a StyleRef field.  Set the type of text you want to copy (say, a sub-heading) in a style you are not using for any other kind of text (say "Heading 2").  Then insert a StyleRef field in your header (Insert>Field...) with a parameter of "Heading 2".  Each time a different subheading appears in the document, it will automatically be copied to the header of that page.

Once you get this working, you really will be a Word Expert :-)  Most people can't do headers or footers at all!

7) Press 'Apply'  then 'Close' and then close the other 'Network and Sharing Center' window.
