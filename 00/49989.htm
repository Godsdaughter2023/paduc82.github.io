camnweez say: I think I figured it out.  I noticed that the color-changy problem didn’t happen to every image.  Like I mentioned above, it seemed to only happen with GIFs and BMPs.  But, I also noticed that it didn’t happen to every BMP file, just some of them.  After further investigation, I believe it has to do with the color depth of the image.  The images that would exhibit the problem are 256-color images, whereas the ones that worked fine were 24-bit images.

 

I’m getting into the realm of speculation here, but I guess that when you copy part of a 256-color image and then paste it back in, it filters out and replaces the colors that aren’t part of the 256 colors.  I don’t know why it would display different colors in the first place, though.  Whatever is happening, it is definitely bad behavior andshould be fixed .

 

I didn’t see any way to immediately identify the color depth of an image.  If you view the image properties in Paint, it doesn’t offer that information.  But, I did notice one thing. When I would do a “Save As” on some of the files, the “Save as type” would default to "256 Color Bitmap", and some of them would default to "24-bit Bitmap".  This seems to indicate which the image is.

 

Also, if you check the file properties (not in Paint), and look in the Details tab, under the Image section there is an item called “Bit depth”.  The problematic images have a bit depth of 8, whereas the images that worked fine have a bit depth of 24.

 

To eliminate the problem , do a “Save As” of an 8-bit BMP (256-color BMP) to a 24-bit BMP.  Then you can copy and paste and edit to your heart’s content.

 

Paint doesn’t offer choices for saving different bit-depths of GIFs, so I don’t really know how to solve that problem.
