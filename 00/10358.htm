Hi Emmalie,

Thank you for posting your concern here on Microsoft Community.

 I would be glad to assist you regarding your concern of eliminating junk/phishing emails on your account.

The Junk Email Filter is a component of Outlook.com that is on by default, and which processes each and every incoming email that is received, regardless of the source or type of email account.

Any email account that Outlook.com is configured to use will be able to take advantage of the Junk E-mail filtering. This includes accounts using Exchange’s MAPI or OA, IMAP, and POP3 protocols, as well as Hotmail and Lotus Domino accounts when using the DAMO Connector.The Junk E-mail filter is turned on by default in Outlook for every email account. By default, it’s set to Low, so that while it can filter out the most obvious spam, it won’t catch it all.

Outlook.com has several features to stop receiving junk/phishing messages. In order to filter your relevant emails from the junk ones, I would suggest you to follow these steps given below:

Option A. Block a sender or a domain name
1. Sign in to your account. Go to inbox.
2. Click the Gear icon located at the upper-right side of the page.
3. Select More mail settings.
4. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.
5. Click Blocked senders.
6. Enter the email address or domain on the box labeled Blocked email address or domain: and click Add to list >>.

Option B. Set the filtering level of the messages

Please follow these steps to setup your junk e-mail filter in Outlook.com:

1. Sign in to the Outlook.com website with your Microsoft account.
2. In the upper-right corner of the page, click Gear icon, and then click More mail settings.
3. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.
4. Under Filters and reporting, select the options that you want, and then click Save.

Notes:

> If you choose Exclusive for your junk e-mail filter, only messages from e-mail addresses on your Contacts or Allowed senders lists appear in your Inbox. Messages from senders not on those lists are automatically sent to the Junk e-mail folder.

> You should occasionally check your junk e-mail folder to make sure that good messages are not sent there by mistake.

Option C. Sweep function- This feature allows all received or even future emails coming from the specific sender be deleted. Here are the steps to activate the Sweep option:

1. Sign in to your Outlook.com account. Go to Inbox.
2. Check the specific sender you would like to filter.
3. On the upper part, click Sweep
4. Choose Delete all from
5. On the pop-up box, just click Delete all.
Note: You can also block future messages from that sender by checking the box beside Also block future messages.

Option D. Rules - we can create a rule that can block a specific word from the subject of the spam email .In order to create a rule that will block junk mail please do the following:   

1. Sign in to your Outlook.com account with your Microsoft account.
2. Click on Gear icon then More mail settings... in the upper right corner of the page.
3. Under the Customizing Hotmail section, click on Rules for sorting new messages.
4. Click on the New button.
5. Under Step 1: Which messages do you want this rule to apply to:
                                     a. First drop-down menu - choose Subject
                                     b. Second drop-down menu - choose contains
                                     c. Third field - input the word you would like to block
6. Under Step 2: What action do you want to apply, click on Delete these messages
7. Click Save button.

Microsoft recommends that you follow these guidelines to help reduce junk e-mail:

• Don't reply to junk e-mail, even to ask to be removed from the sender's mailing list. The sender may simply use that response to confirm that your e-mail address is valid, and continue to send you junk e-mail. To report the message as junk e-mail, select the message, click Junk.
• Remove your e-mail address from any web page, newsgroup, or bulletin board available on the Internet.
• To remove your e-mail address from newsletters or mailing lists that are on your safe senders list, you can click Unsubscribe. Microsoft notifies the people who created the newsletter to stop sending it to you.

Note: If you receive junk e-mail that looks as if it was sent from your Hotmail account, someone may have spoofed (Sending messages with fake or stolen account information) your account information.

Hope This Helps.

teylyn say:

Hello,

formatting and cell value are not the same thing. You can format a cell to have a - sign in front of the number, but that number will still be a positive number if the underlying value is positive. Just formatting the number can be very misleading. 

Having said that, you can use the Find and Replace command to find cells with a specific format. Just click the Format button and specify what format to look for. Then you can replace that format with another format. Again, just click the format button and specify the format. Then click Replace All. 

So, replace custom format 0.00 \D\r with custom format -0.00 and replace custom format 0.00 \C\r with custom format 0.00.

This will change the APPEARANCE of the numbers only, not the underlying value.

This little demo file outputs an invoice report, restricted to the current invoice, using the following code:

    Dim strTo As String
    Dim strSubject As String
    Dim strMessageText As String
    
    Me.Dirty = False
    
    strTo = Me.Email
    strSubject = "Invoice Number " & Me.InvoiceNumber
    strMessageText = Me.Customer.Column(1) & ":" & _
        vbNewLine & vbNewLine & _
        "Your latest invoice is attached." & _
        vbNewLine & vbNewLine & _
        "Customer Accounts Department, Widget Supply Company"


    DoCmd.SendObject ObjectType:=acSendReport, _
        ObjectName:="rptInvoice", _
        OutputFormat:=acFormatPDF, _
        To:=strTo, _
        Subject:=strSubject, _
        MESSAGETEXT:=strMessageText, _
        EditMessage:=True

However, the report cannot be filtered, i.e. a string expression cannot be assigned its Filter property by means of a WhereCondition argument, as would be done with the OpenReport method, as the SendObject method does not have such a property.  Instead, its RecordSource query is restricted by referencing a control in the form as a parameter.
