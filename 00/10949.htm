Again, this is for Word 2016 stand-alone, not Word for Office (if that makes any difference); the selection boxes down below didn't give me that option.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

Quotation marks are not terminal punctuation (like periods, question marks, and exclamation points). If they were treated as such, you would have a problem with sentences such as these:

"Wow!" said Jack.

"Actually," she replied, "I preferred the first version."

Word also will not AutoCorrect if the previous sentence ends with a number. This is an unintended consequence of a change (many versions back, presumably in response to requests from users) not to AutoCorrect the beginnings of numbered lists.
