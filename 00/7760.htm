jack cook - aka help_line say:

So as long as you have not deleted your Microsoft ID you can get it all back fairly easily Vicky.

 

You will need to hard reset your device and set it up again using the same Live ID that is currently being used.  When this is done, it will see it as a new device and will place the phone back in your account.

 

If you had the automatic backup enabled you will be given the opportunity to restore the backup (it will use your data connection). READ this article before you do anything just so you understand the process:

http://www.windowsphone.com/en-us/how-to/wp8/basics/restore-a-backup-to-my-phone?signin=true

 

There is an app called Reinstaller that I used once and was pretty happy with it to get back the apps I wanted to reinstall.

http://www.windowsphone.com/en-us/store/app/reinstaller/b9d1115b-2c0d-4e5d-afc6-fa6dcd84dda4

 

From your PC, you can also get apps back by going to windowsphone.com and "hover" on your name in the upper right hand corner.  Move down on the list that appears and click on Purchase History. This is a good way to do it once you setup WiFi on your device after the hard reset.

 

Personally I would rather just set it up fresh (not using the backup) since all your calendar and contact information comes back because it is all tied to your Live account. It will give you an opportunity to pick just those apps that are important and not reload the "junk" we all accumulate over time that we never use. I like reinstalling the apps to my device using the PC as described in the paragraph above. It is just a personal thing that I happen to like.
