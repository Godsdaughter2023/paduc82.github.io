Please follow the steps:

  1. Open the desktop, then tap or click the Internet Explorer icon on the taskbar.

  2. Tap or click the Favourites star.

  3. From the drop-down menu, tap or click Import and export.

  4. In the Import/Export Settings dialogue box, select Export to a file, then tap or click Next.

  5. Select Favourites (and any other settings) that you'd like to export, then tap or click Next.

  6. Select the folders of favourites that you want to export, then tap or click Next. Internet Explorer automatically exports all subfolders.

  7. By default, Internet Explorer creates a file called bookmark.htm in your Documents folder. If you want to store the exported favourites to somewhere other than Documents (such as a disc or a flash drive), tap or click Browse, then select a different drive. Tap or click Save.

  8. Click Finish.

Hope it helps.

ronaldo r. say:

Hi roguedyer,

 

If I understand your concern correctly, you want your Outlook.com to look like a mail client where you can manage your incoming emails.

 

When you add an account to Outlook.com, you will be asked if you want to have a separate folder for your incoming emails. Yes, you can have a separate folder for that account. You can also have them on your Inbox. You can also set rules to move incoming emails to a certain folder. You can manage all incoming messages from the other account to Outlook.com.

 

However, it will not be like a mail client that manages 2 totally different accounts. You can’t have 2 sent folders, 2 junk folders and 2 outbox.

 

It’s just like forwarding all emails received from other accounts to your Outlook.com.

 

If you want to configure your Outlook.com to your Outlook Mail client, the rules you have will still apply as the folders you have in Outlook.com will still show on the mail client.

 

I hope these answers your concern. Should you have further questions, feel free to contact us back.

 

Thank you.

Can I open an access form from outlook - Microsoft Community

tomg690 say:

Can I open an access form from outlook

I would like to create a hyperlink that will open a specific form in an Access database directly from an Outlook contacts notes field.  I do not want the database to open to the form on start up only when clicking the hyperlink.
karl timmermans [outlook mvp 2012-2018] say:

Simplistic short answer: You can't have a link to a specific Access form without invoking the Access program using the runtime with applicable commandline runtime options.

 

What you are able to do depends entirely what the exact nature of the question is

1) Open a blank form related to a given database or

2) Open a specific Access form populated with information related to the contact item in question

The "hyperlink" that would be applicable would be in the form of:

file:///path/programname commandline runtime options

 

So if your question is #1 - opening a specific form for a predefined database using the Access Runtime - answer is yes using the Access Runtime and applicable commandline options

 

If your question is #2 - open a specific pre-populated form with data related to the contact item in question - seriously doubt that would be possible with any out of the box runtime options using the MS Access runtime since that by it's very definition requires that one or parameters also get passed to invoke a (SQL Select) statement against the underlying table/query attached to the Access form to retrieve the desired information corresponding to the contact in question.

 

All that said, not an Outlook related question since it has to do with what you can and cannot do simply invoking the Access Runtime (versus using the full Access program). If it is possible to pass one or more "ad-hoc" commandline options (in addition to the pre-defined options) using the Access runtime - you would need VBA code called by initial macro to read the command line and then process everything accordingly

 

As Diane stated - place to ask that question is in the Access forum

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/office/forum/access?page=1&tab=all&tm=1349505514456
