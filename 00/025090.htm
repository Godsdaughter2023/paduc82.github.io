indyblue93 say:

I was having a similar problem. I could only get two on the top row and two on the bottom to update. I found out that there is a problem using Mail Merge if you have a tablet PC or have a tablet driver installed, such as for a Wacom tablet. This work-around did the trick for me. In summary, don't use your mouse to choose the "Update Labels" button, use your keyboard.

If you are using the Mail Merge Wizard for Labels, proceed as directed until you get to the point where you are instructed to update the labels. Instead of selecting the "Update Labels" button with your mouse, press the F6 key at the top of your keyboard. This will highlight the Mail Merge Wizard window title bar. Then use the down arrow key to navigate to the "Update Labels" button in the Wizard window. Press ENTER. This should copy the Address Block or fields you formatted in the first cell of the label document to all the other label cells on the page, and should appear after the <<Next Record>> that was placed in each cell when you selected your recipients from your Contacts List (I was using Outlook contacts).

You can now toggle the Preview button on the ribbon to see your actual contacts on the page. It will probably only show your last record unless you repeatedly click the left arrow button located within the Preview Results box on the ribbon. If you are satisfied, complete the merge. If you want to see a document with the contact info on the labels, choose Edit Individual Labels and then "all records." You should end up with a label document you can save and print as you choose.

Just as a note, I had difficulty positioning my label fields the way I wanted (such as centered vertically and horizontally). The best approach was to not use Address Block but enter individual fields, and then use the Paragraph Line Spacing commands to finagle them into the best position.
