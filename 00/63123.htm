lak611 say: I solved my issue.  I clicked Start>Accessories>Command Prompt and then typed chkdsk C: /f /r.  Then I clicked Start, typed run, and then typed msconfig.  I clicked boot, and then checked the box to boot in safe mode.  Chkdsk ran, and the PC booted in safe mode.  After it booted, I clicked Start, typed run, typed msconfig, and clicked boot.  This time I booted in normal mode.  After doing these steps, my backup was successful.

Hi terrafrost,

Usually on laptop it has multiple hard drives, one of those drives is in a docking station. This goes back to the whole removable media thing. If you remove your laptop from the docking station then a dynamic disk volume that spanned the laptop’s internal hard drive and the docking station’s hard drive will broke. Hence it doesn’t support Dynamic disks

Dynamic disk cannot work on Firmware, USB or removable disk. It is possible only to convert a Firmware drive in Vista Ultimate. Also Windows Vista Ultimate and Windows Vista Enterprise editions support spanning and striping dynamic disks, but not mirroring.

Dynamic Disk Hardware Limitations
