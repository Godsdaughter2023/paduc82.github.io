goodthings2life™ say:

Windows Defender is built-in to Surface RT (as well as Windows 8), but quite frankly, Surface RT makes it completely irrelevant.

 

Why?

 

Because no traditional desktop apps can run on a Surface RT device, which more-or-less guarantees that malware cannot be installed. Furthermore, the WinRT platform is a curated market, so any bogus apps are quickly dealt with... assuming they make it past the screening process in the first place, but those apps are also sandboxed to ensure that they can't cause harm to the system itself. Since these apps can't run on startup, even a bad app won't cause problems once you reboot, which allow a clean/safe removal path. Furthermore, IE is protected by SmartScreen, which has proven extremely effective in blocking access to rogue web sites (along with limited Flash support and banning Java).

 

On a traditional Windows 8 system, you can run unrestricted Flash and Java and desktop applications, but you get all of the other benefits. On those systems, Windows Defender and SmartScreen, are-- in my opinion AND experience-- perfectly sufficient to protect your system.

 

I realize I'm not a Microsoft employee, but I am an IT guy of 20 years, so I hope this will bring clarity to the discussion.
