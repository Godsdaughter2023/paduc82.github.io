Nobody here can answer that question for you, because it all depends on your browsing habits, and what you have to lose if your protection fails. You obviously don’t need any protection at all if you’re not connected to the internet. And Windows Defender is certainly “adequate” if you spend all of your online time at 2 or 3 trusted sites. But if you’re not strict with your browsing habits, and you have important personal files and/or personal information stored on your PC, then it would make perfect sense to shop around for an AV app that gives you better protection (AV apps aren’t evaluated in terms of adequate vs. inadequate, they’re ranked according their protection (malware detection/removal) scores).

If you dig a little deeper here in the forum, you’ll see that some of the folks who came here looking for help with their malware issues didn’t fare so well with Windows Defender, and might not be so willing to endorse it. But positive or negative, opinions are just opinions, and they can’t really settle the question about how well Windows Defender protects you relative to the third-party alternatives. So if you do decide to look for something better, the only reliable guidance is going to come from people who’ve put these apps to the test under identical conditions to see how well they really measure up. Here’s an article on the subject from someone who understands the need for AV testing; and who tries hard to be “fair and balanced” in his assessments; and who’s not afraid to go beyond the abstract talking points and make some concrete recommendations:

http://www.pcmag.com/article2/0,2817,2388652,00.asp

Follow the below steps to change the protected view settings:

1.         First launch any Office program such as Microsoft Word and then click on File menu present in the ribbon.
2.         Now click on Options tab present in the left sidebar.
3.         It'll open Options window. Now click on "Trust Center" tab given at the end in left-side pane and then click on "Trust Center Settings" button. It'll open a new window to customize Trust Center settings.
4.         Click on "Protected View" tab and in right-side pane.
5.         Enable Protected View for files originating from the Internet Enable Protected View for files that are located in potentially unsafe locations Enable Protected View for Outlook attachments.
6.         All options are enabled by default. Disable all given options to turn off Protected View completely and click on OK button to save the changes.
