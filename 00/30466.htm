a. user say:

NTFS is the standard file system of Windows NT including Windows 2000 , Windows XP and all their successors to date.

http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ntfs

 

Cannot lock current drive means that the drive is in use --- open TaskManager and you will see the active processes by clicking on the processes tab.

 

When you restart Windows, chkdsk will stop the processes until the scan is completed.  The volume will be locked on a reboot so all files can be check for errors and fixed.  /F fixes errors on the volume.  /R repairs damage it finds on the hard drive.

The volume must be locked. If CHKDSK cannot lock the volume, it offers to check it the next time the computer starts
