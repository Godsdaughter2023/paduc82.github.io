Greetings jcanker!  Welcome to the Windows Phone forums!

You are attempting to use the phone as a middle-man for getting your contacts from Hotmail to Outlook.  Why not just do this directly, and leave the phone out of the equation?   Try these steps:

  1. Log on to your Windows Live Hotmail account.
  2. In the left pane, click Contacts.
  3. On the Windows Live Hotmail toolbar, click Options, then clickExport Contacts.
  4. Under Export contacts, click Export contacts.
  5. When prompted to open or save the file, click Save.
  6. Select a location to save the file, and then click Save.               Note   The default file name is WLMContacts.csv.
  7. In Outlook, on the File menu, click Import and Export.
  8. Click Import from another program or file, and then click Next.
  9. Click Comma Separated Values (DOS), and then click Next.
  10. Click Browse to locate the file you saved in step 6, select the file, and then clickOK.
  11. Under Options, choose an option for how to process duplicate contacts, and then clickNext.
  12. Click the Outlook contacts folder where you want the imported contacts to be saved, and then clickNext.  Note   Many people only have one Contacts folder. However, if you have multiple contacts folders, you should pick the folder where you want your Windows Live Hotmail contacts to be saved.
  13. Click Finish.

I hope this helps!

      • The attached template, its folder, or its share is missing.
      • The attached template has been moved.
      • The attached template has been renamed.
      • A linked file referenced by the document is missing, has been moved or renamed.
      When a Word template is missing, Windows will try to locate the missing template multiple times. The process of locating a missing template may cause a Word document to take longer than expected to open.

If Word starts very slowly, or other operations, such as opening or printing documents, also are very slow, this may be due to any one or more of:
