A Content Management system is not a good place for 'dotm/dotx templates'. Microsoft Word can't create a new document and have the 'attached template' reside inside a CM system. Hi Melvin,

 

My apologies for not being able to reply to your post in a timely manner. Let me assist you with your query about POP folder.

 

Please be informed that a POP folder is automatically created when the account is accessed using a mail client like Windows Live Mail or a mobile device through a POP3 (Post Office Protocol 3) server.

 

POP3 is designed to delete mail on the server as soon as the user has downloaded it. However, some implementations allow users or an administrator to specify that mail be saved for some period of time. Messages are sent there when they are deleted using a mail client and kept there until the account holder verifies the deletion as a security feature.

 

We can prevent losing the emails by selecting the option Don't let another program delete messages from Outlook. This way, your emails will not be deleted from your inbox permanently after downloading to your mail program. You can follow these steps to do so:

 

1. Sign in to your account using this link.

2. Click the Cog icon then choose More mail settings.

3. Under Managing your account, select Pop and deleting downloaded messages.
3. Tick on Don't let another program delete messages from Outlook.

4. Click Save.

rohn007 say:
Maybe ...

It depends on exactly what type of license you bought, how you bought it, and maybe how your old machine died.

To install Office you need 2 things, you absolutely need your unique 25 character Product Key, and a matching generic installation file for the appropriate Office bundle.

The key is your "Product Key" (pun intended). If you bought a Product Key Card (PKC), a factory installed and activated "OEM" license, or a "1-computer" online download you are out of luck. Those types of licenses do not allow you to transfer them to new computers.

If you bought a retail boxed CD 'FPP' or "2-computer' license then you are in better luck. This type of license allows you to install on 2 computers at the same time AND to transfer those installations to new computers.  But you need the Product key.  If you bought the Boxed CD, you need the package (which apparently you don't). If you bought an online download, you can return to the web site you bought it from  and use your email address as the login ID.  

Depending on how your old computer crashed, you may be able to temporarily connect the old HD to a new computer so that you can use a freeware tool to extract the product key.

Once you have your product key you can get the installation file from links we can provide. But the links are no use without the product key.

That is a common mistake.

Legal copies of Office Pro 2010 (NOT 2013!) are only licensed to be installed on 2 computers at the same time. You do have the right to uninstall and transfer to a new computer.
