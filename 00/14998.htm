taken from : http://www.pptalchemy.co.uk/vbasamples.html 

Shuffle Part of Presentation

You can use rnd in a similar way to shuffle a section of a presentation. Input the highest and lowest slide numbers to be affected.

Sub shufflerange()
Dim Iupper As Integer
Dim Ilower As Integer
Dim Ifrom As Integer
Dim Ito As Integer
Dim i As Integer
Iupper = InputBox("What is the highest slide number to shuffle")
Ilower = InputBox("What is the lowest slide number to shuffle")
If Iupper > ActivePresentation.Slides.Count Or Ilower < 1 Then GoTo err
For i = 1 To 2*Iupper 
Randomize
Ifrom = Int((Iupper - Ilower + 1) * Rnd + Ilower)
Ito = Int((Iupper - Ilower + 1) * Rnd + Ilower)
ActivePresentation.Slides(Ifrom).MoveTo (Ito)
Next i
Exit Sub
err:
MsgBox "Your choices are out of range", vbCritical
End Sub

then just Call shufflerange at any "initialize" sub

norman david jones say:

Hi Ashish,

Try this formula

=INDEX($A$1:$A$6,MATCH(TRUE,INDEX($D$1:$D$6>0,,),0),1)

In order also to handle the vice versa case indicated in the title of Bob's question, perhaps:

    =INDEX($A$1:$A$6,MATCH($A$1<$A$6,INDEX($D$1:$D$6>0,,),0),1)

amit tandon say:

Try below:

=INDEX(A1:A6,MATCH(TRUE,INDEX(SIGN(D1)<>SIGN(D1:D6),,),0))

This will return the value from column A at the point of FIRST CHANGE of sign after D1. This will cover both -ive to +ive OR +ive to -ive, that is the first instance of sign change AFTER D1. This may be per your query "when a range goes from negative to positive OR vice-versa.
