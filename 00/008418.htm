Unable to view TOC on the navigation Pane - Microsoft Community

bhimabi say:

Unable to view TOC on the navigation Pane

My navigation pane works fine in that it shows every heading after my TOC. But I want to see my Cover Page and TOC appear as a heading on that pane. How do i do that?

The picture on the left is the how it is and right is how i want it to be

jay freedman say:

You can add the TOC's heading to the Navigation pane as follows:

  • Click in the Table of Contents heading and press Ctrl+Shift+S to open the Apply Style dialog. By default, the References > Table of Contents command applies the TOC Heading style to that heading. The style has an outline level of Body Text, which means it doesn't appear in the Nav pane.
  • Click the Modify button in the dialog.
  • In the Modify Style dialog, click "New documents based on this template" and click Format > Paragraph.
  • In the Paragraph dialog, click the Outline Level dropdown and choose Level 1.
  • OK all dialogs.

Because there is no heading on the cover page that literally says "Cover Page", you can't get that in the Nav pane. I don't think you want a visible heading that says that, and making it Hidden means that again the Nav pane won't show it.

If the reason you want these entries on the Nav pane is just to go to those headings quickly, an alternative is to use keyboard shortcuts. Obviously, Ctrl+Home that goes to the beginning of the document will take you to the cover page. There is a command GotoTableOfContents, which doesn't have a default keyboard shortcut but you can assign one. Go to File > Options > Customize Ribbon > Customize. In the Customize Keyboard dialog, select All Commands on the left and GotoTableOfContents on the right, click in the "Press new shortcut key" box, and press the shortcut you want to assign (Ctrl+Alt+Shift+T should be available). Click the Assign button and then Close.
