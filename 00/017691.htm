nsilmike say:
Is MSE safe for shoping, banking and make payments by Internet ?
Let me add to the above...
MSE is a software application that runs on your PC that monitors other files in real-time.  It has the ability to catch and block malware (http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malware) that is in its internal list (which is updated roughly every 24 hours if you are online.)

So, your question is a bit off the mark...  MSE, in combination with a good firewall such as the windows firewall, can protect you from many threats.  What it cannot do is protect you from unsafe computing in all cases.  For instance, if you do online banking or online shopping, you should always make sure it's a legitimate website, and that the connection is encrypted (look for https -not http- in the beginning of the address in the browser when you are doing sensitive transactions.)  If the connection is not encrypted, then a server in the middle (somewhere between you and the bank or shop...) can intercept your information.  Nothing about this is preventable or detectable by MSE or your firewall- it involves no malware on your PC.  Its something the user has to be watch and control.

a. user say:

Hi,

You may try the following steps below to check if the issue is a software related issue.


Perform a soft reset. Press and hold the volume down and Power button until your phone vibrates and restarts. Note that you should not touch the device screen during this process (not even when the 'slide down to power off' prompt appears). After performing a soft reset try to check if the issue persists.

If a soft reset didn't work, try to perform a hard reset instead. You can reset your phone under Settings: Settings  > about > reset your phone. Make sure to back up all of your files before performing a hard reset. 

Go to Settings  > Update and security > Backup >More options > Back up now.


If performing a soft reset and hard reset didn't work, try to use the Windows Device Recovery Tool. Start by downloading and installing Windows Device Recovery Tool (for Windows 10, 8.x and 7).

When the installation has finished, start the application and the main screen will appear:

The procedure to restore your phone using Windows Device Recovery Tool is as follows:

  1. Connect the phone to your PC (using the USB cable that came with the phone).
  2. After a few seconds the PC should find the phone and you'll see the Phone Selection screen. Click your phone in this screen to select it.
  3. After a few seconds more the latest available firmware version for your device will be shown. Click the 'Install software'/'Reinstall software' button to flash your phone
Windows Device Recovery Tool will then download and install the latest firmware + OS available for your phone

Note! Your phone will be reset to factory status when using this tool.

If all the following steps didn't work, it will be best to bring your phone to the nearest Lumia Care Center in your location to have it check by a technician. To check the nearest Lumia Care Center in your location click this link.

Let us know how it goes.
