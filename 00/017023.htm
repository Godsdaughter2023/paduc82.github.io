There is no automatic method to align the body text lines when they're mixed with headings, lists, and other items that have different line heights and paragraph spacings. alun jones say:

There are several tools available for embedding soft-coded subtitles into MP4 files, and although I've once or twice managed to get the Movies & TV app to recognise that these subtitles exist (thereby giving me a "1." option when requesting subtitles), they don't actually display inside the Movies & TV app, unlike separate SRT files.

You can see what I do to embed the subtitles in the MP4 files in this post.

Those same applications can be used to extract the subtitles into an SRT file.

To use FFMPEG to extract the subtitles from "foo.mp4" into "foo.srt", for example:

ffmpeg.exe -i foo.mp4 -codec:s srt foo.srt

Using MP4Box is harder, as you have to get the track ID out first:

MP4Box.exe -info foo.mp4

MP4Box.exe -srt <trackID> foo.mp4 -out foo.srt

("<trackID>" will often be "3", because 1 and 2 are audio and video respectively).

Once the subtitles are pulled out into the SRT file, you can use Movies & TV to play the MP4 file, and then load the subtitles. I haven't yet found a way to tell the Movies & TV app to play the MP4 files with subtitles from the start.

4.       Click on the new key Windows Search.

5.       On the Edit menu, point to New, and then click DWORD Value.

6.       Type PreventIndexingOutlook for the name of the DWORD, and then press Enter.

7.       Right-click PreventIndexingOutlook, and then click Modify.

8.       In the Value data box, type 1 to enable the registry entry, and then click OK.

9.       Exit Registry Editor, and then restart Outlook.

Ensure Windows Search Service is Started

Disable Server Assisted Search

1.       HKEY_CURRENT_USER\software\Microsoft\office\16.0\outlook\search
