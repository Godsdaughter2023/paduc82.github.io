The 5GHz is the radio frequency, not the connection speed.

  • 802.11a is up to 54mbps and can use 5GHz frequency.
  • 802.11g uses 2.4GHz.
  • 802.11n uses 2.4 or 5GHz and up to 150mbps.
  • 802.11ac uses 5GHz and up to 866.7mbps

There is a nice chart here: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/IEEE_802.11 

You can usually select the allowed Wi-Fi protocols in the configuration settings of your wireless adapter in Windows.

I recently came across what seems to me to be a counter-intuitive approach to better Wi-Fi bandwidth. Running the Access Point power output at as LOW of a power setting as is practical will actually improve bandwidth. I have tried this with my wife's PC, which was physically closest to one Wi-Fi router, which was previously set to power level 10. I came across this recommendation on a forum where someone was connecting Wi-Fi in a corporate boardroom.
