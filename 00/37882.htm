FWIW.....Regardless of anything that I or anyone else tells
you ...the best bet is to "Always" maintain current backups
of *all* your personal files...photos, documents, ect...on
external media. (Personally I archive "all" my personal files
on two different external hard drives and on Data DVDs)

First...if you have Picasa and or Photo Gallery open when
you navigate to your Pictures folder and try to rename a
folder...the warning could be because those same folders
are currently being accessed in Picasa and Photo Gallery.

You do not have three photo storage locations...Photo
Gallery and Picasa are simply Photo Viewers / Editors.

Copies of your photos are not archived within those apps.

And....uninstalling either or both programs will not remove
your photos that you have saved in your Pictures folder/s.

Your actual photos would be archived on your hard drive...
more than likely in your Pictures folder...or other folders
of your choice.

However...Photo Gallery does maintain a proprietary folder
that contains original versions of your edited files for the
purpose of reverting back to the unedited version.

If there is no chance you would want to revert from your
edited versions back to the originals you could delete the
files in the Original Images folder.

in Win 7, you can find the Original Images folder at the
following path:

C:\Users\%Username%\AppData\Local\Microsoft\Windows
Photo Gallery\Original Images
