joeu2004 say:

PapaDick wrote:

May i ask if you know historically when it was decided to implement unary operators and thus abandon the rules of algebra and older programming styles like Fortran?

Unary minus has always had higher precedence in Excel, IIRC. (And Multiplan before that.) It might have been following the market leaders at the time, Visicalc and Lotus 123. I don't remember.

Excel did not "abandon" any rules. Every programming language determines its own precedence rules. For example, APL had no precedence rules. Operations were simply performed left to right (or was it right to left; I forget). So 2+3*4 (left to right) results in 20, not 14.

PS.... Note that VBA precedence is different. -3^2 is 9 in Excel, but -3^2 is -9 in VBA. So the precedence choice in Excel seems to be a purposeful decision.

I started at position 2 to skip the quote at the beginning.

=DATEVALUE(MID(A1,2,10))
