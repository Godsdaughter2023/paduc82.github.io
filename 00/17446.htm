If that does not resolve this issue, I'd try resetting your Wi-Fi back to factory defaults and reconfiguring it.  This will sometimes resolve connectivity issues.  We have a router at work that we've had to do this with a few times.  It doesn't really make sense because nothing has changed on it, but it seems to stop working.  By doing a reset to factory defaults and reconfiguring, that works.

If that doesn't work, I'd try deleting your home Wi-Fi network from your Touch2, soft reset and then try finding your network, reconfiguring it, etc.

Finally, if that does not fix it and your comfortable doing one, I'd try a hard reset of your Touch2.

If the hard reset does not work, it's probably time to purchase a new Wi-Fi router.

I hope this works!

Dunno what Microsoft is actually using, but there are multiple ways to triangulate a phones position using towers.

You can check what tower your connected to and give a radius around that.
You can check 2 towers and give an approximate location trough the overlaps of their ranges.
Or 3 towers with their overlap, each tower added making the location data more accurate, but considering in absolute best case a tower's range is like 50-70 km, the overlap region can be quite large.

With more advanced techniques, you can find approximate range from a tower by also calculating in the latency, narrowing the range you phone is away from a tower, depending on the geography and the amount of towers you get a signal from, you can narrow it down to near GPS accuracy, but only in best case scenario's

But considering how thin some providers spread their towers, the chances you get enough data from more then 1 tower gets slim.
