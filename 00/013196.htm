Dependent on your accounts reputation these are the limits

The Hotmail limit for outgoing email messages is 300 (three hundred) messages per day. Maximum 100 per email.

The limit is lower for new accounts and when Windows Live Hotmail has spotted what it deems suspicious activity
(a large and sudden increase in outgoing messages may indicate your account has been taken over, for example)

How Many To, Cc and Bcc Recipients Windows Live Hotmail Allows Per Message

You can add up to 100 (one hundred) recipients per message in Windows Live Hotmail. 
Again, suspicious activity can lead to a temporarily lower limit (as low as 10 (ten) recipients).

If you’ve been notified that you have hit some limit on a Microsoft product, it’s almost certainly part of an effort to reduce spam. 
You can also increase your limit simply by continuing to use Hotmail and other Hotmail services normally.  
It only takes a few days of normal activity to increase the limits—that gives us enough time to detect automated spam machines and accounts that have been hacked. 
We’re constantly working to make things worse for spammers and better for real people.

The send mail limit depends on your reputation within Hotmail. 
The system needs a few days to build your reputation. 
So, there are stricter limitations on new accounts or accounts that have engaged in activity that is representative of abusers sending spam.

If you are a new user, a low sending quota is a temporary restriction and is upgraded to the maximum limit as soon as the user establishes credibility in the system.
If you are an established user, recent account activity may have sent us a false alert.

Note: Recipients is different from emails - 1 email with 5 recipients' counts as 5 emails, not 1.

You can raise the limits by getting a one-time verification code sent to your mobile phone. 
Verify your account with a mobile number (only available in limited markets at this time): Mobile Verification https://sms.live.com/
To get the highest possible Hotmail limits, you might consider upgrading to a paid subscription such as  Hotmail Plus.

Hello PaulPKlax,

 

Welcome to Microsoft Community. We are happy to help you with your concern.

 

We understand that you are having issue with your Microsoft account. You said that you've been getting hundreds of spam emails since your account has been changed to Outlook.com. You also mentioned that you cannot block some of the spam emails because Outlook doesn't recognize them as email address.

 

There are several ways on how you can block spam emails in your mailbox. Here are some options that you can set up in your account:

 

Junk mail filter - Selecting Exclusive option. This feature will let you receive emails from your contact list and safe senders list. To set up a junk mail filter to Exclusive, please follow these steps:

 

1. Sign in to your Microsoft account.
2. Click the gear icon located on the upper right panel of your mailbox, just beside your username.
3. Select More mail settings.
4. Under Preventing junk email, click on Filters and reporting option.
5. Under Choose a junk email filter, select Exclusive.
6. Click the Save button.

 

Email rules - You can set up email rules to filter incoming emails to automatically delete them and not getting them in your Inbox. If you want to create an email rule to delete emails coming from an email address, kindly refer to these steps:

 

1. Sign in to your Microsoft account.
2. Click the gear icon located on the upper right panel of your mailbox, just beside your username.
3. Select More mail settings.
4. Under Customizing Outlook, click the Rules for sorting new messages option.
5. Click the New button.
6. Under Step 1: Which messages do you want this rule to apply to?
On the first box with drop down list, select the Sender's address and in the second box with drop down list, select yes.
7. On the third box, type sender's email address. You may select any option that you want to use at first, second and third box depending on your preference.
8. Under Step 2: What action do you want to apply? Select Delete these messages.
9. Click the Save button.

 

Blocked senders list - You can add email address/es or domain/s in this list to stop receiving emails from them. You also need to delete them from your contact list and safe senders list as well. Here's how?

 

1. Sign in to your Microsoft account.
2. Click the gear icon located on the upper right panel of your mailbox, just beside your username.
3. Select More mail settings.
4. Under “Preventing junk email”, click the Safe and blocked senders option.
5. Click Blocked senders.
6. In the Blocked email address or domain box, type the email address of the sender or the domain that you want to block, and then click Add to list.

 

Sweep features - Sweeping emails for deletion. By using Sweep feature it will clean up all the email from a folder and selected users specially emails from spammers. How to use Sweep feature?


1. Log in to your Microsoft account.
2. Check the email that that you received from spammers.
3. On the top of your mailbox user interface click "Sweep."
4. Once you click "Sweep," select "Delete all from."
5. Check the small box that stating "Also block future messages."
6. Click 'Delete all."

 

All the emails from the spammer will be automatically deleted and it will go directly to your deleted folder.
