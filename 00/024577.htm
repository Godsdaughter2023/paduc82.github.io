hal hostetler - mvp-outlook say:
The problem is using port 25.  This is a very old legacy port that will be blocked by every ISP unless you are directly connected to them and are using their email servers.  It works for Roadrunner only when you are directly connected to them.  To counter this problem, they also use port 587 with STARTTLS encryption, which will be open to you regardless of who you're connected to, including when directly connected to Roadrunner.  So, the fix is to change port 25 with no encryption to port 587 with STARTTLS, and all should work everywhere.  Diane's page, How to Send Email When Traveling or Using Public Wi-Fi, goes into more detail on the necessary changes.

steve rindsberg say:

Hi,

Thanks

Not possible, or at least not as described.  Workarounds:

You could set up one piece of text styled the way you want it, then use the Format Painter to pick up the formatting and apply it to other text.  If you doubleclick the format painter icon, it'll let you apply the same formatting to as many selections of text as you like, across multiple slides.

I have an add-in that lets you create and apply styles in PowerPoint.  It's a bit old and doesn't pick up all the newer formatting tricks that PPT can do (glows and such), but it does quite a bit.  The free ShapeStyles demo lets you create up to five styles and apply them as often as you like (you can delete one and add another as you like; just can't exceed five at once.)
