rick rothstein, mvp - excel say:

There are two ways to specify a range with the information you provided. One way is...

Range("D" & Val).Value = Range("BE" & Val).Value

and the other is...

Cells(Val, "D").Value = Cells(Val, "BE").Value

I kind of prefer the second method as it does not involve concatenation. As a side note, I would not use Val as the name of a variable as Val is the name of a built-in VBA function... it converts String values starting with numbers to that starting number. For example...

LeadingNumber = Val("12.3abc")

will assign 12.3 to the indicated variable.

cbarnhorst say:

16257 is not available on Slow Ring.  It is strictly a fast ring build.  Please note that a ring change is not immediate and may take several hours to be "noticed" by the servers.

Normally when you use the roll back to a previous build feature the build number you left is recorded in the applicability key in the registry and WU knows to ignore that build in the future.  Check the applicability key for a new subkey showing 16257.  It is not necessary to change rings to have this feature be effective.

Start RegEdit and navigate to this key

Computer\HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\WindowsSelfHost\Applicability

and look for a subkey with 16257 in it.  Post back with a snip of your regedit window.,
