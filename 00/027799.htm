Actually you can have more than one overtime rate. It is done via the effective date table for resource costs. Sub SendNew(Item As Outlook.MailItem)

Dim objMsg As MailItem
Set objMsg = Application.CreateItemFromTemplate("C:\link\to\template.oft")


objMsg.Recipients.Add Item.SenderEmailAddress
objMsg.Recipients.Add Item.To
objMsg.Recipients.Add Item.CC


objMsg.Subject = "subject of new email"

objMsg.Send

What am I missing? Thanks for any suggestions.

michael bauer (vboffice.net) say:

The first line adds the sender of the original email, which is what you want. The other two lines add all the recipients from the To and CC fields, which you don't want.

This adds your address as CC:

dim r as recipient

set r=objmsg.recipients.add(your address)

r.type=olcc

catsawyer say: Let me clarify - I don't actually want to respond to the sender,  just to the recipient. The multiple "objMsg.Recipients.Add" lines are to cover my bases because objMsg.Recipients.Add Item.To doesn't seem to work by itself and that's why I wonder if I'm missing something.

michael bauer (vboffice.net) say:
The To and CC fields just hold the display  names. To be sure you add the addresses loop through the item's Recipients collection, and add each Recipient object to the new email's Recipients collection.

catsawyer say: Yes, this is where I get stuck. I don't know who the recipients will be, and they will change from email to email...is there a way to automatically capture both their name and address? I do not know enough about VBA code to figure this out. Thanks.

next
