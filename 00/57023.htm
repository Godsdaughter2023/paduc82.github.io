canadian tech say:

Keep in mind that DVD/CD device failure is one of the most common things that happen to PCs.  This is particularly likely if your PC is say 5 years or so old.

If yours is a desktop PC, the replacement is easy and inexpensive.  Usually about $20 to buy the replacement.  Shut down, remove power, hold the ON switch in for 7 seconds or more.  Lay the computer on it side with the side where all the plugs are located down.  Remove the side panel.  Remove the 2 screws that hold the drive in place.  Remove the two plugs at the back of the drive.  In some cases, you may need to remove the front panel to slide it out.  Do that with caution so that you do not break any of the little holders off.   Reverse the procedure for the new one.

If yours is a laptop PC, replacement is physically not difficult.  Usually one screw.  But this is a proprietary device that is different for every laptop.  Finding one could be a problem and your only likely source will be the OEM.  Price will be high.  Likely like $100 or so.  A great alternative is an external drive that connects via USB.  Usable in any PC.  Cost about $25.
