hansv mvp say:

Excel stores numbers with a maximum precision of 15 significant digits. It cannot handle 16 digit numbers.

If the value is some kind of ID number, you probably don't have to perform calculations with it. If so, you can format the cell as text before entering the value, or prefix the value with an apostrophe ' to force it to be interpreted as text.

If you need to perform calculations with numbers of 16 or more digits, you'd need to install an add-in that provides this capability.

Rounding help - Microsoft Community

terrycampbell_565 say:

Rounding help

Headcount is broken down by location and I calculate a percentage for each location (total equals 100%).  I then take a total $ amount and multiply it by the percentage giving us an amount by location..  What formula can I create that rounds the sum totals by location to equal my grand sum total exactly?  I have to make numerous JE's that balances to the penny.

joeu2004 say: Terry wrote:
Headcount is broken down by location and I calculate a percentage for each location (total equals 100%).  I then take a total $ amount and multiply it by the percentage giving us an amount by location..  What formula can I create that rounds the sum totals by location to equal my grand sum total exactly?  I have to make numerous JE's that balances to the penny.

Suppose the percentages are in A1:A20, the total dollar amount is in T1, and your calculations are in B1:B20.  Enter the following formulas:

B1:  =ROUND(A1*T1,2)
B2:  =ROUND(SUM($A$1:A2)*$T$1-SUM($B$1:B1),2)

Copy B2 down through B20.

The ROUND formula in B20 should mask the fact that SUM(A1:A20) is rarely exactly equal to 100%, despite appearances to the contrary.  But if you really want to be sure, change the last formula to:

B20:  =ROUND(T1-SUM(B1:B19),2)
