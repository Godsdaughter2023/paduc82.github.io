ronrosenfeld say: I don't see good documentation as to the range selection algorithm of the AutoFilter method.

Empirically, I find that the AutoFilter method will always act on all the contiguous rows, even if you select fewer.  That being the case, you could either leave a blank row at the bottom of your list, prior to the row with the SUM function, or you could execute the AutoFilter, then UNhide the last row.

One way to unhide the last row; after the autofilter has been run:

============================
 With ActiveSheet.AutoFilter
    Cells(.Range.Row + .Range.Rows.Count - 1, 1).EntireRow.Hidden = False
End With
==============================

Without your workbook, I'm not sure if the following will work, but this might be one way to embed that snippet.

==================================
...
With ActiveSheet
        With .Range("A5").CurrentRegion
            .AutoFilter
            .AutoFilter Field:=FilterFieldYear, Criteria1:=FrmFilterRecords.cboYear.Text
            .AutoFilter Field:=FilterFieldRegDiv, Criteria1:=FrmFilterRecords.cboRegDiv.Text
            .AutoFilter Field:=FilterFieldProgram, Criteria1:=FrmFilterRecords.cboProgram.Text
        End With
       
       With .AutoFilter
           Cells(.Range.Row + .Range.Rows.Count - 1, 1).EntireRow.Hidden = False
       End With
   
    End With
...
============================

Also, note that you may not need to go through the calculations you do of figuring out the bounds of your list if
CurrentRegion will do the job.  If it does not, because of multiple areas, then it might be simpler to do something like:

   With .range("A5",cells.SpecialCells(xlCellTypeLastCell))
        .autofilter
  ...
