andreas killer say:

If you want to redim the first dimension of an 2 dimensional array, you can use a trick: Transpose the array, change the second dimension and transpose the array again.

Andreas.

 

Sub Test()
  Dim Arr()
 
  'Setup an array
  ReDim Arr(1 To 3, 1 To 5)
 
  'Show the dimensions
  Debug.Print "Arr(" & LBound(Arr) & " to " & UBound(Arr) & _
    ", " & LBound(Arr, 2) & " to " & UBound(Arr, 2) & ")"
 
  With WorksheetFunction
    'Exchange the dimension
    Arr = .Transpose(Arr)
    'Resize the array
    ReDim Preserve Arr(LBound(Arr) To UBound(Arr), LBound(Arr, 2) To 10)
    'Exchange the dimension
    Arr = .Transpose(Arr)
  End With
 
  'Show the dimensions
  Debug.Print "Arr(" & LBound(Arr) & " to " & UBound(Arr) & _
    ", " & LBound(Arr, 2) & " to " & UBound(Arr, 2) & ")"
End Sub

darrenjc78 say:

Hi all,

I had this issue today and fixed it like this.

Run an update!

  1. Inside Outlook Help - >Check for updates
  2. Download and apply all updates for Office products, not just Outlook.
  3. Outlook 15.34 is the current at the time of this post
  4. Close Outlook for the installation
  5. Open Outlook
  6. Edit your connection settings - Outlook > Preferences > Accounts
  7. Find your mail profile with the issue
  8. Check the spelling of your incoming and outgoing mail servers - EG mail.domain.com
  9. Tick the "Use SSL" for incoming and out
  10. Check the ports are 993 and 465
  11. If they are not as above, click the override tick box and type them in - 993 and 465
  12. It doesn't matter if your mail server accepts secure connections or not, it will try to connect securely then fall back to insecure port 25 if needed.
  13. Click More settings button
  14. Authentication - Use incoming settings
  15. Ok back to the main screen
  16. Click send n receive
  17. Accept any certificate pop ups - Yes trust type mess
