ntauthority t.welch say: Hello, Moitra !  You can either power your laptop via the wall plug-in,  or charge the battery, but you should not do both at the same time. The charger was not meant to do both at the same time. As the battery nears it's peak charge, the amount of current available increases to finish off the charge. That extra power is drawn away from your motherboard if you are doing both at the same time, which can cause loss of processing power, and possible data corruption.  Worse, your battery can develop memory syndrome, where a partially charged state becomes the fully charged state.  Then, there is the possibility that your laptop has "speed stepping" enabled in the bios, where, as the battery runs down, the processor "steps" down it's frequency, (speed) to reduce power consumption and extend battery life, another reason not to have the battery in while you are plugged into the wall, unless you are charging it, in which case you should not be using the device...
