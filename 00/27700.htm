problems re dot leaders in TOC in Word 2013 - Microsoft Community

roysyboy say:

problems re dot leaders in TOC in Word 2013

I have spent some days investigating this having looked at previous stuff here about it.

I have observed the following problem:

After removing a TOC and recreating one's TOC, sometimes one gets no dot leaders displayed.  Instead, one simply gets, after the text, a space character followed immediately by the page number (which should be on the right margin with dots leading to it).

If one hits Alt-f9 to display the TOC code, the following entry might have appeared and it will stop dot leaders being displayed and thus should be removed to get default dot leaders back:

/p " "


16 people found this helpful

roysyboy say:

more re dot leaders

I noticed that Word inserted a right tab in my first active TOC element: TOC 2.

The value that it inserted was the right margin minus 0.02 cm.

I then increased the page width. The TOC then displayed with the old right margin being where the page numbers ended.  Updating the TOC then corrects the right margin to its correct, new position/value.  But the old tab entry remains in TOC 2, and this happens to determine its page number position (see problem 1 in my previous post).

roysyboy say:

even more re dot leaders

A third problem (just discovered) is when a heading appears to be "too short" and this stops the correct displaying of the dot leader in the respective TOC entry.  Also, LibreOffice also shows this problem.

I noticed this in a document where I have a sequence of headings each separated by a blank line (which is probably an incidental fact):

Heading 3

Heading 5

Heading 4

Heading 5

Whereas the first usage of Heading 5 style displays ok in the TOC, the second usage of Heading 5 displays without the dot leaders and with the page number close, but not very close, to the heading itself which, in actuality, is short, namely, the 2 letters: II.

In the TOC one gets:

II   4

instead of the desired:

II.....................................................................................................................4

A somewhat crude workaround is to add, say, 6 dots (periods or full stops) to the heading:

II......

but have these dots printed in white ink so that they do not show in the heading but will show (unfortunately) in the TOC.

This induces the TOC to display "II......" followed by a dot leader up to the page number.

Ok, there is a slight gap between this heading and the start of the following dot leader, and the dot spacing is slightly different but, hey, it works!

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

The issue is that there is a (probably left aligned) tab stop at the position where the page number is appearing.  Normally, the text of the heading style would extend past that position so that the tab space that separates the page number from the text of the heading will cause the page number to be located at the right hand side of the page with the dot leaders as defined in that tab stop being displayed.

Perhaps you can remove that first tab stop from the TOC5 style, but it is somewhat inconsistent to have some Heading 5 headings containing heading text and others having only the heading number such as II.
