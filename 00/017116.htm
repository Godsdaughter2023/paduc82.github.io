I'm not clear why you would want to link to a hyperlink. Instead just copy the hyperlink from Excel to Word or re-create it in Word.

To link Excel to Word:

  1. Select the cell or picture you want to link, then use Edit>Copy.
  2. Open the Word file, click where you want the linked item to appear.
  3. On the Home tab of the ribbon, click on the bottom half of the Paste button and choose Paste Special.
  4. In the Paste Special dialog, choose the Paste link: option, then select the format in which you would like to paste the item. Click on OK.

Now the Excel item is linked to a copy in Word. After revising the Excel data, you can update the Word links to see the new information.

I need to count the number of times a date range occurs within a date - Microsoft Community

david_smith4 say:

I need to count the number of times a date range occurs within a date range; i.e., presentation dates are input and I need to know how many occur within our fiscal January

I have reps who are supplying data in the form of presentation dates (they are required to complete so many a month). I would like to auto-count the number of presentations that they have delivered (again, recorded by the date delivered) within our fiscal January. Our fiscal January starts December 27, 2010 and runs to January 22, 2011. Please advise if there is a simple way to accomplish this.
t. valko say:
I have reps who are supplying data in the form of presentation dates (they are required to complete so many a month). I would like to auto-count the number of presentations that they have delivered (again, recorded by the date delivered) within our fiscal January. Our fiscal January starts December 27, 2010 and runs to January 22, 2011. Please advise if there is a simple way to accomplish this.


Try this...

Let's assume you have dates in the range A2:A25.

Use cells to hold the date boundaries:

  • C2 = 12/27/2010
  • D2 = 1/22/2011

Then:

=COUNTIF(A2:A25,">="&C2)-COUNTIF(A2:A25,">"&D2)

[EDIT]:

Or, since you're using Excel 2010:

=COUNTIFS(A2:A25,">="&C2,A2:A25,"<="&D2)
