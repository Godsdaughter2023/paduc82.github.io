Method 1: Safe mode is a diagnostic mode that starts your computer with limited drivers necessary to run Windows. http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows-8/windows-startup-settings-safe-mode

Method 2: If you are not able to create an account in safe mode, enable built-in administrator by following the steps below, create a new user profile, transfer all the data from the corrupted user to the newly created user profile and check if the issue is resolved.

 

Step 1: Enable built-in administrator account.

 

  1. Open elevated command prompt by entering Safe mode with Command prompt. To go to safe mode, use the power button on the sign-in screen and press Shift + Restart. This will take you to the recovery boot menu. Click Troubleshoot> Advanced options> Startup settings. You then see a list where you can choose Safe Mode with Command prompt.

  3. Login into the built in administrator user profile and create a new user profile with administrator privileges.
  4. Delete the corrupted user account from the built in administrator.
  5. Then lock your screen by pressing Windows key + L, and sign-in at the Administrator account.
  6. Check if the issue persist and you can disable built in administrator account by typing the command: net user administrator /active:no

     

    Step 2: To create a new user account follow the steps below:

     

    1. Open Elevated command prompt by right clicking on the Start icon and select Command prompt (Admin).
    2. Type net user NewAccount password /add and click Enter.
    3. Type net localgroup Administrators NewAccount /add and click Enter.
    4. Log off from the system and login to the new user account.

    phantom emails being sent from my email address using my account I - Microsoft Community

    alex palacios say:

    phantom emails being sent from my email address using my account I guess what needs to be done to stop or fix the problem?

    Phantom emails being sent from my email address. being sent to my whole address book. what needs to be done to stop or fix the problem?
    brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

    Two things may apply here.  The first is that, in fact, someone has stolen your credentials because you logged into your mailbox using a public hotspot.  If you can still access your mailbox with a web browser, do so on a private network (like your home network) and change your password to something complex and unguessable, using at least ten characters, those characters being a combination of upper and lower case letters, digits, and punctuation or special characters.  That should thwart those who have stolen the credentials, denying them access to your mailbox.

    The second thing that may apply is that your mailbox is safe, but your address has been hijacked, meaning that the spammers are using their own sending mailers but specifying your mail address as the sending address, something that's very easy to do and something that happens to nearly everyone who allows their address to be visible in a public forum (like Facebook or some online discussion group).  This case if not a serious problem and no threat to you.  Since spammers change sending addresses frequently, they'll move on from using yours.

    You can generally tell the difference between these cases because in the former, you'll likely lose access to your mailbox because the spammer changed your credentials to suit himself.  It's a problem you'll need to address with your mail service provider.   You may also see evidence of the compromise because your targeted friends may send you copies of the messages they've received and you'll be able to confirm the headers in the message shot it originating at your mail service provider's network.  The latter case shows itself mostly because you'll start getting non-delivery reports from addresses you do not recognize or that are clearly bogus.  If this happens, just delete them and stop worrying.  They'll cease after a while.  Even still, it's always good practice to change the passwords to your online services every few months to protect against compromize.  Tools like lastpass.com are beneficial in helping you keep them straight.
