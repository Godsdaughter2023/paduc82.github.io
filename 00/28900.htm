hansv mvp say:

Select the data in columns C and D - in your example C1:D5. I will assume that C1 (or D1) is the active cell within the selection.

On the Home tab of the ribbon, click Conditional Formatting > New Rule...

Select 'Use a formula to determine which cells to format'.

Enter the formula

 

=ISERROR(MATCH($C1,$A$1:$A$5,0))

 

Click Format...

Activate the Fill tab.

Select yellow.

Click OK twice.

 

On the Home tab of the ribbon, click Conditional Formatting > New Rule...

Select 'Use a formula to determine which cells to format'.

Enter the formula

 

=IF(ISNUMBER(MATCH($C1,$A$1:$A$5,0)),$D1<>VLOOKUP($C1,$A$1:$B$5,2,FALSE))

 

Click Format...

Activate the Fill tab.

Select red.

Click OK twice.

kergga say:

I've come up with a hack solution for you without VBA (I didn't look at Cimjet's solution, it might be even better).

 

Use named ranges as your data validation source. For your dependent listboxes, use something like the following to create your named range:

=IF(Sheet1!$A4<>"",Sheet2!$B$1:$B$3,Sheet2!$C1)

 

So the named range source is dependent on the initial drop-down selection.

 

The problem I ran into before is that when Sheet2!C1 is the source, I left it blank thinking that data validation would only accept a blank cell. However, when the source range is blank, data validation will accept any value. What I did this time around is put a single space character in Sheet2!C1, and now it /appears/ blank and will not take any value (other than a single spacebar character) unless your main A4 cell has a selection.
