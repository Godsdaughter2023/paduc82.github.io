How can I duplicate my business card information across all fields in - Microsoft Community

esquared2 say:

How can I duplicate my business card information across all fields in a template?

Original Title: "creating a business card in word"
I am using the template.  I have entered all my information in the first field, how do I get it to duplicate to remaining fields?

a. user say:

The business card templates originally worked as follows:
 a. you fill in the relevant business information in Word->Preferences...->User Information
 b. you choose the relevant template in the template chooser/document gallery. From the point of view of "how it works" It doesn't really matter whether you choose one of the card layouts in Print Layout View->Coordinated Forms or in Publishing Layout View->Business Cards
 c. The template fills in the values in all the cards
 d. Any manual modifications you make would have to be copied from card to card.

AFAICS many of the Business card layouts actually work this way, and that probably means that they all do, but I haven't actually tried them all. I'll go into precisely how they work in a minute.

But the real problem in Word 2011 is that the fields that you fill in in Word-Preferences...->User Information are not necessarily the ones that are used in these forms, and part of the reason for that is that you dont get to see all the fields in User Information. If you are using Outlook, Word stores those values in "your" contact record in Outlook. That record may have more than one address in it (e.g. Home Address, Work Address). One of those addresses may be set up as the default. 
 In Word->Preferences, you see/update the values in the default address.
 In the business card templates, Word always uses the work address.

So it is important that you edit the Word address/phone/fax values in Outlook before creating a new document based on a business card template.

Another problem may be that you are not using Outlook. In that case, Word may store its User Preference information elsewhere, e.g. the Apple Address book. Word also seems to store some of the info. in a "plist" file. I have never quite managed to work it all out.

The business card templates actually use a combination of Word field codes to insert the items from the Preferences/User info. For example the company name will be inserted something like this:

{ PLACEHOLDER { IF { USERPROPERTY Company } = "" "Organization" { USERPROPERTY Company}} \* MERGEFORMAT }

Another way to create the correct information for the business cards is to use VBA. There's a Word form at 
that you can download and use to fill in the various values - Word should store them wherever it would store them from the Word->Preferences form. But it's definitely a "use at your own risk" thing. NB, if you do use it, it's simplest to tab from one form field to another rather than clicking in each field in turn. The properties should be updated as you tab out of each field, i.e. there is no "save"/"cancel" button, and you do not need to save the document when you are done. I would consider writing a better version if there was sufficient demand:

https://skydrive.live.com/redir.aspx?cid=4de3a20a33eea203&resid=4DE3A20A33EEA203!159&parid=4DE3A20A33EEA203!112
