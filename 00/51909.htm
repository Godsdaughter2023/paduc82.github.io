Reboot to see if Windows will start Normally

Choose to keep your files and apps, just your files or to keep nothing

user account you will prevent system damage (e.g. damaging important system
files like the Windows® files), by using a more restricted account programs
that need to perform something on the system level need administrator

yusufat say:

Using administrator-rights user account is risky because you can do aaaalmost anything... such adding files to C:\Program Files, C:\Windows\system32, edit the registry, even removing access to a drive for a specific user. Viruses and malwares benefit from this kind of user account, as they can easily modify your operating system's heart. That is why your antivirus always barks everyday.

But some people feel using standard user account is too restricted and has no freedom. They prefer to use administrator account instead. This is OK if they know what they are doing.

If you don't mind becoming a "standard user" account, make a new account, set its level as standard user, then use that account for your work starting next login. Alternatively, make a new account, set its level as admin, login to that account, then lower your current account level to standard user.

But if you need to continue working as administrator, explore your antivirus program to find an option "disable administrator rights warning" or such. Or, get another antivirus. (I'm using avast! antivirus 5 and he is "quite quiet" lol)
