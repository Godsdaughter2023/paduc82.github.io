dale howard [mvp] say:
Barry --
 
No, it is not possible to get a progress line to spike to the right, which would identify tasks that are started early.  My problem with your methodology for entering progress is that you shouldNEVER be entering future progress for a task scheduled in the future.  If a resource is supposed to work on a task that starts in late February, and the resource does a little bit of work on the task on Wednesday of this week, youshould not merely enter a % Complete value in the future for that task.  Instead, you should enter the Actual Start date for that task, which is Wednesday of this week, and then enter your % Complete value, or whatever method you use to make the task as started early.  Microsoft Project will show that the task started early on the date you indicated in the Actual Start field (indicating early progress or "out of sequence progress" this week), and will split the uncompleted part of the task and honor any dependency relationship with Predecessor tasks.  Using this method will nullify your need to show progress line spikes to the right.  Hope this helps.

andre for directly say:
Hi Jeff,

There are benefits to having a Skype number depending on your needs and usage of Skype:

Subscriptions are one, three or 12-month calling plans that let you make minute-limited or unlimited* calls to selected countries, so a subscription is perfect if you want to make a lot of calls.

Subscriptions offer the best value for calling mobiles and landlines from Skype - you'll save up to 60% off our standard calling rates.

With a Skype Number (previously known as an Online Number), people can call you from mobiles or landlines and you can pick up the call in Skype. It’s perfect if you have friends, family or colleagues who don’t use Skype. Anyone can dial your Skype Number from any mobile or landline and you simply pick up the call on Skype.

If you are making Skype to Skype calls though, you don't really need it.

If your contact has an active Internet connection and a Skype ID, you can just click on their contact then, choose whether to make a voice or video call in the Skype window, choose the option 'Free on Skype'
