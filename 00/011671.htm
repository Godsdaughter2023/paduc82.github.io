danish_bronco say:

With hybrid boot ("Fast startup") enabled, a restart isn't exactly a real restart, more like coming out of hibernation: the OS caches some files that will help resume activity more quickly after "restarting". It's kind of like a super-prefetcher, but with actually caching files instead of just indexing them and keeping tab of their physical location on the disk. 

Try to disable fast startup in Power Options, and you'll get a regular restart. Re-enable it when you're done.
