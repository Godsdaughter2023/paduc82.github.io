kathleen orland say:

If they have their email client configured to prompt them for permission, they can deny. Also, they can configure email to automatically deny read receipts.

Many mail servers deny receipts. What may happen is if your recipient allows the receipt to go and their ISP denies the receipt, it sits in their Outbox trying to send and can't. This in turn backs up their Outbox so no email sends. Or they may see that they're constantly "sending 1 of 4 emails" when they know they sent only one. This is typically a stuck read receipt.

So while you can request a read receipt, there's no guarantee that you will receive one.

Kathleen Orland Microsoft Outlook MVP

kathleen orland say: This also applies to delivery receipts, though delivery receipts are not denied by the recipient because they don't reach them.
Kathleen Orland Microsoft Outlook MVP

Hi Steve,

Thanks for sharing your experience with us. We need some time to check this behavior:

A Section Zoom is link to a section already in your presentation. If you add the zoom to a slide which is already a destination for another zoom, the preview will go the next slide when you just hit the forward arrow key or left click mouse instead of going back to the previous section. To avoid this behavior, you can use mouse to scroll up to the previous slides.
