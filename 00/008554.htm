Hello!

It sounds like a hardware problem, but there's two more things you could try and do.

1) Soft Reset

or 

2) Hard Reset

Both of these are software related, and could fix the issue if it is the OS that's having problems.  The other option is to contact Microsoft about a replacement/repair, if it's still under warranty.

Soft Reset

(No data loss, but back up your phone just in case.  Make sure you have at least 30% battery)

1) Press and hold the POWER and VOLUME DOWN keys until the phone switches off.

2) Release keys and let phone boot normally

Hard Reset

ALL DATA WILL BE LOST!!  BACK UP YOUR DEVICE FIRST.

1) Press and hold the POWER and VOLUME DOWN keys until the phone vibrates.

2) Immediately after the phone vibrates, hold the VOLUME DOWN key

3) When an exclamation mark appears, press the following keys in sequence:

    VOLUME UP

    POWER

    VOLUME DOWN

    VOLUME UP

4) Phone will reboot back to factory defaults.

Hopefully this helps.  The soft reset may be the best bet to try, and then try contacting Microsoft.
