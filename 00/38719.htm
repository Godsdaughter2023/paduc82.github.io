stefan van der loop say:

I had the exact same problem with the current implementation and I've come up with a work-around.

The maximum-length of the inputfield in PIN, is 127 numeric characters.

This means that there are 10^127 possible combinations (well .. (10^127) - (10^3), considering that it has to be at least 4 numbers long ).

This makes logging in by PIN effectively impossible and will still let you use your fingerprint-reader.

Hello bluegrassjubilee,

Thank you for using Microsoft Answers forum.  Most likely your issue is due to the internet congestion or the server.   I suggest download WMP 11.  To increase the buffer size:

1. Press "Alt", "T", and then "O" to access the Options menu.

2. When the "Options" multi-tabbed dialog box appears, select the "Performance" tab.

3. Underneath "Network buffering", click the "Buffer" radio button.

4. Next to this radio button, enter your desired number of seconds Windows Media Player 11 will buffer files before playback (60 is the maximum).

5. Click "OK" to close the "Options" dialog box.
