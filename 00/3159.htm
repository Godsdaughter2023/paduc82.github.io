joeu2004 say:

It is difficult to tell what columns are what in your table.  If 56 is in C3 and 5/7/2018 is in E3, I see "116 HCHV" in F3, not a "due date".  Moreover, I have no idea where the "current formula" appears; the cell name or column.

Be that as it may, perhaps the following would work for you:

=IF(G3="", EDATE(E3, 12*IF(I3<>"Y", 4, IF(C3<55, 4, IF(C3<65, 2, 1)))), EDATE(E3, G3))

formatted as date in some form.

[EDITED] Better:

=EDATE(E3, IF(G3<>"", G3, 12*IF(I3<>"Y", 4, IF(C3<55, 4, IF(C3<65, 2, 1)))))

Caveat:  If MONTH(E3)=2 (Feb) and DAY(E3)=29, EDATE might return Feb 28 of the future year when G3="".  I presume that is desirable.  But it is different from your use of DATE.
