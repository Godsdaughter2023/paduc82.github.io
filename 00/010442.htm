ControlSource:   MemberID

BoundColumn:   1
ColumnCount:    2
ColumnWidths:  0cm

If your units of measurement are imperial rather than metric Access will automatically convert the unit of the last one to inches.  The important thing is that the dimension is zero to hide the first column.  The MemberID is included in the concatenated value for the visible column to cater for the possibility of two or more members having the same names.  By restricting the combo box's list to those members who have not paid for life, the possibility of such a member being inadvertently selected is avoided.

Tilda (~) files are not "overwritten" renamed files  but they are sometimes called "ownership" files that MS Word automatically opens for all Word files you open. They should disappear when you close the session of Word.

Renaming them wont do you any good as the file is only an indicator to allow MS Word to work and they don't actually contain any content as per your working file.

If these files are not being removed when you close files could indicate the file was not closed safely and MS Word did not close correctly.

If the file you had open yesterday is not on the recent list and you cant find them, I think you may have over-saved the previous version. Seen this before even when the user states they did not :)
