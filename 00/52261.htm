jamesdoyen say:

I had this issue, and after a week I finally managed to get past it.  First off, ignore anything you read about disabling antivirus or disabling services for a 'clean boot' etc...  When I was finally able to find the tech source for the error, it's clearly a DRIVER issue.  The system hasn't even reached the point where services or software is being loaded.  So keep your focus on the drivers.  This will help narrow down the problem for you.

You went through and checked every single driver for updates? (right clicked each driver and clicked 'update driver')  If not, it would be wise to do that first.  I would suggest you also find a decent 'driver updater' program--not to update your drivers--but just to see if you really have the latest drivers on your machine. Look at user reviews to make sure the program isn't a scam though.  I don't like programs like this, but it's handy since it can help me narrow down which drivers might be suspect. 

The reason I suggest this is because chipset makers will often release updated drivers, but the laptop manufacturer doesn't bother to update their driver download page with the later releases.  Since the generic chipset drivers aren't tagged for your specific machine, they may never show up in an update scan.  Simple example, my built in bluetooth adapter didn't show any updates available, but I found one on the chipset manufacturer website.  Same for the video card.  And the network card.  After a while computer manufacturers just stop updating their support pages, but don't take that to mean there aren't any updates out there.  Use a hardware diagnostic program if you don't know what hardware you have in your machine.


One other step that may be helpful--which I did--was to open device manager, click on VIEW and then click "Show Hidden Devices".  Now go through and look for device icons that are 'faded'.  These devices are device drivers that are installed, but that the hardware isn't connected anymore.  I went through and deleted any that were like that.  Worst case is that the driver has to be redownloaded if you connect it later.  Sometimes old drivers are left behind, so don't be afraid to remove anything that shows as 'not connected'. (If in doubt, right click device, click 'properties' and the device status box will tell you if it's not connected.)

Also, you should go into your BIOS and disable any and all built-in hardware that isn't essential to run your machine.  Sound card, bluetooth adapter, even your network adapter. (once you have the update downloaded, you don't need it for the update itself.)  Just disable as much as possible without it being essential hardware.  This way you can take the driver for that piece of hardware out of the picture.

When I was done all the above, I clicked 'restart', and disconnected any USB devices that I still had connected.  It rebooted and completed the update.  Keep your focus on the drivers...  This error is specifically a driver issue.

Just in case people are still having an issue with this problem, pls find below a solution which worked for me..

 

I've spent the last mth trying to solve this problem and "youemailme" (another thread herein) had the correct fix for me!! And so Simple!

I have noticed on Blogs that this issue has been around at least since Win98 so it's not your O/S or your computer.

The issue appear to be certain program(s) in your Start up Menu that has a different screen resolution set, thereby causing the screen resolution to change when you start your computer. So it's not a MicroSoft program error but rather a 'settings' issue.

With me what worked were 2 things:

(1) Perform the "clean boot process" as advised by many to determine which software program(s) was causing the problem.. and then,

(2) Right-click on the 'problem' program (as discovered in above) name in Start Menu>Properties>Compatibility.  For some reason, there's an option to "run in 640x480 screen resolution", which was checked.  This particular program was in the startup folder, and was the cause of the problem.  Then unchecked the resolution box. No more problem.
