To add a common watermark such as DRAFT, CONFIDENTIAL, or DO NOT COPY to your document,

  1. Under Design, in the Page Background group, click Watermark.

  2. Scroll through the watermark gallery and click the watermark you want. The gallery includes horizontal and diagonal watermarks for Draft, Confidential,Sample, ASAP, Urgent, and Do Not Copy.

    Word automatically applies the watermark to every page except a designated title page.

If you’d rather create your own text watermark, at the bottom of the watermark gallery click Custom Watermark , then click Text watermark, and in the Text box, type the text you want. You can try different fonts, layouts, sizes, and colors while you’re at it.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

I find MacroButton fields the simplest solution for this providing you are using an actual template (.dotx). Because the MacroButton fields are overwritten when you use them, they are not suitable for documents that will be reused.

The process of creating such fields is described at http://word.mvps.org/FAQs/TblsFldsFms/UsingMacroButton.htm, but here are the steps:

  1. In the document type the following:
    MacroButton NoMacro [Click here and type <something>]
  2. Select what you just typed and press Ctrl+F9. This will convert it to a field.
  3. Press Alt+F9 to toggle the field display. What you'll see in your document is this:
    [Click here and type <something>]

The <something> can be anything you like. In fact, the part between brackets can be anything you like. The way this field works is this: A MacroButton field is intended to run a macro when double-clicked. When you provide the name of a nonexistent macro, it can't do anything, but it does display whatever prompt you put as the third string in the field. It doesn't have to be in brackets (that's just a common convention), and you don't have to include "Click here and type" (another convention). You could have just MacroButton NoMacro Name and it would work just as well because the way it works is that, when you click in it, the entire field is selected, so that whatever you then type overwrites the field.

My suggestion would be to have just a single field for the entire inside address (use line breaks at the end of each line), but you could equally well set it up with a field for the name and an additional field for each line of the address (but then there might be too many or too few fields depending on how many address lines there are).

suzanne s. barnhill say:

If you just want the current date in the document, you don't need a content control. You can use either a CreateDate field (if you want the date to stay the same forever) or a Date field if you want the date to update each time the document is opened. You can insert the Date field with Alt+Shift+D.

But you don't need to be in Design Mode to insert the content controls. You need Design Mode only to change the prompt text from "Click here to enter a date" to "Today's Date" (the Title and Tag can be inserted from Properties without enabling Design Mode, and if you use "Today's Date" as the Title, you don't need to change the placeholder text). You must then exit Design Mode to actually use the control.
