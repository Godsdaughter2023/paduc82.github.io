a. user say:

DNS settings are normally sent automatically from the internet service provider, or, one can manually select different DNS providers.

Different operating systems do not need different DNS settings, and I don't understand why your Internet Service Provider instructed you to change DNS settings, unless it was to restore automatic DNS.

If your DNS is set to automatic, Windows 10 should have no trouble.  Even if your DNS is manual, Windows 10 does not need a different DNS than any other version of Windows, so if your DNS is working, it should continue to work.

Windows 10 can cause networking/internet failure to connect for some people, but it is not because of DNS.

So, if you only have one computer and need your computer to be solid and reliable, and rely on internet connectivity for important things, do not upgrade to Windows 10 right away.  Wait until your ISP has other Windows 10 users and can advise you.
