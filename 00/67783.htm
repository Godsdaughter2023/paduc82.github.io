P90X for Xbox Fitness Review - Microsoft Community

uproar2099 say:

P90X for Xbox Fitness Review

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=KLISx6XX6ns

I've just completed my 3rd week with P90X for Xbox Fitness. Check out the link above for my video review (with super awesome exercise demonstrations for lulz). For those of you that have no interest in high quality, well rehearsed (at least 3 times) review videos, or if you simply like to read... keep reading.

What is it?

P90X for Xbox Fitness is a 30 day workout program designed by Tony Horton, creator of the P90X series of workout videos, specifically for Xbox One. The program consists of 5 separate workouts; 1) Chest, Shoulders & Arms 2) Fighter X 3) Legs, Back & Core 4) Power Cardio 5) Total Core

Each of these workouts are full body workouts with an emphasis on giving you a great cardio workout while strengthening the rest of your body. You won't do a whole lot of running or jumping in place, but I guarantee you will sweat buckets during all 5 of the workouts!

What do I need?

Aside from money (more on that later), you don't "need" anything. All of the workouts can be done isometrically (without weights, just squeezing the muscles). However, for a better workout, you may want to consider purchasing some small weights or bands. If you were only to buy one, I would recommend getting a cheap set of bands as all of the "weight" exercises can be done with bands (whereas some of them can use weights, but not all).

How much room do I need?

Because these routines were designed specifically for the Xbox One, the workouts you'll be asked to do can all be done comfortably in your living room. All you'll need is approximately your height both forward and side-to-side from your TV. I'm 6'2" so I need basically a 6'2" x 6'2" space to do all the workouts. I also have very low ceilings so the fact that there aren't a lot of jumping exercises is a godsend (I have scars on my knuckles from hitting the ceiling during other workouts).

What is the difference between this and P90X3?

The basic P90X3 program is 16 videos that are 30 minutes each for a total of 8 hours of content. You also get Fitness and Nutrition Guides along with the videos for a total cost of $120.00 for the full 90 day program.

P90X for Xbox Fitness is $60 for the aforementioned 5 videos which total just over 3 1/2 hours of content. There are no guides that come with it at this time, though I have heard that a Fitness Calendar will be released soon as a companion to the program.

So you're paying slightly more per hour and you don't receive those guides, but you do get the Kinect feedback (which is huge!) and the Xbox Fitness scoring system and challenges.

Should I buy it?

Like almost all workout video programs, it depends on what you want from it. If you're looking to get super strong and build a large upper body, I don't think this program will get you there on it's own. There isn't a lot of heavy weight work and the upper body workouts are mostly push-up based.

If you're looking for a cardio-centric workout that doesn't involve a lot of running (I HATE running). Then this is definitely for you. You're going to strengthen everything while working up a ridiculous sweat... and it's WAY more interesting than running IMO. This program works your abs so much, yet you don't realize it because you're focusing on 3 different muscle groups at once during almost every exercise. Your lower back and abs are going to get a great workout in all of these workouts.

So there's my opinion of the program. Feel free to post your own experiences or comments about this really exciting addition to the Xbox One. Thanks everyone!

How to change the incorrect email address of Microsoft Account. - Microsoft Community

brendan_999 say:

How to change the incorrect email address of Microsoft Account.

My original Microsoft Account was created with an incorrect email address which appears to belong to someone else. How can i change it?

<Original Title: Change Microsoft Account>

a. user say:

Hi Brendan,

Thank you for visiting Microsoft Community.

As per the description, you want to change the Microsoft account email address as it is incorrect which belongs to someone else.

I certainly understand your concern.

I would like to provide you with the information that if you’ve set up that account with the wrong address, you can’t change it. But you can create another account by logging in to a local account and creating another Microsoft account.

Please refer to the steps given below and check if that helps.

  1. Start by searching user accounts and then selecting Add, delete and manage other user accounts.
  2. Sign-in options for your PC are to sign in with a Microsoft account or a local account. We’re going to create a local account and then log in. Choose Add an Account.
  3. You’ll be asked “How will this person sign in?”
  4. At the bottom of the page, choose Sign in without a Microsoft account.
  5. Fill out the user name and password and choose Next.
  6. You’ll have the option of creating a child’s account, which you won’t choose this time. Click Finish and you’ll now have a Local Account.
  7. Go to you Windows 8 Start Screen and click the user name for a drop-down menu that will let you change users to the local account.
  8. Once you are logged in under the local account, you can follow the create account process again. Only this time, you’ll set up a Microsoft account with the correct e-mail address.

Alternative method:

You can login to a local account, create another Microsoft account with the correct email address and then switch back to the Microsoft account. If you have any other local account, you can login to the same and then create another Microsoft account.

You can also refer to the link to create another local account.

Create a user account

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows-8/create-user-account

Please refer to the section “To create a local account”.

I would suggest you to login to the local account. After you login, you can create another Microsoft account using the same link.  You can follow the steps to switch back to Microsoft account:

  1. Press “Windows + c” keys to open settings.
  2. Select Change PC settings.  
  3. SelectUsers.
  4. On the right side, it will show your current account with an option that says "Switch to a Microsoft account".
  5. Once you have setup your Microsoft account (this requires a Windows Live ID (Hotmail or MSN, Outlook.com), you can logout of the local account and then try to login using the correct Microsoft account.

For reference:

I would suggest you to refer to the following article given below and check if that helps.

User accounts: FAQ

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-ca/windows/user-accounts-faq#1TC=windows-8

Microsoft accounts

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-in/windows-8/microsoft-account-tutorial

Microsoft Account Frequently Asked Questions

http://www.microsoft.com/oem/en/community/mpn/pages/windows_live_id_faq.aspx#fbid=XlyG_8ZUZxI

All the above links holds good for Windows 10 as well.

I hope this information is helpful.

Please do let us know if you need any further assistance, we will be glad to assist you.

Here is a procedure to try to get to Windows Recovery:

go to the sign in screen.

Click "System Restore"

if that does not work try "Startup Repair"

If in Windows 10 your recovery screen will be:

Click Troubleshoot

Click Advanced options

Click "Start up Settings"

You want to start up in safe mode with network capabilities. 

Restart in safe mode. Go to device mgr. and uninstall your display adapter (incl software) That should give you Microsoft HD video adapter. You should be able to restart and get to regular boot. You will need to get latest mfg video driver and install it in compatibility mode.

Right click the setup file for the program, this can be designated as setup.exe or something similar. Click Troubleshoot Compatibility

Depending on the problems you are experiencing when attempting to install or use the software, check off the problems. In my case:

  • ‘The program worked in earlier versions of Windows but won’t install or run now’
  • The program requires additional permissions.

Click Next

Proceed to run the through the programs setup routine by clicking Test the program….

Follow the on screen instructions to install the program as you normally would.

If all is successful, click the option at the end of the wizard ‘Yes, save these settings for this program’

Wait while your settings for the program are saved.

Click Close.

auto change proxy settings from home to work and viceversa - Microsoft Community

rogério fernandes say:

auto change proxy settings from home to work and viceversa

I use my laptop at work and at home. At work I need to configure LAN settings to use a proxy, at home or on the road I must disable the proxy. I do this several times a day opening "Intenet Options\Connections\LAN Settings\...
Is there any way, script, code, anything that can detect my type of connection and configure the LAN settings automatically?
rogério fernandes say:

I post here the answer to this question (posted by me) because it is quite useful (I've helped many colleagues and friends who had the same problem). The answer to this question is at: LINK , it was answered by Bruno Lucas P Rodrigues and here is the translation (only changed the name of the files in order to make sense in english):

 

Hi Rogério,


In this case, I suggest that you performs the following procedures.

Click Start, type notepad in the search field and open Notepad.
Copy the text below and paste into Notepad.

Windows Registry Editor Version 5.00
 
[HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Internet Settings]
"ProxyEnable"=dword:00000001

Click file, then click Save as… and save this file with the name "enableproxy.reg" (with quotes) on the desktop and click Ok.
When you click Ok, it automatically saves the file to your desktop and keep Notepad open.
Now copy and paste the text below into Notepad.

Windows Registry Editor Version 5.00
 
[HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Internet Settings]
"ProxyEnable"=dword:00000000

Click file, then click Save as… and save this file with the name "disableproxy.reg" (with quotes) on the desktop and click Ok.
When you click Ok, it automatically saves the file to your desktop and keep Notepad open.
Close Notepad.

You will see 2 files created on your desktop (enableproxy.reg and disableproxy.reg).
 
Use the enableproxy.reg so that you can activate the proxy. Click 2 times on the file. An alert is displayed on the change of values in the registry. Click Yes and then click Ok.
 
Use the disableproxy.reg to disable the proxy. Click 2 times on the file. An alert is displayed on the change of values in the registry. Click Yes and then click Ok.

Now just use Internet Explorer depending on where you are.

Note: run the file before you open Internet Explorer to prevent problems in your navigation.

Any questions, post again!
