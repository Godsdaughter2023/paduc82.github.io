fukiko mikuto say:

My Yahoo email was just the way it should be but Outlook was not. So here is what worked for me:

Make sure you close Outlook 2013

Go to Control Panel

Select Mail

On the pop-up, select E-mail Accounts

Select your email account (Yahoo in my case)

Click Change from menu bar

On the Change Account pop-up, click More Settings in lower right corner

Click on 3rd tab Advanced

At the bottom, the last category Deleted Items, make sure ONLY the last selection is checked "Purge items when switching folders while online"

Click OK

Click Next on the previous pop-up (will default back to this window)

A test will run and if properly setup from before, should be successful. Click Ok (or whatever the option is).

Last pop-up comes up, click Finish

Close up any remaining pop-ups without changing anything else.

Open Outlook 2013 and now try again for your deletes.

Additionally, if your folders are still showing unread items, even though you have read or deleted items from those folders, just right click the folder, click Mark all as Read, and it should clear the unread number. (Only do this if you actually don't have items that are unread.)

Hopefully this works for anyone with these similar issues. Should work fine moving forward.

This is more a contribution than a question, but I think it's worth recording.

I use Visio at work and at home to draw diagrams.  At work everything works fine.  At home I found that Visio repeatedly freezes.  I also worked out that it only freezes when I have activated my VPN connection to our office network.  I've seen many hits on various websites and tried all of the suggestions, but nothing worked for me.  So I cranked up Process Monitor and traced the problem.

The reason for the freeze is that when I select a drawing object and try to move it, Visio seems to do something that intermittently causes the Print Spooler (spoolsv.exe) to check out my default printer.  The Print Spooler repeatedly references registry entries relating to a printer in the office and opens a pipe to our print server.  I'm not sure what it's doing but I see about 9 seconds of activity over my VPN, and then it stops and Visio bursts back into life.

The workaround is pretty simple; either disconnect the VPN (which for me is a bit of a pain since I'm uploading drawings onto an in-house wiki), or change the default printer setting to the printer at home - I'm sure changing to the Microsoft XPS Document Writer would work just as well.

I hope the above saves someone some grief.

Best regards...Paul
