Hi Manjunatha,

I looked through the situation in the link you mentioned. But to have a further understanding of your situation, I would like to confirm how you created the users and how the on-premises mailboxes were migrated to cloud.

The correct behavior is that before we move an on-premise mailbox to Office 365, the user is synced to Office 365 via DirSync, and exists in Office 365 as a mail user (not mailbox). In hybrid environment, the correct ways to have mailboxes in Office 365 are:

•Create the mailbox from on-premise Exchange and then move it to Office 365 via Hybrid remote move. After that, we assign license to the Office 365 mailbox. After the move, there will be remote mailboxes (mail users) for the corresponding mailboxes in on-premises.

or

•Directly create a remote mailbox from on-premises Exchange. This will create a remote mailbox (mail user) in on-premises and associated Office 365 mailbox that exists in the cloud.

Please let us know if you used either of the ways above to have Office 365 mailboxes and then we can help you move forward for the issue you are having.
