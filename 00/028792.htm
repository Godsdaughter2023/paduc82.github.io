    daniel g. grau say:
    When select File > Print, you may see only two dropdown menus in the Print dialog box. If to, click on the disclose triangle at the top right so a third dropdown menu is displayed. In this menu, choose Microsoft Word. This will let to set the following options. This is the general method: 

    • Print even pages in reverse order
    • Turn them over, reinsert them in the printer
    • Print odd pages

    You may need to do it in two batches: one for the numbered pages and another for the unnumbered ones, so as not to mix them. For instance, page 1 (numbered "8 & 1") should print in the back of whatever page is numbered "2 & 7."

    Check in vs. Checkin - Microsoft Community

    paulchardin say:

    Check in vs. Checkin

    More than a statement than a question: "Checkin" or more appropriately "Check-in" is a noun; "Check in" is a VERB. MS Project Server 2010 appears to use them indiscriminately, and it is mildly annoying.

    Correct usage: Do you want to check in your project?

    Incorrect usage: Do you want to checkin your project?

    THe same holds true for "Log in" vs "Login" or "Log-in" and "Log off" vs "Logoff" or "Log-off"
