lainelee say: You appear to have two questions. At the end of your post, are you saying that a message you forward appears to contain headers but no body, or that if you read messages in Outlook, then try to read them on a different computer or email client, they appear to contain only headers?

Anyway, the main issue appears to be related to the characteristics of a POP email account versus those of an IMAP account, and what Outlook is configured to do when it accesses POP email accounts.

Select Tools>Accounts, then select an account from the list on the left. In the right side of the window, you should be able to see the account type just below the account's name. If it is a POP account, then by default any email Outlook gets will be removed from the server and won't thereafter be available for any mail reader on a different computer or device.. However, you should be able to change that default behavior by clicking the Advanced button near the lower right corner, and in the Server segment tab, check the box next to "Leave a copy of each message on server". That should let you read the mail on other devices even after you have read it in Outlook. You should also decide what choice to make in the pop-up menu "Delete Copies from the Server:", because if Outlook remains set to remove messages from the server, then there's still a chance they will be gone before you try to read them with other email clients.
