  1. Press Win+R to bring up the Run dialog box.
  2. Type ‘regedit’ and hit Enter.
  3. Navigate to the following location.
  4. HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Appx
  5. Next right-click Appx and choose ‘Permissions’ from the context menu.
  6. The Permissions for Appx box will open. You will now have to take full control and ownership of the registry key.

    To assign the permissions follow these steps:
    . Open the registry key till Parameters and right-click on it.
    . Select Permissions.
    . In the Permissions Window, click on the account name under Group or User names.
    . Then click on the box next to Full Control.
    . Click on Apply and OK.
  7. Edit the PackageRoot registry key by right-clicking on it and selecting Modify.
  8. Enter the new path of the folder where you want the Windows Apps to be installed.

Note: Do note that you will not be able to update the apps installed prior to changing the default location, since these apps will still have their data in the original location.

tom van stiphout say:

Try this:

Select the Prospects table. In the ribbon, click on Create > Form. This creates an auto-form.
Keep it in design mode, select Design and from the Controls group grab a combobox and drop it on the header of the form.

The wizard should come up. If not, drop down the Controls group and click the Wizard option. The wizard should have 3 options, including the one you want.

tom van stiphout say:

You wrote in first instance: " I wanted to add a combo box that I can use to search their name". I believe I addressed that.


Beyond that, design your Prospects form and drop your PhoneCalls table on it. Answer the wizard question about linking the two.

Now you have a form with a subform to enter the several PhoneCalls records for each prospect.


Do not try to do this in one form. There is a one-to-many relation between Prospects and PhoneCalls, and a subform is the proper way to implement it.
