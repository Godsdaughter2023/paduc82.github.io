icestorm iii say:

So 802.11n, as a standard, is a mess.



2.4Ghz radios are cheaper than 5.8Ghz radios. It's part of why anything with 802.11a (like the original white 360 wireless adapter) commands a price premium over 2.4Ghz-only devices.



802.11n allows for operation on either 2.4Ghz or 5.8Ghz bands. It does not mandate both radios must be in an 802.11n device.



The 360 S only supports the 2.4Ghz band. For your router's 802.11n to work with the 360, the router must be configured to support 802.11n on the 2.4Ghz band. Verify that in your router first. If that's configured and you're broadcasting an SSID, the 360 will at least be able to "see" the SSID in the wireless network list.



The black dual antenna wireless adapter from Microsoft supports 802.11n on both 2.4Ghz and 5.8Ghz. It will work with the 360 S. It's an option if you want to keep 802.11n on the 5.8Ghz band.
