How to format a cell and column for a phone number style - Microsoft Community

l_squared say:

How to format a cell and column for a phone number style

Dear Excel for Mac community, I have a column for phone numbers and I want to format the cells and columns to automatically add the dashes (-) or parentheses for a phone number, for example 555-555-5555 or (555) 555-5555 so I can enter a phone number without having to always put the dashes or parentheses in. I have a lot of phone numbers to enter and this would help speed entry. I have tried to figure this out on my own but I'm stumped. Can someone help me with this? Thanks!

mike middleton say:

Dear Excel for Mac community, I have a column for phone numbers and I want to format the cells and columns to automatically add the dashes (-) or parentheses for a phone number, for example 555-555-5555 or (555) 555-5555 so I can enter a phone number without having to always put the dashes or parentheses in. I have a lot of phone numbers to enter and this would help speed entry. I have tried to figure this out on my own but I'm stumped. Can someone help me with this? Thanks!

Here's what works for me just now (Mac Excel 2011 version 14.4.4):

Select the range that will contain the phone numbers.

Choose (menu) Format > Cells > Number > Special > (Type) Phone Number > OK.

Select a cell in that range, type only the ten digits (no special characters), and press Return.

The result has the format (123) 456-7890.

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
Additional to Mike's reply, you might want to go a step further to apply Validation to the cells requiring a 10 digit Text entry or requiring a Whole Number [between} 1000000000 and 9999999999. Otherwise you may wind up with incomplete phone numbers such as -55, 5-555, etc. The 2nd option also prevents inadvertent entry of alpha characters, punctuation & spaces.
