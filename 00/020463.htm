tom_bpc say:

Michael,

As you see, inserting a "Recurring" task creates a summary task and a bunch of sub-tasks that are constrained to match the specified recurrence pattern.  The work time of a recurring task is typically a subset of the project-calendar working time, so a "recurring task calendar" is going to be redundant, confusing, or both.  I think they ("recurring" tasks) were originally developed to provide visibility (and a timesheet entry) for status meetings and such.  If you want/need that visibility and an explicit task to assign attending resources, then by all means use them.  I generally don't want to see such support/admin tasks at that level of detail, however - that's the reason for task 16.

I assume by "second bullet point," you mean the reference to the red-squares.  John had already noted the discrepancy there.  As you described, "The predecessors end on July 12, 2019 (Friday).
