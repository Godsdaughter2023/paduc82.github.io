jay freedman say:
The change to 'Author' is a side-effect of the option at Word Options > Trust Center > Trust Center Options > Privacy Options > "Remove personal information from file properties on save".

The mouseover help (the i-in-circle icon) for this option is incorrect when it says that it's available only in documents created in previous versions that used this option -- that's only one way it gets turned on. I suspect one way is that it can be carried over from the Normal.dot template of Word 2003 or earlier during an upgrade, and another way is a random bit-flip that shouldn't happen but sometimes does.

In Word (not on Citrix), open the Normal.dotm template as a document, by using the Open dialog (Ctrl+O) and navigating to the Templates folder (usually %appdata%\Microsoft\Templates). Go to the Trust Center and examine the option I mentioned. If it's checked and active, uncheck it and then save and close.

If it's checked and grayed out, close the dialog. Press Alt+F11 to open the macro editor, and then press Ctrl+G to open the Immediate window. Paste this line into the Immediate window and press Enter at the end of it:

ActiveDocument.RemovePersonalInformation = False

Then save and close.

Shut down and reopen Word, and verify that the option in the Trust Center has been cleared.

This should fix the problem for new blank documents created in the future, but each existing document that shows the behavior will have to be given the same treatment.

Find and Replace Tabs function in Word doesn't work. So after lots of Google search, I tried to "copy and paste" from various sources into a simple macro to do this task (I'm totally new to Word macro and VBA):

Sub Macro1()
    For Each para In ActiveDocument.Content.Paragraphs
        For Each aTab In para.TabStops
            If aTab.Leader = wdTabLeaderDots _
            Or aTab.Leader = wdTabLeaderDashes _
            Or aTab.Leader = wdTabLeaderLines Then

End Sub

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

A TabStop is an attribute of a paragraph definition and it is a tab space (vbTab)  or Chr(9) that you are wanting to replace.

You may be able to achieve what you are after by getting the .Positions of the tabstops with the leaders and then finding the tab spaces in the paragraph and collapsing the Selection and comparing the Selection.Information(wdHorizontalPositionRelativeToTextBoundary) with the position of each tabstop.  The figures will not be an exact match for example for a tab stop at 70.9 pts, the Selection.Information returns 71.5 pts/
