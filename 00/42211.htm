cmdrkeene say:

Hey there, what you want to do in this situation is tell one of the computers to use a different port number than 3389, then set your route to forward that new port number to the 2nd computer.

It's actually pretty easy.  See here for example:  Change Remote Desktop Listening Port

atifa. say:

I have figured it out. But I will leave the question here so people can see it and resolve the issues for themselves.

Solution:

Since we know Remote Desktop only works on port 3389, we cannot change the Internal port from 3389 to any other. But I think you already got the hint. We can though change the external port to lets say, 3390 on the router for one of the two computers.

So now on my router, I have allowed two computers to have remote access from the internet. In the router I have selected TCP/UDP external port 3389 for one computer and 3390 for the other. And for internal ports, I am using 3389 for both, so that Remote Desktop can still work without detecting the change we have for external port. This resolved my issue.

Previously, my router auto-selected the same internal and external IP. There was a little check/button. Once I disabled it, I was able to pick two different ports for internal and external connection for the same local IP.

a. user say:

I would have started to say suspect your keyboard and/or mouse but since you have attempted a reapir, this advice may not be helpful, but I will explain what I think is happening:

When you boot in Safe Mode and the last thing you see is mup.sys and your system hangs, it is not a problem with mup.sys - it is what comes after mup.sys that is causing your system to hang.

As a matter of fact, I have never seen a problem with the actual mup.sys file but people sure like to try to replace it when that is not the problem at all.  The problem is what tries to load after mup.sys. and you can't tell that (easily) since it is not displayed on the screen. 

Experience and lots of testing says that the keyboard and mouse driver i8042prt.sys (i8042prt.sys file is the KB and mouse driver) is usually the next thing to load on most systems, and that's where it hangs - right after mup.sys, so you should suspect a problem in the area of keyboard and mouse.  Especially if they are USB devices.

I would unplug every USB device and replace the USB keyboard with another one that you know is good or use a PS/2 keyboard.  Just plug in a keyboard.  You only need a keyboard to boot and choose Safe Mode. If you are using a wireless keyboard, undo it and plug in a wired keyboard - at least long enough to boot.  Put your 50% keyboard back at least temporarily since you are now at 0%.

Was your suspicious keyboard setup (the one you replaced) wireless?

Your problem is not mup.sys or your registry.  I believe XP seems to think thinks there is some issue with the keyboard or mouse and since that is where you have been messing around before you had this problem (with your 50% keyboard issues), that is where I would start.  It all makes some sense now...

What you will see on a keyboard that has lighted indicators is (in Safe Mode), the mup.sys show up on the screen, then with i8042prt.sys loads, the keyboard lights will blink and then things usually proceed after that.  It may seem to take several minutes (don't wait half an hour).

After booting into the Recovery Console, you should be in this folder:

C:\WINDOWS

For each of your hard disk partitions, you should then run:

chkdsk /r

For example, from the Recovery Console prompt, enter:

chkdsk c: /r

Let chkdsk finish and correct any problems it might find.  It may take a long time to complete or appear to be 'stuck'.  Be patient.  If the HDD light is
still flashing, it is doing something.  Keep an eye on the percentage amount to be sure it is still making progress.  It may even appear to go backwards sometimes.

The chkdsk /r needs to complete without any errors, so you may need to run it multiple times until it does.

Revoce the CD and type 'exit' to restart your system.

You may indeed have to go into a salvage/recovery mode at this point (unless somebody else comes up with some other ideas).
