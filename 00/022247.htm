jay freedman say:
That's a known bug in at least the last several versions of Word. It also happens when you print to paper, and when you use other gradients or picture backgrounds. It may be related to the printer driver, since some people have reported that it doesn't happen for them, but I've seen it with every printer I've tried.

The workaround goes like this: Open the header area. Insert a rectangle shape and drag it to cover the whole page. On the Drawing Tools ribbon, click the Shape Fill button and choose Gradient > More Gradients. Set the pink color on each of the two end markers, and white on the middle marker, and a 45 degree angle. This gradient will print correctly.

n7i2s5 say:

Go to the 'Review' tab, click on the 'Language' button, and click on 'Set Proofing Language'. There may also be a button on the status bar with whatever language is selected (on my computer it usually says 'English (U.S.)'). Both of these open the same dialog box.

The dialog box will show a list of installed languages, a double line, and other supported languages. If there is an icon with a check-mark and the letters 'ABC' by a particular language, then you can check the spelling and grammar for that language.

 

If Arabic does not appear above the double line, and/or does not have the aforementioned icon, go to the 'Review' tab, click on the 'Language' button, and click on 'Language Preferences'.

Under 'Choose Editing Languages' read the list to see if Arabic is enabled. If not, select Arabic from the combo-box labeled 'Add additional editing languages'.

If you want to set it as the default language, select Arabic from the list and click 'Set as Default'.

Look under the 'Proofing' column. If it says 'Not installed' then you may need to buy a Microsoft Office Language Pack: http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/language/. Language packs cost about $25. However, they may not be supported in Office Starter. If you have an Arabic version of Word, then Arabic should be the default language, and proofing should be enabled by default. If you have a French or Hebrew version of Word, then you may already have Arabic proofing tools. Otherwise, you may have to buy the Arabic language pack.

Once the proofing is installed, you should be able to check the spelling and grammar in Arabic.

 

If Arabic is your first language and you would prefer to read buttons, menus, dialogs boxes, and tool tips in Arabic, you can also download and install the Arabic ScreenTip Language Kit for free from the above website.

 

Hopefully this information is somewhat helpful.
