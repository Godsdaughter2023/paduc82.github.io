Ahem, you sound like a troll but I will indulge you.  First and foremost, this is not a support issue to be taken up with Microsoft, if you are having issues playing a third-party game than please contact the publisher, Treyarch and Activision in this case.  Second, you should understand CoD implements something known as lag compensation, meaning the game will purposely lag you if you have a better connection than the rest of the lobby.  It is meant as a game balancing tool, although does cause some issues for many users (this is why you die shooting at someone but when you watch the killcam you don't see yourself shooting at all).  Also you may want to check out your own connection as well, make sure when you are playing you are not performing any other tasks online such as streaming or downloading something on your PC or other devices.  Also make sure your NAT is set to open, this is done within the game itself and the Treyarch support team can help you figure out how to do this.  

Finally, understand that yes hackers do exist and always will, but they rarely cause lag.  What you are complaining about is people using what is known as a "lag switch", which is an artificial way to choke your own connection and cause other players to lag.  If you are in a lobby and notice everyone except one person is lagging at certain intervals (all of your connections will go yellow or red while their connection stays green) then yes, someone is cheating and you should file a complaint against them and then avoid playing with them again by submitting a player review.

Hope all of that helps, I know it is frustrating but it really is a good game, give it a shot.

Non of the Call of Duty games have dedicated gaming servers.  All the Call of Duty servers do are store stats perform matchmaking and monitor games to keep players together.  The games themselves are actually hosted from other Xbox users consoles when the game chooses a host.  A sort of peer to peer system.

Because the game doesn't have dedicated servers, it is highly reliant on good connections, from both you, other players and the host.  If you're suffering from lag on every game, the issue is likely your end, not Xbox LIVE.  As other posters have stated, like all p2p based games, Black Ops 2 has something called 'Lag compensation' built into it's networking code.  I've read that some people believe the code isn't as good as other COD games, I personally have had no major issues.

May I also point out to both the OP and other users, NAT has nothing to do with connection speed.  NAT is a networking tool built into all routers to convert outside IP addresses into internal ones.  It's part of a routers firewall.  It is essentially an 'on/off switch' not a 'fast/slow' switch'.  NAT either makes possible or prevents your ability to join games, it does not in any way cause latency, regardless of it's status.
