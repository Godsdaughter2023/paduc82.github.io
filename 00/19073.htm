http://www.gust.org.pl/projects/e-foundry/tg-math/gust_e-foundry-math_fonts-presentation.pdf

provided there to get an impression of these fonts.

Now the first quirk I noticed is that the \langle and \rangle characters (U+2329 and U+232A) will not stretch in Asana Math in Word as other delimiters such as (), or | |, or ‖ ‖, or [ ] will. Please see the linked document on page 2 to see what I mean. Word certainly can stretch those characters in different fonts, e.g. Latin Modern Math, but unfortunately will not do so in Asana Math or Cambria Math. On the other hand, the document shows that, even in Asana Math the delimiters can stretch, when rendered in different TeX variants. So I am inclined to think that the problem is not with the font but more on the MS Word side.

The second quirk is that, in any font I have tried, big, stretchable characters like \sum or \int will be rendered as 'inline-mode', i.e. noticeably smaller, when in delimiters with a separator. As an example, when typing

{\sum_(i=1)^n a_i}

in a display-style equation, the expression will build up fine and the outer braces will stretch just fine. Putting a separator in there, as in

{\sum_(i=1)^n a_i \mid 1<n<5},

will put the sum to inline-style, which is considerably smaller and not as good-looking. Apart from that, I noticed that those delimiters with a separator can stretch when you put other big things like a_a_a_a or more sophisticated big constructs in there. It just does not work well with \sum, \prod or \int.

To make things even more weird I may add that I am pretty sure that this was rendering just fine in Word 2010 but not in Word 2013 or Word 2016.

The third quirk happens when using predefined function names like sin (for sine). This also happens with user-defined functions using \funcapply. Curiously though, it does not happen when typing lim x (for limit or limes).

I would be delighted to read any thoughts on these quirks, providing a solution or not, and am happy to clarify my problems if anything is still unclear.
