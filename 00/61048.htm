During those communication-less 24 hours he can then think hard about what he said exactly.

Whenever he sends a message that others might find offensive, AND that message is violating the Code of Conduct (which you can read here), the person can report that message. 

The message itself is then attached as evidence to that report, and a real life human being will review the report and the attached evidence. 

Based on that evidence, the person will then decide if the message violates the Code of Conduct or not. If it does, your son will get a suspension. If the message was reported in error, nothing will happen (well, not to your son. The person incorrectly reporting your son will get a lower "trust" rating for future reports). 

So since a real human reviews these reports, and has access to the message sent, your son can't complain that he was reported for doing "nothing". 

Also explain to your son, that if he repeatedly violates the Code of Conduct, the suspensions will get longer.   5. Close Task Manager.
  6. On the Startup tab of the System Configuration dialog box, tap or click OK, and then restart the computer.
