Although you might be able use AND, you might not need it.

But part of the problem is:  your conditions are not mutually exclusive.  You noted one example; but there are others.  For example, what if A1=0 and C1=0?  And some conditions are not covered, if they are possible.  Can A1 and/or C1 be empty (no constant and no formula) and/or appear blank (a formula that results in the null string, as your formula above does)?  If so, how to what to treat those situations (Excel treats empty differently than the null string.)

In any case, the key is to decide on a hierarchy of conditions.  That is, what conditions trump others?  Only you can answer that question.  The following is my guess.

=IF(C1=0,-B1,IF(A1=C1,"",SIGN(A1-C1)*(A1-C1)))

Note that SIGN(A1-C1) is 1 if A1>C1, resulting in A1-C1; and it is -1 if A1<C1, resulting in -(A1-C1) = C1-A1.

***EDIT*** Well, duh:  =IF(C1=0,-B1,IF(A1=C1,"",ABS(A1-C1)))

Also note that if B1 is 0 or empty (no constant and no formula, the formula results in 0 when C1=0.  Is that what you want?

And if B1 is the null string (appears blank) when C1=0, the formula results in a #VALUE error.  Can that situation arise?

Hi Cromwell2,

 

Welcome to Microsoft Community forum.

 

I will be glad to assist you with your concern. It's possible that the filter settings of your account are affecting the delivery of incoming messages to your mailbox, you may check out the steps below for the instructions on how to check and modify your email settings.

 

A. Email Forwarding

1. In your Inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.

2. Under Managing your account, click Email forwarding.

 

Note: If it's set to Forward your mail to another email account, tick on Don't forward then click Save.

 

B. Blocked Sender's List

1. In your Inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.

2. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.

3. Click Blocked senders.

4. In the Blocked senders list, select the name or domain (the part of an e-mail address that follows the @ sign, such as hotmail.com) that you now want to receive mail from, and then click<< Remove from list.

 

C. Add the email address to your Safe senders list

1. In your Inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.

2. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.

3. Click Safe Senders.

4.In the text box, type in the email address or domain you’d like to always receive email from, and then click Add to list >>.

 

D. Check your Email Rules

1. In your Inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.

2. Under Customizing Hotmail, click Rules for sorting new messages.

3. Perform one of the actions as per your requirement:

Click Edit next to the rule that you want to edit.

 

Or

 

Choose the rule that you want to delete and click Delete.

 

Note: Not all international characters are supported as filters.

 

E. Check your Junk Mail Settings

1. In your Inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.

2. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.

3. Under Choose a junk e-mail filter, choose the level of protection that you want, and then click Save. If the Exclusive level was set, messages from senders not on your Contacts or Safe senders lists will have been sent to the Junk e-mail folder.

 

F. Check your Reply-to address

1. In your Inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.

2. Under Writing email, click Reply-to address.

3. Select Current e-mail address radio button.

4. Click Save.

 

Let us know of any result.
