john korchok say:

To refresh a TOC, right-click on it, choose Update Field and when Word asks if you want to update just the page numbers, or the entire table, choose entire table. 

There is no way to change all headings to outline numbered. in one action. To set up outline numbering that actually works, please read this page from the late Shauna Kelly on the subject. This was written for Word 2007-2010, but the steps also work for 2011, with the following changes: 

  • Section 4, step 2 should read On the Format menu, click Numbering, then Customize.
  • 4 paragraphs down, "In the Modify multilevel list dialog, click the More button." should read "In the Modify multilevel list dialog, click the down-pointing arrowhead in the lower left corner."
  • Substitute "Level" for "Click level to modify".

There may be some other menu choices I have caught, I hope you can work through the process.

Re: your current document, if, when you click on one of the numbered headings and the Heading 1 style becomes highlighted in the Style Gallery, then you should be able to update the TOC if the TOC actually uses those styles.

To find out, select the entire TOC including the paragraph mark at the end, then right-click on it and choose Toggle Field codes. You get should get a field that looks something like this: { TOC \o "1-3" \h \z \u }. The \o "1-3" section indicates the TOC uses Headings 1 to 3. If, instead, it has names of styles, it a custom TOC that uses other styles. Here's a page on TOC field codes that can help you understand how it's formatted: TOC Field Codes
