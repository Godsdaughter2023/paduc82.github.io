rowena cam say:

Hello Tim,

About your concern, please follow the steps provided below for Quiet Hours:

1. Launch Cortana> Click on the Hamburger Menu> and then tap Cortana’s Notebook> Quiet hours.

2. Under Break-through rules, you will find Edit Break-through list, click on that if you want to add some exceptions like your family member. Adding your contacts in the list is very simple.
3. Just click on Edit Break-through list> Tap on “+”> Select people you want to break-through. Now, these very people can call, or text you, even when Quiet hours in On.

More Rules to Break Through Calls:

Calls:

  • Tick the box which says , Anyone rings through if they call twice in 3 minutes. This will let anyone break-through if they call you twice in three minutes.
  • Tick the box which says, People on the list ring through. This lets your contacts, which you added in your exceptions list, call you, but can’t text you.

Text:

You can send auto reply. You need to turn on the option “Texts from people on the list get through”. If they happen to send you a message, they get an automatic response saying, Hi, the person you’re trying to reach is busy and may not respond right away.

Let us know how it goes.

hal hostetler - mvp-outlook say:
The standard behavior for deleting an item in an IMAP account is that the message is not moved anywhere, it is marked for deletion with a strikethrough line.  To make Outlook 2016 work like your phone, go to "File > Account Settings", select your IMAP account, click the "Change" button, click the "More Settings" button on the right, click the "Advanced" tab, and in the "Deleted Items" section at the bottom, REMOVE the checkmark from the "Mark items for deletion, but do not move them automatically" checkbox, then click OK.  This changes Outlook's behavior so that messages are moved to Deleted Items.  The reason you cannot currently see Deleted Items that are marked with a strikethrough is because your View setting is set to "Hide messages marked for deletion". if you change this to "IMAP Messages", they'll all appear. With that in mind, you might also want to add a checkmark to the "Purge items when switching folders while online" checkbox in the same section.&nb
