New computers that come preinstalled with Windows 7 often have what is called a recovery partition. This is used to reinstall the operating system in the event of a system crash. To access it, you will need to boot into when you start your computer by pressing a function key. This can be either F1, F2, F9, F10, F11, F12 or even DEL or Tab key. Consult the owners manual that came with your PC for instructions about how to reinstall Windows.

 

 

This is how the recovery partition is accessed for the most popular makes..

For Dell, press CTRL + F11 directly after powering up the unit

For HP, press F11 directly after powering up the unit

For LG, press F11 directly after powering up the unit

For Toshiba, press and hold '0' BEFORE and during the power up

For Acer, press and hold ALT + F10 as soon as you see the logo

For Asus, press F9 as soon as you see the Asus logo.

For Samsung, press F4 at power up..

For Fujitsu, press the F8 key repeatedly directly after powering up

For Advent, restart your computer. Then Press F10 repeatedly until the message "Starting System Recovery" is displayed

For Sony VAIO, restart and press "F8" or "F10" repeatedly until the "Advanced Boot Options" screen appears.

Incompatible Video card.

Uninstall Video Card adapter.

Press Windows key + R

Type: devmgmt.msc

Expand: Display adapters

Right click listed Display Adapter

Click Uninstall

Start setup.

When setup completes successfully, install the latest driver updates for device through Windows Update or from the manufacturers website using compatibilut mode if necessary;

Learn more:

How to: Install and Update drivers in Windows 10

Note: Some older video cards might be incompatible with Windows. It likely means the graphics adapter has or is approaching End of Life status which limits the level of support the manufacturer can provide. The card will likely function just fine for general purposes, just don't expect it to work well for scenarios such as playing games. There might even be glitches with certain casual games not working well at all.

  • How to download official Windows 10 ISO files

a. user say:

Hi Hasnaa,

We apologize for the inconvenience caused.

I would like to inform that the option to go back to previous Operating System will be available for a month after you upgrade to Windows 10. You’ll be able to go back to your previous version of Windows from Settings > Update & security > Recovery. This will keep your personal files, but it'll remove apps and drivers installed after the upgrade, as well as any changes you made to settings. I suggest you to follow the steps provided below for how to go back to previous Operating System:

  1. Navigate to Start>Settings>Update & Recovery.
  2. In the left navigation pane, click on Recovery.
  3. Click on Get Started under the option Go back to Windows 7 or Windows 8/8.1 (depending on the previous Operating System that was running).
  4. Here you can make multiple selections why are going back to your previous version of Windows along with additional details or you can choose just one and click Next. 
Note:

If you go back to Windows 8.1, some apps that came with Windows, like Mail and People, might not work anymore. To fix them, reinstall them from the Store.

To go back, you'll need to:

  1. Keep everything in the windows.old and $windows.~bt folders after the upgrade.                   
  2. Remove any user accounts you added after the upgrade.                   
  3. Know the password you used to sign in to Windows 7 or Windows 8.1 (if you used one).                   
  4. Have the USB drive you used to upgrade to Windows 10 (if you used one).

For more information, refer to the section Go back to your previous version of Windows in the link provided below:

https://support.microsoft.com/en-gb/help/12415/windows-10-recovery-options

Hope the information helps. Keep us posted on the status for further assistance.

Thank you.
