If you have already made a reservation, I recommend that you cancel it until people in the real world report on what is actually happening to their upgrade installs and licensing issues with hardware changes, and whether they are able to revert to their old Windows 7 licenses or not.  You have a full year for the upgrade offer, so there is no rush, despite Microsoft's marketing efforts to shout "limited time offer click here now to reserve your copy".  The time limit is one year.

If you change your motherboard you will probably void the upgrade, but since the final is not released yet, nobody can address personal experience with what "for the lifetime of the supported device" means.

There is a difference between OEM and retail licenses.

If your Windows 7 license was retail, then you can move it from computer to computer, and the Windows 10 upgrade from that retail license likewise can move from computer to computer.

If your Windows 7 was OEM, then the same restrictions for OEM apply, namely it cannot be moved to a new computer.

From http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/insider/wiki/insider_wintp-insider_install/what-are-the-editions-of-windows-10-available/195a4038-c061-4902-bc2b-35922c9d41bc

When I upgrade a preinstalled (OEM) or retail version of Windows 7 or Windows 8/8.1 license to Windows 10, does that license remain OEM or become a retail license?

If you upgrade from a OEM or retail version of Windows 7 or Windows 8/8.1 to the free Windows 10 upgrade this summer, the license is consumed into it. Because the free upgrade is derived from the base qualifying license, Windows 10 will carry that licensing too.

If you upgrade from a retail version, it carries the rights of a retail version.

If you upgrade from a OEM version, it carries the rights of a OEM version.

Full version (Retail):

- Includes transfer rights to another computer.

- Doesn't require a previous qualifying version of Windows.

- Expensive

Upgrade version (Retail):

- Includes transfer rights to another computer.

- require a previous qualifying version of Windows.

- Expensive, but cheaper than full version
