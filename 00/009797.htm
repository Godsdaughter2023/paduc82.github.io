ben schorr say:

It's not that you'd be without email - it's that some email will continue to go to your old tenant until DNS propagation completes. It's one reason why we recommend doing those migrations over the weekend - then late Sunday or early Monday migrating over any legacy email that might be in the old tenant's mailboxes.

https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Plan-your-setup-of-Office-365-eb926624-018b-4486-bf11-5fba6ee4d645?ui=en-US&rs=en-US&ad=US 

Actual downtime should be minimal to non-existent, but there may be a brief window where you need to check the old mailbox and the new mailbox because you may have some mail going to each. Doing it over the weekend (or another quiet period) should minimize the inconvenience to your users.

I've done lots of these migrations in the past - almost always without significant incident.

How do I turn my virus protection back on? - Microsoft Community

jiggyduffy say:

How do I turn my virus protection back on?

I am getting a windows security alert stating that my virus protection is turned off; however, when I open Windows Security Essentials, it appears the virus protection is running just fine. Any tips please?
dr. strangelove say:

Did you uninstall and cleanup all other realtime antimalware products before installing MSE?

Perform these checks:

Verify that you have removed all other  real-time antimalware products that were ever installed on your PC, including free/trial product that were installed when the PC was purchased (Norton, McAfee, TM, AVG, OneCare, Malwarebytes (paid), etc). Then, use the cleanup tools here:  List of anti-malware program cleanup/uninstall tools

 

Verify that Windows Defender is uninstalled (if XP), or Disabled (if Vista/W7).

Verify that you are up-to-date with SERVICE PACKS and updates (including .NET). Yes, it can take some time to do this.

Verify that your PC clock is correctly set.

Verify that you are using version 1963 of MSE (as of June 28 2010). For more information, review http://support.microsoft.com/kb/2254596 .

Remove Zone Alarm if you have it and use the Windows Firewall.

Verify that the Windows Firewall is on, and set to defaults. 

Verify that you are not using a proxy server.

Verify that you have “install updates automatically” selected in Windows Update (even though the MSE definition updates are optional).

In IE, reset all security zones to default level.

 

Then, restart your PC.

 

You may need to remove and reinstall MSE as well.

 

To improve performance, do a disk cleanup and defrag, and then perform a full scan with MSE (this can take several hours).

 

Examine the necessity of the number of startup programs you have. 

To examine your startup programs, go to START, RUN (or START, SEARCH) and enter MSCONFIG. 

Then, use the START UP tab to control the start up programs.

 

If the problem persists, submit a support case to Microsoft: https://support.microsoftsecurityessentials.com/?scrx=1
