Changing the margins through Format > Document won't alter things much, but the text will reflow, so check the pagination after you do that.

In Format > Document you'll see a dropdown menu ("Apply to") that will let you select what sections will be affected by the margin change. You may find you need one margin spec for the opening pages and another for the chapters.

Firstly, as this forum focuses on Office 365 for Business online service, we may provide limited information about this issue. Based on my experience, I suggest you try the steps below:
1. Quit Outlook and all other active applications. Then restart Outlook client.
  1) Press Command-Option-Esc to open the "Force Quit Applications" window.
  2) Select an application and click Force Quit.
  3) Then open the client again.
2. Open Outlook without running schedules or trying to connect to a mail server.
  1) Press Shift key and click the Outlook icon on the Dock.
  2) Quit Outlook.
  3) Open Outlook.
3. Remove Outlook Preferences.
  1) Quit all applications.
  2) On the Go menu, click Home.
  3) Hold down the Option key while clicking the Go menu, and open Library.
  4) Open Preferences and drag the following files to desktop.
    com.microsoft.Outlook.plist

    com.microsoft.outlook.database_daemon.plist

    com.microsoft.outlook.database_utility.plist

    com.microsoft.outlook.office_reminders.plist

    com.microsoft.Outlook.SyncServicesPreferences.plist
Note: If you cannot find one of these files, continue to the next. Some of these files may not have been created if an Outlook feature was not used.
  5) Open Outlook client. If the issue resolves, one of the files might cause the problem. Then you can isolate the file by moving them one by one back to its original location and reopening Outlook client.
