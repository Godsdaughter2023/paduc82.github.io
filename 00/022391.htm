To fix the occurred issue, you have two options. First one is uninstall KB3114409 and another is set a registry key to disable Safe mode.

To set the registry Key:

  • Press “Window” Key + “R” (Combine) >> Type “regedit” without quotes in the run dialog box >> click on “OK” button.


  • Browse to HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\​Software\​Microsoft\​Office\​14.0\​Outlook\​Security\
  • Right click on Security key and choose New, and then DWORD Value.
  • Type DisableSafeMode, and then press Enter.
  • Right-click on DisableSafeMode, and then click Modify.
  • In the Value data box, type 1, and then click “OK”.


  •  Restart Microsoft Outlook or reboot the computer.

Uninstall KB3114409

To uninstall updates: Open “Control Panel” >> “Programs & Features” >> Click on “View Installed Updates” (at left pane) >> Type KB3114409 in the Search Updates field. Select it and click Uninstall.

johnseddon_ejs say:

To update this Post in case it is of interest to anyone else thinking of changing an Office 365 Business Account to Office 365 Home Subscription Account.  These are my experiences/observations but please contradict or advise if you know different:

1.  The move from Business to Home is not supported by Microsoft in the way that the migration from Personal to Business is.

2.  Chat support lines from Business are unable to help about Home and vice versa.  You are on your own.

3. You cannot simply stop Business, you have to wait a period of time as it gradually steps down various stages leading to final expiry. This complicates the transfer if you simultaneously open a new Home account.

4 If you use the same logon email for your Home account as you used for Business then Home simply goes into a continuous Processing/Continued loop.

5. When Business finally expires, the Admin facility is still live.  This means your logon attempts will take you to your Business Admin pages, not your Home.  You cannot access Home Outlook or other apps.

6. If you have used Business to host your DNS, then you loose this too.  This means you cannot access your email account from any other email system.

7. For someone not wanting the full Business sharing functionality but, like me, have downsized operations the Home in theory can save you 50-80% of subscription fee over Business.

8. However tread very carefully - I don't think Microsoft want to encourage this migration route.

joeu2004 say:

ld238 wrote:

I try to count each value of a list containing numbers of 9.58 to 10.09.  From 9.58 to 9.81 everything is fine, from 9.82 to 10 it suddenly shows 0 even if these numbers are in the list!

ld238 wrote:

when I "pull down" from 9.8 and 9.81 it suddenly produces zeros a few lines lower. Just weird.

This is due to rounding issues with the binary representation of numbers internally.

The most reliable way to create the table of numbers from 9.58 to 10.10 is to enter 9.58 into B1, then enter the following formula into B2 and copy down the column:

=ROUND(B1+0.01; 2)

To explain further....

If you temporarily format B1:B53 as Number with 14 decimal places, you will see that starting with the values that should be 9.82 through 10.00, they actually appear to be 9.81999999999999 through 9.99999999999999.

That causes problems for COUNTIF, which treats two values as equal if they have the same appearance when formatted to 15 significant digits.

The infinitesimal differences starting with 9.82 are coincidental:  they arise when we drag the first two cells (9.58 and 9.59) down the column because Excel adds the difference between the 9.59 and 9.58 repeatedly.

FYI, you would get better results (in appearance) if you used the Fill Series operation instead of dragging down the column.  And in this particular case, that might eliminate the problems with COUNTIF.

But again, that is coincidental.  It depends on the starting value, the difference between adjacent values, and how many cells you fill.

Moreover, even the Fill Series results are not exactly the same as the binary representation of the intended constants.  So you might encounter problems with other functions, notably lookup functions like MATCH and VLOOKUP.

So again, it is more reliable to add 0.01 and round explicitly to the precision that you expect to be accurate, using the formula above.
