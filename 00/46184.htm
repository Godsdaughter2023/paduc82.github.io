graye say:

The terms "public" and "private" aren't really talking about the network itself.... instead they are talking about what services are enabled on each PC.  Private means that a PC will advertise its network shares (and other services) to it's neighbor PCs... and Public will not.

So, at home, you'd most likely configure all of your PCs to Private... so other PCs at home can see/use its network resources.   However, when you take a laptop to a coffee shop (via a publicly available WiFi), then you wouldn't want other coffee shop customers to see/use your network resources
