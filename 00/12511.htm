Are you insinuating that 1.5 or 2.0 amp charger is what the specification metrics are based upon?

I have a band but really haven't paid much attention to the charge time.  I wanted to point out that the current a device is capable of using is governed by the device, not the charger. You can't draw more than the charger can deliver, but the device will also not draw more than it is capable of using. The device draws power, the charger doesn't push it. This is a bit predicated on decent design, but I would expect this for this device. A 2-2.5A charger should be no problem. In fact I've used one on mine. Finding one higher than that may actually be difficult. 

For the skeptics, consider a 12v light bulb. You can hook that straight across your car battery, which can clearly deliver 200 or more amps, far more than the bulb needs, or uses. This isn't quite the same as charging a battery, in which you drive current backwards through the chargee, which creates heat, but the charging circuitry, and the inherent resistance of the battery will normally self regulate so that excess current isn't possible. Over voltage is a problem. Don't hook it up to more than 5v.

Installing Office365 Mac when you already have Office2011 - Microsoft Community

phillip m jones say:

Installing Office365 Mac when you already have Office2011

There have been several questions concerning Office365 when you already have Office2011 Standalone installed.

There has not been, Nor can I find any information to the contrary about how to replace Office2011 Standalone with Office365.

Such questions are met with Crickets chirping. So I am going the assumption the only way to convert is to completely uninstall Office2011 before installing Office 365.

Doing a Complete uninstall The following will Happen.

You will lose years worth of customizations:

  1. Menu
  2. Menubar
  3. Ribbon
  4. Any Macros you have added over the years
    (2011 has  been out.  Close to 4 years)

In next few sentences. I will attempt to help you mitigate some of the changes.

  1. Locate the My Templates, folder Copy to the Desktop (see if
  2. Locate The Mud Folder (Microsoft User Data) copy it to Desktop
  3. Look in Word, Excel, PowerPoint  Locate any Macros you have added see if you can find where to re-download them.
    if you have to re buy them That will just be the price of switching.
  4. Open Word, Excel and PowerPoint one at a time.
    Go to view menu in each application go to Toolbars
    Choose customize menus and Toolbars
    Open a screenshot Utility and take a Screenshot
  5. Repeat step 4 Excel and PowerPoint
  6. Now go:
    http://support.microsoft.com/kb/2398768
  7. Print those instructions
  8. Follow all the steps to the letter.
  9. If you haven't downloaded Office365 do so.
  10. Install Office365
  11. Before opening any of the applications Return
    The Mud Folder to Documents Folder
    The My Templates to the Templates Folder
  12. Follow your Screenshots and customize your Menbars, and Menus according to the your previous customizations.
  13. Reinstall all your Macros

In this video I've created I' show how to find the My Templates, and Mud folder:

Look at the video: http://goo.gl/F1xKWE (play movie in full screen mode)
