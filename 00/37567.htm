Does deleting the Compress old files registry key erase the Disk - Microsoft Community

westindian say:

Does deleting the Compress old files registry key erase the Disk Cleanup's ability to compress old files?

Original Title: Compress old files registry key?

I have a question.  By deleting the Compress old files registry key, did I just erase the Disk Cleanup's ability to compress old files?

a. user say:

These days, when hard disks are faster and space is less expensive than the good old days, it does not seem to make too much sense to compress old files anymore. 

If you leave the option enabled and have a million files on your system, your Disk Cleanup is going to take a long time or get stuck forever and nobody seems to know just what the definition of an old file is (how old is old?).

And if XP decided to compress some critical system files (like NTLDR), you will not be booting again in any mode until you decompress the file (how are you going to do that if your system will not boot?).

And if XP compress these old files and has to decompress them to use them, that will slow you down even more.

If you already deleted the key like this KB article suggests, I would leave it like that and your Disk Cleanup will be zoomin'.
