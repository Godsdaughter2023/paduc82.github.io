    Hi Reto,

    For example user@domain.com , alias includes both user and domain part. That means you can have both info@domain1.com and info@domain2.com if these are two different mailboxes. 

    Regarding your question about send as/ on behalf and full access permission, here is the answer:

    • Only Send permissions (without full access) will give you rights to send from that address and not to receive messages/ open the mailbox.
    • Full access will on the other hand give you right to open that shared mailbox and the messages, but you will not be able to send messages.

    So, the most important part to be able to receive the message is to have full access permission.

    • Sign on to https://outlook.office.com/owa with the account which has full access permission.
    • Click on user picture in the upper right corner of your screen.
    • Click on Open another mailbox and enter the full email address.
    • Now you can access to the shared mailbox.

    Option Explicit

    Function maxLeftStr(rng As Range) As Long
    Dim v As Variant, s As String
    Dim nc As Long, nr As Long, nStr As Long, ns As Long
    Dim i As Long, j As Long
    v = rng
    nr = UBound(v, 1)
    nc = UBound(v, 2)
    nStr = Len(v(1, 1))
    '// remove next for-loop if you want
    '// case-sensitive comparison
    For i = 1 To nr: For j = 1 To nc
        v(i, j) = LCase(v(i, j))
    Next j, i
    For ns = 1 To nStr
        s = Left(v(1, 1), ns)
        j = 2
        For i = 1 To nr
            For j = j To nc
                If s <> Left(v(i, j), ns) Then Exit Function
            Next j
            j = 1
        Next i
        maxLeftStr = ns
    Next ns
    End Function
