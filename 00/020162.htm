FIX: Outlook 2016 icon duplicated on launch when pinned to taskbar - Microsoft Community

corenabahr say:

FIX: Outlook 2016 icon duplicated on launch when pinned to taskbar

A few days ago I noticed that suddenly Outlook 2016 was creating a second icon in the taskbar when the app was open, and anytime I clicked on the pinned icon (not seeing that it was already open) another session would launch.

The thread I found on this topic in the MS community is currently locked (and was all the way back from 2015) so I can't respond there. I'm doing it here now to hopefully help others.

None of the suggestions in the previous thread worked for me. But after some fiddling, here's the easy fix I did to solve the problem without all kinds of crazy.

  1. Unpin Outlook from the taskbar.
  2. Go to C:\ProgramData\Microsoft\Windows\Start Menu\Programs
  3. Locate the Outlook shortcut in the list.
  4. Right-click and select Pin to Taskbar.

Note: As I was tinkering, I think I was using "Run as administrator" so not sure if that matters. Afterwards, I unchecked that and it now works fine.

Clearly a bug and I hope MS fixes this soon, especially since this happened back in 2015 and is still occurring in 2018!

You need to give your sequence a name, and that name should be before the *\ ALPHABETIC switch:

davidf2 say:

There is no universal text box resize feature in Publisher. There are some tools and approaches that could make the process easier.

 

Go to View > check Boundaries, guides and rulers. This will let you see the text box boudary even when the text box is not selected plus guide line and margin lines.

 

Go to Page Design tab > under Align To check both Guides and Objects. This will allow you to drag text box and 'snap to' a guide line that you can insert via Guides next to the Align To. You can put a guide line anywhere on the page and use it to line up your text boxes.

 

If you want all content including the text boxes to line up to one inch of the page edge you can also just adjust your margins. Go to Page Design >Margins and click the arrow to see the options. The margin lines will be visible and you can drag and snap to those margins. This includes drag and drop the whole text box or grabbing the resize mark on the side of the text box and dragging to the guide or magin line. If your text box was centered you can hold the Ctrl key down while you are resizing and the text box will resize equally in both directions.

 

Another approach would be to right click the text box > Format text box > Size tab and you can read the size of the text box. If you are using a standard 8.5 inch page and you want your text box an inch from the edge then make the width of the text box an even 6.5 inches. If the height of the text boxes vary from page to page then don't check the 'lock aspect' box. Once you ok out of that dialog after resizing the text box you could drag to snap to a guide or margin line, or you could go to the Home tab > Arrange group > Align > Relative to margin guides and then again click align and you will see the option to Align Center is now enabled. Click it and your text box will be centered on the page.

 

And one other approach similar to the last one. Select your text box and look to the bottom left of the page on the status bar and you will see two sets of numbers/measurements. Mouse over them and the second will say 'object size'. Click on that icon and a small table will popup and you can change the height and width of the text box on that dialog along with the placement on the page. If every text box is 6.5 inches, then you could also change the horizontal position or the x axis to 1 inch. Make changes to both the width and the x position and your text box would be resized to 6.5 inches and centered on your page.

 

Those are just a few ways to work through resizing your text boxes. If all your text boxes are linked from page to page you could also just create a new publication and a single text box on the first page, copy and paste the text from the first publication to the new text box and let Pulisher produce as many new pages as you need with the correct size text boxes.

 

I could have, and perhaps should have just said no to your question. Hopefully something else I suggested will make your task easier.

 

DavidF

What is the difference between Windows Live Mail and Outlook - Microsoft Community

keenyamickens say:

What is the difference between Windows Live Mail and Outlook

I mean besides the obvious, is there difference in the two that is noticeable or is it just a matter of preference?Does on work better with certain applications as oppose to the other?
hansv mvp say:

Windows Live Mail is free. Outlook is part of Office Home and Business and up, and hence not free.

 

Windows Live Mail offers e-mail capabilities and a calendar. Outlook offers these, plus tasks and notes.

 

Windows Live Mail does not have a programming interface. Outlook can be programmed using VBA code, both from within Outlook itself and from other applications.

 

Windows Live Mail doesn't work with Microsoft Exchange (corporate mail server). Outlook has full support for working with Microsoft Exchange.
