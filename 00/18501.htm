willy denoyette say:

WDDM 1.0 won't cut it, Windows 10 1607 requires WDDM 1.3 that's why MSBDA is WDDM 1.3 conformant.

WDDM 1.3 is not new it's a Windows 8.1 requirement.

WDDM defines a graphics feature set, for instance Miracast requires a driver that conforms to WDDM 1.3 (or higher) and a chipset that handles Miracast in the GPU chip (HW).

Same goes for other features like power management (think energy requirements) , video streams, cooling etc .....

When your chipset offers some feature then your driver needs to support it ; ex. the driver needs to probe the temperature in order to drive the cooling fan.

Running a WDDM 2.0 driver on a chipset with a lower feature score doesn't hurt but makes little sense, running a WDDM driver with a lower feature score than required by the Chipset, wil fail to drive the chipset feature(s).

Try this formula for "the minimum of the other numbers":

=MIN(IF(B1:B6<>"",IF(ROW(1:6)<>MOD(COLUMNS($B:B)-1,$A7)+1,B1:B6,99),99))

Note: This is an array formula that has to be confirmed with CTRL+SHIFT+ENTER rather than just ENTER.

99 is just a number greater than the number used in your set of data.

Note: If you only have 1 name per column, you have not defined what "the minimum of the other numbers" will be as there are no other number. If you want to return 0 in that case, just wrap the above formula in this:

=IF($A7=1,0, the above formula)

Hope this helps / Lars-Åke
