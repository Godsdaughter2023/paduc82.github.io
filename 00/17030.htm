Sync SharePoint Document Libraries Using OneDrive for Business - Microsoft Community

mattzaharias say:

Sync SharePoint Document Libraries Using OneDrive for Business

Split from this thread.

Bear in mind that SharePoint Document Libraries are NOT a good solution if your collection of files and folders is greater than 5000 items.  OneDrive for Business Desktop App will NOT sync more than 5000 items from a SharePoint Document Library.

This here makes SharePoint Document libraries a completely unworkable solution for many of our clients.  

Microsoft, PLEASE address this.  This has been a thorn in our side as far as recommending Office365 for over a year.  

Users expect to use OneDrive (or OneDrive in conjunction with SharePoint Online) in the same fashion as basically any of your cloud file sync competitors.   All of Microsoft's marketing states that OneDrive lets you share files between your team and coworkers via either OneDrive or OneDrive+SharePoint.  But there are a TON of asterisks/footnotes attached to that claim that folks are simply not aware of until they get deep in the weeds.  

In fact, the errors and limitations require so much research and time to find out the causes and quirks that I am sure that very few folks who are not actual IT technicians/systems administrators actually wind up discovering the reality.

  • OneDrive for Business 2013 (Groove.exe) sync client/desktop application syncs relatively slowly, has a distinct lack of transparency in terms of what it is actually doing, tends to absolutely DESTROY the performance of any CPU lower than an Intel Core i3 and sometimes, at a later date, completely fails on random client machines--requiring a total uninstall/full re-sync of all document libraries on that machine.
    • Of course, the user isn't aware of this until days or weeks later when their work has failed to upload in the meantime.
    • We (or the client) now have a hazardous and time-consuming consolidation task, let alone the technician time to remove and re-sync all the libraries and the bandwidth/download/sync time required.
    • And of course, the client is pissed because they *rightfully* blame the software/platform.
  • The 2013 sync client/desktop application also does not provide good information on the size, speed, or number of files it is syncing.
  • The 2013 sync client/desktop application will not sync more than 5000 items from document libraries.  This is untenable even if a small business with 3-5 employees uses the desktop application vs working in SharePoint Online via the browser.  
    • I'm not going to try to teach dozens and dozens of small 5-10 person businesses how to use Word and Excel Online.  
    • Reasons why: Folks are resistant to working directly in the browser; have proprietary software that needs access to local copies of the files; need features only available in the full local version of the Office software; or are simply incapable of understanding how to work on documents via a web-browser.
  • The Next Generation Sync client (no longer running Groove.exe in the background) syncs much faster and much more reliably than the 2013 sync client/desktop application.  
    • It behaves almost identically to a program like Dropbox.
    •  Apparently there is also no longer a 5000 item limitation on syncing from OneDrive online to the local OneDrive folder.
    • Nicely done and great progress with that version, OneDrive Team!  
  • However, there is a HUGE problem.  The Next Gen Sync Client REMOVES the ability to sync both SharePoint Document Libraries as well as OneDrive folders and files that are "shared with me".  
    • That's right--you can download and install the Next Gen Sync Client and it will sync fast and work very well, but now it only works for syncing YOUR OWN OneDrive content.  Nothing that is shared with you from other OneDrive users and nothing contained in Document Libraries.  The NGSC only syncs files and folders stored in your own individual OneDrive store.
    • I assume this because Microsoft is working on proper implementation of this feature given the fact that there must be significant under-the-hood changes between OneDriveFB2013(Groove.exe) and OneDrive NGSC2016 (OneDrive.exe), but I discovered these problems about 6 months ago and it does not appear that anything has changed in the meantime.

Dropbox Pro, Egnyte, and Google Drive do not suffer from what--in the year 2016--are ridiculous limitations for the desktop sync client (old/previous version or new/next gen).  I have Google Drive installed on my Windows 10 PC and it is syncing 8 different root folders, several of which have nearly 10,000 items in them.  I have never encountered any sync problems with any of our files stored in Google Drive--shared, unshared, or otherwise.

Apologies if my frustration is apparent.  I am still a big fan of Office365.  If the above could be addressed and Skype for Business brought anywhere near the functional level of a standard Cloud PBX, we'd be raving Microsoft fans (even though we roll a mix of iOS, Android, Linux and Windows at my shop).  The Microsoft products which work well (Windows 10, Office 2013/2016 desktop apps, Exchange Online, Office Online), work VERY well for our clients.

But Microsoft's marketing sometimes gets WAY ahead of the product development.  You could argue that yes, OneDrive for Business and SharePoint work properly together under certain, correct circumstances.  But that doesn't matter.

What matters is the end-user experience and that experience is that OneDrive for Business DOES NOT WORK for sharing files within a company and among coworkers.  The consumer/personal OneDrive application is more functional for this business use, which--frankly--should be embarrassing.

Understanding and using these products properly should not require A+, MCITP/MCP certifications and hours of research and testing.

neha singh msft say:

Hi Shwetha,

If the changes are made in an online document and then later synced with desktop version of Office document, then any change made in the un-synced document will be overridden by the online document. In this case, there’s no way to find the un-synced changes.

The best practice is to make a different copy of the changes which are made in the un-synced document and then save it on your desktop, so that if you decide to sync the original document, you’ll have a backup document. The other way is to sync all the changes in the document, in this way if you need the older version, you can at least restore it from version history.

We appreciate your understanding.

Regards,

Neha
