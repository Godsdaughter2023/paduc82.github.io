christine delaurentis say:

Inserting Images

Is there any difference (image quality, file size, etc) between using Insert/Pictures and dragging an image file into PowerPoint and dropping it on a slide?

Does PowerPoint handle them the same, or is one a better option over the other?

Thank you,
Christine

steve rindsberg say:

Hiya Christine,

The answer is a solid, very definite, uncompromising:  It Depends.

If you use Insert | Picture, you get an inserted picture.  Always.

If you drag/drop, you may get a picture or you may get an OLE object; that can be convenient in that you can double-click it to launch the app that created the image and load the image for editing, and it updates itself when you return to PPT.  OTOH, OLE objects can bloat your presentations, and if you move the pressie to another PC, the doubleclick behavior will depend on whether you have the creator app installed or not.

Other weirdness can result if you drag and drop an image from a web page.  It's fairly rare, but it can unexpectedly create links to the web site the image came from or even some completely different site.  This is not the kind of thing you want to hand off to a client. 

There are likely other differences that arise out of the different way OLE shapes are treated; for example it's possible that OLEs won't be compressed by default the way inserted pictures may be, depending on your options settings.  I haven't tried that one on for size. 

On the whole, if you want Predictable, I'd stick with Inserted pictures.

Or as the bumper sticker says, Beware of meddling in the affairs of dragons, for you are crunchy and good with ketchup.  I assume that holds true for dragondroppings as well.  Ewwwww.
