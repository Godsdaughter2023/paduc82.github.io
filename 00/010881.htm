doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

It sounds more like you have End Notes than Footnotes and if that is correct, on a Windows computer, the following macro would convert them to ordinary text.

I do not know if it will work on a Mac, especially one that is running Office 2011, which has been superseded by later versions that do have more Visual Basic ability, but even then, not yet the equivalent of Visual Basic on Windows.

' Macro created 29/09/99 by Doug Robbins to replace endnotes with textnotes at end of document

' to replace the endnote reference in the body of the document with a superscript number.

'

    Dim aendnote As Endnote

    For Each aendnote In ActiveDocument.Endnotes

        ActiveDocument.Range.InsertAfter vbCr & aendnote.Index & vbTab & aendnote.Range

        aendnote.Reference.InsertBefore "a" & aendnote.Index & "a"

    Next aendnote

    For Each aendnote In ActiveDocument.Endnotes

        aendnote.Reference.Delete

    Next aendnote

    Selection.Find.ClearFormatting

    Selection.Find.Replacement.ClearFormatting

    With Selection.Find.Replacement.Font

        .Superscript = True

    End With

    With Selection.Find

        .Text = "(a)([0-9]{1,})(a)"

        .Replacement.Text = "\2"

        .Forward = True

        .Wrap = wdFindContinue

        .Format = True

        .MatchWildcards = True

    End With

    Selection.Find.Execute Replace:=wdReplaceAll

techfreak_ say:

You get billed 3 days in advance. The minutes are not released until the subscription cycle completes.

Example:  

The first time you purchase the subscription it is January 6th at 11am. The 200 minutes allocation goes from January 6th 11am to February 6th 11am.  On February 3rd at 11 am it will attempt to charge you for the next month. If you used up your minutes before February 6th, your balance will remain at 0 minutes. On February 6th, your renewal will release your 200 minutes at 11am to be used from February 6th 11am to March 6th 11am. Your renewal will attempt to bill on March 3rd.

If you cancel you subscription, the subscription will still finish its cycle. Purchasing the same subscription over and over will not award you any new minutes. You might only double or triple bill yourself. Only purchasing a subscription of a different minute amount (like 60 minutes) before your subscription cycle ends (less than 30 days), will release new minutes for that new subscription. The 60 minute subscription will follow its own cycle and will renew in the same exact fashion, unless cancelled.

Most people do not understand:

1. The 3 day early renewal cycle to ensure payment covers the next month.

2. They cannot purchase the same subscription over and over again to get multiple minutes in < 30 days (200 minutes X how many times they purchase it).

3. Minutes are never released early, even if you consume all the minutes from the prior month. The goal is to find a balance where you consume all your minutes near the end of the month, prior to renewal.

4. Cancelling a subscription early does not allow you to purchase the same subscription early. You have to wait until the original cycle ends.

Ex. Buying a 200 minute subscription for a month and consuming all the minutes in 1 day. Even if cancelled and repurchased, the balance will remain at 0 minutes until the next cycle begins at the beginning of the following month (> 30 days). You will not get another 200 minutes on day 2 if you cancel and repurchase the subscription on day 2. These are time-based subscriptions not anytime minute bundles.

  3. Open Printers & Scanners
