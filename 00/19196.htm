christophmn say:

In the RFC 5322 Standard the field From has a different purpose than in Lotus Notes, because it is always identifying the owner of the mail.box. Instead of using the field Principal to identify the owner of the mail.box in which an email was written, the field Sender is used to identify the effective user who produced the email. The field Principal is not (or better said no longer) accepted in the email header by many email providers and bounced back referring to being not RFC 5322 compliant.

If an assistant/secretary sends invitations for someone else this might lead to the described problem, as the field Principal is showing another name than the field From, which is reflected in the email header. To avoid this, all your invitations should be sent with identical fields Principal and From. If you prefer to show the effective user sending the invitation instead of the owner of the mail.box, where the invitation is sent from, add the field Sender with the email-address of the assistant/secretary to be RFC 5322 compliant. Unfortunately your Lotus Notes recipients will no longer see, that the invitation was sent by someone else than the shown Principal (except you explicitly reprogram the mail template), but at least every person invited will be getting the invitations.

  5. Restart the computer after its updated and verify.
