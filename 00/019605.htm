How do I included an unsuscribe link into my outlook email templates?
  • Hello 
  • Open Outlook
  • Start the Email Merge wizard, move through the steps in the wizard till you reach the step to edit/compose your email
  • Start typing your email
  • Once done, please your cursor where you would like to add your unsubscribe link (I personally recommend right at the moment of your email)
  • Enter a text of your choice for the subscribe link for example “To cancel your subscription please click Unsubscribe”
  • Highlight all the text that you would like to create as a hyperlink. In this example, I will only highlight the word “Unsubscribe”
  • Now click the Insert tab > click Hyperlinks. This will open the following screen:

  • On the right side under “Link to:”, select E-mail Address
  • In E-mail Address, enter the address on which you would like to receive the unsubscribe notification.
  • In Subject: enter “Unsubscribe from Newsletter” or something similar with the word Unsubscribe in it
  • Click OK

This will now create a hyperlink in your emails. Your newsletter subscribers can request to opt out by simply clicking on the link and sending the notification email to you.

How to Manage to unsubscribe using Email Merge

You will now be required to use the Do Not Send To list feature to manage your unsubscribe requests.

To Open the Do not Send List:

In Outlook go to your Inbox, click on the little arrow beside(below) the EmailMerge button on the Toolbar/Ribbon.

Click Do not Send List. The application will open the following screen:

 

1. Add an email address to Do not Send List:

Click Add, the application will display the following screen:

 

You can add an email address(s) to the Do not Send list using 1 of the 3 options below:

  • Manually add the new items to the list – Select this option if you would like to type in the email addresses manually. (Enter 1 email address per line)
  • Email address (sender) of currently selected email(s) – Select this option if you want Email Merge to automatically add email addresses for the emails you have already selected in the current folder. The email address of the sender will be added to the Do not Send List.
  • Emails in the current folder with unsubscribe/bounce backwords in subject

How to use variables in vlookup - Microsoft Community

tom wikle say:

How to use variables in vlookup

I would like to use a variable that captures the selection made by a combo box and use the value in the vlookup vba code. The code is like this:

txtFabric1 = Application.WorksheetFunction.VLookup(Range("ASI_Affiliates!A5"), Range("ASI_Affiliates!Construction_Type"), 4, False)

I would like to replace "ASI_Affiliates!A5" with a variable. Is this possible?

kergga say:

The short answer is Yes.

 

Assign your combobox value to a variable, and you should be able to use it directly in the equation.

 

Have you already attempted this, and if so, what problems did you run into?

 

Did your original (without using a variable) work as expected?

 

Personally, in this type of situation I prefer to use Application.Match and then (on a separate line) return the value from the desired column, something like:

 

MyTargetValue =Sheet1.range("A1").value 'let's say I was using a data validation list, or this cell was linked to a combobox

 

FoundRow = Application.Match(MyTargetValue, Sheet2.range("F:F"),false) 'although in practice, I won't use the whole column- I'll dynamically select the end of the used range, or just pick a fixed value bigger than your data but way smaller than 1M rows)

 

If not(iserror(FoundRow)) then

FinalValue = Index(Sheet2.range("K:K"),FoundRow) 'use the same number of rows as in the prior range for Sheet2

Else

FinalValue = "The value was not Found"

Endif

 

Sheet1.range("A2").value = FinalValue

 

'------------------

The advantage of this approach is that you don't have to worry about what order your columns are in, you can return data from the left of your row with the source data match. Also, if your data layout changes, all you have to change is the selected column, you don't have to mess with changing named ranges or anything else.

HTH,

Keith

rich007a say:

What if there isn't a 1 in A2?  What then?

In which columns are the varied selection of numbers from 1 to 5?

In which columns are the varied selection of numbers from 1 to 3?

Where is this formula to go (B2:B21)??

 

If [varied selection of numbers from 1 to 5] is in D1:L1 and [varied selection of numbers from 1 to 3] is in D2:L2 then this will sum the cells in D2:L2 that have 3 in the cell above:

=SUMIF($D$1:$L$1,3,$D2:$L2)

To wrap that in an IF function to test the value of A2, you can use:

=IF(A2=1,SUMIF($D1$:$L$1,3,D2:L2),0)

Change the last zero to whatever you want the cell to show if A2 is not 1.

 

But I suspect, just entering   

=SUMIF($D$1:$L$1,3,$D2:$L2)

in B2 and dragging down will do what you need.

Hope that helps.
