  • If you used the Format> Paragraph> Line & Page Breaks - Page Break Before method simply click in the paragraph following the break then go back into that dialog to clear the check.

julie fka profjulie say:
When you first get a windows phone, pull it out of the box and turn it on, you'll be prompted to set up a primary Microsoft account on the phone.  This involves inputting a Microsoft username & password into the phone.  It sounds to me like the Microsoft account you set up on the phone is the same windows live account you set up later on under Email+Accounts....so you are syncing to the same data source twice.

"Turning off" a Calendar in the Calendar hub does not stop the calendar from syncing to the phone...it simply turns off your view of that calendar when you open up the Calendar hub.  Since the phone is still syncing to the Microsoft calendar, you are still getting the same appointments synced to the phone twice (once for the Microsoft account and then again for the separate Live account). 

This works the same way in the People hub....if you open up the People hub, tap the three dots in the lower right hand corner of the screen, then tap "Settings" you can then select the "filter my contact list" and "turn off" the contacts that are stored in an account that is syncing to the phone, but this does not remove those contacts from the phone.  When you search the People hub, you'll see the contacts are in there at least two times.

When you add a new appointment or contact, you are given an option to choose the account where you want the new appointment or contact stored (the phone will sync this new information to that account).  The list of accounts you see relate to the accounts you've set up on the phone.

You can edit any accounts you set up under email+accounts and select the specific items you want to sync to the phone. Contacts & Appointments are never synced from POP3 accounts but they can be synced from any kind of Microsoft account (live.com, outlook.com, or hotmail.com account), except for the primary Microsoft account.  If there are any Contacts, Appointments, or Tasks stored on your primary Microsoft account, these items will always sync to your phone and you cannot stop them from syncing to your phone.  The only thing you can turn off from syncing on a primary Microsoft account is email.

If you go edit these accounts by going to Settings > email + accounts, For any secondary Microsoft accounts, you can edit the settings for these accounts (go to Settings > email+accounts) and select the specific items that are stored in that online account that you want to sync to the phone - these options are found under the "Content to sync" heading - you can choose Email, Contacts, Calendar, Tasks. Turning off the syncing of these items here, will prevent the data from ever being synced to your phone.

Since it appears you've used the same login information when you set up the Microsoft account that you used when you created the Live.com account, you should go to Email+Accounts and delete the Live.com account (press & hold your finger on this account until a menu pops up, select delete). (afterwards, you'll want to go back into the Calendar hub and turn the view back on for your Microsoft account so you'll see them again).

It sounds like you've set up the Outlook Hotmail Connector to sync the data from Outlook on your computer to your windows live account. As long as you've only set this up once, using the same windows live username & password on both computers, the same data should be synced amongst each computer and your phone.  I don't know how you initially uploaded your contacts from Outlook to your live.com account, but if you did this by exporting the data from Outlook to a CSV file and then importing that file to your live.com account, then when you got the Outlook Hotmail Connector set up on the computer, you may have duplicated that data when it first started syncing the data from Outlook to your Live.com account.  Could you describe the steps you took or provide a link to the instructions you followed to initially upload your contacts to your live.com account?
