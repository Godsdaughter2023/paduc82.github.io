dan at it associates say:

Retail licenses (the traditional packaged discs you would buy in a store, or if directly purchased and downloaded from Microsoft) for Office Home and Business 2010 permit you to concurrently activate two (2) installations of Office H&B 2010, one on your "main" computer and the other on your "portable" computer.

 

While the retail licenses does permit you to move the license from computer to computer, it does NOT permit more than two concurrent activations.

 

In order to properly activate Office H&B 2010 on a new replacement computer, you need to uninstall one of the other installations still in your possession, decrementing the activation count down to one, in order to make room for the second activation, which would be on the new computer.  The installation on the stolen computer will theoretically no longer count against your allowed activations, but due to the way Microsoft Office Product Activation works, you will most likley have to do Telephonic Activations and speak with a live activation representative to conduct your activations, and it may be necessary to document your rightful possession of the product, by emailing or faxing an image of your Certificate of Authenticity---but the activation rep will explain that if necessary.

My understanding of your situation is that you have Office Home and Business 2010 retail license.  This license permits two concurrent activations, not three.  You activated this license on three computers.  One of those computers has been stolen, and the computer still had that installation of Office Home and Business 2010 on it.

 

Now you have two computers in your possession, and both have your Office H&B 2010 installed.  If that's correct, you have reached your limit of two concurrent activations, nevermid the activation on the computer that was stolen.

 

If want to install H&B onto a new computer, you have to remove it from one of the two that are in your possession that have Office installed.

 

In order to speak with a live activation representative, rerun the Activation Wizard and choose the option for Telephone Activation. Dial the number and when the automated system picks up, do not enter or say anything into the system, which should then transfer you to a live activation representative.

 

If you call the number during normal business hours for your country/region, you are more likely to be connected to an activation center whose staff speaks your language natively.
