You can convert a Publisher print publication to a web publication, but some things do not convert exactly to html and you will probably need to tweak your layout, formatting or design in order for the publication to render correctly as a web page and enjoy cross browser compatibility. In general if your web publication renders correctly in both IE and FireFox then it will render correctly in all the major browsers.

To convert a print publication to a web publication you File > Convert to a web publication and the wizard will walk you through the process. You might be careful to save a copy of your print publication.

Reference:  Convert a print newsletter for use on the Web:  http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/publisher-help/convert-a-print-newsletter-for-use-on-the-web-HA010067644.aspx?pid=CH100793371033 for an introduction to the process.

With all this said, you might want to consider just converting your print publications to PDF files and uploading those to your website and linking to them. This way you won't have to worry about the differences between print and web formats and layouts. You can use the PDF add in to convert the files to pdf files or a third party tool such aswww.primopdf.com which is a nice freebie.
