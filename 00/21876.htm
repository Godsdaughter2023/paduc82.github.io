Here is a macro you can run. Select a range with incorrectly entered times before starting it.

Sub CorrectTimes()
    Dim rng As Range
    Application.ScreenUpdating = False
    For Each rng In Selection
        If IsNumeric(rng.Value) Then
            rng.Value = TimeSerial(rng.Value, 100 * rng.Value Mod 100, 0)
        End If
    Next rng
    Application.ScreenUpdating = True
End Sub

vijay a. verma say:
No. That folder is just for synching to Onedrive not for reading from Onenote. So, even if you take the backup, it will be unusable for you.

swischner say:
Vijay Verma is absolutely correct on this. I can add a few information to it that may be of interest.

The local files you discovered on your mac is the OneNote cache. It is in fact a copy of all opened notebooks but in a special binary and fragmented format for sync purposes only. It's not usable as a backup because you can't restore any information from it.

There IS a way to backup your notebooks fro OneDrive to a local (or external) drive, but those files could only be restored with OneNote 2010/2013 or 2016 for Windows. That Windows version has a built-in backup which is at least working semi-automatic (I won't get to any more detail here assuming you only have a Mac).

Now, another little good news. There is a trick that lets you create local backup copies in the same format that the Windows OneNote uses even with your Mac. A prerequisite is, that your notebooks on OneDrive are stored inside a folder on OneDrive (like "Documents") and NOT in the OneDrive main directory.
In that case you could login to your OneDrive using a web browser (the local OneDrive app on the Mac won't help!) and download that complete folder. This creates a ZIP file that also includes all OneNote data in a format that you could at least restore on OneNote 2016 for Windows (!) and from there re-sync to OneDrive in case of a problem. This is not convenient and not supposed to be possible at all by Microsoft but at least downloading those files to your Mac could make you sleep better. IF the emergency occurs and you need to get hold of a Windows PC with MS Office to restore your notebooks that may be the smaller problem.
