Figure caption numbers based on current section number for multiple - Microsoft Community

jw5000 say:

Figure caption numbers based on current section number for multiple section levels

In MS Word 2013 I am trying to figure out a way to generate figure caption numbers that contain the current section number.  It is straightforward to do this with a single section level (e.g., "Figure 4-3"), but it gets trickier with multiple section levels. 

I have considered tackling this by following these general steps

1. Create a dedicated caption for each level (such as "Figure-L3" for level 3, which might yield a caption/number along the lines of "Figure-L3 4.2.3-1")

2. Delete the "-L3"

3. Highlight the remaining caption/number (i.e., "Figure 4.2.3-1")

4. Save as a quick part

5. Repeat for each section level

The problem with this approach is that if I ever need to change a section level (from Level 2 to Level 3, for example), I would have to re-do all the captions and cross-references.

So, is there a way for the caption to "know" in which section it is located?

Thanks!

Jon

suzanne s. barnhill say:

A given caption can include only one level of outline numbering. You can include "chapter numbering" from Heading 1, in which case you'll get Figure 1-1, Figure 1-2, Figure 2-1, etc. If you use lower levels, then you'll get the numbering from the most recent heading at that level; if you use Heading 3, and the most recent Heading 3 occurs before a more recent Heading 1 or 2, then that's what you'll get. That's one reason we generally advise using the top-level numbering only (the actual chapter number in most cases).

The other (and better) reason is that anything beyond that is so complex that it confuses the reader and doesn't actually add any functionality. Even if your chapters have hundreds of figures, it will be much easier for readers to find them if they're numbered sequentially (Figure 1-1 through Figure 1-99) rather than have to search down through multiple levels of headings.

I bought the Pixel 3XL last week (7 Jun 19), took me 2 days to figure out syncing the Outlook Calendar to Google Calendar. 

1) Remove your account in Outlook. Restart phone.

2) Go to Gmail. Scroll down to Settings. Click on the '3 Dots' on top right corner. Go to Manage Account. Select Exchange (with the Gmail icon).

3) Setup as per your corporate Exchange email settings. 

4) Press Next and you'll come to a page where you can select Calendar, Contacts and Email to sync. I selected only Calendar and Contacts. You will see the sync status complete.

5) Go to Outlook app that you downloaded from Play Store. Setup your email as normal.

6) Go to your Google Calendar. Voila, you should see your sync-ed calendar.
