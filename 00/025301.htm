jay freedman say:

Textured text fill has indeed been removed from Word 2013. There is a rather roundabout way to get the old WordArt, though.

  • Start a separate document and save it as a Word 97-2003 (*.doc) file.
  • In that document, use the old WordArt tools to create the text you want and fill it with a texture.
  • Select the finished WordArt and copy it to the clipboard.
  • Switch to the newsletter document or template (which is Word 2013 *.docx or .dotx format) and click in the location where you want the WordArt to appear.
  • Click Paste > Paste Special and choose Picture (PNG). The old WordArt will be converted to an ordinary picture and pasted at the cursor location.

The resulting picture won't be editable as WordArt. If you need to change the text, go back to the .doc file and make your changes there, then repeat the copy/paste special.

Now, when you look at your document, a Page Break appears as a 2" wide blue line with the words Page Break in the middle. Please confirm whether you actually see page breaks, or something else?

  1. Close Word.

If your email server type is IMAP, then what you only have to do is to set up Outlook as IMAP and everything will be automatically synced when you connect your Gmail to Outlook. If you’re using a POP3 email server then you have to import Gmail to Outlook.

 

Here are the following methods that you can follow to merge your Gmail account and Outlook:

 

Method 1:

 

If you don’t have Office 365 installed in your Chromebook, we suggest that you use Outlook.com to merge your accounts. The hyperlink will lead you to a page where you can log into your Outlook.com account. You can connect your Gmail account to Outlook by following these steps:

 

1.  Once you’re already logged into Outlook.com, click the Gear icon.

2.  Select Connected accounts from the dropdown menu.

3.  Under Add connected accounts, select Gmail or Other email accounts if you have another email you want to connect.

4.  Select where you want to store your imported emails.

5.  Click OK.

6.  Log into your Gmail account.

7.  Click Allow.

 

Method 2:

 

If you have Office 365 already installed in your Chromebook, then you can use it to combine all your emails. You can read the article Ways to migrate multiple email accounts to Office 365 for the methods on how to combine other emails to Office 365.
