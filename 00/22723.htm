ken sheridan say:

If the form has been switched while open from its default state of form view or continuous forms view the value returned will be the sum of the acObjStateOpen (1) and acObjStateDirty (2) constants, i.e. 3 not the value of the acObjStateOpen constant (1).  So you need to use Boolean logic to determine if the form is open:

If Application.SysCmd(acSysCmdGetObjectState, acForm, _
    "frmContacts") And acObjStateOpen = acObjStateOpen Then

This compares the bits of the 1 and 3 values like so:

00000001
00000011

giving:

00000001

While you can't remove the functionality from the application, you can most definitely hide it when you feel distracted by it.

When you select the Mail Navigation (keyboard shortcut CTRL+1), you'll only see your Mail folders.

Objects cannot be inserted/embedded into the cells of an Excel worksheet... It simply cannot be done. Images can be inserted into the file, but they are stored 'on' the sheet, not in cells. PDFs are actually a graphic format, not Word documents, so see Excel Help on the topic: Add or replace a picture.

What you're looking to do overall is possible but requires a fair amount of VBA programming. Quite frankly, IMO you're better off to invest in a Database app that is designed to work in that way.
