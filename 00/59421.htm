kapil arya [directly] say: Hello rjm4,

I'm Independent Advisor and Microsoft MVP, here to help you with your question.

You can use Group Policy to block automatic updates. This ensures updates are downloaded/installed only when you want them:

1. Open GPO snap-in by running gpedit.msc command.

2. Go to Computer Configuration -> Administrative Templates -> Windows Components -> Windows Update.

3. Double click Configure Automatic Updates policy setting, select Enabled. Then under 'Configure automatic updating' section, choose 2 - Notify for download and notify for install.

4. Click Apply, followed by OK.

Multiple reboots will finally disable auto updates.

Note: Above steps not applicable to Windows 10 Home, let me know if you're on Windows 10 Home, I'll provide alternative method.

To disable Microsoft account, try these steps:

1. Open Settings > Accounts and click Your info.
2. After confirming that the account is set up to use a Microsoft account, click Sign in with a local account instead.
3. Enter the password for your Microsoft account to confirm that you're authorized to make the change, and then click Next.
4. On the Switch to a Local Account page, enter your new local user name and password, along with a password hint.
5. Click Next to sign out from the Microsoft account and sign back in using your new local account.

Let us know if this helps!

Your Recovery Partition was imaged on your Hard Drive during the Manufacture of your Computer by the Manufacturer for these reasons.

 

1.To reinstall Windows from in case of a System crash, by pressing particular Key or Keys at Boot.

F10 or F11 or Alt + F10 are a couple of sequences different Manufacturers use.

Your D: Recovery Drive is not to be used for backups, defragged, etc.

Backups should be kept on an External Hard Drive.
