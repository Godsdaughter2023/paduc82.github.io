Hi MichaelMinneci,

Regarding the error “A recipient wasn‎'t found for "*** Email address is removed for privacy ***" on the target. Create a recipient of the appropriate type for this migration on the target and try again”, I would like to share a workaround first:
1. Run Get-Mailbox -SoftDeletedMailbox to list the deleted mailbox users. If the affected users are included, run Remove-Mailbox -PermanentlyDelete to completely remove them.
2. Run Get-MailUser -Identity <user> | FL GUID to get the GUID of the user.
3. Run New-MoveRequest -Identity <GUID> -Remote -RemoteHostName 'mail.domain.com' -RemoteCredential $cred -TargetDeliveryDomain 'domain.mail.onmicrosoft.com' -BadItemLimit 10 -LargeItemLimit 20 to try a new batch to move the mailbox.

Note: please replace all the domain names included in the commands above.

Meanwhile, if this cannot work either, please choose an affected user and run Get-Recipient -Identity "*** Email address is removed for privacy ***" | FL from both Exchange Online and Exchange on-premises. Hi Amir,

Mails are delivered to the Junk folder because of one of the following  reasons:

  • The user has set the junk mail filter to store email messages in the Junk folder.
  • Upon evaluation, the Hotmail anti-spam technology forced the email messages into the Junk folder.

Please sign in to your Outlook web-based account then check your account settings for this might be the cause on why your emails are being delivered to your Junk folder:

To check the junk mail settings:

  1. On the Outlook.com page, click Options, and then click More options.
  2. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.
  3. Under Choose a junk e-mail filter, choose the level of protection you want, and then click Save. If the Exclusive level was set, messages from senders not on the contacts or safe senders lists will have been sent to the Junk folder.

In addition, we suggest that you set a rule for your incoming emails. To do this, kindly follow these steps:

1. Log in to Outlook.com.

2. Click on the Gear icon at the upper right, beside your name.

3. Choose Options.

4. Under Customizing Outlook, choose Rules for sorting new messages.

5. Then follow the instructions on how to set your own rules.

6. After you set up your email rules, click Save.

pamcaswell say:

W2007 has  the ability to create list styles .  Like other styles in Word, list styles can be created, modified, deleted, and shared (copied or moved to other documents).   When a list styles is moved to another document, it will show up in the  multilevel list styles gallery in  a seprate section.  I think this is what you are looking for.

Unfortunately, regular multilevel/outline lists  cannot be turned into list styles, which will have to be created from scratch.   It sounds like you know your way around multilevel list, so I'll just point out that in W2007, after placing the cursor in the first level one heading in the document, click "define new list style", name the style, and then click Format > Numbering.    (I'm looking at W2010 beta screens), which may be slightly different.)

 

HTH,

Pam

 

 

 

 

stefan blom say:
But note that list styles do not replace associating numbering levels with paragraph styles. For stable numbering, you might want to do both in Word 2007: create a list style, define numbering for the various levels, and associate each level with a paragraph style. Then apply the paragraph styles to text as appropriate.
