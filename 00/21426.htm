judy-monkstown say:

Side heads using Frames push adjacent bullet points to right.

I have created a document that uses side headings (i.e. headings in margin) that are created using frames. The 1st line of the paragraph following a side heading aligns vertically with the 1st line of the side heading.

When any paragraphs that are adjacent to the side headings are bulleted, those paragraphs are pushed a few centimeters to the right. This is also true if the paragraphs are numbered instead of bulleted. This occurs only to bulleted/numbered paragraphs that are parallel with the side heading frame. Any bulleted/numbered paragraphs that are lower on the page than the frame are positioned correctly, as are all non-bulleted paragraphs.

suzanne s. barnhill say:
Again, I suggest you adjust either the size of the frame, the position of the frame, or the distance from text. By default, there is 1/8" between the right side of the frame and the text. If your frame is flush to the left margin, then text will be pushed in. If it's 1/8" or more outside of the left margin, document text will be unaffected.
