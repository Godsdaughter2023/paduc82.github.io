Is there a way to delete the SMS backup that is currently "wherever it is" on OneDrive, create a new one and then do another restore to the new device?

vincentchoy say:

CSP is a just a licensing model. There are other licensing models like Online, Open, EA, Syndication.

The difference between CSP and other models is you own the entire relationship as a partner. From billing to support.

For the other plans, support is owned by Microsoft. For Online subscription, Billing is also owned by Microsoft.

1. Business Essentials and E3 are plans that the customer picks. Business Essentials is a basic plan for SMB providing Exchange Plan 1, Sharepoint Plan 1 and Skype Plan 2. E3 is a Enterprise Premium Plan that provides Exchange Plan 2, Sharepoint Plan2, Skype2, Office Pro Plus, Azure RMS, DLP..etc. There is no logic why Microsoft will move a Biz E plan to E3. There are also other plans like Business, Business Premium, E1, E4 and E5, Kiosk that you did not mention.

Office365 allows multiple subscriptions in a single tenant. Meaning you can have 100 Biz Essential, 50 E1, 120 Exchange Online Stand alone...etc.

Managing those licenses are done by customer O365 admin portal. In the traditional licensing schemes, the customer buys the licenses online or via a partner. This licenses then get reflected in his admin portal and he then creates the users and add the licenses from O365 admin.

If customer elects to buy CSP licensing, subscribing with a CSP distributor like Ingram Micro, the partner will be given a portal to order the licenses for a particular customer. Once ordered, the license will be reflected in the customer's Office365 admin portal. The customer can then create users and assign those licenses like the traditional models. As a CSP partner you can also choose to delegate admin into the customer o365 admin portal and assign the license for him.

2. Under the CSP model, I understand Microsoft bills monthly and calculates usage daily. So the customer has flexibility to add or remove at will, unless your CSP distributor sells you license on a annual basis. In which case you should bill your customer on a annual basis with the same back to back term. That is a purely commercial arrangement which you need to talk to your client.

3. If your customer moves to biz premium to E5, a number of things need to happen. First you have to order the E5 licenses for him. The E5 licenses need to then be reflected in the customer's o365 admin portal. Then you have to re-assign the customer user licenses from Biz Premium to E5. Then you can proceed to cancel the Biz Premium subscription. If under CSP then it will be at the CSP portal of your distributor.

However, that is not the end of the story. Business Premium is bundled with Office Standard, while E5 comes with Office Pro Plus. The client users will find that they will get an error message of unlicensed Office Standard since E5 comes with a different MS-Office version. They will need to uninstall the current version of MS-Office and install Office Pro Plus that comes with E5.

Hope that explains

twarden say:

It turns out I solved my own issue. So I'm just posting my solution, as others seem to have the same issue. This worked for me.

 

Rather than trying to get text to wrap within the auto-shape (which Jay confirmed just won't work without manual intervention) -- I created an "exact opposite" image (ie. it takes up all the space where I DON'T want text to show up), and set the border line color to white (ie. to make it invisible). I then set text wrapping on this "invisible" shape to tight. Going back to my original shape, I set fill color to white, and transparency to 100% and voila, my text now wraps within the first, visible shape, and doesn't go outside it's borders.

 

note: I just had to do free text in the word document, as if you were typing a letter or something - I had to forget about trying to add text to the specific shape I had created.
