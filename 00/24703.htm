ken sheridan say:


(1)    Criteria 1 = [StaffID]

(2)    Criteria 2 = [Date]

(3)    Criteria 3 = [AccountCode]


You will need a query, as Duane describes.

Moreover, if the above is correct, you have the delimiters for the Criteria2 and Criteria3 variables transposed in:

    & " AND Criteria2 = '" & Forms!FormName!Control2 & "'" _
    & " AND Criteria3 =#" & Forms!FormName!Control3 & "#")

Also, a date literal must be in US short date or an otherwise internationally unambiguous format, so, unless the database will only ever be used on a system whose regional date setting is to US format, the value of the variable should be formatted appropriately, e.g. to the ISO standard of YYYY-MM-DD.  So, taking both points into account, you should have:

    & " AND Criteria2 =#" & Format(Forms!FormName!Control2,"yyyy-mm-dd") & "#" _
    & " AND Criteria3 = '" & Forms!FormName!Control3 & "'")

Conditional Formating cells to change color based on certain times of - Microsoft Community

jessekyro say:

Conditional Formating cells to change color based on certain times of the year

I'm creating a tracker for some work requirements and need the cells to turn green when the date is in a certain range, and red if it's not.  There's a monthly, quarterly, and yearly block.  I want the monthly to turn red if the date entered is not within the current month, the quarterly if it's not within the quarter and the yearly if it's not in the year.

I just can't figure out how to do that without having to go change the rule every month, quarter or year.

Help please?

jasles say:

Select the block of monthly cells and then select conditional formatting. Use two rules, the first one for the red color and the second one for the green color:

=NOT(AND(YEAR($A$1)=YEAR(NOW()),MONTH($A$1)=MONTH(NOW())))

=AND(YEAR($A$1)=YEAR(NOW()),MONTH($A$1)=MONTH(NOW()))

These are assuming the cell that has the date that you want to compare to the current date is A1.

For the yearly block, do the same, except use these formulas:

=NOT(YEAR($A$1)=YEAR(NOW()))

=YEAR($A$1)=YEAR(NOW())

For the quarterly block, do the same, but use these formulas:

=NOT(AND(YEAR($A$1)=YEAR(NOW()),ROUNDUP(MONTH($A$1)/3,0)=ROUNDUP(MONTH(NOW())/3,0)))

=AND(YEAR($A$1)=YEAR(NOW()),ROUNDUP(MONTH($A$1)/3,0)=ROUNDUP(MONTH(NOW())/3,0))
