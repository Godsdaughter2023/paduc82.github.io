cmdrkeene say:
TV's are designed to "overscan" the area of the input signal.  This is why video editors have to make sure that captions and labels fit inside the "title safe area".

Some TV's designed to be used as product displays or large monitors (such as at retail stores) have options to disable the overscan, but most consumer models do not.

Your best bet is to use the "native resolution" of the television, or the exact number of dots it actually has, so that it doesn't have to stretch or scale the image.  Sometimes your graphics card settings has overscan options too.  I know for sure that most ATI model video cards do.  To access these, right-click your desktop and go to Screen Resolution, then click the Advanced settings link.
