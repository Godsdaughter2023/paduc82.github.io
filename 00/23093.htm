You can use

Format([DateField], "mm/dd/yyyy")

to convert the date/time field DateField to text; replace mm/dd/yyyy with your favorite date format.

boblarson say:

Steve -

In Access you don't need to do any explicit conversions for a Union Query.  You can union a date field and a text field with no problem but the resulting output is text.  You can't do that with SQL Server so you should be mindful of that for potential issues when upsizing.  But in Access the ONLY requirement is that the number of fields match, that's it.

mr cheese say:

I found the cause of this problem from this link:

https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/msoffice/forum/msoffice_outlook-mso_windows8/outlook-2013-the-operation-cannot-be-performed/5de1c079-bcde-4d1f-a5f3-2fdb0dc4eb7c?rtAction=1484788257643

My issue is caused in conjunction with Kasperksy Office Security.

I have found by turning off the COM Addin "Kaspersky Outlook Anti-Virus Addin", then the problem with Drafts appears to be resolved.

I have also found that the problem would only consistently occur if an email was auto saved first, rather than just saving it as a draft first.

I emailed this information to Kaspersky support, and they have confirmed that this is a known issue to them, and that it should be fixed in the next major update.

They also assured me, that even with the addin turned off, that the Kaspersky product will still do its job in detecting malicious emails that may come through.

Hopefully we will see this problem go away one day soon.

mr cheese say:

I just wanted to post this update.

Since removing my security software, Kaspersky, and switching to a different product, I am have not had the problem I originally posted anymore.
