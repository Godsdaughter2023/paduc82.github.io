Each workbook has its own colour palette.

The only ways to change a colour palette are:

  - or the VBA equivalent: ActiveWorkbook.Colors = Workbooks("OtherBook.xls").Colors

josh_onstrategy say:

We need a function that will allow you to know the distance between two sets of GPS Coordinates

While doing some data work, I was curious how far our clients were from us and the data set I had included GPS coordinates.  I thought it would be easy to know how far line of sight two points were but its actually incredibly complicated.  The formula you need to figure this out is:

Miles:
=ACOS(COS(RADIANS(90-Lat1)) * COS(RADIANS(90-Lat2)) + SIN(RADIANS(90-Lat1)) * SIN(RADIANS(90-Lat2)) * COS(RADIANS(Long1-Long2))) * 3959

Kilometers:
=ACOS(COS(RADIANS(90-Lat1)) * COS(RADIANS(90-Lat2)) + SIN(RADIANS(90-Lat1)) * SIN(RADIANS(90-Lat2)) * COS(RADIANS(Long1-Long2))) * 6371

I honestly dont know if I could have understood these formulas when I was in trig 20 years ago in high school.  It would be great to have a formula like =gpsdistance(lat 1, long 1, lat 2, long 2, 1 for Miles and 2 Kilometers) so that you can easily measure the distance between two points.  

nathan li msft say:

Hi Josh,

Because the distance between two points on the surface of the earth is an arc, and calculating the length of which will usually involve application of trigonometric functions. I understand that the complicated process of calculating the length of a arc in a circle could make the formula unfriendly for reading, especially when the formula includes multiple trigonometric functions such as SIN, COS, together with the use of RADIANS function that turns angle degrees to radians.
