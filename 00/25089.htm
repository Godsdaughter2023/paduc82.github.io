rob koch say:

There's no need, since Windows Phone 8.1 was always a far more secure operating system than desktop Windows, so it's unaffected by the types of file based attacks that have traditionally plagued that more ancient operating system design.

In fact, if you download and review the Windows Phone 8.1 Security Overview from the Official Microsoft Download Center, you'll discover that many of the security design principals found in that document are what Microsoft has been slowly adding to the Windows 10 operating system over the last few years.

The idea that antivirus apps are required for these more limited devices is flawed, since it's based on the assumption that the word "Windows" implies malware.  In truth, it's the outdated design of the older desktop Windows of the 80's, 90's and 2000's that has embedded this in the minds of most unknowledgeable consumers.

Even the early attempts to significantly improve this situation such as Windows Vista were in truth far better than what had come before, but the stubbornly stupid assumptions by most consumers, including many of the supposed "techies" and other self-appointed "experts" led to many of these improvements being ignored or even worse, circumvented by the PC owner himself.

In other words, you don't need AV for Windows Phone 8.1 precisely because Microsoft never allowed the levels of access to the filing system and even the operating system itself, which is stored in firmware, that were what had allowed these malware issues to exist in the much older and less secure desktop Windows design from the early 1980's.

Me.Fiscal_Year_14_15_Total.Visible = Me.Start_upFundsRequested
