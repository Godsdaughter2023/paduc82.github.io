rich michaels - mvp (greatcirclelearning.com) say:

Hi Kam,

Personally I wouldn't shy away from using Word for a community directory and the reason I say that is in my experience community directories are usually more than just names, address and pictures. They often have a cover page, acknowledgement page, a TOC (table of contents), and maybe even business card advertising in the back.

For the body of the directory I suggest a 3-column table with each row of the table being a family in the community.

The first column would contain the last name of the family and the column is "hidden" so that it will not be printed but can be used for sorting. The second column contains the name, address, and phone number of the family. And the final (third) column contains the picture the person or family provides you.

To make each row of this multi-row table consistent, you set the height of the row to be "Exactly X measurement high." When you insert the picture the family supplies, this will then automatically resize it to fit and you will have consistently sized rows.

When people leave, you simply delete their row. New community member rows can be easily added at the end of the table and then the entire table selected and resorted based on the non-printing column one value (last name).

Hope this helps

    Going to take a guess and say you dragged over the field called <Name> instead of the individual fields that comprise the name field (click the + sign beside the field to see the component parts). 

    The <Name> field (FullName on the contact detail screen) is a "derived" field from the component parts and formatted according to the default setting for the FullName field.

    Seldom if ever should a <Root> field (one with a + sign beside it) be selected for export.
