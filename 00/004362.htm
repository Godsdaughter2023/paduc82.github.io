Hi MaxxHale,

Microsoft recommends that you follow these guidelines to help reduce junk email:

  • Don't reply to junk e-mail, even to ask to be removed from the sender's mailing list. The sender may simply use that response to confirm that your e-mail address is valid, and continue to send you junk e-mail. To report the message as junk e-mail, select the message, click Junk.
  • To report the e-mail message as a suspected phishing scam (Falsely claiming to be a legitimate business to send you to a fake website or scam you into giving out private information.), click Mark as – Phishing scam.
  • Remove your e-mail address from any web page, newsgroup, or bulletin board available on the Internet.
  • To remove your e-mail address from newsletters or mailing lists that are on your safe senders list, you can click Unsubscribe. Microsoft notifies the people who created the newsletter to stop sending it to you.
  • You also minimize spam emails that you have been receiving by configuring your junk mail filter to Exclusive.

I suggest you to set your Junk email filter to Exclusive. If the Exclusive level was set, messages from senders not on your Contacts or Safe Senders lists will be sent to the Junk folder. By default, the Junk folder will automatically delete messages after 10 days. This is more convenient as we don't have any control if senders are randomly changing their address. Follow the steps below on how to set to Exclusive and check if this helps:

  1.  Sign in to your Outlook.com account.

  2. Click cog or gear icon then select Options.

  3. Under preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.

  4. Under Choose a junk email filter, select Exclusive.

Blocked Sender's List:

  1. Go to your Microsoft account’s Inbox page.
  2. If you are using the old user interface of Microsoft Hotmail, click on Options (located on the upper right corner of your screen, next to a question mark icon) then More Options.   3. From the Hotmail Options screen under the category “Preventing junk email”, click Safe and blocked senders.
  4. Click Blocked senders.
  5. In the Blocked senders list, add the name or domain (the part of an e-mail address that follows the “@” sign, such as hotmail.com) that you now want to block mail from.

 

Sweep:

  1. Sign in to your Microsoft account.
  2. Go to your Inbox.
  3. Right above your Inbox, please click on Sweep dropdown arrow.
  4. Please choose, Delete all of them? Before clicking Delete all, you can tick the box for Also block future messages.

Note: Before doing Step 4, you need to select at least one message before you can do this. To select one or more messages, just check the box before each message.

teylyn say:

Hello,

The Networkdays() function returns the number of weekdays between two dates. It can also take into account holidays.

If the start date is Dec-15-2013 and the end date is Jan-15-2014, the subtraction

=EndDate-StartDate

will return 31 days.

Networkdays returns 23 days.

=NETWORKDAYS(StartDate,EndDate)

If Dec 25 and 26 and Jan 1 are included as holidays, Networkdays returns 20 days.

Excel 2010 and 2013 also has the Networkdays.Intl where you can specify which days of the week should be treated as weekend.

Repeating backgrounds - Microsoft Community

kleach24 say:

Repeating backgrounds

How can you stop a background from repeating when creating a website in Publisher?  I am using just one color fading from top to bottom and looks fine in the editing screen; but when it is previewed, it repeats as you scroll down the page.

davidf2 say:

I can reproduce your issue and it appears that this is by design. Both Publisher 2003 and 2007 appear to produce a background image that is just 865 pixels long that repeats itself if the page is longer than 865 pixels. 

The only workaround I could come up with is to pull the background image from the index_files folder after publishing the web files to my local computer. You can spot the gradient background image if you use the thumbnail view when you open the index_files folder. It is 8 X 865 pixels. I then opened that image in my image editor and resized the image to the length of my page content. For experimental purposes I just doubled it and made a 8 X 1730 pixel image at the same 96 ppi. Then I went back to my Publisher page Format > Background > More backgrounds which opens the Fill Effects dialog > Picture tab > Select Picture and browse to where I stored my new 8 X 1730 pixel background picture. Now when I do a web page preview the background gradient extends just one time through the end of the page, but does fill the padding to the right of the content as it should in the browser window.

The problem with my workaround and what you want to do is that you really destroy the impact or effect of the gradient by stretching it over a 1730 pixel page. The change in gradient is hardly noticeable until you start scrolling down, leaving most of the first screen of my page dark and the second screen light. Perhaps that is why MSFT chose to go dark to light in 865 pixels...so it would be noticeable in the first screen. Perhaps there is not much point in using the gradient if you can't see the change in the first screen and stretch it over a long page???

Maybe consider switching to a vertical gradient background instead of the horizontal. You again get the repeating gradient, but in my case the background image is 1153 pixels wide and in most cases I will not be viewing any web page in a browser winow wider than that...and at worse my content is not any wider than that.

macropod say:

You could use code like:

With Application.FileDialog(msoFileDialogSaveAs)
Reset:
'The second filter is the DOCM filetype
  .FilterIndex = 2
  'Show & test the dialog result
  If .Show = -1 Then
    'If they've changed the file type, disallow the save
    If .FilterIndex <> 2 Then
      MsgBox "You may only save in the docm format"
      GoTo Reset
    End If
    .Execute
  End If
End With
