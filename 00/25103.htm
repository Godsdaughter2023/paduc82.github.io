Again, that is NOT how relational databases work. One of the main principles is to eliminate redundant data. Data should exist in ONE place only. You should have a table of member. cbarnhorst say:

You are confusing "registration" and "activation." 

Previews are test copies but not free copies.  All copies of Windows 10, release or preview, require activation.  If you do not have a Windows 10 license for your device you need to purchase one from the Microsoft Store.

In order to activate Windows 10, your device requires registration with Microsoft's servers as a Registered Windows 10 Device.  You must own or purchase a Windows 10 license to register a device.  Once a device is licensed, it will activate any copy of Windows 10 of the same edition the device is registered for. 

Licensing was changed for Windows 10 as an anti-piracy measure.  Windows 10 no longer has activation data on your hard drive that can be hacked.  The data needed for activation is now kept on Microsoft's servers along with the unique hardware IDs of your device.  That is the registration and is called a digital license.

Note:  The purpose of a product key now is to register a device for a specific edition of Windows 10.  Once the device is registered, the product key is not needed again no matter how many times or by what method Windows 10 (same edition) is re-installed.

jay freedman say:

Please try these two tests, and reply here to tell us what happened:

  1. Open the document that causes Word to stop responding, and also create a new blank document -- either click File > New and click the Blank template, or press  the Ctrl key and the N key together. Copy an equation in the problem document, and paste it in the blank document. Does it succeed, or does Word stop again?
  2. Open a new blank document, and create a new equation there. Copy and paste the equation in that document. Does it succeed, or does Word stop again?

If test 1 causes Word to stop, but test 2 succeeds, then there is something wrong with either the equation itself or the document that contains it. In that case, try deleting the equation and creating it again. If that doesn't help, then try saving the document as a Rich Text (RTF) file, and open that file and save it again as a Word (docx) file.

If both tests cause Word to stop, there is something wrong with Word -- possibly an add-in or a registry entry. In that case, use the troubleshooting steps in http://support.microsoft.com/kb/921541 -- but leave the registry changes (steps 3 and 4) until after you've tried all the other steps without success.
