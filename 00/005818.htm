When

Yes, Office 365 is a general high level marketing name. It is currently applied to bundles of Office programs released in 2016. On the PC side "Office 365" started out providing Office 2013 applications. Later this year PC "Office 365" subscriptions will provide Office 2019 applications when they are released (I haven't heard if there will be MAC 2019 applications this time).

Currently for marketing purposes MS identifies programs by the year they are released, ie on the PC side: Office 2010, Office 2013 (Released Jan 2013), Office 2016 (actually released Sept 2015) and the announced upcoming Office 2019 (to be released some time in 2018).

On the other hand, developers use "version" numbers to identify specific releases of programs. IE Office 2007 was "Version 12", Office 2010 was "Version 14" (someone in marketing was superstitious, hence the skipped version), Office 2013 is "Version 15", Office 2016 is (co-incidentally) "Version 16" etc. Within the "version numbers" there are minor "build" numbers to identify specific changes after the initial release.
