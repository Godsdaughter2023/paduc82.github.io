a. user say:

IP addresses are assigned by your router - they are not assigned by Windows.

To assign an IP address to your computer, sign in to your router's administration console. Each manufacturer's administration console is a little different, but if you look through the settings you will find the section where IP addresses are assigned.

Take note of which IP addresses are reserved for DHCP; there will be a range of addresses that have been reserved. Assign an IP address outside that range to your computer, and that will be a static address.

Your router may identify your computer - actually, its Ethernet adapter - by name so you'll be able to distinguish it. In any case, you can always identify it by its MAC.

While you're at it, it's also a good idea to assign static IP addresses to any printers you have. That will make it easier for devices to find the printers on the network when it's time to print.

Not to worry, this is a standard procedure that all routers use.

Most times it will crash and it will tell you what the driver is, but sometimes it will crash and won't tell you the driver.
