You should NOT be entering this into one field. You should have separate fields for each component of this block. If you need to concatenate into one block then use a function to do so.

marshall barton say:

If you put all that stuff in a single field, you'll have a terrible time trying to use any specific item in the field.  If the database never, ever will have to know about the individual pieces of information, then just enter it as a big text string using Control+Enter to start a new line in the string.

 

Saying "Never, Ever" is another way of saying that it will happen sooner or later.  So it is better to design for it now than it is to have to come back at a future time and redo your entire operation.  Remember that it is easy to put things together and difficult, or even impossible, to take them apart.

 

In your case, you could enter each line in its own field and put the separate fields together by using a query with a calculated field vaguely like:

UnivAddr: UniName & Chr(13) & Chr(10) & Person & Chr(13) & Chr(10) & Title & Chr(13) & Chr(10) & . . .

 

The query result can then be displayed on a form or report for viewing, which can and often should be different from where/how it is initially entered.
