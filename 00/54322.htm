azam k say:

Hi,

 

As per the design you would not able to hear sound from both speakers and HDMI for a single application at one time. Generally, in a normal circumstance we can only use one device as the output device.

 Example, the speakers sound is muted when the earphone is plugged to the computer.
This might be possible if you can have two different sound cards or you might have to use a splitter to get sound in both the speakers and HDMI.

As a work around you can try these steps and check if it helps:

  1. Open Control panel, go to sounds, select Speakers as the default playback device.
  2. Go to the "Recording" tab, right click and enable "Show Disabled Devices".
  3. A recording device called "Wave out Mix", "Mono Mix" or "Stereo Mix” should appear.
  4. Right click on the new device and click "Enable".
  5. Right click on the new device and click "Set as Default Device".
  6. Double click on the new device to open the Properties window.
  7. Go to the "Listen" tab, click on the "Listen to this device" checkbox
  8. Select your HDMI device from the "Playback through this device" list.
