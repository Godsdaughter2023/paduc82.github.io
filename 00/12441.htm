trent mcmurray say:

I actually have a blog post that gives step by step information on syncing Outlook to your hotmail. here are the steps and the link to the information: here is the link to the article http://www.windowsphoneconcierge.com/index.php/2012/02/26/its-a-live-its-a-live-how-to-sync-outlook-2010-with-windows-phone-7/ 

If you follow these steps you should have all your contacts backed up to your device and to your Hotmail account:

 

Unlike previous versions of Windows Phone, Windows Phone 7 does not directly  sync personal data with Outlook on your computer.  All Contacts and Calendar  sync is now performed wirelessly with cloud services like Hotmail/Live, Google,  or Exchange.  For home and small office users of Outlook without an Exchange  server, syncing your Outlook data is still possible, but requires setting up a  Live account in Outlook, and using the Live service as an intermediary. This is  accomplished by downloading and installing Microsoft’s “Outlook Connector” software, available here: http://office.microsoft.com/en-ca/outlook-help/microsoft-office-outlook-hotmail-connector-overview-HA010222518.aspx

1.     After installing the Outlook Connector for Hotmail/Live, you can add an  account in Outlook by clicking “File,” the “Info” tab (if not already selected,)  then clicking “+ Add Account”.

   

 

2.      Enter your credentials in the resultant pop-up box as shown below and select “Next.” Then click “Finish” on the following screen after Outlook verifies the  account information.

   

 

Your Live account is now connected to Outlook.  Now we need to move our  Contacts and Calendar data from the Outlook Personal folders into the newly  created Live folders.

 

3.     Go into Outlook’s Contacts view, and select all of your contacts by  holding down the “Ctrl” key and pressing “A”.  (This is usually abbreviated in  help and how-to guides as “CTRL-A.”)  This will highlight all of your contacts.

   

 

4.    Now select Move > Copy to Folder (you could also select “Other  Folder,” but this would move the contacts rather than copy them.  By copying  them, we’re leaving a backup set in the original folder in case something goes  awry.)

   

 

 

5.    In the pop-up dialog box, expand your Hotmail/Live account with the symbol on  its left, then select the Contacts folder under it and click “OK”.

   

 

6.    You might want to view the folder by selecting it in the left hand  pane to verify your contacts were copied.

   

 

The Contacts will now sync to the Live server, and be available in the  Live web interface, the Windows Live Mail clients, or any phone that supports  Exchange Activesync, like Windows Phones.  Copying your Outlook Calendar data is  a similar process, but requires an extra step to insure we get all of the  Calendar data.

 

7.   Switch to your Calendar, and then select File > Change View > List View  to see all of your Calendar items in a single list.

   

 

8. Now, like with Contacts, we select all of the Calendar items with “CTRL-A”

   

 

9.   Copy them with Move > Copy to Folder

   

 

10.     Select the Calendar folder under the Hotmail/Live Account, then click “OK”.

   

 

11. You might check to see the Calendar appointments copied to the folder by  selecting it in the left pane, like we did with Contacts.

   

 

Now, like our Contacts, our Calendar items are now in the Live folder, and  will sync to the Live server via the Outlook Connector, to be available on our  devices, in Windows Live Mail or the Live.com web interface.

 

Once you are satisfied that all of your data has synced to the Live cloud,  you can now make the Live folders the Default Personal folders in Outlook, so  that they are pre-selected each time you start Outlook.

 

12.     Select File > Info > Account Settings,

   

 

13. Then, in the “Account Settings” pop-up box, select the Data Files tab,  your Live account, and then “Set as Default”

   

 

14.  A dialog box will pop-up warning that you are changing the default  folders.  Select “OK” and restart Outlook.

   

 

   

 

Now Outlook is syncing over the air with Live, almost as if you were  hooked to an Exchange server. Sign in to Live on your WP7 phone (or use m.hotmail.com as your Activesync  server in WM6.x, or other Activesync-compatible phone) and you now have a  two-way, automatic, over-the-air sync with your PC’s Outlook data.  If you have  Outlook installed on other PCs, like your laptop or netbook, install the Outlook  Connector and sign in to the Live account with those PCs, and your Outlook data  will sync between all of your connected PCs and devices.  If they don’t have  Outlook, you can download the free Windows Live Mail software for those PCs  instead, and access your Outlook data on them.  Alternately, you can view your  Outlook data in the Live.com web interface from any internet connected PC
