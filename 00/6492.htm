adrian_321 say:

Microsoft Band 2 randomly shuts down FIX!!!

tl;dr: prevent the charging pins from contacting the metal part on the battery housing with tape or foil or the Force.

Michael Morcos found that out weeks ago in this thread:

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/band/forum/msband2-band_hardware/microsoft-band-2-powers-down-and-wont-power-back/1e4500d4-ce05-46d3-a75a-153535a0b1d7?page=1

Unfortunately his post can hardly be found in the (by now) massive thread. That's why I wanted to open up a new topic to make it easier for people with the problem to find a solution.

Michael found out that the reason are the charging pins touching the metallic battery housing which propably leads to a short circuit (or some sort of unwanted current flow). The fix is quite simple:

Either you put tape on the charging pins directly (which isn't really handy as you'd have to remove the tape every time you want to charge the Band) or you tape the counter part (metal part around the battery) so that any contact between the pins and the housing is prevented.

I posted earlier this weekend that I had the problem. Tried out the tape fix and I didn't have a dead Band a single time throughout the whole day. Although I played around with the mechanism and squeezed it here and there, trying to replicate the short out. Then, at the end of the day with the Band still running fine, I removed the tape, put it back on, squeezed it once and boom - instant shutdown.

I guess that the problem is the battery housing's side. It's built of two different metallic parts and after some time of wearing the Band, their radii don't line up anymore allowing the charging pins of the clasp to physically connect. Although the pins are a little recessed, these 0.5 mm are just not enough. A second version of the Band with non conductive clasp materials, more recessed pins or a different internal wiring (if that's possible) could solve this.

Now, despite knowing the issue and how to prevent, I sent back my Band yesterday. Microsoft isn't some startup that needs to be supported. It's a grown up company where things like a fundamental hardware failure in one of their high-end products should definetly be communicated. And I'm also not willing to tape a 250 pound device after 8 weeks. When I am on my 4th Band (as some people really are) maybe I'll rather tape it than sending it back a 5th time.

Hope I could help some of you out there!
