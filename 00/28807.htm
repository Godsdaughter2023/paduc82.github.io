hi KJ

on an almost totally different track  . i have a sales system that tracks product sales and on the record stores the date/time of the sale . . the week number and the month.

Usually storing the week and month would be considered redundant . . but they act as FK to the weeks table and the months table.

makes creating quearies quite easy.

Your Fortnight pay table sounds like its the same as my weeks table.

yours starts on a sunday - mine on a monday.

below is the function i use to calculate the week number

my table starts with week 7  - end date of 16 feb 2003 ( week 1 was first week in 2003)

for this to work - your payperiod-key MUST be sequential. - I set my table up in an excel - them imported it.

Function KCWEEK(DDMMYY As Long) As Long . .  (Aussie format)
On Error Resume Next
KCWEEK = 0
KCWEEK = Int((DDMMYY - 2) / 7) - 5373
End Function

so the number 2 = the fudge number to end on a sunday ( start on monday)

7 = days in week . . 14 for the fortnight

5373 . . number of weeks from day zero 31/12/1899 to first week in 2003. ( the offset)

im sure you can do the math from here.
