I used to have the same problem with short battery life of my Nokia C5-00 which bothered me for almost a year.     jllatham say:

    Assuming that your columns are A and B, then these two formulas would do it:

    =COUNTIFS(A:A,"A",B:B,"B")   along with    =COUNTIFS(A:A,"B",B:B,"A")

    You said you have a lot of entries, so Excel 2010's ability to accept entire columns without row numbers may be a good thing for you, especially if the lists keep growning.

     

    The Excel 2003 and earlier versions of those formulas would be (and note that in those versions, you must specify row numbers, although you can include empty rows in anticipation of the lists growing)

    =SUMPRODUCT(--(A2:A9="A"),--(B2:B9="B"))   And  =SUMPRODUCT(--(A2:A9="B"),--(B2:B9="A"))

    those could be written like this to allow for growth down to row 100 before having to edit the formula:

    =SUMPRODUCT(--(A2:A100="A"),--(B2:B100="B")) And =SUMPRODUCT(--(A2:A100="B"),--(B2:B100="A"))

    with the same results.

     

    Instead of the "A"  and "B" entries in those formulas, you could reference cells that hold those values so you can easily change to look for other combinations such as A+G (G+A).
