how to make non-OEM CA-42 cable work - Microsoft Community

mycatsisbernie say:

how to make non-OEM CA-42 cable work

I recently purchased a non-OEM supposedly CA-42-compatible cable from eBay. It was shipped with no driver disk or instructions. I guess I shouldn't have been surprised when it didn't work.

I decided to figure out how to make it work. After a few hours of Googling, I was able to solve my problems. Since the solution wasn’t obvious, and I couldn’t find the solution in this forum, or in a single web page anywhere else, I decided to post what I did, in the hope of helping someone else. This solution is for PC Suite 6.85.14.1 and Windows XP.

It turns out that many of these cables use a different USB Vendor and Product ID (VID and PID) than the official Nokia cable. Windows uses the VID and PID to associate your cable with a device driver. So when you plug in your cable, you get an error that Windows couldn’t find a device driver for the cable, even though you have the latest Nokia drivers and/or PC Suite installed.

When the Windows New Hardware wizard reports that it can’t find the driver, uncheck the “don’t try again” box, and exit from the wizard. Right click on “My Computer” and select “Properties”. Click the “Hardware” tab. Click “Device Manager” button. You should see your device marked in yellow. Right click your device, and select “Properties”. Click the details tab. Note the VID and PID (4 hexadecimal digits each)

Next go to
http://www.qbik.ch/usb/devices/showdevcat.php?id=12
Enter your VID into the “Quick Search” box. Look for a match on both VID and PID. Now you know what kind of driver to look for. If it can’t find your VID, look here:
http://www.linux-usb.org/usb.ids
Or try Googling for it.

Most of the drivers can be found here: http://www.citronid.com/download.asp
Or you can use Google to look for one. It seems like Prolific 2303 and Ark 3116 are the most common. Disconnect your cable, download and install the driver, reboot if asked, and reconnect the cable. Now your cable should be recognized by Windows. Plug the other end of the cable into your phone. My 6820 reports "Data Enhancement Detected" when the cable is attached to the phone.

Go back to device manager, and look under “Ports”. You should see your cable with a port number of COM4 (or thereabouts). Note the COM port number.

Here’s the final step: Start PC Suite. Go to Menu item Settings/Manage Connections. Make sure “Serial Cable CA-42” is checked. Click “Serial Cable CA-42” (to right of the check box), click Configure, and select your COM port number from the drop-down list. Click OK and Close.

Now when you “Click Here to connect a phone”, PC Suite should be able to talk to your phone.

Hi,

There could be a number of possible issues related to a mailbox not syncing properly when using Outlook 2013. Below are some common techniques you can try to resolve these kinds of issues.

First Option - Root your mailbox

Unfortunately, Outlook doesn’t detect this automatically but you can set it manually.

To set the root folder path for your IMAP account:

1. File > Account Settings > Account Settings…
2. Double click on your IMAP account.
3. Click on "More Options…"
4. Click on the Tab "Advanced"
5. Option: Root folder path, this should just show "Inbox"
6. Click "OK" to exit windows.

Second Option - Sync groups

Outlook 2013, perform the following steps:

1. Click the "Send/Receive" tab.
2. Click "Send/Receive Groups"
3. Click "Define Send/Receive Groups."  It should be the second option from the bottom.
4. Click "New..."
5. Type in a Name for the Group
6. Click OK.
7. Choose the IMAP account on the left.
8. Check the "Include the selected account in this group" box.
9. Make sure "Send mail items," "Get folder unread count for subscribed folders," and "Receive mail items" are checked and "Download complete items including attachments for subscribed folders" is selected.

The Goal is to make sure both Tabs match and have the folders you want to sync listed.
5. If any folders are missing the Icon you can select that folder and then click on "Subscribe"
