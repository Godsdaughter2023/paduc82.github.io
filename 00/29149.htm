I assume that the "Toshiba Disk Drive" is your Western Digital external USB drive (shown in Device Manager as WD32 003B-00KFA0).

I don't know what the unknown 1 GB partition is on your main (internal) disk, but that's not important now.

I never noticed before the "removable storage - libraries" section of Computer Management, but I found the following --> http://www.microsoft.com/resources/documentation/windows/xp/all/proddocs/en-us/rsm_enable_drive.mspx?mfr=true  You might see what result you get from right-clicking on the entry under "libraries" for the SD card and "enabling" the drive.  Better still, right-click on that entry and post back with the list of options on the context menu.  Perhaps something there will suggest a better solution for you.

Bottom line, however, is that notwithstanding your positive experience in reading the card in your card reader, I suspect that your card reader is not capable of writing to your SDHC card.  SDHC was proposed in 2005, the first card reader controllers were built in January 2006, and the first cards hit the market in August 2006.  If your computer is ten years old (and therefore its card reader is ten years old), then your card reader was made in about 2003 -- several years before the SDHC spec was developed.

Historical note:  MS released a "hotfix" for Windows XP that added support for SDHC cards > 4 GB (KB 934428), but this fix was included in service pack 3 (which you have).  If you're feeling compulsive, check the 4 files listed in KB 934428 and confirm that they are at or newer than rev 6.0.4069.3259.

sarahkong say:
This maybe an issue with a system file being corrupt.
You can try a couple things.

Right click your start button and select Command prompt (Admin) to bring up elevated Command prompt.
In Command prompt type sfc /scannow and press enter.
Restart the computer.

If that doesn't work then try this,

Right click your start button and select Command prompt (Admin) to bring up elevated Command prompt.

In the Administrator: Command Prompt window, type the following commands.
Press Enter key after each command:

DISM.exe /Online /Cleanup-image /Scanhealth

DISM.exe /Online /Cleanup-image /Restorehealth

Important: When you run this command, DISM uses Windows Update to provide the files that are required to fix corruptions.
To close the Administrator: Command prompt window, type Exit, and then press Enter.
Restart your computer.
