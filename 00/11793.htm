Getting Outlook notes (palm memos, originally) onto a Windows Mobile Smartphone

I had a palmpilot for years,, then a Palm Treo smartphone, and never had problems going back and forth between these devices and the palm desktop software.  I recently changed to a Windows Mobile smartphone through Verizon (htc Ozone).  I successfully transferred all my palmpilot information onto Windows outlook on my computer, and then (via ActiveSynch) put as much information as I could onto the new phone.  The contacts and calendar transferred flawlessly.  However, I have a lot of outlook "notes" (what were called "memos" on my palmpilot) that I need to get on my new phone, but have no idea how to move them, or where to move them to.  Any suggestions?  Do I have to get an app for this?  If so, which one?
chris de herrera say: Hi,
When you sync with Outlook, did you enable the option to sync the Notes?  This is part of the options in ActiveSync or Windows Mobile Device Center when you connected your device and setup a partnership.  You can add it when the HTC device is connected to your PC.

There are some limitations that you should be aware of.  The largest note that can be synchronized is approximately 16k.  The notes that contain attachments may not sync.

ortleib say:

I played with this a bit before getting your reply, but couldn't see how to do this from ActiveSync.  I ended up just buying and downloading an app called SmartphoneNotes from Handango.com.  It does exactly what I wanted it to do.  After reading some of the reviews about this app, there seems to be a bit of frustration from others that Windows Mobile doesn't automatically just do this, and that you have to buy an app to fill this gap.  Folks seem especially bothered because Outlook is a Windows program.  The Palm memos-->Outlook notes works well -- not sure why Windows notes doesn't just automatically transfer it to a windows mobile phone.  However, my problem is solved for under $20, so I'm happy now.
