stanimal21 say:

Outlook.PST files are not meant to sync across devices; they're designed to work purely on the machine they're on. The content you want to sync is probably mail, calendar items, tasks, etc. and you need one of two things to sync between multiple devices:

  • Exchange Server - meant for corporate environments
  • Outlook.com, Calendar, People - meant for the consumer

The contents of your mail, calendar items, and contacts will actually be stored on the server while your client machines (desktop, laptop) receive updates from the server, not the PST file (this is how it's worked for years, nothing new). The PST file is purely for storing information specific to Microsoft Office Outlook including archived data, which is data that's no longer stored on the server, only on your desktop/laptop.

 

As for your notes, there's a better option for storing them: OneNote. Moving them would be very time consuming but it allows you to sync them between devices easily. Please note, Outlook 2013 and other versions don't sync EVERYTHING to Outlook.com; only the basic functions like mail, calendar items, and people are synced to Outlook.com whereas if you were using an Exchange Server, it would sync everything (I think).

 

To make your migration successful, you must connect Outlook 2013 to your Outlook.com account and then you can COPY all of the mail from your PST file, using Microsoft Office Outlook, into the folders you want them stored in through your Outlook.com connection; I did this once a long time ago. You'll see two different account connections through Microsoft Office Outlook and you can copy/paste/move mail and items from one set of folders to another; you can do the same with calendar items as well, except a calendar appears for Outlook.com and you copy the items over. Once copied to the other account in Outlook 2013, it will sync everything to Outlook.com and you'll now have access to them on your laptop and web browser, and possibly your phone too.

 

I hope this made sense, it's quite extensive.

Actually, Outlook.com is the "mothership". Outlook.pst files are purely how Outlook 2013 and previous versions actually store the data on your computer. You could effectively delete that file, connect Outlook 2013 to your Outlook.com account again, and it will rebuild itself. The server is always the final storage medium for all information, not your desktop.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

When you insert a manual page break (Ctrl+Enter) in a table, you split the table, and the empty paragraph is the result. To create a page break without splitting the table (which also allows your heading row(s) to repeat), apply the "Page break before" property to the row you want to appear on a new page.

To do this, select the row, go to Format | Paragraph, select the Line and Page Breaks tab, and check the box for "Page break before."

Alternatively, you can right-click in any cell in that row (the first cell is advisable), choose Paragraph, and then select the Line and Page Breaks tab.

If you will be doing that often, you may want to add the ParaPageBreakBefore command to a toolbar. It doesn't have a built-in icon, but one is provided (along with instructions) at http://sbarnhill.mvps.org/WordFAQs/CustomToolbar.htm. This has the advantage that the on/off status of the button is a clear indication of whether or not the property is applied in a selected paragraph.
