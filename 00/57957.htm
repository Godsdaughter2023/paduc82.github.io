Kinect gestures will not work with new Xbox experience - Microsoft Community

lofty who say:

Kinect gestures will not work with new Xbox experience

This might be old news to some but it's the first I've read about it.


The New Xbox One Experience does not use Kinect's motion-control gestures.


It seems that Microsoft has decided that not enough users were utilising Kinect's motion features with the Xbox One dashboard, so are not bothering to use them anymore.

"On gestures, when we looked at the New Xbox One Experience we wanted to prioritise features that customers were asking for, plus areas of improvement from the existing Xbox One UI,’ according to Microsoft’s Mike Yabarra.


"Then, we wanted to look at the use-case model of features that take a lot of investment and say, ‘is it worth continuing to invest in that area?


"With gestures, the reality was the usage was very, very low. So for now, we’ve cut that from the New Xbox One Experience. So when we launch on the 12th, they won’t be in the product. We’ll continue to monitor and listen to feedback to see if people want them in."


So what of Kinect then? What will be it's use in the future? At the moment we use the voice controls for actions such as "Xbox Record That" or to "Take a Screenshot," while other people I have asked use it to "Scan a Code," but that seems to be about its only use these days.


Apparently there are rumours of headsets soon becoming compatible with voice commands, which would just about render the camera useless, which makes you wonder why it was bundled with the console in the first place.


It'll be interesting to see if Microsoft comes up with a way to revitalise Kinect in the future, but at this time, it seems like it's another piece of technology that people didn't take too.
