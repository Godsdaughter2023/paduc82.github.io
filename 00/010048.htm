Often this is caused when an ISP redirects DNS failures to a "search" page, HTML which is identified as XML.  A way to get around that is to point the DNS provider on your network card to another DNS (http://pcsupport.about.com/od/tipstricks/a/free-public-dns-servers.htm has many free and public)  For instance, if your were right-click on your network card and select "Properties", then click on IPv4, and select "Properties", and add (Google's) 8.8.8.8 as your Preferred and 8.8.4.4 as your alternate DNS server, you would not be getting your DNS from your ISP.  The error should magically go away:)
