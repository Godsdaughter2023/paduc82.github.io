john mcghie say:

Your last example is the one you want.

The syntax is Documents.Add(Template, NewTemplate, DocumentType, Visible)

But your last parameter is wrong: it needs to be wdNewBlankDocument

And you have commas in the wrong place. It's parameter comma space underscrore where "space underscore" is your line continuation.

Also, I expect you might have "spaces" between the front of the lines you pated in and the parameter.  On the web, a browser converts more than one consecutive space into the non-breaking space character.  This looks like a space to the eye, but to VBA they are different character codes and will cause your command to fail.

So:  

Documents.Add Template:= _
“Macintosh HD:Users:XXXX:Library:Application Support:Microsoft:Office:User Templates:My Templates:Name of my template.dotx", _
NewTemplate:=False, DocumentType:=wdNewBlankDocument

Check your path very carefully, it must be exact.

This code works on my system:

Documents.Add Template:= _
"Macintosh HD:Users:john:Library:Application Support:Microsoft:Office:User Templates:My Templates:Hills Template.dotx", _
NewTemplate:=False, DocumentType:=wdNewBlankDocument, Visible:=True

Note each line is flush left to avoid leading spaces.

Hope this helps

gord dibben say:

Here are a couple of macros you can play with.

Note how they start at bottom of sheet and work up to find last row with data.

The Offset(1, 0) is the same as your "downarrow once"

Sub findbottom_paste()
Dim rng1 As Range
Set rng1 = ActiveSheet.Cells(Rows.Count, 1).End(xlUp) _
            .Offset(1, 0)
Worksheets("Sheet2").Range("A1:J10").Copy _
             Destination:=rng1
End Sub

Sub findbottom_paste22()
Dim rng1 As Range
Dim Rng2 As Range
Set rng1 = Worksheets("Sheet1").Cells(Rows.Count, 1).End(xlUp) _
            .entirerow
Set Rng2 = Worksheets("Sheet2").Cells(Rows.Count, 1).End(xlUp) _
         .Offset(1, 0)
rng1.Copy Destination:=Rng2
End Sub
