suzanne s. barnhill say:

By default, your Documents will be saved in your Documents folder or subfolders. Those are easy to find, and backing them up is easy. The other settings are stored elsewhere in your profile.

Start with C:\Users\<Profile Name>\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft. In order to find/see this folder, you'll need to have "hidden and systems files and folders" displayed. You may want to take a look in every subfolder for content that looks important, but some specific customizations can be found as follows:

  • Document Building Blocks will have your building blocks.
  • Office will have your AutoCorrect files and (if relevant) .pip files.
  • Templates will have your user templates, including Normal.dotm, which will contain most of your customizations.
  • Themes may have theme customizations (the only ones listed here for me are for FrontPage, so that may be irrelevant to Office 365).
  • UProof has custom and exclusion dictionaries.
  • Word contains the STARTUP folder, which may contain add-ins.

You'll note that this Microsoft folder contains a lot of important-looking things. It wouldn't hurt to back up the whole folder just in case.

A few settings are actually in C:\Users\<Profile Name>\AppData\Local\Microsoft, including Word.officeUI, which stores your menu and QAT customizations. There are .officeUI files for other Office apps as well. You can import these settings to a Word Customizations.exportedUI file using the instructions at https://www.howtogeek.com/72670/how-to-backup-and-restore-your-office-2010-ribbon-and-quick-access-toolbar-customizations/ (written for Office 2010 but still applicable).

I've probably forgotten something since it's been several months since I migrated my files. I got a 2TB external drive and backed up my entire documents and profile folders so I would have access to whatever files turned out to be needed.

This looks like a job for the coding team. They need to either fully allow aliases on unverified accounts, or fully deny them, so this anomaly will not be repeated.

dlbigguy say:

Date Field from Access to Word Mail Merge Changess from Long to Short

I created Table in Access 2007 with a Date/Time field formatted to long date, ex. November 03, 2010. When I try to use this field in a mail merge in Word 2007 the date come out as 11/03/2010. Is there a way to format the date in word to the long date format. I also set up the long date in Windows but no help.  Help please.
macropod say:

Hi DLB,

To get the date format you want, you can add a formatting picture switch. In Word:
. select the mergefield;
. press Shift-F9 to expose the field coding. It should look something like {MERGEFIELD MyDate} where 'MyDate' is your mergefield's name;
. delete anything appearing after the mergefield's name and add '\@ "d MMMM yyyy"' to the field, as in {MERGEFIELD MyDate \@ "d MMMM yyyy"}.
  With this switch your date will come out like '2 August 2008'. Other possible date formatting switches include:
  . \@ "dddd, d MMMM yyyy";
  . \@ "ddd, d MMMM yyyy";
  . \@ "d MMM yyyy";
  . \@ "dd/MMM/yyyy";
  . \@ "d-MM-yy";
  Note: Note: you can swap the d, M, y expressions around, but you must use uppercase 'M's for months - lowercase 'm's are for minutes.
. position the cursor anywhere in this field and press F9 to update it;
. run your mailmerge.

Cheers,
