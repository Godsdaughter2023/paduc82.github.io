a. user say:

Not to worry. I'm not one of Directly's 'independent advisors' so I can tell you the real story.

Every user account has their own Documents folder, which is:

C:\Users\<user account name>\Documents

That's where we've always been supposed to store our documents, and that's where (most of) your programs will store your documents by default if you don't tell them otherwise.

In addition, every Windows PC has a Public account, which is not really a user account. Rather, it's a place to store files that is accessible to every user account on the computer. Comes in very handy for sharing files. So any documents that you store in:

C:\Users\Public\Documents

will be available to every user account on the computer.

But that's not all. Individual programs may have their own folders where they like to store documents.

And if that's not enough, Microsoft hopes that everyone will want to store their documents online, in OneDrive. They even setup certain programs, like Microsoft Office, to automatically store your documents in OneDrive, whether you like it or not.

The most important thing to remember is this: We are always in control. We tell Windows and programs where to store our documents. For me, I want all my documents to be stored in the Documents folder in my user account. So that's what I tell Windows and my programs to do. And if any programs have other ideas, like ... oh ... Microsoft Word, for example, I go into Word's settings and make sure that my documents go where I want them to go.
