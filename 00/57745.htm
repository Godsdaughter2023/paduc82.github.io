WLM would let me move folder under my account name  

I think you mean wouldn't. This limitation is a general one (not specific to the import function), so that's why it isn't mentioned in the article. Many people are content to leave imported messages in Storage folders where they end up.

There is also a good reason to leave archived messages in Storage folders rather than under an account. Removing an account removes all the account folders and permanently deletes their contents. This has often happened accidentally, and it's also different behaviour from that exhibited by other programs (e.g. Outlook Express) that don't store messages as individual files. Although there's a warning explaining this when you try to remove an account, it's often ignored, especially by helpful tech support people who think they know best.

I should not have put in the account info until after the import.  ... I'm getting an error message because it is attempting to go to the servers for those accounts. I'd like to make them 'go away'  

Press Alt-F O E to see all the accounts that have been set up. If you see a duplicate here, you can safely remove it so long as you're sure you have other copies of the messages in its folders. To be completely certain, you can right-click on the name of a superfluous account in the folder pane and select Properties. On the General tab, deselect Include this account when receiving mail or synchronizing. As always, if you need help interpreting an error, please quote the error message verbatim.

To remove an account, right-click on its name in the folder pane and select Remove account. Read and heed the warning! 

When I went back and changed the name (for consistency) it did not resort alpha.   

Folders will always be sorted alphabetically after re-starting the program.

the article also doesn't talk about the file string to find the WLM folders.  such as:  C:\Users\ logon \AppData\Local\Microsoft\Windows Live Mail, 

Again, that isn't necessary in the situation the article covers. The path to the store folder and its name are both user-configurable, so it's not possible to give a general location. This has to be found either from within the program options (press Ctrl-Shift-O for Options, then on the Advanced tab click Maintenance and then Store folder) or from the registry.

It's not really an alternative. It acts differently from installing, and it's usually better at resolving program performance issues. There's not much that is improved by uninstalling and re-installing, and neither of these actions will make much difference to problems with the store folder itself. And again, that's not much to do with exporting and importing messages.
