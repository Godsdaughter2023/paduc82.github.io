doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

If you toggle on the display of the TOC field by using ALT+F9, and add a \n 1-1 switch, so that it appears as

{ TOC \o "1-3" \n 1-1 \h \z \u }

the page numbers will be omitted from the Heading 1 entries.

To have the "descriptive text" for the chapter appear in the Table of Contents, it will need to be formatted with a Style that is other than Heading 1 and that Style will have to be assigned to the appropriate level in the Table of Contents Options dialog.  Likewise with the Figure and Table headings if you want them in the Table of Contents itself rather than in a separate Table of Figures and Table of Tables.

For more on this subject, see http://wordfaqs.ssbarnhill.com/TOCTips.htm

anula d'souza say:

Hi Tony,

 

As I understand you want the emails from the server to be deleted when they get downloaded in Outlook. Correct me if I am wrong.

 

The option to delete emails from server when the emails get downloaded in Outlook is available only in POP account and not in IMAP account. IMAP account is a mirror image of web-mail. If you delete any email from web-mail the same will be reflected in Outlook and vice-versa. Hence if you want the emails from the server to be deleted when the emails get downloaded in Outlook so that the web-mail mailbox size will be minimum and you will be able to view all the emails on Outlook then you have to configure the email account as POP account in Outlook.

 

I hope the above information helps. If you need and further assistance let us know and we will be glad to assist you.

Thank you.

anula d'souza say:

Hi Tony,

Thank you for the reply.

 

For more information about POP or IMAP account refer the link mentioned:

http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/outlook-help/introduction-to-email-account-types-HA010355550.aspx?CTT=1

 

You will not lose any data while switching from IMAP account to POP account. All your data will be present on web-mail and when you configure the email account as POP it will get downloaded into Outlook.

To switch from IMAP account to POP first remove the IMAP account from account and then configure the POP account in Outlook.

To remove an email account from Outlook:

·         Open Outlook.

·         Click on File< Account Settings< Email tab

·         Highlight the IMAP account and click on Remove.

·         Close and open Outlook and configure the same email account as POP account in Outlook. You may refer to the link mentioned which explains how to configure internet email account in Outlook:

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/2028939/en-us

voodoo_doctor say: OK, here is the solution:

1. Go to ActiveSync on your Windows Mobile 6.0 device (not connected to PC)
2. Menu -> Settings (or Properties)
3. Remove check box next to "Calender" and press OK
