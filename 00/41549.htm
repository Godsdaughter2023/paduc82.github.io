Why does my computer shut down as well as locking screen/sleep - Microsoft Community

joeduhr say:

Why does my computer shut down as well as locking screen/sleep when ever it feels like it?

My computer is shuting down and going into sleep mode when ever it wants to, and my Microsoft Edge does not respond?
mala_s say:

Hello JoeDuhr,

Thank you for writing to Microsoft Community Forums.

What is the make and model of the computer?

Sleep mode saves the state of all open applications to memory, then powers down both the display panel and hard disk drive. However, if it often goes to Sleep mode, you need to change the Sleep settings under Power & sleep. You can also refer this link regarding shutdown, sleep, or hibernate your PC.

Having the most current software and drivers installed on your computer is necessary. So, I would suggest you install all the latest updates by going to Settings > Update & Security > click on Check for updates. To resolve the sleep mode issue, kindly follow the below methods and see if it helps.

Method 1: Restore default settings of power plan.

  • Go to Control Panel and select Power Options.

  • Click on Change advanced power settings.

  • Select the option Restore default settings for this plan.

Method 2: Disable fast startup feature.

By turning of fast startup, it resolves most of the startup issues including Sleep mode. To disable the feature, follow the below mentioned steps.

  • Go to Control Panel and change the view type to small icons. Choose Power Options from the list.

  • Click on Choose what the power button do from the left hand side.

  • Next click on Change settings that are currently unavailable and scroll down to uncheck the box in front of Turn on fast startup.

  • Save the changes and Reboot the machine.

Method 3: Adjust your screen saver.

Screen saver function is sometimes baffled with sleep mode. Screen saver will keep your screen from going to whatever your default screen saver is (it will go to black if you have not selected a personal screen saver to display), so we need to set it to None. To do that, follow the below mentioned steps.

  • Go to Start menu and type screen saver.

  • Click on Turn screen saver on or off.

  • Select None in the Screen saver and uncheck the box On resume, display logon screen and click on Apply, OK.

Hope it helps.

Mala S
