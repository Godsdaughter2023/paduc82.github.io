wunders say:

While SD cards all look the same, electrically there are significant differences based ot the type of SD Card that you have.  The types are approximately like this:

     Standard Capacity (SDSC)  < 2GByte

     High Capacity (SDHC)        < 32 GByte

     Extended Capacicy (SDXC) < 2048 GByte

 

The card reader that is in your computer may only support SDSC or perhaps SDSC/SDHC but not SDXC.  Based on the size of your memory card, you likely have a SDXC card.  Early Windows-XP era computers came with card readers that would support only the SDSC standard.  Some of these readers could be extended to SDHC with a driver or firmware update.   What you need to do is get the make and model of your SD card reader on your computer, then go to that manufacturer's website and verify that it will or will not support the SDXC type of memory card.  If you're lucky, it will but a driver update may be required.

 

More info on SD:

  < http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secure_Digital >
