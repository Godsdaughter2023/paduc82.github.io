If you literally want "T00:00:00.00Z" to be displayed after the date, then try:

Sheet1.Columns("Date_Of_Sent").NumberFormat = _
    "yyyy-mm-dd" & Chr(34) & "T00:00:00.00Z" & Chr(34)

Normally, it is not necessary to assign .Value again.  However, if the cell had been formatted as Text before or if the cell value was otherwise interpreted as text before, do the following:

With Sheet1.Columns("Date_Of_Sent")
    .NumberFormat = "yyyy-mm-dd" & Chr(34) & "T00:00:00.00Z" & Chr(34)
    .Value = .Value
End With

Note the leading dot before NumberFormat and Value.

If you really want the format "yyyy-mm-ddThh:mm:ss.ssZ" -- that is, formatting time as well as date surrounded by "T" and "Z", then use the following format:

Sheet1.Columns("Date_Of_Sent").NumberFormat = "yyyy-mm-dd\Thh:mm:ss.00\Z"


With Sheet1.Columns("Date_Of_Sent")
    .NumberFormat = "yyyy-mm-dd" & Chr(34) & "T00:00:00.00Z" & Chr(34)
    .Value = .Value
    .EntireColumn.AutoFit
End With
