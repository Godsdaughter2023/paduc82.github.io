a. user say:

Hi scotga,            

 

Welcome to the Skype Community Forum.

 

It is possible that you've pressed some mysterious key combination which rotated your screen 90 degrees. Identify in which direction your screen needs to be rotated. There are four shortcut key combinations, and each will rotate your screen so that the top of the screen is at the top, bottom, left or right side of your monitor; you need to know which combination to choose.

Hold down "Ctrl + Alt" and the "up" arrow key if you want to return the top of your screen to the top of the monitor. "Ctrl + Alt + Left Arrow" will rotate your screen so that the top of the display is on the left side of your monitor, and using the Right Arrow will put it at the right side. The Down Arrow will turn your screen upside down if your monitor is right side up. Make sure you hold all three keys down long enough; the screen may go back for a moment as the screen is rotated.

rohn007 say:

Short answer: you have to get a key specific to your "region".

Long Answer:

MS has split the world into several sales regions.  Product Keys only work within the region they are sold in.

You will have to exchange your US key for an Asian one. Although a  fundamentally simple concept many stores have policy to not refund or exchange software that has been "opened" or used.  If the store you bought it from has branches in Asia, ask them for an exchange.  If they won't you will have to contact MS Support and get them to exchange the key for you, cancel the current one and replace it an appropriate one. That is not simple, there are only a few people in MS who will do that and it is a problem getting in touch with them.

    Help! Word document is gone!!! - Microsoft Community

    ashley-mae say:

    Help! Word document is gone!!!

    I recently downloaded word for my i pad I have been writing a paper for my final exam and it has been wonderful always there to open whenigo back in to work on it but now it's gone and I can't god it anywhere. I have the auto save setting on but it's gone how can it!!!!please help
    graham mayor say:

    There is no autosave in Word, only the automatic saving of recovery information in the event of a crash, and I don't suppose it is any different in the iPad version. Did you actually save your document? If so the last saved version will be at the location where you saved it.

    If it has never been saved and Word has been closed (perhaps to perform an automatic update) the document will be lost.  Always save your work regularly to avoid such disasters.

    I don't know how the iPad handles autorecovery information, but there is a small possibility that there may be a recoverable recovery file available that is associated with this document.
