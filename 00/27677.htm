rohn007 say:
No you have not lost the license. As Dave said you have to Log in to the MyAccount site. Find the current installation and click on "deactivate". This frees you up to "transfer" the installation to another computer.

Stuff you can Do in MyAccount: Install / Deactivate / Transfer / Offline Installer / Downgrade
https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/msoffice/wi...

log in to the MyAccount site http://office.com/myaccount

This page shows how to install Office AFTER the license has been activated. It also shows a few other things you can do in the MyAccount site.

Some of the answers can also be found in this FAQ, including how to “Share” your 365 Home subscription:
https://products.office.com/en/microsoft-office...

TRANSFER OFFICE 365 TO ANOTHER COMPUTER
https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Downlo...

The “Office Install” MS support site has detailed instructions for this process. Essentially it is a 3 step process which can all be done from the new computer.

The first step is to log in to the MyAccount site on the new computer.

The Second step is to remove permission to use full functionality on the old computer, you “deactivate” it. In Office 365 you do NOT have to uninstall Office. When you use the “deactivate” function, that copy of Office goes into “limited function mode”. When in that mode, you can open files and print them, but you cannot save any changes to the files. In other words, you cannot edit the files

The third step is to install on the new computer. The instal will also assign a license “count to it to “activate” the programs.
Here are the specific links you need:

1. MyAccount - http://office.com/myaccount
2. Deactivate instructions- https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Deacti...
3. Install on new PC - https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Downlo...

ronbarker say:

John

You appear to be using the wrong Microsoft Account to sign in with.  You need to use the email address and password for the Microsoft Account that you used when you first setup your Office 2016.

Try signing into your Office Account at: https://www.office.com/Myaccount Sign in using the same email address and password for the Microsoft Account that you used when you first setup your Office 2016.

You can download your Office 2016 from your Office Account. BUT you will need to contact Microsoft to 'deactivate' your Office on your old PC because Microsoft Servers will still think your Office 2016 is still on your old computer.
