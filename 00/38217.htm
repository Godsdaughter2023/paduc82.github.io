gigabyte motherboard and windows 10 - Microsoft Community

jerrybigness say:

gigabyte motherboard and windows 10

Gigabyte 970a-ds3p motherboard

Windows 10 would not install without the betabios (F2J) update on the bios motherboard.

After the update, automatic updates will not install Windows 10 automatically, you must use the MediaCreationTool X64 or X32 depending on your previous OS.

Windows 10 will not boot unless changes are made in the BIOS screen of your Gigabyte MB:

OS type: other

Boot mode select: UEFI and Legacy

Storage Boot Option Control: Legacy First

other PCI device rom: Legacy OpRom

under Peripherals:

onchip sata: Native IDE

Without these changes, Windows 10 boots into a redundant cycle of diagnostic and repair attempts. Make sure your Bios is the F2J before installing Windows 10, and the BIOS changes are made prior to running the MediaCreationTool.

a. user say:

That's a question we can think our way through.

The headset worked on Windows 8 but not on Windows 10. So it seems like there's something about Windows 10 that prevents your headset from working.

We know that Windows 10 is substantially different from Windows 8, with lots of new and changed technology. Has the manufacturer of your headset tested your headset on Windows 10 to see if it works properly, and has it made changes to the headset to keep it up to date with all the changes in Windows? That's what we mean when we say that a manufacturer is supporting their hardware. Without manufacturer support, your headphones may or may not work on Windows 10 - nobody knows - and if they work today that may stop working after the next big update to Windows.

One way to see if the manufacturer is supporting your headphones on Windows 10 is to look for the headphones' system requirements on the manufacturer's website. That's where you find what versions of Windows will work with your headphones. Another way is to look on the manufacturer's website for a Windows 10 driver. If the manufacturer wrote a driver for Windows 10 then it's almost a sure thing that they support the headphones on Windows 10.

Moreover, if the manufacturer is supporting your headphones on Windows 10, they will help you to get them working properly. On the other hand, if they decided not to support your headphones on Windows 10, you are out of luck.

Unfortunately, there isn't anything Microsoft can do about it. It benefits Microsoft to have as many hardware devices as possible working on Windows 10, but they can't force a manufacturer to support their product.
