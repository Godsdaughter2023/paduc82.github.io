carolyn leonard say:

making a custom table of contents (Mac Word 2016 on High Sierrra 10.13.6)

Instead of this:

Instead of that I want it to be like this:

ONE—1790
Giving Birth to a new Nation……………...1

TWO—1800
The New American People………………10

THREE—1810
The Capitol is Burning!……………………21

How do I get there? 

john korchok say:

I'm assuming that ONE-1790 is coming from Heading 1 and Giving Birth to a New Nation is coming from Heading 2 in the text. Then Heading 1 material is styled by the TOC 1 style and Heading 2 entries are formatted with TOC 2. That's a pretty standard TOC setup.

Here's how to do this:

  1. On the Home tab , click on the Styles Pane icon to open it.
  2. Scroll down to TOC 2, hover over it until a down-pointing arrow appears at the right end. Click on the arrowhead and choose Modify Style.
  3. Uncheck Automatically update (there's a good chance that setting is what messed this up to begin with).
  4. If you want to ensure that all future documents have the same formatting for TOC 2, check Add to template.
  5. Click on the Format dropdown and choose Tabs.
  6. In the Tab stops field, type in the distance from the right margin where the numbers should be.
  7. Set Alignment to Right.
  8. Set Leader to the dot pattern.
  9. Click on the plus sign. OK out.

Your second level TOC entries should now have right-aligned numbers with a dot leader.

If your document is formatted with different styles, the general idea is the same, but the style names may vary.
