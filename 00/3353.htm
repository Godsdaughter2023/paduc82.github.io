First - we can assume your video (i.e., hardware [video card], software [video drivers]) is working because you can see your Microsoft desktop on your desktop monitor.

Second - we can further assume the nature of your complaint concerns not being able to see a video image (i.e., YouTube, ABC Go, etc.)

So we can establish the root cause of your problem is not hardware, due to the fact that if it was a hardware issue, there would be no video images rendered on your desktop do to loss of video signal.

We also can conclude that the software (i.e., video drivers - software instructions) are working due to the fact that you can see your desktop, interact with desktop applications, open an internet browser, surf the internet and see returned results. And the only exception is watching Flash based videos inside your computer's browser.

So the truth of the issue is Layer 6 & 7 of the "Seven Layers" of the OSI model, which are the "Presentation" and "Application" layers, are not working. It is such the case that I can eliminate layer 7, the application because layer 7 is incorporated into Microsoft security schema itself and if it was not working, every Windows 10 computer would not be able to see flash based video inside of their browsers.

For example: layer 6, which is driven by software instructions, interprets the video signal/instructions received by your computer and tell layer 7 how to render the video signal within your computer browser.
