vincentchoy say:

Not sure what the time wasting purposes you mentioned are....

However, among Yammer Community managers, there is such a concept as "Water Cooler" groups or discussions. Meaning these discussions are going to happen anyway whether you have Yammer or not, and from a corporate comms and HR point of view, some of these discussions may actually be healthy in the organization.

Also, I have come across companies that use Yammer purely for non-work purposes. These are again mostly initiated by corporate comms or HR, the idea is to allow employees to engage with each other through non-work. The thinking behind that is a relationship built through non-work (e.g. Book Club..etc) can often lead to better work relationships and a more dynamic workplace.

The way to properly steer a Yammer network towards achieving corporate goals require a certain amount of planning and effort, involving various roles like Yammer Community Managers, Yammer Power Users, Managers and stake holders. It also involves having a good usage policy, manager involvement and leading by example, training and also some form of moderation by the community manager and power users.

Some companies that I work with have the thinking that Yammer can deliver results by just turning it on and leaving it to the users.  Sadly that is often not the case.

I hope you find a better way to benefit from Yammer rather than just abandoning it.

By the way, to effectively use Yammer you also need the Enterprise version. The basic version doesn't have administrative features so it would be lacking in many of the features you need to use it in a corporate environment.

To answer your specific question -

1. If you are using free Yammer Basic, you can't "turn it off" as the company doesn't really own the network. You just stop using it (discontinue), but its still there and the external networks will run.

2. If you are using Yammer Enterprise, you can also select to discontinue using it, by removing the access of the users. The network will continue to be there. It would be another matter ff you terminate your office365 account, or you downgrade to a license that does not include Yammer (that would be standalone plans like Exchange Online Plan 1)
