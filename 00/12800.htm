jay freedman say:
> It's a widely known limitation of the software.

With all due respect, and with apologies to Col. Potter, "that's just pure horse hockey."

If the two-column section starts at the beginning of the document, you can insert a one-column section before it with these steps:

  • Put the cursor at the beginning of the document.
  • Press Enter twice to insert empty paragraphs there.
  • With the cursor just to the left of what used to be the first character of the two-column text, click Insert > Break > Continuous > OK.
  • With the cursor before the section break, click Format > Columns > One > OK.


The first section (currently empty except for paragraph marks) is now one column, and you can enter your text / headline / whatever. The critical part of this is to insert the section break and place the cursor in the correct section before you change the number of columns.


You may want to read http://word.mvps.org/faqs/formatting/UsingColumns.htm for more tips.


As Charles said, Word and WP work in very different ways. Each has its strengths and weaknesses, and each is better suited for certain kinds of tasks. How do I edit/delete a phone number in a contact

I am on Skype 8.15 on Mac and I need to delete an old phone number stored in a contact but I can't figure out how. Tried also with the online interface and hitting same roadblocks. What am I missing?
jannine_p say:

Hello

Were you the one who manually saved the number as a contact or the number was entered by this contact under his profile?

If you're the one who manually saved the number as contact from Skype, you may delete the contact instead and dial again the right number then, save it as a contact.

To add a delete a phone number saved as a contact:

  • Look for the Skype contact that you want to edit the number.
  • Press CTRL/CMD on your keyboard and click on the number.
  • Select View Profile > Delete contact.

However, if this number has been entered by the contact from his profile, we do not have the option to delete nor change it.

Let us know if you have any further questions or clarifications.

vijay a. verma say:
What you need is a pie of pie.
1. Click in your pivot
2. Insert Pie of Pie
3. Right click on pie > Format Data Series
4. Select Percentage Value in Split Series by and choose values less than %age appropriately.
