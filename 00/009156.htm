davidf2 say:

If you opt for inline and 'not move with text' then you have to opt for either a 'left or right horizontal alignment'. Assuming that you chose 'right' alignment then the image will shift to the right margin and depending on how tall the image is the X number of lines of text or space to the left of the image will move with the image down the text box as you add text above the image.

Eventually when you have pushed the image and the associated X lines of text or space down the page you run out of room for the image and when you push it one line too far then the image AND all the associated X lines of text or space will flow to the next page as a group, or more exactly to the next text box. That will leave a gap when there is no more room at the bottom of the text box to move the image down. It won't move just part of the image down into the next text box.

That is your dilemna. Even if you format the image to move with the text you are going to have gaps and the same thing happens when you reach the bottom of the text box. You can't have an image straddling two text boxes. This is the same way Word works except Word does not give you the left or right horizontal allignment option.

I would ask what behavior do you expect? What do you want to happen?

I don't see that you have any choice but to tweak your layout after you finish writing. You may be able to drag the image up or down or you may find that using exact and wrapping is the best choice when you are at the bottom of a text box and dealing with a gap.

DavidF

To test behaviour I tried several things. I clicked into the text above the image, within the paragraph and started adding arbitrary lines of text.   1. Right-click the text box that has the margins you want to use as defaults.

  3. Select the Apply settings to new text boxes check box.
  4. Click OK.

The next new text box that you create will use the settings from this text box.

Soft Reset

The soft reset is the same thing as pulling your battery (on devices where that is possible). This method will not remove anything at all from your phone and is completely safe to attempt in any problem situation.

You perform the soft reset by having your phone turned on. Then press and hold the volume down + power buttons until your phone vibrates and restarts. Note that you should not touch the device screen during the process (not even when the 'slide down to power off' prompt appears).

Example of situations where the soft reset should solve the problem:

•An app that usually starts won't start.

•The camera won't open.

•The device starts using too much battery.

•The device starts behaving oddly.

tommarasmus say:

I've received this error as well. I had to delete my kerberos tickets via Keychain Access.

  1. Launch Keychain Access (/Applications/Keychain Access.app/)
  2. Click "Keychain Access" in the menu
  3. Click "Ticket Viewer"
  4. Delete any and all tickets listed
  5. Restart the Office app and you should be good

At least that's what solved mine and several others problems.
