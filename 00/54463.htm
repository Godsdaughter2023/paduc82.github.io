Mr. Wizard essentially does the same thing as the manual process. If there's a difference, I'm not aware of it.  I personally prefer manual processes as I feel I have more control - but others prefer the automated process because it's simpler.  The choice is yours and I don't believe in the long run it matters.

Question 3 is harder to answer.  As the only Administrator account on the system, deleting it leaves you only with the built-in Administrator account which at the moment doesn't appear to be working as you can't use even it to create another Administrator Account.  The hope here is that the fix will resolve the underlying problem and the built-in Administrator account will function as normal once the fix is complete - however, I cannot guarantee that will be the case as I've never done this procedure where the problem account is the only Administrator account on the system.

It should work, but if it doesn't then you'll be in the situation where you have no enabled and functional Administrator account (if even the built-in Administrator Account doesn't function as an Administrator account - as seems to be the case at the moment but may change once the fix is complete - or for some reason cannot be enabled or accessed) and the only solution to that problem is a clean installation.  For that reason, I recommend you do a full backup before attempting this so that at least your data will be secure.

I should add that I'm not sure you have many choices as this is pretty much the way to fix that problem and I know of no other solutions that will work.
