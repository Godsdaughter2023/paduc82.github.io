Monitor won't wake up - Microsoft Community

heatherhetebry say:

Monitor won't wake up

I used my HP computer last night.  It always goes to sleep at night, and has no problems waking up in the morning.  However, this morning the computer (tower) is on, but the monitor will not wake up.  I really don't know all the techincal terms, just a laymay home user.  I have checked all the cords to make sure everything was still plugged in good.  I have also turned the tower off and back on, but still nothing.   The monitor will not respond to the mouse or keyboard.  It appears as though the mouse is dead, no red light on the bottom.

Please help!!

 

hairyfool say:

The lack of a red light on the mouse would mean there is no power on the USB port which would indicate a problem with the base unit.

 

Remove the power lead from both the monitor and the base unit. Plug the monitor back in and check that it will turn on and normally go into standby after a few seconds.

 

Plug the base unit power back in and then switch on as usual. Lights should come on and the monitor wake up shortly after, see if the HP logo shows. If it does not watch the base unit and see if the drive activity light flashes indicating it is booting. If the lights flash randomly but still no display then there is a problem with the video circuit. The numlock light should also come on.

 

If the monitor lead (large flat blue or white lead normally) is in the group with network and USB sockets in the base unit then the graphics is on board. If it is in the horizontal slots then it is a separate card. I have had to deal with exactly the same problem 2 days ago which was cleared by reseating the card.

Hello,

From the FAQ on Vault Credits:

I claimed Rewards Credits from the vault. When will I receive them?

If you claim Rewards Credits from the MyVIP Vault, they should appear as pending in your account within 96 hours. Once they are pending in your account, the normal deposit process kicks in. Generally if you have over 5,000 Rewards Credits pending in your account in the first half of any given month, that amount will be converted to your local currency and deposited into your Microsoft account on or around the 30th of that same month. If the Credits show as pending in the second half of the month, that amount will be deposited into your Microsoft account on or around the 15th of the next month.

Please note you need at least 5,000 Rewards Credits pending to automatically trigger a deposit, though you can request a deposit for the next scheduled date as long as you have 1,000 Credits pending. Also, if you have ongoing issues with us depositing into your account, such as your account not matching up with your billing location, you might want to consider choosing a different prize from the vault, as we won’t be able to deliver your Rewards Credits until the deposit issue is resolved.

ktb_ say:

I had this problem. In case you're still looking for a solution, the image file (Advanced.Theme-Dark_Scale-100.png) you need is available from the following thread in the SFCFix.zip archive (downloadable from Dropbox) -- http://www.sysnative.com/forums/windows-update/16098-corrupted-files-cbs-panther-post122858.html#post122858

I simply set the owner of the corrupt image file located at C:\Windows\WinSxS\amd64_systemresource-wind..-ui-accountscontrol_31bf3856ad364e35_10.0.10240.16384_none_da155365ee6f0fb5\Advanced.Theme-Dark_Scale-100.png (I suppose it's possible that the directory might have a slightly different name on your PC, but the beginning of the directory name containing the PNG image should match) to my user, granted full control permissions on the image file to my user, replaced the corrupt image file with the one from the SFCFix.zip archive, then set the owner of the image file back to "NT Service\TrustedInstaller" and removed full control permissions for my user. That seems to work, confirmed with the following commands:

dism /online /cleanup-image /scanhealth
dism /online /cleanup-image /checkhealth

dism /online /cleanup-image /restorehealth

sfc /scannow
