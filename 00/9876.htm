    cjlim say:

    When your phone is out of coverage, it will continuously attempt to regain connection and this will drain the battery. I have never actually tried this myself but I wouldn't be at all surprised if the battery goes low or flat after 5 hours of no signal. So, whether or not the phone auto shuts down due to lack of signal or low battery, either way, it will eventually shut down.

     

    One way you can keep the phone on when no coverage without draining the battery is to set it to flight/offline mode. The phone does not attempt to regain network connection when in this mode.

    dan555---01 say:

    Also if you're in an area with no coverage the phone will look for coverage at increasingly longer time intervals to save battery, so if it's without coverage for a while and then you take the phone into an area with coverage it could be something like 10 minutes or so until the phone checks and realises that there is coverage. However if you dial a number and it's been a while since it last looked for coverage it will look for it there and then instead of coming back straight away with 'no coverage', so one thing you could do is dial a number which you know doesn't work.

    DIY Home WEB page for your phone, on your phone - Microsoft Community

    t9rfbb say:

    DIY Home WEB page for your phone, on your phone

    So why would you want to have your own WEB page on your phone?  To save time and money.  Each time you start your WEB browser it goes to your home page.  If that page is on the WEB, and Google is most people's home page, then your phone tries to connect to the Internet.

    If you are using a data connection then that simple act is costing you money.  If you are using free WIFI or your own router to connect to the Internet it is still taking time while your phone connects and downloads that page.  Your phone may even hang for awhile in its attempt to get to your home page when you are not able to reach the Internet.

    Besides, it just may be that you really didn't want to go to that home page first.

    Here is how to avoid all the above problems:

    Write your own home page and save it on your phone.  Open that page with your WEB browser and have the WEB browser save that page location as your home page.  The next time you start your WEB browser it will go immediately to the home page on your phone without having to go to the Internet.  Then you can manually enter any URL into the browser, or choose one of your bookmarks, or even click on a link you've embedded in your DIY home page.

    Below Is text you can copy and paste into a text editor to create a simple but effective home page.  For simplicity I suggest doing this on your PC, though I'm sure it can be done on your phone.

    <HTML>
    <head>
    <title>My phone Homepage</title>
    </head>
    <BODY>
    <br>
    <br><A HREF="http://www.bing.com">Bing</A>
    <br>
    <br><A HREF="http://www.google.com">Google</A>
    <br>
    <br><A HREF="http://www.yahoo.com">Yahoo</A>
    </BODY>
    </HTML>

    After you create and save the text file change the extension from TXT to HTM (can be lower case).  Lets assume you named the file "startup.htm".

    Now copy startup.htm to your phone (which is connected to your PC via USB, or however).  I suggest putting it on your mass storage drive ( drive E: on my E7 phone) in the Download folder.  Actual location is up to you, but my example uses drive E:.

    After you have created and stored statup.htm on you phone open your WEB browser and type in the following into the browser URL bar:

      file://e:/download/startup.htm

    (Note:  change the above file path as necessary depending on the file name you used and where on your phone you stored the file you created)

    Press the enter key and the file should open in your WEB browser.  Now go to your browser settings and set that page as your home page.

    All done!
