Windows 10, How Do I Hate Thee? Let Me Count The Ways

I've been using computers since the days of DOS and the Apple II.  I've seen them through a LOT of changes.  I have used every version of DOS and Windows (except Windows ME).  I've taken the good and the bad.  Windows 8 was the first version of Windows that I actually got fired up enough about to become a full-on cheerleader.  I had been using Windows Phone from the very first (Samsung Focus being my first of many Windows Phones) and I was excited that the Metro UI was coming to my PC and laptop.  I followed Win8 from the earliest--and roughest--previews.  The bugs and not-ready-for-prime-time features didn't phase me because the design was going where I'd hoped. 

Then came Windows 10.  Cautiously, I have been working with the previews on a test machine.  And without any reservation I can truly say I hate Windows 10 from its very design to its execution.  Microsoft, in it's ridiculous quest for a so-called single experience, has completely ruined every single aspect of Windows 8 and, now, Windows Phone with what they are doing.  People who insisted on living on the desktop forever and who couldn't seem to grasp the concept of live tiles and a Start Screen pushed hard for Microsoft to completely abandon Metro. 

I had zero issues with using a keyboard and mouse with Win8.  None.  The mouse gestures, in particular, were a fairly good imitation of what a finger could do on a touch interface.  I quickly moved away from keyboard commands to mouse gestures and gestures on the trackpad on my keyboard.  It was over a year before I bought a Surface Pro 3 and experienced Windows 8.1 in a touch environment.  I easily move between them.  Windows 10 pukes all over that.

I hate the Start Menu, as a concept and as it's being executed.  And what Microsoft laughingly calls Continuum I'm expected to believe that Windows 10 "intelligently" comprehends how I want to use my device.  That's complete bull.  Even if I try to tell Windows 10 to go to Tablet Mode it always forgets, forcing me to frequently turn it back ON.  I never want to NOT be in Tablet Mode.  I want the immersive user experience.  I want live tiles without all this stupid garbage about "recently used" or ridiculously idiotic, virtually never-ending, vertical scrolling through the lame excuse for All Apps.  If I could stand in front of Nadella and the rest of the design team, I smack each one of them to sleep.

With both Metro and Windows 7 everything was at least bold so that you could easily see it.  Now all you have are these ridiculously thin--and often small--vestiges of buttons and icons.

One of the lamest claims about Windows 8 was the "jolting experience of moving between desktop and Metro".  That's just bull.  The same people are falling all over themselves extolling the virtues of endless virtual desktops.  Win8 is no more jarring than that.

On Win8 I could always count on easily swiping from the left to move through--or snap--running Metro apps to the screen, and easily swiping from the right to pull up the Charms, giving me access--consistently--to the context-sensitive Settings.  This was simple, elegant, beautiful.  Windows 10 introduces such a convoluted and idiotic thought process to navigation, settings, etc., that I get angry every time I sit down at my test machine--which I happen to be doing even as I type this.

What would satisfy me?  Fair question.  I want to be able to turn on Tablet Mode permanently regardless of the device I'm using.  I don't want to have to turn it back on every time I reboot or login or a program crashes.  I want Tablet Mode to REALLY put me in immersive mode.  This means I should, by default, see the full Start Screen, with full-blown live tiles and groups, with no ugly "Recently Used", no vertical All Apps (go back to the correct All Apps mode), and no stupid Task Bar.  I want the search function to be exactly as it is in Win8--all I have to do is be on the Start Screen and start typing and I see all results regardless of being local or on the web.  I think it's just stupid that I have to go through all the steps Windows 10 requires just to search for something.  I want Metro apps to run immersive and desktop programs to run on the desktop.  I want Settings for programs to always be in the same place on the screen, completely contextually based.  I want the panoramic views back and I don't want to ever see a stupid hamburger button again.

That is what will satisfy me.  If you choose to NOT be in Tablet Mode, then you can have all the ridiculous design garbage they're executing that you want.  But I know none of this is going to happen.  Microsoft is hell-bent on this course of action and Windows 10 will continue to be the most ignorantly designed and executed operating system I've ever encountered.

E63 - Memory Corrupted - Due to Device Lock / Remote Lock - Microsoft Community

flae63 say:

E63 - Memory Corrupted - Due to Device Lock / Remote Lock

I have a brand new Nokia E63 (same as E71). My memory card is suddenly corrupted, but I managed to fix it. I believe the source of the problem is the Remote Lock and the Device Lock of the unit.

The problem started after I enabled the Device Lock and Remote Lock of my E63 unit. After a couple of hours, I tried opening my apps in the memory card. To my surprise, it's corrupted! I didn't panic because I'm confident I could retrieve the contents of the card with a memory reader device. But when I inserted my memory card to my PC, the card is unreadable. I had a feeling that the card must had been badly corrupted. But how? I just got this unit almost a day ago. I didn't do anything odd. I'm an OOP-Design Patterns oriented programmer so I'm not newbie when it comes to best practices.

As I searched the web, I found that there are people who encountered the same exact problem. And their guess is also the Device Lock.

So what I did is I disabled the Remote Lock and the Device Lock feature. I checked my memory card and it's still corrupted!

I turn off my unit and turn it on again, but sadly it's still corrupted!

I removed the memory card while the unit is on and then reinserted the card, and it worked.

In summary, to solve this memory corrupted problem, try disabling the Remote Lock and Device Lock feature. To disable the Device Lock, revert the password to "12345" (Do not type the quotes). Remove the memory card (Use the remove memory card feature of your unit!). Re-insert the card to your unit and problem should be fixed.
