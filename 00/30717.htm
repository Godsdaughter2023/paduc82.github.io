hellhog say:

Ok, if you are plugging HDMI into your monitor, you need a dongle that will allow you to utilize the RCA cables from your xbox such as this one:
http://www.amazon.com/Audio-Cable-Adapter-XBOX-360-Slim/dp/B002P35JMO/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&qid=1324355611&sr=8-1

Then you connect the Red and White RCA cables (which you can pick up audio RCA cables online as well or at a Radioshack or BestBuy) into the Red and White connections on the adapter you linked. From there you connect the Black, Orange, and Green 3.5mm (the same as a headphone jack cable) into the adapter and into the reciever on the subwoofer. I'm basing this from the following image I found:



The accessory adapter should have come with your speaker system according to Newegg's listing. Notice on the image that it labels what speakers are associated with each cable. The speakers themselves I assume you already have connected. Those are what are called RCA cables. They hold a single channel. The smaller ones for the 3.5mm cables are called TRS connectors. They hold two channels.

Unfortunately, the downside to using the two RCA cables from your Xbox to the 3 3.5mm jacks is that you will NOT get surround sound audio. Your sound will remain in 2 channel stereo only. Your center speaker will probably play both sides, your left speakers will play left channel only, your right speakers will play right channel only, and the subwoofer will still handle the lows. As for the remote for your system, it only has a headphone out jack. That is only for connecting a 3.5mm headphone cable. You cannot input anything from there.


Quick Cable Lesson:

You said that your subwoofer and cables were confusing you. Based on the image above here is what I can say to hopefully help you out. Your subwoofer acts as the reciever in your system. There are Three 3.5mm input jacks and 5 RCA output jacks. The 3.5mm inputs carry two channels of audio. When you input the Green cable, you recieve signals for the Left and Right front channels. When you plug in the Black 3.5mm cable you recieve the signals for the Rear left and right speaker. When you plug in the Orange cable, you recieve the subwoofer and center speaker signals. From their, your subwoofer already receives the signal so no extra cables are necessary. The other 5 speakers need their signals sent to them. By using the single channel RCA cables, each speaker is plugged into the appropriate ouput on the subwoofer. In total there are 6 channels (hence 5.1). When you use the RCA cables from the xbox, you are only sending 2 channels worth of data. Hence why you will only get stereo audio instead of surround sound.
