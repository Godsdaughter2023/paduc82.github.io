MS Edge problem with VPN TAP Adapter (Creators Update) - Microsoft Community

paul coddington say:

MS Edge problem with VPN TAP Adapter (Creators Update)

I have lodged this report through Feedback Hub, but I am putting it to the community to see if anyone else has encountered the problem (and perhaps solved it).


With the Creators Update, Edge now has problems with VPN (TAP-Windows Adapter V9).

One can turn the VPN on and off for days at a time, with no problem. Then one day, for no apparent reason, one can turn on the VPN and Edge will not be able to communicate with the Internet at all.

When Edge is in this state, other Internet applications can still access any website through the VPN (Chrome, PING, etc). So, the VPN is working fine and it is Edge that has the problem.
There is no obvious trigger for this problem and it cannot be resolved by reboot or disabling/enabling the TAP adapter.

Reinstalling the VPN client and bundled TAP adapter resolves the problem.

Deleting the *NdisDeviceType=1 entry for TAP-Windows Adaptor V9 and disabling/enabling the TAP adapter also resolves the problem with no reinstallation or reboot required. The *NdisDeviceType=1 is added back by the VPN client immediately on first use, but this is not the cause of the problem as one can turn VPN off and on for days with this key in place and experience no problems with Edge at all.

Once the problem has been resolved by any of the above workarounds, one can continue as normal for a few days until the problem reasserts itself.

Suspect it is something to do with the new sandboxing in Edge added with the Creators Update. Possibly Edge gets confused about zones/VPNs and requires TAP to be reinstalled as a trigger to straighten itself out and fly right.
