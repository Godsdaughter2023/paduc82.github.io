I think the catch-up scan was originally thrown into the mix just because the developers understood that the many constraints that they had placed on the MSE scheduled scan had seriously impacted its reliability. So another approach would be to finagle the MSE scan into something more reliable:

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/protect/forum/mse-protect_start/mse-scan-schedule-off-by-days/f0e68e51-02d6-4fce-bf0b-999b1f0268c8

1.) Remove conflicting AV products and/or remnants (run the removal tools for any previous products).

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/2688670  

 

2.) Check for undetected malware with third-party scanners:

http://www.selectrealsecurity.com/malware-removal-guide  

 

3.) Uninstall MSE and then reinstall it.

yahoo.com!noreply.mydomain.com!1497830400!1497916799.zip

    Go to File> Save As Pictures

    Confirm any other settings, then click OK
