Well, they are two different concepts. A custom dictionary is a dictionary for "correct" words. By definition it MUST apply to a SINGLE language.

This is not true at all. To work correctly, the default custom dictionary, CUSTOM.DIC must have its language set to All Languages. The whole purpose of that dictionary is to be a collection of words not to be marked as incorrect. It is correct that you can have only one default custom dictionary at a time; this is the one to which words are added when you use Add to Dictionary. As for the language bar, the language at the insertion point can be displayed in the status bar, and clicking on that status bar button opens the Language dialog to allow you to select a different language to apply.

You can, of course, define a No Proofing character style to apply to words you don't want to bother to add to the custom dictionary. A keyboard shortcut can be assigned to that style for ease of application.

Suzanne has covered the dictionary issue, now let's look at the language issue. There is nothing at all unusual about a requirement to type in different languages. There is scope to make it even simpler to switch proofing dictionaries and I may be able to come up with something along those lines, but for the moment, it is possible to switch dictionaries, but only via Word options. Any dictionary designated as all languages will apply to all languages.

Hi Paddy,

If you are using Windows 8.1 follow the steps below on how to create a local account on your PC:

  1. Swipe in from the right edge of the screen, tap Settings, and then tap Change PC settings.
    (If you're using a mouse, point to the lower-right corner of the screen, move the mouse pointer up, click Settings, and then click Change PC settings.)
  2. Tap or click Accounts, and then tap or click Other accounts.
  3. Tap or click Add an account, and then tap or click Sign in without a Microsoft account (not recommended).
  4. Tap or click Local account.
  5. Enter a user name for the new account.
  6. If you want this person to sign in with a password, enter and verify the password, add a password hint, and then tap or click Next.

    If your PC is on a domain, depending on the domain's security settings, you might be able to skip this step and tap or click Next, if you prefer.
  7. Tap or click Finish.
