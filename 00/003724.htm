I am having trouble getting a Marco Repeat Expression to function - Microsoft Community

jim on cape cod say:

I am having trouble getting a Marco Repeat Expression to function properly

The last record on the form has a date greater than the records I want.  I compare the date on the form with a control date to stop the Macro as:

                                                               'Repeat Expression'    Forms![MasterFile]![Date] < Forms![DateCTL]![DateCTL]

 

max meng say:

Hi Jim,

The rule of the “Repeat Expression” is that The macro stops running if the expression evaluates to False. The expression is evaluated each time the macro runs.

For your issue, you make the macro to repeat while the condition “Forms![MasterFile]![Date] < Forms![DateCTL]![DateCTL]” is true.

Is there an action “GoToRecord”,  “FindNextRecord” or something like that in the RunMacro action?
If there is, that’s the problem.

The RunMacro action is like a “Do…while” loop, it will do the RunMacro first, then to judge the condition.

It means that it will go to (find) next record , even if the record doesn’t meet the condition.

For example, if Forms![MasterFile]![Date] = 2/21/2012, Forms![DateCTL]![DateCTL] = 2/22/2012, and the date of the next record is 2/22/2012.

For current record, the date meet the condition, then repeat the macro and go to the next record (date is 2/22/2012).

After that, judge the condition again, the date of the record is not less than 2/22/2012, the macro will not repeat.

But the current record is the date of 2/22/2012.

So this will make the date of the last record not meet the condition.

To resolve this, you can add “GoToRecord” action to go previous record.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

When you copy and paste gobbledygook into a new document, the text that you paste brings its formatting with it; if it is formatted as "Do not check spelling or grammar," then it will not be checked. If you paste as Unformatted Text and it is still not flagged, then the likelihood is that either the Normal style or the default empty paragraph in your blank document is formatted as "Do not check..."

The effect of the language settings and how they are applied is explained in http://WordFAQs.mvps.org/MasterSpellCheck.htm.

suzanne s. barnhill say:
Select "gibberishk gobblygoooke" and go to the Set Language dialog. Make sure that "Do not check spelling or grammar is not checked.
 
Or Ctrl+A to select the entire document and do the same.
 
To better understand how the spelling checker works (in particular, the way language is applied to text), see http://sbarnhill.mvps.org/WordFAQs/MasterSpellCheck.htm.
