tintombstone say:

This worked on the example you supplied, providing the training schedule text is on the same line as the prefix.  Otherwise it's hard to identify whether a schedule exists or not

####

Training Schedule: Tuesdays from 3 pm to 5 pm at Railway Park. Parents are requested not to park in front of the community center.

####

 

It will also allow you to just change the line Selection.Find.Text = "Training Schedule:" to Selection.Find.Text = "Sponsor:" to get other data.  You could tie that in as an argument perhaps

By the way, this is what Databases were created for.

Sub GetTrainingSchedule2()

Dim curDoc As Document
Dim newDoc As Document
Dim rng As Range

    Set curDoc = ActiveDocument
    Set newDoc = Documents.Add
    curDoc.Activate
    Selection.HomeKey Unit:=wdStory
    Selection.Find.ClearFormatting
    Selection.Find.Text = "Training Schedule:"
    Selection.Find.Execute
    Do
        Set rng = Selection.Range
        Selection.MoveRight Unit:=wdCharacter, Count:=1
        Selection.EndKey Unit:=wdLine, Extend:=wdExtend
        If Len(Selection.Range) > 2 Then
            Selection.GoTo What:=wdGoToBookmark, Name:="\para"
            Selection.Copy
            newDoc.Activate
            Selection.PasteAndFormat (wdFormatOriginalFormatting)
            Selection.TypeParagraph
            curDoc.Activate
            rng.Select
        End If
       
    Loop Until Selection.Find.Execute = False
    newDoc.Activate
End Sub

graham mayor say:
For a start, the whole point of PDF is that it is not meant to be editable, so any conversion to Word is going to be laborious. You would be far better working from the original Word document if you can get your hands on it.

Otherwise there are potentially three headers and three footers for each section of the document. You are going to have to open each of them and check the settings so that there are both page number fields present and that they flow through the document in the manner you require.

Note also that Word relies on the printer driver to produce the page size. Custom page sizes are beyond the abilities of most drivers and will impose a standard page size that may be close to the requested size. If your page size is non-standard you will either have to work with a PDF printer driver, such as Acrobat or PrimoPDF, which will allow a custom size, or use a standard page size and make up the difference with the margins.

Each section has a header and footer. If you select the option to have a different first page header/footer, then the underlying one becomes the continuation header/footer. If you have the option checked for different odd and even, the underlying one becomes the odd page header/footer.

So to see all possible headers and footers in a section, that section needs at least three pages.

For more on sections and headers and footers see Sections / Headers and Footers 2010.

Converted documents - from pdf, word perfect, or ocr are a mishmash. Formatting will be done directly rather than through styles and will in general be weird! Section breaks are often inserted seemingly at random. They are very difficult to edit or revise. Consider working from the orgiinal .docx and copying elements that you think are better done in the pdf from the pdf into your docx.
