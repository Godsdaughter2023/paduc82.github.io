If the issue persists, you might want to check some common battery saving tips that can help you improve your battery life:

1. Set a shorter duration before your screen times out.

On Start , swipe over to the All apps list, and then select Settings  > Personalization > Lock screen. In Screen times out after, select the box to change the screen’s time out duration.

2. Reduce your screen’s brightness.

On Start , swipe over to the All apps list, and then select Settings  > System > Display. Turn Automatically adjust display brightness off , and then select the Brightness level that you want to use instead.

3. Use a dark background.

On Start , swipe over to the All apps list, select Settings  > Personalization > Colors, and then select Dark.

4. Turn off background apps.

On Start , swipe over to the All apps list, and then select Settings > System > Battery saver > Battery use. Select an app from the list, select Details, and then turn Allow this app to run in the background off .

5. Turn off location.

On Start , swipe over to the All apps list, select Settings > Privacy > Location, and then turn Location off .

6. Turn off Bluetooth.

On Start , swipe over to the All apps list, select Settings > Devices > Bluetooth, and then turn Bluetooth off .

7. Turn off NFC.

On Start , swipe over to the All apps list, select Settings > Devices > NFC, and then turn Tap to share off .

8. Set a shorter duration before your screen times out.

On Start , swipe over to the All apps list, and then select Settings > Personalization > Lock screen. In Screen times out after, select the box to change the screen’s time out duration.

9. Reduce your screen’s brightness.

On Start , swipe over to the All apps list, and then select Settings > System > Display. Turn Automatically adjustdisplay brightness off and then select the Brightness level that you want to use instead.

10. Use a dark background.

On Start , swipe over to the All apps list, select Settings > Personalization > Colors, and then select Dark.

11. Turn off your screen during calls.

If you're using the speaker phone or a Bluetooth enabled device, press the Power button to turn off the screen.

12. Turn off the viewfinder after you take a picture.

Press the Back button to exit the camera viewfinder, or press the Power button to put your phone to sleep.

13. Turn off Glance screen.

On Start , swipe over to the All apps list, select Settings > ExtrasOff > Glance screen, and then select Off. (Lumia phones only)

14. Sync email less often.

On Start , swipe over to the All apps list, and then select Settings > Accounts > Your email and accounts. Select the account that you want to change, and then select Manage > Change mailbox sync settings. In Download new email, select the box and choose a longer interval, and then select Done.

14. Sync contacts and calendars less often.

On Start , swipe over to the All apps list, and then select Settings > Accounts > Your email and accounts. Select the account that you want to change, and then select Manage > Change mailbox sync settings. In Sync contacts and calendars, select the box and choose a longer interval, and then select Done.

15. Sync only the email, calendars, and contacts you want.

You don’t have to sync email, calendars, or contacts unless you want to. On Start , swipe over to the All apps list, and then select Settings > Accounts > Your email and accounts. Select the account that you want to change, and then select Manage > Change mailbox sync settings. In Sync options, select Email, Calendar, or Contacts to turn them off , and then select Done.

16. Limit what you pin to Start.

It's great to have your favorite things pinned to Start so you can use them quickly. Just be aware that the more live tiles you have on Start, the more battery you use.

17. Upload only the pictures you want to keep.

This saves power—and saves you time later when you want to sort your pictures in OneDrive. To turn off automatic uploads, go to Settings > Devices > Default camera > Manage OneDrive upload settings, and then turn the camera upload setting off .

18. Charge your phone until the battery level is 100%.

To check your phone’s battery level, go to Settings > System > Battery saver.

19. Restart your phone.

This can sometimes fix problems that can shorten battery life.

20. Set your phone to use a 2G (GSM) network only.

On Start , swipe over to the All apps list, select Settings > Network & wireless > Cellular & SIM > SIM settings. In Highest connection speed, select the box, and then select 2G.

21. Check that your apps are up to date.

On Start , swipe over to the All apps list, and then select Store > Menu > Downloads and updates > Check for updates.

22. Turn on airplane mode if you don’t need Internet, cellular service, or other wireless communications.

On Start , swipe over to the All apps list, select Settings > Network & wireless > Airplane mode, and then turn airplane mode on .

23. Keep your passwords up to date.

Out of date or invalid passwords for email and social media accounts can shorten battery life. On Start , swipe over to the All apps list, and then select Settings > Accounts > Your email and accounts. Select the account you want to manage, select Fix, and then type the correct password info.
