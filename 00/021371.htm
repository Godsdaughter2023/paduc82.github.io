scottgem say:
Hi Ann, I'm an independent adviser and can help.

The answer to your question is no. A PDF is an image file, it is not data. So it cannot be imported as data into a record.

What you can do is use an OCR (Optical Character Reader) application to try and "read" the PDF into a text file. Depending on the format of the PDF, you may be able to format the text out from the OCR into a columnar format that can then be imported. But the quality of the image or the formatting if the document may make this difficult.

But there is another issue here. One of the purposes of the PDF format is to protect intellectual property. Pulling data out of a PDF that you do not have rights to, maybe considered copyright infringement. I would suggest going to the author of the document and request the data in an importable form.

Following address recovery strategy for Exchange Online services and SharePoint Online services.

For Exchange Online services recovery strategy, see below:

To retrieve email which has been deleted after 6 month or 1 year from Office 365, you need to enable Litigation Hold for that mailbox before the email is deleted.
To place a mailbox on Litigation Hold, please refer to:
https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/dn743673(v=exchg.160).aspx

To recover deleted messages in a user's mailbox
https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/ff660637(v=exchg.150).aspx

Meanwhile, here are some points you may need to note:

1.The Litigation Hold feature is available for Office 365 Enterprise E3 and E4 licenses.Please refer to Exchange Online Service Description: https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/exchange-online-service-description.aspx

2. The Recoverable Items folder has a quota of 30 GB, which isn't charged against the quota for the user's primary mailbox. when a mailbox is on litigation hold, none of the items in the Recoverable Items folder are permanently deleted. If you reach or exceed the 30 GB quota for the Recoverable Items folder, please contact Office 365 support to request an increase of the Recoverable Items quota for a mailbox on Litigation Hold.

For SharePoint services recovery strategy, see below:
1. If you need to restore a deleted items (doc, list items, site) you can use the Recycle Bin, now customers will have 90 days to recover deleted documents, lists, list items, sites, or even full site collections. 

https://support.office.com/en-au/article/Manage-the-Recycle-Bin-of-a-SharePoint-Online-site-collection-5fa924ee-16d7-487b-9a0a-021b9062d14b?CorrelationId=fedd6abd-6264-4a33-a49c-42228eaf0450&ui=en-US&rs=en-AU&ad=AU

2. When you delete most objects in SharePoint, they’re moved to one of two Recycle Bins: one bin for the site or workspace, and the other for the entire site collection. The Recycle Bins provide a safety net from which deleted objects can be restored.

https://support.office.com/en-IN/article/restore-a-deleted-object-from-a-sharepoint-recycle-bin-dd5c00c2-aef6-4458-9d04-80b185077653

3. If you want to restore previous versions of documents, please use versioning check feature.

 https://support.office.com/en-IN/article/enable-and-configure-versioning-for-a-list-or-library-84dd7adc-0ecb-4e08-9386-38817adc3c22

4. If you want to restore deleted sit collection, you can check the following article to get detailed steps:

https://support.office.com/en-nz/article/Restore-a-deleted-site-collection-91c18651-c017-47d1-9c27-3a22f325d6f1

john mcghie say:
Nothing in a text box or other kind of graphic will appear in the Table of Contents.  The TOC generator cannot look in the "graphics layer".  It's a design bug that has been around for years.

The other thing that will slow it down is unresolved tracked changes.  If the document contains tracked changes, this is likely to give strange results in the TOC.

Hope this helps
