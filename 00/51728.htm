Check your streaming settings on your computer. You need to enable streaming and enable the xbox as a receiver.

You can also try restarting the Windows Media Player Network Sharing Service. You can do this through the Microsoft Management Console using the Computer Management plug-in, or from the command prompt. This command should work from an elevated command prompt:

net stop WMPNetworkSvc & net start WMPNetworkSvc

Also, make sure your xbox is powered on and the video app is up to date. When you start the xbox again, make sure to access the xbox video app through the xbox menu rather than starting it by "Casting".

Note that Xbox video and Xbox music are different apps used for playing cast video and music respectively. These apps need to be updated separately.

"The only thing is It has never been disconnected."

  

In any case, what does, "works well with my other computers" mean given what you have?

  

Just what is the situation with the other computers that differs from the one you are having issues with?

  Different OS, different hardware (and thus different USB / chipset device drivers installed to communicate with the OS), different configuration/use of the drive itself (is it setup as a 'File History' destination on those other computers as well?), different 'power' settings in the computer hat shows the issues with the drive, different overall settings in the BIOS that might affect the way the computer treats everything from power to the external devices that connect to it, etc.?

  

What have you tried in terms of using the drive on this one computer it seems to be having issues with?

  Turned off "File History" on the computer in question and just connected the drive to said for days at a time to see if it drops connectivity (maybe setting it as a periodic/scheduled backup location instead of using 'File History'), changed which USB port it is plugged into on the computer in question, checked the power settings in Windows 10 to make sure it might not be having issues there, verified that the latest manufacturer supported Windows 10 device drivers are installed (manual process, do not just trust that Windows 10 went out and grabbed them for you - Microsoft is unlikely to have been the ones to manufacture the hardware in question, they don't directly support it either), verified that any BIOS settings on said computer do not turn off certain things after a given time and/or setup the USB ports to do things like 'charge devices even if the computer is turned off', tried this with a different USB external drive (USB stick, USB SSD, USB HDD, so on) to verify that it is not just this single drive exhibiting the issues on this single machine, but other drives react the same way),etc.?

  

You have seemingly narrowed this down in your mind to this definitively being an issue with the Operating System, and it may well be the case, but you haven't given the exact messages you get or the exact situation setup either at this point for anyone to suggest viable 'specific' options and/or make a call in any case.
