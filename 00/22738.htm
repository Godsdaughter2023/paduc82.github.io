roto-tom say:

Workday takes advantage of Excel to a great extent, both in its native reporting and for import/export.

Workday standard (delivered) reports and custom reports are both easily exported to Excel format, either interactively by clicking on the Excel icon above the report results, or automatically by scheduling a report to be rendered in Excel.

Many Workday "screens" or "Web pages" are actually reports, and display the interactive Export to Excel icon.

Integrations can take advantage of RaaS (Reporting as a Service) calls.

Imports into Workday and Exports out of Workday can be performed using xml (or Excel) "EIBs" (stands for Enterprise Interface Builder).

However, there is a significant learning curve to developing Workday reports and calculated fields.  In a sense, Workday reports can only go after one business object at a time (though with calculated fields you can look up related values from related objects).

Reporting from Workday requires knowledge of the object model.  Unfortunately (for reporting people) there is effectively no "database layer" which one can leverage in extracting data from Workday.  By design, the data model and the object model are inseparable, so code never gets out of sync with database.

Some options for creating integrations from Workday to data warehouses include Dell Boomi and MuleSoft.

twochi say:

Missing messages

Hi everyone, I have a nokia 5530 just bought early december 2009. It's been 2 days that can't receive and send text messages. So I thought it was just the service provider.  But now all my inbox and outbox messages gone. The phone calls works fine. What is this? Need help what to do? Thanks..

deepestblue---01 say:

Things to check:

  1. Make sure you have enough credit/balance to send texts and make calls
  2. Message center number: Go to Messaging -> Options -> Settings -> Text msg. -> Message center in use. Make sure the message center number is right. Even if it's right, it's a good idea to clear it and enter the number afresh. You can get the Message centre number from your operator. Once that is done, restart the phone.
  3. Network: Sometimes, messages won't be sent even if the above two factors are fine. It might be due to congestion in the network. All you can do is, to wait until late night or early in the morning when there would be less people using their phones. Sometimes, going to Offline profile and then back to a normal profile helps.

If you are sure that these things are not causing the issue, then try these methods:

  1. Clean up your sent items and drafts. It's not a good idea to keep too many messages in the inbox either. I never go over a few hundred texts at any point.
  2. Turn off your phone, take out the SIM, clean the contacts and put it back in and turn on. Now try sending messages.
  3. Turn off your phone, leave your battery and SIM out for 5-10minutes and put everything back in and turn on. Most often this would work.
  4. Restore factory settings: type *#7780# at the standby screen. Enter 12345 for the security code or the one you've changed it to

If the above do not work, then reset your phone. Backup your phone memory as these procedures will erase everything stored in the phone memory.

  1. Soft reset: This will clean up your phone memory. All user data, applications etc stored in the phone memory will be destroyed. Type *#7370# (It's Star - Hash - Seven - Three - Seven - Zero - Hash). Enter the security code when prompted; default is 12345.
  2. Hard reset: This is the last resort. The effect will be the same as that of a soft reset. Turn off your phone, press and hold down these keys: the call (Green) button, the end (red) button and the Camera button. Turn on your phone without releasing them. Leave the keys only after you see the region selection screen.

After reset, see if you can send messages. Once confirmed, then restore the backup - but skip the user files and settings.

amit tandon say:

Refer below image of worksheet MasterList which contains data:

Refer below image of Sheet2 with criteria A & B:

Enter below formula as an array formula (CTRL+SHIFT+ENTER) in cell A4 & copy down:

=IFERROR(IF(COUNTIF($B$3:$B3,$B$1)<COUNTIF(MasterList!$B$2:$B$16,$B$1),IF(COUNTIF($B$3:$B3,$B$2)<COUNTIF(MasterList!$B$2:$B$16,$B$2),MIN(SMALL(IF($B$1=MasterList!$B$2:$B$16,MasterList!$A$2:$A$16),COUNTIF($B$3:$B3,$B$1)+1),SMALL(IF($B$2=MasterList!$B$2:$B$16,MasterList!$A$2:$A$16),COUNTIF($B$3:$B3,$B$2)+1)),SMALL(IF($B$1=MasterList!$B$2:$B$16,MasterList!$A$2:$A$16),COUNTIF($B$3:$B3,$B$1)+1)),SMALL(IF($B$2=MasterList!$B$2:$B$16,MasterList!$A$2:$A$16),COUNTIF($B$3:$B3,$B$2)+1)),"")

Enter below formula in cell B4 & copy down and to the right till column H:

=IFERROR(IF(VLOOKUP($A4,MasterList!$A$2:$I$16,MATCH(B$3,MasterList!$A$1:$H$1,0),FALSE)="","",VLOOKUP($A4,MasterList!$A$2:$I$16,MATCH(B$3,MasterList!$A$1:$H$1,0),FALSE)),"")

Refer below image of Sheet3 with criteria C:

Enter below formula as an array formula (CTRL+SHIFT+ENTER) in cell A4 & copy down:

=IFERROR(IF(COUNT($A$3:$A3)<COUNTIF(MasterList!$B$2:$B$16,$B$1),SMALL(IF($B$1=MasterList!$B$2:$B$16,MasterList!$A$2:$A$16),ROW($A1)),""),"")

Enter below formula in cell B4 & copy down and to the right till column H (same formula as in Sheet2):

=IFERROR(IF(VLOOKUP($A4,MasterList!$A$2:$I$16,MATCH(B$3,MasterList!$A$1:$H$1,0),FALSE)="","",VLOOKUP($A4,MasterList!$A$2:$I$16,MATCH(B$3,MasterList!$A$1:$H$1,0),FALSE)),"")

For criteria D, it will be same as Sheet3 criteria C, just replace the cell B1 value from C to D.

Fast Startup puts your system into a hybrid hibernation state and many systems seem to have issues with hibernation.  I don't know exactly why 2 of the same model of computer would have 2 different reactions to hibernation (normally it's fairly model specific on how successful hibernation can be), but there would seem to be something on your system that simply does not like hibernation.  I personally disable hibernation in all my systems, no matter how well the system handles it, but my systems have SSD system drives, so hibernation doesn't really speed startup any.
