daniel chaplin say: Take a look at these resources to help you fix your problem:

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/windows_vista-pictures/how-to-remove-duplicate-music-files-from-the/5b0e6ce8-034c-421e-ad33-407f82ddd8f0

This could happen if the same folder is monitored twice by WMP, for example, if both C:\Media and C:\Media\Music are in WMP's list of monitored folders.

To view the list of monitored folders, press F3 in WMP, and make sure that the Advanced Options are visible. What folders are in that list?

Another possibility is that WMP's media library got corrupted. To reset it, follow the instructions for WMP 11 on Vista at http://zachd.com/pss/pss.html#medialibrary . Note that resetting the library will make you lose all current play counts, and possibly ratings too.

Also try this: Open the media player and go to the library tab and click the little arrow below it. go to add to library...there should be something on your bottom left that says add files previously deleted from library....if there is a check mark there..you need to uncheck it. also when you connect your external drive to the comp. open this same box and then look to see what's highlighted in the box e.g. C/: my documents or so and see where the music will be added from so you don't end up with them 2 or 3 times. as a drastic measure you could highlight all the song in the media player..delete them, and the download them from the external drive and only have one copy in the player. the same rule applies, uncheck that box or you're in trouble.
