Common questions about weapon collecting:

Do I have to collect all the weapons on this list?
Not at all, just one of each type. This list shows where you can find them in multiple locations. If you find a certain weapon type, you can cross it off the list entirely. Just remember that you have to complete the mission for it to count.

How do I save weapons in "Death of a Showman"?
There are two ICA boxes located at the end of the level. You have to place weapons in the ICA box where you collect your mine and detonator. If you drop them in the first ICA box (below the stairs, with syringe in it), you don't get the weapons in your Hideout.

What weapons are not collectible?
All melee weapons will not be displayed in Hideout. In addition, the Custom 1911, the Nailgun, and the Real WWI Pistol from Curtains Down all do not display, and do not count towards the achievement. However, you may collect and use them in missions.

As I wrote, your problem probably is related to the wireless adapter in your desktop. Perhaps it is as simple as requiring an updated driver, but that remains to be seen.

Your ipconfig results are quite surprising.  Given that you are in a university environment, if we can't identify a problem with your wireless adapter, you will probably have to seek help from the university IT department.  Also see comments below re VPN.

Here -- more as a matter of interest than anything else -- is what some of your settings mean:

Wireless Network Adapter
  • Your adapter is a Realtek RTL8188CE Wireless LAN 802.11n PCI-E NIC (Realtek RTL8188CE 1x1 11b/g/n Wireless LAN PCI Express Half Mini Card Adapter, which seems to be installed in Lenovo ThinkPads).

  • Your adapter has been automatically assigned (normally by the wireless router, but see below) an IP address of 10.92.129.20.  This address is part of what is known as a "Class A" network, which is usually used only by very large organizations (such as a university).  That suggests that this wireless network is coming from a university wireless access point rather than a wireless router connected to the cable modem in your basement. 

    It is thus possible that the antenna on the wireless adapter of your desktop isn't in as good a location as your laptop for receiving the signal. This is especially likely if your desktop uses an internal wireless adapter, with the antenna on the back of the computer under a desk and next to a wall. If you have a USB wireless adapter for your desktop (or an antenna on an extension cable) try moving the antenna near a window, if possible.

  • The "Default Gateway" for this connection (the address of the wireless router) is 10.92.128.1, which is in the same Class A network.

  • The DHCP server (which is what assigns your adapter its IP address) is at 195.130.132.102.  This is a public IP address belonging to Telenet/Pandora, a Belgian ISP.  In almost all home wireless networks, the DHCP server is part of the router, so your situation is quite different.

Virtual Private Network (VPN)

One of my personal favourite online icon converters is https://convertio.co/jpg-ico/
A quick google search for "convert jpeg to ico" will result in hundreds of sites both free and paid.

However if you want to use windows only to do this you can do it with paint or any of your favourite image editors.

1. Open the image in paint (or favourite image editor)
2. Resize to the desired icon size (eg. 32x32px - In pixels)
3. Save the image as a BMP.
4. In file explorer find the file you saved.
4a. In File Explorer ensure you have "File Name Extensions" visible from the "View" tab of File Explorer.
5. Change the file name extension by renaming the file.

eg. MyFile.JPG to MyFile.ICO

And that should give you a usable icon, however the online converters are much easier.

EDIT: As someone has pointed out to me that renaming a file is not how to create an icon, I would like to point out that it was not asked in the question if this was to create a windows icon or not. The method via paint will not provide transparency and would not work for windows icons. In this case I would suggest an online converter or 3rd party software.  However using paint and selecting 24 bit BMP and renaming the file to filename.ICO will create an icon file making my previous answer valid.
