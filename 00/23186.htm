sr_827 say:

Vacation calculation

I'm trying to create a table that would calculate who many days of vacations an employee has accumalated base on the following:

  1. If the employees works 115 or more hours in one month = 1 day ; if not = 0.
  2. Add a total amount of days accumulated
  3. Match the employee name in order to do the calculation, since I can have new employees every month 

Can anyone help me please?

bernie deitrick say:

This assumes your sheet name with the data is named "Work Record", with names in A, Dates in B, and hours in C.

Insert a new sheet, and starting in A2, list your employees down column  A.  Then in B1 enter a starting date, like 1/1/16. In C1, enter 2/1/16, then select B1:C1 and drag the fill handle to create the date headers - extend the date headers by one more column than you need. 

Finally, in B2, enter a formula like this

=IF(SUMIFS('Work Record'!$C:$C,'Work Record'!A:A,$A2,'Work Record'!B:B,">=" & B$1,'Work Record'!B:B,"<" & C$1)>115,1,0)

And then copy to match your employee list and all but the last date headers.  Then just sum the rows to get a summary of vacation hours, like

=SUM(B2:Z2)
