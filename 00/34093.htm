My suggestion - if you have Windows 7 Professional or Windows 7 Ultimate - is to install Windows XP Mode and import your profile into that virtual machine.  At that time you can get all of your stuff off and transfer it to Windows 7 (host OS.)  Beyond that - find a friend who will let you create a new account on their Windows XP computer and import your profile onto their computer so you can properly copy (even if it is just drag and drop to an external drive to move to the new machine) your "important" stuff over.
 
The word "important" is in quotes because it never ceases to amaze me how such data isso important the moment it is lost - but it wasn't important enough to have setup periodic and simple backup procedures to external media so it would be highly unlikely it would ever be lost.
 
How did Microsoft cause you to have only one copy of your stuff (no backups?)  Seems like a poor decision originally and confirmed now.  Exporting your profile is no substitute for proper and consistent backups - especially when you don't understand exactly what it is you are doing and don't test said method to ensure you can get your data back in the probable/possible cases.  A little research can save a lot of time and frustration.
 
Now I realize you made a good-faith attempt by exporting the profile before said machine died.  That's fine.

(Yes - I know your files are likely not corrupt - but that doesn't mean you couldn't extract all the data out using the tools presented so you could at least access it again.)
 
Now I know you will likely get upset/angry with my answer (or parts of it anyway) and that is understandable - but I am just pointing out the facts.  It's been said for *years* that everyone should backup their data on computers.  It's not new and it's not going away.  Not many other things in life give you the opportunity to replicate/duplicate things so easily and efficiently - allowing you to return to a previous state without much thought; not using it is only the individual's fault. ;-)
