trevor rabey say: You don't need a custom field. You just need a filter. There is already a built in filter called the date range filter. Have you tried that? It finds all tasks which start or finish within the date range as well as any which are scheduled to be in progress within the range even if the start is earlier than the left hand end of the range or if the finish is to the right hand end of the range.

tom_bpc say:

Daniel,

1.  I think the IIf statement is not necessary.  In a flag field, TRUE automatically converts to "Yes", and FALSE automatically converts to "No."  Here the IIf obscures the issues with the other syntax.

2.  Because of the way you placed the parentheses, your formula fails to apply the (%Complete<100) restriction to tasks with a start before the two-week cutoff.  I think this is closer to your intent:

([Start]<([Current Date]+14) Or [Finish]<([Current Date]+14)) And [% Work Complete]<100.

3.  This formula will flag any tasks with a start date sooner than the 2-week cutoff, except those that have already been marked as complete.  The finish date is superfluous.  In-progress tasks that are scheduled to finish after the cutoff will be flagged.  If you want to exclude those then you'll need to change the formula. (There are many ways to write a lookahead filter; I don't remember the most elegant off the top of my head.)

4.  An automated version of the "Date Range..." filter that Trevor references could be made using this flag formula:

([Start]<=([Current Date]+14)) And ([Finish]>=[Current Date])

The validity of any lookahead filter is dependent on your progress updating process.  If your process allows incomplete work in the past and/or completed work in the future (i.e. the default process), then a pure date-range filter won't be accurate.  The fact that you feel the need to exclude completed tasks in your filter implies that you follow this default process.

Good luck, tom
