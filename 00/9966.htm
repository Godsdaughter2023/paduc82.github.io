Microsoft Word 2010: highlight different words throughout document - Microsoft Community

kevinkurth say:

Microsoft Word 2010: highlight different words throughout document and then have them printed out

I am trying to print out a document that has various words highlighted in different colors.  When I search the document it highlights that word but it does not print out.  When I manually highlight the word it does print out, but I don't want to go through the document and rehighlight each word manually.  Is there a step by step way to do this?  It seems like there should be.

Also I understand that MS Word can highlight up to 15 different words or phrases in different colors 14 really, black is worthless for this task).  Can this be done using the search function without making the other colors disappear?  And then again can it actually go to the printer without changing each word or phrase manually?

suzanne s. barnhill say:

The issue is the two different uses of the word "highlight." As far as I can see, the "Reading Highlight" in the Find dialog in Word 2007 and above is the same as the "Highlight all items found" check box with Find All in Word 2003. In neither case does "highlight" actually mean "apply highlighting"; it just means "select." You can select specific text and then copy/paste it into another document for printing, but you can't print the current document with a representation of the "highlighting" (selection).

You can, however, actually apply highlighting using the Replace dialog. In the "Find what" box, type your search string. Click More>> to expand the dialog. With the insertion point in the (empty) "Replace with" box, click Format | Highlight. When you click Replace All, Word will apply the currently selected highlight color to the found text. You can then change the highlight color and perform another search to apply that. Word always applies whatever color is currently selected. You can repeat this procedure until you've used all the available highlight colors. They will print.

There are several ways to remove the highlighting. To begin with, you can choose to just not display it (File | Options | Display: Show highlighter marks). If it's not displayed, it won't print. Or you can remove it selectively by rerunning the Replace operation for specific words; in the "Replace with" box, click Format | Highlight again, and the label will change from "Highlight" to "Not Highlight." Or you can set the Highlighter color to No Color and select the entire document to remove it all at once.
