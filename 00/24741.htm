Battery: making it last

The simplest way to make the battery in your Windows Phone last longer between charges is to use Battery Saver.

With Battery Saver, you can turn off all nonessential features and conserve battery in just a few taps. Or, you can limit background usage to save power; you can see how much battery power your apps are consuming and change whether they can run in the background. In this topic we'll tell you how and also give you tips on other ways to conserve battery.

Note

This topic is about Windows Phone 8.1. If your phone is running Windows Phone 8, some options and icons may look a little different, and some features may not be available. Check to see which software version you have and find out if an update is available.

Turn off nonessential features

If you prefer the blanket approach to conserving battery, you can use Battery Saver to turn off the nonessential features that consume battery in the background. When you do this, your phone won't be able to:

  • Automatically receive email or calendar updates. You can still sync your email manually whenever you want.
  • Update some Live Tiles. If a Tile uses push notifications to get its updates, it won't receive those updates in Battery Saver mode.

You can still make and receive phone calls, get and send text messages, and browse the Internet.

To change Battery Saver settings

  1. In the App list, tap Battery Saver 

    .
    Note

    If you don't have Windows Phone 8.1 installed, you can use Battery Saver by going to Settings 

     > Battery Saver.

  2. In Settings, do one of the following:

    • Tap Off to turn off Battery Saver.
    • Tap When battery is under 20% if you want Battery Saver to conserve power when the battery level gets low.
    • Tap Always (limits functionality) if you want Battery Saver to conserve power all the time. Think twice before choosing this option—your phone will prevent most background functions.
    • Turn on Now until next charge 
       if you want Battery Saver to conserve power immediately and then return to normal battery usage the next time you charge your phone.
Tip

You can add Battery Saver as a quick action in action center to quickly change its settings.              

Limit background usage

Some apps can drain the battery even when you're not using them at the moment—this is known as background usage. Battery Saver lets you control your phone's battery usage on a per-app basis.

Note

Limiting background usage in Battery Saver is only available for Windows Phone 8.1.

To allow apps to run in the background

  1. In the App list, tap Battery Saver 

    . Swipe over to Usage to see a list of your apps and their relative battery consumption.

  2. Tap an app, then turn on Allow app to run in the background 

    .
Note

This option won't appear for some apps.

Tip

If you want to allow apps to run in the background even when Battery Saver is on, tap the Allow this app to run in the background even when Battery Saver is on check box.

Battery-saving tips

Here are some more ways to conserve battery on your phone.

Display
  • Reduce the time before the screen turns off. Set a shorter duration before your screen times out and your phone locks.
  • Lower the brightness setting. In the App list, tap Settings 
     > Brightness, turn off Automatically adjust, and then select the Level that you want to use instead.
  • Use a dark background theme. In the App list, tap Settings 
     > Theme > Background, and then tap Dark.
  • Turn off the screen during calls. If you're using the speaker phone or a Bluetooth device, press the Power 
     button to turn off the screen.
  • Turn off the viewfinder after you take a picture. Press the Back 
     button to exit the camera viewfinder, or press the Power 
     button to put your phone to sleep.
Syncing
  • Sync email less often. In the App list, tap Settings 
    , and then tap Email + accounts. Tap the account that you want to change the sync settings for, tap Download new content, choose a longer interval, and then tap Done 
    .
  • Choose which contacts are synced to your phone. You don't have to sync email and social media contacts unless you want to.
  • Limit what you pin to Start. It's great to have your favorite things—people, apps, songs, and more—pinned to Start so you can access them quickly. Just be aware that the more Live Tiles you have on Start, the more battery you use.
  • Upload only the pictures you want to keep. This saves power and saves you time later when you want to sort your pics in OneDrive.  
  • Check social media less often. Each time you visit What's new in the People Hub and Photos Hub, your phone uses extra power to sync and update.
General
  • Restart your phone if you notice short battery life. This can sometimes fix issues that can shorten battery life.
  • Use headphones. If you're listening to music or using a Bluetooth device, try using headphones.
  • Turn off Bluetooth connections you're not using. If you're not using a Bluetooth accessory, you can turn off Bluetooth.  
Warning

Don't remove or replace the battery when the phone is turned on. If you have a spare battery, turn off your phone, and then replace the battery in your phone with the spare. This helps ensure that your data is saved properly. To turn off your phone, press and hold the Power 

 button for three seconds, and then swipe down. The goodbye message appears, and then the screen goes dark.

Top Feedback on Microsoft’s Casual Games in Windows 10

Hello Casual Game Fans!

It is awesome to see all of the feedback you have been sending us, in the Windows Feedback app and here in the forums.  We’re constantly working on improving the built-in casual gaming experience in Windows 10, so we wanted to take this opportunity to respond to some of the top feedback we’ve been hearing.

Do I still have all my old casual games for free?

Many of the comments have asked why the games didn’t just remain free like in the past. To be clear, the classic casual games like Solitaire, Mahjong, and Minesweeper have been free to play for the past 25 years on Windows, and continue to be free to play on Windows 10.  Users can access and play everything within the game for free, including new premium features like Daily Challenges and Star Club.  Even classics like Klondike, FreeCell, and Spider are still provided for free via the Microsoft Solitaire Collection app.  We're also working on bringing back additional games from older versions of Windows, and we recently asked our fans for input on what games they want us to work on next.

Do I need to have internet and an Xbox Live account to play?

You do not need to be connected to the internet or connected to Xbox Live to play.  You can play all your old favorites the same way you have been for decades.  However, by signing in with your Xbox or Microsoft account, you’re then able to save the state of your games in the cloud so that you never lose your progress, in addition to having access to other benefits of the Xbox Live service, such as achievements and leaderboards.

Why are there ads in some of the games in Windows 10?

As for advertisements, the basic game modes do not have any forced video advertisements or any ads visible during gameplay.  The new premium features (Daily Challenges and Star Club) have interstitial video ads roughly every 15 minutes, but only between games.  Users can optionally pay $1.49/month or $9.99/year to get the Premium Edition of these games, which not only removes advertisements, but also gives double coins for completing Daily Challenges, and bonuses in some of the basic game modes.

We wanted everyone to have access to these features without being forced to pay, so we decided a light advertisement model was the best approach. If you compare the ad experience in our games with many of the other free-to-play advertisement based games, we think you’ll find they are more than reasonable as we don’t slam ads in your face constantly during gameplay, or video ads between every gameplay.

As a casual games fan, hopefully you can understand our approach and you enjoy seeing new features, updates, and content in our games. Having advertisements allows us to continue working on these games so you can enjoy playing them for another 25 years. :)

Please continue letting us know how you’re liking our casual games, and we look forward to hearing from you!

Here is code I use to read files from folders on a Mac.

    

Private Function SelectAll() As Boolean
    Dim sDir As String, s As Integer
    Erase strFolderFiles
    #If Mac Then
        Dim RootFolder As String
        Dim scriptstr As String
        On Error Resume Next
        RootFolder = MacScript("return (path to documents folder) as String")
        scriptstr = "return posix path of (choose folder with prompt ""Select the folder""" & _
            " default location alias """ & RootFolder & """) as string"
        strFolderPath = MacScript(scriptstr)
        sDir = Dir$(strFolderPath, vbNormal)
        On Error Resume Next
        Do Until LenB(sDir) = 0
            ReDim Preserve selFiles(s)
            selFiles(s) = strFolderPath & sDir
            s = s + 1
            sDir = Dir$
        Loop
    #Else
        With Application.FileDialog(msoFileDialogFolderPicker)
            .Title = "Select the folder containing the files to rename"
            .InitialFileName = CurDir
            .AllowMultiSelect = False
            If .Show = 0 Then
                SelectAll = False
                Exit Function
            End If
            strFolderPath = .SelectedItems(1) & Application.PathSeparator
            sDir = Dir$(strFolderPath, vbNormal)
            On Error Resume Next
            Do Until LenB(sDir) = 0
                ReDim Preserve selFiles(s)
                selFiles(s) = strFolderPath & sDir
                s = s + 1
                sDir = Dir$
            Loop
        End With
    #End If
    SelectAll = True

End Function
