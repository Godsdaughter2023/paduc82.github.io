The problem arises when I add my Live@edu account to my Outlook 2007.  It receives all the messages from both accounts (Uni + Hotmail), but when I choose to send a new message, it only sends it from my Live@edu account and doesn't give me the option to send it from my hotmail.

Initially I had both on Outlook 2007, and then realised I could add my hotmail onto my OWA, which meant on OWA, I had Live@edu + Hotmail TOGETHER, and then in Outlook 2007 I have Live@Edu + Hotmail TOGETHER (i.e. same as OWA) + Hotmail seperately (i.e. i have 1 set of messages from my Live@edu, and 2 lots from my hotmail).  After deleting my seperate hotmail account, i still have messages from both my Live@edu + hotmail (as OWA), but i don't have the same option to choose the account to send from as per OWA.

Kind Regards

 

Ravi

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

OWA is simply a web interface into your Exchange mailbox.  It's not really a mail client, per se.  If you can see both Hotmail and your school mail in one place, then you must be forwarding your Hotmail to the school's Exchange account or Exchange is reaching out to Hotmail via POP to pull it in.  OWA cannot access Hotmail directly.  If you can send mail so that your messages appear to come from either the school address or Hotmail, then the school must have added the extra address to your Active Directory record.

When you use Outlook to access your Exchange mailbox, you also see both school mail ad Hotmail because both are in the same school mailbox.  When you add the Hotmail account to Outlook, you're not accessing the Hotmail mailbox separately from the school mailbox so you see the messages both in the school mailbox (because you're forwarding the Hotmail there or Exchange is polling the Hotmail mailbox) and in Hotmail, since you're accessing the actual Hotmail mailbox.  If you have two accounts defined in Outlook, you will have the option to choose the sending account.  The word "From" in a new message window should have a drop-down on it that will allow you to choose your sending account.

Log into your Hotmail account using a web browser and see if you are forwarding your Hotmail to your school address.  If so, cancel the forwarding and Outlook will then cease duplicating the messages because you will have cancelled what you did to cause the duplication.  If you are not forwarding your messages, then ask the school's mail administrators to stop polling your Hotmail account.

Brian Tillman [MVP-Outlook]

Usually when the computer loses power unexpectedly, the file system becomes unstable.

 

And when files don't appear on the windows explorer, ie index, then it is because they have become unindexed by the master file table (mft)

 

So, what you need to do is to run a check disk in order to reconcile all the files with the master file table.

 

You can right click on the C drive via explorer, then select properties, then under the tools tab there will be a check disk feature.  Also, you may want to do a defrag after the check disk is completed.

 

Barring any major corruption, the check disk should be able to run after you reboot the system.   then afterwards the system should reboot and launch windows normally again.

tom ogilvy say:

Put your slope (length of hypotenuse) in B2      [I assume this is a linear measure and not an angle]

A2:  slope    (label)      

A3:  ribbon  (label)

B3:  ribbon value

 

volume for your cone would be   C2:  =((0.6*B2)*(0.8*B2)^2)/3

The added  divide by 27 I ignored thinking it was spurious. 

I am not sure what ribbon is but I assume it is the length of top of the pile (part that separates the two halves of the cone) although dividing by 27 is confusing. 

Volume for your body would be  C3:  =B3*(0.6*B2)*(0.8*B2)/27

C4:  =C2+C3

It appears you are assuming the angle of the slope of ~37 degrees

I am guessing you are using the formula for a pyramid for your body, but seems to me it should be a prism.   Also,   ribbon x height x radius  might should be ribbon x height x 2 x radius  but its your formula.
