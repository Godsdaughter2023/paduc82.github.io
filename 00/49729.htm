Hi James. I'm Greg, a volunteer installation specialist and 8 year Windows MVP, here to help you.

At all times what remains most important is the Contacts list and Settings in each of your online web mail accounts (Gmail, Yahoo, Outlook, etc), not in the People app which merely sync's them.

The Windows 10 Mail app is only going to mirror the contacts you have sync'd from your web mail services. You allow it to do this in the Mail Settings (gear icon at bottom of Inbox) > Manage Accounts > select the account > Change Mailbox Sync Settings > scroll down to Sync Options to enable Contacts sync.

These Settings are duplicated in the People app Settings, where there is also a check box to Automatically Add Recent contacts.

Once you set this for each of your Mail Accounts, there should be no more or less contacts sync'd than what appear in each of your Mail Accounts. If there are duplicates because they appear in more than one account, these should be differentiated with the name of the Accounts in which they multiply appear.

If something done in the shop caused these to appear more than what is explainable by the various accounts sync'ing more than one, then what I would do is:

1) Go into each mail account in it's internet home location (e.g. yahoo.com/mail) to use their tool to eliminate any duplicates, then when the list there is clean, export the contacts to a backup file in your Documents.

2) temporarily turn off Contacts sync for each account from the above Mail Sync settings, one all Contacts sync's are disabled,close the Mail app to confirm the changes are duplicated in the People app and no accounts appear (or disable them there), then delete any remaining contacts in People, restart PC, turn each Contacts sync back on in Mail Sync Settings and confirm this in People settings.
