philipradler say:

While we wait for the real experts to chime in, here's my take on the "bug" you described. From my tests, it appears that the PDF creator—in my case, the "Save as PDF" option in Word itself—is updating the image field by retrieving the original Visio file before creating the PDF. (You can use Alt+F9 to toggle that field, which will say something like "[EMBED Visio.Drawing.11}.") Naturally, that will "update" your resultant PDF with the original, two-shape image in the VSD file.

I found a simple solution: Right after you delete one of the shapes, and while still in the Visio "window," press F9 (that's function key F9). That forces an update of the field, and seems, in effect, to cancel the "refresh" call to the original Visio file.  Tests without the F9 step came out as you described, with both images appearing in the PDF. Pressing F9 before leaving the window resulted in PDFs carrying only the surviving shape, as you intended.

Hope this helps.

-Phil
