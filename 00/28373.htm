Build 9879 turning HDD on and off continuously

    I just discovered a really nasty bug in build 9879.  Whenever Windows turns the screen off while running on battery power, it also continuously sends the spin-down command to at least the C: drive, causing it to spin up and spin down every couple of seconds, which will cause the drive to fail prematurely.

    Here's a screenshot showing all the spin-up and spin-down events, and the short list of running processes:


     With the laptop running on battery power, I launched another program I wrote to send the SC_MONITORPOWER -> MONITOR_OFF message (same thing that Windows does after 60 seconds at the Lock Screen).  As soon as the screen turned off (at 5:20:00 PM), the spin-down/spin-up sequence started and continued until I moved the mouse to turn the screen back on.  The behavior is exactly the same if I let Windows turn the screen off in five minutes (or whatever the timeout is set to in Power Options).  However, if the laptop turns the screen off via hardware when I shut the lid, the HDD keeps running as it is supposed to (with my Power Options setting to do nothing when the lid is closed).

    Researching further, I discovered even stranger behavior:

  1. This only happens when the laptop is running on battery power.
  2. While the screen is off, if I unplug the laptop, the HDD starts cycling, the screen turns back on for a couple seconds showing the lock screen, and turns back off.
  3. If I plug the laptop in, the HDD immediately stops cycling, the screen turns on again for a couple of seconds showing the lock screen, and turns back off.
  4. After moving the mouse to wake the screen, the HDD stops cycling, and the lock screen shows for 60 seconds.  When the screen turns off again, the cycling resumes if on battery power.

    Here's a download link to an MP4 video I made of the behavior.  The hum is the HDD, and the whirring is the CPU fan.  Windows 9879 turning HDD on and off.mp4 (6.2 MB).  You will hear the HDD rapidly and repeatedly spinning down and spinning up.  The cycling speed does seem to vary.  In my screenshot, it was approximately every 4-6 seconds; in my video, it is much faster; and when I tested again later, the HDD would stay off for 5-15 seconds at a time.  In each case, the "on" time was usually about a second or two, so Windows is sending the spin-down command frequently, and the drive is spinning back up whenever it is being accessed by Windows.

    I'd love to hear from those of you running build 9879 on a laptop, whether you see this same behavior or not.  The easiest way to test is to press [Win]+[L] and wait a minute for the screen to turn off, and watch the HDD LED/listen to the HDD.  When the HDD is spinning up/down, the HDD LED glows continuously.

    If you would like, you can download HddPowerState.exe from here, and ScreenOff.exe from here.  Both are standalone and small.  ScreenOff.exe passes VirusTotal with 0/56 detection rate.  HddPowerState.exe doesn't do so good, with a detection rate of 8/56 (probably due to its low-level device access at launch to retrieve device names).

jllatham say:

If your table contain many formulas that contain functions like VLOOKUP(), MATCH(), FIND() and others that can reference entire columns, try giving them specific row references to prevent the possibility that they are trying to look through all 1-million+ possible rows in an Excel 2007/2010 worksheet.

For an example, perhaps you have a formula like this

=VLOOKUP(A1,Sheet2!$A:$F,4,False)

and your data on Sheet2 goes down to row 367, you might change the formula to become;

=VLOOKUP(A1,Sheet2!$A$2:$F$367,4,False)

or to give yourself some "breathing" room for more data entries on Sheet2:

=VLOOKUP(A1,Sheet2!$A$2:$F$450,4,False)

Other array type formulas should also be looked at such as COUNTIFS(), SUMIFS() and SUMPRODUCT() for the same type of issues.
