zune folders - Microsoft Community

therjsmiths say:

zune folders

had a zune and zune pass for several years and either i or Zune have changed the way they save to folders over time.  i now have multiple, Zune folders some with mp3, some with DRM some called subscription etc.  What is the default way that Zune is now saving purchases and subscriptions so i can clean this up?  I know where software>collection tells me it saves to but it creates other folders to organise after that.

mysqueakycat say:

I haven't noticed a change in behavior and I've had Zune Pass since 1-1-09.  There is a Zune folder (default under "my music")  Where it saves within that Zune folder depends on a few things:

1)  Zune Pass music that you download from your PC is in the folder Zune/Subscription.

2)  Zune Pass music that you download directly to your device gets moved to the Zune folder once it moves to your PC (not Subscription like it really should--this is a known problem since I've had the Zune Pass).  You'll have to move them to the Subscription folder if you want to keep things on the PC nice and tidy.  I don't download directly to my device so this doesn't happen.  (I either wait until I get to my PC to download or add to cart then download from pc).

3)  If you are subscribed to Channels or download a playlist from the Marketplace these tracks go into the Zune/Temporary Music Folder.  They are not added to your collection until you add them; they then automatically go into the Zune Subscription Folder.

4)  Actual purchases (with credits or Microsoft points) go into the Zune folder.  This is probably where you have a combination of Zune Pass stuff that was downloaded directly on to the device and those that you actually purchased.  (Once you make a purchase the software should automatically delete the Zune Pass copy in the Subscription folder, at least it does for me although I've heard some other people have to delete them as they end up as duplicates)

So I'm guessing that you probably just need to go to the Zune folder and move the .wma files that are Zune Pass (have an expiration date) to the Subscription folder and you should be good to go.

I actually think that OneNote and the family of O365 is the right way to go, but requires a bit of process.  Let me try and explain (or just got to https://diligent.com/en-gb/company-secretary-software/ ) - the job at hand is called "Building Board Books".

So firstly, let me try and articulate the requirement a bit better (now I've done some go-see-and-assess):

  • Company board meetings may be monthly, quarterly or annual and typically consist of 100-300 pages of content in a fairly rigorous structure.
  • The content is typically Word, Excel, Powerpoint and pdf and is created by multiple (read 10+) people within a business.
  • The content is then normally collated by 1-2 people, creating the super-pack (goes through a few versions) which is ready to print.
  • They then the create all the tabs etc to divide up the content, before hitting print, killing 100-200 etc.
  • Each board member receives their hard copy of the materials, that they review and scribble on, before the board meeting, where they decide on actions and decisions, closing out the meeting.

    • Secretary is notified that content is ready.
    • She drags it into their "meeting pack" in a new shared workbook with the director.
    • The agenda page includes the hyperlinks to the subpages (you have to do it manually, but it works), for really easy moving between sections (nice).
    • Director takes their Surface or iPad, and annotates it digitally (highlight, notes etc), in prep for the meeting (and during), giving them a record of their notes.
  • During the meeting, the note taker can then create a shared OneNote page, linked to the original meeting invite, for all attendees, that they write down the actions and decisions, and the attendees, date etc is all linked in, directly from Outlook (out of the box today).

So then, I can sort of get there....its a bit clunky....but it could work with a bit of process.

What I don't have, is the nice experience, templates and underpinning SharePoint that could make it good, or the document structure (remember Microsoft Binder?!) that could create the full document pack, with links and tabs, to make it great.

Not sure if it helps anyone, or someone can help improve my method (madness)?!

daniel jackson say:

Hello Bryan,

Thank you for posting your query in Microsoft Office Community.

I understand your concern and let me answer your query.

1) You cannot synchronize Emails, Contacts and Calendars data with Office 365. However, this can be done through Outlook.
http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/office365home/

2) You can set up Hotmail/Live in Outlook as a MAPI account (Outlook Connector) in Outlook 2010 and synchronize Emails, Contacts and Calendars data on different devices.

In Exchange Active Sync account, you can access mail, calendar, contacts and tasks data on multiple devices.

Refer to the following article for detailed instructions on how to setup Hotmail, Live or Outlook.com email address as an Exchange Active Sync account.  
http://blogs.office.com/2012/11/15/put-the-power-of-outlook-2013-behind-your-outlook-com-email/
