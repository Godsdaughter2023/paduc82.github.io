macropod say:

Since your thread title and the contents of your post both indicate you want rounding up, not rounding down or plain rounding, you'd need a field coded along the lines of:
{QUOTE{=INT({MERGEFIELD Number}/1000+1-(MOD({MERGEFIELD Number},1000)=0)) \# ",0"}}

For plain rounding, you could use:

{={MERGEFIELD Number}/1000 \# ",0"}

and, for rounding down, you could use:

{=INT({MERGEFIELD Number}/1000) \# ",0"}

where 'Number' is the field name.

Note: The field brace pairs (i.e. '{ }') for the above example are all created in the document itself, via Ctrl-F9 (Cmd-F9 on a Mac); you can't simply type them or copy & paste them from this message. Nor is it practical to add them via any of the standard Word dialogues. The spaces represented in the field construction are all required.

scottgem say:
First, you need to understand some terms. A Field is a logical division of a record. As a general rule we don't do calculations in fields. While Access 2010 does have a calculated datatype, for outside compatibility its not recommended that it be used.

Calculations are done in queries or on forms/reports.

Calculations are entered in the top row of a query grid or as the Controlsource of a control on a form/report.

You can use the expression Doug gave as the calcs..

djsteele, mvp say:

Assuming you've got text boxes named Weight and Length, add a new text box and set its ControlSource property to

=IIf(IsNull(Length) Or Length = 0, Null, Weight / (Length * Length * Length))

Assuming you named that text box Result, add another new text box and set its ControlSource property to

=IIf(Result < 0.16, "Seriously Underweight", IIf(Result >= 0.16 And Result < 0.19, "Underweight", "Okay"))

Note that the result and the label cannot be saved to the table (but then, there's no reason to want to do this). As well, this technique will not work on a continuous form (or one in datasheet view).

Hello, 

To resolve this issue, following these steps: 

  1. Close all the Office for iPad apps. To do this, press the Home button on your iPad twice, swipe to find each Office for iPad app, and then swipe the preview screen up to close each app.  
  2. Go to iPad Settings .  
  3. In Settings , tap to select one of your Office for iPad apps such as Word , Excel , or PowerPoint 
  4. Under RESET , tap Reset <app name> .  
  5. Slide to enable Delete Login Credentials .  
  6. Start one of your Office for iPad apps so that your credential is deleted.  
  7. Sign in using your Office 365 Organization ID account.
