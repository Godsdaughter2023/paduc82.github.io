rohn007 say:

I have not seen any official documentation on the "order of precedence" for 365 "consumer" licenses. This information is what I've collected from this forum since 365/2013 was inflicted on us.

365 has been designed to allow you to have one license at a time. If you activate a different new one, it (usually?) will replace the current one. But how the replacement is handled is inconsistent.

The reports other people have made (a while back, 1-2 years ago) is that if you use the same account to activate 365 Home it will REPLACE the university subscription. Your account will be for (up to) 5 computers for 1 year. 

On the other hand, I have also seen reports (recent, the last few months) where people with 365 Personal added 365 Home and ended up with a 5 computer license for the combined time of Home (1 year) plus the remaining Personal time (x months).

Actually, the relevant question is do you really need to buy more licenses? 

So, the question is, how many computers on this license are you going to have logged in to Office 365 at the same time?  If you do not plan to have more than 2 machines working at the same time you can actually take advantage of 365 and play "musical licenses".  You can use the 365 MyAccount site to simply deactivate license from unused machine and activate it on current machine.  You don't have to do a full uninstall, just deactivate the unused one. That will leave it installed, but in "limited function mode", basically read only.  So you can later move the activation back without having to go through the download/install process.

Another piece of info for you. If you read the 365 University license terms, you'll see that it is for while you are a student. Apparently MS checks you student status once a year. It is not totally clear if they check at the start of the school year or on the anniversary of your subscription. Nor is it clear how the check your status. Maybe they send subscription info to the school for confirmation or they get enrollment (email) lists from the school. But in theory, if you are no longer listed as a student, they can cancel your 4yr subscription at that point.  I have not yet had confirmation of that happening. But it may happen without people realizing the root cause of them losing the subscription.
