Query/subquery problem - Microsoft Community

g_a_reaves say:

Query/subquery problem

I have a table with two columns of which one contains sums of money and the other the expense head to which the corresponding sum has been allocated. What I want to do is to calculate the total for each expense head and what proportion of the overall total each expense head total represents. I can create easily queries which calculate the overall total or the expense head totals separately but not a query which performs both functions. Is it possible?
ken sheridan say: Try this:

SELECT ExpenseHead, SUM(Amount) As TotalExpenseHeadAmount,
    (SELECT SUM(Amount)
     FROM Budget) AS OverallTotal,
FORMAT(SUM(Amount)/
    (SELECT SUM(Amount)
     FROM Budget),"Percent") AS PercentageOfOverallTotal
FROM Budget
GROUP BY ExpenseHead;

where Budget is the name of the table, and ExpenseHead and Amount are the names of the columns.  Remember that if you've unadvisedly used spaces or other special characters in object names, they must be wrapped in square brackets in the query, e.g. [Expense Head].

To move the selection to a new line, use SHIFT+ENTER.  If you are using ENTER by itself, that starts a new paragraph and, if there is room for a new paragraph on the page, look at the paragraph formatting as it may be set to have a Page Break before it.
