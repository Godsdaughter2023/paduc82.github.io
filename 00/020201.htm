Any help would be warmly appreciated.

jay freedman say:

The equation editor does not have any way to set different font sizes within the same equation.

The difference you're seeing is due to the behavior of "inline" versus "display" equations. A display equation appears when it's the only thing in its paragraph; if there is even one character in the same paragraph but outside the equation container, the equation switches to inline. You can also use the dropdown menu from the triangular arrow on the right end of the equation container to switch the display.

Fractions in inline equations always have a reduced font size, and upper and lower bounds on large operators (such as summation and product) appear to the right of the operator instead of above and below. The objective is to minimize the amount of extra space between lines of text around the equation.
