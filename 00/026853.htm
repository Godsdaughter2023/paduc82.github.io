A yes/no field is in fact stored as -1 for Yes, 0 for No. A checkbox is not data; it's a data display tool. 

You should not be interacting directly with Tables; tables are for data storage, not fore editing or input. You can use your table (or a query based on the table) as the Recordsource for a Form, and bind a checkbox control to that field.

macropod say:

But the example you gave only has two labels across! And it's the 'across' measurement you asked about. No matter how many labels you print, therefore, the most you'd ever be out for the second label is -0.0025" for the left margin and +0.0050" for the right margin...

If anything matters for perforated sheets, it would be the top margin, label height and vertical pitch. Although your screenshots depict 1.06" for the top margin and 0.88" for the label height and vertical pitch, I suspect the top margin is actually 1/16" = 1.0625" and the label height and vertical pitch are 7/8" = 0.875". The displayed values are due to rounding. Of course, if you overtype any of the displayed values, you'll get whatever you typed.

Finally, when using perforated stock, there should be sheet-feed commands issued to force the paper to feed through to the top of the next sheet. This isn't affected by the nominal label positions so, for your 10-row stock, you should never be out vertically more than 0.0425" for the top of any label.

huni shen msft say:

Hi ColinTalbot,

 

To automatically download pictures from the Internet in Outlook for Mac, you can follow the steps below:

  1. On the Outlook menu, click Preferences.
  2. Under Email, click Reading.
  3. Under Security, select the option to specify when you want to automatically download pictures from the Internet: either in all messages, or only in messages from your contacts.
