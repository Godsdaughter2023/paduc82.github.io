brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

Outlook will attempt to imitate the sender when it replies.  If the sender sends you a digitally-signed message, for any replies you send, your Outlook will also try to sign them unless you specifically disable the option.  If you don't have a digital signature, the message you see will show.  Here's my hypothesis: I suspect your sender has enabled the option to digitally sign every message, which signs the message invitation.  Meeting invitation responses are replies, so your Outlook tries to sign the response, but you don't have a certificate, so Outlook can't sign it and complains.
