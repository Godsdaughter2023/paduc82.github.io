shenan stanley say:

Not sure I understand your question.

Windows 10 was released to the public on July 29, 2015.

For its initial release, there is an offer where existing users of Windows 7 w/SP1 installed and Windows 8.1 with Update 1 installed can get a FREE UPGRADE license for Windows 10 if they UPGRADE their existing system(s) to Windows 10 by July 29, 2016 (one year from the 'released to the public' date.)

There was some debate / confusion surrounding the upgrade offer and whether or not the license was only good between the dates of July 29, 2015 and July 29, 2016 and then there would be some subscription charge or cost associated with it even for those who upgraded using the offer.  The fact is that if one takes advantage of the upgrade offer within the given period, they now own that UPGRADE license of Windows 10 for that specific system for the life of that specific system hardware (as most people who would take advantage of this are running OEM software and not RETAIL.)  There is / will be no additional charge even after the July 29, 2016 date when the offer expires.

Anyone who has a qualifying system (for which the system is actually supported by the manufacturer or component manufacturer(s)) can take advantage of the upgrade offer ANYTIME between July 29, 2015 and July 29, 2016.  Those are the dates of the 'free upgrade' offer.

So to this question: "Did windows 10 reservation depend on date?  Like, after its released, you can' t get windows 10."  

You never needed to make a reservation to take advantage of the ongoing (through July 29, 2016) free upgrade offer.  So, no, there was no dependence on date in terms of that.  The only dependence on date here is *if* you want to take advantage of the FREE upgrade offer, you have only until July 29, 2016 to do so.  If you do not do so by then, upgrading an existing system will cost money.  If you upgrade a system by then, you always have that Windows 10 license for that system at no extra cost.
