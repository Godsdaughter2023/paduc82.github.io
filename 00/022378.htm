Themes may be answer to the Word 2010 Font issue - Microsoft Community

electroscribe_2014 say:

Themes may be answer to the Word 2010 Font issue

My Windows Live Mail showed new responses to an old issue -- Fonts showing up as Arial in Word 2010.

 

So many people had the issue that the moderator had to lock the thread after 16 pages.

 

I had the issue with Aopen Type and Postscript fonts that I need to keep in some of my Word documents.

 

I solved it, finally, by setting up the fonts I use in a special theme.

  1. Click the Page Layout tab
  2. At the far left side of the ribbon, you'll see a Themes area
  3. Click the down arrow for Fonts (in Themes)
  4. At the bottom of the list, click Create New Theme Fonts...
  5. Select the Heading font you wish to use
  6. Select the Body Font you wish to use
  7. Save your custom theme

You can apply the theme next time you want to use those fonts in a Word document (.dotx). It won't work in a Word 2003 (.doc) file.

If you want every document you open in Word to use the theme, locate your Normal.DOTM template in Windows Explorer.

It should be in C:\Users\[You, the home user]\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Templates

Right click the Normal.dotm file icon (DON'T double-click it or a blank document will open_

  1. Select OPEN, to open the actual template and not create a new document
  2. Click the Page Layout tab
  3. Find Fonts in the theme area
  4. Select the new set of default fonts
  5. Close Normal.dotm

That shoulld set the default theme.

julie fka profjulie say:

Okay, I've spent a couple of hours playing around with the Groove app and this is what I've learned.

The Groove app is primarily useful for streaming music to a phone:

  • If you have a Groove Music Pass, you can stream music from Microsoft's online music collection.  You can also download some music from Microsoft's online music collection to play offline on the phone. 
  • If you do NOT subscribe to a Groove Music Pass, you can upload your music collection to the Music folder of your OneDrive account and then use the app to stream music to your phone from your OneDrive account. You can also download music from your OneDrive account to play offline on the phone.
  • Any music that you copy from a computer to a phone via USB cord will not be recognized in the Groove app, so the only way to transfer music to a phone so the Groove app will recognize it and play it offline is by downloading the music either from Microsoft's online music collection (only if you subscribe to a Groove Music Pass) or from your OneDrive account.

If you don't want to upload your personal music collection to OneDrive and then download it to your phone, you can copy your music to the phone via USB connection, but you need to use a different music app to listen to your music on the phone.
