You have a "WISP" or Wireless Internet Service Provider, which simply means that your connection is via radio transmission from a nearby tower rather than via a telephone line (dial-up or DSL) or coaxial/fiber optic cable (like cable TV).  You have an antenna of some sort on the outside of your house and some associated electronics (which presumably is powered by that switching mode power supply). 

You likely use this type of Internet service because you're too far away from your local telephone "central office" to use DSL and you don't have cable television service in your area.

Unfortunately, I'm not familiar with how Zig configures its connections, and most of the Zig Wireless support material is not available unless you're a subscriber:  http://helpdesk.zigwireless.com/cgi-bin/pdesk.cgi

I started composing directions to help you configure your new router, but it got very long and complicated -- as well as requiring some guessing on my part.  I suggest that you get in touch with ZigWireless support and ask them how to set up your router.  There are two potential pitfalls:

  1. Zig recommends a different router (see http://www.zigwireless.com/blog/?p=304), so they may say that they can only provide configuration help if you -- again -- return what you have and buy their recommended unit;
  2. If Zig is willing to help, as near as I can understand the User Guide for your EA3500, the "simple directions" for setting up the router assume that the computer that you're using has a wireless network adapter.  Although this is not a completely baseless assumption -- you did buy a wireless router, after all -- most desktops like your Dell Inspiron do not have a wireless adapter.  I assume that you bought the wireless router to use with other devices, such as laptops and cell phones, which do have wireless networking capability.  Assuming that your Dell Inspiron does NOT have a wireless adapter, you will have to follow the section in the router User Guide under "How to manually set up your router" starting at page 32 (pdf page 35) of the User Guide.

If ZigWireless can't/won't help without coming to your home (which I assume will cost $$), ask them the following question and then come back here and I'll try to walk you through the steps.

What type of Internet connection does Zig use:  PPPoE (which requires a username and password) or DHCP (which doesn't).

And, of course, if your connection requires a username and password, make sure you know what these are (but don't post them here).  FYI, older Linksys router manuals describe the connection types as:

Automatic Configuration DHCP

Static IP

PPPoE (Point-to-Point Protocol over Ethernet)

PPTP (Point-to-Point Tunneling Protocol)

L2TP (Layer 2 Tunneling Protocol)

Telstra Cable

The most common types in the US are the first (most cable Internet connections) and third (most DSL Internet connections);  Telstra is Australian.  I don't know if Zig is more like cable or more like DSL.
