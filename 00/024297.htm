Hello Xuanlong,

 

Welcome to the Microsoft Community!

 

From what I understand you would like to chat to your Google contacts using your Outlook.com account. If so, please be informed that you can do this by connecting your Gmail account to your Outlook.com account. Here are the steps on how to do this:

  1. Sign in to your account in the People page.
  2. Under Add people to your contact list, click Google contacts.
  3. In the pop up window that will appear, click Connect.
  4. Enter your Gmail account and your password then click Sign in.
  5. Click Allow access and click Done for the changes to be applied in your account.

After following the steps I have given, you should now be able to chat to your Google contacts using your Outlook.com account.

a. user say:

Unless something is very broken with his account, starting a subscription by redeeming a code will work.  You can redeem a code on any account, regardless of age.  I can also guarantee you'd be able to purchase a subscription using a credit card, through the website, but you did mention you don't want your card on his account so the prepaid code would definitely be the better option.

Without a card on the account, you can purchase music on a Windows Phone using carrier billing, which can also be used to purchase apps.  That works for any account over 13.

As far as I'm aware, if you were to remove him from Family Safety and he were to try and purchase something, it would prompt to have an adult account add him to Family Safety.  While possible to have a child account with no parent account, that child account wouldn't be able to do much.
