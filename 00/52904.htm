I post here the solution that I found for other people who have this problem.

I again state the problem for me:

https://github.com/sumatrapdfreader/sumatrapdf/issues/1089#issuecomment-436051118

The solution is a little tricky so I try to describe as good as possible.

First, I tried one thing that I hadnt tried, which unfortunately did not solve it.

But maybe it will for you!

Solution 1:

1. Go to a djvu (same applies for cbr) file in File Explorer.

2. Right click the djvu file and select Properties.

4. Next to Opens with:, click on Change.

5. In the next Window that pops up, DO NOT select SumatraPDF. Just click on More apps.

6. Scroll down and select: Look for another app on this PC

7. Navigate to SumatraPDF folder and select: SumatraPDF.exe

8. Thats it! Maybe it can take some time to process this request, as it did for me.

PS: The above did not solve the problem for me. See if it does for you!

Now we proceed to my solution which is a little tricky.

MANY THANKS to the persons from SumatraPDF bug websites who helped me succeed!

Solution 2:

(Remember that we have successfully associates the pdf files with SumatraPDF. If not, then you have to find solution for that somewhere else. Here we will associate SumatraPDF with djvu and cbr files.)

1. Download this piece of software: https://www.nirsoft.net/utils/file_types_manager.html

2. Unzip the zip file and run the executable, preferably as administrator.

3. Wait for all associations to load.

4. Scroll down until you find .pdf association. Click on that line.

5. Now, in the below pane, you will see the actions. I have 3 actions: "open", "print", "printto".

6. Double-click on the "open" action.

7. A windows pops-up. See the Command-Line text:

"C:\Program Files\SumatraPDF\SumatraPDF.exe" "%1" %*

8. Select it, and Copy it. Close the window.

9. Now, back in the associations, scroll up until you find the orphan .cbr association (same procedure for .djvu). Click on that line.

10. In the below pane, you will notice that there are not any actions.

11. Right click anywhere on the below pane. Choose: New Action

12. In the window that pops ups enter the following:

Next to Menu Caption: open

Next to Command-Line: Paste the text that you copied earlier or take it from here:

"C:\Program Files\SumatraPDF\SumatraPDF.exe" "%1" %*

Also tick on: Default Action

13. Now the association should be in place and you can just double-click on cbr files which will open in SumatraPDF:

14. Next we are going to insert the proper SumatraPDF icon for cbr files.

15. Again on the .cbr line, double-click on it.

16. Next to Default Icon insert:

C:\Program Files\SumatraPDF\SumatraPDF.exe,1

(This line, I just copied again from the .pdf file association in case you are wondering.)

Click on OK and close the application.

Thats it! Now you should be OK.

PS: The pdf assocation had also 2 other actions: "print", "printto".

I did not enter those as well for cbr and djvu files, because I do not need them for now. I guess I can print from within SumatraPDF?

If you miss those actions, I guess you can again do the same procedure from pdf to djvu and cbr associations.

By entering those actions in cbr and djvu associations, exactly as they are for the pdf.

I have not entered them, so I cannot guarantee the success of those.

If you do it, you could post here a reply with your success of failure.

I do not know what those print actions do.

Maybe, when right click a cbr or djvu file, you have a print choice?

I do not know.

Good luck!
