Your computer/device is now Trusted. Whenever you use your account on that specific computer/device, you will no longer asked for a code.

wilson lee20122 say:

multi-list and caption in word 2013

1、how to create multi-list in word 2013 like below：

 1.1 xxxx

2、how to create caption like this：

Chapter One、xxxxx

       xxxx（equation）                       eq1-1

       xxxx（equation）                       eq1-2

Chapter Two、xxxx

       xxxx（equation）                       eq2-1

stefan blom say:
Set up a multilevel list of headings as explained in the article at http://shaunakelly.com/word/numbering/numbering20072010.html . Then create a caption label which includes Heading 1 numbers (References | Caption).

The above is the easy part. Unfortunately, Word doesn't easily let you add a caption and an equation side by side. What you can do is create the caption in a separate paragraph (use a tab stop for the position of the caption number), merge the two paragraphs, add an example equation and save the whole thing as an AutoText entry (Building Block).

suzanne s. barnhill say:

In the Caption dialog, click Numbering... In the Caption Numbering dialog, check the box for "Include chapter number." Heading 1 (which is the heading level you want to use) is selected by default. Even though your chapter numbers use One, Two, Three numbering, the caption will use 1, 2, 3.

If you really want to use "eq" as a caption label, you'll need to create a new label, as the default built-in one is "Equation." You won't be able to omit the space between label and number automatically; you will have to remove it after inserting the caption.

If you plan to cross-reference the captions, you can't put them on the same line (in the same paragraph) with the equation (if you do, you'll get the entire paragraph in a cross-reference). Instead, put the equation and caption in separate cells of a two- or three-column single-row borderless table. If you use three cells, you use the center column for the equation and the right column for the caption. You make the right cell just large enough for the caption and make the left cell the same size, then center the equation in the center cell. You won't be able to insert the caption directly in the table; you have to create it outside the table and drag it in. You can then create a dummy equation and save the entire table row as an AutoText entry or Quick Part for ease of insertion.
