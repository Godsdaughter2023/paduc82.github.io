Number the tables using the Caption function as you are already doing. The (i) and (ii) will have to be added manually. But you don't have to use a cross-reference for the second Table 4.9; you can edit the field code.

Table { SEQ Table \* ARABIC }

grschinon say: Operators (virtual or otherwise) enforce 2 kinds of lock on a phone. Firstly there's the SIM-lock, which when lifted enabled you to use a Virgin SIM in the phone that was originally obtained through Tesco. Secondly, there's the branding.

Tesco's branding of your phone is what's responsible for the logo appearing above a soft key on your screen and there is no (legitimate) way to remove it without Tesco's collaboration.

1) You need to ensure that the SIM-lock has been lifted (if you're using a Virgin SIM then it has)

2) You need written permission from Tesco to remove their branded firmware and install generic, Nokia firmware

3) You need to take the unlocked phone and the written permission to a Nokia Care Point where they will change the phone's product code, install generic software into the phone and print out a new product identification label for your battery compartment.

1) and 3) are easy. 2) is "Mission: Impossible".

Note that any "unofficial" methods you may hear about or see on the 'Net are methods that will certainly void any warranty on the phone, and may even preclude your phone from any form of service, under warranty or not, if you go ahead with them. As such they cannot be discussed here.
