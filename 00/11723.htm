We cannot tell you what you are "supposed to do".  That depends on the assignment and what you are currently studying.

If you use the FREQUENCY function directly, you have complete control over the number and limits of the bins.

We cannot say why whatever you are doing "has not been working" unless you provide a concrete example -- and what you mean by "not working".  Obviously, do not post 2000 data values. But at least show us the actual frequency table, complete with cell names, formulas and values.  (Even better:  upload an example Excel file.  See below.)

Abstract descriptions are usually too ambiguous.  For example, you say the "bins (intervals)" are "0 - 0.99, 0 - 1, 1 - 2, 1, 4 and so on".  That is not an accurate description of the bin table that you can use with the FREQUENCY function.  I don't know what you mean.

It is very possible that the data is clustered so much that you cannot "see" the distribution clearly due to the bin limits that you choose.

Without any presumptions or foreknowledge of the distribution of the data, I would "bootstrap" the required bin limits.

Of course, the first decision to make is:  how many bins do you want?  There is no "right" answer.  But 15 or 31 is a good starting point, if the exercise itself does not suggest something different.  (For example, "What is the distribution of babies born in each of the years 1960 through 2000?").

The next decision to make is:  what kind of distribution do you expect?  Again, barring preconceptions, I would start with a uniform distribution that covers the full range of data.

The FREQUENCY function uses the bin limits as the upper bound of each bin.  So with data in A1:A2000, I might set up my frequency table as follows.

Some notes:

1. I round up the bin limits.  That minimizes the effect of internal binary arithmetic anomalies.  You might choose to round up to integers or fewer decimal places.  Again, there is no "right" answer.

2. The FREQUENCY formula is array-entered (press ctrl+shift+Enter instead of just Enter) after selecting D8:D22.  Do not type the curly braces. Excel will display those to indicate that the formula was array-entered.

3. The bin range parameter for the FREQUENCY function is one less (C21) than the complete range (C22).  That ensures that the last bin counts all remaining data; again, it minimizes the effect of internal binary anomalies.

4. The interpretation of the bin limits is, for example: count all data less than or equal to 3.682.  Consequently, the first bin limit is the min data

5. Note how and when I use absolute references.

6. IMHO, it is "good practice" to sum the results of the FREQUENCY function, as I did in D23.  That ensures that all data has indeed been accounted for.

In my example, the data is uniformly distributed randomly.

But your actual data distribution might demonstrate clustering.  That might suggest that you should create additional bins or change the bin interval to improve your view of the distribution.

The bins do not need to be uniformly distributed; and they do not need to be rounded to the same precision.  But that is usually "good practice", even if there might bins with zero data.

(In contrast, if the data is exponentially distributed, for example, it might be reasonable to have varying bin intervals.)

Hope this helps.  If you have follow-up questions, I suggest that you present your frequency table and related formulas in a manner similar to my presentation above.

Each email client renders HTML coding in a different way. Even different versions of Outlook render code differently. For security reasons many users set their email clients for text only and block HTML and pictures.  IMHO the only way to get consistent results is to convert your newsletter to a PDF and attach it.  If you write a simple but effective introduction in the body of your email message and your message is of interest and wanted by the recipient they will open the PDF. If it is spam, then it won't matter whether it is in the message body or a PDF...it will be deleted.

If you must send HTML formatted messages then consider using specialty software such as:

www.constantcontact.com or www.group-mail.com and a free version: http://www.group-mail.com/asp/common/downloads.asp or another popular one is http://mailchimp.com/ - some free services - Store up to 2,000 subscribers & send up to 12,000 emails per month. 

These services will limit the layout and formatting of your newsletter but they do test their HTML coding in all email clients so you will get more consistent results than if you use Publisher. They also will offer text only alternatives for email clients set to text only.
