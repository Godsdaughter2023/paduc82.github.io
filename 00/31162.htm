Sorry, I've just re-read your original question and see that you have uninstalled (not just disabled, as you wrote) Windows Live Messenger. That being the case, you cannot be being prompted to sign in to WLMessenger when you launch WLMail.

You may be being prompted to sign in, but that has nothing to do with WLMessenger. Signing in in WLMail simply dictates which contacts list and which calendar to load, and which SkyDrive account to use for storing photo email. It has nothing to do with WLMessenger (although you would be signed in to WLMessenger if you had it installed) and nothing to do with sending or receiving mail.

a. user say:

AaronDN wrote:
"I know it is not a problem with my profile, system or sound settings, or microphone array.  It is not a problem with Speech Recognition recognizing my voice.  Commands all work, but when I try to dictate its only response is "What was that?"."

Actually it most likely your microphone. The best microphones for speech recognition are close talk headset or handheld microphones used 1/2 to 1 inch from one's mouth. In addition your notebook computer's sound card may be unshielded and picking up electronic noise from within the computer enclosure.

Commands are easily recognized because they are part of a small constrained vocabulary. Dictation uses a large vocabulary and must parse through tens of thousands of words and compare each word to the ones before and after for context clues.
