a. user say:

It's really no mystery: A computer runs slowly when it has been given too much work for the resources it has available. You can solve that either by giving the computer less work to do or by adding to its available resources. Since the latter means spending money on more hardware, it seems logical (to me, anyway) to first try to reduce the amount of work the computer is trying to do.

There are several ways to do this, but let's start with the low hanging fruit:

Open Task Manager [CTRL+SHIFT+ESC] to the Startup tab. What you're looking at are applications that start with Windows and run in the background, using your computer's resources. Do you really need all that stuff? If not, open the application(s) in question and go through the menus until you find the setting that starts the program with Windows; then turn that setting off. Once you're all done, re-start your computer.

A surefire way to harm your computer's performance, instead of help,  is to fiddle with its settings in ways you don't fully understand, or to rip apart your operating system following misguided information you find on the internet. I got suckered into doing that many times. I only hurt myself in the end.

You haven't told us what kind of hardware you have, only that it's three years old. (I can mess up a computer in three minutes, trust me.) For the 32-bit version of Windows 10 you'll need 4 GB of RAM and at least a 128 GB hard drive. For the 64-bit version you'll want at least 8 GB of RAM.

Your internet speed comes from your internet service provider. Windows can't change that.
