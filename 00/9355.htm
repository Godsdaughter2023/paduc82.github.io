petrib say: In case your phone is stolen, you have no way of deleting anything from it, I'd say (because you no longer have it).

Same goes, of course, for a memory card that gets stolen (it is too late to delete anything off it then, either).


So...

1: Change the PIN and PIN2 codes of your SIM card to something else than the operator defaults

2: Change the phone lock/security code to something else than the manufacturer default

3: Enable the setting to require the entering of a PIN code when the phone is turned on

4: Enable the setting to require the entering of the lock/security code when a SIM card is changed

5: Enable the setting to require the entering of the lock/security code to open the keypad lock

6: Set a password on your memory card (to prevent it from being usable on other devices)

7: Record your phone's serial number (IMEI) and your SIM card's serial number (IMSI) so that you can report them to the operator or police, if your phone is lost/stolen.

8: Consider installing/using add-on security applications to encrypt/password protect sensitive information (messages, files, pictures, etc.)

9: Back up or copy your data off the phone to a secure place (like your own PC, which you also have to back up, of course)

10: Attach your phone with a chain or wire or something to your belt, bag or such, so that it is not so easy to accidentally loose it, or for somebody to take it.

If doing all that is not enough, then do not store any sensitive information on your phone at all.
