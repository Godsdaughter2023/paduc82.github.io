jeeped say:
This probably has to do with the internet provider that you receive internet service from at your home. Many ISPs require that all outgoing mail from their residential service be directed through their own SMTP servers. This allows them to efficiently block massive amounts of outgoing spam mail from a zombie home computer on their network. Unfortunately, when you are off their network you can run into this Cannot relay error.

The specifics of a solution will depend largely upon your home ISP and what email address you are referring to. Essentially you will need to either change the Outgoing mail server (SMTP): setting for that account whenever you are away from home or create a copy of the account with the different SMTP server that you use as the default when you are off your home network.

If you provide the domain of your email (the part after the @) and an idea of who your home ISP is, more assistance may be able to be offered. A solution specific to your situation may also be available from your home ISP's support area.
