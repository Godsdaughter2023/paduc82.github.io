Hi,

Thank you for posting your query in Microsoft Community.

Pop-up Blocker limits or blocks pop-ups on sites that you visit. You can choose the level of blocking you prefer, turn on or off notifications when pop-ups are blocked, or create a list of sites that you don't want to block pop-ups on. Pop-up Blocker settings only apply to Internet Explorer for the desktop.

To turn pop-up blocker on or off

a. Open the desktop, and then tap or click the Internet Explorer icon on the taskbar.

b. Tap or click the Tools button , and then tap or click Internet options.

c. On the Privacy tab, under Pop-up Blocker, select or clear the Turn on Pop-up Blocker check box, and then tap or click OK.

To block all pop-ups

Even if you turn pop-up blocker on, Internet Explorer won't block pop-ups from sites that are in the Local intranet or Trusted sites security zones. If you want to block all pop-ups—including pop-ups on Local intranet or Trusted sites—you'll have to change the blocking level.

a. Open the desktop, and then tap or click the Internet Explorer icon on the taskbar.

b. Tap or click the Tools button , and then tap or click Internet options.

c. On the Privacy tab, under Pop-up Blocker, tap or click Settings.

d. In the Pop-up Blocker settings dialog box, under Blocking level, set the blocking level to High: Block all pop-ups (Ctrl+Alt to override).

d. Tap or click Close, and then tap or click OK.

To turn off notifications when poop-ups are blocked

You can turn off notifications if you don't want Internet Explorer to let you know every time it blocks a pop-up.

a. Open the desktop, and then tap or click the Internet Explorer icon on the taskbar.

b. Tap or click the Tools button , and then tap or click Internet options.

c. On the Privacy tab, under Pop-up Blocker, tap or click Settings.

d. In the Pop-up Blocker settings dialog box, clear the Show Notification bar when a pop-up is blocked check box.

e. Tap or click Close, and then tap or click OK.

Hope this information is helpful. Please feel free to reply in case you face any other issues with Windows in future.
