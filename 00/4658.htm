A word about rounding....

Previously, I wrote:  "Generally, it would be prudent to round the payment formula to avoid computer arithmetic artifacts [...].  But with these particular numbers, it does not make any difference by coincidence".

I was referring to the expression 1802.59+500.  However, in my table below, I compute 1802.59 using PMT(3.625%/12,180,-250000), then add 500.  In that case, rounding PMT does make a difference -- a cumulative difference of about 0.30 in the outstanding balance after the last payment.

So I return to the prudent practice of always rounding the payment.

Note, however, that functions like PPMT and CUMPRINC almost certainly do not round payment, which is computed internally.  After all, they have no idea what precision to use, which depends on the monetary system.  Arguably, they could default to the Regional and Language setting.  But that would be presumptuous.

Anyway, that is yet another source of "error" in those functions, where by "error" I mean departure from reality.  That is another reason why I never use those functions.

However, I do not round periodic interest rate, interest and principal amounts, and the outstanding balance.

To a large degree, that is for consistency with the mathematical financial formulas, which are used in the Excel PV, FV, PMT, NPER and RATE functions.  (Although they do permit us to round the periodic interest rate, an input parameter.)  But also, I do not round those formulas because the decision whether or not to round and to what precision is an arbitrary one based on lender policy.

If you choose to round periodic interest rate, interest, principal and/or outstanding balance, it will make a difference compared to financial functions in Excel.  For example, if the latter three are rounded to 2 decimal places, the outstanding balance after 131 payments will be 1779.82, whereas FV(3.625%/12,131,1802.59+500,-250000) is about 1779.78.

There is no way to incorporate arbitrary rounding decisions into the financial equations and their usage in Excel financial functions, except for the regular payment and periodic interest rate, which are function parameters.
