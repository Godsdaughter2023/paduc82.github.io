considerate_guy say:

I believe you are talking about the Video Tools | Edit tab.  Most of those controls apply to video.  When you are editing photos (a static image), the only relevant settings are things like Duration.  The volume / fade-in / fade-out don't apply because pictures don't have audio.  Similarly, all the choices for splitting, trimming, speed are only applicable to video and are therefore grayed out.

 

Think of it this way, if you want to double the speed of a 7 second photo, it will now display for half the time (i.e. 3.5 seconds).  So you can get the same result by halving the duration of your photos.  A 3.5 second photo appears for half the time of a 7 second photo and hence is twice as fast.  In mathematical terms Speed and Duration are inversely proportional.  Another way to adjust the speed of your slideshow is to use the Project tab and choose Fit to music.  That will automatically adjust the duration of all your photos to end when the song(s) end.

 

P.S.  You can set any duration setting you like by typing into the Duration field and then pressing Enter.

 

P.P.S. If you are looking to fade a clip in or out (whether it be a photo or a video), you should be using the Visual Effects tab.  You can choose Fade to/from black or white.  For adding transitions between photos, use the Animations tab.
