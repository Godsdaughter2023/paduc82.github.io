To prevent this problem, correct the FTP proxy settings. To do this, follow these steps:

1.    Click  Start, point to Settings, and then click  Control Panel.

2.    Double-click  Internet Options.

3.    Click the Connections tab.

4.    Click LAN Settings.

5.    Click  Advanced.

6.    Under Servers, click to clear the  Use the same proxy server for all protocols check box.

7.    In the  FTP box, type the Uniform Resource Locator (URL) for your FTP gateway server. The following example illustrates the syntax to use: ftp://ftpgate

8.    Click to clear the Port check box to the right of the  FTP box.

9.    Click OK, click  OK again, and then close the Local Area Network (LAN) Settings dialog box.

10. Click  OK to close the  Internet Properties dialog box.

You can now connect to an FTP site in the Open dialog box of the programs listed in the "Applies to" section.

If you still cannot connect to an FTP site, ask your network administrator whether you are referring to the correct FTP gateway server.

Change font color of italicized words - Microsoft Community

misnole say:

Change font color of italicized words

I've got a Word document (Office 2013) with tons of italicized words in the document.  What I'd like to do is change the font color of all italicized words in the document to red from black.  Is there a way to do this without manually making each change?

Thanks,
jay freedman say:
You can do that with the Replace dialog.
  • Press Ctrl+H, or click Replace at the right end of the Home tab.
  • While the cursor is in the Find What box, press Ctrl+I. You should see the line "Format: Font: Italic" appear below the box.
  • Click in the Replace With box.
  • Click the More button.
  • At the bottom of the expanded dialog, click the Format button and click Font in the menu.
  • In the Font dialog, click the color dropdown and choose the red square, then click OK.
  • In the Replace dialog, click the Replace All button.

Further to Jim's reply, unless you do as he suggested the arguments do not appear until you've typed the complete name of the function followed by a left paren. IOW, typing =index( should trigger what you're looking for.

However, it should be stressed that even following Jim's instruction the function name inserted will be the one highlighted in the AutoComplete List if more than one is displayed. For example, there are several functions that begin with ACO. If you've typed only those 3 characters all of the functions beginning with that string will be listed with the highlight on the first. Pressing Tab will insert that function which may not be the one you want. Use up/down arrow to navigate in the AutoComplete List as necessary before pressing Tab.
