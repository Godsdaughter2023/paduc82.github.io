jay freedman say:
First, the Charformat switch should start with a backslash instead of a forward slash, \* Charformat. On my copy of Word 2007, though, having the wrong slash just makes the switch not work but doesn't give an error.

Anyway, this does work:
  • Add the \* Charformat switch to each cross-reference's field code, like { REF _Ref367888379 \h \* Charformat }.
  • Press Alt+F9 to display all field codes in the document.
  • Make sure the Highlight button on the Home tab is set to the color you want for highlighting the cross-references.
  • Open the Replace dialog, put ^19 REF in the Find What box, and put ^& in the Replace With box. While the cursor is still in the Replace With box, click the More button, click the Format button at the bottom, and click Highlight in the menu.

  • Click the Replace All button and close the dialog. All the cross-reference fields will be highlighted.
  • Press Alt+F9 again to collapse the field codes. The results will be highlighted (because of the \* Charformat switches).


By the way, Word never had a way to shade just the cross-reference fields, but it had and still has an option to shade all fields. It's on the Advanced page of the Word Options dialog, in the "Show document content" section; the dropdown labeled "Field shading" can be set to Never, Always, or When Selected.
