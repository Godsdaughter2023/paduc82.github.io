j.e. mcgimpsey say: I'm not confident I understand your data setup, but making this assumption:

         A                     B
1    Date                Data
2    01/01/14       1234
3    01/02/14       1328
...
33  02/02/14      1476
34  02/03/14      1488

You could use something like:

   =VLOOKUP(TODAY(), A2:B1000, 2, FALSE) - VLOOKUP(TODAY() - 1, A2:B1000, 2, FALSE)

This works because dates in XL are stored as integer offsets from a base date (i.e., 01/01/1904 or 12/31/1899, depending on the system used). Therefore yesterday is an integer one less than today's integer.

Doug's given you the "how to" (which you can obviously wrap up in a macro if you want), but what happens AFAIK in all versions of Windows Word from around Word 97 is that if your FILLIN field is in a /template/, and you create a new document based on that template, Word will execute the FILLIN. Word doesn't do that if you merely open a .doc/.docx etc. with a FILLIN, and it doesn't do the same with ASK fields. 

So if, for example, you had a template that had an AutoNew macro to execute your fields in a new document, FILLIN fields would be executed twice, once before the AutoNew macro executes, and once in the macro. I would guess that's what you remember, but I don't think Word ever executed the fields twice entirely on its own.

 

Peter Jamieson
