Windows Live Photo Gallery (from live essentials 2011, anyway) does indeed support geotags. After some usage, it seems to me that it takes some time for the geotags to be populated--this is more than likely done by Windows Live Photo Gallery via web calls after the folders containing your gps-populated photos are added to the library. So, even though your camera is adding latitude and longitude data to your photos, they won't appear as a coherent place with a name in the Geotag field under Windows Live Photo Gallery has had time to scan through the whole list and figure out where all those points actually are.

 

Furthermore, if you want to map the photos, you can right click the Geotag value (once it has resolved to a place) and there will be an option for "Map it" which takes you to Bing Maps at that coordinate. I believe this was even the case two months ago when Litton_B made his original reply.

 

Lastly, as of recently, SkyDrive also supports geotagging as well in the same sidebar manner that you describe with flickr. Simply download the SkyDrive app at http://skydrive.live.com, and upload your photos there. When you view them on the web, you'll be presented with a thumbnail map in the sidebar for each photo populated with gps data.

 

Hope this helps!

geoffc1 say:

It's true that Photo Gallery will do reverse geocoding (sending the GPS coordinate data to Bing to get back textual address strings, which are then displayed as "geotags" in Photo Gallery).

However, people should be aware that, unlike earlier versions of Windows Live Photo Gallery, these geotags are now only being stored in Photo Gallery's local database - they are not being stored as metadata in the photos themselves, except under very specific conditions.

For more info on what Photo Gallery does with geotags, see

http://gcoupe.wordpress.com/2012/08/20/windows-photo-gallery-geotags-and-other-issues/
