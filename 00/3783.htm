philiptrick say:

The time format is really just a fancy looking number.

So, for example, if you convert your 8 hours into a number format, it will show up as 0.33333 (because 8 hours represents 1/3rd of a day). Then, 1/3 * 5 = 1.67 instead of the 40 you're expecting.

The simplest excel cell solution is then to take any time format that you want to use as a number, multiply it by 24, and then by the number of days. So (A2-A1)*24 = 8 * 5 = 40. Changing A2 to 8:00 reduces the clock by 1 hour you'll change your result from 40 to 35 since you're working 7 hours per day instead of 8.

jeeped say:

Use Find and Replace in multiple steps to create the formatting you want.

  1. Tap CTRL+H to open the Find and Replace dialog. Find what: ^p^p Replace with: xyz123zyx
  2. Next step, Find what: ^p Replace with: <space> (just tap the spacebar once)
  3. Next Find what: xzy123zyx Replace with: ^p
  4. Last, Find what: <space><space> Replace with: <space>

That should clean your document's layout to an acceptable level.
