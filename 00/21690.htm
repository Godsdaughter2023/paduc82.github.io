I have no idea what "old app" you mean. You can use the Outlook for Android app or you can use Android's built-in Email app. Either will allow you to synchronize mail, calendar, and contacts between the Android device and your Outlook.com mailbox. Mobile devices use either the Exchange protocol or the Exchange ActiveSync protocol, which has the ability to synchronize mail, contacts, and calendar. A mail-only protocols like IMAP can handle mail in both directions, but doesn't know anything about calendar or contacts

The old Outlook.com servers understood a protocol named "DeltaSync", which Windows Live Mail also understood ad it was through that protocol WLM could sync mail, calendar, and contacts. Microsoft doesn't want to support two completely separate sets of mail servers, so they've been consolidating Outlook.com into their Office 365 system, and the Office 365 servers don't understand DeltaSync, so you'll need to switch mail clients if IMAP isn't good enough for you and you need calendar and contacts to syn as well. Outlook 2013 or Outlook 2016 understand Exchange and Exchange ActiveSync, so Exchange is your best bet to have a PC, your mobile device, and the Outlook.com mailbox to all remain in sync.
