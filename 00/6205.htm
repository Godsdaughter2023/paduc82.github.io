jay freedman say:
Hi Harry,

Word has this habit of hiding things to "make it easier" and winds up making everything harder.

The simplest way to get to modify the Envelope Address style is to use the Envelopes dialog to make up an envelope (even just a dummy one) and click the Add to Document button. Then click in the address in the envelope on the screen, press Ctrl+Shift+S, and follow the rest of Doug's instructions.

A longer way is to press Ctrl+Shift+Alt+S to open the Styles pane to the right of the page. Click the Options link at the bottom of the pane. In the Options dialog, change the "Select styles to show" dropdown to All Styles, and change the "Select how list is sorted" dropdown to Alphabetical. Then you can find the Envelope Address style in the list and modify it.

Having problems contacting @outlook.com emails? Read this. - Microsoft Community

a. user say:

Having problems contacting @outlook.com emails? Read this.

If you are having difficulty contacting users with @outlook.com emails from another @address.com, then this information applies to you.

If someone else is having problems contacting your @outlook.com address, then this information applies to them. replace all references to 'you' or 'your' with them.

Warning: this information is highly technical, and may require an expert to decipher.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

If the emails are being blocked (cannot be found in junk email), this section may not apply to you. It only applies if emails are being placed in your junk email folder on outlook.com and you cannot receive them in your email client/program.

If you are using a POP3 email client/program (ie: outlook for windows, some mobile apps, 3rd party programs, etc), your junk email will not be downloaded. This is because POP3 clients only download the user inbox. Furthermore, junk email on outlook.com will be deleted after 10 days. You must log in to the outlook.com site, select the email in your 'junk email' folder, and select 'not junk' in the options above. All further mails from that address should be delivered to your inbox.

The above statement does not apply to you if you are using the outlook.com mobile app, or the Windows Live Mail application, or any version of Outlook using the Outlook Hotmail Connector (except 2013), or by selecting the option 'Outlook.com or Exchange Activesync compatible service' instead of POP3/SMTP when setting up your email account in Outlook 2010 or 2013 on Windows. This is because those applications use a protocol called EAS or deltasync to synchronize all of your folders instead.

If you wish to receive junk email on your PC/mobile, you must use a client compatible with EAS (Exchange ActiveSync 14.0) or deltasync, which includes applications such as Outlook 2010/2013, Windows Live Mail, Outlook Hotmail Connector, the default Android email client and the outlook.com mobile application. The outlook.com EAS server is located at m.hotmail.com.

If you absolutely must use POP3 (not recommended), there are several considerations. First, you must set your email client not to delete email from the server. This can be found in your client's options. Each client is different and a description of each is beyond the scope of this article. Next, you should enable SSL (Secure Socket Layer) if your email service provider supports it (outlook.com uses SSL on port 995 for POP3 and TLS on port 587 for SMTP). Finally, you must create a rule in outlook.com as such: select the Gear icon - More mail options - Customizing Outlook - Rules for sorting new messages. Add a new rule. In step 1, enter 'Senders address', 'contains', '@' in the 3 fields, without quotes (''). In step 2, select 'Move to Inbox'. This will deliver all junk mail to your inbox instead, so you can obtain it on your POP3 client. Be advised that this can lead to an increase in malicious emails being delivered to your inbox, and you should have protection installed, as well as checking mails for suspicious activity thoroughly before opening (something you should be doing anyways).

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

If your emails are being blocked OR placed in the junk email folder by outlook.com, then this section applies to you.

Your chances of successful mail delivery depend on multiple factors, including but not limited to the existence of proper SPF records, 1024 bit rsa DKIM keys, SSL certificates, DNS blacklists/whitelists, server reputation, and the content of the message itself.

To determine your chances of not being blocked by outlook.com junk filters and policies, several tools may be required:

Senderscore IP Lookup: http://senderscore.org/lookup.php  - Enter your email server IP address. This can be obtained from your email hosting provider, or by performing an nslookup to your public webmail/smtp/www server in most cases. In order to get a detailed report, you must register for an account.

Senderscore FAQ: http://www.senderscore.org/faq/ - Information regarding senderscore reports.

DNSBL: http://www.dnsbl.info/ - Enter the IP address of your email server. If it appears red (blacklisted) on too many servers, you may have issues sending email from that server.

another DNSBL: http://mxtoolbox.com/blacklists.aspx - Enter the IP address of your mail server. Same as above. This one includes the option to email you your server's IP address.

DNSstuff Toolbox: http://www.dnsstuff.com/tools - under Domain/WWW Tools - DNSreport, enter your @emaildomain.com, watch for related MX, MAIL or SPF errors. - also under Email Tools - Spam Blacklist Lookup, enter the IP address of your mail server.

Deliverability Issue Submission Form: http://support.msn.com/eform.aspx?productKey=edfsmsbl2&ct=eformts - Allows users to attempt to have their provider's email server automatically removed from any block lists, if they have been placed there for incorrect reasons only.

Outlook.com currently uses a number of methods to determine sender 'reputation'.  Information can be found at the following sites. However, most of the information is highly cryptic, and doesn't serve much benefit to end users. See the following articles for more information:

Outlook.com Mail Troubleshooting: http://snt145.mail.live.com/mail/troubleshooting.aspx

Fighting Junk Email: http://snt145.mail.live.com/mail/junkemail.aspx

Policies, Practices and Guidelines: http://snt145.mail.live.com/mail/policies.aspx

Depending on the severity of the problem, you may need to switch servers, or even email providers completely, or wait approximately 1 month for the reputation to improve.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The following services are available to your email service provider/ISP only.

Smart Network Data Services: http://postmaster.live.com/snds/index.aspx  - Allows your provider to determine the 'reputation' as viewed by outlook.com for all of their email servers. In some cases, you may be able to obtain the report from your provider, if you ask accordingly.

Junk Mail Reporting Partner Program: http://support.msn.com/eform.aspx?productKey=edfsjmrpp&ct=eformts - Allows providers to report problems with delivery.

Services for Senders and ISPs: http://snt145.mail.live.com/mail/services.aspx - Information for volume senders and ISPs.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
