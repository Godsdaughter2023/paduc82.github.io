Different settings can be configured in Microsoft Word to change how data is interpreted by the program. You can also perform the steps below if there are specific words or names that you input after the word Judge:

  1. While Word is open, click on File.
  2. Go to Options.
  3. Choose Proofing.
  4. Click on AutoCorrect Options....
  5. Check the box for Replace text as you type.
  6. In the Replace box, key in Judge (lower case name), then in the box next to it where it says With, enter Judge (upper case name).
    • Example:
      • Replace box: Judge judy
      • With box: Judge Judy

  7. Click on Add then OK. Every time Judge judy is entered, Word will automatically replace it with Judge Judy.

  8. You can add the other words or names so Word applies the changes for other names.

Here's another method you can follow:

  1. Highlight the words that you want the first letters capitalized.
  2. Click on the Change Case button. (See image below to locate the button.)
  3. Choose Capitalize Each Word. This option will automatically capitalize the first letter of each of the selected words.

Activate Office 365 Home, Personal, University, Office 2013, or Office - Microsoft Community

nicholas_w_ say:

Activate Office 365 Home, Personal, University, Office 2013, or Office 2016

Technical Level : Basic

Summary

This article provides information and basic troubleshooting steps about Office activation. These steps were originally published in this self-help article: Activate Office 365 Home, Personal, University, Office 2013 or Office 2016. If you have any feed-back about these steps, please leave a comment on this Wiki article (leaving out any personal information, such as email address or product key).

Activation information in this article is specific to Office for PC. If you're looking for activation information for Office for Mac, use one of the following articles:

  • Activate Office for Mac 2011
  • Activate Office 2016 for Mac

Details

In most cases, if you're connected to the Internet, activation of your Office product is done automatically after installation. However, there are certain scenarios where you may need to manually activate the product:

Automatic activation for Office on a Windows computer fails

To manually activate Office, open an Office application, and click File > Account > Activate Product.

Activate a free Office 365 subscription that came with your computer

On the Start screen, click the Office tile and:

1. Sign in with your Microsoft account. If you don't have an account, create one.

2. At the bottom of your screen, click Run.

3. Follow the prompts to install Office.


Activate Office 2013 or Office 2016 on a different PC

If you move Office to a different PC, you'll need to activate by phone. You'll find the number in the Activation Wizard:

  1. In any Office application, click File > Account > Activate Product.
  2. In the Activation Wizard, click I want to activate the software by telephone. And then click Next.
    Tip If nothing happens when you click Next, hit Enter on your keyboard instead.
  3. Select your country/region.
  4. Call the Product Activation Center phone number that's listed in Step 1 in the Activation Wizard window.

Activate a volume license

To activate a commercial or enterprise copy of Office, see Activate volume editions of Office. 

Troubleshoot activation failures

If Office fails to activate, you can continue using Office for awhile, but eventually Office enters reduced functionality mode. Most buttons and features are disabled, and (Unlicensed Product) appears in the title bar. If this happens, see Unlicensed Product errors in Office for help with troubleshooting common activation failures.

graham mayor say:

If you type into Word from the keyboard into a new blank document, the proofing language is that defined in Windows as the default input language. If that isn't happening you don't have UK English set as your default input language.

If you copy text from elsewhere, especially the internet or from other people's documents, or create documents from other people's templates, then the proofing language applied to the source text will be copied with it, or as defined by the paragraph styles of that template.

Every character in a document can have its own separate proofing language. 

The language button, optionally available in the Word status bar, will indicate the language at the cursor. Click that language button and ensure Detect Language Automatically is unchecked.

If necessary, you can select the whole document (CTRL+A) and apply the required language to the selection using the aforementioned language button.
