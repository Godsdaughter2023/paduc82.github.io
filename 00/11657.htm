It is normal for the signal bars on the phone to fluctuate as it jumps between different cell towers, and both the cell towers and the phone adjust their transmit power all the time. ipellant say:

Hi Jon,

I'm guessing that Win 10 has trashed the boot map for your main drive that the system boot loader depends upon. 

BCDEDIT may show the problem.

Do you need to maintain XP on that computer? If so; and you have the original install media then I would disconnect the drive with Win 10 on it; keep the XP drive connected; reboot off the XP delivery disc and try to recover XP... or bight the bullet and do a full format and install. Updating to SP3 and all the extras can take a while... assuming the Microsoft update servers are still running for XP (they were a few weeks ago when I needed to rebuild XP after a disc failure).

With  two hard drives, then you can either download the latest Win 10 ISO - 10130, burn to disc, boot off it and create a dual boot on the second dis. OR: be safe: disconnect the XP drive and install Win 10 on the second drive as a primary OS. It depends on your computer. I'm using my old Shuttle which is designed for easy disc swapping and is easy to unplug / hotplug SATA drives.

I installed the new 10130 DVD onto a second drive partition yesterday - dual booting with Win 8.1 Pro.

The new DVD ISO is different from the previous 10074; different options, fewer drivers are installed - then relies upon Update to find the missing ones. If you have any Realtek networking devices, I suspect you'd be stuck in limbo.... darn thing couldn't find the Realtek sound drivers; so I installed an old set.. and then Update found a newer set... umm... something not right with Win 10 device enumeration / recognition.

One plan is: rebuild Win XP if you need it... otherwise disconnect that drive

Install Win 10 from booted DVD on the other drive as if it is the primary drive.

If you have data on the XP drive; once the Win 10 drive is up and running and Optimisation is set NOT to automatically scan new drives, plug the old XP drive back in and see if you can read and recover the data from it, using Win 10.
