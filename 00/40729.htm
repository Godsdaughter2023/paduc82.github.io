mann_manohar say:

Hello Christopher

 

As per the issue description it appears to be the problem related to Bluetooth radio settings in your computer.

 

Sometimes this problem appears when your computer has a Broadcom-Based Bluetooth radio

And your Bluetooth radio has “Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power” enabled, and the Bluetooth radio is not resuming from a low power state correctly.

 

You can follow these steps to check if this is enabled

a. Click the Start button and type “Device Manager”.

b. Click on "Device Manager" in the Results window.

c. In Device Manager, expand "Bluetooth" and select the Bluetooth radio.

d. Right click on the Bluetooth radio and select Properties.

e. On the Advanced tab, the Manufacturer Id will be 15 if your radio is Broadcom-based.

f. On the Power Management tab, “Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power” is enabled if the checkbox is checked.

 

You can check the HCI and LMP versions of your Bluetooth radio, if these versions are 6.0 or higher you may get this problem solved by just updating the Bluetooth radio driver. If the HCI and LMP versions are 5.X or lower, uncheck the checkbox for “allow the computer to turn off this device to save power”

To check the HCI and LMP versions please navigate to:

Device Manager>Bluetooth>Bluetooth Radio>Properties>Advanced Tab>HCI & LMP Versions

To update the device driver through windows update Click the Start button and type "Windows Update". Click "Windows Update" from the Results window.

If there is no update available, check with the manufacturer of your PC using their support site.

You can refer to the following Microsoft KB article:

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/2758967/en-us

 

Hope this solves the problem, if you need any clarification or any further assistance please write us back, we will get back to you as soon as possible.

  5. Once you're in the System Configuration window, go to Boot tab and uncheck the Safe boot.

  7. Restart your computer.
