sdp_2009 say:

isawar

 

Firstly, I assume you've already ensured that your phone is connecting to your local WiFi. If not, establish a connection to your local WiFi first through Menu > Settings > Connectivity > ... 

 

Then try these in this sequence: 

 

1. Go to Menu > Settings > Connectivity > Destinations > Internet > click Options > click Organise > click Change Priority. Over here, make sure that your local WiFi is your first priority. If it is not, that could be why Nokia Messaging is trying to use your mobile operator's internet service first. 

 

2. From inside Nokia Email Messaging, click Options > Tools > click Settings > General > Service Info > Acess Point. Set your access point to Default (if you have done step 1 above) or to your local WiFi, which you should see there as an option. If you choose the latter,  Email Messaging won't work when you're away from your local WiFi. That's why default is better provided you've already set you local WiFi as your first priority. 

 

Hope this helps!

How to preserve endnote numbers when converting a doc file to html. - Microsoft Community

cjskama say:

How to preserve endnote numbers when converting a doc file to html.

I have a Word doc file that I will convert to an ebook. It consists of 15 chapters with endnotes. I placed section breaks at the beginning of each chapter so that the endnotes begin at 1 in each chapter. But, when I save the file as htm, Word ignores the section breaks and renumbers the endnotes consecutively. As a result, the later chapters have three-digit endnotes, which looks very unprofessional. How can I retain the endnote numbering when saving the doc file as an html file?
doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

A macro containing the following code should convert the endnotes and their references in the text to ordinary text so that they would not change when the document is saved in htm format.

 

Dim aendnote As Endnote
Dim i As Long
With ActiveDocument
    For i = 1 To .Sections.Count
        For Each aendnote In .Sections(i).Range.Endnotes
            Sections(i).Range.InsertAfter vbCr & aendnote.Index & vbTab & aendnote.Range
            aendnote.Reference.InsertBefore "a" & aendnote.Index & "a"
        Next aendnote
    Next i
    For Each aendnote In .Endnotes
        aendnote.Reference.Delete
    Next aendnote
End With
Selection.Find.ClearFormatting
Selection.Find.Replacement.ClearFormatting
With Selection.Find.Replacement.Font
    .Superscript = True
End With
With Selection.Find
    .Text = "(a)([0-9]{1,})(a)"
    .Replacement.Text = "\2"
    .Forward = True
    .Wrap = wdFindContinue
    .Format = True
    .MatchWildcards = True
End With
Selection.Find.Execute Replace:=wdReplaceAll
