doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

Use a macro containing the following code:

Selection.HomeKey wdStory
With Selection.Find
    Do While .Execute(FindText:="December 31", Forward:=True, _
    MatchWildcards:=False, Wrap:=wdFindStop, MatchCase:=True) = True
        Selection.Delete
        ActiveDocument.Fields.Add Range:=Selection.Range, Type:=wdFieldEmpty, Text:="Link Excel.Sheet.12 " & Chr(34) & "Sheet1!R31C1" & Chr(34) & " \a \t"
    Loop
End With

If the way you are testing Outlook or other e-mail programs to see if they wrap when composing a new email is by holding down a key, here is the brilliant solution I found:

JERRY_COX replied on 

. . . After trying all suggestions, I was stumped to find nothing worked.  . . .   After some pondering about the "wrap" function, I realized I was the problem.  . . . there is not a single space anywhere within the continuous "g's" to generate a "word" wrap condition.  . . . holding down the "g" keyboard button continuously so there was a continuous string  of "g's" well beyond the window boundary on the same line without generating a wrap function (carriage return at the page's right margin). 

gggggggggggggggggggggggggggggggggggggggggggggggggggggggggggggggggggggggggg

Since I knew wrap worked fine on my Outlook and it was a generic install for Microsoft Office, I had  to look at what makes the wrap function occur during the typing process.  That's when I had my Ddddaaaa moment.   

Word wrap occurs because a word being typed extends beyond the boundary established by the page margins.  The word wrap function recognizes the last word typed before reaching the right page margin followed by a space at the right page margin.  The next word in the sentence would also have to extend beyond the page martin.   When this space followed by the next word exceeds the right boundary, a carriage return is generated and any text beyond the right margin is moved to the next line at the left page margin.

. . .   I typed the sentence "Now is the time for all good men to come to the aid of their country.”  I opened a New Email window and began pasting this sentence one right after the other forming a continuous line of text.  I continued this process until some part of the last sentence pasted exceeded the right page margin and forced word wrap.   I continued pasting the copied sentences for several more lines to validate the wrapping was working perfectly.   There was no problem with work wrap.  I just had to remember how the word wrap function works.
