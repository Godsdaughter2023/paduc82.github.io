jack o'hara say:

Sharing directory contacts from Office 365 with another Office 365 domain

If it is possible to do so, I would like to be able to share (internal) contacts between three (or more) different domains, each held in different places. Is this possible and how?

As part of a group of companies I need to be able to email all employees in all companies, but I don't want to have to remember or guess email addresses - historically the email address format has not been consistent - initial.surname, forename.initial, forename.surname, ...

Each company has a different email server or provider - Office 365 in some cases, but not the same account, SBS 2011 in other cases. I do have administration rights to each environment. I use Office 365.
rick song msft say:

Hello Jack,

 

Thanks for your post. As you need to send emails to all the employees in all companies, the GAL(Global Address List) cannot be shared to the external users.

 

Based on your requirements, you can take the following suggestion as a workaround:

1. Let each company creates a new Distribution List and add their employees to this Distribution List.

2. Go to Exchange Admin Center(EAC)->recipients->groups. Click and edit the Distribution List. Go to delivery management and choose Senders inside and outside of my organization. This will enable the users outside the organization send message to this group.

 

Then you can use your Office 365 account send emails to these groups. If you still have any questions, please feel free to let me know.

 

Regards,

Rick
