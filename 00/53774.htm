tim waggoner say:

processor power. i5? quad core?

Hello, this question has been buggin me for a while! When you buy a computer, the processor either says dual core, i3, i5 or i7. What do these numbers mean? An i7 will also be called a quad core or a six core. And that, to me, doesn't make much sense... yet.
anil kumar b say:

Hi Tim,

 

Dual (meaning two) core has two processing chips in the CPU. Quad (four) core has four of them. All the cores are on the same chip though. So, they share the same memory, and data path to the motherboard. So, a quad core chip may be able to execute the instructions four times faster than dual core.

 

The Intel i3, i5 and i7 first- and second-generation processors represent the low-end, mid-range and top-tier divisions of Core, which is semiconductor company Intel Corp's premier brand of consumer-oriented central processing units as of mid-2011. Consequently, the three computer chips contain several notable differences. Additionally, the Intel Core i7 has a performance-oriented subcategory dubbed Extreme Edition.

 

Core i7s are better than Core i5s, which are in turn better than Core i3s. Nope, Core i7 does not have seven/six cores nor does Core i3 have three cores. The numbers are simply indicative of their relative processing powers.

 

Their relative levels of processing power are also signified by their Intel Processor Star Ratings, which are based on a collection of criteria involving their number of cores, clock speed (in GHz), size of cache, as well as some new Intel technologies.

 

Hope this information was helpful.

hi JosephDrew,
the tasks shown in task manager are those that are currently running, these can be processes started by windows on boot or scheduled, called from other processes or started by programs that you are running, ending them can cause problems but a restart or a shut down and reboot will restart the necessary processes.

the only processes you need be concerned about are those that are started by schedule (eg backups) these may not restart until the next scheduled trigger.
if everything is ok after a restart, don't worry
