john sr wilson say:
When you use rehearse or record show the timings are stored in an invisible tag.

You can get the settings using this code (which could probably be improved to print out the results.

Sub showit()
Dim osld As Slide
Dim i As Integer
Dim rayDelay() As String
Dim strMessage As String
For Each osld In ActivePresentation.Slides
If osld.Tags("TIMING") <> "" Then
strMessage = "SLIDE " & osld.SlideIndex & vbCrLf
rayDelay = Split(osld.Tags("TIMING"), "|")
End If
For i = 1 To UBound(rayDelay)
strMessage = strMessage & " Delay " & i & " = " & rayDelay(i) & " seconds." & vbCrLf
Next i
If strMessage = "" Then strMessage = "No timings on Slide " & osld.SlideIndex
MsgBox strMessage
strMessage = ""
ReDim rayDelay(0 To 0)
Next osld
End Sub

This is not properly tested so work on a copy

Sub Set_Anims()
Dim osld As Slide

How to decrease heading levels by one and change titles to heading 1 - Microsoft Community

pauljurczak say:

How to decrease heading levels by one and change titles to heading 1 globally

Hi,

I used Title style for chapter titles and Heading 1, 2, 3 for sub-chapters and paragraphs. I'm trying to generate Table of Contents, but the titles are not being included. It seems that I was not following required MS Word conventions of heading levels, so I would like to do so now. I would like to decrease heading levels by one and change title style to heading 1 globally in my document. Is there a way to do it?

Paul

jay freedman say:
  • Change the view to Outline view.
  • Press Ctrl+A to select the whole document.
  • In the left group of the Outlining tab on the ribbon, click the single rightward green arrow (the Demote command). Each "Heading N" paragraph will become "Heading N+1" and all other paragraphs will remain unchanged.

  • Press Ctrl+H to open the Replace dialog. In the dialog, click the More button to expand it.
  • Click in the Find What box.
  • At the bottom of the dialog, click the Format button and click Style in the menu.

  • In the Find Style dialog, click Title (the name of the style you're replacing) and click OK.

  • Back in the Replace dialog, move the cursor into the Replace With box. Then again click the Format button and click Style.
  • In the Replace Style dialog, click Heading 1 (the name of the style that will replace the Title style) and click OK.
  • Back in the Replace dialog once more, click the Replace All button. That will change all the Title paragraphs to Heading 1 style.
  • There's one more step: By default, the TOC field that Word inserts includes only Heading 1 through Heading 3 styles, but you're now using 1 through 4. To keep all the lowest-level headings, you need to modify the TOC. On the References tab, click Table of Contents and click Insert Table of Contents at the bottom of the dropdown. Near the bottom of the dialog, change the Show Levels box from 3 to 4 and click OK. A prompt will ask whether you want to replace the selected table of contents; click Yes.

Now your TOC will include everything.

For completeness, there is another way to include the Title paragraphs in the TOC, without modifying the text at all.

  • On the References tab, click Table of Contents and click Insert Table of Contents at the bottom of the dropdown.
  • In the dialog, click the Options button.
  • In the next dialog, use the scroll bar to find the Title style in the list.
  • In the box to the right of the Title style's name, type the number of a TOC level that you aren't currently using -- for example, type 4. A check mark will appear to the left of the Title style. Click OK.
  • Back in the Table of Contents dialog, click the Modify button.
  • In the next dialog, click the TOC style matching the TOC level you assigned to the Title style. For example, if you chose TOC level 4, here you click TOC 4.
  • Click the Modify button.
  • In the next dialog, change the font and/or paragraph formatting to make the style what you want the chapter titles to look like in the TOC -- for example, 14 pt with no indent.
  • Click OK in each open dialog, and click Yes to the prompt to replace the TOC.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

By default, the Title style has an outline level of 1 and would be included in the TOC at the same level (TOC 1) as Heading 1. You can edit the TOC to use TOC 2 for Heading 1, TOC 3 for Heading 2, and so on, but it probably is more sensible and straightforward to reassign the headings, starting at the bottom level.

  1. Start by selecting all the Heading 3 paragraphs. The easiest way to do this is to right-click on Heading 3 in the Quick Styles gallery or Styles pane and choose "Select All x Instance(s)."
  2. Click on Heading 4 in the Quick Styles gallery or Styles pane to apply Heading 4 to those paragraphs. If you don't see Heading 4, there are ways to make it appear, but it's easier just to "demote" the heading level by pressing Alt+Shift+Right Arrow.
  3. Now select all the Heading 2 paragraphs in the same way and apply Heading 3 style.
  4. Repeat to demote Heading 1 to Heading 2 and Title to Heading 1.

Hi Teo,

This issue may be software related concern, given that the error code pertains to software update. To resolve this, we strongly suggest to perform a soft reset. This method will not remove anything at all from your phone, and it is completely safe to attempt in any situation.

To perform a soft reset:
1. Make sure your phone is turned on.
2. Press and hold the volume down and Power button until your phone vibrates and restarts.

Take note that you should not touch the device screen during the process (not even when the 'Slide down to power off' prompt appears).

After performing a soft reset, try to check if the issue persists.

We're just here if you have further questions.
