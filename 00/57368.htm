You could achieve this if your sound card supports Stereo Mix. Try the steps listed below to play Surround Sound PC Speakers and Xbox One Gaming Headset at the same time and check if it works.

 

  • Open Control Panel and categorize to view by small icons.

  • Choose Sounds and set your Surround Sound PC Speakers as the Default device under Playback tab.

  • Go to Recording tab, right click on an empty space and select Show disconnected devices and Show disconnected devices.

  • Locate Stereo Mix, right click on it and enable the same.

  • Once Stereo Mix is enabled, right click on it and select Properties.

  • Go to Listen tab and check Listen to this device. Click on the Playback through this device dropdown menu and select your gaming headset.

  • Click on Apply > OK. Save the changes and restart the machine to see if it works.

srinivas kr say:

Hi,       

 

 

Thank you for posting your query in Microsoft Community.

 

Based on the information you have provided, I understand that you need information about how to save URL links in Windows Media Player in Windows 7. I will certainly provide you with this information.

 

Please follow the steps to save the URL links in Windows Media Player.

 

a)      Open Windows Media Player by click "Start" and then click "All Programs." Click the "Windows Media Player" application.

b)      Click the "Organize" button and then click "Options" from the drop-down list. The Windows Media Player Options window displays on screen.

c)      Click the "Privacy" tab and then the "Music, Video, Pictures and Playlists" check boxes under the History section.

d)      Click the "Apply" button to confirm the settings and then click the "OK" button to exit the Options window.
