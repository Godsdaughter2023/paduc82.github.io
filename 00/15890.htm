g8zost say:

Of course there are workarounds.

It appears that the problem is actually caused by the new player being more correct in its interpretation of the tags, i.e. that different artists are no longer grouped together into an album just because of a shared album name. Before the update, this is what happened, and could lead to problems when you had two identically named albums in your library on the phone - they would be shown as one. 

Now these conflicts, which I think were very rare in practice (I never once encountered the problems with hundreds of albums passing through my N8), are solved. Different artists are only grouped under a single album if there is a common "album artist" tag. With very correct tagging, for compilations this should be set to "various artists" or some such, while for mix-CDs this is where the name of the DJ who created the mix goes. Unfortunately, almost nobody out there tags their albums that way (including me), so tagging problems with existing tags occur constantly now. 

Nokia have thus, by implementing a more correct solution, solved a highly infrequent problem, but created big problems in everyday use of real-world data.

 

The choices appear to be to either implement a way of tagging that three previous music player phones, two MP3 player and several music player applications on my computers did not demand, or to say goodbye to my N8 as a music player.

 

I'm still debating.
