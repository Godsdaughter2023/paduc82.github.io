Calculates the values necessary to achieve a specific goal. If the goal is an amount returned by a formula, this calculates a value that, when supplied to your formula, causes the formula to return the number you want. Returns True if the goal seek is successful.

 

Syntax
expression.GoalSeek(Goal, ChangingCell)
expression A variable that represents a Range object.

 

Parameters

Name               Required/Optional    Data Type
Goal                 Required                  Variant
ChangingCell   Required                  Range

    Application.MaxChange = 0.000001

andreas killer say:

Your loop is (almost) always doomed to fail, because there is always the possibility that there is no solution. And if so, your loop is endless.

You have to use the Solver for such complex problems.

But if you don't like the Solver, install an error handler and catch the #N/A error.

Andreas.

 

Private Sub Objetivo()
  Dim SaveC470, SaveC469
 
  'Save the values that we change
  SaveC470 = Range("C470")
  SaveC469 = Range("C469")
 
  'Install an error handler
  On Error GoTo ErrorHandler
 
  'Start the process
  Range("C470") = "1"
  Do Until Range("E467") > 0.999 And Range("E467") < 1.001
    Range("AD458").GoalSeek Goal:=Range("C469").Value, ChangingCell:=Range("C470")
  Loop
  'Successfully if we reach this point
  Exit Sub
 
ErrorHandler:
  'Restore the values
  Range("C470") = SaveC470
  Range("C469") = SaveC469
  MsgBox "No solution found", , "Objetivo"
End Sub

Export or backup email, contacts, and calendar to an Outlook .pst file

Import email, contacts, and calendar from an Outlook .pst file

Migrate Email from an IMAP Server to Exchange Online Mailboxes

1. Back up your emails from GoDaddy.

2. Remove your custom domain from the Essentials plan.

3. Cancel your Office 365 plan in GoDaddy.

5. Add and verify your domain in Office 365.

6. Import your emails to Microsoft with pst files.

a. To keep the website, you need to manage your DNS records by yourself.

b. If you use the Migrate function, you need to have both GoDaddy and the Microsoft subscription. Please cancel your plan in GoDaddy after finishing the migration.

ron_jeremy say:

I thought I'd update this thread and let others know how I accomplished moving from one tenant to another, which in my case was from Godaddy to Microsoft. The entire process is very easy to accomplish.

Here's how I did it:

  1. Create your new Microsoft 365 account with whatever plan you need.
    1. Note: while creating your new account on the MS website, you might want to use the same username and company name as your existing Office 365 account in Step 2 of the account setup process. What I mean by that is if your current O365 email address is "*** Email address is removed for privacy ***" you'd enter "james" as the username, and "widgets" as the company name, meaning your new ID will be *** Email address is removed for privacy ***. This might not be possible if the name is already taken, but I thought I'd make it known.
  2. Add your newly created email account to Outlook or whatever email client you use.
  3. Drag email from your current O365 folders to your new account. Example, I "select all" mail in Inbox and then dragged all my Inbox email to my new account's Inbox folder, then did it again for Sent folder to Sent folder, etc. This will take some time depending on the number of emails you have. Make sure you wait for this process to complete for all mail folders.
    1. Note: if you have custom folders you will need to add them first to your new account before dragging email from corresponding folders in your existing account.
  4. Login to Goddady or whoever is your existing O365 tenant and cancel/delete your O365 mail account.
  5. Login to your new "*** Email address is removed for privacy ***" account:
    1. Click on Admin icon
    2. Click on Settings (gear icon) > Domains and add your domain. It might take a little while before your former tenant releases it and it becomes available to you. Use the "recommended" domain connection setting if possible.
  6. Set your newly connected domain as the default.
  7. Click on Users > Active Users and add your new email account as a new user but make sure to select your domain (not onmicrosoft.com) as the domain. Make sure to make it the default or "primary" if there's a setting for that.
  8. Logout and login with your new email account (not the onmicrosoft.com account).
  9. Now back in Outlook on your local computer you will notice that your former email (from old tenant) is no longer connecting, so feel free to remove it. Don't worry about losing your email because you copied it to the new "xxx.onmicrosoft.com" account you added to Outlook, remember?
  10. While still in Outlook you can edit your (new) account settings to reflect your new domain, etc.

Don't worry if you get stuck on any of the points relating to the O365 admin panel because a quick call MS tech support will get you sorted in no time.
