How to enter a date without entering current year? - Microsoft Community

bobzo say:

How to enter a date without entering current year?

Every time I enter a date in Excel, 2007 or 2010, the date automatically assumes the number after the month is a year.  So if I enter Oct 12, Excel assumes I entered October 1, 2012.  I just want to enter Oct 12 and have Excel show Oct 12, but it should automatically assume I entered October 12, 2010.  The older versions of Excel automatically assumed this, what happened?  How do I get a global default the way I want it every time I open a sheet?
ronrosenfeld say:
Every time I enter a date in Excel, 2007 or 2010, the date automatically assumes the number after the month is a year.  So if I enter Oct 12, Excel assumes I entered October 1, 2012.  I just want to enter Oct 12 and have Excel show Oct 12, but it should automatically assume I entered October 12, 2010.  The older versions of Excel automatically assumed this, what happened?  How do I get a global default the way I want it every time I open a sheet?


I suspect that what has changed are your windows regional settings (see control panel for this).

The way Excel interprets date inputs is dependent on the Windows date settings (not the Excel format).  So if your date settings are "US standard"  (i.e. MDY),  and you input Oct nn, where nn is a number between 1 and 31 (or 1 and whatever the maximum number of days in the particular month), then Excel will interpret that as month and day, and assume the current year.

However, if your windows regional date setting is DMY, then the digits following the "month" will be intepreted as a year, and you will see the results you post.

If this is what is going on, you cannot change this in Excel.  You need to change your Windows regional settings to some variation of MDY.

How do I attach/insert a Folder containing photos to an email - Microsoft Community

homers13 say:

How do I attach/insert a Folder containing photos to an email

I have been trying to send a Folder containing photos and each time I "Attach" the folder opens to display all of the photos as attachments.
jeeped say:
I have been trying to send a Folder containing photos and each time I "Attach" the folder opens to display all of the photos as attachments.

First of all, how many photos are you attempting to send and what is the combined size? Are you sending to more than one person? Are you sure that everyone wants every photo? Consider uploading the group of photos to a public access file area with open or limited access and then sending them a link to the public access area.

If you are just trying to send a few photos to one or two people then click the paperclip button to attach files into a new message and then click a single photo within the folder. Tap Ctrl+A to select all then click Insert in the lower right to attach everything in the folder.

In short, you cannot attach a folder (and contents) to an email. You need to attach the individual files.
