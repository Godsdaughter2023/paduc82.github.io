daniel pineault say:

If you look at the IIF Function documentation, you'll see it is comprised of 3 sections

IIf ( expr , truepart , falsepart )

expr: What we are validating

truepart: This would be the If part

flasepart: This would be the Else part

So in your code the & else if is not valid syntax and should be within your IIF() function and if required, you'd have to embed multiple IIF's within the 1st IIF.

So your code would then become something more like (untested aircode)

IIF(Len([PhoneWork])>0,"Work:    " & [PhoneWork],"", IIF(Len([PhoneWork])<0,"Work 2: " & [PhoneWork2],"") , IIF(Len([PhoneWork2])<0,"Cell: " & [PhoneCell],""), (Len([PhoneCell])<0,"Fax: " & [Fax],"")) AS Expr3

That all said, I'm still not following your code.  The logic doesn't seem to make any sense.  Perhaps if you explain a little more what you are after we could help you put something together.

ssaylor say:

I actually got this to work:

IIf(Len([PhoneWork])>0,"Work:    " & [PhoneWork],"") & IIf(Len([PhoneWork2])>0,Chr(13) & Chr(10) & "Work 2: " & [PhoneWork2],"") & IIf(Len([PhoneCell])>0,Chr(13) & Chr(10) & "Cell:      " & [PhoneCell],"") & IIf(Len([PhonePager])>0,Chr(13) & Chr(10) & "Pager:   " & [PhonePager],"") & IIf(Len([Fax])>0,Chr(13) & Chr(10) & "Fax:       " & [Fax],"") AS Expr3_pre, IIf(Left([Expr3_pre],2)=Chr(13) & Chr(10),Mid([Expr3_pre],3),[Expr3_pre]) AS Expr3
