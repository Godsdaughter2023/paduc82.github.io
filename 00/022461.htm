graham mayor say:

Use the following instead:

Dim oSec As Section
Dim oHeader As HeaderFooter
Dim oLogo As InlineShape
    For Each oSec In ActiveDocument.Sections
        Set oHeader = oSec.Headers(wdHeaderFooterPrimary)
        With oHeader.Range
            .ParagraphFormat.Alignment = wdAlignParagraphRight
            Set oLogo = .InlineShapes.AddPicture(Filename:=strLogo, LinkToFile:=False, SaveWithDocument:=True)
        End With
        With oLogo
            .Height = 75
            .Width = 100
        End With
    Next oSec

alleykatz say:

Synchronising with ISP instead of deleting downloaded emails

I recently loaded MS Outlook 2010 to my new computer.  When I download emails, Outlook 2010 does not delete those files from my ISP's server, but rather synchronizes the files between Outlook and My ISP when downloading and again on exit.  Consequently my data allowance is being filled and emails "bounced".  I have tried:  FILE - ACCOUNT SETTINGS - (My Account) - CHANGE - MORE SETTINGS - CHANGE, only to find that the "Delivery" check box normally used to "keep a copy on server" is not present.  I have no desire to keep emails on my ISP's server, but would like them to be deleted from the server on download.  Can someone tell me how I can fix this problem?

a. user say:

You can't. That's what IMAP does - MIRRORS the folders on the Server with the email client on your computer. Email accounts with synchronisation OFF aren't IMAP - they are POP.

So if you want your email client to REMOVE downloaded emails from the server automatically, then you will have to remove the IMAP accounts and replace them with POP accounts.
