The 2 things I can recommend are as follows -

1. In Yammer Admin - go to External Networks  and click "Require admin approval for mycompany.com members to join other companies' external network"

This will effectively prevent a user using  mycompany.com credentials to join external networks of other companies.

2. In your firewall / security device settings, allow only "yammer.com/mycompany.com" access. If you can block dropbox, googledrive..etc you must have some device that is able to do URL blocking.

This will effectively prevent a user who purposely cleared his browser and logs in to a external company website using a private email account.

However, in the case of #2, it is a clear deliberate attempt to circumvent IT usage policy (if you have specified one). Hi Sam,

I’d like to confirm whether you’re talking about an Exchange hybrid deployment, where online mailboxes and on-premises ones can work together and be moved to the other side. If yes, then I suppose the migration you mentioned means a remote mailbox move request which is a hybrid specific feature.

If I didn’t misunderstand anything, then the delay of several hours should be expected. Once an on-premises object (a user account, specifically) is synchronized, it will be saved in the Office 365 Azure directory, but not in the Exchange Online directory, not until the corresponding cloud mailbox is provisioned. Hi Brodie,

 

Please note that the contact list (My Contacts) in your Outlook is different from the SharePoint contact list, they can’t sync to each other. However, we can connect the SharePoint contact list to Outlook, and then the SharePoint contacts will show up in the Other Contacts in your Outlook.

 

To copy the contact list from Outlook to the SharePoint contact list, you may try the steps below.

  1. Go to your SharePoint contact list and click “go to the classic experience” on the bottom left corner.
  2. Go to the top of the website and click List>Connect to Outlook.

   

     3. The pop-up window will turn to Outlook. After that, SharePoint contact list will be shown in Outlook.

  

     4. Now let’s go to My contacts, select a batch, copy, then go to the SharePoint Online contact list in Outlook and use keyboard shortcuts (ctrl+v) to paste the Outlook contacts.

     5. Outlook will then sync the contacts automatically up to SharePoint Online.

     6. After finishing the steps above, we can see the contacts information in the SharePoint contact list.

 

Note: We would recommend not copying too many at a time, about 100 is preferred.
