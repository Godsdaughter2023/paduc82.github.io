rob koch say:

Morash,

Most of these scam popups today aren't due to malware on your PC, otherwise your antimalware and other security programs would already have alerted you to that fact.

In most cases over the last few years, these types of popup redirections are occurring due to advertisements that are being displayed by the websites which you were already browsing.  Even if the websites you're browsing are reputable and relatively secure, the advertising networks that resell the advertising slots may not vet their advertisers well enough, allowing a handful of malicious ads that contain the redirection to the malicious popup sites.

That's why these are so difficult to stop or even detect, since no one can typically tell the true origin of the redirection unless they have tracing tools already loaded and running before they occur  That's also why they can appear to display so randomly, since you'd first have to be targeted by a malicious advertiser, which occurs based on various advertising criteria they've chosen and then need to receive one of these targeted ads with the redirection, followed by the display of the popup.

So no, resetting or rebuilding your operating system typically has no effect and only stopping the use of the website(s) delivering the ads or using an ad blocker to block as many of these as possible are truly effective methods to stop the redirections.

Using the Edge - "Send Do Not Track requests" selection and turning off the Windows 8/10 "Let apps use my advertising ID" setting in Privacy also tend to suppress many of these, though this only works if the websites you're browsing are reputable and so honor these settings, since they're both voluntary and not truly security settings.  These also have the undesired side effect of making whatever ads might still be displayed random and often of no interest, since they reduce the ability for the advertising networks to track and learn your interests.

For me, the tracking is of little value anyway, since I often browse or search for items to answer questions in forums like this one and others, thus my history is filled with items of no real interest to myself.  Combined with the fact that I hate the visual "noise" created by animated advertising, I'd rather just block the ads anyway, since the chances they'll truly be of interest are low and I'll simply search for the items myself if I really want to see them.
