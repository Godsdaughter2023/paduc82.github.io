How can i print my document with a centered margin?

Hi Folks,

I am trying to print a Excel 2010 Document and everytime i try to do so, It comes out too much to the left and not centered.

It is fine with my other documents, and it is just this one that prints too much to the left.

It looks fine as well in Print Preview.

How can i adjust it?

Thanks,

Anthea
ossiemac say:

Check the following in Page Setup:

On the Margins tab ensure that both the left and right margins are the same because the document centres between the margins and if they are not equal then the document will not centre on the page.

On the Margins tab ensure that "Centre on page Horizontally"  is checked.

Also, if you are NOT printing the grid lines, sometimes an Excel document may look like it has a wider right margin if the right hand column is significantly wider than the text in the column and text is left justified. This is because the print position is governed by the column widths; not necessarily by the width of the visible text and you could have a lot of space to the right of the text in the right side column.

bill manville say:

Make sure you have the right paper size (Page Layout > Size) set for the sheet (otherwise it might be thinking it has narrower paper than you are using.

Also, make sure you have set it to centre horizontally:
Page Layout > Margins > Custom Margins > Center on page: Horizontally.
