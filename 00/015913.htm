Add-Ins are NOT saved nor will you receive warning. This is by design as Add-Ins are supposed to be hidden and operate in a supportive manner.

The only way you can guarantee the Add-In is saved while you are developing it is to force a save from the VBE while the project is active. But even that sometimes fails and so toggling the IsAddIn flag will force Excel to go through the save query process.

This has always been the default behavior in all versions of Excel.

I think the error message comes from [Qry_DMS to Service codes]![CountOfServiceCode] that you use in the last column's expression but that's not present in the current query. You have to either change this to something that's present or include [Qry_DMS to Service codes] in the FROM clause.

The real issue is your hard to read query names. ;-)
I would suggest to use an alias and a shorter IIf() e.g. like this:

SELECT A.SUPER_CHAIN_NUMBER, A.CHAIN_NUMBER, A.Merch_Num,
    IIf(A.[ServiceCode] In ("679", "680"), A.[8300],
        IIf(A.[ServiceCode] In("0632","0630"), A.[8200],
            IIf(A.[ServiceCode] Is Null, "0000", A.[ServiceCode]))) AS ServiceCode,
    A.[Merch_Num] & A.[ServiceCode] & [Qry_DMS to Service codes].[CountOfServiceCode] AS [Long Merge]
FROM [Qry_DMS against complete list] AS A
GROUP BY A.SUPER_CHAIN_NUMBER, A.CHAIN_NUMBER, A.Merch_Num,
    IIf(A.[ServiceCode] In ("679", "680"), A.[8300],
        IIf(A.[ServiceCode] In("0632","0630"), A.[8200],
            IIf(A.[ServiceCode] Is Null, "0000", A.[ServiceCode]))) AS ServiceCode,
    A.[Merch_Num] & A.[ServiceCode] & [Qry_DMS to Service codes].[CountOfServiceCode]

where of course you still have to solve the above mentioned problem.
