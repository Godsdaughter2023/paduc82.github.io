Hello jkhcast

First step is defining by what you're actually referring to in terms of "....account is back again."

Frequently the issue is a mis-understanding about what gets removed based on what actually was deleted in <Account Settings>. What wasn't mentioned were the following important items

#1 - Was the account removed from the <Account Settings> <Email> tab>?
or
#2 - Was a data file removed from <Account Settings> <Data Files> tab

#3 - What type (POP3 or IMAP or Exchange account is involved?

Without knowing the answer to any of the above - here is how things work which people mistake for an account "not getting removed" or "returning"

a) if his involves a POP3 account and <ONLY> the mail account was removed, it will not remove the PST data file which is what appears in the Outlook Nav Pane. Far too many people believe that the Outlook Nav Pane shows email accounts when it actually is a list of all the data file configured in the account. To completely remove a POP3 account, you need to remove both the email account under the <Email> tab AND remove the PST file associated with the account under <Account Settings> <Data Files> tab

b) If this involves an IMAP or Exchange account and the .ost file associated with the account is deleted, Outlook will automatically create a new one and sync everything with the server. To remove an IMAP or Exchange account, it needs to be removed under the <Account Settings> <Email> tab
