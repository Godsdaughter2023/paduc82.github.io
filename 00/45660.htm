Hi,

The problem might be a corrupted file in the Skype folder. Did you install any program/software recently? I suggest to make a clean uninstall and reinstall of the Skype app. Here's how:

  1. Open your Applications folder and drag your copy of Skype to the trash.
  2. Then open ~/Library/Application Support * and drag the Skype folder to the trash. The ~ sign represents your home folder. To find your home folder, open Finder and, from the menu bar, select Go > Home.
  3. Quit Skype by selecting Skype > Quit Skype.

Important: If you’d like to keep your chat and call history, skip this step.

  1. Open ~/Library/Preferences and drag com.skype.skype.plist to the trash.
  2. Open Finder and search for “Skype” using the search feature in Mac OS X. Move all of the results returned to the trash.
  3. Click Ctrl and the trash icon, and select Empty Trash.
  4. Restart your computer.
  5. Download and install the latest version of Skype.

*By default, the Library folder is hidden in Mac OS X v10.7 “Lion” and higher. To find your Library folder, open Finder and, from the menu bar, select Go, press and hold the option key, and then select Library.

mariogorgees say:

The way I solved error 0x80070520 was restarting my router then restarting my PC, it seems the issue is most likely due to your router being unable to send/receive the packets for the Xbox App. I just went through 10 hrs of tech support with many different supports from Microsoft and they failed to fix the issue. After running into issues where even the Xbox App wouldn't load I decided to just restart my Router to see if it would change anything. Before the PC reboot: no change, after PC reboot: it worked like a charm.

So here are the steps:

1) Open up the Command Prompt

2) type ipconfig, you should see several numerical addresses along with what they correspond to. You want the default gateway as that is the address to access your modem/router

3) enter in the numerical address in whatever your preferred choice of browser, upon loading the page you may be asked for a username and password. Depending on the modem/router your usernames will most likely be: username or admin or a blank space. The password would be: Password or Admin.

4) once in your modem/router click through the tabs until you find an option to reset your modem/router, with some ISP's you can actually call them up and through the automated system reset your router and completely skip the first 3 steps.

5) after your modem/router has fully reset, meaning you lost connection then regained full connectivity, restart your computer. The issue should now be fixed and you can game away.
