AutoCorrect does what you tell it to unless you allow it to use suggestions from the spelling checker. As long as you don't enable that option, it uses only the built-in corrections and any you add. You can remove any of the built-in ones that causes problems.  It is always more efficient than AutoText (it doesn't rely on AutoText entries being saved in a specific template, and it fires automatically), but of course it isn't right for every application (especially since it's machine-dependent and can't be saved in a document template).

 

There are a lot of humor sites online that capitalize on the "AutoCorrect" feature on iPods and the like. This is actually "predictive texting" or AutoComplete and should not be confused with Word's AutoCorrect feature, which works only for entries you define. You can look at the list of built-in AutoCorrect entries and see the sorts of things that are programmed into it--spelling corrections, by and large--but the real power of AutoCorrect is in being to type, say, "wsj" and have Word produce "The Wall Street Journal," italicized. I find it invaluable when typing reports that require frequent repetition of longwinded names of firms or corporations, and it was a lifesaver when I was typing a report that involved the Dallas/Fort Worth ("dfw") airport, since I can't ever seem to type "Fort Worth" without having to correct it from "Forth Worth."
