davidf2 say:

With all due respect I think you are looking at the ruler incorrectly. Your 100% and 500% scale images shows the blue center guide line aligning with the 21.5 mark on the ruler. When you change the zoom to 71% the increments on the ruler change to increments of two instead of one. Note 18, 20, 22, 24 etc. This means the half way mark between 20 and 22 is 21 and so the center guide line is still aligned with 21.5.

If you are finding that when you print the page content has shifted on the printed page, then that is probably a printer setting. Sometimes it is the printer setting for the 'staple side' of the page. The printer will automatically shift the page content to make room for the staple. Study your printer settings to change or turn off that setting...maybe change the staple side to 'short side' from 'long side', or change the margin setting for the staple to 0.

I would also try changing the overall margins on the page to see if I could adjust for the print shifting on the page. For example decrease the margin on the 'staple side' by .5 cm and increase the other side margin by the same.

I have also found that sometimes the problem is because the printer will not print as close to one edge of the paper as another. The 'printable area' of the page is not equal on all sides.

Test your tweaks to the layout by printing a single sheet to see if you get the results you want.

I have had the occasion where I could not get the printer to quit shifting the page and my quick and dirty workaround was to group all the content on the page and nudge it the quarter inch or so the page was off. It made the Publisher page look offset but the printed page was centered.

That error is returned when you have linked data types. When you use =sum(b2.b5) the dot means that your formula is looking for the data b5 of  field b2, neither of which exist.  Use
