scottgem say:

Ok, so first you need Primary and Foreign keys to relate your tables. Your diagram is a good starting point. I would recommend using Autonumber datatypes as your Primary keys. So you need a CarID field in your Cars table and a TuneID field in your Tune table. I use the naming convention of naming my keys tablenameID.

Now you then need to include CarID as FK in your Tune table. So this will tell you what car is associated with what Tune. Similarly you need a PartsID in your Parts table. But you should not have a direct relation to the Tune table. Since a Part can be used in multiple Tunes and a Tune can have multiple parts you have a many to many relationship so you need a Junction table to manage that. Like so:

TuneParts

TunePartID (PK Autonumber)

TuneID (FK)

PartID (FK)

That should be enough to get you started and work the rest of your relationships. let us know if you need more help.
