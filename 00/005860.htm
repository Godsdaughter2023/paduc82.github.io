First, Is IMAP turned on for your Gmail account? If not, go into your Gmail settings, under the Forwarding and POP/IMAP. Just make sure IMAP is enabled.

Once you have confirmed IMAP is on, we can setup a new account in Outlook 2016.

1. Click "File"
2. Click "Add Account"
3. Select "Manual Setup or Additional Server types"
4. Fill out your information and put this information in:
Incoming mail server: imap.gmail.com
Outgoing mail server: smtp.gmail.com

For you username, don't include @gmail.com (or whatever Google domain you

5. Click "More settings" and go to the "Outgoing Server" tab
6. Check the box that says "My outgoing server (SMTP) requires authentication"
7. Choose the radio button that says "Use same settings as my incoming mail server."
8. Click "Advanced Tab"
9. Put in the following information:

Incoming server (IMAP): 993 or 143
Incoming server encrypted connection: SSL
Outgoing server (SMTP): 465 or 587
Outgoing server encrypted connection: TLS or Auto

10. Click "OK" when finished

That should be it. Your new Outlook Gmail account should work properly now. You can remove the old, non-functioning Gmail account from Outlook.

It will help if you have your mobile's IMEI number to hand, as you can report this to your network provider who can then get the number put onto a blacklist so it can't be used again. Depending on what country you're based in, your network provider may NOT be able to do this. I know they can definitely do this in the UK.

So - if you are in the UK - a plan of action for you is :

1) Contact your network provider first - if you look on their website you should be able to find a contact number for them which you can dial from a landline instead of your mobile.
2) Once you have contacted them, give them your mobile number and tell them your phone is stolen. If you do not have your IMEI number ask them for it - they should be able to give it to you. Ask them to bar the phone from the network if they do not offer this facility to you.
3) Contact your local police station to report this crime (do not dial your police's emergency number !) and quote your IMEI number.

That's about all you can do I'm afraid - I hope you made a note of your important numbers !

You cannot select specific colors for individual headings or for the different slots in the color palette.  You can only select a color/image as a jumping off point and then one of the offered color palettes.  Based on what you select, all headings will be the same color.  With that said, one trick that some people have done is to use a solid color image as their heading background image.  That will take away the ability to have another image as the background (like the cat in your screenshot above), but you can then end up with differently colored headings.
