Try this:

Create the caption as usual.  Put the cursor just before the number and press return. 

Create a new paragraph style called, say, caption label   that looks like and has paragraph and line space settings just like your caption style.

Apply the  caption label   style to the short the  paragraph with  the label word (figure or table). 

Now place the cursor in the first line of the caption before the paragraph mark and press Alt+Ctrl+Enter  to apply the style separator.  Even though you have two separate paragraphs, your caption will appear on one line again, but the TOF will only collect the second paragraph. 

I tested this in W2010, but I used  the style separator often in W2003. So It should work.  Of course you should try this on a copy of your document.

 

Pam

deanh say:

I did a little test and the following does work with the last comment being the only drawback to watch out for.

1. You can add the switch \a to the Table of Figure\Table field code which will remove the caption label and number.

2. Format the Table of Figure style to include the Numbered option.

3. This will now number the items in the List if Tables and Figures, but will be sequential between the two of them. You can restart the numbering, but any field refresh will change this to coninuous again, so Saving/Printing/Opening refresh options on the users machine will affect this.

You can do the restart numbering, then lock the two fields (Ctrl+F11 lock, Ctrl+Shft+F11 unlock).

You will have to decide which is best for your circumstances.

All the best

DeanH

judith_c. say:

Hi Lenore,

 

I apologized for the delayed in response. Let me assist you on how to delete your Profile picture in your outgoing emails. By default, Outlook.com is displaying the account’s profile picture. If you do not have a profile picture, and you have a connected services linked in your account its profile picture will display in your outgoing emails.

 

Since you have mentioned that you already removed your Facebook picture in your Facebook profile, have you set your account's content’s from third party network to “Don't show public content from third-party networks”. If not yet, follow the steps below on how to do this:

 

1. Sign in to https://mail.live.com or https://outlook.com.

2. Click the cog icon.

3. Select More mail settings.

4. Under Reading email, click Content from third-party networks.

5. Choose Don't show public content from third-party networks.

6. Click Save to confirm changes.

 

If the following steps didn’t fix the issue ask them to try to remove the connected services of the Microsoft account. This should remove the picture when sending emails.

 

How to remove the connected services:

1. Go to this link: https://profile.live.com

2. Click the Manage option.

3. Click Edit.

4. Then click Remove this connection completely.

 

Note: The picture in the email sent will also depend on the recipient’s email service provider’s feature. For example, if the sender sent the email using the Outlook.com UI and the recipient is a Gmail account, the recipient may not be able to view the sender’s picture in the email because in Gmail, users have the option to add a picture for their contacts. The picture in the email will only appear in the sender’s Outlook UI and if the recipient is also a MS account with Outlook.com UI.

 

Get back to us with the results.
