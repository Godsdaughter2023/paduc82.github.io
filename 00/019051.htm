jim gordon mvp say:

I think you've done an excellent job at diagnosing the problem and pinpointing it to VPN.


VPN encrypts and decrypts all network communications at both ends of the connection on the fly. This requires a lot of computing power at both ends of the connection and there's plenty of room for things to slow down, not to mention the network speed itself. 


Your observation that Office applications do a lot of read/write while they are running is also spot-on. This is not a bug. Office applications write temporary files on purpose.


You have control over your portion of the VPN scenario. You can increase your computer's RAM and processing power to make what happens on your side as fast as possible. You can connect to the internet with a high speed data connection. 


You probably don't have control over the other end of the connection. The speed of the machine at the other end of your VPN connection might be too slow. Or it might be using a slow connection to the internet.


Your Mac comes with built-in tools to help you figure out where the slowdown is. You have Activity Monitor application, and Network utilities that you can use to monitor the input and output of your Mac. Use these tools to figure out whether the slowdown is a result of your Mac, the network, or a slow computer on the other end of the connection. How to do this sort of diagnosis is beyond the scope of this forum, which is why I tried to convince you to go to a forum better suited for helping analyze networking and hardware issues.

daniel g. grau say:
"Hey Jo, where you goin' with that gun in your hand?" –Jimmy H.

Don't shoot the messenger. Word cannot distinguish a footnote reference in the text from a footnote number in the footnote itself. Both are found with ^f. But:

  • As mentioned, you can perform a one-by-one replacement with the Replace button.
  • Easier still: place your cursor in the text, then do Edit > Select All (Command A) and summon the Find and Replace dialog—the Replace All replacement will be done on the selection only (i.e., the main text flow) and won't affect the footnotes. 

As to the format, click on the disclose triangle in the Find and Replace dialog to reveal additional options. You'll see a Format dropdown menu. Place your cursor in the Replace field, then click on Format and choose Font. Clear the Superscript checkmark so the replacement will be into "not superscript."

Copperplate is a small-caps font. You can obtain a similar effect in any font by selecting your text and pressing Command Shift K or selecting Small Caps in Format > Font.

Footnote numbers are controlled through a character style. Go to Format > Style, select Footnote Reference in the list, click on Modify. Then click on Format (it's all the way down) and redefine it as "not superscript" as described above. If you do this beforehand, then you won't need to use a format specification in your global replacement.

How do I turn off the "Find" feature so the Control-Page Down will just go to the next page?  Every time I try it, it tells me "Word has reached the end of the document.  Do you want to continue searching at the beginning?"  Stupid thing ignores me when I yell at it that I just want to go down a page, not search for something!!!

suzanne s. barnhill say:

This feature is the same in Word 2010 as in several previous versions. The arrows at the bottom of the vertical scroll bar are configured as Browse commands and assigned the keyboard shortcuts Ctrl+PageUp and Ctrl+PageDown. Whenever you run Find or GoTo, the Browse Object changes from Page to Find/GoTo (this is signaled by the arrows changing color from black to blue). You must click the round button between the arrows to open the Select Browse Object palette and reselect Browse by Page. While you're there, note the other options, which can be very useful: Browse by Section, Browse by Table, Graphic, Heading, Footnote, Endnote, etc.
