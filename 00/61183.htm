How can we share a folder between two users in a computer?

Two people share one computer running Windows 8.1. I am the administrator and like to share one of my email folders with the second user. Specifically, how can this be done if possible?

[Original title: Folder sharing on one computer.]

cmdrkeene say:
In short, no.  You can share file folders, but not email folders.  If you are using an Exchange server (an email server that is used commonly by universities, governments, or large companies and business), then you can share a folder using it.  This does not work on a home computer without a corporate Exchange server (in this scenario, the emails are stored on the server, and both users just get permission to read it).

arjay dom say:

Hi,

This issue can be caused by software and hardware conflicts.

Since your keyboard and mouse are not working and you cannot boot into safe mode, try restarting your device for three consecutive times to boot Windows 10 to Advanced Startup Options:

  1. Turn on your computer. As soon as you see the Windows logo with the spinning dots, press and hold the power button until your computer turns off. Do this process twice.
  2. Turn on your computer for the third time, and let it load.
  3. If your mouse will work, select Troubleshoot.
  4. Go to Advanced Options.
  5. Choose System Restore to take your computer back to an earlier point in time.
  6. Choose the restore point related to the problematic driver or update.
  7. Click Next, then Finish.

poutnik say:

I am using windows XP ... would like to know what is the difference between hibernate & shutdown ..  which one to use ?

In book analogy, Shutting down is like closing book.

Hibernation is like closing book, if you previously put a bookmark inside
to know where to continue.

Many people use shutdown, even if it is not exactly
what they would really like to do.
But shutdown is what they know.

Shutdown closes all programs and processes and switch off the computer.

When started again, it does know what programs you had open nor their working status.
You need to launch programs and open documents or whatever
from the scratch.

Notes that if you were requested to reboot after some updated,
and going to switch off PC, this is what to be done. Reboot after hibernation does NOT count as reboot needed by updated.

Hibernation ( simplified ) first makes a snaphot of computer memory to the hiberfil.sys files.
Than it switches of the computer too.
When started, it loads content of hiberfil.sys,
bringing computer to previous state.
