In order to enable this feature in your system, you need to enable it by doing a simple procedure as mentioned below: -

1. Open Command Prompt with Administrator rights. To open Command Prompt, type CMD in Start menu and then hit Ctrl + Shift + Enter to open the Command Prompt with Admin rights.

powercfg /hibernate on

D. Now go back to Start menu to see the new Hibernate entry.

chet814 say:

The Compress Old Files selection will not remove any files, it will compress files that are seldom or rarely used.

 

If you're getting low on disk space, less than 15-20% free space, then you need to reclaim as much space as you can. However, the amount that you'll reclaim will not be a solution, only a temporary fix - it that.

 

From Add or Remove Programs and Windows components in the Control Panel you can uninstall programs that your no longer use - if you have any. Don't get carried away uninstalling programs you do not recognize, many items are needed for other programs to operate properly.

 

Uninstall or change a program
 http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows7/Uninstall-or-change-a-program

 

You might also check to see how much space is reserved for System Restore; by default usually several GBs are reserved. Also turn off Hibernation as the file it creates can be 2-3GB, sometimes more even.

 

You should also consider moving your personal files and folders to removable media, such as DVDs or an external hard drive. Even a large flash drive with 4+ GB of space can help and their prices are reasonable nowadays.

 

Besides Disk Cleaner, you might consider running CCleaner. It's a free program that will remove junk and temp files, temporary Internet files from most popular browsers, and many other caches that can be safely cleaned.

 

Since you are running Windows XP, there will be a setting in CCleaner to remove Hotfix Uninstallers; these are the Windows update files you've downloaded and installed over time and are there should you ever want to uninstall the update. But you can save these to CD/DVD and then safely remove them from your system to reclaim the space. If you have never removed any of these, you could possibly reclaim over 1 GB of space.

 

CCleaner
 http://www.piriform.com/ccleaner

 

Should you decide to try it, by default most everything in Windows and Applications is checked to be cleaned. You should first review the lists (they're compiled by what you have installed) and check/uncheck accordingly.

 

Then click Analyze to see what will be removed (nothing is deleted when you click Analyze); when the analysis is complete you may double-click (or right-click and select View detailed results) any of the entries in the list to get additional details.

 

In the detailed results view, you may right click for options such as Add to Exclude list should you not want the item deleted.

 

Once you are completely comfortable with your selections you can click the Run Cleaner button and then OK to the warning before the cleaner will run.

 

CCleaner also has a registry cleaner which I do not recommend using - even if you have successfully run a registry cleaner in the past, it's highly possible the next time will bring serious problems; they're not worth the risk..

 

TechRepublic recently published this article on CCleaner:
Five tips for using Ccleaner to degunk your system

http://www.techrepublic.com/blog/five-tips/five-tips-for-using-ccleaner-to-degunk-your-system/767?tag=nl.e101

 

CNET Editor's Review:

http://download.cnet.com/ccleaner/

 

I know this hasn't been the quick-fix you may have been hoping for, but my personal recommended solution is a new, larger hard disk drive. In the case of desktops an additional hard drive is useful for your personal files, especially for music, movies, pictures and the like.

 

Hope this helps.
