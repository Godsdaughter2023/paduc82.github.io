phillip m jones say:

I'll put in my 2¢ worth, and Bob can let you know if it is worth nothing, or worth the 2¢.

  1. Go to Find and let drop down menu appear.
  2. Choose Advanced Find and Replace.
  3. When Window Opens, in "Find" type two spaces.
  4. Click in "Replace" and type one space
  5. Now either choose Replace all (will get rid of all double spaces in Document at once.)
  6. Or, if you are still using, 20th-Century Typing Rules, select "Find next," then click on replace.
    (This takes much longer. But some Businesses and institutions still cling to the old rule of two spaces after a period. The rule should be placed along side the "Bug Whip" and the "Model T hand crank." However, some still demand it. It ends up taking extra space and breaks up the flow of reading too much.) 
  7. Once you are finished It should show "end of check" or "end of Document." (You should always start at the beginning of a document.)
