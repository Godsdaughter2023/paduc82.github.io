    How to automatically fill with different colors - Microsoft Community

    quina o. say:

    How to automatically fill with different colors

    I need your help, about how to automatic fill with different colors, the Columm "K" from "Vendor Confirmation Delivery Date" of the material order on the PO - Purchase Order,  that state the "PO Delivery Date" mentioned on Columm "H"

    This is the e.g of what I want:
    - If the date in the cell of columm K is 1 or 2 Weeks before the cell from delivery on same line on columm H, the cell's fill color should change to green.

    - If the date in the cell of columm K is Overdue (more then 5 Days past)  the cell from delivery on same line on columm H, the cell's fill color should change to red.

    - If the date in the cell of columm K is for today (the same day i'm working in the Excel)  the cell from delivery on same line on columm H, the cell's fill color should change to blue.

    - If the date in the cell of columm K is to be delivery in 3 days (from the day i'm working in the Excel)  the cell from delivery on same line on columm H, the cell's fill color should change to pink.

    hansv mvp say:

    Select the cells that you want to format. Let's say that K2 is the active cell within the selection.

    On the Home tab of the ribbon, click Conditional Formatting > Manage Rules...

     

    Click New Rule...

    Select "Use a formula to determine which cells to format".

    Enter the formula =AND($K2>=$H2-14,$K2<=$H2-7)

    Click Format...

    Specify green as fill color.

    Click OK twice.

     

    Click New Rule...

    Select "Use a formula to determine which cells to format".

    Enter the formula =($K2>=$H2+5)

    Click Format...

    Specify red as fill color.

    Click OK twice.

     

    Click New Rule...

    Select "Use a formula to determine which cells to format".

    Enter the formula =$K2=TODAY()

    Click Format...

    Specify blue as fill color.

    Click OK twice.

     

    Click New Rule...

    Select "Use a formula to determine which cells to format".

    Enter the formula =$K2=$H2-3

    Click Format...

    Specify pink as fill color.

    Click OK twice.

     

    Finally, click OK to close the Conditional Formatting Rules Manager.
