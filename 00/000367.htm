joeu2004 say: Jules wrote:
I have three cells

A33 = 1:00 (hard number)

B33 = 15:00 (formula is =MOD($C$2-A33,1)

[....]

Problem is, in cell C33 I have a formula that inconsistantly recognizes B33 and all the other cells.

and

=IFERROR(VLOOKUP($B33,data,2,0),"")


Change MOD($C$2-A33,1) to --TEXT($C$2-A33,"hh:mm") [2].

The double-negative (--) converts text to number.

As I explained in your earlier thread (click here) [1], but after you marked the wrong response as "answer", MOD(...,1) returns a poor estimate of the internal representation of the time, especially when cells like C2 (and perhaps A33) contains a date as well as time, which was the case in your earlier thread.

Although the displayed value might look okay, the binary value does not match the internal representation of time constants like 15:00 in many cases.

Consequently, comparisons will fail, especially COUNTIFS, VLOOKUP, MATCH, etc.


-----
[1] http://answers.microsoft.com/message/22a6c0bf-2b5a-457c-b314-80dc1af7ca79?threadId=2df2680c-afcf-4cb0-a8c7-02769887ab98

[2] Although --TEXT(A1-A2,"hh:mm") is indeed the best we can do, mismatches and errors might still arise depending on how the values in "data", for example, are created.

All is fine if they are constants that are entered manually or by a macro.

Mismatches or errors might arise if they are created by a formula, e.g. =B1+"01:00" in B2, or if B1:B2 are dragged down the column, or if Fill Series is used.  This is because Excel does not correct the usual infinitesimal calculation differences that arise in the arithmetic.

The best way to propagate calculated values is =--TEXT(B1+"01:00","hh:mm") in B2.
