I suspect your Normal.dotm is in the old format.

  1. Close all documents
  2. Use File>Open from within Word
  3. Open /Users/<your login>/Library/Group Containers/UBF8T346G9.Office/User Content.localized/Templates.localized/Normal.dotm
  4. Click Convert on that file.
  5. Save and close
  6. Exit and re-launch Word.

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

...when I open the preferences on those documents, I find that it's greyed out

This typically indicates that the text has a Language attribute applied to it for which Mac Word does not provide a Grammar Proofing Tool. One common cause is that the content has retained a Language attribute when copied/pasted from another source, especially web sites.

In one of the problem documents use Command+A [Select All] then go to Tools> Language to select the correct Language. Also, make certain that the box for Do Not Check... is cleared, then click OK. Does that make a difference? [You also may have to use Tools> Spelling and Grammar> Reset Ignored Words and Grammar.]

Additionally, please keep in mind that, although it has improved substantially, the grammar checking machinery in Mac Office is not reputed to be among the best. There may be some grammatical issues considered to be errors in one venue which it does not catch because they are acceptable forms in other venues.
