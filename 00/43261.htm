You can find the Microsoft account associated with your Xbox LIVE account on your Xbox console. Here’s how:

  1. Sign in using your Xbox LIVE gamertag.
  2. Go to Settings and select Account.
  1. Scroll right to Your Information and select Account Security. The Microsoft account (Windows Live ID) that is associated with your Xbox LIVE account is shown.

If you can’t sign in to an Xbox LIVE profile on your console, here are some other ways to find your Microsoft account:

  • Go to accounts.live.com and try signing in with what you think is your Microsoft account (email address and password). If you still can’t find your Microsoft account information, contact Xbox Support using the Assistance information at the bottom of this page. lazarus101 say: Hi White Wolve,

I realise this answer is about two years too late but I hope that you solved the problem and this may help others do so too.

After several hours of searching the web and messing about with administrator settings and playing with the registry I think I have finally found the answer.

The software causing the issue is microsoft visual c++ 2005. 

1. Ensure that Steam and ETW are completely removed from your system, including in the registry

2. Go to control panel>uninstall programs and remove all the microsoft visual c++ 2005 components (not the 2008 and or 2010 ones)

3. Go to start and in the search box type regedit, open regedit

4. Go to HKEY_CLASSES_ROOT > Installer > Products and search through the files to find any that contain microsoft visual c++ 2005 components. Delete those files (I only found one)

5. Insert ETW Disc and begin install. Let it install steam. You may get an error message about steam, ignore it and just load steam again to let it update. If it says about an issue with running in compatibility mode just click cancel and keep going.

6. Install ETW normally

Hope this helps some people out there who don't want to waste 3 hours on a Saturday just trying to install a game.
