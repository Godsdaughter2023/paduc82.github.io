Yes, you can reinstall and reactivate on the same machine without issue.

When you upgraded from a previous version of Windows or receive a new computer preinstalled with Windows 10, what happened is the hardware (your PC) will get a digital entitlement, where a unique signature of the computer will be stored on Microsoft Activation Servers. Anytime you need to reinstall Windows 10 on that machine, just proceed to reinstall Windows 10. It will automatically reactivate.

Regarding the Device ID, DevId (340e) = Intel 7500 Chipset PCIe Root Port, which is part of the ICH10 Southbridge chipset. This makes sense as it directly corresponds to our Port Type.


----------------


Let's now move onto the fun part, AER (Advanced Error Reporting)!


Uncorrectable Error Status: 00000020 ur ecrc mtlp rof uc ca cto fcp ptlp SD dlp und


^^ SD is the only bit of the UER capitalized, which is a single bit that indicates that a surprise down error has occurred. This implies that there was an unexpected (that's where the word SURPRISE comes into play) loss in connection between the PCI-E hub and the PCI-E device.


So, what does this tell us? Well, we now know your crashes are being caused by a PCI-e (PCI is possible as well) device unexpectedly losing connection with the hub. In most cases, this is the video card as that's what 99% of all users only have installed in their PCI and/or PCI-e slots. However, some users have devices such as TV Tuners, Sound cards, etc, installed. Does this automatically mean the problematic device is faulty? Absolutely not, and in fact this error can be caused simply by a PCI and/or PCI-e device needing to be re-seated due to dust in the slot, bad connection, etc.


What you must do now is go ahead and troubleshoot each of your PCI and/or PCI-e devices. Start by removing all of them, and leaving only one (video card). Be sure you also remove the video card, however clean the slot and re-install it. If you for example have a TV Tuner and you don't crash anymore after removing it, you know it was caused by the TV Tuner. Now, you'll re-install it and see if you crash again. If you do, it's a bad card. If you don't, it was either dust in the slot or just a bad connection.


If you ONLY have a video card as far as PCI and/or PCI-e devices go, remove it, clean the slot, and re-seat it. If you crash again, your video card is likely faulty. You can also try a different PCI-e slot if possible as well to rule out a motherboard expansion slot issue.

techfreak_ say:

All phone contacts have a display name and a number. If at any time you changed the underlying number of a previous contact, it will dial that contact with a different underlying number. If you dial a number that has never been dialed or record that has not been modified, it should dial the number without issue.

Ex.  555-555-5555 is dialed and a contact name of 555-555-5555 is created with a phone number of 555-555-5555. If the number is dialed in the future it will essentially dial the contact name that matches the number. So essentially contact names have a higher priority as your call history/phone book will be referenced first.

1st call: 555-555-5555

2nd call: You decide to change the underlying number to 555-555-5554.  Now you have a contact named 555-555-5555 whose number is 555-555-5554. You might have made these changes today or a month ago.

3rd call: In the future you decide to dial 555-555-5555, it will dial the number 555-555-5554.

To avoid this problem:

1.  You avoid ever changing the underlying number.

2.  Change the display name not to match anything that resembles a phone number, if you do.
