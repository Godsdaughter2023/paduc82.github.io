What is the difference between sleep and hibernate functions - Microsoft Community

soniacassidy say:

What is the difference between sleep and hibernate functions

What is the difference between sleep and hibernate?  I've seen both terms used

**Post moved by the moderator to the appropriate forum category.**

antoneth ber say:

Hello Sonia,

Sleep is a power-saving state that allows a computer to quickly resume full-power operation (typically within several seconds) when you want to start working again.

Hibernation is a power-saving state designed primarily for laptops. While sleep puts your work and settings in memory and draws a small amount of power, hibernation puts your open documents and programs on your hard disk, and then turns off your computer. Of all the power-saving states in Windows, hibernation uses the least amount of power.

For more information, please check this article:Shut down, sleep, or hibernate your PC.
