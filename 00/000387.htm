Hi Pksilver,

It is my pleasure to reply to your post here. 

Generally speaking, if the Exchange On-premises has been made a federation between the Exchange Online (domain contoso.com is federated), then the un-migrated Exchange On-premises users (under the contoso.com) are able to see the migrated users (to Office 365, also under the same domain contoso.com). So, that is to say, if the domain contoso.com has been federated, the un-migrated Exchange On-premises users/mailboxes are not able to see the migrated users from GAL. 

As for your case, please re-confirm whether the leaver A and Leaver B mailboxes are under the same domain and the domain is federated with Office 365.

[Distributions]  - Contains activity for contracts. Has fields for [Plan Name] (pulled when the record is created rather than tied to the live field on the [Plan] table because these sometimes change and we have to track the name as it was when it happened), [Review Link] (a hyperlink to a location on our network where files related to the activity are located), and [Participant Name] (non-employee person the activity is related to). The Email should use the following items:

To: [Email Address] from the [Employee List] of the person selected in the [Plan] table that the [Distributions] record that is currently displayed on the form is linked to.

Subject: A string containing some text, the [Plan Name], and the [Participant Name] fields from the [Distributions] form.

Body: Just the [Review Link] from the current form in hyperlink form.

DoCmd.SendObject acSendNoObject, , acFormatHTML, Me.EmailAddress, , , "Distribution for Approval: " & Me.Plan_Name & " - " & Me.Participant_Last_Name & ", " & Me.Participant_First_Name, Me.Review_Link, False

ken sheridan say: From your description the relationships appear to be:

Distributions>---Plan>----Employee List

As it is the Employee List table which has the Email Address column you should base your form on a query which joins the three tables, and returns the Email Address column from Employee List.  Bind a text box control to that column and reference the control when assigning a value to the To argument of the SendObject method.  Set the control's Enabled property to False (No) and its Locked property to True (Yes) to ensure that it is not inadvertently edited.

My guess is that the email address control is currently a combo box whose BoundColumn property references a hidden Plan ID column, but shows the text Email Address column by virtue of the ColumnWidths property being zero.  This would be consistent with the behaviour you are experiencing.
