hal hostetler - mvp-outlook say:
What kind of email accounts are you using? It would be much better if you were using a sharable email account because .PST files were never designed to be shared. Export/Import is not the way to go because the .PST import/Export feature does not and never has worked well, it loses a good deal of data. The way to do this is for your Boss to create a .PST file with the folders he want you to have and update it. He then closes Outlook and copies this .PST to your share area. When finished, you copy the .PST from the share area to your local machine and open it. If you make changes that he must see, the process is reversed; you close Outlook and copy the .PST file back to the share area, he downloads it and opens it. As to "Private drives", if these are on a network, using .PST files from them is not supported for the reasons Palcouk stated, it can/will lead to .PST file corruption. Having said all this, there are a number of third party applications that address this issue, a quick Internet search on "share Outlook data" will reveal many of them. Should you, in the future, consider either Exchange Server or Office 365, the issue will become a non-issue, as this sort of sharing is built-in.

The requirements for using the system are:

  1. The mail merge main document must be of the Letter type, though that does not mean that the output cannot be sent as an e-mail message.

  1. Create a brand new document based on your template.
  2. Open your problem document in Open Office or Word Online. Copy and paste into the new document as text only.
