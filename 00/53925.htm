Welcome to Microsoft Community. I appreciate your efforts towards resolving the issue. I will certainly help you with the issue that you are facing.

This issue might occur due to the settings that you are using on your computer. When you change a default playback device on your computer while you are running any application which uses sound, you will have to restart that application after changing the default device. This is because that application is sending the audio output to one device and does not switch it real-time.

For example, If you are watching a movie and you change the default playback device in the settings, the audio will not immediately switch. You have to close the application on which the movie is running and play it again. Once you do so, you will notice the sound coming from the newly selected default playback device.

I would suggest you to make the USB headphones as default playback device and once you do that restart the application that you are running on the PC and check if it helps. To do so, follow the steps below:

  1. Go to the desktop screen. Right click on the volume icon at the 'system tray' at the bottom right corner of the screen.
  2. Click on Playback Devices.
  3. Right click on the USB headphones and make them default.

Restart the application and check if you get sound from the headphones.

Run Audio Troubleshooter
