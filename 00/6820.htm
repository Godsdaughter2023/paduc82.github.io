robert__p say:

Hi Tyler,

For your situation, the way we can think of having the lowest impact for mail flow is listed below:
Assuming the domain of acquired company being used in IMAP server and Office 365 is @b.com and the domain you’re currently having is @a.com
1. Create a sub domain in IMAP server, let’s say @sub.b.com and MX record points to it.
2. In Office 365, create a mail contact for each user IMAP user. For example, for *** Email address is removed for privacy ***, create a mail contact in Office 365 using the cmdlets below:
New-MailContact -name user1 -ExternalEmailAddress *** Email address is removed for privacy ***
Set-MailContact -Identity user1 -EmailAddresses SMTP:*** Email address is removed for privacy ***,smtp:*** Email address is removed for privacy ***

3. In the IMAP server, set the user1’s proxy address as *** Email address is removed for privacy ***

After that, the message from @a.com to *** Email address is removed for privacy *** will be dilevered into user1’s IMAP mailbox. And when the IMAP migration finishes, you can go to Office 365 EAC to delete this user and provision Office 365 license. Then, you can make MX record point to Office 365 from IMAP server.

But please note that, some IMAP might not be able to create proxy address. If this cannot be performed by IMAP server, you can change the @b.com to internal relay domain in Office 365. Then the mail flow issue should also be fixed. But, this might cause some potential issue in the future so we still recommend you use the first way to lower the impact.

If anything is unclear, please let us know.

Thanks,
Robert

Enter app password when prompted.
