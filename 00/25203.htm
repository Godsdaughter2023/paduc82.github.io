References with Jr. and Sr or II or III suffix in names - Microsoft Community

ronametung say:

References with Jr. and Sr or II or III suffix in names

How do I include the suffix names in the references/bibliography? 

Lastname such as Dela Cruz or Del Monte are treated as two names, how should I treat this as one when generating the References/Bibliography?

Thanks and God Bless:)

jay freedman say:

For a last name that has multiple parts, insert a nonbreaking space (Ctrl+Shift+spacebar) instead of a space to keep the parts together -- for example, between Del and Monte. The in-text citation and the bibliography entry will treat those parts as a single name.

Word's bibliography generator does not correctly handle suffixes such as Jr. or II in the inverted format for any of the common citation styles. Instead of placing them after the first name in the correct format (APA or Chicago etc.):

Last name, First name, Jr.

Word inserts the suffix at the end of the last name (with or without a comma)

Last name Jr., First name

or

Last Name, Jr., First name

The only way I know to correct this is to wait until the paper is completed and the bibliography is updated for the last time; then click on any of the entries (which selects all the entries because they're in one field) and press Ctrl+Shift+F9 to convert the field to plain text; and edit each entry that contains a suffix to move that suffix after the first name. Note that once the field is converted, the bibliography cannot be updated again -- it must be deleted and reinserted, and all the edits must be repeated.

  2. In the scrolling list to the left, highlight style "Footnote Text"

  1. In the main menu bar, select View > Draft
  2. Repeat, but this time select View > Footnotes
