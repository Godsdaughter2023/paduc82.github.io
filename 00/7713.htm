richardjse say: I had this very annoying problem but solved it using advice from another website. It's caused because Outlook remembers previously used email addresses and won't allow the "recently used" email address to be changed or deleted while it has been recently used. To get rid of the "recently used" email address, create an email, type the first couple of letters of your contact's name until the normal list of possible names appear, put your cursor over the one you want to delete and press the cross to the right of the name. It worked for me - when I went back into Contacts the incorrect email address had gone from my friend's contact details.

Hello,

You should be able to configure other settings for a connected account in Outlook.com. See if you will be able to set up your email account by performing these steps:

  1. Log in to Outlook.com.
  2. Click on the gear icon on the top right.
  3. Select Options.
  4. Under Mail > Accounts, click on Connected accounts.
  5. Select Other email accounts under Add connected account.

  7. Tick the checkbox for Manually configure account settings (POP, IMAP or Send Only account).

  9. On the next pane, choose your connection type and click OK.
  10. A new pane will show asking for further details of the account connection.
