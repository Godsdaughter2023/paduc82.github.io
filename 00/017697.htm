You should use something like


Dim oWord As Word.Application
Dim WordWasNotRunning As Boolean  

'Get existing instance of Word if it's open; otherwise create a new one

Gunner2011 replied on

The reason for this error is because Office SP1 has added a new service: Office Software Protection Plan and will require you to reactivate office. Since the old "tokens.dat" is still registered and the service running, there is no way to fix this unless you follow some steps I did after contacting Microsoft.

Here are the steps you must take:

1. type "services.msc" into the run command
2. stop the service "Office Software Protection Plan"
3. navigate to "C:\Program data\Microsoft\OfficeSoftwareProtectionPlatform"
4. rename "tokens.dat" to "tokens.old" (or whatever strikes your fancy)
5. navigate to "C:\Program data\Microsoft\OfficeSoftwareProtectionPlatform\Cache"
6. rename "cache.dat" to "cache.old" (or whatever strikes your fancy)
7. reboot (this will force the "Office Software Protection Plan" service to start again
8. open any Microsoft Office application and re-type your activation key
9. Office will prompt to to reboot----do so----
10. re-open any application and activate (Office may ask you to retype the key)
11. activate online and you are done.

This worked beautifully for me for Office Professional 2010 without having to remove/re-install the application.
