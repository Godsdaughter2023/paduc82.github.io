<snip>I bought new one Asus laptop, so how is this illegal? </snip>

KMS licenses are for use by the company that bought the license, not for resale to the general public (like you). If the computer you bought has a KMS type license on it, this is an inappropriate use of the license, "illegal". It is like buying a "knock-off" or "bootleg" copy of any other product (purses or perfume or high fashion) when you thought you were buying the real thing online or in a real store (not when you buy a "Gucci" watch from a street vendor for $25). The person who made the "copy" and sold it to you is the one who broke the law, but you are stuck with the consequences. In this specific case, the consequence is that your license will expire and you will be forced to pay (again) for a legitimate license.

That being said, buying a name brand computer, ie Assus, from a legitimate source it seems unlikely that you have KMS license.

I have been seeing a lot of people reporting the same problem. It seems like a "feature" (aka "bug") in Windows.
