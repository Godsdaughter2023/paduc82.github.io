albert d. kallal say:

Unfortunately I really don't have any good answer for you.

As far as I know it's not allowed anymore, and the other hand you can always use page down key, and it does repeat. (so you don't even have to grab the mouse)

 

At the end of the day, this is a very bad habit to teach users anyway. In many cases when your online purchasing something, hitting submit or teaching users to hold down the button over and over until something happens usually can result in disastrous consequences (same for delete types of prompts).

In some forms it's also a known case of instability.

 

I would present your users some type of continuous form (they can use the slider). Or the very minimum build some type of form that asks a person for the account number or particular record there trying to find.

 

It makes little sense to have 10 users on a Network Holding down the mouse repeat and results in dragging thousands of records across the network and then users on a wing and a prayer hope the correct number of records go whizzing by in some un-controlled amount of time before they let go. And, the vast majority of the records whizzing by will not even be read by the user (So you're wasting precious network and bandwidth resources ).

 

At the end of the day, fostering this type of uncontrollable response of UI on users is difficult at best, and often the consequences are bad.

 

With the advent of web based systems, we see this type of UI Behavior more and more rare.

So, I hard pressed to see anything from Amazon, or Google, or eBay or any decently designed Software System that allows this type of record movement. It just too uncontrollable from a user's point of view.

 

It's possible that the introduction of web based forms in access 2010 was a consideration as to why this ability been depreciated (I really don't know the answer by the way).

 

At the end of the day is likely a good thing to discourage users from doing this type of user interface action and behavior in any of your designs anyway.

 

I am not trying to portray a lack of sympathy for your case that you obviously used and liked this feature and now it's gone. However I do think that the fact that the world of computers is changing is likely why the feature was removed.

 

About the only consolation I can come up with, is that you can still use the page down key, and also for the most part you probably want designs that you avoid the need or requirement to need this repeating action in the first place.
