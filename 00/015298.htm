Hi I have a question... Looking for help making a spreadsheet to assign random shifts to employees. I think there is some missing information here. Are there one or more persons on each shift? Are there any restrictions on how may shifts in a row that an employee can work?

If there is only one person per shift and no restrictions on the number of adjacent shifts for a person, here is one way of randomly distributing the shifts of a month to the 12 employees.

In total there are 93 shifts (in a 31 days month). The requirement to have "close to the same amount of shifts per person" leads to that 9 persons will work 8 shifts and 2 persons will work 7 shifts (9x8+3x7=93)

In cell A1 type this formula:
=RAND()

Copy down until cell A93

In cell B1 you put a 1, in cell B2 a 2, in cell B3 a 3, and so on until cell B93 with the number 93.

In cell C1 to cell C9 you put an A, in cell C10 to C18 you out a B, and so on until cell C65 to C72 with a I. In cell C73 to C79 a J, C80 to C86 a K, and C87 to C93 a L.
The letters A to L represent the names of the 12 employees.

Now sort the range A1:B93 (note B93 - not C93).  Then sort the range B1:C93 (note B1 - not A1) in ascending order.

That will give you the list of your 93 shifts with the name of the employee for each shift to the right.

Repeat the two sorting steps if you or the employees are not happy with the random shift allocation.

The employees can now draw their respective names from "papers in a hat" to make it random also who will get the 7 shifts and who will get the 8 shifts. :-)
