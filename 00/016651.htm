superfranky say:

Hi AyoguChibueze9228,

When you add the domain to Office 365, it’s called an accepted domain. By default, if the MX record which points to Office 365 has the lowest preference number (highest priority), the email which is delivered to an email address with an accepted domain will only be routed to Office 365, emails for non-Office 365 recipients are rejected. For the internal emails (both the sender and recipient are created with the accepted domains), they will be routed to Office 365 regardless of the MX records.

To deliver to the non-Office 365 recipients, you need to set up connectors to route mail between Office 365 and other email servers and enable Internal relay in Office 365 as I mention previously.
Then, if the recipient doesn’t exist in Office 365, the emails will be routed to the Internet and delivered to other email provider according to other MX records.

To solve your problem, you needn't verify a sub-domain, you can create two MX records(Office 365 has the lowest preference number,highest priority), set up connector and enable Internal relay so that all users can receive emails.

Thanks,
Franky

(5)Set up forwarding rules to forward the emails from Zimbra to Office 365, for example: from *** Email address is removed for privacy *** to *** Email address is removed for privacy ***.

After these steps, the emails will come to Zimbra first. If there is a forwarding rule, the emails will be forwarded to the Office 365 mailbox (*** Email address is removed for privacy ***). (4)Point the MX record of your domain unxxx.edu.ng to Office 365.

That issue is due to your data, because the numbers have more decimal places as you see on screen.

The solution is to use ROUND on each number, then you get an accurate result if you sum them up.
