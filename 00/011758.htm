How to make PivotTable filter and sort affects the entire Row of the - Microsoft Community

mohamed el sheikh say:

How to make PivotTable filter and sort affects the entire Row of the sheet?

I have a PivotTable and beside it I have added a Comment column beside the PivotTable (outside the pivot and aligned with each row in the pivot), which is a free text column and not connected to the data source of the PivotTable.
When sorting, filtering or refreshing the PivotTable the Comment column stands still, and not aligned with the new order or filter of the PivotTable.
I want to the sorting, filtering and refreshing of the Pivot to affect the entire Row and not only the PivotTable data columns.

Any help?

v_agarwal say:
I have a PivotTable and beside it I have added a Comment column beside the PivotTable (outside the pivot and aligned with each row in the pivot), which is a free text column and not connected to the data source of the PivotTable.
When sorting, filtering or refreshing the PivotTable the Comment column stands still, and not aligned with the new order or filter of the PivotTable.
I want to the sorting, filtering and refreshing of the Pivot to affect the entire Row and not only the PivotTable data columns.

Any help?


Pivot table is independent, thats why its adjacent rows/columns are not considered while doing filtering/sorting/refreshing. Suppose in your pivot table, there are 20 rows and you have given 20 columns, now your data has changed and after refreshing the rows are reduced to 10, then how you would like to have those comments.

 

In normal filter/sorting the rows are fixed/ they are hidden thats why it take effect entirel columns of those rows, but that is not the case with pivot table.

 

Still if you want then either you give comments in your database and then make those part of your pivot table  OR   paste value your pivot table and then put filter in that.  Then it work like a normal table but refreshing will not work.

mohamed el sheikh say: Thanks Vijay,

How to "past value" in pivot table?

v_agarwal say:

Either in the same sheet select entire sheet or the range of pivot table,  do Copy and then at same place go to Paste special/Values.  In this case you will not able to refresh it again.

 

Or just make a new copy the sheet on which pivot table exist, then at this new sheet, do the paste special values,  so that your original pivot table is also there, and you can work on this pasted value sheet.
