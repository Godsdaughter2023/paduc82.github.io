That box is to NOT check spelling and grammar. You are telling Word to ignore any perceived errors.

This is like formatting text green. If you copy it, it stays green. If you insert text into the middle of it, that text is green.

You need to select all of the text in your document, call up the proofing language setting, and uncheck the box. It isn't good enough to have it shaded (which means some is yes, some is no). It has to be unchecked.

By the way, unless you actually have text in different languages in your document, you do not want different proofing languages applied.

suzanne s. barnhill say:
If the check boxes were created as MACROBUTTON fields, they rely on macros and AutoText entries. While the macros can be stored in a document, the AutoText entries cannot; they have to be saved in the document template. If the document template is not present on the Mac, then the AutoText entries will not be available. Moreover, if the document is opened on a Mac with a version of Word that doesn't support macros, then the macros won't work, either.
 
If, on the other hand, the check boxes are check box form fields, then they won't work unless you protect the document for forms, in which case the MacroButton NoMacro fields (click and type) will not work; text form fields will be required instead.
