3G uses more power than GSM only, so best to keep it at GSM only if you don't use 3G.

Other possible reasons:

GSM cell site antennas are located very far from you - the cellphone will require more power to send its (synching) signals to these antennas.

The cell phone is kept within an enclosure that has a lot of metal. The cell phone will require more power to have its signal pass any metal barriers for synching with the cell site antennas.

 

You can charge lithium ion batteries whenever you want, even if they still have 80% or full capacity. This does not diminish the number of possible recharge cycles. One recharging cycle of an Li-ion battery is the sum of charges that would lead to a full charge.

Lithium ion batteries should never be drained til they are utterly exhausted. Not good for their life expectancy. Also, a Li-ion battery will reach its full charging capacity only after a number of charges. They are incomparable to the NiCad batteries which do need a full drain before charging to keep their capacity at maximum.

In both programs, you can turn off Protected View, as Microsoft calls it:

  1. Choose File, then Options.
  2. Select the Trust Center pane, then Trust Center Settings and finally Protected View.
