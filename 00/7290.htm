A Onenote notebook on Onedrive has its master (what you call real) only on Onedrive. If it's open on your Windows client there is a temporary copy as part of the Onenote cache. This allows working offline.

And then there are the backups on Windows we talk about.

For example, I have inputted: =IF(AND(B2>0,B2<3),"D2/3.449"). The B column represents the pump number (this particular function referring to pumps 1 & 2), the D column represents pump price, "3.449" refers to an example price per gallon, and all of this will be inputted into the C column representing gallons.

kergga say:

=IF(AND(B2>0,B2<3),D2/3.449)

Those quotes were unneccesary and force the cell reference and divisor to be treated as text.

 

edit: you may also want to return a specified value if the two criteria cell do not match your AND criteria, e.g.

=IF(AND(B2>0,B2<3),D2/3.449,"ERROR")

or

=IF(AND(B2>0,B2<3),D2/3.449,"")  (to leave the cell blank)
