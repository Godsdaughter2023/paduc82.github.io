Sub CopyFromTemplateToMenu()
   Dim z As Long

   With Sheets("menu").[A10:D20]  'specify menu-sheet and paste area
      For z = .Rows.Count To 1 Step -1
         If .Cells(z,1) & .Cells(z,2) & .Cells(z,3) & .Cells(z,4) <> "" Then Exit For
      Next

      If z = .Rows.Count Then
         MsgBox "No more empty rows"
      Else
         .Cells(z+1, 1) = ActiveSheet.[A1].Value
         .Cells(z+1, 2) = ActiveSheet.[B2].Value
         .Cells(z+1, 3) = ActiveSheet.[C3].Value
         .Cells(z+1, 4) = ActiveSheet.[D3].Value
      End If

      ActiveSheet.[A1].Select
   End With
End Sub

foster303 say:

transferring folders from my computer using MSN to Outlook.com

On 4-1-15 we were notified that MSN premium was going from $10 a year to $50 a year.  So we either have to pay or transfer our email.  I am 66 years old and this is no fun :(.  I have been able to transfer contacts and emails to Outlook, but can't figure out how to transfer our folders that have the emails we want to save.  It says they are on our computer....where?????  And when I find them how do I get them transferred to Outlook???  Another question: is there any way to transfer our Favorite websites?  Thank you for any help you can give!
foster303 say:

I had hoped it would be an easy problem to find an answer.  I assumed that Microsoft had develped a program that would transfer the folders. I did find that in 2007 there seemed to be that sort of program, but it no longer exists. So now it is a tedious process to do this by hand for each folder and their contents.  This is what I found:

How do I back up the email I have stored on my computer?

  1. Launch the MSN Butterfly and sign-in.
  2. Click on the E-mail or Mail & More button in the navigation bar.
  3. Click on a folder under the Folders on Your Computer section in the left pane.
  4. Choose the emails to save by using the individual check boxes. To select all the emails at once, click the link at the top of the check box column.
  5. Click the Put in Folder button (folder icon with arrow moving towards the outside of the folder) and select a destination folder located under the Folders on MSN section.
  6. Repeat steps 2-5 for each folder under the Folders on Your Computer section.
  7. Perform the above steps for each MSN Premium and Windows User account as needed.

Please note that the upload time for your emails saved under "Folders on Your Computer" back to the MSN server will depend on your connection speed.

If you choose not to move your emails saved under Folders on Your Computer, after the service has been terminated you will lose access to those emails and will not be able to recover them.
