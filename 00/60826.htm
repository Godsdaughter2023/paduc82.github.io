a. user say:

A clean install is many times superior to an upgrade but it's also more work.

Before you think of upgrading again, here are some things to look for:

1- Make sure all your hardware is supported in Windows 10. If a hardware manufacturer is not supporting Windows 10, and the hardware can't be easily replaced, you're only asking for trouble if you upgrade. Microsoft can't force hardware manufacturers to support Windows 10.

2- Make sure all your software is compatible with Windows 10. If any of your programs are not compatible, upgrade to versions that are compatible or uninstall the programs.

3- Make sure your computer is free of every known problem. Never upgrade over a problem.

4- Be as sure as you can that your computer is configured in a way that Microsoft supports. 100% certainty is not possible, but the closer you are too 100%, the better the chances of a successful upgrade.

5- Make 101% sure that your computer is free of malware.

6- Make a complete backup before you start.

Anyone who wants to upgrade their operating system - any operating system - without doing these things is better off going to Las Vegas instead, because your chances of success are about the same but in Las Vegas at least you have entertainment.
