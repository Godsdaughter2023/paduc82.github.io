Where does MovieMaker keep its original video clips? - Microsoft Community

a. user say:

Where does MovieMaker keep its original video clips?

MovieMaker somehow imported years worth of video clips and saved them all together as one huge long movie but I can't find all the original video clips?  I didn't mean for them to get pasted together.

I've looked in PhotoGallery and these clips are not there.  PhotoGallery has different clips.  So I can't figure out where all the original video clips in MovieMaker are living and how do I get to them?  Or do I have to separate each clip out of the movie?  The dates of each individual clip are not visible from the movie so will the dates be lost?

I'd like to store all my videos in one place.  Why are there some videos stored in Photogallery and others in Moviemaker and how do I keep them in one place?

Thanks very much for your help, I'm a long time Mac user and appreciate some clarity on this.

considerate_guy say:

You video files are stored on your computer's hard disk (usually) unless you are reading them directly off some external storage (external hard drive, memory card, directly from the camera, etc.)

Neither Photo Gallery nor Movie Maker are actually storing anything. All they do is present the contents of your hard drive and optionally allow you to do things with the video clips and photos on your hard disk. When you are previewing a Movie Maker project, it is dynamically reading the selected video clips and photos from wherever they are on your PC and rendering a preview of what your movie will look like.

When you then save this as a movie, it joins those clips together, with the added captions, transitions, effects, etc. and makes a completely *new* movie. It doesn't do anything to the original source videos.

So, unless you have somehow deleted the source videos (and/or photos), they are still where they were to begin with.

If you want to see where Movie Maker is pulling the clips from, open up the *project* file (not the movie) and hover over each of the clips. You'll see the full path to the source file. In order to load a project (.wlmp file), go to the first File menu (Alt+F) and look under Recent Projects. Find the one that has all the individual clips, not the one that has a single combined movie.

slance310 say:

Hi,

Win 8, 8.1 and 10 have a new feature called Fast Startup. It can sometimes cause problems like you mention as well as others.
The easiest way to tell if this is the problem is to turn it off and see what happens.

This feature only works when you do a SHUTDOWN and then Boot.
It doesn't effect a RESTART.

To turn it off or on...
Go to Control panel...Power Options, and select Choose What the Power Buttons Do on the left.
Then select Change Settings That Are Currently Unavailable near the top center of screen...
Lower down on the window, uncheck Fast Startup.

Basically Fast Startup is a hybrid shutdown\hibernation where drives are not always closed, and settings are stored... This is to speedup the boot as it doesn't need to load all of that stuff again...

The problem is that if the machine loses power after being shutdown, all of that stuff is lost just as if you pulled the plug from the wall on a desktop machine...

The battery running completely down will cause this, as will removing the battery with no external power to the machine...

This can be avoided in the future by turning off Fast Startup if the machine is not going to be used for long periods, or the battery removed...

mike clarke say:

I've owned this game since its original release many moons back but have only just today tried playing it on Windows 10 to show a friend.

The game is trying to look for a now deprecated Directx component known as DirectPlay. It was replaced with Xinput from the XBox Consoles and not included in later versions of Windows from roughly 7 and up.

It is however still available to install as an additional feature in the "Windows Features" dialog.

You can access this by going to your control panel and then into "programs and features" and finally clicking "Turn Windows features on or off". You should then find it under "Legacy Components" just check the box and click on "OK" and it'll be installed.

Try running the game now and it should be good to go. (Note: you may still need to "run as administrator" on the first launch but it isn't necessary for subsequent launches.
