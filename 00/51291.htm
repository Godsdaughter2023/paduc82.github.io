lemp say:

It's not clear what settings your ISP tech support may have changed on your modem/router.  Typically, the ISP is concerned with the link between their server and your modem, but any fiddling with the settings that control that link will have no effect whatsoever on the link between your computer and the modem/router.

If you haven't yet reset your router to its default settings, do not do so unless you know the internal router settings necessary for you to connect to your ISP.  If you have a cable Internet connection, the default settings may work OK, but if you have DSL, they probably will not.

You should not get that "IP address conflict" error when you connect the Ethernet cable.  Please do the following:

Open the "Network and Sharing Center" and then click on the link to "Change adapter settings."  You should see an icon for your wireless network adapter and an icon for your "Local Area Connection" (your Ethernet connection).

  • Right-click on each of the icons and select "Properties."
  • There should be a list headed "This connection uses the following items."  In that list, select "Internet Protocol Version 4 (TCP/IP)" and then click "Properties."
  • The radio buttons for "Obtain an IP address automatically" and "Obtain DNS server address automatically" should both be selected.  If this is NOT the case, make it so and OK your way out.  Otherwise, Cancel your way out.

If one of the adapters was set to use a specific IP address, your "IP address conflict" should now be fixed (you may have to reboot the computer to have the change take effect).

If both of the adapters were already configured to obtain their IP address automatically, the problem lies with your router's configuration.  You'll have to access the router's web-based utility to investigate and make changes.  For help, please supply the make and model of the router from the label on its back or bottom.  Also do the following at a time when you have Internet connectivity:

Open a Command Prompt window (type cmd in the Search box and press Enter)

  1. In the black Command Prompt window, type the following and press Enter after each line

ipconfig /all > "%userprofile%\Desktop\ipinfo.txt"

notepad "%userprofile%\Desktop\ipinfo.txt"

exit

  1. In the open Notepad window, click Edit > Select All (or press CTRL+A) then click Edit > Copy (or press Ctrl+C)
  2. In the body of your Question, Right-click > Paste (or press CTRL+V) the data from the Notepad window
  3. Close Notepad and delete ipinfo.txt from your Desktop
