a. user say:

"...which suggests the problem is with Windows 10 not the printer."

That's your suggestion. Here are the facts.

Hardware manufacturers decide whether or not to support a new version of Windows, not the other way around. Windows can't possibly adjust itself to accommodate hardware devices because:

1- Microsoft can't possibly be aware of all the hundreds of thousands of hardware devices all over the world that can be connected to a computer, and

2- Even if Microsoft did know about all the hundreds of thousands of hardware devices (if not millions) all over the world that can be connected to a computer, Microsoft doesn't have the proprietary knowledge of each device that would be needed to customize Windows to work with each device, and

3- Even if Microsoft did have that proprietary knowledge (through industrial espionage, one presumes) that knowledge is the intellectual property of the respective hardware manufacturers that Microsoft does not have the legal right to exploit.

Instead, Microsoft publishes the detailed specifications of its upcoming operating system and each manufacturer decides which of its devices it will support on the new operating system. Each hardware manufacturer decides this on its own, so if you think that Epson should support your printer on Windows 10, you need to complain to Epson.

Windows Settings

andre da costa say:
Windows automatically removes non-user selected languages after a period of time. If you remove an installed language through the Settings app, the language will be removed from the listed languages, but the language pack will not be immediately deleted. If you want to immediately remove a language pack, use lpksetup.exe.

Note: Lpksetup will uninstall a language pack, but the language will still appear in the Settings app.
Remove a language with the Settings app

In the Settings app, you can remove a language by going to Settings > Time & language > Region & language. Click on the language you'd like to remove, and then click on remove. Windows will automatically delete language pack files the next time it runs its automated cleanup tasks.

Remove a language pack with lpksetup.exe

On the running operating system, open an elevated command prompt.

Run lpksetup.exe with the /u option to uninstall the language pack. Here is how you would remove a Greek (el-gr) language pack.

lpksetup.exe /u el-gr

moarpony say: I found a solution that works! I was looking for a solution to something else, but it also allowed me to open Win7BootUpdater! I'll directly quote the answer from another Microsoft Answers page.




- activate the hidden administrator account by following the steps on this tutorial...http://www.howtogeek.com/howto/windows-vista/enable-the-hidden-administrator-account-on-windows-vista/

- When finished activating in the command prompt, bookmark this websites for further use and log off of Windows.

now there is a administrator option in the logon screen.

- log into the administrator account open a browser and open this page again.

- Click this following link and download it to your desktop... http://www.combofix.org
- disable your internet connection and anti-virus software.

- Now run combofix.exe

A window will appear and numerous scans will take place. It will take a little while. eventually the computer may restart. when it does, log back in to the administrator account and Combofix will resume running for a little while. Don't do anything until the Log file automatically appears. - Restart your pc and log back into administrator account.

- now set the Security center service back to automatic and click apply. then click start and ok.

- restart your PC and now you can log back into your normal user account. 




And then of course, I disabled the Administrator account.

Anyway, this was the fix that worked for me!

rose cal say:

Hi,

Configuration settings may be the possible cause why you're unable to change the location of where you save your photos. To assist you better, we'd like to gather more information:

  • Have you made any recent changes prior to this issue?
  • Are you able to choose other location before aside from This PC or D Drive?

Meanwhile, we suggest that you follow these steps on how to change the location where you save your Photos as a work around:

  1. Go to Libraries then select the Camera Roll.
  2. Right-clicked Camera Roll and choose Properties.
  3. Under Camera Roll Properties, Click the button Add and look for OneDrive.
  4. After choosing OneDrive, click Set as location button.
  5. Click Apply and OK.

Let us know if you need further assistance.
