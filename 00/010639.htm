Alice,

Thanks for taking the time to explain your experience in some detail. That helps and leaves me with a few guesses. The fact that this problem is intermittent suggest to me that it may be associated with your video driver. But first...

Go to View > Graphics Manager > look to the bottom for 'change picture display' > this opens a 'picture display' dialog with three options. If you have 'Detailed Display' checked, then change that to 'Fast resize and zoom'. Ok out and see if that fixes the problem.

Along the same line of reasoning, you might also try 'compressing' your images. You select any image on your page, right click > Format Picture > Picture tab, compress... This will resample and reduce the file size and dimensions of this image and optionally all the other images in that publication. Try this on a copy of an old .pub file as you will not be able to 'uncompress' those images, meaning that if you want to increase the picture box size you will end up with a poor, distorted or pixalated image.

And finally if still no joy try adjusting the acceleration on your video driver. You can go to your Control Panel or I just right click my desktop > properties to get to the Display Properties dialog > Settings tab > Advanced > Troubleshoot tab and drag the Hardware Acceleration slider down two or three notches. Ok out and see if this fixes the problem. If it does it means that the problem may lie with the video driver. 

You can leave your computer running at this lower hardware acceleration, but it may cause problems with other programs.  A better choice is to try and find a driver update for your graphics card. Sometimes the latest drivers from the computer manufacturer or the video card manufacturer will do the trick. Even new computers may have a dated driver. Of course if this does not fix the problem go back and change the hardware acceleration back to full power.

There are my best guesses this morning. Post back and let me know if any work.
