shenan stanley say:

Very simple to answer:
Microsoft does not support your Hewlett Packard ScanJet 3300C.  Hewlett Packard does.  If Hewlett Packard does not release a driver for one of their products for a given operating system (or even a service pack level of an operating system) that is their (Hewlett Packard's) choice.

Microsoft does not create the drivers for *any* hardware device that they do not manufacture.  That would be in the millions of devices and not a very wise decision from ANY standpoint.  The support for your hardware devices in *any* widely utilized operating system comes from the manufacturer of the hardware - as they are the only ones with a vested interest in your using their product over another that happens to support the operating system you have chosen to run.

 

Just to make sure that the lack of support in Windows 7 is understood as being a problem of 'lack of hardware manufacturer support' as opposed to anything else... I decided to quickly do a simple search for your product in reference to Windows 7:
"Windows 7" ScanJet 3300C

And the first thing I found was this:
Windows 7 Compatibility Center report for the HP ScanJet 3300C
(You can also see the 32-bit version by clicking on the tab on that web page.)

Which states exactly what I have:
"The device manufacturer stated this product is not compatible."

If you go to the HP web page for the product: Software & Driver Downloads - HP Scanjet 3300c Scanner
.. the first thing you can see is that HP did not supply drivers beyond Windows XP (click on the drop-down to select your operating system) nor for Macintosh or *nix.  There is a link on the page one may hover over that asks, "I don't see my operating system?" and answers, "If your operating system is not listed, HP does not have software or driver downloads available for this product in that operating system."

You can also see the queries about the HP ScanJet 3300C in the Microsoft Answers forum that shows some people's work-arounds in an attempt to stretch out the life of this legacy product (the state of which and support of which is something Hewlett Packard decided about their product.)

Yep - it's the way it has always been.  You buy a product and sooner or later, if that product works in conjunction with other products, as you upgrade one or more tof the products that it works with you will have to upgrade the original product too *if* you desire to keep the funtionality that product gives you.

<reality> That product (the HP ScanJet 3300C) is old, outdated technology.  The quality it can give you doesn't even compare to the cheap (and I mean cheap - not inexpensive) scanners than can be built-into the most generic off-brand inkjet All-in-Ones on the market today (where the ink will cost you more in a given 6 month period than the printer did initially.)

Looking at the specifications for the HP ScanJet 3300C it is obvious that there are much better products out there in a much more comapact size and more features.  It's time to replace that hardware. </reality>
