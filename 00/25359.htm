I want to put a blank page into a document without effecting the page - Microsoft Community

oliviaalex say:

I want to put a blank page into a document without effecting the page numbers. Please Help!

Original Title:  H E L P!!! INSERT A BLANK PAGE INTO A NUMBERED DOCUMENT BUT I DON'T WANT TO HAVE THE INSERTED BLANK NUMBERED.

I work on a lot of documents which need to have blank pages inserted at certain points, i.e. after the cover sheet, after the Table of Contents, before and after Appendices, etc.  I do not want these inserted blank pages numbered but Word 2007 automatically numbers them.  I've tried everything but I always disturb the numbered pages I do want.

Please help!  Many thanks for taking the time to read my question.

suzanne s. barnhill say:
Doug has told you how to do what you ask, but I just consider it illogical and unwise to have any unnumbered pages in a document; they don't have to display the number, but if they're there, they should be counted. Word is pretty picky about this, too: if it thinks you're duplexing (because you've enabled "Mirror margins" or "Different odd and even") and you try to start a new section at page 1, and the previous section ends with an odd page, Word will insert a blank even page even though your section start is "Next page" rather than "Odd page." Word knows that odd-numbered pages must be recto (right-hand) pages.
One of my clients frequently deals with documents that have unnumbered pages. Usually such documents have been Bates-stamped, so there is a numerical reference even for the unnumbered pages, but it just adds confusion when the unnumbered pages have not been included in the page count, so that the Bates numbers don't correspond to the printed page numbers.
If the blank page is basically just a separator sheet, and you're compiling a single document out of what appear to be several separate documents, each starting at 1, then I have no problem with omitting the numbering, but if the pages before and after the blank page are going to be continuously numbered (that is, the page after the blank sheet has the number that follows the number of the page before the blank sheet), then I would advise against this.

This used to be what Microsoft calls a "known issue". Basically, when you use the right-click on the desktop or in Windows Explorer and choose New Word Document, Word didn't use the Normal.dotm template to create the document. Instead, it used the default settings built into the program.

According to the comments at the end of http://word.herbtyson.com/?p=92, getting the latest updates to Office 2007 should result in a permanent fix
