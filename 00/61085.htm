patrick barker say: Hi,

If chkdsk is stuck well after 72 hours, the only option you have left to do is force a shut down as it's not going to finish because it's hung. The reason for it in the first place being hung can be because the hard disk itself is faulty, or there was just a bug (not too uncommon).

If you force a shut down, what will likely occur is it will just prompt a chkdsk again. If it gets stuck again, it's safe to say at that point to hard disk is on its way out and if you can access a different computer to hook it up to temporarily to collect and back up any wanted data, that would be a good choice.

You can also run Seatools on it (DOS and Windows) but even if you get passes in Seatools a hung chkdsk over and over usually always implies the disk itself is faulty.

Patrick
