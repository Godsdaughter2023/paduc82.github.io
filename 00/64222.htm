If the disk check runs when your system starts, it either means you scheduled a chkdsk to run on the next restart by responding n the affirmative to the question:

Would you like to schedule this volume to be checked the next time the system restarts? (Y/N)

Or it could be that your C drive has been marked "dirty" - probably dues to a power interruption, aborted restart or ungraceful shutdown.  XP checks the dirty bit on every volume when it starts up and will automatically run a disk check with error correction on any dirty volumes (this is a good thing).

If the drive has been marked dirty, the only way to clear the dirty bit is to let the disk check with error correction finish and hope that it will clear the dirty bit when it is done.  Sometimes it will not and you will then need to run chkdsk again or run it from the XP Recovery Console and hope that will clear it.

The disk checking could take a long time depending on what the size of your hard drive, the amount of data on it and what the chkdsk finds to do.

Let the chkdsk finish and correct any problems it might find.  

It may take a long time for chkdsk to complete or it may appear to be 'stuck'.  Be patient.  If the HDD light is still flashing, chkdsk is doing something.  Keep an eye on the percentage amount to be sure it is still making progress.  It may even appear to go backwards sometimes. 

If you become impatient and interrupt it or restart your system before chkdsk can clear the dirty bit on the volume, it is just going to run again. 

Are you seeing a message to press and key to skip the disk checking when your system starts?  If so, press any key to skip the disk checking and figure it out later or wait it out and see how things look when it finishes.

Note that when looking in Add/Remove programs, you can't rely on those Size, Last Used On date and Frequency of use comments.  Also note that you can click the 'Sort by' drop down to sort the  things in a certain order, but don't count on those fields being accurate.

For example, I use Microsoft Office and Adobe Dreamweaver every day and all day long, but Add/Remove Programs says they are both rarely used, which is totally wrong - so Add/Remove Programs doesn't know what it is talking about and I don't believe it - and neither should you.

anil kumar b say:

Hi Rick,

 

-Do you have any Sound Effects enabled (in the RealTek HD Audio Manager)? If you have any disable it.

 

Make sure your speakers are correctly connected to your computer. Many computers have three or more jacks that connect to a sound card or sound processor, including a microphone jack, line-in jack, and line-out jack. Your speakers should be plugged in to the line-out jack. If you're not sure which jack this is, try plugging your speakers in to each of the jacks to see if any of them produce sound. In some cases, you can work around the problem by disabling optional speaker effects that are known as enhancements. If disabling speaker effects solves the problem, you might need to update your audio driver.

 

If these steps don't fix the problem, check the information that came with your computer or speakers, or go to the manufacturer's website.
