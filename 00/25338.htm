talking moose say:

I’m testing this with Office Insider version 15.25 and I do receive results when using the “Find a Contact” field.

While I don’t have a specific answer for you, I can say the lookup you do when composing a message is different from the search you do when searching for a contact.

Outlook uses a lookup to find a simple name and email address from your Exchange server.

It uses a search to find full contact information from your organization’s Directory Service server, which is specified in your Exchange account settings.

Outlook can also cache an offline version of your organization’s contacts to your computer as an Offline Address Book (OAB). That comes from your Exchange server.

So, lookups are working but searches aren’t working.

If you don’t have a Directory Service server specified in your account settings, contact your administrator for the address of a Global Catalog server in your organization’s network.

Or try forcing a download of your OAB using AppleScript. Paste this into Script Editor found in /Applications/Utilities and run it:

tell application "Microsoft Outlook"
tell exchange account "Exchange account name"
download oab now
end tell
end tell

You won’t really see any feedback. Give Outlook a while to connect with your Exchange server and download a copy of the OAB.

Hope this helps!

Although Phillip's general suggestion to confirm & address why MERP is being triggered [assuming it is] is valid, the program can be disabled.

  1. Quit all Microsoft apps
  2. Go to HD:Library:Application Support:Microsoft:MERP2.0
