thewarisnotover say: I found a work-around! This is what you need to do:

1. Click in the top right corner on the "gear wheel" to go to settings.
2. Then click on "Worldwide" on the top of the screen. 
3. Then click on "United States - English" (this will bring you to the U.S. version of Bing)
4. Click on the "gear wheel" in the top right again to go to settings once more. 
5. Now click on "History" on the left. 
6. Now click on "Clear all" on the right.
7. Click on "Yes, clear all history"
8. And the history will be cleared, without the error occurring. 

To get back to your original language, click on "Settings" on the left. Click on "Worldwide" at the top of the screen and click on your country/language. You'll get back to Bing in your language and the history will be gone.

MS Word (Table of Contents - multilevel) - Microsoft Community

a. user say:

MS Word (Table of Contents - multilevel)

In my Table of Contents, how do I get the third level of chapters (1.1.1) to indent and lose that tab space?

Image attached to indicate the current issue.

Thanks

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

Select your table of contents and then go to the References tab of the ribbon and from the Table of Contents dropdown, select Insert Table of Contents

Then in the Table of Contents dialog, click on Modify

Then in the Style dialog that appears, select each of the TOC # styles that are being used (probably 1 - 3), in turn and then click on Modify

Then from the Format dropdown in the Modify Style dialog, click on Tabs

Then in the Tabs dialog, click on Clear All and the insert a suitable position for the beginning of the text (1.25 cm was used hear, then click on Set, then insert the position for a right aligned tab stop with a dotted underline (16.5 cm) and click on Set

Repeat for each of the other TOC # styles.  Then when you get back to the Table of Contents dialog and click on OK and are asked if you want to replace the existing table of contents, click on OK.

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

You will need to set a tab stop at the desired position in the TOC 3 style.  Alternatively, modify the multi-level list so that the level assigned to the style that is used for those paragraphs contains a space after the numbers, rather than a tab space.

Note, the default TOC # styles, use the default tab stops and the spacing that you are getting at the moment results from the heading number extending past the tab stop at which the text of the other entries is aligned, so that the text for the Heading 3 entries is aligned at the next tab stop.  If you want the text for all of the entries to be aligned at the save horizontal position, you will need to set a tab stop in the TOC 2 style as well.

gloria gong msft say:

Hi Nekenpongen,

For the Allow Trusted Locations on the network, when I select true, the "Allow Trusted Locations on my network (not recommended)" will be available. (If select false or not configured, the option is greyed out)

File> Options> Trust Center> Trust Center Settings...> Trusted Locations.

For the workgroup templates path, I have found an article for your reference: Workgroup templates path

I type following path to workgroup templates path:

HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Policies\Microsoft\Office\16.0\common\general\sharedtemplates

Then I export the xml file, download and install Office using ODT.

setup.exe /download configuration.xml

setup.exe /configure configuration.xml

Best Regards,

Gloria

It is not feasible to add the trusted location from the config.office.com directly, you can only configure to enable "Allow Trusted Locations on the network" or not.

You can leave a comment to Office 365 UserVoice to let the product team know that you need to configure the trusted locations for users.
