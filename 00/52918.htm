streaming - Microsoft Community

lizzylady say:

streaming

when watching u-tube or other media live clips or even tv programs via hulu, streaming is an issue. video starts/stops all the way through

cwdjr say:
when watching u-tube or other media live clips or even tv programs via hulu, streaming is an issue. video starts/stops all the way through

Probably something anywhere from the server to your computer is slowing the download of the video to your computer so much that it can not keep up with the playing speed of the video. The video then must stop and load more before it can start playing again. In the case of serious slowdowns, the video may start and stop many times. For modern flv/flash video, such as often is used by sites such as you mentioned, if you will wait until the video plays through and do not move away from the site, the video then often will play through without stopping because it has been downloaded to a temporary cache on your computer.

A dialup connection often is too slow for reliable video playback. A cable or DSL connection usually will play the types of video you mentioned  without problems. However there can be slowdowns on your isp connection  caused by many possible things. The server or isp connections may be overloaded - check to see if you still have the problem late at night when most are not watching video. Since you are having the problem on several sites, I doubt if overload of several different servers is the problem. I have a video at http://www.cwdjr.net/flash/Fatty.php that should  stream without stopping on most decent broadband cable or DSL connections. I checked it just before answering you and it was not having problems. Watch the control slider at the bottom  of the video which gives the playback progress. Just to the right of the slider there is a thin dark line that shows download progress. As the video plays, this line should keep ahead of the slider.  If the slider catches up with the line, download is too slow to keep up with the playing speed of the video and the video must stop until more download is stored in the temporary cache.

It is possible that your computer, especially if an older one, is causing the slowdown. Run any virus scan or malware programs that you may have. Some hacker programs can make heavy use of computer resources resulting in slowdowns. Also be sure you are not running  other heavy usage programs at the same time you are watching videos.

Shadow copy is a point-in-time copy of a replica stored on the Data Protection Manager (DPM) server. You can access the shadow copies to recover previous versions of files in the event of data loss or corruption.
