How do I maintain the basic margins in a word document, but create - Microsoft Community

alexawoods say:

How do I maintain the basic margins in a word document, but create sections with margins I indent to my specifications?

What I said above.  I am writing a piece in which I am using extended quotations.  I need to indent them exactly 5 spaces.  I haven't found Word options that allow me to do that.  Suggestions?

a. user say:

There are two ways you could achieve something along these lines, depending on exactly what you want:
 a. Define a paragraph style (Format->Style...) with the indents you need, and apply it to all the paragraphs in the quotations. Indents are defined in terms of inches/cm (whatever your measurement system is set up as) so you will need to work out what "5 spaces" corresponds to.
 b. use Word sections to differentiate between the "unquoted" text and "quoted" text. e.g. if you Insert->Break to insert a Section Break (Next Page). Then click in the new section and use the "Document" section of the formatting palette to specify the margins you want for the section. (It can be a continuous section). 

I would generally opt for (a) for a variety of reasons, e.g. it is much easier to apply a paragraph style than to ensure that the margins of every section are set correctly.

daniel g. grau say:
When you create a document, it is defined with default page margins, as specified in the template used to create it. For documents created through Command N (File > New Blank Document), that template is Notmal.dotm.

You define page margins through Format > Document > Margins. Think of them as a blank frame around the page where text should never print:





In a document, the Ruler (View > Ruler) shows paragraph margins as distances relative to the page margins. They are indicated by three blue triangles, which are what you need to move to indent your quotations:



Again, keep in mind that paragraphs indents are relative to the page margins (the never-print-here area). Changing the page size or the page margins will yield narrower or wider paragraphs overall, but the will always keep their indents.

Private Sub CommandButton3_Click()

kboeds say:

how do I require first letter Cap in a second word?

I have created a city field and entered >L????????? as an input mask to require first letter CAP. However, there are cities with two parts like Baton Rouge. How do I write the input mask to...

1. Not limit the number of characters in the first word?

2. Know the first letter of the second word needs to be Caps too?

 

Thanks for any advise offered

Kathy

john p spencer say:

You can't do this with an input mask.

You could use some VBA code in the after update event to automatically change the capitalization to proper case.

Private Sub txtCity_AfterUpdate()

   Me.txtCity = StrConv(Me.txtCity,vbProperCase)
End Sub

That will work in most cases (not all cases) to change values like New YORK or new york or NEW york to New York.

john w. vinson say:

That's certainly how I'd do it. Unfortunately it will also turn a correctly entered McDermitt to Mcdermitt.

I don't know of anything (other than a pre-entered list) that will do this without some errors. This may be the reason that the Post Office standards suggest putting city names in ALL CAPS, even as ugly as that looks. If you want to go that way change the vbProperCase to vbUpperCase.
