iymalive say:

In the PCI Express power management options there are 2 levels you can choose from.

The difference between them are power savings versus the time it takes to recover from the sleep state (Latency).

 

If you select  Moderate Power Savings, the power savings are less, but the time to recover from the Sleep state (latency) is much shorter. So, it won't save as much power, but it will be quicker when you try to restart your work.

If you select the Maximum Power Savings, the power savings are greater, but the time to recover from the Sleep state (latency) is much longer, so it will use less power but will take longer when you try to restart your work.

I found this information in one of the other forums, I hope it helps someone out.

louisflood say:

Gigabyte gtx 970 g1 using both shared and dedicated memory.

so i just updated to windows 10 pro and i checked my display properties and found that my computer is using both dedicated and shared graphics memory for a toal of 8gb of graphics memory. 4gb from the graphics card and then 4 from my 8gb of system ram. is this good/bad?

gmreader say:

Hello,

 It's good, since your PC isn't actually using the RAM unless it's needed and unless it's free.

 DirectX 12 improves shared memory allocation, which allows:

  1. Using RAM as VRAM.
  2. Adding VRAM from 2 GFX cards to get more VRAM.
  3. Using different GFX cards together. eg. Using the power of both a NVIDIA and of an AMD card (not yet possible on current drivers).

 In the end, this means possibly less stutter in many games, but remember that your 4GB of VRAM are the fastest part of that 8GB total.
