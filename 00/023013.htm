a j kuehner say:

I'm aware that this is an old thread, but this suggestion may help someone else who is seeking answers to a problem similar to this.

I find it worth verifying that the simple things are set right:

Check your computer's time zone settings. If you recently upgraded your OS, installed a major service release or changed to a new PC, or perhaps a low CMOS battery or other power issue occurred, it's possible that your PC's clock may be in the wrong time zone, even though it shows what looks like the correct time for your location.

Also, make sure that your PC is appropriately set to Daylight Savings Time (or not) if your zone supports that. Simply setting your PC clock ahead or behind one hour without telling it that DST is applied (or not) can be problematic when your time zone requires such a setting.

I've seen many odd networking problems happen due to PC clocks not being set correctly, including refusal to provide services or lengthy delays in service responses.

To remove this annoying feature, create a new e-mail message and from the insert menu, select "Quick parts" and then "Auto text".
