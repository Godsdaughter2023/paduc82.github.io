charles kenyon say:

I agree that it appears your are deleting the section break before your Appendix (Annex?).

Section breaks are a bit counterintuitive in that they contain the formatting for the section that precedes them rather than for the section that follows.

First, work in a copy of your document or save a backup first.

After you have displayed the non-printing characters in Word, go to immediately before that last section break.

Showing non-printing formatting marks in Microsoft Word

Insert a new (continuous) section break just before that.

Go past that section mark to the one just before the Appendix.

Delete to the end of the document.

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

It is only the initial save of a document that kills the undo/redo process. Once the document has been saved, a subsequent save will not kill the ability to undo changes made since the initial save.

If you want to be able to return the present situation of a document, you should always save before carrying out any other action.

Hi,

The easy manual way is to use an AutoFilter on column F or H to show only zero/empty cells (depending on what formula you have used in those columns to hide the zeros).

 

You can then select the rows and delete then before removing the autofilter.

 

Select the whole table including the column headers first, then do Data tab > AutoFilter.

Click the dropdown in F1 (or H1) and then uncheck "(Select All)" then check zero or "(Blanks)". OK.

Select the remaining rows and delete them.
