Recoverable error signature becomes invalid after closing and reopening word 2010

I have an issue with signing by smart card in Microsoft Word 2010 and Windows 7.
When I sign a word document, the signature panel displays the signature as 'Recoverable error'.
It means OK for me, because no trusted CA root certificate is installed yet.

But the problem arises after closing and reopening the signed document.
Now the signature panel displays the signature as 'Invalid' but neither the certificate is revoked nor the content signed has been altered.

I opened this document with Word 2010 on Windows XP, the signature is displayed as 'Recoverable Error'.

Can anyone explain what is the reason of this problem?
raha_b say: I solved the problem.
In the function CardSignData() of smart card minidriver, hash OID must be added to the hash before sending to the card for signing.
It is necessary for Word 2010 but optional for Word 2007. This is the reason why Word 2010 recognizes the signature as Valid at first and then marks the signature as invalid after reopening the file (Word 2010 needs OID to recognize the hash algorithm but it does not exist in the signature).
Word 2007 knows the hash algorithm and doesn't expect the OID to be included in the signature.

After adding OID to the hash, the problem is resolved.
Now Word 2010 verifies the signatures created by Word 2007 but NOT vice versa - probably because of backward compatibility problem!?

john korchok say:

Putting 2 different designs in 1 presentation seems cumbersome and error-prone, especially when you're just getting the hang of it. Why not just 2 separate templates?

You mention 2 ways of creating the slides. For the first "insert and select custom layout", I imagine you're using the Home>New Slide command and selecting the Slide Layout. This approach should be dead simple, so if you're not getting the bullets, fonts or typesizes you expect, you haven't formatted the Slide Master or the Slide Layouts correctly. First set up the Slide Master (the larger slide at the top of the list in Slide Master view) with all formatting that is common to most or all slides. This is the parent, the slide layouts are children: the master formatting ripples through automatically to the layouts. Then format the variations in the slide layouts.

When you're bringing in slides from another source, then you really give up any control over choosing whether Master A or Master B should apply. Separate files for each design will make this a workable scenario.

When importing from another presentation, you might be using Insert>Slides From>Other Presentation (uncheck keep design of original slide), or you might be pasting the slides (click on clipboard icon in lower right after pasting and choose Use Designation Theme. If you're already doing that and the formatting is still off, it points again to problems with your Master/Slide Layout setup.
