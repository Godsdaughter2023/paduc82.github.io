I am creating a complicated Venn Diagram using powerpoint, and as part of this I need to have gradient lines on the borders of the circles in this, so that the inner part of the line is dark and the outer light.

I have found various gradient line options in powerpoint, including one that says it is radial from the center that seemed the most likely candidate, however, none of the options seem to give this effect. Is it possible to do this?

p.s. I was wondering if there's a way of filling in an individual sector of the diagram, so that it can be highlighted.

Thanks.

john korchok say:

A radial gradient is possible. The reason you're not seeing the gradient is that one end of the gradient is the center of the circle. The line border of the circle may only be 1% of the circle diameter. So the line appears to be only one color. You can get the effect you're after is you make the line very thick, then bunch up the gradient sliders so they're nearly on top of one another.

It may be easier to make 3 or 4 concentric circles, each slightly larger and lighter, then group them. The visual effect would be much the same.

john korchok say:

The symptoms you describe fit a background graphic that is behind the text, but not in the header/footer. I believe the graphic is anchored to your text and is positioned relative to it, so as you type text, the graphic moves down.

If the graphic was in the header/footer, it would appear on all pages, not just one. If a header/footer graphic moved on one page, it would move on all.

Try selecting Word>Preferences>View. In the Show in Document section, check Object anchors. OK out and select your background graphic. An anchor icon should appear showing which paragraph it is anchored to. Use Edit>Cut, double-click on the header to open the header/footer layer and click in the header so you see your cursor blinking there. Use Edit>Paste to paste the graphic. It should now appear on all pages in the same position and be unaffected by your text.
