mikegray say:

Can I rename "Heading 1" to "VR_U1"??

Aaargh.

I'm working with a publisher who is demanding that I cease and desist using strange styles with outlandish names like "Heading 1" or "Heading 2." They don't just want me to format the standards to their liking. Instead, they have sent me a Word document containing a set of heading-like styles entitled VR_U1, VR_U2, etc. 

They want me to use their styles instead. 

Oh yeah - and they also want automatic TOC generation.

Naturally, I'm trying to convince them to just let me format "Heading 1" to match VR_U1. That's easy! But I'm getting a lot of skepticism about that. They want their styles, with their names. (I'd bet the farm this has something to do with some early paleolithic page setting program ... )

So ... can I do this? Is there any way of actually renaming a standard style?? 
suzanne s. barnhill say:
Since the VR_U1 style is already present, you would need to delete it (which you should be able to do, since it's a "user defined" style). Before you do this, however, create a VR_U1 paragraph and place the insertion point in it. Then, in the Styles pane, find the Heading 1 style, right-click, and choose "Update Heading 1 to Match Selection." This will reformat Heading 1 to match VR_U1. You can then delete VR_U1 and rename Heading 1 with this alias.
