entegy say:

When you rapidly move far away from your usual log in places, the login is deemed suspicious and the account needs to be verified.

The only way to override a verification is to turn on two-step authentication. 2SA either sends a code to your cell phone immediately, or you have an authenticator app on a smartphone generating codes on behalf of your account. The app solution works because you don't need a network connection for that, whereas you might get charged for receiving a text message while roaming.

I will say up front: Yes, it IS a bit of extra pain. But the security you gain from using it is completely worth it. You can read a bit more about it here.

The reason why 2SA allows you to override verification is because even if someone else has your password, they cannot login to your account on a new device without the code from your phone.

if you want to turn it on, post back and we'll walk through it together.
