john w. vinson say: First off, don't use the reserved word GROUP as a field name (it's part of the SQL syntax "GROUP BY" and can mess things up). If you can't change the fieldname then ALWAYS enclose it in square brackets.

The GROUP BY expression needs to use the actual fieldname for the date field. Your post is confusing - is the field named [OccuranceDate] or [Date] or [Offense Date] or [OffenseDate]? The fact that you're being prompted suggests that your SQL references a fieldname which does not exist in the table.

I would also move the criterion from the HAVING clause (which is applied after all the sorting and grouping is done) to the WHERE clause.

hansv mvp say:

NETWORKDAYS counts a number of days - it results in 3 or 4 for example.

Unless you specify otherwise, 3 days = 3 * 24 hours = 72 hours and 4 days = 4 * 24 hours = 96 hours.

To take 8-hour workdays into account, change the formula to

 

=((NETWORKDAYS(I2,J2,Holidays)-1)*8/24+MOD(J2,1)-MOD(I2,1))

 

But in that case, the next-to-last example will result in 7 instead of 23 hours...
