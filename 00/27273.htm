john korchok say:

Ah, yes, the footnote separator. Here's the arcane method required:

  1. With the document open in Word, click on the View tab and click on Draft View.
  2. Click on the Review tab, then on Show Notes. A split window opens with the Footnote dropdown set to All Footnotes.
  3. Change that dropdown to Footnote Separator.
  4. The actual separator is a mystery character that acts neither like text nor like a drawing object. But you probably don't need to bother with it, because the space below the separator is probably caused by paragraph spacing.
  5. Click just to the right of the separator and adjust the paragraph spacing, perhaps taking out space after.

                                                                                                      Page 1 of 1                                                                   &

It will not stay that way.  Do I need to create a text box for the Ref. Number, or is there a setting to hit so it stays there?

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

By default, the footer of a document, or any Section of it, contains a centre-aligned tab stop at the horizontal centre of the space between the margins and a right aligned tab stop at the right margin .  Therefore, you need to press the Tab key once to move the selection to the Center tab stop and then, by inserting the necessary text and appropriate fields, insert the "Page # of #".  Then press the tab key to move the selection to the tab stop at the right margin and insert the text for your reference.

If for some reason those tab stops are not there, you can insert them in the required positions.
