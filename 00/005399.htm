Okay, here's my take on the build (Lumia 635):

I basically use my phone for college work, calling, texting, and the occasional game. I do not use skype or any video call apps. I found some bugs that would be more of an occasional annoyance than a real show-stopper. Here's the list:

Battery saver: live tile works, but it doesn't refresh until you open the battery saver app.

Cortana: Does not have a live tile yet.

Pictures: this build will not see photos on your SD card when choosing backgrounds unless they are in the same folder structure as on your main phone (Pictures/Camera Roll OR Pictures/Saved Pictures)

Overall, I can say that this is a very stable build. I'm glad I installed it.

david156 say: Oh,

One more very important point that come to mind for those of you who experience extremely long initial-lock-on, this may help:

>> Make sure your time zone, local time and estimate of initial position (which country, state) are set correctly.

GPS has it's own GPS time, which most receivers translate to UTC (Universal Time Coordinate) time. The receiver uses this information to compute the location of the GPS space vehicle constellation, so that it can estimate which of the 24 satellite are most likely above you. The receiver need to perform a blind search the very first time you move from one location to another that are far apart (Search-the-sky, or STS). Giving the receiver accurate time and good estimate of initial position will go a long way to speed up initial search. This is especially important if you are on a fast moving train.

The best way to make sre you have the right time, is to set your handset to receive network time.
