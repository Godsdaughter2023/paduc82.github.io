considerate_guy say: Think of your project like a recipe.  It might contain the words "eggs," "flour," and "sugar" as some of the ingredients, but the printed recipe doesn't contain the actual ingredients, just the instructions for putting things together.  (It would be silly for a cookbook to come with a copy of every single ingredient within the pages of the book.  It would make for a really big book, and possibly multiple copies of the same ingredients.)

Similarly, your WLMM project just contains a list of ingredients (photos, videos, music), but it expects you to tell it where to find them on your computer.  If you move any of those things around, WLMM is unable to find those things anymore.  It's like you decided to take the sugar, eggs and flour from your kitchen and decided to put them all in the garage.  Now when it comes time to try and bake your cake, WLMM can't find the ingredients.  You can either put everything back where WLMM expects to find them, or you can click the 'Fix' button and tell Movie Maker that you moved them to the garage to go look for them there instead.

Along the same lines, your final movie is the result of following all the instructions in the recipe (project) to bake your cake.  Once everything is in the cake you can share that with your family and friends.  Happy baking!  (I mean movie making!)
