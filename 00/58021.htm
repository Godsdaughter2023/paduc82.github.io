This happens with songs, games, even widows sounds like when emptying the trash bin or executing a file as an administrator.

The issues I described don't happen when set to 24bit 48000Hz. My entire audio pipeline is set at 96000Hz and I really need to get it working.

The DPC latency doesn't show anything abnormal with an average of 50u and sporadic spikes to 120u.

The system is running an MSI x299 SLI PLUS with a Realtek ALC 1220, the board comes with an impedance sensor. 

Realtek driver 6.0.1.8245 from my motherboard's support site, I also tried the latest version 6.0.1.8264 but both have the same issue.

[Moved from:Windows / Windows 10 / Devices & drivers]

artins90 say:

I have managed to narrow down the issue now and I found a temporary work around, it works but it's not ideal.
Basically, Windows sends the audio driver in sleep state D3 after 5 seconds of sound inactivity, this is a normal behavior.
The problem is that the driver doesn't come back from the sleep state on time, it takes too long to wake up and all the sound that is supposed to be played in that time frame gets covered by popping.

The workaround I found is here: https://www.tenforums.com/drivers-hardware/96335-after-updating-fcu-i-am-experiencing-sound-popping-issues.html#post1183133

The keys regulating the sleep state are: 

Computer\HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Control\Class\{4d36e96c-e325-11ce-bfc1-08002be10318}\0007\PowerSettings

ConservationIdleTime is for laptops when they are low on power                                                    05 00 00 00             
IdlePowerState tells the sound card which state to switch to, sadly 0 is not an accepted value       03 00 00 00 
PerformanceIdleTime is for desktop pcs and laptops under charge, which is what applies to me     05 00 00 00 

What I did was setting the 5 seconds inactivity timer to ff ff ff ff via registry key, the normal value is 05 00 00 00.
Now the sound driver never goes to sleep and the popping is gone.

There is nothing wrong with my audio hardware setup and the issue happens even after resetting the CMOS at stock CPU clocks. 
According to another user of the MSI forums with my same setup, when he installs the driver on his system, the driver writes 00 00 00 00 instead of 05 00 00 00. I wonder why the driver behaves differently on our systems, we have the same CPU, motherboard, ram amount, and OS.

Delivery to the following recipients failed.


The Diagnostic-Code will often explain the difficulty.


To see the source of the DSN, select it in the message list and press Ctrl-F3. If you can't make sense of the NDR, paste it into your reply here.
