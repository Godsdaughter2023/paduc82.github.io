bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

Without seeing the document, my guess is that the 'gaps' you're seeing are due to paragraphs [¶]which have no text content but have been formatted with one of the Heading styles. In all probability, the empty ¶s have been used for spacing purposes (which typically is not recommended). If you click one of the gaps you should find that the insertion marker jumps directly to the corresponding empty paragraph. When you delete the empty ¶ the gap representing it will be removed from the Document Map listing.

Alternatively, you can use the Clear Formatting listing at the top of the Styles pane in the Toolbox which will eliminate the gap in the Document Map but leave the empty ¶ in the document. If a spacing adjustment is wanted, however, it would be better to modify the paragraph attribute of the Heading style to add Space After. Empty ¶s can cause all sorts of irregularities & issues with text flow.

AFAIK there is no means of changing the spacing the headings listed in the Document Map pane... You can modify the Document Map style to change certain Font attributes, but other modifications are not recognized (even though they're allowed to be made).

guessed say: This is related to why the paragraph appears at the top of the page - was it forced there or just pushed because of preceding content. For instance, the first Heading might follow a new page section break whereas the second one couldn't fit on the page. In the first instance Word would honour the paragraph spacing before, in the second it doesn't. There are also some page layout options which may come into play.
