anna bon say:

Hello,

This could be a relay-prohibited error message. Relaying occurs when an email message is sent to an email address whose domain is not processed by the Simple Mail Transfer Protocol (SMTP) or outgoing server that the sender is requesting to deliver the message. The SMTP server must connect to another SMTP server to relay the message. Your ISP might authorize access to the SMTP server to people who are not connected directly to the network. Each time that you connect, you're not assured of having the same IP address. Your ISP can authorize connections from those IP addresses as approved users.

You must send the message through your current connection's server. This means if you're at work or away from home and not using your ISP to connect to the Internet, and you want to send a message from your home ISP email account, you must change your email account settings to specify the SMTP server used at your location, for example, your work SMTP server.

jay freedman say:

In Word 2013 and 2016, you can click a little triangle next to each paragraph that's in a Heading style to collapse or expand all the text below that heading (down to but not including the next heading of the same outline level). That isn't available in Print Layout view in 2010 or earlier, although you can do it if you switch to Outline view.

An alternative is to mark the paragraph as Hidden format, and then turn on or off the display of Hidden text. However, that will collapse or expand all paragraphs that are marked as Hidden, not just one at a time.

The page at http://gregmaxey.com/word_tip_pages/toggle_data_display.html gives several methods, all of which use macros to control the display.

.Range.Characters.Last.InsertFile
already tells Word to insert the content at the end of the document. As for why the code doesn't work, my guess is that's something to do with TypeName(aCtl). TypeName isn't a standard VBA method, so I assumed it's a user-defined function you haven't posted. If your checkboxes are named CheckBox1, CheckBox2, etc., you might replace:
If TypeName(aCtl) = "CheckBox" And sDocName <> "" And aCtl = True Then
with:
If (aCtl.Name Like "Checkbox#*") And (sDocName <> "") And (aCtl.Value = True) Then
