If not, is there a way to write a macro to find and highlight or circle the newly invalid data?

Penny

rik_uk say:

You could use conditional formatting to highlight the 'now-invalid' job names. eg

Select the range of job names that utilize data validation (not the valitaton list itself) and put this as the equation to test in the conditional formatting for those cells

=ISERROR(MATCH(A1,$B$1:$B$10,0)) and then select the format.

Where A1 is the first cell in your selection, and $B$1:$B$10 represents the validation list (which of course could be a named range)...

So if the job name in the validation list is removed or changed it will highlight the ones that are invalid in the other range.

Guys,

This is a well known issue many Exchange users can experience when using a MIME based mail client, be it on their computer machines or phone devices. IF you are connecting to an Exchange Server, then your Admin can resolve the issue for you by just creating/activating a reg key on your server. The following 2 articles provide the full step by step resolution for this issue for Exchange 2000, 2003 & 2007 Servers. This fix was produced by Microsoft realizing that it will be harder and time consuming for users to be able to get such a fix from their MIME mail client app providers, like Nokia in this case and may take a whole lot of time for Nokia (& other MIME client vendors) to produce/provide a fix to their customers.

 

Now, if you are experiencing this issue with Nokia connecting to a non-Exchange based mail system, you sure do need to talk to Nokia to fix this issue for you.
