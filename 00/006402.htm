Filtering your list:
Above the search box at the top of the Contact list on the left, is a filter option which says "All ". 
If you click on this you should see the groups you created in the past.  
Click on any of these to filter your list to just the contacts in that group.
Reselect "All" again if you need to see the whole list.

Selecting one or many Contacts:
If you click on a contact name in the list, the details for that contact will appear on the right.  
Alternatively, if you click on the check box to the left of the contact picture, you can build up a list of selected contacts on the right by clicking on the check box to the left of multiple contacts.

Adding/Removing from Group membership:
Whether you select one or many contacts, you will see a top menu bar option for Groups.  
If you click on this, then it will display the list of groups with a check box checked for each group that the contact(s) is/are a member of. 

You can uncheck to remove or check to add, the selection of contact(s) to the chosen groups, and then click on Apply to apply the changes.  
Your selected contacts will then be updated to be added or removed from the groups you chose as appropriate.

Delete
When you select one or many contacts, you will see a top menu bar option for Delete.  
If you click on Delete and click Delete in the confirmation box, the selected contacts will be deleted.

Edit:
You can choose edit if you have clicked on a single contact (name or picture) in the list on the left to see their details on the right.  
When viewing their details, click on Edit in the top menu bar to edit or add details.

  1. Go to your People page.

  3. Click Edit in the command bar on top of the page.
  4. To delete a member of the group, click the X button at the bottom right of your contact's picture.

=IF(A2<TIMEVALUE("2:00:00"),"Night", IF(A2<TIMEVALUE("6:00:00"),"Early", IF(A2<TIMEVALUE("10:00:00"),"Morning",IF(A2<TIMEVALUE("14:00:00"),"Noon",IF(A2<TIMEVALUE("18:00:00"),"Afternoon",IF(A2<TIMEVALUE("22:00:00"),"Evening","Night"))))))

 =IF(A2<=TIME(1,59,0),"Night",VLOOKUP(A2,Sheet2!$A$2:$B$7,2,1))

lars-Åke aspelin say:

If you are not particularly fond of formulas like this one (no extra blanks allowed in cell A1):

=SUMIFS(count,profitrank,100,INDIRECT(LEFT(A1,FIND(",",A1)-1)),MID(A1,FIND(",",A1)+3,FIND(",",A1,FIND(",",A1)+1)-FIND(",",A1)-4),
INDIRECT(MID(A1,FIND(",",A1,FIND(",",A1)+1)+2,FIND(",",A1,FIND(",",A1,FIND(",",A1)+1)+1)-FIND(",",A1,FIND(",",A1)+1)-2)),
MID(A1,FIND(",",A1,FIND(",",A1,FIND(",",A1)+1)+1)+2,LEN(A1)-2-FIND(",",A1,FIND(",",A1,FIND(",",A1)+1)+1)))

I would suggest that you put your four "parameters", i.e.  segment, champ, score, and 100 (no quotes!) in four different cells, e.g. A2, B2, C2, and D2.

The SUMIFS formula can then be written as this:

=SUMIFS(count,profitrank,100,INDIRECT(A2),B2,INDIRECT(C2),D2)

which is much more easy to understand and maintain.

If you want you can put TRIM() around each of the references, e.g. INDIRECT(TRIM(A2)) to be less sensitive to leading and trailing blanks in the input.

Hope this helps / Lars-Åke

Howt o make Win + E Open "This PC"...

Well basicly you can delete everything in 'Home' and attach 'This Pc' into it [Afaik not possible to change Home, yet]:

1. Press Windows and type 'regedit'

2. Right-click and open 'As Administrator'.

3. Navigate to:      HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Explorer\HomeFolder\NameSpace\DelegateFolders

4. Delete everything there (Looks like this {2141241-BLABLA}).

5. Right-click on DelegateFolders -> New -> Key: {20D04FE0-3AEA-1069-A2D8-08002B30309D}
6. Click on the key and Double-click on (Default).
7. In Value Data type 'This Pc'.

[BTW: You can find other the keys in HKEY_CLASSES_ROOT\CLSID]

If you want to add the Desktop-folder then create a new key {B4BFCC3A-DB2C-424C-B029-7FE99A87C641} in NameSpace (!Not NameSpace\DelegateFolders)

If you get Unspecified FIles(6):

Look at:

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Explorer\MyComputer\NameSpace

..delete those. (except DelegateFolders)
