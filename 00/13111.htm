techfreak_ say:

Skype for Windows 10 has another component called Skype Host that runs in background to receive IMs and calls.  You can see it in your task manager.  This way the Skype App does not have to be open or on focus to perform such tasks.  If you do not want Skype for Windows 10 to be active to receive IMs and calls you would either change your status to "Do Not Disturb" or sign out of the Skype for Windows 10 App.  You can change your status or sign out of the App by clicking on your profile picture in the lower left pane of the App and selecting those options that appear.

The alternative to this is uninstalling the Skype for Windows 10 App and installing the latest Skype for Windows Desktop release.  I personally leave both installed and sign out of the Skype for Windows 10 App to default to using the Desktop release.  I also sign into Windows 10 locally to avoid any auto-sign in with my Microsoft account by ANY Windows App.

m.wewe say:

There are two processes running when Skype is active: SkypeHost.exe and SkypeApp.exe.

If you just kill SkypeApp.exe and leave SkypeHost running, you'll still be online with the status you set. That means even with the app completely closed and gone, you are still able to receive calls and messages.

If you kill SkypeHost.exe, you'll be offline, but not having tested it, you might have trouble re-starting the SkypeApp.

If you're completely done with Skype Windows, you can go to your settings and set the background tasks to not permit Skype to run in background. And/or you can uninstall the app by right-clicking its icon and selecting "uninstall"

I find the easiest thing to do when I don't want to be disturbed is change my status to "offline" and close the app. I haven't gotten calls in this mode.

Outlook for Mac does not have this feature. At most you can assign priority from the ribbon but nothing for sensitivity.

You first have to validate that the above is correct.

john - project say:

gogopogo,

Like Trevor, on the first read it wasn't clear exactly what you wanted, but after a few more reads, I get what you want to do.

Unfortunately you won't find a way to produce the report in graph form directly in Project, but you can use the following approach to produce the data at summary level, by creating three custom fields.

1. Number1=IIf([Milestone]=True And [% Complete]=100,1,0)

2, Number2=IIf([Milestone]=True,1,0)

3. Text1=IIf([Number2]>0,[Number1]/[Number2]*100 & " %","")

For the Number1 and Number2 fields calculate summary rows with  rollup "sum". For the Text1 field calculate summary rows with "use formula".

Then apply the filter "summary tasks".

If you want to get a graphical representation, you will need to export the data to Excel and graph it there, which is readily done with some VBA.

Hope this helps.
