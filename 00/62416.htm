yolvi ell say:

Hi Muhammad,

The Application Information service handles the launching of any system apps that require additional administrative privileges, and therefore disabling it is highly not recommended. To restart this service, boot into Safe Mode by following these steps:

  1. Click Start.
  2. Click the Power icon.
  3. Hold the Shift key and click Restart.
  4. After your computer restarts, click Troubleshoot.
  5. Choose Advanced options.
  6. Click Startup Settings.
  7. Click Restart.
  8. Press 4 or F4 to select Safe Mode.
  9. Once your PC boots into Safe Mode, click Start.
  10. Type services.msc and press Enter.
  11. Right-click on Application Information from the list and choose Start.
  12. Restart your computer to return your computer to normal.

Let us know if you successfully enable the Application Information service after following these steps.

[quote user="SamsTheGamer"]Now the problem stands that my non-hd doesnt display the video.I can only hear the sound.No gui problem as HDMI works on the HD tv.[/quote]

That awfully sounds as if the D/A video converter broke on your console.

The HDMI port can grab the video data digitally directly from the graphics hardware, however the AV cable has to have the video data first converted to an analogue signal.

Since you get sound, I assume the DAC of the console is faulty.

You can try the following: Turn the console off, and hold both the "Y" and "RT" buttons on the controller, and then press the silver Xbox Button on the same controller.

Hold the Y and RT buttons until you (hopefully) see the dashboard.

If it doesn't show up, you will have to send your console in for repairs.

Locating files that aren't showing up on the desktop - Microsoft Community

pattinelson say:

Locating files that aren't showing up on the desktop

Yesterday I had issues logging onto my computer and when I finally got on my desktop items and favorites were missing. I did some work and saved a PDF as well as an office document. Today I was able to lot on as normal and my old desktop items were there but not the two items from yesterday. How can I find them? I have windows XP.
wunders say: This sounds like a semi-corrupted user profile.

When you first had problems logging on, it was because Windows was having problems reading your profile.  It finally gave up loading your profile and created a *temporary* profile for you to use.  When it did this, your desktop items, Favorites, and anything inside your "My Documents" folder (among others) stayed with the failed profile.  You then worked and were able to create your pdf and Office document which were then saved in your temporary profile.  When you logged out, your temporary profile was deleted and with it the new files that you had just created.  Later you tried to log in again and this time it was successful in reading your original profile and you were back to where you were less the files that were deleted with your temporary profile.

Corrupt profiles are generally accompanied by or caused by a corrupted file system.  Your disk is marginal.  It can sometimes be read, and sometimes not.  To fix this before it gets worse, follow the instructions in the following article for recovering the corrupt profile.  Generally this will involve creating a new user and then transferring your old profile (or as much as possible) to your new, clean, profile.

"How to recover the damaged user profile in Windows XP"
  < http://support.microsoft.com/kb/555473 >

After recovering, you should run a 'chkdsk' operation with the /F or /R option (or both options if using GUI interface)  to find the problems in, and repair, your file system.  Note that this will be performed at next reboot, runs before Windows fully loads, can't be interrupted, and could take upwards of a couple of hours to perform.  Do it when your computer isn't needed for a while...

"How to Perform Disk Error Checking in Windows XP"
  < http://support.microsoft.com/kb/315265 >

P.S.  If you don't have a current backup created, consider this a wake-up call to update your backups.

HTH,
   JW

Cortana interaction on a PC in Windows 10

cmdrkeene say:

I think Joe Belfiore, the Corporate Vice President of the Operating Systems Group at Microsoft, put it best:  Cortana respects how you talk to her.  If you talk to her, she will speak her answer.  If you are trying to keep quiet and type your request, she will only respond by text.  So she bases how she responds based on your lead.  If you're in a meeting or don't want to talk (or you can't talk because you're on the phone or a conference call), then Cortana will keep quiet also.

So in short:  If you type, she types.  If you speak, she speaks.
