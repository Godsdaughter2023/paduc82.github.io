greginmich say:

We’ve actually had this one figured out for about a month now, but folks around here are a little slow to catch on when it comes to technical things: In quite a few third-party AV installs, Windows Defender isn’t getting properly switched off in its registry key, and this causes a group of symptoms that includes a failure to start the service with Error 0x8007139f (in the UI); and a failure to update Windows Defender definitions with Error 0x80070643.

If you uninstall McAfee, run its cleanup utility, and then reinstall it, this should update the relevant Windows Defender registry entries and resolve the issue. But if you don’t want to bother with that, you can use Group Policy to completely turn off Windows Defender. In the Professional edition, you can do this with the Group Policy Editor; and in the Home edition, you can use the Ten Forums Turn_Off_Windows_Defender.reg file. Everything you need is right here:

http://www.tenforums.com/tutorials/5918-windows-defender-turn-off-windows-10-a.html

And there’s also an even quicker method for Norton and Avast users that essentially mimics a reinstall by disabling the third-party AV app; starting Windows Defender; and then enabling the third-party app while Defender is still turned on:

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/windows_10-update/definition-update-for-windows-defender-kb2267602/d4edd314-a438-4ac2-b7a9-dc1c45a1ef62

This easy-as-1-2-3 method might also work with McAfee and other AV products, but we don’t have any confirmation for that yet.

GreginMich
