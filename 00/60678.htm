nielo tm say:

It's not that and the cost is justified. The problem is manufacturing, which spills into two areas. Yield and manufacturing process

-

Current OLEDs are manufactured using the same process used to manufacture LCDs, which is very expensive. The reason why the cost of LCDs have  continued to decline over the years is mainly due to increase in yield. Basically LCD, Plasma and OLED begin their life as a massive glass, we call this mother glass. The glass is then cut into smaller sections. As the glass gets bigger with every gen, we can extract more which means greater yield.

-

ATM we can only extract few large OLED panels from the mother glass. The projected cost of 55" OLED reflect the cost of similar (theoretically) sized LCD back in 2002/3 (there weren't any 55" LCDs back then). At current rate, it will take around 5-7 years for the cost of OLEDs to match current LCD.
