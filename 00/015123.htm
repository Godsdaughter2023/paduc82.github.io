Hi,

You are welcome.  Here are my answers:

1. I did so because in any formula that you write, you should always restrict the range to the size of the dataset.  This way, the blanks rows in your range do not get processed thus increasing workbook calculation efficiency

2. Column A has your dates.  So that comparison returns a TRUE if the dates in column A are >= April 1, 2014

3. Column A has your dates.  So that comparison returns a TRUE if the dates in column A are <= than April 22, 2014

4. The multiplication sign is forcing TRUE's and FALSE's to convert to 1's and 0's respectively.  The SUMPRODUCT() function then add's up these 1's and 0's to return the count.

Sharepoint - How do you link a departmental team site to a corporate - Microsoft Community

ruth mendoza say:

Sharepoint - How do you link a departmental team site to a corporate team site?

Scenario: 

You have a corporate site which is used for General Information.  You create a departmental teamsite which you would like to be accessible (linked) from the corporate site.  Who can access the information on the departmental teamsite would be managed thru system access.  In this example since the information is sensitive only departmental individual would be granted access to the departmental teamsite. 

joanne li msft say:

Hi Ruth,

 

Based on your description, my understanding is that you want to add the department team site as a link on corporate site but only department team members would be granted access to it.

 

If so, you can try the followings to add the department team site link to the corporate site:

Navigate to your corporate site home page,

a.  If it is a classic site page, on the left navigation pane, click EDIT LINKS>Add a link, then input the department site name and its URL, click OK. Then click Save to save the changes on the navigation pane.

b.  If it is a modern site page, click Edit on the left navigation pane>click the plus sign to add a link>input the name of the department site and URL>click OK>click Save.

 

You can check the Add a link to the left-hand menu on a team site section in this article for the detailed information: Customize the navigation on your SharePoint site.

 

Besides, regarding you want only department team member to access the department site, here are the steps you can try to configure this:

1.  On the department team site, go to Site Settings>Users and Permissions>Site Permissions, click the site members SharePoint Group>click New button>enter department team members' email addresses>click Share.

2.  Remove other users who you don't want to access department team site from all SharePoint groups.

 

By doing the above, the department team site will be shown on corporate site but only specific users can access it.

 

Regards,

Joanne
