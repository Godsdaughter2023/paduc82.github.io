Just to elaborate on post by "grayburn", the antenna for the internal GPS on N95 is situated beneath the "#" key so best reception is achieved with slider open and phone at 45 degrees. To achieve best reception go outside away from tall buildings and trees and signal will be adversely affected by low cloud or rain.

Nokia Maps 2.0 User Guide available here:

http://europe.nokia.com/EUROPE_NOKIA_COM_3/Explore_services/Nokia_Maps/Support/Maps_2.0/guide/Maps_S60_UG_260608.pdf

To make up for deficiencies with the Texas Instruments GPS5300 NaviLink chipset, your N95 utilizes Assisted GPS provided that you have a GPRS data connection and only 2-5kb of data is needed for a positional fix. When you launched Nokia Maps it should have asked you whether you want to allow application to go online. To check whether Assisted GPS feature is enabled go to Menu > Tools > Settings > General - scroll down to setting below "Factory settings" which is "Positioning" > Positioning methods - you should find a RED tick beside Assisted GPS, Integrated GPS and Network based.

By default, the aspect ratio is maintained.
