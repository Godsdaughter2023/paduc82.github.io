mimbo mcjimbo say:

Here's what's happening.   There are three "chargers" that could be used.  1.  A wall charger only  -- an AC wall plug with wires ending in a micro USB.  2.  A wall plug with a USB  plug on it to provide power only, plus a USB cable that has the micro USB end.   3.  A USB plug  on a device like a laptop that has power and data, plus a USB cable that has the micro USB end.  

When you are using an actual wall charger in 1, if it is wired correctly (is 640 compatible; which should include the 435, 520, 535, 640, 650 730, 735 etc) and can provide enough amperage (is rated to provide 1 A or more) it should charge and quickly.    Most any recent Lumia-specific charger, with a micro USB end for the phone, should work fast if it is 1 A or above.  It will work slower if it is not at least 960 mA, even slower if it is not at least 480 mA, and the slowest if under 300 mA.  So some chargers that are under 1 amp (.75 or .5) won't necessarily charge quickly even if they don't give that message, but that depends on the micro USB side and what ciruitry if any it has in it. (There's also the 950 that uses USB C on the phone side, and of course these cables won't work at all with the micro USB phones.

For 2 and 3, there are two things to worry about, the power and the cable.   Essentially, assuming the power source on the wall or computer can provide +5V at 1 A or more, it comes down to the cable.  

For the cable, if the non-phone large USB side has all four pins connected inside (1 +5V, 2 data -, 3 data +, 4 ground)  it comes down to how the phone micro USB side is.   If the micro USB is just wires (1 +5V, 2 data -, 3 data +, 4 no connection, 5 ground) the phone will give the message and charge at the "safe power value" of 300 mA (or depending, at 480 mA, but I am not sure when this is the case though).   If the micro USB side has the data lines 2 and 3 tied together but 4 still not connected,  it may charge "fast" but probably at 480 mA only, not 960 mA.    

In order for the phone to charge the fastest (which I believe is at most 960 mA even if the  power source can provide 1 or 1.3 or 2.1 A) it seems the micro USB side not only has to  have pins 2 and 3 not connected, but pin 4 at least grounded.   More so, pin 4 needs to be grounded through a 200 Ohm resistor to provide the "attached device presence indicator".  This makes the phone know it can charge fast.

So, if the phone doesn't know what it is plugged into, which seems most cables not specifically for Lumia phones, it will charge at the "safe" (and slow) rate of about 300 mA  or perhaps 480 mA if you're lucky. I believe most will give that message.

In the case of 3, and you're plugging  into some computer (power and data) connector or like it, if all the wires are hooked up on the cable on both ends, the computer will tell the phone it's a computer and the phone should go into a power/data mode of slow charge and data capacity, in which case the phone will likely not complain about the slow charging.   (This can change if the cable has the power connected but not the data, or the data connected but not the power, or if the computer doesn't support them both or has a USB port that is lower powered)

In short, to get the full charge rate, whatever that maximum, get a power source that provides at least 1 A, and a cable that can tell the phone to charge at the fastest rate. A cable  that is  made for Lumias with micro USB, or made for the 640 specifically.

hal hostetler - mvp-outlook say:
This is more or less normal for an IMAP email account; messages are "Marked for deletion" and must be "Purged" from the IMAP inbox to be permanently removed. Outlook has the ability to hide "messages marked for deletion"; on Outlook's Menu bar, click "View", then "Current View", then "Customize Current View".  On the window that comes up, you should see a button about the middle of the page that says "Filter" (which will likely be "Off"). Click this "Filter" button, then click the "Advanced" tab. The "Find items that match these criteria" window will probably be empty, so we need to make a filter to hide these "Marked for deletion" messages.  Click the "Field" button, slide the mouse cursor down to "All Mail Fields", then to the right to select "IMAP Status".  Set the "Condition" button to say "equals", and set the "Value" button to read "Unmarked".  Click "OK" twice and those strike-through messages should vanish.
