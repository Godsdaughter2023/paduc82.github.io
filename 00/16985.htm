Why does Word capitalise 'clementine's' but no other - Microsoft Community

bibblegosh say:

Why does Word capitalise 'clementine's' but no other product i.e. rosemary, milk etc?

An Example,

 

I went to the shop to buy clementine's and came back with milk, rosemary and cheese.

 

 

jay freedman say:
In Word 2003 and 2007, the spell checker guesses that the apostrophe is intentional and means that the word is a possessive -- that a person named Clementine has something. As a proper noun (a name), it would be capitalised. (Oddly, though, if I type "rosemary's" the checker remains silent, although Rosemary could be a name.)

In Word 2010 the grammar checker recognizes that the apostrophe is incorrect -- a so-called "greengrocer's apostrophe" -- so it proposes "clementines" without either the apostrophe or the capitalisation. That would the the correct form.

This starts off with every cell on the worksheet unlocked. To do that select all cells by clicking the box at the intersect of row/column headers in the top left corner. Right click anywhere on the sheet, protection tab and un-check 'locked'

Now Alt +F11 to open VB editor. Double click 'ThisWorkbook' and paste the code below in on the right. Change the password (2 places) and the sheet name to your password and sheet and exit vb editor.

 Now kick things off yourself by saving the workbook and any cell with data is locked and users can only edit empty cells.

Private Sub Workbook_BeforeSave(ByVal SaveAsUI As Boolean, Cancel As Boolean)
Dim c As Range
Set sht = Sheets("Sheet1") 'change to suit
sht.Unprotect Password:="MyPass"
For Each c In sht.UsedRange
    On Error Resume Next
   If Len(c) > 0 Then
        c.Locked = True
   End If
Next
sht.Protect Password:="MyPass"
End Sub
