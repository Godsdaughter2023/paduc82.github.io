jay freedman say:

Richard, Doug alerted me to your post.

The following steps are needed to transfer AutoCorrect entries from one computer to another.

  • Install the AutoCorrect.dotm template from my web page on the old computer, open it or base a new document on it, and click the Backup button in the popup to create a backup document. You indicated that you've already done this successfully.
  • Copy both the backup document and the AutoCorrect.dotm template to the new computer.
  • On the new computer, open or base a new document on the AutoCorrect.dotm template, and click the Restore button in the popup. In the Open dialog that displays, select the backup document. When you click OK, the entries from the document should be added to the program.

If this isn't exactly what you've done, please try again. Also, if your copy of AutoCorrect.dotm is dated earlier than 16 June 2015, please download a new copy.

If you did follow those steps, what if anything happened after you clicked the Restore button?

jay freedman say:

Up to and including the part where you clicked Cancel in the menu box, everything is as expected. The only part that isn't right is the absence of the entries from the old computer.

Look at your personalized entries in the AutoCorrect Backup document. What appears in the rightmost column on those rows?

  • If it says False, the entries are "plain text", and those entries should be stored in a file with an ACL extension in the folder C:\Users\<your name>\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Office. (If you're using English (US), the file will be named MSO1033.ACL; other languages use other numbers.)
  • If it says True, the entries are "formatted text", and should be stored in your Normal.dotm template.

One or the other of those files may be read-only or have incorrect permissions so that Word isn't saving the changes.

Something that may help is to go to the Control Panel > Programs & Features page, right-click Office 2013, click Change, and choose to repair Office.

The DVD Player app only supports the following: 

a. DVDs from a file backup is not supported, the app currently only plays DVD movies on optical discs.

b. The App will only play DVD's not Blu-ray discs.

If you satisfy both the categories, I would suggest you follow the steps below and see if it helps:

Update DVD/CD-ROM drives from Device Manager.

1.      Press Windows key + R key. Type "devmgmt.msc" and click Enter.

2.      Expand the DVD/CD-ROM drives.

3.      Double-click on the DVD/CD-ROM drive.

4.      In Properties, go to Drivers Tab and click on Update.

If this does not work you might have to Uninstall and then reinstall your drivers in compatibility mode.

Follow the steps to uninstall and reinstall the driver.

1.      Press Windows key + R key. Type "devmgmt.msc" and click Enter.

2.      Expand the DVD/CD-ROM drives.

3.      Double-click on the DVD/CD-ROM drive.

4.      In Properties, go to Drivers Tab and click on Uninstall.

5.      Reinstall your audio driver from Manufacturer's Website in Compatibility mode.

6.      Restart your system.
