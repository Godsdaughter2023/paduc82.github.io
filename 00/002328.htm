jllatham say:

Ok, with that many formulas to deal with, and with the rapidly changing exchange rates, the best thing to do is to put the current exchange rate into an available cell.  For this formula we will say that your Euros are in column A beginning at row 2.

Cell X1 is available for use and we will put the exchange rate in it; and at this time 1 Euro = 1.172594 AUD

In an available cell on row 2, put this formula

=A2 * $X$1

You can fill that formula on down the sheet for all 5000 rows. 

and that will convert the Euros in column A to AUD equivalent.

If I have it backwards and you have AUD in column A, then put .852769 into X1 and use the same formula to convert the AUD in column A to Euros.

As the exchange rate changes, all you have to do is enter the new exchange rate into X1 and all the formulas will automatically update.

Check Excel help topic Fill for fast ways to fill a large range.

One quick one is to enter the entire range to be filled and use Fill --> Down (assuming that you want to fill a bunch of rows in 1 column).  Assume your formula that I gave you is in B2 and you need to put the same type formula all the way down to row 5020:  Type

B2:B5020 into the "Name Box" - that's the place next to the formula bar that usually shows you the address of the cell you currently have selected.  When you do that, that range of cells will be selected.

Next, on the [Home] tab over toward the right edge, in the "Editing" group, there is a Fill entry with a drop-down list.  Click it and choose "Down" to fill all the cells from B2 down to B5020 with the formula, which will adjust automatically for you.  Done!
