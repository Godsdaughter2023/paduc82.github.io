dianeoforegon say: It sounds like you have POP accounts where your messages are stored under "On My Computer" folders. If you have IMAP accounts just enter the addresses in Mail and the messages will download.

You have three options to move messages. 

1) Upload your "On My Computer" messages via an IMAP account (iCloud or Gmail for example). Mail will download these messages when you add the IMAP account to Mail.

2) Export your messages as .olm file and convert to mbox file using a third party app, EmailChemy. http://www.weirdkid.com/products/emailchemy/  If you have a lot of folders this will more than pay for itself.

3) Manually move messages as .mbox file.

Depending on how many folders you have you can drag folders to the Desktop as .mbox files. Note, that any folder with more than 2Gb of data will fail to export. You'll need to reduce the number of messages by making subfolders. There is no way to get the size other than use the general rule of between 6-10K messages per folder.

Use Sync Services to sync your Outlook contacts, calendars to Apple applications. Outlook Preferences > Sync Services.

Hope this helps!
