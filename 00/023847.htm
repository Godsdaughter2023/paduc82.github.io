schmidtfamily6 say:

I don't know why this was soooo difficult to research and find how to do.  I think I spent 2 days and at least 20 to 30 different links to read on this.  but I did find out how to make striped wire for electrical color coded wire as you can see in this picture.  

I will try to remember all the steps it took me to get this far, so here goes;

Just an FYI what we are about to do stays with a specific file not like a template. So if you make all these patterns in one file, as far as I know they are not available in other files.  So create all these then save that Visio as "Multi-colored wires.vsd.  Then always start with that file and save it as another file name.

1. You have to get the "Drawing Explorer Window" to be visible.

      A. Click on File, then Options, way down in the General Section Select "Run in developer mode", click            "OK"

      B.  ON your Ribbon "new name for menues" select the Developer section and on the far right select "Drawing Explorer"

      C. You will see a small window with folders pop up

2. Right Click on Line Patterns and select "New Pattern" the following window will pop up.

You will have to make one of these per wire so name it as to the colors on the wire, I.E. White & Black would be a White wire with Black stripe.  for Type select "Line Pattern".  I choose the first behavior so it will repeat my art over and over again until the end of the wire. Feel free to play with the other for future but for striped wire I went with that one. click "OK"

3. You will see it put that new pattern under Line Patterns in the Drawing Explorer window.

     A. Right Click on it and select Edit pattern shape.

     B. It will take you to what looks like another Visio window, but this is your pattern create window.

     C. Step one is to make the page size something real, remember this is just a wire not a large graphic. I made my page 0.5 X 1.5, that is 1/2 inch by 1 and 1/2 inch.  this is under the Design Section on the Ribbon.

All I did here was use 2 rectangles, one is the large blue one and the other is the smaller yellow one.  I placed the Yellow one at an angle starting from the bottom left.  I did not go corner to corner with the yellow, feel free to try but you will see it looks terrible as a wire.  This will be easy to edit at any time, so just try something and see what it looks like.  there is much to play with, the thickness of the yellow, the angle, adding a yellow strip around the blue as well.  

Very important here notice how my rectangles are larger then the page, it doesn't matter how much larger just need to go past the beginning and the end.

You just created one wire with a strip on it, for the drawing I pasted on here I had to make 6 patterns.  You can see them listed in the Drawing Explorer window in that picture above.

Close that window and you will get a pop up saying "Update "Your Pattern Name" and all shapes that may be using it as a pattern?" choose Yes.

Now we get to use it;

1.  Draw a line

2.  Right click on line go to Format then click on Line.

3.  Click the drop down for Dash Type, scroll all the way to the bottom and you will see the pattern you have created, select it.  You can use all settings on this screen except color, you already did that in you pattern.

You should now see a striped wire.

Some of the things that I learned.

if the lines appear to close, make you page wider on your pattern and move the "strip" to the bottom left and play with the angle.  

White wires require a border other wise you can't see them on the white page.  so in your patters I choose the border to be the same color as the strip.  this would require 3 rectangles; 1 large the color of your strip, 2. smaller the color of your wire and the 3rd the color of your strip. simply layer them Large Strip 1st, wire color second, small wire stripe last.  Here you can see I had 2 different colored stripes and the border.

In the Drawing Explorer window right click on your new pattern and select duplicate, it will automatically create the name by adding numbers to the end of your original pattern.  Right Click and choose Pattern Properties, here all you should have to do is name it on top, choose OK.

Right click on the new pattern again and choose Edit Pattern shape, this will pop out your Pattern editing window again.  simply change your colors to what you need and close.  that same pop up I mentioned before will happen, choose Yes to save your changes. 

Repeat this process for as many wires colors as you need.

Save the actual file as I mentioned before and now as long as you start with this file you will always have your colored wire available in the format wire window.
