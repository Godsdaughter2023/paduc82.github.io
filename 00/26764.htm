rohn007 say:

When you buy Office, you buy a license to use it under specific conditions. You don't "own" it, you just rent it from MS. The 2 relevant terms are how many computers you can install it on at the same time and if you can transfer a licensed installation to a new computer.

How did you buy Office:

  • online download, with optional "backup" CD (sounds like your situation)
  • Retail CD in stupid-big-yellow-plastic CD box

Assuming online download, what type of licensce did you buy:

  • "FPP" (Retail) (allows 2 or 3 concurrent installs, allows transfer to new computers)
  • "PKC" (Product Key Card) (Allows install only on one hardware, dies with hardware)

(you can go back to download site and log in to your account to find license type).

Assuming Retail, what edition of Office did you buy:

  • Home and Student (allows 3 concurrent installs)
  • Home and Business (allows 2 concurrent installs)
  • Professional (allows 2 concurrent installs)

Guessing based on what you have said, you bought an online download of a retail license of Home and Business or Pro. Those license allow you to install on 2 machines at the same time. You can re-install an unlimited number of times on those 2 original computers. 

For your new computer, you have 2 options: either uninstall it from one of your current computers so you can transfer to new one, OR yes you will have to buy a new "Product Key". Online is probably your best bet. You will be allowed to install from your current CD, but you will have to use the new "Product Key" that you purchased. The online download price is typically the same as equivalent Retail box, so I prefer to buy the retail box so I have another effectively "free" installation disk.

 

If I haven't made the point clear enough, when you buy the new license, AVOID paying for a "Product Key Card" (PKC) type license. Although there is a price discount the limitations make it a bad deal, IMHO. You are allowed to only install it on one computer
