jay freedman say:
Success! I was able to reproduce the Compile Error message and track down the problem. The cause is not having the Word97TableBugWorkaround procedure in the module.

[Note: you can't edit the code in the copy of the template that's in the Startup folder. You'll have to make a copy of the template in some other folder, edit that, shut down Word, and copy the edited template into the Startup folder to replace the one that's there now.]

The simplest fix is to create an empty procedure at the end of the module, with just these two lines:

Private Sub Word97TableBugWorkaround(FmFld As FormField)
End Sub

(You could paste in the entire procedure from the mvps.org site, but it isn't necessary because it can't possibly execute from a .dotm template.)

A different easy fix is to locate this line in the middle of the CheckProtectedSection procedure:

Call Word97TableBugWorkaround(FmFld)

and delete it. (Because Word 97 can't use a .dotm template, there's no danger of needing a nonexistent workaround.)

The "purist" fix would be to comment out or delete the entire block in the CheckProtectedSection procedure that calls Word97TableBugWorkaround, but that gets a little nasty because you have to find the correct End If statement to comment out along with the If and Else statements that enclose the block. So just stick with either of the other two fixes.

sglxl wrote later:
> However in periods 1 to 3 NO principal payments are made (Grace Period)
> and the first principal pmt is in period 4.
[....]
> for the interest element there is no Grace Period. Interest is paid for all
> periods from 1 through to 40.

First, I think you are misusing the term "grace period".  What you have is a loan with an initial interest-only term of 1 year.  So in effect, you have two loans:

* An interest-only loan of 100,000 at 5% for 4 payments (1 year).

   During this period, the payment is 100000*5%/4 = 1250.

* A normal loan of 100,000 at 5% for 36 payments (9 years) with a balloon payment of 10,000.

  During this period, the payment is PMT(5%/4,4*9,-100000,10000) = 3244.88.

There is no need to use PPMT and IPMT if you are creating a complete amortization schedule; that is, one line per payment.  In fact, you cannot use IPMT for the initial interest-only term.  IPMT and PPMT assume a normal amortization payment:  principal plus interest.

Suppose B1=100000 (loan), B2=10000 (balloon pmt), B3=10 (term; years), B4=4 (pmts/yr), B5=5% (int rate), B6=1 (interest-only term; years).

Set up the following amoritzation table.

B7,pmt1 [*]:  =B1*B5/B4
B8,pmt2 [*]:  =PMT(B5/B4,B4*(B3-B6),-B1,B2)

A13:A52,payment number:  1 through 40

E12,initial loan:  =B1
B13:B16,pmt1:   =$B$7
B17:B52,pmt2:   =$B$8
C13,principal:     =B13-D13
D13,interest:       =E12*$B$5/$B$4
E13,balance:      =E12+D13-B13

Copy C13:E13 and paste into C14:E52.  The result should look like this:
 
                                                                      100,000.00
   1    1,250.00            0.00    1,250.00     100,000.00
   2    1,250.00            0.00    1,250.00     100,000.00
   3    1,250.00            0.00    1,250.00     100,000.00
   4    1,250.00            0.00    1,250.00     100,000.00
   5    3,244.88    1,994.88    1,250.00       98,005.12
   6    3,244.88    2,019.82    1,225.06       95,985.30
[....]
39    3,244.88    3,043.32        201.56       13,081.36
40    3,244.88    3,081.36        163.52       10,000.00

***EDIT***
I do not like to use PPMT and IPMT for a number of reasons.  But if you insist, change the formulas in C17 and D17 as follows, then copy C17:D17 and paste into C18:D52.

C17:  =-PPMT($B$5/$B$4, A17-$B$4*$B$6, ($B$3-$B$6)*$B$4, $B$1, -$B$2)
D17:  =-IPMT($B$5/$B$4, A17-$B$4*$B$6, ($B$3-$B$6)*$B$4, $B$1, -$B$2)

-----
[*] Real-world consideration:  pmt1 and pmt2 should be rounded (or rounded up or down) at most to 2 decimal places.  That will have an impact on the final payment and potentially the number of payments.

Note that we cannot apply this real-world consideration when using PPMT, IPMT, CUMPRINC and CUMIPMT.  That is one of the principle reasons (no pun intended ;->) why I do not like to use those functions.
