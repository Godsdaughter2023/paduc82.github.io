marilyn-- say: Hello RayInHi,

Thank you for posting to the Microsoft Answers Forum.

There are several things to try before resorting to reinstalling Vista.

1) Run Chkdsk /R
Click on the Start Orb
Click All Programs
Click Accessories
Right-click Command Prompt
Click Run as administrator
Type in chkdsk /R
Reboot and let the program run.

It will take a while to run but it should repair any disk errors.

2) Change the MTU settings on the router to a lower number @1400
Check to see if you can access secure websites, if not, go to the next step.

3)  First register the following Internet Explorer dll files
Click Start
Click Run, type:
Regsvr32 jscript.dll
Regsvr32 softpub.dll
Regsvr32 mssip32.dll
Regsvr32 initpki.dll
Regsvr32 msxml3.dll

Check to see if you can access secure website, if not, go to the next step.

4) Now optimize Internet Explorer for browsing secured sites
Go to IE, Click on Tools, Select Internet Options, make the following changes:
On the General tab, Under Section for Browsing history
Click on Delete Button. This will delete temporary files, history and cookies
On the Security Tab, Reset all zones to Default Level
On the Privacy Tab, Click on Default tab. (If grayed out, move the bar up or down on the left side)
On the Content Tab, Click on Clear SSL State
On the Programs Tab, Reset Web Settings
On the Advanced Tab, under the Security section, Check SSL 2.0 & 3.0, Un-Check Use TLS 1.0 
Check again to see if you can access secure websites, if not, go to the next step.

 5) Go to IE, Click on Tools, Select Internet Options, Advanced Tab.
Click on Button for Reset Internet Explorer Options. This will delete all temporary files, disable any browser add-ons, resets all changed settings back to default. Then reboot.
Check again to see if you can access secure website.

Please post again and let us know these steps have resolved your issue or if you need further assistance.

alice_941 say: Rich is correct.  The sentence could also be worded, "Naomi and Adam live in the Valley with their four children, of which the eldest is 16."  (However, interestingly, when I typed this sentence without the phrase "in the Valley":  "Naomi and Adam live with their four children, the eldest of whom is 16," no green squiggly line appeared.)  The clause "the eldest of which is 16," or "of which the eldest is 16," is a non-restrictive adjective clause.  This means that the information in the clause is not necesssary to identify the children.  In a non-restrictive adjective clause, "which" is used for people, animals, and inanimate objects after a preposition.  You could probably get away with using "the eldest of whom is 16," but "the eldest of which is 16," or "of which the eldest is 16," sounds better.  If you use names instead of "their four children," you can use of whom:  "Naomi and Adam live in the Valley with Tina, Tim, Lisa and Andy, of whom the eldest is 16," or "the eldest of whom is 16," or of which:  Naomi and Adam live in the Valley with Tina, Tim, Lisa and Andy, of which the eldest is 16," or "the eldest of which is 16."

Black Blob during Copy / Paste

My problem is if I copy text from a web site or from Excel, Access etc into Word all I get is a black spot.

I have tried a repair install and checking all the settings against my laptop - where this function works properly.

The problem is caused by a defective Bluetooth add-in for Word, Excel, and PowerPoint, turn off the Com Addin.

Additional Notes:

1.      The defective Bluetooth add-in is usually installed by a Bluetooth driver. (In my case - Intel Centrino Wireless Bluetooth 3.0 + High Speed Adaptor (v1.1))

2.      Updating your Bluetooth driver (or reverting to an older version) may also resolve the problem.

3.      Unfortunately simply disabling the add-in through the Word options may result in a 3 second freeze when loading Word 2007. (If this is a problem, changing to a different bluetooth driver version may be the better option)

4.           

Additional tip - Remove Older Printer Drivers

Oddly enough, this ended up being the cause of my problem. I had an older print driver installed on my Windows 7 machine and once I removed that driver, Word started working without any problems!

You can delete individual printer drivers by going into the registry. Note that simply going to Printers and Faxes and deleting a printer there does not delete the printer driver.

Starting point when Word does something unexpected

As a general rule, when something seems to be going wrong, or at least not the way you want it to go, in Word, any version, go to Tools and then Options and study all the settings on all the tabs. You may even find some settings that you want to try that have nothing to do with the current problem.

When I was teaching Word and other Microsoft Office programs at a private business college, I had three rules:

1.         Always check Tools Options

2.         When in doubt, right-click on something

3.         If you're wondering how to set up a special kind of material in a document, consider whether Word tables are suitable. The placement officer of the school came to me several times and asked how I thought she should set something up. Each time the answer was, "I'd use a Word table." By the fifth time she had stopped asking--she got the point.
