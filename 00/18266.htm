Help on this please . . those are some very important files . .

surya prasad vemulapalli say:

Hi Ruthrop,

 Files created using Works 6.0 and later versions of Works can be opened by installing Works 6-9 convertor on the computer.
 Find more information about works 6-9 convertor at http://support.microsoft.com/default.aspx/kb/315757

The files created in Word Processor from Works 4.0 are too old to be opened using Word Processor from Works 9.0.The following steps might help you recover most of the text from those files.

Method I 

      ·         Open works Word Processor.
·         In the file menu click open, browse to the location where the files exist, select the file then click open.
·         In the file type dropdown menu at the bottom of that window select “recover text from any file” option.
·         Select the Works document and click open.
·         After the file is opened save it with a different name.

Method II 

      ·         Click Start , point to Programs , point to Accessories , and then click Notepad/Word pad.
·         On the File menu, click Open.
·         Browse to the location where the files exist, select a file then click open.
·         Delete any strange looking characters you see at the beginning or end of the document.
·         Click edit menu, click select all copy all the text .
·         Open new Works Word Processor document paste the text to that document and save it with a different name in a desired location.

Regards,
Surya Prasad

sven johannsen say:

The SD card on a WP8 device is only usable by the system for media files, Pictures, Videos, Music. The built in apps and some third party apps will have access to those file types, as long as the types are supported.  You will note I did not say documents in general.

 

If you look at the phone from File Explorer on a PC, you will see the phone and the SD card. On the phone you will see Pictures, Videos, Music, and Document folders. You can put Office Documents, Word, Excel, Powerpoint in the documents folder on the phone, and they will be seen in the Office Hub. You can also put PDFs there, and they will be seen in the Office hub, under location.phone, and if you click them they will either open your PDF application, or ask which one to use, if you have more than one.  You cannot access Documents from the SD card.  You can copy documents to the SD card, or anything you like actually via File Explorer, but only media files can be accessed by programs.

 

Third Party applications, should be able access the media file locations either on the phone, or on the SD card. I have third party apps that can access the camera, take a picture and store that in the pictures folder. It is the responsibility of the developer to provide that option though. I am noit sure to what level that opportunity exists, as I don't have many items that do it. The restriction is for security and stability. So unless the video download app provides an option to save to the video folder or SD card, the files are buried in the app storage area and will not be accessible.

johncellini say:

Formula that will calculate percentages and then average them in a column with blank cells

I created a spreadsheet for a softball team to calculate player averages and team averages. Percentages are based on an On Base number (OB) divided by Plate Appearances (PA). So to calculate a player's average, I simply divide the OB by the PA. If a player was on base 4 times out of 5 plate appearances, his average would be 80% (4/5). To calculate a players average over many games, I added the OB numbers and added the PA numbers in then divided the OB by PA. I realized that this was not accurate since there are different denominators (PA) for different games. Also, a player might miss a game. I needed to calculate the average for each game a player played and then average that number. I have encountered difficulties doing this.

Also, I want to calculate the team average for each game. It's the same problem; averages for each player need to be calculated and blank cells need to be ignored. 

Just to be clear, here is an example of what I am trying to describe:

John  OB     4     6     4

          PA     5     6     5

Mike  OB     4           2

          PA     5           3

Here are 3 games with only 2 players for demonstration. Mike missed the second game. So, to calculate John's overall average, I divided the total OB numbers by the total PA numbers which evaluated to 8.75%. But I realized this was incorrect. By calculated the the batting percentage of each game first, the result is 8.666%.

To calculate the team average, I tried using this formula that would calculate the column of percentages for each player and then average them:

=AVERAGE(IF(ISBLANK(C6), "", C6/C7),IF(ISBLANK(C8), "", C8/C9),IF(ISBLANK(C10), "", C10/C11),IF(ISBLANK(C12), "", C12/C13),IF(ISBLANK(C14), "", C14/C15),IF(ISBLANK(C16), "", C16/C17), IF(ISBLANK(C18), "", C18/C19).... and so on

This not very elegant formula works until it encounters a blank cell. The AVERAGE function skips blank cells but evidently not in this case. 

So, the easy way would be to add another column for each game to calculate the individual percentages and then the average them but I would rather find a formula that will work without having to do that. 

I welcome you suggestions.

Igor

 

ronrosenfeld say:

Isn't a player's batting average the total of ALL hits for the season to date divided by the total of ALL appearances?

I'm pretty sure that's how it's calculated in the US major leagues.

But if you really want to Average the on base percentage on a game by game basis, you could use an array formula:

Assuming John"s data is in C1:E2, is average per game OBP would be given by the array formula (entered by holding down ctrl-shift while hitting enter)

=AVERAGE(IFERROR(C1:E1/C2:E2,""))

Note that we have IFERROR return a null string, and not a zero, as text is ignored by the AVERAGE function.
