"Is there a fast way to randomly generate a list of 126,000 values, or am I going to have to do the corner drag method?"

does that part of your question simply mean that you just want a list of 126,000 random numbers?  If so, here is how to generate a list of 126,000 random numbers:

[1]  In a blank spreadsheet, using Excel 2013 software, type Type A126000 in the Name box, as shown in this screen-print:

[2]  Press the Enter key, and that will move the selection down to cell A126000.

[3]  In cell A126000, type in your random function, for example

=rand()

or some variant of the rand function.  Then press the enter key.

[4]  Be sure that cell A126000 is highlighted (pressing the up arrow key would do that).

[5]  Press Ctrl + Shift + Home (this will select cells A1 to A126000).

[6]  Click on Home -> Editing -> Fill -> Up (as shown in the screen-print below).  You will now have 126000 random numbers in Excel.

[7]  If need your random numbers copied outside of Excel, you can click on Home -> Clipboard -> Copy.  Then paste your list of 126000 random numbers somewhere else where you need them (for example into Windows Notepad).

[8]  Note: If you save your spreadsheet that generated your 126000 random numbers, each time you re-open the spreadsheet, a different list of 126000 random numbers is generated.
