How to stop unwarrented voluentary dialing ? - Microsoft Community

paramaanu say:

How to stop unwarrented voluentary dialing ?

My device Acer F 900. Windows 6.1 mobile.Its a full touch mobile. Starts autodial in the back ground and dont stop. It keeps on redialng madly. How to lock dialing ?

david a matson say: Greetings Paramaanu!  Welcome to the Windows Phone forums!

If it dials when located in your pocket, then the touch screen is likely getting activated and the dialer pushed into dialing outbound.   The best solution to this is to lock your device prior to placing it in your pocket.

If this problem occurs spontaneously while the phone is in your hand or sitting on the desk, then you might have one or more different causes for this.  These are by no means "known problems", but rather situations I could see (or have seen variations of in the past):

1.) If the phone can be observed dialing randomly selected individuals that YOU KNOW (by nature of existing on your phone as "previously dialed" individuals), then the problem could be a screen alignment problem, and the alignment is simply causing the phone dialer to select a previous call as a basis for another outbound call.
2.) Your phone is only doing what you (or some program) asked it to do: the phone is dialing a modem number because you wanted to attempt to connect to one in the past, or some similar situation.
3.) Your phone itself is physically damaged in some way.

If you have a reliable backup program that you trust, I would seriously consider backing-up your phone contents, and then perform a Hard Reset.  This will destroy all of your contact info and other personal data, but it will determine if items #1 & #2 are part of the equation.   If the problem goes away after such a Hard Reset, but then returns after you perform a Restoral of the backup you made earlier, then the problem is definitely software based.  You'd be best to then perform another Hard Reset to "clean the slate" again, and then perform a selective restoral of only your actual data, not any programs or registry settings.

If the problem doesn't go away after the hard reset, then the problem is likely a hardware problem that is beyond your ability to fix and our ability to assist you further.  In that case, I would highly recommend that you talk to your phone reseller to see if they can assist you by way of a Warranty (or "out-of-warranty") repair.

I hope this helps!
