Hi ajn19,

Need help on sheet reference.

In one summary sheet, cell A1 has a sum formula (Jan!A1+Feb!A1+Mar!A1+apr!A1). The problem is i do not have the april data yet, so the result of the formula=REF#. How to solve this. The formula should calculate  only taking values from available sheets.

Thanks in advance

Add a dummy sheet named Start before your Jan sheet and a dummy sheet End after the last monthly sheet. Then your formula could become:

                                 =SUM(Start:End!A1)

This formula returns the sum of the A1 cell on all sheets from Start to End. If any new month sheets are added between the two dummy sheets, the formula will include these in the returned result.

dan at it associates say:

Office Home and Student 2007 could have one of four license terms:  a 3-PC "family pack" retail license, a 1-PC retail license, a 1-PC OEM license, or a 1-PC Medialess Licensing Kit license.

 

Microsoft also sold Office Home and Student 2007 direct to consumers as a downloaded product plus emailed product key in both 1-PC and 3-PC licenses from what was called the Windows Marketplace.

 

If you have a round-cornered clear plastic retail package that has a pivoting disc tray, with the product key printed on a yellow-orange Proof of License label affixed to the pivoting disc tray, then or if you purchased directly from Microsoft thru the Windows marketplace, you have the retail license.  The packaging should tell you how many computers it's good for.

 

The 3-computer retail licensed product is permitted to be concurrently activated on up to three computers used by members of your household.  If you purchase a new computer, or a computer dies and is replaced by a new one, the licensing terms permit you to uninstall it from the old computer (if possible) and install and activate it on the new computer.

 

So in your case, if you have the retail license, just uninstall one of your installations from an old computer and install and activate it on the new computer.

julie sheets say:
When you initially assign resources to tasks, Project will calculate the work for each resource based upon the task duration * assignment units.  For example: if you have a two-day duration task and assign two resources, Project will calculate:
R1 - assignment units 100% - work = 16 hours
R2 - assignment units 50% - work = 8 hours

Total work = 24 hours.

Under this scenario - both do finish at the same time - the 8 hours of work for R2 takes the full 2-day duration.

If I have an effort driven 2 day duration task and assign R1 at 100% - I get all work (16 hours) assigned to R1.  If I then assign R2 at 50%, the work is divided between the two resources based upon the assignment units - R1 has 10.67 hours and R2 has 5.33 hours.  Both resources finish the task at the same time.

I'm guessing you are doing something different?  If you manually enter work as you assign resources, Project will not re-assign the work.
