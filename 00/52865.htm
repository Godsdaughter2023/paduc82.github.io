OK.  I'm surprised that you were unable to paste that list -- is isn't so large.  Nevertheless, I've read through your list of startup items.  The problem that you complained about (starting up the system32 folder) is most likely caused by a bad registry entry from the InstallShield Update Manager.

In addition, you have a number of startup applications that probably are slowing your computer down and at least one potentially bad application.

Here's what I suggest you do.

Download AutoRuns from http://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/sysinternals/bb963902.aspx

Install Autoruns and start it.  Wait until the scan is completed (it will say "Ready" in the lower left corner).

Click the "Logon" tab.  There are 4 columns:  Autorun Entry, Description, Publisher, and Image Path.  There is a checkbox at the left end of each line.  When you first run Autoruns, all of the boxes will be checked.  Unchecking the box prevents a startup item from starting, but it does not permanently remove the entry.  If you later decide you want the item to automatically start, just re-run Autoruns and put a check back in the box.  "Delete"ing a line, however, is permanent.

Click the Options menu and ensure that "Hide Signed Microsoft Entries" is NOT checked.  If this had been checked, click once to uncheck and then click File > Refresh to scan again.

Look in the left column (Autorun entry) for ISUSPM.  You should find two entries.  For one of them, the Image Path column will be

   "c:\program files\common files\installshield\updateservice\isuspm.exe" -scheduler

and for the other, the Image Path column will be simply

     - scheduler

The second entry may not be next to the first one but is likely to be near the bottom of the list.   It is this second entry with just the dash and the word scheduler that is incorrect and should be deleted.  Right-click on the line and select "Delete."

Although the above deletion should make your problem stop, if it were my system, I would uninstall the "InstallShield Update Manager" by using the tool from here: http://consumerdocs.installshield.com/selfservice/viewContent.do?externalId=Q111006&sliceId=1 (if that doesn't work, I would at least UNcheck the other ISUSPM line).

Another thing you probably don't need is "Driver Performer."  There will be a shortcut to this application in Start > All Programs > Startup.  If you delete that shortcut, the program will not automatically start.  In addition, however, there is a related item called Tasktray that will start automatically unless you uncheck its entry in Autoruns.  Or you can uninstall the entire application, probably from Control Panel > Add or Remove Programs.

You might consider unchecking the line for AlcxMonitor (alcxmntr.exe).  Although most sites consider this benign but potentially a waster of computer resources, at least one reputable site doesn't like it:  http://www.bleepingcomputer.com/startups/Alcxmntr.exe-245.html

I am suspicious of the entry named "PC Cleaners".  Although your file has an "S" at the end of the name, it seems likely to be a variant of the following piece of malware:  http://www.bleepingcomputer.com/startups/PC_Cleaner.exe-22575.html

At the very least, you should download, install, update, and run full scans with both of the following free tools. 

MalwareBytes AntiMalware
SUPERAntiSpyware

If you are curious about the other items in your startup list, just Google the names.  In fact, if you right-click on any entry in Autoruns, one of the options is "Search online" -- which will run a Google search for you.  Most of your startup items seem benign (although you might want to stop Microsoft Messenger unless you actually use it).

And you can delete the file from your SkyDrive now if you feel like it.
