joeu2004 say:

Amber wrote:

I am doing an exponential regression and need to display the formula for the line. The display of the line looks like:

y=3269e^-0.115x

[....]

Does anyone know how to get excel to format it without the base e?

Are you sure you simply want to "format" the trendline label without "e"?  That does not make sense to me.  And I do not believe there is any way to do that.

Or do you really want to use that information in formulas in the Excel worksheet?  In that case, yes, you need to write the expression very differently.

Ostensibly, you would write =3269*EXP(-0.115*X1), where X1 is a value of "x".

However, that is not likely to produce a good estimate of "y" because, by default, coefficients in trendline labels are rounded to too-little precision to be useful.

Perhaps the simplest thing to do is:  format the trendline label so it displays coefficients with 15 significant digits.  Then manually copy-and-paste each coefficient into cells in the worksheet.

To reformat the trendline label:  right-click on the trendline label; click on Format Trendline Label, Number, Scientific; enter 14 into the Decimal Places field; and click on Close.

The trendline label might then look like

3.26854321012345E+03e^-1.15320123456789E-01x.

Copy 3.26854321012345E+03 into Z1 and 1.15320123456789E-01 into Z2.  Then your Excel formula would be =Z1*EXP(Z2*X1).

It is important to understand the difference between "E" and "e" in the trendline label.  "E+01" means 10 to the power of 1.  "e" refers to the Euler's exponential constant "e", about 2.71828.

To get around this issue try this:

Type in MSCONFIG and press OK
