That is definitely not normal. There should only be one Generic PnP Monitor.

My apologies. The instructions I wrote out were not very self explanatory. Hopefully this will make things more clear;

Inside Device Manager, Right click on the Generic PnP driver and select the "Update" option as though you were going to update your driver.

On the window that pops up, you will have two options for how to proceed with the update. One of which, the computer selects your driver, the other one will allow you to browse your computer for the driver software. Select the option that will allow you to browse your computer.

You will then be given two more options. One of which, you browse through your files for the driver, the other you select a driver from a list. You will want to select the driver from a list.

On the next screen, it will display a list of drivers. This will likely be a filtered list of drivers that your computer determined are compatible with your TV. It's very likely that Generic PnP will be the only one shown on this list. Above where you see the list is a checkmark box that will allow you to turn off this filter if it is on. You will want to make sure that the box is unchecked.

At this point, you will see two columns in the window. One displays a list of manufacturers who provide their own drivers, and the other shows a list of drivers. You will want to have the manufacturer column set to "(standard monitor types)" 

In the driver column there will be a series of drivers called "Digital Flat Panel" in () they will display resolution and refresh rate. You will want  to select the resolution and refresh rate of your TV. If you don't know it, start with a resolution of 1280x720 and assume your refresh rate is 60hz.
