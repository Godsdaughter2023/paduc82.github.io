Hi Peter,

I think what happened was a Windows 10 update did not complete correctly and they were left with a blank desktop and a couple of icons - sadly, this does happen a lot.

What happens during a major update is the user profiles are moved and a temporary profile is used during the update. Then at the end of the update, the users profiles are activated again. Sometimes this last part does not happen and the user is logged into a temporary profile without realizing it

I think the support guys did not realize this and instead of the proper procedure, they tried to fix the issue in the wrong manner

The correct procedure would be to restart the PC's 3 or 4 times and that usually causes Windows to complete the update and move the correct user profiles back and all is well

As for can a Reset happen without being initiated by a user - no that cannot happen

What may of happened is the update rolled itself back if the update did not take correctly

Either way, there should have been no loss of data, either through an automatic roll back or recovering from a Temporary Profile . . .

davem121 say:
Hi Peter

When Windows boots into a temporary profile, if data is moved around in while in that profile it can get lost forever, especially if someone moves it into the temporary profile and then Windows boots into the original correct profile

Have a look in C:\Users to see if that data can be found, though I doubt it . . .

The advice I always give users in this situation to avoid data loss is below

___________________________________________________

Before you do anything or move any files around
Restart your PC 3 or 4 times, this will fix this issue a lot of the time . ..

Otherwise:

Please open Windows File Explorer
Navigate to C:\Users
Do you see two user folders that look like yours?
Open those folders and check inside Pictures, Documents . . . etc. and see if your files are there

If your files are there:

Create a new user profile with Admin privileges
Log into the new user profile
Copy all your data from the old profile to the new profile (Documents Library, Pictures Library . . . etc.)

If you still have not found your files, look for a Windows.old folder on your C Drive, expand that to open the users folder contained inside - check there for your files, icons . . .etc.

If you do find your files . . . etc. there, copy them out as soon as possible as Windows will remove the Windows.old folder in time

NOTE VERY IMPORTANT: Do Not copy your files into the Temporary profile you are currently in, when the situation is corrected, your files will be deleted with the Temporary Profile and will not be recoverable !!
