hal_sc say:

No, your SAMaccountname and UPN are not the same, and are not linked.

The Microsoft recommendation is to change just the UPN to match primary SMTP, users logon with their SAM which does not change. The only exception would be if you had applications that were using the UPN for some reason, but even so it makes things a lot more confusing for users if the UPN does not match their email, since they will have to type something else into boxes that say email address.

However the format you have put above is not correct, the SAMaccountname has no domain at the end. Example:

This is also worth a read https://blogs.perficient.com/2015/07/07/office-365-why-your-upn-should-match-your-primary-smtp-address/ where the above image is from.

So using your example:

valid domain: domain.com.br

internal domain: domain.local

mail (alternative ID): edson.paula@domain.com.br

SAM: epaula

UPN: epaula@domain.com.br

So users logon to their PCs with e.g. epaula, but the email and UPN is *** Email address is removed for privacy ***. So you change both the first and second part of the UPN. This does not in any way affect users logging on who still use e.g. epaula. Then for any online Office 365 services they will use *** Email address is removed for privacy ***.
