Conditionally round time to nearest quarter hour. - Microsoft Community

sovereigndust say:

Conditionally round time to nearest quarter hour.

Hello, 

I am trying to create a spreadsheet to help keep track of my hours at work. Which by itself is simple enough, but here's where it gets tricky for me:

My employer rounds clock in/out times to the nearest quarter hour,  with a 5 minute window. So for example if I clock in at 9:05, it gets rounded to 9:00. If I clock in at 9:06, it's rounded to 9:15. Same for clocking out, except 4:09 becomes 4:00 and 4:10 becomes 4:15.

Is there some formula I can use to do the rounding for me without having to type the times twice (I'd  like to keep track of the actual times as well)? 

fin_modler say:

I think the rounding arithmetics might be slightly off actually - rounding anything earlier than 9:10 to 9:00 for instance.

For the INT route, I think the adjustment should have been just under 2/3 or 9/15, say A2+199/300 or A2 + 899/1500.

Alternatively, A2-1/3 or A2-9/15 with ROUNDUP should work or simply A2-5/1440 or A2-9/1440.

Also, assuming that anything after 12pm is clock-off time, you can use one formula for both:

=ROUNDUP(A2*96-IF(A2<0.5,5,9)/15,0)/96, or

=ROUNDUP((A2-IF(A2<0.5,5,9)/1440)*96,0)/96

the 0.5 is basically 12/24 for 12pm or half a day.

hansv mvp say:

Let's say you have a time in cell A2.

The formula

=INT(96*A2+1/3)/96

will return the rounded time (you may have to format the cell with the formula as a time if Excel doesn't do so automatically. The formula can be filled down / copied and pasted.

The 96 in the formula is the number of quarters (quarter hours) in a day, and 1/3 represents 1/3 of a quarter hour, i.e. 5 minutes.

Alternatively, you can use

=FLOOR.MATH(A2+1/288,1/96)

You'll have to format the result of the formula as a time.

1/96 day = a quarter of an hour, and 1/288 day = 5 minutes.
