Why is my text moving when I insert a picture and select wrap behind text?

But whenever I insert an image, the text on the label on which I inserted the image moves up and the text on the top of the label below disappears. I have spent HOURS today trolling various support sites without any success in finding help for this. I'm using Word for Mac 2008. 
daniel g. grau say:
There is no such thing as "wrap behind text." What you have is:
  • Wrapping: the way the text flows around the picture. Summon the contextual menu with Control-Click (or Right-Click) on the picture and select None, so the text will overlap the picture. Any other option makes the text skirt around the picture in different ways.
  • Arrange: This controls what happens if text and the picture were to overlap (which is what you want). So who's going to be in front? Again, use the contextual menu to select Send behind text.
Your text is disappearing because labels are actually tables with fixed-size cells. If the picture pushes the text out of the way, there's simply no room to display the picture plus the text trying to avoid it.

daniel g. grau say:
I think you'd need to attach the picture to the first paragraph in the cell, and add the rest of the text below it so the picture will stay fixed in position. Otherwise, if you add text before the paragraph the picture is attached to will, the extra text will push the picture down. Another way to fix this is by defining the picture's position as absolute instead of relative.

Depending on your label's layout, you may need to attach the picture to a blank paragraph at the top of the cell (you can shrink the blank paragraph down to 1-pt height so it won't alter the format). To do this, cut the picture, place the cursor and paste. 

And of course it is best if you work with non-printing characters visible.
