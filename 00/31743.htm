Tips for the newcomer to Minecraft - Microsoft Community

wolfiesden360 say:

Tips for the newcomer to Minecraft

Thought I would pop some tips down for some of the newcomers to Minecraft from someone who has been around the block a couple times (yea, pun intended).  For you old hats, all, or at least most of this is fairly obvious.  For the newcomer, they may not be so obvious or at least, they might not have thought about it so cut them some slack and don't follow up with "old news" sorts of comments.



1.  Right after starting a game on anything but peaceful, an early priority should be light.  Harvest a bunch of trees for the wood.  They may also drop apples which is a starting food source.  Once you have wood, make a bunch of planks but do NOT convert all the wood to planks.  Build a crafting table and put it in your first dirt house.  Make some sticks, but don't use all your planks.  Make a wood pick.  Then mine a small stack of cobblestone from stone blocks.  This allows you to make a furnace.  Do so and place it next to your crafting table.  Put some of the planks in the bottom slot and put the wood you didn't convert to planks into the top slot.  This will cook them into charcoal.  You can now use the crafting table to produce torches from sticks and charcoal.  Light your initial home well.


2.  When you start your game on anything but peaceful, its a very good idea to mine a bunch of dirt right away and build a building to stay in the first night.  You will need to light it (see below) with torches to prevent monsters from spawning inside it.  Dirt requires no tools to collect and can be collected first thing.


3.  When going underground, I always bring a couple stacks of wood (not planks), a bucket of water and a crafting table and a pick.  You will need little else.  From the wood, you can produce the planks as needed for sticks for more tools.  You will collect cobblestone as you go as well as coal to fire a furnace.  There is no wood underground but there is virtually all else you will need.  Wood stacks are more efficient for inventory use than carrying planks, and if needed you can burn some of the wood into charcoal for more torches until you locate coal.


4. One of the early tools you should focus on making is a iron bucket.  It can be used to move lava and water and is reusable.  The next should be an iron pick as its needed for gold, redstone, diamonds, etc.


5.  You can build an infinite pool of water.  Dig a 2x2 hole one block deep.  Fill one corner with water.  Then fill the opposite diagonal corner with water and all 4 blocks will convert to still water.  You can dip from any of the 4 blocks infinitely.  Another method is to dig 3x1 by one block deep.  Fill each end with water.  The only place you can dip from this infinitely is from the center block.


6.  When exploring near edges use the right thumb stick (press down on it to click) to enter crouch mode.  In this mode you can't fall off an edge.  This allows you to go right to an edge, and even past it to build bridges across gaps.


7.  Signs, ladders and doors stop water.  You can use them to control how water flows.


8.  Placing a door in a wall will prevent water from flowing through.  You can use this to make underwater structures and you can open the door and go out into the water without it flowing into your structure.


9.  Ladders form an air pocket under water.  This lets you form a vertical column up from the bottom and put ladders on the side of it and you can breathe.


10.  Jack-o-lanterns can be made from a torch and a pumpkin.  They can be placed under water!  Until you get to the nether to get glowstone, they are the only underwater light and they are easy to grow and produce.  And, unlike glowstone, you can mine them to recover them for re-use.  The drawback is they must be placed on top of something solid, they can't be hung from a ceiling or wall (put a block in place, put the lantern on the block, then remove the block under it to put them on walls and ceilings).


11.  A bucket of lava can fuel a furnace for a very long time!  Its not a good use of lava if its in short supply for you but once you mine deep down or get to the nether, its an excellent source of fuel.


12. To safely mine obsidian, dig around it a couple blocks to make yourself a small working area.  Put a water block in one corner so it flows against the obsidian.  Then you can mine the obsidian and not worry about it falling into lava under it!  The minute its mined, the lava under it is converted to obsidian by the water!  Just be sure to move your water source closer as you mine.


13.  When you are digging down, do not dig straight down in a single block!  If you fall into water, its ok.  But if you fall into lava, it won't end well.  When digging down, dig a 2x2 hole as you go.  Dig out the 3 blocks you are not standing on first, and if they are clear, move onto one of them and dig out the 4th block.  Repeat this 3 then 1 process over and over.  Bring wood to produce ladders to get out :)


14. Cactus make a good fence!  Collect cactus from your desert areas and bring them back to your homestead.  Bring some sand to plant them on!  Then plant them around your area with a 1 block gap between them.  Monsters will have trouble getting through and will take damage when they do.  Very effective against spiders as they are 2x2 and can't get through without hitting the cactus!  You can extend this to two rows of cactus with the second offset from the first by 1 block.  Its almost creeper proof!


15.  Do not waste gold making tools!  The tools are fast but wear out faster than wood ones.  Its an incredible waste of gold!  Stick with stone, iron, and diamond tools.  Skip gold armor as well.


16.  When harvesting trees, leave the first block in place and harvest up until you can't reach any more.  Then you can stand on the first block to access the last pieces of trees.


17.  When going underground bring sand or harvest some gravel as you descend.  Both are affected by gravity and will sink into lava.  Very useful for creating some starter walkways in lava pools.


18.  Ladders are good for traveling up and down but there is a faster way down from ANY height!  You will need ladders to get back out, one sign and a bucket of water.  Dig down to where you will want to return to often.  Clear out an area.  You will need to look at your map and pick a spot for a 2nd shaft, 1 block in size all the way down from the surface and note the X and Z coords on the map.  Its best to put it right next to your initial shaft so you know (or are pretty sure) there aren't lava pits in the way.  You will need the ceiling where the 2nd shaft comes through at the bottom only a couple blocks from the floor, use cobblestone to extend it down if needed.  Return to the surface and dig the vertical shaft straight down.  If you encounter caves, close off the walls around your shaft, you want no ledges or anything to catch yourself on, including ladders.  Once you are at the bottom again, put a sign on the wall inside the shaft at least 2 units from the floor, and no farther than 4.  Above the sign, put a water block.  Now, you can use the ladder (original shaft) to go up, but to return, simply drop into the 2nd shaft.  The water block will slow you down before you hit the bottom and will take no damage from the fall.  Much faster than climbing down a ladder!  I will sometimes put a trap door at the top with a button.  Simply stand on the trap door and press the button, down you go!


19. To quickly mine an entire shaft of gavel or sand, go to the bottom and dig it using a torch.  A split second after the bottom block breaks, place the torch where it was!  When the column collapses it will instantly break apart as it hits the torch.  Scoop it up, grab the torch and repeat for the next column.


20.  To make a spider proof porch, line the bottom all the way around with fences.  Leave a gap for a gate!  Then put another fence post every other rail (allowing only 1 block gap).  Then top with half slabs.  Spiders can't fit through the 1 block gaps and you can strike at them with your sword though the holes.  The half slabs prevent them spawning up on the roof.  Be sure to light your porch!  This will prevent zombies from reaching your door and busting it down as well!


21.  You can keep your survival world but investigate ti in creative mode!  Turn off autosave!  Then save your survival world.  Load it again in creative mode and ignore the warning dialog.  DO NOT SAVE WHILE IN CREATIVE!  You are only using this to explore the map and locate resources.  Grab a paper and pencil.  Go looking around for resources such as abandoned mines, spawners, villages, diamonds etc.  Note the X,Y, Z coordinates of the resources.  You can use the x-ray machine to look around.  To make a machine, you will need redstone torch, TNT, and regular pistons.  Dig a 3x2 hole 1 block deep.  then inside that dig 3x1 one more deeper..  Get into the deep part.  Put 2 pistons on the end wall, one over the other.  The put a TNT on each piston head.  You should be standing in the end of the deep trench.  Lean around an place the redstone torch at the back of the shallow shelf next to the pistons.  The pistons will extend and you will glitch through the TNT and the walls next to you will be invisible.  You will be able to look around and see caves and underground features.  To get out, simply break up the TNT.


22.  Always carry a bucket of water with you underground and have it on your toolbar!  If you fall in lava and make it out, use the water on the floor to put yourself out and stop the burn!  It can save your life :)


23.  For testing purposes, you can copy your save games to a thumb drive as a backup.  Then you can tinker with your world and know you have a copy.


24.  If you find a skeleton spawner, do not destroy it!  It can provide an infinite supply of bones and arrows if you create a killing machine with it inside!  And, no the feather touch tool will not recover a spawner.


25.  Before you go underground, do a manual save!  Can save you the headache of having to re-do all your prep work.

Here are a few more...

  1. Enchanting is totally random!  Yes, you heard right.  Those runes mean nothing as far as what enchantment you will get.  So, before you spend those enchant points, Save your game.  Then enchant your item.  If you don't like the enchantment, load the game again and repeat until you get the enchant you wanted or find one you like as much.  Great way to conserve those diamond tools and enchantment points.
  2. Build kill machines around those dungeon spawners!  These are very fast ways to collect materials and XP points.  But, you can't have the machine do all the killing if you want xp points.  I usually use lava overhead or cactus in my machines.  I usually set them up so I can hit the mobs with a sword wihile the lava and cactus are hitting them too.  Makes faster work of them and you still get XP
  3. Bring saplings underground with you.  If you find a cave large enough, put some light around in it and plant the saplings.  Trees WILL grow underground if there is sufficent light.  Great way to have renewable wood supply near or in your underground structures.
  4. Place a pressure plate on the inside of your fence gates and doors.  That way after you open them and walk through, they automatically close behind you.  No more forgotten open doors for mobs to waltz in your cozy home!
  5. Its usually a good thing to put a couple glass blocks or glass panes on either side of exterior doors.  That way you get a good view as to whats outside before you walk out into a pack of creepers.
  6. Your map does not update unless you equip it and actually have it in your hand.  So when you are exploring, be sure to equip the map in your hand so it will update as you explore.
  7. You can grow giant mushrooms by planting a mushroom (brown or red) and then applying bonemeal to it.  The red mushrooms can be instant houses!  There are several youtube videos with how-to on the subject.  Harvesting giant mushrooms with an axe will yield a lot of mushrooms for soup!
  8. Iron can't be harvested in the nether.  Bring it with you.  If your portal is hit by a fireball it can go out and you will be stuck there.  You will need a flint and steel to re-ignite it.  But you need iron to make it.  You can harvest flint while in the nether but not iron.   So bring it with you.
  9. Cobblestone is impervious to ghast fireballs.  Surround your portal with cobblestone you brought with you from the overworld.
  10. Bring dirt and saplings with you into the nether.  You can plant trees there as a renewable wood source.  Just protect them from ghasts' fireballs :)

Download your Xbox LIVE profile

You might want to download your Xbox LIVE gamer profile in the following situations:

  • You want to use your Xbox LIVE gamertag on another console.
  • You have a Zune or Games for Windows account (download your existing account instead of creating a new account).
  • You accidentally deleted your Xbox LIVE gamertag from your console.

You can download your Xbox LIVE gamertag to any console. Here’s how:

  1. Press the Xbox Guidebutton on your controller (large X button in the center of your controller).
  2. Select Download Profile.

    Note Not seeing Download Profile? Press X to sign out of your profile.

  3. Enter the Windows Live ID associated with your Xbox LIVE gamertag. If you don’t know your Windows Live ID or password, see Manage your Windows Live ID.
  4. Now, enter your Windows Live ID password.

    Note If your account is an Xbox LIVE child account, the parent’s Windows Live ID information is required to download the profile.

  5. Select the storage device for your profile (for example, Hard Drive).

    Your profile will begin downloading. Once your profile is downloaded, you can choose whether or not you want to require your Windows Live ID password to sign into your profile.

Remember Password option

The Remember Password option is off by default, which means you’ll be prompted for your Windows Live ID password when you sign into your Xbox LIVE profile.

  1. Leave the Remember Password unchecked (off), if you want to be prompted for your Windows Live ID password when you sign into your profile on this console.

    Note If you’re downloading your profile to your own console, you can select the Remember Password option so that you aren’t prompted for your Windows Live ID password when you sign into your profile.

You can now sign in using your Xbox LIVE gamertag on this console.

Important If you downloaded your profile to another console and you chose the Remember Password option, you can remotely remove the password so that your profile can’t be accessed on the other console. To learn how to do this, see Password protect your Xbox LIVE profile.

Both M3U and WPL files are types of playlists . The two file types are used to store list of audio files with its associated information like file location, title, album, artists, and other relevant information.

The main difference between WPL and M3U is the principal application that uses them. WPL was created for and is used by Microsoft’s Windows Media Player. It is the default file format and all playlists you create with Windows Media Player uses WPL. M3U files were originally designed to house a list of audio files, such as MP3 files, but they can be used for video files as well.
