Computing Average Compensation

I am computing the average compensation for the highest 5 consecutive years of someone's work service for retirement purposes.  These years are capped at a 10% increase from year to year for computational purposes.  For instance: member made $20,000 first year, $23,000 second year, $25,000 third, ...  With a 10% cap we would only be able to use $22,000 for year two and $24,200 for year three in the computation. (10% above what they actually made or the previous years cap; whichever is less)

Another caveat is that some people don't always work full years but we must have 5 years (60 months) of data in order to accurately compute the final average.  For instance:  member worked full year in year one, 0.85 of a year in year two, 0.97 year year three, one year in year four, one year in year five.  If you add those up you only have 4.82 years so I will need to "take" 0.18 of a year from the preceding year in order for make a full 5 years.  Below is an example of what the data entry portion of the worksheet would look like.  Further complicating matters is there are approved increases that exempt the increase from being capped, from year to year.  What the formula would do is look at the 10% cap and if the salary is capped then look to see if there in a exemption amount to apply.

Fiscal Year              Amount of Credit              Actual Earnings       Exemption Amount
   2011                                 1.0                                   $30,000
   2010                                 1.0                                   $28,000
   2009                                 0.97                                 $25,000                    $1500.00
   2008                                 0.85                                 $23,000
   2007                                  1.0                                   $20,000
   2006                                  1.0                                   $19,000

The final output look like the following

Fiscal Year               Amount of Credit            Earnings Used            Cap Applied?
   2011                                 1.0                                   $30,000                        No
   2010                                 1.0                                   $28,000                        No
   2009                                 1.0                                   $24,200                        Yes
   2008                                 1.0                                   $22,000                        Yes
   2007                                 1.0                                   $20,000                         No
  
To arrive at the final output table the formula began at the top fiscal year (in this case 2011) and began looking at the service credit.  Since FY 2011 = 1.0 then it used all the earning for that year.  Then year two the same.  Year three was not a full year so it needs to "borrow" 0.03 from year four.  This reduces the available amount of that year to 0.82.  I then need to borrow 0.18 from the next fiscal year and then again the following to come up with exactly 5 years.  Whatever portion of a fiscal year I borrow from that % of the salary is added to the previous.  An example (FY 2009 = 0.97 @ $25,000 and 0.03 @ $23,000 from 2008 would equal a total salary of $24,940.00 for FY 2009) (

My math may not be correct for all these but I wanted to just give you the theory.  I know this is a lot of data and I welcome your advice and help.  If you are wondering where this logic was derived; a state legislature!

First, you want to locate the text you want to remove from the document. You may need to use MS Word's "Find" feature to locate the text. Now, double click the text to highlight it. Then, click the "Home" tab.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

With the insertion point in one of the heading paragraphs, open the Styles pane, where the heading style will be selected. Click the New Style button at the bottom left of the pane. In the Create New Style from Formatting dialog, type a name for the style and set "Style based on" to "(no style)." This will preserve the font formatting, but the paragraph formatting will be returned to the Normal default, which means that the Spacing Before will be lost and will have to be restored, but the outline level will be changed to Body Text, which is what you want (it won't appear in the TOC).

Once you have created the style, apply it to the headings you don't want in the TOC.
