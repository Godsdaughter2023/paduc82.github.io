suzanne s. barnhill say:

By default, Ctrl+Page Down is assigned to Browse Next. When the browse object is set to Browse by Page (which it is by default), then you will get to the top of the next page. Page Down alone is assigned to PageDown, which actually skips down by one screen, which can be about half a page.

 

In Word 2013, there are no browse arrows (at the bottom of the vertical scroll bar in previous versions), but the rough equivalent of Select Browse Object is found by clicking the down arrow by the search bar in the Navigation pane. These do not include Page, but the Page Down and Ctrl+Page Down shortcuts still have the same function of advancing one screen and one page, and this is not affected by the selection of a browse object.

Note: The Litigation Hold feature is only available in Office 365 Enterprise E3/E4/E5, Office 365 Education and Exchange Online plan 2. For more information, see: Exchange Online Service Description

Sorry Ron, you are mistaken.  Each PC or MAC Office 365 is installed on counts as a single installation against the total of 5 allowed for 365 Home.  So, in this case the 2 users have a total count of 4 installations.

An exception to this rule is devices with screens 10.1 inches or smaller. They get Office for free. 

Another exception is non PC/MAC devices (like phones), they are tallied under a separate 5 count.
