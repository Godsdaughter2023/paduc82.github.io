jay freedman say:
The Font > Advanced > Position setting does apply to subscripts in the equation editor, and I can also confirm that it works in Word 2007, 2010, and 2013. You can also use it with superscripts, but raising a superscript by more than a point or two causes the top to be cut off in a way similar to using Exact line spacing.

suzanne s. barnhill say:
There's no way (that I know of) to change the default position of superscripts and subscripts, but you can use the Advanced tab of the Font dialog to raise or lower selected text. This applies to ordinary document text. If you are referring to equations created by the built-in equation editor, then this may not apply.

Formatting Page Numbers in a Table of Contents

I need to know how to format the pages numbers within a table of contetns to reflect the page numbers in a document. The document has multiple sections and the pages are numbered by section; for example, the pages in section 1 are numbered 1-1, 1-2, 1-3, etc. How can I get the table of contents to insert the page numbers in this way?

Thanks you very much in advance!


Scott

stefan blom say:
You have to use the built-in heading styles (Heading 1, Heading 2, etc.), apply numbering to them and then make use of "Include chapter numbers" in the Page Number Format dialog box. See http://shaunakelly.com/word/numbering/numbering20072010.html and http://word.mvps.org/faqs/numbering/chapternumber.htm.

pamcaswell say:

The TOC function will pick up chapter-numbered pages if you have used Word's chapter-numbering feature.  If you merely typed the "1-" in the footer, The TOC will not pick it up, and you will have to revised the page numbers each time you update the TOC. 

The chapter-numbering feature works with headings that are mutilevel list numbered.
