Hi,

Well, I had a few problems too but I think I overcame most of them.

My configuration:

  • Win7 Enterprise laptop (Lenovo Thinkpad T410), with Bluetooth, Wireless etc.
  • PC Nokia Suite (apparently the last): 7.1.51.0
  • Nokia E52 with latest SW and (maybe not latest) Firmware: 034.001.221.02
    (29. Apr. 2010)

My situation:

As I received my hopefully smart phone only friday sep. 3, I'm sure I haven't experienced every possible error yet. But, here we go:

  1. I created a PC Nokia Suite backup of my old phone
  2. I opened my new E52 phone, put in my SIM card, and enabled all networks, such as Internet (using both 3G and/or Wireless as I prefer to use this at Home)
  3. I started PC Nokia Suite to let it recognize a new Phone, suing Bluetooth. Of course I allowed my Nokia E52 to shows its presence. I got a password prompt asking to use the '123' password once my Nokia would tell me to allow incoming connection.
  4. I accepted the incoming Bluetooth on my E52, using the '123' password.
  5. And PC Nokia Suite proceeded.
  6. Soon it stopped, with the message 'Device not responding' (wording not precise)
  7. I realized some Bluetooth services on my Laptop wasn't enabled. See Device Manager - Properties - Services. I enabled all except one 'SyncML DM Client'.
  8. I also changed a setting in 'Device and Printers' by choosing the Property method 'Control' for my Nokia E52. I there changed (near bottom): Always allow this device to connect for

    After a while, I found a cure to this problem. I went into (I have to translate menus from Native Danish to English, so please don't bash me for wrong menu titles). I did a deep dive into my Nokia E52 like this:

    • Contr. panel - Connect - Bluetooth
    • RightNavigate, to the next tab (showing all Paired devices) and select your Laptop (with PC Nokia Suite)
    • Select 'Choose' - 'Select as approved'

      And voila ! My PC Nokia Suite now synchronizes without any prompts in my Nokia E52 !

      So, this 'Devices Paired' dialogue is a bit tricky to get into, since one has to righ-click, but it did the job for me :-)
