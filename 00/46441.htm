Hi,

It will not be accepted as a valid product key and it will not activate your Windows 10 license. The Windows 98 product key is for a Windows 98 license only. If you try to insert it on Windows 10, it will only be refuse as a valid product key.
If you want to activate your Windows 10 license, you need to buy a proper Windows 10 Product key (I suggest you to get one from Microsoft Store, that's the safest way).
The Windows 98 product key will be accepted if you install a Windows 98 OS on another device or another partition of your Pc, but can't be used to activate Windows 10.

dick_az say:

A few weeks ago my Media Center started to display the blue screen Low Bit Rate. Over time the frequency of this error increased. I searched the internet for users who were having the same or similar problem and tried several posted solutions; including one from Microsoft. Just the other day I started to get a warning message that one of my hard drives was starting to fail and I should back it up and replace the drive. It was my "D" drive that was getting ready to fail. I have my Media Center use my "D" drive for recording videos.
To make a long story short, I purchased a new hard drive and cloned my "D" drive. Once this was done the Low Bit Rate problem went away.

Often, when a disk drive starts to have read/write problems it will temporarily cause the computer freeze up while it recovers from the read/write error. It usually recovers, but it will get more difficult over time. However, this recovery triggers the Media Center Low Bit Rate. My "D" drive might have been failing because of a mechanical problem, but it was failing and getting worst over time. That's why the frequency of my Low Bit Rate error increased.

So, you might want to check your disk(s) for errors. Or change the drive that Media Center uses to record to see it the problem goes away when only running Media Center.
Anyway. I just thought I'd share my experience and solution with Media Low Bite Rate.
