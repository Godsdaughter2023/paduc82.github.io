suzanne s. barnhill say:

To add to what Paul has said, in order to configure your footnotes and endnotes, you must click the dialog launcher (small arrow) in the bottom right corner of the Footnotes group of the References tab. Do this once for the first footnote you enter and once for the first endnote. Choose the desired settings for footnotes or endnotes and then click Insert to insert the first one.  Once you have the settings as desired, you can use the Insert Footnote and Insert Endnote buttons on the References tab or (more efficiently) use these keyboard shortcuts:

Alt+Ctrl+F to insert a footnote.

Alt+Ctrl+D to insert an endnote.

macropod say:

Hi Danny,

Provided you Chapters are separated by Section breaks and you only have one Section per chapter, this is quite easy to do. If not, it's impossible.

For the footnotes, simply choose the number format (a, b, c or i, ii, iii) and position you want (bottom of page), with numbering re-starting for each Section.

For the endnotes, again, simply choose the number format (1,2,3) and position you want (end of document), with numbering re-starting for each Section.

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
OK, select the word that you believe should be marked, then go to Tools> Language to see which of the languages is highlighted. The odds are it will be something other than what you expected it to be. Make sure there is no check in the 'Do not check spelling & grammar' box. Assuming I'm right about the erroneous language just go ahead & close the dialog.

Select all of the document (Command+A) then open the dialog again to choose your appropriate language formatting. Again, confirm that there is no check in the 'Don't check..' box, click Default, then click OK. You should now see the markup for any text spelled incorrectly as determined by the proofing tools for that language.

If the problem persists there are a few things to verify before going any further:
  • Verify current update level of OS X
  • Verify current update level of Office
  • Repair disk permissions
  • Restart your Mac
Then do the following & answer the related questions;
  1. Is this one specific document or does the feature not work in any document? Create a new blank document then type - Do Not copy/paste - a word that was wrong but unmarked in the other document. Does the markup appear when you press the space bar?
  2. Does the main spell checking feature recognize the words as misspelled?
  3. Launch Word in a new User Account to see if you encounter the problem there.
  4. Hold Shift while you launch Word in your regular account - Does the issue continue?
Regards,
Bob J.

P.S. - More detailed information on how the proofing tools work in Word is available here:

http://word.mvps.org/mac/SpellCheck.html

wotasnoj say: I had the same problem of misspelled words not being highlighted, and the answers provided so far are only partial.

With no text selected, in the "Tools > Language..." menu option, you need to uncheck the box that says "Do not check spelling or grammar" and THEN click the "Default..." button.  You will be asked to confirm, and notified that this will affect all documents based on the template (by default the NORMAL template) on which the document is based.  Click "Yes", and then "OK" to return to the document.

Once you have done this (and subject to the other checks that people have mentioned such as the spelling and grammar preferences), you should find that all new documents based on that template will highlight misspelled words (and questionable grammar) by default.

Note that if you use a variety of templates, you will need to reset the defaults for each of them individually.
