gabriel bratton say:

The reason your calendar and mail is not syncing to your account is because it is connecting as ExchangeActiveSync.  The Outlook Team has blocked Outlook from connecting with EAS. 

To workaround this issue do the following:

1.  Open Outlook

2.  Click File, Add Account

3.  Enter your Name, Email address, and Password and click Next through the wizard

At this point your account will now be listed twice in Outlook.  If you had added any local data such as contacts or calendar items in Outlook you can copy them over to the new account you added which will be syncing.

Finally, you should remove the old copy of your account from the Profile

1.  Open Outlook

2.  Click File, Account Settings, Account Settings, locate the account that is Type Microsoft Exchange and click on it and make it the Default account.

3.  Click on the old copy of your account listed as Type ExchangeActiveSync and click Remove. 

At this point you should only have your new Type Microsoft Exchange account left in your profile.

In case you need it here is the Help Topic for the reconnect process, https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Reconnect-Outlook-2016-or-Outlook-2013-to-Outlook-com-for-uninterrupted-email-access-cda1751d-9503-40bf-bf76-e79454ac5eb3?ui=en-US&rs=en-US&ad=US.  I put the steps above as a modified version of the Alternative option listed in this topic.
