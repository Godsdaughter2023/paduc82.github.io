a. user say:

Is your WiFi adapter supported on Windows 10? If it isn't supported on Windows 10, or the most recent version of Windows 10, it may not work well or it may not work at all.

Hardware manufacturers decide whether or not to keep their devices compatible with new versions of Windows 10. That's what we mean by 'supported.' Once a hardware manufacturer decides to stop supporting a device, they stop testing their device on Windows 10 and they don't update the driver to keep up with the changes to Windows 10.

It's easy to find out whether your WiFi adapter is supported. You'll need the manufacturer's name and the model number. Go to the web page for that model on the manufacturer's website and look for the section that displays drivers.

If you see that the manufacturer has a Windows 10 driver for your adapter, life is good: That means the manufacturer is supporting that adapter on Windows 10. Install the latest Windows 10 driver and follow the manufacturer's installation instructions carefully. If you're still having trouble, the manufacturer's technical support will help you.

If you discover that your adapter is not supported on Windows 10, all is not lost. Sometimes a driver for Windows 8.1 will work fine. Even a Windows 7 driver might work, although it's less likely.

If there's no Windows 10 driver, and an earlier driver doesn't work, it's time to replace your WiFi adapter with one that is supported on Windows 10. I actually had to do that myself, and my new WiFi adapter works perfectly well with the newest version of Windows 10.

          b)      Put a check mark on the last line that says - Connect to non-preferred networks
          c)       Click close.

6. Now go to device manager by typing Device Manager Button in the start search orb.

7. In Device Manager, click on the "+" beside Network Adapters to expand.

8. Right click and select un-install to remove the Wireless Network device and confirm removal on the warning window.

9. Restart your computer.

Upon startup Windows should automatically re-install the Wireless Network Device drivers.
Upon completion of installation, a pop-up window will appear stating "One or more wireless networks detected, click here for more options".

10. Click on the balloon to bring up the "Available Wireless Networks Windows".

You should now be able to see all available Wireless Networks in range.

11. Select desired Access Point to connect to then once connected, you should be able to browse the internet with no problems.

If you’ve turned off your security software, by now you should enable them back.

db-555 say:

I faced a similar issue after upgrading to Windows 10 on my ASUS VivoBook X202E-DH31T Ultrabook.
I rolled back the elan input device driver and it seems to have fixed the issue in my case.
I tried this based on reading the followig thread:
http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/insider/forum/insider_wintp-insider_devices/windows-10-technical-preview-touchpad-issues/835b5427-5e47-44c1-b801-162403074cf2?auth=1

Here are the steps I followed:
1. Go to Control Panel -> Hardware and Sound
2. Open Device Manager
3. Expand the option for Mice and other pointing device
4. In my case one of the devices listed there was Elan input device
5. Double click on Elan input device
6. Click the Driver tab in the popup (in my case, the driver date was 7/7/2015
driver version 15.8.4.3)
7. Click Roll Back Driver button
8. It rolled the driver back to microsoft ps/2 mouse, drive date 6/21/2006, driver version 10.0.10240.16384
9. You now have to restart your computer for the change to take effect
10. After restart the touchpad and buttons on it seem to work ok.
Not sure if there are any negative side effects to rolling back the drver, but it did fix the issue.
