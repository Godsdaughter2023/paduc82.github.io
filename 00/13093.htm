I certainly understand your frustration. I have it myself.  As previously mentioned, I would set up a partnership with your device first using another connection method (depending on the device, usually usb).

I have found the key to this bluetooth relationship is that when you do discovery on the bluetooth relationship between your phone and the computer,  the serving device (the computer) must be broadcasting that it provides the active sync service along with the com port.  At least that is how it is on my Samsung 911.  If that doesn't show up when you discover the PC from your phone ("ActiveSync" that is), then you may need to assign active sync to the com port in your bluetooth configuration on the PC.  That all depends on the version of bluetooth on your PC.   My recommendation is to go to the phone provider or your carrier for support.  They will have the most discrete information on your particular device.

In my case, even though the phone is recognized via bluetooth with the PC and I can ftp files, etc., activesync will not always recognize it.  To resolve it sometimes requires that I actually have activesync scan for connection on all the available com ports, etc.  At the same time, I have my phone discover the device all over again.  Sometimes it broadcasts "Activesync" as a service, sometimes not.  I will also try to "bluetooth connection" on the phone's activesync screen while the PC is attempting to discover the activsync partner... 

Overall, it is extremely time consuming, nerve racking, and frustrating.  I am almost to the point where I will just revert back to USB on the master PC and forget bluetooth on the master PC for activesync.

Good luck

Printing sections of a document - Microsoft Community

oneeasygeezer say:

Printing sections of a document

I have a word document with numerous sections from which from time to time I need to print specific sections only. Is there an easy way to identify the section number I need  to print from and to rather than having to count the section breaks from the start of the document, I thought it may be easier to use print selection but that omits the page numbers in the footer.
suzanne s. barnhill say:
By default, earlier versions of Word put the section number in the status bar. In Word 2007 and above, this is a user choice. Right-click on the status bar and click Section (the second item on the menu). While you're there, you may find other options you want to add. But now you'll be able to see the section number of the portion you want to print.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

If your printer prints last page first, then it will not duplex correctly unless you have an even number of pages. Word will take care of this in the case of sections in which numbering is restarted at 1: even if you use a Next Page break, it will be converted to an Odd Page Break and a blank even page inserted at the end of the previous section if necessary. So if you're printing the entire document, the only real problem will come at the end, where you may need to insert a blank page.

 

In printing sections, however, the problem is a little more subtle: if you use Page X of Y numbering in a section with an Odd Page section start, and there is a blank even page at the end of the previous section, you will find that Word actually considers that page to be part of the following section. So if your section has, say, 25 pages, you'll get Page 1 of 26, Page 2 of 26, up to Page 25 of 26 for the last page (if there is any solution to this problem, I have not run across it). It is doubtless this oddity that causes the problem in printing. And note that Word is not duplexing correctly if it's printing page 1 by itself; the pages are printing double-sided, but not with the correcting pairing, since page 2 should be on the back of page 1, pages 3 and 4 together, etc.

 

There may be other workarounds, but the only one I can think of is to print the last page separately, then duplex the rest.
