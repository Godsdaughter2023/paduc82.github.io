bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

You are correct - Office 2004 was coded to run on Macs with PowerPC microprocessors (G4, G5). When Apple transitioned to Intel processors/OS X they initially provided the Rosetta Emulator which translated PowerPC code to what the Intel chip required. However, Apple withdrew support for Rosetta when they introduced OS X 10.7 (Lion), rendering the legacy software unusable on Intel Macs running that version of OS X or later.

If you want Office you have several options:

  • Although no longer supported, Office 2008 still may be available from some sources.. Just be careful who you buy from :-)
  • Office 2011 can be bought in the traditional 'outright' manner -- the perpetual license entitles you to continue using it as long as technology supports it, but there is no upgrade program, or
  • You can subscribe to Office 365 which provides Office 2011 for local installation as well as an upgrade to the next release when available along with other benefits. You can 'pay as you go' (monthly) or get a reduced rate for an annual subscription.

See this Office:mac web page for more info.

In all honesty, based on your described needs, you may want to consider Apple's Pages (word processing) & Numbers (spreadsheet). There also are other low cost options available such as LibreOffice [donationware] as well as others. If you need compatibility with Office for Windows users, though, I'd suggest staying with Mac Office.
