Is the headset providing sound (i.e. the kid can hear stuff), but the microphone isn't working? Or what exactly doesn't work with the headset?

Be aware, that if your kid uses a headset with a 3.5mm audio jack that has three rings on it, that there are two standards called "OMTP" and "CTIA", where the microphone connector and ground connector are reversed. 

For the headset to work on the Xbox controller, the headset has to be CTIA compliant. If your kid has an OMTP compliant headset, you can go to a cellphone store, and ask for an OMTP to CTIA adapter cable (or since these cables simply cross two contacts, a CTIA to OMTP adapter works as well).

Also be aware, that since the cable simply crosses two wires, if the price for that cable is more than the price for a coffee to go, you're being ripped off.

Also, for more troubleshooting ask your kid to go to the message area, and try to compose a message to anyone from their friend list. 

Instead of typing a text message, have your kid click on the Microphone symbol and then talk into the microphone. After the recording is finished, they should try to listen to their message. 

If they can't hear themselves, it's probably a headset issue like I mentioned above. 

If the voice message could be recorded, your kid might have a network issue. Have them check the NAT status in the console's network settings.
