rohn007 say:

Creating the rules is a little tricky, it took me half an hour to beat excel into submission


You can create 2 conditional rules in the B column

  1. enter some sample dates in col A and B
  2. Select col b by single clicking in it
  3. Home tab > Styles group > Conditional Formatting drop down button
  4. Manage Rules (last option in the drop down list)
  5. New Rule
  6. "Format only cells that contain", "Cell Value", "Greater Than", "=$A1", Format button (pic the color, ie RED)
  7. OK, OK, Apply

That creates the first rule. Now go back to step 5 in the procedure above, 

      6. "Format only cells that contain", "Cell Value", "LESS Than or EQUAL TO", "=$A1", Format button (pic the color, ie GREEN)


NOTE: that the cell condition =$A1  only has 1 "$" sign in front of the column. If there is a $ infront of the row number you won't be able to copy the format (that caused a little hair pulling).

After you have applied the 2 formatting rules to the cell you can OK to close the "New Formatting Rule" dialog.

Now, assuming the B1 cell still has focus (you haven't clicked anywhere else) in the Home tab > Clipboard group > click on the "Format Painter button then click and drag down the rest of the B column to apply the rules to all of the cells.

clare_hs say:

Hi DjWhy,

Thank you for reaching out to us on the community forum. We understand you have trouble paring your Microsoft Band to a new phone, correct?

First, Please try connecting you band to a computer and use Microsoft Band syn app to sync your data to ensure the latest data is synced with your MS account. You can install Microsoft Sync App for Windows or Microsoft Sync App for MAC, depending on what computer you use. After signing in in the app, tap Sync.

After syncing your data, you will have to reset your Microsoft Band in order to pair it with you new phone again. Please see the steps below:

  • Step 1: Unregister your Microsoft Band from your phone
  • Step 2: Remove your Microsoft Band from your phone’s list of paired Bluetooth devices
  • Step 3: Reset your Microsoft Band
  • Step 4: Set up your Microsoft Band again

After the above steps, you can pair it with your new phone.

Here’s how to pair your band with the Microsoft Health app on your phone:

Step 1:    Press the power button on your Microsoft Band, and tap Get Started.
Step 2:    On your phone, open the Microsoft Health app, and sign in with your Microsoft account.
If you don’t have a Microsoft account, tap Sign up now to create one.
Step 3:    Go to Bluetooth in your phone’s settings, and tap MSFT Band in the list of devices to pair.
(On some phones, pairing occurs within the Microsoft Health app.)
Step 4:    Compare the PIN codes on the phone and the band.
If they match, accept the pairing on both the phone and the band.
If they don’t, tap cancel on your Microsoft Band, and repeat Step 3.

If your phone doesn’t automatically return to the Microsoft Health app, tap your phone’s Back button. When the pairing is complete, you’ll see Success! on your Microsoft Band and in the Microsoft Health app on your phone.

Press the action button to start using your Microsoft Band. A flashing arrow will point to the action button.

Your phone may download and install updates to the Microsoft Health app. If this happens, tap next when you see Update complete!

If you still have trouble paring your Microsoft Band and the phone, please see our support articleTroubleshoot problems with Bluetooth and sync.

We hope this helps.

Kind regards,

Clare
