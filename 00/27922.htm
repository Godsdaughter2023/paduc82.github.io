- Click Control Panel.

- Locate Internet Explorer 11 by scrolling  at the  Uninstall an update window or by typing Internet Explorer at the Search install Updates box.

- Right click Internet Explorer 11 to Uninstall it.

- The Uninstall an update window will pop up.  Click Yes to uninstall the update.

- A Microsoft Windows message of "Please wait while the updates are being uninstalled.  This might take several minutes." will be displayed.

- After several minutes, a Microsoft Windows message "You must restart your computer to apply these changes.  Before restarting save any open files and close all programs." will be displayed.

- Restart the computer and follow the instructions.

- After the computer boots up,  click Control Panel (to check if the Internet Explorer 10 was reverted back).

- Click Programs.

- From the Program and Features, click View install updates.

- Locate Internet Explorer 10 by scrolling at the Uninstall an update window or by typing Internet Explorer at the Search install Updates box..

- Another method to check if Internet Explorer was reverted back is to run the Internet Explorer 10 browser.

- Click the Tools and click About Internet Explorer.  The Internet Explorer 10 window will be displayed.

- NOTE:   The Internet Explorer 11 or 10 will not be seen if you click "Uninstall or change a program" from the Control Panel.  The Internet Explorer 11 or 10 can be seen when you click the "Uninstall an updates" of the Control Panel.  This discussion or comment was written for individual with few computer knowledge or experience.  I hope this information will help others.

- After successfully reverting to Internet Explorer 10, perform "Defragmentation" at the local disk.

karl timmermans (outlook mvp 2012-2018) say:
At the risk of ending up with an answer that is not one that anyone wants to hear or considered not to be helpful, going to address some basic points in the description followed by some general comments

>>
.......because SharePoint does not support Contact Groups
<<

Don't know of any viable solution that would deal with Contact Groups but reality is, not sure why it would be necessary. Unless a contact's name or email address was modified - anything else has no impact on a Contact Group. Assumption here is that Contact Groups refers to those created within Outlook using the Create Contact Group process versus Global Distribution Groups.

>>
Is there any way to sync the contacts held in different address books, so that changing the contact in one book would automatically update that same contact in all the other books, if they exist?
<<

There's no native process that provides this capability

>>
Do all the contact updaters just have to remember to communicate with each other?
<<

That's a seriously poor approach to maintaining any kind of data integrity - the potential for things to get messed up even with the strictest of procedures in place is enormous for all kinds of reasons.

Just to give one very simple example of a potential scenario where you have two people are modifying the same contact duplicated in two folders
Person1 - adds info to the Contact Notes field and changes the phone #
Person2 - adds info to the Contact Notes field and changes the phone # with a value is different from that entered by Person1 but both are valid numbers for the contact

Which contact record is considered to be (the most) accurate?

>>
Or is there an entirely different and better way to do this?
<<

The answer to this question would be considered subjective by many but there's no question that the answer is "Yes". However, whether or not a better process can be implemented in the type of environment described in terms of data "ownership and control" is highly questionable.

General comment(s)

Reality is that unless and until there is a single item per contact - there is no sure fire way to properly manage the data. Will always be a scenario where "sync" will be part of the equation where multiple people can make changes to multiple copies of what is ultimately considered to be a single contact item.

One better way - in very simplistic terms.....................

- Maintain all contacts in a central database giving the required users appropriate permissions to a single contact item
- Every XX hrs - (let's say once a day for sake of argument) - replace all contacts in each public contact folder (otherwise referred to as an Address Book/List) with the current records in the database. The frequency of this process would be determined on how critical the changes are from a business perspective

alan wu msft say:

Hi Tom_IU8,

Greetings. 

Please be noted that different Address Book has its own policy (named Address Book Policy). In a general word, each Address Book is independent on the other one. That is to say, there are no synchronization among the different Address Books. 

Although it is not feasible to do so in different Address Books, it also provides a clue that we can put the different users (actually they stand for the same user) in  the same Address Book. Under this scenario, the synchronization can be occurred. 

Here, I'd like to emphasize that the above mentioned is the ideal thought and it should be considered with your actual situation. If you are allowed to do so, you can refer to Office 365 Address Book Policy (ABP).
