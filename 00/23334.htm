    Switching Exchange Online Plans - Microsoft Community

    igor lishnevsky say:

    Switching Exchange Online Plans

    Hello,

    We have about 785 licensed users in the organization, all with Plan 2.

    Soon we are going to change it to 200 Plan 2 license and the rest is Plan 1.

    What is the procedure of subscription activation and switching those 585 users to Plan 1 ?

    Is there an easy way to do it?

    Thanks.

    vincentchoy say:

    "What will happen when I activate 200 Plan 2 Licenses along with my 700 Plan 2 currently expiring?

    I will have 900 for some time and then I will have 200 after the expiration?"

    I suspect Alan reply might be incorrect.

    What happens depends on what kind of license you have.

    If you have CSP license, which is computed on a daily basis, you can switch plans anytime.

    I assume you have instead, Open license, based on your situation. Open licenses are sold in 12 month blocks.

    The effect of adding users to an existing open license subscription is to extend the anniversary date for ALL users.


    Example - 

    If you purchased 700 Exchange Plan 2 Licenses in Jan at USD 8, and added 200 licenses in July. This is what happens.

    You will have 700 licenses from Jan - Jun and 900 licences from July onwards.

    Purchase in Jan 700 x USD 8 x 12mth = USD 67,200. Anniversary will be End Dec.

    Purchase in AUG 200 x USD 8 x 12 mth = USD 19,200. Note you still pay 12 months for these new users.

    Since you can't have 2 same license types expiring different times, Microsoft will instead recalculate your anniversary date-

    Total paid to date : USD 67,200 + USD 19,200 = USD 86,400

    Total used to date : USD 8 x 7 mth x 700 = USD 39,200

    Total Amount left = USD 86,400 - USD 39,200 = USD 47,200

    Total months left for 900 users from July onward = USD 47,200 / 900 users / USD 8 = 6.55 months.

    Your expiry date will have shifted to Mid Feb. (I may get my months confused but you get the idea).

    jeeped say:

    I have made a form where I can enter dates in Column B and the program automatically gives a date 30, 45, and 60 days later in columns C, D & E respectively.


    If the date in question is in B1, try the following in C1,
    =IF($B1>40000,$B1+15+COLUMNS($B:B)*15,"")
    ... modify the row number if necessary and then fill right to column E.

    Dates are numbers with each day representing 1 past 01-Jan-1900. TODAY() is currently 40812 so the formula above checks to see if B1 contains a number greater than 40,000 (e.g. 06-Jul-2009) before adding 30-60 days to it. If your actual date range is wider than that date window, modify the 40000 constant.

      ceroberts75 say:
      well, by standard, synchronization means to make it the same in one place as the other, so that is what is happening because by default, gmail works (as does most accounts on mobile now) as IMAP4 where it will sync the data on your device to match what is on the server. so if you delete a message, it deletes there too.

      the only way around this is going to be to set up your account as an "other/imap/pop" email.

      this means that instead of allowing the device to auto-setup your email account you are going to need to do it manually and not allow it to pull the settings from the server.


      then I believe the settings are these below.

      •Gmail POP server address: pop.gmail.com
      •Gmail POP user name: Your full Gmail address (example@gmail.com, for example)
      •Gmail POP password: Your Gmail password
      •Gmail POP port: 995
      •Gmail POP TLS/SSL required: yes


      that should do it for you.

      then you can choose in your settings to only delete the email from your device and not the server.
