If that doesn't work, press and hold the Volume down and Power buttons at the same time until you feel a vibration (about 10 to 15 seconds), then release the buttons. Your phone will restart automatically.


Please be reminded that:

Your phone may vibrate as soon as you press the Volume down and Power buttons because it's changing your ringtone to vibrate. Wait to release the buttons until you feel the second vibration 10 to 15 seconds later.

If you don't let go of the buttons immediately after the vibration at 10 to 15 seconds, your phone may show a black startup screen instead of restarting normally. If that happens, try pressing the button combo again.

Not all phones restart this way, so check with your phone's manufacturer to find out how to force a restart.


If your phone has a replaceable battery (it's often under your phone's back panel), you can also restart your phone by removing and reinserting the battery, and then pressing the Power button to turn on your phone. jllatham say:

Why not track down 'powerfulvba' and get a copy of what was demo'd in the YouTube video?  Why reinvent a perfectly round wheel?  Personally, I think what we saw was a bit overkill for an expense or travel report.  The company I have my day job with uses a simple one-sheet fixed layout Excel tool with most information entered pretty much free-hand, with a few formulas to make sure the addition of the $$ values is done accurately and it's worked fine for us for over 15 years - it has some header information to identify the employee, the reason for the report (including being able to identify the contract it is to be charged against), where to mail the reimbursement check to.  Then it's just a bunch of rows where data is entered for
Date         Description of Expense                    Quantity         Unit Cost        Total Cost

We don't even break down expenses by category, but it wouldn't be difficult to slip in another column with a drop-down (data validated list) of expense codes for things like airline ticket, fuel, lodging, meals, taxi or rental car, etc.

How much of what we saw there would actually be needed in your tool?  For example, they appear to have monetary conversions going on?  That's going to require some method of keeping the conversion rates up to date - with the best way being a query to an online service providing the conversions.  Otherwise it's a manual process of keeping that table up to date.

A similar issue with the travel codes for airlines - at some point you've got to put the database of ICAO identifiers together to populate those drop down lists.  Although there wouldn't be much future maintenance of them for that list required.

From the final few seconds of the video, it looked like it was outputing a lot of expense reports - but I couldn't tell much about them: for one person over a long period of time or several people for a shorter period; exactly how they were laid out, etc.

All in all, while it looked like a really spiffy Excel tool, it also looks like one better suited to Access.  Especially if you have a fairly large organizationi filing expense reports and you'd like to be able to gather general information at a level other than the individual, such as "how much does Division Y spend on air travel", or "how much did the company reimburse for meals in March of 2011"? 

You need to be able to say HOW the package works: are you going to give a copy to each employee and let them enter their data and just submit a printout of the expense report?  Or would you prefer they send in the electronic equivalent of a single expense report and feed that into a central database type of structure?

For the project to go quickly whether you use Excel, a database like Access or mySQL or SQL Server Personal edition, Word or Notepad you need to do some pre-planning to define your requirements.  You need to at least outline the various data input screens; defining what information is mandatory, what is optional, and how it should be manipulated (as with the currency conversion thing).  Same thing for the various output reports.  If you can have those requirement 'specifications' ready when you start putting it together, it will go much quicker with less regrouping to cover an area that you missed somewhere along the line.  And if you contract the job out, it will give the developer (or whoever might volunteer to assist you) targets to hit and checkpoints to examine to see whether things are going as you intended or not.

Most of what I saw in the video wasn't too far out there in terms of implementing and coding.  Most of the coding would be associated with the actual expense report outputs.  But there is definitely some planning to be done before you even open a clean Excel workbook to begin creating the application; defining all of your expense categories, planning on what types of reports to generate and how they'll be laid out, and as I mentioned previously - the data input screens 'tree'.

In closing, I'll say I'd ALMOST be willing to assist you, but I will be heading out for a 2-week road trip myself at the end of this week and plan on being pretty much totally out of contact during that time.  And to try to give you an approximation of how large the effort might be - I would say, just from what I saw in the video, that with good pre-planning and close coordination between you and any help you get, you could plan on about a week of development to get to a baseline product you could really begin wringing it out.  Longer possibly depending on whether you also centralize a database file to collect individual expense report data into for more generalized accounting reports on various expenses.
