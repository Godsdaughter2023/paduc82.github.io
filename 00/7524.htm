doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

Using CTRL+F9 to insert each pair of field delimiters, use

{ IF { MERGEFIELD [nameoffieldincolumnC] \* Charformat } = "WE WILL CONTACT YOU TO SCHEDULE." "we will contact you to schedule." "your appointment is on { MERGEFIELD [nameoffieldincolumnD] } at { MERGEFIELD [nameofieldincolumnE] } }

Note, the \* Charformat switch is added to the first mergefield to force the result to UPPER CASE (based on the M of MERGEFIELD).  Otherwise the the If...then...Else field is case sensitive.

macropod say:

macropod say:

Try a field constructed as:

{ IF { MERGEFIELD Appointment \* Upper } = "W*" "we will contact you to schedule." "your appointment is on «AppointmentDate» at «AppointmentTime»."}

where:

• 'Appointment' is the name of the field in column C;

• 'AppointmentDate' is the name of the field in column D; and

• 'AppointmentTime' is the name of the field in column E.

Note: The field brace pairs (i.e. '{ }') for the above example are all created in the document itself, via Ctrl-F9 (Cmd-F9 on a Mac); you can't simply type them or copy & paste them from this message. Nor is it practical to add them via any of the standard Word dialogues. Likewise, you can't type or copy & paste the chevrons (i.e. '« »') - they're part of the actual mergefields, which you can insert from the 'Insert Merge Field' dropdown. The spaces represented in the field construction are all required.

Insert all the necessary information into the Microsoft Account or primary Windows Live (Windows Phone 7) profile so it can work as main email address in your Windows Phone

Every time you try to access to the Windows Phone 8 Store appears a message telling you need to set up My Family even though you are an adult? Can't you sign in your Windows Phone because your password results wrong though it is right? Every time you try to sign in your Windows Phone appears an error code?

Most of the times these kind of problems are due to the lack of information in your email address profile. All you need to do is complete it following the steps below.

Note: basically there is not difference between Microsoft Account and primary Windows Live. Both of them are respectively the main email address of Windows Phone 8 and Windows Phone 7. 

1. Go to this web page http://account.live.com and click on the blue link Sign In.

2. It will apprear another web page. Click on Basic Info and Edit then. Insert the following information: date of birth; gender; country; time zone. This will permit to the system to know your reference Store, if you are an adult or not, the reference time zone in order to set up an event on the right time in the calendar. Once you insert all the info click on Save.


3.  Once you finish go to http://www.xbox.com and click on Sign In. 


4. Sign In using the just created email account and related password. If you are asked for accepting the new terms of conditions and create a Xbox Live account, please do it; it is free and necessary.



5. Click on My Account, at the upper right corner of the web page. 


If you can't see My Account at the upper right corner of the web page, you can click on My Account below the picture of your Avatar (the human being image). 

6. Click on the green written Payment & Billing, below the icon of the credit card. 

7.  It will appear another web page which title is “Help Us Protect your Account”, see the emage below. Clicking on the arrow you will be able to select the way how to receive the secure secret temporary code. In the second space you have to confirm the last four digits of the selected phone number (if you have decided to receive the code by phone text message), or the email address you selected (if you have decided to receive the code by email). 

Click on Next then. 


8. Insert the received code in the proper space and click on Submit. 


9. Click on Billing Info. 

10. A window ,with two options at its lower side, will be opened. Click on the option which is at the right side, it could be Add Billing Info or Change Billing Address (if the billing info were already inserted). 

11. Full fill the form with all the required information. I would suggest to insert your mobile phone number, it will make managing your account easier in the future. 

Click on Next when you finish. 

12. Click on Next below the Billing Info resume. 

13. Close the window when you finish. 

Your new email account is ready to be used. 

Now is time to work on your Windows Phone. 

  1. Switch on the phone. 

  2. Take your phone and check the following data are correct: date, time, time zone, region. 

  3. On Date + Time disable Automatic, if the option is not present,it means it is set up on Manually already. 

  4. Connect your phone to Internet. 

  5. Flick the screen from the right to the left.

  6. Tap Emails + Accounts.

  7. Tap Microsoft Account (Windows Live if you have a Windows Phone 7).

  8. Insert your new email account and related password. 

  9. Tap on Sign In. 

Note: Don't set up more than one Windows Live account in your Windows Phone 7. 

If you find this post useful please click mark it. 

Bye.
