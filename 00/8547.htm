Conditional Formatting with 3 options? - Microsoft Community

thomasbeshke say:

Conditional Formatting with 3 options?

I apologize upfront with this question.  I searched countless articles already posted, but it's all making my head spin.  Here is what I'm looking to accomplish:

If a date on a spreadsheet is less than 7 days old, then I want that cell to be filled green.

If a date on a spreadsheet is more than 7 days old but less than 14 days old, then I want that cell to be filled yellow.

If a date on a spreadsheet is more than 14 days old, then I want it to be filled red.

I have a =TODAY() at the top of the spreadsheet.

I'm so confused, I do not even know where to put the formulas - meaning to I make all of the comparisons in a hidden area of the spread sheet and then use an IF/THEN function?

Please help! :)

ossiemac say:
  1. Select the range of dates (A2:A34) in screen capture example below. I am in d/m/y date region so I have used the alpha month in the Number format so as not to confuse. Conditional format is already applied to the screen capture example.
  2. On Home ribbon select "Conditional formatting"
  3. Select "Manage rules"
  4. Select "New rule"
  5. Select "Use a formula to determine which cells to format"
  6. Enter the following formula in the field    =TODAY()-A2 <=7
  7. Select "Format" button
  8. Select "Fill" tab
  9. Select required color (Green)
  10. Click "OK" and "OK" again
  11. Select "New rule"
  12. Repeat from step 4 to 11 above but formula will be   =AND(TODAY()-A2 > 7,TODAY()-A2<=14)  and set color to Yellow
  13. Repeat again from step 4 to 10 above but formula will be   =TODAY()-A2 > 14    and set color to Red
  14. When all 3 conditions are entered, Click "Apply" button and the "OK" button to

    Note that the formula is written as if it applies to the first cell of the selected range and Excel applies the relative formula to the remaining cells in the range. You will need to edit A2 in the formula accordingly for the first cell of the selected range.
