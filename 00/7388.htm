dmsheig say:

Office 365 RMS and DLP

What is the differnce between Office 365 DLP and Office 365 RMS, RMS does include DLP ? And if yes, how is it working ?
linda wu msft say:

Hi DMSHEIG,

No, they are different things, Data Loss Prevention (DLP) & Ativate Rights Management both provides a comprehensive way in which customers can protect their data in during the lifecycle of that data including data in motion, at rest and in use.

1. The DLP Suite comprises a comprehensive data loss prevention solution that enables customers to: it works for specific files which match with the DLP rule.

    a. Discover and protect sensitive data in the enterprise. Leverages common policies across the infrastructure to discover and protect sensitive data in the datacenter, on the network, and on endpoints.

    b. Mitigate risk. Mitigates risk through identity aware policy based remediation and enforcement.

    c. Reduce Total Cost of Ownership. Reduces TCO with industry leading scalability, automated protection of sensitive data, and most comprehensive policy library.

    d. Simplify security operations. Streamlines the security operations process with incident handling and workflows, and by integrating the RSA DLP Suite with enVision.

For your reference:

2. Office 365 RMS is also called Office 365 IRM. Information Rights Management (IRM) allows individuals and administrators to specify access permissions to documents, workbooks, and presentations. This helps prevent sensitive information from being printed, forwarded, or copied by unauthorized people. After permission for a file has been restricted by using IRM, the access and usage restrictions are enforced no matter where the information is, because the permission to a file is stored in the document file itself. It works for all files containing in a sharepoint list or library  which has been enabled IRM.

For your reference:
Management (RMS) in the Office 365 admin center
(IRM) in SharePoint admin center

Linda
