Hello Shridhar:

Thank you for posting to the forum regarding the questions you have about the Windows Phone.  I would be happy to clarify these things for you to the best of my abilities.

In regards to the Denim update.  It does look like your model of phone is currently set to accept and receive the Denim update, https://www.microsoft.com/en/mobile/support/software-update/wp8-software-update/wp8-availability-in-asia-pacific/ . There is a chance that this is related to the developer preview, although the phone should still be able to accept updates pushed to the phone by the manufacturer and the carriers.  Before moving on, please review the document above and check your firmware revision against that listed in the document.  I have seen some reports of customers getting incorrect information from the extras + info application either telling them they had Denim when they did not or indicating they had Cyan when they really were running Denim.

If you are unable to get the phone updated via normal means you will want to try the following options to try and get the most recent firmware update installed.

  1. Use the Lumia recovery tool to freshly install the phones OS and firmware.  When searching the tool you should find that the OS is available with the firmware Denim, if your phone is truly ready to accept the Denim firmware update.  You can install the Lumia recovery tool here, . 
  2. The next thing to do is reach out to Lumia support to see if they have any other options to manually push the firmware update to your phone or to flash your phone with a more up to date firmware.

In regards to the size of the system section in storage sense, this sounds about right to me from my experience.  I have looked at many phones and found anywhere from 2.5 GB to 4 GB of system files storage.  The storage files are going to consist of the OS, firmware, other files needed to run the phone, some base application data and some temporary files.  There are a couple of things you might be able to do to clear up some additional space here but from my experience the amount of space cleared will be minor.

  1. Do a soft reset of the phone, including a clearing of temporary files and IE history.  You can do this by going to settings > storage sense > phone > temporary files > delete > Internet Explorer options > delete history.  Once you have done this, press and hold the down volume button + the power button until the phone shuts off and vibrates.  After the phone reboots, please go to settings > emails + accounts > tap and hold the Microsoft account listed and select the sync option.
  2. Delete applications from your phone and and then do one more soft reset of the phone. 

I did the above on my phone and took it from 4.07 GB in system storage to 3.96 GB of system storage.  Besides the suggestions made the only other thing I can suggest you consider is using the tool referenced above to reinstall your OS and firmware and see if this leads to a smaller system storage on the phone.

Please let us know if you have any other questions or concerns.

Pressing Enter repeatedly in a multilevel list "outdents" the list one level at a time; this is new behavior in Word 2007 (or possibly in Word 2010) and later.
