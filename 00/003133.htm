dale howard [mvp] say:

John --

I can duplicate this behavior.  The reason for what you see if because what you are doing makes no logical sense.  When you rescheduled the uncompleted work into the next week, it makes no sense to say that the Actual Finish date is in the previous week.  Did the work get completed last week or not?  If not, do not enter an Actual Finish date in last week.  If it did finish last week, do not reschedule the uncompleted work into this week.  You cannot have it both ways, my friend.

The reason that Microsoft Project calculates the Actual Duration as 2 days is because of the task split, caused by you rescheduling the uncompleted work until next week.  There is no work and no Duration scheduled for last week, other than the work and Duration already completed (2 days).  So, with the split in place (representing no work and no Duration), and then you enter an Actual Finish date in last week, Microsoft Project counts the 2 days of completed work and Duration, and nothing else.

Hope this helps.

spong say:

I had the same problem (Windows Update checked for updates, but didn't find any new Fast builds, despite toggling to/from Fast and Slow, then rerunning Check for Updates)

I'd also had the FBL_AWESOME problem earlier, and instead of waiting for the update, I'd used one of the registry hacks floating around to remove that and some other keys from the Windows SelfHost registry key.

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\WindowsSelfHost\Applicability

My values were just ThresholdOptedIn (DWORD) = 1 and ThresholdRiskLevel (String) = "low"

I wasn't finding any updates at all.

I went back through a search of articles I'd seen, and noticed that I'd misread them - I'd deleted the BranchName altogether or had fbl_release on it.

Instead, I set it to FBL_AWESOME1501, and then became applicable for a Windows Update to "fix" the "awesome problem", and here's what I ended up with after that was installed:

In HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\WindowsSelfHost\Applicability

BranchName   fbl_impressive

ThresholdOptedIn   1

ThresholdRiskLevel   low

And one WindowsUpdate Check for Updates later, I'm now pulling down 10041. Hope that helps someone!
