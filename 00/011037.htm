jeeped say:

… I'm looking to calculate the number of times 1 turns up in column D when column A equals the 1st of July, in the next row the number of times it turns up on the 2nd and so on …


Excel 2007/2010 has the newer COUNTIFS(…) function which would seem to suit your purposes. Translated from your example,

=COUNTIIFS('data sheet'!$A$2:$A$40,G$4,'data sheet'!$D$2:$D$40,$A14)

This cannot be used in Excel 2003 and previous. For earlier versions you might try this SUMPRODUCT(…),

=SUMPRODUCT(('data sheet'!$A$2:$A$40=G$4)*('data sheet'!$D$2:$D$40=$A14))

Edit: I've only just now noticed that the ranges were not the same (e.g. A2:A40 and D2:D20) in your sample formula. They must be the same size although direct row-to-row comparison is not necessary. I've corrected the two formulas above.

jacobsladder---01 say:

I would check that the SIM reader pins are all at the same level, sometimes one pin can get pushed down into the connector. If you are careful you should be able to lift it back up with something fine and pointed.

 

Check that the holder that you slide the SIM into is holding the SIM tight into the phone, if not a layer of tape on the back of the SIM might help.

 

Finally, check that the phone isn't stuck in Offline mode - Power up the phone and then try to change the mode from offline to General. If Phone reports "Insert SIM to access network" then check the two points I mentioned above.
