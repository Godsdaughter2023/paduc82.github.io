"Office 365" means a subscription version of Office. By paying monthly or annually you will always have an updated version of locally installed Office programs. The opposite is a one-time-purchase installation.

"Click-to-run" means a virtual installation (as opposed to traditional MSI installations). The most recent Office version (2016) uses that technology no matter if you have a subscription or a one-time-purchase version.

So they're not the same (other then how one purchases/subscribes) or they are the same?

I'd say these are different categories. The first category is "how to buy Office" (subscription or one-time-purchase) and second other is "how to install Office" (click-to-run or MSI).

However, as I wrote, most Office 2016 installations make use of click-to-run, which means that you update it via File | Options | Account | Update Options, that is, not via Windows Update.
