steve rindsberg say:

We used to have similar problems with a lot of apps when I was in the 35mm slide business. Yep.  I'm that old. ;-)

With the right printer driver settings, PowerPoint was one of the most cooperative programs we routinely printed from. 

If you checkmark Scale To Fit, PowerPoint prints the slide as large as it can without a) distorting the proportions or b) exceeding the imageable area reported back to it by the printer driver.  The imageable area is the largest area that the printer can apply ink/toner/whatever to on the currently selected paper size. 

If you remove this checkmark, PowerPoint prints the slide (size unadjusted) to the currently selected paper size.  If the slide size is larger than the selected paper, so be it.  The falls off the edge of the paper.

All this is subject to the printer's imageable area.  Most printers are unable to print clear to the edge of the paper, so imageable area is 1/4" or so less all around than the paper size.

It sounds as though you specifically want to AVOID Scale To Fit. Leave it off and make sure your paper size is equal to or better, a bit greater than the slide size.  I'm guessing that your driver/printer allows you to choose any arbitrary custom size you like?
