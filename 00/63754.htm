cmdrkeene say:

When you link/connect an online account, that becomes your password.  The local password doesn't exist at all anymore.  If you unlink/disconnect your online account, you'll be prompted to create a new local-only password.

If you want to never login to the computer, you have a couple choices:

Option 1:  Use a blank/empty password

You could unlink the online account so that you only have a local account.  Since it doesn't need to connect to anything online, you can then leave the password blank/empty (just like Windows 95 to Windows 7).  Of course your web browser/email would still connect, you just wouldn't be able to use any new features in Windows 8/10 like syncing and backing up your settings/profile or using the app store.

If you still want those features but also want to avoid typing your password, you still have other options.

Option Two:  Use an easier login method

You could switch to a PIN entry instead so that unlocking your computer is like unlocking your phone.  And since the PIN is never stored anywhere but the device, it's more secure (it's like two-factor login, that PIN only works if someone also has your device physically, not just finding your PIN/password online).

Option Three:  Only prompt for password/PIN after a full restart.

This is also similar to phones.  You can tell your computer to stay unlocked during normal use.  As you close and open the laptop or put your computer on and sleep, it will never ask for a password.  But after a full system reboot, it will still ask.

Option Four:  Keep the password, but make Windows skip the login screen anyway

This has been around for 30 years too, but it's a well-kept secret.  Just take a look at Automatically Login to Windows [Video] to see how to set it.

You can pick-and-choose your favorite options above and use them all at once.  Which ones sound most like what you want?  Or is there anything you don't see that you wish you could?
