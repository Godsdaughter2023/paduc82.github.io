developer42 say:

ReadyBoost and USB RAM devices?

Is there a USB RAM device compatible with ReadyBoost that would plug into USB and allow normal volatile/dynamic memory modules to be used rather than flash devices? Seems like this would be the next logical step to dramatically boosting the throughput. Sort of a USBRAMDrive. This could be very useful for corporate IT support bumping up against maximum RAM limitation and looking for ways to boost memory without replacing large numbers of systems. A USB RAM disk would be a quick pluggable solution and maybe even a way to recycle older memory into a useful application.

 

cody c. say: Developer42,

Currently, there is no such device. It's important to understand that the limitation is not as much due to the USB device, it's actually the USB speed. USB 2.0 can only support speeds up to 480 Mbps (60 MBps) in a perfect  environment. Compare that to your average stick of DDR2-800 which runs at 51200 Mbps (6400 MBps), and you can begin to see why USB 2.0 might be the problem.

Hope that answers your question!
