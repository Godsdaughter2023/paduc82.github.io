hikingstick say:

This may be a really late reply, but I'm providing it because I found it while searching for a solution to the same issue.

 

I replaced the battery on my laptop, and was surprised to find the red X as soon as I got it charged and powered up again.

 

I followed the tips here (e.g., powercfg), but still could not figure out what was going on.

 

I found the answer under Power Options > Advanced Settings. Expand the Battery area and check each of the settings. On mine, the Critical Battery Level and Low Battery Level were both set at around 98%.  Once I set them to reasonable levels (e.g., 25% low battery warning and 11% critical level), the red X went away.

 

My hypothesis: Windows got used to the old battery (one that was due for replacement for more than a year). I believe it set those thresholds high because of the limited life on the old battery (Windows 7 did/does do a better job than its predacessors at managing the meter on batteries as they age).  So, when I put the new battery in, the OS still managed it as if it were a past-life piece of trash.  That might explain why everything looked fine when you booted under a different OS--it did not have the same set of features (and battery history).

and delete the wallpaper key, then reboot.
