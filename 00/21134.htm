cjlim say:

As far as I know, all Nokia phones use Texas Instruments gps chips. They don't publish detailed specifications so it is difficult to know which models/versions are used in each phone.

 

You should however note that the limitations are more to do with power usage and antenna size. Unlike dedicated gps units which are designed to work plugged into a cig lighter and are usually bigger with more space or a bigger battery, your phone has to work for a reasonable length of time without an external power source when in walk mode, while still be small and light enough. This limitation also affects antenna size and location. While the dedicated gps only has to worry about one antenna, there are multiple antennas in the phone for gsm, gps, wifi, bluetooth, all needing space and vying for prime location. I often wonder how they actually manage to fit it all in such  small package. You could call it a compromise solution, a jack of all trades master of none. 

 

I always use my phone as my sat nav when driving abroad, but I use it with an external bluetooth gps receiver.

Highlighting an entire row with conditional formatting - Microsoft Community

symoore say:

Highlighting an entire row with conditional formatting

I need to add conditional formatting to entire rows in my workbook.  The formatting will be based on whether or not two cells in the columns are equal to each other. So if K1 and L1 are equal, row 1 will be highlighted in red.   Sounds easy enough only problem is, I don't know how to do this, HELP!
hansv mvp say:

Select the (entire) rows you want to format. I'll assume that the active cell within the selection is in row 1.

On the Home tab of the ribbon, click Conditional Formatting > New Rule...

Select "Use a formula to determine which cells to format".

Enter the formula =($K1=$L1)

Please note the use of $ characters to make the reference to the columns K and L absolute, while the row number 1 remains relative.

Click Format...

Activate the Fill tab and select red.

Click OK twice.
