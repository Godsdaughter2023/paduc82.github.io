suzanne s. barnhill say:

Suppressing Spacing Before at the top of a page has always been the default after a "soft" page break, and there has never been an option to prevent it. In older versions, it was also the default to honor Spacing Before after a "hard" (manual) page or column break. The Compatibility Options in older versions included options to "Suppress extra line spacing at top of page" (this applied to Multiple line spacing) and "Suppress Space Before after a hard page or column break." These options are no longer available unless you set compatibility to Word 2010 or earlier.

There are two ways to preserve Spacing Before at the top of a page (at the beginning of a chapter, for example). One is to insert a section break, as Spacing Before is preserved after a section break. If this is a chapter opening, then a section break is not a bad idea for other reasons as well (for example, to use the Different First Page header/footer setting to avoid a running head on the first page of a chapter), but if you want to avoid a section break, you can put the paragraph into a single-cell, borderless table, where the Spacing Before will be honored.

An even slicker trick, which can be incorporated into the style, is to add a borderless frame to the paragraph, as the frame will also preserve the Spacing Before. Recent versions of Word make frames less accessible than earlier ones, but you can find the command in the Legacy Forms tools on the Developer tab, or you can add Insert Horizontal Frame to the Quick Access Toolbar. The article at http://wordfaqs.ssbarnhill.com/MarginalText.htm describes how to create a framed style; in this case it would not be in the margin, however.

a. user say:

I am currently using Microsoft Outlook for Mac, version 15.40 (171108).

I've had this happen a few times, I have had luck deleting the cache of the affected Inbox. I know this may be required when an error pops up with code -17890 on the Sync Errors page. All but once has rebuilding the cache fixed the issue, the one time it didn't work I used OWA to download the attachment but it turned out to be corrupt.

This isn't very efficient because rebuilding the cache requires a full download of your Exchange account, if you could delete the cached version of the affected e-mail it would be more efficient.

Follow the steps below to clear the cache of the affected folder:

  1. Connect to your fastest network connection, you may be downloading gigabytes of data.
  2. Open Outlook.
  3. Right click (or Control click) the Inbox of the account having the problem.
  4. From the pop up, select 'Properties...' near the bottom.
  5. On the 'General' tab you will find a button named 'Empty Cache', click it.
  6. Close the window if required.
  7. Your local copy of your Inbox will be deleted, a fresh download of all e-mails will be performed.
  8. From the 'Window' menu, select 'Sync Status' to monitor the process.
  9. Monitor the 'Sync Status' window, you will note several download threads receiving downloads.

Depending on how much mail is in your Exchange profile and your internet speed it can take 10 minutes or more to recover your Inbox contents.
