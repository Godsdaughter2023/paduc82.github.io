    Hello,

    If your PC is running low on storage, you may want to delete files in Windows 10 using storage sense, or uninstall apps you don't use or need anymore. If storage sense is turned on in Storage settings, Windows can automatically free up some space by getting rid of files that you don't need, like temporary files and stuff in your Recycle bin. Here's how to delete files with storage sense:

    1. Open the Start menu, and select Settings > System > Storage.
    2. You may do one of the following from there:
      • Turn on Storage sense to have Windows delete unnecessary files.
      • Delete unnecessary files manually by selecting Change how we free up space > Clean now.
      • You may specify which files you want storage sense to delete automatically by selecting Change how we free up space, and selecting or deselecting the check boxes for options under Temporary Files and Free up space now.

    However, if you are still low on space after deleting or cleaning up files that you don't need, you may uninstall some apps that are seldom used or move files to another drive.
