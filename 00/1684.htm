I apologize, but the way your question is worded is a little confusing. Since you mentioned Ghost, I am going to assume you are talking about Norton's Ghost which clones entire hard drives or single partitions.

I'm going to assume that you have a partition that is not accessible (visible) in File Explorer.

Partitions cannot hide in this sense, they are simply marked as inactive, not compatible with Windows (like an Apple File System partition), or do not have a letter assigned to them.

To show a partition that is active but does not have an assigned letter (which Ghost can't access since the partition does not have a technical access point), do the following:

Do not do this to your main windows drive or other drives that you have installed apps to (Usually C or D).

  1. Open "Disk Management"
  2. Right-click on the partition
  3. Click "Change Drive Letters and Paths..."
  4. Click Change
  5. Pick a new letter from the drop-down
  6. Press Ok
  7. Press "Yes"
  8. Your drive will be assigned the letter and will be visible in Windows Explorer.

You should be able to clone it with Ghost after that.

Introducing Editor Overview Pane in Insiders Fast

Hello Insiders,

My name is Ken, and I'm a Program Manager on the Natural Language Experiences team.

Thank you for using Editor, our digital writing assistant in Office 365 subscription versions of Word and Outlook on Windows.

Word and Outlook on Windows.

Today, we’re excited to announce our latest addition - the new Editor Overview Pane - in Word for Windows.

What's New? 

When you’re brushing up your document, tackling everything at once can be overwhelming.

What’s more, fixing everything is not always a productive approach. It’s helpful to see the big picture and choose what’s most important.

The Editor Overview Pane can help you quickly find and fix the proofing issues you care about, by:

1. Showing a categorized Overview of writing enhancement opportunities:

  • Corrections are issues you’re strongly advised to fix.
  • Refinements give optional advice on stylistic or situational matters.
  • Counters and Checkmarks track progress & celebrate achievement.

2. Drawing attention to elements of Good Writing:

  • Gives writers a dashboard for self-assessment.
  • Gives teachers a scorecard to drive learning goal conversations.
  • Presents the breadth and depth of checks in a manageable way.

3. Making it easy to fix just those categories that matter to your writing goals:

  • Scrub the whole document linearly – or a Category at a time.
  • Fix in a recommended sequence: Spelling > Grammar > Refinements.
  • Spend time on Categories you want to focus on.

 

Availability

To use the Editor Overview Pane, you must:

  • Be an Office 365 subscriber on the Insider Fast level.
  • Have Microsoft Word for Windows version 1802 (Build 9020.2003) or later installed.

Editor Overview Pane will roll out gradually to Insiders over the next few weeks. So please check again in 1-3 weeks if you don't see it right away. 

The Editor Overview pane is available in languages for which spelling check is supported.

What's more, Editor-specific Gold-flagged Refinement checks are now available in Spanish, in addition to English. (More languages are forthcoming!)


Getting Started

You can call up the Editor Overview Pane by:

  1. Clicking the "Check Document" button in Review tab of the Ribbon.
  2. Pressing the F7 key.
  3. Clicking the Proofing icon in Status Bar.
  4. Clicking the Back-Arrow in the Editor Drill-in Pane.

Scenarios to Try

  1. Click on one Category at a time, to fix just those issues in the document. If you're in a hurry, you can click on "Spelling", to focus on fixing those.
  2. Click on "Corrections" to walk through the issues Editor strongly recommends that you fix… namely Spelling and Grammar errors.
  3. When you're looking for extra refinement, click on categories in the "Refinements" card to see Style and Advanced writing checks that may be more subjective or situational.
  4. Of course, you can still check documents the traditional way - Click on the "Review All Results" button to work through issues in the order they appear in the document.

Known Issues

The Editor Overview will not appear if you have proofing tools installed for any of the following languages. (Support is coming soon)

  • Simplified Chinese, Traditional Chinese, Japanese, Korean

Feedback

We're eager to hear your feedback!  You can leave comments on this post, or on Twitter via the @OfficeInsider handle. You can also submit feedback in Word by clicking File > Feedback.

To print a list of your favorites in Internet Explorer11, export your
favorites to an .htm file, and then print the .htm file:

Here are the steps to export your favorites in the Internet Explorer 11
on Windows 8.1 PC:

1. On the Start screen, click on the ‘Internet Explorer’ tile to open it.
2. Click on the ‘Wrench’ icon at the bottom menu, and then click ‘View on the desktop’
on the menu.
3. Click on the ‘Favorites’ icon on the top right corner of the window.
4. Open the drop down list, and then click Import and Export’.
5. On the ‘Import/Export Settings’ window, select 'Export to a file' radio button, and
then click 'Next'.
6. Select the checkbox next to 'Favorites' and click 'Next'.
7. Select the folder that you want to export your favorites from, and then click 'Next'.
8. Click on the Browse’ button and browse the location where you want to export your favorites.
9. Type the file name, and then click on ‘Save’.
10. Click on 'Export', and then click 'Finish' to successfully export your favorites.

 
To print your list of favorites, open .htm file in Internet Explorer and
then click the Print button.

a. user say:

Hello GarySmit,

Thanks for visiting the Answers forum.

I wanted to add some clarification to your post. Contacts that you can see on your Phone are not necessarily saved on the Phone's storage. They are most commonly 'synced' with the Primary Account on the device.

To demonstrate this, go to your Phone, then note the Primary Account under Settings, Email + Accounts, and then sign into http://contacts.live.com with that same account on your PC.

You should see your contacts there. From that website you can export your contacts as a .csv file and then sign into a new account, and import the file you just exported, thus moving your contacts from one account to another.

Here's a support article that explains how to sync Outlook contacts to a Windows Phone, as well.
http://support.microsoft.com/kb/2844991

Hope this helps.
