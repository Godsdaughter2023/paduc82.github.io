Well, you could do this with MVFs though I can't tell you offhand just how to get at the MVF values; the program doesn't expose them very easily.

 

What I'd recommend is a good old-fashioned many to many relationship: each user can choose zero, one, two or more items, each item may be chosen by zero, one or many users. Hi,

You must have A (at least one) task with a task calendar in your work plan. You can find it when you insert the column Task Calendar - it will by default say None but if you have assigned a Task Calendar to one of the tasks you will see it there.

Now at least one of the tasks with a task calendar will also have one or more resources attached.

With the pool closed, these resources will have their calendar taken from the work plan itself, and visibly this calendar has no exclusion with the task calendar (if it would you would have the message already there and then). ut when you open the pool, the resource will  now inherit its calendar from the pool- and that one does have this sort of exclusion with the task calendar.
