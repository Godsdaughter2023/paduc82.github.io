I don't believe it's the Unimode half-duplex driver (although I could be wrong).  My XP3 box has a "Unimodem full-duplex audio driver" showing in Device Manager under "Sound, video and game controllers," and I've never had the modem sounds come through the main speakers.  [The difference between "half-duplex" and "full-duplex" is whether you have to wait until one party is speaking before the other starts or if both parties can speak at the same time.]

Rather, apparently some -- but far from all -- modem drivers have a checkbox someplace in the modem properties dialog (accessed via Device Manager or Control Panel > Phone and Modem Options) that directs the sound to the system's external speakers. 

While you are in Control Panel > Phone and Modem Options, click the "Advanced" tab and check that the Unimodem 5 Service Provider is installed.  It should have been installed by default, but if not, you might want to add it (using the "Add" button).

I suspect that your previous modem driver -- which limited your connect speed -- had the external speaker checkbox but that the new driver -- which has higher connect speed -- does not.

It's probably not very satisfactory, but you can increase the volume of the modem's tiny speaker (a bit) by using the "Extra initialization commands" box on the "Advanced" tab of the modem Properties dialog.  There are two relevant commands, the M command (which determines when the speaker is on) and the L command (which sets the speaker's volume:

M0     Speaker always off [that's a zero]
M1     The speaker is active when the modem is off-hook and carrier is not detected. [typical default]
M2     The speaker is always active.
M3     The speaker is active after dialing ends and turns off when carrier negotiation is completed.

L0     Low volume
L1     Low volume
L2     Medium volume
L3     High volume

In the "Extra initialization commands" box, commands are entered preceded with "AT" (without quotes) and there are no spaces.  Thus, for example, if you want to hear the most sounds at the highest volume (which unfortunately is usually not very high), you would set L3 and M3 by entering the following in the box:

ATL3M3

With respect to the Dell Service Tag:  on the page linked in my earlier post, there's a link to "Enter a service tag."  If you click that link there's a box to enter your Dell service tag (and yet another link to tell you how to find it if you don't know where it is).  Dell uses the "service tag" to identify your particular computer's hardware configuration, because the same model Dell (e.g., Dimension 2400) can come with a variety of different hardware options.  Using the service tag is supposed to customize the material presented to you so that it is all applicable to your computer.
