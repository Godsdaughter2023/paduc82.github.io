You bet.  There's always a way past the problem.  Really there's several ways to go about it, and it somewhat depends on how deep and technical you want to get (like these instructions), or if you want to use a more general "easy way out" option like doing a factory reset so the computer is basically as good as new.  That's super fast (roughly 20 minutes) but it's probably a little overboard (but certainly less personal effort to fix it).

Truth told, I would recommend this balanced option:  create a new profile for yourself, drag your files to it, and delete the broken one.  Here's how.  These steps are a bit long, but just take it one at a time and let me know if you get stuck at any point.

  1. Open Settings, go to Accounts, then the Family & Other People section.
  2. In the Other People section near the bottom, click Add Someone Else to This PC.
  3. We're not going to create any kind of online account, so ignore the prompt for an email address and click the "I don't have this info" link at the bottom.  Depending your version, this option might also be named "sign in without a Microsoft Account" instead (either way, the point is to not type in any email address in this process).
  4. On the next page, again ignore any option to put in an email address and click the "Add a user without a Microsoft Account" link at the bottom.
  5.   7. Press Ctrl-Alt-Delete and choose Sign Out.
  8. Login to the account you just created (it should be listed at the bottom-left corner).  This should log you in to a working profile account now.
  9. I wouldn't do it immediately just in case you find something you need from it, but later you can go to Settings > Accounts and delete the old/broken account.
