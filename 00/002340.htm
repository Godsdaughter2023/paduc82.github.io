I got this message too. Let me explain how I got it and how I got rid of it. Maybe it helps you.

I had a Nokia N70 with original 2.x firmware and I got PC Suite 6.86.9.0 installed. Everything was working fine. But I knew the firmware was old and I wanted to upgrade for security and stability reasons.

I made a backup of my phone with PC Suite. I downloaded the Nokia Software Updater which installed version 5.x for me. (I believe this is already old too, probalbly because it is Vodafone branded firmware). It scared the hell out of me, because halfway my phone switched off and the updater told me the connection was lost and wanted me to retry. I turned on my phone. All settings were gone and the original Vodafone theme was loaded again. I checked the firmware (type: *#0000#) and it was the new 5.x firmware. I hit the retry button on the Software Updater and it seemed to continue. Phew!

After that I restored the entiry backup. When I reconnected my phone I got the message about PC Suite software not being installed on my phone. I tried many times uninstalling the PC Suite, SMS Sync and MMS Sync from my phone and then reinstall using the PC Suite on my computer. It didn't work. I tried uninstalling PC Suite from my computer and installing an older version. That didn't work either.

I did a hard reset on my phone (swith off, then hold '*', '3' and 'green phone button' at the same time while swithing on). Maybe going back to 'factory defaults' would have worked too. I connected the phone to PC Suite and installed the PC Suite software on my phone. I still got the message about the software not being installed! But after I switched my phone off and on again I could connect without getting the message. Finally things worked normally again! I then restored my backup again, but now I didn't restore the 'settings'. I reconfigured the rest of my phone and all is well now.

Conclusion: Although it seems to work that the settings from an old firmware can be restored with a new firmware, it doesn't work after all. The PC Suite software seems to be installed on the phone, but it simply doesn't work! When you upgrade your phone, just reconfigure your phone manually and don't restore settings from an old version, because it might not work as it seems.

Text field in YYYY format converted to date

I have table that stores the "YearBuilt" for a component in a text field as "YYYY" format.  I would like to be able to convert this text field to a date field so that I can calculate the age of the component from the most recent test date which is in "DD/MM/YYYY" format. I would be happy to just subtract the years since I do not know if the component was built on 01/01/YYYY or 12/31/YYYY.
scottgem say:
If you want to convert you could add a new date field to the table then run an update query:

UPDATE table SET BuiltDate = DateSerial(YearBuilt,1,1);

This will set the new field to Jan 1 for the recorded year (you could use 7,1 if you want mid year).

Then you can delete the old field once that's done. In the future you could then enter exact dates.
