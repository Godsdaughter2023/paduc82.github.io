Hi

You did not mention what type of user accounts the other users have. If they are using Administrator accounts, anything you enable, they can disable.

This will be some work, but here is one idea if you just want to get rid of all the 'clutter'.

There are basically 2 separate locations where all of the Start/All Programs menu shortcuts are stored. One location is where everything appears on All User accounts and the other location is where everything appears on each individual users account.

For the  Desktop, here are the locations.

All Users: C:\Users\Public\Desktop

Individual Users: C:\Users\user name\Desktop

For the  Start/All Programs  menu, here are the locations.

All Users: C:\ProgramData\Microsoft\Windows\Start Menu\Programs

Individual Users: C:\Users\Ron\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Windows\Start Menu\Programs

You can move things around so that you only have your own programs appearing on your user accounts Start Menu and Desktop, and not the others. You can also move all of the other users game shortcuts to their own invidual account locations.

In the Home editions of Windows 7, like you mentioned, the only way to restrict the use of programs is the Parental Controls or by editing the Registry. Most of the Group Policy Editor items are implemented through direct registry edits.
