    vgreen57 say:

    This is due to Skype only supporting the Bluetooth  Headset Profile (low quality audio) and not the Hands-Free Profile (higher quality audio).

    Here's how it was explained to my by a Bose technician:

    "Bluetooth speakers support Hands-Free Profile (HFP) but not Headset Profile (HSP) for Bluetooth profiles. Skype and WhatsApp use the HSP profile specifically. For this main reason, we're unable to use our speakers with these apps."

    Bose has been in the audio business forever, and they would know.

    So, until Skype supports HFP, we're stuck with HSP and its lousy quality.  Of course various headsets work better than others with HSP, so some users may not recognize the difference.

    cbarnhorst say:

    A usb connector is a usb connector regardless of the type of drive installed in the enclosure.  You need a SATA enclosure connected to a SATA port on the computer.  Your comptuer does not scan the usb bus for a bootable device. 

    It may not matter anyway. If you purchased an Upgrade Windows 7 then you cannot reuse the Vista.  The upgrade license supercedes the original license and you have to retire it.  However, if you purchased a full license copy of Windows 7 then you can continue to use the Vista.  How you installed Windows 7 is irrelevant.  The type of license you purchased does.

    cbarnhorst say:

    You cannot boot Vista from a usb drive.  You can from an external eSATA drive connected to a SATA port, if you have one. 

    If you don't have a SATA port or don't want to buy an external SATA drive enclosure, you can virtualize the Vista system and the virtual hard drive can reside on any drive.  You can use VMWare to do the virtualization.  See their website for details and software.
