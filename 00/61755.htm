How to enable BitLocker on the SurfacePro (Windows 8 Pro) such that it prompts for a PIN at every power-on (cannot boot PC w/o keyboard !)

06/25/2013:  By Gleaning info from web research and trial and error I developed these steps that allow you to enable BitLocker on the SurfacePro, such that the Surface prompts for a BitLocker PIN at every power-on.

1.       Before turning on BitLocker for the system drive, to force BitLocker to prompt to “choose how to unlock your drive at startup” you must:

a.       In gpedit.msc, go to Local Computer Policy/Computer Configuration/Administrative Templates/Windows Components/BitLocker Drive Encryption/Operating System Drives.

b.      Set “Require additional Authentication at startup” to “Enabled”.  Unchecked the only checkbox (Allow BitLocker without a compatible TPM…).  Let four drop downs default to “Allow...”.

c.      Now when turning on BitLocker, you are prompted to “Choose how to unlock your drive at startup with “Insert a USB flash drive” or “Let BitLocker automatically unlock my drive”.

d.      If you want to be able to enter a PIN, in the gpedit path above, you must enable “Enable use of BitLocker authentication requiring preboot keyboard input on slates”.  Note that this will REQUIRE a physical keyboard to be able to boot the SurfacePro (either the Surface Type/Touch keyboard or even an external USB keyboard will work).  Now when turning on BitLocker (in the steps below), you will be prompted to select “Enter a PIN” in addition to the two other options mentioned in the previous sub-step. 

 

2.       Now, in Control Panel/BitLocker Drive Encription, click on “Turn on BitLocker”.

 

3.       On the “Choose how to unlock your drive at startup”, I selected “Enter a PIN”.

 

4.       Enter your PIN.  Click “Set PIN”.  (PIN is in PasswordSafe).

 

5.     On the “How do you want to backup your recovery Key?” select “Save to your Microsoft account” and “Save to a file” (saved to “256MB Stick”).

 

6.       To verify the key saved on the “Microsoft account”, go to http://skydrive.com/RecoveryKey.  Login to see the key.  This matches the key saved to a file on “256MB Stick”.

7.       Select “Encrypt entire drive…”.

8.       Allow a “Run BitLocker system check” to run.

9.       Prompted to “Reboot Now”.  Do so.

10.   Upon Reboot, you are prompted to “Enter the password to unlock this drive”.

11.   Encryption of the drive started.
