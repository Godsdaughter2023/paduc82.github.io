The problem is that your old certificate is still registered in your phone. So to resolve this go to

Tools>

Settings>

general>

Security>

certificate management>

Trusted site certificates>

You'll see the old exchange certificate in there. Delete it using the left soft key and then exit.

Connect to your exchange email box once more and you'll get the untrusted certificate error once more. Apply it permanently and voila...no more problem as it will install the new certificate onto your phone (you can obviously check this by going back into connection manager)!

julie fka profjulie say:

Windows Phone 7 is not compatible with My Phone (which serves as a backup for WM6.x devices only).  There is no way to export data from My Phone in any kind of format, so there's no way to get the data on your computer or to move the data to your windows phone. And there's no way to backup text messages that are received to or generated from a windows phone 7 device (at this time).

If you've still got your WM6.x phone:

You can use the Hotmail/ActiveSync Access service to sync the Contact and Calendar data that is on your WM6.x phone to your Windows Live account. Then you can sync the data on Windows Live to your new phone.

1. Click on “ActiveSync” under Start menu or under Programs.
2. Click on the link above to configure the ActiveSync profile for Hotmail access. You may need to delete the existing profile if it is already in use.
3. Disable automatic detection of Exchange server settings.
4. Enter your full Hotmail address in User name field. Enter password. Leave Domain blank.
5. Enter m.hotmail.com in the Server address field.
6. Select the data to sync. Hotmail supports all 4 data types via ActiveSync.
7. The device will connect to Hotmail ActiveSync and perform an initial sync for the selected data.
8. Successful synchronization will result in device showing a screen like this.

Before you do this, download and install PIMBackup to your WM6.x phone (just be on the safe side)
Backup the your Contacts, Tasks and Appointments to your SD card. You may need to delete your My Phone partnership before you do this.

You can also move all your text messages back to the WM6.x phone and then use something likeJeyo Mobile Companion to sync the text messages from your WM6.x phone to your computer.  You still won't be able to move the text messages to your windows phone, but once they are on your computer Jeyo Mobile Companion includes a way to import them to a CSV file - Then you could probably move them to OneNote or Excel and sync that file to your windows phone 7.
