Using the round function:

=round(Arlene!B3-INDEX(Arlene!B3:B370,COUNTA(Arlene!B3:B370)),1)

hi,

Two ways to do this:

Method 1) Right-click on the QAT at the top of PowerPoint > Customize the QAT.  Choose from: Commands not in the ribbon...   select "Combine Shapes"   click Add>>    OK.

 

Draw a rectangle - this will be your background/frame.  Give it a fill like black.  On top of it, draw the heart shape.  Select the rectangle, then holding down Ctrl, select the heart (you must do it in that order!). 

 

Click on the Combine Shapes menu on your QAT.  Choose Subtract.  Place a picture behind.

 

Method 2) Draw a heart.  Copy your picture.  Right-click on the heart > Format Picture > Fill > select: Picture or Texture Fill > click "Clipboard" > Close.  The picture is then "inside" the shape.
