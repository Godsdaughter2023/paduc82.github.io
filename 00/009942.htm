Merging contacts of multiple Skype accounts - Microsoft Community

ted5705 say:

Merging contacts of multiple Skype accounts

Can I merge contacts from a Skype account I created by 'Facebook' sign-in to an account I had to create through Skype (I plan on using the later account)?

a. user say:

Hi,

Welcome to the Skype Community Forum!

If you’ve created a new account for Skype and want to transfer your contacts from your old account to your new one, you can do this in Skype for Windows, Mac and Linux. Merging of contacts is not possible.

There are a couple of ways to transfer your contacts. You can back up your contacts to a file, or you can send your contacts from one account to the other.

To back up your contacts to file (Skype for Windows Desktop):

  1. Sign in to Skype with your old account details.

  2. Click Contacts, then click Advanced > Backup Contacts to File.

  3. Navigate to where you want to save the file on your computer, then click Save.

  4. Click Skype > Sign out to sign out of your old account.

  5. Sign in to Skype with your new account details.

  6. Click Contacts, then click Advanced > Restore Contacts from File.

  7. Find the file that you saved on your computer, then click it and click Open. Your contacts will appear in your new account.

 

To send your contacts from one account to the other:

  1. Make sure that both accounts appear in each other’s contacts lists, then sign in to Skype with your old account details.

  2. Send the contacts you want to transfer to the new account. Get help with sending contacts.

  3. Click Skype > Sign out to sign out of your old account.

  4. Sign in to Skype with your new account details and click Recent. You'll see the contact requests in your recent list.

  5. Send a contact request to each of the contacts.

I hope this helps!

Thoughts on Office 365 vs Google Apps... - Microsoft Community

dmlerner say:

Thoughts on Office 365 vs Google Apps...

I have an Office 365 subscription. It's inexpensive - like $70/year, since I can get by with a personal subscription.

It sounds very attractive because the subscription includes the latest Office apps (Word, Excel, PowerPoint) for my Mac, plus an hour of free world-wide calling on Skype, plus 1 TB of OneDrive space.

But in practice, except for a few personal spreedsheets, I find I prefer Google Apps instead.

What's good about Office 365 vs Google Apps:

1. The native Office apps have some nice features which aren't implemented in the browser-based Google Apps on my Mac. For example, even something as simple as dragging over a selection of cells to select them all doesn't work in Google Sheets. You have to manually enter the cell range for something like summing up numbers. That sort of thing is easier in Excel in the native app. Also, with Google Sheets there is no easy way to get back to the end of a long spreadsheet other than scroll down. It doesn't remember where you were last. In Excel I don't have that problem.

2. And, of course, with Office you always have the actual files on your computer. Unless you download files from Google apps your files are just in the cloud. That sort of concerns me. I don't mind syncing, but I'd also like to have the actual files themselves locally on my Mac.

Problems with Office 365 include syncing for one thing. I was on a customer call with a client last night and we were both editing the same spreadsheet in Google Sheets. It's like magic - you can see where the other person is editing and things are updated in real-time. You simply cannot do that with Office 365. It's not supported on the Mac.

In fact, I can't edit a spreadsheet in Excel on my Mac, that is stored on OneDrive, and then go to my iPad and open it up and edit it. It's locked until I close the spreadsheet on my Mac! Very inconvenient!

With Word it's almost as bad. You can open up the same file on your Mac and iPad and edit, but in order to see the changes you have to close the file and open it up again.

Meanwhile with Google Docs you see the same magical updating in real-time across all the devices.

I've also been disappointed with my experiment with OneNote. I have 1,000+ notes in 23 notebooks with multiple sections and pages. Searching via multiple devices just doesn't work well. Finally I gave up and stuck with Evernote for that. Searching through all the notes in Evernote - even when the notes are in the cloud - while quirky and not perfect - works much better than trying to do the same thing with OneNote.

So in the end, I find no real use for the OneDrive space. And there are so many limitations there as well - such as file size limits, and problems with folders with Japanese names.

I keep wondering what the benefits are of keeping my Office365 subscription. I probably will keep it just for the convenience of having the native apps.

But Office365 cloud features are nothing to write home about.

Hi INTERNATIONAL TRADE LINKS,

 

Recently we are aware of an issue that Outlook search stops working after updating to build 7870.2020 and higher. From your description, I’m not clear if you are using Outlook desktop or Outlook Web App. If you are using Outlook desktop, navigate to File > Office Account to check your version. If its build is 7870.2020 or higher version, the temporary workaround is to revert to the previous build, (16.0.7571.2109). For more details, please refer to this article: Outlook search stops working after updating to build 7870.2020 and higher.

 

If the problem persists after you revert the Outlook client to the previous version, try rebuilding your Outlook client’s index. Here are the steps:

1. Open your Outlook client. Select File > Options > Search > Indexing Options.

2. Click “Modify” to check if “Microsoft Outlook” is marked. If it is marked, you should also try disabling and then re-enabling Outlook. To do so, follow these steps:

a. Deselect Outlook from Indexing.

b. Close the Outlook client.

c. Wait 5 minutes, check in the task manager that Outlook.exe is not in the processes tab.

d. Now open the Outlook client again, go back and select Outlook for indexing

3. Click “Advanced” on the “Indexing Options” page. Select “Rebuild” button on the following page.

Note: When the index is being rebuilt, your search will not work. You should wait until the index is done rebuilding. The time it will take for the index to be rebuilt depends on the system you are using, the amount of files that need to be indexed, and file size.

 

If you don’t use the Outlook desktop application or the problem isn’t solved, I’d like to collect some further information for our further troubleshooting. Please give us a screenshot of the “No Result” interface. Regarding “I cant find any mails in outlook mail box from 2 3 days”, can you find the emails which received before 3 days? And about the emails you want to search, are they the ones you received in two or three days, or other old email messages? Also, please let us know the detailed version of Outlook you are using.

 

Thanks,
