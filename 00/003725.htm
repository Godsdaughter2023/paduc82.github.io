I have purchased notebooks from other manufacturers who provided a "printed" version of the manual that was only 25 pages.

Obviously the manufacturer makes the decision regarding how much documentation is necessary - taking into account the profit margins, and the resources necessary to produce/publish the documentation. Keeping in mind the users who will ultimately be the ones who marketing intends to sell the product. The Zune Learning Center really does have all the information necessary to use Zune Devices and Software.  It may not specifically outline each and every specification and technical piece of equiptment, which isn't good or bad, just different. 

If the manual is what makes or breaks a customer then some customers will be made or broken. Manufacturers like Microsoft know this, and obviously they made the decision that their market would probably not include users like myself who would want a detailed document. But rather users who already understood the general usage capabilities of PMP (personal media players).

I love my Zune just the same - documentation though lacking doesn't make or break me as a customer - To each their own... :-)
