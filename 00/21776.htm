jay freedman say:
To settle one question: Uninstalling and reinstalling Word or any/all of Office is usually a waste of time, because the uninstaller does not remove the things that cause most problems: a damaged Normal.dotm template, a mangled or missing registry entry, or most commonly a third-party add-in containing bad programming. So stop worrying about reinstalling the upgrade Office 2007 until all other possibilities have been excluded.

The first troubleshooting step is to run Word in safe mode by holding down the Ctrl key while double-clicking the Word program's icon or Start menu entry. You should see a message before Word opens, saying "Word has detected that you are holding down the CTRL key. Do you want to start Word in safe mode?" Click the Yes button.

Safe mode starts the program without loading the Normal.dotm template, any of the customization entries from the registry, or any add-ins -- it's as plain-vanilla as possible. Open a document and work with it in the same way that caused the errors or problems before. Do they still occur, or have they disappeared?

If safe mode causes the problems to stop, then there are more investigation steps to find out which of the nasty three is the cause. Use the sections in http://support.microsoft.com/default.aspx/kb/921541?p=1 in this order:
  1. Rename Normal.dotm and restart Word (not in safe mode). If the problems still occur...
  2. Disable the Startup folder add-ins, except do step 7 and steps 3 to 6 first, then steps 2 to 6. (The add-ins are far more likely to be in the AppData area than in the Program Files area.) If the problems still occur...
  3. Delete the Word Data key. For backing up the existing key, you only need to export that specific key (the one listed for Word 2007 in step 3 of that part of the article). When you restart Word, it will build a new key there. If the problems stop, you can safely delete the backup .reg file; otherwise follow the instructions after step 8 to restore it.


Those are the procedures most likely to stop the problem. If it continues, then disabling the COM add-ins would be the next procedure, but that's more complex. Let us know how it goes.
