scott .. b say:

Updating your computer's BIOS was a very important change and should not have caused any of the difficulties you are experiencing, in fact, it should improve your computer's performance..

 

 BIOS stands for Basic Input/output System.  It is the program on your computer that makes sure all of the other components--from your Windows operating system to your central processing unit (CPU) to your RAM and everything in between all function together as one unit.  The BIOS originally installed on your computer was probably "overcome by events" as Windows 8 changed over the past year, and it needed to be brought up to speed, so that's a good thing. 

 

One of the changes HP made in an update was that the wireless button remains white (on) and does not toggle to off (yellow) as it did before.  When it turns yellow, it means that you have lost your wireless connection altogether.  When you toggle f12 now, you will be switching "Airplane Mode" on/off. 

 

(Airplane mode allows your laptop to operate without interfering with air traffic control when you are flying.  By turning it on, you disable your laptop from sending/receiving wireless signals.  So, be sure you have it turned OFF).

 

 

Two things I might suggest you try............

 

1)  turn off your computer, unplug your router, and unplug your modem.  Wait for about 2 minutes, then turn them back on in this order....modem, router, computer, waiting until each one "initializes" before going to the next one. (this is merely synchronizing the three devices and resetting everything).

 

2) Restore Internet Explorer advanced settings and reset Internet Explorer's defaults.  To do this....

 

Open Internet Explorer on the Desktop

Select "Tools" from the menu bar

Select "Internet Options"

Select "Advanced" (tab on top)

Click the button "Restore Advanced Settings"   

Click the button "Reset"  (you don't have to check the "personal settings" box)

Click Apply

Click OK

 

restart your computer for these changes to take effect.

 

         

 

One more thing you should do, if you have not already done so, is to go back into HP Support Assistant and check for more HP updates.  Windows just sent out a bunch of updates this Tuesday, and HP may have to keep pace with those.  You are probably set to receive them automatically, but check for them now to get current.

If you have an anti-virus program,such as Mcafee, Norton, etc, also check for updates in your AV program.
