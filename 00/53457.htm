internet expert say:

For decades, Microsoft has measured the decimal "Kilobytes (KB)", as binary "Kibibytes (KiB)", but always interpreted it as "kilobytes". Disk manufacturers compound the issue by stating the capacity of their products with the actual decimal metric. The International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC) had to invent and standardize the binary prefix of "kibi-, mebi-, gibi-, tebi-, ..." in 1998 to address the confusion, but Microsoft never bothered to interpret it correctly in their operating systems..


In your case, your hard drive is actually 4.0 terabytes in real capacity, but the Xbox OS detects it as 3.6 tebibytes, but calls it as terabytes. (gaming_and_xbox,

Hi CJayAM,

Let’s follow these steps and check if it helps.

Method 1

You may check in the Device Manager if the HDMI Output is set as default device. Follow these steps to set the device as Default Device

a. Click on Start > Control Panel > Click on Hardware and Sound > Now click on Sound.

b. Now under the Playback tab > Right click in the blank space > Select both "Show Disabled devices" and "Show Disconnected Devices".
c. Now right Click on HDMI Output. Click on Properties-> Under the General tab click on "Device Usage" and select "Use this Device :( enable)". Click Apply. Click OK.|

d. Repeat Step a & b. Under the Playback tab > Click on HDMI Output and “Set as Default”.

e. Click Apply. Click OK.

Method 2

You may uninstall the graphics card drivers from device manager and download & install the older version driver for graphics adapter from the manufacturer’s website.

a. Type devmgmt.msc in start search box and hit Enter.

b. In device manager, expand graphics card in the device list.

c. Right click on it > click Uninstall.

d. Install the downloaded driver and check.
