It was necessary to edit the registry file to restore ATAPI drivers (excuse my laymen's language) att HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Services\atapi for Controller0 by adding DWORD(32-bit) Value to EnumDevice1 and from 0 to 1.

a. user say:

What can you do?

You can free up additional disk space so there is at least 15% free space by deleting things, moving things to another volume or getting a larger HDD.  If you are that close on disk space, it will probably just be a matter of time before you gave to revisit this.

The XP defrag program is a slimmed down version of Diskeeper from Condusiv Technologies and it uses that 15% free space as a sorting area for file fragments.  If a volume has less than 15% free space, defrag will only partially defragment it.

You can force defrag to try to work with less that 15% free space, but that doesn't sound like a very good idea to me but if you want to do that you can read this/these KB articles that tell you how to use the -f switch:

http://www.microsoft.com/resources/documentation/windows/xp/all/proddocs/en-us/defrag.mspx?mfr=true

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/283080
