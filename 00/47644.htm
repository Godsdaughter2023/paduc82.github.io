This is usually a driver error though it can be malware, antivirus/antispyware/security programs
and possibly hard drive problems.

Hi Vlad,

Thank you for letting us know about your concern.

If you're unable to get 4K resolution on your Smart TV when connected to your Surface Pro, we suggest that you check the refresh rate on your device. To do this, follow these steps:

  1. Open Settings.
  2. Click on System.
  3. Click on Display.
  4. Click the Advanced display settings link.
  5. Click the Display adapter properties for the external display.
  6. Click the Monitor tab.
  7. Under "Monitor Settings," use the drop-down menu to select 30Hz.

If that didn't work, run the Surface Diagnostic Toolkit. This will run a series of tests on you device and automatically install any missing driver and firmware updates. Click here to learn how to run the toolkit. After running the toolkit, force shutdown and restart your device to ensure all updates are applied.

If running the toolkit did not work, run the Hardware troubleshooter.


  1. Type Troubleshoot in the search box.
  2. Click on Troubleshoot.
  3. Select Hardware and devices troubleshooter.
  4. Click on Run the troubleshooter button.

You may also try to uninstall and reinstall the graphics driver.

  1. Go to the Device Manager.
  2. Expand Display adapters.
  3. Right click the graphics driver and choose Uninstall.
  4. Restart your Surface and let Windows reinstall the driver.

Reset your Surface if none of these steps work. Click here to learn more about resetting your Surface.
