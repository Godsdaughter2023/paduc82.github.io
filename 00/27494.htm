gord dibben say:

Do you have the DV Dropdown list created?

Do you have the VLOOKUP formulas in place?

Do you have a Subtotal formula?

If all you have left is to prevent the #N/A then wrap the formula with ISNA or IFERROR

A post from Jerry Latham gives a couple of examples.

"wrap" your formula in the Excel 2007/2013 IFERROR() function like this:

=IFERROR(VLOOKUP(),"")

Of course you'd put the full VLOOKUP formula into it.

If there is a chance that you'll be passing the workbook to people who have earlier versions of Excel (2003, XP, 2000), then do it this way:
=IF(ISNA(VLOOKUP()),"",VLOOKUP())

The IF(ISNA()) is slower than IFERROR() but will work on all versions of Excel.

marshall-s say:

Here are two solutions that may work.

1.  Go into the OneNote app and choose File->New->Add a Place and choose "One Drive".  Doing this will cause OneDrive to prompt you for your Microsoft could login (in my case I use a Gmail address for my PC and the Microsoft cloud).  Once you've logged into the Microsoft cloud, you may then find that if you choose Open you will have two OneDrives listed under "Open from other locations".  One of these two OneDrives will give you access to your Notebook stored online and the other will not.  (I don't really know why, because they both point to the same OneDrive root folder.)  Does that make sense?

2.  Once you go to OneDrive through your browser and select a notebook, if you choose the command "Open in OneNote" (by right clicking on the file icon) that notebook will open in the OneDrive application on your computer.  From that point on, that notebook will be on the list visible on your computer. 

marshall-s say:

There is another problem which I just discovered.  If you have two Microsoft accounts then your computer may be unsure which OneDrive to use.  In my case, we have a company microsftonline.com account and I have my personal use account.  If you have loaded OneDrive for business, OneNote may default to that file path for OneNote storage.  In my case, it looked the same as the file path for my personal OneDrive and I didn't notice the difference (that was the reason the OneDrive files didn't show up).

To fix this, go into File->Account->Switch Account and see if this allows to you to switch to the account which you have chosen as the default for your OneNote file storage.  If it works, you'll probably see under "Connected Services" the OneDrive associated with the desired account.
