rohn007 say:
You have 2 possible versions that come "free" from the factory.

One is a copy of "Office 2010 Starter". This is a crippleware / adware version of Office that contains stripped down copies of Word and Excel. They do not have to be activated.

The other is a 60 day time limited full function trial.  60 days after you first start any of the applications it will go into limited function mode.  This appears to be your current situation.

You will have to buy a license to "activate" the factory installed copy.   The Office Product Key is similar to the Windows Product Key you have seen on the sticker on the computer.

You can buy Office in several ways:
  • a retail "boxed CD" full version ("FPP" type license)
  • a Product Key Card ("PKC" type license)
  • an online download of either "FPP" or "PKC" / "1-computer" license.

The PKC is trivially cheaper, but is limited to being installed on only 1 computer. No transfer allowed. When the hardware dies or is sold to someone else, the Office license goes with the computer.


The "FPP" type license allows you to install and activate your copy of Office on 2 (3 for Home and Student only) computers at the same time AND to transfer those installations to new computers as needed.


Personally, I prefer to buy the "boxed CD" version of what ever bundle so I "always" have the installation disk. For example, I have and still use Office 2003 which is not available for online download.


You also have to decide which Office bundle you want to buy: Home and Student, Home and Business or Office Pro.
