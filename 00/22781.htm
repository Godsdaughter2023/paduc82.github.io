feelew say: The thing with cut & paste is that the address to the cut cells are updated at their new location, while the address of the cells that are being pasted over totally disappear from the board. This is what creates the #REF! error. It doesn't matter if you're only dealing with 3 rows. If the cells disappearing underneath your pasted cells were linked to formulas in other cells, you get the error.

That's why cut & paste can be good with small regions: you can paste onto empty cells that are not linked to any formula in the sheet, and everything can remain okay, but it's always better to fully understand the effect of cutting & pasting. Then you know if or where damage may occur.

feelew say: If you insert rows (assuming your fields are in columns), make sure to insert either full rows, or at the very least rows that cover all the significant columns of the data set.

If one of your fields contain one or more formula referring to either rows above or rows below (this happened to me several time over the years), please note that your formulas will also suffer from this insert.

As an example, if you have a field that contains a reference to the row below, then inserting a full row will make the row just above the inserted row referring to 2 rows below (i.e. to the same cells containing the same data as before the row was inserted).

Keep that in mind. Test it out for yourself, maybe.

a. user say:

Microsoft Office with no InfoPath or Access??

I have Microsoft Office Professional Plus 2007  and I installed it and it only installed:

Word, Powerpoint, Excel, Outlook, Publisher! I also installed the second part that includes Business Contact manager. But I have no InfoPath or access! Isn't supposed to come with that? I see the access and infopath folder on the disk but it doesn't install.

 

This is not a pre-release or beta version or trial it's the real thing I got.

 

I tried it on my XP, vista, and win 7 machine and they all didn't install it even though I have plenty of space!

kathy s - microsoft support say:

Hi,

You may have already resolved your issue, but in case you haven't........

Yes, if you have the Microsoft Office Professional Plus 2007 suite, you should have both Infopath and Access.

When you did your install, did you go into the installation options to see if Access and Infopath were accidentally set to "Not Available". You could try selecting the "Customize" option and then making sure all products are listed with no Xs.

If you already have Office 2007 installed on your machine, go into your control panel to Add/Remove programs and click the "Change" button for Office 2007. Then select "Add or Remove Features' and see if Access and Infopath have red Xs next  to them. If they do, they did not install and you will need to change those options to "Run from My Computer" or "Run All from My Computer".

IF the software box says Microsoft Office Professional Plus 2007 and lists Access and Infopath but they are not on the disk, I would take the product back to the company you purchased it from.

Hope this helps,
Kathy

How to make an accent mark over any letter Russian alphabet - Microsoft Community

charleygraybell say:

How to make an accent mark over any letter Russian alphabet

I writing some text in Russian (Cyrillic letters) using the Russian keyboard. I want to apply an accent mark over a letter to indicate where the stress should go in pronunciation. I'm finding lot of web information on how to insert standard international characters in several languages, but nothing on this. Thanks!

Maybe there's a special font I can use?

charleygraybell say:

Thanks everyone for your suggestions. Here's the method I found after doing some more research. 

Open mac character viewer: Go to the gear wheel at the top left and select Customize, then check the box for Unicode, then in Unicode go to range 00000300 Combining Diacritic Marks and select 0301 Combining Acute.  Drag/drop or double click after the base letter to add it to your Cyrillic.

To make a shortcut of the above, 

With TextEdit, use the System preferences text substitution using any little used symbol, such as %. For some reason, System preferences text substitution doesn't work with Word, but you can use its autocorrect to do the same thing.
