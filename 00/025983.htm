forwarding an email an arrow appears at the beginning - Microsoft Community

annesewell say:

forwarding an email an arrow appears at the beginning

Original title: forward an email an arrow appears at the beginning

When I forward an email an arrow appears at the beginning of each line of the message (this de-activates any hot-links in the note); how do I get rid of that?

ron sommer mvp 2009-2017 say:

The arrows do not appear when you forward an email.

The arrows can appear when you reply to an email.

Reply to an email.

Click on Options.

Is 'Rich text' or 'Plain text' selected as the format?

If you select 'Plain text', you will get a message that the formatting will be lost.  Continue has be clicked to change the format.  You will note that arrows are added to the email.

If you send the email or cancel the email, outlook.com remembers that you were using 'plain text'.

The next time you reply to an email, 'plain text' will be used.

If plain text is being used to reply to an email, click Options and select 'Rich text'.  Cancel the email.

Now you can reply to an email and 'rich text' (no arrows) will be used.

Note: this is not a browser issue.  Changing browsers will have no effect.

pamcaswell say: For your styles, go to change styes > Word 2010. These are the factory default paragraph  style settings.  Apply the styles to your paragraphs--heading levels, text,  and such. 

To add numbering, with the cursor in the first heading 1 in the document, click the Multilevel List button on the Home tab  then from the List  Library choose the  one with  1 Heading 1, 1.1 Heading 2, etc. icon. The numbering will be applied and you'll be back in the document.  Immediately click the Multilevel List button again, then click Define new multilevel list.  Change the Number style for this level to I, II, III, ...,  the click  the OK button.

If that's all the customizing you want, you're done.

anthony.b say:

You might try making a new blank Word document, and remove all the styles except the ones your want.  Then copy in your text to the document.  One way to do this is to copy your document into Notepad, and then copy from Notepad back to Word.  As an example of this, below is a template that I use for some of my longer Word documents - it has ONLY styles for Heading1-3, and styles for the Table Of contents, and a 36 point style for the heading page.  If you want Roman Numerals, your list of styles would look different.  My idea is, as you work your document, and it grows larger and larger, keep an eye on the list of styles, and be sure that no new unwanted styles creep in (if they do, delete these unwanted styles).  By the way, in case you did not know how to get to Styles, in my screen-print, I put a red arrow on the launcher for the Styles window.
