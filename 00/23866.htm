techfreak_ say:

The majority of people who claim this never:

1. Account for the connection charge once per call.

2. Calls made are rounded and charged to the next whole minute and whole cent.

If you check the Skype rates page under the Skype credit section (See rates link), it will provide you with the connection charge for that location, when dialing Pay As You go.

Ex: 

up to 1 minute call to USA in CAD (No VAT or GST rates) = 6.1 cents connection fee + 2.8 cents a minute x 1 = 8.9 cents = 9 cent charge

up to 2 minute call to USA in CAD (No VAT or GST rates) = 6.1 cents connection fee + 2.8 cents a minute x 2 = 11.7 cents = 12 cent charge

up to 1 minute call to CAN in CAD (No VAT or GST rates) = 6.1 cents connection fee + 3 cents a minute x 1 = 9.1 cents = 10 cent charge

up to 2 minute call to CAN in CAD (No VAT or GST rates) = 6.1 cents connection fee + 3 cents a minute x 2 = 12.1 cents = 13 cent charge


Minute and unlimited subscriptions to numbers that are covered do not impose a connection fee per call. However, all deducted minutes are rounded up to the next whole minute.

You are free to contact Skype support but they will probably relay the same information to you.

benishiryo say:

hi there. assuming your 1st screenshot is in Sheet1. Range of cells are in A2:B10. 

2nd screenshot in Sheet2. Range of cells in A:B.

select the range you want to apply to (say from B2:B10)  go to Home -> Conditional Formatting -> New Rule -> Use a formula to determine which cells to format -> Format values where this formula is true:

=$B2>VLOOKUP($A2,Sheet2!$A:$B,2,0)

This is for over 100% of weight. format it a certain color.

The rest are not very clear. 75% of max meaning exactly 75% or ranging from 1-75%? or 75%-90%? you could use it along the same lines:

=$B2=VLOOKUP($A2,Sheet2!$A:$B,2,0)*75%

=$B2<=VLOOKUP($A2,Sheet2!$A:$B,2,0)*75%

=$B2>VLOOKUP($A2,Sheet2!$A:$B,2,0)*75%

when using the last option, the sorting order of your conditional formatting matters. the one above 100% must come first. and in descending order, the 90% and then 75%. if 75% comes first, all numbers above 75%, 90% or 100% will be highlighted according to >75% format.
