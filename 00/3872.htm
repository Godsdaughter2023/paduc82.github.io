talking moose say:
Outgoing rules will not work with Exchange because of the way the Exchange Web Services (EWS) protocol works.

When you create a message in Outlook and click the Send button you are actually sending instructions to the Exchange server to create the message on your behalf and send it for you. Once sent, the server will then place that message into your Sent Items folder on the server. Outlook will synchronize in about a minute and copy the message to the Sent Items folder on your computer.

This may sound strange but create an Incoming Exchange rule to move your messages. Instead of filtering for All Messages you want to filter for messages from your email address for filter for messages going to your Sent Items folder. When Outlook syncs it will trigger the rule for new messages downloading from the server into your Sent Items folder.

Hope this helps!

Column A in alphabetical order by last name - Microsoft Community

michellekoch say:

Column A in alphabetical order by last name

I have two columns.  In column A I have a list of doctor names, ex. Sam Lee M.D.  In column B I have adresses, ex 1234 Cherry Lane.   I want to list the doctors in column A in aphabetical order by last name.  How do I do so?
ronrosenfeld say:

In general, you would set up a "helper column" adjacent to your data, containing just the last name, and sort on that.  The issue will be how to determine the last name.

In your example, the last name will always be the substring that precedes the last substring.  In other words, the name before the MD.  This will work so long as all of your doctors have only a single word last name, and all of your listings have an MD (or some other title) following the last name.  A formula to return the next-to-last word in a string is:

=TRIM(LEFT(RIGHT(SUBSTITUTE(A1," ",REPT(" ",99)),198),99))

and this will work IF your data, in the relevant portion of the string, is formed exactly as you have displayed it:

 <LastName><space><MD>  or even <LastName><space><M.D.>

 

However, the formula will fail under certain circumstances

  • No MD or other title following the last word
  • Last name that consists of more than one word
  • Last name followed by a comma instead of a space
  • Multiple spaces between LastName and MD, or a space after MD.
  • And there may be other variations that you have not posted.

So, if that formula doesn't work for you, you will need to be more detailed on the variations in your data.

The multiple space issue can be handled by TRIMing the doctor name:

=TRIM(LEFT(RIGHT(SUBSTITUTE(TRIM(A1)," ",REPT(" ",99)),198),99))
