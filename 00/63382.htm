j w stuart say:

Disk Management is what you use to format a drive (Start / Right click on 'Computer' / Select 'Manage' and then click on Disk Management).

However most external USB drives are pre-formatted, already have a drive letter (not C:) and will be displayed in Disk Management beneath the drive that has Windows installed on it.

If the drive still needs formatting Disk management will show it as "Unallocated", in which case you right click on the external drive to format it.

Note: If the 'Initialize' window should be displayed in Disk Management' then for drives 2TB or less you should initialize as "MBR". For drive larger than 2TB initialize as "GPT" and then format the drive.

1. Stop all services beginning with "home" on all Computers.

2. Stop all services beginning with "peer" on all Computers.

3. Delete the content of the "C:\Windows\ServiceProfiles\LocalService\AppData\Roaming\PeerNetworking" folder on allComputers.

4. Shut down (really shut down, be careful with hybrid fast boot) all computer except of one.

5. On this computer restart the home group provider service.

6. No home group should be found now. You should be able to create a new home group.

7. Restart all other computers and join the home group.

8. If the aim is to have no home group: Once you've joined the home group with all computer, they should be able to leave the home group clean.

Although you may see posts from individuals with


under their name, those individuals are not actually Microsoft employees.  They work for some company with which Microsoft contracts to provide tech support services.

By far the greatest number of people responding here -- whether they have "MVP" or "Community Moderator" next to their name or not -- are non-Microsoft employees who volunteer their time and experience to help people.

All of that is to say that you're not likely to get to any official Microsoft tech support here.  If you use this forum to complain about Microsoft tech support, the most you're likely to get is a chance to vent.

What you may get -- if you explain your problem in detail -- is a solution from some volunteer who has come across your issue (or one similar to it) before.  To enhance the likelihood of that happening, you should include

  • details about your system (e.g., make and model of hardware; version and service pack of Windows; version and name of software program involved)
  • the symptoms you are experiencing
  • the complete text of any error message(s) you get
  • what happened shortly before the problem began (e.g., Windows Update, power failure, dropped laptop, ran registry cleaning or tune-up program)

And please try to pick the correct forum to post in.  You asked your question in the "Windows XP | Other apps" section, so I assume that it has something to do with XP.  If not, you should post in a different section.
