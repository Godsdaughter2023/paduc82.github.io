graham mayor say:

If your heading styles are based on the Normal style (which by default they are) then changes to the normal style will be reflected in those heading styles.

There are two possibilities.

1. Modify your heading styles to be based on No Style

2. Don't use the Normal style in your documents. Use instead a body style that reflects your formatting requirements.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

A good reason for leaving Normal alone (changing only the font if desired) and using Body Text as your basic body text style. See http://wordfaqs.ssbarnhill.com/NormalVsBodyStyles.htm

To answer your actual question, however, here's what to do before you make any modifications to the Normal style.

  1. In the Quick Styles gallery, right-click Heading 1 and choose Modify...
  2. In the "Style based on" box, change the style to "(no style)."
  3. Repeat for Heading 2 and as many more levels of headings as you're using in the document.

This will unlink the heading styles from Normal, but you'll still have an indent in every other style based on Normal, which is basically all of them. Much better just to leave Normal alone and use another style for your body text.

charles kenyon say:

First, read the article linked by Suzanne.

Then, open your normal template.

How to Open the Normal Template

Change your styles there.

1. Alter your heading styles so that heading 1 is based on no style, heading 2 is based on heading 1, heading 3 is based on heading 4, etc.

2. As you do that, change the style for following paragraph either to the next heading style or to the Body Text style.

3. Change the style that is in your first paragraph to Body Text. Make sure that the setting in Body Text for next paragraph style is also Body Text.

4. Close and save the normal template.

How styles in Microsoft Word cascade by Shauna Kelly

suzanne s. barnhill say:

You can open the Normal template and change the default empty paragraph to Body Text. Another helpful step is to set the "Style for following paragraph" for the heading styles to Body Text instead of Normal.

You can, of course, modify any of these styles in any way you like, but for my purposes I usually use one of two combinations:

  1. For reports and technical documents, headings are followed by Body Text, which is used for all body text. Body Text has the default formatting, which includes some Spacing After to separate the paragraphs.
  2. For books, Body Text is formatted with no Spacing After. Flush-left headings are followed by Body Text (which has no indent), and Body Text is followed by Body Text First Indent, which is used for the bulk of the body text. For manuscripts that have to be double-spaced, I use the same plan except that I format Body Text and Body Text First Indent as "Exactly 24 points."

It is a general typesetting convention that paragraphs are distinguished either with space between (as in letters and reports) or with a first-line indent, not both.
