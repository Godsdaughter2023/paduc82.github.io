Manual Page Breaks in Word document converted from .doc to .docx. - Microsoft Community

p.squared say:

Manual Page Breaks in Word document converted from .doc to .docx.

Original manual page breaks created prior to Word 2007 are visible in draft view, and appear across the entire width of the page:

 

··············································································································Page Break·······································································································

 

(It looks like regardless of whether or not the formatting marks are displayed.)

 

However, new manual page breaks created in the converted document are not visible in draft view.

When made visible by showing formatting marks, they only appear as

 

 ····················Page Break···················¶

 

at the left, center or right margin, depending on how the paragraph is formatted.

 

Question:  Can all manual page breaks be made to look like they did before Microsoft "improved" this feature?

pamcaswell say:

The earlier  (W2003 & before) page breaks are not in their own paragraphs.  They take on the style of the following paragraph.  This has caused various formatting problems in documents over the years, such as wrong page number in TOCs, page break deletions deleting the style of the following text, etc.  This is one reason many formatters prefer to use  Page break before, instead.

If you turn on formatting marks (click the ¶ in the the Home tab), you'll see that the W2007/10  manual page breaks do end with returns (are in their own paragraphs) and can have a paragraph style independent of the following text.   I think it's an improvement.

 

If you prefer the older page breaks, go to Options > Advanced > Layout Options and  check the box before "split apart page break and paragraph mark".

 

Pam
