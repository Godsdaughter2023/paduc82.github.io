a. user say:

You know what you should do?

Your video driver is crashing but it is very recent and it is not at fault in and of itself.

I'm fairly certain that XP does not have a TDR mechanism to revive the video subsystem such as later versions of Windows do.

For that reason, you can't possibly be getting 0x116 errors like what you are getting is similar to.

So how to fix?

Pull out your video card. Clean the fan all nicely with a q-tip and rubbing alcohol. Be gentle as the fan blades can crack off rather easily.

Make sure there is not a lot of dust and debris around the card. Use a can of compressed air if you must. Do not use alcohol on the electronic parts or the board itself.

Once you are sure it is nice so heat can not build up easily on it, put it back in the machine and give it a nice firm push so it seats well, carefully. Make sure the power cable is attached to it very well too, if it requires that at all.

Let me know how it is going for ya after these things and if you have any more crashes, please do not hesitate to allow me to see the new one(s).

Monitor the card's temperature with MSI Afterburner. Watch some videos on YouTube at 1080p and tell me the highest temperature you see.

Best of luck...

Also, do you know the exact model of your psu at all?
