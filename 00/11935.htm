john w. vinson say:

The WhereCondition argument needs to be... as I said upthread...

ONE SINGLE TEXT string consisting of a valid SQL WHERE clause.

Since you didn't post the actual contents of strCrit1, strCrit2 and so on I'm a bit groping in the dark here. My original suggestion of concatenating all the criteria into strCrit would have worked and would save five variables and a few lines of code. But as I read the code you should be able to use

DoCmd.OpenReport " FacilityIdentifyReportDR", acViewReport, WhereCondition:=strCrit1 & " And  " & strCrit2  & " And  " &  strCrit3  & " And  " &  strCrit4  & " And  " &  strCrit5

What I'm doing is constructing one big text string containing the five strCrits separated by the word " and ". Your proposed code would not incorporate the word AND into the criteria string - it would instead try to perform a (nonsensical in the context) logical AND operation (which works on true/false values) between the five text strings.
