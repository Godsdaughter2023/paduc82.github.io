jay freedman say:

The only way you can have an equation in Display format but sitting in line in text is to fake it by inserting the equation in its own paragraph but positioned so it appears to be within a text paragraph.

One way to do this is to insert a text box, click the layout button next to it, and choose the In Line with Text icon. Then click inside the text box and insert the equation. Drag the edges of the text box until the equation fits properly inside it.

The inline text box's bottom edge will align at the baseline of the surrounding text, which doesn't look good. To fix this, select the regular text on one side of the equation, open the Font dialog and click its Advanced tab, and set the Position boxes to raise the text to align at the middle of the equation -- in 12 pt text, raise it by about 15 pt. Do the same on the other side of the equation. (If you later edit that paragraph, you may have to change which words are raised.)

bill manville say:

I suggest you take a look at Advanced Filter.
It has an option to copy selected columns satisfying a set of criteria to another location.

 

You could use the macro recorder to record a macro while you do the three Advanced Filter operations and then create a Worksheet_Change event procedure for the master sheet that will detect a change in the status column and run your filter & copy macro.

 

I don't have time today to write the macro for you.  Have a go yourself and if you get stuck, come back with what you've managed to do and ask for help to finish it off.
