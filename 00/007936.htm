How do I revert AOL emails that have been downloaded by Outlook 2007 - Microsoft Community

james lemmon say:

How do I revert AOL emails that have been downloaded by Outlook 2007 back to the web-based AOL mail, as they have been deleted from web-based email?

In the process of setting up an outlook account, I have accidentally downloaded my father's emails from his AOL account. How can I safely put them back, so he can continue as if nothing has happened? I have attempted the long-winded method of forwarding the messages back, but somehow these wont send. What other suggestion would you recommend to return the messages safely to his AOL inbox?

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

Usually, AOL access is via IMAP accounts, but what you describe doesn't happen with IMAP accounts.  Messages stay on the server.  I conclude, then, that you connected to AOL using a POP account.  The behaviour you describe (downloading messages, then deleting them from the server) is indicative of a POP account.

Leave the POP account in place.  Create an IMAP account to connect to the AOL mailbox.  Disable the POP account in the Send/Receive Group so that Outlook won't try to download any more messages using that account.  To disable the account within the Send/Receive Group, press Alt+Ctrl+S, select the group (most people have only one, named "All Accounts"), click Edit, select the POP account in the left-hand pane, then uncheck the box at the top labeled "Include the selected account in this group".  Click OK, then Close.

At this point, you should see both your default folders where your POP account delivers and the folders on the AOL server.  You'll then be able to copy the messages back to the server by opening your default Inbox, selecting the messages in it, then dragging the selection to the Inbox showing in the IMAP account folders.

rohn007 say:

external Zipping is no longer a valid option.  The "x" file formats already zip the contents of the file. The article is refering to XLS files, NOT XLSX! Actually, zipping an already zipped file can increase the resulting file size ...  so even zipping the images before inserting them to the XLSX file will probably increase file size instead of decreasing it.

You can reduce the resolution of the image. (these instructions are on 2010, but 2007 should be essentially the same)  Click on any one of the images. In the Pictures Tools > Format contextual tab > Adjust group > Compress Pictures button.  Uncheck "apply to this picture only". Select "email 96 DPI ..." Then click on OK to apply compression to all pictures.  This will make the picture sizes smaller by reducing their "resolution", by reducing the number of "dots per inch" (pixels) of information kept.  This means the images will be adequate for viewing on screen, but will not allow you to blow them up to much larger sizes.

Google is your friend. There are lots of articles on compressing spreadsheets, ie:

https://duckduckgo.com/?q=excel+compress+file+size&t=ffab
