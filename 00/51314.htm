I found a solution!!!

 

It is quite simple.

1. Go to Gmail settings - labels

2. Remove the ckeckmark next to bin/trash

Now the trash folder won't show up in imap-based mail apps (like the Win 8 mail app).

No problem. It's easy - once you know the trick! Actually, there are many ways to do this. Here's one:

If the link is in the body of the email, click your mouse pointer on a blank space to the left of the link and while holding down your mouse button, drag the pointer to the end of the link. That selects the link. Now press CTRL+C to copy the link. Navigate to wherever you want to paste, then click on a blank space and press CTRL+V. That pastes the link.

If the link is an attachment, the way you copy depends on which email client you are using. For example, in Microsoft Outlook (not Outlook.com) right-click once on the attachment and a context menu opens that allows you to copy the link. Paste it wherever you want by clicking on a blank space and pressing CTRL+V.
