rohn007 say:

Nope, they are scams.

By definition, all "365" bundles are subscriptions. You have to renew them periodically.

All of the "Office 2016 ???" bundles are "lifetime" licenses.  There is an Office 2019 Professional Plus one-time payment license but the only way to buy it is as a "Volume license".  Volume licenses cannot be resold to anyone. They are very expensive.  Sometimes people on the internet re-sell volume product key at a steep discount.  These keys will only work until MS recognizes the use pattern is not legitimate. Then MS disables the keys, and the buyer is out of luck.

rohn007 say:

That is one way. But more likely is that they buy multiple product keys and resell unique keys to each individual.

The volume license requires you buy a minimum of 5 products, up to 250 (I think). But there is a specific limitation in the license is that they can only be used inside the company that bought them.  They explicitly cannot be given or resold outside of the company. But that is what is happening in this scam.  They sell a bunch of product key, then shut down the website before MS or the law can catch them. Then they setup under a new name and go on with the scam.  Eventually MS figures out that a bunch of people with hotmail, or free outlook.com email addresses are using the keys and then they void the license and disable the product keys.  The scammer has the money, the user have no way of contacting them and no longer can use the office they thought they paid for.

What is Network Access Protection?

Network Access Protection (NAP) is a platform that network administrators can use to help protect the security of a network. When you connect to a corporate network that uses NAP, your computer is checked to make sure that it has the required software and settings, and that the software and settings are up to date. If anything is missing or outdated, your computer can be automatically updated. Your network access might be limited during that time, but usually this process happens quickly, after which full network access is restored.
