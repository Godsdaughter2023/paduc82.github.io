a. user say:

You have no worries - this is a very common task, and your SSD manufacturer's technical support staff will help you.

If you buy a retail SSD it will come with complete instructions and all the software you'll need. You can also find the instructions on the SSD manufacturer's website.

What you'll be doing:

1. Making an image of your HDD - an image is an exact copy of the entire HDD, including the metadata that makes it bootable.

2. Restoring the image to the SSD.

That's it! And it won't take a long time, either.

It's true that a SSD is inherently faster than a HDD, but in order to realize the full benefit of that speed, the rest of your computer needs to be equally fast. For example, if you buy a SATA III SSD but your computer has a SATA II data bus, you won't get any faster than SATA II speed.

Also, you'll get the most benefit from a SSD's speed with tasks that do lots of reads and writes. On the other hand, for a task that does sequential reads, like viewing video, you're unlikely to notice an improvement.

Last tip: You never defragment a SSD. Instead, let Windows 10 'optimize' the drive, which basically means run the TRIM command.

Enjoy your new SSD!
