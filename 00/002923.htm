how to create new archive policy on exchange online - Microsoft Community

asharaf alishaik3382 say:

how to create new archive policy on exchange online

team 

i would like to create a custom archive policy & like to apply to all users on exchange online tenant.

My request is , move all emails which are older than 60 days to archive mailbox for lifetime. Please help on this.

Thanks 

aaronzhu_msft say:

hi asharaf,

please follow the following steps to achieve your goal:

1. create a default retention tag.

a. sign in eac(exchange admin center).
b. navigate to compliance management>retention tag.
c. click + and choose applied automatically to entire mailbox(default).
d. type the tag name. choose move to archive under retention action. enter the retention period(60). then save.

2. create retention policy includes this retention tag.

a. navigate to retention policies and click +.
b. type the name and click + under retention tags. then add the retention tag created previously. then click ok.
if you have any other requirements, you need to add other corresponding retention tags to this policy. for you reference:
retention tags and retention policies: https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/dd297955(v=exchg.150).aspx
create retention tags and retention policies: https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/jj150573(v=exchg.150).aspx

3. apply this retention policy to all users(to apply a retention policy to all users, we suggest you use windows powershell).

a. connect to exchange online using remote powershell.
b. run the cmdlet: get-mailbox -resultsize unlimited | set-mailbox -retentionpolicy "example" (replace example with your created customer retention policy name).
c. to enable all users' archive, run the cmdlet: get-mailbox -filter {archivestatus -eq "none" -and recipienttypedetails -eq "usermailbox"} | enable-mailbox -archive

if you encounter any problems or error messages when trying the steps above, please provide some screenshots of them so that we can further assist you.

to upload screenshots, click use rich formatting > insert\ edit media.

thanks,
aaron

there are 3 type of retention tags: default policy tag, retention policy tags and personal tags.

the default policy tag applies to all items that do not have a retention tag applied, either inherited or explicit.

the retention policy tags are created for default folders such as inbox, deleted items, etc.

the personal tags are used by outlook and outlook web app users to apply retention settings to custom folders and individual items such as email messages.

regarding “here i don’t see the archive option to move the folder”, yes, the “applied automatically to a default folder” doesn’t have the “move to archive” option indeed. and we are not able to move this folder to archive in this scenario. however, as a workaround, we can create a default policy tag to move the folder to archive because the default policy tag are applied to all items that do not have a retention tag applied. to do this, please click the plus symbol > applied automatically to entire mailbox (default).

Can Excel seperate the last word in a field for the entire sheet? - Microsoft Community

mark123mark say:

Can Excel seperate the last word in a field for the entire sheet? Pull the text til a blank space is found.

none needed
rich007a say:

If you want to extract the last word from each cell in a range of cells you can use the following formula.

Let's say your strings are in column A.

In B2 you could use:

     =IFERROR(RIGHT(A1,LEN(A1)-FIND("*",SUBSTITUTE(A1," ","*",LEN(A1)-LEN(SUBSTITUTE(A1," ",""))))),A1)

You can then drag that down column B as required* and column B will contain all the last words of column A.

*If there are no empty cells in column A, double-clicking on the small handle in the bottom-right corner of the cell will automatically fill down to the last row of data in the column to the left of that cell.

Hope that helps.

Cheers
Rich


PS. If you wanted to do that for a whole sheet, you can just put that formula in A1 of a new sheet, and if your data is in for example "Sheet1", replace each reference to A1 with

'Sheet1'!A1

The sheet name need only be contained in single quote (apostrophes) if there is a space in the sheet name.

Once you have that formula, drag down and across as required.

Set up Find My Phone to save your location every few hours so it's easier to locate your phone, or to use push notifications instead of text messages to send commands (and apps) to your phone faster and more cheaply. You'll change these settings on your phone, not on your computer.

  1. In the App list, tap Settings , and then tap Find my phone.

    • Always use push notifications (not SMS) to send commands and apps to my phone. The Find My Phone service and the Windows Phone Store on the web use text messaging to send commands and apps to your phone. Check this option to use push notifications instead. Why? Push notifications can be faster than text messages, and if your mobile operator charges you for each incoming text message, push notifications might also be cheaper. Also, Find My Phone can only send 15 text messages to your phone in a three-day period. With push notifications, there's no limit.
    • Save my phone's location periodically and before the battery runs out to make it easier to find. Find My Phone can save your phone's location details every few hours so that if your phone is out of range or the battery dies, your phone's last known location will still be on a map.

To find your phone

  1. Go to account.microsoft.com/devices. If you're prompted to sign in, use the same Microsoft account you used to sign in on your phone.

  2. Choose the phone you want to find, and then click Find My Phone.

    Tip
