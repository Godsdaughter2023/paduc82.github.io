lemp say:
Dell's Service Tag -- which is usually on a sticker on the outside of the computer -- is Dell's way to identify the particular unit received from the factory.  It's encoded in the BIOS so that Dell's online scanner can locate it if the owner can't find it or if it has become detached or illegible.

There's no service tag in the replacement mobo because it can be installed in computers with a variety of configurations -- and because Dell apparently didn't feel that it was cost-effective to customize it just for you (assuming that you submitted the original service tag when you ordered the replacement mobo).

Doing some quick Google searching reveals that Dell has -- or at least had at one time -- several utilities that purportedly were able to configure a service tag in a new/replacement mobo.  Most of the links to svctag.exe and asset.com are dead or outdated. 

If it were me, I would call Dell and ask them for the correct utility, explaining why you want/need it.
However, if you want to take a chance on bricking the machine, see http://forums.mydigitallife.info/threads/19369-Dell-Bios-Editing-Removing-Bios-Lock-Changing-Service-Asset-Tag-Software-Here or do your own search using the particular Dell model.  Or try asking on a Dell forum.

In any event, I'd just advise the customer to use the original service tag from the sticker whenever they need to find drivers or other info about the computer.
