kishorenatarajan say:

MS Word: gap between lines from one page to another

jay freedman say:

The usual cause is that the paragraph(s) after the gap have paragraph formatting that includes either the Keep With Next option or the Page Break Before option.

Right-click in the paragraph immediately after the gap and choose Paragraph from the menu. In the Paragraph dialog, click the Line and Page Breaks tab. Do any of the boxes I've highlighted have check marks?

If they do, that's the cause of the gap. The next thing is to determine whether those settings come from a paragraph style, or are direct formatting (possibly brought in with a copy/paste). Press the Shift+F1 shortcut to open the Reveal Formatting pane. At the bottom of the pane, check the box for "Distinguish style source". Then look at the items under the Paragraph heading to find the Line and Page Breaks group. If it's under a "From Paragraph Style" subhead, then you need to modify the style; if it's under a "Direct Formatting" head, you can simply clear the paragraph format by pressing the Ctrl+Q shortcut, or go to the Paragraph dialog and remove the check mark(s).

john p spencer say:

If you don't apply an aggregate function (Sum, Avg, Count, etc) toa field then YOU MUST Group by the field.

Given your SQL you would need

SELECT ( YEAR([percents].[case_date]) ) AS case_dates,
       percents.somPercent            AS som_percents,
       percents.sonPercent            AS son_percents,
       percents.sodPercent            AS sod_percents,
       percents.med_resPercent        AS med_res_percents,
       percents.den_resPercent        AS den_res_percents,
       percents.undergradPercent      AS undergrad_percent,
       percents.gradPercent           AS grad_percents,
       percents.facultyPercent        AS faculty_percents,
       percents.staffPercent          AS staff_percents
FROM   percents
GROUP  BY ( YEAR([case_date]) ),
       percents.somPercent          ,
       percents.sonPercent    ,
       percents.sodPercent     ,
       percents.med_resPercent       ,
       percents.den_resPercent       ,
       percents.undergradPercent     ,
       percents.gradPercent           ,
       percents.facultyPercent        ,
       percents.staffPercent     

However, I cannot see any reason for you to use GROUP BY at all with what you have posted.  A simple DISTINCT operator in the SELECT clause would be all that is needed to return unique records.

SELECT DISTINCT (YEAR([percents].[case_date]) ) AS case_dates,
       percents.somPercent            AS som_percents,
       percents.sonPercent            AS son_percents,
       percents.sodPercent            AS sod_percents,
       percents.med_resPercent        AS med_res_percents,
       percents.den_resPercent        AS den_res_percents,
       percents.undergradPercent      AS undergrad_percent,
       percents.gradPercent           AS grad_percents,
       percents.facultyPercent        AS faculty_percents,
       percents.staffPercent          AS staff_percents
FROM   percents
