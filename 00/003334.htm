    What should my CPU setting be when running a virus scan - Microsoft Community

    joe doyle5456 say:

    What should my CPU setting be when running a virus scan

    What should I be using for a CPU setting when running a virus scan?  My scans pause or stop when users are changes or my computer and web page are left on but Windows reverts to screen saver due to no activity.  

     

    kosh vorlon say:

    Hi Joe,

     

    The more CPU you allocate, the faster the scan will run but the fewer resources you will have for anything else.  I guess the answer depends on when and how you do it.  If you leave your computer on all the time and schedule scans to happen when you're asleep, then you can probably safely raise it to 50-60% or even higher as nothing else will be interfering with it.  On the other hand, it you use a laptop and/or put your computer to sleep or hibernation at night (or shutdown completely) and thus need to run the scan in the background, then you probably want to keep it at the default (which I believe is 20%) so you have sufficient resources to do your other work at the same time it is running without much interference.  I find that I can do almost everything I normally do while it is running without any significant decrease in performance as long as I don't let myself go overboard (like opening 30 IE windows and tabs at the same time which sometimes happen unless I pay attention).

     

    If you change users, it's understandable why the scan would pause or stop.  The CPU is now dedicated to the other user and no longer to the first user - so its availablity to the user who initiated the scan (and that's the user who is using the CPU for MSE) is limited (if it's even available - I'm not quite sure about that).  I believe switching users works similar to putting it in hibernation for the old user. 

     

    I do not understand why it occurs if your computer goes to screensaver (that should not happen of course - otherwise you couldn't use a screensaver the entire time you are scanning and they never would have built that in - plus I know mine continues to work when it goes to screensaver) unless it also goes into some form of power saver mode at the same time.  Check your power saver settings (on advanced) and make sure that you don't have anything shutting down at the same time the screensaver activates (like your internet connection or your hard drive or something like that).  I mean, the objective is to scan exactly at times like that when the machine is idle so it certainly wouldn't pause or stop due to idle or due to the screensaver.  I don't use a password on my screensaver.  If you do, see if removing the password resolves the problem.  I can't believe that's the issue or I'd have heard about it by now, but I guess it's worth a try.  If that works (since it shouldn't matter), please let me know.

     

    If none of the above suggestions keeps the machine from pausing or stopping the scan when going to screen saver (and you know it isn't any other power saver setting), then please submit an email support case to Microsoft Support starting here: https://support.microsoftsecurityessentials.com/.

     

    I hope this helps.

     

    Good luck!
