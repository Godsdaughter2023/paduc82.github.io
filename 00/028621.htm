ikiros say:

Let me break down the questions and their answer:

Is it possible to see e-mails in the "sent message" box or are they hidden?

It depends on the complexity of the malware. If it is a more complex type of malware, then it will most likely send the e-mail, then erase it from the 'Outbox' or 'Sent' folder. This will then make it much harder to detect. However, there is a way to see if someone has LOGGED(not sent e-mails) into your e-mail account. Depending on which e-mail service you are using, they usually keep a log of where and when you've logged into your e-mail account. I know that Gmail, Outlook, and Yahoo have it. You can usually find them in each of the mentioned services' 'Account Settings' page.

Can my system send e-mails without my knowledge?

Yes, indeed it can. For starters, malware could create a backdoor(or remote access point) on your computer. This could then allow 'hackers' to access your computer without you directly knowing. You can usually detect backdoors if your PC suddenly begins to slow down and lag. When a backdoor is put on your computer, the hackers basically have complete access to all your files and accounts(including bank accounts you may have saved). One way you could stop this is by simply turning off your PC's connecting to the Internet. Backdoors are done through the net, so shutting off all internet access will surely stop the backdoor and will give you the ability to destroy the malware. If you happen to have a backdoor and you know of it, start your computer in Safe Mode. Then, if you have access to another computer, download an Anti-Malware program into a USB/CD. Afterwards, INSTALL the program into the USB/CD. Then pop the USB/CD into your PC on Safe Mode and start the program from there.

PM me if you have any questions.
