MF_MT_VIDEO_ROTATION attribute

https://msdn.microsoft.com/en-us/library/windows/desktop/hh162805(v=vs.85).aspx

UPDATE 2

Apparently, not all players recognise the rotation attribute and assume landscape playback- whatever the metadata suggests.  If your video is meant to be played back (i.e. is recorded in) landscape but the phone mistakenly adds the 'rotation=90 degrees' attribute; a player that does not recognise rotation will play it as it should.

Only one that considers the rotation att will play it rotated - 

If your chosen player does recognise the spurious rotate attribute - you CAN correct the metadata or use another player.

Correcting the attribute DOES NOT require you to re-encode/transcode/convert your video. There is no loss of quality as you are not altering the video.

Having said that, it doesn't look very easy, however I believe the link below to contain the answer (not yet tested)

pamcaswell say: Press Alt+F9 to toggle the field codes.  Then inspect the Seq fields for the table captions.  Make sure they say "SEQ Table".  

The \c switch in the TOC code builds a table of figures for the given SEQ identifier. 

(The explanation in the Field dialog for the \c switch says for the given [caption] label, but that is wrong.)

Another  possibility:
If your tables TOC is looking for a particular paragraph style, make sure the right style has been applied to the  table captions.
