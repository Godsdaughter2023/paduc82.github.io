andre da costa say:
200 MB recovery partition
This is for the Windows Recovery Environment (WinRE), which also shipped with Windows Vista only it was available from the Windows Vista installation disc only, although some OEMs made it available on a separate partition on their factory installations. In Windows 7, this feature is installed on all computers, and is stored in the 200MB partition if you install using a single partition scheme. However, if you install on a drive that has multiple partitions, the recovery environment is stored in a folder in the root of your installation drive, so if you install to a partition with a label of C:\ it will be located at C:\Recovery.

And your problem has 'what' to do with the subject?

 

Re overheating:

 

If you have a desktop machine.. Remove the side panel and check for operatinn of all internal fans,especially the CPU fan. Use a can of compressed air to clear dust buildup from the space between the fan and the heatsink.

You might want to use a straightened out paper faetener to remove the worst of it before blowing the rest out. It is also a good idea to wear a surgical mask. Use the compressed air in short blasts to prevent dust ending up on everything inside and outside of the case..

 

If you have a laptop.. There is generally no panel which you can remove that will give direct access to the fan(s), but you may be able to carefully blow air through the grille directly above. If you do not think that the fan is working, you have two options.

 

  1. Take your laptop to a repairer and get the fan fixed. This is the best option because portability is maintained. Unfortunately, it is not a cheap option.  
  2. Use a good quality cooler base from a reputable manufacturer. If your laptop is between 15.6" and 17", get yourself the Coolermaster X-Slim cooler base. It is light, powered entirely by USB power, and has a huge centrally placed fan. It works well and I have one under a Toshiba L500 where the ineternal fan is not working.

Why you should be selective about cooler bases (or any fan assembly)..

The highest flow of air from any fan is generated on the ends of the fan blades. This is important when using a cooler base under a laptop where the internal fan is not turning at all. Laptop fans are positioned towards the edges of the laptop casing, so that it where you want maximum airflow.

A small fan placed centrally under the laptop will not put air where you need it most. The Cooler Master X-Slim has a 160mm central fan which will cover the area where the laptop heatsinks runs and is large enough to keep the hard drive area cool at the other corner of the laptop..
