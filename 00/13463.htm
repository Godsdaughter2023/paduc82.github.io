rohn007 say:
"Licenses" in Office are represented by Product Keys.  Since you are dealing with less than 5 licenses you cannot use the discounted Volume agreement, you must buy individual retail licences.  Can you see need for 2 more license within the next year or so to justify a volume license?

Although there is a "Product Key Card" option, I strongly recommend against it. It is slightly discounted, but for most people the discount is not worth the license rights you give up. Unfortunately most buyers aren't aware of the limitations in time.  PKC licenses are good for installation on one computer PERIOD. Like an OEM (factory install) license the PKC license dies with the hardware. In worst case scenario, a new computer dies and is replaced under warranty, but you will still have to BUY a new Office license!  In contrast, the retail / "FPP" license, allows you to install on 2 computers for the same user (a "primary" device and a "portable" device), and to later transfer those licences to new hardware as required.

If you decide on retail licenses you have 2 options: Online download and Boxed CD/DVD.  They typically cost the same. I normally recommend the Boxed CD so that you don't have to rely on MS maintaining  the download links. People with 2003 can NO LONGER download installation files, and Academic licenses require the original educational email address, so former students can no longer download.  In your case I would suggest buying at least one Boxed CD so that you have the media on hand for future re-installs.

mel77084 say:

Your activation is digital, based on running a certain version, such as home or pro, with your existing motherboard, and is stored on the activation server at Microsoft. Changing your hard drive or doing a clean install doesn't require reactivation. If you do a clean install, be sure to install the version, home or pro, that you now have activated. Early in the install process, when it asks for a key, check the box "I don't have a key", and proceed. The activation will be automatically continued by the server.

My personal preference is to keep all data on a separate drive, redundantly backed up, and the operating system and all installed programs on an SSD of around 120 GB, backed up in a disk image (about weekly) saved on a separate drive. I usually keep the image of the current build and the two previous ones, as well as the last public release.

That allows one to restore to one of the previous builds in a few minutes, and since the data is on separate drive, it stays current, so after restoring to a previous build, the accounting program can be run using current, undisturbed, data.

Also, in case the SSD fails, it only requires a few minutes to recover on a new one.

In your present system, when you unplug your SSD and plug in the new one to do the clean install, the drive that your data is on will not be bothered during the clean install. If you keep all of the newly installed programs on the new SSD, you can then just delete any remnants of old programs from the data drive.

Hi,

The On-premise product keys provided with the Action Pack subscriptions allow a limited number of activations.

Licenses are considered used when a product key is activated in one of the following ways:

If the software is installed on a different machine, one product activation is considered used against the product key.
If the software is installed on the same machine, with no changes made to the hardware, product activation is not considered used against the product key.
If the software is installed on the same machine, with changes made to the hardware, product activation is considered used against the product key.


Once activation of the product key has been fully accounted for, partners are not be able to use it again and won’t be able to request additional keys.



Please refer to this link to view the license table for Microsoft Action Pack Subscription and refer to the screenshot below where you can access it:

https://partner.microsoft.com/en-us/membership/...

If you need to use or deploy more licenses that the ones granted, you will need to upgrade your Partnership level to a Silver or Gold Competency to receive more product licenses or you may contact your Local Microsoft Office for details on how to purchase these licenses through a channel such as Retail or Microsoft Volume Licensing.

jacyintha say:

One problem people see a lot, and don't realize until it's pointed out, is that you need to make sure your time, date, and time zone are all set correctly. The year might be off on the date, or your time zone might be set to something close, but wrong.

Try setting that to manual, make sure all the settings are right, then go to the IE settings and delete the browsing history. Much like a Windows computer, the browsing history stores cache data for your phone. Clearing it periodically can be beneficial. After that, just restart your phone.

If you're still having issues, something might have gone wrong with your firmware install. If you're using a Nokia, you can use the Nokia recovery tool to reinstall the firmware on your phone. Unfortunately, the other manufactures haven't put out a tool to reinstall this yet.
