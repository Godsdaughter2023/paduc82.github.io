a. user say:

Once activated, a Windows 10 PC remains activated forever, no matter how many times you re-install Windows 10 or what method you use to re-install. There are no special steps that you need to perform.

A computer's activation is tied to the computer's hardware. That's how Microsoft knows whether Windows 10 is installed on the same computer or a different computer. It doesn't matter whether you sign in to Windows 10 with a Microsoft account or a local account, or whether you purchased your license or received a digital license. Neither of those have anything to do with the computer's hardware.

A Windows 10 PC will only lose its activation if its motherboard is changed, because a computer with a different motherboard is considered a different computer.

P.S. You don't need to 'wipe' your C: drive to install or re-install Windows 10.
