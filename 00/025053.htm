You are *ABSOLUTELY* *RIGHT* about this!

Often there are time-critical applications that something such as "scheduled things" or "windows update" or "we feel like retrieving data from the cloud right now" would TOTALLY SCREW UP  whatever real-time or gaming process is going on.

Here are a few examples of OTHER things (that aren't games) that ALSO need this:

a) audio and video "live recording" - including "just sitting there waiting for the time to start recording", such as a DVR application.

b) A/V playback in general.  If you're watching a video in the foreground, *NOTHING* may interrupt!  Right?  Unless you like skippy video playback.

c) MIDI and audio 'recording studio' applications.  if you're playing somethign 'live' and recording a track, or even working on a bunch of recorded tracks to mix them down, the absolute LAST thing you need is "some other thing" INTERRUPTING you, even if it's a simple "priority inversion" problem.  How about "turn all that off while I do this" instead?

d) any real-time processing that a Windows application may need to do, including the controlling of devices and whatnot.  let's say you have a security camera that records data when motion is detected, and your windows machine controls it.  You do NOT need interruptions in this process, or your security camera MAY NOT PICK UP WHAT IT NEEDS TO RECORD, ya know?

In *ANY* case, it means *MORE* *CONTROL* *OVER* *OUR* *OWN* *MACHINES*!

(no 'black box' 'we feel you need this' kind of thing.  NO!)
