sirebellum say:

I solved issue 1 by updating my tpm firmware. This firmware update is provided by Dell on their website when navigating to the drivers and software section for your XPS 15.

Below are the steps I followed:

1. Create a bootable DOS usb drive. (This is to install the tpm firmware for reasons elaborated on below). Search for "Create a bootable DOS usb drive" on google to find a plethora of guides on how to do this.

2. Copy tpm.exe (name will be something arbitrary) downloaded from Dell onto the bootable usb. Keep note of the name of this file as you will need to execute it later.

3. Clear the TPM of current ownership. I am not sure as to the implications of this clear, but I cleared mine and nothing went horribly wrong. Again, type "Clear the TPM of current ownership" into google to find some guides on how to do this.

4. Computer will restart as part of ownership clearing process. DO NOT SIGN BACK IN. I found that signing back in automatically assigned ownership to the TPM, and so the installation of firmware would not complete. Instead, restart again and boot into the bootable DOS usb created earlier.

5. Once booted into the usb, type the name of the executable of the tpm.exe you copied earlier and hit enter. If you forgot the name of the executable, type "dir" (without quotes) into the console to list all folders and files in the directory.

6. Let the magic happen

I hope this helps!
