In that range of percentages, the installer is installing your currently installed programs into the new build; so, likely, it is finding one of your programs to be incompatible with the new build. I some cases, it will simply not install that particular program, and go ahead with the others, but, for certain ones that exhibit characteristics of capabilities to deeply modify the new build, the install will abort.

I recommend that you look at the the programs that you installed after you did the clean install, and uninstall any that might fit that description, such as any sort of "mechanic", etc., or, 3rd party security programs, several of which can only be completely uninstalled by the use of its maker's special uninstaller.

Once that has been accomplished, your upgrade will likely complete successfully.
