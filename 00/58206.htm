fas72 say:

After a couple of weeks of trial and error, I was finally able to send my email today addressed to five (5) addressees on the TO: line and sixty-four (64) addressees on the BCC: line.

The success or failure is related to how many times and where duplicate addresses occur in the email heading.

It is quite common to include yourself on an email just to be sure that the email actually goes through the system.  If you have the same email address on the FROM: and TO: lines; the FROM: and CC: lines; the FROM: and BCC: lines, the message will work as expected.

However, if you have the same email address on the FROM: and TO: lines and either the CC: or BCC: lines, the message will fail to send as described in my original post.

Additionally, I also discovered that if the same address appears multiple times in the BCC: line, then the message will also fail to send.

    Also notice, if you were to make a split, the newly formed clip would show you the frame that starts that new clip.

    So to reiterate, there is no issue; that's the way Microsoft designed it.
