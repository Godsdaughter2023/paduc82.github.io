Okay, no takers?  Well, I figured out.  Somehow the following DWord registry entry was set to 1 which prevents the user from using Accelerators.

 

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Policies\Microsoft\Internet Explorer\Activities\NoActivities

 

I set the value to 0 (zero) and all is good.

 

Resetting IE did not set the value to 0 as this setting is a policy setting which should not be reset by the user when resetting IE.

Just about everything on the MS websites about OneNote is pablum on the order of "make sure your computer is plugged in and turned on." Much of what is there confuses the Office and app versions as well as applying only to earlier obsolete versions.

In my case I contacted the on-line chat, was sent an Email and given a phone number and case number.

When I called that number and finally spoke to  someone, no easy feat, I was told Microsoft does not provide telephone support by whatever Asian boiler room call center to which they farm out that service.

Nothing endears Microsoft to end users more than that kind of slap in the face.

The only solution for users who face a similar problem, thousands judging by the number of posts one encounters, is first DO NOT DELETE THE NOTEBOOK THAT WILL NOT SYNC OR THAT DATA IS LOST FOREVER.

Download a copy of Evernote and one by one recreate your notebooks/pages or copy them one by one into whatever program you choose.

There is a software tool on the web from Microsoft that sort of works to import back into OneNote your notebooks from Evernote. It is very slow and does not recreate the notebooks with pages in correct order. It will split large notebooks into random sections but you can laboriously fix that.

Otherwise after copying your notebooks to Evernote or whatever you choose you laboriously recreate your OneNote notebooks.

Once you verify that your "new" notebook will sync across platforms you can delete the original but it might be wise to keep it.
