The Mail Merge feature in Mac Word does not provide an option for hand picking individual recipients. If those to be included have something in common [such as Zip Code, Last Name, or some combination of data] use the Filter Recipients button to specify the criteria for including only those recipients who match the criteria. Sort and Filter Recipients | IT Services Helpdesk | MSU Denver

Sway pupil profiling

Hi,

I'm looking into using Sway to create individual profiles for each pupil in my class. I would like to be able to upload examples of their work and link these to outcomes in the curriculum for assessment purposes.

jason (office engineer) say:

Assuming you're using an Office 365 account (from the school), there are three permission levels a Sway can be set to (unless your administrator has disabled some of them):

  • Those in your organization with the link - (the default) only people that are signed in with an email address in your same organization/school will be able to open the Sway and only if they know the link to it
  • Specific people - with this option, you can choose which accounts have access to the Sway, but you are only able to select people with email addresses in your organization/school; you still need to send them the link after giving them permission to the Sway
  • Anyone with a link - with this option, anybody, signed in or not, will be able to view the Sway, the key here is that they need to know the link to the Sway still; as Sway URLs are random strings, you can't really guess them, you only know them if someone sends you the link

I think the third option will be best for you (all three options are in the Share dropdown when you open a Sway as the author).  People will be able to open other children's Sways, but only if someone has sent them the link to the other Sway.  As long as you send out each student's link to only their parents, nobody else will know the link to it.  As an added level of protection, in the More options section of the Share dropdown, you can set a password on the Sway in order to view it and then you can send parents the link to their child's Sway along with the password.

If my assumption was incorrect and you are using a personal account (e.g. @hotmail.com, @live.com), Sways are all public by default, but again, they're protected by the fact that people don't know the URL to them unless you've shared it.

Hope this helps!

Jason

macropod say:

When you start Word, it gathers information to use for document formatting and printing operations. If the files, drivers, or fonts are damaged, missing, or located on a remote server, Word can require more time to connect to the server and gather the information that it needs. If Word starts very slowly, or other operations, such as opening or printing documents, also are very slow, this may be due to any one or more of:
• Microsoft Windows using a printer driver located on a network print server, instead of a local printer driver on your hard disk. When you use a remote printer driver, you may notice that it takes longer to change your default printer setting, and sometimes Word may print documents more slowly than you may expect.
• Word is installed on your computer, but Word was once running from the network. When the computer is not connected to the network, there is a long delay when you are opening documents.
• Fonts that are used in the Word document are damaged or missing from the system, and Word cannot substitute other fonts in the document.
To resolve such issues, see: http://support.microsoft.com/?kbid=280821

Similarly, when you open a Microsoft Word document when connected to a network, it may take longer than expected to open if the document has a template attached or linked and one of the following conditions is true:
• The attached template, its folder, or its share is missing.
• The attached template has been moved.
• The attached template has been renamed.
• A linked file referenced by the document is missing, has been moved or renamed.
When a Word template is missing, Windows XP will try to locate the missing template multiple times. The process of locating a missing template may cause a Word document to take longer than expected to open.
To resolve such template issues, see: http://support.microsoft.com/?kbid=830561
If you're opening the file from a completely different location (e.g. at home instead of at work), simply disconnecting from the internet while opening the file can improve performance.

Opening a Microsoft Word document linked to an Excel workbook can may take longer than expected to open, especially when connected to a network. For a registry patch to reduce such delays, see: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/kb/970154
Note that the registry subkey referred to in the article depends on the Office version:
• Office 2007 - HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Office\12.0\Word\Options
• Office 2010 - HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Office\14.0\Word\Options
• Office 2013 - HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Office\15.0\Word\Options
• Office 2016 - HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Office\16.0\Word\Options

Disabling hardware graphics acceleration is unlikely to help. It certainly won't make an iota of difference with any of the above...
