  2. Check the Windows.old folder. This folder is being created when you upgrade from one version of Windows to another. To check it, perform the following steps:
    A. Right-click on the Start menu, then select File Explorer.
    B. Go to This PC > Local Disk (C) > Windows.old.
    C. Double-click Windows.old to open the folder.
    D. Look for the Users folder and double-click it. Look for your username, then double-click it to open the folder. 
    E. Check all the folders. Desktop and Documents are the most common folders wherein your files are saved.
  3. Switch to your old user account. In some cases, Windows 10 creates a new account. To check if there's a temporary profile created after the update, click Start, then click your username. You will see another account available on the list. If there is, click on it to switch to your old user profile.
  4. Change Indexing Options. Here's how:
    A. From the Start menu, type Indexing Options, then press Enter.
    B. Click Advanced.
    C. Go to the Index Settings tab.
    D. Under the Troubleshooting section, click Rebuild.
  5. Set the folder temporarily to Hidden and Read-only. To do so, follow these steps:
    A. Go to the folder with the missing files, right-click on it, then click Properties.
    B. In the General tab, check Read-only and Hidden under Attributes.
    C. Click Apply, then OK.
    D. Go to the same folder again, but uncheck Hidden and Read-only this time.
    E. Click Apply, then OK.
  6. Check your hard drive. Refer to these steps:
    A. Go to This PC, right-click Local Disk (C), then click Properties.
    B. Go to the Tools tab and click Check.
