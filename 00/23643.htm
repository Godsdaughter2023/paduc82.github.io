joeu2004 say:

PS....

Generally, the arithmetic anomaly is not considered a bug.

Instead, it is a side-effect of how Excel (and many application) represent numbers internally, namely 64-bit binary floating-point, which is an industry standard.

[....]

The reason is:  most decimal fractions cannot be represented exactly internally; and the approximation of a decimal fraction depends on the magnitude of the integer part of the value.

I forgot to explain the "remedy" (work-around), to wit:

Generally, when you expect the arithmetic result to be accurate to some precision (two decimal places, in your case), you should explicitly round the arithmetic to that precision.

(And not to some arbitrary precision like 10 decimal places, as some people suggest.)

So, the formula in H2 should be =ROUND(SUM(D2:G2), 2).

That would be sufficient for =IF(C2<>H2, "error", C2-H2) to return exactly zero, assuming that C2 is a constant, not an arithmetic expression.

(If C2 is an expression, explicitly round that expression, as well.)

Why remove the Exchange account? You should be able to add the same mailbox with an IMAP connection at the same time as having another Exchange account accessing that same mailbox.
