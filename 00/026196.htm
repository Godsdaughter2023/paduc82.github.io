jay freedman say:

All of the Heading styles from 1 to 9 are always available in every document. However, the default is to show only the first two in the Styles gallery on the ribbon until you use Heading 2, at which time Heading 3 becomes visible in the gallery, and so on for each of the other levels.

If you want all 9 styles to appear in the gallery at all times, do this:

  • Click the little arrow in the bottom right corner of the Styles group (or press Ctrl+Alt+Shift+S) to open the Styles pane.
  • Click the Manage Styles button (the third from the left at the bottom of the Styles pane).
  • In the Manage Styles dialog, click the Recommend tab.
  • Select the names of Heading 2 through Heading 9 (click Heading 2, then hold the Shift key while you click Heading 9).
  • Click the Show button in the bottom part of the dialog.
  • Click the "New documents based on this template" option button.
  • Click OK.

To see all the Heading styles in the gallery at one time, you'll have to click the bottom one of the three arrow buttons on the right end of the gallery to expand it.

Bonus hint: Regardless of whether the styles are all shown in the gallery, you can press Ctrl+Shift+S to open the Apply Styles box, and use the dropdown there to select and apply any style.

·        Change all references from 2007 to 2010, 12 to 14 and 12.0 to 14.0
