lemp say:

As you probably know, Kodak was never really a camera company.  It was a photographic film company that sold mostly inexpensive cameras in order to get customers to buy its film.  First with the advent of Polaroid "instant" cameras and then the introduction of digital cameras, Kodak's film business declined and in 2012, Kodak declared bankruptcy.  Kodak did emerge from bankruptcy, but sold off its film business other than motion picture film.  It's no longer much of a consumer-oriented company, although last year it introduced a service for consumers to digitize film, slides, prints, and videotapes.

If you would like some help in getting photos from your KES camera to your computer, read on.

In your original question, you said that your "Windows 7 stopped supporting Kodak Easy Share uploads."  In your follow-up reply, you said that you could "no longer transfer pics from my K.E.S," and you mentioned "drivers."  The term "upload" generally refers to transferring data from your computer to an online web site, while "transfer pics" -- especially in the context of "drivers" -- suggests getting the photos from your camera to your computer.

Although you won't be able to upload your photos to the EasyShare or Kodak Gallery web site (because it isn't there anymore), you may actually still be able to use your Kodak EasyShare camera and transfer your photos from the camera to your computer.  And regardless of what camera you have, you will be able to use any of a variety of online photo sites.

Usually, a digital camera manufacturer bundles some proprietary software with the camera.  Typically, such software includes the ability to transfer photos from the camera to your computer, view and organize the images on your computer, print pictures, often connect to an online photo service, and perhaps other functions.

However, in general, Windows treats digital cameras as external memory devices and does not actually require the use of the proprietary software.

Most digital cameras use a memory card to store the pictures as you take them.  At least some Kodak EasyShare cameras use both internal storage built into the camera and a memory card.  With respect to the images saved to the memory card, you can easily take the memory card out of the camera, insert it into a card reader, connect the card reader to your computer, and transfer the pictures. 

You should be able to transfer photos using your USB cable without the KES software and you certainly should be able to eject the memory card and use it in a USB-connected card reader.  See, e.g., http://support.en.kodak.com/app/answers/detail/a_id/48148/selected/true

a. user say:

Hi,

 

I’m afraid I have to inform you that it is not possible to open two instances of one file, with the same app.

 

However, there are a couple of workarounds that you can use to open two instances of the same file:

 

Method 1: Open one instance in Windows Reader app and another instance in another application such as Adobe Reader.

 

Method 2: You can make a copy of the file with a slight change in the name. For example, if the file is named FileReader.pdf, then you can make a copy with the name FileReader(1).pdf. This will allow you to open two instances of the same file as well.
