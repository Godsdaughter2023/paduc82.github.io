After using PPT's save as web page feature, close your original presentation, then open the just-saved HTM file in PowerPoint; this is what we call "round-tripping through HTML" or just round-tripping. 

You'll probably need to do some tweaks to the colors/colorscheme afterwards, but sometimes round-tripping can cure the kind of minor corruption your presentation' seems to be fighting with.

You can also move the apparent problem slides around in the presentation to see if the problem follows them (in which case it's likely a corruption problem) or not.
