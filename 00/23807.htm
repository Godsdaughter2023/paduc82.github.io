richardboyerfl say:

Defender History Blank

Why is my Defender History of Detected items blank? Shouldn't it be full of detected items? Quarantined and Allowed items are also blank. Thanks
rob koch say:

Since you installed the Anniversary Upgrade the typical daily Defender scan will have become less frequent, which is a known side effect of a change in the security principal Defender uses to access the PC.  Though this reduces the number of regular quick scans, the fact that real-time scanning is always being performed as you use your PC means that regular scans really aren't necessary.  They are simply a "sanity check" which you could also perform manually if you prefer they happen more often.

The Defender definitions updates still seem to be operating properly though, and typically happen once each 24 hours, while if MAPS is enabled it can also allow the immediate download of a more current definition if one is available.

Since unlike some 3rd-party security applications the Microsoft security applications are free, they spend no effort trying to prove to you that they're paying their way.  Instead they tend to operate vary quietly and don't detect things like cookies which aren't really malware, rather just minor privacy items that can easily be blocked in the browser if you prefer.

The record of any true malware detected will remain for a month by default if you don't personally delete them more quickly, but in most cases a person who browses safely isn't likely to experience malware very often.  You were most likely being informed of such less risky items as cookies or possibly PUPs such as adware which Defender doesn't always detect if it doesn't act in a malicious or misbehaving manner.

Remember, the other companies are trying to sell you something, Microsoft just wants you safe.

ADFS not loading from External networks - Microsoft Community

justinfare say:

ADFS not loading from External networks

Hi All,

I recently ran into a problem where users trying to sign in to Office365 from outside our network were being redirected to our ADFS site for entering their password, but the site would not load. From inside the network, it worked beautifully. After getting caught up thinking it was a firewall problem, I stumbled across something in AD FS Management.

When I click on "Authentication Policies", then look under the "Primary Authentication" section in the middle pane, there is a title "Global Settings". Clicking "Edit" to the right of this brings up "Edit Global Authentication Policy". Here we can set the authentication method for Extranet and Intranet, and I noticed my Extranet was set to Forms Authentication, but the Intranet was set to Windows Authentication. When I set the Intranet to be Forms Authentication as well, my problem went away. Users can then land on the AD FS page and authenticate the session.

What I think is going on is that ADFS couldn't see that the traffic hitting it was actually External traffic, and was then trying to use Windows Authentication. Our firewall, Netbox Blue, doesn't like certain sorts of Authentication methods, and as we are using the Reverse Proxy with this firewall, it may also be then appearing to ADFS that the traffic is coming from inside our network.

Sorry for the long winded post, I thought it best to be specific for anyone else going through the same thing.
