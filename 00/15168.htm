A normal phonebook? - Microsoft Community

moribunde say:

A normal phonebook?

I just got a new Nokia 710 and I'm fiddling with the peoples hub, I didn't want to see all the contacts from my windows live messenger so I blocked this account but then I realized by doing this I can't see any of my contacts that I add because for some reason any number I save to this phone becomes a windows live contact. Is there any way around this? I simply want a normal contacts list (basically an actual phone book) that doesn't get cluttered by my separate messenger contacts.
paulc_1 say:

The very first time you start your phone, it would have prompted you for a Windows Live account to setup as the default account on the phone. If this is the only Windows Live account you have set up, then any new contact you create will be automatically synced to Windows Live online. You can not turn off the syncing of contacts to Winddows Live account that is your default account. That is just the way Microsoft designed the Windows Phone device.

If you "block" this account from displaying contacts (ie, go to "people" hub, tap "..." on the bottom right, then "settings", then "filter my contact list" and you tick "Windows Live") then no contacts will show up on your phone. Newly created contacts on the phone will be still be synced to Windows Live messenger account online, but are not displayed on the phone. However, you will still be able to search for them on your phone from the "people hub". (Tap the "search" charm and type in a name of a contact you have added to the phone to check.) 

In order to do what you want to do, if you want to keep the WIndows Live Messenger account, you'll need to create a new Windows Live account and use that for fresh contact data.

Steps to take:

1. use your desktop web browser to create a second (new) Windows Live account with a new email address and password at live.com and

2. add this new one to your phone as a "Windows Live" account under "Settings--email & accounts--add an account".

3. Once set up, it will show up on your phone as "Windows Live 2".

4. Now got to the "People" hub, tap "...", "settings", then "filter my contact list" and uncheck "Windows Live:" and tick "Windows Live 2". Then hit the back key and 

5. Tap "add a new contact", then "new contact". You will be given an option to create in "Windows Live" or "Windows Live 2". Choose "Windows Live 2" and you'll be sweet.

6. Only with the second "Windows Live" account will you be able to turn syncing on and off for Email, Contacts, Calendar, and Tasks. The default "Windows Live" account will only let you turn Email syncing on or off.

You might want to reset your phone and set the newly created second "Windows Live" account up as your default acccount. But that is entirely optional.

Powerpoint / When I open a powerpoint at work and go to master - Microsoft Community

tjthegeneral1963 say:

Powerpoint / When I open a powerpoint at work and go to master view the Master Layout is greyed out

When I open a powerpoint at work and go to master view the Master Layout is greyed out - why is that?
john korchok say:

If you're having trouble displaying slide numbers, please follow these steps:

  1. Choose View>Slide Master and select the Slide Master (as John Willson has said, the larger slide at the top).
  2. Click on Master Layout and make sure Slide number is checked.
  3. Check the Slide Layouts (the smaller slides below the Master). If a layout is supposed to display slide numbers, there should be a placeholder displaying <#>. If that is not present, check Slide Master>Master Layout>Footers to restore it.
  4. Close Master View.
  5. Choose Insert>Header & Footer and make sure Slide number is checked. If it's not, check it and click on the Apply to All button.
