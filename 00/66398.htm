frederik long say:
Few days ago, I was sharing my laptop with another person. So I created an account for him and set the permissions of Users and Authenticated users to full denial for the local drive C: .

Denying access is one of the worst things you can do, for these reasons:

  • It frequently has unintended side-effects.
  • It can be very hard do undo.
  • System Restore will not fix it.

What you should have done is to grant access to specific folders instead of denying access. Applying "deny" to the whole of drive C: is particularly troublesome. Since Windows resides on drive C:, how should your friend launch Windows?

You can try this method to fix the problem:

1. Keep tapping F8 during the early boot phase.

2. Select Repair from the menu that looks like 3. Log on as Administrator if prompted.

4. Select Repair when prompted.

5. Select the Command Prompt from the menu that looks like

6. Identify the correct drive letter for your Windows installation. It could be a drive other than C: but it won't be X:. You do it by typing the commands

    dir C:\Win*

    dir D:\Win*

    dir E:\Win*

    until you find the Windows folder.

    I will call that drive "Q:".

7. Type these commands:
    takeown /F  c:\  /R

    (to seize ownership of all folders on drive Q:)

    icacls  Q:\  /T  /C  /Grant  everyone:F

    (to grant everyone full access to all folders on drive Q:)

8. Reboot normally then Windows Explorer to grant more specific access to your various profile folders. Stay well clear of the "deny" column.
