Belgian settings:

As I suspected.

In US English, comma is the column separator for an array-constant row; and semicolon is the row separator for an array-constant column.

In languages that use comma for the decimal point, an alternate separator is needed for array constants.  As you and Hans demonstrate, that is back-slash.

But there is no need for an alternate separator for semicolon.  So apparently, semicolon is also used for the same purpose in those languages.
