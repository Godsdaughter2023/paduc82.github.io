lemp say:

As you noticed, your Installer directory is taking up a significant portion of the Windows directory.  Without the Installer files, your Windows directory would be around 32 GB which is relatively normal.

Back with Windows XP, there was a utility called the Windows Installer Cleanup Utility, but that was pulled by Microsoft as being too dangerous even for XP and it definitely does not work for Windows 7.

Microsoft has issued some rather dire warnings about deleting files from the Installer directory:

https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/help/2667628/missing-windows-installer-cache-files-will-require-a-computer-rebuild

https://blogs.msdn.microsoft.com/sqlserverfaq/2013/04/30/do-not-delete-files-from-the-windows-installer-folder/

Nevertheless, articles are still published that purport to describe safe methods of pruning the Installer folder.  I have not attempted any of these methods.  Use them at your own risk (I strongly suggest making a full disk image backup before trying these cleanup methods).

https://www.raymond.cc/blog/safely-delete-unused-msi-and-mst-files-from-windows-installer-folder/

https://www.ghacks.net/2015/12/13/patchcleaner-remove-orphan-windows-installer-files-to-free-up-disk-space/

The C:\Prefetch folder is not likely to be very large and deleting files in that folder is not helpful.  See, e.g., https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/windows_vista-update/can-i-delete-the-data-in-the-prefetch-folder/c54a8e56-c8cf-451d-a88f-07f06a2f2d54?auth=1

The number that are advertized are not of the real functional Network Bandwidth but the "Speed" that the Wireless Chipset works Internally.

As an example 802.11g is 54Mb/sec usually yields at 6 feet in the same room between 18 to 22 Mb/sec., which translates to real transfer of about 2 MB/sec.(b=bit, B=Byte)

If the Router and the client Wireless are both good and real 300 devices you might get around 60Mb/sec. that might translate into 6MB/sec. functional LAN Transfer.

Add to it electrical environmental noise, obstructions etc., and you get less and many times much less.

The best result can obtained by using Wireless Router and client cards that have Dual Radios (2.4GHz +5GHz) 2.3GHz, and are capable to use them simultaneously.
