greginmich say:

Turning off Windows Defender Antivirus notifications will only turn off the “enhanced notifications” that tell you when your device was scanned and that “no threats were found” If you’re still receiving notifications then they’re probably detection notifications that are telling you that some malware was found.

When threat detections start repeating, it often means that Windows Defender just wasn’t up to the task of removing the malware that it detected. Sometimes you can complete the removal process with something as simple as running a scan with Windows Defender Offline. You can query for any additional actions that might be needed by running the PowerShell

https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/protect/forum/protect_defender-protect_scanning-windows_10/windows-defender-notification-saying-it-has-found/e40c5309-d2a1-437a-b205-75c6bc7e77ca

In many cases though, you’ll need to run a scan with a third-party malware-removal app in order to complete the malware-removal process. For example, many of the repeated detections that we’ve been seeing lately are for Trojan:HTML/Brocoiner!rfn. This Beeping Computer thread suggests that just scanning with Malwarebytes in Safe Mode might be sufficient to remove this threat:

https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/forums/t/665035/infected-with-trojan-htmlbrocoinerrfn/

Note the references to Rootkit.Fileless.MTGen in the detections; and also see this thread:

https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/forums/t/665497/rootkitfilelessmtgentrojanhtmlbrocoinerrfn/

If scanning with Malwarebytes doesn’t stop the notifications; then follow the steps in this removal guide, which can be applied to other threats as well as to Trojan:HTML/Brocoiner!rfn:

https://malwaretips.com/blogs/remove-trojan-html-brocoiner/ 

Of course there are some cases where repeated detections appear to be caused by remnants of previously installed or preinstalled AV apps; so you might need to use the removal utilities for any previously installed or preinstalled AV apps:

https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/protect/forum/mse-protect_start/list-of-anti-malware-product-removal-tools/2bcb53f7-7ab4-4ef9-ab3a-6aebfa322f75

In other cases repeating detections might be caused by file system corruption, and you might need to scan with the System File Checker and DISM:

https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/windows_10-update/system-file-check-sfc-scan-and-repair-system-files/bc609315-da1f-4775-812c-695b60477a93

Some cases also appear to be caused by defective definition updates; in which case you would simply have to wait for the issue to be resolved in a future update.

graytc718 say:

Microsoft My Phone Contact Download/Transfer

I have synced my contacts from my Imagio with the Microsoft My Phone website and I can view all of my contacts.  The question is that I have changed phones and want to download/transfer my contacts to my new phone...can this be done?  My new phone is the Motorola Droid.  I  have been up to verizon and they have told me that they cannot transfer my data from my Imagio to my Droid which I cannot understand.  I have also saved the pim file to my SD card and cannot seem to copy it to my Droid either...it is very frustrating and I would like some answers on this.  Thanks. 
julie fka profjulie say: If you have purchased another windows mobile phone, syncing your contacts to a new device would be very easy and straightforward.  But unfortunately for you, the My Phone service is only compatible with windows mobile phones and is not compatible with the Droid (which runs a different operating system). 

Verizon probably can't transfer the data from your old phone to your new phone because your contacts are not stored on the SIM card and they would need to copy the contacts to the SIM card before you could use them in your Droid (assuming you are using the same SIM card).  Some windows mobile devices include a utility that allows you to copy contacts from main memory to the SIM card, and so you might look over the applications that are in your Imagio to see if you've got an application like that.

The PIM.vol file could only be used on the device from which that it was copied.  It is not usually compatible with any other windows mobile device, and certainly won't be compatible with the Droid (which runs an entirely different operating system).

Probably your best bet is to see if you can sync your Contacts to Google Contacts (there are a couple of different ways to do this - I don't use Google Contacts, so I can't really tell you how to do it).  Once you sync your contacts to Google, then you should be able to find a way to sync them to the Droid. Another option, would be to sync your contacts to Outlook on your computer.  Once on the computer, you could probably find a way to sync them either to Google Contacts and then to the Droid or directly to the Droid.

I imagine this would be a frustrating experience for anyone who is moving away from windows mobile to another operating system such as the iPhone, or the Droid, since My Phone is only compatible with windows mobile phones.

Good Luck!
