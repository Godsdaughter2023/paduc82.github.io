Ah, that is a different problem than the bug fix just rolled out.  The bug fix just rolled out has to do with scheduling assistant showing others' availability shifted by an hour.  What you are seeing is the side effect of an iOS 8 bug.  The side effect manifests itself in Mac Outlook, but the root cause is that iOS is sending the wrong time zone to Exchange.

If you use an iOS 8 device with your Exchange account, please upgrade to iOS 8.2.  There were several bugs in iOS 8 that manifested themselves in Mac Outlook after syncing with Exchange, including meetings in “GMT” time zone (which is also UTC).  The calendar-related fixes in 8.2 are the following (according to Apple):

•    Fixes a timezone issue where Calendar events appear in GMT
•    Addresses an issue that caused certain events in a custom recurring meeting to drop from an Exchange calendar
•    Fixes a certificate error that prevented configuring an Exchange account behind a third-party gateway
•    Fixes an issue that could cause an organizer’s Exchange meeting notes to be overwritten
•    Resolves an issue that prevented some Calendar events from automatically showing as 'Busy' after accepting an invite

Note that upgrading to 8.2 will not correct any existing meetings; the organizer will have to send a meeting update.

Full release notes for 8.2 are here:
