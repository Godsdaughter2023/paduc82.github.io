mark ole say:

Hi Ryan,

This issue is typically more software and settings driven than hardware driven.

Here are few things that you can try to resolve the issue:

Check connections

  • Check that the cables connecting your Surface and the TV is plugged in securely. If the connection is secure but you still can’t hear sound, try using different cables.

Adjust the volume

  • Make sure that you've adjusted volume levels in the right places. You can control the volume in several places:
  • Press the volume button on your Surface to adjust the volume up or down. If your Surface Typing Cover or keyboard has volume keys, press the volume-down, volume-up, or mute key.

Restart your device

Restart your device while it is connected to the TV.

Get the latest updates

In many cases, downloading and installing the latest updates for Windows and your Surface will fix the issue. To install Surface and Windows updates, click on this link.

Check your default audio device settings

  1. Select the search box in the taskbar, enter control panel, and in the search results, select Control Panel.
  2. Select Hardware and Sound > Sound.
  3. Select the Playback tab, select the device you want to use for playback (for example, HDMI audio), and select Set Default..
  4. Select OK.

Run the Sound playback troubleshooter.

Download the Sound playback troubleshooter, and in the File Download dialog box, select Open, and follow the on-screen instructions.

Force shutdown your device

  1. Press and hold the power button for a full 10 seconds, until the screen turns off, then release the power button.

    Note: On some Surface models, the screen may immediately turn off when pressing the power button. If this happens, continue to hold for the full 10 seconds and then release.

  2. Press and release the power button to turn your Surface back on. You should see the Surface logo.

If still there is no sound, try restoring or resetting your Surface. For detailed how-to information, please refer to this link.
