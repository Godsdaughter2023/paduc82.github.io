huni shen msft say:

Hi G-r-e-g,

 

As described in this article, a pattern that requires less evidence has a lower match accuracy (or confidence level), while a pattern that requires more evidence has a higher match accuracy (or confidence level).

 

That is to say if you set Match Accuracy at any to 100, confidence level of the rule is high and hard to match. You can refer to Credit Card Number of the following article:

https://support.office.com/en-us/article/what-the-sensitive-information-types-look-for-fd505979-76be-4d9f-b459-abef3fc9e86b?ui=en-US&rs=en-US&ad=US

 

As the above article mentioned, Credit Card Number is defined by two patterns, a pattern with 65% confidence that requires:

  • A number in the format of a credit card number.
  • A number that passes the checksum.

 

A pattern with 85% confidence that requires:

  • A number in the format of a credit card number.
  • A number that passes the checksum.
  • A keyword or an expiration date in the right format.

 

Given this situation, I suggest you set the Match Accuracy at 65 and 65 to make the DLP policy easier to be matched and check if the issue persists.

 

Meanwhile, you can enable the incident reports by editing the DLP policy to notify you when a policy match occurs.

 

In DLP Policy settings > Edit rule > Scroll down to Incident reports > turn on the incident report

  2. Choose the reason you need your password reset, then click Next.
  3. Enter the email address, phone number or Skype ID you used when you made your Microsoft account. This could be any email address, or an email ending in a Microsoft domain like hotmail.com or outlook.com.
  4. Enter the characters you see on the screen (this lets us know you're not a robot), then click Next.
  5. If you have security info on your account, we'll send a one-time code to the alternate phone number or email address you gave us. After you enter that code on the next screen, you'll be able to make a new password.

  1. Go to the Password reset page.
  2. Select the reason you can't sign in, then click Next.
  3. Enter the email address, phone number, or Skype name that you used to create your Microsoft account.

bill manville say:
You should be able to put a formula to the right of your contracts pivot table to pick up the invoiced total for that contract from the other pivot table.  If there is a unique contract identifier "ContractName" in column B with the first item at B6 and the invoices pivot table is on sheet Invoices at A4, a formula something like this in say E6 and copied down might give what you need.
=GETPIVOTDATA("SumOfInvoiceValue",Invoices!$A$4,"ContractName",$B6)
