joeu2004 say:

This is a common mistake, often encouraged by some so-called experts.

"D" represents day of the month, not number of days.  And in Excel, the integer part of time represents a date serial number, which is the number of days since 12/31/1899.  Consequently, the "d" specifier is limited to 28, 29, 30 or 31, depending on the date that is represented by the integer part.

If you want to display dd:hh:mm:ss, you need to display text, not a number with a cell format.

Since your data is already broken down into separate columns -- A:D, say -- you can display the time as follows:

=TEXT(A2,"00") & ":" & TEXT(B2 & ":" & C2 & ":" & D2, "hh:mm:ss")

kohkarthiam say:

remove the number in call divert

Any help will be appreciated. Thanks in advance.

 

 

cjlim say:

Call divert is actually a network function. All your phone does is to facilitate this function by sending codes to your network. Have a look at this link. It lists the codes that are sent by the phone when setting up the call diverts. You also have the option of dialling the codes manually. Call divert settings can also be changed by contacting your network.

 

To change the divert number, you first deactivate the divert and you will be asked for the new number when reactivating. Alternatively just contact your network and ask them to do it for you. I find this simpler because there are just too many different types of diversion and it is easier to tell someone what you want then to try to figure out what each of them mean.
