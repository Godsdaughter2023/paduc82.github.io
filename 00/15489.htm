1. On your Inbox page, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.

2. Under "Preventing Junk email", click Safe and blocked senders.

3. Click Blocked senders.

4. In the Blocked senders list, select the name or domain (the part of an e-mail address that follows the @ sign, such as hotmail.com) that you now want to receive mail from, and then click<< Remove from list.

 

 

C. Add the email address to your Safe senders list

1. On your Inbox page, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.

2. Under "Preventing junk email", click Safe and blocked senders.

3. Click Safe Senders.

4.In the text box, type in the email address or domain you’d like to always receive email from, and then click Add to list >>.

 

 

D. Check your Email Rules

1. On your Inbox page, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.

2. Under "Customizing Hotmail", click Rules for sorting new messages.

3. Perform one of the actions as per your requirement:

Click Edit next to the rule that you want to edit.

Or

Note: Not all international characters are supported as filters.

1. On your Inbox page, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.

2. Under "Writing email", click Reply-to address.

3. Select Current e-mail address radio button.

4. Click Save.

Hello,

The error 80188306 means that the update was installed but cannot be opened. This is likely caused by the update files being corrupted or incomplete.

Since soft and hard resetting your Lumia 640 XL didn't resolve the issue, please use the Windows Device Recovery Tool. Do not forget to back up your stuff before using the said tool as this will restore your phone to its factory default state.

After restoring your phone, set it up without restoring from a back up and update it to Windows 10 Mobile operating system.

After successfully upgrading your phone to the said operating system, perform a hard reset. This will improve your overall experience and will delete the temporary files created in the process of the upgrade. You can then restore from a back up during the phone's set up process.

Important: Some applications will not be compatible with the mentioned operating system. Therefore restoring from a back up will likely show phantom apps that will be in the App list menu. It is recommended that you do a fresh start.

Have you tried deleting the phone from Windows Live and then adding it back?

To delete the phone from Windows Live:

  • Open up your Windows Live account and
  • Click on the Windows Live link in the upper left hand corner of the page,
  • Select "Devices," then
  • Select the Manage link under the phone > Change Windows Phone 7 Settings (under the Windows Phone 7 area) > Remove [phone name] from Windows Live

gr0mmet say:

You can use Windows Media Player to rip content if you really want the content to go to a different place than where you have the Zune software default to. It's actually a better tool to rip, since it lets you fix metadata before the rip.

Anyway...

In the Zune software, you can filter your view by content type. Click on "SHOW" above the first column. It'll flip between "ALL CONTENT", "DRM-FREE CONTENT", "PROTECTED CONTENT" & "ZUNE PASS". If you want to see what will be impacted if you bail on Zune, choose "PROTECTED CONTENT" and "ZUNE PASS".

For Zune purchases, it's basically all unprotected MP3 now, so DRM is not an issue going forward. For content you purchased before it was sold in MP3 form, you can burn it to Audio CD and lose the DRM as well.

If you happen to use Zune Pass content, which you don't own by design, they are already isolated in the "subscription" sub-directory.
