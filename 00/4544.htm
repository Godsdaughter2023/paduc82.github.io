No. Word does not insert hindi numerals (that is, numbers used in Arabic) when typed from a regular keyboard. It inserts the regular Arabic numerals (numbers used in English). HOWEVER, Word is the only Office app that have the power the change the shape (not the code) of the numeral to hindi. ·         Click Start

john - project say:
Courtney,
When I worked in industry our project's were under formal change control with a customer certified earned value system. All changes to the schedules, other than normal monthly status updating, could only be made after formal approval of a change request. These requests were tracked by the customer and our program business office.

Each month after the Cost Account Managers updated their files for status, the business office ran a series of macro based audit checks to detect any incorrect or missed update actions. To assist with this process I created a file compare macro that highlighted all changes from the previous month's file to the current file. The output was printed for a hardcopy audit trail. That was circa Project '97. Since then Microsoft added a Compare Versions utility to Project. The output is different from what my macro produces but nonetheless it would be a very useful tool to help you create an audit trail of change history.

As a simple first approach you could use the Notes field to annotate changes perhaps with a date stamp and authorization code (i.e. when was the change made and who/what authorized the change).
