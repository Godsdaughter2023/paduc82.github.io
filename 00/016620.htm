bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

My reply to Jim has absolutely no reflection on you :-) It's just that every point under discussion, including the title given to the message, pertains to Excel with no reference to Word, whatsoever. Word & Excel are designed in significantly different ways, so anything that applies to one very well may not apply to the other. Also, the majority of those who are most knowledgeable about Excel don't even look at questions in the Word forum.

Your issues are real based on the fundamental fact that compatible does not mean identical :-) Being able to open & revise the file on either platform supports compatibility, but how the document displays & prints is subject to a number of variables. Discrepancies such as what you describe can occur even on separate computers running the same OS.

For one thing, Column Width in Mac Excel has always been based on linear measurement... specifically, .9", whereas Column Width in PC Excel is based on number of characters (8.43) predicated on 64 pixels per inch. Why Microsoft chose to do that when they ported Excel to the Windows platform I have no idea, but it was done at a time when personal computers had nothing to work with but fixed width fonts. At that time, 8.43 characters may well have translated to .9"  but virtually all fonts used today are proportional rather than fixed width.

Another factor is that the Mac driver & the Windows driver for exactly the same printer are not always identical. It is not at all unusual for a document of any type to render differently when printed from a system using a different OS even if both systems are accessing the same network printer. Jim's suggestion to use the Any Printer option merely reverts to the built-in OS X print driver, eliminating influences that may be specific to the dedicated print drivers for individual printers.

For those as well as other reasons I believe you'd get better response directly geared to addressing your issues if the question were to the venue where those most familiar with Excel would be likely to see it.

By the same token, if you have any specific issues pertaining to Word you're certainly encouraged to post those questions in this forum.
