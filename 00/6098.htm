jay freedman say:

It isn't especially obvious, but the proper way to do this is to use the Mail Merge feature.

You can store the list of label texts in a single-column table in Word, in one column of an Excel worksheet, or even in a plain-text document (technically a .csv file, but just type each label on a single line with a "field name" on the top line). In the mail merge function, you select the product name of the paper labels you'll print on, and point to the document that contains the texts.

Whenever you have to update the names, you do that in the document that holds the data (called the "source") and save it, then rerun the merge.

I want to take the monthly salary A3 (1,000), divide by A1 (31) and multiply A2 (12).

joeu2004 say: Andy wrote;
=NETWORKDAYS(A1,EOMONTH(A1,0))
That seems to tell me the number of working days remaining in a month, so if it's on 31-01-13 it says 1.  I want to know how many working days are in the whole month.

As I noted, I assumed that A1 is the first date of the month; so 01-01-13, not 31-01-13.

To allow for A1 to be any date in the month, use:

=NETWORKDAYS(A1-DAY(A1)+1,EOMONTH(A1,0))

or

=NETWORKDAYS(DATE(YEAR(A1),MONTH(A1),1),EOMONTH(A1,0))

Andy wrote;
But better still not how many working days, but how many days in the month less Sundays.

See my previous response.  If you cannot use NETWORKDAYS.INTL, the following should work in A4 (prorated pay for work during the month):

=A2*A3/(NETWORKDAYS(A1-DAY(A1)+1,EOMONTH(A1,0),H1:H12)
+SUMPRODUCT(--(WEEKDAY(ROW(INDIRECT(A1-DAY(A1)+1 & ":" & EOMONTH(A1,0))))=7)))

where:
A1:          any date in the month worked
A2:          number of days worked (excluding Sundays and holidays)
A3:          monthly salary
H1:H12 :  any holidays during the year, notably 01-01-13 (Jan 1st)

May 2079 if you need Excel 2003 compatibility, due to the limit of 65536 rows.

Also, INDIRECT is a "volatile" function.  That means that formula and any other formula that refers to that cell is recalculated every time any cell in any worksheet in the workbook is edited.  That can bode ill if you have a lot of these formulas.  Alternatively:

=A2*A3/(NETWORKDAYS(A1-DAY(A1)+1,EOMONTH(A1,0),H1:H12)
+SUMPRODUCT(--(WEEKDAY(ROW(INDEX($A:$A,A1-DAY(A1)+1):INDEX($A:$A,EOMONTH(A1,0))))=7)))
