itzz pro donkey say:

Get a memory stick that will hold a 25MB file (and that you're happy to erase completely). Make sure it's formatted as 'NTFS' rather than the usual FAT. That threw me a little bit, 'cos I'd no idea what it meant. Anyway, it's fairly straightforward and lots of 'how to' vids online - I use a mac and had to download a free app from Paragon to enable this option via Disk Utility - if you use a PC, I think it's one of your default options. Then, on this xbox website, go to support.xbox.com/.../offline-system-update-diagnostic-tool and choose to download the 'restore factory defaults' file. Once downloaded, copy it onto your memory stick.

Next, unplug power from back of XB1 and wait 30 seconds. Plug power back in, insert your memory stick into one of the USB slots in back of XB1 and, while holding the BIND and EJECT buttons at the same time, press the POWER ON button on console (the white x!). Keeping holding the BIND and EJECT buttons for about 10 seconds until you hear a 'reboot' chime. Release the buttons and you should then hear ANOTHER reboot chime. Then just wait ... the console should spring into life - we got a thin green bar on the TV screen, showing progress. I think, after a while, the console restarted itself - anyway, the rest is all self-explanatory ... just follow the on-screen instructions. I really hope this works for you ...

By the way, the BIND button is the one 'around the corner' on the left side of the console (apologies if you knew that - I only mention it as I had no idea WTF it was!). Good luck...
