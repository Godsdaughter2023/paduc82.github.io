Visio Dimensioning - How to Meet SI Standard - Microsoft Community

dieselgenny say:

Visio Dimensioning - How to Meet SI Standard

When using the built-in dimensioning tools in Visio 2007, 2010 and 2013, there is no space between the measurement value and the units. This is OK for angular measurement units " used for minutes, ' used for seconds, and ° for degrees, but a space must be provided between the value and units for all other units, e.g. 300 mm is correct, but 300mm is incorrect. This is as specified in the SI Units Standard, and IEC/ISO standards, so we need to comply (or our customers are not happy). (See section 5.3.3 on the SI Standard, http://www.bipm.org/utils/common/pdf/si_brochure_8_en.pdf):

The numerical value always precedes the unit, and a space is always used to separate
the unit from the number. Thus the value of the quantity is the product of the number
and the unit, the space being regarded as a multiplication sign (just as a space
between units implies multiplication). The only exceptions to this rule are for the unit
symbols for degree, minute, and second for plane angle, °, ′, and ″, respectively, for
which no space is left between the numerical value and the unit symbol.

So, the question is, how can I use the built-in dimensioning shapes in Visio, and comply with the requirements of the standard?
Has anyone worked out a way yet?

Many thanks.
4 people found this helpful

dieselgenny say: I have worked out one way of doing this now.

You have to change one of the formulae for User-defined Cells in the Shape Sheet, for example, I Engineering Dimensions, Horizontal dimension shape,

Change "User.FormatString" from
"=User.LPFString&IF(User.LUnitsInd=2,"\'",IF(User.ShowUnitsInd=1,"u",""))"
to [add in a space where I've made it bold].
"=User.LPFString&" "&IF(User.LUnitsInd=2,"\'",IF(User.ShowUnitsInd=1,"u",""))"


I've not delved into the formulae in "User.FeetInch" yet to make th change there, as we are working in metric it's not an immediate problem.

Could I suggest Microsoft correct the built-in shapes in the future, to present in the internationally-accepted standard for presentation of units?

Many thanks.

dieselgenny say: Just to note, that in the Size & Position window, and other interactive boxes in Visio, the units are presented as per the SI standard, i.e. with a space, so it's a mystery why the built-in dimensioning stencils should be different?

  • Image backgrounds often consist of pixels that are not all the same color even though the background appears to be a 'solid color'. Only 1 color can be made transparent so those of a color other than the one you click will remain, &
  • All image pixels of the clicked color will be made transparent even if they are not in the background are. That can leave the image looking like a slice of Swiss cheese.

Best advice IMO - Edit images with software designed for the purpose, then insert the finished product into the document of whatever type it happens to be.
