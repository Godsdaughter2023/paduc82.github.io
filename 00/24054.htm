nwcgrad say:

Thanks, Hans, but it's not quite that simple.  The default placement of the numbers is not satisfactory, and if the document contains more than one table, numbers do not automatically restart at '1'.  After considerable trial and error I found a way to accomplish what I wanted as follows:

To number rows of a table

·      Select the left-most column (exclude the top cell if it's part of the header row).

·      Click the Numbering button in the Paragraph group of the Home tab.

·      Sequential numbers will appear in the selected cells, but if the column is narrow they may be squeezed way right and barely visible.

·      If so, click the down arrow (t) on the Numbering button and select Define New Number Format…

·      Alignment will be set as 'Left' by default; change it to 'Right' and numbers will then be properly placed in each cell.

·      If the document contains additional tables, numbering their rows will continue the number sequence started in the first table.  To correct this, click the AutoCorrectOptions button that appears next to the first cell of the new table and select Restart Numbering.

If you're using Office 2016 (whether part of Office 365 or standalone), you should not have Office 2010 Starter Edition on your computer. To uninstall it:

  • Right-click the Windows Start button.
  • In the menu, click "Programs and Features" or "Apps and Features" , whichever appears. (This depends in whether you've been blessed with the Creators Update of Windows 10.)
  • If "Programs and Features" was clicked, scroll down and right-click the Microsoft Office 2010 Starter Edition; click Uninstall, and follow the instructions. When the uninstall is complete, right-click the Microsoft Office 2016 (or 365), click Change, and run the Quick Repair.

After uninstalling Office 2010 and repairing Office 2016, you may need to repeat the setting of the margins for new blank documents.
