rena yu msft say:

Hi Wilfried,

Based on your descriptions, I have three possible suggestions for your scenario:

Option1: Enabling the Teacher-Only section group. The Teacher-Only section group is a private space where only the teacher can see what’s inside. To add a Teacher Only section group to your Class Notebook, go to Manage notebooks page then click Enable Teacher-Only section group. Other teachers that have access to your Class Notebook will also have access to the Teacher-Only section group, but students cannot see anything here. In this way, you can put all the students’ forms there.

Option2: Put all the students’ forms in the Content library - a notebook for teachers to share course materials with students. Teachers can add and edit its materials, but for students, the notebook is read-only.

Option3: Add individual forms to any pages or sections to the individual student notebooks. You can go to each student’s specific section > click Insert tab > Forms > New Form. You can create a performance form, then go back to Insert tab > Forms > select the form. When you create a new form for the next student, just go to click the Insert tab > Forms > New form > select a previous form then click the … button to copy the form so that you don’t need to create it again and again for other students. The student can also response to the form but it will not affect your response. You can go to the form > responses to check your own response.

System.PrivateProfileString is almost certainly calling an underlying WinAPI to do the work.

The Windows API will not be available on Mac.

You can use SaveSetting and GetSetting however:

Call SaveSetting("My_App","ThisSection", "ThisKey", "This is the string to save")

Debug.Print GetSetting ("My_App","ThisSection", "ThisKey", "Default string")

This saves settings to Macintosh HD:Users: <username> :Library:Preferences:My_App.plist

The plist file is XML format.
