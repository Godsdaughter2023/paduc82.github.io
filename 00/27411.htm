steve rindsberg say:

You've run into the wall that divides "How things USUALLY are" from "How things can sometimes be"

A screen full of cells would be almost entirely made up of "runs" of identically colored pixels, which'd make it extremely compressible as a raster image (ie, your PNG).  PNG compression is far smarter than simple run length compression, and might be clever enough to, in effect, recognize that your spreadsheet is just one (highly compressed) block of pixels repeated over and over.  

The same data would be represented in vector form as .. well ungroup that puppy a couple of times after pasting it into PPT to see how many line segments and other bits of stuff it turns into.  For a cell range like A1:P30 empty but for text in the upper left and lower right corners of the range, I get something like 98 or 100 shapes.  Apply borders to all the cells and that more than doubles.
