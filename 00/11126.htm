macropod say:
According to the APA 6 referencing standard (http://blog.apastyle.org/apastyle/2011/11/the-proper-use-of-et-al-in-apa-style.html), all authors are included in citations of 1 - 5 authors; for 6 or more authors only the first, then et al. All authors are listed in the bibliography, regardless of how they appear in the citations. That is also how Word processes them, so there is no problem that I can see.

jacques raubenheimer say:

The Microsoft implementation of this leaves much to be desired. At present, it cannot add the alphabetic suffix, and I am afraid that, with all due respect to Paul, his suggestion of adding them by hand is not really practical, considering the vagaries of academic publishing.

Any decent Reference Management Software (e.g., www.mendeley.com, www.endnote.com, etc, etc) can do that.

Having said that, BibWord (see http://www.codeplex.com/bibword) is a brilliant little tool that greatly improves what Word can do, and the BibWord extender can add the suffix when the document is done.

That is correct. As I said earlier, you're working with Microsoft's interpretation of the APA referencing standard. So far, I haven't found a single referencing standard that Microsoft has implemented correctly and, given how long the problem has been there, there doesn't seem to be any commitment on Microsoft's part to doing anything about this.

If you're stuck with Word's tools, you could, for example, add the a/b/c notations to dates (where required) via the Create/Edit Source dialog and, during your editing, simply accept that this will impact the bibliography as well. Then, when you're done editing, you can convert the citations with:

• a/b/c notations; and

• errant author references for the second & subsequent citations of 3 or more authors in the text,

to text. You can then:

• correct the date notations via the Edit Source dialog (so they'll no longer appear that way in the bibliography); and

• replace the extraneous author references for the second & subsequent citations of 3 or more authors with 'et al'.
