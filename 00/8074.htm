How do I get rid of the green/red underline on my text in - Microsoft Community

chasej.t.v say:

How do I get rid of the green/red underline on my text in Microsoft Office:Mac 2011 ?

On my "Objective" in my Resume my paragraph is all underlined in green. Here is the paragraph used; " To obtain permanent full-time employment and become a member of a team to learn all the facets of the position."

How do I eliminate this issue? 

Thanks
daniel g. grau say:
A red squiggly underline indicates spelling errors, while a green one shows grammar errors (or what Word considers to be grammar and spelling errors). They are controlled as follows:
  • In the main menu bar, choose Word > Preferences
  • Click on the third icon, Spelling and Grammar
  • In the Spelling section, disable Check spelling as you type
  • In the Grammar section, disable Check grammar as you type
  • If you don't want Word to grammatically analyze your text when you spell check it, disable Check grammar with spelling
If you are getting lots of errors, then the applied language is wrong. Highlight the text in question, then select Tools > Language and apply the proper language. There is a checkmark there that makes Word ignore that text when checking.

Note that language is not a global setting but a language attribute of each part of the text, much like a formatting attribute (bold, underline, etc.). Thus, you need to make sure that the language attribute of the text is as you need it. Easiest thing: Select All content, then apply the proper language.

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
I really see no need to change anything about the settings of the feature. Instead, correct the error it has identified... Right-Click in the underlined text & you'll see what that error is.

That particular 'paragraph' is marked as grammatically incorrect because it is incorrect... It is a Fragment -- an incomplete sentence -- which lacks a Subject, but you've ended it with a period. Remove the period (.) at the end of what actually is a phrase & the green markup will go away of its own accord.

Alternatively, you could make it a complete sentence by adding a Subject, such as "My objective is..." or "I would like...", to the existing Predicate. That will rid it of the green markup as well.

BTW -- I interjected this because although what Daniel suggested will eliminate the appearance of the markup in your copy of Word, it may very well appear when the document is viewed in another user's copy... IOW, turning off the automatic features doesn't correct the mistakes, it simply makes you less aware of them.
