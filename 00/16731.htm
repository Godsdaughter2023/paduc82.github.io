Form_type Customer_Name

Beth K. Connors

 

Dates are stored as the number of days since a base date.  so today is  40465 days from the last day of 1899.  Since you are entering a number 123199, excel sees it as a number.  When you format the cell as date, it calculates that data as  4/21/2237.     you number is not a recognized date format.  Excel has no way of knowing that it is a date in mmddyy format.  It just sees it as a number.  You would have to enter something as a recognizable date   12-31-99  for example (use any recognized date format).  If you enter that with your cell formatted as m/d/yy then it will display as you want.   Chip Pearson has written a macro that will allow you to enter the number as you show and it will parse out the parts and make it into a date serial. 

http://www.cpearson.com/Excel/DateTimeEntry.htm

you can also fine out more about dates and times at his site:
