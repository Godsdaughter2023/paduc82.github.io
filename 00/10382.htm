a. user say:

Hi MSITGEEK,

 

No, hybrid migration is not the only option, the article I provided above doesn't use hybrid migration. To help you better understand the differences between these, I will explain more: For hybrid migration, you can imagine it like using an Exchange on-premises server as a bridge ---- after it is setting up, emails can be routed to on premises servers, which enables you to cut off the online tenant with a low mail flow impact. Then you will gradually migrate your on-premises mailboxes to targeted Office 365 tenant.

 

The advantage of the whole process is it has a low impact to your mail flow, and on the contrary, as you can see in the article I provided, after changing the MX records of your domain, it may have a down time to restore according to TTL value and DNS transition time.

 

But with hybrid migration, it may have additional fees if you doesn't have Exchange server before, for example, you will need to buy licenses for using this service. And you can see the advantage of another method now ------ it may have lesser cost than hybrid migration and it is easier to perform, without the steps to move your mailboxes between on-premises and cloud.

 

So it depends on you which migration method you want to use, if you don't have a high demand for interrupt of mail flow, the method above seems a better option for you, otherwise you can choose hybrid migration.

 

Regarding the AD management option, what I suggest is using the method in the above article if you are not familiar with separate management, or you can use soft match to separately manage the two ADs.

 

Besides, please check Vincent's reply about carefully evaluate your services. Hopefully you can get your work done without encountering any issues during the process.

macropod say:

If the file is on a USB drive, the most likely reason for the corruption is that the drive was unplugged without going through the 'safe removal' process. Such files are typically unrecoverable. Your only recourse is to restore it from your last known good backup. Otherwise, you might try:

• setting Word's 'recover text from any file' option to try to recover the text (no formatting/pictures); and/or

rohn007 say:

Not directly.  Onetime payment licenses like Pro Plus are OS, Windows or Mac, specific.

So no, you cannot install your Windows Pro Plus onto a Mac.

But, there is a workaround. Actually, 2 (or more).

First is to simply run a "VM" (virtual machine / virtual computer) like "Bootcamp" or "Parallels" that will allow you to install Windows INSIDE of Mac. Then you can install your current license in the new VM.

The other is to setup your computer as a "dual Boot", Mac or Windows.  Up until a few years ago Macs ran on totally different hardware, hardware that could not run Windows directly.  Then Mac changed. Mac now runs on the same Intel CPU's that Windows does. There are a few specific hardware limits / requirements to run Mac, but none of them exclude Windows.  So what you can do is "partition" your hard drive to free up space. Then install Windows in a separate partition.  After that, when you boot the computer you would have the option of running Mac or Windows. I have actually seen it said that Apple now makes a better windows computer than MS.

The VM approach makes it simpler to switch between Mac apps and Windows apps.

Your third option would be to buy another HUP license, specific for Mac. The rule used to be you could buy a copy once a year. I haven't checked recently if that still applies. Frankly, that would be the best option, the $15 or so price is trivial for a full Office installation. You will probably have to scroll way down to the bottom of the HUP page to find the one-time payment license options.

Perform a Scan Disk maintenance on your computer by doing the following:

  1. Turn off your phone and remove the micro SD memory card. Use an SD card reader and plug it in to your computer.

  3. Right-click on the micro SD memory card drive that you want to check, and then click Properties.
