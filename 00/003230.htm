SUMIF By Dates - Microsoft Community

ronaldornal say:

SUMIF By Dates

I have multiple tabs on my spreadsheet.  All tabs are credit cards that have several transactions on each of them.  I tried the sumif function to add up the amount I spend by year, it always gives me the results 0.  I have multiple years on multiple tabs and one analysis tab to consolidated all the information by date and by monthly expense.  How do I make a formula to add every tabs expenses by year or month?

 

To make things easier, each tab is in the same format whereas expenses are always in the same column.

 

Additionally, I would like the formula to take into account refunds on the credit cards.

 

My format for all tabs.

 

A1: Date

B1: Transaction

C1: Empty

D1: Purchases/Expenses

E1: Refunds/Payments

F1: Balance

 

Thanks in advance for the help.

jllatham say:

To begin with, to make it easy on yourself and your formulas, I would add 2 more columns to each card sheet:  one to hold just the year and one to hold just the month.  The formulas for them are easy:

=YEAR(date)  where 'date' is the cell in column A on the row, as =YEAR(A2) in row 2, and

=MONTH(date), as =MONTH(A2) in row 2.

Lets say they went into columns G and H respectively, so those sheets now look like this - a simulated entry on a row with | to separate columns:

12/15/12 | Meals | [empty] | 18.35 | 0 | 18.35 |  2012  |  12

 

On your summary sheet, you could have this setup

          A                          B                           C                     D

1  Category                YEAR                     MONTH         TOTAL

2    MEALS

3    FUEL

 

You'd enter the YEAR you want to see results for in column B and if you want it by month, enter the month number into column C.

Now, for just one card you would have a formula like this out in D on a row.  For this example, the name of the card sheet is AMEX.  In cell D2 on the summary sheet:

=SUMIFS(AMEX!D:D,AMEX!B:B,A2,AMEX!G:G,B2,AMEX!H:H,C2)-SUMIFS(AMEX!E:E,AMEX!B:B,A2,AMEX!G:G,B2,AMEX!H:H,C2)

 

That formula takes into consideration category, year and month.  If you want a total for the category for the entire year then:

=SUMIFS(AMEX!D:D,AMEX!B:B,A2,AMEX!G:G,B2)-SUMIFS(AMEX!E:E,AMEX!B:B,A2,AMEX!G:G,B2)

 

Now, to see all of your cards activities for a specific category, then you need to just add more of the same formula into the cell, just changing the card sheet name.  Here is the first one expanded to include a sheet named VISA and a sheet named MC:

=SUMIFS(AMEX!D:D,AMEX!B:B,A2,AMEX!G:G,B2,AMEX!H:H,C2)-SUMIFS(AMEX!E:E,AMEX!B:B,A2,AMEX!G:G,B2,AMEX!H:H,C2)+SUMIFS(VISA!D:D,VISA!B:B,A2,VISA!G:G,B2,VISA!H:H,C2)-SUMIFS(VISA!E:E,VISA!B:B,A2,VISA!G:G,B2,VISA!H:H,C2)+SUMIFS(MC!D:D,MC!B:B,A2,MC!G:G,B2,MC!H:H,C2)-SUMIFS(MC!E:E,MC!B:B,A2,MC!G:G,B2,MC!H:H,C2)

 

I changed the format of each "pair" of card formulas so you can see the setup for each sheet in that long formula a little easier.

 

Hope this helps you with your effort.

 

I just uploaded a simple sample file to my MediaFire page, you should be able to download it with this link:  http://www.mediafire.com/view/?cs5y6i9qir0hl25

Filename is for_RonaldOrnald_12152012.xlsx and it's about 13K in size (sorry about misspelling your last name).

In our environment, we have also observed different behavior based on the sender of the file. When it is an internal sender, Powerpoint opens up the PPS or PPSX in Edit Mode as opposed to the somewhat expected Show Mode.

At the same time, I was also trying to figure out why some Word attachments were opening up in Protected Mode and others would not. Turns out that Outlook has a GPO setting that defines whether an attachment should be opened in Protected Mode if it is received from an internal sender. The default apparently is to not use Protected Mode when the attachment is from an internal sender. I configured this GPO to use Protected Mode all the time, and all our Word attachments now use Protected Mode.

But this setting also fixed the opening of PPS and PPSX files! When set to Configured, double clicking on PPS and PPSX files now open them up in Show mode.

The GPO setting I'm talking about is under User Configuration\Administrative Template\Microsoft Outlook 2010\Security and is called "Use Protected View for attachments received from internal senders".

Hope this helps.
