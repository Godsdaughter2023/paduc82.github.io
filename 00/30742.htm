a blue illusion say:

After the retailer purchases the console (or any product, for that matter) from the manufacturer, 'store policy' determines what happens to display models. 

Having worked for and dealt with several major retailers myself, I can tell you that pretty much every retail establishment has a policy for display merchandise and very few, if any of these policies allow employees 'special access' or perks of any kind when it comes to displays.

Most policies are very clear and are typically similar to the last place I worked for; Any merchandise designated as "display" must remain on display until the product is either discontinued, or replaced by a newer model and may not be used for any other purpose. Display models may not be sold unless the product has been discontinued and all other store inventory has first been depleted. Management determines the amount of discount for said display product. Any display products that are damaged, missing parts, or worn out are to be processed as a return.

Generally, if something 'iffy' is going on, management would eventually find out... or be involved... especially with something as expensive as a gaming console, since inventory would be closely tracked and serial numbers would be logged. Not saying something iffy can't, or doesn't happen. Just saying that with more costly items like consoles, if management isn't involved or aware... it's a very high probability they will be eventually. Of course what, if anything, happens if something iffy is going on would be up to management and how good the 'story' they're told is.

cmdrkeene say:

In Windows 10 this is easy, there's an entire touchpad section in the Settings app.

Unfortunately you marked that you are using Windows Vista.  That means it's more than 15 years old, and doesn't have easy settings for these things.  In fact Windows Vista (and even later, Windows 7) have no touchpad settings built-in at all.  You only get options if your computer builder pre-installed them with your touchpad driver.  If this is the case, it's typically made easy to access direct from the Mouse control panel.  So to check, try this:

1)  Hold down Windows logo and type R to show the Run box

2)  Type (or copy and paste) the command main.cpl and click OK

3)  Look for a touchpad or hardware tab on the dialog, and for a way to access touchpad settings.

Still, some laptop makers weren't so great a decade or more ago, so back in the Windows Vista days there were many laptops that could only adjust the touchpad options by using a special extra program that only the laptop builder could provide. Lenovo and Toshiba are very popular among those that did this.

**Solved - Windows 10 bluetooth Stereo no sound after playing - Microsoft Community

shaker2s5 say:

**Solved - Windows 10 bluetooth Stereo no sound after playing video in full screen.

This problem has been solved, but I live it here in case anyone else would have similar issue. Find the solution on the bottom.

I connect my bluetooth headphones to the PC using my bluetooth dongle. It is connected, there is sound everything works fine.

The issue

However, every time when I click on play video in full screen immediately or after a few seconds the sound stops completely in the whole system and even if I exit the full screen it won't make any sound whatsoever. This is the Stereo device that does it. If I swap to Hands-free everything makes sound fine, but then I swap back to Stereo nothing will make sound.

Workaround

To reset the sound in the Stereo device, I can go into bluetooth settings, disconnect from my headphones, then reconnect. After that there is still no sound, so I have to swap to the Hands-free device and then back to the Stereo device. Now it makes sound properly, of course until I open a video in full screen again.

Additional Information

It has worked before, I am just after a clean install of Windows 10. Since then it worked fine for 1-2 days. I have installed Unity, Blender, Visual Studio, Steam and Origin since then. Also a couple of games from Steam.

My headphones: JBL E55BT

Things I have tried:

  • Windows troubleshoot sound
  • Disable and re-enable headphones in device manager
  • Uninstall, then re-install headphone drivers
  • Using other browser to watch video
  • Using another website to watch video
  • Playing downloaded video in full screen (it works fine)

I don't think it has anything to do with the bluetooth dongle or the headphones, since they do work in Hands-free mode.

EDIT: I have also noticed that using the volume buttons on my headphones no longer adjusts the volume in Windows, it rather acts as it has its own volume control. Before if I increased the volume using the button found on the headphones, it increased the volume in Windows.

Solution: I have an AMD graphics card that comes with Radeon ReLive which is a screen recording software. Whenever I entered full screen it turned on. For some reason it caused trouble with my bluetooth headphones stereo. I just disabled the feature and then everything worked fine.
