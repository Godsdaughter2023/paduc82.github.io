Hi,

Thank you for bringing this to our attention.

Allow me to clarify the gray areas that you have mentioned. Verified additional domains in Office 365 should automatically be associated with your Yammer network. Previously, this can only be done by sending a support ticket. Due to recent changes, this has been an automatic process. As long as the system verified that all domains are "managed in Office 365", domain sync will kick in and associate all verified domains to Yammer. More information about this can be found here: https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Manage-Yammer-domains-across-their-lifecycle-in-Office-365-9d9f9ee8-7c56-4f50-81f3-aa0a8d761e14

Although associated, you can still control who can/can't sign in to Yammer by using Enforce Office 365 Identity. I think you are somehow familiar with this process already.

In terms of activating Yammer, there are many things that will happen once this has been completed. For one, all Office 365 global admins should be migrated in Yammer as a verified admin. A verified admin should be able to manage the entire network properly. More information about this is here: https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/administration-and-security-features-in-yammer.aspx.

Lastly, SSO should continue to work. However, be reminded that it will soon be deprecated. This is the reason we now have Office 365 sign in for Yammer which will be pushed to the network if Enforce Office 365 Identity is activated.
