Hello Darren:

 

Thank you for posting to the forum regarding the issues you are having with Outlook Exchange and office 365.  If I understand you correctly you want to connect your local Outlook account to an account that will bring emails, calendar and contacts to your phone.  You currently do this with your Hotmail account via the Outlook Hotmail connector.  You would like to instead connect this local Outlook account to your Outlook Exchange account or Office 365 accounts.

 

From the sounds of it you will not be able to get this to fully work.  The behavior you describe, sync will not complete, typically indicates there is an issue with the sync relationship.  This may be due to incompatibility or simply formatting issues.  There are a couple things I would suggest you try to see if you have any chance of getting this set up as you would like.

  • You should be able to break this stuck sync without resetting the phone.  The best way to do that would be to turn off both cellular data and Wi-Fi, then restart the phone.  This lack of a connection should lead to the sync being broken as there is no connection for the sync to complete.  You can make these settings adjustments by going to the phone and tapping settings > Wi-Fi > OFF and then settings > cellular/mobile > OFF.  Once you have done this, restart your phone and go to the secondary account, the Office 365 or Exchange account.
  • Now that the account is no longer stuck in sync, you will want to see if this issue is related to one specific type of information.  Turn back on one or both of your connections.  When you go to settings > emails + accounts > tap the Exchange or Office account > Uncheck both the calendar and contacts boxes.  Once this is done, tap and hold the account and select sync.  If the sync completes without error, the issue you are having is specific to calendar entries or contact listings.  Go back to the account settings and check one at a time and then sync.  See if you can determine which feature leads to the issue syncing.
  • If you cannot get the sync to complete this is most likely and issue with the two types of accounts being compatible.  From here you can seek additional assistance from Office 365 and Outlook support to see if there are any settings that can be adjusted or you can continue to use the Hotmail Outlook connector.

> So Google can't be made to "see" the data file?

Of course not. Google's web server can't see what's on your PC any more than I can.

Do you mean the Outlook for Android app on a tablet or phone? No for that as well. If you want to share things between devices, you have to connect those devices all to the same mailbox online with protocols that can sync in both directions. Connect all your devices to a single mailbox using a protocol that syncs both ways and understands calendars. IMAP isn't one of those protocols and Google Mail isn't one of those mailboxes unless you pay for the privilege. You can also buy Hosted Exchange service from other mail providers that will allow you to do what you want. One caveat, though: local folders will never sync unless your mobile device manufacturer provides a program that will perform the syncing or you get a sync program like gSyncit.

keeping that in mind, the only way you can use wifi on the phone would be:-
1. Install the mentioned app on your computer
2. Install a bluetooth dongle on the computer
3. Connect the computer to internet using WLAN
4. Use the wifi-bluetooth app to make your computer 'broadcast' the internet over bluetooth
5. Use your phone's bluetooth to hook up to the internet

Now take a look again at points 1 and 2.
A hotspot administrator won't let you install software on their computer. You cannot ask McDonalds to let you install a software on their computer. They won't you.
You cannot install hardware on a hotspot computer. You cannot ask McDonalds to let you attach a bluetooth dongle on their computer, the admin won't let you.
