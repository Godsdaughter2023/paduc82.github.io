roady [mvp] say:

Yes the mailing list should be revised because of errors, but what i find weird is outlook wend sending the 100 plus emails, cant tell me witch  ones are wrong and ony tell me a comom error like the one i show you.

The error isn't coming from Outlook but from the mail server. Outlook will allow you to send emails to wrong or faulty email addresses as long as you are using valid characters. Creating an email addressed to $@&.com is valid but it will bounce because the address doesn't exist. That is up to the mail server to figure out and not Outlook.

Some mail servers have restrictions in which characters they allow because they either don't support the character set or find it (currently) obvious that the domain or address doesn't exist and bounce it directly without attempting to deliver it or not even accept the email submission from the client. That (being stricter than the standards and/or mail client) is up to the mail server as well and this is something the client can't possibly know before it attempts to deliver it to the mail server.

When you use any character which isn't supported by Outlook, you will see that the address won't be underlined and Outlook will throw an error upon sending (File-> Options-> Mail-> Send messages-> Automatic name checking) or when you press CTRL+K as mentioned in my initial reply to you.

When you know that your mailing list generates email addresses with specific characters that you know are wrong for your clients, then you could either create a delay send rule or use a VBA solution to check your addresses upon sending if you don't want to rely on the bounce of your mail server.

How weird it may seem and how problematic it sometimes can be in praxis, there really is little restriction in what can be used in an email address or domain registration and the few restrictions which currently exist are getting even fewer. For a lot of people, it won't be easy to type a Greek or Chinese address either but it is allowed, just like many other special characters on your keyboard, and this is why Outlook won't produce an error.
