Let us try the following methods and check.
Method 1.
If your computer is having problems with a recently installed device or other hardware, try using the Hardware and Devices troubleshooter to fix the problem. It checks for common issues and makes sure that any new device or hardware attached to your computer was installed correctly.
Refr to the following link for steps:
http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows7/Open-the-Hardware-and-Devices-troubleshooter
 
Method 2. 
You can try uninstalling the drivers for the DVD drive and reinstalling the drivers.
If you are using any third party DVD and CD writing software like Nero, uninstall it and then try.
 
Uninstall and reinstall the drivers for DVD drive.:
1. Go to Start and right-click on Computers and select Manage. This will bring the Computer Management window, now on the left sidebar select Device Manager.
2. Then go to DVD/CD - ROM Drives click on it's name it will show the model numbar and the manufacturer and right click on it and click uninstall.
   To reinstall the drivers right click on DVD/CD - ROM Drives name and finally click Update Driver Software
3. You have two options here, click the option that reads "search automatically for updated driver software".
4. You can manually download the drivers from the manufacturers website.
http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows7/Update-a-driver-for-hardware-that-isnt-working-properly
 
Method 3.
Try burning the ISO again, you can try using the Microsoft Store ISO burning tool (which I used, works really well) located here:
http://store.microsoft.com/Help/ISO-Tool
 
For IDE controller drivers I would recommend you to contact your manufacturer for updated drivers.
