The download process of Outlook for Android is not really downloading the file completely. As you can noticed that it's under the .tmp folder which stands for Temporary folder. This is to avoid eating up storage for your phone.

I have tried to replicate your issue and I found a workaround.

  1. Download the attachment from your email and open the file.
  2. Click the hamburger logo and select Save as. Then save it on your desired location on your SD Card.

That way it's not being deleted since it's not stored on the temporary folder.

jay freedman say:

You can install this macro in your Normal template and assign a shortcut key or a button to it. It will save the active document as a plain text file without warnings, even if the document does contain features that will be discarded. I think it will work in all versions of Word from Word 97 onward; I've tested in Word 2003 and 2010.

Sub SaveAsTxt()
   With Dialogs(wdDialogFileSaveAs)
      .Format = wdFormatDOSText
      If ActiveDocument.Path = "" Then
         .Show
      Else
         .Execute
      End If
   End With
End Sub

Hi excel gurus

If I have a defined range called "MCH" being 6 columns and 5000 rows and I'd like to use a HLOOKUP to confirm the headings in row 1 and then use a count formula to count entries under that particular heading...e.g. COUNTIF(MCH,HLOOKUP(J10,MCH,1,FALSE)) where J10 = "Phone" and there are actually 73 entries under the heading of Phone but the result I get is 1. jeeped say:
It's not altogether clear what the values in the Phone column are; numbers or text or "Phone". If they are numbers then try,

=COUNT(INDEX(MCH,,MATCH(J10,INDEX(MCH,1,),0)))

For numbers or text then,

=COUNTA(INDEX(MCH,,MATCH(J10,INDEX(MCH,1,),0)))-1

The -1 is to discount Phone in the header. If you want to count text values of "Phone" then,

=COUNTIF(INDEX(MCH,,MATCH(J10,INDEX(MCH,1,),0)),J10)-1

... again with -1 to discount the header label.
