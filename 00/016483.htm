Edit Pre-Printed Certificates - Microsoft Community

a. user say:

Edit Pre-Printed Certificates

I have a stack of printed, pre-designed certificates that are blank in three areas: Awardee's name, Date, and a place for my signature. 

These blank certificates were printed already on special paper to my liking with particular style design and perforations, etc. 

Now my question is: how do I edit these papers using most likely Microsoft Publisher to add names, different dates, and my signature without having to re-do the designs? I just want to insert the stack of papers into my printer and have the names, date, and signature be churned out in their proper places for each different certificate.

davidf2 say:

Old school approach:

Create a blank page the same size as your certificates. Then do a rough measurement with a ruler on your certificate as to where the blanks are on the page and create text boxes at those locations on the blank page. Fill those text boxes with sample names, dates and signatures and print the page on blank plain paper. Now hold it up against the certificate to see how things line up. Move the text boxes and try printing again until everything lines up. Then try printing on an actual certificate to make sure it works, and if it does save it as your template.

If you have a lot of these to print you could probably use a mail merge for the data, but that is another question.
