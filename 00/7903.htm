Suzanne's suggestion is probably more likely, especially if the document is in fact in Word 97–2003 (*.doc) format.

Hello Jacques,

I just saw this, and I can explain it. In 2013 we went to a more secure algorithm across Office - not just in Excel. Excel 2013 uses SHA-512. rm6262 say:

why do i need to buy outlook to sync phone

why do we need to buy a $150.00 program to sync a phone with computer? is there a cheaper way? not all of us are made of money

julie fka profjulie say: If you want to sync a windows mobile phone to your computer, the software you'd use is Activesync or Windows Mobile Device Center.  These applications are only designed to sync calendar, contact, task data to Outlook.  If you don't have Outlook installed on the computer you can't use ActiveSync or Windows Mobile Device Center to sync this data to your computer since there is no software on the computer to receive it.

You might be able to find other third party syncing software that lets you sync to a proprietary calendar, but you'd have to buy that too and I don't know if such software really exists.

If you've got a windows mobile 6.x device, you can sync contacts and tasks to a windows live account.

sylvie liang msft say:

Hello ASD11_111,

The Teams guest access features can be controlled by your organization administrator to achieve different organization policies. Therefore, if you are not able to add/search guest in your Teams, please kindly contact your admin to check if the guest access for your organization is enabled in each authorization level.

For your reference: Authorize guest access in Microsoft Teams

Meanwhile, the guest access feature should be enabled for both organizations. Please also confirm with the recipient side (the organization you want to add) to check their settings.

After doing the above steps, then you can try to adding guests in that organization to your team to check the results.

I hope this can help. Please let me know if you need any further assistance.
