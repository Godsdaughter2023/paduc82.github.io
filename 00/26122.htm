lawrencepohlen say:

My old computer crashed, hard drive dead, how do I load my Microsoft office on to my new computer?

How do I load my version on new computer, can't uninstall on old computer as it is dead?
rohn007 say:
To install Office 2010 you will need your unique 25 character product key and the associated generic installation file / disk.

You don't have to worry about uninstalling it from the old machine. The uninstall requirement is just to ensure you don't continue to use it. A "dead" machine meets the requirement. Actually, the uninstall process does not communicate with the activation system, so they don't know right away that you've uninstalled.

Put the installation disk in the new machine, or copy the installation file to it. Run the installation process and activate with your product key.

Note: your product key must be a "retail"/"FPP" / 2-computer or 3-computer online download to allow you to transfer the installation to a new device.

If you have a factory installed and activated "OEM" license, or a 1-computer or PKC online download, you cannot transfer / install on a new device. Those licenses are trivially cheaper to purchase because they are "disposable". They are permanently tied to the first device they are activated on.
