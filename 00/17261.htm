Sorry, there isn't any way I've found to add new effect combinations to the gallery. You can workaround this in a backwards way, though:

  3. Right-click on the picture and choose Change Picture.
  4. Select the picture and click on OK. The new picture takes on the same formatting effects.

Well, I see one thing Wrong. (I hope you don't have many files on this Drive). You are using a Drive formatted for Windows on a Mac. Although Apple used to claim that you could read and write to any Drive whether formatted for Mac or PC, there were only certain types of  PC formats it would accept. If my memory serves me correctly, it had to be at least FAT32. And I am not sure at the time NTFS was even allowed.

My suggestion is depending on whether your computer uses a USB2 or USB 3 Connection. Purchase a  new drive. A mechanical hard drive at least 500 GB or 1 TB you can pick up fo $100 or less. 

If your computer uses a USB3 connection, you will have reasonable read/write speeds. USB2 will be slow going.

Now before you attempt to use the drive, open Disk Utilities and format for Mac OS try to format so it is capable of install Mac Operating system I believe it is called GUID (?) — if you have USB3 USB 2 just Format it for Mac Format. 

Now hook up Both Drives and Move any Mac Files from the NTFS Drive to the new drive just formatted. And by the way to Properly remove an external Drive from a Mac without risking damaging files is, click on the drive to select. Then control-click (Trackpad) or right-click a two or three button mouse. When Context menu opens, choose eject.
