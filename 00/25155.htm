kosh vorlon say:

Hi yobboy,

 

What appears in detected items History is what occurred - merely a record and has nothing to do with what currently is happening on your system. If something is put in quarantine, it will appear as such in the detected item history report. If you remove it from quarantine (delete it in effect) and go back to detected items, it will still show there as being quarantined even though you removed it because the fact that it was quarantined remains true even if that's no longer the case for that file. It's just a report of what occurred - not a report of the current situation. Deleting it merely deletes the record of events and since that is helpful (or could be helpful later if someone needs to know what else has infected you in the past 30 days which is all that shows in that report before items are automatically removed from the report anyway), I'd not do that (and it will affect nothing except the record of events - the actual files will not change in the slightest).

 

If you click on the radio button for Quarantine and there is nothing there, then that file was either removed or restored after going to quarantine. If it is not there, it is not in quarantine. If it was removed, then it's gone and that's that (and that could occur depending on your quararantine settings). If it was restored, then someone would have had to do that because items do not simply restore on their own from Quarantine (it would defeat the purpose). My guess is that it has been removed and that's why it doesn't appear in Quarantine.
