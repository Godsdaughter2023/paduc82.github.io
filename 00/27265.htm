Just in case what you really want is a formula that counts the number of unique items in a range...

Using the sample data that Bill posted, in A1:C4
a b c
a c b
d b a
a b c

This formula returns the count of unique items:
D1: =SUMPRODUCT((A1:C4<>"")/COUNTIF(A1:C4,A1:C4&""))

For the above data, the formula returns: 4...(representing a, b, c, and d)

Hi Sarah,

Here are the answers to your questions:

Question1: Mail policy tip takes minutes to display.

Answer: As it takes some time for the service to recognize the sensitive information, it is the expected behavior that the policy tip doesn’t display immediately. Thanks for your understanding.

Question2: It appears that can take up to many hours for matches to be registered in the main screen and how to download report. 

Answer: It needs some time for the match of the DLP policy to display in the Compliance center. There is no set time for when the matched policy will be displayed.

To download the matched policy report, please go to Portal (https://portal.office.com) – Admin – Go to the old admin center – Compliance – Reports – DLP policy matches -- … --Export.

Question3: I have never seen the policy tip in my iPhone Exchange client.  Can it show them?

Answer: No, we cannot see the policy tip when using Exchange ActiveSync in a mobile. For your reference: https://blogs.technet.microsoft.com/uspartner_ts2team/2015/10/21/office-365-things-you-need-to-know-data-loss-prevention/
