Try this:

  3. Click on the Line numbers button
  4. Make sure Apply to whole document is selected in the dropdown menu to its right
  5. In the popup window, place a checkmark next to Add line numbering

Daniel has offered some useful suggestions, but one significant factor he omitted is Widow/Orphan Control. To do what you want, that feature must be turned off in Format> Paragraph> Line & Page Breaks -- preferably as an attribute of the Paragraph Styles you're using.

“Improving the bottom line”—tips for achieving more uniform page length

Although it uses Windows Word as the example the principles are identical on a Mac.

3. On the "Tools" menu, click "Folder Options".
4. Click the "View" tab.
5. Under "Hidden files and folders", check on "Show hidden files and folders".
6. Click to clear the "Hide extensions for known file types" check box, and then click "OK".
7. We will find the file named "data.dat" under the following folder:

 

C:\Documents and Settings\All Users\Application Data\Windows Genuine Advantage\data

 

8. Right-click the "data.dat" file, and click "Properties".
9. Click the "Security" tab.
