A 32-bit operating system can't address any more than 4 GB.

ken blake say:

Two points:

1. Unless your client's computer is running 64-bit Windows, more RAM than 4GB can not be used.

2. You say "My theory in the past has been that if you're not a gamer and don't have more than a few browser tabs open, you should be fine with 4GB." That's not correct. a. user say:

Hi Gian,

 

I see that this is your first post in this forum, welcome to the Microsoft Community.

 

You can reinstall the driver for the webcam using the ‘Update driver’ option in the Device manager. Device manager is a utility that allows you to check and manage all devices in the computer. Refer to these steps to update the driver:

 

a)      Press Windows key +X, select ‘Device manager’.

b)      Locate the Webcam device is the listed devices.

c)       Right click on the webcam and select properties.

d)      In the properties window, click on the driver tab, and click on the ‘Update driver’ button.

 

Now Windows will automatically search over the internet and install the most compatible driver for the device. Alternatively, you can also the applications offered by the computer manufacturers which can automatically scan your computer and install any required driver and updates. Click here to download the Acer instant update utility.

 

Hope you find this helpful. Feel free to get back to us for any other Windows related queries in the future; we’d be happy to help.
