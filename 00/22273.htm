joeu2004 say:

Nan wrote:

I have imported a database into excel that contains an epoch date that I need to display in 1/12/2009 10:29 PM format. There are over 900 records.

Are you referring to Unix (Posix) epoch time, namely:  seconds since 1 Jan 1970?

If such times are in A1:A900, enter the following formula into a parallel column (B1, for example):

=A1/86400 + DATE(1970,1,1)

formatted as Custom m/dd/yyyy hh:mm or hh:mm AM/PM (US version; yours might be different).

Then you can copy B1:B900, paste-value into A1:A900, and delete B1:B900.

Or do you want a VBA macro to accomplish the task?

Is the document saved on the local hard drive or on a network?

Method 1:

Ensure there are no multiple instances of Word running in the background with the document already open.

Quit all instances of Word.

1. Press CTRL+ALT+DELETE to open the Windows Security dialog box.

2. Click Task Manager, and then click the Processes tab.

3. Click Winword.exe, and then click End Process.

4. In the Task Manager Warning dialog box, click yes.
