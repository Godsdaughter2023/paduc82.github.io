greginmich say:

Windows Defender is turned off by group policy. How could it be enabled ?

Moved from Windows

Third-party AV apps aren’t supposed to be turning Windows Defender off with Group Policy, although it’s possible that one of their developers might have slipped up. The Group Policy switch is intended specifically for the use of administrators, although on home PCs, Defender is usually getting turned off by malware that's impersonating an administrator after escalating its privilege-level:

https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/protect/forum/protect_defender-protect_start/windows-defender-is-off-i-cant-turn-it-on/c1ef0d9b-7940-4108-9578-de2c996b75ea

As a local administrator, you have access to the Windows Defender Group Policy switch – and you can turn Defender back on again by either directly editing the Policies key; or by running a REG file; or by running this REG command at the elevated (administrator) command prompt:

REG DELETE "HKLM\SOFTWARE\Policies\Microsoft\Windows Defender" /v DisableAntiSpyware

As I recently illustrated in this thread:

https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/protect/forum/protect_defender-protect_scanning/windows-defender-this-app-is-turned-off-by-group/dfa59c2a-b358-4f62-80b6-6b8e4994dc32

As you have probably seen in your research on this issue is that Access and Excel assume a connection that is not necessarily a good option for importing.  The overall best way to avoid this is to not import an Excel file but to import a .csv file instead.  That way you can set up import specifications and force the data into the format you need before appending to your data.  That is why I gave you the link for converting the Excel file to .csv before importing

Another issue may be how you are bringing the data in.  Are you importing into a temporary table and then appending to your actual data?  That is the best way to assure your data is going to fit correctly.  The issue with your Excel file is that even though you are importing into a temp table, the data may still think it is not a date and may skip that field when appending to your actual data.
