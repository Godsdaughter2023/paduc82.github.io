macropod say:

Your field construction is missing a required space after the '='. At a minimum you should have:

{IF{MERGEFIELD HOME}= "" {MERGEFIELD WORK} {MERGEFIELD HOME}}

or:

{IF«HOME»= "" «WORK» «HOME»}

Note: The field brace pairs (i.e. '{ }') for the above example are all created in the document itself, via Ctrl-F9 (Cmd-F9 on a Mac); you can't simply type them or copy & paste them from this message. Likewise, the chevrons (i.e. '« »') are part of the actual mergefields - which you can insert from the 'Insert Merge Field' dropdown (i.e. you can't type or copy & paste them from this message, either). The spaces represented in the field constructions are all required.

john mcghie say:

Currently, you can't.  DropBox is not supported currently.  Microsoft has a competing service named OneDrive that will work, but the other online storage services will not work reliably.

What I do is save the attachment to the hard drive, then open it OUTSIDE of the email program.  You will then be able to edit and save properly.

After you have closed the file, you can copy it to DropBox and it will all get there and work: that's what I do.

Make sure you are running Microsoft Auto Update (or go to Help>Check for updates).  Word will update about once a month, and its ability to handle online storage will slowly improve.

Hope this helps
