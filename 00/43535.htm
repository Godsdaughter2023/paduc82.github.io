a. user say:

You sure have a lot of (120) processes running and many of them are things installed by Dell and some by you.  I only have 28 Processes running but I don't have a store bought system and tend to run very lean...

Let's work on the afflicted svchost Process first...

Behind (or under) each svchost Process are important Windows Services.  The important Windows Services are being hosted by the svchost Process you see and it is fine to see several to many scvhost Processes running.  That's why it is called svc host - it is hosting Services.

One of the symptoms of a known problem with Windows Update is a svchost Process that is running amok - like yours

The scvhost Process that is running amok is probably hosting the Windows Update Services.  When Windows Update is afflicted you will see a svchost Process running amok using too much CPU and RAM.  This afflicted Process can cause your laptop fans to rev up and down and could even result in a thermal event (too much heat).

When things are normal the svchost Processes should be doing very little like this example from my system that has been running all day (Task Manager sorted by Image Name so they all appear together):

There are two different discussions with different amounts of detail that will tell you how to fix Windows Update but they both involve the same thing:

The first part of each is to adjust your Windows Update settings to Never check for updates and restart your system.

That should put a stop to the afflicted scvhost Process so just do that part first, look in Task Manager at your svchost Processes (sorted by Image Name) and see how thinks look and your fans run after that adjustment.

Then the rest of the Windows Update fix is to download and install two updates so you need to be sure to do that part too so you get the entire Windows Update fix.

Resetting lets you choose whether to keep your files or remove them, and then reinstalls Windows. To get started, go to Settings > Update & security > Reset this PC >Get started and choose an option.

Option

Keep my files
What it does

  • Reinstalls Windows 10 and keeps your personal files.

  • Removes apps and drivers you installed.

  • Removes changes you made to settings.

  • Removes any apps your PC manufacturer installed. (If your PC came with Windows 10, apps from your PC manufacturer will be reinstalled.)
Option

Remove everything
What it does

  • Reinstalls Windows 10 and removes all your personal files.

  • Removes apps and drivers you installed.

  • Removes changes you made to settings.

If you're planning to donate, recycle, or sell your PC, use this option and choose to fully clean the drive. This might take an hour or two, but it makes it harder for other people to recover files you've removed.
Option

Restore factory settings
What it does
