a. user say:

Why is it a bad idea to have administrator privileges on an alternative profile?

Good question.  A user account with Administrators permissions has substantial ability to make changes to the computer. The first thing an attacker wants to do is to somehow gain access to a user account with Administrators permissions.

That's why it's always better to do all your daily computing from a user account with Users permissions, which is more commonly known as a Standard account. If your user account somehow gets hacked despite your best efforts to prevent that from happening, at least the attacker won't be able to harm the entire computer or use your computer to harm thousands of other computers.

In practice you'll almost never need to sign in to your Administrators account. If you need elevated permissions only temporarily - to run a program, for example - then you 'run as' the program with Administrators permissions.

To do this, right-click on the program's shortcut (or executable) and choose 'Run as' from the context menu. You'll see a User Account Control prompt for the password to an Administrators account. Enter the account password and you will have elevated permissions for that program only. This is a much safer way to operate than to do everything from an Administrators account.

From the very beginning of computers, long before anyone heard of Windows, people have been following the principal of least privilege. You can Google that, but what it means is that every user of a computer has the least amount of permissions on the computer that are needed for their role. (P.S. you can Bing that too, if you want.)

Whenever you change to a new version of Windows, you need to install
drivers (software that provides the interface between the hardware and
Windows) for printers, scanners, video cards, etc., that have been
written for that new version of Windows.  And also note that you need
different drivers for a 32-bit version of Windows and a 64-bit
version.

It's up to the manufacturer of the hardware to write and make
available for download those drivers. Especially if your hardware is
older, it may  well be that the manufacturer has decided that it's not
worth his time and effort to develop drivers for hardware that he is
no longer selling.

exists. If it doesn't, you're out of luck.

done the appropriate research to find out what drivers don't exist,
and therefore what hardware you will have to replace if you upgrade.
