scottgem say:

I think you are misunderstanding what an Autonumber is and how it is used,. An Autonumber is a system generated number to assign a unique value to a record. That is all it is and nothing more. Because it guarantees uniqueness it is suitable to use as a Primary Key (but must be used in conjunction with a Natural Key, more on that later). The PK value of your table is then used as a Foreign Key to relate the record in one table to a record in another table. However, this assignment is NOT automatic, unless you use an embedded subform with Parent/Child linking on the key fields.

I'm going to try and guess what your tables are because its not clear. 

Purchases. items purchased from you

Inventory: items in stock

Repairs: repairs of purchased items

Is that correct? If so, you may need need some more tables like a customers table. In addition, do customers purchase multiple items at a time? If so, you also need a Details table for the line items purchased at one time.

If my guess of your tables is correct, then Your Purchases table should have InventoryID as a foreign key to indicate what item was purchased. And your Repair table should have PurchaseID as a FK to indicate what order the repair is for.

Please let us know if this is correct or clarify what your app does and what the tables capture so we can help further.

Note, that Foreign Keys CANNOT be Autonumbers. A FK for an Autonumber PK needs to be a Number>Long Integer datatype. If the relationship is 1:Many then the FK cannot be unique.,
