People receiving spam from your address in no way constitutes evidence that your account was hacked.  Only if you see the spam in your Sent folder is it proof.  The most likely scenario is that your address was spoofed - some spammer used your address (NOT your account) as the sending address for the spam.  This happens to most people at some point and it should stop after a short while because spammers change the sending address frequently.

I also don't want to give you a false sense of security.  Not seeing the messages in Sent isn't proof you weren't hacked.  If you do not have the "save Sent items" option enabled, then even if your account did send them, you won't see them, since you have the option to save them disabled.  If the option IS enabled and you don't see them, then your account didn't send them. If the option is disabled, then there's no information one way or the other on whether or not your account sent them.  However, it is much more likely your address was spoofed than your account was compromised.

Hi,

 

No worries, changing it for M2, R2 etc is easy. This does M2, drag right 1 cell for r2 then another for W2

 

 

=OFFSET($M$2,,(COLUMN(A1)-1)*5)

 

Debugging it is also easy when you understand how it works. In the above formula we are OFFSETTING from M2 and we work out the OFFSET with the underlined bit like this

 

COLUMN(A1)-1*5 returns zero because COLUMN(A1) returns 1 so we have

 

1-1*5 so 1-1=0 and 5*0 =0 so as above there is no offset from M2. When we drag 1 cell right then things change and the formula becomes

 

=OFFSET($M$2,,(COLUMN(B1)-1)*5)

 

Now COLUMN(B1) returns  2 so we get

 

2-1*5 which equals 5 so we now have an offset of 5 cells from M2 which is R2.

 

Keep dragging and we get an increase of 5 each time.
