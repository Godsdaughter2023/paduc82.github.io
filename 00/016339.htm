trevor rabey say:

Well, solving this puzzle with this limited amount of information will require some guesses, but here goes.

Format, check the critical tasks box. Are any bars red?

In file, options, advanced, scroll to the bottom and turn on calculate multiple critical paths.

Note that tasks are defined as critical if the total slack is less than or equal to zero.

Make sure that all tasks are visible, ie not collapsed or rolled up under their summaries. Go to view, outline, show all subtasks. Note that filters only work on the tasks that are visible.

Switch to the schedule table. It has the total slack column. Tasks which have zero total slack are critical.

In this table, insert/display the critical field. It will be yes/no depending on total slack.

Go to file, options, general and select a date/time format which displays the time.

Make a new custom group. group, more groups, new etc. Call it "AA Group By Total Slack", and make it group by total slack. Look for tasks which have zero total slack and also those which have only a small amount of total slack. This could indicate a gap in the critical path.

A gap might be caused by durations in edays ("elapsed").

What are the duration units?

Are all of the durations short?

Do any tasks have a date constraint, ie other than ASAP? You can display the constraint type column to check.

Does every task have at least one finish-start predecessor and at least one finish-start successor?

Any help so far?
