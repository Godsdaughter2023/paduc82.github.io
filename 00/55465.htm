arminatorx say:

The "ownership" licensing thing on Xbox works like this: 

Games and Memberships are tied to the account that purchased them. That means, you as the owner of the game or subscription will always be able to play your games, no matter on what console you sign in online to play. 

So you can go to any of your 2 consoles or your kid's consoles, or basically any console in the world, sign in and *you* will be able to play your games and benefit from your memberships, as long as you are online on that console. 

You can also share your games and memberships to ONE SINGLE Xbox One console, by setting that console as your "Home Console". An account can however only have one Home Console at the same time. 

And before you think about alternating the Home Console between your two kids: an account can only change it's Home Console five times during 365 days. 

As for a more or less working solution for your issue:

You don't need your own second Xbox to be your Home Console. In fact, none of the consoles *you* play on have to be your Home Console. 

Set one of your kid's consoles as your Home Console, and that kid can play your games and benefit from your Gold Membership or Game Pass. That kid can always play alone on the console with their own account, and will be able to play your games and play online Multiplayer through your shared Gold membership. 

As for the second kid: You would have to be signed in online on that second kid's console, to allow the kid to play your games. As long as your account is signed in on that console, the console can also share your Gold membership and games with anyone playing on that console. It's like a "temporary Home Console", as long as your account is signed in. 

However, you won't be able to play on your own consoles during that time, because as soon as you sign in on your own console, your account will be signed out of the kid's console. 

So if you plan on playing together with both of your kids at the same time, one kid (the kid not playing on the "Home Console") has to get a separate Gold membership, and their own copy of the game you three want to play.

a. Go to Start, click Control Panel and then double click Date and Time or Right-click on the system clock in the system tray, and then choose Adjust Date/Time from the menu.

b. Click Internet Time tab.

c. In the Internet Time Settings dialog, Change the Server to "time-a.nist.gov" (without the quotation marks).

d. Click Update Now.

e. Click Apply and then click OK.

Set the clock
