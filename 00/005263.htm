How do I transfer an Excel formula from one worksheet to another? - Microsoft Community

crt42 say:

How do I transfer an Excel formula from one worksheet to another?

Possibly a bug here.  I copy a formula from Sheet 1 - the formula refers to cell data in Sheet 1 - to Sheet 2.  In Sheet 2 the formula refers to cell data in Sheet 2 (nonexistant) instead of correctly referring to cell data in Sheet 1.  Any help?

j.e. mcgimpsey say: Probably not a bug - are your references in Sheet1 absolute (e.g., Sheet1!$A$1) or relative (e.g., A1)?

XL's default behavior is to paste relative references relatively.

Instead of using relative references, in Sheet1, use an absolute address, e.g.:

=SUM(Sheet1!$A$1:$B$20)

You should then be able to copy and paste the formula to Sheet2 and retain the absolute references.

phillip m jones say: what you need to do:

If formula is in cell C2 on Sheet one and you want  to use the results in Sheet2 format this way

In Sheet 2 cell C2 type:    =Sheet1!C2


If you just want to use the same formula in sheet 1 in the formula bar Copy the formula.
Switch to sheet 2 and choose the desired cell  the in formula Bar Paste the formula remove any reference to sheets (Sheet1!, Sheet2!)

crt42 say: Thank you for your response.  If default behavior is relative to sheet (as it is with cell), I understand.  Next question:  if I have a lot of different formulas that, when written, did not make absolute references to a Sheet, is there a speedy way to insert the absolute references?  Other than copying all of the Sheet 1 data to Sheet 2?

jim gordon mvp say:

I'm going back to your original question.


Click in the formula bar and highlight the formula there. Use Command-C to copy the formula, then press Return or Enter to exit the formula bar. Then switch to another worksheet or workbook and paste the formula into a cell.


Alternatively, double-click in a cell to activate in-cell editing. Select the formula in the formula box that appears. Use Command-c to copy the formula, then press Return, Enter, or Escape to leave the cell. Then switch to another worksheet or workbook and paste the formula into a cell.
