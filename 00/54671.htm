adrian3a say:

Using the previous suggestion could work, and I used to actually do it that way myself.  However I have found a different way that has given better results.

You must start with setting up all 3 monitors operating initially (meaning the 2 monitors and the Surface Book 2) 

Next, go to display settings and select one of the monitors, (#2 or #3) then choose "Make this my main" checkbox.

Next,  select the monitor that is your Surface Book 2 (usually the #1 monitor)

Then Scroll down to where it has the setting to set it to display as duplicate, extend, etc...  there will be an option listed as "Disconnect this display"  thats the one you want. 

After you select it, you should sign out then close your SurfaceBook 2 "lid". When it comes back on, your 2 monitors should be operating as you wanted. 

The problem with the Do nothing when lid is closed method is that the display is still "connected"  when the lid is closed..  So that can cause unwanted side effects.  Very apparent if you have surface pro with type cover and you happen to place a pen on the type cover while it's closed. It will start reading the pointer and curser starts to move around.     

Hope it helps,

Hello,
 
      Thank you for choosing Microsoft answers forum for windows vista.
 
A smart card is a small plastic card containing a computer chip. People use smart cards along with personal identification numbers (PINs) to log on to a network, a computer, or a device. Using a smart card adds a level of security above using a password because it's more difficult for someone to steal a smart card and learn your PIN than to learn your password.

Smart cards are generally issued by information technology (IT) departments in large organizations. To use a smart card, you also need a smart card reader—a device that’s installed in or connected to your computer and that can read the information stored on a smart card. To log on to a Windows Vista-based computer with a smart card:

1. Connect the smart card reader to your computer, if necessary.

2. Insert your smart card into the smart card reader.
 
3. At the logon screen, click Switch User.
 
4. Type your PIN, and then press ENTER.
