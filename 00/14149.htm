Thanks in advance for your help.

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

The issue was that there were Continuous Section Breaks in the document and the numbering had been set to re-start at 1, rather than continue from the previous Section.

By running a macro containing the following code, the page numbering was changed so that it was continuous throughout the document, which then causes the cross references to the page number of the bookmarks to display the expected result.

With ActiveDocument
    For i = 1 To .Sections.Count
        With .Sections(i)
            For j = 1 To .Footers.Count
                .Footers(j).PageNumbers.RestartNumberingAtSection = False
            Next j
        End With
    Next i
End With

Picture Shape - Microsoft Community

mamamoore say:

Picture Shape

Using the Picture Shape button, I'm trying to make a picture into a hexagon shape, but I want the point to be at the bottom of the picture instead of the top.  How can I flip the direction of the shape?

david marcovitz say:

This is very easy, but the exact instructions depend on which version of PowerPoint you are using. The general instructions are to draw the shape, rotate it to the orientation you want, fill the shape with the picture, and choose not to rotate the picture with the shape. When you draw a shape, most recent versions have a green dot above the shape. Drag the green dot to rotate the shape. In 2007, you can then go to the Format tab on the ribbon and choose Shape Fill and Picture to fill the shape with a picture. Next, you need to right-click on the shape (which now has a picture in it and choose Format Picture. Now click on the Fill tab and uncheck the box for Rotate Picture with Shape.

--David

 

 

-- David M. Marcovitz, Author of _Powerful PowerPoint for Educators_, http://www.PowerfulPowerPoint.com/ Microsoft PowerPoint MVP, Associate Professor, Loyola University Maryland

echo s say:

Based on your mention of the Picture Shape button, I'm betting you're using PPT 2007.

The Picture Shape option doesn't let you rotate the shape without rotating the image, too. If you need to do that, you must use a different technique (that gives you pretty much the same results).

1. Insert Shape. Choose hexagon or pentagon or whatever shape you want. Click and drag on the slide to draw the shape.

2. Select the shape, right-click it and choose Format Shape.

3. In the Format Shape dialog, choose Fill on the left and Picture or texture fill on the right.

4. Click the File button. Navigate to your picture and click Insert.

5. At the bottom of that dialog, uncheck Rotate with Shape.

Now you can rotate the shape as you wish and the picture will stay upright.

Tab to flush right - Microsoft Community

mike_3046 say:

Tab to flush right

I need to type a few words on a line with left justification, then tab to flush right with a dot leader on the same line.  I can't remember how to do this.  When I try to set it up in the Tabs menu, the "Set" button does not illuminate after pressing the Right and dot leader radio buttons.
hansv mvp say:

Select the paragraph(s) for which you want to do this.

On the Home tab of the ribbon, click the arrow in the lower right corner of the Paragraph group.

Click the Tabs... button in the lower left corner of the Paragraph dialog.

Enter the Tab stop position where you want to flush right.

For example, on a Letter size (8.5" x 11") page with Portrait orientation and 1" left and right margins, that position would be 6.5".

On an A4 page (8.27" x 11.69") page with Portrait orientation and 1" left and right margins, that position would be 6.27".

Select Right as alignment and pick a tab leader.

Then click Set.
