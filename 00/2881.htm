What is the formatting symbol that looks like a rectangle inside a - Microsoft Community

josh craig say:

What is the formatting symbol that looks like a rectangle inside a rectangle?

Someone sent me a Word document the other day and it had a strange formatting symbol in it that I've never seen before. It's only visible when I show paragraph markers and it looks like a grey rectangle with a grey fill insides a clear rectangle with a grey border:

Can anyone tell me what it is and does?

suzanne s. barnhill say:

The character called "no-width optional break" on the Special Characters tab of the Symbol dialog (which is U200C, zero-width non-joiner) will be displayed as a rectangle inside a rectangle, but on my screen (in both Word 2003 and Word 2010 and at any zoom), the inner rectangle doesn't have a grey fill like yours. U200B (zero-width space) displays the same.

What appears to be the function of your character? Is it at the end of a paragraph? Some fonts have very odd paragraph marks. Indeed, some of the clones that shipped with early versions of Corel Graphics have hideously intrusive space characters (a large empty square instead of a dot, for example).

In any case, if you select the symbol and press Alt+X, you will get the Unicode number of it. Or if you select it and open the Symbol dialog, the symbol will be selected; the name of the symbol is at the bottom, to the left of the character code.
