lifechariot say:

FIXED!

A few hours ago I wrote how my choice of HDMI, instead of Auto-Detect, caused me to have a blank screen.

The instructions that 'ScoBis' posted above proved useful as I unplugged power cable from the Xbox for 30 seconds, then plugged the power cable back in before  PRESSING and HOLDING the 'Eject' & 'Start' buttons together for approximately 20-30 seconds.

The screen 'lit up' and I changed the audio/visual setting back to Auto-Detect, as they had been reset to low-resolution from this rebooting-like procedure.

So I apologise for my previous statement as there is, although badly articulated by Microsoft and a bit fiddly, a fix for this problem.
