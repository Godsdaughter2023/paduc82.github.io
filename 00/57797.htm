A ping of below 50ms is more than reasonable. All games should play without any issue at that latency. You only start to have issue with ping when you approach the 100ms mark in regards to first person shooters generally. There is nothing you can do to alter or change your ping to external destinations outside your router.  Latency is inherent in all systems and is usually the result of a bad hop or node between you and the destination. If you are overly concerned you could log into your router and run traceroute to your DNS servers. When the trace is done skim down the list of the hops and look for one that has a latency deviation well beyond that of the others. Then you

will know where you culprit resides. Take note however that the distance from you to the final destination physically will generally be the primary factor in latency outside of a congested node or  excessive line noise.
