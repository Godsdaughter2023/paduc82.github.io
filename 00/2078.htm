alan wu msft say:
Hi Shingo198199,

Firstly, I’d like to clarify that only four countries/regions that support Office 365 PSTN Calling. (You can understand it as those 4 countries/regions can make/have the PSTN Calling). They are: France, Puerto Rico, United States and United Kingdom. Other countries/regions listed in that article stands for the places where you (a customer in any of that 4 countries) can make the call to them.

And, PSTN Conferencing and PSTN Calling are totally different services. PSTN Conferencing can make people inside your organization dial in a meeting via a phone instead of a PC (or a Skype for Business client); PSTN Calling is an add-on telephone service that, when combined with Skype for Business Cloud PBX, can become your phone system. PSTN calling provides the people in your business with a primary phone number, and lets them make and receive phone calls outside of your organization. So, the biggest difference is that PSTN conferencing is a service/feature used for joining a meeting but PSTN Calling is used for making/receiving phone call.

You may also refer to What is PSTN Calling via https://support.office.com/en-us/article/What-is-PSTN-calling-3dc773b9-95e0-4448-b2f1-887c54022429?ui=en-US&rs=en-US&ad=US.
