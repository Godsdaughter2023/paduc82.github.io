jllatham say:

Try this method to convert any numeric entries that may actually be numbers displayed as text into true number entries.

Choose an empty cell and enter a 1 (digit one) into it.  After you've finished all of this, you can simply delete the 1 in that cell.

Select that cell again and use Edit-->Copy or [Ctrl]+[C] to copy it to the clipboard.

Select all of the cells in column 1 of your lookup table (don't choose the label at the top of the list of numbers).
You can do that quickly by entering the full range such as A2:A4000 into the Name Box.
The Name Box is that area at the left edge of the formula bar that usually shows the address of the cell you have selected.

Use Edit --> Paste Special and choose the MULTIPLY operation and click [OK].
This will convert any numbers displayed as text to become true numbers.

Repeat this process for the column of values that can be used as the lookup value in your VLOOKUP() formula.
This should result in your VLOOKUP() formula comparing numbers to numbers consistently.

    I believe I have discovered a bug in the way sub-second timestamps are rounded by the Excel formula bar and chart axis labels.
