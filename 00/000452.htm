Moving around in Templates using Tab key - Microsoft Community

sheryl kreilkreil say:

Moving around in Templates using Tab key

I have a template that has check boxes and text form fields but the Tab key does not move the cursor to the next field, it expands the field.  How can I stop this?
jay freedman say:
When you insert form fields of any kind, you must "protect" the document to make it work as a form.

The short way: Right-click the Quick Access Toolbar and choose "Customize Quick Access Toolbar". Set the category in the left list to All Commands. Then find the Lock command in the list and click the Add button in the center of the dialog, and click OK. This adds the Lock icon to the toolbar. Now you just have to click that button to turn forms protection on and off.

The long way: On the Review tab, click Protect Document, which opens the Restrict Formatting and Editing pane. Check the box in section 2 "Editing restrictions". Open the dropdown below it, and choose "Filling in forms". Click the "Yes, Start Enforcing Protection" button. Enter a password if you want one, or leave it blank for no password, and click OK. To turn off protection, go back to the Restrict Formatting pane and click the Stop Protection button.

When a document is protected for forms, you can use the Tab key to move from field to field, but you can't edit anything that's outside the fields. It also interferes with other functions such as spell-checking.

If you turn on forms protection in the template and save it that way, all documents you base on the template will automatically be protected.

More at http://www.computorcompanion.com/LPMArticle.asp?ID=22.

Ignore the fancy header and footer and page number building blocks (the problem is that they are mutually exclusive). Instead, double-click in the header area to access the header. Hi Ahmed,

Based on our encounters recently, it appears the portal has a bug whereby it will not update the progress and just hangs/appears stuck.

After about 24 hours it magically comes right and displays complete, or complete with errors etc.

We ended up avoiding the GUI all together and confirming if the jobs were complete by checking the powershell import progress.

Fire up a PowerShell Exchange Online connection and do a get-mailboximportrequest to view the actual state of the request.

There is a side effect to the GUI stalling, it means you can't add or remove litigation or archives while the job is stuck.  The work around for that is to enable archiving or litigation hold before commencing the PST import.

# Script to get import progress from powershell

# Note that "Failed" may simply mean some items have been skipped etc.

$usercredential=Get-Credential

$proxysettings=New-PSSessionOption-ProxyAccessTypeIEConfig

$Session=New-PSSession-ConfigurationNameMicrosoft.Exchange-SessionOptionImport-PSSession$Session

Get-MailboxImportRequest|ft-AutoSize

Hope this helps you or someone else in the future... the Office 365 support team are a bit of hard work to get anything out of.
