Maximum number of times activated - Microsoft Community

juliehumphrey say:

Maximum number of times activated

I just purchased a new computer (my old one crashed) and am now trying to install my Office Home and Business 2010 and Publsher 2010 but get the message that I have activated too many times. I installed on my laptop and my old computer, is 2 activations all you get?
dan at it associates say:

Office H&B 2010 does not include Publisher, so in fact you are installing two separate products.

 

How many concurrent activations are permitted depends upon in what form you purchased your Office products.  Office H&B can be purchased as traditional packaged discs, as a direct purchase and download from Microsoft, or as a Product Key Card (PKC) or Original Equipment Manufacturer (OEM) licensed product.  The license that comes with the packaged discs and the direct download permits two concurrent activations (it can be activated on two computers at the same time) and you are permitted to move the licensed activations to different computers.  OTOH, the PKC and OEM licenses permit only one activation, and once activated the license is permanently tied to that first computer and is not permitted to be moved to any other computer.

 

If you have purchased Publisher 2010 separately, then we know it is a retail stand alone license for Publisher, which is like the packaged prodct license in that you are permitted two concurent activations and are permitted to move the license to different computers.

 

If you are getting the "activated too many times" message and you have purchased either the traditional packaged disc or direct download license, rerun the Activation Wizard on the new computer and this time select "activate by telephone" and follow the prompts.
