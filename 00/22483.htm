jay freedman say:

You can't remove just the Smart Lookup button, but you can remove the Research group (which also contains the Researcher button). If you want the Researcher button, you can then insert a custom group containing only that one button.

Right-click the ribbon and click Customize the Ribbon. In the list on the right side of the dialog, expand the References item, click on Research, and click the Remove button between the lists.

If you want the custom group, click on Footnotes (so the new group will be added below it) and click the New Group button. Click the Rename button and name the group Research (or anything else). While the new group is selected, set the dropdown above the left list to Main Tabs; expand the References item; expand the Research item; click on Researcher, and click the Add button between the lists.

Click OK, and the ribbon will be changed.

john mcghie say:
Hi Bryan:

Hmmm...  Nothing to do with AppleScript then :-)

OK, Word can't "actually" do regex.  It can get up to some fairly cool tricks with wild-card searches, but regular expressions are out of its league.

There's a very good basic primer here:
http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/help/add-power-to-word-searches-with-regular-expressions-HA001087305.aspx

You can use the Find function to find your fraction.  However, you will have to drop into VBA to convert it into an equation.

The following module runs and works in Word 2011:

Sub Macro2()
'
Dim objRange As Range
Dim objEq As OMath

Set objRange = Selection.Range

objRange.Text = "Celsius = (5/9)(Fahrenheit – 32)"

Set objRange = Selection.OMaths.Add(objRange)
Set objEq = objRange.OMaths(1)

objEq.ConvertToMathText

objEq.BuildUp

End Sub


To modify it for your purposes, you would need to retrieve the text of the Selection object (which will be the text you were searching for after a successful Find).

The trick is going to be to spot the end of the string.  Finding the "\frac" part is no big deal.  If they are all in the format you show, then you could simply extend right for the first space and take that as the end of your string.

Substitute that for the objRange.Text = "Celsius = (5/9)(Fahrenheit – 32)" line and it will work.

Coding a Find in VBA is a chore: everyone simply "records" the Find they want and pastes the result into their macro :-)

Note:  There is a design weakness in that code: Set objEq = objRange.OMaths(1) refers explicitly to the FIRST Equation in the document.  If there's more than one, you will need to fix it.

I don't have to try this now, but:

Set objEq = Selection.OMaths.Add(objRange)

Will set objEq to the equation you just created (rather than number 1 in the document) provided you change the Dim statement for objEq to "Object" instead of "OMath".  You then need to feed your retrieved string into it.

Let me know if you find this method acceptable and I will produce a working one for you when I have a clearer head: it's way past my bedtime currently :-) 

Hope this helps

There is a group policy for signing replies - according to this, the default behavior is to reply with a signature if the original was signed.

"If you enable this policy setting, the option to respond automatically to a signed message with a signed response will be overridden, and an unsigned response will be the default reply to a signed message.

The key set by the policy is [HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Policies\Microsoft\office\16.0\outlook\security]
"nosignonreply"=dword:00000001
