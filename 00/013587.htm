entegy say:
Open the start menu and type "Snipping tool" without quotes. Press enter. Your screen will be overlayed with white and yoru mouse will turn to a cross. Drag and highlight the section of the screen you want to take a screenshot of. A window with the picture you just made will show up. Choose File>save and save in any format except BMP.

Another method is to press PrintScreen on your keyboard. PrintScreen will place a screenshot of your entire screen on your clipboard. Alternatively, use ALT+PrintScreen to take a screenshot of just the active window. So for example, click on the Internet Explorer window and press ALT+PrintScreen. Now you'll just have a screenshot of IE instead of your entire screen.
Once you have the picture on your clipboard, open Paint, paste the picture in and save it and send it.
