If no startup item causes this problem, a Microsoft service most likely causes the problem. To determine which Microsoft service may be causing the problem, repeat step 1 and step 2 without selecting the Hide all Microsoft services check box in either step.

How did you first stumble upon Grand Theft Auto? What are your favourite missions? And what has been your favourite moment of all time from within the series? Here are a few notable points I can take away from my Grand Theft Auto career:

 

-

 

Grand Theft Auto (Original) – This is an obvious one to begin. Loading GTA, and getting in the ‘Bulldog’. The preset radio blasts funky disco as I scream around the corner and consecutively run over about 12 guys in orange suits. Gouranja! Not until years later did I realise what Gouranja meant, and that these dudes were Monks. What a great introduction to GTA! And then I discovered the burp and fart button…

 

-

 

Grand Theft Auto San Andreas – So, I am pretty far into GTA: SA here. I have infinite money, all safe-houses unlocked, and a game-save for 100% completion. Before I did this, I did legitimately complete the game, by the way. Anywho, I come across a cheat which makes everyone go crazy like in the infamous LA Riots. People are even running around with TV’s! Combining this with the cheat which gives NPC’s weapons, and makes them attack each other, and San Andreas is a warzone. As I fly my chopper across the landscape, I see plumes of billowing smoke erupt from the ground as NPC’s kill each other with an insane bloodlust. I parachute to the ground, draw my dual Sawn-off shotguns, and go to town. Whenever I got a little bored, cheats in GTA always spiced things up.

 

-

 

Grand Theft Auto IV – So, me and my friends used to do this thing ‘Stand-off’ thing in GTA IV Free-Mode. We would arm up, go to an open hotel lobby opposite Central Park, and then try to get as large a wanted level as possible. Once we had done this, we then had to make a getaway from the Police, and whoever got the furthest without being killed, won. One time we were doing this, and others in the lobby caught on. Despite knowing they could’ve killed us, everyone just got along. Eventually, there were about 11 of us inside the lobby, holding off the police whilst an armada amassed outside the door. When SWAT broke through, they were cut down, but eventually, our numbers thinned. I was eventually left on my own. The bodies of my team lay around me, and I was running low on ammo. Police still converged on the door, that was until my two friends who had re-spawned, arrived outside in a four-door van, whilst other players cleared a path for me to get out. I ran from the hotel, being clipped by wavering bullets on the way, and got in the van. Still with a 6-star rating, the police chased us through the city. At one point, a SWAT van had our number, that was until a team-player in a Fire Truck smashed him into the ocean! After about an hour total, my friend parked the van, and we had lost the police. This marked the only moment in playing this little game that someone had made a clean getaway with 6-stars.

 

-

 

Please feel free to share anything and everything you have done in the world of GTA!

How did I first stumble upon GTA? I believe it was around the time of Vice City. That, I recall, was the first ever time I had entered the 3D era of GTA. My first ever GTA was that of GTA 2; an instant classic on the PS1. Anyways, I really started to get into GTA with Vice City; this was when I actually started to enjoy the series instead of just thinking of it as another TPS (Third Person Shooter). From then on I moved onto San Andreas, which is still my all time favorite today. And so no and so forth, I now am up to GTA IV, which IMO, is exactly what I expected from the leap to 3d to HD era. Bravo R*, keep it up.

-----

What were my favorite missions? That's a very hard question to answer within a few lines or sentences. I'll try to sum it up so as not to drone on about every mission. To me, I personally enjoyed the ending missions, as they, to me, were the most actiona packed ones of them all. Since I started with VC, I instantly fell in love with "Keep Your Friends Close", an obvious recreation of the famous "Mansion Shootout scene" from the movie Scarface, which at the time, I never saw yet. The feeling that I got from killing those tow backstabbing pricks (Lance and Sonny) was more than satisfying. Altogether, however, I'd have to say that I still liked all the other missions that led up to the big finale. Moving on to San Andreas, since I mentioned it, was by far the most epic mission to date; it was a bit farfetched but that's video games for ya! The irony of Tenpenny dying in a car accident was satisfying story wise and emotionally. The death of Big Smoke I found ironic because he was the one who wanted to bring the Families into the drug trade, only to be killed by Carl, who refuted against the idea of the Families getting into the drug trade. There was a lot of poetic irony in San Andreas and "End of the Line", which I thought was perfect since I'm a fan of that. Relating to this idea, you can probably guess how I felt when Dimitri/Pegorino got their "just desserts" in the final mission.

-----

What was my favorite moment of all time in the series? Again; another tough question as there were so many that it'd take pages to explain them. Besides the final missions, I'd have to say it was the moment I stumbled upon the "rare vehicles" of Vice City. It was the mission "Guardian Angel", and I had successfully protected Diaz and completed the mission. As I went to leave, I noticed the vehicle he was driving was just sitting there, empty. At first I thought it was locked, but I investigated anyway. Amzaingly, it was unlocked so I got in. Being a newcomer to GTA, I had no idea that some vehicles possessed special abilities. The first time I discovered the Admiral I was driving was special was when I robbed a store and got into a feirce pursuit with the VCPD. Naturally, I was low on funds and needed some more, so I decided to steal money frome the local Hardware store. I grabbed the money and ran, Three stars appearing as the sound of the store alram was activated. Immediately I jumped in my Admiral and sped off, only to be nearly sideswiped by an oncoming VCPD Cruiser. After about a good 15 minutes, I noticed my car was in pretty god condition; this was strange to me, considering that I'd traded a lot of paint with several VCPD Cruisers already but no signs of physical damage to the car were visible. I was shocked. After doing some research, I found out why. From that point on, I've become somewhat of a "Rare Vehicle Hunter", searching for the ellusive rare vehicles of each GTA to come out.

    Now, when San Andreas came out, I was still hunting vehicles, but another favorite moment emerged. Still fairly new to the series, I started dating Barbara in El Quebrados; much to my surprise, after 100% dating status I obtained the police uniform. Now I had remembered that it was possible to utilize a police uniform in VC, with some helpful results (limited Wanted Level). So, I figured I'd give it a try. Not much difference. Around ths time, I had alos been into wathcing cop shows like "World's Scariest/Wildest Police Videos" (ahh memories). After beating the game, I played through with cheats; to my surprise, I came upon a "Never Wanted" cheat, and thought to myself "This is too good to be true!" Naturally, my curiousity got the best of me and I tried it out. To my surprise, I noticed that I did it with no wanted level already. After observing and studying the effects, I realized I could actually be something that you normally couldn't: an LSPD police officer. From then on, I've always tried to find at least one way to be "on the other side" in every GTA game that came out. I've successfully been able to do so with VC, SA, LCS (on PSP), and IV (to some extent).
