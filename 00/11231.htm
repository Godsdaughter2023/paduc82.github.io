bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

The paper sizes listed in Page Setup are those that are supported by the printer you're using. That is not a standard paper size so it is not directly supported by many [if any] printers. I believe it's primarily a contrived specification used for e-book publishing, not printing. (Odd sizes for print purposes generally are printed on larger stock, then trimmed.)

To use a document size that isn't directly supported by your printer, select Manage Custom Sizes from the Paper Size list, then define the size you want.
