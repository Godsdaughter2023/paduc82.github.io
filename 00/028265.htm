Setting up MS Office Outlook 2007 for Outlook.com pop email - Microsoft Community

tryx3 say:

Setting up MS Office Outlook 2007 for Outlook.com pop email [migrated & non-migrated accounts]

Technical Level : Basic

Summary

The aim of this Forum article is to enable users to set up MS Office Outlook 2007 for sending & receiving Outlook.com email using pop.

These instructions can be followed step-by-step by novice users.  Nothing destructive is done at any stage.

This article is about using the pop method to retrieve email in Outlook 2007.

  • Online accounts themselves are not pop or imap or Exchange ["EAS"]. 
  • The terms pop, imap & Exchange refer to the methods used to retrieve emails. 
  • I have included a brief reference to Exchange accounts ["EAS"]
  • Towards the end of the article, I also start wittering on about imap email and about smartphone email because I always have those issues at the back of my mind when I am setting up Outlook 2007.

If you are having any problems setting up MS Office Outlook 2007 for sending & receiving Outlook.com email then

  • Search the Office Outlook 2007 section of the forum for existing guidance.
  • Start a question of your own [Click on Ask a question at the top of that page or just use this link - Ask a question]
  • In your question title, make it clear that the problem is Setting up MS Office Outlook 2007 for sending & receiving Outlook.com pop email
  • State whether or not your Outlook.com account has been "migrated" - use section 2 of this article to identify this.
  • Insert a link to this article by copying the rest of this line into your question - Setting up MS Office Outlook 2007 for sending & receiving Outlook.com email
  • State which steps [which numbered paragraphs] you have been able to complete successfully and which you have not. 
  • Copy any text & diagrams from this article that help you to explain your problem.
  • In the dropdown category selections underneath your question, check / select Office, Office 2007, Outlook. 

If you have a suggestion for improving this Forum article then please post a comment below.

Denis


Details

1  Outlook 2007 settings in Windows 7 & Windows 10. 

1.1  I use pop access to retrieve email.  I have never had the "Outlook Connector" / "Hotmail Connector" installed. 

1.2  All Outlook.com accounts are being migrated to different MS servers.  This is happening on a rolling basis with no published timescale or even any prior warning. 

1.3  You can set up a new email account or reconfigure an existing email account in Outlook 2007 by following the steps below. 

  • go to Tools, Account settings, New... 

  • choose an email service type

  • enter a name, email address & password

then follow either para 1.4 or para 1.5 below.

1.4  Exchange accounts only  This article is about setting up email for pop retrieval not about Exchange accounts.  I have included this short reference to Exchange accounts merely to explain what would happen if you clicked on the Next button in the Add new email account dialog box above.  I am not trying to suggest that doing so is worthwhile.

  • Setting an account up to use Exchange ["EAS"] allows synchronisation of many additional features such as calendars. 
  • Outlook 2007 can only handle a single Exchange account.  To use more than one you would have to create a new profile and would have to switch between profiles to use different email accounts. I think this is too awkward for routine use.  I believe that Outlook 2010 onwards can have multiple Exchange accounts.
  • There is guidance on setting up Exchange accounts in  Outlook 2007 Set up email  and  Set up email on an Android phone or tablet - Office Support.

Before setting up an email account as an Exchange account for the first time, ensure that you have backed up any emails that remain on the server because several users have reported disruption from the changeover.

To set up the email account as an Exchange account, click on the Next button in the Add new email account dialog box above.  That's it.  No further setting up is required.

1.5  Pop accounts  You can set email accounts up to use pop retrieval by following these steps:-

1.5.1  Initial settings dialog - Tools, Account settings, Email tab.

 

After migration, the required pop3 & smtp entries are supposed to change to pop-mail.live.com and smtp-mail.live.com [see para 2.2 below] but I found that I did not have to alter my existing settings.  If I do get any problems then I will change them.

1.5.2  More settings, General tab

1.5.3  More settings, Outgoing server tab

1.5.4  More settings, Connection tab

1.5.5  More settings, Advanced tab

 

After migration, the required pop3 server port entry 995 does not change but the smtp server port entry should change to 587 [see para 2.2 below] but I found that I did not have to alter my existing setting.  If I do get any problems then I will change the smtp port entry.

1.5.6  After setting up the email account, you can also change the folder that you want its emails delivered to by using the Change folder button shown in the lower section of the Tools, Account settings, Email tab.  You could choose to set up all your email accounts to use the same InBox or you could choose different folders.

2  Online settings  For pop retrieval, you also need to log in online to authorise pop downloading.  Log in then click on the icon for account settings [the gearwheel] and select Options.

2.1  Non-migrated account [indicated by the title bar entry Outlook.com in the online account] 

For a non-migrated account, the setting is in Options, Managing your account, Connect devices and apps with POP.

Just set pop to enabled then decide whether or not you want email deleted from the server once it has been downloaded.  This setting also exists within Outlook 2007's email settings [it is shown in the para 1.5.5 diagram] so make sure both settings are the same.  I do not know what happens if the settings are different.

2.2  Migrated account [indicated by the title bar entry Outlook Email in the online account] 

For a migrated account, the setting is in Options, Mail, Accounts, Pop & imap.

Just set pop to enabled then decide whether or not you want email deleted from the server once it has been downloaded.  This setting also exists within Outlook 2007's email settings [it is shown in the para 1.5.5 diagram] so make sure both settings are the same.  I do not know what happens if the settings are different.

3  Migration happened by surprise.  There was no warning email.  One day I found that one of my outlook.com email accounts kept giving receive errors in Outlook 2007 so I logged in online & discovered that it had been migrated.  I now had a mixture of both migrated & non-migrated Outlook.com email accounts being managed successfully by Outlook 2007. 

3.1  If it has been installed, the Outlook Connector /  Hotmail Connector must be removed when the account is migrated to the new MS servers.  I had never installed the Connector so I do not know any details about it.

3.2  I logged in online to re-authorise pop email retrieval [para 2.2 above].  I then logged out and returned to Outlook 2007 on my computer. 

3.3  I went to Tools, Accounts, Email, <selected the relevant email account>, Repair, Next and let it run through its test & adjustment procedure.  The account then started working correctly. 

3.4  When I checked all my Outlook 2007 settings, I found that they had not changed.

3.5  One side-effect of migration is that the online account now shows what the recommended pop & smtp server settings are so we can check them very easily.

3.6  I also had a look at the article Outlook.com migration changes - What do I need to know and do but the only difference was that they suggested it would be easier to delete the original Outlook 2007 email account then create a new one instead of just adjusting the existing one.

4  Pop & imap email  There is a summary of the differences between these two systems at What are IMAP and POP? - Office Help 

4.1  If you set up Outlook 2007 to use pop email, the emails are downloaded onto your computer. 

  • As shown in paras 1.5.5 & 2 above, you can choose to leave your emails on the server or delete them once downloaded. 
  • If you want to access your email from more than one computer / device then you would have to decide how to delete old emails from the server yourself. 
  • Personally, I have one computer set up to delete emails from the server [so it is, in effect, the master computer] but the other computer & my smartphone leave emails in place on the server.  I therefore check my email before leaving my master computer, I use the others to check for new emails while I am away then, when I return, I recheck email on the master computer [which deletes them from the server].

4.2  If you set up Outlook 2007 to use imap email, the emails are viewed from Outlook 2007 but remain on the server. 

  • Imap emails cannot use common InBox & Sent items folders if there are multiple email accounts set up in Outlook 2007.  Each imap email account needs its own InBox folder & its own Sent items folder in Outlook 2007 so that it can sync between them & its server.
  • The emails are synced when you have an internet connection and, once synced, the emails can still be viewed in Outlook 2007 whether the internet is still connected or not.
  • You can download copies of emails to a device but they still remain on the server.
  • Email attachments can also be downloaded but this is done as a separate action as I understand it but I imagine that I am merely missing an imap setting somewhere.
  • This system allows you to have several devices accessing your email without much risk of missing anything. 
  • To remove emails requires special procedures.  See Purge IMAP items marked for deletion - Office Help and note that purging them from the server also deletes local copies on your device[s].  "Deleting" emails is merely an interim step, it marks emails for deletion but only purging actually removes them.
  • The information provided in the [post-migration] Options section of the online Outlook.com account implies that Outlook 2007 can be set up to use imap email just by replacing the pop settings with the IMAP settings shown in the diagram at para 2.2 above.  The smtp settings do not change.
  • If you use a mixture of pop & imap settings to retrieve emails on several devices then any pop email decisions about deleting emails from their server could be disruptive therefore such interactions between devices need to be checked.
  • I have found disputed claims that changing from pop to imap resets the server so all emails must be checked before any such change so that any copies left on the server are merely duplicates. 

4.3   I also noticed some time ago that when I set up GMail to allow pop email retrieval, it referred to pop as being an insecure method so I looked into the subject.

  • pop is insecure in that others can listen in to internet connections and so read pop email as it is downloaded. 
  • Imap, on the other hand, uses secure internet connections [just as https internet addresses do]. 
  • However, I have also seen lots of online discussions referring to the security imap uses as being ineffective. 

Given the contradictory information I found, I decided to stick with pop retrieval and to continue never putting any sensitive information in emails. 

5  Android, other devices & other email apps  I just copy the settings I have used in Outlook 2007 to my Android smartphone's default email app and it works.  There are instructions for doing this, as well as guidance for alternative devices & apps, at Set up an email app with Outlook.com - Outlook.com Help  whilst direct web access to the Outlook.com account is discussed at Welcome to Outlook on the web - Office Support

norman david jones say:

Hi JOGENDRAKAPAL,

Right-click the worksheet's tab, select View Code and paste the following code:

'==========>>

Option Explicit

'---------->>
Private Sub Worksheet_SelectionChange(ByVal Target As Range)
    Dim srcRng As Range, destrng As Range, Rng As Range
    Const sDest As String = "W6"
    Const sSource As String = "A1:A20"                               '<<==== Change to suit 

    With Me
        Set srcRng = .Range(sSource)
        Set destrng = .Range(sDest)
    End With

    Set Rng = Intersect(srcRng, Target)
    If Not Rng Is Nothing Then
        destrng.Value = Rng.Value
    End If
End Sub
'<<==========

Change the value of sSource to reflect the list range.

Alt-Q to return to the worksheet.

How do i move the Home, Favorites, and Tools icons to the left side of - Microsoft Community

shmince say:

How do i move the Home, Favorites, and Tools icons to the left side of my screen?

Upon downloading of the new IE9 our family found that the Home, Favorites, and Tools icons have moved from the left side of the screen to the right side, and we can't figure out how to move them back.  Sifting through forums on others sites has given the impression that there is no fix. (We are aware of the "Pin" feature that opens Favorites on the left, but the icons remain on the right).  If there is a solution, please post it here; if there is no solution, please consider the following reasons for creating one:

 

The Practical Reason

The browser design of IE as well as the web page design of most sites orient the majority of their clickable buttons and menus on the left side of the screen; that's one big reason why we liked the Home, Favorites, and Tools icons on the left side as well.  With a 24 inch monitor, zipping from one side of the screen to the other and back again 500 times per day is inconvenient.

 

The More Important Reason

We like it on the left.  As the technology is improving, it seems only logical that the options available to the end users to customize their online experience should be improving as well.  Why force changes in formatting onto customers who enjoy the formatting as it is without giving those customers the option to maintain the web experience as they enjoy it?
