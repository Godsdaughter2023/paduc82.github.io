martin birrane say: Hi Vidz SH,
  The only factor I can think of to explain your short battery life may be down to the 64bit install resetting your "Power Options". Try setting it back to "Power saver" for maximum battery life.
Control Panel\All Control Panel Items\Power Options

The 64bit install may also have wiped out some drivers optimized for Win7. I'd check your laptop manufacturer's website for 64 bit drivers - install those and any other software updates available specifically for Win7 64 bit for your laptop

Lastly the install may have affected your laptop's screen brightness settings - some laptops give you the option to tone down your brightness. If you've noticed that the screen is brighter than it normally was using Win32 bit - it would be a good idea to use your laptop specific brightness controls to tone the brightness down.

If none of the above works a clean install using Win7 64 bit paired with the correct drivers is your best bet.

This issue might occur if the Microsoft account is not synchronized properly. Hence all the applications which requires Microsoft account and password to access is also not synchronized.

I suggest you to convert your account into Local account and then change it back to Microsoft account. This will help your Microsoft account to get synchronized properly.

Follow the steps to do so:

1. Click on the Start Menu and select Settings.

2. Now select Accounts.

3. Click on the Your e-mail and accounts.

4. Now click on the option Sign in with a Local Account instead.

This will change the Microsoft Account into local Account. Now restart and follow the same steps to convert the user account into Microsoft Account by selecting the option Sign in with Microsoft Account instead. Once you select the option, follow the onscreen instruction and enter your correct Microsoft Account e-mail address and password.
