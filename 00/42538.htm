slance310 say:

Hi,


The KEY is located in the Registry which is basically a listing of virtually everything in your machine, and the conditions under which they function.


To get to it, right click on the Windows Icon in the lower left corner and select  "RUN", type in regedit and hit OK. You will see several folders, one of which is HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE. Click on the arrow next to the folder to expand it, and keep going down the list for the next links....


When you get to {4D36E965-E325-11CE-BFC1-08002BE10318} click on it. The upper filters and a bunch of other stuff will appear on the right side of the window....


Be careful here as any error could make your machine useless..... It would be a good idea to right click on the COMPUTER at the top of the registry when you first open it and select "EXPORT".

This will make a backup file of your entire registry... just in case...


You can also do the same thing to the {4D36E965-E325-11CE-BFC1-08002BE10318} key to backup just that small part. Name them anything you want, just so that it will be easy to remember what they are for...


There are things that can cause this that can sometimes be fixed without messing with the registry...

A problem with older versions of iTunes is one of them...


Sometimes the name in the upper and lower filters will give a good clue as to what program\device is causing the problem. If so, you try a newer version to see if that works....


Sometimes just deleting a filter will cause a program to stop working, so looking foran updated version may b a better solution, but not always possible.



Also... Toshiba machines have  programs called

TOSHIBA Recovery Media Creator

TOSHIBA Disc Creator


that need to be uninstalled

mohdrafi say: Method One

1/ Right Click on a vacant part of the desktop .

2/ From the Drop Down menu , Click the View option.

3/ In the View context menu , Click one of the following options:

  • Large Icons.
  • Medium Icons.
  • Classic Icons.

The Classic Icons option will reduce the size the most.

Method Two

If you really want to control the size of the icons on the desktop then here is a really simple tweak to do just that.

1/ Make sure the desktop is in focus. In other words click on the desktop button to bring the desktop to the front (assuming you have other programs open).

2/ Place your mouse on a vacant part of the desktop and then Hold down the CTRL (control) key.

3/ Keep the CTRL key pressed down, and then, with your mouse scroll button, Scroll the mouse scroll wheel up or down to increase or decrease the size of the icons.

4/ Once the icons are at the desired size, release the CTRL button.

5/ Your desktop icons should now appear in a more appropriate size.

lemp say:
If you have Vista -- even though you posted in a Windows XP forum -- dwm.exe is the Vista Desktop Window Manager and is responsible for the graphical effects in Microsoft Windows Vista operating system such as 3D effects, live windows previews and windows transparencies.

What version of Windows do you have, including service pack?

Ignore anything in C:\Windows\prefetch ... those are not "real" files but are used by Windows to speed loading of applications.  Do not delete anything from \prefetch with the idea that doing so will improve performance.  It won't.

You say that your anti-virus software warns about this file but then say that "Avast doesn't see it as a virus when scanned."  If you are running two anti-virus applications simultaneously, don't.  Pick one.

What antivirus and antimalware applications are you using?  Which are configured to run all the time (i.e., in the background scanning)?

What program is complaining about dwm.exe and what is the exact message?

Finally, it's always possible that malware has disguised itself using a legitimate file name.  Where is dwm.exe located (other than \prefetch)?  Right-click and take a look at its properties and report what you see.  If it's not in C:\Windows\system32, it probably IS malware.
