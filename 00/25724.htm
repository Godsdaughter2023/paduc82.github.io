sanwin2 say:

The question is did you close down Word and then eject the USB stick prior to removing it?

Word and Windows both use temporary files and can take a while to shut these down.  This is one reason that the advice always given is to save Word documents to a hard drive and then copy them to USB after closing Word.

I am afraid that you may have removed the USB stick prior to all the writes to it being completed - thus corrupting the file you were working on.

allibaster12 say: Did you safely remove the USB prior to removing it from the PC?  If not, and you just pulled it out without properly ejecting it, you most likely corrupted the document you were working on.  In the future, always safely eject the drive before removing it from the PC.

stefan blom say:
You should never save directly from Word to removable media, because doing so will lead to a corrupt document. Instead, save to the hard drive and then copy to the removable media as needed.

graham mayor say:
As others have indicated saving (opening and printing) Word documents directly to or from removable media will lead to document corruption. Always copy to and from the hard drive to work with documents. http://www.gmayor.com/SaveInTwoPlacesAddIn.htm will facilitate the copying of the active document to removable media.
