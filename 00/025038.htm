danida_u say:

Hi there jeeung and welcome to the new home of the Skype Community!

You have two options on how to change your Skype account's display name, either through the Skype client or through the Skype website.

To change your display name using your Skype client, follow these:

1. Open up the Skype application and click on your profile picture.

2. On the right pane of the client, you will see your account details, above your Skype name will be your account's display name, click on it and the display name will be highlighted.

Note: To distinguish the Skype name and the display name,  your Skype name will be after the word Accounts while your display name will be in a larger font size.

3. Change the display name to a name you prefer and click on the check icon on the right side of the box.

To change your display name on the Skype website,

1. Log in to your account using to the Skype website: Skype website

2. Click on Sign in and choose My Account.

3 Scroll down and locate Edit profile under Account details.

4. Under Personal information, you will see your current display name, click on Edit profile on the right part of the page.

5. Enter your desired display name and click on Save.

Note: If this doesn't work on your current browser, you might want to try to use a different browser to change your name.

Hope this helps! Let me know if you have encountered any issues further so I can check on this.

Last Name = Smith

Last Name = Skype

tnpir4000 say:

Windows 10 doesn't work from license keys anymore--it scans your system and ties its activation to your specific hardware configuration.  If your hardware upgrades were significant enough, the hash code for your system will have changed, and Windows thinks it's running on a different system.

The question is--what hardware did you upgrade?  Most of the time when this happens a change to the motherboard is the culprit, but it can happen if you change enough of the peripherals too.

In any case, I suggest you start by trying the activation troubleshooter.

cbarnhorst say:

Any license key "verified" in January 2015 would be invalid starting in the late Summer of 2015.  Those special keys were retired immediately and cannot be used to activate Windows any more.  At that time MSFT adopted "digital entitlement" for licensing all users upgrading to Windows 10 from Windows 7 and 8.1.  All insiders who had been using the insider product keys used before then were advised to use their Windows 7 or 8.1 product keys to re-activate Windows 10.  That procedure was only good until July 29, 2016.  Those who did not do so or had no Windows 7 or 8.1 product keys were advised that they could continue to receive insider builds for as long as they did not clean re-install Windows 10 or opt out of the Insider program.  Doing so would require purchase of Windows 10 to continue.

Further, if by "significant hardware upgrade" you mean replacing the motherboard and cpu, be advised that doing that would cause your Windows license to become invalid because the Get Windows 10 free offer was an upgrade license good only for the life of the device.  New mobo, new device, old device gone, license tied to it gone.
