doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

While this does not address your issue, do not be lulled into a false sense of security by the autosave feature.  Contrary to popular opinion, Word has no integral means of automatically saving the current document.  What it does have is an option to save AutoRecover information after a specific and configurable interval and it is the version of the document that is saved by that process that will appear if the Word program terminates in an abnormal manner.

You may find the save performance improved by going to File>Options>Advanced>Show document content and temporarily checking the box for "Show picture placeholders".

I realize that your question, or complaint is probably mostly rhetorical, but here are some things to consider.

Publisher as a basic DTP may not have the template you want, but it has the tools you need to build your own.

It appears that a 'flat plan' is a single sheet and perhaps a double page. You can start with a single sheet layout but if you want to print your publication after you design it, there is no easy way in Publisher to convert a single sheet to a booklet format without copy and pasting the content.

Perhaps start with a Catalog template or a plain booklet of the size you want. If you want to design and layout specific size advertising spaces, text boxes or other page components for different content you can design each design element once, group it together > right click and Save as a Building Block. Now in all future publications you can go to Insert > Page Parts and you will have quick access to all those previously designed components.

Of course once you have your 'flat plan' publication designed you can also save it as a personal template.

You might also check out the Catalog Merge functionality in Publisher. It might be helpful in designing and producing your publication. I noticed there are several YouTube tutorials on the subject if you want more information.

Again Publisher may not offer the specialized template you want, but if you roll up your sleeves you can design most anything you want. If you don't want to build your own template with Publisher I noticed there are some specialized programs available for 'flat plan' production when I googled to find out what the term meant.
