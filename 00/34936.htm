[Guides] Forza Horizon 3 3 Stars Guide

Speed Traps 

Getting 3 stars on all Forza Horizon 3 Speed Traps is easier than getting 3 stars on Drift Zones and Danger Signs PR Stunts; however, some of the Speed Traps Stunts in the game may require multiple retries.

In order to help you achieve this task we have created the following Forza Horizon 3 guide which reveals useful tips and a video showing how we got 3 Stars

on these specific Forza Horizon 3 Stunts.

The video game developed by Playground Games for PC and Xbox One, includes 29 Speed Traps.

On the map, a Speed Trap location is marked by an icon showing a road safety camera, as you can see in the image above.

 

 

To perform this type of stunt you have to drive as fast as you can through the road safety camera.

Similar to the Speed Zones, each Speed Trap in Forza Horizon 3 has 3 difficulty levels for which you will be awarded one, two or three stars.

Obviously, for the first two levels the requirements are lower, but for the last one you will have to drive very fast.

Here are some of the factors that will affect your speed while driving through a FH 3 Speed Trap.

First and the most important is the car you use. For your convenience, in our video guide we also reveal the models and the upgrades used by us when working on these PR Stunts.

The terrain is the second most important factor you have to consider when attempting a Speed Trap in FH 3.

Based on the first two factors we recommend buying the Ferrari F12TDF for the Speed Traps placed on asphalt and the Lamborghini Aventador LP700-4 for the traps placed on dirt and sand.

The upgrades you buy for the cars you play with, are very important as well. Look for upgrades that maximize your car’s speed, acceleration and launch, instead of braking.

Your starting point and the route you follow are also crucial for these stunts. Try starting from an area placed in a straight line with the Speed Trap in order to avoid unnecessary brakes, and pay close attention to the environment.

Avoid the traffic as much as you can or you will fail getting 3 stars. When you have to choose between hitting an opponent or hitting the brakes, always choose the latter.

Therefore, before running through a Forza Horizon 3 Speed Trap make sure you:

  • Check the leaderboards and see what cars have been used by other players to beat the required scores.
  • Watch our video guide below and pay attention to our starting points and the routes we have followed.
  • Upgrade your cars based on the terrain.
  • Use the rewind button every time you hit an opponent or object.
  • Practice and be patient. These stunts are accessible even to new drivers.
Speed Zones

The difference between Forza Horizon 3 Speed Zones and FH 3 Speed Traps is that for the first type of PR Stunts, you have to maintain a cumulative speed which has to be higher than the one required by the said stunt.

Simplified, this means that for a Speed Zone Stunt, the score is determined by your overall speed while driving on a specific segment of a road.

 

 

These PR Stunts are more difficult than the Speed Traps but easier than the Danger Signs and the Drift Zones available in the game.

In order to get three stars on such a stunt, you will have to beat the required speed, and below we will share several tips that might help you while working on achieving 3 Stars on all Forza Horizon 3 Speed Zones.

Forza Horizon 3 for PC and Xbox One features 21 Speed Zones, which are marked on your map by icons showing two road safety cameras, similar to those in the image above.

When you scroll on one of these icons you will notice a marked section which represents the zone where you have to drive as fast as possible.

Your performance on these Forza Horizon 3 Stunts is affected by multiple factors and the most important one is the car you decide to use. This factor works in correlation with the second one, which is the terrain.

While in the video guide below we use various models, we suggest you to try the 2012 Lamborghini Aventador LP700-4 for Speed Zones on difficult terrains (mud, sand and dirt) and the 2015 Koenigsegg One: 1 for Speed Zones placed on asphalt.

The second car is quite expensive, but you can use our method to get fast credits in Forza Horizon 3, if you don’t have enough funds. Alternatively, you can get more CR from the free Wheelspins awarded when you level up.

To level up faster you can start smashing XP Boards or simply use our Fast XP method.

Obviously, the car you decide to drive must be upgraded accordingly; therefore, you should maximize your car’s speed, acceleration and launch.

The launch is important because some of the Speed Zones feature multiple turns, thus you will be forced to hit the brakes often.

When you play a Speed Zone Stunt you have to remember that you are allowed to leave the road but you are not allowed to get too far from it. This means that you are also allowed to cut corners as long as you don’t go too far.

This trick will help you conserve your speed so remember it whenever you see a corner on the road.

Another important factor that will determine your overall speed is your starting point. It is a good idea to pick a starting point located in a straight line and as far as possible, because if you enter a Speed Zone at high speed, you won’t have to worry about the speed you might lose when hitting the brakes.

Remember these five tips if you wish to get 3 Stars on all Forza Horizon 3 Speed Zones:

  • Always check the leaderboards to see what cars have been used by other players.
  • Cut the corners if possible but stay close to the marked route.
  • Avoid all obstacles you might encounter.
  • Upgrade your cars accordingly.
  • Enter each zone as fast as possible.

Last but not least, be patient and watch our video guide below to see our starting points

, as well as the cars and the upgrades that helped us achieve 3 Stars on all 21 Forza Horizon 3 Speed Zones.

Danger Sign

    Drift Zones

    Getting 3 stars on all Forza Horizon 3 Drift Zones is almost as hard as getting 3 stars in the Danger Signs PR Stunts and the tips and tricks in the guide below are meant to help you achieve those stars.

    Unlike most of Forza Horizon 3 Speed Zones and FH3 Speed Traps, which are easier to complete with 3 stars, the Drift Zones Stunts will require quite a few tries, so be patient before attempting them.

    There are 17 Forza Horizon 3 Drift Zones PR Stunts in the video game developed by Playground Games for PC and Xbox One.

     

     

    To access all Drift Zones you have to upgrade each Horizon Festival location to its max level, unlike the Barn Finds, Reward Boards or Beauty Spots which are revealed by world discovery.

    The Forza Horizon 3 Speed Zones are marked on the map with an icon depicting a sliding car and when you hover over one of these icons it will highlight the area in which you are allowed to perform the drifts.

    On the actual road, such a zone is marked by green flags on both sides of the road. Your drift is calculated as soon as you hit the area between the first two flags, hence it’s a good idea to begin the drift a bit earlier in order to maximize the amount you can get in the zone. Also, it doesn’t matter from which end you begin the drift, so experiment until you find what’s the most comfortable path for you.

    To initiate a drift you press the E-brake button while turning left or right on your joystick.

    Then immediately you have to counter-steer in the opposite direction to maintain control.

    Press the throttle gently to prevent over-steering.

    Moreover, don’t approach a Drift Zone at a high speed, because you will overshoot the designated area and you will fail the drift. While it is safe to decelerate or even stop inside a zone (though not recommended), you will also fail the drift if you turn around the car.

    To make a car drift with ease you can turn off the Traction Control and the Stability Controlsettings in the difficulty menu.

    An advanced setting would be to turn the shifting to manual. This way you can keep the car in the desired gear all the time. When the RPM drops significantly shift to a lower gear to keep the wheels spinning and prevent them from gaining grip.

    When you play solo use the rewind function to save some time and return to the start position fast. However, the Drivatars are the most annoying thing while playing solo, thus you can ask a friend to play in a private online freeroam session where you will not be bothered by AI traffic.

    While your skill can be an important factor in the Forza Horizon 3 Drift Zones, the car you decide to use is the essential one and can save you from a lot of frustration while attempting the PR Stunts.

    If you are a beginner don’t jump to the fastest and most powerful car. Instead, choose a B-class or lower tier car that has a rear wheel drive (RWD). Tune up its power to make it more potent.

    Invest in handling, launch and acceleration, because you don’t need top speed when you drift. Alternatively you can try a AWD car (all wheel drive), but it tends to have more grip which is not a good thing if you intend to drift.

    If you don’t know how to fine tune your car for drifting search for a custom upgrade/tune file that has been developed by other players. Usually, hardcore players know their way around a car and will amaze you with the results.

    We got 3 stars on all 17 Forza Horizon 3 Drift Zones using a 1974 Toyota Celica GT, which was tuned using a AWD Drift Tune. You can check the upgrade file that we downloaded in the video below.

    While it can seem underwhelming at C-class we can assure you it was fine tuned to have an insane amount of power. You can also use the video if you want to see other cars suitable for drifting, along with the suggested tuning options.

    The terrain is another factor that you should take into account. The car behaves differently on asphalt and gravel. When the rain starts it affects all the car physics and you will lose control more easily. The night makes the matter even worse due to low visibility.

    To get around the issue use the next trick. Fast travel to an already discovered Beauty Spot and the weather and time of day will change instantly. We suggest using the one at the crossroad in the Outback since it will make the weather sunny. Then return to the Drift Zone.

    Take these tips into consideration when attempting to get 3 Stars on all Forza Horizon 3 Drift Zones:

    • Check the global leaderboards to see what cars (and their performance index) have been used by other players.
    • Properly tune your chosen car.
    • Use the rewind button if you hit any obstacles.
    • Be gentle with the controls, especially the throttle.
    • Practice a lot and be patient.

Make the changes, save your work, and exit.  Put the CD in the drive and reboot.  When prompted, push any key to boot from the CD.

    If you don't have either disk, you can make a bootable Recovery Disk using     http://neosmart.net/blog/2008/windows-vista-recovery-disc-download/ along with burning software like: http://www.snapfiles.com/get/active-isoburner.html and, of course, a blank CD.

    Go to Start / All Programs / Accessories / Command prompt and right click on command prompt and click run as Administrator.

    Type sfc /scannow and enter and let it run.  It will scan and try to fix some of your system files.  Hopefully it will complete with no corruption it could not repair (if there is such corruption post back here or try to analyze it to find the problem file(s) using     http://support.microsoft.com/kb/928228.  If there are corrupt files, try to identify them by name.

    If that doesn't work, then please post any repeating error messages from the Event Viewer concerning name changes (Start / Control Panel / Administrative Tools / Event Viewer).  Here's how to use Event Viewer: http://www.bleepingcomputer.com/forums/topic40108.html.  Post the results here unless there are too many - then just post a sampling of those you think are most relevant.

If the other procedures don't work, THIS one is almost certain to work.  You may have a lot of updates to re-install (including any service packs you had to remove).  If the version on the system cane with SP1 or SP2 pre-installed and the disk is an earlier version, then you'll need to make a slipstream disk as follows: http://www.vistax64.com/tutorials/151606-vista-sp1-slipstream-installation-dvd.html.
