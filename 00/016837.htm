The difference you see in Office documents between .DOC and .DOCX is due to the versions of Office in which the document was created.

karl timmermans [outlook mvp 2012-2018] say:

This is one of those cases, where you can either <import from> Outlook Express into Outlook using the Outlook import process or <Export to> Outlook from Outlook Express.

Son't have a system to test that has multiple OE identified populated to know for sure what gets exracted by the Outlook import process. However going from OE to Outlook, the following should work

---------------------------
If you have created multiple Outlook profiles - each using its own PST file, then the Inbox folder for each profile will be unique. Hence, the following should work

a) set the default profile in Outlook to correspond to its matching identity in OE
b) export from OE to Outlook
c) repeat the process for each identity changing the default profile in Outlook accordingly
---------------------------

There is one major difference between OE and Outlook in terms of the "AddressBook". The AB in OE (or in Outlook parlance - the contacts) is shared among identities whereas in Outlook, the AddressBook (which in Outlook is actually a "view" of one or more contact folders designated to as "Show as an email addressbook" is unique to each Outlook profile UNLESS, the contacts are maintained in a PST/Folder common to all profiles.

Rather than continue writing an encylopedia of information which may or may not be relevant - the first question is "Does each identify in your OE scenario really have their own set of contacts on a logical basis regardless of whether those are contained centrally in OE?"

Understanding the Outlook AddressBook
http://www.contactgenie.info/understanding-outlook-addressbook

One point to keep in mind - the "best way" to ensure that things will work the way you want is to simply test everything given that both OE and Outlook are on the same machine. Exporting anything from OE to Outlook (via OE) or importing anything from OE into Outlook (via Outlook) does not delete anything. If things don't work the way you want, then it's a simple matter of creating a new Outlook profile and starting over. Given the age of OE - that's what anyone here would have to do just to answer the questions with any degree of certainty unless they are still using or have recently stopped using OE (doubt that to be the case for most of people who respond here on any kind of regular basis).
