You've pretty much backed yourself into a corner with those requirements.

You can try applying a "condensed" setting to the one merge field that may contain a long entry, to make the longest value fit onto one line. To condense the text, select the merge field, right-click it and click Font, go to the Advanced tab of the Font dialog, and set the Spacing to Condensed by some amount -- try 0.5 pt. Hi JackOrsi,

 

Welcome to Microsoft Community. I understand that you wanted to lessen the spam emails that you are receiving in your account.

 

I highly recommend that you block the sender's address or create an email rule to stop receiving email messages to certain senders. Follow the steps below on how to do this:

 

To block a sender's address:

 

1. In your Outlook.com Inbox, click the cog icon beside your Profile name.

2. Click Mail more settings.

3. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.

4. Click Blocked senders.

5.Type in the domain address in the Blocked e-mail address or domain: field (ex. @lizy.com).

6.Click the Add to list >> button.

7. Repeat steps 4 and 5 to add more entries in the "Blocked senders:" window.

 

To create an email rule:

 

1. In your Outlook inbox, click the cog icon beside your Profile name.

2. Click Mail more settings.

3. Under Customizing Outlook, click Rules for sorting a new messages.

4. Click New.

5. Under Step 1. Which messages do you want this rule to apply to? Select Subject, then Contains word, then on the text box provided; type in the subject line that you are frequently receiving. (You can change this options using the dropdown provided depends on your choice)

6. Under Step 2. What action do you want to apply? Tick on Delete these messages—don't even put them in my Deleted folder.

7. Click Save.

 

You can also set your email filter to Exclusive level. Only messages from your contact list and safe senders will be forwarded to your inbox folder. To do this, follow the steps below:

 

1. In your Outlook.com Inbox, click the cog icon beside your Profile name.

2. Click Mail more settings.

3. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.

4. Under Choose a junk e-mail filter, choose Exclusive then Save.

 

Good luck and let me know how it turns out for you.

 

Thank you.
