Can I have 2 auto-correct dictionaries open at the same time, or can i - Microsoft Community

mmshrinker say:

Can I have 2 auto-correct dictionaries open at the same time, or can i combine the dictionaries (English)?

I'm referring specifically to English (UK) and English (USA) - i would like to be able to combine those entries ithat I have inputted manually to the dictionaries.  At the moment, English (UK) contains some of my personalised autocorrect entries and English (USA) contains others.

jay freedman say:

Any time you type the "name" of an AutoCorrect entry in your document, Word consults the AutoCorrect list of the language that's the proofing language applied to the current cursor location. It won't look first in one "dictionary" (ACL file) and then in another to find a match. The idea of having two or more AutoCorrect lists "open" simultaneously just doesn't apply.

It's fairly cumbersome to move or copy entries from one language to another. The process to copy an entry from the English (US) list to the English (UK) list is this:

  • Apply the English (US) proofing language to a paragraph in a document.
  • Type the name of the AutoCorrect entry followed by a space to expand it.
  • Select the paragraph and apply the English (UK) proofing language to it.
  • Reduce the selection to just the expanded AutoCorrect, without the space or paragraph mark.
  • Go to File > Options > Proofing > AutoCorrect Options (where the selected text will be in the "With" box), type the name in the "Replace" box, make sure the Plain Text option button is selected, click the Add button, and click OK.

Repeat this operation for each entry that exists only in the US list. If you often write in English (US) text, then also move the uniquely English (UK) entries to the US list.

If there are a great many entries to be duplicated in either direction, it may be worth the effort of writing a macro to do it.

Refer below image:

The above returns the Largest 3 by Amount (column D).

Enter below formula in cell I2 & copy down:

=IF(ROWS($D$2:$D2)>3,"",LARGE($D$2:$D$100,ROWS($D$2:$D2)))

To return the Largest 10 values, just replace 3 by 10 in the formula.

Enter below formula as an array formula (CTRL+SHIFT+ENTER) in cell F2 & copy down & to the right columns till column H:

=IF($I2="","",INDEX(A$2:A$100,SMALL(IF($D$2:$D$100=$I2,ROW($D$2:$D$100)-ROW($D$2)+1),COUNTIF($I$1:$I2,$I2))))
