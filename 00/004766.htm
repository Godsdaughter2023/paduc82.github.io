doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

Assuming that you are using Content Controls and that the number is at the end of each of the DropDown list entries, if you use DD1, DD2, DD3 as the titles for the Content Controls and where you want the total to appear in the document, you insert a { DOCVARIABLE Total } field, using CTRL+F9 to insert the { } inside of which you type the DOCVARIABLE Total, and in the Visual Basic Editor, you insert the following code in the ThisDocument object

Private Sub Document_ContentControlOnExit(ByVal CC As ContentControl, Cancel As Boolean)

Dim Total As Long
Total = 0
With ActiveDocument
    For Each CC In .ContentControls
        If Left(CC.Title, 2) = "DD" Then
            Total = Total + Val(Right(CC.Range, 1))
        End If
    Next CC
    .Variables("Total").Value = Total
    .Range.Fields.Update
End With

End Sub

as you exit from each of the Content Controls, the total will be progressively updated to show the sum at that time of the items selected in the dropdowns.

Note, if you have other Content Controls in the document, do not assign titles to them that begin with DD.

Mail on device doesn't sync with Outlook - Microsoft Community

jbov say:

Mail on device doesn't sync with Outlook

Mail is handled completely separately on my HTC TouchPro and Outlook.  I'll receive messages on both, but they have to be deleted separately from the device and Outlook.  There is no synchonization once the messages are received.  Help ... this is very frustrating.

julie fka profjulie say: If you are using POP3 email accounts that you've created, the mail in those accounts will never sync with your computer.  The way to sync email between your computer and windows mobile device is to select the Email data type in ActiveSync or WMDC and to not program POP3 email accounts on your device.

If you select the Email data type on your device to sync your incoming and outgoing email between your computer and windows mobile device, email will be handled in a batch process.  The only way to get incoming email on your device is by syncing it from your computer and the only way to send email is through your computer.  You can reply to or create new email messages on your windows mobile device, but all that email will be saved in the Outbox until you sync to your computer.  Then the outbox items will be synced to your computer and sent from there.

If you want to download and send email directly from your windows mobile device, you'll set up POP3 messaging accounts on the device.  Email that is downloaded to your device will still be retained on the mail server, so when you turn on your computer, the email will also be downloaded to your computer.  Email that you create and send from your mobile device will never be synced to your computer. 

I prefer to manage mail directly from my windows mobile device, so I always bcc myself on any sent email.  Then this email will be downloaded to my computer later on and I'll have a complete history of email stored on my computer.
