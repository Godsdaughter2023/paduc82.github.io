Continuous device connect--device disconnect sound in Windows - Microsoft Community

william cook 3 say:

Continuous device connect--device disconnect sound in Windows

I have been using personal computers for many years and I have seen the subject raised repeatedly over the years.  There have been various explanations, but most seem to revolve around a device, most often, a USB device.

I went through trouble-shooting such an occurrence on my wife's computer and eventually solved the problem.  I had not seen this particular solution before, so I thought that I would offer it.

I will try and keep this short.  My wife's computer had 3 USB cables connected--a wired mouse (Logitech); a wired keyboard (HP); and a laser jet printer (Epson).  I had seen at least one instance where the discussion revolved around Logitech mice and basically mechanical failure.

Since I had seen the USB mouse discussion before, I swapped out the wired mouse for another wired mouse and there was no change.  Still the annoying sound...

Prior to my starting on the troubleshooting session, I had my wife open an email and she had complained that it would not let her type.  So, after trying the mouse exchange, I thought that I would try the keyboard.  I tried to type in email and then MS Word.  I could not type in either application.

So, I disconnected her wired USB keyboard and connected what I knew to be a good wired keyboard...and, I could type in both email and Word.  Even more importantly, the computer stopped making the connect/disconnect noise.

I have never had the printed circuit board on a keyboard go out before...ever.  So, they can break down.

Everyone should be aware of this as a possible solution to the thoroughly annoying connect/disconnect sound that won't stop.
