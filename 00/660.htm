jay freedman say:
At the far right end of the Home tab, click Select, and choose Select Objects from the dropdown. Then you can drag a box around the text boxes you want to delete, and/or hold Ctrl while clicking another text box to add it to the selection. (Note for other readers: this doesn't work in Word 2010 unless you save the document as a Word 97-2003 (*.doc) file first.) Then press Delete.

The second part may be more trouble. The floating text boxes are anchored to a regular text paragraph, which you can see if you turn on nonprinting marks (click ¶ on the Home tab) and then click the edge of a text box -- there will be an anchor symbol in the left margin next to the paragraph that is the anchor location. Now...

IF the paragraph that was the anchor of the deleted text boxes is otherwise empty (that is, it's just a paragraph mark with no text and no other text boxes anchored to it), then you MIGHT be able to shift everything up by deleting the empty paragraph mark, I say MIGHT because the text boxes in the lower paragraphs could have the "move with text" setting in their properties turned off -- it's impossible to predict what Crystal Reports is doing with the properties until you try it.

Just as an aside, I'm disappointed (maybe disgusted) to hear that a reputable program like Crystal Reports is putting each value in a separate text box instead of using a table. There's no excuse for such sloppy, user-unfriendly programming.

If you must have it in a Word document, you can then copy/paste the table from Excel and it will paste as a table into word.

  Dim Base As Range
