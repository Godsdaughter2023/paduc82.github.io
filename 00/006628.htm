What you call the "unpacked" format is the old format from Onenote 2003 and 2007, when Onenote notebooks could be stored only locally (not in the cloud).

This format can still be used for local notebooks, by the Windows desktop clients Onenote 2010, 2013 and 2016 (only by the paid versions).

Local Onenote notebooks which are later moved to Onedrive get a format transformation into one file on Onedrive. This new format is usable by all Onenote clients.

aaron zhu msft say:

Hi Dean,

 

Yes, you can share specific folders with your colleagues. Firstly, please refer to the Sharing your folders using OWA part in the following article to share the permissions of the folders which you want others have access to with the rest 2 users. Please note, since there are some sub folders under the main folder, you also need to share the permissions of these sub folders. After that, let your colleagues refer to the Accessing another person's folder(s) using OWA part in the following article to add the shared folders.

 

Sharing An Office 365 Mail Folder With Another Office 365 User.
