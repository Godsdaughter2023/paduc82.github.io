It has been in this option dialog since at least Word 97. However, these references are to the Header Styles, not the running page header.  I know that this is confusing.

If for some reason you were to apply the Header Style to text in the body of your document, that will show up in the TOC. The TOC field will only pick up things that are in the text layer or "story" of a document. Thus, they also will miss styles in tables and textboxes (but not frames).

As a workaround. I would suggest using Heading 1 at the start of each section and having it show the text you want in your header. Perhaps have a different first page header set to have no header on that first page of a section. Use a StyleRef Field  to reflect the text from the Heading 1 in your header.

Most of what I know about Tables of Contents is summarized in How to create a table of contents in Microsoft Word by Shauna Kelly.

Another great page on the TOC is TOC Tips and Tricks by Suzanne Barnhill

My own thoughts can be found at Generating a Table of Contents - Complex Documents
