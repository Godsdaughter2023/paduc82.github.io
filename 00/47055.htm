Overview

If you see status code 80072741 when you’re using Xbox LIVE, it could mean one of these things:

  •    Some Xbox features are not available because of system maintenance or a temporary Xbox LIVE issue.
  •    You can't access some Xbox features because of a network configuration issue.
  •    There might be a problem with your payment method.

Problem

If you get this message and status code, it could mean one of these things:

  • Some Xbox LIVE features are not available because of system maintenance or a temporary Xbox LIVE issue.
  • You can't access some Xbox LIVE features because of a network configuration issue.
  • There might be a problem with your credit card. For example, your billing information might be incorrect, or your credit card might have expired.
Solutions

Solution 1: Check for Xbox LIVE service alerts or tests

To make sure there are no problems with the Xbox LIVE service, check the Service status page.

If there are no service alerts, there might be a problem with your connection. Try the next solution.

Solution 2: Test your connection

Test the Xbox LIVE connection on your Xbox 360 console:

  1. Press the Guide button on your controller.
  2. Go to Settings and then System Settings.
  3. Select Network Settings.
  4. Select Wired Network or the name of your wireless network, if you are prompted.
  5. Select Test Xbox LIVE Connection.
  6. Do one of the following:
  • If the test fails and you can't connect to Xbox LIVE, see Xbox LIVE connection problems.
  • If the Xbox LIVE service is available, try the next solution.
  • If there is an Xbox LIVE service alert, try again later when the Xbox LIVE service is available.

If you still get the error, try the next solution.

Solution 3: Open network ports

If you have a firewall or are connected to a workplace or school/college/university network, you might need to open network ports for your Xbox 360 console to communicate with Xbox LIVE. See Network ports used by Xbox LIVE for help opening network ports.

If you still get the error, try the next solution.

Solution 4: Delete your gamertag and clear your cache

  1. Unplug any accessories attached to your console, such as a headset, the Xbox 360 Chatpad or the Kinect Sensor.
  2. Delete your profile. For more information, see Download, move or delete your Xbox 360 profile.
  3. Clear your system cache twice. See How to clear your Xbox 360 system cache.
  4. Recover your gamertag. See Download, move or delete your Xbox LIVE profile.
  5. Restart your console.

If you still get the error, try the next solution.

Solution 5: Check your billing address and payment option expiry date

Make sure that the billing address for your Xbox LIVE account matches the billing address used by your credit card company. Also, make sure that your card’s expiration date has not passed.

To change the billing address for your Xbox LIVE account, see Change your Xbox LIVE account information.

Like you, I use my mobile as my internet connection for my laptop and I stop off at the local library for Windows Updates on my way to Sainsbury's.  But I do use my mobile to keep MS Security Essentials [MSSE] updated daily to give me half a chance of protecting the PC between trips.

As others have written, anti-malware applications such as AVG can protect you from a lot of attempts to plant malware on your PC.  However, if Windows or application components have security vulnerabilities then hackers can attempt to use what appears to be a perfectly valid communications exchange to corrupt those components to circumvent your protection.  Your anti-malware application might not react to the intrusion attempt if it disguises itself well even though AVG & MSSE do look for general malware-like activity not just the existence of known pieces of malware.  You need to keep Windows and all applications as up-to-date as possible so that these vulnerabilities are kept to a minimum.  You can never be certain that there are no vulnerabilities, all you can do is reduce your exposure to them.  Unfortunately, the publication of an update triggers an entry in a hacker's to-do-list in the hope of exploiting a vulnerability before people like you & me have made it to the library so we are pretty well forced to update fairly swiftly.
