Yes, I meant the "didn't". To clarify, the Different First Page option might have become checked when you copied material (including the section break at its end) from another document and pasted it into your document, not realizing that it had the option turned on. If you don't really want a different header or footer in that section, then you would need to turn it off.

As Charles says, you can stop the page numbers where you want. You'll need a section break between the end of the main text and the beginning of the end-matter. What you do next depends on what type of footer you want in the end-matter:
  • If you want no text in the footer, or completely different text: With the cursor in the footer of the first page of the end-matter, you must turn off the Link to Previous button on the Header & Footer Tools tab. If the end-matter has Different First Page enabled, then you must also go to the second page of the end-matter and turn off Link to Previous there.
  • If you want the page number in the same place and format, but you want it to start again at 1: With the cursor in the footer of the first page of the end-matter,go to the Header & Footer Tools tab and click the Page Number button; in the menu, click Format Page Numbers. In the dialog that opens, click the Start At option and select the starting page number. At the top of the dialog, you can also choose a different format (such as roman numerals).
