Sure, the general answer for such a question is Yes.

A formula in a conditional formating can also be used in normal cells, means you get a result of True or False for each conditional formating formula. You can combine the results of each formula with the OR function, e.g. =OR(A1,B1,C1)

After that you can filter your list based on these results and get the numbers you want.

That means that the formula, 1st quoted row, is applied to all the cells in the 2nd row.

To accomplish the same in a sheet, write the formula into any cell in the 3rd row, e.g. BA3

Then drag the formula down to row 25.

Do the same with the other formulas that are applied to the same cells, but start at BB3, BC3, etc.

The result formula in e.g. AZ3 could be =OR(BA3,BB3,BC3, etc.)

That is (more or less) the same what the conditional formating does to decide if a cell should be colored or not.
