Lets go:

2) Put the sentences of List1 and the sentences of List11, List12, ... in each list;

3) Create the names below for each list.

Tip: select the ranges $D$1:$D$6, $F$1:$F$5, $H$1:$H$3, $J$1:$J$6, $L$1:$L$6 and $N$1:$N$7 and choose Selection (tab Data/Group Defined Names) and click Ok.

List13 - Refers to: =$J$2:$J$6

List14 - Refers to: =$L$2:$L$6

List15 - Refers to: =$N$2:$N$7

=List1

=INDIRECT("List1"&MATCH(" "&$B$1&" ",INDEX(" "&List1&" ",),0))

andreas killer say:

can anyone help me or explain what the below formula means?

Both. ;-)

That is a combined formula from different steps, not easy to understand. If we disassemble the parts I becomes easy and simple.

It is obvious that A1 contains a date, and A2 is the day after, so let us use this formulas in a new file:

A1:  =TODAY()
A2:  =A1+1

Drag it down till row 50.

The next step is to create the criteria, your roster has a length of 7 days * 4 weeks = 28 days:

B1:  =MOD(A1-$A$1,28)

Drag it down till row 50.

As you see you'll get the numbers from 0 to 27 and then it starts again at 0. So all we have to do is to create a table for the VLOOKUP formula:

Means we have to start with 0 (the 1st day), then 6 days "on" 1 day "off"means the 7th day is 6, then for three weeks, means  7, 13, 14, 20. Then have 7 days offmeans our last lookup value is 21, which is the 22th day (the lookup table is 0 based, so we have to subtract 1). Sounds complicated, just write this values into the sheet:

E1:  0
E2:  6
E3:  7
E4:  13
E5:  14
E6:  20
E7:  21
F1:  a
F2:  b
F3:  c
F4:  d
F5:  e
F6:  f
F7:  g

I suggest not to use "on" and "off" for this, because it is easier to understand how it works. The last step is to create the VLOOKUP formula to search for the MOD values in the table:

C1:  =VLOOKUP(B1,$E$1:$F$7,2)

Drag it down till row 50.

That's it.

In your file, replace the chars from a to g with "on" or "off" and you're done.

I do not recommend to combine the formula as in your sample, because it has no benefits.

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

When you "reconnected" Outlook 2013, I suspect you created a new mail profile. This cleared the autocompletion cache.

"Autofill" (actually, autocompletion) is not connected to your contacts in any way whatsoever. The autocomplete cache is merely a list of the addresses to which you have sent mail in the past, whether or not those addresses are associated with your contacts. Since you started over with the list, it will take some time to rebuild it as you send out messages. Obviously, you don't have to memorize the addresses of your contacts because they're in your Contacts folder and will appear when you click the To button. Addresses for recipients who aren't contacts, however, are gone and won't reappear in the cache until you send another message to that address.

If you wish to populate your autocompletion cache with your contacts' addresses, open your Contacts folder, select all your contacts by pressing Ctrl+A, then dragging the selection to your Inbox. Outlook will open a new message window with all of your contacts' addresses in the To field. Click in that field and press Ctrl+K to resolve all the addresses, then close the new message window, discarding any changes when prompted. The act of pressing Ctrl+K causes Outlook to load the autocompletion cache with the addresses in the To field.
