robert__p say:

Hi Rob,

In Office 365, it is not feasible to only give users the right to add or remove members for a specific shared mailbox.

I understand it would be nice if we have such feature in Office 365. Therefore, I suggest you submit feedback about the feature via the link below:

http://office365.uservoice.com/forums/273493-office-365-admin   

Understanding your experience helps us to make our product and service better for you and others. Thanks for your understanding.

As for your requirement “the email's that go into the shared mailbox need to be forwarded to the members InBox.”, follow the steps below:

1. Sign in to Exchange admin center (https://outlook.office365.com/ecp) with your admin account. Navigate to recipients—shared, double click the shared mailbox, select mailbox delegation, and add your admin account under Full access. Click save at last.

2. Sign in to Outlook Web App(https://outlook.office365.com/owa) with your admin account. Click your picture in the upper right corner of the page, select Open another mailbox and enter the address of the shared mailbox, click open. Click Settings (the gear icon in the upper-right corner of the page) – Options – Mail –Automatic processing-- Inbox and sweep rules. Click “+” to create a new rule. Set “When the message arrives, and it matches all of these conditions” as “Apply to all messages”, set “Do all of the following” to forward to the email accounts you want. Click Save at last.

lifesaver1 say:

I just did this successfully for the 2017 college football bowl games. 

1) Create a spreadsheet with appropriate headers that same as calendar entries in Outlook 2016. Examples: Subject, Start Date, End Date, Start Time,  End Time, Location, Description. You don't have to include them all, but I assume you must include the date and time at least.

2) Populate the columns in your excel sheet with your data, formatting appropriate columns for date and time.

3) Save the Excel spreadsheet as a csv file.

4) In Outlook, you may wish to create a new calendar file for what you are importing. If so, do so. If not, you can use your regular calendar.

5) In Outlook, go to File, then Open & Export, then Import and Export, then Import from another program or file, then choose comma separated values, then select the csv file, then select the destination calendar (be sure it's the one you want and not one in the archive or deleted folders), map custom fields to make sure you have it right.

6) Finish

Note: Outlook imports times and dates as your current time zone. So all the times and dates should be in accordance with your current time zone.
