doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

Use File>Open to open your Normal.dotm template and save it with a new name in the Word Startup folder.  Then delete the Normal.dotm so that Word creates a new one the next time that you start Word.  The macros that you had in the Normal.dotm will then be available for use in all of your documents.

The advantage of doing the above is that the template containing your macros is far less likely to become corrupted, which is something that can happen with the Normal.dotm.

This disadvantage is that you will need to use File>Open and open the template in the Startup folder if you want to modify one of the macros, or create a new one.
