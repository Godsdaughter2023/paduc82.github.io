Custom parameters

"{Param1:Potty Training} for Toddlers"?

microsoft advertising - shelly say:

Hi jrcollins01,

Thank you for your question!

Custom Parameters are URL parameters that are used to track information about the source of an ad click. By adding these parameters to your ads and campaigns, you can learn if people who click on your ads came from mobile devices, where they were located when they clicked your ads, and much more.

What you are currently describing sounds more like a dynamic text ad, where you use the {Keyword} placeholder to make your ad title, ad text, and display URL display keywords that match, or closely match, the text that a customer types. Write your ad, and then insert the {Keyword} placeholder where you want the keyword to appear. Default text is mandatory in {keyword} dynamic text parameters. See example below:

                “{Keyword:Potty Training} for Toddlers”

If the customer searches for “Toilet Training” and you have the keyword ‘Toilet Training’. The Ad will appear as Toilet Training for Toddlers; however, if the customer searches ‘Toilet Training for my Toddler’ the Ad will appear as ‘Potty Training for Toddlers’. “Toilet Training for my Toddler for Toddler” has exceeded the maximum character limit and ‘Potty Training’ is the default keyword. If a customer searches a term that is not a current keyword your ad will not be shown.

I hope you find this information helpful. Please let me know if you have any additional questions as we are always happy to assist.

Sincerely,

TIP Vertical scroll bar in Word 2013 only partially visible? - Microsoft Community

stymied2 say:

TIP Vertical scroll bar in Word 2013 only partially visible? Scroll bar there but not effectively there?

Halfway through composing a question for the Community, I found out what's causing the problem but as I couldn't find this advice anywhere else in my searches, I'll go ahead and describe it anyway, so it's in the ether just in case someone has this problem.  And yes, I'm miffed with myself that I didn't get the solution sooner.

By partially visible, I mean that I can just glimpse the left edge of the scroll bar, but there's not enough of it there for me to be able to locate the moving 'box' and to grip and move it. I can scroll using the wheel but sometimes you just need to see the bar.  

I'd already taken the Options>Advance>Display route several times, on different days, double-checking that the scroll bar was well and truly ticked.

If you've done that, and you still can't see the vertical scroll bar you've grown to love, take a look at the row of about five buttons that's at the very, very top right of your screen.  They're above your username.

Look at the Maximise button to the left of the cross that is the close button.  If it looks like a single page and not two overlapping pages, then click on it.  This should reveal your vertical scroll bar.

Hope this helps.
