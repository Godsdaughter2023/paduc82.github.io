When exporting to images in Publisher, I cannot obtein a good quality. - Microsoft Community

ramiro tau say:

When exporting to images in Publisher, I cannot obtein a good quality. Even setting a 300 dpi res, I obtain a pixeled and bad quality image. Any suggestions?

I am trying to export a .pub to .tif, setting the resolution in 300, but I obtain a bad quality image, specially looking at the fonts more than the images.
john korchok say:

David mentioned capturing an image from a PDF. I use this a lot to get good quality bitmaps of files:

  1. Use File>Save As>PDF in Publisher to create the PDF.
  2. Open the file in Acrobat Reader (or Acrobat, if you have it).
  3. Zoom to  a percentage that will give you good final results. If your monitor is set to a resolution of 96 d.p.i. and you want an image that is 300 d.p.i., zoom to 312 percent in Reader.
  4. In Reader or Acrobat, choose the Edit>Take a Snapshot tool.
  5. Click in one corner of the area you wish to capture, then hold the mouse button down and drag to the opposite corner. This area can be much bigger than what can display on your screen. This is the advantage of this technique over a screen shot.
  6. Let go of the mouse mbutton and wait until the screen flashes dark and then light again.
  7. Open your image editing software and paste in the image. Resize it to the correct D.P.I. and save.
