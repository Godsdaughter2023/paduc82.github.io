toddallcock say:

The Outlook.com email account has to be added to every device or PC you want to sync.  Therefore, it has to be added to the home PC in addition to your POP3 account, and to the work laptop using Exchange in addition to the Exchange account.

 

Adding this account will create a new set of contacts and calendar folders in Outlook that sync with Outlook.com.  Outlook keeps the data from different sources separate, so the Exchange contacts on your work laptop won't "cross-pollinate" with your personal (Outlook.com) contacts and vice-versa. 

 

On the home PC, the contacts in your Outlook default folder (what you previously called your POP3 account contacts, which is really a misnomer since Outlook can't sync contacts with a POP3 account) will *not* sync with Outlook.com or the phone. You need to move the contacts from the Outlook default folder to the Outlook.com folder created when you added the account.  Then, if you like, you can go into Outlook's options and change the Outlook.com account to the default data file. (This won't change your default email account in Outlook- it'll still be the POP3 account, just what contacts and calendar folders are the default in Outlook.)

 

What's confusing me is when you say changes on Outlook.com aren't syncing to the phone, then you mentioned groups and links. Only the contacts themselves sync to the phone. Outlook.com groups do not. (You can also create groups on the phone, but those groups don't sync back to Outlook.com either.)

 

Tasks will sync between Outlook.com and the phone, but not between Outlook on the PC and Outlook.com.

 

I'm not sure how Facebook and LinkedIn figure into this, but you can add those accounts on the phone, and those contacts will be added to the phone contacts, but do not sync to Outlook.com- separate accounts don't cross-pollinate.

ronrosenfeld say:

The CONCATENATE function does not retain formatting.  And your A1 entry is being interpreted as a number by Excel.

Another solution, in addition to Jaeson's, would be to use this more involved formula:

=TEXT(A1,"#,##0;;@")&","&TEXT(B1,"#,##0;;@")

If the values are numeric, they will be formatted with comma's at the thousands separator; if the value is not numeric (as in your B1 entry), then it will be passed unchanged.

Hi Rob,

Since the issues that you have on your device started after updating your phone, you have the option to revert to the previous build of your phone using our Windows Device Recovery Tool (WDRT).

To download WDRT, you can go to this link.

We would also appreciate if you could report this to our engineers using our Feedback Hub, so they could look at this and use it as a reference for future versions of our updates.

To send a feedback, follow the steps below:

  1. Go to All Apps, search for the Feedback Hub app.
  2. Open the menu icon at the top left side.
  3. Select Feedback, then at the top right side, tap the add button (+).
  4. Select Problem and fill up the box with the details needed.
  5. Select the right category for the suggestion and then submit.

Note: Similar to a hard reset, running WDRT on your phone would mean that every file on your phone would be deleted, so we suggest that you back them up first.

Let us know if you encounter any challenges with using WDRT.
