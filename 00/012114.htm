Windows Account Not Syncing Error - Microsoft Community

jay_answers say:

Windows Account Not Syncing Error

Technical Level : Basic

Summary

This is a error that happens fairly often. Most of the time the issue is something simple like data connection being turned off or the password is incorrect. The article looks at various ways of fixing this.


Details


The issue can be caused by the following 'problem points'


  1. Data connection is not working.
  2. Wi-Fi connection is not working.
  3. Two Factor authentication.
  4. Account is not configured properly.
  5. Text message back up.
  6. Phone OS has gone corrupt.


Primary Account


Before we can look at any of the issues here, first step is to identify the primary Microsoft Account on your phone. On your phone, go to SETTINGS > EMAIL + ACCOUNTS. The very first account at the top is the primary account. Here is a snap of how it would look.


 Your primary account is what powers the entire phone, purchases and all that. Do note that, you can add even more Microsoft Accounts to your phone but this is the one that matters the most. For instance, in the above screenshot, the phone has a second OUTLOOK account that is added. The existence of this account does not affect the primary account at the top in any way.


 Data Connection


If your phone only works via data connection, then check if the internet is working. Checking this is easy. Press the search button on your phone - the third button at the bottom portion of your phone - and that will bring up BING search engine. Type anything - cat, dog or mouse - and hit search. Did the search return with results?


If so, your data connection is fine.

If the search did not results, that means your data connection is dead. That is why you are getting the account not syncing error.


 Wi-Fi Connection


If your phone only works via Wi-Fi Connection, then check if the internet is working. Checking this is easy. Press the search button on your phone - the third button at the bottom portion of your phone - and that will bring up BING search engine. Type anything - cat, dog or mouse - and hit search. Did the search return with results?

 

 If so, your Wi-Fi Connection is fine. 

 If the search did not results, that means your Wi-Fi Connection is dead. That is why you are getting the account not syncing error.


Two Factor aAthentication.


It is possible that you may have enabled Two Factor authentication on your MICROSOFT ACCOUNT. If so, first you will need to download the AUTHENTICATOR app on to your phone and use the code generated from that.


Alternatively, you could try disabling Two Factor authentication by going to this link. Get your account working and then re-enable two factor authentication.


 Account Configuration


If step 1 and step 2 are done, then the issue is not connectivity. It could be an account issue. Open the internet explorer on your phone. visit outlook.com. See if you login using your PRIMARY ACCOUNT that was discussed in the beginning of this article.


If you are able to login, that means the account is fine.
If you are unable to login, that means the account has a problem. At this point, try and reset your password. If that fails, then contact support. Use this wiki article to contact support and find out what is wrong with your account.


Text Back Up


A lot of users are reporting a scenario around this, based on which I have added this segment.


Go to SETTINGS > BACKUP > TEXT MESSAGES. Here, turn of the facility to back up text messages. Now, restart your phone and see if the account works fine. If it does, go back and re-enable TEXT MESSAGES backup.


 OS corruption


If step 1 to 3 worked out okay and you reached here, that leaves with only one conclusion. The OS has gone corrupt. Make sure you have backed up everything. Then, do a phone reset. Phone reset can be performed by going to SETTINGS > ABOUT THIS PHONE > RESET.
