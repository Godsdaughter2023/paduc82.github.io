nicoleburton say:

PERCENTAGE TOTAL

I am in need of an excel formula to get the grand total for this row.    The answers is 14% but unsure how they arrived at it.  Help Please.

joeu2004 say: Nicole wrote:
I am in need of an excel formula to get the grand total for this row.    The answers is 14% but unsure how they arrived at it.  Help Please.
office supply    Cost/Unit    # of units    Total cost    % of grand total
Copy Paper        2.99             25             $74.75

First, 74.75 = 2.99*25, as you might know.  In Excel, if 2.99 is in B2 and 25 is in C2, it would be prudent to write the following formula in D2 [1]:

=ROUND(B2*C2,2)

As for the 14%:  Presumably it is based on their knowledge of the grand total.  If the grand total were in D10, the percentage of grand total might be calculated in E2 using the following formula [2]:

=D2/$D$10

formatted as Percentage with 0 decimal places.

The problem is:  you do not see to know the grand total.

We can infer the grand total from the percentage given, 14%.  But the problem is:  14% is likely a rounded value.  The actual percentage is probably between 13.5% and 14.5%.

So the grand total might be between =ROUNDUP(D2/14.5%,2) and =ROUNDDOWN(D2/13.5%,2), which is 515.52 and 553.70.

We might be able to narrow down the grand total range if you have the percentages for some other line items.


-----
[1] The use of ROUND in D2 avoids infinitesimal anomalies ("errors") that often arise in Excel arithmetic involving non-integers.  It is a good practice, although it is not needed with these two particular numbers, 2.99 and 25.

[2] The use of $D$10 (absolute reference) in E2 makes it easy to copy the formula down the column to calcuate the percentage of grand total for other line items.

jan de messemaeker - project mvp say:

Hi,

First, by the way, what leveling does IS a heuristic approach, and like most heuristic approaches it will (only) sub-optimize. Glad you found out that leveling itself is not the major problem but rather the attempts to level manually (percentage allocations, start not earlier than dates)

Sorry to say but if you plan tasks not to happen as soon as possible Project 's favorite way is through  a constraint, generally a SNET constraint. There is one noteworthy exception: Leveling itself does not introduce a constraint when shifting tasks.. it creates a "leveling delay" which it adds to the start date.

Thinking about your problem to have constraints after using the Team planner: you cannot avoid a constraint in the scenario you describe BUT you do not have to scroll back to the start of the project: drop the task to start TODAY and the constraint generated will not influence leveling (since you found out Reschedule Work has to be used anyway).

Did you notice that Reschedule Work DOES create SNET constraints as well?
