lemp say:

That depends on what your D: drive is.  On many "brand name" systems (particularly HP), the D: drive is occupied by either the recovery image or related system tools provided by your computer's manufacturer.  In these cases, there usually is very little extra space on the D: drive.

Click Start and then click Computer

Is there an icon for the D: drive?  If yes, right-click on the icon and select "Properties."  What are the values for

  • Used space
  • Free space
  • Capacity

If it turns out that you have a lot of free space on the D: drive, one fairly simple thing you can do is move your "personal" folders, such as My Documents, My Music, My Pictures, etc.

http://www.techsupportalert.com/content/how-move-windows-7-personal-folders-my-documents-another-drive.htm

https://www.howtogeek.com/howto/windows-vista/moving-your-personal-data-folders-in-windows-vista-the-easy-way/

Note:  Step 9 in the first-linked article is "Click the folder where you want to store the files (not the root of a drive)."  That means don't make the new location of your folder just the drive letter, like D:  I haven't done this in a while.  If you get a message that the new location can't be found, you'll have to create it first.  Do this by going to D: in Windows Explorer, right-click and select New > Folder.

Hi Firesniper,

 

To transfer and save your music files to a different hard drive, follow the steps below: -

1.       Copy all your music files to your second hard drive, then open explorer and on the left side of the navigation pane, right-click on music under Libraries and click on Properties.

2.       Under Properties, click on Include a folder.

3.       Browse through Computer to select the music folder in second hard drive which you copied from first hard drive.

4.       Click on Include folder.

 

Note: - Change the name of the music folder in second hard drive once it is copied successfully from first hard drive, and after transferring your music files to second hard drive and saving them, right click on first hard drive music folder and click on Remove location from library to free space on your first hard disk.

1.       Open Computer, on left navigation pane of explorer, click on Music.

2.       On right side of the window, below the heading Music Library, click on Location.

3.       Right click on the music folder (any name given by you) and click on Set as default save location.

Hi,

Since other sites are still accessible except ROBLOX.com, the issue is not with the internet connection nor the computer. There's a possibility that the site was down temporarily for some reasons like website/server maintenance. To further isolate the issue, you can do the following.

  • Check if ROBLOX.com is accessible using other web browsers.
  • Check if the website is accessible using other computer.

You can check and disable proxy settings on your computer's browser by following the steps provided below.

Internet Explorer

  1. Open Internet Explorer and click on Tools.
  2. Select Internet Options.
  3. Click the Connections tab and click the LAN settings button.
  4. Remove the check mark on the box for Use a proxy server for your LAN.
  5. Then click OK button to save the changes.

Google Chrome

  1. Open Google Chrome and go to the menu options which is the three-line icon at the top right corner.
  2. Choose Settings from the drop-down list.
  3. Select Network and click Change proxy settings.
  4. Click the LAN Settings button.
  5. Uncheck the box for Use a proxy server for your LAN.
  6. Then click OK button to save the changes

Mozilla Firefox

  1. Click the Firefox drop-down menu button which is the three-line icon at the top right corner and choose Options.
  2. Click the Advanced panel and then click the Network tab.
  3. click Settings button on the Connection section and select No Proxy.
  4. Click OK to close the Connection Settings and Options window.
