saltgrass say:

Any standards regarding apps behavior during Modern Standby?

I have a modern standby system which is a Dell.  I noticed something the other day I had not noticed before and was wondering if applications are given some type of standard as to how they should behave when on a modern standby system.

The situation involves being in modern standby and an active sleep timer setting.  I know modern standby systems do not sleep but it appears some applications pay attention when sleep timers are configured.

In my case this concerns iTunes and Groove music.  My system is set to sleep after 30 minutes and when the lid is closed.  The iTunes app will continue playing when the system enters modern standby (display dark) but will stop playing when the lid is closed or when the 30 minute sleep timer expires.

However, Groove music will continue to play even if the lid is closed or the 30 minute timer has expired.  I would think all apps should behave as iTunes does and pause when sleep timers are hit..  This type of situation may be involved in some complaints regarding battery drain when the lid is closed or in modern standby.  If and app was running which was not visible to the user, the app might continue to run and deplete the battery or cause the system to get hot when in a travel case.
