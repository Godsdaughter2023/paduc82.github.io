A "standard user" can only access files in his or her own profile.

In order to make it easier for multiple users of a single computer to access certain files, Windows creates a profile that includes folders that can be accessed by other users, even if they are "standard users."  In Windows XP, this was called the "All Users" profile.  In subsequent editions of Windows, this was changed to the "Public" profile.

ken blake say:

I largely (but not completely) disagree with your father. Several points:

1. All software (except the trivial) has bugs. A month from now, it may have fewer bugs, but it will still have some. There is never some magic date when *all* the bugs are gone. Especially with something as complex as an operating system, it will *always have some. They  will never all be fixed. Even your 1809 still has some bugs.

2. Waiting a month is a common thing people recommend, but a month is a completely arbitrary amount of time to wait. Waiting a month may let you avoid some problems, but still leave you vulnerable to others. Or it may do nothing for you.

3. There are bugs and there are bugs. They are far from all being the same. Some bugs are very serious: they may cause Windows to continually crash, or they may cause loss of data. Other bugs are much more minor: they may be misspellings of words in error messages.

4. Most bugs only affect some users. Whether they affect you or not usually depends on what your hardware environment is or what software you run. That's largely why a released version of an operating system may still have serious bugs: it is nearly impossible to test it with every possible combination of hardware and installed software.

5. As a general rule, people with older hardware are more likely to run into problems with updates than those with newer computers. How old is your computer?

6. So should you install it now, or should you wait? If you should  wait, how long should you wait? For most people, it's safer to wait for a while--at least for a month, maybe longer. There is seldom a pressing need to install a new version as soon as it's released--not unless you need or crave some new feature it has.  My recommendation is not to install it immediately, but wait and stay abreast of the published reports on how good it is and what problems it may create. That will help you decide when it's safe to install it.

7. Despite all the above, I installed it yesterday, and have had no problems with it.
