  • Do Not Rebuild Identities with Exchange and IMAP accounts. Delete the accounts (this deletes the cache files in Outlook). Do the rebuild then add back accounts. jay freedman say:
Go to Office button > Word Options > Trust Center > Trust Center Settings.

Click the Macro Settings tab on the left of the dialog, and click the option for "Disable all macros except digitally signed macros".

Then click the Trusted Locations tab and look at the list of folders under User Locations. For documents and templates stored in trusted locations, macros will run even if they aren't digitally signed. Macros in documents or templates stored anywhere else will be disabled.

You can add any folders you want to the list of trusted locations. But then you have to be careful to use those folders to store macro-containing files only if you know those macros aren't malicious, preferably only ones you've written yourself.

It's a good idea to sign your own macros with a certificate created by selfcert.exe (an optionally installed part of Office) so they don't have to be stored in trusted locations. (But they still won't be trusted on any other computer -- selfcert certificates are valid only on the computer where they're created.)
