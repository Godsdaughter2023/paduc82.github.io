Just to be clear, automatic updates for any 1 program cannot be stopped. It's a matter of whether the Microsoft AutoUpdate [MAU] program checks for updates automatically, but it check for all Office programs.

Also, none of the Office programs update automatically unless the option is selected to Automatically Download and Install in MAU.

To disable MAU go to Help> Check for Updates in the main menu of either Office program. Once MAU launches select Manually Check rather than the one mentioned above. If you do choose that setting be certain to launch MAU periodically to determine whether any updates are available... Mac Office is evolving constantly with new updates being released on a monthly basis - sometimes more.

IMO, however, Automatically Check is the best choice for most users & is the default setting. That option will notify you when updates are available but whether to install each is up to the you.

Once you've chosen an option, simply Quit MAU.

julie fka profjulie say: When you sync files from your computer to a mobile device ActiveSync and Windows Mobile Device Center can only sync to the My Documents folder in main memory.  If you want to sync to a different place (an SD card), you'll need to use some third party software.  MobSync (cost $8) and Mighty Sync are two different 3rd party applications that allow you to sync from different folders on your computer to different folders on the mobile device.
