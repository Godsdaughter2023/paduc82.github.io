tom_bpc say:

Rui,

When introduced (in 2010 version), inactivating a task was promised to remove it from the network calculations entirely.  In practice, however, what got ignored were links from inactive tasks to active successors (on the forward pass) and from inactive tasks to active predecessors (on the backward pass).  With this approach, users could inactivate whole branches of the schedule while still preserving and displaying their logic and early dates - e.g. for what-if/scenario analysis.

Also with this approach, any active task whose only predecessors are inactive automatically returns to the project start. Unfortunately (for me and perhaps a few others), most users don't want this behavior.  Beginning with the 2013 release, Project inserts clandestine finish-to-start links between the predecessors and successors of inactivated tasks - at calculation time only. Consequently, inactivating tasks with links that are NOT finish-to-start can actually DELAY the successor tasks.  You may have observed this.

I'm surprised that your first post seems to report the initial (2010) behavior on a work computer with the 2013 version installed.  Perhaps later updates have included a setting for toggling between the two behaviors, and your home and work computers have the setting changed.....  I never "upgraded" from 2010, so I can't check.

Bottom line: it's not a bug, it's what the overwhelming majority of users have demanded.
