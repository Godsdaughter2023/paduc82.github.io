bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

It looks like the bulk of what you're seeing is being generated by Microsoft Error Reporting Program [MERP]. It could be due to forced shut downs, improper disk permissions, or minor problems with the Main Identity for your Office installation, but doesn't appear to be serious.

See if it will clear up if you;

  1. Hold the Shift key while you log in using an Admin account
  2. Quit all programs
  3. Go to ~:Applications:Microsoft Office 2011:Office, then launch Microsoft Database Utility.app to Rebuild the Main Identity
  4. Run Disk Utility to repair disk permissions on your Mac's HD
  5. Shut Down & wait a moment before starting up.

No bragging, but unlike some, I rarely see a Recovered Files folder, and when I do there is usually a valid explanation (such as crash while the program was in use, forced shut downs due to testing, etc.). Some things that may help if you aren't already in the habit :-)

  • Keep your software & OS X fully updated
  • Make sure to Quit Word (and any other program) when not in use... Don't just close the document windows
  • Never Shut Down or allow the computer to 'sleep' while programs are running
  • Restart your Mac at least once per week. Quite frankly (unless there are network connectivity considerations or the need for extended unattended tasks) IMO it's best to shut down at the end of the day & launch fresh in the morning.
