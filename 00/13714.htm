john korchok say:

No macros are necessary with legacy checkboxes. After inserting the checkboxes, the document has to have forms protection applied using Review>Restrict Editing>Editing Restrictions>Filling in forms.

You can insert a Section Break (Continuous) between the non-editable area contained the checkboxes and the editable area. Then when you are applying protection, click on the Select sections... link and uncheck the editable section.

Form protection does reduce the amount of formatting you can do to a document, so if that is important, you can also take a look at the ActiveX checkbox control. This can be inserted in a normal Word document and does not require protection to work. It works with all Windows versions of Word, but not Mac.

How do I multiply hours and mins worked by an hourly rate to get a - Microsoft Community

lucindaedwards say:

How do I multiply hours and mins worked by an hourly rate to get a total pay cost

I have devised a time sheet which gives me the total hours worked for each day. I now want to create a formula to multiply the total hours worked by an hourly rate to give me my pay costs. please can you tell me the formula I need to do this please.
j.e. mcgimpsey say: Excel stores times as fractional days, so 6 hours = 0.25, 9 hours = 0.375, etc. To convert

To get a total pay cost, assume hours in A2, Pay Rate in B2, and your total Pay Cost in C2:

C2: =A2 * 24 * B2

You'll probably need to format C2 as General or another non-time format after entry.

Of course, that works for round numbers of time and pay, but you may need to do some rounding yourself. For instance, to round to the nearest penny:

C2: =ROUND(A2 * 24 * B2, 2)

or

C2: =ROUNDUP(A2 * 24 * B2, 2)

to round up to the nearest cent. You might also round to the nearest 15 minutes:

C2: =ROUND(ROUND(A2*24*4,0)/(24*4) * 24 * B2,2)

or, a bit more succinctly:

C2: =ROUND(ROUND(A2*96,0)/4 * B2,2)
