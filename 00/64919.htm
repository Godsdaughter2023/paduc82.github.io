Managing your Payment Options

What are valid payment methods?

Currently you can use credit and debit cards with major labels (Visa, Mastercard, and American Express) as well as PayPal. These should be current accounts, and the billing information should match that of your Xbox Live account. Prepaid credit/debit cards may not work on Xbox Live as the service requires the card be able to accept online and recurring charges, something prepaid cards sometimes cannot do.

How do I add a payment method?

You can add payment methods on the console or on Xbox.com (click on Manage payment options and Add credit card for this). For the console, just follow the steps below:

  1. Navigate to the Settings Hub and select Account.
  2. Under the Your Billing Options panel select Manage Payment Options.
  3. Select which type of payment method you wish to add: Add PayPal or Add Credit Card.
  4. Fill out the required info and select Next.

You can also use the options found on Step 3 to update or remove a current payment method.

I can't add a payment method!

First, verify that you are entering all the information correctly and that it matches your Xbox Live account. This includes your name, address, card info, etc. For instance, if the name on your card is "John Smith" and the name on your Live account is "Master Chief", then there could be some issues when authorizing or using the card. Also, the billing region on the payment method must match the region on the account.

If you are sure that all the information on the account and payment method are correct, you could try to remove the payment method entirely and re-add it back to your account. You could also try using a different payment method. 

I’m getting a specific error code or message. 

There are several different messages you could be getting. These errors might look like:

  • Can't authorize the selected payment option. Please select a different payment option or add a new one.
  • Can't retrieve information from Xbox Live. Status code ######## 

Here are some specific things to double check before posting about this issue:

Your account billing address does not match your billing address for the credit card.
You can check your billing address on Xbox.com. 

Your credit card billing address and the account region do not match.
You can check the region of your account by visiting https://billing.microsoft.com and clicking on “Overview”. 

Your card has not been activated, or
Your card was declined due to insufficient funds, or
Your card cannot accept online purchases or automatic billing, or
Your credit card company is intentionally preventing the card from being used.
You will need to get in touch with your payment method provider to check on these issues. Two things that a lot of users don’t realize is that not all credit cards are created equal. Your card must allow for online purchasing and recurring fees (like auto-renewing Live time). Also, some prepaid credit or debit cards do not work with Live as they cannot be counted on for automatic billing. 

You have too many unsuccessful purchase attempts, and will need to wait 24 to 48 hours.
Pretty self-explanatory, but if you have been trying to authorize a card too many times, the fraud protection on the card might kick in, preventing any further attempts. You may need to also call your provider to see if you need to personally authorize the use of this card for this purpose. 

I get "Can't Authorize" errors for multiple payment methods.

If you are getting this same error with multiple payment methods from multiple providers (PayPal, different banks or credit cards, etc), you should contact phone support in your region for more information and possible resolution. This could indicate some kind of issue with your account that only phone support will be able to assist you with.

I'm getting charged $1 when adding a payment method!

These are just test charges that are sent to verify that your payment method is currently accepting charges. They will be reversed within a few days. If you don't see them leave your account, first verify you didn't make any $1 purchases, and then contact either your bank or Xbox Phone Support using the link above.

Is there another way I can purchase my content?

In addition to purchasing content directly on Live, you could purchase a pre-paid card from a local retailer or online. These cards can be redeemed for Microsoft currency or Xbox Live Gold time. 

I can't remove my payment method.

Before taking any steps, you need to know if your account is currently on an auto-renewing subscription to Live Gold. If you purchased Gold through the console with a payment method, possibly some kind of promotion, a valid payment method must be attached to the account to keep auto renewal on. You can switch the payment method by adding a new one and then removing the original method.

If you wish to cancel your auto-renewal so you can remove your payment method, start by using our auto-renewal process in our sticky on "Managing your Xbox Live Gold Auto Renewal". If you run into any issues with removing your card after auto-renewal has been turned off, please contact us via phone or chat support for further assistance. There may be an issue with your account that they have to look into.

If you are having issues with adding PayPal first check to make sure that your account meets all the requirements for using PayPal. Please ensure that all of the following criteria are met before continuing to other troubleshooting steps.

Funds
In order to properly use your PayPal account, it must either have a valid credit card attached to it or a positive money balance. If you have an attached bank account and are still having trouble adding/using your PayPal method, try transferring some money from the bank account to PayPal so the PayPal account actually has a positive balance.

Account Information

I screwed around with the SP3 Dock for a few weeks, including assisted support from MS.  After burning such time on both my side and the MS side, I gave up on the dock.  

My conclusion is that the dock utilizes a hub chip from a small chip manufacturer, and that chip simply does not work.   I have not opened up the dock, but in some research on such hub chips I see that the manufacturer doesn't even really exist any more, having essentially been closed and acquired and the chip no longer exists in the acquirer's catalog (though it may still be getting sold).  Across the industry, you can see complaints on this issue from other docks, e.g., Dell, ThinkPad, because they use the same/similar non-working chips that do not work in the configuration with MDP and the nice high resolution Dell monitors.  

In support cases, the PC and dock manufacturers will have you jump through hoops updating firmware on the dock, on the computer, replacing the dock, and most of the time you the customer just gets defeated and goes away.  Too much nonconverging love, and best case for them (better than recalling their docks and acknowledging the defects).

As a substitute for the dock, I bought a standard power cord and a 7 port Amazon USB hub, and just plug the three connectors (MDP, USB3, and power) into the SP3 when I sit down.  That works great.   The configuration is also nice because the SP3 can lay flat and be used with the stylus as a note taking device.

I love my SP3 but the dock is a nightmare.

I have a U2413. Apparently there are "issues" (talked about in the Dell forum) with DDC/CI (the controls that allow a computer to change the monitor's settings) on this model using DisplayPort 1.2. There is no workaround other than finding another way to connect your monitors, not involving DP 1.2, e.g. an HDMI connection. Having the U2413 as last in the chain should be OK too, since that doesn't require DP 1.2.

Hi,

You may follow the steps below to reduce the touchpad sensitivity on the computer and see if it helps.

1.      Turn on the computer and log into Windows as normal. Open the "Start" menu and click on the option labeled "Control Panel."

2.      Double-click on the icon labeled "Mouse.". The "Mouse Properties" window will open. Navigate to the tab labeled "Device Settings." Click on the button labeled "Settings." A window labeled "Properties" will appear.

3.      Examine the left panel of the "Properties" window and expand the option labeled "Sensitivity." Click on the option labeled "Touch Sensitivity."

4.      Reduce the touch sensitivity setting by moving the slider to the right, towards the "Heavy Touch" label. Experiment with different levels of sensitivity until you find one suited to your needs.

5.      Click on the button labeled "OK" to close the "Properties" window; repeat with the "Mouse" control panel. Your new touchpad sensitivity settings are now saved.
