In addition to Mary's answers, you can gain even more control over the hyperlink by selecting it > right click > Change text > font and in that dialog you will see the option for "underline" where you remove it, change it to other types of underlines and change the color.

I don't use anything Apple, but I suspect what you are seeing is a "hotspot zone" which is the way some pdf files show active links. I don't know that you can change it as it is probably by design. I also don't know that you *should* change it or remove the underlines of hyperlinks.

There are many things you cannot do in a protected document, even in an unprotected section. What you can do is apply so-called Quick Styles from the Styles group of the Home tab. Applying styles via keyboard shortcuts would be a different approach. For example, there is a built-in key assignment, Ctrl+Shift+L, that applies the List Bullet style.
