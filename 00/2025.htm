Hi Boris:

I am not sure that I understand your question:

1)  You have a file with "This is a heading" and "This is another heading"

2)  You change "This is another heading" to "This is a NEW heading"

3)  You save the file as a new file name.

You are saying that your hyperlinks point to the old file?  Yes: I am afraid that will always happen: Word hyperlinks store an absolute, static path.  They will not change unless you manually change them.  Word will do "relative" hyperlinks, but not well, and only if the external resources are in the same folder as the document.

However, I am puzzled by your reference to hyperlinks in the TOC.  Word for Mac TOC generator cannot generate hyperlinks.  There will be NO hyperlinks in a TOC generated in Word for Mac.  The hyperlinks will be there if you generate the TOC on a PC, but removed if you generate it on a Mac.

This is a design bug we have been shouting at for many years.

I don't believe we will be able to solve your problem: however, if you were to change that document to use the Built-in "Heading 1" to "Heading 9" series of styles.  Those styles have some secret sauce baked in that make TOCs much, much more reliable.

You still won't get hyperlinks in the TOC.  But the TOC will regenerate correctly automatically.

Hope this helps

Hi Boris:

The \o switch sets the TOC generator to look for "outline levels".  If you look in Format>Style...>Modify>Format>Paragraph ... up the top right corner of the dialog you will see "Outline Level".  That's what it's using.  In the Heading... series of styles, that property is baked in for extra stability.

Sorry, the entry is slightly different on the PC.  From the Styles chunk, you can right-click and Modify, then it's the same.

You can use \t and a quoted, comma-delimited list of style names and levels, or the \o switch and a range of level numbers.

If you set your heading styles to have the outline levels equivalent to their heading levels, then the Outline View and TOC features will work with them.

If you use Outline Levels, there is no need to set the TOC level you want each to be.  If you are using Style Names, then you have to set a corresponding TOC level for each style.

If you are going to add outline levels to your non-standard styles, make sure you brief anyone else using the document that that is what you have done, otherwise they may break it.

Those switches, by the way, need to be \o and \t -- back-slashes, not forward slashes.  I suspect that forward slashes don't work.

How do I eliminate pop up that asks if I want to close current tab or - Microsoft Community

gloriapelec say:

How do I eliminate pop up that asks if I want to close current tab or close all tabs.

Please help. How do I eliminate pop up that asks if I want to close current tab or close all tabs. Thank you.
david vinod say:

Hi,

If you click the Close button in the upper-right corner of Internet Explorer 8, you will be asked whether you want to close the tab you're currently viewing, or the entire browser window.

If you have more than one tab open, click the Close Tab button on the tab you want to close. If you have a mouse with a wheel, and have more than one tab open, you can click a tab with the wheel to close it. If you only have one tab open, you must close the browser window to close the tab.

You may also close the current tab by pressing Ctrl+W.

If you do not want to get the prompt of “close current tab or close all tabs” when you close Internet Explorer, then you may follow these steps.

a)    Open Internet Explorer and click on Tools.

b)    Click on Internet Options and under General tab click on settings next to ‘Tabs’ at the bottom.

c)    Uncheck "Warn me when closing multiple tabs"
