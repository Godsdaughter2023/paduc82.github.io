john korchok say:

A design department needs every weight, but users get confused. It's a better approach to design templates for users with only 4-6 weights, you'll get more consistent and professional documents and presentations from ordinary users.

Windows font families have 4 members. Traditionally these are Regular, Italic, Bold and Bold Italic. Office is geared to use font families, where you switch fonts by clicking on the Bold and/or Italic buttons. If you have more than 4 weights, you must have more than 1 font family. A common strategy is to package the Light and Extra Bold in a separate family. Typographers have the tools to create font families.

Proxima Nova is by Mark Simonson. He's a reasonable person and will be able to repackage the weights in whatever families you prefer. Expect an additional charge for this service. Make sure he knows this is for cross-platform use. Here's his web site: Proxima Nova by Mark Simonson
