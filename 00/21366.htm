john korchok say:

Pages are arbitrary constructs created by Word on the fly. So you can move content, but you can't move pages. 

If page 7 ends with a Page Break or Section Break, select all the text on page 7 and include the break. Cut it, go to page 2, click at the top of the page and paste.

If page 7 is just part of the text of the document, without page or section breaks, select all the text on the page, cut it, click at the top of page 2 and paste.

Okay I was afraid I didn't quite have it right. The following formula will work as long as only one of the criteria (i.e. "on hold", "NA" or "descoped") is in the Notes field string. If multiple criteria appear, the formula will grab the first one and ignore the others. Another caution about working with text strings. A simple spelling error in the notes string (e.g. "in hold" instead of "on hold" or "descopped" instead of "descoped") will not be caught. And finally, looking for text string might give unexpected results if that text string appears anywhere in the full notes text. The "NA" is most likely to be prone to this but if the notes wording your show in your last reply is the exact wording that is used, you will be okay. I've done a lot of work with text strings, so I tend to be wary of the little things that can cause problems.

IIf([Number1]>0 And instr(1,lcase([Notes]),"on hold")>0,"on hold",IIf([Number1]>0 And instr(1,lcase([Notes]),"na")>0,"na",IIf([Number1]>0 And instr(1,lcase([Notes]),"descoped")>0,"descoped","")))

Go for it!

Yes I can help you with the time span formula. Use this formula in an extra text field (Note: this assumes you have a normal work day of 8 hours or 480 minutes and that you want to measure the difference in working days, not calendar days)
IIf(ProjDateDiff([Current Date],[Finish])/480<=45,"now",IIf(projDateDiff([Current Date],[Finish])/480<=90,"soon","later"))

Also, one of your original requirements was to check the start date of tasks and see if it was in the current year. If not, return "TBC". The following formula will do that:
IIf(Year([Start])>year([Current Date]),"TBC","")

Further, if you want to concatenate the above two formula results into a single text field, this combined formula will do that: Note the comma and space to separate the result of each formula part.
IIf(ProjDateDiff([Current Date],[Finish])/480<=45,"now",IIf(projDateDiff([Current Date],[Finish])/480<=90,"soon","later")) & ", " & IIf(Year([Start])>year([Current Date]),"TBC","")
