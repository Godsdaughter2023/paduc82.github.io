If you want to associate another account to your OneDrive app in Windows 10, you need to remove the account that is currently setup before you associate another OneDrive account. To remove the current account, please perform the following steps:

  1. Right-click the OneDrive icon in the notification area, at the far right of the taskbar. (You might need to click the "Show hidden icons" arrow next to the notification area to see the icon.)

rob koch say:

Contact the bank that issued your father's checking account and ask them what you should do.  Most likely they will simply stop payment on the check, but if they want to cancel the account and open another, then follow their recommendation.

Overreacting to the mistake is just as foolish as the original error, so stop, take a breath and don't panic.  This is exactly what the malicious actors (e.g. scammers, really just another form of con men) want you to do.  So stop doing what they want and try to think!

Sit your father down and ask him exactly what they had him do, what information he provided to them, including any account or login information for his PC.  If he truly can't remember at all, only then do you need to do use the nuclear option and change or reinstall absolutely everything.  In most cases the issue is smaller than it at first seems, with only a single check, account or credit card at true risk.

The computer is a more difficult decision though, since the question of what the scammer actually did can't be easily confirmed once remote access has been allowed.  That's why most people will tell you to completely reinstall the operating system from scratch, since that's the only way to be sure the scammers can't gain access in the future, even if the true chances of this seem unlikely.

Worry about contacting the bank about the check and any other financial concerns first.  Just leave the computer turned completely off and unplug the power cable until you have the time to properly address that, since they can't access a PC that's powered off and unplugged.

Use your own PC to review any instructions and download any software required to place on a USB or other removable media, since the compromised PC can't be trusted if remote access had been allowed.

Bruce's references will contain more details, but the above is basically an outline of what this will entail, so start with the bank and do your best to protect his finances first, the rest can wait.

roberth---01 say: Way around this i think via pc suite drag tone to your cpu right click on tone go to properties in properties you should be able to change the protection

petrib say: There is no actual "protection" on the file itself to change. If the file is on the phone, and is of a specific type (specific file extension in the name is the only thing that matters, actually), the phone will not send/forward it.

On some phones you can actuall change the file name to something else (e.g., change .mid to .xyz) and then send it. The receiving device must then be able to save it and name it back, or it might not work as intended.
