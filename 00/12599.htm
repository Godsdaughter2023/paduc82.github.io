To recover from the issue, you must run the Microsoft Windows Resource Checker tool, also know as “System File Checker” or “sfc”. This tool scans and verifies the integrity of all protected system files and replaces incorrect versions with correct versions.

  1. Right click on the Start button of Windows and choose: Command Prompt (Admin).
    • If you don’t see this, instead choose: Windows PowerShell (Admin)
  2. Behind the prompt type: sfc /scannow
    (note the space in the command)
  3. Wait until the process completes. This usually doesn’t take longer than 20 minutes with a regular hard disk and takes much shorter when you have a fast SSD drive.
  4. Restart your computer.

Does anyone know how can i downgrade my lumia 610 from existing windows 7.8 to 7.5 mango? (cause of slow speed)

a. user say:

Hello maxangelo32:

 

Thank you for the follow up.  There are a couple things you might want to try to improve the response of your phone.  Before I get to those I will discuss the option of restoring the previous OS to your phone.

 

If the phone back up was removed when you did the uninstall, I am afraid the option of restoring is going to be difficult to impossible.  Before sending you elsewhere to attempt this I would like you to review your computer for the back up file.  The phone back should be saved to the following location, C:\Users\yourname\AppData\Local\Microsoft\Windows Phone Update.  If you do not find a back up here, the only option is to contact your mobile operator or phone manufacturer.  They may be able to install a previous OS manually to the phone.  If they cannot we will need to try and improve the phone's performance the best we can.

 

There are a few things I might suggest you try to get the phone working a bit faster:

  • Try uninstalling apps from your phone.  I have seen reports of behavior like yours being caused by a specific app or by having many apps on the phone.  I would suggest you try uninstalling any apps from the phone that are still available in the marketplace.  You can verify the apps are currently available here, http://www.windowsphone.com/en-us/store/overview.  Once you have uninstalled all apps, restart the phone and monitor behavior.  If you notice faster response, start installing apps one by one and monitor behavior after each install.  You may be able to pinpoint the app that is causing this.  If behavior is not improved, install all your apps again.
  • Perform a clearing of your temp files.  First, go to settings > applications > Internet Explorer > delete history.  Once you have done this, soft reset your phone by pulling the battery for about 3 minutes, replacing and rebooting the phone.  Monitor behavior of the phone after this.
  • Consider a hard reset of the phone.  Keep in mind that this will set the phone back to it's factory settings.  Media can be backed up to your PC, http://support.xbox.com/en-US/zune/software/syncing-media.  Contacts and calendar events should be backed up to the cloud.  You can verify this by going to http://contacts.live.com and http://calendars.live.com.  The information on your phone should be listed here.  Text message history and game progress will be reset.  To reset the phone, tap settings > about > reset your phone.

Please let us know if you have any other questions or concerns.

 

Mark W.
