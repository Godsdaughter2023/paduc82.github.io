Hi J.B.M.,

Having some problems in pairing your Bluetooth devices with your Windows 10 laptops may have been caused by outdated drivers or possible that the Bluetooth Support service is not enabled.

For us to diagnose and address this issue, please follow the methods below:

  • Restart the Bluetooth Support Service
  1. In your Cortana search, type in Services.
  2. Click Services in the search results.
  3. Under Services (Local), look for Bluetooth Support Service, right-click it, and click Restart.
  4. Right-click it again and this time, click Properties.
  5. Make sure the Startup type is set to Automatic.
  6. Click Apply, then OK.

  • Enable the Bluetooth Audio Service
  1. Right-click the Windows key and click Control Panel.
  2. Under Hardware and Sound, click View devices and printers.
  3. In Devices and Printers, locate the Bluetooth speaker device. Right-click on it, click Properties and navigate to Services tab.
  4. Make sure to select Audio Sink, Hands free Telephony and Remote Control.
  5. Click Apply.

  • Update Bluetooth Device Driver
  1. Right-click the Windows key and click Device Manager.
  2. Expand Bluetooth.
  3. Right-click your Bluetooth connection, click Properties, and then select the Driver tab.
  4. Click Update Driver.
  5. Click OK.

For more information about updating drivers in Windows 10, check out this article.

Let us know if you need further help.
