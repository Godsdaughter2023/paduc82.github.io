[New Office 365 portal] Adding and managing domains - Microsoft Community

maor bracha say:

[New Office 365 portal] Adding and managing domains

Technical Level : Basic

Summary

Adding domains and enabling services is probably the first action taken when setting up a new Office 365 tenant, and may also be required if you would like to add a new domain to your existing service.

This Wiki article will describe the process of adding, verifying and enabling new domains for Office 365 services using the new Office 365 portal.

*Note: This article refers to "the new" Office 365 portal, if you're here looking for ways to access the old domain management page- please use the button to the upper right of your screen, labeled "Go to the old admin center" (as shown in figure #1).


Details

Getting Started

Accessing the domain management page

First thing's first- navigating to the domain management page.

If it's your first time around you will be prompted to "go to setup" (as shown in figure #1). Using this button will take you directly to add your first domain. 

On the other hand, you might want to add multiple domains, add new ones or modify existing settings on a later stage. To access the main domain management page at any time, please click the settings button on the navigation pane to the left of your screen and choose "Domains".

Figure #1 - Main page.

Adding a new domain

Once navigated to the domain management page, you will notice a list of your existing domains and their status (which may vary between "completed", "setup required", etc.). In our example, highlighted blue in figure #2, we only see the default domain assigned to me upon registration as we did not add any more domains yet.

To initiate the process, we click on the "Add Domain" button (highlighted green in figure #2).

Figure #2 - Domain management page, adding a new domain.


A new interactive box will appear, asking you to specify your desired domain name. In our example, the domain to be added is "O365lab.Knowit.Se" (highlighted green in figure #3)

Figure #3 - Adding a new domain. 

After specifying the domain, we have to prove the we own the domain.

In order to do so, we are required to create one of two records- TXT or MX. It is strongly suggested to use TXT records, as those have no actual technical meaning other that plain text, while MX records are used for mail exchange (hence: may degrade service if adding a domain that already exists on another platform for future migration.

To select what kind of record you would like to use, simply toggle the switch between "TXT" and "MX", as shown in Figure #4. The records will show under.

If you do not manage your own domain and expect waiting times, it is safe to use the "Save and close" button in order to keep the progress so far and allowing you to return (using the domain management page) once the records have been created.

Figure #4 - Verification DNS records.

DNS Management

Verifying domain ownership

It is now time to prove ownership over the designated domain in order to enable it for Office 365 services. To do so we have to create the DNS record provided (as explained and shown above).

In our example I am using "o365lab.knowit.se", so I have to create a TXT record in the "Knowit.se" DNS zone, with the "o365lab" as host name. In case you are using a top level domain, your record name would show "@" (in other words: empty).

Once the record is created, you can navigate back to your domain management page, and start the domain setup wizard (or, if you didn't close the window yet- click "verify" as shown in figure #4 above).

You will notice your domain shows as "setup incomplete", as we did not verify the domain or add relevant service records yet. Simply click it to initiate the wizard.

If successful, you will be taken to the added DNS records page, where you will be instructed to created relevant records to enable services such as Exchange online, Skype for business etc. 

Adding DNS records to enable services

Once your domain is verified, you would probably like to add the relevant records to enable services.

For your ease of use, the records are now grouped into categories. You can click each category to maximize and minimize for easier reading.

However, please note- if you're currently using Exchange on-premise or other mailing solution, Skype for business on-premise etc. then creating records should be done with care as it may effect your current environment. 

As an example, if you have Exchange 2013 on-premise and planning a hybrid, you should not add the MX records that will change your mailflow, autodiscver that will change Outlook behavior etc.

If in question, please advise the relevant deployment and\or hybrid infrastructure integration documentation. 

In case you conclude that you shouldn't create some of the records as explained, or wouldn't like to activate some of the services- you can mark the "Skip verification" button (highlighted at the bottom of figure #5)

Figure #5 - Adding DNS records to enable Office 365 services.


Possible services issues?

If you decided not to create some of the records at this stage for any reason (or in the improbable case of actual DNS issues) you will see that your domain is highlighted with "Possible services issues".

Clicking your domain will allow you to inspect issues. The inspection page will list all faulty and\or missing records, with an explanation as to why they are recognized as a possible service issue.

Go over your records and make sure that there aren't any actual errors- if you found any records that required changes and now fixed you can use the "Refresh" button to rerun the check.

In our example, we can see that the faulty record is the MX record, which I knowingly did not create - therefor, after verifying other records I can use the "Ignore incorrect DNS"

Figure #6 - Remediate DNS 

Wrapping up

Setting default domain

Congratulations! You have now configured your first domain. Now you might want to consider your default domain.

When opening a new Office 365 tenant you are assigned an @onmicrosoft.com domain as default. This means new users, mailboxes and so on will default to this address. 

Clicking your domain will allow you to set it as default.

(Note: this is optional and is up to you to decided which domain you'd like to be used as default)

Figure #8 - Set as default

Now you can see that my new domain "o365lab.Knowit.Se" is configured correctly, setup completed with no issues and is assigned as default.

'=CONCATENATE(Sheet1!C7,".",C6)'.
