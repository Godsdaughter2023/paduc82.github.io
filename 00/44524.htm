a. user say:

Windows' own backup utility (File History) was originally designed to take snapshots of individual data files that you could restore, if needed. It's great for individual files, but an awkward method for backing up all your data and it lacks important features that should be part of a complete backup process.

To backup all your data files at once, it's best to use a third-party backup application. With this type of software you can quickly and easily:

  • define and save multiple backup jobs;
  • schedule complete backups to run automatically, at a time you choose;
  • maintain a history of backups that you define; and
  • be notified when backups succeed and fail.

And that's just the beginning. In time you'll learn to do incremental backups, store backups in multiple locations, verify backups and many more advanced procedures.

Backing up your data folders is part of a complete backup process that includes backing up entire drives, which is the best way to backup and restore Windows and your installed software. This is easily accomplished with third-party software.
