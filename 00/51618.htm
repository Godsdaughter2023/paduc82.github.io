zigzag3143 (ms -mvp) say:
Americast123

With 32bit you can only get a maximum of around ~3.25gigs, windows then takes about half of the free ram to put in fast cache (for when an application requests memory), your video then takes whatever "shared memory" it can. 

SO 3.25 minus ~1.6gigs for cache minus ~600Mb shared video=~ 1gig which is what you are seeing.

This is perfectly normal and since it is 32 bit you cant add more ram.

All of this said RAM doesnt make your system faster, the only way you can change that is to change CPU.

considerate_guy say:

There is a known issue in the full screen preview.  When you are playing a clip that has text added to it, it causes the full screen preview to go blurry.  Essentially the preview just takes the small thumbnail version and shows that blown up to the full screen.

As a workaround, you can save your project, reopen it and it will fix the blurriness (at least until you add text to another clip).

However, this blurriness in the preview will have no effect on your final movie and that is where you should really judge the final movie.  You can save your movie at anytime (even before your final edits are done) to see how the movie is looking.  I often do that to confirm there aren't any issues/differences between the preview and the final movie.

To save your movie, use File > Save movie.
