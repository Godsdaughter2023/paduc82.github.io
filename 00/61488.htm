That is a registry cleaner, and is not related to Microsoft.  Don't waste your money on these...most are a complete scam, and the best of them can only make an extremely minor difference in PC performance, and at great risk.    They claim to speed up your PC by cleaning out old obsolete registry entries.  The registry is a database, so if it has a few extra entries that should have been removed when you uninstalled something, the only difference is a few bytes (very few!) of wasted storage on your hard drive and that's it.  But if you mistakenly clean the wrong entry, you can severely damage the operation of your PC.  It's not easy to identify obsolete entries vs. good entries, thus the risk is not worth it even for a free registry cleaner unless you know a lot about your PC and have backups, etc., etc.   Skip it.

sumit (volunteer moderator) say:
Hello,
Activation depends on what type of license you have of Windows 7/8/10.

If Windows 7/8/10 came preinstalled with the computer, that is called an OEM license. For an OEM license, the license dies as soon as Motherboard dies. So essentially it is non-transferable. If that is the case you need to get a new license.

However, this is not same for retail licenses i.e. you purchased the license through Microsoft store. That does carry transfer rights. If that is the case, please see the below steps:
(Not applicable to you)
What you need to do is to grab the key using any Software like Showkey Plus, uninstall that key from older PC and use it on another computer.

Download Showkey Plus:
www.thewindowsclub.com/showkeyplus-product-key-finder


Uninstall the key
https://winaero.com/blog/how-to-deactivate-wind...

Use the key by Showkey Plus on the newer computer.

Alternatively, there are two more options available other than this:
(Not applicable to you ends)
Contact Microsoft at http://www.support.microsoft.com/contactus through chat support. They would take control of your PC and activate(might need a proof of purchase)
Use the Activation troubleshooter on the new PC signed in with same MS account.
Go to Settings>Update and security>Activation and run the troubleshooter.
Select I recently changed hardware on this device.
