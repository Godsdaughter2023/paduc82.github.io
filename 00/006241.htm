rich michaels - mvp (greatcirclelearning.com) say:

Hi Nancy,

Your description is confusing... at least to me. :-) So let's go back to the basics.

The reference point for these setting (margin & edge) is the edge of the paper. Thus a bottom margin set at 1 inch mean that it is set 1-inch up from the bottom edge of the paper. The edge settings control where the last paragraph of the header or footer begins in relation to the edge (top or bottom) of the paper. So if you have a 1-inch bottom margin and an edge setting of 0.5 inches, the page number should be appearing 0.5 inches above the bottom edge of the paper and approximately 0.5 inches (you must adjust for the point size of the text) below the page margin. 

Using the above example if you make the "edge" setting 1-inch, the page number will be appearing very close to your last line of text in the body of the document.

What I am confused about is your saying the "bottom margin should be one inch... page number should be half and inch from the bottom margin..." but then you go on to say "which is one and a half inches from the page edge." If the page number is to appear 1.5 inches above the page edge and 0.5 inches below the bottom margin then your bottom margin needs to be set at 2-inches and the bottom "edge" set to 1.5 inches.

BTW - Gutter has nothing to do with this. It is a setting that is used along with "mirrored margins" for adding a little extra room that allows for binding the document via spiral binding, ring binding, jiffy binding, etc. For example, to allow for binding a document that is going to be printed 2-sided (front and back) and your left and right margins are set to the narrowest (0.5 inches) you would check the "mirrored margin" option and add a "gutter" of 0.3 inches to the outside edge (left). This provides the minimum amount of space required so that the binding does not destroy any text.

Hope this helps

firewuff say: The only Difference between SD and SD-HC is the disk format they use. Fat16 can only address less than 2 gig hence needing Fat32 to address it correctly.

The SD-HC is only marketing to make it easier for consumers. Depending on the devices (like a lot of cameras) SD-HC cards will still work once formatted in the device but you will only see 2Gig of capacity not the full 4 Gig or more.

How do I create a climate graph?

Original title: Climate graphs 

When I used excel office in past, I could produce what we call a "climate graph"
that is a column graph (for rainfall data) AND a line graph (for temperature data) in one, using two "y" axes
Now I am using Mac office I can not seem to do the same thing... any advice?
j.e. mcgimpsey say: One way:

Insert a column chart with both series (rainfall and temperature).

Right- or ctrl-click on a column in the temperature series and choose "Format Data Series". Select "Axis" in the left-hand listbox, then click the "Secondary axis" radio button.

Again, right- or ctrl-click a column in the temperature series and choose "Change Series Chart Type". Select a line graph style.
