greggrotyohann say:

Why is Internet Explorer so inconsistent?

Why do favicons suddenly stop displaying and then weeks later start again? Same thing with clicking a hyperlink in a web page. Usually the hyperlink will change from blue to purple. When favicons stop displaying, hyperlinks no longer change color.

robert aldwinckle on forums say:

When favicons stop displaying, hyperlinks no longer change color.


In fact, those are essentially independent features.   Favicons are stored (mainly) in the TIF (e.g. unless you have actually saved the site as a Favorite).    And the link coloring (where allowed) depends on your History (e.g. Ctrl-Shift-H, not the TIF).

BTW I really wish that IE had not confused the implementation description by calling both of these features "Browser History".   As I say, they are essentially independent features.   So, for example, you could have a favicon cached for a host even though it did not appear in your History, or, more likely, you could have a host listed in your History which no longer had a cached favicon, e.g. because the TIF was being cleared more often than the History.   Etc.

So, to answer your question you need to look at what has happened to both your TIF and your History separately.
