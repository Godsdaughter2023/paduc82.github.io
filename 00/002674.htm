Outlook 2016 Rules - Microsoft Community

tcathf say:

Outlook 2016 Rules

I am running Office 365 on three PCs, lets call them A,B, and C.

In Outlook 2016 I set up a rule to automatically move mail from the Hotmail Inboxe to a Local Folder named Mail In on the PC.

The Hotmail Inbox rule automatically appears on PC's A,B, and C, but only works on PC A where, in the rule list for Hotmail it is marked as (Client only).

On PC's B and C, where it doesn't work, it is marked in the Hotmail rule list as (Client only) (for other computer).

Any thoughts on a fix for this would be welcome.

david posso say:

Hi TcatHF,
I’m hopeful you’re feeling good today.

Let me first get a bit technical and then I’ll suggesting a fix for this issue.’
There are two types of rules, Server-side vs. client-only rules. Client-only will only work on the PC they were originally created on, they don’t run until you log into Outlook with the account that you used to create the rule and Server-side will work on any Outlook client independent of the PC you’re working on. That is because Server-side uses conditions and actions handled by the Exchange server, and these rules run whether or not you log in to Outlook on your computer.

Since you have your Hotmail, account configured as POP/IMAP (Only option) the rules you create on that Outlook profile are only going to be working on PC A in this case.

Source: https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Server...

Solution/Workaround:
You can import and export rules for Outlook messages so that others can use them on PC B and C.
Please follow these steps to export rules on PC A and then import them on PC B and C.

Source: https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Import...

Ultimately, you can just replicate those rules on PC B and C and that will just work just fine.

I really hope this information is useful to you.
- David
