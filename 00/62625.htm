Here's how I fixed the "remote procedure call failed" error when launching the windows store app.

I tried all the suggested fixes on my Surface Pro 4 (DISM, SFC, wsreset, powershell, etc).  None of them worked.  What did was a repair install of Windows 10.

Windows 10 has the ability to re-install itself over an existing copy without touching any of your files or installed applications.  It does this by installing off an ISO that is on your machines hard drive.  This fixed my RPC error, and it didn't mess up any of my installed applications.  I'd expect it'll fix your PC, and it has the added advantage to fix anything else that might be wrong that you don't know about (in my case, a few months prior to this issue, I had the windows app tiles grayed issue, which I fixed with a powershell command, but in retrospect I see that was only a temporary fix).  Considering what this does, I'm surprised it isn't recommended more often.  Here's what to do

1) get yourself a copy of the Windows 10 ISO (download from here)

2) save it to your hard drive, then double click it from file explorer (to mount it), then launch setup.exe from the mounted ISO

3) Follow the recommended settings ('get all updates', 'keep my existing files', etc.)

4) Let it run for about 30-45 minutes and then you will be good as new.

rajesh govind say:

 

Hi,

 

This could be because, you might have opted to have the small buttons on the Taskbar from its properties.  If you uncheck the option to have small buttons on the Taskbar, then that would increase the taskbar size and will display the date with the time on the notification area on the taskbar. You may follow these steps:

 

a)       Right click on the Taskbar and select “Properties”.

b)       On the “Taskbar” tab, uncheck the option “Use small Taskbar buttons”.

c)        Click on “Apply” and then “OK”.

d)       Now check if that display the date with the time on the notification area.

 

Hope this is helpful.

expertbigwheelpilot2 say:

Rajesh Govind, your suggestion of using larger tray icons to make the taskbar wider helped me to figure out how to fix this.  I went to taskbar properties, unchecked the “Lock the taskbar”.  Grab the edge of taskbar with mouse and pulled it wider by two millimeters and the date appeared.  I went back to taskbar properties, checked the “Lock the taskbar”.  Done.  Problem solved.

 

Thank you Rajesh Govind, I would not have thought of the solution without you.
