josh-h say: Hi, 

From what I understand, the allocated RAM is not always exactly what Minecraft is actually using. 

So for example, if you allocated 3GB of RAM to Minecraft and run the game - the game may only be using 1GB, or 2GB or anything up the 3GB that you've allocated. The remaining RAM will not be used by the game unless it's needed. 

When the game is not running, that 3GB of RAM should be available for anything else running on the PC. You're not reserving anything for just the game, unless the game is up and running.

susan de leeuw say:

am pm displayed on welcome screen.

 nowhere on my pc have i opted for anything other than the 24 hr clock but my windows 10 welcome screen before logging on  gives the time am pm and I want to change it.   how do i do this?

steve cochran say:

You have to change a setting in the registry.  Go to this key:

HKEY_USERS\.DEFAULT\Control Panel\International

Then change the value of the "sTimeFormat" from

h:mm:ss tt

to

H:mm:ss tt

and change the "sShortTime" from

h:mm tt

to

H:mm tt

You just need to change the value from lower case "h" to upper case "H", which will denote 24 hour time.  Then reboot and it will show the 24 hour date prior to logging in.

steve
