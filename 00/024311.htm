how can i find the intersection between two graph? - Microsoft Community

kinan zayat say:

how can i find the intersection between two graph?

actually i have a series of number and a function that i plotted them into the same graph and i need to find the intersection between the two lines, is it possible

Thanks in advance
j.e. mcgimpsey say: AFAIK, there's no way to find the intersection based on the graph itself. However a tiny bit of algebra may come in useful.

Assuming your series of numbers/function describe linear functions (this will work with higher-order functions, but linear makes the example simple), the equation of the line is

y = slope * x + intercept

For your two lines:

      y1 = m1x + b1 and  y2 = m2x + b2

where the two functions intersect (assuming they do), y1=y2, so

    m1x + b1  = m2x + b2    or, rearranging:    (b2-b1) / (m1-m2)

To keep things simple, assume you have X's in a range named Xs, your first Y's in a range named Y1s, and your second set of Y's in a range named Y2s. Then the X value at the intersection (entered into a cell named, say, X_int):

    =(INTERCEPT(Y2s, Xs) - INTERCEPT(Y1s, Xs)) / (SLOPE(Y1s, Xs) - SLOPE(Y2s, Xs))

Then the Y value at the intersection is just

    =TREND(Y1s, Xs, X_int)

Note that if your data points vary significantly from linear, the calculated intersection may be widely off the mark from the actual point-to-point intersection.

Another approach might be to set up a Goal Seek or Solver scenario where you iteratively approach Y1 = Y2, but for this example, IMO, it's overkill.

Setting indents for multiple bulleted lists at once - Microsoft Community

wildetudor say:

Setting indents for multiple bulleted lists at once

I have several bulleted lists in my document, and they all for some reason have different levels of indentation, with regards to the distances between the bullet point and the (1) paper margin to its left, and (2) text to its right; settings I thought were governed respectively by the Left and First Line settings under Paragraph. I would like to homogenise these indents across the document, for all bulleted lists.

To that end, I right clicked the style ListParagraph and chose Select All Instances. The ticks ruler disappeared so I could not set the indents from there, which would have been easiest. I then tried playing around with the two settings mentioned under Paragraph - but since the values dont mean anything to me, and there is no apply button to instantly see the effect, it is very hard to adjust to an appropriate value.

Can anyone help? many thanks!

suzanne s. barnhill say:

Although you can change the indents in normal paragraphs (and styles) in the Paragraph dialog, in bulleted or numbered paragraphs/styles, all indents at the left must be set in the Define New Multilevel List dialog.

The List Paragraph style is applied when you apply bullets using the Bullets button in the Paragraph group on the Home tab. You can use Adjust List Indents to change the indent settings of paragraphs in this style, but you haven't actually modified the style (if you select a paragraph and press Ctrl+Q, you not only get the default indent back, but you remove the bullet). Although you can modify many aspects of this style, in order to change the indents, you would have to link it to a level in the Define New Multilevel List dialog, but it is not listed as an available style there, so this isn't possible.

All of which is to say that the built-in bullets and the List Paragraph style are not suitable to your requirements. If, instead, you apply the List Bullet style (which you can do by pressing Ctrl+Shift+L), you will get a bulleted paragraph that is flush left with a quarter-inch hanging indent, which may well be what you want. If not, you can modify the List Bullet style both through the Paragraph dialog (for everything but indents) and through the Define New Multilevel List dialog (where you set indents and link the level to the List Bullet style).

For more on this (and probably clearer), see http://shaunakelly.com/word/bullets/controlbullets20072010.html
