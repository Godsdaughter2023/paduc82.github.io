SP3 Docking Station Audio Distortion/Clipping - Microsoft Community

sp3owner say:

SP3 Docking Station Audio Distortion/Clipping

Hi!


Audio at about >80% output level from the 3.5mm jack of the SP3 docking station is distorted by excessive amplification and clipping in the driver (both with usbaudio.sys and the newest usbaud64.sys which is available through an optional Windows update) or a lower-level stage. This happens regardless of the audio API (WASAPI shared/WASAPI exclusive/WaveOut/DirectSound) or any driver sound enhancements. It is not a speaker problem and independent of the speaker volume level.


You can reproduce this by comparing 2 scenarios:


1. Connect speakers to the docking station output jack, right-click the speaker symbol, under "Playback Devices/Playback/Microsoft Docking Station Device/Properties" set "Levels/Speakers" to 100 (max) and click "Test" under "Advanced". Notice the distorted sound. Now set levels to 80, test again - no (audible) distortion. Don't forget to make sure any driver sound enhancements (particularly bass boost) are disabled.


2. Now connect the speakers to the SP3 headphone jack instead, under the properties of this device set levels to 100 (max) and test again - no distortion/clipping. Expectedly, the overall maximum output is about as loud as the docking station output at level 80, therefore it seems there's some unwanted amplifying followed by clipping of the signal done by the docking station driver. Using the Windows high definition audio device driver (hdaudio.sys) or the newest Realtek drivers makes no difference.


Both usbaudio.sys (distortion occurs at about >80) and hdaudio.sys (no distortion at 100) were version 6.3.9600.16384. The corresponding docking station driver to usbaudio.sys was 6.3.9600.16490 and 1.31.22.5 to usbaud64.sys. Neither resetting the SP3 nor disconnecting all USB devices solved this problem.


Please let me know if you experience this, too. Insights/Suggestions/Solutions are greatly appreciated, thanks!

EDIT:

This person already had the same issue with the SP2 dock: http://anewdomain.net/2013/10/26/surface-pro-docking-station/

"I also found that when I plug into the audio connectors on the stand instead of directly into the machine, I’m over-driving the audio on my speakers and have to bring the volume down to reduce distortion. Otherwise, when connected directly to the machine and not the docking station, the audio is really well-done and enjoyable."

Please fix this with the next update!
