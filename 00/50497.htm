This could be due to sound card failure or sound driver missing/corrupted/outdated. Let's try the following methods to update/uninstall/re-install the sound driver.

Method 1: Update the sound driver from Device Manager

a. Click Start and type Device Manager in search bar, click it

b. Click Sound, video, and game controllers to expand.

c. Under Sound, video, and game controllers, double-click the entry for your sound card, and then click the Driver tab.

Note: If a Driver tab is not present, the entry that you clicked under Sound, video, and game controllers may not be the correct entry for your sound card. Try clicking any additional entries listed under Sound, video, and game controllers.

d. Click Update Driver Software.

e. Follow the Update Driver Software Wizard to update the sound card driver.

Method 2: If the issue persists, uninstall the sound driver from Device Manager

a. Click Start and type Device Manager in search bar, click it

b. Click Sound, video, and game controllers to expand.

c. Under Sound, video, and game controllers, double-click the entry for your sound card, and then click the Driver tab.

d. Right click the driver and click Uninstall

e. Restart the computer

Method 3: Visit the computer manufacturer's support website and install the latest sound driver available for the same computer model as yours.

Hope this information is helpful. Please feel free to reply in case you face any other issues with Windows in future.
