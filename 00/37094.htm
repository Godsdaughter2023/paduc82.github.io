dhavelyn mer say:

Hi Margaret,

Bluetooth pairing errors can be associated with both hardware and software issues. The Remove Device option can be missing due to incompatible Bluetooth drivers that should be installed first before you can successfully connect it to your PC. Other possible reasons are outdated hardware firmware, numerous data shared between devices, and interference or proximity issues.

For us to further isolate the concern, we will be needing answers to the following questions:

  • What is the exact error message that you received when trying to pair the Bluetooth device on your Windows 10 PC?
  • Are there any wireless devices that can cause interference on the pairing process?

As an initial step, let's make a setting to show all hidden devices. This method is useful in detecting unknown devices that can block the pairing action. To do this, kindly follow the procedures below:

  1. Press Windows logo + X.
  2. Click on Device Manager.
  3. Click on View.
  4. Click on Hidden Devices.

Windows 10 comes with a Snap assist feature which is similar to the Split option that you used in Windows 8.1.

When you drag a window to the left or right, just move it towards a corner, and you'll see an animated transparent outline of the window fill out the quadrant. As soon as you release the mouse button, the window will snap to that position.
