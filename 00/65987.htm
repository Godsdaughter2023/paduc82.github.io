kerry kay say:

Mikayla,

 

I came across your question after several unsuccessful late late nights of failed attempts to load audio and PPT files to Panopto (my university's recording program).  I bailed on that and came across your question on the forum.  I tried different combinations of PPT setting options overnight (I'm THAT fed up with Panopto).  Here's what I learned:

  • You can save a .wmv of your PPT and insert it into Movie Maker; then add your audio (as a separate file); then align the audio with the start point of your .wmv within Movie Maker before you save the combination as a movie.
  • Alternatively, you can embed (or link) your audio file to your PPT, then save a .wmv of it with the audio. 

I tried combinations of the following options.  One resulted in a smooth-flowing nice quality .wmv movie file with audio.  One combination resulted in a movie -- minus the audio.  Another resulted in a movie, but the video quality was poor. 

 

I won't have time again for awhile to to sort out which combination worked.  Maybe you can?  Play with these PPT combinations, and it should give you a recognizeable file for Movie Maker (where you can add the audio) -- or a combined audio/video .wmv directly from PPT:

 

  • Engage your "Rehearse Timings" feature.  It's under the "Slide Show" tab).  Click on "use timings" and "play narrations".  Then, play your audio file in the background while you advance the slides as appropriate.  Finally, confirm when asked that you want to apply the rehearsal timings each time it plays.
  • Insert your audio to slide 1 of your PPT (Insert --> audio --> audio from file).  Go to custom animation, then change the timing of your audio settings (start playing from beginning, hide symbol, play through all slides -- or set it to stop after slide X).  Play the presentation to verify it's engaging the audio and advancing as desired. 
  • Verify your resolution is the way you want it.  That may have been part of my problem with the lower quality version.  Seems like the "current resolution" probably gave me that, whereas I tried once in the highest fidelity option setting.
  •  Under the "File" tab, there is a "Send and save" option.  Within that, you'll find an option to "Create a video."  It takes awhile for it to save/convert, so be patient.  (I was using a 20-min. lecture)  Eventually, it saved a .wmv with audio and rehearsed timings intact. 

 

I also messed with the "Set up slide show" and "Record slide show" options at one point.  However, I don't know that either of those factored into creating a functional .wmv.  I could be wrong about that. 

 

Either way, it looks like I can pass on Panopto entirely.  That just made my year!  :-)
