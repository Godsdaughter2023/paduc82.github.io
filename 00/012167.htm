hansv mvp say:

ThisWorkbook refers to the workbook that contains the code. So if you store the code in your personal macro workbook Personal.xlsb, ThisWorkbook refers to Personal.xlsb, not to the workbook containing the pivot table.

You could use ActiveWorkbook instead of ThisWorkbook if you want to refer to Sheet1 in the active workbook.

If you want to refer to the active sheet regardless of its name, use ActiveSheet:

Set Data_Sheet = ActiveSheet
Set Pivot_Sheet = ActiveSheet

vic_vs say:

Hi PaulS_36,

 

Thank you for coming to Bing Community today regarding your concern with the Bing Picture of the Day.

 

Bing works with a number of different image providers to license images for use on the homepage which is also the same images featured when you have the Bing Desktop app installed on your computer.

 

It is indeed a great option if we can handpick an image from the Bing images to set up as our desktop wallpaper.

 

You may opt to choose your preferred Bing wallpaper through the Bing Desktop. However, this setting only lasts a day due to the automatic ticking of "Make the Bing homepage image your desktop background" once you select your preferred image.
 
Here's how:
1. Open the Bing Desktop application.
2. Click on the (i) icon (when you hover your mouse, it will show “select desktop wallpaper”)
3. You’ll then be able to view the different Wallpapers the past 9 days.
4. Click on the right or left arrow to navigate and select your preferred image for your desktop.
5. The check box for “Make the Bing homepage image your desktop background” will be checked automatically.
 
 

Please note though that the available wallpapers on the Bing Desktop are the images for the last 9 days so expect that after 9 days, the image you wish to be your default would no longer be in the list.
