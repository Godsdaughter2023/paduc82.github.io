daniel g. grau say:
Try this:

  1. Download the Harvard Anglia source code from BibWord (it's the blue link at the top).
  2. The downloaded file will be called "HarvardAnglia.XSL.xml." Delete the last four characters in the name (the file name should be "HarvardAnglia.XSL").
  3. Quit Word, if it's running.
  4. In the Finder, go to Applications > Microsoft Office 2011 and make a duplicate of Word (select it and press Command D). The duplicate will probably loose it's icon, but this is just cosmetic.
  5. Control-click (or right--click) on the copy of Word and select Show package contents.
  6. Locate this folder: Contents > Resources > Styles.
  7. Drop the downloaded (and renamed) file in there, together with APA, Chicago, Turabian and the rest. (You may notice and extra copy of Turabian outside this folder; leave it alone, but I believe it's a distribution error.)
  8. Close Word's Finder's window and launch the new copy of Word. There will be a slight delay as it rebuilds its font database.
  9. You should now have a new "Harvard - Anglia*" option in the Ribbon's Document Elements tab, under References.
Test-drive the hacked copy of Word for a while. Work on a copy of your file! If all runs well, go back to Applications > Microsoft Office 2011, control-click (or right-click) on the original version of Word and choose Compress. (This will let you keep a copy of the original software around, in case the new copy crashes or fails to update for whatever reason.) Finally, rename the hacked copy to "Word," as another name may prevent if from interacting wit other Office components.

What is Click to Run

Click-to-Run is a new Electronic Software Distribution (ESD) technology for Office 2010 that utilizes Microsoft’s streaming and virtualization, “computer within a computer”, technology. Click-to-Run provides a much faster and simpler download and installation experience for customers trying or buying Office 2010 over the Internet. Click-to-Run allows customers to maintain multiple versions of Office so customers can easily try Office 2010 side by side with an existing version of Office. Click-to-Run automatically downloads and installs any software updates when connected to the Internet, helping to maintain a more secure and up-to-date Office experience. At this time, only Office Home and Student 2010 and Office Home and Business 2010 will be available through Click-to-Run and limited based on broadband speed requirements.

NOTE:  CTR is a relatively new approach to installing and running Office, Introduced with 2010. So, very few technicians are aware of it, let alone familiar with trouble shooting it. As well, it introduces a whole new set of errors, like the one that you encountered. My personal recommendation is that unless you have a clearly identified need for it and explicitly requested the download, DON’T USE IT.
