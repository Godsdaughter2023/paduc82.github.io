Updating citations to Chicago Manual of Style. - Microsoft Community

amymortensen say:

Updating citations to Chicago Manual of Style.

My Office 2016 has the 16th edition of the Chicago Manual of Style loaded and I need the 17th edition.  How do  I update this?  I have googled and I find no information!

jay freedman say:

Microsoft appears to have fallen behind in updating its citation styles to the most current versions. Among the most used style manuals, Chicago is in its 17th edition but the latest builds of Word have the 16th; the MLA manual is in its 8th edition but Word has the 7th; and Turabian is in its 8th edition but Word has the 6th. The APA style is still current at the 6th edition, but that dates from 2009.

The citation generator in Word relies on an XML style sheet (a file with extension .xsl) to handle the logic of the various types of citations (books, articles, web sites, multiple authors, etc.) for each style. The article at https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/office/vba/word/Concepts/Objects-Properties-Methods/create-custom-bibliography-styles explains -- more or less -- how to create your own style sheet. Although starting from an existing .xsl file (in this case, C:\Program Files (x86)\Microsoft Office\root\Office16\Bibliography\Style\CHICAGO.XSL) will be easier than starting from scratch, it'll still be a daunting project to (a) figure out exactly what parts of the style changed from the 16th edition to the 17th and then (b) figure out how to express those changes in a copy of the .xsl file.

A program named BibWord for generating .xsl style sheets was created more than a decade ago by Yves Dhondt. Its remains are still at https://bibword.codeplex.com/, which was shut down last year. It has migrated to https://github.com/codingo/BibWord.
