Dim db As DAO.Database

Dim frm As Form

For Each frm In db.Forms

   If frm.Name <> "Globals" Then

Next frm

This has worked in the past and continues to work on other documents, however in this document, my footer has remained the same. Is there, perhaps, a setting I am not aware of or something I might have done incorrectly? Thanks in advance for the help!
jay freedman say:
Verify the section numbers in that footer, the preceding one, and the following one. It's possible that there's a Continuous section break hiding somewhere on one of those pages, and that the footer that isn't changing belongs to a different section than you think it does.

If that turns out to be the case, use the Find dialog to look for the code ^b (representing a section break). Sometimes you won't be able to see the break in Print Layout view, even with nonprinting characters displayed, but Draft view should reveal it.

jay freedman say:
Temporarily insert two manual page breaks (Ctrl+Enter) somewhere inside the two-column section (which is Section 6), separated by a paragraph or two. Then there will be one complete page that's entirely within Section 6, and you'll be able to see and manipulate the Section 6 header and footer.

  • Turn off Different First Page for Section 6.
  • Make the Section 6 footer Same As Previous (which it should already be), so the Section 5 footer text will appear in Section 6 and pass through to Section 7.
  • Turn off the Same As Previous for the Section 6 header. Then select the text there and delete it.
  • In Section 7, turn off Same As Previous in the header and insert the new text. Make the footer Same As Previous so it will get its text from Section 6, which in turn is getting it from Section 5.


Finally, delete the manual page breaks.
