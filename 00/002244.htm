Carl's Wife:

 A couple of clarifications.

You can continue to edit your files created in Office 2003 in ALL SUBSQUENT versions of Office up to and including 2013.  You can keep them in the 2003 format or "upgrade" them to the newer format at your will.

Although you can go back and forth between versions it is my experience that you are better off if you don't. While editing a document back and forth in multiple versions does work, I have found that infrequently, but still too often, that subtle document file structure corruption can sneak in when you hop between versions. The "official" party line is that you can do it with impunity.

Your (2003) Office Pro "Retail" license is good for install on up to 2 computers at the same time.  You also have the right to transfer those installations to new computers.

Office 2003 does work on Windows 8 (I am running that combo on my machine at this moment).

Personally I would suggest you skip 2007 and go directly to 2010, unless you can get enough 2007 licenses really cheap.  Functionally 2007/2010/2013 are essentially the same when it comes to features used by "the average user".  2010 does have a number of refinements over 2007 and since it is newer it will have a longer life.
