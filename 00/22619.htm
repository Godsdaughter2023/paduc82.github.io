marshall barton say:

As John said, there is no way to tell if the report actually printed.  A standard way of dealing with this uncertainty is to have a Date/Time field (named Printed)  in the employee table.  Then just before you run the OpenReport code to print the report, set that Printer text box/field to Now indicating when you intended to print the report.  If the report fails to produce an acceptable printout or someone needs another copy, you can then try to print it again by filtering on the Printed field.

For a historical record, if the data might be changed, you may also want to copy the data that is to be printed to an archive table so it can be printed again at any time in the future (e.g. year end, next year or whatever).  If you do this, then the Printed date should go in the archive table at the time the data is copied.

carloseap say:

Hello,

 

Sounds like a data corruption problem on your 5800xm.

The first thing you need to do is a full backup of your phone and memory card. Search the forums on how to do that, using Nokia PC Suite.

Have you installed any themes or applications recently? Try uninstalling the themes first, could be that a non compatible s60v5 theme is causing the problem.

If that does not solve the issue, you will need to format your phone. Make sure you know how to recover any licenses (Nokia Maps) or songs downloaded from the Nokia Music store, since this procedure will wipe out everything on the phone, (contacts, applications, bookmarks, etc.). Again, search the forums for help on how to backup and restore information before you try anything.

 

Once you have successfully backed up your info, turn the phone off.

With the phone off, simultaneously press and hold the green, red and camera shutter buttons and without releasing, turn the phone on (it's a bit tricky). Keep pressing and holding the three buttons until you see the Nokia logo on the phone screen. Release the buttons. You will know you have successfully formated your phone if it asks you for your location and local time immediately after reboot.

 

When I do this I also format the memory card before the reset, just to make sure I start with a fresh OS. In this state, you can also check for new firmware updates using Nokia Software update, and apply them as needed. Again, make sure you fully understand how to backup and restore your data before trying it.

 

I hope this helps you to solve your problem.
