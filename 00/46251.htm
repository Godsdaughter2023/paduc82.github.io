dub dublin say:

No, don't do any of that stuff - it won't work, and you'll finally fall through to the last step, which is a full "Reset" of your PC, which is a huge pain in the butt that requires you to reload your data and worse, all your programs.

Instead, try this first (this was learned by multiple trips to the local Microsoft Store to fix this problem):

1) Make sure you have all your data saved and no apps running, then right-click on the Start Menu and Select Device Manager

2) Double-click on "Human Interface Devices" to open that section

3) Scroll down to "I2C HID Device"

4) I'm betting that you'll see the little yellow warning triangle with an exclamation point on that item - if so, you're in luck - here's how to fix it  (If you don't see it, disregard the rest of this - I don't know how to help you):

5) Right-click on the I2C HID Device entry and select "Uninstall".  Yes, this will uninstall the device driver from the system, but we need to do that, because the one you've got has somehow become corrupted.  (I2C is an embedded device bus used in the Surface to connect the accelerometer and other position sensors, so this makes sense.)

6) Right-click on the Start Menu again and select "Restart" under "Shut down or sign out"

7) After the restart, Windows should have noticed the missing driver and reinstalled it.  On rare occasions, you need to restart again for unknown reasons.

This process has worked for me four times now as my Surface (either Surface Pro 3 or Surface Pro 4) has lost the ability to detect its rotation.  I've been able to fix it this way every time.

I have no idea why this sort of thing keeps happening or why Microsoft doesn't fix the underlying problem, but this is a reliable, if slightly fiddly, fix you can do yourself, and it works. 

(Well, it works until it doesn't again.  You might want to save this, or at least a link to it - I didn't the first time, which is why I had to go to the Microsoft Store for this twice - the first time, I didn't make them show me what they did, and I didn't realize the problem will crop up again every couple of months...)
