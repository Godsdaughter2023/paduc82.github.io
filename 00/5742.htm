You can do this with conditional formatting.

Select all the columns and rows that you want this to apply to then:-

=MOD(ROW(),2)=1

Click the format button | Fill tab, choose a colour and OK out.

=MOD(ROW(),2)=0

a. user say:

Hi, 

Thank you for posting your query in Microsoft Community Forum.

Please be aware when you are trying to search for the email account names in the Outlook mail search box, you will be able to view only the email accounts which you have used to sent the emails in past.

By default, the Bcc box (blind carbon copy) is not displayed in Microsoft Outlook.

Note: The following example shows how blind carbon copy works.

From: Richard
To: Julie; Chris
Bcc: Phillip; Patrick

In this example, Richard has sent the message to four people: Julie, Chris, Phillip, and Patrick. Message recipients that are listed in the To box are not aware of message recipients that are listed in the Bcc box. In this example, Julie and Chris are unaware that Phillip and Patrick have received the message. Message recipients that are listed in the Bcc box are also not aware of each other. In this example, Phillip is not aware that Patrick has received a copy of the message, but he does know that Chris and Julie have each received a copy of the message.

How to reveal the Bcc box in Outlook

https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/kb/299804

I hope the above information helps. Kindly let us know if you need any further assistance with the issue. We are glad to assist you.

anthony.b say:

Here are a few ideas you might try:

[1]  Remove the back of the phone, and remove the battery.  Use a pencil eraser to clean the battery contacts, and put the battery back in, pressing it in tightly.

[2]  Get one of those USB cable that light up.  I have one, and when the phone is charging, the cable lights up (sparkles) and when the phone is fully charged, the cable stops lighting up.  This verifies, visually, that the USB cable is sending a charge, AND that the phone is pulling a charge into itself.  These lighted USB cables are expensive, so you might try bringing your phone to the store, and trying it out first, to be sure that this cable works on your phone.  The lighted charger cable I bought cost me $20.  Here is a link that shows the "Light Pulse" Micro USB cable that I use:

http://www.pilotelectronic.com/electroluminescent-cable/el-1400b

[3]  Use the USB cable that came with your phone, and not one from some other product.  That will probably send a charge OK (unless the end of your USB cable was damaged, then it will not work).

[4]  If the battery is simply dead, the phone cannot be turned on.  If the problem is that one or more of your chargers do not work on your phone, your phone can never be charged up until you get a charger that works on your phone.  Just because a USB charger works on another phone, does not mean it will work on your phone (the contacts to/from USB chargers can vary by a little bit, and sometimes become bent and/or worn down).

[5]  Find another Lumia 535 phone that works, and put the battery from that phone into your phone.  If this makes your phone work, you might need a new battery.
