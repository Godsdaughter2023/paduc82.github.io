lemp say:

Your question is not entirely clear (at least to me) and you also seem to misconstrue the purpose of Faronics' Deep Freeze.

Deep Freeze is not really intended to protect systems from outside hackers (although it can also do that).  It permits a workstation to be configured as desired and then restored -- upon rebooting -- to that configuration.

Deep Freeze doesn't prevent changes to the computer's configuration, it just ensures that any such changes aren't permanent.  You can make changes to the system, including installing programs (as long as they don't require a restart to complete the installation).  But if your system is "frozen," when you restart, it's as if you never installed the program or made the change.

As far as administrative accounts are concerned, what do you mean by the "last administrative account."  If you are referring to the built-in account named "Administrator," why do you believe you had to enable it in the first place?  Most users of Windows 7 don't even know that it's there, let alone enable it.
