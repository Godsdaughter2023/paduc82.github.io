john w. vinson say:

Access is quite capable of doing this... but it's got a pretty steep learning curve. It's a good bit more work to set up then, say, a typical Excel spreadsheet! I'll post some links to helpful tutorials and othr resources below.

I will say that the MOST important thing is to have a properly normalized set of tables FIRST. An Access "Form" is like a window, a data entry screen; but if you design your tables to match a typical paper "form", you're very unlikely to get a good table structure. The rules and design features of a good, user friendly paper form are quite different from those of a good relational database (and, to be honest, vice versa). You will probably need several tables in one-to-many relationships, and a Form with multiple subforms, combo boxes, and other tools to hide the table complexity from the users.

You will also need to split the database into two: a shared "Backend", containing only the Tables; and multiple copies of a "Frontend", linked to the shared backend, containing the Forms, Reports, Queries and code. These can be set up so that the only thing the user sees is the forms that you choose to let them see (unless they're pretty skilled Access developers determined to hack around your safeguards); but it's certainly true that "easy is difficult" - setting up such a frontend will take a fair bit of work and learning on your part (or you can hire an Access expert to do it for you or help you do it).

You'll probably also want a separate frontend just for you or any other "administrators"; this would have the data entry people's forms, but also other forms for viewing and editing the data in ways you don't offer to the end users, and with the design mode enabled so that you can improve on the forms and reports.

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

451 is definitely a server-side error.  It is a response provided to Outlook from the SMTP server during the SMTP handshake.  Without being able to see the actual raw message, I'm guessing it has no headers because Outlook is reporting the error it received from the server via the NDR and since Outlook is generating it on the server's behalf, there are no headers.

The "4" means it a transient error. Normally, clients should hold the message for a period of time and retry sending periodically when they get a transient error from the server, then report the failure after several days of retrying.

grschinon say: Operator branding is not removed by resetting the phone. Branded phones have a completely different firmware in them.

The official procedure is to start by getting the phone's SIM-lock lifted. Judging by your vocabulary I'm guessing you mean T-Mobile UK and not T-Mobile USA, so the procedure is to call 150 and ask for the restriction code. They will send it to you along with the way to input it and unlock the phone via Royal Mail within 28 days.

Once the phone has been unlocked you need to ask T-Mobile for written permission to remove their branded firmware and install generic, unbranded firmware in its place. Note that you will have to do this in writing because the front-line support personnel you will be speaking with on the phone don't have a clue what firmware is and will think you're trying to get a new phone out of them. Also note that T-Mobile may well refuse to give you this permission as long as you're within the initial contact period, even if you no longer are. It's not easy to get operators to cooperate this way in an industry so tightly controlled by them.

If, however, you do succeed in getting this permission, you take the unlocked phone and the letter from T-Mobile along to your nearest Nokia Care Point. They will do the necessary to the phone, update their databases with the phone's new product code, and print out a new product identification label for the phone's battery compartment.

There are unofficial methods of doing this yourself, but they will void the warranty on the phone and potentially exclude it from any form of service from Nokia in the future regardless of warranty concerns. Because of this, we are not allowed to discuss those methods here. If you do run a google search on how to do this, you'll be on your own with all the potential risk of bricking the phone.
