mickey segal say:

Start menu tiles for Office have 2 styles; how do you control which you get on Windows 10?

Before the Fall Update to Windows 10, the start menu tiles for all the Office 2016 / 365 programs had the default background color and small images, but some programs such as Foxit Reader and Microsoft Solitaire had different color backgrounds or large images, as shown in the left 3x3 tiles.   

After the Fall Update, the tiles for Office programs got the background colors associated with the programs and larger images (right 3x3 tiles).  I saw this as an improvement and guessed it was due to some new capability of the November Update. 

I was surprised after setting up a new computer (installing the Fall Update and then installing Office 2016 / 365) to see that the Office program tiles had the default color and the small images, as in the left 3x3 tiles.  I prefer the style on the right with different color backgrounds and bigger images.

***Post moved by the moderator to the appropriate forum category.***

mickey segal say:

I found a way to fix this problem.  As detailed at http://www.makeuseof.com/tag/6-ways-hack-customize-windows-10-start-menu/

Right-click the tile, select Open file location, then ... right click the file, select Properties, in the Shortcut tab click Change Icon…, and select a given icon or Browse… for an alternative one on your computer.

You don't even need to change the icon image.  you can just re-select the current one.  The colored background appears in the tile and the icon image size increases to the size it should be.

I still don't know why the tile images were messed up in the first place, but this provides a way of fixing the problem.
