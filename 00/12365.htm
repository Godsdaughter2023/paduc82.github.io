donabed kopoian say:

Shut off BIOS updates on your laptop's software.  Right now, flashing BIOS from Windows or flashing firmware from Windows is unreliable.

I almost bricked my Corsair M65 after installing drivers (and updating the firmware is part of installing the drivers).  I had to by-pass that step when I got my mouse working again.

The reason is, updating BIOS and firmware requires exact processes where the software must be able to tell the OS exactly what to do and when to do it.  If the shell changes in any way that changes the way peripherals communicate (which it does anytime a new Windows is released either for security or efficiency or both), then you'll have a hiccup in this process and either get a recoverable error (like you and I did), or a brick (where your computer would have completely failed, and my mouse would have completely died instead of going to limp mode where unplugging it and plugging it back in recovered it).

At least you got your BIOS reflashed properly.  When I downloaded all of my mobo drivers and software, I skipped the BIOS update for this very reason.

It's also why I'm a firm believer in Gigabyte motherboards, since they have used Dual BIOS for quite some time now, and a botched BIOS update will cause the backup to load and repair the main BIOS.

Hopefully the following answers your questions/misconceptions..........

#1 - Email accounts have no impact on the number of user licenses. You can have as many email accounts as you want whether it's from your own custom domain, Outlook.com, Yahoo etc etc etc. Office licenses are for computers/devices that are actually going to use the software. Any devices & programs that simply send an email notice have their own basic SMTP software built in.

#2 - What you have to enter for your SMTP settings for any device such as a printer, NAS etc is governed by the device but SMTP protocol which would include email account userID/password, outbound server settings - server name/port# etc. That has nothing to do with Outlook.

#3 - As far as other people sending "legit email" - that problem is solved by you never publishing or using that email to subscribe to anything. If you get anything other then what you expect then it's spam. I have several email accounts for various purposes that are never used to actually send/receive email. You can use any account from any provider for that - just need to configure it in Outlook somewhere so you can read what you want

#4 - When it says that you only need to enter your email address(what you're calling UserID) and password as part of configuring an email account in Outlook, means that Outlook is going to use <auto-discover> to configure the email account. As long as the server on which the email account resides is configured to allow that, it will respond with the best protocol supported for the account. For Outlook.com = Exchange, Gmail/Yahoo etc = Imap, custom domains = most typically IMAP but depends on the provider.
