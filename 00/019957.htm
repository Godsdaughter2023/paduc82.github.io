pgoelz---01 say:

I have noticed this behaior on both my 820 and on my (Android) Atrix 4G.  Reboot the phone and the battery meter changes significantly.  It used to puzzle me but I think I might know what is happening.  

 

There are two primary ways to determine the state of charge in a lithium battery.  A) you can look at the voltage and calculate the approximate state of charge or B) you can actually measure that power delivered during charge and the power removed during discharge and determine the state of charge that way.  

 

"B" is more accurate that "A" but it is also much more prone to errors if the power measurement is not accurate and/or if it is not reset each time you reach full charge.  

 

I suspect that the phone uses BOTH methods.... measuring voltage after a reboot and before accurate power measurements are available and switching to measuring power charged / discharged after some time.  That would explain why the battery level jumps if you reboot (I'm assuming a reboot wipes the charge/discharge history and the phone has to start over) and it would also explain why mine will read 100% for between 3 and 7 hours after it comes off a full charge.  

 

This is just a guess but it is an educated guess.  Doesn't make it any less annoying though.
