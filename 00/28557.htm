To create a personal e-mail signature with image in your account, follow the steps below:

1. Sign in to your account at http://www.outlook.com

3. Under “Customize your mail,” click “Message font and signature.”
4. Ensure that “Rich text” is displayed on the format bar.

7. Go back to the browser where you've opened your account and paste the image in the “Personal e-mail signature” box by pressing "Ctrl" and "v." Create your e-mail signature.
8. Click “Save.”

FYI: You can only add an image/ picture in your e-mail signature that’s Web based (picture that is available in existing websites or stored in an online storage). This is the reason why on step 5, I’ve asked you to open a new browser where the image is located.

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

I would advise against inserting page numbers manually.

If you send me a copy of the document, referencing this thread in the covering email message, I will investigate the issue.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

You can unlink footers if they are in separate sections, but note that each section can have up to three footers (First Page Footer, Footer/Odd Page Footer, Even Page Footer). If you have Different First Page or Different Odd and Even enabled, you will have these separate footers even in a single section. If you have more than one section, each footer is linked to (or may be unlinked from) the corresponding footer in the previous section.

In each of these footers, you need to go to the Page Number Format dialog (via Insert | Header & Footer | Page Number | Format Page Numbers... or Header & Footer Tools | Design | Header & Footer | Page Number | Format Page Numbers...).

It appears that you're including the chapter number in your page number. This does not require section breaks, since the chapter number will change automatically, anyway, but if you want to restart numbering with each chapter, then you do need section breaks (preferably at least Next Page). In each section, you will need to restart the numbering or continue the numbering as desired.
