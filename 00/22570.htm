a. user say:

Hi BG,

 

It's always our pleasure to provide our helps to our customers.

 

For Lync migration, generally there are two methods:

 

1. Deploy Skype for Business Hybrid. The method can be a little complex and SSO is required. In a hybrid deployment, the DNS records are pointed to Lync on-premises. When a Lync account is moved to Lync Online using Lync Server Management Shell, the contacts and meetings will not be affected. There will be less down time. After all the users are moved to Office 365, you can refer the following steps to decommission Lync on-premises:

a. Make sure all the users are moved to Lync Online.

b. Point SRV records to Lync Online.

c. Decommission Lync server by preferring to the article: https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/gg195815(v=ocs.14).aspx

 

2. Without deploying Skype for Business hybrid, you need to disable the Lync accounts from Lync on-promises and enable them in Skype for Business Online directly. In this situation, there will be some service down time. Here are the detailed steps for your reference:

a. Disable the Lync on-premises account. For example, you can run the command “Disable-csuser” using the Lync management shell. Normally, this command will also remove all Lync related attributes from local AD.

b. Run AAD Connect, sync the user accounts to Office 365 or you can create accounts directly in cloud.

c. Assign the Skype for Business Online licenses to synced user accounts.

d. Change the Lync related DNS records such as SRV records and CNAME records to Office 365.

 

============

mike h.. say:

Hi,

If we can get away with copying the entire row; and I suspect we can't because there may be data in 'other' cells you don't want to copy then we can get away with this which inserts a row below the active cell and copies the row above into it.

Sub InSert_Row()
ActiveCell.EntireRow.Copy
ActiveCell.Offset(1).EntireRow.Insert Shift:=xlDown
End Sub

If we have to copy only those cells you mention then we can use this

Sub Insert_Row_2()
R = ActiveCell.Row
ActiveCell.Offset(1).EntireRow.Insert Shift:=xlDown
Range("O" & R + 1 & ":AA" & R + 1).Formula = Range("O" & R & ":AA" & R).Formula
Range("AN" & R + 1 & ":AO" & R + 1).Formula = Range("AN" & R & ":AO" & R).Formula
Range("AQ" & R + 1 & ":AR" & R + 1).Formula = Range("AQ" & R & ":AR" & R).Formula
End Sub

I wouldn't use a button to implement this because often the button wouldn't be visible. The way I'd implement this is. Alt+F11 to open vb editor. Right click 'ThisWorkbook' and insert module and paste in which ever code your going to use.

Back on the worksheet. Developer tab, macros, in the dropdown at the bottom select 'this workbook' and highlight the macro name. Options and assign a shortcut.
