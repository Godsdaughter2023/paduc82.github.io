roady [mvp] say:

Upon configuring your account, you could have directly told it to reuse your existing pst-file.
See: Restoring a pst-file of a POP3 account

 

There are multiple ways to go from here:

  • Point your POP3 account to use the pst-file you've already re-added:
    File-> Account Settings-> Account Settings-> tab Email
    Then use the Data Files tab to set it as the default and restart Outlook.
    Move the newly added items of your (mostly empty) new pst-file to the one you have now set as the default. Once you've done that, disconnect the empty pst-file via the Data File tab.
  • Manually move everything from the re-added pst-file to the newly created one and then remove the pst-file via the Data Files tab.
  • Remove  (disconnect) the re-added pst-file via the Data Files tab, restart Outlook, and then use the Import wizard.

The first method is the recommended way to go and is the least amount of work.

Is there a difference between Bcc and Cc?

diane poremsky mvp (slipstick.com) say:
You’re saying the messages isn’t delivered to you?

The bcc will work basically the same way - dropping the message into your mailbox - but it’s sent ‘blind’ so your address won’t be visible on the message. Neither your nor the recipients will see it was sent to you. (the sent copy will show it was bcc to you. )

Some anti spam filters may think the message is spam.

It should definitely come back to your inbox - unless a spam filter is grabbing it - its the same as a CC, but is sent blind, so no one else sees it addressed to you. Even in your inbox, it would not show as being addressed to you.

Check the junk folder for it. I'd suggest looking for a rule to delete mail to you, from you, but that would apply to your CC'd messages too.

On Office365 portal, how to edit user permission to a folder on a sharepoint document list?  I just added a new user to my company account and I want to restrict his access to a few folders in a Sharepoint document list.  There are total of 3 users including me  in my company.

Thanks

jerryduan_msft8021 say:

hi shuangliu9209,

to edit user permission for specific folders or files, we need to navigate to the location of the files/folders and manage permissions on the items as below:
1. click the ellipsis beside the item.
2. click share > shared with >advanced
    
3. in the manage permission page for this item, you can restrict user’s access to the item by assigning them different permission levels.

by default, an item inherits permission from its parent. for instance, files inherit the permission from its folder and folders inherit the permission from its library. so if you want to assign users unique permission for an item, you need to break the inheritance at first, and remove the existing persons and groups who have permission assigned on this item, then re-sharing the item via grant permissions in the manage permission page.

here are some articles for your reference about how permissions are managed in sharepoint online:
