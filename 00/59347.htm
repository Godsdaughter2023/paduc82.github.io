jim-s say:

Mahjong workaround fix for 101_ 107_1 error

On 1/20/17, I was successful in resolving the failure caused by an update to the Mahjong app, on my Win-10 laptop.  The error code was 101_107_1 and later 101_105_101.  Running the suggested 'sfc /scannow' was not successful.  It appears that the handshake with the Xbox server is the cause.  I un-installed to Mahjong app and re-installed it, and still got the error.   I went to the MS store for Xbox related games and downloaded another app ( Minesweeper for me) and ran it successfully.  It did need to reload my old winning statistics.  I then re-ran the Mahjong app and it worked without an error message.  My history seems to have been reloaded successfully.  I suspect that installing the new app reset the local parameters for successful Xbox server handshake on my laptop.  I hope this process also works for others.

    I got my wife a small laptop for Christmas (reversible with touchscreen running Windows 10) and this has happened several times now.  None of those other suggestions worked for me either (rebooting modem, Network connections, Properties, etc, etc.  But they are worth trying first.

    Reinstall Windows 7 Home Basic using manufacturers recovery partition:

    Computers that come preinstalled with Windows 7 often have what is called a recovery partition. This is used to reinstall the operating system in the event of a system crash. To access it, you will need to boot into when you start your computer by pressing a function key. This can be either F1, F2, F9, F10, F11, F12 or even DEL or Tab key. Consult the owners manual that came with your PC for instructions about how to reinstall Windows.

    This is how the recovery partition is accessed for the most popular makes:


    • For Dell, press F8 on the keyboard until the Advanced Boot Options menu appears on the screen.
    • For HP, press F11 directly after powering up the unit.
    • For LG, press F11 directly after powering up the unit
    • For Toshiba, press and hold '0' BEFORE and during the power up
    • For Acer, press and hold ALT + F10 as soon as you see the logo
    • For Asus, press F9 as soon as you see the Asus logo.
    • For Samsung, press F4 at power up..
    • For Fujitsu, press the F8 key repeatedly directly after powering up
    • For Advent, restart your computer. Then Press F10 repeatedly until the message "Starting System Recovery" is displayed
    • For Sony VAIO, restart and press "F8" or "F10" repeatedly until the "Advanced Boot Options" screen appears.

    If you need to reinstall Windows 7 Home Basic from scratch on a new storage device (hard disk or solid state device), then this require that you have recovery media. Some manufacturers only provide a recovery partition as a means of reinstalling Windows 7 while some might provide recovery media on DVD even though your Netbook or Nettop might not have an optical drive.
