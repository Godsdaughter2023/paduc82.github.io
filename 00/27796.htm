I have no idea why you're being asked to chat with support... They can't help you here. Nobody at Microsoft, Google or Facebook has your portion of the secret key used in two-step authentication. That's the whole point of 2SA! They can see his account activity, but won't be able to give him access to his account or help with Authenticator or generate codes for him!

You say you have MS, Google and Facebook accounts. It just so happens I have experience with this exact scenario.

Make sure you have your phone number registered with all three companies. In the event you can't get to Authenticator and need a code, each service send you a code via SMS! I HIGHLY suggest you do this from a device trusted by all three services so if you get logged out, you don't have to put in a code to log back in.


For your Microsoft account:
Go to https://account.live.com/p and sit on that page while your phone resets.
When it comes time to input your Microsoft account into your phone after the hard reset, use that page to generate an app password and use that as your password to your Microsoft account.

Once you're logged in and have downloaded Authenticator again on your phone, click "Delete" under Authenticatior app on that same web page. Now just setup Authenticator as usual again.

For your Google account:
Go to https://accounts.google.com, click Security on the left, then Edit for 2-step authentication. Click "Manage application passwords" and revoke the one you currently used on your phone. Generate a new one now and use that to log into your Google account on the phone.

Now go back to the 2SA landing page and next to Mobile Application, click "Move to a different phone". Choose Android as the new phone and follow the usual instructions to setup Authenticator with your Google account.

For your Facebook account:
Go to Account Settings>Security. Next to Code Generator, click Edit and disable the code generator. Now, reenable it and follow the usual instructions to set up Facebook with Authenticator.

Note that with 2SA on on Facebook accounts, your normal log in to Facebook on Windows Phone may fail. When this happens, Facebook *should* immediately send you an SMS message with a code to use in place of the password. However, I have seen this not happen, so if your Facebook login fails and you never get an SMS, just wait a few hours and try again. If the SMS never comes and you get really annoyed, just go back to Account Settings>Security and generate an app password under "App Passwords"

If you previously applied table formatting in the Word Desktop App (such as Word 2010 or the Word 2013 Preview), then open the document in edit mode of the Word Web App, the web app will preserve your formatting.  However, it won't show you that formating or give you the ability to edit it.  (You can always see exactly how the document looks in the View mode of the Word Web App.)

 

Thanks for the feedback that these features are important for you. We're always working to improve the product by listening to customer feedback.
