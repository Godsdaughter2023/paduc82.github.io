Office 2015 for Mac preview space hog - Microsoft Community

davidjb levine say:

Office 2015 for Mac preview space hog

Hello

I have installed and am very happy  using the new Preview but am starting to wonder about a lot of space hogging. Apparently there is over 15gb in my message attachments folders. Any idea how I can reduce this?

talking moose say:

If you open a message with an attachment then it will download and cache the attachment. That’s normal behavior.

You can set Outlook to work in Headers Only mode for Exchange and IMAP accounts, which means only the subject, sender and date are downloaded to display in your message list. You can then empty your account’s cache to free space on your drive.

Choose Outlook menu >  Preferences > Accounts > your Exchange or IMAP account > Advanced > Server tab and enable Download Headers Only. Click the OK button and close the Accounts window.

Exchange:

Right-click or Control-click your Exchange account’s name in the Navigation pane on the left of the Main Window and choose Properties > General tab. Click the Empty Cache button.

IMAP:

Right-click or Control-click each folder in your IMAP account in the Navigation pane on the left of the Main Window and choose Repair Folder.

This will clear all your mail and force Outlook to sync with your server, however, it will only download message headers. This does cause a slight delay to display each message because Outlook is not caching the information ahead of time.

Hope this helps!

I too am using the stock android calendar and my calendars exist online.  I have resolved the issue.

 

Here is what I had to do to get it to work.

1 - From your inbox click on the 3 stacked blocks in the lower right corner and click on Settings

2 - From the Settings menu, select Contacts and Calendar and make sure Calendar has a check mark, same for contacts if you want those too

3 - Close it and wait for the calendar to refresh

Here are the answers to your questions:
1. If I right click on an attachment and choose to remove it, where does it go (trash, recycle, vapor)?
The attachment will be deleted from your mailbox and you can’t find it in your mailbox.

2. Is there any automated process to permanently remove/delete emails/attachments (trying to prevent spreading credit card information) like a secure erase for email??
In Office 365, you can create a transport rule to automatically delete emails with some secure information in the emails for in the organization level.
Mail flow or transport rules
Also you can create an inbox rule in the mailbox to automatically delete these emails.
Inbox rules in Outlook Web App
However, there is no option to automatically remove attachments.
