In Windows, Onenote Data is kept at 2 places. One is on your hard drive and one is in Onedrive.

swischner say:
Vijay is partly correct. But the backup folder he is referring to is nothing you could rely on unless you recently opened all notebooks in OneNote 2013 or 2016 for Windows (NOT the OneNote Windows 10 app) and manually started a backup via the Options - Save & Backup menu. Also a special option (Also backup notebooks on Sharepoint) has to be set to backup notebooks from OneDrive whatsoever. Usually that backup folder is either incomplete or outdated if you do not follow the above instructions.

The good news is: This all is not that important for your specific questions. Indeed there is an additional copy locally on your PC where all actual changes to the notebook are made. It's not the backup folder, it's the OneNote cache. That is a copy of your notes in a very special binary and fragmented format that only OneNote itself can use. It is not meant for a backup.

The critical point is: OneNote regularly synchronizes the content of that cache files (containing your most recent edits on opened notebooks) to the "real" copy on OneDrive. As long as that synchronizing is not finished, all your notes in the local cache are indeed at risk of getting lost if you reset your Windows or OneNote.

So to be on the safe side, it's always a good idea to check if your notes have been fully synchronized. Best method to check that is to open OneNote Online in your browser (go to OneDrive or OneNote,com and open your notebooks in the browser version of OneNote, which is done automatically if you click on a notebook). If you see that your notes are up to date there, you are safe to reset your Windows or OneNote. On reinstalling or restarting OneNote you can open your notebooks from OneDrive and they will get downloaded (creating an additional cache copy as described).

In short:

Don't reset your Windows or OneNote before all your notebooks have been fully synchronized to OneDrive or OneDrive for Business.

You can check that by opening your notebooks in OneNote Online using a browser (usually unsynced notes are shown in your OneNote app as well with a red X sign, but checking online for yourself is better for your sleep)

Only if your notes are ok and up to date in OneNote online, it's safe to reset your Windows or OneNote.
