arminatorx say:

As zTOPNOTCHz already stated, it might be that the console Firmware is out of date, or doesn't match.

In order to protect the games from piracy and modding, Xbox changed the encryption format of the disc and sent out a firmware update that changed the system firmware as well as the drive firmware. 

In a Xbox 360 console, the optical drive and mainboard are cryptographically "married", so if your console was repaired from someone else than an official Xbox repair center, it might be that the drive Firmware doesn't match the motherboard firmware, and therefore the console can't decrypt the newer games with changed encryption anymore. 

If you bought the console new, try to remember if you gave your console for repair to a non-Microsoft repair shop. 

If you bought the console used, it might be that the previous owner attempted a self-repair or the console was modified. 

In either case, the drive doesn't match the console's motherboard, and therefore newer games won't work.
