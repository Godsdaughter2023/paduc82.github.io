a. user say:

The system of permissions, which are also known as privileges, are contained in the access control list for a folder. The current organization of permissions, which includes user and system accounts and group policies, has evolved over many years from industry best practices. The overarching idea behind the industry practice is known as the 'principal of least privileges' which means that each user and system account has the least privileges needed to fulfill its role.

These industry standards and best practices were created to protect data from unauthorized access, which continues to be a serious problem and probably always will be.

Regarding your particular issue: Every Windows user account needs to be globally unique. Were it not so, it would be easy for an attacker to masquerade as someone else and access that person's account.

Practically speaking, it means that your user account on one computer is completely different from your user account on another computer, even though both accounts belong to you. When you transfer files, you are transferring files from one globally unique user to another, different globally unique user. To access those files all you need to do is to take possession of the files. (That's the technical term.) The method for taking possession is described in detail on the internet.

If you don't like the technology industry's standards and best practices, there's nothing stopping you from doing whatever you want to your own computer, as long as you're prepared to live with the consequences. But I think it's naive to think that the technology industry will change its ways for a relative handful of people who don't understand the principles of security and think they know better.
