Method 1: 

Step 1: Check if the Bluetooth Support Service is running.

Sign in to your computer as an administrator user and then follow the steps given below to check the status of the Bluetooth Support Service.

  1. Press Windows Key + R Key.
  2. Type services.msc and scroll down to Bluetooth Support Service in the list.
  3. Right click on it and select Properties.
  4. Set the Startup type to Automatic.
  5. Restart your computer.

Step 2:

  1. Download the drivers for the keyboard from Microsoft website (Choose Windows 10 from the drop list).

  3. Right click on the Bluetooth adapter and uninstall it.
  4. Now restart the computer.
  5. Install the drivers downloaded for the keyboard.

Method 2: Disable Bluetooth power setting from Device Manger.

  1. From the Start screen, swipe in from the right edge of the screen, and then tap or click Search.
  2. Type device manager and tap on Device Manager.
  3. Expand the Bluetooth device and right-click/tap on your Keyboard device.
  4. Tap Power Management and uncheck Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power.
  5. Click OK and restart the computer and check the issue.

Keep us posted with the status of the issue.
