diane poremsky mvp (slipstick.com) say:

Transfer or Install Office on a New PC

Your Office 365 license is linked to your Microsoft account so you only need to sign into https://www.office.com/ using your Microsoft account or business account and click Install Office.  

If you need to remove computers from your installations, log in to Services & Subscriptions using the Microsoft account associated with the Office license to remove licenses and install Office.

If your new computer includes a preinstalled trial version of Office and you have a license for Office 365, you can sign in to activate it using your existing license. If you are installing another version of Office, uninstall the preinstalled version first.

For more detailed information see the following Microsoft Office installation articles. These pages also contain links to articles on installing updates and troubleshooting installation articles. These pages also contain links to articles on installing updates and troubleshooting common issues. 

Download and install or reinstall Office 365 or Office 2019 on a PC or Mac

Download and install or reinstall Office 2016 or Office 2013

Activate Office using the Activation wizard

Deactivate Office 365 if you see a "limit reached" error

TIP: You don’t need to uninstall Office from the old computer. If you have an Office subscription, you should deactivate the installation.  If you’ve used your allowed number of installations, deactivate one or more installations. Deactivate licenses from the Services & Subscriptions page. 

Office Home & Student, Office Home & Business, and Office Professional Retail Licenses

If you have a retail license and need to move it to a new computer, uninstall it from the old computer and install and activate it on the new computer. The retail licenses are limited to one computer and cannot be moved to new computers frequently like the subscription version, so deactivation is not necessary. You are limited in the number of times and how frequently you can reactivate it on new hardware (once every 90 days). 

From Deactivate Office if you see a "limit reached" error:

There is no way to deactivate Office Home & Student, Office Home & Business, Office Professional, or individual Office apps. Instead, you must reinstall and activate using the process described in Activate after reinstalling Office.

The old license is "deactivated" by uninstalling it from the computer.  If you need to reactivate it on the current computer, the old activation is replaced by the new one. 

You may need to activate it by phone if you try to activate it on a different computer or after a major hardware change on the current computer and receive an error message that you have reached the maximum number of activations. 

See Activate after reinstalling or moving Office for more information.

Yeah, I don't really see what Apple can do here though.
 
Here's the situation:
For Syncing, Outlook supports Exchange and Sync Services.
If you have an Exchange account, you should be fine, but Sync Services
have been depreciated by Apple in Mountain Lion.
Apple allows you to sync through CalDAV (which is the protocol
currently used by iCloud and Google Calendar), but MS never implemented
