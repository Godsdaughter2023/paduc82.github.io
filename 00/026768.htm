Faulting module name: MSHTML.dll, version: 10.0.9200.16618 


Unfortunately, crashes in low level modules are often caused by their callers and we no longer get to see a Stack Back Trace of crash events automatically.   You need to capture a .dmp file of the exception event and then format it, e.g. using a tool such as WinDbg.

 

Alternatively, you can try to guess at the same information by seeing what other modules are loaded in the crashing task.  If there are some obvious unnecessary (in particular, third-party) ones there which were in your Startup then the next obvious step would be trying to avoid loading those.  Since starting in safe mode typically does that automatically that's why you often see this step shortened to retesting in safe mode.

What you are running into is a limit in the program. Those controls will go blank when more than ~32,600 characters are selected. However, that does not prevent selection of a different font or font size. The fact that the change is visible in the text provides but if you then click in a specific spot or select a smaller passage you'll get the further confirmation you seem to be looking for.

Quite frankly, the underlying fact is that you are attempting to apply direct formatting to such a large body of text. Use of direct formatting can be problematic in a variety of ways & should be avoided for all but the most simplistic documents.

If you want to make universal changes of this nature [especially in longer, more complex documents] it's best to modify the Styles applied in the document. If you modify the Styles used the change will be imposed automatically to all text where the Style is applied. Alternatively, create your own Styles & apply them where needed.
