luizlopes say:

Matthew,

Thank you for your solution, which I implemented it in a slightly different way that did not involve safe mode.  While logged in on  an Administrator group account:

  • copied the folders in the C:\Users\Public (on my SSD) to E:\Users\Public (on my hard disk), using File Explorer
  • deleted the folders in C:\Users\Public, using File Explorer

There's good news and bad news.

The bad news is that many frequent contributors to Microsoft Community strongly recommend that you do a complete fresh install of Windows on any used computer that you buy (you don't know what might be hidden on it, from malware that will steal your personal information to illegally-downloaded files).

However, if you're like most people who buy used computers and decide to ignore that good advice, the good news is that your Virgin Media Super Hub is, in fact, a wireless router (made by Netgear).

The User Guide for this device can be found here --> http://www.virginmedia.com/myvirginmedia/superhub-guide.pdf

Right-click on My Computer and select Properties.  Look at the "General" tab to see what Service Pack is installed.  If it doesn't have Service Pack 3 already, that means that the previous owner was not very conscientious about keeping the computer safe from malware.  See "bad news" above.  Post back for advice on installing Service Pack 3 and subsequent critical system updates.

Do you have a current antivirus application installed? Which one?

Connecting via wireless:

See the basic instructions on page 5 of your User Guide and confirm that the wireless portion of your Super Hub is on.

Assuming that your used computer does not have a "SuperHub USB Adapter" (but has some other wireless adapter), skip to page 8 of the User Guide.  If there is a password and SSID (network name) on your Super Hub and you can successfully use these to connect wirelessly, you're done. 

IMPORTANT:  there should be more than one "password" on the label of the Super Hub:  there should be an SSID (network name) something like "virginmedia1234567" and a corresponding "passphrase." This is the information you need to connect wirelessly.  There ALSO should be a "Super Hub Username" and "Super Hub Password" on the label on the back of your Super Hub under "Super Hub settings."  This information is used -- if needed -- as described in the next paragraph

If there is no wireless passphrase on your SuperHub or if the passphrase doesn't work, temporarily re-connect the computer to the Super Hub using an Ethernet cable and follow the directions starting at page 16 of the User Guide.  Here is where you enter your "Super Hub Username" and "Super Hub Password."  Most wireless routers display the wireless passphrase "in clear" in this configuration utility. If yours does not, just follow the directions to change the passphrase to one that you will remember.  The only other thing you should change, if desired, is your SSID or network name.  If you change it, do not use your last name, address, or other personal information (the SSID is -- and should be -- visible to anyone with a wireless adapter).
