This is not a malware issue, it's the "Welcome to BT Hub" initial screen that displays the first time a new device (PC in your case as signified by the "pc-step0" portion) connects to a BT Home Hub.

If you've moved to a new room, then you may have moved into range of a new router that's got an unsecured Wi-Fi connection to the Internet.

Since Windows sometimes likes to grab onto whatever available connection is found, you may need to go into Networks by clicking on the network symbol in the lower right-hand portion of the taskbar and manually disconnect from that BT router if it doesn't belong to you.  Then manually select the correct router and try to connect it it instead.

If the correct router is too weak, then you'll need to deal with that problem instead, which is outside the scope of these forums and should be discussed with whomever supports your Wi-Fi connection.
