So there are prices for quantities ranging from 1 to 1000 in 21 steps

We want to find the price for Q no of magazines

We know the following:

The price of the step below Q (Pb)

The price of the step above Q (Pa)

The quantity of the step below Q (Qb)

The quantity of the step above Q (Qa)

Therefore the price of Q magazines = Pb+(((Pa-Pb)*(Q-Qb))/(Qa-Qb))

e.g.

we want to find the price of 57 magazines

The quantity of the step below (Qb) = 50

The quantity of the step above (Qa) = 60

The price of the step below (Pb) = 300

The price of the step above (Pa) = 353

so

=300+(((353-300)*(57-50))/(60-50))

Therefore for 57 magazines, the price is £337.10.

This process does not back up your hard drive or any of your private files. You will need to take care of that yourself on another flash drive or backup media.

The purpose of creating this Recovery Drive (as outlined in this thread) is to make a 'Windows CD' media, in the event that you need to reinstall Windows 10 (fresh clean install) at some time in the future. Think of this as being like a 'Windows CD' for reinstall, only now it's on a flash drive.

16GB should be sufficient. I have made recovery media for two home desktops and my laptop. My desktops took 8GB flash drives, and my laptop requested a 16GB. I still have no clue why the difference, but all it's doing is putting Windows installation files onto the flash drive and making it USB bootable.

Note that if you make recovery media on YOUR LAPTOP, you cannot use this to recover your daughter's laptop. The new Win10 recovery media is tied to the computer on which it is created. The maximum resolution for images in Publisher is 300 dpi. Reference: http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/help/compress-pictures-dialog-box-HA010036390.aspx?pid=CL100605171033

If you compressed the image to fit the picture box dimensions 450 X 450 then you cannot increase that image. It has been resampled. If you compressed the image and want a larger image, you will have to insert your orginal image and then increase the box size. You can check by right clicking the image > format picture > size tab and checking to see if the current dimensions are different than the original size. If the original size is still 1500 X 1500 then you can hit the reset button.

davidf2 say:

If you are trying to reuse the images from the Publisher publication that have been resized and saved at that size you cannot increase the size of the picture box without distortion.

Did you actually save individual image files of the original images on your hard drive? When you mouse over them do you get a tool tip saying they are 1500 X 1500?

Where did you get those images?

Here is a simple experiment. Right click one of your existing Pictures on your Publisher page > Change Picture > browse to where you saved one of your original 1500 X 1500 images and select it and insert it. Your picture box is not likely to change size. Now right click that picture again > Format picture > Size tab. Under size and rotate that should give you the current dimensions of the picture box. At the bottom of that format dialog you should see the original size and a reset button. Do you see 1500 X 1500? If not then the image you just inserted was not 1500 X 1500. If you click the reset button the picture box will change to the original dimensions of your original image.

I find myself wondering about the dimensions...1500 X 1500 is not a default size for any image I know of. Once again where did you get these original images?

DavidF
