Where is the security in Access Web Apps? - Office 365 - Microsoft Community

roscoejames say:

Where is the security in Access Web Apps? - Office 365

When considering Office 365s' intended use - on the go, remote and on the web - as a small business manager with staff in four countries around the globe, I have to take the Office 365 marketing soundbite - Office 365 is a secure place to store and access your data - with a grain of salt. One comment, if the issue I'm about to discuss has a solution then let me be the first to withdraw my commentary. But I've spent a good amount of time in forums, communities, and looking at videos to try and find a solution. Haven't found one yet.

My topic is Access Web Apps on Office 365. The idea of Access Web Apps is great. Build an App (or deploy one of the many pretty useful templates that are available from Microsoft and their development team) and you can have HR in Scotland, Production in the US, Marketing in Mexico, and Inventory control in Canada. All of it connected. All of it accessible. All of it always up to date. Great idea. And while the current state of Access Web Apps seems more of a beta than something as developed as, say, the old Visual Basic suite, the macro editor in all Office Apps, or Visual Studio, with a lot of determination and trial and error (because there aren't many publications dedicated to Advanced Access Web App development or tips and tricks) you can, in fact, build a killer app that will contemplate most aspects of your small or medium business.

Then you run into the question of security. It all kind of goes downhill from there. You can limit access to the site your access web app resides in, which will limit the number of people accessing your app. Good move. But be it two people or ten, around the globe, you can't achieve the simplest of tasks - limit access to downloading the company app to Access desktop (including tables, data, and designs), saving that app to their own desktop so that even if they no longer work for you organization they still have access to sensitive information concerning your company and how it operates. You can't limit permissions to only certain aspects of the App. You can see HR, but you can't. You get to see Production costs but no one else needs to or can. And since there is (to my knowledge) no way to create separate Apps for each function that share a centrally located set of tables (because it all interacts together), you can't really go in that direction.

So I ask, where is the security of your company's data when deploying in an Access Web App? Because it would seem the most basic of security would be stopping people that know nothing about Access as a whole, less about building or creating an Access Web App, from actually having the Settings gear available that takes you to > Customize in Access. That one seems fairly simple. You limit it by Owner or Creator or Admin and let anyone else get into the app, edit and add data as needed, and that's where it sops.

Where's the security?

roscoejames say:

Yes, that may be part of my concern, but it isn't really what prompted me to start the discussion. My concern is two fold.

First, where's the security on the data in the tables in an Access Web App created in-house and published on an Office 365 site with the express purpose of other people in your organization being able to remotely update records, add new records to the data in the web app?

Second, how is the Access Web App build protected from being altered, possibly to the point of not working, by anyone with Access on their desktop?

Because after extensive digging and searching, unless an admin goes into the Office 365 site and, through custom CSS, removes the Settings > Customize in Access cog, anyone with access to the site and the App with the ability to update records, add records, may also download to their desktop Access.

Once open in their desktop Access they can do at least two things. They can make design changes. Add tables, delete tables. Delete views, queries, datamacros. They can do anything to the App the admin or original builder/owner of the App can do. For my part this seems like a huge risk. Any non-admin Team Member can total wreck an integral component of your site. That seems to be a security risk to me.

But more disturbing is that once an Access Web App is open in Desktop Access any table data may be viewed and, subsequently, downloaded to Excel on your desktop. A blatant security risk.

I know these things to be true because I've tested them on different machines with different users.

A team member that has the ability to create new records or edit existing records in an Access Web App (and is only a read/write team member, not an admin, does not have any owner's privileges) can alter the company's app in anyway they like (including deleting tables, deleting queries, deleting views) and they can download the contents of any table in your Access Web App to their local desktop in Excel.

So where's the security? Table data isn't secure. The man-hours put into the design of your app isn't secure. Anyone can look behind the curtain. And your operation isn't secure because anyone on the team could render your app inoperable at a moment's notice just by deleting all your datamacros. Which can be restored. But just the loss of an hour, a day, or any time where people lose access or the ability to do their job seems like a security risk to me.

Where's the security?
