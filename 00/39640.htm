Setting monitor for "actual" instead of "stretch" - Microsoft Community

bijadideb say:

Setting monitor for "actual" instead of "stretch"

With my wide screen monitor, all images are "stretched" across the full width distorting the images. How did I set it for "actual" with the proper ratio?
ken blake say:

On Tue, 13 Jul 2010 14:00:47 +0000, bijadideb wrote:

With my wide screen monitor, all images are "stretched" across the full width distorting the images. How did I set it for "actual" with the proper ratio?

You are running at an improper resolution for your monitor.
Right-click the desktop and choose "Screen resolution." Choose a
resolution that has the aspect ratio of 16:9, not the 4:3 ratio your
are running at. For example, I run my widescreen 23" monitors at
1920X1080.

Two other points:

1. An LCD monitor should be run at its native resolution. Check your
documentation to see what that is. Running at any other resolution
will diminish the quality of the image.

2. It is possible that when you go to the "Screen resolution" screen,
you will not see any 16:9 choices. That could be for either of two
reasons:

a. You have an old video card that doesn't support such resolutions.
If so, you will need to buy a new one.

b. You do not have the latest Windows 7 driver for your video card. Go
to its manufacturer's web site and look for a newer one there.

Ken Blake
