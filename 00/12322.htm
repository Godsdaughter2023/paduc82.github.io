ken sheridan say:
..............so in the categories list table I have id, category & country code

This means that there is one-to-many relationship type between Categories and Countries, which raises another, more fundamental, design issue.  In relational-speak country is functionally determined by category.  In your Customers table therefore, if you include columns for both country and category, then the former is transitively dependant on the primary key of the table, in that category is determined by the key, but country is in turn determined by category.  Consequently the integrity of the database is prejudiced.

What his means is that the table is not normalized to Third Normal Form (3NF) which requires that all non-key columns are functionally determined solely by the whole of the table's key.  When a transitive dependency is introduced this is not the case, and the table is open to the risk of update anomalies.  To correct this the redundant Country column would be removed from the table, and represented in the form by an unbound combo box.  The top-down selection of a country and then a category can still be achieved by the use of correlated combo boxes, but it requires a little more work in the form's design.  You'll find an example as ComboDemo.zip in my public databases folder at:

https://onedrive.live.com/?cid=44CC60D7FEA42912&id=44CC60D7FEA42912!169

If you have difficulty opening the link copy its text (NB, not the link location) and paste it into your browser's address bar.

In this little demo file there are various options illustrated, using forms in both single and continuous forms view.  The former is relatively simple to implement, but the latter is trickier when the bound column of a combo box is a hidden 'surrogate' key rather than the visible text column.  This can result in data becoming invisible in the combo box in some rows in the form.  No data is lost, merely invisible, but it is nevertheless a confusing and unsatisfactory solution.  The answer is to use a 'hybrid' control comprising a text box superimposed on a combo box to give the impression of a single combo box control.  The example in my demo for a simple 2-level hierarchy would be the appropriate model if you are using a form in continuous forms view.

Whether you wish to normalize your Customers table to 3NF is a matter for your own judgement of course, and in practice people often are prepared not to do so, but if you should decide to accept the lack of normalization, you should at least be aware of the potential risk to the data of update anomalies.

a. user say:

Hi Pol,

 

Actually it's not feasible to directly transfer a tenant from our partner to Microsoft. You may consider manually migrating from GoDaddy to Microsoft. Here are the detailed steps:

 

  1. Export your emails from Outlook client for backup.
  2. Then you need to remove your custom domain (if you have one) from your GoDaddy tenant.
  3. Cancel your Office 365 license in GoDaddy.
  4. Purchase your desired plan in Microsoft.
  5. Verify and add your removed domain to Microsoft.
  6. Import the backup emails to the new accounts in Outlook client.

 

Note: Change to DNS records can take up to 72 hours to let it spread across the internet, so what I suggest is performing this during the weekend to lower the impact to your business.
