=IF(A2="","",formula)

Hi Lou,

Two-step verification is designed to prompt users every time there is a log-in attempt for security measures. This is for us to ensure that your account will not be compromised in any way. If you don’t want to keep receiving this prompt, what you can do is to create an app password from your Outlook.com account and save the app password in the Outlook app. Please take note that you’ll need to activate the two-step verification on your account first (see steps below):  

  1. Log in to your Outlook account and click the Security tab.
  2. On the bottom part of the page, click more security options.
  3. Under Two-step verification, click Set up two-step verification.
  4. Click Next and under verify my identity with, choose A phone number where the code will be sent and under What mobile device do you want to install the app on?, select Other.
  5. Type in your code, click Next and click Done.
  6. Once the two-step verification is activated, go back to Security tab and click Create a new app password.
