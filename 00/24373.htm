rohn007 say:
MS does not provide a way of "de-licensing" or "deactivating" an Office 2010 installation.  The license terms include a requirement that if you are transferring a license to a new machine it must no longer be usable on the old machine. You do that by uninstalling or by destroying the old computer (ie returning to manufacturer).  The "uninstall" process does NOT contact the activation system.

To transfer a 2010 license to a new computer you must have a "FPP" or "Retail" license.  That license type allows you to install on more than one computer at the same time and to transfer those installations to new computers.

When you are transferring a license to a new computer and you are already at the limit of your installation count you will get an activation error message along the lines of "too many activations".  In that case you have to do a "Phone Activation" instead of an Internet Activation.  When you phone in, don't reply to the initial prompts (you'll just get the same error message). Instead, wait until the phone system "errors out" and gives you the option of talking to a live service rep.  Explain that you are transferring the installation to a new computer and that you have uninstalled or retired the old computer.  They should allow your activation to complete.
