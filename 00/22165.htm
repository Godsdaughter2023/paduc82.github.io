Word O365 - TOC on multiple pages but keep the same heading? - Microsoft Community

tamras72 say:

Word O365 - TOC on multiple pages but keep the same heading?

I have a document with the following layout

Page 1 - Cover Page - no heading

Page 2 - TOC - with heading but no page number

Page 3... Contents - with different heading than TOC and starts at page 1 of ##. 

After I finished reformatting the contents (total of 124 pages), I inserted the TOC in Page 2. Since it requires multiple pages for the TOC -- how do I maintain the same heading for all the TOC pages?  Page 2 of the the TOC has the heading meant for the Contents.

Final Document:

Page 1 - Cover Page

Page 2 - TOC (page 1 of 3), heading: "Table of Contents"

Page 3 - TOC (page 2 of 3), heading: "User's Guide - page 1 of 124"  -- the heading still should be "Table of Contents"

Page 4 - TOC (page 3 of 3), heading: "User's Guide - page 2 of 124" -- the heading still should be "Table of Contents"

Page 5 - Contents (page 1 of 124), heading: "User's Guide - page 3 of 124"  -- it should be page 1 of 124

...

Thanks. 


jay freedman say:

For an overview of what's needed, see http://wordfaqs.ssbarnhill.com/FrontMatterRibbon.htm.

First you must insert an Odd Page section break between the TOC and the beginning of the contents. This is necessary to enable the different headers.

Open the header pane on the first page of the contents, so the Header & Footer Tools tab appears on the ribbon. There,

  • Click Page Number > Format Page Numbers and set the page numbering to start at 1.
  • Click the Link to Previous button to turn it off -- you do not want the header of this section to be the same as that in the previous section (the TOC).
  • You don't want the header to show the total number of pages in the document, including the cover page and TOC, because then the last page will say something like "page 121 of 124". To fix that, right-click the total and click Toggle Field Codes. You'll see {numpages}. Change that to {sectionpages} and press F9 to update the field.
  • Click the Previous button to go to the header in the TOC section, delete the existing text, and enter "Table of Contents".


jay freedman say:

Do you have either or both of "Different First Page" and "Different Odd & Even Pages" checked in the Header & Footer Tools ribbon? If so, turn them off in both the TOC section and the contents section.

Each section has three completely separate headers (also footers) (http://www.addbalance.com/word/headersfooters.htm). Each one of the three may be displayed or not, depending on which page you look at. When you insert a new section break, the section after the break inherits the headers from the original section, and I think that's why you see blank headers -- those are headers that didn't have any text before you inserted the break.

If you don't need a different header on the first page of a section, or if you don't need different odd/even page headers, then you turn off the two check boxes and use just the one remaining header for all pages of the section.

Regardless of what you are asking about, you can not use Me in a query.  Me is strictly available only in form, report and class VBA modules.  Instead use the full form reference in this style:

   Forms![name of main form].[name of main form's subform control].Form.[name of control in subform]
