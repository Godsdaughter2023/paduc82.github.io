Thanks in advance for any help you can give.

jerryduan_msft8021 say:

Hi Purura,

Regarding “with "visitor" permission level, I can still "Export to Excel" and make other alterations within libraries.”, I believe that you have granted the user a permission level higher than “View Only”. With at least “Full Control” permission for a site, you can check the user’s permission level at Site settings > Site permissions > Check Permissions > Enter a user/group name or email address to check the permission.

To achieve the requirement, I suggest you create a customized permission level (Let’s say “Visitor View”) and then create a group (Let’s say “Visitors”) with this permission level for those “visitor”. Below are the detailed steps:
1. In the Site permissions page > click Permission Levels in the PERMISSIONS ribbon.

2. Click “View Only” > scroll down to the bottom of the page > click Copy Permission Level.

3. Give a name (“Visitor View”) for this newly created permission level. Then remove the detailed permission items to restrict this permission level such as removing “Create Alerts”.

4. Save the “Visitor View” permission level >Go back to the Site permissions page > click Create Group.

5. Give a name(“Visitors”) to the group with the “Visitor View” permission level.

6. Go back to the Site permissions page > click Grant Permissions > in the pop out “Share” window, click SHOW OPTIONS.

7. Then you can either share users/security groups with the “Visitor View” permission level directly or through the group “Visitors”.

If anything is unclear, please let me know.

Regards,
Jerry
