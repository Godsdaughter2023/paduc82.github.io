Hot Plugging and Cold Plugging - Microsoft Community

shopper1 say:

Hot Plugging and Cold Plugging

I just wanted to double-check the terms.

A USB keyboard with a tangled wire at the back of the computer can be unplugged, untangled, and plugged back in without shutting down the computer first. USB devices are hot pluggable

However, in the case of a tangled PS2 mouse, the computer needs to be shut down first, then the mouse can be unplugged, untangled, and plugged back in, and then the computer can be rebooted. PS2 devices are cold pluggable

Do I have it correct?

a. user say:

Hi,

Either from those two can be use. I'll gave you scenarios.

Scenario 1:

You are using your computer for internet, games, or other documentation. Then suddenly the PS2 mouse or keyboard has been removed by someone at the back of your computer. Automatically the function of mouse or keyboard will not work even your PC is on. If you plugged them back, the mouse or keyboard will still not work until you turned the power off and turned it back on.

Scenario 2:

You are moving your whole PC to a different location. If your PC is off, then you can unplug the PS2 mouse and keyboard right a way. You must plugged them back before you start the computer on the new location. You can unplugged the PS2 mouse and keyboard while the PC is on. The problem is, you cannot shut the PC down properly. Only by removing the power cable to the power socket,

The rule for Cold Plugging is, every time the connection is interrupted you need to reboot them. It allows a device to synchronize.
