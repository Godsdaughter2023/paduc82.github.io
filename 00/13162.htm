Windows Essentials

 

Once Windows Live Mail is installed, just sign in your account and wait for the program to download your emails. Now that the emails have been downloaded, we now go to exporting it so you can save it to your storage. For more info about configuring your account, refer on the link below:

 

How do I add an account to Windows Live Mail

 

1. Create a New Folder on your desktop so you can export the email on that folder.

2. Make sure you are signed in and the email messages are downloaded. On the upper left part of Windows Live Mail, click the blue button and a drop down list will appear.

3. Click Export email. Then choose Microsoft Windows Live Mail. Click Next.

4. Click Browse then look for the folder you created and export it there. You will then be taken to a page where you will choose if you want to export all folders or only what you choose.

2. Click the blue button where you can print, save and see everything else you can do. Then click Import messages, choose Windows Live Mail the click Next.

3. Now you are asked on where you want to import your emails from. Click Browse and locate your file that you pasted on your desktop. Click Ok.

 

Now we go to the auto forwarding. You need to sign in on the web mail to do this.

4. On the Forwarding page, click Forward your mail to another e-mail account.

If you have unsuccessfully tried to recover your files from a recent/prior to infection - external backup - as it's the first and best method to restore your data, or if there wasn't any; all you can do, at this point, is to back up the encrypted files 'as-they-are' on another storage media (like an external HDD) and leave it be until a solution is found (if one is ever found - there is always hope).
