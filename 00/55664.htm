techfreak_ say:

You only need to be concerned with where you are calling to, not where you are calling from, in regards to Skype.  An Internet connection that does not block access to Skype's services is required, regardless of the location.

1. Calling a US mobile that is in South Korea for the month?  

If a US mobile is in South Korea using their home provider's service with roaming, they will be reachable via their US phone number. This makes the call a US call. You would only need a subscription that covers US calls.

However, if the US mobile utilizes a South Korean sim card while on vacation and has a South Korea or other country number, a subscription that covers the price of the call to that number, would be required. The world subscription only cover Canada, Guam, Hong Kong SAR, Japan, Puerto Rico, Singapore, Thailand, and United States mobiles. You would need a subscription that covers the mobile numbers you plan on calling. For example South Korean mobiles, if you plan on dialing a South Korean mobile number.

If you plan to have internet access wherever you go, people can call you Skype to Skype to avoid phone call charges.

2. What is the correct plan for that US Mobile number to use while in South Korea to call either a Mobile number or a Landline in the US?

Skype is not a mobile provider. It is a VoIP provider that is dependent on Internet access. As long as you have Internet access and can pay your Internet bill, Skype to Skype calls will be free. If you plan on using Skype to call US landlines and mobiles, you only need a subscription that covers calls to the US.
