Hi IkeVeles, I see this is your first post here. Welcome to our community!

You can do a "full wipe" from inside Windows -- without the need to get a CD/flash drive.  You can do this from inside the PC Settings app (under the "update and recovery" section), or you can do it in Safe Mode.  Just choose the "PC Reset" option.  The entire hard drive will be erased, so be sure you save anything you need before you start.

If you do need to do this in Safe Mode, here's how:

  1. Press +I to display the Settings charm menu.
  2. Click the Power Options icon, then hold down the Shift Key while you click the Restart option. Keep holding shift until the screen says it is restarting.
  3. On the first screen that appears, select Troubleshoot, then select Advanced Options and then use PC Reset.
