David wrote:

First, the trendline formula y = 2E+07x^-1.563 is a "power" trendline, not an "exponential" trendline, as Excel uses those terms.

If your x data are in A1:A10 and your y data are in B1:B10, select two horizontal cells (e.g. E1 and F1) and aray-enter the following formula (press ctrl+shift+Enter instead of just Enter):

=LINEST(LN(B1:B10),LN(A1:A10))

Then for any x in A11 (e.g.), the estimated y is derived by the formula:

=EXP($E$1)*A11^$F$1

You can confirm the formula by entering the following into C1 and copying down through C10:

=EXP($E$1)*A1^$F$1

Note:  Typically, the values in C1:C10 will not match B1:B10 exactly.  But if you add a series for C1:C10 to your chart, the values should closely match the "power" trendline.  It might not match exactly because Excel does not use LINEST in this way to derive the trendline.

In order to use LINEST with other types of trendline formulas, see http://spreadsheetpage.com/index.php/tip/chart_trendline_formulas.

entegy say:
Zune doesn't override your library settings. That settings page states quite clearly that it uses your Windows 7 libraries to monitor folders for media. There is also a "Manage separately" button on the same page that allows you to setup your own folders in Zune instead of using your Windows 7 libraries.

As well, the default for picture syncing is not "sync everything". Go to Settings>phone>Sync options and you'll find buttons to control what gets synced. You can set music, pictures, video and podcasts to "Items I choose". I say to use "Items I choose" instead of "manual" because any media you do sync will be kept up-to-date, but you have to choose it first.

Once you set that up, your pictures will just sync in Zune to your Pictures folder.
