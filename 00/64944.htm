hazmat radio say:

A Double NAT means you have two routers on the same network connection.


A normal connection, if you had cable as an example would be - Internet > Cable Modem > Router > Local devices (PC's, phones, Xbox)


Double NAT - Internet > Cable Modem > Router #1 > Router #2 > Local devices (PC's, phones, Xbox)


In that second scenario, going from local devices to router, the router number #2 is trying to manage the port traffic for all the devices but then router #1 is not passing the port information on through to the internet and giving you the NAT Type of strict.


You need to try and connect to that first router on your network there.
