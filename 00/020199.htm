Sorry for didn’t deliver the information clearly. “autodiscover.cloud.contoso.com” is just a template Autodiscover DNS record for reference. Office 365 uses a CNAME record to implement the Autodiscover service for Outlook and mobile clients. The Autodiscover CNAME record we need is as following:

 

Alias:autodiscover

Target:autodiscover.outlook.com

    How can I set auto-correct for straight quotes, not curly, in Excel for Mac 2008?


    I'm an editor proofing a website created by coders working with Excel spreadsheets that they dump into HTML somehow. (Not my choice of method.) Most of the apostrophes are showing up backward. In many cases they have extra spaces fore and aft, particularly around possessives. 

    On the Web we want straight apostrophes. For the quality control the client requires, these have to be right. I've been starting to work with Format Painter to transfer formatting from the correct apostrophes to the ones that need attention. But in the sans serif font of the spreadsheets, it's hard to tell just by eye which ones are really correct. I want to be able to check these the way I use the formatting palette to check the typeface and point size of text.

    I know how to find what I'm looking for in Word, but in Excel, the options under Tools/Autocorrect are pretty limited; ditto the ones under Format/Autoformat.

    Ideas, anyone?
    bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
    You cannot find an AutoCorrect setting for curly [typographer's] quotes in Excel because there isn't one :-)

    Excel uses straight quotes exclusively. One exception would be if a Font is used that does not support anything but curlies... and that's a rarity. Another is that in the Mac version of Excel there is a means of "forcing" curly quotes but most users aren't even aware of the capability & AFAIK it doesn't work in the PC version of the program. My guess is that the conversion is being made as a result of something that's being done by the manner in which the HTML is being processed... Especially because of the spacing & reversal you mentioned [These changes may also be related to Language.].

    I'm not clear on whether these files are originating in Excel 2008 or elsewhere or whether you're actually working with the Excel files yourself. If you do have the Excel files & are opening them in Excel 2008 I can only offer 3 suggestions that may help:

    • Even if a sans serif font is used in Excel, curly quotes & apostrophes should be apparent. You may have to Zoom in and/or increase the font size and depending on the font they may appear oblique rather than curly, but they would be noticeable.
    • Make certain that the content in Excel is properly formatted using current Fonts as well as the correct Language [Tools> Language]
    • Use Find & Replace to check the Excel file for curlies by using the keystroke Option+[ to enter a curly left quote in the Find What field. If any are found, use a normal left quote in the Replace With field. Similar keystrokes would have to be used for curly right quotes & curly left & right single quotes [apostrophes]. I imagine that the process could be automated if there are a number of Excel files involved but Office 2008 doesn't support VBA -- whether Apple Script could be used I don't know.
