Before proceeding, we need to know whether you are using Microsoft/Local account to sign in to Windows 10.

If you are using Microsoft account to login to Windows 10, there is no option to disable the password and sign in to Windows 10 directly without entering password.

Step 1:

If you are using Local account to login to Windows 10, you can disable the password and sign in to Windows 10 directly without entering password. Follow the below steps if you use Local account.

  1. Press Windows + I keys to open settings menu.

  2. Click on Accounts.

  3. Click on Sign in options.

  4. Click on Change under Change your account password.

  5. Enter your current password and leave the other two files for new password as empty and close the windows by clicking on OK button.

  6. Sign out and try to sign in again and check.

Step 2:

You may also create the 4 digit PIN which will reduce your time to enter full password. Follow the below steps.

  1. Press Windows + I keys to open settings menu.

  2. Click on Accounts.

  3. Click on Sign in options.

  4. Click on Add under PIN.

  5. Follow the onscreen instructions to create Pin.

  6. Sign out and try to sign in again using PIN as your Sign in option.

Hope it helps. Kindly update the status of the issue with required information, we will be happy to help you.

  1. Open Settings from Start menu.
  2. Navigate to Update & security > Recovery > Advanced startup.
  3. Select Advanced startup. Now, your computer will be restarted in recovery mode.
  4. Click on Troubleshoot and select Advanced options. Go to Startup Settings.
  5. Click Restart button to restart your computer.
  6. You can now choose an option using your keyboard (number keys or function keys). To start up in Safe Mode, press 4 or F4. To start up in Safe Mode with networking, press 5 or F5. To start up in Safe Mode with the Command Prompt, press 6 or F6. If you change your mind and decide you don't want to boot into Safe Mode after all, hit Enter to boot normally.
