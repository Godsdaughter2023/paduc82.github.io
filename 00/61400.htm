No, those are your Power settings.  They do more than blank your screen; they can turn off the computer completely.

As I said, right-click an empty spot on your Desktop, then choose Personalize.  On the Personalize screen, in the lower right corner, you should see an icon labeled "Screen Saver".  Click that icon (or the label) to bring up the Screen Saver window.  In the drop-down menu labeled "Screen saver" you should see at least a half-dozen choices.  One of them is "Ribbons".  Choose this, then click the Preview button.  The small Preview window should start displaying a series of colored ribbons.  Now, click the drop-down menu again and choose "Blank".  The Preview window should turn completely black.  Does this work for you?

Method 1: Update the drivers.

  4. Right click on each display/graphic drivers and select Disable.
  5. Restart the Computer in normal mode

Step 2:  Update the display driver.

  1. "Windows + X" and select "Device Manager”.
  2. Double click the item, "Display Adapter driver" (or the standard driver) under display adapter.
  3. Choose, "Browse my computer for driver software”.
  4. Choose, "Let me pick from a list of device drivers on my computer".
  5. Select the graphics card driver, and click the next button.
  6. Follow the same steps for the additional graphics card as well.
  7. Check if you are able to boot the computer normally.

Download and install drivers
