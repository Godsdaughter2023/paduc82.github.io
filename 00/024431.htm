bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

PMFJI, but I just wanted to point out that a part of what's perplexing you is your expectation of the Colors dialog. Although understandable, it's incorrect :-) In Excel the sliders do not reflect the values of the color currently applied as a Cell Border or Cell Fill in the way they do for Shapes/Objects. That's why you always see the 0,0,0 values.

If you want to analyze the currently applied color, click on the Eyedropper tool in the Colors dialog, then click on the colored Border/Fill in the Format Cells dialog. The sliders then will display the RGB (or CMYK, etc.) values of that color.

dennishogewoning say:

Actually they recently rebuild OneDrive for Business which is now pretty much the same as the consumer one. Maybe this is something you can try. This also includes selective sync, higher file and folder limits and overall much more stable! Please note that this is not yet supported on Windows 8.1 and you can not yet sync Sharepoint libraries.

Download it here: https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Meet-the-OneDrive-for-Business-Next-Generation-Sync-Client-7af500d9-a18e-4abb-8450-b94f4e52c1a0?ui=en-US&rs=en-US&ad=US 

Step by step instruction:

1. Stop syncing your current OneDrive For Business library (first make sure your files are safe instead they were not uploaded yet)
2. Install the software from the above link.
3. When installed open registry and navigate to HKEY_CURRENT_USER\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\OneDrive
4. Create a DWORD key with value "DefaultToBusinessFRE" and give it valuedata 1
5. Create a DWORD key with value "EnableAddAccounts" and give it valuedata 1
6. Start up OneDrive and login to your Office 365 account
7. Enjoy OneDrive without headaches

Dennis Hogewoning
