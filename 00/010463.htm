richardfarr say:

Non-deletable characters in Word

After revising a flush-right header in a Word for Mac doc recently, I discovered I now have two symbols (a small square and a 2) flush-left in the same header throughout the doc. The square can be selected but not deleted, and the 2 can't be selected or deleted. Any ideas where these came from or how I can get rid of them? (See image.) Thanks!
daniel g. grau say:
[Note: The OP sent me a sample file.]

Your pages are numbered 1, 2, 3, 2, 3, 4 … because at the end of the third page of the document you have a "Section Break (Next Page)" whose "Start at" numbering is "2." Somehow this second header became malformed. In effect, the "□2" artifact cannot be deleted or cut as it is, although it responds somewhat weirdly to format changes.

There are several ways to get rid of that stray header:
  1. Save the document as .docx (visually, nothing will change), close it and reopen it to find it fixed (gone). If required, re-save as .doc.
  2. Save the document as .RTF, close and reopen. The header will have changed to an underscore that is deletable.
  3. Access the second header, select the purple "Header and footer" tab in the Ribbon, then place a checkmark on "Link to previous." Word will offer to delete that second header. If you now unlink to leave things as they were, it's does not reappear.
  4. Access the second header, place your cursor before the square, type a Return, then delete the  "□2" artifact.
To change the page numbering, access the header, then use Insert > Page Numbers > Format > Modify > Start at.

If you want to see that Section Break, place a checkmark in Word > Preferences > View > All.

It is better to keep name-dated or numbered versions of your file, then open any two and compare them with Tools > Track Changes > Compare Documents and Save the comparison.
