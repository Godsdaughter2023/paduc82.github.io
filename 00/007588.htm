john korchok say:

Here is how to lock a text box to a particular paragraph:

  1. In Word, choose Word>Preferences>View.
  2. In the Show section, check Object anchors. OK out.

If you select a text box, now you can see the anchor icon to the left of the paragraph to which it is anchored. In Word 2008, the anchor display is buggy: you often can only see the anchor if you select and move the shape. In other versions of Word, the anchor remains visible at all times.

Once get the anchor beside your preferred paragraph:

  1. Choose Format>Text Box.
  2. Click on the Layout tab, then on the Advanced button.
  3. Check Lock anchor. Now the box will remain anchored to the same paragraph wherever it moves in the document.

In most versions of Word, the best way to move a text box between pages is to click on the anchor and move it to the new page. The text box stays in the same position relative to the new paragraph. However, with 2008's disappearing anchor, this is nearly impossible. However, you should still be able to copy and paste. Before you paste, click in the paragraph to which you would like the text box to be anchored. Upon pasting, the anchor should get attached to the selected paragraph.

The commands that changes the list levels in a multilevel list are OutlineDemote and OutlinePromote which already have built-in shortcuts (Alt+Shift+Right arrow and Alt+Shift+Left arrow, respectively).

Now while with the Master Page showing go to Insert > Picture and browse to where you have your certificate picture stored. Arrange on your page. Go back to the Master Page tab and close your master page and the certificate should be in the background and help you lay out your page.

When you are ready to print go back to Page Design > Master Page > and again click the down arrow and this time select 'none' and this will remove/hide the certificate Master Page. You can reapply it if you want at a future time.

When you do print I would print on a blank sheet of paper and hold it up against the actual certificate paper to confirm that every thing lines up as you want it. This way you don't waste any certificate pages. If things are off a bit then go back to your page layout in Publisher, select the misaligned text box and use the Alt + Arrow keys to nudge them where you want them.
