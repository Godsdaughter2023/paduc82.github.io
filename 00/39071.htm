Although by default the task bar appears on the bottom of the screen,

side to another, simply click on an unused part of it and drag it

fernandoyebra1 say:
Hi ,

My name is Fernando. I am an Independent Advisor. I am here to guide you with possible resolution to the issue you are reporting.

The drive you are seeing is the result of update 1803 and is a recovery partition that is supposed to be hidden. The way to resolve this is by removing the mapping.

There are two ways you can do this.

Remove the drive letter (we will assume for an example system is reporting drive D is full)
1. Hit Windows key + R together to go into Run
2. Type in the following: diskpart
3. Type “list volume” (no quotes)
4. Select “volume D” (volume corresponding to the drive letter that is being reported as full)
5. Remove letter=D (corresponding to the drive letter that is being reported as full)

You can also accomplish this by
1. Hit Windows key + R together to go into Run
2. Type in the following: diskmgmt.msc
3. You will see a graphical display of all your partitions and drives.
4. Select and right click on the partition that is being reported as full
5. Select Change Drive Letter and Paths
6. Click on Remove
7. Click Yes to confirm
8. Click Yes at the warning


I hope this helps. Feel free to ask back any questions and let me know how it goes.
