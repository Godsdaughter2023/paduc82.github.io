tom van stiphout say:

That's called a "Cartesian product" in case you want to read up on it. It happens when you put two tables in a query and don't link them. You will get A * B rows.

In your case you probably want to link some field in tableA to some field in tableB. Then append the smaller set to tableC.

Or you meant to simply append data from tableA to tableB, in which case tableB should not be on the design surface at all.
