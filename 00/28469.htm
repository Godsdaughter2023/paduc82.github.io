karl timmermans [outlook mvp 2012-2018] say:
I stand corrected on one thing - the Outlook views do use the Email DisplayName (Name portion if available) as the from address when using the <From> Address in a given view for an email folder. That holds true whether you use any existing view or create a new one.

For the sake of accuracy, Outlook uses the Email DisplayName contained in the email message which can be from anyone inside or outside of the organization - has nothing specific to do with the GAL. In your scenario, it just happens that you're looking an emails from members within your organization. To re-iterate, when someone (anyone - inside or outside of your organization) send you an email, the <Name> portion of the Email Display Name which is in the format of <Name (email address)> will be used but if there is no <Name> - then the email address will be used. "We can't display your message right now" error in Outlook - Microsoft Community

monicaffs say:

"We can't display your message right now" error in Outlook Web App trying to view encrypted email

Split from this thread.

We finally solved the issue on our own, so I'll post it for the benefit of the community - somewhat begrudgingly since I'm not happy with the 1+ month saga trying to get help from Microsoft.

It was a feature included with our outsourced email package called Targeted Attack Protection that apparently strips suspicious content from emails.   It appears that upon receipt to our exchange server, the HTML attachment of the O365 encrypted email was modified by TAP such that it would not work when opened, but was not totally corrupted as to get a message that it was not recognizable as an HTML object.   The solution was to whitelist *** Email address is removed for privacy *** for TAP.

Since Microsoft has no straightforward process for helping a recipient of an O365 encrypted email like us since we don't subscribe to O365 and cannot open a ticket with the O365 team, you need cooperation from the sender.   The key was that we had them send a test email directly to a home address, and it did not have a problem.   Initially we made the mistake of forwarding the email from our business to a private email (and Microsoft).  It did not work from there so we concluded that the issue was not with our firewall when in fact the email attachment was modified as it came into the business email, then would not work wherever it was forwarded to.

Hope this helps,

Roland
