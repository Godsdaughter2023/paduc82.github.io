If you insert the picture into a once cell table whose properties you have set so that it does not "automatically resize to fit contents" and you set the preferred width of the table to the required width for the picture and the row height to the exact amount, the picture will be constrained to the size of that cell.

To facilitate the repetitive insertion of such a table, select it and then via File>Options>Proofing>AutoCorrect Options>AutoCorrect, insert into the Replace control the text that you want to be replaced by such a table.   Then when ever you type that text and press the space bar, a table with those attributes will be inserted into the document.

Before creating the AutoCorrect entry, use CTRL+ALT+u to remove the border from the table if you do not want it.

c. Click on the “Domain Settings” link at the left side of the page.
d. Under MX Record, copy the MX Server values.

 

II. Change the MX Record configured in your domain.

Once you have copied the MX Record, you then need to edit the MX Record configured in your domain.

a. Go to the registrar where you have purchased your domain. 
b. You may contact the technical support of the registrar where you have purchased your domain for assistance in changing the MX Record.

 

If you’ve already configured your domain settings but suddenly encountered an error, please refresh your Windows Live Admin Center account by following the instructions below:

a. Go to http://domains.live.com and sign in to your account.     
b. Click on the domain you would like to manage.
c. Click on the “Domain Settings” link at the left side of the page.    
d. Click on the “Refresh” button.
