Microsoft word audio notes play back

I downloaded microsoft 2011 and even when I slow down the frequency it still plays back to fast and high pitched. How can I fix this audio note to play back normally?
john korchok say:

It's a little involved, but certainly possible:

  1. In Word, open your document in Notebook Layout View.
  2. Choose Tools>Audio Notes>Export Audio. The track will be exported as an .MP4 audio file.
  3. Download Audacity, a free audio editor from here: Audacity Download
  4. Install Audacity.
  5. Open Audacity, then use File>Open and find your exported audio note.
  6. From the Effect menu, choose Change Speed.
  7. To start, choose either a Speed Multiplier of .66 or a Percent Change of about -33.
  8. Preview the result, then adjust the numbers until you get a natural sound.
  9. Save the result.

Unfortunately, there is no Audio Note import in Word. However, in Word 2011, you can use the Media Browser (Standard toolbar in Print Layout view) to import audio and keep it with your Word file.

Appending text to a textbox control source - Microsoft Community

dmkkg say:

Appending text to a textbox control source

A text box on a form is bound to a field in a table.  Is it possible to display the field with additional text?
hansv mvp say:

You could set the control source to something like

=[FieldName] & " with additional text"

You must give the text box a different name than the field name, otherwise Access will become confused.

And the value of the text box won't be editable.

Alternatively, leave the control source set to the field name, and set the Format property of the text box to

@" and additional text"

This will leave the value editable. While you edit the field value, the additional text will be invisible, but as soon as you leave the text box, the additional text will be displayed.
