Hi Hajash,

I'd like to first explain that for internal users accessing SharePoint Online services, users need to pass the authentication. This is important security process to protect contents in tenant's SharePoint Online. So, normally when a user want to access SharePoint Online in his tenant, he needs to pass the authentication. To learn more, see SharePoint Online authentication.

If you want to share a SharePoint Online page with internal user, the proper method should be access the page and click Share button, send invitation emails to your internal users. When they want to access this page, they need to enter their work/school accounts for passing the authentication.

If your initial requirement is that let user access this page without signing, a not suggested method is first access Site Pages library where contains this page, click "...">Share>select Anyone with the link can view/edit>Copy link>send the link to your internal users. With this guest link, they could open the page without signing. However, this method is for external sharing with external users.

Tim

macropod say:

As Doug says, either approach would require macros. Such macros might conditionally add extra fields/controls to the document, or they might simply move the insertion point to a nominated field/control. Those are design choices. Adding extra fields/controls to the document is far more complex than moving the insertion point to a nominated field/control, plus they necessarily result in the document layout changing as a result of the user's input.
