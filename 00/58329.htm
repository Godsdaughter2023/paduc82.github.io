There are a few ways to add steps to the shutdown process, if you wish.

Use the keyboard instead of the mouse.

a. Press the Windows Logo Key to bring up the Start Menu. Press the Right Arrow key once to select the Shutdown Button. If your sure you want to continue, press the Enter Key to shutdown.

b. Press the Windows Logo Key to bring up the Start Menu. Press the Right Arrow key Twice and the Up Arrow Key once to bring up the menu. Use the Arrow keys to select any of the 5 options and press the Enter Key.

c. Press Ctrl+Alt+Del once. You will see several options on the screen, including the shutdown menu in the lower/right corner.

d. If you just want to Lock or Switch User, press the Windows Logo Key+L. This will take you directly to the Logon Screen.

Also, Windows 7 includes a built-in safe shutdown component. When you shutdown the computer, if there are any programs still running that could potentially cause corruption, you will see a screen advising you of the problem. You will have the options of clicking the Force Shutdown Button, or the Cancel Button.

If your Wi-Fi signal mysteriously drops off in certain rooms, it may be due to your home’s construction. Especially thick walls can muffle Wi-Fi signals. Another source of Wi-Fi signal problems is interference. Wireless devices feature the ability to transmit and receive on several separate channels, but in many cases, they shift from the factory set to a single default channel. This can mean that several wireless sources in a small area, such as an apartment building, are all competing for signal in the same frequency band. You may also find intermittent signal problems caused by interference from other, non-Wi-Fi devices. Wi-Fi operates in the 2.4-gigahertz band, as do cordless phones and some remote alarm products. When active, these devices can reduce your Wi-Fi signal strength due to interference.
