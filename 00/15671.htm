ken sheridan say:
While I understand that tables are heaps.........

 

I'd prefer the more mathematically orthodox term 'set', but the point is that a set, and hence a table,  has no intrinsic order.  Don't be deceived by the fact that in a table's datasheet one row appears before another into thinking it does.  The perceived order can be changed at will, and even if the initial order does reflect something about the way the data is physically stored, that's completely irrelevant to the database relational model in which a row in a table is identified solely by the value of a key, never by position.  This is one way in which a relational database table differs radically from a file.

There seems to be some functionality to use the phone as a bluetooth headset for the laptop. I've been asked during establishing the bluetooth partnership if I want to add the headset profile, but I said No. So I cannot tell you what the headset profile actually does.

But as the phone exists in your partnerships the sync should also work.

  1. Make sure the newest version of Mobile Device Center is installed on your Vista machine - currently this is 6.1(http://www.microsoft.com/downloads/details.aspx?FamilyId=46F72DF1-E46A-4A5F-A791-09F07AAA1914)
  2. Make sure bluetooth is enabled on you laptop
  3. If the bluetooth partnership to your phone does not exist yet make sure to set the bluetooth mode on your laptop to be discoverable
  4. On your phone start ActiveSync (Start - Programs - ActiveSync) and tab Menu - Connect via Bluetooth. If you do that for the first time a wizard comes up that searches for the laptop and establishes the partnership. Then sync is started automatically.

If you are already connected to ActiveSync via bluetooth you can force a sync by opening Mobile Device Center, select your device and doubleclick it or open ActiveSync on your device and tab Sync (left softkey).

 

If you have any problems in connecting your device to ActiveSync try to delete the partnerships of your phone (including the mysterious headset partnership) on your laptop and also the bluetooth partnership of your laptop on your phone and reestablish it by selecting Connect via bluetooth inside ActiveSync on your device. The bluetooth partnership should be established automatically by the ActiveSync wizard. If asked if you want to add bluetooth headset functionality for this partnership just select No.

 

I hope this works for you. If not don't hesitate do ask for details. I will try to answer
