videoschabe say: Hi,

weird behaviour, but to Nokia not a bug but a feature.

Example:
Today (18 April 08) you are in Berlin (GMT+1) and add an appointment to your N95 calendar for 25 April 08 19:00.

Tomorrow you'll hop on a plane with non-stop service to Phoenix/Arizona (GMT-7). After the plane has reached its final parking position you switch on your N95 and either you get the new time zone via the network operator or set it manually. It does not matter, the calendar entry for 25 April 08 19:00 will be recalculated to 25 April 08 10:00 (total of 9 hours back). That will be the time where you are at right now, when the appointment will start in Berlin, which is the timezone where you added the appointment to you calendar.

So you won't miss it when you stay in Phoenix.

To round up the example: you are pretty busy, take the next plane back to Munich (GMT+1). After successfully retrieving your luggage, you switch on your N95, get the new time zone and voila: the entry for 25 April 08 will be recalculated to 19:00.

Hope this helps a little understanding Nokia's approach on world-time-management. What is missing basically is the possibility to choose the timezone when adding an appointment to the calendar.

Of course there is a way to circumvent all this, but with the sacrifice of the "world clock". Switch off "get time from network operator", set your time zone to where you added all your calendar entries, and simply adjust the time. Now you will be asked if you have travelled to another time zone. Hit 'No' and you're done. All your calendar entries will remain the way you have entered them.

Cheers,
--Videoschabe
