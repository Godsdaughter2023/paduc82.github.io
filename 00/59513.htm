klondike.net say:

Try this:

For those of you, like me, who lost control of one of your storage spaces and could not access it through the GUI, which would only show an error existed but would not allow you to "Change Settings", I managed to find a solution.

From an Administrator PowerShell prompt:
    Get-StoragePool | Where-Object {$_.IsReadOnly -eq $True} | Set-StoragePool -IsReadOnly $False
Wait for the process to complete, then:

    Get-VirtualDisk | Where-Object {$_.IsManualAttach -eq $True} | Set-VirtualDisk -IsManualAttach $False

It worked for me. I now have my storage space back and the GUI works. I hope it works for those of you who have the same problem.

I should add that you must first install the PowerShell StorageSpaces module from:

    https://gallery.technet.microsoft.com/scriptcenter/storage-spaces-module-for-4be70a0c

Intel G45/G43 Display drivers from Windows Update disables HDMI sound on my media center - any suggestions

I went back to the restore point, the sound started working and the device appeared in the list. I re-applied just the security patches and it was still working. I applied the Intel G45/G43 Express Chipset Display patch (driver id 20289622) again on it's own and sound stopped working.

Sound was incredibly flaky under Vista, regularly getting lost after a sleep/resume, frequently (but not always) requiring a full re-boot.

I'm also getting BSODs on Win7 which appear to be display related (probably needs this patch). It seems to be ok when the media center is doing playback, but if it sits on the menus for any length of time and the MyPictures screen saver kicks in, it seems to go belly up after 15-20 minutes.

It's a very recent system build, using a Gigabyte GA-EG45MF-UD2H mobo with onboard graphics and HDMI, and the BIOS is up to date.

dave street say: That's the problem, the Microsoft supplied Intel drivers broke the HDMI sound, even though they're supposed to be the most up to date. And if Windows Update hasn't figured out the compatible versions of drivers we're in trouble.

I figured it out myself though - the Microsoft supplied drivers broke it. Then I tried the Gigabyte supplied drivers since it's their board, and that broke it in the same way. Then I tried the Intel supplied drivers for the G45 chipset - that worked and appears to have survived a reboot.

The interesting thing is that the Gigabyte download supplied what looks like the same files as the Intel one, but the Intel installer explicitly does the Graphics and the HDMI sound drivers as two separate steps, which the Microsoft and Gigabyte installers don't do (or at least don't say they are doing it).
