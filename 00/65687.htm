chriso747 say:

I assume that your MS Basic Display Adapter is the Intel HD Graphics or similar.   These basic adapters are what they are (embedded in your Motherboard) and are really only suitable for normal everyday functions on the computer and not for playing games that put greater demand on graphics and speed.   If you want better graphics and a card that matches your processor speed, then you would be better to switch to cards from Nvidia, AMD, etc.   

Re:  updating the driver.   If your adapter is the Intel one, as I have suggested above, then you should go to this link:

https://www.intel.com/content/www/us/en/support/intel-driver-support-assistant.html

This "assistant" will scan your system for the Intel product and driver and will suggest a newer driver if there is one required.   If after you complete this process and your game still asks you to update the driver, I would then disregard the message and treat it as an erroneous message.

After all this, if you still find your games "slow moving and freezing", then you will have to look elsewhere in your system as to where there could be a bottleneck (e.g. open programs, running apps, programs being updated, services or tasks opening up and running, any virus/malware, disk needing defragmenting, or even try using lower settings for a high demanding game).
