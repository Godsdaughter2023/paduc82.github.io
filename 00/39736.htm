Is it safe to store your passwords on your computer - Microsoft Community

flight23 say:

Is it safe to store your passwords on your computer

password storage
kosh vorlon say:

Hi Flight,

 

"Safe" is one of those terms that means a lot of different things to a lot of different people.

 

There are degrees of "safe" to just about everything - including storing passwords on your computer.  Here's an article on the subject: http://ask-leo.com/is_it_safe_to_let_my_browser_remember_passwords.html.  Here's another: http://www.howtogeek.com/68231/how-secure-are-your-saved-internet-explorer-passwords/.

 

As a general rule, I'd recommend against it (with some obvious exceptions where the computer requires that passwords be saved in order to verify what you enter to gain access to something that's password-protected - like your user login or a BIOS password if used - but only in the BIOS) or products like Family Safety where the computer needs to know the password to allow access to controls to make changes but that's just how the computer works and not what I think you're talking about.

 

But I think you're talking about online passwords like those used to access various websites and maybe some programs so you can get to them faster by bypassing the need to enter them manually every time. 

 

If you're lucky enough to never be infected by anything that steals your computer information or invaded by a hacker who uses the saved passwords to access all your private sites like forums and banking sites and credit card sites and network access to your company server where they can then access that information (perhaps even top secret info that could cause much harm and possibly get you into a lot of trouble), or fall victim to a scam where they get access to that information, or have your computer stolen and then hacked to gain general access and then have your other passwords all right there in front of them to use as they please while you're trying to change them all (and remember all of them and deal with the crime at the same time), then maybe you'll be OK.

 

If you're not lucky, then you've left the keys to everything right there easy to be found.  And if you decided to show both secure and insecure information because you dislike seeing that notice pop up all the time, you're even at greater risk.  And I'm not even considering the level of risk you're at right now depending on how well you attend to the security of your system and its connections.   It's not only a matter of turn on the Firewall, install a Firewall, and off to the races.  Good security involves much more and I don't have any idea how you've covered yourself aside from storing passwords on the computer.  There are many factors involved.  Start here for some basic information: http://www.microsoft.com/security/pc-security/mse.aspx.  For a variety of articles on how to secure your computer, see the following search: http://www.bing.com/search?q=How%20to%20secure%20your%20computer&sc=6-27&form=BSRTLB&pc=BBSR&QS=n.

 

Bottom line:  I don't know enough about your setup to know how well you are otherwise secured (e.g., are all your files encrypted, are all programs on your computer completely up-to-date in terms of updates, what operating system do you have - if XP you're already at risk because there are known holes that cannot be patched and never will be) to begin to guess at the level of risk associated with storing passwords (which no matter what is going to be an additional risk - perhaps small and perhaps not so small).  But no matter what else you do, almost no matter how perfect you do everything else, it is more risky and less safe than not doing it.  The above articles should help you decide if you feel comfortable doing so.  Many do and many don't.  I can only offer my personal opinion.  The computer, the risk, the potential harm caused if they fall into the wrong hands are all yours to consider and the decision is yours to make.  I can offer no guarantees if you do so no matter how you do it.  I can guarantee if you don't that you will be safer though there is a small risk based on methods of doing it anyway where you have elected not to do it (e.g., someone installs a keylogger or keylogger software/malware to get every single keystroke you make including what you push when entering passwords) - but that's a different issue than you deciding whether or not to consciously and intentionally do so.

 

Incidentally, while MSE will help in terms of blocking and/or removing malware that may allow this to happen on your computer - it is not really very effective if someone gets to you through and in the browser or by other non-malware means as mentioned above.  There are password-managing programs out there that claim to help do this and some work better than others - but in the end they too can be overcome and then someone has the entire list at their fingertips.

 

I hope this helps.  No I didn't give you a direct yes or no answer because I can't with the little I know about your situation and even if I knew everything, I still couldn't provide a guarantee.  I simply will end by saying that I do not and never will do this myself and do not setup my clients to do that (though some change my setup once I walk out the door) and I've been at this for well over 20 years.

 

Good luck!
