Formatting Trendline equation - Microsoft Community

niladri ray say:

Formatting Trendline equation

In a Excel Chart, I have a linear trendline, and now instead of displaying the whole equation (y = mx + c) of the trendline, I only want to display the slope (m). Can I do it by custom formatting ?

 

hansv mvp say:

You can't do that with formatting.

Clear the check box "Display equation on chart" for the trendline.

Let's say that the x-values for your chart series are in A2:A19 and the y-values in B2:B19.

In a blank cell on the data sheet, enter the formula

 

=LINEST(B2:B19,A2:A19)

 

If you haven't specified the x-values, i.e. Excel uses 1, 2, 3, ..., use =LINEST(B2:B19)

This formula returns the slope.

Click on the chart.

On the Insert tab of the ribbon, click Text Box, then drag a small rectangle on the chart.

Click in the formula bar.

Type =, then point to the cell with the slope.

Press Enter.

Drag the rectangle to where you want it.
