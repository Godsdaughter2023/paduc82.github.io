New computers that come preinstalled with Windows 7 often have what is called a recovery partition. This is used to reinstall the operating system in the event of a system crash. To access it, you will need to boot into when you start your computer by pressing a function key. This can be either F1, F2, F9, F10, F11, F12 or even DEL or Tab key. Consult the owners manual that came with your PC for instructions about how to reinstall Windows.

 

 

This is how the recovery partition is accessed for the most popular makes..

For Dell, press CTRL + F11 directly after powering up the unit

For HP, press F11 directly after powering up the unit

For LG, press F11 directly after powering up the unit

For Toshiba, press and hold '0' BEFORE and during the power up

For Acer, press and hold ALT + F10 as soon as you see the logo

For Asus, press F9 as soon as you see the Asus logo.

For Samsung, press F4 at power up..

For Fujitsu, press the F8 key repeatedly directly after powering up

For Advent, restart your computer. Then Press F10 repeatedly until the message "Starting System Recovery" is displayed

For Sony VAIO, restart and press "F8" or "F10" repeatedly until the "Advanced Boot Options" screen appears.

lemp say:

If all of your browsers work properly, you don't need to do anything.  However, there are some items that you should correct.

Because you are connected to your router using Ethernet (at a theoretical maximum speed of 1000 megabits/sec) and by wifi (at a theoretical maximum speed of 72 megabits/sec), Windows chooses the faster connection.  That is, your Internet data is going through your Ethernet cable not you wifi connection.

Your wifi adapter appears to be incorrectly configured.

  1. Open Network Connections (Start > ncpa.cpl > Enter)
  2. Right-click on "wireless network connection 1" and select Properties
  3. Select "Internet Protocol Version 4 (TCP/IPv4)," double click it or click Properties
  4. Select "Obtain an IP address automatically" and "Obtain DNS server address automatically"
  5. Click OK and then click OK again.

If you don't use a VPN, don't worry about the red X on its icon. 

"Wireless network connection 2 - Microsoft virtual wifi miniport" is used to create a "wifi hot spot" so that other wireless devices can connect through your computer.  Because you have a wireless router (your Netgear C3700), you do not need this capacity, so you also can ignore the red X on this icon.

As far as the incorrect display in the Network Sharing Center is concerned, I've seen various purported fixes for this problem but many of them involve editing the registry.  The correct display may come back if you restart the computer, but if it doesn't, you should just leave things alone -- unless you are experiencing additional problems in actually using your computer.
