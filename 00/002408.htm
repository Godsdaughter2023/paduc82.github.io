    foldover booklet - Microsoft Community

    david herbert campbell say:

    foldover booklet

    Is there any way to print a foldover booklet without ordering the pages properly, i.e., taking into account that the first sheet of paper has the back of the booklet on the left side and the first page on the right, he second sheet, backing the first sheet, has page 2 on the left and the next to the last page on the right, and so on? The original document is so much easier to compose and edit in page order and it is very difficult to set up adocument with many pages in the right order to print the booklet.
    daniel g. grau say:
    Imposition is a difficult art, so much so that a few years ago they sold separate programs for doing it in Quark Xpress, back in the time when it was king of DTP. In Word, you'll have to manage manually. Word's own booklets already have the proper layout for printing, two-pages-to-a-sheet:
    • 8, 1
    • 2, 7
    • 6, 3
    • 4, 5

    If your layout is 1, 2, 3, 4 (as four separate pages in Word) and you want 4&1 in the front and 2&3 in the back, you'll need to print 2 pages per sheet using the Print dialog's Layout option:

    • Print pages 2&3 (using Layout 1 2, i.e. in order)
    • Print pages 1&4 (using Layout 2 1) on the reverse face. Enter "1, 4" in the Page Range field.
    • Or vice versa

    To print an 8-page booklet laid out in Word as eight consecutive pages 1-8, you'll need to:
    • Print frontface pages 8&1 and 6&3 (enter a page range of  "8, 1, 6, 3")
    • Turn over the printed stack of two pages and feed them back
    • Print backface pages 4&5 and 2&7 (enter a page range of "4, 5, 2, 7").

    Depending on how your pages are stacked in the output tray (face up or face down), you may need to invert the last sequence and use a page range of "2, 7, 4, 5."

    If your printer does duplex printing, you can do it all at once with a page range of "8 1 6 3 2 7 4 5", still printing two-to-a-sheet... Told you, not intuititive. Or may be your printer includes a booklet-print facility?
