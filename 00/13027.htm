For now, ignore any third party headsets unless they are sold as Nokia compatible.  Either the original Nokia headset is faulty or the 3.5" headset jack is faulty.  How many times have you inserted and removed the Nokia headset?  I have occasionally seen problems where a film of some kind gets onto metal connectors during manufacturing, and causes a bad connection.  Perhaps insert and remove the headset 20 or 30 times to rub off any film?  Or put a little alcohol on the headset jack to aid the connection?

 

Can you take the Nokia headset to a mobile phone shop and test it in similar Symbian phones?  If it will not work in any Nokia phones, then I think Nokia gave you a bad headset.  Talk to Nokia support (or wherever you bought the phone) about a replacement, or just buy another Nokia-compatible headset yourself.  There are compatibility issues with third party headsets, so the best approach may be to buy another Nokia-branded headset, maybe a WH-205?  But if your headset works normally in other Nokia phones, the headset jack is faulty, and you should immediately exchange the E6 for a replacement, assuming the warranty is still good.  Don't wait!
