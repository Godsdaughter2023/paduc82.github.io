"The autocomplete list in Outlook 2010 is stored in the C:\Users\"username"\AppData\Local\Microsoft\Outlook\RoamCache folder in the Stream_Autocomplete_0_"numbers/Guid".dat file. When you get the new profile created in Outlook, open a new message and send it to someone then close Outlook and reopen it and close it. You can also still edit the list with NK2Edit from nirsoft.net and you can convert entries in the list that are EX "Exchange" entries to SMTP entries."

Better SSL/TLS cipher suite support? - Microsoft Community

williamorvis say:

Better SSL/TLS cipher suite support?

Both Outlook 2011 and the current release of Outlook (2015) for Mac only supports SSL/TLS cipher suites that were around in 1999:

  • SSL2_DES_192_EDE3_CBC_WITH_MD5
  • SSL2_RC4_128_WITH_MD5
  • SSL2_DES_64_CBC_WITH_MD5
  • SSL2 RC4_128_EXPORT40_WITH_MD5
  • TLS_RSA_WITH_3DES_EDE_CBC_SHA
  • TLS_RSA_WITH_RC4_128_SHA
  • TLS_RSA_WITH_RC4_128_MD5
  • TLS_RSA_WITH_DES_CBC_SHA
  • TLS_RSA_EXPORT_WITH_RC4_50_MD5
  • TLS_RSA_EXPORT_WITH_DES40_CBC_SHA

I am not the only person who has noticed this: http://blog.ralish.net/2014/05/03/outlook-for-mac-2011-xp-edition/


The “strongest” cipher suite listed here is TLS_RSA_WITH_3DES_EDE_CBC_SHA (3DES-SHA in OpenSSL-speak), which is considered “weak” but not broken (yet). The rest are considered broken (Export ciphers, RC4, MD5, SSLv2?!), and none are recommended by security best practices documents (eg, https://www.ssllabs.com/downloads/SSL_TLS_Deployment_Best_Practices.pdf). It also could indicate that Outlook 2011 and Outlook for Mac may be susceptible to some of the many weaknesses found in SSL/TLS protocols in the past several years (eg, BEAST, POODLE, etc.).

Are there any plans to add support for modern cipher suites to Outlook for Mac? Eg, AES, ECDHE, and ECDSA cipher suites, as well as the newer cipher suites provided by TLSv1.2, such as ones that provide AES-GCM and SHA256 or SHA384 support.
