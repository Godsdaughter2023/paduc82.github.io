xor lx say:

Hi.

Can I ask why the insistence on using AVERAGEIFS, and not, for example, an array formula construction involving AVERAGE and IF?

Agreed, the in-built AVERAGEIF(S) functions are, in general, more efficient than the equivalent array version, though that improvement in terms of efficiency is arguably offset - and certainly here - by the near-tortuous lengths to which you seem to be going in order to derive your "universal" set-up using AVERAGEIFS. 

As you have seen, the peculiarities of the COUNTIF(S)/SUMIF(S)/AVERAGEIF(S) family of functions do not lend themselves well to queries involving mixed datatypes. However, this is not to say that all functions suffer as such. For example, whereas, to count the number of cells within the range A1:A10 as to whether they are non-blank (and by that I mean contain any character other than the null string ""), with COUNTIF we would have to use something like:

=SUM(COUNTIF(A1:A10,{">=0","<0","?*"}))

(whose three criteria account for, respectively, non-negative numericals, negative numericals and text entries).

with e.g. SUMPRODUCT we have simply:

=SUMPRODUCT(N(LEN(A1:A10)>0))

Neither of these will count "genuinely" blank cells, nor those containing the null string "", though of course these criteria can be added, and we're clearly already light-years ahead in terms of lack of convolutedness using SUMPRODUCT.
