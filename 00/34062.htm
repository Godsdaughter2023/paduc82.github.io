tryx3 say:

Those keys are controlled by a utility put on by the PC maker and are not part of Windows 7 itself.  On a Dell, just for example, the utility is called QuickSet. 

 

These controls are often accessible in the Windows Mobility Center [a Windows 7 component that is customised by the PC maker].  Press Ctrl-x to open that item and see if there is a control there.

 

Alternatively, look in your Start menu, All programs list for folders with Samsung's name & see if there is a utility for controlling the keys in there.

 

I imagine that there is also guidance in the PC manual & on Samsung's website.

 

Finally, search this forum for volume goes down and you'll find several similar questions that might assist you.

pedrosoffia say: well, thanks for your post, but I still cant solve the volume control problem; see, as you said, this aparently isn´t a windows problem, and that might be why I havent been able to disable the volume keys using the windows control pannel, or reinstalling the realteck drivers.

tryx3 say:

No, there isn't a way for Windows 7 to do this unless you can identify the utility that is controlling the keys so that you can tell Windows 7 what it is that you are trying to disable. 

 

Try looking in MSConfig for anything with the Samsung name that is loaded during startup - but if you are not familiar with this item then just use this as a source of names and don't try to alter anything. 

You can really mess your PC up by altering things in MSConfig.  Then search for these names on the Samsung website.

 

Go to the Start button, search box, enter MSConfig then right click on the msconfig.exe shortcut that it offered to you & select Run as Admin.  Click on the Startup tab and look through the list there.  Make your notes & close MSConfig by clicking on the Cancel button.

Samsung's utility is what makes those keys do what Samsung has decided they should do.  Therefore, it's Samsung's utility that needs to be identified if those keys are what are causing the problem.
