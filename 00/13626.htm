robert__p say:

Hi NLL,

Thanks for the updates.

I have reviewed the whole thread and understood you want the shared mailbox to show up under your account settings.

Regarding the question “to assign 'full access', do you mean select the permission level to OWNER?”, the answer is no. To assign full access permission, go to Exchange admin center (https://outlook.office.com/ecp/) with your Office 365 admin account. Navigate to recipients – Shared, double click the shared mailbox, click mailbox delegation and make sure your account is added there.

I’m not quite sure about your requirement that you want the shared mailbox to show up under account settings. Since your initial issue is “I found out that the shared mailboxes in my outlook would not update to show all the emails.”. Since the shared mailbox automatically shows in the Outlook 2016 client, I would like to confirm whether all the emails are updated normally. If the emails are updated normally and you do not have special needs for the shared mailbox to show up under the account settings, I suggest you ignore it, as the shared mailbox will be duplicate in Outlook after manually and automatically added.

As for your question “Is this because I am the administrator?”, the answer is no, all users with full access of a shared mailbox can add it under account settings. If you want to continue troubleshooting the issue, please run the Microsoft Support and Recovery Assistant for Office 365. After that, manually add the shared mailbox as Alan suggested to test the issue.

Thanks for your time and efforts.

Regards,
