Bing Merchant Catalog Feed

I am using "mobile_link" column in my Catalog feed. I want to show that I have a different landing page URL for Mobile and for Non-Mobile devices. For that I am using the following URL format in the "bingads_redirect" column. Is that format correct? Will anyone clear the air on this?

"https://{IfMobile:amp.wholesalefashionshoes.com/c/products/102l002-beige-womans-flat?utm_source=bing&utm_medium=ppc&utm_content=Ladies%27+Wholesale+Beige+Bow+Loafer+Slide+On+Wedge+Flat+Size+6&utm_campaign={_mycampaign}}{IfNotMobile:www.wholesalefashionshoes.com/products/102l002-beige-womans-flat?variantid=43399775309&utm_source=bing&utm_medium=ppc&utm_content=Ladies%27+Wholesale+Beige+Bow+Loafer+Slide+On+Wedge+Flat+Size+6&utm_campaign={_mycampaign}}"

bingadselyse say:

Hi Manasi_Dev,

Thank you for your question, I am happy to assist!

Distinguishing Mobile Device Landing Pages from Non-Mobile Device Landing Pages In Your Catalog Feed:

  • The example of the format you previously provided is correct however, you do not need to use the “bingads_redirect” column.
  • To separate your mobile device landing page URL and your non-mobile device landing page URL, you need to have two separate columns in your feed, one being a "Link" column and the other being a "Mobile_link" column. In the “Link” column, you will place your regular non-mobile landing page URL. In the “Mobile_link” column you will place the mobile landing page URL. The device used will determine which URL is triggered.
  • The only time you would need to use the “bingads_redirect” column is when your feed is managed by a third party website, such as Shopify.

For more information on catalog feeds, here is a link to our How Is The Feed File Organized Article on our Bing Ads Help Site.

I hope you find this helpful! Please let me know if you have any additional questions.
