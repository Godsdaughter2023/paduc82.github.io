How can I have a footer only in last page?

jay freedman say:

If you create the field properly, it will update automatically. The syntax of the field is

   { If { page} = { numpages } "This is the last-page text" "" }

where each pair of brackets is created by selecting the code and pressing Ctrl+F9.

This will work properly only if the document doesn't contain any page numbering restarts (that is, sections in which you change the number of the first page). If there are restarts, there's a
different scheme involving a bookmark on the last page and replacing the {numpages} field with a pageref field.

Jay Freedman

Rich Text and a Protected Form - Microsoft Community

margaretwasilisin say:

Rich Text and a Protected Form

I  have a form and the drop-down boxes work great, but the Rich Text cannot be edited after protecting the form.  How can I allow people to add there information in the selected areas (Rich Text)?
jay freedman say:
You need to use content controls for both rich text and dropdown lists -- which you may already be doing (if you didn't use anything from the Legacy Tools button on the Developer tab). When you do that, then DO NOT "protect the document for forms" -- that kind of protection is needed only for legacy form fields, and has some bad interactions with content controls.

Instead, select the whole document (or at least the part that needs to be protected), click the Group button on the Developer tab, and select Group in the menu.

That behaves a lot like forms protection, in that users can't edit or format anything that isn't inside a content control. On the positive side, it doesn't disable spell checking, and it doesn't disable insertion of graphics and other objects in unprotected areas, the way forms protection does.

graham mayor say:
There is no issue - it is by design. Content controls should not reside in a protected area of the document. If you want a protected form, use legacy form fields instead of content controls (and don't mix them with content controls in the same document).

ledgem say:

I just ran into this issue and resolved it.  Many of the posts on here give directions that contradict what's in the manual.  They didn't work for me, but the manual did.

 

Page 14 of the manual (which you should be able to find online) has instructions on resetting the headset in case it stops working.  This is what is written in the manual:

 


To clear the volume and pairing settings from the headset, deactivate
Bluetooth connectivity, and press and hold the multifunction key and
the volume up key (for about 5 seconds) until the Bluetooth indicator
light starts to alternate between red and green.

 

To reset the headset if it stops functioning, even though it is charged,
press the volume down key, the multifunction key, and the rewind key.
The reset does not clear the settings.

 

A reset is what we're going for.

 

Hold down the "multifunction key" (this is the part you hold down to power on the device - the top part of the right earphone's primary button, not the play/pause button), the "volume down" key (if you can't remember which part of the volume rocker was volume down, it's the part of the volume rocker that is lower down, closer to the headphones jack), and the rewind button (this is the key with the "|<<" symbol that is closer to the volume keys).  The manual doesn't indicate if they all need to be pressed down at exactly the same time (as opposed to pressing and holding one, then pressing another, then another...), or how long they need to be held down for.  I pressed them all in unison as best I could, and held them down for about 10 seconds.

 

In my case, something weird happened.  Even though the headset powered up, upon trying to play music through them, they seemingly shut down and wouldn't power on again.  I had to reset them a second time.  At that point I deleted the paired profile with my computer and re-paired my computer and the headphones.  Now they seem to be working fine again.  What a relief!

 

Best of luck to anyone else who is going through this unpleasant experience.

MERGE SECTION - Microsoft Community

alipasha_gh say:

MERGE SECTION

I have many section in my word file for using mail merge. i cant use page number in my document for that i want to have only one section. how can i would be appreciate help me how can i  merge my sections to one section for using page number in file.

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

The execution of a letter type mail merge will result in the creation of a document having a Section Break between the "letter" containing the data for each record.

By default, the Section Break that is created by executing the merge, results in a Section in which the page numbers are formatted to begin at 1 rather than continue from the previous section.

If your mail merge main document has multiple sections, when you insert those Section Breaks, byu default, the page numbering in the new section will be formatted to continue from that in the previous section.  So, you can still have page numbers in the document and when it is merged, as mentioned above, the page numbers will restart at 1 for each record. 

 

If this explanation does not cover your issue, please explain it in more detail.
