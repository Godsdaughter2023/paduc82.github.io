SUM Formula returns (0.00), not '-' for zero SUM - Microsoft Community

firstvette say:

SUM Formula returns (0.00), not '-' for zero SUM

Why does the SUM Function displays (0.00) in Comma Style, not a dash, when the SUM range exceeds 4 cells. When I change the format of the SUM cell, it displays an negative empirical number. When I increase the Decimal places of the cell, it displays a negative number in the billionths.
ronrosenfeld say:

On Fri, 20 Aug 2010 16:12:11 +0000, FirstVette wrote:

Why does the SUM Function displays (0.00) in Comma Style, not a dash, when the SUM range exceeds 4 cells. When I change the format of the SUM cell, it displays an negative empirical number. When I increase the Decimal places of the cell, it displays a negative number in the billionths.

Most likely, what is happening, is that your SUM is not exactly zero
so far as Excel is concerned.  This is usually do to the impossibility
of representing certain values in the binary system (much like the
fraction 1/3 cannot be represented exactly in the decimal system).
Excel's algorithms do not always result in an actual zero.

Several ways to workaround this.
    1.  Round your result to the desired level of precision.
    2.  Test for a small difference between your SUM result and 0,
and if present, output a zero.

e.g:

=((ABS(SUM(cell_range))-0)>0.0000000001)*SUM(cell_range)

or

=IF(((ABS(SUM(cell_range))-0)>0.0000000001),SUM(cell_range),0)
