ellisdon say: I've found a satisfactory solution that gives mp3 files of equivalent quality to 64kbps m4a/aac for the minimum size of mp3 file (approx 35% bigger than the m4a files). The following process will produce files that show the full track info on the 6680 and (if you have the N90 Music Player installed) will display Album Art that you have added to the files. It is as follows:

1. Download and install winLAME then rip the CDs to mp3 using the 'high' quality setting and '96kbps - ABR' (the following link at Moby Memory gives full details http://www.mobimemory.co.uk/Compression.rates.for.MP3.conversion.3.html ). winLAME produces higher quality tracks than Real Player or iTunes at 96 kbps, although it is slower at ripping.

2. Then use MP3Tag (available at http://www.mp3tag.de/en/download.html ) to convert the ID3v1.0 tag stored by winLAME into ID3v2.3. To do this navigate MP3Tag to the file with the tracks you've just ripped with winLAME, select all of the tracks, right click on them and select 'save tag' (this process is described visually in part of the following help forum http://www.mobimemory.co.uk/HOWTO.Convert.your.iTunes.M4A.files.to.AAC.WITH.TAGS.for.playback.on.Nokia.mobiles.like.Nokia.6630.2014.html ).

3. If you want to add Album Art then (and only then) import the files to Real Player (or iTunes) to add the album cover jpg picture (Amazon is a good source of album cover pictures). If you import the files to Real Player before converting the Tags with MP3Tag the track info won't show up in the N90 Music Player.

I hope the above may be of use to other 6680 owners looking to make the most of the space on their memory card whilst being able to organise their music easily. The N90 Music Player works on the 6680 and can be downloaded from the N90 support section of Nokia website. I believe it's a nicer looking player to the one that comes with the 6680, particularly as it displays Album Art attached to mp3 files.
