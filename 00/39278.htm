midmost say:

After a lot of searching I eventually found a method that worked on a Dell Inspiron 9200 to get it out of extended desktop mode.

1. In Display Settings click on monitor 2 and uncheck Extend my window...

2. Click on monitor 1, then Advanced

3 That brings up the Radeon configuration window. Click on the Displays tab

4 Click on the button at top right corner of the icon for monitor 2. It goes red. Click Apply.

5 An error message appears "Your selected configuration is not available.....". Ignore it. Close it by pressing OK.

6 Click on the top right corner of monitor 2 again to turn off the red button. Click Apply again.

7 Step back in amazement. It's back to clone mode.  Just OK to all windows to close them.

midmost say: I doubt that it is too old for clone mode. Cloning was around well before Extended desktop.
I do sympathise with you though if you cannot find how to change back to Cloned mode (also variously called Mirrored or Duplicated) . The problem seems to persist even in Windows 7 where the Display settings dialog is able to switch into extended desktop by checking Extend my desktop.. but is not able to switch back to clone by unchecking it. It is always necessary to open the Graphics chip manufacturers own driver dialog and it is often not clear what you do then. I can only give you general hints based on having done it on a couple more computers since I wrote the earlier post.

Your first step will be to find the tab that displays icons of the monitors. It may not be called the same as for other graphics cards but there will almost certainly be one somewhere.
As far as I can make out it is usually first necessary to turn off monitor 2 because the settings cannot be changed out of Extended desktop until you have done so. If there is no button or checkbox try right clicking on the monitor 2 icon to see if there is a context menu with the option.
You then have to find some way to tell monitor I to delete its setting for Extended desktop mode. Otherwise you will be straight back into it as soon as you turn monitor 2 back on again.
Again unless it is obvious how to do it try right clicking on the monitor 1 icon. It often takes a lot of persistence before you stumble upon the way to do it.

While you are searching around look out for anywhere that allows you to save the configuration  if  you do succeed in setting it back to clone mode. You can often assign a keyboard shortcut to the saved configuration and it makes it so much easier to restore it next time.

Good luck.

1. Check your speaker settings:
Right-click the sound icon at the bottom right corner of your desktop and click Playback devices.
In the Playback tab, ensure there’s a green tick on Speakers. This indicates that your speakers are the default playback device.
If your Speakers are NOT already set as the default, this is doubtless your problem. To fix, just click them, click Set Default, then click OK and test whether you have sound.
If your Speakers are set as the default playback device, and you’re still not hearing any sound, you should to do this >> click Speakers then Properties >> Go to the Advanced tab, and in the Default Format section, select a different sample rate and bit depth, then click Test. On your keyboard, press the Windows logo key and X (at the same time) to invoke the quick-access menu, then Click Device Manager.
In Device Manager, expand Sound, video and game controllers,
right-click on your sound card, and click Uninstall.
Restart your computer and check to see if the problem is resolved.

3. If these steps didn't fix the issue, I suggest to Up Date the driver from the manufacture web site.
