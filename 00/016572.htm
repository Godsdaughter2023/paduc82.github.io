I'm guessing that you are speaking of one of these three designations for the Indian rupee. Most likely the middle one.

 If you are speaking of the symbol or sign in the middle of the three, it is important to note that this was selected by the Indian government on July 15, 2010 from the entries in a contest started on March 5, 2009. The Indian government plans to adopt it within 6 months and would like it globally accepted in 18-24 months from the date of selection.

I would suppose the first thing you will require is a font that has this symbol as one of the characters. Up until now, I've only see images of the symbol used to float with the currency text they are denoting. I would imagine an extended character in a font set will be produced first.

Once you have a font installed in Windows, you can start using the symbol in a more conventional context through its use in the AutoCorrect or AutoText features of Word and Excel. This would facilitate typing in something like INR and having it replaced by the new Indian rupee symbol.

As far as incorporating it into the regional currency settings of Windows, you may have to wait a bit. The Indian government itself is planning for global implementation only within the next 15-21 months.

rich michaels - mvp (greatcirclelearning.com) say:

Hi Christian,

I think you might have multiple issues going with how this document is setup.

The first thing you should do is turn on your non-printing characters so that you can see the paragraph marks. Usually if you insert Equations using the Insert > Equations function of Word they get insert directly onto their own paragraph and the odd behavior you describe of them moving around would suggest they are not inserted in the normal manner. Showing the paragraph marks will reveal that.

The other thing that you should display on your screen is the Styles pane, which you can activate by going to the View menu and locating Styles as part of the Toolbox. This can tell you what exactly is the paragraph style for those that seem to shift left and right. You do that by clicking in the paragraph and looking at the top of the Style Pane, which will show you the styles name.

The screen clips you provide show that your are using a numbered list but the list is not pure because not all paragraphs within the list are numbered. That's kind of okay... but the "kind of" qualifier means it depends on exactly what kind of list this is and how you actually set it up. Looking at it, I would say you created a multi-level list, which is the more complicated and sensitive of the two kinds, to adding and removing paragraphs that are inside the scope of the list.

Using the Styles pane and checking the paragraphs of text and equations that are in between each numbered paragraph you need to make sure that non of them indicate they are list paragraphs. If they are, that is one of the problems. You may have removed the number but not the style and the paragraph continue to behave as a list paragraph as new paragraphs are added or removed. Also check all paragraph styles and make sure non of them have the Automatically Update setting turned on. Hopefully you know how to check for that by indicating on the Stye pane that you want to Modify the style and then looking at the Modify Styles dialog and see if that settings is turned on. If it is then turn it off and if this style is part of Normal template also mark the button to update the template.

Do these things and let us know what you find. Also if you could upload a partial copy of this document to a cloud storage location and share a link to it here. Some of us will download it and take a look and it will help facilitate further discussion.

As I said at the beginning, I think you might have multiple issues going on and since none of us know your knowledge level of using Word, it's difficult to guess how much direction we should provide in the "how to" category of using certain advanced features of Word such as Styles and Multilevel list manipulation.
