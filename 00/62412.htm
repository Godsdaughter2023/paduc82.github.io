internet expert say:

The best video signal you’ll get out of your pre-2007 Xbox 360 is 1080i over component.

The AV cable on your Xbox 360 should have six RCA plugs coming out of it: component (red, green, blue) and composite (yellow for video, red and white for stereo audio). To use the component connection, you would have to flip the switch to “HDTV” (on the Xbox 360 AV connector), then plug the component and audio RCA plugs into the TV, leaving the yellow one out.

I know a lot of 4K TVs nowadays offer component and composite video support, but there’s not enough RCA inputs so you’d have to plug it into a shared RCA jack. (e.g. my Samsung 4K has 5 RCA jacks for component and stereo audio, but to use composite video, you would plug the yellow connector into the green component jack). Check your TV manual to see how these connections are achieved, if this is the case.

If the HDTV setting is not working, then there may be a problem with your AV connector. There are many replacements available online (I’m seeing around $6-7 on ebay when you search “xbox 360 av cable”).

As for getting this on HDMI, I would not bother with all the trouble. Considering the money you’re spending on the equipment to convert, you’d may as well hit up a used games shop and find yourself a Xbox 360 console with HDMI for maybe $20-40. Or, if you have an Xbox One, check if your favorite games are on the Backward Compatibility list, play them on that, and you’ll already be playing in HD.
