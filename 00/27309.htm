I suggest that you check and modify your Junk mail filters for us to make sure that it is not contributing to this issue:

Check your Junk folder
1. Sign in to Outlook.com.
2. Click the "Junk" folder on the left pane.
3. Check to see if the wanted emails are in this folder.
4. If you see any messages that should have been delivered to the Inbox folder, click the box on the left of each message to put a check mark and then click the "Not Junk" button.

Check your Blocked sender list
1. In your Outlook.com Inbox, click the cog icon beside your Profile name.
2. Click "More mail settings".
3. Under Preventing junk email, click "Safe and blocked senders".
4. Click "Blocked senders".
5. In the Blocked senders list, select the name or domain (the part of an e-mail address that follows the @ sign, such as hotmail.com) that you now want to receive mail from, and then click "<< Remove from list".

Add the email address to your Safe senders list
1. In your Outlook.com Inbox, click the cog icon beside your Profile name.
2. Click "More mail settings".
3. Under Preventing junk email, click "Safe and blocked senders".
4. Click "Safe Senders".
5. In the text box, type in the email address or domain you’d like to always receive email from, and then click "Add to list >>".

Check your Email Rules
1. In your Outlook.com Inbox, click the cog icon beside your Profile name.
2. Click "More mail settings".
3. Under Customizing Outlook, click "Rules for sorting new messages".
4. Perform one of the actions as per your requirement:
Click "Edit" next to the rule that you want to edit.
Or
Choose the rule that you want to delete by ticking the check box and click "Delete".

Note: Not all international characters are supported as filters.

Check your Junk Mail Settings
1. In your Outlook.com Inbox, click the cog icon beside your Profile name.
2. Click "More mail settings".
3. Under Preventing junk email, click "Filters and reporting".
4. Under Choose a junk e-mail filter, choose the level of protection that you want, and then click "Save". If the Exclusive level was set, messages from senders not on your Contacts or Safe senders lists will have been sent to the Junk e-mail folder.

Check your Reply-to address
1. In your Outlook.com Inbox, click the cog icon beside your Profile name.
2. Click "More mail settings".
3. Under Writing email, click "Reply-to address".
2. Select "Current e-mail address" radio button.
3. Click "Save".

Check Email Forwarding
1. In your Outlook.com Inbox, click the cog icon beside your Profile name.
2. Click "More mail settings".
3. Under Managing your account, click "Email Forwarding".
4. Choose Don't forward, if you have enabled email forwarding for you to receive incoming emails to your Inbox Folder.
5. Click "Save".

Once done, kindly check if you can already receive incoming emails. Get back to us with the results.

timliu_msft1261 say:

Hi Andrew,

I'd like to explain that the storage for a tenant is 10 GB + 0.5 GB per subscribed user + additional storage purchased. The 1 TB for users is personal site storage. It applies to a user’s OneDrive for Business library and personal newsfeed. This amount is counted separately, and does not add to or subtract the overall storage allocation for a tenant.

Uploading videos to the video channel or team site use the storage of tenant storage. If it reach the limitation (the storage of your tenant), we need to purchase more storage.
Note: Uploading files to the group library (diplay in Outlook, OWA, personal OneDrive for Business site) also takes the storage of tenant storage.

If we upload videos to OneDrive for Business library, it takes the storage of personal OneDrive for Business site storage. The limitation is 1 TB.

To learn more about the storage management in SharePoint Online, see SharePoint Online simplifies storage management.

Best regards,
Tim

Formula for checking if NOT a date - Microsoft Community

a. user say:

Formula for checking if NOT a date

Hello!

I have a table with multiple columns of dates that I pulled from another workbook. When I pulled them over to my new workbook, some of the date formulas did not transfer over so they returned #REF! or #### or just NA.

Is there a function I can write that does the following?

-If a cell is NOT in date format, delete its contents

I currently have a macro written that selects all data, removes BLANKS, and shifts cells up, and converts all remaining cells to the date format type. If I could get rid of the non-dates before doing the rest of the stuff that would be really helpful!

Thanks!

ossiemac say:

Hello again Sydney,

There are 2 code examples below. The first one just clears the cell with non date and the second actually deletes the cell and moves the data up.

Note that when deleting data and moving the data up that it is necessary to work from the bottom up and hence the use of row numbers in the loop in lieu of the For Each method.

I have included a MsgBox for testing but see the comments where you can delete (or comment out) the MsgBox and uncomment the line to remove the non date.

The first sub tests for a date and the second tests for not a date. I am sure that you will work out that either method can be used and I just changed the second example code to provide both methods.

 Where you are seeing #### might simply be that the column is not wide enough to display the date in the format specified.

Sub TestForDates()
    'Note that this method cannot be used if deleting rather than clearing the cell
    Dim rngDates As Range
    Dim rngCel As Range
   
    With Worksheets("Sheet1")
        'Following assigns from row 2 in column A to last used cell in column A to the variable
        'Starting from 2 assumes that row 1 has column headers
        Set rngDates = .Range(.Cells(2, "A"), .Cells(.Rows.Count, "A").End(xlUp))
    End With
   
    For Each rngCel In rngDates
        If IsDate(rngCel) Then
            rngCel.NumberFormat = "dd mmm yyyy"     'Edit format to your requirement
        Else
            MsgBox rngCel.Address & " is not a date"    'For testing. Delete and use next row
            'rngCel.ClearContents       'Can replace MsgBox line with this line
        End If
    Next rngCel
   
End Sub

Sub TestForNonDate()

    'Alternative code to test for not a date and delete cell rather than clear contents
   
    Dim lngLastRow
    Dim r As Long
   
    With Worksheets("Sheet1")
        'Following assigns last used cell ro in column A to the variable
        lngLastRow = .Cells(.Rows.Count, "A").End(xlUp).Row
   
        'Must work backwards from bottom if deleting cells
        For r = lngLastRow To 2 Step -1     'To 2 assumes row 1 has column headers
            If Not IsDate(.Cells(r, "A")) Then
                MsgBox .Cells(r, "A").Address & " is not a date"    'For testing. Delete and use next row
                '.Cells(r, "A").Delete       'Deletes the cell and moves data up
            Else
                .Cells(r, "A").NumberFormat = "dd mmm yyyy"     'Edit format to your requirement
            End If
        Next r
    End With

End Sub
