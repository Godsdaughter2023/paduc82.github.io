cmdrkeene say:

Turning a computer off when you aren't using it is identical on the hardware as hibernating, it doesn't even know the difference.  So it's not any better or worse.  Sleep is less "off" than hibernate (sleep keeps the memory powered), so it is technically 'softer'.  Hibernate is like turning off a car.  Turning it off is like turning it off and taking the key out (basically doesn't do anything different).  Sleep is like leaving it idling.

It generally won't affect the life of a computer in any measurable way to use one method over the other.  It would be much more impactful for you to just consider how much power it uses if left on and unused.  So I say keep doing what you do: hibernate or power off.

Incidentally, in Windows 8 and higher, doing a shutdown is actually a hibernate, you just don't know it.  They do that to speed up the next boot-up sequence by not having to load every file.  Basically in Windows 8 onward, shutdown is really more like "log off then hibernate".  A restart actually does a full boot sequence.  But I digress...

To find out what causes it to wake back up after you put it to hibernate or sleep, do this:

  • Immediately after it "comes back awake" by itself, right-click the Start button and choose Command Prompt Admin.
  • Type powercfg /lastwake and it will tell you what caused the wakeup.
