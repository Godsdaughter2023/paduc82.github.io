lemp say:
If -- and probably only if -- a program's installer includes an option to select an installation location, you can install it in a folder in your account that does not include privileges for other users.

However, consider that a "computer administrator" has access to everything on the computer, so you can't hide or prevent access by other members of the Administrators group.  You can -- if your personal relationships permit -- "demote" the other users to "Power user" status. 

Although in previous versions of Windows, members of the "Power users" group had almost the same level of permissions as "Administrators" except permission to add themselves to the Administrators group or access the data of other users on an NTFS volume unless those users grant them permission, that's no longer true (by default) in Windows 7.  In Windows 7, "Power users" are no different from ordinary users.  See http://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/cc771990.aspx

As a member of the Administrators group, you can, however, add whatever permissions you like to the Power Users group.  How to add permissions, including which permissions you might want to add and which you might not, is beyond the scope of a response in this forum.  You'll have to do this research on your own.
