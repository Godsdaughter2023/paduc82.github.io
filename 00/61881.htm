But if your Windows edition is a retail you can use that license to activate Windows 10 in the new PC.
https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/help/20530/...

Also contact Microsoft Support for activation
https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/contactus/ (click on talk to a person)

Phone numbers by region

If Windows 10 came preinstalled on your computer
you can see your product key doing this:
Press Windows key + X
Click Command Prompt (as an admin)
Type this command:
wmic path SoftwareLicensingService get OA3xOriginalProductKey
Click Enter

You will get the product key
How to enter it:

Click Start, go to Settings go to Update & security click Activation click Change the product key then enter the new product key then click Next to Activate

When Windows is pre-installed then it is the manufacturer's responsibility to provide a valid product key.

If you are unable to activate Windows 10 you will have to contact Microsoft support

https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/contactus/ (talk to a person)

Hi Chris. I"m Greg, an installation specialist, 9 year Windows MVP and Independent Advisor, here to help you.

As long as the underlying license you upgraded or that came with WIndows 10 is retail and not factory preinstalled or builder's OEM, then it can transfer since Upgrade inherits the rights and restrictions.

But did you buy the key from Microsoft or a major retailer who backs their sales so you know it's genuine? Any discount key is most likely bogus. See here How to Tell: https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/howtotell/defau...

The procedure is here: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/help/20530/...

It is possible to do yourself if you'd previously signed in with your Microsoft account and it linked to the Digital License as shown in Status at top of the Activation Settings page. Then when you change motherboard, you'd sign in again and run the Troubleshooter. We recently learned it will even make exceptions for OEM if all else is proper. So that's your best bet.

If not contact Microsoft Support: https://partner.support.services.microsoft.com/...

I hope this helps. Feel free to ask back any questions and let us know how it goes. I will keep working with you until it's resolved.
