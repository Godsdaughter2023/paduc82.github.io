macropod say:

Hi,

The simple solution is to add a SKIPIF field to the Word mailmerge main document, coded along the lines of:

{SKIPIF{MERGEFIELD Rule}= 0}
or
{SKIPIF«Rule»= 0}
where 'Rule' is the name of the field containing the 1s and 0s and '0' represents the records to be skipped.

Note: The field brace pairs (ie '{ }') for the above example are created via Ctrl-F9 - you can't simply type them or copy & paste them from this message. Likewise, you can't type or copy & paste the chevrons (ie '« »') - they're part of the actual mergefields, which you can insert from the mailmerge toolbar.

jim gordon mvp say:

Yep. That's how it works.

Just to clarify the terminology for you:

Office 365 is a subscription program that you use to rent Microsoft products. It includes the ability to use Microsoft Office 2106 while your subscription is active.

When you open a Microsoft Office app, the app checks with Microsoft servers to see if your subscription is still active. In order to pass the check, you must sign in.

Your apps will work for a limited time if you sign in and then sign out, but after a while they will try to check back with Microsoft servers again to see if your subscription is still alive.

Even if you purchase a permanent license instead of an Office 365 subscription, Office apps check to make sure they're not pirate copies.

I recommend that you explore the options you get when you sign in as administrator of your Office 365 account. There's a lot of great stuff besides Microsoft Office. You paid for it, so you might as well take advantage and see what's available to you.
