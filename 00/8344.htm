a. user say:

Hi Hans_M1209,

 

If you want to disable calendar notifications on your phone, follow these steps:

  1. Login to https://calendar.live.com
  2. From the Calendar homepage, click Options at the upper-right corner.
  3. Once on the Calendar options page, look for the category Turn notifications on or off, then uncheck the box next to Get notifications (reminders, changes to calendars, and daily summaries).
  4. Click Save.
  5. You will then be redirected to your Calendar homepage. From the left panel, click the Birthday calendar.
  6. Once on the Calendar settings for "Birthday calendar" page, look for the Notifications category, then check if the option Calendars on your PC and mobile phone is enabled. If yes, tick the X mark next to the time duration.
  7. Click Save.

The error "Microsoft Account does not exist" usually means:

The person is logging in using an Alias. If the user renamed the affected email address into a new account, the original account will become an Alias. Thus logging in using the original account (Alias) will trigger the system to prompt the user that it does not exist. The solution is to log in using the new account.

 The person mistyped the email address. Retyped the correct email address.

 The account was not accessed  (WITH A BROWSER) within  270 days. The account will be deleted permanently in the system due to inactivity. You may be able to re-create the account as a new account if it is still available but the data will be gone.  If you cannot it is because it has not yet cleared the security hold (90 days)

Update:  this also applies to linked accounts.  Your account may have been unlinked and therefore shows as does not exist.
