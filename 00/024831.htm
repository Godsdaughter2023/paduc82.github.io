xjustiinsane say: Hi All.

This is really easy, even with a free Gmail account.
No Sync tool is available, just a small work around.

Go to your Google account and open up the settings in the Gear menu.

In there, you'll see Calendars. Click on it and choose the calendar you want to sync into Outlook (just click on the name).

In the bottom, search for Private address and click on the ICAL button.

A small pop-up will be shown with an URL to the Calendar.
Copy the address and open up you Outlook (2010, 2013, etc.).

In Outlook 2010, go to your account settings and open the 'Internet Calendars' tab.

Click on new, and paste the before copied Google ICAL calendar URL in there.
Press add and you'll be fine.

Voila! Open your calender and you'll see your Gmail calendar in there.

(Edit: THIS IS VIEW ONLY. You cannot edit appointments via Outlook. Back to research).

=ROUND(D17/E17,2)

joeu2004 say: oxicottin wrote:
Hello, if I have text in (D17) it gives me an #VALUE! error in the following formulas. How can I let the formulas know if there is text then treat it as there isn't anything there or as zero. Thanks!
=ROUND(D17/E17,2)
=D17-F20

=IF(N(E17)=0,0,ROUND(N(D17)/N(E17),2))

=N(D17)-N(F20)

The N() function returns zero if the cell contains text.  N() returns the value of the cell if it is numeric.

text "120", =D17-F20 returns the numeric value 3, but =N(D17)-N(F20) returns zero.  If that is a concern, Ronaldo's IFERROR formulas might be better for you.  But note that IFERROR might inadvertently mask other errors that should be corrected.
