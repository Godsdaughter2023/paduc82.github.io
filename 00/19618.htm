Calculating Response Times - Microsoft Community

dlt10960 say:

Calculating Response Times

I need a formula to calculate response times for responding units.  Example:  A call comes in at 23:43:46.  Units arrive on scene at 23:55:52.  Units clear the scene at 01:34:45.  How much time between when the call came in and units arrived on scene?  How much time between when units arrived on scene and cleared scene.  I also need to include a formular for when a call begins before midnight and ends after midnight.  Thank you...

benishiryo say: hi DLT10960,

it's always good to have dates & times together.  Excel recognises dates as integers & time as decimals.  to verify, type in 27feb2013 in A1.  and 12 pm in B1.  Format both cells to General.  you will see that A1 is 41332 (meaning 41,332nd day from 1 Jan 1900) & B1 is 0.5 (meaning half a day).  but now that you know Date is an integer, what happens when you take time on its on & format to a Date Time format?  it will be 0 Jan 1900 12 pm.  so when you take your case of 01:34:45 minus 23:55:52, you are essentially taking a smaller decimal (0.066) minus a bigger decimal (0.99713).  and Excel doesnt allow that.  

so if you could, add date + time.  if you cant, then this formula would work if it's only 1 day apart.
assuming your 3 response timings are in A1, B1 & C1; try:
=MOD(B1-A1,1)
=MOD(C1-B1,1)

joeu2004 say: DLT wrote:
I need a formula to calculate response times for responding units.  Example:  A call comes in at 23:43:46.  Units arrive on scene at 23:55:52.  Units clear the scene at 01:34:45.  How much time between when the call came in and units arrived on scene?  How much time between when units arrived on scene and cleared scene.  I also need to include a formular for when a call begins before midnight and ends after midnight.

If the first cell is A1 (23:43:46), the cell is A2 (23:55:52) and the third cell is A3 (01:34:45), calculate as follows:

1st difference:  =A2-A1+(A2<A1)

2nd difference:  =A3-A2+(A3<A2)

what do you suggest?

young yang msft say:

Hi Vivek,

Upon analysis, this process will not work.

The existing Office 365 user *** Email address is removed for privacy *** is synced with the corresponding AD user *** Email address is removed for privacy ***. The Office 365 user’s ImmutableID attribute is populated with on-premsie ObjectGuid attribute. This is the primary key linking the on-premises user with Office 365 user. For another AD user *** Email address is removed for privacy ***, the ObjectGuid value is different. So the existing Office user *** Email address is removed for privacy *** will not be matched to the AD user *** Email address is removed for privacy ***, via soft match/smtp match. SMTP matching can be used only one time for user accounts that were originally authored by using Office 365 management tools.

Moreover, you said you “will modify *** Email address is removed for privacy *** to *** Email address is removed for privacy ***”. As far as I know, this is not feasible in on-premise AD. It will cause attribute duplicate, because another user *** Email address is removed for privacy *** already exists in on-premise AD.

We have another method called hard match. This method modifies Office 365 user’s ImmutableID, so that the Office 365 user can be matched to another object. For detailed information, please see: https://blogs.technet.microsoft.com/praveenkumar/2014/04/11/how-to-do-hard-match-in-dirsync/. If you perform a hard match, Office user’s User Principal Name (UPN) and email address will be changed to *** Email address is removed for privacy ***.
