richardpullan say:

Solved it for me!

Fortunately I was able to remote desktop into my PC  from my laptop.

Problem was with a component ASUS AI Suite II - It was ASUS AI Charger Plus. This controls the placement of an additional driver into the USB Host Controllers which is not supported in Win 10. The simplest thing to do was to remove this A1 Charger+ driver. This can be done by selecting ASUS AI Suite II from the Apps and features and clicking on it and selecting uninstall. A list of components will appear, put a cross against AI Charger and select uninstall. On reboot all my USB ports worked as normal.

a. user say:

Hi,

 

Thank you for posting on Microsoft Community.

 

This could be because of Drive letter alignment.

 

You may try to change the drive letter for your external drive and restart the system and check whether it shows the free space.

To change the drive letter, follow the steps below.

  1. First connect your USB external hard drive to the computer.
  2. Click on Start button, right click on Computer and go to Manage.
  3. Click on Disk management.
  4. Right click on external drive x (x is your external hard drive letter).
  5. Now, click on “Change drive letter and paths”.
  6. Click on “change” and assign different drive letter.
  7. Click on “Yes” when prompted.
  8. Click on OK.

Restart the system and check whether you can see the free space.
