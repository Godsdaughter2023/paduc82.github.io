bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

I don't understand the first question... By definition, gutters are additions to the inside margins to allow for binding when using facing pages [Mirror Margins]. IOW, binding isn't done on outside edges. This is typically for documents that will be printed on both sides of the paper then bound in some way. It sounds  to me like what you want to accomplish simply amounts to making the Outside Margin greater than the Inside Margin.

Another possibility is that it is a single-sided print job & you want to have a left gutter to allow for binding in which case you do not want to check the box for Mirror Margins. This can also be done by making the Left Margin greater than the Right Margin.

Other possibilities exist but it depends on exactly how the output is to be laid out.

For your 2nd question: While in Section 2 make sure that the Link to Previous box isn't checked for the Header/Footer (whichever contains the page numbering). Go to Insert> Page Numbers, click the Format button, then set the Start At to 1 (or whatever you prefer).
