This is how Relational databases work. You need at least 2 tables here. You need a Guests table and a Visits tables. So when you check in a Guest you enter their info into the Guests table if they are a first time guest. If they aren't, then you go right to the Checkin form and select them from a combobox. Error "this is not a valid action for footnotes" - Microsoft Community

persadv say:

Error "this is not a valid action for footnotes"

In Word 2007 I have one page with a blank line between two footnotes and using "delete" at the end of the line above or in the blank line results in an error "this is not a valid action for footnotes."  I also tried backspacing from the beginning of the line below the blank line.  I have haad blank lines in footnotes on other pages and delete worked.
guessed say:

persadv

As macropod has advised, it sounds like you are trying to delete the end of the footnote marker which can't be done.

Basically, each footnote ends with what looks like a paragraph marker but it is special (ie can't be deleted). When you create a footnote, the entry starts with a number and ends with one of these special paragraph markers. Inside each footnote you can insert text AND paragraphs (which can be deleted).

In short, the fix for you is NOT to delete the empty paragraph itself, instead you should delete the paragraph marker which ends the paragraph ABOVE that paragraph. The easiest way to do this is to put your cursor at the start of the paragraph you want to delete and press the Backspace key instead of the Delete key.

macropod say:

Click on the ¶ symbol on the Ribbon's Home tab, so you can see the paragraph breaks - they'll look like ¶.

If what you see is:

4[1]StA VI, 105¶
¶

5[1]StA VI, 103¶
¶

6     StA VI, 103¶
¶

delete the ¶ at the end of the numbered lines, till you see just:

4[1]StA VI, 105¶

5[1]StA VI, 103¶

6     StA VI, 103¶

Note: You cannot delete the ¶ immediately before the endnote reference.

If what you see is more like:

4[1]StA VI, 105¶

5[1]StA VI, 103¶

6     StA VI, 103¶

The issue concerns the amount of before/after paragraph spacing and/or the size of the line spacing for the notes. Simply select a note, then right-click and choose Style>Modify>Format>Paragraph and correct the spacing.
