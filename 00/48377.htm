Please do not write in all caps and bold.

You seem to have reset the password for your router's wireless access point.

You need to access the router's web-based configuration utility and enter a new password.

STEP 1

With the Ethernet cable connected between your computer and your router, open a Command Prompt and type the following command, then press Enter

    ipconfig

You should see something like the text between the lines of *****

Tunnel adapter Teredo Tunneling Pseudo-Interface:

   Media State . . . . . . . . . . . : Media disconnected
   Connection-specific DNS Suffix  . :

C:\Users\yourusername>

*******************************************************************************************

Make a note of the IP address of the "Default Gateway"

STEP 2

With the Ethernet cable connected between your computer and your router, open a web browser (e.g., Internet Explorer, Firefox, Chrome, etc.) and type the IP address of the Default Gateway (from Step 1) into the address box and press Enter.  This should bring you to the log in page for your router's web-based configuration utility.

Log in to the router's configuration utility and go to the wireless setup setup section.  Select the security mode (preferably WPA2) and enter a password.  Be sure to write down the password.

If you don't know how to log into your router or get to the wireless security setup, please provide the make, model, and hardware version (if any) shown on the label on the back or bottom of your router.

STEP 3

Open the Network and Sharing Center (click the network icon next to the clock display at the right end of the Task Bar). 

Click on the link to "Manage Wireless Networks"

Delete the entry for your wireless network.

STEP 4

Disconnect the Ethernet cable.

Click the network icon and display the list of available networks.

Find your wireless network in the list, check the box to "Connect automatically" andclick the "Connect" button, and carefully enter the new password you created in Step 2.

No. Several points:

1. An upgrade from XP to Windows 7 or 8 is not possible, You can only
do a clean installation to get there (unless you first upgrade to
Vista and then to 7 or 8).

2. Even if you are going from one version to another where upgrades
are supported (such as XP to Vista) you can never change the "bitness"
of Windows (neither from 32-bit to 84-bit, nor vice-versa) by an
upgrade. Again, a clean installation is required.

3. Are your motherboard and CPU 64-bit? Most XP-era computers are
32-bit, and if yours is 32-bit, you can not install any 64-bit version
of Windows regardless of whether you try an upgrade or a clean
installation.

4. If your hardware is 64-bit, hardware that old is likely to be way
underpowered for acceptable performanc
