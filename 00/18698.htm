jay freedman say:

The suggestion to add NoReReg entries to the registry is legitimate, but it's useful only if you have two or more versions of Office on the same computer and you switch back and forth among them. With only Office 2007, you could add the entry for version 12.0, but that would permanently prevent saving configuration changes that you may want.

It usually turns out that there's a permissions problem in the registry that's preventing Office from writing the value that tells it that configuration is complete. Try logging in as the Administrator of your computer and running Word. Let it do the configuration. After that you should be able to sign out, log in as yourself, and run Word without the configuration.

You didn't say before that you had both Office 2007 and Office 365 on the same computer. That changes the situation a bit.

You can only add the contact inside your organization in Skype for Business for Mac,
