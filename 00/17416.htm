Word is not a page layout application. There are no 'pages' in a Word document, but a series of 'layers' that contribute to the whole. Imagine a pile of transparencies that when stacked produce a picture of the whole. davidf2 say:

First I would make sure that proof publication I sent to the submitter for final approval has the same formatting as my main publication. This includes font, font size, paragraph settings, line spacing etc. In fact it might be to your advantage to send each submitter a formatted template to use for submittal to save time.

 

When you get those approved two page publications back then open that publication and your main publication.

 

Insert two blank pages into your main publication.

 

Go to the first page of the two pages you want to copy and select everything on the page. There are several ways to do that. Ctrl + A is probably the easiest. You can also 'lasso' all the elements on the page by left clicking above and to the left of the page in the scratch area and then drag, draw a rectangle around the entire page by dragging down to the lower right of the page.

 

Group all the elements on the page together. Once again several ways to do this. Home tab > Group (within the Arrange group of options) or just Ctrl + Shift + G is the keystroke shortcut. Grouping the elements together before you copy them will maintain the layout of the page.

 

Copy all the group of elements. Home > Copy. or Ctrl + C.

 

Go to the main publication, a blank page, Paste Special > Publisher Object. or Ctrl + V. The page from your submitted publication should paste on the page and be aligned. If not drag the group around until it is aligned as you want it. Once you have it where you want it, ungroup all the page elements if you want. (Home > Ungroup or Ctrl + Shift + G again)

 

Repeat for the second page.

 

This workflow should work for you but the key will be that the final proof you send to the submitter must be formatted the same as the main publication. If it isn't then the line spacing, fonts, etc can vary when you paste the elements on the blank page.

 

DavidF

geoff budd say:

Hi Max Meng.

I've managed to solve the problem!

I had the preview pane open in Windows Explorer, so when I selected the Excel spreadsheet ready to double-click it to open it, the preview window was showing the spreadsheet.  It would seem that Windows Explorer probably has to launch the appropriate application (Excel in this case) somehow to show the preview and, in doing so, the PERSONAL.XLSB file was presumably opened in the background..

So...when I double-clicked it to open it, it opened, but because PERSONAL.XLSB was already open (in the Windows Explorer "preview" version), then it didn't open it a second time.

It's one of those problems that, when you find the answer and think it through, then it makes some sort of sense.  But trying to figure out why it is happening in the first place is fiendishly difficult!

So, the answer is - don't have the preview pane open in Windows Explorer.

Geoff
