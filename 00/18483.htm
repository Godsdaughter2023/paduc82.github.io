steve rindsberg say:

I'm hardly the resident media wizard, but might as well chime in to get the ball rolling.

What exact steps did you use to insert the sound?

By default in 2010 now, media files are embedded where they used to be linked in most cases in earlier versions.  You can still choose to link them if you click the down arrow next to the Insert button and choose the Link option when inserting them.  That should give you something more like what previous versions produced and might be one workaround.

And re your followup post, the Media Object Upgrade window:  it sounds like you accepted the offer.  Once having done that, does the sound play as expected on your PC and elsewhere?  The fact that the sound then becomes embedded is deliberate.  The general idea is along these lines:

You insert a sound or movie.  It plays on your PC but you want to be certain it plays everywhere.  That depends on the link not breaking and on the needed CODECs being available on other computers.

When you accept the upgrade offer, it embeds the media file (sidestepping any linking issues) and converts the media to something that uses standard Windows CODECs, so it plays on most/any PCs.  That's the theory, anyhow.

macropod say:

Correction: Non-breaking spaces (i.e. ASCII 160) in Word 2013 & later have a variable width; in Word 2010 & earlier they have a variable width. Your document's justification will still work, but the results will vary according to which Word version you're using and, if it's Word 2013 & later, whether the document was created in an earlier version or with compatibility set for Word 2010 & earlier.

If the document:

• is opened in Word 2010 & earlier, the non-breaking spaces will have a fixed width regardless of the version the document was created in;

• is opened in Word 2013 & later and the document was created in an earlier version or with compatibility set for Word 2010 & earlier, the non-breaking spaces will have a fixed width; and

• is opened in Word 2013 & later and the document was created in Word 2013 & later using the Word 2013 & later defaults, the non-breaking spaces will have a variable width.

Many programs, including Word, employed ASCII 160 for variable-width non-breaking spaces long before standards such as HTML & CSS even existed.
