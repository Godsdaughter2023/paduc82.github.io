Agree and I understand your system.  My 1st response was a bit off track and I then tried to edit it.  You have what should be a valid and qualifying W8.1 license.  No question about that.  You also have W10 on a separate HDD.  You do not have a vaid license for W10.  You and everyone else has W10 Preview.  All Pro editions of W10 now use the same Product (at least since about Feb) and all Enterprise W10 use the same key but unique to Enterprise.

Here is the determining factor.  It takes a qualifying W7 or W8.1 license to get the free upgrade to W10 Final.  As a matter of fact, all qualifying W7 and W8.1 licenses are getting Reserve notices for the free upgrade to W10.  You should also be seeing this on your W8.1 PC.  It is a little icon that looks like a 4 pane window and should be on the lower toolbar and close to the clock.  You exercise this to reserve your free copy of W10.  My W8.1 PC's have received it and are reserved.  I can still back out.

The route to W10 with a qualifying W7 or W8.1 license is via update.  When this option is exercised, W10 becomes your official operating system and takes away your prior license.  While we are now enjoying W10 Pro or Enterprise Preview, the final install of W10 will also be down grading those that have prior editions lesser than Pro.  There is no free upgrade for any edition of Enterprise.

The W10 on your separate HDD is just for testing and will get you nothing.  You are in the same boat as a person who built a new PC and installed W10 on it.  All W10 Preview editions are now installed with an expiry date and will just cease to operate.

You have a choice.  Give up W8.1 and move to free W10 or keep W8.1 and buy W10 if you like W10.
