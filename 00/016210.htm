samueltannatt say:

So I have come up with a work around for this problem.  It's VERY far from ideal, but it keeps you from having to delete contacts every time that you need to add or update digital signatures for existing Outlook 2013 / 2016 contacts.

1. Open the digitally signed email by double clicking it

2. Click on the red ribbon in the upper right of the message window

3. Click on the "Details..." button in the "Digital Signature: Valid" window

4. Highlight "Signer: *** Email address is removed for privacy ***" and click on the "View Details..." button

5. Select the "General" tab in the "Signature" window and click on the "View Certificate..." button

6. Select the "Details" tab in the "View Certificate" window and click on the "Copy to File..." button

7. In the "Certificate Export Wizard" window:

    a. On the first page, click on the "Next" button

    b. On the next page, select "DER encoded binary X.509 (.CER)" and click on the "Next" button

    c. On the next page, enter a location and filename (I use "C:\Users\User_Name\Documents\Outlook Files" and store the certificate as "LastName_FirstName_Year.cer") and click the "Next" button.

    d. On the last page, click the "Finish" button

8. In the Certificate Export Wizard dialog box (which should be informing you that "the export was successful," click the "OK" button

9. Close the "View Certificate", "Signature", "Digital Signature: Valid", and message windows

10. Open "My Contacts" and select the contact for which you want to add / update the digital signature

11. Double click on the applicable contact to open the contact editor window

12. Under the "Contact" drop-down, select the red ribbon labeled "Certificates"

13. Click on the "Import" button and select the file saved in step 7

14. If updating a digital signature, be sure to select the updated signature (they will often have the same name, so double click to look at the validity dates of the signatures) and click on the "Set as Default" button before proceeding

15. Under the "Contact" drop-down, select the diskette labeled "Save & Close"

Obviously, Microsoft should fix this problem (just as they should fix the problem that occurs when you respond to a group encrypted email and it only links the sender's certificate), but until they do, you don't have to delete contacts anymore just to update PKIs!

Hope this helps!
