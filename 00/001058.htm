woodg say:

To find email from a specific sender there are at least 3 different methods you can use.

 

Method 1: Click on the Search email box and and Advanced search option appears. Click on this to get Advanced search.

 

Method 2: Move the mouse cursor over the emal and pause for a few seconds. A popup should appear giving you several options including Find email.

 

Method 3: Right click on an email, select For this sender (at the bottom of the list) and then select Find email. Please note that some users have reported problems with using right click as the browser tries to give you its own right click option instead of the web page options, so you may have trouble with this method.

 

I've not used the Important email option in Hotmail, but Outlook does have something that sounds similar and that is Flag. When you Flag an email it appears with a red flag and is displayed at the top of your inbox. To flag an email you have at least two methods.

 

Method 1: Click on the email to be flagged and then click on the elipsis (three dots to the right of Categories on the command bar) and then click on Flag.

 

Method 2: In the inbox, click on the small light gray flag icon located just under the date.

ADFS 3.0 is broken by certain AVG feature - Microsoft Community

gmuduckman say:

ADFS 3.0 is broken by certain AVG feature

We setup ADFS 3.0 and DirSync to work with our Office 365.  It was working with no problems for certain users and not at all for other users.  The users it was not working for just continually received the login prompt when trying to login using windows authentication mode.  One solution we found was to disable Enhanced Protection for Authentication, but obviously that's a less secure route to go.  After a week of running Fiddler and combing through logs, we started thinking the Antivirus (AVG) was the culprit.  Sure enough we discovered the most recent version of AVG Business Edition has a feature called  Online Shield. Within that feature there is an option to "Scan encrypted (TLS and SSL) network traffic.  This feature essentially inserts an AVG certificate within the certificate chain and effectively killed the handshake between the client and ADFS server.  Disabling this feature fixed all the problems.  

Hopefully this helps someone, it was definitely a headache for us.
