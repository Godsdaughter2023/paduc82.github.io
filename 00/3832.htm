Hello Deboleena,

Thank you for your forum contribution.

This kind of errors are normally caused because the main microsoft account that you are trying to use on the phone does not have all the information required.

First of all please check what is the main microsoft account on the phone. Please go to Settings > email+accounts > check the email that appears under "Microsoft Account"

From the PC, sign into this web using your microsoft account: https://account.live.com/editprof.aspx

Make sure that all data are filled in and correct, including the date of birth, country, address and time zone. Save it.

Now please sign into this web with the same account: http://www.xbox.com

If you are asked to confirm the Terms of Use or to create an Xbox live account, please follow the steps.

Then please, on the phone go to Settings > email+accounts > add an account > microsoft account > enter the email and password.

If the same issue persist please follow the steps of this article:

Can't add Microsoft account during initial setup of Windows Phone 8

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/2784236/en-us

1. Reset the time

To manually reset the clock on your Windows Phone 8:

  1. On Start, flick left to the App list, tap Settings
    Collapse this imageExpand this image
    , and then tap Date+time.
  2. Tap to toggle Set automatically to Off.
  3. Tap each of the options to set the current Time zone, Date, and Time. When you're done, tap the phone's back button.

    IMPORTANT Double-check that the day, month, and year listed are all correct before you proceed.
  4. Sign in with your Microsoft account again by going to Settings
    Collapse this imageExpand this image
    and then Email+accounts.
2. Connect to Wi-Fi

If the previous steps didn't help, or if the date and time was set correctly, try connecting to Wi-Fi and signing in again:

  1. On Start, flick left to the App list, tap Settings  
    Collapse this imageExpand this image
    , and then tap Wi-Fi. Select a Wi-Fi network and connect to it.
  2. Sign in with your Microsoft account again by going to Settings 
    Collapse this imageExpand this image
    and then Email+accounts.
3. Reset your phone


If the steps above still didn't help, your phone may be in a bad state, which means you will have to reset the phone to its original factory settings and set it up again.

Note More information about resetting your phone can be found in the Help & How to article Reset my phone

      (http://www.windowsphone.com/en-US/how-to/wp8/basics/reset-my-phone) . 

To reset your phone, follow these steps:
  1. On Start, flick left to the App list and tap Settings.
  2. Tap About, and then tap Reset your phone.
  3. Tap Yes, and then tap Yes again.

Then step through the setup wizard again. During the setup, make sure to manually set the date and time, connect to Wi-Fi (if available), and try adding your Microsoft account.

Hope this helps,

Jorge A.M.

jetta48 say:

You can scan individual folders/drives, but not files as follows:

1. Open Windows Defender (either via Charms bar then Search or via Control Panel).

2. On Home tab select Custom under Scan options:

3. Navigate to the folder(s)/drive(s) you wish to scan (click on + sign(s) to expand folder trees) and put a tick in the box(es) as wanted.

4. Click OK and the scan will start.

 

The built in anti-malware program in Windows 8 is called Windows Defender, not Microsoft Security Essentials, even though its effectively just an updated version of Microsoft Security Essentials.
