When you are not actively using your computer Windows jumps into action to get all its housekeeping done. It stops again when you go back to actively using your computer. Therefore, Windows background activities are unlikely to slow your computer unless your hardware barely meets the requirements for Windows 10, and computers like that haven't been sold for years.

    Something else: Task Manager (along with similar Windows utilities) does not provide exact realtime metrics. It provides a close approximation. To provide exact metrics it would require additional processing overhead that would suck up your system's resources for no good reason.

    All that is not to say that there absolutely, definitely isn't something wrong with your computer. But to make that case you would need to have additional, specific evidence, such as in KB3083595. To merely look at Task Manager and see X% disk utilization is no basis for conclusions.

    I'm not saying this paragraph applies to you: Many people look at data or run diagnostics that they don't understand and then blame their computer's problems on Microsoft when the truth is that their computer's performance issues are caused by their own mistakes.
