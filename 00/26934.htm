If you're working in a bulleted or numbered list, or a multilevel list, you can press the Tab key at the start of a paragraph to increase its indentation and Shift+Tab or Backspace to decrease it. To make this work, you must set an option:

Go to File > Options > Proofing. Click the AutoCorrect Options button. In the AutoCorrect dialog, click the AutoFormat As You Type tab. In the bottom section of the dialog, check the box for "Set left- and first-indent with tabs and backspaces". Click OK in each dialog.

Instead of tabbing, you can use the Increase Indent and Decrease Indent buttons in the Paragraph group on the Home tab of the ribbon.

If you're working in a paragraph that isn't part of a list, pressing Tab will just insert a tab character that will push the next character to the first tab stop. If you want to change the paragraph's indentations (left and/or right), right-click in the paragraph and click Paragraph in the menu. In the Paragraph dialog, use the settings in the Indentation section.

The Alt-key pop-up key tips are a nuisance and have been for me forever. You cannot get rid of them. There is no option.

My guess is that your keyboard and perhaps mouse are somehow activating them. I have seen instances where the keyboard vibrations have been sensed as mouse movements. I would guess that they could, in a problem keyboard, also trigger or activate the Alt key. In non-Office programs you would never even notice this because pressing the Alt key, by itself, has no effect.

So, again, see if you can try a different keyboard. There may also be some sort of keyboard sensitivity setting on your computer.
You might want to consider turning on filter keys. I do not know whether it would help with this. It would give a click when the key is pressed, but may not be in time to help.
