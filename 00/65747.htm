syed n say:

Hi Chettj

 

You may have to renew the client DHCP (Dynamic Host Configuration Protocol) lease. The DHCP client will attempt to get a new IP address from the DHCP server.

 

To perform these procedures, you must be a member of the Administrators group, or you must have been delegated the appropriate authority.

 

You can manually release and renew the IP address of the client by following these steps:

 

a.        Click Start, and then click Run.

b.       In Open, type cmd, and then press ENTER.

c.        Type ipconfig /release, and then press ENTER.

d.       Type ipconfig /renew, and then press ENTER.

 

To verify that address conflicts have been resolved.

 

a.        Click Start, and then click Run.

b.       In Open, type cmd, and then press ENTER.

c.        To verify a DHCP client lease, type ipconfig /all to view lease-status information.

d.       The output of the ipconfig command should show an IPv4 or IPv6 address marked "preferred."
