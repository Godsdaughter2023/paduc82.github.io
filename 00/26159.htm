Starting with Diminishing Value.  Year 1 has unique formulas
B9 =(B$2-B$3)*B$4/12
C9 =DATEDIF(B$1,DATE(YEAR(B$1)+IF(MONTH(B$1)>=7,1,0),7,1),"m")
D9 =C9*B9

Year 2 uses the following (These are copied down)
B10 =(B$2-B$3-SUM(D$9:D9))*B$4/12
This subtracts any depreciation already taken

C10 =IF(SUM($C$9:C9)+12<=B$5*12,12,B$5*12-SUM($C$9:C9))
This checks the total months accumulated +12 and uses 12 if the total does not exceed the entered life

D10 =IF(C10=12,C10*B10,B$2-B$3-SUM(D$9:D9))
Multiples B*C to get annual depreciation, except in the last year, where it depreciates the remaining amount
(NOTE:  This appear to be an error with your original last year calculation.  Your total depreciation is not equal to the asset cost..  If the calculations you had were correct and the total depreciation is not supposed to be $63000 just use =B10*C10 as the formula)

You need to copy these down for at least 1 year longer than your longest useful life.


For straight line the same method is used, but the formulas are simpler.  Year 1 is still unique
B16 =(B$2-B$3)/(B$5*12)
C16 =DATEDIF(B$1,DATE(YEAR(B$1)+IF(MONTH(B$1)>=7,1,0),7,1),"m")
D16 =C16*B16

For year 2
B17 =(B$2-B$3)/(B$5*12)
C17 =IF(SUM($C$16:C16)+12<=B$5*12,12,B$5*12-SUM($C$16:C16))
D17 =C17*B17
You need to copy these down for at least 1 year longer than your longest useful life.

The DATEDIF function is a older no longer documented function, but still available.

Actually it should be fairly straight forward.  Create the task and set it up as fixed work.  Assign resource 1 and enter the entire 80 hours.  The duration should calculate to 10 days.  Now assign Resource 2.  Project will divide the work proportionally based upon their max units.

The duration is 6.67 days with the following work split:

Resource 1 (100% max units = 53.33 hours

Resource 2 ( 50% max units = 26.67 hours

If the task starts on 4/24 at 8:00 am  it ends on 5/2 at 2:20 pm.
