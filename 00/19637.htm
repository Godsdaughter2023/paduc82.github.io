faysal farooqui say:

Hello WayneXTalbot,

Few days ago I was facing the same problem and finally resolve it.

Why this problem:

Well, it is a problem of Operating System architecture (32bit or 64bit) versus your installed Microsoft Office architecture (32bit or 64bit) issue.

If you install OS architecture is Windows 8.1 - 32bit (x86) and you have installed Microsoft Office 2016 - 64bit (x64) then you will see this message.

How to resolve it:

Simply you need to install a "Microsoft Office 2016 - 32bit (x86)" in you PC to match your OS architecture with your Microsoft Office 2016 architecture.

¡firedog say:

You have copied the existing entries in your Google calendar into the Windows Live calendar, which is what you asked about. That's a one-off batch process that won't perform any sort of synchronization in the future.

If you want to update your Windows Live calendars with events you've added to your Google calendar, you'll have to share the Google calendar so that the WLCalendar can subscribe to it. To find the link to use,

In your Google calendar,

  1. Reveal the name of the calendar you want to share under My calendars in the left-hand pane.
  2. Click the dropdown arrow next to it and select Calendar settings.
  3. Click the ICAL button by Private address. Right-click on the address given and select Copy shortcut.

In your Windows Live calendar,

  1. Click Subscribe and select Subscribe to a public calendar.
  2. Paste the address you copied into the Calendar URL box, give the calendar a name, select a colour and a charm if you want
  3. Click Subscribe to calendar.
