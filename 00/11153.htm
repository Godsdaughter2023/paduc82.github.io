ken sheridan say: It's not a question of an outer join being better than an inner join, it's a question of the former being former must be used; if there will for each row in the referenced table, then be rows in the referencing table then an inner join should be used.  Sticking with the Northwind example, let's say we want to know the total value of all orders by customers in California, including zero for those who have made no orders, then as there will be no matching rows for those who have made no orders outer joins are necessary:

SELECT Company, NZ(SUM([Unit Price] * Quantity),0) AS TotalAmount
FROM Products
RIGHT JOIN ((Customers
LEFT JOIN Orders
ON Customers.ID = Orders.[Customer ID])
LEFT JOIN [Order Details]
ON Orders.[Order ID] = [Order Details].[Order ID])
ON Products.ID = [Order Details].[Product ID]
WHERE Customers.[State/Province]="CA"
GROUP BY Company;

If in the other hand we want to know all customers in California who have made orders whose value totals 1,000 USD or more then there obviously must be matching rows for those customers, so inner joins are appropriate:

SELECT Company, SUM([Unit Price] * Quantity) AS TotalAmount
FROM Products
INNER JOIN ((Customers
INNER JOIN Orders
ON Customers.ID = Orders.[Customer ID])
INNER JOIN [Order Details]
ON Orders.[Order ID] = [Order Details].[Order ID])
ON Products.ID = [Order Details].[Product ID]
WHERE Customers.[State/Province]="CA"
GROUP BY Company
HAVING SUM([Unit Price] * Quantity) >= 1000;

In the first case it's not that outer joins are better than inner joins; they are essential to return the desired results.  BTW I've not included the Discount column in the expression for the computed TotalAmount column for simplicity.  It's not pertinent to the point being illustrated.

In your case it is possible that a reason for all the rows within the date range not being returned is that there are no matching rows in one of the referencing tables for some rows in a referenced table, but that may not be the reason at all.  We can't see your data, so we don't know.  All we can do is point you towards possible reasons, of which this is one.
