cliffgu_msft5984 say:

Hi Elizabeth,

From your description, I think you may create a custom list named to track time.
Here are the steps.
1. Create a custom list in a SharePoint site collection.
2. In List Settings, under “Columns” area, click “Add from existing site columns”.
3. Add the “Start Date” and “End Date” from “Available site columns” to “Columns to add” area, then click “OK”.
4. Click “Start Date” under “Columns”, make sure that on the “Require that this column contains information:” option, choose “Yes”; And on the “Date and Time Format” option choose “Date & Time”, do the same check for “End Date” column.
5. Now the column “Title”, “Start Date” and “End Date” are displayed on the list.
6. In the “Filter” area, choose “Show items only when the following is true”, then “Created By”->“is equal to”->“[me]”. Thus, a person who creates the item can just see his/her own item.
7. When a person adds a new item to the list by clicking “Add new item”, a time record will be created.

For the “Site Collection Admin”, he/she need to see the all items that others added, so, the Site Collection Admin should create a new view for himself/herself only. He can follow the steps below to create a view just for himself.
1. On the “List Settings” page, click “Create view” at the botton of the page.
2. On the following screen, choose “Standard View”.
3. On the next screen, name the “View” as “Admin Items”. “Do not” check the box of “Make this the default view”, in the “Audience” area, choose “Create a Personal View”, thus, others will not see the view any more.

After that, the Site Collection Admin can change the views to control and get a overview of the time records.

He can also export the data to an Excel file by click “Export to Excel” on the ribbon.

If something is misunderstood, welcome to post back and we’ll provide further help.

Thanks,
Cliff
