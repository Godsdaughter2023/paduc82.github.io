    dir D:\Win*
    dir E:\Win*

    dir

    copy /y software  ..

canadian tech say:

First and most importantly, you must understand that WET transfers only data and settings.  Applications must be installed individually on your Windows 7 system

Second, it is possible that not all your XP applications can run on Win7.  It is even more possible that some of them may not operate the same way as they did under XP.  Some of those applications may be available in more up to date versions that are more appropriate for Win7 64.

Considering the multi disk environment you have described, WET may not be the best tool for your purposes.  WET was designed primarily for a typical consumer computer and yours is not.

I would advise that you should backup your data to an external source, then after Win7 is installed and completely up to date, install your applications individually, then restore your backup to your new system selectively putting things where you want them.

Once you have loaded Win7, all its drivers for your system, installed every update available, installed stable applications that do not change and updated them. I recommend a step you will seldom ever see.  It is called System Image.  The program is part of Win7.  You are going to need about 4 blank DVDs.  I strongly recommend you do not use the most common type -- DVD -R's.  Instead use DVD +R's.  Start globe, All programs, maintenance, backup and restore, create a system image.  It could be up to an hour to complete this.  When done also create the System Repair disk suggested -- you only need a conventional blank CD for this.

The System image you create here will permit you in the future to re-build your drive in about 20 minutes to precisely its current condition.  It is a really great piece of insurance.

Your key is in fact the correct one for a Dell computer (-RMV82) but as Andre says the computer is far too old to have been supplied with Windows 7. This license can only be installed by the manufacturer and does not work for a disk based image. The sticker should actually be for an earlier version of Windows.
