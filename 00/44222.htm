really lexin say:

I can, yes. Note that this is a suggested repair for an "Error 19" message, there are no guarantees and I take no responsibility for anything that may happen to your PC.

OK...first print out the instructions in case you get lost.

This is for Windows 10, but should work for Windows 8.1 too.

Go to the little Windows icon in the lower left hand corner of the screen. Right mouse click and choose "run".

A dialogue box opens up. Inside the dialogue box type, "regedit" (without the inverted commas).

A box opens up and asks you if you really intend to do this. Click 'yes'.

A box opens up called "Registry Editor". This is what you want.

First, take a copy of your registry. Go to "File", then "Export".  Create a copy of your registry in a place and with a name you will remember. You may wish to write this name and place down. Exporting the registry will take a little while, so don't panic if it's not instant.

Once you have done this, start looking down in your registry. Go to HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE and choose that.

Then go to SYSTEM, and choose that.

Then go to CurrentControlSet and choose that.

Then go to Control and choose that.

Then go to Class and choose that.

Now you have to find an entry which reads exactly {4d36e965-e325-11ce-bfc1-08002be10318} and choose that.

Once you have chosen that, there should be entries (values) called LowerFilters and UpperFilters. It doesn't matter what they are, delete them both. If only one exists, delete that. You will be asked to confirm deletion, but do so.

Save your registry and then reboot.

If it worked properly, you should find that your DVD drive comes back on reboot.

There are no guarantees here, but this worked for me when nothing else did.

Here is a solution that worked for my two computers running Windows 10, after upgrading, dvd not working or showing up at all in Windows 10. 

Thanks to another person who posted it on another forum. I cannot remember who, but indebted to him.

(DVD drive must show up in Bios to get to work.  If it is not there, Windows will not find it & won't work.)

Step 1: Open Control Panel-locate Device Manager & click, once-to open.

Step 2: Find IDE ATA/ATAPI controllers in the list & click-twice to open.

Step 3:  Six devices will show up. They are:

ATA Channel 0  & Channel 0  (two devices total)               (To be removed)

ATA Channel 1 & Channel 1   (two devices total)               (To be removed)

Two PCI controllers, (bottom 2 devices) Leave those 2 along. (Don't remove)

To remove the 4 devices-Click "Drivers" of these 4 ATA devices and "Click" remove devices from computer.

Restart computer, Windows 10 should then find the 4 you removed and your DVD drive, too.

Don't restart until you have removed all 4. Windows will ask to restart after one of them is removed,

wait until all 4 are removed, then reboot, otherwise you will be stuck in a loop. Your DVD drive

should now show up in Device Manager as working-along with those 4 you removed.

It worked for me & I am retired & 73 years old-so I am not responsible if you destroy your computer.

Good Luck!
