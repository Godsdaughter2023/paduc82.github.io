swischner say:
You could archive a whole section (in OneNote for Windows desktop only, e.g. OneNote 2016) by exporting it to a .ONE file ("File", "Export", "Section", "*.one") and delete the section in OneNote afterwards.
You could also choose other export formats. .ONE maintains the content and structure in OneNote format and can be imported into any OneNote notebook. Other formats like Word DOC or PDF might be more universal but could seriously affect the layout of the notes.

But I believe you rather want to get rid of (archive) a selection of pages while keeping others and the section itself, right?

Best practice I can think of would be this:

Create a new empty notebook for your archived content
In your work notebook select the pages you want to archive (press and hold Shift or Crtl while selecting them in the list)
Right click the selection and choose "Move or Copy" or pres Ctrl+Alt+M
In the next dialog select your new archive notebook and click on "Move".

After moving the pages "out of the way" you may (but don't have to) close the archive notebook. But for the next bunch of pages you want to archive, you'd have to open it again first to make it a selectable target for the move operation.

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

If you have the Office 2007 installation kit because you licensed it for another PC, then network the two PCs together, share the DVD drive on the desktop to make it available to the netbook, then copy the contents of the DVD to a folder on the netbook.  Alternatively, buy a 4GB flash drive for $10-15 and copy the DVD onto that device, then install on the netbook from the flash drive.  You can also purchase an inexpensive USB-connected DVD drive for the netbook.

You can, at least for now, visit http://www.microsoft.com/office/downloads/ and find Office Home and Student 2007

DO NOT rely on being able to download your version of Office in the future.  Make your own copies of the installation kit and store them offline so that you can always reinstall when necessary.  Keep a hard copy of your product key as well so that a dead PC won't wipe it out.
