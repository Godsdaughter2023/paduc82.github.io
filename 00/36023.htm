johnnyc97 say:

Surface Pro 4 Keyboard/Touchpad not responding - power state solution

Hi all,

Apologies for the longish post but I wanted to share my experience with an incredibly frustrating issue I was having with my Surface Pro 4 Type Cover. The solution that worked for me doesn't seem to be posted on any of the countless support pages I have spent hours trawling through so I feel compelled to share.

My issue related to my keyboard not responding sometimes when it was detached from the Surface Dock. The keys and touchpad would not respond but interestingly the fingerprint sensor still continued to work. No amount of disconnects, reboots, hard resets, cleaning the contacts, driver uninstalls etc as proposed in the various support pages worked, including the various steps in the official Microsoft support page:

https://support.microsoft.com/en-gb/help/4023473/surface-troubleshoot-your-surface-type-cover-or-keyboard

This issue would happen to me regularly and because I travel a bit this would be the single most frustrating quirk about the Surface Pro 4 that I have encountered. Solution #1 worked for me in most cases except in the cases where I had no access to the Surface Dock. That's when I discovered the second solution below.

Instead here are two potential options that you can try:

1. Reconnect the Surface to the dock and perform a graceful shutdown. Disconnect the Surface from the dock and then boot it up. If the keyboard lights up and/or the Fn or Caps key lights work then you should be good to go.

2. Drain the battery completely before plugging in the power cable (not the Surface dock cable) and booting it up. I did this by playing a video on repeat so it did take a few hours. I also made sure the battery was completely drained to the point where the low battery warning doesn't appear on screen when you press the power button.

I stumbled on the second solution after theorizing that my issues related to some power type setting. I noticed in device manager for the keyboard (Control Panel -> Device Manager -> Keyboards -> Surface Type Cover Filter Device -> Properties -> Details tab -> Property = Power data) that the keyboard had a "Current power state" of D1 or D2. I took a stab that maybe draining the battery completely would 'reset' this power state and voila. Upon next book my keyboard lit up and all was right with the world again. I noted that in working mode the current power state shows as D0 so I can only presume that this is what the issue was.

There may be a quicker way to do this through a registry setting or something like that but I didn't go down that path. For the record I also did try looking at some of the power profile settings but couldn't find any options there that related to the keyboard so didn't go down that path. Again - there may be a quicker option through there for someone smarter than me.

I should also mention that I've only had to do this once and so I haven't definitively proven that the solution above works but hopefully this is helpful for someone.
