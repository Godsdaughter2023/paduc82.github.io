pamcaswell say: It's no comfort to tell you that this is not a bug, but by design .    You see, when you insert a section break, Word replicates the header settings and page number formatting of the previous section break (of the same type).   Your first section is Different first page with the page number set to start at 1.  Your second and third sections have the same setup.  That's where the page ii is coming from in your front matter.

To fix this.  First put a temporary page break (not a section break) somewhere within your 2 column sections to expose the headers for the continuous breaks.  Then change the page numbering
to continue from previous. 

I learned how to fix this problem 8 years ago, when I was using W2003.  For a brief time in W2007, clicking next cycled through all the continuous and next-page headers and footers . It wasn't necessary to put the temporary breaks in so we could see the headers and footers for mid page section breaks.  I loved it.  Then they took it away.  I do understand that many may not want to see headers an footers that don't actually appear in the document.  But, surely just as many are confused to see the sections jump from, in your case, 1 to 3.

One more suggestion: There is no need to set the starting number in your first section. The only time you should actually restart numbering is in the first section after the front matter, where you will start at 1.
