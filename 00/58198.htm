BIOS Updating: Usually you can find your BIOS version on the Manufacturers screen, if not enter BIOS and check the version there. Then go to the manufacturers website and check to see if the latest version superceeds your version

Drivers: Yes I meant hardware drivers. A driver is a small piece of software that lets Windows know how to communicate with a hardware component. Windows 10 Automatic Update will update your drivers, but sometimes it will install a driver that does not quite work with your system, so I find it best to get my drivers from the manufacturer of my system or failing that from the manufacturer of the hardware (e.g. Intel, nVidia . . . etc.)

With regard to applying pressure at a certain point on your laptop to get the sound working, this sounds like a loose connection inside your laptop. Laptops are not that hard to disassemble, you will find instructions on the web for most models. You could if you feel competent take a look inside your laptop around the area you apply pressure on. On tip, if you do disassemble the casing on your laptop, do it in a methodical way and make sure everything goes back in its original location . . .
