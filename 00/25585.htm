vijay a. verma say:

If your list starts in A2 and you can put following array formula in B2 and drag down

=IFERROR(INDEX($A$2:$A$100, MATCH(0, IF($A$2:$A$100<>"",COUNTIF(B1:$B$1, $A$2:$A$100)), 0)),"")

Note - Array Formula is not entered by pressing ENTER after entering your formula but by pressing CTRL+SHIFT+ENTER. If you are copying and pasting this formula, take F2 after pasting and CTRL+SHIFT+ENTER. This will put { } brackets around the formula which you can see in Formula Bar. If you edit again, you will have to do CTRL+SHIFT+ENTER again. Don't put { } manually.

rod gill say:

I would say 98% of people do not use PERT charts for the very good reason that there is no time scale. Everyone instead uses the Gantt chart.

My experience is that on site staff can more easily read a Gantt Chart, especially when tasks are roughly sorted by time. Reading through many boxes in a PERT chart to determine what should get done next week just won't happen!
I would also provide task only lists for the next 2 weeks.

Add custom columns for things like trade and location (building, room, floor number etc.) so you and they can filter on a particular trade etc..

Don't display early and late starts etc as I've found it takes 6 months or more to get on site staff to really understand what a critical task is, let alone float, etc. They get the concept but still won't focus on completing critical tasks first, then wonder why dates keep slipping. Once they actually do critical tasks on time, then introduce early and late dates.

Keep it simple. The Gantt Chart is far more friendly.
