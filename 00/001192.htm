Hi P-A Taillefer,

Yes, when you configure your email account in your mobile device using Exchange mode or IMAP, it will sync the read/unread status across all devices. We can only manually mark the emails so it will show as an unread email the next time you open your account on your Outlook client.

As a manual workaround for built in mail app for iOS is to:

- Manually mark emails (tap hold an email), it will display a blue check/mark on the left side, then press the Envelope at the bottom panel (2nd from the left) to mark these selected mails as “Mark as Unread”

- Or In the INBOX, Click the Edit button on the top right corner and manually select the mails you want to Mark, click Mark on the bottom panel, then choose Mark as Unread.

As a manual workaround for OWA for iOS is to:

- Manually mark emails (tap hold an email), it will display a check box on the left side, then press the Envelope at the bottom panel to mark these selected mails as “Mark as Unread”

- Or set a “Quick actions” to swipe the email you want to mark as Unread. To set the Quick action got the “Gear” icon > Quick actions > now set the action to “Mark as read or unread” to either swipe right or left.

As a manual workaround for built in mail app for Android is to:

- Manually mark emails, tap hold an email to show the options, on the top left select the “envelope” icon to mark the email as read/unread.

Re: "Some settings are managed by your system administrator"

the above message indicates that some of your IE settings have been restricted by  a security program or malware.

On company computers users have limited access to IE's settings to make changes to their browser configuration. This is managed centrally, usually by the "system administrators".

On home versions of windows, if you see the message "Some settings are managed by your system administrator" when you try to change any of your Internet Explorer Options, it means that either your security programs or some other 'security' related program, like Parental controls, is preventing you from making changes to IE's settings.

If your bought your computer second-hand it could be that it came from a company network, and still has the security restrictions for IE on it.

It could be your modems' control panel software that has applied restrictions to your IE settings...
