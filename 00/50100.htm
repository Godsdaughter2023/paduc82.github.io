Windows 7 is supported by Microsoft until 2020.

And Computer or hardware manufacturers that supported 7 still do support 7 and continue to supply drivers for 7.

You normally only replace/reinstall drivers if you are having a problem with the existing ones or you require newer features that a newly released driver for a particular device would give you.

A lot of released drivers have never been updated since the year that they were released, but the devices they are for still work correctly.

Go to your Computer or Hardware Manufacture's website > Driver and Software Support Section > look up your Computer or Hardware Model number > then your Operating System > locate the correct Drivers > download and install them.

dancing betta say:

You updated the drivers? If you mean you did that within Windows, that is unfortunately not going to help with BIOS problems. BIOS gets loaded long before Windows and the drivers get loaded.

What you can do: Check with the other keyboard whether USB legacy support is enabled in BIOS. Where you find that depends on the BIOS used for your PC. Toggle this on or off depending on the current state and see what happens.

Also, in some cases other USB devices like card readers can mess with USB keyboard detection, but since it works with a second keyboard I doubt that's it.

As a last resort you may want to consider a BIOS update.

Hi Anmol,

To help you with this concern, we suggest that you try to reinstall first an individual default app in Windows 10 and let's see if the issue still persists. Here's how:

  1. Go to Start menu, type PowerShell, right-click on it and select Run as Administrator.

  3. This will show you a list of of default apps and their appxmanifest.xml file paths.
  4. Find the app you want to reinstall in the list, and double-click its PackageFullName.
  5. After highlighting the string, press Enter or Ctrl+C to copy it to your clipboard.   6. Open the Start menu and check the app you just reinstalled. If still not showing, try restarting your computer.

tricky300 say:

It is not possible for me to know what the red light is. What I can say is that typically there are two lights on the front of a PC and the colours used vary from PC to PC. One is the power light and would normally be on permanently. The other is a disk activity light and would flash randomly. I have heard it said that some PCs combine the two but I have never seen such a PC.

 

If the disk activity light stays on permanently it would indicate a problem. If the power light stays on permanently this is normal.

 

Next time it crashes, just push the power button and let go immediately (do not hold it in) - does the PC shut down?

 

The fact that your monitor goes blank at the time of what you think might be a crash, indicates either a graphics card or monitor issue. I repeat, you need to cross test these two pieces of hardware.

 

Examine the graphics card if it is fan cooled - is it choked up with dust? Don't do this with the PC on, shut down, disconnect the mains lead and take antistatic precautions.

a. user say:

Hello,

Thank you  for updating us on the issue.

If you don’t want Windows 10 automatically downloading updates on your home network connection, just set it as a metered connection. Windows 10 will automatically download updates when you connect your device to an unmetered network. And  Windows will remember this setting for each individual network, so you can disconnect from that network and reconnect all you like.

To change this option, open the Settings app, select Network & Internet, scroll down, and select “Advanced options” below the list of Wi-Fi networks. Enable the “Set as metered connection” option. This option only affects the Wi-Fi network you’re currently connected to, but Windows will remember this setting for each individual Wi-Fi network.

After enabling this option, Windows Update will say “Updates are available. We’ll download the updates as soon as you connect to Wi-Fi, or you can download the updates using your data connection (charges may apply.)”.

You can click the Download button to download and install updates at your leisure.

Hope this information is helpful. Please do let us know if you need further assistance with Windows, we’ll be glad to assist you.
