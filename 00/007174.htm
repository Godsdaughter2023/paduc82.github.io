YTD Totals - Summing a row of numbers through a specified period - Microsoft Community

spsquidward say:

YTD Totals - Summing a row of numbers through a specified period

I have a row of numbers reflecting sales from January (period 1) through December (period 12).  I want to have a column reflecting sales through period (n) in which the user can key in the period number, and the sum will be Jan thru the period specified.  What is the easiest way to do this?
joeu2004 say: SPSquidward wrote:
I have a row of numbers reflecting sales from January (period 1) through December (period 12).  I want to have a column reflecting sales through period (n) in which the user can key in the period number, and the sum will be Jan thru the period specified.  What is the easiest way to do this?

Easiest?  Probably:

=SUM(OFFSET(A1:L1,0,0,1,N1))

assuming your Jan-to-Dec amounts are in A1:L1 and the user-entered period number is in N1.

However, that formula will be recalculated every time any cell in any worksheet in the workbook is modified or Excel otherwises recalculates one or more cells.

That is not so bad if you have only one or a few so-called "volatile" formulas.  But generally, it is better to avoid such formulas.

Alternatively, you could write:

=SUM(A1:INDEX(A1:L1,1,N1))
