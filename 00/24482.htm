_elaine_ say:

Hello, Paulinem55

The circumstances you find yourself in are indeed not unusual!  Let's try to sort this out.

If I understand this thread correctly, it sounds to me like you have used your employer's email address as your personal Skype account's registered email address. The first step is to change your legacy or old (and personal) Skype account's registered email address to something else. 

You can use a personal email address that you already have as long as that email address is not already used with or for another existing Microsoft account.  While this sounds confusing, it really is not: one email address can be used per Microsoft account, which in turn provides a single sign-on for all Microsoft products and services including a Skype account. 

If you want to keep your Skype account separate from any Microsoft accounts you already have, simply create a new Microsoft account.  You can create a new outlook.com email address when you set up the new account, or use an email address that  you already have set up.  This is your choice if you want to use an email address you already have, or set up a new email address ahead of time.  Personally speaking, I do not recommend using a cable service company or wireless service company's email address for a Microsoft account, even though they provide it, because when people move or change service providers and those accounts are closed, they always lose these email addresses that were part of those accounts.

Before you do anything, please read these FAQ articles carefully, because as Noevir has advised, once your Skype account is linked with any Microsoft account, this process cannot be reversed.

  • Bringing Skype and Microsoft Accounts Together
  • Updating your Skype and Microsoft Accounts (A Single Sign-in for Skype and your other Microsoft Services)
  • How do I add or change my primary email address in my Skype profile

So, the steps are:

  1. Change your old Skype account's registered email address.
  2. Update your Skype account to be a full Microsoft account.

I now recommend completing the update process because restoring access to a Microsoft account, should that situation arise, is so much easier, and a Microsoft account offers two-factor authentication (also called two-step verification) whereas legacy Skype accounts do not.

Apologies for the wall of text. 

Kind regards,

Elaine
