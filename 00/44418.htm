What I am saying is this. There is no need whatsoever to go on a fishing expedition for an issue that may not exist. BSODs can and do sometimes happen even when nothing is really wrong with the computer in question. BSODs should, for the most part, be ignored UNLESS they become frequent.

Also, while the dump file states the error was a result of Memory Management, most often this error is not the ram. It is most often a fluke between the installed ram and pagefile. That is not always the case though.

This issue, the BSOD, is in no way related to the BSODs you were getting in your previous thread. Those were caused by conflicting security software drivers.

If Daavee is not right, then I suspect that Norton is preventing you from making these adjustments.  Norton is protecting your system.

If you made the change to go into Safe Mode in msconfig and then you can't get back, you can edit your c:\boot.ini file and remove the /safeboot:minimal switch.

Look for a line like this:

multi(0)disk(0)rdisk(0)partition(1)\WINDOWS="Microsoft Windows XP Professional" /noexecute=optin /fastdetect /safeboot:minimal

and make it look like this:

multi(0)disk(0)rdisk(0)partition(1)\WINDOWS="Microsoft Windows XP Professional" /noexecute=optin /fastdetect

You can get their by right clicking My Computer, Properties, Advanced, Startup and Recovery, Settings, Edit (this opens Notepad on your boot.ini file), make the adjustment to remove the /safeboot:minimal parameter, File, Save.
