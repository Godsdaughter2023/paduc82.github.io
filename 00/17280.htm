hpholm say:

 ,

The most worrying is that pictures freshly shot by the phone camera do not appear in Gallery.

That suggest something major gone wrong.

 

Other than that, the N8 with Nokia Belle has some built-in imaging weirdness:

The storage for camera pictures and videos was changed to \DCIM\100NOKIA\ to work better with PC software transfer.

The Symbian Anna and earlier storage for pictures is \Images\ with subfolders. This folder name is translated into the phone's local language word for "pictures" when seen in the Files manager on the phone, but must always be Images when seen from a computer file manager through USB or Bluetooth.

 

Only still images stored in the \DCIM\ and \Images\ folders and their subfolders are reliably shown in Gallery.

 

Videos has a further twist, since freshly shot videos appear in Gallery, and they are stored in \DCIM\ but otherwise the Video gallery keep track of videos in \Videos\ (which is also translated into the phone's local language in the phone Files manager!)

 

I keep the still image archive in \Images\ sorted into subfolders and the video archive in \Videos\

This way all still images are shown reliably in the Gallery and all videos are shown in the Video gallery.

 

Applications may store images anywhere on the phone, but these pictures do not appear in my N8 gallery.

I am not familiar with WhatsApp and where its pictures are stored.

 

There have been reports of Gallery picking up pictures from other folders, which is not what you want if i.e. the Viewranger application has a ton of map tiles stored on the phone!

 

Even when the N8 is functioning normally, it has its own mind sometimes.

I've just described the broad strokes of what you'll need to do to get it working.
