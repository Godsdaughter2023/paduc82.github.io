considerate_guy say:

Yeah, I think Movie Maker remembers the absolute time when a song starts. Let's say you had a 7 second photo and then some video. And you set the song to start 5 seconds into the project. Now if you reduce the 7 second photo so that it is only 3 seconds, the song will still start 5 seconds into the project. So it will now appear to start 2 seconds after the video starts playing.

It sounds like you would want Movie Maker to remember that the song starts 2 seconds before the video and have it still start 2 seconds before the video, but it isn't relative, it is absolute.

One workaround as you mentioned, is to set your video elements first and then add your music second. Another workaround is to drag your music back to the desired position after editing a duration. You can select a set of music clips on your storyboard area by clicking the first one, holding down Shift and then clicking on the last one. Now when you drag them forward or back a few seconds, they will keep their relative spacing. In that way you don't have to realign them one-by-one.
