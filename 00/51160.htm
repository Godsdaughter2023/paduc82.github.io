obsessive power say:

Hi,

There is a slight memory shortage, but this is by design.  Firstly, your 1TB Xbox One doesn't actually have 1TB of space, 1TB hard drives when formatted are 931GB. All 1TB hard drives are this size, on consoles PCs, Macs and STB's. The system does not see 1GB as 1024MB.  So when you do the conversion a 1TB drive is 931GB.  Same as a 32GB flash drive only has 29GB free.  All manufactures do this, it's not the fault of Microsoft - who doesn't even manufacture the hard drives by the way.

The Xbox One reserves about 150GB of storage for system use.  What exactly it uses it for I do not know.  Part of it is for the Operating System, of which there is actually three.

All games must be installed.  This is mandatory.  The reason for this is simple, while Blu-ray drives speeds have improved, they are not fast enough for next generation games.  They have poor access and seek times compared to even a standard SATA based 5600rpm hard drive.  This is the reason why games need to be installed.

Add to that the size of the games.  Next generation games have better graphics which means the texture sizes are bigger and take up more space on the hard disc. This is why your 9 games are using 338GB.  All current generation games are this size now, on Xbox One, PS4 and PC.

I hope that helps.

{parity} say:

Some of it has to do with the way the storage is calculated but I am not going to try to explain that here.  It would take way too long.  The total amount of storage does include the amount required by the OS.  Just like when you buy a PC the OS is included in the system total storage.  All a game console is is a PC that is made specifically for gaming so why wouldn't the system storage be counted in the total.  As far as the requirements for the games.  That will vary from game to game but in general the games storage requirements these day are massive.  

From what I see you have 9 games installed and almost half of your HDD available for more games.  

Upgrading the storage is really simple and inexpensive.  All you need is a USB 3.0 external HDD with 256 GB or more of disk space.
