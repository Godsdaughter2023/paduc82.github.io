brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

For Outloook 2003 accessing the Outlook.com mail server, you can use a POP3 account if you don't want to keep your messages on the server.  See the "Apps that support POP3 and SMTP" section of the page http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/outlook/send-receive-from-app for the proper settings for the POP3 account you'll create in Outlook.  Be sure to NOT enable leaving messages on the server.

 

You must configure your Outlook.com account so that it allows POP.  Visit http://mail.live.com/, click Options>More mail settings>Connect devices and apps with POP and enable POP access.  Click Save.  By default, the Outlook.com server will keep your downloaded messages in a POP folder on the server unless you tell it otherwise.  Visit Options>More mail settingsAdvanced privacy settings and select the radio button labeled "Don't let me recover deleted messages" and then click Save.  The server will now obey Outlook 2003's request to delete mail after downloading it.

 

If your mail service is not Outlook.com, you will need to visit your provider's web site to find the proper server settings, but the set up should be similar.  Your service may or may not have the same options as Outlook.com.  That's something you'll need to determine.
