Hello Lilly, hope this message finds you well.

1. The first method is to format the external hard drive from File Explorer:
Press Windows + X buttons together and find File Explorer
Tap on This PC to check if the external hard drive is detected
Right click on the external hard drive and tap on Format
Choose the format (NTFS, FAT32) and tap on Start

2. The second method is to format the external hard drive through Disk Management:
Select the Start button and open Computer Management.
Open Control Panel - System and Security - Administrative Tools, and double-click on Computer Management.

Right-click the volume that you’d like to format, and then select Format.
If you want to format the volume with the default settings, select ok, in the Format dialog box, and then select ok again.
IMPORTANT: Before formatting the external hard drive, take back up of all your data because this operation can result in losing important data.

andre da costa say:

New computers that come preinstalled with Windows 7 often have what is called a recovery partition. This is used to reinstall the operating system in the event of a system crash. To access it, you will need to boot into when you start your computer by pressing a function key. This can be either F1, F2, F9, F10, F11, F12 or even DEL or Tab key. Consult the owners manual that came with your PC for instructions about how to reinstall Windows.

If your recovery partition is not available or damaged, you should contact the manufacturer of your computer and request a recovery disc set you can use to reinstall Windows 7. They might charge a small shipping and handling fee.

when i shop online, why do i get prices in dollars and not sterling - Microsoft Community

andywilson271 say:

when i shop online, why do i get prices in dollars and not sterling

When I shop online I keep getting prices in Dollars and not Sterling !! So annoying.

Is there something I am doing wrong. I don't shop on American or Australian sites,

its usually Amazon or Ebay. Please Help.

rajesh govind say:

 

Hi Andy,

 

This could be because you are not using the correct version of the Ebay or Amazon websites or the location settings on your computer are not set correctly. To show the prices in the shopping websites in Pound Sterling, you should be using the UK version of these websites. The UK version of most of the shopping websites ends with “.co.uk” and it is not “.com”. For eg: The UK version of Ebay would be “http://www.ebay.co.uk” and that of Amazon would be “http://www.amazon.co.uk/”.

 

You may also check the Home location settings saved on your computer. If it shows an some other country, then you may change it to “United Kingdom” and then save it. To do this, you may follow these steps:

 

a)       Press “Windows Logo” + “W” keys on the keyboard and type “Region” in the “Settings” search box.

b)       Click on the icon “Region”.

c)       Click on the “Location” tab.

d)       Choose “United Kingdom” as the “Home location” from its drop down menu, click on “Apply”, and then click on “OK”.

e)       Open the Amazon, EBay and any other shopping websites and check if they are opened in the correct version as according to your location and also check if they are showing the price in “Pound Sterling”

 

Hope this is helpful.
