charles kenyon say:

Since this is asked in the Word forum, I presume you are trying to open Word files.

Do NOT use Word to manipulate files on a USB / flash drive.  To make that clear:

  • Do not use Word to save to the drive
  • Do not use Word to open a document on the drive.
  • Do not use Word to edit a document on the drive.
  • Do not use Word to print a document on the drive.

If Word directly touches those files while they are on mobile media, your chances of losing your work goes up astronomically!

Instead, work on them in Word when they are on your hard drive. Copy them to/from the flash drive with Word closed and while in Windows. I have heard of similar problems with GoogleDrive and other cloud utilities.

How do I get rid of a blank page in word 2008? - Microsoft Community

susancarter say:

How do I get rid of a blank page in word 2008?

How to i eliminate a blank page in word document 2008?
bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
It helps if you first can accept the fact that there is no such thing as a page in the structure of a Word document. That's a plain fact despite all the design & development Microsoft has done to make you think otherwise :-) Users want to see pages -- Microsoft makes their wish come true.

I'm assuming that you are not working in Publishing Layout View. If you are, Pub Layout documents are a somewhat different story which calls for a different approach. That view is expressly designed to deceive you even more into being convinced of the existence of pages. My expectation, though, is that you're document is a regular Word document & that you're using Print Layout View.

What you actually have is some type of content -- visible or not -- which is causing a gap between the text that precedes it and the text that follows it. When printed, that gap will render as an empty sheet of paper, so it's being displayed on screen as an "empty page". You need to determine what the content is that is causing the separation & adjust accordingly.

Start by displaying the non-printing characters (¶) -- Either click the Show button bearing that icon on the Standard toolbar or use the Command+8 keyboard shortcut if they are not already displayed. This article describes the various non-printing characters & what they translate to in the flow of your document. It's written from a Windows perspective but it applies equally to Mac Word:

What do all those funny marks, like the dots between the words in my document, and the square bullets in the left margin, mean?

What you're most likely looking for is a manual Page Break. It will appear on the page preceding the blank one. Select & delete the ---------Page Break------- indicator & the document will close up.

There are other possibilities as well, such as:
  • Section Break (Next Page)
  • Formatting of the paragraph following the empty page (such as Page Break Before)
  • A large graphic object interfering with text flow
  • and a few other less likely possibilities
Reply with complete descriptive details if you continue to have a problem.
