Hi Angela,

There's a few ways to do this.  If you have Windows 8 Pro, you an do it using the built-in Remote Desktop abilities.  However, this is quite advanced: you have to know your external IP address that your internet service provider uses for your home, and you have to configure your home router to direct your remote desktop connect to the proper internal IP address on your home network. Plus, your internet provider can (and does) randomly change your external IP at any time, meaning that you would be rendered without a way to remote, or would need a way to keep track of those changes using another service.  I did this in the past using a service like Dynamic DNS so that no matter what IP address my provider used, I could always remote in.
