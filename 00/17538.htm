ken sheridan say: Firstly I would change the basis for returning rows all to all checkboxes being checked; it makes the logic of the query's WHERE clause simpler.  Assuming the Day column contains an integer number form 1 (Sunday) to 7 (Saturday)

WHERE ([Day] = 1 AND Forms!frm_SearchData!chk_Sun = TRUE)
      OR ([Day] = 2 AND Forms!frm_SearchData!chk_Mon = TRUE)
      OR ([Day] = 3 AND Forms!frm_SearchData!chk_Tue = TRUE)
      OR ([Day] = 4 AND Forms!frm_SearchData!chk_Wed = TRUE)
      OR ([Day] = 5 AND Forms!frm_SearchData!chk_Thu = TRUE)
      OR ([Day] = 6 AND Forms!frm_SearchData!chk_Fri = TRUE)
      OR ([Day] = 7 AND Forms!frm_SearchData!chk_Sat = TRUE);

Alternatively add another check box chk_All to select all days, in which case the SQL would be:

WHERE ([Day] = 1 AND Forms!frm_SearchData!chk_Sun = TRUE)

Even when you choose to delete your out-of-date security info, MS don't fully remove it for 30 days.
