How can I turn off the Save File Prompt? - Microsoft Community

gadgetgeekcan say:

How can I turn off the Save File Prompt?

Is there a way I can disable the prompt that asks if you want to save the opened Excel file, when working with Excel documents containing Visual Basic Scripts. I work with hundreds of Excel documents per week that require me to simply place a digital signature on them and forward them to our accounting department. When I attempt to close each file, I receive this prompt.  I never wish to save them.

Any help would be greatly appreciated!
j.e. mcgimpsey say: Simply having VBA macros will not trigger the Save prompt. XL will ask you to save whenever a value changes. Not sure what method you're using to "simply place a digital signature on them", but if it causes any change in the file, you'll get the prompt.

More likely, you have a volatile function in the sheet, which automatically recalculates when the workbook is opened.

Volatile functions: TODAY(), INDIRECT(), RAND(), NOW(), OFFSET(), CELL(), INFO() and, for older XL versions, INDEX(). 

Also, some non-volatile functions become volatile when explicit ranges aren't used. For instance:

   =SUMIF(A1:A10, ">0", B1:B10) and  =SUMIF(A1:A10, ">0", B1)

return the same result, but the first function is non-volatile and the second is volatile (because B2:B10 is not explicitly called out as a reference).

First, you can easily dismiss the "Save" prompt without clicking by typing CMD-d (for "Don't Save"). That may be easy enough to remove your frustration.

Another way, if you have a lot of these files to review at once is to, before you open the workbooks, open a new workbook, choose Preferences/Calculation and select Manually. Uncheck the Always calculate before saving checkbox. Click OK.  Now you should be able to open and close workbooks with volatile functions without being prompted to save. Just remember to return calculation to Automatically afterward.

Let's get this resolved. Some users have told us that the home screen or app tiles do not appear correctly the first time they use their Windows 10 Lumia. We are planning to address this with a software update, but in the meantime resetting your phone will resolve the problem.

In your case, you've probably missed out some buttons halfway when performing a hard reset. Here's the proper steps and what you need to do next after a hard reset:

  1. Disconnect the charger. Press and hold the Power and Volume Down keys simultaneously. Release the pressed keys when phone vibrates and then immediately press and hold the volume down button until the phone displays an exclamation mark (!) on the screen. Release the volume down key.
  2. Then press the volume and power keys in the following order: volume up, volume down, power, and volume down.
  3. Wait for your phone to reset, during the reset you will see gears rotating on the screen for up to 5 minutes, after which the screen goes blank for ~30 seconds and then your phone will restart.
