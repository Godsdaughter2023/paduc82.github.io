    Dropping lowest grade

    In the class that I teach, the students will take 7 major tests and over 30 chapter quizzes.  If the student has an average daily quiz grade that is higher than the lowest of the major grades, I will replace the lowest major grade with the average grade for the daily quizzes.

    EXAMPLE of Grades at completion of course:
    Daily Quiz Average = 84
    Major Test Grades (7 total):  72, 76, 80, 88, 86, 90, 84

    In the above example, the major test grade of 72 will be dropped and replaced with the average daily quiz grade of 84.

    I am looking for a formula that will execute this.  Also keep in mind that if the student has the same exact score for the lowest major grade,
    i.e., two grades of 72, I only drop and replace one test grade, not both.

    Off the top of my head, I am think about a formula as indicated below.  I am not sure if the AVERAGE function can be used in this.

    A1 = 84  (Daily Quiz Avg)
    B1:H1 = Major Test Grades

    Possible Formula:  =(SUM(B1:H1)-MIN(B1:H1)+A1) / 7

    Again, I am thinking that MIN will only reference one lowest number in the event there are two or more of the same lowest number.

    Is the above formula good or is there a better formula?

    Thanks,
    Les

    hansv mvp say:

    That's fine in itself - if the lowest score occurs more than once, subtracting MIN(...) will subtract that score only once.

    But you wrote that you wanted to replace the lowest score only if it was lower than the daily quiz average. To take that into account:

     

    =(SUM(B1:H1)+IF(A1>AVERAGE(B1:H1),A1-MIN(B1:H1),0))/COUNT(B1:H1)

    bernard liengme say:

    With the greatest respect: I think we need

    =(SUM(B2:H2)+IF(A2>MIN(B2:H2),A2-MIN(B2:H2),0))/COUNT(B2:H2)
