pamcaswell say:

Char or unlinked styles (such as Heading 1, Char) occur in W2003 and before, when  a different style is applied to part of a paragraph or when text  in a different style or formatting is pasted into part of a paragraph. The ways to avoid this in W2003 & before is through user training: to  paste as unformatted text and to be careful that nothing is selected when pasting (that is,  paste into the paragraph not over selected text--even a space).

I've used W2007 for  four years on client documents, and in my experience, styles do not unlink in W2007/10. 

(But because  the new improved linked styles are not supported in W2003 and before,  if you use linked styles to make run-in headings that can be  captured by the TOC feature, then when it is opened in W2003 & before, the styles unlinked. )

So another way to avoid them is by never allowing your files to be edited in W2003 and before. I am not able to train users and can't avoid receiving files from  W2003 users, so I clean up all files that come to me from the writers before I put them into my master.

 

Pam
