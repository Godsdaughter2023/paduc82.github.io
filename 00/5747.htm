Shared task is only availbe in Outlook for Windows, it won't sync to Outlook Web App nor Outlook for Mac which is by design.

In Outlook for Mac:

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

Office 2010 will use a different Product Key than the one Office 2007 uses.  When you redeemed the Technology Guarantee, you were given an Office 2010 Product Key.  You will need that Product Key to activate Office 2010 on a second PC or to reinstall it on your first PC.  Product Keys are as important as the software itself and you should be protecting them from loss as diligently as you would protect the PC or software on it by making multiple copies of the key and keeping the original in a safe place so it cannot get lost.

If you cannot locate the product key, tools like The Magic Jellybean or Belarc Advisor can look it up on your PC, but nothing replaces the original Certificate of Authenticity.

16 gigs of RAM is not very much, these days. 

Have another look at Activity Monitor. I bet you're using Swap space as RAM. That's using startup volume drive space as RAM, which is a lot slower than memory RAM.

Another RAM constraint is video RAM. Click on the Apple menu and choose About this Mac. For Graphics, does it say Intel...? If so, that's Apple's polite way of telling you that there is no graphics card at all and that your CPU is doing double duty as a processor and handling all the video RAM and screen drawing.

This is the long way for me to say that I think you may be right, that this is a RAM issue on two fronts: GPU and video.
