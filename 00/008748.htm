rich michaels - mvp (greatcirclelearning.com) say:

Form Controls on the Mac are much more limited versus those available on the PC. For example the legacy ActiveX controls are not available at all. It is not an exaggeration to say that the majority of Form Controls that run on a PC will not run on a Mac.

If you open the Developer ribbon on Word 2011 you will see what is available and you can then compare them to those available on a PC.

If form compatibility between environments is important for your project, then create the form on a Mac and not the other way around. You should also be aware that VBA macro compatibility between the environments can also be an issue. If your form controls trigger macros for processing, do not write those macros in Office 2013 believing they will run with Office 2011. The Application Object Models used for Word, Excel and PowerPoint in Office 2011 are closer in functionality to Office 2003 than anything else.

However, to be more accurate, I am trying to distribute the additional costs relative to the current cost of each pipe.
