I'm not sure buying a new version of Office would be the solution -- in fact, I'm fairly certain it would not be :-)

The error is a Timeout error generated by OS X, but it gives no indication what is specifically causing the timeout to occur. Combined with the message about the start-up disk being full I would tend to think there is a correlation. In order for OS X & your apps to perform they need a certain amount of memory (RAM) as well as a certain amount of free disk space. If the amount of RAM is limited, the greater the demand for free disk space.

Since you didn't provide any specs for your Mac I can't make a specific recommendation, but here are a few points to consider:

  • Depending on how long you've had the Mac it may be time to 'trade up' even though your current unit isn't really broken. That would also mean that you'd have to upgrade Office as well as other software. That may be an expense you don't want to incur, so...

  • Most importantly you need to free up space on your HD. Most users accumulate tons of stuff they don't really need -- old email, every PDF or document ever downloaded from the web or as an attachment, old software they no longer use, dozens of fonts they have no need for, etc. Delete anything you [or your Mac] don't have a need for & copy to CDs/DVDs any stuff you want to hold onto for posterity. You want to maintain at least 20-25% free space on your HD, but...
  • If you can't free enough space you may want to replace the HD with one of higher capacity.

a. user say:

Hi,

Some reasons why the keyboard keeps on popping up on the screen are: On-Screen Keyboard is on or Tablet mode is on.

We suggest turning off the following settings as it may be the one causing the issue:

 Turn off on screen keyboard

  1. Open Start menu and click Settings.

  2. Click on Ease of access and select Keyboard.

  3. Turn off On-Screen keyboard.

Turn off tablet mode if it is switched on.

  1. Open Start menu, type tablet mode settings in search bar and click on it.

  2. Turn off the tablet mode option and check if it helps.

Get back to us if you have other concerns.
