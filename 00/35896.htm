cmdrkeene say:

Technically Windows is moving the icons, but it's due to how Dell implemented the display switch mechanism.  When switched, the hardware tells Windows that your display is literally being unplugged and replaced with an entirely new monitor.  Because of this, Windows ensures that the icons are visible by re-aligning them to fit the "new" dimensions.

A company named StarDock makes a product called Fences that might alleviate some of the issue.  This program saves and arranges your desktop icons for you, and may be worth checking out.

Step 1: Run the game as administrator and check.

a.    Right click on the game file.

Step 2: Another possible cause of network connectivity problems is the mis-configuration or the corruption of the Winsock protocol on the computer. Reset the Winsock

To do this, follow these steps:

a)   Click Start, and in Start Search, type cmd.

b)   Right-click the cmd entry that appears in the search results, and then click Run as administrator. If you are prompted for an administrator password or for a confirmation, type the password, or click Continue.

c)    At the command prompt, type the following, and then press ENTER:

netsh winsock reset

d)   Then, you should see the following message:

e)   Successfully reset the Winsock Catalog. You must restart the computer in order to complete the reset.

f)    Restart the computer.

Step 3: Change the TCP/IP Settings: http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows7/Change-TCP-IP-settings
