jasles say:

Rather than use VBA, I'd suggest improving the speed of your current VLOOKUP formula first. That may be all that needs to be done. 

Right now you are having each VLOOKUP formula search every cell in column B for a match (which is over 1,000,000 rows). This is fine if you have a few VLOOKUPs running, but not for 8,000 or more times (which is what you are doing with the one formula above because you have VLOOKUP twice in the formula).

If you know that your Master File worksheet will only ever have 4,000 rows of data, use that or use a number a bit larger, like this:

=IF(ISNA(VLOOKUP($A5,Sheet1!$B1:$H4000,6,FALSE)="True"),"",VLOOKUP($A5,Sheet1!$B1:$H4000,6,FALSE))

I'd also change your formula to this, which will reduce the VLOOKUPs done to once per cell, rather than twice and still show a blank if there is no match found:

=IFERROR(VLOOKUP($A5,Sheet1!$B1:$H4000,6,FALSE),"")

Hi Toys Solis,

 

Welcome to community, about create a group for your email account on Outlook. If you have office 365 account and this mail account have admin permission, you can create group for your office 365 account, and you can create a group in Outlook from following:

 

  1. Open Outlook 2016.
  2. On the Home tab, choose New Group.


  1. In the Create Group window, enter the required information, including name, email address, description, classification, and privacy level, (Public or Private). You won't be able to change the group email address after you create the group.
  2. Select Advanced options and check the group conversations box to let members receive group emails in their personal inbox or keep it un-checked if you prefer that they access the group's mail only from the group's inbox, and then click Create.
  3. Add the members (you aren't able to add external users at this time), description, and a photo for the group, and then click OK. You can add members after the group is created as well.
  4. Click Add. You'll see your newly created group in the left nav and a Welcome email in your group's inbox.
