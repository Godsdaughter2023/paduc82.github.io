Hi chrisdowling2,

Thank you for reaching out to us with this concern and for providing visual examples, we’re happy to help!

Broad Match Modifier overview:

You’re absolutely correct, our Bing Ads Keyword match types help article does state that in order for Broad Match Modifier to perform, ad to be pulled in this case, is called Stop/function words, which I will explain below.

Stop/function word variation:

Stop/function words are words such as; is, the, on, for, in, you, your, own, my. When advertisers have keywords containing any of these words, Bing Ads will allow for the ad to be pulled without any of those words present, even if they contain a “+” in front of the word. For example, you have the keyword “+design + your + own + house”. As a result of Bing Ads’ stop/function word variations, the words ‘your’ and ‘own’ do not have to be present in the search query which is why the search term “design a house” triggered your ad for that keyword even though the words ‘your’ and ‘own’ are present in the original keyword. This is outlined in our Using broad match and broad match modifier Bing Ads help article.

Search Queries:

I would like to address the concern you expressed with some of the search terms that pulled your ad in the first example you provided. I see that your keyword “+design +my +blueprint” was pulled for the search term 8 stories building design plans and blue prints. This is due to the “broad” aspect of broad match modifier. While the words design and blueprint are in your keyword, the broad match aspect of BMM allows additional words such as 8 stories building plans to be in the search term and still pull your ad. Also, the close variants feature allowed for blue print to be recognized the same as ‘blueprint’.

Negative Keywords:

In the event that you truly aren’t comfortable with those close variants to your BMM keyword pulling your ad, you can always add those close variants to your negative keywords. Doing this will prevent your ad being pulled when these close variants are searched. For your convenience, I have included instructions on how to add negative keywords below:

  1. Click Campaigns located at the top of the page.
  2. Click the Keywords tab.
  3. Select View: Negative keywords.
  4. Click Campaign or Ad group.
  5. Click Add negative keywords.
  6. Select the appropriate campaign or ad group, then add your negative keywords as appropriate.

Please note that when adding negative keywords, you don’t need to use a hyphen ( - ) to specify that the keyword is negative. However, you can use brackets ( [ ] ) to identify exact match negative keywords and quotation marks ( “ “ ) to imply phrase match negative keywords.

I hope this helps! Please let me know if you have any additional questions or concerns as I’m happy to help!

dan at it associates say:

With the recent exception of Office Starter Edition 2010, no installation of Office 2010 is free.

Millions of Windows 7 computers came preinstalled with a Trial of Office 2010.  In many cases, you could turn the Trial installation into either a paid-for edition of Office 2010 (the three common editions were Home and Student, Home and Business, and Professional), or you could have activated a free edition of Office called Starter Edition.

If you had one of the paid-for editions, you would follow my instructions above to reinstall it.

However, since you said you did not pay for it, then you must have had Office Starter Edition 2010.  If so, your computer started out as a Windows 7 computer and it was upgraded to either Windows 8/8.1 and/or Windows 10.  If, after the upgrade, you refreshed the computer, removing all installed programs, unfortunately you have lost the ability to directly reinstall Office Starter Edition 2010, since Starter Edition will not install onto a Windows 8/8.1/10 installation.

In order to get Office Starter Edition 2010 back, you will have to back up and offload all of your data, then put the original software load of the operating system, drivers, and other programs back onto the computer using the manufacturer's recovery solution.  That process will put Office Starter Edition 2010 back onto the computer.  From that baseline, you will then upgrade to W10, which will retain the now-installed Office Starter Edition 2010.
