leannesggirg say:

Formula to Calculate New Stamp Duty

 Does anyone know how I can do this? 

£0-£125,000.00                            0%

£125,000.01 - £250,000.00           2%

£250,000.01 - £925,000.00           5%

£925,000.01 - £1,500,000.00        10%

Over £1,500,000.00                       12%

mike h.. say:

Hi,

With your purchase price in a1 try this.

=SUMPRODUCT(--(A1>{125000;250000;925000;1500000}), (A1-{125000;250000;925000;1500000}), {0.02;0.03;0.05;0.02})

mike h.. say:

Edison Pus,

Thanks for the excellent explanation of my formula. Where others sometimes make a mistake in trying to convert this formula to their own needs is with the 3rd Array which as you say is the percentage increments. What is important to note this that these increments are cumulative so in the original question the OP wanted these thresholds:-

£0-£125,000.00                            0%

£125,000.01 - £250,000.00           2%

£250,000.01 - £925,000.00           5%

£925,000.01 - £1,500,000.00        10%

Over £1,500,000.00                       12%

Therefore 3rd array is:-

{0.02;0.03;0.05;0.02}

Those trying to understand the formula should note that these are added up left to right in the array so first we get 0.02 or 2% then the next is 0.02+0.03 or 5% then 0.02+0.03+0.05 for 10% and lastly all 4 elements add up to 0.12 or 12%

So when doing the calculation everything > than 125000 gets 2% then everything > than 250000 gets an additional 3% etc.

Once again thanks for an excellent explanation.
