[quote user="Whip JR"]My comcast modem ... has one port out that goes to the Dir-815.[/quote]

Make sure that the cable from the Comcast Modem goes into the WAN port of the DIR-815. Make sure, that on this router, DHCP (and if supported) UPnP are turned ON and properly configured.

[quote user="Whip JR"]Connected to the Dir-815 I have the WGR614v9 AND the Dir-615[/quote]

Make sure, that on the WGR614 and DIR-615 DHCP is turned OFF! Then make sure that the cables coming from the DIR-815 are NOT plugged in the WAN ports of the WGR614 and DIR615, but on any LAN Port of the routers.

Using the LAN ports instead of the WAN port will bypass the routing and NAT functions of the devices, so you don't have a double NAT configuration that would impair your Multiplayer experience, letting the WGR614 and DIR615 routers work as switches or wireless access points instead of routers/gateways.

Basically, the only thing you need to set up is to disable DHCP in both "second tier" routers, and let the "primary" Router DIR-815 handle everything.
