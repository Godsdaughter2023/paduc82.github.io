end of win 10 insider preview

I originally upgraded to Windows 10 from a valid copy of Win 8.1.  I then joined the insider program since I thought it would be interesting. cbarnhorst say:

We are not near the end of the Insider Program.  We are near the release of the final Anniversary Update build.  Insider builds will resume following that when the next development cycle starts.

Once the final is released you will notice that you do not have a watermark in the lower right part of the screen.  This means you are on a public release of Windows 10.  You are then on the same Windows as the general public will be on July 29 and after.  You can stay on the public build by changing your Insider status in Update and Security to "Stop receiving insider builds" and you will leave the Insider Program.  No other action will be necessary since the Anniversary Update is NOT a preview build.

You will receive the Anniversary Update a week or more ahead of the general public as a perk for being an Insider.  Note that just because you receive the build through the Insider channel does not mean the build is a preview build.  A lot of people get confused about that.

What is about to happen is that three to four weeks before the public release on the 29th the final build flights to Insiders in the Fast Ring.  It is this early because of Microsoft's commitments to OEMs to release the final to them well ahead of the public release.  This is called "release to manufacturing."  The OEMs need a little time to test the final and begin installing it on new computers they will be shipping to customers.  At the same time the final goes to Software Assurance customers and MSDN subscribers.  

All this means there are only a few (possibly only one) preview builds left before we will be looking at the final ourselves.  However, don't expect Microsoft to come right out and say "this is the final."  We had to discover it for ourselves last year when Windows 10 first released.  We had build 10240 well ahead of the release date but did not realize at first that it was the final.  Long story.

The Windows Version you see when you type "winver" in Cortana/Search is already reading Windows version 1607 (the Anniversary Update) but the System Properties page is still showing "Windows 10 Pro Insider Preview" (or "Home") so 14379 is not the final because of this and that the watermark is still showing on the desktop.

The answer is the same. You have code in the On Current event that assigns the value of the tempvar to the control bound tot he Account number field.
