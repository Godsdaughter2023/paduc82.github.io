Private Sub Workbook_Open()

daniel g. grau say:
Assuming the second row has room on the page, this could solve your problem:

  • Place your cursor on the first paragraph of the second row
  • Select Format > Paragraph
  • Make sure it does not have a checkmark on "Page break before"

Paragraph attributes "Widow/Orphan control" and "Keep lines together" have no effect on paragraphs inside tables. Only "Page break before" and "Keep with next" have an effect, but only if they are attributes of the first paragraph of a row. Furthermore, "Keep with next"  will keep the whole row on the same page as the next row or paragraph (it doesn't work within it's own cell).

hansv mvp say:

The current macro expects the path/filename of the (first) attachment in cell B7. The key lines are

Dim rngAttachment As Range

and

Set rngAttachment = .Range("B7")

and

.Attachments.Add rngAttachment.Value

You can enter the path/filename of a second attachment in another cell, for example in cell B8. Add the following lines to the code, each below the corresponding one mentioned above

Dim rngAttachment2 As Range

Set rngAttachment2 = .Range("B8")

.Attachments.Add rngAttachment2.Value
