robert aldwinckle on forums say:

The problem now is the pop-up "Internet Explorer has stopped working.......?

"stopped working" is a euphemism for "has crashed" and the most likely explanation for that is interference by incompatible programs.  Does this occur in No Add-ons mode?  Does this occur if you boot in safe mode and try to reproduce it then?  Etc.  Those procedures are attempts to diagnose the scenario by fault isolation.  Another tack would be to examine your crash details for clues.  In fact, in the case where the crash is classed as an APPCRASH (e.g. in Reliability Monitor, Events log, or msinfo32 WER section) you may find an associated Report.WER file which also contains a list of loaded modules for the crashing thread.  Such a list can be helpful in knowing what to target for your fault isolation testing.
