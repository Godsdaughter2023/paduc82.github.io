a. user say:

My point exactly - it has nothing to do with where you live, so why would you bring that up as a possibility?

If you are using an LCD/LED monitor you should know that these monitors have a native resolution equal to the number of pixels in the monitor. That native resolution is the video resolution you should be using. Based on the information you provided, the native resolution of your monitor seems to be 1,600 x 900.

If you try to operate the monitor at anything other than its native resolution, the graphics card or chip in your computer has to process the image to make it fit into the non-native resolution. At the very least, the image will not look sharp and at the very worst the graphics chip will be unable to work with the non-native resolution and your monitor will simply go black.

In addition, VGA is the wrong connection to use with a LCD/LED monitor because that provides an analog signal that the monitor then has to convert to a digital signal. The conversion requires additional processing that can interfere with the image on the screen.

To sum up, you should be using a HDMI connection and a video resolution of 1,600 x 900. If your graphics card or chip can't produce that resolution - it must very quite old - then you need to buy a graphics card that can deliver that resolution.

Most any modern graphics card has an HDMI connector, but if your monitor does not have a HDMI connector - it's an older monitor - then you can also use a DVI connection, which is a digital connection. You'll need a DVI cable.
