Although this is well within Word's capability, it's larger than most laser or inkjet physical printers can handle, so their drivers don't allow the selection of Tabloid in Word's paper size dialog. If you don't have a large physical printer, you must first open the Print page and select a software printer (for example, one of the PDF programs such as Nitro Reader) that doesn't limit the page size. With that selection, Word will give you a Tabloid choice in the Page Layout > Size list.

Next, click the launcher button in the bottom right corner of the Page Setup group on the Page Layout tab. In the dialog, on the Margins tab, choose Landscape orientation and set the Multiple Pages box to Book Fold.

Save the resulting document as a template. When you base a new document on that template, the pages will appear in sequential order inside Word, but will appear in the correct order for the folded "booklet" in the PDF. If necessary, you can take the PDF to a computer that does have a large printer.
