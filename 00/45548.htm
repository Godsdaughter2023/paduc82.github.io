lava k say:

Hi karen57, 

Welcome to Microsoft Windows Vista Community site.  

Follow the step below to reset your time for Screen Saver
1. Open the Start Menu..
2. In the Start Search box, type regedit and press Enter.
3. Click on Continue in the UAC prompt.
4. In regedit, go to: HKEY_USERS\.DEFAULT\Control Panel\Desktop
5. In the right pane, right click on ScreenSaveTimeOut and click Modify.
6. Type in how long you want the wait time to be for in seconds and click on OK.

NOTE: The default time is 600 seconds (10 minutes). Just multiply how many minutes by 60 for the amount of seconds to enter.

7. Close regedit.

Follow the step below to change a screen saver

1. Open Screen Saver Settings by clicking the Start button, clicking Control Panel, clicking Appearance and Personalization, clicking Personalization, and then clicking Screen Saver.
2. Under Screen saver, in the drop-down list, click the screen saver you want to use.
3. Click Preview to see what your chosen screen saver will look like.
4. Click OK.

Follow the step below to create your own screen saver

You can create a personalized screen saver that displays selected pictures and videos as a slide show.

1. Open Screen Saver Settings by clicking the Start button, clicking Control Panel, clicking Appearance and Personalization, clicking Personalization, and then clicking Screen Saver.
2. Under Screen saver, in the drop-down list, click Photos.
3. Click Settings to choose the pictures and videos you want in your slide show and select other options. After you make your changes, click Save.
4. Click OK.

Follow the link which illustrates how to change screen saver: http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows-vista/Change-screen-saver

obsessive power say:

Hi,

Game updates can only be downloaded to the drive said game is installed to. If a drive is full or nearly full you won't be able to update the game.

For example: The Game And Apps screen shows total drive space across all storage. So, if you have say 5GB free on internal and 595GB on external, it will show 600GB.

If the game that needs to be updated is installed on the drive with only 5GB, but the update is 6GB, you would need to either free up space on the drive or move the whole game to a drive with free space in order to update it.

Power cycling won't fix this.
