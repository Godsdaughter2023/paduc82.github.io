jay freedman say:

How was the document created? Did you use the mail merge feature, or were the envelopes created through the Mailings > Envelopes dialog and added to the document one at a time?

If it was the result of a mail merge, then each envelope is page 0 in a separate section. That would account for the difficulty in printing -- first, because the "1-8" page range doesn't include 0; and second because all the page numbers are the same, so Word tries only once to print anything.

Instead, tell the print dialog to print the desired sections by entering s1-s8, then s9-s16 and so on.

The blank page may be caused by a section break at the end of the last envelope, or by a "separator page" selected in the printer driver's properties. Look at http://wordfaqs.mvps.org/BlankPage.htm for suggestions.

julie fka profjulie say:

The Microsoft account is used to sync more than just email.  When a sync is performed on the Microsoft account lots of other things happen in the background that you may not be aware of.  For instance:

  • New & updated contacts, appointments and tasks are synced between the phone and the Microsoft account
  • Statuses are updated between the social networks that are linked to the Microsoft account (such as Twitter, LinkedIn, etc.)
  • Installed Apps might be updated
  • Automatic Backups may be performed
  • And there could be other maintenance issues performed on the phone.

It's true that the Manual option is not available for the Microsoft account, but you can select "Daily" or "based on my usage" if you want to be conservative about how how often the account is synced on the phone. I've set the option to "Daily" on my phone since, at the very least, I want to sync this data between my phone on a daily basis.  I think the "based on my usage" adjusts to suit your own personal habits and needs.

The only reason you'd ever need a live tile for the account to show up on the home screen is if you were syncing email....in which case that's how you'd go into the account to view and work with email.  All the other things that are synced via the account can be gotten to through another app or another setting on the phone.  If you don't sync email at all and you want to update the account manually you can always go to Settings > email+accounts > then press & hold your finger on the Microsoft account and select the "sync" option.  I do this when I've spent a lot of time working with contacts, appointments or tasks and I want to sync all my changes between the phone and the Microsoft account (and in turn to Outlook on my computer).
