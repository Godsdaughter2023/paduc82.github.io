Hi,

 

Before resolving 0x800ccc0f error it is important to known the reasons behind these error messages so that they can be rectified properly. Some of the reasons are:

 

  • Account setting is incorrect.
  • E-mail item on the POP3 server is damaged.
  • AV software configuration is incorrect.
  • Personal firewall software configuration is incorrect etc.

 

So, in order for us to resolve this issue, it is important to check if the POP3 settings are correct. For now, please reconfigure your account and see if this works.

 

To removed the account, kindly refer to this link for the instruction on how to do it.

 

To configure a non-Windows Live Hotmail account in Windows Live Mail:

 

1. For 2011 version of Windows Live Mail, go to Account tab in the ribbon menu then click Email. For 2009 version of Windows Live Mail, click the Add e-mail account link on the lower left corner of the screen.

2. Type your e-mail address, password, and display name on their respective boxes and then click Next.

If Windows Live Mail cannot detect your account's settings, you have to manually configure your account and fill out the Incoming Server Information and Outgoing Server Information sections for your e-mail account. You may need to contact your ISP to find out some or all of the following information about your account:

  • The type of e-mail server that you use (POP3, IMAP, or HTTP).
  • The name and details of your incoming e-mail server.

o    Your incoming server name might be mail.<domain>.com or pop.<domain>.com, where <domain> is the name of your e-mail provider's domain.

  • Any relevant security settings.
  • The name and details of your outgoing e-mail server (POP3 and IMAP accounts only).

o    Your outgoing server name may be smtp.<domain>.com, where <domain> is the name of your e-mail provider's domain.

Notes:

o    For Gmail accounts, you must change first your Gmail settings to enable POP access.

o    For Yahoo! accounts, you should have a premium Yahoo! account to be able to access e-mail from Yahoo!

 

3.  Mark the Set this account as the default mail account as per your preference.

4.  Click Finish to complete set-up.
