a. user say:

Does HP support your computer on Windows 10?

If HP decided to not support your computer on Windows 10, and you can't replace unsupported hardware with hardware that is supported, then it's only a matter of time before your computer will stop working properly on Windows 10. HP decides whether or not to support your computer on Windows 10 - it isn't Microsoft's decision to make.

In this case, one of two things can happen:

If you're among the lucky ones, Microsoft will figure out (via the telemetry it receives from your computer) that your hardware can no longer receive upgrades and stop sending them to you.

If you're among the unlucky ones, Microsoft won't be able to make this determination and will try to continue installing upgrades, and the result will not be pretty.

Here's where it gets a little murky: It's in your interest and Microsoft's interest for you to have the latest version of Windows. I won't get into why this is so - too much arguing back and forth - but it has nothing to do with money. (Upgrades are free forever.) Eventually Microsoft will start giving you the evil eye about upgrading or replacing your computer so you can continue to receive upgrades.

We still don't know why your computer won't upgrade. There's always a why, either in the form of an error message that you see or that you can find in Event Viewer. Knowing why your computer won't update is really important to formulating a solution. Otherwise you'll be offered piles of generic suggestions that may or may not be of any help to you.
