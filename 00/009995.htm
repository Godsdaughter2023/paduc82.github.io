roady [mvp] say:

1) No, not true. Outlook.com accounts in Outlook 2013 are configured as EAS accounts which synch Mail, Contacts, Calendar and Tasks. It sounds like you had it configured as a POP3 account instead.

2) No IMAP is needed at all. Outlook makes an Exchange account connection which is a much richer protocol than IMAP. IMAP for instance doesn't support Contacts, Calendar and Tasks folders and lots of other feature which Exchange offers. Mobile devices can connect to Exchange mailboxes via the EAS protocol which is a slimmed down (mobile optimized) version of the Exchange protocol.

3) In Outlook 2013, there is a sync slider for Exchange accounts which allows you to sync 1, 3, 6, 12 or 24 months worth of email or simply all emails. Mobile devices come with a similar option for EAS accounts. Outlook.com in Outlook can be configured to cache either 1 month or all emails.

4) If you already own licenses of Office 2013, then you won't necessarily need an Office 365 subscription which comes with the client software as well. If you want a mailbox with your own domain, then an Exchange Online subscription might already suffice for you. From the information provided, a free Outlook.com account will probably do in your case as well. You can sign up for a new @outlook.com address or even use your current email address with it. For details see:

http://www.howto-outlook.com/howto/outlookcompersonaladdress.htm

You won't need to use Chrome at all on your Android device to access your email. There is an Outlook.com app (and also an OWA app) for Android which automatically configured the account for you just by signing in with your credentials. Synching options are available within the app.

Configuring Outlook on your desktop with an Outlook.com or Exchange account is much easier than dealing with a POP3 or IMAP account. All you have to do is provide your name, email address and password and Outlook will configure itself and build a local cache from the server data. As said before; mail, contacts, calendar and tasks folders are synched and there is no need to delete anything twice.

The issue that you had before where private and business emails got mixed up was because you had your POP3 account set to deliver to your Exchange mailbox.

Also note that various phones (including Windows Phones) support the Remote Wipe feature which allows you to remotely empty and reset your device to factory defaults in case of loss or theft.

njsailor say:

I live in China so this was the first order of business on my ATT Tilt. It's actually fairly easy as long as you have the font files.

First, I put the fonts in \windows\fonts to keep them separate from the fonts that are on ROM and in the Windows directory.

  • Add or change the following registry key:
    [HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\Software\Microsoft\FontPath]
    "FontPath"="\\Windows\\Fonts"

Then enable font linking for the system font. In my case, this was Tahoma and simsun.ttc for simplified Chinese. Traditional Chinese would need a different font file. There are also other simplified Chinese fonts that use less space as simsun.ttc is 10 Mb.

  • Check your system font key to identify the font.
    [HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\System\GDI\SYSFNT]
    "Nm"="Tahoma"
  • Add or change the following registry key:
    [HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\Software\Microsoft\FontLink\SystemLink]
    "Tahoma"="\\Windows\\Fonts\\simsun.ttc,NSimSun"

Since some system fonts have Unicode definitions that you do not want to use, add a skip table to force the use

of the linked font.

  • Add or change the following registry key:
    [HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\Software\Microsoft\FontLink\SkipTable]
    "Tahoma"="005c,00a5,007e,0391-03c9,2026,2116,221a,25a0-25ff"

Some other comments:

  • I tried to point the FontPath to my storage card without success. It seems the fonts need to be in regular storage. WM6 searches Windows and then Windows\fonts by default when no FontPath is defined.
  • I am not sure the skip table is necessary with the Tahoma font that came with my phone. It is a condensed version (c.170k) and may not contain any extended Unicode characters. I needed the skip table because I have installed a standard Tahoma font (c.380k) as I also needed Thai characters on my phone.
  • Standard warning about editing the registry. Be particularly careful not to corrupt your Sysfont key as you will need to use the backup that I strongly recommend you make before attempting any of this.
  • Fonts from a Windows PC can be transferred to WM6 but I am not sure of the licensing issues.

Hope this helps.

Graham
