Sub CopyCSV_in_XLSX_Append()
'Oct 27, 2016
Const sFileCSV As String = "c:\users\tasos\desktop\folder csv\file csv.csv" '<< change path and file name /CSV
Const sFileXLSX As String = "c:\users\tasos\desktop\folder xlsx\append file.xlsx" '<< change path and file name /XLSX
Const wsName As String = "Sheet1" '<< sheet name in xlsx workbook /target sheet
Dim wbCSV As Workbook, wbXLSX As Workbook
Dim r As Long
Application.ScreenUpdating = False
Set wbXLSX = Workbooks.Open(sFileXLSX) 'open  xlsx wb
r = wbXLSX.Sheets(wsName).Cells.Find(What:="*", SearchOrder:=xlByRows, SearchDirection:=xlPrevious).Row + 1 'in xlsx sheet1, find the last non empty row plus one
Workbooks.OpenText Filename:=sFileCSV, DataType:=xlDelimited, Semicolon:=False, Local:=True '<< open csv wb //change semicolon true/false as needed
Set wbCSV = ActiveWorkbook
wbCSV.Sheets(1).UsedRange.Copy wbXLSX.Sheets(wsName).Cells(r, 1) 'copy data from csv to xlsx/append
wbXLSX.Save 'save xlsx
wbXLSX.Close False 'close xlsx
wbCSV.Close False 'close csv
Application.ScreenUpdating = True
MsgBox "done"
End Sub

Ultimately I am going to suggest that you abandon Publisher and move to a different program to produce your website. As of Publisher 2010 MSFT began to deprecate the web building functionality of Publisher. As of Pub 2013 there are only remnants left.

New computer monitors and screens are higher resolution than they used to be which means that the text size is so small it is hard to read at 96 dpi. And thus my original statement in this long reply. You should move to a different program to produce your website. It is not worth your time to keep working around the limitations of working with the hobbled Publisher 2013 IMHO.

jack cook - aka help_line say:

Because you are syncing with MyPhone, if an item is removed from your device it will be Archived at the next sync.  That is the nature of the MyPhone service.  It is a safeguard in case of an error.  If you do not want them in the Archive, then occasionally go into the Archive and delete those items you no longer want.

 

From the MyPhone FAQ:

Archiving is a way to free up space on your phone. An “archived” item remains in the archive section of your My Phone account, but is removed from the phone. To restore an archived item to the phone, sign in to your My Phone account, select the Archive view from the left panel, click or tap the item, then click Restore. The item will be transferred to your phone the next time that you sync.

Previously synced items that are deleted from your phone are automatically moved to the Archive folder in your My Phone account.

Items deleted from the Archive are permanently deleted.
