Hi ScottMollenkopf,

 

The characters you are asked to enter is part of the Hotmail Human Interactive Proof (HIP) validation process. This challenge is used to reduce abuse of Hotmail accounts. Some people use computers to set up their accounts to send unwanted messages.

 

Please note that HIP puzzles are issued on accounts that meet criteria based on the following:

> The number of messages that you send

> Key words in your sent messages that are found in spam messages

> The number of links and attachments that you send

HIP challenges decrease the amount of outgoing spam and help protect you from computers that try to obtain your identity and break in to your account. In these situations, this validation verifies that the user of the account is an actual person and not a computer.   

 

The following are facts and tips about the HIP validation process:

- You may receive multiple HIP challenges in one day or every few days. The number of challenges that you receive depends on the number and types of e-mail messages that you are sending. However, the number of challenges should decrease over time.

- You must correctly enter the text on the screen to be able to access your account. If you have any concerns about seeing the picture, and if your computer has speakers, click the Audio link. An audio file will play the numbers and the letters that you are supposed to enter.

- You will be helping us in our ongoing fight against unsolicited messages or spam.
