Ok... So I've been trying to get this to work on my own and here's what I've discovered.

 

I'm running Windows 7 Desktop with iTunes.  Almost all of my media is iTunes as I'm migrating to WP8 out of curiosity.

It seems the Windows Phone App crashes when it encounters media in the iTunes music library that is not music.  Let me explain...

When you purchase an album on iTunes, it typically includes the CD Liner files, as well as some additional video files (like a music video or documentary or something).  If you notice, these are all stored in your iTunes Music Library.

 

Windows Phone App does not allow you to sync individual songs; just albums.  When it is syncing these albums and hits a video or text type of file, the app crashes.

 

If you go through your iTunes Music Library (in iTunes not the actual file folder) and right click those additional files (usually identifiable by some sort of tag at the end like a video symbol or document symbol) and select delete from Library BUT NOT from the computer itself (in other words do not trash them), the Windows App will re-update itself and will no longer crash during sync.
