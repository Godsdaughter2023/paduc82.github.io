toddallcock say:

While Zune does not sync eBooks, that doesn't mean you can read eBooks on your Windows Phone.  Because of the "sandbox" security model Windows Phone uses, apps are responsible for importing and exporting their own data.

 

If your eBooks are unencrypted ePubs, the best solution currently is the app "Freda" in the marketplace.  It is an excellent reader app, and can download your books from your Skydrive (I keep an "ebooks" folder on my Skydrive to use with the app), from free online resources like Feedbooks, or from a Calibre server on your PC. 

 

(Calibre is an excellent, free eBook conversion/management app for your PC- it's sort of an iTunes or Zune for eBooks, and can also be configured as a server to share your eBooks from your PC to other devices.  I used to do this for Freda until Jim added Skydrive support a few months ago after MS opened up APIs for that. Setting up a Calibre server is more complicated than just throwing books in a Skydrive folder.)

 

Try the free version of Freda out and see if it meeds your needs, and if it does, throw the developer, Jim Chapman, a bone and upgrade to the ad-free version for a buck- he's very responsive to users via email and often helps people out with their eBook related questions in these forums.

a. user say:

Hi Josephine,

Welcome to Microsoft Community.

I understand that you want to block email addresses that are sending junk emails to your own account using accounts same as yours but with different domains. Let me assist you with this.

We can create a rule that will delete emails from accounts with different domain names. Please follow the steps below on how to do this:

  1. Go to your Inbox.
  2. Click the gear icon and select Manage rules.
  3. Click New.
  4. Under Step 1: On the first box, choose Sender's address; on the second box, choose begins with; then on the third box, type in the start of the account name that you want to block.
  5. Under Step 2, choose the radio button Delete these messages—don't even put them in my Deleted folder. 
  6. Click Save.

You can also delete emails from those accounts by using the Sweep option. Here's how:

  1. Go to your Inbox.
  2. Check the spam email and click the Sweep option on the Menu bar.
  3. Select Delete v all from Inbox and block future emails.
  4. Click Sweep.
