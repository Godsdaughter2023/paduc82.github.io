scottgem say:

A principle of relational databases is that data exists in one place only. That you use queries to combine data from related tables. As Don said, what you want is easily achieved using a query. 

However, I question the design of your tables. Assuming that a customer may have multiple jobs, but a job is associated with only one customer then you should have tables like this:

tblCustomer: CustomerID (PK Autonumber), ClientName, CustName, Address, etc.

tblJobs: JobNo (PK), CustomerID (FK), SiteContact, JobType, etc.

Then your query would join on CustomerID so you can see all jobs for a customer.

You can do something like this:

 

Sub Test()
    Worksheets(2).Select
    Worksheets(4).Select Replace:=False
    Worksheets(5).Select Replace:=False

End Sub

 

Specifying Replace:=False adds the worksheet to the selection, keeping the previously selected sheet(s).
