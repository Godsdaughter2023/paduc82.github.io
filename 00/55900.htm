As I pointed out before, Windows 7 devices can print to this shared printer over the network with no problems, so that would implicate Windows 8. In case anyone is wondering, all devices, including the Windows 8 laptop, on an the same workgroup. As a I also pointed out in my original post, "fixing" the problem is relatively easy, getting it to stop re-occuring what it needed.

Here is what I believe is happening. One of the things that got revamped in Windows 8 was printing. Most of development and testing would be focused on the most common configurations. New network printers are going to have their own network card, either ethernet or wifi and most network printing for new printers will be to printers directly attached to the network. Conversely, a smaller portion of network printing is to printer shared from a a PC. In instances where people are printing to a printer attached directly to their computer (say, via USB) a good amount of these will be single PC home user with no need for network sharing. The technology of sharing a printer from a PC is still officially a feature but is on its way to being a somewhat marginalized legacy technology.

Focusing on direct non-shared printing will take care of the bulk of home users (using USB) and businesses (using ethernet and wifi). Very small businesses that fall between these two big categories will be the group most likely to use shared printing and will be hurt by the unofficial deprecation of this feature. People are not very keen to spend money to replace printers that are still otherwise working perfectly well. My advice, and I have a personal stake in this, is that if Microsoft wants to create a favorable impression of their OS is that they keep printer sharing going a bit longer and allocate the manpower to properly test and debug printer sharing for Windows 8.
