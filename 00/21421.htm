Krishna wrote:
I observe that the CPU utilization shoots upto 25-30% during this time.

You probably have a "multi-core" computer, and only one "core" is 100% busy with the Excel calculations.  If you have a 4-core computer and one "core" is 100% busy, Task Manager will display that the computer is 25% busy.

joeu2004 say: I wrote:
Others have also noted that lots of Condition Formatting formulas might have this effect.

Not quite.  The recalculation of CFs is complicated and complicated to explain.

Suppose that a single CF rule with a formula applies to the range A1:C2 (6 cells) [2].  If directly (edited), the CF formulas are recalculated for all 6 of A1:C2.

That is true for both Manual and Automatic calculation mode.  The only difference is the
"Say what?!"  In Manual mode, all of the CF formulas are recalculated only once, as we might expect.  But in Automatic mode, the CF formulas appear to be recalculated multiple times(!) [1].

But no other CF formulas are recalculated, unless those cells are modified indirectly in Automatic calculation mode.

In Manual mode, a CF formula is not recalculated if it applies to cells outside the range that is modified directly (edited), even if the CF formula references that range.


-----
[1] I am being purposely vague and incomplete because I stumbled across some apparent defects when the CF formula references a (non-volatile) UDF that uses MsgBox.  Eventually, Excel crashed.  (Whether or not MsgBox usage is relevant is TBD.)  So there is a possible "observer effect" (often misidentified as the Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle) in my conclusions.


[2] When I speak of "a" CF rule that "applies to the range", I mean the single CF rule, not separate CF rules that are applied to cells in the range individually, even if the CF rule is the same.
