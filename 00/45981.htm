Trouble with an Xbox Gift card/Currency in your account? - FAQ - Microsoft Community

inb4 ownage say:

Trouble with an Xbox Gift card/Currency in your account? - FAQ

Hey Xbox Members!

Do you have a question about using gift cards or Currency Stored Value (CSV) on your account?  If so, be sure to check out these handy FAQ's!

REDEEMING

I received a Gift card, how do I redeem it on my Xbox One or 360?

Xbox One 

  • Power on your Xbox One
  • Go to "Store"
  • Once you're there, scroll down to "Browse all games"
  • Select "Use a code"
  • If you have a Kinect and there is a QR code on the card, select the Kinect option, it's much easier and faster!
  • If you want to type in ther 25 character code, select the "25 Character code" option
  • Confirm it.
  • You're all set!

Xbox 360

  • On the Home screen, press the "Xbox Guide" button in the center of your controler.  NOTE: The big center button on your controller
  • Go to the left once and select "Redeem code"
  • Type in the 25 character code
  • Confirm it
  • You're all set!

Xbox.com

  • Go to http://xbox.com/redeemcode/
  • Sign into the Microsoft account you want to use the currency on
  • Click the "Redeem code" option
  • Type in the 25 character code
  • Confirm it
  • You're all set!

I'm getting an error message when I try to redeem the currency

Here are a few things to try to resolve this issue

  1. Try to redeem the code again
  2. If you're redeeming it on Xbox.com, give it a go on the console instead or vise-versa
  3. Check that it's a 25 character code
  4. Make sure you're not putting any spaces in the code or dashes
  5. Try to wait 24 hours; some codes can take up to 24 hours from the initial purchase to activate
  6. If all else fails, please go ahead and reach out to Xbox Phone Support at 1-800-469-9269 (US & Canada) All other residence, please go to http://support.xbox.com/contact-us/ and request a call.

I redeemed a Gift card, but the funds aren't showing in my account.  Where are they?

Here are some things to try

  1. Double check the currency again
  2. If you're on the console, power cycle your Xbox One/360 and try again.  NOTE:  If you're on Xbox One, please see: How to Power cycle your Xbox One!
  3. Try to go to https://account.microsoft.com/billing/payments/
  4. See if the currency shows there
  5. While you're still on that page, click "Payment & Billing" and select "Billing info"
  6. Depending on what region you are in, make sure it only shows one profile there, for example, if you're in the US, it will say: US - Personal account
  7. If you redeemed the token in the US and purchased it from another region (Such as Canada) it will not show.
  8. If all else fails, please go ahead and reach out to Xbox Phone Support at 1-800-469-9269 (US & Canada) All other residence, please go to http://support.xbox.com/contact-us/ and request a call.

PURCHASING

I am not being given the option to use the currency to purchase a Subscription.  Why?

Apologies, but at this time, Microsoft does not allow the use of CSV to purchase a subscription.  The reason is because by default, the subscription renews and CSV is not a valid payment instrument.

It wont let me purchase an Xbox 360 Game using currency on Xbox.com!

Sorry, but you can not purchase 360 games on Xbox.com using currency

I'm trying to purchase a game on my Xbox One or 360 and it's asking for a credit card!  Why?

You actually don't have to put in your credit card information to use currency, but you do have to enter your billing information (i.e. Name, address, zip code, etc)

How do I use the currency to make a purchase on Xbox One?

If you can use the currency to purchase the content, it will automatically be the default payment option.  If you don't have enough currency to cover the entire purchase, you will need to use a credit/debit card to pay off the remaining balance.

How do I use the currency to make a purchase on Xbox 360?

All you have to do is select the payment instrument first, then before confirming the purchase, you will be prompted with a message saying something along the lines of "You have XX.XX dollars on your account, would you like to use that towards this purchase?"

I'm getting an error message when I purchase a $10 game and I have $11 in currency!  Why?

Some regions and states have sales tax, if the currency is not enough to cover the tax amount, you will receive an error message and you can not split the tax purchase.

It wont let me use my Gift card to purchase something on the Microsoft store!  Why?

Xbox Gift cards can only be used to make purchases from the Xbox Store and can not be used on the Microsoft Store towards a purchase.

I have a $10 promotion code from the Microsoft Store, can I apply this to a purchase on the Xbox Store?

Sorry, but you can not use promotions tokens on the Xbox Store.  You would need to purchase the content on the Microsoft Store website to use the promotion code.

Does my currency expire?

If you purchased the currency or someone else did for you, it does not expire.  However, if you received the currency from the Xbox LIVE Rewards program, it may have an expiration date.  See http://rewards.xbox.com/

I have a good idea for a new feature with using Xbox Gift cards or would like to submit feedback!  Who should I report it to?

Great!  Microsoft loves to hear about your feedback.  Please go to http://feedback.xbox.com/ and post your ideas there!

No, please be sure to spend any remaining currency in your account before migrating your account region.  The currency can not be refunded.

Why the Updates:

Because Windows 10 is not intended to be a static platform in the sense that previous versions of Windows were.

Twice per year there are going to be Creators Updates (aka MAJOR Updates).

These will occur in the Spring and Fall.

There will also be monthly updates and several times per day you can usually update your Windows Defender Antivirus Definitions as well.

This is not going to change.

Incidentally, now that Apple has unleashed its macOS High Sierra platform the folks over there are suffering as well with update issues.

(I find it difficult to restrain my tears of sympathy for them).

This is what progress is like, you know. The old gives way to the new.

For this reason manufacturers of older hardware are going to have to release updated software and firmware drivers so that their devices will continue to function as the various Operating Platforms (whether Windows 10 or High Sierra or whatever) continue to evolve.

Unfortunately there is often a lag in these things. The best thing you can do is contact the manufacturer and tell them to get it in gear.

Some, like Canon, haven't released an updated driver since 2015.

Others are more responsive to the changing technological landscape.

In any case, the first step should be to open your Device Driver, find out who made the darned sound card on your device 

and make sure that THAT is up to date.

Then worry about your speakers and the rest of it.
