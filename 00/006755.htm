How do I go to quarrenteen and delete quarenteed files - Microsoft Community

ellie kesselman say: First, right click on the little green icon that is Microsoft Security Essentials, probably on the lower right side of your screen. Click "Open". 

MSE will open to the "HOME" tab, the one on the far left. Next to that is "Updates", then "History' and finally "Settings".
You want the "History" tab. Click on that.

You should see three selections, in this order
  • All Detected Items: View all problem files that MSE found on your computer
  • Quarantined Items: View all files that were detected as problems and quarantined but not deleted
  • Allowed Items: Files that you told MSE to allow based on your settings.
Okay, now click on the "Quarantined Items". You'll see anything that MSE found there, in a list. These files stay there until you decide what you want to do with them. So now you've found the quarantined files.

rick rothstein, mvp - excel say:

If I understand your layout correctly, you do not need that hidden table... we can construct the apartment number from the labels in Column A and Row 1. My assumption about your visible table... the A thru M labels are in cells B1 thru N1 and your 1 thru 6 labels are in cells A2 thru A7. Here is the VB event code needed to automatically put the apartment number in cell A1 for the cell that selected in range B2:N7 (where I am assuming you are filling in your package received numbers)...

 

Private Sub Worksheet_SelectionChange(ByVal Target As Range)
  If Not Intersect(Target(1), Range("B2:N7")) Is Nothing Then
    Range("A1") = Cells(Target(1).Row, "A").Value & Cells(1, Target(1).Column).Value
  Else
    Range("A1").ClearContents
  End If
End Sub
