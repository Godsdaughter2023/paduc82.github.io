anthony davis (adavis) say:

Hello, YourMelloFello,

I am the official support representative for Killer Networking.

This has been an issue that pops up regularly with Windows Feature Updates. The problem is that the BIOS or the operating system is not enumerating your USB devices correctly. Bluetooth is actually a Wi-Fi device that communicates through the Universal Serial Bus. These steps will usually resolve the issue:

  1. Visit the support site for your machine's manufacturer and download all driver updates, but especially those for the BIOS, chipset drivers, and USB drivers. The USB drivers may not be listed, as they may be rolled into the chipset drivers. Run and install those packages, following the directions therein. You will need to follow the manufacturer's instructions on how to flash the BIOS. 
  2. Once you have those drivers in place, check your Device Manager to make sure that the Bluetooth adapter is listed. You can access your Device Manager by right-clicking Start, and clicking Device Manager. Bluetooth will be under its own heading. If it is not listed, you will need to perform a power drain on the machine. 
  3. To perform a power drain, unplug the machine from the wall, then remove all other power from the machine. If the battery is easily removed, remove the battery. If not, you may need to drain the battery through usage. Either way, once the machine is drained of power, push and hold the power button on your machine for ten seconds, to make sure that the power is completely drained from the machine.
  4. Replace the battery, if removed, and plug the machine into the wall, and turn it on. Once the machine has booted back up, check the Device Manager. The Bluetooth should now appear in the Device Manager.
  5. Visit our website at http://killernetworking.com/driver-downloads/category/bluetooth to download the latest Bluetooth driver for your device. Once it is downloaded, run the installer to install the driver. 

This should resolve the issue. If you continue to have issues, or have any other issues with a Killer product, please feel free to reach out to us directly at http://killernetworking.com/about/contact

-- Anthony with Killer Networking
