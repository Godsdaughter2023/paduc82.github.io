I have been utterly frustrated by the problem of Drop Down Menus scrolling on their own and the inablilty to select one of the choices from the drop down menu.  Though this thread is now months old, perhaps this posting will help someone else who has this problem, because I have a solution! 

On my Dell laptop running Win 7 there was a conflict between the touchpad and the Logitech USB corded Laser mouse model M500 that I was using.  This conflict did not show up in windows, but it was there.  As soon as I deleted the Logitech drivers and/or Logitech Setpoint software, the problem disappeared.  I am able to use a Logitech corded _Optical_ mouse and I am having no problem.  

To see if your problem is the mouse driver conflict, unplug your mouse and try the touchpad.  If the problem goes away then you simply need a different mouse.  If you have installed the Logitech Setpoint software to control your mouse, then you will need to uninstall it (Start - Contol Panel - Programs - Uninstall Program - then find Setpoint and click on it to highlight it and then click on Uninstall).    I suppose another way to solve the problem is that you could keep your mouse and disable the touchpad.   However, I did not try that. 

If you need a good utility program to uninstall USB drivers (because unplugging your mouse might not work for you), then download USBDeview.  This is an easy to use free program that will allow you to uninstall USB drivers very easily.
