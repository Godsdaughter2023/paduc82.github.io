Lumia 920 - proximity sensor fix - Microsoft Community

carbonarc---01 say:

Lumia 920 - proximity sensor fix

I had very good luck this evening, correcting a problem with my 920 screen blacking out during calls. I couldn't enter PINs, end calls, or do anything that required access to the screen during a call.

 

I spent hours this morning reading through dozens of posts from others who report the same problem.  Most of those posts suggested either blowing air into the phone to dislodge dust, or warning those using phone-cases that the case may be blocking the proximity sensor.  I don't use a case, and I don't like the idea of blowing into the phone, since that would only force any dust futher in to the device. I could see quite a lot of dust collected in the camera lens (the one on the same side as the display screen), which is right next to the proximity sensor.

 

I'm in Canada and was quite disappointed that tech support for the carrier here (Rogers) would only suggest a hard reset, which would cause all of my settings and saved data to be deleted.  Rogers would not admit that this is a known problem, and the phone rep said she had no ability to send my complaint up the management chain.  Shame on Rogers, and shame on Nokia, for not owing up to this common problem!

 

Here's what has worked for me.  Instead of blowing into the phone (using lung power or canned air or whatever) I used our Dyson vacuum's crevice tools to suck out the dust.  I put the crevice tool right onto the phone's ear-piece, while tapping on the phone to help dislodge the dust.  I was a little worried that there might be too much suction, which could damage the earpiece, but I was so angry with the so-called support I was getting from the vendor that I didn't care.  After vacuuming, the result is that all of the dust which had been visible in the camera lens was removed, and the behavior of the screen during calls was perfect. Hey, now I can enter my PIN when calling voice mail! Yay!

 

So, this may not work for everyone, but it worked great for me.  Give it a try.

stefan blom say:

Are you saying that only part of the document is formatted in columns? In that case, I see what you mean. When part of a document is formatted in columns, that part must be in its own section (preceded and followed by a section break). When you collapse a heading, subordinate headings following a section break will remain expanded.

I don't see a way around this problem, because you can't remove the section breaks without losing the section formatting.

As an alternative, try Outline view. It lets you expand and collapse headings. It will also show a simplified view of the document; for example, it text ranges formatted in two or more columns will display as one column.
