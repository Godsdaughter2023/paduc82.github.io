jay freedman say:
Maybe a more practical way than locating the section breaks themselves is to put the cursor in the text at a place where you want the user to be able to edit, and let Word tell you which section that's in.

First, right-click a blank area of the status bar (the bottom-most area of the Word window) and check "Section" in the list of things that can be displayed on the status bar. Next, unprotect the whole document. Then click in the beginning of a paragraph that should be editable, and look at the left end of the status bar to see which section number is shown there. Check again at the end of that editable area to determine whether it's all in one section, and that the section doesn't continue beyond that point. Repeat for the other editable areas.

When you have a complete list of the section numbers that correspond to areas that should be editable, you can go into the protection list and uncheck those numbers.
