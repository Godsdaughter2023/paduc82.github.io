ken sheridan say:

Is it necessary to create a separate categories table

Absolutely.  Categories are data.  A fundamental principle of the database relational model is the Information Principle (Codd's Rule #1).  This requires that all data be stored as values at column positions in rows in tables, and in no other way.  Encoding e.g. days of the week or months of the year.

As regards a contact's roles at an event, as a contact might be undertaking more than one role at the same event, there is a many-to-many relationship type between Roles and Attendances.  Remember, Attendances is an entity type, because a relationship type is just a special king of entity type, so can be related to other entity types.  You'd have a Roles table:

Roles
….RoleID  (PK)
….Role

and to model the relationship type between it and Attendances another table:

AttendanceRoles
….RoleID  (FK)
….ContactID  (FK)
….EventID  (FK)

In this RoleID references the primary key of Roles, and ContactID and EventID are a composite foreign key referencing the 2-column composite primary key of Attendances.  The latter relationship should be on these two columns and enforced.

scottgem say:

You need an event table. ALL your events will be listed in that table. You may use a category field to define like events for grouping. I would also have a field that indicates duration of the event. You could then have a related table on a 1;1 basis to show the percentage of attendance required.

How to move Insider licence to another computer? - Microsoft Community

marija znidarsic say:

How to move Insider licence to another computer?

I am selling my desktop and switching to a laptop. Is there any way to keep my Windows Insider licence and how?

Tnx!!!

don cuthbert say:

There is no unique Windows 10 Insider Preview license. A device either has a Windows 10 license or it does not. A digital license will activate both Insider and public builds (but on the licensed device device only).

Stand-alone Windows software can be legally transferred to another device, as long as it is first removed from the original device. If you change your motherboard, your computer looks like a new device and Windows will lose activation. The Activation Troubleshooter can be used to transfer the previous Windows digital license to the new hardware, by first indicating that you have change hardware. However, digital license transfer, via the Troubleshooter, does not work between a desktop and a laptop, as they are obviously not the same device with new parts.

Prior to July 2015, there were Insider builds which could be activated using an Insider Product Key. But shortly before the first public release of Windows 10 they switched to using a Digital Entitlement. Since that time, a device has to have a Digital License for Windows 10 in order to activate Windows 10. That digital license will activate both public and Insider builds for that same Edition (Home, Pro, etc.). During that first year any upgrade from Windows 7 or 8 to 10 could gain a Windows 10 digital license for free. Today, only Assistive Technology user upgrades can still upgrade to Windows 10 for free (from Windows 7 or 8).

There are some devices which began with a Windows 10 Insider Product Key activation (that never had a Windows 7 or 8 license originally) and have never lost their digital license. But since you can no longer activate Windows 10 with an Insider Product Key, this can no longer happen.

But when it comes to testing Windows 10, running without activation works well, except for Settings found under the Personalization section.

lumia 430 dual sim reboot problem

my lumia 430 dual sim have rebooting problem.
joe men say:

Hello Aluma,

Regarding your concern about your Lumia 430 rebooting, try hard resetting it by doing the following:

  1. Press and hold the Volume down and Power buttons at the same time until you feel a vibration (about 10-15 seconds).
  2. When you feel the vibration, release the buttons, and then immediately press and hold the Volume down button until you see a large exclamation mark.
  3. Once the exclamation mark appears, press the following four buttons in this order: Volume up, Volume down, Power, Volume down. Your phone should now reset and restart itself. (It might take a while for the reset to finish.).

If the issue persists after hard resetting your phone, use the Windows Device Recovery Tool to restore it.

Let us know what happens after doing the mentioned troubleshooting steps.
