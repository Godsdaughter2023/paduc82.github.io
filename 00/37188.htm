4 (optional): The type of .DMP files located in the Minidump folder are known as Small Memory Dumps. In %systemroot% there will be what is known as a Kernel Memory Dump (if your system is set to generate). It is labeled MEMORY.DMP. The difference between Small Memory Dumps and Kernel Memory Dumps in the simplest definition is a Kernel Memory Dump contains much more information at the time of the crash, therefore allowing further debugging of your issue. If your upload speed permits it, and you aren't going against any strict bandwidth and/or usage caps, etc, the Kernel Memory Dump is the best choice. Ensure Small Memory Dump is selected and ensure the path is %systemroot%\Minidump.

Start > Search > type services.msc > Under the name tab, find Windows Error Reporting Service > If the status of the service is not Started then right click it and select Start. You can do this by right clicking it, selecting properties, and under General selecting startup type to 'Automatic', and then click Apply.

Hi,

Virgin don't do anything to make Media Centre work. They don't support it at all.

Top tips to getting the remote recognised would be:

  • do this in a naturally lit room with no artificial lights on
  • not have direct sunlight hitting the IR receiver
  • brand new batteries in the Virgin remote
  • hold the remote literally just a centimetre or two away from the receiver when pressing the buttons

Just to check - has the IR receiver been installed correctly by Windows? It shows up in Device Manager in the Human Interface Devices section as Microsoft eHome Infrared Transceiver.

tgbmark1234 say:

Wow, 5-8 feet is probably wall to wall in most UK living rooms. All I can say is that I've always had best results at the couple of cm range.

Ian, I'm confused why it's not working. But one thing to check: are you pointing the Virgin remote at the IR blaster or the IR receiver? They are different, and you keep saying blaster. The IR receiver is the match box sized and has a USB lead to the PC. Point your remote at the front of that. The IR blaster is a tiny bud with a sticky pad to stick to the front of the V box. It connects to the back of the IR receiver using a headphone style 3.5mm socket.

Only one last thing is that whilst my V box is Virgin branded, the remote I've got is a near ten year old ntl branded blue remote from when they first installed digital TV.
