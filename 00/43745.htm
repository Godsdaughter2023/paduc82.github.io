kerry_brown say: Hi aimank

As others have mentioned it's quite easy to limit RDP to a group of users. To limit it to one computer you would have to change the scope of the rule in the firewall that allows RDP. Enable Remote Desktop as you already have. Open the "Windows Firewall with Advanced Security" console. Click on "Inbound Rules". In the Inbound Rules list scroll down to Remote Desktop (TCP-in). You want to modify the ones that are green. There may be more than one. Double click on the top green RDP rule. Click on the "Scope" tab. Under "Local IP" address click on "These IP addresses:" but don't enter any addresses. Under "Remote IP address" click on "These IP addresses:". Click on the Add button and add the public Internet address of your home connection. This will limit Remote Desktop sessions to only connection originating from your home network. If your home public Internet IP address changes you'll have to update the address before it will work. If you have a broadband connection this should be OK. If you have dial-up at home this won't work as the IP address will keep changing.
