rick rothstein, mvp - excel say:
Last modification....
I see column A is sorted smallest to largest, that's great.
Can you do the same for the B column?

See if this does what you want...

Sub MoveDuplicates()
  Dim LastRow As Long
  Const DataCol As String = "A"
  Const StartRow As Long = 2
  LastRow = Cells(Rows.Count, DataCol).End(xlUp).Row
  Application.ScreenUpdating = False
  Columns(DataCol).Offset(, 1).Resize(, 2).Insert
  Cells(1, DataCol).Offset(, 1).Value = "Source"
  Cells(1, DataCol).Offset(, 2).Value = "Duplicates"
  With Cells(StartRow, DataCol).Resize(LastRow - StartRow + 1)
    With .Offset(, 1)
      .FormulaR1C1 = "=IF(COUNTIF(R1C[-1]:RC[-1],RC[-1])=1,RC[-1],"""")"
      .Value = .Value
    End With
    With .Offset(, 2)
      .FormulaR1C1 = "=IF(RC[-1]="""",RC[-2],"""")"
      .Value = .Value
    End With
    On Error Resume Next
    .Offset(, 1).Resize(, 2).SpecialCells(xlBlanks).Delete xlShiftUp
  End With
  Columns(DataCol).Delete
  Columns(DataCol).Sort Key1:=Cells(1, DataCol), Header:=xlYes
  Columns(DataCol).Offset(, 1).Sort Key1:=Cells(1, DataCol).Offset(, 1), Header:=xlYes
  Application.ScreenUpdating = True
End Sub

karl timmermans [outlook mvp 2012-2018] say:

I have Office 365 Version 2016 installed on 2 computers. I also have OneDrive installed on both computers and I thought maybe trying to put the PST file location on OneDrive; and then change the PST files locations in Outlook to reflect this change.

#1 - There is no "horizontal" sync'ing between different Outlook installations regardless of version in use - never has been. You'll need a 3rd party program for that function.

#2 - Onedrive for PST files is not supported and shouldn't be attempted even if Outlook allowed it - almost certain to result data file corruption.

#3 - If you want 2 computers to have identical data - you'll need to change from a POP account to an Exchange account where each computer will sync with the email account as opposed to sync'ing with each other.

a. user say:

Hi politzer,

 

Welcome to the forum. Let me assist you in preventing junk mails from being delivered to your mailbox and in removing them. I've provided steps below on how to do it.

You can add the senders of junk mails to your Blocked senders list. Here's how to:

1. Sign in to Outlook.com.
2. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the Cog icon, and then click on More mail settings.
3. Under Preventing junk mail, click Safe and blocked senders.
4. Under the Safe and blocked senders, click on Blocked senders.
5. In the Blocked senders list, add the name or domain (the part of an e-mail address that follows the @ sign, such as hotmail.com) that you don't want to receive mail from, and then click Add to list >>.

You can  create rules to delete incoming junk mails. Here's how to:

1. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the Cog icon, and then click on More mail settings.
2. Under Customizing Outlook, click Rules for sorting new messages.
3. Click New and then under Step 1, click the drop down arrow on the first box and choose Sender's address.
4. Under Step 1, type in on the third box the email address.
5. Under Step 2, choose Delete these messages—don't even put them in my Deleted folder.
6. Click Save at the bottom of the page.

You may also set your Junk email filter to Exclusive.  If the Exclusive level was set, messages from senders not on your Contacts or Safe senders lists will be sent to the Junk folder. By default, the Junk folder will automatically delete messages after 10 days. This is more convenient as we don't have any control if senders are randomly changing their address.

 

Here are the steps on how to set to Exclusive:

 

1. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the Cog icon, and then click on More mail settings.

2. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.

3. Under Choose a junk email filter, select Exclusive

4. Click Save at the bottom of the page.

 

I hope this helps and if you have other questions or concerns, please do not hesitate to inform us.

Best regards.
