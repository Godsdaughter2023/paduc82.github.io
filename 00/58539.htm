Hi, a few things you can do to figure it out.

First download a free utility called HWInfo64 for 64bit Windows or HWInfo32 for 32bit Windows.

This utility will tell you all about your system, what Motherboard you have (also the CPU type, the socket it uses, important to know for upgrade purposes) the type and speed of your ram, the type of GPU you have and so on.

Then armed with this info I would head over to PC Gamer where you will find reviews on different hardware tested with the latest games.

For gaming an i3 is not always a bad choice, the amount of RAM and the type of GPU (graphics card) tends to be more important, also the resolution you will be running games in HD, 4K.

The most important thing is your motherboard as this will dictate what upgrades if any can be made.

a. user say:

Gaming is about more than CPU. Mostly its about your graphics card, which handles most of the processing. A more potent graphics card requires more power, so you might need a larger power supply unit. And if you upgrade your processor you'll want a stronger CPU fan, since the stock fan usually won't do for a gamer. Ultimately, you'll do much better to buy a new computer.

Whether or not you can upgrade your processor is entirely dependent on the motherboard socket and the chipset. You can only upgrade to something that fits on your motherboard - the socket - and has chipset support.
