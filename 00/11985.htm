I have certainly come across similar problems on a number of versions of Word, but never really got to the bottom of the problem. There may even be two separate problems, and it is also quite possible that Microsoft made changes in specific Office SPs that affect this behaviour.

A few things you could look at: 

1. the type of section break at the end of the document may affect the behaviour - in fact, this is at least hinted at in the following KB article: 

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/109684/en-us

In some cases, merely adding a continuous section break to the end of the mail merge main document may help.

 b. your documents didn't have any pending revision marks (which can sometimes affect the behaviour of fields adversely)

3. This is probably a red herring, but at one point I thought it might make a difference what field code view is switched on at the time when you merge (e.g. whether you have preview data showing, fields showing with chevrons <<fieldname>>, fields showing with field code braces { MERGEFIELD fieldname }.
