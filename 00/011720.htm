But if I run the form as a standalone form, the macro works perfectly.

My Macro:

dirk goldgar say:

Forms that are "on" navigation forms are actually subforms, so they aren't open independently.  That's why it's telling you the form isn't open.

Change your macro so that the GoToRecord action doesn't specify the "Object Type" or "Form Name" arguments, just the "Record" argument.  It should then work on the active data object, which (since it's invoked from a control on the form) is the form itself, inside the subform control.
