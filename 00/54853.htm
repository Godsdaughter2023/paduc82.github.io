a_user_here say:

First off there is no problem if you share one Microsoft account across multiple devices.

That is in fact what you want in most cases so that you can sync some of the settings and files between devices.

The Microsoft account isn't as much connected to the device though as it is to the account.  You could have multiple accounts on your machine all connected to different Microsoft accounts.

You can change what Microsoft account your account on your machine is connected to, by just first changing it to use a local account and then reconnect to the other Microsoft account.

Start -> Settings -> Accounts -> Sign in with a local account instead

Start -> Settings -> Accounts -> Sign up with Microsoft account instead

Now on the Office subscriptions.  These are associated with your Microsoft account.  I'm not sure if you can transfer them to another Microsoft account, but in your case it sounds like they are in the right place anyways.  If that isn't true you would have to contact Microsoft support to see if they can change them to another Microsoft account.

The main thing here is to see that if you are log into the Microsoft account that has no Office subscription, you are not going to be able to install Office on that machine without paying for it (or transfer it from the other Microsoft account).

Past that what happens is going to depend on the subscription.

For instance the Home subscription allows using Office on up to 5 PC and 5 tablets/Phones.  So with that one you wouldn't have to do anything other than install on the second PC and use it.  You have the personal version though, which is for one PC and one Tablet/Phone.  So if you are using that license you would most likely have to upgrade to the Home subscription (I'm making the assumption that there is no problem doing this as an upgrade).

On the business license.  I'm not exactly sure on your exact subscription, but in the cases of the business ones I see, there doesn't seem to be any (reasonable) limit on the number of user, but you tend to pay per user.  You might want to contact Microsoft support to see what best fits your needs.

https://products.office.com/en-us/business/office
