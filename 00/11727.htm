Unfortunately no, there is no easy way to identify the license type from from the product key.  Do you have the paper work or packaging that came with the office licenses?  What you specifically need is the sticker with the 25 Character Product Key on it. On many of them, in the bottom right corner are 3 tiny letters. The "good" letter code you are looking for is "FPP". If you have "OEM", or "MLK" then you are out of luck.

"FPP" or "Retail" licenses allow you to install Home and Student on up to 3 machines at the same time with a single product key, and to transfer those installations to new computers in the future. With a little luck, at least one of your licenses will be a "FPP"

"OEM" and "MLK" licenses only allow you to install on one computer and you do NOT have transfer rights.


The only other possible way I know of finding out what type of license a product Key is for is by calling the phone activation center. christine_ say:

Two different things come to mind...

1. Are you trying to animate the slide or the text box? Slides have transitions applied to them, objects (shapes, text boxes, etc.) have animations applied.

In order to apply an animation to a text box, choose the text box (so that there is a solid line surrounding it, not dotted) and then your options should be available in the animation pane.

2. Is the content on the slides editable at all?

Varying methods of "locking down" content on slides are often discussed here and one way would be to generate a jpg or png file for each slide and reimport them into a presentation.

I could see a textbook company not wanting their content changed in any way and possibly using this method.

It is easy to test, if you can click in one of the text boxes, it is probably editable. If you click on the slide and the entire slide is an object (png/jpg) then you won't be able to animate your bullets.

HTH

Christine
