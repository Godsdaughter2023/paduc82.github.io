jiaxing bian msft say: Hi Akiko,

Here are the answers to your concerns:
1. The total storage of a SharePoint tenant is calculated based on the amount of the licenses that include the SharePoint Online license.
So each license (including SharePoint Online) is regarded as a subscriber, no matter whether it’s been assigned or not.

2. The 1 TB storage of OneDrive for Business is totally separate from that of SharePoint Online.

3. The unlicensed user can access your SharePoint site if you shared the site with them. Actually they are granted rights to use or view the features within the site collection they are invited to and will not be removed unless you stop sharing with them.

However, when unlicensed users are invited as extended project members to perform a full range of actions on a site, they will not have the exact same capabilities as a full, paid, licensed member within your organization.  They are regarded as external users. For example: they can’t create their own personal sites (My Sites), edit their profile. They don’t get their own OneDrive for Business document libraries.

You can see more details about what external users can and can’t do (search for “External Users Can’t”).
