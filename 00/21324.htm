karl timmermans [outlook mvp 2012-2018] say:

Click on the Send/Receive button located in the same location where you have "Send immediately when connected" which is different then doing a full S/R on startup.

When you click on that Send/Receive button - you will be presented with another window in which you need to "uncheck" the "Schedule an automatic Send/Receive every ?? minutes" for both online and offline modes.

(If you want to ensure that there is no accidental download by inadvertently clicking on Send/Receive via the Outlook UI  - also uncheck the "include this group in send/receive (F9).)

Once the above is set, what gets downloaded will be controlled completely by selecting a specific email account via the Send/Receive menu option

The Send/Receive button referred to above is found via

From O'2010 menu bar

File --> Options --> Advanced -->Send and Receive

As a quasi-related sidenote in case it may be of interest/value to you, a program that you may find particularly useful is MailWasherfrom the folks at FireTrust. The beauty of this program is that it enables you to preview all inbound email pending download and can save endless amounts of time. Can attest to that personally since I also only download from specific email accounts as required using the same settings described above eliminating all garbage email before getting processed by Outlook. Can't begin to quantify how much time and effort having this mail pre-processor has saved over the years especially when dealing with more than 20 different email accounts.
