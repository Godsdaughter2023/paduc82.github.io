    a. Insert the USB flash drive into the USB port on your desktop's hard drive tower or your laptop's body. Wait for your computer to acknowledge that the hard drive has been recognized and is ready for use. Install the necessary driver if prompted to do so by your computer installation software.     b. Click on the "My Computer" desktop icon. Double-click to open the USB drive. This will be distinguished by a label that has the name brand of your USB drive on it. Move this window to the lower-right corner of your screen for later use.

    c. Open the folder on your computer which contains the digital photo files you want to transfer to your USB flash drive.

    d. Select all photo files by clicking on them once while simultaneously holding down the "Shift" key on your keyboard. All selected files will turn blue.

    e. Right-click on a file that you have selected. Choose "Copy" from the menu which appears.

    f. Right-click inside the window of your USB drive. Select "Paste" from the menu which appears. Your files have now been transferred to the USB drive (but have not been deleted from your computer's hard drive).

    Sorry that's not possible.  Your system only keeps track of one time using the clock chip that's built into the motherboard.  Each user can decide how much time to add/subtract from that date to personalize their display time zone by the OS.  I suppose in theory the OS could allow a user to customize it by more than the 24 time zones, but I don't know of any OS that allows that.

    If you used a virtual machine you could probably set it to another time, but that's probably overkill for your needs.
