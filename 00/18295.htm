davidfullermuvi2 say:

Hi

I have found a workaround, in Excel v1903. I suspect it works in earlier versions, but not tested yet.

In my case if the sheet is loaded from a trusted location (Options / Trust Centre /Trust Centre Settings / Trusted Locations) then 'normal' memory usage happens. On my machine (Win 10 x64) this was Committed Memory about 2.5GB.

If it's from a non-trusted location, then the high memory condition happens. On the sheet I was testing, at the end of the process the Committed Memory was around 70GB

In my testing I was loading the sheet from my desktop, which as it was on my local drive I had assumed it was trusted.

It wasn't. So I added C:\Users\XXXX\Desktop to my trusted locations and the memory usage returned to normal.

Clearly it's still an issue and my clients are talking to Microsoft Premiere support about it, but trusted locations might be a good temporary solution

Are all with duplicates like the one that you've shown?  That is, they all have area code within () followed by typically formatted phone number?  Or are some missing area code or otherwise different?  If so, we probably need examples of all potential formats -

One thing you might try real quick, in another available column on the same row enter a formula like this (assuming Phone Number is in column C and 1st row is 2)

=left(C2,len(C2)/2)

Even Better!  This would only split the phone number if it looks like it is an entry such as you've shown, but if just a single phone number, would leave it as is:

=IF(LEN(A2)-(LEN(SUBSTITUTE(A2,")","")))=2,LEFT(A2,LEN(A2)/2),A2)

Like most things there are multiple ways to accomplish this.  One way would be to create a data connection from Excel to Access instead of exporting data from Access to Excel.

Open your Excel document where you desire the Access data, click the Data tab and then the From Access button.  This will open a dialog window where you can navigate to your Access database.  Once you have selected your database, it will present another dialog window of objects within that database that can be selected as the data source.  Highlight your query in the list and click OK.  Leave the defaults on the Import Data dialog and press OK.

philippotts say:

How to move a shared Office 365 account to a new computer.

I have a subscription to Office 365.  My son installed Office on his computer when I invited him to share.  Now he has a new laptop.  How do I move Office 365 to the new laptop?

Thank you

rohn007 say:

To summarize:

  • when you "share" an Office 365 Home subscription it is with the other person's email account, not with specific computers
  • when you share with another person, they may use more than one of your 5 installations, up to your combined limit.
  • Office 365 allows you, and your "shares", to manage their use of licenses, ie install, activate, deactivate through the www.office.com/myaccount site
  • on the new computer, your son can log in to the MyAccount page.  There, under the "Install" option, he can "deactivate" the license from the old machine, and then install and activate on the new machine.
  • if your son wants to continue to use both machines, and you don't have "free"/unused installs, he can swap his one activation license between the 2 machines by deactivating and activating on the appropriate machines.  He does NOT have to also UNINSTALL.  When a license is deactivated, it goes into read&Print only mode.
