We have implemented a security update called Familiar location protection to keep our customers’ accounts secure. The message "You haven't signed in from this location recently." will occur if you are signing in from a location more than 300 miles from your normal location. Codes can be the date of birth, or receiving a code sent to your SMS number or alternate email if configured.
 

To prevent this you will need to configure your computer/mobile device as a trusted device 

Given the new security measures, Account Settings requires an extra security, through the use of a security code.  Also known as 2 step authentication

If you mark your computer, phone or tablet as a Trusted Device, you won't be asked for any codes when you access these settings in the future.  To do this

1. Login your account here.

2.  Under Security & Privacy  Click on "Manage Advanced Security" 

3. You will see the "protect your account" page. Select any option to get a code, and request for it.

4. You will see a checkbox that says : "I sign in frequently on this device. Don't ask me for a code". Tick the checkbox, and enter the code.

Your computer/device is now Trusted. Whenever you use your account on that specific computer/device, you will no longer asked for a code.

charles kenyon say:

Here's another more complex macro:

Sub CopyWithFixedNumbers() '   From Graham Mayor
'   Takes selected text with automatic numbers, converts to fixed numbers and places in clipboard
'       undoes conversion in original
'   http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/office/forum/office_2010-word/paragraph-number-converting-auto-number-to-fixed/7933b05b-9143-411a-a763-8ec2a964fda8
'   2014-03-07
'
Dim oDoc As Document ' object varible for current document
Dim oRng As range
    Set oDoc = ActiveDocument
    oDoc.ConvertNumbersToText
    Set oRng = Selection.range
    oRng.Copy
    oDoc.Undo 1
End Sub

Instructions for Installing Macros from Forums or Websites by Graham Mayor, MVP
