a. user say:

Hello luddite1234,

 

Thank you for posting your query in the Microsoft Community. I'm pleased to assist you with your concern on your account.

 

From what I understand you would like to prevent your account from receiving junk emails and you want these emails to be removed before they arrive to your Inbox or Junk folder. If so, I would advise you to add their email address/domain to your Blocked senders list to prevent unknown or unwanted contacts from sending you unwanted messages. Please be informed that messages from blocked senders are automatically deleted. Here are the steps on how to do this:

  1. From your Inbox, click the gear icon on the upper right corner of the page beside your name.
  2. Select More mail settings.
  3. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.
  4. Click Blocked senders.
  5. Enter the email address or domain in the box labeled Blocked email address or domain: and click Add to list >>. Make sure that you enter the correct email address/domain.

It is also possible that this issue occurs because your account's junk filter setting is set to Standard. We advise you to set your junk email filtering level to Exclusive. If you set your filter to this, only messages from your contacts and Safe senders lists will be allowed to go to your Inbox, the rest will go to your Junk folder. Below are the steps on how to do this:

  1. From your Inbox, click the gear icon on the upper right corner beside your name.
  2. Go to More mail settings.
  3. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.
  4. Choose Exclusive.
  5. Click Save.

Note: You should occasionally check your junk email folder to make sure that good messages don't get put there by mistake. Also, your Junk folder is regularly cleaned up. So you need not to delete those emails manually.

 

You can also create an email rule that will either automatically delete the junk message or move it to another folder. Here are the steps:

 

    1. From your Inbox, click the gear icon on the upper right corner of the page beside your name.

    2. Go to More mail settings.

    3. Under Customizing Outlook, click Rules for sorting new messages.

    4. Click New.

    5. Under Step 1, select the email attribute from the drop-down list and type the email address that you want to apply the rule to.

    6. Under Step 2, determine where you want the messages to be moved or what you want to do to that email (e.g. if it would be auto Deleted):

         a. To move the messages to an existing folder, choose Move to Inbox, and then click the folder name.

         b. To move the messages to a new folder, choose Move to a new folder and type the name for the folder you want to create.

         c. To delete the messages, choose Delete these messages.

         d. To forward messages, select forward messages and type the email address where you wish to forward the said emails.

   7. Click Save.

 

Note: On instances where the spammer just changes the domain (sending email address), it would be better if you would create an email rule that will specify the subject instead of the sender's email address. (e.g. If the email contains the word "Viagra", delete the email).

 

Hope this helps. Let us know how things turn out.

 

Thank you.

wmta3 say: I had a similar problem recently with one of my personal e-mail addresses. Messages would come in whenever I had it scheduled to Send/Receive, but I could not send any messages out and it would get stuck if I tried to Send/Receive manually. I fixed it by deleting that e-mail account and setting it up again.

I would recommend deleting the partnership between your phone and the Exchange server and creating the partnership again. You should be able to do this by going to Start > Programs > Activesync > Menu > Options > Delete.

Then, set it up by going into Activesync and clicking the "set up your device to sync with it" hyperlink. You may need help from your Exchange administrator to get it set up again.
