mike h.. say:

Hi,

In excel dates and times are stored as numbers and the fractional part of that number represents the time. For example for me it is now 23 Sep 2015 10:53 and Excel stores that as 42270.4534722222.

The 42270 is the number of days since 1/1/1900 and the fractional part is the time as a fraction of the day. That date is generally formatted to display as a time format of our choosing.

The problem you have arises with the decimal part of the number because computers work in Binary and often there is no precise binary equivalent of a decimal number and the error accumulates giving rise to the problem you're seeing. See the link below for a fuller explanation.

In your particular case a workaround can be this. Enter your date and time in; say, A1 then in A2 enter this and drag down.

=A1+TIME(0,1,0)

If you don't want to keep all the formula after you've dragged down then select all the dates/times and right click and copy, right click again and Paste Special, select values and OK.

https://support.microsoft.com/en-gb/kb/78113
