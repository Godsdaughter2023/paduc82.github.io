deborahriles say:

After several hours, with many MS searches and many google searches of trying to get the famous and wonderful Quick Launch Toolbar that no longer exists in Windows 7, I have figured it out after about 3 hours. Here is how you do it:

Create a Toolbar

-----------------------

1. Create a folder on your system

2. Right click on taskbar, select Toolbar, select New Toolbar

3. New Toolbar - Choose a folder dialog box appears

4. Select the folder created based on Step 1

　

　

Customize the Toolbar

-------------------------------

1. Right click on taskbar

2. Click Unlock taskbar

3. Right click on new toolbar

4. Pop up menu appears

5. Click Show Title (allows user to hide the toolbar name, i.e. Deb's Toolbar)

6. Right click again on new toolbar

7. Click Show Text (allows user to hide icon name, ie. Microsoft Office Word 2007)

8. Right click again on new toolbar

9. Click View (allows user to change the size of icons displayed on toolbar)

10. Lock the toolbar

　

　

Where I have spent many hours and kept getting Quick Launch does not exist in this version, this is true; HOWEVER, you can get just as close with a customized toolbar. No answer came close to telling how to customize a toolbar, but only how to create a toolbar.

　

　

So I hope this helps out anyone who has upgraded to Windows 7 and are missing and looking for that famour Quick Launch Toolbar. This is the closest you are going to get.
