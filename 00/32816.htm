Cod ghost uses more ports than just xbox live, its the issue how the game is constructed so the upnp for the xbox doesn't really open all the ports of the game itself. The port for the cod ghost is 3075 that needs to be manually forwarded usually.

 

But the easiest way to do this , since port forwarding is usually really complicated. Go to your xbone -> settings -> network settings -> advanced settings and retype everything you had on automatic to the manual set up. Basically take a pen and paper write down all you see on automatic and then enter IP ADRESS , SUB NET MASK AND GATEWAY manually.

 

After that go to a pc/laptop

 

First access your router from a computer or a laptop. Type in the gateway ip in any browser as the website. Its probably 192.168.0.1 (Internet explorer, firefox, chrome etc) It will ask you for the password. Default ones at the bottom

Default Username & Password = U: admin P: sky

Sky
Router Settings ALL MODELS

 

After that go to ADVANCED -> WAN SETUP -> CHECK DMZ DEFAULT SERVER and enter the ip address that you put manually on the xbox, (This is not gateway, the ip address so it probably will look like something 192.168.0.9 or basically 192.168.0.X, X is the number that you have it on the xbox)

 

After its done MTU size is 1500 click apply.

 

Then unplug the power from the xbox and the router for 30 settings. PLug the power first in the router, then into the xbox. Wait for the router to start (1-2min) then start the xbox go to settings -> network -> advanced settings -> alternative mac address -> Clear. It will restart the xbox 1 more time. After it restarts go to network settings and make sure all of the information is set up properly again to manual (Ip address , subnet mask, gateway and are the same as they are written on your piece of paper that you wrote down previously).

 

If you done everything properly enjoy open NAT on all xbox one games and on the xbox live.

 

If you are one of the people that doesn't like 'dmz'  and likes to open ports manually. Instead of dmz do manual port forwarding which is usually longer.

mindmadness45 say:

Xbox One: Suggestions

Introduction

I, like many others, like the idea of what the Xbox One strives to achieve. However, there are some things that could be improved - as there is with anything, there is always room for improvement.

The Xbox One has a great swath of features. The idea of jumping between TV, Games and Skype is a great one. To have "One Device to Rule them All", this 'One' device needs to satisfy the demographics of many people.

The Internet Reaction

As it currently stands, the internet is in uproar over the Xbox's DRM and online requirements. However I believe these requirements to be justifiable.

DRM will save the Games industry. Pirating games is illegal, pirating games hurts the industry and in turn, our idea of games will shrivel away. Companies are closing because they are not getting enough return on the games they have invested millions in.

The world is moving online. This is inevitable. Every device has a connection to the internet these days. Why shouldn't the Xbox One? The Xbox One is introducing an online connection requirement. But how would you expect the Xbox to connect to the cloud without the internet? How would you be able to connect to your account on another console without your data there? So thus the requirement is justified. My interpretation of the once per 24hr requirement, is so that when you connect to the internet, you avoid a large buffer of information to build up and you have to wait for your console to 'sync'. (Think about how long it takes your phone to sync to your computer).

These are just some of the problems that are seen with the Xbox One.

Suggestions for Improvement

I would now like to open some ideas as suggestions for solutions to some of the problems I can see with the console.

Xbox 360 Game support:

For those that don't know, the Xbox 360 is built upon technology known as HD-DVD (High Definition/Density DVD) for its video games. This disk format is in decline and has now been replaced by Blu-Ray Discs (A maximum capacity of 30GB for HD-DVD and 50GB for Blu-Ray).

As such, for the Xbox 360 games to be supported by the Xbox One, it would have to use the now deprecated format. Would you like your next generation of games to be 20GB less detailed? That's a very large difference quality wise.

Instead, why not release a expansion kit, containing a HD-DVD drive that connects by USB3? (or some other connector) This expansion kit would be suitable for households that could invest in a Xbox One at the compromise of selling their Xbox 360. I would pay about $80-100 to have this functionality. We have 3 360s to our household (share house). To sell one and replace it with an Xbox One would mean that person now has to rid of their 360 games.

A new console will inevitably start with a measly number of games. Opening it to backwards compatibility would open it to a massive swath of games allowing a high early adoption rate and early return on investment. Other consoles, inclusive of the Xbox 360, supported its previous generation of games. The number of Original Xbox games used on the Xbox 360 is a small amount, simply because many of the good games were re-released as 360 games or a small number of people adopted the Original Xbox before the Xbox 360.

Near Field Communication (NFC):

Near field communication is widely becoming a standard in most devices, ranging from phones to eTags. Why not open the 360 to NFC?

Imagine this, A friend has invited you over to play multi-player on a new game he's got. You tap your controller to the console, and it transfer's your profile's basic info to the controller. You arrive at your friends place. You want to start playing right away. To sync your controller, tap it to the Xbox One. It syncs your controller and sign's you in. The Xbox One checks with MS for verification and you are now ready to play.

Want to connect Smart Glass on your device to your Xbox One, tap it to the console, configurations are transferred and you're ready to go.

Just as a random note: 'Xbox' shouldn't be an unrecognized word with the in-built spell checker.

Other Suggestions

These are just my suggestions. Apart from these, I think Microsoft could do with a suggestions thread. They want the Ultimate Device, "One Device to Rule them All". The community knows what that device features.

A warning though, DRM removal and Online Requirement removal are not valid suggestions, these features may not satisfy all demographics, but until the time that it does, you can still use the Xbox 360 until the end of its life cycle.

BCCode: D1     0x000000D1

Bug Check 0xD1: DRIVER_IRQL_NOT_LESS_OR_EQUAL

The DRIVER_IRQL_NOT_LESS_OR_EQUAL bug check has a value of 0x000000D1. This indicates that a kernel-mode driver attempted to access pageable memory at a process IRQL that was too high.

Important Info If You Have Received a STOP Code

If you received a blue screen error, or stop code, the computer has shut down abruptly to protect itself from data loss. A hardware device, its driver, or related software might have caused this error. If your copy of Windows came with your computer, call the manufacturer of your computer. If you purchased Windows separately from your computer, Microsoft provides support. To find contact info for Microsoft or your computer manufacturer, Contact Support.

If you have experience with computers and want to try to recover from this error, follow the steps provided in the Microsoft article Resolving STOP (Blue Screen) Errors in Windows.

These actions might prevent an error like this from happening again:

  1. Download and install updates and device drivers for your computer from Windows Update.
  2. Scan your computer for computer viruses.
  3. Check your hard disk for errors.

DRIVER_IRQL_NOT_LESS_OR_EQUAL Parameters

The following parameters are displayed on the blue screen.

Bug Check 0xD1: DRIVER_IRQL_NOT_LESS_OR_EQUAL <-- read link to see parameters
http://msdn.microsoft.com/en-us/library/ff560244(v=VS.85).aspx 

Cause

A driver tried to access an address that is pageable (or that is completely invalid) while the IRQL was too high.

This bug check is usually caused by drivers that have used improper addresses.

If the first parameter has the same value as the fourth parameter, and the third parameter indicates an execute operation, this bug check was likely caused by a driver that was trying to execute code when the code itself was paged out. Possible causes for the page fault include the following:

  • The function was marked as pageable and was running at an elevated IRQL (which includes obtaining a lock).

  • The function call was made to a function in another driver, and that driver was unloaded.

  • The function was called by using a function pointer that was an invalid pointer.

Resolution

To begin debugging, use a kernel debugger to get a stack trace.

If the problem is caused by the driver that you are developing, make sure that the function that was executing at the time of the bug check is not marked as pageable or does not call any other inline functions that could be paged out.
