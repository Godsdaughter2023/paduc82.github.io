Do you have “Fun Tools” add-in? A number of people have reported that removing it overcomes problems with 2013

Do you have Toshiba Book Reader installed on the PC?  It has been reported that uninstalling that brings Office back to working status.

rich porter - microsoft say:

Hi Jamie,

Embedded fonts should show up in a presentation in the PowerPoint web app. I've embedded a few truetype fonts that work normally in the web app. I think the problem may be with the specific font you are using. It appears that the Metro Normal font isn't embeddable due to license restrictions.

Try installing our Font Properties Extension (32-bit Windows only) and check the properties on the TTF file for Metro Normal. The extension can be downloaded here:

http://www.microsoft.com/typography/TrueTypeProperty21.mspx

Once installed, you should see an Embedding tab available when you right-click any TTF file and choose Properties. For the Metro Normal font you've linked, the embedding information says "Restricted License Embedding. No embedding allowed". Other fonts that allow embedding should show up in the web app. Just make sure to select the embed option in the PowerPoint full client (under File | Options | Save) and save the file before uploading it to your SkyDrive.

Good luck, and let me know if you run into any other problems with embedded fonts.

 - Rich

My google contacts (via MS account) won't sync with my - Microsoft Community

nader nooryani say:

My google contacts (via MS account) won't sync with my Windows Phone 8 device

My google contacts (via MS account) won't sync with my Windows Phone 8 device even though they used to sync prior to resetting the device.  I've checked permissions on my Google and MS account and everything is correctly configured.  I can add the Google account seperately, though I want them to show up via my MS account as they do when i log on to www.live.com
mike (microsoft) say:

For your Microsoft account, you may have multiple services such Google setup as a "connected service". You may also have Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn, for instance. You can see your connected services by going here: http://profile.live.com/services?view=manage. However, the fact that Google contacts don't sync from your Microsoft account to your phone is currently by-design. That doesn't mean your Windows Phone can't get Google contacts, it just means it can't get them via the "connected service".

 

Windows Phone only syncs contacts from "connected services" that have been added to the phone as well. Services that currently support this are Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn for example.

 

If you wish to get your Google contacts on your phone, you will need to add your Google Account on Windows Phone directly. You could also consider migrating your contacts from Google to your Microsoft account, as well.
