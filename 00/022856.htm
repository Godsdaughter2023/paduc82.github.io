Isolated Outlook tasks disappearing from Mac - here's how to get - Microsoft Community

svk1 say:

Isolated Outlook tasks disappearing from Mac - here's how to get them back

Hi folks

I'm an Office 365 subscriber with a Mac installation.  Recently I've had a couple extremely important tasks disappear from my Mac's Outlook app.  These were tasks that had a lot of information stored in them.  They were my "working tasks" for two major things I was working on, so they were loaded with notes, decisions, sub-tasks, etc.

When the first task disappeared a couple weeks ago I thought it was my fault - maybe I'd gotten a little quick with the keystrokes & did something wrong.  But another one disappeared this morning so I got a little suspicious.  I did a few quick searches and didn't really find anything specific to this issue.  Nevertheless I took some of the suggestions.  I checked my Deleted Items - nothing.  I synced my folders using Send/Receive - nothing.  But then I went to the Outlook 365 web site, logged in, clicked on the Outlook web app, and whaddyaknow - BOTH MY TASKS WERE STILL THERE!  Including the one that disappeared a few weeks ago!  

You heard me correctly.  The Outlook 365 web app still had both tasks, but neither were visible on my Mac's (up-to-date) Outlook installation.

To fix it, I assigned a new category to the tasks in the Outlook 365 web app.  For some reason this caused them to re-appear on my Mac.  Don't ask me why.

Here's what to do to fix it:

  1. Log in to Outlook 365 online (Google "Outlook 365 login" - that should get you there).
  2. In the Outlook 365 web app, click on the task that's missing from your Mac.
  3. Click on "Outlook" to bring up the Outlook 365 web app
  4. In the upper left, where you see the grid of little boxes, click on this grid & select "Tasks"
  5. Search through the list of tasks until you find the one that's missing (it's probably there)
  6. Click on the task
  7. In the reading pane (on the right), next to where it says "Edit", click the drop-down arrow.
  8. Click on "Categorize"
  9. Assign any category to the task that isn't already assigned
  10. Go back to your Mac's Outlook app & check for the task - you'll probably find it's there.
  11. If it's not, click on the Mail portion of your Mac's Outlook app (i.e. as if you wanted to check your Outlook email) and then click send/receive - this may sync the Outlook folders - including the Tasks - with their online versions.  Not but I didn't even need this step to fix the problem in my case, anyways.
  12. Now that your task is showing up in Outlook on your Mac, delete the unwanted category from the task by...
  13. Clicking on the task in your Mac's Outlook task list
  14. In the reading pane, where you see the categories for this task displayed as ovals, right click on the category you don't want to keep, and...
  15. Click "Remove Category"

Now you should have your task back on your Mac, and only the categories you want should be assigned.

Don't ask me why this works.  The whole problem smells like a bug to me.  

BTW, I'm not saying this is the only way to recover the task - or even the best way - just saying it happened to work.  And this morning, that was good enough for me.

Hope this helps someone!

Steve

rick rothstein, mvp - excel say:

If you want to repeat a single character multiple times, you can use the String function. For example, to repeat the letter A 10 times..

CharToRepeat = "A"
Number = 10
MsgBox String(Number, CharToRepeat)

That second argument can be the character itself, as shown above, or it can be the characters ASCII value instead...

CharToRepeat = 65
Number = 10
MsgBox String(Number, CharToRepeat)

There is no VB function available to repeat a series of characters multiple times, but you can use String function coupled with the Replace function to accomplish it....

CharsToRepeat = "ABC"    ' These must be actual character, ASCII values cannot be used
Number = 10
MsgBox Replace(String(Number, "X"), "X", CharsToRepeat)

Note that the "X" character I used can actually be any character you want... it is simply a temporary place keeper for the String function to produce multiple copies of... then all for those repeated characters get replaced with the actual characters you want repeated.
