Not sure about the IMac, but when you indented the paragraph did you move the left indent marker located on the left margin to create the indent? If so all the text in that paragraph will be indented and if you try to add text by pasting within the paragraph the text will be indented. hans.pnw say:

If you paste an indented paragraph into a second paragraph that is not indented, then that second paragraph will inherit the pasted paragraph's indentation. You can either paste text only, or, to fix the indent after pasting, place the cursor to the left of the first character in the now-incorrectly-indented paragraph, and move the indent back out on the ruler. If the ruler is not showing, you could also try simply pressing the Backspace key.

Pasting text only is the way to go, though, as you don't mess up the margins in the first place. If your version of Word has an Edit pull-down menu, look for Edit > Paste Special > Text Only. If you have a version of Word that, when you right-click at the insertion point, displays a list of three paste options, then choose the one called Keep Text Only.

pranavmishra5582 say:

Hi,

You can face this issue when the Bluetooth services are not running or if there is some hardware problem. Follow the methods given below to resolve the issue.

Method 1: Make sure that the Bluetooth Support Service is running.

Make sure that you sign in to your computer as an administrator user and then follow the steps
given below to check the status of the Bluetooth Support Service.

a) Press Windows Key + R Key.
b) Type services.msc and scroll down to Bluetooth Support Service in the list.
c) Right Click and Click on Properties.
d) Set the ServiceStatus as Start.
e) Restart your computer.

Check the issue and move to next method.

Method 2: Follow the steps given in the link below.

Why isn't Windows finding my Bluetooth or other wireless device?

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows-8/why-isnt-windows-finding-device

Check the status and get back to us.

rohn007 say:

As Doug says, you have to click on the picture to see the toolbar. That is one of the "Conditional" (toolbars) tabs that are only displayed when the related feature has focus (it has the cursor in it). The idea is to "simplify" the ribbon.  There are 9 "basic" tabs, and there are at least 13 conditional tabs, plus addin tabs.

suzanne s. barnhill say:
"Picture Tools Format Contextual Tab" (happy suzanne <grin> ).

When I was revising some of my articles for Word 2007, Office MVP Beth Melton, who writes books on Office products and is also a technical editor for such books, told me that the proper (MS-approved?) reference is "the contextual Picture Tools | Format tab." It's absurd that there are two "layers" to this tab since there's only one actual tab, but the format is the same for the contextual Table Tools | Layout and Table Tools | Design tabs (there are two of those).

The pipe is not a universal convention but is one I adopted a long time ago. Others would say "Picture Tools > Format,' and I have to agree that that's probably easier to interpret; I just like the look of the pipe, so have stuck with it.

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:
If you click on File>Options>Customize Ribbong and then under the Customize the Ribbon section on the right had side, you select Tool Tabs and then you expand the Picture Tools>Format item, is there a Picture Styles item?  If not, use the Reset facility at the bottom of the dialog.  Note, if the document is in compatibility mode, there is a separate Picture Tools (Compatibility Mode) tab that does not contain a Picture Styles item under its Format section.
