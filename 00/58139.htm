For your OneDrive concern, we recommend that you do the troubleshooting below: 

Method 1: Reset your OneDrive.

  1. Press the Windows key + R to open Run Command dialog box.

  2. Enter: %localappdata%\Microsoft\OneDrive\onedrive.exe /reset.

  3. Click OK.

  4. The notification area of the taskbar should disappear and then re-appear after a minute or two. Here’s how to check: Right-click the white OneDrive cloud icon in the notification area, at the far right of the taskbar.

    (You might need to click the Show hidden icons arrow next to the notification area to see the OneDrive icon.)

  5. If the OneDrive icon doesn’t re-appear after a few minutes, open the Run window again and enter:

    %localappdata%\Microsoft\OneDrive\onedrive.exe.Note: Resetting OneDrive re-syncs all your OneDrive files, which could take some time depending on how many files you’re syncing.

Method 2: Uninstall and reinstall your OneDrive.

To uninstall your OneDrive:

  1. On your keyboard, press Windows key + R then type Appwiz.cpl.
  2. On your Programs and Features window, look for Microsoft OneDrive.
  3. Select Microsoft OneDrive then right-click and click Uninstall.

An addition to this: My previous Xbox One never had issues with disconnects in two years, although I think the Xbox One and Xbox One S controllers connect differently, yes?

tormaz say:

The controllers connect the same way (which is how they are compatible on both systems).  The S controllers have Bluetooth in them which makes them usable on devices other than the Xbox One, such as a PC.  If you happen to have your original Xbox One controller, I would try pairing it to see if it has any problems.  If it works fine, than it's probably the controller.  If it has a problem too, it's likely something going on with the Xbox itself.
