You can use the query as the source for another query.  The SQL statement for the rank query could be one of the following.

SELECT A.AccountID, A.Frequency, A.DrugID
, 1 + Count(B.Drugid) as Rank
FROM SomeQuery as A LEFT JOIN SomeQuery As B
ON A.AccountID = B.AccountID
AND A.DrugID < B.DrugID
GROUP BY A.AccountID, A.Frequency, A.DrugID

SELECT AccountID, Frequency, DrugID

mohan suryanarayan say:

Hi Louise,

The amount of internet data used in Outlook is mostly determined by these factors:

  • The kind of Internet Account configured in Outlook.
  • The size of emails being received in your email account

The kind of email account determines the amount of Internet data used. Do you have an IMAP account, MAPI account or Microsoft Exchange Account configured in Outlook? If yes, then note that these accounts keep synchronizing with the email server on a more or less constant basis, hence more Internet Data is used.

The size of emails being downloaded may also be one of the factors.

If one or both the above factors are true in your case, then try the option of setting up Outlook to download only email headers, and check if this reduces the amount of Internet data used on your computer.

To do that, follow these steps to download email headers only in Outlook.

  • In Outlook 2010 click on the Send/Receive tab and Go to Send/Receive Groups -> Define Send/Receive Groups.
  • This will open the main window of Send/Receive Groups. Here select the Group Name and hit Edit.
  • Now under the Account Options, select Download headers for subscribed folders. This will download your emails faster by ignoring the attachments, and also download less data.

Try the above steps and let us know if you need further assistance.

- The text values are contain in one column and there are only 3 possible choices at the moment for the text value

- The bottom line goal is to determine how the payment of an item will be broken out across multiple different businesses

Here is an example of what I have been trying to do with no success.  M is the text column.  F is the numerical column.

+COUNTIFS(M2="Enterprise",(F2*0.7),M2=“Health Apps-Epic”, (F2*1),M2=“Health Apps-Non Epic”, (F2*0.82),””))

+IFS(M2="Enterprise",(F2*0.7),M2=“Health Apps-Epic”, (F2*1),M2=“Health Apps-Non Epic”, (F2*0.82),””))

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

I believe your 2nd example might do the trick if you remove the last ) except for one major consideration... The IFS() function isn't supported in Mac Excel. The function is available as a built-in function only in Excel 2016 for Windows. I believe it also can be made available in the 2007 & later PC versions by way of a PowerUps add-in, but AFAIK that add-in doesn't work on a Mac.

If I correctly understand the scenario, you'd need to use a nested IF() statement, such as:

=IF(M2="Enterprise",F2*0.7,IF(M2="Health Apps-Epic",F2,IF(M2="Health Apps-Non Epic",F2*0.82,"")))
