Rules are specific to one email account. When accessing the Manage Rules & Alerts windowof your "zzz.com" email account, you will see all of the rules you created.   2. Click the Copy icon, and then choose your "yyy.com" email account.
  3. Click OK.
  4. Click Apply, and the click OK.
  5. Go to your "yyy.com" email account and make sure that the rule you copied from "zzz.com" email account is active.

We also suggest changing the rule from "where my name is not in the To box" to "where my name is in the Cc box" so that emails that has your name only in the Cc box will move to your Cc folder.

joelhuang_msft say:


hi yvanleblanc,

the from drop-down list only records the email addresses which you have typed in before in outlook web app (owa) and the outlook client. so if it’s the first time you type in your group email address in owa, please follow the steps below:
1. sign into outlook web app (owa) and set up a new email message.
2. click more command on the top-> click show from in the drop-down.
3. click the email address in the from field and delete it by pressing backspace on the keyboard.
4. type in the email address which you have the send as or send on behalf permission for.
5. the group email address will be in the from drop-down list.

this is the difference between send as and send on behalf:
the send as permission allows a user to send emails directly to another recipient. for example, you may have the send as permission for a distribution group called "dg". you can send an email as "dg" even though it actually comes from you.
the send on behalf permission allows a user to send emails to another user, but your name is still associated with the email. the from field will clearly state that the email is from “you sent on behalf of another user” as the screenshot shows below:

jay freedman say:

If you select a picture that has In Line with Text wrapping, you can't then select any other graphic with Ctrl+click. You can select them as if they were text, by Shift+arrow key or similar methods. (To select floating objects, you have to select the paragraph that contains their anchor points.) You can't group them, but you can copy or delete them all at once.

The Ctrl+click operation works only with objects that have floating (non-inline) text wrapping. If there are many of them, it's sometimes easier to display and use the Selection pane (Home > Select > Selection Pane).

The plus sign on the cursor is not reliable for this -- it appears whenever you hold down the Ctrl key, whether it's pointing to a selectable object or not.

jay freedman say:

To do that, click the Insert > Shapes button and choose "New Drawing Canvas" at the bottom of the gallery.

Everything you insert into the canvas will be floating. You can select them all and group them, although there usually isn't much reason to do that because moving the canvas will move its contents while maintaining their relative positions. The canvas itself can be set to be either inline or floating.

If the canvas gets in your way, you can group the objects inside it, cut the group to the clipboard, delete the now-empty canvas, and paste the group where you want it -- it will remain grouped.
