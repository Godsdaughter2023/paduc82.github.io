oliver wu msft say:

Hi Renee,

 

When we created a team in the Microsoft Teams, there will be a generated Office 365 group created at the same time.  We can make the generated Office 365 groups showing in the Outlook and upload files into the group. However, it is not feasible to make all your teams channels showing in the Outlook. So we can’t upload files  into our custom channels created in Teams within the Outlook.

 

Moreover, when you created a team in the Teams, as we talked before, there would be a generated Office 365 group created, and the generated Office 365 groups are not available in the Outlook by default ( as its email addresses were hided in the Global address book in Exchange Online). 2.       Run set-UnifiedGroup -identity "Office365 group name" -HiddenFromExchangeClientsEnabled:$false   replace the Office 365 group name with your real teams/groups email address.

3.       Wait for some time, and then you would be able to see the Office 365 group in the Office 365 Outlook Web App or Outlook Client.

 

If you want to upload files, you can try it in the Outlook Web App (OWA) https://outlook.office365.com/owa/ > Groups.

 

If you have any other concern, please feel free to let me know.

 

Regards,

Oliver

=OFFSET(INDEX(Ranges!A:A,MATCH(G6, Ranges!A:A,0)),1,0,15,1)

1-May-2013

If, as would seem more likely, you have the dates in row 1 of ranges change both A:A to 1:1
