    Fortunately, at some point, I managed to get the computer to boot to Windows and was able to uninstall the recently installed software that I suspected was the root cause of my problems. Since that time I have not had a reoccurrence of the BSoD.

    In Post-Mortem, I found a lot of what happened was my own damn fault. You would think that after all my years in Tesh Support and Change Management I would know better.

    Here is the exact sequence of events that led to my problem. 

    1. I installed supplemental software that came with my ASUS M5A97 LE Release 2.0 Mainboard.

    2. Sometime after the install of the above software, Driver Booster 4,0 (normally a benign and helpful utility) advised that drivers for my Asmedia USB 3.0 hub were out of date. Allowed the program to install new drivers.

    3. Noticed that Windows was not "seeing" any of the USB 3.0 devices attached to the Asmedia hub now. Recalled that there was "USB 3.0 Booster" Utility included in the Supplement ASUS software. It said no devices attached either. Concluded that installation of new drivers via Driver Booster had overwritten or otherwise killed the USB 3.0 Booster software.

    4. Re-installed the USB 3.0 Booster software by itself.

    Shortly after that the BSoD's started.

    Problem was apparently resolved by uninstalling the Supplemental ASUS software in it's entirety.

    Under lessons learned, here are the critical mistakes I made.

    1. Failed to have "Crash Media" (DVD/USB) on hand prior to starting this operation.

    2, Failed to create a Restore Point prior to installing an application that could possibly change the Operating Environment.

    3, The Biggest Mistake: being overconfident.

    Moral of the Story: No matter how much you know, or, think you know about computers and/or Windows, never, ever tinker "under the hood" without a means of falling back. Take it from a guy who spent over 25 years in Tech Support and has been with Windows practically from the beginning, (I was on the Windows 3.0 Beta team,) and who just got bit by his own arrogance.
