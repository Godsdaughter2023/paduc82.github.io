peteaudio say:

E7 or N8 external microphone info

Hello all,

Just to confirm you can get a microphone into a nokia n8/e7. To do this you will need to use a CA-75u which comes with any n series nokia.It is a three way RCA (phono) split giving you a yellow white and red RCA to a 3.5mm (mini) jack.

 

Once you have your CA-75u you will need to plug your microphone to start with into the red RCA and in the software make sure your default accessory is the headset. Then you plug the mic into the red RCA and then the 3.5mm jack in the top it should ask you which type of connection you want, select headset. Now take the mic and plug it into the into the yellow RCA. Now you can use sound recorder to record from the mic!.

 

This is obviously a work around and it would be much easier for nokia to produce a microphone interface or even better bring out a usb x/y configuration microphone like the zoom recorders use. Come on nokia give me a new gadget!

 

Some notes about mics and connections into an RCA.

 

Electret mini mics should be fine, but I am personally using a lo impedance condenser microphone which has internal power (battery) such as a AKG C1000 or Audio Technika AT33a I myself am using a sennheiser shotgun from the early 90's.

 

To make the correct connection from an XLR plug to the RCA you will need to create a mono lead the easiest way to do this is to connect the white or cold or pin 3 on the XLR to the screen or pin 1 on the XLR. Then terminate them to to the screen or outer part of the RCA. Then terminate the red or hot or pin two on the XLR to the center of the RCA. Alternatively go to maplins or radio shack and buy one.

I have the n8, which I think is a fantastic machine.

I work in commercial radio broadcasting, and frequently have used my (old) cellphone to record audio for broadcast when unexpected news breaks. It was "ok" - but with my new Nokia n8, I knew there was potential for something a lot better - so this post is for the radio broadcasters out there.

 

First, after looking at a handful of options, I downloaded and trial tested the ALON MP3 Dictaphone software (available on Handango.com), then I bought it. It's great software and loves the n8. It offers both mp3 and WAV audio recording among several set-up options. Mp3 is great to speedily e-mail audio back to the studio direct from the n8 for quick broadcast (like news). WAV is great for returning to the studio for some post-production (as in recording a client for a commercial).

 

I found this post after setting up my n8 - but made my own mic cord. I ordered a 3.5mm 4 conductor plug/cable assembly from Mouser.com.

To the other end of this cable, I attached an XLR for the dynamic mic I usually carry with me. Wire the XLR as peteaudio indicates. Don't forget the short jumper wire from pin 3 to pin 1 on the XLR. It's important.

On the 4-conductor plug end, you want the wire for the 4th ring of the plug, and also the ground/shield wire. I snipped off the remaining two wires, as I was not in need of them for my set-up.

 

Once you have your cable made and tested, plug it into your n8 WITHOUT the microphone attached. Your phone will have you "Select accessory:"  Select "Headset". Now you can connect the microphone end, open the recording software, and record away!

 

On the n8, this jack can also serve as video out (the 4th ring on the plug) OR as audio in. This is why it's important to leave the mic UNPLUGGED until you have told the phone what circuit you want assigned to the jack (by choosing "Headset") .

 

Another post on this forum indicates a USB mic will work through the USB-OTG port. I have not tried this, as I did not want a mic cable sticking out the side of the phone. I like the top jack for on-the-spot recording as it is out of the way while handling the phone in the field.
