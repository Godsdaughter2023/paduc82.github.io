rob koch say:

The Microsoft security applications such as Defender are known to be highly efficient due to all scanning occurring in RAM, which often results in high processor utilization that can cause overheat, system shutdown and occasionally a restart as well.

It's difficult to know if this is the problem without temperature monitoring or knowing exactly what's causing the BSOD, but some quick questions can probably determine whether this might be involved in your issues.

First, does the full scan cause the fan(s) to operate a higher speed and do the BSOD and restart happen only after these have been running at high speed for some time?

If so, then open Task Manager and watch the processor utilization while scanning the HDD and then the SSD to see whether the utilization is significantly lower during an HDD scan.  Since this can often peg the processor at near 100% during an SSD scan as there's virtually no disk read latency, it's much more common for overheat issues to present themselves while scanning this faster drive.

My own Dell laptop has an SSD I added myself, but though the fans operate at high speed from shortly after the scan starts to just after completion, it's never had any shutdown or restart issues.

It's relatively common for non-commercial "white box" systems to have such problems though, since they aren't typically built or tested to the standards that a large manufacturer uses with their systems.  However, another common issue here can be that settings in the BIOS aren't correct to allow the processor to shut down individual cores or the Virtualization Technology is enabled, which locks out the ability for the SpeedStep Technology to disable or reduce core speeds as required to prevent overheat.
