hansv mvp say:

The colors in the upper part of the dropdown panel are theme colors. You can select a color scheme in the Colors dropdown in the Themes group of the Page Layout tab of the ribbon.

When you apply one of the theme colors to a range, Excel doesn't store the exact color specification, but the position of the color in the dropdown panel. If you then copy the range to another workbook that uses another color scheme, Excel applies the color in the same position of the dropdown panel. This may be an entirely different color than in the original workbook.

This is by intention, to maintain a consistent look across the workbook.

To get around it, you'd have to avoid using the Theme Colors and Standard Colors, and select colors from More Colors...
