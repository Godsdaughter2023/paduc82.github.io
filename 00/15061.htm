bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
RE: "Seems like a missed detail in Excel's design.."

No, this is actually by design. Hyperlinks in Excel are a cell formatting attribute, similar to currency, bold, etc. As a default of the program, however, cell formatting is not carried with the data when that data is read from its source location.

I'm not sure how to pre-format certain individual cells to not automatically recognize & apply hyperlink data. You might have a look at the suggestions offered on j.e. mcgimpsey's site - the page hasn't been updated recently but the code will most likely work or at least point you in a constructive direction:

http://mcgimpsey.com/macoffice/excel/nohyperlinks.html

However, it's reasonably simple after-the-fact by selecting the cells & using;
  • the Edit> Clear> Hyperlinks command, or
  • right-clicking the cell & choosing the Remove Hyperlink command, or
  • going into the Insert> Hyperlink dialog & clicking the Remove Hyperlink button

The removal can also be automated or incorporated in an automated process.

anthonyx26 say:

Two solutions I've found helpful when dealing with this "Contacting [\\server\share] Press ESC to cancel" (or simply slow office 2010 network share access) issue (when selecting File | Open or Save and location is a network drive):

1) For Word 2010: File | Options | Add-ins | Manage: Templates...Go | click Attach, then select a normal.dotm stored on a local drive.

2) Run through the "Add/Remove features" for Office 2010 and disable/make "not available" the option under Office Tools called "Microsoft Sharepoint Foundation Support"

paulbsmith say: I also have the Pionner DEH-70BT. It works great with the 6230i.
You have to transfer phone book contacts one by one.

First Set the Pioneer to wait for you to transfer the phone book, which will then dissconect you from bluetooth.

Then select on the phone 1 number you want to transfer.
Click on the number to bring up its details, then use the left "Options" button and select "Send Business card".
Select to send this by bluetooth, the phone will then search for bluetooth devices. Select the Pioneer when found and then the contact will be transfered to the headunit.

You have to do this for each contact you want to transfer one at a time, which is a bit of a pain.

According to your requirement that you want the emails to be relayed through you own new server so that you can have some logs for the emails, the basic what you need to do is to make connections between Office 365 and your own server.
