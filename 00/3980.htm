You can't put a variable value in a constant. The clue is in the name.
You could create a function to derive MyDate e.g.

Option Explicit
Public Const strFormat As String = "mmddyy"
Public strDate As String
Public Function MyDate(dDate As Date) As String
strDate = Format(dDate, strFormat)
MyDate = strDate
End Function

Sub Test()
MsgBox MyDate(Date)
End Sub

a. user say:

Hi,

 

Welcome to Microsoft Community. I understand that you were unable to receive emails from business associates. Let me try and see what I can do to rectify the problem that you are having. Here are the possible reasons why you were unable to receive mails:

 

A. Junk Mail filters

 

To ensure that this is not contributing to your issue, kindly check if this is not set to Exclusive which sends all mails from non-contacts to junk. Here are the steps:

 

1. Sign in to your account at http://mail.live.com

2. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the Gear Icon, and then click More mail settings.

3. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.

4. Under Choose a junk e-mail filter, tick on Standard - Most junk email is sent to the junk email folder.

5. Click Save.

 

B. Custom Filters

 

Custom filters could be filtering messages to some folder or deleting them. Kindly check your folders and Deleted folder to make sure that your mails are not being sent there.

 

C. Domain has been blocked

 

In some cases, Hotmail may have blocked the 3rd party sender, please refer to this link for more information.

 

D. Mail forwarding is enabled.

 

There is a possibility that Email forwarding was enabled and mails are being forwarded there. Please follow the steps below if you wish to disable this feature:

1. Click on the Gear icon then More mail settings.

2. Under Managing your account, click Email forwarding.

3. Tick Don't forward.

4. Click Save.

 

E. Check your Blocked sender list:

1. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the Gear Icon, and then click on More Mail Settings.
2. Under Preventing junk mail, click Safe and blocked senders.
3. Under the Safe and blocked senders, click on Blocked senders.
4. In the Blocked senders list, select the name or domain (the part of an e-mail address that follows the @ sign, such as hotmail.com) that you now want to receive mail from, and then click << Remove from list.

F. Add the email address to your Safe senders list:

1. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the Gear Icon, and then click on More Mail Settings.
2. Under Preventing junk mail, click on Safe and blocked senders.
3. Under the Safe and blocked senders, click on Safe senders.
4. In the text box, type in the email address or domain you’d like to always receive email from, and then click Add to list >>.

 

Note: The best way to make sure that someone gets in your Inbox is to add them to your contact list

 

If the issue still persists after checking all the settings provided above, please let us know so that we can provide further assistance.

 

Thank you.
