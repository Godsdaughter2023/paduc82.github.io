Have you checked for the exact frequency within Wifi N or WIFI AC your new laptop is actually using to connect to the router?

If several different wireless devices are using the same exact frequency to connect to the router the resulting signal noise would lower the bandwidth (speed) available to all devices and usually lower the speed of frequencies just above and just below the busy one.

Also, other WIFI devices in the area, as well as some electronic devices, cause interference only for some frequencies within the WIFI spectrum.

For example, a 2.4 GHz Wifi N signal maintains its speed further (in terms of physical distance) than a 5 GHz WIFI N signal. Sadly, this same trait is still seen (in my personal experience) with WIFI AC.

You can search Google for WIFI Scanners to be able to see exactly what frequencies your laptop is trying to use, and what frequencies devices in the immediate area are trying to use.
