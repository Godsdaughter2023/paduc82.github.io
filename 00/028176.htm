Try the following code:

 

Dim fldrVersion1 As String, fldrVersion2 As String
Dim strVersion1 As String, strVersion2 As String
Dim docVersion1 As Document, docVersion2 As Document
Dim docCompareTarget As Document
Dim fd As FileDialog
Set fd = Application.FileDialog(msoFileDialogFolderPicker)
With fd
    .Title = "Select the folder that contains the original files."
    If .Show = -1 Then
        fldrVersion1 = .SelectedItems(1)
    Else
        MsgBox "You did not select a folder."
        Exit Sub
    End If
End With
With fd
    .Title = "Select the folder that contains the revised files."
    If .Show = -1 Then
        fldrVersion2 = .SelectedItems(1)
    Else
        MsgBox "You did not select a folder."
        Exit Sub
    End If
End With
For i = 1 To 2
    fldrVersion1 = fldrVersion1 & "\Folder" & i & "\"
    fldrVersion2 = fldrVersion2 & "\Folder" & i & "\"
    MkDir fldrVersion1 & "Compared"
    strVersion1 = Dir$(fldrVersion1 & "*.doc*")
    While strVersion1 <> ""
        Set docVersion1 = Documents.Open(strfldrVersion1 & strVersion1)
        Set docVersion2 = Documents.Open(strfldrVersion1 & docVersion1.Name)
        docVersion1.Compare Name:=docVersion2, compareTarget:=wdCompareTargetNew
        ActiveDocument.SaveAs2 fldrVersion1 & "Compared\" & docVersion1.Name
        ActiveDocument.Close
        docVersion1.Close wdDoNotSaveChanges
        docVersion2.Close wdDoNotSaveChanges
        strVersion1 = Dir$()
    Wend
Next i

julie fka profjulie say:
Your list of Facebook Friends are simply linked to your Microsoft account and you can't manage Facebook only Friends on the phone, you manage that list via your Facebook account.

If you want to delete someone from your Facebook account, you'll do it over there and then when you sync your phone, the contact will no longer show up on the phone.

But if all you really want is to NOT see ALL of your Facebook contacts in the People Hub on the phone and only want to see a subset of your Facebook contacts instead, there's no way to selectively sync just a subset of your Facebook contacts to the phone.

However, if in addition to the Facebook contact you also have a regular contact record for the same person that you can edit via the phone or your Outlook account, you can link any "duplicate" contacts together (so for instance you have a Facebook friend whose name is John Doe.  You also have a separate contact record that you created in your Microsoft account for John Doe. You link the Facebook John Doe Contact with the Outlook John Doe Contact - in the people hub it will appear as one contact record). 

After you link together your matching Facebook Contacts and Outlook contacts, you can filter out any Facebook contacts that aren't linked to an Outlook Contact by opening up the People Hub on the phone, tap the three dots in the lower right hand corner of the screen > settings > filter my contact list > remove the checkmark in the box next to "Facebook".  After you do that, the only Facebook contacts you'll see in your People Hub are those that are linked to an Outlook contact.
