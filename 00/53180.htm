    brent-- say: Hi Lex,

    The "shortcuts" you are referring to are actually symbolic links. (see http://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/cc754077.aspx)  They are mainly there for compatibility between Windows Vista and older Windows operating systems like XP.  For example, some older programs will not install or function unless they can write to the Local Settings folder.  The symbolic link provides a "folder" with that name that actually sends the data to the correct area.

    The way to view where that symbolic link is pointing:

    • Click on Start.
    • In the Start Search box type CMD and press enter.
    • type cd \windows.old\users\(whatever user profile you wish to view). (ex: "cd \windows.old\users\lex") and press enter.
    • type dir /al and press enter.


    To read the display, after the word (JUNCTION) will be the folder name followed by the actual path.

    hey Jairus, I'm no expert as far as techy things go; however, personal experience does come in handy when you want to help others.

    I too have a laptop and have had for several years now (albeit different laptops). I also had a problem of my laptop randomly shutting down. There were two things that I experienced with two separate laptops.

    On one laptop, when it began shutting down, I noticed it was because the lap had gotten quite hot. It had to do with the fans that keep unit cool (it will always have warmth to it when in use, but HOT is NOT cool lol) So, in that case it would shut off as a safety feature.

    For that situation, I got a small fan that blew directly into the laptop via a side vent. However, if you do not have the space or prefer not to clutter your desk with a fan, another option is to purchase a cooler pad (chill mat) that your laptop sits upon. If inner fans are not working, this pad will keep your lap cool.

    I had a Lenovo where I used a chill mat to keep it cool because it constantly overheated (quickly) when in use because the inner fan(s) seized. It was an older laptop (about 5 years old approx) at the time.

    Jairus, if your laptop is not overheating, then obviously that is not the problem. But think about this, Jairus. If your lap randomly shuts down anywhere from a few seconds to a few minutes (if I understood you correctly), but it works for unlimited hours when you use the adapter, instead of using the battery, it is most likely that it is your battery or connector.

When it is plugged in and you are using the adapter, check on your lower taskbar, where the symbol for your internet connection, clock, etc is. You should see the icon for your battery (I always call it the square head farmer holding the pitchfork lol ---but it is actually a symbol of a batter and plug.)

    It will show you how much your battery is charged. If the white line is very low (near bottom or if you do not see any white at all in the battery symbol, then there is NO CHARGE (power) in the battery.  Use your adapter, and allow your battery to charge (icon will be white when it is fully charged).

    Or, you can remove the battery and have it tested (I've had this done where technicians work on computers, whether commercial or private business). The test will reveal the strength of the battery and whether you have to replace it or not.

    They can also look at the back of your lap, where the battery sits normally, to see if any damage has been done to the parts that the battery connects to.

    Sorry for the long post, but I am trying to cover all the bases.  If your lap shuts down when you are only using the battery, but stays on when you plug in the adapter and can play games for hours, I think you answered your own question, Jairus.
