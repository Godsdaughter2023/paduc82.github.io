brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

I would think you'd have to manually enter the carrier and number, or at least the number, for any solution, even a third-party solution. You need to send your message to the correct SMS gateway for the carrier. Keep in mind also that even if you can send the email to an SMS gateway, your recipient will be charged for that SMS message at whatever rate their carrier charges. Also keep in mind that SMS messages are limited to 160 7-bit characters (1120 bits). "SMS" stands for Short Message Service. Some carriers will translate messages longer than 160 characters to multiple SMS messages. Some may convert them to MMS messages, like T-Mobile, which may be covered by a different pricing plan on the recipient's side.

Do you know for a fact that all the people who would receive the SMS messages have unlimited inbound texting? Do you know for a fact that all your recipients' carriers even support mail to SMS?
