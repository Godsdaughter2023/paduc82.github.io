    v0plasma0v say:

    Hello,

    Taking 5-10 minutes to open something seems like quite a problem...

    The explorer, chrome and opening files and apps seems to hint towards a faulty hard drive, since a dying hard drive can result in terribly slow performance.

    You can test this a few ways. First, you'll want to find a few files on your windows drive. Doesn't have to be anything specific, just something that is a few MB in size. Copy the file, and move it over to your secondary drive. Make a note of how long this is taking. Next, copy the file from your secondary drive to another location on your secondary drive, see how long this takes. If copying from your C:\ to your D:\ is a considerable amount slower than copying from two locations on the D:\, then it may suggest a failing hard drive.

    If they seem to be going the same speed, then you can try a refresh. I believe a refresh will keep all of your programs and files, and just refresh everything to do with Windows. Basically a reinstall but with your programs and files already there.

    This might be of some help:

    http://www.tenforums.com/tutorials/4090-refresh-windows-10-a.html

    I would recommend trying the hard drive test first though, since if your drive is failing, you'll want to get everything important over to your secondary (non failing) drive.
