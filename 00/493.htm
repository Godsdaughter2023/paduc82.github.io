kosh vorlon say:

Hi Gwen,

 

I assume you want it protecting you continuously. To verify that, open MSE and go to Settings and click on Real Time protection and (to keep it simple rather than explain why I'm going to suggest this), check the box at the top to turn on real-time protection and then check all the other boxes as well and make sure the dropdown list says "Monitor all files." Then as long as your computer is running (if it is shutdown, in standby, or in hibernation MSE and everything else is shut off because the computer is shut off and there is no risk when that's the case and these programs cannot run with the system off nor can you be infected then - so there is no risk and this is perfectly normal and expected - and unavoidable) and the MSE icon in the notification area shows up as green indicating it is enabled and up-to-date and working right (or you open MSE and it is highlighted in green and the home page says "Security Essentials is monitoring your computer and helping to protect it" near the top and the real-time protection button is checked, you are protected "continuously" (meaning when it is needed).

 

First, let me assume that what you described only occurs at startup and only for a few moments.  If so, this is most likely normal and nothing to be concerned about.  Let me try to explain why that occurs:

 

Sometimes at startup, programs open in different orders (mine seem to have some vague pattern but mostly seem somewhat random). In some cases, certain programs (such as the Security Center or Action Center) will open before MSE is fully opened and enabled. When this occurs, you may get a brief error icon in the notification area that says you are not protected (which is technically true at that moment) but almost always this goes away in a matter of seconds once MSE opens and activates and is detected by Security Center or Action Center which then removes the alert. During thisextremely brief interval you are technically not protected; however, aside from the fact that there is little that can be done to correct this (and would occur with any AV program you used), there is an excellent chance that the network and/or internet connection is probably also not yet up and running yet either (for me at least, that tends to be in the later stages of the starup process though as I said I don't know how to control the order so it could differ for others) and since you are probably not working yet as it's still in startup mode, the risks, if any, are infinitesimally small and should not concern you whatsoever. This happens to me maybe one out of ever 5 or more restarts and typically lasts 2-4 seconds at most and I am not worried about it in the slightest. 

 

One thing that might help is to decrease the number of programs starting at startup so that everything occurs faster and this may also adjust the order of how things open so MSE opens before Security Center or Action Center and you don't get the warning or MSE actually completes the startup sequence before the icon for it appears in the notification area.  To do this you can use MSCONFIG or you can use what I do which is WinPatrol http://www.winpatrol.com/download.html which is not only easier to use, but also can be used in real time to make sure nothing gets added to the start menu without you knowing.  Using either program (or Autoruns http://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/sysinternals/bb963902.aspx if you really want to get an advanced view), remove any program from startup that you don't really need at startup.  When I first checked, I removed 90% of what I found there with no ill effects and in fact found startup was faster, daily work was faster (as less was running in the background), and shutdown was quicker (but it didn't completely solve the issue you raised here though it did happen less frequently).

 

Now, if MSE remains off until you manually restart it (does NOT automatically start after a brief interval), then that's an entirely different matter and I recommend you see this thread: http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/protect/forum/protect_start/on-start-up-microsoft-security-essentials-is-red/e52bbf1f-2145-4a13-83cd-7f4263760c66, and especially the last post there from Stephen Boots.  If those suggestions don't help, then submit the support case as explained in his post in that thread.

 

Now, if at some point while the computer is on and after startup, you suddenly get a message from either MSE or Security Center or Action Center saying that you are not protected, then you need to deal with that immediately since until you do you actually are not protected and may be doing things where that protection is crucial. This would not be a normal occurrence. If it happened once or even twice and restarting MSE worked and that was the end of it and things ran fine from then on (meaning days and weeks), then I'd ignore it as a fluke and move on (though if it happened to me, I'd probably update and run a full scan with every security program I used as early as I could do so just to make sure it wasn't caused by the entry of an infection that MSE not only couldn't detect or block, but literally shut down MSE during the invasion). If it happens daily or even weekly (or, heaven forbid, even more frequently) then you have a problem that needs to be resolved (possibly with Security Center or Action Center, possibly with your system files or hard drive corruption, possibly with MSE, or possibly with malware) and you should post here for help or contact Microsoft Support directly for assistance (using the same method as described in Stephen's post in the thread in the prior paragraph).

 

I hope this helps and that the situation is as described in the part explaining it is normal.  If you still feel uncomfortable, feel free to post back with more details and we'll try to assist you further.

 

Good luck!

I would clarify the detailed mail flow during the cutover migration. After you start a cutover migration batch, the initial synchronization starts. And the corresponded Exchange Online unlicensed mailbox are provisioned after the initial synchronization.

After the initial synchronization, the on-premises mailbox and the corresponded Exchange Online mailbox will sync every 24 hours and this synchronization is called incremental synchronization. During this time, the new emails sent to the on-premises mailboxes still arrive into the on-premises mailboxes. And these new emails will be automatically copied into the Exchange Online mailboxes. That is why you can see the new emails been sent to the unlicensed Exchange Online mailbox before pointing the MX record to Exchange Online.

This is an expected behavior in the process of the cutover migration. It will not affect normal mail flow to the on-premises mailboxes before completing the cutover migration.
