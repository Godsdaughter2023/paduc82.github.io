amit tandon say:

You may try below formula:

=IF(MOD(A1, 1) + MOD(A2, 8)/24 <= 17/24,WORKDAY(A1, A2/8) + MOD(A1, 1) + MOD(A2, 8)/24+MAX(8.5/24-MOD(A1, 1),0)+MIN(17/24-MOD(A1, 1),0),WORKDAY(A1, A2/8 + 1) + MOD(A1, 1) + MOD(A2, 8)/24 - 8.5/24+MAX(8.5/24-MOD(A1, 1),0)+MIN(17/24-MOD(A1, 1),0))

In case you want to consider the end date time after taking into account say a specific lunch time of 2 pm to 2.30 pm, then you may use below formula:

=IF(MOD(A1, 1) + MOD(A2, 8)/24 <= 17/24,WORKDAY(A1, A2/8) + MOD(A1, 1) + MOD(A2, 8)/24+MAX(8.5/24-MOD(A1, 1),0)+MIN(17/24-MOD(A1, 1),0)+IF(MOD(A1, 1) + MOD(A2, 8)/24 - 8.5/24> 14/24,0.5/24,0),WORKDAY(A1, A2/8 + 1) + MOD(A1, 1) + MOD(A2, 8)/24 - 8.5/24+MAX(8.5/24-MOD(A1, 1),0)+MIN(17/24-MOD(A1, 1),0)+IF(MOD(A1, 1) + MOD(A2, 8)/24 - 8.5/24> 14/24,0.5/24,0))

If the start date/time is Nov 4, 2016, 4 pm (Friday), and A2 = 12 hrs, both formula will return Nov 8, 2016 11.30 am, but if A2 = 15 hrs, then first formula will return Nov 8, 2016 2.30 pm while second formula returns Nov 8, 2016 3.00 pm.
