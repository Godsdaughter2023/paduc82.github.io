yoga yao msft say:

Hi Rome,

To prevent users from downloading files, you need to prevent them from reading the file.  If they have access to Read or higher permission, they can download files.  The only way to allow users to read files but not edit or print files is implementing Windows Rights Management.  You can read about that here: http://msdn.microsoft.com/en-us/library/ms458245.aspx.

Besides, you can restrict users to sharing the site and individual files and folders. The steps below are for your reference.

1. Go to the site, click Site Setting> Users and Permissions> Site permissions.

2. Click Access Request Settings, uncheck Allow members to share the site and individual files and folders.

In this case, if users want to share a document with others, you will receive a request. You can choose Accept or Decline.

bernie deitrick say:

Your system date default is m/d/yyyy and the dates are dd-mm-yyyy.  Note that the values that are actually being converted to dates are having their day and months switched, so the date is incorrect.

Don't allow Excel to autoconvert the dates. Paste as text, then use text to columns to split the dates into three columns, and use DATE() in another column to recombine them (in the correct order) into valid dates.
