How to get text to flow in normal paragraphs in Word? - Microsoft Community

david3foxgloveway say:

How to get text to flow in normal paragraphs in Word?

I've copied and pasted some text from a PowerPoint presentation into a Word for Mac 2011 document. The text flows off the right had side of the document, and I can't work out how to bring it back within normal page margins. Help!
rich michaels - mvp (greatcirclelearning.com) say:

Hi David,

Based on your description it appears that you picked up, in addition to the PowerPoint text, the Text Frame of the object. When you do that from PowerPoint the pasted results in Word becomes a "floating" Text Box anchored to a paragraph mark in the Word document and the Text Box floats into the page margin.

Two fixes:

  1. In the Word document Text Box, copy just the text, do not include the frame and paste it. You can then delete the original "floating" text box. The final text in this method will have retained the formatting of the text from the PowerPoint slide.
  2. When copying the text from the PowerPoint slide, do not include the Text Frame. To do this click the text frame so that it activates and then carefully select the text that is within the frame. When just the text is selected... Copy. When you paste that into your Word document the text will assume the formatting attributes of the paragraph into which you are pasting... like a Paste Special performs and you don't want the source formatting.

Hope this helps
