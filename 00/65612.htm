Problems connecting your Xbox wirelessly to the BT Home Hub 4 or Hub 5

When trying to connect your Xbox to the BT Home Hub 4 or Hub 5 you may see this error:

"There is more than one network in range that has this SSID, please change your router SSID before proceeding"

Because the BT Home Hub 4 and Hub 5 are dual band, they broadcast two SSIDs with the same name. As the Xbox was released some years ago, it wasn't set up to deal with this type of dual band wireless connection.

To connect your Xbox wirelessly, you'll need to change one of the SSIDs first. Here's how:

(bt.custhelp.com/.../L2F2LzEvdGltZS8xNDEwNjE4OTY2L3NpZC9GMUVtbmkybQ%3D%3D)

3. Click Continue to Advanced Settings

4. Click Wireless

5. From here you can change the SSID name being broadcast. In this example here we've added 2.4GHz at the end of the name to easily identify this as the 2.4GHZ band. But you can change the name to anything you want. In the Wireless SSID section enter a new name and click Apply

6. Click Yes for the changes to be applied

7. Now you've changed the SSID, you'll need to reconnect any other wireless devices you've previously connected

You should now be able to connect your Xbox to your Hub 4 or Hub 5. Here are instructions if you need: bt.custhelp.com/.../11248

Login Information

  • Username - admin |   root   |   user  |   user   | support
  • Password -  admin | 12345 | 12345 |   user   | support

Restore Factory Defaults

Through firmware

  1. Plug the computer into a LAN port via an ethernet cable.
  2. Open a web browser.
  3. Enter http://192.168.1.1 or http://192.168.1.254 into the address bar. (Or click the link) When prompted enter the admin for the username and admin for the password if the gateway asks.
  4. From the menu on the left, select Management.
  5. Select Settings from the submenu on the left.
  6. Select Restore Defaults from the next submenu.
  7. Select the Restore Default Settings button.

Through Hardware

  1. Locate the reset button on the back of the device.
  2. Using a toothpick or ballpoint pen press and hold the reset button for 30 seconds.
  3. Wait for the device to restart.

sirstephenh say:

Always use DIV over VGA no matter what you're connecting.

As for audio, it depends on your setup. The Connect Xbox 360 Audio support article mentioned by Ohnaka is a good place to start.

If you're connecting via optical then just connect directly to the optical port on the back or your 360 S or original 360 (if it has one). If it doesn't have one then you'll need to purchase an audio adapter like this one and connect it that way. The 360 E doesn't have an optical port.

If you're connecting it via analog stereo then just use the headphone port on the back of the 360 E or for the original 360 or the 360 S, purchase an adapter like this one and connect it that way. You may need an additional adapter or cable depending on the connection to your speakers/stereo.
