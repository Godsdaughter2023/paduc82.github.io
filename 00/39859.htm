eric ber say:
Hi Mandy,

Some apps have to be uninstalled because they could cause problems with the upgrade process. You'll be able to reinstall these apps after the upgrade to Windows 10 is done. Other apps have to be uninstalled because they won't work correctly or might not work at all with Windows 10. You won't be able to reinstall or use these apps after the upgrade is completed. You can try to contact the developer of the program if you will have any issues reinstalling the program after the upgrade.

I would also suggest that you perform a repair upgrade using the latest Windows 10 ISO file to prevent any issues upgrading your computer. A repair upgrade is the process of installing Windows 10 over the existing installation of Windows 10 on your hard disk, using your installation DVD or ISO file. You can follow the steps in this Forum Article that was provided by Andre Da Costa.

Should you need further assistance, don't hesitate to get back to us.
