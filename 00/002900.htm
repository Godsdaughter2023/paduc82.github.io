Set up Auto BCC in Outlook 2016 - Microsoft Community

milesbyrne say:

Set up Auto BCC in Outlook 2016

How do I set up an Auto BCC to my CRM in Outlook 365?

Thanks.

snavely8 say:

You can do effectively the same thing as auto-BCC with a new rule.  This one works for me:

  1. Go to the "home" tab of the ribbon/toolbar
  2. Click the "rules" dropdown and select "Manage Rules & Alerts"
  3. From the "Rules and Alerts" dialog box, select "New Rule"
  4. This pops up a "Rules Wizard" with a number of templates in it.  Pick "Apply rule on messages I send", and hit "Next".
  5. The next dialog shows conditions, leave all unchecked and hit "Next".  You'll get the warning "This rule will be applied to every message you send", hit "Yes".
  6. The next dialog lets you select what to do with the message - check next to "move a copy to the specified folder".  At the window in the bottom of this dialog you'll see the line "move a copy to the specified folder" appear, click the underlined word "specified", from the resulting dialog pick your Inbox and hit "OK".
  7. The next dialog lets you pick exceptions, click "Next" again.
  8. The final dialog lets you name the rule, make sure "Turn on this rule" is selected.  The rule showing in the bottom window should read
    1. "Apply this rule after I send the message"
    2. "move a copy to the Inbox folder"
  9. Click "Finish" and the new rule will be created.
  10. Hit "OK" one more time to leave the "Rules and Alerts" dialog.

Best of luck...

jody.brown say:

An alias is an additional email address that uses the same inbox, contact list, and account settings as the primary alias. You can sign in to your account with any alias—they all use the same password. You can send email from an alias whether you’re using Outlook.com, a mail app like Outlook or devices such as a phone.

If you have a @hotmail.com, @live.com, or @MSN.com email address and would like to add an @outlook.com address to your Outlook.com account, the best way is to add an alias.

Note

  • Some places, like Windows 8 and Xbox 360 will only show your primary alias, but you can sign in with any alias.

  • If you want a new name for your account, you can add an alias, make the new alias primary, then remove the original alias.

Show all
Add an alias to your account

  1. In Outlook.com, click your user name in the upper-right corner.

  2. Click Account settings. You might have to sign in again.

  3. Click Overview.

  4. Click Add or change aliases, and then click Add alias.

  5. Enter a new email address, and then click Add alias. If the address you request is already taken, you’ll be prompted to enter a different address.
    - or -
    Select Add an existing email address as a Microsoft account alias, enter an existing email address, and then click Add alias.

Notes

  • You can create up to ten new aliases per year, for an overall maximum of ten. Deleting an alias removes it from the overall count, but not the yearly limit.

  • Existing @hotmail.com, @live.com, and @msn.com addresses can't be added as aliases.

  • You can create an alias using an existing email address from other providers such as Gmail, Yahoo! Plus, or AIM Mail, as long as you haven't used the address to create a Microsoft account.

Change which alias is your primary

Your primary alias appears on devices such as your Xbox, Surface, and Windows Phone, and is the alias SkyDrive uses when you share.

  1. Go to http://account.live.com.

  2. Click Account aliases.

  3. Click Make primary under the alias you want as your primary.

Note

  • You can change your primary alias up to twice a week.

Remove an alias from your account

  1. In Outlook.com, click your user name in the upper-right corner.

  2. Click Account settings. You might have to sign in again.

  3. Click Overview, and then click Add or change aliases.

  4. Click Remove under the alias.

  5. Click Remove alias.

Warning

  • If you remove an alias, the address will become available in 30 days to anyone who wants it.

Auto-sort mail addressed to aliases

You can have all mail addressed to an alias automatically go to a chosen folder.

  1. Click the Options icon , and then click More mail settings.

  2. Under Customizing Outlook, click Rules for sorting new messages, and then click New.

  3. Under Step 1, In the first box, select To or Cc line, and in the second, select contains.

  4. In the third box, enter the alias you want the rule for.

  5. Under Step 2, select an existing folder from the Move to drop-down list.
    - Or-
    Enter the name of a new folder next to Move to a new folder.

  6. Click Save.

Set an alias to be your default send-from address

  1. Click the Options icon , and then click More mail settings.

  2. Under Managing your account, click Your email accounts.

  3. Under Default "From" address, select a default address.

You can also change what address an email is sent from while you're composing the email.

  1. In your inbox, click New.

  2. Click the down arrow next to the From address.

  3. Choose the address from which to send the mail.

For more help with how to edit your information, contact customer support.
