Microsoft has allowed one year in which owners of Windows 7 and later licenses can upgrade without charge to Windows 10, and they have indicated that the continued use of Windows 10, and the updates to it, will be without charge throughout the supported life of Windows 10. Those same terms will apply after the purchase of the Windows 10 license, in cases in which there was not a qualifying license for the free upgrade.

The things for which one will be charged are subscriptions, such as for Microsoft Office. It is important to note that such subscription items are not actually part of Windows 10, and are not required in order to use Windows 10.

Obviously, Microsoft intends to successfully market enough of such optional subscription items to provide a desirable income, but that does not mean that Windows 10 is being charged for beyond the cost for the license.

Microsoft has never represented the company to be a non-profit entity; rather, they hoped to be profitable. And, they do not require one to be a capitalist or anything of the sort to purchase or subscribe to their products.
