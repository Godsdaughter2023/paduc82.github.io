Thanks for your reply. The LAME MP3 codec was not in Internet Options nor was the K-Lite Codec pack in All programs, nor did a search find the K-Lite Codec pack on my machine. However, I did find a way to disable the popup, which you may want to add as a third option.


In Programs & Features I found Windows 7 Codec Pack 3.4.0. Clicked that, clicked Uninstall/Change and found the LAME MP3 under Audio Codecs & Filters. After choosing "Select None" in the Mass Select/Unselect box at the top I selected the LAME MP3 Codec only, clicked Uninstall at the bottom and restarted as instructed. Next time I went to the page that the popup was appearing on it didn't appear and the background music played as designed.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Try to disable the add-on from the Internet Options.

a.  Open Internet Explorer, press the Alt key and then click Tools.

b. Then select Internet Options. Click on the Programs tab and then on Manage Add-ons.

c. In the list of add-ons, select LAME MP3 codec and then click on Disable.

d. Press ok and restart Internet Explorer to check if the issue reappears.

 

For reference: http://windows.microsoft.com/en-in/internet-explorer/manage-add-ons#ie=ie-10-win-7

 

Here's an alternate method that has worked for some of the customers. You may give it a try if the above steps fail.

a.  Click Start and then click on All programs.

b. Select K-Lite Codec pack and then click on Codec Tweak Tool.

c. Scroll down the list until you find Codec Management (K-Lite Codec Pack).

d. Under this section there will be an "enable" and "disable" section. The program doesn't tell you what is enabled or disabled, but the options move from enabled to disabled when you finish your selection. Locate MP3 (Lame) under the "DISABLE" section and check it. Click Next and then Finish.  

e. Open Internet Explorer to make sure the popup doesn't appear.


Good Luck.
