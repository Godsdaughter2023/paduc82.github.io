'change to suit

End Sub

Background Image size for Publisher banner

I am designing a 2100mm (82.7 inches or 6.9 foot) high by 850mm (33.5 inches or 2.7 foot) wide roll-up banner.

The printing company requires the file in PDF format.

What image size do I use as a background so as to avoid bad resolution? Would a 2mb jpg be OK?  If so, what minimum size in mb do I look for? Would 2mb minimum be good for the resolution not to be blurry when printed?

Thanks

john korchok say:

The perception of resolution varies with the distance of the viewer. The file size of a JPEG tells nothing about its quality or resolution, because compression settings can vary widely. The printing company may have recommendations for picture resolution, check with them first.

For documents that are viewed at arms-length or closer, use a final printed resolution of 250-300 d.p.i. For advertising posters that are viewed from 3' to 10', aim for a final resolution of 72-150 d.p.i. For signs and billboards that are viewed from 10'-100', 30-40 d.p.i. is sufficient.

Of course, if your artwork is smaller than final size and will be scaled up, you have to compensate for the loss of resolution that enlargement brings by making the pictures proportionally higher in resolution.
