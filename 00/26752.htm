technichor say:

There are a few different ways you could do this I think.  None of them are very pretty.  I've accomplished something similar in the past by making pt 1 the data source for pt 2.  I'm not sure that's best in this case though.

You could also add a column to your data table that is just a bit field (TRUE/FALSE) that looks up that job number in pt 1. If it finds it, it returns TRUE, if not, it returns FALSE.  Then you can add that field as a filter in pt 2 and set it to TRUE.

Here is an example of something that would work. This formula would be added in a fourth column and be dragged down for all rows. There might be a better formula for your purposes but this should suffice to demonstrate what I'm talking about. In the formula, A2 is referring to the Job Number. $F$2:$F$18 is the range of job numbers in pt 1.  This should extend beyond the last record so that it updates correctly (either include the total row or extend one row beyond the bottom of your pivot table). 

cell D2:D? =IFERROR(MATCH(A2,$F$2:$F$18,0)>0,FALSE)

Then just add the filter to pt 2 to only include records where that field is TRUE.
