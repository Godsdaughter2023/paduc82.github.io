1. sign in to exchange admin center with your admin account, navigate to compliance management—retention tags.

4. after the tags are created, click retention policies and create a new policy, add the tags to the retention policy and click save at last.

5. after the retention policy is created, refer to this article to assign the policy to the user.

So long as your email is downloading from your Exchange server then you
have nothing more to configure for Outlook for Mac to download the OAB.
Your directory server, however, will not be the same as your mail
server. You need to contact your Exchange administrator and get the
address of a Global Catalog server or LDAP server on your company network.
 
Keep in mind that your Exchange server must be configured to offer the
OAB for download. So long as Outlook for Windows can access it then
Outlook for Mac should be able to access it.
 
To verify the OAB directory open the AppleScript Editor in
/Applications/Utilities and paste in the following three lines:
 
tell application "Microsoft Outlook"
    get oab of exchange account 1
end tell
 
Click the Run button. Do you receive a directory or just "missing value"?
