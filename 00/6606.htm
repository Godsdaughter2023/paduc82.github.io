dan at it associates say:

Dell does sell some computers that do not come with Microsoft Windows preinstalled with an OEM license, but they are definitely the exception rather than the rule.

 

So it's likely that your computer came from Dell with Windows already installed.  To check, look on the bottom or in the battery compartment (if it has one) for a Certificate of Authenticity (CoA) for Windows, it's a 1" x 2-1/2" label that would have the version and edition of Windows that the computer came with, and a product key (don't post the product key).

 

If it does have a CoA, it may have come with Recovery discs.  Backup your data and install Windows with the Recovery Discs.  If this is a netbook type computer, you may have to use an external optical drive to put the Dell recovery version of Windows back onto the computer.  Another possibility is a recovery partition on the hard disk drive from which you can reinstall Windows, but there is the possibility that the process of installing Ubuntu may have damaged or deleted the partition or Dell's special boot loader that lets you install.

 

If it does not have a CoA, you will have to buy a license for Windows and install from a downloaded installation file or (again if you have an optical drive) from installation disc.

 

If you need more assistance getting Windows onto your Dell, post in the Dell community forums or the Windows anwswers forums here.

denisec[msft] say:

Hi Robert--

This is Denise from the OneNote team.  I believe I have the answer to your question.

The feature that you are inquiring about is very possible!

You will first need to create the ‘Fill in the Blank’ content beforehand so that the students will have a chance to copy the materials over to their own personal notebooks. (Like at the beginning of class)

I have listed the step by step instructions below that will walk you through the entire process.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  • Within OneNote, click on the ‘Content Library’ Tab at the top of the page

  • Click on the + to create a new tab, then title the new tab

  • Title your document, then enter or paste your fill in the blank content/questions

  • Once you have created your content (Quiz, test, reading material) the students will then be able to access it from their own personal notebook.

  • In order for the students to work on their assignment, they will first need to copy the assignment page from the content library (which is read only) and paste it into their own notebooks.

  • They can do this by right clicking on the assignment tab in the content library, and selecting ‘move or copy’.

  • They will then need to select the location within their student notebook to copy the assignment (Example, the ‘Class Notes’ tab) then, click ‘Copy’

  • Now the student will have a copy of the assignment in their personal notebook ‘Class Notes’ tab

  • The student will now be able to complete the assignment within their own notebook

  • You will then be able to edit or review the students work by opening each student’s notebook.

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

I Hope this helps! Please feel free to reach out to me if you have any other questions or concerns on this, I will be more than happy to help! Have a great day!
