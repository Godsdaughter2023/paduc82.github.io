Try copying the document content, minus the final paragraph mark (¶), into a new, blank document and see if that overcomes the problem.

If the document contains section breaks, you should delete those first, or copy the document section by section (at all stages omitting the section break and/or final paragraph mark).

a. user say:

Hello Angouby,

 

The option to have multiple mobile numbers per contact is not currently a feature. Here is an alternative workaround to solve the issue:

 

If you'd like for a contact to list more than one mobile number you will need to create a second contact for this person and then link the two contacts together. For example, Your friend "Bob" has two seperate mobile numbers. When you added Bob into your contacts list only one mobile number synced. What you will need to do now is create a new contact with the second mobile number and name it "Bob 2." (Or whatever else you would prefer) 

 

After adding the second contact, you will then need to link 'Bob' with 'Bob 2' together as one. 

 

Steps for linking contacts: 

1. On Start, tap People   , flick to All, and tap the contact you want to link.

2. Tap Link  . Your two contacts are now linked and will show 2 seperate mobile numbers. 

 

** Alternatively you can visit this link for additional detail:

http://www.microsoft.com/windowsphone/en-us/howto/wp7/people/link-two-or-more-contacts.aspx

 

I hope this information helps!
