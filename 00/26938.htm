Is it possible to store a single copy of the template in a centralized
datashare folder, and have Word (and similarly the other Office
applications) include that datashare (and its templates) in the template
gallery?  If so, how?

-- Jeff Zeitlin
rohn007 say:

Yes, simple fix:

Word has long had a provision for exactly this need. Create a folder on the network that all users can read, but not update. Create a common mapping to it for everyone in their log-on script. Now all you have to do is point all installations to this location. Ideally you would have this setup in the installation image.

Word 2003: Tools / Options ... / File Locations tab / Workgroup templates / Modify ... button

Word 2010: File tab / Options option / Advanced option / General Section / File Locations ... button / Workgroup Templates / Modify ... button

Allow only authorized users to update the shared templates.

If this proposed solution has resolved your issue(s), please mark it as  Answered  to let others know the solution that worked for you

aljon_t say:

Hi

In the password reset process, you should provide the credential when you initially created your account.

If you happened to sign in using an email address, which is not your usual sign in credential, it will automatically create a new Skype account under that email address. And based on your post above, you have certainly created a new Skype account, but still unable to access your old Skype account.

Question: why this has happened and how can I cancel the new account and access to my previous one?

Answer: Deleting the newly created account will also affect the existing Skype account as they have the same email address. The account deletion process will require to delete the Microsoft account associated with your Skype accounts. Alternatively, what you can do is to merge the accounts to eliminate the newly created Skype account.

You may refer to these steps on how to merge Skype accounts.

  • Go to https://account.microsoft.com,if you're already signed in, sign out.
  • Enter your Skype name and password when prompted and then select Sign in
  • If your Skype account is not updated already, you will be notified to update your account.
  • Select Next to continue.
  • You will be asked to add an email to your account, and verify that email. (Use your Microsoft account's email address.)
  • Congrats, your Skype account is also a Microsoft account.

And to access your old Skype account, please try to reset the password with these steps:

  • Go to this link.
  • Enter the credential when you initially create the account (Skype name or email address) Continue.
  • On Why can't you sign in? page, choose I forgot my password then hit Next.
  • After that, "Enter the characters you see" > Next.
  • The security info associated with your account shown there, if you have access to these, choose one.

Refer to this link for additional information about account deletion. How do I close my Skype account?

Hope this information helps. Feel free to post back if you need further assistance.
