Stacked   Legend

Are you disabling automatic borders at Office Button | Word Options | Proofing | AutoCorrect Options | AutoFormat As You Type? Note that there are two tabs: AutoFormat As You Type and AutoFormat. Also, if the font and paragraph settings you are choosing and saving as default are not being retained, it may be that an add-in is preventing the Normal template from being saved. Although the best long-term solution is to figure out which add-in is causing the problem and (if it is dispensable) uninstall it, a quick workaround is as follows:

In Word, press Alt+F11; the Visual Basic Editor opens. Press Ctrl+G to activate the Immediate window. In that window, type (or paste)

     NormalTemplate.OpenAsDocument

If the changes you have made have not been recognized as changes (the Undo button is not activated), press Spacebar, then Backspace to "dirty" the file, thus ensuring that the template will actually be saved.

jp ronse say: Hi Hoong Wei,

I fully agree with Hans that it would not be fair to include functions in your workbook without the permission of the author. Even if the add-in is not protected, it remains the intellectual property of the author. There is an exception on this rule: if the author has published the function on the internet and did not made copyright restrictions, you can consider these functions as public (free open source). Anyway, it is always best to mention or to refer to the author when you use such functions in your onw workbooks.

Sending the add-in to your instrcutor and asking to install it, is not sufficient. He has to install it in the same folder as you did. This depends of course how the add-in was written. Your workbook contains links to the add-in and if that workbook is opened on another PC with the add-in installed in a different folder, the functions will show up as "C:\....." and are unusable.

Jan Karel Pieterse has written an article on this:

http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/excel-help/fixing-links-to-udfs-in-add-ins-HA010338114.aspx

Best to send the add-in to the instructor and mention the folder to where it must be installed. But if more students are sending the same add-in with all different installation folders....

You can also send a mail to the author, explain your problem and ask permission for free distribution of his functions. I know by experience that most of them are very helpfull.
