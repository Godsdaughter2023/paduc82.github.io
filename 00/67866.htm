a. user say:

subtleknife,

by default windows is configured to automatically download and install updates. this is fine, as it saves you the trouble of checking for updates and installing them periodically.

but the problem is that, once the updates are installed, windows needs to reboot.

windows usually sets itself to reboot in 10 mins, but it also gives you the option to postpone it. so, if you are working on something important, you can postpone the reboot.

but, what if you have some important and unsaved work in progress and you have to leave your computer for a little while, and right at that moment, windows installed some updates and rebooted itself. the result, you lose all the in-progress work.

here is a simple and easy fix to avoid the above annoyance.

 

1. click start, type windows update, and hit enter.

2. click the changes settings option at left.

3. change the setting to download updates but let me choose whether to install them.

4. Click ok.

that’s it. from now on, windows won’t reboot without your permission, though it may still nag you about installing the downloaded updates.

Select “Troubleshooting”.

Select “View all” on the top left corner.

Click “Hardware & Sound”.

Click on “Hardware & Devices”

Method 2: Uninstall ghost drivers from Device Manager.

Steps:

Right click on the Start icon.

Select Device Manager.

Click on View and Select “Show hidden devices”.

Drop down Human Interface Devices

Right click and uninstall the hidden (ghost) drivers

Drop down Universal Serial Bus Controllers

Right click and uninstall the hidden (ghost) drivers.
