Prevent losing email in Hotmail when you've set up POP3

Hotmail has a feature that prevents losing email messages if you accidentally set up POP3 to delete them after reading them on the server. Here’s how it works:

    The first time that Hotmail receives a POP3 instruction to delete email messages after reading from an email app, Hotmail won't delete the messages.     Hotmail keeps the email messages in the POP folder until you sign in to your Hotmail account and verify whether Hotmail should respect or ignore the POP3 delete command.

Proving it to the developer - this is strictly a "case by case" scenario. It is important to remember that there are a number of web sites that do not consider a mobile-friendly version a priority (or a need at all, for that matter). The best approach is to contact the developer and provide them with initial information (Windows Mobile version, web page causing problems, description of problem) and see what happens.If they are interested in resolving the issue, you should hear back from them.

 

From an IE Mobile perspective, I cannot say for certain if the experience wil ever mimic the desktop. Between differences in processing power (which impacts the rendering of HTML) and differences in screen resolutions (which impacts readibility and usability) alone, the challenge will always be great. Combine that with desktop browsers evolving at a faster pace (therefore supporting newer functionalities) and you are probably looking at a situation where the desktop and the mobile device never being equal.

There are 3rd party browsers available to try to help fill the gap, and they do a very good job of rendering the HTML for a somewhat desktop-like experience. Even those browsers, however, come with some usability issues that at times (for me at least) make me think it would simply be easier to open my notebook computer

jorge e. - wp7 support say:
The Keyboard The Keyboard

To open the phone’s keyboard, hold the phone in landscape orientation with the BACK button on the right, then slide the screen up using one or both of your thumbs.

 

The keyboard contains four rows of keys. The bottom row includes a PHONE key (opens the Dialer), an ALT key, a SPACEBAR, a SYMBOL key, and a SEARCH key. The top row contains the numbers, which you use in combination with the ALT key.

 

 

 

 

Press the PHONE key to open the Phone app. You will go to either the Dialer or the Call Log, whichever screen you visited last. From the Dialer you can make outgoing calls. If you’ve navigated to another screen while in a call, press the PHONE key to return to the in-call screen. Note that this key doesn’t send or end a call.

 

Press the ALT key before typing one of the special characters on the keys,

including the numbers on the top row. To type multiple characters, press and hold the ALT key while typing, or lock the ALT key first by pressing it twice (press again to unlock).

 

Press the SHIFT key before typing an uppercase letter. To type multiple

uppercase letters, press and hold the SHIFT key while typing, or lock the

SHIFT key first by pressing it twice (press again to unlock).

 

Press the SYMBOL key to open the emoticon and special character selector.

The first panel that opens contains special characters; tap the arrow on the

right to see the emoticons. Tap an emoticon or a special character to insert it in your text at the cursor point.

Note: When you’re sending a text message, if you click the SYMBOL key to add an emoticon and then click almost any of the face options, the characters that appear in the text field will look different from those in the emoticon palette. When you tap Send the characters will merge into the matching image from the palette.

 

 

Press the ENTER key to:

·         Create a new line in a text box.

·         Go to a webpage once you’ve typed an address in the Browser address bar.

·         Capture a photo or video.

·         Post a status update.

·         Jump between the subject and message boxes when writing a message.

 

Press the SEARCH key to go to the start search screen. You can search your phone, the Web, or locations near you.

My Office 2010 is stuck in Compatibility mode- How do I get out of it. - Microsoft Community

My Office 2010 is stuck in Compatibility mode- How do I get out of it. It seems my files have been altered.Can I get back my original files?

As Above
suzanne s. barnhill say:
Word 2010 uses Compatibility Mode for documents created in previous versions, either Word 2007 or Word 2003 and earlier. If you have Office 2010 installed side-by-side with a previous version and make changes to the Normal template in Word 2007, it will be saved as a Word 2007 template, which will cause Word 2010 to use Compatibility Mode for new blank documents. You can remedy this by making a change to the Normal template in Word 2010 and resaving it.

graham mayor say:
Better still have separate templates folders for Word 2007 and 2010 and do not share templates.
To remove the compatibility tag, open the document and/or template in Word 2010 and save as with the same filename. If all documents are being created with the compatibility flag, open the normal template normal.dotm in Word 2010 and again use SaveAs to save with the same name.
