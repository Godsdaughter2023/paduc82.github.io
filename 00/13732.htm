trevor rabey say:

The solution that you say "seems clumsy" is actually the best way to do it and it is the most realistic model of how the task will be done and the resources will be managed. It is definitely what I would recommend.

However, a well known short cut is to have one resource named "12 guys" and assign it at 1200% and give it a max units of 1200%. People do this when they are too lazy to model the project properly, and it often causes problems later. Mainly, they are really 12 individual resources which should be able to be assigned to many tasks, but if you group them all up you have to keep them together.

Certainly MSP can change the duration of the task when you add or subtract resources, and they can even be fractional resource additions or subtractions. This result was the default behavior in all versions up until MSP2007, then in MSP2010 the default was changed. See the advanced tab of task information and the combination of the two settings for task type and effort driven. In MSP2007 the default was task type = fixed units and effort driven = on.

Make a task, duration = 10 days, assign a resource, change the settings to task type = fixed units and effort driven = on, then assign another resource. Now duration = 5 days.

Are you in the situation below?

You create a class notebook. Add student 1 and student 2 to that notebook. So, from your side, you can see both of their sections. From their side, they can only see the section related to themselves. Now, you want to know:

  1. Can Student 1 and 2 move notebooks (from their side) to their OneDrive for Business or consumer OneDrive?
  2. After they leave your class, you remove them from the student list (Class Notebook tile>Add/Remove teachers or students), will their notebooks be removed from your side?
