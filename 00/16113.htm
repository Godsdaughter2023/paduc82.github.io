willbeesea say:

Outlook 2016: how to find all items with a Category

In Outlook, I can assign categories to emails, contacts, appointments/meetings, tasks, even notes.  Cool.

But is there a way to search for and list ALL items at once for a particular Category?  I tried using a Search Folder and specifying only the category, but that only returned email.

Thank you in advance.

willbeesea say:

I solved it by using the Advanced Search function:

1.            CTRL-SHIFT-F to get the Advance Search dialog box.

2.            In the Look choices, choose “Any type of Outlook item.”

3.            Click the Advanced Tab.

4.            Click the Field drop-down, mouse over any one of the main lists of fields (see below)

a.            All Mail fields

b.            All Contact fields

c.             All Appointment fields

d.            etc.

 

5.            Choose “Categories” from the list of fields.

6.            Choose the Condition (contains, doesn’t contain, etc.) and type-in your category name.

7.            Click Add to List.

 

8.            Add additional criteria as desired.

9.            Click Find Now.

RESULT: all the Outlook items with that category are listed!

 

WHY DO THIS? Suppose you had a contract with Company X.  You have contacts, appointments and meetings, journal entries, tasks, etc. all associated with that company.  If you mark those items with a Category for that company, you can now quickly find anything in Outlook that is identified with that Category.
