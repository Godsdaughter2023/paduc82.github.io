No, Skydrive will not serve as a means of providing a daily backup for your text messages and contacts.  SkyDrive does not replace My Phone....and you won't be able to "restore" any of the data that gets migrated to your SkyDrive account to your WM6.x phone.  The data that gets migrated to your SkyDrive account will be sitting there for you to download and use as you please.  This will may require your downloading the data to your computer and then importing it into another application or using it with another web-based services (such as importing the contacts.csv file into Windows Live Contacts or Google Contacts).

There is no comparable replacement for the My Phone service....Instead there are other services and applications you can use:

Sync your phone to Outlook on your computer using ActiveSync (for XP computers) or WMDC (for Vista or Windows 7 computers). You can also use third party software, likeJeyo Mobile Companion, to sync the text messages on your phone to your computer.

Use Microsoft's Hotmail/ActiveSync Access Service to sync the contacts & appointments on your phone directly to your Windows Live Contacts & Calendar
  1. On the phone, click on “ActiveSync” under Start menu or under Programs.
  2. Click on the link found in the "If your company supports syncing directly with its Exchange Server you can ....." text to configure the ActiveSync profile for Hotmail access. You may need to delete the existing profile if it is already in use.
  3. On the "Enter Email Address" page, disable automatic detection of Exchange server settings.
  4. Enter your full Live/Hotmail email address in the User name field. Enter password. Leave Domain blank. Tap Next
  5. Enter m.hotmail.com in the Server address field. Tap Next

daniel pineault say:

If your listbox is set to only allow 1 item selection, Multi Select = None.  Then you can simply do:

    Me.TextBoxControlName = Me.ListboxControlName

But why would you need to display this information twice?

If your listbox allows multi-selection then you need a loop, such as :

    With Me.ListboxControlName
        For Each varItem In .ItemsSelected
            If Not IsNull(varItem) Then
                Me.TextBoxControlName = Me.TextBoxControlName & ", " & .ItemData(varItem)
            End If
        Next
    End With

I hope this helps,
