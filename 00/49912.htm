chriso747 say:

I see that no one has been able to help you with this for some time, therefore I give this advice.   

Ntdll.dll error is a mysterious one that has many reasons for surfacing.   So you will have to try various things to see if they help.   You could try:

1.   update your Video Card drivers from their website.   BTW which make/model of card do you use?

2.   check the currency of your other hardware drivers.

3.   run 'sfc \scannow' (exclude quotation marks and note space after 'sfc') in your Command Prompt.  It will take        

      some time to run and will give a report at the end if any System files were fixed.
4.   is your computer/laptop running with higher than normal temperatures?
5.   wrong timings/voltages set for your installed RAM.
6.   are you overclocking your processor?   Bring back to default clock speed.
7.   CPU voltage too high (if you have specially set this in BIOS).
8.   disable UAC (User Access Control) for purpose of testing.   I leave mine off (slider to lowest position) anyway.
9.   does your game have add ons?   You could disable/uninstall them.   Also close all other programs when playing, 
      even shutting down unnecessary Services/Updates from starting.
10.  turn down game settings (for testing purposes).

So as you can see, ntdll.dll error can arise from several corners of your computer, so try them one at a time and if you are not able to handle points 5 & 7, leave them off for now and try the others.  As a side note, when flying with FSX many years ago, I remember seeing this error surface a couple of times and basically I got rid of it by fiddling (adjusting) my Sim settings and any overstretch of graphics or computer resources may invoke this error.   Hope you get success.
