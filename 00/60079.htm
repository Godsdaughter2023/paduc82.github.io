      you are guided through these sames steps when selecting "Fix connection problems".

      In fact, there is a troubleshooter which used to be invoked by that procedure that is now ignored:  the one which lets us test a specific site, e.g. the one that IE says it is having trouble connecting to, hopefully using the same protocol that it is having trouble using.  But even then I think the troubleshooters would overlook what the real problem may be.  For example, if the problem site as seen by the user is this one, Answers, the real problem may be the redirects involving the automatic login via login.live.com.  So, if that's your problem you may have better luck testing that host with HTTPS first.  In any case, it would be useful to work with a specific example, even if redirects and aliases are not involved.  Then do some traces too, so you are aware of the underlying details for your case.

      Also, I noticed the OP mention disabling their AV firewall.  Merely disabling such a service is often not enough.  It still leaves the interferer's place in the protocol stack in place, possibly even still in effect, just not doing its AV scanning.  For example, if the feature is implemented as a pseudo proxy that may still be in effect and hence still be the most likely cause of the problem.  Best to completely uninstall such products to do your tests about whether they may be causing the problem symptom.
