arminatorx say:

...So my 9 yr old son[...]s headphones are dead even the the brand new pair dont work. We have tried all 3 headsets in phones and tablets and they all work fine!! However, he has just pointed out to me that there is a mircophone muted symbol on the screen which isnt normally there! ....

When you say the headphones work with phones and tablets, be aware that there are two standards for 3.5mm headphone connectors. 

These are called "OMTP" and "CTIA", and on both standards, the microphone and ground connections are reversed. 

So if you have a headset that conforms to the OMTP standard, the microphone is sending signals to the controller's ground contact, while the controller is listening on the microphone contact to the ground pin of the headset. 

If the headsets are CTIA compliant, they should work on the Xbox One. However if they are OMTP compliant headsets, you will need an OMTP to CTIA adapter cable. Since the cable simply crosses two wires, they work both ways (so you can buy a CTIA to OMTP and it'll also work), and they shouldn't cost you more than a coffee to go. 

If you are sure that the headsets are CTIA compliant, then try to compose a message on the console. 

Open the Guide menu again by pressing the Xbox button in the middle of the controller, and in the menu that pops in, go to the section with two rectangular speech bubbles. 

Create a new message, and press the Microphone button next to the text entry field. Try to talk into the microphone. Then finish the recording, and try to listen to your recorded message. 

If you hear your message, the headset and controller are working fine. 

Should you still be unable to hear your recorded message, you might have to try a different controller. Maybe the headset connector of the controller is defective.
