valeriew say:

Does PowerPoint have a page break feature?

I have two completely different presentations and want to combine them without changing the formatting of either. Is that possible in PowerPoint?  Or do I have to make different masters within one presentation?

callkathy say:

What I would do (Note that this works in 2007 and later)

  1. Create a new blank presentation.
  2. On the Home Tab, click the drop down for New Slide. From the list that appears, select Reuse Slides.
  3. In the task pane that opens up, click Browse and select the option for slides from file.
  4. Navigate to your presentation.
  5. Check the box "Keep Source Formatting"
  6. Click each slide in turn to add it to your presentation.
  7. Repeat steps 3 to 6 for the other presentation
  8. Save your new presentation under a new name.

At the end of this, you should have all the slides in your new presentation with the original formatting for each slide. In addition, you will have the masters for all the slides in your new presentation.

Let us know if this gets you where you want to be.

First, select an empty cell and insert a stacked bar chart - it will be blank.
