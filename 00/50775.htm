chkdsk all partitions, including those without drive letters - Microsoft Community

frankymacky say:

chkdsk all partitions, including those without drive letters

Is there a way to chkdsk all partitions, including those without drive letters, on the next reboot?
frankymacky say: Hi, James,

My setup (and many laptops, I understand) is that the HDD has several partitions, of which c-drive is one.  It's not c-drive that has many partitions.  So the problem is that I wanted to chkdsk the other partitions without drive letters.

Here is what I ended up doing.

I used PowerQuest Partition Table Editor to change partitions 3 & 4 from 0x17 to 0x07 (unhidden).  They got drive letters after a reboot.  Chkdsk  on them was uneventful.  I changed them back to 0x17.

I then changed partition 1 from 0x27 to 0x07.  It did not receive a driver letter after a reboot, but it did reboot.  I could use Disk Management to assign a drive letter.  Chkdsk issued the warning "WARNING!  F parameter not specified.".  The chkdsk then completed without any interesting messages.  Partition 1 showed up in Disk Management as "System (E:)" and had the tags "Healthy (System, Active, Primary Partition)".

I had to use Disk Management to de-assign the drive letter before PowerQuest Partition Table Editor would let me change partition 1 back to type 0x27.  It now shows up in Disk Management exactly as it did in my original post.  Now I know that the SRP (or Toshiba's equivalent) and the recovery partitions can be assigned a drive letter for read- only chkdsk without harm.

6. Save BIOS changes, restart your System and it should boot from the Installation Media
