Workaround – Fix for Outlook.com duplicate contacts and - Microsoft Community

bill s900 say:

Workaround – Fix for Outlook.com duplicate contacts and improperly linked contacts issue

I've seen numerous discussions over the last year regarding issues with duplicated/improperly linked contacts in Outlook.com mail accounts. Apparently, a considerable number of Hotmail/Outlook accounts were corrupted during the migration, and Microsoft is unwilling to admit the problem, let alone attempt to resolve it. As with the posts from others, I found many, many, hours with MS Tier 2 tech support to be fruitless. Worse than fruitless.

But… I did come up with a workaround. If you like using the Outlook client for mail and want to keep your Outlook/Live/Hotmail address, and sync contacts between devices, here's what I did:

1) Create a new outlook.com account (new email address.) This new account will be used only to sync your contacts (and calendars if you wish.) This account will be created on the new MS exchange servers, and they do not seem to have the issues with duplication/etc. that some migrated accounts do.

2)  Add the new account to your Outlook client (Outlook 2010/2013/2016) on your PC.  Don't delete your existing account, and don't set the new account as the default mail account.

3) Import your clean (non-duplicated) csv contact file into the new account "Contacts" folder on the Outlook client. If you don't have a clean csv copy it is much easier to clean up your contacts using Excel to sort/edit the csv file, just remember to save it as csv (ignore warning.)

4) In the Outlook client, delete all contacts from your old Outlook account to make things simpler and make the next step less confusing.

5) In the Outlook client, you will need to adjust your address book (address book --> tools --> options) so that the new account contacts show and search first. Two "contacts" lists will show in the options, you will want to find the one with your new account contacts. Probably the second contacts set. Test by addressing a new email and clicking "To:" If the contacts list is empty, try the other contacts list in address book --> tools --> options.

6) Add the new account to your phone. On iPhones, you will want to un-sync your old Outlook account contacts (and remove them from phone.) Then adjust the settings on the new Outlook account on your phone so that contacts (and calendars, if desired) sync. No need to sync mail on this new account. Android phones I think have similar settings.

7) If you do decide to also sync calendars on the new account (for consistency,) make a .pst backup of your old calendar on the Outlook client, then re-import the calendar events (.pst file just created) into the new account calendar. Also, on the Outlook client, uncheck the old calendar so only the new calendar shows. Change your phone settings so the old account calendar no longer shows/syncs (remove calendar events from phone) and check that the new account does sync and show its calendar on the phone.

It's sort of a pain, but can all be completed in an hour or two. After many months and countless hours of trying everything else, this worked for me. Good luck!
