You do not normally take a Hard Drive, with an installed OS, out of one Computer and put it in another to Boot from it.

You may be causing yourself all sorts of problems.

There are Motherboard Hardware Drivers involved for each Computer they are installed with.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

And there are Licencing Rules:

If Vista came preinstalled on your Computer, or it was an OEM disk you bought, it is classified as an OEM licence, and is non-transferable.


The OEM Vista Licence is tied to the original Computer it is installed on, and dies with the Computer.
You can NOT transfer it to another Computer/Laptop

If a Retail Vista Licence, you may transfer it to another Computer/Laptop.
But, it may only be installed on one Computer at a time.

Hi

What you are describing is normal.

Anytime you open a very large folder, you will see the behavior that you are describing where the size is enumerated.

There are a lot of mysteries with the Winsxs folder. It's main function is the Servicing Component.

There is no real documentation about exactly how the scavenging process works, except to say that the files in the pendingdeletes folder will eventually be removed. All of the files in that folder are older components that were replaced by a newer update.

I do know that if a recent update is removed, for whatever reason, the files associated with that update will be moved from pendingdeletes back to their original location in the Winsxs folder.
