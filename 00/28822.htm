dianeoforegon say: Gmail will import a POP account. You can then add the Gmail account to Outlook as IMAP. Download your mail, then remove the Gmail account if desired.

Move the messages to "On My Computer" section of Outlook.

Another option:

    In your browser, move mail from  sub folder to the Inbox folder. Do one folder at a time.
    Switch to Outlook  > Select  Send & Receive
    Outlook will download all mail you moved to the Inbox in your browser.
    Move to folder you create in folders “On My Computer”. *see note
    Repeat until you get all your mail.

* Note: Obviously, you’ll have to check for new mail from other accounts that comes in during the process. Sorting by sent should help you distinguish new mail. I would put any other accounts in offline mode.

cody c. say: Hey b.mac,

Is it safe to assume that your email account has folders already created via their website interface?

If so, these folders won't sync via POP to Outlook. You'll need to configure your email account in Outlook to be an IMAP account instead. IMAP supports syncing your Inbox where as POP only supports downloading a local copy. Once you've configured an IMAP account, any child folders will sync with your Inbox.

However, since you haven't shared with us who's hosting your email, it's impossible for me to give you instructions on how to add an IMAP account. In fact, some email hosts don't support IMAP at all. You'll need to contact the company hosting your email and ask them for the IMAP server information. Once you have that, follow the instructions on the link below to add an IMAP account to Outlook:

Add an e-mail account

Hope this helps!
