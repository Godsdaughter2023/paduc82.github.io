Saving a baseline copies the task start, finish, cost, duration, and work into baseline start, baseline finish, baseline cost, baseline duration and baseline work.  Those baseline values are not changed as you go in and enter actual start, actual finish, actual and remaining duration or percent complete.  Project calculates the difference (variance) between the baseline and actual values.

a. user say:

I simulated your inquiries on using the Archive folder. To address both questions, please know that there is no option in Outlook where users can create schedules for folders. It can only be done on messages by using the Schedule cleanup. This also applies only for individual senders that the user choose. You can create subfolders under your Archive folder but not necessarily having them in it. Archive folder basically stores messages from different senders that can come from different folders.

 

We appreciate for you taking the time to share your thoughts about the look and features of Outlook.com. Please know that you can directly provide your feedbacks and suggestions so that the product group will be aware of it and be considered for future releases. To do this, go to this link.

 

With regard to your other inquiry about storing your emails to an offline space, what we can recommend is to add your account to an email client like Windows Live Mail, download your messages locally to your computer and archive it from there.

 

To download Live Mail, please refer to this link. For adding your account to the said client, please follow the steps provided in this article.

 

Here are the steps you can follow to download your messages and archive it from there:

 

Note: Before you start, please create a new folder in any location of your hard drive and please be sure that it is empty. This is where you will save the emails that you will export.

 

1. Start Windows Live Mail.

2. Sign in your account.

3. Click drop down button on the left side of Home tab.

4. Move your mouse cursor over Export email.

5. Select your preferred format of the emails:

  • Microsoft Exchange
  • Microsoft Windows Live Mail

    6. Click Next and then Browse.

    7. Locate the new folder that you have created earlier and then click Next.

    8. Click the button beside All folders and click Next.

    9. Click Finish.
