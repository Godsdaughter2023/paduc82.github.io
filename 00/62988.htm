Windows 7

Here are answers to some common questions about Last Known Good
Configuration.

What s Last Known Good Configuration? 
It's a Windows startup option that uses the most recent system  settings that worked correctly. Every time you turn your computer  off and Windows shuts down successfully, important system settings  are saved in the registry. If a problem occurs, you can restart your  computer using those settings. For example, if a new driver for your  video card is causing problems, or an incorrect registry setting is  preventing Windows from starting correctly, you can restart your
computer using Last Known Good Configuration.
When should I use Last Known Good Configuration?

Try using Last Known Good Configuration if you can't start  Windows, but it started correctly the last time you turned on the 

computer.

If you notice your system behaving strangely and Windows is still
running, try using System Restore to return the system to an əarlier
point in time when things worked correctly.

Unlike Last Known Good  Configuration, changes made with System Restore can be undone  (unless made in safe mode), and System Restore provides different  points in time you can restore your system to.

For more information,  səarch Windows Help and  Support for "System Restore."


Will  Last Known Good Configuration affect my personal files?

No, it only affects system settings and won't change your e mail,
photos, or other personal data on your computer. In the same regard,
it won't help you recover a deleted file or a corrupted driver.

Follow these steps:


  1. Remove all floppy disks, CDs, and   DVDs from your computer, and then restart your computer by clicking   the Start button  ,
    clicking the arrow next to the Shut Down button

    • If your computer has more than one operating system, use
      the arrow keys to highlight the operating system you want to
      start, and then press F8.

    highlight Last Known Good Configuration (advanced), and then press
    Enter.

*** SOLVED ***

Ah, this problem is 2 years old now. One would have thought it would have been resolved by now. Anyway, 3.0 is backward compatible with 2.0. (I tried it on one of my 2 USB 3.0 ports on the back panel, with the same error message.) It looked to me as if the problem might be with the driver for the USB hub, it thinking that my USB 2.0 connector was USB 3.0.

As a test, I tried my Windows 8.1 laptop from work, and it worked with the printer! Therefore I knew the printer as well as the Microsoft built-in driver were okay. What gives?

I was on the phone for hours with Brother and Dell the last couple of days, told them this, but we were not able to resolve the problem. They, of course, suspected the printer and even the USB cable! When I told them it works with the laptop, they were totally confused, too, and had no resolution other than to reinstall Windows. The first support tech wanted me to do a PC Refresh, but the next one thought that would not work. It turned out that neither would have worked. Keep reading.

Last night while on the phone with Dell Tech Support, who wanted me to reinstall Windows but I did not, he was remotely viewing and controlling my PC. He looked at Windows Update and saw one "critical" and 4 "optional" updates. One of them was for the HL-5040 driver! He tried to update, but it failed. I told him I thought it might be because the printer was connected at the time. He stubbornly said no, that if I disconnected the printer, the update would go away. No, it was found because the printer was connected, and I didn't believe it would go away.

Since I had the same driver version as the laptop that worked, I did not think that was the problem. I was surprised even to see that update for the driver. I noticed that one or 2 of the other optional updates were about USB printer support (I searched the KB number for the update). Whatever, the combination of all the updates worked. I hope this helps others, at least to know that the basic built-in driver works, and to always do updates. (I hadn't gotten a notification that any updates were available, but the manual checked found some. I had done updates a week or 2 before, so who would have thought that just the week I was having the problem, Microsoft released an update to fix it!)
