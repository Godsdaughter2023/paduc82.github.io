Mailbox quota will *not* be automatically adjusted after converting a mailbox to shared. But the latest limits are indeed 50GB, so you shouldn't need adjusting the quota to begin with. when you convert a user mailbox whose usage volume is less than 50g to a shared mailbox, the data won’t be lost.

jay freedman say:

Shane, that article discusses how to format existing cross-references, but not how to replace them with different cross-references.

Robert, the part of the field code that starts with _Ref followed by a large random number is the name of a hidden bookmark that the cross-reference dialog creates in the caption. If you choose to refer to the "Entire caption", the bookmark covers the whole caption paragraph; if you choose "Only label and number", the bookmark covers just the figure or chart label and number; and if you choose "Only caption text" then the bookmark starts after the figure number and runs to the end of the caption. You can see the extent of the bookmark by opening the Insert > Bookmark dialog, checking the "Hidden bookmarks" box, selecting the bookmark name, and clicking the GoTo button.

If you delete the whole caption, or the part of it that contains the bookmark, you also delete the bookmark. That's when the "Reference source not found" error occurs.

Instead, if you just edit the part of the caption outside the bookmark -- for example, if you've referenced the number but not the entire caption, and you edit only the text after the number -- then the bookmark will remain unchanged and all the cross-references will still be OK.

If you do have to delete the whole caption or the part with the bookmark, you will have to create a new caption. You'll then need to use the cross-reference dialog, and that creates a bookmark with a different name. However, you can do that just once (to replace any one of the references to that chart) and then use the Replace dialog to change all the other reference fields in the document as follows:

  • Press Alt+F9 to display all the field codes in the document.
  • Select the _Ref<number> part from one of the old references and copy it to the clipboard.
  • Press Ctrl+H to open the Replace dialog.
  • Paste the old _Ref<number> into the Find What box.
  • Click back in the document (you don't have to close the Replace dialog to do this) and select and copy the new _Ref<number> from the cross-reference you just made.
  • Click in the Replace dialog and paste the new number into the Replace With box.
  • Click the Replace All button. Keep clicking it (up to twice more) until the message boxes stop popping up. Then close the Replace dialog.
  • Press Alt+F9 again to switch back to field results.
  • Press Ctrl+A to select the whole document, and press F9 to update the fields.
