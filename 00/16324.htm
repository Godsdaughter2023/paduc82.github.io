How to "Change Data Source" for All Pivot Tables in Different Sheets Within The Same Workbook?? Please help.

bill manville say:

>Is it possible to have the macro change all pivot table data sources to the following range:

'SOURCE DATA'!$A:$AH

That's exactly what the previous macro attempted to do but gave the error.

>The macro runs, but for some reason the data source range does not update for all my pivot tables.

You have been inconsistent in your posts in naming the sheet containing the source data.
Sometimes it has been 'SOURCE DATA' and sometimes SourceData.
The most recent macro assumes the second form.
If it is actually 'SOURCE DATA' then use this:

Sub UpdateLocalPivotSources()
  Dim stSource As String
  Dim PC As PivotCache
  Dim WS As Worksheet
  Dim PT As PivotTable
  Dim stNewSource As String
  For Each PC In ActiveWorkbook.PivotCaches
    stSource = PC.SourceData
    If PC.SourceData Like "'SOURCE DATA'!R1C1:*" Then
      stNewSource = Sheets("Source Data").Range("A1").CurrentRegion.Address(True, True, xlR1C1)
      PC.SourceData = "'SOURCE DATA'!" & stNewSource
    End If
  Next
 
  ' refresh all pivot tables
  For Each WS In ActiveWorkbook.Worksheets
    For Each PT In WS.PivotTables
      PT.RefreshTable
    Next
  Next
End Sub

How to make a call - Microsoft Community

jorge e. - wp7 support say:

How to make a call

How to make a call
jorge e. - wp7 support say:
Open the phone app

There are several ways you can open the phone app and start making phone calls.

·         Go to Apps > Phone, OR

·         Press the PHONE button  on your keyboard

The Dialer and the Call Log

When the Phone app opens, you will go to either the Dialer or the Call Log, whichever screen you visited last. From the Dialer you can make outgoing calls. If you’ve navigated to another screen while in a call, press the PHONE key to return to the in-call screen. Note that this key doesn’t send or end a call.

 

What’s the Dialer?

Use the Dialer to type a phone number.  Simply  tap your number on the dial pad. There’s also a shortcut to call voicemail in the dialer. If you want to add the number to your Contacts, tap the + sign on the top left of the Dialer.

 

What’s the Call Log? From the Dialer, slide your finger left to view the Call Log. The Call Log shows you  a list of your incoming, outgoing, and missed calls, with shortcuts to text or call the number back.

Making and answering phone calls

There are several ways to make phone calls.

Call a new number

  1. Open the Dialer. Go to Apps > Phone, or Press the PHONE button .
  2. Type the number.

Press Call.

Tip: If your call doesn’t go through for any reason (the phone number is busy, you dialed an invalid number, or there is no service), you can redial the number by tapping Call back on the Dialer screen.

Call a contact 

  1. Start typing the contact’s name from any of the Home screens to open a list of all your contacts.
  2. Tap the contact you want to call to open their contact card.
  3. Tap the phone number in the contact card.

Tip: To call a favorite contact, simply tap the person’s photo on the Favorites screen to open their contact card.  From the contact card, tap Call <label>.

Make a call from a recently dialed number

 

  1. Open the Dialer. Go to Apps > Phone, or Press the PHONE button .
  2.  Slide your finger left to find the Call Log. You'll see a list of your incoming, outgoing, and missed calls, with shortcuts to text or call the number back.
  3. Tap a log entry to call the number.

Note: You can also press and hold the 1 key on the Dialer screen to go directly to your voicemail.

Make a call from a text message

From the Messages inbox:

  1. Open the Messages inbox.
  2.  Tap the log entry to call the number.

From a conversation card:

1.      Open the conversation card by tapping the latest message from that contact in your Messages inbox.

2.      Tap the photo associated with the conversation to open the contact card. The contact must have a phone number associated with it.

3.      Tap Call to dial the number.

 

Tip: You can call your voicemail from a contract card by tapping Voicemail on the card.

 

Make a call from an email message

1.      Tap a phone number within the body of an email message. A screen will appear giving you the option to call, text, or add the number to Contacts.

2.      Tap Call.

 

Make a call from a contact card

1.      Open a contact card that has a phone number associated with it.

2.      Tap Call.

  Make a call to a number on a website

1.      Open the Browser and navigate to a website.

2.      Tap a “tel:” link. You will be given the choice to dial, text, or save the number.

3.      Tap Dial.

  Manage incoming calls

When a call comes in, you’ll always receive a notification on the screen.

 

·         To answer the call, peel back the lower-left corner of the screen (where Answer appears) by sliding your finger diagonally on the screen, from lower-left to upper-right.

·         To ignore the call and route it using your Phone settings, tap Ignore.

·         To ignore the call and reply by text message, tap Text.

·         To silence the incoming ring, press any button or key.

  End a call

To end a call, tap End on the in-call screen.

 

Tip: To add the number to your contacts, tap Save contact after the call ends.

 

Save a phone number as a new contact

 

1.      Open the Dialer. Go to Apps > Phone, or Press the PHONE button .

2.      Type the number in the number field.

3.      Tap the + sign to the left of the number.

4.      Tap an existing contact from the list, or tap New contact.

5.      When you’ve finished editing the contact, tap Save.

6.      To call this contact, tap the phone number on the contact card.

v = Application.WorksheetFunction.PermutationA(10, 3) ' number of permutations with repetitions

Clearing Data without removing the formula - Microsoft Community

puzzeledone say:

Clearing Data without removing the formula

Is there a way to clear out the data in a worksheet without removing the formulas? I have a worksheet  that draws data from several other worksheets.  I first enter a name in one cell then a zip code in another cell, at that point information is drawn from several sheets to give me information about a companies order. My problem is that if I enter a new name and zip code the new data received might be shorter than the old, then the cells which should be blank still have old data in them.


 

shane devenshire say:

It sounds like you need to clear all the cells in the data area.  It also sounds like you have formulas which are pulling the data you input to other cells?  What are those formulas?  If you clear data from all of the data area than all the formulas should be blank.  Let's assume you reference 5 cells in the data area, so you clear those and enter data in 3 of those, in that case the formula may pull 0's or some error back.

You can handle that in various ways depending on the formulas you are using. Here are some examples where I am entering the formula in A1:

=IF(R1="","",R1)   this would show as blank if R1 had been cleared

=IFERROR(VLOOKUP(B1,P1:R100,3,FALSE),"")    this is a vlookup formula which will return a blank if no match is found, probably not relevant to your work.

=TRIM(R1&" "&S1&" "&T2&" "&U1&" "&V1)   this will display the concatenated data even if some cells are blank.
