A couple of macros that you assign to the keyboard would probably work OK

For the first one, to insert the [, and turn on the bold formatting and color the [ and the text that you type after it as red, use

With Selection
    .Text = "["
    With .Font
        .Bold = True
        .ColorIndex = wdRed
    End With
    .MoveRight wdCharacter, 1
End With

Then, to insert the closing ] and turn off the bold and red font, use

With Selection
    .Text = "]"
    .MoveRight wdCharacter, 1
    With .Font
        .Bold = False
        .ColorIndex = wdAuto
    End With
End With

How to extract a particular text from a group of text - Microsoft Community

ranjitchowdhury say:

How to extract a particular text from a group of text

I have a haphazard data given below - 

Brand: Titan$Type: Analog$Warranty: 2 Years
Type: Chronograph$Brand: Sonata$Warranty: 1 Year
Warranty: 1 Year$Brand: Timex$Type: Digital

Like above there are 700 row with such data.

Now, I want to extract the whole "brand" portion in one cell, then I want to extract the whole "type" portion in one cell and same for the warranty. All brand will come under one column, all type will come under one column and same for warranty.

Please I need help on this as I have to finish this task within this week.

tom ogilvy say:

RanjitChowdhury,

in B2:  =MID(A2,SEARCH("Brand:",A2),SEARCH("$",A2&"$",SEARCH("Brand:",A2))-SEARCH("Brand:",A2)+1)

brings back  Brand: Titan$

in C2:  =MID(A2,SEARCH("Type:",A2),SEARCH("$",A2&"$",SEARCH("Type:",A2))-SEARCH("Type:",A2)+1)

brings back   Type: Analog$

in D2:  =MID(A2,SEARCH("Warranty:",A2),SEARCH("$",A2&"$",SEARCH("Warranty:",A2))-SEARCH("Warranty:",A2)+1)

brings back  Warranty: 2 Years$

if you want to remove the label you can do

B2:  =Substitute(MID(A2,SEARCH("Brand:",A2),SEARCH("$",A2&"$",SEARCH("Brand:",A2))-SEARCH("Brand:",A2)+1),"Brand: ","")

if you want to remove the $ sign then  remove the +1 from each of the formulas

once the formulas are entered, then select B2:D2 and drag fill down next to your data.

Assumes you data starts in A2

--

Regards,

Tom Ogilvy

bernard liengme say:

Referring to screenshot

Enter the text in E1, I1 and M1

Enter the formulas in B2:E2.

Copy B2:E2 to F2:I2 and J2:M2

Drag B2:M2 down as far as needed - lets say row 500

Select B2:M500 and use Copy, followed by Paste Special Value

ashish mathur say:

Hi,

In range B1:D1, type Brand, Type and Warranty.  In cell B2, enter this formula and copy down/right

=TRIM(MID($A2,SEARCH(B$1,$A2)+LEN(B$1)+1,IFERROR(SEARCH("$",$A2,SEARCH(B$1,$A2)+1)-(SEARCH(B$1,$A2)+LEN(B$1)+1),50)))

Hope this helps.
