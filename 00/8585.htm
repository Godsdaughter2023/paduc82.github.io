In B2:

You could run the following macro. Select the range whose cells you want to name before running it (in the screenshot in my previous reply, that would be B2:G6):

Sub NameCells()
    Dim cel As Range
    For Each cel In Selection
        ActiveWorkbook.Names.Add _
            Name:=Cells(1, cel.Column).Value & "_" & _
            Cells(cel.Row, 1).Value, RefersTo:=cel
    Next cel
End Sub

Delete several email messages

You can quickly delete several email messages from a folder and keep important or unread emails for later.

To select and delete consecutive emails, in the message list, click on the first email, press and hold the SHIFT key, click on the last email, and then press the DELETE key.

Select and delete non-consecutive emails, click on the first email, press and hold the Ctrl key, click on each email message you want to delete, and then press the DELETE key.

Suggestions:

To select all email messages in the active folder, press CTRL + a.
