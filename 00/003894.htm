Hi BanjoStrings,

OneDrive for Business is included in SharePoint Online. Data protection services are provided to prevent the loss of SharePoint Online data. Backups are performed every 12 hours and retained for 14 days. In SharePoint Online, the backup and restoring features are reflected by the following features: version, template, recycle bin.

Here’s article with detailed information: Restore options in SharePoint Online

Furthermore, when a user deletes content from a SharePoint site, it’s not deleted immediately. Instead, it’s sent to the site's Recycle Bin, where you can restore the deleted content if needed. The Recycle Bin is a safety net for deleted content. Content is held in the sites’ recycle bin for a period, and then automatically moved to the Site Collection Recycle Bin.

If you delete content from the Recycle Bin of a site, it’s sent to the Site Collection Recycle Bin. As a SharePoint site collection administrator, you can restore a user’s deleted item from the Second-Stage or Site Collection Recycle Bin to its original location, if the item hasn't exceeded the retention time that the service administrator set. In SharePoint Online, the default is approximately 90 days. If the data is deleted from, second stage recycle bin, it can be recovered by contacting Microsoft support. Assisted support Engineers should help you evaluate the period which will be needed to recover the data.

Reference: Restore deleted items from the site collection recycle bin
