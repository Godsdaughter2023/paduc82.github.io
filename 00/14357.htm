jeeped say:
I think I'm getting a better idea of what you hope to accomplish. The duplicate entries were probably due to not correctly changing the COUNTIF([...],[...]) cell references.

          

The array formula in AA2 is,

=IFERROR(INDEX($B$1:$B$999,MATCH(0,IF(LEN($B$1:$B$999),COUNTIF(AA$1:AA1,$B$1:$B$999),1),0)),"")

The array formula in AB2 is,

=IFERROR(INDEX($C$1:$C$999,MATCH(0,IF($B$1:$B$999=$K$3,COUNTIF(AB$1:AB1,$C$1:$C$999),1),0)),"")

Both are filled down to cover all possible returns. K3 has a data validation list that is based upon AA2:AA10. L10 has a data validation list that is based on AB2:AB10.

You will likely require a VBA clearing routine that clears L10 when K3 is changed. Data validation does not clear values when lists change.

I've uploaded that sample workbook to my SkyDrive here for your reference. The DV_List_John_Mike.xlsm file contains some sheet specific event code for clearing the L10 value.

toddalbertson say:

A shortcut for changing languages is alt-shift (press the alt key and then the shift key), which should toggle through your installed languages and is a lot faster than mousing around in the taskbar.

The easiest way to toggle between the fonts is to create a style for it:

1.  Open Word.

2.  Open the Styles pane by clicking the little arrow in the ribbon:

3.  At the bottom of the Styles pane there are three buttons.  Hovering over them will reveal some ToolTips.  Click on the one that says, "New Style":

4.  This will open a window called, "Create New Style From Formatting."  At the top of the window, there will be some generic name such as Style1.  Type something there descriptive of your text, like "Ancient Greek":

5.  Now, under Style type, we'll change it to Character.  Then, under Formatting, change your font to New Athena Unicode.  Make sure the checkbox is on for "Add to the Styles gallery."  Press OK to close the window and create the style.

6.  You will now be able to switch from Palatino Linotype (which should be defined as your Normal style) and New Athena Unicode (which you just created as the Ancient Greek style).  Click the buttons in the ribbon or the text in the Styles pane to switch back and forth:

Between the shortcut key for the IME and the new style you created, things should speed up considerably for you.  Good luck!
