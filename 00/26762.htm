tom ogilvy say:

what causes cell A5 to change.  Is it a formula or does someone enter a value.  If a formula, you can call your macro with the calculate event.  The challenge would be that it would run everytime there was a calculation and the value of A5 remained in a state to trigger you macro.  You would have to devise a way to prevent that is you don't want that to happen.

 

If A5 is edited, you can trigger your macro by calling it in the worksheet_Change event.

These events are found by right clicking on the sheet tab and select view macro.  Then in the dropdowns at the top of the module select Worksheet and either Change or Calculate. 

http://www.cpearson.com/excel/events.aspx   provides an overview of events.   Take note that the change event has an argument Trigger which is automatically initialized to the cell or cells that triggered the event.  Calculate does not report what action triggered the calculate.

yangzheng_msft6300 say:

Hi Fred,

Here are the answers to your questions.
1. Related to the process for deleting the user's existing mailbox, can you please confirm that the only action necessary is to temporarily remove his Office 365 E3 license?
Yes. You can delete Exchange Online mailboxes by deleting the corresponding Office 365 user account, removing the Exchange Online license, or by running the Remove-Mailbox cmdlet in the Shell. So you can delete the user’s mailbox just by removing the license.

2. If so, for what duration will the license need to be removed for his mailbox to be removed?
Right after the user’s license being removed, the user’s mailbox will be disabled. However, when a mailbox is deleted, Exchange Online retains its contents for 30 days, by default. After 30 days, the mailbox is permanently deleted and is not recoverable.

3. After the mailbox has been removed, if I re-add the E3 license, a new, blank mailbox will be created for the user immediately, is this correct?
Whether the user’s will get a new, blank mailbox depends on when you re-add the E3 license to the user. If you re-add the license in 30 days, the user will get all his former data in his mailbox. If you re-add the license after 30 days, the user will get a new mailbox.

Regards,
Yang

john w. vinson say:

A Date/Time field is stored as a precise moment of time - it's a Double Float number, a count of days and fractions of a day since midnight, December 30, 1899. As such, "2015" is not a valid date. I would agree with Hans that a Long Integer (with a validation rule to select only meaningful years, perhaps) would be best. Sandro's Text field with a four digit input mask would work as well; it just depends on how you will be using the field.
