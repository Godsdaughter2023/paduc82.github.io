How do i lock the anchor on all the images i;ve already - Microsoft Community

ethanrabinowitz say:

How do i lock the anchor on all the images i;ve already inserted?

i hae a bunch of imaged inserted, and need to lock the anchor on all of them...how do i do it at once, instead of 1 by 1?

jay freedman say:
Put this macro in your Normal.dotm template (http://www.gmayor.com/installing_macro.htm).

Sub LockAllAnchors()
    Dim shp As Shape
    For Each shp In ActiveDocument.Shapes
        shp.LockAnchor = True
    Next
End Sub

Open the document with the images, and run the macro (press Alt+F8, select LockAllAnchors in the list of macros, and click Run).

jack cook - aka help_line say:

So at first glance Peter, it seems to me that probably the best and quickest way to get your space back (and more importantly to get your upgrades) is to strongly consider performing a hard reset. It will not take that long and the advantage is that you get a fresh start. If it were me, I would NOT restore from a backup but rather start fresh.

There are a couple of reasons for doing that. All too often we have (overtime) installed a lot of programs that we never use or rarely use so they just take up space. Most likely, if you did not reinstall them, you would not even miss them. If you restore from a backup, it will set all those programs as pending and it always seems to take forever to download.

I find that not restoring from a backup, my device gets setup very quickly and I am up and running in a short period of time.

Use your same Live ID so all your contacts and calendar return and as long as you have enabled auto backup to OneDrive when near a Wi-Fi, all your photos have been stored in OneDrive. You might want to check that your photos are there before you hard reset if you choose to go this route. 

Once it is setup and BEFORE you add back any must-have apps, check for updates. It is a perfect time to install them since there is all sorts of room on a freshly setup device. Once completed you can then add back the programs you need and slowly add back others that are not as important or rarely used.

That is my advice, I have been doing it this way for a long time and it has always worked well for me. In fact, I just hard reset my 950XL two weeks ago. Over time I had accumulated too much and it is nice to start fresh ... it works well!

Good luck with your decision!!

el_loco---01 say: Ok here's the scoop.

In V10 the thumbnails only showed the high quality version but it took much longer to generate so the CPU was busy longer creating slowdown when you loaded a lot of pictures and then tried to use the phone.

In V12 a new approach seems to be taken where the thumbnails are generated in very low rez until you stay in the focus of a picture for a few seconds then it generates the high rez thumbnail. This speeds up the process of generating the first set of thumbnails and thus speeds up the phone when you just loaded a few hundred pictures.

So it's a good trade-off IMHO.
