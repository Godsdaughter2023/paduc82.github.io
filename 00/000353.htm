As some of your questions do involve the Xbox Music service, that we can address.  The Xbox Music Cloud service works on the basis of Matching.  What this means is that the music you rip to your Collection needs to also be available as well in the Xbox Music Marketplace.  Then the service allows you to stream that matched song, playing the version in that's in the marketplace.  If you have a CD that isn't available in the Xbox Music Marketplace, or its metadata can't be read, then it would not appear in your cloud collection unfortunately and thus, would not sync across your PC, phone and Xbox console.  Full cloud matching is also based on the premise that you have an active Xbox Music Subscription.  Now as for Onedrive, that does allow for online storage, but not all devices access it the same way, and typically, you can only view, edit, or play one track at a time.
