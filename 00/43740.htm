    audreyschoeman say:

    Hi Jazz46,

    I hope you've already sorted this out, but if not I managed it this morning! The problem seems to be that although the languages still appear in the list, it has actually deleted the language packs.

    So if you click on the 'English (United Kingdom) option above, three buttons should appear underneath. The ones to the left and right were grayed out on mine, but you want to click on the centre one. That takes you to the language options page.

    If your problem is the same as mine was, you will be faced with three headings with download buttons underneath them. The lower two had sizes in MB next to them and are for handwriting and speech recognition, the top one is for downloading the actual language pack. So you want to click on the gray button closest to the top of the page. A download bar will appear. Once that has downloaded, as long as it is set to be the default language, you just need to restart your PC and the majority of it will be back in English.

    To get this to apply to your whole PC and get rid of any residual Chinese (while it's starting up etc.) you need to go back into 'Region and Language' and click on 'Additional time, date and regional settings' right at the bottom.

    Click on 'Change location' under 'Region' and then click on the 'Administrative' tab in the dialogue that opens.

    In there you have the option to change the default language for your welcome screen, new user accounts, and non-Unicode programmes to English, so that you can understand everything on your PC.

    I hope that helps!
