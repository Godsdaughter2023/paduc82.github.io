How do I chart data and have the data points come from every other column in a row?

I would like to chart a series of data where I need every 4th column of a row to be selected as a data point. I also need the chart to fill in as more data points are filled in. 

For instance the data points are A5,A9,A13,A17

and as I add new information into A21,25 I would like the chart to update.

Any ideas?
bernie deitrick say: You need to have a contiguous range as the basis of your chart, with formulas that extract the data from every 4th row of that column.  For example, to extract A5, A9 etc, use the formula

=INDEX(A:A,4*ROW(A1)+1)

and copy down. Then use that range for your chart.

catalinux---01 say: As promised, I tried what I suggested earlier and it worked. Actually it was a bit easier.
So, what you need is a card reader and Total Commander (or any other file explorer that allows you to mess around with your computer files better than Explorer does).
All the steps I enumerated above are correct except for the last: that one is completely unnecessary. The folders you create and set as system folders will automatically be displayed with their "nice" name (e.g. "Music" instead of "predefmusic" ). That's it!
A few other prerequisites:
- the folder named "Extra" must exist, and the "predef" folders must be inside "Extra"
- the "predef" folders must be marked as system folders, otherwise you will end up having the icon you wanted, but with an ugly and long name; changing the name makes the icon revert to the one you had before (a simple folder icon)
- the names you can use are: predefgames, predefmusic, predefphotos, predefrecordings, predeftones and predefvideos. Inside "predeftones" you can create predefalerttones and predefringtones (also marked as system folders). I think there might be more of them, please post their predef-name and location, if you find any.

Thank you for your patience, enjoy.

catalinux---01 say: I found two more: predefcollections and predefthemes. They should be placed in "Extra", too.

If you wonder how to change folder attributes with Total Commander: select the folder, click on "File" in the menu bar and then select the first option - "Change attributes". Click on the "s System" check box until you see a check mark (usually two clicks). Press OK and you're done.

TIP: you can mark as system folder any folder you don't want to be deleted accidentally by you or by someone who gets a chance to browse trough your phone. This method also works for files.
