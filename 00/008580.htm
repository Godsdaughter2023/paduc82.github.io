jay freedman say:

If the problem is indeed related to a macro (discounting the possibility that the message isn't correctly describing whatever is really wrong), there are two things to do for troubleshooting:

  • Before starting Word, use File Explorer to go to the folders C:\Program Files (x86)\Microsoft Office\root\Office16\STARTUP and %appdata%\Microsoft\Word\STARTUP, and move any files from those folders to some other folder.
  • Also go to the folder %appdata%\Microsoft\Templates and rename the file Normal.dotm to something else such as OldNormal.dotm.

Then start Word. If the message doesn't appear, one of the files you moved or renamed is the culprit. You can try putting them back, one at a time, and restarting Word after each one to try to pinpoint the problem.

PM2 once enabled prevents the hard drive from being "turned on" when the computer is activated until a signal is sent from the motherboard and activates the drive. Creating a bootable DOS thumb drive and installing a hard drive utility capable of activating the hard drive is the only solution as of right now.
