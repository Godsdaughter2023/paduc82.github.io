MicrosoftForum ModeratorCommunity Star

The answer to this question is yes, but it will take a number of steps to accomplish.

First, let’s define the term “alias.” An alias is a secondary email address added to an already existing Microsoft account address. To put it another way, you have a mailbox with an existing email address created when you signed up for your Microsoft account and sometime later you added another email address going to the same mailbox. If you can’t sign in with it, but you can send and receive email with it, it’s an alias.

Next, you will need to know how to remove an alias from an account before you can use it for you primary mail address and sign-in for your Microsoft account. This is how it’s done:

  1. In your browser go to https://account.live.com/ManageAssocIds. You may need to sign in.
  2. Under Other email addresses, click Remove beneath the address you want to use as your primary sign-in address. If you don’t see your alias here, you are probably signed in with the wrong account.
  3. Click the Remove button on the confirmation message.

Now, let’s change the old alias address into your new primary sign-in address. Follow these steps:

  1. Go to http://outlook.com and sign in to your mailbox.
  2. With your Outlook.com mailbox displayed, click the “cog” icon next to your name in the upper right corner of the page.
  3. In the menu displayed, select More mail settings.
  4. In the Managing your account section of the page, select Rename your email address.
  5. In the New Microsoft account box, enter the address you used previously as the alias address.
  6. Click the Save button.

If you received the error in step 6, “youraddress@outlook.com isn't available”, verify that you are signed in with the same account you used in the alias removal steps above. If the alias you removed was associated with a different account than the one you sign-in with for steps 1-6 above and you are signed in this way on purpose, you are not going to be able get past the error right now. When alias addresses are removed from accounts, they are immediately available to be used again only by the account they were removed from. Only after being removed and not reused by the original account for 30 days will the removed alias address be available for any Microsoft account owner to add as an alias or rename their primary sign-in address to, as in the process above.
