doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

You don't add variables to either the header, footer or body of a document.  Rather, you add a docvariable field using code such as

With ActiveDocument
    .Fields.Add .Sections(1).Headers(wdHeaderFooterPrimary).Range, Type:=wdFieldEmpty, Text:="Docvariable varname"
    .Variables("varname").Value = "Some metadata"
    .Sections(1).Headers(wdHeaderFooterPrimary).Range.Fields.Update
End With

However, you could just use

ActiveDocument.Sections(1).Headers(wdHeaderFooterPrimary).Range.Text = "Some metadata" 

to add the text directly into the Header rather than adding a Docvariable field and then setting the value of the variable and updating the field so that it displays the value of the variable.

Sound in Non-linear Presentation - Microsoft Community

adennis516 say:

Sound in Non-linear Presentation

I am trying to make a non-linear presentation for a class.  When I link back to a slide that has already been visited the sound won't play again.  Any ideas how to get the audio to play each time the slide is viewed?  I have the audio clips to set to play automatically and to loop until stopped. 

I am using Powerpoint 2010.  Any help or insight into this would be GREATLY appreciated! 

john sr wilson say:

Two ways

A

Insert a blank slide before the slide with sound and give it a zero second auto transition. Make links to this slide. NOTE This may make it difficult to use "Last slide Viewed" buttons to get back.

B

Make the slide with sound a custom show (Slide Show > Custom Slide Show > Custom shows)

Link to the custom show and select "Show and Return" This is probably the best way in this circumstance.

geetesh bajaj say:

What John said.

Also you can add the same sound twice on the same slide -- to play automatically and on click. Keep the click options less obvious -- maybe the top right area of your slide?

Then as you move across your slides, the automatically options will make your slide play. Once you get back to that slide, click on the sound immediately to play in that case.
