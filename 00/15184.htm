What we can say for sure about such a file is that we expect that it contains text separated by a delimiter. But which encoding or what kind of delimiter (comma, semicolon or whatever) we don't know. There is no standard defined for that.

Now that's weird! 

But I've seen something like this before.  Sometimes an image will have a one or two-pixel band at one or more edges where the pixels are just a wee bit differently colored from the rest of the background.  It won't be visible to the eye.  That can affect the way b/g removal works.

I keep getting error messages about undeliverable e-mail I never sent - Microsoft Community

vmarchiano53 say:

I keep getting error messages about undeliverable e-mail I never sent to begin with.

I would like to know why I keep getting undeliverable e-mail messages on e-mail I never sent to begin with. Most are refering to e-mail addresses that I use to send to, or people that I knew.
brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:
Some spammer found your address in a public place on the Internet or compromised the PC of someone who has your address and is now using your address as the sender address of spam.  Your address has been hijacked.  Many of the addresses to which the spammer is sending are bogus addresses, but the domains are valid.  When the mail routers on those domains receive the spam and see that the address is nonexistent, they send a non-delivery report to the address they see as the sender, which is yours.  There's little you can do about this other than to wait a while.  Spammers change sending addresses frequently.  The NDR traffic should stop quite soon.
Brian Tillman [MVP-Outlook]
