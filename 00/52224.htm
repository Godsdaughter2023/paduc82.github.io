blowi_ydl say:

1709 Update breaks Optimus Graphic Card Switching Causing huge battery drain.

This problem is quite unnoticeable unless you have an optimus laptop running intel iGPU most of the time and switching to dGPU when required and you care about your battery life. I used AIDA64 to monitor my battery discharge rate.

My laptop's got 4720HQ+HD4600, and GTX 980M. Before the update, when dGPU is not in use, the battery discharge rate was around 15-20W under light use. When dGPU is activated but not under heavy load (open an CAD program, etc), it should be 20-25W which makes sense because keeping the dGPU  online consumes power.

After the 1709 update, when dGPU is off and no program straining CPU or harddisk,  it's already consuming 30-35W, making regular work on battery almost impossible. The funny thing is when dGPU is on, the discharge is similar to before, 20-25W.

dGPU off (little CPU and Harddisk load)

dGPU on w/ little load (open an dGPU required program and do nothing)

dGPU disconnected (disabled from device manager. This is the normal status for all windows 10 builds before 1709)

 

Looks like there's serious problem with keeping the dGPU idle. Maybe it's been constantly switched on and off so that the power is even higher than when it's on. The only way to get back to previous 15-20W consumption with iGPU is to disable dGPU in the device manager.

Windows 10 Freeze and Stutter
