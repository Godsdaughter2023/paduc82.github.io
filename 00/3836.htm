jeeped say:

=SUM(IF(Team="Fulfillment",IF(OR(Group="RTS",Group="RTP"),IF(Status="Open",1,0))))


Your array formula is summig either a 1 or a 0 so I am guessing that you actgually want to COUNT() with multiple conditions. There may be better methods that a SUM(IF(OR))) formula.

            

Your supplied formula is in E2 entered as a CSE (Ctrl+Shift+Enter) but hte count is wrong.
The formula in F2 uses COUNTIFS() and doubles the formula as a method of expressing the OR() criteria.
=COUNTIFS(Team,"Fulfillment",Group,"RTS",Status,"Open")+COUNTIFS(Team,"Fulfillment",Group,"RTP",Status,"Open")
The formula in G2 uses SUMPRODUCT() to count with a nested + section that covers the OR() criteria.
=SUMPRODUCT((Team="Fulfillment")*((Group="RTP")+(Group="RTS"))*(Status="Open"))
Your own formula could be expressed as doubled up like the COUNTIFS() is F2 like this,
=SUM(IF(Team="Fulfillment",IF(Group="RTP",IF(Status="Open",1,0))))+SUM(IF(Team="Fulfillment",IF(Group="RTS",IF(Status="Open",1,0))))
... or using an array constant to hold the OR() text values like,
=SUM(IF(Team="Fulfillment",IF(Group={"RTP","RTS"},IF(Status="Open",1,0))))
... both of which would be finalized with Ctrl+Shift+Enter. Neither of the other two formulas needs to be entered as an array formula.
