Had a look round and can't find anything that really answers my question, wondering if anyone here could help. I'm looking to effectively produce a two page spread in the template, so the user would create a new document, populate the first page with 'design option A' and when they move onto the second page, the template would apply 'design option B'. From that point on, the document would apply the designs alternately (so page 3 would be A, 4B, 5A etc.)

Am I missing something simple? Or hoping for too much out of a template?

Thanks in advance!

-Mark

suzanne s. barnhill say:

If the design elements are static and don't require user input, you can achieve this by enabling Different Odd and Even headers and footers and anchoring design elements to the appropriate header. This is most commonly used to have different running heads and page number placement on odd and even pages, but you can also have graphics in the margins, watermarks, and other design elements.

If this would meet your needs, note that you can also enable Different First Page to have a different cover page. Unless your template includes content, you can make it a one-page template (just the first page), as Word will remember the format of the odd and even headers even without those pages being present and will use them appropriately as pages are added. Of course, if you have a different cover page, then your first content page would be page 2, an even page, and would use the Even Page Header design; the facing page 3 would use the Odd Page Header design.
