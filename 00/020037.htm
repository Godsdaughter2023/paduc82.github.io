brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

> So....is this still an issue with Outlook?

It's not an issue with Outlook at all. It's the receiving server rejecting the messages. It's the server hosting the recipient's mailbox that is rejecting.

> can you help me understand how these bounced emails are being directed into my outlook from the

> spammer, he must have my IP address?

It has nothing to do with IP address. The spammer found out your email somehow. You published it in public somewhere or perhaps someone who had your address in their contacts was hacked. However the spammer got your address, he then used it as the sender address in the messages he sent out. The From and To values you see on a mail message have nothing whatsoever to do with the real sending and receiving addresses. They're just text strings tat happen to look like email addresses. A smap tool can be programmed to use any address-looking string. The spammer used yours. Then, because the address the message was sent to didn't exist, the receiving mail system bounced a response back to the only sender it could see: your address.

You can't block the spammer because his sending system can be anywhere in the world. All you can do is, perhaps, block the false non-delivery reports using a rule or rules, if you can figure out enough common strings in the messages to do so. However, it's a poor way to try to control this backscatter.

techfreak_ say:

Since billing consists of either credit purchase or a subscription, actual consumption of credit or minutes appears seperately. You can utilize the two to determine what is consuming credit and what is consuming minutes. 

  • Purchase history - Your manual or automatic charges for services.
  • Usage - Your consumption of those services.

No subscription covers special, premium, shared-cost, or non-geographical numbers. Pay as you go (credit) has an initial connection charge per call + call rate + minute consumption to the next full minute (i.e. 1 minute + 1 second = 2 minutes). VAT/GST charges are applied (inclusive/exclusive) when applicable. Deductions of credit are to the next full cent after the call is completed (i.e 65.3 cents = 66 cents). No subscription covers the cost of SMS texts. If you intentionally or unintentionally send them to a phone number, credit will be consumed. Auto-Recharge occurs when your balance dips below $2.
