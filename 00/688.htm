bernie deitrick say:

With the table that you show, the formula in cell I5

=SUMPRODUCT(B4:D4,F4:H4)/SUM(F4:H4)    (which = 192.12)

will not give you a meaningful number.  How it is evaluated is

=SUMPRODUCT({5," ",200.2},{9," ",208.4})/SUM({9," ",208.4})

The SUMPRODUCT is multiplying 9 and 5 and adding it to the product of 200.2 and 208.4

and the SUM is adding a count (9) and and average weight.

In the cell where you have 838.3 (Cell D16), however, you could get a meaningful weighted average, using the formula

=SUMPRODUCT(B4:B13,D4:D13)/SUM(B4:B13)

All of the numbers in B4:B13 are counts, and D4:D13 are weights, so this is a valid average weight for all 34 members.

When copied to the cell with 838 (H16) the formula will become

=SUMPRODUCT(F4:F13,H4:H13)/SUM(F4:F13)

giving a valid weight for all 41 members.  But - since you have different populations, you can't really draw any conclusions about weight gain or loss - you need to restrict your analysis to individuals that are in both populations.
