Spyware question - Microsoft Community

chicomendez say:

Spyware question

How do i know if my computer is have been used or is been used by hackers? and what can i do to prevent?

[moderator note - changed "hikers" to "hackers"]

kosh vorlon say:

Hi Chico,

 

It's hard to be certain.  Sometimes it's obvious - you see emails being sent using your e-mail account (and appearing in your sent items folder) that you aren't sending - BAM!  Sometimes it's subtle (your system is running much slower or your network usage is much higher than normal including when you aren't using the system and have no background tasks running that you know about).  It depends how you've been hacked (directly, through malware, by access through a hole in your firewall, by getting in through a router that isn't secure or that has been compromised, by allowing someone access to your computer (in person or remotely) who installed and/or did who knows what).  It depends on the results of a bit of research and checking to see differences or changes that you didn't do (like an unknown user or network connection, files and programs that you didn't install - especially if processes are running, sudden increases in SPAM or seeing accounts or websites accessed at times you know you didn't do it or suddenly seeing charges on your financial accounts that you didn't make, system reports that exist and/or that you've installed, and so forth).

 

Start here in terms of Microsoft Security and Safety information and references: http://www.microsoft.com/security/default.aspx.

 

Check out this article: http://www.microsoft.com/security/default.aspx.  Note a dozen or more links to related sites. Check them out and and additional sites they recommend.  When done, you'll know a great deal about the basics.

 

Download and install Process Explorer http://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/sysinternals/bb896653 and Process Monitor http://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/sysinternals/bb896645 and check to see exactly what is running on your computer and confirm that it is all necessary and that you can account for everything listed.  If in doubt, start here for assistance with any unknowns: http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/windows_other-performance?page=1&tab=all&tm=1300700638376.

 

How to prevent?  Well, a great deal of information about that is in the first article above (and the various links in it).  But let me tell you what I do.

 

While my work requires me to take risks that are higher than most so I use some measures that are more than an average user needs (but while some like the bold parts and even some of the others can/should be done by everyone, maybe you don't need as many programs and/or don't need to run scans as frequently), here's what I do (and it isn't 100% but nothing is, and in some cases only catches things after they've already gotten into the system, but it's a good process while remaining reasonable and they can all work together - as proven by the fact that I use them):

 

I use MSE for real-time protection and run scheduled daily quick scans with a scheduled bi-weekly full scan.  If you aren't and would like to switch to it, please post back as it isn't as simple as many programs to do it right and I'll be happy to provide you with detailed instructions if you'll tell me your Operating system and what security software you are using now (all names and versions).

 

I update and periodically (every couple of weeks or so) run full scans with Malwarebytes: http://www.malwarebytes.org/ and SuperAntiSpyware: http://superantispyware.com/ and ESET online scanner: http://www.eset.com/us/online-scanner Get only the free versions. Any that also have paid versions include features like real-time protection that cannot be used along with MSE anyway (in these cases), so there's no point in spending the money anyway.

 

I use Secunia PSI http://secunia.com/vulnerability_scanning/personal/ to make sure all my programs are updated and current (since updates often plug security holes in the software and thus can be important as many people never check and/or update their software unless prompted - and this way I don't need to check everything since notifications and sometimes updates are automatic).

 

I use WOT (Web-of-Trust) http://www.mywot.com/ to help me avoid potentially dangerous sites (to assist SmartScreen).  It's not a guarantee either way, but it's a good indicator - especially with searches.

 

Always use strong passwords (see the first link above which contains information on how to do that) - but not so complicated that you'll forget them without writing them down (which defeats the purpose) and change them periodically.  Do not use the same one everywhere.  Avoid the temptation to have your computer automatically save your passwords and/or login information as that just makes it easier to find and access other things if they get their foot in the door. 

 

If using a wireless router, make sure it is secured as tightly as possible with the best method compatible with the device, your network, and your computers.  Your ISP will often assist you in setting that up (if it's a supported router) or sometimes the router manufacturer will assist you (though unless done with online information or while still under warranty, they sometimes will charge a fee).  If neither will help, it's possible you can get help from these forums starting: http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/windows_other-security?page=1&tab=all&tm=1300700638376 or http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/windows_other-networking?page=1&tab=all&tm=1300700638376. 

 

I always follow the following advice from Stephen Boots - MVP:

 

-Make sure that the Windows Firewall is enabled. No other firewall truly offers better protection or is needed. This one is perfectly fine.  But make sure that you only have the ports you use and need enabled.  The default allows many that most users will never use but each is a hole in your defenses.  Pare it down to the minimum needed (but don't go so far that you block yourself in the process).

 

-Make sure that all important/critical updates, including service packs for the operating system and programs are installed from Microsoft Update (Windows Update).

 

-Make sure Internet Explorer is at version 8 or 9 and updated with all patches.

 

-In Internet Explorer 8 or 9, use the SmartScreen Filter.

 

-Make sure that IE Internet Security settings are at least set to medium-high (default).

 

-Enable the pop-up blocker in IE.

 

-On Vista and Windows 7 make sure that User Account Control (UAC) ON and not running with elevated privileges.

 

-Make sure that Windows Automatic Updates are set to at least notify, but the preferred setting is to download and install automatically. If you update manually, be sure to update as soon as possible after being notified of available updates.

 

-Make sure that installed applications, especially Adobe Acrobat, Adobe Flash, and Java are at their latest versions. Many vendors are regularly updating and patching for security holes.  Secunia PSI mentioned above will help automate this for you - I'd never be without it.

 

-Never click through links from unknown sources and use caution even if they are from a "trusted" source.

 

-Never open unsolicited email attachments.

 

-Practice safe web browsing.

 

-Always use a standard user account unless you have a specific need for an administrator account.  Then switch, do the administrative task, and return to the standard account.  This greatly limits the access of any type of infection of intrusion into your system since administrative access isn’t in place.

 

I hope this helps.  This is a very broad area and to do it right you need to learn more than we can provide you in a forum.  Start with the resources and information above.  Then use Bing or Google and search for (without the quotes) "How do I protect my computer"  "How do I prevent my computer from being hacked"  "How do I secure my computer and my online accounts"  "How do I secure my wireless router" and well, you get the idea and then follow the good suggestions.  Do NOT install or use a Registry Cleaner no matter what some site might tell you!!  Also contact your ISP and your e-mail provider's technical support and ask them the same question (or in their forums) and see what they have to say.

 

Good luck!

 

Kosh

Sub RefreshPivotTables()
   Dim i As Integer
   Application.ScreenUpdating = False
   
   For i = 1 To Sheet2.PivotTables.Count
      Sheet2.PivotTables(i).PivotCache.Refresh
      Sheet2.PivotTables(i).NullString = "0"
   Next i
   
   For i = 1 To Sheet3.PivotTables.Count
      Sheet3.PivotTables(i).PivotCache.Refresh
      Sheet3.PivotTables(i).NullString = "0"
   Next i
End Sub
