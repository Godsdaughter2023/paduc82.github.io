No, there are no native methods for resizing a form. The Anchoring methods are for specific controls - you would select the control in Design view, and then set the anchoring properties. This works if you are designing in the lowest resolution you expect to encounter, but if you're designing forms on a high resolution system, and you'll be deploying to lower resolutions, your forms won't look right.


There are 3rd party utilities that do this, like Peter's Software ShrinkerStretcher (http://www.peterssoftware.com/ss.htm). I've used this in a few projects, and it works very well. It's not perfect, but it's the closest you'll get in the Access environment

toby ornott say:

Unfortunately the recover deleted items Dumpster mode, does not allow for deleting massive amounts at any one time.  The most I have been able to delete in a single action have been 35.  The process goes like this:

  1. After you have sent messages from the Deleted items into the recover...Dumpster, check the first message and
  2. scroll to approximately 25 messages (note: Though I have mentioned 35, it is best first to attempt a smaller amount), and
  3. hold down the Shift-Key, and mark the 25th message.  If everything is done exactly as described, the Dumpster will automatically check all 25 messages.  If for some reason an unmarked message breaks that continuity, then only messages from the beginning to that break will be deleted and you will need to repeat the process.
  4. After you have noted that all 25 messages have been automatically marked, then click purge and they will be gone forever.
  5. This process takes approximately 1-minute of your time.

Now, I note that your primary interest is in greater numbers than 25, so see if you can do larger amounts than 35. When I first began dumping messages, I had hundreds and more were appearing daily.  So I worked diligently for short, annoying, and wasteful periods until I had developed a completely empty Dumpster.  It took me months.  When you reach that level, the miserable software announces "We didn't find anything to show here" as if it expects to be rewarded; but, in all honesty, you will grind your teeth and spit an obscenity for having wasted your valuable time.

There is a lesson here that one learns from all the above: purging the Dumpster needs to be performed daily in blocks of 25 - 35....

Or you can wait until the server purges everything for you.  I have read unofficial remarks that the server performs a purge every 30 days.  Yet I have never seen any indication of that actually happening.  And until there is an official Support.Office.Outlook.com document acknowledging that truth, I will continue with my daily purges, as I do not trust Outlook.
