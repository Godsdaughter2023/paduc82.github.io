gerry c j cornell say:

In the list of Services for that svchost item is SuperFetch. This service has generated debate from time to time about it's ability to cause high CPU activity. Right click the svchost process and select Properties. Click the Services tab and place the cursor on SuperFetch and click the Stop button. If you do that when svchost is generating high CPU activity and the activity stops or substantially reduces you may have found the cause of the high CPU activity. You should test a number of times.

You need to be careful stopping services because a few can have a dramatic result. SuperFetch can be safely stopped. Please note I am not advocating permanently disabling the SuperFetch service. I am only suggesting you test to see if it is the cause of your high CPU activity.
