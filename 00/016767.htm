stefan blom say:

To expand just a bit on what Suzanne has said, it is very easy to inadvertently add content, and/or a manual page break, into a bookmark. This happens when you type, including pressing Enter, at the beginning of a bookmarked/cross-referenced paragraph, because the added content will be added inside the bookmark (more specifically at the beginning of the bookmark).

You can see this for yourself if you experiment with a manual bookmark (inserted via Insert | Bookmark) and keep bookmarks displayed (File | Options | Advanced: "Show bookmarks") during the experiment. Word's hidden bookmarks behave exactly the same way.

Once the problem has occurred, recreating the bookmark (the cross-reference) is the easiest fix.

To prevent the problem from occurring in the first place, make sure that you add content (or press Enter) at the end of text paragraphs instead.
