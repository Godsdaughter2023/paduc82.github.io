So it seems that unfortunately the controller was 'bricked" after a failed update which will not turn on anymore. This happens due to the firmware becoming corrupted since the update failed or was interrupted.

There is a way to get this fixed but it has only been confirmed to work using an Xbox One console, which you do not have but you can try the same method on your PC.

 - Power down the PC by holding the power-button down until it powers off.

 - Unplug the PC from the wall for 5 minutes to be sure everything is 100% discharged.

 - Plug the controller into one of the PC's USB ports using a micro-USB cable.  Make sure there is nothing else plugged into any of the USB ports. The controller should be the only thing plugged in. Make sure you take out any batteries out of the controller.

- Boot up your PC.

- While the PC is booting up, try to turn on the controller by pressing the controller's Xbox button a few times in 3 second intervals until the controller turns on. 

Finally, head into the Xbox accessories app and make sure the controller updated as it usually completes the update during the reboot.
