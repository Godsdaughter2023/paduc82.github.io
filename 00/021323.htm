braintrove.com say:

You should be able to add your page break. However, I think you might be getting your terminology mixed up. Page breaks cannot be inserted into a repeating table (at least not directly). However, they can be inserted into a repeating section.

 

http://www.braintrove.com/id/137


A repeating section can be made to appear like a repeating table by inserting a layout table, and appropriately adjusting the borders, margins, and padding for both the table and the section.

 

Now--as to the "not directly" comment. InfoPath 2013 won't allow you to insert a page break into a repeating table, but you can insert one elsewhere, then carefully grab it with your mouse and drag-and-drop it into the right-most cell of your table. However, while you can do this, it is likely worthless as it will force every row of your table onto a new page. This can be somewhat controlled by dropping the page break into a section in that same cell, and then adding a formatting rule to the section to ensure that it is only displayed every X rows--but that is minimal control.
