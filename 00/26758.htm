bobkiefer say:

Go to this link for more detail:

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/office/forum/office_365hp-outlook/outlook/b996ce2d-8aa9-4ade-9ebf-372221eb865e

In that thread, user 'ebibaud' says:

Here is why Outlook is probably hanging in your environment with the "ssl.gstatic.com\ui\v1\icons\common" loading message.  That happened here inside our environment and users were getting those errors when they were receiving links from Google drive documents.  

Now, when you open up an email that contains that link from Google, it tries to load an image from the link "ssl.gstatic.com\ui\v1\icons\common", but it does so by going through port "445" (not the usual port 80 or 443).  

Since I only allow a certain number of outgoing ports to be opened in our environment, port 445 was not opened and was what caused the timeout issue on Outlook with "ssl.gstatic.com\ui\v1\icons\common".

Now, you can either open up port 445 (TCP - Outgoing only) or simply go within your Outlook security settings (File\Options\Trust Center\Trust Center Settings\Email Security) and enable "Read as Plain Text".  

Doing the later will allow Outlook to open up the email in text mode, but you will still have the URL within the email to download the Google drive document and since the pictures are not being loaded automaticaly, you will not get the timeout issue. (and it is also more secure than opening port 445 imo...)

https://www.windowsphone.com/en-GB/how-to/wp8/settings-and-personalization/reset-my-phone

To reset an unresponsive phone

If your phone is unresponsive and you can't reach Settings , you may be able to reset your phone using the hardware buttons. On Lumia phones (and some others), do the following to reset your phone:

  1. Press and hold the Volume down and Powerbuttons at the same time until you feel a vibration (about 10-15 seconds).

  2. When you feel the vibration, release the buttons, then immediately press and hold the Volume down button until you see a large exclamation mark.

  3. Once the exclamation mark appears, press the following four buttons in this order: Volume up , Volume down , Power , Volume down . Your phone should now reset and restart itself. (It might take a while for the reset to finish.)
