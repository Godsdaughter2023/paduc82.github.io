You've run into Excel's floating point precision error. You need to ROUND() the result to avoid these small deviances.

Understanding Floating Point Precision

mike h.. say:
When I sum three cells the total that Excel gives me is 0.000000000006 less than it should be. My numbers are 102136.15, -50814 and -50814. This error always occurs for these numbers and some others but otherwise the sum function works fine.


Hi,

 

I guess you mean you get the results below. This is caused by the way Excel and nearly all other platforms do math where they use the IEEE 754 standard for handling floating point numbers. Excel works in binary and often there is no precise binary representation of a floating point number. This apparent error is a consequence of that.

 

Have a look at the article below which explains things in detail and in particular have a look at the bit about rounding numbers. For example instead of =SUM(A1:A3) which produces the result below you can use

 

=ROUND(SUM(A1:A3),2)

 

 

and get the result you expect.
