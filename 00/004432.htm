Sumifs, Referencing cells within a quoted logical statement

Hi, I'm trying to keep track of my food spending, and I want to create a neat and efficient feature in my workbook where when I input a date and amount into my "Food Log" sheet, excel will automatically add that to the respective week in the "Spent" column of the "Food Budget" sheet. I have attached some screen shots to help visualize my problem.

I figured the best way to go about this is to use the sumifs function. The sum_range should be the contents of the "Amount" column in "Food Log", the first criteria_range (and also second) should be the contents of the "Date" column of the "Food Log" sheet. The first criteria should be that the Date must be at least the date of the corresponding week cell in "Food Budget", and the second is that the Date must be less than the date immediately below the corresponding the date of the week cell in "Food Budget" (to my knowledge you have to break up the inequality of Week<=Cell<Week+1 into separate Week<=Cell and Cell<Week+1 criteria).

The only problem is that you must put quotes around any logical statements in sumif and sumifs, and doing so appears to render you unable to reference other cells within the quotations. Is there any way around this?

jeeped say:

You should be able to split off the logical operator into quotes and concatenate the remainder so that it remains a cell reference that will adjust with positioning.

=SUMIFS('Food Log'!$E$3:$E$100, 'Food Log'!$A$3:$A$100,">="&'Food Log'!$A3, 'Food Log'!$A$3:$A$100, "<"&'Food Log'!A4)

SUMIF function Adds the cells specified by a given criteria
SUMIFS function Adds the cells in a range that meet multiple criteria

Change the value to -225.

Restart PC.
