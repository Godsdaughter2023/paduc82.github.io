Word count discrepancy between statistics box and status bar - Microsoft Community

cam.gz say:

Word count discrepancy between statistics box and status bar

I am working on a word document and I have noticed that the word count in the status bar sometimes suddenly drops and stops matching the word count given in the statistics box.
According to the statistics box, my document is 7,069 word long including footnotes and endnotes, and 6,156 including footnotes and endnotes.
When I opened the document, the word count in the status bar was 7,069 too, but it suddenly dropped to 6,526. (with "include footnotes and endnotes" checked in the statistics box.) When I uncheck "footnotes and endnotes" in the statistics box, the number matches the one given by the status bar.
It keeps happening and I would be very grateful for some help!

john mcghie say:
Live Word Count (the one on the status bar) and Word Count (the one in the Tools menu) are different "processes" that run the same counting algorithm.

Live Word Count tries to dynamically display the number of words in the document, and the position of the cursor.  Because it runs as a low priority background task, it can sometimes get behind.  Word Count stops all running operations to count the words as a high priority.  By the time the dialog appears, the process has always completed.

So there can sometimes be a discrepancy between the two figures.  If you really need to know, either use the one on the menu, or click the word count in the status bar (same thing): you will get a finite value instantly.

Be aware that what you have selected, and of course, any tracked changes, can dramatically affect the results.

And of course, we both know that if the number of words in a document is excessively important, chances are the content is not receiving the attention it should be :-)
