stephenpsa say:

Hi Stefania,

I agree that such a fine machine deserves a Pro version. There is of course no way to simply flick a switch and turn a Home edition into a Pro edition, nor, as far as I know, can you buy a Home-to-Pro update. So, in order to get Pro on your machine, you will have to install a Pro License.

A bit of general info: Licenses are sold in two ...types:

OEM Licenses, only available to hardware manufacturers, and standalone/full licenses which you can buy. The big difference between the two is that the first is tied to particular hardware, while the latter is not. So, even if you have an 'old' W7/8/81 Pro license lying around, there is no guarantee that you will be able to use that on a new machine.

And even If that old W7/8/8.1 Pro is a 'Full' license, well, the official time for 'free-upgrade-to-W10' is over so it must have been used and upgraded to W10 at some time in the past.

To summarize, provided you have a 'full' W7/8/8.1 Pro License that was, once, upgraded to W10, then you can simply do a Clean Install from W10 Media and use it's key to Activate it. (i.e. no need to start with W7/8/...)

In all other cases you have no option but to buy a full license.

Regards,

Stephen

don cuthbert say:

You can easily and quickly switch from Home Edition to Pro Edition without reinstalling Windows, but the end result will not be activated. With Windows 10, that appears only to eliminate access to the Personalization section of the Settings app, such as changing wallpaper. Running without activation does not appear to ever impact other normal functionality, nor cause the OS to quit working or booting.

So if you are willing to run Pro without activation, entering the generic Windows 10 Pro Product Key in the Change Product Key section of Settings (as shown below) will perform the upgrade from Home to Pro. This Product Key is common to all free upgrades from Windows 7 or 8 Pro Edition. (However, if you ever choose to "downgrade" to your licensed Windows 10 Home Edition, it would require a full reinstall of Windows 10).

Hello there, barinderkhoulia! Thanks for posting your query on Microsoft Community.

About your concern, if your phone stops working or responding like it used to, restart it to get back on track.

To restart, press and hold the Power button until a Slide down to power off message appears on-screen, then swipe down. (It usually takes about three seconds for the message to appear.) To turn your phone back on, press the Power button.

If that doesn't work, press and hold the Volume down and Power buttons at the same time until you feel a vibration (about 10 to 15 seconds), then release the buttons. Your phone will restart automatically.


Please be reminded that:

Your phone may vibrate as soon as you press the Volume down and Power buttons because it's changing your ringtone to vibrate. Wait to release the buttons until you feel the second vibration 10 to 15 seconds later.

If you don't let go of the buttons immediately after the vibration at 10 to 15 seconds, your phone may show a black startup screen instead of restarting normally. If that happens, try pressing the button combo again.

Not all phones restart this way, so check with your phone's manufacturer to find out how to force a restart.


If your phone has a replaceable battery (it's often under your phone's back panel), you can also restart your phone by removing and reinserting the battery, and then pressing the Power button to turn on your phone. The date and time may be off after you restart this way, but you can fix that by swiping over to the All apps list from Start, then selecting Settings > Time & language > Date & time.
