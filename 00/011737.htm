Hi Wayne,

When you say “most of our clients have domains something generic like office.local then their email is something completely different like xyz.com”, do you mean your clients are created in their local AD with domain office.local, and they also have mailboxes with domain xyz.com in Office 365, now you want to get them synced between their local AD and Office 365?

Then edit the local users using SMTP match to get them matched. Here is an article for your reference: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/kb/2641663

If there is any further question, please feel free to post back.

Vivian

mayor of galt's gulch say:

When creating / editing your spreadsheet,

select a cell

at the top of the screen, you will see the bar for typing your data or a function. That bar is labeled "Type here" in light gray text.

to the left of that bar, you will see "fx"

if you click the "fx" (which stands for functions), a drop down list box will display a list of recently used functions.

if you scroll down that list, you will see a complete list of all available functions, grouped by type of function.

Additionally, the ribbon bar appears at the top of the screen. In that ribbon bar, you will see "formulas."

click on "formulas" and you will see a row of types of functions, as well as "recent" functions and "..." for other types of functions, as there are too many types of functions to display all of them on the ribbon simultaneously.
