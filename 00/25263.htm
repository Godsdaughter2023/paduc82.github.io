Live Tiles is about get apps information and app status right away. But you only go to Start Menu to lauch a app so it does not make any sense to hide live tiles in a place you go only when you have a specific purpose. It kills the very reason of existence of live tiles.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

You actually can do this, but it's so much trouble that Charles's suggestion of formatting the text as white would probably be better, especially since it hides the text even when nonprinting characters (including Hidden text) are displayed.

If you want to do it as Hidden, however, here's how to do it:

  1. Double-click each name to select it and press Ctrl+Shift+H.
  2. When you hide nonprinting characters, you'll see that the numbers 2, 3, and 4 also disappear. Despite the fact that you selected just the name and not the paragraph mark following it, the paragraph mark will be included.
  3. But you can select just the paragraph mark and press Ctrl+Spacebar to remove the Hidden formatting, and the numbers will reappear while the names are hidden when nonprinting characters are not displayed.

From what I can understand, your phone is unstable, and your memory card appears to be corrupt.

 

First, backup the contents of your phone memory using the latest PC Suite. Next, backup the music, videos, pictures, etc. on the memory card to your PC.

 

Does your phone work fine without the memory card? If it does, then connect the phone in Mass Storage mode to your PC, and format it. Now see if the phone works fine with the memory card inserted. Once verified, you can copy your music and other files to the card and continue using the phone.

 

If you still face issues, then proceed with the next step

 

Check for firmware updates, here. If there is a new version, install it using Nokia Software Updater. Make sure you have a stable internet connection and a full battery before you begin the update process. And, most importantly, follow the on-screen prompts correctly.

 

Else, try the following:

1. Soft reset: I assume you've made a backup of your phone memory contents. Type *#7370# on the stand-by screen. Enter the security code when prompted (default is 12345). This would wipe your phone memory clean.

2. Hard reset: This is to be resorted to only if your phone has become extremely unstable. Turn off your phone, press and hold down the 3, * and Call (green) buttons, and turn on your phone without releasing them. Let go off these three keys only when you see the Nokia logo or other signs of life on your phone.
