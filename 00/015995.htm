vba cell location - Microsoft Community

fride360 say:

vba cell location

Is there a way to find the cell location on a sheet?  For example whenever I click in a cell that cell location (ex. C12) shows up in cell A1.  So everytime I click in a different cell the value in A1 changes.
hansv mvp say:

The address of the active cell is displayed in the Name box on the left hand side of the formula bar.

If you want to display the address of the current selection (even if it consists of multiple cells, even multiple areas) in A1:

Right-click the sheet tab.

Select View Code from the context menu.

Copy the following code into the module window:

 

Private Sub Worksheet_SelectionChange(ByVal Target As Range)
    Range("A1").Value = Target.Address(RowAbsolute:=False, ColumnAbsolute:=False)
End Sub

 

Switch back to Excel.

Incoming emails are bouncing

I am having trouble with my incoming emails bouncing back..........either with an error or failure message.  A few emails are coming through.  What can be done so all of my emails are coming to my address?  Thanks!

zigzag3143 (ms -mvp) say:
These are the following reasons why emails are missing from your mailbox:
Your account has been marked as inactive 
You have accidentally deleted your emails / being accessed someone.
You configured your account on an email client and disable to save messages on the server.
You have message rules that cleans up your messages.

First you may try to restore your messages using the restore tool of Hotmail.
http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/hotmail/how-recover-deleted-messages

If you have configured your email address on an email client, please  set your option to leave messages on the server.
http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/hotmail/send-receive-email-from-mail-client
(Refer to Prevent losing email in Hotmail when you've set up POP3)

To check, your email rules please follow these steps
(for Hotmail)
1. Sign in your Hotmail account and go to your inbox.
2. Move your cursor over the Folders link on the left pane.
3. Click on the Gear icon, and then click Manage Rules
4. Check for message rule that cleans up your messages and delete it.

(for Outlook)
Gear on the right>more mail settings>customizing Outlook> managing rules....

Lastly, to ensure that someone is not using your account, update your password and reset information.
http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/hotmail/hacked-account-faq
http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/hotmail/security?T1=t2
