negrabi247 say:

 

It worked, I cant believe that all it was.  OMG this phone was driving me crazy, and thats all it was -- the connection between the battery and prongs on the phone.  
Thats what i get for being a cheap **bleep**, my OEM battery was shot and instead of buying one from nokia directly or a proper store, i bought it off ebay, from some seller in china  thinking i got a great deal, because it was only five bucks! with shipping.  I knew it was too good to be true. And i got exactly what i paid for!!  the battery worked fine otherwise, and I couldnt put two and two together, that this problem started shortly after I got the replacement battery.  I thought maybe someone dropped my phone and thats what the cause was.  
but after readjusting the connectors on the battery, using  a needle to make the gaps between the connectors a little more closer the problem was solved.  I cant believe thats all it was, and it makes complete sense because most of the time the insert sim error came up was when the phone was not still, like in my pocket or in my hands, etc.  Its crazy that just a fraction of milimeter difference was causing the phone to do this.  I was sure that it was a hardware problem and I would be re-inserting my sim card forever.  But god bless the individual on this blog who wrote the post to fix this problem .  He was describing the same problem in a different model phone, so it took me forever to find that post, but im glad I did.  
So for anyone having this problem:
you just purchased a battery (from ebay, lol) or it could even be your pre-existing oem
and you get an error message stating 'insert sim card '
you take the sim card out and put it back and the phone is working fine again
but shortly after, the message comes back, and you notice it was mostly because the phone was moving around in your hands, or it was in your pocket, not so much when it is sitting still on a countertop, and you have to take out the sim card and put it back in, 
and this scenario happens over and over again---> THEN:
then chances are it may just be because of a loose connection between your battery and phone,  and just take a needle or something sharp and pull those connectors on the battery closer to each other so they have good contact with the prongs on the phone.  And what i also did was stick some paper about the thickness of a sim card directly under the battery and also on the opposite side of where the battery's connectors are, so it is in there really tight.   
It worked for me, and I tested it thoroughly to make sure that was the case, 
I put that phone in my pocket and ran a mile and when i checked it, it was still on and no damn error message.  whereas before if i just looked at it wrong it gave my the finger.  
So i just wanted to share this solution with everyone in the nokia community, I actually never respond to blogs but this time I definately had to, I guess if we combine everyone knowledge and experience well find the answer somehow.  And I did, so thank you.
