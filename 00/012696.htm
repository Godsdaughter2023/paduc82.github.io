jay freedman say:
When you know that changes have been made to the Normal template but you don't get a prompt to save it, that usually indicates that there's a badly written add-in that's preventing Word from recognizing that there have been any changes.

To track this down, disable or uninstall one add-in at a time, restarting Word after each one and testing whether a change in the template leads to a save prompt for the template. If and when you find an add-in that's preventing the save, you can try to get a later version of the add-in or decide to do without it.

By the way, for better results in future questions, you should know that there is no such thing as an "autotext macro". There is AutoText, and there are macros, and they usually have nothing to do with each other (although a macro -- a sort of miniature program inside Word -- can be created to cause Word to insert an AutoText entry).

Go to the Options dialog, go to Trust Center and click Trust Center Settings, then click Privacy Options. Uncheck the option "Make hidden markup visible when opening or saving". This is a per-document setting.
