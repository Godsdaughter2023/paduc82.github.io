  Range("A1").Calculate

  ActiveSheet.Calculate

  For Each Ws In Worksheets

  Application.CalculateFull

Message: The modem (or other connecting device) was disconnected due to link failure.

This error appears when the modem (in the case of dial-up or broadband connections) or tunnel (in the case of VPN connections) is disconnected due to a network failure or a failure in the physical link to the modem. The following are possible reasons for the failure.

  1. A problem in the network between the modem and the RAS server might have caused the basic dial-up or, in the case of a broadband connection, PPPoE connection, or VPN tunnel to fail.
  2. Check connectivity between the modem and the telephone/cable connection jack. If an external modem is being used, check the physical connectivity between the modem and the computer.

    • The wireless access point might have gone down due to loss of power or for other reasons.
    • The user's computer might be out of the operating range of the wireless network or the RF signal strength might be weak.
  4. The RAS server might have failed or restarted and closed the connection. Check the event logs on the RAS server.

I believe what you see are infinitesimal differences due, in part at least, to differences in the arithmetic implementation.

When I format the trendline equation ("label") and the LINEST results to display 15 significant digits, I see the following for the Set D data (C68:D108) using Excel 2007.

(Note:  Excel 2010 might have different results due to "improvements" in these functions.)



Note that there are infinitesimal differences in most coefficients.  For example, 3rd-order c2 is -1.21977777777778E-01 in one case and -1.21977777777774E-01 in the other.

The "larger" absolute differences are in the infinitesimal coefficients on the order of 1E-14 to 1E-18.  The "greatest" difference is in 3rd-order c1:  -2.48122687955229E-18 v. -6.66133814775094E-15.

Arguably, the order 1E-15 coefficient is 2700 times larger than the order 1E-18 coefficient.

But note that all of those coefficients are infinitesimally small compared to the data (0 to 4).  This is especially significant for the c1 term; but it is even significant for the c4 term in this case.

Consequently, the greatest absolute difference in estimated-y based on each set of coefficients (2nd to 4th order LINEST and trendline) is less than 7E-13.  That is a difference in the 13 decimal place.

-----

Such infinitesimal differences are common in Excel, among many applications.  They arise because Excel represents numbers and performs arithmetic using the native-computer 64-bit binary floating-point.

For example, IF(10.1-10=0.1,TRUE) returns FALSE(!) because of such floating-point anomalies.

And such anomalies are sensitive to the order in which arithmetic operations are performed.  For example, a-b+c might be different from a+c-b.

([EDIT] Usually the difference is infinitesimal.  But really it depends on the relative magnitudes of a, b and c.)

So even if the LINEST and trendline algorithms are identical, simply performing operations in slightly different, but mathematically-equivalent order might result in the infinitesimal differences that we see here.

(And note:  the infinitesimal differences can accumulate and magnify to the point where they make a significant difference, albeit not in your case.)
