xbf amber m say:

Hi Rich,

Thanks for replying to keep us updated. Sorry to hear that you're still stuck on the same screen, regardless of the controller. If your console isn't responding, and you've already tried the physical power cycle as Parity recommended without a result, then you may need to perform another reset to the console to get it back into shape. Since its stuck on a screen and won't go any further, you may wish to bring up the troubleshooting screen in order to do another reset.

  1. Unplug the network cable if you're using a wired network connection.
  2. Power off your console, and then unplug the power cord to ensure that the console is completely powered off.
  3. Wait 30 seconds, and then plug the power cord back in.
  4. Press and hold the BIND button (located on the left side of the console) and the EJECT button (located on the front of the console), and then press the Xbox button on the console.
  5. Continue holding the BIND and EJECT buttons for 10-15 seconds.
  6. Listen for two “power-up” tones a couple of seconds apart. You can release the BIND and EJECT buttons after the second power-up tone.

This should bring up the Troubleshoot screen and you can select to Reset this Xbox again (its recommended you reset & remove everything). Just make sure you have all of your log in information for any profiles (and your network) prior to proceeding. If you have an external HD connected to your console, you'll want to remove that prior to the reset as well.

If this doesn't work, you'll need to bring up the Troubleshoot screen again and its recommended that you perform an Offline System Update to the console. Beyond that and barring that it doesn't work, you would need to set up a repair for your console. Hopefully, it won't come to that, but if it does, we'd be more than happy to help you out!

I hope that this helps a bit more and please let us know the results!

andre da costa say:
Press Windows key + X
Click Device Manager
Expand Human Interface Device

Right click HID-Compliant Touch Screen
Click Enable

If that does not work, right click the same HID-Compliant Touch Screen
Click Uninstall
Confirm the uninstallation

Exit Device Manager
Restart your computer

Restart the computer to reinstall the touch screen driver.

If that does not work, make sure your BIOS and display drivers are updated:
https://support.hp.com/si-en/document/bph07165

Use the HP PC Hardware Diagnostics tests to detect problems with the touch screen hardware.

Plug the AC adapter into the computer.
Press the Power button for at least five seconds to turn off the computer.
Turn on the computer and immediately press Esc repeatedly, about once every second. When the menu appears, press the F2 key.
When the HP PC Hardware Diagnostics screen displays, click Component Tests.
Click Touch Screen.
There are two interactive touch screen hardware tests. Start by clicking Touch Pointer Test.
Read the on-screen instructions, then click Run once.
Touch each of the blocks on the screen to erase them. The test ends after all blocks are erased or after three minutes have passed, and results are displayed.

Click Main menu to return to the main screen and run the second touch screen test.
Click Component Tests, then click Touch Screen.
Click Drag And Drop Test.

Read the on-screen instructions, then click Run once.
Drag each orange rectangle to the box indicated by the arrows with a finger or a stylus. The test ends after all blocks are repositioned or after three minutes have passed, and results are displayed.


If this does not work, I recommend you reset your computer.
