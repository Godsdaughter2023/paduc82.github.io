kyle maston say: I resolved this issue myself. After digging for an hour or so i found that the issue is with Windows Indexing.

To turn off indexing:
  1. Open up "My Computer."
  2. Right-click on your hard drive (usually "C:") and choose "Properties."
  3. Uncheck the box at the bottom that reads "Allow Indexing Service to..."
  4. Click OK, and files will be removed from memory. This removal may take a few minutes to complete.

To disable the indexing service:

  1. In the "Start" menu, choose "Run."
  2. Type "services.msc" and press Enter.
  3. Scroll-down to "Indexing Service" and double-click it.
  4. If the service status is "Running", then stop it by pressing the "Stop" button.
  5. To make sure this service doesn't run again, under "Start-up Type:", choose "Disabled."
Since doing this i have not seen a single error related to this. Seems the Index Database was corrupted

First of all: Unless someone reports you, you can't be suspended. 

And when someone reports you, the consoles involved will gather information and telemetry and send that in along with the report as evidence. 

For example, if you replied to the offending message with an offensive message of your own, and the person reports that, the message gets sent in along with the report. 

Based on that evidence the enforcement team will then decide if a suspension needs to be issued, or if the other person needs to be less trusted for abusing the report system. 

If you still think you did nothing wrong, be aware that you can only file a case review AFTER 24 hours and if the suspension lasts more than 24 hours. 

Since you say this is a two day suspension, you can then wait 24 hours, sign in on https://enforcement.xbox.com and then explain your case. 

When explaining your case review. do NOT waste your only case review telling them something like "But he started it by saying mean stuff to me".

Instead make sure to PROVE THEM how you didn't violate the Code of Conduct!

And keep in mind, that they have a record of evidence on which they based your suspension. So you need to explain how this evidence is "wrong".
