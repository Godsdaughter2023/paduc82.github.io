That's a bad idea and almost certainly, whatever unidentified software you have does not require sp2 rather than sp3.  You probably are reading an outdated description of the software's "system requirements" that should instead say, "requires at least Windows XP sp2."

If XP sp3 was installed as an update (rather than as an initial clean install, then it should be an entry in Control Panel > Add or Remove Programs.  There are other methods of uninstalling service packs as well (see http://support.microsoft.com/kb/950249 but do NOT use Method 3 (System Restore)).

Uninstalling sp3 will increase the vulnerability of your computer to malware.  I don't know what effect uninstalling sp3 will have on the 100s of security updates that were released (and presumably installed) after sp3 was installed, not to mention updated versions of Internet Explorer.  In addition, you may have hardware or software that actually does require sp3.

I strongly suggest that you seek help dealing with whatever problem you have with your unidentified software package rather than uninstalling sp3.
