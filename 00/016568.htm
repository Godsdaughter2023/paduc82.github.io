md. nasifahsan say:

Line numbers in word file differs between windows and Mac OS

I use Office 2013 in Windows OS in my office and MS Office 2016 in my home. As a student, I often send different document files to my professor. I have noticed that the line numbers in word file are changed in a Word document if I make it in my office computer and later work in home computer (or vice versa).

I was wondering if anyone can help me to fix this issue. I am not sure if it is version related problem (Office 2013 vs Office 2016) or something else?

Thank you for your understanding.

john mcghie say:

Hi Nasif:

Paul is correct: this is normal operation for Word.

Word is a "word processor" (not a "desktop publisher").  Word processor software (Word, Word Pro, FrameMaker, OpenOffice, etc) automatically fits the document text to whichever printer is connected at the time.  This happens every time you open a file, and is the way that word processors work.

Any differences in the lines on the page are a result of having a different printer and different fonts available.

Since every word-processor is going to do this every time, what we suggest is that instead of numbering the "lines" you number the "paragraphs".  The paragraph numbers will not change, although the page they appear on might.  If you add or delete paragraphs, the paragraphs will re-number accordingly (although, you can prevent this with a very simple macro, ask us if you need it).

To number the paragraphs, apply the styles Heading 1 through Heading 9 to your text, according to their level in the hierarchy.  Then associate numbering with the styles.  Make your lowest level (usually Heading 4 or Heading 5) have the formatting required for your body text.

Let us know if you need detailed instructions for this.

Regards

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

There's a chance that if you reduce the margins and thus make more space available for the text, there will be no change in the location of line breaks between different computers.

Alternatively, use a hard line break (Shift+Enter) to dictate where line breaks occur so that the wrapping of the text will not be influenced by the printer driver.

Hi,

 

Excel works in Binary and often there is no precise binary equivalent of a decimal number and that is what's happened in this case.

 

It isn't a bug and to deal with this issue Excel uses the IEEE754 standard for handling floating point numbers. Seel link below for a fuller explanation and in particular  the section on correcting precision errors which in the case of your formula would be

 

=ROUND(1.8-1.7946,4)
