ron sommer mvp 2009-2017 say:

Before you travel:

To prevent this you will need to configure your ipad as a trusted device 

Given the new security measures, Account Settings requires an extra security, through the use of a security code.  Also known as 2 step authentication

If you mark your computer, phone or tablet as a Trusted Device, you won't be asked for any codes when you access these settings in the future.  To do this

1. Login your account here.

2.  Under Security & Privacy  Click on "Manage Advanced Security" 

3. You will see the "protect your account" page. Select any option to get a code, and request for it.

4. You will see a checkbox that says : "I sign in frequently on this device. Don't ask me for a code". Tick the checkbox, and enter the code.

Your computer/device is now Trusted. Whenever you use your account on that specific computer/device, you will no longer asked for a code.

eileenjlb say:

Ripping music with Media Player or Media Center

Why is it when I insert some cd's into either Media Player or Media Center the wrong information is shown ?  ie the wrong name of CD and list of songs appear . yet when it is playe dafter ripping even though it still shows this wrong information the correct music is played ?
rdrewd2 say:

Hi Eileen,

What it's playing is information coming straight off the CD.  What it's showing about the CD is information obtained from the AllMusic database (unless they've changed databases recently) based off the digital tag on the album, and that particular database is often incorrect.  Sony's Gracenote and Apple's iTunes databases are much more accurate, but I doubt Microsoft will be relying on either of those any time soon.  To fix incorrectly labeled albums, right-click on the album or any of the tracks and click "Find album info".  From there, manually pick out the correct album info and then just follow the prompts.
