I'd define dynamic named ranges in Formulas > Name Manager that refer to the used part of column A and B only.

XValues refers to =OFFSET(Sheet1!$A$1,1,0,COUNT(Sheet1!$A:$A),1)

YValues refers to =OFFSET(Sheet1!$B$1,1,0,COUNT(Sheet1!$B:$B),1)

You can then use (still as an array formula):

=STDEV(IF(XValues=E2,YValues))

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

It's not likely that the spam mail is from FedEx even though you say it is.  If it is, contact FedEx and ask them to stop sending them.

If it's not from FedEx, there are multiple ways to handle the messages.  One is to create a filter for your mailbox by logging into that mailbox using a web browser and employing the filter options your ISP provides.  If you tell us who your mail provider is, we may be able to suggest the exact page where the options are described.  Another way is to create a rule in Outlook that looks for specific criteria in the messages and deletes them.  Do these messages arrive in your Inbox or in your Junk E-mail folder?  If the latter, there's nothing you need to do.  Outlook is already detecting them as spam and putting them where they belong.  If the former, look for any patterns or commonality in the messages.  If they're HTML messages, it's unlikely searching in the body will work, but searching the headers or the subject and using something found there may give you some relief.  If the sender's domain is always the same, use the "with specific words in the sender's address" or "with specific words in the message header" condition and the "Delete it" or "move it to the specified folder" (where the specified folder is Junk E-mail) and the "stop processing more rules" actions to remove the messages from your Inbox.
