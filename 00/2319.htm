    Will it be implemented in the future?

    We don't know what Microsoft's plans for WP7 are.

    Currently, the way the operating system is designed forces all the available storage, for security/resilience reasons, to be treated as one lump, which prevents the use of such cards in the standard plug-in way.

    WP7 'phones do actually use such cards 'under the covers', in a secure manner, for user data such as music and video, but they cannot be removed or managed by the user.

    The only exception is the Samsung Focus, where a larger card may be swapped in, though existing data can't be transferred across directly (i.e. it needs a hard reset followed by re-sync with Zune).     You lost activation because the emulated hardware in the VM changed.  This produced new hardware IDs that were used in registering the VM as a Windows 10 device with MSFT's servers.  The licensing software is sending the new IDs to MSFT's servers and the servers are not finding a registration with the new IDs.  The device is not activating Windows because it is not registered.
