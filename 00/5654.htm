- Pick 5-6 open cells (say D1:D6 for sake of argument)..in those, I'd enter the times that I though would roughly correspond to the points on the curve.  So for example, in the middle two cells, I'd use 8 minutes and 11 minutes.

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

Autocompletion does not reference your contacts in any way.  As you observed, it simply remembers those addresses to which you've sent mail, whether you pulled those addresses from your Contacts folder or just typed them in the To line manually.  Create a new mail profile and you start the autocompletion list over again.

If you want to populate the autocompletion cache with your contacts, open a new message window, click the To button, select all the addresses in the Contacts list that appear by clicking the first entry, scrollng to the bottom, holding Shift, and clicking the last entry to select all entries, then clicking the To button at the bottom of the Select Names dialogue.  Click OK and all of the names will be added to the To field of the new mesage window and they'll all be underlined, which means they're resolved.  The action of resolution adds them to the cache.  You should be able to close the new messagw window without sending a message and the autocompletion cache should retain the addresses.
