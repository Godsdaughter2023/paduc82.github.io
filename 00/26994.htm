steve rindsberg say:

PowerPoint has no sound editing features.  It can only take the sound that you hand it (ie, from a sound file) and play it on demand.

To do what you're after, you'd have to combine the sounds into a single file, with fades, using a sound editing program.  Audacity is very capable (and free) if you don't already have one.

Then add the sound to your presentation and set it to run across multiple slides then time the slide changes to occur in the right place (and accept that PowerPoint will never give you split-second timing ... the transitions will wander a bit, so make the fades a bit on the long side, and don't bother trying to synch slide-changes to, say, thunderclaps in your soundtrack)
