All the form controls are on the Developer tab, which is not displayed by default. Right-click on the Quick Access Toolbar and choose "Customize the Ribbon." In the Customize Ribbon dialog, under Main Tabs, check the box for Developer and click OK. Then look in the Controls group of the Developer tab. If you want users to be able to edit the entire document, you'll want to use content controls. If you want to prevent users from editing anything except the data entry fields, or if the form must be backward compatible with users who have Word 2003 or earlier, then you'll use legacy form fields. If all users will be using Word 2007 or above, you can use the content controls (but be aware that Word 2007 doesn't have the check box control), and there are other ways of protecting the remainder of the document.

Did you protect the document for forms? Legacy form fields don't work until you protect the document. In Word 2010, you have to click Restrict Editing (in the Protect group on the Developer tab).
