georgy solovyev say:

Windows 10 Insider preview license

Hello, everyone. 

I've taken part in the Windows Insider Program and I've got a question about the system's license.

So, basically what's the situation with the licese on my system, for example, can I uninstall/reinstall the OS? 

Is there a key that I can keep and then enter to activate the system? Is it only for my PC, bound to the hardware? 

Many thanks in advance!

cbarnhorst say:

There are two scenarios for changing an MB.  Repair replacement and upgrade replacement.  If you replace the mobo with the manufacturer's recommended replacement part you can preserve the license.  If you replace the mobo with a retail upgrade you cannot.

Activation is now done by your device, not by your software.  As Joe says, a product key is only used to register your device's unique hardware IDs (think mobo serial numbers) along with the edition of Windows 10 with Microsoft.  Thereafter, when you reinstall Windows 10 the licensing wizard checks with Microsoft to see if the device is registered for that edition and, if it is, activates Windows.  That's why if you change the mobo you have changed the device ID and the device does not activate Windows for you.

Windows 10 preview builds are regular Windows builds for licensing purposes so the same exact procedures apply.

nsilmike say:

Spyware doesn't directly infect email accounts... it infects your PC, and that may cause problems with email, and other applications, etc.
Unfortunately, it may even be that one of your contacts is infected, and their PC is spoofing emails from you...
This is all hard to figure out at times, but there are some simple actions you can take in the meantime.
1) Change your Hotmail account password ASAP.  It is possible that your account was hacked, and there is no spyware involved at all.  A common hack is to capture your login and password from a public PC (internet cafe, for instance.)
2) If you use the same password used for hotmail on other sensitive accounts, change those too.
3) Update MSE and run a full scan.
4) Download the Microsoft Safety Scanner and run it at full scan mode as well.  http://www.microsoft.com/security/scanner/en-us/default.aspx  This one does not install, so can't easily be 'tricked' by installed spyware.
