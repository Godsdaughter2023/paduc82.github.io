Cannot send emails from Outlook - Permission error - Microsoft Community

justin.lee say:

Cannot send emails from Outlook - Permission error

I have Outlook 2016 desktop, and I have added 2 email accounts to it.  My company email (O365), and my personal email (*** Email address is removed for privacy ***).

I cannot use outlook to send emails from my personal account.  They all get returned with this message:

This message could not be sent. Try sending the message again later, or contact your network administrator. You do not have the permission to send the message on behalf of the specified user. Error is [0x80070005-0x0004dc-0x000524].

I am sending from my personal account - is it trying to send from my work account on behalf of my personal account?  If so, why would it do that, and how to I prevent it.  

I have tried removing the personal email account from Outlook and adding it back, but that didn't help.

I can send emails from my personal account in web outlook just fine.  It only happens from desktop outlook.

Any ideas?

justin.lee say:

Hi Alison,

Thanks for you help.  I discovered the issue.  In our Office 365's Azure AD, a user was created for my personal email address. i.e. The microsoft account was added to the O635 AD - probably to be part of a user group, or to grant access to SharePoint.  Regardless, this account became part of the global address book in Outlook.  So when I sent an email from my personal account, outlook found it in the global address book and incorrectly tried to send the email on behalf of my work account.

Its a rare/unusual scenario, but a defect in Outlook regardless.  Luckily, the account was not longer needed in the O365 AD, so I deleted it.  After refreshing the global address book, I was able to send emails from my personal email address.

Thanks!
