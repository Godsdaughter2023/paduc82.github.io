jay freedman say:

If markup (either Simple Markup or All Markup) is showing in the document when you issue the Print command, Word will turn on printing of the markup by checking the Print Markup option on the Print settings (this is the bottom of the menu that pops up when you click "Print All Pages" or whatever is in that box):

If you select No Markup before printing, or if you open this menu and uncheck Print Markup, then the markup isn't printed.

If you don't want markup to appear when you open a document, go to File > Options > Trust Center > Trust Center Settings > Privacy Options and uncheck "Make hidden markup visible when opening or saving". (This is checked by default so you won't accidentally send a document with possibly embarrassing markup to someone else. If you routinely uncheck it, you have to remember to look at the markup before mailing the document.)
