What does this entry in Event Viewer signifiy? - Microsoft Community

chaswey say:

What does this entry in Event Viewer signifiy?

Microsoft Antimalware has removed history of malware and other potentially unwanted software.

Time: 14/05/2012 05:33:33

User: NT AUTHORITY\SYSTEM

 

This appeared today 29/05/2012, yet the date shown in Event Viewer report is 14/05/2012.

 

I have MSE>Settings>Advanced configured to NOT remove quarantined file History.

 

I do use CCleaner but also have Ms AntiMalware, MS Security Client and MS Management Console UNticked

 

Date and time on system is correct.

kosh vorlon say:

Hi Chaswey,

 

You can't configure MSE to not remove detected items History after 30 days.  You have the option of adjusting how long quarantined items remain in quarantine.

 

You're almost certainly seeing a notification about the system clearing up the detected items history file.  This is not anything you can control.  I cannot explain the reason for the date difference.

 

The real question is:  did anything in History that you wanted to retain (like Quarantined items) suddenly disappear?  I suspect not.

 

I don't think this is anything you need to worry about unless you see changes in MSE (forget Event Viewer) that show you've lost items that should not have been lost.  Then it's something to address.

 

Event Viewer is filled with all sorts of entries that are deceptive or don't really mean what they say or are taken out of context and thought to be errors when they are actually perfectly normal.  Scammers call and send people to look there all the time to trick them into thinking their system has all sorts of problems and that they need to pay for support - and it works because people are using Event Viewer for the wrong reasons.  It is not a place to go to find problems that need to be addressed.  It is a place to go to find more information about problems you already know exist.  You can spend 10 hours per day checking through Event Viewer looking for problems and you'll find what look like hundreds or many hundreds and most are just normal events.  If MSE is working fine and you look at History and see it's showing what you expect, then it's fine and the entry in Event Viewer is normal even if the date differences may be hard to explain (perhaps there's a built-in delay of 2 weeks to actually transacting the deletion that just isn't documented because it isn't there for users but maybe for support personnel only and so it may look wrong but is fine). 

 

In essence, use Event Viewer to troubleshoot problems you see and not to look for problems you don't know you have.  Is it possible one might actually be found - sure (anything is possible).  But the fact is that it's using a pipe wrench instead of a hammer to drive a nail - it's the wrong tool for that job.  And it'll drive you nuts finding all sorts of things that seem important but aren't issues and are normal even if it doesn't seem that way.

 

The issue isn't what's in Event Viewer.  The issue is what's in MSE.  If it still shows your History as expected, then you're fine no matter what Event Viewer reported or seemed to report.  Even experts are unable to fully explain some Event Viewer event items - especially if just viewed and taken out of context like that.  I recommend you remove checking Event Viewer for no reason other than to look for problems from your list of things to do.  Use it to research problems you are having, not to find problems that almost always don't really exist despite what you see there.

 

I hope this helps.

 

Good luck!

 

Kosh
