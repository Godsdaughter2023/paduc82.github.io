larocque say:

FWIW, I found the answer to my issue, and a helpful Exchange admin team was able to resolve it in our environment. As you are probably aware from this thread, WP7 does not allow an account to sync if EAS requires a setting WP7 does not support. Even though this is "technically" the correct behavior, it has the effect of breaking sync for many of us. iPhone, etc. work because they lie to the server about polices they don't support, responding that they are in compliance. For instance, when IOS3 (or was it 4?) first supported encryption in the software, it was only for the latest iPhone. Older devices (without encryption support) that upgraded their OS found that EAS quit working.

I digress :)

The point is that if you find that your EAS settings do match the list of supported policies, you may be in a situation like me where you've had Exchange for a while, and some policies are still around that are not exposed in the GUI interface of the Exchange Admin tool.

Have an Exchange Admin get the results of this powershell command for your applicable ActiveSync policy: Get-ActiveSyncMailboxPolicy

Look through those results, and find which policies are restricted. You may find some that were applicable in the Windows Mobile era that don't matter any more, or others that aren't necessary. In my instance, AllowMobileOTAUpdate was disabled; doesn't matter for WP7. IRMEnabled was false, but we're not even using information rights management, so it was unnecessary.

We did have one recent policy change, RequireManualSyncWhenRoaming was enabled to avoid exhorbitant international data fees. However, since iOS has the default setting of manual sync when data roaming, this was a duplicate policy that the admins were nice enough to revert to default so I could sync.

Your mileage may vary, and you'll need a cooperative relationship with your Exchange admins, but hopefully this'll help! It's worth it; WP7 EAS is so much superior to the others.

It all has to do with the intersection between your organizations policy requirements and the policies that Windows Phone 7 supports. All it takes is for one to be mismatched and you won't be able to sync. I tried to do some research to see what policies are not supported by Windows Phone 7. I can't find an exhaustive list, but here's what I've  found out so far. There are some irrelevant policies that are not supported such as storage card encryption (since the device doesn't support user changeable storage cards). There are also some policies that simply are not supported such as disabling the camera or disallowing application downloads. If these policies are enforced the device will not be able to connect.  I just asked a few others for information on what other policies fall into this category. Waiting to hear back from some one.
