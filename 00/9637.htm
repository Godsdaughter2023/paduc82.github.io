bernd p. say:

And your question is ?

But I assume you talk about some confusion you created yourself ;-)

Let us remember:

A Onenote notebook is a folder (not a file) in the Windows file system, it's name is the name of the corresponding notebook.

If you move such a notebook's folder into another notebook's folder it will automatically get a section group for Onenote (within that other notebook) and will disappear from the navigation bar. Because section groups in Onenote are folders in the Windows file system, like notebooks.

To "recreate" your former notebook from the now section group:

  1. Close all your notebooks explicitely in Onenote
  2. Close Onenote
  3. With Windows explorer move your "section groups" back to the same hierarchical level of your file system as your "normal" notebook(s)
  4. Re-open all your old and "new" notebooks with Onenote

Rick, you are not supposed just to put the formula you got from Max into the cell A99 itself, you should use it as the Conditional Formatting formula for that cell.

Here are the steps how to do it in Excel 2007.

1. Select the cell that you want to format, in your case it is cell A99.

2. Click on Home tab -> Styles section -> Conditional Formatting -> New Rule...

3. Select a Rule Type: Click on Use a Formula to determine which cells to Format

4. Format values where this formula is true: =COUNTIF($B$99:$B$110,">100")

5. Click on Format... and then choose the formatting you want, e.g Background color red under Fill, Font style Bold under Font, etc

6. OK yourself out of all dialogues.

Now, whenever any of the cells in the range B99:B110 contain a value greater than 100, the cell A99 will be formatted as you have choosen.
