The terminology is really confusing, I know. However, I didn't mention anywhere about 'signing on for Hotmail'. I wanted you to open the security information page for the Microsoft Account that's giving you trouble. This is quite independent of webmail (where you can access your email through a browser), and is the same regardless of the domain of your email address - @hotmail, @live, @msn, @outlook etc.

When you try and access this page, you will be required to sign in with the email address and password for the account whose security settings you're wanting to check.

You may then be asked to verify that it's you who's trying to sign in and not some criminal who's managed to steal your password. This will involve something like sending a security code to an alternative email address or to your phone.

You say that the codes don't work. If you're alternative is also a Microsoft Email account (i.e. @hotmail, @live, @msn, @outlook etc.), you'll have to be careful not to get the sign-ins confused. You can only be signed in with one Microsoft Account at a time in a browser session. If you sign in to your alternative account to retrieve the code, you must then sign out of that account and into the first to be able to submit the code. The most straightforward way of doing this is to use Internet Explorer's InPrivate mode. This keeps everything separate from whatever other browser sessions may be active and avoids the conflict between different sign-ins.

So:

  • Close down all tabs (apart from this one) and all other browser sessions.
  • Click to open the link to the account security information page in a new tab or a new window.
  • Sign in with the Hotmail address and password you're having trouble with.
  • Ask for a security code to be sent to your alternative email address if you can't receive the code on your phone.
  • Start a new instance of Internet Explorer and open an InPrivate window (e.g. press Ctrl-Shift-P).
  • Go to http://mail.live.com and sign in with your alternative address and password.
  • Copy the security code when it arrives, then return to the verification page and paste the code into the box.
