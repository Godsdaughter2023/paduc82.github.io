Why do my pictures look blurry in sway?

For some reason some of the photo (not all) I add to my sways are blurry? They are sharp in onedrive and sharp when you click on the image in sway......but I want them to look sharp as someone is viewing it.....Why is this happening?

Thanks

jason (office engineer) say:

Thanks for the information, Zoey.  The problem is actually due to the rotation of the image.  In order to get that effect, the image gets skewed a little bit and can cause blurriness.  This blurriness is more visible in certain images than in others.

In order to work around this, you need to do something to avoid the rotation.  The ways to do that are:

  1. Change to a different option on the Design tab, there are many options that don't rotate, but this may also change the overall feel of your Sway
  2. Set the emphasis on the blurry images to be Intense, but this will make them much bigger than they are now and may not be the effect you want either
  3. You could also insert a group of type Grid and put one or more images into it and then no rotation will be applied; this will change the visuals a little bit, but I believe it will be the smallest change to your overall Sway
    • If you put multiple images in the same Grid, it will make things look a lot different, but one image per Grid would be close
    • You'll notice that the images will no longer have a white border and this might not be what you want - if you then emphasize the image in the Grid to be Moderate or Intense, you'll see a border appears, although it won't be the same white border as on the other images

Let me know if one of these works for you!

Hi David,

To create the autofill column, try naming the base list and use this as the data validation list source for the input range. The required auto complete functionality can be achieved by using a combo box. Given the extent of your input range, however, it would be impractical to add a combo box to each cell; instead you could create a single combo box on demand.
