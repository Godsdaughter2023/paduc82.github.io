Step A: Enable “Always search file names and content option”.

Note:  As you have already performed this step, go to Step B.

a.       Right-click the Start button and select Open Windows Explorer.

b.      Press ALT. This will cause a toolbar to appear near the top of the Windows Explorer window.

c.       Go to Tools > Folder Options.

d.      Under the Search tab, click Always search file names and content. Allowing this to process might take several minutes.

e.      Press OK.

f.        Run a test search. Go to Start and type a keyword in the Search Programs and Files box. The results should contain the keyword in more than just the tile.


Step B: Enable Content-Searching for a Specific File Type

a.       Go to Start and find the Search Programs and Files box.

b.      Type "search" then choose the result Change How Windows Searches.

c.       Click the Advanced button just below the list of indexed locations.

d.      Go to the File Types tab.

e.      Select the desired extension and click Index Properties and File Contents to enable content-searching. If you want to search the contents of Word files, for example, scroll down to .docx.

f.        If the extension is not listed, enter your desired file extension, such as ".PDF," into the input box at the bottom and click Add.

g.       Press OK.

Hello Charizard Titan,

I think what you might be experiencing is either some major Cache File clogging or your NAT is closed. The below steps should help you with your issues.

  • Please go to your Xbox Dashboard and hit the middle button on your controller
  • Now go to where it says Settings and hit System Settings
  • Go in to Storage and high light Hard Drive
  • I need you to now hit Y on your Xbox Controller
  • You will see where it says Clear System Cache I need you to hit this option 3 times
  • After you do this I need you to go back in to Storage and hit Hard Drive
  • You will see where it says Profiles
  • Hit Profiles and go to your profile and hit Delete
  • After you have deleted the account from your console please go back to your Dashboard and hit the middle button on your controller
  • Go to Download Profile and follow the steps from there
If this solution has not fixed your issue then your NAT may be closed to fix this you must
  • Turn off your Modem or Router (If you have both a Modem and Router then turn both of them off) for 5-10 minutes  
  • I need you to then turn your Modem and/or Router back on and your Xbox Console back on and sign back in
I really hope this helps out a lot. If you have any further questions or concerns feel free to ask.
