a. user say:

Here's an article on BAK files and how to read them. http://www.fileinfo.com/extension/bak.  You can't actually read them as BAK files, you need to read them as the original program.  It may be as simple as removing the .BAK extension (the original extension may be part of the filename).

Here's another article on dealing with BAK files: http://www.associatedcontent.com/article/800229/how_to_open_a_bak_file.html?cat=15.  It says essentially the same thing - you need to determine the original filetype and rename it in order to open it (and you may have to do this by trial and error unless you recognize the filename).

I wish there were an easier way, but BAK files can be almost any tile type and only once you know the right file type will you be able to open the document correctly.  There's no 3rd party program to determine the original file types of BAK files unfortunately.

I hope this helps.
