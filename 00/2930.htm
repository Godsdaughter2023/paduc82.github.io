=-=-=-=-=-=-=-=-=-=-=-=-=-=-=-=-=

ericqc say:

Stefan's suggestion will work — but with some limitations.

By default, the "Insert Footnote" button on the References ribbon uses whatever settings you have defined in the Footnote and Endnote dialog.

So, to modify a mark being used for your 2nd type of footnote from 1, 2, 3... to a, b, c.., you'd need to insert them via that dialog. (To get at it easily, click the small down-right arrow in the lower right of the Footnotes group on the Reference ribbon.)

Type the letter you want in the "Custom mark" box and click Insert. Conveniently, the next time you click the "Insert Footnote" button, the same dialog will pop up, so it will alert you that the normal number sequence had been temporarily broken. Clicking Insert will add a footnote with the next sequential number — and subsequent uses of the "Insert Footnote" button will continue the numbers.

Here's an example showing how it will appear at the bottom of a page:

Limitations?

1. Footnotes appear at the bottom of a page in the sequence created on that page, independent of the mark used. You can't separate the numbered and lettered footnotes as in your mockup.

2. If subsequent edits cause a "b" footnote to push over to the next page, you'd need to edit the reference to make it an "a" if that is your editorial strategy. If you select and try to change the letter, you'll get a warning with the option to change how the marker is displayed. Be aware that the subsequent dialog will assume Endnotes: clicking Footnote, and entering "a" will create a new "a" footnote. You can then copy the content from the old "b" and delete it.

Hi

 

The issue you encountered may caused by the following reasons:  

  • The inability to resolve the MX records of the recipient domain properly. Check if the MX records of the recipient domain can be resolved using a public DNS lookup tool .If it does not or is not fully propagated, contact the recipient directly.
  • The A or MX records for the sender's domain do not exist or are not fully propagated. Most mail servers do not accept mail from the domains without valid A or MX records. Check if they can be resolved using a public DNS lookup tool. If you are the sender and they cannot be resolved, contact your DNS hosting provider.
  • The IP address of the sending server is blacklisted on the recipient side. Contact the recipient directly to check if that is the case.
