¡firedog say:

1.  My account is not a Hotmail account or a POP account. This means that it must be an IMAP account, that synchronizes the account folders between client and server.

2.  my desktop computer ... uses Outlook Express.  The default for new accounts in Outlook Express is to use POP3, configured to remove messages from the server as they are downloaded to OE.

If you are using these default settings in Outlook Express for the same account, then messages downloaded to OE will be deleted from the server. When you then synchronize the same account in Windows Live Mail, messages no longer present at the server will be deleted from the client - that's how IMAP works.

Could this explain the behaviour you're seeing? If so, the solution would be to set up the account again in OE, this time to use IMAP.

    Is there any way i can download itunes onto my windows surface RT - Microsoft Community

    tompenney say:

    Is there any way i can download itunes onto my windows surface RT ? thanks.

    i have had a new windows surface RT and have recently got an iphone 5. so need iTunes, anyone got advice?
    jplsurfacert say: You can play all your songs on iTunes through the Music application on your SurfaceRT.  You will not be able to play any movies or TV shows that you bought in iTunes because they are DRM (digital rights management) protected.  Using a USB thumb drive is the easiest way to put your music on the RT.

    Step 1 - go into your computer with iTunes and open up iTunes.
    Step 2 - Click on the Music icon in iTunes so all the music you want to copy over to your SurfaceRT is viewed (make sure it is downloaded already and not just in your Apple Cloud).
    Step 3 - Put a USB flash drive in the computer with iTunes.
    Step 4 - Highlight all of the songs in iTunes you want copied to your SurfaceRT.
    Step 5 - Right click on the highlighted songs and click on "copy".  If using a Mac you could also just press the Mac command key down and press the letter "c" after you have them highlighted.
    Step 6 - In Finder or Explorer, right click on your USB drive and select "Paste".
    Step 7 - When finished copying all your music files over, right click the USB drive and select "eject".
    Step 8 - Remove your USB drive and insert it in the SurfaceRT.
    Step 9 - Click on "Desktop" on your SurfaceRT
    Step 10 - Open File Explorer and double click on your USB drive
    Step 11 - Highlight and copy all the music you put on your USB drive
    Step 12 - Click on the "Music" icon on the left column in File Explorer.
    Step 13 - Right Click and select "Paste", and all your music files will be copied into your music folder in your SurfaceRT
    Step 14 - Go to the Start screen, and open the Music folder.
    Step 15 - Enjoy!  The speakers are a little weak, so use headphones - then you can appreciate the music appropriately.  Or even better, you could attach external  speakers!

    If you want to copy videos that are not DRM protected, do all of the above but copy the videos and save to the Video folder.
