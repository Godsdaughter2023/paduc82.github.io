petrib say:

There are no separate, user-installable Flash Lite upgrades.

 

Whatever version of Flash Lite that Nokia delivers, they'll deliver as part of the firmware, and even then, as the name ("Lite") hints, it is not the same as "full" Flash on PCs, but a partially compatible subset.

 

Adobe has at times given Flash developers a version to install manually for some phone models, but it will work only as a "standalone" player (does not come with a browser plugin), and has not generally been available for regular phone users to install.

 

Note also that Adobe has stopped any further development of Flash or Flash Lite for mobile phones (they've stated they continue with "full" Flash on PCs,  but for mobiles they expect HTML5 to take over, and what's accelerating that trend - and contributed to Adobe's decision to abandon Flash on mobile phones - is probably the fact that neither Apple nor Microsoft allows Flash in any form or shape on their mobile phones).
