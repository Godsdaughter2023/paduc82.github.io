steveflu say: When your computer goes into sleep mode, it is off. The TiVo software is off. Nothing gets transferred, because the computer isn't running. The TiVo software doesn't know anything about Windows' "don't sleep if serving media" settings, nor does Windows know anything about TiVo, including the fact that it's serving media.

Away Mode isn't Sleep - it's a fake sleep mode that just turns off the display and audio. There aren't any drivers required for Away Mode since Vista.

Having Away Mode enabled causes only USER-INITIATED sleep requests to initiate Away Mode. Automatically entering Sleep because of an idle time-out does NOT cause the PC to enter Away Mode.  (Also, Hibernate never triggers Away Mode.)

Since you've enabled Away Mode, make sure you have disabled sleep or hibernate after an idle time-out.

Check out http://www.microsoft.com/whdc/system/pnppwr/powermgmt/awaymode.mspx Good Luck!

Family Safety (Parental Control) is built into Windows 8. To turn off Family Safety, log into the parent account and follow these steps:

  1. Press Windows key + W and type “Family Safety’, click on the “Family Safety’ icon.
  2. In the Family Safety window, click on the Child user account.
  3. Click on the ‘Off’ radio button under ‘Family Safety’.

If you want to permanently remove the Family Safety restriction on the child account, you can change the child account in to standard user account. Refer to these steps:

  1. Press Windows key + X, click on ‘Control panel’.
  2. Click on ‘Change account type’ under ‘User accounts and Family Safety’ section.
  3. Click on the child user account and click on ‘Change account type’.
  4. Select the ‘Standard’ radio button and save changes.
