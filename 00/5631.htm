To expand on Jason's idea...

If your second table is in excel cells A1-C6 (Plot# in A1, 4444 in A2, seet packet # in B1, etc).  I will assume that your first table is in "sheet2!A1:D4" meaning it is in cells A1-D4 on another sheet (named sheet2 here, so change the code below to the correct sheet).

then in D1 you can put fruit name, in E1 Color, and in F1 size.  Then in D2 put:

=vlookup(B2, sheet2!A:B,2,false)

and copy it down to the cells below.  Similarly in E2 put:

=vlookup(B2,Sheet2!A:C,3,false)

and copy down.  In F2 put:

=vlookup(B2,sheet2!A:D,4,false)

and copy down.

Now you have something very similar to your desired end state, but in a different order and with columns not quite right.  At this point I would copy the new table you have created and paste it to a different worksheet, just in case.  Now cut/insert cut cells to move the columns where you want them, then sort&filter> "custom sort" to get your data in the right order.

Update: seems it was probably Ctrl+F9 that made it all into a field, so I just need to know how to undo this.

jay freedman say:

You correctly diagnosed what happened (mistakenly pressing Ctrl+F9 instead of F9). To fix it:

- Carefully select everything except the two curly brackets at the top and bottom of the document.

- Copy to the clipboard.

- Move the cursor before the first curly bracket and paste. You now have (apparently) two copies of the document content, and the cursor should be just before the opening bracket of the
document-in-a-field.

- Press Shift+right arrow once. That will select the document-in-a-field.

- Press the Delete key. Your document should now be back to normal.

You may get other advice to paste the copy into a new blank document instead of pasting back into the same document, but that would lose any headers and footers from the original document.

For the future, a safer way to update all fields is to go to Print Preview and back to the Page Layout view.
