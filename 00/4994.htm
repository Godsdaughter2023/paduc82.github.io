Hi Ivan,

If you have set up the “Create items and edit items that were created by the user” option, to let certain users have the ability to edit other users’ item in Calendar List in SharePoint Online, you need to grant them the full control permission. You can stop inherited permission in the Calendar List and then grant those certain users with full control permission. This will only allow them to have this permission in the Calendar List and not the whole SharePoint site. About how to stop inherited permission and grant full control permission, see: Edit and manage permissions for a SharePoint list or library.

If you don't want them to edit the Calendar List settings, things will be a little complicated. You need first create a custom permission level in the SharePoint site and then grant this users with that permission level. To do so:

2.Type a name for this permission level and under “List Permissions”, select all permission except “Manage Lists  -  Create and delete lists, add or remove columns in a list, and add or remove public views of a list.”
3.Go to the Calendar List> CALENDAR tab> List Settings> Permission for this list> Stop Inheriting Permissions> Delete all the users and groups.
4.Click Grant Permissions and give normal users with Edit permission, then give those users who need to administer the list with the custom permission level we just created.

cbarnhorst say:

The three choices stand for

Fast Ring (gets all preview builds released to the development branch)

Slow Ring (only gets preview builds determined as very stable)

Release Preview (still on the public release but gets cumulative updates a few days ahead of the general public)

You only have the latter while you are on the public release.  It does not appear if you are on the Fast or Slow ring and have installed a preview build.

Example, I have a computer in the fast ring.  It has received 20 preview builds in the past year.  It could not be put on RS_Release until rs_1 goes final because it is on a preview build and not on a public release build.

Example, I have a computer in the slow ring.  It has received several preview builds in the past year.  It could not be put on RS_Release either until rs_1 goes final.

Example, I have a computer I decided to put on the Public Release preview a few weeks ago before rs_1 went final.  It stayed on build 10586.  I wanted to receive public release updates to build 10586 a few days early to test them so I elected the Release Preview ring.  The computer did not receive preview builds.  It stayed on 10586.  But it got cumulative updates several days before the general public did so I could preview them.  However, release previews are not preview builds and do not change the major build number.

Right now, build 14393 is the new public build and you have RS_Release (Release Preview) as a choice.  If you stay on fast or slow ring and receive an rs_2 preview build in August the Release Preview choice will disappear because that choice in only available to computers while they are on the public release.

Hello,

 

I understand the issue regarding this feature, and my have a solution to your question.

By referencing this link:

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/winphone/forum/wp7-sync/google-calendar-doesnt-show-events-entered-on-my/19ed2e33-c6d2-43ad-b08d-2c587503e9e3

If you're using a Google Apps account and you want to sync your calendar to your phone too, the Enable Google Sync setting needs to be enabled in the Google Apps administrator control panel. (You need to have administrative privileges to log into the Google Apps administrator control panel or you'll need to contact your system administrator.)

You'll then need to enable Google Sync for your user account. To enable Google Sync for your user account, in your web browser on your PC, go to Mobile settings for your Google Apps account, and then enable Google Sync under Service settings. After doing that, on your phone, tap your Google account name in Email & accounts, tap Calendar under Content to sync, and then tap Done .

 

You then will need to update the sync settings for your Calendar(s):
