vanessa ouyang msft say:

Hi Pstoric,

Just changing a little about the script you provided, we can list mailboxes by ItemCount as the image shows:

But if we want to sort on Totalitemsize, we need to do more because the sizes of mailboxes jumble up from KB to GB. So we need a long script to achieve the goal:

To be more intuitive, I attach the script there: 

Get-Mailbox | Get-MailboxStatistics | Select-Object DisplayName,

@{name=”TotalItemSize (KB)”;expression={[math]::Round((($_.TotalItemSize.Value.ToString()).Split(“(“)[1].Split(” “)[0].Replace(“,”,””)/1KB),2)}} | Sort “TotalItemSize (KB)” -Descending

As this is my test account, the unit of totalitemsize is KB. You can change it to MB or GB depending on your circumstances.

If the mailbox’ size in your organization ranges from MB to GB, you need to change it to the same units via the following command:

Get-Mailbox | Get-MailboxStatistics | Format-Table DisplayName,

@{name=”TotalItemSize (KB)”;expression={[math]::Round((($_.TotalItemSize.Value.ToString()).Split(“(“)[1].Split(” “)[0].Replace(“,”,””)/1KB),2)}}

Hope this helps.

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:
Question: Does outlook send a delivery reciept if a messge is directed to junk mail?
Delivery receipts are handled by the mail server, not by the mail client.  Read receipts are handled by the client.  If you delete a message without reading it, you have read receipts enabled in Outlook, and your mail server passes receipts on, then the sender may get a "deleted without being read" notification.

Hi,

 

Suppose your data is in range A3:E7.  Headings are in A2:E2.  In cell F3, enter this formula and copy down

 

=IF(SUMPRODUCT(MATCH(1,INDEX(($A$3:$A$7=D3)*($B$3:$B$7=E3),,),0))>0,INDEX($C$3:$C$7,MATCH(1,INDEX(($A$3:$A$7=D3)*($B$3:$B$7=E3),,),0),1))
