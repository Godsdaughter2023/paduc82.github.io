julie fka profjulie say:

Have you looked for your data in the My Phone Archive of your My Phone web page?  You should find the data there.  Move all that data back into the active area of My Phone.  Then you can select "Find Duplcates" and remove the duplicate information there.

While you're there, go to the Connected Phones page and delete your phone.  Then open up the My Phone client on your phone and terminate the service.  After that you can open up the My Phone software on your phone and set up a new partnership by entering your live ID and password and selecting the data you want to sync to your phone.  My Phone should recognize your phone as a new device and sync all the data back to your phone.

In the future, if you have problems like you had this morning, be careful about deleting everything from your phone and then syncing it to My Phone.  The way My Phone works is it mirrors the data that is on your phone.  If you delete anything on your phone, and then sync it to My Phone under an existing partnership, the data will also be deleted from you My Phone web account (well, on first sync it will be moved over to the archive section). 

If you ever have the need to delete everything on your phone again, be sure to also terminate your existing My Phone partnership.  Then when you set it up again, My Phone will view the phone as a new device and sync the data on the My Phone web page to your phone - because it views the partnership as a new device, it won't delete anything from the web space.
