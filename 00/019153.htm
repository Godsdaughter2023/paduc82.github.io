jamesbanks4421 say:

Fixed!! This was caused by the Realtek Audio Driver. Windows 10 and Realtek do not work together at this time. Microsoft and Realtek are working on this. Now, in order to have sound, one needs to make sure the Realtek Audio Driver is uninstalled in Program and features. If you have to do it in safe mode then do that. Once Realtek is uninstalled that should cure this blue screen problem. Now in Windows to get sound, one needs to makes sure the Audio driver is using the Windows audio driver. Here is how I did it.

1. Create a restore point to go back to in case you mess up.

2. Go into Programs and Features and uninstall any Realtek Audio drivers.

3. Restart the computer and go back to Programs and Features to check to see if they are uninstalled.

4. Open Device Manager in Control Panel.

5. Go to Sound,Video and Game Controllers and expand it.

6. Right click on High Definition Audio Device and then click on Properties.

7. Click on the Driver tab and scroll down to the Roll Back Button and roll the driver back to a earlier version.

8. Click your way back out to the desktop and see if you have sound by looking at speaker in taskbar.

9. Restart your computer and check to see if you have sound.

10. If it is all good and your sound is fixed make sure to create another restore point. In case like me, you end up with another bug that's screws things up.

 For those who have this problem with Realtek Audio drivers I hope this helps.

Select the range with the forenames and surnames, down to the last row with points.

Press Ctrl+G.

Click Special... in the Go To dialog.

Select Blanks, then click OK.

Let's say that the active cell within the selection is A3 (look at the name box on the left hand side of the formula bar).

Enter the formula =A2 (where A2 is the cell above the active cell) and confirm the formula with Ctrl+Enter.

Click Copy or press Ctrl+C.

On the Home tab of the ribbon, click the lower half of the Paste button and select Values.

You have now filled the empty cells with repeating data, so that you can create subtotals.
