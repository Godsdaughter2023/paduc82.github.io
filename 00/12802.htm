yves dhondt say:

If by bibliography you mean a bibliography generated by Word through the sources you entered, then that is a field. Merging those fields from multiple documents into one, will not work as that would mean you will have multiple bibliographies in one text. What are you trying to do?

Are you trying to have a bibliography at the end of each chapter? If so, convert the bibliographies to static text. This way they can be merged as normal text.

Are you trying to combine the bibliographies of all the chapters into one complete bibliography? Then there are two options in my opinion:

  1. In the original documents, convert the bibliographies to static text. This way, all bibliographies can be easily merged into the final document. All you would then have to do is select all the paragraphs containing the combined bibliographic data and sort them.
     
  2. Move all the sources from each of your documents into your final document. This can be done by importing them from your master source list in your final document. Then just generate a new bibliography in that document.

The second option is probably a tad more complex to execute than the first.

What are the POP3 settings for Outlook.com - Microsoft Community

vasudev say:

What are the POP3 settings for Outlook.com

Technical Level : Intermediate

Summary
If your mail app doesn’t support Exchange ActiveSync or IMAP or you want to use POP3 for your Outlook.com account then here’s what you need to know about configuring POP3 settings manually for Outlook.com
Details

If your mail app doesn’t support Exchange ActiveSync or IMAP or you want to use POP3 for your Outlook.com account then here’s what you need to know about configuring POP3 settings manually for Outlook.com

You have to first turn on POP option for your Outlook.com account. For this, sign-in to your Outlook.com account. Click on the options gear icon at top right and then Options.

Next, under Managing your account, click Connect devices and apps with POP.

Under POP, click to select Enable. As you select it , you’ll be shown an option for Deleting downloaded messages. This will verify the POP3 delete command.

Select Don't let devices or apps delete messages from Outlook.com. If the app or device is set to delete, Outlook.com will move the messages to a special POP folder instead , it’ll override any delete requests Or

you can select Do what the device or app says—if it's set to delete messages, delete them, If your devices and apps allows to delete messages from the inbox.

Once you have selected your choice, click on Save.

Now once you have enabled POP & saved settings in Outlook.com, use these settings in your email app:

Incoming (POP3) Server

  • Server address: pop-mail.outlook.com

  • Port: 995

  • Encrypted Connection: SSL

Outgoing (SMTP) Server

  • Server address: smtp-mail.outlook.com

  • Port: 25 (or 587 if 25 is blocked)

  • Authentication: Yes

  • Encrypted Connection: TLS

For the User name: Your email address and

Password: Your password

After downloading mail to your email app or device, if you don’t want email to be deleted from your Outlook.com inbox then choose Leave a copy of messages on the server option. Until you verify POP3 delete command as shown above earlier, Outlook.com will save a copy of the mail in a folder called POP, even if you accidentally set up POP3 to delete messages after downloading them. So verify POP3 delete command as required. And this is how one configures manually Outlook.com account for POP.
