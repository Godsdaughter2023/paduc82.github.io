rajesh c. say:

There is no direct formula which can help you to achieve this.

However, there is a workaround by creating 2 helper columns and then sorting the data on the second helper column. Pls follow the steps given below

Step1.  Assuming that the "Serial #" is in column-B and assuming that our data starts

            from Row number 2, we shall create our first helper column

            in Column-F.

            Pls insert the following formula in cell F2

                                     =MID(B2,FIND(" ",B2)+1,LEN(B2)-FIND(" ",B2))*1

Step2.  Before dragging the above formula , down below, we need to custom format

            this cell (F2)     to    00000          (assuming that your numbers are going to be

            from 1 to 99999 max.

            (I hope you are aware how to do custom number formatting).

Step3.  Now drag this formula of cell F2 downward up to the row till where your data

            exists.

This will extract the numeric part of your content  for example  00001  or 00525 and so on.

Step4. Now in cell H2, we enter the following formula and drag.

                        =LEFT(B2,5)&TEXT(F2,"00000")

Once this is done, you can then select the entire data range and sort your data on this Column-H.  You can either "white" paint the text colour of these 2 helper columns  OR

Hide these 2 Helper columns.

Hope this Helps.

mroxx---01 say:

Hi all,


please also keep in mind that USB charging depends a bit on where you want to charge the phone.
A laptop USB port most likely not provide the same amount of "power" like a dedicated charger or a dedicated USB charging port!

E.g. You might have experienced that an external HD does not work on a USB port without connecting it to a second one - just to supply more current to the HD.
Same goes for the phone - it will charge with low power supply but it will take longer time.
If you use a dedicated charger or dedicated USB charging port with USB cable it will be faster.

Compare the Nokia DC-18 universal charger - it has an output current of 600mA - a laptop USB port usually can only supply a few 100mA if it is not designed as a charging port...

Please also compare : http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universal_Serial_Bus
