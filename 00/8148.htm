Something got lost in translation.  In Excel, 657:10:00 would represent 657 hours 10 minutes 0 seconds.

If 657:10:00 is the form of your input, which is to be interpreted as 657 minutes 10 seconds 0 frames or some other fraction of seconds, you will need to convert it to a form that Excel recognizes as time in minutes.

If your data are in A1:A100, ignore what you see in column A and simply enter the following formula into B1 and copy down:

=--(LEFT(A1,LEN(A1)-3) & ".0")

formatted as Custom [m]:ss .  To see cell format options, select the cell(s) or column and press ctrl+1.

This assumes that the rightmost part of the input time is always 3 characters.  This formula will work even if the rightmost part might be greater than 59.

Sub CountTrue()
Dim sht As Worksheet, ct As Double

ct = WorksheetFunction.CountIf(sht.Range("A1:L555"), True)' Change the range to your need.
