jay freedman say:

When you type spaces at the start of a line and there is no line break at the end of the previous line, Word just sticks all those spaces at the end of the previous line -- even if they extend past the right margin. You can see this if you turn on nonprinting characters by clicking the ¶ button.

The simple way is to press Shift+Enter at the end of the first line to insert a line break; then the spaces will stay at the start of the second line. If there are three or more lines, you'll need a line break at the end of every line except the last one.

Technically there is a way to force Word 2013 to give you what you want, but it's painful enough that you won't want to do it.

In versions before Office 2013, Word's Options dialog had a long list of "compatibility options" that could modify the layout of a document to look like even earlier versions or like WordPerfect. In Office 2013, Microsoft dumped all but a handful of those options, but there is a way to get them back. The specific one you're interested in is "Wrap trailing spaces to next line". Here's the long list of steps:

  • Press Alt+F11 to open the macro editor, and press Ctrl+G to open the Immediate window at the bottom of the editor.
  • Type or paste into the Immediate window the line ActiveDocument.SetCompatibilityMode 14 and press Enter. close the editor. (Notice that the title bar of your document will now include the label "[Compatibility Mode]".
  • Open the Options dialog and click the Advanced tab on the left. Scroll to the bottom of the window.
  • Change the "Lay out this document as if created in" dropdown from "Microsoft Word 2013" to "Microsoft Word 2010". The list of compatibility options appears below it.
  • Scroll to the bottom of the list and check the box for "Wrap trailing spaces..."
  • Click the OK button.

Existing and newly typed spaces at the start of an automatically wrapped line will now stay there instead of jumping back to the previous line.

Caution: When you use the Save As dialog (or Save for the first time), you must check the box for "Maintain compatibility with previous versions of Word". If you forget to do that, the compatibility setting will be removed during the save, and the spaces will go back to the ends of lines. In that case you can go through the whole procedure again.

Also, when a document is in Word 2010 Compatibility Mode, you lose access to any features that were added in Word 2013 and don't exist in Word 2010. If you're an average user, that probably won't make any difference to you.

Your call.
