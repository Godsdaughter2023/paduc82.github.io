fractions and math in excel.... - Microsoft Community

erik9693355 say:

fractions and math in excel....

I am in excel and trying to get the following fraction 2(10000)/43776 to show the fraction and then divide and give me the answe of 0.4568 how can i do this???
joeu2004 say: Erik wrote:
I am in excel and trying to get the following fraction 2(10000)/43776 to show the fraction and then divide and give me the answe of 0.4568 how can i do this???

A couple ideas come to mind, depending on what you really need.  All of the following assume you are willing to write 2*10000/43776 instead.  Are you amenable to that change?

If so....


1. Select B1, then click on Formulas, Define Name.  In the dialog box, enter the Name eval.  In the Refers To field, enter the formula =EVALUATE(A1).

Then if you enter a fraction of the form 2*12345/43776 into A1, for example, enter the formula =eval(B1) in the cell to the right and format B1 as Number with 4 decimal places.



2. More generally, create the following VBA user-defined function [1]:

Function myEval(f As String) As Double
myEval = Evaluate(f)
End Function

Then you can enter =myEval(A1) in any cell, formatted as Number with 4 decimal places.



3. Alternatively, click on File, Options, Advanced, scroll to the end and select "Transition formula entry".  Then if you enter literally 2*12345/43766 into A1, for example, it will be evaluated just as if you had entered an equal-sign prefix.  Format the cell as Number with 4 decimal places for the desired appearance.

If you also want to see the formula, create the following VBA UDF [1]:

Function myFormula(r As Range) As String
myFormula = Mid(r.Formula, 2)
End Function

Then you can enter =myFormula(A1) in any cell.


-----
[1] To create a VBA user-defined function, press ctrl+F11 in Excel.  That opens the VBA window.  In VBA, click Insert, Module.  Normally, that opens a VBA editing pane on the right.  Copy the text of the desired VBA UDF and paste it into the VBA editing pane.  You can now close the VBA window.  Be sure to save the XL2010 file with the extension ".xlsm".

a) Press “Windows Logo” + “W” keys from the keyboard.

b) Type “Troubleshooting” in the search bar and press “Enter”.

c) In the “Troubleshooting” window, click on “View All” on the left pane.

d) Click on “Internet Explorer Performance”.

e) Click on “Advanced” and then click on “Run as Administrator”.

d) Click “Next” and follow the on-screen instructions to complete the troubleshooting process.

If the issue persists, follow method 2.

Method 2: Enable Enhanced Protected Mode

  1. Open Internet Explorer.
  2. Click on Internet Options.
  3. Go to Advanced tab.
  4. Under Security, locate Enable Enhanced Protected Mode Should be checked.
  5. Restart Internet Explorer.

If the issue persists, follow method 3.

Method 3: Turn off add-ons

Add-ons are apps that browsers use to interact with web content like videos and games. Incompatible or out-of-date add-ons might cause Internet Explorer to run slowly, crash, or prevent sites from displaying correctly. Before you turn off all add-ons, try starting Internet Explorer without add-ons to see if the problem goes away.

1. Click the Windows button on the keyboard, and enter Internet Explorer in the search box.

2. Click Internet Explorer (No Add-ons) in the list of results.

set-ExecutionPolicy RemoteSigned

Get-AppXPackage -AllUsers | Foreach {Add-AppxPackage -DisableDevelopmentMode -Register "$($_.InstallLocation)\AppxManifest.xml"}
