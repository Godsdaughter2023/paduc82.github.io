¡firedog say:

I have sent several emails to myself and they do arrive in my inbox.

These two facts - that a sent message is moved to the Sent items folder, indicating that it has been accepted by an SMTP or mail submission server for onward transmission, and that a message sent to yourself is delivered to your mailbox - show that there is no inherent problem with sending mail from Windows Live Mail using this account. When you send a message to yourself, there is only one Email Service Provider (ESP) involved - the one whose servers you are using to send and receive mail.

The problem arises, therefore, when a different ESP comes into the picture - the one hosting the mailbox you're sending to. There can be any number of reasons why an ESP stops messages from being transmitted; a spam filter is one of them, and another is the registration of your own IP address or that of your mail submission server on one of the many public block lists. If the ESP you're sending to subscribes to any of these block lists, it will prevent your message from being delivered to a mailbox hosted by that ESP.

You can easily check whether your own IP address is being blocked: use a site like whatismyipaddress.com to find your IP address and check whether any records exist.

Hi Rick,

Adding a password to secure your device can be facilitated by the Sign-in options feature in Windows 10. To do this, you can perform the following:

  1. Search and launch Settings using Cortana.
  2. Click on Accounts.
  3. Click on Sign-in options.
