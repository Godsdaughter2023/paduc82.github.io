Hi,

 

I had similar problems with a X5340... I ended up having to reinstall several times to figure out the problem... You may not need to if the following does the trick...

 

One thing you might check first is to make sure the Print Spooler in "SERVICES" is started, and Bidirectional Support is Enabled... If this doesn't help, you will probably need to uninstall\reinstall.

 

The "Print Spooler" can be found in Control Panel... Administrative Tools... Services.

 

To find Bidirectional Support, right click on the printer in Devices and Printers, select Printer Properties, PORTS tab, and it will be near the bottom...

 

I have found that the printer needs to be physically unhooked from the computer to change the Bidirectional Support. Otherwise the change won't stick..

 

If you attempt to reinstall, turn off the Print Spooler, REMOVE your printer from Devices and Printers, and unhook it from your computer, and uninstall the software\drivers.

 

When reinstalling, make sure the Print Spooler is turned off... The reinstallation should turn it back on... It is supposed to start automatically.

babu v say:

Hi Mahendra,

 

The issue could be caused if there is a miss match in the IP address configuration.

 

I would suggest you to follow the steps provided below.

 

Method 1: Let us release, renew the connection.

a. Click Start, click All Programs, click Accessories, right-click Command Prompt, and then click Run as administrator.

b. Enter the below command and hit enter.

Ipconfig /release

c. Enter the below command and hit enter.

Ipconfig /renew

Exit the command prompt and check if the issue persists.

 

Method 2: Reset the modem or the router.

You can reset your modem or router to try to resolve connection problems. To reset your modem or router, follow these steps.

Note: If you use both a modem and a router, these steps apply to both.

1.Disconnect the cable that connects the computer to the modem or the router.

2.Turn off the modem or router.

Note: If the modem or the router does not have a power switch, disconnect the power cord to the modem or router.

3.Reconnect the computer to the modem or router.

4.Make sure that all network cables are securely connected.

5.Turn on (or reconnect the power cord to) the modem or router.

6.Wait 20 seconds, and then restart your computer.

7.Start Internet Explorer.

If the same error message appears after you reset your modem or router, go to the next method.


Method 3: One of the components of the Internet connection on your computer is a built-in set of instructions called TCP/IP. TCP/IP can sometimes become damaged or corrupted. If you cannot connect to the Internet and you have tried all other methods to resolve the problem, TCP/IP might be causing it.

How to reset Internet Protocol (TCP/IP):

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/299357

 

Method 4: Reinstall the network adapter.

How do I fix network adapter problems?

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-in/windows7/fix-network-adapter-problems

Hope the information provided is useful.

Howard

Below is a simple specimen signature, based on my own, in HTML. It shows two tables, 14% of the width on the left with an image (can be jpg, png, gif, etc.), which could be a logo, picture, avatar, etc. and has text in the rightmost 86%. Open Notepad and copy all the text into it. Now edit it to suit your personal details, i.e. change the table sizes (width=), image (<img src….) and email link (<a href….) lines. Also change other attributes (color, text size, font) or remove lines. Save the file with a meaningful name, e.g. signature01.html, preferably in its own folder, e.g. d:\signatures. Also, any pictures you intend to use must be saved in this folder.

Now open the mail program, hold down both Ctrl and Shift and press O for Options then the Signatures Tab > Put ticks in ‘Add signatures to….’ and optionally ‘Don’t add….’ then click New > File (at the bottom) and Browse to the name of your file (make sure the drop-down menu points to HTML files) then Apply/OK your way out. The HTML code can be edited by right-clicking the filename and choosing Notepad and double-clicking the filename will display your signature in a browser window. Finally, send an email to yourself to make doubly sure it displays exactly how you want it to, e.g. the size of the image, color, wording, etc. Here is the specimen code, which is about 1KB in size:

<table width="100" border=0>

<TR><TD></table>

kapil arya mvp say:

Hello,

I believe the messages are syncing; as soon as you delete a message on the phone, it is removed from Your Phone as well.

Here are some troubleshooting steps. 

Your Windows laptop needs to be plugged in and Battery saver turned Off to keep your devices synced. 

To ensure the fastest, most reliable connection, your Android phone and PC must be connected to the same trusted Wi-FI network. This optimization isn't possible on a public connection, for example, in a coffee shop, hotel, or airport. 

When you first connect to a Wi-Fi network through Windows 10, you'll be prompted to set it as public or private (trusted). You can also change it at any time 

These changes might not always be possible - for example, if your workplace controls your network connection. 

Before we begin to change settings, we recommend quickly running through the following checks: 

Double-check you’re using the same Microsoft Account to sign into both Your Phone apps. 

Ensure Battery saver isn’t running on your PC or Android phone, as this might affect connectivity. 

You have an active Wi-Fi connection. Can you open your browser and navigate to your favorite website? 

Still not working? Continue with the following troubleshooting advice. 

On your PC: 

Check if the Your Phone app can run in the background. 

Select Start (or press the Windows logo key on your keyboard), then select Settings > Privacy > Background apps. 

In the list Choose which apps can run in the background, ensure Your Phone is enabled. 

Ensure nothing is stopping you from sharing across devices. 

Select Start (or press the Windows logo key on your keyboard), then select Settings > System > Shared experiences. 

If you see Fix now in Accounts, select it and follow the instructions. 

On your Android phone: 

Make sure you can receive app notifications. 

Go to your phone Settings > Apps (or Apps & Notification on some phones) > Your Phone Companion and check that notifications are enabled. 

Make sure Do not disturb is turned Off. 

Go to your phone Settings > Sounds (on some phones this might be Sounds & Vibration or Sounds & Notifications) and check to see that Do not disturb is turned Off. 

If that doesn't help try unlinking your phone:

https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/help/4459358/windows-10-your-phone-app-help?ocid=YPApp-messages#messages
