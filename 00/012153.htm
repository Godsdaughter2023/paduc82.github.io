booklet printing and binding of the finished product - sheets in the - Microsoft Community

whaug2006 say:

booklet printing and binding of the finished product - sheets in the center stick out when folded - printed content moves too

 

Has anybody found a solution how to compensate for creep when priting a duplex booklet in
Office  by moving margins?  Is there a macro that dynamically does that before it sends it to the printer ?


Some may say .... Huh ? What ?

Most often booklets are bound with staples (commonly known as saddle stitching)
Per printwiki.com
"..
Since [sheets] to be [stapled] need to be inserted within each other [..], the cumulative thickness
of the of the [booklet] will cause the edges of the inner [sheets] to gradually protrude [stick out]
beyond the edges of the outer ones.
.."
This is called creep.  Try it out for yourself .. take 50 sheets of paper and fold in the center of the top sheet.
The pages of the sheets closer to the center will stick out.
With this your print content will move too on the paper when you finish your booklet and
cut off the edges.


The thicker the booklet the worse it gets ..

As per http://www.printindustry.com/Newsletters/Newsletter-31.aspx

"..

So what can you do to avoid this? Subtract 1/32" from the face margin (move everything this far toward the gutter margin) within each successive, interior signature. Keep each signature internally consistent, but make this change from signature to signature.

.."

This offset will increase all the way to the center of the booklet and then decrease back to zero from the center to the end of the booklet.

As per office help you can have different margins in different sections of the document.

Some geek talk for the programmers ..

If you use the open xml SDK and read in the document

you can artificially add sections.

The formatting xml in the document for the margins looks like this.

<w:sectPr w:rsidR="003C4FE2" w:rsidSect="00BF3593">
            <w:pgSz w:w="12240" w:h="15840"/>
            <w:pgMar w:top="1440" w:right="1440" w:bottom="1440" w:left="1440" w:header="720" w:footer="720" w:gutter="0"/>
            <w:cols w:space="720"/>
            <w:docGrid w:linePitch="360"/>
</w:sectPr>

.. End of geek talk.

So there are all the details. Office wizards .. please help.  Do you have a solution for this ?


Thanks
      Hans
