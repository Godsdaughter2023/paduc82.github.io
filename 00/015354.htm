dirk goldgar say:

The next questions are (a) how frequently does the scrolling list need to be refreshed, and (b) should the user be able to act directly on the records in the list somehow, such as by clicking or double-clicking to bring up details?

There are a couple of ways to make a form scroll.  The ones I've played with both involve using the form's Timer event to perform the scrolling.  The simplest one is is just to scroll through the records by manipulating the form's current position in its recordset, using code like this:

'------ start of simple code ------

Private Sub Form_Timer()

    With Me.Recordset
        If .EOF Then
            .MoveFirst
        Else
            .MoveNext
            If .EOF Then .MoveFirst
        End If
    End With

End Sub

'------ end of simple code ------

That scrolls from record to record, but on the first page of records, the top record stays at the top until the current record is the last record on the page; only then do the records themselves seem to scroll.

A more complicated way -- but giving a better-looking result -- is to use functions from Stephen Lebans' "modGet-SetScrollInfo" module posted here to manipulate the form's scroll bar directly.  That keeps the current record always at the top of the form.  NOTE: I have needed to modify the module slightly due to the scrollbar's class name having changed in later versions of Access than Stephen was working with,  So if you want to take this approach, you will probably need to do the same.

rowena cam say:

Hi, SharonEElliott.

Thank you for posting on Microsoft Community.

Find my Phone feature on Windows 10 Mobile allows you to remote lock your phone with a PIN of your own choice. As long as the phone is connected to the internet and accessible, you can reset your PIN lock remotely. If you never enabled Find my Phone feature, the only option is to reset your device.


Enable Find my Phone:

  • Settings > Update and Recovery > Find my Phone.
  • Toggle the options which saves your device location periodically &  more often.
  • Make sure you have SIM in the phone, and it works.


How to remotely change PIN

Let’s say you forgot your PIN, and have entered multiple times with no success. Windows 10 Mobile blocks you out, and sometimes even asks you to restart the phone before trying again.

  • Go to https://account.microsoft.com/devicesand select your device.
  • Click on Find My Phone. The next page gives you option to Ring, Lock and Erase.
  • Click on Lock. Here you can set a 6 digit PIN, along with a message and an optional contact number.
  • Click on Lock again, and your phone should get locked with new PIN. Use the new PIN to unlock.
Interesting to note:
 
  •   Instead of 4 digit PIN lock, Windows 10 Mobile now has 6 digit PIN lock, along with option to use a number. Windows Phone 8.1 has 4 digit PIN lock with only option to send message.
  • The phone restarts once the new PIN is remotely set.

What should I know about the 30-day waiting period?

Even when you choose to delete your out-of-date security info, MS don't fully remove it for 30 days. This waiting period helps to deter hackers from updating your security info and gaining access to your account without your knowledge.

After 30 days, the user of the account will be able to access the security settings and update his/her preferred information.

But the waiting period doesn't mean you're locked out of your Microsoft account.

Check to see if you can still access your emails via https://mail.live.com.
