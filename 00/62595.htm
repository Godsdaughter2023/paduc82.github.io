"How to Refresh Windows 8 and Windows 8.1"

  • The PC boots into Windows RE (Recovery Environment).
  • Windows RE scans the hard drive for your data, settings, and apps, and puts them aside (on the same drive).
  • Windows RE installs a fresh copy of Windows.
  • Windows RE restores the data, settings, and apps it has set aside into the newly installed copy of Windows.
  • The PC restarts into the newly installed copy of Windows.

"When you reset your PC, the drive will be completely reset, and all your apps and files will be removed from it. There are several ways to get a fresh MBR, depending on your technical skills. The most straightforward is to boot your computer from a Windows 10 installation disk. When Windows 10 setup starts, click on "Repair this PC" (on the bottom left.) From there click on "Startup repair."

Usually, I just click on the keyboard icon on tool bar

That is the Touch Keyboard.  So what you are seeing "pop up" is the TK not the real On-Screen Keyboard (osk.exe).  The instruction you were given would start the OSK.  When the OSK is running the TK can not be used.  Then your options would be either Minimizing the OSK, moving it or using its Fade button.  The OSK is not smart enough to "pop up" when you would like to use it.  You would always have to make it show itself from whatever way that you had hidden it.  You could also pin the OSK to your Taskbar and your Start Menu and then be able to start it from there.  Then that would give you the additional option of closing the OSK any time you wanted or needed to, switching to using the TK whether automatically or manually, and then re-activating and hiding the OSK to achieve your desire of keeping the TK out of sight.
