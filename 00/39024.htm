Cannot create a partition in my laptop with windows 10 - Microsoft Community

sachinpandey_94 say:

Cannot create a partition in my laptop with windows 10

Original Title:.Disk partition

cannot create a partition in my laptop with windows 10.

it came with a single C partition.

when i try to make a new partition using diskmgmt it says that it cannot create a partition as space is unavailable although i have 500gb free. please tell what to do

ken blake say:

You are mixing up the two kinds of free space: space that's in  a partition and space that's not in any partition.

The 500GB that you say is free is undoubtedly part of your C: partition and that partition is undoubtedly your whole drive. Assuming that I am right, you have no unpartitioned space and there there is no space available to make another partition. To do so, you must first reduce the size of your C: partition.

But don't reduce C: by 500GB, If you do so, you will have no free space available and you will have all sorts of problems. I would not reduce C: by  more than 250GB.

A final point: what do plan on using this second partition for? For most people having two partitions is a mistake, especially if the second one is as small as 250GB.

I'll take a guess: do you plan on using the second partition for backup? I don't recommend backup to a second non-removable hard drive (especially if it's a second partition on your only drive) because it leaves you susceptible to simultaneous loss of the original and backup to many of the most common dangers: severe power glitches, nearby lightning strikes, virus attacks, even theft of the computer.

In my view, secure backup needs to be on removable media, and not kept in the computer. For really secure backup (needed, for example, if the life of your business depends on your data) you should have multiple generations of backup, and at least one of those generations should be stored off-site.
