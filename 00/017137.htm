    Hi Amy,

     

    Running unnecessary add-ins and corrupted profile may make Outlook crashing and even cause stability issues. As an initial recommendation, we suggest that you perform some of the possible troubleshooting steps listed below on how to resolve your concern:

     

    Disable unnecessary add-ins or plugins in outlook application.

     

    1. Open Outlook, click on File and select Add-ins.
    2. In Manage: COM Add-ins, click Go button and uncheck any add-ins that you’re not using.
    3. Click OK and relaunch Outlook and then try to print to check if there any difference.

     

    Create a new Outlook profile.

     

    • Follow the step-by-step instructions provided in this article on how to create a new Outlook profile. Once you’re done, set up your email account again in the new profile using your email account configuration settings. Then, try to print and check if the resolves.

    1. Go to the Control Panel and click on Hardware and Sound.
    2. Select Devices and Printers and under Printers and Faxes, right-click your another printer and click Set as default printer.
    3. Open Outlook and try to print to check if the issue occurring as well.

     

    Uninstall and reinstall printer.

     

    1. Go to the Control Panel and click on Hardware and Sound.
    2. Select Devices and Printers and under Printers and Faxes, right-click your printer and click Remove Device.
    3. Click Yes to remove your printer and restart your computer.
    4. Once the computer boots up, go to the Control Panel again and click on Hardware and Sound.
    5. Select Add a device under Devices and Printers and click your printer and then click Next. Follow the on screen instructions to complete the install. Once it’s done, try to print using Outlook to see if the issue resolves.
