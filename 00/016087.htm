todd aton say:

One of the limitations of version 1 was that you could only sign into one account at a time. In version 2 you can sign into many accounts and switch between them easily. With this new benefit, you might be able appreciate that signing out isn't as simple as it used to be.

 

You actually have very precise control of signing in and out of accounts and opening and closing notebooks inside the current application. You can close your notebooks and sign out of your accounts without having to go all the way out to the OneNote system settings, forcing the reset and running through the startup screens the next time you launch.

 

***iPad

Closing notebooks:

Starting from the notebook you're working in:

1. Tap the "<-" button in the upper left hand corner.

2. Tap the Notebooks button on the left.

3. For each open notebook, tap the button on the far right in the list item and tap Close Notebook.

At this point you've closed your notebooks.

 

Signing out of accounts:

4. Tap the Accounts button in the upper left hand corner (with account picture). 

5. Tap the Switch Account button in the popover.

6. For each account, swipe right on the list item and tap Remove (in effect, this signs-out).

7. Tap somewhere on the screen to dismiss the account popover.

At this point you have no accounts signed in.

 

Oh, and to sign back in:

1. Tap the Accounts button (which should be labeled "Sign In" since no accounts are signed in).

2. Tap the Sign In button in the popover.

3. Type your email address and tap Next.

4. Type your password and tap Sign In.

 

***iPhone

To close notebooks:

1. Navigate to the notebook list.

2. For each open notebook, swipe right and tap Close button.

 

To sign-out of accounts:

1. Navigate to the notebook list.

2. Tap the gear button in nav bar.

3. Tap Accounts button.

4. Tap Switch Account button.

5. For each open account, swipe right and tap Remove.

6. Tap to confirm.

 

To sign-in:

1. Navigate to the notebook list.

2. Tap the gear button in nav bar.

3. Tap Accounts button.

4. Tap Sign In button.

5. Type email address for account and tap Next.

3. Type password and tap Sign In.

None of the answers given here really helped, so I ended up going in a different direction.  I decided that my basic approach was flawed.  I had been trying to use a single formula that counted up all the rows in a range that met a compound criteria.  After failing to acheive that I decided that I needed a way to mark each line that needed to be counted, and each line that needed to be ignored.  Once marked, I could count up the rows in any way that I pleased.

  1. I added a column to the tab were the raw data gets pasted.

  3.
