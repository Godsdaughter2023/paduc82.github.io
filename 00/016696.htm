dominicsipos say: What this error means is, your Windows 10 installation folder is unfinished and/or corrupted. Now, how do you fix that?
1. Go into "C:\Windows\SoftwareDistribution\Download" and delete everything in that folder.
2. Now, run the command prompt as an administrator. Type in "wuauclt.exe /updatenow".
3. Go to your Control Panel > Windows Update and your Windows 10 should start re-downloading from scratch, hopefully without flaws this time.

EDIT: For those who still have issues, several users reported that setting their time forward (to the First of August for example) and then doing the above steps again helped them resolve the issue

jeeped say:

=IF((HLOOKUP("AHT CAHT",TEAM_DATA!$1:$500,4,0))=0,"",HLOOKUP("AHT CAHT",TEAM_DATA!$1:$500,4,0)) 


Try,

=IF((HLOOKUP("AHT CAHT",TEAM_DATA!$1:$500,ROWS($1:4),0))=0,"",HLOOKUP("AHT CAHT",TEAM_DATA!$1:$500,ROWS($1:4),0))

Edit: have you considered using an Accounting style of number format? Accounting styles substitute a hyphen for zeroes. A Custom number format can also be created that leaves a blank cell in place of zero values. Example:

_(* #,##0_);_(* (#,##0);_(* "-"_);_(@_)    (for a hyphen) 
_(* #,##0_);_(* (#,##0);;_(@_)   (for a blank) 

This would allow you to shorten the formula to 

=HLOOKUP("AHT CAHT",TEAM_DATA!$1:$500,ROWS($1:4),FALSE)
... or possibly,
=IFERROR(HLOOKUP("AHT CAHT",TEAM_DATA!$1:$500,ROWS($1:4),FALSE),0)
... if you do not wish to have errors or #N/A (no match) displayed.

The Microsoft Print to PDF and Microsoft XPS Document Writer drivers may be corrupted. To resolve your concern, please do the following:

Step 1: Run the automatic troubleshooter for printers.

Follow the steps below to run the automatic troubleshooter for printers:

  1. Right click the Windows logo (Start button).
  2. Click Control Panel.
  3. Click Devices and Printers.
  4. Right click Microsoft Print to PDF, and then click Troubleshoot.
  5. After running the automatic troubleshooter, right click Microsoft XPS Document Writer, and then click Troubleshoot.
  6. Restart the computer.
  7. Try to print again to Microsoft Print to PDF and Microsoft XPS Document Writer.

If it is still the same, proceed to step 2.

Step 2: Re-install the Microsoft Print to PDF and Microsoft XPS Document Writer drivers.

To re-install the Microsoft Print to PDF and Microsoft XPS Document Writer drivers, follow the steps below:

  1. Right click the Windows logo (Start button).
  2. Click Control Panel.
  3. Click Devices and Printers.
  4. Right click Microsoft Print to PDF, and then click Remove device.
  5. After removing the device, click Add a printer.
  6. A window will pop up. At the bottom of the window, click The printer that I want isn't listed.
  7. Choose Add a local printer or network printer with manual settings.
  8. Choose Use an existing port, and then click the drop-down menu.
  9. On the drop-down menu, choose FILE: (Print to File), and then click Next.
  10. In the Manufacturer box, choose Microsoft
  11. In the Printers box, choose Microsoft Print to PDF.
  12. Click Next.
  13. Choose Use the driver that is currently installed (Recommended), and then click Next.
  14. Click Finish.
  15. Restart the computer.
  16. Try to print again.
