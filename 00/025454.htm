Windows Defender - Microsoft Community

jehphone1 say:

Windows Defender

How do I turn off windows defender off permanently. I do not want the program running on my system. Please help.

greginmich say:

How do I turn off windows defender off permanently. I do not want the program running on my system. Please help.

The Windows  Security Center was designed to automatically turn off Defender as soon as any another real-time AV app is installed – and then automatically turn Defender back on again whenever that third-party AV app is uninstalled (its removal utility should also be run). This behavior was intended to prevent any lapses in your PC’s real-time AV protection, and it should go without saying that continuous AV protection is a sine qua non for operating a Windows-based PC.

So shutting down Windows Defender with the Group Policy switch can really only be justified in cases where Defender’s CPU utilization is excessive to the point of rendering the system inoperable, so that it needs to turned off temporarily – or – in cases where Windows Defender fails to automatically shut down with the installation of a third-party AV app. In the latter case, you should really try uninstalling the third-party AV app, running its removal tool, and then reinstalling it before you resort to disabling Defender with the Group Policy switch. Reinstalling your third-party AV app is the only way that you’ll be able to retain the potential benefit of Defender’s Limited Periodic Scanning. I’m presuming here that the current problem with Limited Periodic Scanning will eventually be fixed, although that might be an overly-optimistic assumption on my part.

You can also toggle the Windows Defender Group Policy switch directly (and very quickly) with a REG command, if you don’t want to bother with downloading a REG file. Just copy, paste, and enter the appropriate line at the elevated (admin) command prompt to turn Defender on or off:

Turn Off Windows Defender:

REG ADD "HKLM\SOFTWARE\Policies\Microsoft\Windows Defender" /v DisableAntiSpyware /t REG_DWORD /d 1 /f

Turn On Windows Defender:

REG DELETE "HKLM\SOFTWARE\Policies\Microsoft\Windows Defender" /v DisableAntiSpyware
