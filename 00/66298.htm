GoGo WiFi, Airport WiFi, and Windows 10 (Solved) - Microsoft Community

GoGo WiFi, Airport WiFi, and Windows 10 (Solved)

GoGo WiFi, Airport WiFi, and Windows 10 (Solved)

PROBLEM:  

Unable to access GoGo Wifi or Airport WiFi with Windows 10.

PURPOSE:

This posted discussion represents years of “issues” regarding inflight and airport internet connections, with a special emphasis on GoGo, which is the largest US provider of inflight internet service.  It would be helpful if others could contribute to this discussion, if they find additional tips to assist travelers in need of WiFi.  

If you find this comprehensive review helpful, or especially if it saved you time, money and aggravation, then please take the time to click on the "I recommend this discussion" link at the bottom left of this post.

CHALLENGES:

One of the more frustrating challenges to business travelers is the inability to remain connected to work, family, and the world during flights and at airports.  This can be particularly maddening during international flights, wherein the business traveler may be 16+ hours in the air . . . so much time; so much work that needs to get done.  That is why it can be a Godsend to have WiFi access during national and international flights, as well as via airport WiFi – especially during the inevitable flight delay. 

But what happens when WiFi is available on flights (e.g., GoGo), or at airports, but you are not able to login to WiFi? 

This not only results in missed work opportunities, but can also result in wasted weeks of infuriating detective work trying to discover why a simple login to WiFi during flights doesn’t work, even when you are able to access WiFi everywhere else.  Worst of all, it should not be assumed that any Information Technology (IT) genius, or Technical Support personnel is going to be able to help you.  Because if the only time you experience login problems is when you are in flight, then unless you have your IT person on board in the seat next to you, this creates challenges.

As with many other aspects of travel, you are likely on your own.

Finally, another practical challenges is how to access this GoGo guidance document on a plane, if you are not able to access the Internet on a plane.  One option is to access GoGo Internet via your smart phone, and then find this document.  While you will not be able to perform all the measures described herein, this document provides many things you can try on your laptop that do not require an Internet connection.

EXAMPLE ERROR MESSAGES:

Logging into GoGo has resulted in the most amount of error message responses of any website - at least for me. Beyond the periodic certificate errors or warnings over the years, the kind of Windows 10 error messages that I have encountered when trying to connect to GoGo Inflight or Airport WiFi include:

  • Unable to reach wifi network login or sign-in page
  • In Internet Explorer: “This page can’t be displayed.”  
  • In Chrome, “This site can’t be reached.”  
  • In Edge, “Hmm, we can't reach this page.” 
  • “Start your web browser to view the sign-up page, and then follow the instructions to connect to the Internet”
  • “ This computer is set to manually connect to “gogoinflight” “
  • "Wi-Fi” doesn’t have a valid IP configuration”
  • “Windows received an HTTP error message: 403 (Forbidden) from “buy.gogoinflight.com.” “
  • “Windows could not automatically detect this network’s proxy settings.”
  • "This site is not secure."
  • "The hostname in the website’s security certificate differs from the website you are trying to visit." 

SIMPLE SOLUTIONS:

While it may seem obvious, the solution to the problem of connecting to GoGo InFlight and other travel WiFi services depends on the cause – which can be highly variable, frustrating and time-consuming to resolve – especially if you do not know the cause, or if you do not have a systematic way to find and fix the cause.  Also, if your computer is owned or managed by a company, especially if running a virtual private network (VPN), then your inability to access WiFi may be by design.  In this case, before doing anything else, it is probably best to first contact your company’s IT personnel.  Otherwise, the following are some simple first steps:

  • Ensure WiFi is turned on.
  • Ensure Airplane mode is turned off, at least with respect to WiFi.
  • You cannot access GoGo WiFi unless you have WiFi access.
  • Ask the flight attendant if the GoGo Inflight WiFi is working.
  • Caveat:  Due to the number of issues that may make it appear GoGo WiFi is not working, it is not uncommon that passengers will report problems with GoGo WiFi on flights, when in fact, no GoGo WiFi problems exist.  Thus, if the flight attendant announces GoGo is not working, and it really is working, then this might be a paradoxical break for you.  Because after the flight attendant announcement that the WiFi is not working, fewer passengers may attempt to access GoGo WiFi, allowing precious more bandwidth for you.  Especially if you follow applicable measures described in this discussion, experience suggests it best to see if GoGo WiFi is working, even when the flight attendant says it is not working.  
  • If upon review of your potential WiFi connections, you find various passengers' phones listed, as well as the mysterious "Hidden Network," and GoGo WiFi is not listed, then it is likely Gogo WiFi is truly not working. In this case, it may be the flight attendants have failed to turn it on, or that the GoGo WiFi device on the plane needs to be rebooted.  Yes . . . asking the flight attendants to reboot GoGo WiFi when no one can access it can often help.  However, if others can access GoGo WiFi, and you are the only one who can't access GoGo WiFi, then rebooting is unlikely to help - but it will annoy others who were able to access GoGo WiFi, and were using GoGo WiFi, before it was rebooted.
