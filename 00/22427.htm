Moving sections and pages in OneNote - Microsoft Community

hosch250 say:

Moving sections and pages in OneNote

Technical Level : Basic

Summary
This wiki article explains how to move sections and pages in OneNote.  Pages can be moved to other sections within the same notebook or to different notebooks; sections may only be moved to other notebooks.
Details

Windows Store OneNote:

In the Windows Store OneNote, just drag the section/page from where it is to the new section or notebook you wish it to reside in.  If you are moving an individual page into a different notebook, it will be placed in the last active section in that notebook.  Here I am about to move "Untitled page" from "New Notebook" into "General Notebook":

Desktop OneNote:

The Desktop OneNote has the most options for moving sections and pages around.  The easiest way is to just drag and drop the section/page where you want it to be.  When you move a page in the Desktop OneNote, you may only place it within a section, not just drop it in a notebook and have it go in the last opened section, as in the Windows Store OneNote.  The Desktop OneNote also has tools for moving sections and pages.  To open the Move Section tool, right-click the section you want to move and choose the "Move or Copy..." option:

Click the notebook you want to place the section in, then click either the Move or Copy button; this will place your section as the very first section in the notebook.  If you wish to place your section below an already existing section, just click that section before clicking Move or Copy.

You can also merge sections in the Desktop OneNote.  To do this, right-click the section you wish to merge into another section and select the "Merge into Another Section..." option:

Open the notebook containing the section that you want to merge into, select the section, and click Merge.  This will move all the pages in the section into that section.  After the merge is complete, you will prompted as to whether or not you want to delete the original section.

This is the Move or Copy Pages dialog window:

To open this window, right-click the page that you want to move and choose "Move or Copy... (CTRL-ALT-M)."  Open the notebook that contains the section you want to move (or copy) your page into and select the section.  Then, click either the Move or the Copy button, depending on what action you wish to take.

OneNote Online

In OneNote Online, you may only move pages by dragging and dropping them into the new section:

You may not move sections in this version of OneNote.

Feel free to contact me with questions through the comments section and updates through the suggest edits section.

marilynwinter say:

__how to filter query using combo box in Access?

In access forms,I hv created a combo box drop down with say ...A,B,C..When I select 'A' & click submit button I need to retrieve details of 'A' from the Query,similary if "B" is selected I need the details of B from the Query.


Can any one help me do this please...........
scottgem say:
The key to this is that you can reference a value in an open form using the syntax:

=Forms!formname!controlname

where formname is the name of your form and controlname the name of the control.

So if you want to filter a query by a value selected on a form, you would set the criteria in the query the above expression.

If you want to display the filtered result on your form, you Requery the form in the After update event of the combo.

An alternative to this is to use a filter. In the After Update event of the combo use

Me.Filter = "[Fieldname] = " & Me.comboname
DoCmd.RunCommand acCmdApplyFilterSort
