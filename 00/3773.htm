In a relational database table that equates to less than the MIN StartDateTime value where the StartDateTime value is greater than the current StartDateTime value, and greater than the MAX StartDateTime value where the StartDateTime value is less than than the current StartDateTime value.

scottgem say:

Let me try to restate this. You have have records that are entered in chronological order. You want to maintain that order. However sometimes, there is a requirement to change the date/time on a record. When that happens, you want to keep the record in chronological order so you want the updated date/time to be between the previous and next records in chronological order. Is that correct?

If so, first I have to ask why? Why would a) the dates need to be updated and b) why do you need to maintain chronological order? If you are using an autonumber set to increment, then you can use that to maintain the order. Or you have a field that uses sequential numbering to maintain the order. 

Also you state that the form is unbound. If that's the case there is no next and previous records. 

However you might be able to use something like this:

Dim dteNext AS Date, dtePrev As Date

dteNext = DMin("[datefield]","tablename","[datefield] > #" & Me.datecontrol & "#")

dtePrev = Dmax("[datefield]","tablename","[datefield] < #" & Me.datecontrol & "#")

If Me.datecontrol NOT Between dtePrev AND dteNext Then

     MsgBox "invalid date"

     Me.datecontrol.SetFocus

End If

Reference Cell A1 and A3 from Sheet 1 on my charts - Microsoft Community

linda_rq say:

Reference Cell A1 and A3 from Sheet 1 on my charts

I have a couple of Charts and Graphs in my workbook.  There are several Pivot tables.  At the top of one sheet, I update the Harvest Date.  I have a field on each sheet =Sheet 1 Cell A1 so all the sheets update with the current info.  Is there a way to put a text box or another type of contral on my chart sheet and do the same thing?

hansv mvp say:

On the chart sheet, activate the Insert tab of the ribbon.

Click on Text Box, then click on the chart, or drag a rectangle on the chart.

This inserts a text box. Do not type anything in the text box itself.

Instead, click in the formula bar, and type =

Then activate the sheet with the cell you want to refer to, and click on that cell.

You now have a formula such as =Sheet1!$A$1 in the formula bar.

Press Enter, and you're done.

You can of course format the text box the way you want.
