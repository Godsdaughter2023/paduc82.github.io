I'd create a lookup table consisting of two columns:

 

LOGIN1   Aaron
LOGIN2   Adam
LOGIN3   Aidan
...

 

Select this table (both columns), then click in the name box on the left hand side of the formula bar, type a name such as LoginNames and press Enter.

=IFERROR(VLOOKUP('data sheet'!C2, LoginNames, 2, False), "")

 

This formula can be filled down, if required.

jay freedman say:
Do you just want to remove the formatting, or do you want the address to stop being a hyperlink?

It's easiest to make it plain text that isn't hyperlinked -- just right-click it and click Remove Hyperlink.

If you still want to be able to use it to launch a browser, but to be formatted like plain text, you need to remove the character style named Hyperlink that's applied to the address text. First press Ctrl+Shift+Alt+S to open the Styles pane. At the bottom, click the middle one of the three similar buttons to open the Style Inspector. Select the entire address in the text, and click the button to the right of the third box (when you hover over the button, the tooltip says Clear Character Style).

To prevent the address from being made into a hyperlink in the first place, click File > Options > Proofing > AutoCorrect Options, click the AutoFormat As You Type tab of the dialog, and clear the checkbox for "Internet and network paths with hyperlinks".
