natearnold say:

Thunderbird is not a Microsoft application, and there are a lot of different reasons as to why your PC is running Thunderbird as slow as you describe.  For one, you could have a somewhat slow internet connection.  To test that I usually use SppedTest.net by Ookla.

Another factor involved with speed issues is keeping your Internet browser tabs open while you are not surfing the web.  That can easily culminate into slowing down a person's PC and can even allow malware to be installed without the user's knowledge.  There are a lot of "free" antivirus applications out there, but if your PC is slow enough I would really recommend taking it to a retail office supply store that offers free scans.

Could also be that it has been a long time since your PC was properly shut down or rebooted.  By default, Windows 10 has what is called "Fast Mode" shutdown, enabled by default.  What this does is place all your system background services into RAM or in cache on the master boot record of your hard disk and leaves the RAM and/or Hard Disk on while the rest of the motherboard is powered down.  It speeds up your boot up time significantly, but at the cost that updates that require a restart being installed or set up right away after a proper shut down and reboot.  Using "Restart" rather than "Shutdown" may help speed things up a bit afterwards.

Finally, another factor is that Thunderbird itself may be performing poorly in spite of a sufficient Internet connection speed and computer to run it.  It would be easier to probably get help from Mozilla if that seems to be the case.

john john __ say:

The Prefetch Data will rebuild itself providing that the Task Scheduler service is set to Automatic, the Task Scheduler runs a routine every three days and it will rebuild the files.

I don't see how this (deleting prefetch files) would cause problems with Word 2003 or any other applications.  It might make them a bit slower to load but it should not cause any problems with the programs themselves, they don't need the prefetcher to work properly.

Set the Task Scheduler to Automatic and if the files don't rebuild on the next reboot run this command:

Rundll32.exe advapi32.dll,ProcessIdleTasks

This will force a rebuild of the Prefetch Data.

frederik long say:

whats the trick?

The vast majority of sharing problems are due to one or several of the following reasons:

  • People ignore the need to have identical account names & non-blank passwords* on each PC.
  • They do not share specific folders.
  • They do not set the share access rights to "Full" for "Everyone"
  • They do not set the folder access rights to "Read" or "Full Access" for selected accounts.

* Many people do not wish to bother with passwords. In these cases Windows can be configured to remember the password for the default account. However, it then becomes imperative to have a fully tested and documented spare admin account on each machine. Without this spare account it is only a matter of time until the user locks himself out.
