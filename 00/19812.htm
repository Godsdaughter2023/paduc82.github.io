To answer your query about creating rule with specific words, we suggest following the steps below:

  1. Open your Outlook 2010 and then click File.
  2. Under Account Information, click Manage Rules & Alerts.
  3. Under Rules and Alerts, click New Rule. The Rules Wizard window will pop up.
  4. Under Rules Wizard, select Move messages with specific words in the subject to a folder.
  5. Under Step 2, click specific word. The Search Text window will pop up.
  6. Under Specify words or phrases to search for in the subject:, put "ecelerity" and then click Add.
  7. Same step to add "*.NL", click OK.
  8. Once your done in adding a specific word, click specified to move the words to the specified folder. The Rules and Alerts window will pop up.
  9. Under Rules and Alerts, select Junk Email and then click OK.
  10. Click Next, then select an item if you want to add an additional condition.
  11. Click Next, then select an item you want to do with the message. Note: You may permanently delete the message.
  12. Once you're done, click Next until you reach the Finish rule setup window.
  13. Click Finish.
  14. Click Apply and then click OK.
  15. Restart your Outlook 2010 to save the settings.

There is nothing built into Outlook that is automatically going to create a contact item and folder for every sender.

Something that you may wish to explore is "customizing" the view of your inbox - specifically the "Group By" option that can easily group all emails received by sender (From). Welcome to Microsoft Office Community and thank you for posting the question. By the description, it looks like the images are blocked due to which you are unable to view in the e-mails.

 

Let us follow the steps:
1. Open Outlook 2013.
2. Go to File-> Click on Options.
3. Go to Trust Center -> Click on Trust Center Settings.
4. In the Automatic Download section -> Uncheck the option Don’t download pictures automatically in HTML e-mail messages.
5. Click OK to apply the settings.

 

Re-open Outlook and this should allow you to download and display the e-mail message in the e-mails.

tom_917 say:

I found this information from another site, and it works.

If Outlook 2007, 2010, or 2013 displays some - but not all - images, you may need to change the setting in your Internet Explorer web browser or desktop software.

To change the setting in Internet Explorer:

  1. Open Internet Explorer.
  2. Go to Tools > Internet Options.
  3. Click the Advanced tab.
  4. Scroll down to the Security settings list, then uncheck the "Do not save encrypted pages to disk" option.
  5. Click OK.

Before the changes take effect, it may sometimes be necessary to clear the cache in Internet Explorer and close Outlook (File > Exit to close fully). After relaunching Outlook, send yourself another email with an image that did not display previously.
