Access, by default is a multi-user platform. So, yes you can do it. But there is a right way and a wrong way. the RIGHT way is to split the database between back end (tables) and front end (everything else). The back end goes on the shared drive, the front end is deployed to each user's local drive. Any other method is the wrong way and invites corruption and other problems. Splitting is easy. Access includes a wizard to do it.

   ◦If you've never set up any e-mail accounts using Mail, the Welcome to Mail page appears. Go to step 2.

    a.Clear the Take Account online check box, and then click Create.

    d.In the right pane, click Advanced.

jay freedman say:
As you've discovered, Word has the infuriating behavior that inserting a picture watermark in any section automatically removes any existing watermark in any section, and replaces it with the newly selected watermark. There is no way to turn off that behavior.

The workaround is to recognize that a picture watermark is identical to an ordinary picture that is anchored in the header (by having the cursor in the header while inserting the picture) and then made washed out (by increasing its brightness and decreasing its contrast) -- with the sole exception that a watermark has an invisible bookmark that identifies it as a watermark, while the ordinary picture has no such bookmark.

That means you can make "fake watermarks" that are different in each section. You need to unlink each section's header from the previous one, and then insert and format the desired picture and drag it to the middle of the page. You can make fake watermarks that are different on each page by the method you've already used.

What you cannot do is use the page color > fill effects > picture command, for two reasons: First, everything in the page color set of commands always applies to the entire document. You can't even vary it from one section to the next. Second, on many (possibly all) computers, the selected picture won't just cover a whole page, even if it's sized to do so; it will be repeated (tiled) vertically and horizontally. Unless the picture is the sort of "texture" that repeats seamlessly, it just looks awful.

how to remove contact in skype - Microsoft Community

sergefournier-925 say:

how to remove contact in skype

how to remove contact in skype
susan_s. say:

Hello

Here are the instructions on how to remove Skype contact, depending on the Skype version installed on your Windows 10 device.

The new Microsoft Skype for Windows 10 (Skype UWP)

1. Sign in to Skype.

2. In your contact list, find the contact you want to remove. Right-click the contact name and select Remove from Contacts.

3. The contact is removed from your contact list, but will still appear in the Recent tab with a question mark. Right-click the contact name to see the options: Add to Contacts or Block This Person.

The new Skype for Desktop (Windows, Mac and Linux)

1. Sign in to Skype.

2. From your recent chats screen or use Search Skype, find the contact you want to remove. Right-click the contact name, and then select View profile.

3. Select Delete contact.

Skype for Windows desktop (classic Skype)

1. Sign in to Skype.

2. In your contact list, find the contact you want to remove. Right-click the contact name and select Remove from Contacts. Then, confirm by clicking Remove.

3. The contact is removed from your contact list, but will still appear in the Recent tab with a question mark. Right-click the contact name to see the options: Add to Contacts or Block This Person.

Please do not hesitate to post back if you need further assistance.
