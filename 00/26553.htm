paramasivan---01 say:

Buddies, i got a good news to share. The basic thing you need for WPA/WPA2 is a certificate. So here is what i did to connect to my enterprise WPA wifi.

1. Get your certificate. Find for the certificate from your laptop's wifi. Goto wifi properties, under validate server certificate find any certificate related to your enterprise (probably with your enterprise or universitiy name). Select the file, under detail tab, choose copy to file. Save the file under DER format (.cer). In my case i had 4, so i copied all 4 to try out which one works for me.

2. Now copy the certificates to your phone. Goto the certificate and execute it to intall the certificate to your phone automatically. Now your certificates are ready.

3. Goto settings -> network destinations -> "your network" -> wifi security settings -> EAP plug in setting (make sure your WPA/WPA2 = WPA) -> EAP-PEAP -> Select authority certificate you have executed earlier (step 2) -> username in use = user define -> username = domain\username -> realm in use = user defined -> realm = "empty". Now goto EAPs tab and under EAP-MSCHAPv2 key in your username without domain -> set your password.

This method worked for me. Others try and let share your steps.
