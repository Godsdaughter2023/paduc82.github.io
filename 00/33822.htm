john fue say:

Hi,

We suggest that you update Epson Perfection V500 driver.

  • Driver updates for Windows 10 and many devices (such as monitors, printers, and scanners) are automatically downloaded and installed through Windows Update. 
  • While it's likely you already have the most recent driver, if you're having trouble with a device, you can try fixing it by updating the driver or reinstalling it.

Here's how:

  1. Click the Start button, type Device Manager, and select it from the list.
  2. Expand one of the categories to find the name of your device, then right-click (or tap and hold) it, and click Update Driver Software.
  3. Select Search automatically for updated driver software.
  4. If Windows doesn't find a new driver, you can try looking for one on the device manufacturer's website and follow their instructions.

If these steps don't work, try reinstalling the driver: Open Device Manager, right-click (or tap and hold) the name of the device, and select Uninstall. Restart your PC and Windows will attempt to reinstall the driver.

divya r say:

Hi bustblocker,

a. What exactly do you mean by hidden? Is it minimized and hidden or maximized and is in the right side of the Screen?

b. Do you have taskbar on the right hand side? 

Programs like Internet Explorer will open to the same size and placement as they were when they were closed. 

Method 1: If the new window does not start in full screen, simply resize the window by dragging the edges out to the sides of the screen. Do not use the max button.

Now close the last Internet Explorer window. If you do this right, the settings will stick. You may or may not have to reboot, depending on your operating system.

If you want Internet Explorer(IE) to open full-size when you start it up, you must first start Internet Explorer, and then maximize the window. Now click File, and then Close. Now restart IE, and it should start up in a full-sized window. If half of the window is off the screen when you start a program, bring the window to the location you want and then click File and then Close and it will re-open in this position the next time.

You may also try this step:

Method 2: To run Internet Explorer Windows in a maximized state

Click Start-> All Programs right click on the Internet Explorer(IE), select Properties, under Shortcut tab->  change Run->from Normal Window to Run->Maximized.

Thereafter, any time you click the Internet Explorer, it will open full screen.

If you have Taskbar on the right side of the screen, right click on the Taskbar->click on Properties-> Select the Taskbar location on Screen as Bottom. Click Apply. Click OK.

Regards,
