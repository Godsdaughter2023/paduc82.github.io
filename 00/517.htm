daniel g. grau say:
That last page can surely be eliminated. When you add a section break, that "Section character" affects the parameters of the preceding text. There are implicit section specifications contained in the last character of the document, shown as "¶." So, to get rid of that extra page, it's should be enough to go to the end of the document and specify one or two columns at that very point. Of course, all this will be easier to do if you display nonprinting characters.

To control what pages to print, don't use Save As > PDF, as this saves the whole documents as any save operation should. Instead:
  1. Select File > Print
  2. Specify the page range (if such option is not displayed, click on the disclose triangle at the very top)
  3. Click on the PDF button at the bottom of the Print dialog box and choose Save As PDF
Clicking on the Preview button next to it will let you see the PDF file in the Preview app (plus you can Save it from there as well).

marcine lohman say:

I have found a solution that seems to work for me.

Windows 7:
1. Close Outlook 2010.
2. Click Start.
3. In the search box enter "Credential". You will then see Credential Manager.
4. Select Credential Manager.
5. Under Generic Credentials, you will most likely see an entry that has Outlook in the name.
6. Delete it. In fact, you can delete all the entries in Generic Credentials if you like.
7. Close the Credential Manager window and open Microsoft Outlook.
8. If it asks for a password, enter the password and check for it to remember the password.
9. A new entry will be created in the credential vault with your latest login and password.
10. Problem fixed.

The problem for us has been that the saved password in the credential vault file doesn't appear to work (especially if we've changed the password recently) so by deleting it, another credential will be created the next time you log into Outlook with your latest password.

 

I hope this helps.

jocelyn_price say: Hi, this worked for me. I was unable to log into my account for four days, but now I did it. Please spread the word for other users as well.

Note: The following steps will currently only work if your previous account was @Hotmail.com or @Live.com.
 
In your browser, copy this when logged in but unable to get in -->  https://account.live.com/ManageAssocIds

      Under “Other email addresses” click the Remove link below your previous address.

Click the Remove button to confirm the message.

    You should now see a message on the page stating, “You've removed an email address from your Microsoft account.”

    Copy link --> https://account.live.com/ChangeId.aspx in your browser.
    Verify that “Create a new email address” is selected.

    In the New Microsoft account box, enter your previous address and select your previous domain (Hotmail.com or live.com).  

Note:  You must enter your previous address or the process will fail.
    Select the Save button.

Now you can use your original @hotmail.com or @live.com account to sign into your Microsoft services as you did before renaming to an Outlook.com account. 

 The @outlook.com address will now be an alias on your account from which you can still send and receive messages within Outlook or Hotmail.
