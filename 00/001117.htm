Hi,
There are multiple issues with what you want to do:

  1. You can only sync e-mail with the first PC you sync with.
  2. You cannot sync with both PCs if the PCs are synchronizing with Exchange.  This is to prevent loops.
  3. You can only sync the HD2 with one PC if you also synchronize it directly with Exchange.

So the short answer is that Microsoft did not design the WMDC/ActiveSync solution to address using your device as a backup or to move data from one PC to another.

Lex

In the early days of e-mail there was nothing to separate the original post from the replies. People used various items, such as spaces, +, *, /, >, and non-keyboard characters, to separate the original e-mail from the replies. Enough people requested such markers that they became optional - then standard (with the option to turn it off). 

Each reply added another character to all the previous lines. I've gotten some that had so many replies that the original e-mail was reduced to a string of markers with only three (3) characters of text. Situations like this pushed the move to turn off the feature and the development of programs to remove them.

The e-mail programs I use do no added any markers. They include information from the original (or last reply) as separators. Unfortunately, simple plain text e-mail programs (such as used by phones) still use markers instead of the Date/From/To/Subject separators. See RD_P  replied on  May 26, 2013, above.
