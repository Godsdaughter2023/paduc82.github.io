how to round numbers in excel in a cell which already has anothe - Microsoft Community

emmag95 say:

how to round numbers in excel in a cell which already has anothe formula in it?

I have a a formula in cell E2 =((I9-I10)/4.33+(I10-I8)/4.33)/2 the total equals £72561. How do i get the cell to round up to £72000?

 

Can anyone help?

 

Thank you x

jllatham say:

"wrap" your current formula in a ROUNDUP() function as:

=ROUNDUP(((I9-I10)/4.33+(I10-I8)/4.33)/2 , -3)

 

The -3 says you want to "ignore" the rightmost 3 digits - or another way of looking at it is that you want to round up to the 4th digit left of the decimal point.

ROUNDUP(71500,-3)  = 72000

ROUNDUP(71200.72,-3) = 72000

ROUNDUP(71000.01,-3) = 72000

 

But I'm thinking maybe you need ROUNDDOWN() to get 72561 DOWN to 72000; works the same way, just change ROUNDUP() above to ROUNDDOWN(), as

ROUNDDOWN( your formula here , -3)

so

=ROUNDDOWN(((I9-I10)/4.33+(I10-I8)/4.33)/2 ,-3) will return 72000
