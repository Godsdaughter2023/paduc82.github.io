considerate_guy say:

The main problem is in what you are asking Movie Maker to do.  You are taking a high-definition 1080p video clip from your camcorder and asking Movie Maker to downgrade the resolution to DVD quality. For those that may not remember, the DVD was created in the era of analog TVs and has a maximum resolution of 720 x 480p (NTSC).

 

You take your crisp 1080p video with about 2 million pixels of individual resolution and put it into a SD format with about 1/3 of a megapixel in resolution (345,600 pixels).  If you then play that on a large screen or even your PC monitor, those pixels are going to look blurry.

 

For the best results, you should not be saving to DVD.  You should be saving to your PC in HD resolution.  When you choose the save movie option, you will see a choice like "Recommended for this project" and it should give you the full 1080p output.  That version, when played back on your computer will have the crisp quality of the original HD video.

 

One other thing to note: the preview in Movie Maker may have to temporarily reduce the quality of your video playback to show you the video preview in near real time.  While this may make the interim playback look blurry, you'll get that resolution back in the final movie when Movie Maker does the full rendering to the HD file.  So don't judge the quality in the preview of Movie Maker, or on a DVD.  Instead look at the movie when saved back out at 1080p HD quality.
