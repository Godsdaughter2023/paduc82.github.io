suzanne s. barnhill say:
To add to what Graham and Charles have said, there are a number of changes in Word 2013 that affect layout. If a document is created in Word 2010 and opened in Word 2013, the layout options for Word 2010 are used, so any differences will be attributable to the printer driver. But if a document is created in Word 2013, it will use the Word 2013 layout options; although these should also be correctly interpreted in Word 2010, they do mean that a new Word 2013 document may look different from an apparently identical Word 2010 document if the latter is using custom layout options that are no longer permitted in Word 2013.

To get an idea of what I'm talking about, go to File | Options | Advanced in Word 2010 and scroll down to the bottom. Click the triangle to expand the Layout Options. Now look at the Layout Options in Word 2013; you'll see that you have far fewer options. All the others that you see in Word 2010 are set to the Word 2013 default, which may be different from the Word 2010 default (for example, "Suppress Space Before after a hard page or column break" is enabled and cannot be disabled).

Some other new wrinkles in Word 2013: you can't have a tab stop outside the right margin, and you can't have a wrapped graphic in the document body extending into the header or footer.

If you need a Word 2013 document to lay out like Word 2010 (that is, you need access to the Word 2010 Layout Options), you can run a small macro to set it to the Word 2010 format. Here are the instructions, courtesy of Jay Freedman:

Press Alt+F11 to open the macro editor, and press Ctrl+G to open the Immediate window at the bottom of the editor. In that window, paste this line and then press Enter:

 

ActiveDocument.SetCompatibilityMode wdWord2010

 

Close the macro editor. Then open the Options dialog, go to the Advanced options, and scroll to the bottom. Change the dropdown "Lay out this document as if created in" to "Microsoft Word 2010". At that point, the list of 2010 layout options will appear below the dropdown.

  

Full explanation at http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/office/forum/office_2013_release-word/where-can-i-find-suppress-extra-line-spacing-at/70bf7ca3-a884-40c4-ab59-34d2a04a1a8f

As per my snap shot, give this formula in E3 and copy it down.

=INDEX(Sheet2!$D$2:$D$9,MATCH(1,INDEX((Sheet2!$B$2:$B$9=B3)*(Sheet2!$C$2:$C$9=D3),,),0))

=LOOKUP(2,1/((Sheet2!$B$2:$B$9=B3)*(Sheet2!$C$2:$C$9=D3)),Sheet2!$D$2:$D$9)
