doda521 say:

my fan is constantly running.

My fan is constantly running.Driving me crazy.Just started about 2 weeks ago.Cannot figure out what happened.Am running Win7.Any help is appreciated.

bossdweeb say: The fan is supposed to run constantly.
Laptop or PC ?
Has there been an increase in the ambient air temperature, or have you been doing
things on the machine that put it under increased stress ? These things will make the
machine hotter, and that will cause the fan(s) to speed up.
Unfortunately some cpu and graphics card coolers have fans that get very noisy at high revs.

Run a temperature monitor to verify the temps are not getting too high.

If temps  are too high read on -
It could be that dust is blocking the air intakes or cooling fins on the cpu and graphics
card (if you have one) making the cooling less efficient. The dust can be cleaned off the fins
with a small brush or can of compressed air (don't use a vacuum cleaner as they are said to
cause static electricity) .
Disconnect the power to the machine when working inside.
If there are filters on the case fans these can be cleaned with a damp sponge or removed and washed.
If it's a laptop you may have to settle for using a vacuum cleaner ( I know - static, but on the
outside it should not be a problem) on the air intake slots.

Then there is the possibility the fans bearing is worn out and you need a new fan.

moaskefoto say:

It seems I have found the solution to this (or at least in my case) :)

After a lot of contact with Dell support (kudo's guys for the endurance of hunting this down!) it came down to problems with the video drivers. Apparently these control 'sleep' and 'hibernate' to a great extent.

My Dell laptop has a Intel HD graphics card as well as a NVIDIA Geforce GT950M discrete GPU. It turns out that the order of installation of the drivers for these is CRITICALLY important!

So first I got myself the correct drivers: the drivers from the Dell website that were certified by Dell for my system and tested for Windows 10 (this is important!). After that I proceeded in this EXACT order (also VERY important):

-FIRST uninstall the NVIDIA driver

-ONLY THEN uninstall the Intel HD driver and also delete it (check the box in the uninstall prompt!)

-Reboot system

-Check the Device Manager that you are now running on a generic Windows Display Driver and there's an unknown 3D device listed (this verifies your drivers are truly gone)

-Now FIRST install the correct Intel HD driver

-Reboot if prompted (don't wait :) )

-Only then install your correct NVIDIA Geforce driver from the laptop manufacturer. ATTENTION: choose 'custom' install here so you can check the box to make it a full CLEAN install that resets all old settings (if any found).

-Reboot again

In my case the problem was solved with this. Hope this helps others where to look at! :-)
