Just to be sure were clear on the terminology, you don't typically print templates. Templates are simply used as models for generating new documents. Most of the templates supplied with Word provide placeholder content in the document which needs to be either replaced with the user's content or removed altogether.

Some of the items included when the document is first generated are in the Content layer of the document. That is the part that typically presents no problem for the user because it is directly accessible. Most of the Text frames are actually fields whose content 'disappears' when clicked. The newly-typed content completely replaces the placeholder content. It's rather difficult to add content to the placeholder's "Lorem Ipsum" text.

Many of the templates generate documents which also have content on what are called the the user needs to go to the Master Pages layer by clicking the tab in the lower right corner of the document window. The content on a Master Page can be edited in the same manner as what is on a Content page. The difference is that the result is imposed on
Without specific details to go on, it sounds like the content you're referring to may be Master Page content. If there is more than one page based on the Master, "covering up" the content on only 1 of those pages allows it to be retained on the others. Once removed from the Master Page it will not appear on any of the Content pages.
