johnsonsplace say:

Issue resolved. I was able to resolve this issue by going through the following steps:

1. Open Windows Media Center
2. Go to Tasks and click on Settings
3. Click on General
4. Click on Windows Media Center Setup
5. Click on Run Setup Again

The first time I did this, I selected the Express Setup which did not work. I then went through it again and selected the Custom Setup. That seemed to do the trick and I now have the Internet TV and Netflix options.

Pretty simple, and I probably should have tried this earlier, but perhaps this will help someone else.

arminatorx say:

To prevent input lag, you should make it, so your TV only has to process as few things as possible. 

So if your TV is a FullHD TV, you should keep your resolution at 1080p, otherwise the TV has to calculate and scale up your 720p image to 1080p to display a full-screen image. 

Most TVs have a lot of image enhancements turned on by default, like smoothing movement by calculating interpolated frames to get to 120Hz or much higher refresh rates. 

Look into the settings menu or the user manual of your TV to check if it has a so called "Game mode" or "Direct mode", where all image processing by the TV is turned off. 

Every image enhancement feature the TV does to "improve" image quality, will take processing time, which will cause input lag. For non-interactive videos like a TV broadcast or a movie from a DVD or streaming service, it doesn't matter if the video image shows up several microseconds later. So you can still have sharper contrast or whatever your TV tries to do with it's image processor during such non-interactive use. 

But for interactive gaming, turn off as much image enhancements as possible.
