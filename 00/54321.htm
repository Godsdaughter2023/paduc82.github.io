Step 1: Check if the camera is detected under File explorer. To check, follow the steps below:

  1. Connect the camera to the computer and press Windows logo + E keys on the keyboard to open File explorer.

  2. Select Devices and click on the AutoPlay option from the left side panel of the window.
  3. Under Chose AutoPlay defaults, select the option of your choice under Removable drive and Memory card.
  4. Close the window, connect the device and check if the changes are effective.

Step 1: Update the camera drivers from Device manager.

  2. Detect the camera drivers under Universal Serial Bus Controller. Check for the exclamation mark (If the exclamation is shown on the driver icon, it means the driver needs updating as the driver may be corrupted or non-updated).
  3. Right click on the camera driver and select Update this software from the context menu.
  4. Follow the on-onscreen and wait for the update to complete.
  5. After the update is completed, close the window and check if the issue is resolved.

¡firedog say:
I'm afraid you can't. Some properties of a mail message are 'inherent' - its subject, date sent and received by the mail server, sender, addressees and priority, for example - while others are recorded by Windows Live Mail in its message database. These include the message's location, its flags for read, replied to and forwarded, and of course the red flag you may have applied to draw attention to it. The inherent properties travel with the .eml file as an alternate data stream, but the rest are lost if the database index is no longer present.
