Flaw in Track Changes: "All Markup" Can Be Clicked When "Insertions and Deletions" are Unchecked under "Markup Options," Creating a Contradiction

I'm the director of copyediting at a major advertising agency in San Francisco. We use Microsoft Word for the composition, revision and editing of all our copy. We've found what we think is a flaw in the Track Changes functionality that is making us consider discontinuing our use of Word and instead using Google Docs, where the problem I'm going to address below isn't an issue.

Because the vast majority of people we interact with aren't experts in Track Changes, like the copyeditors are, they don't understand the nuances of the functionality. Here is the problem: when users go to open a document and see "All Markups" visible in the Review pane, they expect to see markups. However, one can have "All Markups" visible but also have—at the same time—"Insertions and Deletions" unchecked under "Markup Options." As a result, when users don't see revision or edits, they panic, go back to previous versions to revise, and thereby create version-control issues.

In our eyes, this is a contradiction that is confusing and that seems to state something that isn't true. We feel you should not be able to state that all markups are visible when, in fact, those markups aren't visible. 

This is causing massive version-control problems for us, and we're hoping the Office team would consider fixing this element of Track Changes.
