canadian tech say:

SFC will produce this fictitious result if either or both of these two Windows Updates are installed on your computer.   Remove them.  They are not important to your computer.

SFC : KB3022345
SFC:  KB3068708

Go to Windows Update. Bottom left corner, click on View installed updates.  The display takes a long time to fill out, so wait while the green band moves all the way across the top of the window.  Now type in one of these two KB codes into the search box.  If it is not installed, clear the search box, and WAIT.  Now type in the other.  If either are found uninstall them.  Restart.  Run SFC again.  If neither are installed, then there are real corrupted files in your operating system and the likely cure will be a Windows 7 re-install.

Hello Roda,

I understand that you have two monitors and you're having issues moving the mouse over or across to them. Given that information, we'd like you to follow these steps below on how you can move your mouse between the two monitors:

  1. Right-click on your laptop and select "Display settings".

  3. Drag and hold your secondary monitor onto the left side of the main monitor and select Apply.
  4. Also, make sure that you select "Extend these displays" under the Multiple displays.
