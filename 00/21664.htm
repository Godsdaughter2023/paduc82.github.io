stephenmb1 say:

Hi,

 

Yes it is possible to do both these things on the one conditional formatting rule.

 

You should be able to adjust the background colour of ther cells using the original conditional formatting rule that you have applied to the text. The steps below should help.

 

1. Firstly you can do one of two things,

a) Highlight the cells with the Conditional Formatting applied to the text then click Conditional Formatting, then manage rules.

or                                                                 

b) Click on Conditional Formatting, then manage rules, then click the drop down menu at the top of this tab, select this worksheet, and then click once on whichever formatting you need to amend (if you have more than one Conditional Fornatting set that is).

 

2. After doing either of the above click on 'Edit Rule' tab.

 

3. A box will pop up showing what you've chosen to format and what formatting you've chosen. In this box there is a preview box showing you a preview of your formatting, next to this is a button labelled "Format", click this.

 

4. This will then bring up another box with four tabs "Number, Font, Border, Fill", click on the Font tab to adjust the font to your liking, i.e. which font, font style, font size, colour etc...

 

5. Once above done click on "Fill". Here you can choose what colour you want the cell background to go when the conditional format rule has been met.

 

6. Click Ok when ready, then click OK on the next box, you should be back at your Rules Manager box now, here you can either click Apply then OK, or just OK.

 

Once all the above done, the background and font colour of your chosen cells will adjust itself to your Conditional Formatting Rule once it has been met.

 

Hope this helps,

 

Steve.

ronrosenfeld say: I apologize for the late reply.  This new Answers forum makes following my threads much more awkward than when it was accessible via a regular newsreader.  But TPTB (MS) in their wisdom decided that was too easy, and removed the access, and also made notification of responses difficult.

To Trim the contents of myCell:
    myCell.value = Trim(myCell.text)

To trim the contents of each cell in any particular worksheet you'll need to do a loop through the range you want to trim.  For example:

Dim c as Range
Dim dst as worksheet
  Set dst = 'whatever
for each c in dst.UsedRange
     c.value = trim(c.text)
next c

You could certainly substitute a predetermined range for UsedRange.  e.g.

for each c in dst.Range("A1:A" & LastRowDst)
   c.value = Trim(c.text)
next c
