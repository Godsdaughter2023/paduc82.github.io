If you were able to connect the power cable & data cable from your laptop to the HD you bought, then you have the right one.

Now to the 137Gb. If your motherboard BIOS is dated before 2002, it can have an upper limit on HD capacity it recognises of 137Gb. This doesn't mean that you cannot use the larger capacity when using XP (SP1 or later versions), just that the capacity is not visible until the Operating System (OS) is loaded.

What version of XP is the installation disc you have? If it is not SP1 or later, there is a specific way in which you would have to install XP.

Follow the below steps to put your computer in clean boot state:

1. Log on to the computer by using an account that has administrator rights.

4. On the Services tab, click to select the Hide all Microsoft services check box, and then click Disable all.
5. Click OK, and then click Restart.
