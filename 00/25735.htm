swischner say:

A template in OneNote is nothing else but a OneNote page already containing some content like for example pre-filled or empty tables or images. In the windows desktop versions of OneNote (like OneNote 2010, 2013 or 2016) there is an option to automatically apply a template whenever you create a new page in a defined section.

Other OneNote versions including the Android App do not have that option hence no dedicated template support. But what you can always do is create a OneNote page (maybe in a separate notebook or section you may want to use as a "template library"), design it at will (e.g. as a "travel template") and copy it over to your actual working notebook every time you create a new page. Its some additional clicks or taps compared to the automatic use of templates in OneNote desktop for Windows but the result is the same.
