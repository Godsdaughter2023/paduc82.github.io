Updates for Windows Mobile - Microsoft Community

miketak say:

Updates for Windows Mobile

I noticed the Palm 700wx I just bought doesn't have the most recent version of Windows Mobile. How do updates work? Is there a cost? Is there anything like automatic updates for desktops?
don sorcinelli say:

Mission,

 

Your question is likely one of the oldest standing questions relating to Pocket PC/Smartphone/Windows Mobile devices - "Can I Upgrade My Device On My Own?" The answer has changed only slightly over the years, and has gone from "no" to "maybe - with lots of caveats".

 

Generally speaking, a device owner is mostly at the mercy of the cellular carrier, device manufacturer or both when it comes to updates. The "short version" for the process for updating generally is:

  • Microsoft provides updates to a version of the operating system. These are then made available to all applicable partners (device manufacturers and carriers);
  • The changes made by Microsoft typically require additional customization based upon the hardware and configuration of the device. If the carrier/manufacturer choose to do so, they make the necessary additional changes.
  • The final changes are offered as an update directly by the manufacturer/carrier.

The track record of the manufacturers and carriers varies, but generally speaking has not been very good. For many carriers, the cost of labor required for customization, deployment and support has become the default reasoning for not taking the upgrades on. Believe me - I do not view this as an excusse (personally, I have been appalled at times over the years by this explanation, especially when the upgrade can solve problems and reduce support costs).

 

The change that has occurred that leads to the "maybe" has been related to the efforts of individuals regading porting versions of the Windows Mobile OS to various devices outside of the normal channels. A search of web sites like XDA-Developers will make these efforts very clear. Unfortunately, these efforts lead to discussions regarding the legality of the efforts based upon the Windows Mobile OS and licensing.

 

The simplest conclusions for all of this (and to yours and other posts in this thread) are:

  1. Check with the manufacturer and carrier for you device for the latest updates;
  2. If you feel that a carrier or manufacturer is not supporting your device properly, let them know. Contact their customer support and log your complaint.

I only wish I had a better solution for all of this to pass along.

 

Don

How do i set up a reminder that a cell value is about to expire? - Microsoft Community

welshc say:

How do i set up a reminder that a cell value is about to expire?

I want to know if its possible when i enter a date issued and expiry date in cells how to set up a reminder that will pop up letting me know they have expired or about to
rich007a say:

Your question is not very clear.  I assume you are talking about having cells containing dates, and comparing those dates to today's date???

You can use Conditional Formatting for this.

If your date is in cell A1, do the following:

In the Home tab, click Conditional Formatting > Manage Rules.

Click New Rule.

In the top box, click Use a formula to determine which cells to format.

In the lower box, enter the formula:

    =A1>TODAY()

then click the Format button and format as you wish, for example, you could set the Fill colour to red.  OK.

Click New Rule again, repeat the above, this time enter:

    =A1>TODAY()-2

and set the fill to Yellow.  This is your "about to expire" condition.  The -2 represents 2 days before.  Adjust per your requirements.

Note: if you define "expired" as occurring the day after the expiry date, use the above, but if you define "expired" as occurring on the same day as the expiry date, use  >=  in place of  >  in the formulas.

You need to make sure the Expired condition comes before the About To Expire condition, so use the up/down arrows at the top of the dialog box to put the red condition above the yellow condition.

Hit OK to complete the task.

Now if you want to do the above on a range of cells, select the cells you wish to apply the conditional formatting to, and do exactly the same process, you just need to make sure that the cell you reference in the formula is the cell that is Activated in your selection.  For example, if you select the range G6:G14 by click+holding in G6 and dragging the selection down to G14, G6 will be the active cell, so you would use G6 instead of A1 in the formulas.  You also need to make sure you do not use absolute references (e.g. $A$1) if you want each cell to look at itself.

Hope that helps.

Cheers
Rich
