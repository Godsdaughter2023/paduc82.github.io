Yes, guidelines are always under images, text boxes and other design elements you put on the page. If you put a large page size picture on the page or in the background then it will cover all the guidelines. Sorry but this is by design and has always been the case with Publisher.

Guidelines are helpful for laying out and arranging smaller design elements in relation to each other. Do this before you insert the background picture which covers all the lines.

If you have Rulers activated on your page when you grab an element you will see a temporary guide line from edges of the element to the ruler which is another way to align your elements.

how do you change page breaks to section breaks in manuscripts - Microsoft Community

robert daley say:

how do you change page breaks to section breaks in manuscripts

I have 22 of my books on Kindle, and now wish to publish them on The Nook. On Kindle every chapter ends with a page break, so that the next one starts on a new page. But The Nook does not recognize page breaks but requires section breaks instead. Since each of these manuscripts has up to 50 chapters, I must find a way to change page breaks to section breaks all at once.
daniel g. grau say:

Given the mess you seem to be facing, I think it's "desperate times."

Do a Save As or make a copy of the file in the Finder. I'm assuming you are not using tracked changes—that's a recipe for formatting disasters. If you don't know what those are, then you are not using them!

Let's first do a global replacement to get rid of all the section breaks (make sure you can see them, as explained in the "Setup" portion of my previous solution message). (There may be inactive breaks in the middle.) Go to the top of of the document:

  1. Select Edit > Find > Advanced Find and Replace
  2. Click on the Replace tab at the top
  3. Place your cursor in the Find what field
  4. Type ^b (that's caret b, where caret is above the "6"key in a US keyboard); it stands for a Section Break of any kind.
  5. Place your cursor in the Replace with field
  6. Type ^p, which stands for end of paragraph. So we'll be replacing all section breaks by the ¶ character.
  7. Click on Replace All

Now go to the place where the first section break should go and insert it. Then, place the cursor

Access the headers in that section (1st page and/or running header) and paste them from the original file. Delete any extra ¶s. 

Place the cursor after the first section break and access the header. Click on the purple "Headers and Footers" tab and take off the "Link to previous" checkmark, or add it, as per your formatting specs. The checkmark will keep its state (on or off) as you add new section breaks. Whenever you want to change the running header in a new section just inserted, take off that checkmark. If you want to use the same header as the previous section, enable the checkmark.

Proceed similarly with the rest of the section breaks. Keep visual track of what you insert, to become familiar with such intricacies.

Remember also that you can make the page number start/restart at arbitrary numbers via Insert > Page numbers. Any such change will affect the whole chain of running headers or footers of various linked sections that span the location of the cursor.

Note that all section parameters (page, margin, headers, etc.) are stored at its trailing section break, except for the last portion of the document, where section parameters are stored as the end-of-document ¶ character.

daniel g. grau say:
As long as your documents are in Word, this is how to do it:

For some reason, Word won't let you do global replacements into section breaks, so you need to work around this limitation by disguising the replacement.

Setup
  1. Select Word > Preferences > Compatibility
  2. Scroll down to "Split apart page break and paragraph mark" and place a checkmark (this is so Word won't add an extra Return when replacing)
  3. Select Word > Preferences > View
  4. In the second group of items, place a checkmark in All (this will display nonprinting characters in blue)
  5. Exit the Preferences

You can later undo the above changes.

Replacement
  1. Place your cursor anywhere in the first document
  2. In the main menu, select Insert > Break > Section Break (Next Page). This will display as "======Section Break (Next Page)======."
  3. Select the blue section break so it's highlighted and Cut it to the clipboard
  4. Select Edit > Find > Advanced Find and Replace
  5. Click on the Replace tab at the top
  6. Place your cursor in the Find what field
  7. Type ^m                  ("caret m" is Word's placeholder for "page break")
  8. Place your cursor in the Replace with field
  9. Type ^c                   (stands for "clipboard contents," i.e. the section break; we would normally type the shortcut for a section break here, but Word knows better and won't let you, so we use the clipboard)
  10. Click on Replace All

You can leave the Find and Replace dialog open, and then just click Replace All on each document.
