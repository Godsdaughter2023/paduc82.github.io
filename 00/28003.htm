gmteacher say:

I had this same problem for the last 5 or 6 builds, before that, BT file transfer worked fine using the Broadcom drivers from their site. With these latest builds, they fail to install, crashing halfway through the process.

Here's what I did to make phone to PC transfers work for me;

1: Go to Control Panel / Device Manager and right click to uninstall any Bluetooth devices that you find.

2. When you click "Uninstall", the Confirm dialog box should pop up. Be sure to check the box, "Delete the driver software..."

3. Reboot the computer and I expect that Win 10 will find the BT adapter and reinstall it and its driver upon reboot.

If not, go back to Device Manager, click the "Scan for new hardware icon, and let it find and install the BT Adapter. (Make sure it's turned on if there's an "F" key or something that can be used to turn it on and off.)

4. When it's done installing the drivers, there should be a BT Icon in the notification bar like this.

If not, search for Bluetooth settings in the start menu, click on it, and then select Advanced and this box should pop up and be sure the "Show Icon..." is checked.

5. Now go to that icon and click on it. Select "Receive a file."

That should open this dialog box.

While this dialog is open, initiate the file transfer from your phone. The dialog should change and show the computer receiving the file. Once it's uploaded, you can chose where to store it. I use the Bluetooth Exchange folder in my Documents.

I hope this is helpful for you and solves the problem.  It worked for my computer.

paulgailiunas say:

Hi Paul

A minor issue has been overlooked here.

Your table 2 Attributes . . is already a cross reference table

you have record 102 Adults . .2 . .  and record 105  . . Adults  . .3

So it is possible to have a vehicle  ( i presume this is for RV vehicles ) to have both an attribute 102 and 105. . . IE  Adults 2 and Adults 3

This setup may be quite valid . . but it is messy and prone to error.

A better structure would have your Cross-Ref table containing . .

CUSTUNIQID

ATTUNIQID

ATTRIB_D

 

that is  . . the ATTRIB_D moves from the attributes table to the Cross Reference Table

 

now . . if the actual attributes is finite . . fixed . .you can setup a standard group query that links customer, attributes and cross-ref table.

fill in the columns for customer etc

now for the attribute values . . setup a column for each possible attribute

eg

Adults:IIF(ATTUNIQID=102,ATTUNIQID,"")

RV_Lth:IIF(ATTUNIQID=100,ATTUNIQID,"")   . . .etc

because you get a seperate row for each combination of records - a customer with 3 attributes will have 3 lines . . so

you set the "group" attribute to max for these columns

And only group by the customer data.

this will give you 1 line per customer

 

if a customer has no attribute records . .  then you will need fiddle the inner joins to make sure the customer record appears.

 

hope this helps . .
