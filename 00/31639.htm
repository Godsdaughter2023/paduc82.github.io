lemp say:

I don't know what you mean by "it's gone."  Your USB drive is shown in the list of hard drives

The way your BIOS is currently configured, the computer will attempt to boot

  • first, from the DVD drive; if nothing is in the DVD drive, then
  • second, from the floppy drive; if nothing is in the floppy drive, then
  • third, from the first hard drive (Western Digital 400 GB)

If you want to attempt to boot from the USB flash drive, you either have to make it the first option in the list of boot devices (what happens when you press + or - in the "Boot Device Priority" screen?) or you have to make it the "first hard drive" so that when the BIOS gets to the third option above, it goes to the USB device.

In the "Hard Disk Drives" screen, go to "1st Drive" and select the USB drive as the option.  Reset "2nd Drive" and "3rd Drive" accordingly.

********************************************

On the other hand, it appears that you may not have properly prepared your USB flash drive.  If you are able to force the computer to attempt to boot from the USB device from your "F8 boot menu" and get a "Missing Operating System" error, you may have just copied the downloaded file without making the USB flash drive bootable.  See, e.g.,


http://pcsupport.about.com/od/windows7/a/install-windows-7-usb.htm

http://www.askvg.com/a-bootable-usb-utility-to-create-bootable-usb-drive-to-install-windows-vista-server-2008-and-7/

genepavlovsky say:

Hey Nick!

Have you read my comment? It most definitely works on Windows 7 x64, but it might depend on your Bluetooth adapter.  The mouse uses Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) which is part of the BT 4.0 standard. Pros - really long battery life, fast reconnect from standby, etc. Cons - it's not compatible with BT3.0 or lower.

I think it's Microsoft's fault for not making it very clear in the product description. They should put this info in big bold letters, and offer to download some tool that would check if your system is compatible - before you buy the product. An average consumer doesn't know about differences in BT version, she just know they have Bluetooth in their laptop/PC, and it is a "Bluetooth mouse", so it's intuitive to assume it should work, right? Turns out, it's not always true...

There is a reason Microsoft only list Windows 8 and up as compatible with that mouse: Microsoft didn't provide native support (drivers) for Bluetooth Low Energy in Windows 7 and below, it only appeared in Windows 8. It can work in Windows 7, but that depends on 3rd-party Bluetooth software (drivers) manufacturers, so Microsoft doesn't want to be responsible for testing their mouse with other manufacturers' software.

In my case, my laptop has a Bluetooth 4.0 module, with chipset by Broadcom. I've also tested an ASUS USB BT-400 dongle, also using a Broadcom chipset. So both of them work together with Broadcom's Bluetooth drivers (WIDCOMM). But I can't be sure if it can work with another brand of Bluetooth module/dongle.
