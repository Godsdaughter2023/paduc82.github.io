jp ronse say: Hi PSnipes,

I think that Mike has given the answer.



Let say that the above picture is your table till the end of the month.

Now, over-here December is a special month with a free day on Christmas and Boxing Day (no I'm not from the UK.) I've put these dates in another range (AI):



The formula to calculate (in this model, in B1)

=IF(OR(WEEKDAY(B3,2)>5,IFERROR(VLOOKUP(B3,$AI$1:$AI$2,1,FALSE)=B3,FALSE)),"","WD"&NETWORKDAYS($B$3,B3,$AI$1:$AI$2))

Returns WD1 in C1, WD2 in D1, ....

Let's have a look to the Christmas period:



25 & 26 are skipped because they were in the list in AI.

To explain a bit the formula:

OR(WEEKDAY(B3,2)>5,IFERROR(VLOOKUP(B3,$AI$1:$AI$2,1,FALSE)=B3,FALSE)

Herein 2 parts:

WEEKDAY(B3,2)>5 : elimating the weekends Saturday & Sunday
VLOOKUP(B3,$AI$1:$AI$2,1,FALSE)=B3: the vlookup, part returns the date - if found - from AI, if vlookup returns a value, then convert to a boolean by comparing by its own lookup value.

If vlookup returns an error, the result is false.

So, either it is a weekend day or holiday, do not take this day in account.


The NETWORKDAYS($B$3,B3,$AI$1:$AI$2) part must also know if there are holidays in the period, therefore we have to add the AI range to the formula.
