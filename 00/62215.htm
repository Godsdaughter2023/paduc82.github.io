nicolekee say:

I actually just solved my problem after fiddling around. To help anyone else out, here's what I did.

I went to the Text Service and Input Languages window (Control Panel → Region and Language → Keyboard and Languages → Change Keyboard) and highlighted Microsoft IME under Korean and click properties. The keyboard type was selected as 3 Belosik Final. I switched it to 2 Belosik and now the Korean keyboard is the correct layout!

Along with the suggestion given by Brenda Woollard, I would like to add few more word to the above post.

 

If you have linked the current user account to Microsoft account, you can convert the same account to Local user account. However, keeping at least one Microsoft account with administrative privileges on the PC will let you enjoy all the Windows delightful features like Cortana (your new personal assistant), Spotlight feature, download apps and games from Windows Store and many more.

 

To convert the current Microsoft user account to Local user account follow the steps below:

 

  1. Sign in into your Microsoft user account on Windows 10.

  2. Press Windows logo key on the keyboard, type Settings and select the top most search result.

  3. Click Accounts and under Your emails and accounts click on sign in with a local account instead option.

  4. Enter your Microsoft account's password and click on Next.

  5. Type in the Password and Password Hint that you want set for your local account and click Next. Click on Sign out and Finish button.

 

To create another user account, follow the suggestion given by Brenda Woollard from the above post.
