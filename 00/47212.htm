If those custom folders are part of the "Storage Folders" inside the Live Mail Program, then no, you cannot transfer them to other apps.


What I would recommend is to create similarly named folders within your actual email account (right-click on "Inbox" and choose "New Folder").  This will create a folder on your online account (the email company that handles your email for you, such as Gmail or Outlook/Hotmail).

This also had the added benefit of backing up your messages. The Storage Folders are local to only your computer.  But with the messages stored in the online account, they will be safe even if your computer broke.

For those who already have v.11 installed, they can uninstall v.11 of the IME driver from Device Manager, which needs a reboot, and after that install a downgraded version of that driver, preferably v.9, from the links mentioned in my 1st para.

Hi Wyzard49, you can reinstall Windows 10 at any time, you will not need a Product Key, and it will not cost you anything !

4. If Required enable USB Boot
5. Move the device with the bootable disc to the top of the boot order
6. Save BIOS changes, restart your System and it should boot from the Installation Media
