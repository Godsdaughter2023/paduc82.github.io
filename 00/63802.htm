Hi sam2tone,

• Does this happen only when you type a particular program files or with all program files?

• Were you able to print before without any issues?

You may try the following steps and check if it helps:

Step 1: Replace the Printer drivers.

This issue may occur if you have a damaged printer driver. To complete this procedure, you need your Microsoft Windows CD-ROM, or the media that contains your printer driver. To replace a damaged printer driver:

1. Quit all Windows programs.

2. Click Start, click Printers and Faxes, and then click Add a printer.

3. Right-click your installed printer, and then click Delete. If you are prompted to remove extra files, click Yes.

4. Click Add a printer, and then follow the instructions on the screen to reinstall your printer driver.

 

The Windows Registry may have a Damaged Default Printer Entry:

Step 2: Reset the Registry Entry

1. Click Start, click Printers and Faxes, and then double-click Add a Printer.

2. Click Next, and then click Local printer attached to this computer.

3. Click to clear the Automatically detect and install my Plug and Play Printer check box, and then click Next.

4. Click LPT1 port, and then click Next.

5. In the Manufacturers box, click Generic.

6. In the Make/Model or Printers box, click Generic/Text Only, and then click Next.

7. In the Printer Name box, type TEST.

8. Under Do you want to use this printer as the default printer, click Yes.

9. Click Next, and then click Do Not Share this printer.

10. Click Next, and then click No under Do you want to print a test page.

11. Click Next, and then click Finish.

12. Click Start, click Printers and Faxes.

13. Right-click the Generic/Text Only printer or your other existing printer, and then click Set as Default.

14. Right-click your original printer, and then click Set as Default.

 

This may also occur if a Device Conflict Exists on the Printer Port.

A device may be conflicting with your computer printer port, or the port may not be turned on. You may have to remove and then reinstall the printer port:

Step 3: Remove and reinstall the printer port

1. Right-click Computer, and then click Properties.

2. Click the Hardware tab.

3. Click Device Manager.

4. Double-click Ports (COM & LPT).

5. Double-click the port to which your printer is connected.

6. If the Device status box says that the device is working correctly, your computer may have another problem. Contact your printer manufacturer.

7. If the Device status box indicates a problem, click OK, and then click Remove.

8. Click OK, and then restart your computer.

9. After your computer restarts, click Start, and then click Control Panel. Double-click Add New Hardware, and then wait for Windows to detect the hardware in your computer.

 

If the port does not work after you remove it and reinstall it, examine the printer cable and the BIOS settings on your computer to make sure that the port is turned on. See your computer manufacturer documentation for instructions.

Note: Modifying BIOS/complementary metal oxide semiconductor (CMOS) settings incorrectly can cause serious problems that may prevent your computer from booting properly. Microsoft cannot guarantee that any problems resulting from the configuring of BIOS/CMOS settings can be solved. Modifications of the settings are at your own risk.
