jeeped say:

I would start by being specific about references and not relying upon .Select to govern that a subsequent command is applied to. It also seems that you performed the same operation in horizontal batches when VBA could do all of it in one go. I am concerned about your MyPath ending in a backslash and what file list the Dir(MyPath & "BP") actually returns. If you wanted all workbooks starting with BP it should be Dir(MyPath & "BP*.xl*"). Another concern is that when you open the workbook, you immediately start working on the worksheet and current selection without checking to see what is selected or even what worksheet you are on.

This might help you get started but I do not believe it is a complete solution.

Sub Addenda1()
    Dim MyFile As String, MyPath As String, wb As Workbook
    MyPath = "xxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxx"  'should end in backslash
    MyFile = Dir(MyPath & "BP*.xl*")
    Do While MyFile <> ""
        Set wb = Workbooks.Open(MyPath & MyFile)
        With wb
            With .Sheets("Sheet1")
                'I don't know what to do with this command as it relies on what was selected
                ' when the workbook opened
                'Selection.Insert Shift:=xlDown, CopyOrigin:=xlFormatFromLeftOrAbove
                .Range("C26:AZ26").AutoFill Destination:=.Range("C26:AZ27"), Type:=xlFillDefault
                .Range("O27:AZ27").Formula = "='[Master Sheet.xlsx]Sheet1'!$A$25"
                'I don't know what to do with this either. Perhaps it has something to do with above
                .Range("O28:AW28").Select
            End With
            .Close True 'save and close at the same time
        End With
        Set wb = Nothing
        MyFile = Dir
    Loop
End Sub
