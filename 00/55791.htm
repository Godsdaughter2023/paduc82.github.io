remove letter=X

Close Command Prompt and restart your PC

This is one of the known issues with the update as Recovery partition should be hidden and is accidentally assigned a drive letter.
Right-click Start>Command Prompt(admin)
Type in the following:
diskpart
list volume
select volume 3
Replace 3 with the number of the volume which is assigned a drive letter. (In hundreds of MBs)
remove letter="G"
Replace G with the Recovery drive letter.

Don't worry, removing a drive letter would not cause any harm to your device.

busta_rhodesian say: Thanks for the extensive reply! Unfortunately, I have tried all these methods in the past and they did not work. I think I accidentally stumbled upon the answer after weeks of problems yesterday. ASUS laptops have this utility called "Wireless Console 3" that is a UI telling you if your wifi is on or not when you turn on the Wifi switch. I uninstalled this months ago, but I read in one forum that uninstalling this sometimes confuses ASUS laptops and your adapter will shut off. I had tried re-installing this before and I still had the same problems. In desperation, I used ASUS's "e-driver" utility that came with the computer that I never uninstalled, and it recommended I download "Wireless Console 3". I downloaded it and now I have not had my adapter turn off since. Perhaps the e-driver utility had an updated version of Wireless Console 3. I am not holding my breath, though- I ordered a USB wifi adapter just in case.
