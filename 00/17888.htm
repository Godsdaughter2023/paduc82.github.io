We want the document to have as many pages as necessary, but only start both numbering of x and y on the second page.
stefan blom say:

Assuming that you don't want page numbering on page 1, you can do the following: Set the page numbering to start at zero. Select the "Different first page" option; you can then delete the page number on the first physical page (numbered zero). The "Page X of Y" feature makes use of the NUMPAGES field, which will be apparent if you press Alt+F9 to show field codes. Add an equals sign before the field and - 1 after:

= { NUMPAGES } - 1

Select the whole construct and press Ctrl+F9; this will enclose it in field delimiters. Press F9 to update. Alt+F9 hides the field codes again.

If you are printing duplex, and you don't want your page 1 on the back of the title page, you'll need to create a front page (with a section break) instead. See http://word.mvps.org/faqs/formatting/front_matter_2007.htm. You would then use a SECTIONPAGES field instead of the formula mentioned above.

Omitting page numbers on pages between chapters in Word 2016

suzanne s. barnhill say:
I'm not sure what you read, but those blank pages are always counted and by publishing convention must be. Whenever you use mirror margins, enable Different Odd and Even for headers/footers, insert an Odd (or Even) Page section break, or restart a section at 1, Word assumes that you will be duplexing the document (printing on both sides). In order to do this properly, it insists on making every odd page a recto (right-side) page, inserting a blank even page if necessary in order to make this come out right. That page has to have an even number; otherwise, you would have two odd numbers together.

  1. On your computer, go to

  2. Sign in using the parent's

  3. On the My Family setup page, click Get started.

  4. To add the child to My Family, click Go. On the sign-in page, enter the child's Microsoft account email address and password. If the child doesn't have a Microsoft account, click Sign up to create one.

  1. On your computer, go to windowsphone.com.
