don cuthbert say:

The error "source file not found" is not very accurate, as DISM may be both finding and using the source you are pointing to. The error, as already mentioned, is that the Install.wim is missing a FlightSigning package that DISM tells it to look for. Check C:\Windows\Logs\CBS.log.

I have found that running DISM /RestoreHealth on your Windows 10 install, while it is offline, works and does not complain about the missing FlightSigning package. So if you boot with matching install media (15063) and use a command as below to do the repair it might complete. Use SHIFT-F10 to get a Command Prompt from the first Setup screen that loads.

DISM /Image:C:\ /Cleanup-Image /RestoreHealth /Source:WIM:D:\Sources\Install.wim:1

Your Windows may show on a drive letter other than C:, so change C: and D: above to whatever matches your system. Then you can also run SFC while offline:

SFC /SCANNOW /OFFWINDIR=C:\WINDOWS /OFFBOOTDIR=C:\

Also, If your install media includes both Home and Pro, you may have to choose the matching Index in Install.wim or Install.esd to the Edition that you have installed. Check Install.wim/Install.esd with:

DISM /Get-WimInfo /WimFile:D:\Sources\Install.wim

EDIT:

Create a folder, such as C:\Scratch. Then edit the command line as below:

DISM /Image:C:\ /Cleanup-Image /RestoreHealth /Source:WIM:D:\Sources\Install.wim:1 /ScratchDir:C:\Scratch

john mcghie say:
Dear oh dear...  This issue is not in the "preferences" :-)

Word spelling depends on the language marked in the text of the document.

First let's have a look in the Options and make sure Spelling is enabled...

Go to Word>Preferences>Spelling and Grammar and turn on "Check spelling while you type".

While we are in there, click the "Dictionaries" button, and make sure there is at least one entry in there that is checked, and that it is set to "no language" (which means it accepts all languages).  If not, see below...

OK your way out, and select some words in the document text.

Go to Tools>Language and see which language is highlighted.  If it is not one of the languages you have installed, your spelling checker will be disabled.  That's probably what has happened.  There are 29 versions of the English language.  Documents with text from elsewhere in the world may be set to a version of English your copy of Word does not have.

To fix this document (cheap and nasty way...) select all of its text, and use Tools>Language> to select the language you want to use.  Also click the Default button, and say "Yes" to the prompt.

To fix this document (the proper way) cancel out from Tools>Language and go to Format>Style, choose the Normal style, then click Modify, Format, Language and choose your language.  On the way out, click the "Add to template" checkbox.

This is two ways of doing essentially the same thing.  A Style is just a named collection of formatting, of which Language is one of the properties.  All styles in a document are based on the Normal style until you change that.  By setting the Normal style to the language you want to use, that change will flow through to the entire document, regardless of the style in use.  The "Default" or "Add to template" settings make this change fall through to your Normal template, which will set the language for all future documents unless you specify a different template for one.

If the Custom Dictionary is not There:

1)  Quit Word

2)  Go to your Preferences folder.  Newer versions of OS X won't let you see this unless you go explicitly to it.  In Finder, hit Command + Shift + g and paste in ~/Library/Preferences/Microsoft

The ~ at the front stands for "my home folder" -- OS X will know where that is.

Look for the folder "Office 2008" and whack it.  Just move it to the Trash, and empty the trash.

When you restart Word, it will re-create the content of that folder, including your custom dictionary.

Everything should work now: come back if it doesn't and we'll get serious with it.

Cheers

Hi Kevin Hinton,

 

To set your reminders for your weekly events, please follow these steps:

 

1. Sign in to your email account.

2. In your Inbox, click on Calendar.

3. In your calendar, click on the event to set the reminder and you'll be directed to the Event details page.

4. In the Time setting, click on Set recurrence, choose weekly for Occurs and provide the necessary setting you want.

5. For Ends, please tick on the after (number of occurences).

6. Then click Save.
