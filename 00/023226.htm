hansv mvp say:

Let's say the date is in column C.

Select the entire range that you want to format. Note the address of the active cell within the selection; let's say it is A1 (or any cell in row 1).

Click Conditional Formatting.

Click New Rule...

Click "Use a formula to determine which cells to format".

Enter the formula =$C1=TODAY()-2

If the active cell was in row 7, use =$C7=TODAY()-2

If the date column is column F instead of C, use =$F7=TODAY()-2 etc.

Click Format... and specify the formatting that you want, then click OK.

Click OK again to close the New Formatting Rule dialog.

Sorry if this sounds rude, but it's your responsibility to UPDATE your account, since you CHANGED your phone number.

To update the phone number on your account, you can do the following :

1. Go to https://account.live.com/proofs/manage and login your account.

2. You will be asked to get a code, which is being sent to your old phone number. Click the dropdown box and select the option "I don't have any of these".

3. You can then provide an alternate email or a phone number that will be used to get the code. Follow the instructions (getting a code to the one that you provided), and you will be told that your account will be updated.

Take note that updating your account this way will take 30 days. This is to prevent hackers who can get your password from updating your account without your consent.

Once the 30 day period is over, you will be able to receive codes for your account again. While you wait, you won't be able to access any of your Billing Information or Account Management page, but you will still have access to other services such as Outlook or XBOX.

This cannot be applied if you are being blocked by a message, such as :

"It looks like someone else might be using your account"

"Verify your account, we've detected something unusual about this sign in"
