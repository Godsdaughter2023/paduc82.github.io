techfreak_ say:

In regards to Caller ID, you can only verify a number you own, not just any random number. This type of verification is not accomplished using email. An alternative to this would be to purchase (lease) a Skype number.

As far as I know and can verify, an SMS short code is sent to any number you want to confirm for Caller ID purposes. This proves your ownership to the number, similar to verifying ownership to an email address. If you are unable to receive the code to your mobile, make sure your provider has not disabled support for SMS short codes (usually an anti-spam measure). If they have, request that they at least temporarily remove that block. To verify a landline number (usually only a virtual variant) your landline provider would have to offer a service that receives SMS short codes and either transcribes them to text for your email or audio for your phone.

If your mobile/landline number cannot receive SMS short codes in some manner, then you will not be able to verify that number.

I am unaware of Skype/Microsoft offering an audible or alternative method to verify a phone number for Caller ID purposes.

What do you mean by "all files"?

If you mean the operating system:

If the Laptop/Computer came with the Windows Operating System preinstalled (Dell, HP, Acer, etc) , that Product Key that came with the Laptop/Computer is for the preinstalled OEM License and is non-transferable.

The OEM License/Product Key (preinstalled Operating Systems on Dell, HP, etc machines) is tied to the first machine it is installed on and dies when that machine dies.

The above rule also applies to OEM System Builders Licenses.

Retail Operating System License, that you buy and install yourself, are allowed to be transferred to another machine.

But Retail Licenses can also only be installed on one machine at any particular time.
