My taskbar will only display on the left or the right - Microsoft Community

chibichibi01 say:

My taskbar will only display on the left or the right

Ok, so my fiance has a brand new (literally just out of the box) Lenovo gaming laptop with an AMD radeon.  Its on windows 10 and the problem is exactly what I said in the subject.  His taskbar will only display when on the left or the right and never on the top or the bottom.  Its on autohide, but even when we turn autohide off, we still get nada.  We've opened up the catalyst settings for Overscan but there's no overscan option at all.

Please help because this is seriously ruining our night and we're both starting to get tetchy and frustrated and about ready to take the laptop back and forget the whole thing.

ralf_g say:

Overscan is mainly for CRT TVs but possibly the display resolution is set too high for his laptop.

If you've got the Catalyst Control Center Icon in the taskbar you can right click it, select  the default video output (may be 2 of them) and then select one of the lower resolutions under "Set Desktop Area to".

Something like 1280x720 or even lower will display the entire Windows desktop on the screen. You might have to open Catalyst Control Center and set the flat panel properties to use "Centered Timings" if it isn't already. The display should then look something like this, with the entire desktop well within the edges of the screen:

If the taskbar becomes visible now after you set it to display on the bottom then you know that the display resolution that was being used was set too high for the actual display area of the screen.
