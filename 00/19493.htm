albert d. kallal say:

The location of the full edition (or runtime) is identical. In fact you really can’t install both at once (the install of the runtime will occur, but it is a “fake” install)

The location is thus:

C:\Program Files (x86)\Microsoft Office\Office15

The above of course is for the x32 bit version which most have.

The x64 bit version should thus be:


C:\Program Files\Microsoft Office\Office15

But I have seen

C:\Program Files\Microsoft Office\Office15\root


So a typical shortcut to an application will look like:

"C:\Program Files (x86)\Microsoft Office\Office15\MSACCESS.EXE"
"c:\MyInvoiceSystem\Invoice.accde" /runtime

The above shortcut of course would be on one line. As far as I can tell, the location is the same for win7, win8 and win 10

Unfortunately, you are highlighting limitations of the design of Excel.

First, when you click on a cell and the Advanced option "Allow editing directly in cells" is set, the cell value appears as it does in the Formula Bar. And the Formula Bar has its own formatting rules.

Second, the Formula Bar shows time only to the second. It does not show milliseconds.

So you are correct: we are unable to edit (modify) milliseconds other than by retyping the entire date and time value.

It is what it is.
