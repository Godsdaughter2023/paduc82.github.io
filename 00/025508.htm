ac&sp say:

It took me quite awhile to figure out what was wrong but I had used an alias for my email address at the outset.

This is the process I used to sync folders with my ISP mail server AND also send email from my Laptop.

With Outlook Mail open from OFFICE 365 go to the settings wheel and click "Manage Accounts"  

Go to “+ Add Account” 
Go to “Other Account   POP, IMAP”
Type in your ISP email address* 
[* - This is important!   Use your original ISP email address.  If you use an alias mail will not be sent]
Type in the name to be shown in your messages
Type in your password
Click “Sign in”

(At this point your ISP's Outlook account should appear under Accounts automatically)

 

If not, go back to "Manage accounts"

Select the "ISP" named account or "whatever" account that was created.

Click on "Change mailbox sync settings - Options for syncing your content"

Check that Email Sync options are ON and scroll down.

Find "Advance Mailbox settings" and click on " Incoming and outgoing mail server info"

Here are the server names I used. They should be similar. The ISP should provide this info. 

For incoming server I used:  mail.isp.com:993:1

For outgoing Server I used:  smtp.isp.com:465:1     also :587:1 seemed to work.

(The first three numbers represent the port, and I am not sure what the fourth number is for)

Click on "Outgoing server requires authentication"

Click on "Use the same user name and password for sending email"

Click on the "Require SSL for incoming email”

Click on the "Require SSL for outgoing email”

Click "Done" At this point you can put in your ISP email password again

Click "Save"  

Good Luck.
