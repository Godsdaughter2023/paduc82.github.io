flyingfysh say:

I found the solution. First, I create a Windows install USB drive by using another computer. 

The next step is to get to the UEFI menu. You do that as follows: Force a reboot by pressing the power button and also volume up. When you see the UEFI menu, go to the section on boot options. If you swipe left on USB boot, the computer will reboot from the USB drive - and take you right back to the UEFI menu. That's not what you want..

Go back to the boot options section, and turn off all except boot from USB. Now hit exit, and the computer will reboot - and take you right back to UEFI.

Here's what you should have done: When you get to the UEFI menu the first time, first go to the boot options section, and turn off all options except USB boot. Next, press volume down. If you don't do this, you will be coming back to the UEFI menu again. If you press volume down and hit exit, the computer will this time boot from the USB device, and you can get control of your machine back.

After this is all over, go back to the UEFI menu, boot options, and turn on all the options.
