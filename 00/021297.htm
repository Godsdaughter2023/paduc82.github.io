harry stevens say:

Hi Sandrineeee,

Are you told that the login was successful on that particular address?

If you have Two-step Verification enabled, the attempts won't have a good chance, since even if the hacker was able to guess your password, they still cannot sign in as they'll be prompted for a security code which only you can get. There's not much else that you need to do at this point as the security feature is still protecting your account.

Anyone can attempt to login an account as long as they know the email address. Since the hacker knows yours, you can just change your email address so that the hacker won't be able to login again. But that would mean that when others send you an email, you won't get it as you've already renamed your account.

So instead of changing your address, you can add in another email address and use that as a login. Microsoft allows you to have 'aliases', meaning you can have more than one email address as your login, but still uses a single account. This also works for emails - you can have your contacts send you an email through different email addresses, but still goes to a single inbox.

What this means is that when you have another login, you can disable your old one so that the hacker can't login anymore. They'll be told that your account does not exist, even though it does, as you just disabled the login for it. You won't lose any contacts and emails, you won't need to tell them that you're renaming your address, everything will be the same except for one thing - you login using a different email address.

To do this:

1. Login your account at "https://account.live.com"

2. Click "Your Info" on top of the page.

3. Then, click on "Manage your sign-in email or phone number"

4. You may be asked for a code for further verification, please provide one to proceed.

5. Once you're at the aliases page, click on "Add email alias/Add phone number" (Yes, you can sign in using your phone number)

6. Type in the new email address or phone number that you want as the login for your account.

7. Once you have it added, click on "Make Primary" next to it. This is necessary.

8. The old address won't be the Primary Alias anymore. Click on "Change sign-in preferences" 

9. You will see that the new email/phone that you added cannot be unchecked, as it's your Primary Alias. You can uncheck the old email address that you have then click on "Save".

And there you have it, you disabled the login for your old address. You will now be using the new one as your login.
