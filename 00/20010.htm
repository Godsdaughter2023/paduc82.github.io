How do I automatically insert equation numbers to the right

Most reports or textbooks with with equations have the equation numbers inserted to the right of the equation.  However, Word will only allow me to insert the equation number to the left or below the equation.  How do I have the equation number automatically inerted to the right?
jay freedman say:
Automatic isn't going to happen. Sort of semi-auto, ok.

First the setup: In any document (or a new blank one), insert a 2-column by 1-row table. Press Ctrl+Alt+U to remove its borders. Drag the column edge from the middle of the table over to the right, making it just a bit wider than needed for equation numbers.

With the cursor in the left cell, press Alt+= to insert an equation container, but leave it empty (with the placeholder text showing).

Move the cursor to the right cell. Press Ctrl+F9 to create a field, and inside the field type the code

     SEQ eq

Press F9 to update the field (it will show the number 1). If you want parentheses or brackets around the number, type them as plain text. There are ways to get a chapter or section number, but that's more complicated than I want to explain here.

Now click in the left margin next to the table to select the whole thing. Press Alt+F3 to open the Building Block dialog. Enter a name for this structure -- it should be at least 4 characters long, starting with a letter, and the first 4 characters should be unique among the names of building blocks. Leave the Gallery box set to "AutoText". You can accept the category "General" or create your own category (hint: ones that start with an underscore sort to the top of the list if you display an AutoText gallery on the ribbon or Quick Access Toolbar). Leave the "Save in" set to Normal.dotm (which will get you an auto-complete tooltip when you type the first 4 characters of the entry's name). Click OK.

To use this, start to type the entry's name. When the tooltip pops up, press Enter (if it doesn't pop up, press F3 to get it going). A copy of the table/equation/number will appear in the document. The number should update automatically to the next one in sequence (that's what the SEQ field is about). Then you can click in the equation container and enter the equation.

The next time you close Word after creating the AutoText entry, if you're asked whether to save changes to Normal.dotm, be sure to say yes; otherwise the entry will be discarded.
