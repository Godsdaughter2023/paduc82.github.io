In cell B7 I want to flag if the current year in cell B5 is a Leap Year using the formula

=IF(($B$10/4)=INT($B$10/4),"Leap Year","")

Is there a more elegant, efficient and reliable method to achieve this task?

Thanks for any input.

 

lars-Åke aspelin say:

For which range of years should your formula work?

Not every fourth year is a leap year so your formula will not work for years 2100, 2200, 2300, 2500, 2600, 2700, 2900 and so on which are NOT leap years.  Years 2000, 2400, and so on are leap years so for these years your formula will work.

If you only have dates after 1900, this is a formula that you can try:

=MONTH(DATE($B$10,2,29))=2

This formula compares the month of February 29th for the year in question with 2. If the month is 2 (=February) then we have a leap year.
For years that are not a leap year the month of February 29th will be 3 (March).

Hope this helps / Lars-Åke
