You can solve this for one numerical solution using Solver.  Usually, Solver does not work well with functions like ABS, but in this case it seems to work OK.

In A2:A3 enter the 2 coefficients: 5 and the 2

Designate B2:B3 as the decision variables.

Then, in B4 enter the formula =SUMPRODUCT(A2:A3,B2:B3)

In B5 enter the target, i.e., 52.

In B6 enter the formula =ABS(B4-B5)

In A5 enter the formula =ABS(B2-B3)

In A6 enter the deviation between x and y that you want to tolerate.  I tested with 2 and 1.

Now, create the Solver model:

=MIN($B$6)
=COUNT($B$2:$B$3)
=$A$5<=Sheet1!$A$6
=$B$2:$B$3=INT($B$2:$B$3)
={100,100,0.000001,0.05,FALSE,FALSE,FALSE,1,1,1,0.0001,TRUE}

 

If you allow a difference of 2 (B6 contains 2) you will get a exact match at 52.

If you allow a difference of only 1 you will get a difference of 1, i.e., get a value of 51.

 

If you have multiple such models to solve, put everything above so that it is in a single row.  Then, you can solve for model for each store row by row.

jamesyan_0708 say:

Hi Wouter,

(The following steps only applies to Office 365 Home plan. )

You can refer to the following post (from Gabriel Bratton) in this thread, http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/office/forum/office_2016-outlook/rolling-back-to-2013-from-2016/edda2c54-de95-446f-a306-59c688f15899?page=6&auth=1.

 

Microsoft has added Office 2013 Subscription back to the My Account page. You should now be able to revert back to Office 2013.

 

1. Browse to http://office.microsoft.com

2. Sign in

3. Click Install

4. Click Language and install options

5. Click Additional install options

6. Under Version choose Office 2013 and selected Bitness

 

Soon Office will start advertising Office 2016 upgrade via a Business Bar notification. If after reverting you do not want to see the Business Bar notification set the registry change in this KB article, https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/kb/3097292 . There is a Fix-It in the article. Click Download to have the setting automatically applied for you.

 

Meanwhile, since our forum mainly focuses on Office 365 for Business online services, for Office 365 for Home users, I would suggest you post your question in our Office 365 for Home forum for dedicated support:

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/office/forum/office_365hp?sort=lastreplydate&dir=desc&tab=threads&status=all&mod=&modAge=&advFil=&postedAfter=&postedBefore=&threadType=all&tm=1445350316562
