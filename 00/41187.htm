markinkent say:

In which case you might struggle.  Depends on the fine details of what was done.

The original Windows 8 licence (if OEM) was likely tied to your old motherboard.  Hence when it was updated to 10 the licence carried on.

If a new motherboard was fitted, then the old OEM licence became null and void.  If they installed a fresh OEM windows 10 licence then that is the licence you now have.  This licence may be windows 10 only in which case your stuck for going back to 8.

Lots of "if's" in this so I would suggest checking back with the service centre that did the repair, to check what they did, they should be able to advise on the licence front that they installed.

Also see Ken Blake's reply in this thread, looks like Windows 10 might be a possibility so you might find that's an easier fix!
