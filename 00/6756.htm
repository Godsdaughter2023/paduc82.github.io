There are several places to look for settings that might cause this.

Check the font color for the text that prints in faint grey. Make sure it's set to either Black or Automatic.

Click the Office button (the round thing in the upper left corner), click Word Options, and click Advanced on the left side of the dialog. Scroll down to the Print section. Make sure the box for "Use draft quality" is not checked. Close the dialog.

Then press Ctrl+P to open the Print dialog, and click the Properties button next to the printer's name. The dialog that appears is supplied by your printer's driver software, so it's different for each printer manufacturer and sometimes for different models, and I can't tell you exactly what to look for. But you should find a setting for "print quality" or something like that. Make sure it isn't set for "draft" or "ink saver" or something that reduces the amount of ink the printer uses.

Have you done any test printouts to your printer after selecting transfer material in the printer properties dialog box?  Some inkjets automatically do the mirroring for you when you do that, no need to do anything more.

If your printer won't do the job for you, here's another trick:

Rightclick the text box, choose COPY

joeu2004 say: Jhall wrote:

The formula below will not calculate?
=IFERROR(IF(ISBLANK('Name List'!$B3),"",(COUNT(IF(('QA Data'!$C$3:$C$5001=$C5),
IF('QA Data'!$K$3:$K$5001=F3,)),""))),"")}


What do you mean by "will not calculate"?  I suspect you mean:  it returns the wrong value, perhaps even the null string.

One possible problem (wild-a.s.s guess):  should F3 be $F3, F$3 or $F$3, just as you wrote $C5 instead of C5?

Another problem....  [ERRATA] My mistake.

Do yourself a favor and rewrite this as the following normal formula (just press Enter, not ctrl+shift+Enter):

 =IF('Name List'!$B3="","",
IFERROR(COUNTIFS('QA Data'!$C$3:$C$5001,$C5,'QA Data'!$K$3:$K$5001,F3),""))

I suspect the IFERROR is not necessary.

Note that I replace ISBLANK with 'Name List'!$B3="".  ISBLANK is true only if the cell is truly empty (no constant and no formula).  B3="" also returns true if the cell contains the null string, like the formula above might return.  That is what most uses mean.
