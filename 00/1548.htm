 ~/Documents/Microsoft User Data/Office 2011 Identities/your identity

3) When you open Outlook (Be sure it's fully updated 14.2.4 (see **) hold down the Option key and the Database Utility will open. Select to Rebuild.

File > Export (select the default action to export as .olm file.)

Not all Identities will rebuild when restored. Let us know if you need further help.

hgroover say:

My 6555 has been getting worse over the last few months. I agree that the battery compartment design is poor. It also appears that they opted for a knife-edge battery connector rather than the usual push-contact connector. The bottom edge of the battery latches under the bottom of the compartment ("top" and "bottom" relative to the orientation of the dial pad) and this is supposed to push the v-shaped socket terminals on the battery down over the three knife-edge connectors, but what appears to be happening is that the tension on the sides of the v-shaped socket terminals on the battery has become weak - perhaps the specification for the battery did not include a sufficiently heavy gauge of metal to keep the grip on the side of the knife-edge connectors. All that's left to assert contact is the downward pressure from the thin metal case on the edge of the knife edge with little or no contact on the sides of the knife edge connectors.

 

I have spontaneous rebooting when the phone is fully charged and just lying on a table, but it also occurs when I use the middle navigation button (which appears to produce a slight).

 

I think this could be solved with an improved battery design. I've tried following the suggestions in this thread to see if it solves the problem. I put a folded-over piece of card stock over the sim compartment (although I don't think the sim is loose - that would cause a loss of connection, not necessarily a reboot) then put another folded piece of card stock between the bottom edge of the battery and the metal case (where the battery pushes down over the knife-edge connectors). This makes a very tight fit with the metal case.

 

If this holds up I'll be happy, but I'm surprised that Nokia put an advanced li-po battery in there with such poorly designed connectors. The knife edge connectors in the phone are fairly robust but the connectors in the battery itself seem to have little or no spring left in them - taking the battery out and examining the connectors, they are fairly wide open. They should remain closed or nearly closed, and would be forced open only by insertion of the knife-edge connectors.

brook zhang msft say:

Hi BG,

 

Sorry for the delay.

 

Is the user still in the process of stage migration?

If so, there’s no action we need to do. The stage migration will migrate the mailbox to online.

If not, do you have directory synchronization enabled (Azure AD Connect) and manually assigned an online license to the user so that there’s an online mailbox? In this scenario, you can use Outlook desktop to export all mailbox data to a .PST file and then used Outlook desktop to import the data to the online mailbox.

 

In stage migration, TargetAddresses property will be set on the on-premise mailbox to forward emails sent to on-premise to online mailbox. So there’s no need to manually sync the delta data.

You can refer to the How the staged migration process works part of this article: https://technet.microsoft.com/en-SG/library/jj898486%28v=exchg.150%29.aspx?f=255&MSPPError=-2147217396.

 

You need to stop the current migration process and then delete these mailboxes. There’s an article guiding you to delete mailboxes in Office 365: https://technet.microsoft.com/en-SG/library/dn186233(v=exchg.150).aspx.
