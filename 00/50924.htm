Problem with poor audio quality of music playback after applying the - Microsoft Community

alpho say:

Problem with poor audio quality of music playback after applying the Windows 8.1 Pro upgrade

It seems that the W8.1 Pro (and possibly W8.1) upgrade has caused issue with Windows Media Player, Adobe Audtion CC and iTunes.
The quality of the sound is bad (drop out, buzziness, distant, distorted), when playing back MP3s and WAVs. It may also be bad for other formats, but I have not tried them.

For comparison, I also played the same music through Magix Samplitude Studio 2014 (both 32bit & 64bit) and the sound quality was good, as expected. Magix uses a separate MP3 & WAV codec instance and MP3 licence.

I suspect that Windows Media Player, Adobe Audition and iTunes all use the Windows 8.1 drivers and system software.

Hopefully Microsoft will pick up on this issue directly, as I am posting this as a question, and fix the drivers, otherwise there needs to be an easier method to provide problem feedback, than currently employed by Microsoft.

alpho say: I have made some progress on this issue.

When the Windows 8.1 Pro upgrade was installed, the installation process overwrote the existing audio drivers with older, and consequentially not totally compatible, drivers (dated 2009)

In my case I downloaded and installed the correct set of drivers (dated Dec 2012) for the Creative X-Fi sound card in my PC, and the normal audio quality was restored. For those having similar problems, you may find that re-installing the appropriate drivers for your sound card may restore your sound quality.

As for Microsoft, they ought to ensure that any future service pack does correctly identify that the existing drivers are newer / better than the older drivers they use in the upgrade, so as to avoid this problem in the first place.
