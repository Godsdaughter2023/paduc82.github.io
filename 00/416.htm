    • The quoted text above highlights the areas I would have questions about. I'm guessing you used a page break to insert the two blank pages. FWIW, the Insert tab is not necessary for this: you can insert a page break with Ctrl+Enter. But you don't actually need two blank pages. All you really need is a single empty paragraph followed by the section break (Next Page or Odd Page) between your intended front matter and your main text. I would guess that these hard page breaks are part of the source of your problem.

     

    "Position the cursor in about the middle of this page" suggests that you're using Click and Type; otherwise, you would not be able to type anywhere but at the top of the page. If you display nonprinting characters (formatting marks), you'll see that Word has accomplished this by inserting a lot of empty paragraphs to get to where you clicked. Those will not go away on their own.

     

    Adding a cover page adds additional complexity. Two articles that may help you sort out your problems are these:

     

    http://WordFAQs.mvps.org/BlankPage.htm

    http://www.word.mvps.org/FAQs/Formatting/front_matter_2007.htm
