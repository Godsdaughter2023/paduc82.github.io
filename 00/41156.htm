ping -f -l 1364 www.google.com

smiling cat-fab725ba-e75f-400c-9250-ecd9955c9c75 say:

Yeah it's not that simple. The mic 3.5mm connector on the headset only has one channel while the 2.5mm jack on the controller is two channels. When you plug the mic connector into the converter it shorts the voice and speaker channels together so the controller will not know what to do. It will try sending a speaker signal out only to have it shorted into the mic and then grounded so nothing gets through.

-

You can find adapter designed to plug into the 360 controller and support 3.5mm headset. Look on amazon for 360 controller PC headset adapter or similar and you should find it. It separates the two channels into two 3.5mm inputs one for speaker out and one for mic in. I would save your money and buy a headset designed for the 360 that is meant to integrate Live chat.

wunders say: Your problem is that your System Registry file is corrupt.

Picking up where Halima S left off, since you can't boot from your CD, you need to create another boot CD so that you can copy the backed-up copies of your System Registry files from the System Restore area of your disk and place them in the proper place.  My preferred way of doing this is to create a free "Live Linux" boot disk such as Knoppix or Ubuntu.  Linux disks have the advantage that the "super protection" placed on the "System Volume Information" folder by Windows is not an issue for Linux disks.  It also gets you past a rather risky Part 1 of KB307545.  You create the boot disk by downloading a .iso file and use any computers "Burn Image" feature of its CD-burning program to create a bootable disk from the .iso file.  If you use Ubuntu, take care not to install it but follow the "Try Me" instructions instead.

Once you create and boot from this CD/DVD, you can pick up KB307545 at Part 2, Step 6.  Follow the manual instructions in Part 2 and Part 3 of KB307545 (using right-click Copy, Paste, and Delete instead of the "del" and "copy" commands in step 3).  After Part 3, shut down your system, remove the CD, and boot normally to finish off with Part 4.

Knoppix:  < http://www.knopper.net/knoppix/index-en.html >
Ubuntu    < http://www.ubuntu.com/download/ubuntu/download >

If all else fails, the Live Linux boot disk can be used to backup your personal files on an external USB drive or over the network before finding some way to reinstall Windows.
