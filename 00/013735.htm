Animating SmartArt graphic elements separately - Microsoft Community

cathy4701 say:

Animating SmartArt graphic elements separately

Original Title: "SMartArt Graphic"
Is it possible to use SmartGraphics in a way that each element comes to the forefront and the others fade in the background? I need for the copy in each box to enlarge when I discuss it and then fade back to normal size when I move to the next point. Is this possible?

jim gordon mvp say:
Yes, you can, but it is a bit tricky to do, and you'll need to do some extra work to make it just right.

So let's try an example.

1. On a blank slide, click the SmartArt tab of the toolbar.
2. In the Choose a SmartArt graphic, choose Process > Basic Process
3. Change the text for each of the 3 processes to a, b, and c just to make it easier to see what's going on while you work on this.
4. While the SmartArt is selected, click the Animations tab of the Ribbon
5. In the Animation > Emphasis Effects group choose Grow/Shrink
6. In the Animation Options group on the Ribbon, click the Reorder button
7. For the Diagram that is selected by default, click the SmartArt Graphic Animation triangle near the bottom of the Reorder dialog to expose the additional options
8. For the Group Graphic pop-up item switch it to One-by-One
9. On the Preview tab of the Ribbon, click the Play button

You will see each of the 3 text boxes of the SmartArt object grow.

So now you have to add another animation to shrink them.
10. Select the SmartArt, and then In the Animation > Emphasis Effects group choose Grow/Shrink (yes, add the same animation a second time)
11. In the Custom Animation dialog, with the second Grow/Shrink animation, click the triangle for 12. Change the Property setting to Smaller (50%)
13. On the Preview tab of the Ribbon, click the Play button

Now the SmartArt boxes go back to their original sizes.

We're getting there, but there's more to do.

14. In the Animation Order list at the top of the Custom Animation Dialog, click the triangles next to the two custom animations we put in.

Now you need to carefully keep track of which animation you want to occur in which order, and what Timing option you want to have trigger the animation (On click, with previous, or after previous). You change the order by selecting one of the animations and clicking the up or down arrows at the bottom of the Animation Order list.If you're careful, you can get it so that the grow animation happens, then the shrink animation happens for each text box in the right order.

a. user say:

Hi Amit,

Check your carrier and location

Whether you can use VoLTE depends on a few things:

If network availability remain strong, Users with VoLTE supported mobiles can continue true HD Voice call over 4G network. In case of Jio, VoLTE will remain high -priority for voice service.

If network remain weak, Users may face call disconnection, 2G/3G Switching or WiFi-calling (Using VoWiFi) would be other alternatives.

The carrier, carrier account, location, and phone model of the person you're speaking with on the call.

Some carriers support VoLTE only in certain cities or area codes. Contact your carrier to ask about your area and account. 

Turn on VoLTE

Some carriers turn on this feature by default. To find out if VoLTE is on, go to Settings > Cellular > Enable LTE. If Voice & Data is off, tap it. This turns on VoLTE.

If the issue still persists; Please contact your carrier for help. Thank you.
