charles kenyon say:
There is a huge difference between pasting line or page breaks and pasting section breaks. Among other things, in Word, line and page breaks are contained within paragraphs. On the other hand, paragraphs are contained within sections. Section formatting controls headers/footers, margins and columns. Pagination can be changed with a section break.

You can use paste special and paste only text with no formatting. This will not paste the section breaks and headers/footers. You could also do the paste section-by-section avoiding pasting the section breaks. That would preserve formatting not contained in the section break.

Otherwise, you can go into the document after you paste and delete the section breaks.

Sections / Headers and Footers in Microsoft Word 2007-2013

fetaa say:

Hi REJANAMIT,
 
I need to explain that:
 
The Limited Access permission level is unusual. It enables a user or group to browse to a site page or library to access a specific content item. Typically, the user was given access to a single item in a list or library, but does not have permission to open or edit any other items in the library. The Limited Access permission level includes all the permissions that the user must have to access the required item.
 
You cannot assign Limited Access permission level directly to a user or group. Instead, you assign appropriate permission to the single item, and then SharePoint automatically assigns Limited Access to other required locations.
 
In this situation, if the site admin gives the user a specific permission(e.g. Full Control, Edit, Contribute, Read, View Only) for other required locations, it would override the Limited Access permission.
 
Moreover, if the site owners has limited access permission for the site and has no permission for a library , you may let the global admin grant the owners Full Control permissions for the library. Then the owners can manage the files of the library. To do that:
Open the library>Under LIBRARY button, click Library Settings> Under Permissions and Management, click Permissions for this document library>Click Grant Permissions, input the users' email addresses and select Full Control permission, and then click Share.
 
If anything is misunderstood, you may share the site owners' permissions for the site and the library with us for further investigation.
 
1.To check the owner's permissions for the site: Go to the site, and click the gear on the upper right corner>Site settings>Under Users and Permissions, click Site permissions>Under PERMISSIONS, click Check Permissions> Input the user's name and click Check Now.
2.To check the owner's permissions for the library: Open the library>Under LIBRARY button, click Library Settings> Under Permissions and Management, click Permissions for this document library>Under PERMISSIONS, click Check Permissions> Input the user's name and click Check Now.
