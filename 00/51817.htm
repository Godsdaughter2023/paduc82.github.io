Surface Pro 4 in Login Loop - Microsoft Community

frankenfritz25 say:

Surface Pro 4 in Login Loop

I haven't actually found any other information on this topic while trying to search for a quick solution. So I'd like to post my own finding and solution to my own issue, because I'm sure many others are experiencing it. 

We have just started getting some Surface Pro 4's in for employees who need to work outside the office. So far we only have a handful out there, but they all started to have one thing in common, including my own, a login loop after an update. 

Once you type in your credentials it got stuck in a login loop. It would say welcome, the ring would spin for a few seconds followed by a black screen and the login screen again. This happened for all users, even the local admin of the Surface. The update that was causing this was the Cumulative Update for windows 10 version 1511 for x64-based systems KB3135173. It would be nice for Microsoft to look into this issue as I've experienced it on all the Surface Pro 4's we have here. Each one is not exactly the same as far as installed software, so I can't see that being an issue. 

To resolve this issue, for those who are searching for the answer, you need to boot into safe mode to fix it. To do this do the following:

1. While at your login screen (as thats where you probably are stuck) hold the shift key and then hit restart. 

2. Once you get to "Choose an Option" hit Troubleshoot

3. Hit Advanced options

4. Hit Startup Settings

5. Hit Restart

6. You will get to the Startup Settings Screen. Hit 4 or 5. Doesn't really matter which one. (in my scenario anyway)

7. Once your computer starts into safe mode, login. It should work. Mine had no issues when logging into safe mode.

8. Search for control panel and open it up. Go to Programs and Features and uninstall a program.

9. At the top left you should see view installed updates, click that.

10. You will see a list of updates. If your computer just started doing this after an update, check the 'installed on' date for the listed updates and uninstall the ones that were just installed. In the case of KB3135173, uninstall that update. 

Once I uninstalled that, all of our issues went away. 

A little of my background, I am the network and system admin for the company I work for. It would be nice for Microsoft to figure out the issues with this update as I'm sure we aren't the only ones to experience this issue. Hopefully this helps anyone else out that may experience the same issue with this update or the same issue with a different update in the future.

bill smithers say:

It does not mean that Restore Points that you created a year ago will still be there.

It all depends on the amount of space allocated for System Restore.

New restore points are being created automatically/regularly and as that happens older ones are deleted to fit that space allocation.

If you find that you need earlier restore points as they are no longer there you will have to alter that space allocation to allow that in the future.

You can't alter the restore points that are available presently. What's there is there, what's gone are gone.

But remember that you use too much hard drive space if you wish to keep restore points back to the year "dot" for your machine and which you would have to keep increasing to accommodate them and it is not practical to do that as you would lose installed programs/app, Windows updates, computer settings, etc. when you do a SR onwards from that earlier restore point.
