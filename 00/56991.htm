malkeleah say: There is no point in putting applications on anything except the system drive. If you have to reinstall Windows, you will need to reinstall the applications because there will be tons of references to them in the registry - on the System drive. Your scenario simply will not work.

It does make sense to put data on a separate hard drive or partition.

It makes the most sense to use an imaging program that will also do incremental backups. Store the images/backups on an external hard drive. I like Acronis True Image for this purpose but there are other programs available. Seagate has also just come out with an all-in-one external drive/backup solution called Replica. They have licensed the technology from Acronis. I haven't purchased one of these drives to test (no funds for that right now) but they look good for people who don't want to buy an external drive and an imaging program separately and do the setup themselves.

I totally agree with separating the data frrom the applications and os - this makes it easier to recover from a crash (you can do a clean install and not lose your data) and also easier to backup (if you don't use a full image and just back up the data).  You could also separate the programs, though I don't see the point since they are tied closely with the OS and if you need to do a clean install you need to re-install the programs as well - but it may make things work a little faster by spreading out the workload to several drives so there is some value in this (though it isn't very much but it is there).  There's no way I know to separate the registry - that's just science fiction at this point in time (and I'm not sure what you'd gain by doing so even if it were possible). It seems easier to have it all in one place for searching and resolving problems.

You're a good dreamer. You should consider becoming a designer for Microsoft (they could use someone who thinks outside of the box). But think completely through your ideas. It would also make troubleshooting a major problem because everyone would do things differently and things would be in different places and it would be a nightmare trying to find what you need to help someone in trouble.

Keep thinking and coming up with unique ideas. It's how we make progress and you seem like someone who could contribute much to future designs (if you can get your ideas into the hands of people who make the decisions) - not just ordinary people like us on this forum.

Step 1: Uninstall the Printer's Drivers

a. Press “Windows Logo” + “X” keys on the keyboard.

Update Driver Software from the Manufacturer’s website:
