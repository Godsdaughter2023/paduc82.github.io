But is it possible to do anything so that the converse is also prevented, i.e. not having a single row at the start of a page, before a new heading begins?

stefan blom say:

To prevent single text lines at the top and bottom of pages, select "Widow/orphan control." If you want all lines of a paragraph to always be on the same page, use "Keep lines together." Both of these options are in the Paragraph dialog box.

"Keep with next," on the other hand, ensures that a paragraph is always on the same page as the following paragraph.

Note that a paragraph in Word is created each time you press Enter. You can click the ¶ button on the Home tab to see paragraph marks (¶) as well as other nonprinting marks.
