cody c. say: Hi affricat,

Here's an excerpt from the Add audio or sound to your presentation Help file:

PowerPoint can play a song across the number of slides that you specify and then play a new song starting on another slide.

  1. On the slide that you want the first song to start — slide 1, for example — insert the first audio file.

  2. Make sure that the audio icon on the slide is selected, and then on the Format Audio tab, under Audio Options, on the Start pop-up menu, click Play Across Slides.

  3. With the audio icon still selected, click the Animations tab, and then, under Animation Options, click the Reorder button.

  4. In the Custom Animation dialog box, select the song, and then toward the bottom of the Custom Animation dialog box, open the Media Options panel.

  5. In the After box, change the number from 999 to the number of slides you want the song to play.

    For example, if you want the song to start on slide 1 and play through slide 4 and then start a new song on slide 5, you would enter 4 in the box because you want the music to play for 4 slides and then stop.

  6. Repeat steps 1 through 5 to add other songs.

    For example, if you add a new song to slide 5, and want it to play across slides 5, 6, and 7 and then stop, you would set the Play Across Slides option, open the Custom Animation dialog box, and under Media Options, change the After box to 3 because you want the music to play for 3 slides.

Hi,

Saving all of your emails from your old email account is a great idea, for you to still access some important emails come from it. Here are your options for saving those emails:

Option 1: Transferring the emails to a different folder.

  1. Open Outlook and go to the Sent folder of your old email account.
  2. Press Ctrl + A simultaneously to select all the emails, then using your mouse, drag them all to the other Sent folder of your active email account.

Option 2: Saving the emails (.pst file) on your computer.

  1. On the File menu, click Open & Export and select Import/Export.
  2. Click Export to a file and click Next.
  3. Select Outlook Data File (.pst) and click Next.
  4. Highlight the email folder or the old account that you want to save all emails from it.
  5. Check the box for Include subfolders and click Next.
  6. Click Browse to locate the place where you want to save this .pst file.
  7. Create the file name and click OK.
  8. Select Finish and click OK.
