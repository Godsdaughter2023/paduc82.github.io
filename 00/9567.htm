By setting an Inbox rules, you can automatically delete messages that contains a specific words or phrases, This is applicable on message that goes directly to your Inbox.

To stop receiving emails that contain inappropriate word or phrases that goes directly to your Junk email folder, you need to add the sender's email address to your Blocked senders list.

  1. Sign in to your Outlook.com account
  2. On the upper right corner of the page, click the Cog icon.

As Control Panel is not appearing at all, open a Run window (Windows Logo key+R), type regedit, press Enter and in the left pane navigate to HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Policies\Explorer.

 

If you are denied entry to regedit it’s a classic symptom of infection and you should run your ‘anti‘ programs.

 

If you receive the message 'Registry editing has been disabled by your administrator', you will have to remove the key that prevents it. Open a Run window (Windows Logo key+R) and type or copy and paste the following:

reg delete HKCU\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Policies\System /v DisableRegistryTools /f and click OK.

 

I suggest you create a System Restore point before making registry changes, just in case something goes wrong.

jerry he msft say:

Hi SNC2008,

Office 365 Business account is totally different from personal account.

There is no option to merge the OneNote accounts.

As a workaround,  you can export the notebooks from personal OneNote manually, and save them locally or OneDrive for Business online.

Please follow the steps bellow:

  1. Personal OneNote > File > Export > Notebook >OneNote Package >Export;
  2. Open the notebook package file, restore it to be a new file,
  3. Upload the folder to OneDrive for Business online, open the OneNote client with your business account> Open> Choose your OneDrive for Business > Open that file >open that notebook.
