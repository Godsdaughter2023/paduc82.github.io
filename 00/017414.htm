yves dhondt say:

Well it looks like your document got corrupted. The good news is that, as you saved it as an open xml document, it looks like you are going to be able to recover 99% of your work. The problem is, you do need to have some xml knowledge. docx files are nothing more than zip archives. If you open the file as a zip archive (e.g. by changing the extension of docx to zip) you will find a bunch of files in there. The one you will need to fix will be in the "word" subdirectory and is labeled "footer22.xml". The easiest fix is probably to reduce it to an empty footnote. Hi snow45558,

We sincerely apologize for not being able to answer your concern in a timely manner. We are experiencing high contact volume at this time which has caused our delayed response.

No, once you’ve created a new Outlook.com account there is no way to rename your Hotmail account to that name. If you delete the new account, it will take 365 calendar days for that account name to become available again, and at that point, anyone would be able to get the name.

Instead, you can Auto-forward your Hotmail account to your new Outlook account. You’ll have all of your mail in one place, and can reply to it using your new address.

To auto-forward your email:

1.       Log in to your Hotmail account.

2.       Click Options, then click More options….

3.       Under Managing your account, click Email forwarding. You might have to sign in.

4.       Select the radio button for Forward your mail to another email account.

5.       Enter the email address for your new Outlook account.

6.       If you want, check the box for Keep a copy of forwarded messages in your Hotmail inbox.

7.       Click Save.

Please be informed that free account users need to log into the account they choose to auto-forward at least once every 270 days to avoid having the system remove the mailbox for inactivity.
