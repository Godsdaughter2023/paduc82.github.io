But I thought one of the main purposes of a section break is to format different sections of the document how you want them? jay freedman say:

Like many other things in Word, some people want this to work the way it does now, and other people want it to work the opposite way. The developers studied the relative numbers of users who were likely to have each preference, and chose the one that's more common.

If each section started with Same as Previous turned off, presumably each header and footer would start out empty, right? Personally, I would not want to have to re-enter the header and footer every time I create a new section. It's far less disruptive to turn off Same as Previous in the few sections where I do want a different header or footer.

I could support the addition of an option to tell Word whether new sections should start with Same as Previous on or off. That would help in documents where each section should have a different header/footer. Depending on what that difference is, though, you may be able to do it with StyleRef fields (and then you may not need the section breaks at all).

You're welcome to post your suggestion to https://word.uservoice.com/. If your suggestion gets enough votes, Microsoft will consider accepting it for the future.
