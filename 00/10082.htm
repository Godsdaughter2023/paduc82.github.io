**********************************************************************************************************************************

$UserCredential = Get-Credential

phillip m jones say:
  1. Stop the download.
  2. Go to Public Library or some other Government building that allow the public to use their Internet. (Possibly school, Community College, University).
  3. Make sure it is an Ethernet Connection if possible.
  4. Try download from there.
  5. If you have a Buddy with a Cable modem ask then if you can hook up using their network. (You can have them enter the Username and Password of their network while your looking off.)

POP and DSL won't cut it. About the highest speed you can get out of a Perfect DSL connection is about 5Mb. Many community ISP deliver DSL over Copper (POTS Plain Old Telephone System). Many POTS providers are still using Copper connections all or partially installed back just after WWII. Office 2011 and 365 are are about 100+ GB or more. Say you connect speed is 1.5 MBS and the download is 150 GB. You do the math. (Even if it 5 MBS, still do the math)

The ideal connection is either FOIS (Fiber Optic Internet System) or Cable.  FOIS connection speed can be upwards of 100 MBS.  A Comcast Blast Service Connection is upwards of 35MBS.

And Unless your WIFI Modem or any is within feet of your computer That will have a serious effect on your download.  If your WIFI Router has Ethernet connection on back, connect with Ethernet cable instead. Even if you mac doesn't have Ethernet, get a Ethernet to Thunderbolt connector and connect that way.
