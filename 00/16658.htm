As this is Word 2007 can we assume legacy form field check boxes? In that case you will need a macro run on exit from the first check box to check the second one. You might find it worth running it on entry to the second checkbox also. the macro assumes checkbox names Check1 and Check2


With ActiveDocument
    If .FormFields("Check1").CheckBox.Value = True Then
        .FormFields("Check2").CheckBox.Value = True
    Else
        .FormFields("Check2").CheckBox.Value = False
    End If
End With

Your W8.1 license is a permanent license and will continue for many years until MS reaches a published end of service date well past 2020 at which time it will no longer have support just like XP reached its end of support date recently.

However, you now have 1 year to accept the free update to W10.  If you accept, W10 will consume your W8.1 license and you will then have a lifetime W10 license of your equipment.  When you accept and move over to W10, your W8.1 license is dead. 

After July 29 2016, you would have to buy W10 which leaves your W8.1 license intact.  If your W8.1 license is an OEM, then it cannot be moved to another PC. It follows that if you bought W10, then it is also married to that PC.   Under this senario since you would then have 2 valid licenses, you should then be able remove W10 and go back to W8.1.

Hi,

 

We're sorry for the inconvenience that this issue has caused you. To address your concern, Hotmail has a feature that prevents losing email messages if you accidentally set up POP3 to delete them after reading them on the server. Here’s how it works:

 

The first time that Hotmail receives a POP3 instruction to delete email messages after reading from an email app, Hotmail won't delete the messages. Instead, Hotmail moves the email messages that were successfully downloaded by the email app into a POP folder and out of the inbox. The POP folder is available in the Folders list.

 

Hotmail keeps the email messages in the POP folder until you sign in to your Hotmail account and confirm whether Hotmail should respect or ignore the POP3 delete command.

If you don’t want email messages to be permanently deleted from your inbox after you download them to your mail program, select the Leave a copy of messages on the server option.

 

Please verify if you are using an email client or mobile device to access your emails.
