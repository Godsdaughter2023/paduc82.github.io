Hi Lisa,

Part of the problem maybe due to PowerPoint 2007 behavior when copying a group of slides with hyperlinks.

In PPT 2003, when you copy a group of slides over to a new deck, PowerPoint would update the slideIDs for the link information of the associated slides to reflect the pasted slide values. carolyngaither say:

I don't know if this will help, but here's my setup and what I did to essentially move a notebook from an Office 365 for Business Account to my computer, and then to another cloud:

  • Open desktop version of OneNote

  • Open the old 365 site and open the notebook (book) there.
  • Click "Open in OneNote" at the top of the screen.

  • Log in to your new 365 account
  • Open OneNote and create a new Notebook with the same name as the original one but put a # at the end to identify it (book#).
  • Click "Open in OneNote" at the top of the screen.

In the desktop version click

  • View -> New window
  • Go to the Notebook on your old site (book)
  • Right-click the section from book you want to move.
  • Select Move or Copy
  • Select Book# (your new notebook on the new 365) and click Move.
  • Do the same for all the other sections of your notebook.

This worked for me. Since you have notebooks from both the old and new 365 open in the desktop program, when you Move/Copy, the screen will show notebooks from both locations.
