I want to use Word b/c some of my students take notes on their laptop and I would like my students to be able to enter notes on an electronic version of the text. The disadvantage is that when students enter text, the page formatting changes (so part of the text that was on page 2, for example may get moved to page 3 if I am adding text on page 1). 

Ideally, what I would like to do is leave *some* room on each page for the students to take notes, but limit that by fixing the text so it can't move to another page. Is there some way to fix text to a page (so it can't shift to another page if edits are made)? 

Thanks,

Matt

chitrahaas nalla msft say:

Hi Matthew,

 

Based on your description, I think inserting the Text Box into your pages of your Word document will help you to meet your requirement. Insert the Text Box at top your page at size you required, and the students can enter the content below the text box. When you enter the text inside the Text Box, the content in the document doesn’t move to the next page.

 

Open the Word document> Insert Tab> Text Box> Simple Text Box> Click inside the Text Box and drag the box as per your requirement.

 

Note: After arranging the Text Box as per your requirement, click on the border line of Text Box> Select Layout Options> Select the option Fix the position on the page.

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

Notifications will be received by whatever account is associated with the mail address to which the notification is sent.  If that's a Live account, then you should have three calendar appearing in the folder set for that account: "My Calendar", "Birthdays", and a Holidays calendar associated with the country defined for your Live account.  I see "US Holidays", for example.

However, there can be only one default calendar.  If you wish to save appointments fo a different calendar, open the appointment and click FIle>Move to Folder, choosing the alternate folder as the destination.
