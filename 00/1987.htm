charles kenyon say:

To put it bluntly: You don't (copy your 2007 normal template and use it in 2016 as the normal template).

However, there is much you can do.

AutoCorrect:

Use one of the AutoCorrect backup utilities.

  • AutoCorrect Utility Manager
  • or AutoCorrect2007.zip at The Macro Zone

Either will give you a Word document that contains all of your AutoCorrect entries, both from the .acl files and the normal template. You can then use the utility to restore them on the new computer.

Normal macros:

You could, short term, rename your old normal.dotm file and put it in the Word Startup Folder. That will give you access to the macros and any related keyboard shortcuts. That carries a fair amount of overhead, though. Long term, you would want to move the macros to a new template that could be used as a global.

For more, see Moving (Sharing) Customizations in Microsoft Word and Word 2007 & Later Key Data File Locations.

The Word 2007 normal.dotm would cripple your Word 2016 installation if you simply try to use it as the normal.dotm file for that version, or at least restrict it to compatibility mode and mean you can't use many Word 2016 features.

As for what you are seeing happen...

You are not replacing the real normal.dotm file being used by Word. Again, you do not want to, anyway. How to Find the Normal Template

You may want to use the Organizer to copy styles and macros directly from your old normal template (renamed) into the new one.
