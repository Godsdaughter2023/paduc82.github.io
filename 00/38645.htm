a. user say:

This page from HP's website shows that your printer is not supported on Windows 10. The most recent driver was written for Windows 7.

"Not supported" doesn't necessarily mean that the printer won't work - it means that HP hasn't tested your printer on Windows 10 and hasn't taken any steps to make it compatible with Windows 10. That means your printer may or may not work properly and HP has no solution to offer you, so you're basically on your own.

"User intervention required" typically means that you have to do something - push a button, change a dial, etc. - at the printer itself, rather than at the computer. Look at the printer's display (if it has one) or indicator lights to figure out what it is you need to do.

"User intervention required" can also mean that you're out of paper. Don't ask how I figured that out.

Couldn't Update System Reserved Partition

You get this error if the System Reserved partition in Disk Management is too small. The system reserve partition needs to be at least 450 to 500 MBs.

Press Windows key + R

Type: diskmgmt.msc

Hit Enter

Click the system drive to view the size.

Resolution:

  • Createa system repair disc
  • Download Ease us partition manager and resize it to 300 to 450 MBs.
  • Please ensure you create a system repair disc in advance before doing this.
