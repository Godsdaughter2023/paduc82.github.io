jim ob say:

theresa-- say: Jim,

You can create shortcuts to your favorite web pages in favorites and the favicons will be there. Or you can create a favorite on a links bar in Internet explorer and that will show the "logo". 

The problem you are facing is a common one as far as URL shortcuts are considered. Whenever you create a shortcut to a website, or add it to your IE Favorites, Internet Explorer downloads an icon file named 'favicon.ico' from the website. It is this icon file that contains the icon you see on your shortcuts.

Windows Vista does not support the customization of Internet Explorer shortcut icons on the desktop other than the steps provided in my previous post. These brand specific "logo" icons are called favicons. Favicons do not show up in Vista on the desktop shortcuts by default. If the favicon does exists for your shortcut it will be located in the Temporary Internet files. If you can find it there when you browse after step 2 of my previous post, you can use it. If it isnt there, using another icon, is your only choice.

Internet Explorer stores the favicon file in the Temporary Internet Files folder itself. This means that whenever you clear your Temporary internet files, or after a set number of days when IE clears the folder, the icon file is lost. As a result, since Windows is no longer able to find the custom websites icons, it reverts to the default Internet Explorer icon.

Suggestion for Skype Calls combined with Desktop Sharing - Microsoft Community

schrag4 say:

Suggestion for Skype Calls combined with Desktop Sharing

We use Skype at our office to make audio calls and share our desktops with others, internally.  One thing I've noticed over and over is that after hanging up with someone, they quite often will forget that they are sharing their desktop.  I would suggest that a setting be added that would allow automatically ending desktop sharing sessions between one-on-one (1:1) participants after they hang up with each other.  Obviously this is something that users could disable, but in my view, it should be turned on by default.  To make it more user-friendly, it could prompt the sharer with a 10 or 20 second count-down that they could then cancel if they wanted to continue sharing their desktop after the call has ended.  Also, notice I said this would only be between 1:1 participants, as you wouldn't want someone dropping from a group discussion to shut down any desktop sharing to the whole group.

Why do I think this is a good idea?  Well, besides bandwidth being wasted, there are also privacy concerns.  I won't go into details, but I've seen someone who was sharing with me start typing up a sensitive email shortly after our Skype call was over.  I can think of scenarios where you'd want to keep sharing your desktop after you hang up with them, but it seems to me that would be the exception, not the rule, and if you're doing that a lot, you could simply disable this new auto-stop-desktop-sharing-after-1:1-calls setting.

Thoughts?
