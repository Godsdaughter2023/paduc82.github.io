How do I "get rid of hard returns"? My professor says to - Microsoft Community

christine_17 say:

How do I "get rid of hard returns"? My professor says to get rid of all of the hard returns in my paper--I do not know what that is.

I am writing an action research proposal and my professor  says "get rid of hard returns in the paper
How do I do this?

Thank you:)
hansv mvp say:

A "hard return" is a paragraph break. You insert a hard return by pressing the Enter key.

In Word, a hard return is only needed to start a new paragraph of text. You don't need to press Enter at the end of a line to go to the next line: Word will automatically wrap the text to the next line when the text reaches the right margin.

You can view paragraph marks by clicking to highlight the Show/Hide ¶ button in the Paragraph group on the Home tab of the ribbon (the keyboard shortcut for this is Ctrl+Shift+8). Paragraph marks will be displayed as ¶.

You should delete all paragraph marks that only serve to go to the next line. You should keep the ones that start a new paragraph of text.

 

In the Replace dialog (Ctrl+H), you can search for paragraph marks by entering ^p in the Find what box.
