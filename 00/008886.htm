It can be done by adjusting the form's InsideHeight property. (CanGrow/CanShrink only apply when the form is printed).  But before you can do that, you need to adjust the Top property of all the controls below the one you are hiding to move them up in the form.  And then, of course, you would need to be able to reverse that when you want to unhide the text box

Here's some air code (untested) that might give you an idea:

Const StandardCtrlHeight As Long = .1667 * 1440
Dim ctl As Control

For Each ctl In Me.Controls
   If ctl.Top > Me.DetachedGarageCost.Top _
   Then ctl.Top = ctl.Top - StandardCtrlHeight
Next ctl
Me.InsideHeight = Me.InsideHeight - StandardCtrlHeight

Hi Nathan,

I understand that spam emails can be a nuisance in your inbox and you would like to block them.

There are several ways on how you can block spam emails in your mailbox. Here are some options that you can set up in your account:

  1. Junk mail filter - Selecting Exclusive option. This feature will let you receive emails from your contact list and safe senders list.
  2. Email rules - You can set up email rules to filter incoming emails to automatically delete them and not getting them in your Inbox.
  3. Blocked senders list - You can add email address/es or domain/s in this list to stop receiving emails from them. You also need to delete them from your contact list and safe senders list as well.
  4. Sweep features - Sweeping emails for deletion.

To set up a junk mail filter to Exclusive, please follow these steps:

  1. Sign in to your Microsoft account.
  2. Click the gear icon located on the upper right panel of your mailbox, just beside your username.
  3. Select Options.
  4. Under Preventing junk email, click on Filters and reporting option.
  5. Under Choose a junk email filter, select Exclusive.
  6. Click the Save button.

If you want to create an email rule to delete emails coming from an email address, kindly refer to these steps:

  1. Sign in to your Microsoft account.
  2. Click the gear icon located on the upper right panel of your mailbox, just beside your username.
  3. Select Manage Rules.
  4. Click the New button.
  5. Under Step 1: Which messages do you want this rule to apply to? On the first box with drop down list, select the Sender's address and in the second box with drop down list, select yes.
  6. On the third box, type sender's email address. You may select any option that you want to use at first, second and third box depending on your preference.
  7. Under Step 2: What action do you want to apply? Select delete these messages.
  8. Click the Save button.

You can also      

  1. Sign in to your Microsoft account.
  2. Click the gear icon located on the upper right panel of your mailbox, just beside your username.
  3. Select Options.
  4. Under “Preventing junk email”, click the Safe and blocked senders option.
  5. Click Blocked senders.
  6. In the Blocked email address or domain box, type the email address of the sender or the domain that you want to block, and then click Add to list.

Once you add an email address in your blocked senders list, you may also need to delete the account details from your contact list.
