When I try and reset explorer: before u can reset explorer settings you must first close all other open windows and programs. 


That is probably a good clue.   Just because you don't see an open window does not mean that there is not something running.   Use Task Manager's Processes tab and check  Show processes from all users.   You must have something running, either under your User Name or another one, which is non-standard and interfering enough to cause that symptom.   A related diagnostic is  System Information (aka msinfo32.exe) for its Running Tasks and Loaded Modules lists.  An advantage of using that tool is that its displays are capturable, e.g. via the Edit menu.   Another approach would be to try the same things in a safe mode boot or under a different account.   Then at least in the case of  msinfo32.exe  you could capture the lists of modules and compare them with the list you have for your problem case.

 

Good luck
