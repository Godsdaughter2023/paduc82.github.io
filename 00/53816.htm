daniel strommen [msft] say:

Hi John,

Microsoft has a commitment to users' privacy, and one part of that commitment is being transparent with what data we collect and how we use it. Toward this goal of transparency, we have recently started sharing all activity reports with children as well, as you have noticed. This is similar to the experience in Windows 7 and Windows 8, where kids could go to the Family Safety control panel to view their own restrictions and reports. We believe that family controls and monitoring are a tool for promoting relationships and conversations between parents and their kids, and as such belong in the open.

The only way to prevent the activity report from being shared with your child, is for you to manage their Microsoft account and not share the email password with them. This is a good option for very young children. You can find out more about "How can I set up a child account without giving them email access?" at aka.ms/famsafefaq.

Thanks,
