lemp say:
Although it's possible that your problem is related to 802.1x, that usually results in an error message indicating that you don't have the required certificate.  You said that you haven't seen any error messages.

What "network diagnostics" did you run?

What this sounds like is that you are running both a utility that came with your wireless adapter and Windows Wireless Zero Configuration (WZC).  That won't work; you need to pick one. 

See the discussion here --> http://www.ezlan.net/wzc.html

Most 3rd party wireless utilities have an option/preferences setting that turns them off in favor of WZC; or you can disable WZC.

What's the make and model of your wireless adapter?

Go to Network Connections (Start > Run > ncpa.cpl > OK), right click on the icon for your wireless adapter and select Properties. Then select the Wireless Networks tab.  If you want to use a 3rd party utility to control your wireless adapter, make sure that the box to "Use Windows to configure my wireless network settings" is NOT checked.
