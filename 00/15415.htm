cbarnhorst say:

At the higher end retailers are discounting to be competitive.  At the low end beware.  There is an EU case that opened OEM licenses to retailing (illegal in the US). 

Many of these licenses are actually restricted licenses for system builders and developers. 

A "system builder" is not someone who builds his own system but someone who builds a system for sale to a customer.  These are used by small businesses such as mom and pop computer stores.  Use of a system builder license on one's own computer is in violation of the terms of the system builder license because the system is not being sold to another party.  Since the system builder is responsible for supporting Windows and not Microsoft, using such a license on one's own computer means no support.

My advice is to purchase Windows 10 from the Microsoft Store ($120 for Home, $200 for Pro, $325 for Pro for Workstations) and enjoy with peace of mind.
