Grand total of calculated field doesn't add up - Microsoft Community

trjtr say:

Grand total of calculated field doesn't add up

Using this data:

 

                  100         10                   200         10
Vehicle Annual Cost Age
a                   100         10
b                   200         10

 

And creating a pivot table with a calculated field for life to date costs (= annual cost * age) the grand total for LTD cost shows $6,000 rather than $3,000.

 

                1,000                 2,000                 6,000
Vehicle Sum of Cost Sum of LTD Cost
a 100                 1,000
b 200                 2,000
Grand Total 300                 6,000

 

hansv mvp say:

That's the way calculated fields work. If you have subtotals and grand totals, a calculated field only works "correctly" if you add or subtract fields, and/or multiply with/divide by a constant factor.

For the subtotals and grand totals, the individual fields are summed first, and then the calculation is performed.

In your example, the grand total for LTD Cost is calculated as (100+200) * (10+10) = 300 * 20 = 6000, not as (100*10) + (200*10). There is no way you can change this.

So you should either omit the subtotals/grand totals, or perform the calculations outside of the pivot table.
