john korchok say:

Thanks for the additional detail. Since the advent of Office 2013, Microsoft has adopted a practice in some programs where the program will create a bitmap from a vector file (EMF, PDF or SVG) and display that. I first saw this happening in Microsoft Publisher 2013, a Windows-only program.

This rasterization only becomes evident after you close and re-open the file. I've seen this effect in PowerPoint for Windows when placing SVG files, but it seems to happen more often on a Mac. I've never figured out the exact conditions that trigger this rasterization, it only seems partially connected to the complexity of the vector file.

I have fewer issues with PowerPoint for Windows, so I tend to work in that, then transfer files to Mac versions.

The PDF transparency issue is a different problem that also often affects printing. That one started with the transition to the Mojave OS. I assume there are major changes to the printing routines in Mojave, because Office 2008 and 2011 users have also had printing problems under Mojave.

Neither problem has anything to do with image compression.

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
You don't indicate what "old office" you're comparing to... There have been at least 6 versions for the Mac as well as a comparable number for Windows :-) What I can tell you without doing any research is that the device in Excel 2011 is no different than it was in Office 2008 or 2004 :-)

Excel really doesn't use a 'box' such as the Browse Objects button in Word. It's just a pair of Up/Down scroll arrows. Whether they appear together at the bottom of the scroll bar or separately -- one above, one below -- depends on your choice in System Preferences - Appearance. Regardless of how they're positioned, though, a click scrolls the sheet 1 row at a time up/down, click & hold scrolls continuously. The illustration in step 2 of this Tutorial: Excel basics shows what the Scroll Bar area should look like.

If your Scroll Bar or the arrows are missing or do not work...

Since this sounds like a new installation of the software the first thing to confirm is that Office is fully updated [14.2.5] & that you've repaired disk permissions & restarted since installing/updating.

The next key question is whether the Vertical Scroll Bar is displayed. If not, go to Excel> Preferences> View then check the box to Show Vertical Scroll Bar. [Note that the description at the bottom of the Preferences dialog indicates that this is a workbook setting.]
