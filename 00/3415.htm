Hi Sahel,

Could you try the following steps?

1. Could you check the files and folders not synced don’t exceed the limitations of OneDrive for Business: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/kb/2933738?
2. Could you check if the OneDrive for Business client is up to date (16.0.6228.1007)? You can check via the Task Manager > Details > right-click GROOVE.EXE > Properties > Details? If it is not the latest version, could you try updating your Office via an Office application > File > Account > Office Updates?
3. If you have checked both #1 and #2 all good, you may consider rebuilding the OneDrive for Business cache. For OneDrive for Business 2013, you can use the fix-it tool in this article: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/kb/3038627. For OneDrive for Business 2016, follow Alice’s steps in this thread to manually clear the cache:

If the issue still exists, could you provide the following information with us?

1. Is there any sync issue under right-clicking the OneDrive for Business icon on the system tray > View sync problems?
2. How do you install the OneDrive for Business sync client? As a standalone app or as part of Office Suite?
3. Is the version of your OneDrive for Business sync client same as your Office applications?
4. How many items are there in the library you are trying to sync?

Eric Yang
