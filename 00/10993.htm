shashirekha say:

Follow the steps to create a word outline document with different headers and send it to PowerPoint:-

Open a new Word document and go to the "View" Click "Outline." The document will switch to outline view and a minus sign (-) will be at the top of the page.

Type your first header beside the minus sign. Press the "Enter" key. The cursor will move to the next line, where you will see another minus line. Press "Enter" after each heading, entering a new heading on each line. When you are done, go back to the end of the first heading.

Press "Enter" to go to the next line. Press the "Tab" key to "Level 2" text. Heading text will become the titles for the Power Point slides, while the "Level 2" text will be the bullet points. Continue adding separate entries for each bullet point on each slide.

Add the "Send to Power Point" command to the Quick Access Toolbar (QAT) in Word 2007. Click the drop-down arrow on the QAT and select "More Commands." Select "All Commands," click on "Send to Microsoft Office PowerPoint," click the "Add" button and click "OK." Once the button has been added to the QAT, click on it to send the outline to PowerPoint.
