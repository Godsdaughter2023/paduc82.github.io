john mcghie say:

Hi Angela:

This is quite a complex area of Word.  Let's just tick off the basics, just to cement in your mind what's happening.

A Word document has one Master section break that you can't see, and from 0 to many other section breaks that you can see.  The Master is beyond the last paragraph mark at the bottom of your document.

You do running headers and footers with a STYLEREF field.  A Styleref field links the text of the closest previous paragraph of the specified style into the header or footer.

It's conventional to use the built-in heading styles: Heading 1 for the Parts, Heading 2 for the Chapters, and Heading 3 for the Topics. You don't have to use the built-in styles but you will save yourself a life-time's worth of effort if you do.  Always customise the built-in styles to the formatting you want rather than create your own styles, because the built-in styles have secret sauce baked in that makes everything work.

Each Section has three headers: First, Left, and Right; and three footers, First, Left, and Right. If you turn on "Same As Previous" in the left header of the third section, what you will get is the LEFT header from the second section.

Let me say that another way, because it's the key to your difficulty: The "Same As Previous" button links in the text of the previous header of THAT KIND. Same as Previous in a Right header brings in the previous Right header.

If you change that Right header in any section, it is changed also in the previous section.

Now: for extra entertainment value: the Previous header may not be shown! This happens often where you use a one-page section to contain a side-ways page.  It still HAS three headers and three footers, but you will not SEE them because the only one that appears will be the one appropriate to that section (usually, the First header).

Now:  Here is the quickest way to put that document right. Make a copy of the document to work on: you will be using some unfamiliar techniques, so we need a copy you can go back to if a problem happens.

  1. REMOVE the Parts 2 and 3 Sections (and all the other section breaks down to the end of the document).  Leave only the Intro section break. We will first get the six headers and footers in the body of the document working correctly.  That sets up the Master section break, that we can then use throughout the document.  We may find that we do not need any other sections.
  2. Run through the document and ensure that the book title is Title style, the Part Title has Heading 1 style, the Chapter Titles all have Heading 2 Style, and the Topic paragraphs all have Heading 3 style.  This is the key to getting the StyleRefs to work properly, and they are the key to getting your headers working properly!  Don't think, just do it :-)
  3. Now click in Section 2 (The bit after the Intro, containing the rest of the document).  Go to Format>Document>Layout and set Different First Page and Different Odd and Even both to ON.  Set Section Start to Right Page. OK out.
  4. Go to View>Header and Footer and set Same As Previous to OFF for the First Page, Left, and Right headers.  We do not want the Intro headers in the body of the book.  And we do not want to overtype the Intro headers while working in the rest of the book.  Go to the three footers and turn off Same As Previous there too.
  5. Go to the First Page header and delete it.  Make sure no characters remain.
  6. Now: You want the Book Title on the Left, so go into the Left header and insert a StyleRef field set to "Title" style. http://support.microsoft.com/kb/141994
  7. Now go into the Right header and insert two StyleRef fields.  Set one to "Heading 1" (the Part Title) and the other to "Heading 2" (the Chapter Title).
  8. Click out of the header.

You may not have to do anything else: the headers will now alternate correctly all the way to the end of the book! Save your file!

However, for extra credit, you may want the Parts and Chapters to start on Recto (right-hand) pages.  And you may want to suppress the running header because it looks silly if it appears above a Part or Chapter title which says the same thing.

We accomplish both by adding a Right Page section Break immediately before the Part Title or Chapter Title paragraph.  You don't have to do anything else: if the Master section break was set up correctly in Steps 1 to 8, then the Chapter and Part section breaks will by default force the following paragraph to begin a right hand page.

Now: This gets really confusing.  Word inserts a blank page if it needs to, to force the Part and Chapter starts to a right-hand page.  But because the page is completely blank, it does not appear when you display the document.  Because it's not there!  Word generates it by adding a New Page character into the print stream only when the document is printed!

You deleted the First Page header, so automatically no header will appear above a Part or Chapter title.

Your StyleRef fields will faithfully replicate the text of the nearest Book, Part and Chapter title paragraphs into the headers: you do not have to do anything.  Leave them set to "Same as Previous".

What happens here is that there is in fact only ONE Left Header and only ONE Right Header in the document now, and it's in the Master section break.  The Same As Previous copies it to all of the other headers after the Introduction.  And the StyleRef fields that those headers contain pick up the closest previous text of the correct style and copy it into the headers.

You would normally set the headers in the Introduction by typing.  But before you do that, make very sure that t he headers and footers for Section 2 are NOT set to Same As Previous, otherwise you will overwrite all your hard work.

There you go: do exactly that and it will all work.  I think what was confusing you is that you did not understand that changing a header set to "Same as Previous" changes all the other headers linked to it, and that you do not need to create section breaks whenever you want a new header, the StyleRef field will do that automatically for you.

Hope this helps

tom-s say:

oldscotch,

Depends if you want to be able to do some calcs with the Goluri data, or just make it 'look like' the web table. Just to make it look like, format the Goluri columns as Custom with format [h]:mm

One way to get the Goluri as text but a little involved:

  • import the table to Excel
  • copy the first Goluri column & paste it into a new Word doc
  • in Word, use Find & Replace to replace all of the : (semi-colon) with - (dash)
  • copy the Word table and paste into a new column in Excel
  • apply this formula to the cells in the new column  =SUBSTITUTE(Y4,"-",":",1)  where this assumes the new column data starts in Y4
  • apply this formula to those cells with the previous formula  =LEFT(Z4,IFERROR(FIND("-",Z4)-1,LEN(Z4)))  where this assumes the first cell with the previous formula was Z4
  • copy the second formula column and paste special values back into the original Goluri column
  • repeat steps 2-7 for the other Goluri columns
