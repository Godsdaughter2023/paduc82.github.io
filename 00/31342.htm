andre da costa say:

Click Start, type: CMD
Right click CMD
Click Run as administrator

At the command prompt, type:

slmgr.vbs /ato

Hit Enter on your keyboard

This will force activation.

If that does not work, contact your Network Administrator for assistance or request a Multiple Activation Key and change the KMS license to MAK. This will remove the need to reactivate Windows 7 Enterprise every 180 days.

Once you receive the product key, do the following:

Click Start, type: CMD'
Right click CMD
Click Run as adminstrator
At the command prompt, type the following commands:
slmgr.vbs -ipk xxxx-xxxx-xxxx-xxxx (allows you to replace the current product key with the specified)
xxxx-xxxx-xxxx-xxxx - represents your product key
Hit Enter on your keyboard
Exit the command prompt
Restart your computer

You are using a KMS Client, which means you need to contact your system administrator and have the license reactivated or reconnect to your companies Domain and reactivate the license.

Sometimes you might need to update your BIOS before you can reactivate, but this should be used as a last resort.

john rubdy say:

Hi,

Removing the write protection depends on the source of the problem.
Broadly speaking, it can be any of the following scenarios

  1. There is a write-protection tab on the mini sd card HOLDER. Every time you insert this into your PC or card holder, this tab slides to the write protect position. Either keep resetting, inserting & reinserting until it doesn't slide or tape it down.
  2. There is nothing wrong with the mini sd card. Simply locate the write protection key and push it.
  3. The mini SD card has some error: Go for a low level formatting.
  4. The card has virus: Remove the virus.

There is an easy way to solve this problem: Simply put the yellow lock in the middle of its slider. No sticky tape required, as shown below. But if you’re still having the same problem I suggest you try the sticky tape.

Even though what is written below may work, the problem is probably that on the card reader (if you have one) there is a small switch that has lock written on the side. If this is pushed down, the drive is write protected, push it in the opposite direction and it may fix your problem.

I would suggest you to format the SD card and check if that helps, however please backup your data first.

1.       Insert the SD card.

2.       Open computer.

3.       Right click on the SD card and then click on format.

 

Using flash memory cards

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows7/using-flash-memory-cards

Hope this information is helpful. Please let us know if you require further help.
