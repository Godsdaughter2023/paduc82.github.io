Using a SATA and an IDE drive on an IDE motherboard.

Here's my problem: I have a 500GB EIDE drive that holds two partitions: one for Windows XP and one for Windows 7.  A few days ago, the drive began reporting a SMART error and Windows 7 told me I need to back up the disk immediately and replace the drive.  I ran a full backup procedure, capturing both partitions, placing the output on a 2TB external drive.  So far, so good.

I bought the drive on May 20, 2009 and the manufacturer's web site says the drive is under warranty still until later this year.  So, I contacted the manufacturer and they said they'd replace it.  At this point, the first problem arose: they don't have any more EIDE drives with which to honor the warranty.  They said they'd upgrade me to a SATA drive, but my motherboard doesn't have any SATA ports, only IDE ports.  On one port, I had the 500GB drive as the master and a second drive (holding a Vista installation) as the secondary drive.  The two drives share a single IDE cable and the motherboard uses Cable Select to distinguish them.

I went online and found several devices, one of which is http://www.amazon.com/SATA-PATA-Drive-Interface-Adapter/dp/B002Y2NI4M/.  This product could solve part of the problem: it will allow me to use a SATA drive, converting the SATA connectors on the back of the drive to EIDE (or PATA).  Here's my question: do you think this would allow me to daisy-chain the second drive to the first, still using Cable Select?  At first glance, it seems to me it should, but is any of you have experience with this type of device, I'd appreciate the input.

hula_baloo say:

 You'd think the adapter SHOULD support master/slave, but I can't speak from experience.  I'd think maybe a PCI card that supplies a SATA interface to the new drive would be the best solution.  If your mobo has a PCIe slot free, you could probably get a faster transfer rate with the new SATA drive too.  Since you'll still be using the same mobo, I'd think Windows wouldn't have a problem with authorization.  Probably the ideal solution would be a new mobo, but then you'd definitely have to reinstall, re activate, copy data etc. which would likely be a real pain.  Decisions, decisions! :-)

 

 Good luck.
