Different manufacturers of windows phones provide different options on the phones. For instance, I have an HTC Touch Diamond2, which allows me to press & hold the call end button to turn on or turn off flight mode.  On another phone I have - the HP IPAQ Glisten - there's no way for me to switch to flight mode except by going through the wireless manager (the way you've described). 

To make things easier for me, I've installed a profile manager to the Glisten called PhoneWeaver .  PhoneWeaver lets me set up lots of different profiles on the device to handle and automate many of the different notification, ringing, communication scenarios I use on my phone.  Some of the profiles I've set up are automatic, based on calendar entries or my known schedule (i.e., turn off ringer after 11:00pm/turn ringer on at 7:00am).  Once nice thing about PhoneWeaver is that it lets me put a small icon on the top of the screen.  When I tap that icon, I can select the profile I want to use at that time.  I've got a profile set up, called Flight Mode.  Selecting that profile turns off WIFI, Bluetooth, and the Phone.  Then I've got another profile that just turns on the phone.

I'm using Phone Weaver on the HP IPAQ Glisten, but you might also want to check outPhone Alarm - it's another great profile manager that I've used in the past.
