    brit77 say:

    I was experiencing the same problem for several months and had tried all the same steps you outlined above. I finally stumbled upon a solution when I noticed that the wrong drive was being indexed (so the settings shortcuts were pointing to the wrong place and wouldn't open since they didn't actually exist on that drive). I couldn't simply index the correct drive since my selected locations kept reverting to my old boot drive.

    I found my solution here: http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/windows_10-win_cortana/windows-10-indexing-selects-wrong-drive/685ec87b-e7f3-454b-89c9-c485b94bb69d

    Since I don't really need the old drive, I opted to just disable it, and then re-indexing fixed the issue for me!

    I can't tell from your posts if this is a solution that applies to you, but it may be worth verifying that your indexed locations (particularly for your user profile AppData files) are pointing to your main boot drive. Not sure if this is a possibility, but could be that switching from an online to a local account left part of the index still pointing to OneDrive?

    Anyway, good luck! I'm much happier now with Windows 10 since the start menu search is fully working again.
