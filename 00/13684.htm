Tools>Internet Options - Advanced tab, uncheck "Enable third-party browser extensions".

Restart IE.

You will need to re-enable third-party browser extensions setting once you have upgraded you r Addon toolbars.

hansbrinker say:

Hans,

Excel date format sometimes have mysterious prefixes. Cell B4434 originally had [$-x-sysdate]dddd, mmmm dd,yy but did not display the weekday name. When I changed the prefix to [$-en-US] the weekday name was displayed, the format worked when I used the Format Painter. The weekday did not disappear when I scrolled, and was not lost on closing/reopening the file

 

Canadians use both the US (mm-dd-yy) and the international (dd-mm-yy_ formats – the later is the official one. I use the international one.

 

So before I opened your file I used Control Panel to give me the US format. Because of all of this you may see different date prefixes when you open the Format dialog. But you can always type the US one in Custom Format.

 

Hope I was able to help,

 

Bernard
