davem121 say:
This is very common currently, caused by Windows Update installing an incorrect display driver, and the current fix is rolling back your Display driver rather than updating it . . .

The Fall Creators Update has caused compatibility issues with some dieplay devices, Microsoft say they are aware of this and are working on a patch!

Go to Device Manager (accessible by right clicking your Start Button)

Expand Display, right click your Display Device, and if available, click 'Roll Back Driver'

Restart your system

If that does not work, go to the Support Page of the manufacturer of your device and download a slightly older Display driver and install that . . .

Finally, if none of the above works, turn off sleep mode until Microsoft release the patch . . .

You would save yourself a lot of headaches if you uploaded the files to Onedrive and downloaded from there.
