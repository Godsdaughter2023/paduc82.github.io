mick1951 say:

postmaster "failure to deliver"

I keep getting failure to deliver notices from the postmaster.  These are not messages I have sent and are to people I don't know.  They are all a birthday card video which I did not open.    How can I stop this and what has caused it? Has my account been hacked?

roady [mvp] say:

It could be that your address has been used as the From field in someone's spam run which means any undeliverable notices will be sent to you.

If your ISP hosting your mailbox doesn't have any so called backscatter or BATV protection in place, you will indeed receive these notices instead of them being dropped by the mail server of your ISP. It is best to contact them and ask them why these messages weren't dropped by their security system. That some spam comes through every once and a while is unavoidable but obvious spam and spam floods should be detected by any professional host.

At client level, you can't do much about this as the emails have already been delivered to your mailbox and all Outlook does is collect these emails. You can create a message rule to immediately delete these messages or move them to your Junk folder. Depending on the capabilities of your web based mail client and type of account, you may be able to create such a rule there as well so they'll be deleted before Outlook can download them.

Your account doesn't need to be hacked for this as a mail server can be configured to send out with any email address (you can protect your email domain from this by setting SPF records so that receiving mail servers know that it wasn't sent from an authorized mail server and they'll drop the message). However, if you suspect that your account's password has been compromised, it is highly recommended to immediately change your password.
