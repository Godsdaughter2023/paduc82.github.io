timoteo iv ofr say:

This happens due to an issue with the USB specification for how USB chargers are detected. The USB specification has a strict timing sequence for what needs to happen when a charger is connected. If something interferes with that timing, the sequence is not compliant, and charging is limited to a slower rate for safety reasons.

Even if you're connecting your charger normally, this condition can still happen for other reasons, such as if a phone case was blocking insertion, or if dust/dirt inside the USB port on your phone is preventing the USB plug from being securely inserted, or if the USB plug on your charger is defective or worn out.

We suggest that you try cleaning the USB port of your phone and seeing if that fixes the issue. Use a compressed air can/duster to avoid damage to the USB pins/wires, or you can take it to a professional to have them clean it.
