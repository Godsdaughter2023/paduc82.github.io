don cuthbert say:

With C:\$Windows.~BT\Sources\install.esd, you can make a bootable 10547 ISO file.  Sometimes that file is preserved, even after an install.  With most builds it vanishes after the upgrade.

Since the ESD files produced by Windows Update are not encrypted, they can even just be applied with DISM to a partition (index:4) and the BCD edited to boot it.  Or there are scripts such as ESD Decrypter wimlib 4.7 that are simple to use to create an ISO from this one file.

I always use the Snipping Tool, found in Windows Accessories, to take clips of the screen.  You can capture a screen, a rectangular snip, or a Window.  Now it even has a timer in case you want to click to display a menu before it starts the capture.  You can save these as PNG files or paste into Paint to edit.

=INT(B27-A27) & " days " & HOUR(B27-A27) & " hours"

Yes, you are wrong.  Think of 8/15/12 11:30 as 8/14/12 35:30 (24:00 + 11:30).  Then subtracting 8/1/12 13:00, we get 13d 22:30 (35:30 - 13:00).

The formula above correctly returns "13 days 22 hours".  That rounds down hours.

But you wrote:  "It should be coming back with 13 days 23 hours".

As I suspected, you really want to round or
In order to choose the correct formula, choose the column below that returns the correct result for


The formulas are:
C2:  =INT(B2-$A$2)&" days "&TEXT(B2-$A$2,"hh:mm")
D2:  =INT(B2-$A$2)&" days "&HOUR(B2-$A$2)&" hours"
E2:  =INT(ROUND((B2-$A$2)*24,0)/24)&" days "&HOUR(ROUND((B2-$A$2)*24,0)/24)&" hours"
F2:  =INT(ROUNDUP((B2-$A$2)*24,0)/24)&" days "&HOUR(ROUNDUP((B2-$A$2)*24,0)/24)&" hours"
