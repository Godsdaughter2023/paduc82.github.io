The base vhd, in programfiles cannot be moved or renamed, if done, xp mode will stop booting.

To move the files under c, first boot xp mode, instead of saving the virtual machine., shut it down. (change the close settings to prompt the user for close action). Close the virtual machine., and choose shutdown. This will remove the vsv file. Do not delete the .vsv file, as it contains data if you have not saved inside the virtual machine.

Open xp mode settings, go to hard disk 1 setting, and repoint the hard disk to the new location of the vhd. You should now be able to boot off the virtual machine.

3 people found this helpful

zacabeb say:

It can work, but there will be a few things to consider.

.

1) The VCR may want to turn itself off after a few minutes. Overriding the auto standby mode can take a bit of detective work.

.

2) Any video material that uses Rovi AVP copy protection (aka Macrovision), e.g. DVD Video or streaming video services, may cause severe distortion to the picture, depending on how the VCR routes the signal internally.

.

3) If you live in Europe or Oceania and thus have a PAL standard VCR section, it probably won't accept the PAL-60 signal sent from the Xbox 360, even if it is capable itself of playing back NTSC video as PAL-60.

aydenhendricks say:

also, on a old tv, will S-video look sharper than composite or does it depend on the tv?

zacabeb say:

The conversion from S-video to composite in the VCR might let through more bandwidth in the luma signal than what the Xbox 360's own composite signal does. If the tv has composite inputs and a comb filter, that can give an overall improvement.

.

If the tv has an S-video input, it's even better. Generally, so long as the source has sufficient quality there should be a visible difference between composite and S-video.

.

But if the tv only has composite inputs and does not have a comb filter, the difference between composite and composite generated by a VCR from an S-video source is minimal. Consoles are an exception since they tend to filter their composite output harder than necessary.

.

Over RF, there is already such limitations to bandwidth that there might not be much difference at all.

Hibernation is a power-saving state designed primarily for laptops. While sleep puts your work and settings in memory and draws a small amount of power, hibernation puts your open documents and programs on your hard disk, and then turns off your computer. Of all the power-saving states in Windows, hibernation uses the least amount of power. On a laptop, use hibernation when you know that you won't use your laptop for an extended period and won't have an opportunity to charge the battery during that time.
