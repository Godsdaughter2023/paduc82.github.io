habib alam say: It is a shame isn't it no one has got any answer for my question

paulc_1 say:

Hi Habib,

I love my Surface Pro 3 too.

However, in regard to your question, unfortunately the aspect ratio is a hardware issue. Most movies today would be running at 16:9, which means you are always going to see a letter box effect: black lines above and below the video, when played back on the Surface Pro 3. If you want a video to fill the whole screen, then you need a video player that will run in a Pan and Scan mode (or crop mode), at 3:2 aspect ratio. You'll lose whatever is cropped but the video will fill the whole screen.

You could try something like VLC Media Player for the Desktop. http://www.videolan.org/vlc/download-windows.html  Scroll down to the 64bit version on the linked page, download the file, unzip it and run VLC.

When you play a video through it, it allows you to crop the video according to preset aspect ratios. Unfortunately, it won't do 3:2 aspect, but you can crop to 4:3 while videos play back. At 4:3 you can run at full screen and fill the entire height of the Surface screen, but you'll have thin black lines at the sides instead of top and bottom. 

PaulC

Edit: I noted VLC has a 5:3 crop ratio too. You couldtry playback at a 5:3 crop as that is a bit closer to the aspect ratio of the Surface Pro 3 than 4:3.
