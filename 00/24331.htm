roger carlson access mvp say:

Let me sneak up on it in stages. 

First of all, you can create a query which will create a sequential number by group (this process REQUIRES a unique ID):

SELECT Project_ID, Person_ID, "Person_" & DCount("Person_ID","Table1"," Project_ID = " & [Project_ID] & " And ID <=" & [ID]) AS Person_Num
FROM Table1;


Project_ID Person_ID Person_Num
22             1              Person_1
22             23            Person_2
22            631           Person_3
34            1               Person_1
35            4               Person_1
35            6               Person_2

Next, you can use that query (call it Query1) in a crosstab query to get the results you want:

TRANSFORM First(Query1.[Person_ID]) AS FirstOfPerson_ID
SELECT Query1.[Project_ID], First(Query1.[Person_ID]) AS [Total Of Person_ID]
FROM Query1
GROUP BY Query1.[Project_ID]
PIVOT Query1.[Person_Num];

Project_ID Person_1 Person_2 Person_3
22              1             23          631
34              1  
35              4             6 

Now, these two queries can be combined into one:

TRANSFORM First(T.[Person_ID]) AS FirstOfPerson_ID
SELECT T.[Project_ID]
FROM (SELECT Project_ID, Person_ID, "Person_" & DCount("Person_ID","Table1"," Project_ID = " & Project_ID & " And ID <=" & ID) AS Person_Num
FROM Table1) as T
GROUP BY T.[Project_ID]
PIVOT T.[Person_Num];

*Of course you'll need to replace your table name for "Table1" and your actual ID field for "ID"
