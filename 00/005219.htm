Why do headers and footers sometimes repeat even after I have - Microsoft Community

ronpratt say:

Why do headers and footers sometimes repeat even after I have unchecked the "link to previous" box?

And more in depth...

I have a document with lots of continuous section breaks so I can toggle back and forth between single and double columns. I also have "mirror margins" activated, plus odd and even page headers. How do I keep a continuous sequence of page numbers on the outside bottom corner of each page, across all these breaks and odd/even footers? Word seems to change some pages to odd from even, or odd to even, all by itself. What guru can give me some advice about this? It used be so intuitive, but not any more!
daniel g. grau say:
For odd and even headers, don't do that manually in a one-by-one fashion. Go to Format > Document, click on the Layout tab at the top and enable "Different odd and even." This will give you one set of odd/even footers and headers for all the linked sections.

Once you have a single set of odd/even headers and footers, just change the position of the graphic and the numbers.

Page breaks won't alter anything. Do note, however, that they should only be used when you want to start a new page, as in a chapter title. If you add them simply to keep titles on the same page as the text that follows them, you have a better choice:

  • Select Format > Paragraph
  • Click on the Line and page breaks tab
  • Enable "Keep with next" (to keep at least part of the paragraph on the same page as the next one) and/or "Keep lines together" (to keep all lines of a paragraph on the same page)

These attributes have the advantage that, if you add or delete lines from your document, the structure will be kept and no blank space will appear at the end of the page where you would have inserted the page break. In other words, the document's text will always flow from one page to the next.

It will be useful to assign a keyboard shortcut to those two commands. To do this, select Tools > Customize Keyboard, select Format on the left list, then select ParaKeepWithNext or ParaKeepLinesTogether on the right and choose a keyboard sequence. Word will tell you if the sequence is already taken. Just about all Control + Any_Key combinations are available.
