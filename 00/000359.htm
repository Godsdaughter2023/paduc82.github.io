john korchok say:

Yup, that's how Word works. I'm pretty sure you're thinking that page 3 updates because you scroll down to it and the scrolling updates the field. To demonstrate, re-open the document, then choose View>Multiple Pages. This should give you a simultaneous view of the three pages of the document. If not slide the view percentage slider in the lower right corner of the window to a smaller amount, until you can see all pages of the file.

Now update the first name field. You'll notice only the first page updates. At least, that's how Word 2016 behaves here. However, changing the view percentage, then scrolling through the pages should show an updated result in the header.

You may be familiar with Excel, where updates to a cells content normally automatically updates linked cells (this behavior can be turned off in Excel). Word is different. Because it was designed as a program to create documents for print, fields normally update at print, print preview or screen refresh time, not necessarily in real time.

joshmacd say:

How to mail merge without using a custom field?

I don't want to insert custom fields into my email. I just want to send an individual email to each person. 

What are my options?

Do I have to insert a custom field and put the colour as white like the background?

macropod say:

OK, there seems to be something odd about how mailmerge on the Mac is working. You can get around the problem by inserting a field coded as:

{IF«Contact» = "" ""}

anywhere in the mailmerge main document. This allows you to have a «Contact» there, thus satisfying the 'Finish & Merge' prerequisite, without having the content display in the output.

Note: The field brace pairs (i.e. '{ }') for the above example are created in the document itself, via Ctrl-F9 (Cmd-F9 on a Mac); you can't simply type them or copy & paste them from this message. Likewise, you can't type or copy & paste the chevrons (i.e. '« »') - they're part of the actual mergefields, which you can insert from the 'Insert Merge Field' dropdown. The spaces represented in the field construction are all required.
