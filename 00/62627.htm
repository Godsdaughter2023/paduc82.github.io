a. user say:

Hi Jenni,

Network settings can also affect your system's performance in searching for files and folders. To troubleshoot the issue, we suggest turning off your network's proxy settings and see if the issue still occurs. To do this, simply follow the steps below:

  1. Click the Start button and select Settings.
  2. On the Settings window, click on Network & Internet.
  3. On the left-pane, click on Proxy.
  4. On the Manual proxy setup column, toggle the User a proxy server to Off.
  5. Restart your computer and try to search for files/folders.

Should you have further questions, please let us know.

canadian tech say:

Ken is absolutely correct it is a scam and a dangerous one at that.

It is easily fixed:

  1. Right-click on your task bar at the bottom of you screen
  2. Choose Task Manager
  3. Click the Applications tab
  4. Find the item that is giving you this message in the list
  5. Click once on it, then click End task

I have seen this on client computers many times. It usually does not include an infection, but to be sure, I recommend that you use ADWcleaner to remove any malware: https://toolslib.net/downloads/viewdownload/1-adwcleaner/
