  • Modify birthday in Outlook.com > It crosses over to iOS 12.2 with no issues (DOB & Anniversary dates) ✔

---------------------------------- HISTORICAL INFORMATION (ORIGINAL TICKET) --------------------------------------------------

After experiencing this issue a second time, I was able to stumble upon this thread which is now locked*

Locked Thread: birthday dates move forward one day when entered in outlook.com but synced to mobile (British summer time)

Created a copy of this thread at Apple as well: iPhone (iOS) and Outlook.com synchronization moves contact birthday date forward +1 day

Devices and Applications Affected:

- Contacts in iOS

- Contacts in OSX

Confirmed Contact Fields Affected:

- Date of Birth

- Anniversary*

*steps below use DOB as an example, but the same behavior happens to Anniversary date field.

Below are the steps and actions that make this problem easy to reproduce:

  • Create a completely new account @outlook 
  • Add contacts with birthdays
  • Link this account to an iPhone
  • Contacts on the iPhone will all display birthday +1 day after synchronizing

Contacts created on Outlook.com
  • When the contacts sync to the iPhone the date automatically changes to +1 on the phone for all contacts
  • If a change is made to a contact (any contact fields) on the iPhone, the +1 day DOB will sync back to Outlook.com when those changes are pushed.
  • Going to Outlook.com to verify this info shows the following:
    • Contact View Mode: shows correct birthday
    • Contact Edit Mode: shows dob +1 day

Contacts created on iPhone (iOS 12.1.0)
  • When a contact is created on the iPhone, the date will be accurate and syncs correctly back to Outlook.com
  • If a change is made on Outlook.com, when the phone syncs to Outlook.com it will update the DOB on the phone by +1
  • If a change is made again on the iPhone (any contact field), when the phone syncs changes back to Outlook.com, it will update the new DOB to +1. 
  • Going to Outlook.com to verify this info shows the following:
    • Contact View Mode: shows correct birthday
    • Contact Edit Mode: shows dob +1 day

It seems like the break is on how Outlook.com and iPhone synchronize this information. It's also strange the Outlook.com Contact View Mode shows one date, while Contact Edit Mode shows a different date than the displayed one. 

These steps are easy to reproduce on my end on both my account and the new account created. 

Some people state that since iOS12 that synchronization of Anniversaries are also affected. I did not try to validate those steps in this testing.

Edit 12/14 - Adding Additional Troubleshooting Info

Contacts created on Outlook.com and linking account to OSX and iOS:

STEPS TAKEN / Observation

  1. Create a new contact with a birthday in an outlook.com account
  2. Add account to iOS or OSX and that sync includes contacts to be part of the sync
  3. When looking at the contact in iOS or oSX, the contact’s birthday will be one day after the entered birthday on step 1
  4. Correcting the birthday in iOS or OSX will correct the birthday in Outlook.com when syncing
  5. Make a change to the contact’s field in Outlook.com
  6. When checking iOS or OSX, the changed field has updated and the birthday field will display a day after the date displayed in Outlook.com when the contact was saved

Issue 1: Every time iOS or OSX refreshes from the server, it will add +1 day to the date saved in Outlook.com 

Issue 2: If a contact with the +1 date on iOS or OSX is updated on those devices, they will also update the contact with the incorrect date on Outlook.com

To filter the OrderDate field (it's not clear which field contains the desired date) and still take advantage of indexing on the field, you can use a criterion

>= DateSerial(Year(Date()), 1, 1,) AND < DateSerial(Year(Date()) + 1, 1, 1)

But if your UDF is constructing a multi-record concatenated field of multiple lines, then no, that's not easily done directly in a Query.
