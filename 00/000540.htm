julie fka profjulie say:

You won't find a way to download & save your Audible books to your  SD card. The thing that would need to happen is for Audible to provide a way to stream books from their user online library. The only thing I can suggest is that you check your Audible settings to make sure you are downloading standard quality books (and not high quality books) - this option is found under "settings & support" > downloads.

 

If you convert the books to mp3 format, you will have more options since you will no longer be limited by Audible's limitations. You might also lose some functionality (ie, bookmarking pages), but I've seen some third party apps in the marketplace that appear to manage audio books (that are mp3 formatted books), with some apps even allowing you to stream audio books that are stored in SkyDrive. Audiobookster (http://windowsphone.com/s?appid=00d25b14-f5da-47ef-9578-b52da5752ec0) looks  like a really interesting app (I have not used this app yet so can't provide any information about how well it works).  I've got some mp3 formatted books that I've uploaded to SkyDrive and used the SkyMusic app to test streaming a book to my phone. It's pretty time consuming to upload books to SkyDrive so I'm not sure that I'll pursue that method. I think I'd be more inclined to use File Explorer to copy the books to my phone.  Of course if you do this, your books will show up as music files on the phone, so I'd create a playlist of all the parts of the book, making sure the parts are in the right order and then sync the playlist to my phone from windows media player.
