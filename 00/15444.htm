a. user say:

Hi RobertMyers,

 

I apologize for the delayed response and welcome to Microsoft Community. I recognized that you need to determine where to locate the archived emails. Please be informed that if you use the archive button, the email will go directly to the assigned archive folder.

 

A user is prompted to assign where to designate the emails on the first use of the Archive. Outlook will ask the user if he wants to create a new or an existing folder for the archived messages. Are you not prompted to receive this notification when you use Archive?

 

If so, you can try to locate your archive folder by following these steps:

  1. Select any email on your inbox and take note on the subject of the message.
  2. Click Archive.
  3. Set your reading pane to Off. Click the cog icon located on the upper right of the mailbox and under Reading pane, select Off. This will result to have a full view of the mailbox. 
  4. Now that the email is in the unknown archive folder, try to search for the subject of the message by entering the title of the email in the search engine of Outlook. This is located on the top left of the mailbox.
  5. Once the email has been located look for the folder location of the message in the subject line. That folder is the assigned archive folder.
