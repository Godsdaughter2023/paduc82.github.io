Pogo-sticking is when a user reaches your website from Bing search results and immediately (or within a few seconds) return to the search results and clicks on another page.

A high pogo-sticking bounce rate tells the search engine that your website may appear to be related to the search keywords but in fact isn't. Search engines don't like it and a high pogo-sticking rate is likely to negatively affect your website ranking.

sanketchougule say: What Is Pogo sticking?

Pogo sticking is when a search engine users visit several different search results in order to find a result that satisfies their search query.

Pogo sticking is a VERY strong sign that users aren’t happy with the results.

In fact, Steven Levy’s book “In The Plex” cites how Google engineers used Pogo sticking to help sort the first page results:

Pogo-sticking is defined as going back and forth from a search engine results page (SERP) to an individual search result destination site. In other words, pogo-sticking is when the searcher clicks on a link on a SERP, sees that it’s not what she is looking for, and immediately bounces off by hitting the back button. She then chooses another result from the results page to satisfy her informational need. This event is also closesly measured in Google Analytics under the term “bounce rate.”

Search engines prefer users to discover the content they want on their first try, so pogo-sticking isn’t in the best interest of Google, Bing, the searcher, or the brand publisher. Like this:

Sub MyButton_Click()
    Dim r As Long

Best Time

I am new at this.  I have a very limited budget. I started out with $5 per day but now made it $7 a day. Can someone tell me the best hours to set the ad to display. Ad is about selling photographs. Thank You
rs_bingads say:

Hi Good Photographer,

When you are inside your Bing Ads account, click on Reports at the top of your User Interface.  (Black Banner at the top of your screen).  The Keyword Report comes up by default (You can run other reports by using the report type dropdown or selecting them from the left hand column).  Under general settings, Change Show (unit of time) to Hour.  Then Select an appropriate date range.  Next select Run at the bottom of the report window.  This will generate a keyword report showing impressions and clicks for specific keywords in your campaign.  If you sort by clicks at the top of the report, you can then see what time the clicks occurred.  This is a good way to determine what hours to set your campaign to run.

Please let us know if you have any other questions or feel free to reach out to us via phone or chat for further assistance.
