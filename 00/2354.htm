betty gui msft say:

Hi Exec AssistantOK,

 

Since your boss would like to view the meeting invites in her inbox folder, but not the meeting responses, I recommend you ask her to change the meeting delivery way, change it to be "My delegates and me".


 

Then, your boss can create an inbox rule in her mailbox to block the meeting response from going to the inbox folder.

1. Go to File > Manage Rules & Alerts.

2. Select to create a New Rule.

3. Select the condition as "Move messages with specific words in the subject to a folder" and click

4. Select "with specific words in the message header", you can enter "Accepted" , "Tentative" and "Declined" in the specific words line and click Next.

5. Then select the action as "delete it" or "permanently delete it".

6. Click Next and Finish to complete the inbox rule.

 

After the operations above, your boss will only receive meeting request to her.

It don't think your wording matters - Azure and Office 365 are different services but are linked.

Is the computer joined to Azure AD? Does your email address have an Azure account for other services?

>>

Often happens when you have some services in Microsoft's cloud services and an on-prem exchange server (it may happen with other mail servers too). If you use A subscription based office tied to the same address, it gets confused.

This registry is one way to stop Outlook from checking office 365 -
HKEY_CURRENT_USER\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\office\16.0\outlook\autodiscover
DWORD: ExcludeExplicitO365Endpoint
Value = 1
