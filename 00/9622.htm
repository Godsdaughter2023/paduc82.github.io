davegbasic say: The N97 requires a minimul amount of current to charge properly. Note that the supplied North American charger output is rated as 1200 mA. Most cigarette lighter / USB adapters and other ports and chargers are rated at 500 milliamps are not sufficient to charge this phone. Most consumer issued MP3 players and such use much smaller capacity batteries and can use smaller capacity chargers. And before the complaints come, please recognize that the N97 uses the BP-4L battery. It is one of the highest capacity batteries provided in a phone and is why the N97 can run all day under a fair amount of use after a full charge. It is recognized as one of the best batteries around. As a result it needs a fair amount of juice to charge quickly and correctly. I personally would not use anything less then a 1 amp (1000 mA) charger. Note that the less the capacity of the charger the longer the phone takes to charge fully. sockatume---01 say: I think this is an issue of interpretation of the USB charging specifications. There should be:

1) Standard USB ports on PCs giving up to 500mA via negotiation with the device.
2) Charging USB ports on PCs giving up to 1.5A, depending on whether data has to be transferred, via negotiation with the device.
3) Dedicated Charging Ports giving up to 1.8A but not allowing data transfer. The data pins are shorted and no negotiation happens between the device and power source.

The Nokia USB charger has shorted data pins, which means the phone detects it as a Dedicated Charging Port. Similarly, the micro-USB adaptor probably shorts the data pins on its USB plug. I've not confirmed this.

Evidently Nokia's handsets are only willing to accept actual, active USB ports on computers, or dedicated charging ports. It may be that Nokia interpreted the specification more strictly than other manufacturers.

sockatume---01 say: From some Googling it appears this is an issue with more recent Apple devices too. "Dumb" chargers simply don't do anything with the data pins, which is fine for a "dumb" device, but any device obeying the spec rigorously will try to talk to the charger, get silence in return, and refuse to work with that charger.
