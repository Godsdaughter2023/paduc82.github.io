rob koch say:

No one other than Microsoft can access a sub-domain of the Microsoft.com domain, so that's not a question that needs to be answered, that's a fundamental rule of the Domain Name System.

For example, anythingelse.microsoft.com would be a valid Microsoft sub-domain.

All that matters is whether your browser or email program is properly displaying the true name and that the name isn't slightly misspelled or the URL is otherwise being obfuscated in order to trick you into believing that the domain truly belongs to Microsoft.

And yes, that's a Microsoft address that we've seen questions about for many years, but always has the same answer, as do several others used for specific purposes, always for exactly this same reason.

The real question at the core of any of these is whether the email itself is valid, which is why I headed you in that direction instead, since though any portion of an email is easily spoofed (faked) by scammers and spammers, it's always safer to go directly to the official Microsoft or other company websites for any message you might question.

Even if the e.microsoft.com name itself is valid as well as the rewards program, it's possible that the specific message you received is a fake using these individual pieces of information to gain your trust, while the message itself is instead a fake trying to trick you into giving up some personal information to steal your identity.

I know, I did this type of phishing test professionally against financial and other organizations in order to test their employee's ability to recognize and avoid the fakes.

Rob

Here you can find steps how to do hard reset with phone buttons:

1. Switch off your phone, and disconnect the charger.
2. Press and hold the volume down key, and connect the charger. If an exclamation mark (!) is shown on the screen, release the volume down key.
3. Then press the volume and power keys in the following order: volume up, volume down, power, and volume down.
4. Wait for your phone to reset, during the reset you will see gears rotating on the screen for up to 5 minutes, after which the screen goes blank for ~30 seconds and then your phone will restart.
5. Go through the initial phone setup. Make sure that you have the date and time set correctly on your phone (Settings > date+time).
6. Disconnect the charger.
