joeu2004 say:

JordanCA5707 wrote:

if I understand what your saying and the formula your using, the formula will return the minimum payment that would cover the interest only?  So in other words, using this formula I could calc the interest add it to the balance and then subtract the payments for that month.

You are correct that the weekly balance is the previous balance plus interest minus payment.  The interest is the previous balance times annual rate divided by 52.

But at 3.5% annual rate, the minimum payment to cover interest only is =500000*3.5%/52, which is about 336.54.

The formula =PMT(3.5%/52,261,-500000), which returns about 2089.55, is the "regular" (equal) payment needed to "fully amortize" the loan over 261 weeks.  That means:  pay off interest and some principal with each payment.

There are 261 weeks, not 260 (5*52), in the 60-month period with the first payment on 6/1/2015 and the last payment on 5/25/2020.

But typically, with an adjustable-rate loan, the periodic payment is recalculated each time the interest rate changes.  In general, the weekly payment is =PMT(annualRate/52,#remainingWeeks,-balance).

JordanCA5707 wrote:

I understand that unless extra payments are made the balance might go up.

With regular payments of 1000, the balance would go up ("amortize negatively") only if the annual rate exceeded =52*1000/500000, which is 10.4%.

That is unlikely even over the next 5 years, if your interest rate is the 1-month USD LIBOR plus about 3.32%, as it appears to be.

Moreover, you can tolerate even higher annual rates without negative amortization as time goes by and you pay off some principal.

JordanCA5707 wrote:

I think the are banking on being able to throw extra payments into the balance. However, I need to be able to provide them with the ability to calculate this in any scenario.

rich007a say:

Hi.

You can apply an AutoFilter to your data.

Assuming your data is in A1:B5 (column headers in row 1), select that range, do Data tab > AutoFilter.

Click the dropdown arrow in B1 and do Number filters > Does not equal >Does not equal 0.

 

Providing your chart is set up as follows:

Right-click on the chart > Select data > Hidden and Empty cells (button) > un-check "Show data in hidden rows and columns"

 

then that should now only show the non-zero (i.e. visible) rows in the chart.

 

BUT, when the vaules change, the filter doesn't automatically update!  To make that happen, right-click on the data sheet's tab (at the bottom of Excel) and do Show Code.  Paste the following code on the right of the VBE:

 

Private Sub Worksheet_Change(ByVal Target As Range)
    If Me.AutoFilterMode Then Me.AutoFilter.ApplyFilter
End Sub

Note: I have assumed that your plotted range is linked in some way (e.g. with a formula) to some other data which is changing.  Presumably that data will need to remain visible so may have to be on a different sheet.  In that case, use the code below in the sheet where the data is changed (not in the sheet where the autofilter is applied):

 

Private Sub Worksheet_Change(ByVal Target As Range)
    With Worksheets("Sheet1")  '<--CHANGE TO SHEET NAME WHERE THE PLOTTED DATA RESIDES
        If .AutoFilterMode Then .AutoFilter.ApplyFilter
    End With
End Sub

Hope that helps.
