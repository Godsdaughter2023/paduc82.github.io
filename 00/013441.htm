You can't do it this way - color scales, color bars and icon sets don't support formulas with relative references.

As an alternative, create formulas in another column, e.g. enter =I6/L6 in M6 and fill down to M16.

joeu2004 say: Ossie wrote:
123456789 displays in excel using comma formatting as 123456,789

It is possible that you have two issues:  (a) the "decimal symbol" (separator) is set to comma instead of period; and (b) the "fixed decimal places" edit option might be set.

For #a, that might not a problem at all for your region.  But if you want to check or change it, on you desktop toolbar, click on Start, then Control Panel, double-click on the Regional and Language Options, click on the Regional Options tab, then click on Customize and look for the Decimal Symbol field.

For #b, in Excel, click on Tools, then Options, then click on the Edit tab and look for the Fixed Decimal Places field.  If it is select, you probably want to deselect.

Note:  those instructions are for XL2003.  You might need to adapt them for XL2007.

Once those issues are addressed, be sure that the cell format Number with 2 decimal places, and select the option "Use 1000 separator".

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

AutoUpdate is not broken, but Microsoft's thinking, IMO, most definitely is.

They're now releasing updates on a staged basis. The first to get them are those participating in the Insider program [IOW, those who are beta testing the new updates & those who don't realize they're being used as guinea pigs but have to have the 'latest & greatest' even though it may be flawed]. The updates later are pushed out to those who have Office 365 subscriptions & finally to those who have perpetual licenses.

Long story short, for those on the tail end of the chain, 14.6.1 was not released via AutoUpdate until 16 Feb even though it was published back in Jan. IOW, at the time I wrote that response, 14.6.0 was the latest update available to all users of Office 2011. According to release notes, it's a trivial interim security update that has no bearing on functionality of anything but Outlook. As I wrote, though, running MAU manually would fetch the most recent available update, whether it had been 14.6.0 or 14.6.1 & now will notify accordingly.

Similarly, the 15.19.1 updates for Office 2016 are included in that bundle, but those of us who have the purchased license never saw the 15.9.0 updates.
