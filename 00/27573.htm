johnrigdon say:

Concantenate Formula

I want to concantenate 3 cells with numbers so that it reads 1.2.10. The concantenate cell/formula drops the last digit (0). Any way to keep the zero in the formula?

 

CONCATENATE(D1,".",E1,".",F1)
1.2..9 1 2 0.9
1.2..1 1 2 .10
1.2..11 1 2 .11

mike h.. say:

Hi,

 

When you have (for example) .10 in F1 the trailing zero appears because of the format attached to that cell. When you concatenate then the concatenate formula simply uses the underlying value and takes no account of the format of the source cells.

 

What you have to do is includes the format in the concatenate formula like this. Note I've only done the F1 part of the formula, you would need to do the same for the other cells.

 

 

=CONCATENATE(D1,".",E1,".",TEXT(F1,"#.00"))


Works as advertise, Thanks.

B1 = 1, c1=2, d1 = 0.9.

The end of the number column sequence of numbering is

.9

.10

Hi,

 

If you use the formula below in the concatenate formula the # sign stops the leading zero displaying

 

TEXT(D1,"#.#")

 

So if the cell contained 99.9 then the formula would display that but 0.9 would display as .9 and 9.0 would display as 9.

What is the Office 365 equivalent that I can do so each user mailbox has a separate "archive" section of their mailbox that automatically has a copy of all messages that are stored for 5 years.  These messages must be able to searched and exported in the event of legal action.  The users use the Proofpoint application to search for deleted messages so they can have a nice clean inbox, but still be able to pull up old messages if required.

alexsun_2012 say:

Hi Danny,

As an admin, you can enable archive mailboxes for your users. An archive mailbox is a seperated space and can be enlarged in Office 365 Enterprise plans. Users can move emails to the archive mailbox manually to clean their mailboxes. Also, admins can set up retention policy to move the emails to the archive mailbox automatically based on the time when the emails arrived at the primary mailbox.

In Office 365, if you want to hold your mailbox items, you can enable litigation hold or in-place hold on your mailbox. The items will be kept on the server even they are deleted from the deleted items folder. However, for your requirement that any message that is older that 5 years is automatically removed from the archive, unfortunately, it is not supported. It is not feasible to delete items automatically from the archive mailbox. So we suggest you submit feedback to our relevant team at http://office365.uservoice.com/forums/273493-office-365-admin.

Thanks for your time and understanding.
Alex
