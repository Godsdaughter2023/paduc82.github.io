There must be some value that uniquely identifies the label you want to print. So you use the WHERE clause of the OpenReport method to run the specific label:

john w. vinson say:

Access is NOT designed to work over the Internet - which is what OneDrive is. Access depends on a fast, stable, reliable Local Area Network between the FE and BE, and between the FE and the user. Both databases will be open, constantly passing (relatively small) amounts of data back and forth; OneDrive isn't designed for such traffic, but rather for uploading, storing, and downloading intact files (e.g. documents or workbooks).

You will need to have your front and backends both downloaded onto a LOCAL (fast, stable, reliable) network.

linley meslier say:
Outlook Mobile is not an application that you can install and remove because it is built into  the OS. I assume you meant to say that you lost your data on the device when you connected to your PC to sync. I understand that you still have your data in Outlook on your computer that you want to sync with the phone.

To do that, I suggest that you start by deleting the existing partnership on your device and the computer.

Open Activesync on the phone. You will find that in Start > Programs. Click Menu > Options and select your computer name (Windows PC) and click Delete. Now you need to restart the device. so press the power button until you see a message box warning you may lose data by shutting down. Click Yes. Then press the power button again to switch on the device.

The next step is to delete the partnership on the computer. Open WMDC and click Mobile Device Settings > End a partnership. Select your device name and click End partnership. Restart your computer.

After the restart, connect your device to your computer to create a new partnership. Your data should sync back to your device.
