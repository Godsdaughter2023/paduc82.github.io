where are restore points in Windows 10 - Microsoft Community

tonypeacock1 say:

where are restore points in Windows 10

where are restore points in Windows 10

angelcarreno say: Hi,

My name is Angel. I am an Independent Advisor. Thank you for posting in Microsoft Community.

Restore Points are stored inside a hidden folder called System Volume Information. Windows keeps track of your restore points - you cannot do this separately - and you don't delete them when you make a new one.

It seems you may be confusing a restore point with a backup. They are two different things. A backup is stored wherever you choose for it to be stored - the best place is anywhere that's not inside your computer - and you can manually delete them as you make new ones, although most people let their backup software do this automatically.

A restore point, by contrast, is a copy of certain key system files are registry keys at a point in time, that are chosen to give you back access to the UI as well as the basic functionality of Windows, in case you run into trouble. The original idea was that if you have the use of the Windows GUI, you can use Windows' own tools to (hopefully) work your way out of the problem.

Restore points can still be useful, but with the wider availability of image backups, it's usually easier to simply restore a known-good image rather than try to figure out what's wrong and fix it.

Source:
https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/for...

Hope the information provided is useful. If the issue persists, reply here and we will be glad to help you.

Angel.

Dynamic contrast is an evil best left turned off.

.

In theory, adapting picture levels to compensate for less than optimal viewing conditions (such as strong daylight) could work - but the way it's used by manufacturers just exaggerates contrast at the cost of shadow detail. Like most "enhancements", it's not a real feature but just showroom polish.

Method 2: Run the Microsoft Safety Scanner:

a) Go to the Microsoft Safety Scanner webpage to download the scanner.

A power plan is a collection of hardware and system settings (like display, sleep, etc.) that manages how your PC uses power. The power plans you can use depend on the kind of PC you have.

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows-8/power-plans-faq

a)      Press the ‘Windows + W’ key on the keyboard.
