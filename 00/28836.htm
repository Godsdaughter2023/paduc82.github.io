NOTE:  This is a macro furnished by Adobe -- it is NOT written by Microsoft

The answer is YES to the question, "Should I have used a different method than <Save as Adobe PDF>."

  1. I clicked F12 (I did not use the File menu)
  2. Under Save as Type: I chose PDF (*.pdf)
  3. I then clicked the Options button.
  4. And clicked the checkbox next to Document structure tags for accessibility under Include non-printing information.

How do I solve for X for 3rd degree polynomial in Excel? - Microsoft Community

bmlui say:

How do I solve for X for 3rd degree polynomial in Excel?

How do I write the following equation into Excel to find X, when y = 7 ? y = 0.2308x3 - 2.6968x2   10.942x - 8.456  ????
mike middleton say:
How do I write the following equation into Excel to find X, when
One method is to use Excel's goal seek.

I entered text "X" in cell A1, text "Y" in cell B1, and number 1 in cell A2.

In cell B2, I assumed the missing sign for 10.942 in your equation is plus, and I entered = 0.2308*A2^3- 2.6968*A2^2+10.942*A2-8.456. So, Y=0.02 when X=1.

Next, on the menu I choose Tools > Goal Seek, or on the ribbon I choose Data > Analysis > What-If > Goal Seek.

In the Goal Seek dialog box, "Set cell" is B2, "To value" is 7, and "By changing cell" is A2. Click OK.

The answer is approximately X = 4.14.

In general, there may be multiple solutions, and the solution found by goal seek may depend on the starting value in cell A2, so you should try different extreme starting values, e.g., -1000 or +1000.

 For more precision, on the menu choose Excel > Preferences > Calculation, increase the "Maximum iterations" and decrease the "Maximum change." For example, enter 0.00001 for maximum change.

And, you generally should not use rounded values for the coefficients in higher-order polynomials. Your coefficients have only 4 or 5 significant digits.

-  Mike Middleton, www.TreePlan.com
