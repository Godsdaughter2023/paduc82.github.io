=REPT("0",MAX(0,12-LEN(G1))) & G1

marvin ma msft say:

Hi Susan,

To my knowledge, there's no direct email migration or service transfer channel between Godaddy and Microsoft. In this scenario, you may try to use IMAP migration. And it would be better for you to know that the migration is not to transfer your Office 365 tenant between the hosters. It requires you purchase a new subscription plan in Microsoft Office 365 and manually export & import mailbox data from Godaddy to Microsoft. For your reference, see Overview of importing your organization PST files to Office 365. The main steps are below.

1. Purchase the new Office 365 plan you want to move to.
2. Remove your custom domain from your old Office 365 subscription.
3. Set up your custom domain for your new subscription, and set up your users.
4. Transfer data from your old subscription to your new subscription.
5. Cancel the old Office 365 plan subscription.

For your questions, if you use your own domain as the email address, you can just add the domain to Microsoft Office 365 side and keep using it as your default email address. I'm a little confused about the second question, did you mean you want to receive the email notifications if there're any updates in your post? If so, you can just subscribe this thread and your Microsoft account which you used to create the post will receive notifications. While for the last question, since the migration only transfer your mailbox data, if the 3rd party apps are add-ins in your Outlook client, it won't transfer to your new tenant.

Regards,

Marvin
