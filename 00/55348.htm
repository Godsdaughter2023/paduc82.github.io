Here's what I think is happening:

1- Your power options are set to turn off your display every few minutes, to save energy. To fix this:

Control Panel -- Power Options -- Choose your power options plan -- Change plan settings -- Turn off display -- set a longer time period. I set my display to sleep (turn off) after 20 minutes of inactivity (i.e., no keyboard taps or mouse movement.)

2- Your user account is setup to require a password after resuming from sleep, as a security measure. I think this is a good idea and don't mind typing my password after my monitor wakes from sleep. But then, my monitor doesn't nod off at the drop of a hat. To fix this you'll need to sign in to a user account with Administrator's permissions, then:

Start menu -- Settings app -- Accounts -- Sign-in options -- Require sign-in -- Never (instead of When PC wakes from sleep.)
