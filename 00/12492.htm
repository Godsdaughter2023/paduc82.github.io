Note: The following steps will currently only work if your previous account was @Hotmail.com or @Live.com.

 

    In your browser, navigate to https://account.live.com/ManageAssocIds

    Under “Other email addresses” click the Remove link below your previous address.
    Click the Remove button to confirm the message.

  • Providing even more context and explanation for figuring out the right word choice
  • Letting you scan and proof your entire document at once

What's improved?

It's also redesigned from the ground up to be more accessible to people of all abilities. Key enhancements in the Editor pane include:

  1. Showing the proofing issue in the context of the original sentence within the pane itself (that is, it's much easier to reconfirm the original sentence than to hit F6 key and go back to the document canvas).

  3. Making the interaction model in the Editor Pane more similar to the Right-click Context Menu, so people can comfortably use them interchangeably.

Watch Editor pane feature in action

Availability

  1. Be an Office 365 subscriber on the Insider Fast level

  3. The Editor pane is available in languages with, at least, spell checking. Editor-specific Gold flags are only available for English at this time (more languages forthcoming) 

Getting started

  • Press F7.
  • Click Review > Spelling & Grammar.
  • Right-click any blue underline (grammar flag) or gold dotted line (style flag), and click See More:

jay sun msft say:

Hi Clifford Bergman [MATH],

According to your description, you want to remove the Holiday related calendar form your Calendar of Outlook 2011 for Mac. In this way, you can remove the holiday calendars in Outlook 2011 for Mac by following steps:

1. At the bottom of the navigation pane, click Calendar.
2. In the upper-right corner of the Outlook window, click in the search box.
3. When the Search tab appears, click Advanced on the Search tab.
4. On the Item Contains pop-up menu, click Category, and then on the None pop-up menu, click the holiday-related category that you want. And all items from the category will be displayed.

5. Click a result, and then on the Edit menu, click Select All.
6. On the Edit menu, click Delete.
7. When a confirmation message appears, click Delete. And then, the holidays are deleted and no longer appear on the calendar.
8. On the Search tab, click Close.

Besides, if you use the Office 365 for Business account, you can delete the holiday related calendar on the Outlook Web App (OWA) by right-clicking the calendar in the left calendar list pane and clicking Remove.
