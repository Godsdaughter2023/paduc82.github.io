You need to go to your new contact window called People before you can do anything about contacts and group contacts.......
 
Open outlook.com > top left, place your cursor over the logo Outlook > a down arrow will appear > click at the arrow > click People > here is your People window.

How to access a group :
In the People window, near top left, click at the word " All v " > a drop down box will appear with a list of your groups > click the one you want > all the contacts within that selected group are now on the left side > scroll to see the whole list.

How to add contacts to the group you have created :

Still in People window > click a contact you want to be added to the group > if you have multiple contacts to add, click the box at top left of the contact > continue clicking the top left box of each contact you want to add to the group until you are done> at the toolbar, click Groups > check the box in front of the group name > click Apply > all the contacts you have selected are now in the group.

Note : make sure you click squarely at the check box. If you click outside the box, all previous boxes you have checked will be nullified, and you’ll have to start all over.

How to delete a contact from a group but keep it in the main contact list:

Go to People > click the contact you want to remove from a group > toolbar, click Groups > un-check the box in front of the group in question > click Apply when done.

How to Edit contacts within a group

On the People window, top left, click the “ All v “ > you group is on the drop down list > click the group name > now you have all the contacts within that group listed > click one to view > click Edit at the toolbar to do editing......
Note : Edit option only appears at the toolbar after you have selected a contact.

Ok, after a lot of persistence and no help from Microsoft I eventually got my problem resolved. This is what I did.

1. From the Start menu go into 'Settings' and select 'Accounts'.

2. Now select 'Email & App Accounts'. This shows a list of your set up account.

3. If the account that you are trying to setup is listed select it and delete it.

Now you must set up the account again but this time use 'Control Panel'.

1. Right click the 'Start'  button (hold the 'Start' for a few seconds on touchscreen).

2. Select the 'Search' option and type in 'Control Panel'.

3. The .'Control panel' appear in the list at top of screen.

4. Select the 'Control Panel' and open it.

5. In the 'Control Panel' towards the top right hand side there is a small option 'View by'.

6. In 'View by' select the 'Small icons' option, this changes the view.

7. You should see a 'Mail (Microsoft Outlook 2016)' icon. Assuming you have Outlook 2016 loaded.

8. Select 'Mail (Microsoft Outlook 2016)' icon. This opens the 'Mail Setup' screen.

9. Select the 'Emails Accounts' button.

10. Select 'New'.

11. Enter your account details from here and you should get working.

If the account doesn't setup automatically try selecting the 'set up manually' option in which case choose 'POP3' when asked.

Hope this works for you, it did for me. That said I would not be surprised if it doesn't work but I think you have a much better chance of getting set up if you use the 'Control Panel'.

Good Luck...
