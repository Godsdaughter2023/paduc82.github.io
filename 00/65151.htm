It's on a per-program basis.  For the most part, programs remember however they were the last time they were closed.  For example, suppose you opened several Internet Explorer windows, all of different sizes, and then closed them all one by one.  The next time you open Internet Explorer, it will open in the same place as the last closed one, regardless of all the others.  The same holds true for folders, and almost every program on your computer.

However, if you have a shortcut icon to a program, such as on the desktop, you can override the way that shortcut opens.  Right-click it, then choose a size from the "Run" line (it defaults to "Normal window").
