  • Click on Start button, type “cmd” in the search box,

  • Then close the Command Prompt window

The idea was to use the above troubleshooting links to try to refine your symptom description.

Alternatively, go to the Toontown support and report the problem but I think you will still have to find out exactly what is happening before you can expect to find a resolution to your problem.   E.g. there will be an extreme difference between "constantly get disconnected" which implies a possible networking issue (hence the troubleshooting tips you were given) and your new description "booted from the game" (which suggests a problem happening internal to the game causing your session to be aborted).

According to this, you may already be creating log files which probably would make your symptom description much clearer:

http://toontown.go.com/help/technical/report-bug

<quote>

Your log files are created whenever you try to log in or play Toontown. It contains valuable information that allows us to see what may be happening to you while you are in the game.
</quote>

 

If you can use IE9 you could use its Developer Tools (F12)  Network Capture tool for an impression of what it sees when you get "booted from the game".    If you saw enough of them and noted their similarities and differences you might even be able to infer a possible cause for your problem.

 

Good luck
