3 Options - The first 2 options are simpler. 

Formula in cell G1:

=INDEX(C1:C5,MATCH(E1&"*ZZZ*",INDEX(A1:A5&B1:B5,,),0))

Formula in cell G2:

=INDEX(C1:C5,MATCH(1,INDEX(ISNUMBER(SEARCH(F1,B1:B5))*(A1:A5=E1),,),0))

Sounds like you are looking at or unintentionally creating "pinned website" shortcuts.   Those are created when dragging a tab to the Taskbar.   If you want to drag a URL to your IE Taskbar icon's jump list, either press Shift- when you drag it there or drag an already created Internet Shortcut (e.g. Favorite .URL) to your Taskbar; otherwise you may end up creating another pinned website.  Right-click the pinned website's icon and you may find that it also provides a special jump list and task list.  There are other differences which may be noticed when a window is opened via such a shortcut.  Outlook.com (aka Hotmail) is a simple common example of most of the features associated with a pinned website.

If you want to link to a specified Excel range, copy that range from Excel and paste it into Word using Paste Special, with the 'paste link' option and the paste format of your choice.
