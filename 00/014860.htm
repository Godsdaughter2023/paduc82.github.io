The simplest method for sending simple e-mails is to use the SendObject Method

The basic syntax is:

DoCmd.SendObject acSendNoObject, , , "*** Email address is removed for privacy ***", , , "Your Subject Text", "Your E-mail Body Text", False

Look at the help file for more details.

The last variable (False) is to send the e-mail without user interaction.  If you wish the see the e-mail a click on Send yourself manually (or while testing things until you get it exact as you wish it to be), switch it to True.

Private Sub ButtonName_Click()
On Error GoTo ButtonName_Click_Err
Dim strFirstName As String
Dim strEmail As String

strFirstName = Forms!FORM_NAME!FIRST_NAME_CONTROL
strEmail = Forms!FORM_NAME! EMAIL_ADDRESS_CONTROL

If IsNull([EMAIL_ADDRESS_CONTROL ]) Then
MsgBox “There is no email address to send to”
Else

DoCmd.SendObject acReport, "REPORT_NAME", _ "PDFFormat(*.pdf)", strEmail, _  "", "", "THIS THE EMAIL HEADER SECTION ",  & _ "Dear " & strFirstName & "," & Chr(13) + Chr(10) & _ Chr(13) + Chr(10) & _ "Here is your report.." & Chr(13) + Chr(10) & Chr(13) + Chr(10) &  _ "Many thanks" & Chr(13) + Chr(10) & Chr(13) + Chr(10) & _ "TEB1975" & Chr(13) + Chr(10) & _ "The report Manager", True, """"

Exit_ButtonName_Click:
Exit Sub ButtonName_Click_Err:
If Err.Number = 2501 Then
MsgBox "The report was not sent" & Chr(13) + Chr(10) & Chr(13) + Chr(10) & _
"The e mail doesn't send automatically - I have done this so you can edit the message if you want to" & _ Chr(13) + Chr(10) & Chr(13) + Chr(10) & _
"If you want to send it again make sure you press the Send Button in Outlook", vbInformation, "Sending to " & strFirstName  & " was cancelled"

MsgBox Err.Description

cbarnhorst say:

Please always give the make and model of your device.  Help us help you.

You can roll back to your immediately previous version of Windows in Settings/Update and Security/Recovery if you upgraded fewer than ten days ago.  That does not necessarily mean the Creators Update if that was not the immediately previous version.  If the roll back option is not present or will not take you back to the Creators Update you can use one of the options on the Settings/Update and Security/Windows Insider Program page.

Click on the Stop Insider Preview Builds button on the Windows Insider Program page and you will see a screen giving you options.  The easiest is the last option which lets you stay on the current insider build until the Fall Creators Update releases next month.  You will receive the Fall Creators Update but no longer receive Insider previews after that.

Or you can clean re-install the Creators Update.
