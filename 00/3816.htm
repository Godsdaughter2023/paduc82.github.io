john w. vinson say:

First off... data is stored in Tables, and only in Tables. Forms are just windows, so you can't store a paperform in a Form - only in a table.

I'd suggest that the simplest method would be to have a Paperforms table, related one-to-one to the Client table; or just include the fields relevant to the paperform in the Clients table. They won't take up any space in the database if they're left NULL.

If you use a separate table, you could display it in a Subform on a tab page on your client form, and only display that page when you want to work with paperforms. If you decide to put the paperform fields in the same table, you could likewise display those fields on a Tab page; this is a simple, no-code, no hassle solution.

If you do want a separate popup form you can do it, but it'll take a bit of code; post back if you're interested.

gabriel olteanu say:

I seem to have figure it out a way to fix this.

The incorrect VBA references to fake sheets proved indeed to be the cause of it.

I had to rename the problematic document, open it and do not allow excel to enable macros for it.

Then I had to save it as .xlsx. Only at this point VBA was no longer referencing incorrect sheets.

From here it was a simple step of saving the .xlsx as .xlsm again and now it works just fine.
