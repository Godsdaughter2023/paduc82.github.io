Hey Samuel90,

 

Advanced Configuration and Power Interface (ACPI) is an industry standard that defines power management features and other configuration information for computers. Some previous versions of BIOS don't support ACPI, and so the computer might not successfully enter advanced power modes such as sleep or hibernate.

cbarnhorst say:

What make and model computer do you have?  

The whole idea of a backup is to save everything in the event your hard drive fails.  Obviously you can't make the backups on any part of the computer itself because the backups would be lost with everything else.  So, yes I mean you need an external backup device, preferably an external hard drive (unless you buy a usb stick in the 128GB - 256GB range).  A 500GB external drive is cheaper than a 256GB usb stick so I suggest using an external drive.  You can buy cloud storage on a yearly or monthly basis (in the range of $100/year) but restoring even a 25GB file from the cloud can take 10 to 12 hours and for the money you can get a substantial external drive.  Most system backups require from 30GB to 120GB for a full image backup.  The rule of thumb is that the device should be twice as capacious as your backup file requires, at a minimum.

internet expert say:

The Xbox abruptly shutting off either means an overheating issue or a power supply problem.

Check that your Xbox has plenty of breathing room around it. It needs to efficiently circulate cold and hot air to stay running. Also clean any dust off the Xbox, as dust bunnies can accumulate on the ventilation holes and cause the Xbox to overheat. The Xbox One can also overheat if you stand it up vertically. The power supply brick requires adequate ventilation as well, and dust can accumulate on that.

If you have completely cleaned the dust off your Xbox and its power supply, and it’s still shutting off while gaming, then there is a problem with power. If it’s connected to a power strip or surge protector, the Xbox may not draw enough power and shut off. See if there’s a difference when you plug it directly to the outlet.

If it still shuts off, then your power supply may be defective, and will require replacement. You can submit a repair order through Microsoft or find a used, genuine power supply from a used games shop.

Read through this support page step-by-step to troubleshoot your problem in detail: Console Turns off Unexpectedly
