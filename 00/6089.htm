It’s not feasible to configure additional encryption for sending messages in Microsoft Teams, we have specific mechanism to protect these messages. However, the mechanism is confidential due to privacy concern.

Your conversation history won't be saved, so if you update, refresh, or open up Microsoft Teams on a different platform (like mobile), you won't be able to view your old messages. Also, we have confidential mechanism to protect the existing chat history.

It’s not feasible from my knowledge.

Microsoft Teams supports multi-factor authentication.

Your desktop app will automatically update (so you don’t have to). If you want, you can still check for available updates by clicking Settings and then Check for updates. Your web app will always be up-to-date. It’s just good like that.

    • Go to your Mail Settings/Your Email Accounts section. 
    • In there check under the Accounts you've added section
    • See if any of those email accounts are listed as well in the Outlook aliases section.
    • If you see one in both spots, remove the one under Accounts you've added section, it won't remove your alias even though it gives a warning about deleting it.

They then inform you that you need to pay money to register the software in order to remove these non-existent threats. Different variants may modify various settings on your computer, terminate programs or system services, or block access to websites.

    Try Malwarebytes Chameleon and a good adware remover such as AdwCleaner.

    As seen in some recent related threads (here and here) the attacker would try to trick an unsuspecting user to click on a link that points to a malicious/infected site (named 'servebeer' but it could be whatever as seen here), to download same malicious rogue 'security_cleaner' to (supposedly) remove 3 (non-real) threats named: Trojan-PSW.Win32.launch, HackTool:Win32/Welevate.A (being this one a real MS's detection name) and Adware.Win32.Fraud.

    Whenever you are in a similar situation again with any suspicious fake pop-ups while browsing, do not touch any browser window to close it or browse further, but [by courtesy of Stephen Boots]:
    - Use the key combination <ALT>+<F4> to close all running programs, especially the web browser, and then reboot the machine.
    OR
    - Immediately press Ctrl-Alt-Del and bring up Task Manager and forcibly end all instances of iexplore.exe (if using IE) or the executable for your browser for any other web browser. Then, reboot the machine.
    OR
    - Go to Start/Shut Down and restart the PC without touching any browser windows.
    Then, go to Control Panel > Internet Options and delete all Temporary Internet Files and Cookies. If you are using an alternate web browser, open the browser settings to do the same: delete the local cached files and cookies.
