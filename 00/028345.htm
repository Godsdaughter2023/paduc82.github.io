marshall barton say:

You're right.  I shouldn't have relied on a quick check in Help.

The kind of thing you are trying to do is pretty common and the general;y aggreed  best approach is to use the form's BeforeUgate event.  The problem with using a control's BeforeUpdate (or Exit) event is that when users do not know at the moment what to put in the field, they are stuck there until they enter a legal value.  What do they do now?  If they can't stop everything and go find out what value to use (maybe the person that knows is out sick), they either kill Access or enter an arbitrary value that passes the check and let's them get on with their job.  The result can be a corrupted database with the former action or bad data with the latter action.

To deal with that kind of procedureal issue, it pretty much boils down to allowing just about any field to be Null (meaning I don't know at this time) and creating an option to look for incomplete records.  For those fields that must have a value before you can even think about saving a record, the form's BeforeUpdate is the place to verify that the absolutely required fields are valid and refuse to save the record until these fields are corrected.  If the user can not come up with the correct values, they still have the option of undoing the changes and moving on to another task.

Hi,

The Safe Mode result tells you that a startup program is preventing IE from opening.

You need to disable startup programs and services, using MSCONFIG, which should make the IE problem disappear,  then re-enable those services until the problem returns, telling you which program or service is causing the problem.

The Windows 8.1 section of this article should be (mostly) applicable to your Windows 10 system.

How to perform a clean boot in Windows

https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/kb/929135

You may be able to shorten the process by looking over startup services to see if any of them seem more likely to be causing the problem. Testing those services first may eliminate much of the trial and error process.
