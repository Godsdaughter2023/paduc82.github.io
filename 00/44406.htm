e). Once this is done, restart the computer to check the problem fixed. If it doesn't fix the issue, then Right click on the same driver and select “Uninstall” then restart the system and let the system install the driver automatically on Start. Check and see if it resolve the issue. (Recommended step)

Method 2:

Check and install latest Windows 10 audio drivers for your device from Manufacture's website and check if it helps. You can search them using your device model on Manufacture's website. a. Download the latest and available driver from the manufacturer’s website.

b. Uninstall the existing sound driver from your PC through Control Panel.

c. Right-click on the driver setup file and click on ‘Properties’.

I am answering this question for 32bit Win7 Home Premium.
Right-click on the desktop and then click on Personalization.
Choose a "Basic and High Contrast Theme". Either (1) High Contract White and your desktop icon text will be white. Then select your Desktop Background.
Or (2) select "Basic and High Contrast Theme" Windows 7 Basic and then select your Desktop Background.

If you choose to go with Windows 7 Basic you will have to uncheck the box "Use drop shadows for icon labels on the desktop" by going to Control Panel, System, Advanced System Settings, Advanced tab, Performance box "Settings...". 

smokie23 say: There is a much easier way I found. (win vista)

- R-click desktop
-  click personalization
- click desktop background
- On the window that pops up, click "change background color"
- If you use a black or dark background picture and want white text, choose black as a background color.
- If you use a white or light background picture and want black text, choose white as a background color.

remolanot say:

What keyboard do you have, wired or wireless?

If you used wireless, the Num Lock (a.k.a Number Lock) light is off, to preserve battery life (Try pressing Numerical Keys). If does not work, try removing the battery and leave it for 10 seconds and place your battery and turn on the keyboard. If does not work, replace either the battery (if the battery is dead) or keyboard (if the Numerical Keys does not work).

If you used wired, then the Num lock lights are broken (Try pressing Numerical Keys). If does not work try unplugging and leave it for 10 seconds and plug back in. If the issue persists, replace your keyboard.
