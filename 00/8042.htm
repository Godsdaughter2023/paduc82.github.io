danwnightingale say:

Conditional formatting: compare date value in one column, to the date values in two other columns in the same row

I have three columns, all formatted as dates (MM/YYYY). 

 

I need to compare the date in the rightmost column (I) with the dates in Columns G and E. 

 

If the date in Col I is NOT BLANK AND it is earlier (less than) the date in Col G AND it is earlier (less than) the date in Col E, I want to change its fill color to yellow. 

 

I have a formula in Col G that does the same function with relation to Col E, and it works great. 

 

I CANNOT get the condional formatting formula in Col I to work.  The formulas I'm currently using are:

 

=AND(NOT(ISBLANK(G2)),G2<E2)                This formula works great

=AND(NOT(ISBLANK(I2)),I2<G2,I2<E2)          This formula does nothing

 

The values in cells E2, G2 and I2 are 02/2007(2/1/2007), 02/2007(2/20/2007) and 03/1999(3/1/1999) respectively, so cell I2 should be highlighted.

 

Any assistance would be greatly appreciated.  Thanks

e.j. gun say:

Hmm...your second formula works just fine for me.  Cell I2 gets a nice bright yellow fill color.  There must be something wrong with the way you entered the conditional format.  Try this:

  • Under conditional formatting, select "Manage Rules"
  • Select "This Worksheet"
  • Select the formula that is supposed to cover cell I2.
  • See if the "Applies to" says =$I$2
  • Click on Edit Rule...
  • Ensure that the formula is "=AND(NOT(ISBLANK(I2)),I2<E2,I2<G2)"

Sometimes when you enter conditional formatting formulas, Microsoft changes them because your active cell is not the same as the target range.  It's an annoying thing you just get used to fixing.
