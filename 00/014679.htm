a. user say:

My personal recommendation is to ignore Microsoft answers. They're crappy, generic, copied and pasted, and often do not solve the problem in any way.

A virus scan will take longer to complete as 2 things become more severe:

1) Your computer fills with more things

2) Your hard drive disk becomes fragmented

The best thing to do is to wait out the scan. Making it go any faster would make it miss potential threats.

You can defragment your disk drive, which will make access times quicker, by pushing the Windows logo and R on your keyboard simultaneously, then typing 'defrag.exe' in the Run dialog, then pushing enter. After that, select the hard drive that's playing slow, and click Optimise in the window that next appears.

Something else may also be trying to access the disk at the same time as the anti-virus, which will slow down access and transfer times as the disk tries to keep supplying multiple applications with data from the disk. (Like how a chef can make a meal for one person faster than they can make two meals for two people, or one very fat person.)
