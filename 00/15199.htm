The problem is that MS Word compresses the images when it builds the PDF, resulting in the images having a lower quality (looking blurry/distorted) in the PDF.  One way around this problem, is to use a program that can do lossless compression of images, when building a PDF.  One such program is IrfanView.  Here is one way that someone might do this, using IrfanView:

[1]  Carefully copy the text and images into PowerPoint, and get the PowerPoint pages to look just like the Word document.  To do that, just copy/paste the text/images from Word to PowerPoint, and re-format as needed in PowerPoint.

[2]  User PowerPoint to save the pages as JPEG images.

[3]  Open the JPEG images with IrfanView, and save as a multi-page TIFF file.  Then, in IrfanView, convert the multi-page TIFF file to a lossless PDF.

The end result of steps 1-3 above, should be that the PDF looks just the same as the Word document does.

In case you do not want to use PowerPoint, and alternative method would be to print the Word document on paper, and then scan it in, building the JPEG images (probably not quite as good of quality as PowerPoint, depending upon the quality of your printer, ink, paper and scanner).  Then go to step "3" above.

Here is the web site for IrfanView (it is free for home use):

http://www.irfanview.com/

I hope that the above information might be of some help.
