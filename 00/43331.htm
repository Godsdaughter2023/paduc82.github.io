hairyfool say:

If you are able to work with Vista generally then one option may be to switch to a local account.

Microsoft along with Windows 8 push you towards you using the Microsoft Account for authentication. This effectively links you to their online services automatically when you sign on to the PC.

If you switch to a local account you only sign on to the PC, not to Microsoft's online services. You will lose some of the features but if for example this is the only Windows 8 based PC you use the fact that it does not cross synchronise with other systems you are signed in to will not matter. When you then use a service it will use credentials manager to authenticate wherever possible.
