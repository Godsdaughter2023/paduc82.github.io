    el rey g say:

    Once your Zune Pass and Zune software are set up, you can easily transfer songs between your computer and your KIN phone via a USB cable and the Zune software.

    You control exactly what syncs from your Zune digital media collection to your KIN phone. You can set the Zune software to sync all media of a particular type (all of your music, for example), or you can select to have only the media you choose sync with your phone.

    Configure sync options in the Zune software

    Use the following options to determine how the Zune software will sync to your phone.

    ·         All: When you choose this option, Zune will automatically sync all the music, videos, pictures, and podcasts in your collection with your phone. Your pictures will appear in the Camera app on the Albums screen organized into albums.

    ·         Items I choose: When you choose this option, you must drag items in your collection to the icon that represents your phone to sync them with your phone. Any changes you make to these items in your collection will also be mirrored on your Zune software. When you delete something, for instance, it will be removed from your phone as well as your computer. If you want to keep things on your phone that you've deleted from your computer, choose manual sync.

    ·         Manual sync: When you choose this option, you must add and remove all music, videos, and pictures to your phone individually; any changes made to your collection will not automatically sync to your phone. You may want to select this option if you make frequent changes to your collection that you don't necessarily want added to, or removed from, your phone. With manual sync, items you no longer want must be deleted from both your phone and from your collection.

    The first time you sync, Zune will add all your music, videos, pictures, and audio podcasts to your phone by default, provided there's enough space on your phone. If not, you'll need to choose what to sync and what to leave off your phone. If you don't want to load all your media, just adjust the sync settings before you connect your phone.

    When you decide what you want to sync, just drag it from your collection to the phone icon in the Zune software and syncing starts immediately. You can choose a category such as an album or picture folder, or choose individual items like a track or a video.

    6220 classic: FM radio: Frequencies below 88MHz - Microsoft Community

    kabelgleichung say:

    6220 classic: FM radio: Frequencies below 88MHz

    Hello!

     

    I am wondering if the FM radio hardware of the 6220 classic can be tuned to frequencies 86 to 88MHz, and if there is an application capable of that. 

     

    Thanks!

    illinjah say:

    I am pretty sure that, that is not possible...sorry... 

    grschinon say: It is only possible if you're in Japan. The handset detects that you're there and adjusts the frequencies that the FM receiver can tune in to accordingly. In Japan, the FM frequency range is 76 to 90 MHz (because 90 to 108 MHz is used by three VHF TV channels).

    kabelgleichung say: So the pone's hardware is capable to tune to those frequencies. Where does this frequency restriction take place, in the fm radio application? It must be possible to change the frequency range somewhere, right?

    grschinon say: As I said, it happens automatically.

    If the phone "knows" that it is in Japan (it knows this from the base station it's connected to), it allows you to tune into stations between 76 and 90 MHz. If you're not in Japan it will only let you tune into stations from 87.5 or 88 to 108 MHz. There is no user-changeable setting here.
