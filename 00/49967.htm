Depending on the size of the Seagate, the SATA connection you use may Hide some of the special partitioning that these drives use over 2TB...

If this chip develops a problem you will not be able to access some of the DATA...

This may not be the problem at all... it could be the actual drive..

The problem is that you just can't take the drive out of the enclosure to check due to Seagate's manipulation of the partitions...

If your drive is 2TB or less, this should not be an issue...
