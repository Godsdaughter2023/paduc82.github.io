cbarnhorst say: That's why the usual advice is not to use them with previews.  Third-party programs that use scanners and dig deep into the system will cause problems with preview builds.  Defender is fine for previews and cleaners are not needed when new builds get installed every week (Fast Ring) or every month (Slow Ring).  Use the equivalent Windows tools where available.  Especially stay away from registry cleaners.  Third-party apps eventually catch up with all the changes to Windows during development cycles but in the meantime, they sometimes interfere with testing and often even block installation.
