how do i set up my computer for a wireless network provider? - Microsoft Community

cdmeidinger say:

how do i set up my computer for a wireless network provider?

is my windows XP computer set up for a new wireless network?

lemp say:
Unless you have an external USB wireless adapter that wasn't connected when you ran System Information, you don't have a wireless adapter.  That's not surprising.  Most towers (like your Dell Optiplex 760) don't include wireless adapters as standard equipment.

If you supply your Dell Service Tag, I can double-check your original configuration, or you can look in Device Manager:  click Start, then click Run, then type the following in the "Open" box and click OK
     devmgmt.msc
Click the + next to "Network adapters" to expand the category

My guess is that you will have only one entry under "Network adapters" --> Intel(R) 82567LM-3 Gigabit Network Connection

To get back to your original question, please explain exactly what you would like to do and what networking equipment other than your computer you already have. 

For example, based on the information you supplied, it doesn't look as if you have any sort of router now.  What is on the other end of the Ethernet cable that is connected to your computer? (Or is your network device connected to the computer by USB?)  Look for a label on the back or bottom of the device and provide the make, model, and version number (if applicable). 

Do you want to have multiple wifi devices in your home sharing your Internet connection or do you want to connect directly to the Internet using '"mobile broadband" (essentially connecting via the cell phone system)?

If you want to share your Internet connection, you'll need to buy a router.  If some of the devices that will share the connection are wireless devices, then you'll need a wireless router.  Note that virtually all home wireless routers also permit several wired connections.  A very common setup is to have a wireless router, have a main desktop/tower computer (like your Dell Optiplex 760) connected by Ethernet cable, and have other devices (laptops, iPhones, etc.,) connect to the router via wifi.  All of the devices connected to the router (by either wire or wireless) share the Internet connection and can share files between them.

If you want your tower to connect via wifi rather than by Ethernet cable (for example, the Internet connection comes into the house in a different room than the one where you'd like the tower to be), you'll have to buy a wifi adapter for the tower.  You can either get an internal one that would go in an empty PCI or PCIe slot, or a USB adapter, which you would just plug in.

gokul t say:

Hi Bluesong,

Welcome to Microsoft answers!

A hardware device might be keeping your computer awake. Firstly, I’d suggest you to update all the network adapter and display drivers to the latest Windows 7 compatible drivers. Make sure that you have the most current drivers all of your devices. For more information, see Update drivers: recommended links.

It’s also possible that the network adapter is waking the computer from sleep mode. You may disable the same:

To prevent a network adapter from waking your computer,

1. Open Device Manager by clicking the Start button, clicking Control Panel, clicking System and Security, and then, under System, clicking Device Manager. If you're prompted for an administrator password or confirmation, type the password or provide confirmation.
2. In the list of hardware categories, select “Network adapters”, and then double-click the device name.
3. Click the Power Management tab and then clear the “Allow this device to wake the computer” check box.
4. Click OK.

It is also possible that a program or scheduled task might be waking your computer. By default, Power Option settings do not allow scheduled tasks to wake the computer from sleep or hibernate. However, some programs might awaken the computer if these settings have been changed.
To prevent programs from waking your computer

1. Open Power Options by clicking the Start button, clicking Control Panel, clicking System and Security, and then clicking Power Options.
2. On the Select a power plan page, click Change plan settings for the plan that you want to change.
3. On the Change settings for the plan page, click Change advanced power settings.
4. On the Advanced settings tab, expand Sleep, expand Allow wake timers, choose Disable for both when your computer is running on battery and when it's plugged in, and then click OK.

Refer the link below for more information:

Sleep and hibernation: frequently asked questions
http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows7/Sleep-and-hibernation-frequently-asked-questions

Hope this helps!
