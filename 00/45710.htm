Are you sure you have IPv6 turned on? (I read one article where this was the cause).  To check go to Start / Network / Network and Connection Sharing / Manage network connections / right click on the Internet icon / and click on properties / Click Networking and make sure IPv6 is checked.  When checked on properties it should detect DNS automatically.  When checked on Advanced under IP Settings it should use Default Gatewate on remote network and have a check on automatic metric (but nothing in the box).  On Advanced under DNS it should have Append primary and connection specific suffixes and Append parent suffixes of the primary DNS suffix (and nothing else checked) - unless your system is markedly different than mine.

You won't be able to access ftp.microsoft.com through Windows Explorer - it takes a browswer capable of handling ftp (like IE or Firefox or Chrome) or an FTP client.  As an example of a free FTP client I suggest the following: http://www.tucows.com/preview/195147?q=FTP.  I realize changing programs to do an FTP isn't the most ideal solution, but it may be a workaround until we can find out what's wrong with IE that it won't take an FTP command.  If the FTP client doesnt' work, then you're definitely being blocked from FTP access by something in Vista (though I'm not sure what).  But at least we'll have narrowed down the options.

If these solutions don't help, then I recommend you repost your question in the Internet Explorer Forum at: http://social.answers.microsoft.com/Forums/en-US/InternetExplorer/threads where people who specialize in IE-related issues will be more than happy to assist you with your problem.  Be sure to tell them the steps you've taken so far so they don't recommend things you've already tried.  We've tried to help you here but this is really their area of expertise.

Good luck and I hope this helps.
