bossdweeb say: People all have different wants and needs, but firstly, do not go through any of the three
Program folders and start deleting stuff.
-If you want to reduce the number of programs you have installed use Programs and Features
to uninstall them.
-After uninstalling unwanted, or un-needed software run Disk Cleanup and defragment your HDD.
-You can also use a tool called CCleaner which does a bit more thorough job of 'cleaning'
files than Windows Cleanup does ( Using the registry cleaner is not recommended, and won't save
space in any event )

CCleaner - PC Optimization and Cleaning - Free Download

Here's a brief list of what you should keep, but there may be more -
Note that software is generally not what sucks up space. It's mostly photos, music, video and games.
-Adobe Flash, Reader and Shockwave
-Your Anti-Virus
-Internet Browser, if you don't use IE,  and related 'plug-ins' (including IE plug-ins ).
-Anything that is a "driver"  ie. - graphics, processor, LAN, audio, etc (Intel, AMD/ATI, Nvidia, Realtek, etc)
-Microsoft .NET and Visual C++  - possibly other MS or Windows stuff.


Once the above is done you will have to turn to your User folder and start backing up or deleting
your files from your My Documents,  Photos, Video and  Music folders.
  - I would avoid deleting files from the AppData folder as there is information in
that folder that may be important to the running of your software.

Finally - all the above is a short term fix if your HDD is running out of space as you will be
acquiring more data over time. You will need more external HDD space or another internal HDD
(if on PC ).
Optionally - Use a lot of DVD's to backup the data you want to save and try a Windows reinstall,
but don't count on that helping for long.
