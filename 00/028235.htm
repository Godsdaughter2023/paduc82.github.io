How to get the Windows 10 upgrade for your phone and other queries - Microsoft Community

stranger_hanyo say:

How to get the Windows 10 upgrade for your phone and other queries regarding the upgrade availability.

There is a widespread rumor going on that the windows 10 upgrade for phones are ending on July 29. 

The rumor is pure nonsense. The mobile upgrade remains free without any given given time frame(as of now) and is supposed to continue.

So how to upgrade your phone if you still haven't updated it? Just follow the steps here-

1. Download the Upgrade Advisor app from store. If you can't find it, use this direct link from your phone's browser- Upgrade Advisor.

2. Install the Upgrade Advisor app, run the app to see if the update is available, and then select Enable Windows 10 upgrade. If available, the app will display that the upgrade is available for your device. Now open settings>phone update and check for updates. The update for windows 10 will start downloading. 

3. Remember that the windows 10 upgrade comes in more than one part. So after you have upgraded to windows 10, don't forget to check for updates(settings>updates & security>phone update) and install them. Also updating all the apps after the windows 10 upgrade is over is a good idea to keep your device error free.

Now suppose you installed the upgrade advisor app and it shows the update isn't available for your device or it will be available later. Don't panic and follow these steps-

1. Check if your device is supported. The list of supported devices(as of now) is-

Lumia 1520, 930, 640, 640XL, 730, 735, 830, 532, 535, 540, 635 1GB, 636 1GB, 638 1GB, 430, 435, BLU Win HD w510u, BLU Win HD LTE x150q, MCJ Madosma Q501

2. If your device is on this list but you still can't get the upgrade, your carrier may be the one responsible. The carrier has to make the update available for your device. But don't worry there's a safe workaround for that(you'll find it in Step ).

3. If you still can't download the update or if you get an error code trying to download the update, it's time to reset your device. Use this link here to understand about the different resets and how to do them.

4. If the above step still doesn't help but you know the upgrade is available for your phone, give your phone a complete reset using the Windows Device Recovery Tool(use this link to know more about it) and follow the steps given at the top of this article to get the update. Hopefully it should work now.

If you tried everything given above but still can't get the windows 10 upgrade, there's a final way. Follow these given steps(applicable for all windows phone 8.1 devices)-

1. Download the Windows Insider app from store.

2. Open the app and sign-in using your MS account. You'll be greeted with a page full of warnings and other stuff(read it once though), move on.

3. You'll get an option to choose Insider Rings to get Insider preview builds for your phone. Use this link to know more about them.

4. Now that you have understood about the different insider rings, click on 'Production Ring'. The device should reboot(if it does not, do  a soft reset yourself) and check for updates by going to settings>phone update.

5. If you still don't get the update, repeat the above step but select the 'Release Preview Ring' instead. The device should reboot(if it does not, do  a soft reset yourself) and check for updates by going to settings>phone update.

That's all you should know about getting the windows 10 upgrade on your phone.

Note for users using unsupported devices(Lumia 520,525,530,625,630,635,720,820,920,925,1020,1320 etc.) - You'll get all updates based on the current build(10586 or TH2) if you have used the windows insider app to upgrade your device before. After that your device won't get anymore updates(as of now) and will remain unsupported. You can use the Device Recovery Tool to roll back to windows phone 8.1 or keep using windows 10(which is a lot better).

Anyways, if you have a question please reply back and if this post is of any help don't forget to hit the 'recommend' button!

Cheers!
