I think Arshad didn’t understand your question. As I am reading this question, keyboard shortcuts have been made and are included within the template file.

The problem is that Windows and Mac operating systems intercept keyboard presses and sometimes execute other commands and never pass the keypress on to the Word application. In your Mac OS X preferences you will have to turn off the Mac OS intercepts. 

One place is System Preferences > Keyboard  and check the box to use F1, F2 etc as standard function keys. In the same preferences pane click the Modifier Keys button to control what happens when you press each modifier key. Then head to System Preferences > Mission Control and check what shortcuts are in play there.

You can check the shortcuts in a particular Word document by visiting the Tools menu and choosing Customize Keyboard.

rob koch say:

It's hardware, though it may be aggravated by a driver.

Though I might have suspected the processor overheating as well, this usually occurs after a significant amount of time operating and is accompanied by an obvious increase in fan noise as the thermal detection in the processor attempts to cool it by varying fan speed.  If the fan is operating but doesn't change speed then this may not be the issue unless the fan simply isn't operating properly.

The next most common reason for these issues is RAM.  These are more difficult to determine and find, since they often act differently from one system to the next.  The variety of symptoms you describe do sound like this may be the problem though.

I recommend moving to either the support site for your PC or a hardware forum if it is home built.  There are people there with far more experience trouble-shooting these types of problems and able to recommend appropriate tools to find the offending hardware.
