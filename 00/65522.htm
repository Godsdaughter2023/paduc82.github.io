seithennin say:

not too low tech of an answer , but this may work..

you may have too many ghost entries in your device manager ..

first you need to remove all ghosted entries from "Disk Drives", "Universal Serial Bus Controllers", "Portable Devices", and "Storage Volumes" 

in order to see these ghosted entries, you need to do these steps..

1. press and hold the "windows key" then press the "pause" key , then let the keys go. (This will open your system info window)

2. click on "Advanced System Settings" on the left of the system window.

3. click the "Advanced" tab on the window that opens after step 2.

4. click "Environment Variables"

5. click "New" under "System Variables" at the bottom of the "Advanced" Tab window.

6. for Variable Name, enter "DEVMGR_SHOW_NONPRESENT_DEVICES"   (no quotes , but you must have the underscores between each word).

7. for Variable Value, enter "1"    (no quotes)

8. click ok.

now when you go to your device manager and select "Show hidden devices" under the "View" menu, it will show the ghosted entries.

remove ONLY the ghosts from the areas i have mentioned. DO NOT REMOVE THEM FROM ANYWHERE ELSE!!!

now try your drive and it should be detected correctly!

good luck , hope this helps you.

e. Once done try starting the store and check if you are able to visit Windows Store.

 

 

Method 3: If you've check all of the possible reasons listed and are still unable to find or install apps, I would suggest you to temporarily disable Antivirus security software installed on your computer and check if it helps.
