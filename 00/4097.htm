julie sheets say:
It appears as though neither task 6 or 7 have any successors.  Create a FtoS link between task 7 and task 10 to calculate a more realistic slack value.  I see a total slack of 14 days, not the 28 you show - but it certainly is more realistic that you wouldn't start a rollout until initial training was done.

trevor rabey say:

Task 6 does have a successor in Example 1. It is task 7.

But, as Julie says, 6 and 7 have no successors in Example 2, and clearly they should have because something must depend on 6 and 7 finishing, even if it is only task 10, the last and latest task in the project.

You don't need a custom total slack column. If you build the network model correctly the total slack as calculated by MSP is what it is.

This is why every task should have at least one FS predecessor and at least one FS successor, and probably usually more than one of each.

tom_bpc say:

Hi Steve,

The easiest (and customary) thing to do is apply a Deadline of 3/30/2016 to Task 10.  Then the TS values for tasks 1 to 10 will go from 109d to 0d.  This approach isn't foolproof for every project because your deadline may be less stringent than some other (longer) logic path - leading to the project completion or to some other deadline.  Total Slack only reflects the most stringent one.

Total Slack also becomes an unreliable indicator of the "critical path" under several other conditions (multiple deadlines, variable calendars, leveling delays....) 

Good luck, tmb

Hi Paul,

We suggest that you perform a soft reset. Press and hold the volume down and Power button until your phone vibrates and restarts. Note that you should not touch the device screen during this process (not even when the 'slide down to power off' prompt appears). After performing a soft reset try to check if the issue persists.

If a soft reset didn't work, we suggest that you perform a hard reset instead. You can reset your phone under Settings: Settings  > about > reset your phone. Make sure to back up all of your files before performing a hard reset. 

Windows 10

Go to Settings  > Update and security > Backup >More options > Back up now.

Windows 8.1

Go to Settings > Backup > Apps + settings > Back up now.

If performing a soft reset and hard reset didn't work, we suggest to use the

Windows Recovery Tool. Please see the following steps below.

Start by downloading and installing Windows Device Recovery Tool (for Windows 10, 8.x and 7).

When the installation has finished, start the application and the main screen will appear:

The procedure to restore your phone using Windows Device Recovery Tool is as follows:

  1. Connect the phone to your PC (using the USB cable that came with the phone).
  2. After a few seconds the PC should find the phone and you'll see the Phone Selection screen. Click your phone in this screen to select it.
  3. After a few seconds more the latest available firmware version for your device will be shown. Click the 'Install software'/'Reinstall software' button to flash your phone
Windows Device Recovery Tool will then download and install the latest firmware + OS available for your phone

Note! Your phone will be reset to factory status when using this tool.
