john mcghie say:
On the face of it, the answer is "No".  A Word document does not contain any concept of a "page" -- pages in Word are "made up" on the fly when the document is being sent to the screen or printer.

So you can't actually go to a Word document programatically and ask for "page 7", because it doesn't exist unless you print the thing.

However, for only 85 pages, you can very simply create the layout you want manually, using a table.

Start with a blank document.  Lay it out landscape and place a two-column table in it.  You can hide the cell borders later, but for now leave them turned on so you can see what you are doing.

Now, select the text from page 1 and paste it in the top right cell of the blank document.  Page 2 goes in the left cell in row 2, page 3 in the right cell in row 2, etcetera.

Each time you get to the right cell, hit Tab to create a new row.  Save often, Word can be unstable doing "interesting" things with tables.

Since you only have to do 44 rows, you will have this done in about two minutes.

You will need to create headers and footers to match the content.

Since you are experienced in booklet printing, you should be good from there...  If not, come back and we will fill in the rest of it for you.
