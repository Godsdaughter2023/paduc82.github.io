canadian tech say:

I have seen, heard and read too many tales like this.  The big lesson here is that allowing anyone to remotely work on your computer is a risky proposition.  Do not allow anyone to work on your Windows computer unless you have very good reason to trust them. Best if you can look them in the eye and you have reports of trustworthiness from people you respect.

Unfortunately, Microsoft in recent years has allowed technicians with less than the competence they should have to work on customer computers under the Microsoft name.

I had one client whom Microsoft technician remotely rendered her nearly new computer completely useless -- A door stop.  Calls to Microsoft to complain were useless.

I know that there are some issues with some brands and models that required some special software to be loaded prior to the SP1 update.  I believe it was a type of HP computer.

Once you have re-installed Windows 7, installed all the drivers you need, installed every update that Windows can offer using the including other Microsoft software option, loaded your STABLE programs (e.g. Office), do a defrag, clear out temporary directories and trash......

I recommend a step you will seldom ever see.  It is called System Image.  The program is part of Win7.  You are going to need about 4 blank DVDs.  I strongly recommend you do not use the most common type -- DVD -R's.  Instead use DVD +R's.  Start globe, All programs, maintenance, backup and restore, create a system image.  It could be up to an hour to complete this.  When done also create the System Repair disk suggested -- you only need a conventional blank CD for this.

The System image you create here will permit you in the future to re-build your drive in about 20 minutes to precisely its current condition.  It is a really great piece of insurance.

The true beauty of the image is that you will never have to use a Win7 install disk or activation code again.  You can re-build your system in much quicker time.  The time will depend on how much time has elapsed since you made the image, how many apps you have to install, and how much data you have to load.

When you need to rebuild your system, you start your computer on the System Repair disk, find your way to the option to restore from an image, and restore.  This will literally replace every thing there is on the hard drive with an exact copy of your hard drive at the time the image was done.
