bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

You can get the appearance you want in several ways but it won't have the effect you're looking for in the Sort dialog.

  1. Type Category 1 in cell A1
  2. Select A1:B1
  3. Go to Format> Cells> Alignment
  4. Select Center Across Selection from the Horizontal Alignment options
  5. Repeat for C1:D1

That will result in:

  cat1   cat2  
field1 field2a field2b field3a field3b
xxx 12 18 9 11
bbb 15 21 13 7

However, only the actual column headings [field names] in Row 2 will be shown in the Sort dialog... The program only recognizes field names in a single Row at the top of the data range.

Another option would be to:

  1. Type Category1 in A1
  2. Press Option+return to create a Line Break
  3. Type the first field name in that Category
  4. Apply Center Alignment to the cells
  5. Repeat in B1 for the 2nd field name in that Category
  6. Repeat again in cells C1 & D1

That will result in the field name row being 2 rows high:

Field1 Cat1
field2a		 Cat1
field2b		 Cat2
field3a		 Cat2
field3b
aaa 12 15 12 15
vvv 25 36 25 36

In the 2nd approach the columns will display both the category name & the individual field name in the Sort dialog.

Note that in the first example I specified using Center Across Selection rather than Merge & Center... conventional wisdom recommends avoiding the use of Merged Cells in working spread sheets because they disrupt the integrity of the sheet's structure, interfere with many operations & can lead to corruption of the file.
