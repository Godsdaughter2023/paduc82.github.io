suzanne s. barnhill say:
I disagree. Its being a table prevents it from being wrapped as Behind Text, but it is the wrapping that is the issue. This is standard header behavior: when you increase the size of the header, it pushes the document text down. It doesn't matter whether that content is ordinary text, a table, or graphics so long as it is formatted as In Line With Text. If header content is formatted as Behind Text, then it will be behind the document body.

This can usually be fixed by selecting the last paragaph mark, setting the space before and space after to zero, and/or setting the font size to 1. jay freedman say:
In 20 years of dealing with Word, I've never found that changing the font formatting of the final paragraph mark had any likelihood of corrupting the document, nor have I heard from anyone else that they saw that happen. There are other things that can corrupt documents, and those corruptions often are stored in the part of a binary *.doc file represented by the final paragraph mark; but that isn't at all the same thing as formatting the mark. If anything, it's even less likely in a *.docx file, which is really a zip file containing mostly XML rather than binary data.

In fact, you'll find the advice to format the final paragraph mark in the "Table at the end of a document" section of http://wordfaqs.mvps.org/BlankPage.htm.

jay freedman say:

The flagging of "workers" is caused by Word's grammar checker, not its spelling checker. This seemingly niggling difference is important because it means that the dictionary isn't involved, and the part of the grammar checker options that would let you turn off the flagging is temporarily absent.

When Office 2016 was released more than a year ago, most of the grammar checker was missing because the Microsoft developers had not finished rewriting its code. Today, for most users, it's still missing; only the "Insider" group have received it, starting last month. For background and lots of vitriol, skim the extremely long thread here.

When the completed grammar checker is finally released (maybe in another month??), you'll be able to turn off the "Possessive and Plural Forms" rule in the Grammar Settings dialog:

Until then, you're stuck with clicking the Ignore button for each occurrence -- or turn off the grammar checker entirely by clearing the "Check grammar with spelling" box in the Options > Proofing dialog.
