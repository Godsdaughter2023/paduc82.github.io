Win10 comes with its own antivirus (Defender) so there's no need to do without. I personally haven't seen Defender interfere with any other software. It's usually the third party AV programs that will cause problems. Some antivirus programs interfere with doing the Win10 upgrade, some others need to be uninstalled first then re-installed after the upgrade in order to work properly. 

Those "unsupported browser" or "upgrade your browser" messages you mentioned are typical false alarms caused by antivirus programs or browser add-ons that block scripts or other content from running in web pages (pop-ups, java, activeX, etc.). There is usually nothing wrong with the actual browser at all.

The problems with saving screen captures or copying from the clipboard generally could also be related to AV preventing the contents of the clipboard from being written to your harddrive.
