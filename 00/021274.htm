Thanks

anitaburke say:

I had a similar problem that I was not able to resolve, even after using an IF statement to account for blank cells. So, if this doesn't work youre stuck! Maybe.

If the cells are linked, I am assuming you mean that there is a formula in one column, something like

='sheet1'!a2

For a blank cell, this will automatically give you a value of January 1 1900, when your cells are formatted as dates. Grrr.

Instead, try

=IF(ISBLANK('sheet1'!a2,"",'sheet1'!a2)

in other words, use a formula that tells it, hey if its blank LEAVE IT BLANK. the "" is basically filling the cell with nothing. This is just the basic syntax for an IF statement, telling it what to do if its true, then what to do if its false.
