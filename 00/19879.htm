brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

There are no tools I know that will directly sync your Outlook.com calendar with your Google calendar and keep them in sync automatically in both directions.  You can subscribe to your Google calendar from within your Outlook.com calendar or you can subscribe to your Outlook.com calendar with your Google calendar.

To do the latter, log into http://calendar.live.com/.  At the top, click Share and select your calendar from the drop-down

Next, click "Get a link", the "Create" under "Show event details"

You'll now see several fields.  Select the "Import into other calendar applications (ICS)" link and copy it.

Now log into Google calendar.  On the left, choose the drop-down to the right of "Other calendars" and choose "Add by URL".

Paste the link you copied into the URL field, then change the string "webcals" to "https".  Click "Add Calendar"

When I did this, the contents of my Outlook.com calendar appeared in my Google calendar in moments.  However, This does not appear to be dynamic.  It appears to be an import.  I updated my Outlook.com calendar but the new event never appeared in the Google calendar.

stefan blom say:

You can store data in a separate document and then bring it into several files by adding INCLUDETEXT fields. You can force an update of such fields by selecting them and pressing F9.

Note that you can also type into the result of an INCLUDETEXT field and then press Ctrl+Shift+F7; this saves the modification to the included document.

Exchange Activesync Access Settings - Microsoft Community

steffenravn say:

Exchange Activesync Access Settings

Hello,


In my company I want to get more control on mobil devices users use to get their business mail on.
In Exchange admin center under mobile I can turn on "Quarantine - Let me decide to block or allow later" If I turn this on what will happen to users who have set up ActiveSync on their mobile will it be some issues?

Thanks

yangzheng_msft6300 say:

Hi Steffen,

Here are the answers to your questions:
1. If I have accepted one mobile, is it possible to block it?
Yes, it is possible to block a mobile device if you have accepted it before. However, it is required to use PowerShell to do this with an admin account. Here are the detailed steps:
1. Connect to Exchange Online using PowerShell with an admin account.
2. When an account is firstly been added on a device, the device will get a Device ID. If the device has been added as a quarantined device, the account will receive an email including the Device ID. Or you can use the PowerShell below to get the Device ID.
Get-MobileDevice -mailbox "username"
3. After the device has been allowed, you can use the PowerShell command below to block it.
Set-CASMailbox -Identity "username"  -ActiveSyncBlockedDeviceIDs 'Device ID'
4. And if you want to allow it again, you can use the command below.
Set-CASMailbox -Identity "username"  - ActiveSyncAllowedDeviceIDs 'Device ID'

2. Is it some where in admin center I can see a list of accepted mobiles?
The answer is no. It is not feasible to directly see this in the UI. However, you can also use PowerShell to access the list with the command below.
Get-MobileDevice -Filter {DeviceAccessState -eq 'Allowed'} | ft
