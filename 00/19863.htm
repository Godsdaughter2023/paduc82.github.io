Looks like there's a little confusion over the version numbers you're looking at.

First, let me point out the difference between Office for Mac and OS X: 

  • Office for Mac is a productivity suite by Microsoft that includes Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and (depending on the suite) Outlook.
  • OS X is the operating system your Mac runs to provide a user interface. OS X is the Apple equivalent of Windows. It runs your programs and comes pre-installed on your Mac.

You can find the version of OS X you're running by choosing the Apple icon in the top-left corner of your screen and then choose About this Mac. The OS X version always starts with 10.X.X. The second number represents the version of OS X you're currently running. 10.8 would be the latest version, also known as Mountain Lion. 10.5 would be known as Leopard. The third number represents the patch version you have installed.

You can find what version of Office for Mac you're currently running if you choose Word > About Word from the Menu bar at the top of your screen. If you found that you're currently running version 14.2.4, then you're up-to-date.
