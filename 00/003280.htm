FYI: Fix for broken Mail - People - Calendar Apps - build 10041 - Microsoft Community

andre da costa say:

FYI: Fix for broken Mail - People - Calendar Apps - build 10041

In this build, the Mail, Calendar, and People apps may be broken due to a licensing issue with the Store Beta. To get these apps working again, you need to follow these steps:
  • Open powershell as administrator
  • Run the command Get-appxprovisionedpackage –online | where-object {$_.packagename –like “*windowscommunicationsapps*”} | remove-appxprovisionedpackage –online
  • Re-install Mail, People and Calendar from the Store (green tile)

Source: http://blogs.windows.com/bloggingwindows/2015/03/18/windows-10-technical-preview-build-10041-now-available/

suzanne s. barnhill say:
  1. On the Page Layout tab, in the Page Setup group, click the dialog launcher (the small arrow in the bottom right corner). This opens the Page Setup dialog. An easier way to open the dialog, if you have the horizontal ruler displayed, is to double-click at the top of the ruler.
  2. On the Margins tab of Page Setup, beside "Multiple pages," click the dropdown and choose "2 pages per sheet" as shown below.
  3. With the page orientation set to Landscape, you will have two A5 pages on an A4 sheet. These will behave just like full pages in every way; you can add page numbers, page borders, etc., just as if they were separate pages. Unless you choose to display two pages, you'll see only one page at a time.
  4. If you are creating a booklet, you may prefer to select "Book fold." This has the same effect, and looks just the same when you are editing the document, but when you print, Word will print the pages in the correct order to be folded into a booklet.

Edit: This process has now been detailed at http://wordfaqs.mvps.org/2PagesPerSheet.htm.

ram kumar p say:

Hello,

Thank you for posting your query in Microsoft Office Community.

I completely understand your concern and feel sorry for the inconvenience this has caused.

This behavior is expected because the meeting request was sent to the IMAP account and it will always manage the calendar invites sent to that account. Servers manage ownership of the meeting when the calendar syncs with mail. Since the meeting request was received by the IMAP account, Outlook.com has no idea that this meeting exists and default account status doesn't mean all calendar invites go to one calendar due to the different servers involved.

However, as a workaround when we are accepting a meeting in IMAP account, you can copy the meeting to the Outlook.com calendar this could forward the meeting request from IMAP into the Outlook.com account and accept the meeting from there.
