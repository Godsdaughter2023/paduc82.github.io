edwinamellon say:

Word 2010, when adding title and page numbers in header, one clears out the other

When I go to put the tile and then the page number in the header for an essay, it doesn't work.  I put the title inand then the page number and it clears the title.  If I go back and put the title back in it clears the page number.  Also, the page number doesw not change when I go to a new page.  They are all page one.  What can I do to fix this problem?
suzanne s. barnhill say:

This happens if you're using Quick Parts/building blocks; each one replaces any previous one you've inserted. After you've inserted one of the headers from the Header gallery, instead of inserting a page number from the Top of Page gallery, place the insertion point where you want the number and select Current Position. Even simpler, if you just want a simple page number (1, 2, 3), is to press Alt+Shift+P to insert it. This will insert a PAGE field anywhere in a document. (Note that you must insert the page number as a PAGE field; you cannot just type in a number.)

 

This can be easier said than done when the Document Title is a content control. For example, say you are using the Alphabet header building block. The Document Title is in a content control, and that content control is centered because the header is set up with Center alignment. Now say you want to put a page number at the right margin. In order to do this, you must exit the content control on the left (using the Left arrow) and insert a tab character. Then arrow over to the right and insert another tab character. Only then is it truly safe to change the paragraph alignment to Left (don't ask me why, but I had difficulty inserting a tab before the content control if I changed the paragraph alignment first). This will align the content control at the center-aligned tab stop in the middle of the line. Place the insertion point at the right-aligned tab stop at the right margin and use Alt+Shift+P (or Insert | Page Number | Current Position) there.

 

In some cases, the default tab stops will not even be there. For example, if you use the Austin design, which includes the document title, left-aligned, there are no tab stops; you'll have to add one at the right margin and then insert a tab character and your page number. You definitely need to have the ruler displayed so you can see where the tab stops are (or aren't), and it's also very helpful to have nonprinting characters displayed (click the ¶ button on the Home tab) so you can see tab characters, paragraph marks, etc.

If you're using Lion or Mountain Lion, some users have fixed a related problem by ensuring that there is a Folder in your user Library called Library:Preferences:Microsoft

If I own Office for Mac Home and Student 2011 and I want Office for Mac Home and Business 2011, can I upgrade to Office for Mac Home and Business 2011?

Yes. There are two options for uprgrading Office for Mac Home and Student 2011 to Office for Mac Home and Business 2011. 

Option 1: Purchase a Home and Business upgrade product key (only available in select countries)

Note: Office for Mac Home and Business 2011 upgrade product keys will only work if you already have Office for Mac 2011 installed. There are no version upgrade product keys for owners of Office for Mac 2008 and earlier versions. 

Upgrade keys will need to be purchased for each install you desire to upgrade. For example, if you purchased Office for Mac Home and Student 2011- Family Pack, then three upgrade keys would need to be purchased if you want to upgrade all three installs. 

To purchase an upgrade product key online (not available in all countries), do the following: 

  1. Open Word (or Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook), and then on the Word menu, click Purchase Options.
  2. On the Microsoft Office Setup Assistant screen, click Purchase an upgrade product key online.
  3. Purchase an upgrade product key by following the instructions online. You will need to be connected to the internet to purchase. 

    Note: If you do not see your country in the country list, upgrade product keys are not available in your country and you will have to purchase a full Home and Business 2011 product key from a participating retailer.

    Figure 4 - Upgrade options



Option 2: Home and Business product key

Purchase a physical box of Office for Mac Home and Business 2011 online or at a retail store. Inside the package, you will find your product key.

After you have an Office for Mac Home and Business upgrade product key or an Office for Mac Home and Business 2011 product key, do the following:

  1. Open Word (or Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook), and then on the Word menu, click Purchase Options.
  2. On the Microsoft Office Setup Assistant screen, click Enter your product key, and then complete the instructions on the screens.
  3. To see any new functionality, close all open Office for Mac applications, and then reopen them.
