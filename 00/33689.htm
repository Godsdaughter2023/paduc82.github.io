We have been seeing black screen issues of several types after upgrading to Windows 10. The main causes observed so far are driver issues, output to unexpected port, and possibly also issues with the Lock Screen app. Please use these steps:

If you see the black screen or have a flickering screen before sign-in:

  1. Try connecting to different video outputs on the PC. Sometimes a change in driver can cause the video to be sent:
    • To a different Adapter: from integrated graphics to a discrete card or vice versa.
    • To a different output: from HDMI to DVI, DisplayPort to VGA, or any other combination.
  2. Try to boot into Safe Mode with Networking.

    • Press and hold the shift key while clicking on Restart at the login Screen (Bottom right corner) > Troubleshoot>Advanced Options> Startup Settings> Restart >>Choose Enable Safe mode with networking.

Hi JF,

You may access the Windows Recovery Environment by interrupting the startup of your computer. See steps below:

  1. Turn on your computer, once you see the loading dots, immediately press and hold the Power button to turn off the computer.
  2. Repeat step 1 twice. Twice will be enough for the computer to think that you are having issues with start up.
  3. Once the computer boots up for the third time, you will see the loading dots on the screen and the message Preparing automatic repair.
  4. Your PC may turn blank then it will show Diagnosing your PC, select Advanced Options.
  5.  In the Windows Recovery Environment, go to Troubleshoot > Advanced Options > Startup Settings to access Safe Mode.
