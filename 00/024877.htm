e.j. gun say:

3-D references are defined by a contiguous group of Worksheets; you cannot specify an "array" of individual worksheets.  For example, let's say you do a 3-D SUM from Sheet2 through Sheet4 of your Workbook.  Then you create Sheet5 and drag it to a new location between Sheet2 and Sheet4.  Sheet5 will then be included in the SUM.

It is always going to be StartSheet:EndSheet and will include all Worksheets in between start and end.

OneNote has my notes, records, stray thoughts, ideas, sketches, lists, plans, journals, grocery lists, and more. Notebook-based organization can be fine for small- or mid-sized collections, but it doesn't cut it for me anymore. I need tag-based organization, and OneNote falls dramatically short in this area. It was so clunky that I finally decided to give Evernote a try for the first time. Here's what I found:

  • Evernote beats OneNote in a couple areas: Tags and Web Clipping (and, I'm told, Mobile - haven't played with it yet myself)
  • OneNote beats Evernote just about everywhere else so far: Overall capabilities, multimedia features, drawing, visual possibilities, shortcuts, styles, speed, support, menus, and more.

The only problem is that those two features are the two most important features to me. Are they going to be significantly revamped in the VERY NEAR future, or should I spare myself the pain and switch to Evernote now? If I switch to Evernote, I'll probably abandon the Microsoft ecosystem (office, apps, email, messaging, phone, etc.) completely in favor of Google's, which I REALLY don't want to do.

I guess tags in OneNote were never supposed to be a method for organizing or structuring notes but more like different text markers. Maybe they are just a not-quite-finished-and-not-evolved-any-further-but-abandoned-instead idea like some other features in OneNote The first point in your list clearly shows that. I consider it as the most important one.

The point in Evernote is not the tagging itself but the search and filter capabilities. OneNotes tag summary is far beyond comparism. 

My suggestion as a first step or helpful workaround would be to revise the search function itself, allowing it to search for special characters. This way you could at least add a kind of tag line somewhere at the top of the note with a leading special character, maybe a hashtag. Something like #Tag1 #Tag2...

If the search function would be able (it isn't at the moment) to distinct "#tagname" from "tagname" that would be a first step.

The structural problem I was talking about is clearly seen with the Evernote-OneNote converter and how it handles Tags. It takes the 1st tag of every Evernote note and treats it as a section name in OneNote; creating a new section with that name when needed. Additional tags are added as "#tagname" inside the note which makes them totally useless as you can not search for them. 

To maintain the way notes in OneNote are organized (sections instead of tags as sublevel, but only 1 to 1), the Evernote importer would have to create copies of multi-tagged notes and place a copy in each corresponding section. That would be nonsense of course.

But I do not expect OneNotes basic organisation to be altered in a way that an additional level of organisation is added; so guidelines for Evernote tagging will never apply here. But maybe we will get something in the sense of especially marked search words (#tag1....), maybe like in Wordpress posts. 

Finally: I do not know THAT much of OneNotes internal data structures but what I am quite sure of is that Evernote is based on a (relational?) database with fields and indexes -- a natural environment for tagging. OneNote is based on XML files containing "numbered objects" (very simply put).

And thanks for the compliment about the blog although you do not understand its original language. Maybe try a Google (sorry, Microsoft) Bing search for "OneNote Secrets"; there is an English version of that book :)

Far from perfect, but it's a useful solution while we wait better tags on OneNote. With this "system" I can tag any paragraph I want, not only pages.
