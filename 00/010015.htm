Example of content in one cell is below

(F) Check the value of input XXXX |

(V) Verify the input to obtain the output YYYY|

(O) Test the unit before operating |

from each line i need to extract specific data. 

keratl say:

copy a sample cell to a blank worksheet (let's say you use A1), and delete all but maybe the last 1-2 characters of the first line, and all but the first 1-2 characters of the second line:

XXXX |

(V) V

Then in B1, put in the number 1

in C1, put in

=mid(A1,B1,1)

in D1 put in

=code(C1)

The result is that you are taking the first character in A1, showing it in C1, and showing the corresponding ascii code value for that char.

Increment B1 to the number 2, and you are showing the second char in A1, and the corresponding ascii code

and so on.

Use this method to identify which char is being used as a linewrap in your cell, then use FIND against that char.

Hi John,

Since automatic updates overwrites your top sites, I suggest that you create a backup of your top sites and just import them on your Internet Explorer in case it updates again and overwrites your top sites.

To create a backup of your top sites/favorites, follow these steps:

  1. Open your Windows Explorer and access your Documents and Settings.
  2. Look for your Favorites folder, right-click it and click Copy.
  3. Paste your Favorites folder in a new location. It can be on a backup disk, USB drive, or any other location on your hard disk which will serve as your backup.

You may also export your Favorites to a file. Here's how:

  1. Launch your Internet Explorer.
  2. Click the Favorites icon > Add to favorites > Import and export.
  3. Under Import/Export Settings, select Export to a file and hit Next.
  4. Select the specific folder (Favorites) that you want to export, then hit Next.
  5. Browse for the location or drive where you want your Favorites to be exported, hit Export, then Finish.

Now, in the event that your tops sites were overwritten again, follow these steps to import your favorite sites:

  1. Launch your Internet Explorer.
  2. Click the Favorites icon > Add to favorites > Import and export.
  3. Under Import/Export Settings, select Import from a file and hit Next.
  4. Select the specific folder (Favorites) that you want to import, then hit Next.
  5. Browse for the location or drive where your Favorites were saved, hit Import, then Finish.
