Hi,

A hard drive may crash due to a faulty sector or at times it could also be an issue that is something related to the hardware. That being said, we would need the answers to the following questions for us to identify what troubleshooting steps to apply:

  • Aside from performing a test if the external drives are working on other computers, what are the other troubleshooting steps you've performed?
  • Is the issue happening on a different user profile?
  • Have you tried checking if a USB storage device with small memory capacity has the same issue?

In the meantime, you can perform these steps for initial troubleshooting:

Method 1: Running the Hardware troubleshooter

You can run the hardware troubleshooter by clicking on this link. For future reference, you can keep this article as this also include a list of troubleshooters that you can use.

Method 2: Performing Clean Boot

Perform a clean boot on your computer to ensure that there are no possible conflicting programs that may run in the background that could cause the halt when using your external drive.

After performing clean boot, we advise that you enable each services after each restart on your computer while your external drive is connected. This is for us to see if what program might be running on the background that may trigger the crash specially when accessing your external drive. If all services are already enabled and still the issue persists, best to perform the next methods.

Method 3: Running the Disk Defrag

You can also do a Disk Defragmentation on your Windows to ensure that sectors (Memory on your drive) are organized and would be easy for the system to be read. Doing this won't be affecting your files though it is still a best practice to have all your personal files backed up.

Method 4: Updating your USB Drivers

There is still a possibility why your computer crashes when accessing your external drive as the USB drivers might be having a hard time reading your external drive due to possible outdated drivers. We suggest ensuring that all your USB drivers are up-to-date by manually downloading the drivers from your computer's manufacturer before uninstalling and reinstalling your drivers and firmware. You can also do these steps to update your USB Universal Bus Drivers:

  1. On your keyboard, press Windows + X and click on Device Manager.
  2. Search and expand Universal Serial Bus (USB) host controller.
  3. Next, right click on All the drivers and click Update.

When you connect a previously used File History external drive to a new PC, File History will try to match the new computer and user name to an existing backup on this drive.
