bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
Contrary to what is suggested there objects cannot be locked into any given position in a Word document. Unlike a desktop publishing program there are no
  • In Line with Text: Treated as though they were characters in a paragraph, or
  • Floating: Having some sort of Text Wrap applied, but still anchored to a paragraph

In order to do what you describe you would need to create a Section Break (Continuous) at the very beginning of the document. The document would then have to have Protection applied as though it were a Form with Section 2 unprotected. Along with other possible considerations that would mean that the Header/Footer would not be accessible at all to the user.

You may find this article helpful:

“How to set up a letter template”—how to create a letterhead, have different margins on the first page, set up letter styles, etc.

I would suggest, though, that you save your artwork as something other than EPS. That causes the image file & the resulting template & documents to be larger than they need to be. Additionally, EPS content will not print if not using a PostScript-compatible printer. You can export from Illy in other vector formats such as EMF or even AI which should be fine as long as a reasonably current version of Word is being used.

emgrace say:

Hi, original poster here. Thought I'd just share what happened so anyone watching this chat can see what worked for me and maybe avoid trying to contact any form of Microsoft department. I know that can take a long time and can get incredibly frustrating.

I don't know if this is the best way to fix the problem but I went and spoke to one of the live chat technicians at Microsoft and we tried a few things. This is what ultimately ended up working:

1. I created a second Microsoft account.
2. With that second account, I logged in and added my original account to the second account's family as a 'child'. This meant that my second account was the adult, and the account I wanted to make the purchase with was the 'child' within the family.
3. I then was able to, with my second account, manage the permissions for my original account. From there I changed my settings from 'only able to download free apps and purchases' to 'can purchase anything' - or something like that. (This was blocking me from also buying apps and games, not just Office products).

4. I logged out of my second account, went back into the account I wanted to make the purchase with and was then able to do so.

In short, you have to create another account or use a backup account, and then add the account you want to make the purchase with as a 'child'. You should, using your actual account, be then able to manage permissions.

It's incredibly frustrating and glitched and the Microsoft live chat guy I was talking to in Accounts and Billing couldn't work out why it was happening. It's something they have to fix but until they get around to that, maybe this will help? It's confusing and a roundabout way of doing it but it worked for me, so maybe it might work for others.
