The set up on the HD is different than that of the older models Zunes.  It was not designed to be operated on the dock without TV out turned on.  There is limited operation with TV out off as you have noticed, but not much.

To turn TV out on and have full command of the device with the remote, follow these steps:

1) On the device go to Settings>Display>TV Output and select the method you want.  The device will back out to the previous menu, you won't notice any change.

jay freedman say:

If the area is set up as a table, as Doug suggests, then pressing the Tab key at the end of the last cell in the last row automatically creates another row at the bottom of the table.

In Word 2013 or 2016, you can insert a repeating section content control.

This will wrap a box around whatever is selected when you click the button -- it can be one or more rows of a table, or one or more paragraphs of text -- and place a + button at the bottom right of it. Clicking that + button adds a new copy of whatever is in the box.

ken sheridan say: I can't give you a single example to follow, but by combining the methodologies from two of my online demos you should be able to achieve what you want.

Firstly take a look at NotInList.zip in my public databases folder at:

https://onedrive.live.com/?cid=44CC60D7FEA42912&id=44CC60D7FEA42912!169

Note that if you are using an earlier version of Access you might find that the colour of some form objects such as buttons shows incorrectly and you will need to amend the form design accordingly.  

If you have difficulty opening the link copy its text (NB, not the link location) and paste it into your browser's address bar.

In this little demo file think of contacts as your patients.  The opening form includes a combo box for you to select a patient based on their name and address.  Note that names only are not good enough as they can legitimately be duplicated.  I was once present at a clinic where two patients arrived, having not only the same first and last names but the same date of birth.  You can of course concatenate any other values with the patient's names.

Note that while the demo shows how to enter a new contact by typing their name into the combo box, don't use that method.  Use the alternative of a command button on the form to open another form in dialogue mode.  This allows two or more contacts with the same names to be entered.  In my case the form shows only the contact's personal details, so to include their appointment history you need to look at another of my demos in the same OneDrive folder, the FormsDemo.zip file.

In this you'd use the form opened with the 'tabbed form' button as your model in place of the contacts form in my NotInList demo.  This shows how to show related data per client, events and visits in the demo, in a subform on another page of a tab control.  In your case the subform would show the related data per patient from your 'facts' table in the subform on page 2 of a tab control, the main patient data being shown partly above the tab control so that it remains visible throughout, and partly on page 1 of the tab control.  In my case the subforms are in continuous forms view, but you could use a subform in single form view if you prefer, in which case you'd see one appointment at a time in the subform rather than continuous list.


PS:  You could omit the unbound form from my NotInList demo and change the unbound 'Go to client' combo box's RowSource in my FormsDemo file so that it concatenates the patients address or some other data which allows you to reliably differentiate between two or more patients with the same names.  To add a new patient you'd select <New Patient> in the combo box.
