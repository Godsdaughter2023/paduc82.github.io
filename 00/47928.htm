smiling cat-fab725ba-e75f-400c-9250-ecd9955c9c75 say:

Is this the play and charge? If so could be some oxidation on the contact points or the spring loaded contacts in the controller not pressing as hard. You'll notice the regular double AA pack has two coiled metal contacts at the bottom while the play and charge pack has six copper contact points with the controller having four spring loaded contacts. Squeezing  or tapping the battery pack to where it loses contact for even a blink of  an eye will cause the controller will turn off just the same as if you pulled the pack off. If you want to clean the contacts a regular pencil eraser works great. Also check the spring loaded contacts in the controller and make sure they press out enough. Have heard of it getting so bad even the rumble can cause the controller to lose contact with the battery pack.

-

If all else fails get some rechargeable AA batteries since it's much easier to stretch the other two controller contacts to maintain a strong connection with the batteries.
