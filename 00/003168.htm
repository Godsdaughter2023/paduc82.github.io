julie fka profjulie say:

Unfortunately when you add contacts to a phone BEFORE you create a Microsoft account on it, the contacts are stored as "orphans" in the phone's memory and there is no way to sync them to a Microsoft account afterwards.

In the past, I was able to find a couple of apps in the Marketplace that allowed me to move or copy contacts stored in one account to another account.  ContactCopy was the app I used and it is still available in the Marketplace.

The app was a bit tedious to use since I could only copy my contacts one at a time (versus copying them in a single batch).  If you look around the app Marketplace you might find similar apps to perform this function.

There's also an app for windows phone 8.1 devices that include an SD card slot to backup your text messages and contacts to the SD card, which you can find here (app is called contacts+message backup).

I've never had to use this app, so I don't have any hands on experience with it, but if your phone has an SD card and you decide to use the contacts+message backup app, you'd first backup these items to your SD card, then hard reset your phone, then set up your Microsoft Account, and then restore these items from the SD card.

There are other methods you could use to copy the contacts from the phone and then resave them on the phone so they would be stored and synced back to your Microsoft account, such as sending each contact to yourself as a vcard (this would be require opening up each contact individually, and then sharing the contact to an email address, or some other shareable place.....a bit tedious to do, but would work if your only alternative.
