Sub zoomit()

Hi,


Try this macro


Sub Highlight()
Dim LastRow As Long, c As Range
Dim MyRange As Range
LastRow = Cells(Cells.Rows.Count, "I").End(xlUp).Row
Set MyRange = Range("I1:I" & LastRow)
For Each c In MyRange
If UCase(c.Value) = "DUE" Then
    c.EntireRow.Interior.Color = vbYellow
ElseIf UCase(c.Value) = "OVERDUE" Then
    c.EntireRow.Interior.Color = vbRed
Else
    c.EntireRow.Interior.Color = xlNone
End If
Next
End Sub

e.j. gun say:

You don't need a macro for this.  You can use Conditional Formatting.  Let's say you have data in rows 2:100 (Row 1 being the header row).  Then do the following steps to highlight the rows as you have requested:

 

  1. Select your data (let's say A2:I100).
  2. On the Home tab, select Conditional Formatting --> New Rule...
  3. In the dialog box, select "Use a formula to determine which cells to format"
  4. In the text box below "Format values where this formula is true:", enter the following formula:  =($I2="Due") (The $ before the I is important!)
  5. Click the "Format..." button
  6. Set the fill color to yellow, then click OK
  7. Click OK
  8. All the rows with "Due" in Column I will be highlighted.
  9. Repeat steps 2 through 7 for "Overdue", using a red fill color.

HTH,


Eric

mouserider---01 say:

The numbers are the satellite numbers, in the GPS constellation, there are a total of between 24 and 32 medium orbit satellites.

 

They are numbered 1 to 32.

 

The bars are the signal strength. When they are just outlines, it means that you're receiving data but the full ephemeris has not been received and so a lock to that satellite has not been established. Once the bar is solid black, it means that satellite is now locked. 

 

You require 3 satellites to obtains a 2D lock (lat/long) and 4 to obtain a 3D lock (lat/long/alt).  

 

On Sports Tracker, the second number doesn't indicate the number of satellite your phone sees but rather the maximum number of satellites your phone will make use of. 

 

Your car's window might have a coating which is blocking the GPS signals. Some cars, like some VW/Audi models have that problem, though the sunroof is often not blocked and there is a small section of the window just above and behind the rear-view is often unblocked.

 

If you have a signal blocking window, putting it right next to the window will actually make reception worse while putting it further away might work better because the signal is coming in from either another window or through a deliberate window that has been left open for use of things like GPS. 

 

A few facts here which I hope will help and not confuse you further: 

 

First thing, remember, the N95 GPS works better if the slider is opened to expose the keypad because the GPS antenna is under the keypad.

 

Next, I don't believe the tracking/locking process continues when the screensaver is active, for me personally, I keep the phone active just to be sure.

 

For A-GPS, you should notice a brief data connection indicated by the two-arrow icons when you first fire up any app that uses the GPS. Now, sometimes, certain carriers have slow or busy DNS servers and I've found A-GPS to fail to return the ephemeris data because it can't reach the server.

 

This will result in a longer time to lock since it will have to download the ephemeris data from the satellites, which transmit at 50 bit/s, pretty slow and getting that information could take up to one minute or more depending on when you "tune in" since the messages are repeated at 30 second intervals and if your receiver misses it or doesn't get all of it, it has to wait for the next repeat.

 

Best way to confirm that your GPS is working is to stand in an open space, clear view of a clear sky, without any potential interference sources nearby, like high-tension power lines, radio towers, and the like. This will help possibly eliminate possible external factors that may be preventing your GPS from functioning correctly.

 

Just keep an eye on the Satellite Status indicator and keep the phone awake by pressing a key occasionally. Don't cover the keypad with your hand.

 

There is always a possibility that your phone's GPS may be faulty, though rare, it could happen. The "extreme" precautions I've listed above hopefully will help you eliminate other factors to help you troubleshoot what exactly the problem might be.

 

If you get a lock using the above "extreme" precautions, just remember a few helpful hints for everyday GPS use.

 

- Locks in urban areas will take longer, sometimes may not be possible to get a lock. This is due to the "canyon effect" where the satellite signals are reflecting or blocked and have no clear path to you.

 

- Some cars have windows which are signal blocking and will prevent GPS signals from working properly. GPS signals are much weaker than cell-tower transmissions or radio transmissions.

 

Good luck.
