mrwunderbar---01 say:

Transfer SMS from old Symbian to WP7 - A Workaround

Hey Folks,

 

I recently bought a new Lumia 800. Unfortunately it is not possible to migrate SMS from my old Nokia 5800 to my new device directly.

 

The information I've gathered led to the conclusion, that isn't possible to access the SMS storage of WP7. Even developers can't. Consequently there is no direct migration tool (yet).

 

But my researches led to a workaround solution, which makes it possible to have your old SMS accessible on your new phone.

 

The general idea is to export the old text messages and store them in the windows live account as e-mails.

 

How to go about it:

  • Connect your old phone via Nokia Suite with your PC
  • Use the Back-Up Function, which creates an "nbu" File, that can be used to restore your data
  • Download NBU-Explorer (http://sourceforge.net/projects/nbuexplorer/)
  • Open the Back-Up File (nbu) with the NBU Explorer
  • Extract your SMS-Folders as VMG Files
  • Download ABC Amber Nokia Converter
  • Open the extracted VMG Files with the converter
  • Select all and click on "Save selected messages as EML..."
  • Open Windows Live Mail (http://explore.live.com/windows-live-essentials)
  • Log in to you Hotmail/Live Account
  • Create a new folder "SMS" (or how whatever is convenient for you)

    • Now you can open the Hotmail Account on your Windows Phone and read your old SMS.

    Maybe you have to change the syncing options, that all messages are being downloaded. The default setting is that only messages from the past 7 days are downloaded

     

    I know that this is not very elegant at all, but if you have important SMS that you need to access on your mobile device, then this is the best solution. Or is there a better one? Feel free to comment!

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
Not exactly... you need to format the cells as Text before you paste in the data. Changing the formatting after-the-fact will not revert to expression as a fraction.

Additionally, you need to use Edit> Paste Special as either Text or Unicode Text. The formatting will be revised to Date if you just Paste normally.
