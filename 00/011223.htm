Emerging text from a specific location on a slide in PowerPoint - Microsoft Community

wilmbren say:

Emerging text from a specific location on a slide in PowerPoint

This is a difficult topic to search for so I apologize if this is already answered elsewhere.

I am trying to get a text box that is not visible already on the slide, to emerge upon clicking. 
For example, the slide contains a list of words, I want to be able to click and the definition of that word emerges and fills the screen, and it is obvious that the definition originated from that specific word. And then upon clicking again, the definition disappears and the original slide with all of the words resumes. Then sequentially, clicking would cycle through the rest of the words and their underlying definitions.

Any help with this matter would be greatly appreciated.
sandy johnson say:

You can do this by creating Custom Shows. While there are others (e.g., using Tool Tips in Hyperlinks), this technique simulates a pop up but it's really seamless navigation between 2 slides. Follow these steps:

 

  1. Create the Launch slide, e.g. your list of words.
  2. Duplicate that slide. Without moving any of the text that you copied over from Step One, add the text box with the definition in such a way that "it is obvious that the definition originated from that specific word"
  3. Hide the new slide (right click on the thumbnail in the navigation pane on the left, choose Hide Slide - you can tell if a slide is hidden because the thumbnail number has a gray line over it) Hiding the slide is very important.
  4. Create a Custom show from your new slide (so remember the slide number): go to Slide Show > Custom Slide Show >  Custom Shows
  5. In the Custom Show Dialog, Click on New
  6. Give the Custom Show a name, like "Word" definition
  7. In the Slides in presentation well at the left, select the new slide that you create in Step 2 (if it's hidden -- which it should be -- the slide number will have parentheses around it).
  8. After you've selected the slide, hit Add to move it to the Right well
  9. Click OK > Close
  10. Return to your original Launch slide (the one in Step One)
  11. With the Text box that you want to hyperlink from (there are a few ways to do this, this is just one way) selected, go to Insert > Action > Hy

     

    Now when you click on the word on the launch page, the definition will appear to "pop up." When you've finished reading the definition, advance the slide and it will return to the Launch slide.

     

    Hope this helps.

    To use ScreenTips (I think it's called ToolTips in older versions), follow these steps:

     

    1. On "slide 1" Select the text that you want to define
    2. Right click, Choose Hyperlink
    3. In the insert Hyperlink dialog box, click on the ScreenTip button
    4. Type in the definition in the Screen Tip Dialog, then hit OK (just once)
    5. Stay in the Hyperlink dialog and from the menu at the left, choose Place in this document
    6. From the Slide well choose the same slide (in this case "slide 1") as the text that your are defining can be found (you don't want to leave this slide if you accidentally push on the hyperlink).
    7. Hit OK.

    Now when you hover over the hyperlinked word a ScreenTip, the definition will pop up. If you accidentally click on the link you won't go anywhere because of the hyperlink you set in Step 6.
