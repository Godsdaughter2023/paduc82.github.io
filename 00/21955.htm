That's a very broad question, so you will get broad answers.

The first things you should work on are:

* Defining the requirements, documenting them, discussing them with stakeholders and getting sign-off.

* Design the database that fits these requirements.

The things you should NOT work on include:

* Entering data into tables

* Designing forms and reports.

gord dibben say:

See if this helps.

You must do a tiny bit of prep work on Sheet2.  See notes in macro.

Sub Auto_Fill_Formula()
'assumes header row in both Sheets
'assumes Sheet2 has your formulas in A2:E2
    Dim lrow1, lrow2 As Long
    lrow1 = Sheets("Sheet1").Range("A" _
                 & Rows.Count).End(xlUp).Row
    lrow2 = Sheets("Sheet2").Range("A" _
                 & Rows.Count).End(xlUp).Row
'lines below delete row 3 and below
'code rebuilds formula rows based on number of Sheet1 rows
    With Sheets("Sheet2")
        .Range("A3:E" & lrow2).ClearContents
        .Range("A2:E" & lrow1).FillDown
    End With
End Sub
