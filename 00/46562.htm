    a. user say:

    Oh yeah...

    Looking at another Google search results, in addition to deleting the "rocket" oriented Tasks, they mention using Autoruns to delete any "rocket" oriented startup items you might have:

    https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=mKchQdXjuPA

    This is a good idea and you can get Autoruns here:

    https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/sysinternals/bb963902.aspx

    Autoruns is the Windows msconfig program on steroids.

    Just download it, unzip it to our desktop or someplace you can find it, launch Autoruns.exe, maximize the window so you can see everything and wait for it to finish populating the list of entries.

    When Autoruns is finished scanning your system, it will say "Ready" at the bottom left corner.  Autoruns can be a little intimidating at first if you have never see it before  since it displays a lot of information.  You are really only interested in a couple sections.

    Look at the Everything and Logon tabs and you can also see your Scheduled Tasks.  You can also click the "Find' button (looks like binoculars) and search for anything to do with "rocket" and delete them.

    Items that show up in a shrimpy color are "unsigned" entries from third party programs you have installed which are usually not a problem.  The manufacturer just did not bother to digitally "sign" their files appropriately.

    Items that show up in a yellow color are entries that point to a file, path, location that does not exist so they are a little suspicious since they probably don't make sense any more.  I usually just delete the yellow ones but sometimes they come back.

    Autoruns doesn't install anything on your system - it just runs on demand so you can keep it around or delete it when you are done (nothing to uninstall).
