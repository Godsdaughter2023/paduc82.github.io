It's rather obscure, but you can use the "Four Part Format" to do this. The Format property of a textbox can have four different formats, in quotes, separated by semicolons; these are applied to positive, negative, zero and Null values respectively. So you could use a Format of

"#.000";"\-#.000";"";""

to show positive numbers as (e.g.) 123.456, negative as -876.000, zero and Null as blank. Just leave off the .000 if you want to suppress digits after the decimal.
