    hansv mvp say:

    Cell width is measured in characters - 1 is the width of one digit (0 ... 9) in the standard font.

    The minimum increment is 1 pixel. How many pixels fit in 1 character width depends on the standard font and probably on the font settings in Windows.

    On my PC, the standard font is Calibri 11. The width of a digit in this font is 7 pixels. So column width increments in steps of 1/7 character. For example, column width can be

    10 characters = 10.000

    10 1/7 characters ~ 10.14

    10 2/7 characters ~ 10.29

    10 3/7 characters ~ 10.43

    etc. That's why 10.33 is changed to 10.29.

    Now, if I change the standard font to Calibri 12, the width of a digit is 8 pixels, so column width increments in steps of 1/8 character:

    10 = 10.000

    10 1/8 ~ 10.125

    10 2/8 ~ 10.25

    10 3/8 ~ 10.38

    etc. So 10.33 would change to 10.38.
