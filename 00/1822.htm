Here's how I fixed it:

When the Windows Defender notification pops up, click on the notification. It will prompt you to "Disinfect" all copies of it Win32/SupTab!blnk.  Instead, click on "Action" and a drop down menu will let you select "Remove".  Windows Defender will only let you select and remove one copy at a time.  Select "Remove" and hit the "Apply" button and it will remove one copy.  When the notification pops up again, repeat the steps and remove another copy, repeating until all are removed.
