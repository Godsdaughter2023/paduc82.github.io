jllatham say:

The .FormulaR1C1 operation provides a way to really fill groups of cells with a formula quickly, because quite often the R1C1 format of a formula is the same for all of the cells in a column, although they look different in 'standard' notation.

 

Consider the following 3 formulas that might be in column C on a sheet in C2, C3 and C4

=A2+B2

=A3+B3

=A4+B4

 

But in R1C1 notation, they are all the same!

=RC[-2]+RC[-1]

 

So a programmer could set all of the formulas in a big bunch of cells easily like this:

Range("C2:C200599").FormulaR1C1 = "=RC[-2]+RC[-1]"

and wow! 200,598 cells get a formula assigned to them with just one command.
