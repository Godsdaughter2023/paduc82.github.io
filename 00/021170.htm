    510sami say:

    MicroSD card

    sven johannsen say:

    If you have a Samsung Focus it won't void your warranty and the instructions are provided in the user manual.

    Couple of things to understand.

    The instructions say to use a certified SD card - there essentially are none. Not that there aren't any that work, but there are none certified by Samsung, MS or any SD card vendor for WP7. That may not be entirely true, there were some reports of some Sandisk cards found at some AT&T stores that indicated WP7 compatibility on the package.

    Second, and related to the first. There have been good and bad experiences with replacing cards. Like most drugs, if you read the possible side affects they include many nasty possibilities, often including death. There is the potential that you get your phone into a state only a trpair center can restore from, i.e. bricking.

    Third, the SD card is an integral part of the memory of the phone. Removing one and replacing it will require a complete rest of the phone. It will be like it came out of the box the first time. Everything will need to be re set up. Not as hard as it sounds, but you need to be aware of that. Be aware that if you remove a card, and insert another and it doesn't work right, putting the old one back isn't an automatic fix. The process of trying the new one has made it forget the old one and it needs to be re-integrated. You do at least know it did work before.

     

    That is all for the Focus, the only device with an intentionally accessible SD slot and instructions from the OEM. No other WP& that I'm aware of has an SD slot that is intentionally user accesible, i.e. unscrewing things, prying off panels, breaking seals...voiding warranties.

    Hi Tricia,

    1. IF your account was unused for a few years  Inactivity  would apply.

    To keep your Outlook.com account active you must sign in to the account at least once every 365 days.

    After 365 days of inactivity, your email will be deleted and cannot be recovered.

    After 2 years of inactivity, your Microsoft account will be deleted and cannot be recovered.

    2. Have you a Back up of all your important email data that you can access?

    If your data is important it is prudent to back it up, especially if it is in the cloud and not under your control.

    Backing up your important Outlook.com email data

    Unless you have a back up made of the important email data it will be unlikely that you will be able to retrieve anything.
