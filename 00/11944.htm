You seem to have cued to the idea that you don't "purchase" 365, you just rent it.

There still is a one-time payment Office license that you can "buy". That is Office 2016 (soon to be 2019) Home and Student.

Office 365 and Windows 10 default to using OneDrive. You actually have to work at it to not use them.

Office 365/2016 can read many old formats. But as part of the default "security" it has blocked access to some of the older, less secure formats. Go to File menu > Options command > Trust Center option > Trust Center Settings ... button > File Block Settings. In there you can modify the default blocks.

Yes, there are "free" versions of the Office applications. They are associated with your OneDrive.. If you log in to https://onedrive.live.com/, using your Office email account and password. If you want to create new files in the onlie version you just click on the "New" button on the ribbon.

The online versions of the applications are "feature limited" (aka dumbed down). They only have basic features. They can be used to edit files created in the desktop versions. They will not remove unsupported features, just not do anything with them.
