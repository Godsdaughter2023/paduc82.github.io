rajapss say:

Why does my computer do a disk check every time i start it up? am on xp pro with sp3

Why does my computer do a disk check every time i start it up am on xp pro with sp3.Its really annoying to wait until it does the disk scan every  time i open the pc.Any help o fix this problem is highly appreciated.Thanks in advance.


pranavmishra5582 say:

Hi Raja,

 

You could face this issue when there is improper shutdown of your computer or if your hard disk’s dirty bit is set. When a volume's dirty bit is set, autochk automatically checks the volume for errors the next time the computer is restarted. If you have any external device connected to your computer then disconnect it.

 

Follow the steps given to clear dirty bit on your drive.

 

a)        Click on start, go to All Programs.

b)       Click on Accessories, click on Command Prompt.

 

           Type fsutil dirty query x:. This queries the drive, and more than likely it will tell you that it is dirty. Note x: is the drive you want to clear, usually c:

 

c)        Next, type chkntfs /x x:. The /X tells Windows to NOT check that particular drive on the next reboot. Now manually reboot your computer, it should not do a chkdsk and should take you directly to normal Windows login.

 

d)       Once Windows has fully loaded, bring up another Command Prompt and enter chkdsk x: /f /r. Reply Y if asked if you want this to happen on the next boot. This should take you through 5 stages of the chkdsk scan on the next boot and will unset that dirty bit. Be patient as this can take an hour or more depending on the size of the hard drive or partition.

 

e)        Once booted back to Windows, open another Command Prompt and query the dirty bit again by enteringfsutil dirty query x: and Windows will confirm that the dirty bit is not set on that drive.

You can refer to the link given below.

 

 

Fsutil: dirty

 

http://www.microsoft.com/resources/documentation/windows/xp/all/proddocs/en-us/fsutil_dirty.mspx?mfr=true 


Write to us with the status if the issue. We will assist you ahead.
