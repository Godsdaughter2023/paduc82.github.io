Why Xbox One controllers break so easily - Microsoft Community

bangplaysgames say:

Why Xbox One controllers break so easily

After having 2 controllers stop working out of the blue (one of which was an Elite which I totally babied), I decided to pop them open and see why they aren't working.  I discovered that there are 2 PCBs that are connected by 2 pin headers each.  These pin headers are connected to the PCBs with surface soldered pins.  On both controllers, one side of these pin headers has broken its solder joints and completely disconnected from the PCB.  This is a TERRIBLE design....  I get using 2 PCBs for the controller due to the shape and placement of parts.  That's not the problem.  If you're going to use pin headers,  spend the extra $0.01 and put the pins through the PCB.  Any device that's supposed to be the most mobile portion of a console should not ever use surface soldered pins to connect 2 PCBs together.  As I mentioned, I have babied my elite controller (only transport in its case, no one else used it, never thrown it, or anything else that could cause damage).  This happened just due to the subtle gripping of the controller while playing.  This also happened only 5 months after purchasing the controller.  It's just a bad design that needs to be fixed for a future iteration of the device.
