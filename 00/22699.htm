vasil l. michev say:

Just copy the messages to some folder via Search-Mailbox, then set a "1 day move to archive" or similar policy on said folder and let the MFA process it. It will take some time because of the size and the extended workcycle it works with in EO, but should do the job.

Another way, though a bit scarier, is to delete the mailbox (you can assign a license and put it on hold before the deletion, just to be on the safe side), then use the New-MailboxRestoreRequest cmdlet to copy the content. The point being, New-MailboxRestoreRequest has the -TargetIsArchive parameter which allows you to dump the content directly in the Archive mailbox. You can read more about it here: technet.microsoft.com/.../dn894099(v=exchg.150).aspx

Lastly, you can just export to PST and use Outlook to import the PST directly in the archive mailbox. Which will probably take a day or two :)
