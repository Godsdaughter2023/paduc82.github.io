That's not how Access works, nor do I understand why you would want ti to work any different. The purpose of a form is to interact with data in tables. Forms are just a way to view data in tables. A form is generally bound to a table. when you edit a record or add records to the form the table is affected. Why would you not want that?

How are you putting the filters on? With queries, you generally add criteria (or a WHERE clause) to your queries. The query is then saved with the criteria. Whehever you open that query the stored criteria will be applied.

ronaldoonenil say:

I suspect you have an Excel 97/2003 workbook (.xls) trying to do a VLOOKUP to a .xlsx file and the VLOOKUP uses column letters instead of specific ranges.

e.g. =VLOOKUP(A2,'[New Type.xlsx]Sheet1'!$A:$B,2,0)

.xlsx files have more rows than .xls files and so you get the error message. Specify a range instead and the error will be rectified.

e.g. =VLOOKUP(A2,'[New Type.xlsx]Sheet1'!$A$1:$B$65000,2,0)

Hi Admin - David Covert,

According to this article, when you suppress email notifications, Office 365 won't send notifications when activities or events that match the conditions of the alert policy.
