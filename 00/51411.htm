Note: A ‘Clean Boot’ starts Windows with a minimal set of drivers and startup programs, so that you can determine whether a background program is interfering with your game or program. Your computer may temporarily lose some functionality while in a clean boot environment, however, that functionality will return when you reset the computer to start normally after you have finished your troubleshooting.

If the mouse and keyboard are not working in Safe Mode, then follow Method 2.

If the mouse and keyboard works in Safe Mode and Clean Boot is not fixing the issue, then boot your computer in Safe Mode and perform the below troubleshooting steps.

  1. Click on the Start Menu > Search for Control Panel > Click on Hardware and Sound > Click on Power Options.
  2. From the left pane, click on Choose what the power buttons do and click on Change settings that are currently unavailable.
  3. Then, uncheck the option Turn on fast startup > Click on Save changes > Restart the computer once and check for the issue.

Method 3: Enabling Legacy USB Support in BIOS.

Note: Modifying BIOS/ complementary metal oxide semiconductor (CMOS) settings incorrectly can cause serious problems that may prevent your computer from booting properly.   4. Restart the computer and check if you can use the mouse and keyboard.
