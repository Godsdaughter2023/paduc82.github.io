Sure, do this:

dgfamilyhistorian say:

Using Patriotic Music in my own Movie Maker Project

I would like to use several mp3 or mp4 patriotic music files to use in my Movie Maker project. Downloads and licensing costs hundred of dollars. Is there something out there besides the Free Music Archive website offered through Movie Maker? Free Music Archive does not have songs such as the Air Force hymn, America the Beautiful, etc.? Are these songs free to use without copyright issues?
considerate_guy say:

Movie Maker is just the tool for making your movie. It doesn't decide whether or not you are infringing copyright laws.

Generally if you plan to broadcast your final movie (this includes presentation to a large public audience, or even loading on a public video sharing site like YouTube), you must own the copyrights to all content in your movie, including music. Either that, or they must be licensed from the copyright holder.

Exceptions can be made for your personal use under the fair use clause, but that's only if you aren't loading it somewhere publically accessible and you are only watching it with a few people.

As for the songs you mentioned, America the Beautiful has been in the public domain since 1895. But again, a specific performance of it may still be copyrighted. In other words, you could perform it yourself if you like, but if you download an .mp3 of a band playing the song, you might be violating their copyright unless it was performed awhile ago. In particular, I found a public domain version on archive.org that you could probably use.

The Air Force Song is copyright by Robert Crawford and is not public domain. But I did find an earlier Air Force theme song that is on archive.org.
