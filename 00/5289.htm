daniel pineault say:

It depends on your setup.

Normally, for something like this, I'd create a button and then in its On Click event I'd simply add

Private Sub MyButtonName_Click()
    On Error Resume Next

    Call Export2XLS("MyQueryNameToBeExportedToExcel")

Error_Handler_Exit:
    On Error Resume Next
    Exit Sub
 
Error_Handler:
    MsgBox "The following error has occured" & vbCrLf & vbCrLf & _
           "Error Number: " & Err.Number & vbCrLf & _
           "Error Source: MyButtonName_Click" & vbCrLf & _
           "Error Description: " & Err.Description _
           , vbOKOnly + vbCritical, "An Error has Occured!"
    Resume Error_Handler_Exit
End Sub

That said, Tom's right in the sense that MS Access does have the built-in TransferSpreadsheet Method, but I encountered problems with its use (long ago) and came up with the code I provided as a reliable alternative which offer far more control over the output file as you have complete control over layout, formatting, ... But if your needs are very basic, take a look at using TransferSpreadsheet like Tom mentioned.

lancecai_msft2205 say:

Hi IR,

Based on my test, the retention policy will take effect if the messages are imported via the Outlook client.

The retention runs automatically one time every seven days for mailboxes that are larger than 10 MB.

You can try the below steps to force the retention policy takes effect immediately.
1. Connect to Exchange Online using remote PowerShell via an Office 365 admin account.
2. Run the cmdlet: Start-ManagedFolderAssistant -Identity *** Email address is removed for privacy *** (Replace with the user’s address).

About "if there is a way to automatically move older messages into the "In place Archive" once the mailbox gets full".  the answer is no. 

Thanks for your understanding.

With Application.Dialogs(wdDialogFileOpen)
  .Name = TheTempPath
  .Show
End With

I have tried all sorts of combinations to TheTempPath, with and without the "/" and the "*.*", and with and without quote marks around the variable, and it always goes straight to default directory.  I have also tried .Display instead of .Show and that doesn't work either.  I have checked and double checked that TheTempPath is correct (i.e., such a path exists) and it is correct.

I have used Application.Dialogs(wdDialogFileOpen) in other places and it works fine.  I am totally lost: why could this be happening?

TIA.

jay freedman say:
It's probably the one combination you didn't try yet: keep the backslash after the last folder name in the path, but no asterisks. In your example, use

   TheTempPath = "N:\archive\Smith\"

Any other combination will go to the default directory or, after you've manually chosen some other folder, it will go to that last used folder.
