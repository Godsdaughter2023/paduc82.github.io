dirk goldgar say:

I just did and you are correct, there are some codes entered that don't have a closing "}"

I will correct those instances and test it again tomorrow.

How do I avoid errors like that in the future?

Well, this change to your expression will at least prevent that specific error from breaking it:

    IIf([SIMED ORDERS Followup]![Note] Like "{PT*}",(Mid([SIMED ORDERS Followup]![Note],InStr([SIMED ORDERS Followup]![Note],"{")+1,InStr([SIMED ORDERS Followup]![Note],"}")-InStr([SIMED ORDERS Followup]![Note],"{")-1)),"")

With that, the complex Mid() expression will only be evaluated when there's a "{PT" followed -- after some number of intervening characters -- by a "}".

The above version will not come out right if you have something like this in the field:  "x}y{PTzzz}" (there's a closing brace before the opening brace of the "{PT"; or if you have something like this:  "x{y{PTzzz}" (there's an opening brace before the opening brace of the "{PT").  The results will be wrong because InStr([SIMED ORDERS Followup]![Note],"}") or InStr([SIMED ORDERS Followup]![Note],"{") will find the first brace of that type, which is not part of the "{PT...}" code.  It can be fixed by specifying the starting point of the InStr scan, like this:

    IIf([SIMED ORDERS Followup]![Note] Like "{PT*}",(Mid([SIMED ORDERS Followup]![Note],InStr([SIMED ORDERS Followup]![Note],"{PT")+1,InStr(InStr([SIMED ORDERS Followup]![Note],"{PT"),[SIMED ORDERS Followup]![Note],"}")-InStr([SIMED ORDERS Followup]![Note],"{PT")-1)),"")

But I don't know if I got that exactly right, and I'm leaving it to you to test if you need to guard against extra braces.

A formula that works for a specific instance is easy to devise but I'm now beginning to doubt if this can be done robustly with a formula that would work all the time. Progress Measurement & Reporting Procedure
