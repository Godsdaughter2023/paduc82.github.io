Hi,

you con replace all links with their values by VBA:

Sub ReplaceLinks()
Dim myLink
  For Each myLink In ActiveCell.SpecialCells(xlCellTypeFormulas, 23)
    If InStr(1, myLink.Formula, "[") > 0 Then
      myLink.Value = myLink.Value
    End If
  Next
End Sub

 

This macro replaces in the active sheet all formulas which contains a "[" into their values.

luciftian say:

You can stop the prompts by using a macro to perform the find and replace action in the formula, as long as you insert the line

Application.DisplayAlerts = False

before the Replace line.

For example: 

 

Sub ReplacePartofFormula()

    Application.DisplayAlerts = False

    Selection.Replace What:="OldValue", Replacement:="NewValue", LookAt:=xlPart, _

        SearchOrder:=xlByRows, MatchCase:=False, SearchFormat:=False, _

        ReplaceFormat:=False

End Sub

 

All formulas are updated in seconds without the irritating prompting.

Swapping or changing pictures in Publisher 2016 - Microsoft Community

a. user say:

Swapping or changing pictures in Publisher 2016

I recently upgraded from Publisher 2007 to Publisher 2016.  I have a publication with 8 photos to a page that I change with every new publication.  In Publisher 2007, it was simple to double click on a photo on the page and it would take me to the folder where photos are stored.  In 2016 version, this ends up being a multi-step process with several clicks to accomplish the same thing.  Is there a setting that can be changed to make 2016 function like 2007?

davidf2 say:

No, you can't change the way Publisher works in this case, but you can change the way you work.

In Pub 2007 when you double clicked the picture on the Pub page the default folder location for where you store pictures on your computer opens. This may or may not be where you stored that inserted picture or the replacement picture. You still must browse to where you have stored the pictures you want to insert. For me that involves drilling down into one of the subfolders within the default pictures folder. Then once you select and insert one of those replacement pictures you can double click the next picture on the page and now Publisher will go back to the new folder location where you found your first inserted picture instead of the default picture location.

As of Pub 2010 you can no longer double click the picture, but you can right click > change picture and again Publisher opens the default picture folder just as Pub 2007 did when you double clicked the picture. And again once you browse to where you have stored the replacement pictures and selected and inserted one of those pictures, Publisher will go back to that folder location when you right click > change picture again.

In other words the only difference between the old and new way is you used to left double click the picture and now you have to right click and then left click change picture. Technically you still use two clicks of the mouse to get to the default picture folder.

BTW as of Pub 2010 and the new ribbon menu when you select a picture on the page Publisher 'reveals' the Picture tools format tab. Within the Adjust group of commands you will find the change picture command you can access via right clicking the picture. This work flow would mean one additional mouse click to select the Pictures tools format tab.

I know that changing a work flow that you have used for years can be a pain but in this case the right click option is almost as easy as the double click.

If it were me and you have the option then I would suggest you stick with Pub 2007. You have only just begun to find the differences and bugs associated with the newer versions of Publisher since version 2010. In my view Pub 2007 is superior to any of the newer versions if only because it still uses the traditional menu. But to each their own...

DavidF
