Group Policy in part controls what users can and cannot do on a computer system.

 

For the most part, if you are an administrator then you are "the" group with all the capabilities to configure xp.

 

So basically, if the Group Policy is configured correctly, then you should be able to disable the startup's via msconfig.

 

Also, keep in mind that startup programs is not the only area that can cause startup issues.

 

Look under the Services Tab in Msconfig then click / enable "hide" option and review the third party services that are also scheduled to startup as well.

 

Incidentally, you might want to jot down on paper what you disabled so you can back track, just in case your plans don't go exactly right.
