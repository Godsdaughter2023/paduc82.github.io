jeeped say:

You may be best served to collect the data in the right arrangement but at an alternate location.


Figure 1.0 - Shuffling data in Excel 2007

 The five formulas you need for the above are:

E1 is   ="A"&TEXT(99+ROWS(E1:E$1),"000")
F1 is   =INDEX(B:B,MATCH(E1,A:A,0))
G1 is   =INDEX(C:C,MATCH(E1,A:A,0))
H1 is   =INDEX(B:B,MATCH(E1,A:A,0)+1)
I1 is   =INDEX(C:C,MATCH(E1,A:A,0)+1)

... and those formulas can be copied or filled down as required. With the information safely retrieved and shuffled, you can Copy and Paste Special, Values back to any location you wish.

jay freedman say:

If you have a mouse with a scroll wheel that also can be clicked like a button, point in the Word window and click the wheel. You should see the cursor change into a dot with two arrow heads pointing up and down. Move that cursor toward the bottom of the window to autoscroll down, or toward the top of the window to autoscroll up. If you have a mouse such as a Logitech that offers customization of the buttons, this might not work as with a generic mouse. Instead, you can probably select autoscroll as the function assigned to the wheel or one of the other buttons.
