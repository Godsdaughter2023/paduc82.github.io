Change company name in Word 2010 documents - Microsoft Community

grandpa jim say:

Change company name in Word 2010 documents

The details page of new Word 2010 documents shows the "company" name as Toshiba. This is a new Toshiba laptop, but my "company' (I'm self-employed) is not Toshiba. How can I correct this?
hansv mvp say:

You can change this in the Windows registry. The usual warnings about being careful apply.

Click the Start button in Windows.

Type regedit in the Search box and press Enter.

If you're asked whether you want to allow RegEdit.exe, click Yes.

Navigate to HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Office\Common\UserInfo.

Double-click the Company entry in the right hand pane and change the value as desired, then click OK.

I'm glad you were able to dig your way through to working audio, and I'm sorry we broke it in 10130. Here's what's going on...

There's a bug in the audio stack that causes stream initialization to fail on a very specific cross-section of configurations. As you've discovered, changing the sampling rate fixes it, though not the same sampling rate works on every config (Conexant seems to like 192 KHz, for example). It's not a driver issue, it's really a core Windows audio stack problem. We think we've figured it out and should have a fix in the next flight.

As to the troubleshooter not working, we're also aware of that. It wouldn't have helped you in this case, but until the next flight you can manually run the audio troubleshooter with this command (from an elevated command prompt):

msdt /path %windir%\diagnostics\system\audio
