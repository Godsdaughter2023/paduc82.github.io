benishiryo say:

i downloaded it from the excel 2013 templates.  i didn't go through every parts of the file, so maybe you can point other areas you wish to change.  i simply spotted 3 areas:
1. saturday highlighted grey in the calendar.  change it by clicking on cell H9.  go to Home tab -> Conditional Formatting -> Manage Rules.  double-click the last rule (ie the one with formula the formula below)
=OR(H$8="S", COUNTIF(lstHolidays, H9)>0)
change it to:
=OR(I$8="M", COUNTIF(lstHolidays, H9)>0)

2. Working days in cell L26 excludes saturdays.  include it by what i told you.  change this formula below from:
=NETWORKDAYS(DATE($AN$5,1,1),EDATE(DATE($AN$5,1,1),12)-1)

to:
=NETWORKDAYS.INTL(DATE($AN$5,1,1),EDATE(DATE($AN$5,1,1),12)-1,11)

but the above formula did not exclude holidays.  so if you want to do so, try:
=NETWORKDAYS.INTL(DATE($AN$5,1,1),EDATE(DATE($AN$5,1,1),12)-1,11,tblCompanyHolidays[Company Holidays])

3. cell L30 is similar to point 2.  simply change from NETWORKDAYS to NETWORKDAYS.INTL.  add in 11 to indicate that only sun is a weekend.  add in the holidays if you wish to exclude them

christopher miller say:

Hi Bill,

I'm sorry, but Windows Phone 8/8.1 does not support rSAP, it only supports PBAP.  Unless your car's head unit can be configured to connect using PBAP, you will not be able to pair the Lumia to your car.

SIM Access Profile, commonly referred to as SAP, SIM,  or rSAP
The Head unit has it's own GSM radio and uses the SIM card in the phone.  Supported by older phones and some Android phones.  Not supported by Windows or iOS.

Phone Book Access Profile, commonly referred to as PBAP or PBA
The head unit can send and receive well documented objects from the connected device.  Usually the the head unit will import the phone book from the phone and direct the phone to make a call using the radio in the phone..  Supported by Android, iOS, and Windows PHone
