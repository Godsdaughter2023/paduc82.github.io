john mcghie say:
Hi Uttam:

VBA support in Word 2011 is good enough for me to do some light commercial VBA development in it.  
Officially, VBA in Office 2011 is version 6.5 (same as Office 2007).  However, most of the events provided in VBA 6.5 did not get implemented in the Mac version, so stay away from event-driven programming if you can.

That's the major difference.  Some events are there, and the ones that appear generally work.  But most are missing.  VBA 7 enables 64-bit memory operations: VBA on the Mac does not.

Obviously, anything that depends on Windows functions is not there.  But you can hook Apple functions using AppleScript embedded in VBA.  This is a bit of a minefield: "many things" work, some do not.

There is no documentation at all for Word VBA on the Mac.  The documentation that does exist is for Excel on the Mac (and it's a rough draft: they ran out of time to complete it).

Far better to use the links Jim sent you to to get up the PC Word VBA reference: nearly all of that will work, and anything that doesn't will error immediately.

Learn to use the PC Reference, and the Object Browser.  There's no embedded or context-sensitive help in the Mac VBA IDE.

The effort is worth it -- Enjoy!

I don't know if you have already gotten an answer to this. The Microsoft people don't always know everything about their products. Just in case, I'll explain my "fix" to this.
Two things you need to do to make the highlights look nice on export:

 1. Write/draw as usual, then highlight as you wish. Select the part you've highlighted (with lasso tool) then press the arrows/compass that appears (I think you have to press it with your finger). On the menu that pops up, there should be an arrow/triangle for further options. There you will select "Treat selected ink as" and you will choose "Drawing". This will put the writing ink at the top when you export.

 2. You can highlight as many layers as you want, as long as you don't lift the pen until you're done. Once you lift the pen and highlight again, it will interpret this as two layers. Note that it won't cover the ink as long you have done step one. But it will look ugly and uneven if you lift the pen, as the multiple layers of highlighting will show as a darker color. 

I'm on version 2016, but these options are available on older versions too, though it might look different.
