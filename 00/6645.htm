benjamin cramphorn say:

Project 2013 - Adding colours background to gantt chart for certain dates.

Hi All,

Is it possible to add a coloured background to the gantt chart on a certain range of dates? I want to be able to show when the main building is closed to the public and show it on the gantt chart. Projects will still be happening during these times.

Hope you can help,


dale howard [mvp] say:

Benjamin --

Yes, this is possible, but it requires a small amount of work to make it happen.  Here is what you would need to do:

  1. Open the project in question and apply the Gantt Chart view.
  2. Create a new calendar that is a copy of the Standard calendar (assuming the Standard calendar includes your company holidays).  Name the new calendar something like Building Closed.
  3. In the new Building Closed calendar, mark the building closed dates as nonworking time.
  4. When you finish creating the new Building Closed calendar, double-click the Timescale bar at the top of the Gantt Chart pane.
  5. In the Timescale dialog, click the Non-working time tab.
  6. On the Non-working time page, click the Calendar pick list and select your Building Closed calendar, and then click the OK button.

The preceding procedure will not affect the schedule of the project, but it will show the building closed time period as vertical gray shaded bands, like you see already for weekends and company holidays.  Please know that you CANNOT make the shaded bands a different color for only the Building Closed time period.

Hope this helps.

stefan blom say:
To reference the current heading number at a particular level, insert a STYLEREF field in the footer (Insert tab | Quick Parts | Field). You can insert two STYLEREF fields, one pointing to the numbering of Heading 1 and one to that of Heading 2.

charles kenyon say:
To add to what Stephan Bloom said, the StyleRef can also refer to a Character Style. You could have the number for a subsection tagged with a Character Style (no formatting change) and incorporate that in your footer with a StyleRef.

how to send emails using hotel servers while overseas - Microsoft Community

anonymus_812 say:

how to send emails using hotel servers while overseas

I am currently travelling overseas and find that while I can receive emails at the hotels that provide internet services I am not able to send emails from certain hotels yet I am able to send emails from some hotels. Is there a simple solution to this problem? I would be grateful for the community's response.

 

The hotel service is either by way of an unsecured WiFi service or in conjunction with an Ethernet service

 

I am using Outlook 2010.

jeeped say:
If you are looking for a one-size-fits-all solution then use the web browser mail service of your email account. Outlook will use SMTP/POP3/IMAP with possible custom port settings and you cannot account for every hotels configuration. Any modern mail server has a browser based email delivery that you can use to homogenize the ports used to transfer mail. If you try to accommodate the requirements of one hotel you will a) likely have to modify the account port settings for the duration of your stay and revert them once you leave and b) have to readjust before the next hotel's internet service can be used for similar Outlook conformity.
