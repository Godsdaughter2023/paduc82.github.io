stefan blom say:

"List Paragraph" is the style that Word adds to paragraphs when you format them with bullets or numbers (unless you picked one of the built-in formats that also applies heading styles). The style controls font, color, line spacing etc. for your bulleted (numbered) paragraphs.

However, "List Paragraph" does not store any bullet (or number) formatting, so it can't be used to modify indentation around bullets/numbers. Nor can you use it to decide whether the bullet should be a check mark or a circle, or if the number should be Arabic or Roman numeral, etc.

Instead, you have to set up your own style. You can create a new one and include bullet formatting. Or you can use one of the built-in styles as a basis, and modify it to suit your need. For example, the built-in List Bullet style does not have a left indent, so you might prefer that one.
