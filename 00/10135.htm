    Setting up DKIM for O365 domain - Microsoft Community

    testadmintest say:

    Setting up DKIM for O365 domain

    HI,

    1) Recently i experienced many phishing attempt for my O365 domain despite having SPF record setup. I am planning to setup DKIM and DMARC please provide me the essential step how can i enable it.

    2) Also are there any issues with respect to end users when it comes to receiving and sending emails.  

    3) How DKIM and DMARC will help me if someone is trying to send an email as my domain but actually has email address for an instance of gmail.

    Thanks in advance.

    robert__p say:

    Hi Testadmintest,

    Here are the answers to your questions:
    1. Here are the steps to manually enable DKIM signing for your domain in Office 365:

    http://blogs.msdn.com/b/tzink/archive/2015/10/08/manually-hooking-up-dkim-signing-in-office-365.aspx

    As for DMARC, you don’t have to do anything to enable it, it is enabled automatically. For your reference:

    http://blogs.msdn.com/b/tzink/archive/2014/12/03/using-dmarc-in-office-365.aspx

    2. After DKIM and DMARC are enabled, there will be no issues to end users.

    3. Below are the main details of how DKIM and DMARC work:

    DKIM:
    DKIM permits the person, role or organization, who owns the signing domain, to claim some responsibility for a message by associating the domain with the message. Senders insert a digital signature into the message in the DKIM-Signature header, which receivers then verify. DKIM allows senders to build domain reputation, which is important to ensure email delivery and provides senders a non-spoofable way to identify themselves. It is a critical component of email protection.

    DMARC:
    DMARC is a technology designed to combat email spoofing and is useful to stop phishing. Specifically, it protects the case where a phisher has spoofed the 5322.From email address, which is the email address displayed in mail clients like Outlook and outlook.com. Whereas the Sender Policy Framework, (SPF) catches the case where the phisher spoofs the 5321.MailFrom, which is where bounce messages are directed, DMARC catches the case that is more deceptive.

    So in your case, if the sender actually has an email address for an instance of Gmail:
    a) For DKIM, the email will not insert a digital signature into the message for verification.
    b) For DMARC, it will manage to match the 5321.MailFrom address with the 5322.From address, then it turns out to be a failure.

    You can also refer to the article below for more information:

    https://blogs.office.com/2015/01/20/enhanced-email-protection-dkim-dmarc-office-365/

    Please let us know if you need further assistance.

    Regards,
    Robert

    Whatever happened to the toggle on and off Reading Pane in Outlook 2010

    In my previous version of Outlook 2003, there was Reading Pane button where you could toggle on and off. Now the Reading Pane button seems to be Bottom, Right, or Off. Whatever happened to the toggle on and off Reading Pane? [Note: I have Window XP at work and Vista at home.]

    srinivasu guntimadugu say:

    With the new Office 2010, Microsoft has changed the interface and introduced ribbon. The ribbon interface can accommodate more with new features and functionality options whereas the standard toolbar in Outlook 2003 had limitations.

     

    In outlook 2010, the option to toggle Off and On the reading pane with a single click is not possible; however you may customize the Quick Access Toolbar to add a reading pane.

    Follow the steps:
    1. Click on File -> Go to Options.
    2. Click on Quick Access Toolbar -> Change the “commands from” to “All Commands”
    3. Select Reading Pane -> Click on Add -> Click OK.

     

    Now the reading pane will show in the Quick Access Toolbar.

     

    For more information, refer the following link on How to customize the Quick Access Toolbar:
    http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/outlook-help/customize-the-quick-access-toolbar-HA010362102.aspx
