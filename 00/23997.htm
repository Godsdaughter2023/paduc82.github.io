saberman say:

> the important stuff, is sync'd to the cloud. That is essentially a backup.

Actually, it is not for a number of reasons:

1. You don't have any control on what/when it is backed up as you do with a normal backup systems.

2. It does not maintain muliple versions.

3. If you make a mistake (i.e. delete something or save a bad version of a document) the sync'ed version is destroyed at the next sync.

4. There isn't any option to secure the data.  It is unencrypted and open to the world.

 

There is a fundamental difference between sync and backup.  Sync is intended to keep two locations the same as much as possible.  Backup is intended to keep multiple instances of your enviroment available for as long as needed. 

 

There have also been a number of reports of major data losses if the first connection to SkyDrive fails during a "restore" to a new or reset phone.  The restore option is no longer available and the next connection "syncs" SkyDrive to the phone destroying any information that did not get transferred before the connection failed.

 

In defining everything as a sync Microsoft has made assumptionsabout its customer's needs without providing them with any ability to override the assumptions.  For example, the implementation of the pop3 client does not read and send mail -- it synchronizes mail.  Your inbox matches what is in the inbox on the server.  If you delete an email from the phone it is deleted from the server at the next sync. if you delete an email from the server it is deleted from your phone at the next sync.  There is no way for the user to change that -- at least not on my Verizon Nokia Lumia 822.

echo s say:

To get the pix into your presentation, do this:

1. Insert tab, click Photo Album, New Photo Album.

2. In the photo album dialog, click File/Disk and choose your pictures. Click Insert.

3. In the photo album dialog, click Create to create the photo album PPT file.

 

To get the music to play throughout, do this.

1. Go to the first slide in the presentation. On the Insert tab, click Audio.

2. Select the sound file and click Insert.

3. Select the sound icon on the slide and go to the Audio Tools Playback tab.

4. Click the dropdown next to Start On Click and choose Play Across Slides.

5. Click Hide During Show (or drag the icon off the edge of the slide where it won't show during the presentation).

6. Click Loop Until Stopped (if desired).

 

To add comments to some of the pictures, on the Insert tab, click the Shapes button, then click the rectangle (or other shape or even just the textbox in the Basic Shapes section) and then click on the slide (or click and drag on the slide) to create the shape. With the shape selected, start typing to add text to it.
