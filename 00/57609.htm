crazman say:

I am not sure what you are using for your internet connection, but I don't think it matters. It may be one of many of the problems it could be. However, I would not go out and get new hardware just yet. If you have checked to see if at least one computer can work while wired directly to the cable or dsl connection  then Here are a few things that you can try that may resolve your connection issues.

One thing to know is if all of the computers on your network are not running Windows 7, do not use or join the Homegroup setup that is provided. You can still share with the rest of your network. You just have to set your network sharing manually. But this may not be the problem at least not the only one. The Windows 7 diagnostics will not find the problem and I am sure all of your drivers are ok too. I am sure you have reset your router and modem at least a million times by now, too.

The main problem that you are having sounds like that your router connects to the internet when the first computer logs on the network. Then after one or more computers connect to the network, everything seems to work fine. It is when one of the network computers idle into sleep mode or is logged off or shutdown, things start going bad.

If this is sounding similar to what you are experiencing on your network, then I would check into one or more of the following areas. 

First, I would check to see if your router is set correctly for your type of network and also included in the domain or workgroup for your private network. This is very important. If they do not match each other then they can connect for a while to each other then to the internet but, if something changes on your network, the chain reaction starts.

The domain and workgroup used to be an optional setting on some older model routers. If you are not a part of a domain, use the name of your workgroup in your settings of your router or what ever your IP provider or router vendor suggests. This is in addition to any type of security settings that have to match as well. Also, some older routers do not have the newer WPA security settings but the WPA-personal security setting works with some. If this does not fix the problem up to here continue on.

Next, I would check the firewall settings or just turn it off completly when setting up your network. Having more than one firewall can cause issues too. I would wait untill everything is working, then turn on the firewall and deal with that after everything works.

Finally, I would look at your Universal Plug and Play settings on your router as well. There may be a need to check that also. It sounds to me that all the computers on your network are trying to take control of the router when another computer on your network goes to sleep mode or is shutdown while others are still logged on. Or something like that. I would leave that UP&P setting off for now. Just make sure one computer, usually the one closest to the cable switch (modem) and router has access to the router settings. The rest should be able to connect on their own if the settings are set the same as the rest of the network. All the rest should be set to automatically detect the network settings.

If all of your security settings and the all your network computers and router are a part of the same domain or workgroup, this and setting your firewall to allow your data through, to the internet are key factors in resolving this internet dropping problem.

I know it sounds too easy for this to be the problem causing this connection to drop, but it works on my home network and no more dropping.

So, I hope this works for you and all that are having this problem. Good luck.

Face Recognition stopped working - Microsoft Community

eperry20 say:

Face Recognition stopped working

Since doing the update on my dell Inspiron laptop last week, i am no longer able to log in using my face. When attempting it tries and changes straight to pin, if i go in the settings to update the privacy setting - 'Improve recognition' it says it cant access camera!

Am am worried this has happens because my laptop turned off half way though the update last week?

Is this problem fixable?

Thanks! 

andre da costa say:
1. Reset Facial Recognition and/or Fingerprint Options

Press Windows key + I to open Settings.
Click Accounts and select Sign-In options.
Locate Fingerprint or Facial Recognition options and click Remove under them.
After that, click Get Started and follow the on-screen instructions to reset Fingerprint and Facial Recognition.
Reboot your PC if needed.

2. Modify Group Policy Settings

Creators Update might have modified some of the crucial settings of Group Policy. Therefore, we would recommend to launch the Group Policy Editor and check/edit the settings related to Windows Hello functionality.

Pres Windows key, type gpedit, and press Enter.
Click on Computer Configuration and open Administrative Templates.
Select Windows Components and open Biometrics.
Click on Facial Features and right-click on Configure enhanced anti-spoofing option.
Select Edit and disable the Configure enhanced anti-spoofing feature.
Reboot your PC.

3. Update drivers

If some of the system drivers, most probably webcam and fingerprint reader drivers, were corrupted during the upgrade process, Windows Hello may fail to perform. To fix that, try to update the corresponding drivers:

Right-click on Windows key and select Device Manager.
Locate the Hello, webcam, and fingerprint drivers individually and right-click on each of them.
Select Remove driver software and wait for the removal to be finished.
Repeat the removal with all Windows Hello related drivers and then reboot your PC.
Once done, open Device Manager, click Action -> Scan for hardware changes.
The system should automatically detect and reinstall the drivers.
