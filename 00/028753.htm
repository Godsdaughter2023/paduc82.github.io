jay freedman say:

The grammar checker doesn't have a rule that looks for more than 2 spaces, and I doubt it will be added. The Advanced Find with wildcards is the best way to do this. After clicking the More button in the Advanced Find dialog to expand it, and checking the box for "Use wildcards", enter this expression in the Find What box:

   .[ ]{3,}

There's a space between the square brackets. Then you can do what you want with the instances it finds. For instance, you can use the Replace tab of the dialog and type a period and either one or two spaces in the Replace With box, then click the Replace All button. Or you can click the Find All button in the Find tab of the dialog.

hansv mvp say:

Let's say the first table is on Sheet1 in A1:G13, and the second one on another sheet in A1:K2.

In A2 on that sheet, enter the formula

=IFERROR(LOOKUP(1E+300,INDEX(Sheet1!$A$1:$G$13,MATCH(A1,Sheet1!$A$1:$A$13,0),0)),"")

Format A2 as a percentage, then fill right from A2 to K2.

kchan104 say: it works!!!! thank you very much!!
May I know what "1E+300" is??? How should I interpret this? Thanks!!!!

hansv mvp say:

1E+300 means 10 to the power 300, in other words the digit 1 followed by 300 zeros. It's used here as a very large number greater than anything you will have in the cells.

LOOKUP tries to find this number, and since it fails, it returns the last number it encountered.
