The next thing to check is the "outline level" assigned to those paragraphs. That's usually assigned through the definition of the style applied to the heading.

Click in one of the headings that are missing from the Navigation pane, and then click the little arrow in the bottom right corner of the Paragraph group on the Home tab. Hi SCS CM,

Thanks for the update. However, by default, email messages that are sent from Exchange Online in Office 365 use the Transport Neutral Encapsulation Format (TNEF) format. Messaging systems that aren't based on Microsoft Exchange may be unable to interpret messages that use this rich text format. If the recipient’s messaging system can’t process this format, a file attachment that's called Winmail.dat is added to the message.

As an admin you can use Windows PowerShell to change the message format to prevent the Winmail.dat attachment from being sent to external recipients.
