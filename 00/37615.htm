After trial and error, I have found the solution in Windows 10,

All you have to do is the following:

1) Right click on desktop and select Display settings.

2) Scroll down and select Advanced Display Settings.

3) Scroll down and select Advanced sizing of text and other items

4) Select "set a custom scaling level" (highlighted in blue).

5) In the scaling box enter 124% and click OK.

6) Click Apply and it will ask you to restart.....restart now.....all done.

Problem solved.....Microsoft bumped on this one with setting the default scaling level to 125%. Actual level should be 124% which allows for icons to be placed on the bottom row of desktop.
