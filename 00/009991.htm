marshall barton say:

No.  You are confusing the meaning of the word "field".  A field is a column in a table or query, while controls are the thingies you put on a form/report to display values.  The difference is often confused when you use a control bound to a field and use the word "field" for both the control and the field it is bound to.  To further that confusion, Access defaults to using the same name for both the control and its bound field.  A lot of MS Help topics and articles and use field when they should use control.  Regardless of how the confusion came about, you should keep them straight in your mind and use them correctly in communications so the meaning is not misinterpreted.

The name of the left of the = must be a field in the form's record source table/query.  The code I posted expected you to use a field name on both sides of the = sign, but the right side of the = can be anything that returns a value.

marshall barton say:

A percentage is just a number displayed with the decimal point moved two places to the right.  E.g.  .35 is the same as 35%  So % is just a format and it has no effect on the actual value.

A value that is a subtotal of a total can be is considered a percentage of the total by dividing the subtotal by the total.  E.g.  I Joe sold 4 widgits today and the whole store sold a total of 10 widgits, then Joe accounted for 4/10 or .4 or 40% of the sales.  There really isn't much more to it than that.
