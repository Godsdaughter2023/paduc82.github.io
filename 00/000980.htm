john korchok say:

The "templates" you refer to are Styles.

The text in your document can be reformatted with Styles by selecting text, then clicking on the Style Gallery. The Style Gallery is displaying styles that come from the template attached to the document.

Here are 2 techniques to solve this issue in the future:

Simple but repetitive: 

  1. On the Home tab, click on the bottom half of the Paste button.
  2. Choose Paste Special.
  3. Choose Unformatted Text. The pasted text will take on the style that is active at the insertion point.

A little more complicated, saves time later:

  1. In Word, click on the File tab, then on Options.
  2. Click on Advanced and look for the Cut, copy and paste section.
  3. Beside Pasting between documents, change the dropdown to Merge Formatting. OK out.

Now, by default, pasted text will always take on the formatting of the document into which you are pasting.

john korchok say:

Her's how to clean up what's displayed on your style menu:

  1. Press Ctrl + Shift + Alt + S to open the Styles list.
  2. In the lower left corner of this dialog, click on Options...
  3. Under Select formatting to show as styles, uncheck all 3 boxes. OK Out.

Now the list of styles only displays actual styles.

charles kenyon say:
Not as such. Headers are a section property.

However, you can certainly have replicating text.

Labels in Word are Tables . All of the tools available for tables are available to work on labels and all of the limitations of tables apply to labels. Tables can have replicating rows known as headers but they cannot reproduce within the table, they only show up when the table breaks across a page.

See Repeating Data (Populating Multiple Like Fields).

You can horizontally split the rows that make up the labels to make them your "headers."

You cannot use BitDefender and Windows Defender concurrently on Win 10 (or Win 8.1 either).

On Windows 10 when a third party antimalware program (such as BitDefender, McAfee, Norton, etc) is installed the Windows Defender (WD) program should be disabled.
