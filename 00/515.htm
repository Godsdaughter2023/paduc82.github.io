This is my first Discussion in the MS community section. I wonder where I was that I'm so late to be here ( am I ?)

We all are aware that Windows 10 for PC has been a success. Whatever the critics say or whatever the Bloggers say,

we are comfortable with Environment it offers ( Familiar one )

Microsoft aim's to create "one" platform for all. Even if we have Extremely Synced & Familiar Device the OS will be different.

I've always wanted to help in this regard in any possible way.

Now you must be wondering if I am a insider on not.

Yes I am. I have tested the Win Ph 10 till build Number 10549, than I had to flashback to 8.1 because of stability, Updates and slight Network issues.

When I went to the Insiders Hub I discovered that it lacks people who are actually or genuine Insiders, devoted to make a better OS. Instead they were those who would show off that they have "Windows 10 on their phone...before the release".

While the actual lovers, critics and worthy people are here including the new windows 8.1 /8/7 users (PC and Mobile both. Ignore 7 & 8 for mobile).

Let us discuss what we want to see, what we've seen and what we might see in the Windows Phone 10.

Please Note : Only Windows Phone 10, Discussing only the software part !

I will start off with my Experience with all the Builds I've used/tested or gone through.

Here are a few things that I found and described as Glitches.

1. Seamless lists in few Menu(s) and limited in few.

2. Not a good option for < Cellular Data users. (In terms of data usage )

3. Alarms didn't ring, they had a toast notification when they were supposed to wake me up. Because of this I was late for my tuition twice.

4. I sense that the operating system is made with a "pc" OS in mind more than a mobile OS. Not everyone is going to connect a Bluetooth mouse or Keyboard.

What I want to see

Now, there are plenty of things that I wish to see in the Windows phone 10 and I think that they are important.

The biggest mistake Microsoft is making is that under the "same platform for all" view, they are making a "Windows 10 to Windows 10" OS. What I mean to say is that. I might have Windows 8.1 on my PC but Windows Phone 10 on my Mobile Device. The two devices are not going to be synced.

My Xbox Music's playlist won't show up on my Groove Music & vice versa.

If fact the Photos app is paralysed because of this. If I have Windows 10 on PC, than the Photos app is of no use. It's total waste. All what I can see in my Photos, are my OneDrive uploads, which are not much.

The thing gets worse if you make payments in the Store or in the Video app.

Suppose I buy The Walking Dead Season 6 on my Windows 8.1 PC through the Videos app, the trend right now is that if I jump on my Mobile Phone, for more comfortable experience I can watch it on my Phone as well. Anytime-Anywhere.

But I cannot watch my Favorite show on my Windows Phone 10 device because it cannot "sync" with it's predecessor app.

I have to sit in front of my computer in order to watch the show.

shashirekha say:

Do you have an exchange server account configured in outlook?

Check if Outlook is in a compatibility mode:
1.Open My Computer>Local Disk C>Program Files>Microsoft Office>Office 14>Outlook.exe.
(If you are using a 64 bit machine, go to C:\Program Files x86\Microsoft Office>Office 14>Outlook.exe)
2. Right click on Outlook.exe and click on Properties and then click on Compatibility tab. Check if you have any check mark in any of the compatibility mode options. Make sure you also click on the option "change settings for all users" tab at the bottom of the same screen and look for similar check boxes in the window.
3. If you see any check boxes in the compatibility mode options, uncheck all the boxes and click on Apply and OK.
4. Try to open outlook again

If that fails thencreate a new profile in outlook and check if it helps.
