barkoosh say: Hello

First, to avoid confusion, I'm going to use "Hindi" for the numbers that you call "Arabic" (١٢٣٤), and "Arabic" for the numbers that you call "English" (1234), because this is the terminology used in Office settings.

When you type numbers with your keyboard, you're actually typing the codes for the Arabic numbers (the numbers used in English, French, etc...). Word alone has the option of changing the DISPLAY, not the code, of those numbers to something else. That's why you can, by changing some Word settings, make numbers show as Hindi or Arabic, even Hindi numbers in an English context and even Arabic numbers in an Arabic context. In all those cases, the code remains the same; It doesn't change at all. Only the way it looks in Word changes.

However, this feature is not found in other programs, even in other Office programs. So when you copy from Word numbers that look Hindi, the other programs are going to read the code and find that it is actually Arabic not Hindi.

The solution? For the numbers that you want to copy from Word to a different program, go in Word to the INSERT tab, click "Symbol", then "More Symbols...", the "Symbol" window opens. For "Font" choose "(Complex script text)", and for "Subset" choose "Arabic". Go down few lines till you see the Hindi numbers that start with what is called "Arabic-Indic Digit Zero". Insert your numbers from among those numbers into Word. If you copy those numbers from Word and paste them into another program, you will see that they will retain their form because the code is different from the keyboard's numbers.
