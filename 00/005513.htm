something – next words

 

In both cases, I typed it character-by-character but I went back to the first line to show how I typed it.  I don’t care which hyphen I get, but I don’t want to have to correct it every time I type.  I have noticed that it does not change until I hit the space bar.  I can type a LOT of characters with no change until I hit space.

 

Any ideas on how to stop the correction?

suzanne s. barnhill say:

This is exactly the behavior of the referenced AutoFormat As You Type function. Word will convert a single hyphen preceded (and followed) by spaces to an en dash. Two hyphens, with or without spaces, will be converted to an em dash. In both cases, the conversion is not performed until a space or punctuation is typed after the word following the hyphen(s). In most cases, this is what users want. The one condition when it is often not appreciated is when the hyphen is at the beginning of a word. In such cases, you can reverse the correction with Ctrl+Z (Undo).

This setting is found at File | Options | Proofing | AutoCorrect Options | AutoFormat As You Type.

Upon closer inspection, I realize the route given is not quite the one I wish to navigate so try changing it a little but.....there's no way to change the route?

cjlim say:

There is no option to change the route manually at the moment. However, you can influence the route by changing the Route Optimisation settings. To do this, after specifying your destination and just before you tap the Start button,  you will see a row of icons above the Start button. Tap this to change the settings. 

 

Here Drive+ is still beta so things will still change before the final release. In the mean time, you can always use Google if it suits you better.
