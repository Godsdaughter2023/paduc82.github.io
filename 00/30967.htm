I have tried the hotfix provided by the Microsoft, repeated Windows Experience Index multiple times, tried System Configuration Utility but all in vain. Could anyone please solve this problem?
bossdweeb say: AMD FX 6100 cpu's are divided into three modules with two 'logical' cores per physical core,  so both reports are accurate for different reasons (assuming you have not experienced a real world performance drop).
The disparity in the reporting is due to the power sharing design in these 'modules' of FX series cpu's,
and how Windows views this module/core setup .
You may be able to get six cores reported again though. Check in the BIOS  cpu settings and see
if the cpu core control is set to manual vs auto.
'Turbo' on or off may also change the way Windows reports the number of cores.

j w stuart say:

Dell Mini 9 contain a Solid State Drive (SSD). As such reformating the drive is not the same as reformating a "Hard Drive". Hard drives can be reformatted as many time as you like. Reformating a Solid State Drive can cause premature wear and reduce the life expectancy in some instances (such as a "Full" format). 

 

Since Windows uses a "Quick" format that is not an issue. However formating a SSD does have its limitations as the "blocks" are not truely rendered as free space or empty. Therefore if your drive came with a "Garbage Collection" or "Trim" utility you should run this before re-installing and again after the install. Note that Trim or Garbage collection does not cause wear on the SSD. 

 

If you don't know what is available for maintainence of your SSD then contact Dell support.

http://support.dell.com/support/index.aspx
