I think the best solution depends on your definition of a "blank cell".

Is the blank cell an empty cell, a cell that contains nothing at all, no formulas, no nothing, or is the blank cell a cell that appears empty but might contain a formula that returns a formula blank (="")?

Also, what type of data will these cells contain?

  • text
  • numbers
  • dates
  • times
  • errors
  • any of the above

This formula will evaluate empty cells and cells that contain formula blanks as being the same.

=IF(SUMPRODUCT(--(C42:C47="")),"Blank","All Filled")
