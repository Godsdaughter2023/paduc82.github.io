I've been enduring precisely this problem for a few months on an HTC Touch Pro (Windows Mobile 6.1).  I, too, made do by excluding Notes from synchronization.  I believe that I finally discovered the source of the problem, and the "inkstore.dll" diagnostic was a red herring that was throwing all of us off the track.

The problem apparently stems from the fact that individual Notes are stored by Windows Mobile as individual files (*.pwi), whereas they aren't stored that way in Outlook 2007.  If the Subject field of a Note in Outlook 2007 contains a character that is not allowed as part of a Windows Mobile *.pwi filename, ActiveSync 4.5 silently replaces the offending character with an underscore (_) in the Windows Mobile filename; but it also seems to maintain some record of correspondence between the Outlook 2007 Subject field and the modified Windows Mobile filename.  If that correspondence is lost, two problems seem to arise.  First, multiple versions of notes accumulate.  Second, ActiveSync balks at finding the prohibited characters in a Windows Mobile filename.

I first tried to fix the problem by editing the Subject fields of notes in Outlook 2007.  That failed because such editing only affects the Content field of a note.  It produces the initial appearance of fixing the problem, but the editing is overwritten with the next synchronization.  Evidently because notes in Outlook 2007 are not freestanding files, there is no direct way within Outlook 2007 to modify a Subject field in a manner that won't be undone by the next synchronization.

I then renamed Notes files in Windows Mobile.  That works, but it is tedious.

The most efficient remedy was to establish a connection with the mobile device, choose the "Explore Device" icon in Microsoft ActiveSync, and rename the offending *.pwi files through that interface.  Most of my problems arose from my inclusion of colons (:) in Subject fields.  Oddly, ampersands (&) sandwiched between letters were tolerated, but ampersands (&) with spaces on either side were not.

Hoping that this works for Ted V and others,
Gary

Despite what Microsoft Tech Support had said to Mark, I have actually just managed to launch Office On Demand applications. I have an Office 365 Home Premium subscription and launched on demand apps by doing the following:

  1.    Go to www.office.com
  2.    Click on the tile to launch the application you want to use - I'll use Excel as an example but the same procedure works for all Office applications
  3.    Excel Online will launch and you will be asked to select either:
    1.    a new blank workbook
    2.    browse templates, or
    3.    recent documents on one drive
  4.    Select New blank workbook
  5.    Excel Online will launch - this is only the web app version.
  6.    Next to the Ribbon tabs (File / Home / Insert etc) is a button titled "Open in Excel". Click this button.
  7.    You may be prompted for your Office subscription email address and password a couple of times. Once these have been entered and your subscription verified, Office on Demand will load and you will have the full functioning application to use.

I launched these applications on a PC that did not have any Office applications installed on it. I haven't tested if this works on a PC that already has Office 2013 installed - I suspect it may just revert to the local programs rather than starting up an On Demand version.
