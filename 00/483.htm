    Most likely you had a POP3 configuration in Windows Live Mail, and kept the default setting which deletes mail from the server when it is downloaded to the computer.     Alternatively, just fix WLM to "Leave a copy of messages on the server", and set up Microsoft Outlook 2013 to run as IMAP which mirrors what happens elsewhere, without a download and delete function. Nothing you send from WLM (POP3) would ever be recorded online or in Microsoft Outlook 2013 (IMAP), so you'll need to use the other two options for sending.

    karl timmermans [outlook mvp 2012-2018] say:

    Outlook 2016, is there a maximum number of email addresses which can be in a contact group?

    There are two answers to your question - one theoretical and the other "practical".

    Taking an Exchange environment out of the equation for the sake of this response, everything has its limits somewhere along the line but would be so high that no one would ever reach it under normal circumstances.

    From a more practical standpoint - you also need to be cognizant of the limits imposed by your email service provider in terms of (1) "# of recipients" in one email and (2) # of emails sent to different recipients over a 24 hr period - neither of which are Outlook limitations.

    To reverse the question - how many recipients do you want to include in a ContactGroup?
