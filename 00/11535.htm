jllatham say:

It sounds like you are using Excel 2003 or earlier?

When you go to Tools --> Macro

you may have to expand the list at that point to allow you to see the option "Security" (it's in same list with Macros option).

Choose the Security option.

On the [Security Level] tab, I recommend choosing the Medium setting.  This will alert you to the fact that there are macros in a workbook that has them and allow you to choose whether to Enable (allow to run) or [Disable] (do not allow them to be used) the macros in that workbook.

This is about the safest, most convenient (or least inconvenient) setting - it is the setting I use on my system, and darned near all my workbooks have macros in them.  I consider the one-extra-mouse-click worth the time to remind me that a workbook I might receive from an unknown source has macros in it and might be unsafe.

You choose [Enable] or [Disable] based on your trust of the source of the workbook.  If you trust the source, choose [Enable] otherwise choose disable..

IMPORTANT: After you change the setting and [OK] out of the dialogs, close Excel and re-open it.  The changed setting does not take effect until you do this.

This setting is applied to Excel, not a specific workbook. So you don't have to worry about changing it for all of your workbooks, one by one.

OTHER VERSIONS OF EXCEL:

If you are using Excel 2007, you could put your workbook into a trusted location using the Trust Center settings.

If you are using Excel 2010, you can either put the workbook into a trusted location using the Trust Center settings, or declare it to be a trusted workbook.

The equivalent macro security setting is available in both 2007/2010 also.  If you need specific help with Excel 2007/2010 settings, let us know which version of Excel you are using.  I've assumed pre-2007 based on the wording of post and the reference to Tools-->Macro.
