a)

Don't put CMYK images into PowerPoint.  In the past, it was a guaranteed way to end up with partially digested CMYK images regurgitated onto your shoes.  PowerPoint would become ill or die entirely due to them.

It may or may not still do so, but it's going to convert them to RGB anyhow, so there's no point in using them in the first place.

If you plan on taking a project to CMYK output on a printing press or the like, PowerPoint's not the tool for it.  A dedicated DTP program (MS Publisher, InDesign, Quark, Ventura Publisher, Serif Publisher etc) would be the best choice.
