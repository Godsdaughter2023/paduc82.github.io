artboyle say:

trimming a video in several places

Newbie to video editing! I need some help in learning how to use the trim feature. I need to delete - cut out, trim several places in a video of a kids basketball game. How do I accomplish this? Every time I think I have it, I lose the ret of the video as the whole thing gets cut out.
considerate_guy say:

If you need to make several edits to the same clip, you should use the "Split" feature. On the Video Tools | Edit tab, there is a button for "Split". But as a shortcut, you can use the "M" key on the keyboard.

Here's how I like to edit using the Split feature. Let's assume we had a clip that was made up of good and bad parts where you want to edit out the bad parts and only keep the good parts. Visually, imagine the clip looks like this:

BBBGGGGGBBBBBGGGGGBB

Play the clip until you are at a point where the good part starts. Pause and make a split.

BBB|GGGGGBBBBBGGGGGBB

Now play until that good part ends and make another split:

BBB|GGGGG|BBBBBGGGGGBB

Repeat the process putting splits between the good and bad parts until it looks like this:

BBB|GGGGG|BBBBB|GGGGG|BB

So in this example you have 3 bad parts interspersed with 2 good parts. Right-click on one of the bad parts and choose "Remove". Repeat for the other two bad parts. When you are done, you'll have just the two good parts.

GGGGG|GGGGG

The internet TV does require higher bandwidth. Higher quality video such as video in high definition (720p+) requires higher bandwidth and faster connection speeds. The general accepted kbps download rate needed to stream high definition video is 3500, where as standard definition TV can range from 500 to 1500 kbps depending on the resolution on screen.

Method 1:
You may reduce the hardware acceleration and check if this helps -

1. Close Expression Media if it is open.

5. Click Advanced Settings, click the Troubleshoot tab, and then click Change Settings.

7. Close Personalize appearance and sounds.
