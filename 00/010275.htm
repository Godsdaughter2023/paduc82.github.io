The default (the way Word comes from the factory) for the Normal style and all styles based on Normal includes a setting called "Widow/Orphan control". In printer's lingo, a widow is the last line or two of a paragraph that appears by itself at the top of a page, and an orphan is a line or two that appears at the bottom of a page. These conditions are usually considered to be bad practice in typesetting (see Wikipedia).

When Widow/Orphan control is turned on for a paragraph in Word, if an orphan would occur because of the ordinary layout rules, the program forces the entire paragraph to the next page.

You can turn off Widow/Orphan control for a specific paragraph:
  • Right-click in the paragraph and choose Paragraph from the menu.
  • Click the Line and Page Breaks tab in the dialog.
  • Clear the check box for Widow/Orphan control.
You can, if you prefer, turn off Widow/Orphan control for the Normal style, which will cause it to be turned off in all paragraphs that have that style:
  • Right-click the Normal style in the Quick Styles gallery of the Home ribbon, and choose Modify.
  • If you want this change to affect the Normal template, so it applies to all new documents based on that template in the future, click the option button for "New documents based on this template".
  • Click the Format button at the bottom of the dialog and choose Paragraph from the menu.
  • Click the Line and Page Breaks tab in the dialog.
  • Clear the check box for Widow/Orphan control.
There are ways to keep the Widow/Orphan control turned on (and so avoid the poor appearance that might cause) but still make the text on all pages fill all the way to the bottom margin. For information on these techniques, see Improving the bottom line.
