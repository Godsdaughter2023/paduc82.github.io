Conditionally Formatting the Colour of a Destination Cell based upon - Microsoft Community

dj mutton jeff say:

Conditionally Formatting the Colour of a Destination Cell based upon the Colours of other Cells

I am looking to conditionally format a destination cell Red, Amber or Green based upon the colours of a (Red, Amber or Green) range of cells as returned through conditional formatting applied to those cells. e.g. if 1 cell above is Red then conditionally format the destination cell Red, if 2 cells are Amber then conditionally format the destination cell Amber, if 3 cells are Amber then conditionally format the destination cell Red and finally if anything else then conditionally format the destination cell Green.

bernie deitrick say: You would need to build up a function that would return TRUE for those cases - based on the conditions that CF the cells above. The conditions could be just about anything, so we need more description....

rich007a say:

Hi,

You can't do that based on the cell colour from conditional formatting.

 

You should place a formula in a cell (could be the coloured cell or cell hidden elsewhere) that computes a value (e.g. "R", "A" or "G") to represent the Red, Amber and Green status, using the same logic that you used for when applying the Conditional Formatting. 

 

You can then do conditional formatting based on the value of those cells.

 

e.g. if your three cells are in X1, X2 and X3, then this formula will do the following logic:

 

if 1 cell above is Red then conditionally format the destination cell Red, 
 
    =IF(COUNTIF($X$1:$X$3,"R")=1,"R",
 
if 2 cells are Amber then conditionally format the destination cell Amber, 
 
    IF(COUNTIF($X$1:$X$3,"A")=2,"A",
 
if 3 cells are Amber then conditionally format the destination cell Red 
 
    IF(COUNTIF($X$1:$X$3,"A")=3,"R",
 
and finally if anything else then conditionally format the destination cell Green 
 
    "G")))

 

Put that all together and you get:

 

=IF(COUNTIF($X$1:$X$3,"R")=1,"R",IF(COUNTIF($X$1:$X$3,"A")=2,"A",IF(COUNTIF($X$1:$X$3,"A")=3,"R","G")))

 

If you put a combination of R/A/G into cells X1:X3, you'll be able to confirm I got this formula right.  You can then do conditional formatting of other cells based on the value of the cell containing this formula.

 

If doing CF on another cell, you'll need to do Conditional Formatting > New Rule > Use a formula to determin which cells to format > then enter a formula like:

 

=$Z$1="R"

 

(where Z1 is the cell containing the formula from above), add formatting (red) for that condition > OK > OK.

 

Then go back into CF > Manage Rules > Add rule > and add two more rules for

=$Z$1="A" and =$Z$1="G"

 

Make sense?

Hope that helps.

Cheers
Rich

  1. Select the text inside the text box.

zigzag3143 (ms -mvp) say:
Contacts are now called people.

To access People from Outlook.com, hover the cursor over the Outlook logo in the top left and click on the down-arrow that appears to the right of the Outlook logo.
Select People. 

Returning to your Inbox from People:
Hover the cursor over the People logo and click on the down-arrow that appears on the right of the People logo. Select Mail.  
You can also get to Calendar or Skydrive in the same manner.

Filtering your list:
Above the search box at the top of the Contact list on the left, is a filter option which says "All ". 
If you click on this you should see the groups you created in the past.  
Click on any of these to filter your list to just the contacts in that group.

Selecting one or many Contacts:
If you click on a contact name in the list, the details for that contact will appear on the right.  
Alternatively, if you click on the check box to the left of the contact picture, you can build up a list of selected contacts on the right by clicking on the check box to the left of multiple contacts.

Adding/Removing from Group membership:
Whether you select one or many contacts, you will see a top menu bar option for Groups.  
If you click on this, then it will display the list of groups with a check box checked for each group that the contact(s) is/are a member of. 

You can uncheck to remove or check to add, the selection of contact(s) to the chosen groups, and then click on Apply to apply the changes.  
Your selected contacts will then be updated to be added or removed from the groups you chose as appropriate.

Delete
When you select one or many contacts, you will see a top menu bar option for Delete.  
If you click on Delete and click Delete in the confirmation box, the selected contacts will be deleted.

Edit:
You can choose edit if you have clicked on a single contact (name or picture) in the list on the left to see their details on the right.  
When viewing their details, click on Edit in the top menu bar to edit or add details.
