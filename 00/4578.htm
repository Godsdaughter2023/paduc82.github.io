jay freedman say:
Suzanne, that's true in Word 2003 and 2007 but not in 2010. There, WordArt has been converted to the new kind of text effects that are also available in regular text by use of the "glowing A" button in the Home > Font group. That kind of text supports solid and gradient fills but not patterns, pictures, or textures.

The only workaround I've found is to create a custom watermark (remember to set it to horizontal instead of diagonal); open the header pane and cut the watermark to the clipboard; go into the document body and paste. This magically deposits the former watermark as an old-style WordArt object, and opens a WordArt Tools contextual tab that's otherwise unavailable. On that tab the Shape Fill button offers pattern, picture, and texture choices as well as colors and gradients. The Edit Text button also lets you change the text and choose the font and size. I find it interesting that doing this doesn't force the document into Compatibility Mode.

Also, to answer McCoy's item #1, you can choose any piece of text, click the down arrow on the Shading button (the paint bucket icon in the Paragraph group on the Home tab), choose More Colors, and fill the background of the selected text with any color from the full range (the dialog looks the same as the one for foreground colors).

Here's something I picked up a long time ago so no credit to the original poster. Right click your userform, view code and paste the code below in.

 

Now if you double click the form it becomes transparent. A further single click and it becomes opaque. Note the 2 bold numbers in the code. 255 is opaque and 50 is transparent. Play with the 50 to get the transparenct you want but i would suggest that 50 be your minimum value.

Public Function TranslucentForm(frm As UserForm, TranslucenceLevel As Byte) As Boolean
SetWindowLong hwnd, GWL_EXSTYLE, WS_EX_LAYERED

 lodialog = UserForm1.Caption
