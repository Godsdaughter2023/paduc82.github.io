What is the best practice for handling departing employees? - Microsoft Community

marcrohde say:

What is the best practice for handling departing employees?

As we migrate more users to OneDrive for Business I'm wondering what others are doing to handle files from departing employees?  With our old File Servers we would typically give managers access to users folders for a given time period to clear them out.

There don't appear to be great options to handle this with OneDrive for Business--or I may just now know how to handle this.

Thanks for your input!

linda wu msft say: Hi MarcRohde,

To handle files from departing employees, the best practices is adding your account as the owner of this employee’s OneDrive for Business. Here are the steps:

1.    Navigate to the SharePoint admin center > User Profiles > People > Manage User Profiles.
2.    Enter the user name > Find.
3.    Right click the user’s Account name > Manage Site Collection Owners.
4.    Select your account in Primary Site Collection Administrator and Site Collection Administrators > delete the former user's account > click Ok.

 

Once you become the owner of this user’s OneDrive for Business, you can login to his/her OneDrive for Business and sync files to your computer with the OneDrive for Business sync client to retain the back-up data.

Then you can delete the user’s account. If a user (say a departing employee) no longer needs a license, you can delete that user’s account. After you do this, any licenses that were assigned to that person become available to assign to another user.
 
CAUTION: When a user’s license is removed, all data that is associated with that user account is held for 30 days. After the 30 day grace period, the data is deleted and can’t be recovered, except for documents that are saved on SharePoint Online and the data in the user's mailbox. The user mailbox will remain in Exchange Online until it is deleted, permanently removed or purged by the Office 365 admin. You can reassign a license to the user and make the mailbox active again.

For more information, please see the article below:
https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Assign-or-remove-licenses-for-Office-365-for-business-997596b5-4173-4627-b915-36abac6786dc?ui=en-US&rs=en-US&ad=US

Let us know any update at your convenience.
