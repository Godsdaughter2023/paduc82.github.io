cbarnhorst say:

It sounds like you are building a new Hyper-VM.  There are two ways to activate build 14965 in a vm.  The first is to have an activated copy of Windows 10 installed in the vm first and then upgrade or clean install that to 14965.  The second is to have a Windows 10 product key and clean install 14965.

A Windows 10 Hyper-V vm requires a separate license from the host.  A Windows 10 vm must be a registered Windows 10 device in its own right.

The issue with vm's is that they are portable and therefore cannot take their entitlement from any host.  IT managers depend on being able to move vm's between hosts in order to balance loads and adjust network performance.  No one would want to have to reactivate a vm every time it is moved for these purposes.

The account should be used by one person at a singular location for their personal use.  You are actually not supposed to share the account as per Skype's Terms of Use.  I believe you may be using it in a manner that is unintended.  The call will ring on all signed in clients but there can be a different latency between one client and another based off several variables that are ISP or network-related.  Results may differ if an alternate ISP or network equipment is used.

If you are not sharing a singular account and forwarding is occurring to reach the opposite party, then there may be a delay in a hard-coded amount of seconds that equates to 3 or 4 rings (15+).
