neo zhu- [o365] say:

Hi MichaelRaymond3472,

Just as @Vasil mentioned, currently, it’s not feasible to apply litigation hold to all the newly created users automatically in Office 365. Thanks for your understanding.

Instead, we can merely apply it to all the existing user mailboxes via this cmdlet:

Get-Mailbox -ResultSize Unlimited -Filter {RecipientTypeDetails -eq "UserMailbox"} | Set-Mailbox -LitigationHoldEnabled $True

And for new users created in future, you can use:

Get-Mailbox | Where {$_.LitigationHoldEnabled -match "False"} | ForEach-Object { $Identity = $_.alias; Set-Mailbox -Identity $Identity -LitigationHoldEnabled $True }

Note that it’s required to Connect to Exchange Online using remote PowerShell at first.

Also, as the idea is constructive which may also benefit many other Office 365 administrators, we welcome and encourage you to share your ideas through office365.uservoice.com/.../273493-office-365-admin. Understanding your experience helps us to make our product and service better for you and others. Actually, many features came directly from your feedback.

Neo

It’s not possible to insert or embed the audio in Word Online. However, you can do this in Word client. Please follow the steps below to do so for your online word document:

  1. Click “Edit in Word” to open it in Word client.
  2. Refer to Insert an object in Word or Outlook, click Insert tab in Word client>In the text section, select Object.
  3. Refer to Video: Insert online video, click Insert tab in Word client>In the Media section, select Online video.
  4. Save the document and wait for syncing the change to Word Online.

 

In this way, you can successfully embed or insert audio into your Word Online document.

This procedure was posted under the thread in martin's 2nd to the last reply. Works perfectly!!!

MFG is a hidden admin app on the phone.  Here are the steps -- !!! use at your own risk !!!:

  1. Go to the phone app
