Palcouk got his "5's and 6's" mixed up (this time, he usually gets it right)

#1 365 Home is for personal, NOT business use.  You, the renter of the 365 Home Subscription are allowed to "share" your subscription with up to 5 other people, for a total of 6 people (which can be run on up to a possible 30 computers).

Each person is allowed to have Office 365 installed/activated on up to 5 computers at the same time. They do have control, through the MyAccount page, so that they can "deactivate" one of their 5 current machines to allow them to install on another machine.

#2 365 Business Premium.

this is the bundle offered to "small" businesses. It means you can rent Office 365 Business Premium for UP TO 300 employees. In other words, if you have more than 300 employees you would have to get a different, more expensive, Office 365 bundle.  You pay for a separate license for each of those UP TO 300 employees.  Each license/subscription is allowed to be active on up to 5 computers at the same time. Like 365 Home, the user can control the machines their license is "active" on, so they can move around between more than 5 machines, it just takes an extra step to go to a machine that is not one of their current activations.

You cannot have a single "main" Office 365 license/account shared by 50 people on 50 machines.  That is a complete violation of the license terms. If they are using Office, each user must have their own Office subscription.

Any way to NOT import other presentation template layouts? - Microsoft Community

laura_od say:

Any way to NOT import other presentation template layouts?

I am updating old presentations into a new look/feel with a custom color theme and custom layouts. When I "insert slides from other presentation", other slide layouts (which I have not created) appear in my document. Is this because Powerpoint cannot find an equivalent slide layout, so it brings in the rogue slide layouts from the old presentation? Is there any way to force the slides to only use one of the layout options in the new presentation?

Again, thanks so much for any input. This forum has been very helpful.

steve rindsberg say:
There is.  It's just a bit less apparent than it might be:

Insert | Slides From | Other Presentation

Select the file you want to insert slides from.

Instead of "Insert all slides", choose "Select slides to insert" (even if you do want to grab 'em all)

Click "Insert"

Make sure there's no checkmark next to "Keep design of original slides"

Select the slides you want to insert then click "Insert" or if you prefer, click "Insert All"

Voila.  Slides but no extra layouts/masters.



laura_od say: Hmmm. I did this, but I'm still getting the imported layouts/masters.

steve rindsberg say:
It may be because there are shapes/other content added to the masters/layouts of the incoming slides that are not on the masters/layouts of the presentation you're importing them into.

In a case like that, PPT would have only two choices:  Throw away the extra content or add a new master/layout.  People would get pretty angry if PPT started arbitrarily chopping off bits of their slides, so instead, it adds the extra masters/layouts.

You can get around this after the fact by assigning the new slides to one of the original layouts.  Once no slides are assigned to the "rogue" layouts, you can delete them.  The layouts, that is.  ;-)
