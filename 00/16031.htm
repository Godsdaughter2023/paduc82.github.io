How to change headings that work with an automatic table of - Microsoft Community

joshuaweinstein444 say:

How to change headings that work with an automatic table of contents?

So, I am editing a simple word doc in microsoft word 2016 for mac Home, and when I make a custom heading, and place it in my text, the automatic table of contents generator does not recognize it.

However, when I use the built-in headings, like Heading 1, and Heading 2, the ones with the light blue color to them, those do get recognized by the table of contents.

How does one make a custom heading be recognized by an automatic table of contents?

john mcghie say:

The shortest and easiest answer to your question is "Don't use custom heading styles" :-)

Of course you can, and I will tell you how in a minute.  But customising the BUILT-IN heading styles for your purpose is so much easier and a LOT less work.

The simplest way to customise the built-in headings is to select one of your desired headings, right-click the appropriate level of heading style, and choose "Update to match selection". It's that easy, and your TOC, page-numbering, running heading replication etc will all "just work".

Then apply Heading 1 through Heading 9 as appropriate to your headings.

To create your own Heading style, you need to:

  1. Create a paragraph style
  2. Set "Add to template" to add each of the styles you create to your template
  3. Add the paragraph style to the Styles chunk on the home tab
  4. Modify the Styles Pane to show your new style
  5. Modify the style to set the appropriate Outline Level
  6. Modify the Paragraph/Line and Page Breaks appropriately for a Heading style
  7. Create at least three more paragraph styles for each of your sub-level headings
  8. Modify your level 1 through 4 styles to set up the "Based on" property for each to be the higher style
  9. Insert a Table of contents
  10. Modify the Table of Contents to include your styles
  11. Assign a level in the table of contents to each of your created styles.
  12. Update your page numbering to give it the name of the style you started the Chapter with.
  13. Apply your styles to the headings in the document.

The most important parts of that list are 5 and 11.  The TOC Generator recognises styles by their Outline Level or their Name. It's easiest to get the Outline Levels right (critical to do so if you are using numbering). If you have specified the correct Outline Level, then the TOC Generator will automatically suggest the appropriate TOC level for each style.

To set the outline level for a style, right-click it in the Styles chunk (or display the Styles task pane, change the list to show "All styles", find your style, and choose Modify Style).

From the Modify Style dialog, choose Format>Paragraph>Indents and Spacing and set the Outline Level to something other than Body Text. Each of your heading styles must have a different outline level, starting with "1" for the Chapter headings.

To Customise the Table of Contents, you must first insert a Custom Table of Contents. Beside the Table of Contents button on the Ribbon is a tiny disclosure triangle.  Click it to drop down the list and choose Custom table of contents. The Custom Table of Contents is the only one you can adjust the formatting of.  All of the others will revert to the built-in formatting every time they update.

When the Table of Contents dialog appears, its Format should be set to "From template". If not, change it, otherwise your changes will be wiped out on each update.

Now click Options. From the Options dialog, choose "Build Table of Contents from Styles" if it is not already checked.  Uncheck "Table entry fields" if it is checked.

Now run through and type a level number beside the name of each of your custom heading styles.  The number sets the level in the ToC that headings with that style will appear.

OK your way out and allow the ToC to generate.

You can change the formatting of the ToC entries by changing the styles that Word automatically applies to format them.  You can do this by either directly formatting one of the ToC entries at each level, or by clicking the Table of Contents button on the Ribbon and choosing Modify. The Table of Contents entries have all been formatted by Word automatically applying the ToC styles.  These styles are set to "Update automatically" by default, so if you change a ToC entry, you will automatically update its style. However, unless you set "From template" in the Format options, all your changes will be wiped out the next time the ToC updates (which it should do automatically whenever you print).

It goes without saying that the formatting of the ToC entries has nothing to do with the headings in the document: although some types of character formatting applied to a heading will carry through to the ToC.

As you can see: rolling your own heading styles is a lot of work.  I always customise the built-in headings and use those.  If you need more detail, please post back: I will try to find the time to type all the rest of the information out over the next month or so if you need it. Remember: I am a volunteer, I have a day job :-)

Cheers
