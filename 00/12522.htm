rick rothstein, mvp - excel say:

Try running this macro...

Sub ReplaceInternalSpacesWithComma()
  Dim Rng As Range
  Set Rng = Selection
  Rng = Evaluate("IF(FIND("" ""," & Rng.Address & _
                 "&"" ""),SUBSTITUTE(TRIM(" & _
                 Rng.Address & "),"" "","",""))")
End Sub

I have set it up to process whatever cells are selected, but if you know the specifice range you want to process, then just change the code line that begins with "Set" to this...

Set Rng = Range("B1:U600")

where you would change the B1:U600 part (leave the quote marks) to the actual range's address.

a. user say:

Hi,


There's a lot of possible reason why your emails is automatically deleted.

Here are some:

Schedule cleanup
Schedule cleanup automatically moves or deletes email from a specific sender after 30, 60, or 90 days. If you subscribe to a daily deal site (like LivingSocial or Groupon) or newsletter and never want to see anything but the most recent message, you can use a scheduled cleanup to do the work for you.


Sweep

If you get email from a certain person or business that you want to keep, you can quickly move all email from them out of your inbox and into another folder.


Hacked/hijacked

A hacker had already deleted emails in their account before you renamed the account.

To prevent your account from being hijacked/hacked account, we recommend you to change your passowrd immediately and create a strong password.
