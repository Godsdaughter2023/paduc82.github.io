There are no legal ways to download free music other than purchasing your own and adding it to your phone. Songs are copywrited to protect the legal rights of the artist and companies that administer their works, whether they are living or dead.

Even recordings of songs that are in the public domain can have copyrights assigned to the performing artist(s) and the distributor(s) of the original recording. The recording is often under copyright and these rights were established under the Berne Convention in the 1900s when recorded sound was in it's infancy, not only for music but literature and other intellectual properties.

If you find a site that offers you free downloads of copyrighted materials it is in violation of these copyright laws. The major example here was the seizure of the Napster site following a successful lawsuit from the RIAA which is a US trade organization of media/recording companies.

If you have the right to own a copy of materials and have done so you can make copies for your personal and non-commercial use, but you should have bought these materials. Taping programs 'off air' for later viewing is usually also legal as defined in the so-called 'Betamax' case where in that case it was ruled legal to record a TV show to watch later for your personal use. You cannot sell that recording commercially or show it commercially via a recording, whether you got it from the company producing it or taped it yourself...you can generally buy stuff secondhand at yard sales or thrift stores but not in bulk.
