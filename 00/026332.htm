samwang_msft say:

hi gerard,
i have read through the post and would like to answer your questions one by one.
1. scl being set to -1 means the transport rule to bypass the spam filtering is working.
2. regarding the second question, it’s not feasible to whitelist a bulk mailer without weakening the bcl level directly. generally, bulk mailers vary in their sending patterns, content creation, and list acquisition practices. bcl ratings range from 1 to 9 depending on how likely the bulk mailer is to generate complaints. microsoft uses both internal and third-party sources to identify bulk mail and determine the appropriate bcl. as your bcl filter level is set to 5 and the bcl value of the email is 6, they will be marked as spam. since you don’t want to weaken your bcl level, we recommend you change your spam and bulk email actions to move message to junk email folder at first and also add the sender to allow list as an end user. here are some articles for your reference:



how to change spam and bulk email actions:
https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/jj200684(v=exchg.150).aspx

add senders to allow list in outlook:
https://support.office.com/en-us/article/overview-of-the-junk-email-filter-364853fe-095a-4563-b3ff-0b5b0ac7eeb3?ui=en-us&rs=en-us&ad=us

however, if the issue persists after all these, you can refer to this article to submit false positive to our relevant teams. the spam analysis team will examine your submitted messages and adjusts the spam filters accordingly in order to prevent future abuse.
