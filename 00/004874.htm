suzanne s. barnhill say:

There are three parts to any TOC:

  1. The content of the TOC: This is based on the TOC field code.
  2. The content of the entries: This is based on headings in the document.
  3. The appearance: This is based on the TOC styles.

Naturally, the specific entries will depend on the content of a particular document, but you can save the TOC field code as a building block and the TOC styles in a template. See http://wordfaqs.ssbarnhill.com/TOCTips.htm#SaveWork

charles kenyon say:

Suzanne's article on TOC Tips and Tricks is one of my favorites.

You would save the Table of Contents, which is a field by selecting it and pressing Alt+F3. You could save it as a Table of Contents or as AutoText. If you save as a Table of Contents it should show up in your Table of Contents gallery.

However, if what you are saving is how your table of contents looks, those are the TOC styles. These are completely separate from the field itself. The field controls what goes into the TOC, the styles control how it looks.


I think the easiest method is to simply have a template with these styles and create a new document based on them. Next easiest is to use the Organizer  to copy the styles from an existing document/template into your document. You can also attach to a template and check the box to update styles. I would do this with a copy of your document in case you do not like other styles that are imported.

One way you could save these would be as a custom quick style set. To do this you would want to modify each of the TOC styles to have them appear in the Quick Styles. Then you would likely want to modify all other styles to not appear in the Quick Styles Gallery. Then save the style set.

Another way to update only these styles would be a macro solution. See A Global StyleSheet in Microsoft Word?. That essentially uses a macro to automatically use the Organizer.

Just a bit more code.  Use:

Dim rngfound As Range
Selection.HomeKey wdStory
Selection.Find.ClearFormatting
With Selection.Find
    Do While .Execute(findText:="<[A-Z]{2,}>", Forward:=True, _

        With Selection.Range
