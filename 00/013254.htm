Hi SladeSO and bubbaet,

 

I understand your frustrations about receiving a lot of junk mails after your account has been upgraded to Outlook.com. To prevent getting spam email, set your Junk-filtering level to exclusive. This will block all incoming messages and you will receive emails only from trusted senders.
Please follow these steps to setup your junk e-mail filter in Outlook:

 

1. Sign in with your Microsoft account.
2. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the gear icon, and then click More mail settings.
3. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.
4. Under Filters and reporting, select the options that you want, and then click Save.
 
If you choose Exclusive for your junk e-mail filter, only messages from e-mail addresses on your Contacts or Allowed senders lists appear in your Inbox. Messages from senders not on those lists are automatically sent to the Junk e-mail folder.

 

You should occasionally check your junk e-mail folder to make sure that good messages are not sent there by mistake.

If the mail provider has server-side adjustments you can make, they will affect what you see in the Windows Mail client.
