Hi Kelbob,


The display may be unstable when you connect your Surface Book 2 to your external monitors through the Surface Dock because of a software or a hardware problem. You mentioned that you have updated the Surface Dock to the latest version. Was that done using the Surface Dock Updater tool? Also, have you tried to directly connect the Surface to the external monitor/s, bypassing the Surface Dock? Is the setup working that way?

It will also be helpful if you check and reseat the connections to make sure that the problem is not caused by a poor physical connection. This factor sometimes gets overlooked and therefore results to a shaky output.

Another factor that may affect the display is the resolution and refresh rate of the monitors. I suggest that you try different settings to see if there will be an improvement with the display. You can change the resolution by going to Settings > System > Display > Resolution. To change the screen refresh rate, open Display adapter properties and go to the Monitor tab. You will see Screen refresh rate under Monitor settings. You may check this page for a guide in performing this suggestion. Refer to the section about How many screens can I connect.

Lastly, to check the system for possible software errors, you can run the Software Diagnostic Toolkit. To learn more about how this tool can help in resolving the issue, you can visit this page.
