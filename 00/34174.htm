how to undo a clean boot/diagnostic startup on windows 10 and - Microsoft Community

jensenshoemaker say:

how to undo a clean boot/diagnostic startup on windows 10 and start windows normally (on surface pro 4 with keyboard)

so I came across this problem and couldn't find what I was looking for but ended up figuring it out and wanted to post it to help others.

so pretty much I did a clean boot on my surface pro 4 (windows 10) and I selected "diagnostic startup". I reset and there were no programs or start button or anything, couldn't just undo it because of this.

how I did a clean reboot (keyboard needed):

-run.exe

-msconfig.exe

-general tab

-I pressed diagnostic startup (don't do this, search up how to properly do a clean boot but this way will just give you a blank windows on startup, resetting your computer doesn't undo this)

how I undid this screw up:

-two-button shutdown (this is on a surface pro 4 again. hold down the power button and volume up button until it turned off and turned on again (or just turned on) and there was a new screen with several options and a computer info button

-(here's where it's a bit iffy because I don't know how it's properly supposed to be done but this is what worked for me):

-this screen I'll call a settings screen and it'll go to this automatically when the next part doesn't work

-I changed some of the settings on the settings screen and changed them back

-two-button shut-down again

-press all the f# buttons just running my finger up and down through them all while it turned back on

-new screen again! not the settings screen (if failed, try again. two-button startup, f# keys until you get it, or change and put back the settings on the settings screen. I just messed around until I got it. just be careful not to accidentally change any settings for real on the settings screen)

-it'll say something along the lines of "windows didn't start correctly" and will give you the option of continuing your startup or to change some settings

-click the settings

-new settings! find something along the lines of "configure reboot settings"

-it'll say to do this you need to reset, press the button saying to do so

-next parts is all keyboard:

-it'll reset and give you settings one by one and you enter the stuff with your keyboard

-one of the prompts is a bitlocker recovery key, to find this go to https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/help/17133/windows-8-bitlocker-recovery-keys-frequently-asked-questions (I got my code through my microsoft account log-in)

-continue through settings things until you get numbered options of how to reset

-click "5-reset in safe-mode with network" (4-safe-mode might work too, I don't know)

-after it resets, click the windows key and R

-enter msconfig

-change settings to "normal startup"

-dialog box will pop-up asking if you want to reset, click yes to do so

-and now it should be back to normal!

hopefully this helps someone.

Hi Iannyl,

Based on the information you've provided, you may be encountering a corrupted user profile. Most of the time, the system automatically creates a backup of the user profile on your device. We can use Registry Editor to restore the backup so you'll have access to your computer again. To do this, follow these steps:

This section, method, or task contains steps that tell you how to modify the registry. However, serious problems might occur if you modify the registry incorrectly. Therefore, make sure that you follow these steps carefully. For added protection, back up the registry before you modify it. Then, you can restore the registry if a problem occurs. For more information about how to back up and restore the registry, refer to the following Microsoft Knowledge Base article: How to back up and restore the registry in Windows.

  1. On the Login screen, hold the Shift key on your keyboard, click the Power button and select Restart. Performing this step will bring you to a new screen.


  2. Click Troubleshoot and select Advanced options.



  3. In the Advanced options window, click Command Prompt. This will bring you to a new screen with a black window. On the Command Prompt window, type regedit and press Enter.





  4. Click File, select Export.... This will allow us to save a back up of the whole registry in case we need to change it back again. Choose a location where you want to save the backup and click Save.



  5. In Registry Editor, locate and then click the following registry subkey:
    HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows NT\CurrentVersion\ProfileList

  6. In the navigation pane, locate the folder that begins with S-1-5 (SID key) followed by a long number.

  7. Click each S-1-5 folder, locate the ProfileImagePath entry in the details pane, and then double-click to make sure that this is the user account profile that has the error.



    • If you have two folders starting with S-1-5 followed by some long numbers and one of them ended with .bak, you have to rename the .bak folder. To do this, follow these steps:
      1. Right-click the folder without .bak, and then click Rename. Type .ba, and then press Enter.



      2. Right-click the folder that is named .bak, and then click Rename. Remove .bak at the end of the folder name, and then press Enter.



      3. Right-click the folder that is named .ba, and then click Rename. Change the .ba to .bak at the end of the folder name, and then press Enter.



    • If you have only one folder starting with S-1-5 that is followed by long numbers and ends with .bak. Right-click the folder, and then click Rename. Remove .bak at the end of the folder name, and then press Enter.

  8. Click the folder without .bak in the details pane, double-click RefCount, type 0, and then click OK.



  9. Click the folder without .bak, in the details pane, double-click State, type 0, and then click OK.



  10. Close Registry Editor.

  11. Restart the computer.

  12. Log on again with your account.

Keep us updated with the results of the solution above.
