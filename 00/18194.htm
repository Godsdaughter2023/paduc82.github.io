VLookup is a stupid function, it errors out in many situations with the same useless error.

Another reason might be that Lookup_Range has not enough columns... means your sheet layout doesn't fit to your code.

I recommend to use RANGE.FIND instead, see sample below.

Function GetDept_Name(ByVal strDept_Code As Variant) As Variant
  Dim Here As Range
  'In this sheet...
  With Sheets("HR Data")
    '... search in the 1st column
    Set Here = .Columns(1).Find(strDept_Code, LookIn:=xlValues, LookAt:=xlWhole)
    'Found?
    If Here Is Nothing Then
      'No
      GetDept_Name = "(none)"
    Else
      'Yes, get the value from column B
      GetDept_Name = .Range("B" & Here.Row)
    End If
  End With
End Function

a. user say:

Unfortunately, pivot tables have their own formatting, which will overwrite your formatting until you specifically tell it otherwise.

I think the simplest solution is a very simple macro, assuming you are ok with re-entering the rules for your conditional formatting.

We are not going to program a macro in VBA, just use the macro recorder.  First, hit the record macro button, then refresh your table, put in your conditional formatting, and hit stop recording.

Now any time you want to refresh, use your macro and it will re-apply the formatting afterwards.
