greginmich say:

The basic principle of “Limited Periodic Scanning” can be used in any OS, and with any primary AV app – you just have to run your periodic scans manually in previous OSs; and if you’re using a Microsoft AV app, then you have to do your periodic scanning with third-party malware-removal tools. This is something that’s especially important for people using MSE – you should be aggressively supplementing your protection with third-party apps for the same basic reason that I identified here for Windows Defender:

https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/protect/forum/protect_defender-protect_scanning/windows-defender-onoff/0fef8596-394d-4e43-b7b1-52121235c028

Periodic scanning with third-party AV/Antimalware apps is something that I’ve been recommending in this forum for a long time, starting in the days when third-party AV/Antimalware apps were still considered a verboten topic here – but you can supplement your protection seven ways to Sunday and that won’t help you one bit with this Tech Support Scam issue. That’s because this issue isn’t with file-based malware – it’s with modal dialog loops that are created with malicious scripts that are running on the scam webpage itself – and as such, the responsibility for managing these malicious scripts has been delegated to browser developers.

Most browsers have adopted some form of Dialog Loop Protection that allows users to end the offensive script by putting a tick in a checkbox that appears in the dialog itself after it gets looped for the first time (and then attempting to close the page again). But there’s an amazing lack of interest in educating people about this workaround, and there's also a serious effort on part of the scam coders to work around the workaround – so the near-term prospects for this issue don’t look good at all:

https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/protect/forum/protect_other-protect_scanning/facebook-malvertising-tech-support-scam/bbf384ba-b9a6-4ce9-afe1-eaeb7f35a89e
