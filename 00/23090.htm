Are you using Outlook installed on your desktop? What type of email account(s) do you have configured in Outlook? POP, IMAP, Outlook.com, or Microsoft Exchange?

When Outlook desktop keeps downloading messages over and over, it means something is causing the message tracking to become corrupt and outlook doesn't properly save the last message download count.

How frequently are you checking for new mail? Set it to at least every 5 min, preferably higher for at least the short term.

If you don't need to keep the messages on the server, deleting them off the server will stop the problem. If you do need to keep them and can easily move them out of the inbox, that will also stop Outlook from downloading them.

Otherwise, create a rule to delete (or move to a new folder) mail received before yesterday. This will get it out of your Inbox.

gary zhu msft support say:

Hi Andy,

 

Regarding “The rule you've selected can't currently be viewed in Outlook Web App. Please use Outlook to view it”, this is due to the inbox rule is created in the Outlook client.

To edit it in the Outlook client, please follow the steps below：

  1. Open the Outlook client.
  2. Click Rules > Manage Rules & Alerts in ribbon panel (at the top of Outlook client).

3. Double click the rules that you want to modify in the pop-up window.

 

Please post back if you have any question about edit rules in Outlook client.

 

Thanks,

Gary

andyhojnacki- [o365] say: That just shows my rules. Not the rules of any of the shared mailboxes.

ruel mayoya msft say:

Hi Andy,

Since you have full permissions for the Shared mailbox, you can also view shared mailbox rules on OWA.

To check the rules, follow these steps:

1.)    Login to OWA, click the Picture on the top right corner, then click “Open another mailbox…”.


2.)    Type the email address of the Shared mailbox in the space provided, then click Open.


3.)    After the Shared mailbox opens, go to the Gear icon located at the top right corner, go to Mail Options > Mail > Automatic processing > Inbox and sweep rules.


You can now modify or create your inbox rules for this Shared mailbox.

Post back if you have any questions regarding these steps.

Thanks,

Ruel

andyhojnacki- [o365] say:

Since the rules were created in the outlook client, they are not editable in OWA (They are greyed out).  I found the fix.

1. Open Outlook Client

2. File-->Add Account

3. On Auto Account Setup screen. Enter the email address of shared mailbox.

4. On the credential screen, use the account that has full permissions to the shared mailbox.

5. Restart Oulook.

6. Click Rules-->Manage rules and alerts

7. Click the Drop down box and select the inbox of the shared mailbox.
