bill.p. say: That would be the reason some of them don't work - they're DRM protected.

DRM stands for Digital Rights Management and is copy-protection software installed by the CD publisher to prevent copying, or to limit the number of copies that can be made. It's likely that the WMA files were registered to the PC you originally copied the files to from the CD, but after copying them to a different PC the DRM activates and won't allow playback on a different PC to the one the CDs were originally copied to.

Annoying, I know. But that's how copyright protection works with modern CDs. If you'd like to play the ones that don't work on the new PC, I think you'll need to copy them to that PC from the CDs again!

Hi Marcelo,

 

1.      Does the computer recognize any other usb device?

2.      Do you get any error message when you plug in the usb device?

3.      Do you see the message installing driver when you plug in the usb device?

 

There is a converter involved when you want to use an internal drive as an external drive. The issue could be with that converter. There is also a possibility that a firmware update is pending for the drive.

 

Let us follow these methods:

Method 1.

To open the hardware troubleshooter follow the link given: http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows7/Open-the-Hardware-and-Devices-troubleshooter

 

Method 2.

You may update the usb driver and check if that helps.

Update a driver for hardware that isn't working properly: http://windows.microsoft.com/is-IS/windows7/Update-a-driver-for-hardware-that-isnt-working-properly

 

 

Refer to this article.

Install a USB device: http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows7/Install-a-USB-device

 

Method 3.

Please disconnect all USB devices and then perform the following steps:

1. Click "Start" button, type “devmgmt.msc” (without quotation marks) in the “Search” bar and press "Enter". Click “Continue” if necessary.

2. In Device Manager, double click to expand "Universal Serial Bus controllers", right click on the Host Controller, click "Uninstall" and click "OK".

3. Repeat the step 2 to uninstall all items under "Universal Serial Bus controllers".

Then, restart your computer and Windows 7 will reinstall all USB controllers automatically.

 

Reply with the status of the issue and we will be glad to offer our assistance.
