In my case, that exact same file found in the same location contains a valid digital signature with a certificate timestamp date of December 1, 2015 and indicates that the certificate is OK. This makes sense since that timestamp date is within the range for which the certificate Issued by: Microsoft Code Signing PCA was Valid from: 6/4/2015 to 9/4/2016.

If the certificate displayed in your case doesn't follow these rules, then either it or the file contents may be damaged, but that's what this system exists to determine.  If the file is damaged, it may either contain malware or otherwise be a false replacement for the original file.

Anyone know why these are happening?
entegy say:

SMS is the real name of normal texting. It's an abbreviation of Short Message Service. MMS is the real name of picture messages. It's an abbreviation of Multimedia Messaging Service.

 

When you send a picture text, this uses MMS, so you must have your data connection on in Settings>cellular. If you are using an open market phone or a phone from a different carrier, get the APN settings from your current carrier to ensure data and MMS are correctly set.

 

If the Group Text feature is on in the Messages app's Settings, sending a text to multiple people will result in an MMS. If the other people don't have smartphones (iPhones, Android), you should turn this feature off.

christopher miller say:

Hi wellfrey,

If you send an email message through your Gmail account from your phone, it should show up in the Sent Mail list in the GMail browser app.   Do you have IMAP enabled on your Gmail account?  If you only have POP enabled, then that might be the cause of the problem.

To setup your Gmail account to use IMAP

  1. Sign in to your Gmail account and go to Mail.
  2. Click the Settings button in the upper, right corner of the window and select Settings from the drop-down menu.
  3. On the Settings screen, click Forwarding and POP/IMAP.
  4. Scroll down to the IMAP Access section and select Enable IMAP

If you were not using IMAP before with GMail, after making the changes to your account, you may need to change your gmail account settings on the phone.  Check your account on the phone and make sure that Incoming email server field is set to imap.gmail.com:993:1.   To get to that setting page to the following:

  1. In the App list, tap Settings , and then tap Email + accounts.

  2. Select your Google account

  3. Scroll down and tap the advanced settings button

  4. View the Incoming email server field.

The option that you have would be to remove the Google account from your phone and add it back in again.  You would do this after verifying that you have IMAP enabled on your GMail account.  From the "Set up or delete an email account" page:

To set up a Google account

  1. In the App list, tap Settings , and then tap Email + accounts.

  2. Do one of the following:

    • If you have Windows Phone 8, tap Add an account > Google.
    • If you have Windows Phone 8.1, tap Add an account > Google > Connect.

  3. Enter your email address and password, and then tap Sign in.

  4. Tap Accept.

    Your Google account email, contacts, and calendar will be synced to your phone.

    Notes
    • If you have a Google Apps account, you'll need to set it up differently. Go toSettings > Email + accounts, then tap Add an account > Advanced setup. Enter your user name and password, tap Next, then make sure to tap Exchange ActiveSync. When you're asked for your server, enter m.google.com. For more guidance, see To enter email account settings manually (advanced) below.

    • Getting a "Google error" when you try to set up a Google account on your phone? Learn more
