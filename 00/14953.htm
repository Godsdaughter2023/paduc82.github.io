Email I did not receive but sender has a copy of the sent email - Microsoft Community

markbore say:

Email I did not receive but sender has a copy of the sent email

Is there any way to check if an email was actually sent and then recalled by the sender. Thus the recipient does not have a chance to open and read the email but the sender can claim the email was sent and has a copy as proof?
brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

There is no way to validate 100% that a message was delivered and Outlook.com doesn't support message recall.  Even if the sender recalled a message, if it was delivered, you'd retain the message.

The sender should be aware that having a copy of the message in the Sent folder proves only that he or she clicked Send and that the message went to the next hop in the SMTP chain.  It's not a guarantee of delivery.  Mail is store-and-forward and any of the mail transports between the sender and the recipient could have silently dropped the message for any number of reasons.  There is no guarantee of delivery with Internet mail.

I am a Customer Service Representative that is posting in behalf of my customer. The customer would need to create an IF function. There are five columns and the cells may have a letter but the customer would like to convert to a number then get the totals to compare columns.  For example, IF cell has an X value is 5. Please help.
rick rothstein, mvp - excel say:

Assuming the numbers for these letters are the counting number (1, 2, 3, etc.), then you could use this to translate a letter to its numer equivalent...

=SEARCH(A1,"BKCMX")

Where the letters are listed in order of their numerical position (B=1, K=2, C=3, M=4, X=5)... replace A1 with the actual cell reference contain the letter. Note that this method is not limited ot the 5 letters I have shown... you can extend this for all 26 letters for equivalent values of 1 through 26. This formula can be copied down or across as needed.

g north say:

Hi

Let's say your columns are B-F.  In M enter a table as below

Z      1

X      2

W     3

V      4

U      5

 

You can substitute your own data for the above.

Now in G1 enter the formula

=VLOOKUP(B1,$M$1:$N$1,2,False)

Copy the formula across the columns to K (use the fill handle to drag across)

Then double click the fill handle and Excel will copy the formula down the same number of rows in your existing columns.
