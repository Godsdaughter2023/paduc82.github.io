There is an option to disconnect (without pairing) the Bluetooth audio device using the Sound control panel applet.

Press Start, type Sound and open the suggested control panel applet.  OR, right click the speaker icon in the system tray and select Playback Devices.

You should see an entry or two representing your Bluetooth audio receiver.  Right-click on each of them and click Disconnect.

Now your Bluetooth audio receiver should be usable by other devices.

The 'downside' here is that you'll have to right-click & Connect them back to your desktop when you need them.

Error 0xC004C060 can occur if you entered a product key that can no longer be used to activate Windows, upgraded to Windows 10 but the activation servers were busy, or if you upgraded to Windows 10 using the free upgrade offer but Windows 10 isn't activated after a reinstall. As initial troubleshooting, I suggest that you run the Activation troubleshooter. This resolves most activation errors. To use the troubleshooter, go to Start > Settings > Update & Security > Activation, and then select Troubleshoot.

If the issue still persists even after using the troubleshooter, it is best to get in touch with our Answer Desk team to have this issue sorted out. They can check the product key you have to ensure that it can be used to activate Windows 10 Pro. You may contact our Answer Desk team via call or chat here.
