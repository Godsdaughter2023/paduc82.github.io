The problem is with Silverlight.  To fix the problem you need to enable Silverlight.

In the browser address bar type chrome://flags. 

  1. Scroll down to find chrome://flags/#enable-npapi on the page.
  2. Click on the Enable NPAPI link.
  3. Restart Chrome.
  4. After you open Chrome up again, type chrome://plugins in the browser's address bar and hit enter.
  5. Under Silverlight check-mark "Always allowed to run".
  6. Open a new email window and click on the attachment button to see if the browse window opens.
  7. If you see a small icon in the upper right corner of the new email that is showing a small red X, right click on it and click Always allow pop-ups for this site.

You should now be able to attach files in Google Chrome.

stynkey say:

PROBLEM RESOLVED!!!!

Thanks Robert, but your suggestions didn't work and after months of pulling out my hair I just gave up using the signature or stationary features for many months.

HOWEVER.... I was searching again last night for a potential solution and I finally found the answer to this problem at:  http://www.slipstick.com/problems/the-stationery-and-font-button-doesnt-work/ 

It's posted as a fix for Outlook 2010 & 2013 Signature & Stationary problem but if you read through all the user comments it's easy to figure out how to apply it for Outlook 2007 as well).

I failed to mention in my original question (sorry) that my new windows 8 laptop came with an Office 2013 trial which I didn't like and uninstalled prior to installing and using Office 2007. 

Apparently, when you switch versions, Office (or Outlook) install doesn't reset the registry key value to point to the correct version, so you have to do it manually with regedit (all in all, just a simple resolution once you know about this problem).

In my case for Outlook 2007, the value in registry key:

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Classes\Wow6432Node\CLSID\{0006F03A-0000-0000-C000-000000000046}\LocalServer32 

had to be changed from:  C:\Program Files\Microsoft Office 15\root\office15\outlook.exe,  (the path for the "uninstalled" Outlook 2013)

to: C:\Program Files (x86)\Microsoft Office\Office12\OUTLOOK.EXE (the path for Outlook 2007).

Now the Signature and Stationary options are all working just fine.

A big thank you to Diane Poremsky for posting this fix on the slipstick.com site.
