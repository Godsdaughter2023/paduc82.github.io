marshall barton say:

A subtle point about this kind of situation is that when you change anything in a table's design, you need to either compact the front end db or make any kind of change to all queries that refer to the table.  Either of those actions will clear all or the referring queries execution plan, which, in turn, will cause the queries to pick up on the table design changes.

In your case, adding the new field to the form's query took care of all that as well as making the field available in the form's code.
