The two solutions below depends on how you want to manage your DNS records:

 

Keep letting Office 365 to manage your DNS records

Automatically highlight upcoming and past due dates

A colleague has come to me with a conundrum.  She has a spreadsheet listing staff attendances at mandatory courses.  The attended date is entered under each course and expires in 3 years time.  She wants to use a 'traffic light system' to indicate when

a) when the three years has expired - Red

Name        Manual Handling        Safeguarding        Fire Lecture        Violence and Aggression

hansv mvp say:

Let's say the dates are in B2:E100.

Select this range; in the following I'll assume that B2 is the top left cell within the selection.

On the Home tab of the ribbon, click Conditional Formatting > Icon Sets and select a three-color traffic light set.

For green, select Formula in the Type dropdown and enter

=DATE(YEAR(TODAY()),MONTH(TODAY())-30,DAY(TODAY()))

in the Value box.

For amber/yellow, select Formula in the Type dropdown and enter

=DATE(YEAR(TODAY())-3,MONTH(TODAY()),DAY(TODAY()))

in the Value box.

Click OK.
