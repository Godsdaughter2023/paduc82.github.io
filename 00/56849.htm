anannya podder say:

Hi Ed Franks,

There are four main reasons why a system BIOS will not detect the presence of an internal Solid State Drive. The following are not in any particular order.

  1.  Drive not enabled in the BIOS

  2. Serial ATA drivers are not properly installed

  3. Faulty or unplugged data cable

  4. Faulty SSD


If your drive is NOT detected it may be because it is turned OFF in System Setup. When you add a drive to the system, it may be needed to enable the port by turning it System Setup.

Method 2 - Problems in cables and connections:
