pete_uk say:

If you want the daily cumulative time spent on line you can set up a separate table with dates in (eg in column F) and then this formula in G2:

=SUMIF(A:A,F2,C:C)

Format as [hh]:mm and then copy down to cover the number of dates you have. If in F2 you put the first of the month you are interested in, then you can generate the dates automatically with this in F3:

=F2+1

and copy down to F32.

I'm not sure how you could show that a person has not taken a break - does the 2:48:25 in your example mean that the person was continually looking at Google for 2 hours and 48 minutes, or might they have taken a break and just left the computer connected?

If you wanted a percentage of time spent online in the table described above, you can put this in H2:

=G2

and format as a percentage, then copy down. It might be easier to graph this than the elapsed times.

Hope this helps.

Pete
