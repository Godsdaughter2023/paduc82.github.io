The errors Claude and Dave reported are likely due to a bug we just discovered in the Outlook 2013 June Public Update. 

The problem is specific to connecting via EAS to Outlook.com and it is caused due to an issue with how Outlook is handling some calendar items.  If you heavily use the calendar you are more prone to see it.  To confirm you are running the June PU version it is 15.0.4623.1003 (File, Office Account under Office Updates).

Steps to Revert for C2R:

Disable Updates:

1. Start Word.

2. Click File, and then click Account.

3. In the Product Information column, click Update Options, and then click Disable Updates.

Right click on the Start button and click Run and type CMD and click OK.

For an Office installation in a 32-bit version of Windows:

cd %programfiles%\Microsoft Office 15\ClientX86

For an Office installation in a 64-bit version of Windows:

cd %programfiles%\Microsoft Office 15\ClientX64

Run the following command to revert to May PU:

officec2rclient.exe /update user updatetoversion=15.0.4615.1002

Add an appointment to your calendar for July 11th 2014 to remember to re-enable updates.  The fix for this issue will ship in the July Public Update.

If your version of Office does not have the Update Options button then you are running MSI.  To revert to May PU go to Control Panel, Programs and Features, click View Installed Updates and remove the June update KB http://support.microsoft.com/kb/2881040

a. user say:

Hello Arun:

 

Thank you for posting to the forum regarding your Gmail issue.  This does look to be something that is a known behavior for some customers.  I did some testing with a couple Gmail accounts and could not reproduce the behavior.

 

There are a couple of things we can suggest.  The following are a few basic troubleshooting steps that should be tried whenever encountering issues on your phone.

  • Clear you browser cache, tap settings > go to applications > delete history.  Clearing these temporary files can clear up performance issues.
  • Soft reset the phone, remove the battery for about 2 minutes, replace and reboot the phone.  If you are unable to remove the battery, consult your owner's manual or contact Nokia support.
  • Delete the account from the phone and add the account back to the phone.  When you add the account back to the phone be sure to choose "sync email/calendar/contacts".  You can turn off the options you do not want to sync after the account is set up on the phone.

If the above steps do not resolve your issue, there are a couple of possible work arounds that may work for you:

  • One, you can simply turn of the syncing of this folder.  When in the Gmail application, tap settings > folders > sent items > tap the (...) > select "don't sync this folder".
  • You can try adding the Gmail account as an other account.  Delete the account from the phone and then tap the + symbol in the emails + accounts settings.  Choose that "other account" option and add the requested details.  The account will now show up on the phone as Gmail instead of Google mail.
  • Finally, there was another more elaborate work around I found in this thread, http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/winphone/forum/wp8-wpemail/windows-phone-8-gmail-emails-appear-twice/e4f213ba-3a0e-4cf4-ba8d-591a7e5f9a6e?page=1.  It would be worth reviewing this to see if this works for you.

Please let us know if you have any other questions or concerns.
