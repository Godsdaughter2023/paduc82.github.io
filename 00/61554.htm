andre da costa say:

Should I upgrade to Windows 10 Fall Creators Update 1709 or Wait?

I’m contemplating whether I should upgrade to Windows 10 1709 or not.  Could you give me your views?

andre da costa say:

Windows 10 1709 has gone through a process of intense engineering since April of 2017. A series of preview builds were released to users participating in Microsoft's Windows Insider Program. The decision to upgrade can be a confident one because of this since Microsoft has declared it ready for production.

That said, there should be no rush to upgrade. As with every new release, there can be potential compatibility issues or unknown show stoppers yet to be discovered. You can wait it out a bit and see how the market responds to the upgrade, wait for future cumulative updates to mature and be released; and also check the manufacturers website for your make and model to determine compatibility.

Previous versions of Windows 10 such as 1703 and 1607 will continue to be supported for sometime, so an immediate upgrade is not detrimental. Eventually, you should in order to keep up with the latest in security which is part of the development model for new versions.

Microsoft will also implement a phased in approach to upgrades where users are offered the upgrade on systems certified compatible through Windows Update. Otherwise, wait a few months and always backup before upgrading.
