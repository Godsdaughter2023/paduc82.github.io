russellkrogan say:
Hi 3panda3,
Yes, it accepts charging through the USB-C port.

However, you need a powerbank which can supply a sufficiently high wattage to charge the device while in use. This is usually stated on the battery's description on its sales page or box (for example, 4.5A @ 90W via the DC port). The 15" SB2 needs up to 100W during heavy gaming- just to give you an idea of the power required. When suspended, you may only need 24-30W, although such a powerbank may only charge a small fraction of the battery, and slowly at that.

The sort of powerbank you are looking at is generally larger and heavier than the common tablet/phone powerbank. Capacity usually starts around 20000mAH and up.

Try these steps

  2. Navigate to the System tab.
  3. Select Storage.

Remote Procedure Call (RPC)

Windows Modules Installer

Windows Update
