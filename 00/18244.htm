zorvek say:

Akhilesh,

When working with numbers whether large or small, we have to pay attention to significant digits. Significant digits are the digits that are relevant. For example, if we are measuring something like the distance from point A to point B, then the number of significant digits reflects the accuracy of our measuring equipment. If we are measuring the distances from one wall to another wall in a house, then we are probably not going to get much more accuracy than 0.1 inches or centimeters, and we're probably not going to have a room larger than 50 or 100 feet or meters. So the number of significant digits will be around 4 or 5.

If we are measuring the distance to the nearest star in kilometers, we are probably not going to be able to be more accurate than a few light years which is a lot of kilometers. So, again the number of significant digits--if the distance is about 100 light years--is about 2 or 3. 1 light year is 9.461e+12 or 9,461,000,000,000 kilometers. 100 light years is about 946,100,000,000,000 kilometers. If I asked you to figure out if it was 946,100,000,000,000 kilometers or 946,100,000,000,001 kilometers you would tell me I was nuts. Why? Because it's impossible to measure with any more accuracy than about a few light years. And the same limits exist with really small distances too.

Also, we don't care about a few light years or, if measuring the space in a room, a few hundredths of an inch. That level of accuracy just doesn't make any difference with regard to whatever it is we are going.

So how does this apply to you? You are trying to enter numbers that have 34 digits. If these are measurements, then the number of significant digits can't be more than about 5 which means that scientific notation is perfectly fine and you can do all the math on those numbers that you want.

On the other hand, if you are entering some kind of serial number, you want to display the whole value, and you want to do math with that number, then we'll have to do some tricks with formulas to make that work.

Before we go to far down a rabbit hole, what, exactly, are you entering? What mathematics are you trying to calculate?

Kevin

Hello Ljosalf:

Thank you for posting to the forum regarding the issues you are having with audiobook content through Zune.  I am not sure I will have anything helpful to add at this point as you seem to have done everything in my troubleshooting repertoire. 

As I understand it you have an audiobook that is made up of several individual MP3 files.  The book appears in Zune and on your player as an album and the issue is that the individual tracks/chapters are out of order.  If this is the case, the thing I have seen to resolve this is editing the track numbers.  Specifically by adding a 0 to the front of each number currently listed in the Zune software.  The best way to edit this is to go to your Zune software collection and sort by albums.  Right click on the audio book and select the edit option.  Add the number 0 to the front of each track number and then save.  Once you have done this, please connection your Zune HD and sync this album to your player.

john sr wilson say:
The Master slide controls the background, graphics and text format.

Layouts control ... erm the layout.

To paste into a picture placeholder try moving to the correct slide, select the placeholder and paste into the active view.


Sub Into_Place()
ActiveWindow.View.GotoSlide 2
ActiveWindow.View.Slide.Shapes(2).Select
ActiveWindow.View.Paste
End Sub
