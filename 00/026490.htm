swischner say:
There are several different issues at once here.

1. (@Neftali R.): The OneDrive clients for Windows or MacOS have absolutely nothing to do with OneNote. All OneNote versions are using their own internal sync mechanism to OneDrive and OneDrive to business and won't go through the clients. You could even uninstall them and OneNote would still sync. So updating the clients will bring you nowhere.

2. Saving locally stored notebook files and folders to your Dropbox folder to sync them is not only not supported by Microsoft; it even brings a lot of problems and drawbacks. Dropbox is lacking the needed protocol (COBALT) for OneNote and will only exchange whole files at every little change. Although it looks like it would word (it does to some extend), there are serious risks. If you are interested, here is some background information I wrote: http://onenote-blog.com/onenote-und-dropbox-bes...

3. The OneNote app for Windows 10 does not allow access to locally stored notebooks at all (as well as all other OneNote apps and versions except for the one installed with MS Office for Windows, e.g. OneNote 2016. So you can stop trying to open your locally stored notebooks (no matter if they are in a Dropbox folder) with the app. No way.

4. To connect OneNote 2016 with OneDrive (or OneDrive for Business) you would go through File-Open-Add a place in OneNote 2016. Did you try that?

5. If your OneNote (app or OneNote 2016) is connected to OneDrive for Business (you could create a new notebook there), here is how you transfer your locally saved notebooks to the cloud: Open them in OneNote 2016 (again: not possible in the app) and choose File-Share. Choose the OneDrive account (should be listed now) as a destination and click on the Move Notebook button. This is the only(!) way to move a local notebook to OneDrive or OneDrive for business. Don't try to manually copy the files (or save them to) the local OneDrive folder on Windows -- won't work.

6. To access your notebooks in OneNote Online (those that are already stored in OneDrive for business, not the local ones!), open and login to Office 365 in your browser and from the app menu top left open OneDrive or OneNote.

This has been a lot of information and may still not cover all of your issues. But let me know if it brought you any further.
The most difficult thing is to understand, that OneNote notebooks (for technical reasons that I could explain, but that would get very lengthy :)) are not handled like Word or Excel or any other data files. They are in a very special format and synchronizing needs to treat them in an even more special way. Simply copying around does not help when it comes to sharing and the cloud.
