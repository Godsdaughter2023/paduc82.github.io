cbarnhorst say:

The Windows 7 product key is irrelevant.  

There is no Windows 10 product key except a dummy key that cannot be used for anything.

You upgrade to Windows 10 from the Windows 7 desktop without any product key.  The Get Windows 10 program does not provide one because Setup inserts the dummy key during installation.  Beginning with Windows 10 Microsoft has introduced a new licensing technology in which the product key plays a much reduced role.  It only comes into play when you purchase a retail copy of Windows 10.  This new technology is called device activation.

If you see a screen asking for a pk, make no entry and click SKIP or DO LATER.  Windows 10 will activate automatically and register the computer as a Windows 10 device on Microsoft's servers.  Activation is permanent for the life of the computer or until you change the motherboard.  Later reinstallations, including clean installs, are also done without a product key.  This is new licensing technology.
