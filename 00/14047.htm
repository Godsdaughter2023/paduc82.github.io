rohn007 say:

You are just overlooking a fundamental issue in an computer (not just Windows/Excel).  Computers think in powers of 2, people think in powers of 10. Computer can save equivalent of powers of 10 numbers in powers of 2, but once you start doing math with them, especially division and fractions.  There are numbers that just can't be represented in powers of 2, ie 1/3 = .333333333 ...

What you are seeing is a simple rounding error. And the simple fix is to add rounding to your calculations, ie round to 2 decimal places.

Here are some articles about the issue

How to correct rounding errors in floating-point arithmetic
https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/help/214118
Many combinations of arithmetic operations on floating-point numbers in Microsoft Excel and Microsoft Works may produce results that appear to be incorrect by very small amounts. For example, the equation
=1*(.5-.4-.1)
may be evaluated to the quantity (-2.78E-17), or -0.0000000000000000278 instead of 0.
This behavior is not a problem in or a limitation of Excel or Works; this behavior occurs because the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) 754 floating-point standard requires that numbers be stored in binary format.

joeu2004 say: sparc-man wrote:
I was then told to change to a general format and delete the [$-C09]*******. I did this and everything was good for 8 days. Then just 2 days ago everything went to pack again, and the dreaded [$-C09]dd-mmm-yyyy;@ was back

Formatting as General was a misdirection.  When a cell is formatted as General, Excel does indeed change the format automagically sometimes based on the form of the cell value.

It is not clear to me what you don't like about "the dreaded [$-C09]dd-mmm-yyyy".  I believe the "[$-C09]" part is a localization code, usually based on a computer's Regional and Language Option control panel settings.  See http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/assistance/HA010346351033.aspx#Locale.

If you are only concerned about the "###" displayed in the cell, there are two common reasons:

(a) The column is too narrow.  Simply widen the column.

(b) Your formula is generating negative time or date.  Hovering the cursor over the cell should tell you that.

If #b is your problem, post back here for guidance, if you need it.

But I urge you to ignore any suggestion to change to the 1904 Date System, a calculation option.  That will probably cause more problems than it solves.
