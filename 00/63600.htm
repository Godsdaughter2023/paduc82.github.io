daniel strommen [msft] say:

Hi Mark,

To use the family features in Windows 10, your child does need to sign in to the device using a Microsoft account, so that we can sync the settings appropriately.  However, that does not mean you need to give your child access to email.

There are a couple strategies for this:

(1) Don't give your child the password for their Microsoft account, create a picture password or PIN that they use when signing in to the device.  Block the Mail app and mail.live.com.

(2) Create a Microsoft account using an external email address (like a Gmail), instead of signing up for a new email address, when you create the account.  Don't get them the password for the Gmail.  You can give them the password to the Microsoft account because it won't have its own email inbox.

We switched to this model because more and more of what kids are doing every day is online, using online accounts rather than just local user profiles.  We felt that it was super important to give parents the ability to understand what their kids are doing and keep them safe across all their devices, and especially across online services.  Giving you control over their Microsoft account is really the basis for all the new family features we've added recently, like purchase controls in the Store and the family features on Windows 10 mobile.
