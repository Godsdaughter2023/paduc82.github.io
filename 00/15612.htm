a. user say:

Hi,

 

No, once you’ve created a new Outlook.com account there is no way to rename your Hotmail account to that name. If you delete the new account, it will take 365 calendar days for that account name to become available again, and at that point, anyone would be able to get the name.

Instead, you can Auto-forward your Hotmail account to your new Outlook account. You’ll have all of your mail in one place, and can reply to it using your new address. To auto-forward your mail:

  1. Log in to your Hotmail account.

  2. Click Options, then click More options.

  3. Under Managing your account, click Email forwarding. You might have to sign in.

  4. Select the radio button for Forward your mail to another email account.

  5. Enter the email address for your new Outlook account.

  6. If you want, check the box for Keep a copy of forwarded messages in your Hotmail inbox.

  7. Click Save.

Add Chart Element is still there on the Chart Tools>Design tab of the Ribbon. This is the same place it was in PowerPoint 2013. It didn't exist at all in 2010.

I am guessing you want to see it on the Chart Tools>Format tab as well as on the Design tab. You can customize the Ribbon to put tools where you want them:

  1. In PowerPoint, choose File>Options>Customize Ribbon.
  2. In the right-hand list, set the Customize the Ribbon dropdown to Tool Tabs.
  3. Under Chart Tools, expand the Format tab.
  4. Click on Format to select it, then on the New Group button to add a custom ribbon section.
  5. With New Group (Custom) still selected, click on the Rename button to give the custom group a name like Design.
  6. In the left-hand list, change the Choose commands from dropdown to All Commands.
  7. Scroll down to Add Chart Element and select it.
  8. Click on the Add button to add it to your new custom group.
  9. OK out, insert a chart and test your customized Ribbon.
