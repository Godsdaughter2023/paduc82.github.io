suzanne s. barnhill say:

The page at http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/buy/office-2010-which-suite-is-right-for-you-FX101825640.aspx shows which applications are included in the various retail versions of Office 2010. You do not have to be a student to buy the Home and Student edition (if you actually are a student, you may be eligible for Office Professional Academic 2010; see http://www.microsoft.com/student/en/us/office/default.aspx).

The limitations of the Home and Student edition are that (a) it doesn't include Outlook, (b) it doesn't qualify for an upgrade, and (c) it is for non-commercial use only. The second limitation will be less important if the next version of Office, like Office 2010, is not offered in an upgrade edition. But limitation (c) can be very important if you are using the program to create anything that will result in an exchange of money (and that theoretically includes the Great American Novel that you will be trying to find a publisher for).

The great benefit of the HSE is that it can be installed on up to three computers, which can be used simultaneously by any number of users.

The Home and Business edition does include Outlook and can be used for commercial purposes, but it has the same license terms as Pro, which are defined at http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/products/office-2010-frequently-asked-questions-HA101674631.aspx#_Can_I_install_1.

For answers to other FAQs about these editions, see http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/products/office-2010-frequently-asked-questions-HA101674631.aspx.
