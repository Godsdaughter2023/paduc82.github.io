</snip>

<snip>

After a bit of investigation, I found that the date in the comment has a month of zero. Changing that in comments.xml is enough to unbreak the document. I changed this:

<w:comment w:id=”0” w:author=”Author Name Here” w:date=”2307-00-23T16:48:00Z” w:initials=”ANH”>

to this:

<w:comment w:id=”0” w:author=”Author Name Here” w:date=”2307-01-23T16:48:00Z” w:initials=”ANH”>

and it worked. I found that I could create new broken documents at will by editing the month on a comment to zero.

I still don’t know how the bad date was inserted in the first place, but I’m attempting to find out what version of Word the author was using.

</snip>

******

<snip>

I went a little further and I found that the problem is with the Word 2007/2010 compatibility pack for Word 2003.

The pack sometimes garbles the dates of comments. In my document, I had timestamps over 24 hours set in 1987!

<w:comment w:id=”28” w:author=”Author name” w:date=”1987-10-27T28:06:00Z” w:initials=”AN”>

Word 2010 just ignored the error as of Word 2013 doesn’t show any pages that contain or follow a badly timestamped comment.

</snip>

Do you have more than one equation in the file?

The equation editor has been a source of many "adventures".  It has a LONG history of corrupting files.  Before the change to the DOCX (XML) file format and continuing to the present.

There is one known "fix" to avoid problems with the equation editor. That is, after you exit an equation NEVER open it again to edit it. If there is an error, create a new correct equation. Yes, I understand that introduces more opportunity for error when recreating the whole equation, BUT, better that than having your file become corrupt!  It appears to be a common thread in the reported problems is that an equation had been edited immediately before the problem occurred, ie the problem became visible the next time the file was attempted to be opened.
