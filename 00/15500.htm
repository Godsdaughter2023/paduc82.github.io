I will post a screenshot if no one understands me.

Thanks a lot!

jay freedman say:

Normally, Word does behave exactly that way. If you just press the hyphen key at a point that could be the end of a line, the next character will start a new line.

If you DON'T want the line to break at the hyphen, press Ctrl+Shift+hyphen to insert a "nonbreaking hyphen"; then the entire hyphenated word ("present-day") will be forced to the next line. This is especially useful when a number is separated from a unit by a hyphen, as in "a 5-gram sample".

These things refer to manual hyphenation (where you type the hyphens). Word can also do automatic hyphenation, in which it uses the syllables listed in the spelling dictionary to choose where to put hyphens. That's controlled by the Hyphenation button in the Page Setup group of the Page Layout tab.
