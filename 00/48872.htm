dlbowdoin say: Problem: When you try to use Windows Update or update Windows Defender to check for and install updates, you receive one of the following error messages:

Windows Defender error code 0x80072efd
Windows Update error code 80072efd

The following solution worked for me on a PC running Vista SP1. This was after all of the other suggestions provided by Microsoft Support at http://support.microsoft.com/kb/836941 and http://support.microsoft.com/kb/971058 did not work.

Solution (Vista SP 1):

1. Click the "Start" Button, click "All programs", and click "Accessories".
2. Right-click “Command Prompt” Run as administrator". If you are prompted for an administrator password or confirmation, type the password or provide confirmation.
3. Type "netsh winhttp reset proxy" (without quotes) at the command prompt, and press Enter.
4. Try the update again.

mallhew g say:

After reading your post I believe the best way for a solution is to buy an av dongle. That will give you audio out from your xbox. Then, to run it to your 3.5 mm speakers you will need to buy a red and white to 3.5m jack. Remember though to make sure your cords match correctly. The dongle will most likely be female connections and you will need to make sure the red/white to 3.5 has male ends (red/white). Lastly, you made need a single 3.5mm cord with both male ends to connect to your speakers. So to rehash your setup would be:

Av dongle----> Red/white--3.5mm jack------>3.5mm cord-----> speakers.

I had to use that setup when I used a monitor myself. Do not expect hdmi sound quality in this setting. I was always happy with the sound quality though. I later invested in a good gaming headset and ran an optical to it.

krantzstone say:

Which Xbox 360 do you have, the original or the 'slim'?

The new 'slim' has an S/PDIF TOSLINK digital audio 'out' jack built into the back of the machine.  If you have a sound system (eg. a stereo receiver) which has a TOSLINK digital audio 'in' jack, you can purchase a TOSLINK cable to connect your Xbox 360S directly to your sound system.

Here's a link to a 3ft TOSLINK digital audio fiber optic cable:

www.monoprice.com/.../product.asp

There's more information about S/PDIF, including a picture of the different S/PDIF connectors like TOSLINK, here:

www.pcmag.com/.../0,1237,t=SPDIF&i=50760,00.asp

If you have the original Xbox 360, you'll need an audio adapter dongle, like this:

[View:http://ec1.images-amazon.com/images/I/41lfHItkIVL.jpg]

Microsoft used to make and sell these as official Xbox 360 products, but I can no longer find it anywhere (it's not even on the xbox.com page for accessories), and their Xbox 360 official Microsoft-branded HDMI retail cable no longer comes with the audio adapter dongle (not sure why), so I had to purchase mine through Amazon from a third party which makes/sells just the audio adapter dongle (which is also a lot cheaper).

Here's a link to one, or you can Google 'Xbox 360 audio adapter dongle' and you might find it for even cheaper:

www.amazon.com/.../ref=sr_1_4

You'll notice the audio adapter dongle also has a TOSLINK jack as well.

If your sound system/speakers only has a 3.5mm connector (your standard audio/headphone connector), you will also need another cable that converts Left/Right stereo RCA audio (female) into 3.5mm audio (female).  If you have a stereo system or receiver, chances are it will have 3.5mm audio 'female' input jack, so you'll also need a 3.5mm audio cable (male to male), but if you have a set of PC speakers, they probably come with a 3.5mm cable already, either separately or attached.

Here's a link to one 3.5mm female to 2 RCA stereo male cable:

www.monoprice.com/.../product.asp

Here's a link to one 3.5mm stereo male to male cable:

www.monoprice.com/.../product.asp
