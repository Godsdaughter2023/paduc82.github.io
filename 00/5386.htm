As far as I know, it already has been fixed. For nearly everyone, Word 2011 is running OK on the Retina MBPs.

You haven't mentioned what is in those error reports, which makes it difficult to know where to begin.

Let's first stop the loss of work.  Go to Word>Preferences>Save and switch on "Always create backup copy".  Then get into the habit of manually saving (Command + s) every time you pause to think.  Every time you save, this setting copies the previous version of the file into the same folder labelled "Backup of ...".  If the document blows up, simply double-click "Backup of document name", re-save it as a new name, and off you go again.

Also, make sure Time Machine is on and running properly. There are some corruptions that can get into a document that can save OK, but will prevent the document opening when you come back to it.  For those, there's Time Machine.

Now, we have to figure out what's doing this.  First, please carry out this procedure, exactly as it's written: http://word.mvps.org/Mac/fontweeding.html  That's unlikely to solve the problem: bad fonts normally produce a hang or crash on startup.  But it reduces the "strain" within the system, so we can get a clearer idea of what is happening.

My guess is that this is likely to be a weird file permissions issue that is preventing Word from saving one of its temporary files.  But we have to work our way down to that.

Is this happening on all documents?  A particular kind of documents?  Only one specific document?  I am trying to find out whether this issue is occurring because of the code in the document, or because of some problem with the environment on your computer.

You can pretty much eliminate problems with the Word software: any problems with Word or Office tend to show up almost instantly as crashes or hangs on startup.  For it to run an hour before blowing up makes "temporary files" most likely.

Please report back after you have done the font weeding.

joanne li msft say:

Hi Barbara,

Before we use Office 365 desktop applications, we need to be assigned to a valid license. So let us check if you have a valid license first. Please navigate to Office 365 subscriptions page with your Office 365 account to check if you have the latest desktop version of Office license.

  • If you don't have the license, you need to contact your organization IT department to let the admin assign one license to you.

  • If you have the valid license, please quit all Office applications, then try the following steps to deactivate and re-activate Office:

A.  Remove Office 365 license for Subscription based installs

1.  Find where the ospp.vbs file locates, generally it should be in this folder: C:\Program Files\Microsoft Office\Office16 or C:\Program Files (x86)\Microsoft Office\Office16  (the file path depends on your versions of Windows and Office, here I use the former one as example.)

2.  Run C:\program files\Microsoft office\office16>cscript ospp.vbs /dstatus

The above command line will generate a report of the currently installed/activated license. (See Below)


NOTE: You might see multiple licenses in the /dstatus report.

3.  Make note of value for “Last 5 characters of installed product key”, Run  C:\program files\Microsoft office\office16>cscript ospp.vbs /unpkey:“Last 5 of installed product key”   For example: C:\program files\Microsoft office\office16>cscript ospp.vbs /unpkey:G8YH4 (See Below) Repeat the step above if necessary until all keys are removed.

B.  Remove cached identities in HKCU registry:

Open Registry Editor, navigate to Computer\HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Office\16.0\Common\Identity\Identities and remove all of the identities under \Identities.

C.  Remove the stored Credentials in the Credential Manager:

Open Control Panel > Credential Manager.  Remove all Windows credentials listed for MicrosoftOffice.

After performing the steps above, start an Office application such as PowerPoint, then reactivate Office with your Office 365 account.

If you have the valid license but the message persists after performing the steps above, feel free to post back with the information below:

1. A screenshot of your Office 365 subscriptions page.

2. A screenshot of " it states Microsoft office can't find your license for this application".

3. Go to File tab>Account in PowerPoint to capture a screenshot of your Office product information.

regards,

Joanne

  5. click OK.
  6. In the console, double-click Certificates (Local Computer).
  7. In the console, expand Certificates - Current user>Personal>Certificates
  8. Locate the certificate to use
  9. Right-click the certificate you require, click Export
  10. On the Certificate Export Wizard Welcome page, click Next.

  13. Enter a password (You will need this to install the cert)
  14. Enter a path and file name
  15. Finish
  16. Email this cert to your self (other email address)
  17. Open the cert and install it using the password you entered earlier.

Thank you for sharing your concern in our community page.

 

We appreciate your loyalty to our product and to provide you some tips on how you can take care of your Band while at work. These steps will make your Band last for years.

 

Tips:

  • Keep it clean
    • To clean your Band, lightly wipe the touchscreen, wristband, and other surfaces using any of the following:
      • A specialized screen wipe
      • A soft, lint-free cloth
      • A cotton swab moistened with fresh water
      • (Band 2 only) You may occasionally clean the strap using a lint-free cloth moistened with isopropyl alcohol.
      • Important:
        • Never use a spray bottle to clean your Band.
        • Avoid contact with soap, detergent, chlorinated water, salt water, lotion, bug spray, and sunscreen.
  • Keep it dry
    • You can wear your Band indoors or outdoors – it’s sweat-and splash-resistant. If your Band gets wet, dry it with a soft, clean, absorbent cloth.
  • Avoid extreme temperatures
    • Your Band works best when used in temperatures between 14 and 104 degrees Fahrenheit (minus 10 to plus 40 degrees Celsius). Avoid placing your Band on or near a heat source or leaving it outside in freezing temperatures.
