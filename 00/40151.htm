The box to "Enable IEEE 802.1x authentication for the network" should NOT be checked.

You should have installed Service Pack 3 as part of your restoration efforts.  Unpatched, Windows XP Service Pack 2 is not able to connect to wireless networks that use WPA2 encryption (which is what you are -- and should be -- using).  Older hardware also may be unable to connect using WPA2, but that doesn't seem to be your issue because you connected before the restore and can connect now using Linux.

There is a Catch-22:  Windows Update will not install any updates for XP (including Service Pack 3) unless you have Service Pack 3 installed. 

Connect your computer to your router with an Ethernet cable and do the following:
  • Read http://support.microsoft.com/kb/950717
  • If your PC is an HP Pavilion, Presario or Media Center and has an AMD processor, install the following patch before you install SP3 --> http://h10025.www1.hp.com/ewfrf/wc/softwareDownloadIndex?cc=uk&lc=en&dlc=en&softwareitem=pv-60484-2

  2. Select the Wireless Networks tab
  3. Confirm that the box to "Use Windows to configure my wireless network settings" is CHECKED
  4. Remove all entries in the list of "Preferred networks"
  5. Click "View wireless networks," select yours, carefully enter your WPA2 passphrase, and click Connect

four-channel say:

Your Nat still Strict/Moderate? Check here.

Okay,  there has been numerous posts about opening your Nat. Most of those posts refer to the UPNP issue where you have to have your UPNP Plug and Play connection on through your router. Though there is another hidden settings in a lot of routers that even with that on will still cause you some Nat issues. Well to get to the point of things, there is the Nat, which is a connection through your Gateway allowing multiple devices to connect to the internet with Open Port use. One of these devices is your Xbox 360, your computer, even a network compatible Blu-ray player. Though you get online with these devices and they cause some lag, or they're not getting full connection, like displayed on your console it says, "Your Nat is Strict" even Moderate. After reading a lot of the posts and looking through them I have noticed that there is a basic problem with peoples Nat settings, a lot of times you can turn this on in your router settings yourself (or like my ISP had them hidden).

Full Cone Nat ( http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Full_cone_NAT#Full-cone_NAT )

This setting is a built in controller used by a lot of Internet Service Providers and this will cause restrictions to your Nat settings if these are turned off. What is going to happen is that some of your ports will be turned off automatically by this service only allowing a certain amount of services to be sent through your ISP's Gateway. When you want to fix this issue you'll have to change the settings in your Router or contact your ISP to change them for you. In truth I recommend contacting your ISP about this issue first off. Now how would you know if your Nat is Full Cone or not? Usually these can be found in your Routers information page, under my router (Yours maybe different) is Gateway Overview and in that page is the Wide Area Network overview, when you click the View/Properties of the your Gateway it will come up with a page and on that page it will show usually if your Nat is Full Cone, it should say if this is disabled or enabled. If you can't find it, UPNP is on and you are still having issues connecting to games or with friends this will show that your Full Cone Nat is disabled and you either have a closed Nat Cone or a Restricted Cone Nat, there are also Port Restricted Nat and a Symmetric Restricted Nat, which all these can cause issues to your Xbox Live Connection.
