daniel pineault say:

The RecordSource you are referring to applies to the entire Form, hence it is the Form's RecordSource.

All Ken is doing is placing a few controls in the header, so they appear at the top of the form (only once), and uses them as filters in the Form's RecordSource.

By redefining the Form's RecordSource as he did

SELECT *
FROM CombFinalSignoffQRYquery
WHERE (Country = Forms!YourForm!cboCountry
OR Forms!YourForm!cboCountry IS NULL)
AND (Region = Forms!YourForm!cboRegion
OR Forms!YourForm!cboRegion IS NULL)
AND (Business = Forms!YourForm!cboBusiness
OR Forms!YourForm!cboBusiness IS NULL)
AND (CreditSubStat = Forms!YourForm!cboCreditSubStat
OR Forms!YourForm!cboCreditSubStat IS NULL);

He is applying a series of WHERE clause to filter based on the user's selection in the combo boxes that you place in the header section.  So the form will automatically filter itself based on the choices made in the header.

I Hope this helps clarify thing a little.

stefan blom say:
OK, that is important information, because it tells us that the paragraph style which is applied to the bulleted text is set to update automatically. By default, Word 2007 and later apply a paragraph style called List Paragraph to bulleted or numbered items (unless you specifically picked another style of course). You can tell if you click in your bulleted paragraph and press Ctrl+Shift+S to bring up the Apply Styles pane. Click Modify. In the dialog box, clear the "Update automatically" option and click OK. From now on, the style won't change to accommodate the direct formatting that you apply.
