charles kenyon say:
Sorry for the confusion.

I am not trying to cross-reference from one page to another.

I am trying to mimic one page onto other pages such that anytime I make a change on the "master page", all the pages mimicking the master page also change.

That is one of the ways a cross-reference (REF field) can work.  It can repeat information from the source to the field. It is sometimes used to give the location of the referenced material like a page number or "above" or "below."

A StyleRef Field can fill the same function a little more seamlessly, but involves using character styles to tag your main information. The advantage that the StyleRef field has is that if you are simply referring to bookmarked text (rather than a content control) bookmarks are much more fragile than are styles. People can easily erase bookmarks without meaning to do so, for instance by selecting all the text and typing something. StyleRef fields do not provide the ability to give page numbers or "above" and the like.

When the main information is changed, it is updated throughout the document.  Seriously, read Greg Maxey's page on repeating data. It contains exactly the information you need. You can do it with mapped content controls as he outlines if you want to be able to make changes to the information on any page. If you just want to be able to change on the first page, you would not map the content controls.
