Welcome to the forums

.

To delete your account off your Xbox 360, please follow these instructions:

1. Go to your Xbox 360

2. Skip the sign in, you can do this by pressing B on your controller

3. Scroll right until you get to the settings page

4. Click onto 'Storage'

5. Click onto 'Memory'

6. Click onto 'all storages'

7. Click onto 'Profiles and accounts'

8. Select the Gamer profile you wish to remove from your Xbox 360

.

This will remove your Xbox Live Account from the Xbox 360. If it is an Xbox Live account, it will  not delete the account all together, it will just remove it from the Xbox 360. You can still download your Xbox Live Account in the future. However if you do not sign into your account for more than 365 days, the account will be deleted from the system, and the gamertag will be available for someone else to use.

.

If you are deleting a non Xbox Live account (an account that is not connected to the Xbox Live service), once deleted, its gone and you can not get it back, unless it was stored onto a other storage device like a USB ect..

.

I hope all of this helps you with your question

a. user say:

Hello,

Thank you for contacting Microsoft Community.

Regret the inconvenience you have experienced.

From which version did you upgraded to Windows 10?

What is the exact model of your device?

Is it working fine with the previous OS?

From your description, I suspect that the problem might lies with the Graphics card drivers. Suggest you to cross check for the latest and updated Graphics card drivers for your device from Device Manufacturer website. If any, try to replace the old drivers with the latest on and see if it works fine.

Sometime updated drivers might fails to work correctly due to compatibility. In that case you can try installing the drivers in compatibility mode. Compatibility mode runs the program using settings from a previous version of Windows.

Before we check with this compatibility, let’s try to run the Hardware and Device troubleshooter.

* Plug in the device, press Windows Key then type ‘troubleshoot’ and hit Enter

* Select “View all” on the left panel and run “Hardware and Devices” troubleshooter from the list.

* Click on “Advanced” and then click on “Apply repair automatically”. Also ensure to select Run as administrator.

* Click “Next” and follow the on-screen instructions to complete the troubleshooting process. Now check if they work.

Follow these steps to install the drivers in compatibility mode and check if that helps.

a. Download the latest and available driver from the manufacturer’s website.

b. Uninstall the existing Graphics card driver from your PC through Control Panel.

c. Right-click on the driver setup file and click on ‘Properties’.

d. Go to ‘Compatibility’ tab and click Run compatibility troubleshooter, then select Try recommended setting and click Next again Next. Now it will ask you to Test the program, click on it and continue with installation. Once this is done, check if everything works fine. If it does, go back to Test the program window and click Next, then save the setup file with that settings. Reboot your PC and see it resolve the issue.

Keep us posted if you require further assistance.
