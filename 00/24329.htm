I am having difficult understanding the relevance of much of your description.  So forgive me if I oversimplify the problem and, therefore, the solution.

Essentially, it seems to me that you are saying that when you assign Me.textbox_discount.Value to ws.Cells(iRow, 15), it appears as 0.05, but you would like it to appear as 5%.

I believe the problem is with the format of Cells(iRow,15) before the assignment.  In particular, apparently it is not Percentage (and not General, based on your follow-up comments).

The remedy is to set the cell format before the assignment in VBA.  For example:

ws.Cells(iRow, 15).NumberFormat = "0.00%"
ws.Cells(iRow, 15).Value = Me.textbox_discount.Value

Using the With statement might be more efficient, but be careful to use leading periods appropriately, to wit:

With ws.Cells(iRow, 15)
    .NumberFormat = "0.00%"
    .Value = Me.textbox_discount.Value
End With

Of course, that will display 5.00%, not 5% per se.  Change the format to fit your intent.

ronaldoonenil say:

The formula doesn't make any sense. SUMIF will not produce an error so the 2nd part will never get executed. Also SUMIF is used for looking through a range of values and summing only values that match the criteria. Yours is saying if Z1 is Transport* then get me cell N1, but also saying IF Z1 is Freight* get me cell N1 as well ?

Without seeing your spreadsheet it is difficult to give you exactly what you want but SUMIF is usually used in the following way to get the SUM of all the rows where Transport is in column Z and SUM of all the rows where Freight is in column Z

Transport Total: =SUMIF(Z1:Z30,"Transport*",N1:N30)

Freight Total: =SUMIF(Z1:Z30,"Freight*",N1:N30)
