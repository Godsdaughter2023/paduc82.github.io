slance310 say:

Hi,

What probably caused your problem is a new feature called Fast Startup...

It causes boot errors and\or ChkDsk to run when switching to the other OS...

This happens because it is a hybrid Shutdown\Hibernation, and does not close out the drives like a Restart would, or a normal shutdown...

This feature only works when you do a SHUTDOWN and then Boot.

It doesn't effect a RESTART.

To turn it off or on...

Go to Control panel...Power Options, and select Choose What the Power Buttons Do on the left.

Then select Change Settings That Are Currently Unavailable near the top center of screen...

Lower down on the window, uncheck Fast Startup.

Have you tried the Troubleshoot... Advanced... Startup Repair... on the same screen as the Command Prompt ?

I have had issues in which I had to do the RebuildBCD and then the Startup Repair a couple of times to get my machine to boot...
