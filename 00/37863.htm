j.siebert say:

Open the Services Console from Administrative Tools again, navigate to "Windows Firewall" and open the Properties Window.

On the "Log On" tab of the Windows Firewall service:

  1. check the radio button next to "This account:"
  2. click "Browse..."
  3. click "Advanced..."
  4. click "Find Now"
  5. select "LOCAL SERVICE" from the "Search results:" list
  6. click "OK"
  7. click "OK" again
  8. select and delete the black bullets from both password boxes
  9. hit "Apply"

Note: A message may be displayed: "The account NT AUTHORITY\LocalService has been granted the Log On As A Service right. "
This is okay, because that user is allowed to do that anyway through group membership.

On the "General" tab you should be able to start the service again.

Now you can check the Windows Firewall Settings on the Control Panel, should be back to normal.
