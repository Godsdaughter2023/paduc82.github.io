  • Sometimes, after upgrading to Windows 8.1, the Store App may not start.
  • It might still show the number of available updates on the Live Tile.
  • Starting the app may show a progress ring but it may not finish loading.

 

  • To fix this, run the following in a Command Window (CMD) to re-register the Store App:

powershell -ExecutionPolicy Unrestricted Add-AppxPackage -DisableDevelopmentMode -Register $Env:SystemRoot\WinStore\AppxManifest.XML

 

  • If you get error 0x8007064A after executing this, it means that the registry key "HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Classes\Local Settings\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\AppModel\Repository\Packages" does not have SYSTEM as the owner.
  • Change the owner back to SYSTEM as explained

     

    • To fix three other system apps such as PC Settings, run the following:

    powershell -ExecutionPolicy Unrestricted Add-AppxPackage -DisableDevelopmentMode -Register $Env:SystemRoot\camera\AppxManifest.xml

    powershell -ExecutionPolicy Unrestricted Add-AppxPackage -DisableDevelopmentMode -Register $Env:SystemRoot\FileManager\AppxManifest.xml

    powershell -ExecutionPolicy Unrestricted Add-AppxPackage -DisableDevelopmentMode -Register $Env:SystemRoot\ImmersiveControlPanel\AppxManifest.xml

    joeu2004 say: mdicke wrote:
    Want to calculate the STDEV for a range of scores (1-10).  Column A has the score; column B has the number of times the score appears.  I need the STDEV calculation to all be in one cell (in the example below B36).

    Your formulas should like this:



    The formulas in column B are shown in column C.

    The mean is calculated by summing the product of the frequency (B2:B29) times the score (A2:A29) and dividing by the total number (B32).  That should make "good sense" since the product of the frequency times the score is the same as repeating the score n times in the sum of the scores, the numerator of the mean formula.

    Likewise, the std dev is calculated by summing the product of the frequency times the square of the difference between the score and the mean, then dividing by the total number and taking the square root.  See the formula on the StdDevP help page.

    We use StdDevP, not StdDev (or StdDev.S) because this is the complete data (population), not a sampling.

    [EDIT] Caveat:  Be cautious about how you interpret the std dev.  Some people do use it as a measure of dispersion.  But it might not be very meaningful and useful, since the data are not normally distributed.  I would be inclined to use other measures of dispersion.
