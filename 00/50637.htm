scottcourt say:

"I have 30 hops and the ping at hop 12 is 170 ms. Does this influence gaming and lag?"

the "30 hops" you see are the number of routers that your connection passes through to reach the Xbox server that you're tracing to.  a ping of 170ms isn't that poor, but bear in mind that the ping between each hop can mount up and cause 'lag'.  There is nothing you can do about the number of hops between you and the server (also bear in mind that if you're playing against someone who is in America they may be many more than 30 hops away), that's just the way the internet works.

I have a 10Mbps and rarely encounter lag unless I'm playing against gamers from mainland Europe - specifically France,  Austria and Germany - this is just something you have to accept - everyone has different speeds, service providers and ping/latency.
