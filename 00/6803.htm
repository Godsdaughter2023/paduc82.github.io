hansv mvp say:

SUMIF can handle wildcard characters * and ? in the criteria argument.

[OES1.xlsm]OES!$M$20:$M$99="*"&B$4&"*" compares M20:M99 to the literal value of "*"&B$4&"*", however, i.e. to "*BC*".

You could use

=SUMPRODUCT(--ISNUMBER(SEARCH(B$4,[OES1.xlsm]OES!$M$20:$M$99)),[OES1.xlsm]OES!$B$20:$B$99)
