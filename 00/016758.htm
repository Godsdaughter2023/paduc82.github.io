brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:
For non-IMAP accounts (you don't state your account type), deleting a message will move it to Deleted Items immediately.  They will not stay in the Inbox.  If, however, you are using an IMAP account, they will get marked for deletion only (signified by a line drawn through their subjects) and they'll stay in the Inbox until you purge them with Edit>Purge Deleted Items.  I used Outlook 2002 log enough ago that I don't remember if there was a "purge deleted items when you exit Outlook" setting in the IMAP account settings.  Look on the Advanced tab.  I think that's the likeliest place for it.

Formulas in Excel - Microsoft Community

a. user say:

Formulas in Excel

Howdy,

i have 2500 cells in column T. Each cell has a different price. I want to multiply each cell in bulk by 1.10.

How do i do this?

office 365 e3 dev - excel 2016 

Thank you

hansv mvp say:

Enter 1.1 in an empty cell.

Select this cell and copy it.

Now select the 2500 cells in column T.

Right-click the selection and select Paste Special... from the context menu.

(You can also click the lower half of the Paste button on the Home tab of the ribbon and select Paste Special...)

Under Operation, select Multiply.

Then click OK.

You can now clear the cell containing 1.1.

a. user say:

Brilliant! Thank you. It gave me three numbers after the decimal so when i proceed to import my items it said $6,001, not $6.01 (that would have been a expensive patch cable!) so i formatted those cells to number with two holders after decimal and it works great.

Now if there is any way i can append .99 to suffix of each cell in that column? Ex. $6.99, not $6.01.

Thank you again for that tidbit.

hansv mvp say:

Let's say the data are in T2:T2500.

Insert an empty column to the right of column T (i.e. in column U).

Enter the following formula in U2:

=INT(T2)+0.99

Fill down to U2500. If all cells in column T are filled you can do this by double-clicking the square fill handle in the lower right corner of U2.

With U2:U2500 still selected, copy it to the clipboard.

Select T2:T2500.

Click the lower part of the Paste button on the Home tab of the ribbon, and select Values.

This replaces the values in column T with the result of the formulas in column U.

Finally, delete column U. We don't need the formulas anymore.
