yang zheng msft support say: Hi David,

Firstly I’d like to clarify that the data such as notes stored on the local computer will only exist on this computer. It will not sync with Office 365 Exchange Online or other email services. So when upgrading the Outlook client or re-configuring the email account, the data on the local computer will not sync from Exchange Online or other email services to the Outlook client again.

a) Lost - because nobody warned users to back up notes or other content of the *.ost file before starting upgrade/reconnecting procedure.
The notes stored on the local computer will not be part of the ost file of an email account in the Outlook client. As I said above, they will not sync to Exchange Online or other email services. So before upgrading or re-connecting, users need to backup these notes.

a) Does not Recovered - because "Recover Deleted Items" does not see notes or if a person does not use Office 365 for Business email account in the Outlook.
For an Office 365 for Business account, the notes which are not only stored on the local computer will also sync to Exchange Online. If the notes are deleted from the recover deleted items folder, they would also exist in Exchange Online for 30 days and can be recovered if there is no litigation hold or in-place hold enabled. During this period, only admins can find these notes and end users will not be able to find them from their Outlook client.
For email accounts from other email services, we are not clear about how notes will be managed so we cannot determine whether they are not recoverable.

greg maxey say:

That "Click here to enter text" is called placeholder text.  The fact that it prints when you don't click there and enter text is IMHO a long uncorrected design flaw.


With a bit of effort, you can get it not to print:
http://gregmaxey.mvps.org/word_tip_pages/content_controls_do_not_print_pht.html


In your case, you might want to simply change it to a series of underscores so it looks like a line.


Select the CC tab, click Developer>Controls>Design Mode.


When in design mode put you cursor right after the "C" in "Click" and type your series of underscores.  Then select the rest of the "lick here to ..." and go back and delete the "C"


That may seem odd but, placeholdertext is fickle and just deleting the existing text and typing the underscores may not produce the desired effect.
