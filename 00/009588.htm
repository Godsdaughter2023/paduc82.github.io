Are you installing Office 2010 from the disk or from a downloaded setup file?

Do you have administrator rights on the computer?

 

Do you have any previous or trial versions of Office installed on the computer?

If yes, go to the Programs list in the Control Panel, and remove the previous or trial version of Office from the computer. Once it’s removed, restart the computer.

 

This issue may occur if the Windows Installer becomes unregistered during the installation process. You may unregister and reregister Windows Installer, and see if that helps.

a.       Click Start > All Programs > Accessories > and then click Run.

b.      In the Open box, type msiexec /unregister, and then press ENTER.

c.       Click Start > All Programs > Accessories > and then click Run.

d.      In the Open box, type msiexec /regserver, and then press ENTER.

 

Once it’s done, run the Office 2010 setup as administrator and check if Office installs fine:

Right click on the setup (.exe) file > click on ‘Run as Administrator’.

 

If you still get the same error, follow the steps below to give permissions to the file that’s given in the error message, and then check if you’re able to install Office:

Go to the location given in the error message > right-click on the file, and then click Properties > click on Security tab > click on Edit on the right side > click on Add and type Everyone and then click OK > select Everyone and check the Allow box for Full control > click Apply and OK.

You may have to follow the steps above to give the permission to the folder that contains this file as well (right-click on the folder, then click Properties, and follow the same).
