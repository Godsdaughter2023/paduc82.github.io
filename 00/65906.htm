richard diver say:

Hi, constant disk activity can be usual, the concern is really only when the activity is causing a performance impact to the system. SSDs can sustrain a lot of read activity, wearing out only occurs with heavy amounts of write-activity, even then it would take years to wear out.

You need to isolate what type of activity you see, which processes are causing it, and quantify how much data is being read/written during this constant activity.

When you look at resource monitor, also look at task manager - run them side by side. If task manager shows 0% disk activity, then what you see in resource monitor is purely small back ground tasks that will not have a great impact on the system or on the life expectancy of the SSD. (resmon is a more sensitive tool).

Also, in resmon, go to the Disk tab and sort the columns by  read/write/total to discover the highest comsumers of disk activity - what are they, and what is the approximate values for the Bytes/sec you see?

If you want a view of your disk activity over a longer period of time (2-4 hours, or even 24 hours), I suggest running a Perfmon capture instead, then you can analyse the data and look for patterns of activity.
