mann_manohar say:

Hello RMorneau,

 

Thank you for posting your query on Microsoft Community Forum.

 

Follow the steps below to change the default search provider.

  1. Open Internet Explorer by clicking the Start button . In the search box, type Internet Explorer, and then, in the list of results, click Internet Explorer.
  2. Click the arrow to the right of the search box.
  3. Click Manage Search Providers.
  4. Click the search provider you would like to set as the default (For Ex. Google), click Set as default, and then click Close.

Make sure that you have at least one search provider in the list, and remove the one that you do not use.

Follow the steps below to remove a search provider (For Ex. Bing)

  1. Open Internet Explorer by clicking the Start button . In the search box, type Internet Explorer, and then, in the list of results, click Internet Explorer.
  2. Click the arrow to the right of the search box, and then click Manage Search Providers.
  3. Click a search provider in the list, click Remove, and then click Close.

Put the selection in the text of a comment and then in the Paragraph section of the Home tab of the ribbon, click on the Multilevel List dropdown and then click on the Define new Multilevel list item and in the Define new Multilevel list dialog, click on the More button.  You will probably see the level 1 selected with a closing ) after the 1.   If you want to, remove the ) by deleting it from the Enter formatting for number control, and then use the dropdown under the Link level to style dropdown and scroll down until you reach the Comment Text style.  Click on that and then click on OK to close the dialog.  The comments in the document should then acquire numbers.

hi zuja,

in office 365, forwarding emails can be done by both end users and administrators. please follow the detailed instructions below:

for end users:
1. log in to owa (outlook web app : https://outlook.office365.com/owa )
2. in the top right corner of the owa window, press the   button, and then choose options.

3. from the options page, go to mail > accounts > forwarding.
4. select “start forwarding”, fill out your gmail account, and tick “keep a copy of forwarded messages”.

5. click “ok”.

for administrators:
1. log into the office 365 portal: https://portal.office.com/home.
2. click admin > exchange > recipients > contacts and then press the “+” sign to create a new contact.
3. choose the mail contact.

4. fill out the details for the new external user.

5. go back to the mailboxes section, select the mailbox that you want to set the mail forwarding for and double click.
6. under mailbox features, scroll down and select mail flow, then click the view details option.

7. check the “enable forwarding” checkbox and then press the “browse” button (you can also tick “deliver message to both forwarding address and mailbox” if you want). then select the mail contact you created, and press “ok”.

8. press “ok” and save to close all of the remaining dialog boxes.

if you run into any problems when performing the steps above, feel free to contact us.

jay schlackman say:

We had the same issue and I was able to fix it using the below procedure.

To switch the DKIM d= field to your registered domain:

  • Log into the Exchange Online Control Panel
  • Navigate to Protection > DKIM 
  • Select the yourdomain.onmicrosoft.com record and click Disable in the right hand pane to disable DKIM signing.
  • Select the yourdomain.com record and click Enable in the right hand pane to enable DKIM signing.

The new settings will take a short time to take effect. I tested immediately after making the change and the d= field was still incorrect, but by the time I checked the following day, the d= field showed our registered domain as expected.
