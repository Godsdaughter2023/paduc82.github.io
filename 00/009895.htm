myall_blues say:

Without using a macro you could do it using a couple of helper columns.

Assuming your guest names are in column B starting at row 2, insert a new column to the left of column B and put a 1 in the new B2.

In B3, enter the formula =B2+D2 and copy down to one row past the end of your list.

Commencing from E2, add sequential numbers from 1 to one less than the number in B9. You can also do this using the fill handle by putting 1 in E2, 2 in E3, selecting both and dragging the fill handle down.

In F2, enter the formula =VLOOKUP(E2,$B$2:$D$8,2,TRUE) and copy down.

gord dibben say:

Macro solution.

Sub DuplicateRows()
Dim currentRow As Integer
Dim currentNewSheetRow As Integer: currentNewSheetRow = 1

For currentRow = 2 To 8   'range of data below title row

    Dim timesToDuplicate As Integer
    timesToDuplicate = CInt(Sheet1.Range("B" & currentRow).Value2)

    Dim i As Integer
    For i = 1 To timesToDuplicate

        Sheet2.Range("A" & currentNewSheetRow).Value2 _
                 = Sheet1.Range("A" & currentRow).Value2
        currentNewSheetRow = currentNewSheetRow + 1

    Next i
Next currentRow

End Sub

When you setup a device, you can setup which items are synced ( Email, Calendar ,Contacts ). You can also setup which folders and synched, and how far back you want to go e.g. last two days.

 

As long as the device is setup to sync the item, it will appear in Exchange (Outlook, OWA) and on both/multiple devices. If you modify two items from two different sources, at the same time, then the last one changed will win, and the other item will be rejected.
