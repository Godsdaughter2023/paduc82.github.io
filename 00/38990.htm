When doing anything major on an account or even when trying to recover an account for instance in your situation MS takes security seriously and wants to verify ownership of the account.

How they do that is by sending a security code to what they call security proof(s) this can be a phone number and/or an email you can have multiple security proofs on an account (this is actually a good idea in case you lose access to one of them). 

If you no longer have access to any of your security proofs and you need to do something that requires one (like recovering an account/forgot password) in your situation when going through the account recovery form at account.microsoft.com/acsr and it gets to the screen wanting to verify ownership/send security code there is an option there that you do not have access to any of the current security proofs on the account.

This will then start a form that it will want you to fill out it will first ask you for an email address that you do have access to and send a verification code to that email for you to put in to verify you have access to it (this is where the account recovery team will contact you once they determine if you provided them with enough information about the account).

Then it will start asking you questions about the account itself and any xbox console that the account has been on previously. 

Once you fill this form out and submit it (all done online do not have to mail anything in) the account recovery team will look at that information and determine if you provided enough correct information about the account. 

If you did they will then send you an email to that email at the beginning of the form to reset your password.

If they determine that you did not provide enough correct information they will still send you an email letting you know that.

It normally takes about 24 hours for you to receive the email from account recovery whether you provided them with enough info or not.

At this point you will want to keep that email and contact xbox support by going to support.xbox.com/contact-us and they can (escalate) your request up to the escalations team by filling out another form that will have additional questions about the account on it. It will also include a reference number off of that email (this is why I said you will want to keep it) and some additional information about the console that your account has been on previously.

The escalations team will get back with you within 72 hours letting you know if their was enough information on that form that was correct and send you an email with a link to reset the password if it was enough info. And again if you did not provide enough info on that form then they will also send you out an email letting you know that.

1. Stop the Windows Update service (make sure you are logged on as an Administrator)

Start button >| Control Panel >| System and Maintenance >| Administrative Tools

Double-click Services

(Provide the Administrator password or confirmation if prompted to do so.)

Hi David

#1. With XP and other previous versions of Windows this behavior is usually a simple Refresh issue. If a change is made and it does not immediately appear in Windows Explorer, the solution is to either close and Restart WE. Or you can also simply press the F5 key to Refresh WE.

There are many older Desktop Programs that are not completely compatible with Windows 8x and they do not place a shortcut on the Start Screen or in All Apps.

However, you do not need to cover the Desktop with Shortcuts. There are a couple of fixes.

A. Create a New Toolbar on the Taskbar to hold these shortcuts.

Go to your C:\Users\your name\Documents folder. Create a new Sub-folder and give it a descriptive name.

Go back to the Desktop, right click the Taskbar and select Toolbars/New Toolbar. Navigate to the Documents\new Sub-folder and select that folder. Click OK.

This will give you an expanding Toolbar with all of the Shortcuts that you place in that Sub-folder.

B. Another option is to use the same method to create a New Toolbar, but instead of creating a New Folder, you can use the existing Start Menu\Programs folder.

When you create the new Toolbar, navigate to the C:\ProgramData\Microsoft\Windows\Start Menu\Programs and select this folder as the new toolbar.

This will give you a handy Toolbar that is the same as the Start Button/All Programs menu that appeared in previous versions of Windows.

The real power of Windows has always been that if you do not like a particular designed behavior, there is always a way to change it. This power still exists in Windows 8x.

Have fun. :)
