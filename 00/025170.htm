Sub goFHL(strF As String, strDL As String)  'call as:  goFHL ActiveCell.Formula, ",LEFT"
'(255 chars max) id left of hyperlink friendly name eg: =HYPERLINK("http://www.msn.com",LEFT(H9,1))
    Dim v As Variant
    If InStr(1, strF, "HYPERLINK") = 0 Then Exit Sub
    If InStr(1, strF, strDL) = 0 Then Exit Sub

    v = split(strF, strDL)

techfreak_ say:

Those devices are incompatibe with the consumer version of Skype. Those devices only work with video SIP providers adhering to their native video configuration mode. Consumer versions of Skype do not adhere to the SIP protocol. Skype Connect supports audio-only SIP. Skype for Business supports video SIP but not necssarily natively in the format supported by those devices. Cisco call manager/server software along with that latest release of Skype for Business may allow a certain amount of integration of clients that is probably limited based off what exact combination of software you use. The alternative to the limits of that integration and required software components would be using a third party bridging service that would convert the Skype for Business video into a format natively supported by that device. Those services might even provide a conversion for Skype consumer. It would be best to either contact Cisco or look at their answers in their community on this topic. Research on bridging services can help you determine if their limits and cost outweights the complexity and cost of you doing it yourself.
