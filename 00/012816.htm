ruel mayoya msft say:

Hi Mark,

If you are using the Default MRM Policy as your Retention Policy on your mailbox and you have never changed any retention tags, by default emails will be archived and is recoverable up to 2 years.

Regarding “Today I noticed, when searching for something within Oulook (2016), that nothing was found from more than about a year ago...and I >>know<< the subject matter had earlier emails related to it.”, check if these emails can be searched out via Outlook Web App. If not, it means that these emails are deleted. Let your Admin, check if these emails can be searched out via e-Discovery. If yes the emails are in OWA, it indicates that the issue is related to the Outlook client, and the cause could be the search index is broken.

Now to define the retention tag “Never Delete Emails”, check If your Retention Policy has the “Never Delete” (default) Retention Tag. To check you can go to Portal > Exchange admin center > compliance management > retention tags > find Never Delete. Refer to this screenshot:

 

Next is to link the Retention tag to the Retention Policy. To do this go to Exchange Admin Center > compliance management > retention policies > double click the policy > Click “+” select the “Never Delete” then Save

Note: when changing a Retention Policy to a mailbox, you need to Connect to PowerShell and run the command Start-ManagedFolderAssistant -Identity <mailbox> to immediately apply the Retention Policy.

Next is to apply Retention Policy on the mailbox. On your Outlook 2016, go to Folder Tab and on the Ribbon find “Policy”. Check the Policy Tab and see if the Retention Policy is correct. By clicking “Add or Remove Retention Policies” link it will show the Outlook Web App settings for Retention Policy and you can change the desired Retention Tag you want to link on this Policy. You can refer to this article to and check “Retention Tags”

Also, when you right-click a specific email, you will now see Assign Policy, and select the desired Retention Tag (Never Delete), this is to apply the Retention Policy to a specific message

Feel free to post back if you have questions.

Ruel
