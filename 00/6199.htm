stevenhummel say:

The root cause of the above Smart Folders problem has been determined. Below is an explanation:

  • When you right click on an email in Outlook and hover over Move, you will see a list of your recent folders.
  • If the search criteria of a Smart Folder includes the "Folder is …" option, you can specify any folder within the "On My Computer" section. For example, if Microsoft is a folder in your "On My Computer" section, you can specify "Folder is Microsoft". Doing this will place this specified folder into your recent folders list.
  • When this specified folder drops from your recent folders list, the search criteria in the Smart Folder will change to "Folder is none".
  • Microsoft Level 2 was ambiguous as to whether this is a bug, poor design or an intentional design. In any case, it renders the "Folder is …" option fairly useless.

trevor rabey say:

That can't happen.

When you are tracking and updating you must have a status date, you must be in the tracking Gantt view, you must have the tracking table as the current active table.

The tracking Gantt view will show the current schedule bars, usually in red and blue, and the baseline bars, usually grey, stacked under. Do you see grey bars? They will not move regardless of what you do until you re-set the baseline

It is very useful to show the status date as a vertical red line on the chart (format, gridlines).

Do not enter % complete or % work complete.

Enter actual start date, actual duration. Adjust remaining duration to meet current expectation.

Make sure that any remaining duration on the left of the status date is moved to the right of the status date.

Make sure that there are no actual days on the right of the status date.

Now any actual work must have occurred on days of actual duration, and any remaining work must occur on days of remaining duration.
