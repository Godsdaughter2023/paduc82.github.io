jay freedman say:

First, to see any page or section breaks, you have to turn on nonprinting characters by clicking the ¶ button on the Home ribbon.

If a break occurs at the end of a line of text that's almost full, the visible part of the break will be pushed off into the right margin and leave little or nothing for you to see.

Even an "invisible" break can be selected by using the Find command to look for ^m (a manual page break) or ^b (a section break). If you have trouble remembering these codes, click the More button in the Find dialog and then click the Special button to get a menu of things you can search for.

jay freedman say:

The use of the paragraph mark as a symbol on the Show/Hide button (its real name) has nothing to do with whether the Display option for paragraph marks is checked. Microsoft could have used any other symbol on the Show/Hide button -- and maybe they should have, but it's difficult to think of one that would clearly communicate the button's function.

Checking the Paragraph Marks box in the Options dialog causes Word to display paragraph marks at all times, independently of any other nonprinting characters. Similarly, checking any of the other display options in that group causes the corresponding characters to display. None of them toggle the Show/Hide button. As Stefan said, repeatedly clicking the Show/Hide button shows or hides only the nonprinting characters whose display option boxes are not checked.
