The Activation Troubleshooter is meant for this very purpose. It allows you to find and apply your Digital License for Windows 10 to your PC if you change major hardware such as a motherboard.

Are you sure that you installed the same Edition of Windows 10 as you have license for? If you had Digital License for Pro and not Home Edition, you would need to install Windows 10 Pro (or vice versa). You also need to have logged-in to Windows 10, both from your original motherboard and the new one, using your Microsoft Account and not just a Local Windows login. Then if you do change hardware, you select that option in the Troubleshooter and it should find and offer the Digital License from your old motherboard. It does not work between different device types though, such as between a laptop and a desktop PC.

ronrosenfeld say:

Hi Stacey,

Your ONEDRIVE link did not work for me, so I was unable to examine your actual data.

However, I copy/pasted the data you posted so perhaps this will apply:

In the Arrived column, the years have a trailing NBSP (ASCII code 160).  This will need to be removed (in either Windows or Mac versions) before you will be able to treat the Arrived data as numbers.

Probably the simplest way to do this is with the built-in Find and Replace.

To enter the NBSP, at least on a Windows PC, one holds down the <alt> key and types 0160 on the NUMERIC keypad (not on the numbers above the keyboard).

I replaced NBSP with a normal space (you may want to replace it with nothing); and the values in Arrived converted to numbers, without the necessity of the algebraic conversion method.

Ron
