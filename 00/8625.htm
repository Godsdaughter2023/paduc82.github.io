The question is how to activate the Dictation button in Keyboard settings, for all apps. The button is controlled by the Privacy setting for Speech, inking and typing under "Getting to Know You". robert__p say:

Hi Scott,

In Office 365, shared mailboxes don't require a license and can't be added to Outlook as an independent mailbox. You can't sign in to a shared mailbox. Instead, you sign in to your own mailbox, and then you open the shared mailbox. When you send or reply to a new message from the shared mailbox, Outlook automatically sends or replies from the sender's account. Therefore, messages are stored in the Sent Items folder of the sender's mailbox.

As a workaround, you can modify the registry in the Outlook client. For your reference:

https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/kb/2843677

If you are using Outlook Web App (https://outlook.office365.com/owa/), a suggested way is to open the shared mailbox through “Open another mailbox” and send emails there. To do it, click your picture in the upper right corner of the page, select Open another mailbox and enter the address of the shared mailbox, click open.

As we are not familiar with the third-party software and it is not supported in our forum. Given the situation, you may still need to contact the third-party software support for professional assistance.

Thanks for your understanding.
