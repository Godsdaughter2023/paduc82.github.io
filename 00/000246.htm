Since there are more than 15 digits, you need to enter the data as text.

You cannot simply reformat the cell as Number because:  (a) Excel formats only up to 15 significant digits; and (b) the number was changed when it was entered or read incorrectly.  In particular, Excel substituted zeros for digits after the first 15 digits.

joeu2004 say: Doug wrote:

I said =IF(A2=E2,"YES","NO"), but the "NO" answers displayed "N/A#" instead.  Please explain.


As others have explained, the root cause is a #N/A error in either A2 or E2.

If you want to know how to get "No" regardless, try:

=IFERROR(IF(A2=E2,"Yes","No"),"No")

If you require Excel 2003 compatibility, then try:

=IF(ISERROR(A2=E2),"No",IF(A2=E2,"Yes","No"))
