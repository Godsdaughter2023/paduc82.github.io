david_saul say:

I had this problem on my old Dell.  Ran perfectly for 6 years with XP then 7.  Upgrade to 10 and it's all fuzzy because W10 won't accept my driver.  AMD stopped updating my graphic card driver in 2011.

Some insights:

1. You need to distinguish between the Graphics Card driver and the Screen driver.   They probably come from the same source but are not necessarily the same.

2. Try going to Control Panel (right click the Start / Windows icon), click on Device Monitor and then Display Adaptor (note: in my long journey to solve this problem, different people described this path differently - but on My W10 that's how to get there).

Double click, then Driver tab - and see what is installed.  If its "Microsoft Basic" - then you have a problem.

Try Update Driver.

Chances are slim this will work because W10 updates whether you like it or not, but its the right place to start.

3. Following the same path, try UNISTALL, reboot and leave for a few mins, to see if W10 either installs a better driver or finds a good update.  Your screen may be awful immediately after boot, but W10 will find and install a driver, but probably not the right one.

4. None of the above worked for me.  So now you try and fool W10 into accepting your drivers.

Find your old driver in a folder (Mine was in "c:\dell\R179781"  That took a while to figure out.  You are looking for setup.exe or similar in a "display drivers" sort of location..  You can try downloading from your manufacturer and reinstalling - but watch where it puts it.  You need the setup program.

Right Click the setup program and click on Properties (should be the last option).

Now you click on the Compatibility tab (sounds promising doesn't it!) You might see in a dropdown what OS your driver is good for.

Click on "Run Compatibility Trouble Shooter".  Follow the instructions and DAVE whatever it says.  The end result you want is that the "Run this program in Compatibility Mode for" is ticked and in the dropdown below whatever the trouble shooter suggested (but I suspect it needs to be W7,8 or Vista).

Don't tick any of the bottom three boxes - particularly "Disable Display Scaling..." - that fixes a w8.1 problem.

Save.  Uninstall your driver again (3 above) to force W10 to look for an alternative.  Reboot.  Wait.

4a.  At this point you may want to search for a third party driver that will run your card and W10 likes it.  This sounded dodgy to me so I went straight to 5.

5. None of that worked for me, but you may get lucky.

I researched compatible graphics cards and bought a new one.  Plugged it in and after 10 mins panic because the screen was real gibberish - it all came back to life and is wonderful!!

Ask your local PC Store for advice.  YOU MUST GET THE RIGHT CARD.  There should be several options.

My machine:

Dell Inspiron 530s, slim line (this means the back pane is not full width)

Screen: Dell 1908WFP 

I bought an ASUS RADEON HD 5450 graphics card for $30.  Because my Dell is a slim line I had to unscrew the back plates of the new and old card and swapped them round.  My screen is a Digital so I simply disconnected the RS232 cable off the new card. 

That's my story and it took two weeks to travel it.
