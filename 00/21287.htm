Hi Count,

 

It doesn't work that way exactly.

 

A full scan scans EVERYTHING. A quick scan only scans those things most likely to be infected. A custom scan scans what you select (it's almost like a full scan in terms of the selected parts you choose to scan) but omits certain memory-related aspects that the others do, so I would not use this except for special cases (like wanting to scan only a certain external hard drive or flash drive or a special folder or a special partition or something like that).

 

You can't change what's in a full scan or a quick scan (or add memory aspects to a Custom Scan). But if a full scan is omitting things, then open MSE and go to Advanced and check Scan Archive Files and Scan Removable Drives (those are the only things that could possibly have been omitted to my knowledge - unless you have a Virtual Machine installed or a dual-boot system and those are special cases I assume you'd have mentioned). If you mean things are not scanned in a Quick Scan, then that's to be expected, is normal, and you can't change that.  I'm not quite sure how this would affect a quick scan which would probably omit most of them anyway, but there may be some impact.  It will affect Custom Scans the same way as full scans if you select that area to scan.

 

That said, if the above doesn't explain what you're talking about, please be much more clear on precisely what type of scan you are talking about and EXACTLY what you think it is not scanning (I mean please provide full drive and or partition names and letters, how that device or whatever is attached to the computer, file paths and filenames (or at least folder names and what type of files are in them) and please explain how you know these things are not being scanned). Also include your version of Windows and your version of MSE.

Steve's reply has sensible advice. While it is possible to distribute symbols, chart templates and custom smartart, these all travel a separate files that have to be installed to the correct location at the other end.

  • For symbols, you can create a custom font. This then must be installed in the Windows\Fonts folder to be useable.
  • Right-click on your chart and select Save as template. This chart template needs to go in the Chart subfolder of the user's Templates folder.
  • You can program custom SmartArt templates, then copy them to the SmartArt Graphics subfolder of the Office Templates folder.

To distribute these, the only realistic solution is to create an installer that can move all the files to their correct spot. This is not simple to create. The low-tech solution is to follow Steve's advice and provide samples in the deck that users can copy and paste.
