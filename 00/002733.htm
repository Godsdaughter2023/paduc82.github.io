dukecampbell say:

Multiple variable equation within Excel

I have an equation with 4 variables, (100(a+b+c)/d)/433.67, which will be used in 550 cells, with additional data added daily, over a 4 month period. I can create the equation within the Equation Editor, but I don't know what to do from there. Any help would be greatly appreciated. Thanks!
joeu2004 say: Duke wrote:
I have an equation with 4 variables, (100(a+b+c)/d)/433.67, which will be used in 550 cells, with additional data added daily, over a 4 month period. I can create the equation within the Equation Editor, but I don't know what to do from there.

I am not familiar with Equation Editor.  Generally, you would write the formula:

=100*(A1+B1+C1)/D1/433.67

where A1, B1, C1 and D1 contain the values to be used in place of the variables a, b, c and d in the formula.

You might put that formula into E1 and copy down the column for as much data as you have in columns A through D.  References to A1, B1 etc will change automatically to A2, B2 etc.

Of course, then you need to fill columns A, B etc with the appropriate values.  If you have questions about how, I believe we need more information.

If you want to create a template to allow for additional data in the future, you might write:

=IF(COUNT(A1:D1)<4,"",100*(A1+B1+C1)/D1/433.67)

You can copy either formula down the column.

[EDIT] If D1 might be zero, it would be prudent to write:

=IF(D1=0,0,100*(A1+B1+C1)/D1/433.67)

and

=IF(COUNT(A1:D1)<4,"",IF(D1=0,0,100*(A1+B1+C1)/D1/433.67) )

The choice of resulting in 0 when D1=0 is arbitrary.  You might want to choose something other than 0.

    Assuming that you do not have Option Compare Text at the top of the module:

     

    UCase converts strings to UPPER CASE, so the values you compare with should be in upper case too:

     

        Dim m As Long
        m = wsh2.Range("M" & wsh2.Rows.Count).End(xlUp).Row
        For r = m To 1 Step -1
            If UCase(wsh2.Range("M" & r)) = "N/A" Or _
               UCase(wsh2.Range("M" & r)) = "EXTERNAL" Or _
               UCase(wsh2.Range("M" & r)) = "NOT YET BAU" Or _
               UCase(wsh2.Range("M" & r)) = "OTHER" Then
                wsh2.Range("M" & r).EntireRow.Delete
            End If
        Next r

     

    Or, slightly more elegant

     

        Dim m As Long
        m = wsh2.Range("M" & wsh2.Rows.Count).End(xlUp).Row
        For r = m To 1 Step -1
            Select Case UCase(wsh2.Range("M" & r))
                Case "N/A", "EXTERNAL", "NOT YET BAU", "OTHER"
                    wsh2.Range("M" & r).EntireRow.Delete
            End Select
        Next r

    How do I add weight in different units of measure? - Microsoft Community

    nosniknot say:

    How do I add weight in different units of measure?

    I want to get the sum of a few cells that will have different units of measure ie: grams to ounces to pounds etc.

    I know how to sum cells but if I put 16 in grams and want the total in pounds, I can't get it to convert grams to pounds automatically.
    pbezucha say:

    Use Excel if it is

    -          strict assignment,

    -          repeating task.

     

    As a summary from the first two answerers: If the sum value should be in A1 with the given unit in B1, and addend values in A2… with corresponding units in B2… downwards,

     

    ?          lbm

    24        g

    0.254    kg

    1.2       lbm

     

    use the formula

     

    =CONVERT(A2,B2,B$1)

     

    in C2… and copy downwards. The sum in A1 is a simple formula

     

    =SUM(C2:C10)

     

    (C10 should be the last cell address in you summed array). The C-column can be eventually hidden.

     

    Advantage: dynamic answer to changing inputs, especially that of the output unit (i.e. 'g' instead of 'lbm').

      

    PB
