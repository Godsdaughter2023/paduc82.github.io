    ronrosenfeld say:

    I'm not sure what you mean when you write "how to make a Range work".  If you mean how to automate the selection process of the cells to be processed, it will depend on the precise layout of your cells that contain the addresses.  If, for example, they are in A1:An, AND there is nothing else in column A, then the original code will do the job.  If they are set up differently, that method will not work.  Without knowing how they are set up, I cannot go any further.

    Also you should note that the modification you made in the error handling process will stop the macro at the first cell that does not contain a range reference.  So if there is an error within your list, you will get an erroneous message that the Operation is Complete, rather than an error.

    eg:

    A1:  D3
    A2:  D5
    A3:  XYZ
    A4:  L17
    A5:  X27

    There is an error in A3, but your modification will give you a "Operation is Complete" after processing A2.  My original will report that A3 is not a valid reference, and continue to process A4 and A5.

    Your modification probably worked since you had initially combined both the cell reference and the "words" in column A, and had no errors in your list.  It would be better to devise a process that allows you to select just the relevant cells to be processed, rather than omitting the error checking routine.
