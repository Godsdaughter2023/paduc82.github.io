Yes, I do think the situation definitely affects Arabic and Hebrew users of Office for Mac. The huge success of Apple (now bigger than Microsoft and Google combined) has been relatively fast in happening. Unfortunately, Apple's growth is due in large part to mobile devices, which has put competitive pressure on Microsoft and Google to come up with mobile solutions.


Microsoft has moved resources away from desktop applications and applied them to mobile and "cloud" applications to be competitive with both Apple and Google (eg. Google Docs and Google Drive). The past 2 to 3 years have seen only small improvements to the desktop Office suites. Rather than new features and bug fixes, we saw commands shuffled from toolbars and menus to the Ribbon. 


This past week Microsoft announced its Mac strategy, and it might be somewhat encouraging for users of RTL languages. At least you're not left completely out in the cold.

SkyDrive, Docs.com, and Office 365 do support right-to-left languages (RTL). So as of now, RTL users on Macs have language support except for those who need the full suite. 


SkyDrive uses Microsoft authentication (Hotmail or Windows Live)

Docs.com uses Facebook authentication

Office 365 Is available now, but I think more details about using Office 2011 with Office 365 will be forthcoming for Mac users when Office 2013 for Windows is formally introduced, which should be in the next month or two.


For right now, please give either SkyDrive or Docs.com a try. If they have all the features you need, then you're all set. The next full version of Office for Mac may take another 2 years. Whether or not the full version will support RTL is not known at this time. If "cloud" computing comes far enough along, it is conceivable that everything could move to the cloud and that desktop editions of Microsoft Office could come to an end.
