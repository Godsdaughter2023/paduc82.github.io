jay freedman say:
Yes, that is exactly the problem: by using only Shift+Enter, you have made the entire page one "paragraph" as far as Word is concerned. You start a new paragraph (and enable different paragraph formatting, such as centered vs. left) only by pressing Enter.

You can remove the space between paragraphs, either by formatting the single paragraph (right-click it, choose Paragraph to open a dialog, and set the Space After box to zero) or -- preferably -- by modifying the style that's applied to the paragraph as described in http://www.shaunakelly.com/word/styles/modifyastyle.html.

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

The normal.dotm template will be located in the following folder:

 

C:\Users\[User Name]\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Templates

 

though you may need to turn on the display of hidder files and folders under the Organize>Folder and Search Options>View facility in Windows Explorer to reveal it.

 

With Word not running, go to that folder and rename the Normal.dotm file to OldNormal.dotm and then re-start Word and try printing the envelopes again.

 

Word will have created a new Normal.dotm template when you re-started it, so if it did not make any difference and you had customizations in the original Normal.dotm template, you can delete the new one and rename the original back to Normal.dotm.

 

On the Printing Options tab of the Envelope Options dialog, does the image show the return address located at the bottom of the display, or is the Clockwise rotation box checked so that the return address is located at the top of the display?

 

If the former, is the delivery address fairly close to the right on the Envelope?  If so, then I suspect that your printer is having difficulty picking up the envelope so that by the time Word thinks it is ready to start the printing, the envelope has not travelled through the printer as far as anticipated.
