How to uninstall the Mail & Calendar app - Microsoft Community

ken.g say:

How to uninstall the Mail & Calendar app

This is actually more broadly applicable than just the Mail & Calendar app.  There are a number of apps that cannot be uninstalled from the "All Apps" list on the Start menu.  That is, when you right-click (or long-tap) the app name in the list, you do not get an "Uninstall" option.  This applies to pretty much all of the preinstalled apps.  However, there is another way.

Go to SETTINGS > SYSTEM > Apps & Features. Wait until it lists all the apps and then scroll to the desired app.  There will be an Uninstall button to click/tap.

I used this method to uninstall the Outlook Mail & Calendar app because it has some issues that make it not very useful to me at the moment.  I figured I would uninstall it and install the original Mail, Calendar & People app.  But thatt doesn't do any good.  After I uninstalled it, when I went to the Windows Store (the green one, not the grey beta store) and installed the Mail, Calendar & People app from there, it just reinstalled the Outlook Mail & Calendar app again. richie weldon - msft say: Hi fabulous-one,

Thank you for your interest in using Microsoft My Phone.  The only way to transfer Outlook contacts from your desktop to your phone is through WMDC (Windows Mobile Device Center) or through using Exchange Active Sync.  For the later option you will need to have an account on an Exchange server and need to configure your mobile phone to use that connection.

Microsoft My Phone will not synchronize Outlook or Hotmail/Live contacts.  This is by design.  It will, however, synchronize contacts that were entered on the device using the Contacts application.

Sorry for any confusion this might have caused,
Richie Weldon
