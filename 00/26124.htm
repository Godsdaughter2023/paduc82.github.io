You should be using Print Layout View to edit that document.

I suspect the problem is that the author has constructed the document with "floating" tables and "floating" pictures.  That means that everything is moving around in relation to everything else, and whenever you try to do anything, everything re-sizes and the document becomes impossible to manage.

My first suggestion would be to click in each table, and set its Wrapping to "None".  That's Table>Table Properties>Table and change "Text Wrapping" to "None".  That will place the table in a fixed position in relation to the margin. At least now you will regain control of the document.

You should certainly save in .docx.  There is a myth going around that .doc and .docx are equivalent: they never were, and they are drawing further and further apart.  If you downgrade to .doc, you will remove a lot of formatting information from the file, especially picture information.

How to track button click as a conversion using UET? - Microsoft Community

mike ncda say:

How to track button click as a conversion using UET?

Hi Team,

I want to track button click and phone click as conversions using UET. How can I do it?

Thanks

bingadselyse say:

Hi Mike,

Thank you for your question, I am happy to assist!

Phone Click Conversions:

We currently offer call tracking through a call extension you can place on your ad. We provide you with a call forwarding number that tracks the calls made when people click on the phone number on your ad. You can read more about this on our Call Extensions Article.

If tracking phone clicks as a conversion is something that you would like to see in the future, we encourage you to vote on this feature suggestion and share it so that others can vote too. The more votes received, the more likely this feature is to be implemented. Our engineers are always open to suggestions from our customers on how to improve our platform.

Button Click Conversions:

To track when a button is clicked on your website when someone visits your webpage through an ad, you will need to set up a Custom Event Conversion Goal. The Custom Event Conversion Goal allows you to track custom actions that take place on your webpage. Our help article,Why Track Custom Events, provides detailed information about Custom Event Goals.

This feature requires you to add the UET tag that your account generates for you, as well as the additional JavaScript Tag, found below:

<script>
window.uetq = window.uetq || []; 
window.uetq.push
({ 'ec':'Event category', 'ea':'Event action', 'el':'Event label', 'ev':'Event value' }); 
</script>

You can also find more information on this, as well as the JavaScript above, on the How To Report Custom Events With UET Article on our Bing Ads Help Site.

I hope you find this information helpful!
