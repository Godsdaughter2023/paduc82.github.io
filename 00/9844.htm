How do I make it so the page numbers are always aligned on the right as shown next to the green annotation in the photo below, and not in the position shown next to the red annotation? I.e. how do I force the table of contents to add another line of 'dots' and place the page number at the far right so it is aligned with the others without manually altering the text of the table of contents (as this would revert back to the original incorrect position whenever I update the table anyway)?

Thanks in advance for your help!

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

The issue is that the tab stop at which the word "Comparison" is aligned is to the right of the word "task" and hence to tab space after the word "task" is aligning the number at that tab stop.

You should increase the hanging indent so that it aligns with the tab stop at which the word "Comparison" is aligned and the text on the second and subsequent lines will be aligned with the C of "Comparison"  and then there will be no chance of the first word on the last line of text ending to the left of the tab stop.

If the modifications are not being retained, go to the Developer tab of the ribbon and then click on the Document Template item and make sure that the box for "Automatically update document styles" is not checked.

However, it would probably be a good idea to modify the TOC # styles in the template itself.
