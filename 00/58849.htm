    jamesmayberry say:

    This is probably due to the refresh rate that your monitor is set at. For scaling options to work, in my experience, the refresh rate of the monitor needs to be 60. (I'm using a Sayno 32' 720p TV, which can display, though the edges are outside the viewing area, slightly, 4K resolution, this is with a ryzen 5 1600, 16GB ddr4, rx570 video card. (At 2k (1440p) resolution, the borders are still within the screen.)

    In Windows 10, to change your refresh rate, right-click on the desktop, and left-click on 'Display Settings' (without the quotes).

    Then scroll down (by moving the center mouse wheel down, or hitting the down arrow on the keyboard) until you see 'Display adapter properties' in red. Left-click on that.

    In the window that pops up, you should see three tabs at the top, 'Adapter', 'Monitor', and 'Color Management'. Left-Click on 'Monitor'.

    Now, halfway down the window screen, you should see 'Monitor Settings' and directly underneath that 'Screen Refresh rate:'. The selection box below will allow you to change the rate from '59 Hertz' (or whatever it is currently set at) to '60 Hertz'.

    Hit 'Apply' in the lower right corner of the window, then 'OK' to exit out, and your scaling for higher resolutions should work.

    P.S. Also for anything above 1080p I must turn on 'Virtual Super Resolution' underneath the 'Display' tab in my AMD Radeon Settings.
