rohn007 say:

If it says you have "too many installs" the problem is that the activation system does not recognize you have uninstalled from the old machine. When you uninstall from a computer, there is no notification sent to the MS activation system. So they sometimes let it slip, but eventually say you have "too many installs".  At that time you have to make personal contact with MS

You will have to talk to someone at MS, but not at the normal support phone contacts.  You will have to do another activation and then you MUST select the "Phone Activation" option instead of "Internet Activation".  This will give you the activation support specific phone number for your location/"region". 

When you phone in, do NOT reply to the initial prompt for a "Product ID" (if you do, you will just get the same error message).  WAIT, a long time, until you are offered the option of talking to a live "service" rep.  Explain that you have uninstalled from the old computer and are transferring the license to a new computer.  They should then allow the activation to complete. If they still say no, ask to speak to a supervisor.  As long as you know for sure that you are following the install limits for your license, no more than 2 computers, then they cannot say you have installed "too many times". There is no limit in the license terms on the number of times you re-install or transfer to new computers.  There is a 90 day minimum between transfers to new computers, but you have not said anything that indicates that limit applies.

Hello there, MarthaPoinar! Thanks for posting your query on Microsoft Community.

About your concern, if your phone stops working or responding properly, restart it to get back on track.

To restart, press and hold the Power button until a Slide down to power off message appears on-screen, then swipe down. (It usually takes about three seconds for the message to appear.) To turn your phone back on, press the Power button.

If that doesn't work, press and hold the Volume down and Power buttons at the same time until you feel a vibration (about 10 to 15 seconds), then release the buttons. Your phone will restart automatically.


Please be reminded that:

Your phone may vibrate as soon as you press the Volume down and Power buttons because it's changing your ringtone to vibrate. Wait to release the buttons until you feel the second vibration 10 to 15 seconds later.

If you don't let go of the buttons immediately after the vibration at 10 to 15 seconds, your phone may show a black startup screen instead of restarting normally. If that happens, try pressing the button combo again.

Not all phones restart this way, so check with your phone's manufacturer to find out how to force a restart.


If your phone has a replaceable battery (it's often under your phone's back panel), you can also restart your phone by removing and reinserting the battery, and then pressing the Power button to turn on your phone. The date and time may be off after you restart this way, but you can fix that by swiping over to the All apps list from Start, then selecting Settings > Time & language > Date & time.

a. user say:

I FOUND THE SOLUTION, improving a little bit Doug Robbins - Word MVP code.
---> IMPORTANT: update the Caption Text Stype name with your own name!!

In my case the Caption Text style name is "Didascalia", hence in the third-last row, I wrote:

                 Selection.Style = ActiveDocument.Styles("Didascalia").

You need to update the name with your own name!

Regards and thansk a lot for your support!

_____________________________________________________________

With Selection

    .Range.ParagraphFormat.Alignment = wdAlignParagraphCenter
    With Selection.Borders(wdBorderTop)
        .LineStyle = Options.DefaultBorderLineStyle
        .LineWidth = Options.DefaultBorderLineWidth
        .Color = Options.DefaultBorderColor
    End With
    With Selection.Borders(wdBorderLeft)
        .LineStyle = Options.DefaultBorderLineStyle
        .LineWidth = Options.DefaultBorderLineWidth
        .Color = Options.DefaultBorderColor
    End With
    With Selection.Borders(wdBorderBottom)
        .LineStyle = Options.DefaultBorderLineStyle
        .LineWidth = Options.DefaultBorderLineWidth
        .Color = Options.DefaultBorderColor
    End With
    With Selection.Borders(wdBorderRight)
        .LineStyle = Options.DefaultBorderLineStyle
        .LineWidth = Options.DefaultBorderLineWidth
        .Color = Options.DefaultBorderColor
    End With
    .Range.InsertAfter vbCr
    .Range.Collapse wdCollapseEnd
    .Range.Select
End With
Selection.Collapse wdCollapseEnd
Selection.MoveDown wdParagraph, 1
With Selection
    Selection.Collapse wdCollapseEnd
    .Range.Fields.Add Selection.Range, wdFieldEmpty, "SEQ Figure"
    .Range.Collapse wdCollapseEnd
    .Range.Text = ": " & InputBox("Insert the the description")
    Selection.Paragraphs(1).Range.InsertBefore "Figure "
    Selection.Style = ActiveDocument.Styles("Didascalia")
End With
End Sub

Yammer desktop app gets installed on the user profile not the local machine, so the path of the installation file is located inside the user profile directory.

Please use the below uninstall string to get the Yammer app uninstalled.

"C:\Users\Administrator\AppData\Local\yammerdesktop\Update.exe" --uninstall

You can customize this script as per your requirement  as pass this script via GPO.
