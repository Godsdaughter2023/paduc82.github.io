But... if you have one column of data and you want to separate that out into separate column for each number, you could do something like this:

With your long list in column A, place the unique numbers in row 1, e.g.:

C1: 5
D1: 7
E1: 9
F1:15
G1: 16

(You can use paste special to transpose the list of unique numbers).

Now in C2 enter the following formula:

=IF(COUNTIF($A:$A,C$1)>=ROW(),C$1,NA())

Drag this formula across to G2 then down as far as required (you could use the GoTo and Paste method I described previously).

If you don't want to see the #N/A's then you could use:

=IF(COUNTIF($A:$A,C$1)>=ROW(),C$1,"")

instead, but that may cause issues due to "" being text.

 

Finally, just to check, you are aware of the PERCENTILE function?

You're confusing the folder names. Spam (unwanted bulk mail) and other messages that your filters keep out of your inbox are placed in the Junk folder. Messages that have been deliberately deleted are in the Deleted folder. Both of these folders are emptied at intervals, so they should not be used for storing messages you want to keep.
