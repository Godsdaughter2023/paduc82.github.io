Type your username exactly as you type it when you log in

Right click on it > Select Modify

Type your password into the box

Click Ok

Close Regedit and Reboot

jay freedman say:
If it really is a keyboard problem and not a difficulty with that particular document or the TOA field, you can unlink it from the macro editor.

Press Alt+F11 to open the editor, and press Ctrl+G to open the Immediate Window at the bottom right. Paste this line into the Immediate Window and press Enter:

    ActiveDocument.TablesOfAuthorities(1).Range.Fields(1).Unlink

Then you can close the macro editor and save the document. (The macro line won't be saved.)

a. user say:

I had issue similar in W8.1 recently.  Searched the web for a lot of things and tried a lot of them to no avail.  A common fallback suggestion was always do a reset/restore, replace image, or do complete new install if those failed.  I think that might be overkill!

What is most likely is happening is something got scrambled in the files under your user account versus the system being screwed up.

What worked for me is redo my user account from scratch.  Windows will rebuild it all again and you will most likely be back to like new again!

1) Manually copy any personal files you want to save to a temp folder under Public.  Do not copy any of the files in the hidden AppData since they are the ones most likely scrambled.

2) Make a quick temp user, it can be local, and set it as an administrator.  Or use another user who is administrator.  Don't need to do nothing here other than delete and recreate your user account.

3) Log into this other account and then delete your account and all of it's files.  This may take awhile!  Check under C:\Users to make sure they are all gone.  I had to restart my system and then I was able to manually delete a few stragglers that were in use for some reason. Restart system again just for grins.

4) Log back into your temp user and then recreate your user.  Then log into your new account and delete the temp user and all of its files.  Presto!  All like new again.

5) Manually copy all your personal files back over from Public.

Using this method you have to set up your stuff again but most of it is already installed.  It doesn't take that long.  Especially if you have a MS account you have been syncing everything with. It only took me about three hours and my setup is much more complex than most!

Much quicker than a complete rebuild from Windows 7 all the way to 8.1 with hundreds of updates along the way.
