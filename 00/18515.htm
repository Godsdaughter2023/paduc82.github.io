suzanne s. barnhill say:
It depends on what you mean by "turning off insertions and deletions." If you have accepted the changes, then they are no longer tracked. And if you are viewing in Final view rather than Final Showing Markup, then you will not see them. The correct approach to see only the changed bars is to format insertions as "None" and deletions as "Hidden." Note, however, that these settings will apply to only your machine; anyone else who views the document will view it with his or her own Track Changes settings.

By merging your Skype account to a Microsoft account, you can enjoy the following benefits:

  • You can use your Skype name to sign into all Microsoft services.
  • You will have a single password to sign into all your Microsoft services.

No, it is better to have names and numbers in different columns.

Assuming names in column A and numbers in column B, try this formula:

=SUMPRODUCT((A1:A10="Micasa")*(B1:B10))

or this one:

=SUMIF(A1:A10,"Micasa",B1:B10)

Adapt the ranges according to the size of your data table.

Private Sub Worksheet_Change(ByVal Target As Range)
    Dim Dest As Range
    If Target.Count > 1 Then Exit Sub
    If IsEmpty(Target.Value) Then Exit Sub
    If Target.Column = 3 And _
        Target.Row > 2 And _
        (Target.Value = "Completed") Then
        With Sheets("Completed Log")
            Set Dest = .Range("A" & Rows.Count).End(xlUp).Offset(1)
        End With
        Range(Cells(Target.Row, 1), Cells(Target.Row, 14)).Copy Dest
        Target.EntireRow.Delete
End If

End Sub
