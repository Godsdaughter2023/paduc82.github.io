don cuthbert say:

Not sure how they are handling storage capacity, but basically OneDrive gives you a common place to access your data from multiple devices, as well as serving as a form of backup for files placed there.  Because files are replicated between the OneDrive folders on a PC and the online storage, OneDrive use does not mean that files placed there do not also occupy hard disk capacity, as you have found.

The other thing is that copying files to the PC's OneDrive folder only initiates the upload to the online space.  You will see small icons (like a Recycling symbol) next to files that have not yet been uploaded.  The Cloud icon in the systray at the bottom right of your screen will also indicate how much of what is in your OneDrive folders has been backed up to the cloud so far.  So perhaps your 5GB has not yet been used and you will not be notified until that much has uploaded.  Upload speed is often only a fraction of the download speed offered by your ISP, so it can take a long time.

I had opted-in to keeping the 15GB of storage for free at the time when Microsoft changed that to only 5GB, but said anyone who requested before a certain date could stay at 15GB.  Yet I see my used space is now at 18.3GB.  Perhaps they are like Google and do not count certain photos against your cloud storage limit.

I see that for some reason I am shown to have 140GB of storage.  Sometimes there are offers given for more free storage, such as through your mobile phone due to installing some Microsoft App or from the phone manufacturer.  You can check your current limit through the icon in the systray.

When home users and small businesses buy office, it is my personal preference to insist on the Boxed CD "FPP" or Retail license of your choice of the 3 Office bundles.
