-Make sure that the Windows Firewall is enabled.

myall_blues say: Here is some VBA code to get you started. Obviously instead of hard-coding the value for numCells you could prompt for that value, and the range to work on as well if you wish. 

Sub GetRandomCell()
    Dim Rng As Range
    Dim randomCell As Long, numCells As Long, i As Long, lastrandomCell As Long
    Set Rng = Range("C3:AH52")
    numCells = 10
    For i = 1 To numCells
        Do Until randomCell <> lastrandomCell
            randomCell = Int(Rnd * Rng.Cells.Count) + 1
        Loop
        lastrandomCell = randomCell
        With Rng.Cells(randomCell)
            .Select
            .Interior.Color = vbYellow
        End With
    Next
End Sub

Hi Alex,

To answer your questions, let me summarize it as a few points:
a.    As OneDrive for Business is meant for personal (1 person) usage, we suggest using SharePoint’s team library for better control over permissions for large groups of people. With SharePoint, you can create groups like Marketing Team and Executives that has different permissions to access certain files and folders. This will enable you to have better control over your files and also much more convenient to setup. Using SharePoint, you will also be able to control external access, and what these external users can do with the files. One thing to note is that SharePoint only gives you a small amount of storage, although you can buy more storage from Office 365.

b.    As your files are stored/synced to the cloud, you will be able to share your files between different domains, as long as you have an internet connection.

c.    No, there is no option to add more than 1TB of storage. Just to clarify, the 1TB of storage is given to each user, not each tenant (account).

Office 365 customers on our premium Enterprise plans will receive an increase from 1TB to 5TB per user for individuals in organizations with more than five people subscribing to one of the following plans:
•    Office 365 Enterprise E3, E5
•    OneDrive for Business Plan 2 and SharePoint Online Plan 2

d.    For OneDrive for Business multiple accounts sync question, yes, it is possible to have multiple accounts sync to different folders on one PC, but it is not possible to sync different accounts to the same folders. All libraries sync to a server, and it is regarded as PC on our side. As you are planning on syncing OneDrive for Business using a server, please refer to my reply below.

e.    To run OneDrive for Business as a service, I’d like to say, there is no option to set it up from Office 365.
