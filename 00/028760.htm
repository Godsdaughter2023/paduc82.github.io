john mcghie say:
Bullets and numbers are the same thing: a "List" format.  The question is where the list is defined.

  • It can be directly applied to the text
  • It can be applied by a style created by Word's AutoFormat feature
  • It can be applied by a built-in style
  • It can be applied by a style you create.
Sadly, it can be applied by a mixture of the above, and that is probably what is happening to you.

When you save a document, Word checks the internal code and fixes it up as necessary.  That's what is producing the change: somewhere Word is detecting a conflict and fixing it.

The first thing to look at is the style you are using.  A "style" is simply a "collection" of formatting to which we give a name so we can recognise it.

Click in your bulleted list and note the name of the style that is applied.  On the Home tab, the name of the style will be highlighted.  If it is "Normal", there is your first problem.  You are expecting Word to maintain two collections of formatting, both called "Normal", one has bullets and indents, one doesn't.

Apply the built-in style "List Bullet" to the bulleted text.  The style is named exactly "List Bullet" -- no other style will do. It has secret ingredients that make lists work properly.  (Several other styles do too, let's save them for another rainy Sunday).

Now bear with me here: the Word user interface design is laugh-out-load bad; nobody can understand why Microsoft made this so bad.  But they did...

To apply List Bullet, you must first find it.  If you have never used it before, Microsoft will have hidden it.  Click the "Manage styles that are used in this document" button to the bottom right end of the Styles window on the Home tab.

When the window opens, you can see only five styles, and List Bullet is not one of them.  How on earth did they make this so bad? Towards the bottom of the "Styles" window you opened when you clicked "Manage Styles", just above the drop-down box that says "List" you will see an elipsis (three faint grey dots). Drag it down so you can see more than five styles!

Now, in the List drop-down, choose "All Styles". Microsoft provides three other choices: we can never assume that the style you want will be visible in any of them!

Carefully search the list.  In a final act of idiocy, the list is NOT in alphabetical order!  List Bullet is often about two thirds of the way to the bottom, but don't hold me to that!

Apply that style to your text.

{Whew!}  Now you are back in CONTROL.  Because now you have specified a collection of formatting for your text, and it has a name. So everywhere in that document, if you applye "List Bullet" you will get the same collection of formatting (the name must be unique throughout the whole document!).

And, you will notice that this style has bullets.

You will also notice that the formatting is not what you want.  Easy fixed...

Click in one of the paragraphs that has the List Bullet style, then go to the Styles chunk on the Home tab and right-click List Bullet (which will now be showing up, since you have used it in the document).

You can choose "Modify", or "Update to match selection".

Update to Match Selection is very quick if you have a paragraph that is already correctly formatted (say one of your other bulleted paragraphs that you have not yet applied the List Bullet style to).  Select that paragraph (make sure your selection includes the paragraph at the end, that's where the formatting is...) and choose Update to Match Selection.

The List Bullet style will now be formatted as you wish, throughout the whole document.

I always choose the "Modify" option.  The reason is that the dialog that next appears contains two checkboxes at its bottom edge.  One says "Add to Template", the other says "Add to Quick Styles List".  The Quick styles list is the chunk on the home tab that displays your most-often used styles (once you adjust it a bit...).  The Template writes the style definition into your Normal.dotm template, so your List Bullet style will be available and correctly formatted for any document you create in the future.

Yes, I know it was a lot of work, but guess what?  This is the last time in your life you will ever have to do it (if you back up your Normal.dotm template).

So now each time you want bullets you can apply List Bullet.  And each time you do, Word will not be trying to manage conflicting formatting, so stuff will stop moving around each time you save.

OK, so this was a little cryptic: feel free to ask if you did not understand anything.

Cheers

Perhaps this:

Sub move_sign_right()
Dim c As Range, s As String
For Each c In Selection
    If InStr(1, c.Text, "-", vbTextCompare) Then
       s = c.Text
       s = Replace(s, "-", "")
       s = s & "-"
       c.Value = s
    End If
Next
End Sub
