considerate_guy say:

The first thing I would try is to save the video directly to your computer.  See how that looks when played on your PC.  I'm not talking about how it previews in Movie Maker, but how it plays in a video file when saved (File > Save movie).

 

The next thing to try is to open Windows DVD Maker directly and give it the file created in step 1.  Choose the title, the options, the menu style, etc.  Preview that in Windows DVD Maker.  How does that look?

 

Finally, save that to DVD and see how that looks.  Doing this in two steps (save as movie, then create a DVD manually) will give you the best chance to maintain resolution.  However, even that may not be enough.  The reason is a DVD is a standard definition format.  Remember DVDs came out long before HDTVs were common (or even invented) and thus a DVD's maximum resolution is 720 x 480 (NTSC).  That's about 1/3 of a megapixel.  Think about how low resolution that is compared to a 12 or 16 megapixel image from your camera and it's not surprising that video looks "blurry".
