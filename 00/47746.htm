As far as most consumers are concerned, the main difference between any given 32-bit version of Windows and the corresponding 64-bit version is that the latter is able to use more than 4 GB of RAM.  And with more RAM, it's likely that having multiple applications running at once will seem more "responsive."

Even if you could find a legitimate copy of Windows 7 x64 to install (at this point, these are quite rare and correspondingly expensive), you would have to do a "clean install" in order to change from your 32-bit system.  That would require installing about 200 Windows security updates plus installing all of your application software from original media.  Plus, of course, backing up and restoring all of your data.

I think you're making the right decision to stay with what you have, but don't hold your breath waiting for Microsoft to go back from Windows 10.  That's not going to happen.

One fairly easy way to make your current computer more responsive is to change your current hard drive to a Solid State Drive.  SSDs have dropped considerably in price and it's not very difficult to make the swap ... and you don't need to re-activate or reinstall any software.
