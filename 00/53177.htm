hairyfool say:

The running time is not directly relevant as the duration of a specific sized file will depend on the resolution (image quality) of the film. A video that is twice the resolution of a base specification will take up 4 times the disk space. The 120mins used by manufacturers is primarily sales talk and will not be at high resolution.

When you burn a file to DVD the software has to add additional features, the minimum being lead in and lead out data but also could be adding menus or directories etc.. The size quoted by the disk manufacturers will be the raw data area in total of the disk, you would need to know how much space extra to allow over the basic file size which might be in the documentation but more often or not will be a case of trial and error. Some DVD burning software will tell you how much space is needed to complete the project but not all.
