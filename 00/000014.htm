Hi,

 

The problem is you're getting the time difference then converting it to a text value with that formula. Use this instead

 

=B2-A2

joeu2004 say: Imfego wrote in response to MikeH:
Yes, it worked wonderfully. Thank you for pointing that out — I have no idea why I overcomplicated things that didn’t need any of it. :D

Your "overcomplicated" solution was =TEXT(B2-A2,"h:mm:ss").

You might have been trying to ensure that the resulting time was accurate (rounded) to the second.  In other words, that the result of B2-A2 has the same internal representation as the equivalent constant.

That is a prudent idea in many situations, especially if you might compare the differences or, as you wrote, "counts differences between two timestamps".

The fix might simply be (see [ERRATA] below):

=--TEXT(B2-A2,"[h]:mm:ss")

The double-negative converts the text to a numeric result.  The TEXT expression effectively rounds the subtraction.

Why is there a difference here? 

neha singh msft say:

Hi Emmy,

When you install Office using a USB drive, it’s an offline installer and the Office downloaded from Microsoft site, needs internet connection to install. Error code 30094 usually occurs due to some internet connection issue or corrupted Office files. In future, if you wish to install Office 365 desktop applications from Office 365 portal and you get the same error, then you can follow the troubleshooting steps from this article.  

In addition, when Office applications are the part of Office 365 for business plans Office 365 portal, then click-to-run version is installed which receives latest updates. The version which is installed by store, I am assuming it must be MSI version. You can check your Office apps to confirm, in the Microsoft Office application, such as Word or Excel, go to File > Account. If you see an Office Updates section, the installation is Click-to-run. If there is no Office Updates section, then it is an MSI installation. please see Determining your Office version – MSI vs. C2R for more information.

Neha
