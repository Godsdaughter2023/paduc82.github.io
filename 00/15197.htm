When it appears, you need to:

  2. In the next dialog that appears, highlight the file.

nsilmike say:

the battery charge circuits for lithium powered gadgets can easily lose calibration.  if so, they tend to indicate remaining charge when the battery suddenly dies.  recalibration is easy, and does not indicate any sort of defect...it's just the nature of the lithium system.  But, your case sounds like it may or may not be this... so watch the gauges next time, or if you like just do a recal and see if it fixes the issue.  Otherwise, it may be that the batteries are now aged and potentially in need of replacement.

To recalibrate:

1) charge the device to full.

2) set it to play and play until the battery dies.  turn it on, and set to play again...repeat until it stays on only briefly (a minute or so, or less.) You can accelerate this phase by setting the display to bright, and always on, etc.

3) charge the device to full...this time with absolutely no interruptions.

done.  what you are actually doing here is recalibrating the sensory circuits to correctly recognize empty and full.
