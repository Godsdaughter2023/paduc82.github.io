jeeped say:

... My problem is now trying to use another macro to "clear the contents" of cells with this color. I've tried all sorts of variations and nothing seems to work. I tired color = 6, color = 27. I tried "Cell.Interior.ColorIndex" as well using different color codes and nothing clears the contents ...


As you've discovered, a CF does not change the Interior.Color property of a cell. You need to Evaluate the formula that caused the condition instead. Try this,

Sub mcrCleanCFtrue()
    Dim c As Range, i As Long, sFrml As String
    For Each c In Range("A2:L5000")
        For i = 1 To c.FormatConditions.Count
            If c.FormatConditions.Item(i).Type = xlExpression Then
                sFrml = Application.ConvertFormula(c.FormatConditions.Item(i).Formula1, _
                  xlA1, xlR1C1, , c.FormatConditions.Item(i).AppliesTo.Range("A1"))
                sFrml = Application.ConvertFormula(sFrml, xlR1C1, xlA1, , c)
                If Application.Evaluate(sFrml) Then _
                    c.ClearContents
            End If
        Next i
    Next c
End Sub

Note that the formula is pulled as it applies to A1 as a R1C1 formula then converted back to an A1 formula using the appropriate cell as the reference point and evaluated.

Edit: I just had another look at your supplied code. Why are you applying the CF to A:A but cycling through A2:L5000 to clear the contents? A2:A5000 (or A2 down to the extent of values in column A) should be sufficient.

For those of us who use 'Two Factor Authentication' with Apple, where you have to enter that 6 digit code to access anything Apple, THIS IS THE THING that leads to the problem when connecting your iCloud to Outlook!!!

How to fix: you need to go to your Apple ID login page (https://appleid.apple.com) from any web browser. Log in with your usual Apple username and password that you use for your iTunes account. Once in, scroll down to the Security area, and then look for 'App Specific Passwords.' Click on the link and then create a name for this specific password (like 'outlook_icloud') and then a password will be generated for you. Copy that password into memory or write it down or whatever you need to do to remember it.

Now at the same time, have your Outlook Account Settings window open for the iCloud account (you access that from your File/Accounts area in Outlook or from the Mail settings in Control Panel). You then need to copy and paste that password into the iCloud account password field (make sure your iCloud email username is correct - e.g. *** Email address is removed for privacy ***), and then hit OK to test the account. Should work perfectly and stop the send/receive/sync errors. 

This app specific password essentially does the two factor authentication for you - and stops that dopey error!

Hope this helps!

randomwindowsinsider say:

TiffanyChucky,

I also found a hack to bring back the old start menu (the one from Build 9879 and earlier builds).

If you are comfortable hacking the Registry Editor, do this:

1. Type "regedit" in the Cortana Search Box.

2. Click on the first result Cortana gives you "regedit Windows Application"

3. Navigate to HKEY_CURRENT_USER>Software>Microsoft>Windows>CurrentVersion>Explorer>Advanced


4. Once inside that folder, right click, create a new Dword (32-bit) entry, and name it “EnableXamlStartMenu.”


5. Finally, open task manager (Ctrl + Alt + Del), find “Windows Explorer” in the task list, and click on restart to restart Explorer.exe. OR simply sign out and sign back in.

And that should bring back the old customizable start menu from earlier builds.

My source is: http://mikesgoodstuff.com/2015/01/29/windows-10-preview-build-9926/
