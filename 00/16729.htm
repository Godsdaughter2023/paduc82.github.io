Hello.  This can be considered a rather easy problem, or perhaps a rather hard problem.


Please forget what I said earlier, as these problems can always be deceiving.  I forgot to take into account that the more expensive machine (#3) can also produce more than double what #1 can produce, and hence avoid the initial fixed costs of say two machine #1’s.


The “easy” solution is when {40, 60, 85} have a integer solution that total 750.


This one does.  We “assume” we will use each machine to produce the most it can produce.

The problem becomes harder when we need to produce say 751.  We need to calculate the number of machines at full production, and the number of extra units to make with one extra machine.


If we use just one of machine 1, then it’s cost per full production will be 200 + 40 * 1.50 = 260.


The cost we are minimizing is =Sumproduct(use, FixCost).


The number of units are =Sumproduct(use,PPD)


So, in Solver, this example would be:

ugura say:

If you tap and hold the back button on your phone, it will popup a list of running/suspended apps. You can switch to the app and press the back button until the app closes, or you may use the exit/quit option if the app has either of them.
