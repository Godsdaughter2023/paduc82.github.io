jay freedman say:
By default, hyperlinks are colored by the Hyperlink character style, which is blue and underlined.

You can define your own character style that has its color set to Automatic and is underlined, and apply that to only hyperlinks on dark backgrounds. The Automatic color is black when it appears on a light background, and white when it appears on a dark background. If you prefer, you can choose a light color instead of Automatic.
  • Press Ctrl+Alt+Shift+S to open the Styles pane.
  • Click the first of the three buttons at the bottom, the New Style button.
  • In the dialog, enter a name for your style.
  • Change the style type from Paragraph to Character.
  • Change the Based On dropdown to Hyperlink.
  • Select the color you want.
  • Click the option button for "New documents based on this template" to make the new style available in new documents.
  • Click OK.
There is one standard hyperlink behavior that won't happen with this setup: Normally, when you click a hyperlink to follow it, the color changes to purple because the Followed Hyperlink style is applied to it. After you apply your light-colored character style in place of Hyperlink style, there won't be any change of color after following the link.
