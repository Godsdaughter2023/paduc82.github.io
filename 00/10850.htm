hansv mvp say:

The probability of 0 Kings among 3 cards is =COMBIN(4,0)*COMBIN(48,3)/COMBIN(52,3)

The probability of at least one King among 3 cards is =1-COMBIN(4,0)*COMBIN(48,3)/COMBIN(52,3)

The probability of (exactly) 2 kings among 10 cards is =COMBIN(4,2)*COMBIN(48,8)/COMBIN(52,10)

I believe that is incorrect.  That is the probability that the number kings in 10 cards is not 1. 

The table below shows the correct probabilities for at least some number of kings in column A:

Formulas (copy down the column):

E2:  =HYPGEOMDIST(A2, B2, C2, D2)

F2:  =SUM(E2:$E$6)

If we use just row 2 as variables, changing A2, for example, we can normally-enter (just press Enter as usual) the following formula into F2:

=SUMPRODUCT(HYPGEOMDIST(ROW(INDEX($A$1:$A$5, A2+1, 1):$A$5)-1, B2, C2, D2))

Do not change the absolute references to $1 and $5.  With ROW, they are intended to generate the

Also, do not delete the column that is referenced (e.g. $A).  Alternatively, we could use INDIRECT.  But that is a volatile function, which I try to avoid.

However, I prefer to use the formula that Hans presented in E2, to wit:

=COMBIN(C2, A2) * COMBIN(D2-C2, B2-A2) / COMBIN(D2, B2)

Arguably, the formula in F2 is more complicated, namely (normally-entered):

=SUMPRODUCT(COMBIN(C2, ROW(INDEX($A$1:$A$5, A2+1, 1):$A$5)-1)
* COMBIN(D2-C2, B2 - ROW(INDEX($A$1:$A$5, A2+1, 1):$A$5)+1) / COMBIN(D2,B2))

But I think it better reflects the logic of how we count the combinations, which is essential for any probability calculation.  In this case:  (the number of ways to choose A2 kings) times (the number of ways to choose the remaining number of B2 cards after excluding kings) divided by (the number of ways to choose B2 cards).

This understanding is especially useful for other problems for which we cannot use HYPGEOMDIST.  For example, the probability of choosing B2 cards with exactly A2 kings and G2 queens, assuming H2 queens in the deck, is:

=COMBIN(C2, A2) * COMBIN(H2, G2) * COMBIN(D2-C2-H2, B2-A2-G2) / COMBIN(D2, B2)
