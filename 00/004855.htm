jay freedman say:

This is the "Comments pane" -- a leftover from a long-ago version of Word. There's no way to turn it on or off through the user interface, but a macro can do it.

As shown in http://www.pcreview.co.uk/threads/can-i-view-comments-the-old-way.2160402/, you can turn it on with

ActiveWindow.View.SplitSpecial = wdPaneComments

Once it's visible, you can turn it off by clicking the X in the pane's title bar, by clicking View > Remove Split, or by executing the macro statement

ActiveWindow.Split = False

If the workbook takes up 56MB on disk but swells to 699.3MB (= 731.1 - 31.8) when opened in Excel, there's a lot of stuff in the workbook which maybe shouldn't be there.

Next things to try. In each worksheet, press [Ctrl]+[End] to move to the last (bottom-right) cell in the used range. If that's way to the right or way below the last cell containing anything, try deleting the blank columns/rows. Excel is notorious for wasting RAM on formatting in blank cells.

Hi,

 

Thank you for posting your concern here at  Microsoft Community. I understand that you want to add your Outlook.com account as your send and receive account in your Custom domain account. Please be informed that the settings you wanted to have is not possible.

 

As a work around you can just set your Outlook.com account as you Send only account and set your auto forwarding option. TO set your Send only account, kindly follow the steps below:

 

  1. Log in to your account.
  2. Click the gear icon located at right upper part of the screen.
  3. Click More mail settings.
  4. Under Managing your account, click Your email account.
  5. Under Add an email account, click Add a send only account.
  6. From there you can set up the account you want to set as your send only account.

To set up your auto forwarding option:

 

1. Sign in to here. In the upper-right corner of the Outlook home page, click the gear icon.

2. Then click More mail settings.

3. Under Managing account, click Email forwarding.

4. On the Forwarding page, click Forward your mail to another e-mail account.

5. In the address field, enter the e-mail address where you want to forward your e-mail to, and then click Save. You may also check the box Keep a copy of forwarded messages in your Microsoft account inbox.

6. After you specify an address where you want to forward your e-mail to, you must reply to an e-mail that asks you to validate your account.
