If you use your mic a lot chances are the contacts in the controller are wore down a little bit. if you was to remove the cover of your controller look at where your mic plugs into. You will see some very small metal contacts. One on the side and one on the top of the mic port.

smiling cat-fab725ba-e75f-400c-9250-ecd9955c9c75 say:

Probably not the controller jack. Most likely the enamel coating on the wires in headset cable wore down somewhere. Pretty common near the plug or anywhere the cable gets pinched or stressed. The signal wires go to ground inside the cable. One way to test if you have a multimeter. Simply set it to around 2000ohms testing and use the probes to test the contact points on the 2.5mm connector. Here is a link to wikipedia on TRS connectors. The bottom portion the sleeve is a ground and the other contacts are for the speaker and mic. Just test resistance between ground and the two contacts. Pulled the headset that came with my 360 chatpad to test what you should notice.

  • The resistance for the microphone Sleeve to Tip should be around 720ohms or 1100ohms based on the polarity of the probes, and <10 when set to mute.
  • The resistance of the speaker Sleeve to Ring should range from <10ohms to 550ohms because of the volume control potentiometer.
  • If either stays at less then 10ohms with the mute in both positions or the volume wheel all the way up and down then it's the headset.

If it is the controller jack only decent way to get at it is opening the controller up which voids the warranty.
