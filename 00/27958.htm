brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:
Are you saying that you want a view of your Contacts folder that shows only the name and address?  The Address Book view will certainly give you that, but I want to make sure I understand where you want to see that view.

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

Outlook 2003 doesn't have an address book.  Your contacts are kept in the COntacts folder.  The "address book" is just a view of your Contacts folder.

When transferring from Outlook Express to Outlook, you should export the messages from within Outlook Express and then import the contacts from within Outlook.  The former will preserve the Received time and date.  Importing messages from OE using Outlook will change all the Received dates to the date and time of the import.

RIght-click your Contacts folder and choose Properties.  Select the Outlook Address Book tab.  Check the box labeled "Show this folder as an e-mail Address Book".  Click OK.  Stop and restart Outlook.

If you can't check the box, your mail profile is damaged.  Close Outlook.  Open the Mail applet in Control Panel and click E-mail Accounts>View or change existing directories or address books>Next.  Select the Outlook Address Book service and click Remove.  Then click Add>Additional Address Books>Next.  Select Outlook Address Book and click Next, OK, then Finish.  Exit the Mail applet.  You should be able to start Outlook now and see the Contacts contents when you click Address Book.  See http://support.microsoft.com/kb/287563
