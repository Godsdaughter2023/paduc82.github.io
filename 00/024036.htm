techfreak_ say:

I am sorry to hear that.  Unfortunately Skype only retains chat history and related content for ~30 days.  Any history beyond that time frame is stored in your local history (main.db) file in a sub directory consisting of your Skype name in %appdata%\Skype.  If your history only goes back 1 year and you had your history set to "forever", that usually means either your computer was refreshed over a year ago or the local history file became corrupted, resulting in lost data.  If the images and videos are not saved in your %appdata%\Skype folder or in a computer backup, then there would not be another copy to pull from.

Any images you may see that you cannot download that over 30 days, no longer exist in the Skype cloud.  You can still browse to your local profile folder and the media_messaging subfolder to save copies of the smaller photos that may reside in those cache folders.

See http://www.gmayor.com/scan_into_word.htm (includes Outlook and Excel)

Although not answering your question, if your scanner is WIA compatible, you could use the following macro to insert an image from your scanner into the open message at the cursor position

Sub Scan()
'Set a reference to Microsoft Windows Image Acquisition Library V 2.0
    On Error Resume Next
    Dim objCommonDialog As WIA.CommonDialog
    Dim objImage As WIA.ImageFile
    Dim strPath As String
    Set objCommonDialog = New WIA.CommonDialog
    Set objImage = objCommonDialog.ShowAcquireImage
    strPath = Environ("TEMP") & "\TempScan.jpg"        ' set temporary file
    If Not objImage Is Nothing Then
        objImage.SaveFile strPath        ' save into temp file
        On Error GoTo ErrHandler
        If TypeName(ActiveWindow) = "Inspector" Then
            If ActiveInspector.IsWordMail And ActiveInspector.EditorType = olEditorWord Then
                ActiveInspector.WordEditor.Application.Selection.InlineShapes.AddPicture strPath        ' insert in MESSAGE
            End If
        End If
        Kill strPath
    End If
lbl_Exit:
    Set objImage = Nothing
    Set objCommonDialog = Nothing
    Exit Sub
ErrHandler:
    Beep
    Resume lbl_Exit
End Sub

How do I add multiple contacts to a group? - Microsoft Community

alleycuts say:

How do I add multiple contacts to a group?

Original Title: Contacts.

 

How in the world do I add a lot of email addresses to a group??? How come Outlook is so hard to understand?

jmh3143 say:
Contacts are now called people.

To access People from Outlook.com, hover the cursor over the Outlook logo in the top left and click on the down-arrow that appears to the right of the Outlook logo.
Select People. 

Returning to your Inbox from People:
Hover the cursor over the People logo and click on the down-arrow that appears on the right of the People logo. Select Mail.  
You can also get to Calendar or Skydrive in the same manner.

Filtering your list:
Above the search box at the top of the Contact list on the left, is a filter option which says "All ". 
If you click on this you should see the groups you created in the past.  
Click on any of these to filter your list to just the contacts in that group.

Selecting one or many Contacts:
If you click on a contact name in the list, the details for that contact will appear on the right.  
Alternatively, if you click on the check box to the left of the contact picture, you can build up a list of selected contacts on the right by clicking on the check box to the left of multiple contacts.

Adding/Removing from Group membership:
Whether you select one or many contacts, you will see a top menu bar option for Groups.  
If you click on this, then it will display the list of groups with a check box checked for each group that the contact(s) is/are a member of. 

You can uncheck to remove or check to add, the selection of contact(s) to the chosen groups, and then click on Apply to apply the changes.  
Your selected contacts will then be updated to be added or removed from the groups you chose as appropriate.

Delete
When you select one or many contacts, you will see a top menu bar option for Delete.  
If you click on Delete and click Delete in the confirmation box, the selected contacts will be deleted.

Edit:
You can choose edit if you have clicked on a single contact (name or picture) in the list on the left to see their details on the right.  
When viewing their details, click on Edit in the top menu bar to edit or add details.
