Adding multiple resources instead of individual names - Microsoft Community

abbhishek say:

Adding multiple resources instead of individual names

I am a newbie with microsoft project and trying to plan up projects that involve 100 or more people. For quick planning, I would like to assign multiple employees as per their departments instead of individual names.

Eg. Instead of adding John, David and 20 other artists from Design department, I just want to add resource as Designers and assign 20 of them to a particular project and add an average salary of designers.

Would be great if someone could help with that. Thanks

trevor rabey say:

You may be interested to know that this question is as old as MS Project itself.

Typically, what people often do in this situation, is assign the resource called "Boilermakers" to a task or several tasks at 200% (2 people) or 400% (4 people) or some large % greater than 100%, and then increase the max units until the massive resource over-allocation goes away. So if you have 100 tasks and each one has 1, 2, 3... people assigned to then there will be lots of over-allocation. If you then bump up the max units (in the resource sheet, entry table) to say 16000% you "know" that you need 160 people. But you still don't know when you need the 160 people, or how many people you need on a given day.

I recommend against this approach, which doesn't really achieve anything in the way of managing the resources. It is just convenient for the planner, or worse than convenient it is just lazy and a waste of time, and counter-productive, because it pushes the problem of managing the resources down the management hierarchy to people who have less skill and fewer tools to do it with.

One way which is a compromise, is to define individual artists as artist1, artist2, artist3 etc, and then leave it to the local manager to decide which person wears the badge of artist1, artist2, artist3 etc.

Stand by for alternative opinions but that's how I would do it.

Sub Macro()
Dim ws As Worksheet, strFile As String, NRows As Long

Set ws = ActiveWorkbook.Sheets("Sheet1")
