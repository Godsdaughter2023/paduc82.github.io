1architect-retired say:

This thread may be too old for the following to be applicable, but my limited search did not turn up an answer to the problem of importing a contact list from another email account to MS Mail.  I stumbled on the following rather simple procedure which worked for me.  However, I haven't done research to determine if my solution is a one-of-a-kind or a general solution.  And who knows, it may not work tomorrow.  Anyway, here it is.

The following assumes you have an email contact list associated with a previous email account on your computer.  Mine was MS Outlook 2003, through Yahoo.com, as part of TV, Phone & Internet bundle from ATT.  Although I had a .csv file prepared, I was not asked for it.  In fact, I did not have to supply any information other than selecting the name of my former email program.

 

1.  Start MS Mail and from the left panel, select the "Accounts" title.

2.  Right panel appears, select "Manage Accounts".

3.  Select "Add Account".

4.  Opens "Choose an Account" box, populated with several common email services, including "Other".

5.  Select appropriate service.  (Mine was "Outlook")

6.  This created a new MS Mail account that acts as a dummy account. (No mail received in this acct.)  

7.  Return to the left panel and select your original email account. (Not the newly created account)

8.  Select "+ New Mail" which starts a new email.

9.  To the far right of the "To" line, select the people icon.

10. The contacts associated with the "Choose an Account" selection (Steps 4. & 5. above) should appear in the newly opened "People" dialog box. 

11. Clicking on a contact opens the contact info.

12. Clicking on the internet address puts the associated address in the "To" line.

Since I wrote the above from memory, there might be some slight deviation in the actual step by step process.  The only drawback I don't like, is having the dummy account.  But that is a small price to pay for the use of my original contacts.  Hope this works for a few folks.
