charles kenyon say:

I'm having a similar issue: I have a confidential document that I will be updating and then printing and emailing on a regular basis--the "attorney/client privilege" watermark is visible in the word document, but very faint and difficult to see. I can deselect the transparency option and then the watermark shows up clearly... BUT, when I print the document, it is much much darker and interferes with the text. Brief answer is you can't fix it because it is not broken. (Even though it is not working the way you would like.)

This is how Word works with Watermarks, which are in the Header layer or "story" in Word.

On the screen everything in the header or footer is dimmed when you are in the body of the document. Conversely, if you are editing the header or footer, the body will appear dimmed.

Word is designed to produce paper documents and to a large extent PDF. On-screen display is intended to help you produce those documents.

You can see what it looks like using print preview.

Watermarks do not show up in email at all. They will show up in document attached to emails.

I never send clients or others Word documents unless I want them to be edited by the recipient. I send pdf versions.

You can cut the image for the Watermark from the header or footer and place it, behind text, in the body of the document. This would then only appear on the page where you enter it and would be easily deleted.

Again, this is how Word has always worked with Watermarks.

Hi,

If your VLOOKUPS are returning numbers you can use

=COUNT(A1:A10)

if it's text try this array formula

=COUNTA(A1:A10)-SUM(IF(ISNA(A1:A10),1))

This is an array formula which must be entered by pressing CTRL+Shift+Enter
and not just Enter. If you do it correctly then Excel will put curly brackets
around the formula {}. You can't type these yourself. If you edit the formula
