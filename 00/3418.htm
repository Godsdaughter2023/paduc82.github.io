In theory,ActiveSync reports a Synchronization Error code 85010014 because of one of the following reasons:

Synchronization Fails because Application Data Folder is Redirected
Synchronization Fails because Outlook is in Offline Mode
Synchronization Fails because of Outlook script blocking
Synchronization Fails because ActiveSync is incorrectly registered with Outlook

Did you try runing Detect & Repair in Outlook? This option is found under the Help menu in Outlook 2003. If you have Outlook 2007, run Office diagnostics. When done, uninstall Activesync and reboot your computer. Then install again.



Also you can try running Outlook's SCANPST utility.  Prof Julie, one of our moderators gives these directions:

To run SCANPST, you'll need to find the utility program first and then locate where your Outlook.PST file is. You can search for these items - on my PC:

Office 2003

SCANPST.exe is in the \Program Files\Common Files\System\MSMAPI\1033 subdirectory and

Office 2007
SCANPST.exe is in the \Program Files\Microsoft Office\Office 12 subdirectory

Outlook.pst file is in C:\Documents and Settings\Julie\Local Settings\Application Data\Microsoft\Outlook\Outlook.pst

If you search for these files, be sure you use the advanced search features and select the options to search hidden files and system files
.
When you locate your Outlook.PST file, write down the full path to this file.
  • Then close Outlook
  • Select SCANPST.exe
  • This will bring up a dialogue box
  • Input the entire path and pst file name in the dialogue box.
  • Press the Start button
You may need to run SCANPST a few times to allow the utility to work through all the issues in your PST file.

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

Actually, the task would be quite simple if the article titles were the only text formatted in that particular way. Unfortunately, that isn't the case... The Review - Editorial paragraphs have the same formatting as does the table caption at the end of the document. Otherwise, you could simply;

  1. Display the Styles pane of the Toolbox
  2. Click in one of the article titles
  3. Click the Select All button in the Styles pane
  4. Click the preferred Heading Style*

Excluding the other content is what makes it a bit more involved. You could use Advanced Find & Replace to reformat the Review - Editorial text differently than the article titles, then use the steps above. Depending on how many documents are involved it may be worth creating a macro to automate the procedure, but I'm not able to provide that.

[*] In order for the article titles to appear in the Document Map they must be formatted with a paragraph style that includes an Outline Level assignment, but it need not be one of the built-in Heading styles... It could even be a custom style of your own design as long as it includes an Outline Level assignment.

kyle58 say:

After 10+ different people on your chat support, and countless internet searching and tinkering myself, I have found the solution and I want to share it with everyone. 

Let me be clear, nobody from Microsoft helped me, nor could they figure it out. I had multiple chat support people move me up to "senior tech" level people. They tried many things to my account that had me wait a few hours, try syncing again, and then contact them again when it still didn't work. 

Background:

There is a single underlying problem that is messing with a large portion of windows live account users: People. The People contact system that was, if I remember correctly, introduced around the time of the new Windows Phones coming out, allowed users to connect many different contact profiles from other places. For example, you could connect your contacts with your Facebook contacts on the phone, or at Outlook.com. I at one time had a windows phone, and I also used the People system on Outlook.com. I used the then brilliant Facebook connection to link contacts with phone numbers, emails, websites, etc. 

Problem:

What nobody is talking about is the fact that Facebook changed their privacy settings a while ago that broke the link between Facebook friend's email and contact info. This means that any contact that wasn't a 100% from Outlook.com or People.live.com originally, is essentially not a real contact and should be considered broken. This information was extremely hard to find, and I literally stumbled upon it on some part of the People.live.com site accidentally. There was a single sentence that said "Facebook contacts no longer work on Outlook.com", or something of that nature. 

On your People.live.com account, click a contact and see on the description if there is any links. You might see a Skype icon, an Outlook icon, maybe even a Google Icon, or for me it was a Facebook Icon. This is because when I linked all my contacts on People.live.com years ago through facebook, all my contacts because linked contacts through facebook, not actual Outlook contacts. Which means for syncing to ANY device (which I tried) with the Microsoft Exchange Active Sync system, it will not pull any of those contacts and that they are now broken contacts. 

Solution:

1. Remove your facebook link on your Outlook.com account by clicking the facebook icon in the top right corner where the skype button is

2. Open each contact and remove the facebook link. THEN make any edit. This will force the contact to update, and become an Outlook.com account contact. It will then get the Outlook logo right below their profile picture. Only contacts with this logo are considered a real contact on the system, and they will sync via Exchange Active Sync. 

3. become very sad that none of the information that was linked from facebook like address, website, etc get pulled through, because those fields are not real outlook.com info. They were linked info. 

4. Remove all your contacts like me, and start over from scratch, to make sure every bit of info is linked to ONLY outlook.com

If it wasn't so hard to switch to a new email these days when you rely on people contacting you through it for professional reasons, I would have broken free from Outlook.com. Such a shame that the system is so broken and either nobody knows, or nobody is talking about it.
