concerning 2.1 sounding like sh.. when enabled from the sound card, there might be a frequency cut issue. Let me get this straight:

- 2.1 means that your lower frequencies are routed to the subwoofer, while med/highs are routed to the speakers

- the frequency cut varies a lot depending on the audio gear used. Typical crossover occurs around 60Hz on larger speakers, 100Hz on mid-sized speakers, I've heard it go to ca.150 Hz on low range "computer audio" systems.

- sending the wrong signal to your speakers will strongly degrade your sound by trying to have  them play in a frequency range they were not made for, and as there is a filtering circuit in your audio system, frequencies will just be cut out.

To illustrate, say you have a crossover frequency at 50Hz, and your chipset's crossover is at 100Hz. you are sending 20-100Hz to your sub, which will play only 20-50Hz, and sending 100Hz-20kHz to your speaker. in the end you are loosing 50-100Hz. (numbers are here to illustrate and are random)

 

The bottom line is that you should send a normal 2.0 system to your amplifier (which might be integrated in said speakers and not a separate unit), and let it do the sub/speaker split, as it is configured to do it at the right frequency.
