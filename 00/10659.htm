karl timmermans [outlook mvp 2012-2018] say:

Can I reconfigure my outlook.com email address as a POP or IMAP server?  How would I do that?

Absolutely you can but remember that you will then not be able to sync your contacts from your Outlook.com account with other devices if that's an issue - in essence, you'll otherwise lose the benefits of having an EAS account in general.

You can simply add the Outlook.com account as a POP or IMAP account <along side> your EAS account and then move the contacts from the Outlook.com EAS account to another folder. Whether that's the best approach or not depends largely on how frequently you end up creating new contact groups or changing existing ones.

To answer your question about how to add the Outlook.com account as either POP or IMAP  - just need to add the account manually. All the information you need is contained in the following article

Configure Outlook with an Outlook.com (Hotmail), Gmail, Yahoo, AOL or iCloud account
http://www.howto-outlook.com/howto/accountsettings.htm

The one caveat about configuring an Outlook.com account as an IMAP account in O'2013 is that it uses an OST folder and the contacts (and calendar) folder will have an added description of "This Computer Only" which means that if you ever delete the IMAP account from the profile, you will not be able to access data in any folder with the "This Computer Only" designation. OST files can only be opened/read by the Outlook profile/email account used to originally create it.

You can also just create a new PST file and make it the default "data file" for the Outlook profile and copy the contacts from the EAS account to that contact folder without adding a new email account. The key thing to remember is that this new "default" contact folder will not sync up with Outlook.com but otherwise doesn't impact any data that exists in any Outlook.com EAS based folder. Don't mean to make this confusing but what the best choice for you would be depends largely on your particular situation. I'd be more inclined to go this route then configure a different type of Outlook.com email account - just need to remember where new contacts are added and proceed accordingly.
