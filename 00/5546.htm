dale howard [mvp] say:

KRFoundations -

If the Interdeliverable field a Flag field?  If so, the formula will work as written.  If it is a field with a lookup table with a "Yes" value and a "No" value in the lookup table, then you would need to rewrite the formula as follows:

IIf([Interdeliverable] = "Yes", [Finish], ProjDateValue("NA"))

KRFoundations --

Assuming that you are using the Flag1 field to mark deliverables, the formula would be as follows:

IIf([Flag1] ,[Finish],ProjDateValue("NA"))

suzhugh say:

autotext/autocorrect in word for mac 2011

I am trying to get all of my autotexts into Word 2010 on a pc. I understand MS for Mac 2011  and MS office 2010 are not compatible to bring over a file. Is there a way to get all of my autotexts and autocorrects from 2011 onto a page so I can manually save them in 2010?  I am unable to move 2011 onto my new mac computer because all the user licenses have been used and I can't talk to anyone about switching the code to my new computer and deleting it off my old one.

Thanks in advance!
Suzan
john mcghie say:
Hi Suzan:

Bob has invited the rest of us to have a bite at this question.  The reason he did so is that in their efforts to make Word 2011 "easier" Microsoft has ended up making some important parts of its operation very difficult.

Let's begin by saying that Office 2011 is indeed compatible with Office 2010: its files are not simply compatible, they are exactly the same.  But (there's always a but...) that does not apply to AutoCorrect.  Everything else is exactly the same, but not AutoCorrect.

AutoCorrects are stored in the ACL list: Office 2010 has two, Office 2011 only one, and it's in the Normal template.

AutoTexts can be in one of three places: The Normal.dotm template, the Attached Template, or the document itself.  Ignore the last one: it is possible, but you would know if you had stored any in there.  I think you would also be discussing attached templates if you had used any, so we can probably discount those.  Your attached templates will work just fine on the Mac if you simply bring the files over and put them in your My Templates folder.

First thing to do is to copy your Word 2010 Normal.dotm template and bring it over to your Mac.  Word must be quit when you do this so it does not lock the Normal template.  Put your Normal.dotm in here:
/Users/~/Library/Application Support/Microsoft/Office/User Templates where ~ is your login name.

That will bring in all of your AutoText and most of your AutoCorrects.

Any AutoText you had stored in other templates will come across if you bring the other templates across.  Put them in the My Templates subfolder of the above folder.

And that's all you can do.

Windows Word has a user AutoCorrectList file that Mac Word does not seem to have.  You cannot bring the Windows version over.  I am not sure that even Peter's macro will get the autocorrects out of it.

Hope this helps
