For the O/S values, all you need is a formula field coded as:
{=SUM(LEFT)}
For the running totals, assuming your table has a heading row, all you need is a formula field coded as:
{=SUM(ABOVE)+SUM(LEFT)/2}

jllatham say:

You have to handle combo boxes a little different from list boxes.  Use the .AddItem method with a combo box to add to its list.

A good time to fill combo boxes is during the form's .Initialize event -- which takes place when it is first loaded into memory.  Assume a list on a sheet named [Sys_Use] that begins in A1 and continues down to A5 holding the 5 choices you want to display.  Keep in mind that column A is column 1 when using the .Cells() way of referencing cells efficiently.

Private Sub UserForm_Initialize()
  Dim rowPtr As Long
 ' remove any existing entries in it
  Me!ComboBox1.Clear
  'assume a list on a sheet named [Sys_Use]
  'beginning at A1 and going on down to A5
  'column A is column #1,
  With Worksheets("Sys_Use")
    For rowPtr = 1 To 5
      Me!ComboBox1.AddItem .Cells(rowPtr, 1)
      'could be written like this, but a little slower
      'Me!ComboBox1.AddItem .Cells(rowPtr, "A")
      'or even like this, also a bit slower
      'Me!ComboBox1.AddItem .Range("A" & rowPtr)
    Next
  End With
End Sub

Have you tried installing the driver manually?

There is a KB here: http://support.microsoft.com/kb/2410180 ,here are step by steps to manually install the driver:
  1. Open the Zune software
  2. Go into to Device Manager.
  3. Right click the Unknown Device - your HTC Surround.
  4. Click Update Driver
  5. Click Browse my computer for driver software
  6. Click Let me pick from a list of device drivers on my computer
  7. Click Next
  8. Click Microsoft in the left column list
  9. Click Windows Phone 7 in the right column list
  10. Finish the wizard. The Zune software should now recognize your HTC Surround.
