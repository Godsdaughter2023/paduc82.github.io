hansv mvp say:

I'd use a helper column.

Enter the items you want to look for in two cells, e.g. apple in cell K1 and banana in cell K2.

In cell G2, enter the formula    =ISNUMBER(MATCH($K$1,$B2:$F2,0))*ISNUMBER(MATCH($K$2,$B2:$F2,0))

Fill down to the end of the data (to G6 in your example).

The count you want is returned by the formula   =SUM(G2:G6)

When you change the items in K1 and/or K2, the result will be updated automatically.

benishiryo say:

hi there.  isn't the combination of egg and apple 4 times?  row 2, 4, 5 and 6.  if so, you can input the apple in K1 and banana in L1.  then try this array formula:

=SUM(IF(FREQUENCY(IF(ISNUMBER(MATCH($B$2:$F$6,K1:L1,0)),ROW($B$2:$F$6)),ROW($B$2:$F$6))=2,1))

press CTRL + SHIFT + ENTER to confirm the formula.  when done correctly, it should have curly brackets surrounding it.

Above the search box at the top of the Contact list on the left, is a filter option which says "All ".

Delete

Edit:
