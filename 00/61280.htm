james vinyard say:

There is no operational speed difference.

If you want to install "any" program other than what is available in the Store app, you "HAVE" to upgrade to Windows 10 Pro.  And, you better do that before December 31st or it will cost you $50.

The upgrade is in the Store app and just open the app and in the search box, type in "Windows 10 Pro Upgrade" (without quotes) and it will take you right to it.

DO NOTE: when performing the upgrade (which takes about 25 minutes), you will notice that the upper left corner of the machine will get hot and that will be normal!  Just keep your fingers off of the machine during the upgrade and tilt the screen back as far as possible.

Windows 10 Pro works perfect after that.

However, choosing Google Chrome as a web browser on any Surface device is NOT recommended!  It does work but it eats resources and the machine will overheat.  But, it does work!

As for iCloud; Apple does not keep the Windows version of their software "up to date" like they do with their Macs.  Sometimes a Windows update will come across and knock it out.  Keep an eye on that if it happens and if it does, you can manually go to icloud.com and log in with your Apple ID and upload/download manually plus you can also use the web app versions of Pages, Numbers, and Keynote if you use them.

The Surface Laptop is a great little machine and I have had "ZERO" problems out of it!
