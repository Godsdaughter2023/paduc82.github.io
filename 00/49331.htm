sher amo say:

Hi Dwayne,

When you copy music, pictures, and videos from your PC to a blank CD or DVD, it's called "burning." Burning files to a disc in Windows is a method that allows end-users to have a replica of their important files. To assist you with your concern, we recommend that you follow these steps on how to save files to a disc or DVD. To do this, follow the steps below:

  1. Insert a blank DVD into the optical drive and close the drive.
  2. Open File Explorer, then double-click the drive icon to open the Burn a Disc window.
  3. Type a title for your disc, then select Like a USB flash drive or With a CD/DVD player, depending on how you want to use the disc.



  4. If you select Like a USB flash drive, the files copy to the disc.
    Note: You can save, edit and delete files on the disc many times, but the disc might not work in other computers and devices.
  5. Click Next in the Burn a disc window.
  6. Drag files to this folder to add them to the disc message displays.
  7. When all files are copied, you can eject the disc.
  8. If you select With a CD/DVD player, the files are burned to the disc.
