Hi Slipperynick,

Sorry for the late reply. This issue is monitored by me now. Based on my tests in the lab, when you share the screen in a Teams meeting with a full screen, the task bar will be covered and you cannot click Start menu. 

Only after you exit the full screen, then the taskbar appears. 

If you don't enter the full screen, instead, you maximum Teams client, the taskbar won't be covered.

In a sum, if you're the screen sharer, you can avoid to enter a full screen so that the taskbar won't be covered. 

However, according to your screenshot, it seems you request to take control of the screen sharer. 

If it's in this scenario, from my test, it's not available for the requester to click anything in taskbar. It could be a protection policy to prevent the others from sending commands to your system.
