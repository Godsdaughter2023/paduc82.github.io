ruwim.b say:

Microsoft Skype for Windows 10 (Skype UWP) application stores the chat history both locally and on the Cloud. For the local storage Skype UWP is using a SQLite database file - skype.db. The path to this database file is:

 

%localappdata%\Packages\Microsoft.SkypeApp_kzf8qxf38zg5c\LocalState\<Skype Name>\skype.db

 

I don't think that there is any limit to for how long the chat history is saved in the local skype.db file. The 2 years limit applies only to Cloud storage.

 

You cam make a copy of this skype.db file and keep it as a backup.

 

To open and read the content of this database file, you will need an SQLite browser. As an example, you can make use of the DB Browser for SQLite application.

 

http://sqlitebrowser.org/

 

With an SQLite browser it is possible to extract the saved chat history and import it to a text (.csv) file.

anthony.b say:

Wendy,

Here is one way to make an email template in Outlook 2010:

[1]  Send yourself an email, that looks like the template you want to use.  One way to do this, is to use "___" underlines for the fields that need to be filled in.  Like this: The date today is _____.

[2]  Open the email that you just sent to yourself.

[3]  At the top of the email that you just sent to yourself, click the Developer tab.  If the developer tab is not showing, you might need to click on "File -> Options -> Customize Ribbon" and check the box for "Developer".

[4]  Now, back to the top of the email that you sent to yourself, after clicking the Developer tab, click the "Design This Form" button.

[5]  On the Developer tab, click "Publish -> Publish Form As".

[6]  Under the Display name, give it a name (for example "My Date Template").

[7]  Click the Publish button.

[8]  Close the Developer tab window, and do not save the changes (it has already been published, so no need to save here).

[9]  To use your template, after opening Outlook, click the Developer tab, and click on "Choose Form".  A handy shortcut, is to simply type the letter "P" after clicking on "Choose Form" (that will go to the "Personal Forms Library", without having to use your mouse).

[10]  Look in the "Personal Forms Library", and your template will be available for your use.

NOTES: One way to speed up the process of using this template, is to right-click the "Choose Form" button, and then click on "Add to Quick Access Toolbar".  That will prevent you from having to click on the Developer tab, every time you want to use your template.  Also, after you click the "Design This Form" button, as shown in the screen-print below, there are lots of very advanced things you can do.  One idea is to keep it simple, and just click the "Publish" button, right away, avoiding all the advanced features.  That way you have a very simple template, that you can use right away, without the need to learn all of the advanced features.  If you want more guidance on this, you might try to get ahold of a big book on Outlook 2010 (at least 900 or so pages), and that big book might have a chapter on building your own templates or forms in Outlook.  I don't think the shorter books on Outlook (500 pages or so) will have chapters dealing with Outlook templates or forms. One book that has more information on this, via a chapter on "Outlook Forms", is the 936 page book entitled "Outlook 2010 All-in-One For Dummies".  In that book see "Book VII, Chapter 3".  For a even more detailed explanation, see "Chapter 27 - Designing and using Forms" in the 1111 page book entitled "Microsoft Office Outlook 2010 Inside Out".  In this same book, is Chapter 24 on "Email Templates" [ to do this with a template, at step #3 above, click on "File -> Save As -> Outlook Template" ; then at step #10 above, after clicking the Chose Form button, select "User Templates in File System" ].  I personally prefer the method that uses Outlook Forms, since that is saved somehow in your Outlook settings, in such a way that any computer you log into using Outlook/Exchange will have your Outlook form.  In contrast, Outlook templates, by default, are saved specifically on the c-drive of a single computer only, and are NOT available if you use Outlook on a different computer.  By the way, for the blackened-boxes in the screen-print below, I removed my personal information (first/last name and email address) because I did not want to include that in this post.  You will not see these blackened-boxes in your Outlook.
