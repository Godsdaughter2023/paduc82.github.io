daniel pineault say:

The easiest way to learn and debug this type of issue is to litterally paste your SQL into the QBE and troubleshoot it their, once you figure it out, you can correct you VBA accordingly.  That said try something like:

    strSQL = "UPDATE [ROC TABLE] SET [ROC TABLE].[MODEL REQUESTED] = '" & Me.[EDIT_MODEL] & "', " & _
                                    "[ROC TABLE].[CAGE REQUESTED] = '" & Me.[EDIT_CAGE] & "', " & _
                                    "[ROC TABLE].[REQUESTED BY] = '" & Me.[EDIT_REQ] & "', " & _
                                    "[ROC TABLE].[LAB CODE] = '" & Me.[EDIT_LAB] & "', " & _
                                    "[ROC TABLE].[TCN] = '" & Me.[EDIT_TCN] & "' " & _
             "WHERE ([ROC TABLE].[ROC]=" & Me.EDIT_ROC & ");"

This assumes [MODEL REQUESTED], [CAGE REQUESTED], [REQUESTED BY], [LAB CODE] and [TCN] are all text value, hence they are surrounded by ' ' and that the [ROC] is a number, hence no ' '.

Also, at the end of your procedure, add:

Set db = Nothing

just to cleanup and ensure you don't have any issues.

chad shurtz say:

Ok, the short answer is you are trying to save to a section that has a lot of pages - and because of that, it is taking too long to save and things are timing out.  If you create a new section and clip to that instead then it should start working (and be a whole lot faster).

Here's the longer, slightly more technical answer if you're interested.  As you know, when you clip something into OneNote, it creates a new page in the section you specify.  As the number of pages increases, the time it takes to add new ones to that section starts taking just a little bit longer.  Normally this isn't too big of a deal because generally people don't have an excessive number of pages in a section, and the number has to be fairly large before the performance starts to lag enough to really cause any problems.  However, you seem to have a lot of pages.  Like nearly 6000 pages.  In the clipper, we have set the timeout for each of the API requests to 30 seconds - and in this case that timeout is getting hit.  Odds are that if we bumped it up a bit then things would start working for you again, but that's a long time to wait each time you want to clip something.  I'd say your best bet is to create a new section and start clipping into that.  I think you'll be surprised at how quickly your clips are happening afterwards. :-)

Give that a shot and let me know if you have any other problems.

Thanks!

-Chad

teylyn say:

Insert two columns before column H, so now the winning numbers are in column J.

In G3 enter the formula

=IFERROR(MATCH(B3,$J$3:$J$27,0)^MATCH(B3,$J$3:$J$27,0)+MATCH(C3,$J$3:$J$27,0)^MATCH(C3,$J$3:$J$27,0)+MATCH(D3,$J$3:$J$27,0)^MATCH(D3,$J$3:$J$27,0)+MATCH(E3,$J$3:$J$27,0)^MATCH(E3,$J$3:$J$27,0)+MATCH(F3,$J$3:$J$27,0)^MATCH(F3,$J$3:$J$27,0),"")

Copy down to G13.

In H3 enter the formula

=IFERROR(RANK(G3,$G$3:$G$13,1),"")

Copy down. The result in this column is the 1st, 2nd, 3rd, etc. rank of the players after all numbers have been drawn and entered into column J.

You can have the bingo board light up for matches while the numbers are being drawn.

  • Select the cells from B3 to F25 with B3 as the active cell.
  • On the Home ribbon click Conditional Formatting > New rule > Use a formula to determine ...
  • Enter this formula into the box:
    =MATCH(B3,OFFSET($J$3:$J$27,0,0,COUNT($J$3:$J$27),1),0)
  • Select a fill color and close all dialogs with OK. 

Now clear the winning numbers in column J and enter them one after the other. The bingo board will light up with matches. As soon as one row is complete, a number will appear in column G and the rank of that player will appear in column H. You can hide column G.

ken sheridan say: The point is that a value at a column position in a table is a value of the attribute type represented by the column, so has a semantic significance.  A sequential number therefore implies a sort order as a table, being a set, has by definition no intrinsic order.  The principal of a sequence is that the last number in the sequence indicates the cardinality of the set.  If a row is deleted, however, then that no longer holds good.   So sequential numbers should be computed not stored.

You'll find examples of how to do this in the file RowNumbering.zip in my public databases folder at:

If interested you can do this using a macro.

Sub Move_Sets()

'two columns to 6 with 35 rows snaked

'blank row inserted after each group of 35 rows
Dim iSource As Long
Dim iTarget As Long

    iSource = 1
    iTarget = 1

    Do
        Cells(iSource, "A").Resize(35, 2).Cut _
        Destination:=Cells(iTarget, "A")
        Cells(iSource + 35, "A").Resize(35, 2).Cut _
        Destination:=Cells(iTarget, "C")
        Cells(iSource + 70, "A").Resize(35, 2).Cut _
        Destination:=Cells(iTarget, "E")
        iSource = iSource + 105
        iTarget = iTarget + 36
    Loop Until IsEmpty(Cells(iSource, "A"))

End Sub
