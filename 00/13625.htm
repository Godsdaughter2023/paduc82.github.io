Why does an Excel spreadsheet end at 1048576 and XFD? - Microsoft Community

dani_f1 say:

Why does an Excel spreadsheet end at 1048576 and XFD?

Hello,

Why does an Excel spreadsheet end at 1048576 and XFD?
Rows and columns. Can't go any further.

teylyn say:

Excel 2007 and above supports 2^14 columns, i.e. 16384 columns. They are labeled with the 26 letters of the alphabet, so the labeling is a 26 base system, not a 10 base system like our numbers. 

In our 10 based numbering system we have the digits 0 to 9. We start numbering with 1 and the next number after 9 uses another digit and starts that digit at 0. Hence 1, 10, 100. (Strictly speaking, our numbering system should start with a 0 instead of a 1, but that's a different story).

In Excel, columns are numbered with a system that has 26 digits instead of 10. 

The first 26 columns are A to Z. The 27th column uses another digit and starts that digit at A, hence AA.

column 26 = Z

column 27 = AA

column 28 = AB

column 29 = AC

...

column 52 = AZ

column 53 = BA

column 54 = BB

...

When all the two-digit headers have been used, another digit is added.

column 702 = ZZ

column 703 = AAA

column 704 = AAB

... 

... and finally

column 16384 = XFD

In a 26 base system, the value XFD equals 16384.

Excel 2007 and above supports 2^20 rows, i.e. 1048576 rows. They are just numbered from 1 to 1048576.

john korchok say:

You can post a screenshot by using the Picture tool (circled below):

If the heading style includes a rule below, here is how to change the color:

  1. Click inside the heading, then look at the Styles gallery to see which style is highlighted.
  2. Right-click on the highlighted style and choose Modify...
  3. Check the box beside Add to template. This will change the heading style in the template attached to your document so that all future documents will have the same style.
  4. In the lower left of the dialog, click on the Format dropdown and choose Border...
  5. In the middle of the Borders and Shading dialog is a Color dropdown. Choose a red.
  6. On the right side of a dialog is a graphic with buttons for top bottom and side borders. Since your style has only a border below, on the bottom border button will be grey. 
  7. Click the grey button once to undo the purple border and again to apply the red border. OK out.
  8. Save your document. When you close it, Word may ask if you want to save the changes to the template. Choose Yes or Save.
