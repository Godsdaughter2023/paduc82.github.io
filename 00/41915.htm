bigbenx88 say:

Actually there is an even easier way to test your PSU (Power Supply Unit).  Give this a shot:

Try unplugging your PSU from both the outlet it's plugged into and the Xbox.  Then take it and plug it into a wall outlet directly, no power strips or surge protectors, and confirm the Orange/Amber colored light comes on.  Once you have that, then plug it into the system and watch that light on the PSU.  One of four things is going to happen:

1)  The light will change to White for 5-10 seconds and then go back to Orange/Amber.  This is the ideal outcome.  The white light for that interval means it's charged the capacitors in the system and it should come on by pressing the power sensor on the front of the console.

2)  The light will flash between White and Orange/Amber several times.  This indicates a short in the PSU and it needs to be replaced.

3)  The light will do nothing and just stay Orange/Amber.  This indicates that the PSU is unable to pass power to the system or even recognize it at all.  This usually means that the console isn't accepting power and the console needs to be replaced.

4)  The light will go out completely when you plug it in.  This means a complete hardware failure in the PSU.  It needs to be replaced.

Use these steps to either fix the issue or at the very least narrow down the issue to the actual culprit.

Good luck, let us know how it goes.
