Hello,

Thank you for posting your query on Microsoft community. Let me help you.

As per the description you want to clean Your Microsoft all in one Media Keyboard, I suggest you to refer the below steps and clean your keyboard.  

Cleaning
Step 1: Unplug your keyboard from your computer and remove any batteries.

Step 2: Flip it upside down and give it a good couple of shakes.

Step 3: Blow some compressed air to remove crumbs, dust, and other fun stuff from between the keys.

Step 4: If you happen to have a mini vacuum that's designed to clean keyboards, feel free to use it, but refrain from using a full-size vacuum; you might find your keys in the vacuum's dust bin.

Step 5: Wipe the keyboard keys and palm rest with a lightly dampened lint-free cloth, like a microfiber cloth.

Step 6: Using a dry microfiber cloth, remove any remaining moisture from your keyboard.

Disinfecting
Step 1: Unplug your keyboard from your computer and remove any batteries.

Step 2: Wipe your keyboard with disinfectant wipes. Make sure that the wipes aren't overly damp and that you squeeze out any excess liquid before wiping the keyboard.

Step 3: Then immediately wipe your keyboard down with a lightly dampened microfiber cloth.

Step 4: Using a dry microfiber cloth, remove any remaining moisture from your keyboard.

Windows 10 USB Device Initialization Bug? - Microsoft Community

k2dls say:

Windows 10 USB Device Initialization Bug?

I upgraded a working 64 bit Windows 7 Professional system to Windows 10 Professional.  This system has the following devices connected via USB:

Perseus SDR (software defined radio)

Ten Tec Eagle (amateur radio transceiver)

SignaLink USB (sound card device)

Ten Tec 712 (sound card device)

Quatech 4 port USB/serial device

Upon reboot or unplugging/plugging the cable to the USB hub, nothing will initialize properly and none of the devices show up in the Device Manager list.  What does show up is a USB hub that fails to install properly.  If I unplug the two sound card devices (which use the standard USB codec driver) and then either reboot or unplug/replug the cable to the USB hub, the remaining devices initialize properly.  If I then plug in the two sound card devices to the hub they work and everything else continues to work.

So there seems to be some Windows 10 issue around how these devices are being initialized.  A solution would be much appreciated.

Update:  I have found a workaround.  All the devices initialize properly when I moved the hub from a USB 3.0 port to a USB 2.0 port.  So the issue may have something to do with how Windows 10 handles USB 2.0 and 1.1 devices plugged into a USB 3.0 capable port.

rohit siddegowda say:

Hi,

 

Have you updated the drivers for all the devices?

 

I would suggest you to disable and re-enable the USB controllers. The following steps will uninstall all the USB controllers and then re-install them:

  1. Right click on Start, and then click Run.
  2. Type “devmgmt.msc” without quotes, and then click OK. Device Manager opens.
  3. Expand Universal Serial Bus controllers.
    Note: You might have to scroll down the list to find this item.
  4. Right click the first USB controller under Universal Serial Bus controllers, and then click Uninstall to remove it.
  5. Repeat step 4 for each USB controller that is listed under Universal Serial Bus controllers.

 

Note: Restart the computer. After the computer starts, Windows will automatically scan for hardware changes and reinstall all the USB controllers that you uninstalled.

 

Hope it helps. If you have any further issues on the computer, please post your question related to Windows and we will be happy to help you.
