cortig say:
When you launch Outlook (or Entourage), it launches the daemon, and sets it in the Login prefs to be auto-launched when you open your session on the Mac.

You can manually quit the daemon with the Activity monitor (/Applications/Utilities).
You can also prevent it from auto-launching at boot by removing it from the login items.
Now… next time you launch Outlook, again, the daemon will launch and add itself to auto-launch at logging-in.

The daemon has to quit when you update. If it doesn’t, you get this warning.
The easiest is to quit it using the Activity monitor then update immediately.

The warning about opening with a version older than the identity comes from reverting to a previous version of Office. You need to make sure Office is perfectly up to date (14.2.2 as of today) before launching Outlook again (and double-check that you don’t have more than one install of Office).
