Please answer these questions to get more clarity on this issue.

1.      What is the make and model of the computer?

2.      Did you install the latest, updated driver?

 

The error message that you are seeing is the TDR (Timeout Detection and Recovery).

This behavior can occur for one or more of the following reasons:

 •You may need to install the latest updates for your display driver.

•Visual effects, or too many programs running in the background may be slowing your PC down.

 •The GPU is taking more time than permitted to display graphics to your monitor.

Note: If you are using an older video card, there may not be a video driver that is fully compatible with your version of Windows.

 

The TDR component (Timeout Detection and Recovery) in Windows was added to prevent damage to the graphics hardware or driver.

If the graphics driver fails to respond in a minimum time to a request, Windows will stop and restart the graphics component. If this happens too often in a short period of time, then you will see the TDR Error displayed.

Basically a TDR error simply means that the graphics device is being pushed beyond its available resources.

If you received a blue screen error, or stop code, the computer has shut down abruptly to protect itself from data loss. A hardware device, its driver, or related software might have caused this error. If your copy of Windows came with your computer, call the manufacturer of your computer.   2. Scan your computer for computer viruses.
  3. Check your hard disk for errors.

  • Type Control and hit Enter.

  • Open Hardware and Sound > Choose Sound.

  • Double-click the Active/default playback device.

  • In the device properties screen, select the Advanced tab.

  • Go to Exclusive Mode section in advanced tab. Uncheck the option Allow applications to take exclusive control of this device.

  • Click on Apply and OK.

Method 2: Uninstall and re-install the sound card drivers from Device Manager.

  • Type in Device Manager in the Start Screen and double click on it.

  • Expand Sound, Video and Game Controllers in the Device Manager window.

  • Right click on the Device and select Properties.

  • Click on Driver Tab and select Uninstall drivers.

  • Click OK.

Now restart the computer and install the latest drivers and check. If the issue still persists, reinstall the latest drivers from the manufacturer’s website and check.
