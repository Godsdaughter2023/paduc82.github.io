Basically, Staged migration does not migrate user accounts from AD to Office 365. This is done by Directory Synchronization. Staged migration’s batch only migrate mailboxes. Based on my experience, the following internal email flow scenarios should work but as we discussed above, staged migration does not support internal email flow, if you encounter any unexpected mail flow issue, we cannot provide you further assistance. Let us make an example here. You have A, B two mailboxes in on-premises. And you migrated mailbox A to cloud. User accounts A&B are synced by DirSync tool to cloud.
1). Email from Internet to user A will be directed to A’s Office 365 mailbox via the target address attribute which resides in A’s on premise mailbox.
2). Email from Internet to user B will directly be received in B’s on-premises mailbox.
3). Email from user A to user B will be delivered in B’s on-premises mailbox when the domain is changed to internal rely type in Office 365.
4). Email from user B to user A will be directed to A’s Office 365 mailbox according to the target address.

After you migrate all mailboxes to cloud, you can make the MX and Autodiscover point to Office 365. And convert on-premises mailboxes to mail enabled users (MEUs). Please note that converting on-premises mailboxes to mail enabled users (MEUs) is not to forward email to Office 365. This is to ensure you get seamless Autodiscover experience when connecting outlook client and keep related Exchange attributes in MEUs.
