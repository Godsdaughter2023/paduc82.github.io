Why am I hearing my mic through my speakers? Sound/Recording/Listen is unchecked!

bob is interested say:

I thought it was obvious that I had already done the usual things. 

---------------

Anyway I thought I'd update this with something relevant.  Many people post about this problem when using the motherboard's audio for both Audio In and Audio Out.  The key is to use something else for one of them.  As PCI sound cards are pretty much worthless these days (basic ones) just get one and use it for either your input or our output.  Use the motherboard's audio for the other.  This removes the possibility of bleeding.  Bleeding is apparently caused by poor circuitry layout in the motherboard.  And as its rarely noticed by most users it isn't widely discussed.  To test just blow into your mic making sure the Listen option is unchecked in your sound card's Properties. 

If you're using a laptop or something else without PCI slots, just get a USB sound module.  They're about $3 on Ebay.  If you use a synth/piano with an external sound device, use that for the mic.  Anything that separates in and out should work. 

I do hope this helps others.
