tauheedul ali say:
First Try This

Go to your wireless router, and switch of the Wireless device.

Make sure that the Modem Cable is connected firmly to the router and the other side of the cable is connected to the phone line properly. (If you are using ADSL)

If you are using Cable Broadband and you have two seperate devices (a modem and a router), then make sure that the cables are connected firmly to both devices and both are powered down for a few minutes and switch them on again.

Now the lights on the Modem should be flashing in green, and will remain on. If there is no light, then you'll need to contact your ISP.

Now try connecting to the device from your laptop.

If that doesn't work, try disabling and enabling your wifi device on your laptop

http://compnetworking.about.com/od/windowsxpnetworking/ht/conndisenable.htm

Second Option

Does it do it still when you are in the same room as the Wireless Router?, if it doesn't then it could just be interference.

Sometimes the Walls can act as a barrier to the wireless signal and it weakens the strength of the WI-FI.

If you are using a very old wireless router but have a new laptop, then your wireless router may need upgrading for better signal range.

The really old WI-FI routers were 802.11b they have limited range and speed.

The new WI-FI devices are either 802.11g (54mbps) or 802.11n (nearly 300mbps and longer range)

If your laptop is very old and your wireless router is fairly new, then you could buy a new USB Wi-FI dongle that will give you better reception of the wireless signal.

Most of the Wi-Fi devices made before 2007 are 802.11b (slow, short range)
Wifi Devices made between 2007-2010 are 802.11g (quite fast, good range)
Wifi Devices made from 2010-Present are 802.11n (very fast, best range)
