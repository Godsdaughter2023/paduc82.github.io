a. user say:

Unfortunately, you learned about registry cleaners the hard way. (Me too.) They are at best useless and at worst they can cause irreparable damage.

A Reset will recreate the registry but so will a Refresh. The difference is: In a Refresh your personal folders (music, documents, photos, etc.) are left untouched and your Windows Store apps are left alone. Your other software - what you installed with a .exe or .msi - will be removed and will need to be reinstalled. In a Reset your hard disk is erased and Windows only is reinstalled.

Looks like a Refresh is what you should do. Even though your personal folders won't be touched, it's always a good idea to back them up anyway. Sometimes the unexpected happens.
