wiring diagram - mute or dimmer switch of CK 7 - w - Microsoft Community

leevandren say:

wiring diagram - mute or dimmer switch of CK 7 - w

Hi. Does anyone have a the mute or dimmer switch wiring diagram, basically it cuts the radio off when a call comes through and restores volume when call is ended - automatically.

scoobyman---01 say:
leevandren wrote:
Hi. Does anyone have a the mute or dimmer switch wiring diagram, basically it cuts the radio off when a call comes through and restores volume when call is ended - automatically.
Hi leevandren
If your radio has an ignition sense and is NOT CANBus fed, you can use a 5 pin relay to utilize the mute function of your CK-7W. This diagram is from Page 7 of the installation manual for the old CARK-91. although wiring colouration still applies to the newer kits. RED = 12v +ve (Permanent live), BLACK = GND (Earth),  BLUE = 12v +ve (Ignition sense)  and YELLOW = Mute

The 5 pin relay is an electrical switch that is wired in series with the ignition sense of your radio. You cut the wire and connect the radio end to pin 87a of the relay and the other end of the vehicle ignition wire connects to pin 30. The YELLOW wire connects to pin 85 and a 12v +ve permanent feed is connected to pin 86 of the relay. Just place a blank connector over pin 87 (becomes LIVE whilst muting) as this is not needed unless installing an additional 14 pin relay to handle audio cross-over from your HF5 to the front speakers of the vehicle, or an external alert (audible alerts are illegal in the UK due to ban on sounding a vehicle horn between the hours of 23.00 & 07.00).       

 

5 pin relays are available from most good auto-electrical outlets such as Maplins, Halfords etc, as are the crimp terminals (Female spade connectors) you will need to make the necessary connections to the relay pins.

https://stackoverflow.com/questions/35847049/spell-check-within-a-cell-and-change-color-of-misspelled-words-to-red

Sub SpellCheckUpdate()

Dim cel As Range, CellLen As Long, CurChr As Long, TheString As String

For Each cel In Selection
    For CurChr = 1 To Len(cel.Value)
        If Asc(Mid(cel.Value, CurChr, 1)) = 32 Then
            If InStr(CurChr + 1, cel.Value, " ") = 0 Then
                TheString = Mid(cel.Value, CurChr + 1, Len(cel.Value) - CurChr)
            Else
                TheString = Mid(cel.Value, CurChr + 1, InStr(CurChr + 1, cel.Value, " ") - CurChr)
            End If
            If Not Application.CheckSpelling(Word:=TheString) Then
                cel.Characters(CurChr + 1, Len(TheString)).Font.Color = RGB(255, 0, 0)
            Else
                cel.Characters(CurChr + 1, Len(TheString)).Font.Color = RGB(0, 0, 0)
            End If
            TheString = ""
        End If
    Next CurChr
Next cel

End Sub
