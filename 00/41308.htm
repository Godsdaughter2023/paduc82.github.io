davem121 say:
Hi Larry,

Backup - a backup will only backup your personal files, which you can then restore if you ever need. A backup will not keep a copy of your settings, Windows or the applications you have installed

System Image - is basically like a copy of your hard drive - A system image is a backup that contains everything stored on your computer, including the full installation of Windows 10, settings, applications, and all your files

A backup can be user to copy your files to a new PC

A System Image is only good on the PC it was created on . . .

charlie klausen say:
Hi Larry,

You're right, a Full Backup and a System Image are similar. Here's how I look at it.

If you have your PC just the way you want it, all your files are fine, the OS is working perfectly, a system image would be ideal. It captures everything about the operating system and images it up for you.

If you're OS wasn't running great, but maybe you had all your files on there you needed, a back up would make more sense. You wouldn't want an entire capture of the OS, cause it isn't working great. You'd want to pick and choose the stuff you wanted on back on your PC.

Hope this helps!
