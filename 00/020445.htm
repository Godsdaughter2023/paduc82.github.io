john korchok say:

Sub TextboxRemoval()

  Dim shp As Shape, shpRange As ShapeRange   Dim shpArray() As Variant
  x = 1
  For Each shp In ActiveDocument.Shapes
    If shp.Type = msoTextBox Then
      sString = Left(shp.TextFrame.TextRange.Text, shp.TextFrame.TextRange.Characters.Count - 1)
      If Len(sString) > 0 Then
        shp.Select
        Selection.Collapse wdCollapseStart
        Selection.InsertBefore sString
        ReDim Preserve shpArray(1 To x)
        shpArray(x) = shp.Name
        x = x + 1
      End If
    End If
  Next shp
  Set shpRange = ActiveDocument.Shapes.Range(shpArray)
  shpRange.Delete
End Sub

this is up in the ribbon area.  The bluish green border on the top of the window should have a Greenish highlighted are labeled "Chart Tools".  Just below that is the "Format Tab"  Click on that an make sure it appears selected.  Now go to the left of that area just below the "Format" tab.  In the very first section (labelled at the bottom of that section as "current selection"  are three buttons filling the width of that section.  The first one at the top of the section is a dropdown.  For me it shows "Plot Area", but it could show something else.  To the right of that dropdown is a Down Arrow.  Click on that and all the parts of your graph will be listed.  Part of that list will be the name of any series you have on your graph.  You should have multiple series names since you want to put one on the secondary axis.  Select the one you want to move to the secondary axis.   This series name should now show where "plot area" previously showed.   This shows you have selected one of your series.  Now directly below that is the second button in the "Current Selection" are.  It is labelled "Format Selection".  Click that.

A dialog will pop up.  It will be entitled "Format Data Series"    in the right pane of that dialog you will find

Series Options

A frame with a caption  "Plot Series On"  which holds two radio buttons:

Primary Axis

Secondary Axis

click the secondary axis and click close
