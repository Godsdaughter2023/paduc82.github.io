c)      Set the default download location to the newly created folder, then click OK to save the setting.

e)      Click on Change Permissions and then Add - Enter "Everyone" in the box and click OK

f)       In the deny column (right side) fill in the check mark for "Create Files / Write Data"

g)      Click OK all the way out

 

Method 2:

To Change IE9 Default "Save" Download Location in Internet Explorer 9

a)      Open Internet Explorer 9.

b)      Click on the gear icon in the upper right corner, and then click on View downloads.

c)      Click on the Options link

d)      Click on the Browse button

e)      Navigate to and select (highlight) the folder that you want IE9 to use as the default download location, then click on the Select Folder button

f)       Click on OK.

g)      Click on Close.

anajand say:

I had the same problem. It seems to happen when all the following 3 conditions occur together:

1- there is a picture below a paragraph that has a style (Heading 1 for instance)

2- the picture in anchored (for instance it is placed behind the text) so it can be moved around the page with the mouse

3- the anchor sign is placed at the beginning of the paragraph

I assume that you really want to have a movable picture below your paragraph, thus you cannot eliminate the 1st or the 2nd condition. The solution is to eliminate the 3rd condition, by doing the following 3 steps:

Step 1 - in the "Home" menu, in the "Paragraph" section, enable the "show or hide formatting marks", as shown below:

Step 2 - click on the picture that is under your paragraph, the anchor sign should appear at the beginning of the paragraph or just above it in the left margin:


Step 3 - just click on the anchor sign that is shown on the top left corner of the paragraph, drag it and drop it just after the paragraph(sometimes moving the picture itself further below the paragraph can have the same effect):


From now on the Reference to the paragraph will no more contain the initial "/" sign.

DONE!

suzanne s. barnhill say:

Tolerating the slash is not an option if the result is to bring in the anchored graphic as part of the cross-reference. I think what's happening here is a variation on the problem described at http://www.thedoctools.com/index.php?show=wt_cross-reference_trouble: the object anchor is getting caught up in the bookmark automatically applied to a heading or caption, so a cross-reference that replicates the content of that bookmark will include the anchored graphic as well.

The best solution is to avoid wrapped graphics in this situation. If the graphic is In Line With Text, the problem is less likely to occur and is more easily solved. The second-best solution would be to move the object anchor to an adjacent paragraph before creating the cross-reference. It might also work (though it would be impractical) to create cross-references to the caption/heading before adding the wrapped graphic anchored to the caption or heading.

jay freedman say:
I have Word 2013 from Office 365 running on Windows 8, and the Draw group is on the Table Tools Layout tab as it should be. It's possible that it was accidentally removed, but if that's the case then it should be possible to restore it.

Before trying an uninstall/reinstall, give this a whirl:

  • Right-click the ribbon and click Customize Ribbon.
  • In the Options dialog that opens, set both of the dropdowns at the top to "Tool Tabs".
  • In the Table Tools section of each list box, click the plus sign to expand the Layout item. Presumably you'll see a Draw item in the list on the left, but not in the list on the right.
  • On the right side, click the Table item under Layout. That sets where the Draw group will be inserted.
  • On the left side, click the Draw item
  • Click the Add button between the lists. The Draw item should now appear in the list on the right.
  • Click OK.


Now click in a table, and look at the Table Tools Layout tab. The Draw group should be between the Table group and the Rows & Columns group.

ronaldoonenil say:

The answer below assumes that all the same values will be grouped together in column A.

In Cell B1 enter the below formula

=VLOOKUP(A1,'Table 2'!$A:$B,2,0)

In Cell B2 enter the below formula and copy copy / drag it down

=IF(A2=A1,"",VLOOKUP(A2,'Table 2'!$A:$B,2,0))
