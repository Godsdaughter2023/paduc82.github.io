brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

There is no such thing as "365 Outlook". Office 365 is a subscription service that has multiple subscription levels. All subscription levels give you a mailbox and an address that acts as a username to login and access that mailbox. All subscription levels except "Office Online" permit you to download and install Office Professional Plus 2016. This suite of programs includes Outlook 2016, a mail client that can connect to any type of mailbox, including the one you have via your Office 365 subscription. There are also Office Home & Student 2016, Office Home & Business 2016, and Office Professional 2016 products that are not subscriptions, but can be licensed for a one-time fee. The latter two products also contain Outlook 2016.

Office Online, Office Home, and Office Personal all use Outlook.com for the mailbox hosting. If you have Home or Personal, you can install Outlook 2016 and configure it to access the Outlook.com mailbox. When you do so, you'll also get access to the calendar and contacts services the mailbox provides. The access will be two-way between the mailbox and the Outlook 2016 client. Changes you make in the client will appear in the web interface and vice versa.

Outlook.com also supports two-way connections to client apps on devices running iOS and Android. You can use either the native Email, Calendar, and People apps or you can visit the appropriate app store and download and install Outlook for iOS or Outlook for Android, as appropriate for another interface to the same mailbox. Since all the devices access the same mailbox using two-way connections, anything you do via a web brower, via a client on a mobile device, or via Outlook 2016 on a PC (or a Mac, for that matter) will show up on all places, since all methods access the same data.

Does this help?

You could put this formula in A1:

 

=SUBTOTAL(5,G1:G13)

 

which will give you the minimum value displayed in the range G1:G13 (adjust this to suit your data - you can even make it G:G to refer to the whole column).
