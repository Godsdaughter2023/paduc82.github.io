brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

> are you referring to Outlook 365

No. There really is no such thing as Outlook 365. Outlook.com is a free mail service that is web-based. You don't need to pay anything to use it. Office 365 is a subscription service. For most subscriptions, Office 365 gives you the right to download and install Office 2013 or 2016, which includes Outlook 2013 or 2013. Anyone who subscribed to Office 365 when Office 2013 was the current version and maintained their subscription is eligible to upgrade to Office 2016 on the PC.

An Office 365 Personal (one user) or Office 365 Home (five users) can install Office 2016 and configure Outlook 2016 to use Outlook.com as the mail backend. Office 365 for Business uses Office365.com as the back end. Both Outlook.com and Office365.com use the same Exchange mail server architecture.

Anyone who can use Outlook 2016 (or 2013) can also connect to any mail service they choose, including Yahoo, Gmail, Zoho, or any other mail service.

Clearer or more confusing?

ossiemac say:

AFAIK there is no problem running macros from Office 365. It should not be any different to an individual install of Office.

Ensure that you have macros enabled. More on enabling macros at the following link.

https://support.office.com/en-us/article/enable-or-disable-macros-in-office-files-12b036fd-d140-4e74-b45e-16fed1a7e5c6

Next when you first open a workbook on a different computer (and also after upgrading Office)  you will generally get a message re enable editing and you need to action the confirmation.  If you are opening workbooks from other workbooks via VBA code then you will get an error if you have not already enabled editing in the workbook and saved it. Go to each workbook in turn and open it and enable editing and save it and then run your master project.
