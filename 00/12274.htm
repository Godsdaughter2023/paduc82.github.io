ronrosenfeld say:

footnotes multiple citations separator

With multiple citations at the same spot in the text, Word 2007 has no separation between the different footnote numbers.  Do I need to manually add a superscripted comma (or other separator)  at each point?  Or is there some easier method to specify the separator that I am overlooking?
suzanne s. barnhill say:

If you want commas, you need to insert them manually, but there are other options as well.

  1. Instead of adding commas and spaces to 1234 to give 1, 2, 3, 4, you can insert an en dash after 1 or before 4 and then format 2 and 3 as Hidden (this has no effect on the footnotes themselves).
  2. If these are source notes, you could combine the sources into a single footnote, separated by semicolons. You won't want to do this, of course, if you need to refer to any of the sources in a subsequent footnote ("Ibid." or "Op. Cit. n. 4"); since it is more common for subsequent footnotes just to use the author's name and short title, however, this might not be an issue.
