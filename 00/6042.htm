aljon_t say:

Hi Rob,

Yes, in Skype account deletion, it will also delete the Microsoft account associated with it.

If the email address has a different domain (like Yahoo! and Gmail), the email address per se will not be deleted. What will happen is that if you have used the email address to sign up to any Microsoft products (OneDrive, Office and more) then you can no longer access these services since the products are attached with the Microsoft account created using the said email domains.

On the other hand, if your old Skype account is not yet fully updated (merged to any Microsoft account) and the existing email address is not used with any Microsoft products aside from Skype, you can associate it with a newly created Microsoft account (created within the process of merging), then you can delete the other Skype account.

You may check this link for additional details. How do I close my Skype account?

Hope this information helps. Feel free to post back if you have questions and clarifications.

richie weldon - msft say: Hi Matt,

This seems to be an unfortunately incident which you came across.  Normally when a restore happens, it's because the phone got erased/wiped/lost.  In this case, you will put the My Phone software on your phone, sign in, and the data from the Web Portal will be transferred down to your device no problem.

It seems like in your case, connecting your phone to your computer and having the data erased from your device put the phone into a state where the phone's data is more recent than what is on the Web portal.  (Deletion counts as an update.)  When you next synchronize with My Phone, the service will see these changes and think that you purposefully wanted to delete your data.
