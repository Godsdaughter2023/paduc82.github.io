thudgeon say:

Hi there! Not sure if this helps, I ran into a similar problem recently, and it was a big pain. This is how I solved it:

 

I have 5 computers, all signed in with my live account. I foundthat for whatever reason one day all my computers had out of sync favorites (Probably from some mucking I did, who knows). I tried lots of things to resolve the problem, and ended up doing this:

 

1)Backed up all my personal data from my first PC (I use SkyDrive so this was a synch)

 

2)Created a new local account on the first PC, with a username different than my live ID (Charms Bar -> Settings -> Change PC Settings -> Users -> Add User )  (Say my live ID was *** Email address is removed for privacy ***.....   I named the local account Sammy)

 

3)Logged in to said local account and removed Live ID account from the system (Control Panel -> User accounts ->Manage other account -> Select live account ->Remove account) and told it to delete all files

 

4)Rebooted PC and confirmed that live id user folder was no longer present at C:\Users\ 

 

5)Signed in to local account with live id (Charms Bar -> Settings -> Change PC Settings -> Users) This will convert your local account to a live account. Give it a few minutes and it should sync all your settings across. Took about 5 minutes and my favorites snapped back into place.

 

The alternative at this point would be to refresh your PC, but you lose all installed programs, and this wouldn't work for me so I didn't even consider it an option. If you have few installed programs refreshing your PCs would be the ideal way to go. I wish I had a quick fix , but this is what I had to do after a month of trying to resolve it elsewise.

 

I feel as though this has something to do with me merging my account with a domain account, as it worked like a charm up until I did this. In any case this was a pain in the butt, but it solved my issue and it has worked great since.

 

EDIT: You could also first simply try converting the account to a local account in Users, then rebooting, then converting it back to a Microsoft account with the option in users. This did not work for me, but it might be worth a try before you go to further lengths to resolve it.
