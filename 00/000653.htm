In the Network Diagram (to give it its correct title) the two important ones are: Blue is a non-critical task and Red is a critical task.

Black indicates the task that is selected.  If you start from the Gantt chart and  click on a task it becomes highlighted to indicate that is the task you're working on. If you now view the Network Diagram, that same task will be in view and will have a Black background.

As for the White - I've forgotten how that came about!  Could you refresh my memory on where it came from - which article?

As to its use: the Network Diagram is best used during the planning phase to check that the dependencies are correct and to trace the predecessors and successors of a task.  If you truly mean PERT, then that is a crude technique to estimate risk and probability of a task's Duration by using three time estimating.  It is a very imprecise methodology that doesn't carry much weight in estimating risk  nowadays, but it could be better than nothing..  A full description and usage is in my PERT article.
