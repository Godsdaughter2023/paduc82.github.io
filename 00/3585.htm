Hi Steven,

Yes, you're a little of out luck here. Do you remember whether you purchased the Office 2011 Perpetual product, like the Home & Business or Home & Student SKU, or did you purchase an Office 365 Subscription?

If it was an Office 365 Subscription, you should be able to enter your email address and get the machine reactivated. However, if you purchased the Perpetual product, which uses a 25-digit product key, the only way to reactivate would be to find the product key and re-enter it. The product key itself is not saved anywhere on the computer, so you'd need to find the actual card (and it sounds like you no longer have that).

The reason why the Time Capsule restore didn't work here is that when you enter the product key, a license is created, but it's encrypted with the serial number of the hard disk, so if the hard disk has been changed, the license can no longer be decrypted. Hope this helps.

2.) With the blinking text cursor (the little blinking vertical line that shows where you are about to type) placed immediately after the colon with no spaces, go into the Developer tab (if you don't have it enabled, do some Google'ing to enable the Developer tab)

3.) Under "Controls", click on the middle picture in the 3 by 3 block of options (when hovering over, it should say Combo Box Content Control)

5.) Under "Title", type in whatever you want. For this test, just do "Test" to make things simple

6.) Click on the "Choose an item." option under "Display Name" and click "Remove"

7.) Click "Add..."

8.) Under "Display Name:", type in "Test1". It will automatically fill in "Value:" to whatever is in "Display Name:" and this is what we want

9.) Click OK

10.) Repeat this process two more times, adding in "Test2" and "Test3"

Basically, our ticketing system sees @category, and thinks "Okay, anything after the equal sign is the category for this ticket." In our ticketing system, we have categories and subcategories. But, to the system, it's really all lumped together in the "Category" variable. So, if we want a specific ticket to be categorized under Passwords with the subcategory of Windows (this is if someone forgot their password in Windows), the category in the system is Passwords::Windows. Since the system has a preset list of variables for Category, if there is a single difference in what we enter in, it will error out. So, if I hand the system "Passwords:: Windows" instead of "Passwords::Windows", it will not work. And that's exactly what is happening here and why it is such a big deal.

It is also worth noting that the same thing happens in other Microsoft programs, such as Microsoft Outlook. If you want to see this for yourself, follow the steps above all the way to step 14. After step 14, when you have the text copied, open up Microsoft Outlook, start composing a new email, and paste it in. The space is added here as well. Outlook itself does not have (to my knowledge, anyways) a way to create and modify Combo Box Content Controls. However, it does SUPPORT their use. If you store a contact with the international prefix (eg +679) the caller id will NOT show up on an incoming call but you will find that your incoming messages will correctly show the sender ID.

The problem is only going to get worse as they bring out more and more smart phones.
