no.compromise say: From MSDN:
The SCM makes a list of delayed auto-start services during boot and starts them one at a time after the delay has passed, honoring dependencies. There is no specific time guarantee as to when the service will be started. To minimize the impact on the user, the ServiceMain thread for the service is started with THREAD_PRIORITY_LOWEST. Threads that are started by the ServiceMain thread should also be run at a low priority. After the service has reported that it has entered the SERVICE_RUNNING state, the priority of the ServiceMain thread is raised to THREAD_PRIORITY_NORMAL.
What this means, basically, is that delayed start services start after auto-start services, and that the startup of a delayed start service takes place at a lower priority than "normal" priority threads.  To the extent that I'm unable to find documentation on MSDN supporting a mechanism for doing so, it would not appear that this delay is configurable, or that one can "suggest" to the OS some guideline to use in any delay, either globally, or on a per-service basis.

I'm not sure how the discussion migrated from accessing your Road Runner account with the Mail app to accessing the Outlook.com account with the Mail app.

 

The Windows 8 Mail app generally does not use POP to access Outlook.com accounts, it uses Exchange ActiveSync.  You should allow the Mail app to choose for itself the type of conection by selecting an "Outlook" account in the Mail app when setting it up to access your Outlook.com mailbox.  You should not configure the Outlook.com account (i.e., using a web browser and the Options>More mail settings>Your email accounts>Add a send-and-receive account process) to pull your Road Runner mail to your Outlook.com mailbox.  The Windows 8 Mail app will gather the mail from Outlook.com.

 

Because the WIndows 8 Mail app does not allow you to create an arbitrary POP account, I see issues with this, however, mostly because I haven't tried it myself.  My accounts are with services that the Windows 8 Mail app recognizes automatically (i.e., Yahoo, Outlook.com, and Gmail).  The mail issue that I see and can't answer is how you would reply to a Road Runner message and have the recipient see it as coming from Road Runner and not Outlook.com

 

If Road Runner supports IMAP, then I the approach I would take is to access the Road Runner mailbox directly with the Windows 8 Mail app and not pull the mail to the Outlook.com mailbox first.  This will provide two accounts in the Mail app which, in my opinion, is cleaner and easier to control.  Outlook.com's handling of external accounts isn't all that good.  Moreover, it can't handle any account that requires two-factor authentication from what I've been able to discover.
