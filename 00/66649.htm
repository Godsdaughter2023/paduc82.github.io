red264 say:

As the Xbox One is a 1080p device you will want a 1080p monitor, higher resolutions are available such as QHD/1440p aka 2K and true 4K displays, these are probably a waste as the XB1 cannot natively output these resolutions.

The LCD display panels are typically TN and IPS, most TV's are IPS or some kind of similar technology this is what you should aim for with a monitor. IPS offers wide view angles and good colours and reasonable response times, it should be no different from your big LCD TV.

TN is also common on PC it is often marketed at gamers but has huge trade offs, it is really meant for twitch PC gamers (people who play things like Counter Strike), TN has ultra low response times, is cheap but has crappy colours and viewing angles.

HDMI is present on most computer displays these days along with an audio output (3.5mm cable) for speakers. Actual built in speakers are not common and don't expect much in the way of quality.

BenQ are a well known PC display manufacture, checkout their website as it lists all of the features and panel types, plus they are reasonably priced. They have a gaming line but these are all TN, (unless TN is what your after), their home and office sections lists IPS displays, both product lines feature HDMI and speakers.

As to the other option avoid wireless HDMI it is terrible, it has a very short range and is unreliable. HDMI signal repeaters over CAT6 network cable do exist and would be the ideal way to extend the video but this tech does not come cheap and would require network cables being run to each room.

A  far easier option assuming you have a Windows 10 PC is simply use the built in streaming feature of the Xbox app on Windows, however this is not well suited towards online or competitive games due to the lag/delay but for single player games it is good.
