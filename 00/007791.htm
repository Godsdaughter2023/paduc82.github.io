    bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

    Just some additional info for future reference :-);

    If you turn Track Changes Off before copying, that message will not appear & the copy operation will execute. The selected portion of the balloon's content gets copied but is pasted as a Deletion markup balloon in the other document. After you paste you can right-click the markup balloon & select Reject Change from the contextual menu or choose to use the Review features on the Ribbon. The content of the balloon will be inserted into the document as text at the insertion point location just as though it had been tracked as a Deletion from that document.

    Hi Louise,

    Yes, email service and calendar service are integrated in Exchange account. Typically, the Exchange account refers to Office 365 account (work or school account). This is because Exchange Online service only provides to work or school account. You need to subscribe Office 365 business/education plan to get Exchange Online service. 

    Of course, Microsoft personal account, such as Outlook.com, Hotmail.com and Live.com accounts, also belong to Exchange account. But personal account doesn't have Exchange Online service, it only uses Exchange protocol to sync emails, calendars, tasks, contacts and so on. Also it's totally free.

    Both types of accounts support OOF feature. If you don't want to always open Outlook desktop app, you can try to create a Microsoft personal account and sign in Outlook on the web to create an calendar event with OOF status in your account. Then your account will auto-reply if there're incoming emails.
