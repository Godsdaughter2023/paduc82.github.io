I need to lookup values in one column and compare to values in another.  Example:

1                         Ax

1                         Ax

1                         Bx

2                         Cx

2                          Cx

3                         Bx

Try this...

Assume your data is in the range A2:B10.

List the unique store numbers in a rang eof cells, say D2:D5.

Then, enter this array formula** in E2 and copy down to E5:

=SUM(IF(FREQUENCY(IF(A$2:A$10=D2,MATCH(B$2:B$10,B$2:B$10,0)),ROW(B$2:B$10)-ROW(B2)+1),1))

** array formulas need to be entered using the key combination of CTRL,SHIFT,ENTER (not just ENTER). Hold down both the CTRL key and the SHIFT key then hit ENTER.

The reason for this error is because Office SP1 has added a new service: Office Software Protection Plan and will require you to reactivate office. Since the old “tokens.dat” is still registered and the service running, there is no way to fix this unless you follow some steps I did after contacting Microsoft.

Here are the steps you must take:

  1. 1.    type “services.msc” into the run command
  2. 2.    stop the service “Office Software Protection Plan”
  3. 3.    navigate to “C:\Program data\Microsoft\OfficeSoftwareProtectionPlatform”
  4. 4.    rename “tokens.dat” to “tokens.old” (or whatever strikes your fancy)
  5. 5.    navigate to “C:\Program data\Microsoft\OfficeSoftwareProtectionPlatform\Cache”
  6. 6.    rename “cache.dat” to “cache.old” (or whatever strikes your fancy)
  7. 7.    reboot (this will force the “Office Software Protection Plan” service to start again
  8. 8.    open any Microsoft Office application and re-type your activation key
  9. 9.    Office will prompt to to reboot----do so----

10.  re-open any application and activate (Office may ask you to retype the key)

11.  activate online and you are done.

This worked beautifully for me for Office Professional 2010 without having to remove/re-install the application.

a. user say:

1. Shutdown your computer, power it back up and

Press the key you need to get to your BIOS.

Check your BIOS for a setting called any of these below and set it to ENABLED
"Non-Execute Memory Protection"
"Data Execution Prevention"
"No Execute Memory Protection"
"Execute Disable Function" with a sub-menu of  'No-Execute Page Protection Technology."
or similar wording NX execute memory, etc

If you do not have this setting in your bios - ignore it.

2. Write down your BIOS settings, reset BIOS back to default.

3. Change boot order to have these in front.

USB, CDROM, if you have UEFI then also UEFI- USB , UEFI-CDROM
