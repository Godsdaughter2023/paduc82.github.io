scsmith---01 say: Update: Having previously updated the N73's firmware, and not seeing any improvement, the A6 was recently in the garage and received a number of control module firmware updates, and now, in conjunction with the Contact Download app (Nokia_810_Contacts_Download_S60_3rd.sis, i.e. N95 version), have successfully downloaded the phones internal phone book to the A6's MMI phone directory.

AT LAST IT WORKS!!!

I would note that on the first pass, I saw a large number of phone numbers at the head of the directory list (before the names started). This turned out to be contacts I had in Outlook with just a Company name, and no 'person' name. To cure this, I copied the company name into the 'Last' field of the Fullname. After the subsequent sync with the phone, and contact download into the car's MMI, the phone numbers were nicely replaced with the company name.

So the key to getting the N73, and I guess similar Symbian phones, working is as follows...
1) Update the phone's firmware to the latest (available on the Nokia website, or through your local service centre)
2) Get the Audi MMI control modules' firmware updated. In my case, I would think the Bluetooth integration module was the key component. Audi generally do this routinely as part of a service, but no doubt could be done separately. A specific request should see it done.
3) Install the contact download app (filename above) onto the phone, and enable it for auto-start.
4) Disable BT in the phone.
5) Delete the BT pairing between phone and car in both, then re-enable BT in the phone, pair the two (and set up trusted connection status in the phone), and wait for the contacts download app to do its magic.

As I have 550+ contacts in my phonebook, the download took quite some time, so hang on in there! May be a good idea to only have 10-20 contacts in there on the first try.

After connection, the phone app displays "Processing", which then changes to "Transferring Data" whilst the download takes place, returning to "Processing" after completion.

Office 365 Email Retention

I'm using Outlook 2016 and Office 365

anna ma msft say:

Hi Amy,

 

The retention policy will be taken into effect once you apply it to a mailbox.

 

Since you have set a retention policy that let user unable to access to emails older than 1 year, it seems that your retention tag action is to delete allow recover or permanently deleted. If so, and if there isn’t any hold on this mailbox, you may not able to access to the emails that 2 years ago even if you start a new retention policy.

 

But if your organization supports litigation hold, items in hold will never permanently purged, you will find them via e-discovery.
