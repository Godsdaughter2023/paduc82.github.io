larryyu_msft3110 say:

Hi ForrestGump360,

Here are the answers for your questions:

Q1: I'm talking about 120 recipients. Can I put all those addresses in the BCC box and inform them with just one mail by doing this I dont break the 500 recipients rule. But what is the chance of my mail getting flagged as spam? 

A: Yes, you can put all those addresses in the To, Cc and Bcc box in one email. Emails be flagged as spam have many reasons in Office 365. For example, recipients have set up rules, your email address is in the recipients’ block list or recipients manually flag spam emails. If you have mailboxes in Office 365, they are already protected by default with EOP (Exchange Online Protection). Emails sent to Office 365 will be flagged as spam when there are attributes to be recognized as spam by EOP. For other email systems, the reason to flag emails as spam may be different with Office 365. But it’s not supported in our forum, thanks for your understanding.

Q2: If I send a mail to 35 recipients, does it just send 30 messages in minute 1 and then 5 in minute 2? If not, how does this work?

A: The message rate limit only applied to SMTP client submission. The 35 messages will be delivered at once when sending emails out in normal situation.

Q3: If i do what i discribe in question 1. What is the chance of my address being blacklisted?

A: In normal situation, your address won’t be added to the blacklist by sending emails to 120 recipients at once. EOP uses its own proprietary block lists as well as third-party (partner) block lists. Your users might be placed on our block lists for a variety of reasons. For example, your email address appears in a spam message. And not only can individual users be blocked by the service, but specific websites, domains, and IP addresses can be blocked as well. In some cases, domains or websites can be added to a block list because they appear in a spam message, rather than because they’re the sender of the message.

If anything is unclear, please give more detailed information about your concerns.

Thanks,
Larry
