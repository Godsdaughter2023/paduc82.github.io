Why Back up needs to insert a removable media--Without Inserting that - Microsoft Community

padmaaiyer say:

Why Back up needs to insert a removable media--Without Inserting that Can This Back up Did not run completly ?-

techie bear say:

Hello,

What are you backing up your files and folders to? Whatever it is that you are backing up to must be getting full if the backup is asking for the 'next device' to backup the files and folders to. So whether its another hard disk, or tape, or DVD / CD that the computer is backing up to, when the first is full, it searches for the next device to backup to.

 

As to whether it can be done, the answer is dependent on whether or not you have an alternative media to back up to. Such as a second partition on a hard drive, an external hard drive, or a USB flash drive. If you don't have one of those, it defaults to removable media.

 

For example, say you had partitioned your hard drive into a C:\ and D:\ drive, and told Windows Backup to backup your files and folders to the D:\Drive. And further assume that you had only allocated 30 GB to this second partition, of which you were using 15 GB, which you could backup to use 10 GB (with compression). You do one backup and then want to do a second; you've just used 15 GB (original data)+10 GB (1st backup) = 5 GB is all you have left.

 

Whatever backup strategy you follow, you soon use up that remaining 5 GB of space. And once that hard drive is full, the backup program makes the assumption that you want to back up to removable media to finish the job. 

 

I hope this answers your question. Let me know if this take care of the issue.
