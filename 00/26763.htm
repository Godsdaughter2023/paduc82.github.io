dirk goldgar say:

I'm afraid you can't implement conditional formatting this way, because in a continuous form, when you change the properties of a control you change them for all instances of that control, in all the rows displayed on the form.  Only true conditional formatting, as understood by Access, will allow format properties to vary from row to row, and then only the set of properties that are supported.  Through some trickery, you can usually *hide* a control conditionally, but I don't know a way to do what you're looking for.

That doesn't rule out the possibility that someone has found a cunning workaround, and I'll take a moment to look for one, but I don't hold out much hope.

  2. Locate the last one for Excel 2016. 
  3. Save It.
  4. Then upload to a Cloud based Service (OneDrive,DropBox, Google Drive).
  5. Then provide a Link to it here.

  1. Open Disk Utility and Repair Permissions.

  2. Followed by opening Font Book and validating fonts.
    (Removing all older versions of any duplicates — use directions here at Page FontBook Lion and above)

However in EL Capitan (OS X.11.1-3) They have removed Repair Permissions. Reportedly it supposed to be done automatically.  However I believe you can force a Permission repair  by doing this:

  • Quit all Applications.

  • Restart Computer, holding Shift Key down. ( Safe Mode)

  • If your computer reacts like mine Screen goes Black for about 15-30 seconds.

  • The screen returns with Progress meter, allow to continue and Sign in when asked.

  • Now Restart normally.

  • Now Try Excel. (if Excel works your done, if not continue to Next Step.

  • Now use FontBook as Previous described.
    (If works your Done, if not go to these Final steps.)

Note:Do not follow these steps unless all other steps above have not helped.

Unless you know what your doing and have years of experience, you could do damage to your hard drive or, system. Leading to loss of all your files and software.
