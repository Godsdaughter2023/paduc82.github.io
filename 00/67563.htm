In order to locate temporary folder in windows 7, follow the below steps:

  2. Right click on CMD and choose run as Administrator.

ken blake say:

On Mon, 2 Aug 2010 18:52:56 +0000, oogieb wrote:

I would like to scan a document into word and then be able to edit and change it around.  How do I do this?I have Windows 7 and Microsoft Office 2007

The version of Windows or the version of Word don't matter.

When you scan a document, you end up with a picture of the document,
not the text in the document. You can change that picture into text by
using an OCR (Optical Character Recognition) program. Most scanners
come with a "Lite" version of such a software program. For example,
mine came with "OmniPage SE." Yours may also have come with it, or
something similar, and if you look around you may find that you have the CD and just need to install it.
If you don't already have such a program, you'll need to buy one to do
what you want.
Bear in mind that the accuracy of an OCR program depends on two
things:

1. The particular OCR program

2. The quality, condition, and typeface used of the document you are
scanning.

Never expect accuracy to be 100%, and always check the results very
carefully and correct any errors the OCR software made.

naveen_m say:

Hi Leigh,

Thank you for writing to Microsoft Community Forums.

When you remove a hardware or a service, the service or the hardware still exist as ghost devices.

Remove ghost devices:

1.       Click Start, and type ‘Command prompt’.

2.       Right click on it, and select ‘Run as administrator’.

3.       Enter the following command without quotes, and press enter: ‘SET DEVMGR_SHOW_NONPRESENT_DEVICES=1’

4.       Press ‘Windows key + R’ together to open ‘Run command’.

5.       Type ‘devmgmt.msc’ without quotes, and press enter.

6.       Enable ‘Show hidden devices’ in the view menu.

7.       You can see all the devices registered in your system, and you need to right click on those devices one by one to get the remove option. You can remove the devices which shouldn’t be there.

Hope it helps.

Long message...I hope the info is useful:

VOB (as you know) is the DVD Video format.

You cannot convert Video to a photo format like
.jpg and recover your original image files. And...
no...copies of the original source files are not
archived anywhere on the DVD.

One method (and probably not nearly what you
are hoping for) would be to make screenshots
(captures) from the video. The resolution and
sharpness of the captures will not be equal to 
the originals.

The freeware VLC Media Player can play your
VOB files.

VLC Media Player
http://www.videolan.org/

To setup for your screen captures...open VLC and
go to...Tools / Preferences / Video button / Video
Snapshots section.

In the directory field...Browse to and select a folder
to save your captures in. (for example...you could
create a new folder on your desktop and select it)

At...Prefix...check the box...Sequential numbering.

At...Format...choose JPEG or PNG.

Finally.....left click the 'Save' button.

Now...Play the video and pause by pressing your
Spacebar when you see a screen you wish to capture.

To capture the screen...type...  Shift+S

Press spacebar to advance to the next photo...
ect...etc...

One more possibility might be 'Solution B' in the
following article:

'Extract Still Photographs from Wedding DVD Videos
for Printing'
http://labnol.blogspot.com/2007/02/extract-still-photographs-from-wedding.html

Word to the wise...if you use a software that will
extract frames from video...be aware that thousands
of photos may be rendered and you could fill up all
your free space before you realize it.

Good luck...
