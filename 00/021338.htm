Typesetting for Authors - Valuable Opportunity for Word Feature - Microsoft Community

dotyranchmail say:

Typesetting for Authors - Valuable Opportunity for Word Feature Development (Please!)

Hello

This is a 'suggestion box' item for Microsoft Word developers.  You'll see the value from this common scenario

Keywords:  Word, Typeset, Author, Future Editions

Books that have revisions are the key.  Text books, technical books.  The publishing process 'typesets' these.  A very common method is to use Adobe InDesign.  Typesetting makes things 'fit' and makes subtle changes in character/word spacing to polish the look of the printed material.   But here is the issue that Word can fix. Consider the author viewpoint. 

Edition 1.  Author types the manuscript in Word and submits it.  Typesetting converts it into InDesign and typesets the document.  Automatic formatting is stripped out - page numbering, bullets, automatic footnotes, header/footer, all of it.   This is a one way street and is not an issue for a one-time published book, but is an issue for books that are edited in the future.   During the typeset process, some words are changed, some last minute changes are made.  At the end of the process, the document becomes a book and off it goes, but the book is different than the original manuscript.    May be close, but not the same anymore.    Book is published, years go by, time for another edition.  Then comes the quandary.

Edition 2.  Author has the original Word document but it is not the same content as what the book is.  So, there is a choice:   (1) convert the InDesign document to a Word document, such as an RTF file or maybe a Word document.  This is an imperfect conversion, and at best is unformatted or partially formatted, and includes the manually inserted hyphens, manual spaces, etc. requiring considerable manual editing to force the rough conversion into a Word manuscript that is what the book is.   THEN the editing can begin.  For 20 pages this might not be bad, but for 1000 pages it is a barrier.     (2) Edits can also be printed and scissor cut out and taped to a paper version of the existing book.  OK for minor changes, not big changes.  It also requires re-typing which introduces errors (3) New content can be typed (a paragraph, a section) and saved as a small document and a red pen notation on the book saying "insert document xyz here".    All these are awkward workarounds come from the fact that the Word document has been broken as it is being typeset in the typeset software.

Solution is to have a module attached to Word that does the typesetting - or a separate product that leaves the Word document intact, enabling future editions.  Typesetting is an old art and not simple. . Lots of minutia adjustments to typesetting things, and things like InDesign do that now...at the expense of crippling the author's editing processes for future editions.   If Word does this (and does it well), tweaks in the typeset process make a Word version that is an exact copy of what the book is, so that the next edition can gracefully start right where it left off, electronically, in Word.   That is the value to the author.   People who do typesetting for publishers will have a new choice for software.  Publishers will have the choice of letting the author do their own typesetting, reducing publisher costs.    But from an author's viewpoint, my viewpoint, the huge improvement is enabling future editions by not killing the original Word manuscript.  

Differences between a Word document and a typeset InDesign document don't look huge to me, but are hugely important to the publisher, so to be accepted in industry it has to do all the things that typeset software does, like InDesign.    

I'll be your first customer.  Thank you!

Hey Huckleberry724,

 

Let’s take a look at the programs you have running at the time that the problem occurs. You can use a clean boot to load only Microsoft processes to see if this happens because of something within the operating system or because of a third party program. You can do this by:

·         Click Start

·         Type msconfig into the search field

·         Press Enter on your keyboard

·         Click Continue on the User Account Control (If prompted)

·         Click Selective startup

·         Uncheck Load startup items

·         Click the Services tab

·         Check the Hide all Microsoft services box

·         Click Disable all

·         Click OK

·         Click Restart Now

Once your computer has finished restarting, test to see if you still get the same error in Internet Explorer. If not then you just have to identify which service/startup item was causing the problem.

·         Click Start

·         Type msconfig into the search field

·         Press Enter on your keyboard

·         Click Continue on the User Account Control (If prompted)

·         Click the Services tab

·         Check the Hide all Microsoft services box

·         Check (Enable) the top half of the service boxes

·         Click OK

·         Click Restart Now

Once your computer has restarted, again test to see if the problem is still gone. If so, then the services you enabled are good to stay. Repeat the enabling process for half of the remaining disabled services and continue this process until the problem returns or all services are enabled. If the problem returns then you will need to test the last batch of services you enabled one by one to find which was causing the problem to occur.

 

If you enable all services and the problem still hasn’t returned:

·         Click Start

·         Type msconfig into the search field

·         Press Enter on your keyboard

·         Click Continue on the User Account Control (If prompted)

·         Click the Startup tab

·         Check (Enable) the top half of the service boxes

·         Click OK

·         Click Restart Now

Follow the same process as before, with testing after each restart. If the problem hasn’t returned, go back and enable half of the remaining list of startup items and continue the process until the problem has returned. Then you will need to test the last batch of startup items you enabled one by one to find which causes the problem to occur.

 

Once you have identified which service/startup item causes the problem in Internet Explorer you will need to either disable/uninstall/update the program to avoid further problems.

 

Look forward to hearing back from you.
