julie fka profjulie say:

Music and other such things sync to Zune software on a desktop computer, but contacts and appointments sync to data that is stored in the cloud.  This includes Windows Live, Google Contacts, and other cloud-based services.  There is no way to sync a WP7 phone to the contacts and appointments on a desktop computer, so at the very least, you'd need access to WIFI.

Technically, the answer to your question is "Yes," but in reality, the answer to your question is probably "No."  The requirement of a data plan is really up to the carrier.  As you probably already know, most carriers these days require a data plan for smartphones, so if you purchase a smartphone (any smartphone including an iPhone, a Droid or (probably) a Windows Phone) from a carrier, you will most likely be required to also sign up and subscribe to a data plan.  Honestly, the focus of smartphones these days is on connectivity and social networking.  These kinds of things tend to assume an "always on," data plan.  If that's not what you are looking for in a phone, then any one of these phones is probably overkill for you (and I include the Windows Phone in that mix).

There are still WM6.5 and WM6.1 devices for purchase and those devices do sync contacts and appointments to Outlook on a desktop computer.  You might be able to purchase a new or used WM6.x device that is unlocked and would not require signing a new contract with a carrier that would require a data plan.

entegy say:

First, try doing a restart of the phone. Hold the power button down on until the phone asks you to confirm a power off. If it won't ask you to turn off, hold volume down and power for 10-15 seconds until the phone reboots.

When the phone reboots, setup should try to pick up where it left off. If it doesn't let you enter in your verified account, skip sign-on and rush through all the setup until you can get to the Start screen. From there, go Settings>about>reset your phone to factory reset the phone and start setup from scratch.
