COUNTIF - Microsoft Community

gary's student say:

COUNTIF

Is there any difference between

 

=COUNTIF(A1:A2," 1")

and

=COUNTIF(A1:A2,"1")
hansv mvp say:

If the values in the range argument and/or the value argument look like numbers, COUNTIF trims leading and trailing spaces before comparing them.

So your formulas are equivalent to each other and to

=COUNTIF(A1:A2, 1)

 

But

=COUNTIF(A1:A2, " a")

and

=COUNTIF(A1:A2, "a")

are *not* equivalent.

rick rothstein, mvp - excel say:

To follow up on Hans post....

If you had cells formatted as Text and had a mixture of "1" and " 1" in the cells (no quotes, of course) that you wanted to count individually, you can't use either of the formulas you showed nor Hans' formula with just the number; however, if you put an asterisk in front of the text, you can count each separately...

=COUNTIF(A1:A2,"* 1")

=COUNTIF(A1:A2,"*1")

Of course, this comes with a BIG CAUTION... you now have to be careful of numbers ending in "1" or " 1", although as a side benefit, using the asterisk appears to make the formula ignore "real numbers".
