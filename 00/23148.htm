Hi, Pieter,

Thanks for your follow-up reply with the additional details.

Microsoft began prohibiting the use of a commercial e-mail address as a personal account identifier (and in the case of old Skype accounts, no longer allowing a business address to be used as a legacy Skype account's registered e-mail address) due in part to the account-related complications and problems you are now experiencing.

In addition, people lose access to their business e-mail addresses when they move on to a new employer, and the former employer's unavailable and closed e-mail address becomes useless for account recovery purposes.

Account linking and in-linking is no longer available to us, so the answer to your question is, unfortunately, nothing can be done to re-arrange the accounts and subscriptions.  The accounts can not be separated or re-associated.  To avoid these scenarios, now only one Microsoft account can be associated with an e-mail address: if the e-mail address is detected in Microsoft's account systems, it can not be used for anything else.

I would try to contact the Microsoft Account and Office 365 account services teams to see if they might make any recommendations. 

Best regards,

Elaine
