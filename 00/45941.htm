CLose the services App

sc delete wuauserv

Right Pane double click 'Update Assistant'

Do the same for all triggers on that event

Restart your system, that's It!

When finished and after a system restart, open the services app and make sure the Windows Update Service is now gone . . .

a. user say:

When looking at CDs, two abbreviations are important:

R means recordable - you can burn files

RW means re-writable - you can burn files, erase them, and burn other files

So you're looking for CDs that say CD-R and CD-RW on the label. As a practical matter, re-writeable CDs have not proved to be reliable, so if you can limit yourself to CD-R that would be better.

As an FYI: Most people have abandoned CDs because a USB flash drive is: 1) easier to use - no additional software or technical knowledge necessary; 2) less expensive; 3) more portable; and 4) works everywhere.
