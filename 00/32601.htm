To deal with the WindowsApps folder, try this first...

https://www.ghacks.net/2015/06/17/save-windows-10-apps-to-other-drives/

Change Save Locations in Windows 10

Read through it, & post any further question. You should be able to move the WindowsApps folder location to another drive. After that, new apps will automatically go there. Also, you will be able to move already installed apps to it. And you may delete installed apps no longer wanted.

(1) Hold SHIFT, & click Restart.

(2) Click "Troubleshoot > Advanced Options > Command Prompt".

(4) Exit the DOS box by typing EXIT or clicking its X.

(5) Click Continue to exit the recovery environment to Windows.

Warning: RMDIR is a powerful instruction. Do not remove a folder & its contents unless sure it is expendable.

Pretty simple.  Install the OneDrive app from the Play Store on your phone and then 'sync' your music to OneDrive.  Then, on your Surface Pro, wait for about 10 minutes and you will see the music appear in the OneDrive folder.

Hi Mathew,

 

Thank you for updating us.

 

Have you performed the troubleshooting methods in the above post? If the issue persists, try installing the drivers in compatibility mode and check if it resolves the issue.

Try the below mentioned steps and check if it helps:

  1. Go to your manufacturer’s website and download the Driver setup file for your computer.

  2. Press Windows Key + R on your desktop screen to go to run box.

  3. Type devmgmt.msc and hit Enter.

  4. Look for the Drivers, expand, then right click and select Uninstall.

  5. If you are prompted to Confirm, then Confirm.

  6. Go to the location where you had saved your downloaded setup files of the drivers and right click, then select Properties.

  7. Select Compatibility Tab.

  8. Place a check mark next to Run this program in Compatibility mode and select the operating system accordingly from the drop down list.

  9. Let the driver to install and then check the functionality.
