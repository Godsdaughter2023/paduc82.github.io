There is no secure way to do this. If you give the workbook to someone and they want to view the sheet then it's easy to circumvent any measures you put in place to stopm them. However, this may be what you want.

 

ALT+F11 to open VB editor. Double click 'ThisWorkbook' and paste the code below in on the right. Change the name of the sheet to the one you want hidden and the password to your own password.

 

 

Private Sub Workbook_SheetActivate(ByVal Sh As Object)
MySheet = "Sheet1"
If ActiveSheet.Name = MySheet Then
ActiveSheet.Visible = False
    response = InputBox("Enter password to view sheet")
        If response = "MyPass" Then
            Sheets(MySheet).Visible = True
            Application.EnableEvents = False
            Sheets(MySheet).Select
            Application.EnableEvents = True
        End If
End If
Sheets(MySheet).Visible = True
End Sub

mccbleue say:

One question: Do you get a good signal in the areas where you typically spend your days? Your phone has the capability to connect to 2G, 3G and 4G networks and will by default choose the best one it can find.

 

Assuming your SIM card is 4G capable, it will try to find 4G, in the absence of that 3G and then 2G. If it has a weak signal in any particular band, it will drop to the next one down but keep trying to find the higher speed signal; this is called "hunting". If your 4G signal is not strong and keeps dropping, the phone will be using more battery power trying to maintain it and therefore restricting it to a 3G max speed will help to conserve battery life. Likewise, if the 3G signal is poor in a particular area then restricting it to 2G can help.

 

Go to Settings>Mobile+SIM>SIM settings>Highest connection speed and choose the appropriate speed for where you are.

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
I think Jim may have been misled by the use of the term 'clipboard' in the Subject Line of your message but you're referring to the 'pasteboard' :-)

In order to resolve your problem you'll need to return to Pub Layout View. Unfortunately, the program doesn't provide an explicit feature for locating stuff scattered about on the Pasteboard but try this;
  • Zoom to a small value, such as 25%, so you can see all pages. Adjust the Zoom upward from there in small increments to see any tinier elements.
  • Confirm that in the View> Show menu (or the Non-Printing Characters & Outlines are displayed.
  • With the Selection Tool drag diagonally from one corner of the window to the opposing corner
  • Optionally, select an object on any page then repeatedly press Tab to navigate from one to the next
  • Check the Master Pages layer as well
