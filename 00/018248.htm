jack cook - aka help_line say:

Do you want to share a photo or other file with a friend who has a smartphone, laptop, or tablet with them? There are a couple of ways to do it from your phone—one of them is to share it over Bluetooth.

Notes
  • Before you can share files using Bluetooth, you need to pair your phone with the device you want to share with. For more info about pairing, see Pair my phone with a Bluetooth accessory.
  • To share files this way, the other device needs to have Bluetooth and support the Bluetooth Object Push Profile (OPP) too. To find out if the device you’re sharing with supports this, see the documentation for that device.
  • Another way to share files with another Windows Phone 8 is to tap the two phones together and share files using Tap + Send, which uses NFC (near field communication). For more info about sharing files this way, see Tap to share photos, websites, and more.
To share a photo or music file

  1. Pair your phone with another device that has Bluetooth.

  2. Do one of the following:

    • To share a photo, in the App list, tap Photos . Find the photo you want to share, and then tap it.
    • To share a song, in the App list, tap Music + Videos > Music. Find the song you want to share, and then tap it.

  3. Tap More > Share > Bluetooth.

  4. On the Choose Bluetooth device screen, tap the name of the device you want to share with.

  5. On the receiving device, have your friend accept the file.

To share a contact

  1. Pair your phone with another device that has Bluetooth.

  2. In the App list, tap People , and then find and tap the contact you want to share.

  3. Tap More > Share contact > Share > Bluetooth.

  4. On the Choose Bluetooth device screen, tap the name of the device you want to share with.

  5. On the receiving device, have your friend accept the file.

To share a Microsoft Office document

  1. Pair your phone with another device that has Bluetooth.

  2. In the App list, tap Office .

  3. Do one of the following to find the document you want to share:

    • For a document you already opened, flick to Recent.
    • Flick to Places, and then tap the place that the document is in.
    • Tap Search , and then search for the document.

  4. Tap and hold the document, tap Share > Bluetooth.

  5. On the Choose Bluetooth device screen, tap the name of the device you want to share with.

  6. On the receiving device, have your friend accept the file.

mike h.. say:

Hi,

 

For the P entries

 

=SUMIF(A1:A20,"P",B1:B20)

 

 

and G

 

 

=SUMIF(A1:A20,"G",B1:B20)

 

In both cases the P or G is in Col A and we sum Col B.

todtown say: You should probably be more specific about where your monetary totals are located (in a single column, multiple columns, etc) and where you want the sums to go. However, you can simply do a COUNTIF to get the sums. 

So if the "P" range is B2:B100, you can use this formula to get the sum:
=COUNTIF($B$2:$B$100,"p")

For G, just replace "p" with "g"

Notice that the formula does not care the case of p or g.

scottgem say:

From my book:

Option Explicit requires that variables be declared before they can be used. This prevents situations  where a misspelling or typo can cause a procedure to not work properly.

So its not to help performance but to prevent errors popping up. 

Using variables instead of hardcoding object names, gives you flexibility in certain instances.

Using DoCmd.OpenForm "frmMatters", DoCmd.OpenQuery "qryOpenFiles" is fine if the code snippet only deals with that object. But if you need to vary the object, then using a variable name is helpful. For example:

If Me.optAddEdit = 1 Then

    DoCmd.OpenForm "frmMattersEdit"

Else

    DoCmd.Openform "frmMattersAdd"

End if

could be written as:

Dim strForm As String

If Me.optAddEdit = 1 Then

   strForm =  "frmMattersEdit"

Else

    strForm =  "frmMattersAdd"

End if

Docmd.OpenForm strForm

Obviously this example is bare, but if you have a lot of options it can help.
