Hello MamaDragonOwl - I'm sorry to hear you are having these issues. I really don't know if I will be able to help, but if you don't mind, here are a few things to try.

First let me mention that some of the Xbox games on Windows 10 use a slightly different sign-in mechanism than others.  Games like Solitaire, Minecraft Win10 edition, Tomb Raider and Gears of War - as well as the XBox app - use a newer sign-in mechanism. These games share a single Xbox Live profile that you can change by signing out from the Xbox app settings and back in with a different account.  Older Xbox Live games like Mahjong, Jigsaw, Bingo, and others use your "connected account" that you sign into Windows with and do not have the ability to switch accounts via the Xbox app.

First a couple of questions on your situation:

  • Are you signed into your Win10 PC with a "connected account"?
  • You can check this by going to Settings->Accounts
  • If there is an email address under the name and a link that says "Sign in with local account", then that is your connected account.
  • If it says "Local account" under the name with a link to sign in with a microsoft account, then you do not have a connected account 

If you don't have a connected account:

  • Do you mind trying to connect your Microsoft Account that is associated with your Xbox Live account? Do that, uninstall the games, reboot and see if that works
  • If you DO have a connected account (or after you add it):
  • When you sign into the Xbox app or Solitaire, are you using the same account?
  • Go into Xbox app->Settings and you will see the Microsoft account email address you are sign into

 

Here is another set of steps that I found in a post which might be another thing to give a try:

0) Uninstall the games that don't work - Mahjong, Jigsaw, etc.

1) Go in Start==>Settings==>Accounts==>Sign-in Options==> Change Pin

In this step create the pin. Also change the password there.

2) Then click on the "Manage my Microsoft Account" in Settings->Accounts or open any browser and go to account.microsoft.com/about to Sign in your Microsoft Account and use the new password which you have changed .

3) On the next screen, Scroll down to Devices. There you see a symbol of PC below the word 'Devices' and see all in front of the word 'Devices'. Click on 'see all'.

4) There you see your devices. Remove your PC device by clicking Remove PC and wait for 5 minutes to sync with your PC. Make sure that you are well connected to Internet so that the PC will sync properly.

5) After this you go to account in setting to switch back to Local account.

This will get you back to a local account. From there you can launch the help you login to your Local account.

6) Try installing and launching the Mahjong game now (signing into the store app with your Microsoft account associated with your xbox live profile)

7) Mahjong should ask for a user name and password to sign in - again use the MSA with your xbox profile

8) You can go into Settings->Accounts on Windows and re-connect your Microsoft account (and be sure to verify it as well)

Again I do not know if this will work but if you have time and want to try it, please let us know the results.  Thank you

Re-build Windows 7

If you are going to do a re-install, you may be able to take your data off first.  Remove the hard drive and put it into a USB external drive housing.  Connect to a working PC, copy the data off.

Always check to ensure the hard drive you are about to rebuild is actually sound from a hardware perspective.  Download and run your hard drive manufacturer’s drive testing software.

If you had or have Windows 10 installed, you are unlikely to ever get Windows 7 working again satisfactorily unless you do a complete re-install.  In this case, a System Repair disk is not going to help you.

If you have a corrupted Windows 7 system, you are trying to recover, you want to try a System Repair disk to boot the PC.  It may be more successful in fixing and offer other alternatives.  If you do not have one, go to any other PC that has windows 7 with the same bitness (32/64).  Start globe, All Programs, Maintenance.  You need just a blank CD.  When you boot, ensure that you are in fact booting on that CD and not the hard drive.  Usually pressing F12 will lead you to the option to do so.

Best alternative strategies to rebuild are the following in preference order:

  1. Factory restore — replaces everything on the hard drive with exactly what it had on it when you bought it.  You access this before Windows starts using a special key that can varies by brand.  F11 is the most common. This is an option only if the installed hard drive is the original.

You will need a Win7 install disk for 2 or 3.  You will need a legal product code for 3 and the win7 disk must match the edition of the code and its bitness (32 or 64).  If you do not have the original Windows 7 install disk, borrow one from a friend.
