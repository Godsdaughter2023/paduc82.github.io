If you mean what I think, i.e. access the Internet on your N95 using your PC's Internet connection and transferring data over a bluetooth connection, it can't be done.

The N95 can only access the Internet through a wireless hotspot or your mobile operator's packet data service.

The N95 can, however, be used as a modem allowing your PC to access the Internet through it over a USB or bluetooth connection. allenfei_msft say:

Hi Jeffrey,

Thanks for working with us on this issue. I'd like to summarize the case and share the key information here based on the updates from the escalation team.

 

Symptom:

================

Not able to unfollow sites on social page: https://bea****-my.sharepoint.com/personal/jeffrey_bea*****_com/_layouts/15/Social/Sites.aspx

Troubleshooting Summary

================


1. This issue only happens on your tenant.
2. When you clicks the ellipses next to a site and choose "stop following", there is no response when the user click the ellipses.  Meanwhile you have already tried in IE/Edge/Chrome browsers and several computers.
3. In the fiddler trace, since there is no https requests sent out when clicking ellipses, as a result, we can narrow down to the JavaScript issue.

On your situation, the "followed sites" are displayed in web part, its ID is "WPQ5".
In function RenderListView(ctx6, 'WPQ5'), it renders the list view and put it into structure: ContentFollowingHelpers.renderContextRef = renderCtx;
At this moment, the singleton structure ContentFollowingHelpers.renderContextRef contains correct shared sites in it.

However due to you has another weird web part “Followed documents” in the same page, (at this moment, from my test tenant, I am not able to add this web part).
The “followed document” web part`s id is "WPQ6" (Follow interesting documents by going to them and clicking follow.)

As a result, RenderListView runs twice and destroy the singleton structure "ContentFollowingHelpers.renderContextRef.ListData.Row". It makes the structure contains ZERO items.

========================

When clicking the ellipse, due to the singleton structure "ContentFollowingHelpers.renderContextRef.ListData.Row was not correct in the second time running RenderListView, it cannot identify the (row number) in the listdata.row and does not show popup menu for stop following option.

 

Solution

================

Delete the “Followed documents” web part on social page:

1. Please go to https://bea****-my.sharepoint.com/personal/jeffrey_bea*****_com/_layouts/15/Social/Sites.aspx
2. From top right profile picture, “click personalize this page”.
3. In the edit page, remove the ”Followed Documents“ web part. Then save the page.  This should resolve the issue.

It would be appreciated if you could mark the reply as Helpful. In this way, other community members who are running into the same situation can easily find the useful reference. It will be time-saving for all.

Frank
