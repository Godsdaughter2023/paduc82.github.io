diane poremsky mvp (slipstick.com) say:
>> Mail, email accounts, settings, passwords, etc. Everything, the whole thing.

POP mail is in pst file type, typically stored in Documents\Outlook Files. Move the pst files to this folder on the new computer before setting up the profile. Outlook *should* find the pst files automatically. If not, you can easily change the pst the account use.

IMAP and Exchange mail is stored in ost files that can only be opened by the account and profile that created them. If you use the old registry key, they might work, or outlook will clear the data and re-download it. These are stored in %localappdata%\microsoft\outlook unless you have the Windows Store app pre-installed on a new computer.

Email accounts are stored in the registry. It's not recommended to move this registry key as it can corrupt the profile. Profile key is under HKCU\software\Microsoft\Office\16.0\Outlook\Profiles if you want to try.

Most settings are stored in that key path, a few might be in the default data file.

Passwords are saved in the Credential manager - You can't move them AFAIK, at least not without 3rd party software.

>>I am therefore, seriously grumpy.

Unfortunately, the following suggestions are not likely to make you less grumpy but will very likely save you aggravation trying to find an automated process. For the sake of the rest of this response, going to assume that this strictly relates moving Outlook from 2 machines (W7 and W10) to 2 new W10 machines.

There are a number of things that you need to take into account

#1 - Each of the original machines will have a default data file defined for the Outlook profile. The default contact/calendar folders are contained in the "default data file". To see which data file is set as the default, on each machine, go to File --> Account Settings --> Data File tab

#2 - <IF> the default data file is an .ost file associated with an IMAP account, you will need to export the contact/calendar folders that have "This Computer Only" included in the folder description to a PST file

#3 - Since you have multiple POP3 accounts, you'll need to verify whether each account is configured to deliver to a single PST file or each has its own PST file associated with the account.

To verify your POP3 settings, on each source machine from within Outlook, go to File --> Account Settings --> Email tab

Highlight each POP3 account, look at the bottom of the window where the Change Folder is located and take note of which PST file is being used for the delivery of emails to that account

Info regarding which PST files are to be moved can be found in

How to Find & Copy the Right Outlook PST Files
https://www.contactgenie.info/how-to-find-right...

#4 - PST files simply need to be copied and each POP3 account configured on the new machine should be set to use the original PST file as was used on the source machine.

#5 - In terms of IMAP accounts, there is nothing to copy. Just need to configure the account on the target machine at which point the account will sync with the server.

Understanding Outlook PST and OST Data Files
https://www.contactgenie.info/understanding-out...

In terms of rest of it, while configuring 20-30 accounts will take a bit of time, in the long run, it's far better to configure each of those accounts manually (IMHO) letting Outlook set things up properly rather then trying to use shortcuts which in many cases won't work.or cause issues which will require more time in trouble-shooting then it would have taken to set things up manually,

I am joining the chorus of those having the "disappearing sections" in existing notebooks problem. This always happens in the Microsoft OneNote 2010 application (under Windows 7). My affected OneNote notebooks are based in OneDrive; local network based notebooks have never been affected.

The more I experience this "disappearing sections" problem the more I believe it is all about (problems with ) Syncing with OneDrive.

Here is a summary of my experiences with "disappearing OneNote sections" so far ...

  • The disappearing sections problem happened to me the first time about two months ago
    • Since then I have learned enough about this problem not to have a heart attack when it does happen ... my notes are still OK on OneNote Online <whew!>
  • Since the first occurrence OneNote 2010 was stable until about two weeks ago (near the end of May, 2017)
  • When I open OneNote 2010 my notebooks have all their sections
  • A few seconds later all sections in OneDrive based notebooks disappear with the, now familiar, "There are no sections open in this notebook or section group." message, along with the terrifying offer to "Click to create a new section" (no way!!)
    • Yes, the sections in the open notebook disappear before my eyes! :(

How I have recovered notebook sections ...

  • Force an open, but now empty, notebook to sync <-- Successful about 20% of the time
    • It is truly annoying about 50% of the time when the sync appears to work quickly, but the notebook remains empty!
  • Close and open the notebook that became empty <-- Successful about 20% of the time
    • About 10% of the time the Open Notebook dialogue indicates difficulty connecting with OneDrive, and also a button for "Try Again", which never fixes the problem (so far)
      • Only solution then is to close and reopen the OneNote 2010 application

    • The points below did NOT usually let me open the Notebook in OneNote 2010 (but it did a couple of times (??))
      • Open OneNote Online > Open a notebook > Select "Open In OneDrive" in the browser task bar <-- Worked 100% of the time so far
      • Select "Open in OneDrive" must be done right after the notebook opens in the browser
        • If you select a section first, only that section will open in the OneNote application
  • One time, of the many section recoveries I have done in OneNote 2010, the sections of a reopened notebook came back in a different order than they had been saved in (!)

  • Force a manual backup of the OneNote application at least once per day ... and ...

The successful sync timeout seems to be too short. OneNote seems to give up on the notebook sync nearly immediately. Perhaps something has changed in the network that a sync to OneDrive takes longer than it did a month ago.

a. user say:

Hello there TarkanBulan! Thanks for posting your query on Microsoft Community.

About your concern, if your phone stops working or responding like it used to, restart it to get back on track.

To restart, press and hold the Power button until a Slide down to power off message appears on-screen, then swipe down. (It usually takes about three seconds for the message to appear.) To turn your phone back on, press the Power button.

If that doesn't work, press and hold the Volume down and Power buttons at the same time until you feel a vibration (about 10 to 15 seconds), then release the buttons. Your phone will restart automatically.


Please be reminded that:

Your phone may vibrate as soon as you press the Volume down and Power buttons because it's changing your ringtone to vibrate. Wait to release the buttons until you feel the second vibration 10 to 15 seconds later.

If you don't let go of the buttons immediately after the vibration at 10 to 15 seconds, your phone may show a black startup screen instead of restarting normally. If that happens, try pressing the button combo again.

Not all phones restart this way, so check with your phone's manufacturer to find out how to force a restart.


If your phone has a replaceable battery (it's often under your phone's back panel), you can also restart your phone by removing and reinserting the battery, and then pressing the Power button to turn on your phone. The date and time may be off after you restart this way, but you can fix that by swiping over to the All apps list from Start, then selecting Settings > Time & language > Date & time.

Feel free to reach us again for further assistance.
