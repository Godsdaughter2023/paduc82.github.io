1. What is a Microsoft Account and what does it do?
Your Microsoft account holds your basic information (name, address, sign in & password) along with your security proofs. This allows you to log in, access and verify your activities on all Microsoft products and platforms. It’s all in one place – account.live.com!

What if I can't remember my Microsoft Account information?
Oh no! Let's see what we can do to try to get that information back in your noggin. Head on over to xbox.com/forgot and try out the tools there to recover that information. 

 
 

2. What are Security Proofs?
Security proofs are a system of two factor authentication that keep your account safe and control your account or activities on Xbox.com, your Xbox 360 console, Xbox Live, Windows and anywhere your Microsoft Account is used! Proofs will only ever be used to ensure the protection of your account!

Are security proofs required?
Yes, they are required, but they should be wanted as well! Security proofs keep your account in your hands. It is required that you have at least two verified proofs:

    • SMS phone number (or a voice phone number)
    • Alternate email address


3. What is Two-Step Verification?
Two-Step Verification is an extra layer of security for those who feel like their Microsoft Account may be at risk (popular Gamertags, celebrities, etc). But what if I'm not <insert popular and relevant actor/actress' name here>? Am I still able to use Two-Step Verification? Of course! Anyone and everyone is welcome to add this extra layer of security! Check out our Two-Step Verification support article for more information on how to get started.

  1. Click here to access your security proofs

  3. Type in your computer name

6. What if I can't access my proofs?
If your Trusted PC isn't set (or is out-of-date), and your phone number and alternate email address are both out-of-date and/or inaccessible, then you'll have to go through the procedure to reset your account proofs. Bear in mind, if just one of your proofs are outdated, you can delete it, confirm it with your valid proof, and add a new one easily, as per the steps in part 2. For more information on what to do, check out our Can't Access Security Proofs support article.
