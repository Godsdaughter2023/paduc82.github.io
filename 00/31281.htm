kittymeatloaf say:

OK, I hope this helps other people who are looking for the menu item "View Scanners and Cameras" so easily found in Windows 7, but no longer found in Windows 10.

1. Pin the Control Panel icon to the taskbar

2. Right click the Control Panel icon on the taskbar

3. See the context menu pop up. At the top see "View Scanners and Cameras"

4. Click on View Scanners and Cameras

5. Click on scanner device

6. Click on properties button for that device

7. Click "Yes" button on User Account Control

8. Click on Network Setting tab

9. Enter the IP address of your MFC printer or other Printer/Scanner combo device.

If you are using a Brother MFC printer, I suggest you uninstall the Windows 7 drivers before your upgrade to Windows 10. Reinstall the Windows 10 driver (if available) or the Windows 8 driver. It will make your printer work much better than the already installed Windows 7 driver installed pre-upgrade. The reason I suggest doing it before the Win10 upgrade is because I was unable to completely uninstall those Win7 drivers after my Win10 upgrade. It's interesting to note, however, that the IP problem I was having with my scanner was not resolved with the Win8 driver upgrade (Win10 driver was not available yet for my Brother MFC 8480DN).
