daniel g. grau say:
It's also the behavior of just about all Mac software, including Apple's. If you compare the ruler in TextEdit with the ruler in Word, you'll see they are identical.

The size inconsistency among platforms dates back to the 80's, then Microsoft decided that 72 typographic points per inch (the first Mac's pixel standard) were not enough to ensure a proper rendition of smaller-size characters (and rightly so) and went for 96 pixels per inch, which made most PC displays show images 1/3 larger than actual size.

As screen resolution increases, dot pitch shrinks. iMacs now have up to 109 pixels per inch and MacBooks up to 132. However, computers are still rendering images at 1 typographic point per pixel, resulting in ever-reducing "screen inches."

Rather than estimate, grab a ruler and measure the page size on screen to decide what zoom percentage you need to display it at 100%.

ossiemac say:

Do you mean that cell E8 has the current Day of Month and E9 has the Current Month? If so, then in the CF dialog use the following formula to apply to a specific cell.

Depending on whether you wish to apply the CF to a range of cells, you might need to apply some absolute referencing but at this stage I will leave that to you.

Real dates are required to test the comparisons and therefore the formula requires a year. Provided the same year is used throughout then that will not make a difference to the result where you only want to compare month and day. So that the same year is used throughout, the formula applies the current year to the dates. (You could just use any valid year instead of YEAR(TODAY()).

It then uses E9 and E8 as the Month and Day and applies the current year to create a real date. It then uses months 8 and 9 together with day 15 combined with the current year to create another real date.

=AND(DATE(YEAR(TODAY()),E9,E8)>=DATE(YEAR(TODAY()),8,15),DATE(YEAR(TODAY()),E9,E8)<=DATE(YEAR(TODAY()),9,15))
