jesse vaught say:

just to understand some constraints:

  1. HDMI is electrically compatible with DVI
  2. DP outputs from most PCs have an HDMI compatibility mode that allows for:
    • DP-to-HDMI passive adapters [passive means it just uses wiring (no ICs) between the connectors]
    • DP-to-DVI passive adapters
  3. In contrast, the USB Type-C output on the Lumia 950XL/950 phone and the DP output on the Display Dock do not have HDMI compatibility mode.  To drive HDMI, DVI (or VGA), they require active adapters incorporating bridge ICs.  The tipoff is usually that such an adapter costs $20-$40 instead of ~$10 for a passive adapter.  There’s no good way to tell active versus passive other than by part # and spec.

abi99 (2) say:

Apple offers a straight USB-C to VGA adapter (Model A1620; MJ1L2ZM).

Just to follow-up: Works here for my Lumia 950—both for Continuum for Phones or just Mirroring (former ‘Project my screen’). Image is sharp and clear. However, all I got was a 640 × 480 resolution, the standard VGA resolution. Same with you?

By the way, the Apple USB-C Digital AV Multiport Adapter (HDMI; Model A1621; MJ1K2ZM) works as well. With that, I get a Full HD resolution, of course. For both, no power adapter is required, which creates a small and handy alternative to the bulky Microsoft Display Dock (HD-500). Furthermore, both Apple adapters offer a standard USB port for legacy devices, enabling USB-OTG. Finally, when a power adapter is attached, even the USB port is externally powered then, allowing USB devices which need higher current.

Do Recycle Bins need to be added as a source in Search configurations?

Same question for both SharePoint Online and OneDrive for Business.

jerryduan_msft8021 say:

Hi OnpointSP.com

No. The Recycle Bin in a site collection will not be put on hold. Any restore or delete action in the recycle bin will not produce influence on the preservation hold library. Also, when we create a Discovery Set, we only specify a SharePoint site. eDiscovery places a copy of the item in a specific document library, called the Preservation Hold Library, on the SharePoint site where the content was modified. We are not able to search and put a recycle bin on hold.

For OneDrive for Business library, we put the personal site on hold, so the Preservation Hold Library will be created on the personal site’s site content.

Though Ashish's solution is more versatile as it can be scaled to any number of conditions but if you want to use a quick solution as number of conditions are less in your case -

=IF(B2<=30,"Under 30 Days",IF(AND(B2>30,B2<=60),"30-60 Days",IF(AND(B2>60,B2<=90),"60-90 Days","Over 90 Days")))

The above formula assumes that Column B contains your aging which is today()-Item Receipt Date. I assume that you have already calculated this.
