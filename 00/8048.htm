100, 000 characters is a lot. The Notes sections was meant to contain short notes. You can always tweak the registry on the  device to accommodate your notes. By default, Notes are configured to contain 5k worth of data. Increasing that amount will make more room available at the expense of less memory on the device. Just follow the steps below to increase the memory allocated to the Notes field:

Download a registry editor to your device from http://www.phm.lu/Products/PocketPC/RegEdit/.

Then check the following value:

Hkey_Current_User\Software\Microsoft\Activesync\BodyTruncation\

Set the value for BodyTruncation to 20480 (Decimal) or more to fit your 100,000 characters.

I strongly recommend you reduce the amount of data you put in there though.

jaynewton say:

Yes.  This is a common and known issue between Windows Phone 8 devices and Ford Sync.  It has to do with the Bluetooth profiles.  Sync supports AVRCP 1.3 and WP8 supports 1.4.  There's a miscommunication between the devices once a call connects where Sync does not know where the audio should go so it defaults to Privacy Mode on Sync and sends the audio back to the device.

 

There is a lot of finger pointing between Ford and Microsoft.  I would contact both and complain. 

 

THIS IS NOT DEVICE SPECIFIC!  Don't contact your carrier or device manufacturer.  They don't know what to do and no fixes they propose will work.

 

You can mitigate the issue by not using Sync to initiate the call.  Use the built-in voice commands from WP8 to start the call and Sync will respond properly.  You can also turn off Privacy Mode on a call-by-call basis using the menu prompts in Sync.  Finally, you can hit the Bluetooth button twice on the phone to send the audio back to Sync.

 

I hope this helps a little.
