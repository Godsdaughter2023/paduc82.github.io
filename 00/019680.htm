BECAUSE!!! ALL!!! IPv6!!! ADDRESSES!!! ARE!!! UNIQUE!!! AND!!! PUBLIC!!! [unless you deliberately restrict them to your own LAN somehow, but then WHY bother?]

That is because IPv6 DOES! NOT! NEED! NAT!!!  Everybody gets a PUBLIC IP address.  Get it?

And WINDOWS MACHINES on a PUBLIC IP ADDRESS are VERY VERY VERY VULNERABLE!

Denying that by burying your head in the sand or putting hands over ears and "la la la I'm in my own world" does NOT change the FACTS that it is VERY bad to have a WINDOWS machine with a PUBLIC IP ADDRESS.  Or, in this case, IPv6.

Keep in mind that this is a REAL problem.  Does anybody remember 'Win Nuke'? That's the vulnerability on either UDP port 135 or 139 [I forget which].  It didn't matter if you disabled SMB networking entirely, or just on public IP addresses.  The OS _STILL_ listened to 0.0.0.0:135 and 0.0.0.0:139 and was VULNERABLE to having rogue UDP packets sent at it.  Malform the packet, send it to the windows box with the public IP address, and BOOM!  Instant BLUE SCREEN OF DEATH!

hansv mvp say:

Let's say you have a formula

=VLOOKUP(A1,tbl401,3,FALSE)

and you entered the formula ="tbl"&... in cell K1

You can change your VLOOKUP formula to

=VLOOKUP(A1,INDIRECT(K1),3,FALSE)

jessen p say:

Hi Kandula,

Occasionally, you’ll get an error message telling you that  Internet Explorer won't open a website because of an insecure connection to the site's server. This happens when  Internet Explorer encounters a security protocol that is insecure or no longer supported. This type of a connection will make your PC vulnerable to attacks by allowing data sent between you and the website to be intercepted. Internet Explorer 11 now blocks insecure connections to help protect your PC and personal data.

Open your Internet Explorer browser then go to Internet Options.

2.  Click on Security tab.

3.  Click on Trusted sites, then click the Sites button.

4.  On the "Add this website to the zone:" URL bar, type the complete URL address.

5.  Click on Add button.

6.  Once you already added all URL addresses, click the Close button.

7.  Click on Apply or OK button then close your Internet Explorer browser.
