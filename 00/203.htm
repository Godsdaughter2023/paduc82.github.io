How can i protect some specific cell but not whole sheet - Microsoft Community

manish singh 104 say:

How can i protect some specific cell but not whole sheet

How can i protect some specific cell but not whole sheet ?
trip_to_tokyo say:

 

EXCEL 2007

Take the following actions assuming that the range that you wish to, "lock" is D 83 to D 85.

1. Go into you Worksheet and click on cell to the left of column A and above the number 1.

This should highlight the whole Worksheet.

2. Ctrl + 1 (at the same time) / Custom Lists pane should launch (sometimes this window has a different name because there is a bug in the software here) / go to the Protection tab / remove the tick (check) from the Locked field and hit OK.

3. Now go back to your Worksheet and highlight cells D83 to D 85 only / Ctrl + 1 again / Custom Lists should launch / go to Protection tab again and place a tick (check) in the Locked field / OK.

4. Home tab / Cells group / Format / Protect Sheet / Protect Sheet window should launch / put a password of your choice in / hit OK / put password in again / OK.

5. Now to test that it has worked go back to cells D 83 to D 85.

If you try to enter info into any of those 3 cells you will not be able to but you will be able to see the formulas, for example, there.

6. You should be able to enter data into all other cells in the Worksheet.

julie fka profjulie say: The only files that can be moved to a windows phone via a direct USB connection to the computer are media files (pictures, videos, and music).  You'll need to install the Zune software to your computer to sync those files to your computer.  See this link to learn more about syncing these kinds of files to your phone.

You can sync office files to your windows phone from your SkyDrive account.  You will need to upload the files from your computer to your SkyDrive account, then they will be there to sync to your phone.  See this link to learn more about syncing Office documents to your phone.  If you are using Office 2010 on your computer, it's pretty easy to save these files directly to your SkyDrive account - see this video to learn more about how to do this. (this functionality alone was enough to get me to upgrade from Office 2007 to Office 2010 - it's some really great functionality and not just for windows phone.  It just makes it so easy to keep files in sync with SkyDrive, which I've used for lots of purposes over the last several years).

You can use third party software, like Home Pipe or Tonido, to browse your computer from your phone and copy over the files you want on the phone.

There is no file management software for windows phone.  Documents are stored within "sandboxes" based on what kind of file it is.  For instance, all Office files are stored in and accessible through the Office Hub, photos are stored in and accessible through the Photos Hub, music is stored in and accessible through the Zune Hub.  PDF files are stored in and accessible through the Adobe Reader "hub/sandbox" (you'd open up the Adobe Reader application and then you'd see all the PDF files that are stored on your phone).

There are other ways to transfer files to your phone (emailing the files to yourself, downloading from DropBox or some other cloud-based service) and some related apps that facilitate moving files between the cloud-based service and the phone (for instance, there are several 3rd party Dropbox clients in the Marketplace)
