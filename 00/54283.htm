canadian tech say:

You should understand that shutting down Windows using the ON button is a risky behaviour.  When you do that, you prevent Windows from doing a proper shutdown.  There is a chance that this will cause corruption on the hard drive.  Each time you do this, you increase the probability that this will happen.  Eventually leading to a system that will have to be re-installed.

If you are getting system shutdowns, that means there is something wrong that you will need to get fixed urgently.  It is likely a hardware problem and the most common cause is over-heating.

If yours is a laptop, make certain you do not use it when it is on a soft surface.  A laptop breathes by pulling fresh air into the tiny vents on the bottom of your computer.  

If yours is a desktop design, you need to properly shut down, remove power and check the fans.  They may be clogged with dust and you will need to carefully blow that out, or one or more of those fans may have or is in the process of failing.
