Does anyone understand Microsoft's chaotic approach to Document Properties and Metadata?

Word 2010 has a Document Information Panel "InfoPath Form" that displays a basic set of seven default document properties...Author, Title, Subject, Keywords, Category, Status and Comments.

However, if the Word Doc is in Sharepoint, you can, through a bewildering set of arcane steps, rename six of the seven document properties and then if you create a set of seven lists, and create matching columns in your sharepoint document library, and point the colums to the lists, you can get most of these document property fields in Word to have dropdown menus, allowing point and click assignment of metadata to a word.doc.

But you can't get rid of, nor re-purpose, the Title document property, and you can't add other properties.  Well, actually, you can add others, but they won't work. Except, you may be able to do this if you buy InfoPath editing program for $250 and spend a couple of weeks figuring out how THAT works.  This appears, from what I read on the Internet, to also allow you to create more document property fields, through another arcane series of unfathomable steps.

Linking accounts

1.Sign In to your account page using your Microsoft account and password.
2.Click “Permissions” located at the left side of the page.
3.Under permission, select "Managed linked accounts".
4.Click “Add linked account” under Manage linked accounts.
5.Verify your current information by entering the currently signed in account password.
6.Enter the Microsoft account and password that you want to link.
7.Click the "Link" button.

Note: You can link up to five (5) accounts for 1 year and once you have reached the limit, the system will not allow you to add anymore.

Picture in header shrinks in PDF - Microsoft Community

conlormar say:

Picture in header shrinks in PDF

I have several Excel spreadsheets (separate wookbooks), each with a picture in the header and footer, which reach from margin to margin.  That part is all fine.  But when I print the spreadsheet in PDF format, the header and footer both shrink and no longer stretch from margin to margin.  Why?  How do I fix this?

Thanks.  Connie

conlormar say:

I have found the answer through other technical support and will share it here for anyone who may be interested.  The problem is with Excel 2003.  Unlike the newer versions, i.e. 2007, 2010, etc., it has no "scale with document" option where you can remove the check mark.  So, the work-around is this:  in Excel, go to page setup>Header/Footer>Customer Header>Click in the picture section>Click on the Format picture icon>Scale height and width to 100%>OK>OK>OK. Do the same for the footer.  This is a nuisance if you have many Excel spreadsheets to be continually converting to PDF, but you can create a macro to do it for you by the click of a button, which is what I'm going to do until we upgrade Excel.

adam vero say:

The key sequence Brad gave works for me and I've been using it for many years.

The copy operation is the same, just Ctrl-C, then the second part (after choosing your destination) is essentially the old key sequence to get to this through the old menus. Alt+E (together or Alt then E) would start the "Edit" menu then S open the Paste Special dialogue, then V checks the "Values" box, then Enter to OK this. (I also like using U instead of V for "values and number formats" at the same time, while still losing formulas, colours, borders etc).

I have noticed an annoying behaviour with this though, that might be considered a bug. If you do the copy in one workbook and use the keyboard to switch to another open workbook (such as Alt+Tab), this kills the copy operation and Alt+E,S,V does not work. You need to switch form one to the other using the mouse (via taskbar or if they are open side by side).

It also fails if you try to use it between two instances of Excel running as separate processes.
