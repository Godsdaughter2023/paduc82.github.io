tedfay say:

I had the same problem. After extensive testing I have a solution.

 

1.) Do not use M4A or WAV files, and stay away from MP3s. Only WMA files seem to work reliably. You MUST embed the album art into the WMV or MP3 file and then unistall and reinstall XBOX Music for it to pick up the missing album art.  Immediatley turn off the option to grab missing metadata or artwork from the internet as soon as you reinstall XBOX Music.

 

Also, make sure the artwork is 200x200, no larger. If an album gives you trouble after all of these steps, try coverting it to a different format liek uncompressed WAV and then converting it to WMV, to strip out whatever Music is choking on.

 

WMP works well to embed the album art.  Copy the 200x200 album art into the clipboard (CTRL-C) and then right click on the ablum art in WMP and select "Paste Album Art." It should then pop up a window that says  "Working" and embed the art.  Make sure to first delete any other .jpg files in the folder to make sure you don't have conflicting artwork. For MP3's you may need to use tagging software to delete any embedded artwork that might be larger than 200x200 or have mutliple artwork images.

Here are the step that have worked for me.

Option 1:

1) Use only WMA files, with any custom art being 200x200 album art pasted into WMP.

2) Delete the all tracks in the album from Windows Media Player (from the library, NOT from you hard disk)

3) MOVE them to another directory

4) delete them from XBOX Music

5) Then move them back to their original directory

6) Add the tracks back into WMP

7) Watch them show up into XBOX Music with their correct album art.

 

Option#2

If you have a LOT of track missing artwork, this option may save you some time.

1.) Use only WMA files, with any custom art being 200x200 album art pasted into WMP. (Transcode the files if you have to, stripping out any extraneous or larger artwork than 200x200.

2.) UNISTALL XBOX MUSIC

3.) Go to the App Store - swipe from the bottom of the screeen (or right click at the bottom of the screen) and select "Your Apps"

4.) REINSTALL XBOX MUSIC

5.) Go back to the START Screen. Watch for when XBOX Music moves from "Pending" to "Installing" and then completes.

6.) As soon as it completes, open XBOX Music and then open the charms bar, selecte preferences and turn off "Automatically Retrieve and Updata Album Art and Metadata."

 

The above combination of steps seems to work very well, and I now have custom album art in XBOX Music.

You can download Windows 7 Retail and OEM System Builders ISO from Microsoft.

But, NO, they do NOT contain all the updates since you obtained your DVD.

In other words it is not up to date with security Updates at the time you download it.

Microsoft does not slipstream monthly updates into its ISO download.
