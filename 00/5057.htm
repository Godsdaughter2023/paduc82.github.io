karl timmermans [outlook mvp 2012-2018] say:

The Trim function will remove any leading and trailing <space> characters - a space character has an Ascii value of 32. As soon as it finds any value other then 32 - the trim function terminates for the applicable side. Without going through all your code in detail, simply verify the contents of your variable on a character by character basis. Just because something <appears> blank doesn't mean that it is a space character.

How can I copy headers onto a new document - Microsoft Community

loriubell say:

How can I copy headers onto a new document

I want to copy pages 62-68 from an existing document from a new one, and retain the original header (showing title and page numbers 62-68.) When I do copy/paste, I get the text but not the headers. I can recreate the headers but I'd rather not, as this is something I need to do frequently.
suzanne s. barnhill say:

Headers and footers are stored in the last paragraph of a single-section document and in the second break at the end of the section in a multi-section document. If you include the section break or last paragraph in your selection, the headers will come along.

See http://word.mvps.org/FAQs/Formatting/PasteWithoutSectionInfo.htm, which describes how to do the opposite of what you want; do the opposite of what is suggested!
