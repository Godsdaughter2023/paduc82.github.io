lemp say:

1.  linksys is the default SSID for many Cisco/Linksys routers.  You could be connected to yours or you could be connected to someone else's.

2.  Now that you have pushed that button, you'll have to completely reconfigure your router.  One of the things that pushing the button did was to reset the security (so that now the network supported by your router is unsecured).  Resetting the router also reset the SSID to its default (see #1). 

3.  What sort of Internet service do you have:  cable or DSL? 

3a.  One of the ports on the back of your EA4500 is colored yellow and labeled "Internet."  There should be an Ethernet cable connected to that port.  What is the make and model of the device at the other end of that cable?

If you are connecting using a cable modem (rather than a DSL modem), you probably will not need to configure the portion of your router that connects to the Internet (as compared to the portion of the router that connects to your computers).  The router defaults are usually correct for connecting to most cable modems.  I will assume that this is your situation unless you tell me differently.

Cisco/Linksys routers have the ability to be configured remotely using an online "Linksys SmartWiFi" account.  If you set this up when you first installed your router, you can use this method to reconfigure your router.  Or, if you can find the CD that came with your router, you can use it to set up your router as you initially did.

However, rather than using the online account or starting over from scratch, here's an easier way.  This procedure is described starting at page 15 of your User Guide (http://downloads.linksys.com/downloads/userguide/EA-Series_UG_Full_3425-00125D_EN_FR-CA_Web.pdf).

1.  Disconnect the Ethernet cable from the router's Internet port and the modem at the other end.  Connect one end of the cable to one of the router's 4 blue Ethernet ports and the other end of the cable to your computer (if your computer is already connected to your router with an Ethernet port, just disconnect the cable from the router's Internet port and leave it aside until your configuration process is completed).

2.   Open a web browser on your computer and go to www.linksyssmartwifi.com (when there is no Internet connection -- because you removed the cable to the "Internet" port -- this address should go directly to your router).

3.  You should see a login box.  If there is a space for "user" enter admin.  The password is also admin.  You will want to change this in steps 7 and following.

4.  Under "Router settings" click "Wireless"

5.  Click the "Wireless" tab, then click "Edit"

6.  Enter an SSID (network name).  Use something that's unique to you but not your name or address. 

Enter a password.  Use a strong password -- at least 12 characters, not a word, mix of upper case/lower case/numbers/symbols.  Write it down and keep the paper in a secure location that you will remember. 

Under "Security mode" select "WPA 2 Personal" if that's not already selected.

Click OK to save your changes. 

7.  Under "Router settings" click "Connectivity"

8.  Click the "Basic" tab

9.  Select your correct time zone from the drop-down list and click OK

10.  Go back to the "Basic" tab (if necessary) and type in a new password under "Router password" and click OK.  Be sure to write this down also.  I suggest taping this password to the bottom of your router.

11.  Close your web browser, reconnect the Ethernet cable between your router's "Internet" port and your cable or DSL modem and disconnect any Ethernet cable between your router and your computer.

12.  You should now be able to see your new network name (SSID) in the list of available networks.  Enter your new password, check the box to automatically connect, and click the "Connect" button.

13.  Follow the directions in Canadian Tech's Dec 13 post to remove from the list saved in your computer all wireless networks that you do not recognize, especially any networks named "linksys"
