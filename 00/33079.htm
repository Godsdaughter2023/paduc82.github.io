I didn't do that separately. I did a backup of the windows 8.1 system to a USB drive using the File History settings in the Modern control panel (Go to start screen, charms, Settings, Change PC settings,  Update and recovery, File History.) Select a destination drive, turn it on, and click [Back up now]

NOTE: Although I have made sure I had a backup I never needed it.

 Consider this. If you physically remove your hard drive and replace it with a brand new blank drive, install windows 8, log in with your MS account, and wait long enough, All your tiles, apps, and settings are restored, even your lock screen customizations. What does not get restored is the X86(desktop) programs and their data. So If you are using something old, written for XP, Vista or 7, that stores your contacts on your HDD, you will not have them. If your contacts show up in the tiles or on office 365 ( AND YOU USE A LIVE MS ACCOUNT LOGIN, not a local login) All your contacts, unread mail, Drafts, etc. should be there. Notice, I said should. That is why I make a backup. My experience so far has been good. I bought my second brand new Windows 8 tablet, loged in with may MS live (Hotmail) account and viola, the next morning the new tablet seemed to be an exact copy of the first one. Even the wallpaper, colors, screen saver and lock screen. I'm not saying you should skip making a backup, just that, at least once, it worked for me. I feel much better about what I will do if I ever lose my tablet or have it stolen.

I'm not at all sure this is even required to make a backup. You have everything in the cloud and on another PC. The main worry is OneDrive.

Where some people get into trouble is as follows:

Deleting OneDrive folders:

If you delete the OneDrive folder, or anything in it, thinking you are going to put it on another drive, OneDrive will propagate the deletions to the cloud and then back to your other PC(s). Deletions can propagate real fast. Recovery can be done through File History in most cases, but if the free space on your HDD is not considerably larger than the size of your OneDrive folder, you may not have some files in File History. (Look up Shadow copy on Microsoft to understand why.)

Avoiding the Propagation of deleted files and folders.

Do EITHER (1) create or know another admin account and password, then check the user directory AFTER your account has been removed. (be aware that some files in OneDrive might still be there if they were not already synced to the cloud)

- or -

(2) Go to OneDrive settings while in the account you plan to remove from the PC and Unlink OneDrive. Delete all your OneDrive files and folders. I would then immediately remove your account from the tablet before rebooting. This seems safe but I can't guarantee this. There is the possibility that another user on the tablet might be able to restore the folder and see your files.

Yes. There is a lot to think about when it comes to OneDrive and the data for legacy (desktop) programs. Like all older file synchronization programs going back to windows 3.1, the propagation of "delete", "rename" and "move" can be a problem.

Be careful. and good luck.
