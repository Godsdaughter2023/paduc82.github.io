Roxanna,

Edited - MS have published a fix for this specific problem -

Always prompted to specify Internet Explorer KB3094186

- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -

Your Edge file association settings are correct.  They are not the cause of the problem. 

The http/https entries are those that govern the behaviour of urls when you click on them - so they are unchecked here and presumably checked in the corresponding dialog for Internet explorer.  You don't show that dialog but I assume you have checked it.

The final two entries, the ones that are checked, are special protocols for other types of link that are created by Edge itself and can only be used within Edge [the last one, for example, you'll see if you tell Edge to save a webpage for reading].  You don't have to worry about these entries.

Your tests have proved that Outlook is the problem.  I did a comparison test and my Outlook opens urls without any dialog box appearing [Outlook 2007 & Windows 10 on one computer and Outlook 2016 Preview & Windows 10 on another].

Have you tried Office diagnostics [if that utility exists for Office 2010]?  Start menu, All apps, Microsoft Office, Microsoft Office tools, Microsoft Office diagnostics.  You might also be able to get there in one of the other Office applications - Office button, Options, Resources, Diagnose.

I suggest that you search the Outlook 2010 section of the forum and then, if necessary, post your question there.
