brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

> In a next few weeks will my Hotmail account through Hotmail Connector be stopped?

When your account transitions from the current Outlook.com platform to the Office 365 platform (what is now the "Outlook Mail Preview"), your Outlook Connector account will stop working. When that happens, simply switch from the Outlook Connector account to an Exchange account. You'll have access to your data just as you did through the Connector. Exchange is a full two-way sync account.

Note: Outlook 2003 won't be able to use an Exchange account. For that version of Outlook, you'll have to connect to http://people.live.com/, export your contacts, them import them locally in Outlook 2003 or, while the Outlook Connector still works, copy the contacts to a local Contacts folder in a PST data file. The same applies to the Calendar. You can configure an IMAP account in Outlook 2003, but that syncs only mail, not non-mail folders like Calendar and Contacts.

Let this formula be defined as ~

=column(index(b14:NC14,match(~,index(............

ANSWERED:

Ashish Mathur and E for Excel both provided working solutions to my problem. 

The difference between their two formulas is that Ashish's formula returns the column number relative to col A, while E's formula returns the column number relative to the first column specified in the formula, in my case column B, such that Ashish's formula returns a column number of 32 and E's formula returns a column number of 31. Both work and can easily be manipulated to the user's needs by an addition of a plus or minus 1 at the end of the respective formulas.
