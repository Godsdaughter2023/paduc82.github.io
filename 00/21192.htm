ardn say: Very simply way:

Click START.
In 'Search programs and files' type regedit.exe and press enter.
Agree to any UAC pop ups, Regedit opens.
In the navigation frame, expand HKEY_CLASSES_ROOT
Scroll down until you locate .ppt and click on it.
(alternative: press CTRL+F to open a find dialogue and search for .ppt)
In the data pane you have a value named 'Default'. Double click on it under the Name column.
In the 'value data' field enter PowerPoint.Show.12 and click okay.
The file association for .ppt files is now PowerPoint 2010.

To apply this process to all the old office files, look for the .extensionX variant, find the default value, copy it and then paste it into the relevant .extension (no X).
