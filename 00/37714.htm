Because I had lost my boot disk, I purchased Windows 8.1 and began installation on a new hard drive, which was 1/2 TB larger than the old drive (2TB vs. 1.5 on old drive).  One of the first screens, in the lower left, provides the option to Repair your system.  Clicking on this leads to choices, among them, Restore from System Image.  I selected that, the system found my image on the external Passport USB Drive, and we were away to the races.  "Races" is a poor choice of words, because the copy of data took more than nine hours.  At the end of that period, I entered a strange neverland of (and I am paraphrasing) "Windows Needs to Restart," which would lead to a reinitialization of the restoration process, which would in turn lead to a "Windows Needs to Restore," etc.  I tried various configurations of USB ports, USB drive powered after startup/before startup, etc.  Nothing worked.  Finally I was able to select "Advanced" under the Restore options, and found an option to "Repair Windows Startup Problems."

I selected this.  All of the action, based on sounds and blinking lights, seemed to be taking place on the Passport drive, where the image had been stored.  After a few minutes of churning, a new message appeared: "Windows Can't Fix the Problems."  Subsequently, I reset everything, and decided to go through another Recovery, starting over from boot up, select "Repair" and then wait out another nine hours.  After a few minutes of watching "Windows is Preparing To Restore," the process terminated, and a message announced that there was now a problem with my image.

Local "experts" have opined that because the image was from a time when I had Win 8 instead of 8.1, that could be a problem.  This seems silly.  The new HDD is .5 TB larger than the old, trashed HDD, so that would rule out that problem.  One clue: I have since gotten my system running with another new HDD with Win 8.1 installed, no attempt made to recover from Image.  Hooking up the original "new," HDD, the one that wouldn't accept the image recovery, using an external enclosure through USB reveals Win 8.1 thinks it was never formatted, and has "an unrecognized file structure."  Hmmm...I wonder what was happening for all those nine hours, with that little green progress bar marching across the screen?

I've given up restoring from image.  In fact, based on all the posts with problems, I'm never going to rely on Windows Recovery again for an image (Acronis here I come!).  The need to recover from image occurs so infrequently you must be 100% sure the method you choose will work, and in my case, it didn't.

cmdrkeene say:

Sure thing, just go to the PC Settings app, choose Accounts, and click the "Disconnect" option that you see.  It will ask you for what you want your local username/password to be.

Just keep in mind that you will lose all these Windows 8 abilities:

  1. You will no longer have your settings backed up to your online account.
  2. You will no longer be able to re-download your settings to a new computer if your computer is lost, stolen, or broken.
  3. You will no longer be able to re-download your settings if you ever factory-reset your computer.
  4. Your settings will no longer synchronize between your computers/phones/tablets.
  5. You will no longer be able to get new apps from the Store.
  6. You will no longer be able to use OneDrive online file storage.
  7. You will no longer be able to use the email, calendar, or address book apps.

You can, of course, still use whatever email/calendar program you used in older versions of Windows, that still work just fine.  You just can't use the new stuff without a Microsoft Account.

Hi Robert, please make the following changes to your Windows 10 PC(s) you should then be able to see each device on your network . . .

Click your Start Button, type Services and hit Enter

When the Services app opens, scroll down to find the following services, then double click each one in turn, set their startup type to Automatic and start the service if necessary

3. SSDP Discovery

4. UPnP Device Host

Save that and close the Services app
