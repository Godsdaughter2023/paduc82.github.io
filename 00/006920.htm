Or if this applies only to a few slides, or you need different footer text on each slide, you could add a rectangle over the entire slide, give it no line and 100% transparent fill, send it to back, then add your footer and then send that to back.  

Now you have your footer (clearly visible) but atop it there's a full-slide rectangle.  When the user clicks the footer, they'll select the rectangle instead.  Obviously, this will only be mildly frustrating ... they could easily just delete the rectangle, or select the footer in other ways.  

And another thought:

Add the footer, select it, copy it, then Paste Special, as PNG.  Now you have a PICTURE of your footer text instead of an editable text box.  Delete the original footer, move the picture into place and people can't edit it.  They can still delete it altogether.  

Nothing's perfect.  Sigh.
