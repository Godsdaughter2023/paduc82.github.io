How do I set up windows mail to send only.

How do I change settings in windows live mail so I can " send only."  I do not want to download emails from the web based server.  I could do this in Outlook.  But, I cannot find the settings/preferences to do this with live mail.

 Thank you.

¡firedog say:

There is no Send all command in WLMail. Whether this was an oversight, or whether it's because some mail servers insist on a POP session before allowing an SMTP one, I don't know.

You can work around it thus:

  1. Right-click on the account name in the folder pane and select Properties.
  2. On the General tab, deselect Include this account when receiving mail or synchronizing.
  3. On the Servers tab, change the incoming mail (POP3) server to a bogus one (a.b.com, say).

You might see an error message each time you send, because WLMail will try to initiate a POP3 session at the same time. You can just dismiss it.

How do I sign an on screen signature? - Microsoft Community

lavayuc say:

How do I sign an on screen signature?

I'm filling out an online application and it asks for me to sign my name and initials on the online touch screen. how do i do that?  thanks
voun tia say:

Hi Lavay,

The Surface Pen lets you sign documents directly on your screen and quickly save your signature to them. To do this, simply open a PDF in the built-in Reader app. Locate the place in the document you want to sign, and sign it directly on your screen using the Surface Pen.

When you save it, the Reader app will include your signature and anything else you’ve written on the document. You can then email the saved document, or in this case, finish the online application.

We hope this address your concern, and if you have any other questions, please let us know.


Regards.
