brad yundt say:

If you concatenate the text columns together that need to be searched, then you can use a fairly simple formula to get your sum. Windows Excel 2016 added a nifty function TEXTJOIN that will do the concatenation easily with a formula like:

=TEXTJOIN("|",TRUE,A2:K2)

This formula puts a pipe character between the value in each cell (except blanks) in A2:K2. Mac Excel will eventually get TEXTJOIN, but until then you can use a much uglier formula like:

=A2 & "|" & C2 & "|" & D2 & "|" & E2 & "|" & F2 & "|" & G2 & "|" & H2 & "|" & I2 & "|" & J2 & "|" & K2

The reason for the pipe characters is to prevent the possibility of mistakenly matching a name using text from the end of one cell' value and beginning of the next.

I put the concatenation formula in column L. In column M, I put the names that you want to search for. In column O, I put this formula to perform the desired row by row test and summing of values:

=SUMPRODUCT(B$2:B$25*ISNUMBER(SEARCH($M2,L$2:L$25)))

Microsoft Excel MVP Jacob Hilderbrand suggested a formula that didn't need the auxiliary column, but which gets lengthy quickly if you need to search names in a number of different columns:

=SUMPRODUCT(--(ISNUMBER(SEARCH($M2,$C$2:$C$25,1))+ISNUMBER(SEARCH($M2,$A$2:$A$25,1))>0)*$B$2:$B$25)

Sample workbook showing the above formulas is at: https://1drv.ms/x/s!AoHfCiwUeT98jHJO5m24keas35Uz
