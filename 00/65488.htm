jaymo1978 say:

The issue you mentioned with the hard drive is a concern - as Mal mentioned, when the Xbox is in sleep mode, it is constantly monitoring for updates, and if it happens to be installing one of these updates when you move it, the hard drive will be spinning and installing the content being downloaded.

Moving it with a disc in the drive is a more pressing concern, but if you're worried about the hard drive spinning while the console is asleep, it most definitely has the potential to be spinning, even though it's sleeping. That's one of the reasons I mentioned sleep mode is still not powered down, because various components are still either moving or in stasis/ready mode. (Laptops are a bit of a different animal, as those components are meant to be mobile, resistant to movement and shock, even though the components are still functioning while asleep. The Xbox isn't designed to be mobile, so the danger would be the same as moving your PC tower while it's asleep - things are still working, you're just using less energy.)

It's only good practice to do updates manually, a lot like the 360 had.

jaymo1978 say:

As far as my understanding of it goes, the background updates do not "finalize" until they've been verified through a process of checks with the Live servers. Once the installation is verified, the update is official and sticks. If something happens where the download, install, or verification are interrupted, it wouldn't brick the hard drive, but rather, would revert back to the status of the machine pre-update. The updates can run, get interrupted, and pick up again when the machine is back on and/or in sleep mode.
