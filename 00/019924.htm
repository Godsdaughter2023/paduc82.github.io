SELECT SiteID, Sitename FROM t_Sites;

A bound column of 1, a Column Count of 2 and Column Widths of 0",1". The Controlsource should be empty and the control should be locked and disabled.

Then the RowSource for your Analyst combo would be:

SELECT AnalystID, ITSDA_Lastname & ", " & ITSDA_Firstname, SiteID

FROM t_ITSDAnalysts_AO

ORDER BY ITSDA_Lastname, ITSDA_Firstname;

Also a bound column of 1, but a Column Count of 3 and Column Widths of 0",2";0"

Then in the After Update event of the combo put:

Me.cboSite = Me.cboAnalyst.Column(2)

Put the same code in the On Current event of the form.

Of course you need to use your actual control names. 

If you follow those instructions exactly (except for using your actual object names), then when you select an analyst, the Site name should be display.

On another note, your checklist is not designed well. A lot of Yes/No fields are not good design. Your checklist should comprise at least 3 tables. Second would be t_AuditDetail with AuditDetailID as the Autonumber PK, AuditID as a FK, DetailItemID as an FK and a Results field. Finally you need a lookup table for each Item like t_DetailItem.

One way round this is to upload all your pictures separately to your Webspace.  Then edit your .htm document to replace all your pictures just with links to their Webspace address.  To do this for each picture, select the picture, click on Picture Tools/FORMAT, then "Change Picture", then "From a file". Note that, if this works for you, be careful about deleting any unnecessary "_files" folders as this may well cause your nice new .htm files to be deleted as well!  To avoid this unhelpful effect, give them a different filename when saving.
