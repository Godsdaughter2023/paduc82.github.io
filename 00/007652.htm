  • Select the first cell or contiguous range of cells that you want to unlock.
  • Hold down the Ctrl key and select the next cell or contiguous range of cells that you want to unlock.

  • When you have selected all cells you want to unlock, press Ctrl+1.

  • Clear the Locked check box.
  • Click OK.

First, for monthly compounding, FV(9%/12,36,0,-100000) is about 130864.54, not 130650.00.

Of course, the likely explanation is:  Bernard did not really use 9%.

Instead, he probably computed the monthly rate that would yield 130605 after 36 months, then multiplied by 12.  That is:  RATE(36,0,-100000,130605)*12 [1], which results in about 8.933332%.

And that is the correct thing to do, IMHO.

However, it appears that Bernard used the same annualized rate for all subsequent calculations.  That is inconsistent, IMHO.

Instead, I would compute different annualized periodic rates based on the same assumption, namely:  130605 is the "total return" (principal plus interest) after compounding for each periodic frequency.

Moreover, for daily compounding, Bernard calculated the number of days by 36*31.  That is incorrect, IMHO.

I believe the correct calculation is ROUND(365*B16/12,0) in general, or simply 365*3 in this case, since 36 months is 3 years. [2]

Thus, I would calculate the following:

  1. Quarterly
    Annualize rate in B8:  =4*RATE(B9/3,0,-100000,130605), which is about 9.000000%.
    FV in B10:  =FV(B8/4,B9/3,0,-100000), which is about 130605.00.
     
  2. Daily
    Annualized rate in B15:  =365*RATE(ROUND(365*B16/12,0),0,-100000,130605), which is about 8.901329%
    FV in B17:  =FV(B15/365,ROUND(365*B16/12,0),0,-100000), which is about 130605.00.


Of course, all three result in about 130605, as they should.


The point is:  we know that the bank calculated 30605 interest over 36 months.  The unknown is:  what was the compounding frequency and the (average) periodic interest rate that the bank used?


The answer is:  Use the RATE function as demonstrated above. [1]


Then, to answer bharatparmar's last question (apparently):  once we know those two unknowns, we can use the FV function to calculate the "total return" after 36 months as Bernard demonstrated.


-----


Caveat:  We might also note that the annualized rates determined here might not be the actual annualized rates that the bank used.  There are two methods of annualizing periodic rates.


The method demonstrated above is to report the simple annual rate (called interest rate or APR in the US).


The other method is to report the compounded annual rate (called yield or APY in the US).  In that case, we would compute the same APY in all three cases, namely:  =RATE(36/12,0,-100000,130605), which is about 9.308332%.


But in that case, we would derive the periodic rate differently in the FV function, to wit:  FV(RATE(12,0,-1,1+B2),...), FV(RATE(4,0,-1,1+B9),...) and FV(RATE(365,0,-1,1+B15),...) for the monthly, quarterly and daily examples respectively.


Thus, the result would be about the same.


In contrast, as Bernard might have intended to demonstrate, for the same simple annualized interest rate, the "total return" would be different depending on the compounding frequency.


But that only applies to regions where banks report (advertise) a simple annual interest rate. [3]

5. Click Safe senders.

7. Click Add to list>>.

2. In the upper-right of the window, click the Options icon (gear icon).

3. Click More mail settings.

4. Under Customizing Outlook, click Rules for sorting new messages.

5. Search and select the rules that pertains to the said email address (if there's any).

6. Click Delete.

There's possibility that it has been accidentally send on your account. If the unwanted mails are coming from a certain or specific domain, you can block their domains. Kindly follow the steps below to block their email address or domain:

1. Sign in to your Hotmail account.

2. Click Options located at the upper-right side of the page.

3. Select More Options.

4. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.

5. Click Blocked senders. 

6. Enter the email address or domain on the box labeled Blocked email address or domain: and click Add to list >>
