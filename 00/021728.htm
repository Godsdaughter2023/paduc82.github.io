With a little fiddling, you could probably cut the number of buttons in half by making them toggles.

Click ALL and it looks at the first shape to see if it's visible; if so, it hides it and all the other shapes.  If not, it makes all shapes visible.  Click SLIDE and more or less the same logic applies.

a. user say:

Hello Xenas:

Thank you for posting to the forum regarding the issues you are having with your phone being recognized by your computer.  This type of issue can be caused by many things, an issue on the computer, an issue with the phone itself, a bad digital certificate, a bad sync cable and more.  There are a couple things I would like you to try to determine where this issue lies.

The first thing I would suggest you do is connect this phone to another computer with the Zune software.  If the phone is recognized here we know the issue is NOT the phone but instead an issue on the computer or an issue with the digital certificate.

Regardless of what you find the next thing I would like you to do is perform the steps in this support article, http://support.microsoft.com/kb/2468307 .  If you have any issues completing these steps please detail for us the issues you run into and at which stage in the process you get the error.  The steps from the article above are detailed below.

TRUST THE ZUNE CERTIFICATE

  1. Close the Zune software and disconnect your phone from your computer.
  2. Open Certificate Manager. To do this, click Start, type certmgr.msc in the Start search bar, and then press your Enter key.

    Windows 8 users:     On the Start screen, type Certificate, tap or click Settings in the right column, and then tap or click Manage computer certificates.
  3. In the pop-up window, tap or click the Personal folder in the left column, and then double tap or double click the Certificates folder in the right column.
  4. Click the heading that says Issued By to sort the column, and then look for one or more certificates that read "zune-tuner://windowsphone/...".
  5. Right-click the certificate file and click Copy. If there is more than one file, hold the Ctrl key, click each file, then right-click a file and click Copy.
  6. Tap or click the Trusted Root Certification Authorities folder in the left column, right-click the Certificates folder, and then click Paste.
  7. Click Yes to install this certificate.

Create a new certificate

  1. Close the Zune software and disconnect your phone from your computer.
  2. Open Certificate Manager, if it is not already open. To do this, click Start, type certmgr.msc in the Start search bar, and then press your Enter key.

    Windows 8 users:     On the Start screen, type Certificates, tap or click Settings in the right column, and then tap or click Manage computer certificates.
  3. In the window, tap or click the Personal folder in the left column, and then double tap or double click the Certificates folder in the right column.
  4. Click the heading that says Issued By to sort the column, and then look for one or more certificates that read "zune-tuner://windowsphone/...".
  5. Right-click each certificate file, click Delete, and then click Yes.
  6. Tap or click File in the menu, then Exit.
  7. Click Start and in the search box enter %userprofile%\Application Data\Microsoft\Crypto\RSA, and then press Enter.

    Windows 8 users:     Right-click in the lower left-hand corner of the screen and tap or click Run, or type Windows Key + R, and then type the string above.

    Note For some enterprise customers, the string will be %userprofile%\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Crypto instead.
  8. Find the folder that begins with "S-1-5-21-".
  9. Right-click the folder and then click Rename. Rename the folder so that it ends in .bak. This means you will likely be adding ".bak" to the end of the number.

    Note If there is more than one folder that begins with "S-1-5-21-", try this procedure with one folder at a time. If it does not work, rename the folder to the original name and try the next folder.
  10. As soon as you're done, open your Zune software and connect your phone to your computer. This creates a new certificate and folder.

If you continue to have connection issues, please go to device manager and check for error codes.  You can access device manager by clicking start > right clicking computer > selecting manage > device manager.  The phone should be listed as a portable device, unknown device or other device.  When you click on the phone and go to properties, you should see an error code or a message that indicates the phone is working properly.

Please let us know if you have any other questions or concerns.

Hello Xenas:

Thank you for the follow up.  The fact that no personal certificates are being found tends to indicate an issue with the computer allowing the creation of certificates or an issue with the phone communicating with the computer so they can be added. 

With that said the very first thing we would suggest you do is connect this phone with this cable to other computers.  I would suggest doing this with at least two other computers.  The purpose of this is to verify that you have the same experience in each place.  If you discover that any of the other computers recognize this phone we can rule out the issue being the phone or the cable.

The next thing I would suggest you do is try connecting with other cables.  I would do this on both the primary computer and the secondary computers. 

If you continue to have issues after doing all of the above the issue is most likely the phone itself.  What you would want to do if this is determined is factory reset the phone.  Keep in mind that this will set the phone back to it's factory settings.  Contacts and calendar events should be backed up to the cloud.  You can verify this by going to http://contacts.live.com and http://calendars.live.com .  The information on your phone should be listed here.  Text message history and game progress will be reset.  As your phone is not being recognized by Zune to sync media, there will be loss of pictures, music and videos on the phone.  To reset the phone, tap settings > about > reset your phone.

If you discover that the phone is recognized on other computers, the issue is something on the computer that is preventing the certificates from being created and the phone from being recognized.  There are several things that can lead to this.  A couple things you might consider doing on the computer are as follows.

  • Try a clean boot of your PC.  You will find the process of doing this here, http://support.microsoft.com/kb/929135 .
  • The next thing to consider is related to Antivirus, Spyware and Malware running on your computer.  I have seen several reports of these softwares interrupting Zune's ability to recognize devices.  What you want to do to see if this might be at the heart of the issue is to disable any of these programs running on your computer, reboot the computer and try again.
  • Finally you can do a scan of your computer's system files as something may have become corrupted.  Please review the following document and run this program, http://support.microsoft.com/kb/929833 .  Be aware that this can take quite some time, I have seen reports of up to several hours.  This may be something you want to do while preparing and eating dinner or overnight while you sleep.  In some cases when issues are found the tool will fix them itself or suggest fixes for you to try.

Please let us know if you have any other questions or concerns we can assist you with.

Mark W.
