Re: validation to prevent duplications

It wasn't easy to figure out.  The following seems to work...

  Enter the Validation formula in the first cell (only) of the range (A1 to A10 in this example)
    =COUNTIF($A$1:$A$10,A1)=1
  Note that an absolute reference to the range is used ($x$x)
  Copy the first cell
  Select  the other cells in the range (A2:A10)
  Go to Paste Special and choose "Validation"
  Should now only complain if a duplicate entry is made.

As a general rule, Word uses the header that applies to the first section on a page and the footer that applies to the last, but, although I had not thought about it before, I think you're correct in your surmise that Word considers only Next Page (including Odd Page and Even Page) section breaks and not Continuous breaks when invoking the First Page Header.
