great white north say:

Hi adamvv023:

What is the make and model of your computer? Every manufacturer has a slightly different method for performing a factory reset.

Please be aware that there is a known issue with Vista SP2 computers where Windows Update can hang on "Checking for updates..." for several hours (or even days) and cause serious performance issues - see the FAQ How to Fix Vista SP2 "Checking for Updates..." Hangs and Slow Windows Updates in the VistaForums.  If you reset your computer back to factory condition you will have to restore all your personal files from a backup, remove all the outdated third-party software installed at the factory that is no longer supported (e.g., old trial versions of antivirus software like McAfee, Norton, etc.), update all your hardware drivers and reinstall your third-party software.  Once you reinstall the SP1 and SP2 service packs for your operating system you will find that Windows Update will begin to hang on "Checking for updates..." and prevent you from patching Vista SP2 with several years worth of missing security updates until that problem is fixed.

If you are having issues with your Vista SP2 computer post back with the details.  It might be much easier for you to fix that problem than to get your computer working again after a factory reset.
