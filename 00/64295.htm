considerate_guy say:

The .wlmp is your project file.  A project contains no actual video, photos or music since those are still on your computer's hard disk. The project is like the recipe for building your movie and just has the names of the source files along with information on where you want to apply edits, etc.  But like a recipe, you can't consume it.

When you want to assemble your final movie, that's when you use 'Save Movie' and that creates the movie.  As you discovered, a movie, with all the video, photos and music actually incorporated, is much bigger.  This is the file (.wmv format) that you can then view on your computer (Windows Media Player) or uploaded to a sharing site (e.g. YouTube) or possibly emailed.

However, as you have noted, video takes up a lot of space and is hard to email. If you reduce the size and quality to make something fit into an email, you are going to cause things to look blurrier.

The best solution would be to upload your movie to a sharing site (YouTube, Vimeo, Facebook, SkyDrive, etc.) and then just email people the link to view it themselves. Emailing video is not a good solution except perhaps for very, very short clips.
