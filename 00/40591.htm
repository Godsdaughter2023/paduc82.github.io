This sounds like a defective ink cartridge, or a defective sensor; I've had it happen a couple of times over the years. Probably it's not related to the actual Windows OS. The fact that it printed after a Restore might just be coincidence.

However, the very simplest thing to try would be to fully shut down the printer, let it sit for a minute or two and then turn it on again. It could be due to a memory error and shutting it off should clear the internal memory.

Lastly: You might try to uninstall the printer and driver and then reinstall it with whatever driver is most recent.
