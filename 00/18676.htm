Hello Oracle117,

 

We understand that it is frustrating on your part not to be able to receive email messages from your email account. There are multiple reasons why an account is not receiving emails, these are:

 

A. Filter Settings of the account

 

To ensure that your account filter settings are not causing incoming email not to be delivered to your account, I suggest that you visit this link: I am not receiving Email sent by friends/family

 

B. Inbox is full.

 

There are some user not receiving emails due to there is no enough space from the Inbox. Free account initial storage limit is 5GB and 10GB for premium account. If you've exceeded the initial storage limit, please try to get rid of unnecessary email to free up some space from your account.

 

C. Mail Client Settings

 

User might not also send or receive emails due to incorrect configuration when accessing account using Mail Client. For this reason, please reconfigure your account by following the settings of the link below:

 

Send and receive Hotmail from an email app

 

D. 3rd Party Email account

 

If you are using University email account such as (.edu) or other 3rd party account, it is possible that there is an issue with your network/server. You might need to contact your domain administrator for further assistance. They may have changed some settings in your domain's system that's why you are having problems receiving emails.

 

If the above steps provided did not resolve your issue, I would like to ask additional details regarding your concern:

 

    - Are you trying to access your account using a web browser or a Mail Client such as Windows Live Mail?

    - Have you tried to access your account using another browser or computer?   

    - Have you tried to send a test mail to your own email address? Did you receive the test email?

 

I recommend that you clear your browser's cache and temporary internet files to make sure that they are not causing your issue. This is often caused by a corrupted file in the browser. Please clear your browser's cookies by following the instructions on this link.

 

Lastly, please let us know the affected email address so we could investigate the issue on your account.
