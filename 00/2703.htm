hansv mvp say:

Starting two instances of Excel will increase memory usage, but with most contemporary computers that shouldn't be much of a problem.

Opening workbooks in two instances means that copying and pasting between the workbooks is like copying and pasting between two different applications. You lose the special features that Excel offers when you copy within the same instance. Paste Special will have a limited set of options.

If that doesn't matter to you, running two instances is attractive because you'll have two complete and independent Excel windows. Otherwise, arranging the windows within one instance of Excel is the better option.
