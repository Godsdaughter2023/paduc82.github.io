karl timmermans [outlook mvp 2012-2018] say:

--------------------------------
For the benefit of all those that may read this who are neither familiar with or inclined to read RFC documents of any kind - a very quick summary of the 55 pages in RFC5322 as it relates to your original question........

A valid email address can be composed of two parts

SendersDisplayName <smtpaddress> where SMTP address format is (spaces added between parts to avoid triggering email address removal for privacy reasons)

  -- <username @ somedomainname .  tld (tld = top level domain). -  or in a more familiar format  - <johnd @ somdomainname . com >

so a valid complete RC5322 address would be

"John Doe" < johnd @ somedomainname .com>

The minimum requirement for a valid RFC5322 email address is the SMTPAddress component - the prefix display username is optional
--------------------------------

What gets displayed is based on the email client - nothing to do with whether or not it's part of a proprietary client/server system such as Exchange or Lotus etc.

Using the full valid email address that can be transmitted - any email client will make a choice as to what gets displayed which is any one of three options

1) Senders Display Name only (if one is included in the message sent)
2) Senders Display Name and SMTP email address
3) SMTP email address only

Anyone of the three options above are valid as per RFC5322 - there is nothing that "mandates" a standard for what any given client chooses to use of the 3 options above when displaying values in TO / CC / BCC.

In the case of Outlook - it uses option #1 and if underlined - means that it is resolved to valid SMTP email address.

RE: "I can't believe that I am the only person irritated by this....................willing to stand up and say ...................."

A really strange thing about the human race - for some unexplicable incomprehensible reason, just can't get 100% of the people to agree on much of anything 100% of the time. No matter what choice is made - ends up that there is <ALWAYS> a dissenting point of view. As an aside, there are a great many people that do NOT want the SMTP address to appear (and are not aware that even when it isn't - it can always be easily viewed via the message header). What would you say to someone who doesn't agree with your preferred choice? (Rhetorical question - not looking for a response since nothing will be gained with an extended discussion on which preference is <better>)

Since there is no option in Outlook as to which one of the 3 valid formats should be used when displaying this info (and have never seen such an option in any email client regardless of who provides it), solution to how to get everything to appear the way you want...........

In FROM address - simply inform whomever you get email from not to include a "SendersDisplayName" in their email address and Outlook will only show the SMTP address.

In To/CC/Bcc fields - totally under your control when using Outlook - change each contact's Email Display Name to only be comprised of the SMTP address.

As Charles says, it could be that an add-in is preventing the Normal template from being saved (an example is PDF Complete, preinstalled on many HP computers some years ago). Make sure o have "Prompt before saving Normal template" enabled at File | Options | Advanced: Save. If you don't get the prompt when you quit Word after creating an AutoText entry, then this is likely the problem.

The best approach, of course, is to identify the interfering add-in and, if possible, disable it. If that's not an option, there are two steps you can take to save the Normal template deliberately.

For some purposes, it is convenient to be able to open the Normal template for editing. Because it can be difficult to find (it's in a hidden folder), we often suggest a shortcut:

  1. Alt+F11; the Visual Basic Editor opens.

  2. Ctrl+G to activate the Immediate window.


  3.      NormalTemplate.OpenAsDocument

    and press Enter.

    it. If the changes you have made have not been recognized as changes (the Undo button is not activated), press Spacebar, then Backspace to “dirty” the file, thus ensuring that the template will actually be saved.
