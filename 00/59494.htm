    arminatorx say:

    I have a Xbox One X an don't see pixelated textures or double subtitles when I play on my video projector. 

    However, I know this effect from the TV upstairs, if I forgot to turn the image enhancements off. 

    A lot of TVs try to improve the live TV image by artificially increasing the refresh rate (e.g. 120 Hz or 600Hz or something like that), or enhancing the image sharpness and the likes.

    These image enhancements work well on "natural" images from a video broadcast. 

    However the sharp edges from a computer generated image "confuse" these image enhancers, and cause blurry textures and ghosting in subtitles, when the graphics behind the lettering move a lot. 

    Try to check your TV's user manual if it has a "direct pixel" or "Gaming" mode, and turn that on.
