I solved STRICT NAT with 4 XB1&#39;s on one Comcast - Microsoft Community

a libra diva313 say:

I solved STRICT NAT with 4 XB1&#39;s on one Comcast gateway! Yaaaaaaassssssss!!!!!!~

NOTE: THIS METHOD WAS USED ON A COMCAST GATEWAY (SMCD3GNV) and I have 4 Xbox Ones on one gateway. I have no idea if this will work on any other configuration like router and modem but you can try it.

I was so fed up with the strict NAT on ALL XB1 and not being able to play with someone in the same house, I started looking in forums and all over the web for a solution. Come to find out, there are not a lot of router/gateways that Microsoft says can't support multiple XB1. I was going to buy a separate router and modem but that was extra money I didn't want to spend. So I went in the gateway to look at the settings again. You may have software or use the 10.0.0.1 in your browser to get to the configuration screen. My UPnP was enabled and I had Port Forwarding turned on and set up to open 80 thru 3074. When we had COD I opened 3075 and 4500. 

So I thought well the Port Forwarding is only geared toward only one IP address, why not have the ports TRIGGERED and all the Xboxes that needed to use those ports can trigger it when it needed to. I went to PORT TRIGGERING and put in the settings on the COMCAST GATEWAY (SMCD3GNV) the following once you are in the gateway settings and you are under the ADVANCE TAB: 

1. ENABLE Port Triggering

2. Click "ADD PORT TRIGGER" in the blue area

3. In SERVICE NAME type XBOX

4. Drop down the list for SERVICE TYPE, click UDP

5. "Trigger port to" and "Trigger port from" type 88 in both boxes

6. "Target port to" and "Target port from" type 500 in both boxes

7. Click Save

8. Repeat. But when you get to SERVICE NAME, change that to Xbox1. The settings won't let the names be the same. Continue to step 4, then in the "Trigger port to" and "Trigger port from" those numbers change to 3544 in both boxes. "Target port to" and "Target port from" change to 4500 for both boxes. SAVE AGAIN.

9. Repeat step 3 but change the name to XBox2 for the SERVICE NAME.

SERVICE NAME.

10. Step 4, the DROP DOWN list CHANGES TO TCP/UDP.

11. "Trigger ports to" and "Trigger ports from", type 53 in both boxes.

12. "Target ports to" and "Target ports from", type 3074 then click SAVE.

13. TURN Port Forwarding OFF. If your setting is not turned off, turn it off.

14. Restart the gateway.

Once it reboots, turn on your Xbox and go to the settings and check your NAT type. It should be open. I turned on another Xbox and the NAT was open and so were the others. If you have a static IP address assigned to your Xbox like I did, switch it to AUTOMATIC and see if the NAT stays open. It should still say open.

I have not had any problems with this and I have had no other issues with other laptops, tablets, phones, PCs with this set up either if you are concerned about conflicts.

This worked for me using the Comcast equipment and I don't have a way to see if it works on other equipment so at least you have the numbers to put in your router or modem port triggering settings. 

If this worked for you, YOUR WELCOME.

Hi Joel. I'm Greg, an installation specialist and 8 year Windows MVP, here to help you.

As long as the underlying license you upgraded or that came with WIndows 10 is retail and not factory preinstalled or builder's OEM, then it can transfer since Upgrade inherits the rights and restrictions.
