Hi Exodus_B,

Uninstalling and re-installing Skype-for-Linux did not help.

Furthermore, I tried using Skype-for-Web, and EXACTLY the SAME PROBLEM exists... and seeing that Skype-for-Linux is built on top of Skype-for-Web, I'm 99% sure that the issue is actually with Skype-for-Web.

( My test environment: Unbuntu 16.04 LTS (64-bit), latest Chrome browser 60.0.3112.78 (64-bit), latest Skype-for-Web version 1.95.23 with Assets version 0.0.300 ). (I imagine that exactly the same issue occurs with Skype-for-Web when run on Windows and other platforms).

To summarize the issue:

  • When you call either Skype-for-Web or Skype-for-Linux, it stops ringing after only 30 seconds.
    This is inconsistent with all the other Skype clients, which ring for at least 55-60 seconds.
  • The A-party (caller's) Skype client keeps trying to call for a full 55 seconds (from Skype-for-Windows) or 90 seconds (from Skype-for-Android)... but after just 30 seconds, the rest of the caller's time is wasted, because the B-party (Web or Linux) no long hears it ringing.
  • OLD PEOPLE and DISABLED PEOPLE simply can't answer within 30 seconds. It takes them longer to answer than you or me.

I strongly suggest to:

  • Notify the Skype for Web team and request either:
  • Increase the ringing duration from 30 seconds to 55-60 seconds, to match all the other Skype clients; and/or
  • Expose the ringing duration as a setting within the options (settings) of Skype-for-Web (and thereby, also on Skype-for-Linux).

We really enjoy using your new Skype-for-Linux... and Skype-for-Web has improved. Please pass on our congratulations to your developer teams!


John.
