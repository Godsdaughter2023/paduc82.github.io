jay freedman say:

In Word, footnotes and endnotes are not identical: footnotes always go at the bottom of the page that contains their references, and endnotes (by definition) always go at the end of the document.

You can switch them from one to the other:

  • Click the dialog launcher (the little arrow) at the bottom right corner of the Footnotes group on the References tab.

  • In the Footnote and Endnote dialog, click the Convert button.

  • In the Convert Notes dialog, click OK.

Multiple Table of Contents MS Word 2013

2. The Table of Contents still includes all headers for Figures and Appendices.

2a. Have my Table of Contents not update sections from Figures or Appendices.

john korchok say:

Using one of the preset Tables of Contents formats gives you no control over the setup of your TOC. You can use one, but then all you revisions to it must be made by editing the Field Codes. I included 2 references to using field codes in my last message, please read these, you won't get far without them. Searching Word help for TOC field codes will get you a handy list of all available codes. Creating a Custom Table of Contents (at the bottom of the list of preset TOC styles) allows you to choose which styles you reference.

Word's built-in style is Heading 1, not Header 1. This may be why your preset TOC is not working, it is looking for Heading 1. A header is the area at the top of a page.

I'm not following your question about spacing. Since your TOC, TOF and TOA are separate fields, just put a couple of ordinary carriage returns between them to create a double space that doesn't disappear when you update the fields.

If you need extra space after some headings, but not others, in the TOC, then you'll have to add an extra paragraph containing a soft return (Shift + Return) after the heading text that appears in the body of the document. This extra paragraph must be in the same style as the heading that recedes it.

Then select the TOC, right-click and choose Toggle field codes. It should look something like this: { TOC \o "1-3" \h \z }. Click inside the brackets and add \v, so the field looks like this: { TOC \o "1-3" \h \z \v }. Then right-click on the field and choose Update Field, then choose Entire table. The TOC will display an extra space after the modified heading.
