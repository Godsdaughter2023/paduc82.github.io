a. user say:

You need neither.

What you do need is a daily image backup. An 'image backup' is a copy of your entire computer compressed into a single file. It takes only minutes to make an image backup and minutes to restore it; and when you restore an image backup your computer travels back in time and becomes exactly the way it was when you made the image in the first place.

By contrast, the Windows 10 installer on your USB flash drive only installs Windows. Nothing else. You'll have to re-install all your other software and drivers; and setup your computer all over again, which could take many hours. And you have to backup and restore all your personal data as a separate step.

A recovery drive gives you the opportunity to repair your installation, if you know what needs to be repaired and if the tools available can repair whatever problem(s) you have. You could spend many hours doing this and get nowhere.  And if the repairs don't work you'll end of having to reinstall Windows anyway. It's a big waste of time.

I still have the USB flash drive I used to install Windows 10 in the first place. No reason to get rid of it. But I'll almost certainly never use it again, because after I used it to install Windows 10 that first time I made an image backup of my hard drive.
