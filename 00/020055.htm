The often recommended idea to use a 3rd-party anti-malware application like Malwarebytes is actually just a hedge on the common sense though, since in over a decade of using both Microsoft Security Essentials and Windows Defender, Malwarebytes has never found a single item of significance on any of my own systems.

The true reason for hedging your security bets with Malwarebytes is the fact that most malware today is downloaded willingly by the PC user, so these are the types of items (e.g. adware, downloaders, other less malicious but annoying software) that MBAM is focused on detecting ad optionally removing.  Those who tend to install MBAM proactively are the least likely to actually need it, since unless there are other less careful users of the PC including children, they're unlikely to ever allow the download of such items in most cases.

However, my suspicion would be that one or more of your previous security programs might have left remnants that are causing the issues between MBAM and Defender, so I'd download whatever removal application those other product might provide in order to insure they're completely removed, insuring you reboot the PC between steps to complete the process properly.

qa guy commelec say:
Thanks, Sir. Created table and added 31 records (1-31) since this would be run monthly.

The CountNumber table must include 0 (zero) to include the start date.

These two queries gives a simple output without the totals row --

   qryEMLrptDates --

SELECT tblEMLDates.Soldier, tblEMLDates.EMLTravelStart, DateAdd("d",[CountNUM],[EMLTravelStart]) AS EMLTravel

FROM tblEMLDates, CountNumber

WHERE (((DateAdd("d",[CountNUM],[EMLTravelStart])) Between [EMLTravelStart] And [EMLTravelEnd] And (DateAdd("d",[CountNUM],[EMLTravelStart])) Between CVDate([Forms]![YourForm]![ReportStart]) And CVDate([Forms]![YourForm]![ReportEnd])));

 

PARAMETERS [Forms]![YourForm]![ReportStart] DateTime, [Forms]![YourForm]![ReportEnd] DateTime;

TRANSFORM Count(qryEMLrptDates.EMLTravelStart) AS CountOfEMLTravelStart

SELECT qryEMLrptDates.Soldier, Count(qryEMLrptDates.EMLTravelStart) AS [Total Of EMLTravelStart]

FROM qryEMLrptDates

GROUP BY qryEMLrptDates.Soldier

PIVOT Format([EMLTravel],"Short Date");

 

To have a totals row you need a union query and a different crosstab like this –

   qryEMLrptDates --

SELECT " " & tblEMLDates.Soldier AS [_Soldier], 1 AS EMLTraveler, DateAdd("d",[CountNUM],[EMLTravelStart]) AS EMLTravel

FROM tblEMLDates, CountNumber

WHERE (((DateAdd("d",[CountNUM],[EMLTravelStart])) Between [EMLTravelStart] And [EMLTravelEnd] And (DateAdd("d",[CountNUM],[EMLTravelStart])) Between CVDate([Forms]![YourForm]![ReportStart]) And CVDate([Forms]![YourForm]![ReportEnd])))

UNION ALL SELECT "Total" AS [_Soldier], Count(tblEMLDates.EMLTravelStart), DateAdd("d",[CountNUM],[EMLTravelStart]) AS EMLTravel

FROM tblEMLDates, CountNumber

WHERE (((DateAdd("d",[CountNUM],[EMLTravelStart])) Between [EMLTravelStart] And [EMLTravelEnd] And (DateAdd("d",[CountNUM],[EMLTravelStart])) Between CVDate([Forms]![YourForm]![ReportStart]) And CVDate([Forms]![YourForm]![ReportEnd])))

GROUP BY "Total", DateAdd("d",[CountNUM],[EMLTravelStart]);

 

PARAMETERS [Forms]![YourForm]![ReportStart] DateTime, [Forms]![YourForm]![ReportEnd] DateTime;

TRANSFORM Sum(qryEMLrptDates.EMLTraveler) AS SumOfEMLTraveler

SELECT [_Soldier] AS Expr2

FROM qryEMLrptDates

GROUP BY [_Soldier]

PIVOT Format([EMLTravel],"Short Date");

This solution worked for me. Error 9C59 when trying to install IE11

I had a problem with the above and posted the following message earlier today. I am reposting it here because having read so many posts trying to find solutions, I think this might help others.

"I have had a similar but not identical problem over the past 10 days which I have just resolved today. My problem related to Error Code 9C59, IE9, 10 and 11 and KB 2872074. I am using Windows 7 Home Premium on a 64 bit system. I am a long time user of MS products but would classify my skill/knowledge as between a novice and average. I also use Chrome in addition to IE. 

Like you, I scoured various fora to get the answer to the problem and was struck by the lack of any certainty with the answers. KB 2872074 was full of maybe this and maybe that, try this or that. Not what I would have expected from Microsoft. The interesting point is that this document was reviewed/released on 27 March 2014 so it is very recent.

Briefly, my problem. Unlike you, I had IE11 installed from launch but recently, experienced intermittent problems with the opening tabs. My setting is and has been "Start with tabs from last session". However, from time to time and increasingly so over the preceding 14 days, IE11 decided to open at times with my Home Page - rather than the multiple tabs from the last session. (I am uncertain that I have solved this problem - I have not had time to check - so if anyone has solutions please let me know). Anyway after trying various things, it seemed to me that something might have got corrupted with IE11. So I decided to uninstall and re-install IE11.

My Windows Updates were all up to date but on trying to reinstall IE11, I also got the Error Code 9C59.  I read KB 2872074 carefully as I thought MS must have the answer. Eventually, I came to the section about deleting IE9 before trying to install IE11. Now remember, I started on IE11 but was now on IE10. So I uninstalled IE10, rebooted ran Windows Update and selected updates relating to IE9 to get a working IE9 version. I then uninstalled IE9, rebooted, ran Windows Update and in the Optional Updates section, I was offered IE9, IE10 and IE11. I selected IE11 and .......... I got the dreaded Code 9C59 - IE11 failed to install. Never one to give up, I tried to install IE11 from IE9 and separately from IE10. So I had tried to upgrade to IE11 from IE8, IE9 and IE10. No success. I tried variations on this over several days.

By this stage I was very frustrated. I re-read the MS advisory KB 2872074.  I had followed their advice on how to uninstall the various IE versions. I realised that the only option that I had not tried was the "suggestion" to disable antivirus/anti spyware while trying to re-install IE11. Of course, MS said that they could not recommend that course of action (for obvious reasons) but once I had disabled my Kaspersky Pure 3 in its entirety (including Firewall), IE11 installed without any problem. but I did NOT turn on Windows Firewall as recommended by MS because in my experience Firewalls can cause problems on installation of certain programs. 

You also raised your concerns about losing your bookmarks etc when uninstalling IE 10 and then IE9. It did not happen to me as I was merely rolling back to the previous versions but why not just save your Bookmarks to give you peace of mind for the future?.

Hope that this might be of some help."
