If you find the mode is set to SATA, change the mode to IDE.

If you find the mode is set to SATA Native Mode, disable SATA Native Mode.

Google speech on my Smartphone is excellent. However, WSR, Windows Speech Recognition, in Word is almost as good for me. WSR is easier to use to make corrections as it is done by voice not with the little keyboard on the phone. I can also easily create voice executed macros to enter large blocks of repeatable text using the free WSRMacros. It is also easy to navigate the desktop and Internet using the Show Numbers command.

Having said the above there are certain things necessary for high accuracy with WSR. First you must have a good microphone. The microphones built into notebook computers often pick up background noise and are less forgiving when you turn your head slightly or move closer or further from the unit. A good noise cancelling close talk USB microphone will likely help and are generally not expensive. The next thing that will improve accuracy is to ensure you enunciate each word clearly.

WSR learns when you correct mistakes. It helps if you make corrections in context by selecting the 2 or 3 words around the mistake when you make the correction. This is because WSR uses groups of words in its programming. Think of these two sentences and why the words before and after help decode the homonyms:

1. Two boys went to see a doctor because they ate too much food.

2. They're going to park their car over there.

One last thing, always start WSR before Microsoft Word.
