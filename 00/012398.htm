daniel pineault say:

You could try something like

 

'*******************************************************************************
'Author: Daniel Pineault / CARDA Consultants Inc.
'Purpose: SandBoxMode Configuration for Office 2007
'Revision: 2010-06-23   Initial Release
'*******************************************************************************

 Const HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE = &H80000002

 Dim oRegistry 
 Dim sKey
 Dim sValue
 Dim bAlreadyExists
 
 Set oRegistry = GetObject("winmgmts:\\.\root\default:StdRegProv")
 bAlreadyExists = False

 'standard x86
 sKey = "SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Office\12.0\Access Connectivity Engine\Engines"
 oRegistry.GetDWORDValue HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE, sKey, "SandBoxMode", sValue
 bAlreadyExists = Not IsNull(sValue)
 If bAlreadyExists = True Then
  oRegistry.SetDWORDValue HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE, sKey, "SandBoxMode", 2
 End If

 'x64 - Didn't find x86 Reg Entry so now try x64
 IF bAlreadyExists =False Then
  sKey = "SOFTWARE\Wow6432Node\Microsoft\Office\12.0\Access Connectivity Engine\Engines"
  oRegistry.GetDWORDValue HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE, sKey, "SandBoxMode", sValue
   bAlreadyExists = Not IsNull(sValue)
  If bAlreadyExists = True Then
   oRegistry.SetDWORDValue HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE, sKey, "SandBoxMode", 2
  End If
 End If

 

Note depending on your version, you may have to ajust the 12.0 to xx.0.

 

Also, have you set your FE's location as a trusted location?  This is the first thing to do before anything else.  Then see how your db behaves before going any further.

Hello Eoce,

Thank you for your post.

Can you try to do all the following steps to see if  it's works?

Using a VPN connection  
To get a VPN profile on your phone

  The first step is to get a VPN profile onto your phone. There are two ways to get one:

  • Set up a workplace account to automatically get a VPN profile from your company.
  • Create a VPN profile on your own. (You can learn how later in this topic.)
Note

VPN is only available on Windows Phone 8.1 or later. Check to see which software version you have and find out if an update is available.   

To connect to a VPN

Once you have a VPN profile on your phone, you're ready to connect.   

  1. In the App list, tap Settings > VPN.

  2. Tap and hold the VPN profile name, then tap Edit.

  3. If the Username and Password boxes appear, type your username and password, then tap Save.

  4. To connect to the VPN, do one of the following, depending on what type of profile you're using:

    • If the VPN profile has Automatic listed under it, your phone will automatically connect to the VPN when you try to access information on your company's network or visit Internet sites that have an IP address that's listed in a protected IP range. The IP address determines when the VPN will automatically connect, not the protected domain names.
    • If the VPN profile has Manual listed under it, tap the profile to connect to the VPN, then use the app that accesses data on your company's network, visit a company intranet site or visit protected Internet domains or IP addresses.
Notes
  • The icons at the top of your screen will show you when you're connected to the VPN. This icon appears when you're connected over WiFi, and this one shows when you're connected over mobile data.
  • Want to see your VPN connection status or turn VPN on or off quickly? Just add VPN as a quick action, swipe down from the top of the screen to see your VPN status, then tap it to turn it on or off.
  • If the profile has Send all traffic set to On, all data you send and receive will go over the VPN.  
To create a VPN profile

If you don't have a VPN profile on your phone, you'll need to create one on your own. Before you start, contact your company's support person to get the VPN connection settings for your organisation. If you're using a VPN service, first visit the Windows Phone Store to download the app for that service. You might want to visit the service's website to see if they have additional instructions or the VPN connection settings listed there.

  1. In the App list, tap Settings > VPN.

  2. Set Status to On , then tap Add .

  3. In the Server name or IP address box, type the server name or IP address of your VPN server.

  4. Tap Type, then choose the type of VPN connection you want to create – IKEv2, L2TP with IPSec or the name of the VPN service you use.

    If an SSL VPN app is required and you don't have one installed yet, tap the link to download one from the Store.

  5. If Connect using appears, tap the box and choose one of the following, depending on what type of VPN connection you chose in the previous step:

    • For a connection that uses a username and password, tap Username+password, then enter your username and password in the Username and Password boxes.
    • For a connection that uses a certificate, tap Certificate, then choose a certificate to use to connect to the VPN.
    • For an L2TP with IPSec type of VPN connection, tap Username+password+preshared key or Preshared key, then select or enter that information. (This is only available if you have Windows Phone 8.1 Update installed on your phone. Which version of Windows Phone do I have?)

  6. If you chose to use a certificate for Connect using and you want to validate that the server is authentic by comparing a certificate that's on it to one on your phone, set Server certificate validation to On .

  7. To automatically connect to the VPN  when a company app or site requires it, set Connect automatically to On .

    If Connect automatically is turned off, the profile will be a manual one that you'll have to tap when you want to connect to the VPN.

  8. For Send all traffic, do one of the following:

    • To have all data you send and receive go over the VPN, set Send all traffic to On .
    • To only have data that requires access to your company's network, intranet or certain Internet sites go over the VPN connection, set Send all traffic to Off , tap Domains and IP ranges, then enter the domain names and IP ranges that are protected. Only data that's sent and received from those domains or IP addresses will go over the VPN connection. Other

    • In the Profile name box, type a unique name for your profile.

    • Tap Advanced, then enter any additional settings you need for your organisation's VPN or for the VPN service you use.

      You might need to contact your company's support person or visit the VPN service's website to get additional information, such as the Proxy settings and DNS suffix.

    • Press the Back button on your phone to go back to the Add profile screen, then tap Save.

Whether you like it or not, both suggestions are grammatically correct and make the sentences easier to read. They are however only suggestions and, like all other suggestions, you are free to ignore them.
