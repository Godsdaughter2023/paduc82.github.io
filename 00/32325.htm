thomas ferguson say:

EIDE is extended (some say enhanced) IDE (integrated drive electronics - meaning the electronic circuitry is part of the drive rather than part of a separate drive controller). Also called ATA-2, it supports faster PIO modes (3 and 4) and multiword DMA modes (1 and 2). Also supports logical block addressing (LBA) and block transfers. Both EIDE and IDE use parallel data streams. These are PATA drives but I have not seen the term commonly used for them.

SATA-1 and SATA-2 are other standards using serial data stream and different cables and connectors. eSata stands for External SATA rather than extended or enhanced. A different "kettle of fish" of a meaning of the initial e or E than with EIDE. But I digress.

All IDE drives which use the parallel interface (PATA drives) should function properly with EIDE cables and controllers. However, some which are operating near the limits of the specifications might operate poorly.

Tom Ferguson
