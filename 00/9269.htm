hansv mvp say:

Here is such a function:

Function GetState(Address As String) As String
    Dim v As Variant
    For Each v In Array("Abu Dhabi", "Ajman", "Fujairah", _
            "Sharjah", "Dubai", "Ras Al-Khaimah", "Umm Al-Qaiwain")
        If InStr(1, Address, v, vbTextCompare) > 0 Then
            GetState = v
            Exit Function
        End If
    Next v
End Function

Let's say you have an address in A1. The formula

=GetState(A1)

will extract the state. The formula can be filled down.

Nothing within this or other portions of the statement requires that you uninstall MSE once your small business itself exceeds 10 devices, only that MSE not be installed on more than 10 of them.  That's assuming, of course, that none of the other statements or restrictions apply such as item 3.

 

Since typically the maintenance of differing anti-malware products becomes inefficient as a business grows and servers or other centrally managed application concepts take effect, this is a reasonable number of 'seats' for Microsoft to allow as free to use for a small business, without attempting to define an absolute size that determines what constitutes a small business, obviously a vague term.

 

Funny how difficult it seems to be for people to interpret such a simple statement when for years they've complained how difficult the complex legal statements of the past were to comprehend.  I see nothing either complex or vague within that or any of the other items within that section.

To hide all of the ribbon, this one line of code should work:

DoCmd.ShowToolbar "Ribbon", acToolbarNo

 I am not aware that this behaviour changes when using a accDE.
