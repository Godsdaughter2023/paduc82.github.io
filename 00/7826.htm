Hi Daniel Fukuda,

 

Thank you for posting your concern here in Microsoft Community Forum. It's my pleasure answering your concern regarding junk mail filter in Outlook.com.

 

Basically there is no difference between Hotmail's junk filter compare to Outlook.com junk mail filter. However,
A. Block a sender or a domain name

1. Sign in to your account. Go to inbox.
2. Click the Gear icon located at the upper-right side of the page.
3. Select More mail settings.
4. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.
5. Click Blocked senders.
6. Enter the email address or domain on the box labeled Blocked email address or domain: and click Add to list >>.

B. Set the filtering level of the messages

Please follow these steps to setup your junk e-mail filter in Outlook.com:

 

1. Sign in to the Outlook.com website with your Microsoft account.
2. In the upper-right corner of the page, click Gear icon, and then click More mail settings.
3. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.
4. Under Filters and reporting, select the options that you want, and then click Save.

 

Notes:

> If you choose Exclusive for your junk e-mail filter, only messages from e-mail addresses on your Contacts or Allowed senders lists appear in your Inbox. Messages from senders not on those lists are automatically sent to the Junk e-mail folder.

> You should occasionally check your junk e-mail folder to make sure that good messages are not sent there by mistake.


C. Sweep function - This will allow all received or even future emails coming from the specific sender be deleted. Here are the steps to activate the Sweep option:

1. Sign in to your Outlook.com account. Go to Inbox.
2. Check the specific sender you would like to filter.
3. On the upper part, click Sweep
4. Choose Delete all from
5. On the pop-up box, just click Delete all.

Note: You can also block future messages from that sender by checking the box beside Also block future messages.

D. Rules - we can create a rule that can block a specific word from the subject of the spam email.

1. Sign in to your Outlook.com account with your Microsoft account.
2. Click on Gear icon then More mail settings... in the upper right corner of the page.
3. Under the Customizing Hotmail section, click on Rules for sorting new messages.
4. Click on the New button.
5. Under Step 1: Which messages do you want this rule to apply to,
a. First drop-down menu - choose Subject
b. Second drop-down menu - choose contains
c. Third field - input the word you would like to block
6. Under Step 2: What action do you want to apply, click on Delete these messages
7. Click Save button.

Hoping that the solutions above will help you protect your account from any spam messages you're receiving.
