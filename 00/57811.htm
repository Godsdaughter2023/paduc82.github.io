shenan stanley say:
Can the hard drive in a Toshiba Satellite Pro L300-1FK be replaced with a solid state drive and what would the advantage be if so.


Swap out hardware?  Possibly.  Woud depend on what size hard drive you currently have in it (physical dimensions) and what type of Solid State Drive you got and whether or not it comes with/you'd need an adapter to get it physically put into place.  As long as the cabling is the same on the new drive as the old and it can be physically screwed into the same place - should be good.  There's also the matter of the BIOS supporting the drive - although that shouldn't be a problem for most modern systems.

Advantages?  Probably very little for most people.  It might boot faster and you might gain a few seconds here and there.
