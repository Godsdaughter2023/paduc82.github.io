Refreshing your PC or resetting your PC is also that same that they allow you to choose what happens to your personal files. The reset this PC has three options that allow you too either: "Keep my files", "Remove everything" or "Restore factory settings". Choosing one of the three options will do the following:

  • Reinstalls Windows 10 and keeps your personal files.
  • Removes apps and drivers you installed.
  • Removes changes you made to settings.
  • Removes any apps your PC manufacturer installed. (If your PC came with Windows 10, apps from your PC manufacturer will be reinstalled.)

Method 2: "Remove everything"

  • Reinstalls Windows 10 and removes all your personal files.
  • Removes apps and drivers you installed.
  • Removes changes you made to settings.
  • Removes any apps your PC manufacturer installed. (If your PC came with Windows 10, apps from your PC manufacturer will be reinstalled.)

Method 3: "Restore factory settings"

  • Reinstalls the version of Windows that your PC came with (either Windows 8 or Windows 8.1) and removes your personal files.
  • Removes apps and drivers you installed.
  • Removes changes you made to settings.
  • Reinstalls any apps your PC manufacturer installed on your PC.

dean gibson say:

You may not understand how to use OneDrive effectively. You do not need to have a separate local copy on your computer;  the local copy (sync'ed w/ OneDrive) should be fine.  If you decide to reformat your hard disk, that will not delete the cloud copies.  When you reinstall Windows & it prompts you to login to OneDrive, doing that will re-download all your OneDrive music.

Or, if you temporarily need the space on your local computer, you can use the OneDrive settings to "unlink" your account.  Once that is done, you can delete the local copies.  When you want them back, just start up OneDrive again, & it will download all your music.

Error 1203 is a very interesting error code. Here is what I found out about error 1203

 

Error 1203 is a very interesting cartridge error. It means your catridges overflow even from brand new catridges after a several prints or right after you refilled the catridges. Check to see if ink are smeared on the holder where the contact panel with the cartridge

Solution:
I use the lint free cloth(coffee filter work great) with a few drops of water to dampen and clean off those contact on the holder and my printer work again.
Then before you print anything, you should run aligned the catridges a couple times then run clean catridges a couple times.
Print the test page to see how it's look and way you go.
Be sure not to leave any debris of lint free cloth(coffee filter work great on those holder contact panel.

 

Dell Series 5 ink cartridges have a sensor in them that cannot be reset. Article on refilling Dell ink cartridges

.

Dell Series 5 ink cartridges can be purchased at Staples, Walmart or Dell's Website

.
