davidf2 say:

It sounds like your FP2003 is an upgrade version to a previous version of Office. If so then yes you need a copy of the old version of Office but you will not need to install it.

If you have any Office 97, 2000, 2002 installation discs you can simply switch out that disc in disc drive (stop the Office installation if it tries) when FP2003 asks for one of the old copies of Office. Once Windows finds that old disc you can switch back to the FP disc and continue the install.

I have found it easier to copy both the FP disc and the old Office version disc to my hard drive and install from there, pointing to where I copied the old Office files when FP requests them...or at least copy the old Office files to your hard drive so you don't have to switch out discs during the install. You just point to where you copied the files when the FP install asks for them.

This also means that if you can find a copy of one of those old Office versions you can download and save it directly to your hard drive. Again, you do not need to install it. There are various places on the net where you can buy or download free copies of these old versions of Office, but be careful.

The Reminder window is sorted based on the Reminder Time or the order that the reminders fired and not the Due Date of an item.

 

So apparently, the reminder for item f was before the reminder of items h and j but has a due date which was later.

 

Another reason for this to occur is when you created an item with a reminder time in the past (but these get resorted based on the Reminder Time after a restart).
