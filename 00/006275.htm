The fact that the retailer you bought the license from says you are allowed 3 installations is misleading.   The only way they can sell you a product key that allows install on up to 3 computers is by selling you a "Volume License" product key.  Those keys can be defined to allow 3 activations.  The keys will normally work for the initial install.  But at some point in time MS will no longer accept that key.



The problem is, that the purchaser of the volume license CANNOT resell it or install using those keys on machines that do not belong to them.  So, eventually MS recognizes that keys from a volume license have been illegally distributed, ie resold, the keys are no longer considered valid product keys by the MS activation system.

When you use any of the functions of the Find/Replace/Go To dialog, you change the "browse object." Look at the bottom of the vertical scroll bar. There are two double arrows there; when you first start Word, they are black, and if you mouse over them, the ScreenTips will say "Previous Page" and "Next Page."
 
After you have used Find, Replace, or Go To, they turn blue, and if you mouse over them, you will see that the ScreenTips say "Previous Find/Go To" and "Next Find/Go To."
 
If you click on the round button between the arrows (Select Browse Object), you can reselect Page as the browse object ("Browse by Page"). Then the keyboard shortcuts will again have the expected function. You may want to explore the Browse Object gallery, as there are other "browse objects" that can be quite convenient, such as "Browse by Section."
