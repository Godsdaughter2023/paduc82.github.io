cmdrkeene say:

Hi Kyle,

Try turning off the "automatic reboot during failure" option, which should let you see the blue screen and the details it provides about the source of the error.

 

  1. Open Advanced System Properties
    You can right-click the Computer link and choose Properties, or press +Break to open it directly (ironic shortcut, yes?).  Then click Advanced System Settings on the left.

  2. Change System Failure Options
    On the Advanced tab, under "Startup and Recovery", click Settings.  Then uncheck the "automatically restart" option.  Click OK to close both boxes.

 

This change won't take effect until you reboot, so you need to manually reboot once.  Then if the error happens again, you'll get a blue screen instead of an automatic reboot.
