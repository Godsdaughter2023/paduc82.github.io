scottgem say:

Don't use an Hyperlink. I almost never do. Instead do the following:

First: set a constant at the top of a global module:

Public Const strDefaultPath = "T:\Sales & Customer Services\Customer Documents & Drawings\"

Use the Double Click event of the control storing the document name to run:

Application.FollowHyperlink strDefaultPath & Me.DocNumber & ".PDF"

This is assuming they are all PDFs. Also that the reference number is in a control on the form named DocNumber. 

I'm also assuming that a different part of the process is assigning the reference number and using it to save the document under that name.

ashish mathur say:

Hi,

 

Your formula is correct.  The result is 0 because there are no dates in column B which are within the dates mentioned in L10:L11 of summary sheet.

jeeped say:
... I can't Sumproduct as the email addresses are text, but I don't know how to get around this ...

You shouldn't have any trouble as long as the comparison is text and the actual numbers to sum are truly numerical.

            

The formula in F2 is,

=SUMIF($A$2:$A$12,E2,$C$2:$C$12)

A SUMPRODUCT() alternative would be,

=SUMPRODUCT(($A$2:$A$12=E2)*$C$2:$C$12)

The SUMIF() can be expanded to SUMIFS() to add your weekly criteria. The same goes for SUMPRODUCT(). The problem with your original formula is that you are asking full column references which attempt to compare the text header labels to a number. Change the range references to data only and you should be fine.

A unique list of the email addresses from column A was collected using this array formula in E2,

=INDEX($A$2:$A$12,MATCH(0,COUNTIF(E$1:E1,$A$2:$A$12),0))

... which requires Ctrl+Shift+Enter. All of these formulas can be filled down as necessary.

a. user say:

Hi JJRIH,

 

Thank you for posting your concern here at the Microsoft Community and it's my pleasure to assist you.

 

Based from what I've read, you'd like to know on how to automatically move emails received from your Outlook.com alias to a personal folder.

 

We would like to inform you that you can set up an email rules to filter incoming emails from your Outlook.com alias automatically moved to a personal folder. Please follow the steps provided below on how to do this:

  1. Sign in to your Microsoft account.
  2. Click the gear icon on the top right panel of your screen, it is located beside your username.
  3. Select More mail settings.
  4. Under "Customizing Outlook", click on Rules for sorting new messages.
  5. Click New button.
  6. Under Step 1: Which messages do you want this rule to apply to?, on the first box with dropdown list, select To of CC line and on the second box with dropdown list, select contains word.
  7. On the third box, type your Outlook.com alias.
  8. Under Step 2: What action do you want to apply?, select Move to a new folder, then type your desired folder name.
  9. Click the Save button.

If there's anything else that I can help you with, please do not hesitate to post back here. We'd love to hear from you again.
