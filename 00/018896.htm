jeffcnick say:

How do I kill a print job on a PC?

This easy on my MacBook, but can't for the life figure out how to do this simple task of killing a Word print job on my PC.

When printing from my PC, there is no dialogue box and I can't find anyplace else to kill the job, either from within Work, or outside work.

Help me stop burning up expensive paper and Toner Cartridge ink!
jay freedman say:
See http://www.dummies.com/how-to/content/how-to-stop-a-runaway-print-job-in-windows-7.html

(Sorry about the "dummies" part -- it's just the title of the book series!)

Many printer drivers display a balloon near the task bar while the printing is going on, making it a bit easier to just click one button. Apparently Brother's drivers aren't that friendly.

Word is usually set up to send a print job to the Windows "spooler", which catches all the data and handles sending it slowly to the printer. The only time you can kill a print job from within Word is during the few seconds it takes to send the document to the spooler -- there's an icon in Word's status bar (at the bottom) that you can click during that time to cancel the job. Once that icon disappears, Word no longer has any control of the job you have to go through Windows and the print queue instead.

You can go into the Advanced page of the Options dialog and uncheck the box for "Print in background" so Word won't use the spooler. The icon in Word's status bar will then be visible as long as the document is printing, but you won't be able to do any editing until the printing finishes. Your choice...
