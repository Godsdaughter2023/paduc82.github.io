With mirror margins every other page for my 4 page Table of Contents now falls outside of the right margin....how do I adjust?

I am writing a book to be bound, thus I am using Mirror Pages setup.  With my page setup set to mirror margins every other page for my four page Table of Contents now falls outside of the right margin....how do I adjust?

 

The rest of the manuscript comes out fine.

 

 I have set the Show Document Content to Show Text boundaries, and indeed it shows the TOC exceeding the boundary lines quite clearlly for every other TOC page.  I do want to use leaders with numbers for the TOC with numbers.

 

Thank you for your help.

 

andrea jones - all about resources say: Check you haven't got any extra tabs set in that paragraph (it may be an idea to go into the tabs window and check there's only 1 tab set).  Also, make sure the tab window is showing leader dots as being set on your tab stop.  Other than that, I can only suggest you check indents (click the Paragraph group launcher button) or character spacing (click the Font group launcher button) to make sure there are no strange settings there.

andrea jones - all about resources say: It's probably because you set your mirror margins after adding the table of contents.  The position for the page numbers in the TOC is set using a right tab stop, when you added the gutter for the mirror margin this pushed the right tab outside of the margin.  To move the tab stop, make sure you have your ruler displayed and click in the TOC field.  You should now see a backwards L shape outside of the right margin on the ruler, click and drag this with your left mouse button to a new position inside the margin and this should fix the setting.  Alternatively, once clicked inside the field you can click the small launcer button in the bottom right-hand corner of the Paragraph group on the Home ribbon and then click the Tabs button at the bottom of the window.  Adjust the position of the right tab stop to a setting within your line length.
