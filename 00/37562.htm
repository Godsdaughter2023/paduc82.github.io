karenjune_f say:

Hello Atakan M.,

 

Thank you for reaching out Surface Community. The batteries in Surface Book may charge at different rates, depending on the power state of each battery. Whichever battery has less than 20% charge will be charged first. When both batteries are at 20%, the clipboard will charge to 85%, and then both batteries will charge to 100%. This may be expected behavior based on how the Surface Book charges one battery and then the other. On your case for plugged in not charging, please follow each steps below to see if there are changes.

  1. Wipe the pins on the power connector with a soft, dry, lint-free cloth (microfiber cloths work great).
  2. Plug the PSU back into the wall outlet. If possible, try a different wall outlet.
  3. Try turning the power connector over and plugging into the device from a different orientation. Surface devices are designed to be charged with the power connector orientated in either direction. If the power connector only works in one orientation, there is likely a problem with the Surface PSU.
  4. From Device Manager, expand the Batteries category. There will be two battery drivers to uninstall. Double-tap or double-click Microsoft ACPI-Compliant Control Method Battery, select the Driver tab, and select Uninstall > OK. Restart once done.
  5. To make sure you got the latest drivers and firmware, please go to
    • If there's not an .msi file that corresponds to the build of Windows 10 you have installed, select the .msi file that's closest to your build number. 
    • To find out which version and build of Windows you're using, select Start > Settings  > System  > About , then look under Windows Specifications to find your operating system version and OS build number.
  6. Go to Settings > Update & Security > Windows Updates > Check for updates
  7. Press and hold the power button for 20 seconds. Performing a Forced Shutdown is like removing the battery from a laptop or unplugging a desktop PC. It resets the hardware and clears all the cache and memory for your hardware.
  8. From Start, select Power and then select Shut down. Allow the device to charge for about 45 minutes before turning back on. This will allow Surface to charge as quickly as possible without interference.

Please keep us posted.

Stored Usernames and passwords deleted on reboot

We recently switched over to IBM Thinkpad T410's at work Running XP with all the latest upgrades/patches.  Most of us are having problems mapping network drives to another domain.  When you reboot, you are prompted to reenter your password to explore the mapped drive.  The passwords are not remembered even though you checked the reconnect at login option when you mapped the drive (and added the domain specific username/passwd).
