srh11 say:

Background:  I have very limited knowledge of how I can type an email, hitting the send button and somehow its intended recipient in some far off distant place manages to receive it.  Total magic.

My knowledge has come from scouring forums, trying to decipher the techno speak, convert that into my native language, try that, fail....repeat.  Naturally I hope I will find the solution to my problem or stumble upon it.   Stumbling upon it looks like the case here.  I hope it helps someone.  

The problem of missing folders (Sent, Drafts, Trash, Junk) seems to come up when multiple email addresses are being combined into one mail program.  In this case two email addresses in the desktop email program Outlook 2013. 

From what I have gathered (see background) when a desktop program, outlook 2013, contacts (tech term not known) the servers to see what emails somebody is sending us or what we're sending it.  The server "knows" what folders to put the information in (Inbox, Sent, Trash etc.) and what to do with the emails from there.  I think this is called "MAPPING".  The server and the desktop program and folders (Inbox, Sent, Trash etc.) are "MAPPED" correctly.  Generally speaking, there must be some default "MAPPING" that happens.  How else would email seem to work most of the time? 

It looks like some shenanigans must have happened when Outlook 2013 desktop program was built. Outlook 2013 doesn't seem to like more than one email address especially if it is an IMAP email account (my case).  It doesn't know how to "MAP" the folders for second account.  (Maybe its any second, third or fourth account, POP or IMAP) I don't know why, who or what was responsible.  Only that I have wasted countless hours trying to figure it out. 

I will use the term "Solution" loosely.  It worked for me and I hope it works for everyone who reads this.

The "Solution" for me was in the "Root folder path:".  I really don't know what the "Root folder path:" is.  I can only guess this is where the Outlook 2013 desktop program syncs up the second email and its folders (Inbox, Outbox, Drafts, Sent, etc.)  with the servers to figure out where to "MAP" the folders for the desktop program (Outlook 2013).  The kicker...the desktop program has to tell the server, via the "Root folder path:" where to start the "MAPPING".   When I put the word Inbox into the "root folder path" box in the Account Settings for my second email, everything mapped itself perfectly.  Meaning, Inbox, Drafts, Sent, Trash, Junk etc.  all showed up.

So how do you get there.

  1. Open Outlook 2013
  2. Click on File
  3. Click Account Settings
  4. Click on the email that is not working correctly
  5. Click on Change
  6. Click on More Settings... (lower right corner)
  7. Click on Advanced
  8. About half-way down you will see a section Folders with "Root folder path:" and an empty box beside it
  9. Type Inbox in the empty box
  10. Click OK, this will take you back to the Change Account box
  11. Click Next>
  12. Test Account Settings box will open up to test the account, once completed hopefully green check marks
  13. Click close

It worked for me, I hope it works for all.  More importantly, when things go invariably wrong again and we can't remember how we fixed it the first time, you have a spot to come back to for reference.
