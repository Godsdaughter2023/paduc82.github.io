2) Docking connector physical connectivity is glitchy, if you bump the surface book, you can lose the dock

3) Power sequencing is important. Dock first, then monitor attached to dock, then the surface book boots attached to dock.

4) Any entry into sleep or suspend will confuse the dock and you have to reset / repower everything

My recommendation for microsoft is to create a power profile that disables everything fancy when docked and rework the firmware on the dock to be able to independently detect and connect to a monitor. Hi SzabolcsZ,

If the issue occurs when you send emails from office 365 to Exchange server users, you can try the steps in KB you mentioned above. 

If the issue occurs when you send meeting request from Office 365 users to other Office 365 users, the setting of the reminder is only available to you when you organize or receive a meeting request in the Outlook client. In other words, the setting of the reminder when sending the meeting request cannot affect the meeting attendees’ reminder settings.
So when the calendar default reminders are different, it is an expected behavior that attendee and organizer can view different reminders of the meeting request in the Outlook client.
If you want the reminder to be none when the attendee receives that message, you can change the reminder setting in meeting request and click Save & Close.

Regards,
Poppy

Outlook won't open after Office 365 Update - Microsoft Community

patrick phillips say:

Outlook won't open after Office 365 Update

What is the solution when Outlook only continues to be "Processing" when attempting to initiate after update and does not open?

larry yu msft say:

Hi Patrick,

Usually, Outlook cannot open may be caused by add-ins or profile has corrupted. So please try the steps below to check if there is any improvement:

1. If you are using Windows machine, try to open Outlook in safe mode. To do that:

  • Choose Start->Run.
  • Type Outlook /safe and then click OK.
  • In the Choose Profile dialog box, accept the default setting of Outlook and choose OK.
  • If prompted, enter your password and choose Accept.

2. If you can open Outlook in safe mode, the issue is likely related to one of the add-ins. Try to disable add-ins and then re-enable them one by one in the following steps.

  • Click File->Options->Add-ins in your Outlook client.
  • At the bottom of the View and manage Office Add-ins, make sure the Manage box shows COM Add-ins, and then choose Go.
  • Clear all selected check boxes of the add-ins, and then click OK.
  • Click File-<Exit.
  • Restart Outlook and then check the add-ins one by one to see which one caused the issue.

If Outlook won’t open after that, continue to perform Step 3.

3. Create a new Outlook profile and then configure your Office 365 account on it.

If the issue persists after performing the steps above, we need some information below to troubleshoot it:

1. Can you open other Office clients such as Word? If yes, let us know the detailed Office version by clicking File > Account in Word. An example about the detailed Office version 16.0.6001.1078.

2. Run the SaRA (Support and Recovery Assistant) tool by referring to the article below and then provide the final result to us for analysis.

http://diagnostics.office.com/#/?env=Exrca

Best Regards,

Larry
