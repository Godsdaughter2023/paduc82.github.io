The general name for it is a "memory leak". A program uses some memory, but does not release it when done. 

Part of the memory used is simply because loading a spreadsheet may load some additional features or functions that are not part of the base Excel feature set.  Excel has been told to keep stuff loaded to "speed up" subsequent spreadsheet loading on the assumption you will be using it again.

I haven't checked into Excel itself, but odds are that the problem is with an addin rather than Excel itself.

Hi Techmate1982,

I'd like to confirm which subscription you have subscribed.

There are only two subscriptions including Access, Office 365 ProPlus and Office Enterprise E3. If you are using Office 365 Enterprise E1/Office 365 Business Essentials (Small Business), there is no Office client. If you are using Office 365 Business/Office 365 Business Premium, there is no Access client included in it. You can also take a look at the following article for detailed information.
https://products.office.com/en-us/business/compare-more-office-365-for-business-plans

If you want to use Access, you should update your subscription to Office 365 ProPlus or Office 365 Enterprise E3, or subscribe them directly. Here are two article for your reference:
Business plans you can switch to in Office 365

If you are using Office 365 ProPlus/ Office 365 Enterprise E3, you can download the latest version Office client via https://portal.office.com/OLS/MySoftware.aspx . Here is an article for your reference: Download and install Office using Office 365 for business on your PC
