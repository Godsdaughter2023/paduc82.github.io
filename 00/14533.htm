peter_the_great say:

A bit dumb, but it works. Nokia drive will let you save your current location as a favourite AND if you turn off location services in the phone it will blindly believe that you are in the location that you indicate on a map.

 

1. Turn off your Location services in phone settings.

2. Open Nokia Drive and navigate to the desired point on the map.

3. Press back button once to get out of full-screen map. This will allow you to go to menu and save your location.

4. Press menu (bullet-point-like icon on your lower right corner, if you see gear icon instead read instruction 3)

5. Press Save location

6. You can rename your new favourite place: Menu > Set destination > Favourites > tap and hold > rename.

7. Turn on you Location services in phone settings.

 

kendallandrew say:

You can do this (at least on Win Phone 8.1 on Lumia 635 with latest Nokia Drive), but the interfafce is confusing. You don't need to turn off location services. Here's how to set a favourite at a point on a map.

 

1) Launch Nokia Drive

2) Navigate around the map (by zooming out, flicking, zooming in, etc.)

3) Tap and hold at the location you want the favourite (if not quite in the right place, simply tap and hold again)

4) A pin will appear there

5) Tap on the +*   (the plus next to a star to add this as a favourite)

6) Tap "Drive to" if you like, or

7) Tap Back

8) Tap the menu at the bottom

9) Tap Favourites

10) Tap and hold the favourite just added, and then pick rename.

 

Note, you can only do this from the "home" screen. You can't select a location this way after you've picked "Set Destination". If in doubt, tap and hold <- (the back arrow) and then kill Nokia Drive, restart it, and then pick up instructions at 2)

ovidukeworld say:

change your windows default installation directory. For example: by default it is set to C:\Program Files and you want to make it to D:\Program Files.

 

For that do these followings:

 

1. Go to Run menu, and type regedit and press Enter.

2. Navigate to: HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion
2. Find ProgramFilesDir whose default should be C:\Program Files
3. Change this to D:\Program Files.

 

Restart your windows and then install the Nokia Ovi Suite.
