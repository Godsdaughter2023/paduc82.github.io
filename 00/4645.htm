i yuen say:

I get "Error! No text of specified style in document" when deleting a page from a doc with headers in Word 2007

I am editing an MS Word 2007 template. All the pages of the document have different headers, based on a Styleref pointing to heading styles in the body of the document. The header on the last page of the template contains a Styleref pointing to the appendix title that is on that page. This works fine when a document created from the template has an appendix, but when I remove the appendix page (by going to the end of the previous page and deleting everything that comes after), all of the other headers in the document change their Stylerefs to the Appendix style, and show the error "Error! No text of specified style in document." Can you tell me what I'm doing wrong? Thanks.
stefan blom say:
Presumably you are not just deleting pages, but also a section break, so that the remaining pages get the wrong header. In Word, a section break stores the properties (including headers and footers) of the preceding section. When you delete a break, the properties of the following section will be applied to the "new" section. (For more on Word sections, see http://word.mvps.org/faqs/formatting/WorkWithSections.htm.)
 
In this case, the easiest workaround is to edit the STYLEREF field so that it points to the correct heading.

Stefan Blom
Microsoft Word MVP
