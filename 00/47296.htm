Hi TSN,

Having an outdated device driver would normally caused this issue. You may let Windows to search for updated driver software for your device. For the detailed steps, please see Update drivers in Windows 10. If your Bluetooth connection was working before installing any updates, then you may try to roll back the driver for your Bluetooth device. Follow these steps:

  1. Right-click the Windows key and select Device Manager.
  2. Expand the Bluetooth category.
  3. Right-click your Bluetooth device and click Properties.
  4. Click the Driver tab, then click Roll Back Driver.
  5. Follow the on-screen instructions.

If rolling back the driver won't help, then kindly check the Device Status under the General tab of your Bluetooth Properties and see if it shows This device is working properly. If it does, then go to the Power Management tab on that same window, uncheck the Allow computer to turn off device to save power option, and click OK.
