a. user say:

Hi,
 
Which messenger\program are you using to make a call?
 
When you hear your own voice in your headset, whether you are communicating via VoIP technology or via a networking service, you are experiencing a phenomenon known as "Talker echo."


· The most common cause of talker echo occurs when you are using a headset with imbalanced audio. Generally, this means that the volume in the earpiece is louder than the microphone, which leads to the noise from the earpiece (which is naturally delayed between one or two seconds) transferring over the microphone a second time, thus resulting in the delayed echo sound.
· Another issue that can cause echo is a dirty microphone. Any sort of particulate on a microphone, no matter how big or small, can reflect sound waves back into the microphone, and cause echo.
· If you are using a headset for a VoIP conversation, echo can come about as a result of the connection speeds differing between the different parties. When someone on a faster speed speaks, there is a good chance they will hear themselves speaking when the person using the slower connection receives the audio.
 
I suggest you to optimize your microphone and voice settings.
 
How to adjust microphone volume
 
To adjust the volume for your microphone in Windows 7 or Windows Vista, follow these steps:
  1. Click Start type mmsys.cpl in the Start Search box, and then press ENTER.
  2. In the Sound dialog box, click the Recording tab.
  3. Click Microphone, and then click Properties.

  2. Select or clear the Microphone Boost check box.
  3. Click the Levels tab.
  4. Adjust the volume slider to the level that you want, and then click OK.

Try following and check after each step -

1. Run Keyboard Troubleshooter
Type Troubleshoot in Windows Start Search box > Click Troubleshoot > Scroll down and click Keyboard > Run the troubleshooter > Follow onscreen directions

2. Update Keyboard Driver
Windows Key+X (Or right click on Start) > Click Device Manager > Expand Keyboards > Right click on Standard PS/2 Keyboard > Click Update Driver Software > Click Browse my computer for driver software > Click Let my pick from a list of device drivers on my computer > Choose Standard PS/2 Keyboard > Click Next to update

3. Turn off filter keys in Ease of Access
Type ease in Windows Start Search box > Click Ease of Access > Click Make the Keyboard easier to use > Ensure Turn on filter Keys is not checked

4. Try repairing your Office installation. First try Quick Repair (takes 5 - 10 mins) , if that doesn't help then try Online Repair (Takes more than 30 mins but less than 1 hour in general)

How to repair instructions -

Hi,

Thanks for posting your query to Microsoft forum. I will certainly help you with this.

You can sync your computer's setting through OneDrive. Please go through the Microsoft help article below to learn how to sync PC settings and apps.

Sync settings between PCs with OneDrive

The other way is to get Windows Store apps by signing in.

1. Launch the Windows Store from the Start Screen of the other PC you want to transfer apps to and right-click on any space in the Store window. A bar at the top will be displayed.

2. Click on ‘Your Apps’ to view all apps downloaded and purchased by the particular account till date.

Note: If none of the installed apps are displayed, don’t worry. Make sure the other PC is in your list of trusted devices. All you’ve got to do is click on the confirmation link sent to you once you link your local account to your Microsoft account.

3. Now click on ‘Apps not installed on this PC.’ from the drop-down menu under the ‘My Apps’ section. You can select the apps you want to install.
