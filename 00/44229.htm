daavee say:

Have you considered performing a Clean Install of Windows XP (rather than a Repair Install or a Parallel Install)? This may be the only way out of your conundrum. However, there is a lot of work involved. You might wind up needing a professional technician doing this for you.

If you are interested in performing a Clean Install, it's crucial all your files are backed up to another medium (external hard drive is recommended). It is also recommended to have all your hardware drivers ready to install.

Finally, what is the make and model of your PC? It's possible it has a hidden recovery partition on the hard drive, which (assuming the hard drive is healthy) might be the easiest solution for you.

Partitions can be thought of us subdivisions of your hard drive. Sometimes people (or PC manufacturers) create several partitions on a hard drive. One example (if they were created by a PC manufacturer):

1st partition (very small): Utility partition -- this can be used to check the health of various hardware components

2nd partition (larger): Restore or Recovery partition -- many manufacturers include a hidden recovery partition which enables you to return the PC to its original brand-new condition. If this is the case, there is a good chance (but not 100%) that the manufacturer did not provide an XP installation CD.

3rd partition: For Windows XP operating system and installed programs

Also, there could be a 4th partition (perhaps created by the user but it could also be there from the factory): For data, such as Office documents, mp3s, videos, etc.
