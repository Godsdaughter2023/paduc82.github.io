93tid say:

The problem is that even though it does not support 4G, the OS still tries to use 4G.  

That is simply not possible. If the hardware does not support 4G nobody would waste their time creating and distributing an OS version which does support 4G, especially for a bottom range handset like the 520. If the speed setting option is removed from the firmware version currently on your 520, that means it is locked to the highest possible speed supported by the hardware, i.e. in your case 3G.

Please note that outside the US the term 3G generally includes HSDPA/HSUPA speeds, sometimes referred to as 3.5G/3.75G and displaying on the screen as H or resp. H+. Certain US mobile operators have marketed these services as "4G", although they are not.

Have you checked for available firmware updates for your handset ? It seems to be still running on Cyan and I believe it should be able to get Denim, although I am not sure at present whether AT&T actually approved it for their 520s.
