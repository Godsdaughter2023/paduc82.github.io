How can I keep my photos their original pixel size?

When I put photos in the pages of my power point presentation (Office 2007), they are reduced in size. I mean a 300 KB photo will be reduced to a 30 KB size. This greatly defeats the purpose of my using the best quality pictures that I can. Power Point from Office 2003 does not do this. Can I adjust something to stop it?
steve rindsberg say:

The size of the PPT/PPTX file doesn't necessarily mean anything regarding your images.  It certainly *might* but it doesn't necessarily.

When you insert a picture from file, PowerPoint converts the picture to one of three formats (GIF, PNG, JPG) depending on the original.  All three formats can compress images *losslessly* ... in other words, they might be smaller but there will be no loss of quality.  So depending on the file format of your original images, the PPTX might well be smaller. 

But that aside, I would *never* archive photos in PowerPoint.  Use it for creating slide shows of your photos, sure, but not as their main storage medium.  That's simply too risky.

Data Validation list in a seperate workbook? - Microsoft Community

jdear say:

Data Validation list in a seperate workbook?

I have one master account list and 5 or 6 workbooks that need to reference it with a dropdown list to ensure the correct name is used.  Currently I am copying and pasting the master list into each of the other workbooks everytime there is an update.  I can't figure out how to have the dropdown validation list reference another, Master, workbook so I don't have to update a worksheet in the individual workbooks each time.  Help please!  :)

rajesh c. say:

Try the following:

Assume that you have your five workbooks as A.xlsx, B.xlsx,...E.xlsx (let us call these as source workbooks)

and

you have one master workbook (say M.xlsx)

Now,

1. In each of your source workbook, you need to create a specific place

    where you will have your latest unique account list from that particular source workbook

2. In your Master workbook, you will have to then (using SUMPRODUCT function)

    access the source workbook to fetch the account list.

    The reason I am suggesting to use SUMPRODUCT function is because, SUMPRODUCT

    function when used with INDIRECT function, can help you fetch your desired data even from

    a closed workbook

3. Once the account list is fetched from individual source workbooks, you can have another

    formula in master workbook which will (a) remove duplicates and (b) also sort the data

    (this can be easily achieved using an array formula - you can search for this - there are some readymade formulas available out there)

4. The final output can then be linked as a dynamic range to your data validation

Hope this helps
