Perhaps a few general points will help put you on the right track.

The program provides no means of specifying values for individual Row Heights or Column Widths. The value boxes in the Formatting Palette [Size, Rotation and Ordering?] pertain to the overall dimensions of the table object regardless of what portion of the table is selected when you change the values.

Tables in PowerPoint are graphic objects. As such, dimensions can be changed by using a dragging method:
  • To change the overall dimensions of the table object use the 'grabbers' marked on the table frame just like scaling an image. The midpoint grabbers on each of the 4 sides are used for height & width, the corners for adjusting H/W simultaneously.
  • You can hold the Shift key while dragging a corner in order to maintain proportion. As you change the H/W of the table object all rows & columns will adjust accordingly.
  • The H of multiple rows or the W of multiple columns cannot be adjusted at the same time except by changing the overall size of the table object. IOW, one row/column at a time or all at once.
  • To change the H of an individual row drag its bottom gridline. To change the W of an individual column drag its right gridline.
  • By default, PPT uses a 4:3 aspect ratio for an online show -- 10"Wx7.5"H
  • You may want to have a look at the pertinent topics in Help> PowerPoint Help [Offline Help] available if you use the search term table.
