Hopefully, the other posts have answered your question.

After you get your system restored with all your software loaded, let me recommend that you make an image of your system. I use and teach Acronis True Image to do this. I can't tell you how many times this has saved me over the years.

Acronis can be purchased for under $50 and scaled-down versions are the free tools that are available with the purchase of Seagate, Western Digital and perhaps other external hard drives. Seagate calls their version "DiscWizard" and Western Digital calls their's Acronis True Image for WD. You can purchase hard drives with USB 3 ports starting at about $70 for a Terabyte (1 TB) and you can find 4 TB and 5 TB drives for less than $160. USB 3 is about 10x faster than USB 2 (good reason to update your old drive if they are USB 2). And, if you have the money, it is most advantageous to keep 2 or 3 external drives on hand and rotate your backups among those drives in case one fails on you when you need it most.

With an Acronis image, you can install a new hard drive and simply use Acronis to restore your backup to the new drive. Once that is done, you have ALL of your applications installed as of the date you made that image. This includes your Microsoft Office Professional and all other software that you have purchased, installed, registered and configured. Be sure to make a new image  after you install or update any software; otherwise, you will have to re-install the software that you added after creating that image. 

You can also the Acronis image to recover from system problems such as viruses, when systems get too slow, or you encounter any other problems. Before restoring the older image, make a new image of your BAD system, if that is possible. You can use a rescue disk made from Acronis to do this. After you restore your older image, you can navigate your bad image to find all your current files in your Documents folder or in any other folder on your system and copy them from the image on your external drive to the drive in your PC!

There are other products on the market to create images and they may work just a well. However, I don't have experience with any of the other products.

Some people might recommend using the Restore Points on your system. These may help you with your issue and be easier and much faster to use than reverting back to an earlier image. I favor doing that if it works. But, for the worst case scenario (e.g. a disc failure), if you have an image of your system, you always have the option to restore everything to that time.

embedded_system say: The manual does also tell (section 12. Media/recorder/Record sound):
"Note: If push to talk is not available on your phone menu, you can also press the PTT key to start the Voice recorder."

I guess you have push to talk in the phone menu, so you can't use the PTT key to start the Voice recorder.

It might be that there are SW variants for the 6021 which exclude the PTT feature. Such a SW variant would allow you to use the PTT key to start the Voice recorder.

As a workaround, you could use the right selection key to start the voice recorder:
Menu/Settings/Personal Shortcuts/Right selection key/Voice recorder
