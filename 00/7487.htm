brookzhang_msft8047 say:

Hi RichardSmith6330,

Use the Get-MailboxFolderStatistics cmdlet to retrieve information about the folders in a specified mailbox, including the number and size of items in the folder, the folder name and ID, and other information. It retrieves information based on per folders’ level. Totally items in per folders are listed rather than the whole mailbox.

While, Get-MailboxStatistics cmdlet is aimed to obtain information about a whole mailbox. Besides folders information, a mailbox also includes many other types of information like inbox rules, last logon time, mail tip, etc. In addition, you can get the move history or a move report of a completed move request. It retrieves information based on the mailbox’s level, which means it shows the actual items of the mailbox.

Therefore, it’s normal that the items count Get-MailboxStatistics gets is larger than the one Get-MailboxFolderStatistics gets.

If there is anything unclear, please post back. We will be glad to be assistive.
