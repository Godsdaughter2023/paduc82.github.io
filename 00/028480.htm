macropod say:

Hi Lenny,

One way is to ensure the pictures only have enough pixels for the resolution at which they'll be printed. In general, nothing over 360 dpi is required. So, if a picture is to be printed as, say, a 5"*3.5" image, re-scale a copy of it before inserting into Word, with dimensions of 1800*1260 pixels. Changing the amount of jpg compression (before inserting into Word) may also help.

Of course, you can also minimize the Word file's size by inserting links to the images, rather than the images themselves. This is useful if particular images are liable to frequent changes. The downside is that you need to keep the images in whatever folder they're in when the links are created, or the links will fail (you can get Word to change the link paths though, if necessary) and it won't make the Word file any more responsive during editing. 

Cheers,
