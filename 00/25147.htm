bonair say:

Is there a way to print a summary of the track changes in a word doc?

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:
I have an Office 7 word doc and I want to print a summary of JUST the track changes.  Is this possible?


Try using a macro containing the following code.  It should create a new document that contains a table with the Author, the revision type and the affected text in columns.  You would want to adjust the column widths and probably add a header row to the table manually after the macro has run.

Dim arev As Revision
Dim docsource As Document, doctarget As Document
Set docsource = ActiveDocument
Set doctarget = Documents.Add
With docsource
    For Each arev In .Revisions
        doctarget.Range.InsertAfter arev.Author & vbTab & arev.Type & vbTab & arev.Range & vbCr
    Next arev
End With
doctarget.Range.ConvertToTable

Create multiple copies of business cards on Word 2010 - Microsoft Community

a. user say:

Create multiple copies of business cards on Word 2010

I am posting on behalf of a customer. She's trying to make business cards and would like to know if she can duplicate one that was made to the others rather than typing the information in on every single one of the cards. Please help.

Thank you.
suzanne s. barnhill say:
Since business cards are set up as "labels," and labels are set up using a table, the simple answer is yes. If you copy the contents of one card (table cell), then select the entire table and paste, the contents will be replicated in every cell. More or less. Sometimes there are problems with graphics. At the end of his article at http://www.gmayor.com/graphics_on_labels.htm, Graham Mayor offers a macro to propagate labels of this type.
