Hello,

We realize your concern in this regard.

Your copy of Windows 10 should automatically be activated so long as you didn't make any significant hardware changes to your device (such as replacing the hard drive or motherboard). If you are planning or made substantial hardware changes to your device, such as replacing the hard drive or motherboard, try activating Windows by phone. Type SLUI 04 in the search box on the taskbar, select SLUI 04, and then follow the instructions on the screen to activate Windows.

In simple, operating system will be stored in the Hard disk. It will gets activated with the valid product key and that information will be stored in the BIOS. So, making any hardware changes (I.e. moving and connecting hard disk to different computer) will leave you in activation problems. For further assistance related to hardware changes, you can get in touch with device manufacturer. Hope they will guide you with proper information and assistance.

Hi MissyNeff,

When you say that you would want to disable the touch screen keyboard, are you referring to the On-Screen keyboard? If so, you can disable it by following these steps:

  1. Click on Start and type in Settings on the Search bar.
  2. Click on Ease of Access.
  3. Click on Keyboard.
  4. Under the On-Screen Keyboard option, move the slider to Off.
  5. Unplug and re-plug the physical keyboard.
