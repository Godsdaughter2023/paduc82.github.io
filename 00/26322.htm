joeu2004 say: Pottsz wrote:
I would like to enter a 3 decimal number in one cell, have the number rounded down to x.000, x.025, x.050, or x.075 and the result appear in another cell.
For example, 1.024 would round down to 1.000.  
1.049 would round down to 1.025.
1.092 would round down to 1.075.

Since you explicitly asked for "round down" with good examples to demonstrate clearly that is your intent, we should give you what you asked for.

Ostensibly, you could write:

=FLOOR(A1,0.025)

where A1 contains 1.024, 1.049, 1.092 or whatever.

However, there two points to keep in mind.


1. Those numbers might appear as such due to formatting to 3 decimal places, but the actual value might be more or less.  That might give rise to surprises.  For example, if A1 is 1.0246, it might appear as 1.025.  So it might come as a surprise when FLOOR(A1,0.025) returns 1.000.

To avoid such surprises, it might be prudent to write:

=FLOOR(ROUND(A1,3),0.025)


2. Most non-integers cannot be represented exactly.  In particular, 0.025 cannot be represented exactly.  So FLOOR(...,0.025) might return some surprising results in a few "inexplicable" cases.

To avoid such surprises, it might be more reliable to write:

=ROUNDDOWN(ROUND(A1*1000,0)/25,0)*25/1000
