john w. vinson say:

The checkbox (or yes/no field, to be more precise - a checkbox isn't data, it's a display tool!) should simply not exist in your table.

 

What you can do instead is have two tables in a one to many relationship: your table of people, and a separate table of certifications. This table would have a PersonID as a foreign key to the people table, a certification date, an expiration date, and (if you have more than one type of certification) a certifiction type field. You could display this information using a Form based on the people table, with a Subform (using the PersonID as the master/child link field) to show all that person's certifications. If you wish, this subform could be filtered to show only active certifications, or only expired ones. You could also filter the MAIN form to show only those people with (or without) certifications.

 

The printout of new certificates could simply be a Report joining the two tables with appropriate criteria; it could be exported directly as XML. You could even if you wish include a PrintedDate field to indicate that the certificate has been printed.

john w. vinson say:

Well, you need to go through the exercise of correctly defining your Entities and their Attributes. Each kind of Entity gets its own table; the fields in that table hold that entity's Attributes.

 

In this case, you have at least the following entities: Counselors; CertificationTypes; Certifications; Extensions; CEUs (probably). Each of these should have its own table with its own fields. For example, if you have six types of certifications, could one counselor have more than one certification type?

 

Once you have the tables right then - don't be shocked here - you may never need to look at them again. Tables are for data storage! They are not for data entry or display. You say you want to "go to the main database table" - well, don't! The table stays "under the hood"; you would go to a Form, based on a Query which could use multiple criteria to identify each counselor, their certification status, their due dates, and so on. Everything you're asking for can be accomplished; but it will be accomplished using Forms, Queries, and maybe some code, not directly in the Table.
