You need to select bitstream audio over HDMI, not 5.1 uncompressed.  The optical cable you are running from your TV to the amp can only carry compressed surround sound, like Dolby or DTS.  Optical cables only have the bandwidth for uncompressed audio in 2 channels, aka stereo.  If your TV is only sending the front speakers out of the 5.1 mix, that would be why you hear all kinds of background noise and not people speaking.  Picking either DTS or Dolby should solve this problem.

saltgrass say:

Type the ncpa.cpl command in a run box and open the connections control panel. 

Right click the Wireless adapter and select Status.  From here you can check all the addresses.  For instance, you should have an IP address which agrees with your network.  Normally something like 192.168.1.50 or 192.168.0.50.  If you have something like a 169.x.x.x then you are not on a private network and the system is not recognizing your Network but using a general address which may limit access to certain areas.

Also check for a Gateway address which would be the same as your Router, usually.

Then a DNS address which may be the same as your Gateway or something else but it should be present.

Of course the wireless security has to be configured correctly for your network, are you familiar with that?  Basically, if you left click your wireless icon you should see a list of available networks and whether they are secured or unsecured.  If you try to connect to a secured one it may ask you for a password.
