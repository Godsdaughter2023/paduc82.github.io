Creating pop up boxes - Microsoft Community

dahiestand say:

Creating pop up boxes

I’m looking for a way to accomplish one of the following:

1.  Hover the mouse, over a particular word or icon, in the text of my document; and, have a text box, of my making, appears with information.   Like a mini-help screen.  Press Esc (or something similar), or move the mouse away from the word or icon, to make the text box disappear.

2.  Place the mouse over a particular word or icon; and press either the left or right click.  A text box, of my making, appears with information.  Like a mini-help screen.  Press Esc (or something similar), or move the mouse away from the word or icon, to make the text box disappear.

I’m working with Office versions 2003 to 2010 on Window’s Versions 7

jay freedman say:

Your first proposal can be done only if the information you want to show can fit in 256 characters or less. Insert a bookmark next to the word or icon. Select the word or icon, and then insert a hyperlink that points to the bookmark (so that following the hyperlink won't move the user's cursor). In the Insert Hyperlink dialog, click the ScreenTip button and insert the information in the ScreenTip text box. This holds a maximum of 256 characters; also, when the mouse hovers over the hyperlink, the usual "Ctrl+click to follow link" line will appear at the bottom of the ScreenTip.

The second proposal can be done with macro programming. This is more complex to set up, but also more flexible -- the amount of text isn't limited, and you can include additional programming to make the information interactive. In the document, insert a MACROBUTTON field whose display text is the word or icon, and that points to a macro in the document. The macro must be written (it can't be recorded) to examine the field's code and insert the appropriate text into a userform, which it then displays. If you need help with this method, post back in this thread.
