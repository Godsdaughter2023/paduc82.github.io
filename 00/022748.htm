Well, I couldn't leave it alone after I saw this post on an Mobilitysite thread.  It sounded like a reasonable solution that might be worth considering.  Here is what the poster said:

 

I've been using activesync for a few years now and this is the first time I've had this same problem described here. I recently reformatted my HDD. Activesync synchronised fine (or so I thought), until I discovered some missing contacts on my PPC which were in Outlook. I tried the suggestions posted here but in vain. In the end, I don't know what solved the problem but it's synching fine now. [My system: Winxp SP2, WM6, Activesync 4.5, Outlook 2003.]
Here's what I did :
1. Disconnected the PPC from the computer.
2. Went to C:\Program Files\Common Files\System\MSMAPI\1033. Ran SCANPST.EXE and pointed it to the associated pst file I was using. Allowed it to repair corruptions in the file.
3. On the PPC, went to Start-Programs-Activesync-Tools-Options. Deleted the partnership with the computer.
4. Uninstalled activesync.
5. Deleted remaining activesync folder: C:\Documents and Settings\<YOUR USER LOGIN NAME>\Application Data\Microsoft\ActiveSync\
6. Restart the computer and soft-reset the PPC.
7. Reinstalled activesync.
8. Connected the PPC and allowed computer to detect it and establish a fresh partnership.

I ran a sync afterwards and checked to see if the missing contacts were back in my PPC. They were.
