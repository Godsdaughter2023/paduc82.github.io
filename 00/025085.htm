How to I get the captions in the floating tables to correctly number? Or see the anchors so I can reposition them in order. The floating tables have absolute positions. 
jay freedman say:

I don't believe there is any way to make the anchors of floating tables visible. I'm not sure whether this was intentional, but it's a PITA.

The likely problem is that all the tables are anchored in the same paragraph, so their order (as far as Word is concerned) is determined by the order in which the tables appear in the XML of the document.

If you don't want to mess with the XML directly (and I don't recommend trying it unless you know what you're doing), a possible workaround is this:

  • If there are visible text paragraphs on the page, and there are at least as many paragraphs as there are tables, then skip the next step.
  • Type one new paragraph mark (Enter) on the page for each table on that page. Turn on nonprinting characters so you can see the paragraph marks.
  • One at a time, cut each table to the clipboard, move the cursor to the corresponding paragraph or mark, and paste it. Since the tables are set to absolute positions, they should reappear in the same locations -- but the anchors will now be in separate paragraphs, with no ambiguity about their order.
  • Update the numbering fields in the captions if necessary.

Excel 2010 Simple Math Error - Microsoft Community

bigbreeze say:

Excel 2010 Simple Math Error

I've seen a number of discussions regarding supposedly bad Excel math, but I am finding that simple math involving only one or two decimal places comes out wrong, and I do not see an explanation anywhere. Try this in a new, fresh worksheet:

Cell A1 type in 1.1

Cell A2 type in 2.2

Cell A3 type in 3.3

Cell A4 type in =SUM(A1+A2-A3)

Cell A5 type in =A1+A1-A3

Cell A6 type in =(A1+A2-A3)

Both cell A4, A5 and A6 should result in 0, yet only A5 does. I agree that the value in A4 and A6 is very close to zero, but it is not zero, and this causes a LOT of problems when using conditional formatting, Boolean logic, vlookups, etc., etc.

I thought it might be the parenthesis, but if you change cells A1:A3 to 2.2, 3.3, and 5.5, A4 and A6 will now both correctly calculate exactly 0.

I've tried this on different machines, with the same result. Have not tried on different versions of excel.

How can this be OK? Why different answers to the same simple problem? And why not consistently correct or incorrect, on such simple numbers?

Thanks for any insight. I use a lot of spreadsheet that have lots of formulas, and I'm finding that these "rounding" issues are killing my conditional formatting, logic and lookups, but only sometimes (unpredictable) and this has not always been the case.

joeu2004 say:

BigBreeze wrote:

I still do not understand why, in Excel, using "=A1+A2-A3" results in exactly zero, but when using "Sum(A1+A2-A3)" on the same data the result is something other than zero.

You are correct:  there should not be any difference.  In fact, there is a difference between =A1+A2-A3 and =A1+A2-A3-0 (!) as well as =(A1+A2-A3), as you discovered.

The reason is due to the dubious "close to zero" heuristic that is described in the aforementioned KB 78113.  It is implemented inconsistently, and it applies only to the last subtraction (or addition of a number with an opposite sign).

BigBreeze wrote:

There shouldn't be a difference in the order of operations, or should there?

Yes, order does matter sometimes, albeit not in your example, even ignoring the dubious "close to zero" heuristic.

The reason is complicated.  In part, it has to do with the fact that Intel-compatible CPUs use 80-bit binary floating-point internally, but Excel converts pairwise operations to 64-bit binary floating-point.

Consequently, the same arithmetic operations in Excel and VBA might yield slightly different results.

a. user say:

Hi,

 

Welcome to Microsoft Community.

 

I know how frustrating it is to be unable to receive important emails. We have to consider several causes why you were not able to receive emails such as:

       

A. Filter Settings of the account.

 

To ensure that your account filter settings are not causing incoming email not to be delivered to your account, I suggest that you check your settings by following the steps below:

 

Check your Junk folder

1. Sign in to https://outlook.com

2. Click on Junk on the left pane.

3. Check to see if the missing mail is in this folder.

4. If you see any messages that should have been delivered to the Inbox , click the box on the left of each message to put a check mark and then click the Not Junk button.

 

Check your Blocked sender list

1. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the gear icon, and then click More mail settings. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.

2. Click Blocked senders.

3. In the Blocked senders list, select the name or domain (the part of an e-mail address that follows the @ sign, such as hotmail.com) that you now want to receive mail from, and then click << Remove from list.

 

Add the email address to your Safe senders list

1. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the gear icon, and then click More mail settings. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.

2. Click Safe Senders.

3. In the text box, type in the email address or domain you’d like to always receive email from, and then click Add to list >>.

 

Check your Email Rules

1. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the gear icon, and then click More mail settings. Under Customizing Outlook, click Rules for sorting new messages.

2. Perform one of the actions as per your requirement:

Click Edit next to the rule that you want to edit.

Or

Choose the rule that you want to delete and click Delete.

Note: Not all international characters are supported as filters.

 

Check your Junk Mail Settings

1. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the gear icon, and then click More mail settings.

2. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.

3. Under Choose a junk e-mail filter, choose the level of protection that you want, and then click Save. If the Exclusive level was set, messages from senders not on your Contacts or Safe sender’s lists will have been sent to the Junk e-mail folder.

 

Check your Reply-to address

1. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the gear icon, and then click More mail settings. Under Writing email, click Reply-to address.

2. Select Current e-mail address radio button.

3. Click Save.

 

B. Reply to address option

 

If you don’t want people to reply to an address other than the one you have right now, please follow the steps below to make sure that you are receiving the emails in your account:

1. Sign in to your Outlook account.
2. Click the gear icon on the upper part of the page.
3. Click More mail settings in the right part of the page.
4. Under Writing email, click Reply-to address.
5. Choose Current email address and then Save.

C. Email forwarding

 

You can also check your account if it has enabled auto forwarding to another email address. Follow the steps below to make sure you don’t have this feature enabled:

1. Sign in to your Outlook account. Go to your Inbox.
2. Click the gear icon on the upper right part of the page and then More mail settings.
3. Under Managing your account, click Email forwarding.
4. On the Forwarding page, click Don't forward.
5. Click Done.

 

D. Mail Client Settings

 

You might not also send or receive emails due to incorrect configuration when accessing account using a Mail Client. For this reason, please reconfigure your account by following the settings of the link below:

 

Send and receive Hotmail from an email app

 

E. Your inbox is full

You need to delete your emails and leave the ones that are important to save space.

 

F. 3rd Party Email account.

 

If you are using University email account such as (.edu) or other 3rd party account, it is possible that there is an issue with your network/server. You might need to contact your domain administrator for further assistance. They may have changed some settings in your domain's system that's why you are having problems receiving emails.

> Have you tried to send a test mail to your own email address? did you receive the test email?
