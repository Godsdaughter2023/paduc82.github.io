1. Messages were manually deleted either due to accidental deletion or were moved or deleted due to your account filter settings.

3. You are using an e-mail client or a third party spam filtering software to access your account.

5. If the feature Conversation Threading feature is turned on.

6. If you use Sweep to schedule a cleanup.

amblors say:

It depends on how the network is setup like psychomania said.

In my school, two options exist, if your hall is within range of the wireless network, you could try connecting to the wireless network and see if it connects you.

If it doesnt then you problem would need to register your wireless MAC with the school network so they provide your mobile with access (at least that is the way it works here).

 

An alternative to this, wot i do is since the schools wireless network doesnt get to me and i have the wired connection, i create an adhoc wireless connection (disable internet connection sharing and allow IP set to automatic configuration)

When that is done, you bridge both wired and wireless connection, and the you have an AP.

 

Search for the wireless connection with you phone and you should be able to access the net thereafter.
