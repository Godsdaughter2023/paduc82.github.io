rdrewd2 say:

Just guessing based on your post, but you probably activated the hidden administrator account by entering:

net user administrator /active:yes

It's not deleting any of your data or affecting your other account by merely enabling it.  But it can, based on what you do while using it.  It is activating a separate account that is built into the OS and meant/intended to be used as an advanced troubleshooting measure so that you can still get into your computer and make changes and fix things if your original account has problems or becomes corrupted.

But in general, you don't want to use that unless you really need to.  In the hidden admin account, it's really easy for a novice user to screw up the OS and/or other accounts (as it has pretty much unlimited priveleges) and effectively "brick" a system.  If you're not absolutely sure of what you're doing when using it, I'd advise not using it.  Recommend re-hiding it (until you have a problem and need to access it) by going back into the admin powershell/command prompt and typing:

net user administrator /active:no

If you want to play around with a second account, you can always create a "safer-to-use" one by going to:

Settings/Accounts/Sign in with a local acount instead and creating a second account, or Settings/accounts/Family & other people/ Add someone else...  Then you can choose your main account or your newly created account from your sign in screen (they'll be there listed and selectable on the lower left).

The explorer shell including the non-dpi-scaling-aware win32 applications aren’t refreshed when the monitor is connected. Icons and apps continue to draw at the higher DPI making things huge when monitor is connected; and when restarted or signed out/in, they are correctly sized on the monitor until disconnected.

You may try the following workaround and check if it works:

  • Lock the console and tap ‘Switch User’. This will disconnect your user session from the console and when you unplug your docking station, your user session will be unaware of the resolution changes.

  • Plug the PC back into the docking station at its new location. When logged in, the existing user session will be reattached without going through the intervening PC’s native display resolution.

Error: "Cannot find Extender":
This usually means that the Extender can't be found on the same network as your computer, or that a firewall on your computer is blocking UPnP communication. Try the following steps and check if it helps.

Step 1:

Enable multicast and UPnP technology on your network router

Some routers might be configured to block ports for UPnP technology and multicast network traffic. You'll need to configure your router to allow traffic through these ports in order for your Extender and your computer to communicate.

The steps for changing router settings will vary depending on the type of router. Typically, your router's administration page is accessible via a web browser, and will have options to enable UPnP technology and multicast network traffic. For specific information, check the information that came with your router.

Step 2:

Try to setup Xbox 360 as an extender again.

Set up a Windows Media Center Extender

Step 3:

If you still see the error message, it might be because a firewall is blocking the Extender. If you have more than one firewall installed, check the error message to see if it specifies which firewall is blocking your Extender.
