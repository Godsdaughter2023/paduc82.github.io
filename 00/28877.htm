Barb,

Unlike a small handful of truly unfortunate individuals with stalkers or malicious ex-whatevers, the scammers simply don't or won't take the time to spy on those they attack.

The reason is simple, it doesn't make them any money and so they'd be better off to just move on to the next thousand fake calls or popup messages, which result in the next 10 who take the bait and the one whom actually gives them the money, access or other personal information they're looking for.

The truth is that of the literally millions of such calls daily, only a small percentage actually answer and a much smaller group pay, but since the robocalls are automated and the scammers typically give up quickly on those who don't seem to be fooled, the time spent on the few who do is still worth it.

That's why the many calls a day are to be expected, since literally hundreds of such scam call centers are operating out of countries all over the world, all trying to access the same rich fools in the US and a few other developed countries where the level of wealth far exceeds the mental acuity of many of their citizens, whether due to age or other factors.

None of this is rocket science, it's relatively simple computer science, but since only a small percentage of US or other developed countries' population are trained in computer technology, it's relatively easy to trick most people into doing things they know they shouldn't if they'd just stop and think for a few seconds.  I know, I did some of this professionally for businesses at all levels from small banks to large defense contractors and could often get even bank presidents or other important individuals to give up information they never should, all with mostly simple false emails, web pages, and follow-up telephone calls to numbers found online or that they provided themselves.

So when I mentioned being paranoid, it's a two edged sword, since it's always good to be paranoid that someone may be out to get you in this worldwide malicious environment because they definitely are.  The only thing though is that it's typically not for the reason(s) that the individual might think and more often it's simply for the money or other information that can easily be turned into cash as well.
