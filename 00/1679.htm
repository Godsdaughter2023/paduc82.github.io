Try this. Set the variable NumCopies (Currently 3) to the amount of copies you want.

Sub somesub()
Dim NumCopies As Long, Lastrow As Long
Dim c As Range, MyRange As Range
Dim LastRow2 As Long
'Change this to the amount of time to copy
NumCopies = 3
LastRow2 = 1
Lastrow = Cells(Rows.Count, "A").End(xlUp).Row
Set MyRange = Range("A1:A" & Lastrow)
For Each c In MyRange
Cells(LastRow2, "B").Resize(NumCopies) = c.Value
LastRow2 = LastRow2 + NumCopies
Next
End Sub

To make an active account becomes inactive, it must not been used for 270 days. quikky say: Hi JanKara, there is another way to free up the account for use as an alias on other accounts in just 30 days, rather than 270/360.

Let's assume the original account was a hotmail.com address, and the new one is an outlook.com address. The outlook.com address was created as a new account, when what should have happened is the hotmail.com address was renamed to the outlook address.

To free up the outlook.com address sooner:

1. Sign in to the outlook.com account.
2. Rename it to a new address. The initial outlook.com address will be turned into an alias for the new address.
3. Delete the alias
4. The outlook.com address which became an alias will now be free to use on any account (either as an alias, or as a primary renamed account) after just 30 days, rather than 270 or 365 if it had not been turned into an alias.

This works, and has been suggested on other threads by other moderators. It's what I did, and I'm now using my shiny new outlook.com address as the primary address on my original hotmail account, having previously created a new outlook account in that name.
