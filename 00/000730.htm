The "Next Review Date" is a cell in a table that also includes an origination date (ie the date the policy went active) and a revision date, which is the last date the policy was revised. The revision date may be blank if the form has never been revised.

Essentially, what I want to do is, in the "Next Review Date" box, either add two years to the revision date--if a revision date is present--or add two years to the origination date--if the revision date cell is blank. That way, the form will be reviewed either two years following its origination or two years following its last revision.

I was able to get this to work in Excel using the formula

 =IF(ISBLANK(D3),DATE(YEAR(D1)+2,MONTH(D1),DAY(D1)),DATE(YEAR(D3)+2,MONTH(D3),DAY(D3)))

where D1 is the origination date cell, D3 is the revision date cell, and the formula was pasted in the review date cell. I'm sure this isn't the most elegant way to solve the problem, but it worked.

    hansv mvp say:

    If you first enter the values and then format them as Text, Excel still "remembers" that 1, 2 and 3 were originally numbers, so they are sorted before 1 d, 1G etc.

    If you FIRST format the column as Text and THEN enter the values, they will all be treated as text, and sorted the way you want.
