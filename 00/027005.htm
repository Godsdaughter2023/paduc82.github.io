Here is a fast, non-looping way to do what you want...

Sub Macro1()
  On Error Resume Next
  Columns("F").TextToColumns Range("F1"), xlDelimited, , , Other:=False, FieldInfo:=Array(1, 4)
  On Error GoTo 0
  Columns("F").NumberFormat = "dd mmm yyyy"
End Sub

I'm not quite sure why you think your account has a virus.  I suspect you're experiencing the results of hacking (at the email provider level) or a system infection that allowed access to that information (both perhaps once and it's done or perhaps ongoing in one or both cases).

 

Either your account is infected or has been hacked (the most likely situation) and/or your computer is infected or possibly been hacked and the information obtained from your email program (if you use an email program and not just online access through your browser and possibly even then depending on the infection).  Your e-mail address itself cannot be infected.  If the account is just being spoofed but not actually used to send emails that seem to be coming from you, then fixing the problem becomes more difficult as it's somewhat like locking the barn door after the cows are all gone.  Those "spoofing" your account and using the information in it are likely behind masked computers using TCP/IP Proxy rerouters and could be anywhere in the world.
