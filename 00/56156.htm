The Windows 10 update assistant is something that makes sure that your current Windows 10 build is up to date. If you do not want to update to a newer build, then you can uninstall the utility like a normal program. 

Some users report that the assistant is persistent - meaning that it can not be uninstalled or does not show the ability to be uninstalled and reported that the feedback below solved their problem:

1. Open task scheduler by typing "Task Scheduler" in the search bar next to the windows icon in the lower left corner (start menu). A window will pop up.

2. On the left-hand side, click Task Scheduler Library>Microsoft>Windows>UpdateOrchestrator. Once you click on "UpdateOrchestrator", you will see a list become populated in the center upper portion of the window.


3. What you want to do at this point is DISABLE "UpdateAssistant" and "UpdateAssistantCalendarRun". Voila. Windows 10 Update Assistant will be dead forever and you are free to use your perfectly working as-is PC indefinitely without interruptions.
