n short say:

Short Answer - Pull your second ram stick and place it in the slot right next to the first one.

After extensive googling I found that the Windows 10 Installer is most likely using a Window Management Instrumentation command to get total ram capacity.

http://superuser.com/questions/948320/windows-10-install-not-enough-memory

http://www.walkernews.net/2009/11/21/use-wmi-to-find-out-ram-slot-in-used-and-memory-size-of-each-slot/

Using command as administrator i ran "wmic memorychip get capacity" which would only report 1GB (1073741824) of my RAM, Even though I have 4GB installed.  wmic was showing that bank 0/1 together as 1GB, which led me to believe that my motherboard was only reporting up to 1gb per bank in dual channel mode.  I puled my second chip from dual channel position, and placed it right by the first stick, so instead of slot 0/1, my RAM was now in slot 0/2. My system now reports bank 0/1 as 1GB, and Bank 2/3 as 1GB for a total of 2GB.

Now magically the "Get windows 10 App" showed up in the tray.  I assume that this is an issue with the BIOS of this older pc (Abit AB9 Pro, C2D e6300, 4GB) based on the p965 chipset, accurately passing RAM info to the O/S.  I built this PC back in the glory days of XP, and it is still a solid machine.

Your earlier router failure was coincidental. It may have become defective, or a power outage can have scrambled its settings or it could have even been subjected to a hacking attack from the internet.

None of the internal network settings in Windows have any effect on your router or on any other external devices.
