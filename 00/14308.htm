john korchok say:

It's a known issue that PowerPoint's movie export does not include the narrations. It's not the fault of the online convertors. Playing back your exported .mov file should demonstrate that the narrations are not there before converting. If you have access to PowerPoint 2010 on Windows, exporting from it will include narrations.

For a Mac-only workflow, there are 2 workarounds:

Method 1:

  1. Open the presentation in PowerPoint.
  2. Choose File>Save as Movie.
  3. Click on the Movie Options... button
  4. Beside Background soundtrack, click the dropdown and choose Select soundtrack.
  5. Choose the Audacity file in WAV format that holds your narration.
  6. Click OK, then Save.

Method 2:

  1. Open your presentation in PowerPoint and resize the window to the size you want your video to be (i.e. 640x480 pixels, etc.).
  2. Select Slide Show>Set Up Show...
  3. Under Show type, choose Browsed by an individual (window), then click on OK.
  4. Start your Slide Show.
  5. In QuickTime Player, choose File>New Screen Recording.
  6. On the Screen Recording dialog, click the red button in the middle.
  7. Drag your mouse to select the PowerPoint window.
  8. Click on Record.
  9. Run your slideshow. When you're finished, click on the square dot inside a circle on the OS X menu bar to stop QuickTime. Save the video file.

kleinma say:

I have discovered a little bit more information, including how you can get back your old MSN page.

 

All it takes to screw things up, is to try to navigate to http://dell.msn.com.

Doing that must write some sort of cookie, and all attempts after that to get to www.msn.com will fail because it tries to redirect to a dell version of the site.

 

To fix:

Go into internet options

Go to advanced tab (last tab)

Click "Reset", and make sure to click the checkbox for "Delete personal settings"

Let it finish, then close and reopen IE

 

Now this stinks because it will reset your cookies for all your sites, and you will lose passwords you saved in IE, but it does fix the issue, assuming you don't go back to dell.msn.com and make it happen again. So just be aware of that if you saved your passwords in IE forever ago and don't remember them.

 

Another side note is if you do in fact have a dell, resetting IE to defaults may just set your homepage to a dell MSN page by default, putting you in a loop that you can't get out of. If this is the case, when you do the above to reset your settings, when you close IE, before reopening it, go into the control panel in windows, and then internet options, and change your default homepage in there to something else (like just http://www.msn.com, or google.com, or whatever), then once you reopen IE, it should be good to go.
