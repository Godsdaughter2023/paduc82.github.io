      dim strSQL as string

jason (office engineer) say:

There are three types of layouts available right now in the Navigation pane:  vertical scrolling, horizontal scrolling, and one optimized for presentation.  It sounds like you're asking for a way to get text and an image on one screen in the third type of navigation layout without it being overlaid.  If that is what you're asking for, put your text in its own text card next to the image you want on the same screen and then add both of those cards to a group.  That will cause all cards in the group to display on one screen.

Let me know if this is what you're asking for or if your question was slightly different.

Jason

jason (office engineer) say:

One way to get around this would be to put a new, empty heading right after the one that has your text/image on it.  That will keep it from showing across the top of the next screens.  This happens, right now, because a heading is used to indicate a new section and the heading title is put on the top of all screens of content in that section.

Hope this helps!
