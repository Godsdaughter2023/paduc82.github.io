Hi Fabian_533,

 

Appreciated explanations you made, I want to further explain my reply above.

 

When you create a team site, it will accordingly create a O365 group. This O365 group has 3 permission levels, which are Ownesr, Members, and Visitors. These groups are directly connected to the SharePoint groups (Owner, Member and Visitor ,and the according permission level is Full control, Read and Edit ). When you change the Edit permission to a Member group, it will have conflict with the O365 group permission. Then when SharePoint site sync, it will revert back the group permission and cause the behavior like you said.

 

As a workaround, I suggest you to go to Outlook to remove users you don’t want to give Edit permission and add them to the Site Visitors. (as I said in scenario step 2). For your reference, this article can help you about the group permission in SharePoint:

Default SharePoint groups
