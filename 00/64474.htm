What to do if you forget your Windows password

If you've forgotten your Windows password and you're on a domain, you should contact your system administrator to reset your password. If you're on a workgroup (most home users are on a workgroup), you can reset your password by using a password reset disk or by using an administrator account.

Create a password reset disk:

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows7/Create-a-password-reset-disk

Reset your Windows password:
http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows7/Reset-your-Windows-password

If you forget the administrator password and don't have a password reset disk or another administrator account, you won't be able to reset the password. If there are no other user accounts on the computer, you won't be able to log on to Windows and will need to re-install Windows.

eg x thee elf say:

Are you referring to the apps on the Home tab or the Pins tab? To pin Internet Explorer, go to your My Games & Apps section, click the Menu button (the old Start button) and click Pin to Home. Any pinned apps will show on the Pins tab. To unpin an app, go to the Pins tab, highlight that item, click the Menu button and select Unpin. The apps on the Home tab is more less recent history. On the Pins tab, the only re-arranging you can do (without un-pinning and re-pinning )is using the Move to Front option, which will put the Front app in the upper right-hand corner. Probably the easiest way to do this is to create a list of the pinned apps you want (most important at the top, least important at the bottom), then using that list and going from bottom to top click each app and select move to front. Once you are done the last one you Move to Front will be the top on on your list. Hope this helps!
