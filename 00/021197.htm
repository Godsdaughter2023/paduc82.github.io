The stadarard formulat for when I know the Cost is it:

=SUM ((Net Sales – Cost) / Net Sales) * 100

 

So when I don't have the cost how would I write the formula in Excel?

 

 

 

joeu2004 say: Someone wrote:
I am trying to calculate the GP% in a chart that doesn't have the cost displayed.  The data table displays for example:
Net Sales: $100
GP $: 10
GP%: ?
The stadarard formulat for when I know the Cost is it:
=SUM ((Net Sales – Cost) / Net Sales) * 100

Presumably, "GP" means gross profit.  Gross Profit is Net Sales minus Cost.  So simply substitute:

=(GrossProfit / NetSales) * 100

Notes:
1. There is no point in using the SUM function in that context.
2. I would format as Percentage instead of multiplying by 100.
3. It is unclear what the term "Net Sales" is intended to represent in your "standard formula".  It is not a valid named reference.

Addressing #3 (no pun intended), if A1 contains $100 (net sales) and A2 contains $10 (gross profit), the formula could be written:

=100 * A2 / A1

cwestmark say:

Automatically dialing conference numbers & codes through calendar invites.

I saw a few threads posted in October of 2011 on this and was wondering if anyone has found any way of making this work.   The issue is that when you receive a calendar invite from someone that has a conference bridge number and code, the Windows phone won't pass the pauses and conference code.

 

For example, if the conference bridget is 1.888.444.4444 and the conference code is #1234567, you would want to pass something like 18884444444,,1234567.   Commas are common for pauses in dialing.   Have tried a ton of different options and can't find anything that works.

Thanls!

toddallcock say:

Have you tried adding "tel:" before the number string, with a space after it? (E.g. tel:18884444444,,1234567 )?  I just tried this on my Titan and it worked* from an email and a calendar event. (I didn't create an invite specifically for this test.). Old Windows Mobile phones didn't automatically figure out what was and wasn't a phone number from plain text- the tel: prefix was required to hotlink a pho e number. A space also has to be placed at the end (rather than punctuation) to create the link.

 

*(by "worked" I meant the commas created the expected  pause, and then my phone threw random number tones *very quickly* at my voicemail box. I don't know if the actual digits would've been parsed correctly.  Interestingly, the tones weren't audible from my Titan, but could be heard at the other end.)
