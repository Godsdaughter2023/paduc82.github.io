a. user say:

It is extremely unlikely that the cause of the crash is ntdll.dll for that is a core Windows system file and if it was afflicted millions of systems all over the planet would be crashing and they're not.

Third party analyzers (WhoCrahsed, BlueScrenView, OCR, etc.) often implicate core Windows file but they are never the real cause.  Somtimes they are right but usually they are wrong.

You are also getting bad advice from somebody that don't know how Windows works - probably somebody at Microsoft.

Whenever somebody thinks ntdll.dll is a problem (which it isn't) the advice is often:

regsvr32.exe /u ntdll.dll and press Enter key (this will uninstall the file)

regsvr32.exe ntdll.dll and press Enter key (this will reinstall the file).

Those commands do no such thing and will never work since ntdll.dll is not a registerable dll.  

Just because a file name ends in .dll does not mean it can be or needs to be registered.

It also tells you that whoever suggested that bad idea has not tried it themselves or they would know the idea will fail.

That is a classic Microsoft support suggestion that will never work or help.

The best way to figure out crashes is with the Windows debugging tools but I have never seen anybody at MS that hints they know how to use them - and nobody here works for MS, we are just users and volunteers like you.

Unless you know more about managing memory than Windows the best setting for the paging files is always "System managed size".

Tinkering with the settings is another classically bad idea from Microsoft support (like registering ntdll.dll).

Your video driver is one version behind but I would not change that for now.

Even though you are unlikely to determine the cause of a crash from the Event Viewer but what is interesting about yours is the Exception Code:

Exception Code: c0000005 

Those things that end with 005 are often rights/permissions/access denied kinds of things.

You say you have MBAM and Comodo installed and everybody in the community is likely to agree that it is never a good idea to have more than one AV product installed at a time.  Pick one of the other but never have two running real time protections at the same time.  It could be that they are conflicting or somehow having a permissions/rights issue with the games.

If it was my system I would uninstall MBAM and Comodo (using their uninstall instructions) and put in the free version of Microsoft Security Essentials (MSE) as that would uncomplicate your AV environment.  You can always reinstall them again later if it is your desire.  Be thinking about how you can uncomplicate and simplify the environment by eliminating potential causes one by one until the problem goes away (the process of elimination).

Instead of tinkering with those two and trying things, just get rid of them completely to uncomplicate the crash equation which at the moment has too many variables.

See if you have the same crashes without MBAM and Comodo running, then for protection you can install MSE:
