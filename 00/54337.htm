Uninstall the Device software from the Programs and Features:

a. Press Windows and X key to open a task list from the bottom left corner of the screen.

c. Select Programs and Features.

d. Double click on the program you want to uninstall and follow the onscreen instructions.

Method 2:

a. Press Windows and X keys together.

c. Locate the Device.

https://www.asus.com/Notebooks_Ultrabooks/N550JV/HelpDesk_Download/

a. Press Windows Key + R.

e. Then Click OK.

I think I might have the solution to this problem. Just the other day I remembered that I had the same problem with my old Windows RT tablet, and was able to fix it. I charged it up, found out what I did and applied it to my Surface Book, and it appears to have solved the not waking problem. Everything seems to be working better actually. Here's what I did.

1) Right click on the start button and select device manager. 

2) Go to the bottom and expand "Universal Serial Bus controllers"

3) Right click on "USB Root Hub (xHCI)" and select properties.

4) Click the "Power Management" tab and uncheck the box for "Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power"

5) Click ok, and that should do it. Like I said it permanently fixed my RT tablet, and I haven't have a failure to wake on my Surface Book since I did it a few days ago. I was going to wait a few more days to make sure it was a permanent fix but I saw this topic and figured I'd post it now.
