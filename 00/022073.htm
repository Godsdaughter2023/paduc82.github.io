tkamenick say:

What is the difference between "DRM-free", "Protected" and "Zune Pass" Content?

I have Zune Pass.  I've used song credits to buy the soundtrack for Avenue Q.  But some of it is "DRM-free" and some of it is "Protected".  They showed up as two different CD's until I redid the downloads.

I'm guessing "Zune Pass" content is the stuff I've downloaded but not used song credits for.  So what's the difference between "DRM-free" and "Protected" content?
mysqueakycat say:

If you got the tracks from the Marketplace the DRM-free will be the MP3 songs.  If you have tracks that are "protected" they are probably .wma files with permanent license.  (Right click on them and verify this, the Zune Pass will have an expiration date, the purchased "protected" .wmas will not). 

Little by little the Marketplace is going to DRM-free MP3 but there are still some albums that still have DRM.  What's perplexing is the mix in the same album (why it's done, I have no clue, I have heard others say it is true).  When you buy with points or credits there will be a "MP3" next to the song.  Try making sure you buy them if you don't want to deal with the permanent "protected" .wmas.  They're okay, but you only have a total of 5 downloads to get them licensed on other computers and you'd better "burn" them immediately to a disk which you can rip later (although this is not optimal as each time you convert you lose some of the music fidelity).  If you do get the MP3s, though, be sure you back them up right away as you only get the one download.

You also need to check for duplicates; sometimes when you buy the MP3, the Zune Pass copy will still be in your collection (it's supposed to delete it when it downloads the MP3).  Right clicking on each track in the collection should tell you if there are any duplicates.

I checked the album you mentioned and it shows "MP3 purchase available" next to the album cover which usually means the whole album is MP3.  And I also selected "buy" on each track (then cancelled) but each said MP3.  Did you buy all the tracks at the same time?  It's possible if you bought some last month and some this month that it changed and last month's might have been "protected" .wmas.  If you bought them all today and you don't have MP3s then you'll probably have to call Zunesupport and find out what happened.
