Formula for first four of the five completed calendar quarters - Microsoft Community

soggycashew say:

Formula for first four of the five completed calendar quarters

Hello, I'm trying to make a base period form from a (Start Date) I enter that would show the first four of five completed calendar quarters prior to the date (Start Date) 

EXAMPLE:

(Start Date) = 11/16/2012

June 30-2012  To  April 1-2012 

March 31-2012  To  January 1-2012

December 31-2011  To  October 31-2011

September 30-2011  To  June 30-2011

I wanted to enter the start date in cell (A1) then have the quarters follow in the text/date format.

(A1) = Start date

Cell (A3) = June 30-2012  To  April 1-2012

Cell (A4) = March 31-2012  To  January 1-2012

Cell (A5) = December 31-2011  To  October 31-2011

Cell (A6) = September 30-2011  To  June 30-2011



Thanks!!!
ronrosenfeld say: As long as you have the ATP enabled, here is a somewhat shorter formula that uses the EOMONTH function.  It should also eliminate errors from "inappropriate" dates in A1:

A3:  =IF(AND(ISNUMBER($A$1),$A$1>547),
TEXT(EOMONTH($A$1,-MOD(MONTH($A$1)-1,3)-1-3*
ROWS($1:1)),"mmmm d-yyyy")  & " to " & TEXT(EOMONTH(
$A$1,-MOD(MONTH($A$1)-1,3)-4-3*ROWS($1:1))+1,"mmmm d-yyyy"),"")

and fill down to A6.

Excel stores dates as the number of days since 1/1/1900.  The number 547 is 1 July 1901.  With any earlier date, one or more of the formulas will return an error.  You can change that to any appropriate date.  For example, you could substitute  DATE(2012,1,1) if your earliest valid date in A1 is 1 Jan 2012.
