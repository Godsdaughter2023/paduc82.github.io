Forgot Windows Microsoft Account Email - Microsoft Community

danieldidonato say:

Forgot Windows Microsoft Account Email

I was recently working on a customer's computer and while using the net user command I accidentally deleted the user account.

Frantically I searched online how to recover the email when I had no clue what it was only to find out that apparently I'm the first person to come across this issue (although not likely).

So in case anyone else comes across this issue here is the solution on how to retrieve the email from a deleted microsoft windows login account.

Use your normal method to access the built in administrator account.

Here's how I do mine:

1. Use a windows installation CD/DVD to boot the computer.

2. Press Shift + F10 to bring up the command prompt.

3. Enter regedit to bring up registry editor.

4.  Use the Load Hive option with the browse pane to navigate to the C:\Windows\System32 folder.

5. Go to "sethc.exe" and change it to something like "sethc (2)" or "sethc backup".

6. Go to "cmd.exe" and right click it and copy it and then paste in the same location.

7. Change "cmd - Copy.exe" to "sethc.exe"

8. Restart the computer and at the login screen press the Shift key 5 times to bring up the command prompt.

9. Enter net user administrator /active:yes and restart the computer and login with the Administrator account.

Continuing on:

Navigate to the root of the deleted User's files and find the "NTUSER.dat" file (You will most likely need to activate Show hidden files and uncheck hide protected operating system files to see it.

Right click it and open with > notepad

Use the find function (Ctrl + F), if you can at least remember the domain it is a huge help, I typed @outlook.com and it found the email right away, yours may be @hotmail.com or something of that sort.

Use that email to re associate the account with the Windows installation.

Voila and you're welcome :)

tonypost say:

Office365 AAD Migration to On Prem AD

Hi All,

I need some help with a solution for this.    I will try to explain. 

We currently have an internal AD domain(domaina.com), and its synced to AAD.  We use O365 of this domain.  Now we have another small company who use Office365 and AAD (in cloud) accounts to access it (Domainb.com).  They are not on our AD and have no connection to our office network.  

We want to connect them up to our office and give them a DC (with Domaina.com). As their computers are not on a domain, its easy to join them.  

How do i go about the migration of the mailboxes to their new AD accounts?  Or is there a way to migrate the AD users from AAD to our on on prem AD, and then sync them to AAD again.  How do i link the O365 with their new account? 

ranmo_msft say:

Hi Tony,

Do you mean you want to move a pure Office 365 (domainb.com) to your company’s DC (domaina.com) and then sync both of the users to your current Office 365 tenant (the small company’s Office 365 tenant will not be used)? If it is this case, there is no direct way to link the two tenants. You need to manually migrate the users accounts and its mailboxes content. Here are the steps
1. Back up the small company’s users mailbox content into .pst file.
2. Remove domainb.com in small company’s Office 365 tenant
3. Add domainb.com as the UPN suffix in your company’s DC via the following article and then verify domainb.com into your current Office 365 tenant.
https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/kb/243629
4. Create the same users with @domainb.com suffix in your company’s DC
5. Run the DirSync in your AD. This will sync the new created users with @domainb.com to your company’s Office 365 tenant.
6. Provision the new users licenses and then import the pst file which is obtained in step1 to the new users’ mailboxes.

If this is not your situation, please feel free to let us know the detailed information so that we can provide you corresponding answers for it.

Thanks for your time on this thread.

Best regards,
Ran
