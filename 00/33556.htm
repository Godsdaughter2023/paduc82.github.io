=============================================
Two possibilities:

1) Renaming or deleting the following file:
         "PreviouslyAcquired.db"  
should allow the photos to be downloaded again.

Find the file at the following location:

C:\Users\[username]\AppData\Local\Microsoft\Photo
Acquisition\PreviouslyAcquired.db

Rename to  :  PreviouslyAcquired.OLD

If you cannot find the file you may need to change your
settings to "Show Hidden Files".

Try the following steps in Windows 7:

Go to.....Start button / Control Panel / Appearance
and Personalization / Folder Options / Advanced
settings / Show hidden files, folders, and drives / OK

Now find and rename the file.

Try to re-import your photos.


2) Connect the camera to the PC via USB cable and
turn the camera on.

Close any windows that may open.

Go to...Start / Computer / Devices With Removable
Storage...

Look at the drive letters and see if one of them
represents the camera. It may say Removable Disk

If the camera is recognized...right click the drive letter
and from the menu choose...Open or Explore.

The directory of the memory card in the camera should
be displayed and if you navigate through the various
folders you should find the photo files.

Once found...you can copy/paste the photos to a folder
on your hard drive.

Simple and best solution IMHO is to use an internal
or external USB Memory Card reader.

It won't drain your camera batteries and may be faster.
FWIW...downloading from the camera when batteries
may be low after a long photo shoot could result in
corrupted photos.
