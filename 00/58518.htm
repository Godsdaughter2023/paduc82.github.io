    a. user say:

    A much better way to recover your computer after a problem is to make image backups every day. An image is an exact copy of your hard drive.

    If you want to use Microsoft's own recovery tools, get comfortable. You'll need to make a recovery drive that you'll use to reinstall Windows after which you'll have to reinstall your software and then update everything, following which you'll want to setup your computer the way you like it all over again.

    Before you start you'll need to backup all your personal files - if you still have access to them - and so you'll have to restore them after you're done.

    All this could occupy several hours of your day, and that's if nothing goes wrong along the way.

    Compare this with restoring an image backup: When you restore an image backup, your computer travels back in time to exactly the way it was when you made the backup. There's nothing to reinstall, nothing to setup and nothing to restore.

    Making an image backup of your C: drive takes only minutes, and restoring a backup only takes minutes also. With so little time needed, you may as well make an image backup every day, and the image backup software can do that for you automatically so you don't even have to think about it.

    For me, the choice between restoring an image that's never more than 24 hours old in just minutes, without having to reinstall, update or setup anything; and spending hours to setup my computer all over again - and hoping I get everything right - is a very easy choice.
