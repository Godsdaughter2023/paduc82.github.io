I think it should be possible with today's smartphones to Skype from smartphone to smartphone without paying for a carrier on either one and without having to turn both apps on first.

Also, you have to click on the public WiFi hotspot each time to activate it which is an extra unneccesary step. Municipal WiFi is still a rarity. There are fake WiFi Hotspots like Verizons'. Its claim is false since their software was developed 10 years ago and hasn't been upgraded since. Another impediment is the Internet provider's broad-area WiFi coverage do not overlap (they are contiguous for the most part) . WiFi is different from cell data because of Network Address Translation (NAT), but let me wait a bit for that discussion.

Test: My test situation is two very cheap Lumia 920s. Neither phone subscribes to cell service, that is, neither has a carrier. Skype to Skype comm works fine for this configuration.
