It has been theorized that the problem is rf emitted by the monitor cables, adapters, or the monitors themselves causing the problem.  Here are some things you may not have tried to isolate the problem.

  • Try moving the docking station as far from the sp4 as possible.
  • Route the monitor cables as far away from sp4 as possible.
  • See if wifi returns if you power down the monitors but not unplug them from the dock.
  • Try leaving the monitors powered up and connected to the dock and unplug the dock from the sp4 and see if wifi returns.
  • Try leaving the monitors powered up and disconnect the monitor cables from the dock but still connected to the monitors.  Check the wifi for connection.
  • Since the problem was not present at one time try the older Marvel wifi driver.
  • Try plugging the docking cable upside down in the sp4.

The above steps may help isolate the problem to interference caused by monitor hookups and may suggest a solution.

There is a documented problem with usb3 and 2.4 ghz wifi conflicts particularly in Europe so a usb dock may also fail.  It has something to do with a more aggressive connectivity algorythm to prevent wifi from interfering with usb3.

There is an Intel white paper on this for more info.
