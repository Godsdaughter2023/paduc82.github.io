Lync in-call dial pad disappears - Microsoft Community

a. user say:

Lync in-call dial pad disappears

You may have the following problem

When you are on lync in a corporate office and you dial someone's number thats outside your organization, it rings normally, but as soon as it connects, the dial pad disappears!

Two things i have found, one is easy to spot one is much harder.

Easy is to enable enterprise voice.

If that doesnt work:

Go into lync client and uncheck "Allow Stereo Audio playback"

There is a reg edit corresponding to this

hkcu\Software\Microsoft\Office\16.0\Lync  AllowStereoAudioPlayback 0 Red_DWORD

Explanation:

When lync initiaites a call to the outside world over POTS or a traditional phone system, it negotiates what codecs they are going to use to communicate. For instance, what kind of dial tones it will send and receive, what a hang up is, and what constitutes being put on hold.

One of the sets of codecs deals with how the dial pad works after a call has been initiated. Lync sends out 11 codecs for that option. The phone provider (depending on who it is), may only accept 10. So if the phone provider goes through the list and doesnt find a match after 10 codecs, it just gives up.

You need to reduce the number of codecs being sent. The easiest way is to stop sending the codec about stereo audio playback. That will reduce the number so that the phone provider finds a match before its hard limit.

Its crazy but it works. Ideally you could go to the lync server and figure out how to limit the number of codecs, but this is a way to do it as well.
