Hello PranavLeekha:

 

Thank you for posting to the forum regarding your storage space question.  It sounds like you either have space reserved on the phone to be used as "reserved space" or you have other content on the phone that is being stored here.

 

The first thing we would like you to do is check your reserved space settings.  You will do this by connecting the phone and going to the Zune software.  Click on device/phone > reserved space > set the slider to 0%.  Anything that needs to be put into reserved space will be put here regardless of what you have set.  Setting this to anything but 0% will have the phone set aside additional space that can only be used for certain things.

 

The content that does go into reserved space are the following:

  • Applications and application data.
  • Media synced to the phone as a guest.
  • Podcasts.
  • The OS.

If you notice that you have more content in reserved space then you would like, do the following:

  • Delete games and applications from the phone.
  • Delete podcasts you have already listened to.
  • Make sure all the pictures, music and videos have been synced to the phone properly.  To do this make sure all the media on your phone is backed up to your computer, http://support.xbox.com/en-US/zune/software/syncing-media.  Once you have done this, go to the Zune software and click settings > device/phone > sync options > erase all content > forget this phone.  Now, disconnect the phone and reconnect the phone.  You should be prompted to link the phone with your account.  At this point sync the content from your computer to the phone.

Please let us know if you have any other questions or concerns.

The easiest way is to hover the mouse over each piece of clip art you want, and click the
Add to Basket button in the popup. When you have all the items you want in the basket,

Download button in that popup. If you don't already have the download manager ActiveX
control, you'll be prompted to allow it to load. The manager will open the Clip Art
Organizer and put the clips into the Downloaded Clips collection.
 
Alternatively, if you already have the clip art on the Windows clipboard (or you can copy
it from wherever it is), right-click any collection or subsection in the tree diagram on
the left side of the Clip Art Organizer and choose Paste Clip.

stephen boots say:

The good news is that you were only checking it out. The bad news is that you have encountered two problems with WDO that are common.

1. When you created the USB boot media from the WDO download it installed the latest definitions. WDO doesn't need to update when you boot the laptop from it. However, in some scenarios that I still don't fully understand (one is a multi-boot system where more than one OS is installed) WDO doesn't look on the WDO meda which should be %system% for all intents and purposes, but looks on your hard drive, and thinks it needs the definitions. This is, in my opinion, a defect.

2. Support for updating WDO over a wireless network is pretty much non-existent. I am not aware of any supported WiFi adapters. Updating over the hard wired network connection will only work with a limited number of network cards and there is no published list of supported NICs.

So, unfortunately, you won't be able to use this version of WDO. Perhaps in the future these limitations/defects will be addressed.

In the meantime, there are alternative rescue scanners you can use:

a. user say:

Hi Pierre,

We suggest that you perform a soft reset. Press and hold the volume down and Power button until your phone vibrates and restarts. Note that you should not touch the device screen during this process (not even when the 'slide down to power off' prompt appears). After performing a soft reset, try to check if the issue persists.

If a soft reset didn't work, we suggest that you perform a hard reset instead. If your phone is unresponsive and you can't reach Settings , you may be able to reset your phone using the hardware buttons:

  1. Press and hold the volume down and Power buttons at the same time until you feel a vibration (about 10–15 seconds).

  2. When you feel the vibration, release the buttons, and then immediately press and hold the volume down button until you see a large exclamation mark.

  3. Once the exclamation mark appears, press the following four buttons in this order: volume up , volume down , Power , volume down . Your phone should now reset and restart itself. (It might take a while for the reset to finish.)
