mike hall... always say:

Page numbers, footers, headers etc have to be within the margins that Word sets by default because the default margins are set to the maximum that the installed printer can handle.

Side margins can be pulled out, but top and bottom margins are fixed by the printers ability to to be able to control the rate at which the paper moves through the prniter when the print heads are laying down the image. At the point where the paper rollers have to release the printed page, printing has to stop regardless of what is shown on screen.

Margins vary from one printer to another, depending upon the distance between the print heads and the paper rollers, but the difference will be minimal. Laser printers are not as restrictive as inkjet priters because of the way that laser printers/photocopiers/Litho printers apply the image to the page..

I find that this works but is overkill and may also reinstall apps that you've intentionally removed.

Instead, use the following to reinstall only the apps currently installed for your account:

Get-AppXPackage -User ([System.Security.Principal.WindowsIdentity]::GetCurrent().Name) | Foreach {Add-AppxPackage -DisableDevelopmentMode -Register “$($_.InstallLocation)\AppXManifest.xml”}

Even that may be excessive as I find that the cause is Shell Experience Host, which can be reset via:

Get-AppXPackage -User ([System.Security.Principal.WindowsIdentity]::GetCurrent().Name) *ShellExperienceHost* | Foreach {Add-AppxPackage -DisableDevelopmentMode -Register “$($_.InstallLocation)\AppXManifest.xml”}
