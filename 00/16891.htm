    rhina vib say:

    Hi,

    If you want the date and time in Microsoft Word 2016 to automatically update when you open or print the document, you can insert it as a field that automatically updates. The field can also be updated manually at any time.

    You can easily insert the formatted date and time in Word 2016 by following the steps below:

    1. Open the Word document and select the Insert tab.
    2. Click Date & Time to open the dialog box.
    3. Select a format for the date or time or both from the list.
    4. To have the date and/or time update automatically, tick the Update automatically check box and then click OK.
    5. The date and/or time is inserted into your document. If you chose to have it update automatically, it’s inserted as a field. When you put the cursor in the field, an Update button displays above the field that allows you to update the field manually at any time. You can also press F9 to update the field when the cursor is in it.
    6. If you decide you don’t want the date and/or time updated automatically anymore, you can highlight the field or put the cursor in the field, and press Ctrl + Shift + F9 to unlink the field. You might want to update the field before unlinking it so it has the current date and/or time once it’s unlinked.

    If you have other questions, don't hesitate to reach out.
