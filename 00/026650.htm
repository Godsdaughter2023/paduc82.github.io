I'm visualising a table with 3 columns:

ParentPart Quantity ChildPart

P124 2 P12
P124 3 P14
P1231 2 P123
P1231 2 P124

To make a list, in columns F & G, of the parents of child P12 and the quantity of P12 used you would use code like this:

Range("F:G").ClearContents

With Range("A1").CurrentRegion
  .AutoFilter ' remove any previous filter

  .AutoFilter 3, "P12"
  ' copy parents and paste parent and quantity to columns F and G
  .Columns(1).Resize(,2).SpecialCells(xlVisible).Copy
  Range("F1").PasteSpecial xlValues
  .AutoFilter ' remove the filter
End With

Is it a multi-level bill, as here, or just single level?
The code could be extended to show all ancestors, but no point doing so if you don't need it.

Click File>Options>Mail.  Slide down to the "Send messages" section.  Check the box labeled "Use Auto-Complete list to suggest names when typing in the To, Cc, and Bcc lines".  That should cause Outlook to remember the addresses to which you send mail so that they appear again when you start typing in a recipient field.  It can't fill in from your contacts because it doesn't rference your contacts.  If you want to choose from your contats, check the box labeled "Automatic name checking" in that same section of mail options.  Then when you type the first three characters of a contact and either tab out of the recipient field or when you press Ctrl+K, it will automatically resolve from your Contacts or, in the case of contacts sharing the string you typed, pop up a box where you can choose the contact.

I don't know what you mean by "keep previous send e-mails in the account", but perhaps you mean you want Outlook to add to your Contacts folder the addresses of those people to whom you respond.  If that's the case, Outlook can't do that, but it can come close.  Click File>Options>Contacts.  At the very bottom, check the box labeled "Automatically create Outlook contacts for recipients that do not belong to an Outlook Address Book".  This will add any non-Contact address to which you send a message to the "Suggested Contacts" folder.  If this folder is enabled as an address book (right-click it, choose Properties, and select the Outlook Address Book tab to check), then Outlook will check it also when you autoresolve names.  You can also click To and choose from that folder.
