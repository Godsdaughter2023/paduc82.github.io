jacobthedev say:

Rethinking Photos for Windows 10 on Phones

I recently posted this to Reddit, but I thought this would be the best place for someone at Microsoft to actually see this.

As I'm sure you're all aware, much of the Windows Phone community is very upset by the direction that Windows 10. Just a few of these complaints are:

  • Apps are starting to look very much like Android or iOS apps, instead of using the beautiful design language we've become accustomed to.
  • Interaction menus are moving to the top of the screen rather than staying at the bottom, making it difficult to interact with the app.
  • Sections of apps that used to be in pivots are now being hidden away in hamburger menus, which again, are at the top of the screen and hard to access.

I think we all understand that the design language has to evolve, but I don't think this is the way to do it. There's plenty of room for growth while maintaining the excellent usability we've all grown to love.

To demonstrate this, I created some mock-ups for a design of the new Photos app for Windows 10 on phones. You can view it by clicking here, or looking at the bottom of this post. The top image is what the app currently looks like. The middle is the redesigned app, and the bottom is the redesigned app with it's new app drawer open. If you compare this app to the photos app for Windows Phone 8.1, you'll see that this feels like a natural evolution, rather than completely scrapping our tried and true design principles.

As you can see, I've broken out the "hamburger menu" area in to three pivots where appropriate, and put "settings" and "send feedback" in to the already established app drawer. This UI makes it very easy to swipe between the three main sections of the app, and also makes it easy to access the two other sections that are less often used.

Microsoft, I'm sure you've heard the outcry from your most loyal fans. I love Windows, I've used Windows Phone for years, I use a Surface 2, I stood in line on day 1 to get my Surface RT. I think you guys do great work when you put your minds to it, but Windows 10 looks like a mess. I'm willing to give you the benefit of the doubt, and hope that this is just an artifact of the early stages of the design process. Please, please correct this before releasing.

Thank you.

swap rows and columns - Microsoft Community

gavinreynolds say:

swap rows and columns

How do I change the format of a spreadsheet so the data presently in rows are in columns, and vice versa?

phillip m jones say:
  1. Select entire section or sheet including headings  and Copy.
  2. now if its an entire sheet. go to bottom of page and click on + sign to add a sheet.
  3. switch to new sheet
  4. go to edit menu the paste special and click on check box for transpose

everything will be switched properly.

If the information don't take up the entire sheet:

  1. Select including headers, the content you want to change.
  2. Move to an empty cell at top of sheet (here heading would normal go).
  3. Again choose Paste special and again choose Transpose.
  4. next select all the columns (or rows that had information formatted original way
  5. Go to edit menu choose Delete. (may ask whether to move cells up or down right or left, or it may not.)
