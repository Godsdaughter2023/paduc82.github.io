jeeped say:
Tap Alt+F11 and when the VBE opens, immediately use the pull-down menus to Insert, Module . Paste the following into the new pane titled something like Book1 - Module1 (Code), 

Sub mcrSet_All_Values_and_Save_XLSX()
    Dim w As Long
    For w = 1 To Sheets.Count
        Sheets(w).UsedRange = Sheets(w).UsedRange.Value
    Next w
    Application.DisplayAlerts = False
    ThisWorkbook.SaveAs _
      ThisWorkbook.Path & Chr(92) & _
        Left(ThisWorkbook.Name, InStr(1, ThisWorkbook.Name, Chr(46)) - 1) & Format(Date, "_yyyy-mm"), _
      xlOpenXMLWorkbook
End Sub

Tap Alt+Q to return to your workbook. Tap Alt+F8 to open the Macros dialog and Run the macro. Note that this macro will no longer exist in the saved macro-free workbook.

steve rindsberg say:

MS changed the way slide numbers etc. work in Office 2007.  Where before they came from the master and were unselectable on individual slides (but could be covered up by content on individual slides) they're now sort of on the master and ALSO on the slides.  They're not as apt to be covered up but can be selected/moved/deleted. 

And when moving older files into newer versions of PPT, things can get VERY messy as a result.  In your case, it sounds as though the numbers on the slides have gotten "disconnected" from the master placeholder, so they no longer update.  They're just text boxes.

It's difficult to give exact step by step instrux as to how to fix this, but for starters you'll want to start by deleting all of the numbers on the slides themselves.

Then go to View | Slide Master and delete the existing slide number placeholder from the master and all of the layouts.

Then select the master (the one at the top of the thumbnails pane) and in Slide Master | Master Layout, click Master Layout and add a slide number placeholder.

Size/position/format it to taste, then copy/paste it to any of the layouts where you want slide numbers to appear.

Close Master view, then choose Insert | Header & Footer and add the slide numbers back to all slides.

Luckily, it takes less time to do than to read about and once done, your numbers should behave.
