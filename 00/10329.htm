brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

First, you don't open PSTs by double-clicking on them.  Is that how you're trying to open your PST?  Second, if your PC was wiped, then all of your old PSTs were erased, unless the tech backed them up before wiping the disk and put them back after reinstalling WIndows and the applications.

Open Outlook and click File>Open>Outlook Data File.  Browse to a PST you believe contains your old data, select it, and click OK.  Look around the folders.  Do you see your old data?  If so, you can make the old PST your default using the Data Files tab on Tools>Account Settings.  Stop and restart Outlook and the old file will be the default with all your calendar events, all your old contacts, and e-mail.

If, because the Windows user has changed, there is a permissions issue, you should take ownership of the file and then try to open it.  http://www.vistax64.com/tutorials/67717-take-ownership-file.html
