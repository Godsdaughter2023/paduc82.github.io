Instead of having to manually type each of the formulas, then you can use in D2 this formula:

=($C$2+COLUMN(A1)*100)/($B$2+COLUMN(A1))

If you put that into D2 and then drag the formula across, you should see the same results as achieved with the manual formulas created originally.

About your second concern - taking up too much space in your sheet - it would help to know a couple of extra things: what is in cell B1? and what do you intend to do with the result of the formulas which start in D2 across?

Hi areyouforreel,

Thanks for uploading your file.

If you don't want or need to see an array of results (like in D2 to M2 for the first Ten 100 surveys) but just want to know the actual figure of say X 100 surveys, then you could make D1 header X, enter a whole number into D2, make E1 header X 100 surveys, and then put this formula into E2

=(C2+D2*100)/(B2+D2)

and that will tell you the result of the impact of X additional surveys with perfect score of 100 (over your current score).
