bazz_s say:

How can I stop video rewind with a fade smoothly transition

bazz_s say:

I have found a solution to my problem. Well a work around rather.

 

To recap here is what I wanted to achieve.

Slide A has full screen image, or picture (is a JPG image to be precise).

On mouse click, slide A fades smoothly into the slide B that has the first frame (full screen) of the video clip showing on screen. Once the fade smoothly transition is complete, the video plays automatically and then pauses on the last frame showing this as a full screen image of the last frame of the video.

On mouse click, slide B (with the last frame visible of the video clip) then fades smoothly into slide C as another full screen image, or picture (is a TIFF image to be precise).

 

So how to do it

The problem was that some videos would work and some wouldn’t no matter what I did they would rewind to the first frame or poster frame as one can set, as soon as the transition to next slide was invoked. The problem with setting a poster frame is that I needed it to show the first frame of the video at the start of the slide and then fade smoothly into the next slide showing the last frame of the clip. Poster frame only shows one frame for both ends of start and finish of playing.

 

Here is how I did it.

Inset the video into the slide

Set it to play automatically

Also set it to hide when not playing

Take a screen grad (or snapshot) of both first frame of the video clip and the last frame of the video clip. (VLC and Media Player Classic can do this). If your trimming your clip, you can get the exact time and jump to that time to take screen grad or snapshot in VLC or Media Player Classic.

Insert these to images into the same slide.

Now to make life easier show the “animation pane” (Animations tab and click on animations pane)

And also the “section pane”. (On the Home tab, in the Editing group, click the Select drop down, choose section pane) this makes it easier to manage the layering order of images and videos, ie bring to front or back etc. Also to play around with the order of playing and pausing etc in relationship to other elements. (I had text fading in and out overlaying on top of the video clip during playback of the clip) You can also order which elements are visible at what times.

Now for the clever part

Set the first frame image to be behind the video and also the last frame to behind the first frame ie sent to back. So the layer order going down is video clip, first frame image, last frame image.

Set the first frame to disappear as an animation during the playback of the video but before the playback pauses, I chose 2 seconds.

 

Here is how it works…

Image on slide A fades smoothly on mouse click into the first frame image that is visible on slide B. The video clip is not playing yet so is not visible. The video clip plays automatically (after the fade smoothly transition is complete) and since it is in front of the first frame in layering order the first frame image can then disappear as an animation behind the playing video clip without this action being seen. The video clip then stops playing at the end of the clip and is not visible any more (hide when not playing). This then shows the last frame as the first frame has already disappeared (the animation disappear action)

Then on mouse click it the fades smoothly from slide B into the slide C while still showing the last frame of the video clip, just what I wanted. No jumping back to poster frame.

 

I have also found that if I set a poster frame to the end I would get a flash on screen of the poster frame when the video started playing using this method, However nine times out of ten it didn’t by not setting a poster frame on the clip.

I have also found that some clips behave fine even though they appear to be the same format (file type). This is where we are obviously getting into codec issues.

 

Hope this helps others
