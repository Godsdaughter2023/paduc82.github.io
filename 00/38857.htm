considerate_guy say:

A Movie Maker project file doesn't actually contain any media. When you are adding clips, photos and music to your project, Movie Maker is just remembering their location on your hard disk. Every time you preview your project, it will dynamically load those files and give you a preview of that section of the video.

When you reopen your project, Movie Maker checks the original locations for each of the media files and for any that it can't find, it shows a yellow triangle with an exclamation point.

So what must have happened is that you moved, deleted or renamed something so that Movie Maker can't find it. If you put the files in a new folder, just double-click on the problem video clip and navigate to its new location. If you accidentally deleted the source files, try recovering them from your recycle bin.

The analogy I like to use is it's like giving instructions to your teenager about making a cake where you say get the eggs off the 2nd shelf of the fridge, and get the flour off of the top shelf of the pantry, etc. But in the meantime you've either moved them somewhere else, or you've used them all up. So when your teenager comes to follow that recipe, they won't be able to add those ingredients to the cake. At that point you can just show them that the flour is actually moved to a container on the bottom shelf and the eggs are moved to the door of the refrigerator. And then they can complete the baking of the cake.
