alijbear say:

Windows 10 is killing my hard drives!! Catastrophically!

This is a really strange one..

I have installed WTP on my Dell 9150 desktop several times now since the first release build

However, since the latest round of updates, I have had some very odd HDD failures.

Configuration:

Dual-booting with W7 Pro:

 W7 is on a Samsung 120G 840 EVO SSD

 WTP is on another Samsung 840 (not EVO) SSD.

I have 2 Data HDD drives of 2TB each - these are (were) both HGST OEM drives (Type DS5SAD200)

W7 installed, updated and worked just fine with Office 2010 and many other apps. I configured the USER folder to be on the first of the 2 2TB drives. (I always do this to keep data seaparate from the OS, so I can use the SSDs for the OS.

Then I installed WTP Build 9841 and that went just fine

then I downloaded and installed the latest preview build 9879, and that also went fine with no issues

I then let WTP download updates and it started the process of installing them.

After a while of percentage completion indications, the machine asked for a reboot, so far so good.

After the reboot the 2TB HGST drive (was D:) became undetectable by the BIOS!!

I tried the drive in another machine etc etc, but no dice - it didnt even spin up and just remained inert.

I though this was a huge coincidence, but s#1t happens, right, and at that point I put it down to a coincidental hardware failure during the the WTP reboot.

So, i rebuilt the machine, this time with the remaining good HGST 2TB HDD, reinstalled W7 (OK again), and went through the process of installing and updating WTP again.

HOLY MOLY!! The same thing happened again! the HGST 2TB drive became unrecognisable and would not spin up after the WTP updates reboot!

Now, this is REALLY too much of a coincidence. Something is really wrong here... 2 identical drives failing at exactly the same point in the WTP installation process??

(Note that WTP does run correctly once the failed drive is removed, but, of course, my W7 profile has gone)

I phoned HGST, but they didnt really have much to say because (a) the drives are 6 months out of warranty and (b) they aren't using WTP - at least not in the support area anyway.

So - has anyone else seen this type of behaviour? Should this be a word of warning against using HGST drives with WTP?

I would speculate that something has been written to the drive to render them - er - unrecognisable by any normal PC.

(I also have a dual boot setup on a Dell laptop  - again with samsung SSD and a dual-partitioned 750G seagate and have had no such problems)

Any suggestions? I'd love to send the drive to a data recovery service to see if they can figure out what went on, but the cost is prohibitive.

Cheers, Alistair
