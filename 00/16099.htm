The issue with the document was that by "direct formatting" a number of paragraphs in the document, including that paragraph containing the word "neogtiated", had the setting for "Do not check spelling or grammar" checked.  If, with those paragraphs selected, the "direct formatting" is removed by pressing CTRL+Space, the "Do not check spelling or grammar" attribute is removed and the word  "neogtiated" is then marked as a spelling error.

The procedure detailed under the ATTEMPT TO CORRECT heading in the originally post, of using CTRL+a and then accessing the Language dialog and clicking as necessary to clear the "Do not check spelling or grammar" box,  has no effect on, and will not remove the "direct formatting".

Using the Find and Replace facility is probably the best way of dealing with the issue across the whole document.  In that dialog, click on More and with the selection in the Find what control click on Format>Language to bring up the Language dialog and click on the "Do not check spelling or grammar" box as required until it is checked.  Then put the selection in the Replace with control and click on Format>Language again and clear the "Do not check spelling or grammar" box so that the Find and Replace dialog appears as:

and then click on the Replace All button.

AVERAGE IF BETWEEN TWO DATES - Microsoft Community

rayinman say:

AVERAGE IF BETWEEN TWO DATES

I am having an issue averaging numbers in one column for a specific month based on dates in another column:

example:

O4:O80 = Numbers to average

J4:J80  =  Dates within year 2018

Need to average numbers for  the month of April

This is what I am trying:  =AVERAGEIFS(O4:O80,DATEVALUE,"<=",4/30/2018,"<=",4/1/2018,(J4:J80))

Any help in the right direction would be greatly appreciated,

Thank you in advance!!

joeu2004 say:

Very close! The correct syntax is:

=AVERAGEIFS(O4:O80, J4:J80, "<=4/30/2018", J4:J80, ">=4/1/2018")

Some people prefer the following to avoid regional differences in the form of the date:

=AVERAGEIFS(O4:O80, J4:J80, "<="&DATE(2018,4,30), J4:J80, ">="&DATE(2018,4,1))

More generally, if you intend to calculate the average for each month, put some the date of the 1st of each month into A1 (e.g) -- for example, 1/1/2018 -- which you might format to show just month and year. Then write the following formula into B1 (e.g):

=AVERAGEIFS($O$4:$O$80, $J$4:$J$80, "<="&EOMONTH(A1,0), $J$4:$J$80, ">="&A1)

Copy the formula into B2:B12.

[EDIT] If there might be no data in column O for a particular month, it would be prudent to wrap IFERROR around each of the above formulas. For example:

=IFERROR(AVERAGEIFS(O4:O80, J4:J80, "<=4/30/2018", J4:J80, ">=4/1/2018"), 0)

Unable to authorise domain in O365 - PowerBI Free Conflict - Microsoft Community

aaron tasker say:

Unable to authorise domain in O365 - PowerBI Free Conflict

I would like to share an issue that we came across this week, as it may affect others who are attempting to complete the same.

Symptom

When trying to verify a domain against an Office 365 tenant, the verification fails in both the modern and classic console, even though the TXT record is present and correct. In the modern console, a new TXT record (ms=xxxxxx) is generated each time you attempt to verify the domain. In the classic console, the verification fails with an error and asks you to restart the process, then generating a new TXT record (ms=xxxxxx).

Cause

When someone in your organisation signs up for Microsoft PowerBI Free, they are asked to authorise their account via an e-mail activation. When this is completed, an O365 tenant is created with that user account, so sharing can occur across members of the same domain. While not authorised, this tenant now has members with a UPN of @yourdomain.com. As the domain is partially authorised against this new tenant (of which you are probably un-aware), and there are users with active UPN's using it to access services, it prevents you from authorising the domain against your desired tenant.

Resolution

  1. Sign-up for a free Microsoft PowerBI account using the desired domain name
  2. Using the tile interface, click on the 'Admin' panel and follow the instructions to authorise your account as an administrator for the tenant. This will involve adding a DNS TXT record to your external DNS registrar.
  3. Notify all staff of the impending logon change, and update all existing accounts to use the @yourdomain.onmicrosoft.com domain suffix for sign-on. (Article)
  4. Ensure you are using the Classic Portal and go to domain management, delete the @yourdomain.com domain from the tenant.
  5. Log-off the Microsoft PowerBI tenant
  6. Log-on to your desired tenant, and complete the process of adding @yourdomain.com. This should now complete without errors.
