Press Windows key + X, click Apps & Features or Programs and Features

Close Devices and Printers.

Uninstalling and reinstalling a camera USB driver from Device Manager:


1. Connect the camera to the computer with the USB cable.

2. Turn on the camera.

3. Open the Device Manager:
Click the Start button, in the start search box, type in devmgmt.msc and press enter.

4. In the Device Manager window, at the left of Universal Serial Bus Controllers, click the PLUS (+) symbol.

5. Under Universal Serial Bus controllers, right-click on your Panasonic camera or USB Mass Storage Device to display the drop-down menu.

6. In the drop-down menu, click Uninstall or Remove.

7. In the Confirm Device Removal dialog box, click the OK button.

8. Restart the computer.

NOTE: After the restart, the computer should recognize the camera connection and begin reinstalling the USB driver.

limitless liter say:

Xbox Won't allow me to play games I already own.

My Xbox randomly stopped allowing me to play any of my already installed games.  After continuously trying to remedy the situation I came to the solution, it's called a "soft reset" i.e you don't lose all your games like a "hard reset" rather you just reset the internal info from your xbox.  This includes your xbox's date and time, your location and your profiles.  All your information is regularly backed up to your xbox profile so you won't lose your progress to any games.  however you will need your sign in information due to the fact that it will be deleted!  Step 1: Settings,  Step 2: system,  Step 3: Console info,  Step 4: Reset console,  (it is very important you follow step 5 perfectly or you will end up Factory resetting your Xbox) Step 5: Reset and keep my games & apps.  Confirm that you want it done and the screen should go grey and "power down".  The system will reboot and prepare itself for the initial setup in which you'll have to enter all the necessary credentials for your personal xbox as well as add your account.  You will be asked if you'd like to restore the settings you had or manually setup new ones.  Purely out of fear the same corruption would arrive from reinstating the old settings I personally chose manual setup and even with moderate internet I was immediately able to download all the games I previously owned.
