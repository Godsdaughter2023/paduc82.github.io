Why Is There An Extra Junction Point for My Documents?

Just so I understand:

I realize that C:\Users contains junction points to redirect software that saves files under the "old" folder scheme.

There are junction points for the 'My' folders - My Documents, My Music, My Pictures and My Video - that point to the Documents, Music, Pictures and Video folders (even though Windows Explorer doesn't display the latter.)

There's also an additional junction point for My Documents (it has the shortcut 'arrow' icon). Why is it there?

Just asking, that's all. Thanks for your help.

daddyman say: I want to thank you again for your help.

After further research, I discovered that the My Documents icon with the arrow is actually a junction point, like the other icons with arrows in C:\Users\<username>. I eventually found several web pages, including a question in the Windows 7 IT Pro Forums, that described the junction points in a Windows 7 user profile.

Here are the user profile junction points and their redirected locations (I hope the line spacing doesn't get too messed up):

Documents and Settings     C:\Users
Application Data                 C:\Users\<username>\AppData\Roaming
Cookies                             C:\Users\<username>\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Windows\Cookies
Local Settings                    C:\Users\<username>\AppData\Local
My Documents                   C:\Users\<username>\Documents
NetHood                            C:\Users\<username>\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Windows\Network Shortcuts
PrintHood                           C:\Users\<username>\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Windows\Printer Shortcuts
Recent                               C:\Users\<username>\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Windows\Recent
SendTo                              C:\Users\<username>\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Windows\SendTo
Start Menu                         C:\Users\<username>\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Windows\Start Menu
Templates                          C:\Users\<username>\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Windows\Templates

The four 'My' folders - My Documents, My Music, My Pictures and My Videos - are actually the Documents, Music, Pictures and Video folders, but they are somehow made to appear in Windows Explorer with the 'My' added.

And here's the answer to my original question - why does only My Documents have a junction point: because in Windows XP (and 2000, I presume), the My Music/Pictures/Videos folders were sub-folders of My Documents.

Finally, a little bonus for those who've read this far: It appears that part of the reason for re-organizing the user profile was to provide better support for roaming profiles. Another reason, advanced by Leo ("Ask Leo") Notenboom, is to rename folder locations with spaces in their name. [Except for Start Menu  ;-)]

In addition to Bruce Hagen's post, please note that Windows Live Mail error code 0x800CCC0F can be broken down to two causes:
  1. Incorrect POP settings (usually the SSL option for SMTP). Please check with your provider if it is required or not.
