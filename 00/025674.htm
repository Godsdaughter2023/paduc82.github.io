As for zip vs dmg files... they aren't doing the same thing.  A zip file is a way of bundling a collection of files as a single unit, while also supporting compression.  A dmg file is a mountable disk image, which on OS X is typically how applications get distributed.  A dmg file does support compression, but it would theoretically introduce a lot of overhead in distributing files (when compared to zip files) as you really only need decompression/verification and not all the overhead associated with handling a drive image (mounting, unmounting, etc.)  That said, I am not a Mac user myself, so perhaps I'm wrong on this.

I finally solved my problem!!!!  Here's what I finally did:

1.  Open your Start Menu, open your Control Panel, and open Programs & Features.

2.  Completely Uninstall Microsoft Office (all versions shown in your Programs & Features).

3.  When that completes, Press and hold (Microsoft Button)+R gives you regedit, click OK.

4.  Open 'File', click on 'Export', then Save your Registry Back-up by whatever name you want.

5.  Open 'Edit', click on 'Find'.  Type "excel.exe" and click 'Find Next'.

6.  As each entry show up highlighted, right click on the entry and select 'Delete', then click 'Yes', then when the entry is gone, press F3 to continue searching.  Do this procedure until you get the message "Finished searching through the registry".

7.  Then go back to the top of the page and expand HKEY_CURRENT_USER.

8.  Expand 'Software'; then expand 'Microsoft'; then expand 'Office'; right click on 'Excel' and delete it; then expand all number entries (8.0, 10.0, 11.0, 12.0, 14.0, 15.0 etc.) and look for 'Excel' entries, delete each entry found for 'Excel'.

9.  Next, expand HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE and repeat the process #6 above.

Close the Registry Editor.

10. Then reinstall Microsoft Office 2007 from your CD.

Excel should now work.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

To clarify, you have a new computer with Office 2010 Starter edition installed, and you also own a retail copy of an earlier version of Office that you have installed or can install on the machine. You would like to upgrade your version of Word to 2010 but don't need to upgrade the other Office applications. Is that correct?

 

If so, I don't think you can do this from within Starter (which, as I understand it, offers an upgrade path for Office 2010). You can certainly purchase and install a standalone version of Word 2010. However, you will probably find that the price of Word alone is not  much less than the price of some Office editions (such as the Home & Student Edition).

 

Even if you do decide to upgrade to Office 2010, I would not advise doing it via a download. It is always safer to have physical discs in case a reinstall is needed at some time in the future.   1. Word 2010 cannot use Outlook Contacts as an address book (possibly even for email) unless you have Outlook 2010.
  2. The Home & Student Edition does not include Outlook.
