brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

Apparently, when you used your old PC, you were using a POP account and the account was configured to delete messages after downloading them.  The message would not download on other devices because it would no longer exist in the mailbox, only on your PC.

 

Now, apparently, you are using a different account type or you have configured the PC to leave copies of messages on the server.  The other devices also see the message in the mailbox and so it's available on those devices as well.

 

Many people want to see their mail on all of their devices, no matter which they use.  If you don't want your phone to see the messages, then either don't access the mailbox with your phone or configure your PC to use a POP account and to delete the messages after downloading just like you did on your old PC.

Follow the steps mentioned below and check if it helps:

Open Word application in safe mode:

Example: Open Word in safe mode

1) Close Word.

2) Press the Windows key+R on the keyboard to open Run window.

3) In Run window and type winword /safe

Note: There is a space between 'Winword' and '/'.

 

If you are able to work with Word without any problem then the issue might be due to add-ins. In order to disable the add-ins follow the steps given below:

1) In Word, click on File< Options< Add-ins.

2) Under Manage select COM Add-ins.

3) Click on GO options beside Manage, uncheck all the add-ins and click Ok.

4) Try to open Word and check if it works fine.

Note: Similarly follow the steps to disable add-ins for other Office applications

resourceful say: Hello. I've also experienced the same problems until I realized how it works. The sync appears to be the time stamp, otherwise AS can't tell what change happened first. You change a contact on the PC, sync, it goes to the device. You change it on the device, sync, it goes to the PC. You change the same contact on the device and the PC before a sync (this is considered a conflict) and the change on the PC will overwrite the change on the device (if this is how you have it set up). I always just keep track of when I sync. Yes, you are right, previous versions of AS had better conflict management. Another way to be safe is backup your phone contacts either with the MyPhone service or this free program:
