jay freedman say:

That isn't possible in Word. At best you could insert a bookmark at or near the data file (inserted with "Display as icon" turned on) and then insert a hyperlink or a cross-reference to the bookmark. In that case, clicking the hyperlink would cause the cursor to jump to the location of the bookmark, but it wouldn't cause the data file to open -- the reader would then have to double-click the file's icon.

Also, to return to the location of the hyperlink in the text, the reader would have to press Alt+LeftArrow (or the Back command would have to be placed on the Quick Access Toolbar).

There is very little to be gained, and much to be lost, in placing the data files at the end of the document. Displayed as icons, they would fit nicely in a slightly widened margin without interrupting the flow of the text.

It's not free but it doesn't cost much either.

http://www.myasms.net/index.php

You can either buy it direct from them or through nokia's software market.
