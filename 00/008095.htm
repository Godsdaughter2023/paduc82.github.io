The formatting that you are trying to apply does not work in 2010.  The work around is to use the Visual Based Format() function to apply the formatting to the .Result of the formfield by the use of a macro that runs on exit from the formfield.

 

This is an example.

 

With ActiveDocument.FormFields("formfieldbookmarkname")

   .Result = Format(.Result, "$#,###.00")

End With

 

You may be able to avoid using a separate macro for each formfield by use of the method in the article "How to find the name of the current formfield” at:

combined with a Select Case - End Select construction.

Who is the registrar for your @private domain? If it's not Microsoft, then what's happening is that Outlook is trying to autocreate an account for a mailbox hosted by the registrar, not Microsoft.
