  < http://support.microsoft.com/kb/326688 >

  • The PC boots into Windows RE (Recovery Environment).

How do I change the CPU and GPU fan speed manually? - Microsoft Community

swaggercompost say:

How do I change the CPU and GPU fan speed manually?

I have a Dell Precision M6600 with an intel core i7-2640M 2.4ghz and a Nvidia Quadro 3000M. I have been wondering how to change my cpu and gpu fan speeds manually as I think something is overheating in my laptop when I'm playing a big game. I have tried pressing f2 when rebooting to bring up the bios but I cannot find where to control this fan speed. I have msi afterburner but the fan speed is grayed out and I can't change it.
hairyfool say:

Fan speed control is entirely down to the BIOS. Although often seen with options on system build boards such as Asus, Gigabyte etc. manufacturers such as Dell rarely provide any form of manual control. The BIOS has the ability to adjust the speed of the fan based on chipset temperature and user input is not required. This also means third party utilities cannot make adjustments.

If the CPU/GPU is actually overheating then it is outside the range for the controller. If an older computer it is not unusual for the radiator inlet from the fan to get blocked with dust. With the computer off it can sometimes be cleaned by blowing compressed air IN the exhaust port but should be done carefully in short bursts to break up the plug of dust that builds up. Otherwise the only other option involves stripping down the laptop and removing the fan to pick out the dust.
