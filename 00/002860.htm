yaqub k say:

How to enable the “Hey Cortana” feature on a Lumia 930 or 1520 with Windows 10 Mobile Insider Preview

If you are running Windows 10 Mobile Insider Preview on a Lumia 930 or 1520 device, Cortana might not recognize your voice if you try to “train” it, and the “Hey Cortana” feature for bringing up Cortana by voice won’t work.

 

You can resolve this issue by following these steps:

 

  1. Go to Settings, and select Time & Language, and then Language.

  2. Use + Add Languages to add another language, such as English (UK). Tap the check mark at the bottom to start the download, then choose Yes when prompted to download.

  3. Return to Settings > Time & Language > Language.

  4. Press-and-hold on your original language, such as English (United States).

  5. Choose Remove from the menu that comes up.

  6. Press Restart phone.

  7. Return to Settings > Time & Language > Language.

  8. Use + Add Languages to add your original language –in this example, English (United States).

  9. Press Restart phone.

After the phone restarts, you should be able to go to Settings, choose Extras, and then select Hey Cortana to start training Cortana.

 

We’d like to acknowledge and thank Daniel Rubino and his site Windows Central for publishing this workaround.

I have managed to fix it and here's the summary of what I've learnt for anyone else with a similar issue.

First mistake... I had been trying to fix OneDrive for Business. It's only possible to selectively sync folders in ...

The latest OneDrive client is a unified replacement for the old separate consumer and business OneDrive apps.  I'll call this 'modern OneDrive.'  It is part of and updated by Windows 10 otherwise you need to download the client (this is linked from an Office 365 account's OneDrive online).

From SharePoint, it is possible to selectively sync with modern OneDrive and it looks like this: Sync SharePoint files with the new OneDrive sync client.  OneDrive for Business will only sync the whole document library.

My problem was that OneDrive for Business (aka groove.exe) somehow became important again or my setup for modern OneDrive had lost its configuration.  I've some ideas what was happening but it isn't relevant to fixing the problem.

This article outlines how to have modern OneDrive take over from OneDrive for Business: Transition from the previous OneDrive for Business sync client

Summary:

  • Make sure OneDrive for Business is running.  Helpful to have at least one SharePoint sync running.
  • Ensure you have the latest modern client.
    • If on W10 - just make sure you sign into an active Office 365 account (personal or work).
    • Previous versions, log into Office 365, click the OneDrive tile to get into OneDrive and follow the link to download (it's bottom left of the screen for me).
  • Probably worth a 'Win + R' and entering (Win is the Windows key on keyboard) and pasting in 'C:\Users\*USERFOLDER*\AppData\Local\Microsoft\OneDrive\OneDrive.exe /takeover'
