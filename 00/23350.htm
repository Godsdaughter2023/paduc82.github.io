However, the idea of searching these lists by hand, when I anticipate less than half a dozen duplicate folders is daunting. roady [mvp] say:

No, there is no way to do that in Outlook.

You can do an Advanced Find (CTRL+SHIFT+F) across your mailbox and then search on the corresponding email addresses or domains (or whatever you use to identify a client). You can then move these items to the correct folder.

Afterwards, right click on the root of your mailbox-> Properties-> Folder Size...
Here you can then review your empty or small folders produced by your moving actions.
