This is more complicated than an INDEX() function, though, because there is the potential for duplicate score numbers.  In that case, doing an INDEX() on the score number will return the row number of the first instance of the score, which isn't always what I'll be looking for.

So what I'd like is to have a way to find the cell reference that the LARGE() or SMALL() function identifies.  Then I can do an OFFSET() to move to the correct column, so I can pull the matching text.

ashish mathur say:

Hi,

 

Try this

 

1. Suppose your data is in range C3:D18 of sheet1 (headings are in row 3)

2. On sheet2, in cell B3, enter the following formula =MAX(Sheet1!$C$4:$C$18)

3. On sheet2, in cell C3, enter the following array formula (ctrl+Shift+Enter) and copy down

 

=IFERROR(INDEX(Sheet1!$C$4:$D$18,SMALL(IF(Sheet1!$C$4:$C$18=$B$3,ROW(Sheet1!$C$4:$C$18)-ROW(Sheet1!$C$3)),ROW(1:1)),2),"")

 

4. On sheet2, in cell E3, enter the following formula =MIN(Sheet1!$C$4:$C$18)

5. On sheet2, in cell F3, enter the following array formula (ctrl+Shift+Enter) and copy down

 

=IFERROR(INDEX(Sheet1!$C$4:$D$18,SMALL(IF(Sheet1!$C$4:$C$18=$E$3,ROW(Sheet1!$C$4:$C$18)-ROW(Sheet1!$C$3)),ROW(1:1)),2),"")

 

By the way, you can get your desired result by filtering as well.  You may select C3:D18 of sheet1 and under the filter drop down of the scores column, select Top 10 under Number filters.

 

Hope this helps.

macropod say:

macropod say:

You could use a ContentControlOnExit macro triggered by updates of the dropdown to insert the corresponding picture immediately after the dropdown, or at bookmark location, or in another content control, for example. Indeed, it's even possible to use such a macro to replace the dropdown text selection with the image.

stu_677 say: I have no skills using VB, however I do have some experience with other languages. Is there a simple set of commands I can run to get this to work?

macropod say:

Suppose, for example, you have a dropdown content control titled 'Fruit' with entries for 'Apple', Banana' & 'Cherry' and jpg pictures for these are located in your 'Pictures' folder. To insert the picture selected from the dropdown into the following paragraph, you could use a ContentControlOnExit macro coded like:

 

Private Sub Document_ContentControlOnExit(ByVal ContentControl As ContentControl, Cancel As Boolean)
With ContentControl
  If .Title <> "Fruit" Then Exit Sub
  With .Range.Paragraphs.Last.Next.Range
    While .InlineShapes.Count > 0
      .InlineShapes(1).Delete
    Wend
    .InlineShapes.AddPicture LinkToFile:=False, SaveWithDocument:=True, _
      FileName:="C:\Users\" & Environ("Username") & "\Pictures\" & ContentControl.Range.Text & ".jpg"
     
  End With
End With
End Sub

I cannot see any email addresses that you posted.

When you created a Microsoft account using your delta.com address, your delta.com emails still go to the delta.com server.  Outlook.com checks for other account emails every 30 minutes and will download up to 50 emails.  If there are more than 50 emails or there is a problem connecting to the delta.com server, it will be another 30 minutes before downloading more emails.

To get your emails when they arrive at the delta.com server:

add an alias to your Microsoft account.

after the alias becomes active, login to your delta.com account and setup email forwarding to the alias address.

Note:

Do not leave a copy of the forward message on the delta.com server or you will end up with duplicates in outlook.com.
