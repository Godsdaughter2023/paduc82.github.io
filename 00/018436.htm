Hello,

I have figured it out.  I used the OFFSET function per your suggestion:

=OFFSET(Architecture!$B$2,0,0,COUNTA(Architecture!$B:$B)-1,1) and this did not work.

Reason:  In my "Sub-table", I have "empty" cells that actually have formulas that were "FALSE", so no value was showing in the cell.  So it looked "empty".  The "COUNTA" was counting this as an non-empty cell.  I did not want this.

=OFFSET(Architecture!$B$2,0,0,COUNT(Architecture!$B:$B),1)

steve rindsberg say:

I can reproduce what I think you're seeing here (Win7 + PPT 2016/365).

I used the same photo twice:

Added as the slide background on one slide

Add as a photo ON the slide on another slide

The difference between the two isn't huge (at least not with this particular photo) but it's there; the image used as the slide b/g is somewhat less sharp/more pixelated than the same image used on the slide directly.

Both images are lower resolution than the original (not surprising, since the original image and the PPTX I created are both about 4.5mb, where the resulting PDF is only 250kb).

One caveat:  most of these alternative PDF makers are simply printer drivers; they probably won't preserve interactivity (hyperlinks/action settings and such) when converting your presentation to PDF.
