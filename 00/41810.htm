hazmat radio say:

NAT stands for Network Address Translation. 

Simply put, the Xbox is trying to communicate out through your router on several ports. Since the router manages your network traffic, that's where the issue usually exists.

There is more info to explain about the process but we'll skip that for now


The NAT Types you'll run across on the Xbox are - 

 - OPEN - meaning the Xbox is able to communicate on the ports that it needs

 - MODERATE - meaning some of the ports are closed and some games/features may not work

 - STRICT - meaning that most ports are closed and most features like chat or connecting to a game server may not work

 - UNAVAILABLE - meaning the router is not allowing the xbox to communicate on any ports


Troubleshooting - 

 - Since it sounds like your using the wifi "at my new apartment complex (don't have access to the modem". The router there may limit your

 - Set your Primary DNS as 8.8.8.8 and Secondary DNS as 8.8.4.4

 - Go back to Alternate MAC Address > Clear - and let the Xbox restart and try your games again


The other issue is that the NAT on the Xbox can be OPEN and when you try and launch a COD game, the game is unable to connect out on the port that it needs through the router and the game will show a NAT Type of STRICT


The other thing that you posted is that you don't have access to the router and then later you post that you "opened eight different ports through port forward triggering," 

I'm not sure if you're saying you do have access to the router? Or if you have a second router in your network setup? 


If the steps above don't work, give Xbox Support a call and reach out to the Networking group for Xbox. They may be able to help.

Most of the time when clients tell me their notebook PC is running hot and the fan is on high, I ask them on what surface is the computer normally used.  In most cases it is on a bed, a cloth on a table or on a lap.  WRONG.  Your notebook (I call it that because it should never be used on a lap) has vents on the bottom.  It needs those vents to be unblocked to suck air in to cool the system.  When you block them by placing your computer on a soft surface, you stop the airflow.
