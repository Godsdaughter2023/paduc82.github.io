davy-delliott say:

Eddie your question is more like 3 questions all asked in one post.

Because your motherboard is the item that is tied to your copy of Windows; if you change your board you will have to change your Windows licence.

If you have genuine copies of either Windows 7 SP1 or Windows 8.1 Update you are eligible for upgrade to Windows 10. But you have the choice here. You can keep either or both Windows 7 or 8 if you wish. Or you can continue and dual boot with Windows 10 and either of Windows 7 or 8.

If you have the Pro versions of Windows 7 or 8 it will upgrade into the Pro version of Windows 10.

As for hardware changes, you can change most hardware except the motherboard. Your copy of Windows is tied to that.

You will be able to use an ISO file burnable to a DVD or USB to clean install if you wish.

One more thing you have not mentioned. Are your copies of Windows 7 & 8 Full Retail versions or OEM versions?

If they are full retail versions they can be transferred from computer to computer. Motherboard restrictions do not apply to them.

OEM versions are tied to that particular motherboard and cannot be transferred.

Andre Da Costa has written an excellent Wiki article on this. Please read this.

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/insider/wiki/insider_wintp-insider_install/frequently-asked-questions-windows-10/5c0b9368-a9e8-4238-b1e4-45f4b7ed2fb9
