Hi MBW8891,

I'm Tyler, an independent advisor trying to help.

If you are seeing this error, it means that OneNote can't sync one or more large files because they are over the size limits{2GB}.

There's a fix that can help you, but following the steps below will remove all earlier versions and backups of your notebooks. I recommend that you make sure that you don't need them. After all these steps you won't be able to revert to any earlier versions of your notebooks. If you rely on the automatic backups that OneNote creates, I suggest that you make copies and keep them in a safe place.

Now, when you delete a page or a section from your notebook, it's then moved to the Notebook Recycle Bin. This feature gives you a chance to recover data that is accidentally deleted. However, when large files stay in the Notebook Recycle Bin for too long, they cause sync errors. You can resolve this issue by emptying the Recycle Bin of Notebook.

Do one of the following:

Click History, then check Notebook Recycle Bin and then go to Empty Recycle Bin.

Click Share, then check Notebook Recycle Bin and then go to Empty Recycle Bin.

OneNote stores cached versions of pages so you can go back to previous versions. If you deleted a large file, it may still be in an older version of the page. You can resolve this by deleting your notebook history.

Do one of the following:
Click History, then Page Versions and then click Delete All Versions.

Click Share then Page Versions and then click Delete All Versions.

After you’ve completed both of these steps, you will have to restart OneNote and then try syncing your notebooks again.

I hope this helps you.

rik_uk say:

I think there is some confusion here, you've mentioned colomns C&D, but there is no reference to them in your query else where... so asuming you meant H&I...

If you change the equation in G4 to =IF(F4=0,0,F4/$F$30) it will stop the #VALUE error in H4 and I4 , returning 0 (zero) instead.

Also, as a note, putting =G4*$I$2 in I4 will give the same result you are currently getting in I4.

max, singapore say: ... > the target column has text #VALUE!
Think you probably have zero length null strings ("") returned by other formulas into either F4 or F30
These are TEXT hence #VALUE! results as Excel cannot calc arithmetics with TEXT
As a fix, you could just use ISERROR, viz : =IF(ISERROR(F4/$F$30),"",F4/$F$30)
