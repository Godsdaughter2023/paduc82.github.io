Encryption on Windows Ultimate

<Moved from Windows XP Security & Privacy to Windows 7 Security & Privacy>

lemp say:

OK.  Did you simply "download" the Windows 7 Ultimate DVD or did you actually install Windows 7 Ultimate?  Even if you have installed it, as I wrote earlier, nothing is encrypted until you take positive steps to make that happen.  Your system will not be "fully encrypted" (whatever you mean by that) merely by installing Windows 7 Ultimate.  You have to take specific steps to encrypt your data.

What is it that you want to do? 

Are you referring to BitLocker, which encrypts the entire drive, or to the Encrypting File System (EFS), which can encrypt individual files and folders?  Typically, BitLocker is used in corporate environments (which is why it's only available in Ultimate and Enterprise versions).

And again, I strongly urge you NOT to encrypt anything until you fully understand what you're doing.  Both BitLocker and EFS are designed with no "back doors" or other means of data recovery if you lose or forget the encryption key.  Because there is no way to recover data that has been encrypted with a corrupted or missing certificate, it is critical that you back up the certificates and store them in a secure location. You can also specify a recovery agent. This agent can restore the data. The recovery agent's certificate serves a different purpose than the user's certificate.

In Windows 7, Devices and Printers opened almost instantaneously. In Windows 8 Pro x64, Devices and Printers takes almost 30 seconds to open, and it takes this long every time I open it. How can I shorten the time needed for Devices and Printers to open?

Before answering, please note the following:
  • I do not get any error message when I open Devices and Printers.
  • I have not made any recent hardware or software changes to the computer.
  • I experience the same behavior when starting Windows 8 in a clean boot environment.

a. user say:

My problem is solved.

Instead of wasting time on generic solutions, I should have looked for a specific cause from the very start. In my case, the problem came from my scanner. In Devices and Printers I opened my scanner and selected Scan Properties. In the Events tab under Actions, Windows had selected "Prompt for which program to run." I changed this to "Start this program" and selected my scanner software from the dropdown list.

Now Devices and Printers opens just about instantaneously.
