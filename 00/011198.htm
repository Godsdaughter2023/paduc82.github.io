I am using MacOS High Sierra.  I also just wrote up this summary of what worked (and didn't):

MS Word “Read Only”/Zero Byte Problems and Method of Reinstalling Office (and saving  Outlook account emails and accounts)

 

(useful when MS office has been corrupted for some reason)

 

  1. Description of the “Read Only” and “Zero Byte” saving problem with MS Word 2016
  2. Attempted solutions that didn’t work
    1. Rebooting computer
    2. Checking Read & Write privileges Mac OS Get Info
    3. Checking “Locked” status in Get Info
    4. Reinstalled MS Word without deleting MS Word app first
    5. Deleting MS Word App, Icon from Dock, and MS Word specific files from Library/Containers and then reinstalling MS Word 2016.
  3. Called Microsoft support and discussed the issue.
    1. They emailed back the solution as one where I had to delete all MS Office apps & Skype for Business (if you have it), and many related system files located in the User/Library folders and subfolders.
    2. Microsoft send a uninstall pdf the detailed the uninstall directors and files to be removed.
      1. Here is an uninstall website for MS Office 2011: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/help/2398768/how-to-completely-remove-office-for-mac-2011
      2. Here is a similar website to the uninstall pdf for MS Office 2016 but doen’t include Skype uninstall: https://www.cloudsupport.help/hc/en-us/articles/227006947-Uninstalling-Office-2016-on-a-Mac
  4. Backing up files for Outlook and why
    1. Deleting Outlook and reinstalling it is the most problematic of the MS Office programs because you may have multiple Exchange, POP, and IMAP email accounts and subfolders. Setting up all the accounts again is a pain, and you may have deleted many emails from IMAP accounts that is still on the email server and you don’t want to download all them again. Therefore, I found the following solutions.
    2. The best solution is to copy and store the following Outlook folder from the user/Library/Container Group folder so that in can be reinstalled later. This folder contains all of your emails and account info. (VERY USEFUL).
      1. /Users/<username>/Library/Group Containers/UBF8T346G9.Office/Outlook/Outlook 15 Profiles/Main Profile
        1. I copied
      2. For other versions of Outlook checkout this web page: /Users/<username>/Library/Group Containers/UBF8T346G9.Office/Outlook/Outlook 15 Profiles/Main Profile
    3. (Do this as well) Another backup procedure to do just in case of issues, is to use the Export function built info Outlook 2016 to back up all of the emails, calendars, notes, etc.  Do this even after you have copied the Outlook/Outlook 15 Profiles folder detailed above.
  5. Use the uninstall pdf guideline to delete all of the listed MS Office apps and files. Reboot. The download the latest MS Office or the one that you have and install all the files.
    1. Launch MS Word and check to see if this reinstallation has solved the Read Only and Saving problem. (it worked for me).
    2. Then Launch Outlook but don’t set up any email accounts. Close/quit Outlook. Then you need to do the following:
      1. Go to Finder and go back to /Users/<username>/Library/Group Containers/UBF8T346G9.Office/Outlook/Outlook 15 Profiles
      2. Backup the new Outlook/Outlook 15 Profiles folder in some other folder just in case something doesn’t go according to plan
      3. Now copy the previous version of the Outlook/Outlook Profile 15 folder that you had copied before with all of your emails (You will notice it is a much larger file than the new one) and paste it back into the /Users/<username>/Library/Group Containers/UBF8T346G9.Office folder.
      4. Relaunch Outlook and is should work with all of your data and accounts.

Conclusion: The actual cause of this MS Word sudden problem was not found, but a complete reinstall of MS Office worked.
