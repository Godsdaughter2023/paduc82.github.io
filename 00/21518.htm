brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

Having AV software on your PC has absolutely no effect on Microsoft's mail servers.  Scanning your PC for malware has absolutely no effect on Microsoft's mail servers.

Spammers want you to see their messages.  Since people don't ever block themselves, this is a common trick to circumvent any antispam filters.  Sometimes there is enough information in the message for Microsoft's junk filter to be able to classify this type of stuff as junk and sometimes there's not.  Spam is a fact of life and some spam is harder to typify for one reason or another.  It's a never-ending battle.

A spammer can make his spew appear to some from any address whatsoever.  It's easy.  It doesn't mean that your account sent the messages, only that your address was hijacked.  Spammers change the sending address all the time so if you're patient, the junk should taper off.  One way to combat it is to make sure your do not include yourself in your Contacts and to set your junk filer to "exclusive".
