As you are using Office 2010, it would be better to use Content Controls rather than Legacy FormFields as the former do not require that the document be protected for filling in forms.  At the same time however, you can prevent parts of the document from being modified by applying Grouping to that part of the document which then only allows dated to entered into the Content Controls themselves.

graham mayor say:

You can do this with a macro easily enough and you don't have to unlock the form in order to do so but the inserted form will be unlocked, so it is a plan to unlink the fields having done so. At its most basic the following code will insert the active document (the form) into a new document. It is but a small step to insert into an existing document, by opening it and setting the range (orng) to where you want to insert the form

Dim oDoc As Document
Dim oTarget As Document
Dim oRng As Range
    Set oDoc = ActiveDocument
    Set oTarget = Documents.Add
    Set oRng = oTarget.Range
    oRng.FormattedText = oDoc.Range.FormattedText
    oRng.Fields.Unlink

If you don't want macros then Content Controls are perhaps the better option, but check boxes are not available to Word 2007 users, and the controls themselves are not availoable to users of Word versions before 2007. I know some of my colleagues have embraced Content Controls with rather more enthusiasm than I have, but for me there remains a lot of mileage in legacy forms.

I think I see a possible fix for you on this issue, but I'll need to make some presumptions as you were not specific enough in your initial posting.

I have seen this problem plenty of times here in the forums.  

If you can see that the actual transmission time of the e-mail to your friend is the same time as actual appointment time, and the e-mail was sent via Outlook Mobile on the PDA, then there is not much that me or my fellow moderators can do, because the problem is a known issue between activesync and outlook mobile.

However, if you did not send the e-mail via Outlook Mobile, and instead synchronized the appointment from your PDA to your PC, and sent the resulting NEW APPOINTMENT that appeared on your PC Outlook to a friend, then (for reasons unknown) it will not carry the same appointment time.   You are better off sending the e-mail from your PDA, and invite all attendees that you wish to know about the appointment in the initial e-mail.
