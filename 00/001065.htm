Ken wrote:

Assume that the original loan amount through the annual insurance premium are values in B1:B8.  Note that interest rate should be entered as 6.25% or 0.0625 in B4, not literally 6.25.  I assume that USA "truth in lending" rules apply.  Further, I assume that 1550.00 is a typo, and the monthly payment is really 1580.00 [1].  I also assume that the monthly payment includes 1/12 of the annual property tax and insurance premium, since 1550.00 [sic] is more than the 1193.26 monthly payment needed to retire the loan in 30 years.

When you say you are "looking for just one value", I assume you mean one formula.  I would not recommend that.  But if that is truly what you want, the following formula calculates [2] the outstanding balance after the last regular payment due on 1/15/2012, and it adds interest and the prorated property tax and insurance premium for the partial month through 2/1/2012 [3].

=ROUND(FV(B4/12,DATEDIF(B3,B6,"m"),B2-ROUNDUP(B7/12,2)-ROUNDUP(B8/12,2),-B1)
*(1+B4*(B6-EDATE(B3,DATEDIF(B3,B6,"m")))/365),2)
+ ROUND((B7+B8)*(B6-EDATE(B3,DATEDIF(B3,B6,"m")))
/(DATE(YEAR(B6),12,31)-DATE(YEAR(B6),1,0)),2)

formatted as Number or Currency with 2 decimal places.

The following might be a more reasonable implementation, which also explains the breakdown of that monstrous formula above [3].

B9,monthly tax and insurance payment:     =ROUNDUP(B7/12,2)+ROUNDUP(B8/12,2)
B10,number of regular payments:               =DATEDIF(B3,B6,"m")
B11,date of last regular payment:                =EDATE(B3,B9)
B12,balance after last regular payment:     =ROUND(FV(B4/12,B10,B2-B9,-B1),2)
B13,interest for partial month:                       =ROUND(B12*B4*(B6-B11)/365,2)
B14,tax and insurance for partial month:
    =ROUND((B7+B8)*(B6-B11)/(DATE(YEAR(B6),12,31)-DATE(YEAR(B6),1,0)),2)
B15,total last payment on 2/1/2012:             =SUM(B12:B14)

Format B9 and B12:B15 as Number or Currency with 2 decimal places.  Format B10 as General or Number with 0 decimal places.  Format B11 as Date with an appropriate subtype.

Although the earlier one-line formula and the formula in B15 have the same result with these particular numbers, there is a potential for a difference of as much as a few cents due to rounding.

-----
[1] The following explains why I believe the monthly payment is not really 1550.00 [sic].  The monthly payment needed to retire the loan in 30 years is 1193.26 = ROUNDUP(PMT(B4/12,12*B5,-B1),2).  The monthly property tax and insurance premium is 384.50 = ROUND(B7/12,2)+ROUND(B8/12,2).  Thus, the total monthly payment should be 1577.76, not 1550.00.  The difference seems to be too large to explain by one or two typos in the annual amounts for property tax and insurance premium.  So I assume that 1577.76 was rounded up to 1580.00, a simple typo.  That results in a monthly loan payment of 1195.50 = 1580.00 - 384.50, which is more than the minimum.  That monthly payment would retire the loan in 358 months = ROUND(NPER(B4/12,1195.50,-B1),0), with a final balance of only 36.00 which the lender might intend to forgive.  (Most loans do not run to full term anyway.)

[2] In general, the computation represented in B9 has the potential for totaling over 12 months to as much as 24 cents more than the annual tax and insurance amounts.  In actual practice, the difference would go toward reducing the loan principal one way or another.  For that reason, these formulas are only approximations.  However, that is not an issue with these particular numbers.

[3] [EDIT] I usually round all dollar-and-cents calculation in order to avoid anomalies that arise with Excel arithmetic.  So I would add one outer ROUND(...,2) to the "monstrous" formula as well as to the formulas in B9 and B15.  This rounding does not alter the visible result.  It merely eliminates infinitesimal numerical errors (in the mathematical sense; not a defect) that can accumulate and affect later dependent calculations, if any.

alicia williams00 say:

Mail Merge - Group and save multiple documents by manager

I have a file with many employees on it - each employee is on its own row, and their manager and other data is on the same row. 

I need to do a mail merge and save the documents down, not as individual documents per employee, but I need each employee to be on their own page, but the file name to be saved as the manager.

Ex. Manager Stacy King has 5 employees. That's 5 individual employee rows with their different salary data, but all having Stacy King listed as the manager. Stacy's file should have 5 pages in it, one for each employee, but she should only have one file.  

So far I've found macros that slip each row into a separate document, but not to where I can get one file with 5 pages in it, saved as "manager's name.

This is the code I've been running but isn't what I need; it gives me 5 individual documents, one for each of Stacy King's employees, instead of the one, 5 page document with all of her employees (one on each page). - I have no experience with macros - please help!

Sub Merge_To_Individual_Files()
'Merges one record at a time to the folder containing the mailmerge main document.
' Sourced from: http://www.msofficeforums.com/mail-merge/21803-mailmerge-tips-tricks.html
Application.ScreenUpdating = False
Dim StrFolder As String, StrName As String, MainDoc As Document, i As Long, j As Long
Const StrNoChr As String = """*./\:?|"
Set MainDoc = ActiveDocument
With MainDoc
  StrFolder = .Path & Application.PathSeparator
  For i = 1 To .MailMerge.DataSource.RecordCount
    With .MailMerge
      .Destination = wdSendToNewDocument
      .SuppressBlankLines = True
      With .DataSource
        .FirstRecord = i
        .LastRecord = i
        .ActiveRecord = i
        If Trim(.DataFields("Updated_Manager_01")) = "Manager01" Then Exit For
        'StrFolder = .DataFields("Folder") & Application.PathSeparator
        StrName = .DataFields("Updated_Manager_01") & "_" & .DataFields("CEO_3")
      End With
      .Execute Pause:=False
    End With
      For j = 1 To Len(StrNoChr)
        StrName = Replace(StrName, Mid(StrNoChr, j, 1), "_")
      Next
    StrName = Trim(StrName)
    With ActiveDocument
      .SaveAs FileName:=StrFolder & StrName & ".docx", FileFormat:=wdFormatXMLDocument, AddToRecentFiles:=False
      ' and/or:
      .SaveAs FileName:=StrFolder & StrName & ".pdf", FileFormat:=wdFormatPDF, AddToRecentFiles:=False
      .Close SaveChanges:=False
    End With
  Next i
End With
Application.ScreenUpdating = True
End Sub

Hi ElvisCandic,

 

Welcome to Microsoft Community. Let me assist you with stopping spam on your account.

 

There are several ways on how you can block spam emails in your mailbox. Here are some options that you can set up in your account:

 

1. Junk mail filter - Selecting Exclusive option. This feature will let you receive emails from your contact list and safe senders list. To set up a junk mail filter to Exclusive, please follow these steps:

 

1.     Sign in to your Microsoft account.

2.     Click the gear icon located on the upper right panel of your mailbox, just beside your username.

3.     Select More mail settings.

4.     Under Preventing junk email, click on Filters and reporting option.

5.     Under Choose a junk email filter, select Exclusive.

6.     Click the Save button.

 

2. Blocked senders list - You can add the sender's email address to your Blocked sender list to stop receiving emails from that contact. Here's how:  

 

1.     Sign in to your Microsoft account.

2.     Click the gear icon located on the upper right panel of your mailbox, just beside your username.

3.     Select More mail settings.

4.     Under “Preventing junk email”, click the Safe and blocked senders option.

5.     Click Blocked senders.

6.     In the Blocked email address or domain box, type the email address of the sender or the domain that you want to block, and then click Add to list. Once you add an email address in your blocked senders list, you may also need to delete the account details from your contact list.

 

Note: You will not be able to block trusted sites domains such as Yahoo.com, Hotmail.com, and Gmail.com. If the domain that you want to block is one of the trusted sites, I recommend adding the complete email address to your Blocked senders list. If they are too many, I suggest creating an email rules to filter your incoming emails.

 

3. Email rules - You can set up email rules to filter incoming emails to automatically delete them and not getting them in your Inbox. Below are the steps on how to do so.

 

1.     Sign in to your Microsoft account.

2.     Click the gear icon located on the upper right panel of your mailbox, just beside your username.

3.     Select More mail settings.

4.     Under Customizing Outlook, click the Rules for sorting new messages option.

5.     Click the New button.

6.     Under Step 1: Which messages do you want this rule to apply to? On the first box with drop down list, select the Sender's address and in the second box with drop down list, select Yes.

7.     On the third box, type sender's email address. You may select any option that you want to use at first, second and third box depending on your preference.

8.     Under Step 2: What action do you want to apply? Select Delete these messages.

9.     Click the Save button.

 

4. Automated tools. Our Automated tools will also allow you to clean your inbox easily. You can choose from setting Sweep option, schedule clean up, Instant actions and filter.

 

·         Sweep features - Sweep lets you delete all the emails you get from a specific sender - past, present and future. It also allows you to choose to move or delete all the messages from the sender. You can also move current and future messages from a sender to a certain folder.

 

·         Schedule cleanup - Schedule cleanup automatically moves or deletes email from a specific sender after 30, 60, or 90 days.

 

·         Instant actions - In this feature, you can just simple point to any email and you can flag messages for follow-up, delete them, or mark them as read.

 

·         Filter– You can easily filter your messages.

 

For more details and steps on how to setup Automated tools, please click here. (http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/outlook-clean-inbox#1TC=t1)
