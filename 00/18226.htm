david a matson say:

Greetings Brenda,

Caller-ID on cell phones is different than what you would be used to on your residential land line.   By virtue of privacy laws, Mobile Operators (wireless companies) will not typically include a party's name on the call, just their telephone number.  Most phones have a software program that will look at the telephone number of the party calling you, and if you have a list of contacts residing on your phone (internally), it will look through those numbers to see if any match.  If it does, it will typically display the info about the party.  

True Caller, to the best of my knowledge, does the opposite: it reaches-out (externally) on the Internet to a large repository of info, into which it dips for personal information about the telephone number in an attempt to provide you with as much info as possible on that party based solely on the calling telephone number.

By reaching out to this 3rd party repository, you are making your incoming calls (the telephone number, your number, and the time of the call) known to a 3rd party (the repository or database owner), who could track your phone usage and possibly gather personal information.  Please keep this in mind when installing this 3rd party Caller ID type program...

I hope this helps!
