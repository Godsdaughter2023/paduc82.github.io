terfar say: That's a symptom of a Word Data Key corruption - usually because Word has
been running when updates are installed.

Run Regedit and expand by double-clicking to the key below. With DATA
selected, press DELETE. Word will automatically recreate a new key when it
is restarted.

HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Office\12.0\Word\Data

Symptoms of Data Key corruption include:

   •    The mouse does not work when you use Word.
   •    You cannot open a Word document from the Search window in Windows
Vista.
   •    You cannot open a Word document from Windows Desktop Search.
   •    Word crashes when you try to start or exit Word.
   •    Word crashes when you open the Open dialog box.
   •    Word crashes when you save a document.
   •    Word crashes when you close a document.

If you feel uncomfortable editing the registry yourself, Microsoft have a
fix that will do this for you.

C:\Program Files\Microsoft Office\Office14\STARTUP

Your Word Startup can typically be found in your profile. Copy/paste the following in the address bar of the Windows Explorer and press Enter:

%AppData%\Roaming\Microsoft\Word\STARTUP

Note the above is the default location and it's possible to modify. bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

I'm truly sorry that you're having difficulties but I'll try to clarify whatever I can. It may be a bit difficult to do for some of your points because (a) some appear to be user-specific & (b) most of the 'whys' having to do with software design are something we can't answer... We don't write it, we just use it :-)

Styles:

1. Styles are not re-applied after undo'ing a delete action: why are styles not re-applied after a delete action is undone?

I don't see this here with Paragraph Styles nor Character Styles. For example, If I delete a paragraph that has Heading 1 applied to it, then Undo the deletion the text is restored with the Heading 1 style applied.

2. Style management is horrendous: why is there no option to send multiple styles to the Quick Styles menu in the ribbon? Also no option to send styles to normal.dotx or a new template en masse.

No argument that there is much room for improvement, but even PC Word doesn't offer such a capability, at least not through the Format> Styles dialog, itself. However, styles can be copied from any one file/template to another in groups using the Organizer. Use the Shift Key to click a continuous listing and/or use the Command Key to select multiple non-consecutive items. See the Word 2011 Help article: Copy styles and AutoText between documents or templates

Inclusion in the Quick Styles gallery is a property of the individual style. The feature is new in Word 2011 so I would expect that wasn't considered or couldn't be implemented in time for release. If you aren't already familiar with them you may find this Word Help article useful: Apply, change, create, or delete a Quick Style set

3. Why does 'New from template' not retain styles from the selected template?

Again, I don't see that here... Any documents generated from a template include the Style saved in that template.

4. Word crashes after adding styles to the normal.dotx template. Not sure why, but this has happened more than once.

This suggest that your Normal.dotm template may be corrupt. I can't be sure without knowing exactly how your going about it.

5. I have tried numerous times to assign a keyboard shortcut (in this case cmd+shift+s) for the 'Styles' command, and despite the key combination appearing correctly in the menu (or disappearing entirely if saved to normal.dotx instead of the current document), when the specified key combination is pressed the old command fires instead of the new command. (edit: I have now fixed this by changing Apple keyboard settings / shortcuts / app shortcuts, but this does not explain why it does not work in Word settings)  

That particular keystroke has been hard-coded to the File> Save As command for as long as Mac Word has been in existence. The 'bug' is that it gives the impression that it can be reassigned when in reality it can't be. Your method works because OS assignments trump those in applications programs.

Themes:

1. Why can't themes be defined in Word?

Themes were forced onto Mac Word by the PC side. It was [IMO] a last minute requirement which the developers didn't have time to fully implement. PowerPoint can be used to create themes [which are global to the suite]. It allows for a fully customized set of Theme Colors as well as your choice of the Built-in Theme Fonts, but you can't pick fonts of your own choice. Also, Themes created on a PC can be brought to a Mac and if you know XML you can write your own.

2. Why can't themes include styles?

Themes & Styles are completely different species. One of the major differences being that Themes are an Office feature available to PPT & Excel as well, whereas those programs do not support Word Styles. You may find further explanation in: Differences between templates, themes, and Word styles

3. Why can't a theme be removed once selected? Namely, there is no option to 'clear theme', only to select another theme.

As of Word 2007 [PC] & Word 2011 there is no such thing as a Word document that uses no Theme. New documents you create use the default Office Theme. If you want to 'remove' a Theme you've applied reselect Office from the gallery.

If you want to pursue any individual issues it would be best to post each as a separate question. Include a detailed description of the steps taken as well as your version update level of both Office 2011 & OS X.
