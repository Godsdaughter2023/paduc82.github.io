Get-AppXPackage -AllUsers | Foreach {Add-AppxPackage -DisableDevelopmentMode -Register "$($_.InstallLocation)\AppXManifest.xml"}

get-appxpackage Microsoft.Windows.Photos | remove-appxpackage

Get-AppxPackage -allusers Microsoft.Windows.Photos | Foreach {Add-AppxPackage -DisableDevelopmentMode -Register “$($_.InstallLocation)\AppXManifest.xml”}

%LocalAppData%\Packages\

LCD display screens on notebook computers are made of two thin layers of glass with dark liquid crystal material in between.

 

When the LCD display glass is broken any of the following may happen:

 

1. Lines or patterns may appear on the screen. There may be many lines or only a few lines.

 

2. Black “spots” may also appear. This can be liquid crystal material spilling out of a crack. A crack may be present which causes lines to appear but no liquid crystal spots are apparent. The spots may be small or may appear later or grow larger in time. Customers often say that there were no black spot(s) when the unit was sent for repair, so it must have occurred during or after shipment.

 

3. The screen may be totally black; however, some sort of pattern can usually be seen if you look closely.

 

Based on this situation, I would suggest you to contact the manufacturer of the laptop for better assistance on this issue.

thahaseena m say:

Hi BONQU,

Welcome to Microsoft Answers.

Unfortunately you cannot change the resolution for the logon screen. The resolution that is set for the desktop (for e.g. 1280 x 960) is taken as a default resolution for the logon screen also. Changing only the resolution for the logon screen is not possible.

You can change the resolution for the desktop to (1280 X 800) and check whether the resolution for the logon screen has changed or not. Follow the article mentioned below to change the resolution of the desktop.

Change screen resolution

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows-vista/Change-screen-resolution

Hope this information is helpful.
