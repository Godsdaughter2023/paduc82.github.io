graham mayor say:
Maybe you would have more luck with a macro? http://www.gmayor.com/installing_macro.htm   

Const sChar1 As String = "bbb"
Const sChar2 As String = "ff"
Dim oPara As Paragraph
Dim strCount As String
Dim i As Long
strCount = vbNullString
For i = 1 To ActiveDocument.Paragraphs.Count
    Set oPara = ActiveDocument.Paragraphs(i)
    If InStr(1, oPara.Range.Text, sChar1) > 0 Then
        If InStr(1, oPara.Range.Text, sChar2) > 0 Then
            strCount = strCount & i & ", "
        End If
    End If
Next i
strCount = Trim(strCount)
If Right(strCount, 1) = "," Then
    strCount = Left(strCount, Len(strCount) - 1)
End If

strCount = Left(strCount, InStrRev(strCount, ",") - 1) & _
" & " & Right(strCount, Len(strCount) - InStrRev(strCount, ",") - 1)
MsgBox "The strings " & sChar1 & " & " & sChar2 & " appear together in paragraph numbers " & strCount

I write lengthy reports and have an extensive set of Quick Parts that I regularly drop in as boilerplate. These are on my Win 10 Desktop -- I have Office 365.

I have purchased a new Surface Pro 4, also with Office 365 installed (same MS account.) How can I move my Quick Parts Gallery to this new machine?

Thanks in advance.

Chris

jay freedman say:

All Quick Parts are stored in one or more templates. To see where your existing Quick Parts are, click Quick Parts > Building Block Organizer and look at the Template column:

Typically, they'll all be in your Normal template (%appdata%\Microsoft\Templates\Normal.dotm) or in the Built-In Building Blocks template (%appdata%\Microsoft\Document Building Blocks\1033\16\Built-In Building Blocks.dotm, although the "1033\16" part depends on how Office was installed).

To bring those Quick Parts to the Surface, copy each template listed in the Organizer and paste it into the corresponding folder on the new machine. Overwrite the existing file if there is one (which would just be the out-of-the-box Microsoft version).
