kosh vorlon say:

Hi RW,

 

Not precisely.  I'm saying that the Windows Firewall is perfectly adequate and I don't know of any others that provide better protection (but others may have more configuration options or bells and whistles, some will provide essentially equivalent protection, and some will not be as good as Windows Firewall).  Of course, some may argue that there are Firewalls that are better than Windows Firewall, but that's more a subject for debate and discussion than anything I've even seen firmly and convincingly proven.  And we know Windows Firewall works well with Windows and other Microsoft products and has great support.  And I've never heard of software conflicting with Windows Firewall where I have heard such things regarding 3rd party brands - especially involving browsers, email programs, and security programs as well as network setups (not just the internet, but the internal intranet you may have in your home using cables, wireless routers/modems, infrared devices, bluetooth devices, remote deskop (internally or externally) if used, parental controls, interfaces with home security and/or monitoring systems, wireless devices like printers or scanners or fax machines, and other such things).

 

Remember, your computer without a Firewall grants anyone who tries access to the system through any network or remote connection through ports.  There are many ports, most of which average users never need.  The firewall essentially blocks ALL ports except those that are specifically excluded.  That's where you come in.  Each has a default list of excluded ports.  You need to review that list and remove any exclusions that you know you don't need (and be sure you KNOW what you are blocking before you remove an exclusion).  Every exclusion represent a hole in your security through which a hacker could gain access.  The fewer the better.  When I first went through the list, I cut probably just a bit under 50% of those listed - but I cut some I used but so rarely that I'd rather have the Firewall blocking it and take the extra effort to unblock the port temporarily those rare times I need it than to leave it open at all times.  Although for Vista, this article will help you understand the basic concepts http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows-vista/Firewall-frequently-asked-questions.   Pay specific attention to the last one on the list as it provides links to other articles where you can get better and more detailed information that may help you choose and decide.

 

I'm not going to suggest that here since you already have the product and the suite.  It's not what I'd have chosen, but it's probably one of the better suites on the market and far, far better than some other choices you could have made.  I believe it will probably do the job.  I quite simply do not know how well it's firewall works as I've never used this suite before.  It may in fact be just as good and offer more features or it may not provide the same level of protection or cause conflicts or problems with other programs or with your networking (depending on how you are setup).  I just don't know.  You bought the suite because you wanted more features than just antivirus protection.

 

I think you can guess what I personally would do, but I cannot advise you on what to do here - it's your software, your computer, and your decision to make.  Keep in mind that you can always disable the KIS Firewall and enable the Windows Firewall (or do the reverse) any time you choose.  That comment in my post was directed at all recipients and I didn't edit it for you specifically - I didn't mean it to come across as specifically urging you to switch now that you already have purchased another product.  I was trying to tell people in general there was no need to spend money on a separate firewall when Windows Firewall was excellent and would do the job perfectly well.  On the other hand, Windows Defender does NOT provide adequate protection and some type of antivirus program must be added (which may or may not disable Defender during installation) to be safe.

 

In short, the decision is yours to make.

 

I hope this helps clarify things.

 

Good luck and best wishes!

Error message " Unable to save display settings" mainly indicates that you try to extend or duplicate the desktop to the two external monitors in addition to the laptop screen. (A). Delete registry key.
1)Press Win + R to open a run box.
2)In the Run box, type regedit, and then click OK.
3)Locate the following registry subkey:

HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\MobilePC\TMM

4)Right-click the subkey that you located in step 2. Then, click Delete.
5)When you are prompted to confirm the delete operation, click Yes.
6)On the File menu, click Exit to exit Registry Editor.
7)Log off the computer. Then, log on to the computer.

(B).  Extend your display by changing display settings.
1)Open Screen Resolution by clicking the Start button , clicking Control Panel, and then, under Appearance and Personalization, clicking Adjust screen resolution.
2)Click the drop-down list next to Multiple displays, click Extend these displays, and then click OK.
