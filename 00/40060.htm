a. user say:

The prevailing wisdom is to ignore, do not install and then hide any optional hardware driver updates (video, graphics, audio, network) offered by Microsoft Windows Update.

Microsoft does not know what the best hardware drivers are for your system and is notorious for sending driver updates that can cause your system to malfunction or not boot at all.

Only get hardware driver updates from the hardware or computer manufacturers WWW page.  Most have online scanners that will analyze your system and if there are updates available they will be offered but that doesn't mean you have to install them unless they fix a specific problem you are having.

Allegedly Windows 7 can be configured to not even offer hardware driver updates - see this KB article:

https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/kb/2500967

Sometimes that might not work so you can do it yourself like this (or do both):

Click on System Summary, click Edit, Select All (Ctrl-A), Copy (Ctrl-C) and then paste (Ctrl-V) the information back here in your next reply.

For video driver information, expand the Components, click Display, click Edit, Select All (Ctrl-A), Copy (Ctrl-C) and then paste (Ctrl-V) the information back here.

There will be some personal information (like System Name and User Name), and whatever appears to be private information to you, just delete it from the pasted information.

3. On the General tab, click Selective Startup, and then click to clear the Load startup items check box. (The Use Original Boot.ini check box is unavailable.)
