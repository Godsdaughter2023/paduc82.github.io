daniel g. grau say:

I don't use W2016, so it may offer an easier way to do this.

Make sure you have the current keyboard showing in the main menu, next to the clock. To do this:

  • Access System Preferences > Keyboard, click on the InputSources tab and enable "Show input menu in menu bar."
  • Now click on the Keyboard tab and enable "Show Keyboard, Emoji & Symbol Viewers in menu bar."

After that:

  • Click on the flag icon next to the clock and select "Show Emoji and symbols."
  • If you see a small window, click on the Command icon at the top right.
  • In the leftmost column, highlight Unicode.
  • Scroll to "2776" and click once on the circled 1.
  • In the menu of 1's, select the one you need with a double-click.

If you will be doing this a lot, define such numbers as AutoCorrect entries, so that whenever you need to invoke them, you'd type, for instance, 1\ .
