ken sheridan say: I've never tried saving a form as a report myself; it's not something most developers would generally do.  But from your description it sounds like your form is beyond what this particular piece of functionality will cope with.  The problem may simply be that the controls as they appear in your form, when transferred to a report, exceed the physical length of a report's design area (558 mm if I remember correctly).

I suspect you may have to bite the bullet and design the report yourself.  You can use the form's current RecordSource property as that of the report, and then add the relevant bound controls in whatever layout you wish.  Unlike in a form, in a report a bound control does not have to be as deep as necessary to hold all the data for it to be all visible.  The controls can be shallow, enabling you to fit many in the available design area, and provided that the CanGrow property of the control itself, and of whichever section of the report it is in (almost certainly the detail section in this case) is set to True (Yes) then the control will grow when the report is opened to allow all the data to fit, so it will be different depths in different rows depending on the data in each.

You can use the report wizard to create a report, but you might find that by doing this you come up against the same problem of fitting the controls into the maximum design area allowed.  Using the wizard is worth a try as it can cut out a lot of tedious design work, but I suspect you might have to resort to laying out the report manually.  You may be able save a lot of effort by using the existing report as the starting point and amending its design.

ken sheridan say: I think the real problem is that you are seeing a report as an expression of a form.  While Access includes the functionality it is no more than a cheap and cheerful solution to provide an instant report.  Forms and reports are really different types of expressions of the data intended for different purposes, and a form layout will rarely be entirely suitable for a report layout.

Your problem will be solved if you create a new report based on the form's query and lay it out specifically as a report rather than a reproduction of the form.  The fact that the form has two pages will then be completely irrelevant.  As I said before, you can use the report wizard to avoid much of the tedious work of designing the report, though you'll probably find you have to do some fine tuning of the layout.  I'd be doubtful of your reaching a satisfactory solution by tweaking the current report.

Hi,

Before starting the troubleshooting steps just a quick question:

1. Only the Word files which are opened get closed or all the files (including other Office applications) get closed when closing the Word document?

2. Only the files those are maximized gets closed or even the files which are open and are minimized also gets closed?

3. Did you click on Office Icon and Close or Office icon and Exit Word?

 

If you click on the Office Icon and click Exit Word it will close all the Word documents that are open. If you have opened multiple Word documents then click on Office icon and click on Close or click on the X mark on the top right hand side corner of the window.

 

Also open Word in safe mode and check if the issue occurs in safe mode too:

·         Close Word.

·         Click on Start< All Programs< Accessories< Run

·         In Run window and type winword /safe

Note: There is a space between 'Winword' and '/'.

 

If Word works fine in safe mode then the issue might be due to add-ins. In order to disable the add-ins follow the steps given below:

·         Click the Office Icon and Click Word Options.

·         In the left hand side of the Word Options dialog box, click Add-Ins.

·         Under Manage, click the COM Add-ins.

·         Uncheck all the Add-Ins listed under COM Add-ins.

·         Click Close and restart Word.

 

I hope the above suggestions helps to resolve your issue. If you need any further assistance reply and we will be glad to assist you.
