Resource Assignment Based on Skill Level - Microsoft Community

michael_project say:

Resource Assignment Based on Skill Level

What are the best practices for assigning resources with varied skill levels to a task? 

If you have a task called Paint Room and assign it the following resources (they are all working full time on painting the room):

Foreman (1 person) 

Painter (3 people) 

Helper (3 people)

Project treats all trades/resources equally. Adding another helper, in Project's eyes, is the same as adding another painter. Another resource, regardless of skill, is calculated the same and adjustments to duration and/work are carried out by Project equally.

The problem is that although this task needs a foreman and the helpers, only the 3 painters are actually applying the paint to the walls....thereby directly driving the completion of the task. The foreman needs to be involved in the task, managing the crew, keeping time-sheets, etc, and the helpers need to be involved in the task for mixing paint, masking off areas that don't get painted, etc......but adding more foreman or helpers will not make the paint get on the walls any quicker. Only adding more painters will make this happen (at which point you might need more helpers for additional support)

Would you break out the Paint Room task into multiple sub-task as shown below:

Paint Room - MGMT

Foreman (1 person)

Paint Room - Apply Paint

Painters (3 people)

Paint Room - Support

Helpers (3 people)

MSP has no way to know who is who, or that a foreman is different from a painter.

You are better off with resources defined as individual people

Painter 01

Painter 02

Painter 03

etc rather than assigning Painters at 300%.

Yes you are better off breaking the task into smaller pieces This is always the magic solution to almost any problem. But then you have the problem of making the MGMT and Support tasks matching the duration of Apply Paint, as you add or subtract painters.

I don't think so. I think you are over thinking it. You seem to be on the verge of blaming MSP for not being real world enough.
