Ok!

I am having this exact same issue and it has always been this way. I usually just use Photoshop to change the color, but that gets dragged out. There has to be a solution! I do not know if this is the correct method to suggest to you, but it has gotten me around the issue temporarily and it works.

You basically need to make your photo or object completely "white" by using brightness. Brightness has to be set to 100%. I even tried 99% and my clip art instantly went back to a solid black color. Contrast effects the resulted darkness of the color.

SEE PHOTO

 

Right click image and select "Format Picture". Select "Picture Corrections" or the mountain symbol. Set brightness to 100% and verify that your image is white (or invisible). Then select "More Variations" and choose your new color. Utilize contrast, saturation and temperature to get the color to your liking.

Your photo is more susceptible to the "More Variations" feature of colors when it's white. Try it and let me know if it works for you.
