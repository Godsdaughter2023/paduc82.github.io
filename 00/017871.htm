My lab has frequently exported entire slides generated in PowerPoint as images (in both TIFF and JPEG formats).  However, with our recent upgrade to Windows 8.1 and Office 2013, these images are now being cropped during the export process.  Although the PPT slide is in a widescreen-like aspect ratio, the images that export look more like the traditional 4:3 ratio, and are cutting out content on the right side of the slide.  How do I change the settings to export the entirety of the desired slide?
steve rindsberg say:
Yep, that'd be the culprit, unquestionably.   It's broken.  Badly.

If you have access to a PPT 2010 install, try that instead.  It should work fine.

Also, "300 dpi" is PublisherSpeak for "We have NO clue what we're doing here.  Or at least we don't trust our authors to have a clue."  

If they told you what size they'll be running the image, 300 dpi might mean something ... if 1" wide, then that'd only be 300 pixels.  If a cover image, well ... different story altogether.

IAC, if you're stuck using 2013, try this:

Save your slide/presentation as a PDF.

Open the PDF in Reader/Acrobat, zoom in, then use the Snapshot tool to select the area you want to save as an image.  That'll put a rather high rez version of the selected area on the clipboard (the closer you zoom, the higher the resolution).

Then you can paste the image into an image editing program and save from there.
If you don't have a program for this, download and use the free IrfanView, which'll work perfectly.
www.irfanview.com
