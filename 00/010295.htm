That's a symptom of a Word Data Key corruption - usually because Word has
been running when updates are installed.

is restarted.
 
HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Office\11.0\Word\Data
 
Symptoms of Data Key corruption include:
 
   •    The mouse does not work when you use Word.
   •    You cannot open a Word document from the Search window in Windows
Vista.

   •    Word crashes when you try to start or exit Word.
   •    Word crashes when you open the Open dialog box.
   •    Word crashes when you save a document.

Addendum from a thread in another forum:

This is built in to Windows and works as far back as XP (not sure in which service pack it appeared, but I'm guessing 2).

Basically, any "dangerous" file downloaded through Internet Explorer (can't speak to any other browsers) gets a small piece of information added to an "alternate data stream" which says "This is a downloaded file." When you attempt to run/open such a file in XP you get a dialog telling you that this is a downloaded file and may be risky and asking you if you want to allow it to run/open. There is also a check box which says something like "Always ask before running this file." If you uncheck the box it has the same effect as using the Unblock button you described above, and both methods simply remove this alternate data stream from the file. I'm surprised you've never seen this before.

jacksontong say:

Are the email addresses of closed accounts recycled?

When the new Outlook web service (outlook.com) was released 2 months ago I quickly made an account to get the name I wanted.  But I realize I want to use that new account name for my current account with has the 25 GB skydrive.  So I did some researching and found that Hotmail/Outlook accounts are recycled once they've been closed for 270 (approx) days.  So I immediately closed my new account so I don't have to wait as long to rename my current account with the new name.

I just want to confirm that after waiting 270 days, I will be able to rename my current account with the address name of the now closed account's address, correct?  Just wanted to confirm this because 270 days is a long time and I will be deeply disappointed if after waiting that long it turns out I can't after all, use the closed account's address to rename my current account.

a. user say:

Hi,

 

Yes, you can use your @outlook.com as a primary account of your @hotmail.com account. However, you would need to delete your @outlook.com and you will wait for 360 days for the account name may be permanently deleted. Then you can proceed to rename your @hotmail.com account to your previous @outlook.com account name.

 

Note, after 360 days the deleted @outlook.com will become available in the system for pick up but you may not necessarily get it back in case someone else takes it before you! In short, you bear the risk of losing the account name.

 

You may refer to these steps for better understanding:

 

1. Delete your @outlook.com account and wait for 360 days for the account name may be permanently deleted.

2. If the @outlook.com is still available in the system to pick up after 360 days, rename the @hotmail.com immediately. (If the @outlook.com is not available after the deletion process, you need to rename your @hotmail.com to a different @outlook.com account name).

 

Renaming your account causes your sign in name to change from your existing @hotmail.com address to your new @outlook.com address. Your existing address becomes an alias of your new account so that mail delivery is uninterrupted.  After renaming your account you will sign in with your new account to all services you previously accessed with your old account.
