keith a. miller say:

Read through this thread with interest. A lot of people are having issues with setting file associations.

Searching the registry for wordpad.exe, I found several ProgID's that are set to open in Wordpad: docxfile, odtfile, rtffile, txtfile, and Wordpad.Document.1

So, if you create a new key under HKEY_CLASSES_ROOT named .srt and give it any one of the above as its (default) value, you should get the behavior you want.

If you want to customize the icon & description, locate of of the above keys, export it, decide on a unique name, i.e. srtfile, and then open the .reg file in your favorite text editor, and do a search & replace on the original name. You'll then have a registry file you can import. Then edit the newly-created key to your liking.

https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/desktop/shell/how-to-register-a-file-type-for-a-new-application

nikbuescher say:

I'm running Microsoft Spider Solitaire v 6.1 (Windows 7 Home Premium), and it is not impossible.

Out of the 44 games I have played, I have beat 28 of them, with a 14 game winning streak and 4 game losing streak.  I'm sure there are plenty of people with better scores.

 

Please keep in mind that when you press M or H for a hint, the computer is not necessarily pointing out a game-winning move.  It is only pointing out a move that is technically possible. It will point out any opportunity to play a card onto another of the same suit, and will point out some opportunities to play a card onto another of a different suit.  In other words, the computer does not indicate EVERY possible move (except on your last play of your last deal) and it certainly does not indicate the best move you can make.  I only use the hint system after making all of my moves on a hand, just to make sure I haven't missed anything obvious.

 

Determining the best move comes from exploring every possible move with each dealt hand, trying your best to open up free spots on the table and minimizing straights of multiple suits that will be difficult to consolidate and clear up in the late game.

 

The four suit game requires that you sometimes for example place a diamond on a spade instead of a spade on a spade in order to get at the valuable card underneath the diamond.  Sometimes on a deal there are no possible or desirable plays.  When this happens, you may need to undo the deal and move cards around on your previous play to "create some luck" so that the next deal will put the same cards back down in a way that most benefits you.

 

Versus the easier versions of spider, winning the four suit game can take up to a half hour and 300 moves.

Hi CactusDuud,

The recent driver update may have caused this behavior of your Apple Airpods. For us to isolate this problem, please try these steps and see if fixes the problem:

  1. Right-click the speaker icon on your system tray and click Playback devices.
  2. Select your Apple Airpods, then click Properties.
  3. Click the Advanced tab, then uncheck the Allow application to take exclusive control of this device option.
  4. Click Apply, then OK.

If the issue persists, then I suggest rolling back the driver of your Bluetooth device. Here's how:

  1. Right-click the Windows key and select Device Manager.
  2. Expand Bluetooth.
  3. Right-click your Bluetooth device, then click Properties.
  4. Click the Driver tab, then click the Roll Back Driver button.
  5. Follow the on-screen instructions.

If you continue to have this issue even after performing the above steps, please let me know by replying to this thread. This way, I'll get notified and respond back to you as soon as I can.
