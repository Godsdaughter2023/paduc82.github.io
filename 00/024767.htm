rohn007 say:

Sounds like a common mistake. And an easy one to fix.

  1. You bought a new computer.
  2. It came with a factory installed "free trial" of Office 365 (aka "crapware").
  3. You bought a copy of Office 2016 Home and Student
  4. You installed it on the new computer.
  5. A month later you see that informational message.

The problem was created at step 4.  BEFORE you installed your purchased copy you MUST uninstall the free trial.  This is a long standing problem. When there are 2 unactivated copies of Office on the computer the activation system is too stupid to recognize the situation. It does recognize that a valid key was submitted, it just does not apply the key to the right Office installation ... oops.

The fix is simple:

  • Go to Control Panel > Programs and Features
  • Find Office 365 in the list of Installed programs
  • single click on that entry
  • select the Uninstall option
  • after uninstall completes, find the Office 2016 Home and Student entry
  • singe click on that entry
  • select the "Repair" option

Your copy of Home and Student should be activated and work normally. You may have to activate it one more time:

  • In any Office 2016 application Go to File menu > Account command
  • look on the far right of the displayed "Backstage Pane". At the top of the display you'll see the Office logo. Below that you should see the Office bundle name and activation status.
