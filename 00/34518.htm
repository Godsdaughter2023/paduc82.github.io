Welcome to the Microsoft Answers forum.

 

 

If your edition of Windows Supports Aero keep it enabled.

 

If your computer’s hardware is capable of supporting Aero, and the computer has a compatible graphics processor, you will get better performance with Aero enabled.

 

Aero, performs onscreen rendering and processing in the graphics processing unit (GPU), which frees your system’s microprocessor for other tasks. This applies event if your graphics card uses shared RAM. The shared memory will still be allocated to the graphics adapter regardless of whether you are using Aero or not.

 

Additionally, the Aero rendering process is inherently more reliable than a software renderer.

 

If you are determined to not use Aero try this.

 

Try modifying the hardware acceleration for you display adaptor.

 

1.     Right-click any open space of your Desktop, and then select Personalize.

2.     Click on Display Settings, and then click the Advanced Settings (button).

3.     Click on the Troubleshooting (tab), and then click on Change settings.

4.     Move the slider to the left towards None, and then test your system.

5.     If the issue is resolved you can incrementally increase this slider to Full until the issue reoccurs.

6.     Once the issue reoccurs move the slider down one notch.

7.     This should resolve your issue, but allow you to use the Non-Aero theme.
