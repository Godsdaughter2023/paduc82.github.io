  5. Open the application and choose your profile. Set it as the default and quit.

The "Filling in forms" editing restriction is not intended for use with content controls. This is excellent advice.  Some more details from the poster will help too.

PCs have a built-in BIOS that assists in installing an O/S.  This is how a new PC comes to life.  BIOS has to be told where to find the O/S.  Under normal circumstances,  BIOS is told to find the O/S on Drive C.  

There are times when the O/S can get screwed up.  The normal routine then is to get a bootable device and load through BIOS.  When this happens to me, I use the Media Creation Tool on another PC and I ask to give me W10 on an USB stick.  This gives me a bootable devise. 

I then change BIOS  to load from the USB.   When the data is transferred from the USB to Drive C, I am then asked to remove the USB.  At this point I go back to BIOS and change the boot order back to C.  Without the USB  in place, the PC  will likely default to C in any event.

You can see the trap here.  If the load order gets changed to USB, then the PC will try to load from USB anytime an USB is found in place.

Just re-trace your steps here.  Try to load with no extra devices in place.  If your W10 is useless, then you will have to re-install public build.  Never found a dead W10 that I could not bring back to life in the time it takes to just re-install public build.

Not sure of your knwledge level.  Lots of help here, just provide as much detail as you can.
