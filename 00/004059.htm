macropod say:

For a simple AND test with text strings you could use a field coded as:
{IF{={IF{MERGEFIELD Field1}= "True Text" 1 0}*{IF{MERGEFIELD Field2}= "True Text" 1 0}*{IF{MERGEFIELD Field3}= "True Text" 1 0}*{IF{MERGEFIELD Field4}= "True Text" 1 0}}= 1 "All conditions met" "At least one condition not met"}

For a simple OR test with text strings you could use a field coded as:
{IF{={IF{MERGEFIELD Field1}= "True Text" 1 0}+{IF{MERGEFIELD Field2}= "True Text" 1 0}+{IF{MERGEFIELD Field3}= "True Text" 1 0}+{IF{MERGEFIELD Field4}= "True Text" 1 0}}> 0 "At least one condition not met" "No conditions met"}

Note: The field brace pairs (i.e. '{ }') for the above examples are all created in the document itself, via Ctrl-F9 (Cmd-F9 on a Mac); you can't simply type them or copy & paste them from this message. Nor is it practical to add them via any of the standard Word dialogues. The spaces represented in the field construction are all required.

To see how to do a range of other tests, check out my Mailmerge Tips and Tricks thread at:
http://www.msofficeforums.com/mail-merge/21803-mailmerge-tips-tricks.html
or:
http://windowssecrets.com/forums/showthread.php/163017-Word-Mailmerge-Tips-amp-Tricks

PS: The above assumes you're using a mailmerge, as per the link in your post. You'd need a different approach with formfield checkboxes.

To set up a conference with two other callers:

1.       Make your first call from the Dialer screen or a contact card.

2.       Once your call has connected, tap Add call to open a list of your contacts with phone numbers.

3.       Tap the contact you want to add, or type the number in the dial pad and tap Call.

4.       Once the call is connected, the in-call screen displays a Flash option. Tap it to “merge” the calls.

So, I played around and found this easier way to resolve this issue in 5 easy steps:

1) Open the Outlook.com app and disable folder sync for your junk mail folder.

2) Exit the Outlook.com app

3) Open your Android Application Manager and STOP the Outlook.com app process

4) Clear your junk mail folder through the web interface

5) Open the Outlook.com app, navigate to the junk mail folder and select the SYNC arrows at the bottom

Your mobile junk mail folder should now resemble what you have on the web.

judith_c. say:
Hi William Diamond,

Welcome to Microsoft Community. We apologize for the delay in response. 

We would like to know what connection you are using in Outlook 2007 to access your account. Are you using Outlook Hotmail Connector, POP, etc? Is the deleted folder also accessible in Outlook.com or it is a local folder in Outlook 2007?

Please be informed that once you delete a folder it cannot be recovered but you can simply recreate it. 

However, if you are using Outlook Hotmail Connector and the deleted folder can be access before in Outlook.com. Emails included in that folder will be moved to your Deleted folder and will be stored there for 5 days. You can check your Deleted folder if emails are still there. If not, you can try to recover the missing emails through web using the auto-restore option. Click here for the steps on how to do this. 

Outlook.com will recover as many messages as possible and put them in your Deleted folder. When the recovered messages are back in your Deleted folder, you should move the important ones back to your inbox or created folders because the Deleted folder gets cleaned out periodically. Be reminded that self-recovery may not work when deleted emails are more than 5 days ago.

If you are using POP connection and the deleted folder is a local folder, I'm sorry to inform you that emails are already purged in the system. 

Let us know if you have further questions.
