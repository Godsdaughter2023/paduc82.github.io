cabgx3 say:

On sheet 1 you could put a helper column adjacent to the right end of your data, say column D if you have nothing beyond the names, and put this formula in D2 (assuming a header in D1) and copy down.

=IF(ISNA(VLOOKUP(C2,SHEET2!C:C,1,FALSE)),"",VLOOKUP(C2,SHEET2!C:C,1,FALSE))

Then do an Autofilter on that column for the "blanks" and there you have all the ones that do not have a match on Sheet2.  You can work with them there or copy to another sheet for further manipulation.

macropod say:

I think there may be some inconsistencies in how the Chicago Style specification is being interpreted. There are two formats:  'Author-Date'; and 'Notes and Bibliography', both of which you can see at: http://www.chicagomanualofstyle.org/tools_citationguide.html.

What you say Word 2013 outputs for the bibliography is a format that conforms to the 'Author-Date' system, but my copy of Word 2010 outputs it more like the 'Notes and Bibliography' system. You could probably get the Word 2010 output by using that version's Chicago.xsl file.

Caveat: I've seen other posts where it's been asserted that Word 2010 uses the 'Author-Date' system; not the 'Notes and Bibliography' system. See, for example: http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/office/forum/office_2010-word/chicago-style-notes-bibliography-vs-author-date-in/4e14986f-7b90-4b5d-9df6-1e612c58d7dd. My experience is to the contrary. That said, Word 2010's handling of citations doesn't conform to the Chicago Style specifications for the 'Notes and Bibliography' system. It seems the MS coders took bits from each system and produced a mish-mash of the two.
