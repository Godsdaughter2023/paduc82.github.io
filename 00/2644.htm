richard xu msft say:

Hi FredPoon,

I have reviewed all information you posted. Then, let me help to explain the Litigation hold for you, but I need tell you that you can't use the Litigation hold to hold the mail data before you enable Litigation hold, another words is you can only hold the mail data after you enable this feature.

Place a mailbox on Litigation Hold to preserve all mailbox content, including deleted items and original versions of modified items. When you place a mailbox on Litigation Hold, the user's archive mailbox (if it's enabled) is also placed on hold. Deleted and modified items are preserved for a specified period or until you remove the mailbox from Litigation Hold. And Litigation Hold preserves items in the Recoverable Items folder in the user's mailbox (The default size for this folder is 30 GB). 

About how does Litigation work, there is an article descript it in detail, I suggest you can read how it work from "More information" in this article: https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/dn743673(v=exchg.160).aspx

About additional cost or license for this feature, you can see: To place an Exchange Online mailbox on Litigation Hold, it must be assigned an Exchange Online (Plan 2) license.
