phillip m jones say:

Office 2011 does not handle Right to Left Languages (RTL). It is only just very recently that RTL Languages were added to Office 2016. 

Office 2011 was written in the old binary code Microsoft first used around 1985 or so.  At the time there was no such thing Right to Left language in the Mac system.  And only after a PC version was written was RTL language support added to the PC version software. For years Apple didn't support Right to Left while using Motorola 680X0 Processors because the system was written in Objective - C  and C++. When Mac made the switch to Intel Processors and OSX was created to take advantage, Apple added Support for RTL. Microsoft never did add Right to Left support, until Office 2016 when they quit using the 30 plus year old Binary code and Embarked on using Apple's Cocoa language to write the Office2016 version. So support was already built in. It took Microsoft a little while to create the code to initiate the Right to Left system just adding it about 3-4 version back.  

So, while Office2011 doesn't have RTL support, it is known If a document is written and saved in other non-Microsoft Office packages such as LibreOffice or Pages, then exported to Office 2011, Word will honor RTL  as long as there has been no additional editing. 

If you are contemplating upgrading to Office 2016, my advice would be "do so" with one caveat. Keep 2011 Around for as long as it will work. 2016 is still a work in Progress missing many features you have gotten use to, in 2011. Right now about 85% of what you can do in 2011 is in 2016. I am not sure whether 100% will ever be achievable. It will depend on how vocal the Mac users are. If they stay timid and settle for half-baked like in past 30 some years, What we have in 2016 will stay like it is now.

zigzag3143 (ms -mvp) say:

We have recently implemented a security update called Familiar location protection to keep our customers’ accounts secure. The message "You haven't signed in from this location recently." will occur if you are signing in from a location more than 300 miles from your normal location. The Microsoft account service will prompt you for an additional challenge to complete the log in. Codes can be the date of birth, or receiving a code sent to your SMS number or alternate email if configured.

 

To prevent this you will need to configure your computer as a trusted device 

Given the new security measures, Account Settings requires an extra security, through the use of a security code.  Also known as 2 step authentication

If you mark your computer, phone or tablet as a Trusted Device, you won't be asked for any codes when you access these settings in the future.  To do this

1. Login your account here.

2.  Under Security & Privacy  Click on "Manage Advanced Security" 

3. You will see the "protect your account" page. Select any option to get a code, and request for it.

4. You will see a checkbox that says : "I sign in frequently on this device. Don't ask me for a code". Tick the checkbox, and enter the code.

Your computer/device is now Trusted. Whenever you use your account on that specific computer/device, you will no longer asked for a code.
