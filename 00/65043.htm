The truth is that the Event Log often shows a great many important and critical errors which were then corrected by the system (but still reported because they happened).  While it would be nice to see no such errors, they do exist but do not necessarily mean that you need to take any action about them.  The action you took with SFC is good but it revealed nothing of major importance from what you posted.  If you are booting normally without getting any error during boot (I don't mean later from the event viewer, I mean during boot itself) - then there's probably nothing significant wrong with the boot process.  And if you lose performance monitoring that's not really a major problem - the system will function fine without it (you just may not get all the notifications you might otherwise receive and may not be able to check your performance against established standards but that is somewhat optional though the performance messages can sometimes help).

The recovery disk works a bit different from the above procedures but if you follow the prompts from the System Restore menu option with the above information you should be able to restore with no problems.

g.       Log off the computer.
