stanyau say:

It looks like further information has been released regarding how firmware is updated on the Surface Dock. I have to admit, the solution is very clever indeed!

This article describes how the update process works - if you're using Windows Update, then the firmware is actually flashed in two stages. https://technet.microsoft.com/en-gb/windows/mt703783.aspx

It looks like there is a tool that will allow you to manually flash the dock as well, the aptly named "Microsoft Surface Dock Updater". Alas, it is not yet available to download as of today (20 April) despite the article linking to the appropriate Surface Tools. However, I'd expect that this would come online soon, given the depth and details of the instructions and screen-grabs - the software must be all but finished... https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/mt703784.aspx

The April 2016 updates (released 19 April) for Surface Pro 4 and Surface Book include new firmware for the dock - (version 76661360 for the main chipset and version 76634736 for the DisplayPort). If you look very closely at the dock, when disconnected (which is when the firmware gets flashed), you can see a very subtle light flashing inside the network port - this stops flashing when the firmware has been updated. Very sneaky, and very cool!

I hope that helps - you may or may not even notice that the Surface Dock gets firmware flashed in this way, but the article (especially the registry entries which show current and pending firmware) is very insightful!
