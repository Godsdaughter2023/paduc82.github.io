OK, applying tags to table shapes is a non-starter.  Some day, MS will finish wiring together the PowerPoint OM for tables, but for now, it's got some major holes in it.
 
However, supposing you had text in a table cell like your current forEach.  You could apply a tag to the table or the slide containing it:
 
oTbl.Tags.Add "forEach", "<wr:forEach Select ... etc etc". 
 
Your code reads the text in the cell, looks for a matching tag:
 
If len(oTbl.Tags("forEach)) > 0 Then ' it's a tag
   strYourCode = oTbl.Tags("forEach")
   ' do what you need to with your code
End If
 
Your character formatting remains whatever's been applied to the text in the table cell.  The tag and code remain independent of it.

PowerPoint has a limited amount of storage allocated to hyperlink storage.  Exceed the limit and it starts corrupting previously entered hyperlinks.  And as you've seen, it introduces formatting issues.

hi sensei,

per your requirement, you can use onedrive for business sync app to sync files between local disk and your onedrive for business library or sharepoint online site libraries. once the synchronization connection is built, files online will be synced to local disk, as the same, files put in the synced folder will be synced to the cloud automatically. see: sync onedrive for business or site libraries to your computer

whether storing files in your onedrive for business library or your sharepoint online site (such as team site) libraries depends on the purpose of using of those files. files stored in user’s onedrive for business library are private, they are in private usage. if a document is a collaborative effort related to a project, the sharepoint online site might be a better choice. see this article to learn more: should i save my documents to onedrive for business or a team site?
