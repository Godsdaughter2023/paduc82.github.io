hairyfool say:

When AC power is provided the laptop system only runs on the AC supply because the battery voltage will be slightly lower than the AC voltage. However the supply may trickle charge the battery continuously although this is less common.

What tends to happen in practice is when the laptop is 1st powered up even with essentially  a fully charged battery some charging will take place. This is needed in order to confirm that the battery is in fact fully charged, the charge current shows the state of the battery far better than just measuring the voltage.

The degree of control available to the operating system is very limited with it primarily a function of the laptop electronics (it still charges even if you don't switch on).
