Once you are sure that you have all the data you need safely backed up (and ideally readable/verifiable in another PC), you can then re-install the OS, set up whatever user accounts/passwords you want and restore the data from the removable device.

[FIX] Laptop dosen't restart (MSI GE60) - Microsoft Community

dec_bot say:

[FIX] Laptop dosen't restart (MSI GE60)

Hey,

So my computer's just about 2 years old, and I have an issue with restarting.

Basically, about 1 in 20 times that I press restart, the Restarting... screen hangs, to the point that I've had it like that for 30 minutes. Every time this happens (It's happened about three times now) I have to hold down the power button, which I know is incredibly bad for the computer, but I have no options. 2 of the 3 times it's happened, the computer has had a refresh thing, and deleted recently installed .exes, with no other consequences.

Now, I've noticed some strange symptoms. Firstly, It only happens when I restart, never when I shut down, and it also never happens when it needs to update.So basically, I've been updating the computer and shut downing so that it has minimal chance of happening.

All I want is just a reason why this is happening, and if maybe upgrading to Windows 10 would change it.

Thanks!

Specs:

MSI GE60

OS: Windows 8.1

CPU: Intel Core i7 4700HQ

GPU: Nvidia GeForce GTX 860M 2GB GDDR5

Memory: DDRIII(L) 8GB

FIX

Ok, I thought I should update people of my current situation in terms of this issue incase someone else has it.

First thing you should do is go into your power settings and make the time it takes to go into sleep mode and the time it takes for the screen to turn off to be incredibly long, as it seems to be an issue of the computer not realising it's restarting and going to sleep during this time. Also check your hibernating settings and make that take a long time too. One more thing, and this seems a bit contradictary, but go into change what the power buttons do, then click Change settings that are currently unavaliable, then disable fast startup. This will make shutting down completley flush everything, meaning no where near as many processes need to be on for as long.

If that doesn't work, I found refreshing my PC to fix it, ever since I refreshed it I've had no problems whatsoever. If you are in a situation where you cant refresh your PC, sign out of your account (and make sure everyone else is signed out) and shut down the computer. If this is a problem, just close everything and hold the power button down and you should be good.

One more thing, if you do end up having this happen, watch your disk usage light (usually labelled with a cylinder). If you wait long enough, it should start flickering every now and then rather than staying on for long periods of time. At this point, you can usually hold down the power button safely.

Lesson to take away from this is that you need to refresh your PC to compltely solve the problem.

Good luck.
