alexmobile77 say:

Cortana "Something's not right ..." => Finally fixed for me => International users

Hi folks,

 

I just want to briefly share what has helped in my case to fix Cortana on my SP4 after it had worked flawlessly and then was only responding with the dreadful “something’s not right …” sentence. I was able to start Cortana with the “Hey” command, but everything after opening she wouldn’t listen to what I had to say.

 

HW info and Setup:

 

Surface Pro4 with initial Win 10 Installation, about 8 weeks old.

I am using the device in Germany and have reset everything to location US on day one. This worked perfectly to enable Cortana and I had no problems at all for the first 3 weeks. After a while, Cortana would behave in the above described way. I have tried ALL available tips and couldn’t enable her. For some reason I was suspecting the Realtek drivers on the SP4 in the end, because I had been messing around with them recently. But honestly, do you guys recall all the system changes you made on a new OS after a few weeks?

 

Like I said, I’ve really tried everything in the past weeks, uninstalling and reinstalling language packs, rebooting a zillion times, disabling/enabling Cortana and what have you.

 

I think I finally found something which does the trick last night.

Some time ago, I made an adjustment on my side which seemed to cause problems with Cortana’s regional settings. In order to have my home banking software only working properly (Germany) I changed “.” to “,” for decimal symbol. While I was at it, I also changed the time format and Measurement System.

Just reset these settings according to the following steps:

1.      Open the classic Control Panel

2.      Select Clock, Language, and Region

3.      Select Change date, time or number formats

4.      On the opening tab “Formats” select the button “Additional Settings” at the bottom of the tab.

5.      The opening tab is “Numbers” (where I made some changes weeks ago). Select the “Reset Button”

6.      Confirm the change.

In my case, Cortana was instantly listening properly. I have then step by step made my desired changes again, decimal symbol and display of long date format. Cortana still works. I am convinced that this was causing the problem. It may well be that developers never thought of someone using everything US settings but change to metric, decimal symbol “,”, and different time formats. Or that they forgot to make Cortana work independently from these small changes.

I must say that I am quite disappointed with MS to see that there is no automatic troubleshooter or checking routine coming from the company. I have been using Cortana on my WP 8.1 for almost a year now and there are never any problems. I would expect MS to make the installation much less depending on such little locale settings or at least help Windows fans with detailed error codes coming from her. There are larger numbers of people who have similar problems.

I hope to have helped a few. I think that Win 10 is the way to go.

Cheers, aleX

ajvc say:

I just bought a new computer with Windows 8 and Internet Explorer 10 pre-installed. I installed Quickbooks Pro 2012 and used it for a month without any problem. Then suddenly the warning message "This version of QuickBooks requires Microsoft Internet Explorer version 6.0 (or later)" kept appearing every time I tried to open QuickBooks, preventing Quickbooks from working.  I searched the Internet, found the instructions below, followed it carefully, and got Quickbooks to work again.

 

Click Start, Run and type REGEDIT. This opens the Registry Editor.

 

Navigate to the following location:

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\App Paths\IEXPLORE.EXE

(Backup the key by exporting it to a REG file. See here for more information).

In the right-pane, double-click (default) and type-in the full path where Internet Explorer resides (in my case, C:\Program Files (x86)\Internet Explorer\IExplore.exe). Don't type the value in 8.3 format, it should be in Long File Name format. If IExplore.exe key does not exist, create a new key and complete the above procedure. Restart Windows for the changes to take effect, and then launch QuickBooks. The error should no longer be seen.
