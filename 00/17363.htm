al edlund say:

Richard,

A couple of things to start with. When you pull a shape from a stencil to the page it doesn't get dropped on the page directly, a copy of it is made and stored in the 'document stencil'. Then a copy of the shape in the document stencil is dropped on the page. Visio does this for performance reasons (keeps i/o to the hard drive down). From then on whenever that shape is being used it is pulled from the document stencil, not the the original source. So if you make changes to the master shape, they may never get onto the drawing unless you clear the copy from the document stencil. ( welcome to the world of shape developer :-) ) In the shapes window => more shapes => show document stencil.

The second thing to be aware of is that add-ins very often have a specific custom stencil that they will require the user to work with - - MyShapes does not apply to the operations.

The correct way is to make a copy of the stencil and rename it to something usable (like .BAK) and then update the copy of the stencil.The OrgChart wizard has it's own stencil that a developer has to work within. It's not a Visio issue, it's an add-in issue.

I'll check back later - something just came up,
