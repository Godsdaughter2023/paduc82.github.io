hansv mvp say:

Activate the Developer tab of the ribbon.

In the Controls group, click Insert and select the check box in the Form Controls section of the dropdown.

Click on the upper left corner of the cell where you want the check box.

Right-click the check box and select 'Edit Text' from the context menu.

You can change the caption of the check box, or clear it altogether.

Right-click the check box again and select 'Format Control' from the context menu.

Click in the 'Cell link' box and point to a cell or type its address. You could use the address of the cell with the check box.

Click OK.

Click outside the check box.

You can now click the check box to toggle between ticking it and clearing it.

The 'Cell link' will contain TRUE when the check box is ticked and FALSE when it is cleared.

You can use this in your formula: let's say the 'Cell link' is A2.

Change the formula from

 

=someformula

 

to

 

=IF(A2,someformula,"")

Learn about tables of authorities
A table of authorities lists the references in a legal document, along with the numbers of the pages the references appear on. To create a table of authorities, you mark citations and Microsoft Office Word inserts a special TA (Table of Authorities Entry) field (field: A set of codes that instructs Microsoft Word to insert text, graphics, page numbers, and other material into a document automatically. Conferting Excel spreadsheet into a bar chart.

It is a simple chart, but I was asked to convert into a bar and am having trouble.   Any help is greatly appreciated!

Date of Invoice   Project Number     Client     Job Description                              Invoiced Amount

 

Q2 2009

6/26/2009              151245                  XXXXX    Lead in coatings analysis                          $250.00

 

                                                                                                             

rich007a say:

Hi Valerie,

So you want to plot a bar chart (with horizontal bars - if you want vertical bars, that's called a column chart in Excel).

You need to choose what you want to have on the categories axis.  This could be the project number, the date, or the job description.  I assume you have just given one line as a sample of your data (it's not going to be much of a chart if you only have one bar!).  Let's assume you want to plot Invoiced ammount (as the length of the bars) and each have bar plotted by "Job Description".  Click in the cell BELOW the cell that contains "Job Description" and drag the mouse down to select the title and all of the of data, only the right-hand column with a title at the top.

Now, in Excel 2010, click the Insert tab at the top of Excel.

Now, in the "Chart" group, click on on "Bar" and then click on the top-left icon in the drop-down menu that appears.

Bingo, you should now have your chart.

 

If you actually wanted vertical bars (a column chart), just do the same, but click on the "Column" button.

 

Hope that helps.
