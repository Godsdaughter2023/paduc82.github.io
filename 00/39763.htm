Hi,

 

Have you updated the display drivers to check if the problem persists?

 

Windows chooses the best display settings, including screen resolution, refresh rate, and color, based on your monitor. These settings differ depending on whether you have an LCD or a CRT monitor.

 

I would suggest you to perform the steps provided and check.

 

 

Method 1: Run display quality troubleshooter

 

It makes sure that your video card driver is compatible with Windows 7, and checks to see whether your color calibration, video effects, and display settings are correct.

 

Refer:

 

Open the Display Quality troubleshooter

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows7/Open-the-Display-Quality-troubleshooter

 

Method 2:

 

Try to update the video drivers to the latest by visiting the manufacturer’s website.

 

Refer:

 

Update a driver for hardware that isnt working properly

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows7/Update-a-driver-for-hardware-that-isnt-working-properly

Update drivers recommended links

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows7/Update-drivers-recommended-links

 

 

Method 3: Uninstall and reinstall the Graphics adapter:

 

a. Go to Start and right-click on Computers and select Manage. This will bring the Computer

b. Management window, now on the left sidebar select Device Manager.

c. Then go to Display Adapters and right click on its name and click on uninstall.

d. To reinstall the drivers right click on your card’s name and finally click Update Driver Software

e. You have two options here; click the option that reads "search automatically for updated driver software".

 

Download the latest drivers from the manufacturer’s website.
