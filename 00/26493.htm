bernie deitrick say:

This will force the values to be treated as strings:

Sub TestMacro1()
    Dim r As Range
    Dim c As Range

    Set r = Range(Range("F1"), Range("F1").End(xlDown))
    r.NumberFormat = "@"

    For Each c In r
        c.Value = "'" & c.Text
    Next c

    r.Replace What:=" (*", Replacement:="", LookAt:=xlPart, _
    SearchOrder:=xlByRows, MatchCase:=False, SearchFormat:=False, _
    ReplaceFormat:=False

End Sub

After reinstalling Windows 10 10130 on a reformated drive, I found that Spartan had obtained an obsolete Favorites list from somewhere not intiuitively obvious to me.

I'm looking for a way to remove this old favorites list so it does not reinstall with every new installation of Windows 10, but of course, I first need to figure out where it got stored given the disk was erased in between.

The list does not appear to be on Onedrive, but seeming is saved somewhere on the network.

Any hints?

thanks!

 Bruce

a. user say:

Hi Bruce,

Thank you for answering questions.

Microsoft enabled the ability for users to sync their personal settings such as Internet Explorer history and Favorites, and App settings etc. Once the user signs back in to their Microsoft account, all of their personal settings will begin syncing from the cloud again.

To delete these settings follow below mentioned steps.

1. Click on https://onedrive.live.com/win8personalsettingsprivacy

2. Log in using Microsoft Account details.

3. You will go to “Delete your personal settings from the cloud” page.

4. Before you go and click the “Remove” button, you do have to first turn off syncing.

Follow below steps to turn off syncing

  1. Swipe in from the right edge of the screen, tap Settings, and then tap Change PC settings.
    (If you're using a mouse, point to the lower-right corner of the screen, move the mouse pointer up, click Settings, and then click Change PC settings.)

  2. Tap or click OneDrive, and then tap or click Sync settings.

  3. Under the Sync settings with OneDrive heading, turn off Sync your settings on this PC.

Note: Make sure that you are certain about erasing your synced settings before clicking “Remove”.

Hope it helps. Please let us know if you need further assistance.

Thank you.
