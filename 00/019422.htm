Hi John_Stone,

 

Method –

 

Refer the steps-

a) Right-Click the red X on your screen. Choose "View image info" from the menu.
b) Now, Press "Ctrl + C" (on the keyboard) to copy the Internet address that appears in the image info box next to "Location”.
c) Now, open a new Internet Explorer window and press, “Ctrl+V” (on the keyboard) to paste the address and then hit “Enter”.
d) You may be able to view the image in this fashion.

 

Sometimes an image appears as a red X because the image is not located on the Web page you are browsing. Instead, the webmaster may be directly linking to a photo on another website. This practice is called hot-linking, and the image webmaster may block hotlink attempts on his images. If he does this, the image appears with a red X.
