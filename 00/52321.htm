hamilton bia say:

Hi whatever4507,

Mouse and keyboard lagging might be a system related issue or a device related issue. To determine the root cause of your concern, we suggest you to boot your device in Safe Mode and see if the lagging still occur.

You can start your PC in Safe Mode using the Advanced startup options. To access it by turning on your computer and pressing the F8 key before the Windows logo appears. On the Advanced startup options screen, select Safe Mode.

For more information on Safe mode in Advanced startup options, click here.

If you don't encounter mouse and keyboard lagging while in Safe Mode, your concern might be caused by recent changes you made on your PC. To resolve this, we suggest you to perform a System Restore.

Follow the steps below on how to do a System Restore:

  1.  Type "Restore" in the Search programs and files dialog box from the Start menu.
  2.  Click System Restore from the list of results.
  3.  In the System Restore window, click Next.
  4.  Select the date and time (it should be the date and time before you experience mouse and keyboard lagging) from the list of restore points.
  5.  Click Next, then click Finish.

However, if you still experience the same in Safe Mode, your concern might be caused by outdated drivers for your mouse and keyboard. We suggest you to manually update the drivers of the devices in the Device Manager.

For information on how to update drivers manually, click here.

Let us know how it goes.
