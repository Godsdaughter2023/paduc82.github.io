gord dibben say:

User Defined Function

=AddItUp(range)

Function AddItUp(Range_to_add As Range)
'Sandy Mann Feb 26, 2007
'SUM numbers in text strings
    Dim Cell As Range
    Dim x As Integer
    Dim cVal As Double
    Dim Tot As Double
    For Each Cell In Range_to_add
        For x = 1 To Len(Cell)
            If IsNumeric(Mid(Cell.Value, x, 1)) Then
                cVal = cVal * 10 + Mid(Cell.Value, x, 1)
            End If
        Next x
        Tot = Tot + cVal
        cVal = 0
    Next Cell
    AddItUp = Tot
End Function

rohn007 say:
Most consumer printers have a certain amount unprintable margin that you cannot change. To get full edge to edge printing you need a special printer.  They need the margin for "paper handling", ie to hold the paper in place as it is moved through the printer.

Another option is to print on a larger sheet and cut it out to required size.

graham mayor say:
Not only cannot most printers print to the edge of the page (other than in specific photo modes) Word will only print to paper sizes defined in the printer driver. If you choose a paper size other than one shown as available, Word will print to a standard page size. Word will only print what the printer driver will allow.

Going back to the initial reply, only pictures that consist of grouped objects can possibly be ungrouped. Depending on where the picture of the ace of spades came from and how it was created, it may be made up of grouped objects, but more likely it's just a single object that can't be decomposed into anything smaller.

Even among the pictures you can get from Microsoft's clip art collection, some are grouped (and so can be ungrouped) and others are a single object. This "luck of the draw" is entirely a matter of which picture you pick, not a matter of which program you use or whether it "works".

If you need a small piece of a picture that can't be ungrouped, use a screen clipping tool to copy just that piece. At worst, open the picture in Microsoft Paint and crop it to just the piece you need, saving that as a separate file.
