tom_bpc say:

Hi MadpigPOW,

I think your problem lies in the fact that, like all duration values, TS is stored in units of (working) minutes.  So, whatever IIf formula you tried may have appeared unsuccessful because you were trying to differentiate 0 minutes from 5 minutes and 10 minutes.  You need to multiply your "days" criteria by the number of minutes in a day within your formula.  Typically (though not always) this is 8 hours x 60 minutes/hour.  (The 8 hours is the default value for the project's HoursPerDay field.)

You can use the formula: IIf([Total Slack]<=5*8*60,1,IIf([Total Slack]<=10*8*60,2,3)) to define a new number field - call it Slack Indicator maybe.  Then assign the graphical indicators (red, yellow, green) corresponding to the field values of 1,2 and 3.

john korchok say:

Page size information is not part of the program. Wordpad reads the paper sizes available in the currently selected printer, then places that in its list of available page sizes.

It's possible to create an alternative print driver instance for your printer and edit the printer description to remove all page sizes except 4x6. But then you could only print 4x6 from any application, not just WordPad. And if you switch printers, the full list of page sizes returns.
