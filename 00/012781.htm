charles kenyon say:

Sometimes the simplest method is to enter the header/footer for editing and insert the fields directly. You can enter a header or footer by double-clicking in that part of the page (when in print preview) or under the Insert Tab.


See Sections, Headers/Footers and Page Numbering in Word 2007-2013 for more on this topic.


You can insert a { PAGE } field with the key combination Alt-Shift-P. Pressing the combination Alt-F9 will display the field codes which can be edited. If you do that, remember to press Alt-F9 again to toggle display back.

You can also write fields manually. For instance you could type:
page of numpages

Then select "page" and press Ctrl-F9 to make it a field. Then select "numpages" and press Ctrl-F9.  You should then see:

{ page } of { numpages }

Then select the phrase and press F9 to update the new fields. In a 17-page document you should see:

1 of 17

Once you have this method down it is one of the quickest ways to create fields that you know.

rohn007 say:
That was known as the "x of y" error many years ago. It was fixed long ago, at least it was supposed to be.  Here is the old fix:

Making X of Y Work : Take 1

I have a three-page document, and when I look at the footer on the screen, it looks as it should (Page 1 of 3, Page 2 of 3, Page 3 of 3).  However, when I print the document, the footer comes out as Page 1 of 1, Page 2 of 2, Page 3 of 3.  What gives??  It also looks okay in print preview mode.

***************

This is a bug in Word 2000 & I don't know about 97.  In any event, do the following by trying step 1, then adding step 2, etc. until you solve it:

1.      Turn off background printing under Tools/Options/Printing (this alone may correct the problem)

2.      Turn off show/hide so you don't see hidden text.

If that doesn't correct the problem & your printer can sort pages in reverse:

1.      Sort pages in reverse & change Tools/Options/Printing to print in reverse order (by changing both, you still get the correct order).  THIS SHOULD WORK.

2.      Some people have reported that using the following field:

{PAGE} of {IF {NUMPAGES} <> "" {NUMPAGES}}

forces Word to recalculate the numpage field, just like reverse print order.  The bug is reportedly fixed in SR-1.  [This assumes that you have Word 2000.  I'm waiting until the dusk clears before I apply the patch.]
