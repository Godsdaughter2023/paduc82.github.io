marvin ma msft say:

Hi Akhsan,

Theoretically, there're two ways to migrate mailboxes from Office 365 to on-prem Exchange server.

The first option is to deploy Exchange hybrid between on-prem and Office 365. Because in hybrid deployment, you can use EAC to Move mailboxes between on-premises and Exchange Online organizations. So the steps are:

1. Deploy on-prem Exchange server in your local AD. You can refer to Planning and deployment for Exchange Server.

2. Federated on-prem Exchange server with Exchange online as hybrid environment. For your reference, Create a hybrid deployment with the Hybrid Configuration wizard.

3. Follow the article to move mailboxes via EAC.

While the second option is that you don't plan to deploy Exchange hybrid. In this scenario, it could be some difficulty in the procedure. Since they're different organizations, you need to manually migrate mailboxes data from Office 365 to on-prem. 

1. Deploy on-prem Exchange server in your local AD.

2. Export mailboxes data from Office 365 as PST file.

3. Import the PST file to on-prem Exchange server. For your information, Procedures for mailbox imports from .pst files in Exchange Server.

However, in Office 365 online, there's no such feature to export mailboxes data to PST file. If there's a small number of people in your organization, you can use Outlook to export their mailboxes data to PST files. However, if there're  hundreds or thousands of people, you may need to seek third-party solution provider or tools to help export.

jeeped say:

I think I got this worked out. Due to your finite range, you have to adjust the range being looked at from three angles.

In column B, you want to look at B and the next columns for three numbers.

=COUNT(OFFSET(B3,0,0,1,MIN(3,13-COLUMN(B:B))))


That will only look one column forward in column J and only at itself in column K.

For column C onwards you need to look one column back and the next two but only up to column K.

=COUNT(OFFSET(B3,0,MAX(-1,2-COLUMN(B:B)),1,MIN(3,13-COLUMN(B:B))))

For column D you need to look two columns back and three forward,

=COUNT(OFFSET(B3,0,MAX(-2,2-COLUMN(B:B)),1,3))

For columns B & C that will only look back to column B.

Put altogether as,

=(COUNT(OFFSET(B3,0,MAX(-2,2-COLUMN(B:B)),1,3))>2)+(COUNT(OFFSET(B3,0,MAX(-1,2-COLUMN(B:B)),1,MIN(3,13-COLUMN(B:B))))>2)+(COUNT(OFFSET(B3,0,0,1,MIN(3,13-COLUMN(B:B))))>2)

jeeped say:

One of the problems with your example was that column J could only look right to column K and columns K could only look at itself when evaluating the third of the back 2 and look at three right, back one and look at three right, look at three right conditions. If the cells to the right do not necessarily stop at column K and there are blank cells there that may be filled in the future then you can use them in the counts. They just won't return anything.

If the cells to the right of column K are truly blank and may be filled in the future then the formulas can be simplified to,

=COUNT(OFFSET(B3,0,0,1,3))
=COUNT(OFFSET(B3,0,MAX(-1,2-COLUMN(B:B)),1,3))
=COUNT(OFFSET(B3,0,MAX(-2,2-COLUMN(B:B)),1,3))

  ... or,

=(COUNT(OFFSET(B3,0,0,1,3))>2)+(COUNT(OFFSET(B3,0,MAX(-1,2-COLUMN(B:B)),1,3))>2)+(COUNT(OFFSET(B3,0,MAX(-2,2-COLUMN(B:B)),1,3))>2)

Those formulas will start left either 0, 1 or 2 columns (depending upon whether you are in column B, C or D and higher) but will always look at the next three columns. With that in a conditional formatting rule you should be able to simply copy the formatting of existing cells into new cells or cell ranges and the conditional formatting rule's Applies to: will adjust.
