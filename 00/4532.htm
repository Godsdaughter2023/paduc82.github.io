Hi,

I visited the local ancestory.com web site and noticed that they redirect the login credentials to secure.ancestory.com.... IE has a security setting preventing navigation into a security zone of lower integrity... this stops fake websites stealing your login credentials from legitimate websites.

However... if you have ancestory.com but not secure.ancestory.com in your IE Trusted Sites list, it appears that the site asking for your credentials (secure.ancestory.com) is different from the one you are visiting and IE prevents you from navigating into it....

from IE select

Tools>Internet Options>Security tab, click "Reset all zones to default"

then

Trusted Sites Icon, 'Sites' button.

remove http(s)://ancestory.com from your list of Trusted Sites....

save your changes then navigate to http://ancestory.com

the Trusted Sites zone actually has less security than the Internet Zone.... (it allows scripted and unsigned activeX controls for example, while the Internet zone does not).

Generally home users should not add sites/domains to the IE Trusted Sites zone...

Publicly accessible websites like ancestory.com, Facebook.com and yahoo.com are designed to work just as well if they are not in the Trusted Sites list.

The Trusted sites list is mainly used by large companies for Business to Business commerce.... where one company is using an intranet site to send information to another publicly accessible internet site.
