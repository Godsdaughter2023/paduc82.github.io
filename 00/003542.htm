karl timmermans [outlook mvp 2012-2018] say:

#1 - Individual address fields are a mere click away - instead of right clicking on the single address box - left click on the Address Button (Business, Home, Other) which will bring up the detail address screen. 

Putting individual fields on the main contact detail screen would take up a lot of needless space for something that is typically entered only once and personally is not something I'd want to see. If there's a criticism to be made it's that Outlook allows entry into the single field at all given the inherent confusion and potential ensuing problems. Of course if this <was> disallowed, people who "cut 'n paste" the entire address in the single address box will complain because individual field data has to be entered.

#2 -  instead of hitting the <Enter Button> after entering <123 Any St> causing the address field box to appear on the assumption that the complete address was entered (the reason the detailed address field screen appears at this point is because Outlook considers something to be incomplete). Pressing <Shift> <Enter> between all lines and <Enter> at the end - using your example will also result in the address sample being parsed correctly:

123 Any St <Shift> <Enter>
AnyCity, AnyState Zip <Enter>
*** the Address field dialog will not appear since the address is considered "complete"

Unfortunately, it's a big world with lots of different standards in "address format completion" and lots of North American people have contacts worldwide where the address does not conform to US/Canada standards so it cannot automatically be assumed that any address format should be based on the country on which the system is based (of course the people who use the field for driving directions in addition to any address info will also have to be ignored etc).
