karl timmermans (outlook mvp 2012-2018) say:
>>
I received an email on 13/11/2018 from '*** Email address is removed for privacy ***`, asks me to click a link to activate my email account, I guess it is a junk/phishing email because I have no problem with my account. Am I right?
<<

Without seeing the entire message or more specifically where the actual link points to - would dbe impossible for anyone to make a definitive statement but it would most likely be the case.

There are 2 significant telltale signs

#1 - The <sending email address>. Email addy's in posts get blocked for security reasons so can't see what that was

#2 - The actual link. This would be done by hovering over the link and taking a look at the actual link (not just what may appear as underlined text in the message). If it's not a valid MS site - it's a phishing scam. The alternative to that would be to right-click on the link --> select <Copy> --> paste it in any text editor (i.e. Notepad) . MS doesn't use "link shorterners".

The above would be applicable to any email from a major vendor whether Microsoft , Apple, PayPal, your bank/credit card, NetFlix etc etc etc. You should never accept anything on blind-trust alone. Start doing that on a regular basis and it becomes second nature.
