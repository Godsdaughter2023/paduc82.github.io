jllatham say:

Then I believe you will have to combine 10 separate VLOOKUP() formulas, somewhat like this:

= VLOOKUP(for sheet1) + VLOOKUP(for sheet2) + VLOOKUP(for sheet3)...

all the way out to the last sheet.  Naturally your "for sheet#" would be actual formula parameters.  And it may get longer because you need to account for any #N/A! error that might take place which would make the result #N/A!

Since you are using Excel 2003, then the formula for just one sheet looks somewhat like this:

IF(ISNA(VLOOKUP(A2,Sheet1!$A$1:$E$1000,5,False)), 0, VLOOKUP(A2,Sheet1!$A$1:$E$1000,5,False))
