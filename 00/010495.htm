dan at it associates say:

To install only OneNote 2010 on your current computer, start Office Professional 2010 Setup, choose Custom Installation, and uncheck/deselect everything but what you want to install.

 

Like this:

http://2.bp.blogspot.com/_Rf56AL-pm3Y/S8y799RE_TI/AAAAAAAADV8/rJbIef__m2g/s1600/install-office-2010-3.PNG

 

You'd want to end up with a red X next to everything but OneNote.

 

 

Regarding your second question, most new computers come with an installation of Office 2010 Single Image on them, a special installation that can be activated in a number of ways. So when you get your new computer, look in the Start menu for Microsoft Office 2010.  If you see it click on it.  You'll get a dialog that has you choose from these three options:

1.  I already purchased Office 2010

2.  Go online to purchase Office 2010

3.  Use Office Starter 2010.

 

Choose the first option, enter your Office Professional 2010 product key when prompted, and in about 3-4 minutes Office Professional 2010 will be up and running on your new computer.
