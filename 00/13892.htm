linda wu msft say:

Hi AlMullings,

Delve is powered by the Office Graph. Changing OneDrive client has no effect on it. Delve use your permission to crawl the documents and files from OneDrive for Business or SharePoint in Office 365 which you already have access to. It organizes these documents according to what’s likely to be most relevant to you right now. And Delve is much sensitive regard of the activities of theses documents, so the more you and your colleagues working together on it, the more signals the Office Graph can collect. Also, Delve can take up to 24 hours for new documents to show up in Delve.

To your situation, please perform the following steps to check the outcome:

1. Make sure the you have access to the files.
2. I suggest the users in your organization make more changes to these documents, such as read and modify. These changes will make files become popular.
3. Manually re-indexing the SharePoint site:
Navigate to the SharePoint site > click Gear in the right-upper corner > Site Settings > click Search and offline availability under Search section > in the Reindex Site section, click Reindex Site > when a warning appears, click Reindex Site again to confirm.

If the issue persists, I would like to confirm the following information:
1. Do all users who have access to your SharePoint site cannot see files in their Delve or just some specific users?
2. Do all files cannot be searched after you updated OneDrive client or just the files created for your Clients? Try creating a new file in another sites to see if it will show in Delve.

Linda
