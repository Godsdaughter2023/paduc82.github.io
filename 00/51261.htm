nirmal s say:

Hi billyodell,

 

Thank you for keeping this posted.

 

 

Anyone who uses a desktop computer with a full-sized keyboard knows that the keys are broken in to two main sections. There is the typical QWERTY section that is reminiscent of something we used to call a “typewriter” and has all the keys you need to compose things like email, documents and so forth on your computer.
The other section is the numeric keypad, also known as a 10-key, which is designed like the interface on a calculator. The numeric keypad makes it easy to input numerical data and calculations into spreadsheets and other accounting type applications.
Most laptops and netbooks combine these two sections in to one to save on space and weight. All of the keys that you would find on the numeric keypad are combined with the QWERTY keys on the right hand side of the keyboard and are activated by using the Fn key on the keyboard.
If you use the numeric keypad function a lot you are not going to want to keep pressing the Fn key the whole time so the keyboard will let you lock this key. That way you can use the numeric keypad on the laptop just like the one on a desktop keyboard and, when you are done, you just unlock it again and the keyboard goes back to normal.
To lock and unlock your Fn key all you need to is press Fn + Num Lk. This will toggle the numeric keypad on and off.
It is also worth noting that if you use a laptop and find yourself in need of a dedicated 10-key there are blue tooth and USB add-ons available at many computer retail outlets, especially online.


Make sure you tap the Num Lk key a couple of times while pressing the Fn key.


Also, you need to make sure that the light on the Num Lk key is not lit.

 

You may also refer the keyboard manual that is provided with the laptop.

If that does not help, then you need to contact the manufacturer.
