rob koch say:

Does your Telstra dial-up modem connect with at least 50kbps speed?  If not and the speed is something more like 28kbps, it's nearly impossible to download anything at a reasonable rate and issues like you describe would be common.

In the past we've heard similar issues when using MSE with dial-up modems, though it's been a long time since I've seen any such posts.  Typically this happens most when the PC isn't logged onto the Internet at least once a day, since then MSE must download a larger definitions update package of several megabytes, which on dial-up can take at least several minutes to download if not more.

At least once a month, MSE must download a much larger package including a new engine update which is now around 100MB I believe, so this could be why you're seeing such a long delay as well.  Once this is successful, the daily updates should be much smaller for the following month, but will then again have a large download around the 3rd week of the next month.

In the past I would typically suggest using an alternative AV program such as Avast with dial-up, since that used to require far smaller updates, but since I haven't personally used this program in several years now, I no longer know whether this is true.  You'd need to confirm that Avast or someone else actually has smaller update packages before bothering to try this as an option for dial-up.

Also make certain you are connecting at the best dial-up speed possible before updates occur.  If at all possible, if the PC is a laptop then force a daily update when elsewhere on broadband, since then MSE won't attempt to update for another 24 hours.
