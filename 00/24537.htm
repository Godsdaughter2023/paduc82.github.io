Windows used to have one that worked for that but it was deprecated several versions ago.  It is still in Windows for legacy work (images two or more years old) but anything made more recently won't restore anymore.  It will soon be removed.  Don't use it for making new images.

Sub CopyActionItemMtgMinutes()

jay freedman say:

Word does some strange things "behind the curtain". Although the end-of-cell symbol appears as a single "character", internally it consists of two characters: a paragraph mark (ASCII 13) and a BEL character (ASCII 7). I think that's because the end-of-cell can also function as the paragraph mark of the last paragraph in the cell, and Word needs the internal data structure to store its paragraph properties.

This also applies to the end-of-row symbol that appears to the right of the last cell in each row.
