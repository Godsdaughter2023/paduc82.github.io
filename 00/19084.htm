davidf2 say:

Generally the files will be interchangeable, but a few comments.


As of Publisher 2010 MSFT made a big change from traditional menus to the ribbon menu. He may get frustrated trying to find the menu options he used at school when he gets home.


Both Pub 2010 and Pub 2013/365 offer different feature sets than Pub 2007. There are some new and differents and some of the old have been stripped out of the program. If he uses a feature set that is found in Pub 365 but not in 2007, then that edit may not be present when he opens the file on Pub 2007.


I found myself resistant to the changes, especially the new menu. Even today I prefer the traditional menus and use the older versions of Publisher for my production work. Sometimes it feels like I am playing 'whack a mole' trying to find menu items on the new ribbon. In my personal opinion I think finding a used version of Pub 2007 would be a better choice...but then I don't know your son. Some people argue that Pub 2007 was the 'best version' of Publisher, but then some people love the new ribbon menu and find it easier to use. He may find Pub 365 easier or more fun. Furthermore the ribbon is here to stay so he may as well get used to it.

Here are some references that might help.


Reference: Learn where menu and toolbar commands are in Office 2010 and related products: http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/outlook-help/learn-where-menu-and-toolbar-commands-are-in-office-2010-and-related-products-HA101794130.aspx - There is no comparable reference for Pub 365/2013 but this will help understanding the differences in the menus. Download the material for Publisher.

Reference: Make the switch to Publisher 2013: http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/support/make-the-switch-to-publisher-2013-RZ102925043.aspx?CTT=5&origin=VA104055398 - This is a course you can download that may help in understanding the differences.

Within the course material is this video which may be especially helpful. Reference: Get to know the ribbon: http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/publisher-help/video-get-to-know-the-ribbon-VA104055398.aspx?CTT=1

Reference: Keyboard shortcuts in Publisher 2013: http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/publisher-help/keyboard-shortcuts-in-publisher-2013-HA102840106.aspx?CTT=1 - In many cases the shortcuts will be the same, but this article may help if he prefers keyboard to mouse.

Reference: Basic tasks in Publisher 2010: http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/publisher-help/basic-tasks-in-publisher-2010-HA101830007.aspx?CTT=5&origin=HA010370236 - This will introduce you to some of the changes made from Pub 2007. Though it is for Pub 2010 much of it applies to Pub 2013/365.

Reference: Getting Started - What’s new in Publisher 2010: http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/publisher-help/getting-started-whats-new-in-publisher-2010-HA100802352.aspx?CTT=5&origin=HA010370236

I would like to offer a few other suggestions. In general it is best to open from and save to your local hard drive. Opening files from and saving to removable media such as thumb drives can corrupt your files. He should get into the habit of copying the file to the local hard drive, opening it from there, saving his work and then copying the file back to the thumb drive for transport.

He should also develop good backup procedures. Perhaps do a Save As and add the days date to the file name or something similar? Bottom line is he is likely to loose some work transporting files to and from school and you can minimize the frustration if he has a recent backup.

If your machine at home is Windows 8 then any Publisher file is going to render the text differently than on machines with older operating systems. There is a 'bug' associated with the new Win8 fonts. You can right click MY Computer > properties to see what operating system you are running or the Windows key + Pause key. I am betting you are running Win7 which should be fine.

I hope this does not make it clear as mud for you.
