cofialan say:

Hello future internet-searchers!

It's taken a couple weeks of back-and-forth scheduling, calls, and tiers of support to reach a resolution on this issue. A big shout-out to Harshad D. from Tier 2 for working with me and spotting the problem.

In the end this was caused by the OpenDomainRoutingEnabled property of the transport config (Get-TransportConfig) in Exchange Online. For reasons unknown our organization's setting was TRUE when a properly-configured environment is FALSE. This property is only settable by internal Microsoft engineers, so it must be run through support and then moved even farther inside the company to be changed. We're not sure how or when it was modified, but we have been using Live@Edu/Outlook/Exchange Online/Office 365 for ten years and I suspect it may have been improperly set a long time ago. (Since we're new to hybrid mail, and maintained two separate systems for students and employees, it would never have caused trouble until now.)

While the main symptom I described was that the headers and SCL were incorrect, leading to false Junk Email filtering, there was also evidence logged in Message Trace records. Mail sent from on-premises accounts was being sent to Office 365, then forwarded to an "external" mailbox for delivery. Since it's all in the same environment it should have been accepted the first time and sent to the mailbox store, not forwarded back out of the Office 365 environment to another Office 365 gateway. This double-hop, which appears as two independent Message Trace entries for one email, is why messages dropped their authentication.

Hope this helps someday!
