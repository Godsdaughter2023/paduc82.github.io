el_loco---01 say:

Well it's not half-duplex, but it's not full duplex either.

 

This is a common issue in speakerphones, in order to minimize audio feedback the microphone gain is controlled by an AGC algorithm, there are also some echo cancellation and noise suppression algorithms going on.

 

So the bottom line is that if you are in a noisy environment the AGC will pretty much disable the microphone when the speakers are ON (i.e. the other party is speaking) and enable it in the silences. OF course if you start speaking it will try to turn up the gain of the microphone just enough to enable you to talk while maybe disabling the speakers for times. Even in a quiet environment the AGC will turn off the mic when the speakers are on.


The resulting effect is that both parties hear cuts or intercuts. This happens with many hands free devices, I haven't noticed the N97 being significantly worse than others at this.

 

Also, it's not easy to carry out a conversation when both sides speak at the same time :smileywink:

jeeped say:

=OFFSET('MCA&Accessories Monthly Data'!$D$2,0,0,COUNTA('MCA&Accessories Monthly Data'!$D:$D),1)


Assuming that there is no possibility of any blanks in D:D, then simply subtract 1 from the COUNTA(...) to compensate for the fact that you are starting in D2, not D1. You likely have a header label in D1 that is being counted in the COUNTA(D:D).

=OFFSET('MCA&Accessories Monthly Data'!$D$2,0,0,COUNTA('MCA&Accessories Monthly Data'!$D:$D)-1,1)

If there is the remotest chance of a blank occurring within column D:D then the following would be better,

=OFFSET('MCA&Accessories Monthly Data'!$D$2,0,0,MATCH("ž",'MCA&Accessories Monthly Data'!$D:$D)-1,1)

This formula will return the last non-blank row in column D:D assuming text values. The same can be done for numeric values or dates with,

=OFFSET('MCA&Accessories Monthly Data'!$D$2,0,0,MATCH(1E100,'MCA&Accessories Monthly Data'!$D:$D)-1,1)

jim thomlinson say:

=OFFSET('MCA&Accessories Monthly Data'!$D$2,0,0,COUNTA('MCA&Accessories Monthly Data'!$D:$D)-1,1)

 

or

 

='MCA&Accessories Monthly Data'!$D$2:INDEX('MCA&Accessories Monthly Data'!$D:$D, COUNTA('MCA&Accessories Monthly Data'!$D:$D))

 

Your offset formula is volatile. The second formula posted uses index which is not volatile so it is more efficient if you use the range in a calculation.
