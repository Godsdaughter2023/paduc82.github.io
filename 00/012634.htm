Behavior hasn't changed between 2013 and 2016 - it worked in older versions but they removed the ability in 2013 because users found it confusing when categories didn't sync to the server (and other computers) or were lost when they removed and readded the account.

You can use macros and other tweaks to make it work.

alex chen msft say:

Hi Beth,

 

Thanks for your update.

To address your concern, I will try my best to explain the 3 scenarios you listed.

 

1."I save in Beth's One Drive.  If I want someone else to see it, I can go into properties and share it with them."

Yes, in your own OneDrive, the files are all belongs to you. Without Admin Privilege, the other users won't be able to access your file until you share it with them.

 

2. I save it in a shared library where my team has access to it.

The shared library is a feature within SharePoint Online services, which provide you a workspace for your team members to collaborate. This is pretty the same as the question 1, but in this scenario, your files will be shared with your team members automatically.

You could also sync this shared library with OneDrive sync client.

 

3.I save it in my drive and email it someone else.

This is a traditional way for sharing a file. Comparing it with scenario1&2, you will miss the co-authoring feature:

Document collaboration and co-authoring

Meanwhile, in this way, your control to the shared file will be limited. While in the OneDrive/SharePoint, it's much easier to manage user's permission to the files/folder which enhance your document security.

 

IMHO, the scenario 2 is most suitable for your requirement.

Alex Chen
