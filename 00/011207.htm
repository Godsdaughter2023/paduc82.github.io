When you delete items from quarantine, you are removing them from the "jail" or safe/protected storage location that they have been placed in. The point of a quarantine folder is to allow recovery in case of a false detection.

When you delete items from History, you are clearing the log display.

A quick scan will look for malware in the most likely known places that malware infects.

A full scan will scan everything on local attached drives.

A custom scan is similar to a full scan except that it only scans those locations you specify.

greginmich say:
Why does MSE say that I have not run a scan "for a while" when I ran a custom scan just yesterday? Also, why does the History tab not keep record of previous scans and what items where removed?

Running a Custom Scan simply doesn't qualify as “scanning your PC”. MSE can’t take the time and trouble to evaluate the contents of every Custom Scan in order to see if it covers all the bases, so it generically considers a Custom Scan to be nothing more than a scan of an arbitrary set of files selected by the user. In order to qualify as having scanned the PC, the scan has to cover not only a specific set of critical System and User folders, but also a large set of registry locations, including all of the startup keys. So a Quick Scan is the minimum requirement for “scanning your PC”, and for resetting the timer (or counter) that triggers the “Scan now” prompt.

 

 

GreginMich

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

 

And is limited to the scheduled scan task:

 

There are several viable options for dealing with this issue:

 

1. Just ignore the problem and wait for a fix (there’s little to no risk involved in relying solely on Defender’s real-time protection).

 

2. Run manual scans occasionally.

 

3. Schedule a scan with the Create Basic Task Wizard that runs under the (default) local administrator account:

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/protect/forum/protect_defender-protect_scanning/automatic-update-broke-windows-defender/fad7be45-cf9e-4e5c-a6cd-cf2c2d83c345

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/protect/forum/protect_defender-protect_scanning/windows-defender-daily-quick-scan-windows-10-os/af9b6bf8-7880-48c1-afde-4043534b55d3
