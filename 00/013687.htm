Work around for Google calendar entries being one hour off - Microsoft Community

christopher miller say:

Work around for Google calendar entries being one hour off

Technical Level : Basic

Summary

If you are connecting to Google Calendar with the CalDAV protocol and you are in a time zone that uses Daylight Savings Time, when you enter a new calendar entry from Windows Phone 8, that calendar entry may be one hour off.  

As a work around, you can change the time zone on your phone to the closest time zone that does not use Daylight Savings Time.

This problem has been addressed in Windows Phone 8.1



Details

There is problem when you create a calendar entry on your phone for a Google Calendar, where the time for that event on the Calendar is one hour off.  The problem occurs when you connect to Google calendar using the CalDAV protocol and appears to be connected to how daylight savings time was defined in some of the time zone definitions on Windows Phone 8.  This problem has been addressed in Windows Phone 8.1

If you are connecting to Google GMail and Calendar using EAS, this problem does not occur.  Since Google has deprecated their use of EAS, the default for Windows Phone 8 since the GDR2 update is to connect to GMail using the IMAP protocol and to connect to Google Calendar with CalDav.


A work around would be to change your phone's timezone to use a timezone that does not use Daylight Savings Time (DST).  Microsoft has a list of non-DST time zones under Knowledge Base Article KB982987.  The following zones are listed in that article:

  • (UTC) Coordinated Universal Time
  • (UTC) Monrovia, Reykjavik
  • (UTC+01:00) West Central Africa
  • (UTC+02:00) Harare, Pretoria
  • (UTC+03:00) Baghdad
  • (UTC+03:00) Kuwait, Riyadh
  • (UTC+03:00) Nairobi
  • (UTC+04:00) Abu Dhabi, Muscat
  • (UTC+04:00) Port Louis
  • (UTC+04:00) Tbilisi
  • (UTC+04:30) Kabul
  • (UTC+05:00) Islamabad, Karachi
  • (UTC+05:00) Tashkent
  • (UTC+05:30) Chennai, Kolkata, Mumbai, New Delhi
  • (UTC+05:30) Sri Jayawardenepura
  • (UTC+05:45) Kathmandu
  • (UTC+06:00) Astana
  • (UTC+06:00) Dhaka
  • (UTC+06:30) Yangon (Rangoon)
  • (UTC+07:00) Bangkok, Hanoi, Jakarta
  • (UTC+08:00) Beijing, Chongqing, Hong Kong, Urumqi
  • (UTC+08:00) Kuala Lumpur, Singapore
  • (UTC+08:00) Perth
  • (UTC+08:00) Taipei
  • (UTC+08:00) Ulaanbaatar
  • (UTC+09:00) Osaka, Sapporo, Tokyo
  • (UTC+09:00) Seoul
  • (UTC+09:30) Darwin
  • (UTC+10:00) Brisbane
  • (UTC+10:00) Gua

    As noted in the KB article, this list is current as August, 2010.  The list on your phone may not have all of the same values.

    For example, if your time zone is Eastern European Time (EET), you are are 2 hours ahead of UTC, or UTC-02:00.  You would want to use a UTC-02:00 time zone from the list above.  For UTC-02:00, that would be "Harare, Pretoria".  After changing the time zone, you may need to change the time to reflect your local time.  After making this change, calendar times for new events created from your phone should be listed correctly on Google Calendar.

    How to check to see if you are connecting with CalDAVOn Start, flick left to the App list, and then tap Settings .

    1. Tap email+accounts

    2. Tap your GMail or Google Apps account

    3. Scroll down and check the value of the Incoming email server field.  If that field starts with "imap", then you are using IMAP for GMail and CalDAV for Google Calendar.

    To change the time zone:

    1. On Start, flick left to the App list, and then tap Settings .

    2. Tap Date+time.

    3. Turn off Set automatically.

    4. Set the correct values for Time zone, Date, and Time.

    Caveats:

    When you manually set the time zone, your phone will no longer be retrieving the current time and date information from your mobile operator.  If you are travelling and you cross time zones, your phone's time will not reflect the difference in time zones.
