Osaro,
Thank you for visiting the Microsoft Answers community forum.

If this problem just started recently, you can do a restore back to a point in time before it happened.  Here's how to do that:
To restore the operating system to an earlier point in time, follow these steps:
Click Start, type system restore in the Start Search box, and then click System Restore in the Programs list.
If you are prompted for an administrator password or confirmation, type your password or click Continue.
In the System Restore dialog box, click Choose a different restore point, and then click Next.
In the list of restore points, click a restore point that was created before you began to experience the issue, and then click Next.
Click Finish.
The computer restarts, and the system files and settings are returned to the state that they were in at the time that the restore point was created.

If it has never worked since you have had the computer, or the restore does not work, you should delete the drive from Device Manager in Control Panel and then reboot the computer.  It should find new hardware and reinstall the driver for it.  If it does not, you may have to manually reinstall the driver for the CD drive.  Check the manufacturer of the CD drive to see if they have a newer driver you can download and install.
