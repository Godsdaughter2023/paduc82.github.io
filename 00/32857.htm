cmdrkeene say:

It will not work well if you are using POP3 in more than one place.  The reasons why are outlined here:  http://cmdrkeene.com/use-pop-email-windows-8#details

Specifically, it can’t sync among multiple devices (like a phone, PC, and tablet).  That’s actually where it gets the name “Post Office Protocol”, because it works like a real post office.  Your courier can’t deliver the same message to two houses simultaneously, he can only drop it in one mailbox and that’s the end of it.
the message, your computer will never see it. The mail has already been delivered.There are ways around this, some POP email servers can be told to deliver the same email to multiple end-points, but it’s a “dumb copy” function. If you get a junk mail and delete it on your phone, you’ll have to delete it again on your computer. And again on your tablet. And again anywhere else.  There’s no synchronization.

But that is tangent to your problem.  You don't need a POP account, you need an SMTP outbound account.

Long story short:  if you can tell us who your email service provider company is, we can help you.  This is probably something like yahoo, google, outlook, live, msn, or something like that.
