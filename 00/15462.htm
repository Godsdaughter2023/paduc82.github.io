How do I force a footnote to break across two pages? - Microsoft Community

kerryb29 say:

How do I force a footnote to break across two pages?

Is there any way to force a break in a footnote in Word 2007? 

 

I am working on a document for my boss.  Footnote one has 11 lines.  Just above the text for footnote one is a seven-line paragraph that is splitting across the pages.  Boss wants footnote to split across pages, not the 7 line paragraph.

 

I have managed a rigged manual fix for the moment, but that's not good enough for me in the long run.

 

Is there a way to do this or was our tech department right when they told me it can't be done in this version of word?  Word 2007 still has footnote continuation separators and footnote continuation notices, so I've got some hope!

macropod say:

I'd suggest moving the footnote reference to the end of the paragraph concerned. Then, at the original reference's location, insert a cross-reference to it. Finally, format the actual footnote referfence as hidden text. That way, the paragraph will have priority over the footnote for pagination and, if necessary, the footnote will wrap to the next page. Footnote continuation indicators etc will all work as per normal.

IMHO this is much simpler than the other workarounds and doesn't risk breaking the document.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

Yes, of course, Word would do this. <sigh> And if you format the paragraph before it as "Keep with next," then they'll probably both go to the next page (perhaps even taking the footnote with them).

I rather think it may not be possible to accomplish this except by stealth, and I have to say that I do not recommend the stealth that would be required, as it would make the document very difficult to maintain and probably unstable. But if your boss is adamant, you might try this:

Break the footnote into two pieces. Insert the first footnote normally and include as much text as will fit on the page with the seven-line paragraph intact.

On the next page, somewhere in the first line, insert another footnote that will contain the remainder of the text. For this footnote, instead of using the next footnote number, assign a custom footnote reference mark. It doesn't really matter what it is because you're going to format it as Hidden both in the text and in the footnote, but it is definitely preferable to have something rather than nothing (I have dealt with documents where users deleted the footnote reference marks, which resulted in a huge mess).

One disadvantage of this approach will be that the "footnote continuation" on the second page will have the Footnote Separator rather than the Footnote Continuation Separator. But that is only one of many possible flaws of this approach.

If your Footnote Text style is justified and/or has a first-line indent, you will have further issues to deal with. Wrt justification, on the first page you can end the last line with a line break and then format the paragraph mark on the next line as 1 pt and hope for the best. In the footnote on the second page, you'll need to manually format the paragraph to remove the first-line indent.

I don't guarantee that any of this will work, and I repeat that I don't recommend it even if it does, but it might be worth a try.

palcouk say:

Depends on the type of mail account you configured on the individual PC's

A pop mail account downloads, by default all mail from the server, unless you set the option to keep a copy on the server.

It will not be in sync across multiple devices, and will download mail on whichever device its first opened on.

An IMAP account is a copy of what is on the server and as such is the same on all devices.

An Outlook.com mail account will be in sync across all devices

(Obviously a full exchange account will sync across multiple devices)

The formula I wrote is a single cell formula, but I mixed up your ranges for headers and headers :-)

Try this formula instead in cell O4:

=SUM(MMULT(0+E4:N4,--(TRANSPOSE(E3:N3)=O2:U2)))

Note: This is an arrayformula that has to be confirmed with CTRL+SHIFT+ENTER rather than just ENTER.
