Hi Mia.girl1

My name is Andre Da Costa; an Independent Consultant, Windows Insider MVP and Windows & Devices for IT MVP. I'm here to help you with your problem.

Since October 2016, Skype accounts are now linked to Microsoft Accounts just like other Microsoft Services such as OneDrive. Due to this change, it is no longer possible to delete your Skype account without also closing your Microsoft Account.

The best thing you can do is no longer sign into that account and use the available options of transferring your contacts and credits to the new account.

In the Select a reason drop-down list, choose the reason you're closing the account.
Select Mark account for closure.
Your Microsoft account will be closed in 60 days. We wait 60 days before permanently deleting accounts in case you change your mind or need to access something before it's gone. suzanne s. barnhill say:

If you're asking whether that a drop-down list be be fully displayed before the content control is selected, then no, that is not possible. And while "Remove content control when contents are edited" is available for text CCs, it is not offered for other types (though the list is no longer displayed).

 

If your concern is that all drop-down lists by default show "Choose an item," then you can change the default display label in Design Mode ("Choose an item" will still be displayed when the control is selected unless you delete this item). For example, if your list includes the items Red, Green, and Blue, you can have it display "Color" or "Choose a color."

 

If you are interested in ensuring that a selection is made from each list, there's an article about validating CCs at http://gregmaxey.mvps.org/word_tip_pages/validate_content_control_entries.html, but it does require some VBA expertise.
