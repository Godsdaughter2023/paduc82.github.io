adambabinec say:

It is a known bug. It was present in all versions of the camera app (even the Lumia Camera app shipped by Nokia 2+ years ago) and it was not fixed ever since. General recommended fix is to do one or any combination of the following:

  • restart phone
  • hard reset
  • reinstall/fix/reset camera app (impossible at the latest version of the OS)
  • change where apps and photos are saved - to device instead of SD card

My general response is however:

  • give up

None of the previous solutions ever worked neither for me or my friends with Lumia phones. The responses on the feedback hub were (during the times when the MS staff was eager to respond) always in the same way: do soft reset, hard reset, or just do not use the feature. None of that ever worked, and even when someone pointed that out in the original feedback, they just repeated the same answer, despite the failed attempts to do so being explicitly stated in the feedback.

I believe that the problem is in the software architecture of the app and the "background" computing processes that renders the tasks of "background computation" of such data to be highly unreliable, or such reliable implementation to be very difficult to implement. The staggering is, however, that the intermediate files that are captured (*.nrg) are in fact saved immediately after the capture, but when the "background" hdr process (that tries to stick the photos together to produce the hdr photo) will delete them after the failed attempt - just to clean up the mess after itself. This functionality had to be programatically implemented - so it looks like the MS developers decided to delete the data to cover up for the mistakes of the app, instead of fixing the mistake. The worst thing is that if you are lucky and copy over the nrg files right after the capture and just before the "background" hdr process starts to handle them, you can recover the input images to the HDR process, so that you can do the HDR stitching by yourself on a PC. That is however impossible because the files being deleted when the HDR stitching process fails.
