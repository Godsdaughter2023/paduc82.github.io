That being said, here is a round-about way, using various free software, to get your Word document converted to a PDF (I assume for my example that you have access to an iPad, and a Windows PC):

[1]  For this example, am using the free Microsoft Word that can be downloaded onto an iPad, via the App Store.  Lets say you wrote a long school paper on your iPad, using Word, and saved it on your iPad.  Now you needed to have a PDF to email to your teacher.  On your iPad, open the free Word software that you downloaded, and make any final changes to your Word document that you might need, and save your Word document to OneDrive.  One way to do this, is to click the left-pointing arrow at the top left of Word, on an iPad, and it will save your Word document to OneDrive (this saves it to the cloud).

[2]  For this example, you would now need to get on a Windows Computer (Windows 7 for my example), and log into OneDrive via

https://onedrive.live.com

(I used Internet Explorer 10 in this example) 

[3]  After this, on your Windows computer, open your Word file in OneDrive.  Then click Edit Document -> Edit in Browser.

[4]  Then click on File -> Save As -> Download as PDF.

[5]  Your computer will now say "Your PDF is ready".

[6]  Click on the link to "view the PDF of your document".

The very first troubleshooting step is to display nonprinting characters (click on the ¶ button in the Paragraph group on the Home tab or press Ctrl+Shift+6). This will allow you to see what, if anything, is in that space--spaces, tabs, paragraph breaks, etc. (see http://wordfaqs.ssbarnhill.com/NonprintChars.htm).

Since you are using Word 2010, you can also take advantage of displaying text boundaries to see whether any stray frames, text boxes, or other objects show up. Go to File | Options | Advanced: Show document content and check the box for "Show text boundaries."
