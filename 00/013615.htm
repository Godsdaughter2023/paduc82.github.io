rajesh c. say:

The "F:F" which you are referring in your formula, actually refers to a column letter.

When we drag a formula down in the same column, only the row numbers (which are not fixed) get changed.

In your formula, the columns will not get changed to G:G or H:H or I:I as you drag your formula down.

However, there is a workaround for this.

To make this happen you will have to take help of a Helper column.

In this Helper column you will have to list down the column letters which you want the formula to refer.

You need to make use of INDIRECT function to make this happen.

Pls have a look at the screen shot given below.

In this example, I am making use of Column-D as a Helper column, where in I have listed down the column letters F,G, H, I, etc.

In cell E5, I have put the formula

                   =SUMIF(Sheet2!$B:$B,"PHONE",INDIRECT("Sheet2!"&$D5&":"&$D5))

When this formula is dragged down, the column letters get changed automatically as you want.

Hope this Helps.

amit tandon say:

Option 1:

=SUMIF($B$2:$B$10,"PHONE",OFFSET(F$2:F$10,,ROW(1:1)-1))

You may start at any cell & copy down. The column F will become G, then H, and so on. Please note that Offset is a volatile function (increases processing time). Another alternative:

=SUMIF($B$2:$B$10,"PHONE",INDEX(F$2:Z$10,,ROW(1:1)))

This will give the same result except that it avoids using Offset. You may update the range "F$2:Z$10" because this will restrict upto Z column. You may update to "F$2:ZZ$10" or any other.
