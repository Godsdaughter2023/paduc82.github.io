Wrapping text in a footnote in Word 2016 - Microsoft Community

ellensilverberg-brennan say:

Wrapping text in a footnote in Word 2016

Footnotes wrap back to the left margin. I want the text to wrap back so the second line is flush with the first word in the previous line, not wrapped all the way back to the left margin.

Thanks for your help.

jay freedman say:

You need to modify the Footnote Text style to add a hanging indent. The easiest way to do that:

  • Click in an existing footnote.
  • Press Ctrl+Shift+S to open the Apply Style dialog box. The Footnote Text style will be displayed in the style name box.
  • Click the Modify button to open the Modify Style dialog for the Footnote Text style.
  • Click the "New documents based on this template" option button to make the modified style affect the current document's template (usually Normal.dotm) in all future documents from that template.
  • Click the Format button at the bottom left of the dialog and choose Paragraph in the menu.
  • In the Paragraph dialog, set the Special dropdown to "Hanging" and enter the amount by which the second and later lines should move to the right. For most fonts, 0.1" is about right.
  • Click OK in each dialog box.

In some fonts, the indent that's appropriate for one-digit footnote numbers is too small for two-digit footnotes. If that affects your documents, you can choose the larger indent or try to split the difference.
