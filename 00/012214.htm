Office Small Business Edition 2003 contains Word, Excel, Outlook, PowerPoint, Access and Publisher.  The closest current Office suite to that is Office Professional 2010.  There are no upgrade licenses for Office 2010.  If you don't need Access and Publisher, then Office Home and Business 2010 would suit you.

Office Professional or Home and Business 2010's full retail license allows you to install and activate on two systems: a "licensed device" (desktop, laptop, netbook, etc.) on which you are the main user and a "portable device" (i.e., laptop or netbook) on which you are the only user.  If your two systems fit those criteria, then a single license for Office Professional or Home and Business 2010 would cover them both.

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

Rohn,

There have not been any changes in the native mail merge facility.

You can "merge a single record", by previewing the merge of that record and then saving the mail merge main document with a different name and using CTRL+A and then CTRL+Shift+F9 to unlink the fields in the document from the data source, then from the start merge facility, change the document type to a normal Word document.

The reason for saving with a different name is to preserve the original mail merge main document with the data source attached to it.

Or, save the mail merge main document as a template and use File>New to create a document from it, then preview and then use CTRL+A and then CTRL+Shift+F9, then from the start merge facility, change the document type to a normal Word document.
