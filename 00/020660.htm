yoyohanna say:

Found the answer! It has to do with Text to Columns function:

The way fix this behavior is:

  1. Select a non-empty cell
  2. Do Data -> Text to Columns
  3. Make sure to choose Delimited
  4. Click Next >
  5. Enable the Tab delimiter, disable all the others
  6. Clear Treat consecutive delimiters as one
  7. Click Cancel
  8. Now try pasting your data again

suzanne s. barnhill say:

Well, there are many defensible reasons for not upgrading (through from Word 2000, I think an upgrade is certainly in order), but this is not one of them!

You have two choices:

  1. The courteous thing to do when saving documents you need to send to users who don't have Word 2007 or 2010 is to use File | Save As to save in Word 97-2003 format. Ideally, you should create your document in this version from the outset so that you won't be tempted to use features that will not be backward-compatible.
  2. You may find that many users, even if they haven't upgraded to Word 2007 or 2010, will have downloaded the Microsoft Office Compatibility Pack for Word, Excel, and PowerPoint File Formats. This add-in allows users to open .docx documents for reading and editing in Word 2000, 2002, and 2003. You might suggest this to some of the people you deal with, noting the incentive that the add-in includes the six new C fonts that ship with Word 2007 and 2010. These fonts will be installed on their system, and they'll be able to use them in their .doc documents.
