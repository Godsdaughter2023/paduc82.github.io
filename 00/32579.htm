mountain dew say:

The lasers that are built into consumer electronics are not powerful enough to literally burn a disc. Even when we're talking about consumer 'disc burners' they don't actually burn the disc. In a professional environment there is a laser etching process that occurs for mastering and duplication. Home devices however use a much lower powered laser that merely affects a layer of dye on the consumer grade discs and makes the dye turn darker. That's why oftentimes home 'burned' discs are unreadable after several years.

The risk that happens when standing up a console 'upright' is that it is easier for the disc to bump into the mechanisms inside the drive and get damaged from physical contact. That's a problem with pretty much any optical disc based system and not one that can be easily worked around due to regulations on strength of the read/write lasers in consumer products. Other things you can try:

First, try restarting your graphics driver:
Press Windows key + Ctrl + Shift + B

If that does not work...

Press Windows key + X
Click Device Manager
Expand Display adapters
Right click the current display adapter
Click Properties
Click Driver tab
Click Rollback driver if the option is available

If not
Right click the current display adapter
Click Uninstall
Check the box that says, "Delete the driver software for this device.”
Exit Device Manager
Restart

Go to Start -> Settings -> Update & security, then Check for updates and install any available updates.

You might need to update the display driver from the manufacturer of your computers website.

If you have an nVidia, AMD ATI video card or Intel HD graphics installed, you can download the latest drivers from their website.

First, determine what type of graphics you have installed.

Press Windows key + X
Click Device Manager
Expand Display adapters

Once you have determined the graphics driver, go the vendors website and search for the driver in the download section:

Intel - Downloads for Graphics Drivers - Drivers & Software - Intel
https://downloadcenter.intel.com/product/80939/...

nVidia - http://www.nvidia.com/Download/index.aspx

AMD ATI - Download Drivers - AMD Support
http://support.amd.com/en-us/download

Some of these sites offer autodetect options.

Note: This is a non-Microsoft website. The page appears to be providing accurate, safe information. Watch out for ads on the site that may advertise products frequently classified as a PUP (Potentially Unwanted Products). Thoroughly research any product advertised on the site before you decide to download and install it.
