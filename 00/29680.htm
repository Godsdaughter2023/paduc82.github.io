Never never, never start with the Support Engineer "expert" who are engaged by Microsoft to implement KB307545.  You are likely to be in for a frustrating waste of time and could just make things worse.

C:\WINDOWS

chkdsk /r

The chkdsk /r needs to complete without any errors, so you may need to run it multiple times until it does.

Remove the CD and type 'exit' to leave the Recovery Console and restart the computer and report back with your progress.

simon hess say:

Fortunately, changing the language in Windows 10 is not rocket science.

So, even if you don't understand anything you should be able to change the language to English.

1. Press "Settings" (gear symbol) from Start menu.

2. When the Settings app is displayed, click on "Time & language" (time, and letter symbol).

3. After Time & Language settings screen is on the display, switch to "Region & language" tab (second from above).
On this screen click "Add a language" (+ symbol) to add the English language. The language selection on the next screen will be displayed in the respective local language.

That's it. The language will be downloaded and installed automatically from Windows Update. When it's finished you just have to restart the computer and you will be set with the English language.
