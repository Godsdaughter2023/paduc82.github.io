brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:
What type of account do you use?  Unless you use an Exchange account, Outlook's built-in Out-of-Office Assistant will not work, because it's part of Exchange.  For non-Exchange accounts, you can create a rule to do the work (see this: http://support.microsoft.com/kb/311107) but keep in mind you'd need to leave your PC and Outlook running the entire time you're gone.  Your better approach would be to see if your mail provider has the ability to create an automatic message.  Log into your mailbox using a web browser and examine the options and settings for your account.  In Google Mail, for example, the page https://mail.google.com/mail/?shva=1#settings/general contains a section labeled :Vacation Responder".  Hotmail/Live also has this option.  If you log into your Hotmail/Live account and click Options>More Options at the right above your Inbox listing, you'll see a link labeled "Send automated vacation replies".  Yahoo mail also has this.  Log into a Yahoo account, click Options>Mail Options and you'll see "Vacation Response".  These choices are better because you don't have to leave your PC on while you're gone.
