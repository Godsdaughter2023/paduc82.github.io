Hi Dan-Y,

SharePoint Survey is a special kind of list in SharePoint Online. It doesn’t have detailed limitations. In this case, the limitation of Lists applies to SharePoint Survey.

For your situation, there is a list view threshold of 5,000 items, which means that only 5,000 responses can be viewed in a list while 15,000 responses are stored in the survey. confused_with-windows10_mobile say:

I had this exact issue. Not only WhatsApp, but practically all the Silverlight apps were not starting (but the universal apps were not affected). I tried everything, including factory reset. The only thing that finally worked was the following sequence:

Before you do this, make sure the problem is the same. First, see if there are any updates available for Whatsapp in the store. Download some other silverlight app, like WeChat, and see if that gets stuck on the loading screen as well.

If the problem is the same as mine, this is what will help. Find a nice quiet weekend, and do the following:

1. Back up your phone. Just to be safe, write down your contacts on paper, save your One Notes to your PC etc., in case the phone fails to load the backup once this procedure is done. Reverse encryption on your SD card and remove it.

2. Create a temporary Outlook account using your PC.

3. Use Windows Device Recovery Tool to flash your phone back to the days of the big bang, ie win 8.1 (when using WDRT, make sure you use a direct USB port, not a hub, and a high quality cable). If the reset fails and your phone gets bricked, use a different PC, cable, USB port etc until you succeed.

4. When the phone starts up, sign in using the temporary Microsoft account. This will prevent your backup from loading.

5. Open the store app and kill as many of the updates as you can, and download the Upgrade Advisor.

6. Open upgrade advisor and request the Windows 10 Mobile upgrade.

7. Run the upgrade, and still use the temporary account to sign in.

8. Now do a hard reset (Settings>system>About>Advanced>Factory reset)

9. After the hard reset, log in using your original Microsoft account

10. Select "restore from backup" when prompted, and pray to Kerberos, the god of the internets

11. If the backup succeeds, you are all set. If not, like me, you have a whole weekend of transcription work ahead - yay!

12. Now, set your phone back on your company MDM etc as necessary, and insert the SD card, install all apps that didn't back up.

The WDRT flashing to 8.1 and subsequent upgrading was what solved the issue for me. Just factory reset alone didn't work.

If your WhatsApp was not recently updated before this crash, it could be the version that was archiving chats to the phone or SD card. The latest version backs up to OneDrive. So if you were using the old version, and you now get the new version, you will lose all your chat history. Deal with it, because neither Facebook nor Microsoft will show you the least bit of sympathy.
