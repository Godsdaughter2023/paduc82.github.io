1. Click in the top right corner on the "gear wheel" to go to settings.
2. Then click on "Worldwide" at the top of the screen.
3. Then click on "United States - English" (this will bring you to the U.S. version of Bing)

6. Now click on "Clear all" on the right.

To get back to your original language, click on "Settings" on the left. Click on "Worldwide" at the top of the screen and click on your country/language. You'll get back to Bing in your language and the history will be gone.

alex chen msft say:

Hi Carsten,

 

We are deeply sorry about the bad using experience of Office for Mac.

About your concern, I would like to explain how auto recovery works:

1.When you manually save or AutoSave your file, all the previous AutoRecover files will be cleaned up at the same time.

2.The AutoRecover won't save your file whenever there are changes been applied to your files, it save your files every 5-15 mins (By default, it's 10mins.).

 

My assumption on this issue is: When you last save your work, it clears the autorecovery cache, and after that, for some unexpected reason, your presentation can't be saved manually or automatically.(This also impact the auto recovery.) Eventually, when your PowerPoint crashed, the data in the presentation is corrupted. So it opened as a new blank presentation. In this kind of scenario, we would suggest you work with Apple agents, see if they can retrieve some of your works in their method.

 

We understand your concern in keeping your files stored on your local hard drive. Actually, uploading your files to OneDrive won’t influence your material on the local drive. It just create a copy of your file in the cloud, you can keep working on the presentation on your Mac and any changes applied to this presentation will be synced to the cloud at the real time. If you are interested, please check the following link:

 Sync files with the OneDrive sync client on Mac OS X

 

However, to prevent this kind of data loss scenario occurring in the future, you could also refer to Back up your Mac with Time Machine.

Hi there!

Unfortunately the new version of Skype can be difficult to setup but with a bit of playing around with it, it's possible!

You can test your microphone by doing the following:-
1. Launch Skype
2. Click on the round profile picture at the top left hand corner of Skype
3. Click settings
4. Click Audio & Video
5. On this screen you can see the state of your microphone and speakers. If you can see dots going up and down when you're talking into the microphone then you can rest assured that your headset is working.
An example of this is here:-
https://gyazo.com/e96e70e279920c96af9895ff1c4e8e0a
