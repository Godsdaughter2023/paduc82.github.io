suzanne s. barnhill say:

Disregard AndreePin's reply. There is nothing "corrupted or broken" about your template; it is working as designed.

Templates in the startup folder are global templates rather than document templates. They can provide a customized Ribbon and QAT, custom AutoText and building blocks, macros, and the like, but they do not provide any layout or content. For that you need a specific document template.

No matter how many templates you have in the Startup folder, the new blank document created when you start Word will be based on the Normal template. If you want a new document that contains specific content (such as custom headers and footers, a TOC, sample content, etc.), or a different layout (margins, orientation) from the default Blank Document based on Normal.dotm, you have to create a document template and store it in the Templates folder so that you can create a document based on it through the New dialog. See http://wordfaqs.ssbarnhill.com/CreateTemplate.htm
