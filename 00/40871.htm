ralf_g say: The problem may be because there are no Catalyst drivers that support your 9600 card under Windows7. The newest Catalyst drivers don't include support for DirectX 9 generation cards. AMD recommends trying the Vista drivers for it if you want to use Catalyst, or if Windows 7 has legacy drivers for the card you can use those.  http://support.amd.com/us/gpudownload/windows/Legacy/Pages/radeonaiw_vista32.aspx?type=2.4.1&product=2.4.1.3.24&lang=English

All that said it sounds like when Windows starts loading the video card is switching to a refresh rate/resolution combo that the TV can't handle . The DVI output on ATI cards is often the secondary output, which is another reason why it can go blank after booting, especially if the card is autodetecting a TV (connected or otherwise) as the primary display on the S-Video connection. You could try (with a 2nd monitor attached) setting the displays to run in "clone mode". That way both of the monitor connections on the card will display the same output. Set the refresh rate to 60HZ or whatever is normally required for your plasma TV and the resolution to whatever the TV needs.
