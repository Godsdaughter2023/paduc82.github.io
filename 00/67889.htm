dazza 433 say:

Hey zmaddawg

Your console has overheated and most likely damaged some of the solder joints on the motherboard.

I know from experience this can be fixed, But it does require some skill/knowledge.

If the warranty is out and you don't want to send it in for repair then there are 2 possible fixes you can try, The first is to open the console up and remove the heatsink, What you then need to do is clean off all the thermal paste from both the heatsink and the chipset (a little rubbing alcohol works great)

After you have clean all the old thermal paste off you will need to apply some more, ICE Diamond or even better is the new nano aluminium paste's, Put a small amount on and then carefully apply the heatsink evenly on to the chipset, after reassembly you can then see if it worked.

I won't go quite as far as saying the thermal paste used on the consoles is poor quality but i will say that the way some of it looks to have been applied is questionable, If you can see air bubble or areas of the chipset that have no thermal paste applied then you know why your console failed.

The 2nd fix is more complicated and requires aluminium foil and a heatgun that can put out over 200 degrees C, For this fix you would need to watch some youtube video guides on reflowing the motherboard, This is something i have performed on 3 consoles and it has worked everytime.

Press Windows key + X

Click Command Prompt (admin)

Enter the following command:

wmic path SoftwareLicensingService get OA3xOriginalProductKey

Hit Enter
