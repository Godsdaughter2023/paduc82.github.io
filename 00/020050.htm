milos raskovic say:

Hi Jeff,

Thanks for reaching out to us and for explaining in detail the tools that you are using. That is always very helpful!

Sway currently doesn't have its own video solution, but relies on OneDrive video solution. This means that when you upload a video, Sway adds that video to OneDrive and then adds the embed link in Sway. OneDrive (obviously) doesn't allow custom thumbnails. Additionally, the video is stored in original format on OneDrive, but OneDrive does some processing to enable video streaming in different bitrates.
(Note that this applies only when you are logged-in to Sway with you Microsoft account; when you are logged-in to Sway with your work or school account, there is currently no option to upload your local videos).

 

As for YouTube videos added to Sway - when you add a YouTube video to Sway, that videos is not specially processed by Sway, but is embedded within a YouTube player in Sway. This means that however the video shows and playbacks on YouTube (with or without the custom thumbnails), it will show in the same way in Sway.

 

I hope this answers your questions. Please let me know if you have any additional questions.

There is a way to access controls on the Ribbon, but it is very very very very complicated and pretty much impossible to understand.  Investigate further here at your own risk... http://www.wordarticles.com/Shorts/RibbonVBA/RibbonVBADemo.php

The ribbon does not work the same way as the old Menus and Controls.

However, if you have a piece of software developed by you or your company, you just need to call the same macro that is being called when the button is pushed - you shouldn't really ever have to "push" buttons with code.

So your first step should be to identify the file that contains this code.  The fact that it "currently sits in the mailings tab" means you have some kind of add-in or template installed.

First, press Alt+F11.  In the Project Explorer pane (top left) are there any projects that are named anything familure?  If there are, you probably wont be able to access them while they are loaded in word.

In the developer tab, click Document Template (right hand end of ribbon).  Is there anything listed under Global Templates and Add-ins?  If yes, by clicking on it and making the window wide enough, you can see it's path.  If you can locate that file, you'll be able to open it directly, and if it hasn't had its VBA project protected, you'll be able to see the code.   Unless it's a COM add-in, in which case you may be back to square one.

If you can find a .dotx file, you could open it in the CustomUI Editor (http://openxmldeveloper.org/blog/b/openxmldeveloper/archive/2010/08/10/23248.aspx), then you could see the XML that has added the extra button to the built-in Mailing tab.  That would point you to the VBA macro that is being run.

I'll stop here, because if you can't get this far, there's little point in me continuing.  Let us know what you find.

To check Blocked senders list:

 

1. Go to "More mail settings."

2. Under "Preventing junk email" click "Safe and blocked senders."

3. Click "Blocked senders."

4. If the email address of the sender added to you Blocked senders list, remove the blocked address or domain, select it in the list, and then click "Remove from the list."

 

If the email address of the sender is not added, we suggest you to add the email address of the sender to your Safe senders list, so all the incoming email from that sender will automatically go to your Inbox.

 

To add contacts on Safe senders list follow the steps below:

 

1. Go to "More mail settings."

2. Under "Preventing junk email" click "Safe and blocked senders."

3. Click "Safe senders." To allow an email address or domain into your inbox, enter it in the box, and then click "Add to list."

 

Once you added the email address of the sender on your Safe senders list, we advise you to set your Junk mail filter into '"Exclusive," so that messages from your contacts and Safe senders will automatically go to your Inbox not to your Junk folder.

 

To set up a junk mail filter to Exclusive, please follow these steps:

 

1. Sign in to your Microsoft account.
2. Click the gear icon located on the upper right panel of your mailbox, just beside your username.
3. Select More mail settings.
4. Under Preventing junk email, click on Filters and reporting option.
5. Under Choose a junk email filter, select Exclusive.
6. Click the Save button.

 

Note: Junk email is automatically moved into the junk email folder, where it is deleted after ten days.
