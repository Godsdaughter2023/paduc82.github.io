dan at it associates say:

Has nothing to do with links to the internet.

 

Run all from my computer simply means that all functions of Office will be installed to the hard disk drive.  Unchecking several items so that they are not running from the computer means that if you click on an Office command that calls a specific function, and that function is not installed, you will have to dig out your installation discs and feed them to the computer for Office to be able to perform that function.

 

Giving you this choice is pretty much a leftover from when hard disk drives were small and a full installation of a software package consumed a significant portion of available disk space.  Now that hard disks are measured in terrabytes, it's almost superfluous to offer these choices of what to install and what not to install.
