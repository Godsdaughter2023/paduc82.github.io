andreboll1982 say:

I guess my problem was similar but I found a solution.

Apparently my device was disabled and windows did not know I had a mic, but at the same time all louder sounds from the speakers would overfeed sound to the mics and create that high pitched noise.

Step 1 - Identifying the Microphone
Right-click on the volume icon, choose "recording devices" - right-click on the devices area and make sure that the "show disabled devices" option is checked. The mic should appear on the list. Select it, click "properties", and make sure the device is now enabled on the "Device Usage" drop-box menu.
(Alternatively, you can right-click on the volume icon, choose "Troubleshoot sound problems" and follow the wizard - it found out the device was disabled and corrected it automatically, but you still might have to do the next step)

Ok, so now I still had the overfeeding problem. If you have this or your voice cannot be heard through the mic, try this:

Step 2: Configuring the Microphone
Still on the Microphone Settings, go for the tab Levels. Although the Mic was now working, my voice would not be recorded. So on Levels I put the "Microphone" setting on 100, and now the Mic was finally registering my voice. And I set "Microphone Boost" to +20dB - This effectively ended the overfeeding issue, which ocurred all the time if the boost was set to +30dB.

Step 3: Cortana still did not listen to me
I was still not sure why Cortana could not hear me, so still on the device properties, I went to "Enhancements" tab and checked the "Disable all sound effects" box. Closed both windows, "Mic Properties" and "Sound", and tried again... and YEEES IT WOOOORKS! :)

Note that Cortana will not hear you when the mic properties/sound window are open. Close both windows and try after each step.

Hope this helps!
