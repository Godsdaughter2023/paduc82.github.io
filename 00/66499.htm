robert aldwinckle on forums say:
the surface pro seems to detect the display and it can even show the name of the display.

When that happens to me the monitor shows "HDMI No Signal".  Previously I was able to make it retry (without abusing my connectors) by pressing Win+Ctrl+Shift+B or by changing the Display configuration (e.g. by pressing Win+P, CursorUp, Enter  etc.).  However, lately HDMI does not work at all.  I have had to switch to VGA; e.g. put an HDMI-VGA converter on the path and used a VGA cable to the monitor's VGA port.  As with your case, I don't think it is the hardware because the same HDMI connection works fine for my Surface 2 RT.  The only difference would be the HDMI-HDMI adapters the two Surface devices need.  (The RT has a micro-HDMI jack and the Pro has a mini-HDMI one.)

BTW not only does W10 detect the display it will try to use it.  E.g. you can switch to Second Screen Only and then tell it that everything is fine with Win+P, End, Enter and then with an appropriate pause Tab, Enter.  Then you get to use your black screen.  Make sure that you have Narrator running though (Win+Ctrl+Enter) but also make sure that your default audio port is not associated with the problem HDMI video device or you won't be able to hear it helping.
