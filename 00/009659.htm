lr007 say:

Yes it's free, but it's not exactly the same as the retail version.

If you did an upgrade from Windows 7 or 8.1 to Windows 10 Beta, you should be able to convert to Retail without having to do anything since the key is stored during the upgrade. 

If you did a clean install, you have two options:

A. Reinstall Windows 7 or 8.1 and then do the upgrade using the upgrade tool to obtain a valid Windows 10 install, and then you can create a backup medium from there to do a clean install of the OS. 

Note: There is a possibility that if you have a newer computer with UEFI instead of BIOS, Windows 10 may read the key from your system without having to reinstall Windows 7 or 8.1, but I don't know if this is true or not. In my case, I'm on an older motherboard that does not store my product key.

Note 2: You have up until only 1 year after 7/29/2015 to do this.

B. Keep yourself as a Windows Insider where you will always be on the testing branch. Basically, you'll be continuously testing Windows 10 patches before they hit the retail people. Each build will have an expiration date, and if you ever forfeit to upgrade your computer on time, you may lose access to using your computer until you upgrade to the newest build. 

So the trade off of "free" is that you'll have a version of Windows that may have bugs that you'll have to deal with at any given time. They'll probably not be system breaking bugs because I'm sure they have to do internal QAing, but you're pretty much opting to always test for the people who paid for Retail.

Source:

http://blogs.windows.com/bloggingwindows/2015/06/19/upcoming-changes-to-windows-10-insider-preview-builds/

Personally, I'm going to stay as an Insider for a week or two to avoid the slow servers at launch, but I will eventually just bite the bullet and reinstall Windows 7, upgrade to Windows 10, and create a backup medium to do a clean install.

I think it would have been cool if Microsoft would just allow me to enter my Windows 7 product key during the transition to retail, but I do not see any indication of this.
