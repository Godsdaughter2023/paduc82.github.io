In order to use a link in a text box you would first have to select the text box. Hyperlinks and text boxes are not really appropriate. What you are describing is often the result of a screen protector covering the proximity sensor. This will blank and lock the screen as soon as a call comes in or is set up. The reason is that e.g. when you hold the phone to your ear you could inadvertently select something on the screen.

 

If you use a screen protector or have the Lumia in a case check if the sensor is covered. The sensor is located to the right of the Nokia logo on the front. You can use the diagnostic tool to check it's function. Dial ##634# and when the tool starts select the Proximity test. You should see a yellow circle which will turn green when you cover the sensor. If the circle comes up green straight away the sensor is either blocked or defective. Either remove the object covering the sensor or visit your supplier or a Nokia Care Point to have the device checked.

 

Hope this helps, let us know how you get on!

I spent some time coming up with an Applescript that gets Word to Save As in PDF then stitches together the bits of PDF that Word emits into a single PDF in a prespecified folder under you home folder, with the name "thedocumentname.All.pdf". You can use this to Preview, and/or rename and save.

The following VBA calls this routine. I suggest you try it. There are some slightly out of date instructions for creating a Word 2011 VBA Macro here:

http://word.mvps.org/Mac/InstallMacro.html

(There are other pages on that site dealing with some aspects of Applescript)

Since it looks like you are new to Mac, I should warn you that getting these scripts to work can be very tricky. A lot of things have to be rightt. Even if you are familiar with VBA, the MacScript call does not usually return useful debug information when anything goes wrong.  So if anyone else can provide feedback on this, and/or suggestions for improvement, that would be helpful. Since it's generall easier to debug using the Applescript-only version, I provide that below.

Sub CreatePDFAndPreview()
' Peter Jamieson 2014-10-12
' Mac Word 2011 VBA routine to save a document in PDF format,
' join its parts, then open it in Preview.
' Releies on a number of things, but particularly:
' 1. the existence of the Mac Automator action "Combine PDF Pages"
'     and a Python subcomponent called list.py
' 2. that Word always generates the PDF using the naming convention
' docname.pdf for the first or only part
' docname.n.pdf for subseqeunt parts, where n is 1, 2,..,3,..10,11,..
Dim s As String

' Create our Applescript

s = ""
's = s & "--help make a unique name for the output" & vbLf
s = s & "property OutputPDFNameModifier : ""All""" & vbLf
s = s & "property PDFExtension : ""pdf""" & vbLf
's = s & "--the name of our PDF output folder" & vbLf
s = s & "property WordPDFPreviewFolder : ""WordPDFPreviews""" & vbLf
's = s & "-- First, get our active document name and construct the" & vbLf
's = s & "-- pdf output name" & vbLf

's = s & "--(*" & vbLf
s = s & "tell application ""Microsoft Word""" & vbLf
s = s & "    set theStem to the name of the active document" & vbLf
s = s & "    set AppleScript's text item delimiters to "".""" & vbLf
s = s & "    if number of text items of theStem > 1 then" & vbLf
s = s & "        set theStem to text items 1 thru -2 of theStem as text" & vbLf
s = s & "    end if" & vbLf
s = s & "    set thePDFName to theStem & ""."" & PDFExtension" & vbLf
s = s & "end tell" & vbLf
's = s & "--*)" & vbLf

's = s & "--(*" & vbLf
's = s & "-- Create our folder if necessary and" & vbLf
's = s & "-- clear out any existing PDFs with the name stem we just built" & vbLf
s = s & "tell application ""System Events""" & vbLf
s = s & "    if (exists folder WordPDFPreviewFolder of home folder) then" & vbLf
s = s & "        set theFolder to folder WordPDFPreviewFolder of the home folder" & vbLf
s = s & "        -- this requires that any .pdf we output actually has the extension "".pdf""" & vbLf
s = s & "        delete (files of theFolder whose ((name begins with theStem & ""."") and (name extension is PDFExtension)))" & vbLf
s = s & "    else" & vbLf
s = s & "        set theFolder to make new folder at home folder with properties {name:WordPDFPreviewFolder}" & vbLf
s = s & "    end if" & vbLf
s = s & "    set thePath to the path of theFolder" & vbLf
s = s & "    set thePosixPath to the POSIX path of theFolder" & vbLf
s = s & "end tell" & vbLf
's = s & "--*)" & vbLf

's = s & "-- generate the output" & vbLf
's = s & "--(*" & vbLf
s = s & "tell application ""Microsoft Word""" & vbLf
s = s & "    save as active document file name thePath & thePDFName file format format PDF" & vbLf
s = s & "end tell" & vbLf
's = s & "--*)" & vbLf

's = s & "-- generate a list of files to be merged" & vbLf
's = s & "--(*" & vbLf
s = s & "tell application ""System Events""" & vbLf
's = s & "    -- we make an assumption here, that the files are named" & vbLf
's = s & "    -- stem.pdf, stem.1.pdf, stem.2.pdf, etc." & vbLf
's = s & "    -- We need to get them in sequence, so what we will" & vbLf
's = s & "    -- do is get the count, then generate the names we need" & vbLf
's = s & "    -- get the count of all the PDF fils with the specified stem" & vbLf
s = s & "    set theFiles to (files of theFolder whose ((name begins with theStem & ""."") and (name extension is PDFExtension)))" & vbLf
s = s & "    set theCount to the count of theFiles" & vbLf
s = s & "    set theFullName to thePosixPath & ""/"" & thePDFName" & vbLf
s = s & "    set theFileList to the quoted form of theFullName" & vbLf
s = s & "    repeat with i from 2 to theCount" & vbLf
s = s & "        set theFullName to thePosixPath & ""/"" & theStem & ""."" & (i as text) & ""."" & PDFExtension" & vbLf
s = s & "        set theFileList to theFileList & "" "" & the quoted form of theFullName" & vbLf
s = s & "    end repeat" & vbLf
s = s & "end tell" & vbLf
's = s & "--*)" & vbLf

's = s & "--create the final output name, generate it and display it" & vbLf
's = s & "--(*" & vbLf
s = s & "set theOutputFullName to thePosixPath & ""/"" & theStem & ""."" & OutputPDFNameModifier & ""."" & PDFExtension" & vbLf
s = s & "do shell script ""'/System/Library/Automator/Combine PDF Pages.action/Contents/Resources/join.py' -o "" & the quoted form of theOutputFullName & "" "" & theFileList" & vbLf

's = s & "--*)" & vbLf

Here is the Applescript version:

--help make a unique name for the output

property OutputPDFNameModifier : "All"

property PDFExtension : "pdf"

--the name of our PDF output folder

property WordPDFPreviewFolder : "WordPDFPreviews"

-- First, get our active document name and construct the

-- pdf output name

--(*

tell application "Microsoft Word"

set theStem to the name of the active document

set AppleScript's text item delimiters to "."

if number of text items of theStem > 1 then

set theStem to text items 1 thru -2 of theStem as text

end if

set thePDFName to theStem & "." & PDFExtension

end tell

--*)

--(*

-- Create our folder if necessary and

-- clear out any existing PDFs with the name stem we just built

tell application "System Events"

if (exists folder WordPDFPreviewFolder of home folder) then

set theFolder to folder WordPDFPreviewFolder of the home folder

-- this requires that any .pdf we output actually has the extension ".pdf"

delete (files of theFolder whose ((name begins with theStem & ".") and (name extension is PDFExtension)))

else

set theFolder to make new folder at home folder with properties {name:WordPDFPreviewFolder}

end if

set thePath to the path of theFolder

set thePosixPath to the POSIX path of theFolder

end tell

--*)

-- generate the output

--(*

tell application "Microsoft Word"

save as active document file name thePath & thePDFName file format format PDF

end tell

--*)

-- generate a list of files to be merged

--(*

tell application "System Events"

-- we make an assumption here, that the files are named

-- stem.pdf, stem.1.pdf, stem.2.pdf, etc.

-- We need to get them in sequence, so what we will

-- do is get the count, then generate the names we need

-- get the count of all the PDF fils with the specified stem

set theFiles to (files of theFolder whose ((name begins with theStem & ".") and (name extension is PDFExtension)))

set theCount to the count of theFiles

set theFullName to thePosixPath & "/" & thePDFName

set theFileList to the quoted form of theFullName

repeat with i from 2 to theCount

set theFullName to thePosixPath & "/" & theStem & "." & (i as text) & "." & PDFExtension

set theFileList to theFileList & " " & the quoted form of theFullName

end repeat

end tell

--*)

--create the final output name, generate it and display it

--(*

set theOutputFullName to thePosixPath & "/" & theStem & "." & OutputPDFNameModifier & "." & PDFExtension

do shell script "'/System/Library/Automator/Combine PDF Pages.action/Contents/Resources/join.py' -o " & the quoted form of theOutputFullName & " " & theFileList

-- if we try to tell Preview to open this file, we sometimes see a permission

-- error. Could be a timing problem, but instead, we will do this...

do shell script "open -a Preview " & quoted form of theOutputFullName

--*)
