graham2014 say:

Pros and Cons of subscription-based software

What are the pros and cons (if any) of subscription-based software e.g. Microsoft Office 365?  In the event that one falls in arrears and subscriptions due are not paid, does the software then become inoperative and access to all saved files will consequently also be denied?

If I only need Word, Excel and Outlook, would it be a waste of money to get the whole software package either on subscription or outright purchase? But then, can these programs be purchased outright at a discounted price directly from  Microsoft?  Am I correct that previously Word and Excel came with Microsoft Works but now that's no longer available? 

I have tried the free alternatives like FreeOffice and WPS but my correspondents always complain that they cannot open attachments in these formats on their computers or that the formatting had gone haywire/ 

stefan blom say:

A subscription version lets you install Office on five devices and it will also give you the most recent functionality updates (which you may or may not want).

You won't lose your files if you cancel a subscription, but of course you'll need some other program to edit them. An expired version of Office can only be used to view the files.

A so-called one-time purchase, such as Office Home & Student 2016, will be cheaper. You will get security updates but not new features. Home & Student can only be installed on one computer.

For all options, see https://products.office.com/en-us/compare-all-microsoft-office-products?tab=1.
