danida_u say:

Hi Alistair Scott and welcome to the Skype Community!

Skype for Windows 10 uses the default settings of your device. If you are using a headset, make sure that the device is using the headset and is chosen as the default audio settings. Please make sure of the following: 

  • Use the latest version of Skype available for your device. If Skype users have an outdated version of the app installed, they could experience issues with calling, messaging and the over-all performance of the application. 
  • See to it that the audio device used by the app is the headset you are using on your laptop. Click your profile picture then click Settings. Under Microphone choose your device then re-launch the app. 
  • Make sure that the default audio that is set for your computer is your headset. 

After making sure of these things, try the following steps below. 

  • Choose any contact and send /dumpmsnp as an instant message. 
  • Then send /msnp24 and re-launch the application. 

Please let us know if the issue persists so we can check on this further.
