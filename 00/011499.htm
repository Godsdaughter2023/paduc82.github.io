shyamal debnath msft support say:

Hi Asharaf,

Regarding “what we planned is " first we will assign the license, keep in litigation hold & then remove license ", when a user’s license is removed, all data that is associated with that user account is held for 30 days. After the 30-day grace period, the data is deleted and can’t be recovered.

If you just want to preserve and access the contents of those disable 5000 mailboxes in Exchange Online.

The right procedure is turn the mailbox into an inactive mailbox by placing it on In-Place Hold or Litigation Hold and then delete the corresponding Office 365 account. The contents of an inactive mailbox are preserved for the duration of the hold that was placed on the mailbox before it was made inactive.

After mailbox is deleted, the license that was associated with it will be available to assign to a new user. Inactive mailboxes don’t require ongoing licenses.

For your reference: Manage inactive mailboxes in Exchange Online

As an administrator you can access the contents of the inactive mailbox by using In-Place eDiscovery in the Exchange admin center (EAC). You can preview the search results, copy the search results to a discovery mailbox, or export the search results to an Outlook Data (PST) file. For step-by-step procedures for searching mailboxes, see Create an In-Place eDiscovery search.

Note: In-Place Hold and Litigation Hold are available only Office 365 Enterprise E3/E4/E5 plan.

If anything is unclear or there is any misunderstanding, please feel free to let us know and we will be glad to provide further assistance.

Best regards,
Shyamal
