[Original title: import]

don varnau say:

Microsoft recently help with an account on my computer, which I had to create a new account, how do I import my favorites from the the old account

Hi,

Open both Favorites folders at the same time.

They will be at

"C:\Users\old account name\Favorites"

and

"C:\Users\new account name\Favorites"

Don't try to just copy the entire Favorites folder. You may have permissions issues if you do this. Select a number of Favorites/Internet Shortcuts from the old folder, copy them and paste them into the new folder. Or right-click those Favorites, drag them to the new folder and select Copy.

a. user say:

Hi peachyxn,

 

I will be glad to assist you in blocking an email address and/or create an email rule to delete spam messages.

 

If you wish to block a specific email address which sends an example of spam message, please follow these steps:

 

1. Sign in to your account at https://mail.live.com or https://outlook.com

2. Click the Cog icon then select More mail settings

3. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders

4. Click Blocked Senders

5. In the text box, type in the email address or domain you wouldn't like to receive emails from, and then click Add to list >>

 

You can also create a rule that will delete those spam messages if their subject is bulk. Here's how to do so:

 

1. Sign in to your account.
2. Click the Cog icon then select More mail settings located at the upper right corner of the page.
3. Under the Customizing Outlook, click on Rules for sorting new messages.
4. Click the New button.
5. Under Step 1: Which messages do you want this rule to apply to, 
    a. First drop-down menu - choose Subject
    b. Second drop-down menu - choose contains
    c. Third field - input the word you would like to block
6. Under Step 2: What action do you want to apply, click on Delete these messages-don't eben put them in my Deleted folder.
7. Click Save.

 

Furthermore, I also suggest that you check out this link to know how to report Abuse or Spam in Hotmail.

 

Please feel free to post back if you have other questions or clarifications.

 

Thank you.

tony jollans say:
In general this is a problem that occurs more and more often these days. In this case, the simplest way is, I think, via the VBE ....
 
Press Alt+F11 to open the VBE
Press Ctrl+G to switch to the Immediate Window
Type: Commandbars("Styles").top = 0
Press Enter
Type: Commandbars("Styles").left = 0
Press Enter
Press Alt+F4 to close the VBE
 
That should position it at the top left of your screen, but there may be some unusual circumstances where you need to use different numbers.
 
Enjoy,
