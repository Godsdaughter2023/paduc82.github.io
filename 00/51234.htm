No matter what console a profile is signed into it will always be able to play its digitally bought games and use any subscription it has.

arminatorx say:

From your other post I already understood you properly set up your stepson's console as your Home Console and vice versa. 

You can now stop buying Gold for your stepson, as the Home Console will share Xbox Live Gold to anyone playing on the Home Console, as long as you have a valid Gold membership. 

As for the signing out issue: You and your stepson both have each their own separate Microsoft Account and Gamertag? Or is your Stepson signing into your account?

An account can only be signed in on one console from the same generation at any time (so your account can be signed in on a Xbox One and a Xbox 360 at the same time, but not on two Xbox One consoles). 

If you do have two separate Microsoft Accounts and Gamertags for your stepson and yourself, please check the network settings of each of the consoles, and choose a custom Xbox Live port for each console. 

By default a Xbox console will use Port 3074 to communicate with Xbox Live, but some routers don't handle it well, if two devices try to receive data on one and the same port. 

So choose a custom port for one console, so the two consoles are using two different ports. 

Also make sure you actually do have a router/gateway to connect to the internet with multiple devices. Not that you're using a broadband modem, that only can support only one device connecting to the internet.
