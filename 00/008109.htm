jay freedman say:
You need one variable to hold the total of all the entered values, and another variable to hold the number of values that were added.

Within the loop, each time a person selects an option button, add the value of that button to the total variable, and add 1 to the count of how many values. Then the average is the final total divided by the final count.

One thing to be aware of is that the variable that holds the average must be declared as a floating data type (Single or Double) or as a Variant in order to retain the decimal part. If it's declared as Integer or Long, it will be rounded to the nearest integer.

  • Process MSOSYNC (document cache) 0% CPU and still restarting (on bacground)

1. disable service click-to-run (in services - set disabled)

5. enable service click-to-run (set automatic)

6. restart computer

Hi,

 

Often the most common cause why messages in Inbox are constantly being moved to POP folder is that customers are accessing their e-mails via POP3 protocol.

 

Since POP3 deletes the e-mails from the server after they are downloaded on to their mail client (Thunderbird, Mobile Phone), it can appear like these mails are automatically deleted or moved.

 

In case you accidentally set up POP3 to delete emails after reading on the server, there is a feature to prevent losing the emails due to an incorrect setting. The option Don't let another program delete messages from Hotmail will enables the option for the emails not to be deleted from your inbox permanently after downloading to your mail program.

Here are the steps to confirm POP3 client settings:

1. Log in to Outlook.
2. Click the cog icon then More mail settings.

3. Under Managing Your Account select Pop and deleting downloaded messages
4. Tick on Don't let another program delete messages from Hotmail.
5. Click Save.

This setting is not mail program specific and would apply to all the mail clients/programs that the customer may use. By default, this setting is set not to respect the delete command from the mail programs.For added information, please click here.


Note: The option will not take effect to lost/deleted emails. This is a preventive measure to avoid losing emails again.
