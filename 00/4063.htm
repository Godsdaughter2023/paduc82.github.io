Phone as modem - slower than a dongle? - Microsoft Community

spile---01 say:

Phone as modem - slower than a dongle?

I am using a Nokia 6301 as a bluetooth modem with my laptop. Would a USB broadband dongle be any faster?
psychomania---01 say:

A dongle will be much faster than using the 6301 as a modem in most cases.

 

Modern dongles support 3.5g (HSDPA) which depending on the network can give you speeds up to 7.2mbps. It can also fall back to 3G which still gives you fairly fast speeds.

 

 The 6301 doesn't even support 3g so it's limited to GPRS (or EDGE if supported) which are much slower, typically 56-114kbps.  So it's almost like comparing the old slow dial up internet to broadband.

 

In areas where there is no 3g or 3.5g signal then the dongle and the phone will roughly be equal as the dongle will fall back to GPRS.

hansv mvp say:

The formula for the catenary function is y=a*COSH((x-n)/a), and the formula for its arc length is 2*a*SINH(n/a) where n is half the distance between the poles. The problem is to find the a for which the drop is equal to the specified value. I don't know of an analytic way to do this. I used Goal Seek to determine a; its value is ~3.094. The arc length is calculated as 3.094*SINH(5/3.094) ~ 14.958.

See https://www.dropbox.com/s/a9ahws291jl5wwn/Catenary.xlsx?dl=1
