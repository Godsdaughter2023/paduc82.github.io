There are three processes that can suspend the user in Yammer and cause the status to be displayed as suspended by System Administrator.

  1. Yammer user not mapped in office 365:  Yammer creates an association (or mapping)between the Office 365 user and the Yammer user. During this process, Office 365 users who have multiple Yammer user accounts may be suspended
  2.  Yammer user life cycle management in Office 365: Users who are deleted in Office 365 will be suspended in Yammer. These users will be shown as deactivated by System Administrator. When user is deleted in Office 365, he or she can be restored within 30 days. Yammer users will be suspended in Yammer for 90 days. 
  3.  Bounced email messages: When Yammer sends an email message to a user, and the email message bounces multiple times, the user will be suspended in Yammer. These users will be shown in Yammer as suspended by System Administrator.

Note: Users can reactivate themselves if they have access to their accounts. For example, they can reactivate themselves if they use Office 365 credentials or Yammer single sign-on (SSO).

john korchok say:

In day-to-day use, I find the significant difference is between Content and Picture placeholders. When a graphic is inserted, the Content placeholder will resize to the photo. If the photo is smaller than the placeholder, you'll get extra space around it.

When a Picture placeholder is used, the graphic is resized to the placeholder. The smallest dimension of the graphic will fill the width or height of the placeholder, then the longer dimension of the graphic will be cropped.

This makes the Picture placeholder more useful for tightly constructed image pages, the kind that designers love so much. The picture grid remains constant. Content placeholders are better when you want to be sure the entire image is included and the layout design is less important.

I've never noticed any difference in function with any of the other placeholders compared to the Content placeholder, which is probably why there isn't anything written about them.
