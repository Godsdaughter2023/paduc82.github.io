I managed to fix the problem; perhaps this fix will work for you as well. 

First of all, here's what I experienced: 

I have a OneNote Notebook that is stored on SkyDrive.  I typically access it from my WORK machine where it does NOT show any sync problems - ever. 

I tried loading it tonight on my HOME and got the message that says "Some sections... could not be synced".  

Here's how I resolved it: 
  1. Closed the notebook
  2. Went to sky drive and selected "Open in OneNote"
  3. Waited for it to open in OneNote (I.e., waited for it to download and open locally on my machine)
  4. Waited for sync to complete and observed the same message.
  5. Opened NoteBook Sync Status and cliked on the Error tab.  Here I noticed that it was complaining that one particular section couldn't be synced.
  6. I browsed to the particular Section which I had buried in a subfolder.  I noticed that, initially, it looked like it was all there, but when I clicked on a particular page I noticed that it was blank; however, within a few seconds it popped up there.  Apparently it synced this page after I clicked on it.
  7. One by one I clicked on each of the pages in the offending section.  Each time there was a pause of 10-15 seconds while the page downloaded from skydrive.
  8. I right clicked on the notebook and selected "Sync Now".  The sync process completed successfully - no errors.

The "offending section" had a lot of pictures in it so my theory is that skydrive->local sync is timing out on large pages/sections.

eric hicks say:

WEP, WPA and WPAII along with 802.1x (PEAP & Certificates) are supported by the Windows Mobile 6 OS.  The thing to keep in mind is that the manufacture has the option to install a card that may only do WEP & WPA but not WPAII.  The majority of newer devices available which have built in wifi support WEP, WPA and WPAII so you shouldn't have many problems but if you use a device that's a year or so old, you may wish to make sure that the wireless card supports the encryption you need.  802.1x is standard no matter what encryption is used and works very well but with Windows Mobile Standard devices certificate installation can be tricky depending on how much the cellular carrier has locked down the device.
