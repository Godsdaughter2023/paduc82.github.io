The battery keeps a clock "ticking" in your PC and preserves BIOS settings. If the clock was not right until you connected to the internet this is further confirmation that the battery is spent. Once on-line, your PC can connect to a "time server" and correct the date and time, but if the battery is spent then when you switch off the date, time and BIOS settings will revert to default.

Your manual can be downloaded here...

ftp://europe.asrock.com/manual/K8%20Combo-Z.pdf

...BIOS setup starts on page 22 and would imply that F10 should be used to save BIOS changes.

Yes you should first power down and disconnect the mains lead. You would be wise to take antistatic precautions too before replacing the battery.
