You should be able to add your second criteria to this line:

Me.RecordsetClone.FindFirst "[Last Name]Like" & Chr(34) & Me.Text164 & "*" & Chr(34)

Which would be something like:

Me.RecordsetClone.FindFirst "[Last Name]Like" & Chr(34) & Me.Text164 & "*" & Chr(34) & " AND [First Name] LIKE" & Chr(34) & Me.YourTextBox & "*" & Chr(34)

ken sheridan say: As you are filtering the form you don't need to bring the RecordsetClone or Bookmark properties into play at all:

Private Sub Command176_Click()

    Me.Filter = "[Last Name] Like """ & Me.Text172 & "*""" & _
        " And [First Name] Like """ & Me.Text174 & "*""" & _
        " And [Address] Like """ & Me.tx183 & "*"""

    Me.FilterOn = True

End Sub

However, as you have an 'anonymous' donor I assume they will not have any value in the First Name and Address columns, in which case the above expression will evaluate to NULL, nether TRUE nor FALSE.  This is because a NULL is not a value, but the absence of a value, so in any comparative operation always returns a NULL.  The way to get around this is to examine each parameter for NULL:

Private Sub Command176_Click()

    Dim bkmk As Variant
    Dim strField As String
    
    Me.Filter = "([Last Name] Like """ & Me.Text172 & "*""" & _
        " Or IsNull(Me.Text172)) & _
        " And ([First Name] Like """ & Me.Text174 & "*""" & _
        " Or IsNull(Me.Text174)) & _
        " And ([Address] Like """ & Me.tx183 & "*"""
        " Or IsNull(Me.Text183))
 Me.FilterOn = True

End Sub

Each OR operations will evaluate to TRUE if the parameter is NULL.  Note that the parentheses are crucial to force the OR operations to evaluate independently of the AND operations.

There is another simple way of doing the same thing, and it's the way I'd do it, which is to base the form on a query which references the parameters, e.g.

SELECT *
FROM [Donations]
WHERE ([Last Name] LIKE Forms![YourFormName]!Text172  & "*"
OR Forms![YourFormName]!Text172 IS NULL)
AND ([Last Name] LIKE Forms![YourFormName]!Text174  & "*"
OR Forms![YourFormName]!Text174 IS NULL)
AND ([Address] LIKE Forms![YourFormName]!Text183  & "*"
OR Forms![YourFormName]!Text183 IS NULL);

You then just need to requery the form in the button's Click event procedure:

Private Sub Command176_Click()

    Me.Requery

End Sub
