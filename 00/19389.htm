When you have a third-party AV app installed, the Windows Defender UI is extremely confusing – because it indicates that you have to uninstall your third-party AV app in order to use Defender. That statement completely ignores Defender’s ability to run alongside your third-party app in its Limited Periodic Scanning (on-demand) mode. The UI should actually tell you that you only have to uninstall your third-party AV app if you want to use Defender’s real-time protection.  

When you click on the big Turn On button in the UI, or when you enable Periodic Scanning in the Windows Defender Security Center, Defender will start to automatically download definition updates, and it will also occasionally run an automatic Quick Scan on your PC. But it’s important to understand that Defender's Limited Periodic Scanning mode doesn't just provide automatic scanning for PCs running a third-party AV – it also enables all of Defender’s other on-demand features, including the Command Line Interface. The only feature that will be missing is the real-time protection:

https://www.tenforums.com/tutorials/51514-turn-off-limited-periodic-scanning-windows-10-a.html 

Of course you should be warned in advance that the usual Windows/Windows Defender dysfunctionality will fully apply – and that this might be a little disconcerting. hansv mvp say:

In a query based on the table, add a calculated column like this:

 

Cost: [Duration]*[PricePerMinute]*1440

 

where Duration is the time in minutes and PricePerMinute is the price per minute.

Set the Format property of the column to Currency.

 

Explanation: times are stored as fractions of 1 day. So 1 minute = 1 / (60*24) = 1/1440 of a day. Hence the multiplication factor 1440.

 

Alternatively, set the Control Source of a text box on a form or report bound to the relevant table to

 

=[Duration]*[PricePerMinute]*1440

Hi,

 

If you sign in to Skype using the Microsoft email address, the system will automatically create a Skype account for you that has a “live:username” pattern. I believe this is what happened. The password used for this account is your Microsoft account password.

 

You can merge your Skype account that has a personalized Skype Name to your Microsoft account email address. However, if one of the accounts has already been merged to other Microsoft account or has a Skype Credit or subscription, this is cannot be done.
