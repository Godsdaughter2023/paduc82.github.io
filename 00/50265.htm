Important: NETSTAT(Network Statistics) is a command-line utility for displaying network statistics. Here are some of its more useful flags:

C:\> netstat -ao |find /i "listening"

C:\> netstat -a (or netstat -ao in XP/Win2k3)

It sounds as if you have something else going on with your computer rather than a problem with either WMP or "those big video players" (VLC loads in less than a second on my Windows 7 Pro system -- and I don't consider my system to be particularly speedy).

I generally suggest avoiding Best Buy and similar places, and "doing a fresh install" will -- at a minimum -- require you to re-install all of your programs (assuming that Best Buy backs up and restores your data, which they may or may not do; I suspect that BestBuy may take the easy way out and "restore your computer to its factory conditions" because doing this does not require a Windows Product Key).

To reinstall the programs, you'll need the original installation media (CD/DVD) or some way to download an installation file.  For at least some of your programs, you will also need to have the corresponding license information.  If you don't have license info on paper documentation, you can get it for many programs (not including Windows if it came preinstalled on your computer), by running free BelArc Advisor (https://www.belarc.com/products_belarc_advisor). 

To reinstall Windows, you (or BestBuy) will need the Windows Product Key.  If you bought a retail copy of Windows 7, the Product Key was on a sticker on the packaging and BelArc Advisor will also be able to show it to you.  If Windows 7 came preinstalled on your computer, the Product Key is on the COA sticker on your computer.  This is on the side or back of tower/desktop computers and on the bottom (or possibly inside the battery compartment) of laptops (the reason it's inside the battery compartment is because many people discovered too late that when the sticker was on the bottom, it became illegible and thus they were unable to reinstall and activate Windows). 

And of course, once you successfully re-install both Windows and your programs, you'll have to update them.  For Windows itself, this can be a pretty tedious task (another reason that BestBuy is likely to take the "factory restore" option).

A less drastic step -- but still pretty drastic -- would be to do a repair install.  In theory, this should preserve all of your programs and data, although it would be prudent to backup your data first and make sure that you have all the program license info just in case.  You will need your Windows 7 Product Key.  For repair install directions, see https://www.sevenforums.com/tutorials/3413-repair-install.html

A message such as "Windows needs to configure your computer" sounds like you're having a problem with Windows Update.  Before you go off to BestBuy, you might want to ask a new question in the "Windows Update, Recovery, & backup" section.  If you do that, in addition to describing the circumstances that result in the "Windows needs to configure your computer" message, you should include at least:

  • Your version of Windows (Windows 7 ___)
  • Whether or not you have Service Pack 1 installed (to get the information for this and the preceding question, click Start, right-click Computer, and select "Properties")
  • The last time a Windows Update was successfully installed (click Start, type Windows Update in the Search box, press Enter, and click "View Update History" in the left panel; give the KB numbers and dates for the top two or three entries).
  • Any other symptoms that you have noticed

Good luck!!
