That's because most of these aren't a virus or malware at all, they're simply a pop-up browser hijack, exactly as you mentioned in your title.  That means that most antivirus or antimalware programs aren't able to detect them, since they are seen by these as normal operation and that's what a pop-up blocker in the browser is used for.  However, setting your browser's pop-up blocker to block all pop-ups has the negative side effect of causing certain important website pop-ups not to work as well.

Typically the reason that these pop-ups are being displayed has more to do with the websites you are visiting and the policies of their advertising networks than anything your security programs might be doing.  This is due to the fact that most of these are now delivered via such advertisements, being injected through the ad streams that normally create the small targeted ads around the screen, which often seem to have nothing to do with the page being displayed itself.

The best way to stop these is to stop the advertisements themselves, which means using an ad blocker.  Since the Windows 10 Anniversary Update was released by Microsoft in August of 2016, it has been possible to add such

Extensions for Microsoft Edge browser.  The popular AdBlocker and AdBlocker Plus are both available there among others.

Learning how to properly exit these pop-up scams once they are displayed, as already discussed, is another good way to handle them after the fact, though some can be persistent and difficult for a novice to exit easily.

In either case, I'd get rid of SpyHunter and return to using Windows Defender, since that's a much better free security program.

v = Array("jan", "feb", "mar", "apr", "may", "jun", "jul", "aug", "sep", "oct", "nov", "dec", "summa")
For Each lo In ActiveSheet.ListObjects
