bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

The ability to assign keyboard shortcuts has been eliminated in Excel 2016. The ability to create custom Toolbars & to modify the menu has been excised as well.

The most comparable alternative is to use the OS X functionality for assigning key combinations. However, that capability is restricted to assigning shortcuts only to commands expressly included in the main menu & its submenus... The commands you gave as examples are not among them.

The controls to increase/decrease decimals can be added to the Quick Access Toolbar. Also, in the subscription release of Office 2016 it should be possible to customize the Ribbon. Both of those options, though, require use of the pointing device. AFAICT, the only means of assigning a keystroke to something not in the main menu is to create a macro to execute it (if possible) & assign a keystroke to the macro.
