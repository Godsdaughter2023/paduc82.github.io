RESOLUTION -- Can't rename a "Section" in OneNote - Microsoft Community

josephsweeney say:

RESOLUTION -- Can't rename a "Section" in OneNote

Hello,

there are several threads that treat this same issue. 
They are all locked and community couldn't find a good answer.

I have had this problem multiple times.  Today, I found a potential "work-around" fix.

ISSUE:  when you try to rename a "SECTION" in OneNote (by right click --> "rename"), the RENAME modification is seemingly taken, but then rejected within milliseconds.  RESULT:  you can't change the name of the Section.

This issue occurs occasionally and sometimes the issue arises, and sometimes the SECTION is renamed without issue.
When it doesn't work, it is frustrating and you just can't get it to work -- but then, often when you come back another day, it works!

ROOT ISSUE:  the MSFT team seems to suggest that the issue may involve the syncing between local and cloud versions of the file.  That seems reasonable, but this work-around will only allow you to do the desired action -- it doesn't fix the underlying code issue (if it is a bug, which seems likely).

Note, I'm in Windows 10.

WORK-AROUND:
Whatever, the root-cause reason, try this work around.

First off, a bit of terminology.  OneNote describes its hierarchy as:   Notebook --> Section --> Page

You can have many Notebooks; and for each Notebook, you can create many Sections;  and for each Section, you can create many Sections.

Resolution ---

1. Before going to "right click" on the Section-to-be-rename, put your cursor on the "Notebook" in which your Section-to-be-renamed lies.  (This seems to be unnecessary as you are already in the given "Notebook" based on the fact that you are trying to rename one of its Sections -- but bear with me.)

2. Then go back to the Section-to-be-named, right click on it and rename it -- don't even highlight it first -- just touch it with a right-click straight on.  It should now take the 'renaming' action.  Can't guarantee 100% but it does seem to work everytime for me.  And when I don't do it that one, it often doesn't rename.

CONCLUSION

Appreciate feedback from all and any toughts from the MSFT Team.

Thanks.
Joseph
