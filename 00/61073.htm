canadian tech say:

DVD/CD drives are a common failure point in PCs.  In desktop PCs the solution is pretty simple and not expensive.  You can buy a new one for about $20.  For example:

https://www.amazon.com/Internal-Support-GH24NSC0B-Essentials-Software/dp/B00N02BAV8/ref=sr_1_3?ie=UTF8&qid=1547651618&sr=8-3&keywords=internal+dvd+drive


  1. Unplug the power to your computer
  2. Hold the ON button in for 7 seconds
  3. Lay the computer on its side with the side where the wires are, down
  4. Remove the side panel
  5. Remove the two plugs in the back of the drive
  6. Remove the two phillips head screws that hold the drive in place

Usually opening the case is relatively easy.  Find the DVD drive.  Unplug the two plugs on the back end of it.  In some systems, you may have to remove the front panel to remove the drive.  The drive will likely be held in place with two phillips head screws.  Slide the new one in it is place.  Put the screws in.  Reconnect the cables (they cannot be interchanged and only go one way).  Restore the front panel and the side panel.  Start the system.  It will "discover" the new drive and install  all the drivers it needs without your intervention.

These drives are very standard and easily compatible in almost all cases.  All in all about 10 minutes and $20 and you have a brand new working DVD drive.  Worst case scenario is this does not correct your problem.  You are only out of pocket a bit.
