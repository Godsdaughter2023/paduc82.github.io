The "Hidden" option is accessible by right clicking on a cell and selecting "Format Cells."  Within the "Protection" tab, you can then select (or not select) "Hidden."  Once the sheet is protected, the cell itself is visible but nothing is displayed in the Formula bar when the cell is selected (so "hidden" really refers to the cell's formula as opposed to the actual cell itself..).  The goal of doing this at least in my case is to not bog down my end users with the complicated formulas contained within each cell.

A way to modify the context menu on Excel's "Recent - Microsoft Community

johnqflorida say:

A way to modify the context menu on Excel's "Recent Workbooks" menu to include "Open Read Only"?

I use Open Read Only a lot.  It's enormously helpful, to take away the risk that you might save something by accident.  Also, after messing up a few times (where later on, you WANT to save, but you had opened read-only) you get used to it.  A great tip, incidentally, is to add Excel read-only to your "Send to" menu for when you're using Windows Explorer, but that's another story.  (See p.s.).

QUESTION: Is there a way specifically on the Recent Workbooks screen to edit the context menu that opens up on a right click, that would include "Open Read Only".   I know in the past, there were various tips and tricks where you could alter context menus.  Is there one for this context menu?  (FYI, I do know the other ways to open read only, in this case, I'm asking specifically about modifying the Recent Workbooks context menu.)

P.S. - Here's how to add "Excel read only" to your Send-To menu: (slight variations based on your version of Windows).

  • Right click the shortcut to Excel from your Start Menu and choose Copy.  (If Copy isn't an option, then take the longer path to the shortcut, via Start Menu, All Programs, Microsoft Office, Excel instead).
  • Open Windows Explorer, go to your Send To folder. 
    The fastest way to get to your Send-to menu, is to type shell:sendto into the address bar.  This takes you to your send-to folder, which actually controls what shows up when you choose "Send to" from Explorer
  • Paste the Excel shortcut
  • Right click, choose properties, and modify the "Target" setting to add /r at the very end.  For example, "C:\Program Files (x86)\Microsoft Office\Office14\EXCEL.EXE" /r
  • Also, go to the GENERAL tab, and rename it "Excel READ ONLY" or something similar.
  • Click OK, until you're done editing.
  • And now, from Windows Explorer, when you right click something and choose the Send-to option, Excel Read Only is one of your choices.
