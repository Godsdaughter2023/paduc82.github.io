N97 wrong keyboard mapping

I would like to inform you of a bug that maybe you are unaware of. It affects the greek version of N97 (I don't know if it occurs also to other versions). In greek language we put accents on vowels, either on capital ones. To write an accented vowel, one has first to press ' (the key on the right of L) and then the corresponding vowel. Everything works right when the vowel is small. But when it is a capital, the following problem occurs. When you press ' and I, you get 'Ο instead of Ί. You have to press ' and Θ (U) to get 'Ι. In case of e.g. 'Ε, you have to press ' and ς (W), to get 'Ε, otherwise you get ' E (one accent and one E, not an accented E). The same applies to every other vowel. In other words, to get the accented capital vowels right, you have to press the accent (') and the key that is on the left of the vowel (or at the end of the previous row, if the vowel is first on a row).

 

I note this bug, as it is likely that you are unaware of it. Please, fix it in the next firmware.

In reply to deleted message

I can address one of these points.

7. Synchronizing between Outlook instances is accomplished by connecting each instance to the same mailbox. If you use a synchronizing protocol like Exchange, which can handle two-way synching of contacts, calendar, and mail, then all of your Outlook instances that connect to the same mailbox will stay in sync. Mail-only protocols like POP or IMAP don't handle non-mail items like contacts or calendar, so those items won't sync. IMAP can sync mail folders, but POP can't sync anything. This is a limitation of the protocol, not of Outlook.
