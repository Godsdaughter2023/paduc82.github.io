¡firedog say:

... set up a IMAP account and manually configured the servers,  

You don't need to configure anything to set up a Gmail account to use IMAP - the program does it automatically.

Gmail servers reject the sign on. 

Yes, they would. X-OAUTH authentication failed. None of the authentication methods supported by your IMAP Server (if any) are supported on this computer. Windows Live Mail doesn't support OAuth, which is what Google wants you to use.

OAuth is a similar system to that used at this site. When you sign in to answers.microsoft.com, you are redirected to a different site - login.live.com - to enter your credentials (username and password). Once they have been verified, you're sent back to answers.microsoft.com with a ticket saying that you've been authenticated. Microsoft Community never sees your credentials, so even if a hacker managed to get in to it, he wouldn't find any credentials to steal.

I'm gunshy of using the "allow less secure apps to access my account"

I don't think there's any need to be. Windows Live Mail isn't an 'app' like Twitter or Facebook or LinkedIn that have enormous online databases that need protecting. It's a simple mail client that sets up direct communication with a mail server. You have to be authorized to access your mailbox so only you can do it, and you do this by submitting your username and password. It's just the same as when you access any other mailbox - at your ISP, at Outlook.com or wherever.

Whether you like it or not, you won't be able to access your Gmail mailbox from Windows Live Mail without telling Google that you want to even though OAuth isn't supported.
