    Just a small point...

    My understanding is that jumpers are primitive 'electrical on/off switches'. Andrew E's instructions suggest to remove the power cord and remove the CMOS battery before moving the jumper about. At that point there is no electricity for the jumper switch to redirect, therefore moving a jumper from one position and then back to its original position can have no effect.

    The BIOS will get reset, but only because all electrical power has been removed from the mainboard, not because of the jumper switch movement. In my experience BIOS jumper switches are to force BIOS recovery from floppy drive or USB device at boot time, e.g.
