Xbox One S Disc Drive Problems: I have found a method that works - Microsoft Community

vigharthur skarpaason say:

Xbox One S Disc Drive Problems: I have found a method that works for standard DVD's not loading

Firstly, hello people I'm back again and this time with some good news that I know some of you will desperately be needing since the last youtube video I posted got a tonne of hits on this particular problem.

Sometimes, your Xbox One S fails to load DVD's right? 

Sometimes, it will display an HDCP or similar error right?

I have found a method that works to resolve this. 

So, I seen a few users posting video's about holding the disk for two seconds and letting the motor on the disk tray speed up. I cannot be any clearer 

DO NOT DO THIS 

It puts unnecessary strain on the motor and the mechanical parts that actually operate the slider are made from plastic and can snap. 

To fix this issue, also, don't be elevating your Xbox backwards or forwards, the center of gravity between the disk, the disk tray and the actual laser is primarily designed to be operated with the console flat on its bottom. Elevating this, as my experiments have shown is actually one of the causes of this error. Now maybe users have been cleaning and dusting? Moving it around or whatever the case may be, the problem isn't actually with the One S that so many people including myself have been convinced for so long was the case. 

The problem is when you move your console, if theirs a disc inside of it, the next time you load up your console it may be tricked into thinking it isnt a DVD your trying to play, but something thicker, thicker than a DVD.

Now to fix this, you need to firstly go to;

} Settings

   } Disc & Blu-ray 

      } Disc

You want to DISABLE both [Resume Playback] and [Play Disc Automatically] 

Now come out and select 

} Settings 

   } Disc & Blu-ray

      } Blu-ray

        } Persistent Storage

Make sure Persistent Storage clears, make sure that [Let my receiver decode audio] is NOT selected or for some reason when you load the DVD's next time there is no sound at all which trips the sound for Games too for some reason I have yet to discover. 

Now take out your DVD. 

Do a full shutdown, cable out, hold the power button in afterwards for at least 60 seconds and take the HMDI cable out and your ethernet cable should one be connected. 

Now after 5 to 10 minutes, firstly connect ONLY the power cable and leave that for 30 seconds, then the HDMI cable

Start up your console, connect your Ethernet should you have one and head back to 

} Settings

   } Disc & Blu-ray 

     } Blu-ray

Now select the feature [Enable BD Live to improve Blu-ray playback], make sure thats on. This stops all those weird Blu-rays from not working like before. 

Again one final time, full restart on your console, power cable out, everything out. 

Connect it all up after 30 seconds and yay! 

Regards, feedback from this either positive or especially negative is much appreciated as this problem i have been working on for a while.

gerry c j cornell say:

Here are some notes about Event Viewer Reports which may help. When you have a lot of errors you need to concentrate on system errors and warnings, even if it is applications that are giving you grief. Sorting system problems first can make resolving application problems easier. Note the time the computer is last booted and deal with those at the beginning of the boot first. Correcting the earlier errors can resolve later ones.

1. Normally when an error occurs on your computer looking in Event Viewer should be your starting point for finding a solution. Most system related errors are logged and getting an exact copy of the relevant report is important. Unfortunately understanding the reports is not easy and most computer users need help with their interpretation. I have more to say later on interpretation.

2. Event Viewer comprises four main Windows logs. These are Application, Security and System with Applications and Service logs as a more detail source.. For troubleshooting purposes System is by far the most important.

3. To access the System log select Start, Control Panel, Administrative Tools, Event Viewer, from the list in the left side of the window select Windows Logs and System. Place the cursor on System, right click and select Filter Current Log. Check the box before Error and click on OK and you see only Error reports. Click on the Date and Time Column Header to sort. You may need to click a second time to see the latest Report at the top.

4. A tip for posting copies of Error Reports! Run Event Viewer and double click on the error you want to copy. Click on the Copy button on the General tab to place a copy on your Clipboard and close Event Viewer. Now start your message and paste into the body of the message. Make sure this is the first paste after exiting from Event Viewer.

5. There are three types of Report, being Information, Warning and Error reports. In most situations it is Error Reports that offer the best information but occasionally Warning Reports provide useful clues.

6. All reports have date and time stamps and when troubleshooting it is important to concentrate on more recent reports. Study reports since the point when the computer was last booted and then check whether a similar report appeared in the previous session. If errors do not repeat investigation as to why they occurred is wasted effort.

7. Within individual reports the more important information is Event ID and Source as these help when looking for help on the internet. The description is equally important and copying the exact text for use as the search criteria greatly helps getting better results when using Google. Do not paraphrase descriptions when asking others for help.

8. For Vista and Windows 7 Google is the best source of information. Looking for text selected from the Description part of the Report (phrases not individuals words) as "this exact word or phrase" gets the best results. Concentrate on reports from Social Technet and Microsoft Answers within the last 12 months. Look elsewhere if an answer is proving difficult to find.

chris nh say:

The only physical modification I've made to my Surface Pro 3 is to attach a report cover  "binder bar" around the edge of the kickstand, which makes my Surface more comfortable in "laptop" mode...especially when I'm wearing shorts!. The particular binder bar that I found fits perfectly after I reduced its length just a little. Once installed it seems to stay in place naturally and adds only an almost invisible gap between the kickstand and back of the Surface when the kickstand is closed. I should also note that the kickstand still snaps into and stays in place when closed.

My suggestion to Microsoft is to add a soft (plastic or rubber) edge protector feature to the kickstands used in the Surface Pro 5 and future models with the goal of improving "lapability." BTW, I'm not sure how well my report cover binder bar idea will work on the Surface Pro 4 or other Surface models but I suggest that others give this a try.
