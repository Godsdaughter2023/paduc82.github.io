There is no DMZ feature on my router and you're right in that it would break everything else I needed ports forwarded to.  I did, however, find an acceptable solution.  I modified my miniupnpd.conf to deny all the 360's requests altogether and deny any other clients access to the ports the 360 wants (namely 3074).

deny 0-65535 192.168.1.20 0-65535      <--- 360's static IP denied for all ports

deny 3074 0.0.0.0/0 3074                      <--- All other devices denied on 3074

allow 1025-65535 0.0.0.0/0 1025-65535 <--- Allow all other devices ports 1025+ (except 3074)

I then used manual port forwarding for the 360.  So I get uPnP for everything on my network that plays nice with it and manual port forwarding for the 360 since it's not.  I also blacklisted the 360's ports from being assigned to other devices, to avoid conflicts.  Connection tests are all passing now and my other devices seem happy with the arrangement so far.
