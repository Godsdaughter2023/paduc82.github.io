If your monitor supports HDMI then you just do it like that. However not all monitors support that or have speakers so in order to have sound you'll need a workaround.

If your monitor only supports DVI or HDMI displayport you'll need to get a cable which converts from HDMI to DVI or HDMI to displayport

If you're in the market for a monitor, there's a few things to consider.

1. If you play a lot of really fast-paced games, or are into competition, you would probably want to get a TN panel monitor. These monitors offer the fastest response times, and high refresh rates (120hz) meaning little to no ghosting and really smooth movement on screen.

2. If you would rather sacrifice that extra speed and go for a more accurate color palette and a better overall picture, then you should look into IPS monitors. These are the monitors industry professionals use for photography and graphic works. They tend to be a more expensive than TN panel monitors, and can get really expensive for high end models, but you can find a decent model for a decent price. The sacrice in response time also isn't too bad. You're looking at like 6ms on an IPS compared to 0ms on a TN. I think the quality in picture is worth it.
