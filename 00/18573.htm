pamcaswell say: The TOC feature collects heading level text and some font formatting (such as bold and superscript) but not paragraph style formatting.  A heading typed in caps and lowercase with all caps formatting applied in the style will appear in the TOC as caps and lowercase.  So, If you want the all caps headings in the text to be all caps in the TOC, the heading text must be typed in caps.   On the other hand, if you want  the headings to be caps and lowercase in the text but all caps in the TOC, you will have to add the all caps font formatting to the TOC style for that heading, which, unfortunately, will give you capital roman numbers for front matter pages.

You didn't mention multilevel list numbering, but if the  A,B, etc. "preceding letters" are multievel list numbering, their being bold could well be the result of the bold being applied either to the number level from the define new multilevel list dialog or  to the number level in the text. The fix is to enter the define new multilevel list dialog (while the cursor is in the first level 1 heading in the document, click the multilevel list icon, then Define new multilevel list), click level 2, the Font button, and then clear the font style and size boxes and, back in the text, to select the level 2 heading numbers (not including the level 2 text) and press Ctrl+spacebar to clear direct font formatting.  When the TOC is updated the  numbering will no longer be bold.
