The poster *tells us* he is opening Outlook twice. Is it any surprise that Outlook then opens twice?

It sounds to me like the poster’s computer is slow or hanging at start up time, and the poster may be being a little too impatient in waiting and opening the program twice.

To rule this situation out, open task manager at startup (ctrl alt del > task manager > details tab under win 8.x or processes tab under win 7). Make sure the processes are sorted alphabetically, scroll down to “O” where outlook.exe would pop up. Then and only then launch outlook one time.

Does Outlook.exe pop up in the list? Then sit on your hands and do nothing else. Do not click it again. Wait and see if Outlook pops up. Wait 5 or 10 minutes if you have to, but wait. My guess is that Outlook will eventually start.

Why does it take so long? That’s a question for your IT person. Have them perform a cleanup of your computer including all temp files, browser history, defragment your HD, uninstall unnecessary programs, perform virus/malware scan. Have them bump up your RAM. Reimage your system. Replace your C: drive with a SSD HD. There are many things you can do to revitalize and improve a computer’s performance without replacing it entirely.

If Outlook does not start after 10 min, and the process is still there, it may be hung. This could be a windows or office installation issue.  You could try a repair or a removal and reinstall. You mention you are streaming office, perhaps if you are using some cloud version there is a network performance or adapter issue. Again, involve your IT person if you have one.

Hopefully this will give you better idea of where to start to troubleshoot this.

  3. My own UDF add-in

hansv mvp say:

This is a bit crude, but here is a macro to close ALL windows in the Visual Basic Editor (VBE):

 

Sub CloseAllVBEWindows()
    Dim i As Long
    With Application.VBE.Windows
        For i = .Count To 1 Step -1
            .Item(i).Close
        Next i
    End With
End Sub

 

After running it, select View | Project Explorer. If it's a floating window, move it to the left edge of the VBE window to "dock" it.

Then select View | Properties Window and do the same.

If desired, repeat for the Immediate, Locals and Watches windows.

Finally, open those modules/userforms that you want to see.

Select each of the open workbooks in the Project Explorer, and select File | Save or press Ctrl+S to save the current state of the windows.

If you have problems docking the windows, see http://www.jkp-ads.com/Articles/VBEDocking.asp.
