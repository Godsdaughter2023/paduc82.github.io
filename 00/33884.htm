andre da costa say:

The "Installing features and drivers" phase is the trickiest part of upgrade. If you see a failure c1900101 x300xx, it means we failed during this phase. The most common failure is x30018, which generally means a driver crashed when we loaded it for the first time. Any driver could cause the failure, but it may not crash 100 percent of the time. If you try again, it may just work.

also see:

  • How to: 10 Tips before installing Windows 10
  • How to: 10 things to do if Windows 10 Setup fails.

dndn_803 say:

Problem eventually solved - it was self inflicted pain!!!!

Due to some issues with a different PC upgrade to Win 10, I had concentrated on upgrading this latest machine using a DVD created from the ISO file.  I always skipped the download update option as the PC appeared to be current with regards to Win 7 updates..  This upgrade procedure always failed as described above (0xC1900101 - 0x30018)

When I previously tried to do an online update it took 5 hours to download the files and then appeared to get stuck at 25% point on 'preparing for installation'.  A number of on-line people suggested that if you disconnect from the internet it can free this up.  I did this and things proceeded, but eventually the upgrade failed in the same way (0xC1900101 - 0x30018).

My latest successful attempt was another on-line update.  Again it took the 5 hours to download.  When it appeared to get stuck at the 'preparing for installation phase' I just waited ~1.5 hours and it slowly completed.  The rest of the installation then proceeded smoothly.

I have read that some people have needed to wait 4 hours at the 'preparing for installation phase'.  My advice is just wait.

Anyway - now have this PC on Windows 10.

My thanks to Andre above and several Microsoft Answer Desk technicians who steered me in the right direction for the eventual fix.
