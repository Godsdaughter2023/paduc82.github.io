Surface 3 Touchpad First-Touch Delay/Lag - Microsoft Community

metal450 say:

Surface 3 Touchpad First-Touch Delay/Lag

I just bought the Surface 3 & Type Cover (note: that's Surface 3, not Surface Pro 3), and every time I begin using the touchpad, there's a delay/lag before the pointer reflects my finger's motion; i.e. I place my finger on the touchpad, begin moving, but the pointer remains still - only after dragging some initial distance does it actually start to reflect motion.  This occurs on every 'first touch' - i.e. if you place your finger on the touchpad, move it only slightly, remove your finger, touch again in the same spot, move it very slightly, repeat & repeat - you could literally get your finger across the whole width of the touchpad without the pointer ever moving a pixel.  This first-touch delay often leads to overcompensation; i.e. if I need to adjust the pointer position slightly, I move my finger slightly...but the pointer doesn't budge, so I keep moving my finger, then finally the pointer overshoots what I was after.

A bit of Googling has revealed numerous others complaining about this issue on previous Surface models, as far back as 2012.  i.e.:

  1. https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/surface/forum/surfacc-surfcover/trackpad-delay/d62419be-a6fc-4a4a-80fe-8a902ada0c8a?page=1&tm=1431830511956
  2. http://www.surfaceforums.net/threads/touchpad-lag-on-first-touch-can-it-be-fixed.13705/
  3. http://www.surfaceforums.net/threads/trackpad-delay.12499/

As per the second link above, this frustrating behavior is caused by the "Microsoft Input Configuration Device"; if you go to Device Manager->Human Interface Devices and disable it (and reboot), the delay goes away.  Unfortunately, this also disables all gesture support, palm rejection, and makes the touchpad SO finicky that the opposite issue arises: just attempting to tap/click often results in click-and-drag.  Now you might be tempted to say "so you're not satisfied when there's a delay, and you're not satisfied when it's super-responsive?"  All I can tell you is that I've been working exclusively on laptops w/ trackpads for over a decade, laptops from Dell, Toshiba, Apple, and others, and have never once experienced such behavior.  On the Surface, whether the driver is enabled or disabled, it's so cumbersome to manipulate the pointer that it really just sours the whole experience of using the device.  Which is a shame, because it's an awesome device - and this issue is clearly just software.

So not wanting to give up, I drove to the Microsoft Store & made an appointment with a tech.  What we found was that about half of the in-store demo systems didn't exhibit the delay...which we then realized was because those in-store units had the Input Configuration Device disabled too!  To me this seemed incredibly odd, almost as if MS is trying to 'hide' the issue from prospective customers.  Why on earth would the public store demos have a driver explicitly disabled (and only this particular driver)?  Unfortunately the conclusion of our appointment was more or less as expected: he classified this as a 'quirk' (his word for 'bug') with the driver, with no resolution other than simply waiting for MS to release an update (his word for 'fix').  Until then, I either disable the buggy driver and deal with the lack of gestures/palm rejection, enable the driver and wrestle with trying to get the pointer moving, or return the Surface and just go back to my other ultra-portable.

So why am I posting this when it's already been posted?

1) Because this is a new model Surface, and all previous posts were relative to older/pro models

2) Because the other thread here on the Microsoft forums didn't mention the Input Configuration Device specifically

3) Because Microsoft neglected to respond to the previous thread, despite the fact that it's existed for over two years.

My strong hope is that providing this further information will prompt them to take the issue seriously, as it really does ruin the experience with an otherwise great machine.  But if I can't get the touchpad to work properly, I can't see any solution but to return the Surface 3 and go with an alternative from Dell or other.  Which I'd really rather not do...

Addendum 1: The 'delay' setting under PC Settings->PC and devices->Mouse and touchpad does not resolve this issue; the first-touch delay exists regardless of the option selected there.  But on a related note, I do think that the ultimate solution will be a simple user-facing option to select the 'first-touch drag delay,' which I'm assuming is currently just hard-coded, and to a sufficiently high value as to cause this issue.

Addendum 2: A similar/seemingly related issue created by this same driver can be reproduced by going to the desktop, and dragging your finger smoothly across the touchpad - while you do, periodically click and release (while continuing to move your finger).  You'll note that each time you click or release, the pointer's motion will 'freeze' for a moment.  With the device disabled, the cursor moves smoothly, regardless of intermittent clicking/releasing (as it should - and as it does on other laptops).
