john mcghie say:
Hi Jerry:

A Couple of Secrets :-)

1)  You must disable "Widow/Orphan control" and enable "Keep lines together" if you want this to work.  Allowing a paragraph to split across a page negates the ability of "Keep with next" to keep the caption with the picture.

2)  The picture must be inline with text, in a paragraph, preferably its own paragraph.

3)  Word is NOT any other program: drive Word like you drive FrameMaker and you won't go far wrong, but it's NOT FrameMaker, and when you hit the outer edges, you have to do it Word's way or it won't work.

4)  You are expecting Word to have "pages" in a file on which you can put things.  It doesn't.  The "page" is an unknown concept to Word: it does not exist in the Word document.  Word makes up the pages on the way out: either to the screen or the printer.  Since the pages don't exist, you can't anchor anything to them.  The fundamental atom in a Word document is the "Paragraph".  Everything is in, or anchored to, a paragraph, in Word.  Think about this for a moment: knowing it will enable you to easily and dependably achieve what you want.

5)  You are expecting text to flow backwards to fill the gaps where pictures moved forwards.  Word can't do that.  No word-processor can.  For that, you need a Page Layout program (a desktop publisher, if you will).

6)  Most people these days would use InDesign rather than LaTeX for this kind of thing.  If you ensure that the style names in InDesign match the stye names in Word, it will simply suck the text straight in, discard Word's formatting and apply the InDesign formatting.

Hope this helps
