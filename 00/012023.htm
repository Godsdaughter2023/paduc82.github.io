savvy xavi say:

How to create a Video Playlist for your Zune

This is for those loyal Zunesters who are still using their Zunes in 2018! :)

So I wanted to add videos to my Zune and wanted them to play in succession and at first it seemed impossible, but it's actually pretty easy!

Add the videos to your Video folder, ideally in a subfolder. 

Open the Zune software. Go to collection view. Go to videos. 

Right click on the first video (you'll repeat this process for each video) and click edit. Once the edit dialog box appears, select the category drop down menu and select TV>Series.

For each video you want to be grouped together, assign the same name for "series title." Very important. Assign all those videos the same Season number as well. And as for each video, assign an episode number for the place you want them to play in the order.

By editing each video to be categorized as a TV series, part of a Season, and each having its own episode number, you'll deceive the Zune HD into grouping the videos together.

You won't see anything special happen in the collection view of the Zune software but once you sync all the videos to your Zune HD, you'll see the magic. Make sure to make all the edits PRIOR to syncing.

When you go to your Zune HD, go to videos> TV > and then you'll see your videos are grouped like an album. Simply hit the play button on the album and all videos contained therein will play in succession without prompts. Hope this wows someone as it did me.

Agreed CCleaner is useful but thankfully is very discriminating in removing cookies. For a better understanding of cookies, read the following from quietman7 at bleepingcomputer.com(January 2, 2011)

Cookies are text string messages given to a Web browser by a Web server. Whenever you visit a web page or navigate different pages with your browser, the web site generates a unique ID number which your browser stores in a text (cookie) file that is sent back to the server each time the browser requests a page from that server. Cookies allow third-party providers such as ad serving networks, spyware or adware providers to track personal information. The main purpose of cookies is to identify users and prepare customized Web pages for them.

Persistent cookies have expiration dates set by the Web server when it passes the cookie and are stored on a user's hard drive until they expire or are deleted. These types of cookies are used to store information between visits to a site and collect identifying information about the user such as surfing behavior or preferences for a specific web site.

Session (transient) cookies are not saved to the hard drive, do not collect any information and have no set expiration date. They are used to temporarily hold information in the form of a session identification stored in memory as you browse web pages. These types of cookies are cached only while a user is visiting the Web server issuing the session cookie and are deleted from the cache when the user closes the session.


Cookies can be categorized as:

Trusted cookies are from sites you trust, use often, and want to be able to identify and personalize content for you.

Nuisance cookies are from those sites you do not recognize or often use but somehow it's put a cookie on your machine.

Bad cookies (i.e. persistent cookies, long term and third party tracking cookies) are those that can be linked to an ad company or something that tracks your movements across the web.


The type of persistent cookie that is a cause for some concern are "tracking cookies" because they can be considered a where they have placed ads. They can keep count of how many times you visited a web page, store your username and password so you don't have to log in and retain your custom settings. When you visit one of these sites, a cookie is placed on your computer. Each time you visit another site that hosts one of their ads, that same cookie is read, and soon they have assembled a list of which of their sites you have visited and which of their ads that you have clicked on. Cookies are used all over the Internet and advertisement companies often plant them whenever your browser loads one of their banners.

Cookies are NOT a "threat". As text files they cannot be executed to cause any damage. Cookies do not cause any pop ups or install malware and they cannot erase or read information from a computer.

Flash cookies (orLocal Shared Objects) andEvercookies are a newer way of tracking user behavior and surfing habits but they too are not a threat, nor can they harm your computer.

An Evercookie is a Javascript API created and managed persistent cookie which can be used to identify a user even after they have removed standard and Flash cookies. This is accomplished by creating a new cookie and storing the data in as many storage locations (currently eight) as it can find on the local browser. Storage mechanisms range from Standard HTTP and Flash cookies toHTML5's new storage methods. When evercookie finds that other types of cookies have been removed, it recreates them so they can be reused over and over.

evercookie: the one cookie that you...just...can't...DELETE!

Evercookie, Extremely Persistent Cookies


Flash cookies are cookie-like data stored on a computer and used by all versions of Adobe Flash Player and similar applications. They can store much more information than traditional browser cookies and they are typically stored within each user’s Application Data directory with a ".SOL" extension, under the Macromedia\FlashPlayer\#SharedObjects folder. Unlike traditional cookies, Flash cookies cannot be managed through browser controls so they are more difficult to find and remove. However, they can be viewed, managed and deleted using the which erases any information that may have been stored on the computer. To prevent any Flash Cookies from being stored on your computer, go to theGlobal Storage Settings panel and uncheck the option "Allow third-party Flash content to store data on your computer". Sync Yahoo Contacts on Window Phone

I cannot sync my Yahoo contacts with my Windows Phone.  I have noticed this is an ongoing issue in previous posts.   Is there a fix for this other then buying someone else's software package????   It would seem this would have be corrected by now or Microsoft should allow users to get the vendor application free.
julie fka profjulie say:

Okay, you've confirmed what I thought. The default Yahoo account settings under settings > email+accounts does not support Contacts or Calendar syncing, so those items are not synced to your phone... the only thing that syncs through those account settings is email.

But there is a work around....I have tested this on my yahoo account this afternoon and can confirm that it works. You will leave your existing Yahoo account settings alone.... you are going to create another account on your phone that will only handle your Yahoo Contacts and Calendar.

  • On your phone select Settings > email+accounts
  • Tap "add an account" > select iCloud
  • Input your Yahoo email address & password. The attempt to sync will fail, but a new account will be created.
  • Now tap the so called iCloud account you just created to edit the settings to the correct Yahoo settings
  • Tap "advanced settings" > change the "Contacts server (CardDAV)" and "Calendar server (CalDAV)" settings as follows:

CardDAV server:      carddav.address.yahoo.com

CalDAV server:         caldav.calendar.yahoo.com

While you're editing the account, you can change its default "Account name" from iCloud to something like Yahoo2 or Yahoo Contacts - a label that makes sense to you.

After this new account syncs, you should be able to find your contacts in the people hub.
