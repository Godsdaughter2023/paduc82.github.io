marshall barton say:

You can calculate an incrementing number IF your data can be sorted in the order you want the numbers.  This would use a subquery and may get complicated if your sorting needs more than one or two fields.

An alternative that may or may not be acceptable is to make your To table's number field an AutoNumber field with a unique index.  Then, you don't have to do anything to get it filled in, but, while very unlikely, the numbers could possibly have a gap in the sequence.

john sr wilson say:
No, you cannot change text color in textboxes from the master (I'm assuming you know the difference a lot of folk use the terms interchangably!)

You would need to use vba code probably

Sub allchange()
Dim osld As Slide, oshp As Shape
For Each osld In ActivePresentation.Slides
For Each oshp In osld.Shapes
If oshp.Type = msoTextBox Then
If oshp.TextFrame.HasText Then
With oshp.TextFrame.TextRange.Font
.Color.RGB = RGB(0, 0, 0)
'add anything else here eg size
End With
End If
End If
Next oshp
Next osld
End Sub
