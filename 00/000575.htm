ordnance1 say:

Query calculated field, using IF statement

My query has 2 fields:

 

VacDate

AllowedOFF

 

What I need is a third field which will be called Status that performs  a calculation similar to the one below. The calculation returns  #error. Not sure if that is because VacDate uses the Count funtion.

 

IIF(VacDate >= AllowedOFF,2,1)

 

 SELECT qryDaysOff.VacDate, Count(qryDaysOff.VacDate) AS CountOfVacDate, Format([VacDate],"ddd") AS [Day], DLookUp("PeriodID","tblMarkupPeriods","StartDate=#" & DMax("StartDate","tblMarkupPeriods","StartDate <=#" & [VacDate] & "#") & "#") AS Markup, DLookUp("NumberOfPeople","tblPeriodDays","PeriodID=" & DLookUp("PeriodID","tblMarkupPeriods","StartDate=#" & DMax("StartDate","tblMarkupPeriods","StartDate <=#" & [VacDate] & "#") & "#") & " And DayOfWeek='" & Format([VacDate],"dddd") & "'") AS AllowedOFF, IIf([VacDate]>=[AllowedOFF],2,1) AS Status
FROM qryDaysOff
GROUP BY qryDaysOff.VacDate, Format([VacDate],"ddd"), DLookUp("PeriodID","tblMarkupPeriods","StartDate=#" & DMax("StartDate","tblMarkupPeriods","StartDate <=#" & [VacDate] & "#") & "#"), DLookUp("NumberOfPeople","tblPeriodDays","PeriodID=" & DLookUp("PeriodID","tblMarkupPeriods","StartDate=#" & DMax("StartDate","tblMarkupPeriods","StartDate <=#" & [VacDate] & "#") & "#") & " And DayOfWeek='" & Format([VacDate],"dddd") & "'")
ORDER BY qryDaysOff.VacDate;

rick rothstein, mvp - excel say:
Hey, I usually drag the formula in the first cell and excel changes the cell numbers/references itself. But now I am trying to multiply a whole series with a particular cell.ie B1 – B10 with only A1 but auto fill changes the references automatically - B1 with A1, B2 with A2 & so forth.. How do I do this??  

Use $ signs to freeze either the column or row or both. For example, if you were dragging your formula and you wanted each formula to always reference cell A1 in each new formula, you would use $A$1 and no matter which way you dragged, that reference would not change. You do not have to put $ signs on both elements of the address... it depends on what part you need frozen and which direction you plan to drag the formula. This is called absolute referencing as opposed to what you do now which is called relative referencing... you might want to look these terms up in the help files (or google for them) in order to learn more about them.
