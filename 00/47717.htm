Let’s understand the difference between refresh and reset on windows 8.

Refresh: When you refresh the computer the Apps you installed from websites and DVDs will be removed. Apps that came with your PC and apps you installed from the Windows Store will be reinstalled. Windows puts a list of removed apps on your desktop after refreshing your PC.

Reset: If you reset the computer all of your personal files will be deleted and your settings will be reset. All apps that you installed will be removed. Only apps that came with your PC will be reinstalled.

All the new computers are with GPT partition and UEFI based firmware interface and there should be no issue in reset or refresh.

Check the article on UEFI:

What is UEFI?

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows-8/what-uefi

Windows and GPT FAQ

http://msdn.microsoft.com/en-us/library/windows/hardware/dn640535(v=vs.85).aspx

If you are unable to reset or refresh the computer follow the steps given below.

This issue could have occurred due to these reasons.

1. Corrupt Windows System Files.

2. Due to bad sectors on hard disk.

I would suggest you to run check disk and then try to refresh or reset again.

Follow the steps to check disk for errors.

a) Move the mouse pointer to bottom left of the screen where you see preview of start menu and right-click to bring up a list of Windows settings.

b) Click on ‘Command Prompt (Admin)’ to bring up elevated command prompt.

Note: Click ‘Yes’ if it prompts for permission.

c) In the command prompt, type ‘chkdsk /r’ and press Enter.

Answer Y when ‘chkdsk’ asks you if you want to check the drive next time computer boots (restarts).

After check disk try to refresh or reset.

How to restore, refresh, or reset your PC.

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-IN/windows-8/restore-refresh-reset-pc

Refresh: The above mentioned article contains steps to Refresh and Reset PC. The apps that came with your PC or you installed from Windows Store will be reinstalled, but any apps you installed from other websites and DVDs will be removed. You'll find a list of the removed apps on your desktop after refreshing your PC.

Reset: When you choose reset your PC, you'll be prompted to choose whether you want to erase data quickly or thoroughly. There is no such thing.  Your can re-install Windows 7/8/8.1, using your old Windows key (if necessary) and it will still activate.  And you can later go back to Windows 10 and it will activate.  I have read this in various FAQs about Windows 10 such as this one .

Warning: Following these steps will remove all of your data, programs, and settings.
Start your PC and select the Power icon in the bottom right-hand corner of the lock screen.
While holding down the Shift key, select Restart.
On the Boot options menu, select Troubleshoot > Reset this PC > Remove everything.
Your PC will restart and will reset itself.
