It should look like:

/Drive Shafts Rear Wheel & 4WD/BMW Rear Wheel Drive Shafts For Manual Shift Transmissions/6 Cylinder

But instead looks like:

/Drive Shafts Rear Wheel & 4WD 00/BMW Rear Wheel Drive Shafts For Manual Shift Transmissions/6 Cylinder0

The problem has become un-necessary "0" zeros appearing in the text strings. The 0's appear where a cell is BLANK.

I have set the page in the Excel options to show each empty cell as BLANK.

They do not have 0's in them but they appear when the CONCATENATE function groups more than 2 cells together.

The 0's alter the address and will throw an error in the server because the address with extra 0's does not exist.

It is like a Postal worker randomly adding 0's to addresses on envelopes - they will not get delivered. 

What I have tried:

Checking the formating on the sheet

Copying to a new sheet

Changing from CONCATENATE to stringing the cells together using & - same result

Deleting one at a time to see which column is the issue - they all are

Is there some trick i am not aware of?

Please let me know.

teylyn say:

Hello,

you are using settings that format cells to hide zero values returned by formulas. You need to understand that this formatting is only of cosmetic nature, used for viewing the sheet on the screen or when printed. The underlying values of the cells are still 0 if the formula returns a 0. Therefore, you need to factor that into your concatenation formula. 

One approach is to inspect each cell with an IF statement and only use it if it does not contain a zero.

=IF(B101<>0,B101,"")&IF(C101<>0,C101,"")&if(D101<>0,D101,"") etc...

Another option is to wrap the formula into a Substitute function that replaces all zeroes with empty strings, like

=Substitute(B101&C101&D101&E101&F101&G101&H101,"0","")

Note that the latter approach will remove all zeroes from the text, even ones that appear legitimately in some of the cells.

michal (skype) say:

Actually, some answers here are not completely correct.

You have 2 options - cancel the number and get a new one, or contact customer support and ask them to refund the number, explaining your problem.

  • Cancellation is faster, but you won't get money back and the number will stay with you until end of the paid period.
  • If you contact customer support, it might take a bit more time, but they can refund your purchase (if you provide good reason) and they can mark the number as bad, so that no one else will get it.

I think the second option is better both for you and also for future.

I am sorry for the inconvenience and I hope this helps you solve your problem.

Michal
