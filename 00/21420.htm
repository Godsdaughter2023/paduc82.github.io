mehoop1506 say:

How do I create a sales tax chart for 7.25%?

What is the formula to create a sales tax chart at 7.25%? 

From amount - To amount      Tax

joeu2004 say: mehoop wrote:

This is not what I wanted.  I want to know in Column (A) From, Column (B) To, Column (C) Tax
From     To       Tax
0.07     0.20      0.01
0.21     0.34      0.02


Try the following.  Formulas are described below.



A2:  =ROUNDUP(0.005/7.25%,2)
B2:  =ROUND(ROUNDUP((C2+0.005)/7.25%,2)-0.01,2)
C2:  0.01
A3:  =ROUND(B2+0.01,2)
B3:  =ROUND(ROUNDUP((C3+0.005)/7.25%,2)-0.01,2)
C3:  =ROUND(C2+0.01,2)

Copy A3:C3 down as needed.

Format columns A:C as Number with 2 decimal places or something similar.

As a double-check, you might also use the following formulas:

D2:  =IF(ROUND(A2*7.25%,2)-$C2=0,"","error")
E2:  =IF(ROUND(B2*7.25%,2)-$C2=0,"","error")

Copy D2:E2 down as needed -- the same number of rows as in columns A:C.

Suppose you copy down through row 20000.  Then the following formulas should return zero:

=COUNTIF(D2:D20000,"error")
=COUNTIF(E2:E20000,"error")

Notes:

1. It would be prudent to put 7.25% into some cell (e.g. D1), and replace all instances of 7.25% with absolute references to that cell (e.g. $D$1) in the formulas above.

2. The use of ROUND(...,2) avoids infinitesimal "errors" (anomalies) that arise with Excel arithmetic, for example when adding or subtracting 0.01.

3. It might seem more efficient and intuitive to use the following formulas:
B2: =ROUND(A3-0.01,2)
A3:  =ROUNDUP((C2+0.005)/7.25%,2)
B3:  =ROUND(A4-0.01,2)
But it is somewhat harder to copy A3:C3 down as needed.  In the last row (e.g. row 20000), you would still need the following formula:
B20000:  =ROUND(ROUNDUP((C20000+0.005)/7.25%,2)-0.01,2)

How can I reduce the size of a photo to say passport size - Microsoft Community

richwin say:

How can I reduce the size of a photo to say passport size

I would like to reduce a personal (face) photograph  to a small size that can be used on a membership card.

volunteer j say:
I would like to reduce a personal (face) photograph  to a small size that can be used on a membership card.
==================================
If you are creating this card with software on your
computer, Microsoft Word for example...you may
be able to insert a full sized photo and just left
click/drag the corner handles of the photo to reduce
it to any size you need.

Three possibilities:

1)  The following online site may be worth a try.

OddPrints - Print your own passport photos
http://www.oddprints.com/passport
(Begin by scrolling down to the bottom
of the page and left click "Get Started")


2)  Here's another online site...

Try this online site for creating passport photos.
You can create a 4x6 page with 6 pictures and
then download it to your computer and print it
as a 4x6 image.
http://www.epassportphoto.com/

Follow these steps:
A) Get My Free Passport Photos /

B) Browse to the picture / Next /

C) When the picture is uploaded...
drag a box around the face / Next /

D) Download and Print.

E) Save the photo to a folder on your hard drive
and print it as a 4x6.


3)  One last method using the freeware
FastStone Image Viewer

Creating a Passport Photo:

You need to crop your image to 2x2"
and print it exactly that size....

The free program, FastStone Image
Viewer can do what you request.

(FWIW...it's always a good idea to create a System
Restore point before installing software or updates)

FastStone Image Viewer
http://www.faststone.org/FSViewerDetail.htm

First you will have to download and
install the program.

Now, open the program and select
the picture you wish to create the
passport photo with. The selected
image will show in the bottom left of
your screen.

Now go to...Edit / Crop Board. (or type...X)

Click the "Define Paper Ratios" button.
(it's just to the right of the Paper Ratio
drop window)

Click the "Add" button and enter...2 x 2"
click...OK...

Open the "Paper Ratio" drop window
and select 2 x 2".

Left click / drag a box around the exact
portion of the image you wish to include
in your finished passport photo. You can
resize by dragging the corner handles
and reposition by dragging the 4-way
arrow. To finish...click the "Crop" button.

Now...go to...File / Print...

Open the "Size" drop window and choose
"Normal".

Click "Print".
