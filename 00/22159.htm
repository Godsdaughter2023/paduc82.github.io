Hi SJ,

Yes, my family sounds similar to yours! Anyway, here's the deal:

1. Office 2011 is fully compatible with Sierra, but you need to be running the latest Office 2011 update, which is 14.7.1. The latest full install package (already patched to this version) can be download from the Microsoft network at https://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?linkid=273962

2. Your main problem though is that his product key (i.e. the one that he lost) is associated with the serial number of his old Mac (which is burned in and encrypted in the /Library/Preferences/com.microsoft.office.licensing.plist file). The Migration Assistant (if it works) will copy that file to his new MBP but of course it will contain the wrong serial number, so the product will refuse to activate itself. If he had the original product key, it would be possible to call support and have them transfer it, but without knowing what the key was (and it cannot be reverse engineered from his old machine), it won't be possible to do that.

3. Office 2011 support ends on 10/10/17, so after that date, we'll no longer supply security patches or bug fixes, nor will it be compatible with versions of macOS after 10.12 Sierra, so now would be a good time to get him on the 2016 release. If he's not sold on the benefits of Subscription, he can still buy the 'Perpetual' (i.e. non Subscription) version of Office 2016 for Mac. You can find this online and at retailers for ~$229 (see https://www.microsoftstore.com/store/msusa/en_US/pdp/Office-Home-Business-2016-for-Mac/productID.323023600?ICID=Office_Mac_ModE_HB16Mac)

Hope this helps!
