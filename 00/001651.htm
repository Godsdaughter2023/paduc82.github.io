karl timmermans [outlook mvp 2012-2018] say:

Can anyone explain how I can synchronise calendar and contacts so that a change on one device will show up on the other(s)?  

#1 - <Email clients> (i..e. Outlook desktop is just one) do not sync data to other <email clients> on other devices. Data is sync'd to a device from an <email account> depending on the protocol used to connect the <email client> with the <email account>

#2 - Summary of protocol functionality (regardless of email client used)

POP - uni-directional download from <Inbox> to device

IMAP - bi-directional sync of email folders only

Exchange ActiveSync\Exchange - bi-directional sync of email/contacts/calendar items from account to device

To answer your question - configure your Outlook.com account in Outlook (desktop client) using <auto-discover> (entering your email address & password). If your account has been migrated to the Office 365 backend, it will be configured as an Exchange account otherwise it will configured as an Exchange ActiveSync. 

To determine if your Outlook.com has been migrated, log into it with your browser (webmail) and if it shows as <Outlook Mail ({Preview)>, it has been migrated.
