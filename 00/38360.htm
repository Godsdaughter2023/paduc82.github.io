There's no such thing as a "Documents and Settings" folder.  It's only there as a re-direct.  If a program is hard-coded to look or save in something under "Documents and Settings", that program would crash on Windows Vista or 7 because that folder doesn't exist.  To fake a little bit of compatibility with these badly made programs, this re-direct tricks them into saving/looking into the Users folder instead.  Since it's not really a folder, you can't access it.  No one can. Not even the super mega awesome plus administrator.  When you "use your software to get into" documents and settings, the software is seeing the Users folder.  It's being redirected.  The way it was designed to work.

You will break your computer irreparably if you try, and you'll have to reinstall your system to regain proper function.  Please quit trying to hack it.  You shouldn't even see that folder unless you're already doing some advanced things.  Usually folks that go turn on hidden/system file display already know about what they should and shouldn't be hacking at.

After you've thoroughly broken the functionality by mucking around, you'll come back and complain about how much Windows sucks.  If you had just used your computer instead of trying to hack a folder that doesn't even exist, you'd be so much happier and have a working computer.

a. user say:

Hi,

You can transfer files from your phone to Surface via Bluetooth. Here's how:

1. Make sure that your Surface's Bluetooth adapter is turned on and visible to Bluetooth devices. Go to Start > Settings (Gear icon) > Devices > Bluetooth > More Bluetooth options.

2. On your phone, turn on Bluetooth and search for devices. You should see your Computer name (Surface), tap on it and tap pair.

3. You should see a notification on the Surface device to show the pair code. Click "OK" if the code on your phone matched with what is shown on your Surface.

4. After pairing and you're ready to transfer files, tap or click "Send or receive files via Bluetooth", then just choose "Receive files" for transferring from phone to Surface or "Send files" if from Surface to phone.

5. By this time your Surface should be on standby waiting for transfer. It will automatically receive the file(s) and should show you a loading page saying "Receiving files". After it completes the transfer, a page saying " Save the received file will show, click or tap "Finish" to save the file(s).

6. You're all set. The file(s) you transferred will be save in the Documents folder by default.

If you encounter issues with Bluetooth devices, check out this article.

arminatorx say:

If I understand your question correctly, you do NOT want your brother to be able to play online through your gold membership? 

In that case, in addition to steeledwand7664's answer, be aware that the first Xbox One console you sign in on, will become your "Home Console". 

The Home Console will then share all your games INCLUDING your Gold membership to anyone playing on that console, including your brother. 

If you don't want your brother to play online, BUT be ABLE to play your games, your only way of doing that would be to add your brother to an adult family account at https://account.microsoft.com/family and then disallow him to play online in the online and privacy settings on https://account.xbox.com/settings

If you want to completely disallow your brother from playing your games and Gold benefits, you need to go to the console your brother plays on, sign into your account, and under Settings-->Personalization remove the Home Console setting for that console on your account. 

Or simply find a different console that you will set as your Home Console, as your account can only have one Home Console at any time. 

And if you want to prevent your brother from signing into your account and again benefit from your games and Gold, make sure to protect your account with a passkey as steeledwand7664 described.
