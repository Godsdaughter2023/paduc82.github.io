Discs in DVD players spin at 1x, and they generally not on nearly as much as a Xbox console, unless you're watching one film after another all day.

PC DVD drives can still fail though, believe me, I repair PCs and I've replaced a fair few.

 

If you install your 360 games you can extend the life your consoles DVD drive immensely.

The BSOD PAGE_FAULT_IN_NONPAGED_AREA (ddkmd.sys), is caused by the reasons below

  • Installation of faulty hardware or in the event of failure of installed hardware (usually related to defective RAM, be it main memory, L2 RAM cache, or video RAM).
  • Another common cause is the installation of a faulty system service.
  • Antivirus software can also trigger this error, as can a corrupted NTFS volume

Before we proceed, I would like you to provide the information provided below:

1. Did you make any changes to your PC prior to this issue?

2. What task were you performing when you encountered the BSOD?

For now, I suggest that you log into Safe Mode with Networking via Media Creation Tool, and check if you can rectify the cause of the issue.

To log into  Safe Mode with Networking, create a media tool by referring to the link below

http://www.microsoft.com/en-us/software-download/windows10

(Refer to the section "Installing Windows 10 using the media creation tool " in the above article for creating the tool).

  • Once done start your computer from the installation media. Insert the disk or USB stick and restart your computer. If you see a message asking you to “Press any key to boot from DVD,” do so. If you don’t see the message, you might need to change the boot order in your computer's BIOS settings so that it first starts from the disk or USB. When you see the 'Install Windows' page, tap or click 'Repair your computer' link to start the Windows Recovery Environment. 
  • Under Choose an option screen, select Troubleshoot > Advanced options > Startup Settings > Restart.
  • After your PC restarts, select a startup setting from the list of options by pressing the corresponding number on your keyboard.

When you have successfully access Safe Mode with Networking, I would suggest that you run Hardware and Device Troubleshooter to check if there are any faulty hardware in your PC. This tool will also help detect and fix any error that might have led to the BSOS

  1. Click on the Start button, and type “Troubleshooting” on the search bar.
  2. Click on troubleshooting, then click on “Hardware and sound”.
  3. Under Hardware and sound, click on “hardware and devices”.
  4. A pop-up will appear, click on the “Next” button and your hardware troubleshooting will start.
