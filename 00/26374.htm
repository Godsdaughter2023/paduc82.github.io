kevgallacher---01 say:

Basically there are 5 different network modes the phone can work in

 

Wi-fi, is just connected to a local wi-fi connection

GPRS, is a 2G connection and is the slowest you can have (ofton called 2.5G)

Edge, is again 2G but better than GPRS (often called (2,75G)

3G, is a proper fast connection, it can use more power and has limited availablity depending on location.

 

and finally H, which means evolved HSPA is often called 4G but is more like 3.9G, it is the best possible connection that the phones currently offer and can even outperform a lot of peoples home broadband connection is even more limited than standard 3G and uses even more power this is a step towards true 4G.

 

Data connection just means you are connected to the internet through one of the last 4, (not wifi).

3G is one of the last 2.

 

If you aren't concerned about high speeds, are running low on power or are in an area with patchy 3G availablity, it is sometimes better to turn 3G off completely.

michelmoacanin say:

I too had the same problem on each of 5 computers.  Here's a link to a workaround that MS posted:  https://support.office.com/en-us/article/outlook-stops-syncing-aol-email-989114f0-2c6f-45f8-b403-f5e991d1603f

I contacted AOL and they basically told me to pay up or pound sand.  So, I did some research and found the process above.  

I find that I only needed to rename a couple of folders under my inbox (rather than all of them) in each of my accounts to start the process of syncing to AOL.

Once synchronization starts, everything is fixed except for any rules you may have created to automatically move mail from any source to a specified folder.  You'll need to fix the rules as the method below breaks links to the folders.

In case the link is broken, here's the text of "Method 1" which worked for me:

Description:  

Starting in September 2018 some Outlook users with AOL accounts found that email stopped syncing.  This issue is caused by a change in the AOL service.  Please contact AOL to report this issue and ask that it be investigated further.

Workaround:  

For most affected users renaming or removing folders under the Inbox mitigates the issue.  

Method 1: Rename folders (recommended method)

  1. Open Outlook and make note which folders are under the Inbox

  2. Close Outlook

  3. Go to https://www.aol.com and log into web mail

  4. Rename each folder under the Inbox folder (e.g. for a folder named “Recipes” rename it to “Recipes1”)

  5. Launch Outlook, allow a few minutes to allow sync to complete before confirming if the issue is now resolved
