kerwinyang_msft7476 say:

hi denis,

for the shared files in the shared with me section, they are actually stored in the onedrive for business library of the people who has left your company. if you don't want to see the shared files, you can consider the two following options.
1. for the people who has left the company, if their user accounts can be deleted, you can delete them completely to erase the data, then other users will not see the shared files in the coming days.

2. if you want to keep these user accounts, you can consider accessing their onedrive for business library and stopping sharing the files or remove them completely to let other users don’t see the shared files.
to stop sharing, you can select the file and click stop sharing option, or click advanced option to remove the users’ permission. you can also go to site settings > under users and permissions > site permissions, remove all the users’ permission to stop sharing the whole onedrive for business library.

 

to access other users’ onedrive for business, the user account and password are required. if it’s not convenient, as global admin or sharepoint admin, you can go to sharepoint admin center > user profiles > under people part > manage user profiles, input the user name you want in the find dialog box, then click the manage site collection owners option to add yourself (the admin) to the site collection administrators dialog box.
 

if you have been added to the box, you will also be the administrator of the user’s personal site just as same as the specific user to control or manage the users’ onedrive for business library.

if anything is unclear, feel free to post back and let us work together to sort out the query.

thanks,
kerwin

vincentchoy say:

Latency will be an issue for you.

That being said, local internet access speeds in certain parts of Asia may also be an issue (as compared to Europe or USA). So certain users may have their problems compounded by latency issues as well as local internet speeds.

I suppose the best you can do is to ensure the users in Asia do not have their own local WAN issues to add to the latency issues.

For that, check with the client if their normal internet access is also slow, and if they have WAN Optimization devices have a look at their Utilization Charts to see whether there is evidence of WAN congestion on their network.
