kentcarter say:

Outlook 13 - Problem with Duplicate Sub Folders when Archiving

I am using Outlook 13.   In order to organize my emails I use many subfolders.  The structure of the subfolder tree evolves and changes all the time, as I add/delete folders, rename them, or move them to different locations in the tree - in other words I work in the real world and not one where there is a perfect filing system that never changes.  

However, this causes a big problem when I archive my mailbox, which I need to do to stop it from exceeding its capacity.  For example, if I have moved a subfolder, when I archive the mailbox I will now have 2 subfolders with the same name - one in the old location and one in the new location.  If I rename a subfolder, same thing - I will now have 2 of them in the archive, one under each name.  

This is a major headache as it means the archive subfolder tree is a complete mess every time I archive.  I have to search thru the tree, remember what folders I moves/renamed, manually transfer all emails under the old location/name for that subfolder to the new one, then delete the (now empty) subfolder with the old location/name.

Has anyone come up with a better way to do this? Appreciate any advice.

kentcarter say:

Hi Nayana

1.  My email account is configured as IMAP.

2.  I used the default archive folder.  

I have come up with a solution that solves the problem:

1.  Archive your mailbox including all subfolders.

2.   From then all, just drag any new email (sent or received) you want to save directly to relevant Archive subfolder.  Subfolders can be added, deleted, moved around as you wish in Archive.

3.  The subfolders should then be deleted from the mailbox.  The mailbox is now just used as a temporary inbox for new mail.

This frees up a huge amount of space in the mailbox, and solves the problem of duplicate folders/emails in the Archive.  The only disadvantage is the archive emails are not available online, so can't be accessed thru webmail and are vulnerable to be lost if anything goes wrong with your computer.  So, regular backups of the Archive folder are needed.
