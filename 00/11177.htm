Automate extraction of data from word files to excel table cells

Greetings! I have an problem.  I work in a factory.  I have library of parts.  Information on each part is stored as a product data sheet in .doc format.

What's the fastest way that I can map the data from the individual product sheet, and turn it into a record or row in a spreadsheet.  The data sheets don't employ tables or anything, they're just typed in free-hand.

 

Product Data Sheet

  1. Part Number
  2. Quality Report Numbers
  3. Open Issues Report Numbers
  4. Size

Spreadsheet

Column1:  Part Number

Column3:  Open Issues Report Numbers

Column4:  Size

Column5:  Weight

Column6:  Vendor

Column7:  Purchase Order

Column8:  ProblemNotation1

Column9:  ProblemNotation2

Columnn:  ProblemNotationn

 

I would prefer not to just manually migrate the information line-by line, if I can help it.  Do you see this as being a potential "nightmare project" as I do?

jay freedman say:
It probably does have potential to be a "nightmare project" but there are some things you can do to smooth the path.

A lot is going to depend on just how uniform (or not) the individual documents are: what characters were used to separate the data items, whether "creative" formatting was applied, and so forth.

Do the items include words that identify the values? If so, are the words always the same, or might they vary a bit or contain typos? If there are no such labels, can the items be identified by their positions (i.e., are they always in the same order)?

It may be possible to use the Convert Text to Table feature to turn all the contents of a document into a Word table, which can then be copy/pasted into Excel. If this works manually, it can be programmed in a macro.

Turning to the batch processing of multiple documents, it's important to know where the document files are stored, whether all in one folder or spread across many folders. It might also be necessary to know whether their names follow a pattern.
