Hello Linda,

I can understand the inconvenience you have experienced.

The "Safely Remove Hardware" icon can sometimes disappear from the notification area.

This behavior can occur due to incorrect settings.

I would suggest you to follow the methods given below and check.

Method 1: Firstly, make sure that the Safely Remove Hardware icon is set as 'Show icon and notification' Follow the steps to given below to do that.

Click on the upward arrow by the clock in the taskbar. This will show the hidden items and allow you to safely remove the devices.

This will allow you to see the remove hardware icon. If you want the icon to appear permanently on display then click on the up arrow in the notification area and then, when the list of icons appears, click the Customize option. A list of all of the icons will now appear. Locate the safely remove hardware icon and click on the arrow in the dialogue box to the right of this option. From the drop down list select 'show icon and notification' and then press the OK button. The safely remove hardware icon will now permanently be displayed in the notification area.

if issue still persists try method 2.

Method 2: The "Safely Remove Hardware" icon can sometimes disappear from the notification area. Even though there are devices installed that require it - it's nowhere to be found.

When this happens, you may perform the following steps:

1. Press the Windows Key+ X key. and select Run.

2. Enter the following command and hit Enter:

    RunDll32.exe shell32.dll,Control_RunDLL hotplug.dll

This will bring up the "Safely Remove Hardware" dialog box.From this dialog box you can click on the device you want to remove, and press Stop. Once Windows is done with it, you can then remove the device.

 

If it happens often enough, you can create a desktop shortcut for the “safely remove hardware” command. 

  1. Right click on your desktop, click New, and then click Shortcut.

  2. In the resulting dialog box enter the following command:

    RunDll32.exe shell32.dll,Control_RunDLL hotplug.dll

  3. Click Next and change the shortcut name to Safely Remove Hardware.

  4. Click Finish. 

Add icons to the desktop or taskbar

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows-8/create-desktop-shortcuts-icons

 

Now, whenever you need to remove hardware, and the icon isn't present on the taskbar notification area, you can simply double click on this new shortcut and get the job done.

diskpart
list volume
select volume 3
Replace 3 with the number of the volume which is assigned a drive letter. (In hundreds of MBs)
remove letter="G"
Replace G with the Recovery drive letter.

Don't worry, removing a drive letter would not cause any harm to your device.
