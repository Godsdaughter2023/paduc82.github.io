brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

In my opinion, it would be a mistake to get a new account.  Instead, create an alias for your existing account.  Click the Options gear at the right end of the Menu bar, choose "More mail settings" or "Options" (you might have either), then "Your email accounts".  Click "Create an Outlook.com alias".  You'll probably need to reenter your passowrd, but you'll then see this:

Choose the alias you want.  You can change the domain drop-down to hotmail.com or live.com if you wish, as well.  AFter you've chosen your name, click "Create and alias".

Now, back on the "Your email accounts" option page, at the very bottom, you'll see a "Default 'From' address" drop-down.  Change that to be your new alias.  Now, anyone who uses either your old address or your new one can reach you and when you reply, or send a new message, the recipient will see your new address.

joy patra say:
Everybody,

Thanks for trying to help me. I've found a workaround that should work for most of the people - in fact, many people may not even notice that something is amiss.

The visibility setting is maintained correctly if I goto Comm Manager and choose the Settings->Bluetooth settings from there, and manage the visibility settings.

It's not maintained correctly if I go via Settings->Connections->Bluetooth and modify the visibility settings from there.

So now I always go through Comm Manager if I have to make any change.

Hope this post will be helpful to other people facing similar issue. Am not sure whether the issue is with Microsoft or with Sony's customizations.
