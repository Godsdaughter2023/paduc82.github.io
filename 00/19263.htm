What I want to do is have the Membership Status field calculate the Membership Status based on the current date.  In other words, if the Expiration Date is less than today's date, then the Membership Status field would have an "E" entered into it indicating that the membership had expired..  But if the Expiration Date were greater that today's date, then the Membership Status field would have an "A" entered into it indicating that the membership had not yet expired.

Try this expression:

IIf([Expiration Date]<Date(),"E","A")

hansv mvp say:

Instead of creating a calculated field within the table, create a query based on the table.

Add all fields from the table to the query grid, or use the * entry in the list of fields for this purpose.

In the first unused column, enter the expression proposed by Duane:

AorE: IIf([Expiration Date]<Date(),"E","A")

Save the query, and use it as record source for forms and reports, and as the basis for other queries.

Resolution via linkhttps://support.microsoft.com/en-au/kb/2933402/en-us?wa=wsignin1.0

 

there are more tips in the link, but what worked for me was the below instructions:

 

To sync your Deleted items folder or your Trash folder: 

  1. Go to the Inbox for your Microsoft account.
    1. Tap the Ellipsis button […], and then tap Folders.
    2. Tap Show all folders.
    3. Select the Deleted items (or Trash) folder. Then, tap Sync this folder.
      Note Alternatively, select the folder you frequently moving emails to from the phone, then sync that folder.
    4. Go back to the inbox of your Microsoft account and tap the Sync button. If you continue to get the same error, tap the sync button several times. It may take a while until the phone syncs all the information it needs to sync. 

I hope this helps other users that may have encountered a similar issue :)
