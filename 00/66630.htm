Pavan is quite correct. As it is the ROOT drive, the SYSTEM OS that you are imaging, (along with any other drives you wish to image) it cannot be saved to itself. You will note when creating the image, the root drive shows up only as a drive to be copied. It does not show up in the destination drives.

You cannot save an image of C: drive, on C: drive, or for that matter F to F, G to G etc. This would defeat the purpose of creating an image. If it were possible to create an image of Root (C:) and store it on C:, when C: fails, the image goes with it, which is why it is not permissible in the first place.
