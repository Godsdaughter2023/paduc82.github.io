Well, it still makes no sense to insert a Section Break at the beginning of the 1st page... that totally superfluous & unnecessarily complicates the structure of the document :-) Additionally, a Section Break (Continuous) is not an effective means of varying H/F. bernie deitrick say:

You might be able to use dependent data validation - you need to be able to figure out the correct Data Validation named range from a previous entry.  For example, if cell O2 could contain

Men's T-Shirt

Boy's T-Shirt

Women's T-Shirt

Girl's T-Shirt

etc.

then the named ranges for the Brand list for each of those should be consistent and calculable:

Mens_T_Shirt_Brand

Boys_T_Shirt_Brand

Womens_T_Shirt_Brand

Girls_T_Shirt_Brand

In this case, "calculable" means apostrophes are removed and dashes and spaces are replaced with underscores, and the attribute is appended with an underscore.

Then your DV list for P2:Pxxx could use the formula (if Brand is in cell P1):

=INDIRECT(SUBSTITUTE(SUBSTITUTE(SUBSTITUTE(O2,"'",""),"-","_")," ","_")&"_"&P$1)
