1. uninstall WMDC 

2. Open the registry and navigate to:

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\WindowsNT\CurrentVersion

Look at the permissions make sure the local machine Administrators group AND the TrustedInstaller have FULL permissions.

3. Click on the advanced button and change the owner to the administrators group (you will need to be in this group)

4. Replace all child object permission entries with inheritable permissions (I got some error message here but never mind that)

5. Navigate to HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\WindowsNT\CurrentVersion\Svhost

6. Look at the permissions make sure the local machine Administrators group AND the TrustedInstaller have FULL permissions. (previously the trustedInstaller did not have permission to this folder for me)

7. Re-install WMDC

8. Plug in you Mobile device, and it should now fire up WMDC and connect

How do i allow bold and underline in fillable areas of a word - Microsoft Community

mikeyeze say:

How do i allow bold and underline in fillable areas of a word form?

I am looking to allow users to change the bold and underline in a editable text box, while preserving the uneditable text. I do not want to allow users to change my text in the locked area of the form just the fill in sections. Is this even possible?

jay freedman say:
The text entry form field that you can use in "legacy" protected forms does not support any formatting that isn't applied to the entire field.

If all your users have at least Word 2007, you can use content controls instead of legacy form fields, and in that case you can use a "rich text" content control that supports the same kind of formatting as in ordinary text. To lock the parts that are outside the content controls, select the whole document, click the Group button in the Controls section of the Developer tab, and click Group in the dropdown menu.

If a form using content controls is opened in Word 2003 (with the Compatibility Pack applied), the controls will be treated as ordinary text and the grouping/locking won't be supported. For those users, you'll have to stick with a legacy form, and you can't have formatting in the fields.
