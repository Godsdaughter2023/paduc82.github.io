Convert mobile OneNote 2010 to OneNote 2007 - Microsoft Community

konstintine say:

Convert mobile OneNote 2010 to OneNote 2007

I have a new HD7 with OneNote 2010 installed.  I am trying to email/save notebooks from phone to a computer that is running OneNote 2007.  I cannot find any information on how to convert the mobile 2010 version to the 2007 version installed on my laptop.
steve silverwood say:

On Thu, 20 Jan 2011 15:38:38 +0000, Konstintine wrote:

I have a new HD7 with OneNote 2010 installed.  I am trying to email/save notebooks from phone to a computer that is running OneNote 2007.  I cannot find any information on how to convert the mobile 2010 version to the 2007 version installed on my laptop.

First, check the version of Office you're using.  Some, such as Office
2010 Home and Student, provide a license that allows you to install it
on as many as three separate computers without purchasing additional
licenses.  That may give you the legal right to have it installed on,
say, your home system, work computer and laptop, all without having to
shell out the additional money for more licenses.

If that's not an option, you can convert your OneNote 2010 files to
2007 format using the following process:

1) Create a new notebook in OneNote 2010.
2) Right-click on the notebook, and select Properties.
3) Under the Properties window, click the "Convert to 2007" button.
4) Copy the data from your 2010 notebook over to the new 2007-format
notebook.

(An alternative to the above is to use Windows Explorer to go to your
My Documents folder, open the "OneNote Notebooks" folder and create a
COPY of the notebook you wish to convert, then open that notebook
again in OneNote and use the Properties window to convert that copy to
2007 format.  I prefer the other process to this one, but it's up to
you.)

Once you've done either of the above, you can then either make a copy
of the converted notebook and transfer it to the 2007 system, or you
can:

1) Select the notebook to be exported;
2) Click the File menu and select Save As;
3) Under "Save Current," select "Notebook;"
4) Under "Select Format," select "OneNote Package (*.onepkg)" and
choose the file name and destination;
5) Open that new .onepkg file in whatever other location you wish,
which will create a copy of the new notebook at that location using
the data from the .onepkg file.
Using the .onepkg format is also the handiest way to send an entire
notebook to someone, should you need to do so in the future.  It's
also handy if you have to archive an entire notebook and burn it to
DVD-R or CD-ROM media.

Hope that helps.

-- /Steve/

Synchronizing n900 with Google Mail, Calendar, and Contacts - Microsoft Community

drew_freyman say:

Synchronizing n900 with Google Mail, Calendar, and Contacts

I was unable to find any instructions on how to sync Google Mail, Calendar, and Contacts with my n900.  So, I tried using the instructions for the Symbian v3/v5 phones.  Two of three were successful.  One of the successes does have a catch.  So others don't have to spend the time I did experimenting here are the instructions for synchronizing Google Mail, Calendar, and Contacts with the n900 firmware v1.2009.42.11.

 

Gmail sync settings can be created on the n900 by either:

A.  Activating the mail wizard by clicking on the "email" icon for the first time.

or

B.   Within the "Settings" menu in the "Email" submenu press on the top status bar with "E-mail" pulldown and another submenu with a "New account" button will appear.

After you input your Gmail e-mail address most of the fields will populate themselves.  In fact, almost ALL the fields will automatically populate themselves (finally effortless mail!). Just in case, check these settings:

For incoming server: port 993

For outgoing server: port 465

 

Calendar AND contacts sync settings can be created by:

1st  Open settings menu.

2nd  Open "Email for Exchange" submenu.

3rd  Filling in the fields as:

a. Server: m.google.com

b. Port: 443

c. Secure connection: check yes

d. Conflict resolution: your choice

e. User name:  *** Email address is removed for privacy ***

f. Password: your password

g. Domain: leave blank

h. Synchronize e-mail:  leave unchecked.

i. Synchronize calendar and tasks: check yes.

k. Calendar:  N900 (to populate existing calendar) or create a new one

j. Synchronize calendar back:  your choice

k. Synchronize completed tasks:  your choice

l. Synchronize contacts:  check yes

m. First synchronization: your choice

A note of caution:  For this sync to work your calendar must be empty.  If you have already used the synchronization tool to transfer the calendar data from your old phone to your n900 it will not allow you to write the Google calendar data from the server into it. The n900 calendar will remain as a local, unsynchronized calendar and you must create another calendar for synchronization purposes.  However, I believe this is not the best way to go.  Instead, if you have already transferred your calendar data from your old phone open the n900's calendar, select all, and delete.  Then the Google server will synchronize its data with the N900 calendar.
