How do I set a permanent IP for my wireless printer?  Not very computer savvy

Two ways...

 

1 - On the front panel of the HP J4680 press the Tools key, ie. the small wrench. Press the right arrow key (next to the OK button) till you get the Wireless Menu. Press OK. Press the right arrow key until you see the IP Settings menu. Press OK. Press OK at the IP warning message then press the right arrow key until you see the Manual setting. Configure as required. Document your changes...

 

2 - On the front panel of the HP J4680 press the Tools key, ie. the small wrench. Press the left arrow key (next to the OK button) till you get the Print Report menu. Press OK. Press the left arrow key until you see Network Config. Press OK and the report will be printed. Note the IPv4 IP address reported on the report sheet. Open Internet Explorer or your favorite browser and type the address in the address window. When the printer web based admin page opens click on the Networking tab and configure as required. Document your changes...

 

Note your static IP address must be in the same range as assigned/used by your router but outside the range of addresses assigned by the router DHCP server.  For example I have my HP J4680 configured for 192.168.2.10 since my routers DHCP server assigns IP addresses starting at 192.168.2.33 going up. The gateway address is generally your router address, ie. 192.168.1.1 for example (router dependent), and the mask is 255.255.255.0. Get the DNS addresses from your router or your ISP if you set those otherwise keep it set for Automatic.

1. Navigate to the %systemroot%\Minidump folder.

2. Copy any and all DMP files in the Minidump folder to your Desktop and then zip up these files.

3. Upload the zip containing the .DMP files to Onedrive or a hosting site of your choice and paste in your reply. Prefered sites: Onedrive, Mediafire, Dropbox, etc. Nothing with wait-timers.
