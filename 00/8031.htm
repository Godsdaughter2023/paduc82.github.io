yosedesh say:

Is it possible to add Timezone in Outlook timestamp?


"...emails time-stamp displays the time it was received and in accordance with the time-zone of receiver."

https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/outlook_com/forum/oemail-osend/email-timestamp-what-does-it-mean/646a916c-d5fd-4e08-bb08-395d67d39b59

Is it possible to add the Timezone specification to the email timestamp?

For example, I sent an email earlier today at 8am Pacific Time.

However, when someone replied, their email made it seem as if I sent the email at 11am (since they are in Eastern Time).

Unfortunately, since there are various people in different timezones in the emails, it gets confusing after various replies (each with different timestamps without the timezone specification).  In the end, it may seem as if I sent the email at 11am when it was actually sent at 8am in my timezone.

Is it possible to standardize/default all Outlook email timestamp to display UTC standard time or include the timezone information?

Thank you in advance!

jennifer shi msft say:

Hi Yosedesh,

Greetings, based on my experience and test result, it will always show the time based on the recipient's local timezone setting and currently, there isn't an option to change the timestamp to to display UCT standard time in Outlook.

However, the timezone information is included in the mail header and the recipient can analysis the article, even the item is displayed at the recipient's local times of their calendar, it is saved in UTC.

Meanwhile, if you think the ability to set the time of the sent items is important and convenience for you, we recommend you share this idea with our development team by posting a new comment in activity, your feedback and comments are the motivation of the improvement of our products.

Looking forward to your understanding.
