a. user say:

Hello SagarAntala,

 

To save power, Surface Laptop uses Adaptive Hibernate. Instead of waiting a fixed amount of time to move from sleep to hibernation, Surface Laptop uses a predicted user presence service. If the prediction service indicates that the user is away, the device will hibernate without waiting a fixed amount of time. When the prediction service indicates that the user is expected to use the unit, it will wake the unit a few minutes prior. In normal usage, the customer gets the best of both worlds; an instant-on experience and great battery life.

 

Surface Laptop will also move from sleep to hibernate if it detects a 5% or greater battery drain over a 6-hour period.

This setting can be changed at Settings > Power & Sleep > Save me power when my device knows I’m away. If this setting is Off, Surface Laptop will only move from sleep to hibernation after a cumulative 6 hour period sleep session has elapsed (or) if a 5% battery drain occurred while in the sleep state – whichever happens first.
