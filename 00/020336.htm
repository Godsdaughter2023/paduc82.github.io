a. user say:

Application database project

I am creating a database for a a bank which gives out loans to clients and staff members and also includes a marketing department which distributes questionnaires to clients in order to receive feedback. Just looking for peoples opinions on relationships and wondering do they think I need a department table since I have a Personnel department and also marketing as well as the main department which distributes the loans. Appreciate a reply!

gina whipp say:
Well, I would combine Staff and Customers into one table and have a field to identify what type of Customer, i.e. CustomerTypeID.

scottgem say:

1) I'm guessing the Rejection File is a listing of rejected applications. That shouldn't exist. An applications should be in the Customers table. If they don't have a loan record then they were rejected.

2) A questionnaire structure is very specific and not what you have. The basic strucutre should be as follows:

Questions; QuestionID (PK Autonumber) Question

Respondent, this could be pulled from the Customers table

Response: ResponseID (PK Autonumber), RespondentID (FK), ResponseDate

Answers: AnswerID (PK Autonumber), QuestionID (FK), ResponseID (FK), Answer

3) Yes you should have a department table.

a. user say:

So in terms of just the loans your suggestion makes sense but I feel for this system it needs to keep customers and staff records separate. I appreciate the reply however!

gina whipp say:
