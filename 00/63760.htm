peterbaenziger say:

Found the solution.


First off, the obvious way does not work. Restarting the PC in safe mode (SHIFT-restart) fails - the restart menu does not allow any other choices than to run Windows normally, because it lacks the space to display the others, and the cursors can only be used to select visible options on the screen.


I did not try connecting another monitor which I assume would have allowed me to solve the issue from that monitor (use Extend, not Duplicate).


I was about to try starting with a USB recovery stick when I found another way that worked. When launching the desktop (Windows+D) and holding the tablet in landscape mode, the screen is all purple (your color may vary). However, in portrait mode the screen was all purple, same as in landscape mode, but unlike landscape mode, there was a small stripe at the bottom where the desktop was visible. (Before that I changed the orientation while on the Start Screen a few times and fiddled with the rotation lock - the stripe may always be there in portrait mode, or it may have to do with entering the desktop in landscape first and then changing to portrait).


That stripe of the desktop allowed to long-tap, on the desktop (not the taskbar) then scroll down the context menu and select "Screen Resolution". Then scroll around the window and find the entry for Resolution (240x320). If you click it a pop up opens which can not be dragged and fills the whole screen, including the formerly purple area. Pick the highest possible resolution in the visible area (1280x720). To close this part you need a keyboard and hit enter. Keep in mind the keyboard cover of the Surface deactivates while in portrait mode so you may need to turn to landscape, where the screen disappears again, press enter, then turn the tablet into portrait again for the next step. Then, in portrait mode, scroll down all the way (you may need to use the scrollbarbar on the right) and press "Ok".


After that, the resolution changes to something usable and you have 15 seconds to confirm in a dialog on the desktop that you want to keep it that way. Confirm. Then change the resolution again, this time to 1920x1080.
