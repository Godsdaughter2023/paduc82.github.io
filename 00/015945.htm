Most easily described with example:

At the left is the data. An individual's name in one column, the company they work for in second column.
The left two columns in the table at right is a simple pivot--a count of all the values for each of the companies.

This simply count is without regard to uniqueness. What I want is: how many (unique) people are in company ABC.
I need to do this without knowing ahead of time who the people are or who the companies are.

The 3rd column in the table at the right show the 'correct' answers to the question.
In plain English, the question is "count the number of unique values in col A associated with each unique value in col B".

My mental approach is sort of 'loop' oriented--first get an array of unique values in col B (i.e., the company names), the pass this array as an argument to a second process that loops through the values in the array, counting the unique values in col A for each matching value it was passed in the array.

I can certainly do this through Advanced Filter->Unique Records Only into another location, but that's not a dynamic, automatically-updated solution and prone to failure/error. So, this needs to be done dynamically on the raw data--preferably no macros, etc. A calculated column in a pivot seems the way to go.

Suggestions?
