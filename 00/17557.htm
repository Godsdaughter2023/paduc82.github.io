error calling method on NPObject

When a plugin crashes, content script may still have a reference to JS objects provided by that plugin. keratl say:

How many options do you have in the first column? Over 1024?

My suggestion would be to sort your data set based on the first column (either manually, or with a macro depending on how often this data is updated)

Then to get a dependent DV, use a named range, something like the following; I'll build it out a step at a time to reduce the chances of aircode errors and so you can see the logic and fix any errors more easily:

Let's assume that your data is in columns A and B, and we use C1 as your existing DV cell and C2,C3,C4,etc as temporary helper cells to build out the formulas (eventually you won't need that because the formula will move into your DV)

C2=match(C1,A:A,False) gives you the first row on which the selected value is found)

C3=countif(A:A,C1) tells you how many cells match the selected value

      note: if your actual data range is thousands of cells, there is a more complicated way to write this formula that will return results faster, but I won't combine different answers in this post as it might cause confusion)

So, the actual range you want in your DV is

=offset(B1,C2-1,0,C3,1)

the -1 is necessary because we are offsetting from row 1, so if you offset by the matched value in C2 your start point will be one row lower than where your data actually starts.

you can replace the cell references with their source formulas and get

=offset(B1,match(C1,A:A,False)-1,0,countif(A:A,C1),1)

and replace C1 with a reference to your initial cell where people select a value from column A. Then push this all into a named range, and use that as your DV source for your second dependent range.

Note: "I have too many options in the first columns"  - in your first DV cell, are you providing all of the options from column A including duplicates, or are you de-duping and sorting the options so that your users can easily select what they need? If you are already de-duping and still have too many options, you might even consider adding a third DV (to the start of your process) where the user just selects EU, AM, AS, etc., then only show them the values from your first list based on that selection- just a thought.
