jay freedman say:

In Word (2013 and 2016), all the built-in Heading styles automatically offer collapse/expand capability. This is based on the Outline Level assigned by default to the Heading styles. When the mouse pointer hovers over a paragraph with one of these styles, a small triangle appears in the left margin. Clicking that triangle collapses or expands everything below that paragraph until the next occurrence of the same level. The right-click menu of a collapsible paragraph offers an Expand/Collapse submenu.

Working from this, you have two alternatives:

  • In your document, modify the font and paragraph formatting of the Heading styles to look like ordinary (Normal style) text. Apply Heading 1 style to each paragraph at the outermost level, Heading 2 to the next level, and so forth.
  • Create new custom styles based on the Normal style, and modify each one to have the desired outline level: In the Modify Style dialog, click the Format button at the bottom, click Paragraph, and change the Outline Level dropdown in the Paragraph dialog to Level 1, Level 2, etc. You'll probably also want to change the Left Indent setting.

f. Exit and then restart Internet Explorer.
g. If issues do not reoccur, repeat steps a through c.
h. Click Enable for a single add-on.
i. Repeat steps f through h until you determine which add-on causes errors to occur.
