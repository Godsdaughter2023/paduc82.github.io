steve rindsberg say:

I would create the titles in PowerPoint rather than incorporating them into the photos themselves, if that's an option.

For computer/tablet use, the photos don't need to be much larger (measured in pixels) than the resolution of the computer/tablet's display and depending on the photos, can be smaller.

For example, suppose my computer has a 1280x1024 display.  Ideally, for full-screen photos, I'd want the photo resolution to be the same ... 1280x1024 pixels.  Photos that aren't going to be full screen can be proportionally smaller.

But I've found that in most cases, I can make the photos considerably smaller w/o suffering a huge quality loss (and in return, the presentation itself is smaller and each slide loads faster).   I'd probably run some tests with sample images at 1024 pixels wide rather than 1280.   

Incidentally, for this kind of thing I use a free program called IrfanView (www.irfanview.com) that lets me convert whole batches of images at a time.  With a bit of sharpening, even smaller than ideal images look quite good.  And by adjusting the quality settings down from 100% to low 90's, you can get considerably smaller images.

And again, the important thing is the image dimensions in pixels; the size in KB is not exactly random, but for JPGs, it's not directly tied to the image resolution.  However, I'd stick with JPGs (they'll be the smallest and will give you the smallest PPT files).  

As long as you know that the size in pixels is correct, don't worry about the size the images come into PPT;  grab the corner of the image and enlarge it to what you want.
