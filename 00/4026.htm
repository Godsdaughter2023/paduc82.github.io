jeeped say:

... I can't for the life of me find how to determine where the user has clicked on the screen ...


In Word VBA, the current cursor position or insertion point can be referred to as Selection and you do not need to assign it to a variable to use it.

Sub InsertAtCursor()
     Dim strTextToInsert As String
     strTextToInsert = "this is a test"
     Selection.TypeText Text:=strTextToInsert
End Sub

If you need the variable assignment to the current cursor position for some reason in a more complicate macro, then you need to Dim discRange As Selection, not as Range.

Sub InsertAtCursor()
     Dim strTextToInsert As String, discRange As Selection
     strTextToInsert = "this is a test"
     Set discRange = Selection
     discRange.TypeText Text:=strTextToInsert
End Sub

Here's a macro which places various things in a footer and header.

Sub Stuff_In_Footer()
With ActiveSheet.PageSetup
    .LeftFooter = _
    ThisWorkbook.BuiltinDocumentProperties("Title") & " " & _
    ThisWorkbook.BuiltinDocumentProperties("Subject") & " " & _
    ThisWorkbook.BuiltinDocumentProperties("Author") & " " & _
    Format(ThisWorkbook.BuiltinDocumentProperties("Creation Date"), _

    .LeftHeader = ThisWorkbook.CustomDocumentProperties("Client")
End With
End Sub

arnie est say:

Hello suraj bhim bhansingh,

As for your concern, you may try the following steps below:

  1. Go to account.microsoft.com/devices. If you're prompted to sign in, use the same Microsoft account you used to sign in on your phone.

  2. Choose the phone you want to erase, and then click Find My Phone.

  3. Click Erase.

  4. If you're absolutely, positively sure, select the I'm ready to erase my phone check box, and then click Erase.

Note:

It's a good idea to let your mobile operator know that you've lost your phone. If you have credit cards on your lost phone, contact your bank to report them as lost.

Hope this helps.

jason (office engineer) say:

The good news is that we haven't yet found a case where a Sway actually disappeared.  Most cases turned out to be that the Sway was actually created under another account and the user was not aware.  Can you try signing into Sway with every Microsoft and Office 365 account you have to see if the Sway shows up?  Sway will automatically use the credentials of who you were last signed in as and sometimes that catches people off guard.
