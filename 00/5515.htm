ronrosenfeld say:

Without seeing how your data is set up, it is difficult to know how to advise you.

I would suggest uploading a workbook to a public site (e.g. SkyDrive) and then posting a link here.

 

If a date is counted just once each time it is entered, then the COUNTIFS formula I provided earlier can be used.

 

If a date may be counted 1 or more times, depending on the contents of an adjacent cell, then the SUMIFS function should be used, where the criteria ranges are the dates, and the sum range is the adjacent cell.

 

I don't think you want to translate the full date to a month, unless you are wanting to count monthly occurrences without regard to the year.  If that is the case, the solution is different.  Let us know.  Something like Andrews formula might be appropriate.

E3:  =SUMIFS($W$2:$AF$2,$W$3:$AF$3,">"&EOMONTH(E$1,-1),$W$3:$AF$3,"<=" & EOMONTH(E$1,0))

 

This assumes that the dates in row 1 are "real dates" and not merely text strings.

This also assumes that the contents of W2:AF2 are relevant and will not necessarily be one's.
