Windows 10 free version - Microsoft Community

camysiahorne say:

Windows 10 free version

I need to do a factory reset on my desktop pc. I have the free version of Windows 10. Is there a way to reinstall it after the reset?

cbarnhorst say:

Yes.  Just reinstall Windows 10 same edition and when you reach the Product Key request note the line at the top of the window.  Since you are reinstalling, simply click on the metalink at the bottom that says, "I don't have a product key" and the installation will proceed.  When the computer first connects to the internet Windows will activate.

Explanation:  Your computer is permanently registered as a Windows 10 device for the edition you first installed and you may reinstall Windows 10 as often and by whatever means you wish without need of a product key.  The licensing software sends the computer's unique identifiers to the Microsoft licensing servers and when they find the registration the servers send back an affirmative which allows Windows to activate or remain activated.  The registration was done when you upgraded to Windows 10.  Windows 10 activation is provided by the computer (called "digital entitlement") rather than by entry of a product key.  It is good for the life of the computer (i.e., motherboard).  Even if you reformat and load Ubuntu or whatever, whenever you return to Windows 10 it will activate because activation has nothing to with the hard drive.  This big change is an anti-piracy measure.  If there is nothing in the software to hack then the hackers cannot steal Windows without stealing the whole device.

camysiahorne say: Thank you so much!  But I have one last question... Once the factory reset is complete, will the pc automatically prompt me to install Windows 10? If not, how do I get there?

cbarnhorst say:

No.  You have to start that yourself because the deadline for the first upgrade to Windows 10 for free is past.  If the factory reset installs Windows 7 sp1 or Windows 8.1 simply go to the Media Creation Tool page at

https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/software-download/windows10

And click the Update Now link.  That will force the upgrade to Windows 10.  Since you took advantage of the offer of the free update before July 29th of this year you don't have to worry about deadlines in doing this.  Its just that the upgrade is not automatic now like it was before.

The licensing software will behave as described above.
