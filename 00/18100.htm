stephenpsa say:

Whoever selected the components of your system, knew what he/she was doing. All top notch stuff such as I would choose myself. And I am pretty choosy when it comes to these things. I will make the minor remark that I am an Intel user myself, but I have had/work with AMD's, and I never had an issue resulting from that. So while I cannot say for certain, I will say that it is extremely unlikely that your AMD Phenom has compatibility issues which could explain your long upgrade time. 

That external HD is almost certainly the cause of the issue. So much so that I see no point in exploring other possibilities until we've eliminated it. It could be some other (external) hardware attached to your system, so just before the next upgrade, simply disconnect (power off should be enough but is not guaranteed to be enough) all but KB and Mouse. Also, unplug any SD-Card and USB-Memory sticks. I will await the result of this before searching any deeper for a cause.

You also asked about whether you would benefit from buying an SSD and whether that might cause issues with Activation and/or being able to run Insider builds. The latter is easily enough to answer: No, it would not be a problem at all. But your OS license  is linked to your hardware (including the type/nr of your HD), but the main factor in this is your MB. Change that and the matter becomes more complicated. You will find plenty of places where is says:

      New Motherboard? You will need a new license!

But that only part of the story. It will depend on what kind of license you have. Roughly put, there are two types: OEM *) Licenses which come with a particular device and standalone licenses that you can buy separately from hardware. If you have the latter, which is likely, given your machine, than you can replace everything without any Licensing issues but you will need to take some action to transfer that License and once transferred, you cannot use it any longer on the old machine.

So try to find out which kind of License you have and if you find it's an OEM License, then expect to have to dish out some $100 (for Home) to $250 (for Pro) if ever you decide replace your PC.

As for would you gain much by buying an SSD? Well, that one is a bit tricky to answer: I'd say not much in performance but there is the fact that an SSD does not make any sound, which, depending on your work environment, is maybe more important than you might think. For that, and the fact that there are practical advantages of having one physical device to run your OS of and another for bulk data storage and backups. If you do decide to bay an SSD, do take care in your choice. Let me know if I can be of any help with that.

Regards, and unless you have any more questions, I will await the result of 'test One'...

Stephen

*) OEM = Original Equipment Manufacturer, OEM licenses cannot be bought by the general public.
