"Tile Icons" instead of "Mini Screen-Shot" - Microsoft Community

paulcrivell say:

"Tile Icons" instead of "Mini Screen-Shot" representation for open apps in Modern (Metro/Tile/Mobile) Interface

My understanding is that Microsoft is considering eliminating the open app listing on the left side of the display within "Modern" (Metro/Tile/Mobile) interface/environment.

I believe this aspect of the UI should be maintained, but recommend that open apps are represented by their "Tile Icons" instead of a "mini screen-shots".

These mini screen-shots do not represent consistently and it is difficult to identify which app each mini screen-shot represents even on a relatively large display.  It would be a functional and aesthetic improvement to represent each open app with a uniformly or proportionally sized box showing a "tile icon" for each open app (open app tile icons could be same size, location, and function as current "mini screen shots").  This would allow the user to more easily (without eye strain) and quickly identify which apps are open.

I understand that there could be two instances of an open app, and that the open app icon boxes would look identical (i.e., both represented by an identical tile icon), but I do not see this as a negative when one considers that currently, it is difficult to identify which apps are open and ascertain which instance of an open app is represented with which mini screen-shot when multiple instances of an app are open.    

I believe Microsoft nailed the ergonomics with charms on the right and open apps on left with Windows 8 for touch and then for both touch and mouse and keyboard with Windows 8.1.   After spending a little time using the UI, its superior functionality and user experience really shine.

After becoming familiar with and using Windows 8.1 modern interface, I find the user interface on an iPad frustrating.  As an example, it is so much easier to adjust the screen brightness with Windows 8.1  and to switch apps.  The UX on Windows 8.1 is fast and fluid.

In summary, don't throw the baby out with the bathwater.  Just give the baby a bath.

Hope this reaches the right folks at MSFT for serious consideration.

Paul

garym123 say:

Restore works and phone backup works.

You only get one chance to restore your phone. This is when the phone asks for setup information during the initial startup after a phone reset or when a new phone starts for the first time. These are the times when your microsoft account has to be entered, the account you backed up to.

 

You have two options, reset your phone and enter the account information on initial startup. You have to have a good data connection for restore. Not WiFi

Or go to windowsphone.com and reinstall your apps from 'purchase history'

All your email should still be at outlook.com.

Either way you can not get app data back

 

What was backed up is your app list, SMS and phone settings.

Your photos, documents, music and videos should have been backed up to your PC or Skydrive. They are not included in your online phone backup.
