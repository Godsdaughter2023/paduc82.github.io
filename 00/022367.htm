How do I get the last sentence of a paragraph to justify - Microsoft Community

annesommers say:

How do I get the last sentence of a paragraph to justify

My paragraphs are justified. Usually it works. Sometimes, however, the words in the last line spread out. How do I stop this?

jay freedman say:
If you're using the Ctrl+J shortcut to change the paragraph alignment from Left to Justified, be careful not to press Ctrl+Shift+J. That shortcut applies Distributed alignment, which justifies the last line as well as the rest of the paragraph but adds huge spaces between the letters.

If you get a Distributed paragraph by accident (or from somewhere else), click in the paragraph and press Ctrl+J to make it Justified.

Another way to avoid the whole problem is to define or modify a paragraph style to include Justified alignment, and always apply the style instead of applying the alignment separately. Note that the Alignment dropdown in the Paragraph dialog doesn't offer Distributed as a choice.
