You can probably use a macro containing the following code.  There is no wdMagenta color however.

Dim arev As Revision
With ActiveDocument
   For Each arev In .Revisions
       With arev.Range.Font
           .Italic = True
           .ColorIndex = wdDarkRed
       End With
   Next arev
End With

john korchok say:

If adding a comment disrupts the layout too much, try adding a callout. These are text boxes with handles that can point to specific text or graphics. Since they float above the document, they don't disturb the layout:

  1. In Word, click on the Insert tab (not the Insert menu at the top of your screen).
  2. Click on Shapes, then scroll down to the bottom.
  3. Choose a Callout shape, then click and drag on your document to draw it. After drawing it, you can click on the handle or extension and move that independently to point exactly where you want it.

The first time you create a callout, it will wrap text around it by default. It will also have a colored fill. To fix these:

  1. With the callout selected, click on the Shape Format tab, then click on Wrap Text and choose In Front of Text.
  2. Still on the Shape Format tab, click on Shape Fill and choose white.
  3. Right-click on the callout and choose Set as Default Shape. Now all callouts will not wrap text and will have a white fill.
