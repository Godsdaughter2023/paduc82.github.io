toby ornott say:

Albert06,

 

This will clear up everything.

 

Go into Outlook's mail preferences settings and locate Rules*.  Then set the following rule in Step 1:

(a) In the first box, select "sender's address"

(b) In the second box, select "DOES NOT CONTAIN"

(c) In the third (and final) box, insert the symbol "@" (without quotes).

 

Your final rule will read: "sender's address DOES NOT CONTAIN @"

 

 In Step 2, direct everything to go to either a pre-dump folder**, junk, or trash.


Also, monitor your Safe box contacts and Block Senders contacts and begin blocking everything that appears in your Inbox which is not caught by the above rule.

 

Another Outlook feature which you will find to be beneficial is the "Instant Action".  Instant actions appear beside sender names and subject lines in your message list.*** They make it so you don't have to open a message to take action on it.  They also allow an undesirable sender's address to be blocked instantly.  Create an instant action for "Junk".  Then when something unwanted appears in your Inbox, click the instant action icon and proceed to Agree to have the sender blocked etcetera. 

 

Notes:


*Rules are located in this manner: (a) Click the gear-icon top right corner of the Outlook screen (b) select "more mail options", (c) locate "rules for sorting new messages" in the Options main menu.

 

**Creating a pre-dump folder allows you to monitor the activity.

 

***Instant Actions are located on the Mail Options main menu page beneath the "Rules for sorting...."
