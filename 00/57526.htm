shenan stanley say:

In other words, a dual processor CPU (he says) has only one half of the CPU active and the other is not automatically turned on and must be manually activated.

Are you speaking of two physical processors or a single processor with multiple cores?  There is a distinction - and it is an important one.
 
Given your second paragraph , it leads me to believe you are speaking of a dual core processor and not dual processors.
 
Windows XP Home supports and automatically adjusts for a single multiple core processor.  Windows XP Home does not support more than one processor, however.  Multiple cores - yes.  Multiple processors - no.
 
Windows XP Professional supports and automatically adjusts for up to two physical processors and multiple cores.  Windows XP Professional does not support more than two physical processors.  Two processors - yes.  Multiple cores per processor - yes.
 
Windows 7 has similar limitations per edition of Windows 7.

mnewsome say:

Sound in Safe Mode

Noted that audio service does not automatically run in safe mode.  Audio Service does not show in the list of services.  Could it be listed under another name?  Can audio services be enabled in safe mode?  Guidance sought
arya s asok say:

Hi,

 

  • Are you able to hear the sound and audio when you boot in normal mode?

 

When you boot the computer in safe mode most of the services would be disabled automatically. By default audio service will be disabled in safe mode and this is by design. There is no way you can enable the audio service or hear sound when you boot the computer in safe mode.

 

Windows Audio service will be enabled and started only when you boot the computer in normal mode.

 

You may boot your computer in normal mode and check if you can find Windows Audio service in services windows.
