If the dates are in the past:

=DATEDIF(A1,TODAY(),"y")>=2

works

 

If the dates are in the future

=DATEDIF(TODAY(),B1,"y")>=2

And a few other variants, all of which did not work.

scott mcdaniel say:

You shouldn't use the ControlSource to do this, because it won't store anything. Instead, set the ControlSource of your textbox to the table column where you want the data to be saved, and then do this in the AfterUpdate event of your Combo:

Me.YourTextbox  = Me.YourCombo.Column(1)

Remember too that the Column collection of a combo is zero-based, so Column(0) is the first column, Column(1) is the second, and so forth.
