cmdrkeene say:

When you use two fingers, the system basically interprets it the same as if you had used a mouse with a scroll wheel.

So in this case, you can open your Mouse Properties and adjust the scrolling speed for that scroll wheel, and it will affect your speed when using two fingers also.

One quick way to reach those options is to do this:

1) Right-click Start button and choose Run

2) Type main.cpl and click OK

3) Go to the Wheel tab at the top and adjust the speed.

If the company that built your laptop included more touchpad software than just the basics, you might also be able to adjust those options in the touchpad-specific options. Those are sometimes integrated into the same mouse properties you open with the 3 steps above, but it can also be in a totally separate app provided by the manufacturer.

You can also adjust your scrolling speed from the Settings app:

1) Open Settings app, click Devices

2) Choose Mouse

3) Adjust the scrolling speed.

The drag operation for a file is always to move (if dragging to another folder on the same drive).  The fact that it's an EXE makes no difference at all, EXE has never had it's own special drag action in any version of Windows ever.

burrwalnut say:

Rotating the screen image is a feature of the graphics card and is normally caused by accidentally pressing a combination of keys. To correct it, hold down Ctrl and Alt and press one of the four arrow keys (up, down, left or right) until you have it the correct way up.

 

Alternatively, you probably have a Rotation setting in the display properties of the graphics card.
