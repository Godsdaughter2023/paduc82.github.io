Hi Mary,

Most likely you are logged into a Temporary User Profile . ..

Before you do anything or move any files around

Restart (not shut down) your PC 3 or 4 times in a row, this will fix this issue a lot of the time . ..

On Thu, 13 Jun 2013 14:45:36 +0000, pashya30 wrote:

I m using HP430 (i core 3) laptop with win7 64bit professional installed.

recently I ve upgraded to 8gb ram, however i dont see too much system performence issue.

That's no surprise. How much RAM you need for good performance is
not a one-size-fits-all situation, and 8GB is more than most people
can make effective use of. You get good performance if the amount of
RAM you have keeps you from using the page file significantly, and
that depends on what apps you run. Most people running a typical range
of business applications under Windows 8 find that somewhere around
2-4GB works well, others need more. Almost anyone will see poor
performance with less than 2GB. Some people, particularly those doing
things like editing large photographic images, can see a performance
boost by adding even more than 4GB--sometimes much more.
