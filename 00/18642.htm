Slow Word 2013

I experienced a similar issue with slow typing speed in Office 365 Word. I edit a substantial number of Word and Excel documents for work and found the SkyDrive storage incredibly beneficial for working remotely.

I realized though that documents which are being edited from Skydrive, not a local copy, experience the slow typing speed issue.

The fix:   From word:  File, Options. Word Options has an advanced tab on the left labeled 'advanced'. About 3/4 of the way down, a category for 'Save' options exists. The default setting has 'Allow Background Saves' checked. I uncheck this option and instead check 'Always create backup copy' and 'Copy remotely stored files onto your computer, and update the remote file when saving'.

I assume that if you're storing the files on Skydrive, or Dropbox, or web, or maybe even some external drive or flash memory this background save could be lagging and result in typing slowness. Either way, I changed the advanced options, and no more problem. Maybe it'll work for you.

Excel didn't have the option, and I usually save a local copy anyways due to the working file size.

tom van stiphout say:

Drop the parentheses:

DoCmd.OpenForm "QuantATRSum",...

That said, this may still not work. The classic solution to your dilemma is to have the subform on the form, but without a recordsource. Then set the recordsource in Form_Load.

Me.mySubformControl.Form.RecordSource = "myqueryreferencingparentform"

That said, you may have another error. It is VERY rare for a subform to require anything from the parent form other than the PK, which is already handed to it via LinkMasterFields/LinkChildFields properties, which act to automatically filter the subform to the records for the current parent record.

geoffchappell say:

Now that I understand a little more about MAPI, I see a tolerable workaround.

The Background:

You have the Mail options in Outlook 2016 set to "Never send a read receipt" or to "Ask each time whether to send a read receipt". A spammer sends a message that requests a read receipt. You use Outlook's Send/Receive feature to download only headers for inspection. You see this message's header and mark it for deletion. You click Send/Receive again to delete the message at the server. It's now be gone without its contents having ever been a potential problem at your computer.

The Problem:

Outlook generates a "Not read" receipt, telling the spammer that the message was deleted without being read. Outlook doesn't ask. Neither does it show the receipt in the Outbox as a message that's waiting to be sent. On your next Send/Receive, Outlook just sends the receipt, confirming to the spammer that your account is active despite what you might think have been your best attempts to have Outlook guard your privacy.

The Workaround:

At the first Send/Receive, don't just mark the header for deletion. First, mark it as read. (If you have the "ask" option set, Outlook will tell you the spammer wants a read receipt and ask whether you assent.) Then, mark the header for deletion.

The Fix?

It's not credible that Microsoft intends that Outlook generate a "Not read" receipt in this circumstance. Nobody who sets this Tracking option to defeat the sending of read receipts is going to think they're still open to sending non-read receipts. Presumably, Microsoft will acknowledge the above behaviour as caused by some coding oversight, and will fix it (if it's not fixed already) in some update.

Or not. First, it doesn't seem much of a stretch to say that hiding the receipts (instead of showing them in the Outbox) is just asking for errors in relevant code to go unnoticed. Worse, the hiding appears to be very much intended. See, for instance, Microsoft's documentation of the ReadReceipt method (https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/office/client-developer/outlook/mapi/imapisupport-readreceipt): "Storing read and nonread reports in hidden folders enables you to implement tighter security." Really? Security? Whose? Not mine: I feel that Outlook has not so much tightened my security as abused it.

Second, Microsoft seems to have been content to let problems with "read and nonread reports" go unattended for many years. You don't have to search the Internet far and wide to find complaints around this issue going back as far as 2007. This long thread from Microsoft's own site, https://social.msdn.microsoft.com/Forums/en-US/82024df4-d5ec-4f89-b268-f824dc26c370/outlook-2007-imap-bug?forum=innovateonoffice, is certainly not alone from the time. Not all old complaints about this issue will be the same, and some may be the "old bug" that Diane refers to above (which presumably was thought to have been fixed). But in the decade since, there has clearly been more going on than an occasional "stuck" receipt and there clearly has long been "read receipt" behaviour that users have found very unwelcome but which has not got fixed. I might hope for a fix now, but I won't hold my breath: the subject looks like it's had deep and lengthy neglect.
