tim liu msft say:
Hi Beth,
 
I'd like to firstly clarify the following information for you:
1.Currently we have two sync clients of OneDrive for Business.
Legacy OneDrive for Business sync client
This sync client is included in Office suite or a stand-alone version. It is Groove.exe. We can use it to sync SharePoint Online team site libraries and SharePoint server content.
The new sync client of OneDrive for Business
We use this new sync client to sync our SharePoint Online OneDrive for Business libraries and consumer OneDrive content. It is OneDrive.exe.
To identify which sync client we are using, see this article.
 
 
2.The error message which says you use the most recent version.
It seems at first you use the legacy sync client to sync your OneDrive for Business library. After it stops syncing, you download the new sync client. Windows 10 already have this sync client in its system, so you get the error message. If your OneDrive for Business library is in SharePoint Online, you may follow Method 2 in this article to sync it with the new sync client. On the other hand, if your OneDrive for Business library is in on-premises SharePoint server, for the “incorrect version error message”, we suggest you post a new thread in our SharePoint Server TechNet forum for further support. It’s the specific channel that handles SharePoint server related issues and questions.
 
3.The error message when you enter local path to the legacy sync client.
We cannot use OneDrive for Business sync client to sync a local drive path to cloud. So, if we click Sync a new library>enter a local drive path>Sync Now, we will get an error message.
