Having quickly googled, and looked at the link you mentioned in your original post, a more efficient way might be to use the following formula on an empty column in your File1 sheet (in row 1):

=IF(ISERROR(MATCH(M1,[book2]Sheet1!$A$1:$A$2000,0)),"xx",M1)

  8. Legacy Form Fields must be in a protected document to work correctly. Choose Developer>Restrict Editing.
  9. Under Editing restrictions, check Allow only this type of editing in the document.
  10. In the dropdown below, choose Filling in forms

    yves dhondt say:

    How do you perform the splitting operation? If you use the "Split Cells" button on the "Table Layout" tab, you should get a popup asking how many rows and columns you want.

    If your data consists of 3 paragraphs and you choose 3 columns and 1 row, your data will be split over the 3 columns. If you choose to select 1 column and 3 rows, the data will remain in the top most row and the two other rows will be empty.
