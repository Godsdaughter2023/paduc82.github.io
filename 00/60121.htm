tryx3 say:

This sounds like you have told Windows Media Player to look at the external drive and it's now complaining that you've disconnected it.  In other words, you did not copy the music to your hard drive, you told WMP that your external hard drive was the place to look for music.  You then disconnected your hard drive leaving WMP very confused.  In the instructions that follow, please remember that WMP contains no music files itself - it is just a set of links to the folders where you have put your files [WMP is really just an index].

To sort this out is fairly easy so don't worry.  It can take some time so make a pot of tea.

1  Close WMP

2  Connect the external hard drive

3  Open Windows explorer by clicking on the shortcut within the Start menu

4  Get to your user files by clicking on:  Computer, then the C:\ drive, then C:\Users folder you see near the bottom of the list, then click on your user name so that the right-hand pane of the Windows explorer window is showing all your personal folders, then select the one called My music

5  Open another Windows explorer window by clicking on the shortcut within the Start menu again

6  Go to the folders on your external hard drive that contain the music you want on your PC. Select the highest-level folder containing the music & press ctrl c to copy it [or select each folder separately if they are spread around then repeat this & the step 7 until you've done them all].

7  Go back to the first Windows explorer that you left selected at My music.  Press ctrl V to paste the copied folder & its contents into the My music folder.

8  Disconnect the external hard drive.

9  Open WMP, click on Organise in the upper left of the window, then click on Manage libraries, then click on Music.  This will show you the folders that WMP looks in to find music.  My music is probably already there [and WMP will be searching for files in it as you are looking - this will slow you down so drink the tea you just made].  You will probably find that the external hard drive is there as well.  You can select it and delete it [you are just deleting the instruction to look in it - you are not deleting any files].  If My music is not there then click on the browse button & find it then select it.  When you've checked it all then press OK.  WMP may start searching again and that will slow you down again.  Have another cup of tea.  Then press alt T to get the Tools menu visible, then click on Advanced and then on Restore media library.  This will ensure that WMP lists just those tracks you have told it to look for [in the My music folder] and deletes the rest from its library.  This will take quite some time so have a look in the pot to see if there's any tea left.

10 When WMP has finished sorting itself out, you can listen to some music while you make another pot of tea.

RECREATING_BCD_FROM_SCRATCH:
When I booted with Windows Install disk, and went into Repair Computer Mode, Command Prompt.
Found that System Reserved Partition was on C: drive.
And my Windows Install was on E: drive.

So I followed the steps below to recreate BCD in my system Reserved Partition in C drive.
These are the steps I did, since the import command in the links above didnt work, followed Nathan Brown's suggestion).
bcdedit /createstore C:\Boot\BCD
bcdedit.exe /store C:\Boot\BCD /create {bootmgr} /d "Windows Boot Manager"
bcdedit.exe /store C:\Boot\BCD /set {bootmgr} device partition=C:
bcdedit.exe /store C:\Boot\BCD  /timeout 10

bcdedit.exe /create /d "Microsoft Windows" /application osloader
bcdedit.exe should return a message with a GUID for the newly-created entry, something like this:
The entry {c0dfc4fa-cb21-11dc-81bf-005056c00008} was successfully created.
In my case, Windows was installed on E drive.
bcdedit.exe /store C:\Boot\BCD /set {c0dfc4fa-cb21-11dc-81bf-005056c00008} device partition=E:
bcdedit.exe /store C:\Boot\BCD  /set {c0dfc4fa-cb21-11dc-81bf-005056c00008} osdevice partition=E:
bcdedit.exe /store C:\Boot\BCD  /set {c0dfc4fa-cb21-11dc-81bf-005056c00008} path \Windows\system32\winload.exe
bcdedit.exe  /store C:\Boot\BCD  /set {c0dfc4fa-cb21-11dc-81bf-005056c00008} systemroot \Windows

And, last of all, tell the bootmgr bootloader to list the new entry or else it’ll remain hidden:
bcdedit.exe /displayorder {c0dfc4fa-cb21-11dc-81bf-005056c00008}
