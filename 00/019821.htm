jay freedman say:

Windows and Office support thirteen (!) different variants of English, each with a different numeric Locale ID value (listed here). The plain text AutoCorrect entries are stored in files whose names are MSOnnnn.ACL, where the nnnn is the Locale ID for the specific language.

If some parts of your document are marked as English (US) or English (UK), and other parts are in English (New Zealand), the AutoCorrect entries that you added while in one part would not be available while in another part with a different variant.

The first step is to select the whole document (Ctrl+A) and apply your preferred English variant to everything in the selection (Review > Language > Set Proofing Language). Then check the AutoCorrect list, and reenter any entries that are "missing" (i.e., only in the other variant's list). In the same dialog, it's a good idea to clear the check box for "Detect language automatically" because that feature is unreliable and can change the selection's language without warning.
