¡firedog say:

I followed your instructions and have begun downloading my Hotmail account to my Windows Live Mail  ... I have been able to download only 456 MB. 

The problem may be that there are throttles in place to ease the load on the servers as many thousands of users are going through a similar procedure. When I added a new Outlook.com IMAP account, I noticed that the message download eventually slowed down to a crawl or stalled completely after a few thousand messages. This is how I dealt with it:

  1. Open the send/receive progress window by clicking once on the status message Checking email ...
  2. Click Stop to halt the sync.
  3. Close the progress window, then close down Windows Live Mail.
  4. Wait half an hour and then re-launch.
  5. Click Send/Receive if it doesn't happen automatically.
  6. Repeat the stop/close/re-launch cycle as often as necessary to complete the download.

 

It was frustrating while this was going on, but I got there in the end. It looks as if the throttle is eased some time after a session is ended. I don't know if half-an-hour between sessions is excessive, so you might try with, say, 15 minutes or even 10. If the download gets going at full speed with a shorter interim, well and good.

 

This only happens with the initial sync; once all the old stuff is downloaded and in place, new messages come in as usual. If you have a lot of folders, you might also consider whether you need to sync them all every time; this can take a long time. The setting is on the IMAP tab of the account's properties dialogue.

vanessa yar say:

Hi,

Yes, it is ticked by default. This allows your computer to connect to any known or existing wireless network without providing its credential. However, if you wish to disable this, you may do so by following the steps below:

  1. Click on the Action Center icon and select All Settings.
  2. Select Network & Internet.
  3. Choose Wi-Fi.
  4. Under Related Settings, select Change Adapter Options.
  5. Double-click on the WiFi connection to open the WiFi status box.
  6. Below the General tab, click the Wireless Properties button.
  7. Under the Connection tab, uncheck the Connect Automatically When This Network is in Range.

Let us know if you need further assistance.
