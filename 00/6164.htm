stefan blom say:

You can avoid most of the problems with (seemingly) blank pages if you work with nonprinting marks displayed. You can then easily delete any superfluous paragraph marks as soon as you spot them.

Ultimately, though, the behavior is part of how Word was once designed, and no one here can change that. You can always post feedback in the Uservoice forum at https://word.uservoice.com.

Also, note that blank pages can be forced by section breaks or by paragraph formatting. Section breaks will become visible when you display nonprinting marks. For assistance with the paragraph formatting, the long-term solution is to make proper use of styles, but you'll find the Reveal Formatting pane (Shift+F1) helpful as well.

macropod say:
Word does not arbitrarily create new pages. It will only create a new page if what you've added to a page is sufficient to require a new page, or if you do something like inserting a manual page break or a 'next Page' Section break. In other words, it is you who is responsible for this.
