john w. vinson say:

Step back a bit. This is NOT how databases work! You should certainly not store the supervisor information redundantly in two tables. That's what Queries are for.

Each Employee record should probably have an SupervisorID field, a link to the supervisor's record in the Employee table itself. Similarly you would have a Locations table, and each employee record would have a LocationID linked to it. Data display and entry would be done on a Form - not directly in the table datasheet. Avoiding the use of combo boxes ("drop down lists") and forms would be like typing the data in every cell of an Excel spreadsheet and not using any formulas - skipping the most powerful and useful features of the program!

scottgem say:

There is NO need for a separate table of supervisors. I have 2 fields in my employees database; Supervisor (Y/N) and SupervisorID (Long Integer). If an employee is a supervisor the Y/N field is marked True. The employeeID of the Supervisor is then entered into the SupervisorID field.

You can display the supervisor name using a self join. Just add the employee table a second time to the query's table pane. It will append a _A to the name. Then just join SupervisorID of the main table to EmployeeID of the +A table.

As far as Location goes you should have a separate table of locations and have LocationID as a foreign key in the employees table.

As John noted, a spreadsheet is a different animal. You probably will not be able to directly to the Excel table. You will probably need to run Append queries to get the spreadsheet data into a properly normalized database,

There are several ways on how you can block spam emails in your mailbox. Here are some options that you can set up in your account:

To set up a junk mail filter to Exclusive, please follow these steps:

  1. Sign in to your Microsoft account .
  2. Click the gear icon located on the upper right panel of your mailbox, just beside your username.
  3. Select Options.
  4. Under Preventing junk email, click on Filters and reporting option.
  5. Under Choose a junk email filter, select Exclusive.
  6. Click the Save button.

If you want to create an email rule to delete emails coming from an email address, kindly refer to these steps:

  1. Sign in to your Microsoft account .
  2. Click the gear icon located on the upper right panel of your mailbox, just beside your username.
  3. Select Options.
  4. Under Customizing Outlook, click the Rules for sorting new messages option.
  5. Click the New button.
  6. Under Step 1: Which messages do you want this rule to apply to?, on the first box with drop down list, select the Sender's address and in the second box with drop down list, select yes.
  7. On the third box, type sender's email address. You may select any option that you want to use at first, second and third box depending on your preference.
  8. Under Step 2: What action do you want to apply?, select Delete these messages.
  9. Click the Save button.

You can also add the sender's email address to your Blocked sender list to stop receiving emails from that contact. Here's how: 

  1. Sign in to your Microsoft account.
  2. Click the gear icon located on the upper right panel of your mailbox, just beside your username.
  3. Select Options.
  4. Under “Preventing junk email”, click the Safe and blocked senders option.
  5. Click Blocked senders.
  6. In the Blocked email address or domain box, type the email address of the sender or the domain that you want to block, and then click Add to list.

Once you add an email address in your blocked senders list, you may also need to delete the account details from your contact list.
