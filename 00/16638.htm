If the one word lines are actually paragraphs (Press Ctrl+Shift+8 to toggle on the display of formatting characters and see if they end with pilcrows ¶), then you could use a macro containing the following code:

With ActiveDocument
    For i = 1 To .Paragraphs.Count
        If .Paragraphs(i).Range.Words.Count = 2 Then
            .Paragraphs(i).Range.Font.Bold = True

    Next i

stefan blom say:

Your previous message refers to problems with the Document Map (in Word 2007 and older versions), and applying "Outline levels" to text paragraphs is exactly what the Document Map used to do. When formatting is applied "on top" of a paragraph style like that, it's called direct formatting.

In Outline view, paragraphs with direct "Outline level" formatting would look like headings, but the PDF converter may only recognize the built-in headings as "real" headings.
