brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

To the best of my knowledge, there aren't any sync add-ins that will work with 64 bit Outlook yet.  ActiveSync won't and, when last I checked, Windows Mobile Device Center won't either.  If you use a different sync tool, check with its vendor.

There's little reason to use 64 bit Office.  At this point in time, its only advantage is that it will handle very large documents.  If you don't need support for documents that large, then use the 32 bit version of Office.  The 32 bit Outlook can use PSTs up to 20 GB as the default and much larger if you adjust registry settings.  Your sync software should then work.

Here's a simple way to format multiple pictures in a document to a single style such as shadow and border size.

1.  Select some general picture that you'll remember and call it your 'Framing Style' or whatever.

2. Use the picture formatting choices to make it look like you want for your standard with all appropriate sizes, shadows, etc.

3. Save it as the standard picture for future use in this and/or other docs where you want your pictures to look like this.

4.  In your working (new or editing) document insert this standard photo.  I usually put it at the bottom of the page where I'm composing so it always is just ahead of where I am putting new content.  You can put it anywhere, just remember where you put it. 

5.  Insert a new photo or image as you add content.

6.  Go to your 'Standard' photo and select (click on) it. 

7.  Go to the Home tab and locate the 'Format Painter' brush icon in the first group (or where ever you have it if you've put it on the quick access bar etc.

8.  Click on the 'Format Painter' brush to load the format (style).

9.  Scroll back to the recently inserted photo/image and click on it to apply the format standard you have just selected in (Step 8.).  Your new content (photo) should get the shadows, lines, etc. that were on your 'Standard' photo.

10.  Do this each time you insert a new photo/image and they will all have the same appearance.

11.  As a final step in your editing remember to delete your 'Standard' photo from the document since it is only a working tool and not a part of the final content.

While it isn't possible to add new new effect combinations to the picture style gallery, you can work around this:

  1. Format a picture using your preferred combination of effects.
  2. With the photo still selected, click on INSERT>Quick Parts.
  3. Choose Save Selection to Quick Parts Gallery.
  4. Give it a name and click OK.

In day-to-day use:

  1. Choose Insert>Quick Parts
  2. Click on your formatted picture to insert it.
  3. Right-click on the picture and choose Change Picture.
  4. Select the picture and click on OK. The new picture takes on the same formatting effects.
