When running Windows 10 on low capacity devices such as those with 32 GBs, you need to augment it with at least a larger SSD and cloud storage. 

For example, you could connect a 64 GB SD Card and offload apps from the Windows Store to the SD Card.

Open Start > Settings > Apps > Apps & Feature 

Select the app downloaded from the Windows Store then click Move

Choose the SSD

The next option is to use OneDrive to keep files in the cloud and only sync those you need. A feature coming to OneDrive in the next version of Windows 10, 1709 called Files on Demand lets you do that:

https://www.groovypost.com/howto/enable-onedrive-on-demand-files-windows-10/ 

Having an external drive can help to offload and archive large files such as videos and images. 

  • How to manually backup and restore your files

A feature in Windows 10 1703 called Storage Sense can help you manage files over a period of time, so you should enable: Start > Settings > System > Storage.

You should also keep in mind low capacity devices should be used specifically for what they are intended for, which more consumption than a work horse traditional larger capacity device. 

The Cloud, External Backup should really be your assets when using these devices.
