"How to Refresh Windows 8 and Windows 8.1"

  • The PC boots into Windows RE (Recovery Environment).
  • Windows RE scans the hard drive for your data, settings, and apps, and puts them aside (on the same drive).
  • Windows RE installs a fresh copy of Windows.
  • Windows RE restores the data, settings, and apps it has set aside into the newly installed copy of Windows.
  • The PC restarts into the newly installed copy of Windows.

marcus j s say:

Fix for Hibernation issues in Windows 7 ultimate.

Original title: WHO has ultimate HIBERNATE problem????

alright, those who have problems hibernating their system, win7 to be exact, this is not a question, but a solution post..

if you don't have any problem regarding this, or are just not interested, i suggest you stop right here to save your time and attention.

if you simply don't know about hibernation, or try it not even once, and wonder the necessity of making it work, i can tell you why this is a must for mobility..
1st, like shutting down, but you can start your system much faster than normal startup; 
2nd, like a power-off pause, you can continue with what you were doing before hibernation, and return to your previous status; 
3rd, almost 0 power consumption compared with sleep, and much safer to move around than sleep, esp long distance and bumpy way..

i've encountered an ultimate problem for this long since i transfered my old sys to a new hdd, and it failed to hibernate everytime even after i tried everything i can possibly find in every forum where people with the same problem discuss this but cannot come up with a solution that does help and work.

here i'm not telling you to try any of the following, as you may have tried already before you google or search the answers here

-update your display(monitors) drivers *not always to the latest!
-reset your BIOS to default
-elevated cmd: powercfg -h on *i was thinking why win7 does not have a GUI for turning on/off this! such a retrogress..
-switch power plan/disable the hybrid sleep (in power options)

coz even starters who would think of these can help themselves out and the hibernate opt will show up (again), which is no more a problem.

NOW IF YOU HAVE REAL PROBLEMS HIBERNATING YOUR SYSTEM(WIN7)
like

-lock instead
-cannot resume
-keep hibernating but cannot turn off power
-some functions(like network connections/sound/usb etc.) do not work after resuming

the following shall be an ultimate solution for you and would keep updating.

-first of all, check if your mainboard support hibernation using elevated cmd: powercfg -a.
 normally s1/s2/s3 means supporting deep/deeper/deepest sleep, s4 means supporting hibernation,
 and most clients would see s3 supported.
 if not, skip all these and waste no more time on this issue.

-set your win7 partition as an active one in disk management.

-update your power(batteries) drivers *not always to the latest!

-upgrade your BIOS *do it with extreme caution and under strict instruction, lest you cannot startup.

-try also elevated cmd： powercfg -h -size <PercentSize> if you expand your memory recently *not less than 50 (50% of your memory size), rec 75, 100 or up to be full.

-clean up your system drive also if you tried the previous but helps nothing, cos not adequate space will result in the failure of storing data thus cannot complete hibernation/resume.

-disable all the power management functions(esp. allow this device to wake the computer) of each possible device, like network adapters, sound, video and game controllers, usb hub etc. even try mouse, to clear the factors that hinder hibernation or its completion.

-try to sleep once and resume, then most functions shall come back and work again. if not, try to update the drivers for the specific functions.

if none of these solve your problem, then congratulations! you've reach the ultimate level of this problem!

to solve this, you can either

-remount your system volume for system to relocate your hiberfil.sys if you transfered your system or change your sys hdd letter, using elevated cmd: diskpart(enter) automount(enter), if reported disabled, type automount enable(enter) then exit(enter), reboot your system and try hibernation.

-fix your computer using your win7 installation DVD

-reinstall win7

or you can try this ultimate solution

-edit your bootmgr/BCD file *shall be your last chance before reinstallation

if you have difficulty using cmd or fail to modify with cmd, i'll recommend visual bcd editor for you.

(remember to tick show all items, then) check if Hibernate resumers is there,
if not, you'll need to create one in order to truly enable hibernate function(this finally get me out of my headache)
applicationpath \Windows\system32\winresume.exe
hiberfiledevice \Device\Harddisk Volume 1(C:)
hiberfilepath \hiberfil.sys

for those whose such item has existed and those who have multi-system, try to relocate your hiberfil.sys of each winsys to a correct and valid path.

this hopefully will do the work before you have to reinstall your system to enjoy the convenience of hibernation.

if all these above helps nothing, just don't reprove me for c'sake, Mr. Ballmer/Gates will possibly be more than happy to help you out..
