Please clarify why you believe a change needs to be made. I'm afraid we can't help resolve a problem or determine why your efforts are fruitless unless we know what it is that you perceive the problem to be.

If you're having difficulty with spell checking it most likely is not associated with a custom dictionary. Custom dictionaries basically are text files which store nothing other than what the user decides to add in order to supplement the main spell-checking dictionary to keep it from flagging the term as being incorrectly spelled.

Typical examples are a technical term or a company name such as Macally which is not included in the main dictionary supplied with the program. For anyone who uses that term with any degree of regularity it's a nuisance to have it flagged constantly as a misspelling. Adding it to a custom dictionary prevents that. Otherwise, custom dictionaries have no bearing on ordinary spell checking.

This article may help you resolve the issue on your own:

Spelling, Language, Dictionary

Also, it's unlikely that anything from another source will be compatible with Word unless the file is expressly designed for the purpose.

This error is usually caused due to corruption in the PST file. Scan the PST file and check if it helps:

http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/outlook-help/repair-outlook-data-files-pst-and-ost-HA010354964.aspx?CTT=1

C:\WINDOWS\system32\mspst32.dll

Win7 32-bit:

C:\Program Files\Microsoft Office\Office14

Win7 64-bit:

C:\Program Files (x86)\Microsoft Office\Office14
