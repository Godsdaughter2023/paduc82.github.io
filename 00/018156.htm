When I50 is blank Excel treats it as 0 when evaluating H50-I50

To avoid this check for blank first

=IF(ISBLANK(I50),"", IF((H50-I50)>-1,"Yes",IF((H50-I50)<0,"No","Diff is zero")))

I have also added a bit to say what happens if H50-I50 is zero

BTW, if I50 is blank because of  a formula in I50, it will fail the ISBLANK test

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

Your email address is unique and your account cannot be used by anyone else if you have it protected properly. However, that's not to say some can't have made a mistake and supplied your address to an online service, thinking it was their own.

For example. let's say your address is "me @hotmail.com". Now suppose someone in Austrailia has the address "me @hotmail.co.au". Those are two completely separate mail addresses because what comes after the @ is part of the address as much as what precedes it. Now suppose the person in Australia buys something online and enters "me @hotmail.com" instead of the correct address. If the online merchant doesn't validate the address, guess what? You'll get the notices of purchases until the person in Australia realizes they're not getting the acknowledgements they expect.

This very thing has happened to me. I have an address containing the word "bear", while someone else has an address containing the word "beer", with all other characters being exactly the same. That other person purchased tickets to a concert. I received the notification of the purchase. I wrote to the company issuing the tickets and told them the other person was using an address they didn't own. At the time, I didn't know about the one-letter difference. The concert company removed my address from their records.
