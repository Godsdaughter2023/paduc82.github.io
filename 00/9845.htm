jtelliott94 say:

Hi Allanah, 

It seems that the actual registry entries are possibly corrupt. This may have affected TCP/IP as well and you say you have tried the reset cmds to no avail so I would try repairing them instead. Just so you know, this is considered a manual winsock reset of sorts, and involves modifying the registry. Please backup the full registry before you ever touch it. Also backup/export keys that you are intending to modify or delete. Thanks. So the main goal is to use the registry editor to delete (backup first!) the winsock and winsock2 subways and then remove and reinstall TCP/IP on Windows. To do this, follow these steps:

1) Start > Run > type "regedit" > hit enter.

2) Locate the following subkey:

           HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\System\CurrentControlSet\Services

3) Right click and delete the entire Winsock key and the Winsock2 keys.

4) Close regedit. 

Now, re-install TCP/IP overtop itself (it's a core component and can't be uninstalled, so we improvise..)

1) Login as admin. In control panel open up, "Network and Sharing Center"

2) Right click "Local Area Connection", and then click "Properties"

3) Under the This Component Uses the Following Items dialog box, click Install

4) Click Protocol, and then click Add

5) Click Have Disk

6) In the Copy Manufacture's files from box, type C:\windows\inf and then click OK.

7) In the list of protocols, click Internet Protocol (TCP/IP) and then click OK. Restart computer.

Let me know if this helps.

antoneth ber say:

Hello x96merrill3,

The error message"550 5.5.0 Requested action not taken: mailbox unavailable" is a bounce message that might be due to the following reason:

  • The email address that you're sending to doesn't exist.
  • There's a problem with your/the recipients account.
  • The recipient have too much message and the server hosting it isn't accepting any more.

Meanwhile, you may check the following: 

  • Make sure the recipient's email address is correct.
  • Remove the recipient's email address from your auto-complete list and try re-sending the message.
  • Make sure the recipient doesn't have an email message forwarding rule in place.

Also you may check the troubleshooting steps on this article: Fix Outlook.com email sync issues.

x96merrill3 say:

Thank you for the reply.  None of the suggestions you offered were of any use as they are the same cut and paste solutions given time and time again on this forum.  I had already double checked each of them three times over.  

What I found:
When the new Outlook.com - Beta connected to my personal account, hosted by GoDaddy, Outlook's servers changed the MX destination of the DNS Management.  

From GoDaddy:

To change an MX record for domains registered at another company
  1. Log in to your My Products page.
  2. If you aren't automatically directed there, make sure to select the My Products tab:

  3. Click Manage All:

  4. From the top menu, click the DNS tab, and select Manage Zones:

  5. Enter the domain name you want to use and click Search.
  6. On the DNS Management page, in the Records section, click the  (pencil icon) next to MX.
  7. Make any changes to the record, and click Save when finished.
 

What I did:

Went to Manage DNS zone files

Clicked on the domain with the email issue

Found the line in the DNS management labeled: MX and clicked on the pencil to edit. 

Here is where I found the MX server was pointed to a hotmail account.  The INCORRECT SETTINGS were: 

Host: @
Points to: 616771824.pamx1.hotmail.com
Priority: 0
TTL: 1 Hour

I changed the "Points to" section to my mail server (the same one used to set up smtp mail and can be found under your GoDaddy email configuration settings) which was p3plcpnl0048.prod.phx3.secureserver.net 

Click "Save" and send yourself a test message from another account to verify. 

Good luck!
