lemp say:

I'm not sure where canadian tech got the idea that the device in question was a telephony modem.

According to http://www.pcidatabase.com/search.php?device_search_str=29c4&device_search=Search  the device is the Intel Management Engine Interface, which has something to do with remote management of your computer (and almost certainly is not needed in a typical consumer situation).  https://software.intel.com/en-us/articles/architecture-guide-intel-active-management-technology/

I suspect that you are seeing a warning icon in device manager for something such as an unknown PCI communications device.

The driver for this would generally be part of the "chipset" driver.  See, for example, this HP page (note that I'm not suggesting that you use the HP driver, which is for specific HP notebook models).  http://h20564.www2.hp.com/hpsc/swd/public/detail?swItemId=ob_102185_1&swEnvOid=4058

In general, you should go to the website of your computer's manufacturer, look up your specific model (use the "Service Tag" if you have a Dell) and get the chipset driver there.  If you have a custom-built computer, see https://downloadcenter.intel.com/download/22541/How-to-find-Chipset-download-and-support-information
