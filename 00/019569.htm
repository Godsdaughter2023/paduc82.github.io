julie fka profjulie say:

My Phone serves as a backup to your phone insofar as if you lose or break the phone, your data can be easily restored to a new windows phone directly from My Phone....but if you mess around with an existing phone that has an existing relationship with My Phone, syncing to My Phone creates a "mirror" image of the data on the phone to the My Phone web portal.  If you delete items on the phone, then sync to My Phone, the data on My Phone gets "deleted" (actually the 1st time you sync it gets moved to the archive).  If you delete items on the My Phone web portal, then sync to your windows phone, the data on the windows phone gets deleted.

If you ever accidentally delete data on your phone and then sync to My Phone, My Phone has no way to know that deleting the data was an accident.  All it recognizes is that the data was deleted from the phone so it deletes it from My Phone (well, moves it to the archive).  Luckily, when you do something like that, the data is not really deleted from My Phone, it gets moved to the archive, so if you figure out you accidentally deleted something, the  best thing you can do is go out to the My Phone Archive and move the data back to the active area of My Phone.  Better still, though, as soon as you realize you've deleted something accidentally from your phone and BEFORE YOU SYNC THE PHONE TO MY PHONE,the best course of action is to Stop the My Phone service on your phone .  Then create a new partnership between your phone and My Phone.   Then when you sync the phone to My Phone, My Phone will not delete any of the data since it will view the phone as a new device and sync the data to the phone.
