As Sangeeta Sarkar said, this issue is often hardware related, and when you fail to give information about your hardware, you don't help us to help you.

This particular error for build 14332 has occurred in some Dell laptops, for example, because the installer, correctly or incorrectly, has seen certain adapters as incompatible, so it aborts the upgrade at the restart at the end of "installing features and drivers".

One specific case was in a Dell XPS M1530 in our lab. We determined the fault to be related to the wlan adapter, so turned wlan off in bios setup, which allowed the upgrade to succeed because the wlan adapter was no longer seen by the installer.  After the upgrade was complete, we turned the wlan back on in bios setup, then uninstalled it and deleted its driver, and rebooted, to allow it to reinstall with a new driver.

Hi Andrew.

If your emails were deleted recently you can try the steps below:

- Open Outlook.
- Select the Deleted Items folder.
- Go to the Tools / Recover Deleted Items from server
- Select the email(s) that you would like to recover.
- Click the Recover Selected Items button (the icon is an email message with an arrow).

The email will go back to the Deleted Items folder it was in.

diane poremsky mvp (slipstick.com) say:

If the mail is no longer in the Deleted Items folder and you use an Outlook.com or Exchange mailbox, look for it in the recovery folder linked at the top of the mailbox in Outlook on the web.
https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Restore-deleted-email-messages-in-Outlook-com-cf06ab1b-ae0b-418c-a4d9-4e895f83ed50

Outlook.com accounts have a recovery period of 14 days, Office 365 business accounts may have a longer period of time in the recovery folder (it's configurable by the email admin.)

If you have an Outlook.com or Office 365 business mailbox and are just past the period for the recovery folder, you can try a Office addin called Email Recovery. It can recover mail that is out of the recovery folder and waiting for Exchange to purge it - but the time frame for recovery is short.
https://appsource.microsoft.com/en-us/product/office/WA104380447
