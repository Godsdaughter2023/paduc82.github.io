davidf2 say:

It is impossible. If you want to send the newsletter as the HTML formatted body of the email, then you must manually combine the two pages into one. Older versions of Publisher would try to automagically combine them but the results were most always a total mess. I would suggest you use an 'email template' instead of a newsletter template as it will automatically truncate the length of the publication after the last design element when you send as an Outlook email.

Now after you have done all this and think you have finally got your email looking good, then realize that there is a good chance it will not look good when viewed in other email clients or if the recipient has their email client set to read only plain text and no pictures for security reasons. Each email client renders HTML coding in a different way. So test using all the major email clients...

The best way to send a two page newsletter is to convert it to a PDF and attach it. This is the only way you can maintain the layout and formatting. If you write a simple but effective introduction in the body of your email message and your message is of interest and wanted by the recipient they will open the PDF. If it is spam, then it won't matter whether it is in the message body or a PDF...it will be deleted.

If you must send HTML formatted messages then instead of Publisher consider using specialty software such as:www.constantcontact.com or www.group-mail.com and a free version: http://www.group-mail.com/asp/common/downloads.asp or another popular one is http://mailchimp.com/ - some free services - Store up to 2,000 subscribers & send up to 12,000 emails per month.
