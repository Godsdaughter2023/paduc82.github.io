You've still not indicated the version update level of your Office 2016 installation. Please go to Word> About Word in the main menu bar to determine that information. It should be version 15.35 [or later if you are a participant in the Insider program].

It also would be helpful to know whether the printer is connected directly to your computer or whether it's being accessed remotely over a network.

That info is important, but you're chasing the wrong suspect :-) Duplex printing is not controlled by the program you're printing from. It's a function of the printer, itself, controlled by its driver software. The program you're printing from merely provides the convenience of adjusting the print parameters from within the Print dialog window. In a nutshell, neither Word nor any other program really knows or cares whether the  output is printed on one side or two :-) However, printing is a process handled by a subsystem of the OS which requires that the driver, the application software & the OS, itself, be 'in sync'.

Printing issues have been exceptionally rife with OS X Sierra. It would be a good idea to visit the printer mfr's web site to see if there is a driver and/or firmware update for your model. If there is no update or if updating doesn't correct the problem, remove then reinstall the printer.

Should the issue persist, contact the support services offered by the printer mfr.

rohn007 say:

Your second problem right now is being able to come up with a way of recreating the problem so the technicians can then trouble shoot. Until then it is completely your own problem, or a problem specific to your configuration. Recreating includes finding someone else reporting the same problem.

So you have to do your own trouble shooting.

Remember the printing process is more complex than you suggested:

  • Word formats the document in it's own terminology
  • using the printer driver, it translates it's formatting to printer languagte (ie HPCL or Postcript)
  • that is sent to the Windows Printing subsystem
  • the printer subsystem holds on to it temporarily, "spools it"
  • Windows Print subsystem sends the print job on to the printer
  • the printer interprets the printing commands it get.

That the problem goes away for a while after re-installing Office is a strong suggestion the problem is in Word/Office. The trick is for you to try to narrow down where the problem is appearing. That is trouble shooting:

  • Try connecting your computer to a different printer.
  • Try connecting a different computer to your printer.
  • Change the connection from your computer to the printer, ie if wired, change the wire, if wireless, switch to wired.

So, keep looking around to see if you can find anyone else with the same problem.

jeffpearce say:

You can transfer your iTunes music to your Windows 8 phone, but not through Zune - it doesn't know how to talk to a Windows Phone 8.

As Entegy said, there's an update to the Windows Phone connector available in the Mac App store for OS/X, so if you're a Mac user, that's what you'll want to use.

 

If you're using Windows, you have several options.

There's a Windows Phone app for Windows that interacts seamlessly with iTunes to find all of your music and playlists, and you can use that to sync directly. If you're running windows 7, you will be prompted to download it the first time you plug in your phone. If you're running Windows 8, you can get it from http://www.windowsphone.com/en-US/How-to/wp8/windows-phone-app-for-desktop. The app will automatically exclude any music that has Apple DRM, so you won't accidentally transfer stuff the phone can't play. This is by far the easiest option if you primarily use iTunes.

If you want to manage it yourself, your phone is visible in Explorer, and you can use drag and drop to copy it. By default, iTunes stores music in My Music\iTunes\iTunes Media\Music if you're going to go that route. You can browse your phone from Start... Devices and Printers

Finally, Windows Media Player can copy stuff to a Windows 8 phone, so that's also an option.

holzlondon say:

Hmmm, I think that unless you are receiving literally hundreds of emails per day, an update once per day will not overburden your 200 MB data plan. 

 

You always have to confirm the download of attachments separately, so they are never included anyway.  Looking at my inbox, most "pure text" emails are around 5 KB each.

 

Let's be generous and say they're 10 KB each, and you get 200 emails a day.  You'd have about 2 MB per day, and assuming a 31 day month, you'd use up all of 62 MB. 

 

Plus, whether you get the 200 mails per day, or 1400 in a weekly sync, the amount of data will be the same.  The main thing is the attachments, which you only download as you need them.

 

I think that things like having facebook, twitter, other online services, and constant web browsing will cost more serious data than the daily email sync.
