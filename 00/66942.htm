xircal say:

The problem here is that with an OEM machine like Dell, the product key is located in the BIOS although the latter has all but been replaced by UEFI.

When you run a recovery, the software retrieves the product key from the BIOS which obviates the need to place a sticker containing the key to the outside of the laptop. It's also a means of reducing fraudulent installations where the product key can be stolen or copied.

This site deals with activating Windows for the different versions of Windows: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/kb/950929

By the sound of it though, you already went through that process.

Regardless, your best bet might be to call Microsoft again, but do it via the steps in the following post marked as Answer in the following thread: http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/windows_8-windows_install/says-windows-8-isnt-activated-after-refreshing-my/d7ce96db-fb97-4339-a335-81d4a7161060

Utilities like Nirsoft were only of any use when Windows 2000 was around and are next to useless these days since Microsoft has taken appropriate steps to prevent fraudulent installations.

Here are a few other reasons why activation may fail: http://windows.microsoft.com/en-gb/windows-8/why-activate-windows

What might be worth your while doing is to contact Dell and get a set of original recovery discs. More info on that option here: http://www.dell.com/support/article/us/en/19/SLN130027?c=&l=&s=
