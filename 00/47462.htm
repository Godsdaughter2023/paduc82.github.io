    considerate_guy say:

    Movie Maker attempts to maintain the correct aspect ratio of your video.  As noted above, your source video is a standard definition movie in full screen (4:3) aspect ratio.  There is no cropping feature or stretching feature in Movie Maker that would be able to turn that into a widescreen (16:9) aspect ratio.  Even if you pick that ratio for your project, Movie Make will just put the 4:3 video (nearly square) into the 16:9 rectangle (widescreen) and the result will be black on the sides.

     

    This is the correct behavior of Movie Maker.  If you have a 4:3 movie, you should use a 4:3 project.  But remember if you upload that to places like YouTube, the resulting video will again be put in a widescreen rectangle and will show blank on the left and right.  The only fix is to use video of the correct aspect ratio (e.g. widescreen) or to find a separate program that might allow cropping to that ratio.  If you think this is a good feature to add to Movie Maker, suggest it at feedback.live.com.

    considerate_guy say:

    Some clarification on terms:

     

    Old style TVs were nearly rectangular and used a 4:3 aspect ratio. This format (especially when viewed on from a DVD) was called "full screen" because it filled the full analog TV screen.  Today with widescreen HDTVs the term "full screen" is a bit of a misnomer.  If you show 4:3 video on a widescreen HDTV or monitor today, it does not fill the full screen.

     

    The video that fills a 16:9 aspect ratio, like most modern HDTVs is called "widescreen".  Movie Maker does let you create a movie that is widescreen (16:9) by going to  the Project tab and choosing the 16:9 aspect ratio.  But if your original video is not widescreen, it won't fill the full widescreen (16:9 rectangle).

     

    If your video *is* widescreen, but it isn't showing as widescreen, it may be the format of the source video. Try converting it to a widescreen aspect ratio and a format that is 100% compatible with Movie Maker.  My recommendation would be to use Freemake Video Converter to convert to .wmv format.

    My problem is solved, by doing the following steps.

    1. Remove the SSD and put the old HDD back in -start up the laptop with the old activated config.

    2. Ran slmgr.vbs /ato as admin and installed all Windows updates.

    3. Make sure you're logged in on your Microsoft account -I also turned on the sync setting to all my devices(not sure if it's needed).

    7. Reset your pc & remove all files.
