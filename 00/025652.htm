move footnotes to appendix

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

The following macro will replace footnotes with ordinary text at the end of the document from where you could move it into an Appendix.

' Macro created 29/09/99 by Doug Robbins to replace footnotes with textnotes at end of document

' to replace the footnote reference in the body of the document with a superscript number.

'

    Dim afootnote As Footnote

    For Each aFootnote In ActiveDocument.Footnotes

        ActiveDocument.Range.InsertAfter vbCr & aFootnote.Index & vbTab & aFootnote.Range

        aFootnote.Reference.InsertBefore "a" & aFootnote.Index & "a"

    Next aFootnote

    For Each aFootnote In ActiveDocument.Footnotes

        aFootnote.Reference.Delete

    Next aFootnote

    Selection.Find.ClearFormatting

    Selection.Find.Replacement.ClearFormatting

    With Selection.Find.Replacement.Font

        .Superscript = True

    End With

    With Selection.Find

        .Text = "(a)([0-9]{1,})(a)"

        .Replacement.Text = "\2"

        .Forward = True

        .Wrap = wdFindContinue

        .Format = True

        .MatchWildcards = True

    End With

    Selection.Find.Execute Replace:=wdReplaceAll

Yes, it is possible. There are two ways to approach the problem.

One would be to use a picture as the background. Format > Sheet > Background.

The plus side of this is that the background appears on all pages in the worksheet. The bad side is that the picture is tiled, so you might design your picture with a lot of white space around the central content. Of course, be sure the picture is not so strong as to dominate the page.

Another way would be to insert a picture and make it transparent so that only the watermark is visible. The picture will sit above your worksheet in a layer, which is inconvenient for editing. You could use a macro to hide the picture while editing and display the picture when you're done editing. 

The best way to do it is not available in Excel 2016 because of someone at Microsoft making a bad decision to kill the feature.
