Somebody at Microsoft -- probably a former art major <grin> -- thought that the new default blue was "prettier". Apparently they never gave any thought to the idea that someone might want to search for "blue" text, and that specifying RGB(0,0,255) wouldn't find anything.

kenfischer2869 say:

Hi John,

I would leverage the web-based tools to make this transition smoother and take the pressure off.  You can set up the new accounts on Office 365 and plan for a cutover time to happen during off hours (make sure your DNS TTL is lowered to help with this).

Once DNS changes propagate, any new messages will start delivering to the Office 365 accounts.  Users can use their current version of Outlook to access the old mailbox while using the web-based version to access anything new.

As you upgrade each user, configure the new version of Outlook desktop to connect to both accounts.  Migrate the messages from the old account to the new one, then remove the account configs for the old account.

kenfischer2869 say:

Wim's suggestion is also a valid approach.  

You'll also likely be using IMAP to migrate at the end if you don't do it at the beginning.

I have found that it's better to get the users on the new system / experience sooner in the process, while they still have access to the old email for a short time.  You will inevitably have someone who can't figure out how to send a critical email and being able to say "send it the old way" has saved me more than once.

It's also comforting to know that the MX transfer works and email is flowing into the new accounts before you pull the plug on the old system.  :)

The message "You haven't signed in from this location recently." will occur if you are signing in from a location more than 300 miles from your normal location.

The Microsoft account service will prompt you for an additional challenge to complete the log in.

A code can be sent to your SMS number or alternate email if configured.

1.       Log in to this link.  

2.       Click Delete on the security information you do not have access to. (Alternate email, Trusted PC, Mobile number)

4.       Click Delete Security Info.

5.       Update the account with new Security Info.

6.       Click Save.

You just need to wait for 30 days for all the information to be removed and the new security information to be automatically confirmed.
