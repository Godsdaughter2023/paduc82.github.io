dpaz_tech say:

I know this thread is old, but hopefully some info here can help those us bewildered by this new behavior in Windows 10 (desktop and mobile).

I replied with details in this thread http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/windows_10-networking/why-doesnt-the-vpn-work-on-windows-10-mobile/607d23f9-eff0-44f6-9308-bbd250569966, but here are the basics:

  1. This is a feature called Split Tunneling, and will send traffic through a VPN only when it is inaccessible to the underlying connection (wired, WiFi, LTE, etc).
  2. The way to get around it is to avoid selecting any specific protocol for your VPN connection. Instead, choose Automatic.
  3. Along with that, you must choose Automatic when the connection is first setup. Changing and saving it to any other option will re-enable split tunneling and the only option to return force all traffic through the VPN is to create a new VPN connection with Automatic.
  4. With Automatic, the protocols are tried in this order until a connection is made: IKEv2, SSTP, L2TP, and PPTP. Been this was since Windows 7 as those are most to least secure protocols.

Again, more details are in my reply in the link above. Hope this helps.
