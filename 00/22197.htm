Has anyone ran into this sort of scenario before?

Abdul

young yang msft support say:

Hi Abdul,

You’re right. After the migration, the objectGUID will change. So, the new user will not match to the corresponding Office 365 user. This will cause conflicts.

Based on my experience, you can refer to the steps below:

1. Temporarily deactivate DirSync in Office 365 admin center. Therefore, the local migration will not affect Office 365 users.

2. Complete your local user migration.

3. Match new AD users to the corresponding Office 365 user. We can use hard match: blogs.technet.com/.../how-to-do-hard-match-in-dirsync.aspx

For hard match, the general steps are:

Get the ObjectGuid from the new AD user

•Rearrange the ObjectGuid, and convert the ObjectGuid format to an ImmutableID

•Update the cloud user with the new ImmutableID

So, new AD users can match to the existing Office 365 users.

Anything unclear, please let us know.

Thanks,

Young

a. user say:

Hi idumabest,

 

Please accept my apologies for the delay in responding to your query as we are currently receiving a high volume of support request.

 

Please be informed that if your Microsoft account was automatically upgraded to Outlook.com interface, the Archive folder is not visible in the folders section of your mailbox . It will be only visible once you've moved an email to the Archive folder. You have two options to select once you choose to archive an email:

  1. Archive my messages in <Select folder>
  2. Create a new folder, called Archive

If you select the option to Create a new folder, called Archive, you will see the Archive folder in the folder section on the left panel of your mailbox.

 

If there's anything else that I can help you with, please do not hesitate to reply here and we will further assist you. We'd love to hear from you again.

 

Thanks!

 

a. user say:

Hello meme and me,

 

Thank you for posting your query in the Microsoft Community.

 

From what I understand you accidentally archived some of your emails but you don't have an Archive folder. I would be happy to help with that. As the previous moderator has mentioned, when you select an email and click Archive in the toolbar, you'll be asked whether to archive your emails in an existing folder or a new folder or to archive the messages in a folder named Archive. It is possible that your emails were just moved to one of your existing emails. You can try to find these emails by typing the email subject or the email sender in the search box located above the Folders pane on the left of your mailbox.

 

Once you've located your messages, you can move them again to your Inbox or to your desired folder just by ticking the check box beside View: All v and clicking Move to v then selecting the folder name where you would like the message to be moved in the list that will appear.

 

Hope this helps. Let us know if you need further assistance.
