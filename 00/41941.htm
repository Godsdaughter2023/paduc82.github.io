arminatorx say:

That is because the Xbox One uses a different file system format than the PC. If you need to use the drive on the PC again (and NOT use it on the Xbox One), you can use the drive management console, to erase the drive's partitions, and re-format it to NTFS again (erasing all content on the drive).

In order to get to the drive management, open up an Explorer window, and (depending on your Windows Version) right click on "My Computer" or "This PC", and in the context menu choose "Manage". Confirm the UAC prompt, and then go to the drive management console.

Find the USB drive, and delete the partitions on it. Be careful to double check that you are doing that on the correct drive. Then create a new partition and format that for NTFS to be able to use the drive on the PC again.

You can't use the drive for Xbox One storage and PC at the same time.
