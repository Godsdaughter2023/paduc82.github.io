That screenshot is actually not of your "My Documents" folder.  It's your "Documents Library."

Basically, "libraries" are a way to look at files that are in multiple locations.  Usually, the default for each of the libraries (Documents, Music, Pictures, and Videos) is to include two locations:  your personal documents folder, which is called "My Documents" (even though its actual name is just "Documents") and the folder that contains documents that are available to all users and is called "Public Documents." 

You seem to have removed "Public Documents" from your "Documents Library."

With respect to the battery issue, even if you were living in Frostbite Falls, MN, it's not as if you are using your desktop computer outdoors.  Besides, the problem you describe is not the typical symptom of a failing "CMOS" battery.  This battery maintains the system clock when the computer is powered off.  With a bad battery, the time will reset to some unusual value in the past every time you boot up the computer.  Your problem is that you can't get to the control panel function that sets the time.
