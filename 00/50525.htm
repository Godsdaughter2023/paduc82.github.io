Actually, there is a simple solution.

Turn on your Windows Explorer (winkey+E), and in the address bar, enter

shilohbob say:

Depending on the brand of laptop, your disk drive configuration will be something like the insert:

The insert is from a Asus laptop, which is currently running Windows 10 (C:) via the Insider Program

The 8gb SSD is a reserved partition and will most probably be labelled as "OEM Partition" and as you can see it has no volume label, normally there is no requirement for users to modify the unlabelled partitions (can be done, need to know what you are doing).

Windows 10 will not attempt to load into these partitions, it will load onto the 1TB HDD.

As you can see I have partitioned the 1TB HDD, into 2 smaller partitions (C & D), with C: being the drive with the OS installed, the D: is used to store data.

You could partition the 1TB HDD into maybe 3 partitions - 1 x 200GB for the OS, and then split the remainder into partitions of maybe 400GB each.

If you require additional info please advise.

shilohbob say:

PS:

The drive setup is quite common on newer laptops with hybrid drive setups.
