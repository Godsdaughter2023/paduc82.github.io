Header Record Delimiters - Microsoft Community

ubasti say:

Header Record Delimiters

I'm trying to create a data file (from my client list that is in word 2003). When I try to merge my document and boxe appears that says Data fields must be separated from each other by a character called a field delimiter, then it says the same for a record delimiter, use the list below to select appropriate delimiter. I don't know which delimiter to use for the data field and the data record. Can anyone help??

Thanks

 

a. user say:

If you can open your data document in Word, you should see something like

columnname1,columnname2,columnname3

value11,value12,value13

value21,value22,value23

Where of course your file will have different column names, probably a different number of columns, different values and a different number of rows.

In this example, the comma "," is the field delimiter and the the record delimiter is Enter. But you could have

columnname1;columnname2;columnname3,value11;value12;value13,value21;value22;value23

In that case, the field delimiter would be ";" and the record delimter would be ",". But that probably would not be a very good idea.

If you are asking which delimiters to choose, I would say "use tab characters for the field delimiters, and Enter (carriage return/line feed) for the record delimiters. If there are any tab characters in your data, you have to wrap the field in "text delimiters", which are normally double-quote characters "". If you have double-quote characters in your data, double them up.

Word has two methods for opening delimited data sources. One method is the one you have described. It lets you choose which delimiter characters you want to use. The other opens the file using an OLE DB provider, and only allows the field delimiter to be a carriage return/line feed.

Reset a retail mode phone

A phone in retail mode can be made sellable by resetting it. Resetting a demo phone deactivates retail mode, erases demo content, uninstalls apps, and restores factory settings. To reset a phone that is in retail mode, follow these steps:

Open Settings > About and scroll to the bottom of the screen, then tap Update demo phone.

Next, tilt the phone in the following directions:

  1. Tilt the phone to the left. Give the phone a second or two to redraw in landscape.

  2. Tilt the phone upright. Give the phone a second or two to redraw in portrait.

  3. Tilt the phone to the right. Give the phone a second or two to redraw in landscape.

  4. Tilt the phone upright.

Notice the text in the dialog changes from confirming an update to confirming a reset. Resetting a demo phone removes all of the demo content and apps and restores factory settings. To continue, tap Yes.

Next, you’ll see the goodbye screen. The phone should restart and display rotating gears as it removes the demo content and apps. After the phone restarts, it returns to the setup Welcome screen. At this point reset is complete, and the phone is no longer in retail mode.

scottklair say:

B -

I struggled with this issue a year or two ago on a larger scale while attempting to consolidate, sync and link a number of disparate sets of contacts.  What I ended up doing was fairly involved, and without checking my notes from the time I can't give a comprehensive solution. 

That said, your issue is much less elaborate.  If memory serves, the zombie contacts are coming from the actual Apple application "Contacts".  I never used it, which is why it wasn't on my radar.  But, because it is integrated into the OS, whether you use it or not it populates with contact information via the Internet Accounts Pane in your System Preferences. 

First suggestion:

In System Preferences>Internet Accounts select the profile which you're using with Outlook.  Make sure that the checkbox for "Contacts" is not checked.  Delete the zombies from outlook and reboot the program to see if they reappear.  Even if that relationship was the cause, I also suggest the following.

Go to Applications>Contacts.app and delete the zombies from your contacts.

If the deleted contacts continue to repopulate, more than likely your iCloud account is also involved, defaulted within the Contacts app to be the primary contact list and selected in the Internet Accounts Preference Pane for contact syncing.  Uncheck contacts in the iCloud Internet Account Prefs Pane options, and/or set the Contacts app preference to indicate the local contact db is the primary source, which should then update the online copy to match.  Of course, this won't be relevant if you've disabled the linking between the two.

Hopefully you won't need to even take that many steps.

In my situation, I wanted to keep the System Pref link active because the Internet Account I was using was a Gmail account, and I wanted my Google Contacts to continue to sync with my iCloud account so that they would be  backed up and updated on my iPhone.  This setup utilizes the Contacts application on my laptop for that, and is useful for merging duplicates between the lists.

As such, I was still experiencing a few strange anomalies even after solving most of the issues.  If you are still having random unexplained zombie contacts, the following is likely the issue:

My original apple account was a .mac email address from the mid 90's.  At some point, that account was migrated to the iLife service, which was then shifted to the iCloud setup currently being used.  However, when I initially signed up to use iTunes many years ago, I used a different email address.  At some later date Apple decided that they were going to integrate these logons and your iTunes user id was going to become an all things apple id.  All good, except that there is no way to combine different apple id accounts.  I was, however, able to find a way to link them and finally get rid of the last digital glitches causing Outlook to act so weird . . .

If you decide multiple apple ids might be causing you problems, you'll have to make on an alias for the other on your mac.

First, open System Preferences>Users and Groups.  Click the lock, bottom left, to make changes.  Right click on yourself (current user) and select Advanced Options.  Almost at the bottom, you should see your apple id (likely what you're using with icloud, itunes, or both.  Under that, you should see the same id listed as an alias, as well as some other Hex and numberish stuff you don't wanna mess with.  But, you do have the option of adding aliases, and that's what you'll want to do, using the email address which is the logon for your other apple id.  Save all of that and restart everything and you should be good to go.  Now, depending on whether your iCloud or Contacts is the master, you should be able to eliminate duplicates, link and consolidate everything, which in the end will mean that your Oulook won't be adding contact information via these various linked databases after you've deleted the contaccts.

Good luck.  I hope you'll be able to easily solve what I know from dealing with it is an extremely frustrating and baffling phenomenon.

Cheers,

Scott
