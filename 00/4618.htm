hansv mvp say:

You can search for "( )([! ]@62)([!\\])" with MatchWildCards:=True.

This will find words ending in 62 preceded by a space and followed by any character except \. The leading and trailing character are included, so to get the word itself, you have to move the Start 1 character forward and the End one character backward.

macropod say:

If you use:

(<[! ^t^l^13]@62>)([!\\])

That will avoid the inclusion of the preceding character, leaving only the last one to be trimmed off. Strictly speaking, the '^t^l^13' part of the expression isn't needed for the data as specified, but including them provides for '62' words that may be preceded by tabs, paragraph breaks or manual line breaks. Even the parentheses are not needed unless you have a Replace expression that includes some rearrangement/deletion of parts of the found content.

scottgem say:

I think what you are looking for is not a subform, but a synchronized form. A Subform is a form EMBEDDED on a main form and linked to the record shown in the main form.A synchronized form is a a SEPARATE form that can be opened and synchronized to be filtered for a value shown in the calling form.

Having said that linking the form on SalesPersonID should work to show only contacts for a salesperson. Assuming a contact is assigned to ONE salesperson only you shouldn't need a third (junction table). However if you are looking to do a synchronized form where the salesperson can open it only if they want to, Just use this code:

DoCmd.OpenForm "frmContacts",,,"[SalesPersonID] = " & Me.txtSalesperson

john w. vinson say:

I'm sorry, but I think you're on the wrong track. Hyperlinks are rarely appropriate in an Access app; Queries, on the other hand, are absolutely essential and universal. Your data should be stored in Tables; combined, selected and sorted using Queries; displayed and edited in Forms, using combo boxes and other tools to zero in on which records you want to edit; and displayed (output only) using Reports. 

It sounds like you're trying to design the Tables to fit a particular Report, and back-engineering the forms to update that Table. The proper design is to study your data and your business rules to identify the "Entities" - real-life things, persons or events - of importance to your work; define how those Entities are related (e.g. "Every Task will have zero, one or many Employees assigned to it; Every Employee will be assigned zero, one, or many Tasks". You could then use a Form based on (say) the Employees table, with a subform based on the Assignments table to create a record in Assignments with links to the table of Tasks (what is this person being assigned to do) and to the table of Employees (who's gonna do it). No Hyperlinks are needed or appropriate.

Version 2.90 of Arial is a Font Suitcase file.  This file actually contains all of the infromation for the Regular, Bold, Italic, and Bold Italic typefaces.  Since the file was located in your User directory it had presedence over the Standard version and the version that shipped with Office.  When you disabled and attempted to remove just the Regular face, the file was still hanging around because the system was still relying on it for the Bold, Italic, and Bold/Italic faces.  Also when you disabled just the Regular face, the various mismatched faces could have slightly different internals.  Office will blackflag this as a bad font and therefore will not list it in the Font Drop Downs.  You could still possibly use the font in Office if you manually typed in the Name in to the drop down  (It could possibly have been listed in the Format->Font dialogs also and used that way).
