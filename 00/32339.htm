milagrosm2020 say: Hi. John
My name is Mila
Independent Advisor
I hope to help.

Hello inquiring about the subject, you can perform the following actions:

1. On the PC, start the Xbox application.

2. Select Connection in the panel on the left side of the screen.

3. The Xbox application will search the home network if Xbox One consoles are available.

4. Select the name of the console you want to connect to.

Note: You need the following to make the most of the transmission of games:

• An Xbox One console: the transmission of games must be enabled in Settings.

• A PC with Windows 10: you must have logged in to the Xbox application with the same Gamertag you used in the Xbox One console.

• Network connection: the Windows 10 PC and the Xbox One console must be on the same home network. Options:

• Wired: Ethernet connection (recommended for PC and Xbox One console)
• Wireless: 802.11 wireless connection N / AC

For optimal performance, it is recommended that the Windows 10 PC have the following:

• At least 2 gigabytes of RAM

• 1.5 GHz CPU or more

• Connection to the home network:

• Superior performance: Ethernet connection with cable.

• Good performance: wireless connection: wireless access point 802.11 N or 802.11 AC 5 GHz.

• Limited performance: wireless connection: wireless access point 802.11 N or 802.11 AC 2.4 GHz.

How to start the transmission of games from the XBOX ONE on your pc with windows 10.


Activate the settings on the Xbox One.

To use Xbox game streaming, you must first activate it from the Xbox One console. To do this, press the Xbox button to open the guide, select Settings> Preferences> Connectivity from the Xbox application. Do these two actions:

1. In This Xbox, select Allow play to other devices.

2. Under Other devices, select Allow connections from any device or Only profiles with session started on this Xbox.

Connect the PC with Windows 10 to the Xbox One.

After checking that the console configuration is correct, you will have to establish a connection from the Windows 10 PC to the Xbox One console from the PC's Xbox application:

1. On the PC, start the Xbox application.

2. Select Connection in the panel on the left side of the screen.

3. The Xbox application will search the home network if Xbox One consoles are available. Select the name of the console you want to connect to.

Note: All Xbox One consoles have the default name "MyXboxOne". If you have multiple consoles, you should change the name of each of them in order to correctly identify the console you are trying to connect to.

You can change the name of the console if you press the Xbox button to open the guide, select System> Console information> Console information, and then select the name of the console in the Name box.

4. Once connected, you will see that the icons change to indicate that you are connected. In addition, you will see the new options for transmission, power and multimedia control. Now you can also control the console remotely.

techfreak_ say:
Blocking occurs at varying degrees.  Generally it is not considered blocking, simply considered "bandwidth throttling", "protocol filtering","packet shaping" or some other similar naming convention.  This type of "network management" I have personally done before on large networks.  Just like people utilizing QoS (Quality of Service) to prioritize VoIP traffic in their local network, an ISP can perform the same to deprioritize or greatly limit the bandwidth of any specific traffic it is programmed to detect.  This can cause this traffic to greatly suffer in quality and create delays, all the time or at certain pre-programmed times of the day or year.  Some countries mandate their ISPs to do this to maintain a level of dominance in the communication market.   Other countries outlaw or severely limit the ability to shape traffic in this manner at the ISP level.  Certain network tunneling masks the traffic being sent and received, possibly bypassing such restrictions.  This is why people can have greater success using a VPN (Virtual Private Network) as long as that VPN is not greatly bandwidth-limited by the host.  This is simply an observation, not a justification for using VPNs or other tunneling to solely bypass ISP/network restrictions.
