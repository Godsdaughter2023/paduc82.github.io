johnwaddell4155 say:

For anyone who using Outlook 2016 with an IMAP Office 365 account here is how I got the Clutter Folder to appear, with no help from MS.

1.  Make sure that your Office 365 Account has the appropriate permissions e.g. Owner.  Sign-in to Office 365 online, go to Mail, right click Clutter folder and select permissions, click add permissions and insert email address of Office 365 account.

2. Go into Outlook 2016, File, Account Settings and select current Office 365 Account and remove (take a note of all the settings first though if you have not stored them anywhere).

3. Close down Outlook.

4. Open Outlook.

5. Go to File, Account Settings and add new account, re-input your Outlook 365 Account. Follow instructions to close down Outlook and restart.  This will take a few moments and once started Outlook will start to synchronise to populate all your emails, folders etc.  You should now see that the Clutter Folder has appeared although initially empty, this will populate in a few moments.

6.  Go back in to Account Settings and make sure that your Office 365 Account is set to Default as mine wasn't.

7.  You should now have all your emails and folders, including the Clutter folder.

Hope this helps.

It looked really bad.  Fast forward to around the year 2000, in the early versions of Word for Windows (I know for sure it was present in WinWord 6.0, and I'm fairly sure it was present in earlier versions) Word added an "option" to "Do justification like WordPerfect". There is also input from a select group of technical resources, like long time MVPs (no not me <grin> )
