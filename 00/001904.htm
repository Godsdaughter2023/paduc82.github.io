daniel g. grau say:
By default, a new document has only one section (unless you've otherwise edited the Normal template). A section defines margins, headers, footers and page size, so if you want to change any of those for some pages as you work down in the document, you'd just add a new section.

To access those parameters, use the menu bar's Format > Document. You'll find an "Apply to" dropdown menu that let's you specify where to implement them in various parts of the document.

In there you'll also find two tabs at the top: Margins and Layout. Select the latter and place a checkmark on "Different first page," which will give you a separate header and footer for page 1.

For further details, see this.

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
Daniel is quite correct, but if your document has only 1 Section there is another approach that may be a bit quicker :-)

  1. On the first page, double-click the Header space
  2. In the Options group of the Header & Footer contextual tab check the box for Different First Page... The blue Header tab in the document will now read as First Page Header.
  3. Go to the Header area of the 2nd page [whose blue tab is simply labeled Header] to add your Header content. That content will appear in the Header of the remaining pages as well leaving the First Page Header blank.

juan_o789 say:

Hello

I am sorry to hear about your Skype issue. When you've said that the contacts disappeared, were you pertaining, when you logged in, your Skype account looks empty? If that is a yes, this is possible if you signed in to a different Skype account. Our workaround for this, is to sign out and then sign back in manually.

For more information about this, please go through this link: Why are my Skype contacts or credit missing?

For your other question about your Skype online status, that may look inaccurate. Please try to use this troubleshooting steps and let's know if it will work.

  1. Make sure that Skype is no longer running. Quit Skype.

  2. On your keyboard, press the Windows key + R key. Then, the run windows will appear.

  3. On the run window, type in %appdata%\Skype and hit enter. Look for a file named shared or shared.xml and delete it.

  4. Open the folder that has your Skype name.

  5. Locate and delete the config or config.xml

  6. Open Control Panel and look for Internet Options.

  7. Go to the advanced tab and select Reset.

  8. Put a check mark under "Delete Personal Settings", then hit okay and enter.

  9. Close it when prompted.

  10. Restart Skype.

Once you are done with this steps, please try to check your Skype application again.
