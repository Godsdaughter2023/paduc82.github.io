john mcghie say:

No.

We have been asking for this for 20 years.

You can of course repeat "multiple" rows at the top of a table: as many rows as you select contiguously. You can use this to fake the continued line: but it means splitting your table into two pieces, and manually adding the "more" rows.  Too much work and maintenance.

I believe that most readers that we write books for do not need the "more" and "continued" tags on tables.  That was a really ancient device that was used back in the days of hot metal composition with moveable type, as a sign to the compositor that they should continue the table structure onto the next plate.

Readers have never needed it: they will figure it out by visual inspection (or they shouldn't be left alone with your book in case they hurt themselves... :-) )
