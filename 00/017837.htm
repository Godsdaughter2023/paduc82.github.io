a. user say:

Hello Raj:

Thank you for posting to the forum regarding the issues you are having syncing videos.  The error you are getting is most often associated with DRM protected content or corrupted files.

If this is happening with videos taken on the phone this is likely that the video files have become corrupted and may not be able to be synced.  At this point I am assuming you are trying to sync from the phone to a computer using the Zune software.  If that is not correct, please let us know.

If I am correct the next thing I would suggest you try is to see if the video can be uploaded from the phone to OneDrive (formerly SkyDrive).  You can try this by going to your camera roll on the phone where the video should be found.  Select the video and tap the (...), followed by share on SkyDrive.  From here you will have the option to add a caption and then upload. 

If the above works, you can then download from OneDrive by going to your computer and signing into http://onedrive.com with the same account used on your phone.  From here you can download the video to your computer.  This would effectively do the same thing as syncing it to the computer.

If the upload fails this is further evidence that the video file is corrupted.  One other thing you can try is to go to the video on your phone and tap (...) followed by share.  Select the Hotmail option and send this video as an email attachment to yourself.  You can then check you email on the computer and download the video file to the PC.

If you get this error with other content you attempt to sync, music and pictures specifically, there may be a larger issue with your phone that needs to be addressed with a soft or hard reset.

  • Soft Reset:  Press and hold the volume keys, power button and camera together for 3-5 seconds.
  • Hard Reset: To reset the phone, tap settings > about > reset your phone.  Keep in mind that this will set the phone back to it's factory settings.  Media can be backed up to your PC, http://support.xbox.com/en-US/zune/software/syncing-media.  Contacts and calendar events should be backed up to the cloud.  You can verify this by going to http://contacts.live.com and http://calendars.live.com.  The information on your phone should be listed here.  Text message history and game progress will be reset. 

Please let us know if you have any other questions or concerns we can assist you with.

Mark W.

Different page number formats in different sections - Microsoft Community

vagulus say:

Different page number formats in different sections

At https://onedrive.live.com/redir?resid=CAD78704467531B3!145&authkey=!AFsGWS2RC-0uRD0&ithint=file%2cdocx there is a three-page document divided into three New Page Sections.  I would like to format the page numbers differently in each section.

Section 1: no page number (no header or footer).

Section 2: pages numbered in lowercase roman numerals starting at page 1 e.g. Page i of iv

Section 3: pages numbered in Arabic numerals starting at 1 e.g. Page 1 of 1

Would someone please step me through this.  I have tried all the formatting tricks I know but I am not having any luck.

Thanks

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

You need to unlink the headers\footers in the second and third Sections from those in the previous Sections and then insert the page number fields in each of the second and third Sections in the location where you want the numbering to appear, using CTRL+F9 to insert the field delimiters { }

{ PAGE } of { SECTIONPAGES }

and then via the Format Page Number dialog available from the Page Number dropdown in the Header & Footer section of the Header & Footer Tools>Design tab of the ribbon select the number format and set the Page Numbering to Start at 1

For the second section, you will need to add a \* roman switch to the SECTIONPAGES field to get the total of pages in that section displaying in lower case Roman numerals

{ SECTIONPAGES \* roman }

Use Alt+F9 to toggle off the display of the field codes.
