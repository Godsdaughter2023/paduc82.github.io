a. user say:

You don't want to backup your hard drive to CDs. You'll need dozens of them, maybe hundreds. And if any one of them gets lost or scratched, there goes your backup.

You want to backup to an external USB hard drive, which is also known as a portable drive. These are inexpensive, and they'll make it easy for you to store multiple backups.

To get started, copy your Documents, Music, Pictures and Videos folders to your portable drive. Just copy and paste - it's that simple.

Once you get the hang of that you'll want to move up to a third-party backup program, which will make it so easy to backup you'll ask yourself "why didn't I know about this before?" A third-party program will let you:

  • create and save different types of backup jobs
  • schedule backup jobs to occur automatically, even while you're sleeping
  • send you messages so you'll know how each backup went
  • setup a retention schedule, so you'll automatically maintain the most recent 10 backups (for example) or the last week of backups

and much more.

Soon you'll be ready to take backup to the next level, with image backup software. An image is an exact copy of your hard drive. That means everything, including what you need to make the image bootable, all packed into one file. With an image backup you have a complete copy of your computer, ready to restore any time somethings goes badly. And trust me, things can go very badly. I know this because I ... uh ... read it in a book  ;-)
