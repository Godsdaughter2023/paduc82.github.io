How do i redirect my existing hotmail to a new account - Microsoft Community

I have a hotmail account with my maiden name in it can i set up a new hotmail account however have the mail redirected from my old one also?

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

What I would do is add an alias address to your existing account.  Visit https://account.live.com/names/Manage and click the link that says "Add alias" at the bottom of the page.  Add the address you want.  Now, visit http://mail.live.com/, click Options>More mail settings>Your email addresses".  You should see your alias there.  At the very bottom you'll see a dropdown labeled "Default 'From' Address".  Change that to the new alias so that people see your new address when you send messages.  Mail sent to either address will arrive in the same Inbox.  If you wish, you can create a rule that moves messages sent to your old address to a separate folder.

 

An alternative is to rename your account, which will change your primary Microsoft ID to the new address and make your existing address an alias of that new ID.  This is also done at https://account.live.com/Names/Manage.  Normally, this would be the preferred choice, but Microsoft has been having tons of trouble with that process and people who have renamed their accounts have found all of their mail unrecoverably wiped out.  I now advise against account renaming.  I think its best to modify your account as little as possible and adding an alias is a fairly minimal change.

 

Just a word of caution on adding an alias as well, though.  Some peope have reported message loss when all they did was add an alias (at least, that's what they say they did).  I've added ten different aliases and I've never had a problem with message loss, but I want to to be aware.

I already created a new outlook account, now trying to insert that under the alias it's telling me name already taken. I actually didn't know of this process you mentioned before creating the new mail, how do i then proceed concerning this?

Thanks

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

You can't turn an existing account into an alias of another account.  What you could do, however, is:

  1. Forward the old account to the new account by logging into the old account, clicking the Options gear, then Options, then "Email forwarding" and forwarding the messages to the new address.
  2. Importing the messages from the old account into the new account by logging into the new account, clicking the Options gear, then Options, then "Import email accounts", then "Other email provider", and supplying the old address and password.  While I haven't tried this myself, it should work and there are other import options (like importing via POP) if you click "Advanced options", then "My provider doesn't support IMAP".  Outlook.com does support IMAP and that's why I believe it should work.  Note, however, that if you have two-step authentication enabled for the old account, you'll need to generate an app password in order for the import process to log into the old account successfully.
  3. Transfer your Contacts from the old account by logging into http://people.live.com/, clicking Manage, then "Export for Outlook.com and other services", saving the OutlookContacts.csv file to your PC, logging out of the old account, logging into the new account at http://people.live.com/ and clicking "Start import" from the "Import contacts section in the right-hand pane, selecting Outlook.com from the list of choices, then browsing to the CSV file you saved when you exported.  Note: if you have conncted any social networks to your old account (i.e., Facebook, LinkedIn, Google+), this export will not include contacts from those services.  Only contacts added directly to your Outlook.com account will be exported from the old account.
  4. Transfer your Calendar by logging into http://calendar.live.com/ using the old credentials, clicking Share in the menu bar, selecting your calendar from the drop-down you'll see, clicking "Links to event details", copying the link in the "Import into other calendar applications (ICS)" field, logging out of the old account, opening a new web browser tab or window, pasting the link you copied into the address bar, changing "webcals" to "https", then pressing Enter to download and save a file called calendar.ics.  After that, log into http://calendar.live.com/ with the new account, clicking Import on the menu bar, selecting "Import into an existing calendar", then browsing to the ICS file you saved previously, then clicking Import.

Once these steps are finished, you should have all your data moved over.  You can then either count yourself finished and after a year, the old account will be closed, or you can deliberately close the old account.  No matter which of these choices you take, were I you, I'd delete all of the data in the old account - email, contacts, and calendar events.  Outlook.com allows reuse of terminated account names after a security period following their deletion.  You don't want to allow the ability of anyone who requests the old name to inadvertently get your old data, in the event it doesn't get cleared properly. 

You also want to make sure that if you used the old address as credentials for any other services (i.e., Facebook, Twitter, et al) or for password recovery settings, that you update those services or you will never be able to recover your password for that service, since you will no longer control the old address.  In fact, you should change this data, if it exists, before you close or lose control of the old account.
