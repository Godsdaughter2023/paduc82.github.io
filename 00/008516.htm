john w. vinson say:

Multitable queries are very often in fact not updateable. What one would typically do in this situation is to use a Form based on the "parent" table, with one or more Subforms based on the related tables, using the SID as the master/link field. It would NOT be typical - and in fact would be bad design - to fill in empty "placeholder" records in the related tables; the SID would automatically populate when you enter data in the subform. This consultant should be flogged with a wet noodle!

However... it sounds like you may be using the SID as the Primary Key of all three tables, which would almost certainly be a mistake. What are the three tables? What real-life entities does each table represent? How are they related?

john w. vinson say:

If each Subject has one and only one SID, and each SID applies to one and only one Subject, then the SID is certainly at least a candidate Primary Key for the Subject information table. At the very least I'd make it required and uniquely indexed in that table.

The medical history and visits table, on the other hand, would presumably have zero, one, or (typically) multiple records for each subject; if SID is the primary key of the Subject data table then it could also be used as a Foreign Key (non-unique!) in these tables. You can also use the (autonumber?) Primary Key to link to a long integer foreign key (in which case the child tables should NOT contain the redundant SID). In either case, you could use a Form based on Subject, with two Subforms, one based on History and the other on Visits - certainly NOT a single form based on all three tables.

Do note that in the US (and most other jurisdictions) clinical trial data with personal identification information is covered by the VERY stringent HIPAA patient privacy regulations. Unless this database has proper encryption and built in security (not trivial in Access), and/or is on a secured server, you may be in violation of these rules. I've worked with clinical research data (in a heavily secured corporate Oracle database) and a whole lot of effort went into security; I'd be reluctant to trust Access to be compliant!
