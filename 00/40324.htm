craiglong say:

Hi Drew Angell,

With Office 365 Personal you can only have 1TB of data in your OneDrive.  With one of the Office 365 for Business plans you can have unlimited storage.  You can check out the plans below:

https://products.office.com/en-us/compare-all-microsoft-office-products-test?tab=1

https://products.office.com/en/onedrive-for-business/compare-onedrive-for-business-plans

Personally I have Office 365 Home with 6TB of OneDrive storage.  It's really designed for 6 family members, each with 1TB of OneDrive storage.  So in order for me personally to use all 6TB of storage I need 6 accounts.   I share an empty folder with editing rights from each account to my main account.  Then I sync all 6TB by adding these 6 folders, each 1TB, to my OneDrive on my main account.  Then I set up OneDrive sync which downloads these folders to my computer.

You can move your OneDrive folder from it's default location to your external drive.  On one of my computers I have done that.

I wouldn't copy OneDrive files from one computer to the other computer.  I would setup OneDrive on my computer and let the files download from the online website.  It could be that the properties of files on different computers may be different and that may cause sync problems.

Check out this article about restoring previous versions of files:

https://support.office.com/en-us/article/restore-a-previous-version-of-a-file-in-onedrive-159cad6d-d76e-4981-88ef-de6e96c93893

I don't have a backup of files other than my OneDrive but I have 2 computers with up to date files and older versions of my OneDrive stored away. 

I hope that helps.

https://support.office.com/en-us/article/learn-about-onedrive-files-on-demand-0e6860d3-d9f3-4971-b321-7092438fb38e

If you don't have local storage space somewhere then downloading is the only way to get access to your files.


You can connect to a network drive to access files on another device or computer.

People use Network Attached Storage (NAS) devices to access files on the local network.  I don't use a NAS because I have desktop PCs with lots of storage space.  Everything is also synced in OneDrive. 

Also I can access files on another PC on my local network if the need arises but I prefer not to do that.  

Another way to get files from another device:  https://youtu.be/r3LaMOMFRP8

With most of the OneDrive and Office 365 plans you can't go beyond 1TB per account.  But I can sync 6TB to my main account with my plan using the workaround I described.  

You will have problems with OneDrive on an external drive if you intend to unplug the external drive and then access OneDrive without that drive plugged in.

  1. Shut the computer down.
  2. Disconnect the power cord from the computer itself.
  3. Disconnect the power cable from the hard drive in question.
  4. Reconnect the power cord to the computer itself.
  5. Boot the computer up to see if the drive sound was audible before. And,
  6. Then repeat, only reconnect the hard drive and listen for it to spin up.
  7. Switch power cables with a device like a CDROM or DVDROM drive, so that you are using a known-good power cable.
  8. Check the power supply on your computer to determine whether it is providing sufficient electrical power to operate the drives and devices you have in your computer.
  9. If the drive is still not spinning, connect it in another computer if possible and check.
