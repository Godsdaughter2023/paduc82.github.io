Outlook Web and Outlook desktop work differently

In the web version, suggestions will be made from both <People> and <sent items> whereas when you start to type a name in the Outlook desktop To/Cc/Bcc boxes, it retrieves the names from the auto-complete cache (recipients of sent messages) which is completely separate and distinct from the Outlook AddressBook (which appears when you click on the To/CC/or Bcc button).

If you click on the To button in Outlook desktop, don't your Outlook.com contacts appear in the AddressBook? (assuming you only have one contact folder defined to be a member of the AB and the Outlook.com contact folder is set to display when the AB is opened).

nigelsharman say: Some contacts do, yes, but many others don't. For example, I have an email group that I send messages to regularly. karl timmermans (outlook mvp 2012-2018) say:
>>
As soon as I start typing the group name in web Outlook, the rest of the email address autocompletes. But it is not there when I press "to" in office outlook and search for it.
<<

First step in getting an answer is making sure that we have the question clearly defined and not changed along the way.

The initial post made no mention of a "Contact List" as used in Outlook.com (web) or "Contact Group" as it's referred to in Outlook desktop.

In terms of Outlook.com - when a contact list is added - a + appears before the "Contact List" name and when the + is clicked, it expands the list and the members of the list are added to the To field. At least that's the way it works in the version of Outlook.com I'm using (the new version - post beta).

In Outlook desktop, if the Contact Group is not in the list of contacts when you click on the To button, simply means that the Contact Group doesn't exist in the contact folder that is being shown in the AddressBook. It either exists or it doesn't in the contact folder. If it exists and is selected, it too will be added to the To box with a + sign beside it and if clicked, the list will be expanded in the To box.

Now as mentioned in the original response, Outlook,com will suggest entries from <both> your People list <and> from the messages you've sent previously. If the Contact List no longer existed as a Contact List it would still be suggested from Sent items if it was previously used. In Outlook desktop - clicking on To <only> shows the Address Book (the equivalent of the Outlook.com <People> list> whereaas typying the name in the To box, <only> the auto-complete cache will be searched.

linley meslier say:
Instead of synchronizing, there is a better and faster way to transfer your pictures from device to PC and vice versa. It is possible to connect the device and make it appear as a removable disk or mass storage device. The same as connecting a USB drive to the computer. To do that, go to Start > Settings > USB Connection mode on the phone. Choose Mass Storage and My storage in the drop down list. If you have a storage card, it will alse appear in that list. Now, all you have to do, is connect your device, open Windows Explore on the computer to copy/paste your pictures to the location you want. Again, this is a much faster way to transfer a lot of files rather than using WMDC to sync.
