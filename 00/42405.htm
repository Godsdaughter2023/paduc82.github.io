arminatorx say:

While your dad could give you "adult rights" in a sense that your account can for example talk to internet strangers, and play adult games, the real "adultness" is determined by the birthdate of your account that was entered when your account was created.

If your account hasn't reached legal adult age according to that birthdate (usually over 18 years, except Japan and Korea, where you have to be over 20), you are still considered a child/teen, and your parents have to manage your account. 

Please go to https://account.xbox.com/settings and if you get instructions on how to add your account to a parent account, you still have a child/teen account, and you need a parent to go to https://account.microsoft.com/family and add you to the family. Then your parents can go to https://account.xbox.com/settings and modify your account's online and privacy settings, until you are over the age of 18, where you then can leave the family yourself.
