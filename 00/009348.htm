talking moose say:
The Sent Date is always the date stamped by the sender's computer. This is embedded in the message source itself.

The Received Date is always the time when you last received or copied the message. This is not part of the message itself but rather your own time stamp when the message was created on your computer in Outlook. Copying a message will mean the copy gets a different Received Date.

I always use Sent Date to keep things in better perspective.
