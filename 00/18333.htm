john korchok say:

Usually, there is very little space between the two unless your footer contains something that is creating space above it, or unless your footnote style has space after (which you have already eliminated).

Items in the footer to look for include extra carriage returns above whatever content is there, footer text with space before added to the paragraph style, a shape with a text wrap applied to it, or a table. It will be easier to spot if you use File>Options>Display and check Show all formatting marks.

suzanne s. barnhill say:
To add to what John said, there should be no extra space between footnotes and footer if you have the footnotes set to "Bottom of page" rather than "Below text." But for a complete rundown on the factors affecting space at the bottom of a page, see http://WordFAQs.mvps.org/BottomLine.htm.
