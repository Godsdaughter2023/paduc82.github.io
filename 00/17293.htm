  1. Defintion of 'page break': this is a break indicating that any text which follows after the break should be displayed on the next page in your document.
  2. Unable to type a letter with header and footer on first page only: use the "Different First Page" option. In Word 2007, create a new document, and add some text until you end up with at least two pages. Go to the top of your document and double click in the header section to activate the header. Type in something in your header. Now, while your header is active, there should be a new contextual tab on the ribbon, called "Header & Footer Tools" "Design". On that tab, there is an "Options" group, with a checkbox labeled "Different First Page". Make sure it is checked. Now, close the header using the "Close Header and Footer" button on the ribbon. If you check the result, you will see that your header is only on the first page, and not on the second page.
    By using section breaks, you can have different headers/footers for different parts of your document. In such a case, you will have to use the "Link to Previous" button on the same ribbon tab.
