swischner say:
Sometimes (I only experienced it twice after years of intense OneNote use) parts of a page or section may come corrupted, although there is no visible fault on it.

Best practice here is: Create a new empty section and copy (better not move) all pages from the one that refuses to sync to the new one. You may use drag and drop from the unfolded and pinned notebook list holding the CTRL key or use the context menu command of the pages. Check if the new section syncs. If so, you may password protect it and delete the old one.

If the new section also refuses to sync (unlikely but possible), do the same but copy the pages one by one and wait for the sync. Eventually it will fail and you identified the faulty page. Using the same method (create a new empty page and move the content from the faulty one to the new one using the clipboard) you should be able to save all data and get a working section in the end.

How to divide column in excel. - Microsoft Community

tony_etpn say:

How to divide column in excel.

I want to divide a column or a row with 30 numbers on each tab into 3 rows or 3 column of 10.
I have millions of tabs need to be divided into several rows of 10.
How can I do that? Please help. Thank you.
gord dibben say:

Tony

An Excel worksheet has 1,048,576 rows. . . .not millions.

If you have a single column filled with data and wanted to split that into columns you would wind up with data in 16384 columns in 64 rows.

To get a single column into 3 columns enter a formula in B1

=INDEX($A:$A,(ROWS($1:1)-1)*3+COLUMNS($A:B)-1)

Drag/copy across to D1.

Select B1:D1 and drag/copy down until you get zeros appearing.

Change the 3 to get more columns and fewer rows.

i.e.  In B1, change the 3 to 6 then drag across to G1 and down
