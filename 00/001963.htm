It isn't part of Word, but you already have predictive text in Windows 10. It's part of the onscreen keyboard.

The Writing Style option will be disabled whenever the proofing language at the insertion point is one for which the proofing tools are not installed. I think (but I'm not sure) that it may also be disabled if the proofing tools for that language are installed, but include only a spelling dictionary and not a grammar checker.

If the rightmost column says Not installed, you can click that link to launch your browser and download the necessary proofing tools (which, unlike in previous Office versions, are free).

a. user say:

Hi,

To answer your questions,

Yes, You can always create a new Outlook.com alias for yourself. Please use the steps below to help you with the same:

  1. Click on the Gear icon, next to your name on the top right hand corner.
  2. Click on Options.
  3. Under Managing your account, Click on Create an Outlook.com Alias.
  4. You may choose an Alias now.

Once an alias has been created for your account, you may use any of these email addresses to sign in at Outllook.com. Both Email addresses will direct you towards the same Outlook.com account.

The top right hand corner of your Outlook.com webpage should be displaying your name and not an email address. Let us know if you see otherwise.

If you send an email now, the recipient will see the alias set as primary, which is currently your third party email. To set your new alias as the primary, please follow the steps below:

  1. Click on the Gear icon, next to your name on the top right hand corner.
  2. Click on Options.
  3. Under Managing your account, Click on Aliases: manage or choose a primary.
  4. Under your new alias, click on Make Primary.

Note: This change may take upto 48 hours to take effect.

Once the transition is complete, any email that you send using Outlook.com will be sent using your new alias and you will be able to receive response on the same.

Hope I have answered all your questions. Feel free to reply if you need more details or have any other queries regarding Outlook.com or Windows, we are here to help you.
