swischner say:
OneNote (and its editor) have their very own data format which is not as "feature rich" and also not compatible to the internal MS Word doc/docx format.

So there is no way to import Word documents and maintain all formatting or the chapter/subchapter/heading structure.

There is one small trick that might at least help you a little bit. There is a method to automatically create OneNote pages (not subpages though) based on a list of page titles (e.g. a list of your headings in Word which should be able to get created in Word. I'm thinking of creating a temporary TOC and copying the list of headings or some similar method like collapsing all headings as described here: https://support.office.com/en-us/article/collap...).

If you have that list copied over to a OneNote page, you can use it to create (empty!) pages with the corresponding page titles. There are instructions on this blog article (written by myself): http://onenote-blog.com/neue-seiten-automatisch...
Don't worry about the German link title; the article is in English.
Also, the article uses a list of page names from Excel but the procedure is identical to copy a list of names from Word.

I'm sorry that I have no better solution for you than creating empty pages with the correct titles and copying and pasting the contents manually afterwards. But at least this method eliminates one inconvenient step.
