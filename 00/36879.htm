wunders say:

This isn't a Windows problem.  This is a problem with your monitor.

 

Your monitor has an optimum resolution...  perhaps 1280x960  with a 60 Hz refresh rate.  When you start in Safe Mode, the resolution on the video card is forced into a mode that virtually any monitor will support (640 x 480 ?).  This is done to allow recovery in case you accidentally set your monitor resolution to something that isn't supported and you end up with a blank screen.  When your monitor detects this less-than-optimal resolution coming from your computer, it takes it upon itself to display a notice on the screen to inform you that you will get a better looking screen if you adjust the display resolution on your computer to match your monitor.  This may not be possible in Safe Mode,  so look at your monitor manual and/or settings to see if there is some way to deactivate the warning message.

 

HTH,
