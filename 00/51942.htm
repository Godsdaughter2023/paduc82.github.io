1  Check why it is waking up

When you find it on, open a Command window by right-clicking on the Start button and selecting it then enter powercfg -lastwake.  The response should tell you what woke the computer up last time.

There might be something else waking the computer up but one known item is Automatic maintenance.  You can stop it waking the computer up routinely and you also can alter the time is runs but, if you do not let it run at all, you will find after a couple of weeks that it will start running while you are using the computer instead and using up your CPU for hours on end [no schedule is published for Windows 10 but, for Windows 8, Automatic maintenance forced itself to run while you had the computer on after 15 days]. 

To adjust Automatic maintenance -

  • Right-click on the Start button,
  • Control panel,
  • Click on View by in the upper right and select Small icons, just so you can see everything,
  • Security & maintenance,
  • In the main body of the window, click on the dropdown arrow to the right of the heading Maintenance,
  • Click on Change maintenance settings,
  • Clear the checkbox for Allow scheduled maintenance to wake up my computer at the scheduled time,
  • Click on the time entry and select a time when the computer will be on but you don't mind not being able to use it for a while,
  • Click on OK and give Admin permission for this change.

2  Deal with it not sleeping afterwards

If you search for sleep problems in this forum you will find a whole host of problem reports.  You could easily spend weeks studying them yet still get nowhere.  You might be better off just considering these suggestions in the first instance [when it comes to investigating sleep problems, everything is just a suggestion and nothing is a definitive solution] -

2.1  Many sleep problems are caused by problems with the display driver.  If this is the cause I would also expect to see your computer having problems going to sleep when you told it to as well as it refusing to sleep after the timeout period you have set.  Nevertheless, even if only to eliminate it as a cause, download the latest display driver from the computer maker or display card maker and install it - this should deal both with cases where the driver had become corrupted and with the existing driver not being compatible with the latest Windows updates [assuming, of course, that your display card is still being actively supported by its maker].  Please note that doing this achieves much more than simply getting Windows to check for updated drivers as this would not achieve anything if the driver is up-to-date yet corrupted. 

2.2  I have a computer that needs to be rebooted each day for it to go to sleep reliably.  This problem has persisted for years despite all updates / reinstallations of the display driver or the OS and no problems ever show up in memory tests.  I have wasted several days testing this, that & the other to no avail.  Try rebooting every day and see if it makes a difference.  I have never found a logical explanation for the problem so I have just carried on with my rebooting habit. 

2.3  See the guidance by SpiritX.

Best of luck,

Denis

Hi JackSmith0,

 

Good Day!

 

When you use Skype for Windows Desktop, you will have the option to automatically add people from your address book who are also on Skype.

 

If you chose to automatically add friends, you might notice that when you visit your People list, you'll see suggested contacts that weren’t already in your Skype contact list.

 

These suggested contacts are people who are in your other address books who are also on Skype. Don't worry -- we didn't send invites to them, and you can remove them if you'd like by deleting individual contacts or turning the feature off in your Skype settings.

To turn this feature off:

  1. Click Skype > Privacy.
  2. Under Privacy settings, select how people find me.
  3. Uncheck your phone number or click on the Delete icon to remove it completely.

When you tap a suggested contact in your People list, you might see a button to Connect on Skype. This is because we haven’t confirmed that you know one another, so you’ll need to send an invite before you can chat and call on Skype.
