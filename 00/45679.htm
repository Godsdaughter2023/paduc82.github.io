cbarnhorst say:

Welcome to the world of SSD.  They are not like your father's HDDs.  Some traditional practices are inappropriate.

Does Adata offer a migration kit?  Something like Crucial's kit,

http://www.thinq.co.uk/2010/7/14/crucial-promises-easy-ssd-migration/ 

In my opinion you are better off reinstalling Win 7 (the installer will adjust the installation for SSD), reinstalling your software, and migrating your files and settings with WET.

From what I have been reading SATA to SSD migrations are tricky.  But what do I know, right?  :)

Here are some threads about using Acronis.  There seems to be a partition misallignment problem.  It causes severe perf problems.  That's why I recommend a new installation.

http://forum.acronis.com/forum/12866

http://forum.acronis.com/forum/8703

It does not appear that doing a traditional image cloning, at least with ATIH, is a good idea.
