pamcaswell say: In W2003 and before, in use styles are any that were ever created, modified, or used in a document and its template.  They are listed regardless of whether the style is actually applied in the document.  I remember learning this from a MS support (or KB) article that I read years ago, but I can only find this one today.  (BTW a similar support article was issued for W97.)  Although it does not mention user-created styles in the template, my experience is that they do carry into new documents as in-use styles, unless they were deleted from the template before the document was created. 


When I was first learning about the Styles pane changes in W2007 (not sure whether it was  with the beta or the final version), I checked out the difference between in use and in current document.  At that time, in use was the same as in W2003, and in current document was styles actually applied, created, or modified in the current document.  In W2010, it seems to be the reverse. 

Nevertheless, to get these not-really-in-use styles out of the styles pane, you must delete them from the document or template, well most of them. Both user-defined and  nonmandatory built-in styles can be deleted from a document in w2007/10.   The mandatory (MS also calls them "standard") built-in styles are, AFAIK, Headings 1 to 9, Table Normal, and Normal. 

The macro I use deletes unused custom styles. If you use such a macro, be sure that the styles you want to keep are actually applied—that includes numbering list styles and custom table styles.  I manually delete each unused nonmandatory built-in style.  I also have a macro that deletes all unused styles except Headings 1-9 and Normal, but it stops for approval of each deletion, and so is much slower.

Another method:  If you have a clean template, paste all but the last paragraph of a messy document into a new document created from the template. That can leave behind most unused  styles. Once again, make sure all the styes you want are actually applied and that the ones you don't want are not applied before you paste.

Here's one macro you might consider:  http://word.tips.net/T001337_Removing_Unused_Styles.html
