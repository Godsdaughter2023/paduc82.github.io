At logon time Magic Jack probably gets invoked from here:

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Run

When you invoke it manually then it automatically creates a value under the Run key which is why it's back again at the next reboot. You could trick it by replacing the current data in that registry location by this one:

c:\Windows\System32\cacls.exe

Cacls.exe will not do anything useful (or damaging!) when used without any parameters. It is, of course, possible that Magic Jack checks that data and replaces it with the correct value . . .

halima s say:

Hi ctctc,

You can only use this feature when you connect camera to the computer to transfer photos to the computer.

If your camera can detect whether you take pictures vertically or horizontally (usually by using a sensor that can tell how the camera is oriented), your computer can automatically rotate JPEG pictures to the correct orientation when they are imported. If you use this option, you will not have to manually rotate pictures that appear sideways on your computer, and it does not affect the image quality of your pictures.

To turn on automatic picture rotation

1. Open Windows Photo Gallery by clicking the Start button, clicking All Programs, and then clicking Windows Photo Gallery.
2. Click the File menu, and then click Options.
3. Click the Import tab.
4. Check the Rotate pictures on import check box, and then click OK.

For more information refer the below link
Windows Photo Gallery: frequently asked questions
http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows-vista/Windows-Photo-Gallery-frequently-asked-questions

However you may also rotate the pictures that are already available in the windows photo gallery by opening each photo and rotating manually.

1. Open Windows Photo Gallery by clicking the Start button, clicking All Programs, and then clicking Windows Photo Gallery.
2. Click the picture you want to rotate.
3. Click one of the Rotate buttons to turn the picture 90 degrees clockwise or counterclockwise.



Hope this helps!
