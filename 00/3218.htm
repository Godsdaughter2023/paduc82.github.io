kristine arp say:

Hi Robert,

 

 

IMAP is designed to allow the users to read the emails via the email service. As a result, the users can check emails from different devices anywhere they are.

 

POP is designed to download all new messages from the email service. After the email is downloaded, it will be stored into one device and will be deleted from the email service. This means that the users will not be able to see the previously downloaded emails if they will be using a different device.

 

Here’s an article that talks about the difference between IMAP and POP.

 

That being said, using POP3 server will not actually help you with your request. Aside from buying more space on your server, you can also create an offline folder in Outlook 2016 desktop app. This way you can move your emails to the offline folder before deleting the emails from the server. Follow these steps:

 

  1. Click File.
  2. Choose Info.
  3. Select Account Settings.
  4. Click Account Settings.
  5. Click the Data Files tab.
  6. Click Add.
  7. Enter the offline folder name and hit OK.
  8. Manually move the emails to the Offline folder.

 

Please take note that this workaround is only applicable to the Outlook desktop app. You will only have issues creating Outlook offline folder if you have no permission to do so.

 

If you have other questions, feel free to reach out to us.

I suggest you upgrade to Activesync 4.5.

 

Before connection the device, open Activesync on your device and click Menu > Options. Select your the partnership with the old PC and delete it. Reboot your device.

 

Just a safe measure, locate a file called Pim.vol or simply Pim on your device. Make a copy of this file, which, btw, contains all your data.

 

Connect your device to sync.
