lemp say:

The directions assume that you booted using your Windows 7 disk or a System Repair Disk and selected Command Prompt from the System Recovery Options menu.

The command prompt in the Recovery Environment will have a prompt that begins with drive X, like this:

X:\windows\system32>

bcdedit should work, but here's another way.  Just type regedit at the prompt.

When regedit opens, the registry that is being edited is the one that was loaded from your Windows 7 disk, and modifying that won't help you.  That's why you need to load the "hive" of the registry that's on the system partition of your hard drive.

So, in the Registry Editor, select HKey_Local_Machine and click the File menu.  Hopefully, this time the "Load Hive" command will be active.  Click it and you should see a Windows Explorer window.  If "Computer" isn't already selected, select it from the left pane:

At this point, you have to figure out which drive is the one where Windows is located (normally your C:\ drive).  If you can't tell by looking at the size, you'll have to select a drive and look at its contents.

Once you've found the Windows directory, browse to Windows\system32\config and click on "SYSTEM"

Give the hive-to-be loaded a name, such as offline or temp

Here's an important step that I forgot earlier.  When you open the hive you just loaded, you'll notice that there is no "CurrentControlSet" but rather only "ControlSet001" and "ControlSet002".  A little lower down, you'll see a key named "Select".  Click that and look at the value for the "Current" Reg_DWORD.  If it's 1, you want to make your modifications to ControlSet001 and if it's 2, you want ControlSet001.

Select the proper control set, delete the UpperFilters and LowerFilters values from the key I described earlier (both values may not be present).

Then close regedit and reboot.
