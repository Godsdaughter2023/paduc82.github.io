a. user say:

The 8191SE is an internal card so you'll have to open up the laptop to see it. There's no point in trying to replace it because, since it's a proprietary part, you won't find a third-party replacement.

You'll have to buy a USB WiFi adapter that is supported on Windows 10. That means giving up one of the USB ports on your laptop for the adapter.

USB WiFi adapters are very common and pretty much all the same. Buy one of the major brand names, e.g., Netgear, Linksys, Asus, D-Link.

The only detail to know is that you must match the WiFi specification of your router. For example, if your router is 802.11n then you need to buy an 802.11n USB adapter; and if your router broadcasts on both the 2.4 GHz band and the 5 GHz band you'll need a USB adapter that does the same. You'll find these details in the documentation that came with the laptop. There are other technical details but they only matter to network geeks.

I don't know what is available in Ireland. I replaced the RTL8188CE in my son's Toshiba laptop (that shipped with Windows 8) with an Asus USB-N53 adapter. A good place to ask is in the forums of the SmallNetBuilder website. That's where the experts (and not-so-experts) go.

Of course, you can always use an Ethernet cable at home.
