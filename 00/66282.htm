I'm afraid that I cannot say that this is a fix.  I'm not that much of a techie.  However, I did have this problem and I seem to have found a solution.

The symptoms in my case were that Windows 8 apps (Metro Apps) such as Calendar or People would start and then if I switched (Alt+Tab) to another application then tried to switch back, either the app (Calendar/People) would have vanished or would have gone back to the splash-screen they show when they are starting.

I think that's the key right there - things like Thunderbird and Pale Moon are 'proper' applications.  Those I had been starting from the Windows 8 start menu are known as Apps.

When these Apps are deselected (not closed, but switched away from) then after a period of time they go into a mode called "Suspeneded".  That's fine and if all were well with the world when they get switched back to, they would come out of their suspended mode at the precise point that they were at when they entered suspension.  However, sometimes these apps actually close when they are suspended.

From experimentation, this seemed (on my system at least) to be caused by my firewall (Comodo).  If I exit Comodo then try to run these apps, they seem to stay running for longer.

Like I say, I'm no expert, but I have found two programs that need to be allowed.  They are livecomms.exe and WWAHOST.exe.  I have made sure these two EXE files are classed as "Allowed Applications" and all seems to be running just fine (for now at least).

You might have a difference Firewall (or it could be some spyware or antivirus program) which causes similar symptoms.
