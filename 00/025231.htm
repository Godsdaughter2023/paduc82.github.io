scottgem say:

What are they registering for? It would appear to me that Registration is one entity, Workshops is another entity and workshops taken a third entity. And that third entity is a many to many relationship since a registrant can take multiple workshops and a workshop can have multiple registrants. So you should have:

tblRegistrations

RegistrationID (PK Autonumber)

FName

LName

other info about registrants

tblWorkshops

WorkshopID (PK Autonumber)

WorkshopName

tblWorkshopsTaken

WorkshopTakeID (PK Autonumber)

WorkshopID (FK)

RegistrantID (FK)

DateTaken

Fee

Your query of all people to take a specific workshop becomes easy:

SELECT tblRegistrant.Fname, tblRegistrant.LName, tblWorkshops.WorkshopName. tblWorkshopsTaken.DateTaken, tblWorkshopsTaken.,Fee

INNER JOIN on tblRegistrant.RegistrantID = tblWorkshopsTaken.RegistrantID, tblWorkshops.WorkshopID = tblWorkshopsTaken.WorkshopID

FROM tblRegistrans, tblWorkshops, tblWorkshopsTaken

WHERE tblWorkshops.WorkshopID = x;

To do this in Query Design mode add all three tables. Join then on RegistrantID and WorkshopID, then add the fields you want and set the criteria for the WorkshopID.
