bernd p. say:

Onenote works different from all other Office programs because Onenote has its own method of synching local changes to Onedrive. The other Office programs synch or update their files on file level. Onenote synchs only the changed parts to Onedrive, for performance and for enabling shared updates by different devices. Additionally Onenote uses a local (more or less hidden) cache for additional performance and for offline use.

This cache is not part of the local Onedrive folder. From the viewpoint of Onenote you can ignore the local Onedrive folder

Text to column and transpose - Microsoft Community

archway debbie say:

Text to column and transpose

I need to convert text to columns but may have too many entries for the number of columns in Excel. Is there a way to convert tect to columns and transpose at the smae time so they appear in the next column?
chip pearson say:

There is no built in way to do this. However, you can write code to do it. Below is some simple code to split the content of the active cell delimited by spaces and fill the active cell and the cells below it with the elements of the active cell.

 

Sub TextToRows()
    Dim SS() As String
    If Len(ActiveCell.Text) > 0 Then
        SS = Split(ActiveCell.Text, Space(1))
        ActiveCell.Resize(UBound(SS) - LBound(SS) + 1, 1).Value = _
            Application.Transpose(SS)
    End If
End Sub

john korchok say:

Make the action button something you would have on every page anyway, like your logo or wordmark. Here's how:

  1. Get the dimensions of the logo on your existing slide logo.
  2. Open View>Slide Master.
  3. Scroll up to the somewhat larger slide at the top of the thumbnails. This is the actual master slide.
  4. From Insert>Shapes, draw a rectangle the size of your logo.
  5. With the shape still selected, choose Drawing Tools Format>Shape Fill>Picture>From a File. Select your logo, click on Open. Your logo appears in place of the button.
  6. With the logo button still selected, choose Insert>Action.
  7. Select Hyperlink to: and type in your choice of search engine.
  8. Close Slide Master view.
  9. Go into Slide Show mode.
  10. Click on the logo on any slide that displays the master graphics. A browser window should open to the search engine.

On slides that don't use the master graphics, copy and paste the button to the Slide Layout for that slide. If your logo is an EMF or SVG with transparent areas, the button will be transparent. If your measurements were out, your logo will be distorted, because a Picture fill gets stretched to the size of the shape. You may be able to fix this with Format Picture>Crop.
