Turn your Xbox on. On the dashboard make your way to the "Settings" page. Under "Preferences", go to the middle tile under "Games & Apps". Once on the Dashboard, go back into "Settings" and "Preferences" and check back on the two options. This should fix all recently known associated problems with the Xbox One DVR. Hope it helps.

2. If I were to buy used dvd's to play games, can someone recommend any website here?

1. Do NOT follow Ezzn's suggestion about upgrading the HDD by opening the case and whatnot. 

You can NOT easily replace the harddrive, as the original Xbox 360 hard drives have a special security sector on it. So you can't just buy a regular PC 2.5'' hard drive, as it won't work. 

What you CAN do however is to simply buy an external USB hard drive up to 2TB capacity, and use that on the console. Simply plug it into an USB port of the console and in the settings under storage have the drive be prepared for storage. Be aware that this erases all data on the drive!!

2. We don't know in which country you live, so I can't really give a lot of recommendation. Ebay is an international marketplace for used goods. So you can surely find stuff there. 

If your country has a GameStop, they also often sell (overpriced) used games. 

Simply look in your local newspaper if there are flea markets or garage sales in your area. That's where you can get games really cheap. 

3.) What exactly do you mean by "heavy noise"?

The Xbox 360 has a lot of heat to get out of the small casing, so there are two fans in the case that cause a lot of noise. 

Additionally, some game DVDs (especially if you buy used game discs), might have become warped, and will "rumble" in the disc drive, causing additional noise. 

You can try to install some games on your (external USB-) hard drive. That way, you only need the disc in the drive as a "proof of purchase". The console will check the presence of the game disc during game launch, and then stop the drive. 

Most games have a save feature. It depends on the game if you have to reach a certain checkpoint to be able to save, or if you can instantly save at any time. 

You can then turn the console off and continue later from your last saved position. 

If you also have a Gold membership, I recommend using the Cloud Storage drive, in case the storage of your used console fails, or if you eventually want to move to a Xbox One or the upcoming next Xbox generation. 

Thanks to backwards compatibility, you can play some of the Xbox 360 games on the newer consoles, and if you have stored your savegame "in the cloud", the new console can use that. 

Also, if you have a friend with a Xbox 360, you can go over for a visit, and after you signed in with your account, can continue to play on his console.
