charles kenyon say:

First, nit-picking on terms. A header is something that goes at the top of pages, sometimes called a running head. A heading goes at the beginning of a logical section of text. Headings are imported into Tables of Contents.

The formatting of tables of contents is controlled by the TOC styles. I suspect that your TOC 1 style has a tab setting close to the beginning of the line that is interfering with the dot-leader tab to the right margin page number. If I am correct and you press the tab key before your page number you will get what you want. (This is just a quick way to be sure that this is the problem, not a solution.)

To make this automatic, you will need to remove the ordinary tab stop from the TOC 1 style.

guessed say:

IMO, the problem you are experiencing is due to a 'Jason tab' appearing in the TOC entries despite this tab probably not being part of the style. You can verify whether this diagnosis is correct by selecting the TOC and pressing F9 to update it. If the page number is sitting in the wrong place on very short entries then try pressing Ctrl-Q to remove local paragraph formatting. Clearing local paragraph formatting should clear the extra tab stop that has appeared in each of your TOC paragraphs.

If resetting the paragraph formatting doesn't resolve the positioning of the page number then you should ensure that there is only ONE tab stop in each TOC style (the one where the page number SHOULD appear). If it does resolve the issue, you might want a macro to use to update the TOCs which incorporates the Jason Tab removal step. Such a macro would work along these lines.

Sub UpdateTOCs()
  'update toc and remove the jason tabs (local formatting tabs inserted in toc with hanging text)
  Dim aToc As TableOfContents

Another factor is that the TOC style may have a hanging indent. In most cases this is desirable, especially if TOC entries are numbered. But there is a Compatibility Option "Don't add automatic tab stop for hanging indent." The existence of this option indicates that the default behavior of Word is to add an automatic tab stop, and this appears to have been the default for every version of Word for Windows (this option is invoked only by changing the Compatibility setting to Word for MS-DOS or any of the WordPerfect versions). And ordinarily one does want a tab stop there: it's what permits tabbed text following a number to align with the hanging indent. But the placement of the hanging indent/tab is crucial in TOC styles.
