a. user say:

Hi 3mudy,

 

For us to help you with your concern, please confirm if you did rename your account to Outlook.com? If so, please be informed that if you renamed a Hotmail or Outlook account, Outlook.com created an alias with your original email address. Remove the alias and then rename your account using the original address. Here's how to remove the alias:

 

1.      Sign in to your primary account.

2.      Click your user name in the upper-right corner.

3.      Click Account settings.

4.      Click Notifications, and then click Manage email preferences.

5.      Under Other email addresses, click Remove for the alias you want to remove.

 

Make sure you rename your account with the original address within 30 days of deleting the alias, or that email address will become available to anyone.

 

But, if you renamed an account from another domain, such as Gmail, no alias is created in this case, so you can simply rename your account again with your original address. You’ll want to do this before that address becomes available to anyone. Here's how:

 

1.      Sign in to your primary account at outlook.com.

2.      Click your user name in the upper-right corner.

3.      Click Account settings, and then click Overview.

4.      Click Update email address.

5.      Select Select a different address.

6.      Enter your email address, and then click Save.

 

For additional information about your concern, you may refer to the article we have provided you below:

 

Aliases and renaming accounts
