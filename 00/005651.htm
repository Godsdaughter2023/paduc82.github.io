Public Sub IENavigate(ByRef objIE As Object, surl As String)
  Dim dtmp As Double
  objIE.Navigate surl

  dtmp = Timer()
  Do

    End If
  Loop Until Not objIE.Busy And (objIE.ReadyState = 4 Or objIE.ReadyState = 4)

End Sub

=DLookUp("TITLE_NAME","Title_Name","Title_NameID=" & [Forms]![FRM1_Employee_Input]![FRM2_Director].Form![Title_NameID])

The way I think about that syntax is that forms!myForm!mySubformControl accesses the subform control itself, with its Left property or SourceObject property, whereas forms!myForm!mySubformControl.Form steps into the subform itself and now we can access the controls on the subform.

=DLookUp("TITLE_NAME","Title_Name","Title_NameID=" & [Forms]![FRM1_Employee_Input]![Title_NameID])

 

Since Title_NameID contains a number I am assuming it is a number in the table as well. Therefore we don't wrap the value in single-quotes.
