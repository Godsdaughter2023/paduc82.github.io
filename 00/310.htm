rob rolnick say:

With OneNote 2010 you can sync notebooks to SkyDrive the same way you'd do so for a local/network notebook.

To create a new notebook on SkyDrive

  1. Click the "File" button in the ribbon
  2. Click New on the left column
  3. Under "Store Notebook On" select "Web"
  4. Enter a name for your notebook.
  5. Under "Web Location" you may need to click the "Sign In" button under SkyDrive (if you aren't already signed in.)
  6. Enter your SkyDrive credentials
  7. Select the folder where you'd like the notebook to be saved
  8. Click "Create Notebook"

To move a local notebook to SkyDrive
  1. Open the notebook you want to move
  2. Click the "File" button in the ribbon
  3. Click "Share" on the left column
  4. Select the notebook you want to share
  5. Choose to "Share On" the "Web"
  6. Under "Web Location" you may need to click the "Sign In" button under SkyDrive (if you aren't already signed in.)
  7. Enter your SkyDrive credentials
  8. Select the folder where you'd like the notebook to be saved
  9. Click "Share Notebook"

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

Data files and email accounts do not have a one-to-one correspondence.  Folder sets are not accounts.  With Outlook 2010 and 2013, by default, Outlook will create a new data file (folder set) for each POP account you add unless you explicitly tell it not to.

Outlook 2007 (and earlier), however, does not behave that way.  By default, it will use a single data file for all POP accounts configured in the mail profile.  You must take steps to separate incoming mail into separate data files.  Find those steps here: http://www.howto-outlook.com/howto/sortmail.htm.

The solution is to log in using the new account or to revert back to the original account (case to case basis).

 

> The user mistyped the email address. Retyped the correct email address.

 

> The user did not access the account within 365 days. The account will be deleted permanently in the system due to inactivity. You may recreate the account as a new account if it is still available.

 

> Your account password expired. You need to reset your password.
