 HELP ME please.....

james vinyard say:

Okay, to keep it simple; the true speed of the CPU is the lowest speed advertised (1.6).  The CPU will automatically burst up to 2.4GHz to maintain a smooth performance on demanding software (mostly games).

You cannot control it, you cannot upgrade it at a repair shop.

This is the same technology put into the Intel Core i5 series and will probably be put into other future processors as well.

The burst mode only lasts a few seconds when it kicks in and then drops back down to normal.

You can go to http://www.intel.com and search this particular CPU for all the technical details and they will also explain the burst mode as well.

It gives the ability of a low end CPU to maintain a smooth performance level on demanding software.  The CPU itself detects when the demand is needed and will kick it in.  Again, it only lasts a few seconds and you will not notice it when it does.

Xbox 360 Extender Connection Problems (Extender Not - Microsoft Community

steve in seattle say:

Xbox 360 Extender Connection Problems (Extender Not Found)(Cannot Configure Extender) - Solved

I had a bazillion hours problem trying to reconnect my Xbox 360 as a Media Extender.  I had been using it fine for over a year.  Suddenly, the other day, it stopped working and I could not get it to connect to my Media Center PC no matter what I tried.  I deleted certificates, port forwarded every port known to be associated with the Xbox 360.  Nothing worked.  I did a repair Win 7 install.  Nothing.  I did a clean Win 7 install, nothing.  I factory reset my networking on the Xbox 360.  Everything failed.  Then I happened upon one link that worked and figured out a problem with the Xbox 360.  I have revised my post hoping to help others with this very frustrating problem that literally took away 36 hours of my life (over the course of a week):

This fix: Microsoft kb 916180 made it so that my Media PC could finally attempt to connect.  Apparently, it resets the registry in the Media PC to actually attempt to acquire the Xbox 360 from scratch.  But that wasn't my only problem and not why a reinstall didn't work.  I think that link will fix most of the problems most people with similar issues have encountered.

The other half of the problem:

I kept going into Network Settings on the Xbox 360 and resetting the network issues to the factory defaults.  It finally dawned on me (as my router kept reporting that the Xbox was connected wirelessly, even though I was connected on Cat 5 cable and my Xbox said that I couldn't connect wirelessly because I was on a wired network)...there was something screwed up on the Xbox 360 in the network settings, despite the factory default reset.

The fix:  I went into Xbox network settings and did the factory reset.  THEN, I disconnected the Cat 5 cable which allowed me to go to the wireless network settings.  I did a factory reset within those settings as well.  That instantly cleared my problem (even though I was not connected wirelessly, this caused some kind of problem with the Cat 5 connection) and Media Center connected and began streaming. 

I hope this helps someone because it was a very odd solution and I'm not sure how I came to that.  I never would have guessed that I would have to restore factory settings under BOTH the Wired and Wireless network settings on the Xbox.
