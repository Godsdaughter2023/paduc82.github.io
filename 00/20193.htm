I'm not sure what your goal is but if it is to show a resource graph with 100% resource allocation, then this is how you can set that up.

In January you have 95 available hours, there are 184 working hours in the month, assuming a normal 8-5 workweek. In February, you have 391 available hours in a 160 hour month and in March you have 387 available hours in a 176 hour month. That converts to an equivalent man loading of 52%, 244% and 220% for each month respectively.

If you set up the "shop" resource availability for each month with those percentages and also assign 3 fixed duration tasks (Jan, Feb, Mar) with the "shop" resource at those same assignment levels, you will get a Resource Graph that shows 100% allocation for each of the three months (within rounding error).

john - project say:

John,

First of all Project will NOT automatically allocate resources over multiple tasks given a fixed availability such that the resource is 100% utilized. It simply does not have that functionality.

However here is an approach that might be close enough to what I think you want. Let's say we have shop availability of 95 hours in January and 391 hours in February. We'll just stick with the two months to keep it simpler. enter the 52% and 244% availability for January and February respectively for the Shop resource. Now let's say we have 4 tasks in January and 2 in February that need to be done.

1. Set up 4 fixed duration tasks in January and 2 in February. I've shown them linked for convenience, ordinarily you'd only show a link if there were a true relationship.

2. Assign the Shop resource to all 6 tasks. For now ignore the work values and the assignment level shown by the resource name

3. Now switch to a split view with the Resource Usage on top and the Resource Graph on the bottom. Note the Shop resource is shown as overallocated both in the Usage view and in the graph.

4. Start allocating specific hours for each task. As you do so, you will see the graph change. Keep adjusting until it is at 100% with no overallocation. As can be seen, a few more hours are available for January and a bunch of hours are available for February. Or, you could create additional fixed duration tasks in the applicable month and allocate hours to those tasks to reach the optimum 100% resource uasge.

Could this be automated? Yes, but it would require an algorithm running in a VBA procedure.

Hope this helps.

John

john - project say:

John,

You're welcome and thanks for the feedback. I'm not quite sure where you see the automation with 100% (or multiples thereof), at least the kind of automated resource assigning I think you are after. A user can set the Max Units for a resource to any desired value. Project's leveling function can move task assignments around based on priority but it will not re-allocate "unused" resource availability to achieve 100% allocation.

sven johannsen say:

To do what you suggest would require the USB port on a WP to act as a Host, which it does not. Some mobile devices do have that capability and use a USB On-The-Go (OTG) cable/adapter to turn the normally client USB port into a Host port. This is not supported on WP. Even if it were, there would need to be drivers installed on any device to support an Ethernet dongle. Given that WP doesn't support USB OTG, there would be no incentive for anyone to provide such drivers for WP. 

If you are in a situation that you ONLY have access to wired ethernet, the most reasonable solution is to pick up a little WiFi travel router like this, Travel Router  hook it t the ethernet port/cable and connect your phone to it via WiFi. Make sure that is OK with whoever owns the network though.

jmh3143 say:
To access People from Outlook.com, hover the cursor over the Outlook logo in the top left and click on the down-arrow that appears to the right of the Outlook logo.
Select People. 

Returning to your Inbox from People:
Hover the cursor over the People logo and click on the down-arrow that appears on the right of the People logo. Select Mail.  
You can also get to Calendar or Skydrive in the same manner.

Filtering your list:
Above the search box at the top of the Contact list on the left, is a filter option which says "All ". 
If you click on this you should see the groups you created in the past.  
Click on any of these to filter your list to just the contacts in that group.

Selecting one or many Contacts:
If you click on a contact name in the list, the details for that contact will appear on the right.  
Alternatively, if you click on the check box to the left of the contact picture, you can build up a list of selected contacts on the right by clicking on the check box to the left of multiple contacts.

Adding/Removing from Group membership:
Whether you select one or many contacts, you will see a top menu bar option for Groups.  
If you click on this, then it will display the list of groups with a check box checked for each group that the contact(s) is/are a member of. 

You can uncheck to remove or check to add, the selection of contact(s) to the chosen groups, and then click on Apply to apply the changes.  
Your selected contacts will then be updated to be added or removed from the groups you chose as appropriate.

Delete
When you select one or many contacts, you will see a top menu bar option for Delete.  
If you click on Delete and click Delete in the confirmation box, the selected contacts will be deleted.

Edit:
You can choose edit if you have clicked on a single contact (name or picture) in the list on the left to see their details on the right.  
When viewing their details, click on Edit in the top menu bar to edit or add details.

Browsers:
We recommend that you keep your web browser up to date to get the most out of the People experience.
If you’re not using an updated browser, some parts of People might not work and it might not display properly.

 To update your browser, click here:
http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows/outlook/supported-browsers

If you are unable to upgrade your browser then you can view your contacts using the mobile link:
