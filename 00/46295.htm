robert aldwinckle on forums say:

black screen with the pointer. 

 

One conclusion you could draw from that is that it is not in Second Screen Only.  That is what I often have to work around when I get a black screen (with no pointer).   Then a related possibility would be that you were looking at the second half of a Lock screen; e.g. your tablet would be (inexplicably) being treated as a secondary monitor.  The workaround would be the same for each case.  First start Narrator (press Win+Ctrl+Enter).   Pressing Alt-Tab would normally show you that there were at least two tasks active and Narrator would describe them for you.  This would be necessary because they would only appear on the primary monitor.  The next problem would be getting past the Lock Screen to the Login Screen.  Pressing Ctrl+Alt+Delete may be necessary but again Narrator could assist in saying what had happened or whatever else might be possible.  E.g. use Caps Lock F2 to find out what you could do.  Once you get past the Lock Screen you can use the Project dialog to confirm the assumed state of the display configuration and fix it--e.g. make it Duplicate or PC Screen Only.  So press Win+P and hear "Project...Extend"; then press CursorUp and Enter (to switch to Duplicate so you can finally see what you are supposed to be seeing.)

Other workarounds are possible for other hypotheses for the same symptom.  Given sufficient details it may not be necessary to resort to a forced reset.  In any case, since you do seem to have a flakey system which you can lose control of (a common situation BTW) it would be a good idea to enable Hibernate as a default Sleep state.  Then you will be less likely to lose anything if you do decide to resort to a solution which involves the Power button.
