talking moose say:
That helps a lot.

First, Outlook for Mac has no equivalent of a PST file. Outlook for Windows can open, modify and close PST files as if they were regular documents but Outlook for Mac can neither use them nor perform any sort of open/modify/close operation on an archive file.

From your comments above I take it that your goal is to keep two computers running Outlook for Mac in sync with each other. The simplest and most elegant solution is to have both copies of Outlook connect directly to your mail servers as either IMAP or Exchange clients. The nature of these two protocols is to sync mail and folder changes with the server and vice versa.

IMAP and Exchange accounts in Outlook for Mac will both receive mail. If one reads the message, flags it, moves it to a folder or deletes it then the other machine is updated within a few seconds to a minute or will be updated the next time Outlook is launched.

Unfortunately, Skype Names cannot be changed once it is registered. You may go to Skype Website to manage your Skype account discoverability. Here’s how:

 

  • Sign in to Skype Website using your account.

  • On your account main page, scroll down to Account details.

  • Under Settings and preferences, select Edit profile.

  • Scroll down to Profile settings and check the box that says Appear in search results and suggestions.
