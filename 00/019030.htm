Please be informed that by upgrading to Outlook.com, you are only upgrading the user interface of Hotmail to the new user interface hence all your emails, contacts, etc. should be migrated.

 

Here are the possible reasons why emails are missing from your account:

 

1. Emails got deleted due to Inactivity.

When users don't sign in for more than 365 days or within the first 10 days after signing up for an account, the system will flag it as Inactive. After an account becomes inactive, all messages, folders, and contacts are deleted. Incoming messages will be sent back to the sender as undeliverable.

 

Also, if you were accessing your emails on a mail client such as Windows Live Mail or through your mobile device, but didn’t log into your web account for over 365 days, you may lose emails due to inactivity in web account.

 

2. Account has been hacked or compromised.

You may lose emails if your account was hacked and the hacker had already deleted emails in your account.

 

3. Conversation threading is enabled.

If the feature Conversation threading is turned on wherein all emails with the same subject line are grouped together. You can click here to know more about this feature.

 

4. Schedule Cleanup is set.

This feature can be set to automatically delete emails. You may refer to this link for more information about it.

 

You can use the recover deleted or lost emails feature in Outlook.com to bring back your lost emails. However, when Outlook.com recovers missing or deleted emails, our system restores as many emails as it could regardless of its dates.

 

Please be reminded that there is no guarantee that the messages that you are looking for will all be recovered. You can refer to this Solution Article for the steps to retrieve your lost or deleted emails.

 

Note: The system can recover messages but not the folder itself. Recovered messages will be presented in your Deleted folder. Also, emails will not be recovered via recovery link if they were deleted from email clients like Windows Live Mail, Microsoft Office Outlook, etc.

 

If you have other questions or clarifications, please let us know.

Hi,

One thing you can try is to cast your date as a string in the query.  So, if DT is your DateTime field, and Num is your number field, you could create this query:

SELECT Table1.Num, CStr([DT]) AS DateAsString
FROM Table1;

Then, in the Chart Settings pane, select the query, choose 'DateAsString' for your axis, and 'Num (None)' as your value.  If you have data that looks like this:

Table1
1 7/6/2018 1

mike sullivan [msft] say:

That's unfortunately not a property we currently expose, but I agree that it would be nice to have.  I'll pass that piece of feedback along to our team for consideration in the future.

There are a few ways you could make the negative symbol a bit more obvious.  We do expose font properties for the value axes, so you could make the labels pop a bit more with a different color.  Another option is to set the axis range to "Fixed" and specify a minimum/maximum such that '0' appears roughly in the middle of the chart.

Finally, one other option, though a bit of a hack, is to include an empty column in your query.  Then add that column to the value axis of your chart in addition to the existing currency values.  In the chart settings pane, switch over to the Format tab and select the blank column.  If you set "Missing Data Policy" to "Plot as Zero" in a line chart, you'll get a straight line directly across the chart at '0'.  You can then format this line with a different color, use a different dash type, etc.
