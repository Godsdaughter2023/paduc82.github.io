trevor rabey say:

Orion,

This is a very common routine situation and I am surprised it took your guys so long to find it, but I suppose I should have mentioned it too.

Here is my take.

People often change the format of the text and/or bars in the Gantt Chart View. All of the formatting, including text, bars and everything in page setup are associated with the current active view. If you change any of it you are essentially changing the design of the view "on the fly". It is impossible not to change the design of the Gantt Chart View to some extent, but if you take it too far you can end up with a very ugly and un-functional Gantt Chart View, and of course you no longer have the well designed and functional original.

My usual advice is to leave the original largely as it is. If you have preferences which are a big departure from the original, make a new view by copying one which is close to what you want, and modify the copy.

So, I would go to More Views, select the Gantt Chart View and copy it. Give the copy a distinctive name such as "AA My New View 01" and modify that to your heart's content. If you have already messed up the original you can recover a clean original from the global template using the organizer.

This housekeeping advice also applies to tables, filters, groups, reports, calendars and everything else that you see in the tabs in the organizer.

grschinon say: I'm not sure what you mean here, but here are two basic facts you need to bear in mind:

1) Nokia does have manufacturing plants in the PRC.

A Nokia phone with a "Made in China" label is therefore not necessarily a fake.

2) Chinese cloners are perfectly capable of finding an IMEI number of a Finnish-made Nokia.

They can then build a counterfeit phone with that IMEI number, and it doesn't even have to be the IMEI number of a phone of the same model as that being cloned. I've seen cases of cloned 8800s with the IMEI of a 6233 for example. They can also stick labels saying "Made in Finland" in the battery compartment.
