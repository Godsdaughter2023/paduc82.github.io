                    nagapow say:

                    I figured out why this was happening on my latest Windows 10 PC...

                    The full version of OneNote is now bundled in every Windows 10 installation, whereas before it had to installed through Microsoft Office 365 subscription as part of the office installation. During the office installation process, the button is added to Outlook as an "Add-In" extension which gives you the button. However, as my laptop came with the latest version of Windows, and I used the latest Office installer from Microsoft's website (i am a 365 subscriber), it no longer installs OneNote by default, which means the "Send to OneNote" button is not installed either...

                    I was able to find the OneNote 2016 installer here: https://www.onenote.com/download/?MajorVer=14

                    There is an option on the button of the page: "Other Download Options" where it allows you to download the OneNote installer. Once installed, there will be a separate OneNote 2016 application, and the "Send to OneNote" button will now appear in your Outlook 2016 application....
