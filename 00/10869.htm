One thing that I noticed is that you declare that lngID As Long, but if you look at the VBA help file you'll find out that the MID function returns a Variant(String), so in fact your lngID = Mid(Node.Key, 2) is returning a string not a Long (You can verify this by simply doing(   VarType(Mid(Node.Key, 2)) ).  Yes, in certain instance Access is able to work around this and still appear to be running properly - not care, but you should change your code slightly to lngID = CLng(Mid(Node.Key, 2)) just to be safe.  Also, add error handling and indenting (it just help in troubleshooting).  Anyways, try this and post back

Private Sub TreeView0_NodeClick(ByVal Node As Object)
On Error GoTo Error_Handler
    Dim lngID   As Long
    Dim rs      As DAO.Recordset
   
    lngID = CLng(Mid(Node.Key, 2))
    Set rs = Forms![WSP_Info_Rich].RecordsetClone
    With rs
        .FindFirst "[module_id] = " & lngID
        If .NoMatch Then
            MsgBox "." & Node.Key & ". " & lngID 'using to verify information
        Else

Error_Handler_Exit:

Error_Handler:
    MsgBox "MS Access has generated the following error" & vbCrLf & vbCrLf & "Error Number: " & _
    Err.Number & vbCrLf & "Error Source: TreeView0_NodeClick" & vbCrLf & "Error Description: " & _
    Err.Description, vbCritical, "An Error has Occured!"
    Resume Error_Handler_Exit
End Sub

azrael's kiss say:

You need to turn off fast startup... WinTP, like Win8(.1) before it, uses a hybrid sleep/hibernation when you "shutdown", so it bypasses the BIOS startup of a cold boot.

Control Panel > Power Options > Choose what the power buttons do > Change settings that are currently unavailable > Uncheck Turn on fast startup > Save changes

This will now do a full shutdown when you click "Shutdown".

If you want to retain fast startup, you'll need to use a more convoluted way to shutdown fully when you want to enter your BIOS.

PC settings > Update and recovery > Recovery > Restart Now and when your system restarts into advanced startup, Click Turn off your PC.  When you power your system back on, you should be able to enter BIOS.

The audio issue that causes stuttering in games - Microsoft Community

fettaallatte say:

The audio issue that causes stuttering in games

There's this problem with Realtek High Definition Audio drivers and ANY Generic Windows Audio driver (ie. High Definition Audio Device, Generic USB Audio Device) that's been happening in the last years: I'm personally always experiencing the issue since 2011 on different systems! This problem still hasn't a proper solution.

Basically, it doesn't matter how fast the system is, if the system has a NVIDIA video card and any device with ANY generic audio driver installed, a lot of games will experience framerate stuttering, expecially single-threaded ones (ie. Saints Row: The Third, Saints Row IV, Mafia: The City of Lost Heaven, and so on).

This problem has been happening to me since 2011, changing a lot of computers, and I still have this particular problem on my 2017 computers. The only proper way to fix it is installing a dedicated sound card (ie. a Sound Blaster or an USB Logitech Sound Card) and entirely disabling the HD Audio Controller in system's Firmware/BIOS. Make sure that you don't have any generic audio driver installed on any device.

Consider that installing generic audio drivers on the USB Logitech Sound Card makes the issue happen again, it doesn't matter if you disabled the HD Audio Controller in your Firmware/BIOS: The problem happens whenever you try to use any generic audio driver in any sound device.

I ended up having only 3 audio devices in this system: Logitech G430 Gaming Headset (with proprietary drivers), NVIDIA High Definition Audio, NVIDIA Virtual Audio Device (Wave Extensible) (WDM), and there's been no stuttering for days.

A workaround that doesn't require spending money, disabling your HDA Controller or removing generic drivers, is reinstalling NVIDIA drivers everytime the issue happens: A fresh reinstall of NVIDIA drivers deletes stuttering until system reboot.

Also, booting up the system with integrated Intel video card and unplugging the NVIDIA video card entirely fixes the problem.

And also, while you're experiencing the issue, if you try to open Steam's Music Player and try to play and immediately pause any song, the stuttering in your entire system will disappear forever until system reboot, even if you close and re-open Steam.
