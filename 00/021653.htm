robert aldwinckle on forums say:

[12336]"-error in Internet Explorer 11. What does it mean - and why does it appear ??

12336 = 0x3030  Those are two ASCII 0 characters.  So I would suspect you could be getting a buffer overflow on to a register stack.   On the other hand there is nothing which could prevent a program from issuing such a code for its own purpose.

Check the Reliability Monitor to see if it has any more detail for you.  Then if it says APPCRASH you could look for even more detail in the associated .WER file.  .WER files for APPCRASH events contain lists of Loaded modules seen under the crashing tasks.  That can give you an idea about whether there would be something you could try avoiding.  E.g. if you saw something and took steps to avoid it and didn't see the problem symptom again (after a reasonable time for testing) then chances are that it would be the cause.  Of course then reinstalling whatever it was might or might not cause the symptom again.
