chriso747 say:

Hi Michael,

It's not possible to have two installations of FSX as the Sim that is activated on your laptop is the one that is registered.  You will need to fully delete both your installations (i.e. remove all files and Registry items associated with FSX) from your system.  If you have Service Pack 2 installed, uninstall that first via Control Panel.   Then uninstall FSX via the option on Disk 1 or through Uninstall programs through Control Panel and then do a search of your system for any leftovers of FSX.

With that said, you may still encounter problems doing a fresh install on your PC and activating it.   Sometimes, several installs and uninstalls cause problems and I've heard of simmers having to wait several weeks for the old activation to be completely nullified.   So, it's difficult to suggest what you should do in such a situation as it could be that in the worst case, you may not get FSX installed and/or activated at all.

In your case, and since the laptop installation produces very weak performance and you can't activate it on your PC, I would pursue what I have suggested above and hope that it goes through.   If however, it can't be rectified, then you may need to consider another Sim (e.g. FSX-Steam Edition, Prepar3d, X-Plane, etc.) as an alternative.

Let us know how you go.

Chris.

How connect HP Laserjet 1012 to Windows 7 ? - Microsoft Community

barry komisaruk say:

How connect HP Laserjet 1012 to Windows 7 ?

I am trying to connect my HP Laserjet 1012 to my new Lenovo laptop with Windows 7, but
it will not load the necessary program from the original HP Laserjet program disc.  What
do I need to do to connect the printer to my laptop?
ken blake - microsoft mvp say:
I am trying to connect my HP Laserjet 1012 to my new Lenovo laptop with Windows 7, but
it will not load the necessary program from the original HP Laserjet program disc.  What
do I need to do to connect the printer to my laptop?


The "necessary program" is a driver. And a Windows 7 driver is certainly not on the original HP disk, since Windows 7 didn't yet exist when the HP 1012 first came out.

 

Apparently there is no Windows 7 driver for this printer, and you'r eout of luck. Here's my standard message on this subject:

 

 

Whenever you change to a new version of Windows, you need to install drivers (software that provides the interface between the hardware and Windows) for printers, scanners, video cards, etc., that have been written for that new version of Windows.  And also note that you need different drivers for a 32-bit version of Windows and a 64-bit version.

 

It's up to the manufacturer of the hardware to write and make available for download those drivers. Especially if your hardware is older, it may  well be that the manufacturer has decided that it's not worth his time and effort to develop drivers for hardware that he is no longer selling.

 

 

Check with the manufacturer or on their web site to see if a driver exists. If it doesn't, you're out of luck. Also check their web sites in other countries, if there are any; sometimes you can find the driver you need that way.

 

So in the future, before you do such an upgrade or buy a new computer with a new version of Windows, be sure that you've done the appropriate research to find out what drivers don't exist, and therefore what hardware you will have to replace if you upgrade.

Hi Sean,

We're glad to know the everything is updated. However, since your issue still the same after installing all the updates, we suggest you to follow the troubleshooting steps provided below:

  1. Clean the Screen. Use a dry cloth or screen cleaner to clear the screen of any debris, or smudges that may be impacting touch functionality.
  2. Restart the Surface. Restarting the Surface refreshes the drivers for the device. If this recovers touch functionality, make sure you tries this step in the future.
  3. Perform a Two Button Shutdown. With the device turned on, press and hold the Volume up & Power Buttons for 15 seconds, or until the device shuts off. If the device boots to UEFI, go to Exit and Restart the device.
  4. Disable and enable HID-Compliant Touch Screen Driver in Device Manager.
    1. Open Device Manager.
    2. Go to Human Interface Devices.
    3. Look for HID-Compliant Touch Screen.
    4. Right click and then choose Disable.
    5. After disabling, right click on the driver and choose Enable.
      • Note: There may be two HID-Compliant Touch drivers. Disable both before enabling
  5. Disable and enable HID-Compliant Touch Screen Driver in Device Manager.
    1. Open Device Manager.
    2. Go to Human Interface Devices.
    3. Look for HID-Compliant Touch Screen.
    4. Right click and choose Uninstall.
    5. After uninstalling, restart Surface.
    6. After the first restart, restart once more, for a total of two restarts.
  6. Reset Touch Screen Calibration
    1. Go to start, and type in Calibrate.
    2. Locate Calibrate the screen for pen or touch input, and then select it.
    3. Click on Reset.
    4. Click the Yes button to confirm reset, and follow on screen instructions.
    5. If the reset button is greyed out, continue to Step 7.
  7. Check for electromagnetic interference. Sometimes, other electronic devices can negatively influence touch screen performance. To test this, move to a location that has minimal electronic devices in the environment and test the touch screen.

Reply to us with the status of your issue. We'll be happy to assist you.
