Just for sake of clarity - there is no separate Outlook.com EAS and IMAP account - it's one and the same email account simply accessed via a different protocol by the email desktop client which could be anything (in this case Outlook). When you access your account via WebMail - it displays what's on the server and EAS/POP/IMAP protocols don't come into play.

Taking Hals' response/suggestions into account - what happens if you simply create a new folder under the Outlook.com IMAP tree? Does it appear in the Outlook.com EAS tree? #1 - As you have painfully discovered - a "MOVE" is a 2 step process, the first of which is a "Copy" followed by a "Delete" from the source (only re-stating this for anyone else who is reading this - I never recommend anyone use a "destructive" process until they are sure of the outcome first)

#2 - Another after-the-fact item I keep forgetting to mention is when copying large amounts of data to an Outlook.com account configured with 2 protocols (i.e. EAS & IMAP) - better to ensure that the IMAP account does not automatically sync. Again, this is more for any one else who is reading this since it unfortunately doesn't help you at this point
