mike hall... always say:
Even though you might install programs elsewhere, some still ends up on the C drive because it has to be there.
 
If you move restore points to another drive, you may as well not have them. They too have to be on C.
 
Then there is the pagefile. By default, Windows places the page file right into the thick of it on C. This reduces head travel when accessing it which in turn helps speed. Unless you have another physical drive, it should stay where it is on C.
 
So, there is more to Windows 7 than the visible files./
 
Patches, updates and stuff which installs on C no matter what also take their toll of available drive space. Basically, 30gb is not enough, especially as a hard drive will perform less well if free space drops below 25%.
