voodoodrul say: 'Display driver stopped responding and has recovered. Display driver Intel(R) LDDM Ultra Mobile Media stopped responding and has successfully recovered'

This message simply means that the Intel graphics driver has crashed, but the driver was restarted. It may be tenuously related to the GE problem, as in GE causes the driver to crash, but the problem is ultimately the driver. 

Unfortunately, you probably cannot use Intel's latest driver from their site. Sony does not provide timely driver updates and many of their machines do use custom drivers. For example, my VAIO has a hybrid integrated/dedicated pair of GPUs and the driver support for toggling between these is why I cannot use official Intel drivers. I must use Sony's OEM drivers. 

In short, you're probably stuck with using Sony's official drivers here - 
http://esupport.sony.com/US/p/model-home.pl?mdl=VPCX131KXB&template_id=1&region_id=1&tab=download#/downloadTab

You'll see that the video driver there hasn't been updated since 7/14/2010. Asking Sony to update this driver would be a good start, but likely not a timely one. 

If you aren't ready to get too creative with your solution, try simply starting GE in OpenGL or Direct3D modes. https://support.google.com/earth/answer/176176?hl=en

One mode may work while the other may trigger a crash.
