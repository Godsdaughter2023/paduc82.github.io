The POP folder does not get emptied unless you do it. There is no automatic empty process for that folder, despite the fact that Deleted Items is its parent.

If you see the POP folder, then you are accessing the mailbox with a POP client configured to delete the messages after downloading them. If you have Outlook.com configured to "disobey" the deletion request, it will move the messages to the POP folder instead of deleting them. If you have Outlook.com configure to obey the deletion requests, you'll lose the messages permanently.

Normally, as with Code39, a barcode is just a font displaying the characters.  The barcode reader is just an alternate keyboard entry device, such that when it scans the barcode the characters are input just like you would have typed it.

 

Different barcodes are usually just different font renderings. 

 

Barcode 128 uses a calculated check digit added to the actual characters.

jomel_g say:

Hi Mark,

You can activate voice messaging from your Skype account by following the steps below. 

  1. Sign in to your account.
  2. In the Manage Features section, select Voicemail and Call forwarding.
  3. Enable call forwarding by clicking the button to on and then select how many seconds to forward calls after.
  4. Select Voice messaging from the selection, then Confirm. 

Note: We no longer support custom greetings for your voicemail. Instead, Skype will use a default message for your app language.

I hope this helps.
