Microsoft has published revised minimum storage capacity requirements for desktop Windows 10. Just for your information, RAM storage is just temporary storage that your computer uses to run programs that are currently running. It does not get erased when you turn off your computer. Typically, a computer will only have anywhere from 2 GB to 16 GB of RAM, and the hard drive will usually go from around 128 GB to a few terabytes of storage size. This is a very crude explanation, but I hope it helps to clear things up a little for you.
