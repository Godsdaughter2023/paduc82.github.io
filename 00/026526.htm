dirk goldgar say:

I'm sorry for the delayed response -- I didn't receive the usual e-mail notification that you'd replied to this thread.

As I understand it, you have a Timer event running on your form, firing every n seconds.  I'd guess that the event procedure currently looks something like this:

    Private Sub Form_Timer()

        Me.Requery

    End Sub

You can have the procedure check for changes to the number of records after the requery by modifying it like this:

    Private Sub Form_Timer()

        Dim lngRecordCount As Long

        ' Get number of records before requerying.

        With Me.RecordsetClone
            If .RecordCount <> 0 Then .MoveLast   ' ensure accuracy of RecordCount
            lngRecordCount = .RecordCount
        End With

        ' Requery the form.

        Me.Requery

        ' After requerying, has the number of records changed?

        With Me.RecordsetClone

            If .RecordCount <> 0 Then .MoveLast   ' ensure accuracy of RecordCount

            If .RecordCount <> lngRecordCount Then

                ' The number of records on the form has changed!

                Call MyFunctionToHandleTheCase

            End If

        End With


    End Sub

a. user say:

Hi Nomis Nikmul,

 

Let me assist you in your concern about merging Inboxes. Please be informed that currently, Microsoft account does not support merging accounts. However, we have other ways where you can still be able to access the emails from one account to another. Kindly see below:

 

Customers can link accounts with the following steps:
1.0  Autoforward all old account mail to new account. This way at least customers will have all of their mail in one place, and can reply to it using their new address.

 

2.0 To forward mail, log in to the account from which you want your mail forwarded, and click the gear menu at the upper right. Choose More mail settings from the dropdown, then Email forwarding from the Managing your account list.

 

3.0 Link your old account to your new account. Log in to one of your accounts, then click your user name in the upper right. Choose Account settings. On the left side of the screen, click Permissions, then click Manage linked accounts. In the new screen, choose Add linked accounts.

 

4.0 To switch between two linked accounts, click the user tile at the upper-right of your Outlook window.

 

5.0 Create email rules for all the messages that will come to your Inbox. Since it seems that you have created an alias on your account, this worjkaround is applicable to you. By creating rules, your Inbox will be organized. Here are the steps:

 

1. Sign in using your Microsoft account.
2. In the upper-right corner of your Inbox, click Options, and then More options.
3. On the Hotmail Options page, under Customizing Hotmail, click Rules for sorting new messages.
4. Click New.

 

I hope this helps and do not hesitate to respond to this post if need further assistance.

 

Thank you.
