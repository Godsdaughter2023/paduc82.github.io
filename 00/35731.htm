You're on the right track.

The two "physical drives"are the SSD and the HDD. These are what you see as Disk 0 and Disk 1 in the Disk Management screen.

"1.256 TB (1x 1 TB 7200 rpm; 1x 256 GB SSD)"

The "1 TB 7200 rpm"is your HHD which is called Disk 1 and the "256 GB SSD" is Disk0.

The confusion arises because what we usually call a "drive" in Windows is called a partition in the Disk Management screen. When you bought the PC there was one partition on the SSD and three on the HDD. In  Windows Explorer, you saw the 4 partitions as C:. D:, E: and F:.

You them merged the 3 partitions on the HDD(Disk1) into one called E:. That's where you are now. One partition on Disk0 called C: and one partition on Disk1 called E:.

Because C: is on Disk0 and E: is on Disk1, you can't merge them. What you should be able to do is format the Unallocated space on Disk0 as a new partition and then merge that with C:.

Your E: partition on Disk1 is using the whole disk, so you're not wasting any space there.

I agree with you that the vendor had no reason to divide up the HHD into 3 partitions and it's strange that they only allocated part of the SSD space as C:. What you should have seen out of the box is 2 drive letters in Explorer, C: and D:.

As we've explained, you can't join the 2 drives (SSD and HHD) into one partition so you only see C: for example. That's just a limitation of Windows, though it applies to most consumer operating systems. The electronics just don't work that way!

I hope this has cleared up some confusion for you.
