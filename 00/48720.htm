cmdrkeene say:

I'm not sure you understood my issue. I was moving files on the flash drive from one folder to another on the flash drive. When I move tons of files from one folder on the Windows install drive, regardless of where the folders are or how many files/folders are being moved, it usually happens instantly, and only occasionally takes maybe a fraction of a second or two; I realize this is just changing the indexes (not physically moving the files). With a USB flash drive, it takes seconds to minutes, depending on how many are being moved, which is usually far less than I do on the Windows install drive. That's because a flash drive has to physically move the files on the drive, it has no index to switch like on a fixed drive.  Plus the only way to move them is to copy them to memory, then write them to the new location.  That means every bit moved has to go through the USB connection (which is thousands of times slower than a SATA cable on the real harddrive), and since it has to go twice (in and out), it's even half as slow as that again.

There's no transferring between the hard drive and the flash drive that I know of.

What this indicates to me is the indexes either take longer to rewrite, or the files are actually being moved. What I'm asking is if Windows is somehow transferring data (even if it's indexes) from the flash drive to the hard drive then moving them back, slowing things down. I think even moving them through RAM would slow it down, but I'm not sure.  Also consider that the internal drive has caching. So even if a bit has to be moved, Windows will just note "yeah move this bit later".  The progress box says it completed instantly, but really it's using lazy write-behind to just finish the job later.  That's why if you lose power to your computer, it corrupts files that got reported as done, but were not really done writing to the drive.  Flash Drives don't use the lazy "write behind caching" because they never know when the user is going to unplug them unexpectedly, so they take the slow way and verify the entire write has finished before they tell the progress bar to advanced. 

You can change this second item by setting your USB device to "best performance" under Device Manager, like below.  But I don't recommend it because it can't handle you unplugging the drive anymore.
