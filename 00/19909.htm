bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
Minor OS glitches like this aren't at all uncommon nor are they anything to be overly concerned about :-) They can occur for any number of reasons but can usually be resolved by simply changing the focus temporarily, then coming back to the program involved. Try any of the following:

  • Quit any programs you aren't using
  • Open a new document then close it
  • Save & close the active document then open it again
  • Switch to or launch a different program then come back to Word
  • Quit & relaunch Word
  • Restart your Mac if necessary

Also, make sure you have your version of OS X & your installation of Office fully updated.


Some of the more common causes are:
  • Low system resources
  • Keeping programs launched for extensive periods of time
  • Not restarting the Mac occasionally
  • Conflicts with other software
  • Plus some other less likely possibilities

Normally it's nothing to worry about unless it becomes frequent or persistent. If it does, it could be symptomatic of a deeper problem that should be addressed, but updates, daily launching of programs & an occasional restart are all it takes to avoid them.

graham mayor say:
The whole point of PDF is that it is not an editable format. PDF does not lend itself to easy conversion to Word format (or depending how it was created any conversion to Word format). You have three options for filling this form

1. Print it out and use a pen.
2. Print it out and use a typewriter.
3. Obtain a copy of Acrobat X Professional and use its form function to create editable form fields on the PDF. Depending on the form layout it may work automatically, but in any case it is a lot of trouble for a one off.

Conversion to Word format is simply impractical and far too much effort for a one-off.

Why do they not provide an editable version? Either they simply don't know they can, or they intend for the form to be filled by hand.
