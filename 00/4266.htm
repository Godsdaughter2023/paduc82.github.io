babajanny say:

Inserting page numbers in one section only in Word 16.2 for Mac

I do want page numbers on the rest of the pages, starting with page 1 appearing on what is page three of the document. However, I cannot get rid of page numbers on the first two document pages. They keep numbering themselves as 1 and 2. How do I eliminate page numbers in the first section (first two pages of doc), and keep the numbering I have in the rest of the document?

Thanks!

Jan

john korchok say:

When you insert a section break, by default the headers and footers before and after the break remain connected. You must break this connection first. These steps assume the page numbers are in the footer. If they're in the header the steps are the same, just substitute header for footer:

  1. Go to the page following the section break and double-click on the footer to open it. On the right side of the page, you'll now see a blue tab that says Same as Previous. This shows the the section footers are connected.
  2. On the Header & Footer tab, click on the Link to Previous button to deselect it. The right blue tab disappears in the footer.
  3. Go to the pages before the section break and delete the page numbers.

These steps assume you haven't set a different first page or even and odd headers and footers. These attributes are in Format>Document>Layout under the Headers and Footers heading. The idea is similar for those, but there are more steps.
