obsessive power say:

Hi,

The days of U.S NTSC 60Hhz European and PAL 60 are long behind when it comes to gaming on the the Xbox One console.  All games on Xbox One support 60Hhz refresh, regardless of which country/region you are from.  Indeed, there is no such standard anyway with HDMI.

The allow 50Hz is for video playback.  This is a standard refresh rate in Europe, the Middle East, and Africa, and many places in Asia.  This so you if you are in one those territories and hook up a set top box to the HDMI in on the console, the signal will play correctly on the TV.

Allow 24Hz.  This is mainly for playback of movies on Blu-ray or DVD as 24Hz is the natural frame rate for this content.

As already stated, the above is for video playback, either on a DVD/Blu-ray, HDMI pass-thru or a external video computer file.  These settings are there to insure video plays correctly, it does not affect games.
