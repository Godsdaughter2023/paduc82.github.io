julie sheets say:
Hi Tom,

To give you an concrete example that may help:

Create a new project using the Standard calendar.
Start date is today, April 22, 2013
Create a file with two tasks auto scheduled tasks linked F to S.  Each with a one-day duration.
Apply critical path and not surprisingly - both tasks are on the critical path.
Increase the duration of the first task to 10 hours.  Again, both tasks are on the critical path.  Task 1 starts at 08:00 am on April 22 and finishes at 10:00 am on April 23.  The start date of Task 2 is 10:00 am on April 23.

Now assign the 24 hours calendar to Task 1.
Task 1 now ends at 6:00 pm on April 22.  It drops off the critical path as it's finish date could extend until 7:59 am on April 23 before it pushes Task 2 later.  Because Task 2 cannot start before 8:00 am due to its calendar - that build Total Slack (float) into the schedule.

I hope this helps.

Julie

ramz aftab say:

Hi Ruslan,

This is the default feature of Office applications that selections are based on Active Window. The reason is obvious that if you're working on Window1 (Active window), you can see visible selection (highlighted). The selection would allow you to change Cell formats, Copy, Cut or Erase data. Excel doesn't want to take any liability of the inactive window changes, whether it works side-by-side or in background. Perhaps due to this reason, you can only view 'Selection' in active window at a time unlike Notepad.

Alternatively, you can use Fill-Color handle to highlight in both workbooks. This might be a little tedious job, but will serve the purpose.

I hope this helps.
