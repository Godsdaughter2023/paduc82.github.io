jetta48 say:

Have you tried formatting the card using the camera rather than the PC?

Please confirm size of card (for example 2GB) and that it is definitely a SD card and not a SD HC Card. Some card readers (usually older ones) are not fully compatible with SD HC cards.

When you try to format the card which file system are you using (for example, FAT or NTFS).

Finally, SD cards (and card readers) are not very robust and do just die. Try a known good card in your PC and see if you can format that.

 

All answers and suggestions are provided by an enthusiastic amateur and are therefore without warranty either explicit or implicit. Basically you use my suggestions at your own risk.

a0n1d2y say: Hi!

You can format the card via camera. In most cases, you just go to Menu --> Settings --> Card --> Format and select 'Yes'. On your camera, it may be diffrent. Then, make sure that the reader in properly connected to the motherboard. Open the computer panel or unscrew the back panel and follow the cable, leading from reader to motherboard. Then, unplug the cable and plug back in. After that, it should work. You can also go to Start and enter Device manager into search field. Then go to Readers or something like that and right-click and select Update driver. I fthat doesn't help, try to right-click --> Disable device in Device manager. Then enable device back on. If this is yours computer, try to upgrade to another version of Windows, if you got Windows 7 Starter or Home Basic. You can download trial version from Microsoft's webpage and then you can buy serial number from Amazon or Ebay for a very low price. If it doesn't help, the problem might be in the camera. Check, if your SD card doesn't have the Lock switch set on 'Lock' (switch is on the left side of the card). You can try external card reader, which is connected via USB cable. Maybe, your computer's got some viruses, like 'Driver virus', which will disable some drivers without even knowing that. Download anti-virus, like Avast or AVG, if you haven't.
