tryx3 say:

1  Close WMP

2  Open Windows explorer.  Set it to show Hidden files & show System files using Organise, Folder & search options, View tab, click next to Show hidden files and clear the checkbox next to Hide protected operating system files [you'll be made to confirm this change].

3  Connect external drive

4  Copy all the music folders & files onto the hard drive from the external one.  C:\Users\YourUserName\My music is a common place to put them but it is not compulsory.

5  Then disconnect the external drive.

6  Still in Windows explorer, find the top-level folder that you have copied to [C:\Users\YourUserName\My music or whatever you decided on]

7  Still in Windows explorer, look in Libraries, Music.  Is the folder you've just looked at included already?  If so, omit step 8.

8  Still in Windows explorer, find the top-level folder that you have copied to then [if it was not already in the music library], right-click, select Include in library, select Music.

9  Open WMP.  Let it update its library - it will show you that it is doing this in the lower part of the screen just to the right of the volume control.  It will eventually show a completed sign.

10  If WMP now shows a mish-mash of what you had before as well as what you've got now then press alt-t {the alt key then the t key} [to get the Tools menu available] then Advanced, Restore media library.  This should do a new library-building search to correct the mish-mash. Once again,  it will show you that it is doing this in the lower part of the screen just to the right of the volume control. It will eventually show a completed sign.

11  As soon as you are happy, go back to Windows explorer & reset the show settings you put on before.  You probably don't want to have hidden files & protected operating system files visible normally as it can lead to serious problems when accidental changes or deletions are made.  You only needed this setting to ensure you copied the hidden album artwork that was on your external drive along with the music tracks themselves.

It should now all be in order.
