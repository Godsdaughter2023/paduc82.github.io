How many times do you want to be told that your hard drive is done before you will believe it?

 

Your first mistake was continuing to run the laptop even though you knew that it was overheating. This should have been fixed immediately.

Now, you are at the stage when overheating has finally taken out your hard drive. It was inevitable that overheating would take something out of the game, and in many ways you are lucky that it was just the hard drive SS and not some other component which would mean replacement of the entire motherboard. $$$$$.

 

However, the hard drive is in a state that it can't be cloned, so you have lost your recovery partition for good.

 

Your next step is to get the overheating issue fixed, otherwise it will all happen again. Then you replace your hard drive, get recovery didks from the manufacturer, and start over.

 

Note that a Windows 7 repair disk will only repair corruptions on the hard drive. It will NOT fix major faults on the hard drive, and certainly not a drive damaged by heat and then a hard knock while being in a fragile state. CHKDSK will not run because the drive is terminally broken. Windows hasn't corrupted because of crashing out, something that CHKDSK can actually fix.
