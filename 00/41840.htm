techfreak_ say:

If you input any single charcter in your message that does not comply with GSM 7-bit encoding, your total message will be forced to be sent in a 16-bit format known as Unicode Transformation Format-16 or simply, UTF-16 (a successor to UCS-2, an older 16-bit format). Since your message is allocated over twice as many bits to send in this format, this limits your message to 70 characters instead of 160. Hebrew would definitely fall into this category.

If your message contains GSM 7-bit alphabet characters but also includes extended characters like ~,^, |, {, }, €, [, ], or \, each instance of these characters will take the space of two characters. So a standard formatted message that contains two of those characters will consume four character spaces bringing the 160-character limit down to 156 characters.

If you plan on purposefully sending a long SMS message exceeding the maxium character limit, the message created will convert over to a multi-part/concatenated SMS message. Extra bits used for message ordering are consumed for this task. This lowers the character limit for 7-bit encoded message parts from 160 to 153 characters and 16-bit encoded message parts from 70 to 67 characters.

These rules are not Skype-specific but specific to SMS in general (GSM 03.38).

Your long, 16 bit encoded SMS message breakdown:

93 - 67 = 1 UTF-16 encoded message at 67 characters and a second message at 26 characters = 2 charges

173 - 134 =  2 UTF-16 encoded messages at 67 characters a piece and a third message at 39 characters = 3 charges
