Inserting a picture into an email message in Outlook 2013 - Microsoft Community

ted-duke say:

Inserting a picture into an email message in Outlook 2013

Outlook 2013, change from earlier versions.

Microsoft Help refers to an "open message." Microsoft has slightly changed the ways that emails are composed. In earlier versions, selecting Reply in the ribbon automatically "popped" out a new window which the words "open message" are intended to mean, but those words don't aptly describe the difference. In Outlook 2013 the "open" message can be composed in the Preview/Main window itself. In that case, the Microsoft help may confuse new users like me who want to Insert/Paste an image into the body of the emai,  because it can't be done there. Help says:

Insert a picture from a file

  1. In an open message, on the Insert tab, click Picture.

  2. Browse to locate the picture that you want to insert.

  3. Click the picture you want, and click Insert.

    Note    Microsoft Outlook automatically embeds pictures in a message. You can reduce the size of your email by linking to the picture. In the Insert Picture dialog box, click the arrow next to Insert, and click Link to File.

What Help does not clearly say is that the insertion can only be done in a "Pop Out" window that the help calls "Open Message." The "Insert" option isn't automatic for replies in the Preview (main) window. It is still automatic for New Email. The change is likely convenient for most email replies, because it does not auto-generate a new window.

However, to follow Microsoft's instructions, above, requires a separate pop out window for New and Reply emails. You can get that pop out in Reply messages in either of two ways:

  1. Double click on the incoming email in the current list to pop out a reply window.

  2. After your reply email appears in the Preview pane, select the small gray "Pop out" option in the upper part of the Main Preview message as shown below.

julie fka profjulie say:

You will have to delete your existing Exchange partnership before you'll be able to sync to My Phone or to Google Sync (both of these services use an Exchange type of service and only one Exchange type service can be set up on a phone).  But if you delete your Exchange partnership, you will lose the data that is on the device.

So before you delete the Exchange partnership, download and install PIMBackup to your phone.  Then use PIMBackup to backup this data to your storage card.

After you've backed up your data to the storage card, delete your Exchange partnership, then restore the data back to your phone through PIMBackup.  Then you can use either My Phone or Google Sync to sync this data to whatever service you choose.

Some other answers to your questions:

If you want to sync Contacts, Appointments and Tasks directly with your computer, you MUST have Outlook installed on your computer

If you use Windows Live to sync data, you will only be able to sync Contacts and Email (assuming you have this stuff set up under your Windows Live ID).  Windows Live only syncs between the phone and your Windows Live account.  Data will not be synced to your computer.

There isn't much more that I know of to do.  Your primary slow down is caused by removing the filtering and that process is out of our control to a large degree.  I think that much of the delay is probably caused by recalculating the sheet after the filter has been removed, and that is going to have to be done sooner or later at the expense of time.
