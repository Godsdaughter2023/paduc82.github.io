macropod say:

The fundamental issue you have with trying to output record 2 immediately after record1 is that record2 isn't updated until after record 1 and the all the other content before the {NEXT} field have been written. once record2 becomes available, Word is no longer able to access the content from record1.

The approach you need is along the lines of:

{SET PrevName {MERGEFIELD FullName}}{SET PrevRecord {IF{MERGEFIELD rep}={MERGEFIELD party} "{MERGEFIELD rep}↵
{MERGEFIELD repPhone}" "No Rep↵
{MERGEFIELD partyPhone}"}}{NEXT}{REF PrevName}↵
{MERGEFIELD FullName}¶
{REF PrevRecord}¶
{IF{MERGEFIELD rep}={MERGEFIELD party} "{MERGEFIELD rep}↵
{MERGEFIELD repPhone}" "No Rep↵
{MERGEFIELD partyPhone}"}¶

or:

{SET PrevName «FullName»}{SET PrevRecord {IF«rep»=«party» "«rep»↵

«repPhone»" "No Rep↵
«partyPhone»"}}{NEXT}{REF PrevName}↵
«FullName»¶

{REF PrevRecord}¶

{IF«rep»=«party» "«rep»↵

«repPhone»" "No Rep↵

«partyPhone»"}¶

Note: The field brace pairs (i.e. '{ }') for the above example are all created in the document itself, via Ctrl-F9 (Cmd-F9 on a Mac or, if you’re using a laptop, you might need to use Ctrl-Fn-F9); you can't simply type them or copy & paste them from this message. Nor is it practical to add them via any of the standard Word dialogues. Likewise, the chevrons (i.e. '« »') are part of the actual mergefields - which you can insert from the 'Insert Merge Field' dropdown (i.e. you can't type or copy & paste them from this message, either). The spaces represented in the field constructions are all required. Instead of the ↵ and ¶ symbols, you should use real line & paragraph breaks, respectively.

Note too, the lack of unnecessary \* MERGEFORMAT switches.
