Hyperlinks will wrap if they have hyphens in them. For those that don't, you can insert a No-Width Optional Break (U200C) at a convenient point. Make sure that the break is inserted only in the display text and not in the underlying HYPERLINK field, and the link will still work.

hosch250 say:

You can use both, but you may want to change some of advanced settings so that Office 2013 doesn't always run in compatibility mode.  You are saving documents onto your C: drive.  When you set up SkyDrive, just set it so that it saves nothing to the cloud.  Office 2010 was not removed for me, but I had to change certain settings, which are described below, so that they would run separately, and you cannot use both Outlooks at the same time.  Otherwise, everything works as two completely separate applications.  To change your settings so they do not share the NORMAL.DOTM template, go to the File Explorer and show the hidden items.  Then go C:/Users/Username/AppData/Roaming/Microsoft/Templates/ and create a folder called Office 2010 and another called Office 2013.  Now open Word 2010 and go to Options/Advanced and scroll to the bottom.  Open the File Locations box and click User Templates.  Click Modify and change the location from Templates to Templates/Office 2010.  Do the same for Word 2013.  Now your Offices both work and are completely separated from each other.  You needn't create anything in those folders as nest time you open the application, a new NORMAL.DOTM template will be created.
