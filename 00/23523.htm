Sub ExportToWord()
     
    Dim appWrd          As Object

    Dim SheetRange      As String

    FilePath = ThisWorkbook.Path

    If objDoc Is Nothing Then

        Prompt = "Copying Data: " & x - 1 & " of " & LastRow - 1 & "   (" & _

        Application.StatusBar = Prompt
         
         'Assign the worksheet names and bookmark names to a variable

        With ThisWorkbook.Sheets("Summary")
            SheetChart = .Range("A" & x).Text
            SheetRange = .Range("B" & x).Text
            BookMarkChart = .Range("C" & x).Text
            BookMarkRange = .Range("D" & x).Text
        End With
         
         'Tell Word to goto the bookmark assigned to the variable BookMarkRange
        appWrd.Selection.Goto What:=wdGoToBookmark, Name:=BookMarkRange
         
         'Copy the data from Thisworkbook
        ThisWorkbook.Sheets(SheetRange).UsedRange.Copy
         
         'Paste into Word
        appWrd.Selection.Paste

         'Copy the data from Thisworkbook
        ThisWorkbook.Sheets(SheetChart).ChartObjects(1).Copy
         
         'Paste into Word
        appWrd.Selection.Paste
    Next
     
     'Turn everything back on
    Application.ScreenUpdating = True

    Application.StatusBar = False

    appWrd.Visible = True

    Set appWrd = Nothing

Am I correct in assuming that the error is on the following line and it is telling you that wdGoToBookmark is not defined.

appWrd.Selection.Goto What:=wdGoToBookmark, Name:=BookMarkRange

The easy method of fixing this is in the VBA editor select menu item Tools -> References and scroll down to Micorsoft Word 16.0 Object Library and check the box against it and then click OK. (Ensure you click OK after checking the box otherwise it is not set.)

Your code is using late binding in lieu of early binding. There are advantages and disadvantages of both. Personally I develop with Early Binding and then convert to Late Binding. Early Binding gives you all the advantages of intellisense dropdowns during the development of the code. Late Binding keeps it backward compatible with earlier versions of Office but requires you to obtain the values of some parameters used in the code.

I suggest you google early binding vs late binding for more explanation.
