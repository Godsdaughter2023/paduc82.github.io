considerate_guy say:

A little further down in that menu should be a choice for 'Save Movie'.  That's the choice you want.

The project file (.wlmp) just contains the list of files used to make your movie, the animations applied, titles added, etc.  It actually doesn't contain any video, audio or pictures.  The only thing it has is a reference to their locations and file names on your hard disk.  In that sense, it isn't a movie at all.  It is like the recipe for making a movie.

It's not until you save your project as a movie (Alt+F, Save Movie) that it follows the instructions in your project file (the recipe) to make the final movie (bake your cake).  At that point it pulls all the items together and renders the final .wmv file.

By the way, don't worry about the leading zero.  It's the same version number.  There is a later version (15.4.3538.0513) if you wanted to update (download.live.com), but that's left up to you.  You can still save a movie (.wmv file) with your version.
