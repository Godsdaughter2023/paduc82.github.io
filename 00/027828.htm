el_loco---01 say: Well you got the gist of it right.

Reason why it's difficult to put an FM aerial or antenna (if you live on the other side of the pond), is because of the FM frequencies. They are low even though officially they are in the VHF range (Very High Frequency), compared to cellular frequencies they are very low. So the aerials would have to be very big (long) to be effective.

A quarter wavelength antenna for FM has to be about 1 yard long. It's difficult to get this into a small device that has a bunch of other radios built in that use antennae as well.

A typical mobile phone this days has:

BT radio
WLAN radio
GSM 800/900/1800/1900 radios
WCDMA 2100 radio (or others as well)

Plus all the noisy circuitry needed to power all the other stuff.

So it's not easy to put an FM antenna that will work. The headset is much better as an antenna.

How do I change the target of several footnote cross-references - Microsoft Community

dcc2017 say:

How do I change the target of several footnote cross-references (i.e., the footnote they are pointing to)?

How do I change the target of a cross-referenced footnote? E.g., suppose I have a citation to, say, a book in fn 10. In several subsequent footnotes, I use the cross-referencing feature to cite back to fn 10, which is the first appearance of the citation (where the bibliographic information is written out in full). But later on, it turns out that I want to cite the source in footnote 8. Thus, since the first citation is now in footnote 8, that's where all the cross-references should point to when they are updated. In WordPerfect, this would be easily handled. I would just cut the Target code from fn 10 and move it to fn 8, and when I updated, the subsequent cross-references would now automatically change to say "note 8" where they used to say "note 10". But Word doesn't work that way. What do I do to make the subsequent cross-references now point to fn 8 when they are updated? (Please don't say, "Go and change them all one by one." My question is whether there is some easy way to do thi
suzanne s. barnhill say:

Cross-references do update automatically but not dynamically. To update them, select them (or the whole document) and press F9. If footnote 10 became footnote 8 because you had removed two earlier footnotes, your cross-reference would still be okay.

This case is a little more complex since you are changing the actual target. But it can still be done. Press Alt+F9 to reveal the field code of the cross-reference and make a note of the bookmark. Then select the footnote reference mark for footnote 8 in the document body. Open the bookmark dialog and click the radio button for Location, which will cause Word to display all the bookmarks. Find the one associated with your cross-reference and click Add. This will move the bookmark to the new location. Then update your cross-references.
