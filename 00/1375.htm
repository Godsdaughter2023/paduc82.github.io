First, youu need to understand you don't have fields on a form, you have controls that may or may not be bound to fields in a table. This is a subtle but important distinction.

If Me.datecontrol < #1/1/91# Then

  2. Scroll down to find the entry for Office 365
  3. Left click the Office program entry
  4. Click on the “Change” button at the top of the scroll box

  5. Pick either "Quick Repair" or "Online Repair"

  6. After the repair is done, this dialog will display, click on the "Close" button

  7. In theory, you should be able to now use the applications in your Office 365 installation

jay freedman say:
The MSDN documentation seems to be simply wrong. The ActiveX buttons in the Legacy Tools menu of the Developer tab always insert the controls as inline shapes. Once a control is in the document's text layer, though, you can right-click it and click Format AutoShape/Picture to open the Format Object dialog box. In that dialog, go to the Layout tab and click one of the buttons other than "In line with text". Then you can drag the control to the desired location, or click the Advanced button in the dialog and set the position numerically.
