Since October 2016, Skype accounts are now linked to Microsoft Accounts just like other Microsoft Services such as OneDrive. So, signing in now should use your Microsoft Account email address and password.

If you are seeing random contacts you don't remember adding or communicating with, they might have added you through the public directory.

Sign in to your account in the Skype website.
https://secure.skype.com
Scroll down and click Edit profile.
Scroll down to Profile settings then disable discoverability

You can delete those contacts you don't want by right clicking on the contact
Click View profile
Scroll to the bottom then click 'Remove contact'

Knowing if your account was hacked would be difficult to say and using Skype as an attack point would be pretty difficult considering you haven't even been signed in for several years.

he conversation history (where sent and/or received messages, calls and data are located) are saved in the cloud for a period of time (last 30 days). Any history older than 30 days will be saved in the device's local hard drive depending on your history settings.

Moving forward, in your conversations, as well details of any calls you've had on Skype are stored in the cloud for 2 years.
