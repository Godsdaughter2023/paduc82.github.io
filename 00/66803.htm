gts-nj say: There are utilities to securely delete files from the outset.  They overwrite the data.  I can't recommend a particular one as best, but Eraser is popular and works with Windows 7 for one example.
http://eraser.heidi.ie/download.php

Files that were in your recycle bin, which was then emptied, may be recoverable until that disk space is overwritten.  To destroy that data you have to securely wipe the free space on the hard drive.  A good choice for this is SDelete v1.6
http://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/sysinternals/bb897443

The belief that files, once overwritten, can be recovered is an urban legend.  (It's based on a theoretical academic paper discussing floppy diskettes and obsolete MFM computer drives from decades ago.) 
Note however that when top security is involved there are areas where remnant pieces of files may be found like in the windows swap file, temp file areas, etc.

Note also that there is some recent research finding that secure deletion on SSD drives may involve some special difficulties.

Well, you could try plugging straight into the wall and see if the problem goes away.
