Prevent losing email in Outlook.com when you've set up POP3

Outlook.com has a feature that prevents losing email messages if you accidentally set up POP3 to delete them after reading them on the server. Here’s how it works:

    The first time that Outlook.com receives a POP3 instruction to delete email messages after reading from an email app, Outlook.com won't delete the messages. Instead, Outlook.com moves the email messages that were successfully downloaded by the email app into a POP folder and out of the inbox. The POP folder is available in the Folders list.

    Outlook.com keeps the email messages in the POP folder until you sign in to your Outlook.com account and verify whether Outlook.com should respect or ignore the POP3 delete command.

a. user say:

Hello mike WARE:

Thank you for posting to the forum regarding the issues you are having with your music collection.  What sounds to be happening is that the music that was moved from your other computer has either missing metadata or metadata that is not compatible with the Zune software.

This is likely going to require some manual addition or manipulation of the current meta data available.  Before we have you do this there are a couple things you might look into.

  • The first thing to do is go to the software and click settings in the upper right > software > meta data.  Make sure the box related to "Automatically retrieve and update album art and meta data." is checked.
  • The next thing to try is to go to your collection and sort by album.  Right click each of the listed albums and select the "Find album info." and the "Update album art".  This will sometimes allow the content to be matched and for you to automatically get information pulled to your Zune collection.

If none of the suggestions above help, the next thing to do is start manually adding meta data.  You can do this via the Zune software by right clicking each album and then selecting edit.  This will give you a series of fields where you can add the album title, artist info, album art and song information.  If you prefer you can also do this via the File Explorer by locating the files on your computer drive and then right clicking and going to properties.  Within properties you can then go to details where you will be able to update the details of individual song files.

graham mayor say:
There are a number of ways to do this. My preference would be to have a Master document containing the table and use an IncludeText field to include all or part of it in each of the two documents.

Make the changes to the master document only and save the two 'slave' documents as macro enabled documents in a trusted location (to avoid the macro security prompts). In the This Document module of the slave documents add the following macro to update the fields upon opening the documents, to reflect any changes in the master.

Option Explicit
Sub Document_Open()
ActiveDocument.Fields.Update
End Sub
