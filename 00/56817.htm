After working with Verizon's Customer Service via a PM forum, a work around for this problem has been discovered.

Note I said "work around".

First things first, the default server settings supplied by Microsoft for verizon.net Emails is incorrect if a user is not being migrated to AOL or Yahoo servers. Verizon stated that users in areas where Verizon is selling off local networks to other providers are being migrated off of Verizon's servers.

The work around for Email addresses that are not migrating is to manually build them in your device and use the correct settings from the get-go.

The following are the steps to accomplish this:

Email -> Settings -> Manage Accounts ->Add Account -> (scroll down to the bottom of the list where you will see "Advanced Setup").

. Select Advanced Setup

. Select Internet Email (POP or IMAP accounts.......)

. Enter the Email address you wish to add

. Under "username" just re-type the name of the account.

. Under "password" type the password to the Email account.

. Under "Account Name" I entered "Verizon 1" or "Verizon 2" etc...

. Under "Send your message using this name" I added my name in a formal layout.

  For example "Smith, John A.", but, that is simply personal preference.

. Under "Incoming Email Server" type "pop.verizon.net:995:1"

. For "Account Type" choose" POP3"

. Under "Outgoing (SMTP) Email Server" type "smtp.verizon.net:465:1"

. Make sure all four boxes below that are checked marked.

Click "Sign in", refresh the Email.

Again, this work around procedure applies to non-migrating Verizon Email accounts as both of my Surface units are now able to sync to my Verizon Emails.
