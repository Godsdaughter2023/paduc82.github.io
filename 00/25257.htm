chip pearson say:

Try

=D1-CHOOSE(WEEKDAY(D1,1),2,3,4,5,6,0,1)

where D1 is the date in question.

 

hansv mvp say:

Or, again with a date in D1:

 

=D1+1-WEEKDAY(D1,15)

 

This works in Excel 2010 and later only.

Excel wrongly recalculating when changing filter conditions - Microsoft Community

daveboltie say:

Excel wrongly recalculating when changing filter conditions

Excel 2013 64 bit (and other older versions) recalculate all dirty formulas when something changes, using a clearly defined methodology as described at https://msdn.microsoft.com/en-us/library/office/bb687891.aspx . This makes good sense. 

However, changing the filtering criteria causes Excel to recalculate, even when no Excel functions that depend on the filtering criteria (such as SUBTOTAL etc) are present in the spreadsheet. Also no volatile functions like TODAY() or RAND() etc. which could give different results each time, and thus make dependent cells dirty.

Evidently the recalculation algorithm is flawed. There are no dirty cells after changing a filter criterion, so no recalculation is needed, and yet Excel recalculates. Am I doing something wrong, or is it a bug?

Many forums on the internet give workarounds such as disabling automatic recalculation, but this is like making the patient comfortable, rather than curing his brain tumour. 

Of course this is only really a problem when working with big spreadsheets and lots for formulas. The one I'm using right now is typical, with 110,000 rows x 75 columns. It can take several minutes to recalc on my i5 machine, so very painful. In case you were wondering, memory usage is way below the amount of physical RAM available, so no swapping to pagefile.sys.

EDIT: Further to that: With automatic calculation OFF, the recalculating the entire spreadsheet with no filters is much faster than recalculating with a filter on. Once again, no functions that give different results depending on filtering (such as SUBTOTAL) and no volatile functions (like NOW).
