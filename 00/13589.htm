Vlookup that references cells on multiple tabs in another workbook - Microsoft Community

skeet ponder say:

Vlookup that references cells on multiple tabs in another workbook

Hi. I'm trying to do a vlookup that references cells in another workbook, but want the tab from which it pulls to come from a cell in my original sheet that the formula is being typed into. Does such a thing exist outside of macros? 

Thanks!

hansv mvp say:

You can use INDIRECT for this purpose, but it will only work if the other workbook is also open in Excel.

Let's say that:

  • The lookup value is in A2
  • The name of the tab in which you want to search is in B2
  • The other workbook is named Other.xlsx.
  • The lookup range is A2:D100.
  • You want to return a value from column D.

The formula would be

=VLOOKUP(A2, INDIRECT("'[Other.xlsx]" & B2 & "'!A2:D100"), 4, FALSE)

Not receiving e-mail from contacts:

If you aren't receiving e-mail that has been sent by people you know, it may be because your Junk e-mail filter or your Blocked sender list has excluded them.

Please check your junk mail filter settings and your blocked sender’s lists to ensure that they are not preventing the emails sent to you from reaching your mailbox. You will also want to add the address to your safe senders list once again and check if that helps.

Check your Junk folder

1. Sign in to your Outlook account.

2. Click on the "Junk" button in the left pane

3. Check to see if the missing mail is already in this folder.

4. If you see any messages that should have been delivered to the inbox here, click to put a check in the box to the left of each message and then click the "Not Junk" button

Check your Junk Mail Settings

1. In the upper-right corner of the page, click Options, and then click More options.

2. Under Junk e-mail, click Filters and reporting.

3. Under Choose a junk e-mail filter, choose the level of protection that you want, and then click Save. If the Exclusive level was set, messages from senders not on your Contacts or Safe sender’s lists will have been sent to the Junk e-mail folder.

2. Click Blocked senders.

Mail Merge spaces

Greetings,

I'm doing a Mail Merge in Word with Excel as the de facto database.  If I start an Excel cell with a space, the Mail Merge blows it away like it was never there.  All that survives the Mail Merge are the remaining characters after the space.  I've learned that I can use an underscore as a space keeper instead.  Is there an alternative to the underscore as a space-keeping first character in an Excel cell, please?

Alvis6

macropod say:

This behaviour is by design and AFAIK you can't change it. If the need is to insert one or more spaces before the mergefield when it has data, but not otherwise, you can achieve that by adding a formatting switch to the field. For example, assume there's a «MiddleName» mergefield. Click on this and you should see:

{ MERGEFIELD MiddleName }

Add a \b switch to this with however many spaces (and/or other characters) you need, thus:

{ MERGEFIELD MiddleName \b "  " } (for two spaces)

There is also \f switch for adding conditional content after the field.

All of the domain aggregate commands, including Dlookup, are very slow compared to queries. This is true in any version of Access, and any version of Windows I've seen. But the performance I've seen with Windows 7 is comparable to XP using my same databases. 

Access is very network intensive. I've seen problems with Win7 network drivers being very poor, especially in 64-bit environments. Perhaps try updating your network driver?

One of the oldies but goodies as far as speed goes is to make sure the Track Name Autocorrect is not checked (under Access Option - Current database).
