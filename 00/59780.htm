a. user say:

Did you look in the windows.old folder?

Something to know: The desktop was not intended to be a place to store files, even though everybody does it (including me.) The desktop is a system folder, not a user folder. The place to store your files is the default user folders: Documents, Music, Pictures and Video. You can store your stuff anywhere, but only the contents of those folders are going to be preserved because that's where your user files are supposed to be. The desktop is for shortcuts to files, not the files themselves.

You can get away with storing files on the desktop on a day to day basis because, even though it's wrong, it doesn't matter. When you upgrade your operating system, it matters.
