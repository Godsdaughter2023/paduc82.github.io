How To Use Ctrl + Tab To Switch Between Tabs In Recent Order In Internet Explorer

Internet Explorer 8 and 9 lets users to use the Ctrl + Tab combination to switch between tabs quickly.Many users want to use combination to switch tabs in recent order.

1. Launch IE 8 or IE 9.

2. Click on the Tools In IE8 and small gear icon on IE9 on the top right of the program interface, and then go to Internet Options.

3.Now go to the Advanced tab.

4. Scroll down to the Settings section, and put a check next to the Use most recent order when switching tabs with Ctrl + Tab option.

 

 

  

5. Now, click OK to save the setting. Enjoy!!!

How do I transfer an activation for Office 2010 home and student from - Microsoft Community

fernandl5610 say:

How do I transfer an activation for Office 2010 home and student from one PC to another?

I installed a copy of the program on my new laptop, the old one crashed, and now Microsoft tells me I cannot activate it as the maximum number of activations has been reached. Why can't I transfer an activatiob, other software companies do have that option.
brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

That depends on the type of license you purchase.  If you purchased a three-activation license, then simply install it on the new PC and activate it.  If you purchased a Product Key Card license (one activation), you may be out of luck.  PKC licenses are permanently tied to the hardware on which you first install it and they are not transferrable to another PC.

That said, install the product and use the Activate by Phone option.  Do not respond to any of the voice prompts until you hear one that asks if you want to speak to a person.  Take that option, explain what happened, and the person who answered may allow the new activation.

dan at it associates say:

Your rights to transfer the licensed activation(s) depends upon which license you purchased.

 

1-User 1-PC licenses acquired as downloads from the online Microsoft Store and Product Key Card licenses do not permit moving the license from the device (aka computer) on which the license was first activated, regardless of the reason.

 

1-User 2-PC licenses acquired as downloads from the online Microsoft Store and traditional packaged disc licenses permit moving the licensed activation(s) from computer to computer as needed.

 

Which did you buy?
