With the auto forwarding feature, we are unable to choose specific emails to be forwarded because it will forward all of your emails to another email account. Although there is a feature where you can create a rule to forward mails from a specific sender or specific Subject line to your main account, but that will not achieve your concern as it will not forward all of the content of you junk mail whenever there is a new junk mail received in your secondary email account. Here's how to set the auto forwarding in yout account (Set this on your secondary account):

 

1. Sign in to https://www.outlook.com/, In the upper-right corner of the Outlook home page, click the gear icon.

3. Under Managing account, click Email forwarding.
4. On the Forwarding page, click Forward your mail to another e-mail account.
5. In the address field, enter the e-mail address where you want to forward your e-mail to, and then click Save. You may also check the box Keep a copy of forwarded messages in your Microsoft account inbox.
6. After you specify an address where you want to forward your e-mail to, you must reply to an e-mail that asks you to validate your account.

 

And here is the rule that you can set on your main account:

 

1. Sign in to your Outlook account
2. In the upper-right corner of the page, click on the Gear Icon

3. Click More mail settings

4. Under Customizing Hotmail, click Rules for sorting new messages
5. In step 1 in the first option choose Sender's address line then on the second option is then on the third option put the email you are receiving from another sender.

6. Then on Step 2 click the first option Move to, click it then pick the folder you prefer.

7. Click Save.

 

You can set up multiple rules to sort emails you receive from your secondary account.
