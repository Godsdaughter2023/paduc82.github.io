Hi again,


I turned on the macro recorder in Excel and recorded the following:


Sub Macro3()

    Range("B4").Select

    ActiveCell.FormulaR1C1 = "=SUM(R[-2]C:R[-1]C)"

    ActiveSheet.Shapes.Range(Array("TextBox 3")).Select

    Selection.ShapeRange(1).TextFrame2.TextRange.Characters.Text = "=SUM(B2:B3)"

    With Selection.ShapeRange(1).TextFrame2.TextRange.Characters(1, 11). _

        ParagraphFormat

        .TextDirection = msoTextDirectionLeftToRight

        .FirstLineIndent = 0

        .HangingPunctuation = msoTrue

    End With

    With Selection.ShapeRange(1).TextFrame2.TextRange.Characters(1, 11).Font

        .NameComplexScript = "+mn-cs"

        .NameFarEast = "+mn-ea"

        .Fill.Visible = msoTrue

        .Fill.ForeColor.ObjectThemeColor = msoThemeColorText1

        .Fill.ForeColor.TintAndShade = 0

        .Fill.ForeColor.Brightness = 0

        .Fill.Transparency = 0

        .Fill.Solid

        .Size = 18

        .Kerning = 12

        .Name = "+mn-lt"

    End With

End Sub

Short story: when Microsoft had finalized the design specs for 2008 Microsoft had decided VBA was going to go away in Office Mac and PC. There was a huge outcry from developers. Microsoft decided to keep VBA, but by then it was too late to put it back into 2008. Even worse, Apple switched from PPC to Intel processors. That meant the entire VB editor and compiler had to be re-written from scratch, which is exactly what happened for the Office 2011 release.


Considering what Microsoft had to do to get VBA working again in Office 2011, I think they did an amazingly good job in a very short amount of time.
