Hi NervEasy,

If the issue started after the Fall Creators Update, rolling back the driver may help resolve the issue.

  1. Open Device Manager.
  2. In Device Manager, expand Sound, video and game controllers.
  3. Right click the audio driver and choose Properties.
  4. Select the Driver tab and click on the Roll Back Driver button.

If the Roll Back Driver button is disabled, Windows does not have a previous driver to roll back to, so you won't be able to complete this process. Reinstall the audio driver. Right click the audio driver and choose Uninstall, do not check the box for delete driver software. Restart your device and let Windows reinstall the driver.

If the issue still persists, install the latest Surface drivers.
