barb way say:

Unfortunately, Visio 2003 is not able to use indexed searching on 64-bit operating systems.  It relies on the use of an IFilter to tell the indexing service how to find the metadata to index, and the 64-bit IFilter is not supported by Visio 2003 (or Visio 2007).  This is noted in support article : http://support.microsoft.com/kb/2395490

What this means is that your computer uses only the keywords found in the stencil files, since it cannot see the tags in the shapes themselves.  As you have seen, not very good results, and sometimes confusing.

I'm sorry that there is no tool to correct this limitation of the older versions of Visio.  It isn't a problem for Visio 2010, in case you are considering an upgrade in the future.

Barb

jason (office engineer) say:

Hi Holly,

We're glad you're trying Sway out!  I will do my best to answer your questions, but first I want to share a little about how Sway works.  One of the primary goals for Sway is to produce output that looks great anywhere - from a small mobile device up to a large projection screen.  No matter the device or screen size that a user views it on, Sway will produce a layout that is tailored to it.  This means that the layout can slightly change depending on where the Sway is viewed.  In order to accomplish that, Sway does not allow the author to specify things like content size or precise positioning, instead it has you put the content in the desired order and asks you to communicate the intent of that content and handles the rest.  As such, you may not be able to directly control every aspect of your Sway that you are asking about, but we hope you get an output you are pleased with.

1. One thing you could try is changing the emphasis on your image (the buttons on the upper right of the image card).  In most layout/style combinations, more emphasis means that the image will be larger and placed onto its own line.

2. Try the image card emphasis along with a combination of focus points to get your image sized the way you want.  The logo feature is meant to add a small logo-sized version of the image to the beginning of your Sway.  There is more flexibility for the image when it is in its own card.  You could also try grouping it with your text or even putting it in a grid for a more fixed layout.

3. Header size cannot be directly controlled.  Different styles result in different sizing, but so do background images.  You could try including a short and wide solid colored background image on your heading to see if that gets it closer to what you want.

4. A couple of the styles do not put the title over the background image, but most do.  If you don't want it to display like that, you might need to forgo the title background image.

5. Currently, the logo only has one size.  You could try uploading a larger version of it with/without some blank space padding around the logo to see if that it affects it.

Let me know if you have further questions.

Jason

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

Are the documents that are exhibiting this behaviour received as attachments to email messages?  If so, perhaps it is in Protected View that they are opening, rather than as Read Only.  You can prevent that happening in two ways:

  1. Save the attachment to the local drive and then right click on it in the File Explorer and then select Properties and check the Unblock box.
  2. In Word, go to File>Options>Trust Center>Trust Center Settings>Protected View and uncheck the box for "Enable Protect View for Outlook attachments" 

    You should first be aware of the warning that appears under the Protected View heading in that dialog.
