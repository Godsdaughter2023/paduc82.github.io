Hi,

 

It is a pleasure to help you with your issue. I understand how difficult it can be, when you are not receiving emails.

 

It is possible that the emails sent to your account were received on the other folders that you have. I suggest you to check all the folders that you have on your Inbox page.

 

In addition, please refer to the steps below to know what you can do when you aren’t getting emails from friends/family.

 

A. Email Forwarding

1. On your Inbox page, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.

2. Under "Managing your account", click Email forwarding.

Note: If it's set to forward your mail to another email account, tick on Don't forward then click Save.

 

B. Blocked Sender's List

1. On your Inbox page, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.

2. Under "Preventing Junk email", click Safe and blocked senders.

3. Click Blocked senders.

4. In the Blocked senders list, select the name or domain (the part of an e-mail address that follows the @ sign, such as Outlook.com) that you now want to receive mail from, and then click<< Remove from list.

 

C. Add the email address to your Safe senders list

1. On your Inbox page, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.

2. Under "Preventing junk email", click Safe and blocked senders.

3. Click Safe Senders.

4.In the text box, type in the email address or domain you’d like to always receive email from, and then click Add to list >>.

justinnale say: Frustrating indeed!  Here's a way to avoid retyping the filename:

1.) Click the Down arrow button to the right of the "Save As: Untitled" text box
2.) This will show the Finder dialog for the current working directory, such as "Outlook Temp"
3.) Click on the greyed out name of the file you wish to save, e.g. "some_long_name_0114.xlsx"
4.) This will grab the name to the the "Save As" field above
5.) Select the location you want to save to, such as Desktop, Documents, etc.
6.) If desired, set the Format type
7.) Click Save


And there we are, no retyping filenames after an unexpected edit session.
