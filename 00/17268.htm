Hello,

is that happening in all Excel workbooks with pivot tables, or just one workbook?

If it's in all workbooks, the field list may have been undocked from its normal position on the right of the window and be floating somewhere else on your monitor. If you have multiple monitors, it may even be on a different monitor. 

If you can see the field list as a floating window, double click the top bar of the field list to dock it back to the Excel window.

If you can't see the field list at all, navigate to the username\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Excel folder, and delete the excel15.xlb files from both that folder and the XLSTARTUP folder. Close Excel, and re-open Excel. The field list should now be back in its usual (docked) place.

If it only happens in one file, it may well be that the creator of the file has taken measures to hide the field list.

andreas killer say:
IMHO Excel is the easiest program to setup/configure the basics and you can use macros to create Word documents or PDF's directly.

The question is how complex your configuration should be and how large the combinations are.

Chipotle is good example, let us assume we like to build a meal maker:

The basis are the main ingredients, let us write "Main" in cell A1 and "Burrito" and "Taco" below, see this as a table.

Now we have to choose a kind of meat and vegetables. Write "Meat" in cell A5 and "Steak" and "Chicken" below. In cell A10 write "Vegetables" and "Tomatoes", "Pepper", "Corn" below.

The question is how would you combine these tables? Well, a simple idea is to write "Meat" in cell B2 and "Vegetables" in cell C2.

Based on this a macro can start in the main table and let you choose "Burrito" or "Taco", then go to the next column on the right of what you have choosen, finds "Meat", searches that table and let your choose between "Chicken" and "Steak". etc. etc.

If you want exclude the "Corn" for "Taco"s, create a second table "VegatablesB" with "Tomatoes" and "Beens" and assign that in the "Main" table, write "Meat" in cell B3 and "VegatablesB" in cell C3.

This is just a simple idea how to combine individual tables. The essence here is that there must be a simple structure which can be recognized with a macro, then there are (almost) no limits of what you can do.

Think about it and find a concept that seems to be possible for you. Then try to configure that on a piece of paper. Only when you are sure that it is possible on the paper start to program. To modify a concept retrospectively is (almost) impossible.
