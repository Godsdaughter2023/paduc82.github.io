a. user say:

When you do that on Windows Word, Word inserts a "\t" option in the HYPERLINK field that it creates - I would guess you are using \t "_blank", but it does not really matter

Unfortunately, that option is not available on Mac Word, and even if you  insert \t "_blank" (or whatever) into the Hyperlink field  manually, 
 a. I don't think Mac Word honours the option
 b. if you save the document as HTML, Mac Word does not honour the option. On WIndows Word, Word puts a target="_blank" attribute in the HTML <a> element

So what to do depends on what exactly you are putting on your site.
  a. If you are saving your Word docs as HTML, you'll need to add that target attribute to all the links after you have saved the HTML. 
  b. If you are putting copies of your Word documents on your site (e.g. in .doc or .docx format) then I suspect the best you will be able to do is
  - put the \t "_blank" option in the field manually
  - rely on the user client that opens that .doc to do the right thing (i.e., if the user opens your document on a Mac, it probably won't; if they open it using Word on a PC, it might (I don't know, sorry).

You cannot make a PDF with live hyperlinks in Word Mac 2011.

The Word:Mac Table of Contents generator does not generate hyperlinks. This will never be fixed.

You can generate the TOC in Word for PC, then save as PDF from Word PC -- you will get your hyperlinks.
