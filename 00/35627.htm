considerate_guy say:

The .vob files are the "video object" files which contain the video content (chapters, menus, etc.) encoded in an MPEG-2 video format. It's a standard definition file format and requires a license to decode/encode.

Before you do anything, make sure you aren't violating any copyrights by trying to copy/edit commercial movies. Also, be aware that it may be hard to deal with uploading files of that size to a website and they may have restrictions on size or content.

Depending on your version of Windows, you may not have the requisite codec to decode the .vob files for editing. But if you do, make a copy of the .vob file you want to edit by copying it to your hard disk. You don't want to use it directly from the DVD or else you'll be trying to have Movie Maker preview all your edits from the DVD disc which may not work.

Movie Maker can accept .vob files as input, but can only output in HD formats like .wmv or .mp4. As a result, you'll probably find that even though you are trimming the content of the movies, the ultimate file size goes up. But if you are okay with that go ahead. Add the file(s) a project, make your edits and then save the final results back out as .mp4 video (the default in Movie Maker 2012).
