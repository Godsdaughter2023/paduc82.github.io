  1. Right click on the folder you need to share and click on Properties.
  2. Go to Security tab. Click on Advanced tab.
  3. Click on Change.
  4. Click on Advanced option.
  5. Click on Find now and select the User account name that you want to add or allow all the permission for.
  6. Click on OK. Click on Apply and then on OK.
  7. Check the box that says “Replace owners on sub containers and objects”. Click on OK.
  8. The permission will be changed. Check replace owner on sub containers and objects too.

  1. Right click on the folder you need to share and click on Properties.
  2. Click on the Sharing tab and then click on Advanced Sharing.
  3. Place a check mark on Share this folder.
  4. Click on Permissions and then click on Add and type Everyone and press ok.
  5. Select Everyone from the list and then select Full control and then press Apply and Ok.

"Access Denied" or other errors when you access or work with files and folders in Windows

Stop One Note 2013 from making Tables with TAB button - Microsoft Community

jessrobso say:

Stop One Note 2013 from making Tables with TAB button

How do I stop this feature?

I use TAB frequently while making my notes. There doesn't seem to be an undo autocorrect option when this happens.

This advice doesn't work for me. I am using 2013:

Create an Auto-correct rule that turns your chosen character sequence (e.g. "\t") into a regular TAB:

  1. Open Notepad and type one TAB. Select it and copy it.
  2. Go to OneNote and click Tools > Auto-correct Options...
  3. In the dialog, put your desired sequence of characters (e.g. \t, or \tab, or TAB, or <t>, or whatever you like) into the "Replace:" field.
  4. Paste the TAB that you copied from Notepad into the "With:" filed. Click Add.

Now, any \t followed by a SPACE will be replaced by a regular TAB.
