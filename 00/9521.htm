Hi there,

You have the arguments backwards. You're getting the #NUM! error because the start date is later than the end date. Put your reference first.

That being said, you can use something like this instead to overflow years, months, and days:

=IF(DATEDIF(F4,TODAY(),"y")>0,DATEDIF(F4,TODAY(),"y")&" years, ","")&IF(DATEDIF(F4,TODAY(),"ym")>0,DATEDIF(F4,TODAY(),"ym")&" months, ","")&DATEDIF(F4,TODAY(),"md")&" days"

alan wu msft say:

Hi Udi,

Greetings. I am glad to assist you for your case here.

Via your description, I have understood that you want to hide your email server (ie, onmicrosoft.com). Please be noted that it is not feasible to do so. As you know every email server (recipient side) needs to check the incoming email from the sender. For example, the recipient email server needs to check whether the sender has the legitimate right to send from the domain. That is the sender's SPF record of the domain plays such an role (making it is legitimate for the sender to send from the domain). Suppose if the sender's server is hidden, in other words, the recipient side can't check the sender's legitimate right. The email from the sender will not be successfully delivered. 

Here, I come across that only the hacker can "hide" the real sender's server. Actually, it is more accurate to say he just mask his server to be another sender's server (no way to hide a server). But it is not legitimate to do so. 

So, your understanding is highly appreciated. 

Regards,

Alan
