How do I create a special size template 3.5x2.75 to print cards with - Microsoft Community

a. user say:

How do I create a special size template 3.5x2.75 to print cards with text and photos?

I want to create a special size template 3.5 X 2.75 business card with being able to print on both sides using text and photos. How do I do this? Can it be done in Word?
doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

I would suggest that the best thing to do is check the Staples or Office Depot to determine their capabilities.

That advantage of using a standard Business Card product is that they are pre-perforated so that you do not need a guillotine to cut the cards to size.  However, the standard size is only 2" x 3.5".  If however Staples and\or Office Depot have facilities to crop the cards to the required size, you would not then be restricted to that size.

As far as Word is concerned, the way to set up the document is as a table with fixed cell dimensions, in a document that has identical left and right margins.

Two 3.5" wide cards will take up 7", which leaves 3/4" for each margin on an 8-1/2" x 11" sheet

You may need to reduce the height of the cards to 2.5" as 4 at 2.75" comes out to exactly 11" and you may run into difficulties with the print being cut off.  That's with the document in Portrait orientation.

On the other hand, if you have the document in Landscape Orientation, you could have 3 columns of 3.5" with a 1/4 margin at either side and 3 rows at 2.75" with a 3/8" top and bottom margin.

Your document will need to have two pages, each with the identical table on it, one for the front of the card and the other for the back.

Windows Mobile 6 will run most applications initially designed for WM5. WM2003 applications will not work though.

 

Pocket PC was used to name a device with a touch screen runing Windows Mobile Pocket PC Phone edition or just Pocket PC (devices without phones). Let's say that Pocket PC reflects more the hardware and Windows Mobile is the software, the OS, running on it.

Any ideas?
boblarson say: Welcome to Microsoft Answers.

I believe you may be misunderstanding how Access stores records.  Access does not store records in a sequential manner.  It may APPEAR to do so many times, but it really does not.  In fact, it really doesn't matter to Access where it stores it.  So, what you have to do, if you want a certain order, is to use a QUERY and the appropriate field to sort on to ensure that the records are in the order you want.  For example sorting on a date/time stamp field so that the last record entered can be selected.  You can also (perhaps) use a numeric primary key as the sort, but that doesn't always guarantee (if using autonumbers) that the number will be consecutive, nor will it guarantee it to be a positive number.  All an autonumber will guarantee is a UNIQUE number.
