a. user say:

Hello ramysedky:

Thank you for posting to the forum regarding the issues you are having with your Windows Phone.  I will do my best to provide some things to try for both your issues.  I do hope that some progress can be made.

I will start with the phone being recognized as this is likely the most important of the two.  The phone should indeed be seen in file explorer as an external device.  If it is not, that indicates you have larger issues with your computer, the sync cable or the phone itself.  Go to File Explorer by clicking Start and then My Computer on your computer.  The phone should display here and you can double click it to see the folders that contain you photos, music, videos and documents.  If you do not see the phone listed as a drive here you will want to go to device manager and see if there is an issue with the phone connecting.  You can get to device manager by click start > right clicking My Computer > selecting manage > going to device manager.  Look for the phone to be listed as portable device or other device.  Double click the listed phone and look for an error reported in the general tab.  You should have some steps provided here, if not, let us know the error or message you see.  If you can get the phone to a working state here you can simply drag and drop files to and from the phone.

If the phone cannot be recognized by this computer I would suggest you try the following.

  • Try connecting the phone via other USB ports on this computer.
  • Try connecting to this computer with a different sync cable.
  • Try connecting this phone with this cable and with other cables to other computers.  This is the most important thing to try as this will best tell us if this is related to your phone, your computer or possible the cable.

When it comes to the issue with the SD card there are only a couple things I can suggest outside of a factory reset.  Try each of these and see if any progress can be made with the card being recognized.

  • Format the SD card and try adding it to the phone.  I would suggest you try formatting this via your brothers phone first, do a safe removal and try again.  If this does not help, try formatting the phone via the computer.
  • If the issue continues, try a soft reset of the phone by going to settings > storage sense > phone > temporary files > delete > Internet Explorer settings > delete history.  Once this is done, press and hold the down volume button + the power button until the phone vibrates and reboots.  Once rebooted, try mounting the card one more time.
  • If possible, try a different SD card in your phone.  I see you confirmed your card works in another phone.  It would be good to know if other cards work in yours.
  • Finally, turn your phone OFF and connect the phone to a charger.  Remove the battery from your phone while connected.  Remove the SD card and then reinsert the SD card and the battery.

Please let us know if you have any other questions or concerns we can assist you with.

Mark W.
