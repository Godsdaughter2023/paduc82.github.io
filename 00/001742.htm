Try the below troubleshooting methods and check if this helps.

Method 1: Show And Enable The Headphones Manually.

  1. Right-click on the Sound icon from the taskbar > Click on Sounds > Go to Playback tab > Right-click on an empty area in the Playback tab.
  2. Select Show Disconnected Devices and Show Disabled Devices > Check if you are able to see the headphones, else restart the computer once and check.
  3. If you are able to see the headphones, then right-click on the headphones > Click on Enable > Click on Set Default > Then, check if you are able to hear from your headphones.

Method 2: Reinstalling The Audio Drivers.

  1. Unplug the headphones and the speakers from the computer and go to Device Manager.
  2. Double-click on Sound, video and game controllers to expand > Right-click on the audio device and click on Uninstall.
  3. Note: Make sure to check box the option Delete the driver software for this device when uninstalling.
  4. After the uninstall, restart the computer once > Check for Windows Updates to install the audio drivers and check if the issue persists.
