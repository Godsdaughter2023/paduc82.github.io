Based on your description, it sound like you create a number of rtfs (e.g.t hat you aren't trying to create the PDF on the fly each time you create an rtf).

If your rtfs open with the correct format in Word (test this to be certain) then you can write a macro in Word to go to a specific folder , set of folders, or a user-defined folder (each time you run the macro) and convert every rtf in that folder to a pdf using the built-in pdf export function in the office products. As part of that macro you can move and/or rename the source files so you don't have to worry about making duplicate pdfs.

vincentchoy say:

Journaling is available, and has been available for years on Exchange Online.

However, do take note of the new architecture change moving to Exchange Online. In the on-prem world, both your Exchange and Archive System is on-prem. This means journaling occurs via LAN.

Once you move to Exchange Online, the Exchange Server is no longer in your data centre. This means that every mail sent will be sent to an external mail service (Microsoft datacenters) and every mail is also journaled back to your on-prem Archive. You have to take a look how this might impact your internet bandwidth.

A better way might be to migrate to a cloud based archive service, so that mails can be journaled direct to the cloud archive service from Microsoft datacenter without coming back to your company.

In the on-prem world, you backup your own exchange servers. For Exchange Online servers, Microsoft provides the redundancy.  There is no requirement for you to backup the Exchange Servers on Microsoft's behalf.

For data on your on-prem exchange servers, I believe you rely both on your backups as well as your Archive. For Exchange Online, Microsoft provides a retention policy of deleted mails for from 14 days up to a max of 30 days after a user empties his recycle bin. To preserve deleted mail, you either put the mailboxes on "hold", which requires E3 or above or a separate Exchange Online Archive subscription or you rely on 3rd party archive services like what you have.

Hope that explains.

vincentchoy say:

OK now I understand where you are coming from.

Exchange Online allows Journaling to external mailboxes - we have done that with Veritas EV.Cloud archive solution for many clients.

You mention your current solution requires journaling to a Exchange Mailbox. Microsoft states that you can't set a Exchange Online mailbox as a journaling mailbox.

As mentioned, Microsoft handles the exchange online server backups and its avaliability.  However, it does not keep deleted mails for you after the max retention period (which is 30 days).

So I guess the solutions available to you are -

1. See if your current mail archive software has a solution for Exchange Online. Do note my comments about journaling back every mail from exchange online and the bandwidth it requires.

2. Change to a 3rd party Cloud Archive solution, that Office365 can journal to direct from Microsoft datacenters. 

3. Change to Microsoft's Exchange Online Archive, available as a add on to lower Exchange Online Plans, or comes with E3 and above. This will give you legal hold capability and unlimited Archive storage. However, to preserve all deleted mails, you will have to enable Hold from day one. Any mailbox without hold turned on would not preserve deleted mails beyond the system retention periods.
