jay freedman say:

I have no idea why the article has that disclaimer -- particularly since Word 2004 was a Macintosh version, not a Windows version, and I know that Suzanne isn't a Mac user. It is true that the article discusses only the menus and toolbar buttons in Word 2003 and earlier, but the fundamentals of inserting a section break, turning off the Same As Previous linkage, and changing the number formatting in only one section remain the same in Word 2007 and 2010.

The main difference is that in Word 2007, the commands for working with headers and footers are on the Header & Footer Tools tab of the ribbon, which appears only when the cursor is in a header or footer. Suzanne discusses this in the "What's new for Word 2007 Users" section of http://sbarnhill.mvps.org/WordFAQs/HeaderFooter.htm

To summarize the steps for formatting the frontmatter page numbers differently from the body page numbers:

  1. Place the cursor at the beginning of the page where the Arabic numbering is to start. Click Page Layout > Breaks > Next Page. If there was a manual page break at that point, delete it.
  2. Double-click in the footer (or header) area to open it. The  Header & Footer Tools tab of the ribbon appears.
  3. Put the cursor in the footer (or header) of the second section -- click the Next Section button if necessary. Note the "Same As Previous" tag on the dashed line.
  4. Click the Link To Previous button to turn it off.
  5. Go to the footer (header) of section 1, right-click the page number, and choose Edit Field. In the dialog, choose the roman numeral format. Click OK.
  6. Close the footer (header) pane.
