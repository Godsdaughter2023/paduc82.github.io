Hi Beinn,


This issue might occur if the headset is not connected properly or the headset pins are not getting recognized. As the Microphone Line-in shows that it’s not plugged-in, probably, it could also be due to incorrect driver or the driver isn’t installed properly.


  1. What is the Make and Model of the computer?
  2. When you say after downloading some audio drivers, are you referring to driver for the headset or for the computer?
  3. How exactly are you setting up the connection?


Check if the same issue occurs when you connect the headset to a different computer. Also, check if other headsets are getting detected on this computer.


Let’s start off with the basic troubleshooting. I see that there is an adapter to be used during the setup. 


You have mentioned that the audio input that you have used is green. Try it with green port. That might be the reason for the issue. Usually, for desktop computers:

a) The microphone connector is usually pink.

b) The line-in connector is usually blue.


Also, check if the follow settings are appropriate:

  • Click Start, and then click Control Panel.
  • Click Hardware and Sounds, and then click Managing audio devices.
  • A Sound window opens. Click the Recording tab.

black flash say:

how do I remove worksheet from desktop calculator

While using the desktop calculator in Windows 7, I selected one of the worksheets just to see what it looked like. Now I cannot get rid of it.
cmdrkeene say:
It's not very intuitive, but when you look at that View menu, the Worksheets is part of the last section.  It can be any of the first 3 options:

  • Basic
  • Unit Conversion
  • Date Conversion

    ...or it can be one of several worksheets, all of which are grouped into a single heading:

  • Worksheets


To go back to a normal view, choose the Basic option (or press Ctrl-F4, which is the keyboard shortcut for "Basic" view inside calculator).
