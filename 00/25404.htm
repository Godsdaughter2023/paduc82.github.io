diane poremsky mvp (slipstick.com) say:

Method 3 should fix it and is done in regular mode (i forgot that kb has users try other things first)  - The short version of the fix is below, see the KB for the full steps.

  1. Locate and then click the following registry key:

    HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Protected Storage System Provider
  2. On the Edit menu, click Permissions.
  3. Click the registry key for the user who is currently logged on. Then, make sure that the Read and Full Control permissions are both set to Allow.
  4. Click Advanced. Then, verify that the user who is currently logged on is selected, that Full Control is listed in the Permissions column, and that This Key and Subkeys is listed in the Apply to column.
  5. Click to select the Replace permission entries on all child objects with entries shown here that apply to child objects check box.
  6. Click Apply, and then click Yes to continue.
  7. Click Ok two times
  8. Double-click the Protected Storage System Provider key to expand the key. Click the user subkey folder that is directly underneath the
    Protected Storage System Provider
    key, click Delete on the Edit menu, and then click Yes in the warning message dialog box.

Windows Live Contact Sync Issues with HTC Wing Phone - Microsoft Community

s wf say:

Windows Live Contact Sync Issues with HTC Wing Phone

I have several issues with Windows Live  - Mobile 6 and I will start with the latest...

1. When the Windows Live Sync fails due to server connections, etc. It will delete all my contacts until it is able to establish an updated sync. How can I retain my contacts while sync fails?

2. Originally I had my contacts sync with my work outlook, but found that my personal email information was being posted back to my office PC (which is not allowed by our company). I tried to remove the outlook sync and it removed all my phone contacts as well. That is why I went to Windows live contacts. I need contacts on my phone without dependencies, are their any options?

Updated information 12/31/08

Problem:
Now that I know that when you sync your contacts with Windows Live or Outlook (using exchange or your windows PC version) they own your contacts. When syncrohinization fails to these external sources, your phone contacts are lost until reconnection. There are work arounds like resetting your Live Account email and password over again on your mobile. But to be perfectly honest, I was in a situation where I needed to call my family immediately and I could not since the contacts were not sync'd at that time. I do not have time to spare when I need to make a call. 

Resolution:
Have the phone owns it's own contacts and do not sync with anything, only back them up often.
