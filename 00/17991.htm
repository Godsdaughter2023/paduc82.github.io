greg wilkerson say:

HTC Mogul by Sprint ROM Upgrade Results

 

Well, I just finished upgrading my Mogul by Sprint ROM to version 3.35.651.2 (EV-DO Rev.A).  Overall, the upgrade went well.  The instructions provided by the install program were detailed and procedurally accurate.  The upgrade took about 15 minutes.

 

Here's what didn't work, or got screwed up.

 

First, don't rely on ActiveSync to backup your personal information.  It only does a partial job, at best.  I had it set to sync my contacts, tasks and calendar.  That data is safely back on my upgraded Mogul.

 

I did not have ActiveSync setup to sync my favorites.  If you're like me, I have hundreds of favorites on my desktop, all of them directed to non-mobile sites that would be of very limited usability on my Mogul,  I don't need all that clutter on my Mogul.  The upgrade completely trashed all the mobile specific link in my favorites.  I am not happy!

 

I had the files on the storage card set to be encrypted.  I did not copy them off the storage card to my local PC because I didn't see any reason why the ROM upgrade would mess with those.  Well, that was a bad assumption.  The upgrade didn't do anything with the files, but the files on the storage card are unrecognizable by their associated programs.  My guess is that the encryption key is different.  I can browse them, copy them between main memory and storage and copy them to the desktop, but I can't open them.  This is a big deal to me.  I had business related spreadsheets on the storage card, and unless I can find a way to get those files back, they're gone.  I lost my ringers, mp3, xls, doc and jpgs that were on the storage card.  I am not happy about that, either.  Actually, me not being happy is a gross understatement.

 

I also lost all my messaging credentials.  This might be more of an issue with the storage card than the upgrade, but I have no real way of knowing.  This is more of an annoyance than a real problem.  I just had to look up all those credentials again.  I'm not sure I even had a way to back those up.

 

I liken the  behavior of this upgrade to upgrading my motherboard BIOS and the process erasing my hard drive.  Totally unacceptable.  I don't know who to blame.  HTC, Sprint or Microsoft.  It's probably a joint effort.  I wonder if this is a systemic problem and a lack of respect by the manufacturers for the personal data on the phone.  Sorry for the bad attitude, but I really get tired of reentering stuff.  I have better things to do.

 

I just wanted to provide a heads up for those who are considering this upgrade.

macropod say:

By design, there must always be at least one paragraph after a table. Where that's an empty paragraph that causes an unwanted extra page to appear, you can format that paragraph:

1. with no space before or after;

2. single line spacing; and

3. a 1pt font size with the text formatted as hidden.

That will be enough to prevent the unwanted page displaying or printing on most systems.

suzanne s. barnhill say:
If the text is formatted as Hidden, the other formatting isn't necessary. macropod say:
It can be if the user has Word configured to print hidden text and, even without that, if Word is configured to show all formatting (which typically happens when you click the ¶ on the Ribbon's Home tab), the appearance of the unwanted page could be misleading.
