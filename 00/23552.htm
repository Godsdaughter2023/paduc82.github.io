steve rindsberg say:

The difference will be more in maintenance than the effort of creating the slides in the first place.

If you put the shape on the master and put the transparent hyperlinked shape on the slides, 1) you won't have to worry about accidentally selecting/deleting/moving the visible shape when working with your slides, and 2) if you ever need to change the shape, you'll only need to do it on the master, not on every slide, and 3) if only some slides require hyperlinks, you can base them on the same master as the ones that don't.  Just don't add the transparent shape to the slides that don't need hyperlinks.

On the other hand, anything that sits in the same position on the slide as the shape on the master will obscure the shape.  If the shape is on the slide itself, you can always select it and bring it to the top again.
