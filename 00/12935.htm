Since Microsoft released Windows 10 at July 29, 2015, we have been got lots of questions about activation and license for insiders in Russian locale of this Community.

subtleties of the license agreement. But our attempt to do it failed.

For now we have such situation:

1) Insiders without license for Windows 7, 8, 10, who installed Windows 10 Insider Preview before July 29, and didn't reinstalled it after it, now have activated system. No matter, what build they're running now, it's activated.

2) Insiders without license for Windows 7, 8, 10, who installed Windows 10 Insider Preview before July 29, and reinstalled Windows after it, lost their activation.

So, we have a question: is using Windows 10 by the first type of insiders now legally?

On the one hand, nobody forbade them to use Windows 10 after July 29. There aren't any documents or even posts, which forbid using it. So, they use it legally. Everything is OK.

"...You are authorized to use this software only if you are properly licensed and the software has been properly activated with a genuine product key or by other authorized method."

Insiders, whom we are discussing about, haven't got a license. So, they can't use Windows 10.

"If you withdraw from the Program and if your registered device is running a commercially available version of Windows provided through the Program, your continued use of that version of Windows is governed by the Windows license terms (http://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?LinkID=529064) and Privacy Statement (http://go.microsoft.com/fwlink/?LinkID=521839)",

situation with insiders, who got activation before July 29 and now are running Insider Preview builds is unclear.

Is using Windows 10 by them legally?

Text effects in Microsoft Word 2010 - Microsoft Community

yvette_yrh say:

Text effects in Microsoft Word 2010

 

How do you turn on "text effects" in Microsoft Word 2010?

graham mayor say:
There is no built-in method with Word 2010, buy you can do so with a macro

Sub AnimateFont()
Dim sAnimation As String
    If Len(Selection.Range) = 0 Then
        MsgBox "Select text first!", vbCritical, "No Text Selected"
        Exit Sub
    End If
    sAnimation = InputBox("Which animation? Enter the number: " & vbCr & _
                          " 1. Blinking Background" & vbCr & _
                          " 2. Las Vegas Lights" & vbCr & _
                          " 3. Marching Black Ants" & vbCr & _
                          " 4. Marching Red Ants" & vbCr & _
                          " 5. Shimmer" & vbCr & _
                          " 6. Sparkle Text" & vbCr & _
                          " 0. None", "Font animation")
    Select Case sAnimation
        Case 1: Selection.Font.Animation = wdAnimationBlinkingBackground
        Case 2: Selection.Font.Animation = wdAnimationLasVegasLights
        Case 3: Selection.Font.Animation = wdAnimationMarchingBlackAnts
        Case 4: Selection.Font.Animation = wdAnimationMarchingRedAnts
        Case 5: Selection.Font.Animation = wdAnimationShimmer
        Case 6: Selection.Font.Animation = wdAnimationSparkleText
        Case 0: Selection.Font.Animation = wdAnimationNone
        Case Else:
    End Select
End Sub

I am not "MS", I am not apologizing for them, and I'm not denying your experience.

That being said, your experience is unusual. Most people do not have the same crashes that you are experiencing. So, at first blush it seems that there is something specific to your computer that is causing these problems.

The most common cause for "weird" crashes is Office addin applets.  Little applications that provide additional features to the Office applications.  There is a simple test for that theory, start the application in "safe mode". This disables ALL addins. if the problem goes away, you know the cause and just have to figure out which specific addon is the cause of the problem.
