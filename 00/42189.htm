shenan stanley say:

Not going to happen.  When that restore point reaches the 'edge' of the list and is the next to go - it's gone.

You cannot backup restore points to use later, you cannot save a specific one, etc.

I suppose, with a little persistence and tenacity - you could use something like CCleaner to delete enough restore points to keep the one you want from ever reaching the edge (by keeping the space unused) - but you would just be hurting yourself at some point - after all - a restore point could easily get old enough that restoring it would likely cause more chaos than it would resolve.
