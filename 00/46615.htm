bossdweeb say:

"I verified I'm using a Directx v 11.something and that should mean I have shader level 5. "

Yes, Win 7 and 8 both have DX 11 (and DX 9&10), but the error is more likely to do with your

graphics card not supporting Vertex shader 3.0. However, it would be a very old graphics card that

does not support shader 3.0. Some 'older' Integrated graphics chips may not support shader 3.0 either.

"I checked for an update for my driver for my vid card and there isn't one"

No Win7 driver at all, or just nothing more recent than what you have installed ? If there are

no drivers for your card that suit Win7  you will need a new card (or possibly a new system)

to play most games.

- More details of your system specifications may be helpful to determine what the problem is.
With the make and model graphics card your machine uses, we should be able to confirm if it uses Shader 3. This is your main consideration as this game will run on XP which means it uses DirectX 9  ( XP does not have DX10 or 11).

The cpu model would also be good to know.

"I don't play with drivers too much, is it safe to 'uninstall' or 'disable' then reboot reinstall/re-enable the driver?  "

Graphics drivers are frequently updated, but with old cards they don't generally offer much advantage as driver optimisations are mostly for newer cards. 

- Note that graphics drivers that may be installed automatically when you uninstall and reboot are

Window generic VGA drivers, not the drivers for your graphics accelerator (graphics card or integrated graphics chip) . 

When running on Windows VGA drivers your graphics card is not functioning.  It is only acting as a connection to your monitor for Windows VGA graphics (so no shader 3.0).

While updating drivers is quite safe you do need to follow the correct procedure.

- Two methods of updating drivers that you should not do with graphics drivers are

1) Update via Device Manager

2) Update via Windows Update ( this is found in the 'Optional' Windows updates and should

be disallowed by hiding the update).

- The best procedure for updating graphics drivers is to uninstall them via Programs and Features

and Reboot.

After rebooting the resolution may be very low (depending on your system), but you can re-set

it to at least 1024x760 while you go about reinstalling the drivers you have downloaded

(Right click on the desktop and click on Resolution in the drop down menu).

- Drivers are best downloaded from either the mfg of the graphics card, or in the case of laptops,

from the mfg of the laptop.

Sometimes it is necessary to download the graphics drivers from the mfg of your PC if you

are using an OEM machine (DELL, HP, etc.), as they often use their own OEM versions of various

graphics cards and modify the drivers to suit.
