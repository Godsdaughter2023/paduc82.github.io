Here are few methods we can try to fix the issue with downloading apps from Windows store.

These are the requirements to download and install apps from Windows Store:

a.       UAC (User Account Control) must be enabled.

b.      To access the Windows Store and to download and run apps, you need an active Internet connection and a screen resolution of at least 1024 x 768.

c.       To snap apps, you need a screen resolution of at least 1366 x 768

d.      Requires Microsoft Account.

 

Method 1: Clear Store Cache.

a.       Press Windows key + R.

b.      Type in “WSReset.exe”.

c.       Press ENTER.

d.      Restart the computer and check.

 

Method 2: You may try the steps provided in the article and check if it helps.

What to do if you have problems with an app

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-IN/windows-8/what-troubleshoot-problems-app

 

 

Method 3: Make sure that “Allow apps to use my location” option is enabled. Follow these steps to enable it.

 

a.       Press the “Windows + W”.

b.      Then type “Allow apps to use my location” without quotes in the search box and hit ENTER.

c.       Turn on “Allow apps to use my location”.

d.      Then turn on “Let apps use my location” option.

 

 

Method 4: If you are using proxy connection, please disable proxy.

 

Follow the steps to disable proxy:

a.       Click the Gearbox at the top right corner in internet explorer.

b.      Click the Tools button, and then click Internet Options.

c.       Click the Connections tab, and then click LAN settings.

d.      Uncheck the box next to “proxy server for your LAN”.

e.       Click OK to save and close the window.

 

 

Method 5: Try disassociating the Microsoft account to make it a local one then check if you are able to complete the purchase.

Note: In the Windows Store, you will need to click sign in and use the online credentials.


a. Hold the Windows key + C to bring up the charms bar, click Settings and then More PC Settings.

b. Within PC settings, click Users and under where it says Connected to <email address> click Disconnect your Microsoft account and confirm.

c. After testing, try to re-connect by repeating the steps and click Connect your Microsoft account to re-associate.

 

 

Method 6: Verify if the Date, time, Time zone and location is set correct.

Step 1: Follow the steps to set the date and time:

a.       Press Windows key + C on your keyboard to show the Charms bar.

b.      Type “Date and Time” in the charms bar search.

c.       Click Change date and time button.

d.      Click Apply and Ok to save the date and time.

 

Note: Click on the Time Zone tab to check the Time Zone.

 

Step 2: Check if the region is correct. Follow the steps mentioned.

a.       Type "Region" on the Start screen. Click "Settings" and press ENTER.

b.      On the "Format" tab, select "English (United Kingdom)".

c.       Click "Languages Preferences" > "Add a language" and add "your language".

d.      Once added, select it and click "Move Up" to make it the first language. Close the Language window.

e.       On the "Region" window, click "Additional Settings" and check if the values are same.

f.        Click the "Location" tab and change the Home Location to "your country" and then click OK.
