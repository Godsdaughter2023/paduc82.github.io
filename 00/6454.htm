btedj say:

Found my missing notes!!! So, it looks like there's no option to bring the notes saved through your old Hotmail account directly back into your Notes app with Outlook. But you might be able to find a folder named "Notes" under the Outlook account in the Mail app. My notes were incomplete at first, but I was able to get them all back. 

The key to getting the lost notes back is to go into the Mail settings of your Outlook account and select "No Limit" in "Mail Days to Sync" — the default is 1 week. (This way it'll show all the notes from the past instead of only the ones that had been recorded within the week.) Then, go back and refresh the "Notes" folder in your Outlook account. Ideally, your lost notes will come and load into your folder. Again, it won't recover it all directly into the Notes app but at least it'll still be accessible through your phone. 

I don't know if this will fix everyone's problem, but I  hope it'll help at least some people. 

SUMMARY

Settings > Mail, Contacts, Calendars > Outlook > Mail Days to Sync > No Limit 

Refresh "Notes" folder in Outlook account in the Mail app then (hopefully) lost notes load into folder

ron sommer mvp 2009-2017 say:

Don't.

While you can create a Microsoft account using a Gmail address, I do not recommend it.

You already have a Hotmail and an outlook.com address.

A Microsoft account that was created with a Gmail address will not receive any emails.  All emails will go to the Gmail server.

If you went to mail.google.com, your email address and Gmail password would be used to access your email.

If you went outlook.com, your email address and Microsoft account password would be used to access your Inbox.  You would be able to send emails, but the account could only receive emails if the emails were sent to an outlook.com alias for that account.

I hope that you noticed that there would be a Gmail password and a Microsoft account password.  They could be different passwords.
