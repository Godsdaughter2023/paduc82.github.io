Hi Nishanth,

It depends on which website you are trying to view 'videos' on.... modern websites use a 'Video player' widget on their sites, that test the capabilities of your web browser and try to send back a 'video' format that is supported by your browser.

https://www.youtube.com/html5

In the above screen shot, note that youtube is using a 'YouTube HTML5 Video Player'.... other websites may be using another or their own custom 'Video Player' widgets on their web pages..... basically the general answer to your question is "It depends on how the website you are visiting has been programmed"

AND on

Your web browser settings.

The settings for video codecs can be found on the Advanced tab of Internet Options.

(note: you have to restart your computer for the changes to the 'Alternative codecs' setting to take hold.)

Outcomes for the Quality of the videos that you want to view (montage, stuttering, sound quality, picture quality etc) also depend on

1. The size of the screen you are viewing the video on... to view a high definition video on your small laptop screen, your computer has to decode the pixel map of each frame and 'resize' it. This takes some (be it tiny) time.

On youtube you can choose the Quality of the video you want to receive to suite the size of your computer screen or TV (if you are projecting from your PC/xbox to TV).

Click the 'cog' icon on your you tube video player to Choose a different quality 'video' format to suite your needs. You must be logged in to you tube for the site to remember your preferences. Choose a lower quality video format if using a small computer screen. Choose a higher quality video format if using your larger TV.

2. Video data sent to your computer over the internet is sent in discrete packets (chunks). Your computer 'buffers' these packets of data and then decodes the data to display a frame of the video. This takes some (be it tiny) time, but if your internet connection is interrupted or 'packets' of data are lost, the process of decoding the buffered data is slowed down... your video may slow down, waiting for the packets of data to be sent to your computer.

viz: It depends on the speed of your current internet connection, the size of your computers memory and the amount of memory that is available. Check your current internet connection speed if you are experiencing stuttering (stop/start) of your videos OR click the pause button to wait for all of the video's packets to be downloaded.

If you have more than one 'Video' playing at the same time or if you try to use two different web browsers to view the same videos, your computer has less and less memory available to buffer the packets of data for decoding. Avoid playing more than one video at a time.

Some websites like youtube have a auto-play setting, that will automatically start downloading and playing videos without you having to click the play button on the video viewer. If you experience problems, turn that you tube setting off.

Exchange Server email not synching when connected with Activesync via - Microsoft Community

zlatic say:

Exchange Server email not synching when connected with Activesync via USB

Dear All,

I have had a lot of problems on the HTC TYTN II with WM 6 when trying to sync my email on the exchange server, via the activesync USB connection (pass through). I tried all of the suggestions on this forum:

1. Delete partnership
2. Change connection setting in activesinc to be "internet" and not automatic or vice versa
3. toggel advance USB networking in the TYTN's connection settings (USB)

NONE of this worked.

What did work? well by accident I found the newtork card settings when setting up the wifi connection, I switched the selection "my network card connects to:" from work to internet. This worked perfect! Here are the steps:

1. Go to settings>connections.> wifi (not wireless LAN)
2. Select the "network adapters" tab
3. Select in "My network card connects to :" "Internet" (not work). I selected also the Activesync NDIS WAN setting and just opened and closed it "for good luck"

This worked immediatly.

The above are good ways as first steps (solved my problem in WM5) but the last step solved the new problem. Good luck
