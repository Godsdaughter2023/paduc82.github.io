The Xbox system cache contains temporary files used by your Xbox 360 console.  

Note: Clearing the system cache doesn't remove your gamertag, downloaded

When you clear the cache, the following things occur:

Achievements stored in the cache are removed, although not permanently.

The achievement information will be automatically downloaded from Xbox LIVE.

Previously downloaded game updates are removed; however, you'll be prompted

to download these updates again before you play the games.

Hello,

Usually if you are getting this error and you are trying multiple discs it means that your XBOX 360 hard drive is either genuine and the emulation files on the drive are damaged or missing in some way (no way to correct this issue unfortunately), or you have a knock off hard drive (usually purchased from second hand retailers or places such as eBay or Amazon Marketplace for quite a cheap price). If this hard drive did not come with your XBOX 360 and was purchased later from one of the above second hand retailers or online merchants and did not come in an official Microsoft XBOX 360 box then I would assume that it is a knock off and it will not be able to play those original XBOX games.

Only the Microsoft genuine hard drives contain the emulation files needed to play the original XBOX games. The knock off hard drives will only be able to play standard XBOX 360 games and DLC.

Hope that helps you in solving your question.

legoo say:

booting from memory stick

if (big if) my computer runs into a problem and fails to boot and i insert the recovery USB i created. will the computer boot straight into it? or would i have to do something in the bios before booting into my recovery stick, if i do, what jind of stuff do i need to do?
davem121 say:

You will have to make changes to your BIOS to boot from a USB Flash Drive

If you have legacy BIOS, you will have to plug in the Flash Drive, enter your BIOS and move that device to the top of the boot order list

If you have a new system it will have UEFI (BIOS), in this case you will have to disable Secure Boot and move that device to the top of the boot order list

To enter BIOS or UEFI, as soon as your system starts booting, look at the screen for text like 'Press F2 to enter Setup', it may also be Del, Esc or some other key . . .
