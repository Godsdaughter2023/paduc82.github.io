Set of random numbers that average a set number and sum to a number - Microsoft Community

bella_bcba say:

Set of random numbers that average a set number and sum to a number

Can you help me create a formula or macro?


I want to create a variable schedule; create a set of random numbers with a specific min and max that average to a set number. For example, Over a period of 5 minutes, I want to be prompted between every 20 s to 60 s; with an average of being prompted every 40 s.  

 

Put another way, I want a set of numbers to equal X (300 s) and average Y (40 s) and have a specific min (20 s) and max (60 s).

 

I then want to be able to at times modify all aspects of the formula because next time I may want to have a period of 10 minutes and be prompted on average every 20 s with a min of 10 and a max of 30. 




bernie deitrick say:

In A2:A5, enter the four labels (Min, Max, Average, Total)

In B2, enter your Min  (20)
In B3, enter your Max (60)
In B4, enter your desired Average (40)
In B5, enter the length of Total (300)

 

Starting in A7 and continuing down, enter Time 1, Time 2, etc.

Then in B7, enter the formula

=RANDBETWEEN($B$2,$B$3)

In B8, enter the formula

=IF(SUM($B$7:B7)>($B$5-$B$3),$B$5-SUM($B$7:B7),RANDBETWEEN($B$2,$B$3))

and copy down to match your labels in column A for at least your worst case (most times) scenario - your 10 minute example would require on average 30 rows and possibly up to 60 (600 seconds, split into 10 second blocks - unlikely to the extreme, but possible.)

 

In C2, enter the formula

=MIN(OFFSET(B7,0,0,COUNTIF(B7:B70,">0")))

(Change the 70 in all these formulas to the actual row of your last label)

In C2, enter the formula

=MAX(B7:B70)

In C4, enter the formula

=B5/COUNTIF(B7:B70,">0")

These three formulas will  give you the actual values. Recalc (press F9) until you are happy with the actual Min, since the last value in the set may be too low (that's the nature of random numbers!) because of your requirement for an exact sum based on random numbers (Again, that's the nature of random numbers!)

petrib say:

Qt is, so called "middleware" (in a fashion). Namely some user interface and utility libraries originally developed to ease making applications easier to write for multiple operating systems.

 

Nowadays, Qt is a key component of most Nokia's new applications (such as Store) and must be installed before they can run (Qt can come with the firmware, a firmware update, or the application if it depends on Qt may download and install it - or a newer version - "on the fly").

 

In an ideal situation, a phone user shouldn't need or care about Qt, but due to various installation issues, problems with different Qt versions, and upgrading them, one unfortunately is often exposed to it.

a. user say:

Hello,

 

Welcome to Microsoft Community and thank you for posting your query.

 

I suggest you to follow below steps to reinstall the preinstalled apps on Windows 10.

 

1.Click the Start button to open the Start menu, navigate to All apps > Windows PowerShell and right-click on Windows PowerShell.

2. Click Run as administrator from the dropdown menu. Click Yes if a pop-up window asking you to make changes to your computer.

3. Type or paste the following code into the PowerShell window:

Get-AppXPackage | Foreach {Add-AppxPackage -DisableDevelopmentMode -Register "$($_.InstallLocation)\AppXManifest.xml"

4. Hit Enter to execute and this will reinstall all your default preinstalled apps.

5. Wait till the execution is complete and then check your Start menu to see if the Windows 10 default apps have reappeared.

6. If any error message prompts, restart the system and check to see if the issue is resolved.

 

If issue still persists, follow the steps in below article to re-install the apps and check:

https://msdn.microsoft.com/en-us/library/windows/hardware/mt185364%28v=vs.85%29.aspx?f=255&MSPPError=-2147217396

 

You may also login to Microsoft account to check the status.

 

Hope the information helps. Do reply if you need further assistance.
