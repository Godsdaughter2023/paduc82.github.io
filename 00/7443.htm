jay freedman say:

The Normal.dotm template is susceptible to damage or replacement by updates, by Word processes that store style definitions, building blocks, AutoCorrect entries, and other things in the template, and by malware. For that reason it's best not to store macros there, especially large collections of them like yours.

Instead, save the macros in one ore more different template (*.dotm) files, and store those files in the folder %appdata%\Microsoft\Word\Startup. All *.dotm files in that special folder are automatically loaded into Word each time it starts, and the macros are available in all documents. Those templates can also store ribbon and Quick Access Toolbar customizations and keyboard shortcuts, which will also be available. Files in the Startup folder are much less likely to be trashed by random events.
