Setting space between heading number and heading - Microsoft Community

rickduley say:

Setting space between heading number and heading

I have a MSW07 template with outline numbered headings.  I cannot find a way to control the gap between the number and the text of the heading.

How can I do that?

cindy meister say:

Hi Rick

This is controlled by the Number formatting. I'm going to make a number of assumptions about how you've applied the numbering, so details of the following may not be exactly what you have, but I hope it gets you started

1. Click in a numbered paragraph (formatted with a Heading style?)

2. Home/Paragraph, Outline numbering button: click the arrow to show the list, then choose "Define new multilevel list". This displays a dialog box.

3. Down the left, you see a list of "Levels" - one for each outline level. You need to select the level, then change the settings for that level. Start with "1" (pre-selected)

4. The section "Position", box "Text indent at" is where you regulate the distance between the number and the text.

5. The button "Set for all levels" will let you quickly define a "pattern" for these settings for all nine levels.

Cindy Meister, VSTO/Word MVP

How To Make My Printing Preview The Same As My Original Word Document - Microsoft Community

christian leonhardson say:

How To Make My Printing Preview The Same As My Original Word Document

I've used microsoft word for nearly anything but recently i wanted to add a colour images to it. and when i open the printing preview some of it is cut.
suzanne s. barnhill say:
If you mean that there is a white frame around the page in Print Preview, then blame this on your printer driver. Word's Print Preview pretty much reliably shows what the currently active printer is capable of printing--or at least what it tells Word it can print. Every printer has a certain irreducible unprintable area required for paper handling. This is generally greater on inkjet printers than laser printers and usually larger on the trailing edge (bottom of portrait documents, one side of landscape), even more so if duplexing is enabled. There are some photo printers that have a "borderless" setting, though generally its for sizes smaller than Letter and may require a special carrier. For most printers, the only way to print "full bleed" (all the way to the edge) is to use larger paper and trim the edges, which is what commercial printers do.
