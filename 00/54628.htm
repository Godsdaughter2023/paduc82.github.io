No guarantees...but since you can save the project successfully
in the lower resolution and cannot save at higher resolution, I would
suspect a video driver issue.

May be worth a try search the support site of your computer
manufacturer for an updated video driver...

One way to verify that the video driver is problematic is to reduce
the graphics hardware acceleration: If this improves the issue...
installing an updated Video Driver may be the fix. Acceleration
can be returned to Full after the updated driver is installed.

Windows 7 - Reduce Graphics Acceleration
(see the following article)

(Basic steps should be :
Start / Control Panel / Appearance and Personalization / Display Settings./
Advanced Settings.button / Troubleshoot tab / Change Settings button...
Left click/drag the Hardware Acceleration slider to the left, one notch to
the right of None / OK.)

Checking Hardware Acceleration Settings - Microsoft Vista/Windows 7 Instructions
http://iq-im-project.com/%28S%28n4lpyihklz2nkgu30lkjr0f3%29%29/help/273025.htm

The following article may be worth a look:

Update the graphics driver - Windows 7, Vista
http://helpx.adobe.com/creative-suite/kb/update-graphics-driver-windows-7.html

Sometimes...Windows Update will install a Video Driver that is not
as compatible with your system as the one you already had. In this
case...the solution would be to Roll Back to the previous driver:

Windows XP - Vista - 7 - Video Driver Rollback/
Removal Instructions
http://www.nvidia.com/object/driver_rollback.html

How to Roll Back a Misbehaving Device Driver
to a Previous Version in Windows XP, Vista, and 7
