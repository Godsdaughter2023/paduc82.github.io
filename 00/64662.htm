¡firedog say:

I've highlighted them all but the delete  X in the ribbon bar remains grey ...

If there's a single undeletable contact in the highlighted selection, the Delete option won't be available. You can only delete contacts that you put there.

You can see where a contact came from in the contacts window in Windows Live Mail: select From Address Book can be deleted. Any others are being retrieved from somewhere else and will have to be manipulated there. If the contact you're trying to delete comes from another account, like Skype or Google or LinkedIn, you must either
 

  • Delete the contact in the account it came from, or
     
  • Break the link between the other account and your Microsoft account.
     

Breaking the link happens for Skype accounts on this page: How do I unlink my Skype account from my Microsoft or Facebook account? For other accounts, visit your profile page and sign in with the same account you sign in with in Windows Live Mail. Remove any connections there to remove the associated contacts from your contacts list.

You may not be able to delete Facebook friends, because Facebook broke the Connect mechanism last year. If this is important to you, you'll have to ask a Microsoft Account support agent to remove them for you. Start here: Microsoft Account Support

Once your contacts list is as you want it on the People page, in Windows Live Mail use the right-most button on the ribbon to Sign out of Windows Live Mail and wait for the default contacts list to load. Sign in again; the new contacts list should now download from People.
