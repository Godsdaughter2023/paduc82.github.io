Continuum - My impressions - Microsoft Community

bob grosh say:

Continuum - My impressions

Continuum - Yet another great Idea.The screenshot is my dual monitor system with 10TP and Continuum turned on. This PC uses a mouse and keyboard, no touch.

Basically, the left monitor shows the new Continuum start menu, a combination of the apps screen and part of the Start Screen along with power buttons and stuff you would find on the 7 start menu.

The left most column.

It looks like the apps list on the 8.1 start menu, however, a big improvement is, it now has Jump Lists, just like windows 7. On the down side, at least with this build, it does not have all the nice sorting option that the apps view of windows 8.1 has, so, sorting by most recently installed, or most frequently used is not something you can just get with a single click. At least not yet! There are not as many settings in the start menu properties as there was on 7, so you don't get a lot of options for the jump lists, but basic functionality is there.

The next 4 columns on the left monitor.

 Here are the active tiles, Notice that the top row of tiles mirrors the group names on my modern start screen. For now, the Continuum menu does not support any groups. That is, at least for now, a real pain when setting up the groups. Hopefully, the group functionality from the modern start screen will be added. A huge improvement here is that you can scroll through the entire list of start screen tiles, so, power users can use the Continuum menu to access any program they have pined to start. This works really well if you put your active tiles in the first 2 or three groups. I like to see at a glance all the recent activity of my family, business contacts, friends, and current posts on blogs I follow. It saves me hundreds of clicks a day. The weather, news feed and other stuff is just frosting. I don't think I ever needed to launch a weather site since getting 8.

Take a look at my right monitor.

This is just some leftover stuff. It doesn't fit well in the 8 - 10 world. Like most power users with tons of icons on their desktop, I arrange them in groups to keep them organized.

The icon at the top of each group is an empty folder just there to serve as a label for the group. Most of the groups you would have found on my Windows 7 desktop got migrated to the modern start screen. Eight is much better at organizing groups of icons because they don't get scrambled when there is a power glitch or an update or resolution change. Being able to drag a group to a new position or easily move a tile/icon to a new group and have it automatically arrange neighboring tiles is also a plus for the modern interface. Why are these still here? Well, the desktop now serves as a handy place to pile up stuff I will need in the coming day or week. For example. I do a weekly tech show on a local radio station. During the week, If I happen to get an email question, or see something on the web I might want to talk about, I just drad the address to my desktop and leave it in the column under the heading for the show. After the show, I drag them into the heading folder and then drag that weeks show into an archive folder. I'm ok with using the desktop to organize stuff during the week. Eight and 10 make it easier, and less cluttered because all my shortcuts are now managed in the start menu on Continuum or the start screen on 8.

What I'd like to see next in Continuum:

The group management from the 8.x start screen.

More options for displaying the apps.

Text Labels for the columns (groups).

While titles can be done using tiles as I did, the fact that there are no groups means there is no separation between the groups and arraigning tiles can cause the tiles to end up in the wrong place. While it may be easy to use Siena to make an app for the tile, there is no easy way to manage the group of tiles under the heading.

Also the tile sizes are limited to 4 fixed sizes, so the headings waste space. I added a little iconic flourish just to fill in the empty space, but I'd rather keep the headings clean and modern.

All in all, I'm impressed by the Continuum effort so far.

dann ave say:

Hello Tammy,

Just to confirm, are you trying to create a recurring event on your calendar?

Outlook.com has lesser features than Office desktop apps, though you should still be able to create the recurring event.

First, select calendar at the bottom left of Outlook.com's main screen to change to the calendar view.

Click on New > Calendar event. Fill up the details of the event that you want.

To have it repeated, select Other under the Repeat drop down list.  On the recurring Select repeat pattern, set the following details:

  • Occurs: The same week each month.
  • Every: 1 months on the last Monday.

For the event that recurs every first of the month, create a new event and perform the same steps. Just change the settings to Every 1 months on the first Monday.
