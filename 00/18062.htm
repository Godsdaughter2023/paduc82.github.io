fin_modler say:

This is quite different from "I need a formula to divide my average handle time by the number of calls offered".

A1/C1 is you calls per agent. A1/C1*B1 is your average time handling calls per day per agent.

You have one number missing which is how many hours your agents work per day, say in E1.

E1/B1 is the number of calls per agent per day. A1/E1*B1 is the number of agents you need to handle A1 calls in a day with E1 hours for calls averaging B1 hour of handling time.

Actually, unless your duration per call has a tight spread, I would be wary of using averages for capacity planning.

e.j. gun say:

To calculate the number of people you need to staff your call center, we need to know what the working hours are for a specific shift.  Let's say a shift is a total of 9 hours, but 1 hour is lunch, so every worker can take calls for 8 hours.  The formula you would use to estimate the number of people required is given below.

Since people are not perfect, you may also want to include an "efficiency factor" and not assume that a person can take calls continuously for all 8 hours.  Something like:

=ROUNDUP(A2*B2*24/8/0.75,0)

which says that the average person will be 75% efficient taking calls.
