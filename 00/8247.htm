brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

The "upgrade" to Outlook.com is an interface change. You will know you have been "upgraded" if you log onto hotmail.com and see "Outlook" in large letters at the top left of the web page.

I advise you strongly to not change your address. Keep your hotmail.com address. These forums are chock full of posts from people who changed their addresses and then discovered that all of the email, contacts, and/or SkyDrive files were deleted. If you wish to use an Outlook.com address, log into your account, and add an alias that ends in @outlook.com. That way your mailbox will not change, minimizing the chances that your data will be deleted, plus you will be able to receive and send mail using the new address. You can even set the new address as the default sending address if you wish. You should also be able to log in with the new address and your existing password in addition to the old address and your existing password.
