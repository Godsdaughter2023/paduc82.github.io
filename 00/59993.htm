davem121 say:

Start your System, then just after the Manufacturers logo disappears and Windows 10 tries to boot, press and hold the power button down for 5 - 10 seconds to perform a hard shut down

Do this Twice

On the third boot attempt, Windows will boot into the Windows 10 Repair Environment, from there you can access Startup Recovery, Safe Mode, Command Prompt . . . etc.

Go to Troubleshoot - Advanced Options - System Restore, see if you have a recent restore point you can revert to

If you do not have a recent restore point, go to Troubleshoot - Advanced Options - Startup Settings, click restart.
Upon restart press 4 to boot into Safe Mode, if you can get into Safe Mode, backup your data, reboot and see if your system boots normally

If all this fails go to Troubleshoot - Advanced Options - Reset this PC, choose to keep all your files and Apps
This will re-install Windows 10 whilst keeping your data and applications

To clean install Windows 10 should your Hard drive fail:

If you do not have Windows 10 Installation Media Click HERE to download the Media Creation Tool (Click on Download Tool Now), with that you can download the latest Windows 10 ISO, choose 'Create installation for another PC' to create Installation media on either a Flash Drive (min 4GB) or DVD

 

Since you previously had Windows 10 installed and activated on that PC during the installation process skip the steps that ask for a product key and select 'I am Reinstalling Windows 10 on this PC', and activation will not be an issue, your PC will have a digital entitlement stored on the Microsoft Servers

steeledwand7664 say:

Let me explain it for you.

You have 2 consoles 

CA = console a

CB= console b

A gold subscription allows you to play multiplayer games and chat with other players online. 

Subscriptions go with accounts Not consoles

No matter what console an account is signed into it will always be able to use any subscription and any digital games that have been bought on that account.

Each account can set one console at a time as its home console and any other profile on that console can then use (share) its subscriptions and play its digital games.

I'll use your case as an example.

You said your daughter has a One S and are about to get a One X for the rest of the family. 

I am assuming that your daughter has a Gold subscription already so no matter which console she is signed into she would always be able to use the gold subscription.

Now if you want any of the other members of your family to be able to use her gold subscription without having to purchase another one AND the only console that they use is the One X then you would set the One X console as your daughters profiles home console.

Now keep in mind that Each profile can switch which console it has set as its home console only FIVE times a year. 

Hope this explains things a little better for you if you have any other questions feel free to ask.

shawn - support engineer say: Hi Joe in Tampa,

Thanks for posting. If you are syncing with a Windows Live account your blocked senders will be stored online with your account. If you are using IMAP or POP the list will be contained in this key HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Windows Live Mail\PerPassportSettings\0\Junk Mail\Block Senders List in the registry.
