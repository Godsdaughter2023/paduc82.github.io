The quickest way to verify you have a CTR installation is go into Windows Explorer and look for an unexplained Q:\ (or R:\) drive.

Another method of checking: Go to help panel of any working app: File tab / Help command and look at the Right side of the Backstage panel. It should say "Product Activated" for MSI install or "Click-to-Run Product Updates".

CTR Introduction (Identify CTR installation)

https://office.microsoft.com/en-ca/excel-help/click-to-run-introduction-HA101850493.aspx?CTT=3

What is Click to Run

Click-to-Run is a new Electronic Software Distribution (ESD) technology for Office 2010 that utilizes Microsoft’s streaming and virtualization technology. Click-to-Run provides a much faster and simpler download and installation experience for customers trying or buying Office 2010 over the Internet. Click-to-Run allows customers to maintain multiple versions of Office so customers can easily try Office 2010 side by side with an existing version of Office. Click-to-Run automatically downloads and installs any software updates when connected to the Internet, helping to maintain a more secure and up-to-date Office experience. At this time, only Office Home and Student 2010 and Office Home and Business 2010 will be available through Click-to-Run and limited based on broadband speed requirements.

NOTE:  CTR is a relatively new approach to installing and running Office, Introduced with 2010. So, very few technicians are aware of it, let alone familiar with trouble shooting it. As well, it introduces a whole new set of errors, like the one that you encountered. My personal recommendation is that unless you have a clearly identified need for it and explicitly requested the download, DON’T USE IT.

How to sort a table of contents in alphabetical order? - Microsoft Community

tonjemnm say:

How to sort a table of contents in alphabetical order?

I am working on a document and have created a table of contents based on my own headings. I want to sort the table of contents alphabetically, but get the message "Word cannot sort fields in the selection".

Does anyone have a suggestion/answer to how to do it?

thank you!!

stefan blom say:

A table of contents is organized in page order, not in alphabetical order. You will have to unlink the TOC and then sort it.

However, the standard choice if you want an alphabetical list of words would be to create an index instead.

tonjemnm say:

Thank you, I have done that for earlier documents, but was hoping it was possible to use table of contents which is based on a style. 

Do I have to mark every heading , or is there a way to mark all with a defined style at once?

stefan blom say:

What, exactly, have you tried before? If you have done an index before, you know that it is basically a manual task. Entries must be marked one by one. You can make use of a so-called concordance file, but it would then auto-mark every occurrence of a listed word (which you may or may not want).

Given that you want a TOC but sorted alphabetically, maybe unlinking and then sorting the remaining text would work better, after all. Once the TOC has been created, select it and press Ctrl+Shift+F9 to unlink the field.

tonjemnm say:

I've used index before, but is just seems a lot of work to go through the whole document and mark the headings.

Unlink the TOC was a great idea, but is it also possible to "link it back" if I do changes on the document later?

stefan blom say:
No, you can't "re-link" a TOC once it has been unlinked. However, it is a fairly simple task to just create a new one.
