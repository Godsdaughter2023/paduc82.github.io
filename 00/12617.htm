Hello palmerj41,

Just wanted to second what Noel posted regarding Office...it is a nongenuine Volume Licensing edition.  If you purchased it with the computer, then whomever sold it to you had no standing to sell it, as it is pirated software.

If you received genuine Microsoft hologrammed installation discs for Office Professional 2003 with the computer,and the discs don't have "volume licensing" or "Illegal withou separate license from Microsoft" or "All use subject ot separate volume licensing agreement" printed on them,and the product key you have does not end in 3HFDY, then the discs may be for genuine software, so it would be worth the effort to uninstall the nongenuine software, restart twice, then install from the discs you have.

If no discs, or if your discs are volume licensing discs, then you're stuck.  Microsoft's free replacement program which lasted for almost three years, ended on Decemebr 16, 2010.  In order to run genuine Office software on your computer, you would have to purchase Office from a trusted local or online retailer.

Regarding the validity of Windows on your IBM ThinkPad, your installation should be Valid if the partial product key reported above   *****-*****-PCRXX-DPMD6-6DBYY matches the corresponding portion of the product key printed on the computer's Certificate of Authenticity (CoA).  IME the majority of IBM ThinkPads of that vintage came from IBM with XP Pro installed, as the ThinkPad line was a premium product generally marketed to business types.
