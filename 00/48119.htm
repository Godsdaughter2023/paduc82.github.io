gmsof1 say: Trying to figure that out now. w/o success. How/what do I do to trim time?

considerate_guy say:

On the right side, in your storyboard/project area, the song is represented by a thin green rectangle below your main video track.  If you double-click this rectangle, it will automatically bring up the Music Tools | Options tab.

 

Let's say you have a song that is 4 minutes long starting at the beginning of your movie.  Then you'll see that it has a start time of 0.00 seconds (it's right at the beginning of your project) and the song starts at 0.00 seconds and ends at 240 seconds (4 x 60).

 

If you drag this song so it starts later, it will change the start time.  In other words, let's say you wanted 10 seconds of silence, then the song should start.  You can do this by changing the start time to 10 seconds.  That doesn't affect the length of the song, just when it starts relative to everything else in your project.

 

The more useful settings are the start point and end point.  So let's say you just want to play the chorus which starts a 1 minute in and goes for 30 seconds.  You would set your start point to 60 seconds and the end point to 90 seconds.  But rather than typing numbers, it's probably easier to just play the song.  As soon as you get to the place you want it to actually start, click the 'Set start point' button.  That will trim the song down so that it skips over the first part of the song and starts right at this point.

 

Similarly, continue playing the song until it gets to the end of the part you want to keep and click the 'Set end point' button.  That will trim the song down to just the part you want.  Other options are to Split the song (say you want to play the song, then stop, then continue, or if you want to have two parts).

engsupport say:

I know this is an old post, but thought I'd add my solution in case it helps others.  I had this same problem after upgrading my Dell laptop from Windows 7 to Windows 10 (File Explorer and Control Panel not showing anything) and the system file checker showed nothing wrong.  In my case Task Manager showed the TdmService using 33% CPU.  When I researched that service it was part of some obsolete Wave/Embassy security software that came with the laptop that I removed years ago (and thus it didn't show up in Programs and Features) so it must have left some services running.  Here's what I did that fixed it:

- in Services app set TdmService to disabled

- ran msconfig, and on the Services tab disabled/unchecked all services from Wave Systems Corp (SecureStorageService, Wave Authentication Manager Service, and TdmService), then restarted the laptop  

After that both File Explorer and Control Panel worked as expected and I haven't noticed any other problems with those services turned off.

riel alc say:

just tell me how to turn it off.

Hi Tony,

To turn off the Windows 10 hardware acceleration, please follow the steps below:

  1. Press Windows key + R. Type regedit and then hit Enter to open Registry Editor.
  2. Browse HKEY_CURRENT_USER\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Avalon.Graphics\DisableHWAcceleration.
  3. In the right pane, right-click on DisableHWAcceleration and then click Modify.
  4. Change the Value data to 1 to turn off the hardware acceleration.

Note: To turn on the hardware acceleration, follow the same steps and then change the Value data to 0.
