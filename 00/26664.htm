1. Click in an entry you want to change.
2. Right-click on that entry and select Paragraph.
3. Change the space before and space after settings to what you want.
4. Click on an entry for a different level and do the same.
5. Repeat as needed.

This should fix it for that document.

This is controlled by the TOC styles for each heading. By default, when you change the paragraph formatting for one of these styles it updates the style for all entries of the same level.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

Are these built-in or custom styles? What do you mean by "go missing"? Are they not listed in the Styles pane? Or are they no longer applied to the appropriate paragraphs? Or are they still applied but no longer have the formatting you defined?

If the problem is the last, it could be that "Automatically update document styles" is enabled. If you modified styles but saved the changes only in the document and not in the template, then every time you open the document, the styles would be reset to their format in the template. If that's the case, then on the Developer tab, in the Templates group, click Document Template. In the Templates and Add-ins dialog, make sure "Automatically update document styles" is not checked. (If you don't have the Developer tab displayed, you can display it by checking it in the Customize the Ribbon dialog.)

If the problem is just that the styles disappear from the Styles pane, then perhaps you need to change the Style Pane Options to display "All styles."
