Break spreadsheet into weeks and months - Microsoft Community

kattosha say:

Break spreadsheet into weeks and months

Column A is the date and Column B is the amount, both will continuously grow. I need to create Column C so it will automatically calculate totals for week 1, week 2 etc...then I need another column that will total months. The formulas for this are totally evading me and I would appreciate all the help you can give.

Thanks
bernie deitrick say:

This is a case for Pivot Tables - select your table and insert a pivot table, then use column A (date) as the row field and column B (set to sum) as the data field. Then group the dates by month. No formulas needed - you will just need to update the source range when you add more values. Look in help for more on how.

 

Note that to group by week, you need to decide what your week is - you can add a column to your data table with WEEKNUM functions to assign the dates to week numbers - and then use that as the row field.

jasperp say: I concur with the above, but like to add that if you transform your source data into a table (Insert > Table), you don't have to adjust the source range every time you add rows.
The range adjusts itself, you only have to refresh the Pivot table after you added the new data.

When signing on to outlook.com, it is possible to bypass the useless "blue page" that is first displayed.

Edit the shortcut/bookmark that you use to sign on to outlook.com, and replace the Location/Target/Address/URL (which might look like this: 
https://www.outlook.com/) .... with this:

https://login.live.com/login.srf?wa=wsignin1.0&wreply=https%3a%2f%2foutlook.live.com%2fowa%2f%3fnlp%3d1

(the replacement above is one continuous line of characters ... copy and paste the above characters into your shortcut/bookmark)

Then save the edited shortcut/bookmark.

Clicking on the updated shortcut/bookmark should now take you to the sign-in page and bypass the useless "blue page".

You might need to turn on the notification in your Windows 10 settings.
