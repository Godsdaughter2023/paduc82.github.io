andre da costa say:

Hello Andre and thanks for your response

So your saying that when I install Windows 8.1, it creates a copy of my old data.

It moves your data to a folder at the root of your hard disk called Windows.old

1. Assuming Im installing on the C: drive partition, will this copy include the whole of the original, and if not what is included?

All personal data you created will moved there, this includes: Documents, Music, Photos, Videos etc. You should still do a backup though

2. What will happen to data on any other drive (e.g D:)

Only the target drive where Windows 8.1 will be installed will be affected (C:\), data on other drives or partitions will not be affected. If its an actual physical drive, you can disconnect it as a precaution. Sometimes users end up selecting the wrong partition or drive during setup.

3. Can I choose which partition the Windows.old goes to ?

No, this is defined by Windows setup routine, it automatically goes to the root of the C:\ drive.

After installation is complete, open Computer > open your Local Disk (C:\) and the Windows.old folder and you will see your old user folder with your data.
