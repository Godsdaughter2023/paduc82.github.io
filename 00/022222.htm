paulineburr say:

How do I transfer all the data from my Nokia Lumia 800 to my laptop, before it goes off to repair?

How do I transfer all the data from my Nokia Lumia 800 to my laptop, before it goes off to repair?

a. user say:

Hi PaulineBurr,

 

  Thanks for taking the time to post your question in the Microsoft Answers forums!  In short, most of the data on your phone will be tied to the Microsoft Account you set up and the rest will be synced to your PC using the Zune software.

 

  The Zune software client can be downloaded at this link (http://www.windowsphone.com/en-us/how-to/wp7/zune-software) and is used to sync pictures, video, and music files between your phone and computer.  Once installed, the program will start automatically when you connect your phone to your PC using the USB cable that came with your phone.  In the software, you will be able to select Phone, where you will see the content on the device and have the option to copy it to your PC.  Details steps on this process can be found on the Windows Phone website here: http://www.windowsphone.com/en-us/how-to/wp7/music/sync-files-with-my-phone.

 

  Your Contacts, calendar items, and app purchases will be saved to the Microsoft Account (also called Live ID) you first set up on your phone by default.  By setting up your phone with this same account you will be able to download the data again.  If you're unsure of this account, you can confirm it by tapping Settings > Email + Accounts, where it is usually listed as the first account (when you tap and hold the account, you will see a Sync but no Delete option). 

 

  You can see which contacts are listed on this account by signing in at Http://contacts.live.com.  After setting up your new phone, any apps you previously installed can be reinstalled from the marketplace in one of two ways.  One is to locate the apps in the marketplace individually, where you will see the option to reinstall the app instead of purchasing it again.  The second is to reinstall the apps from your app history on Http://www.windowsphone.com.  Once signed in, Click Purchase History in the My Phone menu.  Under Purchase History, find the apps you want to reinstall and click Reinstall. 

 

  Lastly, any documents you have saved on your phone in the Office Hub can be saved to your SkyDrive account and re-downloaded to your phone by setting up the new device with the same Microsoft Account.  You can upload the documents by tapping and holding for the Share > SkyDrive option, details on this process are available online here: http://www.windowsphone.com/en-us/how-to/wp7/office/sync-documents-with-skydrive .

 

This should cover everything to back up the data on your phone, please don't hesitate to let us know if you have any further questions!

 

Regards,

 Greg

If the messages are no longer on the Hotmail server, you must be using a POP account to access the mailbox.  If the messages stay on gmail, then you must be using an IMAP account to acces that mailbox.  Download and install the Outlook Hotmail Connector (http://office.microsoft.com/en-gb/outlook/microsoft-office-outlook-hotmail-connector-overview-HA010222518.aspx) and then define your account through it.  Your Hotmail data will then stay on the server just like it does for gmail.  Unlike the gmail account, you can also access your Hotmail contacts and calendar that way also.  You can even copy your downlaoded messages back to the Hotmail server with a simple drag-and-drop.

chrismcghan say:

Migration from Windows Live Custom Domain to Office365.com


If you’re planning to migrate to Office365 from Outlook.com, fka live.com, fka hotmail.com and used domains.live.com to power your domain and finally plan to migrate to Office365 know that there is now a bug. When Outlook.com was recently switched over and Office365 and it began being powered by the same engine, well, they began being powered by one engine! If steps aren't taken in the settings of the Microsoft account it can be a cause of confusion for the system and you may lose access to your data stored in the Outlook.com service.

You may experience the follow error messages:

X-ClientId: [removed]
X-OWA-Error: Microsoft.Exchange.Data.Storage.DatabaseNotFoundException
X-OWA-Version: 15.1.453.31
X-FEServer: [removed]
X-BEServer: [removed]

or

X-Auth-Error: ADDriverStoreAccessNonLocalException

When you attempt to access your old data, you need to have an alias on the Microsoft account that is not in the custom domain’s namespace and it MUST be set as primary. You may have to sign out and sign in with the new primary alias (I did).
