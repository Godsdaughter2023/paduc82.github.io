Printing a mirror image

I would like to create a T-Shirt transfer in either Popwerpoint, Word or Publisher. How do I reverse the image onto a transfer sheet?
steve rindsberg say:

You may not need to.

Try this:

Go to print your file from Powerpoint.  In the print dialog box, choose your printer, click Properties and in your printer's properties dialog box, you may find an option for choosing the paper type.  See if there's a choice for transfer material there.  If so, choose it.  Then print a test page (you can use regular paper for this if you like).

On the two inkjets I've used that support transfers, both automatically mirror the image for me when I select transfer material.

Naturally, I didn't find this out until I'd spent a silly amount of time working out how to mirror the graphic before printing... whereupon it came out a mirror image of a mirror image and was a waste of paper.  <g>

If your printer won't help, try this:

Select everything on your PPT slide, copy it (Ctrl+C), insert a new blank slide, then use Paste Special | as EMF to paste in a graphic version of your slides' contents.

Rightclick, choose Size and Position.  Note the width of the graphic.

Then grab the selection handle in the middle of the left side and drag it to the right ... keep dragging PAST the right side and you'll see that you're flipping the graphic ... mirror image city.

Now go back into Size and Position and set the width to match the original (this ensures that the graphic isn't distorted).

Try pasting as PNG also.  See which gives better results.
