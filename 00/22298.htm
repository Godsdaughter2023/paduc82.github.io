Traditional / Legacy Get External Data vs new Get and Transform External Data

Greetings folks,

I found a thread related to this (that was never truly resolved - just closed by Microsoft to prevent replies...) - and I have a workaround for those of you who want the Legacy "from Text" option to be available.

Like many of you, I work with computers and Office quite frequently.  In fact, I'm a computer science professor, and although I teach mostly programming and software engineering courses, I like to teach an MS Office course occasionally.  The apparent removal of "Get External Data" and replacement with "Get and Transform External Data" dialog also threw me (and students, and staff, and colleagues) off.

I did find that the options, for example, the legacy "from Text" is still available but has to be added manually.

Instructions:

  1. Right-click an empty area of the Ribbon while on the Data tab and select Customize the Ribbon...

  2. Add a new Group to the Data Tab with the New Group button on the right side of the dialog.  I named mine "Get Data"


  3. Make sure you have Choose Commands from... set to "All Commands" on the left side of the dialog.  

  4. With the custom group you just added selected on the right side, select "From Text (Legacy)" (or whichever legacy command you want) and then click the Add >> button.

  5. Finally, hit OK

  6. Now you have the old From Text option available:

display document pages side by side - Microsoft Community

fredeew say:

display document pages side by side

How do you display multiple Word document pages side by side in the same window?

jay freedman say:

It's not quite clear what you're asking.

If the pages are in different documents, you cannot get them in the same window (that ability was removed from Word from 2013 onward). Open both documents; then, in either document, click View > View Side by Side. By default, scrolling in either document will cause both documents to scroll, but you can turn that off by clicking the Synchronous Scrolling button (in the same group on the View tab).

You can get the same effect by right-clicking the Windows taskbar and clicking the appropriate arrangement item in the menu.

If the pages are in the same document, you can display multiple consecutive pages in a single window by clicking View > Zoom (or clicking the percentage number in the right end of the status bar), clicking the Many Pages button, and choosing the number of pages to show. 

To see  pages in the same document that are not consecutive, click View > Split. This can show the pages in the top and bottom, but not side by side. You can scroll the sections independently.
