john w. vinson say:

That field setup violates normal form: the value of a field in a table should depend ONLY on the table's Primary Key, and if you have two mutually exclusive fields, then one of the fields depends on the other. With two fields there's nothing to prevent a record from being added with neither checked, or both checked.

I'd suggest collapsing this into a single RiskLevel field with (say) 0 being Low Risk and 1 being High Risk, and making the field Required. To keep your current checkbox interface you could bind this field to an Option Group control with two checkboxes; selecting one stores a 0 and selecting the other stores a 1.
