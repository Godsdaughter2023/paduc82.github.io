Hello Deb,

Thank you for posting.  The purpose of grouped tabs is simply to show you easily what tabs are related.  In other words, if you are on cnn.com and you click on a link for a story and it launches the story in a new tab, IE will “group” these tabs together and change the color on the tabs to green.  If you have more than one tab open and click links that open new tabs the parent tab and the spawned tabs will be grouped and assigned a color for the tabs.  There is no significance to the colors other than the first grouped set will be green, the second blue, the third yellow, the fourth purple and so on.  There is a lot more information available on the subject by click on Help on the IE toolbar and searching “Tabbed Browsing” and then click the “Tabbed Browsing FAQ”.

bobbronson say:

Using the INDIRECT function in an Array

Why does INDIRECT(“65”) work at the end of the first Array, but it does not work at the end of the second Array.

=CORREL(E12:INDIRECT("E65"),SPX!E12:INDIRECT("E65"))        Of course, SPX! is a different Worksheet.

INDIRECT("E65") is supposed to end the 52-row Array at E65 no matter if additional rows are inserted in between later. 

It is a truly absolute cell reference that does not change under any circumstances because Excel does not interpret the string "E65" as an address, but rather it treats it as plain text and therefore does not change it.

BUT, it's not working when reference is made to the same size Array in a different Worksheet, and I don't know why.

If I am interpreting the question correctly and E65 is simply plain text so that the formula always references E12:E65 and you do not want the E65 reference to change when rows are inserted in between E12 and E65 then I think it should be like the following.

=CORREL(INDIRECT("E12:"&"E65"),INDIRECT("SPX!E12:"&"E65"))

Hi A.Olague,

 

Based on your issue, I’d like to confirm whether the problem occurred in Skype for Business, Windows 10 or both.

 

To connect Bluetooth devices to Windows 10, please follow below:

 

  1. Open your Bluetooth device and make it connection status.
  2. On your computer, navigate to Start> Settings>Devices> Bluetooth.
  3. When your Bluetooth appears in the Bluetooth window, select it and click Pair.
  4. Click yes to confirm the passcode matches on both the Bluetooth device and computer and wait a few seconds.

 

For detailed information of connecting Bluetooth device to Windows 10, please refer to: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/help/15290/windows-connect-bluetooth-device.

 

In addition, to connect Bluetooth device in Skype for Business, just navigate to Options> Audio Device and select your Bluetooth device.
