mrcatfluf say:

Custom text tones overridden by system default

With Windows 8.1 setting customised text tones worked as it should. You could set an individual text tone for a contact, and every contact you DIDN'T customise had the system default.

This is broken in Windows 10, and I would call it a bug.

Setting the system text tone to anything other than "default" makes your per-contact tones useless.

You have two choices:

1: Keep the system on "default" and customise EVERY SINGLE CONTACT to avoid a default sound you don't want.

2: Change the system tone and defeat the purpose of having custom tones.

jllatham say:

Assuming that your main table is on a sheet named Sheet1 and the word Country is in cell A2 then try this on another sheet:

In cell A2 put this formula:   =Sheet1!$A$3

In cell B2 put this formula:   =COUNTIF($A$2:$A2,$A2)

In cell C2 put this formula:   =INDEX(Sheet1!B:F,MATCH(A2,Sheet1!A:A,0),B2)

Change B:F to include the last column on Sheet1 with a Yr# in it.

Fill the formula down the sheet.  When column C shows #REF! error, it means you have gone too far, just delete those formulas.

If you wanted to start the entries for the next country right below that, then you can fill the formula down to the first time it shows a #REF! error and then change the formula in column A on that row from =Sheet1!$A$3  to  =Sheet1!$A$4  to pick up the next country's identifier and then use those 3 cells on that row to continue filling down the sheet until you see another #REF! error.

Maybe the BeforeClose in the xla is being triggered by an action initiated from the xlam as a result of a 2007 menu item.
The xlam cannot be closed while a macro is running in it.
Maybe you need to use
  Application.OnTime Now, "MainCode.xla!ExitNow"
Rather than
  Application.Run "MainCode.xla!ExitNow"
or equivalent, so that the xlam macro will have completed before the BeforeClose tries to close it.
