how to make a text search box in ms access form

hello friend i want to make a search box on my form. form source is a table which name is "tblregister_Complaints" it has many field but i want if i type something in search box it will only look in some field like "IDno"  , "Caller_Name" , "contact_No" and "Address" and then show all related results .

I need a immediate help.

scott mcdaniel say:

I'd suggest adding a Button that will launch your search. Assuming your button is named "cmSearch" and your textbox is named "txSearch", you can do this in the Click event of your cmSearch button:

Function csSearch_Click()

  Me.Filter = "FieldName=" & Me.txSearch 

  Me.FilterOn = True

End Function

If the "FieldName" you use is a Text field, use this syntax:

Me.Filter = "FieldName='" & Me.txSearch & "'"

If it's a Date field use this syntax:

Me.Filter = "FieldName=#" & me.txSearch & "#"

If you need to search multiple fields:

Me.Filter = "Field1='" & Me.txSearch & "' OR Field2='" & Me.txSearch & "'"

If you need wildcard searches, use the * operator:

Me.Filter = "Field1='" & Me.txSearch & "*'"

That would locate any record in the recordsource that began with the value you enter in txSearch

Not sure what you mean by that - an AutoNumber field should have no "value" to your database, and should only be used as a pointer to a specific record. Any use other than that should be reconsidered.

If you need to search multiple fields:

Me.Filter = "Field1='" & Me.txSearch & "' OR Field2='" & Me.txSearch & "'"

You can mix and match fields in there, of course. For example, if Field1 is a Text field, and Field2 is a Date field, and Field3 is a Numeric field:

Me.Filter = "Field1='" & Me.txSearch & "' OR Field2=#" & Me.txSearch2 & "# OR Field3=" & Me.txSearch3
