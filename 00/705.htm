a. user say:

Hi AggiePierce,

If you want to transfer your domain name to Windows Live Admin Center, you need to register your domain account. Please make sure that the MX record and CNAME record that you configured stay up to date.

To register your domain account, please follow the instructions given in this link.

Often, when your domain is already registered under Admin Center, old records are removed automatically. If this happens to your domain, your e-mail services and other Windows Live services will be disabled until you add the appropriate records.

For more information about how to change the MX records and CNAME records of your domain, on your registrar's website, search for DNS, CNAME, or MX.

If you don't have the information that you need to set up your MX record or CNAME record, you must reregister your domain name.

For more information, see Set up an MX record to authenticate your domain or Set up a CNAME record to authenticate your domain.

After you update your MX record, you can use Windows Live Admin Center to create new e-mail accounts on your domain name. Your users will then stop receiving e-mail through your former e-mail provider and will receive their e-mail through Admin Center.

Your users can activate their e-mail accounts by signing in to the Windows Live Hotmail website with the user name and temporary password that you assign when you create their accounts. Until an e-mail account is activated, all e-mail sent to that account will be returned as undeliverable.
