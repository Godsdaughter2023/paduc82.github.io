1 going to Windows Start Page "Default Page Windows 8"
2 Hover Mouse in upper-right corner of the screen then click on the "Search" button.
3 Type in "Control Panel" after a brief search you will see a button in the top left labled "Control Panel" click this and it will switch you to desktop mode and allow you to navigate the Control Panel.
4 From there click the tab labled "System and Security" 
5 Next click the second option in the list labeled "Windows Firewall"
6 The first thing you want to do is check to see if you are on a "Private Network" or a "Public Network" (A private Network is generally the most secure and while on this type of Network you are less likely to be vulnerable to attacks, Public Network's are accessible by anyone and give a greater risk.)
7 On the right it will say Private Networks Connected/ Not Connected, and below that it will say Guest or public networks Connected/Not Connected. Most computer's security settings are different based on your type of connection if you are on a Public Network your computer settings will be more strict and might be preventing your programs from connected to the internet. (If it say's you are Connected to a Private Network you can most likely rule this out.)
8 If you are connected to a Private Network and your programs are still not working correctly In the Top-Left of the window under "Control Panel Home" you want to click the option "Allow an app or feature through Windows Firewall"
9 A list will be displayed of all your "Internet Capable Programs" quickly scroll through and find the one's you are having trouble with and take note of the Check-Boxes next to them. If neither box is checked you will want to allow them to connect. Do this by clicking the "Change Settings" button located at top right
10 You will then want to allow your program through the Firewall, do this by simply checking the box next to it. (Note that if on Public Network doing this may leave your computer vulnerable or at a greater-risk) 
11 After you have done this click ok, Now go back and try to run your program and see if the problem is solved.
12 If you cannot find your program click the "Allow another app...." button and navigate to your programs location (Generally in  the C:/ drive in the Programs folder.) and select your program and adjust the settings to allow it through the Firewall.
13 If this doesn't solve your issue and the problem persists try going into Internet Explorer, clicking the Gear in the top right and opening Internet Options. Go to the security tab and check to see how strict your settings are. You should be able to click "Reset To Default" to fix this if it is the issue. (Located in bottom right)


I hope this helps if not let me know and I will give you a few more suggestions.

Resolving a bad block problem: An I/O status code of 0xC000009C or 0xC000016A typically indicates that the data could not be read from the disk because of a bad block (sector). If you can restart the computer after the error, Autochk runs automatically and attempts to map the bad sector to prevent it from being used anymore.
