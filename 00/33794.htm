david vinod say:

Hi

Check if the sound card is detected in device manager, refer the steps provided below to check the sound card in device manager.

a)    Click Start, and then click Control Panel.

b)    Click System and Maintenance, click System, and then click Device Manager.

Method 1: Follow the steps provided in the following link and check if that helps.

No sound in Windows’

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows/help/no-sound-in-windows

Also refer the following link for more information.

‘Tips for fixing common sound problems’

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows7/Tips-for-fixing-common-sound-problems

Method 2: Update the sound card driver’s and check if the issue persists. Refer the following link to update the drivers for the sound card.

‘Update a driver for hardware that isn't working properly’

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows7/Update-a-driver-for-hardware-that-isnt-working-properly

Refer the following link for more information.

‘Update drivers: recommended links’

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows7/Update-drivers-recommended-links

I would also suggest you to download and install the sound card drivers from the manufacturer’s website.

You may also try to unplug the sound card from the PCI slot and then, plug it back and check if that helps. If you’re not comfortable to do that you may contact your computer manufacturer regarding the issue.
