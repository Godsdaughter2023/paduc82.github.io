Two things:

1- What's your rush? Is there something in the new update that you must have now? The conventional wisdom - for all versions of Windows - has always been to wait at least 2 or 3 months after an upgrade has been released before installing it.

2- Microsoft pushes out new feature updates in waves. It's not practical to push out multi-gigabyte updates to 700+ million Windows 10 PCs all at once. Microsoft chooses target groups of PCs to upgrade and waits for the telemetry from those PCs to tell it how well (or poorly) the upgrade is working. Then another group of PCs gets the upgrade, and so on until eventually all Windows 10 PCs are upgraded. It can take months for a new upgrade to reach all Windows 10 devices. However, independent research consistently shows that more than 90% of Windows 10 PCs get the upgrade within a few months.
