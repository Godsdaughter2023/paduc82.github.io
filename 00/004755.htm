diane poremsky mvp (slipstick.com) say:

The Help files are wrong when it comes to Office 2013. The display name is the email account name and it can't easily be changed.  You can change the display names in the Account list, which reflects what you see in the From dropdown when composing a message.

 

If you know what you are doing, you can edit the registry - before editing it, export the key so you can easily fix it if the display name is wrong.

HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Office\15.0\Outlook\Profiles\Outlook\[big number, one for each account]

Find value name 001f3001 on the right side.  Rather than deleting the account name and reentering it, I replaced the letters, using spaces it fill in if the email address was longer than a display name - success, I think. The display names changed but some accounts in my profile are not working. (Could be unrelated.)

 

On the drafts issue, you can't remove it - that indicates the folder is not synced with the server.

 

Update: You can change the display names in the Folder list for IMAP, EAS, and POP3 from the outlook interface.

http://www.outlook-tips.net/tips/tip-1057-rename-accounts-outlook-2013/ 

 

Exchange server accounts can only be changed by editing the registry.

acadiamoose say:

Old Office license to new pc.

We purchased a 3-license version of Office 2007, Home and Student, in 2009.  I have used up all three licenses.  Two of the pcs that I used those licenses on are about to retire to the old pc graveyard.  Is there anyway that I can "reclaim" two of the licenses (actually, to think of it, all three since the first pc to have this Office died two years ago) and use this Office 2007 on two new pcs that we are now purchasing.

Thank you,
AcadiaMoose.
rohn007 say:
No problem.

The 3-computer Home and Student license (FYI aka "Retail" or "FPP") does allow you to transfer the installations to new machines.

To transfer Office you need 2 things, your unique 25 character product key and the generic installation file for the matching Office bundle.  As well, to comply with the license terms you have to make Office unusable on the old machine.  Scrapping the machine qualifies, but it is best to uninstall Office, or completely wipe the HD BEFORE scrapping or reselling an old machine.

All you have to do is install on the new machines. For the license from the dead machine you will probably be able to use internet activation. For the other 2 machines, you can try internet activation, but if it doesn't work you will have to use phone activation. When you phone in, don't bother with the initial prompts (you already know they won't work).  Wait, a long time, until you are offered the option of talking to a live "service" rep.  Explain that you have uninstalled office from the old machine and are transferring the license to the new one. They should allow it to complete.

Unfortunately, MS did not include a mechanism for users to "deactivate" license installations.  That is partly fixed in 2013, but that fix is Office 365 2013 specific and doesn't help you.
