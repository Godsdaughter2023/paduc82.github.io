graham mayor say:

The only folder of interest at the moment is
C:\Documents and settings\owner\Application Data\Microsoft\Templates

1. You cannot replace normal.dotm with normal.dot. The file structures are entirely different from one another.
2. The only files of interest are normal.dotm and the normal.dot with your personalisations.
3. It is probable that the macros etc are already present, but let's start from scratch.
If you only have Office 2007 installed. Close Word then move anything resembling normal*.* out of that folder to a safe location, leaving only your original Word 2002 NORMAL.DOT template.
Start Word
From Word Options > Advanced > Save - Check the check boxes for 'Always create backup copy' and 'Prompt before saving Normal template'.
Whilst opening, Word will have read the macros and autotext entries into a temporary template. You can check they are there by clicking ALT+F8 (and in the case of autotext entries from the building blocks organizer)
Close Word.
At the prompt to save the normal template - save it.
Remove the normal.dot template from the user templates folder to the aforementioned safe location, retaining only the newly created NORMAL.DOTM
Restart Word.
Your macros  will still be present.
The exclamation mark on the normal.dotm template indicates that it is a macro enabled template.
