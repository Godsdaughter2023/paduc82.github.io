greg maxey say:

Bomber,


Yes.  Before taking to the time show you how (which required VBA), may I ask why you are using legacy formfields instead of content controls?  Will all of your document users be using Word 2007 or higher?  If so there is really no need for legacy form fields in the document and a mapped content control will work perfectly for this application.



bombercanuck say: We have been using the Legacy fields, because most of our documents are recent conversions from older versions of Word. We are familiar with the formatting controls, and we can easily manipulate them to suit our needs (e.g. formatting phone numbers to be Number fields with the proper spacing and punctuation).

greg maxey say:

Bomber,


You are missing out on whole lot of advanced features by sticking with formfields.  However, you are in the pilot seat so:

You have three text fields Name1, Name2 and Name3.  Assign the following macro to run on exit from all three:

Sub RuplicateNames()
Dim oFld As Word.FormField
  If Selection.FormFields.Count = 1 Then
    Set oFld = ActiveDocument.FormFields(Selection.FormFields(1).Name)
  ElseIf Selection.FormFields.Count = 0 And Selection.Bookmarks.Count > 0 Then
    Set oFld = ActiveDocument.FormFields(Selection.Bookmarks(Selection.Bookmarks.Count).Name)
  End If
  Select Case oFld.Name
    Case "Name1"
      ActiveDocument.FormFields("Name2").Result = oFld.Result
      ActiveDocument.FormFields("Name3").Result = oFld.Result
    Case "Name2"
      ActiveDocument.FormFields("Name1").Result = oFld.Result
      ActiveDocument.FormFields("Name3").Result = oFld.Result

    Case "Name3"
      ActiveDocument.FormFields("Name1").Result = oFld.Result
      ActiveDocument.FormFields("Name2").Result = oFld.Result
  End Select
End Sub

Sub MonthlyWorksheet()

    'If Y < 2000 Then Exit Sub

bernie deitrick say: Change your formula in B7 from

= Right($B$1,5)


to 

=DATEVALUE(RIGHT($B$1,5) & "-" & YEAR(K2))

When you use an incomplete date string as the input to NETWORKDAYS (or any date function, for that matter) the default assumption is that the year is the same as the system year. So your 12-01 date from the sheet tab is converted to 12-01-14, and not 12-01-13 - hence the 259 instead of 1.

techfreak_ say:

There are only 4 methods Skype accounts are/were created:

1. A Skype name you created long ago (sign in with your Skype name, not email address)

2. A live:emailid Skype account with or without additional numbers automatically generated from your email address (sign in with your email address, not your Skype name).

3. A live:alphanumberic  Skype account generated from your email address (sign in with your phone number, not email address).

4. A facebook:emailid Skype account with or without additional numbers automatically generated from your email address when registered with your Facebook account. (sign in through the Facebook sign in, in the client software - no longer possible).

In regards to number 4:

Any Facebook-generated accounts with the Skype name prefix of facebook:, can no longer be signed into, in any fashion. Users were notified to backup any data for those accounts, as they would be inaccessible after January 2018.

Can I sign in to Skype with my Facebook account?

Since these accounts were terminated, so would their related services.

All the other methods are still in play. Depending on how or when you signed into Skype and the selections you chose, you may or may not have 2 or more separate accounts. Separate subscriptions could be in any of the 3 main account types mentioned above.

If you know the current password to your Skype name account, you can still sign into it. You are not prevented from doing so. If you are currently signed into Skype in your web browser using your email address or phone number, and do not find the active subscriptions you are looking for, you are signed into the wrong account. All you need to do is sign out of that account and sign in, to the correct one.

If you are unable to sign in, due to an incorrect or unknown password, you can only regain access to your account by performing an account recovery using the account recovery form. If you need access to your Skype name account you would initially specify recovering that name, not your email address. If you specify recovering your email address account it will only recover your live:email id account.

If you are unable to successfully recover your account due to not having matching information on file, you will not regain access to your account. You can attempt to explain the situation to Skype support over live chat but I am unaware of any other available methods of standard recovery beyond that form. The only remaining options would be to make an official dispute (methods vary by country), other legal action or terminate the billing source associated with the account to attempt to avoid future billing.
