steve rindsberg say:

Shapes on the master appear behind everything on slides.

The only exception (and this might be the solution you need) is footers/slide numbers/date&time placeholders.

Go into the master, select the footer placeholder and format it to fill it with the picture that represents your watermark.  You'll need to adjust the size so it doesn't distort the picture.

Then go to normal view, choose Insert | Headers and Footers, put a check next to Footer and then apply or apply to all.

Your watermark image will appear in front of everything on the slide.

If you later add more stuff to the slide, it will sit in front of the watermark; in that case, either select and send the watermark to the front on the slides that need it OR go back to Headers & Footers, turn the footer OFF, apply, then repeat and turn it back ON again.

No. As for the workaround mentioned in the link article, we need to create a mail contact for the external domain address and assign the Send As permission of the contact for Office 365 mailboxes.
