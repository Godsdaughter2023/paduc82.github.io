Double NAT is typically caused from having more than one networking device doing NAT, such as modem/router from an ISP doing NAT, then having a second router doing NAT.  If this is the cause, the best ways to resolve this is to either disable NAT on the first device, or to remove the second device altogether.

What is the make and model of your computer?

Method 1: I suggest you to run the Power troubleshooter and check the issue.

The power troubleshooter can automatically detect and fix any incorrect power settings. Refer to these steps to run this troubleshooter:

1) Type Troubleshooting in the search box and select Troubleshooting Now select view all option on the left pane.

2) Click on Power troubleshooter and follow the prompts.

 

Method 2: Reset and restore the power plans to default.

1. Click “Windows Key + X”, and then click on command prompt.

2. In the command prompt, type powercfg –restoredefaultschemes and hit Enter.

3. Exit command prompt.

 

Note: This would reset the power plan settings to default. Any previously customized power plans would be removed.

 

Method 3: I suggest you to Change Screen and Sleep settings in Current Power Plan in Settings app and see if it helps: 

1. Open Settings, and click/tap on the System icon.

2. Click/tap on Power & sleep on the left side, and set the Screen and Sleep settings to what you want for your current power plan.

3. When finished, you can close Settings if you like.

 

Hope this helps. If the issue remains unresolved, please get back to us and we would be happy to help.

 

Thank you.

Hi,

If the PIN is still not being required even after configuring it in the Sign-in options in Settings, we recommend that you follow the steps below:

  1. Press Windows key + I and select System.
  2. Under Power & sleep tab, click Additional power settings.
  3. Click Change plan settings located next to your Preferred plan.
  4. Click Change advanced power settings and click the Sleep tab to expand it.
  5. Expand the Hibernate after option and set the time for the hibernation.
  6. Click Apply, and then OK.

erwinjames san say:

Hi MrPopa,

Is this a new computer that you're building, or just a re-installation of your Windows? If so, yes it can be possible. You can take advantage of adding partitions on an existing hard drive as one option. However, it isn't advisable to have your Windows reinstalled on an external drive as this would slow down it's operation. I would recommend that, after reinstalling your Windows 10 on your main drive, you can install programs along on your main drive. And as for saving your files, you can have your programs designate the path to be saved on your external drive instead.

It would be best to have your external drive used as storage for your files, instead of your OS installed on your external. This way, you'd be able to ensure your Windows is running as expected. In backing up your files, you might want to consider the steps suggested on this forum article to be able to manage them to your convenience.
