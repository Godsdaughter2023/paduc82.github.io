tom van stiphout say:

It is by design.

Long text is not just short text but then longer, it is a completely different data type and works by different rules. For example you cannot put an index on such field either. Why? Because the field could potentially be 2 GB long, and doing all that searching or indexing would slow Access down to a crawl. So only use long text what it was intended for: storage of lots of text without too many features.

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

First of all, do not use Right Justification [i.e., Paragraph Alignment] for this purpose... That's what's causing your problem. Use of anything other than the default Left Justification causes tab settings to behave differently than what you expect.

To get the effect you're describing requires 2* tab stops: One at the spot where you want the items to align, one at the spot where you want the prices to align. The important consideration is that you need to press the tab key at the beginning of each line before entering the item, then press tab again to enter the price. Press return to go to the next paragraph (i.e., line). The result will be as shown here:

This example uses a right-aligned tab stop @ 2.5" & a Decimal tab stop with a Line Leader at 6". Adjust their position to suit yourself. Note that unless the prices are strictly in whole dollars I strongly recommend using a Decimal tab rather than a right tab. Otherwise the $s & the 'column' of decimal points will have an obvious, unprofessional snaking effect rather than being sharply aligned vertically.

*If you really want to do it right, IMO it's better to use 3 tab stops - a Center-aligned tab for the $ which provides a much better visual impact. That same example with a right tab @1.75", a center tab @ 5.75" & a decimal tab @ 6" would look more like this:

*I suggest avoiding a right tab for the prices... As mentioned above it can cause the appearance of a snaking column of decimal points due to the fact that not all numbers are the same width.

Also, my personal opinion is that a Table for this purpose is considerably more fiddly to deal with, especially if you want to maintain uniform alignment of the price column.
