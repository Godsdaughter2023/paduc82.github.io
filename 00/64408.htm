If your external hard drive enclosure came with an optional mains power cable connect it. Similarly, some come with a cable that has 3 plugs: USB3 at one end and (USB3 + USB2) at the other. The additional USB2 plug is to boost the power. Insufficient power supply to external hard drives is a known reason for them not to be recognised.

considerate_guy say:

That model uses a Digital8 tape format, correct?  The USB cable is only for retrieving digital stills that you take on the camera.  In order to import the video, you must use the Firewire connection.  This requires:

  1. PC with a Firewire port (or an add-on Firewire card to add those ports).
  2. A Firewire (IEEE1394 / iLink) cable, probably of the 6-pin to 4-pin variety.

Turn off your computer, turn off your camcorder.  Now connect it to the computer with the Firewire cable.  Start your computer.  Once Windows starts, put your camera in playback mode and it should then offer you a choice to import your video.


Remember, capturing from tape is a real-time process.  Unlike memory-based camcorders, you have to playback the tape and for every hour of video it will take an hour to capture.  I used to love my mini-DV camcorder (also tape-based SD video) and later my HDV camcorder (tape-based HD video).  But if I were to recommend a video camera today, I have to highly recommend a memory-based HD camcorder.  In fact, that's mostly what you'll find on the market and it is hard to find anything else.

 

But if you have existing tape footage you want to capture from your Digital8 camcorder, the Firewire connection is the way to do it.
