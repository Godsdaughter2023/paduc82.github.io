First of all, your interpretation of the standard is correct.

Under normal circumstances, most of the traffic should be going

are some weaselwords in the standard about a sender's right to

cache information for a while so that, if they discovered that

the path to autodiscover.abc.com was unavailable, unreliable, or

slow, they could go to a lower-preference system and remember

that for a while.  From your description, I don't think that is

happening in your case (either).

the Sophos box and it was inclined to try delay, graylisting, or

other temporary timeout tricks, the sender would be perfectly

justified giving up (or, in some cases, ignoring the 4yz

message) and trying the second preference server.   I have

trouble guessing what might get them to the third preference,

but you get the picture.
