rohn007 say:
There isn't any really clearly defined process for doing that.

For Programs, the best approach is to simply install every program you want on the new computer. It gives you a clean system, eliminating all of the junk, addons and programs you no longer use, that has collected on your old system, which tends to slow it down. In the old DOS days, it was relatively easy to copy programs from one computer to another. But in Windows, there are lots of registry entries associated with the program installtation. That makes it much more difficult to simply "copy" the program from one computer to another. Many programs have "configuration" files that you CAN copy (ie MS Word Normal.dotm) to the new computer to bring over at least some of your old customizations.

For personal files, if you used the default windows setup, all of your personal files will be collected in folders under your personal userid in C:\Users\userid.

You can copy that folder to your OneDrive (if it is less than 5GB total for Free OneDrive, or less than 1TB for Office 365 subscribers). Or you can copy that folder to an external USB drive.

Linking to your email provides you many benefits.  If your hard drive crashes or gets damaged, you can just login to a new computer using your email and all your settings and files will come down to the new computer.  This backup system is very valuable.  The email login also connects you to many other abilities like free music, OneDrive (formerly SkyDrive), provides email syncing, and much more.  Also if you have more than one computer, using your email login means your preferences sync to all of your computers (and even Windows Phone if you have one).
