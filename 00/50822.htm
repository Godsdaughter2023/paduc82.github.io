All in favor of MS re-introducing the Trackball Optical, say hey! - Microsoft Community

greggeshelman say:

All in favor of MS re-introducing the Trackball Optical, say hey!

The Microsoft Trackball Optical 1.0, one of the best thumb trackballs ever made, with the exception of one fatal flaw - the steel bearing balls that support the thumb ball.

You'd think steel would be tough and wear resistant, but a little oil from the human thumb, combined with regular old dust and other crud made a sort of lapping compound that wore flat spots on them, which allowed the ball to drag on the back of its socket.

The key to MSTBO1.0 longevity is to *always* wash your hands before using it and *always* pop the ball out and clean the socket before you use it.

Other brands of trackballs use hard ceramic or silicon carbide or even synthetic gemstone for those support balls. There are people who charge $60 and up to do a repair/upgrade of the MS trackballs with silicon carbide support balls.

Another issue it had was a high manufacturing defect rate due to the placement of the transistor providing power to the sensor LED. A person not taking proper care during assembly could hit that transistor with part of the housing and break one or two of its solder connections. Since the device also has an LED simply to illuminate the socket, assemblers plugging them into a USB power outlet would think them functional, even though the illumination LED is much dimmer than the tracking LED. I picked up a bunch of them on an auction website after MS discontinued the model, and fixed them all with a little bit of solder on that transistor.

But now I'm down to only one unworn one left, despite all the care and cleanliness.

I'd love to see Microsoft re-introduce this trackball exactly like the original, with the exception of using something much tougher than steel for the support balls.

If you want to see that, respond affrimatively!

ericdmadsen say:

Thank you so much for the suggestion!  I rarely post questions on these forums, but this one was REALLY weird.

I had a time skew between my ADFS server, the domain controller, and the client machine I was testing from.

So I went on my domain and made sure all VM hosts and guests had the same time settings, and BANG it started to work again!
