darleen pen say:

Hi Tom,

If you are using the POP3 configuration, all of your emails will be downloaded onto your Macbook and they will be deleted from the email service. This means that after the email is downloaded to your Macbook, it can only be accessed using the same computer. If you try to access your email from a different device, the messages that have been previously downloaded won't be available to you.

If you want to access your emails on multiple devices, I suggest using the IMAP configuration in your Macbook and iPhone. IMAP allows you to access your email wherever you are, from any device. When you read an email message using IMAP, you aren't actually downloading or storing it on your computer; instead, you're reading it from the email service. As a result, you can check your email from different devices.

Outlook.com / Hotmail.com / Live.com IMAP Settings:

Incoming Server: imap-mail.outlook.com
Port: 993
Encryption: SSL/TLS

Outgoing Server: smtp-mail-outlook.com
Port: 587
Encryption: STARTTLS

You might need to get in touch with the support team of your devices since they are the experts when it comes to navigating and changing the settings of the default mail apps in your iOS and Mac devices. Ask them to change the email settings of your Macbook and iPhone's mail app into IMAP and provide them the IMAP settings listed above.

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

Documents generated using label templates contain nothing but a Table. When the insertion point is anywhere in the Table a Layout contextual tab appears on the Ribbon along with a Table Design contextual tab. Click the View Gridlines button in the group at the left end of the Layout tab or go to the Table menu & select Gridlines.

If you're doing individual labels [rather than a mail merge] it may be easier to use the Labels tool on the Mailings tab. In that dialog you can select the Row & Column of the label sheet you're going to use. See this Word Help article for details: Create and print labels in Word 2016 for Mac - Word for Mac
