a. user say:

Hi Khalid,

 

We greatly appreciate your time and effort for posting here in the Microsoft Community Forum and it's a pleasure assisting you in trying to restore your erased emails.

 

Please sign in to your account on the web browser and in the left pane of the Outlook window, click the Deleted folder. At the bottom of the window, click recover deleted messages. Outlook will recover as many messages as possible and put them in your Deleted folder. If you don't see the deleted messages that you wanted, it means they are permanently lost.

 

When the recovered messages are back in your Deleted folder, you should move the important ones back to your inbox or personal folders because the Deleted folder gets cleaned out periodically.

 

With regard to your inquiry about the Junk folder settings, it is by default that it deletes emails every 10 days. However, you can set your junk email filter. Please follow the steps below on how to do this.


1. In the upper-right corner of the page, click the Gear Icon, and then click on More mail settings.
2. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.
3. Under Choose a junk e-mail filter, choose the level of protection that you want, and then click Save. If the Exclusive level was set, messages from senders not on your Contacts or Safe senders lists will have been sent to your Junk e-mail folder.

Note: The best way to make sure that someone gets in your Inbox is to add them to your contact list. Find a message from the sender’s email address and open it. In the message header, you’ll see a link to 

Add to contacts beside the sender’s name. All you have to do is click that link and the email address will be on your contact list.

jay freedman say:

There are a couple of possibilities for the Normal text and pictures appearing in the TOC.

  • The paragraphs in the text may have an "outline level" different from "Body Text". Right-click in each of those paragraphs, click Paragraph in the menu, and look at the Outline Level box in the Paragraph dialog. If it isn't "Body Text", set it to that.
    • If the dropdown is grayed out/disabled, that means the paragraph isn't really in Normal style but in one of the built-in styles, such as Heading 2. That could be because the text is separated from the preceding heading by a manual line break instead of a paragraph mark, so it has the same style as the heading, but the appearance has been overridden by direct formatting.
  • The paragraphs in the text may be erroneously included within the hidden bookmark that should include only the heading. (This should never happen in Office 365, which has been coded to avoid it, but it could have been created in an earlier version.) Go to the Insert > Bookmark dialog and check the box for "Hidden bookmarks", and select the option button for Location. In the list of names, you'll see ones that are _Toc followed by a long number. Click on the one for the heading preceding the Normal text (this will take some trial-and-error) and click the Go To button. If the highlighted area includes both the heading and the text, delete that bookmark and then remove and re-insert the TOC.

The page numbering problem is usually the result of a section break that restarts the page numbering in the text. It could be a Continuous page break, which can be hard to locate. To find it, put the cursor on the page between the Transfer Pipe Design heading and Transfer Pipe Bolted Joints, and click View > Draft. The section break will appear as a double dotted line across the screen with "Section Break" in the middle. If the break isn't necessary for changing the page layout, delete it. If it is necessary, click in the text after the break, go back to Print Layout view, and open the header pane. Then click Header & Footer > Page Number > Format Page Numbers, and click the option button for "Continue from previous section".

By the way, in your screen shot I notice that the heading 700/1-4.13.2 is repeated. That shouldn't happen if you're using SEQ fields to create automatic numbering, so I'm guessing that the numbers have been typed manually or pasted from some other copy of the document.

asmodeus01 say:

It still sounds like your driver is incorrect. when you insert a USB device, the device's UART negotiates the correct current for that device. i.e if it needs charging it negotiates up to 500mA @5V (USB2) or upto 3A on USB3. The MS USB3 drivers are flaky depending on what motherboard your PC has installed. Personally I have found the Eltron USB3 drivers do not install properly under Win10, which is why I suggested you find your Motherboard Manual, and see how the USB sockets are conected to the internal hub(s). Then have a look at your device manager for these hubs and confirm that they have the latest MOTHERBOARD MANUFACTURERS DRIVERS for the correct board revision. You could perhaps look at reflashing your bios if is is more than 2 revisions old. (New machines can still have unupdated BIOS chips). Look at this motherboard's forum pages for any discussion of this problem.

I can't really offer anymore advice other than consider using a standalone iphone charger. Nowhere have you stated what type of PC you are using or any other details, or if you have tried other leads. One note though, I have come across iphone USB leads that won't charge when connected to USB socket THEN plugged into phone. You have to have your phone connected then plug the USB side in. I suspect this is due to bad wiring on the 30pin side. I haven't seen this issue with the lightning connector.
