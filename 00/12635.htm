jerryduan_msft8021 say:

Hi Matthew,

Neither getting user’s information via registered computers or getting a report to show all computers which are registered is feasible. Administrators are only able to see the registered devices information via Office 365 admin center > USERS >Active Users > Tick a user > Office Installations.

Regarding the activation question, if a user is assigned the same license with the former user who used the Office suite, then it’s not necessary to re-install the whole Office suite to re-activate it. The current user can activate the Office suite with his own license.

As described above “the computer didn't prompt for office 2013 registration.  later the user received a message about unlicensed product on the computer.”, it is because once the computer is activated, the computer connects to the Activation and Validation Service to verify the license status and extend the product key every 30 days. Office will still remain fully functional in this period until the next time extend the product key. After you delete the former user’s license, it still needs time for the Office suite to go into reduced functionality mode which can be reactivated with a new license.

If you want to uninstall the product key for the left user, you can Click this Fix it to uninstall your product key and then reactivate the Office suite in the PC client.

For the detailed information, please refer to:

Overview of licensing and activation in Office 365 ProPlus
