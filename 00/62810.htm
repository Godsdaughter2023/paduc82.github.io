.

Anyway, playing a new character on Legendary is a great decision and a solid challenge. Good luck and have fun with it. It must be noted, however, that Legendary resets to Novice when you start playing Skyrim after having turned your console off. You have to manually set it to Master, return to game, then set it back to Legendary. After that you're welcome to continue playing.

.

Vampires are extremely difficult to take out in pretty much every encounter you could possibly have with them. Take a melee-using companion with you and do your best to get assistance when dealing with them. Also, don't bother engaging them from the front when you can avoid it... Drain Life will murder characters pretty much effortlessly for your early levels, and that's just talking about one vampire. Take a companion, equip them in Dawnguard Heavy Armour if it's available, let vampires focus on them while you attack from behind and heal your companion, get any nearby guards or animals to help... use archery... use healing spells, a Staff of Paralysis... go ahead and use anything you've got at your disposal but you'll really benefit from keeping their focus on someone else. You're also going to want some Dawnguard armour... that Drain Life spell really doesn't do you any favours, nor does it take long to exterminate you.

.

Identify the key skills in each tree and work on them: Block's Block Runner and Elemental Protection, Alteration's Magic Resistance, Conjuration's Summon Spells in accordance to your enemy's weaknesses IF AND ONLY IF summoned creatures scale based on the difficulty... Restoration's Novice Restoration, Recovery, Respite and Regeneration in combination, Heavy Armour's Conditioning if it's relevant, Archery's Power Shot, One Handed's Savage Strike and Fighting Stance, possibly Paralysing Strike, Speech's Merchant and Pickpocket's Extra Pockets.

.

The above perks are all well-worth considering.

.

There are lots of more specialised perks that could work wonders too, and a little grinding would give you early access to them. I hate to think about the contrast between a bandit with a Steel Warhammer and a Bandit who just had that Warhammer stolen out of their hands, for example.

.

Other than Perks, be careful about your gameplay. You can comfortably strafe and block/bash your way to victory in almost any straight melee fight against a single opponent with anything larger than a Longsword. If you're careful you can easily outdo several of those opponents, though you'd better not make a mistake. Keep enemies between you and any archers and feel free to save during the long fights you're going to be in. As a note, you're going to want to level Health and Stamina almost equally for a melee-based character as they're both extremely important. A little Magicka (I typically use 150) helps immensely with your ability to heal yourself and case other supplemental magic as you're going to be unable to rely on Alchemy unless you've got a hell of a lot of ingredients and some serious potions, though you'll want a few on hand to get out of those "I paused the game as his sword is making contact with my neck" moments.

.

Doing certain things in the environment wreaks havoc with the enemy AI as long as they don't have ranged options available (jumping onto a big enough object often causes groups of enemies to scatter in different directions, mysteriously). You can almost always reset an enemy's approach by doing something with the geometry in the area and arrows are remarkably powerful, especially with Faendal's help leveling yourself to 50 (and the very useful Power Shot). You'll never run out of Steel Arrows as you'll never run out of Guards.

.

Dragon Priests and other mages are all easy to take out with Archery as long as they aren't throwing Lightning at you. You can keep mages suppressed with a Shock Weapon and ample usage of your bash attacks. Be careful with charging Frost Mages unless you're confident about it, resistant to it or close to cover. Things like Draugr Deathlords are actually highly underwhelming unless they've got bows in which case Block makes them, amusingly enough, highly underwhelming. They're also remarkably fragile and don't take much damage compared to other opponents you could come across.

.

Giants? What are Giants? Mammoths are scarier and other than easily peppering them to death they're actually very capable opponents.

.

In particular, assuming enemy levels aren't a problem and it's a reliable spell, being able to turn an enemy into a distraction with Fury/whatever would be extremely useful.

.

I love the use of Ice Form to freeze several opponents and leave just one to take out by yourself, though it often doesn't work as well as it could. Enemies are too tough to get rid of easily in the time Ice Form will give you and companions love shooting frozen enemies. Soul Tear is an extremely useful finishing move as it resurrects any given opponent, if I'm not mistaken, and Unrelenting Force is always a useful way to remove people from the area or kill them with a fall.

.

Hmm. What else?

.

Oh, Dragons!

.

Dragons are pathetic once they get on the ground. They're very easily bashed to death and Block Runner/Elemental Protection make their attempts to kill you before you get close underwhelming. If you're using an appropriate shield, or you've got both Fire and Frost defense shields on you (thank that Pickpocket perk) AND you're using Block Runner/Elemental Protection, well... I believe that's going to be 15% damage, which isn't very scary. You can interrupt every one of their attacks on the ground with a bash and take cover while they're in the air. Just be sure to give them space to land, or else it'll be a pointless fight.

.

Your own Drain Health weapon can be very useful. After acquiring the Ebony Blade, I've been able to stand toe-to-toe with most possible opponents comfortably regenerating the damage they've done while dealing the same to them. Turning and sprinting out of battle to heal sounds ridiculously implausible, but... it's extremely effective. You can also strafe enemies rather well even while using both hands to cast whatever the default healing spell is (Dual Casting sucks, by the way), along with regenerating the stamina you're about to use to defeat whichever enemy it is you're healing against. Isn't it convenient that your Magicka regenerates while you're bashing something, making it very easy to back out, recover your Health and Stamina and continue bashing them while your Magicka recovers to let you do it all over again?

________

.

As far as those low levels go, be extremely aware of what you've got at your disposal, whether it be in the inventory or not, and focus on your general gameplay and tactics. Worry less about leveling Stamina than Health for a while as if you're constantly being OHKd by a deathblow then you're not going to get much further. This means less bashing and more strafing, so be careful as even a blocked blow can kill you at around 80 Health but you'll get better at it with practice.

.

Consider leveling your Health by raising passive or crafting skills for a while as although it's commonly warned against, the crafting skills can all carry low combat skills and you really are going to need that Health. Even Pickpocket, entirely passive, will guarantee about 10, 000 gold in the hand when you unlock the Thieve's Guild. Also, combat skills go up extremely quickly when enemies take minutes to defeat rather than single hits so there's no need to stress out about maintaining them. If you have the Magicka and healing spells available to you then feel free to spend some time raising your Armour/Block to hasten unlocking the best Perks.

.

Be careful about choosing to loose Dragons upon the world as they're exponentially more painful the lower level you are. At about 20 or so you should be perfectly capable of taking them out but if you have to battle them at level 10 it might get a bit ugly.

.

If you already know any early-access item locations or fixed spawns for something that may help, go and get it as soon as possible. Though you won't be able to obtain it as soon as it becomes available, the Black Star is one such item. Some things just make a massive difference and they're worth having them if you can identify them and know where to find them. If you know about a guaranteed source of gems or other items of wealth, enchanted items, weapons or armour of above-currently-available strength, go and get them.

.

That's good, because you'll be doing it a lot. You have to manually set it to Master, return to game, then reset it to Legendary, after which you're welcome to continue playing.

.

Take anything anyone says under advisement and incorporate it into your playstyle but make sure that at the end of the day, it is your playstyle. Everything I wrote is also only my opinion and other people may have different thoughts about much of it.

.

Good luck, and make sure you have fun with the experience.
