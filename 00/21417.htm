glennnz_007 say:

I managed to narrow it down to OneDrive that was causing the issue. After reading Millewa's post I hunted down a way to delete the cache, and so far so good.  Fingers crossed as this has been dragging out over a few days now.

I followed these steps to delete the cache (yes - I know its for SkyDrive but it still worked)

To clear your cache, close all your Office processes like groove.exe (which is SkyDrive Pro), msouc.exe, msosync.exe, office library sync,

Browse to “C:\Users\%username%\AppData\Local\Microsoft\Office\Spw” and delete all the content. (You will see that this is all related to groove and spw = SharePoint Workspace).


Next, go to “C:\Users\%username%\AppData\Local\Microsoft\Office\15.0\OfficeFileCache” and also delete all content from this folder. This is where all the caching from your files are stored.

After you deleted everything, you should be able to start SkyDrive Pro again (using “C:\Program Files\Microsoft Office\Office15\GROOVE.EXE“).

Good luck! I will re post if it starts playing up again :|

Resolved my specific issue of slow printing in Excel.

After upgrading to Excel 2016 for Mac I experienced incredibly long print times on my older HP b&w laser printer - longer than 15 minutes for a single sheet. In Excel 2011 this same document takes 3 seconds to print.

This slow printing issue didn’t happen on every Excel document (only on some specific spreadsheets), and it never happened on my newer color Ricoh laser printer. 

Starting with the spreadsheet that consistently printed slow, I removed elements one by one until it did print quickly. For me the issue was the thin, dotted cell borders I had used extensively in Excel 2011.  (This one with the red arrow in the graphic below)

After I replaced all of these dotted line cell borders with the solid line cell border (and colored it a light gray to keep it subtle) the print speed was back to the normal 3 seconds on the HP printer. My daily reports template used this same dotted line cell border, so once I made this change to the template everything was good. This may not solve your specific issue but it did for me.
