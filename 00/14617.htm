Hi Mohamed,

 

The Outlook for Mobile application supports shared calendars for Office365 and Outlook.com users.

 

If you already have a shared calendar, you’ll need to upgrade it so that it syncs to your mobile phone. It’s simple to do though:

a. Ask the calendar owner to re-share the calendar to you.

b. Click the Accept button in the Outlook app.

c. After accepting, you’ll be able to see the shared calendar on your mobile phone.

 

Note: This feature is only available if both the calendar owner & the sharing recipient are hosted in Office365 or Outlook.com.

Hello! I want that every time I type a given word, the MS Word automatically turns it into italic for me. Is it possible? Maybe with a macro?


Type the word, format it as Italic and then select it and then click on the Office Button and then on Word Options and then on Proofing and then on AutoCorrect Options and with the AutoCorrect tab, the word that you type should appear in the Replace section.  Click on the Formatted text radio button (and the word in italics should appear in the With section) then click on Add.  Then click on OK and exit from the Word Options facility.

I suspect what you are running into is that underline and bold in Word are toggles. They are turned off and on rather than being something separate. You may be pasting from something that has underlining and bold in the formatting and it is turned off by direct formatting. It is also possible that this is the formatting for the style of the spot in the document into which you are pasting and it has been turned off by direct formatting. (Confused? I am.)

You can see what formatting has been applied and how using Shift-F1 and checking both boxes at the bottom of the pane. Click on the word in question in both locations.

The more you use styles to do your formatting rather than direct formatting, the less you'll run into problems like this.
