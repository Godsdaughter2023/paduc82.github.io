a. user say:

If someone has physical possession of your computer, they can't be stopped from accessing the information on your hard drive. It's only a matter of time.

The only protection you have against that is full disk encryption, but that won't do you any good if your computer is swiped at a time when you are using it (so the encryption is off.) Moreover, full disk encryption has certain disadvantages: It will take longer for your computer to start because it has to be decrypted. And it involves changing the metadata of your hard drive, which will make lots of things more complicated, like backing up, and will be another possible point of failure (from which you may not be able to recover.) Full disk encryption as a routine precaution only makes sense if you're carrying around state secrets with you.

Ultimately, you can limit, but not eliminate, the risks involved with a stolen laptop. For example: Don't store sensitive personal data on a computer that leaves the house, or store it in the cloud where it's protected by a password.
