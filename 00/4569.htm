Status code: 550 5.1.10

This error occurs because the sender sent a message to an email address hosted by Office 365 but the address is incorrect or doesn't exist at the destination domain. The error is reported by the recipient domain's email server, but most often it must be fixed by the person who sent the message. If the steps in the How to Fix It section above don't fix the problem, and you're the email admin for the recipient, try one or more of the following:

The email address exists and is correct - Confirm that the recipient address exists, is correct, and is accepting messages.

Can't add to dictionary a word marked as mistake. Why/how? - Microsoft Community

giulio. say:

Can't add to dictionary a word marked as mistake. Why/how?

For example, I write a name or a word that the program marks as a mistake, right click on it, and the option "add" is grey and I can't select it. What do I have to do?
Thank you
john mcghie say:
For example, I write a name or a word that the program marks as a mistake, right click on it, and the option "add" is grey and I can't select it. What do I have to do?
Thank you
You need to mark the text of your document with the same language as one of your custom dictionaries.

Usually Word has only one custom dictionary, but it can have as many as you wish to create.

Normally, we advise you to have only one custom dictionary, and set its language to "no language" so that it can accept words from text marked in any language.

Your problem is either you have set the custom dictionary to have a language, and your text is not in that language, or the text you are working on is in a language you do not have installed.

Select a word or two in the text and use Tools>Language to check which language it is in.  If it's in a language you don't have, Word can't add it to the dictionary so it greys out the command.

Hope this helps
