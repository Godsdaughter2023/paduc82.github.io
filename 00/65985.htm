franzmcb say: Failing a response from Microsoft, I did the proactive thing and mucked about until something worked :P.

Solution:

1. Go into Control Pannel -> Hardware and Sound -> Manage audio devices.

2. Right click on Sound Device in question (in this case, my sound card, E-MU 1820m) and select "Properties" from the drop-down.

3. Under the "Advanced" tab, in the Default Format my sample rate had somehow been set to 48000 Hz: a sample rate not supported by my sound card.

4. Change the sample rate to 44100 Hz (a supported sample rate) and audio starts working immediately.

The whole rerouting of audio was a bit of a red herring. I thought that the signal was being sent the the wrong place by Windows, when really Windows [i]couldn't[/i] send the signal to my card (due to conflicting rates) and was therefore automatically selecting devices which would support the audio.

Glad I resolved that one.
