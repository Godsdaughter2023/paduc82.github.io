Deleting an old network ID - Microsoft Community

profecks say:

Deleting an old network ID

When logging into Windows, I have to manually add my network's password (sometimes more than once) in order to connect to my home network.  Windows will not allow me to permanently delete and older and no longer used network even though the modem has been replaced.    How do I get Windows to override the old network and automatically pick up my new carrier's modem??

lemp say:

Open the Network and Sharing Center and click the link at the left to "Manage wireless networks."

You can delete old network profiles and/or move your desired network up in the order.

https://www.groovypost.com/howto/microsoft/manage-your-wireless-guide-wifi-networks-in-windows-7/

profecks say: Thank you for reply.  I regret that i have done exactly what you suggest (many times).  The old network disappears but only reappears shortly thereafter. The old network was associated with a Netgear modem (it’s long been discarded).  Any other ideas greatly apprecuated. 

lemp say:

If the old network "reappears" in the list at the "Manage wireless networks" window, you probably have a third-party wireless management utility running.  See, for example:  the answer from "harpreets" here:  https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/windows_7-networking/windows-7-wireless-networking-keeps-returning/621d4c06-8185-4e10-9ecf-50d02aa4d141 

Take a look at all of the icons in your System Notification area (next to the time display).  Be sure to click the up arrow to show the icons that normally are hidden.  Hover your mouse over each icon.  Is there one that seems related to networking?

You can read through some of the other posts in that thread, but I suggest that you not attempt to edit your registry -- at least not without make a System Restore point and/or registry backup first.
