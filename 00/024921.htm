ronbarker say:

Lola

In 2013 or thereabouts Microsoft came up with a complete new approach to the way it wanted to do business.  In short it wanted to become an online service rather than a provider of software.  To this end it provided Office 365 which is a paid service.  Not to upset too many people all at once it also provided various version of Office 2013/2016.  Office 2013/2016 is completely different from previous versions of Office software.  It is only in very few cases can you purchase Office 2013/016 on disk.

What you purchase for Office 2013/2016 is a Product Key, it really should be called a Redemption Key because it is not actually the Office Product Key.  You can purchase the 25 character Redemption Key online or from a store.  With the Redemption Key you go online to: https://setup.office.com/where you must use the email address and password for a Microsoft Account as well as inputting the 25 character key.  Office downloads onto the computer.  That is it.  In most cases activation is automatic.  You can use office  to do whatever you desire.

However, that is not really all that has happened.  During the setup/installation process a Microsoft Account is created so that the office on the computer is associated/tied with/to the Office Account.  You can find the Office Account by signing in with the same email address and password that was used to setup Office for the first time.  You can sign into the Office Account at: https://account.microsoft.com/services/ From you Office Account you can reinstall Office as many times as you desire.  Also, you will find there the actual Product Key for the installed Office.

You can do a quick repair of Office on Window 10 by>Right click on the Start Button>Apps and Features>Click on Microsoft Office 2016 in the list>Chose Modify>Choose Quick Repair.  DO NOT choose Online repair unless you have the email address and password for the Office Account because an online repair will reinstall a fresh version of Office and it will require you to sign in with the email address and password for the Office Account.
