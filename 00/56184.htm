shumi_is_back say:
Follow these steps to troubleshoot the microphone:

I)
1. Click on Windows icon on the bottom left of your screen and type sound.
2. Now click on ‘Sound’ which is under ‘Control Panel’.
3. Click on ‘Recording’ tab, Select ‘Microphone’ and then click ‘Properties’.
4. Now click on ‘Levels’ tab and then increase the default Microphone Boost.

II)
1) At start up go into your bios and disable your audio.
2) Delete the audio drivers
3) Reboot
4) At start up go into the bios and enable the audio.
5) When windows detects the hardware, do not install the driver through the windows detection system.
6) Download and install drivers from your sound card manufacturer.
7) Now go into the setup for the mic it should now be up and running.
