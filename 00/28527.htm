teylyn say:

Hello,

the ranges in the Sumif formula must be the same number of rows. Also, the sum range can only be one column. So, maybe

=SUMIF(A2:A100,"Night",C2:C100)

in words: Sum C2 to C100 for all rows where A2 to A100 has the word "Night". That will give you the total of 15 hours time at location.

To sum travel time, you need

=SUMIF(A2:A100,"Night",B2:B100)

Combine the two formulas to arrive at a a total for travel and babysitting

=SUMIF(A2:A100,"Night",C2:C100)+SUMIF(A2:A100,"Night",B2:B100)

It happens with ActiveWorkbook.save. Evidently the format of the file does not change even though the extension is changed as it does when you do a File > Saveas.  I made some changes in vba to isolate the file types as a work around; so far things are ok.

[CODE]
        extension = Mid(ActiveWorkbook.Name, InStrRev(ActiveWorkbook.Name, ".") + 1)
        ActiveWorkbook.SaveAs Filename:=ActiveWorkbook.Path & "\Contacts SS_Backup." & extension
        ActiveWorkbook.SaveAs Filename:=ActiveWorkbook.Path & "\Contacts SS." & extension
[/CODE]
