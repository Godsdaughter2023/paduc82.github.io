Word formatting--smart quotes - Microsoft Community

cindy vr say:

Word formatting--smart quotes

In dialog, after a dash indicating the speaker was interrupted or stopped speaking abruptly how do I get smart quotes to curl in the right direction, i.e. close quote. The marks keep curling like open quote. I've tried the straight quote/smart quote options but nothing addresses this particular problem.
jay freedman say:

The algorithm that Word uses for changing straight quotes to smart (a.k.a. stupid) quotes doesn't recognize the dash as proper punctuation. However, you can force your choice by not giving it a straight quote to begin with. Instead, insert a closing quote directly.

You can do this the long way by clicking Insert > Symbol > More Symbols, selecting the closing quote character, and clicking the Insert button.

After you do that once, the character will be added at the beginning of the display on the dropdown of the Insert > Symbol button, where you can just click it to insert it.

An alternative is to look at the Special Characters tab of the dialog box, which shows that the default shortcut key for the double closing quote is Ctrl+',". This means that you press the Ctrl and single quote keys together, release them, and type the double quote key.

The best alternative is to click the Shortcut Key button in the dialog box, which opens another dialog box. There you can assign any shortcut you like to the closing double quote, instead of or in addition to the default shortcut.
