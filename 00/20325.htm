ken sheridan say:

So that particular block of code doesnt disable the filter further on, it just some how "toggles it off" in the Load?????

That is what would be expected.  The Filter property is persistent.  If you close a form, and reopen it its Filter property will be whatever was set previously.  Unless the forms's FilterOnLoad property is True its FilterOn property will be False, however, so the filter is not applied.

This is one reason why I am questioning your use of the OpenForm method when you know the form is already open, rather than simply moving focus to the form and setting the FilterOn property to False to turn off the filter.  Reopening an open form dosn't achieve anything which moving focus to the form and turning off the current filter does not.

Is there actually any cogent reason why the user needs to be told the form is open?  If not you can avoid a lot of unnecessary code by executing a single line:

    DoCmd.Close acForm, "Update Multiple Positions (Beta)-2"

If the form is open it will be closed, saving any uncommitted record; if it's not open no error will be raised.  So you don't need to check to see if it's open or inform the user of anything.  The routine will simply continue as if the form is not open, and the user will be unaware that the form has been closed behind the scenes.

A couple of general recommendations:

1.  With If….End If, Select Case…..End Case , For……Next etc constructs use indents in code to clarify the logical flow, e.g.

    If x=y Then
        a=b
    Else
        c=d
    End If

2.  Do not use spaces or special characters in object names.  Use CamelCase or represent a space by an underscore character, as you've done with Filter_Open_Position for instance.  I know Northwind and other sample files and templates regularly do this, but these all too often include more instances of bad practice than you can shake a stick at.

Windows Update installing drivers automatically

On behalf of the Windows Update team, here’s the latest information about the current state of the update process in the January Technical Preview build (9926).

 

In the January release of Windows 10 Technical Preview (build 9926) all optional updates which apply to a given machine will be automatically installed on that machine.  This is not the intended behavior and we are working on a fix now, which we hope to include in the next build.  Thanks for raising this issue & helping us to understand how painful it was.  It drove us to get the fix going immediately.  If this doesn’t address your concerns, please continue providing feedback via the Feedback app.

 

Also, even before the next Technical Preview build, if there are particular drivers that are causing problems with your machine, please post the information about them in this thread. We’ll try to get them re-targeted so they don’t keep getting re-installed on 9926.

 

Windows Insiders, keep your feedback coming about the overall Windows 10 update experiences.   In the Insider Hub app, you’ll find a post with more details.  The new (fixed) behavior on the next Technical Preview build will not show “Optional” updates in Windows Update, although Optional drivers will be applied in a few cases.  For example, Optional drivers will be installed automatically after a clean install, or after plugging in a new peripheral. Otherwise, when we, or the hardware vendor, believe that specific drivers are ready to go to all customers, they will be published as Important updates on WU for automatic distribution. While this may be the right behavior for most customers, we’re looking closely at feedback about maintaining certain driver versions intentionally (for example, gamers who have specifically optimized their driver installs for maximum performance).  Please do keep your feedback coming through the Feedback App.

Adding names from my contact list to form a group, then sending a - Microsoft Community

kathryn ss say:

Adding names from my contact list to form a group, then sending a group email???

How do I add people from  my contact list to form a new group?  I checked the people I wanted in the group but only the first name is accepted.  I'm missing something!  

a. user say:

Here is a complete set of How to manage groups :

 

How to go to People window in Outlook.com:
Open outlook.com > top left next to the logo Outlook, there is a down arrow > click the arrow > click People.

 
How to create a group :
Go to People window > toolbar at top, click Manage > click Manage groups > click the plus (+) sign, a new box will appear, enter a name for the new group > click Apply > a new group is created.

How to access a group and see the contacts within that group:
In the People window, near top left, click at the word " All v " > a drop down box will appear with a list of your groups > click the one you want > all the contacts within that selected group are now on the left side > scroll to see the whole list.

How to add contacts to the group you have created :

Still in People window > click a contact you want to be added to the group > if you have multiple contacts to add, click the box at top left of the contact > continue clicking the top left box of each contact you want to add to the group until you are done> at the toolbar, click Groups > check the box in front of the group name > click Apply > all the contacts you have selected are now in the group.

Note : make sure you click squarely at the check box. If you click outside the box, all previous boxes you have checked will be nullified, and you’ll have to start all over.

How to delete a contact from a group but keep it in the main contact list:

Go to People > click the contact you want to remove from a group > toolbar, click Groups > un-check the box in front of the group in question > click Apply when done.

How to Edit contacts within a group :

On the People window, top left, click the “ All v “ > you group is on the drop down list > click the group name > now you have all the contacts within that group listed > click one to view > to edit, click at the contact’s name ( not its check box ) > click Edit at the toolbar to do editing......
Note :
1. Edit option will not appear until you have selected a contact.
2. Edit option will not appear if you click at the contact's check box instead of the contact's name
