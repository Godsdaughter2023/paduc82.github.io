scottgem say:

They are called comboboxes. There are two basic ways of populating a combobox. Either a Value List or a ta table/query. A Value list is only for short, static lists of items. Like Months in a year. If the list is long and/or subject to change, you want to store the values in a lookup table.

A lookup table differs by how its used. Generally a lookup table has 2 fields; an Autonumber code field and a description field. For example:

tluStatus

StatusID (PK Autonumber)

Status

the table might look like this

StatusID Status
1 Not Started
2 Pending
3 In Process
4 Completed

Lets say you have a Projects application with a Projects table. In that table you have a field also named StatusID as a Foreign Key. So you build a combobox using tluStatus and store the StatusID from the selected status in that field. that's how they are related.

Actually, yes you can.  User management of licenses is one area that 365 really has improved things for users.

Using your university email, log in to the MyAccount page. Click on Install. - install and re-install Office 2016, Office 2013 and Office 365, or manage your Office 365 subscription, register Product Key

Effectively all Office licenses allow you to re-install on the same computer, even after limited system changes like “upgrading” the OS or installing a new HD. Some, not all Office licenses allow you to “transfer” the installation to a new computer.

To re-install Office in general you need 2 things. Your unique product key and the matching generic installation file for the appropriate year and bundle.

putting headers and footers in a book manuscript Word 2016 Mac - - Microsoft Community

katherinefbell say:

putting headers and footers in a book manuscript Word 2016 Mac - please help ASAP

I have my chapter titles formatted such that they start on a new page from a styles formatting instruction. I have not used manual section breaks. Is there any way to put in my page numbers starting with Ch. 1 and not starting with the Introduction and other front matter without using section breaks? Ch. 1 starts on the 27th page of the doc.

Also I want to put in headers with the title on the left hand page and the chapter titles on the right. Is this possible given that I do not have section breaks throughout but have used styles and Page Break Before to format the entire book with all of its chapters?

john mcghie say:

Hi Katherine:

Yes, to both. Work slowly and carefully through this: read the instructions a couple of times first, every detail is important.  It is easy to do this, but it's also easy to miss a step and get into a very confusing state!

  1. You need to add a RIGHT PAGE section break immediately before Chapter 1 (can be anywhere on the page before Chapter 1, but it must be before it, and of course, after the preceding text).
  2. Then click after the section break (in or after Chapter 1).
  3. Then double-click to open the footer.
  4. In the Header and Footer tab of the Ribbon, turn the Link to Previous button off (this button will not appear until you add the section break above).
  5. Now click the Page Number button.
  6. Choose Page Number (in the dialog).  If you are in the Footer, the Position field will be greyed out: if it isn't greyed out, you are not yet in the footer, and this won't work until you are).
  7. In Alignment, choose Outside.
  8. Enable Show number on first page.
  9. Click the Format button.
  10. Enable the Start At checkbox, and add the number "1" to start your page numbering at 1 on the first page of Chapter 1.
  11. OK your way out.

You will now see page numbers showing left and right starting with the first page of Chapter 1. If page numbers appear in the front matter, you forgot to do Step 4: leaving Link to Previous on causes whatever you do in this footer to appear in the previous footer.

Now to do the running headers:

  1. Ensure that your book title is formatted with a style, and you know the name of that style.  There is a built-in style named Title designed for this purpose: customise its formatting to your taste.
  2. In Chapter 1, double-click the Header to put your cursor in the Header.
  3. Turn OFF Link to Previous (to stop the headers appearing in the front matter).
  4. In the Header and Footer tab that appears, ensure that both "Different First Page", and "Different Odd and Even" are turned on. The first enables you to suppress the header above the Chapter title on the first page of each section.  The second sets you up for left and right headers.
  5. Currently you are on the First Page header of Chapter 1.  Click the the Next button to get to the Left page header (you do not put a header above the Chapter title, it looks silly).  If you have the bug I have, the Next button will do nothing: place your cursor in the first left page, manually!
  6. Now click the Field button in the Header and Footer tab.
  7. In the Categories column, choose Links and References.
  8. In the Field Names column, choose the bottom item, StyleRef.
  9. De-select "Preserve formatting during updates".
  10. Click the Options button.
  11. Do nothing in the Options tab.
  12. Choose the Styles tab.
  13. Select the style you used for your book title.  E.g. Title.
  14. OK your way out.
  15. Click in the Right Page header.
  16. Repeat steps 6 to 14, but this time choose Heading 1 as your style (assuming you used Heading 1 for the Chapter title).

There you go, told you it was easy!  One last thing: we need to force the first page of each chapter onto a right-hand page.

  1. To do this, add a RIGHT PAGE section break immediately before the first paragraph of each chapter.
  2. Now:  Critical: Chapter 2 is different.
  3. Go to Chapter 2, add your section break.
  4. Then turn ON Link to Previous for the First, Left, and Right headers.
  5. This copies the headers you set up into Chapter 2.
  6. Do NOT touch its headers or footers, just insert the break.
  7. Now, go to each Chapter and insert the RIGHT PAGE section break immediately before it.
  8. This time, leave the Link to Previous buttons alone.  Don't touch the headers and footers, you have done them, just insert the break.

Note: It really is important to get your headers and footers right before you insert these inter-chapter section breaks, so each section break becomes a copy of the one you did for Chapter 1.

That's it!  You do not need to do anything else.  The StyleRef field will automatically copy the correct Chapter Title to each right-hand page except the first in each chapter. It picks it up from the most recent occurrence of the style you nominated. So it will work from Chapter 1 all the way to the back of the book.

And if you have used Heading 1 at any place within a chapter, you will now see why that was a bad idea! :-)  If there are any, find them, and use the correct style.  Heading 1 is for Chapter Titles.  Only!  :-)

Give yourself a pat on the back: most folks would regard this as very advanced Word usage.  You just joined the 1% who know how to do it!

Get back to us if you need more detail or whatever!  (Note: If you have a follow-up question, post it to the group, not the person: that way, the first person who knows the answer will respond and you will get your answers sooner...)

Cheers
