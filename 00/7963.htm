The Office Starter edition and the Office 2010 Trial are two completely different packages.

The Starter edition does not have a time limit, and because it can be installed only by the computer vendor on a new computer, it does not have a product key for you to enter. You don't pay for it (other than having advertising along the side). However, there are features of the full version that are not present or are reduced in functionality.

The trial edition does have a time limit, after which you have to buy a license and enter its product key to convert it to the fully paid version. The trial, as long as it lasts, has all the features and functions.

Because you were able to use Office from 2010 until now, it must have been the Starter edition and not a trial of the full version. When the disk was formatted, the Starter edition could not be put back (unless the formatting was done by HP, and that would be at their discretion). It seems that you now have the trial -- so you need to talk to the person or company that did the formatting to find out whether they have the product key for Office.
