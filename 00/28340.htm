FK stands for Foreign Key. Tables are generally related from PK to FK. So yes you want to link by EmpID. Date is a reserved word in Access so shouldn't be used as an object name, so you change that to something like LogDate. You also need another table:

tblProjects
ProjectID (PK autonumber)
ProjectName

You should then change Project in tblTimeLog to ProjectID as a FK and join those 2 tables.

Your next step is to build a form bound to tblTimeLog. Use comboboxes so you can select the Employee and Project.

You mentioned on your post that you renamed your account twice. As for renaming an account, once you did it, the old account will automatically be an alias to your new account. If you want to get back your @outlook.com email address as a regular account, you need to remove that as an alias first. Please be informed that the recently removed alias would become unavailable for registration for 30days. Only after the 30-day waiting period that the account name could be registered as new Windows Live ID.

1.  Sign in to your Microsoft account using this link: https://account.live.com/ManageAssocIds

2.  Under “Other email addresses” click the Remove link below your previous address.

3. Click the Remove button to confirm the message.

4. You should now see a message on the page stating, “You've removed an email address from your Microsoft account.”

5. Select the Save button.

 

You can also visit this link for more information about it.
