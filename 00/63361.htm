You didn't give a budget, but yes you would do better to build or buy a new PC . Most games will run on dual core

cpu's, but as time goes by quad core are being used more effectively and a few games are already restricted to quad core.

Equally, games are starting to use more RAM and 4GB can already be a limitation with some.

The 375W power supply ( CMT model ) is just adequate for the low end of the gaming grade cards, but it is

very old and may not be able to produce steady current under full load of gaming, which can cause crashes

and lock-ups ( USDT model = 135W & SFF = 240W )

OEM builders are notorious for using cheap psu's on business machines such as your HP.

- If I were going for new pre-built machine I would go with a reputable on line seller such as Newegg

(or similar in other countries) over HP, etc.  You get better performance for your money.

- That's a starter. Now go forth and research. There are heaps of graphics card, and should you decide to go

for a new machine, component reviews to read. And browsing sites such as Newegg helps guide toward the

hardware that suits your budget.
