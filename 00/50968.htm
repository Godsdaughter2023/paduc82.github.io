jack mvp say:

Hi

From what I read, it seems to me that you are connected directly to a DSL Modem.

While it is working, it is a bad choice.

1. It is not very secured, your computer is directly connected to the Internet with no external protection which make it easy target for hackers..

2. It needs PPPOE software authentication done and supported by the Computer, which provides mediocre (and at times unstable) Internet performance.

The solution is to use Cable/DSL Router even if you are using only one computer and No Network.

This page will tell you a little more about the issue.

http://www.ezlan.net/PPPOE.html

Currently your User ID and password are embedded in your old computer so it can connect to the Internet.

Depending on how it was set 6 years ago, and the software used by the ISP. Itimight be that the ID/PW cannot be retrieved at all from the computer, or they might be retrieved by someone that is familiar in the way that your DSL ISP used to configure the connection 6 years ago, and he/she is sitting in front of your computer.

My recommendations.

Get yourself a Cable/DSL Router. Call you ISP and ask them to reset your account. They will give you the ID a new password. Write it down .

Configure the with it the WAN side of the Router, and your DSL connection will not be anymore computer dependent.

P.S. I hope that you understand that it is Not Win 7 fault that you forgot your ID and password.
