I actually tracked down and found the keys and solution shortly after posting this.

I searched the registry for the phrase "DisableHardware" to see if there was anything I could find. There was 4 matches on that phrase, I discarded 2 of them since they were notification entries (i.e. "DisableHardwareNotification"). I then set the dword value to 0 on them and opened PowerPoint. I found that PowerPoint reads these keys on open, so you can make the changes without having to reboot the machine. I did double check to make sure thigns stayed in place by rebooting a couple of times after changing the keys back and forth though for thouroughness.

here are the keys I ended up changing, I jsut edited the dword value to "0" to ENABLE and "1" to DISABLE

HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Office\14.0\Gfx\DisableHardware

HKEY_USERS\S-1-5-21-165822833-1632583300-1373009395-208910\Software\Microsoft\Office\14.0\Gfx\DisableHardware

ossiemac say:

I am assuming that the data has been imported into Excel and that the middle initial is in a column of its own. Is this correct?

If so, then firstly remove the period from from where ever they exist. To do this select the middle initial column and using Find/Replace enter a period in the "Find what" field and leave the "Replace with" field blank and Replace all. Now all of the periods have been removed.

Assuming that the middle initial is in column B, insert a column beside the middle inital field and on row 2 (Assuming column headers in row1) enter the following formula to concatenate the initial and a period.

=B2&"."

Copy the formula down and now you have the period on all records. Select the column and Copy -> Paste special -> Values and then delete the original column.
