a. user say:

I solved to problem in following the way described in forum - https://social.technet.microsoft.com/Forums/ie/en-US/c9673cdc-0fa9-4126-9acc-b9ec42222ae3/after-windows-10-fall-update-1709-outlook-search-error?forum=outlook

The steps:

1. Cached Exchange mode (already had)

2. Go to Indexing Options, Modify. Remove Outlook from items being searched. DO NOT reindex yet.

3.Go to Add or Remove Programs. Select Microsoft Office, Change. Then select REPAIR option.

4. Reboot.

5. Use SCANPST.EXE (often found in "C:\Program Files (x86)\Microsoft Office\Office14\SCANPST.EXE").
Scan and repair every OST and every PST used by any of your profiles.

6. Reboot.

7. Go to Indexing Options and pause indexing. It often takes a little time for the Indexer to actually pause.

8. Open the Control Panel/Administrative tools/SERVICES. Find the Windows Search service and restart it. Indexing Options counter will indicate that Search has restarted indexing counter.

9. Go to Indexing Options, Advanced. Click on REBUILD. Let it run to completion (you will get an Items Indexed count and it will tell you when it is completed).

10. Start Outlook, and let your OSTs synch (i.e. wait until Outlook says it has downloaded your mail and such and says all folders are up to date).

11. Go to the Outlook Search Ribbon, and click on Search Tools/Locations to Search. Make sure all of the locations you want indexed are checked. It’s a good sign if they are already, but not necessarily a bad one if they aren’t.

12. Go to the Outlook Search Ribbon, and click on Search Tools/Indexing status to to ake sure indexing process is done.

13. Test the email search speed :)

By reading these forums and performing some checks on https://testconnectivity.microsoft.com/ and rebuilding mij database for outlook, I figured something out. (The connectivity seems to be allright and rebuilding had no effect.)

The sync process in Outlook works in a certain way, I read on the forums. In that process some folders have more weight than others and will be synced more often or not. In the end al folders and subfolders must be synced. (forgive me if this is not 100% accurate)
With that in mind I asked myself: could I have a folder or subfolder that is very large or has a massive amount of messages? in other words: a folder that could cause trouble? Turns out I had such a folder, with 12K unread messages and 34K messages in total. (it is a reporting tool that reports everything in our systems that happens). Messages like that go through a rule in my outlook, directly to a subfolder. I deleted the contents of this folder. (it took some time). I also logged in to Outlook Web App (closed Outlook on Mac) and made sure also all of these messages where deleted there, if still visible. I also did this for my Trash folder after that. 

After that the sync performed way better and only gave me 1 error in the last 48 hours. (where before I had this error 5 to 6 times per hour and no incoming or outgoing mail)

Maybe this helps for some of you having the 17886 error on Mac.
