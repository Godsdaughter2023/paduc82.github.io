Excel formula to match word in cell to similar header titles and copy column data into another worksheet.

I have a very large list of data contained in one worksheet called "data", the columns are titled 2002 profit, 2002 debt, 2002 capital, etc. and then it goes on for each year 2003 profit, 2003 debt, 2003 capital, etc. I would like to enter the word "profit" into cell a1 of worksheet called "analyse"and all columns of data containing the word profit woud be pulled into the worksheet. i.e. 2002 profit, 2003 profit, 2004 profit, etc. columns would appear, if the word "capital" was typed then 2002 captital, 2003, captital, 2004 captital columns would be pulled into the worksheet. Is there cell formulas that would work for this? the challenge is that i would like to avoid macro's or VBA. 
tom-s say:

BargeBerger,

Say on the data sheet you have the column headers 2002 profit, 2002 debt, 2002 capital, 2003 profit, 2003 debt, ..., 2004 capital in cells A1:I1, with the relevant data below.

Then on the analyse sheet in cell A1 you enter a year, while in cells B1:D1 you have the column headers profit, debt, capital. Then in cell B2 have this formula, and drag-fill across and down:

=INDEX(data!$A2:$I2,MATCH($A$1&" "&B$1,data!$A$1:$I$1,0))

 

Adjust the ranges to suit how many columns you really have on the data sheet.

j.e. mcgimpsey say: Hmmm...  In all the charts I used at sea, I seem to remember degrees-minutes-seconds...

Nevertheless, there's no way to do this using formatting - 'm' is already doing double-duty with minutes and months, depending on context.

You can use a formula to display it in another cell (or a similar UDF to return a text value):

   =TEXT(TRUNC(A1), "0") & CHAR(176) & TEXT((ABS(A1) - INT(ABS(A1)))*60, " 00.0' ")
