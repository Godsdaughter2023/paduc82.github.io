Move Rule for Invitations "Received for" Outlook 2016 - - Microsoft Community

thatadmintho say:

Move Rule for Invitations "Received for" Outlook 2016 - SOLVED!

I have an issue that I can find somewhat of a solution to for the previous versions, but it is not compatible with the 2016 Outlook version.

I work for several executives for whom I receive calendar invitations as I manage their calendars full-time. I need to receive these in my inbox, and I do. I ALSO cover a coworker's executives when she is out of the office (and vice versa). A few weeks a year, I need to receive her execs' invitations, but the remainder of the time, I do not need them. SO, I have created a folder for these invitations to be dumped in. I cannot figure out how to redirect the incoming invitations to this folder. I'm currently moving them manually. To illustrate how insane this is, I received 50+ email invitations yesterday alone that I don't need to see. But, we don't want to ask the execs to change their permission settings 2x every time one of us takes a day off.

TL/DR: I need a rule that moves all invitations "Received for [specific exec]" to a folder. Help.

To Outlook: I have a list of issues I encounter with your software. Email me, or any other admin. We know this program and what it needs.

thatadmintho say:

With our combined efforts, the new rule works and my problem is solved! Thank you for your time and effort and I hope this is useful for others as well.

I have pasted the step-by-step rule creation below:

1. Go to Microsoft Outlook Inbox Home.

2. Click on the "Rules" dropdown on the top ribbon in the middle, in the "Move" box.

3. Click "Create Rule."

4. Click "Advanced Options" in the lower right of the pop-up box.

5. Check the box next to "specific words in message header."

6. Click on "specific words" in "Step 2" box on the bottom.

7. Into top "Specify a word or phrase…" box, type the following language inside the quotation marks (case sensitive, add the email address of the person whose invitations you do not want to show up in your Inbox where you see "*** Email address is removed for privacy ***"):

"X-MS-Exchange-Inbox-Rules-Loop: *** Email address is removed for privacy ***"

NOTE: Must be exact address, including capital/lower-case letters. I had to create 3 rules for each of my leaders: *** Email address is removed for privacy ***, *** Email address is removed for privacy ***, and *** Email address is removed for privacy *** to catch all of the invitations.

8. Click "Add" or hit enter after typing each rule into the box. This will move the rules to the "Search list" box.

9. When you have typed the exact language and the email addresses for each of the people whose invitations you want to redirect and have added them to the "Search list" box, click "OK" on the bottom right, and the pop-up will close.

10. Click "Next."

11. In the next set of options, choose whichever choice (or choices) is appropriate to meet your needs. I use "move it to specified folder" and "mark as read."

12. If you "move it to specified folder," you have to click on "specified folder" in "Step 2" box on the bottom and choose a folder from your folders list. (I have a "Jane Cover" folder I use to move them to.) Click "OK."

13. Click "Next."

14. Check any appropriate boxes on this step to meet your exception needs.

15. Click "Next."

16. If you want to apply this rule to your Inbox right away, check the box next to "Run this rule now…." Click "Finish," then "OK" on the pop-up. (This is a great way to watch the invitations disappear from your inbox, and make sure it worked.)

17. All of the invitations that come in for the addresses you specified will now automatically move to the folder you specified. Magic!
