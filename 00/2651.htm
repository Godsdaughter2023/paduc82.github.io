doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

With the exception of the first word in a document, you can do it with a couple of Wildcard replaces.  One that looks for

(^13)([a-z])

which will find the first word in a paragraph  that starts with a lower case letter and the other that looks for

(. )([a-z])

which will find the first word of sentence (that is not the first word of a paragraph) that starts with a lower case letter, with both of them replacing the found text with

\1\2

with the Font Format for the replacement set to All Caps.

You could record a macro of the two replace operations.
