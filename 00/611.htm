cpitchford say: eeep.. you will have stuff left over..

but you're probably ok..

The emmc stores installed apps and settings and application data..
So, imagine you installed blahapp..

blahapp is put on the emmc, but it is referenced by the main OS rootfs (hence you can see an icon for blahapp on the application menu)

If you reflash the OS, the phone no longer knows about blahapp.. the package manager won't show it as installed and you will no longer have an icon to launch it..

But..

all its files are still on the emmc.. why would they be removed.. you only flashed the OS!!

So.. they might be stagnent and eating space, but it probably won't cause you problems if you haven't already seen them..

It's when people flash the os and there are broken files on the emmc that the os tries to use.. this causes reboot loops and stuff.. all very bad.. but as you're going, you're probably ok...

I'd recommend flashing the emmc to anyone looking to flash their phone, though.. just incase it seems to "brick" it..
