stefan blom say:

What I see is what Doug's screen shots illustrate: either Excel gridlines (which do not print in Word but display as Word gridlines) or Excel borders (which do print in Word, converted to "true" Word borders).

In Excel, there is an option to print the gridlines (without adding "true" borders). As far as I can tell, even if you keep that option selected, there is no way to get the gridlines to convert into (printable) borders after pasting the range into Word.

Are you sure that you haven't had text boundaries displayed in Word before? In the Word Options dialog box, click Advanced; you'll find the option under "Show document content."
