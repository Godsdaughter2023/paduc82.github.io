hover cat dave say:

Getting DHCPNACK for IP on other router

I don't know why the router here at 192.168.0.1 is booting me for an address lease of 192.168.1.7 when they're different routers... isn't it supposed to automatically assign me a .0 lease or use the .0.11 lease? I'm confused.
wunders say:

The only problem here is that Windows has declared this message as an Error instead of "Informational". 

 

When you turn on your machine, it will always send a message to the DHCP server that basically says:  "Hey, Server:  I need an IP address ... and, by the way, my last IP address was 192.168.1.7 and I would prefer that address if possible".  The DHCP server receives that message and evaluates the request and, in your case, decides that this IP address is on the wrong subnet and therefore your request to keep this IP address must be denied.  So it sends a denial message back so that your computer does not use that address and it sends a new, valid, address on the correct subnet back to you for you to use.

 

All this is normal DHCP operating procedure and absolutely nothing is wrong except that the status in the System Logs shows as "Error" instead of "Informational".

 

HTH,

   JW

1) Swipe from the left side to show the Windows Charms (or Windows Key + C)

c) Now you should be able to go back to the Windows Store and sign-in with your new email address.

 

After your new email address is accepted by Windows Store you can go back and change Windows to a local account.

Uninstalling a windows 8 printer driver - Microsoft Community

lyn8 say:

Uninstalling a windows 8 printer driver

How do I uninstall a Windows 8 printer?


rajesh govind say:

 

Hi Lyn,

 

We can uninstall the printer drivers from the Device Manager Utility in Windows 8. To do this, you may follow these steps:

 

a)       Press “Windows Logo” + “X” keys on the keyboard.

b)       Click on “Device Manager” from that list.

c)       Search for the “Printer” device from the device list, right click on it and then select “Uninstall”. On the un-installation window, if you have an option: “Delete the driver software for this device” you may select that and then remove its drivers from the computer.

d)       Follow the on-screen instructions to complete it and the restart the computer if prompted.

 

You may also uninstall any related software provided by the printer manufacturer from the computer from “Programs and Features”. For this, you may follow these steps:

 

a)       Press “Windows Logo” + “X” keys on the keyboard and select “Programs and Features” from that list.

b)       Search for any software provided by the printer manufacturer in the program list and if available, you may select it and click on “Uninstall” to remove it from the computer.

c)       Follow the on-screen instructions to complete it and the restart the computer if prompted.

 

Hope this information is helpful. You may post all your queries related to Windows on Microsoft Community Forums and we will be happy to help you.

how do i get the audio through my tv while watching a video with media - Microsoft Community

ashleyhartlen say:

how do i get the audio through my tv while watching a video with media centre

so i attached the VGA cord from PC to TV and i get the picture and videos perfect buti only get audio from my laptop. how do i get the audio through my TV?

hairyfool say:

If it is a VGA connector which normally has a blue core with 15 pins/holes then that connection method has no audio incorporated in the lead. You would have to use a separate audio connection.

Most TVs have a small 3.5mm, sometimes with a green ring, to feed audio into the TV when using that input. If so you need to connect the computer headphone/line out socket to the TV with a 3.5mm to 3.5mm lead.

Only some DVI and HDMI ports incorporate audio and even then it depends on specific hardware to do so.
