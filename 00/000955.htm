howieem say:

how do I convert a sub routine to a macro

I have a sub routine in visual basic.  I want to convert it into a macro where I can assign a control-letter  (Like Cntl-a) to this sub routine so that I can just highlight the cells that apply, and press Cntl-a to run the sub.  Any suggestions?
hansv mvp say:

A macro is a subroutine (Sub) in a standard module that has no arguments. So a subroutine that begins

 

Sub MySub()

 

is a macro, it should be listed in the Tools | Macro | Macros dialog. But a subroutine that begins

 

Sub MySubToo(n As Long)

 

is not a macro because it has an argument. It will not be listed in the Macros dialog. To make a subroutine / macro available in all your workbooks, store it in a module in your personal macro workbook Personal.xls.

 

You can assign a keyboard shortcut to a subroutine / macro in the Macros dialog: select the macro in the list and click Options...

        On Error GoTo jamun

             mypath = Range("B1").Value & "\" & stname
              Application.Workbooks.Add.SaveAs Filename:=mypath
              ActiveWorkbook.Close

            Range("B" & i).Value = mypath & "_assigning..."
            Application.Wait Now + TimeValue("00:00:02")
            Range("F" & i).Value = "Done"
            Range("B" & i).Value = mypath & "_assigned"
            Application.Wait Now + TimeValue("00:00:01")
            Range("B" & i).Select
            ActiveSheet.Hyperlinks.Add Anchor:=Selection, Address:="mypath", TextToDisplay:=Range("B" & i).Value
              Range("B" & i).Select
               Selection.Hyperlinks(1).Address = Range("B1").Value
                Next
              MsgBox "Test assigned successfully"
