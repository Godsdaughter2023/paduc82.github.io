Desktop wallpaper images have varying colors and it is easy for the font colors to be unreadable if they are on top of the same color as the wallpaper image. You can follow the steps to change your font:

  1. Right-click on your desktop.
  2. Select Personalize.
  3. Click Windows Color.
  4. Go to Advance Appearance Settings.
  5. Click the Item drop-down, select an item to customize.
  6. Change the font type, size, and style based on your preference.
  7. Click the Apply button, then click OK.

You may need to restart your computer in order for the changes to take place.

You can select a specific option on the Item drop-down depending on which part of your Windows 7 you would want to customize like the font on your Desktop. After selecting, you have to restart your PC for the changes to take effect.

 

If it’s only on your Outlook, you can follow the steps below to change the font size of the Outlook Message List:

 

  1. In Outlook 2003, choose View > Arrange By > Current View > Customize Current View.
  2. Click Other Settings.
  3. Click on Row Font.
  4. Choose the desired font, font style and size.
  5. Click OK.
  6. Optionally, change the font for the column headings as well by clicking Column Font.
  7. Click OK.
  8. Click OK again.
