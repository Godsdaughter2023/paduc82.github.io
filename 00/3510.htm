jay freedman say:

Most of the "preset" gradients are really just two-color gradients with preselected pairs of colors. There are a few, such as Rainbow, that do have more than two colors, but the Microsoft designers chose not to make that flexibility available to users. I don't know the reasoning behind that, as multi-color gradients are available elsewhere in the program (more on that below).

The story behind the disabled transparency controls is that the Page Color > Fill Effects dialog in Word 2010/2013/2016 is the same one that was used in Word 2007 for both page fill and shape fill effects. In that version, the transparency controls were enabled when you invoked the dialog for a shape (such as a text box, a rectangle, or a circle), but disabled when you invoked it for the page background. That's because the background is behind everything else, so transparency makes no sense for it -- there's nothing behind it to show through.

In Word 2010 and later versions, shapes have a different Fill Effects dialog (later a task pane) but the old dialog is still used for the page background. In the new dialog for shapes, you can add multiple colors, control where in the shape those colors take effect, set the transparency separately for each color, and more. 

Even better, this provides a workaround for a longstanding bug that occurs with some printers: If you use a gradient or picture in a page background and then print that on paper, the gradient is repeated every inch or so down the page; or the picture is made small and tiled all over the page. This doesn't appear in the Print Preview, only on the printer. The workaround is to insert a text box or rectangle shape while the cursor is in the header, make it large enough to cover the whole page, and apply the gradient or picture fill to the rectangle.
