Hi Raj,

Your symptom indicates that a Policy change has been made to your PC that

excludes the function of Windows Defender.

The change has probably been made by either the presence of "another"

antivirus, or by malware.

You can determine if this is your case, by observing a particular Key in your

PC's Registry. But first you must ensure that malware does not exist on the PC.

And that there is no "other" antivirus present. Even in fragmented form. If you

have uninstalled another antivirus, you must obtain a "removal" program from

its Web site and run that too. Windows Defender sees "fragments".

Also ensure no malware exists. Here are a couple of programs for that purpose.

http://www.eset.com/us/online-scanner/

https://www.malwarebytes.com/trial/

When you are confident that your PC is clean, enter the Registry using this procedure

Click "start" and type "regedit". Select "Run as administrator".

On the Registry Editor screen, navigate through the following branch.

Hkey_Local_Machine\Software\Policies\Microsoft and select the Windows

Defender folder.

Any key that is present in this folder other than "Default" is a policy change that

has been made by Malware, 3rd Party Antivirus, or by you.

Since you just eliminated the cause, you can now disable the policy, that

is causing your problem. Expect to find "DisableAntispyware" and/or

"DisableAntiVirus".

If you do you can disable both by modifying the value of their DWord from

1 to 0.   Do that by right clicking "Dword" and select "modify". Type 0 to

replace the highlighted 1.      Then click OK.

If there are "other" keys in the folder, you may need to delete the folder.

If that is the case, please return to this thread and we will discuss it.

It is best to back out of the Registry, by retracing your steps. This keeps 

the Registry from becoming cluttered. When you reach HKLM, click "File"

then "Exit".

Restart your PC and access Windows Defender.

If it is still not enabled, perform the following commands in Command

Prompt (admin).

Click "start" and type "cmd". Select "Run as Administrator. Type the 

following commands one at a time, hitting <enter> after each.

CD\     <enter>

CD Program Files\Windows Defender     <enter>

MpCmdRun -wdenable     <enter>

Exit     <enter>

Restart your PC and try Windows Defender again.
