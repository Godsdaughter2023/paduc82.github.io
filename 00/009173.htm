ken sheridan say: With personal names it is better to represent each literal quotes character by a pair of contiguous quotes characters, as personal names can include apostrophes:

chgreqrec![StartTime] = DLookup("StartTime", " tblSchedules", "[Employee]=""" & Me.Employee & """ AND [Day]=""" & Me.txtWeekDay & """")

However, personal names can legitimately be duplicated and consequently should not be used as a key.  The tblSchedules table should include a numeric EmployeeID column as a foreign key referencing the primary key of an Employees table.  In a form bound to the tblSchedules table the control bound to the EmployeeID column would then be a combo box set up as follows:

ControlSource:   EmployeeID

RowSource:     SELECT EmployeeID, FirstName & " " & LastName FROM Employees ORDER BY LastName, FirstName;

BoundColumn:   1
ColumnCount:    2
ColumnWidths:  0cm

If your units of measurement are imperial rather than metric Access will automatically convert the unit of the last one to inches.  The important thing is that the dimension is zero to hide the first column.

Alternatively, you can concatenate the names so that the last name is first, which would be better with a large list of names as it allows the user to enter the initial characters of the last name and progressively go to the first match as each character is entered:

RowSource:     SELECT EmployeeID, LastName & ", " & FirstName FROM Employees ORDER BY LastName, FirstName;

Hi Peter–K

Skype is one of a range of consumer services that is accessed through a Microsoft account. It is not possible to separate your Skype profile from your Microsoft account.

f you close your Microsoft account, in addition to not being able to sign in to or access Skype, you'll no longer be able to sign in to other Microsoft consumer products or services such as Outlook.com, Office Online, OneDrive, Xbox Live, or Windows.

You can close your Microsoft account by following these steps:
1. Go to our Microsoft account closure page. You may be prompted to sign in or verify your password.
2. Make sure you are signed in to the Microsoft account you want to close and select Next.
3. In the Select a reason drop-down list, choose the reason you're closing the account.
4. Select Mark account for closure.
5. Your Microsoft account will be closed in 60 days. We wait 60 days before permanently deleting accounts in case you change your mind or need to access something before it's gone. You can change your mind any time during the 60 days, just sign in to cancel the closure.
