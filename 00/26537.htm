Hi Corinne,

Since you have asked three questions, I will answer them one by one.

Q: How can I ensure that I do not lose any data.

A: Generally, the data won't lose when you transfer it, but in case this happens, we suggest you download the data to your local drive and check all the files after you finish the transfer.

Q: How can I transfer all the data produced to my new Office 365 account.

A: We’d like to inform you that you need to manually transfer the data to your new Office 365 account since the data is related to the current Office 365 account. For example, you can download all the files from your current account and then upload them to the new one. While for the notebooks on your Office 365 school account, you can follow the steps below to merge the section to your personal account.

1. Add both your school account and your private account on OneNote, open all the notebooks in your school account and your private account.

2. Right click on a section and select Merge into Another Section to merge it to the notebook stored on your private account. Repeat the action until all contents are copied to your notebook stored on your private account.

Q: How can I make sure the school I am at cannot access my data from the past.

A: After you transfer or save all the data to your new account, you can ask your school IT admin to delete your account.

I have used Microsoft products for 25 years (Beta tested Windows early NT products and Windows 95 even) and Apple now for 14 years.  My background is computer science and software development as well as management and UI/UX design.

I can guarantee that if these relatively simple efforts were taken that it would dramatically increase the flow back towards Microsoft.  There are some others as well, but these are the core and primary efforts that need to be taken.

 b. Click Mailings->Start Mail Merge->Normal Word Document. This disconnects the data source, any filters/sequences, any Mapping/Matching you may have had to do if you use ADDRESSBLOCK or GREETINGLINE fields, but leaves your merge fields intact.
 c. Save and close.
 d. Re-open
 e. Re-attach the data source in the usual way.
 f. Save and close
 g. re-open and see if that has fixed the problem.
