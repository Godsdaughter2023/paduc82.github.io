internet expert say:

The HDMI inputs on all Xbox One models only do up to 1080p, as they are HDMI 1.4. There are no planned upgrades (or updates) for the input port, and I’m not sure if it’s even possible.


I’d suggest plugging your cable box into a different HDMI input on your TV instead, if available.

jinxed violynne say:

HDMI and 4K aren't synonymous. HDMI is a technology to push bandwidth, while 4K is a resolution. The XBox One X uses HDMI 2.0, which is compatible with 4K resolution.


You might want to look at the settings of your cable box to ensure it's pushing native resolution received on the input. If the cable box is downscaling, it will push out to 1080p.


I'm not familiar with Xfinity products, so if there's no setting, you may need to contact Comcast to find out how their input/output signal push is handled. Some boxes will have multiple inputs, and one is usually dedicated to native signals.


Hope this helps.

lizette ags say:

Hello,

If the update gets stuck at a certain percentage, and the PC does not respond at all, there's a possibility that your Windows Update components are corrupted or your Internet connection is unstable/intermittent.

We advise that you download and run the troubleshooter for Windows Updates. The troubleshooter will reset the Windows Update components for you. After the troubleshooter finishes, try running Windows Update again and install any available updates.

We also suggest connecting to your network via wired setup (Ethernet) to avoid disruptions while updates are downloading and installing.

Let us know if the above solutions work for you, or if you need additional assistance.
