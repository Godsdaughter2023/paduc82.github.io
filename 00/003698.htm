Yes Dawn, you're correct that was just another scam, regardless what accent or part of the world it may have come from.  It's the story and supposed need to pay to fix it that's fake, not the people trying to con you.

The Federal Trade Commission explains these in general terms on their website as follows, while the stories themselves vary in thousands of ways in order to sound slightly different in the attempt to trick the unwary.

Tech Support Scams | Consumer Information

Your description had components of not only a support scam via phone, but also a refund scam also mentioned near the bottom of that same page where the scammer offers to refund money they've (supposedly) already gotten from you, which again is always a false promise.

Nothing anyone states over the phone can ever be trusted, since you can't verify any of it, so hanging up is always the correct thing to do.

Try this:

SELECT Table1.Material

  , [Material] Like '*-P*' AS IsCandidate

  , InStr([Material],'-P') AS TrimStart

  , Left$([Material],IIf(InStr([Material],'-P')>0,InStr([Material],'-P')-1,Len([Material]))) AS Result
FROM Table1;
