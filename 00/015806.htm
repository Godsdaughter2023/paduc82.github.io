Reistallation if the computer crashes - Microsoft Community

crdchaser say:

Reistallation if the computer crashes

If computer crashes with Office on it can and if your licenses have been used will microsoft give you permission to reinstall on your new computers? Or do you have to purchase new software
rohn007 say:

It depends, on several things.

Just because your computer has had a problem doesn't mean you have to replace your computer. In that case you don't HAVE to get a new license, but you may have to due to not having your Product Key. More on this in a minute.

OK, assuming replacing your computer is the right way to go (it's too old, parts too expensive, it's the wrong color ...), again you may not Have to get a new license.

Now for the details. MS sell 2 types of License. One is "portable", you are allowed to install it on another machine. The other is not, it is only good on the first machine you installed it on.

The non-portable licenses come several ways. You buy a computer and pay for a full license pre-installed. You buy your license online from MS or other online store. You go to a store and buy a "Product Key Card" type of license.

The easiest way to identify the Portable license is you bought it in a plastic box with an installation CD. There are some other portable license but you don't need to worry about them because you can't buy one of them. Another feature of the portable type of license is that some, not all, versions of Office allow you to install using a single license on 2 or 3 machines.

OK, so now you've confirmed that your computer really had to be replaced, and that you had a "portable" type of license. Since you have a portable license, regardless of how many machines your specific license allows you to install concurrently (2 or 3), you are allowed to transfer your "first" license to a new machine by uninstalling it from the first machine or scrapping (which is what you are doing) the first machine.  In order to transfer the license to a new machine you need a 25 digit code called the "PRODUCT KEY". It is on the CD box or on your purchase receipt or purchase confirmation email.  If you do not have your product key, even though you qualify to transfer your license to a new machine you won't be able to.  Depending on how your old machine crashed, you MAY be able to extract the product key from the old HD. There is also a process where you can contact MS, and if you can prove your original purchase and license type to THEIR satisfaction they MAY re-issue the product key. They dont' make it easy.

To summarize: you may transfer your office license to a new machine if you have the right type of license. This type is normally identified by the installation CD you bought with it.

wyman w say:

A little explanation for you to understand how time works in Excel.

Excel regards 1900 Jan 1st as day 1, if you have time it will add decimal places behind it, you can go to Format Cells and change to "General" to display the time serial.

To make criteria on time, you need to extract the time only (the decimal part), to do that you can type

=G2-INT(G2)

Since 10 AM equals to 0.41666... (10/24), 12

12 PM equals to (12/24) 0.5

which means your criteria must be between 0.41666 to 0.5

so you basically add an additional column to extract the time part and then use normal filter to filter off the desired value.

   Dim f        As DAO.Field

  

   With CurrentDb.QueryDefs("query1")

      Next

And above means you don’t even via code open the query in design mode.
