Delete or deactivate microsoft account

My problem now is my onedrive backup is synchronized on her phone we now sharing a backup, i tried to remove my account from the phone and from ondrive web but the phone still getting updates from onedrive, everything i do on my lumia 830 its synchronized on her phone and vise versa. Anyone who can assist please. 

julie fka profjulie say:

Well you've got a few choices....you can:

You can turn off all the backup options on her phone so her data doesn't get intermingled with your.  If she ever needs to perform a hard reset of her phone, she'll have to set her phone up again from scratch since none of her data will be backed up to OneDrive and you wouldn't want to restore your data to her phone.  To turn off the backup options on her phone, go to Settings > backup (this will prevent her phone from backing up her text messages, apps, and photos to your Microsoft account and then her data will not show up on your phone and your text messages and photos won't show up on her phone).

But even if you turn off all the backup options on her phone, any of the contacts and appointments that are stored in your Microsoft account will still sync to her phone and any changes you or she makes to those items will sync to your Microsoft account and on to your and her phone (there's no way to turn off the syncing of contacts & appointments that are stored on a Microsoft account).

OR

Hard reset her phone and set it up with her own, unique Microsoft Account....but then you won't be able to share apps with her....she'll have to purchase her own apps.  [In my option, this is the best choice and what I recommend you do)  To perform a hard reset, go to Settings > about > tap the "reset your phone" button on the bottom of the screen.

nich zella pwah say: There's only one way to remove a Microsoft account associated with a Windows phone, you'll have to perform a hard reset.

Conditional Formatting for three duplicate items in a row? - Microsoft Community

aojoi say:

Conditional Formatting for three duplicate items in a row?

I am creating a spreadsheet to keep attendance of board members.  Each member has a column and each row is the date of the meeting.  If the member is in attendance they get a "1" in the cell.  If the member is absent it's a "0".  Members can be kicked off a board if they are absent 3 times in a row or they have a total of 4 absences all together.  Right now I have the spreadsheet set up to highlight any cell that has a "0" in it yellow.  I also have their "Total Meeting Attendance" cell set to change color if that number dips to more than 4 absences. 

What I would like to do is if there are three zeros in a row, those three cells will turn from yellow to bright red in order to draw attention to the fact that that board member was absent for three consecutive dates and needs to be removed from the board. 

My sheet is set up like this

                A                      B

        Bob Jones          Jim Smith

1               1                        1

2               1                        1

3               0                        0

4               1                        0

5               1                        0

6               0

7               0

8               1

9               1

10             0

So, every line that has a zero is filled yellow (Bob Jones could be removed since he has a total of 4 zeros).  Jim Smith has three zeros in a row, each cell is being filled yellow as a zero is entered.  However, now that he has three zeros in a row I would like it to be filled bright red in order to flag the fact that he should be removed.

hansv mvp say:

To make things easier, insert a row or rows so that the attendance 0/1 values start in row 3.

Select the range. I'll assume that A3 is the top left cell in the selection.

On the Home tab of the ribbon, click Conditional Formatting > New Rule...

Select 'Use a formula to determine which cell to format'.

Enter the formula

=OR(COUNTIF(A1:A3,0)=3,COUNTIF(A2:A4,0)=3,COUNTIF(A3:A5,0)=3)

Click Format...

Activate the Fill tab.

Select red.

Then click OK twice.
