Sorry to know about your experince. I can only imagine how this is causing issues with your personal relationship.

Regarding the status, are you logging in to different devices? Or is it possible that you've signed in to Skype for Web?

The only time that an account will show online status is when it is signed in to a device that is connected to the internet.

To prevent this from happening, alway smake sure to sign out from any devices you were logged in previously.

As for your name showing up on the directory, we have no control as to whom will search you on the Skype Directory. It is possible that the person who's trying to add you searched random people and your name came up. You can prevent this by disabling the discoverable option on your account settings. However, by doing so, only those who are on your contact list can search you in Skype.

Regarding the recent conversations, all messages that you see on your recent conversations are those that were either initiated from your account or from the other party.

As I mentioned earlier, you may have been logeed in on a different device and there's a possibility that the account was left signed in. It can be possible that it was someone else who were sending those messages and eventually deleted it to make sure there were no traces of the conversation.

To ensure that nobody else gets access to your account, it would be best to change your password. This will automatically log you out from any devices assuming that you were logged in on multiple devices.

vijay a. verma say:
Try following and check after each step (Note - None of the steps below will delete any data / picture. All your data / pictures will be preserved)

1. Run Windows Store Apps Troubleshooter
Type troubleshoot in Windows Start Search box > Click Troubleshoot > Scroll down to bottom and click Windows Store Apps > Click Run the Troubleshooter and follow on-screen directions.

2. Reset Photos App
Windows+X > Settings > Apps > Apps & Features > Locate Photos by Scrolling Down > Click Advanced Options > Click Reset
Go to Microsoft Store > Click 3 horizontal dots on top right > Downloads and Updates > Check if there is any update for this app > If yes, update it.

3. If Reset doesn't work, then you should uninstall using Powershell and reinstall the Photos App
Windows+X > Click Windows Powershell (Admin) > Copy the below command and right click in Powershell to paste it

get-appxpackage Microsoft.Windows.Photos | remove-appxpackage

Now, you need to reinstall it
Use below Powershell command to install

Get-AppxPackage -allusers Microsoft.Windows.Photos | Foreach {Add-AppxPackage -DisableDevelopmentMode -Register “$($_.InstallLocation)\AppXManifest.xml”}

4. Reset the Database of Photos App
Copy following and paste in Windows Start Search box
%LocalAppData%\Packages\
And now locate Microsoft.Windows.Photos_........folder
Make a copy of Microsoft.Windows.Photos_........folder (for the sake of backup) and then Delete everything from inside the folder

Source - https://answers.microsoft.com/thread/e8be2a12-f...
