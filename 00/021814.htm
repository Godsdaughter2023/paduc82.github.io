First- do not regularly deplete lithium batteries.  this reduces their lifetime, and can also affect the charge calibration.  Try to use them 'gently' like from 20 to 80%.  (The deplete all the way scenario is false... that disappeared with NiCd batteries years and years ago.)

Second, if it won't hold a charge at all it probably is a bad or dying battery.   However it could also be a calibration issue.  If it is you would observe a charge indicator saying it's got reasonable capacity left... and moments later it shuts down.  If you see this, you should recal.  To do this:  set it to play until it shuts down. restart and play again... and repeat until it won't stay on longer than a few minutes (5 or even less.)
Connect it to the charger, and let it charge until full WITHOUT any interruptions. 

Good luck!

I think I would do it with an invisible text box (named txtLineCount) in the report (or a group) header that calculates the number of records divided by 2:

   =IIf(Count(*) > 31, (Count(*) + 1) \ 2, 999)

Then add an invisible text box (named txtLineNum) to the deatail section.  Set its expression to =1 and RunningSum property to Over All (or Over Group).

Also add a page break control (named pgSkip) at the top of the detail section.

Finally, add a line of code to the detail section's Format event:

   Me.pgSkip.Visible = (txtLineNum = txtLineCount)

 

With this kind of approach, you do not have to make any changes to the report if/when you add or remove patch panels.
