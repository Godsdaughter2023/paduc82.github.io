There are several reasons that may cause your storage device to become corrupted. It might be due to a malware infection or by unplugging a device from your computer without properly ejecting it. For us to efficiently assist you, we would like to ask a few questions:

  • Are you getting any error messages when you're trying to plug the hard drive in your computer?

For the meantime, we suggest that you follow the steps provided below on how to repair a corrupted external hard drive:

  1. Press the Windows + X keys.
  2. Select the Command Prompt (Admin) option.
  3. Click Yes when you are presented with a User Account Control window requesting permission to launch the Command Prompt as Administrator.
  4. In the new Command Prompt window, type chkdsk E: /f /r /x.

In the last step, you should replace E with your drive letter. This option will run chkdsk to check and repair disk errors in Windows 10. drunkenacid say:

Hi Everyone,

Have you all tried to completely stop and cancel the installation and clear the cache on the Xbox? To clear the cache, press and hold the power button on the Xbox for about 10 seconds until it completely shuts down, wait about 10 seconds and turn it back on. This should clear anything that is remaining in the cache that could be causing an issue with the install. Once that is done, just to be on the safe side, I would check for any recent Xbox System updates as well. Once that is complete, then try to download the game again and see what happens. If it get stuck at 98% again, come back and let me know!
