mouneshwar r say:

Hi Ralph,

 

You probably might have a hidden drivers setup from a failed or corrupted install. You can use the article below to show the extra hardware profiles and remove them.

 

Step 1:Device Manager does not display devices that are not connected to the Windows XP-based computer

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/315539

Note: The steps are applicable to Windows XP, but you can still use it for Windows 7.

 

Step 2: Try the following things

a.    Make sure that you and your Internet service provider use the same 56K protocols. 56K requires that both ends have 56K modems, and they both have to use the same kind of 56K technology (x2, K56flex, or V.90). The setting on your windows 7 computer might be different. Despite what you may have heard, V.90 is not the same as x2 or K56flex. It is a third and distinct 56K protocol. Your ISP will be able to tell you which protocol they use.

b.    If there is a telephone, answering machine, etc., plugged into the back of the modem, unplug it from the back of the modem. The modem is supposed to ignore devices plugged into it when it is online, but that is not always the case.

c.    Try disconnecting additional telephony devices (fax, phones, answering machines, alarm systems, etc.) from the phone line,even if they're in a different room. I've heard from readers whose connect speed jumped 10K when they unplugged a fax machine or cordless phone in another room.

d.    Also try running the phone line directly from the back of the modem to the wall, without passing through surge suppressors, splitters, phone line extenders, etc. This is a basic and useful step in modem troubleshooting.

 

Hope this information is helpful and let me know if you need any further assistance.
