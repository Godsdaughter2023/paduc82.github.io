michaelatoz say: I have found a work-around.
Using Sysinternals ProcessMonitor I found that Windows was accessing the following Registry path;
HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Control\GraphicsDrivers\Configuration

My system had three entries;
DELF003YY7707BR0MUL_30_07D7_6A^9A3774EB79DEE3E3E38496CC7DF4D936
QHD32000001_31_07D6_D5^63E1ABDD175E7871DCAEB710418A0F75
SIMULATED_8086_2A42_00000000_00020000_1010100^CDE365D1B3F0942F0CF38BFB8E127AB4

Under each is a tree called "00" two of the keys are
PrimSurfSize.cx
PrimSurfSize.cy

Under "00" was another branch also called "00" two of the keys are;
ActiveSize.cx
ActiveSize.cy

The first two of the configs (ie DELF00... & QHD3...) the above keys were 1440x900,
so they were not involved.

The third (SIMULATED...) were set to 1024x768.

I changed these to 1600x900 and the problem was solved.

Further I changed resolution (via control panel) to 1920x1080, the moving/resize issue returned,
but the lower right corner was set to 1600x900, ie the SIMULATED... settings.

So for some reason one of the configs does not get set correctly.

I don't know why there are three configs, I have only ever used one monitor.

Hope this helps someone out there...

You'll see that I only quoted two sections of the log.

1. The first section shows a non-specific delay (in downloading at least two updates) caused by excessive demand on the servers ("Update is not allowed to download due to regulation"). This is NOT indicative of any sort of problem or issue on your end. See...

   • Updates are not downloaded or the “Automatic Updates” icon does not display the status of downloads when you enable the Automatic Updates service
   http://support.microsoft.com/default.aspx/kb/910340

Translation: The update servers are swamped. Automatic Updates will try again later.

2. That being said, the second section (3 minutes later than the first section) suggests that at least one (1) update
What's that all about, you ask? If some but not all of the (approved) updates have downloaded, you may encounter a "Click to install important updates and then shut down" button when you go to turn off your computer.  Doing so will install the already-downloaded updates but there's no way of knowing which updates they are, unfortunately. See...

   • Microsoft Update Product Team Blog: Update Notifications and Install-at-Shutdown Behavior 
   http://blogs.technet.com/mu/archive/2009/06/26/update-notifications-and-install-at-shutdown.aspx

In short, it would appear (at least) that Automatic Updates is working, despite what you may be seeing when you try to run Windows Update manually. Wait a few more days (till Sunday the 13th, at the very least) and see if Automatic Updates finally kicks into gear & installs the Dec-09 updates, after which everything may be OK when you try to check for updates manually.
