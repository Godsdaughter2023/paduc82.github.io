First thing is to be certain that your <email account> has been "hacked" versus your <email address> having been "hijacked".

If "hijacked" - just means that a spammer is "spoofing" your email address in the <From> portion of the spam messages to make it "appear" that the emails are coming from you. Not much you can do in this instance beyond just waiting until the spammer moves on to another "hijacked" email address which they always do in time.

If your account has been "hijacked" to send spam to others, you would see the messages in the <Sent Items> folder. At a minimum, you would need to change your password as you've done and suggest also enabling two-factor authentication.
