Setting up your e-mail (POP3) - Microsoft Community

jack cook - aka help_line say:

Setting up your e-mail (POP3)

So you have your first device and now you want to start “doing stuff”, like sending and receiving your email.  It is a pretty simple process and within a few moments you will be sending (and receiving) your emails with your device.

Most folks like to start using their email capabilities using their personal accounts first and then move on to setting up their corporate email accounts.  If this is your FIRST device then my advice is to “go slow”, do one thing at a time and once you’re comfortable, move on to the next item you want to accomplish.  So let’s go through the setup of a POP3 email account.

For the most accounts that you might have (i.e. Comcast, Verizon etc) the E-mail Setup Wizard that will appear is the best way to setup your account.  It is a painless process that works pretty well.  There are a few instances that it will NOT be able to detect your account settings but my experiences have been very good so I would expect it to be the same for you.  If the Auto Configuration does not work for you, obtain the settings for your email from your ISP and you will be able to enter them manually. So let’s get started!

Note:  The following steps are based upon my Pocket PC Phone which is running Windows Mobile 6.  Your device may be slightly different in the steps that you would follow depending on what OS it is using but the steps should be pretty much the same and easily recognizable on what you should do if there are differences.

1.       1.       Turn on your device and press Start.  Then scroll to Messaging.

2.       2.       Select Outlook E-mail > Menu > Options > New Account.

3.       3.       You may be asked if your email source is a corporate Exchange server or some other e-mail provider.  If your email provider is NOT on the list choose Other (for POP3).

4.       4.       Now a 5-step process will begin.  Step 1: Enter your e-mail address.

5.       5.       After pressing NEXT, Step 2 will begin an auto configuration process.  (It should last about 30 seconds or less.)  When it says COMPLETED, press Next

6.       6.       Step 3 asks for your User information.  Enter your Name, User name and password (check the box Save Password if you want it to be saved). Press Next.

7.       7.       Step 4 verifies the account information.  You can rename the account name if you choose. Press Next.

8.       8.       Step 5 will show the Server information.  Leave the Domain BLANK. Click the Option button.

9.       9.       Options will give you a 3 step process for connection.  I would suggest accepting the default values.  If needed, you can go back to change them. After step 3, Click Finish and you will be given the option to check your mail.  Press Yes.

10.   10.   You should see a message indicting that you are Logging On and your mail should begin.

If by some chance the auto configuration did not detect the setting correctly, you can go back into the Menu, Press Tools, Options and Tap the name of your account.  You will then be able to verify (and change if necessary) your settings.

p_haze say:

Thanks ! I had to do a couple of extra steps to make it work. I essentially needed to add the last 6-months of emails to my current inbox. So here is what I did:

1. Restore the Main Profile folder from a timepoint I know had the emails I needed to the dekstop.

2. Disconnect mac from the internet and turn off sync on phone/other devices.

3. Delete the current Main Profile folder at ~/Library/Group Containers/UBF8T346G9.Office/Outlook/Outlook 15 Profiles/

4. Move the restored Main Profile folder from desktop to ~/Library/Group Containers/UBF8T346G9.Office/Outlook/Outlook 15 Profiles/

5. Restart outlook and checked that all the emails that were missing were now there (except the ones since the restore point till today)

6. Export .olm file 

7. Connect to internet

8. I noticed that as soon as Outlook started syncing it removed all the emails I had restored ! So that is why important to export .olm file before bringing outlook online

9. Import .olm file. The imported mails are now in a folder called "On my computer". This folder has the missing emails.

11. I moved the emails I need to my main Inbox and deleted the emails under "On my computer". Now everything is how it should be.

This may be a convoluted way to get this done but it worked !
