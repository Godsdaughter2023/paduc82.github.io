cmdrkeene say:

Undoubtedly your new screen has many more pixels than your old one, which means that you have more "real estate" on the screen to work with.  An image that is 100x100 pixels square will take much less actual room on the new screen, thereby appearing smaller.  This is why websites and fonts look smaller.

It's similar to how HD TV's have more pixels than TV's from 15 years ago.  And are a different shape too -- wider.  That's why movies on older TV's had the black bars at the top and bottom. The movie is wide but the TV is square, so the broadcaster would "zoom out" so that you could see the whole wide movie, thereby making black areas appear at the top and bottom.

You can go to your desktop and right-click, choose "Screen resolution" to see how many pixels your monitor has.  On that page too is a link that says "make text and other items larger or smaller".  Click that link to adjust the scale of the desktop, which offsets by enlarging everything by a certain percentage.

To know the activation key of Windows without any program you must follow these steps:

Open the start menu and type "regedit", right click on the search result and select the option "run as administrator".

After opening the registry editor, open the path: HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE> SOFTWARE> Microsoft> Windows NT> CurrentVersion> SoftwareProtectionPlatform

After clicking on SoftwareProtectionPlatform look for the entry BackupProductKeyDefault and the value that appears as a third party in the list is your Windows activation key.

The key has a format of five groups of five characters each.
