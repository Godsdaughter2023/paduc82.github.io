cbarnhorst say:

Please always give the make and model of the device or at least the mobo if this is a custom rig.  Help us help you.

What happens when you "reload" Windows?  Do you mean it won't install or do you mean it will install but not activate?  In case you mean the latter, please remember that Windows 10, unlike previous versions, is activated by licensing the device (computer).  The "computer" is defined for licensing purposes as the mobo/cpu.  The hard drive doesn't matter.  What matters are the unique identifiers of the mobo, like the serial number.  The unique identifiers are recorded by MSFT and activation is tied to that.

If you purchased a retail license for Windows 10 then use the product key that came with it at the time you install.

If your Windows 10 is the free upgrade from Windows 7 or 8.1 you need to know that the license is only valid for the life of the device and that the mobo was that device.  If you have replaced the mobo with the same make and model then you have a repair replacement and you can reinstall the Creators Update and if that does not activate you can get help from MSFT using the Get Help app on your Start menu.  The MSFT Answer Desk agents usually will not assist you with a preview build installed but by installing build 15063 you will qualify for free installation support since 15063 is the currently supported production build.  

If you replaced the mobo with a different retail mobo, called an "upgrade replacement," then you have a new computer and not just a computer with a replacement part.  The free Windows 10 upgrade is not transferrable to a replacement upgrade mobo.  In that case you may need to purchase a license.
