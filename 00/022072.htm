The IMAP protocol only supports synching email folders and doesn't support synching Calendar folders.

To synch Calendar folders, you'll need an Outlook.com or an Exchange mailbox.

palcouk say:

Nothing to do with the win o/s or any MS Program, but everything to do with the mail protocol you are using.

hal hostetler - mvp-outlook say:
This has nothing to do with either the Windows OS or Outlook, and everything to do with the POP and IMAP protocols.  POP synchronizes nothing at all and it must be fudged in order t get even email to sync, IMAP syncs email and folders, and neither have the ability to sync Contacts or Calendars regardless of what OS or email client is used.  Roady's answer is correct, you need an email account that uses a protocol which supports Contact and Calendar sync such as Outlook.com or an Exchange Server account.

Sub Duplicate_Number()

  Set Source = Sheets("Sheet1").Range("A" & Rows.Count).End(xlUp)
