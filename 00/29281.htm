greg carmack - windows mvp 2010-2020 say:
Hi Laurie. I'm Greg, an installation specialist and 9 year Windows MVP here to help you.

You said that you wanted to use an OEM license that hasn't been installed yet on another PC than what it's intended for. What indicates that it is intended for another PC, just that it was bought for that purpose, or does it have the PC maker's branding on the packaging or license? A retail builder's OEM license can be used anywhere you want but is locked to the first motherboard installed upon. Factory OEM normally comes preinstalled with the key embedded in BIOS/UEFI firmware chip on motherboard, but if it's branded with the PC maker's name it still might have restrictions you can check with them.
