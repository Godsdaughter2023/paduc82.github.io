daniel g. grau say:
To complement Phillip's instructions:

You cannot insert page breaks in text boxes. You can link a text box to another text box so that text will flow between them, but their breaks are controlled by resizing the textboxes.

You should not use text boxes unless they are to contain text that requires very special formatting (like sculptured text flowing around a graphic) or that must be placed as an inset atop the main text flow. Place the text as text, next to the ¶ mark that you see in a blank document (it will help you to display nonprinting characters by clicking on the ¶ toggle button in the Standard toolbar, above the Ribbon, which is enabled through the menu bar's View > Toolbars).

It is not clear to me if you are using a real header (View > Header and Footer) or are referring to your letterhead by its function. Page breaks cannot be inserted in document's headers, yet you inserted one. For Phillip's method to work, your letterhead must be placed in the header.

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

Well, there is no preference to change the default, neither is there a command that can be added to a Toolbar nor a Macro Recorder. What's worse, though, is that I'm not clear on what the actual problem is...

The default in PPt 2011 is to Use Destination Theme, so any text pasted into a Placeholder or a Text Box should take on the attributes of what is called for by the specs of the destination file. IOW, the norm should be that you get exactly what you're asking for :-)

The exception would be if you're pasting into a container that has been directly formatted to something other than what the Theme of the destination file calls for. In that case the text still will take on the attributes of the Theme being used although that will be different from how the previous content may have looked.

The quickest option I can suggest is to avoid that is to paste normally, then use the Paste Options button to select Keep Source Formatting or whichever option is appropriate for that instance.

However, the best way to insure consistency throughout a presentation is to apply the formatting to the Master Slide elements rather than directly to the slides, themselves. In that way content conforms to what the Master (Theme) calls for regardless of whether typed or pasted.
