Make sure you keep your data when moving from Office 365 Personal to - Microsoft Community

alexandru cazacu say:

Make sure you keep your data when moving from Office 365 Personal to Office 365 Business

I noticed that there is no article on the internet to help those that transition from a personal Microsoft Account and a personal Office 365 subscription, to a business account and Office 365 for Business, so I thought why not write one.

EDIT: We do have an article about this: https://support.office.com/en-us/article/switch-from-an-office-365-for-home-to-a-business-subscription-9322ffb8-a35d-4407-8ebe-ed6ea0859b9f. Please consider my article complementary to the aforementioned one.

One thing that I need to make clear from the start is that when you cancel your personal Office 365 subscription, your Microsoft Account (outlook.com, live.com, hotmail.com etc), will NOT be disabled, however given the fact that you were paying for an Office 365 personal subscription, this offered some advantages to your personal account, which will be lost after the subscription is disabled (following your cancellation).

Since the cancellation of the personal subscription will bring some changes in terms of storage for both your Outlook.com mailbox and OneDrive personal, you might want to read further, so that you don't end up in a situation where you suffer data loss.

When purchasing an Office 365 Personal subscription, this is linked to your Outlook.com account and the benefits are that:

- your Outlook.com mailbox size is increased from 15GB (free version) to 50GB (with Office 365 Personal subscription enabled)

- your OneDrive personal storage is increased from 5GB (free version) to 1TB (with Office 365 Personal subscription enabled)

Outlook.com storage: https://support.office.com/en-gb/article/storage-limits-in-outlook-com-7ac99134-69e5-4619-ac0b-2d313bba5e9e

OneDrive Personal storage plans: https://onedrive.live.com/about/en-us/plans/

OneDrive for Business storage plans: https://products.office.com/en-us/onedrive-for-business/compare-onedrive-for-business-plans

You have subscribed for an Office 365 Business subscription that will give you email, file storage and Office apps. You now have 2 email accounts, Outlook.com and the Office 365 one, plus 2 OneDrive storage plans: personal (1TB because of the Office 365 Personal subscription) and business (1TB with the Office 365 Business subscription).

The issue comes when you want to cancel the Office 365 Personal subscription, because the following actions will take place:

- your Outlook.com mailbox will have its size reduced from 50GB to 15GB, which is the maximum quota for the free mail service

- your OneDrive personal storage, will have its size reduced from 1TB to 5GB, which is the maximum quota for the free storage service

What you can do if you currently have files and emails in your personal storage and mail accounts, that exceed the free quotas. Example: we have 10GB of files in OneDrive personal and 20GB of emails in Outlook.com mailbox.

1. Let's begin with OneDrive Personal. We will have to transfer the content from OneDrive Personal to OneDrive for Business and this can be achieved by following the instructions in this article: https://support.office.com/en-ie/article/move-files-from-onedrive-to-onedrive-for-business-7fb28cad-7e25-451f-8b4b-2d1a71e5c0e9

2. In the second situation, where the Outlook.com mailbox is bigger than the maximum free quota of 15GB, we will have to use Outlook desktop client application to make a PST backup of older emails, and then delete them from the mailbox, in order to reduce its size under 15GB and avoid losing data at the moment when the Office 365 Personal subscription gets completely disabled.

First of all, we need to make sure that the entire Outlook.com mailbox is downloaded on your computer, by Outlook desktop client. This can be done by:

- checking that on for the Outlook.com account, Exchange cached mode is turned on; refer to this article https://support.office.com/en-us/article/turn-on-cached-exchange-mode-7885af08-9a60-4ec3-850a-e221c1ed0c1c

- make sure that after Exchange cached mode is turned on, we cache All emails; refer to this article https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/help/2733062/only-a-subset-of-your-exchange-mailbox-items-are-synchronized-in-outlo.

- allow Outlook to completely download the content of the mailbox, by browsing each folder of the mailbox, after you have set the option "Mail to keep offline" to All; it will take a while depending on how much content was already cached on your computer and how big the mailbox is

Secondly, after Outlook desktop client has completely downloaded the mailbox content, we are now able to export its content to a PST file, by following this article https://support.office.com/en-us/article/export-or-backup-email-contacts-and-calendar-to-an-outlook-pst-file-14252b52-3075-4e9b-be4e-ff9ef1068f91.

After the export has finished, check the size of the PST file and confirm that it is about the same size as what you see in Outlook.com settings/view full settings/general/storage. Refer to the used value, not the maximum value. We are now safe to delete older emails from the Outlook.com mailbox, in order to free up space and have its size within the free quota limit of under 15GB.

More details here: https://support.office.com/en-gb/article/storage-limits-in-outlook-com-7ac99134-69e5-4619-ac0b-2d313bba5e9e

Enjoy :).

“Verbose logging” is a setting that exposes more information during the installation process. It will capture “warning” as well as “error” messages that provide us with clues to your problem. 

 

You can post the generated Logs in Onedrive and give a link to them to your technician if you need technical help.

 

I found a pattern in one verbose log file set that I have not yet had a chance to confirm in others, but you can give it a try.  The pattern is each log file is for a separate component. If you open the files from the start and jump to the bottom of each file you will see that they report successful install of a component.  There should be an odd number of log files, find the one in the very middle. It is also probably the largest. It is most likely to be the one reporting the error that caused the install to end and back out. All of the log files after that point will be reporting the uninstall of previously installed components in reverse order.

 

 

Here is an example of the error message:

<snip>

MSI (s) (48:2C) [18:15:25:059]: Product: Microsoft Office Single Image 2010 -- Installation operation failed.

 

MSI (s) (48:2C) [18:15:25:059]: Windows Installer installed the product. Product Name: Microsoft Office Single Image 2010. Product Version: 14.0.4763.1000. Product Language: 0. Manufacturer: Microsoft Corporation. Installation success or error status: 1603.

</snip>

 

Do a google search for “error status: 1603”

 

If you don’t recognize an error, upload the entire log file set to Onedrive.

 

The thing is, the “useful” part will be very small and very specific and you probably won’t recognize it. So posting extract may catch the problem, but often miss the important info, so it is best to give us access to everything captured.

Diagnosing When Setup Stops Responding

At times, Office Setup stops responding (hangs), and you do not receive any error message. The best thing to do in this situation is to restart your computer, and run Office Setup again with complete verbose logging turned on (with one additional option). To do this, start Office Setup. To do so, follow these steps:

  1. 1.            Click Start, and then click Run.
  2. 2.            In the Open box, type the following command-line, and then click OK:

path\Setup.exe /L*v! C:\Verboselog.txt

Note that Path is the full path of your Office source location.

To enable Windows Installer logging yourself, open the registry with Regedit.exe and create the following path and keys:

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\Software\Policies\Microsoft\Windows\Installer

Reg_SZ: Logging
Value: voicewarmupx

The letters in the value field can be in any order. Each letter turns on a different logging mode. Each letter’s actual function is as follows for MSI version 1.1:

v - Verbose output
o - Out-of-disk-space messages
I - Status messages
c - Initial UI parameters
e - All error messages
w - Non-fatal warnings
a - Start up of actions
r - Action-specific records
m - Out-of-memory or fatal exit information
u - User requests
p - Terminal properties
+ - Append to existing file
! - Flush each line to the log
x - Extra debugging information. The “x” flag is available only on Windows Server 2003 and later operating systems, and on the MSI redistributable version 3.0, and on later versions of the MSI redistributable.

”*” - Wildcard, log all information except for the v and the x option. To include the v and the x option, specify “/l*vx”.

 

Note This should be used only for troubleshooting purposes and should not be left on because it will have adverse effects on system performance and disk space. Each time you use the Add/Remove Programs tool in Control Panel, a new Msi*.log file is created.

Value 1 = Success
Value 2 = Cancel
Value 3 = Error
