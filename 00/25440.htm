But I usually deprecate the use of PAD for many reasons.  In a nutshell:  it is error-prone, and it does not obviate the need to deal with infinitesimal binary floating-point differences in some contexts because PAD applies only to the final value in a cell.

Your watermark does fill the page in Print Layout view (access the header to
see it better), but it will not reach the edges in Print Preview because
most printers are incapable of printing "full bleed" (clear to the edge)
because of the irreducible nonprintable area required for paper handling.
Even printers that can print "borderless" (for photos) can often do so only
for smaller paper sizes, sometimes with a special tray, and in general
require a special setting in the printer Properties. Even commercial
printers have to print on oversize paper and trim the printed page in order
to get full bleed.

        If typOS.dwPlatformId = VER_PLATFORM_WIN32_WINDOWS Then
            bytKeys(VK_NUMLOCK) = 1
            SetKeyboardState bytKeys(0)
