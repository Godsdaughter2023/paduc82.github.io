Hi CarloEspino,

Thank you for reaching out to us with your concern, we’re happy to help!

There are many reasons as to why your Bing Ads campaign may be eligible but not generating data, and upon these many reasons, lie many factors. The most common reasons why advertisers have this issue include: The account being under 24 hours old, recently enabled campaigns/ad groups, any editorial disapprovals, poor ad position due to low keyword bids, strict keyword match types, and budget issues. Below, I will go into detail about each of these reasons. ***Please note, there may be a latency in traffic data from the time you receive a click or an impression, to the time you see it reflected on the platform***

Account creation, changes, or recently enabled campaigns/ad groups within the last 24 hours:


When advertisers create new accounts, it usually takes 24 hours for their ads to receive any traffic due to the fact that all new ads and keywords go through an extensive review process that can take several hours. With that being said, most are usually approved and eligible within 24 hours. During this process, our Bing Ads Bots take the time to crawl your website to ensure that everything meets our editorial guidelines and to evaluate the quality or your ads and keywords to verify that they are relevant to your business. A review will also take place if your campaigns/ads have recently been enabled from a paused status. If it has been more than 24 hours since you have created your account or made any changes to your account, the issue could be something greater, which I would be more than happy to look into.


Editorial disapprovals:

Even though your campaign may have an eligible status, you could very well have an editorial disapproval within that campaign which could cause a cease in traffic. While our Bing Bots are performing the review I mentioned above, they look out for anything that is or could be potentially harmful or offensive to our search users. Anything that is found to be or have the potential to be harmful or offensive is immediately flagged and will not run. Our Bing Ads Editorial Policy help page

****Please keep in mind that the reasoning for the disapproval could vary****


Poor ad position due to low keyword bids:


We always suggest for our advertisers to bid competitively, especially if your business is common and you have a lot of competition. Bidding competitively increases your chances of securing one of the eight spots we have on the first page of search results and receiving traffic. Advertisers generally run into an issue getting traffic when their bids aren’t sufficient enough, landing them on the second page or greater of search results. We find that most search users don’t search past the first page, which in turn can cause advertisers to not have any traffic if they are on the second page or beyond, leading them to believe that their ads aren’t running. Restrictive keyword match types:

Keyword match types impact how and when your ad is pulled. At Bing Ads, we have four different types of keyword match types which are: Broad, broad match modifier, phrase, and exact. Broad is our least restrictive match type and exact is our most restrictive. If you have a long or very specific keyword on phrase or exact match, chances are, your ad isn’t going to be pulled and as a result, you won’t receive any traffic. To learn more about our keyword match types, our What are keyword match types, and how do I use them? Bing Ads help article is a great read.

Bid to budget ratio:

Another reason why you may be struggling to receive traffic, is due to a poor bid to budget ratio. A poor bid to budget ratio means that you may have a bid that is too high and a daily budget that is too low for your ad to run, for example: A $3 keyword bid with a $5 a day budget. This ratio isn’t balanced and won’t give your ad any funds to run.

The location you see in the Recent Activity log can change and doesn't have to match where you live.  The address you see is the address of your ISP's gateway to the wider Internet.  Since your ISP has more than one route to the wider network, you may see more than one location in the log.  That's not a sign that someone else is logging in as you.  For example, I see logins from two cities that are 150 miles apart.  Neither of those two cities are the city in which I live.  I know, however, that both of those logins are mine because the IP address from which they occurred are for two of my ISP's hubs and the time matches the times I logged in.

Storage Card? - Microsoft Community

rottiegrrl say:

Storage Card?

Dropped my HTC HD7 phone.  Now it says my 'storage card is damaged'. 

Is that a.k.a. my SIM card?

If so, what is typically 'damaged' on it and would anyone know if there is anything I can do to get it working again myself?

I really, really don't want to have to send it out again...ugh!

Thanks.

a. user say:

It's most likely the 'internal' memory/storage inside the unit has become fragmented or unusable at some point.

Two things to try.

a) Soft Reset -- power down (press the power button until the 'Slide down' message appears.  Slide it down, then wait until after the 'Goodbye' message disappears.  Then press the Power button again until it vibrates.  This will reset/reformat the system's internal memory map.  If THAT doesn't work then try:

b) Hard Reset -- if the phone works, go to Settings, About, and tap the Reset My Phone button at the bottom.  NOTE: This will take it to 'out of the box' condition, so you'll lose personal data, videos, music, etc. and half to reload them.  If THAT doesn't work, it would seem there's a hardware problem and you'll have to contact your supplier for a repair/replacement.
