jetta48 say:

The CPU is the Central Processing Unit. You can think of it as the central brain of your PC. The CPU will have to work harder (higher CPU usage) the more programs and other activities it has to run.

For example, if your PC is not doing anything then the CPU usage may be as low as less than 1%. However, if you have several programs running, then it will be much higher.

All CPUs are not the same. Some run at higher frequencies than others and are therefore quicker. Also, it is possible to have "multiple core" CPUs (and most modern PCs do) that are in effect quicker for slightly different reasons.

Hi ylama,

 

Thank you for clarifying the issue. Please be informed that there is no way to directly download your emails into your local hard drive. You need to export the emails from your mail client once it is downloaded from the server. To do so, you can follow the suggestion provided by Brian Tillman [MVP-Outlook]. Here are the step-by-step procedure:

  1. Open Windows Live Mail 2011 or 2012.

  2. On the Accounts tab, click Email.

  3. Enter your email address, password, and display name. Check Manually configure server settings and then click Next.

  4. Configure your server settings. (Refer to the server settings after this steps.)

  5. Click Next and click Finish.

 

Here are the server settings you can use:

 

Incoming (POP3) Server
Server address: pop3.live.com
Port: 995
SSL Encryption: Yes


Outgoing (SMTP) Server
Server address: smtp.live.com
Port: 25 (or 587 if 25 is blocked)
Authentication: Yes
TLS or SSL Secure Encrypted Connection: Yes

 

Before downloading your emails after successfully configuring your account in Windows Live Mail as a POP account, make sure to disable the option to Leave a copy of messages on the server. In doing so, refer to the steps below:

  1. Start the Windows Live Mail program from your Start menu.

  2. In Windows Live Mail, click on Menu, Options, then Email accounts on the Toolbar.

  3. Highlight your *** Email address is removed for privacy *** account and click Properties.

  4. Click the Advanced tab, then uncheck the Leave a copy of messages on server checkbox.

  5. Click OK to close the "Properties" window, then click Close to close the "Accounts" window.

 

Once done, you can now download your messages on the server to your Windows Live Mail client. From there, you can follow the steps on the article below to know how you can export your emails from your mail client and save it on your local hard disk.

 

How do I import and export email, contacts, and calendars with Windows Live Mail?

 

Hope this helps you with your concern. Should you need additional assistance, let us know.
