What you found were some generic scripts to rename files. They do not in any way adress the issue of renaming files that were generated by the File History mechanism. To do the job properly, the script would need to do this:

  • Maintain the original directory structure.
  • Work recursively.
  • Pick the most recent among a group of similarly named files and ignore the rest.

video-download-capture (2018_07_29 20_43_31 UTC).exe

innovativeone say: Thanks for the reply Varun J., I think I figured it out. It turns out I lost the display settings after logging off, not just after reboot, so it wasn't related to the BIOS and probably not even the display driver.

What I found was that my Generic PNP monitor had multiple entries in the registry, and thus there were multiple display configurations elsewhere in the registry that Windows was managing.
