How do I close my account for Outlook.com, Hotmail, Messenger, and other Microsoft services?

When you close your Microsoft account, you delete your account information, including your ID and password, your registered information, and your contacts. After you delete an account, you can't use that Microsoft account anymore to sign in to Microsoft products and services.


To close your account

  1. Go to the Microsoft account overview webpage, and then sign in with your Microsoft account.

  2. Click Close account.

  3. Verify that the correct Microsoft account appears, type your password, and then click Next.

  4. Click deactivate your Hotmail account.

  5. Click Close account.

To close only your Outlook.com or Hotmail account

Your Outlook.com or Hotmail account will become inactive if you don't sign in for more than 270 days or within the first 10 days after signing up for your account.

After an account becomes inactive, all messages, folders, and contacts are deleted, and incoming messages are sent back to the sender as undeliverable.


If you want to close your account right away:

  1. Visit the close account webpage.

  2. Follow the instructions, and then click Close account to close your account.

MS Office Update 14.2.1 when I try to install it says I need to close - Microsoft Community

paulahowell say:

MS Office Update 14.2.1 when I try to install it says I need to close Microsofr Database Daemon and SyncServicesAgent

I've downloaded the Microsoft Office 2011 update 14.2.1, but when I try to install it says I need to close Microsofr Database Daemon and SyncServicesAgent before I can finish the install. I looked for these two files, and it looks like they're part of Outlook - and are nothing I can open and close. Any ideas?
stratacorps say: If that doesn't work...

The two MS services "Microsoft Database Daemon" and "SyncServicesAgent" will not stop even by holding down the SHIFT key when logging in.  Even if you KILL the services, one of them will start back up before you have the chance to click continue on the update.  Its amazing that MS' own installer can't control its own processes.

Anyway, you have two applications running:

/Applications/Microsoft Office 2011/Office/SyncServicesAgent.app/Contents/MacOS/SyncServicesAgent
/Applications/Microsoft Office 2011/Office/Microsoft Database Daemon.app/Contents/MacOS/Microsoft Database Daemon

You can use the Activity monitor utility to find these processes, or use the Terminal shell and the command line.

From the command line,

killall "Microsoft Database Daemon"

That will take care of that one.  The SyncServicesAgent.app however restarts..that's the most frustrating part of this.  You can't kill the little monster.

So I just rename the actual file SyncServicesAgent.  In Finder, browse to the application and then right-mouse and select Show Contents. Go to Contents, MacOS and rename SyncServicesAgent to something like SyncServicesAgent.disabled so it can't be relaunched until you're through.

Now kill the process.  Again, if your using the command line:

killall "SyncServicesAgent"

Otherwise just search for the process in Activity Monitor and select Quit Process.

Now you can update.  Don't forget to rename the SyncServicesAgent back...the update may have added a new version, so there's probably already a new file there and, of course, the process is running again!
