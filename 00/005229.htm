hansv mvp say:

By default, Word uses a different color for each author who edits a document. In that case, you have *no* influence on which color is assigned to you (and it will change from computer to computer).

Alternatively, you can use a fixed color for all authors. You then have a choice from some 15 mostly garish colors.

 

To switch, activate the Review tab of the ribbon.

In the Tracking group, click the lower half of the Track Changes button and select Change Tracking Options...

Select your preference from the Comments dropdown.

Sheet3 row3/col3 = FORMULA...here is what I need help with:

countif (Sheet1 col E4:e1003) = Sheet3/col2 AND (sheet1/column E4:e1003 is between ((Sheet3 col3 minus 6 days and sheet3 col3)))

Hope this helps explain my dilema.  I am repeating this formula across columns for the project spreadsheet that I am maintaining and can adjust if it grows further than what I have allowed for.  I don't know visual basic or anything else....I want an excel formula to be used in the spreadsheet to do my counts.

paulcordts say: You need to use a COUNTIFS function which allows you to specify multiple criteria

The general format is
COUNTIFS(criteria_range1, criteria1, [criteria_range2, criteria2]…)

Your formula would be
=COUNTIFS(Sheet1!$D$4:$D$1003, B3, Sheet1!$E$4:$E$1003,">" & C$2-6,Sheet1!$E$4:$E$1003,"<=" & C$2)

The 3rd condition <=C2 is just to make sure that no new hires past the week ending date are counted.
You did have your first condition checking column E which is the date and not the title so I changed this to column D

You will need to change the Total 4/14 to an actual date and not mixed text and number (ie 4/14/13) to make things easier

paulcordts say: If the 4/14 is an actual date you should by OK.  

I missed the total column so there are two small changes 
=COUNTIFS(Sheet1!$D$4:$D$1003, $A3, Sheet1!$E$4:$E$1003,">=" & C$2-6,Sheet1!$E$4:$E$1003,"<=" & C$2)

The three checks being made are
Sheet1!$D$4:$D$1003, $B3  - this checks column D on sheet 1 against the title in column A on sheet 3

Sheet1!$E$4:$E$1003,">=" & C$2-6 - this checks the date on Sheet 1 column E against the heading date in row 2 on sheet 3

Sheet1!$E$4:$E$1003,"<=" & C$2 - this checks the date on Sheet 1 column E to make sure it is not greater than the heading date in row 2 on sheet 3

Only when all three criteria is a value counted

This does assume that Tte heading Role is in cell A2 on sheet 3 (Row 1 is not shown or empty on you example)
and that the Empl ID # heading is in cell A3 on sheet 1 (since your count starts in row 4)

Enter this formula in Cell C3 on sheet 3 and you can copy it across and down and it will keep the correct references

davidf2 say:

When you overlap text boxes on top of images in older versions of Publisher you end up with a low resolution .gif copy of the combination. http://www.mliska.com/img1.gif 

 

Move the text box off the image. Also in a third party image editor resize and optimize your image for the web prior to inserting it. You are currently using a 896px × 581px version of your image. Create that size at 96 dpi and save as a jpg and insert at full scale/size in your page. Then when Publisher produces your web files it should create a jpg of the same quality and size. If you don't have an image editor try www.irfanview.com for a good and easy to use freebie.

 

Also for what it is worth...do not include the link to your second page in the image. This is called 'mystery meat' navigation. Make your links easy to understand and find. I just happened to notice the link as I moused over the picture.
