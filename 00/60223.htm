Fan speed varies according to the temperature of key components.  Your system is designed to do that.  If you are hearing a louder sound when the fan is on high speed than you remember, it could be because of a dying fan.  It could also be from too much heat in some major component -- usually the CPU itself.  

If this is a laptop, make certain you never use it when it is setting on a soft surface.  That blocks the tiny vents on the bottom that are the source of the cooling air.

Also, this is likely a pretty old system.  I would be happy to advise you if you will provide this information:

  1. Laptop or Desktop
  2. Age of computer

  6. Service Pack 1 (SP1) installed? Same place as 5 above.
  7. Capacity of disk drive, how much is in use?  Pie chart shown in Computer
  8. Model number of hard drive (click Disk in Device Manager)
  9. Age of hard drive if not the original
  10. How much memory is installed? (Right-click on Computer, choose Properties)

The problem obviously with this method is that what you see on the keyboard doesn't correspond to what you see on-screen (the Swiss keyboard isn't too bad, about 90% of the keys are the same as a UK keyboard). If you touch type, that isn't a real problem. If it is a problem then you might consider using a separate, external Portuguese keyboard. I've also seen people put little stickers on the keys (though they tend to wear off pretty quickly!)
