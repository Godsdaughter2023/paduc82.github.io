zombie010 say:

I have started receiving calendar invites from users of Outlook 2010 and there appears to be a new behavior in Outlook 2010 versus previous versions.  Namely, Outlook 2010 sends calendar invites using base64 MIME encoding rather than plain text.  This causes the actual invite to be invisible when the message is rendered in Mozilla Thunderbird 3.1.

Previous versions of Outlook sent meeting invites with a plain text rendering so that users of non-Outlook mail clients would still receive sufficient information to know that a meeting was being scheduled.

With Outlook 2010, users of e-mail clients such as Thunderbird will think they have received a blank message.  Given the amount of e-mail that people receive in a day, it is not surprising that such empty e-mails will be simply ignored as an error.  Only the most savvy of users will save the base64 encoding of the meeting invite and then decode it in order to read it.

The bottom line: Outlook 2010 should send a plain text non-encoded version of the meeting details for consumption by older and/or modern non-Outlook e-mail clients such as Thunderbird 3.1.
