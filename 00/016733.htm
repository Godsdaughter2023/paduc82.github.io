suzanne s. barnhill say:

Sorry I missed your earlier replies, but you seem to have gotten there in the end. To answer your question about the formatting of the TOC, the formatting of a TOC entry is based on the relevant style (TOC 1, TOC 2, etc.), but the entry also picks up direct formatting from the referenced heading. Direct formatting is any formatting not prescribed by the heading style. This behavior is by design, so that, for example, italics in a bold roman heading (for emphasis or for a title) are retained in the TOC entry. See http://wordfaqs.ssbarnhill.com/TOCTips.htm#DirectFormatting

A common problem, however, is with capitalized headings. The correct way to have a heading in all caps is to include the All Caps property in the heading style. When you do that, no matter how you type the heading, it will be in all caps. The downside to that is that, if you type it all lowercase, that's how it will appear in the TOC. Or if you turn on Caps Lock and type the heading, it will be in all caps (rather than caps and lowercase) in the TOC. Any other random capitalization will also be reflected. I have an All Caps toggle button on my toolbar so that I can toggle the property off and back on in capitalized headings to make sure that they have been capitalized properly, so that they will appear properly in the TOC.

There have nearly been "religious wars" about whether "MDI" (multiple docs, 1 app window) or "SDI" (single doc per app window) is better.  Too bad Office is schizophrenic, both types of design on different apps in the same office bundle.

Word is SDI, Excel is MDI, as you've found, except there are techniques to beat it into submission.

In Excel in a single spreadsheet you can use View tab > Window group > New Window button to "split" the current worksheet into 2 "windows" (sorta). You can then use View tab > Window group > Arrange All > tiled option to display the now 2 views on the same spreadsheet in the application window. You can now look independently at different worksheets or different parts of the same worksheet. You can stretch the application window across the 2 monitors. It just gets a little tricky lining things up if they are using different screen resolutions or orientation (I like one landscape and one portrait).

Here are some tips that have worked for me to allow me to open spreadsheets in separate "SDI" windows. These are tips that have been submitted by a few different people:

Change MDI to SDI
