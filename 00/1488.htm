susan gabriel say:

Is there a way to put "TOC (continued)" on the top of the second page?

My table of contents is more than one page long, and I'd really like to have a heading at the top of the second page that says "Table of Contents (continued)". Is this possible in Word 2010?
jay freedman say:

First, insert a section break (probably the Next Page type) between the TOC and the start of the regular text.

Click in the regular text after the section break and open the header area. On the Header & Footer Tools ribbon, click the Link to Previous button to turn it off.

On the same tab, click the Previous Section button. Just to the right of that, check the box for "Different First Page". In the header area (with the blue tag "Header -Section 1-"), type your text "Table of Contents (continued)".

If you click the Previous Section button again, you'll see an empty header with the tag "First Page Header -Section 1-". Leave that the way it is, and close the header area.

You should now have no header on the first page of the TOC, the continuation text on the second page (and any more pages that get added to the TOC later), and no header in the regular text. If you want to, you can add a header to the regular text; it won't affect the TOC's header.

hi sounder,

to change the default retention tag "moves items that are 14 days or older from the recoverable items folder in the user's primary mailbox to the recoverable items folder in their archive mailbox.", we need to use powershell. here are the steps:

1. connect to exchange online using remote powershell.

2. run the command to create a new retention tag:

new-retentionpolicytag -name "recoverable3" -type recoverableitems -agelimitforretention 30 -retentionaction movetoarchive

the above example creates a retention tag named recoverable3 to archive the emails in recoverable items folder older than 30 days. note: we can assign only the move to archive retention action to tags for this folder. for your reference: https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/dn783294%28v=exchg.150%29.aspx?f=255&mspperror=-2147217396

3. after the retention tag is created successfully, go to exchange admin center (https://outlook.office365.com/ecp) with your office 365 admin account, navigate to compliance management – retention policies. double click default mrm policy and + to add the newly create tag. remove the tag  "moves items that are 14 days or older from the recoverable items…”, click ok at last. a screenshot for your reference:

please note that the emails in the recoverable items folder are retained for 14 days by default, if you want to change the policy to more than 14 days, you should extend the retention period for this folder for all the users, otherwise the emails will get purged before archived. we can use the exchange powershell to change this setting to a maximum of 30 days. here are the detailed steps:

john korchok say:

As I understand your question, when you're at 100%, you want 1 inch on the on-screen rulers to be 1 inch if you hold an actual ruler beside them.

To do this, you need to adjust your display settings in Windows. Start by measuring 10 inches of on-screen ruler with an actual ruler. Then divide the on-screen by the actual size. If 10" is actually 101/4" long:

10/10.25=0.9756

This means you have to set the resolution to about 95% of its current setting. Please follow these steps:

  1. In Windows, click on the Start button, then on Control Panel.
  2. Select Appearance and Personalization, then Display.
  3. In the left-hand panel, click on Set custom text size (DPI).
  4. Multiply the number in Scale to this percentage of normal size by the factor you calculated earlier. As an example, of Scale to this percentage is set to 100% and you calculated that this should be reduced to .9756 of that amount:
    100 x 0.9756 = 97.56
  5. Type 97.56 into the field and click OK.
  6. Go back to Publisher and verify whether the rulers are now sufficiently accurate.

jay sun msft say:

Hi Freequez,

Thanks for your post. Based on your description, the email sent from one listserv you trust is verified as spoofing message with the warning message. In this case, we suggest you add the email address or domain names in the Safe senders List of Outlook 2016 client:

1. Click Junk on the Home tab.
2. Click Junk E-mail Options….
3. On the Safe Senders tab, click Add…  to add the domain name and email address you want and check the Automatically add people I e-mail to the Safe Senders List checkbox.

4. Click Apply and click OK to close the window.

In addition, you can try to check and remove this listserv email address or domain from the Blocked Senders list under Blocked Senders tab of Junk E-mail Options window.

Otherwise, the cause of this problem may be related to the Sender Policy Framework (SPF) record. If you use the Office 365 for Business account, you can contact the admin in your organization to add the SPF record in the domain register setting by the following steps:

1. Login to Outlook Web App via the admin account.
2. Go to Admin center > Setup > Domains.
3. Click the domain name > click + New custom record.

Meanwhile, the SPF record is created as a TXT record and it will look similar to:

v=spf1 include:spf.protection.outlook.com –all

For related SPF TXT record information, you can refer to Set up SPF in office 365 to help prevent spoofing.

If the problem persists, to further look into it, we need to confirm what the type of your email account configured in Outlook 2016 is, such as POP3, IMAP or Exchange (go to File > Info > Account Settings > Account Settings…) and if you use Office 365 for Business account or other email provider’s account.

Jay
