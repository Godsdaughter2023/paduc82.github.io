How about this?

  • You have a master template with no pictures
  • You place the cursor at a certain position in your document
  • You type a special sequence for sender John and you get all of John's pictures in position

That can be done as follows:

  1. Place the pictures corresponding to a single person in the blank master
  2. The pictures need to be floating (except if you only use one)
  3. Place the cursor at what will be your reference spot (all pictures will be placed relative to this position and they all need to be on the same page)
  4. Select the pictures (Command Click for multiple instances)
  5. Go to Word > Preferences > AutoCorrect
  6. Select the AutoCorrect tab at the top
  7. Place a checkmark at the top left (next to "Automatically correct…")
  8. In the "Replace" field, type a memorable sequence, plus optional framing characters to avert accidental replacements (for instance, john\ or /smith/)
  9. Click on the "Formatted text" button (a picture is the opposite of plain text)
  10. Click on Add
  11. OK your way out

To test, open a no-pictures document, place the cursor at the reference spot and type the sender's sequence.

Repeat the above steps for other sets of pictures.

You didn't mention what columns were being used for the VLOOKUP() so I'll assume columns A and B. Try,
=VLOOKUP(A2,INDIRECT(CHAR(39)&Main!B3&CHAR(39)&"!A2:A51"),2,FALSE)

I would imagine that the issue of your first point (proximity sensor) is by design.  Some people do not like to hold the phone close to their ear while they are talking, or the distance from the phone to their face may vary.  If that's the case and the screen appears too soon, there's a good chance that they'll press the "end call" button with their cheek.

 

In other words, the phone's sensor needs to be sure that it's being held away purposely before the keypad appears.  With the sensor operation, things are either near or not-near, there doesn't appear to be a notion of distance.

 

It's not a question of processor speed, it's a Human Interface Design feature.
