understanding dates in Infopath

Can anyone explain to me the following function:

substring(date, 9, 2) + ((153 * (substring(date, 6, 2) + 12 * ((14 - substring(date, 6, 2)) / 12) - 3) + 2) / 5) + 365 * (substring(date, 1, 4) + 4800 - ((14 - substring(date, 6, 2)) / 12)) + ((substring(date, 1, 4) + 4800 - ((14 - substring(date, 6, 2)) / 12)) / 4) - 32083

Is it changing the date from YYYY-MM-DD to its day equivalent? Can anyone explain some of the constants? In that case, that number does not look like the number of days from year 0. Thanks

jimmy rishe say:

That's the formula for the Julian Day Number of any date, which is the total number of days since the start of the Julian calendar.  You can find more information about it here:

http://stason.org/TULARC/society/calendars/2-15-1-Is-there-a-formula-for-calculating-the-Julian-day-nu.html

In your formula substring(date, 9, 2) is the day of the month, substring(date, 6, 2) is the month, and substring(date, 1, 4) is the year.

There's actually a much simpler way to get a value for determining the min or max of a bunch of dates in InfoPath.  Just use this instead of the formula you have there:

translate(date, "T:-", "")

jimmy rishe say:

I can only guess at why she did it that way (I assume you got this from S.Y.M. Wong-A-Ton's blog), but it could be that the Julian Day is used as sort of an absolute value for the number of days since 4713 BC and is used in the scientific community for calculating the difference between two dates.

As I pointed out, it's overkill here, because InfoPath won't even let you use dates before the year 1601, but it could be useful if you want to calculate the number of days between two dates.
