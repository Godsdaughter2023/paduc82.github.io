There are 2 factors that determine if an account is a child account or an adult account.

1. DOB when the account was originally setup. If under 18 HAS to have an adult account attached to it.

2. If an account is in a family as a child account. Even after the DOB on the account (when originally setup) has reached its 18th birthday it will continue to be a child account until the parental account signs into account.microsoft.com/family and removes the child from the account.  If an accounts DOB has not reached 18 yet and they remove themselves from a family that has an adult in it then it will not be able to do anything until it is put back into a family as a child.

So you will want to figure out what family you are currently in (you can do this by signing into account.microsoft.com/family and it will tell you what adults are in your family currently then you would need to sign into that adult account and remove yourself from the family. If you sign into that account and do not see yourself in a family that means the DOB on the account when it was originally setup is still under 18.

fx17 say:

My god that was the worst series of replies I've seen in my life. I'm almost certain it was a bot and not a human.

I know this was from two months ago, but I guess you may still need help.

In Favourite Places (under "About Me" in Cortana's Notebook), you need to Right Click on the favourite you wish to change.

You are presented with a dropdown menu with two options: Edit, and Delete.

Click Edit and you are given a number of options, one of which is "Set As Home."

Simply deselect this setting in your existing "Home" address, and then go to your *Correct* address and click "Set As Home" within that.

Then delete the previous, incorrect address.

You can also rename the favourites in this edit window (in case one is incorrectly named "home")

Hope this helps!

F

(JUST TO ADD: You can't actually edit an actual address as such. You can only rename the favourite, or set it as home, or work. If you wish to change the address's location, you have to delete the entire favourite, and start again. Your guess is as good as mine as to why they designed it that way.)
