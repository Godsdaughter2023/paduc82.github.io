There is a check box in Word's Options (File | Options | Save) to exempt "common system fonts" from embedding. Make sure this isn't checked. Well, the idea of Arial and TNR is that they should be portable, and I don't know why Lulu would reject the file, but there are plenty of other serif and sans serif fonts out there (including many that ship with Office), and most book designers would throw up their hands in horror at the idea of using Arial and TNR in the first place, so perhaps you would be better advised to experiment with different fonts. bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
No offense, but before you go any further it might be a good idea to provide a description of the document, exactly what you need to do, and - equally as important - a link to the Online Help you're referring to. I've never found any reference to removing Master Pages so I'd like to see that information - it may be erroneous :-)

Master Pages in a Word Publishing Layout document do not physically exist such as they do in a desktop publishing program. They are merely viewable representations of what is actually a conceptual layer of the document. That layer is actually the substructure containing [and generated by] Headers, Footers, Section Breaks, etc. Accordingly, content on Master Pages can be modified & some of those modifications may cause one Master to be 'substituted' for the one previously used for those content pages, but Master Pages can't simply be 'deleted' - at least not without serious impact to the document content. Every "content page" of a Pub document is based on one Master or another.

Suppose your data start in C3.  Then, use the formula =IF(COUNTIF(C3:E3,">1")>1,"yes","no")  Copy this as far down as you have data in C:E.

The above yields yes if 2 or 3 of the cells are >1.  If you want exactly 2, use COUNTIF(C3:E3,">1")=2
