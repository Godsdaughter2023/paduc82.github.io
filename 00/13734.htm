Hi zesanta,

To help protect you from fraud or abuse, the system might have temporarily suspended or locked your account because of some unusual activity. To unblock your account, Microsoft asks you to enter a code that you receive via text message. The phone number you use to receive this text message doesn't need to be associated with your account. It doesn't even need to be your own phone number. If your phone doesn't receive text messages, ask a friend or family member if you can use theirs. Microsoft won't save this phone number.

Follow these steps to unblock your account:

  1. Go to https://account.microsoft.com and sign in to your suspended account.
  2. Enter a phone number to request a security code be sent to you via text message. This can be any phone that can receive text messages.

cliff gu msft say:

Hi Sunitha,

 

 

Thanks for your updates.

 

As the issue has gone away, to benefit other community members who encountered the same issue, I’d like to summarize the issue as follows.

The symptom of the issue

When uploading an excel file containing links to a SharePoint Library, editing files in the Excel application, saving changes, opening the files in Excel Online and clicking on the links, the error message” Sorry, we couldn’t open this links. The link address may be invalid, or you may not have permission to open it.” appears.

Root cause

In the Excel client, all hyperlinks to files are automatically updated before you save to ensure that all the links are up-to-date at the point of save.

Solution

Use the =HYPERLINK() function. If you use this function, the hyperlinks will not break.

Disable the updating of these links in the Excel client, by going to Excel Options > Advanced > General > Web Options > File names and locations > and uncheck Update links on save.  If you disable this, the hyperlinks will not be updated in the Excel client and therefore will not be broken in Excel Online.

For more detailed information, please refer to Excel Online – “Sorry, we couldn’t open this link. The link address may be invalid, or you may not have permission to open it.”

 

Thanks,

Cliff
