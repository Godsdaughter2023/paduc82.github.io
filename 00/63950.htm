cmdrkeene say:

There's no difference, just shortcuts to reach the same place.  You can get to your Documents folder by any method you choose:

  1. Digging down the full path of C: hard drive > Users > Username > Documents
  2. Going to This PC > Documents
  3. Clicking Documents of the left side of any folder (in the navigation area to the left)
  4. Clicking the arrow just to the right side of the file icon in the address bar to show the drop down, then choosing either Libraries or your username (both will then let you reach your Documents folder)
  5. If you decided to show it, you can click "Libraries" on the left side of any folder, which also has a shortcut to your Documents folder.

There's other methods using the "Run box" or command line, but they like those above all go to the same place.  The only difference is your own preference of what you like.

As for the original question of "Someone went to some trouble to design new icons for File Explorer in Window 10... What do they mean?"  The answer is "the new design of new icons is only for design only to give new colors and styles.  The meaning has not changed at all, only the colors."
