daniel g. grau say:

My suggestion does not correct "two spaces to one," but "two periods to period space space." You save one keystroke.

Word cannot distinguish between "F.B.I. Headquarters" and "… you and I. However…" so you cannot define an appropriate auto-replacement sequence.

I would go with John's global replacement. You could use a non-breaking space after acronyms, so you can use wildcards for a global replacement of "period space to period space space" (unlike a regular search, wildcard searches make a distinction between regular spaces and non-breaking spaces).

If you feel adventurous, test the following wildcard Search and Replace (minus the quotation marks):

Find:    "([!.]?.) ([A-Z])"

It changes single spaces between sentences to two spaces, while skipping acronyms. It works in the cases I tested  but there could be exceptions. It can be executed several times in a row and the spaces won't accumulate.

There are several ways of doing this.
