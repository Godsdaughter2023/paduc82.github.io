rohn007 say:

The "non-commercial" part is normal. It just means you have a consumer license, like Home Premium.

The "unlicensed product" part is the important message. It means that your installation is not active. 

MS defined sales regions around the world.  Licenses bought in one region, like Australia cannot be activated in another region, like the UK.  This is to prevent people from taking advantage of currency exchange and price differences to save money. MS has to maximize profits.

Office 365 has to connect to the internet at least once every 30 (or so) days to confirm the activation status of your license.  Could it have been more than 30 days since the last time you logged in?

But, you should be able to simply connect to the internet and log in to 365 with your australian email account.  This would allow 365 to connect to the activation system.  Since you are not actually activating using a product key, you may get lucky. But I have not had any way of testing this myself and have not heard of anyone explicitly doing it.

Hello Jan,

You can turn off the web results in the start menu. Simply go to zPC settings > System > Search and Change 'Use Bing to search online' setting to off.

In the current build, the control used in the start menu was not given access to the keyboard shortcuts that are available when the start menu is turned off.

Without the start menu in the way; Windows key + W (Windows' settings), Windows key + F (files) or Windows key + S (everything) will present you with a search control tailored to their respective usage, without taking you to the start screen and, if the feature is turned off, without the web results.
