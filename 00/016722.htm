rohn007 say:
You have 2 problems to resolve.

First is understanding change in license terms.

MS made a significant change in the way Office 2016/365 is licensed compared to 2013 and earlier.

Prior to 2016, you ran the installer program, it loaded the Office program files onto the computer then requested the product key to confirm you owned a valid license to use Office. This "activated" Office on the computer. The Office product key was associated with the hardware. Depending on the type of license you were allowed to install and activate on one or two computers.

Starting with Office 2016 all of that is turned on it's head. (On first installation, and use of the product key.) When you start installing Office, at the very start of the install process, you see the prompt you screen captured, requesting the email address the key is going to be assigned to. After you provide the email address you are prompted to provide the product key.

At this point the Office license is now associated with the email address, NOT the hardware it is installed on.

Second problem is pre-installed Office.

Many/ most new computers do come with a factory installed "free trial" of Office. I have not seen official confirmation, but my best guess is that this trial is for consumer verisons of Office. If your product key does not match the installed version, the key will not activate that preinstalled version.

So, in situations like yours, where you have a license for a different business bundle which you have to install you must FIRST uninstall the factory trial. Otherwise the activation system becomes confused.

Then you can start the installation process and activation. When you are starting installs on a new machine you must provide the email account (and it's password) to assign the license to. Then you provide the product key to prove you have a valid license. This "traditional" method has become very impractical in light of the license change that requires the email account (and password) at the start of the install process. These changes are designed to "encourage" businesses to switch to the 365 pay-forever "subscription" license model (more money for MS)

MS has designed the business version of Office 2016/365 (and I believe this includes the volume licenses like Office Standard, but this needs confirmation) to be managed from the Business Administration Portal. In this situation, the general process is:
. * the Office administrator logs in to the portal.
. * they buy some Office licenses / product keys
. * they assign the licenses to specific business email accounts
. * the first time the user tries to use an Office application the specific application and other associated applications in the bundle are installed in the background by the "Click to Run" service.

Office 2016 standard is licensed to be run on only one computer at a time.
Most Office 365 Business licenses allow each licensee / user to run on up to 5 Desktop/laptop class machine AND up to 5 tablet (keyboardless) class machines AND up to 5 Smartphones.

For any wrapped graphic you can specify a position relative to the page or
the margins. What you can't specify is the page it will be on: it will
always be on the same page as the paragraph to which it is anchored. The
position settings are in the Advanced Layout dialog (reached variously
depending on your version of Word). How you would achieve this in code is
another matter.

byhamc say: Vijay's response gives you how to format to your requirement.

If you're requirement is to list dates for the whole year, I'd go one step further and use a formula to populate the dates - keeps it nice and simple then.

So, for example, put the Year parameter in cell A1- for example 2014.

In cell A3, put the formula =DATE(A1,1,1) which will return 'Wednesday 01.01.2014'.

In cell A4, put the formula =A3+1 and then copy down to cell A367.

Finally, in cell A368, put the formula =IF(YEAR(A367+1)<>$A$1,"",A367+1) which will then only return a date on a Leap Year.

This then means you just have to change the year in cell A1 and everything else is already set up for you, including 29.02 on a leap year.
