From your description, as you still can’t see the NGSC setup pages after installing, it’s may be related to Group Policy Editor, please first confirm the scenarios below and let us know the outcome:
1. If your computer is not in a domain environment
Since users can disable OneDrive in Windows 8.1 or Windows 10 via the Local Group Policy Editor, you can check if the value is set to Enabled in the Local Group Policy Editor. If the value is set to Enabled, change it to Disabled to see the outcome.

Here are the steps:
a. Press (Windows key) + R to open the Run box.
b. Type gpedit.msc and click OK.
c. In the Local Group Policy Editor, in the folder list under Local Computer Policy, navigate to Local Computer Policy > Computer Configuration > Administrative Templates > Windows Components > OneDrive.
d. On the OneDrive screen, under Setting, click Prevent the usage of OneDrive for file storage.
e. In the Prevent the usage of OneDrive for file storage box, check if the value is Enabled. If yes, select Disabled, then click OK.

2. If your computer is in a domain environment
OneDrive may be disabled by the IT administrator via the Group Policy. You can contact your IT administrator to check it first.
For your reference: https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/dn921901.aspx

Connect-SPOService -Url https://domain-admin.sharepoint.com -credential $credential

Set-SPOTenantSyncClientRestriction -GrooveBlockOption "OptOut"

---Note---: the second command may take about one hour to take affect.
