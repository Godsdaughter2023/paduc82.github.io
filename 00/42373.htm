3 - Disable and enable USB controllers
Windows Key + X > Device Manager > Universal Serial Bus controllers > Right click on each entry beneath this one by one and Uninstall > Reboot your computer to automatically install these

Windows Key+X > Click Device Manager > Expand Universal Serial Bus controllers > Right-click USB Root Hub option and click Properties > Go to Power Management tab and uncheck the Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power. 5 - Update / Reinstall Generic USB Hub
Windows Key+X > Click Device Manager > Expand Universal Serial Bus controllers > Right click on USB Hub > Select Update Driver Software > Browse my computer for driver software > Click on Let me pick from a list of device drivers on my computer > From the list, select Generic USB Hub > Next and follow onscreen instructions > Reboot your Computer

A Complete Comparison of XBOX One and Playstation 4

Update: When I started this thread, I noted that all was subject to change at the bottom. It unexpectidly happened on the 19/06/2013 when Microsoft reversed many of their policy decisions. This list has now been updated with new policies.

# Development:
XBO: Developed by Microsoft, Development started in 2010.
PS4: Developed by Sony, Development started in 2008.

# Specifications:
XBO: 8 Core AMD x86 CPU, 8GB DDR3 RAM, 500GB HDD, Blu-Ray Drive
PS4: 8 Core AMD x86-64 CPU, 8GB GDDR5 RAM, 32MB eSRAM, 500GB HDD, Blu-Ray Drive

# Power Differences:
- The CPU in both systems are nearly identical.
- The GPU in the PS4 is 50% more powerful than the XBO. (XBOX's 12 CU vs PS4's 18 CU)
- This converts to PS4's 1.84 TFLOPS vs XBO's 1.20 TFLOPS of raw power. 
- The PS4 has 3.5% more peak bandwidth. (XBO's 170GB/s vs PS4's 176GB/s).
- The PS4 has 176GB/s bandwidth between GPU, CPU and DDR5 Memory.
- The XBO has 68GB/s bandwidth between GPU, CPU and DDR3 Memory.
- The XBO has 102GB/s bandwidth between GPU and eSRAM.
- The PS4 will allow 40% more RAM for games (XBO's 5GB vs PS4's 7GB)

See for details of the XBO Hardware:
http://www.eurogamer.net/articles/digitalfoundry-spec-analysis-xbox-one 

See for details of the PS4 Hardware:
http://www.eurogamer.net/articles/digitalfoundry-sony-details-final-ps4-spec

Both consoles will feature a similar architecture making game creation similar on both consoles. In contrast to previous generations were the architecture of the PS3 made it vastly most difficult to create games. The developers will decide how much of an effect the increase power of one console over another will have as they program and utilize power accordingly in both consoles, especially the eSRAM of the XBO which if poorly utilized drops the consoles bandwidth drastically.

# The Power of the Cloud (Distributed Computing)
- Microsoft have claimed that 'The Power of the Cloud' will make up for hardware differences.
- However Digital Foundary finds that distributed computing has vast limitations.
- The useful functions of distributed computing are possible for both the PS4 and XBO.
- The article confirms that it is impossible to make up for the hardware differences via 'The Cloud'.

See for details on 'The Power of the Cloud' and Distributed Computing:
http://www.eurogamer.net/articles/digitalfoundry-in-theory-can-xbox-one-cloud-transform-gaming
