bluekarana say:

Well, I finally figured it out from reading another question on the forum I hadn't seen before.

The problem was that I had two-step authentication turned on for my Microsoft account. The Activation dialog on the Mac isn't smart enough to work with that and just reports back that your password is wrong or you don't have a valid account.

Steps to correct:

  1. Sign in to your Microsoft account
  2. Go to your Microsoft Account page
  3. Click Security & password in the menu to the left
  4. Mid-way down the page under the header "Two-step verification", there should be a link that says something like "turn two-step verification off." Click it.
  5. Go back to the Office Activation dialog and try again.
  6. Life is good

cpitchford say:

Having owned an N97 and 5800 and all the Maemo devices from 770 to N900 I think there are some significant usability issues with both at the moment!

 

The N900 is fantastic, imho.. It is open and hackable.. if stuff doesn't so what I'd quite like I can go looking for ways to change it.. then find that the stuff I'd most like to change is closed source and guarded by Nokia.. which sucks and will possibly only get worse..grr

 

Usabiltiy wise, it isn't as fluid in day to day situations.. The N900 is fantastic to demostrate to others.. the UI is relatively smooth and the layout seems logical and structured..

 

however, using it in real life can be frustrating some times.. for example, answering a call can be a bit irritating when the screen rotates and holds you up presing the answer button.. then when the 3G coverage gives out you have to wrestle with the device to call the person back (which invariably they are able to do so ages before you... the rotate the device to launch the phone service is intermittent at best)

 

This is the first incarnation of Maemo5 and is infinitely more finger friendly than previous Maemo releases, but it is by no means a single handed operation, nor is it likely to be soon..

 

My previous phone (ok before the N97) was the Nokia 7710 (series 90 symbian) and was portrait touch screen.. so I have no problems using a phone this way round!

 

The hardware in the N900 are fantastic compared to the Symbian offerings.. first huge difference is the GPU.. Maemo is built on top of X and the seperation of applications within Maemo is built around UNIX technology.. radically different to Symbian..Launching a Symbian application (the music player on the N97) can lead to font size changes (on the main menu)  or opening a application (messages) leads to screen corruption.. incredibly poor graphics support in Symbian makes the user interface tired and troublesome..

 

The hardware of the N900 is an order of magnititude larger than the top end Symbian models..

 

The N97 has something like 70 meg of storage for applications and many piece of data.. such as browser cache, calendar, message storage, some installed applications.. my device with only Nokia apps installed has 3.4meg free and is essentially useless.. often refusing to store large data (such as emails and messages) on the huge 32gig of storage.. you can store messages there, but the moment you attach the device to a PC in USB mode, all hell breaks loose and you end up with stuff moving about  horribly.

 

 

the N900, by contrast utilises some of the 32gig of storage for data and applications.. infact 2gig is allocated and (if you're a dab hand) you can actually increase it..

 

Symbian is heavily policed.. to develop and released an official app that everyone can install you actually need to be a business and pay a fair bit of cash for a certificate!!

 

Maemo, anyone can.. and that means many more people are developing apps that are built on love, not on dreams of cash from advertising or Ovi sales..

 

Maemo is built upon Debian, one of the oldest Linux distributions going.. As such it is made from packages.. each little fragment of the OS is a package.. from themes, to fonts to the calculator..

 

Now, rather than treating packages as packages (ie things that can be upgraded) Nokia have jumped back in time and are only releasing Maemo firmware in large lumps.. if there is a bug in the calendar application, they could release an updated package that would install via the built in app manager.. but it seams they want to treat Maemo like Symbian.. so you could be in a for a long wait between updates.. But maybe that will change.. point is, Maemo gives them the ability to change their release practises..

 

Got to bare in mind, the Nexus one was released at the start of Jan and an update was out less than a month later..  Nokia have a long way to go to match release schedules like that!

 

Maemo5 is not the final product.. So neither is the N900.. Nokia have said that Maemo6 is what they are aiming for.. something which will give a much more polished product.. but don't expect the N900 to be a Maemo 6 candidate..

 

People think this is bad.. but it applies to Symbian^3 which was released a few days ago..

No existing Nokia device is going to support that.. There are still Nokia S60 5th edition handsets that haven't had a firmware release in a year because Nokia don't release firmware for variants..

the UK blue unbranded sim free 5800 is stuck on v21 of its firmware.. where as other 5800 handsets are on v40!! Seriously.. they do like having completely different firmwares for phones.. even if the difference is the colour of the case.. it is frankly unworkable when there are 300 different "types" of a single handset..

 

The N900 introduced this to Maemo.. prior to that there was Maemo for the N810.. Now there are 5 different versions of Maemo 5 for the N900.. TWO are in the UK alone?!?!?!?! incredibly unnecessary and only goes to delay support for users.. What ever reason Nokia think is justifying that, I'm not sure a single "end user" would possibly aggree.. but that is a different matter

 

So.. Symbian is a much more polished phone product, and maybe the Symbian foundation's open source initiatives will help.. but Nokias Symbian handsets are particularly feeble these days.. tiny OS flash, not graphics acceleration, such unreliable firmware

 

(the alarm clock on ALL my Symbian S60-5th edition devices goes off at the wrong time.. and one of them can't reliably use my choice of ring tones.. seriously.. how do they manage to introduce these problems.. it would be like Microsoft introducing a problem into notepad.. something that has worked for so long!!)

 

With Maemo, I'm more than happy (for the moment) to put up with the quirks and uneasy operation at times because it is very clearly an evolving platform that has new things to discover and who's hood can be removed to reveal its inner workings for all your hacking and exploritory joy!

 

Symbian is a working product but has lost so much of it's appeal due to the lengths Nokia and Symbian went to to suck the joy out of the platform...

 

It really depends what you want.. if you want a phone that works.. then Symbian or in fact any other phone might be worth a look..

 

If you want to really play, the N900 is about the most interesting device I've come across in a long time
