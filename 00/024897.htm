jeeped say:

In the following example, I used a 'helper column' to get the first instance of each name in column B. After that, it was a simple matter to use COUNTIF() to count all of the 1's in column C.

 

In C2 I put in the function =COUNTIF(B$2:B2,B2) and filled down to accomadate the names in column B. In B8 I used =COUNTIF(C2:C7,1) to count all of the 1's in column C.

Note the use of both relative and absolute referencing in  =COUNTIF(B$2:B2,B2). This is used so that the base row (B$2) will not move, while the expanding row (B2) will grow as it is copied down the column.

Remove/reset lock screen PIN

I forgot my pin on my lock screen on my Lumia 1520, how can I reset it.

*** Moderator's note: Changed title from "recover my microsoft password" to "Remove/reset lock screen PIN". ***

joe men say:

Hello,

Regarding your concern, you will have to hard reset your Lumia 1520 and it will remove the lock screen PIN that you've set up.

Important: Hard resetting your phone will delete all the data stored in it.

To hard reset your phone, do the following:

  1. Make sure that the device is powered on and have full battery.
  2. Press and hold the Volume down and Power buttons at the same time until you feel a vibration (about 10-15 seconds).
  3. When you feel the vibration, release the buttons, and then immediately press and hold the Volume down until you see a large exclamation mark.
  4. Once the exclamation mark appears, press the following four buttons in this order: Volume up, Volume down, Power and Volume down. Your phone should now reset and restart itself. (It might take a while for the reset to finish.).

Feel free to post back if you need further assistance.

As far as I know, Access creates a temporary file in the folder

The file has a .tmp extension and its filename is not necessarily recognizable, probably something resembling
