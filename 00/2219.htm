mike h.. say:

Hi,

 

If you select the bottom formula in Col N you will see a small black box in the bottom right corner, this is call the fill handle.

 

Double click the fill handle and the formula in Col N will fill down as far as there are data in column M

 

If that doesn't solve the problem if because (say) you have no data in column M then you can attach this small piece  of code to a button on your worksheet

 

Dim LastRow As Long
LastRow = Cells(Cells.Rows.Count, "C").End(xlUp).Row
Range("N2:N" & LastRow).FillDown

 

Change Col C to the one that contains the data you want to fill down to

charles kenyon say:
Search for the pillcrow (¶) by searching for ^p. You can find this by clicking on "More" and then on special. Line breaks are ^l.

I would recommend (using a copy of your document) replacing ^p^p with xxyxx. Then replace ^p with nothing. Then replace xxyxx with ^p. See what that does for you.

You can also run a replace for five spaces with ^t and then for two spaces with one. Run the two for one several times to clear out unnecessary spaces.

linley meslier say:
Hi, It is not possible to move emails from an Inbox to another on the device. The POP3 accounts on the phone requires a data plan or another type of internet connection to sync; which you oviously have. So, a new email that you send will use whichever connection you have to send it and receive emails. The Outlook account on the device can also use a wireless connection to Send/Receive emails if your device is connected to an Exchange Server. In your config, you sync with Outlook on your desktop. Therefore, you cannot send an email without connecting the phone to your computer. What happens when you type a new email and hit Send, it just sits in the Outbox folder waiting for you to connect to the PC. I recommend you do not sync emails from the PC to your device and keep the POP3 on the phone. This way you'll be able to sync wirelessly. Use Outlook desktop to view your emails when you're at home/Work. Hope this helps you understand your configuration better. Linley.
