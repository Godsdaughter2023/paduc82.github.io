In the case of Windows Phone, it's true that renaming your email address will require a hard reset of the phone, which erases all personal content. In fact, it's clearly stated on the page. I know that's little comfort when your device is no longer working, but I'm just saying...

Anyway, for those considering renaming your Hotmail account, you may want to think about just reserving an alias instead. Aliases provide many of the same benefits of renaming your account, but without the risks to your associated devices. People can still send email to the new alias, and you can reply and send mail from the alias via the Outlook.com UI. Apps on devices such as Windows Phone, however, can only send mail from the primary address associated with the account.

That's not to say you can't rename your account back to your old address, but whether that old address will be easily available is questionable. When you originally rename your account, your former email address is converted to an alias of your new one, so it's not exactly available for you to reclaim. I've heard some folks say that they've successfully deleted an alias and then immediately used it to rename their account, while others have indicated that released aliases aren't provisioned for reuse for up to 60 days. Your mileage may vary. You may be better off just resetting your phone and continuing with your current email address.

If you want to give it a shot, you can find the tools to manage your aliases at https://account.live.com/ManageAssocIds, and the page to rename your account is at https://account.live.com/ChangeId.

Best of luck!

Set textbox value to 0 for each record on continuous form - Microsoft Community

ordnance1 say:

Set textbox value to 0 for each record on continuous form

On my continuous form I have a bound textbox  txt_ChangeTest.

What I would like to do is to have the value of the textbox changed to 0 when the form is opened, I was able to get the first textbox to change by using:

Me.txt_ChangeTest.Value = 0

But how can I get this to set the textbox value to 0 on all records displayed on the continuous form

dirk goldgar say:

Probably you would execute an update query against the form's recordsource table or query.  If, for example, your form is based directly on a table named "Table1", and the control "txt_ChangeTest" is bound to a field in that table named "ChangeTest", then you might have code in the form's Open event like this:

    Private Sub Form_Open(Cancel As Integer)

        CurrentDb.Execute "UPDATE Table1 SET ChangeTest = 0", dbFailOnError

    End Sub

If your form is based on a query that does some filtering of the table, then you have to be sure to include that filtering in the update query, so as only to update the records that would be displayed on the form.

Doing something like this, you have to bear in mind that you run the risk of accidentally updating records you didn't mean to update.  For that reason, this sort of thing is most commonly done when the form is bound to a temporary or work table, and not to a main table.

john w. vinson say:

Just remember that there is no data stored in the Form. The Form is just a window, a tool to manage data in your Tables.

To set all the records' ChangeTest value to zero you will need an Update Query to update those records in the table. Depending on your needs, this Update query could be run without any criteria to update all the records in the entire Table to zero, or with criteria matching those used to populate the Form to just update the displayed values.

This would seem to be a rather odd thing to do - changing the data in many records by just opening a form to look at them!? Could you explain the meaning of the ChangeTest field and why it needs to be zeroed out on such a hair-trigger basis?
