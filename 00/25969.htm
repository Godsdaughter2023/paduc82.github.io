bill '-) say:

Well, it's up to you of course. I've upgraded  (Lumia 830). I've not had the heating problem you have, nor a shorter battery life (well not that I've noticed). I find there is little new in Windows 10 that I value and many of the "enhancements" do not seem to be improvements, just changes.

As it's had no adverse effects I'm not inclined to waste my time reverting, but if I had two Lumia phones (one on 10 & the other on 8.1), I'd prefer them to be on the same version and this would force me to choose between them. On balance I'd go for 8.1, but that may just be familiarity.

For me, the only real downside to not upgrading is that at some stage MS will stop supporting 8.1 and may make it harder for us to remain on 8.1 (probably unintentionally, but maybe deliberately).

The situation with WP8.1 & WP10, is very much like that between Window 7 and Windows 10 on a PC, apart from touch screen support, little has been added which makes Windows 10 a more productive environment than Windows 7. Maybe I'm just old and cynical, but I really do wonder why MS have bothered. The changes they've made have very little practical advantages and the cosmetic tat (the most noticeable change) is of little value or interest to me. I suspect the answer is that Windows 10 is more convenient for them and the cosmetic changes are their attempt to make the user community feel they are getting something out of it.

On the whole, the Window 10 experience is underwhelming. Naturally this may change over time as further upgrades are added and familiarity increases. Hopefully we'll all be pleasantly surprised, but I for one won't be holding my breath.

Bill
