larryyu_msft3110 say:

hi labinz8665,

below are the answers to your questions:

q1: even though we've never had any type of retention set in the past on our user mailboxes, does this mean it will start to delete their mail or aren't these specifically applied yet?

a: i’d like to explain that mailbox items will obey the default retention policy. however, you needn’t worry that your emails will be deleted after a week because there are three types of retention tags:

a. default policy tag which is automatically applied to the entire mailbox. usually, the default tag is ‘default 2 year move to archive’. it means that all items without a retention tag applied directly won’t be deleted in 2 years unless you delete them manually. they will move to archive after 2 years if you have enabled the archive feature.

b. retention policy tag which is automatically applied to the default folder (it belongs to the tags that are applied directly). for example, the 30 days delete tag applies to the junk email and deleted items folders. it means that items in these folders will be deleted after 30 days, and you can choose to recover them.

c. personal tag which needs to manually assign to items and folders (it belongs to the tags that are applied directly). for example, you can right-click a folder or a single email to assign the personal tags to it. they will be deleted after 7 days if you assign the 1 week delete tag to them. it means personal tags allow users to determine how long an item should be retained even the item has had a default policy tag or a retention policy tag applied.

you can find more information about these tags in the article below:

https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/dd297955(v=exchg.150).aspx#rt

q2: why wouldn't ours have started with "no policy" since we're new to office 365?

a: exchange creates the retention policy default mrm policy in your exchange online organization. the policy is automatically applied to new users in exchange online. we need to manually change it to ‘no policy’ if we want. thanks for your understanding.

q3: is any kind of retention active and when it is does it act on the "cloud account first and then sync it back to the user's local outlook 2013 and also delete the emails?

a: personal tags should do this. you can right-click on a folder or an email and then assign one to it like the screenshot shows below:
