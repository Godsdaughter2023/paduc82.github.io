lemp say:

Open Network and Sharing Center (click Start, type network and sharing in the Search box, and press Enter)

Click the link at the left to "Manage wireless networks"

Delete the entry for your network

Close the page and attempt to connect to the network again.

If the above doesn't help, you probably are using some third-party application such as Intel ProSet or Dell's Wireless Connection Manager to configure your wireless network adapter.  You'll have to open that utility, delete the profile, and then connect again.  Right-click on the icons in the Notification Area (near the time display) to determine which is the network configuration utility.  Don't forget to click the up-pointing triangle in the Notification Area to show all of the icons.
