The INACCESSIBLE_BOOT_DEVICE . This bug check indicates that the Microsoft Windows operating system has lost access to the system partition during startup.

Hi

Codec:

The name codec is short for coder-decoder, which is what a codec does. Most audio and video formats use some sort of compression so that they don't take up a huge amount of disk space. Audio and video files are compressed with a particular codec when they are created/saved and then decompressed by the codec when they are played back.

Codecs include .MPEG, .AVI, .WMV, DIVX, .MP4, etc, etc, for video files and .WAV, .AIFF, .MP3, etc, for audio files. Codecs can also be used to compress streaming media (live audio and video) which makes it possible to broadcast a live audio or video clip over a broadband Internet connection.

Codecs - Frequently Asked Questions:

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/codecs-frequently-asked-questions#codecs-frequently-asked-questions=windows-7

The bottom line is that when you use a particular media file that has been created using a particular codec, that codec must be installed to play the video file.

You need to try and isolate what you are doing, starting a program, media application, game, utility, or other application when this error occurs. This will reveal which component is causing the problem.

You cannot 'get rid' of the "RegSvr32" (Microsoft Register Server). Any .dll or ActiveX components installed in Windows use this component to register the .dll (Dynamic Link Library) in Windows before it can be used.

First you need to understand that your 8 year old laptop is statistically in its latter years.  50% of laptops are no longer usable after 5 years.

The sound problem may be the beginning of a hardware issue.  If your laptop has an audio output for head phones, you can likely hook a set of speakers in to that.  If the problem is the internal speaker(s), that would help.  If the problem is the sound generation, it would not.

The missing device in Device manager is usually the SD card reader and is not really essential.

You already replaced the most common failure in laptops -- the hard drive.  The next most likely thing is the DVD drive.  That is fairly easily rectified with an external drive.  You can get them for about $30.  Get one that takes its power from the USB port so it does not need a separate power supply.  They work well.
