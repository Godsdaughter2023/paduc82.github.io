a. user say:

I understand what you are trying to do, but I should say that my experience with packaging is primarily with Word packages, not Excel packages. I don't know how much of what I am about to say is applicable to Excel, but as far as I can see, you have actually worked all this out already.

With Word, the main problem with this scenario is that when you are working in VBA with an open document, the document file is locked and you cannot look inside the package to work directly (at the XML level) with the parts and relationships in it. To do that, you would have to close the Active document, unzip it, work with the parts/relationships, re-zip it, then re-open (if appropriate). You can only work with the things inside the package that are exposed by the Word object model.

Assuming Excel is the same, you will have to make a choice as to whether you
 a. keep your images outside the workbook and use VBA along the lines you have suggested or
 b. close the Workbook, unzip it, work with the parts and relationships, zip it, and then re-open it. 

As far as I can see, when you insert a picture in a header/footer,
 a. Excel does not let you paste an item in. And that's despite the fact that...
 b. ...Excel does not record the file/path name of the original (except for a name derived from the file name)
 c. Excel puts a copy of the .jpg (or whatever) in the /media folder
 d. Excel creates a .vml file in the Drawings folder and a relationship from that tot he media file
 e. Excel creates a relationship from the relevant sheet.xml to the .vml file, and puts reference to that relationship in the sheet.xml

My assumption would be that in order to re-use an image in a header,
 a. the .xlsx would have to have a copy of all the necessary .jpg s (or whatever), and all the necessary .vml files. 
 b. you would have at least to make the correct relationships to existing parts. Excel should not really care where the parts are as long as the relationships are correctly defined. In other words, if you can keep all the parts you need in a "myparts" folder, you may find that Excel does not discard them when they are not needed. If Excel discards unreferenced parts wherever they are, then I can imagine that you might be able to regenerate the .vml parts, but you would presumably have to put the images outside the package.
 c.  OTOH it seems to me that the simplest way to store the parts you need would be to create one sheet for each image, or if the .vml is the same for each header/footer area, 1 sheet for each 6 images (or whatever).

If you do (c), then I think that at some point during your processing, you'll have to close the .xlsx, unzip the package, fix the refs and rels, perhaps delete unused and unreferenced parts yourself (at least I do not think you will need to adjust the [Content_Types].xml, rezip the file, then reopen it.
