matthew gaylen say:

section numbering problem

I have a document that is 39 pages. 

On page 25 I added a short section of three columns to accommodate numbering for an illustration, then I went back to one column, normal style.

So up to page 25 is section #1.

Page 26 starts on section #3 and the page numbering changes to "start at page #1" and I cannot figure out how to get it to continue on to page 26...

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

With the selection in the header\footer of Section 3, go to the Insert tab of the ribbon, and expand the Page Number dropdown and then click on Format Page Numbers and select the Continue from previous section radio button.

If you have a Continuous Section Break on Page 25, you may need to temporarily change that to a Next Page Section Break, so that you can also access the header\footer of that section and change the page number start setting in that section as well.  After doing that, change the start of that Section back to Continuous.  To make those changes, go to the Layout tab of the Page Setup dialog.

Alternatively, you can use a macro containing the following code:

With ActiveDocument
    For i = 2 To .Sections.Count
        With .Sections(i).Footers(1).PageNumbers.RestartNumberingAtSection = False
    Next i
End With

suzanne s. barnhill say:
You do not have to have the insertion point in the header or footer to format page numbers, nor do you have to change the Continuous break to Next Page to get a header or footer to access. You can place the insertion point anywhere in the section between Continuous breaks, and use Insert | Header & Footer | Page Number | Format Page Numbers... to access the dialog.

john w. vinson say:

Yes, you're going about this in the wrong way, in at least a couple of ways!

For one thing your table structure is wrong. It's a common error in questionnaire type applications; it's so tempting to have one field per question. But a normalized design uses three tables: a table of Questionnaires (with one record for each time someone answers the questionnaire); a table of Questions, with one record per question (21 questions in your case, until you decide to add two or three more questions); and a table of Answers, with one record for each time an individual question is answered.

The reason your combo boxes are not working as you expect is that they're either all bound to the same field (in the combo box's Control Source property) or they're unbound (nothing at all in the Control Source).
