swischner say:
Just to add a bit information for clarification. Microsofts naming conventions regarding Office have gotten really confusing. Here is the thing:

- Office 365 is not a program or bundle of applications at all. It's just the name for a subscription model. Based on the type of subscription you buy, it *may* contain a full version of Office 2016 (Word, Excel, Powerpoint...) as well as a few other services (e.g. storage space on OneDrive, hosted Exchange server for business variations of Office 365 and so on). If you have a subscription (which you would have to pay monthly or yearly), you also get a local, installable copy of Office 2016.

- Office 2016 is the generic name of the application bundle that consists of Word, Excel, Powerpoint and so on..

Now, there are in fact two versions of Office 2016: the one that you get as part of an Office 365 subscription or a perpetual, standalone license that you only have to pay once at purchase time. In theory, those two Office 2016 are identical. In fact, they aren't. The standalone version (I assume that is the one you are owning and talking about) is technically stuck at the status of its first release in 2015. It gets only security patches and "serious" bugfixes. The version that is installed with an Office 365 subscription, on the other hand, gets new features on a regular basis.
