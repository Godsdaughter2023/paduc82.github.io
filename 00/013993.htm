FAQ: Mouse Mischief add-in for PowerPoint

Microsoft Mouse Mischief is a new add-in for PowerPoint 2007/2010 available beginning April 30, 2010. The Mouse Mischief add-in allows teachers to work with Microsoft Office PowerPoint to make interactive presentations that engage every student in the classroom. With Mouse Mischief, teachers can create multiple-choice, matching, and drawing question slides that students, each with their own mouse, can answer on a shared screen.

 

Q: How much does it cost?

A: Mouse Mischief is available as a free download, found here.

 

Q: Why is Microsoft offering it free of charge?

A:  We believe that by putting Mouse Mischief in the hands of more teachers and schools around the world, we can support our efforts as a company to make Microsoft technology more relevant for the classroom.

 

Q: How can I get support for Mouse Mischief V1.0?

A: Questions about Mouse Mischief should be asked here in the “Creating and Formatting Presentations and Slides” forum on Answers.

 

Q: What else do you have to buy to use Mouse Mischief in the classroom?

A:  To have a Mouse Mischief presentation up and running with the best performance possible, all you need is:

·         a Windows 7 or Windows Vista-enabled PC running PowerPoint 2007/2010

·         the free-of-charge Mouse Mischief software

·         up to 25 mice, and USB expansion hub(s) (powered hubs are required for more than 8 mice).

Note: If you are a Windows XP user, you can use all the functionality of Mouse Mischief with approximately five mice.

 

Q: Why isn’t Mouse Mischief just offered as part of PowerPoint?  Are there plans to do so in the future?

A:  We wanted to release Mouse Mischief now, when it was ready, to give teachers the chance to benefit from this software immediately.   We are exploring the possibility of Mouse Mischief being fully integrated into PowerPoint in the future, though we do not have anything further on that to share at the moment.

A:  Mouse Mischief offers a variety of benefits over other common classroom response systems.

  • With technology like clickers, your options for response are limited to multiple-choice—A,B,C, or D questions, and so on.  This is limiting to both the creativity of the curriculum presented and the collaborative possibilities in the classroom.  With Mouse Mischief, teachers can create a broad variety of multiple-choice, matching, and drawing slides that encourage students to work and learn together.   • Clickers and other interactive response systems tend to be expensive., Mouse Mischief uses the cheapest common peripheral that most schools already have—standard computer mice, allowing for a substantial cost savings.
  • Clickers are accompanied by proprietary systems teachers have to learn—with a familiar authoring environment in PowerPoint, teachers have little to no learning curve to get started.

As Rich indicated, turning off Track Changes & resolving those in the document is the first concern if the feature has been use.

Before executing his 2nd point, though, you might try this to avoid the concerns he mentioned;

  1. Remove the Bullet Formatting from the Table content
  2. Select the Table
  3. Go to Table> Convert> Convert Table to Text (or use the button on the Layout contextual tab for Tables)

This method often removes corruption from the table structure without having to rebuilding an entire Section or an entire document.
