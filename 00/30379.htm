It depends on how Windows 10 was originally configured on your computer.

If its a full retail store bought license online or offline, it is transferable to a new computer or motherboard.

If its the free upgrade from a retail store bought Windows 7 or Windows 8 license, it is transferable to a new computer or motherboard.

If its the free upgrade from a OEM preinstalled Windows 7 or Windows 8 license, it is NOT transferable to a new computer or motherboard.

Installation with a local account:

There are many factors that cause this issue. Driver corruptions, compatibility issues, Bluetooth support service corruption, Power management settings etc.

To get a better understanding of the issue, please reply to the following questions.

  1. What is the make and model of your computer?

Method 1.

Check for Bluetooth support service.

  1. Press Windows key + letter ‘R.’

  2. Type Services.msc and hit OK.

  3. Look for the service called Bluetooth support services.

  4. Right click on the service and choose properties.

  5. Set the Startup type to Automatic.

  6. Start the service.

  7. Hit Apply and OK.

Method 2.

Check for Bluetooth power Management settings.

  1. Press Windows key + letter ‘R.’

  3. Look for Bluetooth adaptors and expand it.

  4. Right click on the Bluetooth driver and go to properties.

  5. Select the Power management tab.

  6. Under that, uncheck the option “Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power.”

  7. Click OK and check if the issue persists.
