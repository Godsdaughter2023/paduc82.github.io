russ valentine say:

Certainly. Methods for migrating Outlook data are posted here daily and in many Help files.

It is unwise, however, to "upgrade" an Outlook version because you think it will correct problems. It won't. It will only make them worse. Fix the problems first. Then make sure you instal a new Outlook version clean, not over an older one.

The file you need to migrate your data is your Personal Folders file (*.pst) It's where all the mail, calendar, contacts etc are stored.

Take a look at these pages for info on Outlook data backup or transfer:
http://www.slipstick.com/config/move_outlook.asp
http://www.howto-outlook.com/Howto/backupandrestore.htm
http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/assistance/HA010771141033.aspx

Note that some of the information on the Microsoft page is outdated and contains errors, particularly when applied to Outlook 2003, 2007 or 2010. Specifically:
1. Ignore any advice to copy your PST file to Outlook's default location to connect it to your profile. Doing so will often corrupt your Outlook profile. Copy it anywhere BUT the default location and then open it within your Outlook profile.
2. Ignore any advice that tells you to use export or import to transfer an entire Outlook data file. That has never been sound advice, but the process has become too deeply flawed to be trusted.

Also, when you use manual configuration for your mail accounts in Outlook 2010 (always preferable) it has a nice new option that lets you connect to a pre-existing data file when creating a new profile instead of having to jump through all the hoops of creating a new file, connecting to the pre-existing file, then disconnecting the new file.

jal35 say: I do not have a direct answer to your question but some of your issues are caused by the multiple tracking protection lists you are using. Sites that are allowed in all tracking protection lists (except a manual personal tracking protection list) trump all disallowed sites. If a site is allowed in one of your lists and blocked on another, it is allowed. Go to your tracking protection lists, click on one of the lists and then more info in the box below.  Check the list, usually toward the bottom you will see "+d" before a string, this allows the adds through even if there is a "-d" on another list and there is no way to modify a downloaded tracking protection list of which I am aware as it would be nice to just create a list without the "+d" items.  Pick the list you like best, I use "Easy Standard Tracking List" and live what they allow.  I am sure there is a workaround but I haven't figured it out yet.
