In my opinion, these backup solutions are short term or interim solutions....for example, if you are on the road and have to perform a hard reset...you can restore your PIM data to the handheld without having to sync to a computer.  But these solutions won't give you easy access to the data if you have no way to restore the data to a mobile device (the device is lost, broke or stolen). 

In addition to backing up the mobile device, you should also sync the data regularly to Outlook on your computer.  Then backup the Outlook.pst file regularly.  Then if a sync goes bad, or if the device is lost or stolen, you have some redundancy and don't risk losing your data.

      Dim result As Double

      For i = 1 To 11

    Private Sub cbxPart_Change()

      Dim R As Range

      'Find the name in column D

      Set R = Sheets("Inventory").Columns("D").Find( _

        cbxPart.Value, LookIn:=xlValues, LookAt:=xlWhole)

      'Clear quantity

      txtQty = ""

      'Found?

      If R Is Nothing Then

        'Clear price

        txtPrice = ""

      Else

        'Fill the price

        txtPrice = Format(R.Offset(, 1), "$0.00")

      End If

    End Sub

    Private Sub txtPrice_Change()

      Dim Price, Quantity

      'Errors off, that's easier as to parse the textbox contents

      On Error Resume Next

      'Assume failure

      txtAmt = ""

      'Do the calculation

      Price = CDbl(Replace(txtPrice, "$", ""))

      txtAmt = Format(Price * Quantity, "$0.00")
