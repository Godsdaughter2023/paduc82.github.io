Hi Aries,

From your description, you are using the new OneDrive sync client in Windows 10 to sync files and you want to hide the OneDrive – Personal or OneDrive – xxx company icon from the Windows Explorer.

To do that, you can follow the steps below.

1. Open the Registry Editor by clicking the Windows + R button and enter regedit > OK.
2. Please navigate to HKEY_CLASSES_ROOT > CLSID. Inside the folder, please search (CTRL + F) for OneDrive.
3. Find next until you find one that has the OneDrive – Personal (The shortcut in the Windows Explorer linked to your personal OneDrive) or OneDrive – xxx company (Linked to your OneDrive for Business account)  as the Default value and looks something like the screenshot below:

4. Modify the System.IsPinnedToNameSpaceTree value to 0 > OK.
5. Restart your computer to see the outcome.

WARNING: Using Registry Editor incorrectly can cause serious problems that may require you to reinstall your operating system. Microsoft cannot guarantee that problems resulting from the incorrect use of Registry Editor can be solved. Use Registry Edit at your own risk.
