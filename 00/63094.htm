ccazang say:

Mr Goetz,

 

There is nothing wrong with your graphic card drivers. The TV is the one that renders the video file not the graphic adapter on your PC. The reason for which you have a bad video quality is the limited support of media formats that your TV supports. Usually, Sony Bravia TVs have a limited set of media formats supported and because of this, most of the time the PlayTo and Win7 will send transcoded versions of the video file to the TV. Usually, the way how PlayTo works is the following:

- PlayTo queries the TV for the media formats supported

- If the TV supports the media type that you are trying to render, the PlayTo will send the native stream to your TV. The quality will be the one provided by your video file.

- If the TV does not support the native format, the Win7 also offers transcoded versions of the same video file: MPEG 2 and WMV related versions.

- PlayTo preffers to make the playback possible anytime so it will choose a transcoded version if the native format is not supported. DLNA mandates support for MPEG NTSC or MPEG PAL formats, so these will be the common denominator.

I have tested several Sony Bravia TVs in the last couple of years of PlayTo development, and many times the playback was transcoded. I assume that is the case for you as well. Unfortunatelly, there is nothing you can do, unless Sony decides to support your media file nativelly. To make things worst, the qulity of the transcoded version depends very much of the quality of the native files (win7 transcode to lower resolution than the native. It will not upscale). If the native file is HD content, the resolution of the transcoded stream will be good enough for a 40" TV. If the stream resolution is SD or lower than that (usually streams that you can download from BitTorrent), the transcoded resolution will be lower than that. On a big screen TV, this will be streched and the quality will be very bad. The lower the resolution of the native file, and the higher the resolution of the TV, the bad your experience will be.

So, could you please tell me what is the type of your media file?

Hope this helps
