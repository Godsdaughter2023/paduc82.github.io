a. user say:

a little more detail on how the licensing for digital games works:

on 360 you would get a license to the gamertag that bought it (which can be played on any console with that gamertag as long as you are connected to xbox live), then you get a license to the console, so anyone can play on that console, offline or online.  you could do a license transfer, which just took all the licenses from your gamertag and assigned them to one console. for example if you bought a new system and kept your gamertag, so you can keep all your games.  however you could buy games on your secondary console with your gamertag, then go to play on your main console, and someone else could play with you using the license for the second console.  this is one way people do gamesharing, which is probably against MS policy.  of course the gamertag is the main owner of the licenses.

on xbox one it's very similar, but slightly different.  you get the same gamertag license, then you get a console license as well, but it goes to whatever console you have set as your Home Console.  this makes it so people can't go around to friends houses buying games, and having stuff license to random different console.  everything is licensed to one console and one gamertag, but you can change the home console at any time.  that would be the equivalent of doing the license transfer on 360.

what this means for you:

WWE is licensed to a different gamertag, that you don't have access to.  it also was once licensed to this console, which is how it was downloaded, but most likely the owner has set a different console as their home console.  so there is no way for you to use this game ever, without buying your own license (or physical disc copy).  just having a game on the console doesn't mean you own it.
