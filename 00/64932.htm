Hello douglas19672005,

Thank you for posting to the Microsoft Answers Forum.

We do not have your username or password. You setup your username/password when you installed your system and it is not reported to Microsoft.

Please review Microsoft's Policy on forgotten passowords. In short Microsoft will be unable to assist you with a lost or otherwise forgotten password. That being said if you have a Password Reset Disk or you know an Administrator account password you will be able to successfully access the system.

If you forget the administrator password and don't have a password reset disk or another administrator account, you won't be able to reset the password. If there are no other user accounts on the computer, you won't be able to log on to Windows and will need to re-install Windows.

How to use a password reset disk to reset your password:
First and foremost you need to have created a password reset disk before you’ve forgotten your password. If you did not create a password reset disk skip to the next group of troubleshooting.

1.  If you have typed the wrong password, you’ll receive a message that says your user name or password is incorrect.   
2.  Click OK when you see this message.
3.  Now click on Reset password, and then follow the instructions.
4.  Once complete you will have reset your password, and can now successfully login.

How to use an administrator account to reset your password:

NOTE: If you use another administrator account to reset the password, you will permanently lose access to any e-mail message or encrypted files that are on that account.

1.    Click Start, click Control Panel, click User Accounts and Family Safety, click User Accounts, and click Manage another account.    

2.    Select the account with the password you want to change or remove.
    
3.    Click Remove the password, and read the onscreen warning message prior to proceeding.

4.    Confirm removal of the password, and you should now be able to successfully access the account.


Third-party password reset tools…

Some third-party companies claim to be able to circumvent passwords that have been applied to files and features that Microsoft programs use. For legal reasons, we cannot recommend or endorse any one of these companies. If you want help to break or to reset a password, you can locate and contact a third-party company for this help. You use such third-party products and services at your own risk.
