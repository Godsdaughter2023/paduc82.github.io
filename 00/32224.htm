canadian tech say:
  1. Start globe (bottom left)
  2. Control Panel
  3. Network and Internet
  4. Network sharing center
  5. Manage wireless networks
  6. Find your network in the list, remove it
  7. Click on the 5 bar icon and find your newly discovered network
  8. Enter the security password for the network

That should work.  If it does not, 

  1. Click start globe again
  2. Type device into the text box that pops up, click on Device manager
  3. In device Manager, click on the arrow next to network adapters
  4. Right click on your wireless network item, choose uninstall
  5. Restart your computer
  6. It should then discover the wireless device and install it.
  7. Again, find your wireless network and enter the security password
  8. If it still does not connect, I would presume the wireless hardware facility itself has failed.
  9. You can buy a USB wireless device for about $20 that will replace its function. They are a nice thing to have.  They usually work better and are portable.

job sam varkey say:

Hi GregTomLou,

 

I would suggest you to follow the below steps and check if it helps.

 

Step 1: Reinstall USB (Universal Serial Bus) controllers.

 First, please disconnect all USB devices and then perform the following steps:

a) Click "Start", type in Device Manager the Search bar and press "Enter". Click Continue if necessary.

b) In Device Manager, double click to expand "Universal Serial Bus controllers", right click on the "Host Controller", click "Uninstall" and click "OK".

c) Repeat the step b to uninstall all items under "Universal Serial Bus controllers".
