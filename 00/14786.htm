gord dibben say:

Sub DeleteDublicates()
    Dim i As Integer
    Application.ScreenUpdating = False
    For i = 1 To ActiveSheet.Cells(1, Columns.Count).End(xlToLeft).Column Step 1
        ActiveSheet.Range(Cells(1, i), Cells(Cells(Rows.Count, i).End(xlUp).Row, i)) _
                .RemoveDuplicates Columns:=1, Header:=xlNo
    Next i
    Application.ScreenUpdating = True
End Sub

Your instructions for running the code will do the job.

Hello Steve

Select the cell(s) to format rightclick the selection, and open the time group and select the desired format(hh:mm)

 

With this format excel will show 05:45 when you enter 5:45.It is the only way you can enter a time in excel. The format is only used to tell excel how to show values as time when you enter a true time! If you enter 5.75 excel will just see it as a decimal number.

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:
The phone cannot sync directly with the desktop Outlook 2016. To have the phone and the desktop show the same data, just connected them both via Exchange to the same Outlook.com mailbox. Your Android phone will allow you to choose "Corporate" or "Exchange" as an account type when you add accounts in Settings. Since both the phone and the desktop Outlook will sync with the mailbox, anything you do on one will be synched with the other. This means, though, that you'll have to move your POP and IMAP data to the Outlook.com account if you want that data to sync between the desktop and mobile.

This same approach applies to an iPhone as well. It, too, has an "Exchange" account type in Settings>Mail, Calendar, Contacts.

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:
The Mail, Calendar, and Contacts apps on Android are all built in. There's nothing more to install. Let's assume you have an email address than ends in @outlook.com (which you'll need, either as a main address or as an alias to an account that uses some other non-Microsoft address as the username). To connect Outlook 2016 to that mailbox, open Outlook 2016, click File>Account Settings>Account Settings. On the Email tab, click New, then Next. Enter your name (what you want others to see in the From field when you send mail), your @outlook.com address, and your Outlook.com password - whatever you use to log into the web interface. Click Next. Outlook should go looking for the mailbox based on the address and configure it for you. When it has configured the account, it will tell you "Congratulations" and you can then click Finish. A set of folders will appear in the Navigation Pane that matches the folders you see when you log into
