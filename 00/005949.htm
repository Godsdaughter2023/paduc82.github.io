jay freedman say:

Double-clicking the template on the desktop does not open the template itself. It starts Word with a document based on the template. 

I suggested the New Document or Template button -- which you can put on either the ribbon or the Quick Access Toolbar -- because it's far more user-friendly than the File > New page in Word 2013. But even that won't give you access to a template on the desktop; it displays only templates in the Templates folder.

There's one more option, which displays such typical Microsoft convoluted logic that you probably wouldn't like it. If you go to File > Options > Save, and fill the "Default personal templates location" with the path to your desktop, that will add the label "PERSONAL" next to the label "FEATURED" in the File > New page. When you click PERSONAL, you'll see a folder icon labeled Desktop, and you can click that to see any templates and documents on your desktop. Clicking a template there will create a document based on the template. Unhelpfully, the PERSONAL page doesn't give you any way to tell which of the items it's showing are templates and which are documents; there are no extensions in the names, and the icons are all identical (unless you make the effort to save a thumbnail in each file).

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:
To specify a location on your computer for templates, go to File>Options>Advanced and scroll down to the General section of the dialog and then click on File Locations and select the User Templates or Work Group Templates items and click on the Modify button and browse to the desired location.

I think there might also be a bit of confusion, based on the show.  For some shows, specifically ones from BBC, what I have been referring to as a Season (that is, one year's worth of episodes) is called a Series.  So in your market it might be labeled as a Series Pass, just because of what that content provider calls "One year's worth" of episodes for that show.  I've seen this with shows like Dr Who and Top Gear, among others.  If we were to have some sort of box set containing multiple seasons, it would be pretty clearly marked showing what is included.
