Also had issues with not being able to access FTP site  with W10 & IE11  So while exploring both trying to solve my problem, I found out how to access FTP in File Explorer. 

This is in Layman terms as I am not an IT person.  Just self taught.   I clicked on the icon in the task bar (where you click to shut down the computer or to get to your programs that don't have a shortcut on the desktop.)  In there I saw "File Explorer"    I clicked on that. toward the top of that window it says "Quick access" next to a little blue star.  In that box I typed my FTP site address (ftp://yourdomain.com). Where I just typed address, it starts loading and changing to green, (on mine color is green)  Then a dialog box appears asking for user name and password.  Filled in those and the FTP site opened with all the file folders showing on the FTP site.

I have a short cut to "my computer" on my desk top. I clicked on that.  This accessed my C: drive. (If no short cut you can click on the Original location (File Explorer) to open the window for your C drive.)  You might have to click on "This PC"  to see the following (this PC is on the left side of that window/screen). I see folders at the top, then below that are "devices and drives"  I then clicked on "Local Disk (C:)"  all the folders I have on my laptop appears.  I then went to the folder with all my website files located and opened it up.  As a test I previously had created a folder with a test page (including pictures)  to see if I could upload it to the FTP site this way.  I highlighted that file in C drive and dragged that file to the FTP window which was on the right of my screen.  Files uploaded.
