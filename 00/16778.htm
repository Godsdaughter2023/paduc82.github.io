Cody is right. There is no formal process for "deactivation".

To transfer an installation, you are required by the terms of the license to make sure that it is unusable on the old machine. That means either you uninstall it from the old machine or make sure the old machine is unusable (ie it is dead).  But, the thing is, the activation system does NOT track uninstalls.

To complete the transfer to the new machine, install and activate Office.  If internet activation doesn't work (Too many installations), you have to do a phone activation.

When you call in, don't enter any info (you already know it doesn't work), just wait (a long time) until you are offered the option of talking to a "service" rep. iris9290 say:

Hi genom219,

 

Thanks for your post and welcome to the forum.

 

Please completely uninstall Nokia Suite from your PC by following the instructions below.

 

Windows XP:

  1. Click Start, point to Settings, then click Control Panel.
  2. Double-click Add/Remove Programs.
  3. Scroll through the list of installed applications, then click Nokia PC Suite
  4. Click the Add/Remove button.
  5. Click the Uninstall button from the Nokia PC Suite window, then follow the on-screen instructions until the setup wizard has completed removed the program.
  6. Restart your computer to complete the removal process.

Windows Vista / Windows 7:

  1. Click Start, then click Control Panel.
  2. Click Programs, then click Uninstall a program.
  3. Select Nokia PC Suite from the list of installed programs, then click Uninstall/Change.
  4. Confirm that you want to remove the Nokia PC Suite program from your PC.
  5. Restart your computer to complete the removal process.

Once Nokia Suite has uninstalled, please reinstall via this link. 

 

Now connect your phone with Nokia Suite and create a back up of your phone's data via Tools > Back up. Make sure not to tick the Setting box. 

 

Now disconnect from Nokia Suite and enter *#7370# into your phone and default security code 12345 (unless you've changed this code yourself).

 

Once the phone has rebooted, reconnect with Nokia Suite and click on the green arrow icon where an update will be displayed. Updating the software should work after a reset and reinstalling Nokia Suite, if you're still having issues after performing the above, use Nokia Software updater instead. Now go to Tools > Restore in Nokia Suite to restore the back up you've made. 

 

The phone should display your favourite contacts on the home screen now. Please get back to me if you manage to resolve the issue, otherwise I can provide further support.

 

Iris9290
