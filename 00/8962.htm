chuckchern---01 say:

For anyone who might still be fighting this, here's my experience. I just bought a brand new N8 and it came with a new SIM card. From the very beginning when I started the phone, the SIM card was not recognized. I was pretty certain the phone was faulty, especially after reading so many posts about others having the same issue. Just out of curiousity, I tried installing some old SIM cards that I have. Virtually all of them were recognized by the phone. After thinking about it, I decided the issue might be the thickness of the SIM cards. I have a set of high quality digital calipers for precision measurements of this type and sure enough, there was a 0.003 inch (0.07mm) difference between all of the old cards and the new card that didn't work. To test the theory, I thought of a way to make the new card thicker. I cleaned the back side of the card thoroughly and then applied some heavy duty Scotch shipping tape to this side of the SIM card, and accurately trimmed all excess tape from around the edges of the SIM card with a very sharp knife. This tape is exactly the thickness needed to make the new card thickness match the old cards. With the tape applied to the new card, the card now works in the phone. It appears that the SIM tray in this phone might be slightly off spec and very sensitive to even microscopic differences in SIM card thicknesses. Anyone who tries this should take great care to insure the tape sticks to the card. You don't want that tape to end up stuck in your SIM tray. Since the SIM card is rarely removed from the tray for most people, I wouldn't expect any issues with the tape, as long as a high quality product is used.

javed a say:

1.The cause of error:Actually two error

a) The Worksheet_Change event will fire at line Target.Offset(, 1) = Now.You should set EnableEvents to False to stop the trigerring of same event.

b) The Worksheet_Change will fire if any change occurs in any cell.But if the sheet is protected then this event will not be fired at all.Becasue Excel will prevent any change if sheet is protected.

 

2.I think you want the time entered in C no to change but value in B can change.There is a property Locked which can be used here.Just uncheck the Locked (from Protection Tab of Format cells) cells of B column befor starting

Also further assumed that one can overwrite previos entry.

 

Private Sub Worksheet_Change(ByVal Target As Range)

    Application.EnableEvents = False
    Sheet1.Unprotect Password:="Secret"

    If Target.Count > 1 Or IsEmpty(Target) Then Exit Sub
    If Not Intersect(Target, Range("B:B")) Is Nothing Then
        With Target.Offset(, 1)
            .Value = Now
            .NumberFormat = "HH:MM:SS"
            .Locked = True
        End With
    End If
    Sheet1.Protect Password:="Secret"
   
    Application.EnableEvents = True

End Sub
