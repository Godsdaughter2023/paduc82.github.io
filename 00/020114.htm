How, in an equation, do you select an entire column except the - Microsoft Community

mashuganah say:

How, in an equation, do you select an entire column except the header?

I have a spreadsheet in which the amount of data may vary from month to month.  In another spreadsheet in the workbook I have an equation that references the first.  In order to correctly count the variable amount of data from month to month I would normally select the entire column in the equation (e.g., =COUNTA(E:E).  However, there's a header row that would throw the equation off.

How do I set the equation to count the whole column except the header row?
jllatham say:

Simply subtract the value of any extra rows of information that you are certain will be in the included list:

=COUNTA(E:E)-1

Or if you had 3 rows at the top that might or might not be filled in for that column, you could do something like this:

=COUNTA(E:E)-COUNTA(E1:E3)
