The underlying problem is the unfortunate terminology created by Microsoft.

Sharing means what everyone means, sharing with other people.

But this requires that the notebook to be shared is stored on OneDrive (or SharePoint).

And Microsoft calls the necessary step of moving a local notebook to OneDrive also Sharing.

So the File -->Share feature has two variants: moving and (real) sharing.

Your user has omitted the 1st step (including at first the selection of the move target, OneDrive or SharePoint on the Share page).

imperplexed say:

DNS lookups (for MX or A records) can fail intermittently.  For infrequently-mailed-to domains, they are more likely to fail because it's unlikely that the domain will be in the DNS cache, so that an actual query to another DNS server is needed (which again, if it's not the authoritative name server, may or may not have it in cache).

 

Nonetheless, these failures should be infrequent, though when they occur, they generally result in an immediate bounce.  This contrasts with a failure to connect or complete a relay to another mail server, which generally simply causes a retry after some delay.

 

Now here's the catch:  every time the transfer is retried, there's a new DNS lookup, which means another chance for a DNS failure.  So a DNS failure (especially one that occurred after more than one hour after the message was sent) suggests as one possibility that connection attempts are getting rejected.  Probabilistic rejection of connection attempts from IP addresses that have been deemed to be "spammy" is one strategy for reducing spam, so this is a possible explanation.

 

Unfortunately, the infomation about IP addresses used for unsuccessful connection attempts (or for failed DNS lookups) is not generally available.  Also, even though other Yahoo users may be sending to you, their messages are probably going out through different Yahoo mail servers.

 

It seems that "outlook.com" and Yahoo can't get their acts together.  Things will work fine for months at a time, then begin to show erratic behavior or sometimes show more persistent failures.  If you need to have reliable email service (and unreliable email service is certainly not very useful), it seems that you need to avoid both of these email service providers.  I realize this is a huge nuisance, but these two entities refuse to work out these problems, so the end users are just stuck in the middle.
