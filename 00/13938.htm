stefan blom say:
Built-in building blocks are stored in the Built-In Building Blocks.dotx template in your Windows user profile. Try renaming or deleting the template with Word closed. Restart Word. If it still doesn't work, try repairing the installation.

jay freedman say:
Stefan, the default Built-In Building Blocks.dotx template doesn't contain any entries in the AutoText gallery. The AutoText entries won't be there unless you created them there, and removing the template from the user profile and letting Word replace it (from the copy in the Program Files area) won't restore the AutoText.

The default in the Create New Building Block dialog is to store AutoText and all other custom building blocks in the Normal.dotm template. If the current document is based on another template, you could change the Save In choice to that template. If the AutoText entries were in Normal.dotm or another template and are now missing from it, something has overwritten the old copy of the template with a new one that doesn't contain the entries. The only way to get them back is to restore an old version of the template from a backup.

johann eva say:

Hi Michael,

When a sign-in error message always prompts every time you launch OneNote, it is caused by an incorrect credential stored on your computer. To resolve your concern, we suggest that you remove the cached credential used by OneNote to refresh your connection to the server. To do this, follow the steps below:

1. Click Ask me anything.

2. Type Control Panel then click it to open.

3. Click User Accounts.

4. Click Manage Windows Credentials under Credential Manager.

5. Click Windows Credentials.

6. Under Generic Credentials,

  • Expand MicrosoftOffice16_Data:SSPI, click Remove, then click Yes when prompted to confirm.
  • Expand MicrosoftOffice16_Data:live:cid, click Remove, then click Yes when prompted to confirm.
  • Expand OneDrive Cached Credential, click Remove, then click Yes when prompted to confirm.

7. Sign-out your Microsoft account from your computer.

8. Open all your Internet browsers and clear all cache and cookies.

9. Restart your computer.

10. Log-in your Microsoft account to start syncing again.

Let us know if the solution above works for you.
