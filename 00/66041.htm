$Windows.~bt\Sources\Rollback\setupapi\setupapi.dev.log

The end of that file got corrupted while writing a log for one of my devices:  ASMedia USB3.1 eXtensible Host Controller

Just to note, when we say 'Xbox Live Region' we are referring to countries, United States, United Kingdom, France, etc. 

If you've downloaded over three times, and each one has failed, it could indicate an issue with the hard drive.

1. Clear the Xbox 360 cache, three times.  Turn the console off, and then turn it back on.

2. Delete the corrupted download.

3. Don't download this game again straight away.  Go to the marketplace, find a game demo, or a couple of game demo's.  Download these completely. When they are complete do not delete these demo's yet.

4. Try downloading the game again.  If it works, delete the demo\s you downloaded earlier.

If you're wondering why you are downloading demos, let me explain.  When you download anything on to a hard drive it will go to where the first available space is, if this hard drive has a hardware issue, the game may become corrupt going to this location.  Downloading the demo's first, will put these demo's where your game used to be.  Your game now resides on a different location on the hard drive.

If this works, problem solved, at least for now.  But I would suggest you replace your hard drive soon.
