john - project say:

John,

Unfortunately there really isn't a good way to do what you want. You could keep your subproject as the "test master" to which you make changes. If you save it under a different names, you will be able to insert it multiple times into the master, but I wouldn't recommend that approach since the whole structure is ripe for corruption.

I think your best bet is to keep everything in a single file. Depending on the changes needed for each of the test sequences, it may be possible to handle that with links between tasks, or if that isn't appropriate, a VBA routine could be created to take the edited data from one test group and replicate that into each of the other test groups.

John

john - project say:

John,

You're welcome and thanks for the feedback.

You are right that others have requested similar functionality. As I indicated previously a subproject can be inserted multiple times but each must be a separate file because the link structure cannot point to the same file more than once. However, the following process will work without the probability of file corruption.

1. Create a "master test" file.

2. Create a new blank file.

3. Insert the master test file into the new blank file and uncheck the "link to project" option in the lower right corner of the Insert Project window.

4. Repeat step 3 as many times as needed to create multiple identical test sequences

5. When a change is required, edit the master test file and follow steps 2, 3 & 4 again.

This process will create a static master each time and will not be prone to file corruption because there is no linked structure. If you attempt to do the above by creating a dynamic master (i.e. leave the "link to Project" option checked), you will first have to save the test master multiple times under a separate name for each before inserting. The big downside of attempting to do this as a dynamic master will unfortunately also guarantee file corruption. File corruption occurs in dynamic master structures when the user renames, moves, saves off, or replaces any of the files in the linked structure.

John

-Make sure that the Windows Firewall is enabled. No other firewall truly offers better protection or is needed. This one is perfectly fine.

 

-Make sure that all important/critical updates, including service packs for the operating system and programs are installed from Microsoft Update (Windows Update).

 

-Make sure Internet Explorer is at version 8 or 9 and updated with all patches.

 

-In Internet Explorer 8 or 9, use the SmartScreen Filter.

 

-Make sure that IE Internet Security settings are at least set to medium-high (default).

 

-Enable the pop-up blocker in IE.

 

-On Vista and Windows 7 make sure that User Account Control (UAC) ON and not running with elevated privileges.

-Make sure that installed applications, especially Adobe Acrobat, Adobe Flash, and Java are at their latest versions. Many vendors are regularly updating and patching for security holes.

 

-Never click through links from unknown sources and use caution even if they are from a "trusted" source.

 

-Never open unsolicited email attachments.

 

-Practice safe web browsing.

 

-Always use a standard user account unless you have a specific need for an administrator account.  Then switch, do the administrative task, and return to the standard account.  This greatly limits the access of any type of infection of intrusion into your system since administrative access isn’t in place.

Yes this is a phishing scam.

For some reference items going forward in the future

#1 - The most obvious indicator would be whether or not you are receiving any emails in your account
#2 - MS would never send you a message with the words "for the final time"
#3 - Look at the email address that the message came from - was it from a Microsoft domain? Even if it was, that's not a qualifying indicator since the sender's email address can always be spoofed
#4 Most importantly, look at any URLs in the message to determine the ultimate destination that the URL is pointing to. tim liu msft say:

Hi Justin,

Firstly I'd like to explain that there are two different kinds of accounts for Microsoft services. One is Microsoft account and the other is work/school account. Microsoft account is for consumer service such as consumer OneDrive. It looks like name@outlook.com or other email addresses. Work/school account is related to Office 365 for Business service such as OneDrive for Business. It looks like name@domain.com or name@domain.onmicrosoft.com.

In your left screenshot, notebooks are stored in your OneDrive for Business library (URL is domain-my.sharepoint.com/) related to your work/school account. The top right screenshot is that you open a shared notebook which is stored in consumer OneDrive (the URL is onedrive.live.com). It means you colleague share with you a notebook stored in his Microsoft account not work/school account.

If he shares a notebook from his OneDrive for Business library with your work/school account, you are able to see the notebook in your OneDrive for Business library> Shared>Shared with me section. When you edit that notebook in OneNote Online it will automatically save your changes. You could also click Open In OneNote to keep this notebook open and syncing with your OneNote desktop application. For the shared notebook from consumer OneDrive, you could only edit it with OneNote Online.

Moreover, I'd like to explain that a shared notebook won't appear in your OneDrive for Business library>Files section. It means the shared notebook is not in your OneDrive for Business library. So, when you click More notebooks, you could only see notebooks stored in your OneDrive for Business library. The shared notebook won't appear in that situation.

You could let your colleague create a notebook in his OneDrive for Business library and then share it with you. So, you could see the notebook in Shared with me section or directly open it with OneNote desktop app.

Note: We can say Shared with me is a view for items shared with us. It doesn't mean the item is stored in our OneDrive for Business library.

Tim
