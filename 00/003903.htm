Hi Jamie,
 
Which of the operations below do you grant permission to these users?
Operation 1. In the library, click setting gear>Site settings>Site Permissions>Grant Permissions and give these users Contribute permission level.
Operation 2. In the library, click Library tab>Library settings>Permission for this document library>Grant Permission to these users.
Operation 3. Click the Share button to share the whole site.
Operation 4.Click LIBRARY tab>Shared with to only share the library.
 
I’d like to explain that If the library has stop inhering permission, it has unique permission. The users who have permissions with site level (operation 1 and 3) also may not be able to access the library. They need to send request to the site admin for the permission of the library.
Note: To check this, use operation 2 to access the permission page of library. If you see Delete unique permissions, it means the library stop inheriting from the site.
 
On the other hand, if the library stops inheriting permissions from site and you use operation 2 or 4, the users cannot access the site home page but can directly access the library with its URL or from the link in the invitation emails.
 
So, you may choose to let the library inheriting the permission and directly share the site to users. If you choose let the library stop inheriting permission, you may use operation 2 or 4 to grant permissions.
 
If you are in the situation mentioned above, but users still get the access required page, please capture three screenshots of as below:
1. The site permission list page.
2. The library permission list page.
3. Click Check Permission to check one of the user who get the access required page in both site and library permission page.

Try these two array formulas:

=SUMIFS(OFFSET(DataSheet!W$1,MAX(IF(DataSheet!E$242:E$962<>"",ROW($242:$962)-225,0)),,225),
                OFFSET(DataSheet!E$1,MAX(IF(DataSheet!E$242:E$962<>"",ROW($242:$962)-225,0)),,225),C2)

=SUMIFS(OFFSET(DataSheet!W$1,MAX(IF(DataSheet!E$242:E$962<>"",ROW($242:$962)-120,0)),,120),
                OFFSET(DataSheet!E$1,MAX(IF(DataSheet!E$242:E$962<>"",ROW($242:$962)-120,0)),,120),C2)


Explanation:
The part

OFFSET(DataSheet!W$1,MAX(IF(DataSheet!E$242:E$962<>"",ROW($242:$962)-225,0)),,225)

defines the range in the W column with the 225 last entries.

This is the range to be conditionally summed. The condition is defined on a similar range in the

Note: The SUMIFS can now be entered as ordinary formulas, just ENTER

=SUMIFS(INDIRECT($AF$1),INDIRECT($AG$1),C2)

=SUMIFS(INDIRECT($AH$1),INDIRECT($AI$1),C2)

EDIT: You should be aware of that the formulas in cells AF1:AI1 do not correctly adjust themselves if you make changes to your DataSheet worksheet like inserting och deleting columns or renaming the sheet.

hansv mvp say:

Activate the Page Layout tab of the ribbon.

Click the little arrow in the lower right corner of the Page Setup group.

Activate the Margins tab of the Page Setup dialog.

The Bottom margin is the distance between the cells and the bottom edge of the page.

The Footer margin is the distance between the page footer and the bottom of the page.

The difference between the two determines the distance between the cells and the page footer.

Change the margins to suit your preference.

Please note that Excel will never split a row over two pages. So if row 56 is too tall to fit between row 55 and the footer, it will be pushed to the second page, leaving an empty space.
