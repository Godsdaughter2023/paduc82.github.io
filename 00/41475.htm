tryx3 say:

Intro stuff

My rather exhaustive list of questions was intended to make sure that I understood what you wanted.

Since you have no other user accounts on your computer and can live without OneNote, you can convert your user account back to a local account [since OneNote seems to have converted it to an MSAccount from your description] and remove the password from it. 

If you had no spare Admin account[s] on the computer and your user account ever suffers from user profile corruption than you will only be able to recover by taking your computer to a repair shop or by reinstalling Windows 10.  

  • Powering off your computer by holding down the power button to force it off [rather than just pressing it for a moment to let it close down in its correct manner] is one of those events that can corrupt the user profile.  Whilst the occurrence is not exactly frequent, it does happen. 
  • I have included the instructions for creating an Admin account further on down.
  • Any spare Admin account  must be password protected. 
  • Write the password down somewhere secure so you can find it if you need to use that Admin user account in an emergency. 
  • You cannot allow an unused account to be password-free or any hacker who chances upon your computer online [they scan for them] can log into this account behind your back and you would know nothing about it. 
  • Similarly, if you ever have a need to log into that spare Admin account, you will have to disconnect from the internet first because otherwise some hacker would be able to log into your normal account while you were in the spare Admin one.

There would be no point having an extra account just to be used as an MSAccount as you clearly do not want to use OneNote or other MS Apps again.  I just asked about this as an option in case you did. 

I think the message about connecting to the internet is probably, as you suspected, because of your use of an MSAccount and OneNote. 

[[[Just in case you hear mention of using "metered" connections to avoid automatic Windows updates, do note that this would have the side-effect in your case of never updating your already installed Windows Apps.  This is something you can post back here about if you are ever going to be in that position and I'll explain this in more detail.  If you are never going to consider "metered" connections then there would be no point in having the rather involved discussion about them that would be needed.  If, as I expect, your response to this para is "what is he talking about" then the answer is that you do not need to bother about it.]]]

Actions

1  Add the spare Admin [as a local account not an MSAccount] before anything else so if anything goes wrong you can use it to sort things out.  Go to -

Start button,

Settings,

Accounts,

Family & other users,

[Other users section],

Add someone else to this PC,

I don't have this person's sign-in information,

Add a user without a Microsoft account,

Write the intended password down on a piece of paper now,

Enter a username, password & password hint,

Back in the list of other accounts you can now see in Family & other users, [Other users section], 

Click on the new account,

Click on Change account type,

Select the Administrator account type,

OK.

2  Revert to local account from one converted to MSAccount - see my posts of 23 & 26 September 2016 [the way this forum operates, you'll have to click on the All replies (10) entry at the end of the initial part of the thread to open it up so you can see them all.]  You may find this difficult to follow but it does work - just study each step, post back here with any questions then do that step and move onto the next one.

3  To remove your password -

3.1  Go to Settings, Accounts, Sign-in options, Password sub-heading, Change and follow that through.  You will have to start by entering the current password [which will be the one that MSAccount made you use].  To enter a blank as a new password you need to click in the password box rather than just ignore it.

3.2  If you find that you cannot complete this because the old MSAccount password does not work for it then sign-out from your normal account and  log into the new spare Admin and go to

Start menu,

scroll down to Windows system & click on it to open it,

Control panel,

User accounts,

Manage another account,

[give it Admin permission to proceed by click in the Yes button],

click on the icon for your normal account,

Change the password,

Enter the new password twice [your new password will be blank rather than simply deleted so just click in the two entry boxes, I think it will still force you to put in a hint that is not blank]

Change password,

[I think there is a final confirmation box where you confirm that you want to to do the change].

Now you are free to log out of the spare Admin and back into your normal account.

3.3  Where you have any online passwords saved by your web browser such as for entering this forum then they can get wiped by this resetting procedure [para 3.2] so you will have to put them in the next time you visit any such websites.  They will then be remembered again - it is just a by-product of having to use another account to reset the password that causes this loss.

4  Consider making an installation USB in case you do ever have such serious problems that you decide to reinstall - http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows-10/media-creation-tool-install ]

That is all I think you need to do,

Denis
