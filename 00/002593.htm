john w. vinson say:

The third argument to DLookUp should not be just a number - it should be some True/False expression which evaluates to True for the record you want retrieved. Since the ID will be a nonzero number, this will essentially retrieve all records in DashboardQuarterly; it's just pure chance that you're seeing the most recent.

If you want to retrieve the CommFIRM value for the most recent date in table DashboardQuarterly then you do indeed need a DMax() nested within the DLookUp; and if TradeDate is in fact date data it should - MUST! - be stored in a Date/Time field not a Text field. The text string "9/28/2014" looks like a date to us, but it's just a string of characters to Access; it will sort AFTER "10/03/2014", since 9 is bigger than 1!

john w. vinson say:

To get the current quarter with a dlookup, you could use a DateTime field for TradeDate and:

=DLookUp("CommFIRM", "DashboardQuarterly", "[TradeDate] >= #" & DateSerial(Year(Date()), 3*(Month(Date())\3)+1, 1) & "# AND [TradeDate] < DateSerial(Year(Date()), (Month(Date())+3\3)+1, 1) & "#")

To directly answer your question...

This will return the Excel version number:

=--INFO("release")

Excel 2007 is version 12 so you'd test for versions >=12.

You could incorporate that in a formula like this:

=IF(--INFO("release")>=12,IFERROR(AVERAGE(D1:D10),""),IF(ISERROR(AVERAGE(D1:D10)),"",AVERAGE(D1:D10)))

However, IMHO, I think you'd be better off just writing the formula with functions that are available in ALL Excel versions.

How do we left or right justify a number across a block of text - Microsoft Community

honest abe say:

How do we left or right justify a number across a block of text without merging

We have a situation where we need to left justify telephone numbers and dates across a block of cells.  We use number formats to format the content and would use merge to also justify the content, except that the merge feature has so many undesirable consequences when sorting.  We can center the numbers using the alignment option to 'Center Across Selection', but cannot similarly left or right justify the number across a selection.  Does anyone know how to accomplish left or right justification across a selection without merging the cells?

 

OBSERVATIONS:

First, text entries will left justify in a cell and then spill into empty cells to the right, so left justifying text across cells is actually possible.  Nevertheless, numbers behave differently and will be cut off at the right cell border or displayed as asterisks (if too large to display in cell).

Second, merging cells is a great feature, but merging always seems to have adverse consequences such as preventing certain range selections, preventing sorts, and disallowing the implementation of tables.  If the problems were corrected, then the merge feature would become the logical tool for many formatting dilemmas.

 

SUGGESTIONS FOR MICROSOFT:

When selecting cells that intersect merged cells (i.e., split or do not include the full merged cell), then the selection should not be tainted by the merged cell attribute by forcing a wider or taller selection than desired by the user.  Instead, subcells of the merged cell should be included in the selection as if they were still individual cells.  Understandably, formatting changes made to any subset of cells, within the merged cells (except the primary cell), would go undisplayed until they are unmerged.  Likewise, if a copy and paste selection splits merged cells, then the subset of any merged cells would be pasted as individual cells without the merge attribute.

jllatham say:

I'm afraid your only alternative option would be to enter the dates/phone numbers as text (format the cells as Text or precede the entry with an apostrophe [']).  This, of course, has other potential detrimental downstream effects since they are no longer true numbers behind the scenes.
