The apparent complexity of what is really a simple log entry is exactly why no one but experts should ever look at the Windows Event Logs without guidance.

 

In this case the description is very clear, indicating that the McAfee Event log which has a maximum size setting of 16MB is full and so might fail to log future entries. To resolve this either clear that log or if what's already within it might be important, increase its size or make certain that the action taken when the log is full is to either overwtire old events or archive the log.

 

If you don't understand this last paragraph you shouldn't be messing with the logs, since these are some of the simplest maintenance concepts for the proper operation of the Event Logging system.

 

Rob

rob koch say:
I assumed the log entries would be overwritten, I guess not.


I depends on the settings for the specific Event Log made by the McAfee installation script.  Though the overwrite setting should be the default, it may have been changed later.

 

However, the log entry you posted above never attempts to make that determination, it simply informs that the log is full and leaves it to the reader to confirm the specific settings.  The Event Logs are filled with this kind of possibly unimportant warnings, which is why they can be so confusing and even scary to typical users not used to reading them.

 

Any one who actually understands how to use these logs knows enough to simply ignore them unless they already are experiencing some sort of specific issue and then to use them only to look for entries that are likely specifically related to the problem at hand.  Attempting to understand and "fix" absolutely every log entry of even an Error Level could typically require days to weeks of research and remediation effort and in most cases wouldn't result in any noticeable improvement in operation of the PC.

I discovered the solution, which might help others who are having similar problems:

If you are using 2-step verification on your Hotmail account you have to TURN IT OFF before reinstalling Hotmail on your phone. Then you can turn it back on again, following the instructions in Outlook. In short:

Delete Hotmail from phone

On a different device, turn off 2-step verification

Reinstall Hotmail on phone

Turn 2-step verification back on.
