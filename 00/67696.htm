Most batteries, you will never get 100% of the rated charge.  Each battery is different and the rating is based at the factory with no load.  Since the batteries are in the tablet and have a load on them at all times, that can knock it down a small bit.

No company can guarantee a full rated charge rate batteries.  They can only build them the best they can and they are warranted if they totally fail or if there is a defect.

By the looks of your numbers, you should be within the full charge range.  Do remember that in order to see that listing, your tablet is in use and that will knock the numbers down even with the A/C adapter attached because you are always on battery power no matter what.

Usually, a computer will log each time the A/C adapter is attached and will automatically begin a charge even if you are at 100%.  That should only last a few seconds and it will shut off the charge of the batteries.

If in doubt, monitor your listing about once a week and if those number keep declining, then you may have a battery issue and contact Microsoft Technical Support.  Those batteries cannot be replaced easily and they replace the whole tablet.

Lithium-Ion batteries are unpredictable and I would monitor that (just in case).  The cycle count, as I mentioned, will probably correlate the same time you attached the A/C adapter to the tablet.
