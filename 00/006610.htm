Reuse the license from the old laptop - Microsoft Community

fredboudreau say:

Reuse the license from the old laptop

My old laptop died with Microsoft Office 2010 on it. How do I install that software on my new laptop and reuse the license from the old laptop?
dan at it associates say:

Office Home and Business 2010 sold via packaged discs is a full retail license for Office H&B 2010.  This type of license allows you to move the product from computer to computer as necessary.  It is preferable to uninstall your Office from the old computer, but since it died that won't be possible so it's not a big deal.

 

On your new laptop, if it came from the manufacturer with the Office 2010 Single Image Installation, you can activate Office Home and Business 2010 without the bother of installing.  Go to the Start>All Programs menu and click on Office 2010.  A dialog box will come up with three choices:  I already purchased Office 2010, Go online to purchase Office 2010, and Use Office Starter 2010.  Select I already purchased Office 2010, enter your product key from your Office Home and Business 2010 package, and let Office setup do the rest.

 

If your new laptop did not come with the Office 2010 Single Image Installation, use the installation disc and product key in your Office Home and Business 2010 package to do a normal installation.

 

Your full retail H&B 2010 license permits you to activate the product on another of your computers (in addition to the new computer), so if you have another operational computer that needs Office, you can activate it on that additional computer.
