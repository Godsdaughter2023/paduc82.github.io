a. user say:

The are a couple of ways you may be able to work around this limitation.

One is a real kludge, and relies on the reader's machine not reformatting the document in some way. To do that, when you have finished your document, insert new paragraphs at the end of the final Endnote until you reach the next page. Then insert the Bibliography. You will notice that the formatting is not the same as usual and the "Bibliography" title is missing. You will probably also see an Endnote continuation marker that you probably don't want (see below). That's all because some things either can't be inserted in an Endnote (e.g. you can't put a section break in) or don't work normally (e.g. a page break character has no effect). You can make manual adjustments to fix these problems. You could for example insert a "dummy" Endnote at the end of your text, just to hold the Bibliography, and perhaps hide its reference number.

Personally, I would try to avoid that method.

The other method assumes that your document only contains a single Word Section

In that case, you can specify that the endnotes are listed at the end of each Section. They then appear after Section 1.

You can insert a page break at the end of the section to ensure that the Endnotes start on a new page, then insert a new Word Section after the endnotes and put your Bibliography in there.

If you do not want the "Endnote Separator" at the top of the first Endnotes page, you can remove it, but you have to get the relevant pane on screen first. The easy way to do that is to use View->Views->Draft, then References->Footnotes->Show Notes. A new pane is displayed and you can select the relevant Separator/Notice from the dropdown and modify it.

Alternatively you can put the relevant commans to View/Edit the separators in the Quick Access Toolbar (QAT). One way to do that is via File->Word Options->Customize Quick Access Toolbar. Under Choose Commands From, select All Commands. Then find and select "View Endnote Separator" in the list and use the Add button to add it to the QAT. You may want to add buttons for editing other stuff such as "View Endnote Cont Separator" and "View Endnote Cont Notice". You can then click the QAT button to see a Windows that lets you edit the separator.

I suspect the only other way you could could deal with this is if you are submitting a PDF (or even a paper document!) rather than the .docx. In that case you could consider generating the bibliography in front of the Endnotes and using a PDF editor to move the Bibliography pages to the end. Unfortunately, you would probably also have to fix the page numbering. Or you could try to get your professor to change their mind :-)
