psychomania---01 say:

You didn't have a backup?! :smileyindifferent:

 

Unfortunately if the phone cannot boot then there is no way to recover data from the phones memory.  There are no external or software options to try and nothing can run if it cannot boot.

 

The only possibility is to use a data recovery agency to extract the data.  It's not cheap and doesn't always yield the expected results.  You can find those agencies by searching google.

 

The only thing you can try to get your phone working again is a hard reset, this will wipe out all of your data but may allow the phone to work again.

 

Hard reset:

1. Switch off the phone.

2. Hold down the following three buttons: Green (the call answer button), * button, and '3' button.

3. While holding these buttons, hold the power button for a second to switch on the phone.

4. Release when the phone shows the nokia hands logo or shows other signs of life like the language selection screen.

It may take a few attempts and very flexible fingers.

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

Exporting to a PST isn't advised if you're already using a PST for your data file.  Exporting always loses data, mostly linkages between various Outlook records, but also things like Contact Groups.  If you already have a PST, simply close Outlook and copy that PST.  If you use, say, an IMAP account, then create a new PST and copy your existing folders to it.

Importing is also not ideal.  Instead just open the PST in Outlook.

Exporting mail messages shouldn't lose "whole chunks", but if you somehow include a limit of some kind when performing the export, the export will only include the limited data.  Since we don't know what steps you used for the export or how you reincluded the data into Outlook, it's not possible to explain exactly what happened.  However, if you tell us what type of account (i.e., POP, IMAP, etc.) you used with the previous provider and what type of account you now use, perhaps we can suggest ways to recover at least some of the data.
