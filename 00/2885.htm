jay freedman say:
In cases like this, what you don't see is often the culprit. For example, if the word Paragraph was followed in the text by a nonbreaking space (entered with Ctrl+Shift+spacebar), but your search includes an ordinary space character, the text would not be found.

Go to one of the cross-references, and click the ¶ button to display nonprinting characters. An ordinary space is shown by a dot at center-height, while a nonbreaking space is shown by a degree sign (°).

If that's the cause, you can use the code  ^s  in the Find What box of the Replace dialog to represent a nonbreaking space. Other such codes are available in the dialog by clicking the More button and then the Special button.
