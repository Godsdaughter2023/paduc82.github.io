In general, I don't believe it's a good technique to leave the constraints alone and base them on previous runs.   A good technique is to Reset the Solver prior to setting up your model in vba using:

SolverReset

' then

SolverOk SetCell:=  ...etc

When one adds constraints, there is an "Integer" option.   The constraint option is called "int"

In code, it would look like this:

SolverAdd "A1:A3", 4

(4 being Integer)

What you seem to want is an integer output between -10, and +10.

What you need to do is re-scale your model to work with integers, and interpret a solution of -9 as -0.09, or -9/100 = -9%

For example, in a financial model, where Dollars and cells are required, but you don't want fractional cents,   ie $45.3333333,   one solution is to rescale the model to think in terms of integer pennies.

The Solver would use integers constraints with the idea that integer 4533 is better than 4534. Once Solver found 4533, another cell would hold the real solution of 4533/100, or 45.33.

Another key point to remember is that in Excel 2007, one cannot really use functions like IF and ABS.

These are known as discontinuous functions. Solver cannot keep track of the large jumps in values due to small changes and input.

As you can see, I like to use Range Names in Solver.

(Just to give you some ideas).

Even if you want to find a solution of 0, I will often use minimize.

(something lilke x >= -.001).

The reason is that even though we see the solution as zero, Excel might skip a solution due to precision.

For Example, this simple demo will return false, even though mathematically it is zero within precision.

= (2.6-2-0.6)=0   (Excel says False)

Sub Demo()
    With ActiveSheet.Names
        .Add "Ordered", [D9]
        .Add "Actual", [G7]
        .Add "Target", [D23]
        .Add "Units", [D13:D17]
        .Add "Available", [F13:F17]
        .Add "Chg", [G13:G17]
    End With
   
    [Units] = [{400;472;521;620;857}]
    [Available] = 30 ' My guess as to what's available
    [Actual].Formula = "=SUMPRODUCT(D13:D17,G13:G17)"
   
    [Chg].Interior.ColorIndex = 6
    [Ordered].Interior.ColorIndex = 8
    [Target].Interior.ColorIndex = 15
   
    '// An initial value to avoid #Div/0! Errors
    [Chg] = 1
   
    SolverReset
    SolverOk SetCell:=[Target], MaxMinVal:=2, ByChange:=[Chg]
   
    SolverAdd [Chg], 4              'Integer
    SolverAdd [Chg], 1, [Available] 'Chg < Available
    SolverAdd [Chg], 3, 0           'Chg > 0
    SolverAdd [Target], 3, -0.1     'Target >= -0.1
   
    SolverSolve True
End Sub
