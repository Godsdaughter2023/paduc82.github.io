Storing info on USB device - Microsoft Community

2blue2cry# say:

Storing info on USB device

How do I store information on a USB device from my computer

loyd_g say:

Hi 2Blue2Cry#, 

Here's how to store information on USB device ( Windows 10 ).

  • Plug the flash drive into your computer. Your flash drive should plug into one of the rectangular USB ports on your computer's housing.
  • Open Start. Click the Windows logo in the bottom-left corner of the screen.
  • Open File Explorer. Click the folder-shaped icon in the lower-left side of the Start menu. 
  • Click This PC . It's a computer-shaped icon on the left side of the File Explorer window. This will open a list of your computer's attached drives, including your flash drive. You may have to scroll down on the left-hand column of options in order to find This PC.
  • Open your flash drive. Under the "Devices and drives" heading, double-click the name of your flash drive. This will usually be the flash drive's manufacturer's name and/or the model number.
  • Select files to move. Click an individual file to select it, or hold down Ctrl key while clicking each file that you want to select. You can also select every file in the flash drive by pressing Ctrl key + A. 
  • Click HOME. It's a tab in the upper-left side of the flash drive's window. Doing so prompts a drop-down toolbar at the top of the window. If you're comfortable with Windows' click-and-drag feature, you can instead click and drag the selected files onto your desktop or into your preferred folder. If you do this, skip to the last step in this method. 
  • Click Move to. You'll find this option in the "Organize" section of the toolbar. A drop-down menu will appear.
  • Click Choose location. It's at the bottom of the drop-down menu. Doing so opens a new window.
  • Select a folder. Click the folder into which you want to transfer the selected folder. You can also create a new folder by selecting a destination folder, clicking Make New Folder, and entering a name for the folder. 
  • Click Move. It's in the bottom-right corner of the window. Your files will begin transferring into the selected folder; once they complete, you can proceed.
  • Eject your flash drive. In the flash drive's window, click the Manage tab at the top of the window, then click Eject in the resulting toolbar. Once you receive a notification that the flash drive is safe to remove, you can physically remove it from your computer by gently pulling on it.

Hope this answers your query.

Solution: How to remove 'sent on behalf of' text from all - Microsoft Community

joseph marino say:

Solution: How to remove 'sent on behalf of' text from all outgoing email

This post is not a question, it is a solution to a problem that many people have been having with removing the "sent on behalf of" text from all e-mail sent via Outlook.com for custom domain e-mail accounts.

I know that a lot of folks had a lot of questions on this board about how to remove "sent on behalf of" this post explains how to solve that problem, and also provides a screenshot/proof that the SMTP Authentication fields that solve the problem have finally been implemented by Microsoft.

In a recent Outlook.com upgrade Microsoft finally added outgoing SMTP server authentication so that folks who are using their own custom domain name e-mail addresses (ie @verizon.net, @mybusiness.com, @gmail.com) can remove the "sent on behalf of" that was always tagged to their outgoing e-mails when sending e-mails from the Outlook.com interface.

To remove "sent on behalf of" simply
  1. Login to Outlook.com, then click on the cog wheel on the upper right hand corner, a menu will appear, click on "more mail settings"
  2. Under "manage accounts" click on "your e-mail accounts"
  3. Click on "add a send-and-receive account"
  4. A new page will appear then click on "advanced options"

You will see then see Incoming POP3 settings and Outgoing SMTP server information fields.

  1. Input your POP3 settings for your e-mail account provided by your ISP for example (*** Email address is removed for privacy ***, mail.verizon.net etc)
  2. Then go down to SMTP server information
    VERY IMPORTANT: make sure that you select the option "send email using your provider's server (receipient will not see your Outlook address)". This is the option that removes "sent on behalf of"
  3. Enter your SMTP server settings provided by your ISP for example (smtp.verizon.net)
  4. Click Next.
  5. Send a test e-mail to someone, you'll notice that you no longer see "sent on behalf of" in your outgoing e-mails
  6. You're done

Thank you Microsoft for adding this feature to Outlook.com :-)

Try double checking your SMTP port and the SSL/TLS secure connection options.  I had to change the default options to SMTP port 25 and no SSL/TLS.  It worked for me after that.

Also, check the credentials you're using to connect to your SMTP server.  There is an option to use the same credentials as your POP server ("Use the same username and password to send and receive emails" checkbox), or optionally provide different credentials by deselecting the checkbox.

Lastly, Joseph Marino's instructions show you how to add a send-and-receive account.  If you had previously added your external e-mail account to your Outlook.com, you need to edit it by clicking the Details link, then the Edit button.

Otherwise, Joseph Marino's instructions are spot on!
