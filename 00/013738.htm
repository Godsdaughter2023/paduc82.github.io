alice_941 say:

Donna, first you need to find "Table Properties."  There are two ways of finding it:

 

     One is to left-click anywhere on the table.  At the top of your screen you will see Table Tools.  There are two tabs, Design and Layout.  Click on Layout.  In the far left-hand corner you will see Table Properties.  Left click on it.  A window opens up.

 

    The other way to find "Table Properties" is either to hover your mouse over the table or to click in the table.  A very small square appears outside and near the upper left-hand corner of your table.   Right-click on that tiny square.  A small menu opens up.  All the way at the bottom you will see "Table Properties."  Left-click on it.  A window opens up.

 

    In the Table Properties window, there are five tabs:  Table, Row, Column, Cell, Alt Text.  The window should open up with the first one, "Table," showing.  In the middle you will see "Alignment."  You will see Left, Center, and Right.  If you want your table over to the left, click on Left.  Then click O.K.  If your table is still not to the left side of your page, then you need to check a few things:

1)  In that same "Table" tab of the Table Properties window, to the right of Left, Center, Right Alignment, make sure there is no indent set.  Also, in that same "Table" tab, I would set the preferred width of your table and not leave it up to chance.

2)  Make sure that the table is not wider than the area between your left and right margins.  

If you still have problems, look at Table Properties, "Table" tab, click on Positioning, and make sure the settings are what you want them to be.

  1. On your inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.
  2. Under Customizing Outlook, click Rules for sorting new messages.

Click Edit next to the rule that you want to edit.

Or

Choose the rule that you want to delete and click Delete.

 

Note: Not all international characters are supported as filters.

Check your Reply-to address

  1. On your inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.
  2. Under Writing email, click Reply-to address.
  3. Select Current e-mail address radio button.
  4. Click Save.
