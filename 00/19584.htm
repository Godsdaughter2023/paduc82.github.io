ken sheridan say:

Assuming you have the employee's names are in columns in the table already you don't need to include their initials in another column.  Simply use distinct numbers in that and concatenate the initials to the formatted value of the number to get the final ID.  That avoids the redundancy, and consequently any possible inconsistencies between the names and initials.  If the eight characters include the initials the expression would be something like this:

Left(FirstName,1) & Left(LastName,1) & Format(IDNumber,"000000")

which could be used in a computed column in a query, or in a computed control in a form or report.

To generate the IDNumber you could use an autonumber, but this might not necessarily be within the 6 or 8 digit limit.  It would be better to generate your own sequential number when a record is inserted via a form.  Roger Carlson has a simple method for this, which caters for both single and multi-user environments, avoiding possible conflicts in the latter, at:

http://www.rogersaccesslibrary.com/forum/topic395.html

and there's a slightly more complex one of mine, which also allows the next number to be used to be 'seeded' at:

http://community.netscape.com/n/pfx/forum.aspx?nav=libraryMessages&tsn=1&tid=23839&webtag=ws-msdevapps

Ken Sheridan, Stafford, England
