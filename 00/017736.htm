rob koch say:

For those looking for a simpler way to protect themselves from a potential scam, there is a much simpler way in this case.

The "Private Caller" designation specifically means that the caller is trying to hide their true identity from CallerID, which there is simply never a good reason to do.  If you know someone personally who does this tell them to get it changed to identify themselves, because it makes their calls suspicious as well.

I tell everyone I know never to answer a call identified this way, since most scammers will never leave a message and those rare individuals who do this typically will leave a message.  So you won't ever miss a call from anyone reputable who really wishes to reach you and thus there's simply no reason to ever answer such an unidentified caller.

It's that simple and avoids the need for you to waste anymore time than a quick look at the phone to determine the caller doesn't care enough about you to identify themselves, so why should you care about their call?

Tom

There is a great replacement for WLM that does it all and will continue to work.  It is free for 2 accounts and there is a "paid" version.

There is a great replacement for Windows live mail that is free for personal use (up to 2 accounts) that DOES work with the new outlook mail format called emclient.  There is also a paid version. You can get either here

http://www.emclient.com/

It works with Exchange, POP, IMAP, and looks similar to Windows live mail.  Frankly it is the first replacement I have seen that actually works.  I have switched.

They have their own support forums, a decent product, and seem to be the only replacement for WLM that has the look and feel of it.

You can import all your windows live mail into emclient easily by doing a file>import>windows live mail.  This will import your current WLM data into emclient

Out of an abundance of caution I would make a copy of the entire WLM folder and save it to a safe location.
