suzanne s. barnhill say:
Assuming you have rows in pairs as in the illustration, you can do this in the Modify Style dialog. You can select Odd Banded Rows and Even Banded Rows separately, then use Format | Borders and Shading to apply the desired border. Actually, the easiest way to do this is to select your desired border color and weight and use the All Borders button to apply borders to the Heading Row, Odd Banded Rows, and Even Banded Rows in turn, then use the dialog or the buttons on the Borders dropdown menu to remove the top/bottom borders selectively. In other words, you want the top row of the pair to have all the borders except the bottom one and the bottom row to have all the borders except the top one.

cbarnhorst say:

No, it does not expire.  Build 14393 in any form does not expire.  

If the license (which is another matter) were in notification it would say so in Settings and say how many days are left to activate.  to see that go to Settings/Update and Security/Activation.  There you are likely to see that Windows is activated with a digital license and linked to your Microsoft account.  That means the device is a registered Windows 10 device for the edition listed at the top of that page.  That registration is on Microsoft's servers, not on your computer, so anytime you reinstall Windows the software checks with the servers for the registration, finds it, and activates.  That is why you don't need a product key if you clean re-install.

The "Microsoft" at the bottom of your winver output is the grantor of the right to use the license.  Mine shows my email address.  Yours shows simply the default "Microsoft," probably the only information left after all the re-installations.  On computers still using software pre-installed at the factory (or recently upgraded from the pre-installed software) the name of the OEM appears as the grantor of rights to use the license.  The only important point is that the license is granted and you have the right to use it.

You can also check your activation status by opening a Command Prompt(Admin) by right clicking the start button and choosing it.  Type 

slmgr /dli

and hit Enter.
