Hi Camille,

We greatly appreciate your time and effort for the details that you have provided.

Based on the information that you have posted, it appears that you are referring to Windows Live Mail and Microsoft Office Outlook which are both mail clients that you use to add and manage all your email accounts.

To set your expectations, if you have configured your accounts as EAS (Exchange ActiveSync) on your Windows Live Mail and Microsoft Office Outlook, all your emails, contacts and calendars will automatically sync and any changes that you will make on those mail clients will automatically reflect on the web and vice versa because EAS is a two way connection. So when you delete an email on your Microsoft Office Outlook, it will be deleted on the web too. And since your account is also configured on Windows Live Mail (which also pulls up your emails from the web), the emails that were deleted will be deleted from it too. This means that even though you will uninstall your Windows Live Mail, as long as you are seeing the same emails or your emails are still intact in the web, they will stay in your Microsoft Office Outlook. Take note that this behavior only applies if your account was configured as EAS.

On the other hand, if your account was not configured as EAS and it was connected as POP, only emails from your inbox will be downloaded / automatically sync and it will be deleted from the web unless you have enabled the option to "Leave a copy of messages on the server". In this case, we highly recommend that you back up your emails first before you uninstall your Windows Live Mail to make sure that you will not lose any of your emails.

We still advise that you back up all your emails or files before you remove or uninstall Windows Live Mail from your PC to avoid losing any of them. Also check the connections of your accounts and also check them on the web and make sure that your emails are still intact in the web.

You may refer to this article to learn more on how to back up your emails on Windows Live Mail.

amit tandon say:

2 Options.

Option 1: If you want to do a case-sensitive count which will count only an UPPER "X" or "Y", then use below formula:

=SUMPRODUCT(LEN(A2:A10)-LEN(SUBSTITUTE(A2:A10,{"X","Y"},"")))*0.5

Option 2: If you dont want a case-sensitive count, so that it counts both Lower & Upper "X" & "Y":

=SUMPRODUCT(LEN(A2:A10)-LEN(SUBSTITUTE(UPPER(A2:A10),{"X","Y"},"")))*0.5

Note:

The formula is valid for and counts multiple occurrences of "X" & "Y" in a cell and covers the range A2:A10. If you want to count in a single cell only then change A2:A10 to just A2: =SUMPRODUCT(LEN(A2)-LEN(SUBSTITUTE(A2,{"X","Y"},"")))*0.5.

Each cell can have other letters also besides "X" or "Y".

How do I open and edit an attached excel file in OneNote from my MacBook Pro using my Office Home 365 Account?  It always opens as "Read Only"
swischner say:
Unfortunately this is a limitation of OneNote on MacOS (rather a limitation of MacOS itself). The reason is, that the attached excel file is a copy of the original one, stored in OneNotes "own" file space. Now MacOS (like iOS or Windows UWP apps) does not allow to write data into the storage space of another app. But this is exactly what Excel would have to to after you edited the file: save it back into OneNotes storage space. I talked to the OneNote team about it; they are aware of the limitation and are thinking hard about a way to override that.
So your only option for now is:
Open the excel file from the notebook in excel, make your edits, save it under a different name to a folder where you have access rights, delete the embedded file in OneNote and replace it (re-attach) with the edited version you just saved.

>> I have a 'OnSlideShowPageChange' macro and when I have the presentation containing it open but press F5 in another PowerPoint it still runs the code.

Yes, because it's an *application-wide* event.

karen_[01] say:

Hello

Since Skype migrated to Microsoft, the option to sign up and in to Skype using a Microsoft credential is now possible. Also, you will no longer have an option to create your Skype name because the new simplified account sign-up method only requires an Email/Password or Phone Number/Password as minimum requirements for sign-up. This new method aims to reduce the sign-up effort by presenting a faster and more flexible experience for new users.

With that being said, if you signed up in Skype using your MS credential, it will generate a new account with pre-defined username. Since this account follows your MS account, deleting the generated Skype account will also close the associated MS account.

In this case, you can link your existing Skype account to a different MS account or avoid signing in using your email address as much as possible. In a few years of inactivity, the generated account will be closed automatically.

In addition, it's not possible to consolidate the generated Skype account and your existing Skype account.

Let me know if you have further questions and clarifications.
