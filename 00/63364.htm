Thanks for providing the screenshots, Nemanja. As what the device status, states, there are no drivers installed for the device so you need to manually search and download the driver for your multimedia controllers. To do that, the device's Hardware ID should be obtained first. Follow these steps:

  1. In Device Manager > Other devices, right-click the Multimedia Controller, then click Properties.
  2. Click the Details tab.
  3. Under Property, look for and select Hardware ids.
  4. Copy the first item under Value. It serves as the Hardware ID.
  5. Search for the device driver online using the Hardware ID and save it on your PC.
  6. Do the same for the Multimedia Video Controller.

Once the drivers are saved, you may now go ahead and install or manually update the drivers for your Multimedia Controllers.

Thanks for getting back, Nemanja. Since only Windows 7 driver is available, you can still try installing those drivers in compatibility mode. To install it, follow these steps:

  4. Click on Windows 7, then hit Next.
  5. Click Test the program.
