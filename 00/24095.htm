connie_840 say:

POP Settings when using Mac Mail

Am using my Mac Mail to access Hotmail account.  What POP settings should I have if I want to access my messages in Mac Mail but retain them in my Hotmail Inbox?  In addition, I don't want to keep receiving my Hotmail messages over and over again in Mac Mail!  In my Virgin account (Virgin Webmail) there's an option to "Enable POP for mail that arrives from now on".  Is there an equivalent in Hotmail?

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

See the last entry on this page for the settings: http://windows.microsoft.com/en-id/windows/outlook/send-receive-from-app.

 

There is no Outlook.com equivalent of the Virgin option you describe.  If you elect to connect via POP, everything in your Inbox will be considered new to the client and it will download it all.  If you don't want that to happen, log into http://www.outlook.com/, create a new folder, and move everything you don't want downloaded from the Inbox to the new folder.  Leave in the Inbox only those messages you want the Mac to download.

 

Aother side-effect to watch out for is that, by default, most POP clients will delete messages from the Inbox after downloading them.  If you don't want that to happen, you have two choices: the first is to configure the account on the Mac to leave copies of messages on the server.  Since I don't use a Mac, you'll have to discover on your own where that setting is.  The other chose, controlled by the web interface to your Outlook.com mailbox, is to enable the option "Don't let another program delete messages from Outlook."  Find that by logging into http://www.outlook.com/, clicking the Options gear, then "More mail settings", then "POP and deleteing downloaded messages".  If that option is enabled, the messages will be moved from Inbox to a folder named POP.  They'll still be available online, but not in the Inbox.
