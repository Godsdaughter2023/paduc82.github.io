 Re performance, most laptop hard drives spin at 5400 RPM which is slower than the 7200 most desktop computers run at.  No matter how much RAM you have, Windows still has to load a pot full of stuff from your hard drive when you boot up, and no amount of RAM is going to speed up that process.  Once everything gets loaded, you can keep more in storage so you have less paging and fetching, but the initial load of software still has to wait for that disk to rotate enough times to bring everything into RAM.
