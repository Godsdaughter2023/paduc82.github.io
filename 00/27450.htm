petrib say:

Here is five ways for you (or your network provider's support staff):

 

Install Nokia PC Suite ( http://www.nokia.com/pcsuite ) on your PC, and copy the photos to your phone that way. See also the tutorials on the PC Suite web pages. Hint: Nokia Phone Browser in Windows Explorer.

Turn on Bluetooth or infrared (IrDA) on the phone (depending on the model), and send the picture over Bluetooth or infrared. Right-click on the picture in Windows Explorer and select the appopriate option in the "Send To" pop-up menu. The photos will appear as new messages in the Inbox.

 

Email it as an attachment to an email account which you have configured the phone to access.

 

If your phone supports USB mass storage (data transfer) mode with an USB cable, connect in that mode, and then copy the photos to the phone memory card that way.

 

If you you have a separate USB memory card reader, or your PC has a built-in card reader, then take the card out from the phone, instert it in the reader - possibly with a required adapter - and copy the photos to the card.
