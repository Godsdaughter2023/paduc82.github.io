jim gordon mvp say:
No cajoling here. This is a user-to-user forum. All helpful suggestions and solutions are welcome.

PowerPoint 2011 for Mac is capable of opening 97 through 2004 presentations without any converters, translators, or what have you. Any presentation that was saved in 97 through 2004 format should just open.

It would appear that the presentations that you are trying to open were last saved by a version of Power±Point older than 97, or they may have been exported from a "compatible" program with an old code base, such as old OpenOffice or NeoOffice versions. The same file extension (.ppt) was used prior to PowerPoint 97, but the internals of the file are different enough that PowerPoint 2011 won't open these particular files.

As you discovered, LibreOffice makes an excellent bridge between old and new. LibreOffice internals are still ancient, but it has been adapted so that you can choose to save in the now standard Office Open XML file format (.pptx). Opening the old files in LibreOffice and saving them in (.pptx) format gives you a way to open files that were saved with pre-'97 internals in  PowerPoint 2011.

You should test each file that LibreOffice saves in standard Open Office XML format, as LibreOffice does not adhere strictly to the Office Open XML standard (Microsoft Office products do adhere strictly to the open standard). Just now, as a test, I made a simple presentation from a LibreOffice template in LibreOffice 3.3 for Windows on Windows 7 and saved it in Office Open XML format. When opened in Microsoft PowerPoint 2011, all of the text, all of the animations, and most of the formatting was missing.

PowerPoint 2004 is also a bridge product having the ability to open pre-97 presentations and with the capability (with free converter installed) to save in standard Office Open XML (.pptx) file format. LibreOffice gives you the ability to perform the conversions in Lion, while Office 2004 does not.
