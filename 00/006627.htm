Create formula to determine projected cost based on current cost and a fixed inflation rate.

I need to know how to write a formula that will take the current cost of an item and project the replacement cost five, ten, fifteen, twenty or twenty five years in the future based on a fixed annual inflation rate.

brad scott say:

=FV(0.04,5,0,-100000,0)

.04 is the interest rate

5 is the duration -

0 - ongoing payments

-100000  is the current cost (make this number negative)

0 - switch that could be either 0 or 1 depending on your requirements on the what future value you want to see - most likely you will want zero.

This assumes that the cost doesn't change over time (either it getting more or less expensive)

It is advisable to use the following format:

=FV(A1,A2,A3,A4,A5) - where A1-A5 are the values listed above.

In the new email message, in the File Ribbon click, Format Text and click the small drop down in Paragraph group to open the dialog box to chose the spacing and indention.
