noevir_l say:

Hi,

 

I regret to inform you that it is not possible to delete just the Skype account and not the Microsoft account.

 

Once a Skype account has been merged to a Microsoft account and marked for account closure, the Microsoft account will be deleted as well.

 

However, if the Skype account is not yet merged to a Microsoft account, you can create a new Microsoft account, merge it on your Skype account and mark it for account closure.

 

Here are the steps on how to merge a Skype account to a Microsoft account.

 

  • Sign in to https://login.live.com using your Skype Name.

  • A message that says “Your Skype account is now a Microsoft account” will appear.

  • Read the instructions carefully and enter your newly created Microsoft account.

  • The system will ask you to enter a single password for your Skype and Microsoft account.

  • Once done, you can now go ahead and mark the account for closure.

MSA account, but can't sync settings? Problem solved.

There have been a few threads over time about not being able to sync settings even though there is a valid MSA account.  Well, here is what I have found.  I have an Exchange Online (Plan 1) account I use for my email.  When I add that account to Windows Mail, it takes control of the security settings and disallows settings sync.  But, Exchange Online (Plan 1) does not allow its management console to access the setting to enable sync.  To get that you have to buy a business plan.  I have a family plan Office 365, so I can use Outlook to access the Exchange Online account without it seizing control of settings.  Apparently, a business plan Office365 will also allow access to those security controls.
