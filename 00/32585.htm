vijin v say:

Hi,

 

I would suggest you to create a Create a Task Scheduler to Shut Down the PC and check if it helps.


Follow the mention steps:

a.    Open the Start Menu, then type taskschd.msc in the search box and press enter.

b.    Create Task link on the right-hand side

c.    Now you’ll want to give the task a name, and make sure to check the box for “Run with highest privileges”, since the shutdown utility requires admin access.

d.    Now switch to the Triggers tab, click New, and then setup a schedule that makes sense for you, like every night at midnight.

e.    Now flip over to the Actions tab, click New, and then enter shutdown into the Program box, and /S into the parameters box. If you want to force a shutdown and kill every app, you can use /S /F to force it to happen right away

f.     Next head to the Conditions tab, and make sure to check the box to only start the task if the computer is idle for more than x minutes. You can also choose to stop the task if the PC isn’t idle to help make sure it doesn’t kick in if you are on the PC

g.    Lastly, head to the Settings tab, and you can choose to restart the task every 30 minutes if the task fails—that way, if you are on the PC for a while past the normal time, the job will start up later on and shut down the PC.

Adding your Yahoo! Mail account to the Windows 10 Mail app:

-Go to the Mail app, and click on the gear icon at the bottom left.

-Under "Settings" click "Manage Accounts"

-Click "+ Add Account"

-Choose "Yahoo! Mail"

-Enter your Yahoo email address, your name for sending messages, and the password of your Yahoo account.

-Click "Sign In."  Your email account will be set up.

-When completed, click "Done."

-Under "Settings" click "Manage Accounts"

-Under "Settings" click "Manage Accounts."

-Click on "Change mailbox sync settings." (If this option is grayed out, the app is attempting to sync your mail.)

Incoming email server: imap.mail.yahoo.com:993:1  -or-  imap.mail.yahoo.com

Outgoing (SMTP) email server: smtp.mail.yahoo.com:465:1  -or-  smtp.mail.yahoo.com
-All the checkboxes should be checked below this section.

6/5/13: I just called Surface RT tech support. It is NOT possible for your other computers to browse Surface files so don't bother with all this research. And the Answer post should be deleted. This is from the Surface RT User's Guide, p. 75--note the sentence in bold.

Join a homegroup
A homegroup is a group of PCs on a home network that can share devices (such as printers), and libraries (your Documents, Pictures, Music, and Video libraries). Homegroups make sharing easier.
If a homegroup already exists on your home network, Surface can join it. Here’s how:
1. Open the Settings charm, tap or click Change PC settings, and then tap or click HomeGroup.
2. Tap or click Join. You can get the password from anyone else in the homegroup.
After you join a homegroup, you can access files on computers that are part of your homegroup. However, you can’t share content on Surface with computers in your homegroup.
Notes
 For more info about homegroups, see the topic Homegroup from start to finish on Windows.com.
 Surface RT can’t create a homegroup, but you can join one.

An obtuse workaround is to use SkyDrive and upload your Surface RT files from there: On the Surface RT, click Desktop to go to Desktop; from Desktop, launch Internet Explorer and open SkyDrive; in SkyDrive, click Upload;  in Choose Files to Upload window, you are accessing Surface RT files; browse to the desired folder and/or files and upload.

Another workaround: On the Surface RT, click Desktop to go to Desktop; from Desktop, launch File Explorer; browse to Network, [ComputerName]\Users\[UserName]\SkyDrive; add it this location to Favorites in the left pane on File Explorer; right-click the new favorite, Send to, Desktop (create shortcut); from now on, just click this shortcut on the Surface Desktop and copy/paste any desired folders and files into SkyDrive.

ken blake say:
On Mon, 13 Sep 2010 14:28:55 +0000, Jimary wrote:
 
> I have tried several options for printing a list of the document folders in a library.  No problem copying the list, but it will not past to either Word of Excel. Is there an easier way to do this than to simply Print Screen?
 
 
Here are four ways:
 
1. Go to a command prompt and issue the command
 
dir [drive:folder] > c:\tempfilename (you can use any name and put it
in any folder you want)
 
Then open notepad, open tempfilename, and print it from there.
 
2. Write (for example in Notepad) a 1-line text file:
DIR %1 /O >LPT1:
 
Save it as "printdir.bat" in the "Send To" folder.
 
Then, to print list of files in any folder, right-click that folder
and select Send to | printdir.bat
 
To include subfolders, change the command to DIR %1 /O/S >LPT1:
 
3. Go to
http://support.microsoft.com/default.aspx?scid=kb;EN-US;Q321379 and
follow the instructions there.
 
4. Download and use any of the several freeware/shareware utilities
that can do this, such as the popular
http://www.karenware.com/powertools/ptdirprn.asp
