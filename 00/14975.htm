Typically, you would use the DateAdd() function as it provides more universal functionality by being able to add months (m), years (yyyy) or even weekdays (w) and/or quarters (q), etc. but for all intents and purposes you simply need to add 90 to your [Begin Date] as Access treats dates as a 1 for every day past Dec 31, 1899. These two queries should result in the same thing:

SELECT DateAdd("d", 90, [Begin Date]) AS [Expiration Date] FROM myTable
... or,
SELECT [Begin Date]+90 AS [Expiration Date] FROM myTable 

This will only work when adding or subtracting a number of days.

stefan blom say:

The Navigation pane displays paragraphs whose "Outline level" is something other than "Body Text" so this is the setting you have to watch out for.

If the title above the TOC is showing in the Navigation pane, then change its "Outline level." On the other hand, if actual TOC entries show up duplicated in the Navigation pane, look at the "Outline level" for the TOC styles.

The TOC styles (TOC 1, TOC 2, etc. up to and including TOC 9) are used by Word to format the entries in a table of contents (TOC 1 determines the formatting for level 1 entries, TOC 2 determines the formatting of level 2 entries, etc.).

To modify any paragraph style, type its name in the Apply Styles pane (Ctrl+Shift+S) and click the Modify button. In the Modify Style dialog box, click the Format drop down and then click Paragraph. The "Outline level" is on the Indents and Spacing tab.
