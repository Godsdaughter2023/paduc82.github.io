router password change - Microsoft Community

aaroncox2 say:

router password change

How do I change a wifi router password?

a. user say:

What a great question - this is something that everyone should do.

Begin by signing in to your router's administration console, which holds all your router's settings. Do this by entering your router's IP address into the address bar of any browser and pressing Enter.

Your router uses one of the handful of standard IP addresses assigned to consumer routers. You can easily find your router's IP address with a web search; just search for <make and model of your router> IP address. For example, my router's IP address is 192.168.1.1.

You'll be brought to the sign-in screen for your router, where you'll need to enter your router's username and password. Routers' default usernames and passwords are posted on the web, which is why you need to change them. Just search for <your make and model of router> username and password.

For example, the default username and password for my router were admin and admin. Real secure, huh?

Once you've entered your router's administration console, look through the screens - there will be lots of them! - until you find the screen that lets you change the admin console's username and password. Choose a long, complicated, un-guessable password.

Once you are in your router's admin console, if you're using a WiFi network in any way (even if only for your printer,) and if you haven't already done so, you should password-protect your WiFi network. Choose a different long, complicated, un-guessable password and make sure your network is secured with the WPA2-PSK method. Note: Once you password-protect your network you need to enter that password to every device on the network.

Don't forget those passwords!
