when is it safe to connect peripherals or disconnect - Microsoft Community

kimwaller say:

when is it safe to connect peripherals or disconnect

when connecting to a usb or other bus ,when can i disconnect safely?
tryx3 say:

When a USB device is connected to your PC, a USB icon appears in your System tray [i.e. the right-hand portion of the Taskbar where the clock also appears].

 

When you want to disconnect a USB device, single click on that icon, look at the list of USB devices that appears, click on the desired device, wait until the confirmatory message bubble appears telling you that it is now safe to disconnect the device.

 

There are some useful exceptions.  Some devices, such as USB memory sticks, can be disconnected without having to go through that routine.  You can check your devices by connecting them then looking at Control panel, Device manager*, Disk drives section [click on the little arrow to the left of the entry to open up the list of disk drives], select the device you want to check, right-click, Properties.  Check that the Policies tab entry shows it as being optimised for quick removal - that means you can just pull it out at any time [but it would still be wise to see if its light has stopped flashing to tell you it has finished doing whatever you have told it to do].

 

* If you are not logged into an admin account then the PC will give you an annoying beep & a warning message telling you that you will not be allowed to change anything in Device manager & only then will it open the thing for you to look at.

 

If you are ever unsure then shut down the PC.  All devices can be disconnected when the PC is off.
