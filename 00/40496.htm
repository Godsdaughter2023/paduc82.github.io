a. user say:

Hello,

Thanks for keeping us updated on the issue. I would advise you to go through the following steps and check if it helps.

 

Use the steps below and verify that the default sound device is set correctly -

a. Open Sound by clicking the Start button, and then clicking Control Panel. In the search box, type sound, and then click Sound.

b. Click the Recording tab, click Microphone, and then click Properties.

c. Click the Levels tab and make sure the Mute button is on, if off click it to turn on sound for this connection, and then click Apply.

d. To listen to microphone sounds through your speakers or headphones, click the Listen tab; click the Listen to this device check box.

e. Also click the advanced tab, click to clear the Allow applications to take exclusive control of this device check box, and then click OK.

 

It might be that sound drivers are corrupted. I would suggest you to uninstall the drivers and download and install it on the system. Follow the steps mentioned below.

  

a. Click of the Start menu and type in Device manager in the start search box and press enter.  

b. Click on ‘Sound, video and game controller’ and right click on the driver to uninstall it.

Note: While you are uninstalling the driver make sure that you select the option to delete the driver software as well.

 

Download and Install the driver from the manufacturer’s website.

Go to the manufacturer’s support website and search for your system model and download the latest drivers for the same. For additional information, refer the following link

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows/no-sound-help#no-sound=windows-8&v1h=win8tab4&v2h=win7tab1&v3h=winvistatab1&v4h=winxptab1

 

I would also suggest you to contact your system manufacturer once, and have a diagnostics run on the system to check if there is any issue with the hardware.

 

Hope the information helps. Let us know if you need further assistance with Windows related issues, we’ll be glad to assist you.

Thank you
