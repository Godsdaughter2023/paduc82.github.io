considerate_guy say:

Again, you can't use the overall Project | Audio mix settings to adjust this because that is a global setting.  That's just to set the overall balance between the original audio and the added audio.  You can also use the Project tab to pick one track to emphasize, but it can't change dynamically throughout the project.

I really wish I could see a screenshot of your project storyboard to know exactly what you have happening when you want the video volume to be quieter... but I've given you steps that should help you.

Find the section of the baseball game video that is too loud.  If it is only happening at the same time as the music and nowhere else, then just select that video clip, go to the Video Tools | Edit tab and set the Video volume lower.  The Video volume applies to the selected clip, not all clips.  So in that way, you can lower the volume on the noisy clip so the music is heard instead.

The second part of the equation is what to do if that clip extends for longer than the music.  As I showed in the pictures above, the way to compensate for that is to split the video clip into parts.  They will still visually look like one clip when played, but now you can adjust the Video volume on just the clip you want without affect other parts of the clip.
