Hi nweissma,

 

If the drive shows unallocated under the lower section of the disk management, then it won’t appear in the upper section of the disk management window and in Windows Explorer.

You’ve to format the volume to make it appear under Windows Explorer.

 

Steps to format a partition/volume

a.    Click on Start; in the start search type diskmgmt.msc and hit Enter.

b.    Right click on the ‘Unallocated’ space and choose New Simple Volume.

c.    Follow the instructions further to make the partition healthy.

 

Once the volume is created, you’ll be able to see the partition appearing both on the upper section of the disk management window and on Windows Explorer.

 

For more information, visit Formatting disks and drives

Hi ezed9,

The reason that I make the distinction of upgrade vs. update is because the WAU is indeed a complete new OS installation along the lines of the same procedure as the upgrade from a previous qualifying OS (Windows 7, 8, 8.1).

If during the update (upgrade) installation you choose to save your files and apps it is exactly the same procedure along with the same potential outcome(s). The main difference being that the resulting Windows Old folder that is created for the purpose of rolling back if you want or need to is good for only 30 days as opposed to 90 days.

The check that was run on your device for compatibility with the v.1607 Win 10 tells you that the device hardware is compatible with the OS, not necessarily all of the software, drivers, and potentially corrupted files that exist on the device.

Therefore, when an upgrade type of installation (in this case the WAU) repeatedly fails do to such conflicts there are basically 2 avenues of choice available. One is to chase down and identify and correct the conflict(s) and try again and repeat as needed, which is sometimes successful and other times you just end up realizing that the other choice would have been the better, faster, less headache way to go in the first place.

The other choice (and the best by far) is:

A clean installation with disc erase and reformat, this is the best and most trouble free, both during and after, type of OS installation that is, for any OS, as even a seemingly successful upgrade type of installation can often turn out to be, shall we say, a bit buggy after the fact.
