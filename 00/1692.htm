alice_941 say: I assume you are using a table in Word, not Excel.  First make sure the margins of your cells are all the same.  Click on or select a cell with which you are having trouble.  Then find the tab at the top called Table Tools -- Layout.  Then click on Table Properties.  On the Table Tab, click on Options.  You will see the default cell margins (which you can change if you want to).  Then find and click on the Cell Tab.  Click on Options.  The box that says, "Same as the whole table" should be clicked.  If it is not, then see if the left and right margins for the cell are the same as they were on the Options for the whole table that you looked at before.  You may have a right cell margin that is larger (that is, indented more fromt he right-hand order of a cell) than the rest of the table.  (It would only be that way if you set it that way.)  Another thing, as Ms. Barnhill suggests, is to look in the Paragraph group (on the Home Tab) and make sure there is no Right Indent or Mirror Indents set.  Clear those if any were set.  A third thing you could do is to highlight (by clicking and holding down the left click button) all the cells you are concerned about, and clicking once on the Right Justification button ("Align Text Right") in the Paragraph group (on the Home Tab).  This way you will be sure that all the cells are Right Justified.  Finally, one more solution is to put the text (such as Page) in its own row at the top of the table or actually anywhere within the table and put the numbers in a separate row, or rows, below that row.  If you don't want to see the line, you can go to the Table Design tab and get rid of (or just lighten) the line.  When the numbers are kept separate from the text, you can set alignment (Left, Center, or Right) (or even left or right indentation) differently for the text and for the numbers.  I hope one or more of these will help you.

steve rindsberg say:

>> Just don't get why the entire theme would change when you just want to change the slide size.

Because the theme is what's called a SuperTheme ... it actually has multiple themes built in, including versions for both 4x3 and 16x9.  Each of these versions may have graphics positioned differently and so on, so that distortion is minimized when you switch sizes. 

If you or someone else has modified the 4x3 version of the theme, say by choosing a different color theme, when you switch to the other built-in size, PPT applies the built-in theme for that size, including color and font themes.  That'd be why your colors and such change.

Another possible fix (I don't recall whether this will work or not) but its' worth a try:

Open your presentation, save it as a new theme, then apply that them to a copy of the presentation.  PPT tosses out the multiple themes when we civilians save a theme. 

Or start a new blank presentation and insert the slides from the current file into it, electing to preserve the original formatting.  Again, not positive that this'll fix it, but I think it might.

Or take the quick way out and follow my original suggestion.  Two seconds out of your life and it's fixed.

jllatham say:

Why do I think either the answer is very simple, or much more complex than I can believe.

The very simple answer: Type your title into a cell near the top of the worksheet; use Format -> Cell to size and color the text the way you want.  To take it a step further you can select that cell and several more in the same row and use Format -> Cell and on the alignment tab for horizontal alignment you can choose Center Across Selection for a nice centered at the top of the sheet appearance.

The next answer I might give would be to use File -> Page Setup and choose the [Header/Footer] tab to create custom header (title) and/or footer for the sheet.  These do not use up worksheet space, and they aren't visible until the sheet is printed or print-previewed.

If neither of these suggestions hits the mark, then please explain a little more about how you want to get your title set up.
