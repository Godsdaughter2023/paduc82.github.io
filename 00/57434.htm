hal hostetler - mvp-outlook say:
No, you DO need a filter when connecting an older analog device like phones and modems.  DSL adds Internet service through essentially a radio signal put on the phone line.  This signal must be filtered out before the phone line will work correctly with a modem or old fashioned analog telephone.  The signal is NOT filtered out on the line that connects to your DSL modem.  If your DSL modem is built into your computer (rare, but there are DSL modem cards), then you'd need two phone lines, an unfiltered one to connect to the DSL modem and a filtered one to connect to the fax modem.
