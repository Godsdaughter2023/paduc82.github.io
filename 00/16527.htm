yves dhondt say:

Try using non-breaking spaces instead of ordinary spaces. You can enter them using CTRL+SHIFT+space in Word.

jeeped say:

Use a non-breaking space in place of a regular space between the periods to avoid expansion in a justified paragraph.

The non-breaking space can be inserted by holding the left ALT key down and typing 0160 on the numpad, then releasing the ALT key.

You can use Search and Replace (CTRL+H) to replace all instances of .<space>.<space>. with .<non-breaking space>.<non-breaking space>. or setup an Auto-Correct entry in Word Options to replace as you type.

I had actually found a way to make Office 2010 my default Office program for Windows 7.
  1. Click Start.
  2. Click Control Panel.
  3. When everything has loaded, scan through to find Microsoft Office [Office edition (e.g. Home and Student, Home and Business, etc)] 2010. Click on it when you see it.
  4. Click 'Change' on top of all the programs.
  5. Depending on your setting, a UAC window might pop up. If your account is not an administrative account, ask the administrator to provide the password. Click on Yes.
  6. A window will say, "Please wait while setup prepare necessary files." When done, click on Repair. Wait for it to get repaired.
  7. After which, restart your computer.
These steps can also help you to clear all the problems you experience with Microsoft Office 2010.
