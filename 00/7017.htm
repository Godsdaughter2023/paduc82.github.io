chip pearson say:

Jim,

 

That looks like some of my code. The problem is that you are closing and then re-opening the workbook, which causes VBA to stop running and reset its global variables. Normally, you would get a message prompt asking whether to save and close the workbook before re-opening it. But you have DisplayAlerts = False, so no message is displayed and Excel takes the default action, which is to save, close and re-open the file without notifying you.

 

This causes RunWhen to get initialized to 0. Then, by calculation, you set RunWhen to Now+"00:00:10". This is not the same time value that was used previously by StartTimer to initialize the timer.  Therefore,  OnTime can't find the timer to stop it. Since OnTime is an application method, it will get executed as long as Excel is open, regardless of whether the workbook that initiated the timer is open. Excel will open it if necessary. The only way to stop a timer whose start time is unknown is to stop Excel.

 

If you put a debug statement at the very beginning of StartTimer,

 

Debug.Print "StartTimer: " & Format(RunWhen, "hh:mm:ss")

 

you'll see in the Immediate window (CTRL G to view the window) that RunWhen is 00:00:00 when StartTimer is executed. The time that was used to initialize the timer no longer exists.

 

Get rid of the line

 

 Workbooks.Open ActiveWorkbook.FullName

 

and everything will work fine.

zune updates - Microsoft Community

kertguiliani say:

zune updates

My first computer crashed that had my zune music on it so i got a new computer but cant sync with out updayting the zune player will i loose all my music by doing the update??

 

mysqueakycat say:

My first computer crashed that had my zune music on it so i got a new computer but cant sync with out updayting the zune player will i loose all my music by doing the update??

 


When  you do the update through the Zune software on your computer it does not delete your music.  I would make sure, though, before you connect your Zune to first go into the software and change your sync option to manual (if you leave it on "all" as soon as the update is done it will likely start deleting the music).  Once the update is complete, you can reverse sync your music to your computer (and you could subsequently change your sync options as you wish). 

If any of the music is Zune Pass, though, you'll probably have to go into your history and redownload as I don't believe the licenses transfer with the reverse sync.  Same goes for any protected music you got from the Marketplace.

nsilmike say:

My first computer crashed that had my zune music on it so i got a new computer but cant sync with out updayting the zune player will i loose all my music by doing the update??

No.  Your device content will be recognized as guest content (content from another PC.)  You do not have to switch to manual sync...etc.
When you are all set and updated, if you want it recategorized to music (vs. showing up in the reserved space as guest content) then you can easily do so:
Link to this PC (so it will become the 'master' and will be able to automatically sync if your settings allow this.)
Reverse sync some or all of the content to your PC.
Choose a sync mode- automatic or choose (or manual...but manual does nothing... you do it all manually)
If you choose 'automatic' then everything you reverse sync'd will recategorize to music, and will be managed by the zune software.  New music will automatically sync.  Music you have reverse sync'd and then remove from the PC will be removed from the device (music you have not reverse sync'd remains unmanaged as guest content in that reserved space category.)
If you pick 'choose' mode, then anything you reverse sync and choose will be managed by zune software like it was automatic.  Anything not chosen will remain unmanaged in reserved space.
And the same rules apply to your podcasts, pictures, and videos.
