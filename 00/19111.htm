bingadselyse say:
Hi DaneHeald,

Thank you for reaching out to us with your question! I completely understand your concern and I’m happy to help!

Given the information that you have provided me with, it seems that you may be experiencing this issue due to having sub-accounts under one main customer account. This occurs as each of the sub-accounts created under the main account shares the same customer ID. As the help article states, UET tags are stored at the customer level, therefore, any accounts that share the same customer ID will have access to each accounts UET tag.

To answer your concern, the security of the subaccount’s are not compromised, as the only thing that is shared and the only thing that the other account can see is the UET tag.

If you wish to avoid your UET tags being shared across all of your accounts, we suggest creating completely separate accounts and linking each account to the main account. This is a common route taken by agency accounts when managing their client accounts and I will provide instructions on how to do this, below. Doing this gives each account their own customer ID and eliminates the issue of sharing UET tags across all accounts. The only time all UET tags will be accessible at the same time, is when they are being viewed in the main account. Linked accounts WILL NOT be able to view UET tags for other accounts. You can then give whoever you wish access to whichever account(s) you would like for them to have access too, without having to worry about them having access to ALL accounts.

Creating and linking accounts:

  1. To start, you will need to create your main account. This is the account that you will use to linking your “client” accounts.
  2. Then you will need to go ahead and create your client accounts. Please keep in mind that you will need to use a different email address when creating each account.
  3. From your main account, you will send a request to each of your client accounts, requesting permission to manage those accounts. The steps on how to do so are as follows:

A. Click the gear icon , and then click Accounts & Billing

    B. Click the Requests tab.

C. Click Link to account.

D. Under Linking detail, enter the Account number. The account number is an 8-digit mix of numbers and letters.

Enter the Start date, which is the date you want to start managing the account. Even if you select Immediately, you cannot start managing the account until the account owner approves the request.

Click Next if you don't wish to take over billing.
Otherwise, select I will take over billing for this account. Provide your payment details in the remaining section, and then click Next.

E. Review your request and click Send request.

4. The client will accept your request and you will be able to access their accounts from your main account. To learn more, our Managing your clients as an agency with Bing Ads help article is a great resource.

Now that you have all of these accounts separate, you can create UET tags on each account for the associated websites. Please note, you will need to remove the previous UET tag and replace it with the new one.

I hope this helps! Should you have any further questions or concerns, please feel free to let us know as we’re happy to provide further assistance!

I had a similar issue once with my old phone, a 5610 (both phones are on s40 operating system). The reason for it was that I used up the phone memory, and that the phone was having a hard time to access both phone memory and memory card. If your friend has the same issue, what he should do is to try to turn on the phone with the memory card not inserted. After which \, he should free up phone memory by deleting messages or moving the pictures and videos he has into memory card. Also, to prevent future problems, set the pictures and videos to default save into memory card.
