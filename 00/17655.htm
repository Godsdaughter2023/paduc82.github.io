Sub ListBox_MultiColumns_FilteredData()
'06 Feb, 2015

Dim obj As Object
Set r = Selection
Set obj = ActiveSheet.OLEObjects.Add(ClassType:="Forms.listBox.1", Link:=False, _
DisplayAsIcon:=False, Left:=r.Left, Top:=r.Top, Width:=150, Height:=80)

With obj.Object
.ColumnCount = 3
.ColumnWidths = "50;50;50"

For Each rr In ActiveSheet.ListObjects(1).ListColumns(1).DataBodyRange
If ActiveSheet.Rows(rr.Row).Hidden = False Then
With obj.Object
.AddItem rr.Value
.List(.ListCount - 1, 1) = rr.Offset(, 1).Value
.List(.ListCount - 1, 2) = rr.Offset(, 2).Value
End With
End If
Next

a. user say:

Hi,

We greatly appreciate your time and effort for posting here at Microsoft Community. Let me see what I can do to help you with your concern.


I completely understand that you want to transfer your old emails from your old Hotmail account to your new Outlook.com account. Here are some options that we can suggest:

First, you can try to create an alias under your new Outlook.com account. Then switch them so that your original Outlook.com account will be a secondary alias of the alias that you have created which is now the primary alias. Since your original Outlook.com account is now a secondary alias, you can now delete it and use it as an alias to your old Hotmail account. However, you have to wait for 30 days before you can use that alias again and to set your expectations, it will be available to any user after 30 days. If you have successfully added your original Outlook.com account as an alias to your old Hotmail account after 30 days, you just have to switch them so your original Outlook.com account will be your primary alias.

The next option is you can use Windows Live Mail to move your emails from your old account to your new account. Windows Live Mail is a mail client that you can use to add and manage all your email accounts. It's as easy as typing in an email address and password, and then following a few steps. You can download Windows Live Mail by going to this link for free.

Once you've downloaded Windows Essentials (It is a suite of products available in one easy download. You can download them all, or choose just the ones you want) and installed Windows Live Mail, you are now ready to add your accounts to it. You may refer to this article for the instructions on how to add accounts on Windows Live Mail.

After you've finished adding both of your Hotmail and Outlook.com accounts, all your emails, contacts, and calendars from those accounts will automatically sync to your Windows Live Mail. Once the sync is complete, you can now start moving your emails by dragging the emails from your old Hotmail account to your Outlook.com account.

Take note that old accounts have a higher reputation than newly created accounts. If you will move your emails from your old Hotmail account to your new Outlook.com account, it means that you will be using a new account that might need to build reputation to be able to use all the features of Outlook.com. However, if you will just switch aliases, the reputation of your old Hotmail account will be carried by your Outlook.com account.

On the other hand, please be informed that email forwarding only applies to new incoming emails. For example, if you set up email forwarding today, all emails that you will receive starting today will be forwarded to the email account where you want it to be forwarded. Emails that were received before this day or before the time you have set up email forwarding will not be forwarded.

In addition, you have to disable email forwarding on your Hotmail account before you close it (if you will decide to close it) to avoid problems with your Outlook.com account. 

Feel free to post back if you have further questions or clarifications.

The most common reason for a Certificate Error is that your computer date and time are incorrect. Check that via Control Panel and verify that the full date and time are correct - month, day, year, hour, minute and time zone. Correct it if wrong and reboot.
Then go to Windows Update and check for the optional update for Root Certificates and install them as offered (as well as any critical and important updates you have not yet installed).

And, no, the certificate error has nothing to do with hacking.
