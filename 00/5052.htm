christopheroxford say:

How to round to nearest .5 or .0, with .1 and .6 break points?

For production purposes, we need to round length dimensions like this:

for values x.1 to x.599, we round to x.5

for values x.6 to (x+1).099, we need to round to (x+1).0

(please excuse my sloppy syntax)

put another way:

x.099 rounds to x.0, while x.1 rounds to x.5

x.599 rounds to x.5, while x.6 rounds up to next .0 ie, (x+1).0

I feel like there should be a way to do this fairly easily with some If/Then statements testing the first 4 digits past the decimal.  We keep 4 digits past the decimal for accuracy (metric to inch conversion), and we need to maintain that accuracy right until we do this rounding. Sometimes it makes a difference.

Please let me know if I'm on the right track.

Thanks, Chris
