greginmich say:

I have McAfee Security installed on my computer but just installed Windows Defender.   Will both of these work together?

Yes, they will work together in Version 1607 of Windows 10; and anyone using a third-party AV app in the current version should definitely enable Windows Defender’s Limited Periodic Scanning mode. Automated on-demand scanning with a secondary AV/Antimalware app is really the next-best thing to having two real-time AV apps, because every AV/Antimalware app is actually designed to detect and remove malware that’s already been installed on your PC (in this case, because your primary AV app missed it):

https://www.tenforums.com/tutorials/51514-limited-periodic-scanning-turn-off-windows-10-a.html

https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/protect/wiki/protect_defender-protect_scanning/limited-periodic-scanning-in-windows-10-to-provide/3dcfc8dd-36b4-43f2-9a0a-732e43191cf3

The same principle of periodic scanning also applies if you’re using Windows Defender in a standalone configuration. In that case, you should be running periodic scans with one or more third-party AV/Antimalware apps in order to catch anything that Defender might have missed. And you should really make a habit of running your third-party AV scans at regular intervals (periodically, proactively) rather than waiting until you see signs of a problem (reactively); because lots of malware is specifically designed to hide its presence from PC users:

https://www.bleepingcomputer.com/forums/t/407147/answers-to-common-security-questions-best-practices/?p=2413189

Unfortunately, Limited Periodic Scanning doesn’t appear to be working properly at this point – because the Windows Defender Scheduled Scan task isn’t running consistently under its default System account. See my reply here:
