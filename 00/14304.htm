explain TRIMMEAN - Microsoft Community

joeu2004 say:

explain TRIMMEAN

Can anyone explain the design of Excel TRIMMEAN?

The help page states:  "TRIMMEAN rounds the number of excluded data points down to the nearest multiple of 2. If percent = 0.1, 10 percent of 30 data points equals 3 points. For symmetry, TRIMMEAN excludes a single value from the top and bottom of the data set.".

I confirmed that this is indeed how TRIMMEAN operates in XL2003.

Does it still work that way in XL2007 and later, when TRIMMEAN was mainstreamed?

Why is excluding 1 at each end of 30 data points any more symmetrical than excluding 3 at each end?

Why is excluding a total of 2 any more a multiple of 2 then excluding a total of 6 -- if that is indeed the "number of excluded data points" being rounded down to "the nearest multiple of 2"?

I understand the need to round a non-integer number of data points (e.g. 10% of 45); and in that case, rounding, rounding up or rounding down are equally valid design choices, IMHO.

But why "round down" an integer number of data points (e.g. 20% of 5 becomes zero!)?
hansv mvp say:

In the example, if you specify that you want to exclude 10% (0.1) of 30 data points, you'd have to exclude 3 points in all, not from each side. However, since 3 is odd, you can't do that symmetrically. We want to exclude the same number of data points from each side, so the total has to be even.

We could round down, up or to the nearest even number, but we don't want to exclude more than the specified percentage, so TRIMMEAN always rounds the number of excluded points down.

In your last example, with only 5 data points, 20% would be 1 data point. If we excluded 1 on either side, we would actually exclude 40% of the data points, double the specified percentage; this is undesirable. In this situation, it's preferable not to exclude any data points.

alkenn say:

Hi,

You may find what you want in the Developer tab in the ribbon.  First, click on "File", and then in the list on the left click on "Options".  In the "Visio Options" window, from the column on the left, select "Advanced".  Scroll top the bottom, and under the heading "General" tick the box "Run in Developer mode".  Click "OK".

Now, your ribbon will include the developer tab.  In the "Shape design" section, you can select the rectangle tool, the line tool etc. by clicking on the drop-down next to "Rectangle".  Select a shape in your drawing, and you will be able to click on the drop-down next to "Operations", and get to all those old tools like "Trim", Join" and so on.  The only one that is missing is "Fit Curve".  The only way to get that is to customise the Quick Access Toolbar by adding "More Commands" and choosing from "All Commands".

Hope this helps.
