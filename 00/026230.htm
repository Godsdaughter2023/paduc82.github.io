bill manville say:

Given that it doesn't crash in safe mode, it is almost certain that the problem is due to an add-in or startup program.
Disable all your add-ins (File > Options > Add-Ins > Manage : Excel Add-ins > Go ) and similarly for COM Add-ins and then restart Excel.

If the problem no longer occurs, try re-enabling the add-ins one at a time until the problem recurs; you then know the culprit.

Alternatively it could be a workbook in an XLSTART folder.
Look in C:\Program Files\Microsoft Office\Office14\XLSTART
and in whatever VBA tells you is Application.StartupPath
(possibly C:\Documents and Settings\<username>\Application Data\Microsoft\Excel\XLSTART )

Or, least likely, a file in the alternate startup folder ( Application.AltStartupPath ).

Good luck, and please tell us what the culprit was.

It works if

WORD 2010 brochure background graduated color prints in bars, not a - Microsoft Community

jimgleason say:

WORD 2010 brochure background graduated color prints in bars, not a smooth gradation as on the screen

After applying background color with fill as graduated (one color), my WORD document (Office 2010) looks great on screen, but shows a series of horizontal repeated bars when printed. Any ideas on how to ge **** smoothly graduated as it appears on the screen?
guessed say: This is known as "colour banding" and is due to not enough colour depth on the colours. Word is not really the sort of tool that gives you a great deal of control in this area. Your choices to resolve this include:
1. Make the gradient more extreme. If the colour change is more dramatic, the bands will be closer together and hence less visible.
2. Make the blended area change faster (eg. use a different colour in the middle). This again makes the bands closer together and hence less visible.
3. Use a bitmap in the same place and put this together in Photoshop (or similar). Add noise/dithering to the blend so that the bands are broken up.

Some further reading
http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colour_banding
http://slodive.com/photoshop/how-to-correct-banding-in-your-gradients-using-photoshop/
