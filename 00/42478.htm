cmdrkeene say:
It should work the same way.  If the folder location is on a network server drive, you can click in the address bar to copy/paste it. 

It's up to your email program for whether that link becomes "clickable" or not, but even if they can't click it, the email recipient could just copy/paste the link.

  • Tip: You can also hold Shift while you right-click any file or folder, and the right-click menu will show a secret "Copy as Path" option.  After you copy-as-path, you can paste it into your email app.


  • Extra Tip:  Microsoft Office (Work, Excel, Outlook) will convert paths into links, but only if the path begins with \\something or http://something.  If the path uses a drive letter (such as W:\Something) it won't become a link, because the email recipient might not have that same drive letter (or it might be a totally different drive with the same letter.


  • Extra Bonus Tip:  If the path has a space in it, type it in quotes.  After you type the final quote and press space, Office will convert it into a clickable link, and even automatically remove the quotes.  For example, type this into an Outook email, quotes included, then press space:

    "\\server\folder\some file name with spaces.doc"
