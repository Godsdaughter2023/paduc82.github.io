hula_baloo say:

 The drive may well be failing, but don't give up yet.  First take it to another PC and see if you can access it.  If you can, copy what you need.  If you can't access it from another computer, the best you can do is to contact WD and see if they can help.  There are services that recover data from failed drives, but in most cases they're prohibitively expensive. 

  I had a 1 TB Seagate drive to fail on me a few months ago, but was able to connect it to another PC and copy the data before having to reformat it.  Once I reformatted it several times, it became usable again, but I know it's living on borrowed time.  :-(  These things do wear out from time to time.  The disks rotate at 5400 to 7200 RPM and the access arms move in and out to access different sectors.  Anything with moving parts eventually wears out unfortunately.  Fortunately hard drives are much much cheaper than cars and houses in that regard!
