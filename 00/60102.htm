I am observing this behavior as well. I can only assume it's considered "by design", but I sure would like to know why someone thinks this is good "design".

Windows is handling uninstall shortcuts explicitly. Interestingly, it is going so far as to identify the appropriate version of msiexec.exe for the target platform of the executable. I accidentally misconfigured the installer for my x64 (64-bit) version of my program, such that it was using CSIDL_SYSTEMX86 instead of CSIDL_SYSTEM for the folder in which to find msiexec.exe, and Windows did not hide the shortcut in that case. But it does hide the shortcut in the Start page when using CSIDL_SYSTEMX86 for the x86 (32-bit) version of my program, and when using CSIDL_SYSTEM for the x64 version.

I.e. it's matching the target platform of msiexec.exe to the target platform of the program itself and only if they are the same is the shortcut hidden.

Note also:

  • the shortcut is simply being hidden in the Start screen's Apps page; it is correctly installed in the Start Menu directory on the disk (and can in fact be successfully run from there).
  • Running the 32-bit msiexec.exe will in fact uninstall the 64-bit version of the program (at least for my program, as there are no custom uninstall steps). So ironically, by misconfiguring the installer, I can work around this issue for the x64 version of my program.

EDIT:

I think I know what the reasoning is. I never noticed this before, but if you right-click on a program shortcut in the Start screen's Apps page, there are several options, including an "Uninstall" option. So I guess the idea is that since the user can uninstall the app via the right-click menu, they can "tidy up" the Apps page by hiding the installer-provided uninstall shortcut.

Of course, there are at least two problems with their approach:

  • The right-click menu uninstall is not very discoverable, especially to those already familiar with Windows (i.e. who already know and expect to find a specific kind of user interface)
  • Selecting the "Uninstall" options from the right-click menu does not actually uninstall the program. All it does is open the Add/Remove Programs control panel, where you then have to go find the program you wanted to uninstall, select it, and then click the "Uninstall" button there.

Why they didn't just map the "Uninstall" menu item to the uninstall link they know is there and are hiding from the user, I have no idea. I guess it's just part of the usual half-assery that lies behind much of Microsoft's Windows UI design decisions.

But at least I think there's no mystery anymore about why Windows is doing this (at least not for me).

... While messing with diskpart in an attempt to solve my problem I noticed AUTOMOUNT in the list of commands.  This command "enables and disables the automatic mounting of basic volumes."  On my system, automount was turned off.  After enabling it using the command, I found my remote hard drive in the device manager, and uninstalled the driver.  Unplugging and plugging the drive caused the computer to again install the driver, but this time it mounted it in My Computer as well.  Now my device was visible in said location, but for some reason my access was denied.  I resolved this by enabling sharing under the advanced sharing tab in the device properties.  Problem solved.  I can now use the drive and did not have to use disk management or format anything.

 Yay! This worked for me using Windows 7! Here is the step by step version:

1. From RUN, type diskpart and click OK
2. At the DISKPART prompt, type automount
3. From the Device Manager , find the device not being recognized. Mine was under Storage Devices, I think.
4. Right click on the device and choose Properties
5. Click on the Driver tab
6. Click Uninstall
7. Unplug the device
8. Plug the device back in

 The driver was automatically installed, it's mounted to My Computer and I immediately got the AutoPlay window."

My photos Have Disappeared from MovieMaker--How do I restore?? - Microsoft Community

francisnc2014 say:

My photos Have Disappeared from MovieMaker--How do I restore??

Hello,

I have used Movie Maker for many movies and love the functionality, however, the program does not save the photos, even though I save the project until publishing.  How do I restore my photos?

Thank you!

considerate_guy say:

What do you mean when you say that Movie Maker doesn't save the photos?

Movie Maker isn't actually storing *any* media within the project file. It only references the files as they exist on your computer. Usually you import your photos and videos onto your PC. Then you add them to a project so you can preview how they will appear together. But the preview is dynamically loading the actual media from your computer each time.

If you save your project and reopen it, Movie Maker will recheck the original locations to find the source media files. If it can't find something, it won't be able to preview the clips and photos anymore. Is that what you are seeing? Are you seeing a yellow triangle with an exclamation point? If so, that's what's happening.

One common way this happens is if you are adding photos or videos that are stored on removable media (e.g. directly from a camera or SD card) and then you remove the card but still expect them to still be in the project.

Another way is if you rename folders of files, delete files or change there location. In any of those cases, Movie Maker can't reload the media the next time you start up the project.

In Skype (version 8) for desktop: Right click on your contact and click view profile or you can go to your chat with them and click their name at the top. Click Start translator. A new translated chat will be created.\

In Skype on Windows 10: Right click on your contact and click view profile or you can go to your chat with them and click their name at the top. Click Start translator. A new translated chat will be created.

In Skype (version 8) on mobile: In the chats screen, tap and hold on your contact and tap view profile or you can go to your chat with them and tap on their name at the top. Tap Start translator. A new translated chat will be created.

In your translated chat you will be prompted to choose your preferred languages and whether you want a female or male voice to represent you during voice and video calls. The languages will be pre-populated but you can also change them.

During your conversation, you will see your messages as they are originally typed and the translation in your preferred languages.

Note: Call captioning is available on Skype (version 8) and on Skype for Windows 10. To make a translated audio or video call, click on the audio or video call icon in your conversation. Your voice will be translated and the translation will also be shown as a caption in your call window.


Your translated chat will continue to be available in your recent chats with that person. If you want to start a translated chat with someone else, you'll need to start translator with them in the same way.

Important: A translated chat will only support you and a single contact. If you try to add another participant or bot to your translated chat, you will get a message that the translations will no longer work until the added participant is removed. Once they are removed, you will see your translations continue as normal.
