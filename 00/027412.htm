mickhen say:

How do I calculate a winning streak?

I have a sheet with stats on my sports league and I'm looking to keep a running count of winning / unbeaten / losing streaks.

 

I have a table set up for inputting all of the weekly scores and another matrix which shows a W/D/L for each team like so:

 

Round 1 2 3
Team A L W L
Team B W W W
Team C W W W
Team D L L W
Team E W W W
Team F W L L
Team G W W L
Team H L L L

 

What is the most effecient way to ask excel to count the conseutive wins / losses / draws to keep a running count on which team is performing best?

Thanks in advance,

Mick.

mike h.. say:

Hi,

 

Let's say the team name is in A2 and the results (W, D, L) are in B2 to N2. Put these at the end of that row, ARRAY enter them and then drag down for other rows. See below on how to enter an ARRAY formula.

 

Consecitive wins

=MAX(FREQUENCY(IF($B2:$N2="W",COLUMN($B2:$N2)),IF($B2:$N2<>"W",COLUMN($B2:$N2))))

 

Consecutive draws

=MAX(FREQUENCY(IF($B2:$N2="D",COLUMN($B2:$N2)),IF($B2:$N2<>"D",COLUMN($B2:$N2))))

 

Consecutive losses

=MAX(FREQUENCY(IF($B2:$N2="L",COLUMN($B2:$N2)),IF($B2:$N2<>"L",COLUMN($B2:$N2))))

 

This is an array formula which must be entered by pressing CTRL+Shift+Enter
and not just Enter. If you do it correctly then Excel will put curly brackets
around the formula {}. You can't type these yourself. If you edit the formula
you must enter it again with CTRL+Shift+Enter.
