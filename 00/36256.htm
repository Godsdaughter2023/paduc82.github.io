(0) Uninstalled both Netgear Wireless Config Utility Software and MA111  driver

- In your Wireless Connection Network Properties, "Networking" tab, disable QoS Packet Scheduler. Don't need it.
- In your Wireless Connection Network Properties, "Networking" tab, disable Internet Protocol Version 6 (IPv6). You must leave IPv4 enabled, you need this.

- Turn off Windows Firewall. Some folks think the handling of packets by Windows Firewall (and other firewalls) *might* cause connection problems. I've since turned this back on just to be safe, even though I'm behind a router.
- Turn your Wireless Adapter's romaing tendency to "conservative" or "minimum". Don't let it roam in "aggressive" or "maximum" or "seek best connection" mode.
