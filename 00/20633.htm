karl timmermans [outlook mvp 2012-2018] say:

How do I configure Outlook on the new laptop to do the same..........................

Starting point would be to take inventory of your Outlook configuration on the old system to see what you have

From the Outlook '2010 menu (on old system)

File --> Account Settings--> opens a window with multiple tabs, the ones that are of primary interest for this will be <Email> and <Data Files>

Based on your description, you are only using one email account so there should be only one listed. What's important on that page is the email account type (POP/SMTP or IMAP/SMTP). If the Account type is set as POP - look at the bottom of the page where you'll see a "Change Folder" button - the piece of information you want is the path/file name that follows the words "In Data File"

Click on the <Data FIles> tab and take note of all the data files that are listed. There should be at least 2 PST files on that list with one marked as the Default for the profile - take note of that path/file name for future reference.

How you proceed exactly on/with the new system depends on a few factors

1) The type of email account in use (POP or IMAP)

2) Do you remember your GMail username and password? Same ones you would use to access your Gmail account online via your browser

3) Whether your ultimate objective is to use both computers going forward or just the one

4) What additional files you may have in your existing Outlook profile (as listed under your Data Files tab)

Will be a lot easier to provide more specific info with the above answers rather than providing possible solutions to scenarios which may or may not be applicable
