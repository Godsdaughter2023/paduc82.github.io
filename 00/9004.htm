jllatham say:

First, have you simply tried making the column wider - but noting the way they were entered, probably not going to work.

 

I thought about some fancy text work involving adding a ' to the beginning of the text in the cell in another column, then doing replacement of the E+ and the decimal points.  But that isn't going to help because at best 4.90E+98 becomes 490E98, and yet you started out with 49E97.  But don't despair just yet - someone else may think of a way to perform some math magic combined with text magic to do it for you.

 

This macro MIGHT do it for you, if the entries are all similar to what you have provided; that is 2-digits, an E and 2 digits.  Change the C3:C4 to encompass the cells that you need to change, and do this with a COPY of your workbook since it's going to overwrite the current contents of the cells.  But the routine does work for the 2 examples you provided.

 

Sub FixIt()
  Const CellsToFix = "C3:C4" 'change to address holding cells to repair
 
  Dim fixitRange As Range
  Dim anyEntry As Range
  Dim currentEntry As String
  Dim endValue As Integer
 
  Set fixitRange = Range(CellsToFix)
  For Each anyEntry In fixitRange
    currentEntry = "" & anyEntry.Text
    'get rid of the decimal point
    currentEntry = Replace(currentEntry, ".", "")
    'change 0E+ to just E
    currentEntry = Replace(currentEntry, "0E+", "E")
    'now reduce the scientific notation part by 1
    endValue = Val(Right(currentEntry, 2)) - 1
    Mid(currentEntry, Len(currentEntry) - 1, 2) = Format(endValue, "00")
    With anyEntry
      .NumberFormat = "@" ' text
      .Value = currentEntry
    End With
  Next
   
End Sub

You need to replace the <spaces> with %20. A space character is ASCII 32 or 0×20 hex. You've got a long formula and I don't want to foul up something I cannot test but this is what you are looking for:

=HYPERLINK(SUBSTITUTE(<your formula>, " ", "%20"), "GOTO Next Available Cell")

To maintain a proper URL your file path and name should be prefixed with file:// much as a web address should start http:// . Maybe you should concatenate that prefix in.
