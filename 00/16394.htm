el_loco---01 say: Couplers are very sensitive lo location.

Depending on the type of coupler there are "near field" couplers and so called "far field" couplers.

Most consumer couplers are near field as they are simpler and cheaper. They require that the antenna is almost touching the coupler. Also the orientation matters a lot, as the RF signal is polarized (trust me unless you are a telecom engineer you don't want to know).

Lastly couplers tend to have a significant loss of signal, the loss of signal could be greater than the gain the antenna provides, thus nullifying the effect of gain. But since the antenna can be located in a better place there are other benefits.

What antenna are you using to test it?, antenna gain is also important. Some antennas require a ground plane (or metal plate) to be effective. Antennas come in Unigain, 3, 5, 9 12 dB, etc. But they are also sensitive to where they are pointing and how they are installed. Antennas of the type that have two parts and go through your car windows tend to not be very good.

There are some antennas that are "elevated" they have an extra part, this part makes it so that they don't need a ground plane or reflective plane to work well.

Yagi antennas are highly directional so you have to be pointing exactly where the base station is in order to get any gain. The length of cable you use and the connectors is also very important, you can loose more signal in long cables with poor connectors that what you gain with the antenna.

If you need more specific information feel free to send me an IM.
