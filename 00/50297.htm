arminatorx say:

Please check the follwing:

Sign into your gateway's configuration, and take a note of the WAN IP that your router is getting from your ISP. 

Then point your browser to a website like https://whatismyipaddress.com/ and compare the public IP of your gateway with the IP shown on that website. 

If the IP is different, then your ISP is causing the double NAT by using CG-NAT or DS-Lite IPv4 Tunneling through IPv6. 

If you found that your ISP is using CG-NAT or DS-Lite, call their support and demand a public IPv4 address. 

Since IPv4 addresses are basically "used up", a lot of ISPs have to hide several of their customers behind a single IPv4 address, which is fine for normal web surfing and email use. But multiplayer and server applications will have issues with this CG-NAT and will show you the double NAT error.
