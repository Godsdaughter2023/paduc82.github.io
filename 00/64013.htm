considerate_guy say:

It can't be a problem with Movie Maker if it looks fine on your computer. Instead it is a problem with the encoding/bitrate playback over an internet connection.  A dissolve transition involves a lot of change in a short perior of time of non-contiguous pixels.  Most encoding schemes rely on compressing similar neighboring pixels, but if you have a lot of differing pixels that are non-neighboring it can lead to a blurry, blocky transition instead.

 

I've noticed a similar issue on broadcast TV when there is a lot of change of a multiple pixels on the screen.  For example, when they show a lot of falling confetti or rapid strobe lights, you instead see a blocky display.

 

I think this is a side-effect of the online compression schemes, playback bitrate and the fact that a dissolve has a lot of changing pixels across the screen.
