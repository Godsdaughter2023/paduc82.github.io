considerate_guy say:

There isn't a way to do that directly. You can use the shortcut 'M' on the keyboard to make a split of the selected item (audio clip or video clip), but you can't do both together.

One workaround is to merge your video and audio together first (without splits) and save that as a new movie.

Then go back and start a new project, import the movie with the merged audio and video and make splits in that. Video and its embedded audio is treated as one item and both will split. But added audio clips (narration, music or sound effects) are treated as separate items and can be split separately.

timp32 say:
I had the same problem with my Samsung NP740.

My solution was to boot into Safe Mode and disable the Intel HD Graphics. I was then able to boot normally.

I then downloaded Samsung's Intel HD Graphics drivers for Windows 8 (through the SW Update tool) and installed them, and now Windows 8.1 works fine.

Clearly there are some big problems with Intel's latest Intel HD drivers for Windows 8.1.
