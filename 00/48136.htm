I too had the same issue with installing Windows 10 from a USB.  After two days of exchanging components, trying different sata ports, different usb sticks with the win10 installer, what finally got everything working was after I went into BIOS on the motherboard and disabled as many non-essential peripherals as I could.

When I got the installer to function correctly I only had the usb 2.0, sata controller in IDE mode, and my south bridge for pci-e (using video card hdmi port for monitor instead of onboard hdmi) enabled on my motherboard.  Every other non-essential peripheral was disabled during the install.  Once you have completely installed windows 10, ran windows update to ensure you have all the current updates (I had to run this twice to get everything) you can enable all of your peripherals on the motherboard, and they will install in win10 just fine when you turn your computer back on.

One thing to be aware of when organizing with these, is that Libraries and Folders are very different from each other, even though they look similar.

Libraries are really best described as search results.  In fact before Windows 7, Windows Vista called these "search folders".  What they are is essentially a way to combine files from several places into one combined view of search results.  For example my "Video" library includes several folders:

  • My own Video folder on my PC
  • My Movies folder on my external USB hard drive
  • My roommate's videos folder (over the network)
  • My other computer's video folder (over the network)

By having all these "included in" my Video library, I can see all the movies available to me combined into one view (a library view).  The files stay stored in their original location, the library just searches those locations and shows me the results.

The reason you'd have different kinds of libraries is to customize how they look all at once.  For example your Movies might show large thumbnail icons, sorted by release year.  Your documents library you might use small icons with details like the author name, sorted alphabetically.

So given all this, it might make sense for you to use just a Documents library, and just put the specialized kinds of documents like Bible Workshop into folders inside it, rather than as an entirely separate library.  But it really is totally up to you.
