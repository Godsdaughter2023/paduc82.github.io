darrell and cherylbarksdale say:

Bluetooth MEDIA Problems

All of my other phone I have (not a smartphone) I could listen to my media player through my bluetooth but my smartphone (Windowsphone7) will not play on my Motorola H720. How can I make it play if I need a new bluetooth or what? My son got a Head set for his phone I paired it with my phone and it would play on it bit is a HEAD SET, not a small bluetooth device.
sven johannsen say:

Bluetooth supports various profiles that are distinct. The one for making and answering phone calls is Handsfree/Headset (HF/HS), is typically monaural, supports a micrphone and answer and end button presses. The one for listening to media, music, audio books, is A2DP (advanced audio distribution profile), is generally stereo, no mic and allows pause play, skip track inputs.

Most headsets that are designed for phone use have only the handsfree/headset profile, though some support A2DP as well. BT devices that are designed to be headphones, to listen to media, have the A2DP, and some also support the phone functions, requiring a mic. In each case they are independent capabilities.

 

You may find some phones that route media to the handsfree profile, so you can hear it with a BT headset, but you won't find many smartphones that do so, as they invariably support the A2DP stereo profile. The quality of sound is much better over A2DP, as it is designed to send stereo audio in one direction only, whereas the HS/HF profile does voice in two directions.

 

Your Motorola H720 is designed for use as a phone extension, not a stereo headphone, and does not have the A2DP profile. It was just fortuitous that your old phone sent media audio over that profile. You can certainly use your H720 to make and answer calls, but you will need a different device if you wish to support media audio from a WP7, or an iPhone, or Android smartphone for that matter. If you want to do both media and phone functions, be sure the BT device does both HS/HF AND A2DP. A Samsung Modus hm6450  is one such device that is much like many headsets, but has the additional media capability.

 

One other relevent profile is AVRCP (Audio Video Remote Control Profile). Helps with some more control, but not essential.
