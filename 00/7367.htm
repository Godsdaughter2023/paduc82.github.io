How to Set Up 'My Family' on Windows Phone 8! (A - Microsoft Community

archaic light say:

How to Set Up 'My Family' on Windows Phone 8! (A Guide)

Greetings. It's that time again. I'd like to provide some steps to get your 'My Family' working on your new Windows Phone 8 if you're using a Child Account as I'm seeing that happen more and more and with the release of the new economy models of the Lumia 521 and 620.

 

My account is filled out but I keep getting 'Internal Server Error' on Windowsphone.com!? Why is this happening?!

 

Easy there. That's a sign you're doing it wrong. The only reason that 'Internal Server Error' would happen is you're attempting to set up 'My Family' on a child account. A child can't accept a contract, not by law in the USA at least, so the error appears. You, the adult, has to set up their Microsoft Account and then log in at Windowsphone.com after getting it all honky-dory so to speak. I'll outline the steps if you don't have an adult account for yourself to get your child's account working.

 

1. Head to account.live.com and select 'Don't have a Microsoft account? Sign up now' or click the conveniently placed link. Create your Account using an existing Email or get a new outlook.com or live.com email. Fill it out correctly! If you're in a rush, you could miss a detail and have two child accounts.

 

2. Head to Xbox.com and sign in with this account. This accepts the Xbox.com terms of service as well as creates a gamertag for yourself. You're thinking 'I don't use it why do I need this?!' Well, you can't accept a license agreement for a child when you haven't accepted it yourself.

 

3. Select 'My Account' in the upper right corner and then 'Billing Contact Information' in the bottom left. This needs to be filled out and set to the correct region.

 

4. Head to Windowsphone.com, sign in with your adult account, then select 'My Family'.

 

5. 'Get Started' will help you start adding your family members to your account.

 

6. Add your child's Microsoft Account there with the email/password.

 

7. Select 'Fix It' in red from the list that appears there (Should be the right side showing the child.) . Modify your child's restrictions such as Free Applications, Paid, etc. (Trust me, you will want to set this up otherwise your account will be billed.)

 

******If Fix It does not appear, on the phone select 'Settings > Backup > Applications+Lists > Back up now.' If over WiFi, this will take 30 seconds. Log out of Windowsphone.com wait 2-3 minutes, then log back in and go to 'My Family'. It'll load up the grey part and the Fix it will be there.*******

 

8. Power the phone down, then wait 1 minute and power it back up.

 

9. Go into the Store and download a free application.

 

10. Enjoy applications!

 

But Archaic Light, you dashing fellow, I am anything but a child! I clearly am of age and my phone thinks me as a mere toddler! Why do I need to set up 'My Family'?!

 

Very valid concern. The truth is you shouldn't! I get excited when I get to play with new technology all the time. Who doesn't? That just means that you put the incorrect birthdate on your account when you first activated it at the store, or the lovely sales rep didn't bother to fill out your account details.

 

Head over to account.live.com and fill in the correct information, then profile.live.com with the corresponding birthdate, and year. Once you do this, head to Xbox.com, sign in. Once you do this, you'll sign in with your account and create a 'Gamertag'. This is essential for your Microsoft Account as the devices themselves require you to have a valid Xbox Gamertag to use the Games hub, as well as Xbox Music.

 

After signing into Xbox.com, select 'My Account' in the upper right corner and then 'Billing Contact Information' in the bottom left corner as well. This needs to be updated with your correct information for your account. The reason for this is your account may reflect a marketplace on Mars or the middle of the Atlantic Ocean! Setting it to the right country ensures that you get to the right marketplace for your device.

 

Once we've done all this it's pretty easy! Turn off the phone for 1-2 minutes, then power it back on. Once you do this, you should be in the good for getting applications and using Live Services as your age should reflect correctly. If you stilll run into issues, your account's locked as a child and you need to either create a new one or head to the 'Microsoft Account, Skydrive, Outlook' section of these forums and request them to look at your account for modification/removal of child restrictions. That may work, or you're better of just creating a new account...

 

Hope this helps everyone in the future or otherwise. Feel free to link to it and be sure to select 'Me Too' if this helped you at the top!

 

Edit: Many people are stating they're using an existing account from an Xbox console that's been stuck as a child. You'd need to speak with the answers team over at the other subforum to see if there's a way they can salvage your account if you can't promote it using your adult information. greginmich say:

Windows 10 shouldn’t be susceptible to Alureon threats, because all of the later variants in this threat category were DOS/Alureon “bootkits” – which means that they modify the MBR (or the VBR of a self-created boot partition) so that it passes control of the boot to an Alureon control module rather than the Windows bootloader.

In any case, Windows Defender Offline should emphatically NOT be used to scan for Alureon variants; because we’re not sure that the prevalent boot failure issue caused by WDO’s attempted remediation for DOS/Alureon variants was ever resolved. The only safe and effective tool for  DOS/Alureon variants is TDSSKiller (TDSS is an alias for Alureon). But there are also manual methods for removing these bootkits that only take a few minutes – so there’s never really any need for reinstalling the OS.

http://support.kaspersky.com/viruses/utility#TDSSKiller
