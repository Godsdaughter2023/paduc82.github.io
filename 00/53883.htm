ovi dey say:

Hi Lois,

Thank you for posting in Microsoft Community. We are glad to have the opportunity to help.

It can be very confusing or frustrating when you delete messages from your portable device still you find that they still arrive on your computer. Problem like this occur because your devices aren't set up properly to coordinate, probably because some (or all) of them are using the POP email protocol.

If you want your devices to see the same email messages, and be able to delete on device and have those messages disappear from all the others, then all of the email needs to be configured as IMAP.

POP

The POP protocol is designed to download messages from the server. Since this is a one-way action, there are some limitations of this protocol. For example, emails that are read on the one computer are not marked as read on the server. Also, email sub-folders are not downloaded to the computer.

IMAP

Unlike POP accounts, IMAP accounts sync information. This means that if you delete an email from the computer, it is also deleted from the server. The IMAP protocol also supports pushing email to the computer so that you can receive email on your computer as it arrives on the server.
