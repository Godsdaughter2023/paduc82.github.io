john_barnett say:

Hello Asim,


I am at a loss to understand how you have got two OEM Partitions unless they have been copied.


You can't access the partitions because they do not have a drive letter. Therefore you need to give the partition a drive letter first, then you should be able to access the data on them. The only problem is, if you give the drive a drive letter and then you come to restore your PC back to factory conditions using the OEM partition the restoration may not work - well not without removing the drive letter again.


You are not going to easily change the drive letter in the Windows 8 disk management console. The best option is to download a third party partitioning application. I use the free version of AOMEI Partition Assistant Standard edition ( http://www.disk-partition.com/free-partition-manager.html ). I suggest that you install this partition application and launch it. Once AOEMI has started click on the drive you want to change the drive letter on, i.e., OEM Partition and then, in the Partition operations section on the left of the screen, click on the Change Drive Letter option. Select a vacant drive letter and then press OK. Next look at the top toolbar and click the Apply button. The drive letter will then change to the one you have given it.


Once you have a drive letter assigned you should be able to access the drive from File explorer. Personally, I would be inclined to remove any personal data that you have saved to this drive and only have the OEM image etc., stored on it. Once you have removed any personal data that you need you can always change the drive letter back to 'none' to preserve the OEM Partition.

Hi,

There are times that your USB doesn't get detected by your Surface Pro 3. If this happens, you'll need to download and install a USB driver. When you plug a device in to your SP3 for the first time, Windows automatically identifies your device and installs the proper drivers.

If you're having trouble with a device, you can fix it by updating the driver or reinstalling it. Here's how:

  1. Select the Startbutton, typeDevice Manager, and select it from the list of results.
  2. Expand one of the categories to find the name of your device, then right-click (or tap and hold) it, and select Update Driver. For graphics cards, expand the Display adapterscategory, right click your graphics card and select Update Driver.
  3. Select Search automatically for updated driver software.
  4. If these steps don't work, try reinstalling the driver: Open Device Manager, right-click (or tap and hold) the name of the device, and select Uninstall. Restart your PC and Windows will attempt to reinstall the driver.
