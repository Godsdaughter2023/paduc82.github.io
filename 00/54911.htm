WSR (Windows Speech Recognition) is dependent on having the purest audio. This means first of all you must have a good soundcard. Many soundcards built onto the motherboard of a computer are not well shielded and will pick up electronic noise from within the computer enclosure. The best way around this is to use a USB Sound pod which is a sound card into which you connect an analog microphone and the digital conversion happens in the USB Sound pod and injects the audio directly into the CPU. You also must have a good microphone that works within the frequency range of the human voice and rejects background noise.
