Slowness, freezing, booting, and performance issues as you describe are not uncommon. There are various reasons for this - i.e. disk fragmentation, disk errors, corrupt system files, too many startup programs, unnecessary services running, not enough RAM, dirty hardware components, etc. As you use your system it becomes filled with more files/programs and has a natural tendency to slow down and behave oddly so cleaning and regular maintenance is essential. tomasz sibilski say:

I bought second hand one. I had the same problem and it's probably why someone sold it. I just repaired it. The reason is that the central and two side poles are too thick. Also the middle one has back and front rails which are over the top and don't help. Solution, remove the space bar with a paper clip, a spoon or another hard but not sharp tool. Take a thin nail polishing bar and scratch about 10% of plastic from around all poles of the space bar (underneath). Sand the rails down as well. Keep all in nice, round shape. Bare in mind, that at the top of the middle one on the sides there are small hatches. DO NOT modify them. Last touch is to put some Vaseline or a similar lubricant on the poles and on metal bar sliders. Put the space bar back sliding the metal bar into it's rails first. If it's still stiff, repeat the process. Time: 5-10 min. Tools: nail polishing bar, lubricant. Good luck!
