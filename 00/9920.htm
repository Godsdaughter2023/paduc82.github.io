    Your question was about "the total page count for 2 (or more) sections"

    A { NUMPAGES } field will give you the total number of pages in the document.

    jeeped say:
    Remove the manual line highlighting and replace it with conditional formatting.

    The CF rule would be either,

    =AND(ROW()>1,MOD(ROW(),2))
    ... or,
    =AND(ROW()>1,NOT(MOD(ROW(),2)))

    The first highlights (aka Fills) odd rows starting at row 2, the latter even rows. You could also add a condition of NOT(BLANK(...)) so you could define a larger Applies to: range that will accommodate new data being entered.

    As Phillip wrote, Word cannot open a PDF file. In order to open the PDF, itself, you need to use a PDF reader program such as Apple's Preview.app or Adobe Reader.
