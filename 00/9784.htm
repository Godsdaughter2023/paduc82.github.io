I FOUND A WORK-AROUND :)

  1. Ensure no Microsoft, Outlook or Office accounts are stored on your device.
  2. Reset/reinstall Word (or any Office app), this will force it to do a first time set-up.
  3. Log in to Word using your Microsoft account. This will then save your password as an "Office" account on your device. (The web-based sign-in screen won't crash as if it asks for an email first.)
  4. Open Outlook and go to the Add Account page.
  5. Outlook will search your device for accounts. Select the account you want to add and tap "Add Account"

To add multiple accounts to Outlook simply follow the steps above then sign out of Word and follow steps 3-5 again using your other account's details.

The above method also works for the OneDrive app. (OneDrive only uses the web-based page for the password so it'll still crash if you don't use the above method.)

Once you've added all your accounts remember to go back to Word and sign back in to your preferred Office/OneDrive account. (You'll definitely have to do this if you have a paid Office subscription.)

To fix To-Do simply follow the above steps, reset/reinstall To-Do, then open it and select your account. It will automatically find your password and sign in.

How to remove strikethroughs - Microsoft Community

dcs say:

How to remove strikethroughs

How do you remove strikethroughs in ms word?
suzanne s. barnhill say:
If the strikethrough was created as font formatting and by "accept the change" you mean that you want to delete the struckthrough text, then you can just delete it. If you want to do this globally, then you can use the Find dialog to find that formatting.
 
In Word 2003, click More in the Find dialog to expand the dialog. Leave the "Find what" box empty and check the box for "Highlight all items found." Click the Format button, then Font, and check the box for "Strikethrough," then click OK. Under the "Find what" box you'll see "Format: Strikethrough, Not Double strikethrough." Click Find All. (The equivalent in Word 2007 is the "Find in" button.)
 
This will select all the struckthrough text. Click on the title bar of the document to return focus to the document without unselecting the text and press Delete to delete it.
 
Alternatively, you can use the Replace dialog to find the struckthrough text as described and replace it with nothing (leave the "Replace with dialog" blank).
