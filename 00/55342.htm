mwewe say:

It's possible you have a frequency mismatch problem. My Logitech C310 showed similar symptoms here. If I reconfigured or disabled/re-enabled the mike it would work for awhile but eventually Cortana went deaf.

So I experimented and made a recording during a Cortana-deaf episode and to my surprise I sounded like Alvin the Chipmunk.

I decided that the chipmunk sound happens because the applications think the mic is recording at 48 kHz, but in reality the mic is operating at 16 kHz, so your voice is played at triple speed. It looks like Windows 10 is maintaining the 48 kHz setting correctly, but the microphone re-initializes at 16 kHz.

If I set the mic properties (advanced tab in mic properties in recording devices) to 1 channel, 16 kHz, then Cortana works through every sleep cycle with no problem, as do all my other apps. Cortana hasn't faltered once since I made the change.

I'm sure the microphone won't really hear 48 kHz, and probably won't even hear 16 kHz. Most people can't hear 16 kHz sounds unless they're in their teens or early 20's. So there's really no cost to leaving it in the 16 kHz mode... if that works for you.
