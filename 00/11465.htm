[Edit]

 

try and this..

both lists (filtered) have the same number of visible Cells

now, copy / paste from list1 on sheet1 (column A)

into list 2 on sheet2 (column A)

(in row 1 are headings)

 

(make a copy before you run this code)

 

 

Sub BothColumnsFiltered()
Dim c As New Collection
Dim r As Range
Const colSh1 = "A" '<<< data on sheet1 in col A, change
Const colSh2 = "A" '<<< data on sheet2 in col A, change
Dim ws1 As Worksheet, ws2 As Worksheet
Dim r1 As Long, r2 As Long, t As Long

Set ws1 = Sheets("Sheet1")   '<<< change name
Set ws2 = Sheets("Sheet2")   '<<< change name
r1 = ws1.Cells(Rows.Count, colSh1).End(xlUp).Row
r2 = ws2.Cells(Rows.Count, colSh2).End(xlUp).Row
For Each r In ws1.Range(colSh1 & "2:" & colSh1 & r1).SpecialCells(xlCellTypeVisible)
c.Add r
Next
t = 1
For Each r In ws2.Range(colSh2 & "2:" & colSh2 & r2).SpecialCells(xlCellTypeVisible)
r = c(t)
t = t + 1
Next
End Sub

jay freedman say:
Given the algorithms buried in Word's layout engine, I think you're unlikely to get exactly what you asked for except by accident or by extensive manual intervention.

The layout of each line starts with the character spacing specified by the font file. If you leave the text left justified and ragged right, that's what you'll see throughout.

If you change the alignment to Justified, Word has to alter the spacing of most of the lines of each paragraph, compressing them or expanding them to meet the margins/indents on both sides. The last line of the paragraph isn't subject to that adjustment; it retains the font-specified spacing. Unless the line or two above the last on happen to fit without noticeable adjustments, they will be spaced differently.

If you want to tweak a particularly bad match, you can select the last line, open the Font dialog's Advanced tab, and change the Spacing setting. It will compress or expand the text in steps of 0.05 point. Of course, if you do this and later edit any part of the paragraph, you may have to readjust the spacing.

rohn007 say:
"justified" / "justification" can have a couple of meanings in this context. I think there may be some confusion.

In general, justification refers to how the words are aligned relative to the left and right margins of the page. Left justified, means the words all line up "straight" on the left margin, words at the right margin are "ragged", nothing special is done to control where the line wraps to the next line.

Right justified means the opposite. Right margin straight, left margin ragged.

Where it gets tricky is the use of "justified" or "full justification" or even "Distributed".  They all share one attribute, they both try to extend text all the way so that the left and right margin are "straight". Exactly how that is done varies.  Distributed stretches every thing out to fill the line.  Justified/full justification depends on a "Hyphenation margin". As soon as the sentence reaches the hyphenation margin the line is stretched out and words are wrapped to the next line or split using a hyphen.

Exactly how do you want to do your justification?
