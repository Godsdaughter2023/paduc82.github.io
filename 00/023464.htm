jay freedman say:

In your template, insert the following code in the ThisDocument module:

Private Sub Document_ContentControlOnExit(ByVal ContentControl As ContentControl, Cancel As Boolean)
    ContentControl.Range.Shading.BackgroundPatternColor = wdColorAutomatic
End Sub

Private Sub Document_New()
    Dim cc As ContentControl
    For Each cc In ActiveDocument.ContentControls
        cc.Range.Shading.BackgroundPatternColor = wdColorYellow
    Next cc
End Sub

When a new document is based on the template, the Document_New macro applies yellow shading to all the content controls. (I would have preferred to use highlighting, but VBA complains if you try to apply a highlight to certain kinds of content controls.)

When the cursor exits a content control, the OnExit macro removes the shading.

Where "Full Time" appears in one column and the two numbers appear in another column. "Part Time" is on another row and the 40 (77%) appear in the next column. I really don't want to have to put the 23% in another column.

To make things even more complicated, the numbers actually originate on a different worksheet. So, I would need to have the 35 pulled from a certain cell in a different worksheet and the 23% pulled from a different cell on a different worksheet.

So, how do I pull data from two cells from another worksheet into the same cell in the format listed above....i.e., with the parenthesis, etc...

Thanks so much for your help!!
jllatham say:

Assume that "formula1" is the formula that is giving you the 35 value, and that formula2 is the formula that's giving you the 23% value

You could then set things up in the cell like this:

=formula1 & " " & Text(formula2,"(0%)")

In the real world it might look more like

=SUM(B2:B10) & " " & TEXT(SUM(B2:B10)/G5,"(0%)")

hope that helps.
