john - project say:
Megan141,
It doesn't sound like you are really scheduling anything if you are only making a list to compile "time spent in meetings each week". Maybe Excel would be a better application for that.

A group resource and the Group field are two different concepts.

With a group resource you do not have individual names, all you have is a generic description for the whole group. So if you have 50 people in the accounting group, for example, the max units would be 5000%. If you then assigned the accounting group to a particular meeting at 500%, that would mean 5 people from the accounting group are assigned to attend the meeting, but you will not know which 5 of the 50 people they are.

The Group field on the Resource Sheet is used for grouping, sorting, filtering or editing resources by their group, it is not used for any assignment function, it is simply a label.

John

john - project say:
Megan141,
As I said, it doesn' t sound like this exercise is really a scheduling exercise. It sounds like a good exercise for a simple Excel spreadsheet. I'd lay out the days or weeks across the top (columns)and the resources down the side (rows). Then in simple form put the number of hours in any cell where a particular resource is attending a particular meeting. Given the length of each meeting, you can easily sum up the hours-in-meetings for each resource and cut the data lots of different ways, more than you could do with Project.

With regard to your last question, I thought I already answered that but let me try again. For a group resource, you do not "assign" resources by name to the group, you simple call the group by its group name. For example, let's say the Sally, June, Marge, and Connie are in the business group. On the Resource Sheet you would create a "Business personnel" resource with a max units of 400%, assuming all 5 people in the group could work full time at the same time. If you want to identify who is in the Business personnel group, you could use an extra text or the Notes field for that resource and list them by name. But, let's say that for a particular meeting there are only 4 of the people in the meeting (i.e. assignment level for the Business personnel resource is 400%), there will be no way to determine which of the 4 people are in the meeting, unless you specifically identified them by name using the task Notes field. So yes, I suppose you could make some type of stab at a time-in-meetings report, but it would require some VBA and in my opinion be a lot more work than the Excel approach.

John
