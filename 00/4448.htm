minhaj jamaldeen say:

Internet Explorer doesn't load any web pages or doesn't respond

Hi All,

For most of you Windows 7 users, and for those who have set their Windows Updates to install updates automatically, your Internet Explorer would have received an update for the latest version, i.e Internet Explorer 11. As per my investigations through many scenarios, I've gone through many customers come rushing with problems that they cannot access any web pages on their Internet Explorer, or that the browser doesn't load fine or freezes.
The main reason is that Internet Explorer 11 is not compatible with Windows 7 and so these problems occur.

The method to resolve this is by downgrading Internet Explorer 11 to a lower version.

Please follow these instructions:

1) Click on Start
2) Click on Control Panel
3) Click on Program and Features
4) On the left pane, click on Turn Windows Features on or Off
5) Untick Internet Explorer 11
6) Click OK
7) Then click on View Installed Updates
8) In the list of updates, look for Windows Internet Explorer 11, click on it and select Uninstall on the top.
9) Restart the computer, and most of the time the previously installed version will be automatically gets enabled and you can find it under the Start Menu.

bernie deitrick say:

Let's say that your tower names are in column A of your "Master Sheet", and that on each of your 18 sheets the appropriate tower name is in cell A1.

So, to get column K's values, array-enter (enter using Ctrl-Shift-Enter)

=IFERROR(INDEX('Master Sheet'!$A:$X,SMALL(IF('Master Sheet'!$A$1:$A$1000=$A$1,ROW($A$1:$A$1000)),ROW(A1)),COLUMN(K1)),"")

For Column L's data, array enter:

=IFERROR(INDEX('Master Sheet'!$A:$X,SMALL(IF('Master Sheet'!$A$1:$A$1000=$A$1,ROW($A$1:$A$1000)),ROW(A1)),COLUMN(L1)),"")

etc., then copy those formulas down until they return blanks.

If your tower names are in a column other than A, only change the 'Master Sheet'!$A$1:$A$1000 - and increase the 1000 to a number higher than your amount of data.
