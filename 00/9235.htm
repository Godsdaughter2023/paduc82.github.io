How To Find Values Greater Than Selected Value - Microsoft Community

mcswain410 say:

How To Find Values Greater Than Selected Value

I have a spreasheet with Names in Col A and number values in Col B-F with various statistics.  I want to find all vaules greater then a desired number in each Col B-F and then provide me with the names from Col A that match this criteria.  Is this possible?

Thanks!

bernie deitrick say: Use data filters on your table, and from the drop-down at the top of the column choose the custom option, with "Greater than or equal to" using the value you want as your cut-off. You can combine the criteria in any way you want, and the visble cells in column A will have the corresponding names.

mcswain410 say: That certainly helps!  How do I combine the filters between Col B-F?  Each statistic represents a different value and I am trying to get the names that meet the minimum requirement for each column. 

bernie deitrick say:

If you use the filters one at a time, that is like OR logic - if you apply them all at once, that is like AND logic.

 

So, if you want OR type of logic, apply the filter to B, copy the names that meet the requirement and paste elsewhere, then clear the filter on B and apply the filter to C, etc.

 

If you want all the names that meet the min for any column, you could use a column of formulas like this, for example:

 

=OR(B2>=3,C2>=4,D2>0.5,E2>=2.5,F2>1)

 

and copy down. That will return True if any of the values in any of the columns meets the minimum, and you can filter your entire table based on that column of formulas showing True.

a. user say:

Hi david,

 

Yes, you can totally block or reject an email in Hotmail by following one of the options below:

 

A. Block a sender or a domain name

1. Sign in to your account. Go to inbox.
2. Click Options located at the upper-right side of the page.
3. Select More Options.
4. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.
5. Click Blocked senders.
6. Enter the email address or domain on the box labeled Blocked email address or domain: and click Add to list >>.

B. Set the filtering level of the messages

Please follow these steps to setup your junk e-mail filter in Hotmail:

 

1. Sign in to the Hotmail website with your Microsoft account.
2. In the upper-right corner of the page, click Options, and then click More options.
3. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.
4. Under Filters and reporting, select the options that you want, and then click Save.

 

Notes:

 

> If you choose Exclusive for your junk e-mail filter, only messages from e-mail addresses on your Contacts or Allowed senders lists appear in your Inbox. Messages from senders not on those lists are automatically sent to the Junk e-mail folder.

 

> You should occasionally check your junk e-mail folder to make sure that good messages are not sent there by mistake.

 

C. Sweep function - This will allow all received or even future emails coming from the specific sender be deleted. Here are the steps to activate the Sweep option:

1. Sign in to your Hotmail account. Go to Inbox.
2. Check the specific sender you would like to filter.
3. On the upper part, click Sweep
4. Choose Delete all from
5. On the pop-up box, just click Delete all.

Note: You can also block future messages from that sender by checking the box beside Also block future messages.

D. Rules - we can create a rule that can block a specific word from the subject of the spam email.

1. Sign in to your Hotmail account with your Microsoft account.
2. Click on Options then More options... in the upper right corner of the page.
3. Under the Customizing Hotmail section, click on Rules for sorting new messages.
4. Click on the New button.
5. Under Step 1: Which messages do you want this rule to apply to, 
    a. First drop-down menu - choose Subject
    b. Second drop-down menu - choose contains
    c. Third field - input the word you would like to block
6. Under Step 2: What action do you want to apply, click on Delete these messages
7. Click Save button.

Hoping that the solutions above will help you protect your account from any spam/unwanted messages you're receiving.
