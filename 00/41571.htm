Hi Douglas,

May I know are you referring to the Photo Tile in Windows 8?

 

Method 1: How to enable or disable Photo Tile Slides.

 

1.       Click on the Photo Tile.

 

2.       Click on Windows Key + C Key on the Keyboard.

 

3.       You will see a Charms Bar on your Computer screen.

 

4.       Click on the Settings Options.

  

5.       You will see Settings window.

 

6.       Under Settings Window you will see Settings, click on it.

 

7.       Now you will see Photos Setting.

 

8.       Under Photos Setting Window you will see an option that says Shuffle Photos on the app Tile.

 

9.       Make sure that the option is Shuffle Photos on the app Tile is Turned On.

 

 Method 2: Use your own photos as a screen saver.

 

You can create a personalized screen saver by selecting some photos to use in a slide show.

 

1.       Open Screen Saver Settings by swiping in from the right edge of the screen, tapping Search (or if you're using a mouse, pointing to the upper-right corner of the screen, moving the mouse pointer down, and then clicking Search), entering Screen saver in the search box, tapping or clicking Settings, and then tapping or clicking Change screen saver.

 

2.       Under Screen saver, tap or click Photos.

 

3.       To change one or more of the screen saver settings, tap or click Settings, and then do one or more of the following:

 

• To specify the photos to use for your screen saver, tap or click Browse, choose the folder that contains the photos you want to use, and then tap or click OK.

 

• To change the speed of the slide show, tap or click the list next to Slide show speed.

 

• If you want the photos to appear in a random order, select the Shuffle pictures check box.

 

4.       Tap or click Save to save the setting changes you made. Then tap or click OK in the Screen Saver Settings dialog box.

vijay a. verma say:
Hi ,DebCorley
I am Vijay, an Independent Advisor. I am here to work with you on this problem.

1. Windows Key+I to invoke Settings > Network & Internet > Wi-Fi (or any other choice as per your requirement) > Manage known networks > Click the required network > Properties > Here you can make it Private / Public (Catch is that Private / Public option will appear only if you are connected to it)

2. Windows Key+R > Type regedit and Enter > Navigate to following key - HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows NT\CurrentVersion\NetworkList\Profiles > Expand the Profiles folder
This contains a list of Sub keys. Click each sub-key > On right side, click ProfileName > When you find the sub-key with the correct ProfileName value > At the top you will see 'Category' - double click that > Change its value from 0 to 1 and OK > Reboot
Credit - DaveM121

Note - It is recommended that you create a backup of your registry before modifying so that if you commit some mistake, you will be able to restore - https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/help/322756...

3. If above doesn't work -
Windows Key+X > Windows Powershell (Admin)
Copy paste below line and Enter
Get-NetConnectionProfile
Pick up the InterfaceIndex number from here
Copy and paste following line and Enter
Set-NetConnectionProfile -InterfaceIndex <InterfaceIndex number> -NetworkCategory Private

Example - Set-NetConnectionProfile -InterfaceIndex 17 -NetworkCategory Private

Source - https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/for...

Do let me know if you require any further help on this. Will be glad to help you.

Hi Deb

My name is Andre Da Costa; an Independent Consultant, Windows Insider MVP and Windows & Devices for IT MVP. I'm here to help you with your problem.

If you recently visited a coffee shop or cyber cafe or connected to a wi-fi network in a park, Windows 10 might have assumed you are on a Public network.
