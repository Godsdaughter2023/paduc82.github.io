golfgazoo say:

Obtaining copyright for spreadsheet

If I develop an Excel spreadsheet using VBA programming, am I allowed to copyright the spreadsheet? Sell the spreadsheet?
chip pearson say: Yes, you can copyright your work and sell or distribute it as you see fit without permission and/or royalties to Microsoft. You can't sell Excel itself, but you can sell any workbook or add-in created with Excel. Copyright protection begins from the moment the work is put into tangible form, which for a workbook (or any computer software or document) would be simply saving the file to disc. You don't need to register the workbook for copyright protection, but it is wise to do so as it gives you more rights of recovery for plagarism than an unregistered work.

Install the zune software onto your laptop, then connect your zune.  You will be a guest on this pc, unless you link (which is fine...)  When the software recognizes your zune is connected, click device near the top left, then go to music.  Select a bunch, or all, and right click->copy to collection.  This is called reverse sync.

You can restore at least some purchased videos as well.

You can also reverse sync your photos...but only the reduced resolution version that is on your zune, unfortunately.

I'm not sure you can reverse sync audiobooks... but give it a try.

And, next time: back up!  Good luck.
