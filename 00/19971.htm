paulc_1 say:

               

Deleting Photos from your "SkyDrive Camera Roll" folder on SkyDrive:

Method 1

From your phone, download the SkyDrive app from the Market Place (Micrsoft Store). Start the app and sign in to your SkyDrive account, navigate to your SkyDrive Camera Roll folder, and tap the "select" charm at the bottom of the screen. This will display check boxes beside all the thumbnails of your uploaded photos. Check each photo you want to delete, then tap the Trashcan charm at the bottom of the screen.

Method 2
From your PC, login to your Skydrive account via your web browser. Navigate to your Skydrive Camera Roll folder. Select all the photos you want to delete by placing a check mark beside each one. Then right click on a picture, and select "Delete".      

 

Deleting Photos from your Phone's "Camera Roll" folder:

With a Lumia 800, an "auto upload" to SkyDrive will reduce the resolution of the uploaded photos considerably. This may be OK if you just want to view them online. But if you later want to print them, you will probably prefer to keep the higher resolution original photo. Before you delete any original photo from your phone, back them up in full resolution to your PC. First open the Zune software on your PC, and under "Settings--Phone--Pictures & Videos", under "Image Quality" choose "Original". Check the "Import Settings" option "Delete pictures and videos from my phone after copying them to my computer". Next, plug in your phone to your PC and sync the photos from the phone to your PC via the Zune software.

Once you have synced all the ones you want to keep, the Zune software will delete them automatically from your phone. However, if you don't want to keep the original, then from your phone, you can tap the Pictures Hub, and then "Camera Roll", and then long press on each thumbnail of the photos to bring up the context menu with a "delete" option. Much quicker to delete via the Zune software IMO.

I am not totally sure, but if these are the 3 options you are getting when you long press a photo, then it sounds like you are NOT viewing photos from the Camera Roll in the Pictures Hub albums on the phone.

It sounds like you are viewing photos from the People Hub that are being pulled in from your SkyDrive "Mobile Uploads" folder on SkyDrive or from a Facebook or Twitter photo album.

 

The SkyDrive "Mobile Uploads" folder are for photos that you uploaded manually from your phone while viewing them. If you long press on any of these photos in full screen view from the Pictures Hub, then you will get the 3 options you mentioned. The "SkyDrive Camera Roll" folder on SkyDrive contains photos that were automatically uploaded when you first took the photo. These are two different folders on your SkyDrive. If the "SkyDrive Camera Roll" folder is empty, check the SkyDrive "Mobile Uploads" folder using either Method 1 or Method 2 as I described above.

If you are seeing any photos that you uploaded to Facebook, or photos of you are tagged on Facebook, they will show up under the"Pictures Hub" in the "People" folder albums. If you long press on any of these photos, then you will also get the 3 options you mentioned. But you will not be able to delete them from here. You must log on to Facebook and delete them from there if they are your photos. If they are photos of you that belong to someone else that you are tagged in, then you will need to untag them so that they do not show up on your phone.

If you have deleted the Camera Roll pics on the phone, then they are definately no longer on your phone. steve rindsberg say:

When you install Office 2007 as an upgrade, it can make this type of mess.  And does. 

As a general rule, I'd remove the existing version of Office, then install the new version, but that's advice for the future.

It looks like you've had one version of PPT or another on that PC for quite some time.  Some of these bits go back to PPT97.

Add-ins can load from several places (always in the registry) and depending on where they're loaded from, they may or may not appear on PowerPoint's add-ins lists.

If you're ready for a bit of registry-diving, here's what you want to do:

First, start PPT and list all the add-ins it's unhappy about (well, scroll up and grab the list you've already posted, and thanks for going to the trouble of doing all that; it was very helpful!)

Then go into PowerPoint Options | Addins and get a list of all the addins, COM and PPA/PowerPoint, that ARE currently loaded.  You don't want to delete them by accident.  Then start REGEDIT and go to both:

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Office\X.X\PowerPoint\AddIns

HKEY_CURRENT_USER\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Office\X.X\PowerPoint\AddIns

(for X.X above, substitute each of 8.0, 9.0, 10.0, 11,0, 12.0 14.0  (there's no 13.0)

Under each of these, you may find further folders, one for each add-in that PPT's complaining about.  Each of the PPA add-ins will have a Default, Path and AutoLoad values.  Verify that the path points to the PPA that PowerPoint's barking about then delete the entire folder, three values and all.

You may also find an entry pointing to an add-in called MASTTBAR.PPA  Probably not, but if you do, delete it also.  I'm its father.  Ego te absolvo. <g>

Again, do NOT delete the folders that point to add-ins that PPT's already been able to load; you only want to whack the ones it's complaining about.

Also check HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Office\PowerPoint\Addins

OK.  Deeeeep breath.  Dive!

Let us know how it worked out when you get back to the surface.
