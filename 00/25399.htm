theinfy say:

Database structure question - pseudo BOM

I would like to organize:

  • Many (hundreds) of raw materials
  • About a hundred completed products consisting of up to about 20 raw materials.

I'm struggling with this 'many to many' relationship where multiple materials are in each construction and multiple constructions call out a specific material. I think I need a connecting 'middle man' table, but I'm not sure what this will look like. Hopefully I will add more features to this basic structure, I am just trying to untangle a giant excel mess for now. I can post more details if necessary. 

I appreciate any tips or resources! Thanks!

marshall barton say:

Hopfully you don't have a hierarchy BOM where you have to deal with components of components.

A standard many to many relationship indeed uses a "middle" table, often called a junction table.  Your junction table (let's call it the ProductIngredients table) must contain two foreign key fields, one to link to the products table and the other to link to the materials table.  You can use these two fields as a compound primary key or a compound unique index with another field as the auto number primary key.  The junction table may contain other fields related to the combination of a product and its ingredients (e.g. the amount of an ingredient).

The common user interface for this structure is a form based on the products table and a continuous (or datasheet) subform.  The subform needs a hidden text box for the product foreign key field, a combo box to select a material and other text boxes for any other fields in the junction table.  The subform control's LinkMaster/Child properties link the subform to the material in the products form's current record and also automatically fills in the hidden foreign key text box when you create a new record in the subform.

malcolmpaton say:

My phone will not connect to my computer. This has been an ongoing problem.The computer is recognising tjhe phone but all I get is phone cannot connect.I won't get another one of these

All I get is failure to connect, but the computer is recognising the phone.Zune comes up when I connect the phone.

a. user say:

malcolmpaton -

 

Thank you for your post!

 

Just to verify the issue you are having, is this the error that comes up?

 

CONNECTION ERROR
Can't connect to your phone. Disconnect it, restart it, then try connecting again.

 

If this matches what you are seeing, this means that your Windows Profile on your PC is having problems registering the certificate for your Windows Phone.  This support article details the steps to fix this:

 

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/2468307/en-us

 

You will want to make sure that you are using a USB data cable, and not a charging cable.  If possible, try a different USB cable.  Also, check the USB port to make sure there's no blockage and try using a different USB port.

 

If that doesn't fix it, follow these steps as detailed in that support article:

 

1.  Close the Zune software and disconnect your phone from your computer. 
2.  Open Certificate Manager, if it is not already open. To do this, click Start, type certmgr.msc in the Start search bar, and then press your Enter key.
If you use Windows 8, then on the Start screen, type Certificate Manager, tap or click Settings in the right column, and then tap or click Manage computer certificates. 
3.  In the window, tap or click the Personal folder in the left column, and then double tap or double click the Certificates folder in the right column. 
4.  Click the heading that says Issued By to sort the column, and then look for one or more certificates that read "zune-tuner://windowsphone/...".
5.  Right-click each certificate file, click Delete, and then click Yes.
6.  Tap or click File in the menu, then Exit. 
7.  Click Start and in the search box enter %userprofile%\Application Data\Microsoft\Crypto\RSA and then press Enter.
Windows 8: Right-click in the lower left-hand corner of the screen and tap or click Run , or type Windows Key + R , and then type the string above. 
8.  Find the folder that begins in "S-1-5-21-". 
9.  Right-click the folder and then click Rename . Rename the folder so that it ends in .bak. This means you will likely be adding ".bak" to the end of the number. 
10.  As soon as you're done, open your Zune software and connect your phone to your computer. This creates a new certificate and folder. 

 

If you still have issues after that, you may need to reinstall the Zune software.  Another thing we usually try with this error is to use a different Windows profile.

 

Let us know if you continue to experience issues with this!

Kevin J S
