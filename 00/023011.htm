Hello Tristan Bentley,

 

Thanks for your post in Microsoft Community. Regarding your description about “Having trouble with O365 Outlook Online Autocomplete and Contact features”,  The AutoComplete list for Outlook is specific to Outlook and is not shared by Outlook Web App (OWA). OWA maintains its own AutoComplete list.

For OWA, if you want to clear the cache for specific user, you may follow the below steps and check the result.

1. Log in to Office 365 Outlook web app.

2. Click on "New" (email).

3. In the "To:" field, type a fictitious email address and send the email.

4. Click on "New:" (email).

5. In the "To:" field, type the first few characters of the email address you typed in Step 3.

6. Click the "X" which appears at the end of the auto-complete entry.

7. Type a few more characters of the email address and note that the auto-complete entry persists. If the entry is gone, check back in 24 hours to see if it has returned.

davidf2 say:

TreeKid,

Publisher forms require FrontPage Server Extensions on your web host and that you use HTTP uploading protocol. The FPSE are the form handlers and in your case I doubt that your ISP is providing them for a personal space, but you can test by going to: http://www.yourdomainname.com/_vti_inf.html  and seeing if you get aconfirmation page. I also suspect that you did not use HTTP uploading and instead used FTP which won't work with FPSE.

If you want to use the Publisher forms and your ISP does not provide FPSE, then it is possible to change the properties of the submit button to work with other forms control programs or form handlers if your ISP provides them. Reference: http://office.microsoft.com:80/en-us/publisher-help/CH006357689.aspx andhttp://msmvps.com/blogs/dbartosik/archive/2006/01/07/80564.aspx
