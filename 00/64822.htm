Here is solution that worked for me on several Win10 PCs ...

In Win10, get to the Network Connections ...

  • Right click start icon on lower left
  • Click "Settings"
  • Click "Network & Internet"
  • Click "Ethernet"
  • Click "Change adapter settings"

You will see your network adapters now.  For each adapter that you would use to access your NAS do the following.  There are likely two, the Wi-Fi adapter and maybe you have wired Ethernet adapter ...

  • Right click the adapter
  • Select "Properties"
  • Ensure that "Internet Protocol Version 4 (TCP/IPv4)" is checked.  Right click it and select properties
  • Click "Advanced", then click the WINS tab
  • In most cases, the button labeled "Default" may be selected.  Instead, select the option, "Enable NetBIOS over TCP/IP"
  • Click "OK" and close all open windows.

This should fix the problem.

TASKHOST is a generic process which acts as a host for processes that run from DLLs rather than EXEs. At startup TASKHOST checks the Services portion of the Registry to construct a list of DLL-based services that it needs to load, and then loads them. There can be many instances of TASKHOST running, as there will be one instance of TASKHOST for every DLL-based service or grouping of services (the grouping of services is determined by the programmers who wrote the services in question).
