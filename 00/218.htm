macropod say:

Assuming the percentage data are expressed as decimals, you could use a field coded as:

{IF{=ABS({MERGEFIELD Performance}-0.5)}<= 0.1 "Text for 40-60%" "Text for <40% or >60%"}

or:

{IF{=ABS(«Performance»-0.5)}<= 0.1 "Text for 40-60%" "Text for <40% or >60%"}

If the percentage data are expressed as numbers (with or without trailing decimals), you could use a field coded as:

{IF{=ABS({MERGEFIELD Performance}-50)}<= 10 "Text for 40-60%" "Text for <40% or >60%"}

or:

{IF{=ABS(«Performance»-50)}<= 10 "Text for 40-60%" "Text for <40% or >60%"}

In each case, the above field code representations assume your data field is named 'Performance'.

Note: The field brace pairs (i.e. '{ }') for the above example are all created in the document itself, via Ctrl-F9 (Cmd-F9 on a Mac); you can't simply type them or copy & paste them from this message. Nor is it practical to add them via any of the standard Word dialogues. Likewise, the chevrons (i.e. '« »') are part of the actual mergefields - which you can insert from the 'Insert Merge Field' dropdown (i.e. you can't type or copy & paste them from this message, either). The spaces represented in the field constructions are all required.
