a. user say:

Anytime your computer is shutting down all by itself, it is usually hardware related.  The most common cause of computers powering down unexpectedly is they are overheating.  The manufacturer built protection into the system so that if they get too hot they will shut down to protect themselves.  If your computer is a laptop, make sure it is getting good ventilation and that the bottom is not sitting on a soft surface where air can not flow into the vents on the bottom and sides.  The small feet on the bottom raise it up just slightly off a hard surface so air can flow underneath.  If you put it on a soft surface like a bed or even your lap, that can hinder the airflow.  If it is a desktop computer, you may need to shut the power off and open the case up.  Get a can of "canned air", also know as computer duster at a computer or electronics store, and blow the dust out of your computer case.  If you have never done this, there could be significant dust inside so you might want to take it outside to do this.  Pay particular attention to the fans as they are vital to your computer's cooling system and must stay clean and clear.

It could also be bad memory, a bad power supply, or a bad motherboard.  Those are all things that you would need a qualified, trusted computer technician to diagnose and repair.  I would suggest contacting the manufacturer is the computer is under warranty.

Other than that, here are some tests you can run on the operating system to rule out any software problems.

To run the System File Checker tool, follow these steps:
Click Start, and then type cmd in the Start Search box.
Right-click cmd in the Programs list, and then click Run as administrator.
If you are prompted for an administrator password or confirmation, type your password or click Continue
At the command prompt, type the following line, and then press ENTER:
sfc /scannow
When the scan is complete, test to see whether the issue that you are experiencing is resolved.

How to troubleshoot a problem by performing a clean boot in Windows Vista
http://support.microsoft.com/kb/929135

Memory test:
Click Start, type "memory" in the search box.
Open the memory diagnostics,  then choose your Options.
