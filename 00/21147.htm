sweeneysm say:

Kevin -

I just experienced the same thing, and found a hack to get them back (I accidentally deleted all Contacts, Calendar, and Tasks). 

1.  On the bottom of the left hand navigation where you switch between Mail, Calendar, Contacts, Tasks....  there is an Elipses ("...").  Clicking on this reveals a "Folders" Nav item.  Click on "folders". 

2.  Now you should be able to see "Trash" in a folder view.  (It was confusing for me at first, because this looked exactly like Calendar view in the main real estate of the screen, but keep your focus on that left hand panel - there's a tree-folder view here).  Expand the "Trash".

3. Here, I would have expected to be able to drag my Contacts folder out of the trash and into my top level.  Not so.. BUT, I can right click on my Contacts folder, and in that fly out menu, I have the option to "Copy" that... click on "copy folder"..

4.  It will give you the option to select where you want to copy.. Select the top level (for me it was *** Email address is removed for privacy ***). 

5. You can repeat this for each folder you accidentally deleted.

6. Confirm that you can go back to "Contacts" as you would normally navigate to, and see your contacts there.

7. You can now safely "empty trash" and remove the old folders. 

8. Good as new.

Styles and Direct Formatting

How can a paragraph use an applied style but also be direct formatted?
bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

Very simply :-) First apply a style, then make changes to the formatting of the paragraph and/or the text. Also, content in a styled paragraph can have a Character Style applied as well. It should be noted, though, that direct formatting is best avoided & always should be done sparingly... some say not at all.

Whether any of this has been done can be determined in either of several ways;

  • Display the Styles Pane. When you click in the para, Current style at the top of the pane will display the name of the style followed by a ✚ and the attributes that deviate from what the style specifies
  • View> Reveal Formatting. When you click text a balloon displays the formatting that has been applied based on Paragraph Styles & Character Styles as well as any direct character & paragraph formatting.
  • The check boxes at the bottom of the Styles Pane display guides [markup] that indicate where Styles or Direct formatting have been used.

Outlook Rules -- identifying blank subject lines - Microsoft Community

mikelee101 say:

Outlook Rules -- identifying blank subject lines

I'm looking for a way to create a rule in Oultook 07 that will identify messages from a specific sender with a blank subject line.  Unfortunately, I get hung up in the "subject contains specific words" part, because I can't seem to specify blank.

I tried doing a rule for all messages from that sender with an exception for subjects containing * (wildcard character) in the subject, but it doesn't seem to treat the asterisk as a wildcard, it just looks for messages with asterisks in the subject line.

If anyone knows of any way to accomplish this, I'd appreciate it.

Thanks for the help.

 

office07, xppro sp3

 

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

Rules to not support wildcards.

You were on the right track.  Use the exception.  Your rule will look like this:

Apply this rule after the message arrives
from <your person>
assign it to the NONBLANK category
except if the subject contains 'a' or 'e' or 'i' or 'o' or 'u' or 'y'
stop processing more rules

Of coure, the action you chose won't necessarily be the "assign it to the category".  It will be the one you want.

Of course, if the person sends you a message with a subject composed ot only digits or punctuation, then this rule won't work, but it's pretty rare for somone to send a message with no English words in it, for English speakers.  If it has words in the subject, it will contain vowels and this rule won't fire in that case.  If it's blank (or all digits/punctuation), this rule will fire.
