john mcghie say:
Hi Ron:

No, it's the "other" report we need, the crash dump that gives the blame module and the cause.

However, I am prepared to guess right now that the problem is either document corruption or font corruption. Word 2008 is getting a bit long in the tooth to handle the modern documents.

We need to conduct a process of elimination to get to the cause.

1)  Is it ALL documents, or just SOME documents?  If it's all of them, suspect fonts first.  If it's only some of them, suspect document corruption first.

2)  If it's all documents, carefully perform this procedure: http://word.mvps.org/Mac/fontweeding.html

3)  Go to the Help menu in any Office application and choose "Check for Updates".  Looking at your build number, it should tell you you are fully up to date, but it can't hurt to check.

4)  Then run Apple's Disk Utility and Repair Permissions on the boot drive.

Try now: that might have fixed it.

If only some of the documents are crashing, then it's a corrupt document for sure.  The fastest way to fix them is to send them back to PC Word.  Read the rest of this before continuing...

When you get them there, open the document and save out to the "other" format.  If they are .docx save to .doc.  If they are .doc, save to .docx.  Close the documents on the PC, re-open, change one character (e.g. add and delete a space) and save again (that forces Word to save the file).

If you saved to .doc, re-save the file as .docx.

Now, you need to be aware that if you save from .docx back to the obsolete .doc format, you will lose some data.  Mainly, picture information.  The text should not be changed, although some colours may change.  Sorry:  But the obsolete format just can't store all the things the later versions of Word can create.

The other thing that will be removed is the tracked changes, if there are any.  Which is just fine, because if there are any, chances are that's the cause of the crashing.

Hope this helps: Don't hesitate to get back to us if this does not fix the issue.

Cheers

mel turco say: Which PowerPoint version are you using? Your subject line indicates 2010, but the Applies To line states 2007, and you mention "ppt 201" and "ppt 7", which are neither. Nevertheless, version 2010 is the only version that grants independent window control to do what you want. If you have that version and it's not working, there may be an issue.

That aside, a point of confusion to technical experts might be that the need for independent windows is being called "independent programs" or "two instances of PowerPoint". It might throw the technical help person off track because PowerPoint has long been a single-instance program and remains so even with version 2010 – it's just in v2010 they added independent window control for working with multiple files.

Pointing out that any pre-2010 PowerPoint version lacks independent window control for multiple file viewing is a legitimate complaint (and so they added that very feature in version 2010). But to cite you can't get "multiple instances" or "multiple independent programs" with PowerPoint might fall on deaf ears to some techs because technically it's never been that way.

This may sound like technically ticky semantics, but you hold up Excel as an example of multiple instances that you want PowerPoint to be like – that validates this is more than semantics. PowerPoint cannot operate like Excel in that regard. Excel always has been a multi-instance program where you can launch several instances of the program (albeit not a good idea). PowerPoint, on the other hand, since at least version 97, has always been a single-instance program. (Even though v2010 now allows separate windows, it is still only one instance of PowerPoint driving them all.) So, it does no good to compare the two because one is a multi-instance program and the other a single-instance program.

Try it and see. Hit the Excel icon twice and hit the PowerPoint icon twice (any version – including 2010). Now check Task Manager, and see there are two instances of Excel but only one of PowerPoint. You cannot get two instances of PowerPoint running by launching the icon again.

The bottom line is pre-v2010 PowerPoint will not do what you want, but MS added your desired feature to v2010. It's not technically multiple instances, but it will do what you want, and it's found only in version 2010. If that's what you have and it's not working, then there's an issue. If not, it's just a matter of needing v2010.

Hello, doncow

What you have now: a monthly subscription paid via credit card, bank transfer, or other method.

What you want to do: pay that monthly subscription using your credit balance.

How to do this: Skype does not have a way to change a subscription's payment method. So, you will need to cancel your current subscription and start a new subscription, this time using your Skype Credit as the payment method.

So you can make an informed decision, please remember,

First, if you have a payment method on file on your account dashboard at skype.com, date.

Second, if you wish to stop the subscription when your credit balance is depleted, remember to stop automatic top-up (also called automatic re-charge) of your credit balance and to remove any payment method from your account dashboard at skype.com.  Very simply, when you run out of money, the subscription will be cancelled. You should receive several emails from Skype reminding you about this. Check your account dashboard at skype.com to be sure all of your account details are up-to-date and accurately typed.

Third, when you cancel your current subscription, you may find it is no longer available, and that if it is available, the price may have increased.

This FAQ article explains further:

Can I change my subscription

You can’t switch subscriptions or change your subscription to Skype Credit. However, you can cancel your subscription at any time and buy as many new subscriptions as you like.

If you have more than one subscription, Skype uses them in the following order:

  • By subscription type – unlimited subscriptions are used first, then limited ones.
  • By expiry date – subscriptions with the nearest expiry date are used first. 

Note: When you upgrade to a new subscription, the previous one will be cancelled and replaced with the new one once you've used all the minutes or the expiration date has passed.
