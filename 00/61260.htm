tryx3 say:

Solving this issue depends critically on how you installed & activated Windows 7 before.  Was Windows 7 preinstalled by Lenovo? 

  • If so then Belarc is useless - it can only recover product keys from activated copies of Windows 7 that have been activated by manually entering a product key [such as the key you are trying to read now].
  • In other words, if it was preinstalled by Lenovo then Belarc would only have been able to help if you ran it on your previous hard drive and only if you had for some reason already entered the product key from that label at some stage on the past. 

- - - - - - - - - - - -

If Windows 7 was preinstalled then the following is relevant:-

These characters are not used in Windows 7 product keys      

Letters         A   E   I   L   N   O   S   U   Z

Numbers     0   1   5

Your only hope of success lies in

1  being able to decipher the product key on that label

or

2  getting a Windows 7 reinstallation disk for your computer model directly** from Lenovo [as this would self-activate just as it did when your computer was originally first switched on].  They might not stock them any more but it is worth asking.

[** No other installation disks will have the ability to self-activate on your computer]

or

3  buying a new Windows 7 licence & hence a new product key [using Amazon or another trustworthy supplier not an anonymous seller who might sell you  a counterfeit or stolen product key].  There are a lot of fake keys being sold and they can pass inspection at first but then get identified as fake months or years later.  Personally, I would trust Amazon but not an Amazon marketplace seller as they can disappear overnight.

lemp say:

Depending on the nature/cause of the power outage, your network adapter may or may not have been physically damaged. 

If your adapter is physically OK, use the computer you used to ask your question and go to the web site of your afflicted computer's manufacturer.  Put in the identifying information for your particular unit (e.g., Dell Service Tag, Serial Number, Lenovo machine type/model, etc.) and locate the download page for your computer.  Download the driver for your network adapter, being sure to get the one appropriate for your Windows 7 64-bit system.

After you download the driver, copy it to a USB flash drive and move it via sneakernet to the afflicted computer and install it.  Installation instructions usually are on the download page.

If all you have is the generic model designation, you may get to a download page that has drivers for multiple network adapters.  In this case, you'll have to identify the particular adapter you have. 

You may be able to find this in Device Manger > Network adapters, or by clicking Start and typing msinfo32.exe into the Search box and pressing Enter.  In the System Information window, you normally find the network adapter under Components > Network > Adapter.

You can also click Start, type Network Connections in the Search box, and press Enter

If you can't identify the network adapter using software, you'll have to open up your computer and take a look at it.

You can save your Windows Live ID password on your console so that you can sign other Windows Live Services like Windows Live Messenger.

If you no longer want to store your Windows Live ID password on your console, you can remove it. Saving your Windows Live ID on your console is not related to signing into your profile or Xbox LIVE gamertag—it only affects Windows Live services like Messenger. Note For information on requiring your Windows Live ID password to sign into your profile, please see Password protect your Xbox LIVE profile.

  1. Sign in using your Xbox LIVE gamertag.
  2. Go to Settings, and then select Account.
  1. Go to Your Information, and then select Windows Live ID.
  2. Select Remove Password.

Then you can re-add the Windows Live ID to the new account.

colllecionador say:

Greetings elfwarrior

You can associate my windows live ID to a different gamertag by following these instructions.

Before you can switch your gamertag in this case, you must first remove the existing gamertag from that Microsoft account:

  1. Create a new Microsoft account. You will only need this new account as a temporary means of moving your gamertags. After the gamertags have been switched, you will no longer need this Microsoft account.

    Example:
    Gamertag A is linked to Microsoft account 1.
    Gamertag B is linked to Microsoft account 2.
    To swap gamertags A and B between Microsoft accounts 1 and 2, first create a third temporary account: Microsoft account 3.

  2. Switch the gamertag that is linked to the Microsoft account you want to use to the new temporary account you just created.

    Example:
    Gamertag A remains linked to Microsoft account 1.
    Switch gamertag B to Microsoft account 3.
    Microsoft account 2 is now free.

  3. Now switch your gamertag to the Microsoft account you want to use.

    Example:
    Switch gamertag A to Microsoft account 2.
    Gamertag B remains linked to Microsoft account 3.
    Microsoft account 1 is now free.

  4. To complete the swap of gamertags between the two original Microsoft accounts, you must wait 30 days. (A gamertag can be switched only once every 30 days.)

    Example:
    Gamertag A remains linked to Microsoft account 2.
    After 30 days, switch gamertag B to Microsoft account 1.
    Microsoft account 3 is now free and no longer needed.

dustinclaeys say:

Sign into your Microsoft account (formerly known as windows live id) on account.live.com

On the OVERVIEW page that says "Account Summary" at the top, click on Add or Change Alias (found under the name and birthday).

On the next page click on Add Alias and then create a new Microsoft email address.

Make the new email the primary alias and then remove the old email that you want to use.

This will then associate your old/unused gamertag to that new email and will make your old email available to use on the different gamertag.

There will be a 30-day waiting period before you can create a new Microsoft account using that removed email address.
