tjorrel say:

joeu2004 say: Tjorrel wrote:
I am using dd:hh:mm formatting to add calculated time but when total days are more than 31 the format function drops 31 days - how can I get Excel to dispaly more than 31 days in a dd:hh:mm sum calculation?

First, see if XL2007 supports the Custom format [d]:hh:mm.  XL2003 does not :-(.

If XL2007 does not as well, you might have to resort to the following:

=INT(A2-A1) & ":" & TEXT(A2-A1,"hh:mm")

I do not know of any other way to present the value in the form d:hh:mm.

However, note that that returns text, not a numeric value.  You cannot use it directly in arithmetic expressions because Excel will interpret that form as hours, minutes and seconds.

Clear Footnote Formatting - Microsoft Community

wadsdeirder say:

Clear Footnote Formatting

I have a question. Perhaps someone has an answer. I have some footnotes that contain italicized text and would like to clear the formatting for them. I see that using the select all command (Control-A) disables the Clear Formatting button in the ribbon. Is there a way to clear footnote formatting without removing them or going through them individually (although if I have to do the latter, then it will simply take a matter of time)?
suzanne s. barnhill say:

You could switch to Draft view, display the Footnote pane, select all the footnotes, and press Ctrl+Spacebar, but if you're using the default Superscript formatting for the footnote reference numbers, that would also be removed, which you don't want. Here's another way to do the same thing:

  1. Press Ctrl+H to open the Replace dialog.
  2. Click More>> to expand the dialog.
  3. With the insertion point in the "Find what" box, click Format, then Style, and choose Footnote Text.
  4. With the insertion point in the "Replace with" box, click Format, then Style, and choose Default Paragraph Font.
  5. Click Replace All.

Unfortunately, that has the same unfortunate result of removing the Superscript formatting. Here's how to restore it:

  1. In the Replace dialog, in the "Find what" box, type ^f (the code for a footnote reference mark).
  2. In the "Replace with dialog, click , then Style, and choose Footnote Reference.
  3. Click Replace All.

If italics are the only kind of formatting you need to remove from the footnotes, then the problem is considerably simpler. Follow the exact same instructions given in the first sequence above, with this addition: In the "Find what" box, press Ctrl+I. Below the box you'll see "Format: Style: Footnote Text, Font: Italic." This search will find only italicized text in footnotes and will restore the Default Paragraph Font.

Alternatively, if you just want to remove the italics and leave any other direct formatting, then, instead of selecting the Default Paragraph Font style for the "Replace with" box, just press Ctrl+I twice to give "Format: Font: Not Italic."

johnnyk6845 say:

How can I empty or delete a corrupt .pst file in Outlook 2003?

I have a corrupt Inbox. I tried using the Scanpst.exe app and got a message saying that there was an unknown error in the >pst file and Scanpst could not make the repair.

I uninstalled the entire Office program and reinstalled it and the old Inbox showed up again with the same problem. How can I delete the pst file and start over?

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:
Open the Mail applet in Control Panel and click Data Files.  Add a new PST there.  Click OK or Finish until you're back at the initial Mail applet screen and click E-mail Accounts.  Select View or change existing e-mail accounts and click Next.  At the lower left you'll see a drop-down labeled "Deliver new e-mail to the follow location".  Change that drop-down to indicate the new PST you just created.  Click OK or Finish until you're back at the initial Mail applet dialog.  Click Data Files again.  You should now be able to delete the broken PST.  OK or Finish and exit the applet.  Now you can start Outlook and it will open with an empty set of folders.

kosh vorlon say:

Hi Cindy,

 

If you posted here because you thought this was a virus, it is remotely possible but highly unlikely.  This sounds much more like a setup, configuration, and/or compatibility issue.  But as you are here, I'll try to answer your questions anyway.

 

To determine the version of Kodak EasyShare, since you can't open it to check, download and run Belarc Advisor http://www.belarc.com/free_download.html and it should provide that to you (assuming it is recognized if it isn't compatible).  To check if that version is compatible, you can either go to http://support.en.kodak.com/app/answers/detail/a_id/36670/selected/true (and then either search for your version or upgrade if your device can support more recent software or contact them for advice) or you can try the Windows 7 Compatibility Center http://www.microsoft.com/windows/compatibility/windows-7/en-us/default.aspx or download the Windows 7 Upgrade Advisor http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/downloads/upgrade-advisor and get it done automatically.

 

You can do the same for Incredimail 2 (with Belarc and Windows 7 Compatibility and Upgrade advisors).  Also, you can find Incredimail 2 support at http://www.incredimail.com/english/help/support.aspx#.  The automated chat seemed pretty good answering receive e-mail questions, but I'm not sure your provider so I couldn't get very far.  If you have the free version, I don't know how much direct support you will get from them - but it's worth a try.  They also offer Forum Support (like here) at: http://forums.incredimail.com/ where you might be able to find or post for further assistance from other users and their support personnel who presumably show up periodically.  According to what I read, the current version (version 6) supports Windows 7 (but I'm not sure what version you have - you may need to upgrade).

 

After that they suggest contacting your ISP, but who you really want to contact is your e-mail provider (who may be the same or it may be someone different or you may have several accounts at different providers (as I do)).  After (maybe even before) compatibility, the highest probability is a problem with your settings.  I realize you probably copied them from the other machine so you assume they must be correct (and the fact that you can send mail is a good sign because it suggests you are making contact with the e-mail server and hints that the problem may be resolved).  As you didn't provide information about your e-mail provider, I can't help you there.  While malware will most likely not be the problem (it's remotely possible, but you have a new machine and I haven't seen many that do just this and you aren't even getting e-mails so your exposure is more limited), it could be an issue with your Firewall setup blocking the port you need to use for receiving mail (especially if it isn't a commonly-used one), so check with your provider and/or Incredimail to see if that might be an issue.  It could also be your general internet setup or the setup on whatever you are using for your e-mail (an e-mail program or a browser) - and there either the e-mail provider or perhaps your ISP (if different) may be able to assist you.  While you're checking Incredimail support and forums, you might want to look into the export or transfer options to see if there's a way you overlooked to get those things moved while you're dealing with receiving e-mails as well. 

 

Did you by any chance try XP to Windows 7 Easy Transfer http://www.microsoft.com/download/en/details.aspx?displaylang=en&id=4503 and http://www.sevenforums.com/tutorials/115321-windows-easy-transfer-xp-vista-windows-7-a.html?  At this point you probably won't need all of it, but it may help with the Incredimail and may also help transfer some of your other program settings (if using some of the same programs and after they've been installed on the new machine) that couldn't be just copied over.

 

If you're getting no e-mails, then you will not receive e-mail notifications of replies here, but you can always just check the forum to see if you have any responses.  The simplest way to do that is to go to your profile by clicking on your name at the top (after you login) and then click on the My Questions tab to find this thread and then double-click on the thread to come here and see any new activity periodically.

 

I hope this helps.

 

Good luck!
