Okay.  The formula in G7 is:

=SUMIFS('RAW DATA'!D4:D14589,'RAW DATA'!A4:A14589,A7,'RAW DATA'!B4:B14589,D7)

That says:  sum D4:D14589 where corresponding A4:A14589 matches A7 (R11971) and B4:B14589 matches D7 (9/21/2011).  Of the 6 records in A3323:A3328 that match R11971, none has a date of 9/21/2011 in D3323:D3328.

So G7 should return zero, not because SUMIFS is treating anything as text, but because the criteria do not match.

As for G8, the formula is:

=SUMIFS('RAW DATA'!D4:D14589,'RAW DATA'!B4:B14589,">D8")

That should be written:

=SUMIFS('RAW DATA'!D4:D14589,'RAW DATA'!B4:B14589,">" & D8)

Then that should return 39868.  I did not vet that result; I'll trust it ;-).

But if you want to combine both criteria (i.e. only R11971 records > D8, 9/21/2011), you would write:

=SUMIFS('RAW DATA'!D4:D14589,'RAW DATA'!A4:A14589,A8,
'RAW DATA'!B4:B14589,">" & D8)

That should return 3 because there is only one R11971 record with date > 9/21/2011, namely A3328 (10/13/2011).

james m bayley say:

Navigare1954. I suspect the root cause is that our Primary Alias is not an email address ending in @outlook.com. However I cannot test this with my account at present because it is locked.

I suggest

  1. Change your Primary Account email to one ending in @outlook.com
  2. Remove your Outlook 2016 account from Outlook 2016, close and reboot your PC
  3. Add your outlook.com account to Outlook 2016 using your @outlook.com email address

You mailbox should appear in Outlook 2016 with the correct username. Your secondary alias will be available to use in Outlook 2016 by clicking the FROM header.

Know issues

  • You cannot make a secondary email address your default "From"
  • emails sent in Outlook 2016 using your secondary email addresses may appear to the recipient as " yourname @ outlook.com on behalf of yourname @ yourdomain.com"

If you do test this fix please Reply to this thread with the outcome.

James
