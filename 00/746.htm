First contacts must be already added to your main contact list before they can be added to a group.

How to go to the new contact window, People.

Open outlook.com > top left, place your cursor over the logo Outlook > a down arrow will appear > click at the arrow > click People > here is your People window.

 

How to create a group :
Go to People window > toolbar at top, click Manage > click Manage groups > click the plus (+) sign, a new box will appear, enter a name for the new group > click Apply > a new group is created.

How to add contacts to the group you have created :

Still in People window > click a contact you want to be added to the group > if you have multiple contacts to add, click the box at top left of the contact > continue clicking the top left box of each contact you want to add to the group until you are done> at the toolbar, click Groups > check the box in front of the group name > click Apply > all the contacts you have selected are now in the group.

Note : make sure you click squarely at the check box. If you click outside the box, all previous boxes you have checked will be nullified, and you’ll have to start all over.

How to delete a contact from a group but keep it in the main contact list:

Go to People > click the contact you want to remove from a group > toolbar, click Groups > un-check the box in front of the group in question > click Apply when done.

How to Edit contacts within a group

On the People window, top left, click the “ All v “ > you group is on the drop down list > click the group name > now you have all the contacts within that group listed > click one to view > to edit, click at the contact’s name ( not its check box ) > click Edit at the toolbar to do editing......
Note : Edit option only appears at the toolbar after you have selected a contact.
 

How to access a group :
In the People window, near top left, click at the word " All v " > a drop down box will appear with a list of your groups > click the one you want > all the contacts within that selected group are now on the left side > scroll to see the whole list.

john korchok say:

You haven't included many details of the scenario that makes you think locking is necessary, so we can only offer general advice.

Using the program interface, you can protect the document. There are several varieties of protection, with the most flexible being protecting for forms. This allows you to create unprotected sections where users can enter text. You can also use text form fields, dropdowns and checkboxes in the protected sections. These are useful for forms.

The alternative is to hack the XML. This will work iwth any .docx or .dotx document, but not with .doc or .dot files. Here's my article on the subject: XML Hacking: Locking Graphics I wrote this about PowerPoint, but the principles are the same for locking graphics and text boxes in Word are the same.

After opening the file in a suitable editor, items in the header will be in the word/headerX.xml where X is a number from 1 to 3, depending on what type of headers are in use. Items in the body of the document will be in word/document.xml.

In Word, pictures locks must be applied to the graphic frame that holds the picture, rather than to the picture itself. Here's what a locked graphic frame will look like:

<wp:cNvGraphicFramePr>

openxmlformats.org/drawingml/2006/main" noSelect="1" />

With XML locking, the document remains a normal Word file that is completely editable. You just can't select the locked items.

I finally figured out how to renew my Microsoft Outlook Premium subscription.  I was in the pilot and just got a 30-day renewal notice.  The issue is that if you go to https://account.microsoft.com/services , you can't 'renew' or change your subscription that's showing to 'auto-renew'.  I know everyone's been looking for this info and since I couldn't find it anywhere, I thought I'd give a thorough write-up.  The info below is current as of 1/3/2018.

To renew, you have to get into MSN's billing area.  Note this renews for 1 year from the date of renewal - it does not extend your subscription for some reason but it will pro-rate it based on your renewal date eventually.
  1. Visit https://premium.outlook.com
  2. Login with your PRIMARY Outlook Premium Outlook (the master account)
  3. Once logged in, you should see the v2 dashboard (manage your domain, your account invites, your aliases, etc.)
  4. Visit https://signup.msn.com/pages/ChoosePlan.aspx (I know - trust me. MSN??!?!)
  5. It will say 'change from outlook.com premium to outlook.com premium' - sounds ridiculous, but it works.
  6. Confirm your payment info and place your order
  7. You should immediately receive an order confirmation
  8. Note that the renewal date will be change to be 30-days from when you do this.  At first, I thought I had lost a month (since I still had one month left before my renewal) but the payment just came through and was less than $19.95 (they pro-rated it to deal with the one month I had left).  Also of note this took just over a week to process
  9. Now when I go back in to https://premium.outlook.com/ , scroll to the bottom and click 'manage subscription underneath the  'Your Subscription' ', then 'change your billing information or cancel your subscription', it'll bring you to https://account.microsoft.com/services/ where under Outlook.com premium, you should see a new expiration date.  If you click on 'Payment and Billing' under Outlook.com Premium, it should show your billing history and the transactions now (note this took just over a week to show up on my account despite receiving the order confirmation immediately after submitting my info.

Remember that Outlook Premium Help is different than other Microsoft areas.  To reach them, go back to your Outlook Premium Dashboard (https://premium.outlook.com) and click 'Contact Us' (for me, it's a small tab in the LOWER right corner of the screen).  It'll pop-up a form, fill it out and submit it, and you should receive a confirmation to your email with a ticket#.  That said, they only help with "engineering and onboarding" issues and referred me back to a useless support phone number for billing support.
