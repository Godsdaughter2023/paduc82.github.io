This is actually a standard technique called Cascading or Synchronized comboboxes. How do to this is well documented if you look up those terms.

ken sheridan say:

If you want to restrict the search on the basis of a Boolean AND operation, then you would use correlated combo boxes.  You'll find an example in ComboDemo.zip in my public databases folder at:

https://onedrive.live.com/?cid=44CC60D7FEA42912&id=44CC60D7FEA42912!169

If you have difficulty opening the link copy its text (NB, not the link location) and paste it into your browser's address bar.

This little demo file includes a form for 'drilling down through a hierarchy' which uses unbound correlated combo boxes in the form's header.

To restrict on the basis of a Boolean OR operation is much simpler.  You'd simply have two combo boxes, not correlated, and restrict the result set on the basis of:

WHERE ProductType = Forms!YourForm!cboProductType
OR Manufacturer = Forms!YourForm!cboManufacturer

In my demo the result set is returned in a bound form, in your case it would be reurned as the RowSource of a list box or combo box from which the user can select an item.

DLL ("dynamic link library") files such as dnsapi.dll are small programs, similar to EXE ("executable") files, which allow multiple software programs to share the same functionality.

Most dnsapi.dll errors are related to missing or corrupt dnsapi.dll files. Because dnsapi.dll is an external file, it presents a great opportunity for something undesirable to occur.

HTC HD2 back it up from myphone what can i do?

julie fka profjulie say: There is little you can do at this time.  Unless you synced your phone to Outlook on your computer via ActiveSync or WMDC, or unless you used some third party backup software to back up the phone to a storage card, you won't be able to sync or restore your contacts to your phone. 

My Phone is being shut down, and effective August 7th the abilty to sync between a phone and the the My Phone service stopped working.  Now Microsoft is migrating My Phone info to user SkyDrive accounts and the service will be completely shut down in October.  According to Microsoft's timeline, your contacts will be migrated to your SkyDrive account as a csv file and your text messages will be migrated to your SkyDrive account as txt files.  The migration will be completed by October.  Once the csv and txt files are migrated over to your SkyDrive account, you still won't be able to sync them to your phone, but you'll be able to download the files to your computer.  Then you can import the csv file into Outlook to get your contacts there or you could import the csv file into the Windows Live Contacts and then sync your phone to that.  You probably won't be able to get the text files back to your phone so they display as text messages there..

See this knowledgebase article for more information about the My Phone shut down.
