chris cupler say:

That's something that has been asked for (for years, too) over at thegreenbutton (among other places.)  There was hope that Homegroup would be the thing that would finally make it possible (it is, after all, a protected, 'trusted' sharing system, and the WM-DRM (what MS uses for CablaCARD tuners) supports local protected streaming); but, it didn't happen.  We don't know why (and, frankly, probably never will.)
Best theory is that there was the worry that, because the file might need to hit a hard drive (if even temporarily) on the remote system, copying would (maybe) be possible.  :(

Why can your 360 do it, though?  Because an Extender runs as a local user on its host Media Center; because the stream never hits a hard drive on the Extender (it all goes to memory); and because there's a secure (encrypted) connection between the Extender and its host.
That is the difference.

Hello i_wenwen,

The Microsoft Wireless Display Adapter works like your typical USB stick. It draws power from the device where it is connected to. Once the adapter is unplugged, the power is cut. The adapter may heat up due to constant transferring of information. Also, the button located on the adapter is the reset button.
