hansv mvp say:

This is to be expected. If the number format of a cell is set to Text, anything you enter or edit in this cell is displayed as entered. So if you enter or edit a formula, the cell will display that formula, not its result.

Solution: do not set the number format of a cell with a formula to Text.

frustrated onenote say:

I have answered my own question.  Answer will seem simple to those who know it, but for others who might have this problem and waste hours, the answer is to click on the upper left of the screen, to the left of the file tabs you're working in, on the name of the notebook you're using (don't click on File, which is what I had been doing).  When you click on that, you will see a drop down list of your open Notebooks.  if you now click on the folder you were working on to drag something into a notebook on the notebook list, the active screen of your list of notebooks will disappear, which frustrated me for ages.  All you have to do is to hit the little pin icon on the right of the screen that lists your notebooks to keep it active so you can drag and drop.  Because the pin is adjacent to the command 'add notebook', I'd assumed that it would pin that 'add notebook' command to the working screen.   Come to find out, it pins the entire page, with all the notebooks, so you can work between them while in your folders.  I was going to give up on OneNote and just use Windows Explorer again, but OneNote's functionality just quintupled for me.  Eureka! 

I still think OneNote should enable drag and drop of notebooks into other notebooks, but this is almost close enough. 

I hope this saves somebody time.

tarandeepkaur say: This is common in all windows mobile devices(both professional and standard). When the device goes into standby mode, the operating system tells the WiFi radio to switch itself off. That is why you loose the connection. When you again turn it on, the OS tells the wifi radio to turn itself on again. This time, it may or may not reconnect by itself. If it does not, there is no need to turn the phone off and on. Just follow these steps:
  • go to start> settings
  • go to connections tab
  • go to wifi
  • select your wifi network. tap and hold on it and select "connect".
