If you've lost years and years of very important work, it would seem as though you didn't do much to to protect it. If you are referring to data files, they should never be left in with your operating system and installed programs, but should be kept in a separate data partition, and redundantly backed up in a different location than your computer, such as online.

That allows upgrades/updates to be made to your operating system without any effect on your data. Further, the described backups protect against failing hard drives, house fires, and computer theft.

Example: My accounting program is installed along with the other installed programs in the operating system partition. The data files it generates are in the data partition and backed up as described above. When I upgrade to a different operating system, the accounting program simply begins to be operated by the new OS, but the data files aren't moved or changed in any way, they continue being operated upon by the accounting program, which is operated by the new operating system.

You should also maintain a current disc-image type backup of your operating system partition, saved on a different hard drive, to protect against hard drive failure, etc.

Then, for example, if you upgrade from Win 7 to Win 10, but have a serious problem, it only takes about 10 minutes to restore back to your Win 7, and your data remains unscathed.

P.S.  If you have troubles with a USB Boot, I've found that 90% of the time it's because the BIOS have not been configured to boot to a USB device OR (as is the case with my own computer), the computer and the BIOS cannot be made to boot to a USB drive (completely impossible) and I need to create a CD/DVD from another computer when I need to do this.
