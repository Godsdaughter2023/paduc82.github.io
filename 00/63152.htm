¡firedog say:

MS Windows Hotmail. the one that comes with all Windows, this is ver. #11

Hotmail had several different names during its lifetime, which ended in 2013. The last name it had was Windows Live Hotmail. That changed to Outlook.com, which in turn changed again over the past couple of years to Outlook Mail. This is a webmail service available for free to anyone with an Internet connection. It doesn't 'come with Windows' or any other operating system; you just need a web browser to access it.

Because it's a web service, all your messages are stored on an Outlook Mail server. This means you can't do much with them except read them, reply to them and forward them to someone else. One way to do more, though, is to use a mail client - a program on your computer that can communicate with the Outlook Mail server. Using the communications protocol IMAP, you can both download messages to your computer and upload them to the server. This is perhaps the easiest way of moving your stored messages from one Outlook Mail account to another.

Which mail client to use depends on your operating system and how much you want to spend, but it's your choice.
