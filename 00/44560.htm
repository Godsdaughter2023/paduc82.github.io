canadian tech say:

In my humble opinion that's huge overkill, unless you are a bank or a business backing up an accounting system or inventory.  For usual home users, 2 or 3 is plenty.  My personal habit is a once a month backup.

If you are about to buy an external hard drive, consider these issues:

Many of them actually cook themselves because they have inadequate ventilation.  They can only practically be used for short periods.   So look for one with a big case.

The smaller ones have the 2.5" format drives which are normally installed in portable computers.  They are much more delicate and less reliable.  I would go for a larger 3.5 because you don't need portability and they are more reliable and last longer.

My favoured way of doing this is to buy an External hard drive housing -- they are way better built.  For example:

Then buy yourself an OEM type 3.5 HD of any size you choose.  2Tb is quite reasonably priced.  It also has its own power supply and tends to be way better ventilated. 

Also make certain you get one that is USB 3 capable, capable of the size drive you buy and has a USB 3.0 cable that comes with it -- they are different from the USB 2 cables.

Do not use an SSD for this function.
