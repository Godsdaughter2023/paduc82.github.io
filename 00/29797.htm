Hi searsh,

These are my pieces of advice.
First, try to reset your browser. Don't just optimize it, reset it.
If you're using IE6:
1. Click Start, Run, type INETCPL.CPL.
2. Click Programs Tab.
3. Click Restore Defaults.
4. Close Internet Explorer 6 and re-open it.

If you're using IE7 or IE8:
1. Click Start, Run, type INETCPL.CPL.
2. Click Advanced Tab.
3. Click Reset.
4. A new window will appear. Click Reset.
5. Click OK when resetting is done.
6. Close the browser and re-open it.

If resetting the browser does not work, try pinging a website to check if your internet connection

is still working. This is how to do that:
1. Click Start.
2. Click Run.
3. Type CMD and press Enter.
4. Type PING WWW.BING.COM then press Enter.
5. At the end of the results of pinging www.bing.com, you should read: Packets Sent=4; Received=4;

Lost=0.
If you read that, that means your internet connection is still working. Because of that, you need to

do the steps below:
1. Since you are still on the command prompt, type IPCONFIG and press Enter.
2. Look for the IP Address. An IP Address looks like this: 192.168.2.1. If your IP Address starts

with 169 like 169.12.1.2, do Steps A. Otherwise, do Steps B.


Steps A.
1. Turn off your computer.
2. Turn off the router. Unplug the router's cable from the power outlet.
3. Unplug the cable connected from the modem going to the back of the tower of the computer.
4. Allow it that way for about 3-5 minutes.
5. After 3-5 minutes, plug in the power cable of the router to the power outlet.
6. Plug in again the cable connected from the modem going to the back of the tower of the computer.
7. Turn on the computer and try to browse. Let me know if you could go online.

Steps B:
1. While you're at the command prompt, type IPCONFIG /RELEASE and press Enter.
2. Type IPCONFIG /RENEW and press Enter.
3. Restart the computer.
4. Try to go online and let me know if you could go online.

If the above-mentioned steps do not work, let's do clean-boot. Perform these to do that:
1. Click Start, Run, type MSCONFIG, and press Enter.
2. Click the Startup Tab and click Disable All button.
3. Click the Services Tab, check the Hide All Microsoft Services box, and click the Disable All button.
4. Click Apply or OK button then restart the computer.
5. Try to go online and let me know if this works.

If none of these steps works, let me know immediately. I'll give you further instructions then. This is all for now.

I do hope these help.
