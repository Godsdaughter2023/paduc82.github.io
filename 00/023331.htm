astra2025 say:

Hi easyv, 

 

Thank you for your contribution to the forums. 

 

We are sorry for the inconveniences regarding your USB port. 

 

In fact, this is what the small white light means: when your device is overly discharged, and you plug the charger into it, the light will blink. This means that several minutes are needed for the phone to get minimum power and that you may not be able to start the phone. After a few minutes, the light will stop blinking and will be just static: this means that the phone still has very low battery but at this time you can try and turn on the phone. And the last stage is that the light will disappear, this meaning that the phone has now a normal battery level and can be started and used at anytime. 

Obviously the phone is probably not always used until overly discharged before re-charging it. In these cases, the phone will charge just normally without the white light first turning on at all. 

 

If there is a fault with the USB port of the device, the sequence of light pattern may not be as above and may be interrupted due to occasional cuts of power supply. Therefore, if your device does not follow the sequence above in the appropriate circumstances, please visit you local Nokia Care Point for a check up.  

 

As for the repair/warranty issue you have had, and for complaints, please contact your local Careline and ask to speak with the After Sales department.

john korchok say:

A bug doesn't produce general symptoms of problems with data sheets. A bug is a specific programming error that consistently occurs in the same circumstances. So if everyone with a given version of Windows, running a given version of PowerPoint, has the same symptoms of data sheets not remembering settings, that's a bug. If only 1 person in 1000 (of the population that has the same OD and Office version) has that specific problem, it's more likely a configuration, installation or registry problem rather than a bug.

Configuration problems can often be solved by an Office Repair:

  1. Close all Office programs.
  2. Click on the Start button, then on Settings, then on Apps.
  3. Click on Microsoft Office 2013, then on the Modify button.
  4. Select Online Repair, then click on the Repair button.
  5. Allow the process to complete, restart your computer, then see if PowerPoint retains data sheet selections as expected.
