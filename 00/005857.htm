Do you have some good reason to store this field in a table, as opposed to just displaying it for the user? It would appear to violate two basic relational principles: that fields should be "atomic", storing only one piece of information (you're concatenating six), and - even more important - that data should not be stored redundantly! suzanne s. barnhill say:

The usual cause of this discrepancy is an extra, empty paragraph. The way this comes about is a Word idiosyncrasy so maddening I would have to describe it as a bug: when you select header/footer content and copy, even though you have carefully selected the text exclusive of the paragraph mark, when you then paste the content into another header/footer, even though you have carefully included the paragraph mark in the selection to be overwritten, you will still always get an extra, empty paragraph.

Because you are operating with nonprinting characters displayed, this should be very obvious, so I don't think you would have missed it, but it occurs to me that if both headers had an extra paragraph and one of them had been formatted as Hidden, you would get the effect you describe from toggling the display of nonprinting characters (including Hidden text).

If the horizontal line is a paragraph border (as I would assume), then there could be a discrepancy in the "Distance from text" setting for the border, but this would not be affected by toggling Show/Hide Â¶.

Is the text formatted as Automatic (so that it automatically reverses from black to white on a dark background) or White? In either case, try setting it to the other. As a general rule, especially for an inkjet printer, it is advisable to format the text as bold (and use a sans serif font); otherwise it may be too thin to show up well when reversed out of a dark background.
