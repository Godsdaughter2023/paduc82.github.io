rohn007 say:

Create a new shortcut on the desktop

Paste the following command into the "Target" field. You will have to customize it to point to the location your Office is installed

"C:\Program Files (x86)\Microsoft Office 14\Office14\WINWORD.EXE" /w

The key is the " /w" or " /n" at the very end.  They are called command line parameters. It is very old school.

The /w starts Word with a blank document, /n starts word without a blank document.

Here is a link to the full documentation for startup parameters

Word 2000 - 2010 command line startup switches
