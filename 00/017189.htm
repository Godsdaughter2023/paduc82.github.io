Note: Workbook1 and Workbook2 should be saved in same location in the memory.

Wb1.xlsx is the name of work book 1, sheet1 is the name of the sheet in work book 1 and B6 is the cell. Adding this formula in workbook2_sheet1_cell A12 will get data from cell B6 in workbook1_sheet1.

Formula using greater/less than or equal to with CountIFS - Microsoft Community

jedi4life say:

Formula using greater/less than or equal to with CountIFS

I was able to create formulas to calculate time, ie 24 hours, 36 hours, 48 hours, etc.  This formula worked to count all cells resulting in 24 hours or less:

=COUNTIFS('FY16-17'!F466:F552,"<=24.00")

This worked to count all cells resulting from 24.01 hours to 36.00 hours,

=COUNTIFS('FY16-17'!L553:L682,"None",'FY16-17'!F553:F682,">=24.01")-COUNTIFS('FY16-17'!L553:L682,"None",'FY16-17'!F553:F682,">=36.00")

This worked to count all cells resulting from 36.01 to 48.00 hours

=COUNTIF('FY16-17'!F466:F552,">=36.01")-COUNTIF('FY16-17'!F466:F552,">=48.00")

and this worked to count anything equal to or greater than 48.01.

=COUNTIFS('FY16-17'!F466:F552,">=48.01")

But now the formulas will not work if the result happens to be equal to the time itself.  I got one which resulted in exactly 24.00 hours and another was exactly 36.00 hours. But those cells were NOT counted. Why didn't it count them? Can someone help me with my formulas?  I'm lost.

Appreciate the help!

myall_blues say:

There are gaps in your formulas so that certain times are not accounted for.

For example, in the first formula, you have criteria of <=24.00 and in the second you have >=24.01. Anything greater than 24 and less than 24.01 (24.005 for example) will never be counted.

My guess is that although your value of 'exactly' 24.00 looks exact, it is probably due to your cell formatting and the actual value is somewhere between 24 and 24.01.

To fix it, try making the lower bounds of the second, third and fourth formulas just ">" the whole number, not ">=" the number plus a bit.

e.g. =COUNTIFS('FY16-17'!L553:L682,"None",'FY16-17'!F553:F682,">24")-COUNTIFS('FY16-17'!L553:L682,"None",'FY16-17'!F553:F682,">=36.00")

Incidentally, there is no need to construct these formula like this with a substraction. For COUNTIFS the criteria are implicitly AND so all conditions must hold for it to count. So you can construct it as:

=COUNTIFS('FY16-17'!L553:L682,"None",'FY16-17'!F553:F682,">24",'FY16-17'!F553:F682,"<=36")
