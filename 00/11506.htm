hansv mvp say:
Sub GoToStart()
  Dim rng As Range
  If Selection.Information(wdWithInTable) Then
    Set rng = Selection.Tables(1).Cell(1, 1).Range
    rng.Collapse Direction:=wdCollapseStart
    rng.Select
  End If
End Sub

or as an alternative (as is often the case with VBA).

Sub GoToStart()
  If Selection.Information(wdWithInTable) Then

End Sub

Upgrades for Office 365 for business customers
If you're an Office 365 for business subscriber, and you're still on Office 2013, you may have started seeing this upgrade notification recently. Here's the explanation for that:

  • On May 4, 2016, we turned on Microsoft-initiated upgrades for business customers, which caused the notification to appear in Office 2013 apps.
  • On May 9, 2016, we paused the upgrades to address some issues. Fixes for these issues will go to business users in the June product update.

Upgrades for Office 365 for home customers

IMPORTANT   You can block the "Get the new Office" banner using the methods described above.
