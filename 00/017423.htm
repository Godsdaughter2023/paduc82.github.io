 turned off my anti virus program.

Unfortunately,  "turning off" the AV function does not remove all of a security package's interfering effects.  It is often necessary to remove a package completely, sometimes even necessary to find a "cleanup" tool from the manufacturer and then retest.  I'm not sure it is even enough to test in safe mode and then assume that you are free of its effects but that could be a simpler test to try short of uninstalling the package.  

In any case, just as is sometimes a good idea to uninstall and then reinstall add-ons where you suspect that there are incompatibilities between them and a new version of the host program, it can similarly be a good idea to do the same thing with your security package--e.g. get its hooks set by the new environment (assuming an API) instead of just depending on whatever compatibility features the new environment is trying to provide to perpetuate the old ones.

I've looked at the separator under "show notes" in draft view, and it doesn't appear to have any extra spaces associated with it. This actually happens with all of my Word documents.

Thanks for your insight.
stefan blom say:

When viewing the footnote separator (in the footnotes pane), right-click and select Paragraph from the context menu. In the Paragraph dialog box, click the Indents and Spacing tab, and make sure that Spacing Before is set to zero. You may also want to change the Line Spacing to "Single."

   

Also, to make sure no blank paragraphs is included with the separator, click the ¶ button on the Home tab. Paragraph marks are represented by pilcrows, ¶. Delete any excessive paragraphs if present.
