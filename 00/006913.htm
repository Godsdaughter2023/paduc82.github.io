Assuming the 12% is the APR (annual percentage rate), them payment =PMT(12%/4,5*4,5000).

The term APR has different meanings in different regions and contexts; and in any case, it does not help to indicate how to compute a periodic rate.  It is best to remove that term from your vocabulary, IMHO.

In the US, APR is a simple interest rate, as Bernard used it above.  But in the EU, it is called "effective annual [percentage] rate" or EAR, which is a compound interest rate.  Ironically, in Canada, APR is a mix:  twice the semiannual compounded monthly rate.

In any case, in all regions (AFAIK), the loan rate denoted by the term APR per se might include other finance costs amortized over the full term of the loan.  It might be useful for comparing "the true cost" of various loan choices (not IMHO).  But such a rate is not the interest rate applied to the outstanding balance each period, which is what we need for the PMT and other financial functions.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

You must insert the merge fields using the Insert Merge Field button. When field codes are displayed, you will see:

{MERGEFIELD First_Name } { MERGEFIELD Last_Name }{ IF { MERGEFIELD Guest_First } <> "" "and { MERGEFIELD Guest_First } { MERGEFIELD Guest_Last }" }

When field codes are not displayed, you will see either

«First_Name» «Last_Name» and «Guest_First» «Guest_Last»

or

«First_Name» «Last_Name»

depending on the results of the IF field for the first record. Once you complete the merge, the merge fields will be replaced by the data in your Excel data source.
