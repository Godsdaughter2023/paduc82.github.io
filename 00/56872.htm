How do I protect a document with a password?

While you can restrict access to your files to yourself using the Windows permission tools, anyone with the right knowledge can get around this restriction. The same goes for passwords applied to Word or Excel documents: They are easily cracked. If you need real protection then you should look at a product such asTrueCrypt :

http://www.truecrypt.org/downloads.php
http://www.practicalpc.co.uk/computing/windows/xpencrypt1.htm

You should apply it to a whole folder, not just a file. Here is how it works.

  1. You create a TrueCrypt "container" of a size sufficient to take your pictures (plus future expansion). This container will be visible as an extra drive, e.g. drive P: ("P" for pictures).
  2. You move your pictures to drive P:.
  3. When you wish to view your pics you open TrueCrypt and enter the password you specified in Step 1.

Note this:
- TrueCrypt is largely crack-proof. If you forget your password then you're history.
- It is possible to automate the opening of drive P:.
