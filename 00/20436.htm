What Authentication Methods are supported? "The SMTP server - Microsoft Community

nicholasjenkins1 say:

What Authentication Methods are supported? "The SMTP server does not recognize any of the authentication methods supported by Outlook"

Year over year, Microsoft has increasingly changed Outlook to have new baseline requirements when adding a new imap or pop3 email account.

One such example I ran across today...

I host my own home server, based upon freeware products.  A service that I enabled provided imap folders.

In trying to add access to those imap folders (add a new account) to Outlook, I found that Outlook would not let me without also specifying an SMTP server. (Arguably, IMAP provides "inbound" email access, and SMTP offers "outbound" email access...so you can see why outbound should not be necessary to establish access to inbound content.)

So, on my home server, I activated SMTP services as well, yet I kept getting an error from Outlook which prevented me from adding the account to Outlook (latest version from my Office 365 subscription)…  the error was "The SMTP server does not recognize any of the authentication methods supported by Outlook."

Ok, ...so, what ARE "any of the authentication methods supported by Outlook?!"

In spite of much searching on the Internet, and at the Microsoft site, I could not find an answer. I could find the question and problem restated many times, but no answer.

So, I tested and proved that Outlook is saying that it is expecting:

  1. the SMTP server supports the "EHLO" (Extended Hello command, circa IETF RFC 1425) - this is a "given"...it's inconceivable in 2019 that an SMTP server would not.
  2. the SMTP server presents the keyword "AUTH" in the EHLO response.  AUTH indicates that the SMTP server supports Authentication of the email client to the SMTP server.  The most common (and current) authentication service is SASL...
    Simple Authentication and Security Layer (circa IETF RFC 4422)
  3. the specific Method of authentication supported by the SMTP Server/SASL is also supported by Outlook - typically, this would be a username + password.

I found that for myself, I simply needed to activate SASL support in the SMTP service settings on my home server, AND I had to activate a SASL service (to actually do the job of authenticating the username + password on behalf of my SMTP server) for Outlook to then allow me to "Add a New Account."

The above explains:

  • What Outlook is trying to tell you with the message (AUTH missing or using unsupported method)
  • How you or your email provider can likely remediate the issue (activate SASL with username/password support)

Technical NOTE about Outlook:

So, it turns out that AFTER finally succeeding in Adding a New Account to Outlook, when I edited the "Server Settings", I now had an additional option available under "Outgoing mail," which was "My outgoing (SMTP) server requires authentication."  This checkbox could be un-checked!  So, note that the entire requirement for SASL support is only required as a minimum bar/tax to initially add the account to Outlook, not to persistently use the account.  Aside: Microsoft...not sure if this is a coding error or intentional, but either way, this is bad customer service...please remove this needless burden (namely, forcing SASL support + authentication success before adding an outgoing email account).

2 ways to access your contacts.

 

1. If you want to do editing, go to the People (contacts) window......

Open outlook.com > top left next to the logo Outlook, there is a down arrow > click the arrow > click People > all your individual contacts are listed on the left.

 To add a new contact :

In the same People window, click New at the toolbar > fill in the info into the required boxes > click Save when done.

To Edit your contact list :

Click at the contact's name ( NOT its check box ) > toolbar at top, click Edit.

Note :

1. Edit option will not appear until you have selected a contact.

2. Edit option will not appear if you click at the contact's checkbox.

 

To delete a contact from the list :

Just click at the contact > toolbar at top, click Delete.

 

*** ( If you want to know how to manage \groups, please post back ).

 

 

2. If you want to select contacts for sending emails .........

Open outlook.com > click New , or Reply or right click at an email and click Forward.

Left side, click at the blue letters To > your complete contact list will appear.
If you see the "frequent contacts" list instead, click at the blue letters To again.

If you want to use Cc or Bcc options ......

left side, click Cc & Bcc > you'll see Cc and a "frequent contacts " box which covers the Bcc option > left click anywhere inside the window and you'll see Bcc > click at the blue letters Bcc and your contacts will appear > scroll to select your contacts.

john korchok say:

I get precisely the same display in Word 2011 in Page Layout view. However, I discovered that at the point where the display disappears from view, you switched from entering carriage returns at the end of each equation to using soft returns (Shift + Return). Replacing the soft returns with normal carriage returns makes everything visible again.

I recommend that you follow these steps:

  1. In Word, select Word from the menu, then Preferences.
  2. Click on View.
  3. In the Nonprinting characters section, check All. OK out.

Now you can see the normal carriage returns as backwards Ps and the soft returns as bent arrows. Replace all the soft returns with regular returns and it should display and print correctly.
