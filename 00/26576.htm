khd2016 say:

Are you looking at a straight up 6 months in days (365/2 = 182.5 days if you ignore leap years), or are you looking at something like "any date in March should get flagged if the other date is before the prior Oct or after the subsequent June"?

If the dates are more than 6 months apart, what do you want- just a flag, or the actual number of extra days?

Dates in Excel are shown to the user as a calendar date, but behind the scenes each day is just the number 1. So, you could use something like

=if (OR(A1+182<B1,A1-182>B1),"The value is outside of range","")

Or if you want it to show the number of days, you can replace the "the value is outside of range" with a formula to calculate the days... although in that case it might be easier to write the formula something like

=if(A1+182<B1, (A1+182)-B1,if(A1-182>B1,(A1-182)-B1,""))
