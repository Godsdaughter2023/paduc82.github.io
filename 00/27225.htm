When you install Office 2013, you have two options.

maor bracha say:

Hi Tenttu, happy to help!

The idea remains the same as described above and in the linked thread with the slight difference of having to prepare your users for migration:

  1. Since users were created manually, you will have to populate related attributes to allow the migration to run properly:
    • Enable Office 365 mail-users for all users\mailboxes.
    • Export a list of the following attributes from your providers environment: MsExchMailboxGuid (mailbux GUID) , ProxyAddresses (Email Addresses, including X500s), PrimarySmtpAddress (primary address) and Mailnickname (Alias).
    • Populate appropriate attributes to your mail user accounts.
  2. Passwords:
    • Since DirSync is not running, make sure your users are aware of their passwords.
  3. Migration batch:
    • If your provider is willing to assist in adding the @XXX.onmicrosoft.com addresses to the accounts, it is recommended to do so as described in the above linked thread. If not, you can use your primary domain as "Target Address" when creating a migration batch, just be aware that you have to migrate all mailboxes at once and treat as you would a cutover DNS wise.

Mailboxes will be migrated as user mailboxes, so you should also be aware and convert your shared mailboxes accordingly.

Hope this helps,
