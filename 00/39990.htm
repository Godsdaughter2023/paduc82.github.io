In the situation where Windows 10 says that you need to Uninstall Samsung Easy Settings, but won't let you do so.

In the list, I looked at services were using PID 1060 and then disabled them one at a time through services.msc

Disabling the Automatic Updates service immediately fixed the svchost using up all the cpu.

Rechecking the tasklist /svc shows that the wuauserv (Automatic Update service) was the only service missing from PID 1060 to fix the svchost problem.
