How to Fix "Something went wrong and we can't sign you in - Microsoft Community

cyberphonics say:

How to Fix "Something went wrong and we can't sign you in right now" Errors

I wanted to post this for others to save time digging and hopefully avoid the kind of unhelpful exchanges I had with support (e.g. "I cleared out the cache" "But did you try clearing out the cache?"). This error occurs for a variety of reasons, which means there are a number of solutions out there. It also means that some solutions you come across won't work for you.

I went through a boatload of troubleshooting that didn't help. I'll post other solutions here at a later date or other users can add some. For now, I'm only focusing on the solution that helped me because I didn't see it mentioned anywhere else. It may or may not help you. Feel free to skip to the bottom section titled "The Solution" to find out.

The Problem


When trying to access any Microsoft website that first navigates to https://login.live.com for sign in, that page refreshes itself rapidly a few times, then displays the error "Something went wrong and we can't sign you in right now. Please try again later."

My Details


  • It only occurs when using Internet Explorer. The page is fine in every other browser.
  • It started between the end of April and the beginning of May and it's been happening ever since.
  • I didn't install/uninstall/change anything on my computer or in Internet Explorer prior to the problem occurring. The only changes were whatever Windows Updates were automatically installed in that period. 

Conclusion: Something Microsoft recently changed, either in the updates or on the backend of their products, was causing a conflict with Internet Explorer or with something tied to it (e.g. an extension). 

Troubleshooting

Tip: Check if the problem might be a browser add-on before doing anything more involved or tedious like uninstalling IE, clean boots, etc.

Launch Internet Explorer without any add-ons enabled. You can do this quickly by typing the command iexplore -extoff into the "run" box. Bring up that box by hitting the Windows Key+R or click the Start Menu button and type it in the search field. If done right, Internet Explorer will launch and the page will show something like this:


Check if you still have the problem. If you do, it's probably not an add-on that's causing it. In my case, the problem went away, so I focused on the add-ons and narrowed it down.

Starting with all add-ons enabled, I disabled the first add-on in the list, then checked https://login.live.com. I received the same error. I repeated this step, disabling each add-on and checking, until I got to an add-on called TrueSuite Website Log On/AuthenTec TrueAPI. Unlike the other add-ons, the option to manually disable it was greyed out.

A quick web search informed me that add-on is tied to an HP program called SimplePass that often comes preinstalled on HP computers. My computer is an HP from 2011. I found HP SimplePass in the list of programs and features. It wasn't updated recently. I uninstalled it and went back to Internet Explorer. The TrueSuite add-on was gone from the list.

I checked https://login.live.com and it was fine! I enabled all add-ons to be sure and had no further issues, so I believe that add-on was the culprit. Since it's been there a long time without any changes, I'm leaning towards Microsoft changing something in the past month that the add-on couldn't play nice with anymore. 

The Solution


I uninstalled HP SimplePass, a security/biometrics program that comes preinstalled on certain Hewlitt Packard computers/laptops. It's tied to a browser add-on called TrueSuite Website Log On/AuthenTec TrueAPI or a version of that. There are add-ons for other browsers. I only had one in Internet Explorer.

Even if you don't have an HP computer, it's a good idea to try to rule out browser extensions. Especially if you're only seeing this behavior in specific browsers.
