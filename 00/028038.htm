betterdan say:

Hi all,

I wonder if anyone can help. I have been tasked with recreating a form that uses an '=IF(ISNA(VLOOKUP' function to pull data from one tab into another.

The company I work for has particular 'income codes' that are relevant to certain 'departments' - I need a relevant cell, to read from another and pull from a separate sheet the relevant information.

For example - If cell A1 has a drop down and '0001' is selected, and 0001 is associated with department 'A' then cell A2 will need to go to a separate tab, locate what department '0001' relates to and auto fills A2 with the relevant information.

On the excel sheet, I have a separate tab that has all the income codes and what department they are relevant to.

The original form used this formula =IF(ISNA(VLOOKUP(E11,DeptLookup,2,FALSE)),"",VLOOKUP(E11,DeptLookup,2,FALSE)) - I have tried to reverse engineer this as best I can correcting the cell and tab location/names, but can not get this to work.

If this makes sense to anyone, please help me.

bernard liengme say:

Let's concentration the VLOOKUP first.

In VLOOKUP(A1, Sheet2!$A$1:$B$100,2,FALSE) find where the row in Sheet2!A1:B100 where the same value as A1 is located and takes the data from the second (that is what the 2 means) column (that will be column B). The FALSE says find an exact match not just the nearest match. The range A1:B100 has been made absolute with $ symbols so that we can copy the formula down the column.

Now if you had named Sheet2!A1:B100 as DeptLookup you could use  VLOOKUP(A1, DeptLookup,2,FALSE)

If the llokup fails you want the cell to look blank - that is what the ISNA/IF is all about

But why not use =IFERROR(VLOOKUP(A1, Sheet2!$A$1:$B$100,2,FALSE), "")
