srimadhwa b say:

Hi Amy,

I realize that it must be difficult for you when things are not working as it should. Sorry to know that you are facing this issue.

Please answer these questions to get more clarity on this issue:

  • Did you make any changes to the pc prior to this issue?

  • Have you installed all pending updates on the pc?

A lot of Dell touchpads have an area at the top left of the pad which when double-tapped will toggle the touchpad On/Off.

Further, follow these steps to check if that helps:

  • In the Search box, type touchpad.

  • Touch or click Mouse & touchpad settings (System settings).

  • Touch or click the Touchpad toggle to turn off or on the Touchpad. 

Note:  When there is no Touchpad toggle look for Additional mouse options at the bottom of the page.

  • Touch or click Additional mouse options.

  • Touch or click the Dell Touchpad or Click Pad tab.

Touch or click the link, Click to change Dell Touchpad settings, there may be an option to enable or disable the Touchpad.
