doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

By using TC Fields, you can get more than 9 types of headings into a Table of Contents, but there is no way of increasing the number of levels in the Table of Contents, which is what I guess you would need to do to be able to have them coloured differently.

stefan blom say:

As Doug has said, there are ways to include, in a table of contents, paragraphs using other styles than the built-in headings, but you can't add more TOC levels. By default, any style that has an "Outline level" different from "Body Text" will be in the TOC, and you can add custom styles in the Table of Contents dialog box. TC fields are useful for text that isn't necessarily visible in the main body of the document but still should be in the table of contents.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

I will repeat here what I said in my email replies to your direct email question:

There are only 9 TOC styles, so it is not possible to have a TOC with more than nine levels.

You can certainly have the headings the way you want them in the document provided you abandon the idea of having different colors in the TOC. You can have a Blue Heading 1, Blue Heading 2, Blue Heading 3, etc., based on Heading 1/2/3, and a Green Heading 1/2/3 based on Heading 1/2/3. They will have outline levels of 1, 2, and 3, and a default TOC (which includes headings based on outline levels) will include them using TOC 1/2/3, which you can format to your taste (but without color). That’s as far as you can get with a TOC field that you can update automatically. If you want to make a manual TOC, then of course you can format it any way you like, but you’ll have to update the page numbers manually, too.
