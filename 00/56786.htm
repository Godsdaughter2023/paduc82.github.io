Hi ZeistIsBad,

External hard drives should be "plug and play" to Windows. Issues encountered from external devices are mostly hardware related and should be handled best by the service team of its manufacturers.

Here’s what you can do for the software side:

  • Make sure that your USB cable is plugged to the appropriate USB port (ex. USB 2.0 or USB 3.0).
  • Press X and click Device Manager. Expand Universal Serial Bus controllers. Right-click USB Root Hub > Properties > Change settings > Power Management > Uncheck Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power. Click OK to apply changes.
  • Run the Driver Verifier. Press Windows + S to open Search Box. Type verifier, then press Enter. When prompted by UAC. Click Yes to launch this utility. Visit this thread: for more information.
  • Reboot your computer. Go to your BIOS interface screen. Look for the Power Management > USB Wake Support and check the option Enable USB Wake Support.

Otherwise, you’ll need to contact your external hard disk drive manufacturer’s support team. Ask them directly or confirm if they have service warranty regarding undetected hardware devices such as this.
