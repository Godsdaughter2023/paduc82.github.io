stefan blom say:

Word cross-references make use of hidden bookmarks. In the situation you describe, one or more of those bookmarks have expanded to include additional content, which can happen if you add content, or press Enter, at the beginning of a bookmarked paragraph. To fix this, you can delete and recreate the affected cross-references.

To avoid the problem in the future, do not add content at the beginning of a cross-referenced paragraph. Instead, do so at the end of the preceding paragraph.
