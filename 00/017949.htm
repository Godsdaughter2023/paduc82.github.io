stjoemi say:

How do I get a Security Warning to stop popping up?

A couple of days ago a dialogue box started popping up almost every time I go to a new internet page.

Thanks.

amrita m say:

Hi,

 

To Disable or Turn Off ‘Do You Want to View Only the Webpage Content that Was Delivered Securely Security Warning’, you need enable ‘Display mixed content’ option in Internet Options.

 

Mixed content situation can happen on HTTPS secure websites such as online stores or financial sites that display images, banners, or scripts that are coming from a server that is not secured. IE has defaulted to prompt user for next course of action when encounter mixed content as displaying mixed content carries risk that non-secure web page or script will be able to access information from the secure content.

Having to click “No” (or “Yes” to block non-secure content) button for every visit to website with mixed content can be troublesome and annoying. Besides, its possible user accidentally clicks “Yes”, which prevents some insecure elements from loading and displaying. For user who confident that he or she does not visit possibly malicious site, it’s possible to instruct Internet Explorer to display mixed content automatically, instead of asking for input. 

 

Follow the steps below to enable ‘Display mixed content’ option:
a.    Launch Internet Explorer.
b.    Going to Tools->Internet Options->Security.
c.    Select the ‘Security’ tab.
d.    Click the ‘Custom Level’ button.
e.    In the ‘Miscellaneous’ section change “Display mixed content” to Enable

 

For detailed information, refer to the suggestions provided by Vinod Sundarraj on Wednesday, May 20, 2009 in the below link:
http://social.answers.microsoft.com/Forums/en-US/InternetExplorer/thread/3f5bc58a-30d2-4dd8-a395-c556d19973f6

 

Hope this information is helpful.

hippytesla say:

The definite fix for this for me was using Windows Media Player to sync music to my phone. Which is fine, since that is my preffered method for years now anyway (keeping a tidy tagged library, heheh).

Here's how:

  1. Add the folder in which you store your music on your PC to your WMP library folder list.
  2. From your WMP library, select the desired albums/artists/songs you want. You can add everything from an artist, individual albums or songs as you wish.
  3. Right-click your selection.
  4. Choose "Add to" and then "Sync list".
  5. On your sync list, be sure the desired device is selected for syncing (it's very easy to choose which device to sync to from here). You can also see how much space your music will take up on the phone/SD card even before you sync it (love this feature, BTW).
  6. When you have added everything you want, click "Start sync" at the upper-left corner of WMP, above the sync list.

That's all there is to it. No duplicates.

If you have any problems: try formatting your SD card from your phone and deleting everything directly from your phone's music app AND Windows Phone app on your PC (whatever OS and app version you have on your PC), just to be sure that no traces/junk is left. This helped me delete all of the songs Windows Phone Metro app showed I have on my phone even when I didn't.
