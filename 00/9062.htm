brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:
Colors are per-user. While others who share the calendar with you will see the category names, they cannot see the colors you assign. If they have your categories in their master category list, they'll see the color they have assigned to it, not the one you have assigned. Otherwise, the they'll see "category name (Not in Master Category List)" when they examine their categories. While you can export your category list (it's in the registry) as a .reg file and give that to others who can add it to their own registry, it's not ideal. See the section entitled "Keeping Categories Consistent" at http://www.slipstick.com/outlook/outlook-categories-and-color-categories/#consistent

michael bauer (vboffice.net) say:

Since Outlook 2007 categories are not stored in the registry anymore. Exporting them isn't as easy as it was for Outlook 2003.

For a few categories, and if the list doesn't change too often, I'd manually ensure that all users have the same master category list. If you want to sync it automatically, try Category-Manager.

Hi *,

I had the same issues.
Fixed that sucker.

Download the latest Surface Pro 3 dockingstation ( usb\vid_045e&pid_07c6&rev_3000 )
Ethernet driver with INTERNET EXPLORER from:
catalog.update.microsoft.com/v7/site/Search.aspx?q=Microsoft%20-%20LAN%20-%20Surface%20Ethernet%20Adapter
or go to catalog.update.microsoft.com/ and search for "Microsoft - LAN - Surface Ethernet Adapter".
Make sure that you use the driver for your platform (32bit or 64bit). The driver is downloaded to a DIRECTORY.
I used version 8.18.303.2015 for 64bit.

Remove your Surface Pro 3 from your docking station.
Open the Device manager (right click on the start button and select Device manager).
Go to View. Select "Show hidden devices". Select "Network adapters". Select "Surface Ethernet Adapter". Select uninstall. Reboot. Leave your Surface Pro 3 outside the docking station.
Locate your downloaded driver DIRECTORY. Open a command prompt (windows key+R. Type cmd and hit enter) and go to this directory (cd path-to-the-driver-directory).
Execute the following command:
expand the-name.cab -F:* x
cd x
pnputil -i -a msu30x64w8.inf
Shutdown your Surface Pro 3.
Dock it in your station. Turn it on. Done.
