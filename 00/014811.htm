If you are using PhotoShop to create transparent images, use the File>Save for Web command.

  1. Set the format to PNG-8
  2. Select the image color that should be transparent in the Color Table area
  3. Click on the small grey and white checkerboard icon below the Color Table area. The image preview should show the transparent background as a grey and white checkerboard.

Print to PDF creates problems at section breaks - Microsoft Community

sharonuga say:

Print to PDF creates problems at section breaks

I have a user trying to create a pdf of his thesis. He created the thesis on Microsoft Word 2011 for Mac. It has a number of section breaks where he switches from portrait to landscape and back. When he tries to print to PDF, not only does the thesis get split into an individual document at each section break (annoying, but not a big issue), but when he tries to reassemble the document there is an adjustment in margin and several extra lines of space at the top of each section. I've seen workarounds such as opening the individual sections in Preview and stitching them together that way, but it still leaves the extra space at the beginning of each section break, as well as the adjustment in margin. Has anyone found a workaround for this?

Thank you!

john mcghie say:

Guys:

Just to explain...  This is not really Microsoft's bug to fix.

A section break in Word looks almost exactly the same as a new job to a printer.  If your section break makes ANY change to the margins, or the content of the headers or footers, Apple's PDF engine will start a new print job.

As Phillip points out, the Adobe PDF writer will not do this, but unless you have paid a lot of money for Adobe Acrobat, you will not have that on your system.  Apple's PDF writer is a low version of PDF; Adobe wants a lot of money to give Apple the licence to write a higher version that does not have the bug.

The simpler way is to:

  1. Open the first of the PDF "pieces" in Apple's Preview.
  2. In the View menu, choose "Thumbnails"
  3. Drag the next PDF piece into the correct place in the thumbnail sidebar
  4. Drag the remaining pieces into place in the same way.
  5. When you have finished, SAVE.

You will now have a single PDF of your Word document.  You must use Preview for this, Adobe Reader won't do it.

Cheers
