barry xie msft say:

Hi Isobel,

 

Per checking the message headers, we found that the sending IP’s PTR record is on our good PTR list:

 

  168.***.**.*** | o168-***-**-***.outbound-mail.s***d.net

 

Our implicit intra-org and cross-domain antispoofing protection excludes sending IPs that are on our good PTR list because they belong to valid senders who are known to sometimes relay messages that spoof and fail authentication. Therefore, we suppress antispoofing enforcement for them.

 

If you want to block this and go strict for DMARC, you can use Exchange Transport Rule that looks for "dmarc=oreject" or "dmarc=quarantine" in the Authentication-Results header and sets the SCL to 9.

 

Also, if other users use the customer’s email address to send spoofing email, EOP will change the SCL value to 9 and the recipient will get the email in Junk folder as the IP address is different.

jay freedman say:

If you're using forms protection because there are fillable form fields in the protected sections, you can't use picture content controls. For that matter, you also can't just insert pictures in the table and use the "Change picture" command on the right-click menu. It doesn't matter that the pictures are in an unprotected section; it just doesn't work. Probably form fields won't work, and I'm not sure that content controls would pass correctly through the deconstruction/reconstruction sequence either. However, you don't need either of those kinds of things; the tables by themselves are sufficient.

The idea I want to get across is that you can apply any one of several kinds of protection, even without fields or content controls anywhere in the document. However, the "filling in fields" kind of protection will always interfere with the ability to replace pictures anywhere in the document. You have to use one of the other kinds of protection.

The one I'll recommend for this specific application is the "restrict editing - no changes method" , where you mark the areas that should be editable and grant permission (to "Everyone") to change those areas. You can use field coding to conditionally show/hide the watermark according to the file's name. Such a field might be coded as:

{IF{FILENAME \* Upper}= "DRAFT*" "watermark or its anchor goes here"}

Simply replace 'watermark or its anchor goes here' with the picture or its anchor - the double quotes are required.
