a. user say:

That's a good question.

In the first place, that setting is really for laptops because they are commonly used on battery power. And when you're on battery power, every last bit of electricity you can squeeze out of your battery matters. For desktops, the setting doesn't really matter. There's nothing wrong with conserving energy, of course, but a desktop is always connected to electricity, so it's not going to drop dead on you in the middle of a presentation.

There's no correct answer. You're making a trade-off: saving battery vs. faster resume. Up to you. Mine is at the default setting, whatever that is.

I don't think heat should be an important concern. If a laptop has adequate ventilation and it still runs hot when idle, that's either a sign of trouble or a sign that you need to open the back panel and clean out the shmutz.

As for why most of the information you're finding on the internet is old, here's my guess: Today's hardware has progressed to where minimum processor state isn't a consideration anymore.
