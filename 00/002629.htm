yangzheng_msft6300 say:

Hi Scott,

I understand that you want to keep your emails for 5 years. And you have found that litigation hold can achieve. So here are the answers to your questions:


1. Is doing so against the terms of service?
No, it isn't.

2. Is there an easy way to apply this to all users?
Yes. If you are the admin, you can use PowerShell to enable litigation hold for all the mailboxes. If you are not, please let your admin do it. Here are the detailed steps:
1) Connect to Exchange Online using PowerShell with an admin account.
2) Run the command below:
Get-Mailbox -ResultSize Unlimited -Filter {RecipientTypeDetails -eq "UserMailbox"} | Set-Mailbox -LitigationHoldEnabled $true -LitigationHoldDuration 1825

3. Can we have this applied as we're migrating a mailbox into Office 365? Is it retroactive?
No. After the mailbox is migrated to Office 365, you can enable litigation hold for this mailbox.

4. Will the litigation hold capture all emails, even those that are immediately deleted? I think this is yes, but I'm asking it anyway.
Yes, you are right. After enabling litigation hold, the emails that are immediately deleted will be captured.

5. I saw that litigation holds have unlimited drive space. Would that be true in this situation?
You understanding is correct. I would like to add that  in Exchange Online, the quota for the recoverable items folder is automatically increased to 100 GB when you place a mailbox on Litigation Hold or In-Place Hold.

If the recoverable items folder reaches or exceeds the 100GB quota, you can contact Office 365 support to request an increase of the Recoverable Items quota for a mailbox on hold.

Yang
