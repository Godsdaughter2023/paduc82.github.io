It sounds to me like your system of unspecified make and model might be overheating (for the shutting down part).

If you are performing video intensive operations like watching videos or playing games, etc. that put an extra load on your CPU and the video subsystem.  Mostly I think systems should be able to handle this, but if your system is not getting enough fresh air circulation because of a a dust accumulation in the cooling vents, a dust clogged heat sink(s), a defective cooling fan, etc.  If it is a laptop, laptops aren't really made to be run from your lap since that may block the the air circulation.

If your system runs fine as long as you are not doing these things and only overheats when performing video intensive things, your system is designed (hopefully) to power off so it can get a chance to cool off, then things will work okay again for a while until it overheats again.  There will usually be no warning, nothing will get logged in any log file...  it just shuts off so no components are permanently damaged.  That is not the same as shutting down - it just completely shuts off. 

Your system may not start up again right away until it sits for a bit and things cool off.   After it cools of a bit, it will start back up and then shut off again.

Depending on your system make and model, it may present you with an Alert! message to let you know that something happened that you need to look into.

You may need to open up your system and blow it out with a can of compressed air, wiggle all the connections that you can get to and make sure they are tight, reorient the system so that it can get some fresh air and breath, replace a defective or low capacity cooling fan and/or reapply thermal compound between your CPU and the heat sink that is probably clamped down to the top of it.  Sometimes there is also a heat sink on your video card/chip that may need the same treatment.
