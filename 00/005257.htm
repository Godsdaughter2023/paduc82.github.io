Start > Type Regedit > Press Enter

cliff5555 say:

Legal Pleading Template does not work

I have Windows 8.1, Office 365 Home, with MS Word 2013.  If I try to use the pleading paper template, the numbering and lines along the left side of the paper do not show in Print View, do not print, and do not convert to pdf.  How can I get the legal pleading template to actually work?  
suzanne s. barnhill say:

At File | Options | Advanced: Show document content, make sure that "Show drawings and text boxes on screen" is checked.

At File | Options | Display: Printing options, make sure that "Print drawings created in Word" is checked.

If the lines and numbering are (as is the case with most pleading templates) anchored to the page header, they will not be displayed if you have "white space between pages" hidden.

If the lines and numbering are created as a "page background," they won't be displayed unless you have "Show background colors and images" checked at File | Options | Advanced: Show document content and won't print unless you have "Print background colors and images" checked at File | Options | Display: Printing options.

    charles kenyon say:

    Following up.

    I would recommend changing the right-indent rather than the tab stop.

    Here is a macro that can be used to change the right-indent setting for all of the TOC styles.

    Sub TocStylesIndent()
        ' Change Right Indent setting in TOC styles to user input
        ' Charles Kenyon 2018-August-01
        '
        Dim i As Integer
        Dim MyIndent
        MyIndent = InputBox("What right Indent setting do you want? (inches)", "Change Right Indent in TOC Styles", _
            ".025")
        For i = 1 To 9
            ActiveDocument.Styles("TOC " & i).ParagraphFormat.RightIndent = InchesToPoints(MyIndent)
        Next i
    End Sub

    Installing Macros
