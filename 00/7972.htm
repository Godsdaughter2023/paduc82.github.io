moranyan say:

Hi Karen,

Thanks for your quick update.

I understand that you want to move a copy of the emails sent to the secondary email account to the primary email account’s inbox.

In my test, I set up 2 Office 365 accounts into my Outlook 2016 client, and found the Outlook 2016 client didn’t move a copy of the emails sent to the secondary email account to the inbox of the primary email account. Finally, I achieved it by setting up a client-side rule. Here are the detailed steps:
1. In Outlook 2016, click Home > Rules > Create Rule > Advanced Options.
2. At the Rules Wizard page, tick the box “sent to *** Email address is removed for privacy ***”, replace the *** Email address is removed for privacy *** with the email address of your secondary email account. Then click Next.
3. Tick the box “move a copy to the ‘xxx’ folder”, replace the ‘xxx’ with the inbox of the primary email account.
4. Click Next, and finally click Finish to save the rule.
Note: The rule above is a client-side rule. It only works when the Outlook 2016 client is running.
If both the primary and the secondary email accounts on your Outlook 2016 client are Office 365 accounts (both Office 365 accounts are mail-enabled), and the Outlook 2016 client is closed, you can set up an inbox rule for the secondary Office 365 account in the Outlook Web App to achieve it. Below are the detailed steps:
1. Sign in to Outlook Web App.
2. Click Options > Mail > Automatic processing > Inbox and sweep rules.
3. Click the “+” button, select It was sent or received > Sent to …, input the email address of the secondary email account.
4. Under Do all of the following, select Forward, redirect, or send > Forward the message to.., input the email address of the primary email account.
5. Click OK to save.

Please let me know if anything is unclear.
