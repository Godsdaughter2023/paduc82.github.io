This is a common issue especially when you are accessing Outlook from a place which is out of your Internet Service provider (ISP) coverage area.
Ideally the Internet Service provider (ISP) provides you the incoming and outgoing server with authentication to setup the e-mail account in Outlook 2007. As long as you are within the ISP coverage area you will be able to connect to their server directly.

 

If you are accessing Outlook to send receive e-mail from a different location, the ISP of the new location will not allow connecting to your current ISP servers. Most of the Internet Service Providers block the Outgoing e-mails, if the SMTP server (outgoing server) is 25.

 

When you are accessing e-mails from the webmail, you are directly accessing your mailbox from the server. For Outlook or any other e-mail client’s, the ISP’s will have different server allocated for incoming and outgoing servers.

 

You will need to contact your ISP and check if they have any alternate settings for SMTP server (Outgoing Server) so that it can bypass and connect directly to the server.

What exactly you mean by unsent emails?
