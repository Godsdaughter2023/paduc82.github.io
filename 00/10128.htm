john korchok say:

Documents viewed on OneDrive use Word Online to display. I did a quick test with a document formatted with Calibri and placed several shapes anchored in different ways, but they all displayed fairly close to the expected position.

It's possible that if you are using a font in your desktop file that isn't available in Word Online, it could reflow the text and your shapes could end up in a different position.

Every floating shape is anchored to a paragraph. You can see the anchors by choosing FILE>Options>Display and check Object Anchors. Shape positioning is most predictable if the anchor is attached to the same paragraph as the object you are emphasizing. You can ensure that a shape remains anchored to a particular paragraph if you drag the anchor to where you want it, then choose DRAWING TOOLS>FORMAT>Position>More Layout Options... On the Position tab, check Lock Anchor.
