com_microsoft_outlook_author_email_addresses == joe @ example.com || kMDItemAuthors == *Richard* || kMDItemAuthors == "Mary Worth"

thomas von fäke say:

Hi @sbeb,

Yes, the solution is a symbolic link to a version of "Windows Defender" installed on your system.

I had a very similar problem than you: after some "Windows Insider Program" updates, "Windows Defender Antivirus Service" didn't start automatically and continuously crash if manually started.

The reason of the problem: "services.msc" indicated that the "Path to executable" for "Windows Defender Antivirus Service" targeted "C:\ProgramData\Microsoft\Windows Defender\Platform\4.14.17639.18041-0", whereas the "Platform" folder only contained folders "4.16.17656.18052-0" (May 30) and "4.18.1806.18062-0" (June 27).

Solution: I create a symbolic link from "4.14.17639.18041-0" needed by "Windows Defender Antivirus Service" to the most recent installed on the computer "4.18.1806.18062-0". Then I successfully manually started "Windows Defender Antivirus Service" and was able to apply every updates.

Steps:

  • check needed "Windows Defender Antivirus Service" version in services.msc (line "Path to executable")
  • go to "C:\ProgramData\Microsoft\Windows Defender\Platform\" to verify the installed version(s)
  • use "Command Prompt" as Administrator
  • execute the following command (YOU MUST ADAPT TO YOUR VERSIONS)

How do I recover lost Entourage folders? - Microsoft Community

richard douglas carpenter say:

How do I recover lost Entourage folders?

I have MS Office 2008 Entourage running on my iMac under OS X 10.7.4 Lion. 

In order to solve a printing problem I repaired my disk permissions using Disk Utility.  This solved one problem but created another, as when I reopened Entourage I had lost all my mailboxes and mail accounts!

I managed to recreate my mail accounts and restore my inbox from the server mailboxes, but the "Sent Mail" folders are empty (as I send all my messages from the Entourage client).

I also had a number of folders for storing incoming mail by category etc. but these are gone as well (although i have got the messages in my inbox)

Is there any way I can recover the Sent Mail and other folders in Entourage?

Many thanks
dianeoforegon say: Under Entourage in the Menu bar, select "Switch Identity". Do you see other Identities listed. If yes, try switching to see if you are in the wrong Identity.

As Anax explained any mail that's on an Exchange server can be downloaded again into Entourage. Because of these odd issues you might want to start over in a new Identity.

1)  Make a copy of your Identity in the Finder first.

2)  Delete your IMAP and Exchange accounts. This deletes the cache files in Entourage. Your data is still on the server. You are left with only data that is ”On My Computer”. We are working on your Copy so your data is safe in the original Identity.

Tip: Take screen shots of your accounts especially the advanced settings so you can easily enter your accounts into your new Identity.

3)  Under File > Export select the default action to export all items as .rge file. In the export process, do not select the option to delete.

4) Switch to new Identity. Import the .rge file. All data but messages will be imported into the local folders. You’ll need to select the messages in the default folders (Inbox, Drafts, Sent, Deleted Items) and move to the local folders. You can drag any custom folder to it’s correct location. You can delete the imported folder structure after you move all messages and folders.

5) Add your accounts to Entourage.

POP:

The new Entourage Identity will not know you have downloaded all the old messages and will download all messages left on the server again. Before you enter your POP account in Entourage, log into your account with your browser. Move all the older mail out of the Inbox.Entourage  only downloads from the Inbox. Leave only those new messages you have not downloaded.

IMAP and EXCHANGE:

Before adding your IMAP and/or Exchange accounts, you might want to log into your account with your browser and clean up your Inbox. It’s best to keep your Inbox as clean as possible. Archive older messages to a subfolder at the root level. Do not make subfolders under the Inbox for Exchange accounts. Also not advised for IMAP.

Any folder with more than 2GB of data is going to be difficult to rebuild and can cause database rebuild failure. I find that between 6,000-10,000 message will hit the 2GB limit. Sorting by year is usually helpful to reduce large folders. The folders by year will never change. You can drag these folders to the Desktop as .mbox files and you’ll never loose that data. Do the same for your Sent folder. I rarely see other folders that’s over the 2GB limit unless it’s an archive folder.
