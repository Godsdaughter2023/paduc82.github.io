suzanne s. barnhill say:

As Jay suggests, for Word versions where the style separator is available (the original version of the referenced version was written before the style separator was introduced), it is the best approach. Simple instructions:

  1. Type your heading in a style that has an outline level other than Body Text (such as one of Word's built-in heading styles).
  2. Press Enter to start a new paragraph.
  3. Format that paragraph as Normal (this will be the default when you press Enter at the end of a paragraph in one of Word's built-in heading styles) or any other style with an outline level of Body Text.
  4. Click in the first paragraph and press Alt+Ctrl+Enter to insert a style separator at the end of the paragraph.
  5. The two paragraphs will appear to join, but only the heading text will appear in the TOC.
