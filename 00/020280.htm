Hotmail has a feature that prevents losing email messages if you accidentally set up POP3 to delete them after reading them on the server. Here’s how it works:

j drury say:

For those experiencing this issue, I believe I've found a workaround:

  • Open up Internet Explorer, go to Tools->Internet Options->Security
  • Click Trusted Sites, and add the name of the SharePoint site the notebooks are stored on, e.g. https://sharepoint.mycompany.com
  • Change “Security Level for this zone” to Low
  • Restart OneNote

This has resolved the issue for us.  Obviously you should only trust sites that are safe, and there's clearly a setting somewhere in IE Security to change to make this work without having to set the whole Zone to low, but that is to figure out another day!  In the meantime, best of luck.

How can I transfer data from one page to another in Excel 2010?

joeu2004 say: Karen wrote:
I have 3 pages in an Excel spreadsheet.   I would like to transfer a total from one sheet to another sheet.

Depends on what you mean by "transfer".

If you want to reference A1 in Sheet1 in a formula in Sheet2, for example, you can write Sheet1!A1 in the cell Sheet2.  for example:

=Sheet1!A1

If, instead, you want to literally transfer the total found in A1 in Sheet1 into a cell in Sheet2, first copy A1 in Sheet1, then right-click on the cell in Sheet2 and click on "paste value", the icon labeled "123" under Paste Options.

In either case, you might also need to set the cell format so that the value will display the way you want it to.  Right-click on the cell and click on Format Cells, then the Number tab.

Set-ExecutionPolicy RemoteSigned

 

Connect to Exchange Online PoweShell:

$UserCredential = Get-Credential

$Session = New-PSSession -ConfigurationName Microsoft.Exchange -ConnectionUri https://outlook.office365.com/powershell-liveid/ -Credential

Check whther ADAL is off:

Get-OrganizationConfig | fl *Oauth*

Set-OrganizationConfig -OAuth2ClientProfileEnabled:$true

 

Finish the session:

Remove-PSSession $Session
