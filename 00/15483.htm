bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

Accomplishing what you originally described should not present a problem. It sounds like the result you're getting is due to some incomplete guidance or the order in which you're attempting to execute it... And no offense to Sheen V. Ali, but all that business about splitting the table, content not fitting, etc. is nonsense. Also, equating a Word Table with a graphic object just isn't valid.

The conflict arises from the fact that you're attempting to squeeze A4 Landscape (297 mm) content into A4 Portrait (210 mm) space - An A4 page in Word is no smaller than an A4 page in Excel or any other type of document. Depending on the amount of excess width you may be able to make it fit by using a smaller font size, reducing margins, and/or making the columns narrower.

However, the approach I'd take is to change the Orientation of the table pages to Landscape rather than Portrait in order to have those page(s) accept the content without having to make any change to the structure of the table. See if this procedure works out better for you - I'm assuming the entire document is A4:

  1. At the spot in your Word document where you want to insert the table, go to Insert> Break Section Break (Next Page). If other content is to follow the table but you want to keep it in Portrait orientation, create another Section Break (Next Page)
  2. Make sure that the insertion point is in Section 2, then
    1. Go to Format> Document
      • Confirm that your margins are narrow enough to accommodate the width of the table
      • Confirm that the Apply to dropdown is set for This Section
    2. Click the Page Setup button
      • Click the Landscape orientation icon
      • Click OK in both dialog windows
  3. Now copy/paste your table of Excel data as you did before. The Section (Section 2) will expand vertically to as many pages as necessary based on the length of the table.

[NOTE: When working with a sectioned document it's a good idea to have the non-printing characters (¶) visible as well as having Section information displayed on the Status Bar.]
