Should I cover the One when not in use? - Microsoft Community

vengeful wyvern say:

Should I cover the One when not in use?

Just recently got an Xbox One and was curious as to whether or not it should be covered when off to prevent dust from getting in. It's in a well ventilated spot but I wanted to know for sure if it was needed or at least harmful/helpful if I do.

xi xbox one ix say:

Hello Vengeful Wyvern. The top of the Xbox One does collect a fair bit of dust over time, just like every item in your house would (you can see that if you look at the left part on the top of the console, the black cover next to the vent cover, there usually is a thick layer of dust after a week or so for me) so I'm sure that dust can also fall into the console through the vent cover.

But the vent cover is made in such a way that it almost holds back every single bit of dust that tries to get into your console from the outside. Also I'm pretty sure that any dust that enters your console through the vent cover will exit through the vent cover again once you turn on your console and the fans start spinning.

So it's not needed to cover your Xbox One when you are not using it. Can you do it? Yes, if you want to. It is in no way harmful. But remember, if you are going to cover your console ONLY do it when the console is completely turned off. You don't want to block the ventilation of your console when the console is powered on or still hot after use.

Furthermore I recommend cleaning the outside of your console every once in a while to remove the dust that is on it. Use a soft dry paper towel or something like that, you don't want to be messing around with water near your console.
