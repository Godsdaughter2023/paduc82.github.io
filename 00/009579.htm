john mcghie say:

It's reverting to the Document Default font.

Sadly, the document default font is not exposed to the user in Mac Word.

However, you can set it, by setting the font in the Normal style, then saving, closing, and re-opening the document.  You may also have to remove any directly-applied font from the document text by selecting the text and hitting Ctrl + Spacebar (or "Clear All Formatting" on the Ribbon).  When you use Clear All Formatting, you will be able to see the document default formatting (because that's what the text will acquire).

Note that the font you set is likely to be inherited by all the other styles in the document (which is what you want, right?).

Also, be aware that to function in Word, a font must be in TrueType format, or in OpenType but containing TrueType outlines.  And it must be correctly encoded in Unicode.  If either or both of those conditions are untrue, it won't work properly or reliably.

rohn007 say:
MS has split the world into several sales regions. The product key can only be activated inside the region.

The article suggests contacting MS Support. My experience with support on this problem is not encouraging. Front line support, the people you first talk to won't be able to help. They don't have the authority. Ask to be transferred to a supervisor. A couple of people have reported that they were able to find someone in second level support who was willing and able to give them a new key in exchange.

Or, you could contact the store you bought it from. Ask them to exchange the key for one for your location.

Or, you could sell/give the key to someone located in the original region and buy a new one in your current location.

I would not try these next tips if it is a one-time payment license for Office 2016. With a 365 subscription, you can let it expire and start a new one in your new location.

Or, you could try using a VPN located in the original region to do the activation.

Or, if you are lucky enough to live close enough to the border of the region to cross over to do the activation.
