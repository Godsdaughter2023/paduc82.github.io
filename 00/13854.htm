jerrymooney say:

If your still interested, I found someone who discovered the answer last July.  Go to "dbForums" and search on  `Access wizard' for the details.  In brief, he determined Access installed the ACCWIZ folder (where it stores the various wizards applets) in a different folder location than is the default one the MS Office Trust Center (File-Options-Trust Center) looks at (and allows programs, such as the missing wizards, to run from). 

In his (and my) case, the ACCWIZ installation folder was placed in "C:\Program Files(86)\Microsoft\Office\Office 15" folder, not the "C:\Program Files\..." one [pervious Office versions default].  The MS staff didn't update that information when they created separate 32- and 64-bit Win 7 OS `program file' locations.  I run the 64-bit version of Win7 OS.  So much for Quality Control!

As soon as I added the new location to the `Trusted Location' listing, all the Access Wizards worked.  Less than 5 minutes effort, not counting the > 20 hours spent searching the net and wasting time with MS Tech Support, which said I should call back and pay $50 for a `possible' answer. 

Hope this helps.

How did you configure your email account in Outlook (POP, IMAP, etc)?

 

Outlook is an email client used to send or receives emails based on the settings provided by the Email Service Provider (ESP) or the Internet Service Provider (ISP). Once the emails are sent from Outlook, Outlook doesn’t play any role regarding the delivery of emails.

 

Open Outlook and verify if the new email sent to <email>@live.com is stuck in the Outbox or in the Sent Items folder. If it gets stuck in the Outbox then Outlook will throw an error message based on which further steps can be suggested.
