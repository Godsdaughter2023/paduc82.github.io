**UPDATE** (Hopefully you read through this far)

My findings:

1) NVidia RAID on the Mobo is less then stellar...in fact, I ended up buying SYBA SATA II NCQ Host Controller cards....WHAT a difference.  Found that early NVidia chipsets claimed to have RAID, but in newer OS installs (like Win7,Vista, XP) it did not support well.

 

2) Updated Card (Syba card listed in #1):  Turn OFF the NVidia on the mobo. Enter the Syba bios settings and configure the RAID-1 Pair.   A 1TB pair will take some time to mirror, but do this as it is worth your time in the end.

 

3) Windows 7 will see the Syba RAID-1 Pair as one drive -BUT you need to have the downloaded drivers from SYBA for Windows 7 (remember - I am using 64 bit also, and this is CRITICAL!).  Load driver on install, and BOOM!  You see the drive pair as a SINGLE drive. 

NOTE:  For those of us from the old school, this should be no surprise.  IF you do a HARDWARE  RAID setting, the HARDWARE does *not* report to Windows that there are 2 drives, but will only be seen as one drive to the OS.  This is because it is at a deeper instruction level then the software OS or Software mirroring that Windows does...(it is also faster than software mirroring!).

+ Advantages for HW mirroring: Faster, deeper instruction level for better buffering control and sync controls. Agnostic management of drive pair and recovery (not reliant on OS recovery disks).

 

+ Advantages for SW (OS) mirroring: controlled by OS (Win7) and dynamic allocation can be done. Native (Win7) management of drive mirroring and allocation.

4) Speed:  Since the Syba card was made controller, the OS installed very quickly and with no issue.

 

5) Testing;  literally, I pulled a drive cable off of one of the drives, and my Windows kept on running!. IMPORTANT: when you do this, the MASTER of the pair is the drive number that is left running!! On the BIOS, (after a reboot) make sure the working drive is the one to mirror TO, NOT from!!  I also recommend a remirror in the BIOS, not the passive/background option (if available). At 1 TB, it takes a long time, but will not complete under a background task as things keep changing too frequently to record AND mirror at a respectible pace.  It's old school, but it works, and it tried and true!

Hopefully this will help you in your selection and Mirroring management.
