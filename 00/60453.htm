redrailgun say: I already fix the problem.

I follow these steps on YouTube and download the appropriate troubleshooter for Windows Store.

(For having error code 0x80073cf9)
• Go to C:\Windows, delete the AUInstallAgent, then re-create the folder again, cut the folder and move to the root folder C:\, go to C:\Windows again, create again the folder AUInstallAgent.

---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

• Go to Control Panel, on System and Security category, click Find and fix problems and then click Fix problems with Windows Update.

• Open Command Prompt (Admin) and type sfc /scannow* to scan for errors.

• Run System Configuration by typing msconfig on the Search charm or Run.
On the Services tab, tap or click Hide all Microsoft services check box, and then tap or click Disable all.
On the Startup tab, tap or click Open Task Manager, click each startup item and Disable (including your anti-virus), and close the Task Manager.
Close System Configuration, and DON'T RESTART!

• Simply run the troubleshooter.**

• Follow these final steps:
- You must have to login your Windows 8 PC by your Microsoft Account.
- Make sure the region and time are correct.
- Open the Windows Store and tap or click the Settings charm on the Charms bar and tap or click App updates.
- On the App updates screen, tap or click Sync licenses.
- Make sure that your PC is a TRUSTED PC, which you can view on the PC settings.

• RESTART your PC. (DON'T RESTART until you DIDN'T FINISH THESE STEPS)


* If the Scan File Checker detects a missing or corrupted file, you can fix this by typing DISM.exe /Online /Cleanup-image /Scanhealth, DISM.exe /Online /Cleanup-image /Restorehealth. For more information about fixing corrupted files, you can follow my thread: http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/windows8_1-windows_store/i-cant-download-apps-from-windows-store-because/7f795949-27bc-4ffc-adb8-ef0bca95ca48. Restart your PC if necessary. Just restart from the beginning of the process just to make sure it will work.


It worked for me. I hope it will work on other PCs.
