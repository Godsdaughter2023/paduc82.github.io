vijay a. verma say:
The largest positive number which Excel can show is 9.99999999999999E+307. This limit gets exceeded on number 171 for factorial. Hence, largest factorial which you get is for 170.
=FACT(170) = 7.257415615308E+306

FACT(171) will exceed 9.99999999999999E+307 and will give #NUM error. Hence, you can not use any number greater than 170. Hence, you can not use 200 and 195 while calculating factorial.

amit tandon say:

Refer below image:

Formula in cell G2 - using SUMIFS:

=SUMIFS($E$2:$E$100,$B$2:$B$100,$A$5,$C$2:$C$100,YEAR(TODAY()),$D$2:$D$100,"<"&MONTH(TODAY()))+SUMIFS($E$2:$E$100,$B$2:$B$100,$A$5,$C$2:$C$100,YEAR(TODAY())-1,$D$2:$D$100,">"&MONTH(TODAY())-1)

This separately adds 2 SUMIFS - (i) for year 2016 & month > 1 (today's month less 1 ie. 2-1=1); and (ii) year 2017 and month < 2 (Today's month of Feb ie. 2);

Alternatively, array formula (CTRL+SHIFT+ENTER) in cell G4:

=SUM(IFERROR((DATE($C$2:$C$100,$D$2:$D$100,1)>=(EOMONTH(TODAY(),-13)+1))*(DATE($C$2:$C$100,$D$2:$D$100,1)<=(EOMONTH(TODAY(),-1)))*($B$2:$B$100=$A$5)*($E$2:$E$100),0))

This converts the Year & Month in columns C & D to date (1st of each month) and then for dates equal to and after Feb 1 2016 (EOMONTH(TODAY(),-13)+1) and equal or less than Jan 31 2017 (EOMONTH(TODAY(),-1)) it SUMS up the sales.

If I've understood correctly you want the sum of sales for the previous 12 months not including the current month/year, so for example if your given month/year is 2/2017 (Feb 2017) you want the sum from 2/2016 to 1/2017?

I'll stand corrected, but with your months in a separate column I don't think you can achieve this with SUMIFS because you need to build up a date that Excel can recognise (eg using the DATE function) but SUMIFS won't recognise this as criteria when it is integral in the formula.
