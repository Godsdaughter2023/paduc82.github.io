You may pin an Office folder to the taskbar on Windows 10 and create Office 2013 application icons in this folder. You may add Office 2013 application icons from the following location:

Note: If you are on a 64 Bit computer (64 Bit Windows 8.1) then you will find the Office15 folder in the following locations.

Once attached to the taskbar you can click on the folder icon on the taskbar to access the Office folder and then access the individual applications.

When you click on the folder icon on the taskbar it will show you all folders pinned to the taskbar and you would have to click on the Office folder to access the Office application icons.

 

Creating Office application shortcuts directly on the taskbar and accessing it to open Office applications would be a simpler and direct option.

shanellem. say:

I had a user with this same problem (Office 2010). Her boss shared her calendar to her but it would say no connection and she couldn't view or add anything to the calendar. I realized the issue was on the boss' end. I went to the boss' computer, clicked on calendar permissions to open calendar properties. I Clicked on the assistants name and removed her, click ok. I Clicked on calendar permissions again and added the assistant back in again, gave editor permissions and voila. That did it.  The no connection error went away and she was then able to view what was on the boss' calendar.  Hope this works for you. FYI - This problem happened after they got new pc's.

So you are basically going to remove the assistant, then add him/her back in again on the boss' calendar.
