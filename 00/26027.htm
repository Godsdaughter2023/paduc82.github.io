Problem with outlook not recognising a .pst file as a personal folder when I try and import it from a .pst 
brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:
When Outlook declares a PST as not being a PST, that signifies your PST has been damaged (usually when transferring it from one PC to another) in such a way that Outlook cannot recognize it.  When that happens, neither import nor Open nor scanpst.exe will be able to recognize it.  If is contains things you can't afford to lose, you should be backing it up very regularly so an accident like this doesn't hurt.  Absent backups, you'll need to purchase a commercial PST repair tool.  There are several vendors.  Start here: Repair a damaged Personal Folders PST file - Tools.

To verify you own this account, kindly fill out the "Recover your Microsoft account" form. This means that the system will only grant access to the person who will be able to pass the verification process.

Even if your original information has changed, be sure to use the information you had associated with the account originally. 

 

Kindly consider below tips when answering the said form. This has helped my previous customers:

 

  • Fill out the form from a computer and from a location (i.e. home Wi-Fi or work network) where the previous successful sign-in occurred.
  • Make sure that you use an alternate email address that you have access with in order to receive the security code and not the email address of the account you want to recover.
  • The 4 basic information (First Name, Last Name, Birthdate and Current/Old Passwords are the best and easiest way to prove your Online Identity)

 

Please be reminded that you just need to fill out information that you are just 100% sure and in the best of your knowledge. You don't need to fill out everything but make sure that you have provided enough for you to pass this validation. The 4 basic information is more than enough to prove your identity - as long as it matches the system's record.
