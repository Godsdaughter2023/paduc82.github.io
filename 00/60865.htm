a. user say:

In Vista, Administrators don't have all the access rights as they did in XP.  MANY folders are denied access.  There are junction point folders where everyone is denied access.  All of this is intentional to decrease the chances someone will change or move or delete something they shouldn't (even Administrators).  Some of these can be changed to gain access and others require special changes to gain access - neither of which should be done without a VERY good reason (like being told to do so by Microsoft Support).  What you've missed is that permissions have changed a great deal in Vista from XP.

Here's some information about permisions and ownership in Vista that may help:

To see your permissions, right click the folder/file, click on properties, and check out the security tab.  Check to see what permissions you have by clicking on your username (or user group).  Here are the types of permissions you can have: http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/windows-vista/What-are-permissions.  You need to be an administrator or the owner to change permissions (and sometimes being an administrator or even an owner isn't enough - there are ways to block even them from access (though a smart administrator knows these ways and can get around them - but usually shouldn't because they were denied access usually for a very good reason).  Here's how to change folder permissions in Vista: http://www.online-tech-tips.com/windows-vista/set-file-folder-permissions-vista/.  To add take and grant full right permissions and ownership to the right click menu (which will make it faster to get in once it is set up), check out the following article: http://www.mydigitallife.info/2009/05/21/take-and-grant-full-control-permissions-and-ownership-in-windows-7-or-vista-right-click-menu/.

To fix this problem with the folders, take Owner tab.  Click edit and then click the name of the person you want to give ownership to (you may need to add it if it isn't there - or it may be yourself). If you want it to apply to sub-folders and files within that folder/drive, then check the box for replace owner on sub-containers and objects and click OK.  Back out and now there's a new owner for the folders/drive and files who can change permissions as needed.  Here's more information on taking ownership of a file or folder: http://www.vistax64.com/tutorials/67717-take-ownership-file.html.  To add take ownership to the right click menu (which will make it faster to get in once it is set up), check out the following article: http://www.howtogeek.com/howto/windows-vista/add-take-ownership-to-explorer-right-click-menu-in-vista/.

Many of the general system groups are included by inheritance instead of being listed in every file and folder - that's why they aren't always there -but they are.  If you check advanced, you'll see inherited permissions from other folders.

I have no idea about the arrow on the guest account - I don't know what it means.  It may have something to do with any security or AV programs you have installed (I know Norton does something similar with files to show if they've been backed up).  Personally I don't use guest accounts and recommend against them because they are too easy to use to hack into the sytem (and other reasons).  I'd keep the guest account deactivated and delete any duplicates but the one deactivated one (in Start / Control Panel / User Accounts / Manage other users).

I'm not sure why you can't see the other computers on your LAN - that sounds like a network connection or setup issue that just redoing the connection from scratch will fix (and then you can delete the one that isn't working right).  I don't know why your printer rigths changed - but you can easily set them back to how they should be with the security tab in printer properties (and the other settings in Start / Control Panel / Printers).

I hope this helps.

Good luck!
