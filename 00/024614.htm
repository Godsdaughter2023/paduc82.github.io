What you have there is a case where you need to reformat your device to clear it out. Otherwise, even if you remap all your stuff, your going to most likely have error messages due to not enough memory. Do a soft reset first - *#7780# and then check it. If that doesn't clear it out, you will have to perform a hard reset *#7370#. You will just need to make a backup through PC suite. You don't have to reformat but, it makes it faster to clear everything out in one shot. 

 
Once that's done, now your C drive will show all available capacity. Then, go into messages/ options/ settings/ other and remap it to your memory stick.

Some say not to store on the Mass Memory as it doesn't back up from there through PC suite, however, using the memory stick does so, just remap your stuff to the memory stick (if you have one) If not, you can map to mass storage or, you can leave it set to C drive but, just be sure to delete them after reading. Over time, they take up space. I just delete all mine after reading.

There's also a movie on there someplace that can be moved or deleted as well. It is the default demo movie. Blow that out. I would also remove any app that is removable off the C drive to free up more space. Anything your not using or plan on using- delete.

NOTE: some apps have no uninstallers so, you can not remove them. FYI

There's a huge thread on this around here that we added information to for how to do it all step by step. I would look for that as I can't recall all the directories for everything at this time.

When I reformatted mine- it was 50MB free. Email clients take up space as they default to the C drive and, there's no way to remap some clients. At least, we haven't been able to do so. But, all your TEXT, SMS, MMS can be remapped to save on another drive of your choice as well as ridding of the movie and uneeded apps.

Good luck. Oh, one more thing: do not forget to delete all your browser cache as that is what fills up the C volume the fastest.

bartontony say:

Drawbacks and Benefits in using chched mode of Outlook 2007

We are a large corporate Organisation and have just completed the full deployment of MS Office 2007 to approximately 3,000 Users.  We are now considering pushing-out (via the Network), Cached Mode of Outlook 2007 and am trying to pre-empt a few questions Users may raise with our IT Service Desk.

diane poremsky mvp (slipstick.com) say:

See http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/outlook-help/about-cached-exchange-mode-HP001000067.aspx 

Are you familiar with IMAP accounts? Cached mode is like this, in that it keeps a copy locally and mail remains on the server. If the desktop disk dies, they'll resync the mailbox from the server.

The biggest benefit (IMHO) is  when the network is down or the user unhooks a laptop,*all* mailbox data is available to the user. "can users access my mails?" - mailbox owners can access mail which was previously downloaded to their outlook. They can't send new mail (but can compose it  and it gets sent when they connect to the server) or access newly delivered mail until they reconnect. There is no time limit for disconnection from the network and there is no limit to the amt of mail your outbox can hold while it waits to reconnect.

Shared mailboxes can be cached - i think in 2007 you'll only get shared calendars - the dialog in the Account's More Settings will say for sure. 

The biggest problem sites encounter with cache mode is how long it takes for large mailboxes to sync.
