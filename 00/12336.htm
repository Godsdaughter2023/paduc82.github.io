Tasks which should recur "on the x day of the next month" (being this the chosen rule) really recur "on the same day as the day of current due date of the next month".

A numeric example is as follows :

1. Set start date and due date for any date, let's say July 13th.

2. Set recurrence as monthly for the 13 day of every month.

3. Obviously, next recurrence should be on August 13th., once the task is marked complete.

4. Imagine that the task could not be completed on the 13th and it's new due date will be on July 15th.

5. Modify the due date accordingly. Check the recurrence rule. It will still indicate that recurrence is set for the 13 day of every month

6. On the 15th. (or earlier if you want) mark the task complete and it will recur.

7. On which date would you expect it to reappear as due ?

8. Yes, you would expect it to reappear on August 13th. but it will reappear on August 15th.
