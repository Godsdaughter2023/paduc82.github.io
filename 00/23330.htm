davidf2 say:

Generally trying to copy and paste images directly from the internet is a security issue and you should change your workflow. A better and more secure work flow is to right click the picture > Save Image As and you will get the original image format of a PNG, GIF or perhaps a JPG. which are going to be better options than bitmaps. Save the image(s) to a folder on your hard drive and then insert > picture (browse to where you saved it) instead of trying to paste the picture onto a Pub page. You will end up with a better quality image and you will have a copy of the original image if you need to go back and redo the page. Yeah, it takes a few more seconds but it is a much safer workflow...especially vs the convoluted 'paste into Word, copy and repaste into Publisher' approach.

Alternatively go to Home tab > Clipboard and click downward arrow. Now when you try to copy a picture it will show up on the clipboard if indeed you managed to copy it. You can copy up to 24 items in the clipboard at one time. Then paste from the clipboard. Again trying to paste a picture copied from the internet is both a quality and a security issue and I would discourage the shortcut.
