ronrosenfeld say: You can use "helper columns" to split out the number and letters, then sort on the helper columns.

e.g. with your data in Column B  (using A1 notation):

C1:   =LOOKUP(1E+307,--MID(B1,1,ROW(INDIRECT("1:10"))))
D1:   =MID(B1,LOOKUP(2,1/ISNUMBER(-MID(B1,ROW(INDIRECT("1:10")),1)),ROW(INDIRECT("1:10")))+1,10)


And fill down as far as necessary.

Note the the number "10" in the above formulas must be any value that is at least as large as the length of the longest string in your data set.

Then sort on Column C for the first level; Column D for the second level.

roady [mvp] say:

If you kept your Calendar and Contacts in the mailbox store of your IMAP account and did not export it prior to removing the IMAP account, then the data is lost.


The process of adding a pst-file doesn't do anything other than just that. It is a requirement when you remove the last data file (in this case your IMAP mailbox) from your mail profile. So there is no "automatic export" here for all folders which are marked with "This computer only".


To prevent such a loss in the future see: Don’t risk losing your Contacts and Calendar when using IMAP in Outlook 2013
