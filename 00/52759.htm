pa bear - ms mvp say:

Monthly rollups for Win8.1 and Win7 beginning October 2016

<QP>

Monthly Rollups

From October 2016 onwards, Windows will release a single Monthly Rollup that addresses both security issues and reliability issues in a single update. The Monthly Rollup will be published to Windows Update (WU), WSUS, SCCM, and the Microsoft Update Catalog. Each month’s rollup will supersede the previous month’s rollup, so there will always be only one update required for your Windows PCs to get current. i.e. a Monthly Rollup in October 2016 will include all updates for October, while November 2016 will include October and November updates, and so on. Devices that have this rollup installed from Windows Update or WSUS will utilize express packages, keeping the monthly download size small.

Over time, Windows will also proactively add patches to the Monthly Rollup that have been released in the past. Our goal is eventually to include all of the patches we have shipped in the past since the last baseline, so that the Monthly Rollup becomes fully cumulative and you need only to install the latest single rollup to be up to date...

Security-only updates

Also from October 2016 onwards, Windows will release a single Security-only update. This update collects all of the security patches for that month into a single update. Unlike the Monthly Rollup, the Security-only update will only include new security patches that are released for that month. Individual patches will no longer be available. The Security-only update will be available to download and deploy from WSUS, SCCM, and the Microsoft Update Catalog [but will NOT be offered via Windows Update]...

</QP>

canadian tech say:

  1. Your mouse uses a laser to detect minute differences in the surface it is moving on.  You need to use some sort of surface that provides that.  Glass for example is bad surface.  Best is a fabric that has some sort of weave to it
  2. Your mouse could be failing or wearing out
  3. You are using a wireless mouse that is having signal problems between the mouse and its receiver
  4. You are holding or moving the mouse sub-optimally.  I will provide here a bit of a tutorial on mouse use....

If you find that sometimes your hand or wrist becomes tired or sore, the way you use your mouse may be the cause.  The following are some tips that can help you. 

 

1.      Rest your hand on your mouse and relax.  Don’t grip or squeeze it.  Use your entire arm to move it.  Don’t bend your wrist.  Don’t bend your fingers to press the buttons. If you are using a track-ball, use your finger tips to move it.

2.      From time to time, try using the keyboard instead.  There are keyboard “equivalents” for most things you do with a mouse.  An example is Ctrl+B for making characters bold on some word processors.  You can also use the Tab key to move the pointer around in a window in many applications.

3.      If your mouse seems to be moving in a jerky, uneven movement, it may be dirty inside. Try taking the ball out and cleaning it on some lint-free fabric and blow into the cavity.  If you don’t know how to do this, ask around.   Mouse “cleaning kits” are commercially available that do a nice job of cleaning a mouse.  Better yet, if you have a mouse with a ball inside, consider buying one that uses a laser light instead.  They are not expensive and far more reliable.

4.      To minimize hand effort, you should optimize the ratio of the movement of your mouse to the movement of the pointer on the screen.  Some mouse drivers have “acceleration” adjustment.  Acceleration causes the ratio to be higher when you move the mouse quickly, which also reduces hand effort.  Experiment with different settings until you find the combination that feels best for you.  Remember to change the settings periodically.  Your mouse skills will change with time and the work you do.

5.      The mouse should be at the same height as your keyboard and beside it on the right or left side.  Use a mouse pad that is made for that purpose.  If you are left-handed, use the mouse with your left hand.  If you are right-handed, use the mouse with your right hand. 

6.      Switch your computer’s software to left or right-handed operation so that the first or primary button is under your index finger.

Ø  Change left or right-handed operation by doing the following: Click on Start, Control Panel, Printers and Other hardware, Mouse.  You should find the way to switch in this window.  The precise steps will vary depending on software versions. To increase speed, click on Pointer options.  You can also change the double click speed and the size and colour of your mouse pointer in this dialogue window too.

 

If you are still having difficulty with your mouse, ask for help.
