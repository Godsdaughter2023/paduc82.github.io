talking moose say:
Your recipient needs a certificate for you to be able to encrypt messages. He will provide you a copy of his certificate that you can add to his contact record in Outlook. Using that you will be able to encrypt messages to him.

Certificates with email can serve three purposes:
  1. They can act as a "certificate of authenticity" to prove to the recipient that you are indeed the person who sent that message.
  2. They can protect the content of the message by encrypting it.
  3. They ensure the message was not changed during transit by verifying the integrity (signature) of the message.
Free certificates are available for personal use but since you stipulate this is for regulatory and legal requirements then you need to purchase a certificate. You can get an email SSL certificate for about $20/year from VeriSign or a variety of other secure email vendors online.

Well, that's not the way Word alphabetizes. It does a strictly numeric sort, with space as the first (highest-ranking) character (U0020, ASCII 32. Welcome to the new world order!

One of the projects I work on frequently is dictionaries, and we have to hand-order the entries (numbers have to be alphabetized as if they were spelled out, for example) because machine sorting is not acceptable.

jay freedman say:

The Index field handles one-word vs. two-word entries exactly as you see in your post. That's because the space character (ASCII 32) has a lower value than any letter character, so it sorts first. There is no provision in the Index field's switches or in the VBA Range.Sort command to ignore spaces in the entry text.

In fact, most manually created indexes in books are sorted the same way that Word does it. As a (former) professional indexer, I'm quite familiar with it.
