Here is the response from some conversations with Microsoft's support desk....

It is a compatibility problem..... and they have raised to a higher level to look at further.

It was agreed that

Some office files 2010 which are saved as xml versions (suffix x) with a password on a PC cannot be opened using Office 2016 on a Mac.

The workaround which I have already found is to open the files using Office 2011 on Mac.

The test we carried out were

1.  Uninstall both Office 2011 and 2016 on Mac

2.  Reinstall 2016. 

3.  The offices files could not be opened

4.  Open the file using Office 2011 and change the password and resave.  The file still cannot be opened using Office 2016.

5.  Open the file using Office 2011 and remove the password and save.  Reopen the document add a password and save again (with the password).  The document could now be opened with BOTH Office 2016 and Office 2011.

6.  Create a new document in Office 2011 and save with a password.  Document can be opened using Office 2016.

Microsoft could not say what the differences were with the way Office 2011 and Office 2016 decrypt.

So the workaround is to use Office 2011 to open the document and if needed by other Mac users to remove the password, save, open again and reapply the password.

Moving zune software from one computer to another. - Microsoft Community

sigjerm say:

Moving zune software from one computer to another.

I want to move my Zune library and software to another computer.  All my current content is located on an external hard drive.  What all do I have to do to make a seamless transition to this new computer?  Just download the software?  I dont want to do this until I know all the steps to take.  Thanks for any input.  
nsilmike say:
I want to move my Zune library and software to another computer.  All my current content is located on an external hard drive.  What all do I have to do to make a seamless transition to this new computer?  Just download the software?  I dont want to do this until I know all the steps to take.  Thanks for any input.  
If all of your content is on the external, it couldn't be easier.
Download and install the zune software, then go into the settings (collection) and add the external drive as the location of your content.
Connect your zune, and you should get asked if you want to link it. Say yes, and follow the prompts.
Set your sync mode in the zune software settings.

Now a few things will not transfer over quite so easily...
Podcasts:  they will be there in collection since they were on the external.  But you will have to resubscribe to each one, and set each one's series setting (how many episodes to download, etc.)  Podcasts that are already on the external will show up as 'In Collection' in the software.  That means they won't be managed by the software, and will sit there until you delete them.  You can right click and delete any you don't want permanently.  And if the series settings say they should download, they will do so, and now will show as 'automatic' (and the software will now manage them.)
Playlists:  You can reverse sync them to the new PC, but if they are autoplaylists, they only copy over as regular playlists.  You will have to recreate autoplaylists from scratch, or find them on the old PC and copy them separately.  Search on *.zpl to find them.
Playcounts:  your playcounts on the new PC will reset to zero, so autoplaylists that use playcounts will not work until you get play counts going again.
