suzanne s. barnhill say:

Even if you could make the numbers hyperlinked (and I don't think you can if they're auto numbers), users would be much more likely to click on the text. You can have the text as an active hyperlink without the blue/underlined formatting by modifying the formatting of the Hyperlink and Followed Hyperlink character styles.

Alternatively, if the text "Some of the buttons on my headset don't appear to be working anymore. What should I do?" appears in the location where this topic is addressed, you can, instead of using a simple hyperlink, use a hyperlinked cross-reference to that text. Cross-references do not display the Hyperlink character style.

A third option would be to create a TOC based on these headings (if they appear in the text). TOCs are hyperlinked by default, and, like cross-references, they do not display the Hyperlink character style.
