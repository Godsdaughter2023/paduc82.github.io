lemp say:

Microsoft's automated "troubleshooters" rarely if ever help, at least with respect to printing problems.

I see that the last response on the HP forum, on Jan 2, was to call HP.  Did you do that and if so, was the phone tech any better at resolving your issue than the online tech?

From your description on the HP forum, the problem really seems to be communication over the USB link.  What's confusing about this is the ability to run scanning software over this link ("I can scan documents in Windows").  That seems to rule out a problem with the cable itself or the USB port on the computer.

My guess is that there's a hardware problem in the printer, and if it's under warranty, you need to continue to press HP for help and if no resolution is forthcoming, to replace the printer under the warranty. 

Repeatedly uninstalling and reinstalling will not have much effect, partly because you're almost certainly not completely uninstalling the software.  I doubt this will help, but you can try the following:

  • Go to Control Panel > Programs and Features and uninstall all HP software
  • Go to Devices and Printers, right-click on the HP printer and select "Remove device"
  • While still at Devices and Printers, click one of the other icons in the "Printers and Faxes" category (e.g., click "Send To OneNote").
  • When one of the "printer" icons is selected, a "Print server properties" link will appear in the tool bar at the top of the Devices and Printers window.  Click on that link
  • Click the "Drivers" tab
  • Select all drivers that relate to HP (there's probably only one) and click the "Remove" button (you may have to first click the "Change driver settings" button and respond to a UAC prompt).
  • Select the radio button to "Remove driver and driver package"
  • OK your way out and reboot the computer.
  • Reinstall the computer using the file you downloaded from HP, being sure to not connect the USB cable until instructed to do so.
