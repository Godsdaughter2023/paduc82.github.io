adiegarden say:

wifi adapter keeps needing to be reset

For the last week I've been having WiFi issues. I have a brand new HP and for two months its been perfect. The symptoms are, it goes to a nice speed it then sags, the connection flicks from private to public and wont reconnect without trouble shooting which returns the error WiFi adapter reset. I have done ipconfig and dns flush, restarted the network, reinstalled the driver, checked another computer to see if its a hub fault and I'm now getting ****** off. Any suggestions other than welcome to windows 10?

***Post moved by the moderator to the appropriate forum category***

adiegarden say: I've fixed it.  The problem lies in the use of 802.11b/g protocol for the wireless config.  It appears that in the last few weeks it has suddenly started causing problems with the hub.  I'm assuming it was the update near the start of the month.  Changing the settings to either 802.11 b or 802.11g and not giving windows 10 the opportunity to switch between the two configurations as and when it wants solves the problem.  For anyone experiencing the issues recently goto Network connections, right click on the wifi adapter go to properties.  Click on configure, then advanced, click on wireless mode under properties and select either of the two options above.  802.11g appears to be a little quicker MB wise than the b protocol but they both work a treat.  As for Microsoft, you need to review the changes that you have made to the 802.11b/g protocol and see where the coding mistake is because it wasn't there prior to the update.  Hope this solves every

Laptop Keyboard and Touch Button Re-enable - Microsoft Community

charlesfuller say:

Laptop Keyboard and Touch Button Re-enable

Hi,

For those of you whose tablet/laptop’s keyboard and touch button cursor have suddenly stopped responding, like what happened to me when I accidentally hit the wrong combination of keys on my keyboard, I have found a potential solution for you.

1) Open up your soft keyboard in the Tablet mode. 

2) Press the soft Control and O keys simultaneously.  

This simple solution worked for my ThinkPad Yoga after I tried a number of more complicated solutions I found on line that didn’t work for me.  I hope it works for you!

Chaz

1 person found this helpful

charlesfuller say:

Hi again,

Bad news/good news.

The bad news, I have to report the solution above worked pnly once, the next I opened my laptop my screen flipped and my built in keyboard became unresponsive again.  

The good news, I got it to work again by changing the orientation of my display in my settings from “Landscape (flipped)” to “Landscape”.  I had to do this through my laptp’s touch screen function as my built in keyboard wasn’t working, but once I did my display flipped back to right-side up, and my keyboard became responsive again.

Btw, my guess to why this worked is that my laptop was automatically put into the Tablet mode when the display orientation was changed back to “Landscape (flipped)”.  After changing the orientation back to “Landscape”, my laptop was put back into the Keyboard mode and it’s built in keyboard and button cursor control worked again. 

Cheers, Chaz
