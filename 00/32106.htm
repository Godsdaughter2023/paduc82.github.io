a. user say:

There are three components to the problem:

Windows' own balance controls

The driver for your sound chip or card (which may have its own balance control)

The headphones themselves, including the wire and the jack.

You'll need to do a tedious process of elimination (which will involve another computer as well as communicating with the headphone manufacturer's tech support) to isolate the source of the problem.
