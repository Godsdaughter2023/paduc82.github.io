slance310 say:

Hi,

Whether UEFI is necessary for BitLocker or not, I don't actually know...

However... I have spent a lot of time on my machine with various Legacy and UEFI installations...

One method to switch to UEFI from a Legacy would be to make an image of the Legacy installation with a bootable imaging program... Some are free, but I personally use Acronis TureImage..

A lot of the free ones require installation to make the bootable media... This just mucks up your Windows installation in my opinion...

Once you have your image, do a clean UEFI installation of windows either on the same HDD, or another...

Just do the basic Custom Install and delete everything on the HDD...

You will now have everything you need for a UEFI machine... Just load the Legacy OS image to the UEFI Windows partition and reboot...

BTW.. To do a UEFI Installation, your Installation media must be created specifically for UEFI installation, or it will fail...

kilgetty say:

Graphics Tablet in Vista

After installing my Wacom Bamboo Graphics Tablet driver and Vista has started up its built in Tablet programme I now have a Keyboard displayed on the logon screen (this is so you can use the pen to login). My question is how can I remove this keyboard from the logon screen?
david vinod say:

Hi,

Follow the steps below and check if you are able to get rid of the logon screen keyboard.

1). Open up Control Panel and type in “windows features” into the search box, or just browse to Programs \ Turn Windows features on or off.

2). Find Tablet PC Optional Components in the list, and remove the check mark next to it.

3). Click OK, wait a while, and then you’ll have to reboot your computer, but the onscreen keyboard will be gone.

srimadhwa b say:

Hi,

Thank you for posting your question in the Microsoft Community Forums.

The open Internet Explorer window stacking is dependent on the space available on the Taskbar and also the screen resolution.

Once the space on the Taskbar is filled with open windows the stacking comes into picture automatically. As of now you do not have the option to manually set the stacking option on open windows.

Keep us informed to help you further.

Set it to always combine and hide labels

Now they will stack regardless of how much space is left.
