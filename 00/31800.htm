considerate_guy say:

Both Movie Maker and Sony Movie Studio are good programs for beginners.

Movie Maker has most of the basic features that you would need to add/delete/trim/split/rearrange clips. It also lets you mix in photos and video. You can have the original audio plus two added audio tracks (say music + narration). It lets you add animated text along with transitions and effects. The UI is pretty intuitive with a hybrid storyboard/wrapped timeline interface. I would suggest trying it out and seeing if it does what you need.

Sony Movie Studio isn't that much more complicated to learn. There are lots of tutorials on YouTube, from what I can see that will walk you through all the basics and more.

Where Sony Movie Studio excels is when you want to get fancier with multiple video streams. For example, you might want to overlay the video with a different image or video clip while the sound from the original video continues. Or you might want to do a "Picture-in-Picture" look with a second photo or video in a rectangle.

So if you plan to get fancier than what Movie Maker can do, I'd make the investment in learning Sony Movie Studio, especially since your wife was nice enough to buy it for you. I've used their higher end product (Sony Vegas) and I think you'll find it a very useful tool for editing your movies.

The biggest drawback to Movie Maker is that you can do the basics but nothing fancy. A basic thing like having a different video appear while the audio from the first video is playing isn't easy to do in Movie Maker. And things like overlaying a picture or video just isn't possible because Movie Maker is stuck with a single video stream.

But if you are more concerned about simplicity then maybe Movie Maker is the better choice. At least it is free and you can try it out to see.
