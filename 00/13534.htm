a. user say:

Hi Lululin,

 

I can imagine that you are having a difficult time in dealing with the junk mails that are coming-in to your Inbox. It’s understandable to be cautious about this since most hacked accounts were rendered compromised due to the viruses sent to them. Let me guide you on ways to deal with those junk mails.

 

For starters, the spammers use special software which scans webpages for email addresses. The most common targets for this are message boards and social networking websites. To avoid your email being picked up by this, never give your email addresses to untrusted sites and use "AT" instead of the "@" symbol (e.g. johnATyourprovider.com).

 

Microsoft has a feature to set up your account to receive the emails of your contacts and safe senders directly in your Inbox.

  1. Go to your Inbox and then click the gear icon located on the upper right corner of the page.
  2. Choose More mail settings.
  3. Under the Preventing junk mail category, choose Filters and reporting.
  4. Tick Exclusive under the Choose a junk email filter section.
  5. Click Save.

In addition, you can block untrusted sender or spammers in your account to avoid receiving future messages from them. Here are the steps:

  1. From the Options screen under the category Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.
  2. Click Blocked senders.
  3. In the Blocked senders list, add the name or domain  that you want to block messages from. (Ex. @domain.com)
  4. Click Add to list.

Note: The same steps apply to your Safe senders list. If you want to add your contacts to it, just click Safe senders on step 2.

 

You can also create a rule that will automatically delete incoming emails from spammers. This will not work on old messages received. Here are the steps:

  1. From the Options screen under the category Customizing Outlook, click Rules for sorting new messages.
  2. Click New. On Step 1, select Sender's address, contains on the dropdown menu for the first and 2nd box respectively. In the third box type the common username, domain name or keywords in the sender's address. On Step 2, you can either pick Delete these messages—don't even put them in my Deleted folder or move it to a specific folder.
  3. Click Save.

You can also use Sweep in blocking the spammers. Here are the steps:

  1. Go to your Inbox.
  2. Place a check mark on the email in your Inbox that you want to block emails from to enable the toolbar on top of the page.
  3. Click on the Sweep dropdown arrow.
  4. Choose Delete all of them? Before clicking Delete all, you can tick the box for Also block future messages.

I hope this helps. If you have clarifications, feel free to post back for further assistance.

 

Thank You.
