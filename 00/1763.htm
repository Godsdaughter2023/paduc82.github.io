dirk goldgar say:

I have one table that contains multiple yes/no fields. Based on selections made, the report must return the total number of "yeses" chosen for each day in a given month.

Example of query:

Day   NewClient     Repeat    

1             Y

According the above query sample, the report should return 2 for Day One under the heading NewClient. 1 for Day Two under the heading NewClient, and 1 for Day Two under the heading Repeat, etc for subesequent days.


Are these true yes/no (boolean) fields?  if so, it's simple; if not, it's not much harder. <g>  A query that gives the counts, assuming the fields are yes/no fields would have SQL something like this::

    SELECT
        [Day],
        Sum(IIf(NewClient<>0, 1, 0)) As CntNewClients,
        Sum(IIf(Repeat<>0, 1, 0)) As CntRepeats
    FROM YourTableOrQuery
    GROUP BY [Day]

If these are actually text fields that contain "Y" to indicate True, then the SQL would look more like this:

     SELECT 
        [Day], 
        Sum(IIf(NewClient="Y", 1, 0)) As CntNewClients,
        Sum(IIf(Repeat="Y", 1, 0)) As CntRepeats
     FROM YourTableOrQuery
     GROUP BY [Day]

If you want this on a report, and all you want to show on the report is the totals by day, you can just base your report on one of the above queries.  On the other hand, if you want to show the detail on the report as well as the totals, you would probably base your report on the original query, but in the report set up grouping on Day, and in the group footer put calculated text boxes with controlsources along the lines of:

    =Sum(IIf(NewClient<>0, 1, 0))

and

    =Sum(IIf(Repeat<>0, 1, 0))

 

dirk goldgar say:
As Tom noted, the value of a Yes is -1 so I would take a slightly different approach

SELECT tblConnie.Day, Sum(ABS([NewClient])) AS nc, Sum(ABS([Repeat])) AS r, nc+r AS Total
FROM tblConnie
GROUP BY tblConnie.Day;

The ABS function takes the absolute value of the Boolean field which will be 1 or 0 (note, if you allow Nulls, this could blow up).

I used to do it that way, but over time I've become persuaded that it's better not to rely on a specific representation of boolean fields.  To some extent I still do it, in trusting that non-zero always represents True, and anything else represents False, but I think that's safer than relying on specific values of -1 or 1.  Also, Sum(IIf(BooleanField<>0, 1, 0) is immune to Nulls.

vanderghast say:

Not necessary the standard definition of a percentile, but if you have the following definition: 

 the x-percentile over y values is Y, such that Y is the minimum y-value for which at least x% of the records have a value less or equal to Y, then you can use:

 

 

DMin(FName, TName, _
             "DCount(""*"", """ & TName & """, """ & FName & _
             "<="" & [" & FName & " ]) >= " & _
            INT( X * DCount("*", TName)))
 

 

where FName is the field name (in our example, it would be  "y" )  and TName the table name, and X the percentage  you seek ( ex:  0.70   for 70%).

 

Note that with that definition of 'percentile', the result is always a member of the set of values present in the data.

Incredimail Help Center

Q: How do I export email messages from IncrediMail to another email client?

A: Similar to other email clients, exporting email messages from IncrediMail is not possible.

If you want to move email messages from IncrediMail to another email client, you will need to save each specific email as an '.eml' file, then move the '.eml' file to another email client. To do so, follow these steps:
In the IncrediMail main window, select the message you would like to move.


Click the 'File' menu and select 'Save As...'. At this point, the 'Save As' dialog opens. Save the email message to your Desktop.


You can now drag the '.eml' file from your Desktop to any email client's Inbox folder.


Repeat the steps above for each email you want to move to another email client's Inbox folder.

-----------------------------------------------------------

The above could be a serously long process if you have a of emails that you want to migrate.

Lumia 625 WP8.1 general performance and stability issue - Microsoft Community

angezerus say:

Lumia 625 WP8.1 general performance and stability issue

Hello!

I have a Lumia 625. When it had WP8 it had some issues, many apps crashed, including built in ones, like Internet Explorer, but after I updated to WP8.1 (Cyan update) when it got released about half a month ago, I experienced a significant drop in performance. Much more apps crash or lag. The phone sometimes is very unresponsive. There is something wrong, obviously.

Some examples:

  • Sometimes I have to press the screen lock button multiple times to unlock my phone. It just don't want to react.
  • Sometimes after pressing the screen lock, the display becomes scattered. No background image, the whole bg is white and there are horizontal lines. It looks almost like a video card failure. If I re-lock and unlock again, usually it disappears. Today it didn't even show any background it showed the tiles with the date/time and keypad above it. It was quite strange, and disturbing.
  • Navigating between apps and menus is sometimes slow and laggy. Even the home screen can freeze and lag from time to time.
  • Pressing buttons on screen, like in menus and similar, is sometimes not working. I have to touch them multiple times to actually get the phone do something.
  • Navigating between web pages (in IE) is slow, and unresponsive. Sometimes I click on a link or type in a new url, and the phone just don't respond. I have to wait several, not once more than 10 seconds to something to happen.
  • Back button erratic/unpredictable operation. It happened to me not even once in many apps. For example I opened a web page in IE, on that page I clicked on a link that opened a new tab and and loaded a new page. Now, I tried it 3 times in a row with different results all the time. I pushed the back button, and instead of closing the new page and switching back to the first one, it closed the whole App. After this I opened the pages again in the exact same way, pushed back button, it closed the new page and went back for the first one. The third time was the same as the first. After this I opened a different page. Using only 1 active tab, I navigated through some links, but pressing the back button sometimes only stepped back sometimes closed the app (I wasn't on the first page that I opened originally). This happened many times. In addition to this, there are some apps that close after pressing back, but some just minimize (facebook, calendar, etc...). SUPER ANNOYING.
  • Constant crashes. Apps, and sometimes even the phone itself crashes. It looks like as if my phone was short on performance/hardware. Fact, that lumia 625 has only 512MB RAM, but from what I know it shouldn't affect basic things like menus, and built in apps, since they are supposed to work on this device. I can accept if some high-end games and apps crash sometimes or don't start, because they require more ram, but I don't accept this in case of the standard  apps. From my experience it seems that developers don't bother with the RAM question. They release something and if it crashes, than it's your bad luck. From what I know, they have the chance to distinguish low and high RAM devices, and make restrictions according to that, yet I haven't seen any app so far, that I couldn't download because I have low RAM. Did they forget this? Do they test on/develop for high RAM devices only?
  • Random restarts. I noticed not even once that the phone restarted itself and it was asking for my pin. It happened in my pocket and on the desk as well, so I'm fairly sure it wasn't an accidental button push, not to mention that the phone was locked. If this happens abroad and I don't notice it in my pocket and accidentally enter random numbers as pin and lock the phone, that would mean a disaster for me.

And this is only the tip of the iceberg. There are many many many problems, bugs, errors, annoying things. It almost seems like a beta/alfa stage OS because of these issues (note, that I upgraded after it was released officially and the provider approved it). To sum things up, there are a lot of bugs, and a major drop in performance after the upgrade. Half of these problems existed in WP8 as well, but now everything is worse.

I am very much disappointed in this phone and OS altogether. There are some brilliant ideas, and cool designs, but because of the problems, I feel like as if I had bought some kind of a poor quality replica. In overall it is not fluent, not intelligent, not easy to use. There are areas where it is, but in the big picture it is not.

Sometimes I pray not to get into a situations where I depend on my phone, because I might not make it. There were instances when I had to look something on the map or call someone fast and the phone crashed leaving me helpless for minutes, while it rebooted. This has NEVER happened to me on any other phones before. It can be also quite humiliating when I want to show someone something and the phone keeps crashing, lagging and not working in general.

I have posted some reports regarding some of the issues, but I haven't received any real answer to any of those. Usually the support tries to get me to use workarounds and tricks to counter problems, instead of saying that they will relay the info to the devs. This is my second huge disappointment. Based on this, I predict that most of the issues will either never be fixed or only be fixed when the core developers discover it and work on it, which may be months or even years.

A would like to ask every contributor, developer and anyone related, to please pay some more attention and put some more effort into fighting these problems, because the current situation is far from comforting.

Based on the details provided, Insider build Windows 10 Enterprise issues are best handled if a feedback will submitted on our Feedback Hub to aware our engineers and developers of this matter. 

In order to submit a new feedback, kindly follow these steps:  

  1. On a Windows 10 device, search for "Feedback Hub" in Cortana search, and then launch the app. 

  2. Navigate to Feedback in the left menu, and then press + Add new feedback. 

  3. Select the Problem, and share any details you think are relevant, and then choose an appropriate category and subcategory. 

  4. Important: If possible, reproduce the problem(s) after clicking Begin monitoring(or Start capture) near the end of the form; Stop monitoring when you're done. 

  5. Once you've completed the form and monitoring, click Submit. 

  6. Click Continue using Feedback Hub. 

  7. Click My Feedback at the top, and then find the item you submitted (it may take a while to appear). 

  8. Open the item you submitted, and then click Share. 

  9. Click Copy link, and then paste the link in your response (it will look like https://aka.ms/<unique link>).

Let us know if you have any further queries.
