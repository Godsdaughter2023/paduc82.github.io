FIXED ON SAMSUNG GALAXY S2?
 
I had the same problem with hundreds of duplicated emails in my outlook sent mail folder. I have a Samsung Galaxy S2 and I use the native email app (which I prefer after trying many alternatives). Domain\user name: \[Your outlook email address]
Password: [Your outlook password]
Exchange Server: s.outlook.com
Use Secure connection (SSL) [Tick yes]
Use client certificate: [Leave blank]

All your emails will be back in place on the phone within a few minutes once you have worked through a few more settings which are all ok at their default values.

Excel 2016 Conditional Formatting - Microsoft Community

maria web say:

Excel 2016 Conditional Formatting

Hi, 

Can someone help me with the below? I have a license for Office 2016. I am trying to create a spreadsheet which does the following.

1) Make G and H column = A and B column (So, whatever is typed in A and B column appears automatically in G and H column)

2) If I column is not the same as C column - then colour the cell K green.  If not highlight the cell red and display text  'missing part' in column K.

Thank you for any tips you may have. 


hansv mvp say:

Assuming that the data begin in row 3:

1) In G3, enter the formula =IF(A3="","",A3)

In H3, enter the formula =IF(B3="","",B3)

Select G3:H3, and fill or copy down as far as you need.

2) In K3, enter the formula =IF(C3=I3,"","DISCREPANCY")

Fill or copy down as far as you need.

With the cells in column K still selected, select green as background color.

On the Home tab of the ribbon, click Conditional Formatting > New Rule...

Select 'Format only cells that contain'.

Leave the first dropdown set to 'Cell Value'.

Select 'equal to' from the second dropdown.

Enter DISCREPANCY in the box to the right.

Click Format...

Activate the Fill tab.

Select red.

Click OK, then click OK again.
