suzanne s. barnhill say:

Perhaps the first row of the table is formatted as Page Break Before? Or have you restarted numbering at 1 on the page containing the table? If so, Word will treat the Next Page section break as Odd Page.

Note that in your description, unless you want to have numbering/header/footer on those "blank" pages, it would make more sense to use Odd Page section breaks at the end of pages 3 and 5. Then Word will automatically insert blank pages 4 and 6. As is the case with the current page 8, however, you won't actually see those pages, but they will be inserted when you print. You can actually see them if you (a) have enabled either "Mirror margins" or "Different Odd and Even" and (b) display two faces in Print Preview Edit Mode, which will then display true facing pages.

dianeoforegon say: Entourage 2008 comes in two versions for Exchange users.

Entourage v12.3.1 is for Exchange 2003

If you are on Exchange 2007 or higher you can upgrade to Entourage EWS v13.1.1 for free.

UPGRADE TO EWS

You need to manually download the EWS 13.1.1 updater and run it over your 12.3.1 install.

http://www.microsoft.com/download/en/details.aspx?displaylang=en&id=27361

Once Entourage is updated to v13.1.1, any future updates will recognize Entourage as v13 and update as needed.

What is confusing is both Entourage v12 and Entourage v13 share the same Office 2008 Identities folder.

When you open Entourage after updating it will ask you to import from a previous version. Same as updating from Entourage 2004. Once Entourage is updated it can no longer open any v12 Identity without importing first. Even the database utility cannot 'see' a v12 Identity.

I suggest putting EWS in the Identity name so you don't get confused.
