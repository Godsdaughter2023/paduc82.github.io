kenneth_f say:

Hi

There's a difference between the accounts you created before Skype merge with Microsoft. Your Skype account is a legacy account that has a unique Skype name (username) which you should be using to sign in to Skype. If you are signing in using the Hotmail email and password, that is not your Skype account, that is your Microsoft account alone, but can be used across all Microsoft products including Skype. This is the reason why you are being asked for Two Factor Authentication when signing in your Hotmail because it is set up only on this account. While your Skype account doesn't have any Two Factor Authentication set up. In other words, you have two accounts, the Skype account (legacy account) and your Microsoft account (your email).

For Skype account, yes it is possible to set up a Two Factor Authentication. But this process needs your Skype account to be converted to a Microsoft account. To convert your Skype account > just open this link login.live.com > Sign in your Skype name (username) and password > you will be asked to merge your Skype account to a new Microsoft account, just follow the steps given on the screen. This process needs for every legacy account to enjoy

Once you're done, you can proceed setting up a two factor authentication or the Two-step verification. You will be doing this under Microsoft website. Here is the link for the complete steps in setting it up: About two-step verification.

Let me know if you have concerns or let us know any updates.
