david everingham say:
The foundational level is the Language set in the pertinent Template.  (In Tools/Language, click the Default… button (or actually edit the template file).)

I have just done a bit of experimenting; generally, the language of any new text is a function of the surrounding existing text.  However, there is one thing that I have found that goes with the template rather than the surrounding text — Insert Text Box.  (Also, Captions, and the contents of a Table of Contents, apart from the headings’ text… but not Cross-references to Bookmarks.)

I suggest, then, that you make sure that the template content is set to the desired language.  

• Styles can have a Language set.  (One of the failings of Word is that you can not delete an element of a style, once it has been added.)  Perhaps you should check your document styles; the Organizer is probably the quickest way to do this.

• Of course, any text you Paste in will also bring a language setting with it (if it has one).  This seems to happen even if you do a Paste - Unformatted Text or a Paste and Match Formatting.

• If someone changes the template, all documents based on that template will reflect the change if subsequently opened.  One would expect that this effect would include any content that goes with the default Language setting; given the above, however, this would be worth checking.  (Conversely, changing the template of an existing document does not seem to affect the Language.)

For things like styles, the information is copied from the template when the document is created, after that the styles in the document are independent from the template.

This article provides more details if you are interested:

What is the relationship between a Microsoft Word document and its template?

http://www.shaunakelly.com/word/templates/templaterelations.html

Every Microsoft Word document is based on a template, whether you choose a template explicitly or not.

Understanding the relationship between documents and templates is crucial to understanding how Word works.

To "force" the styles you can copy "correct" styles from any other template or document. You use the "Organizer" tool to do that. This article is talking about copying macros, but the same process applies to styles.  To launch the organizer display the styles pane by clicking on the dialong launcher button in the bottom right corner of the styles group in the Home tab. Then click on the "Manage Styles" button at the bottom of the styles pane. Click on the (illogically named) "Import/Export" button in the bottom left corner of the Manage Styles dialog.  You can also use the path described in the article below, it may be easier, then click on the styles tab.

Using the Organizer to Manage Macros
