 When we talk about our video displays on our computers, I find that they are often referred to as Video subsystems.  This is because there is more going into making them work than just the display.  Before anything gets to the monitor it first has to be processed by the video card.  The better the information Windows has to work with the better you display can work.

 First, I try to make sure that the operating system has the correct and latest driver for the video card.  Then I try to make sure that Windows knows (has the driver for) exactly what kind of monitor it is dealing with.  Once that is done you can make sure that the Monitor's setup includes the refresh rate that your new hardware will support.  The default 60mHz is probably much lower than your display can actually handle.  Too low a refresh rate translates to flicker.
