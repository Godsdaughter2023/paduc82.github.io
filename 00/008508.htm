Out of date "Unread Emails" notification

I have a corporate email account that I access through Outlook on my PC and via the Android email application on my phone.
I have a corporate email account that I access through Outlook on my PC and via the Android email application on my phone.
When I read an email on my phone, it appropriately marks the email as read in Outlook on my PC. The problem is, I'll come in to the office next day and I'll have a notification icon telling me I have new unread emails, but all of my emails are read.
 
It's easy enough to get rid of: I just right click on my inbox and "Mark All as Read". It's just annoying that I have to do this every morning to "reset" my notification for the day.
 
Is there a way to fix it so that reading an email, regardless of where you read it, causes the unread emails notification to close on the PC (if that was the last unread email)?

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

The display of the "You have new messages" notification icon in the Notification Area is toggled on by the arrival of a new message and toggled off by the marking of any message (not just an unread one) as read by Outlook.  Outlook cannot detect that you're reading it on your phone and so does not know it has been read.  Thus, the action that turns the notification off doesn't happen until you open a message or, in yor case, use the mark as read option.

andrew_coonce say:

> Outlook cannot detect that you're reading it on your phone and so does not know it has been read.


I assumed that was the case at first, but the emails aren't highlighted "bold" as unread when I read them on my phone. So it appears that it knows I'm reading them, it's just not clearing the "notification" flag.

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

I don't know complete details on the internal workings of the notification, but it appears to be a separate process thread that doesn't have the information it needs to turn off the icon when the message is read by another device, even though the bolding gets removed.  Are you using an Exchange server for your mailbox?  That could explain why.

andrew_coonce say: It's an Exchange server. It just feels like an odd oversight on Outlook's part. All of the information is there: it knows the email has been read because it clears the flag, it knows there are no more unread emails because it manages the [count] flags, it just doesn't clear the notification.

My theory: they could hook the close notification event to the message being marked as read but it's only currently hooked into the physical act of marking the message as read. Maybe that'll change in a future release...
