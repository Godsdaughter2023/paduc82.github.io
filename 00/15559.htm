OneNote Ink Shifting issue - analysys and conclusion based on 11 years - Microsoft Community

gomdolius say:

OneNote Ink Shifting issue - analysys and conclusion based on 11 years of OneNoting, plus (temporary) SOLUTION and SUGGESTION.

Hi,

As OneNote for Desktop 2016 will not be updated any more (even in the upcoming 2018 for Desktop) I would like to discuss about that dreaded ink shifting issue.

PROBLEM:

Sometimes OneNote ink strokes can shift its position with no warning. Inks gets overlapped and the overlapped parts are, like, completely destroyed.

For those who do not understand what this issue is, refer to these links:

https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/msoffice/forum/msoffice_onenote-mso_win10-mso_365hp/ink-notes-shifting-during-sync/44074a6c-39bc-4522-9a7b-93505a24eac2 and https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/msoffice/forum/msoffice_onenote-mso_imobile-mso_mobapps/ink-shifting-when-shared-between-onenotes-on/f31d7116-1362-410f-b25c-4f5fc43383cf

This is a bug, introduced in OneNote 2010 (OM*G it has been NINE! YEARS!!), which:

- is related to inaccurate PARAGRAPH HANDLING, and,

- is not 100% related to syncing, as syncing with OneDrive contributes to this issue but it happens when you save notes locally.

ANALYSIS:

When you write a lot in ink, OneNote basically thinks the cluster of ink strokes as handwriting, and treats the cluster as paragraph. Therefore,

1) OneNote tries to calculate the spaces between the ink clusters (and indent of ink clusters), but it really can't do that accurately. 

2) Some ink clusters gets grouped as one paragraph as OneNote sees fit (not accurate, either).

3) Finally, because those ink clusters are paragraphs to OneNote, THEIR POSITIONS ARE NOT ABSOLUTE, BUT RELATIVE in OneNote.

This is the source of problems.

So, any changes (including) that are made (either by OneNote app, OneDrive sync, or USER, deleting or modifying or substituting missing font or doing whatever) to the contents that preceed the ink cluster, can change the position of the ink clusters after them. If you are not sure what this means, just think of Word. You have paragraph A, B, C in your document, in that order. If you delete paragraph B, paragraph C will go up. If you delete paragraph A, paragraph B and C will go up as paragraph A no longer exists and does not take any space.

Almost same thing is happening with OneNote inks. The only thing that is different is that the order of paragraph is invisible to user's eye, as it is automatically, and internally set by OneNote. Even if you see ink clusters A, and B, then C, it might be internally ordered like A-C-B. So if you erase some ink strokes in cluster(=paragraph) C, cluster B can shift.

SOLUTION?

Though it is somewhat not satisfactory, you can change pen behavior (pen mode) TO 'CREATE DRAWINGS ONLY' (default is, Create both handwriting and drawings). You are 'painting', instead of 'writing' in this mode. OneNote does not treat 'paintings' as paragraphs (obviously!), therefore, paintings never move (they have absolute position).

You can do this only in Desktop app, and this setting applies to Desktop app only. In other words, UWP app will not have this option and any ink you write in UWP app has potential to shift.

You will LOSE ink-to-text (handwriting recognition) feature for any ink strokes you made in 'Create drawings only' mode.

SUGGESTION? (to Microsoft)

Please make all ink positions ABSOLUTE. Inks should NOT MOVE even if any TEXT, IMAGE, INK contents preceeding other ink contents get deleted or modified.

If you can't do that for whatever reason/purpose, please add 'Create drawings only' in UWP app.

p.s. SERIOUSLY, Microsoft, can't you fix this 9-year-old-issue already?
