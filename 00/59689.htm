a. user say:

You cannot stop the restarts. You can control them, but at some point your computer is going to need to restart.

You can control restarts in two ways, one of which requires that you have the Professional edition of Windows 10.

In all editions of Windows 10, you can set active hours. These are times when you are considered to be actively working on your computer. Windows 10 will not restart during active hours. You can set aside up to 18 hours per day to be your active hours (and that number may increase.) For most users, their non-active hours fall during the middle of the night.

In the Professional edition of Windows 10 you can delay quality updates for up to 30 days and feature updates for up to 360 days.

Between these two features, you can exercise substantial control over when your computer restarts. But eventually, it needs to restart.

If an update fails to install, it will try to install again at the next conclusion of active hours. If this is happening often, you need to find out why the update is failing and fix whatever needs fixing.

Finally, you can minimize data loss by backing up frequently. Third-party backup software makes this easy and you can continue to use the computer while the backup is happening in the background. You could, for example, schedule incremental  backups to occur every 15 minutes.
