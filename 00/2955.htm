It isn't clear to me exactly where your problem lies.  If you're trying to get ColumnHistory for a field that isn't a memo field with AppendOnly = Yes, then you're out of luck without programming.  BUT, if the only problem is that your primary key field is a text field and not an autonumber (or other number type), then you may just be failing because the expression to extract the column history needs to be modified to take that into account.  A correct expression to use a text key rather than a numeric one might look like this:

 

    =ColumnHistory("Citations","Comments","[ID]='" & [ID] & "'")

 

Note that the above assumes that ID is a text field, and that it won't contain the single-quote character (').

 

scottgem say:

In that template, the primary key "ID" is autonumber.  In my instance I cannot use an autonumber field, so I wanted to change it to text. 

What makes you think that? The purpose of a primary key is to uniquely identify a record. An Autonumber is perfect for that purpose since it is almost guaranteed to be unique. That does not mean that you can't use another field to display a document ID that can be visible to users and just keep the autonumber as your PK in the background. 

You can also set a unique index on the Document ID to prevent it being duplicated. However, I suspect that your document ID may be comprised of other fields already in your table. If that's the case, then you shouldn't be storing it, but rather displaying the combination of fields. And, again, still using the autonumber for your PK.

If you are using Excel 2007 or later, have a table that lists table in column A, date and time in column B and number of people playing in column C, you can use the sumifs function to get how many people were playing in all tables on April 1st between 10:00 and 11:00:

 

=SUMIFS(C:C, B:B, ">=" & DATE(2013, 04, 01) + TIME(10,00,00), B:B, "<"& DATE(2013,04,01) + TIME(11,00,00))

 

It means "sum column C when what you have in column B is greater than 10 am and what you have in column B is also less than 11:00 am".
