Default Paragraph Spacing in Word for Mac - Microsoft Community

koray g. say:

Default Paragraph Spacing in Word for Mac

I have Microsoft Word 2016 for Mac and I can't figure out a way to set the default paragraph settings. Is there a way to do that?
john mcghie say:

No Rodney:

Your response did not answer his question :-)

He wants to set the "Default paragraph spacing".

That is: the space between paragraphs.  Not the line height (which is what you are talking about).

And he wants it as a default, which means he wants to set the spacing of the Normal style in the Normal template, not directly in the document.

Koray:

The following will work:

  1. Ensure your cursor is in a paragraph that is formatted with Normal style.
  2. Click the Styles Pane button on the Home tab of the Ribbon to open the styles pane.
  3. In the Current Style box at the top, drop down the disclosure triangle at the far right of the name "Normal" and choose Modify style.
  4. In the bottom left of the Window that appears, open the Format drop-down list and choose Paragraph.
  5. In the Spacing section of the dialog that opens, set Before to 0, After to 10 pt, and Line spacing to At Least 1.15 li.
  6. OK that.
  7. In the Modify Style dialog that appears, at the bottom left, check the Add to template checkbox.
  8. OK the dialog.

Step 7 is the critical step: checking that box writes your updates back to the Normal template, so what you have specified here in the current document will actually become the "Default" for the documents you create in the future.

The spacings I have given you are what I find quite nice, and many people use: feel free to vary them if you wish. For example, 10 pt is appropriate if your font height is 10 or 11 points: if you use 12 points font, open it up a bit -- maybe 12 points, or 14 for a more open look.  A line height of "At Least" allows Word to grow the line height if you have some letters in a larger font on the line, otherwise you will suffer cut-off letters.  If you want a more compact look, you can specify "Single" here, or for a more open look, 1.5.

Hope this helps

david a matson say:

Hello Ani.  Welcome to the Windows Phone forums!

Actually, this is an example of a security issue being remediated.  The ASUS device is simply attempting to prevent unauthorized access between your two networks by only allowing one connection at a time.   This is more or less normal in today's world of security.  

For example, if your WiFi was connected to a business network instead, and you were the IT guy, you would not want users inside your building with WiFi access to open-up a backdoor to the internet via their phone's GPRS connection so that an outside hacker could access your network. 

Also, in your specific case, were you to create a two-way connection, then it's conceivable that someone else on a laptop using the home WiFi *could* be re-routed through your GPRS connection to the internet instead of the regular internet connection.  Not very likely, since the best path (in terms of efficiency and speed) would always be through the regular internet connection...

- David A. Matson
Moderator, Windows Mobile phone Forums
Product Reviewer, Blogger, Moderator www.mobilitysite.com

jaap v. ekris say:

This is a challenging scenario: the OS automatically disables the GPRS connection when they detect the WiFi connection. The underlying assumption is that WiFi is the cheaper and faster alternative, so when it is available you don't want the GPRS connection to be used.

Theoretically you can set specific exclusions to your routing based on the Settings >> Connections tab >> Connections >> advanced tab. There you can set specific rules when to connect to the home network and when to connect to GPRS.
