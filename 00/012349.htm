bcj say: I had the same problem and fixed it just now. First, I deleted all Outlook contact cards with the offending address by opening a new email message to the address, right clicking on it, then selecting "contact card" and deleting it. Next I deleted all emails in both the Inbox and Sent folders to or from the offending address. Finally, I opened a stored email (from another folder) that had the offending address, right clicked on the address and used the mailto: *** Email address is removed for privacy *** hyperlink to send a test message. I think this final step was the one that corrected the error.

BTW, this error was only occuring on an email address with a .mil domain and is one I use daily many, many times. This problem has plagued me for many months and was isolated to only my home desktop computer. A home laptop sitting a few feet away did not have this problem and both use Outlook and Windows. It had nothing to do with with anything beyond my desktop system and appears to have been a bogus substitution for the email address character string I was typing in. I would type in the correct address and Outlook would replace it with the same string but underlined. Right clicking the underlined string gives the option of displaying the related contact card but deleted that card did NOT correct the problem. It was not until I both deleted the card AND used the mailto: hyperlink that the test emails were sent without error.

Good luck to all who are having this same frustrating problem!
