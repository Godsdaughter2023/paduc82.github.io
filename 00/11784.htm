yves dhondt say:

One way around it is by replacing the @ with its hex equivalent. Like a space is substituted by %20 in hyperlinks, you can substitute an @ by %40.

If you hover your mouse over the hyperlink in Word, Word will still display the @, but it will no longer automatically parse it as a mail link.

graham mayor say:

With a pasted link, change the @ to %40 and remove the mailto: before OK-ing out of the dialog.
If you type the link using %40 in the first place mailto: will not be added.

Hold CTRL key and click on application icon and don't release the CTRL key till it asks you for Safe mode confirmation.

Close all office applications and change your default printer through Start > Devices and Printers to another printer. It may be some physical one or One Note or XPS or PDF or anything else.
