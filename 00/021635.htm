You're yet another victim of Microsoft's infuriating, misleading "Table Lookup Field". See http://www.mvps.org/access/lookupfields.htm for a critique.

timliu_msft1261 say:

Hi Yiwei,
 
Yes, it’s possible to share the files in your OneDrive for Business library to external users and let them edit the files in Office Online.
 
To achieve this requirement, your tenant need to enable the external sharing feature. Global admin or SharePoint admin can navigate to SharePoint admin center>settings>enable External Sharing.
 
After enabling the external sharing feature in tenant level, we also need to enable it for specific site collections. In this case, we need to turn on the external sharing for https://domain-my.sharepoint.com. In SharePoint admin center>site collections>choose that site collection>click the Sharing button>choose one of the two Allow options. To learn more, see Manage external sharing for your SharePoint Online environment.
 
Now, we can share files to external users. Click "…">Share>Invite people>enter that user's account>Choose Can edit>Share. External users will receive an email and can directly open and edit the file in Office Online.
Note: Please tick the check box of Send an email invitation.

Other methods for external sharing, see Share sites or documents with people outside your organization.
