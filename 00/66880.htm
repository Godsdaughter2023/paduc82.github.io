jetta48 say:

Unrequested phone calls of this nature are almost always a common scam. In the future, do not give them any info, do  not give them access to your PC, do not give them any money and do  not go to any websites they suggest. Hang up.

 

Please see:

http://www.microsoft.com/en-gb/security/online-privacy/avoid-phone-scams.aspx

 

Microsoft never makes unasked for support or security phone calls.

 

These types of callers may use completely fictitious names or bogusly claim to be from genuine companies other than Microsoft as well.

 

If you gave them access to your PC then what you do will depend on the sort of information you store on it and how you use it. Personally, I would, as a minimum:

·        Change all passwords that may have been compromised. Do this on a different PC as yours may

         have been infected and send the new passwords to the scammers.

·        Do a clean install (including a format) of Windows or restore to factory condition*. You will lose all

         data, so back up to a separate drive. If you do not feel confident doing all this get

         professional/expert help.

·        Check the above back up for viruses using at least two independent up to date anti-virus scanners

         (only install one real-time scanner though).

·        Tell your bank, credit card companies, etc. what has happened.

·        Watch for suspicious charges/payments on your credit cards and bank account(s).

·        Watch your email accounts for suspicious activity.

·        Consider telling the police/local authorities.

·        With the main exception of cleaning up your PC, much of what you need to do is common sense

         stuff. Think how the bad guy could misuse anything that was on your PC and take steps to

         counteract it.

 

 

 *Some people recommend less drastic action involving standard antimalware checks. The choice is yours - it is a matter of confidence in the security of your system.
