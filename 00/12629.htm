Requests are authorized using an access control list associated with each bucket and object.

Bucket names and keys are chosen so that objects are addressable using HTTP URLs:

  • http://s3.amazonaws.com/bucket/key
  • http://bucket.s3.amazonaws.com/key
  • http://bucket/key (where bucket is a DNS CNAME record pointing to )

Because objects are accessible by unmodified HTTP clients, S3 can be used to replace significant existing (static) web hosting infrastructure.[16] The Amazon AWS Authentication mechanism allows the bucket owner to create an authenticated URL with time-bounded validity. That is, someone can construct a URL that can be handed off to a third-party for access for a period such as the next 30 minutes, or the next 24 hours.

Every item in a bucket can also be served up as a BitTorrent feed. The S3 store can act as a seed host for a torrent and any BitTorrent client can retrieve the file. This drastically reduces the bandwidth costs for the download of popular objects. While the use of BitTorrent does reduce bandwidth, AWS does not provide native bandwidth limiting and as such users have no access to automated cost control. This can lead to users on the 'free-tier' S3 or small hobby users amassing dramatic bills. AWS representatives have previously stated that such a feature was on the design table from 2006 to 2010[17] but have recently stated the feature is no longer in development.[18]

Emailing Groups I'm a Part of and Getting Copy of Email

I send emails in Outlook 2016 to my team all the time, both to inform them of what's going on, and to let them know I've handled the email they were included on too. When I reply back to the group, of which my email address is a part, I get a copy of my reply back in my inbox. Can I turn that off?
chris peng msft say:

Hi Jeremy,

 

The email sent to a distribution group will be delivered to all the members in this group. It’s an expected behavior that you receive the email sent from yourself to the group of which you are a member of. If you are using an Office 365 group and subscribe to this group, the email sent from yourself to this Office 365 group will be delivered to your inbox as well.

 

It’s not feasible to turn it off. However, we can use an inbox rule to delete such emails. In the Outlook client:

1.  Go to File -> Info -> Manage Rules & Alerts. Click New Rule… and select Apply rule on messages I receive.

2.  Tick the condition from people or public group and fill in your address. Tick the condition sent to people or public group and fill in the related group.

3.  Tick the action delete it.

 

You can also create such rules in Outlook Web App (OWA). Click the gear icon on the upper-right -> Mail ->　Automatic processing -> Inbox and sweep rules.

 

Thanks,

Chris
