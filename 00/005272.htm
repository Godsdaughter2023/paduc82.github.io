Keep with next in Word for Mac 2011 - Microsoft Community

alanwbrookes say:

Keep with next in Word for Mac 2011

I have a style in my Word for Mac that makes sure that all headings are kept with the next paragraph, which avoids me having to put a preceding page break if, by chance, a heading ends up at the bottom of a page.  However, it is not working in the particular document that I am creating at present (it works in other documents).  Why is that?  Can anyone help with a solution?
john mcghie say:

Hi Alan:

Two things can prevent "Keep Next" working:

*  Widow/Orphan

*  Too many "keeps" in a row.

When you assign "Keep with Next" to a Heading style, you must also assign "Keep Lines Together".  It may not be obvious, but if Word splits the paragraph, keep-next is actually working but the result is bad anyway.

If you have "every" paragraph on a page set with Keep Next, Word will eventually ignore it.  If Word can't find an acceptable split point within two pages, it will suppress keep next and split anyway.

It's important to turn on display of the paragraph marks so you can see what is going on.

There's also a nasty little thing called a "Style Separator" that you may find in very old documents that have been edited on a PC.  The style separator looks like a paragraph mark, but it isn't; and keep next will fail if it encounters one (because that text division is not a "paragraph").

Hope this helps
