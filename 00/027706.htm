cortig say:
It's a tricky thing:
- Hotmail/Live offers a calendar that can sync in Outlook for Windows,
and mobile devices (through Active Sync), but that's it! You can't sync
it with anything on your Mac.
- iCloud (and Gmail and tons of other servers out there) sync with
CalDAV. Outlook for Windows can sync to CalDAV through a third party
plugin, but Outlook for Mac doesn't support plugins. You therefore
cannot sync to iCloud (or Google Calendar) through Outlook for Mac.
 
This is a VERY serious limitation as far as I am concerned. I suspect
MS is dragging their feet because CalDAV is a competitor to their
Exchange platform, but that's a problem for tons of users out there.
 If that's an important feature for you, I would strongly encourage you
to use the Help > Send Feedback command from within Outlook to let MS
know, and help them prioritize future development.

cortig say:

Here's the deal.

On your Mac:

  • Outlook only supports Exchange through Web Services.
  • Calendar (the built-in calendar in OS X) supports CalDAV and Exchange through Web Services.

On Windows:

  • On the Windows side, from what I know, Outlook supports Exchange through MAPI and Web Services, and through some weird trick can hook on Outlook.com/Live.com. Through a plugin (e.g., the Apple iCloud one), it also supports CalDAV.

On iOS:

  • The phone natively supports CalDAV and Exchange through ActiveSync

Now on the calendar side:

  • Outlook.com/Hotmail/live.com offers Active Sync for mobile devices and some weird hook for Outlook for Windows.
  • iCloud is CalDAV all the way
  • Google Calendar is also CalDAV.

Depending on what you use then, you can be just fine on Windows and on your phone, but not on your Mac.

  • There is no solution other than using a web browser to view the Outlook.com/Live/Hotmail calendar.
  • With an Exchange account you'd be able to sync everywhere.
  • With iCloud or Gmail, you need to use the Calendar app on your Mac.
