ossiemac say:

Individual cells cannot be expanded horizontally. It must be the entire column.

The following event code will AutoFit the column width. Wrap text is turned off for the actual target cell before the Autofit column width.

Code edited since initial posting so that the column Id only needs editing in the one place.

Private Sub Worksheet_Change(ByVal Target As Range)

    'Edit "A" in following line to required column Id
    If Not Intersect(Target, Columns("A")) Is Nothing Then
        Target.WrapText = False
        Target.EntireColumn.AutoFit
    End If

End Sub

You can use a flash drive for your purpose but that needs thorough handling of the synchronization process, so your fears are real ;-)

Since a Onenote notebook can only have one master (and avoiding a flash drive) leads to the decision where to store it: on the desktop or the laptop. A prerequisite for this solution is a (WiFi)-network at your home, so you are able to connect your 2 computers if you are at home.

I suggest to store the master notebook at the safer place, your desktop at home. Now it must be accessible over your home network by your laptop. Windows shared folders are the answer. I suggest to use the default path/folder for the master notebook:

C:\Users\<user>\Documents\OneNote Notebooks

and make this folder a shared Windows folder for access by your laptop. Move/copy your notebook (its folder !) into the shared folder. Now go to your laptop and navigate to the shared folder on your desktop and open the master notebook from there. You'll get a copy into the Onenote cache on your laptop for online (while in your home network) or offline use (while in school). 

The working procedure should be:

Before going to school while both computers are running and connected start Onenote on the laptop for synchronisation.

Coming home do the same.

I said in my reply

A prerequisite for this solution is a (WiFi)-network at your home, so you are able to connect your 2 computers if you are at home.

So both computers are connected to your WiFi network AND connected with each other by accessing the shared folder of your desktop from your laptop. These 3 connections should happen automatically after their 1st creation. What you only have to do is

  1. Power on your Desktop (connects with your routers WiFi)
  2. Power on your Laptop (connects with your routers WiFi, then connects with the shared folder on your Desktop)
  3. Start Onenote on the Laptop (Onenote now accesses the master notebook in the shared folder on your Desktop and compares the content of the master notebook with its locally cached copy of the notebook. If you changed the master Onenote will copy these changes to the notebook copy on the Laptop. This process is called synchronization).

Now the Laptop is up to date and you can go to school. 
After coming home do the same. Under the hood the synchronization works in the other direction. Changes are copied from the cached notebook copy on the Laptop to the master notebook on the Desktop.

By the way: if you forget one of these synchronizations Onenote will usually handle this the next time.

pc-learner say:

I have pretty much given up on using the adapter manufacturer drivers for wireless adapters.

The manufacturer usually stops updating them long before the chipset OEM stops, so in general for an adapter that was introduced more than a year ago you are better off finding the latest drivers for that same chipset, no matter who releases them. The chipset drivers almost always support every adapter ever released that uses that chipset, and it is unlikely the adapter manufacturer has made any proprietary changes that didn't flow back to the chipset OEM version.

So, step 1 is to find out what chipset your wireless adapter uses. Step 2 is to find out what other adapters use the same chipset (the Wikidevi site can be a big help here). Step 3 is to look at the other manufacturer sites to see which has the most recent driver. Step 4 is to see if the Station Drivers web site has a more recent version (they almost always do, but make sure it is a WHQL version). Step 5 is to download, install, and test the version you want to try.

In your case, Station Drivers has Realtek RTL8812AU/8192RU/... Version 1030.12.0526.2016 WHQL, which I have used successfully with that adapter. It also has 1030.13.704.2016 WHQL, which I haven't tried yet.

Happy hunting.
