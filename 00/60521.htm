          rundll32.exe shell32.dll,Control_RunDLL hotplug.dll

First of all, because there may be more than one Microsoft Knowledge Base article on a given topic (there often are), you should specify which "Microsoft page" you are referencing.  I assume that you mean "How to disable the Autorun functionality in Windows," KB 967715: http://support.microsoft.com/kb/967715/en-us

As that article explains, you need to have the security update for KB967715.  If you have been using Automatic Updates, you should already have it; if not download it from the link in the article.  The easiest way to determine if you have this update is to look for the hidden uninstall file:  $NtUninstallKB967715$ (this is in C:\Windows, but you have to configure Windows to show hidden files or, if you search, to select the option to look for hidden files and folders).

According to the article's description of the manual method for disabling Autorun, the registry can be configured to disable Autorun on all types of types of drives or any combination of the following types of drives:

     unknown type
     removable drives
     fixed drives
     network drives
     CD-ROM drives
     RAM disks

On the other hand, Group Policy appears to have only three choices if the "Turn Off Autoplay" policy is enabled: (1 - the default) Autoplay disabled on removable drives (such as the floppy drive) and network drives but not CD drives; (2) Autoplay disabled on CD drives (in addition to network and removable drives); or (3) Autoplay disabled on all drives.  I have not run the FixIt to determine if it permits you to select option (2).  There is, of course, a fourth option:  if the "Turn Off Autoplay" policy is not enabled or not configured, Autoplay will NOT be disabled on any device.

Your video or still camera is treated the same as an external USB drive.  I believe -- but haven't specifically checked -- that any USB-connected storage device (whether a hard drive, a flash memory stick, a camera, a memory card, or any other type of device) is considered to be a "removable drive."  Thus, if Autorun is disabled for anything, it will be disabled for all such devices.

I don't know what you mean by a problem with double-clicking on a device with Autorun.inf.

360 License Transfer Ghost Error - Microsoft Community

stephenrouse say:

360 License Transfer Ghost Error

Ok, I checked the forums and noticed that something I brought to CS's attention had not been posted as far as I know. So I'm gonna post this here just to make sure that people know about this. Now I don't know for sure if people know about the error that occurs during a license transfer; but it is a system error that prevents the profile you want to transfer from, from being downloaded. The reason I call this a Ghost Error is because it's not actually an Xbox Live error nor is it committed by Xbox Live; the following paragraph will instruct you on the work around I discovered to prevent this error from occurring. 

If you recall the fact that your 360 account is linked to your email service provider, then you know that when someone tries to access your email account from a foreign computer or "device" that the email service provider will require proof that the person attempting access is actually yourself. License Transferring is in essence, you accessing another person's email, but the email service provider's security program interferes with your downloading of the other's account by attempting to ask you for email verification through Xbox Live.  

Understanding this all you have to do is simple; first you must attempt to access the other individual's email directly through the service provider on your Xbox console. When you get the email verification request you must click the request verification code button and type in the alternate email (If one exists) belonging to the individual you want to Transfer from. Second, once your compatriot accesses his alternate email, he needs to quickly send you the verification code ( without typographical errors obviously ) due to the possibility of an expiration time limit on the code itself. By the time you receive the verification code you should have backed out back to the email provider's sign in screen.

Now you try to sign into your friend's/associate's email again and once again get the verification request form; to which this time you will click "I already have a code" followed by typing in the verification code you were given minutes ago. Once you access that individual's email account, your Xbox 360 becomes what email provider's call a "recognized device". This work around works one hundred percent of the time and to test it to make sure it does; you can go to the sign in screen on xbox.com and attempt to sign into your friend's/ associate's account. If it lets you into his account on xbox.com it means you followed my instructions correctly, and you will be able to go through the License Transfer without any error stopping you in the middle of the downloading process.
