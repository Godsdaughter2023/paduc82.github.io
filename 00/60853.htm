    I already fixed my issue,
    it seems like on default, Windows 8 and 8.1 were using the Microsoft version of both my Wifi and my Video card drivers.

    most of the time, Windows automatically replaces the installed Manufacturer's driver with Microsoft versions of its driver when applying Windows Updates. Hence the correct version of my drivers were being replaced and does not work correctly. The Update needs to be manually turned-off or unselected during Update.

    so what i did was to override it manually with the drivers provided by the Manufacturer and it worked.

    for the sake of everybody experiencing similar problem heres what i did;

    1. GO to device manager,
    2. Right click on the device and choose "Update software driver"
    3. Choose 'Browse my computer for drive software'
    4. Instead of specifying the path to the driver, click on "Let me pick from the list..."
    5. Assuming you have installed the drivers from your manufacturer, then the driver (not the microsoft version) will appear on the lists.
    6. select the driver EVEN if Windows flags an error that the specified driver is not Certified. As long as its the correct driver from the manufacturer it'll work.
