In these cases, the smartphone or an Android model will influence, since smartphones not only have this utility, they also offer many other applications that can be very useful for the user. This in turn, through your device, allows the download of applications, games and a host of programs.

Knowing a little about this, follow these steps to get a good synchronization of your android to your PC with Windows 10.

Steps to follow:

• To start synchronizing Android with Windows 10, the first thing you should do is go to your computer and from the search bar type "Phone add-on" to find the application that will allow you to synchronize.

• You will find several alternatives for mobile devices, you must click on the Android option to carry out the process.

• Once you have selected the Android option, two windows will open automatically. You must log in with your Microsoft account, in case you do not have any you can create it at this time.

• Remember that if you create your account the system will ask you to confirm your email address from your mail, this step is very important because if you do not, the system will not allow you to continue with the process.

• If you already have your account created and the user and password are saved, it is very likely that the system allows you to enter directly to the next step.

• Once you have logged in you will see all the available options to synchronize Android with Windows 10. You have the possibility to synchronize your photos, task lists, your Skype, Word or Excel in addition to your email or calendar, you will only have to decide what you want to synchronize for the procedure to start.

• When you have chosen the service you want to synchronize the system will ask you to enter your email address again, the same with which you have logged in. Once the scribes press next.


Depending on the alternative you have chosen to synchronize your Android with Windows 10, it may be necessary to download an add-on to your tablet or mobile.

For example in the case of wanting to synchronize the images on your computer with your mobile devices, the system will ask you to log in to OneDrive, therefore it is important that you have this application downloaded to your tablet or smartphone.

Once you have downloaded the app and logged in, then you can check the box indicating that you have logged in and press the next button to continue the process.

Now you just have to make sure that the camera's charge is active in OneDrive, this can be done from the option of OneDrive Settings / Options / Activate the load.

Once done, press the box and hit the next button. That easy! The photos of your Android are already synchronized with your Windows 10 computer, so whenever you want to see one or use them, it will be enough to go to the OneDrive folder from your computer to access them.

All the alternatives offered by the system can be synchronized in the same way, the only difference is that the requested application must be installed in each of the cases.

  1. Open up the Charms Bar by moving your mouse to the upper right or lower right corner. Touch users should swipe in from the right edge of the screen. Click Settings on the Charms Bar.

  1. Click Update and Recovery from the available options.

  1. On the screen that follows click Recovery to select the recovery options screen.

  1.  The recovery options in the right hand pane from top to bottom are Refresh, Reset, and Advanced startup. Click on Get Started under Refresh your PC without affecting your files.

  1.  You will immediately see the following screen.

  1. After a short period of time you’ll see the following screen if you are using Windows 8, or if you have created a custom refresh image in Windows 8 or Windows 8.1.

  1.  Windows will explain what is about to happen. Notice they claim your settings and install desktop programs will be lost, which is true if you are a Windows 8 user and have not created a custom refresh image. After a few short moments you’ll be back at the Start Screen and your system will have returned to the state it was in when you created the image – including all desktop programs, Metro apps, documents, and settings. All in all it’s a reasonably quick and painless operation that all Windows 8 (and 8.1) users should be aware of!
