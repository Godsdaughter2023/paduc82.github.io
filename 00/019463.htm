How to obtain a new product key for a Microsoft Office 2010 suite, Lost Product Key support – lost key

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/823570

You need 2 things to install Office, your unique personal 25 character “Product Key” and the generic Office installation file / disk for the matching Office bundle.

The above link describes the formal MS support available.

To summarize the article, it depends on how you bought Office:

If you have a 1-computer (online download), “PKC” (Product Key Card), “MLK” (Medialess Kit), “OEM” (factory installed and Activated) type license you cannot transfer the license to a new computer. It is only good on the first computer it is installed on and stays permanently with the computer when it is sold or scrapped.

If you have a 2-computer or 3-computer for Home and Student only (online download), “FPP” (online download), Boxed retail CD/DVD (which is usually the”FPP” type), these types of license may be transferred to new comptuers as required.

john mcghie say:

Hi Thomas:

The command is called "GoTo" and it can be accessed two ways:

1)  Edit>Find>GoTo

2)  Via the Browse buttons at the bottom right corner of the Word window.

I don't think there's any way to get to it from the mostly-useless ribbon.  At least in Mac Word you can close the ribbon to get it out of the way!

Now, to get GoTo working for you, you need to know that a cross-reference is a "Field".

On the Edit menu, choose Find>GoTo and choose "Field".  Now where it says "Any Field" drop down the list and choose "REF".

Click Next or Back to go to the next or previous cross reference in the text. You may now close this dialog, or leave it open to use again.  If you choose to close it, you can use the "Browse" command.

Having "pre-loaded" the GoTo command this way, what you specified has now been loaded into the Browse facility at the bottom right corner of the Word window.  You can click the upwards arrow to go back to the previous cross-reference, or the downwards arrow to go to the next.  To change what you are going to, click the round button in the middle.

Browse (and GoTo) are two very powerful editing functions that of course have not made it into the help.

Yet another example that separates real word-processors such as Word from the irritatingly feeble webbified online versions that pretend to have the same capability.

Hope this helps
