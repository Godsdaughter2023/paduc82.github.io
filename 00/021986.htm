I just realized an apparent contradiction of terms in your question.

On the one hand, you speak of "rounding  to the nearest  nickel", which suggests rounding up or down   based on proximity to multiples of 0.05.

On the other hand, you mention ROUNDUP; and your example does indeed  round up   29.915 to the next higher  0.05 (29.95), not to the nearest 0.05 (29.90).

=ROUND(A1*A2/0.05,0)*0.05

I believe we can eschew ROUND(A1*A1,2) in that case.

jay freedman say:
Apply a style to that one content control that is not used anywhere else in the document. It can be one of the built-in styles or a style that you create, and it can have the same formatting as the rest of the document -- the only critical part is that it has a different name from the styles used in the rest of the document.

Then, click in the header pane and, on the Header & Footer Tools tab, check the box for Different First Page. Go to the header on the second page and insert a StyleRef field that contains the name of the style applied to the one content control.

For more information, read http://wordfaqs.mvps.org/StyleRef.htm.
