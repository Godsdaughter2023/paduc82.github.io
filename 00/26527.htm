You can pin just about anything you want to Start: apps, pictures, songs, map locations, favorite websites, OneNote notes, and even contacts. When you pin a contact to Start, you'll get all of that person's feed updates right from that tile, and it's like speed dial: it takes only two taps to make a phone call.

 

To pin an app

1.      On Start, flick left to the App list.

2.      Tap and hold the app you want to pin, then tap Pin to Start.

 

To pin a contact

1.      On Start, tap People, then flick to All.

2.      Do one of the following:

 

·        Tap and hold the contact you want to pin, then tap Pin to Start.

·        Tap the contact you want to pin, then tap Pin .

 

To pin a website

1.      Open the website you want to pin.

2.      Tap More , then tap Pin to Start.

 

To pin a location

1.      On Start, flick left to the App list, then tap Maps.

2.      Type the location you want to find.

3.      Tap the flag that identifies the location, then tap Pin .

 

To pin an album

1.      On Start, flick left to the App list, then tap Music + Videos .

2.      Under Zune, tap Music.

3.      Tap the album you want to pin.

4.      Tap and hold the album art, and then tap Pin to Start.

 

Tips:

·        If you'd like to move a tile somewhere else on Start, tap and hold the tile, then move it wherever you'd like.

To remove a tile, tap and hold it, and then tap Unpin.

Calculating Profit/Loss and Open Positions/Inventory - Microsoft Community

sphinx123 say:

Calculating Profit/Loss and Open Positions/Inventory

Below, trading history of a particular symbol.  Some shares were bought.  some sold and then some more bought.  I need to know the profit/loss earned from the shares sold and amount of shares still not sold. Sequence column is of no value to me.  please feel free to add a column for referencing if required.

 

 
lars-Åke aspelin say:

Below, trading history of a particular symbol.  Some shares were bought.  some sold and then some more bought.  I need to know the profit/loss earned from the shares sold and amount of shares still not sold. Sequence column is of no value to me.  please feel free to add a column for referencing if required.

 

 







































































































This is best solved by a macro, but here is one way how it can be solved by worksheet formulas:

I assume that the first bought stocks are always the first that are sold.

If the trading history is in Sheet1 with Buy/Sell in Column A, Trd.Qty in Column B and Trd.Price in Column C, all starting on row 2 (row 1 being the header row), put the following formulas inanother sheet, Sheet2 :

In cell A2: =(Sheet1!A2="Buy")*Sheet1!B2

Copy cell A2 down as many rows as you have rows in your trading history

In cell B1: =OFFSET(Sheet1!$A$1,COLUMNS($B:B),)

In cell B2: =IF(AND(B$1="Sell",A2<>0),MAX(0,A2-OFFSET(Sheet1!$B$1,COLUMNS($B:B),)-SUM(B$1:B1)+SUM(A$1:A1)),A2)

Copy cell B2 down as far as you did for column A.

Copy column B cross as many columns as you have rows in your trading history.

With the trading history from your example, Sheet2 will now look like this:

 

The non-zero rows of this overview shows the number of remaining stocks from each "Buy" after each "Sell". Example: After the second Sell (Column G) all stocks from the first 3 Buys have been sold and 64 stocks from the 4th Buy remains.

Now go back to Sheet 1 and enter the following:

In cell F1: Inventory

In cell G1: Profit/Loss

In cell F2: =SUMIF(A$2:A2,"Buy",B$2:B2)-SUMIF(A$2:A2,"Sell",B$2:B2)

In cell G2: =N(G1)+IF(A2="Sell",SUMPRODUCT((C2-C$2:C$100)*(OFFSET(Sheet2!A$2:A$100,,ROWS($2:2)-1)))-SUMPRODUCT((C2-C$2:C$100)*(OFFSET(Sheet2!A$2:A$100,,ROWS($2:2)))),0)

Copy cells F2 and G2 down as far as you have data in columns A to E.

The last row of column F will hold the Inventory, 240 in your example, and the last row of column G will hold the accumulated Profit/Loss, 1159.53 in your example.
If you want the Profit/Loss for each Sell rather than the accumulated Profit/Loss, just skip the N(G1) part of the formula in cell G2.

a. user say:

Hello Masamune3210:

 

Thank you for posting to the forum regarding your Xbox issue.  This may end up being an issue that is related more to your Xbox then Xbox Music and you may need to seek additional support here, http://forums.xbox.com/?xr=shellnav.  With that said, I will provide a couple thoughts on what may be happening and things you can try that may resolve this type of issue.

 

First, I will address the playing of content via your iPod and possibly the issue with music from your PC as well.  When you connect a device to a Xbox 360 you will actually be using the system media player and not Xbox Music.  The best troubleshooting I can give you for this is to install or uninstall then install the optional media update.  If you go to your system settings and then to storage > system items this update will be listed if installed.  If you do find this, delete it and then go to the marketplace and search out "optional media update".  Install this update, restart your Xbox and try playing your music again.  If the content from your player is not playing properly the next thing I might consider is deleting all content from the iPod, reloading that content and then connecting the player to the Xbox again.

 

When it comes to playing your music from a computer to the Xbox there may be more at play here.  If the option media update steps above do not resolve the issue then this could be connection related.  Whether you use Zune, Media Center or Xbox Music to play music to your Xbox you will be streaming this music.  This process will require a sound connection to our servers and a connection that is being interrupted could lead to this type of experience.  I might suggest you try a full power cycle of your router and modem by shutting them down for 5 minutes and then rebooting.  I might also suggest you remove the connection you currently have set up between the media player on your computer being used to send music to your Xbox and the Xbox.  Once you break this down, set it back up and try again.

 

Please let us know if you  have any other questions or concerns we can assist you with.

 

Mark W.
