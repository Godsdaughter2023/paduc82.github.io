You can use a combination of PrintScreen (keyboard shortcut) and the freeware version of IrfanView to create images to be displayed in Microsoft forums etc. IrfanView can be downloaded here:
http://www.irfanview.com/ 

 

To create an image press the PrintScreen key to place the image in your Clipboard. Open IrfanView and select Edit, Paste. More often than not you do not want the whole screen, as it contains unnecessary detail and a smaller image enables the creation of a magnified view of the selection you wish to display. The steps that follow enable you to quickly edit the image.

 

Place the cursor on the top left corner of the area you wish to select. Depress and hold down the left mouse button and drag the cursor vertically down the left side of your selection and then horizontally to the right along the bottom of your selection until you reach the bottom right corner of the selected area. Release the left mouse button and select Edit, Cut –area outside of the selection. Select Edit, Crop selection (Cut out).

 

To post images in the Microsoft Community forums they need to be no more than 630 pixels wide so select Image, Resize/Resample. Check the boxes before Preserve aspect ratio (proportional) and Apply sharpen after Resample. These changes become defaults so that you do not need to apply them when creating subsequent images. Under Set new size you will see the existing size. Change the width to 630 and click on OK. The height automatically adjusts.

 

To save your image select File, Save as. My preference is for JPG Format.

 

To insert your image in a post to the Microsoft forum click the button second from the right on the Reply toolbar, click the Browse button to locate the saved image and click the Upload button.

 

This approach is more involved than using the Snipping tool but can produce better results in many situations.

Sometimes an error occurs on your machine and you are immediately hit by a error. The only problem is the screen flashes by so quickly you rarely get to see what may have caused the error in the first place. Once we get to know the error we would be in a better position to troubleshoot on the issue.

 

First we will try to disable automatic restart so that we get the complete error. Follow the steps to disable it.

 

1. Click Start button

2. From Start menu Click Control panel

3. In Control Panel Click the System icon

4. In the System Window look on the Left hand side of the window and you will see a list of options. Click on the Advanced System Settings option

5. Because Computer management requires administrative privileges you may find the the User Account Control will pop up. If it does then enter the required information (if you are not the administrator) or, if you are the administrator, click the Continue button

6. In the Advanced Settings window look for the Startup and Recovery section and then Click the Settings button

7. In the Startup and recovery window look for the section marked System failure and remove the 'tick' mark from the 'Automatically Restart' option. Now press OK button

8. Finally click the OK button on the System properties Window and then close the system window.

 

Let us know the error message you get after you resume from standby.

 

You can try the Microsoft article which fixes most issues with standby

 

How to troubleshoot performance issues with standby, hibernate, and resume in Windows Vista:

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/950686

To restore the operating system to an earlier point in time, follow these steps:

 

1. Click Start, type system restore in the Start Search box, and then click System Restore in the Programs list.If you are prompted for an administrator password or confirmation, type your password or click Continue.

2. In the System Restore dialog box, click Choose a different restore point, and then click Next.

3. In the list of restore points, click a restore point that was created before you began to experience the issue, and then click Next.

4. Click Finish.

 

The computer restarts, and the system files and settings are returned to the state that they were in at the time that the restore point was created.

System Restore: frequently asked questions
