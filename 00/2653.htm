In Outlook 2010, the ‘autocomplete’ list of contacts with email addresses is totally divorced from the Contacts list.

Since you are using an Exchange account for email (almost all Hotmail users have been migrated to Exchange-based Outlook Mail), the autocomplete list is synched between most clients such as Outlook.

It is possible your Outlook 2010 is linked to Exchange via EAS* instead of 'full' Exchange protocols (EAS is the default after migration). If so you should delete your mail account in Outlook and then recreate it. 

To delete the autocomplete list in Outlook 2010 and anything synched to it:

File / Options / Mail / Send messages …   and then click on the Empty Auto-complete List bar on the right, and let Outlook synch with Outlook Mail.

 

Whether this will synch (via Exchange EAS) to Outlook Mobile I know not.

 

* - the lightweight protocol used by mobiles to talk to Exchange

Apparently, those are not duplicates.  It's just a copy of the original mail from the inbox.

If you delete the mail in the inbox, it deletes the ones in the other folders as well.

If you delete the copy, let’s say, from the "Important" folder; the original mail in the inbox remains in the inbox.  However, if you delete the mail from the "All Mail" folder, it'll delete the original mail in the inbox along with the copy shown in the "Important" folder.

 

I'm pretty sure that the Outlook is very similar to the Windows Live Mail.  If you have your Gmail folders such as "Unread Mail," "All Mail," and "Important" shown, it'll show up as duplicates in the Outlook mail.

 

My sanity has gone down to normal level and avoided the coo-coo bin!

You have two areas on the E71 which cause "flashing", the first is the notifications...

This can be found under:-

Tools | Settings | General | Personalisation | Notification light.

This will let you know when you've had txt msgs, calls etc..

The other (Breathing light) is found under:-

Tools | Profiles | (select yr profile) General | Personalise | Breathing light (on/off)...

If you switch off the "breathing light" then when the screen drops into power saving it will display a clock screensaver.
