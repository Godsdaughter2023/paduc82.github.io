Word for Mac 15.20 and Hebrew - Microsoft Community

dgar1113 say:

Word for Mac 15.20 and Hebrew

Many thanks to the team for releasing Word for Mac 15.20 with right-to-left capability. As some have noted, you need to select Hebrew (or Arabic) as a system language in Mac system preferences for this to work. However, I have found two problems:

  • Word for Mac R2L only works with a Hebrew keyboard that is part of the pre-installed system from Apple, such as "Hebrew–QWERTY". There are other Hebrew keyboards out there. For example, one that is popular in biblical studies is called "Hebrew Keyboard-SIL," and it does not work properly with Word for Mac (it works properly with Word for Windows).
  • Some Hebrew fonts do not display properly. The font "SBL Hebrew" is popular in biblical studies, but diacriticals do not position correctly in Word. Other fonts are fine in Word for Mac, such as the Hebrew capability built into Times New Roman, Arial, and so forth.

Note that these problems are unique to Word for Mac. Other Mac programs that do Hebrew (such as Apple Pages) work fine with the "Hebrew Keyboard-SIL" and fonts such as "SBL Hebrew." I realize this is the first release of R2L in Office for Mac. I hope improvements will be made to address the above issues.

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
How does the sheet display in Page Layout View? Also, in Normal View where do the dashed blue Page Break lines appear? If they aren't displayed go to Excel> Preferences> View to check the box for Show Page Breaks.

You may need to recheck your column widths... Keep in mind that even though no cell in the column contains more than a single skinny character & that there may appear to be 3" of available space, if the column width is 3.01" its content will be forced to the next page.

On the Layout tab of the Ribbon, check the Print group for the Fit To & the Scale settings.

Are you using any Headers/Footers? They can impose on the actual amount of space available for sheet content. [Although that normally occurs vertically it can come into play when using Landscape.]

Additionally, check all of the settings in the Print dialog, including those on the Paper Handling page selectable from the Copies & Pages list. Confirm that the Destination Paper Size jives with what your document is intended for, that Scale to fit... isn't checked, etc.

If everything checks out but the issue persists confirm that you have both OS X & Office fully updated. And -- equally as important -- check the mfr's web site for an update to the driver software for your printer. It's responsible for virtually everything provided by OS X print services... Programs such as Excel merely send the layout dimensions & data to be printed.
