I agree, I would classify a change in size as a bug. Microsoft's attention to detail seems to be on the decline.

I'm glad you found the post informative.

An EMF file can have a larger file size if the data has many curves. One example might be a legal disclaimer that a client wants to be unalterable. You can convert the text to curves and create and EMF, but it will be much larger than an indexed color PNG the same dimensions.

If you access your account from a location more than 300 miles from home it will look like someone else is using your account. You must be able to provide enough correct info to prove that it is your account before Microsoft will unlockthe account

The message "You haven't signed in from this location recently." will occur if you are signing in from a location more than 300 miles from your normal location. The Microsoft account service will prompt you for an additional challenge to complete the log in. Codes can be the date of birth, or receiving a code sent to your SMS number or alternate email if configured.


To prevent this you will need to configure your computer/mobile device as a trusted device

Given the new security measures, Account Settings requires an extra security, through the use of a security code.  Also known as 2 step authentication

If you mark your computer, phone or tablet as a Trusted Device, you won't be asked for any codes when you access these settings in the future.  To do this

1. Login your account here.

2.  Under Security & Privacy  Click on "Manage Advanced Security"

3. You will see the "protect your account" page. Select any option to get a code, and request for it.

4. You will see a checkbox that says : "I sign in frequently on this device. Don't ask me for a code". Tick the checkbox, and enter the code.

Your computer/device is now Trusted. Whenever you use your account on that specific computer/device, you will no longer asked for a code.
