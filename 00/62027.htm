trevor j lawrence say:

2 days means that Windows is giving you two days in which to do the restart at a time convenient to you.  If you don't do it yourself, Windows will force the restart in two days time.

 

To do a restart, Open the Charm Bar (with touch, swipe in from the right edge, or with a mouse, move the mouse to the lower right corner, then slide up  bit).  Select Settings, then Power.  You will be given around three options, including Restart.

 

As other have mentioned it shouldn't take long.  Depending on how many updates are queued it can take anything from a minute or two to 15-30 minutes.  Since this appears to be the first update set since you initially fired up the machine, there may be a number of updates and it may take towards the longer end of the time range.

 

It is possible you will get one or two more sets of updates over the next few days until everything is completely up to date.  After that you should normally expect updates to occur once a month, since Microsoft bunches important security updates together and releases them on the 2nd Tuesday of the month, the so-called Patch Tuesday

To fix the lock screen issue, we suggest that you access the Windows Recovery Environment. To do this, you can either press F8 while your computer is booting or restart the device a couple of times.
