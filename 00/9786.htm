How to send a certified email - Microsoft Community

essvee say:

How to send a certified email

I tried to send an email account that receives only certified email. When I sent email, I received the following reply from the email id.

Message rejected by the system . The boxes indicated below target only receive e-mail messages are certified or service boxes reserved for the manager *** Email address is removed for privacy ***- box that receives only Certified Email Final -Recipient : Action : failed Status: 5.0.0 ( box that receives only Certified Mail )

Can any one help?

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:

A digital signature is a cryptographic method for assuring a recipient that a message they've received that appears to come from you actually did come from you and is exactly as you sent it, without modification. It also allows others to send messages to you that only you can open and read. Both functions are handled via a digital certificate.

Digital certificates for email are issued by Certification Authorities, companies whose job is to verify to other people that you are who you say you are. More well known CAs are Verisign (Symantec), DigiCert, and Thawte (also Symantec). One Certification Authority that will issue a free email certificate is Comodo.

All that said, nothing in what you said originally promises that what I've described has anything to do with what the owner of the mailbox you described considers "certified mail". I made the tie-in based on the word "certification". It could also mean that you must register your address with the recipient before that person (or automated mailbox) will accept a message from you. This is known as a Challenge/Response system. If you send a message for the first time to a mailbox using challenge/response, you'll get back another message containing some way to attest that your address is not a spambot. You'll get a link or a reply address that you click or respond to and that will "certify" your address. Subsequent messages will get through.

So, it's really anyone's guess what "certified" means as you first described it and that's why I suggest you contact the recipient in some other way first to determine just exactly what they're requiring of you.
