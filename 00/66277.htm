brb4ever say:

I had the same problem, except that "This PC" didn't take 2-3 minutes but rather NEVER showed anything. To solve it, I did the following, which I found by searching other threads on this site (or maybe some other site):

1. Hit Windows+X to reveal whatever that menu is called and choose Control Panel.

2. In Control Panel, there is a little drop-down menu that says View by: Category by default. Change it to View by: Large Icons or View by: Small Icons (it doesn't matter which).

3. Choose Indexing Options.

4. A new window will pop up showing any hard drives you have, Internet Explorer history, offline files, and Start Menu. Click the Modify button below these.

5. Another window will pop up. Select all of the choices for drives and folders to be indexed. Click OK.

Once you've made your choices, Windows will immediately start indexing your files. The time it takes will depend on the number of files. I have 5 hard drives in my computer, and this process took at least a couple of hours. Afterward, I no longer had the issue of "This PC" being completely blank and not ever doing anything. A caveat: It has only been two days since I found this apparent fix, so I can't be sure that in the long term this is the solution to this problem (or that it's the solution to your particular case).

Hope that helps.
