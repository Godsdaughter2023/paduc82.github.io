Use "Recovery Media Creator" to create a USB recovery drive - Microsoft Community

trybird say:

Use "Recovery Media Creator" to create a USB recovery drive

If you run into problems with your PC, a USB recovery drive can help you troubleshoot and fix those problems, even if your PC won't start.


Your PC might have come with a recovery image that’s used to refresh or reset your PC. The recovery image is stored on a dedicated recovery partition on your PC, and is typically 3-6 GB in size. Windows 10 includes a built-in tool which is named "Recovery Media Creator" to create a USB recovery drive. Windows will let you know how big the recovery partition is, and you'll need a USB flash drive at least that big.


Warning
Creating a recovery drive will erase anything already stored on your USB flash drive. Use an empty USB flash drive or make sure to transfer any important data from your USB flash drive to another storage device before using it to create a USB recovery drive.

Follow these steps to operate:


1.
Run
Recovery Media Creator
%WinDir%\System32\RecoveryDrive.exe


tap or click the Cortana's search box which tips the words "Search the web and Windows", and type or paste "recoverydrive" in there, and then tap or click on the words which reveal on the Cortana's pop-up parts:
recoverydrive
Run command
or
Settings
Create a recovery drive

Then tap or click "Yes" button, then the "Recovery Media Creator" is running.

2.
After the recovery drive tool opens, make sure the Copy the recovery partition from the PC to the recovery drive check box is selected, and then tap or click Next.


Note
The checkbox is greyed out when your PC doesn’t have a recovery partition. The recovery drive will include only the recovery tools and a bootable image, but not a recovery image to use for refreshing or resetting your PC.


3.
Insert a USB flash drive into your PC that is at least as large as the size indicated on the screen.

4.
Tap or click the USB drive you would like to use for your recovery drive, then tap or click Next.

5.
Tap or click Create.


The recovery image and necessary recovery tools will be copied to your USB flash drive, which will take a while, depending on your PC and the size of the recovery image.

6.
Tap or click Finish.


7.
Remove the USB flash drive.


This is now your Windows 10 recovery drive, and you'll need it if you ever need to refresh or reset your PC. Keep it in a safe place and don't use it to store other files or data.

Some of my sways disappeared

What's the possible cause for this?

Is there a way I can get them back?

Thanks in advance for your help

Sarah

jason (office engineer) say:

Hi Sarah,

There are a few possible explanations for this:

  • You have more Sways than can fit on the page - try clicking the More button in the lower right until you can't show anymore to see if they are there.
  • The Sways were deleted - try checking the Deleted tab to see if you can find and recover them.
  • The Sways were created by someone else and you were a coauthor - if this was the case, they would have sent you an edit link and you would have had to have opened it and chosen to edit them.  If this is the case, these Sways are completely under the control of the owner who can choose to delete them or change the URL on them if they want which will cause them not to show in your list anymore.  Another possibility in this case, is that the owner's account was deleted which would also cause the Sways to go away.
  • The Sways were created in another account - when you go to sway.office.com, it will automatically sign you in as the last user you signed into an Office app or email with.  You should try logging in as every email address you have (each time checking the … menu to make sure it signed in as who you expected) to make sure they are not in an unexpected account.

If you don't think it's any of these, then we can dig a little deeper into it from our side.  Knowing the URL to one or both Sways would make it much easier.

Jason
