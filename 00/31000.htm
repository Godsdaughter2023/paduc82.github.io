jannine_p say:

Hello

Welcome to Skype Community forum!

Microsoft accounts are email accounts created under Outlook, Live, MSN and Hotmail domain.

Due to the migration of Skype and Microsoft last October 2016, Microsoft announced that they are blocking the ability to create a new personal Microsoft account using a work/school email address. On your case, you're still able to sign in with the account because you've created it before the migration of Skype and Microsoft. However, the primary alias should be a Microsoft account, because, it will only accept a Microsoft account to be set as primary sign in to log in to Skype.

To add an alias to the account:

  1. Sign in to the account.live.com. (e.g. abc@someplace.com)

  2. Select Your info.

  3. Select Manage how you sign in to Microsoft.

  4. Select Add email.

  5. Follow the instructions to set up and verify your new alias. If you chose Add email, you have the option to use an email address that you already have, or you can create a new Outlook.com email address.

To change which alias is primary:

  1. Sign in to the account.live.com. (e.g. abc@someplace.com)

  2. Select Your info.

  3. Select Manage how you sign in to Microsoft.

  4. Select Make primary next to the alias you want as your primary. 

Once done, adding and setting the new primary alias at account.live.com, log on at www.skype.com. Scroll down you mouse until you see Account details > Settings and preferences > Edit Profile. Scroll down to the Contact details section and click Add email address and set it as primary email. In the box that appears, type your new email address and click the green Save button at the bottom of the page.

Once done setting the email address on the Microsoft and Skype website, your log in to Skype will be the new primary email address and your old password. Also, changing of ISP doesn't affect the log in to your Skype account.

Please let us know if you have any questions or other concerns.

Thank you!

The Quick Launch bar and the open programs have dotted dividers. Hover the mouse cursor on the left dotted divider of the Quick Launch bar until a double headed arrow appears, left click and drag it up, it should snap to the top border.  Do the same on the left dotted divider of the  open programs, but this time drag it to the right then pull it up.
