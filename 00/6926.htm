bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

Well, think of it as actually making text breaks.

Word documents are a flow of text from start to finish. The document comprises one or more Sections & Sections consist of paragraphs. How many sheets of paper are required to print the document depends on a number of variables, such as font size, line spacing, margins, paper size, etc. IOW, without adding or removing any content you readily can change how many 'pages long' a document is by modifying any or all of those variables.

If you're curious enough you might want to review this article... especially if you intend to use the program with minimal frustration: Word Doesn’t Know What a Page Is

The program is designed to present the content as though it were being typed on sheets of paper because that's what Microsoft believe users want to see. The volume of code required to do that is a major portion of what causes Word to occupy so much disk space & exhibit many of its perceived performance issues. If you want to see the document in more the same way Word does, switch to Draft View :-)
