estelahernandez1 say:

How to undo messed permission levels in SharePoint Online

Split from this thread.

Hi Jim, I have read all the warnings not to mess with the permission levels. It's too late though and as you said "I am having a bad time." How do I undo it? I think that is what OP was asking. How do we undo it?

alex wang msft say:
Hi Estela,

Basically, most of the permissions in SharePoint Online inherit from a higher level. List inherit permissions from the site, folders inherit permissions from the library.

If you changed the permissions of the item under the site collection, you must break the inheritance and assigned unique permission. If that is the case, the solution is simple, you could make it inherit permissions again. Here is how: 
If it's a library > the gear button > Library settings > Permissions for this document library > Delete Unique permissions


If you have edited the permission in the site collection level, there is no direct solution to undo it. You could change it back by editing the permissions individually. The default permissions are assigned like this:
Owners > Full control
Members > Edit
Visitors > Read
If you have edited the permission levels of the site collection, you could edit it again and change it back.

For more information, you could read this article: Understanding permission levels in SharePoint

Changing Report Widths - Microsoft Community

jjv43 say:

Changing Report Widths

I am working with Access 2003.  Most of the reports I have created work the way they are supposed to.  However, a couple of my reports are giving me an error that states that the report width is larger than the page width.  When I change the borders to .25 inches all around, the margins change back to large margins when I run the report.  I can't see what is different with these few reports compared to the other reports that are running correctly.
boblarson say:
I am working with Access 2003.  Most of the reports I have created work the way they are supposed to.  However, a couple of my reports are giving me an error that states that the report width is larger than the page width.  When I change the borders to .25 inches all around, the margins change back to large margins when I run the report.  I can't see what is different with these few reports compared to the other reports that are running correctly.

It isn't necessarily the margins, so-to-speak.  But your report area in Design View shows a ruler at the top and left side.  The width of those does NOT include margins.  So, if you have an 8" wide paper and your report in design view is at, or close to 8" then it will give you that error.  For quarter inch margins the design view report would need to be 7" or less.  And as some printers won't work with a quarter inch margin on one side (sometimes it needs like .34 inches instead) that can be a problem still.  So try making it slightly under the width of the design width and the margins.
