If you have one of the following AV, anti-virus, programs installed, it will shut down your Windows Defender. Reason being those AVs have their own defenders. 2 defenders running in the same machine will create conflicts.
In other words, you don't need Windows defender if you have one of the AVs listed below installed.

List of AVs that will shut down Windows Defender :
Norton
McAfee
AVG
Microsoft Security Essentials
 

If you do not have the above AV programs, here is how to restart Windows Defender:

Start button > in the search box, typeServices > press Enter key > scroll to find Windows Defender, click it > ....
under Status heading, it should say ......... Started
under Startup Type, it should say ............ Automatic
If it does not say that, Right click at Windows Defender > click Properties >
at Startup Type, click the pointer at far right and select Automatic
at Service Status, click Start
click OK when done.

My Prediction for Xbox One Marketing 2013 - Microsoft Community

ma8688 say:

My Prediction for Xbox One Marketing 2013

I just want something on record. Please note the keyword here: "Prediction"

.

  • Xbox was launched in November 2001. Microsoft strategy was to take over the living room environment. They wanted a gaming console to put their foot into the market.
  • Xbox Live (XBL) was launched in November 2002. Microsoft strategy to introduce online services for gaming proved to be a incredibly key feature and success story.
  • Xbox 360 was launched in November 2005. It was a far more successful console than its predecessor  and thanks to XBL, started a incredibly robust digital environment for digital consumption (games, dlcs, movies, tv shows and music) within the video gaming industry.
  • Kinect was launched in November 2010 as a peripheral addition to the Xbox platform. It became one of the fastest selling peripheral of all time with its promise of motion gaming and interaction.
  • XBL during 2005-2013 evolved into a distribution platform in its own right. This is ultimately what Microsoft is selling now. And it's no longer just for the gamers. They're looking at the key tech demographic of 18-35 enthusiast. These are the same people who are purchasing content on iTunes, paying for a brand new smartphone with apps, subscribing to Netflix-like services and tied into a variety of ecosystems developed by Apple and Google. The average gamer is also between the age of 18-35. The average gamer also falls into these particular categories. The Xbox One also happens to provide video games.

.

Chronology:

.

May/June/July 2013. The product announcement and E3 focuses was to gauge the general opinion of the ardent tech community and specifically the traditional gaming crowd. As demonstrated in past upheavals (see Diablo 3 and Sim City), huge surges of negativity concerning DRM practices quietens down over the course of a few months. But they're never forgotten. It's also prudent not to fully announce any specific details in regards to plans not yet finalized. There's plenty of work to get publishers and retailers on board with a new business model.

.

Microsoft will simply take the flak from the very vocal gaming community until a time where it is perceived that emotions are no longer running high.

.

.

Nearer the release of the console, around August & September 2013, Microsoft will begin finalizing some details of business models. They will trickle specifics down through tech media outlets (Engadget, The Verge etc.) and internet gaming sites. Most importantly, they'll be sharing these information to specific gaming YouTube channels. The key component messages will be relating wholly to the gaming aspect of the machine in regards to the industry. A concrete launch list would also be expected around this time or by the end of October.

.

It's also possible that we will see heavy Q&A sessions and much more details about compelling arguments for their digital philosophy in regards to ownership rights and management of purchases (e.g. the Circle of 10, sharing/lending, renting and selling - things no other company has done in regards to digital content). Gamers will be able to decide with a lot more information at hand if they'd want to buy into this new philosophy.

.

Ultimately, the message of a "All in One" box must be established into a gamer's mind in order to understand this philosophy. Arguments like: "A video game console should be a video game console." should be equated to "A phone should just be a phone." Fine arguments and a right of any consumer to buy what they want. However, Microsoft will market the Xbox One as more than a video game console. Just like Samsung Galaxy S4 is more than just a phone.

.

.

October - December 2013. Microsoft's Xbox One marketing strategy will heavily target the core demographic of tech enthusiast. Most, if not all their advertisement for the Xbox One, will feature the non-gaming related functionality of the system: Kinect 2.0, Skype & Sports will play a heavy part in these advertisement.

.

Xbox One advertisement will just be about the system itself. Launch games adverts will just be about the launch games with targeted unique selling messages (e.g. Forza adverts will openly express the capability of Microsoft's Cloud computing in regards to player-like AI behaviour and learning).

.

The mentality attempted to be establish from these marketing campaigns: a video game console like the PS4 is a console for the teenage brat screaming at their parents to buy them a new toy. A multimedia box like the Xbox One is specifically designed for your 18-35 year old tech users of iTunes & Netflix.

.

However, that market of parents buying expensive toys for their children is incredibly lucrative and Microsoft certainly wouldn't want to miss out on that. Regardless of whether or not the 14 yea old wants the Xbox One, why would a parent pay $100 for essentially the same toy? So Microsoft will switch the actual target: The parents.

Marriage of Parental Locks capabilities with Kinect 2.0 technology as a viral advert on YouTube and social media. Example YouTube ad video: Kid is playing a videogame. Xbox One requires the Mom to unlock console with a passcode or facial recognition - this feature only available to the Xbox One because of the Kinect being able to recognise the mom's face and having parental lock authority which is very easy to set up once you buy the console. Distribute links of this viral ad to parent social networks and general/traditional media outlets that household mums may watch on TV who will pick up on this new feature like a forest fire.
