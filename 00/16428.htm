Insert one helper column, assumed it is D.  Now whatever column you want to sort, in D1 give this formula referring the cell reference to that column.

dan at it associates say:

Your company acquired what is a "classic counterfeit" of Office Professional 2010.

 

The packaging and materials are all designed to help the product pass themselves off as Office Professional 2010.  However, the installation bits pressed onto the installation disc are for a Volume-Licensing product called Office Professional Plus 2010.  In many cases you can spot the counterfeit product by looking very closely at the Certificate of Authenticity (CoA) and seeing that the security foil is behind the CoA rather than woven into it, and that the hologram on the disc is a very thin label affixed to the top surface of the disc rather than embedded within the plastic of the disc.  You might need a very sharp or pointy object (straight pin, razor blade, etc) to feel close to the edge of the disc to detect the edge of the label on the top surface.

 

The product key was generated by a program used by software pirates called a "KeyGen" (key generator).  It uses the same alorgithm that Microsoft used to generate Office Professional Plus 2010 product keys. From the info you have posted, it's unclear why the product key activated the first time around if it is "invalid."  Some counterfeits use product keys acquired thru the Microsoft Developer Network and since these are genuine keys, the product will activate, but because the counterfeiters then use the keys on hundreds or thousands of counterfeits, Microsoft soon sees them being abused and blocks them. This is a common practice with Windows 7 counterfeits.  Another explanation could be that the product key was legitimately issued to a customer, but the key was compromised and abused by the counterfeiters who acquired it, so again the product key was blocked.

 

Regardless of why, there is no question that your computer has a nongenuine installation of Office Professional (Plus) 2010.  Report this to your company so the responsible parties can pursue whatever remedies are available thru the supplier.

 

PS--One additional indicator---after installation but before activation, this product is on a 30-day Trial basis, which is typical of Office Professional Plus 2010.  The retail product Office Professional 2010 would be on a 60-day Trial after installation but before activation.
