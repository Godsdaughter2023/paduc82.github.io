tim liu msft say:

Hi George,

It seems that your wife gets a work/school account from her institution and she is able to use that work/school account to install Office 365 ProPlus on her computer. However, there is already an Office suite installed on your wife's computer.

If my assumption is correct, I'd like to explain some my personal experience:

1. Office suite from different plans may have different edition name, for example Office 365 ProPlus, Office 2016 standard, but their basic usage are the same (Office suite from Office 365 plan normally will have the latest updates and feature).

2. We say there are Microsoft account for personal services and work/school account for business services (work). "Work" or "home" is not decided by the Office suite but the account. We sign into Office clients with different kinds of these two accounts for personal or business(work) services.

Now, about some your questions:

1.how will it affect all our personal stuff on the computer i.e. our contacts, emails and all our documents. Will these be overwritten by all her work stuff or will they all be combined?

The key point is to make sure when you want to save files to cloud, choose the collect location with the collect account. Actually, we can easily see Microsoft account and work/school account related cloud services when using Office apps. So, when you want to open, save them, make sure the save location is correct. In Outlook, we can add that work/school account and the former Microsoft account, it's easily to see contacts, mails, calendars under each account.

2.If I create a separate profile on the Home PC which is running the latest version of Win 10 for her, will then Office 365 load separate version of all the Office Apps which will then require additional space on the HDD?

You don't have to install two Office suites on your computer. We also don't suggest customer to install two different editions of Office suite on the same computer. It will cause some unexpected errors. As we discussed above, the key point is the account not the Office suite. You may choose to use the former Office suite. And then just add work/school account to Office apps. It will let you be able to open, edit, save files from work.

You may also choose to use the Office suite from the work/school account, remove the former Office suite and install Office 365 ProPlus. Add your wife's Microsoft account to the Office suite. It also will let her using both home and business (work) services.

3.Or will Office 365 override the Office currently on the Home PC? If so will I then be able to use Office 365 with our personal stuff?

The same answer as question 2. If you choose to use the Office suite from work account, first you need to remove the former one and then install the new one. Just add your Microsoft as services in Office apps (in any Office app, click File>Account>Add a service). It will let you use both services during your daily work.

I hope my answers don't confuse you. A summary is that personal or work is related to the account not the Office suite. Office suite can be signed in (connected) with both consumer services or business services. We just need to make sure when we saving something, we select the proper cloud location with the correct account.

Best regards,

Tim
