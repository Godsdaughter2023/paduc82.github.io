¡firedog say:

I occasionally get mail intended for someone else with a gmail address. ...

I don't even have a gmail account.


If you don't have a Gmail account, where are you receiving these messages? You can only receive messages addressed to an email account that you own. If you're seeing messages that appear to be addressed to, say, jbloggs @ gmail.com but you're receiving them in an account called jbloggs @ hotmail.com, then your Hotmail address has been used as a BCC address by the sender. It's a common spammer trick. Delete the spam and forget about it.

As regards the 'legitimate' messages, the sender may have used a scattergun technique in the hope of hitting on the right address. Many people have accounts with different mail services using the same username part (the bit before the '@' symbol) - jbloggs @ gmail.com, jbloggs @ hotmail.com, jbloggs @ live.com, jbloggs @ yahoo.com and so on. The good-neighbourly thing to do in these cases is to reply to the message explaining the sender's mistake and asking him to remove your address from his address book.
