In the first edition of windows 10, the sound problem was hidden behind the following “hello“ problem which could be worked around by switching off fast startup too:

After shutdown and reboot windows “hello“ would start to try and identify by biometrics.

It would search and search until you logged on with PIN or otherwise which then led you to a restart. On this or any other restart, this would not happen, only on shutdown-startup. Simultaneously, the windows sound would not play, a fact that largely stayed unnoticed, as this is turned off by default anyway.

Fast startup depends on pre-activation/ -initialisation of several drivers and your profile, which of course includes biometric data - leftovers from the previous session.

On shutdown, some of this data somehow got lost, but the fast startup routine would of course not reinitialise them. Result: logon failure and no sound!

The problem was fully corrected around April or May with a firmware update and seems to have partially reappeared.

I suspect, the problem was not correctly identified at the time as most comments conentrared on “hello“ not working. It was probably solved “en passent“ and not systematically and has now reappeared in part.

Things like that have happened to me in the past, the phenomenon: “Oh super, it seems to work now. Only god knows why, but it does. Lets not touch it anymore ...“ Sounds familiar?

Give MS some time, it will probably be solved in a future firmware update!
