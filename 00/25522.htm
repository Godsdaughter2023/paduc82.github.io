david a matson say:

Greetings Priscilla! Welcome to the Windows Phone forums!

If your girlfriend can access her Windows Phone account (windowsphone.com), then she can use the Find My Phone service to locate the relative location of the phone.

Three subfunctions in the Find My Phone area are RING, LOCK, and ERASE.

RING: If you know it's somewhere nearby in her private area, or in a public area but just can't see it, then you can use the RING subfunction to ring the phone. Handy for when it gets lost under a sofa.

LOCK: The phone could be with someone who might return it to your friend if it wasn't so darned interesting a phone. The LOCK function would work here, preventing anyone from accessing the phone. If it's locked, maybe they'll return it (although highly unlikely).

ERASE: It could be in someone's home, which presents some awkward issues getting it back. If your friend is over the loss, but simply wants to prevent her contacts from being publicly accessible, then this would be the feature for her.

Bear in mind, this other person had access to her account, so she should take immediate steps to retain control of her accounts. The access afforded this other person by using your friends lost phone does not allow them to change passwords (that I'm aware of), however, she should immediately go into her various social networking accounts and change the passwords prior to affecting any of the above setting changes just to be safe.

This includes changing her password on FaceBook, Twitter, and any other social network in order to retain control of those accounts. After this, she can go back into those accounts and remove any content or media added or changed by this other person.

Hi,

 

This behavior can occur on some computers if the Use smooth scrolling option is enabled in Internet Options.

Note : By default, the Use smooth scrolling option is enabled in Internet Explorer.

 

The Graphics Device Interface (GDI) subsystem on the computer is responsible for rendering information on the screen. GDI accepts requests for the display of information from several applications or services. When you scroll through a Web page by using a mouse, a keyboard or a touchpad, and the Use smooth scrolling option is enabled, requests are made to redraw with every line that changes. This behavior may cause high CPU use.

 

Perform steps mentioned below and see if it helps.

a: Open Internet Explorer

b: Then Press Alt+X.

c: Then click the Internet Options.

d: Then click the Advanced Tab 

e: Under Browsing uncheck the option for Use Smooth Scrolling

f: Click Apply and then Ok.
