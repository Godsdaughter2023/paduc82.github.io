Index(Match) problem

I am trying to use an Index Match combination to grab values from one worksheet to use in another.  Because I have multiple rows of data I am trying to automate it so I do not have a bunch of copying every time I get new data so I am setting the value worksheet name (raw_data) in $AH$3 of the formula worksheet and the range within the value worksheet in $AH$2 and $AI$2 of the formula worksheet.

 

The formula works as follows using no references to the cells with the worksheet name and range:

=INDEX(raw_data!$K$10009:$K$10404,MATCH(1,INDEX((raw_data!$C$10009:$C$10404=$A12)*(raw_data!$A$10009:$A$10404=$A$2),,),0),1)

=INDEX($AH$3 & "!$K$" & $AH$2 & ":$K$" & $AI$2,MATCH(1,INDEX(($AH$3 & "!$C$" & $AH$2 & ":$C$" & $AI$2=$A$12)*($AH$3 & "!$A$" & $AH$2 & ":$" & $AI$2=$A$2),,),0),1)

 

In evaluating the formula the referenced cells are being read correctly BUT I am getting "False" for the second INDEX such that the MATCH looks like this:

 MATCH(1,{0},0),1), which as the row_num in INDEX = #N/A.

In the evaluation I see  "raw_data!$K$10009:$K$10404" with the quotes that I do not see in evaluating the formula without the cell references.  What am I doing wrong in trying to reference the cells that I am getting those quotes because I think that maybe a problem.  I am over my head (and it hurts)!

 

TIA

jeeped say:

... I tried 'wrapping' the first part like so =INDEX(($AH$3 & "!$K$" & $AH$2 & ":$K$" & $AI$2) and still got the quotes and I was not sure how I would wrap the part after the *! ...


I guess I wasn't too clear. You need to wrap every one of the text concatenations with INDIRECT() in order to convert each one. I guessing your data looks something like this,

AH3 contains raw_data
AH2 contains 10009
AI2 contains 10404

... so your formula would have to look like this,

=INDEX(INDIRECT($AH$3 & "!$K$" & $AH$2 & ":$K$" & $AI$2),MATCH(1,INDEX((INDIRECT($AH$3 & "!$C$" & $AH$2 & ":$C$" & $AI$2)=$A$12)*(INDIRECT($AH$3 & "!$A$" & $AH$2 & ":$A$" & $AI$2)=$A$2),,),0),1)

Note that the formula you supplied in your original post has a ":$" instead of a ":$A$" in the last string concatenation. I've done the best I can to parse your text strings together with INDIRECT() and the syntax is correct. I cannot check against actual data without some sample data.

BTW: while it may be good for a visual reference, there is no need to include the $ absolute references within the text portions of the formula. The text portions will not change when copied or filled to another location. For example, you could just have,
=INDEX(INDIRECT($AH$3 & "!K" & $AH$2 & ":K" & $AI$2),MATCH(1,INDEX((INDIRECT($AH$3 & "!C" & $AH$2 & ":C" & $AI$2)=$A$12)*(INDIRECT($AH$3 & "!A" & $AH$2 & ":A" & $AI$2)=$A$2),,),0),1)

WSR, Windows Speech Recognition, is built into the operating system. Dictation works for Word and most text boxes. In addition you can navigate (move from screen to screen and program to program) with simple voice commands. Commands also allow you to voice the names of menus and buttons.
