charleskenyon (ia) say:

Hi,

A Table of Contents (TOC) is a field inserted with switches to identify which items in the text should be displayed. By default it displays things that are in Heading Styles.

Here is a link that may help you figure things out.

Table of Contents Tips and Tricks by Suzanne Barnhill, MVP
http://wordfaqs.ssbarnhill.com/TOCTips.htm

It sounds as if the original TOC was manually formatted rather than using the TOC styles. I would recommend applying Heading 2 to the subsections and going from there. You can modify the TOC styles to suit, but you cannot have Heading 1 appear differently in the TOC. The TOC1 style will be applied.

There are built-in keyboard shortcuts you can use to apply the first three heading styles:

  1. Ctrl+Alt+1 = Heading 1
  2. Ctrl+Alt+2 = Heading 2
  3. Ctrl+Alt+3 = Heading 3

suzanne s. barnhill say:

The Navigation pane is accurately reflecting the heading structure in your document: all headings are at the same level. The TOC has been manually edited to have the desired appearance, and inevitably this manual formatting will be removed when the TOC is updated. You can get the format shown by applying the Heading 2 style to the subsections, and formatting the TOC 1 style as bold.
