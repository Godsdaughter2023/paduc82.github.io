4. Click Decode button

5. In the box at the bottom, copy everything EXCEPT for this text at the start:
    https://nam02.safelinks.protection.outlook.com/?url=

6. Open new tab in your browser

7. Paste the decoded hyperlink in the address bar and press Enter.

Et voila!

I'm using is =(c2-d2)*24 since I have a column for time in and another for time out. What do I need to do different with my formula to remove the negative answer I'm getting? And if I should ever have to work overnight again, what formula should I use for an overnight time frame?

Presumably, the two questions are the same.  That is, I presume that time is negative only when you start before midnight and end after midnight.

If you ever work more than 24 hours, you should record date as well as time in C2 and D2.  You can still format C2 and D2 to display only time.  But then your formula will work as-is.

If you never work more than 24 hours, you can get away with recording only time in C2 and D2, as you have been doing.  But the formula should be:

=(C2-D2+(C2<=D2))*24

Some people might suggest MOD(C2-D2,1)*24.  But that does not work if we should interpret C2=D2 to mean a full 24 hours.

I assume that C2 is the clock-out time, and D2 is the clock-in time.  If it's the reverse, then your formula should be:

=(D2-C2)*24

or

=(D2-C2+(D2<=C2))*24
