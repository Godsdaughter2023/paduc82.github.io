Mails from same domain going to junk folder in outlook - Microsoft Community

a. user say:

Mails from same domain going to junk folder in outlook

we have configured Exchange for office365 mails but some users mails from same domain is going to junk folder.

how?

nemanja pantelic msft say:

Hi Santosh,

Sorry for the misunderstanding.

Since email message in internal correspondence has SCL -1, it should skip exchange online protection and go directly to inbox, or a personal folder if you have inbox rules set. Since your message went to junk folder, I have two possible ideas:

  • As a possible cause, there is a possibility that your SPF DNS record is missing or corrupted. You could check and repair this from your Office Portal admin page> Setup> Domains> The domain in question> Check DNS. If the SPF is wrong, please copy the expected value to DNS providers Edit DNS page.
  • Second possibility is again related to DNS records. If your MX record is pointing to external anti spam software, email server, etc, mail flow could skip Exchange online and SCL rules wont apply to messages you recieve.

For the further troubleshooting, please provide me with the following information:

  • MX record.
  • SPF record.
  • Internet header from the message in question (double-click the message to open full screen> File> Properties> Internet headers).
  • Is your Office pure on cloud or you have Exchange server hosted on prem or hybrid?

To protect your privacy, please send me the information in Private Message.

Best regards,

Nemanja

aravinda balakrishna say:

Hi Rawoolf,

 

Let me provide you the information you need.

 

Outlook is an email client and you can configure your email accounts in these methods depending the configuration settings for your email account.

POP3, IMAP, Exchange Active Sync(EAS) or with an Exchange Server.

 

It is not necessary that you need to have an Exchange Server account to use Outlook.

You can setup any email account of your choice either with POP3 or IMAP configuration. You may have to contact your email service provider to get the account settings to setup on Outlook for your email account.
