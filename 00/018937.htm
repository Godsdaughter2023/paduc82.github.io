MS Project 2010 Completed Future Tasks - Microsoft Community

dan_smith_pmp say:

MS Project 2010 Completed Future Tasks

I ran into an unexpected scheduling result when leveling remaining effort for a resource who has a future dated task that was marked as complete.  The issue occurred in a plan (MS Project 2010 - Standard) that uses a shared resource that is linked to two other project plans.  A resource that is used in multiple plans had a future dated task on another plan that was completed ahead of schedule.  When the task was marked complete, updates to the Start and Finish dates for the actual task execution were overlooked.

Subsequently when I leveled the remaining effort for the resources used in my plan, no effort was scheduled for the resource with the future dated completed task during the duration of the completed task.  Even though the resource has capacity and there are no constraints on the work I am leveling, the resource is assigned 0 hours per day during the duration of the future dated completed task.  Once I found and corrected the Start & Finish dates for the completed task on the other plan, the leveling process in my plan behaved as expected.

There were no errors or warnings issued when I was leveling the resource in my plan, even though the scheduled work was interrupted during the future dated completed task.  Is there a known issue in the leveling / scheduling process related to future dated completed tasks?  Because a number of plans are linked to the same resource pool, it was difficult to identify the root cause of the issue.  Short of creating a master plan, the only place that I know of to review the combined resource allocation by task is in the resource usage view.  I added the % work complete to that view to help identify the issue if it should occur again.  Please let me know if there is a better view to use for identifying this issue.  If there are any configuration changes that I can apply to throw a warning or hard edit when a future dated is marked complete, please advise.

dale howard [mvp] say:

Dan --

What you saw is definitely not a bug.  The flaw was in the process of marking the future task as completed.  If you manually enter 100% in the % Complete field, Microsoft Project will assume that the task started and finished AS SCHEDULED, even if the task is in the future relative to the current date.  To avoid this problem, I recommend that you use the Task table in any task view for entering progress.  For each task, I would recommend you manually enter the date that work actually started the Actual Start date column, the estimated percentage of completion in the % Complete column (do not enter 100%, however), and when the task is completed, enter the date in the Actual Finish column (which will set the % Complete value to 100%).  Using this process would eliminate future tasks with actual progress.

Regarding the leveling process, Microsoft Project levels uncompleted work around tasks that are completed.  This means the leveling process factored into account the future completed task.  And no, the software will not warn you about future completed tasks.  That is your job.  Hope this helps.

marites_p say:

Hi klig999,

 

 

Good Day!

 

 

I understand that you would like to change your status using the Skype for your iPhone. Please be advised that with the new Skype there is no option to change your status.

 

 

We automatically set your status for you. When you are signed in and online, your status will show ACTIVE NOW. 

 

 

When you are signed out or offline, your status will show LAST SEEN and how long ago you were online.

 

 

You still have control over when people can contact you. Turning off the Notification for conversations option will show your presence as Do Not Disturb, which lets your contacts know you aren't currently available. 

 

 

This will also stop all incoming message notifications, however, you will still receive Skype calls. 

 


To manage the Do Not Disturb presence:
 

  1. In the Chats screen, tap your profile picture. 
  2. Tap the bell   button, and then tap Turn Off.

 

If you have further question about your status do not hesitate to contact us back.

 

Thank you!

 

Marites P.
