malkeleah say: Since you've apparently tried everything from the software end, the next step is to examine the issue from the hardware end. With the computer unplugged, open the case (I'm assuming and hoping this is a desktop computer) and check the connections on the cable going from the motherboard to the CD drive to make sure they are tight. Also make sure the connection from the CD drive to the power supply is tight. It would be a good idea to actually disconnect/reconnect them. Now restart the computer and go into Setup. You will see a prompt on the screen when you first turn on the computer that says something like "Press F2 to enter Setup". Press whatever key the prompt says and you will enter the BIOS. Look to see that the CD drive is recognized in the BIOS. If it isn't, then either the drive itself has failed or the connection on the motherboard is no longer working. If it is seen, then reboot into Windows to see if the drive is found now. If it is, great. If it isn't, then swap out the drive for a known-working one.
