    LastRow = sh.Cells.Find(What:="*", _
                            After:=sh.Range("A1"), _
                            Lookat:=xlPart, _
                            LookIn:=xlFormulas, _
                            SearchOrder:=xlByRows, _
                            SearchDirection:=xlPrevious, _
                            MatchCase:=False).Row
    On Error GoTo 0
End Function

                            After:=sh.Range("A1"), _
                            Lookat:=xlPart, _

                            SearchDirection:=xlPrevious, _
                            MatchCase:=False).Column

How can I combine two dictionaries from Microsoft WORD 2016 - Microsoft Community

robertgjudge say:

How can I combine two dictionaries from Microsoft WORD 2016

Hello:

I have WORD 2016 on two computers.  I would like to combine the dictionaries from the two installations.  Can I accomplish this and if so, how do I do so?

I will appreciate advice.

jay freedman say:

There is no direct way to combine two dictionaries into one file. (Technically, with access to a hex editor it could probably be done, but I wouldn't be sure it would function correctly unless I took the time to try it.) However, it isn't necessary.

Word can work with multiple dictionaries simultaneously, and the controls for doing so are quite simple. The custom dictionary on each computer should be %appdata%\Microsoft\UProof\Custom.dic.

  • Make a copy of Custom.dic on computer A, and rename the copy by at least one character. Transfer that copy to the same folder on computer B.
  • In Word on computer B, go to File > Options > Proofing and click the Custom Dictionaries button.
  • Click on one of the dictionary names in the All Languages group, and then click the Add button.
  • Select the copy transferred from the other computer and click Open.
  • Put a check mark in the box next to the added dictionary to activate it, and click OK.

Then repeat the operation in the other direction to transfer a copy of computer B's Custom.dic to computer A.
