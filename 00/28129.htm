john mcghie say:
Hi Tibor:

No, it's not a common problem, you need to correct the page set up.

Go into Format>Document>Page Setup and check that you are set to A3 there.

Then adjust your margins to suit.

If you set the page margins to 0 all the way around, an A3 image will fit.

However, your printer driver will probably throw an error saying the "Margins of the document are outside the printable area".  Most printers won't go closer than 1 cm to the edge of the paper.

You can choose "ignore" and Word will print it anyway.  Set it to DRAFT quality and look to see what you are getting: you often have to fiddle to get a good result, and you do not want to burn a whole set of ink cartridges while you experiment: A3 full colour burns a LOT of ink :-)

Don't set the resolution too high, or your printer may run out of memory trying to make up the impression (so might your Mac).  If in doubt, try 220 dpi -- most home/office colour printers can't go much above that in colour.

Hope this help

The article you cite

shows two uses of the hyphen.

The Negation use precedes Exponentiation use. For example, =-2^2 is equivalent to =(-2)^2 and Excel yields 4.

Exponentiation use of the hyphen precedes Subtraction use. For example, =0-2^2 is equivalent to =0-(2^2) and Excel yields -4.

Excel's precedence operations may or may not agree with what one might call "standard math convention," but Excel precedence operations are well documented as Negation before Exponentiation before Subtraction.
