In more than two decades I've never seen any update to Windows or Office that caused any change in the mouse's speed setting.

First, visit the web site of the manufacturer of your mouse, look for a "Support" or "Drivers and Software" page, and download the latest driver for your specific model of mouse. rohn007 say:
The problem isn't in Word, it is in Windows. Windows controls mouse sensitivity.

You want to find your mouse driver in control panel. See if you can find a setting that works better for you. Some mouse drivers even allow you to save application specific settings.

Some "high end" mice have a DPI setting right on the case of the mouse, while others have the setting in the driver.  Changing the DPI may help with your problem.

If you continue to the mouse too fast you could also try using the arrow keys.  Place the insertion point with the mouse, then use the arrows and the <SHF> (select) and <CTL> (move a word rather than a letter at a time) and <CTL><SHF> (select a word at a time) modifiers

VLOOKUP to find closest date match - Microsoft Community

yesmaybe say:

VLOOKUP to find closest date match

I have just started using VLOOKUP and found it to work very well on my last project.  So I am now trying to use it on my accounts sheet.

However the problem is my LOOKUP table is in descending order and comes downloaded each day so cannot easily be changed. Furthermore I need to find the closest match, not the exact match.   So what is the alternative?  I searched for some alternatives in an order list, but it seems MATCH and OFFSET can only find the exact match, I believe?

EXAMPLE:
My excel accounts sheet has a date in cell B24 (01-01-2012).  In cell K24 I want to find the exchange rate (from a csv file) on that given day.
The csv file has dates in Column A and USD exchange rate in Column B.  A2 is 02-01-2013 and A999 is 30-12-2011 *so the dates are descending.

So, in Cell K24 I entered this formula:
=VLOOKUP(B24,'eurofxref-hist.csv'!$A$1:$B$259,2,FALSE)

This works on any date that is an exact match.  If I change the formula to TRUE, the lowest date is matched.

It should be noted the csv file has a "worksheet size beyond 65536 rows" so I couldn't select the whole column.

It normally works, but when my date falls on a bank holiday (or when the exchange rate for that day wasn't published) then the result should be the next lowest day, i.e the day before.

Luckily (or not) I was at work on 1st January, so my little formula collapsed on the very first date.

Any idea what formula I can use instead?
Thanks,
Andy

http://www.ecb.int/stats/exchange/eurofxref/html/index.en.html

hansv mvp say:

Try

 

=IFERROR(VLOOKUP(B24,'eurofxref-hist.csv'!$A$1:$B$259,2,FALSE), INDEX('eurofxref-hist.csv'!$B$1:$B$259,MATCH(B24,'eurofxref-hist.csv'!$A$1:$A$259,-1)+1))

2. Uninstalled and reinstalled the browser
3. Reset the browser defaults

I seems to me that something is preventing my account login at Outlook.com from setting a cookie that tells each of my browsers that I am already verified in that browser, and that no further verification is necessary.  (I have actually specified that the cookie should be set, by checking the box provided).  Considering that Microsoft Internet Explorer, Mozilla Firefox and Google Chrome are totally different browsers, built on completely different codebases, I seriously doubt that this is a browser problem.

Also, I should mention that all my browsers are configured to accept all cookies, and not to delete them on exit, (which is default behaviour on every browser I have ever used).

Furthermore, I also should mention that I have other accounts configured with Two-Step Verification, (2FA, or Two Factor Authentication), e.g., Google, Facebook and Twitter, and I only ever have to go through the 2FA procedure once, provided that I make sure that the appropriate box is checked on the respective signin page; subsequent logins to any of those accounts do not generate further requests for security codes, unless I have performed any (or all) of the browser procedures mentioned above.

I hope that my description of this problem is sufficiently clear and detailed for any readers of this post, and that someone might be able to point me to some account setting that I may have overlooked.  Far be it from me to suggest that my problem might be Microsoft's fault; I am quite open to the possibility that I may well have misconfigured something in my account.

Thanks in advance for any help that any of you might be inclined to give me.
