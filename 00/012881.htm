In PowerPoint 2011 the Standard toolbar and the Formatting toolbar are locked within the window. If you put your commandbar buttons on either of these two toolbars then they will be minimized & maximized right along with the window.

You can add a menu to the menu bar. The menu bar is always visible. This is probably the best solution.

Programmatically you can check the window state and whether or not a particular command bar is visible, but I don't think closing a toolbar is an event that VBA notices, so you would have to come up with some other criteria to trigger your check to see whether or not your toolbar is visible.

gary zhu msft support say:

Hi Tek-nerd,

Thanks for your reply.

Yes, you need to hold MX records to point to your 1&1 IMAP server until finishing the migration. In this period, all new emails are still delivered to the 1&1 IMAP server. After changing the MX records to point to Office 365, the new emails will be redirected to the Office 365 mailboxes.

Regarding “How to migrate new emails from Migration time to MX record change time?”, please let the migration batch run for at least 72 hours before you delete it. This is because the source email system (1&1 IMAP server) and Office 365 mailboxes will be synced every 24 hours. The new emails can be synced to the Office 365 mailboxes automatically. Once all emails in the 1&1 IMAP server are synced to Office 365, you can go ahead and delete the batch.

Moreover, please refer to the detailed steps of following article to perform the IMAP migration:

https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Migrate-other-types-of-IMAP-mailboxes-to-Office-365-58890ccd-ce5e-4d94-be75-560a3b70a706

I understand you want to migrate your users into Office365 in phased manner. Since we do not know the exact migration time, it is recommended that you can pilot Office 365 by moving just a few people to Office 365 and keep the rest of your staff on your 1&1 IMAP server. After that, everyone’s email can use one custom domain name, whether they get their email using Office 365 or your existing email provider. Here is an article for your reference:

Pilot Office 365 with a few email addresses on your custom domain
