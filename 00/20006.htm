First, you absolutely need to update Outlook. The current version is 14.4.8. Use the auto-updater to get all the way to the latest version (it might take several run).

Then, if the issue persists, it could be related to the servers themselves.

By nature with POP accounts, the email client downloads all the emails, then sends a notification telling the server all the messages have been downloaded. Next time you connect, it only offers the new ones (or it should).

Some servers have glitches that resets the label on the messages and re-tags them as new.

If the problem cannot be corrected on the servers themselves, I would definitively look into configuring the accounts as IMAP to workaround the issue (with IMAP, the messages are mirrored on your Mac, but remain on the server. The read/replied, etc status syncs across — quite convenient actually, especially if you connect from more than one place).

cortig say:

All your POP emails are already downloaded on your computer. You're not going to loose them in the process.

Actually, I suspect that in the process, connecting through IMAP should reveal all the past emails that might not have been deleted from there and create duplicates (though the POP emails will be shown in the On My Computer section of Outlook whereas all the IMAP data will be displayed separately in the left side section under the IMAP account).

When you connect through POP, you have two options: Download and make the emails as downloaded, but dont do anything else, or Download and delete from the server (some older servers with very limited spaced requested that sort of setting). This implies that depending on how your POP account was setup, old mails might or might not still be on the server.

Deleting emails through IMAP is really simple: send them to the trash and empty the trash.

Through POP, since nothing is mirrored, nothing you do on your Mac is being reflected on the server. You need to connect to the server through their webmail page to check what's still there and clean things out.

If you decide to connect through IMAP, you can also clean things out through IMAP once the account is setup (though, obviously, all the emails will be mirrored/downloaded to your Mac first, which might be problematic for very large accounts and if you are under a data cap).

That's not possible. Wrapping text around an object works only if the text is in the text layer and the object is in the "draw" layer. However, a text box is already in the draw layer and so is the text inside it, so technically there is nothing to wrap around the picture.

Instead of a text box, use a one-cell table. If you need the table to position like a text box, open the Table Properties dialog and click the Around button.

  1. Choose Insert>Picture(s) to insert 3-4 photos on a slide. If you use more than 4, you won’t have enough space to lay them out in a pleasing way. It’s nice to have a variety in orientation, which means that at least one will be landscape (wider than it is high) and at least one will be portrait (higher than it is wide).
  2. Resize the photos from the corner handle and move them so you can see all of them. Some overlap is fine.
  3. Select one photo.  Click the Format tab, then choose the Rotated White option in the Photo Gallery. This option adds a number of settings, including a thick white border, a shadow, and a light gray edging (called a contour).


Repeat for the other photos.


Now comes the fun–trying to arrange the photos so that they look good. Here are some pointers:

  • Layer the photos so that they go from back to front (or vice versa) rather than having the middle photo in front or back. Think of piling up real photos like a deck of cards and fanning them out. To change the order of a selected photo, right-click and choose Send to Back or Send Backward, or Bring to Front or Bring Forward.
  • Rotate the photos so that they are slightly rotated at different angles. No photo should be exactly the same angle as the others. Try out variations until you like the result. To rotate, select the photo and then drag the green rotation handle.
  • Feel free to let the corner of a photo be off the slide slightly.
