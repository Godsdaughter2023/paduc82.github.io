In the 2007 version there were (apparently) some nice audio recording control options such as the ability to manually set the recording level.  I understand that these were removed with 2010 and later.

In their place, Microsoft has unhelpfully provided an audio compressor/limiter software code.  The objective of this code is to reduce the dynamic range of a recording by increasing the gain when the signal is quiet and reducing it when it gets overly loud.  Handy for people who don't understand setting recording levels, as well as for non-professional narrators whose voice may get too loud or soft at different times.

The problem seems to be that Microsoft's compressor is a very crude, brute force implementation.  It cranks the gain up dramatically whenever the narrator pauses - I'd guess by at least 30 or 40dB, and up comes any background noise with it, making even quiet environments sound noisy.

I've tried a number of professional microphones, including noise-cancelling mics, in some quite quiet environments.  At this point in my career I don't have access to a recording or radio station vocal booth, however surely someone who does doesn't need Microsoft to ride their mic gain! 

Separately recording audio for each slide, then inserting that audio into the ppt file works, but seriously adds to the complexity of what should really be a very simple operation (and apparently was in Powerpoint 2007).

I'd be interested in hearing from someone who understands what I'm describing and has an actual solution, short of going back to an earlier version of Powerpoint which would cost us some other features.
