Workaround: Sort No Date Tasks to Bottom - Microsoft Community

technicallyedits say:

Workaround: Sort No Date Tasks to Bottom

Others have also noticed that it's a design flaw of Outlook that it sorts "No Date" tasks at the top of your task list.  (If it doesn't have a due date, it's low priority, and should come last!)  A common workaround suggestion is just to set an artificial due date on the task that is way in the future.  I find this annoying on a per-task basis, since it requires an extra menu and the date may still expire if I don't go out super far.  It is not as good as if No Date sorted in a logical way, since setting a No Date deadline is right in the quick options menu. 

My faster workaround is to set a placeholder task with a due date several years in the future. This keeps open the "later" category so I can create new tasks and just drag them into that group.  It's a more streamlined way to get these "do this whenever there's time" tasks out of the space at the very top of my to do list, where my highest priority tasks should go!
