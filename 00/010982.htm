jerry (a) valladares say:

I've found the solution after spending 4+ hours with MS on the phone.  It's so simple and stupid.

If you migrated your mailbox (regardless if it was shared or not) and at some point you gave it a K1 license, it retains the App permissions and disables MAPI and Exchange web services. Simply go into the Active Users, select the account you want to change, and give it an E1, E3, E4, or E5. Save it, go back into the Shared mailbox settings and Voila! The MAPI and Exchange web services are back on. You can remove the license again and it will retain the permission of the E license.

Usually it helps to put the anchor in a paragraph close to the middle of the page, where it is less likely to move to the preceding or following page (it will move if you add or remove text paragraphs surrounding it).
