harshc10 say:

But, I want to combine or merge my multiple accounts.

a. user say:

Hi,

 

There's no way for us to merge old and brand new accounts. Once you’ve created a new Outlook.com account there is no way to rename your Hotmail account to that name. If you delete the new account, it will take 360 calendar days for that account name to become available again, and at that point, anyone would be able to get the name.

 

Instead, you can Auto-forward your Hotmail account to your new Outlook account. You’ll have all of your mail in one place, and can reply to it using your new address.

To auto-forward your email:

1.       Log in to your Hotmail account.

2.       Click Options, then click More options….

3.       Under Managing your account, click Email forwarding. You might have to sign in.

4.       Select the radio button for Forward your mail to another email account.

5.       Enter the email address for your new Outlook account.

6.       If you want, check the box for Keep a copy of forwarded messages in your Hotmail inbox.

7.       Click Save.

 

Note: Free Hotmail or Outlook.com account users need to log into the account they choose to auto-forward at least once every 270 days to avoid having the system remove the mailbox for inactivity.

 

Please verify if the alias that you want to create under your Outlook.com account is your Hotmail account? If yes, you would need to wait for 360days for the account name may be deleted. After 360 days the deleted Hotmail account will become available in the system for pick up but you may not necessarily get it back in case someone else takes it before you! In short, you bear the risk of losing the account name. You may refer to these steps for better understanding:

 

1. Delete your @outlook.com account and wait for 360 days for the account name may be permanently deleted.
