dexter nelson say:

Cortana's listening capability is separate from the rest of Windows, and she has her own speech recognition.

As a result, her volume control is managed there and not with the rest of Windows 10.

Go into Cortana > Options > Microphone, then clicking "Get started" under Make sure Cortana can hear me.

It will start a microphone troubleshooter and she will give you her own sentence to read.

When you're done, the "problem" will be marked as fixed in the troubleshooter, and from here on out, whatever the mic volume was when it was fixed, Cortana will adjust to when you say "Hey Cortana", or press the microphone.

I am on a Lenovo laptop with a built in array microphone and basically had to move in very close to the mic for it to work. This time, when I did the training, I stayed back a ways and Cortana adjusted my microphone volume up.

Haven't had a problem since.
