ronrosenfeld say: The results you post do not make sense in reference to the NetWorkDays function.

Your first line shows Code DD so that should be Pickup Date to Delivery Date
    Pickup Date is 11/16/11
   Delivery Date is 11/30/11
Networkdays applied to those two dates --> 11 vice 8.0

You define DA as Pickup Date to ATS Final, but there is no column headed ATS final.

You express your Days in Transit to one decimal place, but Networkdays precision is only to full days.

If the above are merely typos and miscalculations, then you can try this formula:

This formula must be **array-entered**:

=NETWORKDAYS(IF(OR(A2={"DD","DA"}),B2,
IF(OR(A2={"AD","AA"}),C2)),
IF(OR(A2={"DD","AD"}),E2,
IF(OR(A2={"DA","AA"}),D2)))

----------------------------------------

To **array-enter** a formula, after entering
the formula into the cell or formula bar, hold down
<ctrl><shift> while hitting <enter>.  If you did this
correctly, Excel will place braces {...} around the formula.


=NETWORKDAYS(IF(LEFT(A2,1)="D",B2,C2),IF(MID(A2,2,1)="D",E2,D2))

In both of the above, you may still had the optional holidays argument.

Note that in Excel 2003, you are limited to a maximum of seven nested functions, so if you have many more codes, a different approach will be required.
