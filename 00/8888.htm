jay freedman say:

The most likely answer is that the paragraphs in those cells (or maybe all the cells) have a non-zero right indent.

A quick way to test that guess is to select the table and press Ctrl+Q. That resets the paragraph formatting to that of the underlying paragraph style (probably Normal). If the lines unwrap, that's because the right indent was removed.

The Ctrl+Q may also make all the rows' heights larger, because the Normal style has some Space After (usually 8 points). To get rid of that, click the Line Spacing button on the Home tab and choose "Remove Space After Paragraph".

daniel g. grau say:

You need to add those words to a custom dictionary. Then, as long as that custom dictionary is open (which would be until you specifically close it, given that it remains active between Word sessions and computer restarts), any word it contains will be skipped by the spell checker. Upper case and lower case terms are considered different words. To create such dictionary:

  • Select Word > Preferences > Spelling and Grammar.
  • Click on the Dictionaries… button to open the "Custom Dictionaries" dialog.
  • Click on the New… button and save the dictionary using a logical name. For instance, I have several dictionaries with various vocabularies (Medical English, Legal Spanish, Technical, etc.).
  • Make sure to add an enabling checkmark next to this new dictionary. (If you have more than one dictionary checkmarked, they will be in effect all at once.)
  • You can associate your dictionary to a language via the "Language" dropdown menu, but leaving it at "None" will make it valid for any and all languages in your document.
  • Click OK to close the "Custom Dictionaries" dialog.
  • Select the new dictionary in the "Spelling and Grammar" dropdown menu titled "Custom dictionary." This is the dictionary to which new words can be added through the "Add" button the next time open at once, you can only have one "add-to dictionary" at a time.

Now run a spell check and add the questioned words to a matching custom dictionary… and get rid of them once and for all!

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

Despite what another responder stated, what you are reporting is not 'normal'. What you describe wanting is the default [i.e. normal] & expected behavior. Specifically;

  • When Ignore is used it applies to the specific instance. If other instances exist or are added later they will remain/be flagged as incorrect spellings
  • When Ignore All is used all existing instances are ignored as are those created in later sessions.

Both are persistent demarcations which do travel with the document. It's also imperative, of course, that the document be Saved before closing in order for them to be retained. However, if anyone opens the document & uses Word> Preferences -- Spelling & Grammar to click the Recheck Document button, all imposition of Ignore & Ignore All anywhere in the document will be cleared.

You don't give any indication of what 'very large' means or the nature of the document structure & content so it's just speculation that there may be some degree of corruption in the document.
