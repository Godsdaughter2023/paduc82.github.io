The type of barcode it reads are called Data Matrix codes, they look like a square made of lots of random black & white pixels (see http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Datamatrix)

Although these kinds of codes are not so common here in Europe, they are more popular further accross the globe and getting more common here. Some magazines in parts of Asia have lots of these codes throughout which allows the reader to find out secret information or gain access to secret websites or even to get free stuff!

Although it's easy to say 'why would anyone want a barcode reader on their phone' it's probably better to have it on your phone for those odd occasions when you do come accross a Data Matrix code and can scan something, rather than not - after all it's only a tiny application on the handset; just see the fun side of the possibilities it provides.

bernie deitrick say:

If you know that you will either have 2 digits, 1 digit, or no digits, then array-enter (enter using Ctrl-Shift-Enter) a formula like

=SUM(IFERROR(VALUE(LEFT(B3:F4,2)),IFERROR(VALUE(LEFT(B3:F4,1)),0)))

If you might have 3 or 4 digits (or more!), then you need to account for those, too.
