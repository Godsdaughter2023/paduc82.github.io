cmdrkeene say:

There's a couple ways you could.  Inside your Pictures Library you'll see a "Public Pictures" folder.  Anything you put here is shared with all other user accounts.  It's just a shared folder that automatically shows up in everyone's libraries.

If you want, you could also leave your pictures right in the same place, but right-click the folder and choose "Share with: (other account name)" on the menu.  Then the other person will have access to all your pictures right where they are.

wjl24ms say: I have tried to use the "share with others" but nothing showed in her account. Where should it appear in her account?

cmdrkeene say:

If she opens the C:\users folder, she'll see your folder and be allowed to access the ones you've shared (ones you haven't shared with her will be 'access denied' unless she overrides the security as an administrator).

She can also right-click the folder and "include in library" so that she has even faster access in the future.  Doing that means her library will be a combined view of 3 places: Her Documents, The Public Documents, and Your Documents.

What’s the best way to share files with everyone on my - Microsoft Community

john rubdy say:

What’s the best way to share files with everyone on my computer?

I want to share some files, but not all, files on my computer with other people on my home network. What is the best way to do this? I want to share some files, but not all, files on my computer with other people on my home network. What is the best way to do this?
waseemulla shariff say:

One of the best ways to share folders with all users on your computer or on your home network is to use the Public folder.

When you share files from the Public folder, you don't share them with specific people. Anyone with a user account on this computer can access the Public folder, but you decide whether to allow access to people on your network. 

You can only grant access to everyone or no one on the network. For more information about controlling access to the Public folder and determining how people can access it, see Sharing files with the Public folder.

To share files from the public folder:

Copy or move whichever files you want to share to the Public folder or one of its subfolders, such as Public Documents or Public Music. 

Your files in the Public folder are now shared with anyone who has access to the Public folder. 

Notes:

You can't restrict access to some individual files and folders within the Public Folder but allow access to other files and folders there. If users have access to the Public folder, they will have access to everything in it.

When you share files and folders with people using other computers, they can open and view those files and folders just as if they were stored on their own computers. Any changes you allow them to make to a shared file or folder will change the file or folder on your computer. However, you can restrict people to just viewing your shared files, without the ability to change them.
