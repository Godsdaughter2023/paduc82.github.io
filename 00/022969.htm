For a list starting in cell A2, do this.

In B2, enter this formula to identify the number of "words" in the address

 

=LEN(TRIM(A2))-LEN(TRIM(SUBSTITUTE(A2," ","")))+1

 

and copy down, then sort your list based on column B ascending. Note that your sample address had an extra space before the comma, and would confuse the formula.....)

 

For those rows where column B is 6 (3 "words" for the street, the city is 1 word, and state and zip are each one)


C2    =TRIM(SUBSTITUTE(A2,E2,""))
D2    =TRIM(SUBSTITUTE(SUBSTITUTE(C2,F2,""),",",""))
E2 (Street address)   =TRIM(LEFT(A2,FIND(" ",A2,FIND(" ",A2,FIND(" ",A2)+1)+1)))
F2  (City)  =TRIM(LEFT(C2,FIND(",",C2)-1))
G2  (State) =LEFT(D2,2)
H2  (Zip)   =TRIM(MID(D2,4,LEN(D2)))

 

Then copy all those down to match your 6 values in column B.

 

These formulas will work on addresses where the city name is two words "South Bend, IN 33333", so if you copy them down to match the 7s, at least some will work. For the other 7s, you need to change column E to

=TRIM(LEFT(A2,FIND(" ",A2,FIND(" ",A2,FIND(" ",A2,FIND(" ",A2)+1)+1)+1)))

Obviously, the row 2 cell references would need to change to the row where you are entering the formula. This would also work for a lot of your 8 word addresses, where the streets have an extra word and the city is 2 words "4444 Happy Valley Road South Bend, IN 33333"

 

The rest you can probably do by hand...
