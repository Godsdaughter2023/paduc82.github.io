Minor corruptions of the NT File System (NTFS) are common after the described incident and you will not boot in any mode and until you fix it by booting in some other way or on something else.  Running a chkdsk with error correction will usually fix things,

What do you see if you choose 'Disable automatic restart on system failure'?  (that is what it is for) so you can see what problem Windows is complaining about and the error message.

From the Command Prompt (which you can get to, right?) enter the following command (assuming Windows is installed on your c drive:

chkdsk  c:  /r

This disk checking can take a long time (perhaps several to many hours) depending on the size of the volume, the amount of data on the volume and what the disk checking finds to do.

Let chkdsk finish and correct any problems it might find. 

Depending on the the speed of your system, the size of the volume, the amount of data on the volume and what chkdsk finds to do, it may take a long time for chkdsk with error correction to complete (several to many hours) or it may appear to be 'stuck'.  Be patient.  If the HDD light is still flashing, chkdsk is doing something so don't interrupt it.  It will finish eventually one way or the other.  Keep an eye on the percentage amount to be sure it is still making progress.  It may even appear to go backwards sometimes.

You may have to run the chkdsk  /r  it more than once.

When the chkdsk runs clean, remove the CD and restart your system.

a. user say:

Hi,

 

If you are using Windows Live Mail here are the steps to delete all messages:

1. Left click an email in your inbox to make it highlighted.

2. Then on your keyboard, press Ctrl + A. This will highlight all the emails.

3. Press Delete on your keyboard.

 

If you want this action to reflect on your web mail then do this steps before the above instructions:

1. On your Outlook, click the gear icon then click More mail settings.

2. Under Managing your account, click POP and deleting downloaded messages.

3. Choose "Do what my other program says—if it says to delete messages, then delete them" option then click Save.

 

This will make any action you make in your Windows Live Mail reflect on your web mail.
