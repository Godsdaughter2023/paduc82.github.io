jeeped say:

The Redo button is tied to the Undo sequence. If one or more operations have been undone, they can be redone. This behaviour closely duplicates an internet browser's History where you can go back and then forwards but never further than the newest list entry in history.

The CTRL+Y keystroke shortcut is tied to the Redo operation, but it allows to continue forward, as in Do Again.

Example: highlight a cell in yellow then move to the cell next to it. The Redo button will remain disabled, but tapping CTRL+Y will highlight the new cell in yellow.

IMHO, the Redo button and CTRL+Y should be more tightly woven together, but that is the way they decided to do it. I would suppose it is to be able to pick a point in the foward/reverse undo/redo path without cycling each action, although I do not know why that precludes the ability to Do Again.
