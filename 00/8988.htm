eg. If I have this date from last year:

Saturday, 9 February 2013

I would like the formula result for this year's anniversary to be:

Sunday, 9 February 2014

(PS. these are the weekdays for Australian dates)

jeeped say:

I'm assuming that the non-standard date (e.g. Saturday, 9 February 2013) is left-aligned in the cell. This means it is text. If it is right-aligned, then it is an actual date.

With a left-aligned Saturday, 9 February 2013 in A1, use this formula in A2,

=EDATE(DATEVALUE(RIGHT(A1, LEN(A1)-FIND(",",A1))),12)


With a right-aligned Saturday, 9 February 2013 in A1, use this formula in A2,

=EDATE(A1, 12)

With A2 selected, tap Cltr+1 and when the Format Cell dialog opens, go to the Number tab (aka Property Page) and select Custom from the list down the left and supply the following for the Type:

dddd, d mmmm yyyy

Note that A1 is left-aligned (e.g. text masquerading as a date) and B1 is right-aligned (e.g. an actual date formatted as dddd, d mmmm yyyy)

hansv mvp say:

Click in the last section.

Activate (double-click) the header or footer that contains the page number.

This will automatically activate the Design tab of the ribbon under Header & Footer Tools.

Click to remove the highlight from the Link to Previous button in the Navigation group.

You can now delete the page number from the last section without removing them from the main part of the document.
