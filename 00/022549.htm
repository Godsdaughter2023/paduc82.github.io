The secondary SIM in all dual-SIM Lumia phones is limited to 2G, due to hardware restrictions.

Either SIM 1 or 2 can be set up as secondary, but at least one of the mobile operators must support 2G on their network, for the phone to work as expected.

tom-s say:

h0mely,

Another, fairly easy, way to go is to use a pivot table.

Create data table first. A1:D1 column headers - Date, Month, Week, Day

Then in A2 enter 01/01/2012 and fill down to 12/31/2012

In B2:D2 enter formulas:

=MONTH(A2)

=IF(ISODD(WEEKNUM(A2)),"Odd","Even")

=LOOKUP(WEEKDAY(A2),{1,2,3,4,5,6,7},{"Sunday","Monday","Tuesday","Wednesday","Thursday","Friday","Saturday"})

Fill down formulas.

Click anywhere in table and select from Insert tab of Tables group > Pivot Table (see this Help page for more info on creating: http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/excel-help/create-or-delete-a-pivottable-or-pivotchart-report-HP010342375.aspx?CTT=1).

Place Pivot Table in new sheet and then Add Month and Day fields to Page Fields area, add Week field to Row Field area, and Date field to Data area. Ensure the Date field setting is set to Count.

Then, for example, Select Month 1, Day Monday, and Week Odd, and the Total (Count of Date) shows as 3.
