You are wasting your time & effort. 

I should have been using the term RAM instead of the general term memory.  RAM is directly connected to the motherboard.

Your motherboard's limitation is that specified amount of RAM.  It cannot address larger amounts of RAM - its addressing components cannot count higher than that.

Nothing you can do can get your motherboard around this limitation.  Later or more highly specified motherboards have higher RAM limitations.  Just for example, my 11 year old Dell Inspiron 9300 can take 2GB RAM - I did not select it on this basis alone but that happy circumstance has allowed me to upgrade it to Windows 10.
