    Private Sub DTPicker1_Change()

    Enter the code below under this heading, this enters the datepicker value in cell A1 on Sheet1, cell A2 on Sheet2 and cell A3 on Sheet3, you should be able to adjsut the Sheet and Range values to solve your problem.

    Private Sub DTPicker1_Change()
    ActiveWorkbook.Sheets(1).Range("A1").Value = DTPicker1.Value
    ActiveWorkbook.Sheets(2).Range("A2").Value = DTPicker1.Value
    ActiveWorkbook.Sheets(3).Range("A3").Value = DTPicker1.Value
    End Sub

    Hello,

    Aliases are additional email addresses associated with your Outlook.com account and is like a nickname for your account which can be an email address, phone number, or a Skype name. You can use the same inbox, contact list, and account settings as your primary email address and you can also sign in to your Outlook.com account with any alias as they all use the same password.

    By default, the email address or phone number that you use to sign up for your Microsoft account is considered the primary alias. Your primary alias shows up as the main account name when you view your profile information. This can be changed at any time.

    You can have up to ten aliases on your Microsoft account at any given time.
