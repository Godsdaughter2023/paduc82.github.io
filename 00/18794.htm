jason (office engineer) say:

Sways do not exist anywhere as files that you can save locally.  You can export a version of the Sway as a Word/PDF file, but it is a down-level copy of the Sway that is meant to preserve the content.

If you are using a premium or Office 365 account (like from a school or business), your Sway is only available to people inside your organization by default.  You can change this to available to everyone which is also the default for Sways created in a personal account.  When available to everyone, anyone who knows the URL to the Sway can open it to view.  Because the URL is a random set of characters, it is very hard to guess and people will only know it if you share it out.  That URL is only available on your My Sways page until you share it.
