graham mayor say:

As Jason indicates, you cannot delete an open document, but you can use a macro to reproduce the steps to do so e.g. as follows - see http://www.gmayor.com/installing_macro.htm  

Sub DeleteCurrentDocument()

'Graham Mayor - https://www.gmayor.com - Last updated - 27 Jan 2019
Dim oDoc As Document
Dim strPath As String
Dim lngAsk As Long
    Set oDoc = ActiveDocument
    If Not oDoc.path = "" Then
        strPath = oDoc.FullName
    End If
    oDoc.Close wdDoNotSaveChanges
    If Not strPath = "" Then
        Beep
        lngAsk = MsgBox("You are about to delete '" & strPath & "' permanently." & vbCr & _
        "Are you sure you wish to do this?", vbYesNo)
        If lngAsk = vbYes Then
            Kill strPath
        End If
    End If
lbl_Exit:
    Set oDoc = Nothing
    Exit Sub
End Sub

You really need to wrap your head around the difference between "downloading" as used by a POP3 account and "sync'ing" as applied when accounts are configured using IMAP or Exchange protocols.

POP3 is a "one-way - one-time" download of the <Inbox> data only for esch device. Nothing you do on any device where the account is configured using POP3 will impact the data on the server.

IMAP is a two-way "sync" of email data creating a "mirror" in the Outlook data file of what exists on the server. Delete something and it'll get deleted on the server the next time it connects with the server. Delete something on the server and no device will see it going forward.

 

Understanding Email Protocols (POP, Imap, MAPI, EAS, Exchange)

a. user say:

Hi Deb

 You may have more success directing this question in the Office category. At the top of the page click Categories>Office then follow links from there, you will get your answer no doubt about that. However if you know the weight of the bottle and the weight of a shot you simply weigh the bottle and do a simple sum. If you know the bottle weighs 20oz and a shot 1oz then a bottle weighing 40oz will have 20 shots in it, assuming all the bottles are the same net weight. If the actual weight of the bottles vary then you will get false readings especially dealing with a small weight such as an ounce, this is only viable if the bottles have an equal Tare weight. If possible weigh some clean dry bottles and see if can establish a mean average. Gross weight-Tare weight=Net weight

martmcd say:

weigh an empty bottle, enter the weight (in ounces) into one column B

weigh a partial or full bottle enter the weight (in ounces) into column C 

in cell D2 enter =C2-B2 to give the weight (in ounces) of liquor in the bottle (number of 1 ounce shots)

suggest you weigh a few empty bottles to get an average.

if you want to work from full bottles,

try this,  E2 contains =D2-(B2-C2)

which subtracts the weight of the bottle (B2-C2) from the current weight

[edit] hold on a second, I haven't taken into account weight of a 1 (fluid) ounce shot.

[edit 2] you might be ok (depends how accurate you want it) liquor is roughly 0.98 ounces to 1 fluid ounce (assuming you are in the US)

yes.

if you use weight per shot rather than weight per 1floz (just semantics) and as long as you are consistent across the row it doesn't matter what the units are, or the size of the shot. eg merlot 125ml glass, 750ml bottle.

C-B=weight of liquid in a full bottle

weight of liquid in full bottle/D (number of shots)=weight per shot

F (measured weight) - B (weight of bottle) = weight of liquid in open bottle

weight of liquid in empty bottle/E (Weight of a shot) = number of remaining shots
