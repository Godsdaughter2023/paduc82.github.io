annabellepeters say:

entourage- how do you delete emails?

I have 35,000 emails banked up in my entourage. how can i delete majority of them to free space? I would like to keep the most recent ones

dianeoforegon say:
Create a new category "Older Emails"
Create a new Saved Search (smart folder) under File > New > Saved Search.
Select Date Sent > Any Date
Give it some time to populate
Scroll down to time you want to start deleting emails then select all those emails and assign your category "Older Emails".
Under File > Export select to export by category "Older Emails"
Click right arrow to go to next window option.
Select Yes, delete items from Entourage after they are archived.

The .rge file can be saved if needed. Emails are saved in the folders you have setup.

Deleting too many messages via the Deleted Items folder can cause Entourage to crash especially if you are having issues with your Identity.

Note: If you have more than 2GB of data in a folder (that's approx 6-10K messages per folder depending on attachments) this can also cause problems with Entourage and with exporting your data.

Are you trying to free up space in your Identity or on your computer?

Deleting emails in Entourage leaves what's called 'wasted space'. Rebuilding can help or it can totally destroy your data. Your best option is to export your data and import into a new Identity. This removes all wasted space.

Wasted space

The database will be left with 'wasted' space in it every time you delete something. However, this is not all bad, since the wasted space is used up with new data as it is added. This way, over a period of time (assuming that you are deleting stuff as you go) the database will probably reach an optimum size, after which the stuff you delete is roughly equal to the stuff you add, and no further growth takes place.

If you need to recover the wasted space on your hard disk, you will have to do a 'typical' rebuild to compact the database. This will create a compacted version of the database without the wasted space. You will then have to manually delete the backup copies of the original database that the rebuild process creates AFTER you are happy the new one is OK.
