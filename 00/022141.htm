It might not be necessary to list each day of the year.  I would need a better of understanding of why you need that much detail.  Certainly not for the reason stated.

But given your stated requirements, I would set things up as follows.





The formulas are:

A2: 1/2/2012
B2: =IF(OR(A2="",A2<$I$1),"",VLOOKUP(DATEDIF($I$1,A2,"y"),$H$4:$I$8,2)))
C2: =IF(B2="","",N(C1)+B2)
A3: =IF(A2="","",IF(A2=DATE(YEAR(A2),12,31),"",A2+1))

Copy A3:C3 down through A367:C367 to allow for a leap year with Jan 1 as the first day of the year (my preference).

YEAR(A2)-YEAR($I$1)-(A2<DATE(YEAR(A2),MONTH($I$1),DAY($I$1)))

myall_blues say: A problem with any of the ??IFS functions is that they only allow AND logic - when you provide multiple conditions, all conditions must match to be included in the result. So in your case it is trying to average where column Q is NMX and NMP, rather than NMX or NMP.

To get around it, you can put the criteria in as an array constant by enclosing them in braces. This returns multiple results, which you then need to average by using a second AVERAGE formula.

For your case, try:

=AVERAGE(AVERAGEIFS(DTPO!E:E,DTPO!A:A,"1",DTPO!Q:Q,{"NMP","NMX"}))

Note also with the criteria for column A if you are trying to match a numeric value 1 then remove the quotes. At present it is treating the 1 as text.
