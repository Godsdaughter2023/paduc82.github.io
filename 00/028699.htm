var word = new ActiveXObject("Word.Application");

var merger = doc.MailMerge;

merger.Destination = 0;
merger.Execute(false);
word.Visible = true;
word.Activate();
doc.Close(0);
doc = null;

I have a Nokia Lumina 800 operating Windows 7 (no updates outstanding), which has been fine - BUT - I have used a specific website on Explorer to track vehicles for some time but last weekend when trying to access it a MS security certificate warning page came up. Due to the early hour I clicked through to the site as it's never been a problem previously (never had a certificate issue either) to find that the site was a fake. With the site working fine on my laptop and office PC and other websites working without apparent issue on the phone my first thought is a targetted viral attack, but according to the Nokia support forum this isn't possible - oh really I'm thinking at the moment! I've tried clearing the history as this is as far as you can get with no way of getting any advanced settings on Explorer.

So with supposedly no virus attack possible and no way of getting into the depths of Explorer I'd be pleased if anyone could explain what the problem is and how I cure it as I'm a stones throw of ditching the phone and opting for an Android or similar phone.

A quick visit to their site to upload the certificate worked wonders. Hardly a singing the praises of MS and Windows, but at least haven't got to go to the trouble of changing my phone just yet.
