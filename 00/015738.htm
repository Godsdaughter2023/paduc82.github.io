tooolnut say:

End-of-line word-division

Why does Word divide words (you know, with a hyphen) at end of lines in Read Mode but not in Layout Mode, and can end-of-line word-division be turned on and off in either one of these modes, somehow?
jay freedman say:

Word always hyphenates in Read Mode, and there is no way to turn it off there. If this is an issue for you, post a suggestion at https://word.uservoice.com/.

In Print Layout mode, go to the Page Layout tab of the ribbon, click the Hyphenation command in the Page Setup group, and choose Automatic to turn it on, or None to turn it off. (The Manual choice steps you through the document and asks whether to hyphenate where it's possible in each line.)

You can also insert an "optional hyphen" by pressing Ctrl+<hyphen>. The character will appear only when the line breaks there.
