a. user say:

Hi,

 

Sorry for the late reply. We are currently experiencing high volume of inquiries. By the way, let's find out what are the possible options that we can do to resolve your issue.

 

It seems that the junk mail filtering of the custom domain email accounts is set to exclusive. All emails that came from senders who are not in the contacts list will be directed to junk folder. You have to add your actual Outlook.com account in the contacts of the custom domain accounts. You can also adjust the junk mail filtering to standard. Here's how:

 

1. Go to www.outlook.com and sign in with your custom domain account

2. Click on the Gear Icon and select More mail settings

3. Under Preventing junk email category, select Filters and reporting

4. Under Choose a junk email filter category, select Standard

5. Click Save to confirm changes

 

We also opt you to check the custom domain email accounts if the messages from your actual Outlook.com account are still on the junk folder. You have to remove the messages from the junk folder and put it on the custom domain email inbox so that the system will not tag the emails from your actual Outlook.com as junk.

 

Let us know if you need more help.
