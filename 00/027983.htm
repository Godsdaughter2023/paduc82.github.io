Hi ChuckIt2Me

Some background info is in order.

First, MS didn't intro any kind of "integration" between Outlook desktop (part of Office), Win 10 Mail app, Outlook.com and anything else related which is one thing that confuses a great many people

Regarding the integration aspect.........

Outlook desktop and the Win 10 Mail/people apps are two entirely different programs both of which are <email clients> that connect to <email accounts>. Outlook desktop does not directly connect to the WIn10 People app.

So where are contacts located given the above?

Answer is that "it depends on the type of <email account> involved. More specifically,

For POP and Imap accounts, contacts are located on the local computer/device where the <email account> is configured. To be more specific, if you configure the same <email account> in Outlook and Win10 Mail/People - eah retains their own copy of contacts on the same computer.

In order to share contact/calendar data across devices (whether computer, mobile or tablet), the account needs to be an Exchange based account which includes Outlook.com. Accounts on CenturyLink could only be configured as either IMAP or POP3.

Outlook.com is an <email account> which is accessed by many people only via a browser BUT these accounts can be configured in Outlook desktop or in the Win10 apps so it would "appear" that the apps are sharing data when in fact, the apps are accessing the same data on the server.

To get to your question/issue - not at all clear about the following sentence in your post

" the contacts are still in the Outlook app, Outlook.com, the Windows 10 People app, etc. Right where I can do nothing about them, because the Windows 10 People app, Outlook.com, etc. In the Outlook app, Outlook.com,."

If the same Outlook.com account is configured in all your <email client> apps as an Exchange account, then those would be accessible by all client's so going to need some clarification as to what the exact problem is that you're running into.
