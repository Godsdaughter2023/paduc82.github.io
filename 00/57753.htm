Hi Danish,

Usually this is caused due to hardware or software related issues.

To check for the cause and error message for the improper shutdown, I would suggest you to check for the event logs in Event Viewer. Event Viewer tracks information in several different logs. Windows Logs include:

  • Application (program) events. Events are classified as error, warning, or information, depending on the severity of the event. An error is a significant problem, such as loss of data. A warning is an event that isn't necessarily significant, but might indicate a possible future problem. An information event describes the successful operation of a program, driver, or service.

  • Security-related events. These events are called audits and are described as successful or failed depending on the event, such as whether a user trying to log on to Windows was successful.

  • Setup events. Computers that are configured as domain controllers will have additional logs displayed here.

  • System events. System events are logged by Windows and Windows system services, and are classified as error, warning, or information.

  • Forwarded events. These events are forwarded to this log by other computers.

Follow the steps to check the Event Viewer:

a. Press Windows + X keys.

b. Click Event Viewer.

a. user say:

Hello Danish,

Thank you for the quick reply.

The effects of a forceful shutdown are mentioned below. It not only effects the software but, also the other features in the computer. So, its always prefered to shutdown the computer through the Shutdown option in Windows.

a) Improperly shutting down your computer can cause some harmful effects for your software. Though the operating systems are better equipped to handle an improper shut down, there are still negative effects associated with it. Computers should be shut down improperly only when there are no other options available, such as in the case of a your computer freezing.

b) If a computer is improperly shut down, for instance by a power failure, then registry files cease operation while they are still performing a function. This is the main cause of potential problems. Any applications or processes that were running at the time of shut down also will be interrupted and any files that were not saved will be lost. Any settings that may have been changed just prior to shut down also will most likely not take effect.

c) The hard drive may be effected negatively by an improper shut down. This is because the read and write heads will have been in the middle of a process, meaning that they will be rotating at high speeds when your computer shut down.

d) The computers system restore process may be interrupted. System restore allows you to restore your computer to a previous state if it becomes corrupted.

e) Hardware will only be effected if it is in use.

If updating the printer drivers did not resolve the issue, what we can do is clear the print spooler files and enable the spooler service. To do this, follow the steps below:

  2. Double-click "Printer Spooler" in the Services list.
