Well, you have to tell it to do something...

You could CD (or CHDIR, which is CHange DIRectory) to get INTO said directory, but that is unlikely what you want to do; you just want to remove that directory, so RD (or RMDIR, which is ReMove DIRectory) is likely what you want to tell it to do...

  C:\WINDOWS\system32>cd /?
  Displays the name of or changes the current directory.
   
  CHDIR [/D] [drive:][path]
  CHDIR [..]
  CD [/D] [drive:][path]
  CD [..]

. . . and . . . 
  

  C:\WINDOWS\system32>rd /?

  Removes (deletes) a directory.
  
  RMDIR [/S] [/Q] [drive:]path
  RD [/S] [/Q] [drive:]path

Specifically, you want to type in:

  C:\WINDOWS\system32>RD C:\Users\michael\AppData\Roaming\Mozilla
  

. . . or . . .

  
  C:\WINDOWS\system32>RMDIR C:\Users\michael\AppData\Roaming\Mozilla

. . . either of those commands should remove said directory and/or come back with a message if you cannot.  At worst, you may need to boot into SAFE MODE to make sure nothing you want to remove is 'in-use' or reported that way (it will not be loaded automatically in SAFE MODE.)

In any case, if you wanted to use the CD or CHDIR command, you could do it one folder deep at a time like this:

  C:\WINDOWS\system32>cd \users

  C:\Users>cd michael

  C:\Users\michael>cd appdata

  C:\Users\michael\AppData>cd roaming

  C:\Users\michael\AppData\Roaming>cd Mozilla

  C:\Users\michael\AppData\Roaming\Mozilla>

. . . or do it in ONE command:
  

  C:\Windows\System32>chdir \Users\michael\AppData\Roaming\Mozilla
  
  C:\Users\michael\AppData\Roaming\Mozilla>

** tip: the \ at the beginning (after the CD / CHDIR command itself) tells the command to start at the ROOT of the C:\ drive - not the directory you are currently in (which is shown before the ">"...)
