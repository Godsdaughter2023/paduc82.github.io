Hi Diane,

Thank you for writing to Microsoft Community forum. 

An AAE file contains edits made to an image using the Photos app on an iOS device. It is used to transfer non-destructive edits a user has made to ‘.jpg’ images.  Basically these are additional files that are created next to the actual jpg photo file. 

If you import pictures from an iOS device to Windows, only the JPEG images will get transferred and not the AAE files, which will cause to lose your edits. Also, AAE files can be deleted without erasing the pictures but any edits made will be undone.

If you want to view a .aae file on your PC, you need to have the appropriate program installed. You may use your favorite search engine and look for the appropriate program that supports .aae files.

<how can that be when media Center finds the tuners and it receives digital just fine.>
Because, for an analog tuner, Media Center requires that said tuner has a hardware MPEG2 encoder (or, that the manufacturer creates a software encoder that emulates this.)  The Theater HD 750 chip doesn't have this, and it appears (after some looking) that ATI hasn't created a software encoder (side note:  they're unlikely to do so, as they've exited the tuner market.)

Out of curiosity, what was the 'other tuner' that was tried previously?

Honestly, my recommendation would be to return the tuner (if at all possible), and get a combo or hybrid tuner that's known to work properly.  My first recommendation would be for a Hauppauge HVR-2250 (dual combo/hybrid board), as it works quite nicely in MC, as a combo/hybrid NTSC/Clear-QAM tuner.  MC knows how to treat it as what it is, and it works very, very well (I do have one.)

That, or an AverTV Combo G2 (which isn't as flexible as the '2250, hence its slightly lower price.)

Method 2:

 

You may follow the steps below to check for the Network Adapter in the device manager. I would suggest you to uninstall and reinstall the network adapter drivers and check:

 

a. Click Start, and then click Control Panel.

b. Click System and Maintenance, click System, and then click Device Manager.
Note If Control Panel is in Classic View, double-click System, and then click Device Manager.
If you are prompted for an administrator password or for a confirmation, type the password, or click Allow.


c. In Network Adapter, expand Network Adapter, right-click the devices listed, and then click Uninstall.
Check if you have any exclamation mark alongside the devices listed. If you do have then there is issue with the adapter installed.


d. When you are prompted to confirm that you want to remove the device, click OK.

e. Start the computer.

 

After the computer restarts, the drivers will be automatically installed. Check after this procedure if you are able to get the network access.

You may also check for driver updates through windows updates. Refer to the below mentioned link for further assistance on updating the drivers:
