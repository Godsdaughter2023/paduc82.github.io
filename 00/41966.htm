If this is your ISP or your e-mail provider you would not be asked to do this (unless they made some sort of change - so you need to contact them safely to see what is happening).  This may be someone else trying to break into your accounts.  Do not reply to anything they send or open any attachments or click on any links or e-mail addresses in the email.

Contact your ISP and/or e-mail provider and report this (and confirm it really isn't them causing the problem or what to do to resolve it if it is them).  Contact them directly - not through whatever is asking you for this information but at their actual published sites and technical support numbers.  But you do need to notify them immediately so they can take the proper action (and be sure to discuss how you will get access to your files if they need to close your account - you may need to keep it open long enough to move the information elsewhere or to just a different account - or you may discover all of this is unnecessary and they can explain the situation to you or help you resolve the problem if that's what it is).
