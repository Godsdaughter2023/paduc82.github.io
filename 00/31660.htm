franciscoch say:

Actually, I found the solution!

For those of you with the same problem: Apparently, the problem WAS interference from my router - keep in mind that the Wireless Display Adapter doesn't use wi-fi, but its own wireless connection with the computer. The trick is to set your router to broadcast in a different channel from the one it's currently using, but to stick to channels 1, 6 or 11 (I kept trying all the wrong ones hehe - this article explains why these particular channels should be used: http://www.metageek.net/support/why-channels-1-6-and-11/).

My router was using channel 1. After setting it to 6 (I haven't tried 11 yet), it stopped interfering and the WDA worked like a charm (it may be my head, but I see the slightest lag sometimes... I'll try channel 11). If you're not sure how to do this, check this article: http://www.howtogeek.com/howto/21132/change-your-wi-fi-router-channel-to-optimize-your-wireless-signal/

Hope it helps!

Windows stores your Internet Explorer favorites in the folder:

C:\Users\[username]\Favorites    (where [username] is the name you log into Windows with)

When in doubt, check every "Favorites" folder under each username in the C:\Users folder.

Your favorites are stored as files, which may be further organized into a folder substructure in the favorites folder. Once you locate your files, just move the entire content of the old favorites folder into the favorites folder of your current username folder in windows 10, and they should appear next time you open Internet Explorer. The only thing that won't copy is the order in which the favorites are displayed in the IE menu - you will have to re-organize them to some degree in IE.

Also note that Microsoft Edge (which may be your default browser in W10) has a much different (and less friendly/useful) way of displaying shortcuts/favorites that may not even use the favorites folder, so installing/setting-up IE as your default browser may be necessary to get your favorites back the way you like them.  

Good luck!  =)
