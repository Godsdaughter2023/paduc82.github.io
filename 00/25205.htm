Malwarebytes specializes in finding the things that AV apps miss, so it’s always a valuable addition to your toolbox. But it’s important to understand that Windows Defender’s PUA Protection is disabled by default, and that you’ll need to turn it on manually with the PowerShell Set-MpPreferece cmdlet:

Copy, Paste, and Run this command line at the elevated PowerShell Prompt to enable PUA Protection:

 Set-MpPreference -PUAProtection 1

Then confirm that the value was reset by returning the current state for PUAProtection:

 $Preferences = Get-MpPreference

 $Preferences.PUAProtection

v_agarwal say:

Your numbers are stored as a Text in your range. See my snip shot, where Column E is my different formula while column F is the formula Text. When you sum individually with "equal to" sign excel smartly recognizes those text numbers and convert it in to number for solution.

To solve it, you have to select your range and then you will see a small yellow pop in right corner, click it and from there select convert to number.

Most functions ignore Text, including number stored as text.  SUM is one of them.

Sum vs. +
