If you regularly update your Windows 10 operating system than there is no chance you will get this error. So always update your Windows 10 to the latest so that all Windows enhancements and fixes can be applied to your system.


I searched for the error and found that this error occurs due to incompatible SSD drivers that comes with the Windows 10 Operating system.

So today i thought to write a solution for this error. If you are facing the same issue, then here is how you can fix DPC Watchdog Violation error.

  • The first thing you want to do is open the Device Manager in Windows 10. Just go to Cortana and search for Device Manager.
  • Once you have it on your screen, expand IDE ATA/ATAPI controllers as you can see in the below screenshot.


  • Now right click Standard SATA AHCI Controller and click on Properties.


  • Now go to Driver tab and click on Driver Details

  • Make sure that the driver listed is iaStorA.sys and click OK


  • Now click on Update Drivers


  • Select “Browse my computer for driver software" in the next window.
  • Next, select “Let me pick from a list of device drivers on my computer“.
  • Select Standard SATA AHCI Controller and click Next

.

  • In few seconds your SATA AHCI driver will get updated.

That's it now RESTART your system once and the DPC_Watchdog_Violation in Windows 10 is fixed.

Can not get Sound Recorder or Audacity to record in Windows 7 - Microsoft Community

qlts4488 say:

Can not get Sound Recorder or Audacity to record in Windows 7

I have tried troubleshooting both programs, but neither one will record. Sound is coming out of both microphone & speakers. Any idea's? Drivers? Updates? Encoders? Thanks
premkumar somalinga say: I followed the procedure given in the Audacity forum:
  • Windows Vista, Windows 7 and Windows 8
  1. Right-click over the Speaker icon by the system clock then choose Recording Devices to open the Recording tab of "Sound".
  2. Right-click anywhere inside the Recording tab and choose "Show disabled devices" then right-click again and choose "Show Disconnected Devices".
  3. Right-click specifically over the input device you want to record with (in this case "Stereo Mix" or whatever alternative you have), and if visible, choose "Enable".
  4. Sometimes it helps to right-click over the "stereo mix" or similar device again and choose "Set as Default Device". 
Even then I was not able to record.

Then I right clicked on the speaker icon and clicked on the Volume Control Options and found the box near the speaker was not checked.  Once I checked it, I was able to record speaker sounds on Audacity.
If you find the microphone sounds are also being recorded, you have to right click on the microphone in the Recording Devices and disable it.
