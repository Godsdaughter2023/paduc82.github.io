cmdrkeene say:

It's going to be very hard to pinpoint if it's just a matter of milliseconds. Even if your process was the highest priority process and no other task was allowed to execute, a few milliseconds could be the cache on your processor performing garbage collection.  If the poor performance was noted to be in conjunction with high I/O disk activity or CPU utilization in task manager, we could more easily identify the offending process or lower its priority so that your python execution took precedence.


You might try using the SysInternals tools to identify specific activities that are blocking execution of your code, but when it's on such a microscopic scale you may not be able to find it.
