How do I update my Windows Phone 7 - Microsoft Community

emin collaku say:

How do I update my Windows Phone 7

Please who can tell me how to update my windows phone ?
mridul sharma say: Here is the answer: 

To learn what's included in each update and determine which version of Windows Phone you have now, see Phone updates FAQ.

Notes
  • If your phone uses Windows Phone 7 software, it can't be updated to Windows Phone 8. See Which version of Windows Phone do I have? if you're not sure what's on your phone.
  • If you use a Mac, you'll need to use the Windows Phone application for Mac (formerly the Windows Phone Connector for Mac). To learn more, see Synchronising and getting updates with your Mac.
  • Windows 8 Pro supports the Zune software, but Windows RT does not.
How to prepare for an update

  1. Install the Zune software on your PC. You'll need this software to download the phone update.

  2. If your battery level is low, give your phone a quick charge before starting the update. For more info, see My battery is too low to update my phone.

  3. Make some room on your phone for the update. If necessary, remove a few unwanted apps or other large items.

    To see how much space you have on your phone, on Start, flick left to the App list, tapSettings  > About and look at the number to the right of Available storage. For more info, see Making room on your phone to update it.

  4. Clear some space on your computer.

    We'll need some room to download the software update, to save a backup of your existing phone software and settings and possibly to sync media files from your phone to your computer. For more info, see Making room on your computer for phone updates.

  5. Make sure your computer has a reliable Internet connection.

    In some cases, you may need to switch from a wireless (WiFi) connection to a wired (Ethernet) connection. Or you may need to adjust your computer's firewall or proxy settings. For more info, see the Network connection problems in Windows tutorial.

  6. Verify that your phone is set to the correct date and time. That'll help us determine which updates you need. For more info, see Correcting the date and time to update your phone.

How to install an update

  1. When an update is available, we'll notify you on your phone. After you see the notification, pick a convenient time to install the update on your phone. The process can take up to an hour.

    If you miss the notification or don't have time to install the update, we'll remind you again in a few days.

  2. Connect your phone to your primary computer – the first one you connected your phone to – using the USB cable that came with your phone.

    If you're using a different computer than you normally use, please see some important information in the Notes below.

  3. If the Zune software doesn't start automatically when you connect your phone, start it manually.

  4. We might ask you to update the Zune software. If we do, just follow the on-screen instructions. Then restart the sync software.

  5. In the Zune software, click Update Now.

    Make sure you keep your phone connected to your computer until you see a notification that the update is complete.

Notes
  • When you connect your phone to a PC for the first time, the Zune software will automatically create a primary sync relationship between your phone and that computer. Other computers you connect to after that will have a guest sync relationship with your phone. Your primary computer remembers your linked phone and all of its settings and history, so it's the only computer that will create a backup of your phone during the update process. That's why we recommend that you only use your primary computer to update your phone software. If you need to use a different computer, see Synchronising your phone to another computer.
  • To see if an update is available for your phone, on Start, flick left to the Applicationlist, press Settings , then press Phone update.
  • By default, your phone uses your mobile data connection to check for updates and notifies you when an update is found. To change the type of connection for future updates, do this: on Start, flick left to the App list, tap Settings , then tap Phone update.
  • Although we notify you "over the air" (via your phone's data connection) when an update is available to download, we don't deliver the actual software update in this way. You must connect your phone to your computer to download and install available updates.
  • When you install an update, all of your settings, apps and media files are preserved.
For more info go here - http://www.windowsphone.com/en-in/how-to/wp7/basics/how-do-i-update-my-phone-software

john w. vinson say:

What Karl is saying (and I and most serious Access developers would agree) is that your table structure is wrong in its design logic: nothing to do with the field limit. I gather that you have a number of Fields, each pertaining to one particular compliance area; and you're storing multiple compliance scores in each record. 

But what happens if The Powers That Be declare that there are two more areas needed? You change your table structure, rewrite all your queries, curse and swear when this gives you a Query Too Complex error, fix that, redesign your form, redesign your reports...!? OUCH!

I may be misinterpreting, but I'm guessing that you have a very standard "Many to Many" relationship: there are many instances of some entity that you're scoring (employees? something else?), and each entity is scored against many targets ("Address Compliance" for example).

The proper design for this uses THREE tables:

tblEmployees (or whatever the entity is that you're measuring)

  EmployeeID (primary key)

  <information about the entity being scored>

tblTargets

  TargetID (autonumber primary key)

  Target Text ("Address Compliance")

   <other fields about this target, e.g. categories, or weights if you want some targets are more important than others>

tblScores

  ScoreID (autonumber primary key)

  EmployeeID (link to tblEmployees, who's being rated)

  TargetID (rated on what)

  Score (Number/Long Integer, NOT Text)

  <other info e.g. RatingDate, ID of the rater, whatever>

This "tall thin" table would then have (say) thirty-one ROWS for each employee being rated, rather than thirty-one FIELDS. This makes it very easy to create a simple Totals query to sum the scores, with no code and no big messy IIF statements.

This duplicated URL can be created if your SharePoint user profile was removed and your account was recreated. After we remove the user account from the Office 365 portal, My Site Clean Up job will run for removing the user’s personal site. If the user profile is removed before the My Site Clean Up job runs, the previous personal site won’t be removed and when a new user account is created, a new user profile will be synced and a new personal site with the suffix “1” will be generated. 

So, for best practices, we suggest restoring the deleted users instead of recreating a new user with the same account. Or you can select to use a different user principle name. Also, based on the article: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/kb/3042522, the personal site will be removed after 30 days since the user profile is removed. After that, recreating the same user account will be fine.
