xircal say:

Hi,

Well, as you can see from this Google link, you're not alone.

It's probably caused by your graphics processor. Most laptops these days have a dual graphics solution which comprises of a nominal graphics chip on the motherboard which handles common tasks like surfing the Web and a dedicated graphics card for high intensity graphics like games.

But an external monitor usually has a higher resolution which translates to many more pixels than what the screen supplied with the laptop has. Because the graphics chip on the motherboard is a very low power module, it cannot handle the external monitor's resolution and power requirements. So when you add to that by going on the Internet, everything is going to slow down to a crawl.

Not a lot you can do about it unfortunately since you can't force the computer to switch to the dedicted graphics card unless you run a graphics intensive program and even then, you need a higher end machine with at least an Intel i5 processor. 

Hope this helps.

charlie vx say:

Solution 1: Adjust your privacy settings

If the account that’s being used is an adult account, sign in to the account and then make sure it has unrestricted access to content. You can adjust your settings on your Xbox 360 console or online.

Adjust your online privacy settings using your console

  1. Sign in to the account that is having the issue, go to the Settings hub, and select Privacy.
  2. In the Online Safety panel, select Change Settings.
  3. Select Customize.
  4. Select Member Content, and then make sure the setting is Everyone.

Adjust your privacy settings online

  1. Go to the My Account page.
  2. Click Sign In on the top right corner of the page to sign in to your Xbox LIVE account.
  3. Under Settings & Preferences, click Privacy and Online Settings.
  4. On the Adult tab, under Privacy Controls, make sure that Profile Sharing is set to Everyone.

Note Xbox LIVE child accounts will require parental approval from a parent Microsoft account (used to be "Windows Live ID"). For more information, see Xbox LIVE online safety and privacy.

If you keep getting this error message, try the next solution.

-

Solution 2: Adjust your family settings

Family settings can block the display of content that is rated lower than the setting for the console. Make sure that content is not hidden because of your family settings. You can adjust your settings either using your Xbox 360 console or online.

Adjust your family settings using your console

  1. Sign in to the account that’s having the issue, go to the Settings hub, and select Family.
  2. If you have set a Family Settings pass code, enter your code.
  3. Select Restricted Content, and then make sure the setting is Show All Content.

Adjust your family settings online

  1. Go to the My Account page.
  2. Click Sign In on the top right corner of the page to sign in to your Xbox LIVE account.
  3. Under Settings & Preferences, click Privacy and Online Settings.
  4. On the Adult tab, under Content Controls, make sure that Programming is set to Standard.

If you keep getting this error message, try the next solution.

-

Solution 3: Purchase the content on Xbox.com

If you're using an Xbox LIVE Family Pack secondary account, you might need to use the Games Marketplace on Xbox.com to purchase the content and queue the download to your Xbox 360 console. Here’s how:

  1. On your computer, go to the Games Marketplace.
  2. Sign in using the Microsoft account for your Xbox LIVE account.
  3. Search for and select or purchase the content that you want to download.
  4. The download should begin on your Xbox 360 console within 5–10 minutes. To check download status on your console:
  5. On your Xbox 360 controller, press Guide.
  6. Go to Games & Apps.
  7. Select Active Downloads.

If you keep getting this error message, try the next solution.

-

Solution 4: Contact Xbox Support
