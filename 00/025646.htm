mccbleue say:

Unfortunately, once deleted they are deleted. If your husband was suspected of a serious crime whilst carrying the phone, then a police forensic team might be able to at least partially recover the messages, but that's really it and with no guarantees.

 

In computer hard drives, data is not deleted when you hit delete - the computer simply deletes references to the files in its file system directories, the data remains physically on the disc until overwritten with new data. The types of memory used in phones are not the same, deleted is deleted.

rich michaels - mvp (greatcirclelearning.com) say:

Look at the paragraph style that you commonly use and if "Do not check spelling" has accidentally gotten marked on the Language attribute of that style name. If it has you will need to correct that style and get it applied to the Normal template.

To do this open the Styles toolbox (from Menu Bar choose View > Styles), locate it in the listing of style names, hover your cursor over the name, then use the down arrow that appears to open the modify style dialog.

You'll look in the description area for the "Do not check spelling..." indicator. Then use the Format menu to go to the Language setting and remove the "Do not check..." setting. Finally make sure you mark the checkbox to "Add to template," which registers this into your Normal template. Finally click OK. If you are prompted to save the Normal template when you quit Word, make sure you do that.

Hope this helps
