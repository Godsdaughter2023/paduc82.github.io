greginmich say:

What you’re describing is most likely just a generic Tech Support Scam page. These malicious webpages don’t actually download any file-based malware – they just lock up your browser with an onboard script that reloads the fake alert dialog every time you click on the OK button in order to close it. Since the alert dialog is application modal, looping it like this prevents the browser from ever receiving focus, and you’re effectively locked out.

So if you just end the browser process with Task Manager, and then go directly to your home page when you restart Internet Explorer, the page won’t be reloaded, and neither will the malicious script – and you shouldn’t have any more problems with it.

GreginMich

What is an active template in Word? - Microsoft Community

jcmu99 say:

What is an active template in Word?

When attempting to view macros in Word and I choose macros in "all active templates and documents", I just want to be sure that the word "active" means  a document or template that is open at the time. I don't think I have ever heard of a way to deactivate a template. Thanks in advance.
charles kenyon say:

The term active template includes:

  • The "attached template," normally the template upon which the document is based.
  • The Normal Template, which often is the attached template.
  • Loaded Global Templates (also called Add-Ins).

The first two of these will have macros that you can edit or step through. You will not be able to edit or step through macros located in Add-Ins.

Macros in all three of these locations are available to you. Also available would be any macros stored in the document, itself (.docm format).

scott mcdaniel say:

I would strongly suggest you name your controls with meaningful names. "Combo21" does not give any indication of what that combo does, but "cboLastName" certainly does.

You can use the AfterUpdate event of your combos to "cascade" your downstream combos. For example, if I wanted to show ONLY those users in cbCName based on a value I selected in cboCenName, I might do this in the AfterUpdate event of cboCenName

Me.cbCName.RowSource = "SELECT * FROM tblCName WHERE tblCName.CenName='" & Me.cboCenName.Columns(0) & "'"

Of course, you'd have to change those Table, Column and Control names to match the ones in your application.

There are other ways of doing this, of course. I just find the AfterUpdate event "chain" makes the most sense to me.

Also, if the above tables are an actual illustration of your table structure, then be sure that you're storing the value of the Primary Key in your Child tables. While it's not entirely clear which column is the Primary Key of s_school, if it's TheFirstColumn, then then you should store the value of s_school.TheFirstColumn there. You should always store the Primary Key value(s) of the Parent table in the Child table's foreign key column.
