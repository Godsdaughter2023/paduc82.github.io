I can't quickly locate the thread you mentioned, but the macro should be this:

Sub SpellCheck()
    On Error GoTo errhdl
    Dialogs(wdDialogToolsSpellingAndGrammar).Execute
    Exit Sub
errhdl:
    If Err.Number <> 4198 Then
        MsgBox "Error " & Err.Number & vbCr & Err.Description
    End If
End Sub

Use the instructions at http://www.gmayor.com/installing_macro.htm to install it in your Normal.dotm template.

cpitchford say:

I really like it, but I've been using Maemo for years on Nokias other Maemo devices (770, N800 and N810).. It isn't mature, some features still need to be developed..

 

Some people don't like that fact it has only limited portrait mode you can't send messages or emails in portrait mode.. you can make phone calls and now, with an unlockable feature, you can use the web browser in portrait mode although not all features work..

 

the maps software is really a bit basic and has very limited navigation support

 

It doesn't work with Ovi Suite so syncing messages music and images isn't supported at all..

 

It doesn't support multiple profiles (meeting, pager, general, silent) only general and silent

 

It doesn't support MMS

 

Those are the main bad points, I think..

 

but the other stuff is really interesting.. like VoIP and Skype support, integrated instant messenger that combines SMS  and internet instant messaging (MSN/ICQ/AIM are available after installing an update package)

 

It supports numerous video and audio formats and more can be added as codecs

 

The browser is fantastic and support the basics like flash, but also plugins (like adblock)

 

It's a great device, even if it is a bit rough.. but you have to think about exactly what you want.. and DO NO assume it supports something because other Nokia's do.. unless it actually says it in the specs, it might not.. 

 

The lack of MMS has fooled a lot of people..

My ID and my email happen to be the same, let's say *** Email address is removed for privacy ***, so my new skype ID is listed as live:abc123. I don't need this, as I have given my credential as abc123. Is there any way to merge these two IDs into one?

    tldr; I have one Skype account, and one Microsoft account Skype with the same name. How can I merge both of them?

    andre da costa say:
    Why not log in with the existing Skype ID you were previously using?

    Open Start > Settings > Accounts > Your info
    Click sign in with a local account instead

    Enter your current password
    Click Next

    Enter a name, password, confirm it, enter a hint
    Click Next
    Click Sign out and Finish

    Sign into the account

    Open Start > Settings > Accounts > Your info

    Click sign in with a Microsoft account instead

    Enter the email address of the account you want to use
    Click Next
    Enter your Microsoft Account password
    Enter the current Local account password used to sign into the account
    Click Next and you should be signed in with the new account.
