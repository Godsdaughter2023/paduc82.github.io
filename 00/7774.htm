richard xu msft say:

Hi sameerkattel,

 

Thanks for your post. For further help, may I know the detailed steps or information about “I created a retention policy for Skype for Business content for a user from security and compliance”?

 

Generally, In place hold and retention policy do not effect each other and they have no direct relationship. Retention policy works for “delete”, “move”, “archive” for the messages you want. An In-Place Hold preserves all mailbox content, including deleted items and original versions of modified items. All such mailbox items are returned in an In-Place eDiscovery search. When you place an In-Place Hold on a user's mailbox on, the contents in the corresponding archive mailbox (if it's enabled) are also placed on hold, and returned in a eDiscovery search.

The Win 8 interface made long strides in the usability of the PC and, finally, a move out of the hobbyist phase - realizing that it is now a tool more focused on the work done in the LoB applications than the PC itself. It has reduced week long training sessions to a few seconds of instruction - "Press the Windows key and start typing the name of the program or document you want to use..." Since the interface is largely controlled from the keyboard, mouse problems are a thing of the past.

Add to it that every thing looks the same (though controlled differently) on all the devices we require employees to use and all training becomes a breeze.

The visible parts of the PC are now just the monitor and the keyboard, however, the power of todays PC hardware is certainly not wasted. It is simply used in the applications that run on the PC not in support of the PC itself.

The set and forget capabilities of the new OS's are important also. Again, it is reduced to simple instructions "Log in when you sit down, log out when you leave - The PC and the IT department have taken care of everything else for you." The PC is configured, installed at the desk and that is it. The user does not even concern themselves with how to turn it on or off.

It should also be noted that the simple instructions and training steps are now the same, whether the employee is on real-estate provided by us or a thousand miles away in their own home or office.

BTW: I do see the current trend back to a start menu a terrible waste of time.
