in outlook 2010, my inbox "to" column sometimes shows an email address and sometimes my name, since i use one inbox for several email addresses depending on the purpose of the emails, i would like to always see the incoming email address. is there a way to configure this? thank you.

 

What gets displayed in the To/CC fields depends on the following

#1 - if there is <both> an Email DisplayName and an Email Address - Outlook displays the Email DisplayName
#2 - if there is no Email DIsplayName then the Email Address is shown

What comes in on a given message is determined by what info the "sender" has/uses for a given contact. There are no options that over-ride the behaviour of what gets displayed (i.e. always show email address). Your only option is to have unique display names for each email account

 

If you want to ensure that all emails sent to a given email address of yours are shown together,  one approach is to set a custom view for your inbox grouping your emails by "E-Mail Account" creating a unique Account name for each email account (default is email address so you may not want to change anything).

Done by 

    File --> Advanced Settings --> 

         Email Tab --> Highlight each email account -->

                Change --> clcik <More settings> button  --->

                     give each <EMail Account> a unique name (found on the General Tab after clicking on <More settings>

 

Once that's done - customize the Inbox view via

   --> View Settings
      --> Group By - Email Account (and whatever other <group> settings you desire

By removing someone from your contact list, you’ll always appear offline to each other but you’ll still be able to contact each other. To stop someone from contacting you, you’ll need to block them.
