davem121 say:
Hi Michael,

When building a new system and installing Windows, here are a few tips:

Simplify your system:

Connect the SSD
Remove the Second Hard Drive - if you have 2 drives
Remove the Graphics Card - if you have a dedicated GPU
Connect you Monitor to the on-board graphics
Make sure your RAM is seated correctly

Go into BIOS and make sure SATA mode is set to AHCI

Install Windows - let Windows look after the Partitioning during the installation
(If partitioned from previous attempts at installing, remove all partitions)

After Windows boots to the Desktop
Install all necessary Drivers, except the Video Card Driver (if Applicable)

If you have a second Hard Drive:
Turn off your PC
Connect the Second Hard Drive
Boot your PC and make sure you can see and access the second hard drive

If you have a Dedicated GPU:
Turn off your PC
Insert the Graphics Card and connect all applicable cables
Boot your PC
Install the Video Card Software and Drivers
Your system will restart

Hopefully now you will have a fully functioning system . . .
