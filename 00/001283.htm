alexsun_2012 say:

Hi Fulvio,

Yes, you can choose either to create a new retention policy or edit the default retention policy. To force the retention policy to be implemented, you can use the Start-ManagedFolderAssistant cmdlet to immediately force messaging records management (MRM) processing of mailbox that you need.

The "Recoverable Items 14 days move to archive" tag is included in the default policy and it only applies to mailboxes with archives enabled. Messages are automatically moved from the Recoverable Items folder in the user's primary mailbox to the Recoverable Items folder in the archive mailbox after 14 days. So if you don't want it, you can delete this tag from the retention policy.

Incorrect settings for the retention policy could lead to a lot of confusion of user's mailbox. So it is suggested you create a seperated retention policy and test it before you deploy it on your users' mailboxes. If anything is unclear or you run into any problems, please let us know.
