After many hours of installing, uninstalling, and reinstalling driver updates, multiple runs of the Surface Diagnostic Toolkit, and thinking I had the problem solved only to have it reappear after a reboot, I think I have finally fixed the issue. I hope this is helpful to anyone experiencing the same problem.

In Windows Device Manager (right-click on Start and select "Device Manager"), I expanded "Bluetooth" and uninstalled "Qualcomm Atheros QCA61x4 Bluetooth," including deleting the driver files from the tablet.
