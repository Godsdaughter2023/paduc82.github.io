Hi Frank,

 

Besides turning the printer on and off, there may be settings which need to be done (on the printer most likely or possibly on the computer software associated with the printer) to get them reset and realigned and working.  I know my own printer requires such action even when changing just the ink cartridges let alone the printheads (but it's not an HP or that type of printer).  The bottom line is that this is almost certainly not a computer or Microsoft issue but an issue with the HP printer itself.

 

You need assistance from HP technical support with their printer.  I recommend starting here: http://h10025.www1.hp.com/ewfrf/wc/product?product=2512008&lc=en&cc=us&dlc=en&lang=en&cc=us which is specific to your printer and then proceed from there to check all available options including the forums.  This is by far your best option and the one most likely to succeed.

 

Another option is to start over from scratch with the printer as if it were brand new and run a full setup from scratch including attaching it to the computer/network and all all appropriate drivers, firmware, and software.  If you can find a reset button or option, that's probably where to start (but be sure you know all the proper settings before you begin - or at least the ones being used - though you should use the appropriate new settings as the ones in place now may be part of the problem).

 

If they can't/won't assist you (or it's too expensive for your taste if the printer is out of warranty - though I suspect you can find some way to get free or inexpensive help or maybe it's still best to pay since they're truly better than the next option which is not likely to be much cheaper anyway), then perhaps you can try a local reputable computer repair shop (NOT Geek Squad or anyplace like that) that says they can handle your printer and see if they will cost less (though it will be an estimate and if parts are needed those will come from HP anyway and will be additional and, of course, there's generally no absolute guarantee).

 

After that, I'm afraid it may be time for a replacement printer (but I'm sure either HP or a computer repair shop will be able to confirm that for you - or give you a repair cost that's essentially the same as a new printer anyway).

 

I hope this helps.

 

Good luck!
