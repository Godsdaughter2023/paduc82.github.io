brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:
Windows 10 Mail can use Office 365, if you have a subscription, because it's a mail service. Outlook 2016 is also a (completely different) mail client (produced by a completely separate product group) that can use an Office 365 subscription. Windows 10 Mail supports three mail protocols: POP, IMAP, and Exchange ActiveSync. This latter was actually designed to keep the old Windows Mobile platforms synched with an Exchange service and so isn't as robust as some other protocols. Outlook 2016 supports these three protocols plus the Exchange protocol. The Exchange protocol is more fully-featured than Exchange ActiveSync for connecting to and Exchange service. Thus, you can do more with Outlook 2016 connected to an Outlook.com mailbox that you can do with Windows 10 Mail connected to that same mailbox.
