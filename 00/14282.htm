frazzydee say:

> Like somebody else wrote above, why not just tap the 'end call' button?

 

The 'end call' button declines the call.  You can do this by tapping "end call" or the top-right button.  Declining a call is different from silencing it.

 

Declining the call sends communication to your network that it's declined, and the caller gets a busy signal or voicemail.  He'll know you declined the call.

 

Silencing a call allows your phone to still ring (shows as ringing on the screen), but no audible/vibration notification.  Caller will not know you're ignoring their call.

frazzydee say:

PROBLEM SOLVED: WORKAROUND

 

As usual Nokia has done something weird.  But as usual they've given us enough flexibility to work around it :smileyvery-happy:

 

1) Download "silence.mp3" from here: http://csclub.uwaterloo.ca/~f2damji/silence.mp3

It's just 1 second of pure silence.

 

2) Transfer the mp3 to your phone.

 

3) Create a new profile, you can call it "Silent2"

 

4) Set ringing & video call tones to silence.mp3

Ringing type: Ringing

Message & email alert tones: silence.mp3

Vibrating alert on

Keypad & warning tones: Off

 

Now as long as you are on that profile, whenever you get a call it will vibrate, but not ring (same behaviour as vibration mode on silent).

 

You can silence the call by flipping the phone, or by tapping the top-right button, which will now say "Silence" (instead of "Decline" like it says on Silent mode)

 

Hope this works, post any questions here.
