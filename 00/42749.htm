Does your system have a SATA drive for the hard disk?

If yes, you will need to go into your computer BIOS and locate the Serial ATA or SATA  configuration section (or sometimes is called On Chip Config) and change the mode from the AHCI (Advanced Host Controller Interface) to IDE or Compatibility and then your XP CD will see the drive just fine.  Make a not of the original setting so you can change it back when you are done.

You have to change it to something the program on the CD understands.

Formatting wipes your Hard Drive of the Operating System, your Data, and all Programs.

It wipes it clean of everything.


Here is how to do a clean install:


Go into your Bios/Setup, or Boot Menu, at startup, and change the Boot Order to make the DVD/CD drive 1st in the Boot Order, then reboot with the disk in the Drive.

 

At startup/power on you should see at the bottom of the screen either F2 or DELETE, to go into Bios/Setup, or F12 for the Boot Menu
