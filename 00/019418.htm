bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

Just to be clear, AutoCorrect does have a number of built-in corrections which it makes automatically, but it isn't equipped or intended to correct every conceivable typo/misspelling any given user might make.

What Phillip described is the proper means of adding more items to the list, but IMHO it should be used only for specific errors you make on a consistent basis -- we all have our little list of 'pet words' which we inevitably mistype or spell wrong :-) It's also handy for short, specific custom items [e.g., acronyms, company names, etc.] but for longer bodies of text or graphic content AutoText or the Scrapbook are more appropriate.

Errors such as you cite which occur only on a random basis are usually caught by the Spelling & Grammar feature. It doesn't make corrections automatically but does offer alternatives from which to select the correct replacement.
