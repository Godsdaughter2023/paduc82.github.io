jllatham say:

Since you are reporting worksite, drive/prep time, vacation and overtime hours each separately, it's fairly straight forward.

Assuming your table is laid out with the word "Period:" in cell A1, that puts Gross Pay at E7 and the amount for E7 at E8.  In E8 Put this formula

=(F3*B4)+(F4*B7)+(F5*B4)+(F6*D4)

 

You don't actually need the () in it, but it helps identify what's being done:

(F3*B4) is worksite hours * worksite rate

(F4*B7) is drive/prep hours * hourly drive rate

(F5*B4) is vacation hours * worksite rate

(F6*D4 is overtime hours * overtime rate

 

With 16.5 hours worksite hours PLUS a different 16.5 hours of drive time, the Gross Pay would be $268.13:

16.5 * 9 = 148.50

16.5 * 7.25 = 119.63

0 for vacation and overtime.

 

 

By the way - check your formula in B8 (Total Taxes and Regular Deductions).  It should be something like

=B5+D5+B6+D6+D7

and then the formula over in F8 you could have

=B8+D8

to add in the "other deductions" to the taxes+regular deductions calculation.

graham mayor say:
There are several questions here on several levels.

1. Your 'computer' cannot possibly change Microsoft Word for Open Office. It can associate files with Open Office in preference to Word, if both are installed, and each will squabble over the registry association, but it will not replace Word 2007.

2. You can install Word Perfect is it is compatible, and you don't need the permission of Microsoft to do that. It will however only tend to confuse matters further by having yet another program that will vie for control of documents.

3. Microsoft Office 2010 is not free.

If you have Office 2007 than it would make sense to use that. Uninstall Open Office and repair Office 2007.
