pamelam. say:

Why would deleting hidden networks increase battery life?

I connect my Samsung Focus to a "hidden" network at work. When I clicked on the advanced setting my phone shows a warning of sorts stating "deleting hidden networks can increase battery life". I certainly like an increased battery life, but I don't want to broadcast the ssid of our work wireless router. Any ideas why this is the case?
Thanks,
Pam
entegy say:
With a hidden network, the burden of the discovery falls onto the client (in this case the phone), so a phone must always broadcast, searching for the access point. Whereas on a normal network, the access point is broadcasting, allowing clients to discover it much faster and save power.

Hidden networks are dumb. They provide no extra security and because of the constant broadcasting of the client, they can be revealed anyway. If you have this power, unhide the network and just make sure you're using WPA2 encryption, maybe MAC address filtering if you really want to. Your battery devices with WiFi will thank you.
