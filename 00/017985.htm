Possible Sync Error 86000107 Solution - Microsoft Community

z_fisherww say:

Possible Sync Error 86000107 Solution

I successfully used Activesync 4.5 for a long time to sync my PDA with my home and office computers. After replacing the PDA with a Pantech Duo smartphone, error 86000107 reared its ugly head. I'm writing this to publish my solution, which probably applies to other devices.

First of all, the number of discussions and suggested solutions on error 86000107 implies to me that the error number doesn't tell you much. About all it means is that "sync failed." Good luck to figure out why.

If you are in my situation, i.e., trying to sync a device with two different computers, a likely cause is that Activesync can't figure out how to reconcile the various appointments, tasks, contacts, etc. that exist on the two different computers. When Activesync gets confused, it just gives up and gives you error 86000107. The following steps worked for me and assume that Activesync 4.5 is installed on both PCs:

1. BACK UP the Outlook files on both computers, and back up the device if it will let you. The following steps CAN result in loss of data (specifics below), so you need a way to start over if disaster happens.

2. Delete all devices from Activesync in both computers, and delete all computers from Activesync on the device. Caution: this may cause you to lose data copied to that computer/device by Activesync. That's why you need to do step 1. It may be necessary to manually re-enter some deleted data.

3. Make one of the computers the "master", preferably the one that seems closest to having everything you want after step 2. Clean up Outlook on that computer, i.e., archive or delete all unneeded entries, empty the deleted items folder, and compact the data file. BACK UP Outlook again, preferably to a location separate from what you used for step 1.

3.a. It is best to also delete any recurring tasks, since they tend to confuse Activesync when a device syncs to two computers. Write them down for re-entry after you get sync working on both computers.

4. DELETE all Outlook entries from the mobile device and the second PC. It can be challenging to find them all, but doing so improves the chances of a successful sync. In Outlook 2007, the calendar will show "next appointment" and "previous appointment" entries to help you find them all.

4.a. Be sure to delete all "all day" appointments, especially those that are recurring (they make a mess when you try to sync). I found it necessary to step through every day of the year on Calendar to get them all.

Objective: when steps 3 and 4 are done, you have a clean and complete Outlook data file on ONE PC. The mobile device and second PC have EMPTY Outlook files.

5. Re-boot both the master PC and the mobile device. Open, then close Outlook on the master PC. Connect the device, which should initiate it's initial setup in Activesync. When setup is complete, Activesync should copy all data in the selected categories from the master PC to the mobile device. This may take a while.

6. After Activesync reports "Synchronized", disconnect the mobile device and verify that the desired info is indeed there. If not, check the discussion boards for other solutions besides this one.

7. Be sure you have done steps 2 and 4 on the second PC (delete all devices from Activesync and empty all parts of the Outlook data file). Re-boot the phone and the second PC.

8. Open, then close Outlook on the second PC. Connect the device, which should initiate it's initial setup in Activesync. When setup is complete, Activesync should copy all data in the selected categories from the device to the second PC. This may take a while.

9. Disconnect the device and check for whether the second PC contains the desired Outlook data. If not, check the discussion boards for other solutions besides this one.

10. It may be necessary to do some cleanup in Outlook on one or both computers, especially if you did not delete all recurring tasks. At this point, Outlook entries can be made to either PC or the mobile device, and Activesync should transfer the entries to the other machines at the next sync.

Good luck!

Comments/improvement suggestions welcome.

I have a sneaking suspicion that there is a rather big flaw in OneDrive that doesn't appear to occur in Dropbox or Google Drive. I don't think it's quite the same problem that you are reporting but I'll stick it down anyway.

Imagine you have a virtual machine running with synchronised folders and you take a snapshot. Or you take an image of a real PC using something Macrium Reflect. Time then passes and changes are made to the document library (or your own OneDrive). Files are added, deleted and updated. You then, for whatever reason, you restore the snapshot or restore the image - not exactly uncommon but not entirely unusual. I routinely image a computer if we're about to try something new, like a major Windows update and then roll it back.

The flaw - the resumed OneDrive system incorrectly assumes it's the master copy and then proceeds to remove all changes made since the snapshot/image was taken. It merrily deletes any new files, restores deleted files and replaces updated files with old version. This is a horrible bug.

I really do need to see if I can repeat it and get a report in as it's an accident waiting to happen.

But it indicates to me that OneDrive's logic for handling updates is not robust.
