how to configure anitvirus to alow eTax - Microsoft Community

debgibbon say:

how to configure anitvirus to alow eTax

Ho do I configure my antivirus to alow eTax?
kosh vorlon say:

Hi Deb,

 

That really shouldn't be required (removing MSE protection - I download and install all sorts of programs all the time and have never needed to disable MSE to do anything) - or you can try it anyway and see if works without actually blocking you or causing a problem even if it says it might and only do this only if it really does block you. That is what I'd do first and then contact eTax technical support to see if you can simply exclude a file or process rather than disable protection completely - even if I honestly don't think that really will prove to be necessary. The caution may be there because some other AV product has caused this in the past and was just never removed or one still does so today, but I truly doubt it applies to MSE. However, if you can find no other alternative, you just need to disable MSE from working in real time.

 

To do that, open MSE and Go to Settings and click on Real Time and then uncheck the box at the top to disable real time. Click on Save to save the changes. It should not be necessary to reboot - you'll know if the MSE icon turns red and the MSE home page also gets a red border instead of the normal green one and says that MSE is not enabled and not protecting your system.

 

Actually, before you do that, close all IE windows and tabs except perhaps the one needed for a download (if that's the issue - otherwise close them all). Close your email program if you use a separate program from IE. Close anything else that you're using that's open and using the internet. If you don't need the internet, disconnect from it entirely as the safest way to go.

 

Do what you need to do and then immediately re-enable real time in MSE by rechecking the box you unchecked and saving it and confirming that the MSE screen goes from a red border to a green border, the Home Tab says MSE is enabled and protecting your system, and the icon in the notication area returns to a green color.

 

After the download (if it was a download), right click on it and click on scan with MSE to verify the file isn't infected.

 

Because you were unprotected for a while, I recommend you update MSE and any other security software you use and run full scans as soon as you can to be sure nothing snuck past during that period of risk.

 

I hope this helps. If using a product other than MSE, please contact technical support for that product for further information - but first I'd try to do the installation without making any changes to see if it works and the notice you may be seeing is just from the past and all works fine - unless you have clear evidence from trying to install and/or use it that there is a genuine conflict of some sort. At that point, I'd begin to wonder if the problem is turning off your AV protection or believing your AV protection which may be protecting you from potential infections (as I noted quite a few different companies producing "eTax" and don't know which you are using or where/how you got it).

 

Good luck!

 

Kosh
