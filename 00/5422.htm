jay freedman say:
None of those files are actually your documents. The ones with .TMP extensions are temporary files that Word creates for its own use, and they're supposed to be deleted automatically when you close the document. The ones that start with ~$ are "owner" files that tell Word when a document is in use and can't be opened by someone else. They, too, should be automatically deleted. For a description of what temp files are used for, read http://support.microsoft.com/?kbid=211632.

If Word crashes in the middle of a session, these files can be left behind. Here's a page about how to clean up after it: http://www.gmayor.com/what_to_do_when_word_crashes.htm

You mentioned "plugging in" your hard drive. Is that an external drive, connected to the computer by a USB cable? If so, it's possible that in previous sessions you turned it off or unplugged the cable while Word was still open. That would explain all the orphaned temporary files. You should always close Word first, and then use the Safely Remove Hardware icon in the task bar to eject the drive before you turn it off or unplug it.

How to turn off "Microsoft Office Upload Center" in Office - Microsoft Community

robertconnor say:

How to turn off "Microsoft Office Upload Center" in Office 2010

As stated, I've recently installed office 2010 32-bit on Windows 7 UltSP1.  Now I have an orange icon in the tray which highlights as "Microsoft office Upload Center" and I see no way to disable this "mini-app"  Any ideas?  I'm sure there must be a way.
robertconnor say:

I found out how.  You can block its startup by unchecking it in msconfig> startup, the MSOSYNC entry,   Also it can be blocked in a registry edit:

HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Run

Remove the "OfficeSyncProcess" DWord entry

 

Following a restart the system tray icon did not appear, nor do I see any process for it running.  This issue has been discussed in some previous posts related to Office 2010 integration wiht sharepoint and SkyDrive, apparently.  I had just not encountered it before having Office 2010 on a machine.
