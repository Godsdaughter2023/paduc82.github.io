brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:
In reply to deleted message

Move the mouse pointer to the beginning of the link.  Depress the left mouse button and hold it.  Drag the pointer across the letters of the link, which will turn blue as you drag, until you reach the last letter of the link.  Release the mouse button  The entire link should be selected and show as white letters on a blue background.  Now, either press Ctrl+C (that's the Ctrl key and the letter "C" at the same time) to copy the selection or position the mouse pointer over any portion of the selection and depress and release the right mouse button.  A context menu should appear and one of the selections should be "Copy".  Drag the mouse pointer to that choice and depress and release the left mouse button.

Now, move the mouse pointer to the Address field of your web browser (where you enter URLs or web links) and click on any text that appears there.  The text should become selected (white letters on blue background).  Either press Ctrl+V (that's the Ctrl key and "V" at the same time) or right-click the selection and choose "Paste" from the context menu that appears.  Now your link should be in the browser's address field.  Press Enter.
