Yes that 'tweak' is easy but it doesn't agree with what you said in your first post in which we ignored the first $1000, you now seem to be saying for 6000 we calculate a commission on all of it i.e 5000 @ 10% and the next $1000 @ 12%. In the first post your said

10% of the sales price over $1,000 but less than $5,000; plus

15% of the sales price for anything over $10,000

 

=SUMPRODUCT(--(A1>{0;5000;10000}), (A1-{0;5000;10000}), {0.1;0.02;0.03})

What this now does from

0 to 5000 = 10%
5001 to 10000 12%
10001 > 1.5%

You should be able to alter it yourself as long as you understand how the formula works. the first 2 array are the thresholds. When we get to the percentages note they sum. i.e the first is 0.1 or 10% the second 0.02 is added to the first by the formula and gives 12% etc
