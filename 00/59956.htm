Settings Explanations - Microsoft Community

py687 say:

Settings Explanations

Can someone explain what some of the options for Microsoft Minesweeper do? Mainly "Double tap to Chord" and "Allow Quick Mode change."

2 people found this helpful

ideas man 360 say:

A "chord" is when you reveal all adjacent squares around the tapped/clicked one if all apparent mines have been flagged, or the cell is empty. Therefore, "Double tap to Chord" means you must double tap the cell to invoke this behaviour, rather than single tap. This option only applies to touch devices, mouse clicking will still require double-clicking regardless (Or single middle clicking). This should be turned on if you want protection from accidental revealing from unintentional taps of the game board's numbers.

 

"Allow Quick Mode change" is also only for touch users, and enables you to change the tapping action between tap to reveal or tap to flag (As an alternative to tap and hold to flag) by tapping in a revealed region. In the top-right is the toggle button for this behaviour which you have to tap when you don't have Quick Mode change turned on, however, if you turn it on, you don't need to tap that button, just tap the revealed cells to toggle the behaviour.

 

"Use swipe in controls", also a touch only option, allows you to swipe down cells to toggle the flagged/questioned state, rather than using Quick Mode change or tap and hold.

 

Personally, I leave the first two off and have the last one on when I'm playing it on a touch screen, it's much more fun and quicker.
