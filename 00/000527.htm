Microsoft Wi-Fi is now available for Windows 10 Insiders

We are excited to announce that Windows 10 Insiders have a new way to get online – Microsoft Wi-Fi!

 

Microsoft Wi-Fi is a new way to connect to the Internet. At various supported hotspots, you can buy a Microsoft Wi-Fi access plan that best meets your needs.

Some frequently asked questions about Microsoft Wi-Fi:

Q. How do I purchase a Microsoft Wi-Fi plan?

 

A. You can only purchase Wi-Fi plans at Microsoft Wi-Fi hotspots.  These hotspots are identified by the special annotation they are given in the Wi-Fi SSID list found in Network Fly out and Network Settings.  The annotation reads “Buy Microsoft Wi-Fi from the Windows Store”.  When you see one of these networks and you would like to purchase, select the network and follow the on-screen instructions.   Note: The Microsoft Wi-Fi app from Windows 10 Store is required to complete the purchase.                            

 

Q. Where do I find a purchase confirmation or see my Microsoft Wi-Fi receipt?

 

A. Microsoft Wi-Fi purchases happen through the Windows 10 Store.  You can see a receipt of your purchase at account.microsoft.com.  We also send you an e-mail receipt for convenience.

Q. Can I add more time or data to my current plan before it expires?

 

A. At this time, customers are not able to add minutes or data to active plans. As such, you will need to use all of your current access Microsoft Wi-Fi plan before purchasing another plan for the same area.

 

Q. Where is my Microsoft Wi-Fi plan valid to use?

 

A. Plans are valid at all Microsoft Wi-Fi hotspots in the country in which the plan was purchased.

 

Q. Can I suspend my Microsoft Wi-Fi plan if I am not using it?

 

A. Once you have made a purchase, your Microsoft Wi-Fi plan is active and counting down to expiration regardless of whether you are in range or connected.

 

Q. If I purchase a Microsoft Wi-Fi plan and travel outside of the region, can I still access the internet via my current Wi-Fi plan?

 

A. Plans are only valid in the country in which they were originally purchased.  If you travel outside of that country you’ll need to purchase a new plan specific to the new country.

 

Q. My purchase failed, what happened?

 

A. The most common reason could be that you are no longer within range of the currently selected plan. Try moving back to where the plan was first identified to complete your purchase or select a newly available plan for your current area.

charles kenyon say:
Hi,

Simplest is probably to get it into a pdf and run OCR on it.

Since Word 2013, Word has had the ability to directly open pdf document. However, some pdf documents (from scanners mostly) are really pictures of text rather than the text itself. Just as you can't look under the hood of a picture of a car, you can't edit a picture of a document.

In that case, you need to convert the picture to text. This is a process known as optical character recognition. This is built into Adobe Acrobat and is also in Office OneNote. Most scanner software comes with an OCR component as well. Once you have text, you can edit it with Word.

If you simply want to write on the document (but not in it) you can add a Text Box floating on top of the document layer, whether or not it has been put through the OCR process.

Word documents that have been saved as PDF will not need the OCR process, they retain their text, although not all of their Word structure and formatting.

Finally, documents converted from pdf (or really any other format) to Word can be tough to edit because the conversion process never has a one-to-one matching of how formatting is done. This means that a converted document will seldom be formatted in Word in a way that makes sense. An example is multiple section breaks to change margins, where in Word you would simply change the paragraph indent.

I hope this information helps. Please let me know if you have any more questions or require further help.
