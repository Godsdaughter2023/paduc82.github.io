joeu2004 say: Karen wrote:

I have to figure out how many years it will take for my savings to increase from $1,000,000 to $1,850,000 at 3% interest with no interim payments.  I have tried the NPER formula but get an error


=NPER(3%,0,-1000000,1850000)

The key is that sign for PV and FV must be opposite.  I use negative PV and positive FV.  But you could use positive PV and negative FV.

[EDIT]....  You will notice that NPER returns a non-integer.  You need to decide what to do with the fractional part.  There is no single "right" answer.  It depends on your goals.  Perhaps the following will meet your expectation:

=ROUNDUP(NPER(3%,0,-1000000,1850000), 0)

hansv mvp say:

The present value and future value must have opposite signs:

 

Enter -1000000 in A1 (you put money in the bank), 1850000 in A2 (you get money from the bank) and 3% in A3.

In A4, enter the formula =NPER(A3,0,A1,A2)

It should result in ~ 20.8, i.e. you'll reach your goal in 21 years.
