My (and your) Honest Opinion of GoW: Judgement - Microsoft Community

quanfreeman say:

My (and your) Honest Opinion of GoW: Judgement

I apologize ahead of time for people who saw this in another thread, but, man! This has become the argument of the century, and I felt that my previous post deserved it's own thread. I'd like to hear more from the community. Here's what I posted earlier:

 

As a person who went out and bought the limited editions for all 3 GoW games, I would say that I'm a fan of the series. I bought it Tuesday afternoon and took Wednesday off, played for about 6 hours and ran through the Judgement Campaign. My honest opinion, which I will try and give as objectively as possible, is that it's a better-than-average game. Here's what People Can Fly dropped the ball on:

1. They packaged what could have been a good year's worth of DLC for GoW3 into Judgement. Everthing from the game modes, MP maps and campaign missions, could have been periodic DLC for GoW3. This game as DLC for Gears 3 would have been disgustingly awesome, but as a prequel stand-alone game, it's just too short and shallow to justify the $60 price tag (I guess this is why there was no limited edition of Judgement: There would've been riots)

2. I understand that People Can Fly probably wanted to put their own stamp on the game, and make it more fast-paced, but they completely missed the ball on what made Gears unique. This game was not made to be a run-and-gun type of game. Not a huge fan of Cliff B., but paraphrasing his intro in Gears of War 1, he said that the idea for Gears came from playing paintball. To get the player to feel that rush of adrenaline and make them think twice about strafing around characters like 1st person shooters. As a result, the Gears gameplay always had an underpinning of strategy, in both the campaign and MP modes. I rarely felt the need to HAVE to take cover in Judgement until the last boss. Part of this reason is that most of the Horde just bum-rushed you during the whole game, like they didn't need to take cover (What is that about?).

3. One of the things that kept me coming back to the GoW series was the story. I got so immersed in the story that when Dom died in GoW3, I teared up a little bit (don't lie and say you didn't either). In previous installments of GoW, the game just flowed, and the story along with it. Judgement obliterated that. My first reaction when I played Judgement and going from one declassified skull cog to the next, I said to myself, 'This feels like playing Lost Planet, then playing Lost Planet 2.' Every portion of the game was segmented, taking away from the story. Don't get me wrong, you can skip the declassified missions and play straight through, but what's the fun in that, where the character unlocks and goodies are tied to the stars you receive from de-class missions?

4. The emphasis on making the game faster and multplayer gaming took it's toll on the weapons. When I first played GoW, I hated having to use the D-pad to switch weapons, after about a half an hour in, it was the only way to implement it. The controls felt right; D-pad to switch weapons, LB to see where your squad was, Y-Button for event looks. How many of you, the first time playing Judgment pressed the LB to see where your squad/objectives were and almost blew yourselves up when you tossed a grenade? Now with the dedicated LB-grenade button, it takes some getting used to, to say the least, especially when you're trying to plant a grenade as a proximity mine. The other thing is; Where is my Snub pistol? Why'd they only allow you to carry two weapons? How much fun was it to use someone as a meatshield, whip out your Boltok and dome a couple enemies? You can still do that, but you must use the weapon you're carrying; if you happen to have a Snub or a Boltok, then you're in luck. Otherwise, you're using whatever other weapon you're carrying. Plus, I always felt that by always having a pistol, no matter what, you can get out of any situation if you run out of ammo for your other weapons. Well, that's gone.

There are a couple of other things that I can nitpick regarding this game, but it's not really worth the extra keystrokes. Overall, the game isn't terrible. It's slightly above average. But People Can Fly's focus on Multiplayer effectively wiped out the identity of the game; sure it feels like Gears for the most part, in terms of character controls, but it doesn't FEEL like Gears. Baird's really serious, Cole is eerily quiet (another "What?" moment), and the other members of Kilo are interesting, but never fleshed out.

Not, bad, but not worth $60.

I don't really want to put in the openDNS servers in case I decide to revert back to my ISP servers. I'd rather keep it automatic and make the changes on the router.

a. user say:

Go to Start / Control Panel / Internet Options / Connections / select the connection and click Settings.  Click on Properties and go to Networking and select TCP/IPv4.  Under TCP/IPv4 select Properties and make sure the first page says Obtain DNS Server Address automatically.  Click on Advanced and click on DNS.  Make sure the only two boxes checked are Append Primary and Connection Specific DNS Suffixes and Append parent suffixes of the primary DNS suffix - NOtHING ELSE.  Click OK and save this.  That should resolve the problem.  But I suspect you've already done this (probably many times) so let's move on to something else.

The only other option I can think of is to reset the TCP/IP stack which may get rid of the incorrect addresses and start it up fresh with the correct settings.  Here's the procedure:http://geekswithblogs.net/mattcampbell/archive/2007/09/30/Resetting-your-whole-IP-stack-in-Vista.aspx.  I don't know if this will help, but if you're having troubles with TCP/IP, this is one way many people use to resolve them.  You will then need to resetup the TCP/IP settings and then you can see if this did the trick.

I hope this helps.

Good luck!

mouneshwar r say:

Hi Dennis,

 

Instead of uninstalling and re-installing the drivers, you can try updating the drivers.

The Code 10 error code is generated when Device Manager has insufficient information or the error that is bubbled up by the device drivers is not recognized by Device Manager. Additionally, this error may occur when one of the drivers that the device needs does not start. If many drivers are being used by a particular device, it can be difficult to determine the one that failed.

 

I would suggest you to follow the steps from the link given below which would help you to fix this issue.

 

Step 1: To update the device drivers

 

Description of Code 10-related errors that Device Manager generates in Windows-based computers

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/943104

 

Also, you can try the steps mentioned below to see if this helps you to fix the issue.

 

Step 2: To disable IPv6

Try uninstalling IPv6 on all interfaces, removing any IPv6 virtual adapters, and resetting the TCP/IP stack.

a.    To remove IPv6, go into the properties for each network adapter and either uncheck the box next to the protocol "Internet Protocol version 6 (TCP/IPv6), that will disable it, or select it and click uninstall, which will remove it off the computer.

b.    Then go into device manager and remove any 4to6 adapters, miniport adapters, or tunnel adapters.

TCP –Transfer Control Protocol

IP –Internet Protocol

 

Note: You should do this for each network connection. Also, disabling IPv6 will disable homegroup if your computer is on a homegroup.

 

For more information, you may refer the following KB articles:

How to disable certain Internet Protocol version 6 (IPv6) components in Windows Vista, Windows 7 and Windows Server 2008

http://support.microsoft.com/kb/929852

 

Hope this information is helpful and let me know if you need any further assistance.
