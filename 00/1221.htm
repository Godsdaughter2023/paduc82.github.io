Mailboxes - Recover Deleted Mailbox - Microsoft Community

alex rizeanu msft say:

Mailboxes - Recover Deleted Mailbox

Technical Level : Intermediate

Summary

Scenario

This workflow deals specifically with scenarios where the customer has a soft-deleted or inactive mailbox that they want to recover. Mailboxes that have been permanently purged from Exchange Online are not recoverable.


Details

Data Collection

Follow the steps in Collect Recipient Info from Powershell

Solution Analysis

Review the possible ways a mailbox can be disconnected/deleted. You will need to understand what has happen to the mailbox and possible reverse action.

Mailbox Recovery

Mailboxes Disconnected by License Removal

Any mailbox that a license is removed for, with the exception of resource and shared mailboxes which don't require a license by default, will be disconnected and hidden from view. You have 30 days to reapply the license in order to reconnect the original mailbox. If 31 days or more pass, you may end up provisioning a new mailbox.

Soft-Deleted mailboxes or Soft-Deleted user accounts in AzureAD that still exist in the RecycleBin can be restored easily through the portal or through PowerShell.

The soft-deleted mailbox in Exchange Online will still have a populated and matching ExternalDirectoryObjectID; see Mailboxes - Sync Anchor Valuesfor a discussion of this attribute.

Hard-Deleted Mailboxes

  • An account that was hard-deleted in AzureAD was removed with the -RemoveFromRecyleBin switch, or 30 days passed since its soft-deletion.
  • Because the account is flushed from AzureAD, you cannot restore this user account any longer
  • The Exchange Online account will remain in a soft-deleted state, but its ExternalDirectoryObjectID will be cleared, indicating a hard-delete.
  • There are three possible ways to purge the mailbox from EXO at this point:
    1. 30 days pass from the initial soft-deletion time, according to the account's WhenSoftDeleted timestamp.
    2. You run Remove-Mailbox -PermanentlyDelete against it.
    3. You synchronize a new account from on-premises that has a matching ExchangeGUID or ArchiveGUID and apply an EXO license to it.
  • There are two ways to recover the mailbox before it is purged:
    1. You can recover it (connect the mailbox to a brand new user account)
    2. You can restore it into another mailbox (contents are copied, inactive mailbox remains - so now you have an active and deleted mailbox with the same contents)
      1. If you need to recover an archive mailbox, use the SourceIsArchive switch and specify the ArchiveGUID.

Inactive Mailboxes

  • An inactive mailbox is a hard-deleted mailbox that was on Litigation or InPlace Hold before it was deleted.
  • The mailbox itself cannot be purged while on hold, the admin must remove the hold first and then run Remove-Mailbox -PermanentlyDelete to purge it.
  • Recovery options are the same as a hard-deleted mailbox:
    1. You can recover it (connect the mailbox to a brand new user account)
    2. You can restore it into another mailbox (contents are copied, inactive mailbox remains - so now you have an active and deleted mailbox with the same contents)
      1. If you need to recover an archive mailbox, use the SourceIsArchive switch and specify the ArchiveGUID.
      2. To verify if all of the data was copied from the source to the target mailbox, you can use the move report from the MailboxRestoreRequestStatistics.
        1. $restoreStats = Get-MailboxRestoreRequest -Identity <> | Get-MailboxRestoreRequestStatistics -IncludeReport
        2. $restoreStats.report.SourceMailboxSize
        3. $restoreStats.Report.MailboxVerification
        4. $restoreStats.Report.MailboxVerification | select folderPath,source,sourceFAI,target,targetFAI

Potential Complications

Account Matching

An admin may require that a newly restored mailbox be matched to a specific user account that they are synchronizing from their on-premises Active Directory. For guidance on this see AzureAD Account Matching - Source of Authority Transfer to On-Premises

If the above has not helped, then you have likely hit a blocking issue that is preventing you from recovering the mailbox. The types of issues frequently encountered are:

Synchronization failures between AzureAD and EXO occur when attempting to restore deleted users or relicensing the existing user. Changing Exchange Attributes
Inability to create new mailboxes to merge into Creating Mailboxes

Related Scenarios

How to link a restored mailbox to a specific on-premises user account AzureAD Account Matching - Source of Authority Transfer to On-Premises
Multiple mailbox (mbx on-prem and in cloud) My User has Two Mailboxes?

  2. Check the Office365 community pages.

emilycurious say:

Thanks

(Office for Mac 2017)

jim gordon mvp say:

This is easy to solve.

1. Turn the on the hidden Developer tab of the Ribbon.

a. Go to Excel > Preferences > Ribbon and Toolbar'

b. In the right side of the window, in the Main Tabs section click the box to enable Developer

c. Click the Save button to close the dialog and effect the change

d. Now you can use the Developer tab

2. On the Developer tab of the Ribbon, click the Record Macro button.

3. In the Record Macro dialog, give the Macro a short name having no spaces or special characters, such as InsertMoveRight

4. In the Record Macro dialog, assign your Macro a Shortcut. Optionally, type a more complete description of your macro.

5. Click OK to close the Record Macro dialog

6. From the Insert menu choose Cells and choose Shift Cells Right in the Insert Dialog then click the OK button.

7. On the Developer tab of the Ribbon click the Stop Recording button.

8. Save the workbook.

Your workbook will now have the shortcut you assigned.
