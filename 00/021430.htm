faysal farooqui say:

- Select your particular cells assume that, A1:H16.

If there is no text:

- Click on Home | Conditional Formatting | New Rules | Use a formula to determine which cells to format and enter this formula: =ISTEXT(A1)=FALSE

- Select background color as Red with black border and Click ok.

If there is text:

- Click on Home | Conditional Formatting | New Rules | Use a formula to determine which cells to format and enter this formula: =ISTEXT(A1)

- Select background color as Orange and Click ok.

If there is 'N/A':

* A little confusion about your 'N/A' word. If you mean that, it is a simple text, then:

- Click on Home | Conditional Formatting | New Rules | Use a formula to determine which cells to format and enter this formula: =A1="N/A"

* If you mean that, it is an Error Text (#N/A), then:

- Click on Home | Conditional Formatting | New Rules | Use a formula to determine which cells to format and enter this formula: =ISNA(A1)

- Select background color as orange (accent 6, darker 50%) and Click ok.
