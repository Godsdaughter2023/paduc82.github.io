Tap to pay is a feature for Windows Phone that allows you to set up a credit or debit card, then tap your phone to an NFC (near field communication) reader to make wireless transactions. Not all phones support NFC, and even fewer support making NFC transactions. Here's what you'll need to use this feature:

  • A phone with a built-in NFC chip – Many Windows Phones have one. To find out if your phone does, go to the phone has a chip inside it.
  • A mobile operator that supports NFC transactions – At the moment, only a small number of mobile operators offer phones that support this feature. However, we're working hard to get additional mobile operators on board, and we expect the number of Windows Phones that support NFC transactions to grow.
  • A payment SIM card in your phone – This is a special, secure SIM card that you can only get from your mobile operator. If your phone supports multiple SIM cards, the payment SIM must be in SIM slot 1.

If any of these point is missing in your phone then Tap-to-Pay wont work in your phone.

racker chris (uk) say:

Hello Nicole,

Your absolutely correct, I'm afraid with the Outlook 2011 Junk Email protection function you can only specify e-mail addresses or individual domains that you want Outlook to classify as junk not outright block them. The only workaround I have found is contacting your email provider and asking them to add a blacklist rule server-side. For example, most email providers such as Rackspace Email & Apps & Microsoft Office365 allow users to add wildcard rules to block all domains from a certain country. Perfect example for you, if a customer wants to block all emails from Russian email addresses they could add the wildcard rule: @%.ru  (% = indicating the wildcard)

It's important to remember that Outlook 2011 does not apply the junk mail filter to messages from a Microsoft Exchange account. For these accounts, junk mail filtering occurs on the Exchange server and as such you will more than likely need to consult with your Exchange Email provider.

Alternatively if you see a pattern emerging with the spam you are receiving (e.g. They seem to be coming from the same domain) - If you are able to add the domain/('s) to your 'Blocked Senders' list then you can change the 'Delete messages from the Junk E-mail folder' frequency so Outlook can automatically purge them.
