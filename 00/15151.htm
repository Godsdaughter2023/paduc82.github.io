iconist say:

PC Suite/Outlook contacts SMS Synch tip

Through a process of trial and error, I discovered that Outlook will automatically remove the leading zero & insert a space between the country dialing code (in this case +44) and the number of your contact. This looks pretty and all, but if you sync up outlook to your phone with these numbers, the phone (in this case N70) won't recognise it as being one of your contacts if they reply. The solution I found to this was to manually edit my contacts on the phone to include the +44 WITHOUT a space and of course without the leading zero, then to Synch up to PC with settings set that the phone overides the PC. You may also then have to go into the PC suite contacts list & re-edit one of your PC Suite contacts in the same manner, which then asks if you want to "update contacts". When you click yes you'll then see that ALL of the numbers that you've manually edited will show as they should, on your phone, on PC Suite contacts, and more imporatntly, in Outlook. Bit of a pain, but I'd rather use a keyboard to type SMS texts than my thumbs...
By the way, I couldn't see a way to stpo outlook inserting the space. Hope someone else finds this useful :-)
