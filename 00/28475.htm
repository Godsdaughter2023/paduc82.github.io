How do I recover an overwritten quick-key?

I made the mistake of assigning ctl-F to a macro I wrote. This action replaces the Find quick-key and I can't get it back. I've tried deleting the macro I wrote but the quick-key is still inoperative...how do I recover the quick-key? Where does the script for quick-keys reside? Must I reload Word?
jay freedman say:
"Quick keys" (which Word calls keyboard shortcuts) are not script-related.

Click File, then Options, then Customize Ribbon on the left side of the Options dialog. Click the Customize button at the bottom left.

In the Customize Keyboard dialog, in the Categories list, choose the Macros item. On the right side, click the name of the macro. In the Current Keys box, click Ctrl+F. Click the Remove button at the bottom of the dialog, and then click Close.

That should restore the default assignment of Ctrl+F to the Find command. If it doesn't, then reopen the Customize Keyboard dialog, choose All Commands in the Categories list, and then click NavPaneSearch in the Commands list. Click in the "Press new shortcut key" box, and press Ctrl+F. Then click the Assign button and click Close.

Ah, your last para sheds a bit more light on the issue :-) Draft View is designed to maximize performance, so by design it is restricted in a number of ways, one of which is the UI. It's expected behavior for the Sidebar to close when the Footnotes/Endnotes Pane opens & vice-versa.

What you might consider is assigning a custom keystroke to each of them. You can then continue use Draft View -- highly commendable, btw -- but just 'toggle' between display of the two panels rather than the 2 step approach of closing one then opening the other. I.e., I use:

Control+Option+D toggles the Document Map
Control+Option+E toggles the Endnote Pane

Unfortunately, that doesn't help with the width of the Document Map. However, if you go to a bit more trouble to create a macro you could include adjustment to the preferred width.
