I have checked the SR and I’d like to share the progression here so that other community members may benefit from it in the future. After our SR engineer analyze the ADFS event logs you have provided, the problem may be related to small cookie data limits on Safari. Below are the findings and possible resolutions.

Findings:

The Safari browser doesn’t allow cookies over 4096 bytes and ADFS makes use of cookies to maintain authentication state. If we are passing through a lot of claim data into the cookies, then those cookies are very likely to fail authentication. If the user is a member of too many groups, it may exceed the cookies limit of Safari.

Possible Resolutions:

1. Reduce users’ group membership so that less MSISAuth cookies are used and safari can handle it better.

2. Modify ADCP (Active Directory Claims Provider) trust in AD FS to only pass a limited number of groupsid claims (i.e. those used for claims rules based on group membership).

3. Upgrade to AD FS 3.0 (Windows Server 2012 R2) because this version doesn’t ship group membership in large MSISAuth cookies. Then the cookie size will keep small so that Safari can handle it better.

In addition, I notice that our SR engineer is still working with you in the SR. Please keep working with him and we will share the final solution at here once the problem is solved.

steve rindsberg say:

>> Why would a ppt with pictures, audio, annimation and transitions NOT have the Media Size and Performance icon on the File/Info screen?  

 

Pictures, animation and transitions are not what PPT considers media.  Unless there's something that PPT considers media, it won't offer to compress media.

Only audio and video are considered media, and only EMBEDDED audio and video are candidates for compression. 

Try inserting an audio or video file but don't choose Link.  The option to compress should become available (though it may not if the audio/video is already so compressed that PPT can't do anything further with it ... I'm not sure of this.)

Unless you beat it severely about the head and registry, PPT automatically compresses your images when you save the file.
