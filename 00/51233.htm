songoku'sson say:
Hey there Phil, to remove Auto-Hardware detect just follow the following guide:

1. Log in to your computer, click on the Start button and click on the Run option. In the Run window type "SERVICES.MSC" and press Enter.

2. Search for the service called "UPNP Device Host" or "Universal Plug and Play Device Host" depending on which version of Windows you are running. Right click on the service, select the Properties option and under the Startup Type, select the Disabled option, and then click the OK button.

3. Search for the service called "SSDP Discovery" or "SSDP Discovery Service" depending on which version of Windows you are running. Perform the same actions you took to disable this service as you did to disable the Universal Plug and Play service in the previous step.

4. Restart your computer to ensure that the settings changes you have made are applied to the operating system.

syed n say:

Hi Se7ven16,

 

·        Do you want to add music files to the playlist?

 

Check to see if the following helps.

 

a.       Open the folder containing your music files.

b.      Select the songs you want to add to the playlist by clicking on them. Hold down the "Ctrl" button on your keyboard to select multiple tracks.

c.       Right-click on any of the songs after you have selected all the tracks you want to play. This will bring up a small menu of options.

d.      Click on the "Add to Windows Media Player list" option. Your songs will open in a default "Now Playing" playlist in Windows Media Player.

e.       Arrange your songs in the desired playback order by clicking on each track and dragging it into the correct position; songs will play in descending order from the topmost position to the bottom.

f.        Save your playlist if you want to use it again in the future. This can be done quickly by clicking on the button labeled "Now Playing," located just above the playlist track listing. Next, click on "Save Playlist As" to save it.
