steven _fyiguy_ hughes say:

Have you checked to see if you port 3389 open/forwarded in your home router for the IP address of the computer you are trying to connect to?

This generally listed under port forwarding and make sure to set the start/end port to 3389.

This will allow you to connect locally via the IP on your home network if you enter in the local IP number in the computer name. You can find this on your computer at the Windows run command line type -> CMD ->then run IPCONFIG. This should show your local IP address.

To connect via your phone off your network you will have  to use the non-local IP address as the computer name. This can be generally found by going to sites likehttp://www.whatismyip.com/ on your desktop computer to find your internet provider's IP address for your home.

If you don't want to use that port on your device you can change the default port in the Windows Mobile RDP to something else say 3398 or any other by applying a simple registry hack.

  1. Open the device registry using a registry editor
  2. Open the key HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Terminal Server Client\Default\
  3. Create an entry of type DWORD, called Server Port
  4. Set the value to the required port number (3398 as menitioned above)

Hope this helps.

Do you mean to change the Skype Id? If so, unfortunately you can't change your Skype ID (Skype Name), but you can change your Skype Display Name.

If you want to change your ** Skype Display name **

1. Launch the “Skype app”.
2. Click your “Skype profile image” or “display name”, both of which are in the top-left corner of the screen.

From a PC/Mac users can also change their Skype display name on the Skype website:
1. Open your web browser and go to “Skype.com”.
2. Click your name in the top-right corner of the screen.
3. From the drop-down menu that appears, click “My Account”.
4. Click “Edit profile” located on the bottom left and the bottom-right of the screen.
5. Click “Edit profile” again.
6. Change your name, then click “Save”
