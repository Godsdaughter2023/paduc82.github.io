Please make sure you have followed these steps to map a SharePoint Online library to your computer:

1. Sign in to the SharePoint Online site by using your Office 365 work or school account credentials. Make sure that you click to select the Keep me signed in check box.
2. Browse to the library that you want to map the drive for.
3. Click the LIBRARY tab on the ribbon, and then click Open with Explorer.

5. Right-click Computer or This PC from the Desktop, or within Windows Explorer, and then click Map Network Drive.
6. Click the Connect to a Web site that you can store your documents and pictures link, and then click Next two times.
7. Paste the URL that you copied in step 4, click Next, and then follow the instructions in the wizard.

Note: "Open with Explorer" feature only works in Internet Explorer.

suzanne s. barnhill say:

Having looked at this template, I can see why you're tearing your hair out. Given that I'm an expert Word user and am having trouble making it work, I can see how it would be even more frustrating for a novice. As I suspected, like most MS-provided résumé templates, it's set up as a table, so it will help to have table gridlines displayed if you don't already. Here's what I'm seeing:

The problem with the bullet points is that they're inserted as content controls, which are easily messed up. The style applied is Bulleted List (not a native Word style but one invented for this template, apparently), but if you press Enter at the end of one of the bullet points to get another paragraph (and to do that you have to arrow-right in order to get out of the content control), you get a Normal paragraph. If you then apply the Bulleted List style, you get a numbered rather than bulleted paragraph--not surprising since the actual formatting of the Bulleted List style prescribes 1, 2, 3 numbering and a 0.5" indent, neither of which these bullet points are actually using!

It's hard to know where to start with this, but assuming you are capable of entering paragraphs without needing content controls (which are just making your job harder), I'd suggest just getting rid of them altogether. Since the Bulleted List style is not working as it should, apply the List Bullet style instead. You can format it to have a square bullet and a smaller hanging indent (the Bulleted List style also has the font condensed 0.25 pt, which you may or may not need).

Sub Split_In_NewWBks()

Const N As Long = 2    '<<  data starts in row 2 (row 1 headers), change as needed

Set ws1 = Sheets(1)
Set ws2 = Sheets(2)

ReDim r(1 To 2)

Set myWB = ThisWorkbook
Dim myPath
myPath = ThisWorkbook.Path & "\"

myWB.Sheets(1).Range(rCol & N - 1 & ":" & rCol & r(1)).AdvancedFilter _
Action:=xlFilterCopy, CopyToRange:=myWB.Sheets(1).Range(Hcol & "1"), Unique:=True

rH = myWB.Sheets(1).Cells(Rows.Count, Hcol).End(xlUp).Row

For x = 2 To rH
Set WB = Workbooks.Add
Application.SheetsInNewWorkbook = 2 ' << create new workbook with 2 sht tabs
For y = 1 To 2
With myWB.Sheets(y)
.AutoFilterMode = False
r(y) = .Cells(Rows.Count, rCol).End(xlUp).Row

' in each looping autofilter, criteria range each value in helper column
.Range(rCol & N - 1 & ":" & rCol & r(y)).AutoFilter field:=1, Criteria1:=myWB.Sheets(1).Cells(x, Hcol)

.Range("A" & N - 1 & ":" & LastCol & r(y)).SpecialCells(xlCellTypeVisible).Copy
WB.Sheets(y).Range("A1").PasteSpecial xlPasteValues
WB.Sheets(y).Range("A1").PasteSpecial xlFormats
Application.CutCopyMode = False
.AutoFilterMode = False
End With
WB.Sheets(y).UsedRange.EntireColumn.AutoFit
Next y

 

'save new wbk in same path with this workbook
