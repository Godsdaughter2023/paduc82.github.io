david scammell say:
Hi BrookeFlowers & XFANDREW,

I've seen similar issues before. Here's a few things to try which has resolved the syncing issue for me on several occasions.

  1. If at all possible, insure you are using the OEM cable received from Nokia with your phone. Sometimes secondhand cables are problematic.
  2. First, try restarting your by pressing and holding the power button for 10-12 seconds. Do not release the power button until you see the 1st splash screen after it reboots. Now see if it will work. This will let your hardware, as well as your software, reset.
  3. Next, turn off your computer and unplug it from the wall for 10-20 seconds. Replug and boot up. See if it works now. This will allow the computers hardware (like the USB port) to reset.
  4. If still not working, move the USB sync cable to a different USB port on your computer. Wait 2-3 minutes to see if your computer detects a new device and loads new drivers. Try this with each USB port on the computer until one works.
  5. If all else fails, I would suggest this excellent Windows Phone "can't Sync" Wizard to perform additional troubleshooting and/or fixes.

I hope this helps!
David

jan de messemaeker - project mvp say:

Hi,

Project is not very sophisticated when you use the term "month"

A "month" when shown in duration is simply the number of working days shown in the Calendar options (default is 20) regardless of the "real" month

When you don't care about working days you can use "emonths" which calculate in calendar days but again, a fixed number of days (default is 30)

Conclusion: you are absolutely right, month is not the appropriate unit for you. I would use 26 eweeks - you then might be wrong one or two days but that may be close enough I hope.
