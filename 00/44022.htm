alfonsoo say:

Hi Jdogg79,

 

The 0x0000007B Blue Screen Error is one of the most common windows stop errors and can have its source in a bad hardware, outdated driver, corrupted registry or a virus. The stop error occurs accompanied, along with the BSOD, with one of the following factors:

  1. Windows restarts and you see the message: A problem has been detected and windows has been shut down to prevent damages.
  2. Windows restarts and you see the message: INACCESSIBLE BOOT DEVICE Error.
  3. Windows restarts and you see other less common error messages ( like hal.dll error).

As bad and scary as it sounds...it is curable and fixable...most of the times anyway.

I have put together a check list of steps to perform when you get the 0x0000007B Stop Error and recommendation on what to do to avoid getting BSOD in the future.


Causes of BSOD and 0x07B Error

The 0x0000007B Error, commonly, can be triggered by the following factors:

  1. Outdated or Corrupted Drivers - A device driver was poorly installed or outdated.
  2. System File Corruption - A program or a virus corrupted windows system files.
  3. Hardware malfunctions - New Hardware might not be compatible with existing or it just stopped working from other reasons.
  4. Registry Corruption - A virus or a program overwrote the registry keys or deleted some keys.
  5. Viruses - A virus had deleted, overwritten or changed important files that are need for Windows to function properly.

a. user say:

Even if it were possible to increase the rollback period ... you wouldn't want to do that. Even 10 days is likely too long for a successful rollback.

The truth is, regardless of the number of days, the more you have used your computer after receiving a feature update, the harder it is to roll it back. Even one day would be too long, if you used your computer extensively during that day. This forum has plenty of posts from people whose computers got messed up after rolling back.

When you attempt to roll back, Windows has to undo all the changes you made, in order to get back to what you had before. There's only so much that can be undone until, like Humpty Dumpty, even all the king's horses can put you back to the way you were.

The only sure method to go back to the way your computer was before an update, regardless of how long ago, is to make image backups every day. An image backup is an exact copy of your computer. When you restore an image backup, your computer travels back in time to exactly the way it was when you made the backup, no matter how much your computer has changed in the intervening period.

A rollback, on the other hand, is best executed shortly after a computer was updated, as soon as you realize that the update didn't work out well. The less time that passes, the better.
