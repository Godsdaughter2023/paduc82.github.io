jay freedman say:

There are a couple of ways to do that.

One way is to insert legacy text form fields in the places where you need to enter data, and protect the form "for filling in forms" in the Restrict Editing pane. In the File > Options > Advanced page, under the heading "When printing this document", check the box for "Print only the data from a form".

Another way is to paste the scanned picture of the original form in while the cursor is in the header pane, and position it to match the location on the preprinted paper. In the body of the document, set up a table that matches the form's layout, and put form fields or content controls in the cells where data will be entered. Turn off all the borders of the table. In File > Options > Display, uncheck the boxes for "Print drawings created in Word" and "Print background colors and images" so the scanned picture won't print.

The fiddly part of either of these methods is matching the positions and sizes of the entry fields/controls to the exact locations on the paper. Once it's done, though, it shouldn't need any more attention.
