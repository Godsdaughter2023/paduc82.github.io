john mcghie say:
Hi Evea:

Hmmm...  You "cannot" be working on a .docx file with built-in macros, because a .docx cannot contain Macros (not allowed to, Word will remove them on opening the file).

The message "Error! Reference source not found" is a standard broken cross reference.  What it means is that the paragraph that was the source for the cross reference has either been deleted or moved.  This often happens when people are using tracked changes.

All you need to do is re-make the cross reference.

If you have been typing into the area where the cross reference field is, then it will delete what you type every time the cross reference is updated, which it will be every time you print.

To fix this, copy what you have typed, and paste it into TextEdit.  Hit Command + Shift + T in TextEdit to strip the formatting, then copy it and paste it back into the Word document, OUTSIDE (either before or after) the REF field.  The key is that you should not type inside fields, because Word will generate their content and delete yours.

To make this easier, turn on Word>Preferences>View>Field Shading to "Always".  That was, things like TOC and cross references sow as grey shading so you will not type over them.

If you are the owner of the live domain and you can afford a minute of downtime with your email address, this is how I was able to get around my "corrupt outlook.com database" issue (for lack of better description, I believe this was the problem).

The fix was to move my mail from the corrupt account into a new account of the same name. It would be nice if one of the "Professionals" could have run some scans on my outlook.com database (or whatever method they use to store my email/calendar data) and correct the problem themselves.

To do this:
Perform a send/receive on your active account in outlook.
Verify that everything has been downloaded locally before proceeding further. (* I performed this action on 2 different computers in order to have 2 separate backups of my mail)
I then removed the accounts from the send/receive group in order to save my data locally. I did not want the deletion of the account to somehow wipe my data if an accidental send/receive occurred.
Log into your account and make note of any rules or blocked/allowed senders you may want to keep.
Also verify you have not received any new mail since you last performed send/receive. If you have, turn the send/receive back on and re-sync your mail, then continue on. (I performed this initial few steps late at night to minimize the amount of new mail I may have received while in limbo of an account existing)
Once you are confident you have the latest copy of your email downloaded. Log into the domain and delete your account. Then Re-create your same account. You will want to do this quickly in order to keep from missing any important emails.
Once your new account is recreated, log into the web interface with your new credentials and continue to perform the required setup prompts.
You can now start to import all your email/calendar events/Contacts from your backups into your newly created account.
If you are lucky, the easiest way to do this is:
In outlook view your old account and perform an export to a pst file.
Once the export has finished you can add in your new account to outlook and perform an import from that file you just created. 
Once you hit send/receive your mail, calendars, and contacts will start to sync back to outlook.com.
This process will take a very long time if you have a lot of mail.
After you get everything pushed to outlook.com you can add back in your rules, blocked senders, allowed senders, etc. (I would wait until you verify all your mail is fully restored in case you have to delete it and start again just to make it easier to find any new mail you received since the backup process)

Issues I ran into:
Performing a mass send/receive to upload my initial seed always seems to be a hit and miss. I let it run the first time around. At one point outlook locked up and I had to restart the send/receive process again. When it finally finished I noticed, as I was looking through my folders to verify my mail was fully uploaded, that not all my mail uploaded correctly. Many of my mail folders were empty on outlook.com. I attempted some repairs on my new ost file and a few other things and was never able to force those missing items to push to outlook.com
I deleted every folder and email from outlook.com, excluding any new mail since I performed the initial backup, and started over. I even removed and added back in my new account to outlook for good measure.
This time, like times before, I opened the exported pst file in outlook and performed a copy from the backup file to the new account a folder or 2 at a time. You can perform a move, but make sure you have a backup copy of your export pst file in case you have to do a round 3...100. I performed a send/receive/verification between each copy. I tried not to upload more than 300 emails at a time. It seems to be faster that way.
You can manually copy your calendars/events as well.
This is a very tedious way to get your mail pushed back to outlook.com but it does work.

I also had an issue during the account recreation step. It appears that outlook.com doesn't actually delete your account but rather they inactivate it. So during the creation step if you try to use the same password you will get an account creation error with poor description as to why it failed. So just use a new password.


It may be possible to just perform a backup of your account, delete all mail and folders, then restore the data back. But the majority of the time, as I have experienced, is spent pushing data back to outlook.com which is the same for both efforts. Also, I didn't want to worry about new mail getting sorted into a missing folder or lost by accident nor did I want to chance having this alternate way fail and still having to fall back to the new account creation method.

Since performing these steps I am able to perform all actions in outlook including moving folders and the original error has gone away. I feel sorry for those that my run into this issue with a standard outlook.com address or one created with the same name as their favorite account( a gmail account or whatever). If this happens to them there is no process that I know of to delete and recreate those types of accounts and Microsoft doesn't seem to have the ability to troubleshoot their own services effectively.

Hopefully this will be useful to someone.

josh craig say:

shortcut key for fill handle

Hi

 

Is there a shortcut key equivalent to double clicking the fill handle on a cell?

 

Thanks

 

Josh
bill manville say:

I don't think there is, but you could add a macro to your personal macro workbook and give it a shortcut key.

The macro could be something like this:

Sub FillHandle()
  If IsEmpty(ActiveCell.Value) Then Exit Sub
  If ActiveCell.Row = Rows.Count Then Exit Sub
  If ActiveCell.Column > 1 Then
    If Not IsEmpty(ActiveCell.Offset(, -1)) And Not IsEmpty(ActiveCell.Offset(1, -1)) Then
      Range(ActiveCell, ActiveCell.Offset(, -1).End(xlDown).Offset(, 1)).FillDown
      Exit Sub
    End If
  End If
  If ActiveCell.Column < Columns.Count Then
    If Not IsEmpty(ActiveCell.Offset(, 1)) And Not IsEmpty(ActiveCell.Offset(1, 1)) Then
      Range(ActiveCell, ActiveCell.Offset(, 1).End(xlDown).Offset(, -1)).FillDown
      Exit Sub
    End If
  End If
End Sub
