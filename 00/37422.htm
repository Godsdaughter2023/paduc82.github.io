canadian tech say:

Your first priority ought to be finding out why the shut downs and fixing that.  Every time that happens there is a chance that your hard drive is corrupted.

How old is your laptop?

A problem that causes this is the thermal paste that should be between your processor and the heat synch that takes the heat away.  The surface of the processor is perfectly flat and smooth and should match precisely the under side of the surface of the heat synch that sits on top of it. 

It is not uncommon that the paste was not applied correctly by the manufacturer, or as the computer ages and heats and cools, the thermal paste gets dry and no longer does the job.

Doing this in a notebook PC, may be a bit challenging, but you can fix this rather easily with a very small tube of thermal paste you can buy at the computer store for $10.  The challenge is disassembling.  Once you disassemble, you need to clean the two surfaces extremely well with rubbing alcohol, but being very, very careful not to use too much.  Once clean, put four tiny dots of the paste on the CPU face and press the heat synch down on it.  Pull it back up to check to see the coverage.  You absolutely do not want too much and you need to completely cover both surfaces, so do this carefully.

Remember also, that the name laptop is extremely misleading.  It should never be used on a soft surface.  The source of cooling air comes from small vents on the bottom of your notebook PC.  If they are blocked by a piece of soft cloth, you are surely going to overheat your computer.
