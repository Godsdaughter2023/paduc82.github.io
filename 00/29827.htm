Svchost.exe, wuauserv (Windows Update) bloat - Microsoft Community

peter thornton say:

Svchost.exe, wuauserv (Windows Update) bloat

Windows 7 Ultimate, SP1, x86
After startup, and sometimes during a session, Svchost.exe hogs resources, with memory maxed out, high number of "Hard Faults" (AIUI formerly Page faults), the system is almost unusable. a. user say:

The Media Creation Tool doesn't affect activation.

Your computer's activation code is a hash computed from details about your computer's motherboard, CPU and TPM (if your computer has one.) Once computed, the activation code is stored in the cloud.

Once activated, Windows 10 can re-computer the activation code at any time and compare it to the value stored in the cloud. That's how Windows 10 knows it's still on the same computer on which it was originally activated.

The point of this long-winded discussion is: Once your computer is activated, it's activated for life, as long as you don't replace the motherboard or CPU. You can reinstall or clean install Windows 10 on the same computer - you and whomever gets the computer after you - as many times as you like with no time limit.

james vinyard say:

That is actually normal for an external mic volume to be much lower than a built in mic to eliminate feedback and is reliant on the user to adjust to the level they want.

You can quickly get to it by double-clicking the speaker icon on the task bar (desktop) and it will display the volume levels for all your devices.  Have the headset plugged in when you do this so that it will display mic #2.  At that point, slide the slider all the way up if you wish to increase the volume.

They do that so that when you jack in a headset (and your wearing it at that time), that you do not get a loud pitch feedback sound coming from the headset straight into your ears.

So, the issue you have is actually normal.  Regular computers (laptops and desktops) are supposed to do the same thing (chances are, they are set at 100% by default).

You may also get additional control in volume by launching Windows Media Player and adjust the sound/recording settings within it as well.  Even if you don't use WMP, do it anyhow and check the volume settings.  You may also find that you get an increase in volume output from the speakers when listening to music or watching a video as well when doing that.
