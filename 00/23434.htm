Extract last word in a text string - Microsoft Community

tomnixon1218 say:

Extract last word in a text string

To extract the last word in a text string, I found the formula, =TRIM(RIGHT(SUBSTITUTE(A1," ",REPT(" ",999)),999)).

It works great but I do not understand how it works.  Please help me understand the process

Thanks

Tom

mike h.. say:

Hi,

The 999 in the formula can be reduced and to make it easier to understand I' reduce it to 10 here. The reason 999 is used is that it must be a bigger number than there are characters in the last word.

Let's say our string in a1 is this

A big dog

We start with the formula evaluating from the middle like this

=TRIM(RIGHT(SUBSTITUTE(A1," ",REPT(" ",10)),10))

the bold underlined bit adds 10 spaces between each word and we get this.

=TRIM(RIGHT("a          big          dog",10))

Now the bold RIGHT bit evaluates and we take the 10 rightmost characters and we get this

=TRIM("       dog")

The 10 rightmost characters will be 7 spaces and out word and then TRIM removes the excess spaces.
