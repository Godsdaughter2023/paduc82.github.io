usa - united in justice, liberty, opportunity say:

pivot table conditional formatting

MS Office 2007 was just installed on my PC and I'm testing for any potential impacts to automations we have.

I have a pivot table with a conditional format based on a formula that covers both the row area (set up as categorical variables only) as well as the column area (set up as data/numeric variables only) in one contiguous range. The range of the results in the pivot table appears in the Conditional Formatting Rules Manager as " Applies to $A$6 : $J65536" where $A$6 : $E$65536 is the row area while $F$6 : $J65536 is the column area.

In Office 2007, whenever the pivot table is changed (by refreshing or making filter selections), the contiguous conditional format range is automatically split by Excel into the separate row and column regions as "Applies to $A$6:$E$65536, $F$81:$J$65536" and the conditional format for the column region (which is listed second) stops working. Then, if I manually make one contiguous range again by changing the conditional format range back to "Applies to $F$6 : $J65536", the problem is fixed temporarily until the next time a change is made to the pivot table (again by refreshing or making new filter selections).

Is there an option that governs this to stop it from happening? "Preserve Cell Formatting on Update" does NOT resolve this.

If there's no option, I imagine my only alternative would be to detect any change to the pivot table in VBA and then reset the conditional format's range.

 

Thanks

 

 

usa - united in justice, liberty, opportunity say:

I found a resolution.

 

It appears that I must separate the specification for the column/data region from the row/categorical region as follows

The column/data region applies to $F$6 : $J$112 (based on the extents of the data/numeric results)

The row/categorical region applies to $A$6 : $E$65536 

 

Furthermore, the column/data region must be specified by highlighting the data columns as follows (which I found in another answer on this site):

-left click the top cell in the data column

-push the cursor to the top of the cell until it turns to a down arrow

-left click

-the column of data is selected and maintains the formatting when the selection criteria is changed
