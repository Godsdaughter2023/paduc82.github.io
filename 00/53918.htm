intoxicated moa say:

With good security, port forwarding can be safe. In fact, it is generally thought of as a lesser risk, as you're much more likely to get a virus (or anything for that matter) from a browser security flaw than from port forwarding. It's just the inherent risk that comes with being online, I guess.

Just remember though, if your computer gets attacked, the others on the network may be at risk, so it would be best to schedule the port to be forwarded only during the times when you need it / when security is up (if your router allows for this).
