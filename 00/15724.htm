rbrtfortenberry say:

Is it possible to add a bulleted list into an existing line of text?

Ok so I'm working on a resume and trying to add a bullet list to the word file. I have everything I want exactly the way I want it except for this one part. I need the bulleted section under skills to line up with the word skills. I don't want to use a table but everytime I try to change it, it either deletes the first bullet point or adds a bullet to the word skills... any solutions or work arounds?

stefan blom say:
To change the indentation for a bulleted item, do the following: Click the actual bullet symbol; Word selects all bullets belonging to the same list. Right-click and choose Adjust List Indents from the context menu. In the Adjust List Indents dialog box, "Bullet position" is the location of the bullet symbol, measured from the left margin. "Text indent" determines where the second and subsequent text lines start. Assuming that "Follow number with" is set to "Tab stop," the "Add tab stop at" value determines where the first text line starts (again, measured from the left margin).

suzanne s. barnhill say:
  1. Ascertain which character Word is using for the bullet in the bulleted list.
  2. Set a tab stop in the SKILLS line at the same position as the left indent of the bulleted list.
  3. Set a hanging indent in the SKILLS paragraph at the position of the hanging indent in the bulleted list.
  4. After SKILLS, tab to the first tab stop and insert the bullet character used by the bulleted list (using the Symbol dialog), then tab to the hanging indent and insert the content for the bullet point.

FWIW, I think a table is much easier and more satisfactory, which is probably the reason most résumés are laid out with tables.

woodrick say:

Unable to Receive E-mails from a Specific Sender after Transition to Outlook.com

a. user say:

 

Hi woodrick,

 

Welcome to the Microsoft Community.

 

I understand you are unable to receive emails from a specific sender on Outlook.com. Let me help you.

 

There are a lot of factors that causes this issue. Try sending a test email to yourself to check if you will receive the email or not. If you don't receive the message, are there any error message received or bounce emails sent on your Inbox? Please post it on your reply.

 

Furthermore, you can check the settings of your Microsoft account such as follows:

 

It is possible that some of your messages go to your Junk folder. To check if the messages are on your junk folder, refer to the steps below:

  1. Log in to https://mail.live.com
  2. Click Junk on the left navigation pane of your inbox.
  3. Check to see if the missing emails are there.
  4. If you see any messages that should have been delivered to the Inbox , click the box on the left of each message to put a check mark and then click the Not Junk button.

Email Forwarding

  1. On your inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.
  2. Under Managing your account, click Email forwarding.

Note: If it's set to Forward your mail to another email account, tick on Don't forward then click Save.

 

Blocked Sender's List

  1. On your inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.
  2. under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.
  3. Click Blocked senders.
  4. In the Blocked senders list, select the name or domain (the part of an e-mail address that follows the @ sign, such as hotmail.com) that you now want to receive mail from, and then click<< Remove from list.

Add the email address to your Safe senders list

  1. On your inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.
  2. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.
  3. Click Safe Senders.
  4. In the text box, type in the email address or domain you’d like to always receive email from, and then click Add to list >>.

Check your Email Rules

  1. On your inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.
  2. Under Customizing Outlook, click Rules for sorting new messages.
  3. Perform one of the actions as per your requirement:

Click Edit next to the rule that you want to edit.

Or

Choose the rule that you want to delete and click Delete.

 

Note: Not all international characters are supported as filters.

 

Check your Junk Mail Settings

  1. On your inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.
  2. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.
  3. Under Choose a junk e-mail filter, choose the level of protection that you want, and then click Save. If the Exclusive level was set, messages from senders not on your Contacts or Safe senders lists will have been sent to the Junk e-mail folder.

Check your Reply-to address

  1. On your inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.
  2. Under Writing email, click Reply-to address.
  3. Select Current e-mail address radio button.
  4. Click Save.

If you have checked all the settings and the issue persists, please provide us your affected email address and the sender's email address on the private message tab that I have created so we can investigate.

 

Click the notification states "You have a private reply to this message. Click here to read it on our secure private message" section or by clicking the "Private Messages" tab above the first post. Make sure that you are currently signed in to Microsoft Community to view the private message.

 

Let us know how things turn out.
