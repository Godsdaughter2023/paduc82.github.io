Storage of Sways - Microsoft Community

davidsingletary1 say:

Storage of Sways

I plan on using Sway for training materials. There will be several people creating the content. It seems like every sway is tied to an individual. What's the best way to collaborate and store each sway in a central location?

jason (office engineer) say:

Hi David,

Every Sway must belong to a single active account.  However, a Sway can have many coauthors who all have edit access to it.  To add a coauthor, open the Sway as the owner, click the Share dropdown, and copy the edit link.  Then, send that link to anyone you want to be a coauthor and when they open it, they will be given those permissions.

Jason

davidsingletary1 say:

Jason,

Thanks for the information. So if I wanted to keep a library of Sways, I'll have to send each link out individually?

jason (office engineer) say:

Yes, each coauthor will have to add themselves to each Sway via a Sway-specific link.  That is, unless you want to create a single account that all your authors share a password for and login to whenever they are working on this library.

Jason

aubrey allmond say:

Jason,

What happens to the Sway if you leave the organization and your profile is deleted/archived?

Aubrey

jason (office engineer) say:

Hi Aubrey,

When an O365 account is reported as deleted, the Sway goes into a holding pattern where it appears as if it was deleted and nobody can access it.  During that time, the organization admin can move Sways from a deleted account to an active one.  After that time, the Sways are permanently deleted.

Jason
