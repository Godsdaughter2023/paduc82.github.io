GoddyLM

 

anna bon say:

Hello,

There are several factors why this issue is happening. The fans purpose is to cool down the interior of the PC or, in the case of the CPU fan, to keep the processor cool. Most motherboards have temperature sensors which regulate when and at what speed the fan works. Initially the fan spins at full speed until the desired reduction is achieved and then the fan either slows down or, in some cases, stops completely until the temperature builds up again. Once the temperature hits its top limit the fan is initiated again and the cooling process starts all over again.

If the fan is on continuously then there is a possibility that the inside temperature of your PC is hotter than it should be. Usually the PC BIOS will have a diagnostic section where you can check the actual internal temperature and the revolutions per second of the fan(s). If the temperature is consistently high it may be due to dust sitting on the internal components. It looks temperature related and it could be a faulty temperature sensor on the motherboard. It could also be a faulty fan.

We suggest that you turn off the PC for a couple of hours. After this, you can boot your PC in Safe Mode and then observe the performance.

Should you need further clarifications, feel free to contact us back.
