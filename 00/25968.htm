    Hi,

    Tools>Internet Options>Security tab, click "Reset all zones to default", then click the Trusted Sites icon, 'Sites' button.... are any of your 'apps'/websites listed there? eg. google, skype?

    Remove them.... the Trusted Sites zone is actually less secure than the Internet zone eg... Unsigned ActiveX controls are allowed to run....

    Many websites now use a sub-domain or a third-party service to validate your credentials... eg. google.com uses account.google.com , MS Services (*.Microsoft.com) use account.live.com...2

    IF the site is in your Trusted Sites list and you try to login (using a domain that is not in your Trusted sites list), then that site can't read the other sites' cookies.... IE has a security setting preventing navigation into a zone of lower integrity... eg... you have google.com in your trusted sites list, but account.google.com is in your Internet zone (by default)... as far as IE is concerned, account.google.com cannot read the cookies for google.com (because they are in different IE security zones)... poorly programmed websites (that have not been properly tested with MSIE browsers) will think that the user has turn off their site cookies, when in actual fact, it is IE's security settings that is preventing one domain from reading the cookies of another domain, because the user has placed on of them in their Trusted sites list.

    The Trusted Sites list is mostly used by large companies for B2B (business to business) commerce.... Public websites (that anyone can visit) are designed to work with the IE Internet zones' security settings eg.... only signed ActiveX controls are allowed to run, and other restrictions such as safe cross site scripting.

    the secure protocol (https) works just as well (that is your data is encrypted) no matter which IE security zone a domain maps to (File>Properties menu in IE will tell you which IE security zone a domain is mapped to)....

    For home use, there should be no need to add sites to the IE Trusted Sites list.... it IS actually less secure....
