vincentchoy say:

Sometimes, the user needs to also be alert.

The ones that seem to get through, are the ones that, assuming you work for contoso inc

1. Contain legitimate business language and text (eg concerning your invoice blah blah blah)

2. Comes from a seemingly legitimate external email service, but not from your company eg ben(a)icloud instead of ben(a)contoso

3. Only the sender name impersonates the actual internal user (there is a real ben who works at contoso)

4. The sending email address is also a fresh account, specifically created to target your company, hence not found in spoofing databases

Since SPF is legitimate (ben@icloud really has an account and sent from icloud), DKIM is legitimate (the email is legit, the mail was not modified), it is deemed to be a legitimate mail in terms of SPF & DKIM Framework. Hence most Anti-Spam services may pass this mail. The user needs to be educated to look at whether the sender email is really who they claim they are, and also to personally validate any request to send money to unknown bank accounts.

So for example, assuming Ben is the real CEO, and Alice is the finance head. The ones that get through will be as below.

Based on every security service, the below mail is actually legit, all except Ben@icloud is not Ben your CEO

From : ben(a)icloud.com

To : alice(a)contoso.com

Note I use (a) instead of @ as this forum will mask all email address for privacy reasons

Dear Alice,

We have successfully won the project.

Please immediately wire pre-payment funds to kick off this project to this

Account : 123-456-78909

Ben

CEO

The recommended way of doing DMARC is as follows

1. Subscribe to a reporting tool if you don't have one. Try Dmarcian

2. Initially set policy to monitor only. ie p=none

Make sure your sound output is set to the correct number of speakers in your sound settings. Right-click the little speaker icon in the bottom right of your screen and choose 'Playback devices'. You can run various tests to ensure the sound is being output correctly.

 

Another source of this problem could be that you just have the speakers plugged into the wrong sound outputs at the back of your computer.
