  1. Open Word, and you should open to templates gallery.

  3. At the top would be a circle with either first letters of you First and Last name.
    Or your picture or whatever Avatar you added to your profile.
  4. Also, your first name should show.

If these are not there, you are not signed in. 

To sign in you have to use the email and password you used to purchase  Office 365. If Office was purchased through an educational institution, and they installed it, they will have to supply you with the sign in credentials. Also, make sure you have an excellent internet connection. If Office  365 happens to call your link to verify your membership and you have no internet, the attempt will fail, and you will not be signed in to Microsoft.

If you know that you have used the right sign in items, it's possible the keychain entries may be corrupt.

To remedy, follow this:

Password alteration in KeyChain Access,

by Microsoft software installers.  

Here lately, for some reason, the installers for Office 2016 updates, has been messing with Office 2016 Passwords stored in KeyChain Access. The following procedure might help. Also, it might not make a difference, or you may find items I suggest be changed, have been unaffected. Also, I have had one report doing this cause the application to crash, and the person removed the changed item and resigned in.:

Quit all open Applications (including Microsoft Office applications).

Next open KeyChain Access.

Click on: Passwords.

In the search box type in: Office.

The should be 3 to 4 items that appear to have a serial or code number.
 (In the Description area you should see reference to Version 15.0 or 15.2, or 15. with another number related to current 2016 series)

Now click on the icon with an i in the center.

Click on the checkbox next to Show Password.
 (this is a Sample of window that will open)
 

(I've Blacked out sensitive info so you could see window.)

Check, If the password you used to activate, does not show in each of the files, or is mangled.

If so then enter the correct Password. 

Repeat, above steps, starting at step 5.

Once finished, make sure to save changes. Quit KeyChain Access, and try Word, Excel, PowerPoint, or OneNote. It's still possible you may have to enter the password; but, then only have to do so once, and others should already be signed in okay.
