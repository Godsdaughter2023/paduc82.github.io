Subtracting two negative numbers - Microsoft Community

preschool mike say:

Subtracting two negative numbers

I have two unbound text boxes (i.e., txtNetCost and txtIncomeLessExpense)on a report in which each display the results of a calculated expression.  In this case both answers happen to be negative numbers - Note this may not always be the case.  I have another unbound textbox which calculates the difference between the two: =txtIncomeLessExpense - txtNetCost.  For some reason Access is not calculating this correctly and displays the results as a positive number.

In this example we'll say txtNetCost = ($2500.00) and txtIncomeLessExpense = ($1000.00).  The results should be: ($3500.00), but are being displayed as - $1500.00

How do I fix this?

Mike

john w. vinson say:

-1500 is the correct result (formatting being irrelevant here).

Goes back to elementary arithmetic: if you subtract a negative number, that's equivalent to adding a positive number - the two minuses cancel:

-(-1) = +1

So (-2500) - (-1000)   =   (-2500) + (1000)    = -1500.

If you want -3500 as the result you should be ADDING the two (negative) numbers, not subtracting them.

  1. Sign-in to your Gmail account using a computer.
  2. Click the "Mail" or "Gmail" drop-down arrow on the top left of the screen, and choose "Contacts".
  3. In Contacts page, click "More" menu and select "Import".
  4. Now press the "Choose File" button.
  5. Navigate to the location where you saved the Windows Live.
  6. Contacts file (OutlookContacts.csv) in the previous step and "Open" it.
  7. Press the "Import" button and wait until the import process is completed.
  8. And finally to your phone.
  9. On the Android phone, go to "Settings" > "Accounts and Sync" or "Settings" > "Accounts".
  10. Under "Manage Accounts", select (or "Add") the Google account you used to import WLM contacts.
  11. Finally press "Sync Contacts".

You can also download a Third party application to transfer your contacts to Android phone.
