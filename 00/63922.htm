Windows 10 1803 wrong ProductName - Microsoft Community

caleb.thompson say:

Windows 10 1803 wrong ProductName

So it appears that (regardless of patch version currently) Windows 10 release 1803 has a string value mis-match between the data in registry property "HKLM:\Software\Microsoft\Windows NT\CurrentVersion".ProductName and "HKLM:\Software\Wow6432Node\Microsoft\Windows NT\Currentversion".ProductName

This device has a Windows 10 Pro KMS key. WMI reports Windows 10 Pro, Get-WindowsEdition reports Windows 10 Pro, Winver.exe reports Windows 10 Pro, System Information/About reports Windows 10 Pro.

Any 32 bit application or management client querying the 32-bit registry reports Windows 10 Enterprise. Check the keys, the native registry has "Windows 10 Pro" as the string data and the wow6432node has "Windows 10 Enterprise"

This could be a costly mistake....may want to think about fixing that in an upcoming patch.

mark ole say:

Hi Dylan,

If the keyboard only works at a certain angle, then this can be a hardware issue. However, here are few things you can try to resolve the issue:

Clean the connectors


  1. Detach the keyboard.
  2. Wet a cotton swab with rubbing alcohol. (Use 95% isopropyl alcohol or higher if possible.) Clean the silver pins. Rub the cotton swab back and forth vigorously to clean the pins. Use a fresh swab surface to clean each of the connector faces.
  3. After you're done, make sure the pins are dry and that there are no cotton fibers on the connectors. Reattach the keyboard.

Disconnect and reconnect the keyboard 3 times

This will help re-establish connection between your Surface and the keyboard.

Run the Surface Diagnostic Toolkit

The toolkit runs a series of tests to diagnose your Surface device. The diagnosis and repair time averages 15 minutes or less, but can take an hour or longer. This will also download the latest updates for your Surface. Click here to learn how to run the toolkit.

Restart your Surface

  1. Press and hold the power button until the screen turns off (about 10 seconds), then release the power button.
  2. Press and release the power button to turn your Surface back on. You should see the Microsoft logo.

If that doesn't work, use this shutdown process to make sure your Surface completely turns off. Here’s how:

Press and hold down the power button until your Surface restarts and you see the Windows logo screen (this takes about 20 seconds), then release the power button.

Run a fresh start

Fresh Start does not remove files, or personal information from the Surface. However, you will need to reinstall your apps.

  1. Go to Start, and search for Windows Defender.
  2. In Windows Defender, go to Device Performance & Health. Under Fresh Start, select Additional Info > Get Started

If your device is not on Windows 10 Creators Update or newer, this option will not be available to them.
