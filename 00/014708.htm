Max units set in the Resource Sheet does not impact assignment units to tasks.  If you change the resource's calendar - the schedule will shift.  But if you drop max units from 100% to 80% (for example) and the resource is assigned at 100% -- all that will happen is the resource will still be assigned at 100% - above their max and the resource is over allocated.

I've solved my own problem, and I am posting here in case anyone else has this same issue. It turns out the reason it would say "We are unable to connect right now. Please try again later." is because I was getting the certificate errors in Safari.

Whenever I would log in to www.onenote.com and attempt to open a notebook, I would be constantly bombarded by requests to trust various websites (like the ones listed in my problem).

It turns out, when you click "Show Certificate" on one of those errors, you can see a hierarchy of certificate authorities. (I'm attaching the screenshot below purely as reference - not an actual screenshot of my problem)

You need to select the top level certificate authority in the hierarchy, and then check the box that says "Always trust..." When you click continue, it will add that certificate authority to trusted list and then you no longer get the "Unable to connect" error in Mac OneNote.
