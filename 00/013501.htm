suzanne s. barnhill say:

If your settings are not being saved, then the likelihood is that the Normal template is not being saved when you exit Word. Make sure that the box for "Prompt before saving Normal template" is checked (File | Options | Advanced: Save). Then, when you quit Word, if you are not prompted to save the Normal template, you'll know that something is interfering with the save. Most often this is a poorly written add-in, and ultimately you need to figure out which one it is and disable it. As a temporary workaround, however, you can open the Normal template itself for editing, make your desired changes, and then save it. Here's an easy way to do that:

 

In Word, press Alt+F11; the Visual Basic Editor opens. Press Ctrl+G to activate the Immediate window. In that window, type

     NormalTemplate.OpenAsDocument

and press Enter. Normal opens, but note that it may not be on top of all windows. You can close the Visual Basic Editor. Make the desired changes to the Normal file, and then save and close it. If the changes you have made have not been recognized as changes (the Undo button is not activated), press Spacebar, then Backspace to "dirty" the file, thus ensuring that the template will actually be saved.

Note, however, that F12 is also assigned to FileSaveAs, or you can add the Save As button to your Quick Access Toolbar.

john korchok say:

This is an issue that everyone faces in printing to PDF. Your document information is not just copied to the PDF, it is re-interpreted. I like printing PDFs with Acrobat because it gives me much great control over the attributes of the final file, including different methods of resampling and compressing images to reduce file size. You give up those options when you use one of the built-in methods, like Word's Save as>PDF and OS X's Print>PDF>Save as PDF.
