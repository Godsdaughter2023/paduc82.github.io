bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

Although the Shift+F5 keystroke [GoBack command] is a global feature & may work for you much of the time, it's dependent on having made some sort of edit... Actually, it will toggle you back through as many as the previous 5 edits. However, if you're simply reviewing or reading a document & stop at, e.g., 'page 68' the feature may not remember that location if you haven't done something there. Here's another option, although it is document-specific...

Use Shift+Command+F5 to create a specific Bookmark anywhere you want it. [I give mine a name of 'Last'.] Whenever you want it at a different spot just click at that spot, use the same keystroke, then select Last in the list of Bookmarks & click Add -- The Bookmark will be reset to that spot rather than the spot at which it was previously set. When you next open the document use Edit> Find> Go To... [Option+Command+G] to go to the precise spot at which your Bookmark is set.

haytham amairah say:

Dean,

It's possible to make data validation range of values dynamic!

Use this formula:

=AND(A2>=$C$2,A2<=$D$2)

With regard to the error message, you can also make it dynamic, but you need some lines of code, and this will cost you some performance in your worksheet!

You need to something like the below in worksheet code area:

Private Sub Worksheet_Change(ByVal Target As Range)
    On Error Resume Next
        r = ActiveCell.Address
        ActiveCell.SpecialCells(xlCellTypeAllValidation).Select
        Selection.Validation.ErrorMessage = "The Discount Range is from " & _
        Format(Range("C2").Value, "0%") & " to " & _
        Format(Range("D2").Value, "0%") & "."
        Range(r).Select
    On Error GoTo 0
End Sub
