john rubdy say:

Hi,

seehcri.sys is a Sony Ericsson driver.

1. Roll back the drivers on ALL of the USB hubs that showed they were using Sony Ericsson drivers (Device Manager > expand Universal Serial Bus Controllers > right click on USB Root Hub > properties)

2. Uninstall the SEEHCRI devices selecting the remove device driver software option (or words to that effect)

3. Then go into your Windows\System32 folder and delete the SEEHCRI.SYS file.

4. Reboot, Windows will detect new hardware and the 2 SEEHCRI devices will revert back to normal USB hubs after you allow Windows to search for new drivers.

 

If you cannot roll back the drivers in step 1 or Windows doesn't find the drivers in step 4, you'll need to reinstall them from the original mainboard driver disk for your computer or off the manufacturer's website.

Apparently, when I stream, I don't have a Constant Bitrate (CBR). At first I thought "this can't be. I live in a 30/5 Mbps down/up stream. Xbox Live ports are forwarded in my router, my Xbox lives in a DMZ, and I have all of my QoS settings turned off because this seems to cause more issues than it solves in my opinion.

Now, in doing some research I discovered that Twitch requires you to stream in a CBR rather than a Variable Bitrate (VBR). When I look at the yellow flag error code I notice two things.

1. My average stream bandwidth is about 2.4-2.5 Mbps. This might sound good at first but that's an average. That means that I'm not getting a bitrate consistently higher than 2.5 Mbps.

2. It also points to a max stream of about 4.5 to 4.8 Mbps.

As far as I understand from my poor lowly internet research (LOL) CBR gives you a preset bitrate that your clients (viewers) need to match. Since not everyone can match a bitrate for a 1080p stream and since there is no downsampling done on Twitch's end on our streams, Twitch made the choice to let us stream at 720p at different bitrates. This makes it easier on the end user in the long run but has also created a deeper issue for those of us too cheap/poor to afford elgatos.

If Twitch allowed a VBR for us on Xbox through their app, those with fluctuating bitrates could still sustain good image quality. The downside here is that there would have to be buffering at the beginning of your stream which would add delays and is the main reason Twitch wants CBR instead since that is just parsed directly as is to your viewer.

I'm sure my ISP (Time Warner Cable) is to blame here. For as great as their service may be (not really) they rely on the short bursts their customers get at times. Try uploading to youtube. You can see where I'll hit the mythical 5 Mbps or higher at times in the speed rate but if you have a diagnostics open, you can see file transfer speeds in real time and it's not pretty. You're really uploading chunks of information with a huge disparity in size. 538 bits here, 1458 bits there (figuratively of course).

As far as solutions, I have tried lowering the bitrate all the way down to 800 Kbps. That is the only bitrate that allows me to keep an excellent rating according to Twitch. Any higher and you get a really choppy picture as you play. Here's a highlight I uploaded to youtube.

Pay attention to how grainy the video gets when there is a lot of motion. This happens because at the time this was happening, my upload speed was not in a burst where I needed at least the 2500 Kbps or more. I may get high rates but not very often as it seems.

Now why is this important. I could care less about the choppy video. What I care about is the fact that Twitch limits your audience to just PC users, cutting you off from mobile (where a lot of people watch) and especially from consoles. That's where it hurts because for me, my target audience are the people watching from their consoles wondering whether the game they want to buy is fun. If I can't achieve a CBR like Twitch wants, they essentially cut me off from the people who are more likely to watch me. People on PC are going to focus on those streaming at 1080p most of the time. I can't compete with that. My market is the other Xbox One users and the 360 users who can't find me.

I ask you now fellow reader, is there an answer to this dilemma? Am I to just give up? I can't get a better internet service. Time Warner has shut out all of it's competitors in my area other than AT&T. They don't even have U-Verse here yet. So, it's either terrible ADSL or the worst broadband in the world. Should I just give up?

Let me know your thoughts on this and if there is any way around the CBR thing that I may have overlooked. Thanks in advance guys.

It seems the problem is that windows 10 laptops are getting restarted even in sleep mode after installing updates.

This seems to fix the problem

Step 1 – Search for Task Scheduler and run this application.

Step 2 – Browse to the following location path from the left menu.

Task Scheduler Library –> Microsoft –> Windows –> UpdateOrchestrator

Now, in the middle panel find and Double click on Reboot .

Click on conditions tab and then uncheck the option which says wake the computer to run this task.

Click OK to save.
