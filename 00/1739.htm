dan at it associates say:

You wrote, "I have the windows xp product key that was on a label stuck to the old pc's case."

 

Regarding Windows XP, the label with the product key is the Certificate of Authenticity (CoA).  When the product key is printed on the CoA, that tells us the license for Windows XP is an OEM license, which according to the End User Licensing Agreement (EULA), is NOT permitted to be moved from the original computer to any other computer.  The motherboard is the prime component that defines "computer", not hard disk, so your use of Windows XP on the replacement computer is a violation of the Windows EULA.

 

Regarding Office, it's likely that over the years updates and Service Releases/Packs have been applied to your Office installation and the individual files on the original installation disc are no longer the versions needed by Office to re-activate itself.

 

Which brings us to the installation of Office on the hard disk drive.  If your Office installation is likewise an OEM license (the disc would have "For distribution with a new PC only.  For product support, contact the manufacturer of the PC" embedded in the holo label), then the same OEM rules apply:  you are not permitted to move the license from the original computer to any other computer.

 

What you should have done instead of the ghosting, is to simply purchase a brand new PC (which you apparently did), but with a licensed installation of Windows on it, then install the old hard disk drive into the new PC and copy your essential data from the old HDD to your new computer.
