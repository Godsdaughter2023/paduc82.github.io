Document layout (including line and page breaks) depend heavily on the printer driver (see http://wordfaqs.ssbarnhill.com/TextReflow.htm) but, regardless of what printer you have selected, Print Preview should accurate reflect what will actually print. Printing as a PDF uses a different driver (a virtual one); if you're using Word's built-in "Save as PDF" feature, there's not much you can do, but if you're using Acrobat or another PDF-authoring application, you can select Adobe PDF or the other application's virtual printer as the active printer when you're laying out the document.

smittypro1 say:

Your code isn't creating a new sheet, but posting to sheet 6 (which was probably created when the macro was recorded).  You can try something like this, where you explicitly create a new sheet and rename it:

    Dim ws As Worksheet
        
    Set ws = Worksheets.Add
    Set ws = ActiveSheet ' It's OK to reference the ActiveSheet here, because an added sheet is always the ActiveSheet
    ws.Name = "PT" & Sheets.Count
   
    ActiveWorkbook.PivotCaches.Create(SourceType:=xlDatabase, SourceData:= _
        "Sheet2!R1C1:R6C2", Version:=6).CreatePivotTable TableDestination:= _
        ws.Range("A3"), TableName:="PivotTable1", DefaultVersion:=6

Where ws.Range("A3") is the reference to where you want the PivotTable placed on the new sheet.

ron sommer mvp 2009-2017 say:

You are getting several benefits.

One benefit is the ad pane and ads are removed when you access your account using a browser.

Another benefit is that your account remains active as long as you pay for the Plus membership.

A third benefit is increase account storage.

If you stop paying the yearly fee, you could lose your account if you do not access the account at least once every 365 days.

There will be an ad pane with ads in the browser.

The available account storage will be reduced from 10 GB to 5 GB.  If your account was using more than 5 GB of storage, emails will be removed and your account will have problems because you have a full mailbox.

Not loading ads in the ad pane speeds up the Outlook Mail page and gives more space.
