jllatham say:

Martin_F, the logic in this one gets more difficult to see.

Here's the formula that would go into column C starting at the 2nd Balance Due row (where 55 is at, and later where 51 is at)

=INDIRECT("C" & MATCH(INDEX(INDIRECT("C1:C" & ROW()-1),MATCH(9.99999999999999E+307,INDIRECT("C1:C" & ROW()-1))),INDIRECT("C1:C" & ROW()-1)))+SUM(INDIRECT("A" & (MATCH(INDEX(INDIRECT("C1:C" & ROW()-1),MATCH(9.99999999999999E+307,INDIRECT("C1:C" & ROW()-1))),INDIRECT("C1:C" & ROW()-1))+1) & ":A"&ROW()-1))-SUM(INDIRECT("B" & (MATCH(INDEX(INDIRECT("C1:C" & ROW()-1),MATCH(9.99999999999999E+307,INDIRECT("C1:C" & ROW()-1))),INDIRECT("C1:C" & ROW()-1))+1) & ":B"&ROW()-1))

The key is the MATCH(INDEX()) parts of the formula.  It's basically finding the last numeric entry in the rows above where you enter the formula.  It returns that row number.  That's used to get the address of the previous Balance Due entry as the starting point, then it's incremented by 1 to get the row number of the start of Debit and Credit entries that come after that previous Balance Due entry.  The ROW()-1 simply gives us the row number just above where the formula is entered, which is the last row for the Debit and Credit entries associated with the last Balance Due entry.

If you want to "clean up" the formula a little, replace the 9.99999999999999E+307 entries to a number that you arecertain is going toalways be larger than the largest possible Balance Due value.  That doesn't simplify the formula, just might make it easier to read/understand.

 [Edit] Here's the formula broken into it's 3 major parts:

This is the part that picks up the last Balance Due entry from column C:
=INDIRECT("C" & MATCH(INDEX(INDIRECT("C1:C" & ROW()-1),MATCH(9.99999999999999E+307,INDIRECT("C1:C" & ROW()-1))),INDIRECT("C1:C" & ROW()-1)))

This is the part that then gets a SUM() of the entries in column A (Debits) that start 1 row down from the row found with the last Balance Due entry in it, and down to the row just above where the formula is entered:
+ SUM(INDIRECT("A" & (MATCH(INDEX(INDIRECT("C1:C" & ROW()-1),MATCH(9.99999999999999E+307,INDIRECT("C1:C" & ROW()-1))),INDIRECT("C1:C" & ROW()-1))+1) & ":A"&ROW()-1))

And finally, the part that gets a SUM() of the entries in column B (Credits) that start 1 row down from the row found with the last Balance Due entry in it, and on down to the row just above where the formula is entered.  It's exactly same as the previous formula except we reference column B instead of A:
- SUM(INDIRECT("B" & (MATCH(INDEX(INDIRECT("C1:C" & ROW()-1),MATCH(9.99999999999999E+307,INDIRECT("C1:C" & ROW()-1))),INDIRECT("C1:C" & ROW()-1))+1) & ":B"&ROW()-1))

keith- say:

If you use more than one antivirus or antispyware program at the same time, your computer may experience decreased performance, become unstable, or restart unexpectedly. Before you install Microsoft Security Essentials, you should disable or uninstall other security-related programs.

 

Caution:

When you remove your currently installed internet security programs, your computer in in an unprotected state.

To uninstall an existing antivirus or antispyware program:

1.      In Windows XP, clickStart, clickRun, typeappwiz.cpl, and then press ENTER.

-or-

In Windows Vista and Windows 7, clickStart, and in theStart Search box, typeappwiz.cpl, and then press ENTER.

2.      In the list of installed programs, uninstall any third-party Internet security programs.

3.      Restart your computer, and then try to install Microsoft Security Essentials again.

Note:
  • Some Internet security applications do not uninstall completely. You may need to download and run a cleanup utility for your previous security application for it to be completely removed.
  • If you use another antispyware program together with Microsoft Security Essentials, we recommend that you turn off real-time scanning in the other program. For more information, see the antispyware program documentation.

 

Important:

If your antivirus program is disabled or if you don't have one installed, you should follow these guidelines:

  • Don't download or open files from sources that you don't trust.
  • Don't visit Web sites that you don't trust.
  • Don't open e-mail attachments from contacts you don’t trust.
