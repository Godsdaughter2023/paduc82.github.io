All of the tasks shown, including those two marked 100%, are scheduled (or actual) for September 2015 - January 2016 but the status date is in April 2015.

The status date should be a date in the more recent past, like yesterday.

No, you don't have to update the remaining duration for every task, only the ones which have actually started, have actual duration and have not yet actually finished. And if the remaining duration is already what you think it should be then leave it.

However, it is essential that all of the actuals are left of the status date and all of the scheduled is to the right of the status date.

Why? Because the status date is the boundary between the past and the future, and you cannot say that you plan to do something in the past and you cannot say that you made progress in the future.

Bring the status date up to, say, yesterday, or last Friday.

Format gridlines to show it as a vertical red line on the chart.

404 is 75% complete and has a duration of 1 day, so it has an actual duration of 0.75 days and remaining duration of 0.25 days. Since the remaining 0.25 days can only be done in the future, that part of it should be moved to the future, relative to the status date, ie to the right of the status date. It is on the menu of the move button on the task ribbon.

Since 404 has a successor at 0%, when you move the remaining 0.25 days of 404 to the status date 405 and all of the all of the following successors will be re-scheduled.

garyzhu_msft3613 say:

hi rwu,

you can turn off pop3 and imap services via windows powershell cmdlets (not feasible in office 365 admin center ui temporary) with the steps below:

1. connect to exchange online using remote powershell

2. run the following cmdlets:
get-mailbox -recipienttypedetails usermailbox | set-casmailbox -popenable $false
get-mailbox -recipienttypedetails usermailbox | set-casmailbox -imapenable $false

3. you can also run this cmdlet: get-mailbox -recipienttypedetails usermailbox | get-casmailbox to check the result. the following screenshot is from my side:
 

please feel free to post back if there is any problem when you perform the steps above.
