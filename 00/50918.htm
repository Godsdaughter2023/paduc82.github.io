chris cupler say:

That's actually easy to explain.  Nearly all Comcast markets have gone all-digital (save for the "budget basic" channels - they're still analog, and end at anywhere between 13 and 20-ish, depending on market); everything above that cut-off is digital.  And, many of those Comcast markets have started to encrypt all of the digital channels.
In your case, everything above 13 is digital, and it sounds like it's encrypted, too.  But, there is something that you can do to check what all is (or is not) encrypted):
those 'odd' channels that TV Setup found (such as 108-3)?  Those are Clear-QAM (unencrypted digital) channels.  Go tohttp://www.silicondust.com/support/channels/ , plug in your ZIP code, and select <your cableco, digital> for the listings.  You can use this info to map Guide listings (via the Edit Channels list in MC) to the 'unlocked' channels that it found (but couldn't map to a listing.)  If you also have your cableco's channel map available (you can get it from the Comcast website), you can change the displayed (Guide) channel #, to make it more usable (this is what the Channel Number field in Edit Channel changes - the displayed channel number, not the actual (physical) channel number.)

A TiVo (or similar), though, won't help here.  It would still require a CableCARD installed to be fully functional.
If all that you're after is being able to tune the full basic package that you subscribe to, get a DTA from your local Comcast office (they're generally free for the first unit), and get a full remote kit for Media Center.  Run TV Setup as described in the 'Comcast with DTA' link that I posted earlier.  It'll get you your full cable package (in SD), in Media Center.
And, if you connect the DTA to the '2250's A/V input board, you can still use the tuner's digital tuner to tune any Clear-QAM channels that it can get (which is subject to change at any time by Comcast.)

HTH,
Chris
