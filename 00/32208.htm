irfan h say:

Hi soon_cougar,

·        Have you connected a USB printer to the desktop?

Try the below methods to fix this issue.

Check the Spooler service on the desktop:

It appears to me that the Printer Spooler service is not starting on the desktop when you send a print job to the desktop. When you print from your laptop, check the status print spooler service, before restarting the desktop. If it’s started, stop and restart the same follow the steps:

1. Click Start, type services.msc and hit Enter.
2. Look for the Print spooler.
3. Restart the print spooler service.

Also make sure that the service is set to Automatic:

a.     Right click on service and click on Printer spooler service and click on Properties.

b.     Under the startup type, set it to ‘Automatic’.

Disable Bidirectional printing:

It could also be possible that you are having issues with bidirectional printing. Disable bidirectional printing and check if you have the same issue:

If you encounter a problem with bidirectional printing, disable bidirectional printing and resend your print job.

To disable bidirectional printing, do the following:
1. Click Start, and search for Devices click Printers.
2. Right-click the printer, and then click Properties.
3. Click the Ports tab, clear enable bidirectional support checkbox, and then click OK.

Hi Jihhny, all the issues you mention could be attributed to a bug in the new version which affects video device drivers. The effects in Windows 10 rely heavily upon that device driver . . .

Open Device Manager (accessible by right clicking your Start Button)
Expand the Display Adapters Section
Right click your video card and choose Properties
On the Driver Tab, choose 'Roll back Driver'

Restart your PC once done . . .

How do I remove recent project Lists from the clipboard in Movie maker - Microsoft Community

harry1945_67 say:

How do I remove recent project Lists from the clipboard in Movie maker

How do I remove the list of recent Projects that appears alongside the Clipboard of project options, in Movie Maker.
considerate_guy say:

There is no option within the program to do that.  The values are stored in the registry, so if you must delete them, be sure you have made a registry backup and are comfortable making changes there.

  1. Close Windows Movie Maker if you have it open. Click "Start," type "regedit" in the "Search" box and press "Enter." Type your Windows administrator password if prompted.

  2. Double-click the "HKEY_CURRENT_USER" folder in the left pane, then double-click "Software," "Microsoft," "Windows Live" and "Movie Maker."

  3. Click the "Recent" folder under "Movie Maker" in the left pane. The right pane displays entries for the history items, labels them in capital letters starting with "A" and displays the history item's path under the "Data" column.
  4. Right-click the history item you want to delete, then click "Delete." Click "Yes" on the "Confirm Value Delete" prompt. The item will no longer appear in the "Recent Projects" history in Movie Maker.
