mikerichards_uk say:

Aha! A fix.

This is down to Apple's two-factor authentication which isn't supported in the setup process.

But there's a way round it.

  1. Go to your iCloud account, log in and choose Edit your Apple ID. You might be asked to log in (you'll need a trusted device such as an iPad capable of receiving a confirmation code).
  2. When you're logged in choose Password and Security from the left-hand menu.
  3. Then choose the last option, Generate an app-specific password. Apple will now create a unique password that you can key into your Windows Phone.
  4. Your phone should then sync with the iCloud servers and you can get to your data.

Hope that helps anyone else who comes along with this issue.

I have 3 hotmail accounts and want to consolidate them into 1 - Microsoft Community

dandereux! say:

I have 3 hotmail accounts and want to consolidate them into 1 Outlook.com account. Is that even possible?

I have 3 hotmail accounts and want to consolidate them into 1 Outlook.com account.  Is that even possible?

a. user say:

Hi dandereux!,

 

No, once you’ve created a new Outlook.com account there is no way to rename your Hotmail account to that name. If you delete the new account, it will take 365 calendar days for that account name to become available again, and at that point, anyone would be able to get the name.

 

Instead, you can Auto-forward your Hotmail account to your new Outlook account. You’ll have all of your mail in one place, and can reply to it using your new address.

 

To auto-forward your email:

      1. Log in to your Hotmail account.

      2. Click Options, then click More options….

      3. Under Managing your account, click Email forwarding. You might have to sign in.

      4. Select the radio button for Forward your mail to another email account.

      5. Enter the email address for your new Outlook account.

      6. If you want, check the box for Keep a copy of forwarded messages in your Hotmail inbox.

      7. Click Save.

 

Note: Free Hotmail or Outlook.com account users need to log into the account they choose to auto-forward at least once every 365 days to avoid having the system remove the mailbox for inactivity.
