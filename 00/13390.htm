Private Sub Worksheet_Change(ByVal Target As Range)
    Dim rng As Range
    Dim cel As Range
    On Error Resume Next
    Set rng = Intersect(Target, Me.Range("H7:J1000"))
    On Error GoTo Errhandler
    If Not rng Is Nothing Then
        Application.EnableEvents = False
        Application.ScreenUpdating = False
        For Each cel In rng
            If Not cel.HasFormula Then
                If Not IsNumeric(cel.Value) And Not IsDate(cel.Value) Then
                    cel.Value = StrConv(cel.Value, vbProperCase)
                End If
            End If
        Next cel
    End If
ExitHandler:

The error you post indicates that the DOE's mail server is telling you that a message that appeared to come from you specified an invalid address. Some server (not necessarily Outlook.com) tried to deliver a message to a @doe.gov address, but it could not because @doe.gov said no such address exists.

 

Now, if you have your mailbox configured to save sent messages and you see the original outgoing messages in yur Sent folder, then, indeed, your account appears to have been compromised, but I think that's unlikely because you can still access your account and you updated the password.  Instead, what is more likely is that your address has been spoofed by some spammer somewhere.  When the spammer targets invalid addresses, the receiving mail servers have no choice but you send you the non-delivery report, since it is your address that appears as the sender.  This type of spoofing usually goes away after a few days to a couple of weeks because the spammers are constantly changing the sender addresses in the messages they send.

 

This doesn't explain in any way why you would see messages sending limits being exceeded unless, in fact, your account has been compromised.  Only Microsoft can examine the sending history of your account to determine why the limits appear to have been exceeded and I wish they would do that instead of holding hoops through which they want you to jump.

 

If you have updated your password, you should also make sure you update your secret questions, provide several alternate addresses to which change approvals should be sent, connect a cell number with your account (also for change approval), and enable two-factor authentication to make sure your account cannot be compromised.
