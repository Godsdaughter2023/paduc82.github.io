The Restrict Permission features pertain to Information Rights Management (IRM) which has not been implemented on Macs... The implication is that eventually may be. As I understand it, it also pertains to files restricted by the PC versions when accessed from a SharePoint site, but cannot be invoked on a Mac.

To apply the type of password protection you describe, though, you don't need IRM. Just go to File> Passwords, then enter a Password to Open &/or a Password to Modify. Click OK, confirm when prompted, then Save the file.

Passwords to Open/Modify also can be set by clicking the Options button in the Save As dialog - just keep the same file name & location, then confirm replacing when prompted.

ron sommer mvp 2009-2017 say:
Install an identity verification app

Out of cell phone range, or on airplane mode? Use an identity verification app to access your account. These apps use industry-standard security code generation, and work with various services and providers. To get the app, go to your Microsoft account security settings page and click ‘Set up’ under “Identity Verification Apps.”

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/Windows/identity-verification-apps-faq
Travel with a device you trust

If you use Microsoft account services while traveling, we recommend you take a personal device. It’s more secure than public computers, and Microsoft will recognize the device, greatly reducing the chances of you having to verify you’re the account owner.

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/what-is-trusted-device#TXT
Keep security info up-to-date

Accessing your account from a new or unknown location may require you to verify ownership; something that’s likely to occur while traveling. With security info in place, simply use the unique code sent to your mobile phone or email account to sign in.
