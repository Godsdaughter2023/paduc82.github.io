a. user say:

Hello ToddWaterhouse,

 

Thank you for contributing to the Microsoft community!

 

Song purchases within the Zune marketplace are one-time downloads only, so I would strongly advise you to back up any content you purchase within the Zune marketplace, so you do not lose your collection.

 

The purchase of mp3 files in the Zune marketplace is like a purchase of a CD in a regular retail store. You purchase the songs on the CD, but not the music rights of the songs on the CD. So if you were to lose the CD or break it, you unfortunately won't get a new copy of the CD by the retailer.

 

The Restore All feature is used for content you downloaded via a Zune Pass.

As the option Erase all Content would suggest, it deletes all files on the Zune device, disregarding where you purchased those files from.

 

If you had the files backed up somewhere, be it your PC, an external hard-drive or burned on a CD you can of course re-add the songs to your Zune.

 

Otherwise there is unfortunately not much you can do.

 

Cheers!

Continue to hold down the shift key while clicking Restart
Continue to hold down the shift key until the Advanced Recovery Options menu appears

Click Troubleshoot

Click Advanced options

Click Go back to the previous version of Windows
