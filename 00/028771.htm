groverparkgeorge say:

Wow, This seems like a lot of steps to have to perform, and  I'm still not 100% sure what the entire procedure is.

 

The new students table should have a Primary Key field different from the StudentID field. (All tables need primary keys.) And that primary key should have been assigned when the students were first added to the table. It need not be the same as the "StudentID" which you assign only at the point where the student is completely registered. But ALL records should have a separate unique primary key which you can use to uniquely identify all students, whether they are complelely registered or not. To make this process more straightforward for you, that Primary Key should be included in the export into the SIS sytem so you can easily relate records in the two systems without having to worry about whether the StudentID has been issued or not.

Here's where I'm still confused though. Shouldn't you have created and added the StudentIDs in the new student table already when you export the records into that SIS?

 

I can tell you that the update query you need will be something like this -- based on the fact that you apparently aren't able to use a Primary Key to match records, you'll have to match on other fields.

UPDATE tblNewStudent INNER JOIN  tblExportedRecords ON

tblNewStudent.LastName =tblExportedRecords.LastName   AND

tblNewStudent.FirstName =tblExportedRecords.FirstName AND

tblNewStudent.MiddleName= tblExportedRecords.MiddleName AND

tblNewStudent.DOB = tblExportedRecords.DOB AND

tblNewStudent.Gender=  tblExportedRecords.Gender AND

tblNewStudent.GradeLevel = tblExportedRecords .GradeLevel

SET tblNewStudent.StudentID  = tblExportedRecords.StudentID,

tblNewStudent.Exported = tblExportedRecords.Exported

I think the "exported" field is actually redundant and, therefore, unnecessary.
If the StudentID has been issued, and updated in a student record, that is sufficient indication that the record was exported because, as I understand it, you can only get that StudentID by exporting the record into the SIS.

A lot of times, a situation like this is a bit of a head-ache to manage because nobody foresaw the need to track records uniquely in both locations. If you only had a valid primary Key which you could export along with the other fields from each student record into your SIS, then you could use that to quickly and easily update the newstudent record with the newly issued StudentID from the SIS database.

katsuya82 say: As Microsoft ****** up Brisbane's time with DST (We have no DST in Brisbane = ="), apparently there is no fix on the forum, so I've found the registry that deals with it.

1.  find your time zone record the timezone ID and name of your time zone from here:
http://msdn.microsoft.com/en-us/library/ms145276(SQL.90).aspx
For example, Brisbane is E. Australia Time, the timezone ID is 260


2.  open up regedit on your computer NOT MOBILE PHONE
and go to
[code]HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows NT\CurrentVersion\Time Zones[/code]
and locate your own timezone by looking for the name of your timzone

on the right tab, you can see a TZI value, copy that value.
eg. Brisbane is [code]a8 fd ff ff 00 00 00 00  c4 ff ff ff 00 00 00 00 
00 00 00 00 00 00 00 00  00 00 00 00 00 00 00 00 
00 00 00 00 00 00 00 00  00 00 00 00 [/code]

3.  open up registry of your WM device, and go to
[code]HKLM,SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Timezones\[/code]

4.  create a new registry key using your timezone ID. Then create a Binary value called TZI, and insert the TZI value you've obtained from step 2.


5.  SAVE and QUIT your registry editor

6.  on your WM Machine, open up Settings => Clock & Alarms and Change your timezone to any other timezone that is NOT your current time zone.

7.  Reset your phone, and change your timezone back. NOW your DST should be the same as your computers.

Enjoy~
