charles kenyon say:

Word, in any of its versions, does not give different headers for every page. There is no option to do that.

  1. You may want to use the StyleRef Field to change the information in the header to match information in the page. That way, the same header gives different infomation on different pages.
  2. Every Section of Word has three headers and three footers, whether or not you see them.
  3. A page can have multiple sections, each with its own set of headers and footers.
  4. The link to previous control is separate for each header and each footer in any section after the first and refers to a link to the header/footer of the same type in the previous section, not to the previous header/footer of a different type in the same section.

Header/Footer Settings Recap

charleskenyon (ia) say:
Pages are not linked in Word. Headers and footers in sections following the first section may be linked to the same type of header/footer in the previous section. That means they share the header/footer.

Again, look into the StyleRef field.
