considerate_guy say:

You are correct. Windows DVD Maker was part of Windows Vista and Windows 7. But it has not been included in later versions of Windows (8, 8.1 or 10). 

If you are going to play a DVD in a standard DVD player, it has to be in a special format. You can't just take the movie directly from Movie Maker and burn it to a disk. Because standard DVD players expect MPEG-2 video (standard definition) and Movie Maker outputs WMV or MPEG-4 video (high definition), the resulting disk is unplayable in a DVD player.

There are a couple choices:

  1. You can play the disk in a Blu-ray player. Modern Blu-ray players understand the MP4 format created by Movie Maker and will be able to play your disc.
  2. You can find older software that can create the legacy video DVD format. (DVD Flick and DVD Styler are a couple that come to mind.) Just be aware that the DVD format can't go beyond 480p since it was created before the wide adoption of HD video.
