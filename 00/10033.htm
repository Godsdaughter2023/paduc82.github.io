ray gao msft say:

Hi JoeR_CA,

 

To create a custom DLP Policy, you can try the steps below:

  1. Navigate to the Office 365 Security & Compliance center and click Data loss prevention in the Security policies tab.
  2. Click the Plus icon and select Custom and then Next.
  3. Select the services and then Next.
  4. Click the Plus icon to customize rules.
  5. In the conditions tab, click Add condition and select Content contains sensitive information.
  6. Click the Plus icon and select the Sensitive information types.
  7. Select your Actions, Incident reports and finish the rule.
  8. Click Next to finish the policy.

 

For more information, you can refer to the following article.

Create a DLP policy from a template

ronrosenfeld say: How do you want to deal with the issue of a tie?
If two entries occur the same number of times, are they "tied for first"  or are they allocated to first and second?

Without dealing with ties, you can use:


=INDEX(Class_A,MATCH(LARGE(FREQUENCY(Class_A,Class_A),Place),FREQUENCY(Class_A,Class_A),0))

anneliese [msft] say:

Help us test drive co-authoring in Excel Mobile

Hi, Office Insiders. Here's an update on co-authoring from the Excel team:

Charles Darwin said "In the long history of humankind those who learned to collaborate and improvise most effectively have prevailed." Who are we to argue?

Test drive co-authoring in Excel Mobile

The Excel team have is working hard to bring co-authoring to Excel. We want to give our fans a sneak peek at all this goodness in Excel Mobile. Using co-authoring, Excel Mobile users can collaborate with other Excel Mobile users, so get your friends or colleagues and try editing the same workbook at the same time. We would love to hear your feedback.

Where is all this goodness available?

You can use co-authoring on spreadsheets stored on OneDrive or OneDrive for Business, provided that co-authoring is enabled on your copy of Excel Mobile. Excel Mobile ships with Windows 10 Mobile, but if you turned off automatic updates, please use this link to enable co-authoring.

How to enable co-authoring

First, get Excel Mobile here and open Excel. Then, do the following:

Step 1 - Click the File Menu Step 2 - Click Settings Step 3 - Choose Options Step 4 - Turn on Preview Features, and then reboot!

Note: To co-author with other users, all users have to turn on co-authoring on their devices.

What to expect from a sneak peek

We still have further to go to realize our vision of collaboration in Excel. What does that mean?

  1. Co-authoring supports many Excel features, but as part of being prerelease, co-authoring in Excel Mobile does not yet work with every feature in Excel. Performing unsupported operations may require the document to be reloaded.

  2. At this time, Excel Mobile users can't collaborate with users of Excel on other platforms (Win32, Online, Android, etc.), and vice versa. We expect to add that functionality in a future update.

  3. Real-time co-authoring isn't yet available, so updates between users may take several seconds.

Thanks,

The Excel team
