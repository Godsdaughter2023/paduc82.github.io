Disabling download accelerator - Microsoft Community

kelseycrew say:

Disabling download accelerator

I'm about to update my video card driver and am being advised that my "download accelerator utilities" should be disabled.  How do I do this on Windows Vista?
malkeleah say: You probably don't have any download accelerators. Download accelerators are third-party programs (which means not part of Windows) that were particularly popular during the days of dialup. The website probably says something more like "if you have any download accelerators, disable them" and not that you specifically have one. Websites will do this because download accelerators work by making multiple connections to a website to all download bits of a file. The program would then put all the bits together for you.

So unless you are connecting to the Internet with dialup and unless you installed a download accelerator program yourself, you don't have one and can safely ignore the warning.

srimadhwa b say:

Hi,

Windows Updates will not cause catastrophic hdd failure. Further, I would like to let you know that Hard drives are mechanical devices with moving parts, so they are subject to expected wear and tear. It can potentially fail mechanically at any given time. However, most manufacturers will label their product with an expected average lifespan (usually provided in total expected number of functioning hours). But if a part is faulty or there are some manufacturing defects, a drive's lifespan can be over in as little as a day.

Other factors are primarily external in nature. Power surges can cause drive failure, as can fire or water damage, along with a number of computer viruses. Exposure to high levels of magnetism can disrupt the normal functions of a drive, along with sharp impacts. If dust gets into a drive, it can become the catalyst for an eventual failure.
