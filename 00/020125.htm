How to add security proofs to your Microsoft Account

Technical Level : Basic

Summary

We all know you want to do anything to keep your account safe, as well as your personal information and billing information. Keeping your information safe is what Microsoft wants, which is why Microsoft has added a section on your account called "Security Proofs". This means that if you forget your password, or need to access your sensitive information, Microsoft will ask you to verify one of your Security Proof options like this:

This way you are always able to have access onto your account, and keep the hacker out of your personal information etc...

This is why throughout this Article I will explain how you can:

  1. Add security proofs onto your Microsoft Account
  2. How to update your security proofs on your account
  3. What to do if you loose access to your security proofs (Such as email, phone etc...)


Details

Adding security proofs onto your Microsoft Account

To add security proofs onto your Microsoft Account, you first need to go to account.live.com, When the page loads, you will be asked to sign into your account like you would when logging onto your Outlook.com email account.

Once you have logged into your account, you will be brought to a screen like this:

This gives you the basic information for your account, such as your date of birth and your country and region. However, it does not give you your bank and billing and all of your personal details on this page.

On your left hand side, you will notice your navigational bar:

The navigational bar will allow you to navigate through the Microsoft Account site quickly and easily.

To add security proofs, these will be located in the Security and Password option on the navigational bar. Once clicked, you will be redirected to he Security and Password page on your Microsoft Account. Here you will be able to: change your password, see when you last changed it, see what numbers you have linked to your Microsoft account and trusted devices you have set up.
