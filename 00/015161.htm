If the table is currently open - as a table datasheet, as a recordset in code somewhere else in your database, as the Recordsource for an open form - you are going to get this message. It seems to happen most often when you have code on a form trying to update the table upon which that very form is based. Just be sure that the form is closed, or at least that you have saved the current record (DoCmd.RunCommand acCmdSaveRecord, or Me.Dirty=False, will work for that).

jeeped say:
 (example: = Offset($A$1,0,0,-12,0). $A$1 would need to be dynamic also. It could be driven by the vlookup cell reference. Does this approach make sense or is there a better solution?

I believe you are interpreting the offset rows and height parameters of the OFFSET() function incorrectly. It should be closer to,

=OFFSET($A$1, -11, 0, 12, 1)

Offset from A1 -11 rows and reference a range from that point 12 rows high and 1 column wide. You cannot offset from A1 -11 rows, so a MAX() or MIN() may have to be introduced.

A version of MATCH() that is close to your VLOOKUP() may be a good choice to couple with INDIRECT(). Something like,

=OFFSET(INDIRECT("A"&MATCH(<user input cell>, A:A, 0)), -11, 0, 12, 1)

Post some sample data and we can help with the specifics.

Hi,

 

 To add contacts in a group, here's how:

 

1. Sign in here.

2. Under People logo on the upper left portion of the page, you will notice "All" label with dropdown arrow. Please make sure that "All" is selected. 

3. Click/select the contact/s that you want to add in your group.

4. As you click on each contact, some options in the upper portion of the page will appear such as “Delete”, “Groups”, “Link” and “Send email”, click on Groups.

5. Choose the group that you want the selected contacts to be added.

6. Click Apply button. The selected contacts will be added in that group.

 

To view the contacts in that certain group, click the dropdown arrow that I mentioned in step 2. A dropdown menu will appear. Click on the group that you recently managed and all contacts under that group will be shown.

 

To delete contacts in a group, just click on the dropdown arrow to view the contacts in that group. Click the contact/s that you want to delete and click on Groups (same option mentioned in step 4). Uncheck the group and hit Apply button.
