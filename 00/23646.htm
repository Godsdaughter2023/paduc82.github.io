jan de messemaeker - project mvp say:

Hi,

 

I'm afraid your further questions show that you do not entirely believe what I said.

Entering a duration in DAYS will be automatically, inevitably, immediately, translated by Project into a duration in MINUTES using the "hours per day" parameter. That value is stored internally, NOT the original value in days you entered. And all of this is totally independent of applicable calendars.

So yes, when after that you change hours per day, duration in days change because the minutes are stored internally and now they are translated again using the new value. OTOH, since you did not change the hours per week there is no reason why duration expressed in weeks would change. No side issue, just a logical consequence of the basic rule: for Project all durations are in MINUTES. What you see in days or weeks is merely a translation using the hours per... constant.

Fortunately, there is an interesting solution to your problem about the 24/7 calendar. You can express durations in eUnits (for instance edays). They will then be translated as 24 hours per day, in ther words, e stands for elapsed, edays are calendar days whatever calendar or options.

howardhagen say:

How do I change the IMAP Server for Outlook 2016

My current IMAP SERVER is saying outlook.mail

Where do I go to make the changes?

I have to change it to the settings below:

  1. Select POP or IMAP. Click Next.
  2. Enter your name into the Your Name: field and your full email address into the E-mail Address: field (including the @comcast.net portion of your email address).
  3. Select IMAP in the Account Type: field.
  4. Enter imap.comcast.net into the Incoming mail server: field.
  5. Enter smtp.comcast.net into the Outgoing mail server (SMTP): field.
