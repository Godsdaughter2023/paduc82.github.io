rohn007 say:
Starter does not interfere with the activation of the trial.  But a factory installed trial DOES interfere with user installed Office, full or trial version.

Follow Annula's instructions to get into Programs & Features.  Ignore MS Starter, and the "Microsoft Office 2010" entry (which is related to Starter). Look for your installed trial and possibly something called like "Microsoft Office 2010 Single Image".  That is the name of the factory installed trial.  If you find 2, keep single image and uninstall the other.

Reboot the computer, then Repair Single Image (or Starter).

The factory trials are designed to be "single use". They check if a similar trial has been installed on the computer and abort the install. You can install one of the other trial packages: Home and Student, Professional, or Professional Plus.  Once you run out of trials, you can re-install Windows to clear out the hidden flags.

john w. vinson say:

This is a classic symptom of a missing reference. It really doesn't seem to matter WHICH library is Missing, and whether it's the library containing the Date function or not - IME, if any reference is MISSING, Date() will fail to be found. (Oddly, Now() seems to be exempt).

Type Ctrl-G to open the VBA editor, and select Tools... References. Is anything marked MISSING? Which library is it? If you uncheck the MISSING line and compile, does your code compile or does it now have errors?

mccbleue say:

Perhaps and didn't understand the question. He doesn't say he can't hear the phone, or that the volume reduces. He says that it turns off. By that, I would hazard a guess that he means the phone stops ringing.

 

The length off time that the phone rings for an incoming call is not decided by the phone, it is decided by the network. Windows Phone has no settings to influence this, and the network codes to change the network settings are not supported, so if you want to increase the length of time an incoming call rings before terminating or diverting to voicemail, you will need to contact your network operator for assistance. Typically, though, about 30 seconds is normally the max.
