If it is happening any time you try to open Excel, this is quite likely to be caused by one of your Add-Ins.

Go to the [File] tab, choose the "Options" choice.  In the Excel Options window, choose "Add-Ins".  Down at the bottom of the dialog is a drop-down list to select what type of add in to manage and a [Go...] button.

Start with the "Excel Add-Ins" and disable them.  Close Excel, re-open it and see if the error continues to nag at you.  Possibly repeat for other types of add-ins, such as COM Add-Ins.

When you finally get it to a state where it's opening without error, you can go back and start activating the various add-ins, one at a time and test each to see if it's causing the problem.

I understand that you would like the emails from a non-contact to go directly on your inbox without adding them on your contacts list. Please be informed that if you set your filters to Standard all emails from a non-contact will go directly to your inbox.

 

If you have set your account filters to Exclusive, emails from a non-contact will go directly to your junk folder. When you access your junk folder, you will see a notification that looks like the screenshot below:

 

 

You have to click the notification that states, Wait, it's safe! and this will move the email on your junk folder to your inbox. Please note that if you select this option, the contact will be added to your Safe list. All other incoming emails from your contact will go directly to your inbox.

 

You can check the settings on your account filters by following the steps below:

  1. On your inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.
  2. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.
  3. Under Choose a junk e-mail filter, choose the level of protection that you want, and then click Save.

 

To learn how you can add contact's on your Safe list without adding them on your contact's list, refer to the steps below:

  1. On your inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.
  2. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.
  3. Click Safe Senders.
  4. In the text box, type in the email address or domain you’d like to always receive email from, and then click Add to list >>.

    Word 2010 -Footers on one page only - Microsoft Community

    southwicker say:

    Word 2010 -Footers on one page only

    Hi, I have a 40 page document and I want a footer ONLY on the 2nd page.

    I cannot workout how to delete the footer from the rest of the document.

    Can anyone give me step by step instructions please?  Many Thanks ;)

    stefan blom say:

    If a specific page number should have a footer, you can use a field construct in the footer as follows:

    { IF { PAGE } = 2 "Add content for page 2" "" }

    To insert each pair of field delimiters, you have to press Ctrl+F9 (you can't type the delimiters). Use F9 to update fields in the selection. You can use Alt+F9 to show/hide field codes.

    The alternative is to add a section break on the previous page and then another section break at the end of the current page. The next step is to unlink footers so that you can change the content of only this one footer. For more on the general idea, see http://wordfaqs.ssbarnhill.com/FrontMatterRibbon.htm.
