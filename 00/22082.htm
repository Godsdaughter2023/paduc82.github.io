teylyn say:

The row numbers are out of sequence because some rows are hidden. 

You cannot change the row heading numbers. They are part of the Excel fabric. If you hide rows, then the row heading numbers will reflect that.

If you want consecutive numbering, consider deleting rows instead of hiding them. 

Even with a formula like Ashish suggests, if the formula is applied in the hidden rows, the numbers of the visible rows will still not be in sequence. 

That is simply how Excel works.
