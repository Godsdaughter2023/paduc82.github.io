To clear Office credentials for macOS

Sometimes credentials can be damaged, causing OneNote to continually ask you to enter your password. To solve it, do the following:

Exit all Mac Office applications, except OneNote.

In OneNote, close the session. This will only close the session, but all the notebooks will still be there.

Exit OneNote.

Start "Key Access" and search for all items that contain "Office16". They should be just a couple of elements, one for each account in which you have logged in.

The name will be a long alphanumeric code and the type will start with "MicrosoftOffice16 ...".

Remove these elements. Only authentication credentials are being removed, not data. When you log in again, they will be recreated.

Open OneNote and sign in to your accounts.

Since all Office applications share the credentials (Word, Excel, PowerPoint), you will also have to log in again in those applications.

talking moose say:
Outlook for Mac requires your mail to be in one specific location in your home folder (where " ~ " denotes your home folder):

~/Documents/Microsoft User Data/Office 2011 Identities

It does not allow you to move this or rename it.

Please be sure to let Microsoft know you'd like to see this feature in future versions by using the Help --> Send Feedback mechanism in any Office application.

An unsupported way of moving your data is to copy it to your new location and create an alias to it in the original location. You will be on your own to make that work.

Hope this helps!
