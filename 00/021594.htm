So, here is the resolution I came up with.

First of all, a Word document supports four different types of text wrapping breaks.

  1. 1. Clear to next line.
  2. 2. Clear wrapped item (such as an image) on the left.
  3. 3. Clear wrapped item (such as an image) on the right.
  4. 4. Clear all wrapped items.

The first type can be insert by pressing Shift+Enter. The last type can be added by inserting a Text Wrapping Break. As far as I know, Word doesn't provide any means to insert the other two. (Of course, as far as I know doesn't mean no means is provided.)

Yes, Office 2007 will continue to function after the end of extended support for Service Pack 3. (By the way, according to https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/lifecycle/search/8753, that's October 10, not October 16.)
