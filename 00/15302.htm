Option Explicit 
 
Sub FindWordCopySentence() 
    Dim appExcel As Object 
    Dim objSheet As Object 
    Dim aRange As Range 
    Dim intRowCount As Integer

    Set aRange = ActiveDocument.Range 
    With aRange.Find 
        Do 
            .Text = "shall" ' the word I am looking for
            .Execute 
            If .Found Then 
                aRange.Expand Unit:=wdSentence 
                aRange.Copy 
                aRange.Collapse wdCollapseEnd 
                If objSheet Is Nothing Then 
                    Set appExcel = CreateObject("Excel.Application")

Converting from POP3 to IMAP in MS Outlook 2013 - Microsoft Community

msmr say:

Converting from POP3 to IMAP in MS Outlook 2013

I use Windows 7 Pro (64bit); Office 2013 (32 bit) and MS Outlook for Email.  My Outlook has an extensive local folder structure which would exceed my ISP (Comcast) email limits if on the server.  My account is POP3 with Comcast.  I purchased an IPad Air2--initally all incoming emails appeared on IPad but after a month, suddenly only some of my incoming email appears on the IPad.  In addition i cannot synchronize  IPad email with computer email which is annoying.  I was told it was because i was using POP3 and should convert to IMAP.  I  researched this conversion both with Comcast and MS Outlook and confusion reigns !  I use only one email address. Questions/concerns:

1. Comcast says i should delete my POP3 account before setting up IMAP account.  Deleting my account before setting up a new one scares me!  First question:  Do i need Comcast to migrate my email from POP3 to IMAP ?  Some articles say "yes"--others say "no".
2. Will my local folder pst structure migrate (or attempt to migrate) to the server ?  I do not want this obviously because of its size and i only need this structure on my computer.
3. Will my current POP3 inbox migrate automatically to the IMAP account ?  or will i have to do something to migrate it ?  What about "Sent Items".  In the past i periodically drag and drop Sent Items to a local folder because i [mistakenly?] thought these items resided on the server and i wanted to keep size down.
4. Will Outlook still show my local folder structure after the conversion?  If so, will i still be able to forward mails from local folders ?

5. If local folders intact, and i drag email from Inbox to local folders will it be deleted from the server or do i have to manually delete from server by going to server website ?  Same for "Sent Items" ?  

These are initial concerns/questions about which i've read conflicting/confusing information.  Thanks for any assistance.

roady [mvp] say:

1) No, Comcast has no role in this. You can leave your POP3 account at first but you'll have to remove it when you start copying data to your IMAP account or you'll end up receiving the message again in your POP3 account as well.

2) No, the emails that you downloaded to your pst-file via POP3 will remain there, even when  you remove the account.

3) Nothing migrates automatically. You'll need to add the IMAP account and then remove the POP3 account. POP3 and IMAP are just 2 ways of accessing your mailbox just like accessing it via a web interface is a 3rd option. All can be used at the same time.

4) Yes.

5) Dragging them out of your IMAP folders and into a folder within your pst-file will indeed remove them from the server during the next send/receive interval.
