brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:
You cn upgrade from Outlook 2002 to Outlook 2007 on an XP machine.  I think it would be worth the effort if you've spent the money for the license already.  There are other features in Outlook 2007 that I find valuable that are not in Outlook 2002.  Whether or not it would be worth it to you, though, only you can say.  Your PST already contains your calendar, mail, contacts, notes, journal, and all your other folders.  While uninstalling Outlook 2002 and installing Outlook 2007 should not tough that data, it's always a good idea to back up first.  With Outlook closed, just make a copy of the PST to another folder.  http://www.howto-outlook.com/howto/backupandrestore.htm .  If you do upgrade, be sure to create a new mail profile.  http://www.howto-outlook.com/howto/newprofile.htm .  Also, it would be a good idea to convert your PST to the new Unicode format Outlook 2007 uses.  http://www.slipstick.com/outlook/ansi-to-unicode.asp
