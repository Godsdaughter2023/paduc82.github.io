jay freedman say:
(Assuming you have either Word 2007 or Word 2010)

Do you also see the symbol ¶ at the end of every paragraph? If so, you have all the "nonprinting characters" displayed. The dots and ¶ symbols don't print -- they only appear on the screen.

If you do see both dots and ¶ symbols, click the Home tab of the ribbon. About in the middle of the ribbon you should see a button with the ¶ symbol on it. Click that button to turn it off.

If you only see dots, then you need to do this instead: Click the File tab (if you have Word 2010) or the Office button (if you have Word 2007). Click Options (or Word Options). Click Display on the left side. In the section labeled "Always show these formatting marks on the screen", uncheck every box. Then click OK.

Apparently you are displaying non-printing characters. The dots represent the spaces between the words.

To turn this off click to unhighlight the Show/Hide ¶ button on the ribbon/toolbar, or press Ctrl+Shift+8.

    Sure this is easy and can be done with one line of code.
