iandmacpherson say:

In Outlook, what you can do if you receive a lot of spam or vicious emails from addresses containing invalid characters.

When you first start using Outlook, you will find that it is configured to receive all emails, except those from the senders that you block. Blocking is impossible when the sender’s address either contains an invalid character or replaces their address with yours.

The solution (as Microsoft seem unwilling or unable to spend the time and effort to automatically bin any emails with sender address containing an invalid character), is to re-configure Outlook to only accept emails from addresses on your Safe List, which may already exist but will need to be edited and augmented.

Depending on the size of your contact list, this process may take a couple of hours to complete.

xxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxxOpen Outlook

Select the little cog icon(top right of screen)

Options

Mail

Safe Senders (under Junk Mail)

Open a second Outlook session and Copy and Paste  the email addresses from your contact list into the Safe Senders list. Save the file regularly, just in case you can’t do it all in one go.

Once you are happy that your Safe Senders list is complete, select Filters and reporting. Change the Junk Mail Filter from Standard to Exclusive. Share Reports is optional, but you probably will want to Block Attachments in the first instance till you are happy with the operation of your new Outlook configuration.

Finally, select Options (top left of screen) to return to normal email screen.
