xbf diego m say:

Hi Luke,

Headset troubleshooting can be somewhat complicated but I'd be happy to try and help.

It seems that right now the controller's 3.5 mm audio port is not detecting that specific headset. Audio jacks have two basic standards with the difference being where the microphone ring on the jack is located. Most of them have the correct standard, but I think the first thing you want to try is make sure the jack on the headset is clean and there is no dust inside of the port in the controller.

Once you clean the port and jack, see if you can try the headset on your phone or laptop to make sure the stereo sound works and on the phone you can test the microphone by calling someone. If you confirm the headset is working fine, then hard reset your Xbox and power cycle the power unit.

If the headset is still not being picked up then you may need to find another controller and test it out there to see if the controller needs to be replaced.

The only other option would be to find an Xbox One Stereo headset adapter that connects to the port at the bottom of the controller.
