If it disappears, you will have to either keep all instances of browers closed or you will have to disable Skype plug-in in browers

Windows snipping tool also sometimes cause the above problem. Open Snipping tool > Options > Uncheck "Always copy snips to the clipboard". MS Outlook is another known culprit. Shut down MS Outlook and see whether it impacts.

2. If above are not the case, it is time for some detective work. Start your Task Manager by CTRL+SHIFT+ESC or by CTRL+ALT+DELETE and choosing Start Task Manager (or right click on Windows Task Bar and choose Start Task Manager). Look into Processes tab. Remote Desktop applications are known culprit. You can try locating rdpclip.exe and kill it. Also look for TightVNC. If this is not listed, then you have to do detective work. Now kill the processes one by one and monitor whether the problem has disappeared or not. After killing the process, restart your Excel to determine if problem has disappeared or not. This way you will be able to locate the culprit process / application. 

3. Type msconfig.exe in search program/files box above Windows icon (bottom left), look into Services tab and check "Hide all Microsoft Services" to hide Microsoft Services from the list. You can disable / enable services one by one as in step 2 and monitor whether the problem has disappeared or not. After diabling the service, restart your Excel to determine if problem has disappeared or not. This way you will be able to locate the culprit service. If you don't prefer msconfig.exe route, you can click on Services tab on Task Manager (in step 2). At the bottom, you will notice Services button. This button can be clicked to reach to all Services. What is meant by a "work account" and how is it created?

tim liu msft say:

Hi Hairy,

Let me answer your question as below:

Work/school account & Microsoft account

Work/school account is related to Office 365 for Business services. Admin can sign up a tenant of Office 365 for Business. Subscribe Office 365 for Business plans for this tenant. And then he creates accounts in this tenant. These accounts are called as work/school accounts.

Microsoft account is the account related to Microsoft consumer services. User signs up this email him/herself. They can create a new account like Outlook.com or use existing email address. So, all email accounts can be signed up as Microsoft accounts.

Different login page

When we log into Microsoft services, we can see some words like “sign in Microsoft account”, “Sign in work/school account” or “sign in with Work/school account or Microsoft account”. They are related to different Microsoft services. With Microsoft account means consumer personal services. With work/school account means Office 365 for Business services. With Microsoft account or work/school account, means the service is related to both consumer user and enterprise user. The login page and authentication process will identify if you enter the proper account for that service.

OneDrive & OneDrive for Business

OneDrive is consumer personal service related to Microsoft account.

OneDrive for Business is personal storage in a tenant related to work/school account. OneDrive for Business is the service included in Office 365 for Business plans.

If you just want to upload files to OneDrive for personal use, you can share files with that two users’ email addresses. If their accounts are not Microsoft accounts, they will be asked to sign up the email accounts as Microsoft accounts. The steps are easy.

If you want to use Office 365 for Business services, you can sign up and subscribe a plan. During signing up process, you create an admin account. You use this account for Office 365 for Business services such as OneDrive for Business, SharePoint Online. And then you can share files with that two users with their existing email address or create two work/school accounts for them in your tenant. In this situation, you need to purchase another two licenses for those two newly created work/school accounts.  

Best regards,

Tim

Please try the following:

  1. Find the file size of the presentation.
