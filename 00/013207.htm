jllatham say:

The "why I have to separate DB2" answer is that SUMPRODUCT() doesn't work across multiple sheets.  It will give you a #REF! error if you try -- I verified this with a couple of simple tests to make sure my memory wasn't playing tricks on me.

Your next problem is that TEXT() and even date functions suchs as MONTH() and YEAR() only want a single cell to reference unless you use an array formula.  Given all of that, to simplify your database, I had to complicate it!

First, I added 2 columns on both DB and DB2 right after column A to give me new columns B and C.  In B1, I put the formula =MONTH(A1) and in C1 I put the formula = YEAR(A1) and filled them down.  Then on the SUMMARY sheet, instead of a text entry of JUL10, I entered July 1, 2010 as a date, and formatted the cell to just show me the month and 2-digit year but at it's heart it's actually a date.

Keep in mind that you could hide the new columns B and C or even put them way out at the right end of all of your data where they'd be more or less out of view if they distract you.

Now, for the formula in C2 on the Summary sheet, I ended up with:

=SUMPRODUCT(--(DB!B$1:B$4=MONTH($A$1)),--(DB!C$1:C$4=YEAR($A$1)),--(DB!D$1:D$4=A2),--(DB!E$1:E$4=B2),--(DB!$F$1:F$4>0),(DB!F$1:F$4)) + SUMPRODUCT(--('DB2'!B$1:B$4=MONTH($A$1)),--('DB2'!C$1:C$4=YEAR($A$1)),--('DB2'!D$1:D$4=A2),--('DB2'!E$1:E$4=B2),--('DB2'!$F$1:F$4>0),('DB2'!F$1:F$4))

and for the formula in D2 on the Summary sheet, I ended up with:

=SUMPRODUCT(--(DB!B$1:B$4=MONTH($A$1)),--(DB!C$1:C$4=YEAR($A$1)),--(DB!D$1:D$4=A2),--(DB!E$1:E$4=B2),--(DB!$F$1:F$4<0),(DB!F$1:F$4)) + SUMPRODUCT(--('DB2'!B$1:B$4=MONTH($A$1)),--('DB2'!C$1:C$4=YEAR($A$1)),--('DB2'!D$1:D$4=A2),--('DB2'!E$1:E$4=B2),--('DB2'!$F$1:F$4<0),('DB2'!F$1:F$4))

And those gave me the exact output results that you show in your sample data in the initial posting.  My apologies for complicating your sheet, but at least we have one result that works?

jeeped say:

... I am trying to do a lookup on a column in which some of the numbers start with zero. However, when i put a number in B16, i sometimes have a problem with returning the right value because of the zero not reading ...


Let's start by setting the record straight on the cell value. If the cell's value has one or more leading zeroes as a prefix and those leading zeroes are part and parcel of the cell's value and not simply part of a modified displayed value through a custom number format, then you do not have numbers in your cell at all. You have text that looks like a number and these cannot be treated in the same manner that you would treat an actual number. A text cell with 012345 in it needs to be treated the same as if dog was in the cell. In this manner, you can use =IF(B16="012345",... but not =IF(B16=12345,...  or =IF(B16=012345,...  

...  I also have to make up for missing numbers on the back end.  If the number is short of 10 digits, i have to add zeros at the end to make it 10 digits. So, before i go adding zeros at the end, i need to make sure that the zero at the beginning of the number is being recognized ...


I honestly do not know what you will do if half of your column are actual numbers and the other half are text that look like numbers, but perhaps something like this will help,

=IF(LEN(B16),IF(LEFT(B16,1)="0", B16&REPT("0",MAX(0,10-LEN(B16))),B16),"")

You might also want to investigate the ISTEXT() and TYPE() functions so you do not have to rely upon a leading zero to determine if the cell's value should be adjusted. Perhaps an actual number could be adjusted with TEXT() rather than concatenating a number of trailing zeroes.

jose est say:
You are using two email clients: your mobile phone's email app and Outlook 2016. These email clients are in sync with the email server but not with each other.

When an email is sent to you, the email server will send copies to your email clients (phone's email app and Outlook 2016). The emails will be saved on your email client and will not be deleted on the email server unless configured otherwise (if supported).

That being said, you will have two copies of email (one for your phone's email app and one for Outlook 2016) every time an email is sent to you. If it is configured and supported, the email will be deleted from the server once you delete it from your email client (phone's email app or Outlook 2016). But this is not true for two email clients even if it's for the same email profile since they are not in sync.

In short, your preference: "if I delete a message from the inbox on my iPhone, I would expect it to be in the trash folder when I login to that email on Outlook 2016," is not yet possible.

Hi Claire,

There are several ways on how you can block emails in your mailbox. You may follow the different steps mentioned below in order to block these emails:

  • Junk mail filter - Selecting Exclusive option. This feature will let you receive emails from your contact list and safe senders list.
  • Email rules - You can set up email rules to filter incoming emails to automatically delete them and not getting them in your Inbox.
  • Blocked senders list - You can add email address/es or domain/s in this list to stop receiving emails from them. You also need to delete them from your contact list and safe senders list as well.
  • Sweep features - Sweeping emails for deletion.

Method 1: To set up a junk mail filter to Exclusive:

  1. Sign in to your Microsoft account.
  2. Click the Gear icon located on the upper right panel of your mailbox, just beside your username.
  3. Select Options.
  4. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.
  5. Under Choose a junk email filter, select Exclusive.
  6. Click Save.

Method 2: If you want to create an email rule to delete emails coming from an email address:

  1. Sign in to your Microsoft account.
  2. Click the Gear icon located on the upper right panel of your mailbox, just beside your username.
  3. Select Options.
  4. Under Customizing Outlook, click Rules for sorting new messages.
  5. Click New.
  6. Under Step 1: Which messages do you want this rule to apply to? On the first box with drop down list, select the Sender's address and in the second box with drop down list, select yes.
  7. On the third box, type sender's email address. You may select any option that you want to use at first, second and third box depending on your preference.
  8. Under Step 2: What action do you want to apply? Select Delete these messages.
  9. Click Save.

Method 3: You can also add the sender's email address to your Blocked sender list to stop receiving emails from that contact:

  1. Sign in to your Microsoft account.
  2. Click the Gear icon located on the upper right panel of your mailbox, just beside your username.
  3. Select Options.
  4. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.
  5. Click Blocked senders.
  6. In the Blocked email address or domain box, type the email address of the sender or the domain that you want to block, then click Add to list.

Once you add an email address in your blocked senders list, you may also need to delete the account details from your contact list.

You can also optimize the browser to help solve this issue.
