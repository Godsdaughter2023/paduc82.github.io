MINI REVIEW / MY PERSONAL THOUGHTS:

I bought this phone in the begining of July, and reported several problems in a post called:
Nokia 6110 Navigator - Design, Firmware & Software Inconsistencies - Final Report (28-07-2007)


For three weeks (15/08 till 03/09), during my holyday, I used it across Europe intensely for navigational purposes.
Afterall and accordingly to its name - Navigator - this phone is supposed to help and guide you from one place to another, and in my opinion, this feature is the main reason for the purchase.
So if you consider buying it for its gadjets features, which include the routing facilities, please don't read any further...
If instead you need its route guidance, maybe you should be aware of what you will be getting...

Now the facts:
Phone: Nokia 6110 Navigator
Firmware: Version 3.51
Software: Route 66
Maps: World map, Iberia 2006 Q4 (included with the phone)
France 2006 Q4, Italy 2006 Q4 (bought for this trip, €49,99 each)
User: Me, a experienced navigator (I navigate for more than 30 years (sailing) and use GPS systems since their appearence, 1988-89 if I remember correctly...)
I have used other GPS gear (mainly Garmin), when sailing, diving or driving
Trip: 8000 Km by car (ferry distance not included)
Lisbon-Barcelona

Requirements: Nokia Charger DC-4
...and lots of calm for not going insane, most of the time!

I - First a small note about prices...

Is the phone well priced?
Well... The answer is yes and no...
If you are using it only in your country (with the maps included with the phone), I think it can be considered a bargain, feature wise considered.
Now if you begin buying maps, lets say 3, you already paid more than Route 66 maps of Western Europe (9 countries) ... In fact, if you do your calculations correctly you are paying more a less four times more for the same maps!
So if you need several maps, this phone will be 2, 3 times more expensive than a PDA (or Symbian Phone) with for instance, TomTom software and all European maps (the same goes for Route66 similar software). Have this in mind before buying...


II - Planning a Trip...

The problem begins...
First, you have to run the Navigator Application, which was perfectly normal if you weren't in doors... because the program will try to make a fix (get your position) with no sattelites in view, draining the phone's battery and there is no way to stop it!
Second, as only one map can be active at time, the Navigator Application can not calculate a route from one country to another, for instance, it's impossible to set a trip from Marseille (France) to Salamanca (Spain).
To overcome this you need to have a map (a paper one), choose the road and then discover it on your phone's display, zoom in and save a "Landmark" in the position where the road finishes (at the border, between countries); This will be the "Destination" of "Trip 1a".
Then switch maps, discover the beginning of the same road in the other map (country), again saving this "Landmark" as the "Departure" of "Trip 1b".
With this work around you will navigate, but you don't have distance, time of arrival, etc.; besides when passing the border you have to switch from one map to the other, choose the new trip, for instance, "Trip 1b" which is quite unfriendly and dangerous while driving...
The menus won't help either, as the most used functions are somewhere at the end, so you need to scroll and scroll again...

Even with practice it takes several hours (8-10) to plan a trip like this one and be sure to have (fisical) maps, paper, pencil, a lot of patience and the calm not to through your phone into the garbadge bag or smash it against the nearest wall of your room...
Yes, it's that hard, and in my opinion this is caused by bad software engeneering designed by people that don't have a clue, in practice, how to navigate, plan a trip, etc..
I even doubt that this navigation mess was tested by competent users!

To give you an idea of this mess, let's plan a simple trip in map [XXX] from point [A --> B --> C]:

From practice the best way to begin with is to define these 3 points as "Landmarks":
01) Open the [Navigator Application]
02) Goto [Options]
03) [Search]
04) Choose [Address], first option fron the 6 available
05) Type the search text followed by and highlight your option
06) Goto [Options]
07) Move the highlighted option 3 times down to [Save] and make
08) Save as "landmark"
09) Type a name/number for this "landmark" and save

Now repeat this procedure for the other 2 points and you already entered 34 imputs of keyboard


10) Goto [Options]
11) Move the highlighted option 4 times up the menu and enter [Trips)
12) Move the highlighted option 1 time up the menu and enter [Plan new route)
13) Enter name of route and save
14) The highlighted line is positioned in [Departure]
15) Enter this option and you are asked again to set [Set departure]
16) Choose one option from the 6 available, for instance [My Landmarks], the fifth, so move the highlighted option 4 times down; choose A and [enter]
17) The highlighted line is positioned in [Destination]
18) Enter this option and you are asked again to set [Set destination]
19) Choose one option from the 6 available, for instance [My Landmarks], the fifth, so move the highlighted option 4 times down; choose C and [enter]
20) The highlighted line is positioned in [Destination]; choose
21) Move the highlighted option 2 times down the menu and enter [Add waypoint)
22) Choose one option from the 6 available, for instance [My Landmarks], the fifth, so move the highlighted option 4 times down; choose B and [enter]

Now you already entered 75 imput of keyboard (obviously I'm not counting the typed text for save, search, etc.).
This is too much for a 3 point trip! Try this simple exercise with 3 known places in your home town and see the time you spend!
In conclusion and just to name a few problems, the order of the menus is baddly disposed, no copy paste of text available, the phone doesn't remember the last typed text, etc., etc., which translates itself in a very unfriendly use.

III - Lets move on and see what you will face after these preliminary steps, at least from my experience with Firmware 3.51:

01-The phone will freeze 1-2 times a day for aparently no reason.
Solution: Most of the time remove the battery and restart the phone, the Navigator Application, activate your trip, etc., etc..
02-At two times though, the Navigator Application started but ended immediately afterwards; all the other functions of the phone aparently ran without problems.
Solution: Shutdown the phone, remove the battery, remove the memory card, turn the phone on without memory card, open and close the Navigator Application, shutdown the phone again and remount the memory card, turn on the phone again and restart the Navigator Application again, choose the same trip, etc..

Both are a good test to your patience and to one's temper, especially in the havy traffic of Napoli or Rome!
Believe me, I know...

03-The phone will give wrong directions after you come out to clear sky from a more than one kilometer long tunnel, like "Turm around when possible", or indicates a "Now make a U turn" in a highway, or even will instruct you to "Take the next exit" + "Turn Left" + "Follow the road" + "At the roundabout take the 3rd exit" and put you 500 meters after in the same highway you were before!
Sometimes it will only stop the navigation, and or change the display setings.
In a word, a very temperamental navigator!

04-In Corsica and during some time (and more than 30 kilometers distance) gave wrong route indications to the point we lost an hour (and almost the ferry schedule) with the error.
It could not get a fix in clear sky conditions; be aware that the small compass will be kept frozen in one direction, so look at the sun (or moon, or stars) and figure out in what direction you are travelling...

05-Sometimes it takes you out of the main road and puts you in second/third class roads, aparently with no reason at all...

06-Other times it doesn't provide clear information about which way to go, so you end up not knowing which side of the road to place yourself - bare right or left would be useful in this situations...

07-It's also quite common to come out of a tunnel that has 2/3 exits and not having any indication of what road to take...

08-Many times you just take the most complicated and longest way to arrive to your destination; if you zoom out you clearly see it on the display, where strangely both ways are showned...

09-When moving towards a waypoint, the display will also show other routes to other waypoints (if they are close enough or cross the the same zone), what turs the visual indication very confusing, to say the least!

10-Sometimes you turn the Navigator Application on and achieve a fix in 30 seconds - other times it takes 4/5 minutes - and we are talking about clear sky for both cases, so no problems with sattelite reception.
This was confirmed by another GPS I took with me, a 4 years old Garmin...

11-If a road is blocked or you cannot make that parte of the route by any other reason, it doesn't offer you an alternative way most of the time, and continues again and again driving you to the same point or blocked road; you have to stop navigation, drive a little further and reinitiate the trip again!

12-No low battery (or recharge) indication while navigating... The phone simple turn off without warning, when the battery becomes exausted - No time to connect the recharger!

13-Doesn't provide any information (distance, ETA (estimated time of arrival), for intermediate waypoints...


CONCLUSION:

When everything works as supposed, very handy indeed.
It's like if you were young again and your parents took you by hand to all those nice places.
No concerns, no need to read the road exits, numbers, indications, etc.

I really would like to watch the engeneer's software team planning a trip like this, or driving in heavy traffic in cities like Rome or Naples and resolving all the situations I encountered with this scrappy «Route66» software!
Nokia should have better (beta) tested the phone prior to release.
The 1-2 crashes per day are inadmissible!
This navigation software needs urgently a redesign or advice from people that know how to navigate...

So, would I buy this phone again?
Working this way and with this Navigation Software, with concern!

 " will be due upon acceptance of this proposal. "

barb reinhardt say:

This should get you started:

 

Dim myStart As Long
Dim myEnd As Long
Dim myCell As Excel.Range

myStart = Len("An initial deposit in the amount of")
myEnd = Len(" will be due upon acceptance of this proposal.")
Set myCell = ActiveCell

myCell.Font.Bold = False

myCell.Characters(Start:=myStart, Length:=Len(myCell) - myStart - myEnd).Font.Bold = True
   

 

I'm not sure how you determine the cell that's formatted though.   Is it in the same cell all the time?
