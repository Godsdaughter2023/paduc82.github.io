cu ninjaneer say:

No Sound on DVD

I've had a sound problem with my Xbox 360 for several weeks now.  First, the back story.

  • Xbox hooked up to a "new" TV, works fine, no issues for several weeks.
    • TV is several years old (3 - 5 maybe), but was recently given to me.  It's a Westinghouse LCD.
    • Xbox is connected via an HDMI cable.
  • Xbox taken to my in laws house for one night, works fine there, brought back home, hooked up to same Westinghouse LCD.
  • Suddenly there is no sound at all from the Xbox
Peruse Xbox forums and try the following troubleshooting tips:
  • Disable display discovery - no improvement
  • Enable display discovery - no improvement
  • Try a new HDMI cable - no improvement
  • Try every combination of three different HDMI cables in 4 different HDMI input ports - no improvement
  • Switch to RCA connection - sound comes back along with a sad, grainy square picture
  • Swap back to HDMI - no sound again
  • Try connecting laptop over HDMI - no sound
    • Aha, it's the TV, right?  Well, maybe...

So I peruse forums for Westinghouse TV troubleshooting tips:

  • Press and hold TV power button for 10 seconds to "reboot" TV - now get sound from Xbox most of the time

So now I have sound in the menus, in games, on netflix, on amazon prime, and seemingly everything, but...

  • I play a DVD - no sound
  • So I tinker with the settings and discover the following:
    • If I change the audio digital output to anything other than "Digital Stero" (other options are "Dolby Digital 5.1" and "Dolby Digital with WMA Pro"), then I completely lose all sound again.
    • When I change it back to "Digital Stereo" then the sound comes back as before (still no sound on DVD).

Finally, before posting this, I try one last thing i read, but hadn't tried yet:

  • Turn off console, turn on console with remote, hold down "Y" and "RT" until console restarts - Problem solved!

So, I actually solved my problem about 5 minutes before posting this, but I thought I would post it anyway in case it helps anyone else with a similar problem.

hula_baloo say:

 It's a part of Windows that contains numerous performance related settings for visual effects, allocation of task performance preference, and something called DEP (Data Execution Prevention).  It isn't going to hurt anything to allow it to start, but my advice about changing things in it would be "if it ain't broke, don't fix it".  I've seen a lot sad tales of woe in these forums from those who tinkered around with things they had no reason to fool with.

sean desktop pro say:

Hi DavidRVA,

If your old laptop is 64 bit you can easily move your data to the Windows 10 laptop with an external hard drive. Copy the C:Windows\System32\Migwiz folder from the old Windows 7 laptop on to the drive. Run the Windows Easy Transfer on the old laptop and when it asks you, tell it to put the "Windows Easy Transfer - Items from old computer.MIG" file on the external drive as well. When it finishes copying your data, connect the drive to your new laptop. Run the "Migwiz.Exe" file within the "Migwiz" folder you copied from the Windows 7 laptop and continue with the Easy Transfer Wizard.

I have tested this procedure on a 64-Bit Windows 10 PC copying data from an old Vista 64-Bit PC and a Windows 7 PC 32 and 64-Bit to a Windows 8 PC 64-Bit with success. The new Windows Edge browser will ask you if you want to import your Favorites from Internet Explorer. 

Notes: A Windows Easy Transfer's "Migwiz" folder taken from a 32-Bit PC will not run on a 64-Bit PC and vice-versa. However, the 64-Bit version will accept the "Windows Easy Transfer - Items from old computer.MIG" file created from a 32-Bit version. 

I hope this helps.
