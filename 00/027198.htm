doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

Let's get the terminology straight.  There are two features in Word:

  1. The saving of AutoRecover information for which the interval can be set under File>Options>Save.  This feature will retain a copy of the document, as it was when "saved" by that feature, IF Word shuts down in an abnormal manner, without the user being given the opportunity to save their work.
  2. Also under File>Options>Save, there is a "Keep the last autosaved version if I close without saving" box, which if checked, will retain the last copy of the document, as it was when saved by the feature in 1 above, if the user closes the document without saving it.

In your case, it was the first of these features that would have retained a copy of your document.  

When Word is restarted after abnormal program termination, there may be two copies of a document in the Document Recovery Pane, those being the last version saved by the AutoRecover feature and also the copy that was last saved by the User.  Each copy will be identified with the relevant information pertaining to the way in which it was saved and you do need to pay attention to which of those copies you select, particularly before saving a copy that you select.

There is, in addition, a place where you can go to look for documents that you may be able to recover under File>Info>Manage Versions>Recover Unsaved Documents.
