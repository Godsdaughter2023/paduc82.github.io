Prevent losing email in Outlook.com when you've set up POP3

Outlook.com has a feature that prevents losing email messages if you accidentally set up POP3 to delete them after reading them on the server. Here’s how it works:

    The first time that Outlook.com receives a POP3 instruction to delete email messages after reading from an email app, Outlook.com won't delete the messages. Instead, Outlook.com moves the email messages that were successfully downloaded by the email app into a POP folder and out of the inbox. The POP folder is available in the Folders list.

    Outlook.com keeps the email messages in the POP folder until you sign in to your Outlook.com account and verify whether Outlook.com should respect or ignore the POP3 delete command.

Don’t let another program delete messages from Outlook.com. (If your other program is set to delete messages from the server, we’ll simply move them to a special POP folder. They won’t be deleted.)

swischner say:

Romel Ram obviously did not even understand your request which seems rather normal for this forum.  

And what you (and many other OneNote users) need to understand is the following:

OneNote is NOT a PDF editor/notifications program. In fact as soon as you add a PDF to a OneNote page (or several pages) it is no longer a PDF anymore. It's nothing but an bitmap image, a picture.

Think of printing out your PDF to paper, put it in a scanner, save the scan to a JPG or PNG file and place that in a OneNote page. This is exactly what happens. So there are no PDF pages anymore, just a bunch of pictures.

Now OneNote has the ability to try(!) and recognise text out of a bunch of pixels in an image (OCR, optical character recognition) so you can search for content ... as long as this content has been correctly recognized / converted. How accurate this recognization is, depends on a range of factors, for example image quality, colors, fonts.

You noticed that sometimes you can search for a term like "page 5" and sometimes you can't. That's because the OCR mechanism may have  successfully recognized the bunch of pixels at the end of the image as text ("Page 5"). Or not. 

In short: OneNote does not know about page numbers from your original PDF, in fact it doesn't know anything about that original PDF. It just has pictures of the PDF pages and tries to "read" text in those pictures.

To annotate PDFs I use a software which is made for this purpose like FoxIt reader or some similar PDF editor. In those programs you load the actual PDF and are able to navigate in it, annotate it, modify it. If I want to keep printouts of certain PDF pages (images) in OneNote, i do the annotation in the PDF editor first, save the modified PDF and THEN print out a copy to OneNote. 

You may also attach the actual PDF file to a OneNote page (insert it as file, not as printout) and then open it from there with an external PDF editor by just double clicking it. This is similar to a file you attached to an email. You even can put both in your notes, the attached file and a printout.
