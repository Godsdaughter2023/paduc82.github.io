rfcomm2k_ say:

Hi Ashish,

 

Early in my post I stated there were several dozen columns I wish to do this for. And there are at present 1954 line items.

This is a job estimating program I am developing. Imagine I am building a house. I try to treat each different part of the job separately because each part has different needs. Each column represents a different aspect of the job (example a wall or a floor) There could be up to 12 different floors in a job. Each floor is different (a bathroom floor may have ceramic tile with radiant floor heat, a den could have hardwood, a bedroom over a garage may have carpet but it also needs insulation). As you can see the choices are endless.

So I want to summarize the labor and material totals for each separate column. So lets imagine column C is 'Wall1'. This wall will need 9 2x4x8' studs, 4 sheets of 1/2" drywall, x amount of mud and tape for the drywall joints, y amount of trim, and z amount of ceramic tile on one side of it.

Each material is chosen in column C. In column DQ I sum up all the 2x4 studs for entire job across line 10. In column DR I multiply that by cost. In column DS I multiply the quantity of studs across line 10 by the amount of time required for handling each in order to construct this wall. In column DT I multiply that time (in minutes) by the cost per minute. And in column DU I add up all the dollar amounts. At the bottom of column DU I sum everything to give me the cost for the entire job (my cost) and elsewhere I add my markups.

Now I want to summarize my costs for each separate part of the job (what will it cost me to build Wall1). So I need to multiply every line item in column C by the cost for that item and also by the labor for that item. These are done on a separate sheet (called summary). What I started to do on that sheet is in cell A1 (first item in entire database) I have the quantity of 2x4x8' studs from the main sheet (called project) cell C10 (wall1 column C, 2x4x8' studs line 10). I multiply that quantity * the cost and that is in cell A1. Cell B1 has the quantity from project!C10 * the labor needed (in minutes) * the cost per minute. Cells A2 and B2 have same info for project!C11, and so on down to project!C1954 in cells A1944 and B1944 on summary sheet. Then I repeat the entire process for project!D10 in cells C1 and D1 of summary page.

As you can see, doing it my way will take a LOT of typing, and even if I copy & paste I still need to modify a lot because a copy & paste will not skip columns. So if I copy column A to column C, even though I wish to reference only one column over from the project sheet, it will increment by 2 columns because I am skipping a column in the summary sheet.

OMG, I think I just answered my own question! Copy and paste the item cost formula from column A into columns B thru DP. then build the labor formula in column DQ and copy & paste that al the way across to column HH (or wherever it ends).

Thanks for making me explain this, it made me realize an easy way to do it.
