grschinon say: You won't be able to move your messages back to C:.

When you switch from one drive to the other, the firmware looks to see if the directory structure has been set up to receive the messages on the target drive. If not, it creates that structure and offers to copy or move your existing messages from the original drive over to the target. This happens when you first decide to start using E: instead of C:.

However, if the directory structure already exists on the target drive -- as it will do invariably on C: since a reset sets it up -- then it doesn't offer to copy anything over.

Netgear GS724T.  I bought mine refurbished a year or more back.

I guess 'pricey' all depends on what you are into and what matters to you.  If the speed of your network is important - investing in quality products to make sure said network is working at peak performance makes sense. 

Also going the extra step to make sure that the device drivers you utilized are the recommended ones from the manufacturer of the device(s) in question is always good to do.

Drive read/write speeds can make a huge difference too.  When a HDD can be anywhere from 2 to 5 times (or more) slower at reading/writing (than an SSD) and that speed (of the HDD) is 80MB/s to 120MB/s (when not in a RAID) on average - well, your gigabit network is not the only choke point.  The hard drive can (and could well be in your case) the slow part in the equation - given what you have.  Most of my systems have SSDs for the majority of the daily stuff - with transfers of long-term or just extremely large stuff going to (in some cases) an internal HDD.  Most things end up on the NAS - where the RAID does help increase performance slightly (for reads.)

 

So look at all of your equipment, check the specs on it...
