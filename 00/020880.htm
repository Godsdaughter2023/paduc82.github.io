swischner say:
About the parallel existence of Office 2010 and 2016 (part of Office 365, more on this soon): While they should be able to live side by side it is not recommended and cause some trouble under certain circumstances. Best practice is to uninstall 2010 first and then install 2016. Should be possible to remove 2010 afterward too, but not sure if there could be complications. Other experts here may be more clear on that.

About the difference of Office 2016 and 365: This is confusing a lot of people. Microsoft did an awful job here from the beginning to make that clear. I try a simple explanation:

Office 2016 is a software package consisting of modules like Word, Excel or Powerpoint that are installed on your PC. So exactly like your Office 2010, just newer versions of the programs.

Office 365 isn't software at all. It's more of a marketing term describing a bundle of programs and services and also a subscription model to purchase those programs and services (paid on a monthly or yearly basis instead of paying a one-time purchase price). There are several subscription models of Office 365 (called Office 365 "plans") that differ in includes services and programs and also determine on how many devices you are allowed to use the included software.

Most Office 365 plans include the Office 2016 programs to install on your (or several) PC(s) but also offer more features like mobile apps, an amount of cloud storage space or (for some business targeted plans) even hosted exchange mail or Sharepoint servers.

So what you need to know is: Office 2016 is the actual software package that you install on your PC and may be bought as a standalone one-time down-payment package (like Office 2010) or as part of an Office 365 subscription.

One additional important difference: If you buy a single one-time-paid license of Ofice 2016 it stays as it is and will only get security updates and bug-fixes in the future. If you buy it as part of an Office 365 subscription, there will be constant updates that add new features. So if you always want to have the most recent version of Office 2016, go for Office 365.

I hope I did not add to the confusion :)
