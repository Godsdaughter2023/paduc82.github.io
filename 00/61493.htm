frederik long say:

Windows maintains only one backup copy of the registry files (apart from the ones kept by System Restore). In view of the lack of reliability of System Restore I expanded this to five versions, using my own script. If you have extra backup copies then you could use them, provided that they are true backup copies of the registry.

Why your Restore Points became corrupted I do not know. I run an extremely tight ship yet it happened to me too. In some other thread a Microsoft engineer advised that some Automatic Updates will delete all Restore Points (which means that you cannot go back if the update goes bad . . .). Perhaps one of those updates corrupted our restore points.

In general I am disappointed with the three services that are based on the VSS facility under Windows 7 (System Restore, System Image, Backup). They all have extremely basic functionality and they all appear to have reliability problems. Which is rather sad: You would want your backup facility to be rock solid.
