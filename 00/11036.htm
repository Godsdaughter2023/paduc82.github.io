How can I get a sequential list of figures or tables if some of the - Microsoft Community

jillelhonsali say:

How can I get a sequential list of figures or tables if some of the figure/tables are marked "hidden text"?

I have a long document where some of the figures/tables and captions are marked as hidden text. When I update the fields in the document, the lists of figures and tables are out of sequence because the hidden text captions are hidden.

hortonbillie say:

AFAIK that is how it works.

Check in new file and see if works.

jay freedman say:
Just marking figures/tables and their captions as hidden text does not change their automatically generated numbers. Look at the unhidden items in the text -- they'll still have their old numbers after other items are hidden. The tables of figures/tables are just reflecting the numbering in the text.

The only way to make this work as you want it to is to change some field codes. The numbers in captions are generated by SEQ fields. You need to prevent the SEQ fields in the hidden captions from going to the next number, so that the next unhidden caption will follow on from the previous unhidden one.

If there are only a few hidden captions, you can do this manually. First click the ¶ button so you can see the hidden text. In a hidden caption, right-click the number and click Toggle Field Codes. You'll see something like { SEQ Figure \* ARABIC } or { SEQ Table \* ARABIC }. Put the cursor before the backslash and type in  \c  followed by a space. The result should look like { SEQ Figure \c \* ARABIC }. The \c is a switch that tells the field "use the same number as the preceding SEQ field."

After you fix all the hidden captions this way, press Ctrl+A to select the whole document and press F9 to update all the fields -- that will renumber the unhidden items properly. Then update the tables of figures/tables, which will reflect the new numbering.

Of course, if you unhide one or more captions, you'll have to remove the \c from those SEQ fields to restore their numbers. That's too easy to forget!

The alternative is to write a macro (or have someone else write it for you) to insert/remove the \c according to whether each caption is hidden or unhidden, and then do the necessary updates.

ronaldo r. say:

Hi,

 

I understand that emails you received from your mobile device do not appear in your Outlook.com. Let me help you on this.

 

Since you are using a mobile device to access your emails, there’s a possibility that it was set to delete messages from the server after it was downloaded. Kindly check your Ipad settings and make sure to configure it to “leave messages on the server”.

 

As for your Outlook.com, you may set it to save downloaded messages. Here’s how:

  1. Sign in to your account.
  2. In the upper-right corner of the page, click wheel/cog icon, and then click More mail settings.
  3. Under Managing your account, click POP and deleting downloaded messages.
  4. Tick Don't let another program delete messages from Outlook.
  5. Click Save.

This will save downloaded emails to a special POP folder when you access your email account using a device or a mail client even if they were set to delete messages from the server.

 

I hope this helps. Should you have further questions, feel free to reply to this thread.

 

Thank you.
