Uninstalling and reinstalling Office will do nothing about blank documents. The cause of the blank documents is that the fonts available on your system have changed. The font that was used when the empty looking document was made is no longer the enabled font on your Mac, or the font has become corrupted.

Font problems can be resolved using a font utility such as Font Book. Use Font Book (it’s in Applications > Utilities folder) or other font utility to validate ALL your fonts. Delete old versions of duplicate fonts. Delete fonts that don't validate completely. Restart your Mac and empty the trash to get rid of the unneeded fonts and free up disc space.

The Excel problem has nothing to do with Microsoft Word. Please ask that question in the Excel forum. This forum is exclusive to Microsoft Word.

What you see in the screenshot is the Turabian format for academic papers. No doubt others in the academic community would be pleased to know if this could be done automatically! Thanks! - Stan

 

stefan blom say:

You will have to add the following two macros to your Normal template:

Sub InsertFootnote()
'Code by Dave Rado, modified by Stefan Blom
ActiveDocument.Footnotes.Add Range:=Selection.Range
With Selection
.Paragraphs(1).Range.Font.Reset
.Paragraphs(1).Range.Characters(2) = ""
.InsertAfter ". "
 .Collapse wdCollapseEnd
End With
ActiveWindow.ScrollIntoView Selection.Range
End Sub

Sub InsertFootnoteNow()
'Code by Dave Rado, modified by Stefan Blom
ActiveDocument.Footnotes.Add Range:=Selection.Range
With Selection
.Paragraphs(1).Range.Font.Reset
.Paragraphs(1).Range.Characters(2) = ""
.InsertAfter ". "
 .Collapse wdCollapseEnd
End With
ActiveWindow.ScrollIntoView Selection.Range
End Sub
