solar257 say:

The Zune software only reads the metadata embedded within your files. If something is missing, by default, the Zune software will try to augment your track metadata (this setting is within settings>software>metadata and ratings). Since your album art is "missing" the software looks at the other information within your tracks and tries to associate your tracks with album art from the Zune Marketplace. Album art isn't embedded to your music, just associated with it. If your album art is organized using the folder.jpg method you may want to look into embedding the album art into your files. This overwrites any associations the Zune software may have made. If you use the option to add album art within the Zune software your selections will not be embedded within the songs and just associated with the files.


Even though you're not interested in working on your albums individually it is what you will have to do if you want the album art you associated with your albums to show up in the Zune software. I had to do the same thing when I first used the Zune software, it stinks but in the long run it is more efficient. One program you may want to look at the Zune Social Tagger which can let you see which albums the software is associating with your tracks, https://github.com/leetreveil/Zune-Social-Tagger (look in the installer folder). Another is Mp3 Tag, http://www.mp3tag.de/en/index.html which helps with embedding album art. 

In this case I would recommend leaving the software settings alone as it is doing it's job in the absence of the information it can see.

marshall barton say:

An alternative to highlighting only the control with the focus or using Conditional Formatting on every control is to add a text box with BackColor set to whatever BackColor you use on the other controls.  Size this text box to cover the entire detail section and use Format - Send to Back to put it behind all the other controls.  Then select all the other controls and set their BackStyle to Transparent.

Next, add an invisible text box (named txtCurrent) to the form header or footer section (or even the detail section) and set its value in the form's Current event:
   Me.txtCurrent = Me.[your primary key field]

Now, you can use CF on the one big text box to highlight the entire row.  Use the Expression Is option with an expression like:
   [your primary key field] = [txtCurrent]
and selecting the back color of your choice.

a. user say:

Hi Oarneer,

I understand none of your DVD drives work after installing Windows 10 Technical Preview.

This issue is basically caused due to drivers.

  • What is the make and model of the computer?
  • When you open explorer, do you see the DVD Drive?

Windows will automatically load the drivers, but in some cases if the drivers are not found or if the current driver is corrupt, you might encounter this problem.

  • From the desktop press Windows flag key + R.
  • Type devmgmt.msc and click ok.
  • This will open device manager and check if you can find the DVD drive.

This will give you clear cut information about the cause of the problem.

IF this issue is caused due to drivers not available, it is recommended to visit the computer manufacturer website and download the drivers for the DVD drive.

Hope this information helps, for further assistance please reply.
