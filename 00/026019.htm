suzanne s. barnhill say:

In this situation, I'd go with the Odd Page Breaks. Assuming the page numbers are in the footer, you'll need to unlink the sections where you want a page number from the ones where you don't. Unless you're using roman numerals for the front matter, your pagination should be continuous throughout the document, so there should be no reason to define or restart the page numbering.

I assume the "title pages" are part titles. Even though they may not bear a folio, they should be included in the numbering. Treat them the same as the front matter: Odd Page Break to get to the title page, Odd Page Break to start the content after the title page, header/footer unlinked so as to omit page numbers.

If your page numbers are in the header, you'll want to omit them on the first page of a part/chapter, anyway, so if you enable Different First Page, you'll get a First Page Header/Footer that you can leave blank, and that will take care of the part title pages as well.
