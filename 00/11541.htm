Mail merge formula to determine whether "a" "an" or - Microsoft Community

addedvaluation say:

Mail merge formula to determine whether "a" "an" or "" should proceed a field.

We use mail merge to create reports which often contain lists of things.  We need this list to use correct grammar.  Often the issue will be choosing the use of the word "a" or "an".  Sometimes the field might need neither.  

Example:

Option 1: "an" - eg Can I please have "an" ice cream 

Option 2: "a" - eg Can I please have "a" chocolate bar

Option 3: "" - eg Can I please have "" two ice creams

So our document would contain the words: "Can I please have" just a regular text.  

Then we need a formula along the lines of {if "«Food.Wanted»" begins with letter a or e or i or o or u then insert "an", or if begins with the word one, two three ... ninety then "", otherwise insert "a"}

Then we would have the mail merge field «Food.Wanted»

eg Can I please have [formula needed] «Food.Wanted».

macropod say:

You could insert a series of six fields in the mailmerge main document after the 'a', coded as:

a{IF{MERGEFIELD Food.Wanted \* Lower}= "a*" "n"}{IF{MERGEFIELD Food.Wanted \* Lower}= "e*" "n"}{IF{MERGEFIELD Food.Wanted \* Lower}= "i*" "n"}{IF{MERGEFIELD Food.Wanted}= "o*" "n"}{IF{MERGEFIELD Food.Wanted \* Lower}= "u*" "n"}{IF{MERGEFIELD Food.Wanted \* Lower}= "h*" "n"}

These six fields all have the same construction: {IF{MERGEFIELD Food.Wanted \* Lower}= "a*" "n"}

When working with numerals, change the '\* Lower' switch to '\* Cardtext'.

Note: The field brace pairs (i.e. '{ }') for the above example are all created in the document itself, via Ctrl-F9 (Cmd-F9 on a Mac); you can't simply type them or copy & paste them from this message. Nor is it practicable to add them via any of the standard Word dialogues. The spaces represented in the field construction are all required.

hansv mvp say:

I suspect this would be difficult in Word itself. If the data source is a table in an Excel worksheet, I would recommend creating the formulas within that table.

For example, if Food.Wanted is in column B, insert a blank column in column C,  and enter a field name in C1, e.g. Food.Wanted2.

Enter the following formula in C2:

=IF(ISNUMBER(MATCH(IF(ISNUMBER(FIND(" ",B2)),LEFT(B2,FIND(" ",B2)-1),B2),{"one","two","three","four","five","six","seven","eight","nine","ten","eleven","twelve","thirteen","fourteen","fifteen","sixteen","seventeen","eighteen","nineteen","twenty","thirty","forty","fifty","sixty","seventy","eighty","ninety"},0)),"",IF(OR(LEFT(B2,1)={"a","e","i","o","u"}),"an ","a "))&B2

and fill down to the end of the data. Use the new field in your mail merge.
