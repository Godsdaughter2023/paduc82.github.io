Hi,

Thanks for posting your concern to Microsoft forum, Let me help you with this.

Generally it will open on the monitor it was closed on. Sometimes you can "train" a reluctant program by disabling the second monitor, and opening and closing the application on the 1st monitor a few times.

It's been my experience that most programs remember what monitor (screen position) they were last used on. If you close a program (open window) on your second monitor, it will open there the next time you launch it again. So I would suggest, move it to your main monitor before you close it, then see which monitor it opens on the next time you use it. 

You may try this:

1. Open the application.
2. Re-size the window so that it is not maximized or minimized.
3. Move the window to the monitor you want it to open on by default.
4. Close the application. Do not re-size prior to closing.
5. Open the application.
It should open on the monitor you just moved it to and closed it on.
6. Maximize the window.

Follow the steps below and check if the settings are correct in your PC.

Step 1: Region and Language settings.

To use Cortana, your region and language settings have to be aligned. Check the following list of regions where Cortana is available, and the corresponding language for each of those regions.

  • Cortana is available in these regions for these languages:
    • Australia: English
    • Canada: English
    • China: Chinese (Simplified)
    • France: French
    • Germany: German
    • India: English
    • Italy: Italian
    • Japan: Japanese
    • Spain: Spanish
    • United Kingdom: English
    • United States: English
  • Note: If you change your region, you might not be able to shop at the Store, or use things that you’ve purchased, such as memberships and subscriptions, games, films, TV and music.

Step 2: Setup

  • To use Cortana, all these settings have to be set to the same language:
    • Languages (this is your device language)
    • Speech language
    • Country or region
  • To confirm that your settings are correct or to make a change:
    1. Open Settings.  Select Time & language, then Region & language.
    2. Check the Language (set as default) setting for your Windows display language. To get a new language, select Add a Language, then choose one from the list. It may take a few minutes for the language pack to load. If you remove a language, you can always add it back later.
    3. Check the Country or region setting. Make sure that the country selected corresponds with the Windows Display language set in the Language setting.
    4. Go back to Settings > Time & language > Speech and make sure that the Speech language setting is aligned with the previous settings. If the language you want isn’t available, follow the steps on the next tab to add it.
    5. Sign out and then sign back in for the new settings to take effect.

Step 3: Language packs.

For some languages, you might need to manually download a speech pack.

  1. Go to Start and open Settings.
  2. Select Time & language > Region & language.
  3. Select the language that you want to add speech to, then select the Options button.
  4. Under Speech, select Download. It may take a minute or two for the speech pack to load.
  5. Sign out and then sign back in for the new speech pack to be added to speech options.
  6. Go back to Time & language, select your new language, then Set as Default.
  7. On Settings > Time & language > Speech, make sure that the Speech language setting is aligned with the previous settings.
  8. Sign out and then sign back in for the new settings to take effect.

The person who created the template may have incorrectly used a section break instead of taking advantage of the Different First Page setting for headers and footers. If the section break on the second page is a Next Page break, you may be able to remove the last page by changing the section start type to Continuous (on the Layout tab of the Page Setup dialog), but I've found that in such situations sometimes section breaks can be balky, and in any case the results might be unwanted (you might end up with the first-page footer on the second page). A better approach would be to delete the section break (if it is not needed) and set the template up properly; see http://wordfaqs.mvps.org/LetterheadRibbon.htm#MultiPage.

SUMPRODUCT N/A error - Microsoft Community

lizbeef say:

SUMPRODUCT N/A error

Hi I have used this sumproduct in cell AT1 and it is giving me the sorrect result. =SUMPRODUCT(--($A$10:$A$5000>=40360)*--($H$10:$H$5000={"OP","NOP"}))
However when I add *--($AT5:$AT5000="Y" and enter formula as =SUMPRODUCT(--($A$10:$A$5000>=40360)*--($H$10:$H$5000={"OP","NOP"})*--($AT$5:$AT$5000="Y")) I get #N/A.
As thias first formula gives the right result I think the error is something to do with AT but the only values I have there are Y's. What am I missing please?
jeeped say:
First, you can use double minuses coupled with commas or you can use asterisks as multipliers. You do not need both. Second, your formula uses rows 10:5000 for columns A and H, then 5:5000 for column AT. They all have to match.

Your formula should look something like,
=SUMPRODUCT(($A$10:$A$5000>=40360)*($H$10:$H$5000={"OP","NOP"})*($AT$10:$AT$5000="Y"))
... or,
=SUMPRODUCT(--($A$10:$A$5000>=40360),--($H$10:$H$5000={"OP","NOP"}),--($AT$10:$AT$5000="Y"))
... where all three criteria have matching sized ranges.

For what it is worth, the mismatched range sizes were causing the #N/A errors. The first thing noted was just redundant syntaxing.
