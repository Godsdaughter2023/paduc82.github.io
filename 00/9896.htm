ken sheridan say: It's not unusual when importing legacy data to then have to process it post-import into a form which is acceptable to Access.  Your case is a relatively simple example.  I'd suggest that you import the data into a holding table with a single column of text data type.  You can then insert rows into the final table, using the Split function to parse the string expression.  You can see how it would work in the immediate window:

Test$ = "20!0-1248808-9þ02999þHIGH VOLTAGE PROBE, HV40B"
MyArray = Split(Test$,"þ")
? MyArray(0)
20!0-1248808-9
? MyArray(1)
02999
? MyArray(2)
HIGH VOLTAGE PROBE, HV40B

You can easily undertake the import and the subsequent insertion of rows into the final table in a function, making the complete process a one-click task.

One thing to look out for is any rows which have inadvertently been delimited into two rather than three elements.  If this is a possibility it would raise a 'subscript out of range' error.  To cater for this I'd suggest that the function's code inserts the rows into the final table row by row by looping through a recordset of the holding table's rows.  You can then easily trap and ignore the error as the code builds the INSERT INTO statement.

An alternative to using an SQL INSERT INTO statement would be to use DAO to insert the rows into the final table.  Again you could easily handle any 'subscript out of range' error in the code.

Random Alarm Ringing

Since I have updated to Dev Preview WP8.1 update 1 on my Lumia 1020 it has been triggering the alarm sound at random. It happens once or twice a day at no specific time and is only switched off by activating the screen and then pressing a volume button. As mentioned, there is no set time this does it and it can happen once or twice, somedays even more. It even happens if there is no alarm set in alarms (in particular - it's not a snooze!) I am waiting for the Dev Preview Cyan to rollout to the 1020 and will monitor it then, anyone else noticed this at all?

a. user say:

Hello BryanW82:

Thank you for posting to the forum regarding the issues you are having with your phone alarm.  I have not found any other reports of this specific behavior.  There is certainly a chance this has something to do with the firmware.  As you do not have Cyan now, there are a couple other things to explore.

One thing to do is to review not just your alarms but your reminders and calendar events.  The notification sounds for these can sound like alarms.  If you do have reminders and events set, it may be a good idea to review the settings to see if any of them my be leading to these random sounds.

The next thing I would suggest you try is to manually set the time and date on your phone.  You can do this by going to settings > time + date > turn automatic setting to OFF > set the time, date and time zone.  Once this is done, reboot the phone and monitor.

Next, if you have any third party alarm apps on the phone it may be a good idea to uninstall them and monitor the phones behavior.  I have seen some other reports of various Cortana or alarm issues related to apps installed on the phone.

Finally, I would suggest you try a soft reset of the phone.  You can do this by going to settings > storage sense > phone > temporary files > delete > Internet Explorer settings > delete history.  Once this is done, press and hold the down volume button + the power button until the phone vibrates and reboots.

For anyone that has had this experience, please provide the details below.

  • Make and model of your phone.
  • Current OS version.
  • Current firmware revision.
  • For Nokia users, have you installed Cyan?
  • Country you are located in.
  • Regional setting of your phone.
  • Language setting of your phone.
  • Speech setting of your phone.

Please let us know if you have any other questions or concerns.

Mark W.
