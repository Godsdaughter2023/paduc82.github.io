First of all, when you see that message, the problem is very rarely with the hal.dll file in spite of what the message says.

The problem is usually your NT file system (NTFS) has somehow gotten corrupted and XP just can't find the file.

Or, your c:\boot.ini has gotten corrupted and XP just can't find the file where it is being told to look.

What you need to do first is tell us about your system, or you are going to quickly get off in the weeds following advice from people that may have good intentions, but have no idea what the problem is or how to properly fix it.

 52,563 HALMPS.DL_       152.576  (multiprocessor)
 37,780 HALSP.DL_     77,696
 
Each one of those can expand into a file called hal.dll file, so if it is your intent to replace your hal.dll, you would have to either figure out or know in advance which one is the right one for your hardware, motherboard and CPU.  If you just picked one at random, you would have a one in seven chance (14%) of getting the correct one.  Maybe you will get lucky.

If you just expand the hal.dl_ from the XP installation CD, that is the simplest, most generic of all the choices (probably not the right one).

The hal.dl_ file on the CD is the most boring of them all:

HAL.DL_

    * Standard PC


That is a good choice for some virtual machines where there is no real "hardware" (like VMWare), but it is a very poor choice for most other environments.

You system may in fact boot with it, but you will be missing a lot of functionality that is built in to the other hal.dll files.

For example, if I wanted to replace the hal.dll file on one system here, I would need to expand this file:

HALMACPI.DL_

    * Standard PC
    * Advanced Configuration and Power Interface (ACPI) PC
    * ACPI Multiprocessor
    * MPS Multiprocessor


If I wanted to replace the hal.dll file on another system here, I would need to expand this file:

HALAACPI.DL_

    * Standard PC
    * Advanced Configuration and Power Interface (ACPI) PC
    * ACPI Uniprocessor
    * ACPI Multiprocessor


One of those two are what you will probably find on most modern systems desktops/laptops.

If you check the Properties, Version, Internal Name of the hal.dll on a working system, you can see which kind of hal.dll file you have and it is probably not going to be just "hal.dll" (check yours and see).  It is either probably halmacpi.dll or halaacpi.dll.

bossdweeb say: "but windows insists that it has the most up to date driver applicable."

Windows could be right, but it ain't necessarily so.... ;).
If you are using Device manager to update the audio drivers, don't.

For HP's integrated audio you would want to download the driver for your model HP
from the HP web site downloads section.

h20565 audio drivers

If after installing the drivers you still have no sound verify it is enabled in -
Control Panel > Sound > Playback, Speakers (or headphones).
If Speakers is enabled and your 5.1 setup is verified functional then the audio chip may be bad.
Is there a warranty on the refurbish ?

The alternative would be a cheap sound card. You can get a decent sound card for as little
as $15- from on line retailers. Something a bit more flash (still not 'audiophile' level) for up to $45-.
Be careful not to get older sound cards if buying second hand as a lot of them don't have Win7 compatible drivers.
