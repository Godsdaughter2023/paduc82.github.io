ericlaw [msft edge] say:

Lately, Google Chrome maybe blocked for Microsoft.com site and Microsoft Download Center which has 599.4M monthly visitors, by the way.

This is a bug in Microsoft.com, not in Chrome. The problem is that the Microsoft website does not control the size of the cookies that it sends you, such that it often exceeds 16kb of cookie data (or slightly less. When slightly less, the size of the request URL and other headers becomes relevant). 


Microsoft has configured their frontend servers such that they automatically close the connection if they get a HTTP request that is over 16kb in size.


As a consequence, Microsoft is denying service to themselves. They need to do two things:

1> Limit the size of the cookies they send.

2> Not reject requests under 32kb or more.

For what it's worth, this has been a problem since 2006 at least. The scope of the problem ebbs and flows as Microsoft tweaks their cookies and as cookies naturally expire on client PCs, etc.
