This procedure will not affect your personal files.  It basically digs out a backup copy of the registry from the "System Volume Information" folder and replaces your current registry files with the backup copy.  Your keyboard works when you first start up because you are running under BIOS.  After Windows takes over from BIOS, your keyboard and mouse do not work (as I'm understanding it).  I find it a contradiction when you say XP works and starts but you have no mouse or keyboard control.  How else do you control Windows?

System Restore:  Start -> Programs -> Accessories -> System Tools -> System Restore.

If you are worried about your personal files then create a free Bart PE , Ultimate Boot CD for Windows , or Live Linux CD such asKnoppix , boot from that CD, then back up your personal files to a USB drive or Network Drive.  You would then be completely free to reinstall Windows if necessary without fearing losing personal data.  You must build the first two CDs ... the Knoppix disk is as simple as downloading a .iso file and burning it using a program that burns image files.

  JW

Transfer lincense

Ok so my sister played Batman Arkham city on my account and bought some DLC and redeemed some code for some preorder bonus dlc but recently bought her own console and made her own account. What I want to know is how can I transfer the DLCs to her account so it can be hers to use as I don't want it or need it. I want the DLC to be hers. a pretty straight forward question.

a. user say:

Unfortunately there isn't a straight forward answer.

DLC is licensed to two things. The account which first bought/redeemed it and the console it was first downloaded to. So in both cases the license belongs to you, your gamertag and your console.

Things can't be transferred between accounts, so the only way to make that content belong to your sister would be to buy it again with her gamertag.

You could always transfer your licenses to her console and download the content there, which would allow her to use it, but that would require that you always be online with your console in order to use the DLC with your profile.

tonythetiger831 say:

So if I move my account to her Xbox and download it there then she can freely use the DLC as long as she is online?

smwutches say:

If you move your account to her Xbox, your account would have to be signed in in order to be able to use the content unless you do a license transfer. However, if you were to do a license transfer you would only be able to use your content if your account is signed into Live on your console.

tonythetiger831 say:

So let me get this straight. Downloading the content through my account to her xbox doesn't let her use it unless I do a license transfer to her console which would only let her us it online?

I don't know if this would affect this but I downloaded this content through the Gears of War Xbox which I decided I didn't want and so now have a completely different xbox.

We don't care if she has to be online since she pretty much will always be connected to live.

a. user say:

The transfer cable is a separate issue to a license transfer.

-

As mentioned a license transfer moves the console license. What that one does is enable other profiles to use the content, online or offline. On other consoles only the purchasing account will be able to use the content, as long as the connection to Live is there.

-

So if your sister wants to use that content, without buying it again, this is what needs to be done. You will need to have your profile on her console and preform a license transfer (http://www.xbox.com/drm or under Account Management on your console.

-

That will give her the console license which lets other accounts use the DLC. You will then need to keep a connection to Live if you wish to use it on your 360.
