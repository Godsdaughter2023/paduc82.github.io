a. user say:

Windows 10 is a continuous series of updates.  Regular updates are one of the reasons it exists in the first place. That's why Microsoft goes to great lengths to ensure that its users receive those updates. If you have the Professional edition of Windows 10 you can delay updates - as I do - but Microsoft doesn't allow users to permanently stop receiving updates.

Many 'solutions' to stop receiving updates have been published here because Directly's 'independent advisors' will say anything to earn a poster's approval so they can collect their payout from Directly. But the fact is, taking steps to permanently stop updates has consequences now and in the future.

Microsoft didn't invent regular updates, they merely joined the bandwagon. All of Google's software updates automatically in the background, as does most cloud-based software like Adobe Creative Cloud. At least Microsoft lets us set Active Hours and delay receiving updates. Try and get that concession from Google.

The way to deal with Windows 10 updates is no different than it's been for preceding versions of Windows: Unless you're testing the software for your organization or you're a tech enthusiast, nobody needs a new update on the first day it comes out. One of the ways you can spot someone in this group who's clearly unprepared to deal with an update is that they want to have it now.

The correct way to deal with updates, since before Windows, is to wait a few months before installing so that early bugs, which are inevitable in software being used by 700+ million different configurations, can be identified and fixed. That's why the Professional edition of Windows is the one to own.
