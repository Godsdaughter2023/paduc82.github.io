hansv mvp say:

If you want the field to be equal to the value selected in the combo box, you can use

[Forms]![FormName]![combName]

in the Criteria line. If you want the field to be greater than the value selected in the combo box, use

>[Forms]![FormName]![combName]

Similar for >= (greater than or equal to), < (less than), <= (less than or equal to) and <> (not equal to).

If you have a text field and you want it to begin with the value selected in the combo box, use

Like [Forms]![FormName]![combName] & "*"

For ends with:

Like "*" & [Forms]![FormName]![combName]

And for contains:

Like "*" & [Forms]![FormName]![combName] & "*"

It doesn't really matter whether you start with a combo box or convert a text box to a combo box.

beth melton say:

It's important to only have one Word document open when you make changes to Word Options. Try exiting Word, restarting it, and make your change in Word Options without opening another document. After your change is made, exit Word, restart, and see if your change stuck.

Some background on what is happening: The last document window you close when you exit Word is the session that updates where the settings are stored in the Windows Registry. If you have two documents open, DocA and DocB, make your changes to Word Options in DocA, close DocA, and then close DocB, the settings being "held" in DocB are those that make the update. Any settings you changed in DocA were discarded since it was closed first.
