I am pleased to say I have found the answer to my query. Firstly replacing the hard coded location with a variable is flawed when used with the named range. This is because you are moving the pivot table from position A (named range) to Position B, doing your refresh and then moving back to position A (named range). It is flawed because after you move from position A to position B - the named range of position A is now that of position B. Therefore you cannot move it back to where you want it, because you have lost the co-ordinates of position A!

However with the offset condition this is no longer an issue. After many trial and errors I have finally found the correct construct and syntax:

ActiveSheet.PivotTables("PTable").Location = "'Summary'!A35"

becomes

ActiveSheet.PivotTables("PTSalary").Location = "'Summary'!" & Range(Range("lblNewPosition").Address).Offset(1, 0).Address

.. where lblNewPosition is the named range single cell maker position.

Silly me.
