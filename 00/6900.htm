based on our experience, when we manage photos in ad and sync thumbnailphoto attribute to office 365, sometimes, the photo might not display correctly in office 365, due to some unexpected factors.

upon researching, i find there are some limitations about the thumbnailphoto attribute, for example:

•the thumbnailphoto attribute is synced only one time between azure ad and exchange online. any later changes to the attribute from the on-premises environment are not synced to the exchange online mailbox.

for exchange online mailboxes, we need to directly set user photo in office 365. user photos are synchronized from active directory to the cloud only once during a mailbox migration. therefore, even in hybrid environments, don't try to manage user photos in active directory.

therefore, currently, it is recommended that we directly set user photo in office 365, even it is a dirsync environment.

end users can directly change photo by clicking the gear icon on the right top cornet of the portal -> office 365 settings.

admin can also change user photo in exchange admin center -> manage another user, or via exchange powershell command: set-userphoto "username" -picturedata ([system.io.file]::readallbytes("c:\test.jpg"))

note: once we change photo in office 365, the local ad photo will not be synced to office 365. office 365 photo has a higher priority. later, we can just manage the photo in office 365.

anything unclear, please let us know.

Would a macro solution be acceptable?  I have in mind a User Defined Function - that is a macro that you use just like a built-in function like SUMPRODUCT() or SUMIFS(). 

Basically for this, we're going to need to know:

What 2 rows your vertical criteria appear in, and what column your row criteria show up in.

Now, generally macros run more slowly than built-in functions, so there may be a performance hit with such a solution, but it would get you beyond your current lost-in-space situation.

Here is a link to a sample workbook showing how such a thing could work.  This looks for 2 matching text entries across the sheet in rows 1 and 2, and then for matching dates in column 1.  Result is the total of all values where these matches intersect.
