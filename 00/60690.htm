Hi,

Thank you for posting your query on Microsoft Community.

I understand your inconvenience. We will assist you with it.

 

What is system image?

A system image is an exact copy of a drive. By default, a system image includes the drives required for Windows to run. It also includes Windows and your system settings, programs, and files. You can use a system image to restore the contents of your computer if your hard disk or computer ever stops working. When you restore your computer from a system image, it's a complete restoration—you can't choose individual items to restore, and all of your current programs, system settings, and files are replaced with the contents of the system image.

What is system repair disc?

A Repair Disk is a bootable disk that is used to boot the computer if there is a problem and you are not able to boot into windows. The System Repair Disk can also be used to replace the windows installation with the System Image backup.

    I had a similar problem with a two album anniversary release of "The Sound of Music."  What I discerned was that WMP 12 reads the title of the CD from your drive, then goes online to the media data base to extract track information.  If the information in the media data base is incorrect (for whatever reason), you can drive yourself nuts trying to get the right stuff into your own library.  I tried everything, but here's what finally worked for me: go to the individual song files in your Explorer>UserName>My Music or wherever you store that particular album.  Change as needed the FILE NAMES and Titles of the songs THERE to match your CD.  Then reload your music library.  You should be good to go.

    WMP looks at the  Album and  Album artist fields. If these fields of two songs match, then they are considered to be on the same album. If they come from the same CD, that has been ripped with WMP, then WMP might also 'remember' that they were on the same album, regardless of those two fields. It may also be caused by the fact that the songs are located in the same folder on the hard drive.

    When clicking  Edit in  Find Album Info , you should also see the list of tracks for which album info has been found. That could be useful to determine if other songs will be affected, but I don't use Find Album Info myself, so I could be wrong here.
