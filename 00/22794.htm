Hi AlbertAimer,

I’d like to explain that Quarantine filter is at an organization level, it’s not feasible to directly turn it off for specific users. If you don’t want any incoming emails to be quarantined, I suggest you create a transport rule in EAC (Exchange admin center) to achieve that:

1. Login to EAC (https://outlook.office365.com/ecp/)

2. Click mail flow->rules

3. Click Add (+), select Bypass spam filtering...

4. Select The recipient...->is this person under Apply this rule if…, add your email address to the add box, then click OK

5. Click Save

Please note that a transport rule can only be created by an admin. If you aren’t an admin in your organization, let you admin help you to complete it.

Based on your description it sounds like problem is probably related to restoring the phone settings (including connectivity settings i.e. Access Points) between old and new phone firmware versions.

 

Since you did manage to get connected with EAP-TTLS after a hard reset (without restoring the old backup) I would suggest making sure that you still have all your important data backed up and then do another hard reset on the phone.

 

After a hard reset you could probably restore all other data (contacts, calender, messages etc.) to the phone but leave the "Settings" part of the backup unselected while choosing what to restore. Then try re creating your IAP's and other settings manually, which hopefully works now that you haven't restored your old settings made with the older firmware version.

 

Once you get your settings and IAP's set up again it's probably a good idea to take a new complete backup (including settings) so you don't have to go through the manual settings restoration again in case you need to reset your phone later on.
