steve rindsberg say:

First off, you might consider using the Insert Picture command instead of dragging pictures into PPT, particularly if you need to share the presentations with Windows PPT users.  Dragging the pictures into PPT can make them incompatible with Windows PPT versions.

As to the specific issue: you'll see the three icons in question IF you drag a picture onto a slide with a content or picture placeholder AND the picture is larger than will fit in the placeholder (based on how PPT determines the picture size).

Once the placeholder is "filled" with a picture or other content, any further pictures will come in as regular PPT shapes and will be treated differently; since there's no placeholder to size the pictures to, they come in at whatever PPT figures their natural size to be or in some cases, are sized to fill the slide in one dimension or the other.

As far as I know, JPGs normally don't contain any size information, so PPT is probably looking at the number of pixels in the image, dividing by (probably) 72dpi, the more or less standard default DPI for Things Mac, and using that as the natural size of the image (in inches).
