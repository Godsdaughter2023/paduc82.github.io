wunders say: I keep finding myself making too many assumptions.
First, I am assuming that your clock drift occurs during the time while the computer is running and not during the time that it is turned off (or immediately at start)?
Second,  I am assuming that when you executed the "w32tm  /resync" command above, that the clock did return to the correct time and no error was reported?
Third,  You say that your clock drifts 5 minutes or more but you do not tell us over what period of time this gain occurs.  Does it gain twice as much if you wait twice as long to check it?  Is the gain gradual or sudden?  Does it keep gaining or does it stop gaining once it gets ahead?

Under default conditions for a non-Domain connected computer, the time will only network-time update at power-up and then once a week.  If you're gaining too much too quickly, you may want to adjust how often your computer polls for time.  Domain computers must be more accurate and default to once per hour for a time sync.  You might want to readjust your W32tm settings to that of a Domain computer in order to resync your computer more often (SpecialPollInterval).  Here are two articles that describe the clock adjustments:

"How to synchronize the time with the Windows Time service in Windows XP"
  < http://support.microsoft.com/kb/307897 >
"Windows Time Services Tools and Settings"
  < http://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/cc773263%28v=ws.10%29.aspx >
