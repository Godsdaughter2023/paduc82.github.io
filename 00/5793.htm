Assuming that
- row 1 is used for headers in all sheets and that the codes start on row 2
- that the interstate codes occupy cells interstate!A$2:A$7 
- that the intrastate codes occupy cells intrastate!A$2:A$9 

you can try the following formulas for the resulting list of codes and rates in a new sheet.

In cell A2:
=IFERROR(IF(ROWS($2:2)<=ROWS(interstate!A$2:A$7),INDEX(interstate!A$2:A$7,ROWS($2:2)),
INDEX(intrastate!A$2:A$9,MIN(IF(COUNTIF(OFFSET(A$1,,,ROWS($2:2)),"="&intrastate!A$2:A$9)=0,
ROW(intrastate!A$2:A$9)-1,999999)))),"")

Note: This is an array formula that has to be confirmed with CTRL+SHIFT+ENTER rather than just ENTER

In cell B2: 
=IFERROR(VLOOKUP(A2,interstate!A:B,2,FALSE),"")

In cell C2:
=IFERROR(VLOOKUP(A2,intrastate!A:B,2,FALSE),"")

Copy cells A2:C2 down as far as needed.

Column A will now hold the codes, first the codes from the interstate sheet followed by the codes from the intrastate sheet that are not present in the interstate sheet.

Column B will hold the corresponding inter_rates or blanks where there are no inter_rate stated for the code.

Column C will hold the corresponding intra_rates or blanks where there are no intra_rate stated for the code.

  • Click OK.
  • Locate and select the backend database.
  • Click Open.
