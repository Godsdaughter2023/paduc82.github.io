Yes, you can rename your account back to your previous @Hotmail, @Live or @Outlook address. To do so, just complete the following steps:

Note: The following steps will currently only work if your previous account was @Hotmail, @Live or @Outlook ending in .com, .jp, .kr, es, .au, .ca, etc. If you are trying to rename back to a hotmail.com or live.com address please make sure to set your "Country/region" in your Microsoft account profile (click here) to "United States" or those domains will not show up in the steps below.

This rename process will not work if you previously renamed an @MSN.com account. There is no way to rename an @Outlook, @Hotmail or @Live account back to an @MSN.com account at this time.

  1. In your browser, navigate to https://account.live.com/ManageAssocIds
  2. Sign in with your new @outlook, @hotmail or @live account. (if you are already signed in with your outlook.com account this step will not apply)
  3. Under “Other email addresses” click the Remove link below your previous address.
  4. Click the Remove button to confirm the message.
  5. You should now see a message on the page stating, “You've removed an email address from your Microsoft account.”
  6. Navigate to https://account.live.com/ChangeId.aspx in your browser.
  7. Verify that “Create a new email address” is selected.
  8. In the New Microsoft account box, enter your previous address and select your previous domain (hotmail.com or live.c Note: You must enter your previous address or the process will fail.
  9. Select the Save button.

Now you can use your original @Hotmail, @Live.com or @Outlook account to sign into your Microsoft services as you did before renaming your account.
