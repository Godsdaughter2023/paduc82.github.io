jllatham say:

Assume the $30,000 is in A2

then the 0-5000 = 50% formula is

=IF(A2>5000,0.5*5000,0.5*A2)

the 5001-10000 = 55% formula is

=IF(A2<5001,0,MIN(A2-5000,5000)*0.55)

the 10001 - 15000 = 60% formula is

=IF(A2<10001,0,MIN(A2-15000,5000)*0.6)

the 15001-20000 = 65% formula is

=IF(A2<15001,0,MIN(A2-20000,5000)*0.65)

and the over 20000 = 70% formula becomes

=IF(A2<20000,0,(A2-20000)*0.7)

You can even put them all into a single cell:

=IF(A2>5000,0.5*5000,0.5*A2)+IF(A2<5001,0,MIN(A2-5000,5000)*0.55)+IF(A2<10001,0,MIN(A2-15000,5000)*0.6)+IF(A2<15001,0,MIN(A2-20000,5000)*0.65)+IF(A2<20000,0,(A2-20000)*0.7)

I'd be tempted to enter a table of Limits / percentages and then string a formula off of this - that way, you can amend percentages / bands with ease, and also provides clarity and transparency around the calculation itself.

In the version above, I've entered the lower limit's of each band, together with the % commission applicable (note I've dropped the 5001 - a pet hate of mine with commission type exercises (what happens to the dollar between $5,000 and $5,001?)). I've then entered a formula in column F to calculate the cumulative total commission earned by reaching the upper limit of each band (formula fairly straight forward, but can be seen on screenshot below).

The Commission formula is then:

=OFFSET($F$2,MATCH(A3,$D$3:$D$7,1)-1,0)+(A3-OFFSET($D$3,MATCH(A3,$D$3:$D$7,1)-1,0))*OFFSET($E$3,MATCH(A3,$D$3:$D$7,1)-1,0)

OFFSET($F$2,MATCH(A3,$D$3:$D$7,1)-1,0) looks at the sales value in A3 and returns the Maximum commission value from the table for the band immediately before the one hit - so a value of $7,500 dollars would look up the maximum value for the 0 - 5000 band.

(A3-OFFSET($D$3,MATCH(A3,$D$3:$D$7,1)-1,0)) gives the amount of additional sales value to have commission calculated (total sales value in A3 less the lower limit of the applicable commission band).

Finally, OFFSET($E$3,MATCH(A3,$D$3:$D$7,1)-1,0) looks up the applicable commission % for the 'additional' commission value.

Note the 'blank' grey line D2:F2, which needs to remain empty for the first part of the formula to calculate correctly.

Also, the company names and addresses are duplicating with each entry and I'd like to find the code that will eliminate that problem. I found a few codes online that are similar but I couldn't get any to work. Any help would be appreciated!

macropod say:

The data duplications are likely to be the result of inserting the mergefields multiple times.

The mergefield \b & \f switches don't work with other switches. Presumably you have conditional content to go with the # (e.g. 'Phone:'). In that case you could use a field coded along the lines of:

{IF{MERGEFIELD Phone}<> "" "Phone: {MERGEFIELD Phone \# "'('###') '###'-'####"}"}

Note: The field brace pairs (i.e. '{ }') for the above example are all created in the document itself, via Ctrl-F9 (Cmd-F9 on a Mac); you can't simply type them or copy & paste them from this message. Nor is it practical to add them via any of the standard Word dialogues. The spaces represented in the field construction are all required.

julie fka profjulie say: There's not a direct way to send a vcard to another phone.  I've worked out a couple of different ways to send my vCard but not necessarily directly from the phone.  If you want to send your own business card to someone (in vCard format), probably the best way to do is by creating a vCard on your computer and then uploading it to SkyDrive or Dropbox or any other cloud based service that will let you email a link to the card to anyone you want to send it to.  I also created a business card for myself, using Microsoft tag and my own picture and I use that with my phone.  Anyone who has bing vision on their phone, gets a nice vCard that can be imported directly into their contact listing when they use bing vision (which is a feature on every windows phone 7.5 device) to scan the picture.  To see how it works, you can use bing vision right now to scan my avatar and get my Answers forum contact vcard. There are some applications in the marketplace that can be used to send contact information via email or text message.  I've tried out several of them and the app I'm presently using is Forward Contacts, which works pretty well and lets me send phone numbers, email addresses and addresses of contacts (many of these applications don't send addresses).  There is another app, called Send vCard that sounds promising, but it doesn't really send an actual vCard - you can send a link to a vCard. ContactXchange is another app that works pretty well, and you might also check out My Contact Info, which includes a bar code that can be scanned, similar to my Microsoft tag vCard.
