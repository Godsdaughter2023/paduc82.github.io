david a matson say:

Greetings Fuze4Ry!  Welcome to the Windows Phone forums!

It sounds like you have selected at some earlier point to automatically resolve these conflicts with the settings that you have now, and also checked the box not "bother you again" with this question.

You can confirm this by editing the registry and navigating to this key on your Desktop PC:

HKEY_CURRENT_USER\Software\Microsoft\Windows CE Services\Partners\DeviceID\Services\Synchronization subkey Conflict Resolution.   If it's set to 0, then that is your problem.  Change it to a "1" and you should start receiving prompts when there is a conflict.

WARNING: Messing with the registry can cause huge problems if you make a mistake.  I would highly recommend backing-up your device and creating a restore point on your PC before attempting any Registry changes.  Undertake Registry editing at YOUR OWN RISK!
