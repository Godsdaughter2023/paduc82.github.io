cmdrkeene say:

You can back up your emails using one of several options, but you will need to do it using a desktop-based email program such as Windows Live Mail (free) or Microsoft Office Outlook ($99/year).

If you have Microsoft Office Outlook already, my suggestion would be to use that, because it makes archiving and transferring emails easier.  But you can still do it in the free Windows Live Mail too.

Basically download and install Windows Live Mail, then open it up.  It will walk you through adding your Internet Provider-provided email account and start downloading your lifetime of emails.  It might take a while.

When it's done, you can go to File > Export and save a copy of all your emails.  Later you can import them into your new account so all your emails are still in one place.

---

There is another option, depending on where you want to host your email in the future.  Microsoft's own Outlook.com is like an email program online.  Like an email program on your desktop, Outlook.com can connect to and pull emails from any Internet Provider's email account, and hold onto them for you.  Then it can make those emails available to all your devices (phone, PC, tablet) and keep them in sync.  And the bets part is that you don't need to even use an @outlook.com email address to do this if you don't want to, it's totally agnostic, like a desktop email program.

To try that out, go to Outlook.com and login with your Microsoft account (the one used to get into this website) and you can add an account and get started.

This is my recommendation, because it means your emails are safe online, even if your computer were to crash and burn :)

Solution for PC users: Does it work ?

The solution is as simple as installing the right drivers for it. Apple supplies these along with many other PC drivers on their OS X installation disc. Why? So when you install Windows on a Mac all Apple peripherals and devices will work.

So here is what you got to do:

  • Run to your nearest friend or colleague who owns a Mac.
  • Borrow his/her original installation DVD that came with that Mac.
  • Insert it on any PC with a DVD-ROM drive.
  • Copy/Paste to a thumb drive the file AppleODDInstaller.exe located at \Boot Camp\Drivers\Apple if you have Windows 32 bit or \Boot Camp\Drivers\Apple\x64 if you have the 64 bit version.
  • Run that file from the thumb drive on the PC you want to use your Apple MacBook Air Superdrive.
  • That’s it! You are done. Now that the drive is not only recognized but also powered and ready to burn.
