Hi sophie bel,

 

Once an account is renamed, the original Microsoft account becomes an Alias. But technically all data, inbox messages,contacts are still accessible using the new account. You could actually revert back to your desired email address and make it the primary email address. But first you have to delete or remove the ALIAS before you can rename the account your desired email address.

 

Please refer to the steps below:


Remove an alias:


1. Login your account using this link "https://account.live.com".

(You may use the old account name or new account when logging in)
2. Click "Add or change aliases".
3. Click "Remove" under the Alias you wish to remove.

 

Please note if you remove this alias, you won't be able to use it to send or receive email using that email address.


Renaming accounts:

 

1. Sign in to https://account.live.com
2. Click "Add or change aliases".
3. Click "Rename".

*you will be asked if you want to add an alias or rename your primary account.

4. Choose "Yes, rename my primary alias".

 

*You will have 2 options, "Create a new email address as your primary alias" or "Use an existing email address as your primary alias"

 

Create a new email allows you to rename your account to Hotmail, Live or Outlook.
Use an existing email address allows you to rename your account to other domains (Yahoo, Gmail, etc.)

 

5. Choose your desired option to rename your account.
6. Hit "Rename primary alias" to save.

Hi, the code modified is

 Private Sub Workbook_Open()
        Const sAPPLICATION As String = "Excel"
        Const sSECTION As String = "Invoice"
        Const sKEY As String = "Invoice_key"
        Const nDEFAULT As Long = 1&
        Dim nNumber As Long
       
        With ThisWorkbook.Sheets("Invoice")
            With .Range("B7")
                If IsEmpty(.Value) Then
                    .Value = Date
                    .NumberFormat = "dd mmm yyyy"
                End If
            End With
            With .Range("B8")
                If IsEmpty(.Value) Then
                    nNumber = GetSetting(sAPPLICATION, sSECTION, sKEY, nDEFAULT)
                    .NumberFormat = "@"
                    .Value = Format(nNumber, "0000")
                    SaveSetting sAPPLICATION, sSECTION, sKEY, nNumber + 1&
                End If
            End With
        End With
     End Sub
