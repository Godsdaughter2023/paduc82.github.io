what is the difference between for 2 pc's and for 1 user, 2 - Microsoft Community

gailhubbard say:

what is the difference between for 2 pc's and for 1 user, 2 pc's

the 2010 office for home and business says for 1 user, 2 pc's.  Prior office suites were for 2 pc's.  Is there a differnce?
dan at it associates say:

Office retail licensing hasn't changed for quite a while.  It's just difficult to convey the licensing terms in short expressions.

 

Office Home and Business (and Professional) 2010 is licensed to be concurrently installed on up to two "devices", the first device being the designated licensed device (used to be that was almost always a desktop, but people are using more laptops than ever so it can be a laptop), and the second device being the primary product user's portable (usually a laptop) computer.

 

Other users can use Office on the first device, which allows for it to be "the family computer" for example.  However, the installation on that second device must be only for use by the primary user.

 

The licensing language is "device" to accommodate things that are not desktops or laptops, but right now that's what people are mostly using.  If there was a tablet that had the power and storage to run Office, you could install Office on it and be covered by the license.

 

In reality, since the Microsoft Activation servers cannot know which device is primary and who is sitting at what keyboard and are they the primary user etc etc, the servers simply count activations.  So it still comes down to a retail license is allowed to be concurrently activated on two computers.

 

Other perks of the retail license still apply, meaning you are permitted to move the license from computer to another to accommodate a computer that crashes and burns, or just getting a new computer.

 

Read the terms here:  http://www.microsoft.com/download/en/details.aspx?displaylang=en&CTT=5&origin=HA101817777&id=13653
