ken blake say:

First, note that an upgrade from Vista to 10 is not possible. To put Windows 10 on your computer, you would have to do a clean installation, not an upgrade.

Second, the age of the computer is not the issue.  There are three issues: whether all your hardware is compatible with Windows 10, whether all needed Windows 10 drivers exist for your hardware (that's more likely to be an issue if it's a laptop than if it's a desktop), and whether your hardware will give you acceptable performance with Windows 10.

If not all hardware is compatible with Windows 10, and you want Window, you can almost certainly replace any incompatible hardware with newer devices.

Similarly, you can make hardware changes as needed to upgrade the computer's Windows 10 performance.

With both issues, it's likely that hardware upgrades will be needed.

Regarding your "should we" questions, if it were me, I would not want to do anything to an 11 year-old computer. I would just replace the entire computer with a new one. An eleven year old computer is an ancient one, and replacing pieces of it is likely to cost just as much as, or even more than a whole new computer. And after 11 years, components are likely to fail soon. And I wouldn't go to 7 or 8.1; in my view it's best to go to the current version, not an obsolete or obsolescent one.
