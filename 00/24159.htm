jeeped say:

... I need a little help with an IF/COUNTIF formula that worked in one worksheet but is no working in another worksheet ...  


Your use of the terms "WORK DATE (in Date format)" and "PUNCH DATA (in Time format)" led to believe that you were taking a date/time stamp and using a date or time number format to achieve the visual results that you expected to see while leaving the underlying values as the original date/time value. I reproduced your sample data by adding the dates and times together in column A and then parsed out true dates and true times in columns B and I.

          

The formula in B2 is,
=INT(A2)
... and the formula in J2 is,
=MOD(A2,1)
These formulas take the date/time value from A2 and create a date (i.e. integer) and a time (i.e. decimal) from the date/time (i.e. mixed number). With the true dates and times in place, the formulas you provided worked well.

Excel treats dates as 1 for every day past Dec 31, 1899. Time is a decimal portion of a day. Today happens to be 41022 and today at noon is 41022.5. Tomorrow at 8:00am will be 41023.333. Changing the displayed number to a date format like m/d/yy does not change the underlying value so when comparing one date/time with another (e.g. COUNTIF()) they will not match. Using INT() to strip out the time portion of a date/time allows one date to be directly compared to another.
