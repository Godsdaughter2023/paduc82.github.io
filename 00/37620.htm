a. user say:

I have almost everything checked and I don't get that message (but I'm at 1920x1200 pixels).  The thing is, you probably don't really need all of those items so given the size limitation, you will just need to identify the items you really need/want and uncheck the others until the message goes away.  Most of those items can be added as shortcuts to the desktop or to the All Programs menu so getting to the options is a click on Show desktop and a click on the option you want rather than a click on Start and a click on the option (still two clicks either way - more if you put the shortcuts in All Programs rather than the desktop).  If you're having trouble creating the appropriate shortcuts, just post back exactly what options you want as shortcuts and we'll see if we can assist you (if you even need that).

As far as what option you wanted to add, I have no idea and no way to find out.  You should only include what you're actually going to use frequently.  Remember, there are other ways of getting to all of these options so even if they aren't in the Startup Menu, you can still get to where you want to go (it just may take a little longer).

The only other choice is to upgrade your video card so it supports a higher resolution (assuming one is available that is compatible with the rest of your computer and its devices - especially the monitor and the motherboard - and also compatible with your version of Vista).  But that seems a high price to pay just to be able to see a few buttons in the Start Menu.

I hope this helps.

Good luck!

neilpzz say:

Windows 10 activation is registered against hardware and is only transferrable if it is a retail licence. You can only activate one device with one licence unless you are using a corporate licence for multiple devices. So you cannot activate more than one device with a single use licence at a time.

Having a digital licence linked to your Microsoft account makes activation of digital licences easier if you can transfer it, but it still is required to be a retail licence.

If you are using an app on the new Win 8 'modern' interface then only the app in focus stays running, the others stop until brought back to the foreground.  (unless you have two in the split-screen mode.)
