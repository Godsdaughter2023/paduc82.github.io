jeremystar say:
I must apologize to Adobe for implying that Lightroom's not running after my upgrade to Windows 10 was Adobe's problem.
What happened to me was that in the process of upgrading from Win 8.1 to Win 10, the update somehow changed ALL my data files to "Read Only" AND removed my User Permissions in ALL my data files.
The way to fix this for any data file including the Lightroom Catalog file is:

1. Right click on the data file and choose Properties.
2. On the General tab, check that Read Only is not checked and does not have a square black box in the check area.
3. Switch to the Security tab; wait a few seconds for the dialog box to fill.
4. In the upper box on the Security tab, select HomeUsers, then click Edit under the box.
5. Now look at the lower box, and if you see anything other than Allow Full Control for users, select the Full Control box so it is checked.
6. Then click Apply at the bottom of the box. Then click OK until you are out of the Properties boxes.

Hope this works for you too....

julie_s1 say:

Hello

Question 1: What can I do with all my credit on such account?

You can transfer your Skype Credit from one account to another. You may follow the steps on this link: How do I transfer Skype Credit from one Skype account to another Skype account?

Question 2: Who can access all my Skype history currently? It seems ridiculous I cannot unlink this from someone else's company MS account.

You are the only one who will be able to access the Skype history on your account, not unless your company knows the password of your account. If they have intentions in accessing the account, you will know it as they will try to change the password just to get access. So until your password is not changing, rest assured only you is accessing your account.

Question 3: Can a potential NEW Skype account that I would have to create, remain unlinked to a MS account?

Currently, when creating an account you will not to use an email address or phone number. This information will be used as your Microsoft account. If you have a Microsoft account that is not yet associated to any Skype account, you can simply use it to create a new account.
