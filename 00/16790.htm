If this is Office 2010 Home and Student with a stand-alone Access here is the possible fix.
 
Microsoft Office Home and Student 2010 by default will also install a trial of Office Professional 2010.  The actual Home and Student product programs, One Note, Word, Excel and PowerPoint will activate and function as purchased.  The issue is that you will be nagged by the trial software until you remove it.  The trial consists of Outlook,Access and Publisher 2010.
 
To uninstall the trial software installed with your retail H & S product, start your activated Word 2010 program.  Click File, Help and you should see a box that says Licensing Operations.  Click it.  You should see two items, purchase Professional Product or Remove Trial.  Click to Remove Trial. Answer the Yes to the "You are about to remove your trial product..." to remove the trial. Once completed ("Programs have been removed...") click OK to reboot the computer to finish the uninstall.  You should have the 4 programs left that were included in H & S.
 
Once you remove the trial software attempt to reinstall your Access 2010 using the product key for Access stand-alone.  If it runs you are home free.  If it fails you need to totally uninstall Office 2010, reboot the computer, install your Access software first then reinstall Office 2010 H & S but ensure that you remove the check mark prior to clicking Upgrade.
 
To prevent installing the trial or overwriting any of the programs that the trial has on the 2nd screen after entering the product key and the screen immediately after accepting the license terms you will receive a screen that has two boxes.  One is an Upgrade box and a slightly smaller one that says Customize.  Below that in about 6 point type is a checkbox, already checked, and the words �??Include a free trial of Microsoft Office Professional 2010.�?�  You must uncheck the checkmark or the installation will over install anything that is currently installed on the computer even if you check the Customize button and turn off the Outlook, Access or Publisher installation.
