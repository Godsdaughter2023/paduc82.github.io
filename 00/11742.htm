putr_slave say:

Hi Caidliche,

Happy to help. I recorded a macro to copy the information across from Sheet 1 to Sheet 2. Now (in my case) Ctrl + D saves about 150 keystrokes!

There are a couple of ways to record macro's in Excel, I enabled the 'Developer' tab in Options because all the functions are laid out and easily accessed from it.

Recording Macro's sounds like scary stuff, but it needn't be! Remember first to save your workbook as a 'macro-enabled' one by choosing the correct file type in the "Save As' dialog box.

Then simply think through the steps you need to highlight the information you need, select 'Ctrl + C' navigate to the sheet and location you need to place the data, highlight the cells and press 'Ctrl + V'. The idea being you are trying to record as few actions as possible to keep the macro code clean and simple.

Once you have a good idea of the steps you need to copy and paste the information you want and where - go ahead and press that 'Record' button, move through the steps from beginning to end and press 'Stop'. In some cases you need to click 'Use Relative References', but I didn't need to use that for this task. If you data copy and paste / format extends down 2400 rows, then click 'Use Relative References' to give the macro code the right directions (the coding goes on in the background, unless you want to lean some Visual Basic for simple things it isn't necessary - the recorder does it all for you!) There are some things you can't record but most fundamental data manipulation is easily recorded.

If I can do it, you can!

If you need more information than this you can PM me. There are HEAPS of resources on the internet but I admit I do have a lynda.com account. You could even scour YouTube for instructional videos because there are bound to be some.
