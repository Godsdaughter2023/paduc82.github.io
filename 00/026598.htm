a. user say:

When you change the orientation of section 2 to landscape and a different paper size, the blank sheet printed between sections 1 and 2 is landscape, which suggests that for most purposes the sheet is regarded as part of the following section. But from a double-sided printing point of view, that doesn't really make sense because you need it to be portrait (or you need the printer to be able to work it out - but I'm not sure the printer has enough information to be able to make the correct decisions).

Also, even with "all portrait" sections, if you are printing double-sided and you want to repeat print a single section, e.g. using s2, you get the wrong combinations of sides.

e.g. if you print the whole document, you get
sheet 1: section 1 pages 1 and 2
sheet 2: section 1 page 3 and the blank page
sheet 3: section 2 pages 1 and 2
sheet 4: section 2 page 3 and the blank sheet.

If you now reprint just "s2", you get
sheet a: the blank sheet and section 2 page 1
sheet b: section 2 pages 2 and 3

There is doubtless a way to work around all that but some of these things could usefully be a bit more straightforward IMO.
