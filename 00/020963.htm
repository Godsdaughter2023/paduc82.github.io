Admittedly, I'm a Windows 10 and Windows fanboy of 22 years duration. I've tried OsX and Linux, and I always come back to Windows. However, that's me. I'm also a Windows Insider, and we are a small percentage of the computing population.

Why am I laying all this out ? I upgraded to Windows 10 willingly during the Windows Insider program and coped well with the to-be-expected bugs. I'm still coping with them, because I enjoy this stuff. However, the vast majority of the computing population are not Windows insiders or tech enthusiasts. They upgrade because they buy a new computer, or become convinced somehow that they would like to upgrade or need to upgrade.

Some people are still running Windows XP, fer crying out loud. A lot of people LIKE Windows 7 and see no need to upgrade, ever. Some people like Windows 8 and see NO need to upgrade at this time.

Microsoft, gently nagging people with a small notification once a week or so is fine. Most people can tolerate that or turn it off. Forcibly downloading 3 gigabytes of data to upgrade to W10 and then eventually making it a recommended upgrade, imho, is too aggressive. The download is taking up their hard drive space without their explicit permission, and the recommended upgrade push is going to result in people getting pissed off when they can't surf the web or watch Netflix because of an upgrade that didn't go well. You see the results of flawed upgrades all the time on these forums, but we signed up for this annoyance. The vast majority of the computing public wants an annoyance-free computing experience, full stop.

I have probably wasted my time typing all this out, and I'm sure I'm going to be attacked for it. Microsoft, you CANNOT force people to upgrade. Some people will NEVER upgrade until their machine breaks and they don't wish to repair it, a la the Windows XP holdouts. 

Let the upgrade happen organically. For more commentary, read this and the comments: http://www.theregister.co.uk/2015/10/31/windows_10_recommended_upgrade/

My comments derive from wanting to help, not harm. Have a good day.
