oneoreleven say:

The battery icon in the top right of the screen indicates your charge.  If you have it plugged in and the battery icon is full (white), and the plug icon is no longer showing, then you are fully charged.  Battery length will vary based on your usage, phone settings, mail push settings, and the quality and configuration of your wireless carrier's network.   Usually the SmartPhone rule of thumb is to charge your phone every night.

I have a Samsung Focus and based on my usage patterns I can go 1.5 - 1.75 days without charging, but I charge every night.  I have three email accounts checking every fifteen minutes, minimal location and "find my phone" settings, screen brightness set to Low, and use Wi-Fi when available.  I average 1 hour of talk time per day. I frequently surf the web and send text messages.   I can generally watch about 175 minutes of streaming NetFlix video when on WiFi on 1 battery charge.

My opinion is that there is nothing abnormal about your 1 day charge.
