john rubdy say:

Hi,

When you plug two monitors into a desktop PC or one monitor into a laptop, Windows should automatically detect each new monitor and provide you with multiple display options. You can choose to:

  • Extend your displays. This spreads your desktop over both monitors and lets you drag items between the two screens. This is how most people use two monitors, and it's the default setting.

  • Duplicate your displays. This displays the same desktop on both monitors. For a laptop, this is the default setting. This is useful if you're giving a presentation with your laptop connected to a projector or large monitor.

  • Show your desktop on only one monitor. This is most commonly used with a laptop if you might want to keep your laptop screen blank after you connect to a large desktop monitor.

When using multiple touchscreen displays in Windows 8 the touch may not work properly and require calibration. Use the following steps to calibrate each touchscreen display independently:

  1. Press the Windows () key + C to open your Charms., then select Search.
  2. Tap or click Settings, and then type Tablet PC Settings.
  3. In the search results, select Tablet PC Settings.
  4. In the Tablet PC Settings dialog box, choose the Display you want to configure.
  5. Click Setup.
  6. Follow the on-screen instructions.
  7. Repeat steps 4-6 for each additional touchscreen display.

Hope this information helps.
