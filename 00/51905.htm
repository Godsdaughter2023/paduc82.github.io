Using remote desktop from anywhere - Microsoft Community

santiagohazaña say:

Using remote desktop from anywhere

Hi, i was wondering if i could connect via the remote desktop app for iPad to my windows PC from anywhere. I need to access my windows from the college with my ipad. At home I can connect with no problem via the same network, but I want to connect from other network. Is this possible? 

cmdrkeene say:

You absolutely can.  The easy way is to use a service designed for it, my favorite (free for personal use) is TeamViewer.  Just install it on your computer, and later you can remote-into your computer from any tablet/pc/phone.

The harder way is to use just the plain built-in Remote Desktop.  It works the same way, but when you are away from home and want to connect, you can't use your internal IP address (every home in America has a computer with an IP address of 192.168.1.10 for example).  You have to know your public IP address, the one your internet company assigns to you.  Then you go into your route settings and set it up to forward incoming remote desktop packets to your actual internal address.  After that, you can connect from anywhere unless your Internet provider company changes your IP address, which is known to happen often.  So my advise is to take the free, easy option above.
