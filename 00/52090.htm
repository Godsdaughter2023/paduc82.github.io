Is there an auto button to double space text? - Microsoft Community

tampotter say:

Is there an auto button to double space text?

Hi!  I am writing a manuscript that has to be submitted with the text double spaced.  So that I don't have to physically go through all 40,000 words and insert another space is there a button or function I can use to do this for me?
Your help is much appreciated, thank you!
Tam

john_barnett say: Hi Tam,

Writing a manuscript is the same as any published work, the additional space is required between 'lines' not letters.

You don't say which word processing application you are using to type your manuscript in. If you are using Microsoft Word then, Yes there is a button that will double space your lines for you.

If you are using Word 2007 I suggest you open your manuscript and then press CTRL + A (That's hold the CRTL button on your keyboard down and press A. This will select 'all' of your text. Next go to the Toolbar ribbon (make sure the Home tab is selected) and then click on the button that has lines on it and an up and down arrow (this is situated next to the Justify text button and the paint formatter (paint can)). Click on this and select 2.0 Your lines will now be double spaced throughout your manuscript.

If you are using Word 97 or 2003 then you will find the line spacing button on the formatting toolbar (that's the one with font, font size, bold, italic, on it.) Again the button is right next to the Justify button. The procedure for double spacing is the same as Word 2007.

remove entry from add/remove programs - Microsoft Community

qiloff say:

remove entry from add/remove programs

The uninstall utility might have entries which point to non existing programs (apps). Programs which were deleted from inside the program, but it's uninstall procedure did not clean out the registry entry. I have found no way of removing the offending entry with out editing the registry. The conventional place to search the registry for these errant entries is:

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Uninstall

I also found additional uninstall registry entries at:

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\WOW6432Node\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Uninstall

Use safe practices when editing the registry.
