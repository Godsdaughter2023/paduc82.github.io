After a lot of internet searching, I narrowed the issue down to the touchpad and SD card reader turning off / going into sleep mode. So it had to be something about the power management of the USB card reader and USB contollers. So I hunted for everything on my laptop that dealt with USB or power and this is what I came up with.

So here is what I did:

Went into 'Device Manager' and went into 'Human Interface Devices' and then into the Properties of the 'I2C HID Device'. The last tab at the top is 'Power Management'. I then UNCHECKED / UNTICKED the option 'Allow the computer to turn off this device to save power'. 

I also did this also for 'Human Interface Devices > USB Input Device',

and also 'Universal Serial Bus Controllers > Intel USB 3.0 eXtensible Host Controller - 1.0',

and also 'Universal Serial Bus Controllers > USB Root Hub'.

And now the lag/delay after 30sec of inactivity is gone from my touchpad/keyboard! It's been about a week now and no issues.

I am not an IT / Computer Science guy so have no idea what those devices/controllers do, but it has worked for me.  I also haven't tried to TICK/UNTICK the various combinations of those devices - I figured it works now, don't mess with it! I also have no idea what this will do to battery life, but so far I haven't noticed a difference in my SB's battery life (I usually can get 8-9 hours on battery and it seems to be the same after my workaround above).

PS - when I do check / tick those options again, the lag/delay comes back after 30 sec of inactivity. So obviously it has something to do with the power management. If anyone could explain this, would be awesome - am just curious.
