macropod say:

What you're after is not possible. The TOC entry format is controlled by the TOC Styles and you can't apply more than one shade to a Style. Although you could manually highlight or shade each entry, those highlights/shades would be lost next time the field was updated (e.g. by doing a Print Preview or by printing the document) - as you have discovered.

The only thing that would even come close to what you're after would be to apply highlighting (not shading) to the headings in the document. Even then, however, the highlighting wouldn't extend to the page # - it would stop where the heading stops.

macropod say:
The 'shading' can be applied via the Format button, then Border>Shading, on the dialog displayed in your post but, as Doug and I have already said, you can't apply more than one shade to a Style.
