Yes, that is a known "feature", aka

This problem was discussed on another question. At "random" times, the downloaded theme files are deleted again.  Forcing you to go through the whole redownload process again.  A bother.

I can think of 2 workarounds. One is to simply copy all of the theme files to a backup location. You can automate the copy back process by creating a DOS batch file to do the copies that you put on the desktop.

Another option that may or may not work is to change the Windows Access rights on the files.  I have not tested it myself.  There are 2 places you can do this.  Both are in the properties dialog on each file.  Turn on the "Read Only" attribute.  If that doesn't work, click on the Security tab and alter the access rights to "Read" for all of the IDs. (You will require admin userid and password to do this).  There are a couple of potential issues.  One is that your Windows ID may require update access to theme files for Office to work.  The other is that even with altered access rights, Windows may grant itself enough rights to still delete the files ...

See this chart "Compare Sync options for POP, IMAP & Exchange Accounts"

The time it's taking to copy depends on several factors. Is your drive full or fragmented. How much RAM do you have? I would say that 8 hrs is outside normal, but It's best not to stop this once it's started.

I always advise users to make a copy first so if anything goes wrong you have a file that was not damaged by the rebuild. Even though the rebuild makes a copy, I've seen it merge the copy with the original leaving you with a huge mess that will not rebuild. Don't expect the rebuild to be a miracle cure. Often all it does is allow you to open your Identity to recover your data. I never trust a rebuilt Identity. I always move the data into a new Identity for best results.

How to move your data to a new Identity
Rebuild Basics:

  • Do Not Rebuild Identities with Exchange and IMAP accounts. Delete the accounts (this deletes the cache files in Outlook). Do the rebuild then add back accounts. Better yet start over in a new Identity. If the rebuild does not work then you could sync unwanted changes or deletions to your server and lose data.
  • Make sure you have plenty of free space on your drive. Make sure that you have at least three times as much hard disk space available as the current size of your Outlook identity PLUS 10% free for virtual memory.
  • Rebuilding does not fix all issues. It can make some problems worse. Even though the Database Utility will make a backup,
    Tip: If your Identity will open, before you rebuild, check "On My Computer"Inbox and Sent folders to be sure they are under the 2GB limit.
