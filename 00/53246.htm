Hi

I know this is very late, but maybe someone can learn from this thread in the future....... Today I was asked to fix someone's laptop after they had formatted it and reinstalled Vista.  They did a backup prior to reformatting, but also could not restore any of the data for all the same reasons as in this thread.  I was shocked to find how many people have had the same problem with this, but I am one of those people who does not give up easily when it comes to troubleshooting PCs!  As the file is a VHD file, I knew there would be some other way to mount this drive without using the Backup and Restore centre..... I decided to go to my laptop which runs Windows 7.  If you right click on My Computer and click Manage this will take you into Computer Management.  Click on Disk Management in left hand pane.  then Click Action on the menu and click "Attach VHD".  Then browse to your external hdd where you did your backups and select the .VHD file.  This VHD will then be mounted as an additional drive on your computer.  Using Windows Explorer, browse the newly mounted drive and copy all the data you need!
