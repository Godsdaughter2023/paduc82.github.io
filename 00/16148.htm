OPPOSITE OF NETWORKDAYS - Microsoft Community

areohwhy say:

OPPOSITE OF NETWORKDAYS

Do we have functions that is opposite of NETWORKDAYS, like counting only the number of weekend in a range of date? Thanks.

hansv mvp say:

Let's say the start date is in A1 and the end date in B1.

In Excel 2010 or later:

 

=NETWORKDAYS.INTL(A1,B1,"1111100")

 

counts the number of Saturdays and Sundays in the date range from start date to end date.

In Excel 2007, and in Excel 2003 or earlier with the Analysis ToolPak installed:

 

=B1-A1-NETWORKDAYS(A1,B1)+1

 

Format the cell with the formula as General.

v_agarwal say:

Is it you are looking for. Formula at B2 is    =IF(MOD(A1,1)<0.5,INT(A1)+0.49,INT(A1)+0.99)   copy it down.

tom ogilvy say:

If you are only checking two conditions you don't need IFS.  You can just use IF

if((A2-Trunc(A2))>.5,result, other result)

This would check >.5 and <=.5

if you want three conditions, then you could use IFS

IFS((A2-Trunc(A2))>.5,Result,(A2-Trunc(A2))=.5,Result,(A2-Trunc(A2))<.5,Result)

although doing an exact equality check on a floating point number might not give you the results you expect.
