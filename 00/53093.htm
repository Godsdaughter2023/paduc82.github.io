It happens often that there is a disagreement between the account name and the name of the profile folder in C:\Users. I recommend you ignore the discrepancy. It is not normally visible anyway.

Here is a way to delete an unwanted user profile folder. Be very careful when using this method - it is irreversible:
1. Log on under an admin account.
2. Click the Start globe.
3. Type the three letters cmd into the Search box.
4. Press Ctrl+Shift+Enter
5. Click "Run as Administrator".
6. Type the following commands and press Enter after each:
cd  \Users
dir
(to see the list of profile names)
rd  /s  /q  "John Doe"
(to delete the "John Doe" user profile folder)

By the way - do you have a fully tested spare admin account? Few people think ahead this far and lots of them get into very serious trouble when the main admin account develops a password problem.

nuked ogre say:

Alright, there are a few things you can try for this. By the way

Green -- Ideal power

Orange -- Stby mode

Red -- Inadequate power or other fault

Off -- No power

A flashing orange light can occur if an accessory is connected and it is damaged. You said you were charging a controller. If you have another cable or another controller, try it and see if the light blinks again.

Unplug the Xbox from a surge protector and try going directly into a wall outlet when charging. Depending on where you live the neutral in your house may work better than the one in your surge protector as it is a more direct route.

It's almost certainly being caused by either a damaged charging cord, USB port, or controller. It could also be your power brick or surge protector. Very remote chance it could be fluctuating voltage in your house, but I doubt this unless you have problems with other electronic devices.

If all else fails try Xbox Support.

nikolisaltana say:

I would also suggest trying Nuked Ogre's troubleshooting. If all else fails it could possibly be that your rechargeable battery pack has seen better days. I have had the same problem in the past and after trial and error it turned out that the battery pack was past it and would no longer charge.

If you can get hold of another rechargeable battery pack that you could borrow from a friend or something, I would suggest trying the borrowed battery pack in your controller and see if you get the same problems with the charge lead. You will more than likely find that it is the pack that is at fault.

I hope I have been of some help and as Nuked Ogre said... If all else fails give the support team a call or email they will be more than happy to help.
