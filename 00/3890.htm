Counting occurrences outside of normal business hours or on the - Microsoft Community

davidsarnowski say:

Counting occurrences outside of normal business hours or on the weekend

I have a spreadshet that includes a list of date/times. I am trying to count how many of them occur before 9:00 AM, after 5:00 PM, or on the weekend.

Here is a link to my sheet on OneDrive: http://1drv.ms/1vgLuRB

I can get COUNTIF to work if I split the times out and use just one criteria (before 9:00 AM for example), =Countif(B:B,"<9:00 AM")

However, If I try to use COUNTIFS and do both before 9:00 AM or after 5:00 PM, I get a value of zero =COUNTIFS(B:B."<9:00 AM",B:B,">5:00 PM")

I also need to countif the date falls on a weekend. I know I can do that with WEEKDAY.

Can anyone help me put this into one formula, ideally using just column A:A with the date/time format together?

howard31 say:

Hi

The following solution will allow you to have the date & time in one column, but you'll need 2 extra columns with formulas (they can be hidden)

Just so you understand how the functions work I make the following suggestions (you can put the formulas where ever you feel more comfortable)

Your dates/times is in column A

In column B enter the following: (copy down)

=IF(OR(WEEKDAY(DATE(YEAR(A2),MONTH(A2),DAY(A2)))=7,WEEKDAY(DATE(YEAR(A2),MONTH(A2),DAY(A2)))=1),TRUE,FALSE)

This will result in True if the corresponding cell in column A is in weekend

In column C enter the following: (copy down)

=IF(OR(TIME(HOUR(A2),MINUTE(A2),0)<TIME(9,0,0),TIME(HOUR(A2),MINUTE(A2),0)>TIME(17,0,0)),TRUE,FALSE)

This will give True if corresponding cell is out of office hours

The function to give the final result is:

=COUNTIF(B:B,TRUE)+COUNTIF(C:C,TRUE)

I ended up with this that worked great!

=IF(OR(WEEKDAY(A2,2)>5,AND(WEEKDAY(A2,2)<6,TIME(HOUR(A2),MINUTE(A2),0)<TIME(9,0,0)),AND(WEEKDAY(A2,2)<6,TIME(HOUR(A2),MINUTE(A2),0)>TIME(17,0,0))),TRUE,FALSE)

Then did some counting from there and nailed it.

When using an HP Pavilion Elite (m9600t) desktop PC with Windows Vista Home Premium 64bit, and an HP Printer and/or HP Camera that both utilize HP Photosmart Essential 3.5 software: TO PREVENT the ATT Tilt from automatically trying to sync any pictures (from the Tilt to PC) and automatically bringing up the HP Photosmart Essential 3.5 software during the sync (which in my case would lock up my Tilt and PC) I used this following procedure:
