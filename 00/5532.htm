dale howard [mvp] say:

SimonPhillips7469 --

The answer is yes, but my assumption is that Saturdays and Sundays are considered nonworking days.  Based on that assumption, here is how to create the calendar you seek:

  1. Open the project in which you want the recurring staff calendar.
  2. Click the Project tab and then click the Change Working Time button in the Properties section of the Project ribbon.
  3. In the Change Working Time dialog, click the Create New Calendar button.
  4. In the Create New Base Calendar dialog, select the Create a New Base Calendar option and then enter an name for the calendar in the Name field, such as 5 On 5 Off, or something like that.
  5. Click the OK button.
  6. In the calendar grid at the top of the dialog, select the first day the next 5-day nonworking time period, such as November 17, for example.
  7. In the Exceptions grid at the bottom, enter a name for the nonworking time, such as Off Time or No Work, or something like that, and then press the Right-Arrow key on your computer keyboard.
  8. With that row still selected, click the Details button.
  9. In the Details dialog, select the Weekly option in the Recurrence Pattern section.
  10. Set the Recur

    Completing the preceding steps should create a calendar that meets your scheduling needs.  At this point, you would only need to specify this calendar as the Base Calendar for resources who will work the 5 days on, 5 days off work schedule.

    If this is not what you were looking for, then please give us more information about your scheduling needs, and we will do our best to help you.  Let us know.
