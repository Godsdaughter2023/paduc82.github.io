Hi Barry,

 

To enable the classic logon style, follow these steps:

 

a.     Login to your computer using an administrator account.

b.     Type “secpol.msc” in the Start Search box and press “Enter.”

c.     In the Local Security Policy window, navigate to “Local Policies\Security Options.”

d.     Double-click the policy “Interactive Logon: Do not display last user name" and change the setting to “Enable.”

e.     Double-click "Interactive Login: Do not require Ctrl+Alt+Del" and change the setting to “Disabled.”

f.      Type “gpupdate.exe” in the Start Search box to apply the policy.

 

However, please note that it is not recommended to use the administrator user account as your default user account. It’s advisable to use a standard account instead.

 

See: Why use a standard user account instead of an administrator account?

Sounds like you installed Windows 7 on the Recovery Partition.

Click Start, right click Computer
Click Manage
Expand Disk Management

Select the Recovery Partition
Right click it, click 'Format'

Once complete, the drive will be displayed as a ‘Logical Drive’ in Disk Management.

Right click the ‘Logical Drive’ in Disk Management, on the contextual menu, click the ‘Delete Volume’ option. Click ‘Yes’ when the warning dialog appears.

Right click the Free space partition, and click Delete, click Yes on the Disk Management warning that appears.

A wizard will now begin that will guide through the steps to merge back the unallocated space with your system partition.

The wizard provides a simple procedure to merge back the unallocated space with the system drive. Once you have selected the space, click Next, at the end of the wizard, you will see the amount allocated.

Custom Clean Install Steps 

1. Backup all your files and settings to avoid losing docs, photos, and other information. http://windows.microsoft.com/windows-easy-transfer

4. After Windows 7 is installed you can use Windows Easy Transfer (or another backup method you used) to restore your files & settings, and use your installation disks to reinstall all your programs. Then you're done - enjoy Windows 7.
