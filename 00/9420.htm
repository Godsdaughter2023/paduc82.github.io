jim gordon mvp say:
Hello Hannes,

Of course you can do this in Office. Microsoft Office has some sophisticated tools to help you with these tasks.

First lets talk about colors. You can make custom color Themes that are available in Word, Excel, and PowerPoint, so that you can have consistent colors within all the applications. There are many pages that cover these topics in the Microsoft Office 2011 All-In-One reference book, so here I will give a quick synopsis of what to do. Office Help also has documentation on these topics.

You make color Themes in PowerPoint. A theme consists of a set of colors and a font family. In PowerPoint, a theme also includes certain elements such as slide masters and slide design layouts. To make a Theme (see page 640 of the All-in-one):
1. Open PowerPoint to a new, blank presentation
2. On the Format menu choose Theme Colors
3. Change the colors as desired
4. Give your theme a name
5. Click the Apply to All button
6. Quit PowerPoint

The next time you open PowerPoint the color set you made will be available when you click the Themes tab of the Ribbon and click the Colors button in the Theme Options grouping.

Likewise, the color set you made will also be available in Theme colors in Word and Excel.

Moving on to the chart formatting.

Format your chart with the lines and shadows and things the way you like them to be. Then right-click on the border of the chart and choose Save As Template. Give the template a name and then save it. Your template will be available in the Ribbon. To apply your template to any chart, select the chart. Then on the Charts Tab of the Ribbon, in the Insert Charts group of buttons click the Other button. Your custom chart will be at the bottom of the pop-up menu. Click it to apply the saved template to your chart.

Neither one of these things was hard to do, was it?

If you need to distribute these customized files, you can save a Theme as a (.thmx) file, and you already know how to save a chart as a chart template file (.crtx) from the previous paragraph.
