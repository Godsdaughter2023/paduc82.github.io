vijay a. verma say:
Before proceeding with the troubleshooting steps, Connect your Printer to the computer and Switch it ON. In case of Wireless printer, Switch it ON and connect it to the Wifi network.

1. Run printer troubleshooter
Type Troubleshoot in Windows Start Search box > Click Troubleshoot > On the right side, Click Printer > Run the troubleshooter

2. Update Printer Driver
Download printer driver from printer manufacturer's website and reinstall.

3. Uninstall and reinstall the Printer (Credit to Andre Da Costa, MVP for providing steps)
A. Uninstall Printer
Click on Windows Key+X > Apps and Features > Scroll down and click Programs and Features > Select your printer > Select Uninstall
Type Printer in Windows Search box > Printers & Scanners > Select your printer > Remove device
Type Printer in Windows Search box > Click Printers & Scanners > Scroll down and click Print server properties under Related settings > Under Driver tab, look for your printer > Select it and click Remove button at the bottom

B. Install Printer
Go to the Printer manufacturer’s website and search for the latest Windows 10 drivers > Reinstall the Printer driver > Reboot the computer
Type Printer in Windows Start Search box > Click Printers & Scanners > On Right side, Click Add a printer or scanner > If Windows detects your printer, it will be listed > Select the printer and follow on screen directions to set it up (In case of Wifi printer, your computer should also be logged into Wifi network)
If Windows doesn't detect your printer, then you will get a blue message - Click The printer that I want isn't listed
If you are using Bluetooth / Wirelss printer > Select Add a Bluetooth, wireless or network discoverable printer > Choose the printer > Select your Printer and follow on screen directions
If you are using wired printer > Select Add a local printer or network printer with manual settings > Select Use an existing port > Select your Printer and follow on screen directions

4. Clear Spooler
Type Services in Windows Start Search Box > Click Services > Scroll down to Print Spooler > Right-click and choose Stop for the Print Spooler service > Go to C:\WINDOWS\System32\spool\PRINTERS. You can also type %windir%\System32\spool\PRINTERS into the address bar in Explorer if the C drive is not the default Windows partition > Delete all the files in this folder > Services console and right-click and choose Start for the Print Spooler service

(Source - https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/help/401540...)

Cluster Chunk - Microsoft Community

elitenoobforce say:

Cluster Chunk

Credits

 

We did not come up with the basic idea of Cluster Chunk since it was originally created for the PC. However, we added many mechanics to improve the game. The two primary creators of the map are Melonnade and EliteNoobForce. Other people who helped create the map were Impulsive Dream, Ha1oReach4ever, and VICCEN509. There is an extensive list of people who have test-played the map, but it would be too long to name here. The primary people to test the map, though, are the five people already mentioned. This map has been worked on since October 2012 when the creative mode update was released. It is constantly being updated and improved as Minecraft 360 is being updated.

Here is an overview of red's side of the map.

 

Basics

 

Our version of Cluster Chunk takes place within a gigantic, lava-filled arena. There are two teams: one red and the other blue. Each team starts out with one bed which every person on the team needs to sleep in. That way they will spawn inside the arena instead of the outside. The goal of each team is to collect the necessary materials to defeat the enemy team. In order to defeat an enemy player, their bed must be destroyed, and then the enemy player must be killed. When an enemy player spawns outside of the arena, they are out of the game. However, if someone dies and spawns inside the arena because their bed is not destroyed, they are still in the game. In other words, you can die an infinite number of times, but you are not out unless your bed is destroyed when you die.

 

Home Diamonds

 

The entire arena is divided in half by a cactus wall (the nature of the cactus wall will be discusses later). On one side is red team and on the other is blue. Home diamonds are where you first start the game and where your bed is first located. Each home diamond is made out of every block in the game (before the enchanting/brewing update). The home diamond is where your team will collect most of the materials for the game. The red and blue home diamonds are 93 blocks apart from one another.

 

Satellites

 

Surrounding the home diamonds are three smaller satellites. In order to reach these satellites, you need to build a bridge to them. Each satellite contains a chest with useful materials in it. Satellites are 30 blocks away from home diamonds.

 

Lapis Lazuli Satellite (Armory)

 

This satellite contains TNT, Bows, and Arrows. In each side of the satellite there are also melons which can be used for food. However, the lapis lazuli satellite is filled with spider webs. In order to get inside of it, you will need to destroy the webs with a sword. If you want, you can turn the string you collect from the webs into wool.

 

Redstone Satellite (Alchemy)

 

This satellite contains potions and bottles o’ enchanting. However, the redstone satellite is also filled with leaves. In order to get inside of it, you will need to destroy them. You can do this by punching the leaves with your fist or burning them. If you want, you can collect the leaves with shears and use them for building material.

 

Obsidian Satellite (Engineering)

 

This satellite contains wool, bricks, wood, and bookshelves. Since this satellite is made out of obsidian, you will need a diamond pickaxe to get inside.

 

Drops

 

There are three different types of drops that occur throughout the game. All three of these drops take place in the home diamonds.

 

Wool Drop

 

The first drop is called a wool drop. Every few seconds, a single piece of wool will fall into the home diamond.

 

Supply Drop

 

The second drop is called a supply drop. Every few minutes a chest will drop inside of the home diamond containing useful materials. In general, the drops get better as the game progresses. For example, the first drop contains food, shears, and coal while the last supply drop contains an ender pearl. Ender pearls are extremely useful in this game because they allow you to teleport to the enemy base safely and without alerting the enemy team.

 

Diamond/Iron Drop

 

The third and last drop is called a diamond/iron drop. Every time a supply drop falls, diamonds and iron will fall with it. The amount of iron and diamonds that fall increases as the number of players in the game increases.

 

Buying Items at the Shop

 

Located above the obsidian satellite there is a shop that operates using sand as currency. The shop contains six useful items (prices in parentheses): ender pearl (90), enchanted bow (60), enchanted sword (60), block of diamond (30), 3x bottles o’ enchanting (1), and web (1). Prices at the shop are steep so you will need to save up sand and spend it wisely if you want to buy an item like an ender pearl. The following list contains places where you can obtain sand:

 

1. There are four pieces of sand in each supply drop. There are ten supply drops for a total of 40 pieces of sand if you collect them from every supply drop.

 

2. At the back of the shop there are 20 free pieces of sand.

 

3. At the bottom of the lapis satellite there are 26 pieces of sand.

 

4. There are 17 pieces of sand in each mob cage. There are two mob cages for a total of 34 pieces of sand if you collect them from both cages. Mob cages are simple structures: one contains zombies, and the other contains skeletons. Fight the hostile mobs to earn the reward inside.

 

That’s a grand total of 120 pieces of sand to collect. Make sure you spend sand strategically!

 

Other Important Structures

 

Cactus Wall

 

As mentioned earlier, there is a cactus wall that divides the entire glass dome in half. It functions as a grace period indicator. Neither team can cross it until it falls. The wall is connected to a timer that will automatically destroy it after a certain amount of time. In other words, the grace period is over after the cactus wall falls.

 

End Stone Tunnels

 

The last important structures are the end stone tunnels. They are a quick and stealthy way to reach the enemy team. End stone tunnels can be found at the back of both the lapis lazuli satellite and the redstone satellite. In order to enter the tunnel, you need to spend 20 pieces of sand. After spending 20 pieces of sand, the door will only open temporarily. If you want to use the tunnels twice, then it will cost you 40 pieces of sand or 60 pieces if you use it three times…etc.

 

Secret Buttons

 

Not only is the obsidian satellite useful because it contains building materials, but it also contains three secret buttons in the back. Two of the buttons set off a huge wall of fire charges to burn down any bridge the enemy team is making. The third button sets of an extra supply drop—for the enemy team. Basically, you don’t want to press this button. You have to stop the enemy team from pressing it. However, you should press the enemy team’s extra supply drop button. In order to do this, you will need to either sneak in or fight your way inside the enemy’s obsidian satellite.

 

Tips

 

Prevent Falling

 

It is very easy to fall off the edge and into the pit of lava at the bottom of the arena. To prevent this from happening, you need to be very careful. For example, make bridges two blocks wide instead of one. Crouching helps when trying to collect blocks in the home diamond. However, collecting blocks in the home diamond will create a lot of holes that are easy to fall through. When collecting blocks on the bottom half of the home diamond, it is recommended that you replace them with building material like wool. Furthermore, don’t carry armor and weapons on you unless you have to. If you die with important materials, there is a good chance you won’t be able to get them back.

 

Enchanting

 

The redstone and lapis lazuli satellites are not only useful because they contain chests. They can also be mined to collect experience. Experience can then be used to enchant armor and weapons. Of course, you will need to make an enchantment table to do this.

 

Bed

 

You can make multiple beds and move them wherever you want in the arena. For example, you can put a bed inside the obsidian diamond so it is harder for the enemy team to reach and destroy.

 

Cactus Wall

 

Always check the cactus wall every once in a while to see if has fallen. If you don’t pay attention, the other team may attack without you being prepared.

 

Enemy Mobs

 

Because the game is played on easy mode, enemy mobs can spawn in the arena. Be especially careful for creepers so that they don’t blow up your home diamond and bridges.

 

TNT

 

Your home diamond is made out of a couple of TNT blocks. It may be wise to collect those TNT blocks so that the enemy team does not bring a flint and steel/redstone torches and blow them up.
