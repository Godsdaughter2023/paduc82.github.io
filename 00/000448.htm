shane devenshire say:

Hi,

If I just need this once I might try this:

Here are the steps.  Suppose you have Shane in A1 and 333 in A2 and you want Shane 333 in A3 as a formula, with Shane Bolded Black and 333 Bolded, Red, Italic.

1.  In cell A3 enter =A1&" "&A2 and widen the cell to the desired width and note that width.  Then delete the contents of A3.
2.  Change the column widths of F and G so that their combined width is the same as that which you noted in step 1.
3.  In F1 enter =A1 and bold it.
4.  In G1 enter =A2 and make it bold, italic and red.
5.  Select F1:G1 and choose Format, Cells, Borders and choose white as a color and click Inside and Outside.
6. Add the Camera tool to your toolbar - choose View, Toolbars, Customize, Commands tab, Tools Category, find the Camera tool (about 3/4 of the way down.  Drag and drop it on any toolbar.
7. With F1:G1 selected click the Camera tool
8.  Click near cell A3
9.  Hold down the Alt key and drag the picture to A3 - it should snap to grid and fit exactly into A3.
10. With the picture selected choose Format, Picture, Color and Lines, and set the Line color to No Line and if you want, the Fill color to None.

This picture is dynamic - if you change the content of A1 or A2 it will change, if you change the formatting of F1 or G1 it will reflect those changes.

Hi,

In Outlook for Mac, IMAP e-mails are cached locally and indeed remain on the computer even if you cannot connect (for off-line reading for instance).
If you are worried about confidentiality, make sure to log out of your account on the Mac. You can’t access the data from another user account. For even more privacy, use FileVault to make sure everything is encrypted.

I don’t believe there is a true “online only” mode (which is what you’d need).

As for the other question: IMAP us supposed to sync everything up between computers, so in essence, I’m not surprised e-mails sent from one computer appear as such (and are filed in the Sent folder) on the other computer too. I’m not quite sure what the problem, is there.

On the third point: there is a way but it leads to a LOT of problems. I would very strongly recommend simply using different user accounts on the Mac itself, each with its own Outlook identity. That’s how the software was designed to work and this is the only option that will avoid nasty indexing, etc issues.

What is the value that is displayed in the control FileFolder?

 

Also, some things that are wrong.

 

you can't use strFile & "_" & Now()

 

because NOW uses characters which cannot be used in a file name (i.e. forward slash - / and colon : )

So you need

 

strFile & "_" & Format(Now, "yyyy_mm_dd_hh_nn_ss")

 

as an example.
