Sub Remove_Duplicates_Special()
  Dim MyColumn As Long, r As Long
  Static Exclude As String
  Exclude = InputBox("Exclude duplicates that match:", "Remove_Duplicates_Special", Exclude)
  MyColumn = ActiveCell.Column
  For r = Cells(Rows.Count, MyColumn).End(xlUp).Row To 1 Step -1
    If Not Cells(r, MyColumn) Like Exclude Then
      If WorksheetFunction.CountIf(Columns(MyColumn), Cells(r, MyColumn)) > 1 Then
        Cells(r, MyColumn).ClearContents
      End If
    End If

End Sub

rohn007 say:

Starting with Office 2013, MS introduced a huge change to how they do licensing. 

With Office 2010 and earlier ALL licenses were associated to the hardware they were installed on. No user ID's involved. You could also use a tool to "extract" the Product Key from the computer.

Starting with Office 2013, switched to associating (some of) the license(s) to an email ID (rather than the hardware).  You provide the key once, it gets linked to your email account and you don't have to worry about it any more.  The email account is used to sign in to the (consumer) MyAccount page:

http://office.microsoft.com/myaccount

You do all of your license management from there. Install on new computers, share (365 Home) with other users, "deactivate" on a specific computer, etc

Help with Your MS Account â Manage MyAccount

http://windows.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/accounts

  • Learn more about My

     

    As part of the switch MS also "improved" the way product keys are stored.  Both in MyAccount and when you extract the key from the hard drive you only see the last 5 char of the key. So for people with one-time payment licenses, if they don't key track of their full product key they are totally out of luck when it comes time to reactivate Office.

    AFIK, ALL Office 2016 installations require an email account as part of the activation process. This is causing a lot of pain for computer support staff because they no longer can pre-install Office on computers before delivering them to users.  I do recall seeing one comment that said there was a way to install 2016 1-time-payment licenses without an email account but I have not done it myself an don't have the details on how to do it.
