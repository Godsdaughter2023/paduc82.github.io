There is no Excel supplied function but a formula can be built which works for right to left mid.

1. Assume the string is Yasser Lababidi.

2. A MID formula works left way and syntax is MID(Text, Starting Point from Left, Number of Characters)

Hence =MID(A1,3,2) will give ss.

3. Now, you want a right to left MID functionality. Hence, if starting point is 4 (from right) and number of characters is 3, your answer should be bab from Yasser Lababidi. Assuming you value is in A1, the formula syntax would be

=RIGHT(REPLACE(A1,LEN(A1)-Starting Point from Right +2,LEN(A1),""), Number of Characters)

Hence, your formula would become

=RIGHT(REPLACE(A1,LEN(A1)-4+2,LEN(A1),""),3)

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

Well, you can avoid them altogether if you select the target cell then press return/enter to paste. Keep in mind though, that they serve a purpose. It's an indicator that the information is still on the clipboard available to be pasted again. Using that method to paste clears the content from the clipboard.

Other than that, just about any action should make them go away (along with the content on the clipboard). Use of the esc key is one of the more common & works fine here. Also, double-clicking a cell [to go into Enter/Edit mode] should clear them as well, as should just selecting a cell & typing content.

If none of the above are working for you it suggests that you have a local issue. Make sure your installation of Office & OS X are fully updated Also, If Excel has been running for an extended period or if you haven't restarted your Mac recently gremlins like this are prone to come along.
