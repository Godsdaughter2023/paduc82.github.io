If the folders are empty after you rename them, then there appears to be some sort of permission issue on your machine or some 3 party application is blocking the ability to write to those folders (ex. anti-virus).  You could try launching your machine in selective startup mode and disable all 3 party services and startup applications from running.  After you do this, you could then go through the same steps again (rename the folders and then launch Access again)

 

To start your machine in selective startup mode and disable 3 party services / startup apps you can do the following:

1.       Click on your Start menu and type in msconfig.  Launch msconfig.exe.

2.      In the msconfig dialog, click on the Services tab and check the “Hide all Microsoft Services” box.  After checking this, click the “Disable All” button.  This will disable all of the non-Microsoft services.

3.      Click on the Startup tab and then click the “Disable All” button.  This will disable all of the startup application.

4.      Click Apply and Ok.

5.      It will then prompt you to reboot your machine.  Accept the restart.

6.      The machine should restart in selective startup mode.

 

Once in selective startup mode, go through the steps of renaming the folders again and launching Access.  Once Access starts test the help system and also check the folders to see if they were recreated with files in them.  Once you have tested this, reboot your machine in normal mode again (do this no matter if Access works or doesn’t work).

1.       To boot into normal mode, once again launch the msconfig dialog.

2.      In the msconfig dialog, click on the General tab and choose the Normal startup option.

3.      Reboot the machine.
