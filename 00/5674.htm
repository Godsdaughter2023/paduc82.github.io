Excel 2010 - Extract from Text String and Convert to Date Format - Microsoft Community

skydv1 ks say:

Excel 2010 - Extract from Text String and Convert to Date Format

Hello.  I have a text string, and need to extract and convert to date:  1998-05-21T00:00:00  I understand that dates in Excel are numbers and how to use them once I have the number, but can't figure out how to do the extraction and conversion.  I have no experience using macros, so am trying to do everything with the built-in functions.  Thank you.
ronrosenfeld say:

If the format is exactly as you show, then:

=DATEVALUE(LEFT(A1,10))+TIMEVALUE(RIGHT(A1,8))

If not, you can probably use the T to split the string.

By the way, although the above is more clear, because of Excel's default behavior, you can also use:

=LEFT(A1,10)+RIGHT(A1,8)

or even:

=--SUBSTITUTE(A1,"T"," ")

If you need only the date, then:

=--LEFT(A1,10)
