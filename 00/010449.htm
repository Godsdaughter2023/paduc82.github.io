bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
There are a number of variables that could be involved but the main underlying factor is that word processing employs a completely different technology than typewriting.

I have a feeling that what you're encountering has nothing to do with Margins or Footers. It sounds more like a matter of Paragraph Formatting referred to as Widow/Orphan Control. In the overwhelming majority of documents it is a desirable feature which keeps the first line of paragraphs from being stranded preceding a page break & prevents the last line of paragraphs from appearing alone at the start of a new page. The greater the Line Spacing the more exaggerated the effect. Variations in font/font size, Paragraph Spacing, the use of tables & graphics, etc. also have an impact on what results in being 'the bottom line' on any given page.

See if the Word Help information re Control pagination is useful. There are also some tips from MVP Suzanne Barnhill in the article entitled “Improving the bottom line”—tips for achieving more uniform page length

steve rindsberg say:

You might also try running this bit of VBA on a COPY of your original file:

Sub ManualUpdate()
    Dim oSl As Slide
    Dim oSh As Shape
    
    For Each oSl In ActivePresentation.Slides
        For Each oSh In oSl.Shapes
            If oSh.Type = msoLinkedOLEObject Then
                oSh.LinkFormat.AutoUpdate = ppUpdateOptionManual
            End If
        Next
    Next
End Sub
