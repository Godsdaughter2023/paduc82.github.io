That clarify's it. If your amounts are in column F of Sheet1 (which you stated in your original question) then you can array enter:

=INDEX(Sheet1!F1:F1000,MATCH('10062'!A10&"|"&'10062'!A2,Sheet1!A1:A1000&"|"&Sheet1!C1:C1000,0))

[Enter with CTRL + SHIFT + ENTER, not only with ENTER, otherwise you will only get a #VALUE!]

Of course you can omit the '10062'! if you enter this formula in Sheet '10062'. BTW: The "|" parts protect you against false hits like BS1910 and 4010062 ...

danielpatton say:

I have discovered a fix for this problem that appears to work for the Outlook Live service. Given both Office 365 users and Outlook Live now use the Exchange Server, you may find the solution works for you. 

Rather than some unforeseen technical issue, the issue with sending emails from certain programs (eg including Outlook for Mac 2011) appears to be the result of a convoluted security measure developed by the Outlook team. To curtail the spread of spam and junk mail, the team require you to "sometimes" validate your account on the exchange server for use with certain email programs. 

Step 1. Create a new identity in Outlook for Mac 2011 as the previous poster Arshad Khan suggested. 

Step 2. Add your Outlook account in Outlook for Mac as an IMAP account. None of your contacts, calendars, notes or tasks will sync over or be viewable by selecting tabs on the navigation pane in Outlook for Mac. 

Step 3. Attempt to send a new email using your Office 365 Outlook account, which has been added as an IMAP account. 

Step 4. You will receive a slightly different automated error message in Errors. Select "tools" on the Office ribbon to view the error in "Errors"

Step 5. In addition to the different error message, you should also receive the following auto-generated email from Outlook: 

"Hello Person,

To continue sending messages, please "sign In" and validate your Outlook.com account.

This helps us stop automated programs from sending junk email.

Thanks for your help and patience!

The Outlook.com Team" 

Step 6. Complete the procedure by logging into your Outlook 365 account  in your web browser. Close the web browser. 

Step 7. Return to your create identity. Try to send an email. You should be able to successfully send an email. 

Step 8. Navigate back to your original identity in Outlook for Mac. Delete the Outlook account. Remove the Outlook account. Add the account again as an Exchange account so you can sync all your contacts, calendars and tasks. 

Step 8. Attempt to send another email. You should now be able to successfully send emails. Make sure you are using the correct Application Password if you have two-step verification enabled on your Office 365 Account. Same applies to Microsoft Account users.
