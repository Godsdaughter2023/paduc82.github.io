jllatham say:

You can copyright pretty much anything that you create.  What terms you distribute it are up to you.  I'd simply put it out there and say that you can use it as you want, but (please) don't remove acknowledgement that I created it, and don't add cost to your product if you sell a product that it is added to because of it, and do not sell it by itself at all.

As we've noted so often in these forums, once you put an Excel workbook of any type out on the internet, it's pretty much up for grabs and no amount of protection that you can put on it in the way of Excel passwords or protection is going to keep anyone who puts forth just a little effort from "getting inside of it" and doing pretty much what they want to with it.

There comes a point where you simply have to have a little faith in humanity and simply trust that they will handle it in the fashion/with the terms you place on it.  If you're like most of us, we certainly don't have the resources or tools available to the likes of the RIAA, MPAA, Microsoft, IBM etc to try to enforce our terms of use.

Even packages such as Visual Suite that can be used to distribute products that may not be complete in themselves and depend on not-so-usual support like VB or C+ runtimes come with a stated license to distribute what are known as the redistributables.  In effect, Excel workbooks are the "redistributable" and there's no restriction on creating and distributing them.
