How do I freeze and unfreeze panes in Excel 2010

I like to know how to freeze panes at the toop of the excel table or unfreeze when you don't need it.
jeeped say:

Freezing some rows at the top of a worksheet or columns at the left of a worksheet is accomplished by first selecting a cell that is one of the following:

  • To freeze one or more rows at the top of a worksheet select either the entire row or a single cell in column A just below the row(s) you want to freeze
  • To freeze one or more columns at the left of your worksheet select either the entire column or a single cell in row 1 just to the right of the column(s) you want to freeze.
  • To freeze both one or more rows and one or more columns select a single cell just down and to the right of the rows and columns you want to freeze.

 

Now the instructions differ somewhat between Excel 97-2003 and Excel 2007-2010.

  • For Excel 97-2003, select Window, Freeze Panes.
  • For Excel 2007/2010, click the View tab. From the Window group click the Freeze Panes button and select the Freeze Panes command.
  • To unfreeze, retrace your steps and select the Unfreeze Panes command.

 

If you want to have rows or columns to repeat on sequential pages while printing you can:

  • In Excel 97-2003 click File, Page Setup and move to the Sheets tab.
  • In Excel 2007/2010 click the ribbon's PAge Setup tab and click Print Titles from the Page Setup group. On the Sheet tab of the Page Setup dialog, input some Rows to repeat at top, Columns to repeat at left or both.

 

More information at: Freeze or lock rows and columns (97-2003) and Freeze or lock rows and columns (2007/2010).

The outlook.com Messenger web interface works differently than the hotmail interface. It has removed some capabilites that were in hotmail and added a few new ones.

 

After you login to outlook.com, the system will then login to Messenger web. Unlike hotmail, you cannot sign-off Messenger web.

 

If you click on your name in the upper right corner you will see two options for status, Available and Invisibe. If both are greyed out then the system is still trying to login to Messenger web or was unable to successfully connect. These are the only two status options you can set.

 

Clicking on Aailable will set your status to "Online" to your Messenger friends. A green bar will also be displayed on the left of your profile picture next to your name to remind you that you are "Online" to your friends.

 

Clicking on Invisible will set your status to "Offline" to your Messenger friends. The green bar will be removed from your profile picture. You cannot use the Messenger web to set yourself invisible to some friends and available to others.

 

If you are Available (Online) and you do not interact with outlook.com for a length of time then the status your Messenger friends see will be changed from "Online" to "Away". Sorry, but I don't remember what the length of time is... it might be around 10 minutes or so.

 

To see the status of your Messenger friends you need to click on  the cartoon balloon next to the gear icon in the upper right corner. The Messaging pane will open and you will see a "Start new conversation" box and possibly see a ist of the friends you have chatted with in the recent past.

 

If the cartoon ballon has a number on the right, this means that there are that many messages that have been sent to you while you were either logged off from outlook.com or "Away".

 

To start a new chat session, click on the box and it will display a drop-down list of all your friends that are online. Or you can select a friend from the list below the box you have previously chatted with. Note that even if a friend is offline, you can still select him/her and send offline messages.

 

Under each friend's name will be the last message sent or received with that friend. If the message is in the same color as your outlook.com title bar, then this is a new message you received while logged off or away (relates to the number after the cartoon balloon).

 

Before each firends name in the list may be a green box. This box tells you that the friend is online. NOTE that when I use IE8 I cannot see this online indicator. When I use IE10 I can see the indicator. I don't know if my IE8 browser is malfunctioning or that iE8 does not support this feature. It may be due to IE8 does not provide full html5 support (what outlook.com is programmed in)  like IE9 and IE10 do. I have not tried Firefox or other browsers yet.

 

Another way to chat with a friend is to go to your "People" list and click on the contact you want to chat with. In the details pane you can then click on "Send a message" to open a chat (even if the contact is offline).

 

For additional information on chatting history and other info, fclick on this link to another post in this forum.

 

I hope this helps or answers your questions.
