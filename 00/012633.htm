chris marron say:

Ok I believe I have separately found a work around that appears to have resolved the issue. I will update this thread if I do not. 

For anyone else having this problem the workaround that we have found is as follows: 

Go to office.com and open sharepoint. 

Click the button at the top right corner the icon is your account picture, click the "My profile" button that appears under your name and e-mail address. 

Then click the blue update profile icon that appears in the box containing details about you. 

Having clicked this there is a small blue link that says "How can I change language and regional settings?" click this then click the blue "here" on the click here that appears below. 

Then press the three dots (...) to the right of details and select Language and Region from the drop down menu that appears. 

Under Region select the radio button for "Always use my personal settings" rather than the one that says "Always use regional settings defined by site administrators."

Let the page reload for a moment. 

Then select English (United Kingdom) as your locale and set your time zone. Make sure English (United Kingdom) is set as the first item on "My Display Languages". Go to the bottom and hit "Save all and close". Then wait a while. For me it seemed to only work from the next day when I came into work. It does display a warning that it may take some time for your settings to take effect.

scottgem say:

This is the advantage of a split form! The datasheet portion of the split form allows you to filter on any combination of fields. I call this datasheet filtering. Each column in datasheet portion has a drop down triangle. When you click the triangle you get options for filtering that column. The filters are cumulative, so for example, you could filter on State, then filter on cities within the state, then streets within the city. Thus you can drill down to as few records as you need to find the record you want.

No code is needed and the user has ultimate flexibility in filtering. Filtering options are also flexible. For example, if you have a text column, you can use Contains to search for a match within the field.
