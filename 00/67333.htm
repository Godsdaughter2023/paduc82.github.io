[dse]circlestrafe say:

1. In the Display settings, configure your Surface and external LCD to duplicate screens, rather than disable the Surface screen altogether.  This should return the Surface to it's default resolution when disconnecting the external display.  If this works, and you really don't want the Surface screen showing, connecting an external keyboard and flipping up the Surface keyboard to block/shut-off its display takes care of that.  Personally, I prefer a good external keyboard at my desk rather than the Surface keyboard, which I REALLY don't care for out in the field!

2. Go to Control Panel> Display>Change Display Settings and then make sure that the "Allow the screen to auto-rotate" is un-selected.  We've found that having auto-rotate enabled sometimes messes with icon placement (custom icon placement reverts to everything on the left of the screen) along with screen resolution and, this has happened on 2 of the hundreds of Surfaces our school district uses, upon disconnecting an external LCD or undocking, the Surface autos into Tablet Mode, requiring it to be disabled, as the switch option becomes no longer available. Of course, if you like the auto-rotate feature, this may be a last resort option for you.

You didn't say whether or not signing out and signing back into the Surface restores the correct resolution.  That may not be the ideal solution, but I'm guessing it may work, although the external monitor should be disconnected after you sign out.  Then sign back in, and you should be good to go.
