usasma say:

The STOP 0x124 error is an error that's discovered by your CPU.  Then CPU then reports it to Windows.
This is not necessarily a CPU problem - rather it's most likely a hardware problem of some sort.  But it can also be a compatbility issue (more common in newer OS's) - or it can be a number of other, less likely things.

The information passed by the CPU doesn't contain much in the way of readable information, so the diagnosis is primarily involved in ruling out different things.  This means removing programs and/or devices and/or using replacement parts to rule out different hardware/software scenarios.  Eventually you will stumble upon the problem part/software.

This is the best advice that I've seen on STOP 0x124 errors:  http://www.sevenforums.com/crash-lockup-debug-how/35349-stop-0x124-what-means-what-try.html

I would also research the maximum temps for your processor.  I recently read that the Phenom II is supposed to shut down on it's own at 62ºC (~143ºF) - but wasn't able to find the official AMD specifications.

Good luck!

I'm glad to help you with your concern on Windows Live Mail about transferring/forwarding emails on the other account. I suggest you create your Message rules in Live Mail. To do this follow the steps below.

5. On the 3rd option edit the rule description, for example for forwarding emails click on the underline words in the box and a pop up window will appear for you to choose the contact/email address you want to forward the emails.
6. On the last option you can enter the name of the rule you created.
7. Click Save.
