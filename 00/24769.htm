Delete

If you click on Delete and click Delete in the confirmation box, the selected contacts will be deleted.

Edit:

jay freedman say:

It shouldn't be difficult at all, unless there are some special circumstances.

In an ordinary document that's just text and graphics, you can select the pages' content (by dragging with the mouse, by using the down arrow key or the Page Down key while holding Shift, or by clicking at the beginning of the first page, scrolling down, and shift-clicking at the end of the last page), and then press the Delete key.

There are a few ways that text in a document can be protected. Most of them involve the Restrict Editing and Formatting pane, which opens when you click the Restrict Editing button on the Review tab of the ribbon. If you open that pane and there's a Stop Protection button at the bottom, click it. Once protection is turned off, you should be able to use the select-and-delete process.

If the document is displayed in Reading mode, you can't make any changes in it. If all you see at the top left of the Word window is the File, Tools, and View tabs, that's Reading mode. In that case, click the View tab and click Edit Document. That puts you into Page Layout view, and you can delete pages.

If none of the above works, it's possible the document is damaged in some way. You can try selecting everything in the document except for the paragraph mark at the end of the last paragraph, copying it to the clipboard, and pasting it into a new blank document; sometimes that leaves the damaged part behind and gives you a clean version.
