john - project say:

John

Yes, but it will take a little work. This question, or a variation thereof, has been asked many times in Project forums. There are at least two ways to do what you want, one is with a custom calendar and milestones while the other is with VBA.

For the non-VBA approach, do the following:

1. Create a custom calendar with Monday 8:00 AM, or whatever time the truck leaves, as the only work day.

2. For each item that must be on the shipment, add a "ship milestone" with the custom calendar as the Task Calendar

3. Make the "ship milestone" the successor of each part that must be shipped.

Unless I forgot something, that should do it.

If you're interested in a VBA approach, let me know. I have a macro already written that can be modified to meet your exact requirements. The VBA approach doesn't require any custom calendars or extra milestones. It detects specific tasks that you identify as parts to be shipped and delays their shipment to whatever day/time the user needs.

karl timmermans [outlook mvp 2012-2018] say:

Adding UDF's in Outlook is done by clicking on <All Fields> in a contact. The default group shown will be "User-defined fields in this item" which is somewhat of a misnomer depending on what actions you take when you click on the "New Button".

 

- any new field will automatically be added to the "User-defined fields in folder" group

- the UDF will only be added to a given contact item if a value is entered in that field

- deleting a field from the "user-defined fields in folder" group does not delete the field/value from any contact item that already has an assigned value to that field (those would become what is known as "orphans")
