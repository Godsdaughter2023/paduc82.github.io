mencken say: I don't think that you're going to have any luck booting Win8 in UEFI mode as long as that Win7 drive is:  (1) SATA; (2) using an MBR partition table; and (3) present at boot.  

I'd suggest booting with some type of Linux rescue disk (SystemRescueCD comes to mind) that can mount both the win7 and win8 drives and once booted with the liveCD/DVD. copying your most critical data over to the win8 disk..  (You might need to flip your firmware into BIOS/CSM-mode instead of UEFI to get the rescue/live-DVD to boot correctly .... that was the case for me due to some hinkiness with the way my nvidia card presents through the UEFI firmware.)

Once you have your critical data copied over, I'd suggest converting the Win7 disk from MBR to GPT.I'd back the whole drive up first, if possible, before conversion.  There are ways to convert the win7/MBR disk to GPT without data-loss, but I think they're best regarded as "high risk."  Once your data is backed up, it couldn't hurt to try them though. (see:  http://www.rodsbooks.com/gdisk/mbr2gpt.html for a general procedure using the GPT fdisk tools, which happen to be included in the SystemRescueCD liveCD.)

To get the system to boot at all in UEFI mode using the Win8 installation, that win7 disk will (from what I've seen) need to be removed or converted to GPT.  

And here's something different that I've never tried, because I'm working with a laptop ---  if you have an eSATA port, and an eSATA cable you could connect to the win7 disk; you might want to consider just attaching the win7 disk AFTER win 8 boots to get at your data.  (I think that the "problem" with SATA/MBR disks only occurs at boot-time, but I don't know for sure.)

Good luck!
