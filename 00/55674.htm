Why are my Administrator rights not detected? - Microsoft Community

ritam_hi say:

Why are my Administrator rights not detected?

I am the Administrator on my Windows 7 computer and the only user.  When trying to install other programs, it states the "Access Denied, insufficient rights".  How do I correct this if I am the "Administrator"?

lead3 say:

There are really two levels of administrator.

Your normal sign on in your administrator account gives you a certain level of privilege but not all.

This is done to prevent malware from running in your administrator account.

When programs are installed and they are written correctly for Windows 7, they get Trusted installer rights and usually only require your approval, as administrator, to complete the install.

If they are not written correctly and you trust them,you must manually raise the level of privilege to complete the install.

 You do this by Running the installer as administrator, that is by right clicking on the install .exe and selecting "Run as Administrator". This raises the level of permission.
