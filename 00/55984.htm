Hello Dennis,

The VIDEO_TDR_ ERROR indicates that an attempt to reset the display driver and recover from a timeout failed.

TDR is theTimeout, Detection, and Recovery component in Windows.What happens is the Graphics Display driver for the installed graphics card stops responding. When this happens, windows will stop and restart the driver to fix the problem.

The most common reason for this issue is that the graphics device is being overloaded or used beyond its capabilities.

This happens most often when playing graphic intensive games, but can happen with any process that uses a large amount of graphic resources, such as when editing or creating videos.

raymond stone say:

I had the same big blue question mark on several of my machines and know that the bonjour service is not installed on any of them -- all clean installs of Win7, Win2k8R2 and Win7Home.  The only thing they had in common was RealTek Network cards (or at least thats how they're marked in device manager).

If your NIC is a RealTek PCie or similar you might try going into device manager, and clicking on the advanced tab of the RealTek network device.  There you should be able to browse down to an entry labled "Priority & VLAN".  If it is enabled, try setting it to VLAN Enabled.  I was able to make this fix on 3 of my machines with RealTek NICs and it seems that the network mapping problems have gone away.

[I'm not saying the bonjour service isn't causing a ? mark for others but I had it without bonjour on my machines.  The above fix eliminated the ? from all maps on all machines in my network.]
