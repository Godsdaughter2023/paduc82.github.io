ronbarker say:

The idea of having an Office Account was introduced with Office 2013 with its various software most notably Office Home and Student 2013 and Office Home and business 2013.  This method continued with Office 2016 and Office 365.  There are a number of differences between Office one-time-payment and Office using the subscription service.  I think that you are referring to Office 2016/2016 one-time-payment version of Office.  Previous version of Office do not have an Office Account, that included Office 2010.

When a person  purchases Office 2013/2016 on-time-payment that are provided with a Product Key which really should be called a Redemption Key, because that is what it is.  It is not the Product Key. The Product Key can be obtained from the Office Account.

The purchaser uses the 25 character Product Key/Redemption Key to set up their Office at: http://www.office.com/setup  The purchaser is practically forced to use the email address and password to setup their Office. In the early days of Office 2013 this was not the case and it caused big problems because failing to use the email address and password of a Microsoft Account BELONING to the user will not create an Office Account.  Quite a lot of vendors install Office for the purchaser using their email address and password for one of their Microsoft Accounts.  This means the user can no longer access the Office Account and cannot reinstall Office.

When the purchaser enters the 25 character Key when setting up Office, that key is transferred to the newly created Office Account.  That key can no longer be used to install Office on that computer.  NB: that 25 character key is proof of purchase and should be kept safe.

The  licence can be transferred to a new computer but first the Office on the old computer must be uninstalled first.  If Office is not uninstalled from the old computer the licence cannot be transferred to the new computer and the user need to go to the Microsoft Help desk for them to deactivate Office on their old computer.

Once Office is uninstalled from the old computer the user can reinstall office on their new computer from their Office account at: https://www.office.com/Myaccount  (they need to sign in with the email address and password for the Microsoft Account that they used when they first setup Office.

NB:  The problem that you have is caused mostly by vendors installing OEM version of Office which cannot be transferred to another device, or they install the retail version using their Microsoft Account, which locks the user/purchaser out of the Office Account.  This can only be sorted out by Microsoft help desk and they will not help if the person does not have the 25 character Product Key/Redemption Key and they may also need to provide other form of proof, emails from vendor etc.

jay freedman say:

Desiree, please don't send people to TechNet for questions like this.

Andrei, Word 2010 cannot automatically convert PDF files to Word documents. That feature was first introduced in Word 2013. There is no update to Word 2010 to make the feature available there. 

In Word 2013 and 2016, when a client chooses to open a PDF file, the PDF text converter runs automatically (provided that the Advanced option "Confirm file format conversion at open" is not checked).

If the PDF file was created from text (i.e., not a graphic format such as a scan), the result will be an editable Word document. This is called "reflow" in that the page layout is the same as if the document was created within Word; it won't necessarily maintain the same line and page breaks as in the PDF.

If the PDF was created directly from a scan or graphic image, Word will not be able to make an editable document from it. The result will be a Word document containing only images.
