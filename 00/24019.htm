Excel, Word, and all the office apps are individual automation servers.  In automation terminology, it has nothing to do with Centralized computers providing Exchange mail services or database services.  In an automation situation you have the server and the client.  If you write a script in Excel to manipulate Word, then Excel is the client and Word is the automation server. 

 

don't get the terminology confused with networks of computers - it is all happening on your PC

 

go to the run box in Windows Start, then type in

If you uninstall office and reinstall it, that may be more robust in terms of registering the application - I can't say. But i would try reregistering it first.

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

In Windows Explorer, click on the Organize drop down and then on Folder and search options and in the Folder Options dialog, go to the View tab and select the item "Show hidden files, folders, and drives".  While there, it is a good idea to uncheck the box of "Hide extensions for known file types".

Then, navigate to:

C:\Users\[User Name]\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Word\STARTUP

where in place of [User Name] you would select your log on name under the C:\Users folder.

You should find the AVWiz14s.dotm file in the STARTUP folder. 

Note: You will have to shut down Word to be able to delete it.

how do I import apple contacts into hotmail/outlook.com - Microsoft Community

meftab say:

how do I import apple contacts into hotmail/outlook.com

I have a macbook and it comes with "contacts" that is my address book. How do I import them into outlook.com?

a. user say:

Hi meftab,

 

We appreciate your time for posting here at Microsoft Community. Let me assist you with your concern about importing your apple contacts to Outlook.com.

 

We recognized that the file type of the contacts in your Mac address book when you export it is different to the file type that Outlook.com supports. Currently, Outlook.com supports .csv file. To be able to import your contacts to Outlook.com, you have to export them as a .csv file.

 

If ever you have successfully exported your contacts from your Mac address book into a .csv file, you may follow the steps below on how to import them on Outlook.com.

  1. Sign in to your account on Outlook.com.
  2. Click on the v beside the Outlook.com logo at the top left corner of your Outlook.com page.
  3. Then select People.
  4. Under Add people on your contact list, click on Import from file.
  5. Follow the steps provided on the page.
  6. Click Import contacts.

Let us know if you have any questions or clarifications.

 

Thank you.
