hansv mvp say:

You're already sorting on column K. Did you mean another column, let's say L? If so, you can do it like this:

 

    wsh2.Range("C1").CurrentRegion.Sort _
        Key1:=wsh2.Range("L1"), Order1:=xlAscending, _
        Header:=xlYes
    wsh2.Range("C1").CurrentRegion.Sort _
        Key1:=wsh2.Range("C1"), Order1:=xlAscending, _
        Key2:=wsh2.Range("D1"), Order2:=xlAscending, _
        Key3:=wsh2.Range("K1"), Order3:=xlDescending, _
        Header:=xlYes

 

Alternatively, you can use the new Sort object:

 

    With wsh2.Sort
        .SortFields.Clear
        .SortFields.Add Key:=wsh2.Range("C1")
        .SortFields.Add Key:=wsh2.Range("D1")
        .SortFields.Add Key:=wsh2.Range("K1"), Order:=xlDescending
        .SortFields.Add Key:=wsh2.Range("L1")
        .SetRange wsh2.Range("C1").CurrentRegion
        .Header = xlYes
        .Apply
    End With

 

(This won't work in Excel 2003 or earlier)
