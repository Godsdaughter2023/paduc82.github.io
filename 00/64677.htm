jjjjwn say: Hi,

Turns out this issue is quite common with notebook users. I have found a fix for this:
  1. Press the Windows key + R simultaneously to launch Run.
  2.  Type in regedit and press Enter to launch the Registry Editor.
  3. Press Ctrl + F simultaneously to open the Find dialog box.
  4. In the Find dialog box, type in this: Display1_DownScalingSupported then click on Find Next button and Windows will automatically search for you the registry key and high light it for you.
  5. Click on the high lighted key (Display1_DownScalingSupported) and change the Value from 0 to 1.
  6. Once finished, exit the Registry Edit, restart your PC.
  7. After you've restarted, go to your Desktop, right mouse click, choose Screen Resolution and you should be able to choose 1024x768. Please choose this option, save and close.

mafubu say:

This also affected every PC I've updated to the Fall Creator's Update. What I discovered was that for "convenience" Microsoft changed a particular setting under your sign in options.

Open the Settings app and go to Accounts > Sign-in Options. Scroll to the bottom until you reach the Privacy section and UNSELECT the option to "Use my sign-in info to automatically finish setting up my device after an update or restart." Do this for EVERY account on your PC.

This should resolve the issue, or at least until another Windows update comes along and resets it again. Either way, spread the word and I hope this was helpful.
