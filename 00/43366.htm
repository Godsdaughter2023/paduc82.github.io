bossdweeb say: I'll elaborate, if I may,  on the term, Libraries
(not moving the My Pictures, etc folders to a new location )

- The Libraries are really only a collection of short-cuts to similar folder/file types (or
anything you wish to access from that particular Library),  that may be in different locations
on, or off, your machine. - ie - My Documents, D\: ,  or an external drive. 
- They take up very little HDD space.  While a particular Library may show  XX GB's  that is
only the size of the actual folder/s, not the Library itself.

- You can alter or delete the 'real' files from folders via a Library, but if you delete the whole
library folder or remove a 'location' from the Libraries Window you only delete that Library, not the folders/files that were  listed in that Library. They will still be in their original location.

- You can, as the article linked by Julia will describe, move your User \ My Documents, My Pictures
etc. to a different drive though. 

btw -
- If you don't use external drives the Libraries are not really much different to adding a folder
to the desktop and popping a bunch of short-cuts into it.
 - Another alternative to Libraries is to add a short-cut to a  'real' folder, such as
your User \ My Documents or My Photos, in the Task Bar Windows Explorer icon
(R/click on the icon), by dragging and dropping the short-cut to the Windows Explorer icon.
This only works with folders and the root of drive partitions ( C:\ , D:\ , etc.), not executables.

d3ath prone say:

Hello TheSliceHouse! What model Xbox 360 do you have? If it is the Original Xbox 360 and it overheats the ring of light should flash 2 red lights like this

 

 

If you are getting one or more flashing red lights on the console, please use the Flashing Lights Wizard.

 

If it's the New model Xbox 360 S it should flash the center light on the power button red.

 

From what the issue sounds like, I believe you are having an issue with the power brick overheating. When the power brick is in standby mode (console is powered off) the light on the power brick should be orange. When the console is powered up normally the light on the power brick should be Green. If there is a fault in the power brick ( when the xbox just turns off on it's own with no warning) the light on the power brick should turn red.

 

Make sure the power brick is in an open area where the vents are not obstructed as there is a fan inside to keep it cool.

 

Play the console until it shuts off than take a look at the light on the power brick. If it's anything other than green or orange there is an issue with the power brick and you should contact Xbox Customer support for a replacement.
