bp211 say:

Since clicking "Me Too" on this question I've found the easy fix on Word 2010-1016 (on W8, 8.1 and 10). YMMV but try this:

Word>File>Options>Advanced>Save>turn on "Copy remotely stored files onto your computer, and update the remote file when saving" by checking the box. Check OK and exit. 

Restart your computer for good measure. Give Word a try. The problem should be fixed.

From what I can tell, none of the other settings in the Save section matter for this purpose but here's how mine are set:

ON "Prompt before saving normal template"

OFF "Always create backup copy"

ON "Copy remotely stored files..." as above

OFF "Allow background saves"

Explanation as I understand it: By default, Word constantly updates local changes to OneDrive by comparing local and online drafts. Checking the "Copy remotely stored..." box above tells the computer to save changes remotely only when you manually save (or save by closing) the local file. If you're concerned about losing work, increase frequency of your local auto-save or just hit ctrl-S more frequently. Better yet...don't worry, be happy about being able to type without janky lag.

Google the setting if you want Microsoft's lengthy explanation that doesn't address lag. Or just make the change and get on with life.

Based on your experience, it would appear that restarting page numbers is just not going to work with a Continuous section break. I would have expected the footer to reflect what is below the break, but apparently that is not the case.

gerald mal say:

Hi,

To delete an account from the Mail and Calendar apps.

  1. In either the Mail or Calendar apps, choose Settings in the lower-left corner. 

    NOTE: If you're using a phone or tablet, choose More  at the bottom of the page to see the Settings option.

  2. Choose Manage Accounts, and then choose the account that you want to remove.

  3. Choose Delete account. If you don't see the Delete account option, you're probably trying to remove the default email account added when you logged on to Windows 10. You can turn off email from this account instead.

    NOTE: The Delete account option removes the account from the Mail and Calendar apps, but does not affect the account itself. For example, if you remove an Outlook.com account from the Mail and Calendar apps, you can still access that account using another email client or on the web at https://outlook.com.
