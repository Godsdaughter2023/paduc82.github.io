No, unfortunately. Workgroup security is not supported in the new .accdb format.

Access 2007 and 2010 continue to work just fine with .mdb files, and workgroup security IS supported in that format. But the .accdb simply doesn't.

Workgroup security has always been a bit weak, and a chore to maintain; if you really need strong security on the data in the tables, consider using SQL/Server (including the free SQL/Server Express) as the backend to your Access application. daniel g. grau say:
I'm guessing that you don't see a page break because there is none causing the jump.

There are three paragraph formatting attributes that can produce a similar issue (a jump to the next page). Place your cursor on the first paragraph that starts the new page (even if it's empty), then check for these attributes in Format > Paragraph > Line and page breaks:
  • Keep lines together (this will force all lines of the paragraph to be the same page)
  • Keep with next (this will keep at least the last line of a paragraph on the same page as the next paragraph, and more than one line if "Widow/orphan" control is enabled)
  • Page break before (forces the paragraph to start a new page) 

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
That's correct, just displaying the non-printing characters does not resolve anything. What it does do is help reveal the reasons for why the document flows as it does... You can't address the cause unless you know what the cause is :-) Especially if you did not create the document yourself, the process of elimination usually is the most productive approach & having the non-printing characters displayed is immensely helpful in that regard.

Manual Page Breaks & Section Breaks would be made visible if they were present. If they do not appear it means that you can rule them out as the cause.

The next possibility they identify is the set of paragraph formatting attributes Daniel pointed you to... a paragraph having one or more of those attributes will have a small square bullet at the left of the first line. Again, even if no mark appears adjacent to the paragraph that follows the 'mystery page break' it helps narrow down the list of suspects.

You also have to take into consideration the unresolved changes that have been tracked in the document. Until that is done it's impossible to determine exactly how the document will flow once they're cleared... even when the Final display option is selected.
