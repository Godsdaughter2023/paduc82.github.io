It's a problem with the Apple Print System and it has been around for a long time; it is the default maker of the PDF when you save. If you have Adobe Acrobat and create your PDF from it or save from Word as an Adobe PDF you won't have the problem.

jim gordon mvp say:

A hang at open is usually caused by one of two conditions:

A corrupt file crashed your App. The App is trying to open that crashed file and recover it when the App opens, but because the file is corrupt, the App simply hangs or crashes again, and the cycle repeats until broken. To break this particular cycle, hold the Shift key down when you click the PowerPoint icon. That skips the attempt to recover a bad file.

The other condition that is common is that there is a duplicate or bad font in your collection. Usually, when performing a Safe Boot, a limited set of known good fonts become active. However, you implied that the problem persisted for you in a safe boot, so in your case, I have doubts that a bad font is involved. Furthermore, your app is running OK in another user account without changing your fonts, so a bad font is not high on my list of troublemakers for you.

In the "old days" I would recommend deleting preferences to solve the sort of problem you're having. These days, that solution has not proven to be entirely effective.

Please try the holding the option key down while opening PowerPoint first. If that takes care of the problem, then Yay!

Otherwise, for you (and ONLY for you) I will suggest completely removing Office and then reinstalling.

Uninstall Office 2016 for Mac - Office Support
