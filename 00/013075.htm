When inserting Blank pages in a Word Doc.

 

At many places I found that it was not recommended to use Hard Page breaks. Instead use 'page break before'  on the item supposed to come on next page before expected blank page. It does'nt help always specially if you already have one blank sheet before it and want to add one more extra blank sheet before stuff.

 

 

Somewhere recommended to use Section break-odd page   and section break -next page also used multiple times to cause a blank sheet be inserted.

This creates lot of sections and danger of imporper handling of sections and causing formatting mess.

 

Q. What is the proper way for this purpose that one can add as many sheets and at the place of choice.

 

 


If the aim is to have a particular part of the document start on an Odd (usually) or Even page, then to make use of the appropriate type of Section break is generally the best thing to do.  There is no reason for the judicious use of Section Breaks to lead "improper handling of sections" or to cause formatting mess.

Like wise, hard page breaks do have their purpose, but in some circumstances, to use one rather than to have a paragraph (possibly in a particular Style) formatted to have a page break before, can, depending upon later edits in the text, result in an unwanted page break.

I do not believe that you can say that any one of the various methods of  causing a page break to occur is the only one that should be uses as it really depends upon the circumstances.
