john w. vinson say:

It's right at the bottom:

 

FROM Internship INNER JOIN Students ON Internship.ID = Students.ID

 

This will ensure that only those Students who have an Internship record will be displayed on the form.

 

Your Internship table has WAY WAY too many fields: it's badly denormalized! It looks like you grew the table wider as you had more types of internships. This is incorrect design. Instead you should have a "many to many" relationship - one Student can have zero, one, or (I presume) several Internships, and each Internship can have zero, one or many Students. "Records are cheap, fields are expensive"! With your current design what will you do if the school has a new kind of internship? Add a field to your table, curse and swear when it hits the 255 field limit, delete another field, redesign your form, redesign all your reports... ouch!!!!

 

Step back, and read up on "Normalization". The problem is because of the incorrect design of your tables - "committing spreadsheet" - and it will be resolved when you restructure your table correctly.

john w. vinson say:

Don't confuse data storage with data presentation. They are different tasks with different requirements!

 

From your SQL I see:

 

Internship.[Amtrak Yr], Internship.[Amtrak Program], Internship.[Am 10th], Internship.[Am 11th], Internship.[Am 12th], Internship.[Am Graduate], Internship.[Am PT], Internship.[Am FT],

 

and on and on. Evidently the Intership table has a different field for each type of internship - presumably Yes/No fields, right? That's what I'm concerned about!

 

A much better design would have three tables:

 

Students

  StudentID (primary key)

  LastName

  FirstName

  <other biographical and contact data>

 

InternshipTypes

  InternshipTypeID (autonumber primary key)

  Type (text, e.t. "Amtrak Yr", "Amtrak Program", "Am FT", "Database Design", "Interface Design")

 

Internships

  InternshipsID (autonumber primary key)

  StudentID (which student is doing this particular internship)

  InternshipTypeID (long integer, what internship is she doing)

  (other info about this student's experience with this internship, e.g. date started, date completed, outcome, supervisor comments, etc.)

 

You could have a Subform based on Internships, with a combo box based on InternshipTypes bound to the InternshipTypeID.

 

With this normalized design, you can very easily add, delete, or rename internship opportunities; do Totals queries to count how many students took the Am FT internship; etc. etc. With your current wide-flat design this is much, much more difficult.
