Other way of recovering the old conversation is by decoding the main.db file from your Windows device. If you still have this file, you can give it a try looking for a third party app that will decode it. so you can get the conversation you need.

To locate the main.db file on your device, follow the steps below:

  1. Quit Skype on your device.
  2. On your keyboard, press the Windows key and R at the same time. The Run window opens.
  3. Type %appdata%\skype and click OK. 
  4. Open your Skype Name folder and look for main.db file.

Once you get the file, try to look for available third party app on the internet and give it a try decoding the file. Let me know if that works.

ashish mathur say:

Hi,

 

I am sure there is a easier way to accomplish this but here is my attempt

 

1. Suppose you have a two column database in range B3:C8.  Headings are in B3:C3.  B4:B8 has text values and C4:C8 has numbers.  Create a bar/column graph from this data set

2. In range B11:C11, type B6 and B7 respectively

3. In range B12:C12, type C6 and C7 respectively

4. In cell B19, enter ="Sheet1!"&B12&":"&C12

5. In cell B20, enter ="Sheet1!"&B11&":"&C11

6. Save this file as Book1 on your desktop

7. Press Ctrl+F3 > New.  In the name box, type text_range and in the Refer to box, enter =INDIRECT(Sheet1!$B$20).  Click on OK and Close

8. Press Ctrl+F3 > New.  In the name box, type data_range and in the Refer to box, enter =INDIRECT(Sheet1!$B$19).  Click on OK and Close

9. Click on chart so that all charts/columns get selected.  In the series formula, change the second argument of the SERIES() formula to Book1!text_range

10. Click on chart so that all charts/columns get selected.  In the series formula, change the third argument of the SERIES() formula to Book1!data_range

 

Now change the cell references in range B11:C12 and the chart should update.
