Welcome to Microsoft Answers!

Even though Blu-ray disc (BD) is currently the only high-definition movie format on the market, we do not support its playback natively on either Windows Vista or Windows 7. “Blu-ray [playback] support will be provided by Personal Ccomputer makers or Blu-ray Disc drive makers. We have new extensibility in media center for those that provide Blu-ray Disc playback to support integrated playback.
Microsoft has always supported Blu-ray’s competitor, HD DVD. However, reason of the lack of support of Blu-ray playback in Windows 7 does not seem to be ideology (otherwise Windows 7 would be unable to burn BDs or read them), but royalties that Blu-ray disc association demands from player manufacturers. As a result, Microsoft offers optical disc drive makers as well as personal computers suppliers to include Blu-ray disc software players with their products.

     1.    Open Audio Devices and Sound Themes by clicking the Start button , clicking Control Panel, clicking Hardware and Sound, and then clicking Sound.

     2.    Click the Recording tab.

3.    Double-click the microphone that you use for Speech Recognition.

     4.    Click the Levels tab, and then use the slider to set the input level for your microphone.

 

·         The microphone connections are not properly attached. Make sure the microphone wires are intact and unbroken. The connections to the computer might be loose, so unplug the wires and reconnect them, making sure they fit snugly.

·         A different microphone is selected for use with Speech Recognition. If you have more than one microphone installed on your computer, the wrong one might be selected for use with Speech Recognition. Make sure that you're using the microphone selected for Speech Recognition.

To change which microphone is used with Speech Recognition

     1.    Open Audio Devices and Sound Themes by clicking the Start button , clicking Control Panel, clicking Hardware and Sound, and then clicking Sound.

     2.    Click the Recording tab.

     3.    Select the microphone that you want to use with Speech Recognition, and then click Set Default.
