bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
It sounds like the problem could be that you're pasting to a paragraph immediately above or immediately below a Table. That causes a new cell to be appended to the table.

OTOH, If you're pasting into another table cell, a new cell is being created because you're selecting & copying the End of Cell marker as well as the text.

One way to avoid either is to use Edit> Paste Special> Styled Text.

Another option is to avoid selecting the End of Cell Marker in the first place. There are numerous ways of precisely selecting just the text, such as;
  • Double-click a word to select it
  • Triple-click to select a paragraph
  • Command+Click to select a sentence
Use Shift-Click to extend a selection in the same manner as you started. For example, double-click the first word to be selected, then hold Shift while you click anywhere in the ending word.

Perhaps the most efficient method is to position the pointer at the beginning of the cell's content so that the pointer appears as a black arrow leaning to the right. Click to select the entire cell, then hold Shift while you press Left Arrow to deselect the End of Cell marker before you Copy.

It also helps to have the non-printing characters [¶] displayed so the markers are visible.
