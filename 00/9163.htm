Outlook not responsive, cannot end with task manager, re-boot, etc. - Microsoft Community

lmheimendinger say:

Outlook not responsive, cannot end with task manager, re-boot, etc.

Alot of posts indicate that it is not an uncommon error when Outlook 2010 becomes non responsive, especially when Task Manager cannot end it.  It got to happen so frequently on one of my systems that I was spending more time uninstalling and reinstalling than doing any useful work.  I have solved the problem or at least had it return to normal operations, and I wanted to share it with you.

First basic ecosystem of the problem machine:

  • Windows 7 Ultimate 64-bit, domain joined
  • SBS 2008 so Exchange 2007 SP2
  • Office 2010 Pro Plus installed after running 2010 Beta for several months
  • One Exchange mail account
  • Opened two other Exchange mail account users
  • Two moderately large .pst files
  • IMAP account for a second email address on Exchange account

All of the cleaning to remove all traces of Office and re-installing did not make any difference; in fact, the problem seemed to happen faster each time I did this.  That said,it may be worth doing so no other problems surface.  Search for a KB that tells you how to remove completely Office 2010 installations.  It is a Mr. Fixit solution that is easy to use.

The culprit for me was the .pst files.  They were on a shared volume on a NAS/iSCSI device, one was about 1GB and the other about 4GB.  So here is what I did.

  1. Followed the procedures to completely uninstall Office 2010. 
  2. Re-installed it.
  3. Did NOT open Outlook just yet.  Instead, used Control Panel to configure mail.
  4. I don't remember whether I had to delete the profile or not (meaning did it survive the removal of Office), but I wanted to start with a fresh one.
  5. Added the Exchange Account.  Made it use a new .ost.
  6. Started Outlook, everything ok.
  7. Modified Exchange account to open other users.  Still all ok.
  8. Added IMAP account which really is the same account as the Exchange one, but want to send with a different email address (note in order to make this work, you need to set the negotiation for send and receive ports on the advanced tab to auto-negotiate security).  Everything still ok.
  9. Added the .pst files a data.
  10. Got the non-responsive error.
  11. Re-booted.  Control panel->mail to delete the two .pst data files.
  12. Moved them to local storage.
  13. Started Outlook.  Opened the two .pst files.
  14. Immediately right-clicked on those folders, chose properties, click on advanced button, then did a compress on the file.  Repeated for the second .pst.

Since then, only perfect performance.  And runs much faster and more smoothly. This is about a week so far.

My conclusions.  Almost all the posts about this problem seem to have some .pst file mention.  I don't know why I didn't connect those dots sooner.  In my case, the entire problem got isolated to the .pst file(s) because I could do everything else.  Just a long process of elimination trying all sorts of suggestions that did not work.

I am not certain whether it was moving the .pst files to local storage or compresses them that ultimately solved the problem.  I really don't believe whether they are on a shared network resource or local makes a difference, but maybe copying did something in terms of underlying properties that made this work.  I think I vote for the compression.

Anyway, if you have this problem, pay attention to your .pst files.
