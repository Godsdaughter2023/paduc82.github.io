Most people are using 1 of 3 Office versions: 2003, 2007 or 2010.  There is a major split between in the user interface between 2003 which is the “old” Menu system and 2007 which uses the “new and improved” Ribbon.

I will provide simple guidelines on how to quickly identify which version of Office you are running.

 

How can I Identify Office / Word 2003

In the title bar you will see the Word Icon, then the file name followed by “ – Micorosoft Word”, starting at the left side of the application window. Immediately below it you will see text labels for the menu: File, Edit, View, Insert, Format, Tools, Table, Window, Help

 

How can I Identify Office / Word 2007

The Word (application) Icon has ben replaced in the Upper left corner of the application window by a big round 4 color “Office” button (it is clickable!) that spans 2 rows. Beside that are some icons for the “Quick Access Tab (QAT)” (note: optionally it can be relocated below ribbon). Beside the QAT in a light background is the Application title. The File name followed by “ – Microsoft Word” is centered in the Application title bar. Immediately below that is the “Tab” bar. Each tab is a graphical redesign of the old menus. The “active” tab has a light background, the rest of the inactive tabs are in a darker contrasting background color.

The Tab names are Office Button, Home, Insert, Page Layout, References, Mailings, Review, View

Immediately below the tab names is the new “Ribbon”. It is subdivided into “Groups”. Within the group, small and large icons and text lables.  The icon organization is dynamic. As you resize the width of the application window, the icon layout is adapted. Icon sizes and locations will change.

 

How can I Identify Office / Word 2010

The Word (application) icon has returned, to the left corner of the application window, eliminating the “Office” button. The rest of the application title bar is the same as 2007: QAT beside Application icon followed by the Filename and application name centered. Unlike 2007, there is very little contrast in the background color of the inactive tabs compared to the title bar. The active tab is still highlighted by the light backgound color.

The Tab names are: FILE, Home, Insert, Page Layout, Refernces, Mailings, Review, View

The ribbon is essentially the same as in 2007. The main difference is that the contrast between buttons a the background that was apparent in 2007 has been muted so that the buttons completely merge into the background.

 

Am I running Click-To-Run?

The quickest way to verify you have a CTR installation is go into Windows Explorer and look for an unexplained Q:\ (or R:\) drive.

Another method of checking: Go to help panel of any working app: File tab / Help command and look at the Right side of the Backstage panel. It should say "Product Activated" for MSI install or "Click-to-Run Product Updates".

If you installed Office 2010 using Click to Run technology, there are no Office files on the C drive, but on a virtual Q drive. "Microsoft Office Client Virtualization Handler" will appear in the list of programs. Associate the Office files with that

Also see http://www.officeforlawyers.com/outlook/tips/2010beta.html for tips about uninstalling Click-to-run version.

 

CTR Introduction (Identify CTR installation) - https://office.microsoft.com/en-ca/excel-help/click-to-run-introduction-HA101850493.aspx?CTT=3

 

What is Click to Run

Click-to-Run is a new Electronic Software Distribution (ESD) technology for Office 2010 that utilizes Microsoft’s streaming and virtualization technology. Click-to-Run provides a much faster and simpler download and installation experience for customers trying or buying Office 2010 over the Internet. Click-to-Run allows customers to maintain multiple versions of Office so customers can easily try Office 2010 side by side with an existing version of Office. Click-to-Run automatically downloads and installs any software updates when connected to the Internet, helping to maintain a more secure and up-to-date Office experience. At this time, only Office Home and Student 2010 and Office Home and Business 2010 will be available through Click-to-Run and limited based on broadband speed requirements.

NOTE:  CTR is a relatively new approach to installing and running Office. Introduced with 2010. So very few technicians are aware of it, let alone familiar with trouble shooting it. As well, it introduces a whole new set of errors, like the one that you encountered. My personal recommendation is that unless you have a clearly identified need for it and explicitly requested the download, DON’T USE IT.

 

Office 7, Office 10 vs Windows 7

Many people find saying and writing 2007 or 2010 part of the Office marketing names too long, so they shorten them to “Word 10” or “Office 7”. Unfortunately, that shorthand causes confusion for people with a technical orientation.  Those shortcuts look like “Version numbers” used in technical discussion.  Office Version 7 was known by the marketing name “Office 95”. And Office Version 10 was known by the marketing names “Office XP” and “Office 2002”. Those 4 versions have very different problems and have very different specific trouble shooting instructions.

Office 2007 is also known as Office Version 12, while Office 2010 is known as Office Version 14. You may ask, what happened to version 13? The marketing department happened, they bowed to fear of superstition by buyers, not to actual superstition.

On the other hand, Windows 7 is another marketing invention designed to confuse people. It looks like a version number but isn’t. If you check, you’ll see that the software version for Win7 is actually 6.1.  “Windows 7” is technically a minor revision  / improvement of Vista, Windows Version 6.0.

Hi Mspm,

I assume you have a custom domain abc.com and sign up an Office 365 tenant with abc.onmicrosoft.com. Now, you want to add abc.com to your tenant. If you only need to create account in Office portal, you can firstly create account with abc.onmicrosoft.com and then add your custom domain, switch user accounts to the custom domain. For example, you can create user1@abc.onmicrosoft.com and after adding abc.com to Office 365, you can switch it to user1@abc.com. So, User1 will have two email addresses. All emails which are sent to these two addresses can be seen with the account user1@abc.com in OWA or Outlook. It means those existing emails are still in the mail box.

Note: Now the login account is User1@abc.com, but this account has two related email addresses user1@abc.com and user1@abc.onmicrosofot.com. If you don’t want to change their login accounts, you can just add alias for User1 after adding custom domain.

Normally, it doesn't matter when you adding custom domain to Office 365. The key point is after adding your custom domain, you will choose to use the existing email service provider or Office 365 Exchange Online. If you choose to use Office 365 Exchange Online, you need to add Office 365 MX records. If you host DNS records outside of Office 365, you need to add MX records to the DNS records hosting. If you host records in Office 365, the MX records will be added automatically. To learn more, see Add users and domain to Office 365. It should not influence the existing emails no matter you choose which email service. But you need to pay attention that typically it takes about 15 minutes for DNS changes to take effect. However, it can occasionally take longer for a change you've made to update across the Internet's DNS system. This will influence the email service (sending and receiving).

rob koch say:

The problem here is that the WDO scan doesn't operate within the normal Windows operating system environment, but rather the WinPE environment on the Recovery Partition which is also used for the operation of any of the Windows 10 Recovery functions accessible during boot.

This means that any logs would also exist within that environment and so would not be found within the normal operating environment as are those for the more typical quick or full scans which are completed within the full Windows 10 operating system.

The question then becomes, does the WDO scan report back to the normal Windows Defender logs the results of its scan history?

Though we don't know the answer to this directly, it should be possible to test the theory that detected items are reported back to the main Windows Defender logs relatively easily by simply downloading either the Eicar or another safe antimalware test detection file and placing this in important folders like the Desktop, Download or Documents where they might be found during a scan.

After disabling the Defender real-time protection, cloud protection (likely unnecessary) and the Edge browser SmartScreen protection, which would normally block or alert for these, I downloaded the Eicar.com executable test file into all three folders mentioned above.  Upon configuring a WDO scan this proceeded normally with a reboot, eventually indicating; "Preliminary scan results show that a malicous or potentially unwanted software might exist..." near the end of the scan process.

Upon completion of the scan, there was no apparent display that malware had been detected within the WDO quick scan results screen itself, which only displayed that the system was being protected.

Once the PC had rebooted back into the normal Windows 10 operating system, I gave it some time to settle and then checked both the Windows Defender Event Viewer log, as well as the History in the Security Center.  The event log displayed the configuration items to begin the WDO scan, followed immediately by the entry which re-enabled the Defender real-time protection at start-up of the normal operating system a few minutes later, so nothing was logged there as expected.

However, there was no indication in the Defender history log either, even though there'd clearly been some sort of potential detection indicated by the preliminary results.  This was confirmed by the fact that the Eicar.com file had disappeared from the Desktop folder as well, though those contained in Downloads and Documents folders still remained, since these apparently aren't included in the Quick scan performed by WDO.

So since the WDO scan operates completely within the WinPE environment, it's apparent that neither of these logs are available to or at least accessed by WDO and it instead simply removes any malware it finds silently without attempting to notify the user.

UniqueFunction should hold a list of Unique functions from your database (column E)

the code is looping through this list and adding blank worksheets for each one.  As it does that, it attempts to name the sheet using the name of the function.  So if you are getting an error, the value of  UniqueFunction(c)  is not a valid sheet name.  This is a data problem.  So you would have to check what the values are .
