evett slanders say:
Hi Kristen,

I'm Evett an Independent Adviser, willing to assist you with your issue.

If you delete photos from Microsoft Photos, the files will be deleted from the original location. But Microsoft Photos always displays the photos from the folders added to Photos application. Remove the folders from the Microsoft Photos that you don’t need:
1. Open Microsoft Photos app
2. Tap on three dots (Ellipsis) on the top right corner of the window and tap on Settings
3. Locate Sources, then click on X to remove the folder that has duplicate photos.
If you want to remove duplicate photos from your PC, please go to the location where the photos are saved and delete the duplicates.

techfreak_ say:

Some people have more than one account and are unaware of it. For example:.

1. An account could exist with the Skype name of bob and have a primary email address of bob@skype.com. The sign in name would be bob.

2. A second account could exist with the Skype name of live:bob and have a primary email address of bob@skype.com. The sign in name would be bob@skype.com.

Reactivation notices for either account would still go to bob@skype.com but the details of that email would state which account's credit is to be deactivated. For this reason, it is best to check over the details of that email, rather than browse through an individual account's history.

To manually verify you are signed into the right account:

Every account has purchase and usage history since the beginning of the account. Please check that history. From your purchase history you can seen when your credit was purchased using what card. The usage history will let you know if any of that credit was consumed. If you find none of this history, you would be signed into a different account, in which this credit was purchased. If you find conflicting information, this will provide evidence towards a claim that the credit was deactivated and should be made recoverable.

You would need to sign into the correct account, as stated in your notice, to reactivate that credit.
