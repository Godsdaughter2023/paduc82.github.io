How can I pull just the last four numbers from a text string? - Microsoft Community

kelly_996 say:

How can I pull just the last four numbers from a text string?

My data exported entirely into Column A in Excel, and I have formulas pulling the Total lines, which list one or two words followed by four numbers, all separated by a space. I would like to pull the last 4 numbers into their own cells, one number per cell. Is there a formula that will give me the last number, then the next to last number, etc.? Thanks!
gary's student say:

Say A1 contains:

asdsd xcvc 12 34 56 777

In another cell:

 

=--RIGHT(A1,LEN(A1)-LOOKUP(LEN(A1),FIND(" ",A1,ROW(INDEX($A:$A,1,1):INDEX($A:$A,LEN(A1),1)))))

 

will display 777, the last number.  Remove this value from the string using LEFT() and repeat the formula to get the next to last, etc.
