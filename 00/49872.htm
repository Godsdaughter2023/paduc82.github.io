Unfortunately there isn't a really easy way to do this in Windows Live Movie Maker.  In the prior "classic" versions of Windows Movie Maker, you could put a copy of your video onto the audio track. You would then mute the original video track and replace video with other content as needed.

In WLMM, you can do something similar, but it first requires turning your video into an audio-only file.  The easiest way to do this is with a conversion program.  Freemake Video Converter, for example, can convert a video file into an .mp3 (audio) file.  There must be other choices too if you want to use your favorite search engine to look for video to audio converters.  If you do install FVC, I recommend paying attention during the install. Now that you have your video (no volume) playing along side your audio (from the original video), you can make video edits as needed.  Just remember the two are completely separate now, so it is easy to get things out of synch.  If you make any edits to the video track, keep the duration the same as before.  So if you plan to add a 10 second clip, be sure to trim out an equivalent 10 seconds of the original video.  Other edits (such as adding a transition or trimming out video completely) will require you to make similar edits to the audio, if you want them to stay in synch.
