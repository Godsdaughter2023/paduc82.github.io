Hello WakeyD.

Do not fear, as long as you do not click any links or open / save any attachments, the email can do no harm.

However, to check that this is a scam email, please do the following:

1) Check the email address thoroughly. Chances are, if the sender(s) are trying to scam you, they will change one or two letters around. An example - instead of having "paypal", it gets changed to "paypel".

a. user say:

You could try installing it in another WP7 phone, but I don't think that's going to work.

With WP7, the O/S actually 'includes' and manages the SD card storage area as part of the overall memory of the phone.  So everything -- internal and card memory is managed as one unit.

And since WP7 and WP8 don't use a typical folder/file memory management strategy, there's no way for other devices to read/interpret the data stored on the card.

Also, when you 'formatted' the card on the PC, you likely blew away everything (or at least any significant pointers) that were on the card.

Unfortunately, the answer to your question is 'Yes', there are no commercial programs (that I'm aware of) that will read/recover the strangely formatted data from the card.

Easiest is to start PowerPoint.  Open both presentations.  Select the presentation that you want to insert into the other (source) and then click on the slide sorter view.  To select all the slides to be copied in the source presentation press and hold Ctrl and press the A key.  Then Press and hold the Ctrl key while pressing the C key (to copy in the clipboard.)  To select individual slides press and hold the Ctrl key while individually clicking on the slides you want to copy over.
 
Go to the target presentation and again in the slide sorter view position the mouse cursor on the space in front of the location you want the slides to be placed and click once.  Press and hold the Ctrl key and tap the P key.  All the selected and copied slides will be pasted in the new location with the background and master slide information on the new slides.
 
You can also read "Use Slide Libraries to share and reuse PowerPoint 2007 slide content" in Help in PowerPoint if you have a Share Point Server available otherwise, if you don't know what share point server is and you are not in a business setting don't bother.
