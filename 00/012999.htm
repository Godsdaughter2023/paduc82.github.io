As psychomania correctly points out, the reason you can't use a bluetooth headset is that the phone has no aerial for FM radio, the wired headset cable acts as that. When you plug in a wired headset, it automatically divers sound from speakers to the headset, so it can't be re-diverted to a bluetooth set.

A full connection is established when you receive a call and deactivated at the end, but listening to radio would need that connection permanently live.

Actually software will not avail you.

 

I checked with a person that works at Nokia and he informed me that the FM radio audio is not routed via the digial audio path. It's only analog so it goes from the analog FM receiver to the Headphone amplifier.

 

Bluetooth audio comes from the digital audio part of the phone as the audio is sent in a digital format to the BT headset and then decoded there. MP3 and music is already in digital format so it goes through but FM radio is not digital.

 

 To be able to send the FM radio audio via Bluetooth it will need a big redesign of how the audio is handled inside the phones.  An ADC will need to be implemented and other changes as well. So it won't be happening any time soon.

 

Internet radio, on the other hand, is digital so it CAN be routed to a Bluetooth headset.
