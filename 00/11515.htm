If you want the entire formula to be in a cell then using Indirect like the following works if the Linked workbook is open but if closed then it gives a #Ref error.

=INDIRECT("'C:\Users\User\Documents\Excel\Test Macros\[Linked Workbook.xlsm]" & C5 & "'!$C$395")

However, if you are creating the formula with VBA and using VBA to assign the formula to the required cell then the following can be used to concatenate the values and it works.

(Note that VBA line break as used  can be used outside of the individual strings with double quotes; cannot break a line within the double quotes without closing off the quotes and adding an ampersand and re-opening the quotes.)

Range("A2") = "='C:\Users\User\Documents\Excel\Test Macros\[Linked Workbook.xlsm]" _
        & ThisWorkbook.Worksheets("Sheet1").Range("C5").Value & "'!$C$395"

I have used the fully qualified workbook, worksheet and range. If C5 is on the active sheet then can simply use Range("C5") without the workbook and worksheet names.

The formula that is assigned to Range("A2") with the above code is like the following where ThisWorkbook.Worksheets("Sheet1").Range("C5").Value  is 18.

Hi MontePark,

 

It is a pleasure to help you with your issue in creating groups in Outlook.com. Let's find out what are the possible options that we can do to resolve your issue.

 

Please be informed that the Contacts page has been updated to People. You can access your contacts through http://people.live.com. For more information on the recent update, please refer to this article:

 

Contacts are now People: What’s new and different?

 

Here’s how to add a group:

 

1. Sign in to your Microsoft account

2. Go to www.people.live.com

3. In the People window, click Manage

4. Click Manage groups

5. Click Group and type the group name you want

6. Click Apply to add group

 

Here’s how to access your group and see the group contacts:

 

1. Sign in to your Microsoft account

2. Go to www.people.live.com

3. In the People window, near top left, click " All v ". A drop down box will appear with a list of your groups. Click the one you want and all the contacts within that selected group are now on the left side. Scroll to see the whole list.

 

Here’s how to add contacts to the group you created:

 

1. Sign in to your Microsoft account

2. Go to www.people.live.com

3. In the People window, click a contact you want to be added to the group. If you have multiple contacts to add, click the box at top left of the contact and continue clicking the top left box of each contact you want to add to the group until you are done

4. At the toolbar, click Groups and check the box in front of the group name

5. Click Apply and all the contacts you have selected are now in the group.

 

Note: make sure you click squarely at the check box. If you click outside the box, all previous boxes you have checked will be nullified, and you’ll have to start all over.

 

Here’s how to edit a contact within a group:

 

1. Log-in to your Microsoft account

2. Go to www.people.live.com

3. On the People window, top left, click the “ All v “ > your group is on the drop down list

4. Click the group name. You have all the contacts within that group listed

5. Click one contact to view it

6. Click Edit located at the toolbar to do edit your chosen contact

 

Note: Edit option only appears at the toolbar after you have selected a contact.

 

Here’s how to delete a contact from a group but keep it in the main contacts list:

 

1. Sign in to your Microsoft account

2. Go to www.people.live.com

3. In the People window, click the contact you want to remove from a group

4. At the toolbar, click Groups and un-check the box in front of the group in question

5. Click Apply

 

Let us know if you need more help.
