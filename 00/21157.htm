debbie21 say:

Problem merging zip codes

I’m trying to create envelopes in mail merge, Excel 2010 to Word 2010. (I’ve done it successfully many times before in previous versions.) Because most of the envelopes are going to the Northeast US, the zip codes start with a zero, i.e. 02108.

 

In Excel I have my columns formatted as a zip code, which gives me the zero (02108). However, when I put the field merge into Word, it drops the zero (2108).

 

How do I get complete zips?

macropod say:

Hi Debbie,

I take it you mean that the 5+4 zip codes aren't formatted correctly. To ensure they are, you can use a field construction like:

{QUOTE
{SET Zip {MERGEFIELD ZipCode}}
{IF{Zip}> 99999 {SET Zip {Zip \# "00000'-'0000"}}}
"{=-{=-{Zip}-Zip}/2 \# 00000;;}{=-({Zip}*(-1)-Zip)/2 \# ;-0000;}"}

The above field code correctly formats 5-digit and 5+4-digit Zip Code data, even where the source data may have incorrect formatting such as a hyphen followed by trailing 0s after plain 5-digit Zip Codes (eg because a db only accepts Zip Codes in the 5+4-digit format) or is formatted as a 5-digit or 9-digit string without hyphens. A data field named ‘ZipCode’ is assumed.

Note: The field brace pairs (ie '{ }') for the above example are created via Ctrl-F9 - you can't simply type them or copy & paste them from this message.

Cheers,
Paul Edstein
[MS MVP - Word]

cbarnhorst say:

Please turn off the caps lock.  All caps is considered rude (shouting) in forums.  We already know your issue is important to you.  Why else would you post?

If you upgraded from a previous version of Windows to Windows 10 Pro then you do not have (and do not need) a product key that can activate a copy of Windows.  The key you see with BelArc or Keyfinder type programs is a dummy key and is the same for all Get Windows 10 Upgrade copies.  When you upgraded, the licensing software registered your device as a Windows 10 Device with Microsoft for the edition you upgraded to.  Any time you re-install Windows 10 Pro on the same device the licensing software asks Microsoft's servers if the device is registered.  When the servers acknowledge that it is, the licensing software activates Windows.  Any installation of the same edition of Windows 10 on the device is a re-installation (yes, even clean installations and parallel (multi-boot) installations).  Re-installations do not require (and you should not use) a product key.  Instead, if you see a product key page during installation simply click the link at the bottom that says "I do not have a product key."

This is an anti-piracy change by Microsoft.  They got tired of playing whack-a-mole with hackers so they took activation out of software entirely.  Now it is based on the unique identifiers of the device.  No software activation, nothing to hack.
