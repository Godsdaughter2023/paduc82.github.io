Yes, those are all SATA drives for which there is no "Master" or "Slave" settings.   With SATA, there's only one drive per channel.

Your indication of "Ultra ATA/100" as an "interface option" refers to your motherboard; that spec doesn't apply to SATA drives.  All of your SATA drives are rated at 3.0 Gb/s, so connect the drives to 3.0 Gb/s connectors on your mobo (if available).

Connect your boot drive to the SATA port on the motherboard with the lowest number (0 or 1) -- or see your motherboard manual.

*******************************************
Looking at your mobo manual, I suggest connecting the boot drive (drive 2, according to the posts above) to SATAII0 and the other two drives to SATAII1 and SATAII2.  Be sure to configure the BIOS to use AHCI rather than RAID. 

You will have to set the BIOS SATA mode to "disabled" in order to install Windows XP because the XP CD doesn't include SATA drivers (or put the SATA drivers on a floppy disk, assuming that you have a floppy disk drive or create a slipstreamed CD that includes the SATA drivers).  Once Windows XP has been installed, you can install SATA drivers and then reconfigure the BIOS.

See pages 43, 73-74 of your manual.  Also see http://news.softpedia.com/news/Install-Windows-XP-On-SATA-Without-a-Floppy-F6-47807.shtml  Also see the section with the green check mark here --> http://superuser.com/questions/427340/how-to-enable-ahci-in-windows-xp-without-reinstalling

The manual doesn't say one way or the other, but it's probably better if you use the Intel South Bridge SATA controller first before using the GIGABYTE on-board SATA controller.
