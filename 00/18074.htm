suzanne s. barnhill say:

The default spacing in Word 2010 (as in Word 2007) is 1.15 lines, with 10 points Spacing After. You can get single spacing by pressing Ctrl+1, but you will still need to remove the Spacing After.

There are several ways to achieve the result you want:

  1. Right-click in a Normal paragraph and choose Paragraph. In the Paragraph dialog, change the line spacing and Spacing After. If you want this to be the default spacing, click Set As Default and choose "This document only" or "New documents based on the Normal.dotm template."
  2. Or, in the Styles group on the Home tab, choose the No Spacing style.
  3. Or, to revert to Word 2003 defaults, in the Styles group, click Change Styles, then Style Set, and choose Word 2003.
  4. Or use Change Styles | Paragraph Spacing | No Paragraph Space, then click Change Styles again and choose Set as Default.
  5. Or use the dialog launcher in the Styles group (the tiny arrow in the bottom right corner) to open the Styles task pane (you can also do this with Alt+Ctrl+Shift+S). Click on Manage Styles (third button from the left at the bottom), select the Set Defaults tab, change the settings as desired, and choose "Only in this document" or "New documents based on this template."
