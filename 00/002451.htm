djgreedo say:

Go to Settings > Device > Sync Groups. There you can see how much disc space each sync group will take on the Zune or Windows Phone when synced. A sync group can be an artist, a playlist, an album, or  a song. You create a sync group when you drag an item into your phone/Zune icon to sync it.

 

For example you might see a group like this:

Playlist: New Music - 25 songs 120.5mb

Artist: Smashing Pumpkins - 435 songs - 1.53gb

 

etc.

 

40GB of storage is lot. Even with a bit of video you can fit a large amount into 40GB. 40GB is several thousand songs. With 40GB you really shouldn't need to worry about file sizes at all unless you are planning on syncing something like 5000 songs to your device. I currently have almost 3,000 songs, 30 videos, and over 100 podcast episodes synced to my 32GB Zune. Unless you want to sync considerably more content than that you don't need to worry about storage capacity.

 

You can also set the Zune software to convert your music to a lower filesize to fit more on the device. This is a good idea if your music library is in high bitrate format but you're happy to listen to it on your phone in slightly lower quality.
