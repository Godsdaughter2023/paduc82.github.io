john sr wilson say:

Just a quick warning:

You are getting into difficult stuff!

The setting you have chosen with Duration = SmoothEnd are not usual BTW

Anyway:

           Set myEffect = sld.TimeLine.MainSequence.Item(x)

            If myEffect.EffectType = msoAnimEffectFly Then

                myEffect.Timing.Duration = 0.5

                If myEffect.Timing.SmoothStart = msoFalse Then myEffect.Timing.SmoothStart = True

                myEffect.Timing.Decelerate = 1

            End If

You can see how this sets the duration of the animation to 0.5 sec but the SmoothEnd is (a lot) less obvious

The Decelerate setting indicates for what % of the duration the object is slowing down. In this case 100% of the time. You can do the same with Acelerate to get Smooth Start but the total cannot exceed 100% - NOTE the USA cannot spell ;-)

sheenali_msft say:

Hi Neelesh,

The reason it is not suggested to delete a user’s OneDrive for Business site on your own is because there are references related with the user’s personal site that can sometimes be affected. This happens because when you delete his OneDrive for Business site, you are actually deleting the user’s personal site.

We had previous incidents where users report issues after deleting their personal site, namely an ‘Error 404’ when opening OneDrive for Business on an already deleted personal site.

To summarize, I would like to explain what to do if you want to delete a user’s site:
1. If you want to delete a user’s OneDrive for Business because of an issue, you may contact us to resolve the issue from the backend.

2. If you are deleting a user’s OneDrive for Business because the user is no longer in the company, we suggest deleting the user directly from the Office 365 admin center so that SharePoint will automatically schedule the user’s site for deletion.


3. If you have a specific reason for wanting to delete a user’s personal site, you may clarify the reason and we will help you find a workaround.


Regards,
Sheen

Error '0X800CCC78' usually occurs due to incorrect Outgoing server authentication settings in Outlook. Check the account settings and change the authentication settings in Outlook as per your Internet Service Provider.

If the option “My outgoing server requires authentication” is checked, uncheck it or vice-versa and try to send the emails and check if it works fine
