Hello NorbertSabic:

Thank you for posting to the forum regarding the issue you are having using the play to function with your Xbox One.  It sounds like this is related to your connection as the Play to function is going to utilize streaming from your PC to your console.  With that said, there are a couple things I can suggest you look into to improve your situation.

The first thing I would look into is your computer time out.  I am not sure that your computer going to sleep should disconnect you but it is worth looking into.  If you are currently set to have your computer go to sleep after 30 minutes try increasing this time and see if that directly impacts the time before you see your connection break up.

Next, you will need to have the Xbox Video application on the computer remain open and signed in.  As a test, I would monitor the computer while you are utilizing the play to function and see if you notice the app crash or sign you out at the same time you notice the connection drop.  If you do, this could be an indication of issues with the app or conflicts on the computer that are leading to disruptions in this application.

Finally, this could be due to an unstable connection.  I would consider the following to try and strengthen your overall connection:

  • Try and move your set up and router closer to one another.  Proximity of the hardware often decreases potential interference and strengthens signal.
  • Power cycle your network.  We suggest you do this by disconnecting your router, modem, Xbox console and PC for 5 minutes.  Reboot all and try again.
  • This one is a stretch but if you can, try setting up your computer and Xbox One on another network.  I know this likely requires physically moving your hardware but this is one way to determine if this is related to your network or to your devices.

ken sheridan say: Don't use a multi-valued field.  Model the many-to-many relationship type between invoices and journals by means of a table in the conventional way of the database relational model.  In broad outline the relevant tables would be:

Invoices
....Invoice (PK)
....InvoiceDate
....etc

Journals
....JournalID (PK)
....Journal
....etc

and to model the relationship type:

InvoiceJournals
....Invoice (FK)
....JournalID (FK)

The primary key of this last table is a composite one comprising both columns.

For data entry you simply need a subform, bound to the InvoiceJournals table and in continuous forms view, within the journals form.  The subform would be linked to the parent form on JournalID as its LinkMasterFields and LinkChildFields properties.  For an example of this basic type of many-to-many relationship type and various ways of entering data via forms see StudentCourses.zip in my public databases folder at:

https://skydrive.live.com/?cid=44CC60D7FEA42912&id=44CC60D7FEA42912!169

If you have difficulty opening the link copy its text (NB, not the link location) and paste it into your browser's address bar.

The first option in this little demo, of a conventional form/subform, is the one appropriate to your situation.

And BTW never enter data directly into a table's datasheet in an operational database, only via forms.
