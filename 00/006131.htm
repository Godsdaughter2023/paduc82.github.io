MS is getting better about automating activation. But they still have a way to go.

Windows and Office activation are similar, but totally independent.

For both Windows and Office MS treats a new motherboard as a "new computer".

For Windows, take another close look at your Windows installation disk and packaging. If you were careful, and lucky, you really do have a Retail license. If you were unlucky and cheaped out you bought "deal" priced "OEM" license. That is a problem for you 2 ways. First is that those licenses are not intended to be sold to end users, computer builders only. The second is, one of the specific license terms is that the license is permanently tied to the first computer it is installed on.  If you really do have a Retail / "FPP" license, then part of the extra price allows you to transfer it to new hardware.

The "new" electronic license management for Windows 10 is supposed to make re-installing Windows easier. But one situation it does not handle is new hardware. You will have to contact MS support to help moving the activation to the new hardware.

For Office 2016, the license was (dis)"improved" to allow it to be installed/activated on one computer at a time. So yes you have to uninstall it from the old computer before installing on the new computer. Of course, if the old computer is dead/completely unusable, that also meets the license requirement.

Retail licenses for one time payment Office have a couple of relevant license terms:

  • install/activate on only one computer at a time
  • right to transfer to new computers
  • limit on right to transfer to once per 90 days
  • exception on limit to transfer if the hardware died

One limit that is NOT there is a "maximum" on the number of times you can activate it.

' ###################################

Option Explicit
Sub AHighLightRowWSpecifiedText()

        If Selection.Information(wdWithInTable) = True Then

doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

The display of the Table Gridlines is not saved with a particular document, but the setting is retained from one document to the next.

If the display of Table Gridlines is turned off, when a new document is created by clicking on the New Document button on the Labels tab of the Envelopes and Tables dialog, the Table Gridlines are visible when that document is created, regardless of the display of the Table Gridlines having been turned off.  However, when that document is saved, closed and then re-opened, because the display of the Table Gridlines was originally turned off, the Table Gridlines will not be visible when the document opens.
