The 100MB System partition has only about 50% free space remaining and needs to be resized to 350MB which is the standard size for Windows 8, 8.1 and clean installs of Windows 10.

You can do it all in disk management.  It just takes time to do each step one at a time.

1. Find unallocated space and move the partition to left of it to the right,  Apply. If no unallocated space find a partition that has extra unused space and reduce the size of that partition and apply giving you some unallocated space, then proceed.

ma. lau say:

You can follow the steps below to reduce junk mail in your Outlook.com inbox by adding people and domain names to your safe or blocked sender list.

When you add people or domains to your safe senders and blocked senders lists, email that you want appears in your Inbox, and unwanted email is filtered into the Junk folder.

Add a person or domain to your blocked senders list

  1. At the top of the screen, select Settings > Options.

  2. Under Junk email, select Blocked senders.

  3. In the Enter a sender or domain here box, enter the email address, and press Enter or select the Add icon .

    • For example, to block all email from addresses that end in contoso.com, enter contoso.com in the text box.

    • To block all emails from a particular person, enter that person's full email address. For example, to block all messages from KatieJ@contoso.com, enter KatieJ@contoso.com in the text box.

  4. Select Save .

If you no longer want to receive email from a person or domain, or you accidentally added someone to your blocked senders list, you can remove that person's address from either list. Removing someone from your safe senders list won't guarantee that his or her emails are filtered, however. To prevent emails from that person reaching your Inbox, add the person or company to the blocked senders list.

To remove an address from your blocked senders list

  1. On the navigation bar, select Settings > Options.

  2. Under Junk email, select Blocked senders.

  3. Choose the email address or domain that you want to remove from the Blocked senders list, and then select Remove .

  4. Select Save .

To remove an address from your safe senders list

  1. On the navigation bar, select Settings > Options.

  2. Under Junk email, select Safe senders.

  3. In , choose the email address or domain that you want to remove from the Safe senders list, and then select Remove .

  4. Select Save .

    Note: To guarantee that you don't receive any further emails from this address or domain, add the address to your blocked senders list.

ashmsport say:

Hi,

I opened an MS case and they confirmed it will not work in SPO. Here's their reply:

"\\servername\folder will not work in SharePoint online. Shared Drive in your network is the Shared resources in your domain network.

In SharePoint online you can only add the link with fully qualified domain. When you add the shared network drive like \\servername\folder as link to SharePoint online, it consider it folder hosted on its own web server. When you click on the link SharePoint add prefix domain.sharepoint.com\\servername\folder to make it FQDN. This sends the query to own WFE. This is by design."
