    Is it possible when leveling a resource to change the resources % avaiable on a task to change.  

    Meaning:

    task 1 and task two are scheduled for the same time period and same resource.   When I level, it just changes task two to start when task 1 is finished.  I want these tasks to be worked in parallel.  I would expect the Resource % to be changed to 50% on each task and then that would automatically extend the finish date.  

    This is possible to do manually, but with the number of tasks that I have, it would be very complex and time consuming to figure out the resource %.  

    Thanks in advance for any assistance. 

    dale howard [mvp] say:

    zradams1 --

    No, what you want leveling to do for you is not possible.  When you use the built-in leveling tool in Microsoft Project, this tool can only do two things to resolve overallocations:

    • Delay tasks or assignments.
    • Split tasks or assignments.

    The software cannot change the Units value for the overallocated resource to resolve the overallocation.  That decision is left up to you.  Hope this helps.

    trevor rabey say:

    Your question comes up in one form or another quite often.

    However, it it due to a misunderstanding of what MSP can do, or will do, or should do.

    Your expectation is incorrect. MSP will not change assignments and there is a very good reason for that and it is because it cannot know how you want it done. It does not read the project manager's mind. Maybe in the future Google will invent software that does read minds but for now this is what we have.

    MSP does not optimise. However, it does highlight the problem and does point the way to the action you must take.

    And you will have several options which MSP does not know about without your input.

    Actually, MSP does not even highlight the problem because it does not know which aspect of the situation you regard as the problem.

    When you level, you get one solution out of what may be many possible solutions. In your case, it delays one task, Over-allocation problem solved. If you want it to delay the other task instead, you change the relative priorities and level by priority. Again, over-allocation solved.

    If you want other outcomes, you can choose to turn on or off the check boxes in leveling options.

    But the combination of possible settings and options, and the outcomes are limited and does not include the one you want.

    You say it would be very complex and time consuming but actually it is just fine tuning. We do it all the time.

    The more complicated your plan, and the more clever you try to be with scheduling, the more fine tuning is necessary.

    You can avoid some of it by having less complicated plans which are a bit less optimal but still feasible and easier to control.

    You always have to compromise. It's a trade off.
