Starting point when Word does something unexpected

As a general rule, when something seems to be going wrong, or at least not the way you want it to go, in Word, any version, go to Tools and then Options and study all the settings on all the tabs. You may even find some settings that you want to try that have nothing to do with the current problem.

When I was teaching Word and other Microsoft Office programs at a private business college, I had three rules:

1.     Always check Tools Options

2.     When in doubt, right-click on something

3.     If you're wondering how to set up a special kind of material in a document, consider whether Word tables are suitable. The placement officer of the school came to me several times and asked how I thought she should set something up. Each time the answer was, "I'd use a Word table." By the fifth time she had stopped asking--she got the point.

jsssssssss say: You didn't uninstall Microsoft Security Essentials (MSE) before you upgraded to Windows 10 which now disables Windows Defender(WD).

This is happening to many people, it seems to be a bug, and the best solution for now is to back up your data files and to do a reset of Windows 10 which will turn your upgrade into a clean install.

Unfortunately, at the moment I have bad news for you.

There have been several reports of the Windows 10 upgrade from Windows 7 with Microsoft Security Essentials installed has left MSE on the machine and disabled Defender. The uninstall procedures for MSE do not appear to work.

See this Wiki article:  Uninstalling Microsoft Security Essentials

In another thread, someone reported that they booted to safe mode and removed all registry keys related to Microsoft Security Essentials manually and disabled the startup service from services. While this worked, I suggest that you back up your data files and do a reset of Windows 10 which will turn your upgrade into a clean install. You will need to reinstall all programs and restore your data.
