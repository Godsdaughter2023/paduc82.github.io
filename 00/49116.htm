Normally PCIe devices don't require BIOS changes... but PCIe devices are not usually Boot devices or drives... That is something fairly new...

Sometimes when you add a new drive it doesn't show up even in Bios until the machine is restarted...

One thing that may be complicating things for you is a Windows feature called Fast Startup... It retains settings... Adding something like a drive can sometimes cause issues because of it...

For instance... when I make a image of my OS with bootable media, I have to be sure to do it with a Restart... Doing so after a Shutdown causes all kind of issues when attempting to reload the image...

This feature only works when you do a SHUTDOWN and then Boot.

It doesn't effect a RESTART.

To turn it off or on...

Go to Control panel...Power Options, and select Choose What the Power Buttons Do on the left.

Then select Change Settings That Are Currently Unavailable near the top center of screen...

Lower down on the window, uncheck Fast Startup.

BTW... In your BIOS you may also have something called Secure Boot and Fast Boot...

You may need to turn them off to get a boot with a new drive... Try Fast Boot first, but you may need to turn off Secure Boot to do that..

Not all 3.5-mm audio jacks are made to the same standard. There are two audio jack standards:
