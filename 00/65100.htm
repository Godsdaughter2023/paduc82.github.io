cmdrkeene say:

In short:  not really.

The cursor you pick in the mouse control panel (for the text select cursor, sometimes known as an "I-beam" cursor) is indeed used in Word, but only in specific places along certain margins.  In most other areas as you mouse around the page, Word is actually "helpfully" replacing that cursor with a custom one that shows an additional little lines next to it (to indicate what left/right alignment would be used if you clicked there).  If you could somehow zoom in on it, it would look something a little bit like this example:

Since Word can't add those indicator lines to the default cursor, it replaces the entire i-beam with the custom "thin" pointer.

Exploring, the only way I see to avoid this and only use your chosen cursor is to leave "page layout view" and go to Draft View (using the buttons near the left side of the View toolbar tab).
