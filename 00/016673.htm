I believe it converts the QT movie to a WMV, or does if you have Quicktime installed on your PC when you do the conversion.  This also means that (formerly)QuickTime movies will now play on Windows computers that don't have Quicktime installed.

Of course, if the movies are embedded, they will need PPT 2010, the 2010 viewer or PPT 2007/SP2.  Previous to that, PPT didn't do embedded movies.

By the way, a PPTX is really just a ZIP file.  You can tack .ZIP onto the end of its file name, after the .PPTX then click "Yes, dear, I know.  Trust me, it'll all be ok" when Windows screams at you about changing the extension.

After that, you can open the ZIP file, look for a Media folder, and in it you'll find the embedded media files.  You can poke at them to find out what PPT's changed your QTs into, I expect.

If the document has been saved in the docx format, Word won't display the INCLUDEPICTURE fields - it would have to be re-saved in the older doc format first.

macropod say:
If the document is in the older doc format, simply pressing Alt-F9 toggles the field code display on/off. Any linked pictures will be displayed as INCLUDEPICTURE fields (e.g. {INCLUDEPICTURE "C:\\Username\\Desktop\\Image.jpg"}). You can use Find/Replace to update however much of the filepaths need changing. Having done that, press Alt-F9 to restore the display, followed by Ctrl-A, F9 to refresh the links.
