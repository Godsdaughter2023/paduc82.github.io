iris he msft say:

Hi Eric,

1. After signing in, Office will attempt to obtain a SCA license from Microsoft’s cloud licensing service. A user who has been assigned an Office 365 ProPlus license (or Project Pro or Visio Pro licenses) by their admin will successfully obtain it and the license will be stored in their Windows user’s profile on the machine. If the user doesn’t log on to the shared computer for several days, the licensing token can expire. The next time that the user tries to use Office 365 ProPlus, Office 365 ProPlus contacts the Office Licensing Service on the Internet to get a new licensing token.

2. You may check the expiration date via checking the licensing Data. Please follow the steps below:

(1).Find the licensing token. The current user’s license files can be inspected by going to the following locations:
C:\Users\<username>\AppData\Local\Microsoft\Office\15.0\Licensing
or:  %localappdata%\Microsoft\Office\15.0\Licensing

(2).In the Licensing folder, you can find {ACID}.authString.txt.

(3).Open it with Notepad. It contains the expiration date and other information.
Start Date: indicates when the license was issued
(example: 2014-06-18T16:42:13.5520000Z)

End Date: indicates when the license expires
(example: 2014-06-26T16:42:13.5520000Z)

3. That is because, for multiple users, on the same PC (for example a Windows Server with an RDS role enabled) can obtain their own licenses concurrently and the Office client adjusts its activation state to each individual user.

 No, as the article mentions, it doesn’t count against the five computer limit.

What are the 3 engines which Onenote uses in OCR for pictures - Microsoft Community

murali r say:

What are the 3 engines which Onenote uses in OCR for pictures

What are the 3 engines which Onenote uses in OCR for pictures What are the 3 engines which Onenote uses in OCR for pictures
somasundaram pandiaraj say:

The three OCR engines OneNote uses are:

1. Scansoft engine that shipped previously with Microsoft Office Document Imaging - Designed for black and white high contrast high DPI images

2. Screen clipping engine - Designed for color, low dpi images that contain text and images with variable contrast

3. Low resolution engine - Designed for low resolution images like those taken from a camera phone
