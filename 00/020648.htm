multicolumn endnotes - Microsoft Community

jonathan handel (la) say:

multicolumn endnotes

Hi -

I had a question but finally found an answer myself, so I thought I'd share it, since I hadn't seen it addressed in any of the articles or threads.

I'm using Word 2003, but wouldn't be surprised if the following also applies to Word 2007 and 2010.

I'd like my endnotes (not footnotes) to appear in multiple columns. I figured out how to do this, but there's a blank line that appears in front of my first endnote that I can't seem to delete.

Here's what I typed:

-----Section Break (Continuous)------
-----Page Break---------
                                                                        Chapter 9
blah blah blah
-----Section Break (Continuous)------
-----Page Break---------
                                                                         Endnotes
-----Section Break (Continuous)------
-----Page Break---------
                                                                         Index
[index appears here]

(How do you get the Index to appear after the Endnotes? That's discussed in articles and threads on this site.)

Note that my chapter/section titles are centered. Also, note that for some reason, I can't get Section Break (New Page) to work, so my simple workaround is Section Break (Continuous) followed by Page Break.

Here's what gets displayed in Page View

[Page Break appears here]
                                                                        Chapter 9
blah blah blah
[Page Break appears here]
                                                                         Endnotes

                                                 27 Doe (1985)
1 Smith (2012)                      28 Schmoe (1967)
2 Jones (1996)
3 Rogers (2002)
4 [etc]

So, the problem is that Endnote 1 has two blank lines above it, whereas Endnote 27 has only one blank line above it. Is there any way to fix this? Yes.

I find that if (in Normal view) I go to the Page Break that precedes the Index, and set Line Spacing to 0.06 lines (the minimum), the unwanted line break (when viewed in Page View mode) becomes essentially invisible. This is odd because it implies that the Endnotes appear after the Page Break, whereas they actually appear before it. But it works. Now the Smith endnote appears to be on virtually the same line as the Doe endnote.

Now, there is another problem that can arise. If your Endnote numbering restarts after each chapter, then you'll want to put Chapter breaks in your Endnotes, like this:

                                                                         Endnotes

                                                 10 Doe (1985)
Chapter 1                               11 Schmoe (1967)
1 Smith (2012)
2 Jones (1996)
3 Rogers (2002)
4 [etc]

Chapter 2
1 [etc.]

Now there will be an optical problem. The "10" before "Doe" is a superscript, which will make "Chapter 1" appear to be lower than the Doe footnote. There's no direct solution, because (as others on this site have explained) there's no way to make the numbers in the Endnote section (such as the "10") not be superscripts while keeping the numbers in the body itself as superscripts.

But the workaround is to put "Chapter 1," "Chapter 2," etc. in larger, bold type. That's reasonable and desirable (unless someone's dissertation committee objects), because these are subheadings -- and it eliminates the optical disparity.

BTW, even the single blank line after the heading "Endnotes" seems impossible to eliminate, but this isn't a problem, since spacing is desirable after the heading.

Hope this helps somebody!

Jonathan Handel

Hi IsaacFL,

Is the spam filtering that you mention here refers to the junk mail filtering?

Have you set your junk mail filter to exclusive?

 

One good way to eliminate if not minimize junk emails is to set your junk mail filtering to exclusive.  Here are the steps on how to do that:

 

1. Sign in to your Windows Live Hotmail account.

2. In the upper-right corner of the page, click Options, and then click More options.

3. Under preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.

4. Under Filters and reporting, select the options that you want, and then click Save.

 

Note: If you choose Exclusive for your junk e-mail filter, only messages from e-mail addresses on your Contacts or allowed senders lists appears in your Inbox. For this reason, I suggest that you check your junk e-mail filter from your Windows Live Hotmail.
