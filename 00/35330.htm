canadian tech say:

It is common for people to label their LCD screens, as LED.  Yours is actually an LCD.  LCD stands for Liquid Crystal Display.  LED stands for Light Emitting Diode.

LCD screens made when yours was, had a fluorescent back-light inside.  That kind of backlight fails after a while.  In your case, 7 years is quite an old age.  Sometime not long after yours was manufactured, the makers switched to LED lights.  So, newer ones rarely fail in the way yours has.

You need to replace your screen.  Currently screens that are the most popular and best priced are sized at 23" to 24".  Those sizes are diagonal measurements.

Keep in mind that 17" in your old screen's case and the newer 23" are very different.  Your old one was designed in what is called the 4 by 3 format.  All the new ones are 16 by 9.  That means you need something closer to 23" to get the same height as your old 17" one.
