Those numbered items, 1 & 2, were not meant to be alternatives.  Rather, both are required for this approach.  I'm sorry if my wording misled you.  You need to *both* set up the form's recordsource to use the combo box as a criterion (which you appear to have done correctly) *and* requery the form in the combo box's AfterUpdate event.  Did you do both, or only one at a time?

Excel Formula - Microsoft Community

tenndave38301 say:

Excel Formula

I need a formula that takes the last 10 values in a row, eliminates the highest two and the lowest two values and averages the middle six values
amit tandon say:

Same formulas, updated to make them amenable to adding row / column in range:

Average last 10 values in a column ($A$1:$A$20), excluding the 2 highest & 2 lowest values:

=TRIMMEAN(INDEX($A$1:$A$20, LARGE(IF($A$1:$A$20<>"", ROW($A$1:$A$20)-ROW($A$1)+1, ""), 10)):INDEX($A$1:$A$20, MATCH(9.9E+307,$A$1:$A$20)),0.4)

Average last 10 values in a row ($A$1:$T$1), excluding the 2 highest & 2 lowest values:

=TRIMMEAN(INDEX($A$1:$T$1, LARGE(IF($A$1:$T$1<>"",COLUMN($A$1:$T$1)-COLUMN($A$1)+1, ""),10)):INDEX($A$1:$T$1, MATCH(9.9E+307,$A$1:$T$1)),0.4)

Enter both formulas as array formulas (CTRL+SHIFT+ENTER).

how do i insert crop marks for the printer? - Microsoft Community

meimeinz say:

how do i insert crop marks for the printer?

My printer is asking for crop marks and 3mm bleed. does anyone know how this is done?
john mcghie say:
With Adobe inDesign.

Your printer is being hard to live with.  If he needs crop marks and bleeds, his machine can generate them for you with the click of a button.

What I think he's trying to do is to "Up-sell" you to more expensive "Pre-Press" services, that I am willing to bet quite a lot of money that you do not need!

You "can" do this with the ColorSync Utility which is in your Applications/Utilities folder on your Mac.  But you need to know exactly what you are doing, because it will ask you to make a series of settings that are all inter-related and you need to know what values you will need, which vary with the printer, paper, and ink used (and the temperature and humidity of the day if we want to be really pedantic).  Your printer could do all this for you, in a couple of minutes.  But he's spent a lifetime learning how and he knows his equipment :-)

I would try another printer.  A commercial printer should not require either crop marks or bleeds for typical small business print runs.  Anything under about 20,000 sheets they are going to run off on a machine that is effectively a high-speed laser printer.  These do not need any bleeds or crops or separations.  My friend who IS a printer would run anything up to about a quarter-million sheets on a fast laser printer. 

If you ARE going for a huge print run and do need accurate colour-reproduction, then you should retain a Pre-Press Bureau (separate from the printing company) to do this for you.  They will hand you a PDF file, already cropped and colour-separated with bleeds added.  The full job will be quite expensive (Pre-Press can run a few thousand dollars, depending on your requirements including number of pages, paper stock, colour accuracy, number of colours used and the output technology involved).

You have to be doing frighteningly-large print runs to justify the cost of making up offset plates in four colours for every page (each plate is about $5.00, you need four per side, so $40 a page; the Pre-Press is about the same: you can be getting into serious money).  Oh:  Unless you are making posters, where you want a large number of only one or two pages: then it might be worth going down this path.

I have a feeling that if you really needed all of this you would not be asking in here.  You would already know that Word for Mac does not have these functions, and you would not be trying to use Word 2008 when Word 2011 would do a better job (but still does not have these functions).

So I would be asking at another printer, if I were you.

If I have misunderstood, post back with a more extensive description of the job you have in mind; it makes it easier to give you a definitive answer.

Cheers
