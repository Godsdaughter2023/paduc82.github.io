maxwellsteele say:

This one is a pretty easy fix.  There are two ways to do this.  Both methods require that you sign into your "hotmail" or online "outlook" accounts online (meaning via a web browser).

Method 1 (preferred): After signing in, click on the preferences/options gear button at the top right.  Click "Options".  A list will open (usually on the left hand side).  Find "Junk E-mail" section and click on "Safe Senders".  Here you can add either an individual email address (myfriendatwhatever.com) or if you are a forum user and want ALL mail (the usual case) then just add the domain (whatever.com).  Don't use the @ symbol for the domain entry.  That's it...all mail will now go to the inbox rather than junk.  This only needs to be done once for each individual or domain.

Method 2: After signing in, find the mail item that was sent to junk and right click on it and select Create Rule. Here you can easily create a rule that moves all mail from that sender to whatever mail folder you like.

Finally:

  • The first method is preferred and is exactly the same way Microsoft Outlook (the actual mail program) handles junk email...by setting up a safe sender for the odd time mail is incorrectly sent to junk.
  • Microsoft really SHOULD make these option adjustments available from within the mail program on our PC's rather than having to sign on to do them.  It is a shortcoming of the MS Mail program.
  • I would despise having to add a contact within the mail program just to get this done...they're not a "proper" contact.  Hence, use one of the two rules above.
