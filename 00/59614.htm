4 (optional): The type of .DMP files located in the Minidump folder are known as Small Memory Dumps. In %systemroot% there will be what is known as a Kernel-Dump (if your system is set to generate). It is labeled MEMORY.DMP. The difference between Small Memory Dumps and Kernel-Dumps in the simplest definition is a Kernel-Dump contains much more information at the time of the crash, therefore allowing further debugging of your issue. If your upload speed permits it, and you aren't going against any strict bandwidth and/or usage caps, etc, the Kernel-Dump is the best choice. Do note that Kernel-Dumps are much larger in size due to containing much more info, which is why I mentioned upload speed, etc.

  6. Click or tap OK to confirm.

Option 2: Completely reset and restore Power Plans to default.

  1. Open a command prompt.

  3. Close the command prompt when you're done.

arminatorx say:

You say your son is already added to your parental account on https://account.microsoft.com/family, right?

If so, please go to https://account.xbox.com/settings and sign in with your parent account. 

You should see your son's account to be selectable. 

Click on your son's account and change his online and privacy settings. 

You will have three categories: Privacy, Xbox One/Windows 10 Online Safety and Xbox 360 Online Safety.  

On the Privacy Tab, set the settings to what you want your son to be able to do online in general. But to be able to visit realms or other online players, he would have to be able to "See and upload community creations" (at least Friend's creations, if playing on public servers he'll need the permission for everyone).

Save the settings you made on the Privacy tab, before you click on the Xbox One/Windows 10 Online Safety settings. 

There you need to allow at least the ability to "join multiplayer games".

Also save the settings. 

If your son was signed in on the Laptop while the changes were made, he needs to sign out of the laptop and then sign back in. 

Then your son should be able to play Minecraft online on the PC.

If you need to change your online and privacy settings in order to play Minecraft on a realm or servers, you'll have to go to https://account.xbox.com/settings. 

There you can then make the necessary settings. 

If you don't see a table with settings, but a page explaining how to add a child account to a parent account, you have a child account.

After your account was added to your parent's family account, the parent has to go to https://account.xbox.com/settings and sign in with their parent account. Then they select your child account and make the necessary changes for you.
