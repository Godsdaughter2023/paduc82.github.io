The problem is this -

Yammer is designed for internal company use.

To identify you as an employee, Yammer uses your corporate email address. If you do not possess the same corporate email address as the rest of the employees, Yammer treats you as a non-employee. 


So usually in the setup, an administrator will add all related organisation email domains (that are allowed to access the specific Yammer network) into the admin panel. So admins do not want to do that, as the Network may be exclusive to a particular subsidiary or country office.

However, in circumstances where the company wants to admit outsiders to the specific company Yammer network, such as external directors..etc, they can add them as guests.

What you need to do is to get the owner of the International GT network to add you as a guest.

If he has done it, then when you login to your GT Israel Yammer network, when you click on top left Gear Icon, and a window will pop up and you should be able to see additional networks you can access.

Clutter Folder Not Visible from Either OWA or Outlook - Microsoft Community

dwaynemonroe- [o365] say:

Clutter Folder Not Visible from Either OWA or Outlook

Recently I enabled the clutter feature for a user.  After a week or so of use, the clutter folder disappeared from both Outlook (2010) and OWA.

Of course, this has become bothersome since there is mail within this folder he can no longer see.

Any troubleshooting suggestions?

alanwu_msft say:

Hi Dwayne,

 

For this situation, please try to reconfigure your Office 365 account in your Outlook 2010 with a new profile and check the result.

1.      Sign into your Outlook 2010.

2.      Delete the ost file of your previous profile for avoiding a interference of your new profile. Click “FILE”> “Account Settings”> “Data Files”> “Open File Location”> “Delete the ost file”.

3.      Re-configure your Office 365 account via “Exchange Autodiscover”. Click “Control Panel”> “User Accounts”> “Mail”> “Show Profiles”> “Add a profile”> “Type your new profile name”> “Type your email address and password”> “OK”.

 

Moreover, you can search the missing emails via “eDiscovery”. Note: The following steps should be performed by an admin of your organization. If you are not an admin, please ask for help from your admin or involve your admin in the post.

1.      Assign “Discovery Management” role for an admin account.

1)      Sign in to Exchange Admin Center (EAC) with an admin account.

2)      Click “permissions”> “admin roles”> “Discovery Management”> “Members”> “Add one of the admin accounts in”> “Save”.

2.      Perform “eDiscovery” for the missing emails.

 

Any update is appreciated. We are glad to provide further help if it is needed.

 

Regards,

Alan
