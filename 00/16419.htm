The fault lies not in the formula but in the data.

Before the D of Director there are two hard-spaces (ANSI 16) and you are looking for a normal space (ANSI 32) This often happened when data is taken from the web.

In my screenshot I convert the double 160 to a single 32 (two ways are shown)

In B2, the formula ends quote space space quote, quote space quote to convert double spaces to single.

Then I use formulas to display the two strings of text

Of course, you now have formulas but I expect you really want text

Select C2:D2

Right click on the selection and choose Copy
Right click on the selection and choose Paste Special
Check "Values"
OK
Use Esc to clear the clipboard

Now you can discard columns A and B

takmilulihsan say:

Hi,

I'm assuming your data are in column A, starting from A2. I'll use some helper columns to keep it simple.

In B2 enter this formula and copy down: =TRIM(SUBSTITUTE(A2,CHAR(160),CHAR(32)))
In C2 enter this formula and copy down: =FIND(" ",B2,FIND(" ",B2,1)+1)
In D2 enter this formula and copy down: =LEFT(B2,C2) [extracts text before the second space]
In E2 enter this formula and copy down: =RIGHT(B2,LEN(B2)-C2) [extracts text after the second space]

Copy columns D and E and paste as values. You can then remove the helper columns.

BTW, If you have Excel 2013 or newer, also give flash fill a try. If there is a pattern in the data, Excel will fill in the data according to the pattern identified.

Hi,

Try these steps- test after each.

1. Shut down the computer, modem and router for a few minutes.

2. Internet Options> Browsing History> Delete> (next screen) uncheck "Preserve Favorites website data." then delete the History, Temporary Internet Files and Cookies. Restart.

3. From Start> All programs. Accessories, right-click Command Prompt and choose "Run as administrator". At the flashing cursor type inipconfig /flushdns (note the space between config and / ) hit [Enter] then Restart.

4. If amazon is listed in either Trusted Sites or Restricted Sites, remove it (Internet Options> Security.)

5. Go to Internet Options> Privacy and try a cookie setting of Low or Accept all.

6. Verify that the computer's date, time and time zone are correct. These settings can affect cookie handling.

7. If you have a program running that has a cookie manager feature (CA, CCleaner, etc.) disable that feature.

8. With the warning that a reset will delete AutoComplete data, such as passwords and you will have to reinstall or re-enable browser add-ons, use the Reset Internet Explorer Settings button (Internet Options> Advanced.)
