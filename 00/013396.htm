swischner say:

I would not recommend trying to synchronize .ONE and .ONETOC files with a storage provider like Google Drive, Dropbox or the like.

They are lacking the (delta-) synchronising mechanism which is on OneDrive, so every little change would transfer the WHOLE file. Even worse: concurrent edits almost certainly result in conflicts (double files with different filenames, as this is what dropbox for example does) and therefore doubled content in OneNote.

Simply put: OneDrive "knows" what a OneNote Notebook is (and its contents), for every other cloud storage service its just a bunch of files.

Omer Atay, one of the OneNote developers and creator of the great Onetastic addin explains this very well: http://omeratay.com/onetastic/?i=onenote-sync

Stefan Wischner

macropod say:

This is possibly due to the table properties rather than being anything to do with the content control. If the table cell has a preferred width, the content control cannot expand beyond the cell margin. If you're using a plain text content control, the properties can also be set to allow or disallow paragraph breaks.
