Subtracting times with fractional seconds - Microsoft Community

dickhess say:

Subtracting times with fractional seconds

I want to find the time difference when times are in the format hh:mm:ss.000. This is a custom format, but the subtraction returns #VALUE. Excel doesn't appear to recognize the input values.

bernie deitrick say:

It is probable that your times are actually strings that look like times.  Times, in Excel, are decimal values, the fractional part of 24 hours, with 24 hours being 1 (thus each day is an integer value, leading to how Excel handles dates).  

If you apply a different numeric format and the display does not change, then the value is actually a string.  In that case, you need to apply the custom format hh:mm:ss.000  to all the cells, and then reenter all the values by entering 1 into another cell, copying that cell, then selecting all the cells with times and using pastespecial / multiply.

Still, entering decimal seconds is dodgy in Excel, because Excel really likes things to the nearest second - if you ever press F2 / enter the decimals will be dropped.  So you would be better off entering just seconds with the decimal values, and modifying your math.  Subtraction works the same, just with seconds...

Converting multi day time intervals into quarter hour intervals - Microsoft Community

kubaszmigielski say:

Converting multi day time intervals into quarter hour intervals

I have spreadsheets that measure depth of water in a well at 10 sec, 30 sec, 1 min and 5 min intervals for multiple days. I am interested in simplifying these spreadsheets into 15 minute intervals ie. 1:00:00pm 1:15:00pm etc.. Since the spreadsheets may not contain cells exactly on the quarter hour increments I would like to grab the associated depth of water value from the nearest time available. Any suggestions please and thank-you??  

bernie deitrick say: Say that your date/time values are in column A. and the measured values are in column B, and are sorted so that the date/time increase down column A. 

In C1, enter the date and time for the starting time of interest, and in C2, enter the formula
=$C$1 + 15*ROW(A1)/1440
and in D2, enter the formula
=VLOOKUP(C2,A:B,2)
and copy down until the formula return in D begins to repeat (meaning the date/time in C is greater than the max in A) - you can also modify the formula to check for that.
