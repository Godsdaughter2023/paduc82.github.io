Change backup email on my Microsoft Account in win10pro - Microsoft Community

philosophiae say:

Change backup email on my Microsoft Account in win10pro

I have recently changed my internet provider and subsequently my email.  The email was discontinued.  Unfortunately it was my backup email on my Microsoft Account.  How do I change backup email on my Microsoft Account in Windows 10 Pro?

cmdrkeene say:

There's a few ways.  You could open an entirely different Microsoft Account using your new email address (but that means any purchases/games/backups tied to your old username/email will be gone).  So better is to create an alias in your existing account.

1)  Go to https://account.live.com/names/Manage

2)  Add your new email address to the list as an alias

Then if you want, you can sign-into Windows 10 using that new alias instead.  To do that:

1)  Go to Settings/Accounts

2)  Click "Sign in with a local account instead" and set it up

3)  Go back to Settings, click "Sign in with a Microsoft Account" and sign in using your new email alias.


It'll still be the same account, just with a new username (your new email address).  That way you don't lose anything tied to your existing account.

My quiver is almost empty . . .

It is a little unclear from your post whether you did actually have access to your hard disk or not while in Repair Mode. If so then the process below lets you restore a backup set of your registry files. The process is safe because it is completely reversible. It is based on a set of Console commands but since you are not familiar with them I shall spell them out in detail. Just be careful with your typing and don't take any liberties unless you know what you're doing.

1. Keep tapping F8 during the early boot phase.
2. Select Repair from the menu.
3. Log on as Administrator if prompted.
4. Select Repair when prompted.
5. Select the Command Prompt.
6. Identify the correct drive letter for your Windows installation. It could be a drive other than C: but it won't be X:. You do it by typing the commands
    dir C:\Win*
    dir D:\Win*
    dir E:\Win*
    until you find the Windows folder, e.g. D:\Windows. I will call that drive "Q:".
7. Type these commands:
    cd /d Q:\windows\System32\config
    xcopy *.* Q:\RegBack\
    cd RegBack
    dir
8. Examine the dates of the Software, System and SAM files. Were the files created before or after your problem became apparent?
9. If they were made before the problem became apparent then you can type these commands:
    copy /y software  ..
    copy /y System  ..
    copy /y Sam ..
    (the two dots are part of each command)
10. Reboot normally. If things do not work out then you can backtrack by copying the original registry files from Q:\Regback to Q:\Windows\System32\config.
