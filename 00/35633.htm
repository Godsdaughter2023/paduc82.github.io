sanmartin2016 say:

I suppose you get this error when trying to launch the game via Steam, not when downloading a recently bought new game. If that's right, the most likely causes are:

1) Your video card / driver doesn't support some needed rendering features (only an example which should be obsolete on today's hardware: no shader 3.0 support, limited to shader 2.0). Those issues can be avoided by updating to the latest driver version. Sometimes it could be enough if you simply uninstall and reinstall your current driver, only to make sure that the game recognizes your (new) video card and doesn't apply settings from another (old) video card.

2) Even if it seems so, you can't change all settings by the game launcher alone. You must allow permanent changes to your SkyrimPrefs.ini first (the one in your \My Documents\My Games\Skyrim folder, not the one in your game folder); to do so, make sure that the file isn't set to "read only", or any changes made in the launcher won't be stored.

3) Make sure that your in-game resolution fits your monitor's resolution.

4) Make sure that your new computer also has a complete DirectX 9.0c distribution installed (should come with the game, can be downloaded from MSFT). This doesn't happen automatically when you switch to a new OS. It is needed, however, for a lot of games.

BTW: As you can see, none of these issues are related to Windows 10. Those are associated with hardware changes and game adaptation to them which posed the very same problems on Vista and 7.
