john w. vinson say:

You can do this with the help of an auxiliary table containing a record for every date; you can create this easily in Excel using Fill Down for a range of dates (be generous, 100 years is only 36525 records). Create a Query joining this table to your data table with a Left Join - it will contain a record for every date, with a NULL for the tips on that date. You can include a field for holidays or days when you're closed and omit these holidays if you wish.

You really need to reconsider your table design, however. Storing data - a month - in the table name is bad design and will come back to bite you (at tax time, for example, when you'll want to sum records for the entire year). You should have just ONE table of sales info; you can easily create queries to group this by month or by year. I'd also suggest changing the name of the field Date - that's a reserved word for the builtin Date() function and can cause errors.

There was a Microsoft update to Excel 2010 in December that caused ActiveX controls to stop working.

serds_p say:

Hi,

I'm sorry to hear about your experience with Skype. Let me help you.

If you are using the classic version of Skype on Mac, you have to delete the generated chat conversation with the specific contact you called to have the logs deleted. You will also see an option to delete the entire chat history with all your contacts to make it easier for you to delete the generated conversation from your recent chats list. To do this, select Skype > Preferences and then select Privacy, from there you will see "Delete All Chat History..."

For more details on how to manage your conversation history, including steps on how to delete conversation history, click this link: https://support.skype.com/en/faq/FA10888/ 

If you are using Skype version 8.27, there is no option to delete the call logs from the "Calls" list. The only option you have is to delete the generated chat conversation from your Chats list. From your chats list, click on the name of the contact or the number you called. Click on the name of your contact or the phone number for a new window to appear. Scroll down and find "delete conversation" to delete the entire chat history and call logs with that contact/phone number.

I hope that helps.
