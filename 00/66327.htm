First of all, I was incorrect.  The problem service is the PML Driver HPZ12, but it appears in the Services list of msconfig under its own name (not as Net Driver HPZ12, which is what I wrote earlier).  I apologize for the error.  The linked article, however, had the name of the service and its registry key correct.

If not, you must have another service that is misbehaving in the same way.  Because I have never come across any other such service, you'll have to find it by trial and error.  Uncheck (disable) half of the services shown in the msconfig Services tab and reboot.  If you don't get the error, then the misbehaving service is in the half you left active; if you do get the error, it's in the half you unchecked.

Narrow down by halves until you find the culprit.

Now start up regedit and go to

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\System\CurrentControlSet\Services\<name of service you identified>

and follow the directions in the article to edit it.
