larryyu_msft3110 say:

Hi WilliamLogan,

This error message occurs usually because we don’t have permission to send emails use the specified user. In this case, the error occurs because outlook.com email account and Office 365 email account are two different types of accounts, you cannot send emails by changing the From email address to an outlook.com email account in an Office 365 email account. This is the same reason that why you cannot add the outlook.com account in the Exchange admin center->recipients.

However, if you want to use your Office 365 account and outlook.com account at the same time, there are two ways for your reference:

1. Configure your outlook.com account as an addition email account on your Outlook client:

https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Add-your-Outlook-com-account-in-Outlook-for-Windows-642c1902-bdd9-4dc3-abe7-76d60b148b23

After that, your outlook.com email account will displays under your Office 365 email account in the Outlook client.

2. If you use OWA (Outlook Web App), you can add your outlook.com email account as a connected account to your Office 365 email account by referring to the article below:

https://support.office.com/en-my/article/Connect-email-accounts-in-Outlook-on-the-web-d7012ff0-924f-4f78-8aca-c3912d886c4d

After that, emails send to your outlook.com account will be sent to your Office 365 account as well. You can read and reply emails in Office 365 account only.

If anything is unclear, feel free to let us know.

Thanks,
Larry

How can I delete a "Table Name" from a workbook? - Microsoft Community

stephanie_gina_white say:

How can I delete a "Table Name" from a workbook?

Name Manager displays all named ranges and Table Names, the latter created by the ListObjects.Add command.  However when copying the range that was defined by the Table Name, it created a SECOND Table Name (e.g. "Table1(2)").

The question is how to delete this second Table Name as is shows up in the Name Manager as a Table Name and not as a Defined Name?

stephanie_gina_white say:

Well it is ugly, but it worked:

 

Dim xxx As Name
For Each w_wsht In ThisWorkbook.Worksheets
    For Each xxx In w_wsht.Names
         xxx.Delete
    Next xxx
Next w_wsht

After running the above, closing and opening the workbook, I received an error about the workbook.  By accepting it and opening the workbook the Table Names are gone.  Looking at it now I could have deleted the offending Table Name directly.  Oh well, live and learn.

I have several spreadsheets that contain numerous alphanumeric items and in several cells to the right  the number of those items.

 

           A          F

e.g.  P1234      5

 

The items can occur numerous times in a sheet but cannot be moved to bring them together. Is there a way to enter P1234 as a search item and be given the total units required (B) in the whole sheet.

N.B. This is not just a count of how many times P1234 occurs in the sheet.

 

Hope you can help

paulcordts say: SUMIF should work

The syntax is SUMIF(range, criteria, [sum_range])
 
from you example the indicator is in column A and the number to total is in column F
=SUMIF(A1:A10,"P1234",F1:F10)

This will add up the values in F1:F10 only where A1:A10="P1234"

paulcordts say: Ferdy2,

2 things 

1) Please mark this answered so anyone else searching will be able to find in the answered section.

2) Yes, you can summarize all the indicator values.  The trick is to get a listing of all the unique indicators.
There are two way to do this.  Neither are 100% automatic since you have to do a little work when data is updated

A - Copy the list and use the Sort and Remove Duplicate function in the Data menu.  

B - Use a formula to get the unique values.  Chip Pearson has a method posted on his site
http://www.cpearson.com/Excel/ListFunctions.aspx

Then use the SUMIF function for each item.

You should also check out Chip's sections on Array Formulas.  They can do lots of very interesting things that you cannot do with standard Excel formulas.
