how do i get rid of #VALUE! in a cell with a formula when the - Microsoft Community

laura l.salazar say:

how do i get rid of #VALUE! in a cell with a formula when the other cells are blank.

it's a simple formula to obtain the product of two cells but when the cells are blank, the #VALUE! is displayed.
joeu2004 say: Laura wrote:
it's a simple formula to obtain the product of two cells but when the cells are blank, the #VALUE! is displayed.

It one or both cells were truly empty, the product would result in zero, not a #VALUE error.

Instead, I presume one or both cells contain the null string (""), which appears blank.  Even then, PRODUCT(A1,B1) would return zero.  So I presume your formula is of the form =A1*B1.

In that case, you could write =N(A1)*N(B1).  Alternatively, you could use the PRODUCT function.  Or you could write:

=IF(OR(A1="",B1=""),0,A1*B1)

It is difficult to find the Help page for the N() function in XL2003.  I have to search for "text and data functions", click on the T function, then click on See Also.

john korchok say:

Sorry, while it is possible to set the z-order (stacking) of PowerPoint shapes, all these objects are on the same layer. PowerPoint doesn't have a multi-layer feature and so is unable to export layers to any other format.

You could save to PDF, open that PDF in Illustrator or InDesign, create layers and export to a layered PDF. But then why not just create the graphics in one of those programs and skip PowerPoint?

Or, if you have Adobe Acrobat 9 or better, you can open a PDF saved from PowerPoint and create layers using it. There are also some other PDF editors that can create layers, like PDF Studio.

You could copy and paste a PowerPoint diagram to Microsoft Visio, if you have that software, then create layers. Visio has a layers feature and can export them to PDF if you have Adobe Acrobat 6 or better.
