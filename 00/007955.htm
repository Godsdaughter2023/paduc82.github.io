We've seen a number of reports of this recently for both Defender and MSE.

A recent post by mchampfl on page 11 of this long running thread - http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/protect/forum/mse-protect_scanning/microsoft-security-essentials-error-code/d8d69ac3-ca4a-40ae-9c0d-15c97e64cea3?page=11&tm=1441106019496 - suggests that MSE is having problems with large compressed/zipped archive files due to the fact that MSE needs to open these in memory to scan them.  The crash, resulting in 0x800106ba can be avoided, per that reply, by disabling the scanning of compressed archives in MSE settings.

Unfortunately, there is no corresponding setting in Defender on Windows 10. You could try excluding a variety of compressed file types such as ISO, CAB, RAR from being scanned, but I don't recommend that. You could exclude locations where large compressed files reside, such as your recovery partition.

On the other hand --

As long as real time protection is enabled, a full scan on a frequent basis is usually overkill. A quick scan looks for malware in the most common locations. A full scan has to touch every file. Malware found in a full scan, not in a quick scan is typically not active. Personally, I only ever run a full scan if an infection has been detected or if one is suspected.  I use a different scanner for the full scan (the malware was missed when it arrived, so why should I expect it to find it on a full scan?)

andrea jones - all about resources say:

You could insert a new column to the right of your numbers and format the blank cells as text.  Now enter the formula

="0"&A2

where A2 is the cell containing the old mobile number and copy down.  You can transfer the resulting values to a new column formatted as text by copying and using Paste Special to paste the values.

hansv mvp say:

You could apply a custom number format 0000000000 with the appropriate number of zeros to the column.

This will only alter the display though, not the stored values.

 

Alternatively, select the data and run the following macro:

 

Sub PrefixZero()
    Dim rng As Range
    For Each rng In Selection.SpecialCells(xlCellTypeConstants)
        rng.Value = "'0" & rng.Value
    Next rng
End Sub

omar10594 say:

Migrating from Microsoft Teams free edition to O365 if we use G Suite microsoft accounts.

The company that I work for uses G Suite.  We want to try Microsoft teams free edition so we will create new microsoft account with our accounts of google (*** Email address is removed for privacy ***).

If in the future we want to migrate from G Suite to Office 365, we will be able to create our accounts with the same email and use on microsoft teams?

For example, i have my email *** Email address is removed for privacy *** on gsuite, i create a new microsoft account with that email to use Microsoft Teams free edition, then the company migrate to Office 365 for Business and they will try to create my account again (*** Email address is removed for privacy ***) on office 365.

There will be two accounts with the same email?, they can be merged?, we will no be able to create the accounts on office 365 because they already will exists?

anna ma msft say:

Hi omar10594,

Yes, there will be two account with the same email address. They can’t be merged, you can still create accounts on Office 365.

In my view, your account is G suite account, when sign-up Teams for free, as you have mentioned above, Microsoft will create a new Microsoft account with the same address so you can access Teams for free and set it up.

If you migrate to Office 365 for Business, you are in a new Office 365 tenant with the default domain (***.onmicrosoft.com), your default account address should be user@***.onmicrosoft.com. Your admin can move the custom domain from G suite to Office 365, in this way your new Office 365 account address can be the same as original.

Microsoft account and Office 365 account are different type of accounts and provide different services. So they will not merge, you can still be able to create accounts in Office 365. Office 365 for Business provide Teams for Business version, so you can use your Office 365 credential to login Teams, it will automatically identity your account type and navigate you to the Organization Teams.
