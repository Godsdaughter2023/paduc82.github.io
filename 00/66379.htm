OEM drivers do not follow the same track as the generic releases, so they will not be automatically installed, unless forced.   While generic releases are not tested for your particular hardware revision, not many options are available, when newer drivers are no longer provided by your OEM.  Sometimes contacting them and requesting a newer release than what is on their website, might prove fruitful.  In the case of the Intel drivers, the generic releases closely matching your particular hardware, work in the majority.  If you run into complications, you can either roll back your drivers or try an earlier generic release.

You will tend to receive 3 prompts similar to the one shown below:

To remove the prompts from startup, you have the option to either:

1.  Uncheck "Always ask before opening this file" for all three of them.

These prompts occur as a warning since these files were originally downloaded from the Internet and might be considered "unsafe".  In this particular case, these files are official and expected.  The constant warning prompt is completely unnecessary.

2.  Stop these applications from executing at startup.

Unchecking one or more of the Intel startup items, is one way to stop them from executing.  If you are trying to minimize your constant memory consumption, you can uncheck them.  If not, you can leave them.  They do provide some functionality and may cause some minor annoyances or inconveniences, if not left running.

These 3 items provide things like an icon in your system tray to quickly manage your graphics settings (igfxtray.exe), the support of hot keys that control display changes (hkcmd.exe), and the ability to retain resolution configurations when monitors are disconnected then reconnected (igfxpers.exe).

Windows 10 laptop battery icon missing from taskbar since last update - Microsoft Community

phuhn say:

Windows 10 laptop battery icon missing from taskbar since last update

Within the last 10 day, I installed an update and since then the battery icon in the task-bar disappeared.  I just when into the configuration to correct the problem and the turn-on slider is disabled.  How to correct?

andre da costa say:

Press Windows key + X
Click Device Manager

Expand Battery

Right click your ACPI compliant battery
Click Disable
Right click it then click Enable again

Restart then check if you can turn on the slider in Taskbar Settings.

You can also refer this support article:

https://support.microsoft.com/en-in/help/4027340/windows-10-add-the-battery-icon-to-the-taskbar
