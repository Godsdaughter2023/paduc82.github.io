jllatham say:

A couple of relatively easy to implement and understand ways:

First way would be to set up a Sheet with the individual possible entries (HD, PSD, PS, AV and DSD) and set up a COUNTIF() formula for each data sheet.  Assume that your entries is on the data sheets in A2:A17, and on the summary sheet you make the entries into A2:A6.  For 3 data sheets we would have formulas like:

    A            B (formula shown for 3 sheets)
1  ITEM       Total Count
2    HD        =COUNTIF(Sheet2!A$2:A$17,A2)+COUNTIF(Sheet3!A$2:A$17,A2)+COUNTIF(Sheet4!A$2:A$17,A2)
3   PSD       =COUNTIF(Sheet2!A$2:A$17,A3)+COUNTIF(Sheet3!A$2:A$17,A3)+COUNTIF(Sheet4!A$2:A$17,A3)
4     PS       =COUNTIF(Sheet2!A$2:A$17,A4)+COUNTIF(Sheet3!A$2:A$17,A4)+COUNTIF(Sheet4!A$2:A$17,A4)
5     AV       =COUNTIF(Sheet2!A$2:A$17,A5)+COUNTIF(Sheet3!A$2:A$17,A5)+COUNTIF(Sheet4!A$2:A$17,A5)
6    DSD      =COUNTIF(Sheet2!A$2:A$17,A6)+COUNTIF(Sheet3!A$2:A$17,A6)+COUNTIF(Sheet4!A$2:A$17,A6)

Now that could get to be a long formula if you have 20 data sheets to deal with, although extending it is just a matter of copying a section and changing the sheet name.

One alternative would be to set up a series of formulas on each data sheet to give you the total just for that sheet and then use a '3-D' formula to add all of those up.  For this example we assume the data sheet setup is with the individual entries in column A, and the summary for that sheet is set up in columns C and D like this:

       A            B            C          D
1                               Item       Total This Sheet
2                                  HD        =COUNTIF(A:A,C2)
3                                 PSD       =COUNTIF(A:A,C3)
4                                   PS       =COUNTIF(A:A,C4)
5                                   AV       =COUNTIF(A:A,C5)
6                                  DSD      =COUNTIF(A:A,C6)

Then back on your summary sheet you can have a setup like this:
       A             B
1   ITEM        Total All Sheets
2      HD          =SUM(Sheet2:Sheet4!D2)
3     PSD         =SUM(Sheet2:Sheet4!D3)
4       PS         =SUM(Sheet2:Sheet4!D4)
5       AV         =SUM(Sheet2:Sheet4!D5)
6     DSD         =SUM(Sheet2:Sheet4!D6)

In the SUM() formulas, you use the sheet names of the first and last data sheets.  The values on those 2 sheets and all sheets in between them will be totaled up for you.  Easier to maintain, since all you have to do is change the sheet names involved and use normal formula filling/copying methods to take care of the rest.

I'm sure there are other ways to do this, and no doubt someone else will offer another possible solution.
