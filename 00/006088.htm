How do I unlink footers in Word for Mac 2011 - Microsoft Community

bahman(2) say:

How do I unlink footers in Word for Mac 2011

I am trying to get the page numbering right and for that, I need to unlink each footer from the the previous. There is a perfect video for this but only for the Windows version: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TaEk_8I6vuY

Look at 2:04, that is what I want to do. There is a "Link to header/footer" button under "Header & Footer Tools".

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

It isn't clear what you mean by "get the page numbering right"-- What do you consider to be right? What about the current page numbering do you consider to be wrong?

Page number formatting isn't controlled by unlinking footers. It's a property of the page numbering controlled through the Insert> Page Numbers - Format dialog on a Section-by-Section basis. Not to say that it's wrong, but what's done in that video is excessive... There's no need to remove the Link to Previous check for Headers or Footers unless you want to vary the actual content in the footer of a Section. E.g., you may want to have certain text in the footer of one Section but have different/no text in the footer of other Sections or you want to have page numbers in some Sections but not in others.

In fact, the first Section Break she creates isn't even necessary... The same result can be achieved more efficiently by formatting the TABLE OF CONTENTS paragraph with the Page Break Before property & setting the Header/Footer property for that Section to Different First Page to start the page numbering on the 2nd physical page.

IOW, to do what is demonstrated in the video you need only;

  1. Have the appropriate Section Break where the formatting style of the numbering is to change
  2. Go into the footer of Section 1
  3. Check the Different First Page box
  4. Go to the footer of the 2nd page in that Section
  5. Use Insert> Page Numbers - Format to specify the settings
  6. Repeat Step 5 [only] in the footer of Section 2

The Header & Footer tab is contextual... It appears on the Ribbon only when you're active in the Header or Footer layer of a document.
