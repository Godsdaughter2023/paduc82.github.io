lemp say:

Number 1 (WPS2.0 HW PBC Service) is an Edimax service associated with your Edimax wifi adapter



Number 2 (Wired AutoConfig) is the built-in Windows service that is used with your wired (Ethernet) network adapter.  It first appeared in XP service pack 3.

Number 3 (Wireless Zero Configuration)
 is the built-in Windows service that configures your wireless adapter.  You can only have one service running to configure your wireless adapter:  either WZC or the Edimax service.

"WPS" stands for "WiFi Protected Setup" and "PBC" is Edimax's term for "Push Button Configuration."  Both of these relate to a method of configuring security for wireless networks that was designed to allow "home users who know little of wireless security and may be intimidated by the available security options" to set up security on their wireless networks.  Unfortunately, WPS was shown to have security holes (click the link to the Wikipedia article for details).

In any event, you do not need service number 1, especially once your network has been configured with appropriate encryption.  As for why installing an HP printer might have caused the problem, I can only speculate:  Is your printer a wireless printer?  If so, it very well may have included HP's version of WPS ... which may have somehow corrupted the Edimax installation.
