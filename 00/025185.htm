How can i avoid the first Tab, 0.25 inch, or set a new value for the first Tab?

john korchok say:

One or more of the TOC styles may contain an unwanted tab. In your first post, you referred to TOC-Styles, but I'm not sure if that's a custom style you created and are attempting to format your TOC with. Normally the styles TOC1, TOC2, etc. are automatically connected to the TOC, so you would be setting your tabs in those styles.

By default, the Normal style is the parent of TOC1. So if a 0.25" tab is set in Normal, it will flow through to TOC1. So start with these steps:

  1. With your document open in Word, right-click on Normal in the Quick Style Gallery and choose Modify.
  2. Check Add to template, so your change is added to the template and is available to future documents.
  3. Click on the Format button, then on Tabs...
  4. If you see a 0.25" tab, select it and click on the Clear button, then OK out. If there are no tabs, then Cancel out.

Then check the TOC1 style:

  1. Just to the right of the Quick Style Gallery is an icon with a backwards P. Click on it to open the Styles list.
  2. Change the List dropdown to All Styles.
  3. Scroll down to find TOC1, then click on the down-pointing arrowhead on the right side and choose Modify Styles.
  4. As you did earlier, check Add to template.
  5. Click on the Format button, then on Tabs...
  6. If you see a 0.25" tab, select it and click on the Clear button, then OK out. If there are no tabs, then Cancel out.

If there were not 0.25" tabs in your styles, then you're experiencing the Word bug I referred to. This article that I posted last time has the fix, adding a switch to the TOC field: Word: Getting rid of the ‘Jason tab’ in a TOC
