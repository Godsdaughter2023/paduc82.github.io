ianrg say:

As a result of removing the Display CP there are setting that are not currently possible (outside of Registry edits). These are not trivial or replaced settings but ones that can be necessary.

This is a bad and backwards change and has been discussed here.

However, the Personalisation CP being lost is not a problem. All the settings that are available in 1607 in the CP are available through Settings in 15031:

  • You can save the current background, colour, sounds and cursors as your current theme.
  • You can select the default Windows 10 and Flowers themes, get more themes on line or select a previously saved theme.
  • You can jump to where you can change the background picture, the colour, the sound, cursor and desktop icon settings (the last three still in Win 95 tabbed dialog box format).
  • The jump to Screen saver settings has moved to the lock screen setting page but is still there.
  • The jumps to the Display, Taskbar and navigation and ease of access settings app pages are not on the Theme settings app page but these pages are all still there. The 15031 display and ease of access pages do more than their equivalents in 1607 and there has been no loss of functionality between the two taskbar settings pages.

Initially Excel 2003 would truncate text that is too wide for a cell when printing, but with SP3 the extra characters are printed over top of characters in the next cell or cells (same for Excel 2010) .  How can I force the printing to end at the cell boundary?  It looks all right in Print preview - only the characters that fit are showing, but when printed the extra characters overlap with whatever is in the cell or cells to the right..  I don't want to use word wrap - there are several celIs with "excess" data, but I want to see just the amount of just the initial part of the text for such cells.
anula d'souza say:
Did you follow the option to use word wrap and change the row height to 15 so that the text will be truncated. For Excel 2010 you may follow the steps given below and check if it helps:
  • Select the entire sheet (ctrl+a).
  • Right anywhere on the sheet and choose the option Format Cells.
  • Select Alignment tab.
  • Under Text Control group check the option Word Wrap.
  • Select the entire sheet (ctrl+a).
  • Right anywhere on the sheet and choose the option Row Height.
  • Under Row Height enter 15 and click Ok.
