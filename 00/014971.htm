langware say: Deleting existing cookies will not stop sites from creating new cookies in your browser.

If you really want to stop cookies, then look for a setting in your browser that will disable all cookies. But be warned .... some sites will not work if cookies are disabled.

A better strategy is to enable the setting in your browser that clears all cookies when you close the browser. The only downside of that strategy is that you may have to re-enter information into your browser when you re-visit a site.

One other suggestion:  you can disable third party cookies with very little negative impact. Third party cookies are used (by web sites) to track your browsing and to display customized ads based on your browsing habits. If you disable third party cookies, then you just won't get customized ads (because the web sites will not be able to track your browsing). Your browser should have a separate setting to disable third party cookies.

Most browsers also have a "private" mode .... you can use that and cookies will not be saved.
