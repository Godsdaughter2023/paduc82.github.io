  1. Open Disk Cleanup by clicking the Start button. In the search box, type Disk Cleanup, and then, in the list of results, click Disk Cleanup.
  2. If you're prompted to choose a drive, click the drive you just installed Windows on, and then click OK.

  1. Click Start
  2. Click Computer
  3. In the address bar, paste this location: %userprofile%
  4. Press Enter on your keyboard
  5. Make sure you have a Favorites folder listed in the %userprofile% folder
  6. Right click on the folder, select open and you can delete the data listed under the folder.

Hello, Biggy! There are a few other things to consider. If you were able to connect previously with no issues... mimbo mcjimbo say:

If the modem lets you, you might want to try setting the lease to 0 (or whatever) so it never expires.    It's probably preferable though to give the XBOX a static address even if it's just to remove that as an issue.    Especially if you're port forwarding or putting it in the DMZ.    You might also want to check that both your  DNS servers are available, stable and sufficiently fast, and that what the XBOX is using matches what your modem is.   There are a number of DNS connection/speed test programs you can find online.

In addition if you're staying wireless, you should check to see who else in the area is wireless.    Being on the same channel as somebody "nearby" or even on one of the overlapping channels  that's "close and strong" can cause all sorts of issues with your wireless setup.    If you've got a wireless laptop or phone, there are programs that can tell you what is is near .   Also, you can try setting your modem to a specific 802.11 (be it b or g or n) as well as whatever other settings it has that can be changed.   That is mostly trial and error though since there are so many pieces to fit into each particular situation.
