john mcghie say:

I do hope you will ignore Mr. Zeng's response: it seems he has no clue about this :-)

This is very likely caused by "Automatically update document styles".  If you leave that turned on after updating the styles in the document, Word will update the styles again when the other user opens the document, wiping out the styles that you installed.

Go to the Developer tab and then Word Add-ins and uncheck "Automatically update document styles" and save the document again.  (So if you are updating a document's styles, you turn that on, save the document, then turn it OFF and save the document...)

The other thing that will cause this is if you have styles set to "Automatically update".  Go to Format (menu)>Style>Modify and switch off the "Automatically update" checkbox at the bottom. If that is left ON, as soon as the other user makes a change, their formatting salad will become the new setting for the style and every paragraph it is applied to. Sadly, you may need to make this change to every style in the document!

Word has many "Automatically trash your document" settings that were designed by marketing people who have a very limited understanding of good document practice: you have to learn to switch them off before they cause trouble.  I suspect your problem is one of these two (or both!!).

I persevered and figured it out for myself, (I love it when I do that).

With Internet Explorer open, Go in to

  • Tools
  • Internet Options
  • 'Content' Tab
  • Click the 'Settings' button in the Autocomplete section
  • Click the 'Delete AutoComplete History' button
  • Tick the 'Form Data' box only. (untick all the other ones if you need to)
  • Then click the 'Delete' button

All done, this will also erase all the other Autocomplete names that you have entered into the system, so you will have to re-enter them again, but at least you got rid of the one you don't want anyone to find out about.

Hello masuzyonyirongo,

 

  Thank you for contributing to the Microsoft Answers community!  If you are encountering an error when updating apps on your phone, there are a few steps to take which can help identify and resolve the issue. 

 

  First, please ensure that you are able to manually sync the primary Microsoft Account on your phone.  This can be done by tapping Settings > Email + Account, then tapping and holding your primary Microsoft Account.  Usually, this is the first on the list and will only have the option to Sync (not delete) when you tap and hold the account.  You'll see a notice the account is syncing and if successful, no error will appear.

 

  Next, check to see if you encounter the app update error on both a Data and Wi-Fi connection.  Your Mobile Data connection can be disabled by tapping Settings > Cellular, and the Wi-Fi settings can be accessed under Settings > Wi-Fi.  If you only have the error on Wi-Fi, check to see if you continue to get the error after restarting your network hardware or on a different network.  If the error is only appearing under the Mobile Data connection, you may consider contacting your mobile operator to ensure there are no issues with your data connection.
