john w. vinson say:

Two points:

Date criteria MUST be in either the American (irrational, I admit) mm/dd/yyyy format, or the unambiguous yyyy/mm/dd format. 03/05/2015 can be interpreted by a user as the third of May, but WILL be seen by Access as March 5!

Secondly, wildcards and LIKE are appropriate for substringing Text fields, but NOT for date calculations.

Try

PARAMETERS [Forms]![SearchForm]![Date:] DateTime, [Forms]![SearchForm]![End] DateTime;

SELECT...

WHERE [fieldname] >= [Forms]![SearchForm]![Date:] AND [fieldname] <= NZ([Forms]![SearchForm]![End], [Forms]![SearchForm]![Date:] )

to find all dates starting with Date: and prior to or including [End].

tom van stiphout say:

> [Staus]

Is that a typo for "Status"?

Your field naming convention makes me shudder. All those trailing colons are really not needed, and just create notational difficulties.
If you decide to clean this up: Date is not a proper field name, since it is a Reserved Word. Use "OrderDate" or some such.

"Contains" searches such as:

Like *[field]*

are non-sargable and thus slow. Perhaps you can change them to "Begins With" searches:
Like [field]*

which can use an available index and thus are fast(er).
