opt in vs opt out - Microsoft Community

port a say:

opt in vs opt out

A few basic facts from my perspective.

Change for change's sake is not always progress, mostly it  is not.

People do not like change just because someone tells them they need to change, not even from family members and friends, definitely not from corporations who are more interested in profit then their customers. This does not make them luddites, it makes them human.

Businesses especially dislike change just for change's sake, it disrupts their business flow, and in very many cases is dangerous. (refineries, grain elevators, power companies, hospitals, law firms, banks etc. The list goes on.)

So the question is, why not make the switch to a new windows as changeless at base start as possible?

Instead of having to go back and learn a whole new methodology of input why not make it the same methodology?

From where I sit, changes of such a drastic nature as Microsoft is making in the interface should be opt in, not opt out.

Telling consumers, business or private "if you don't like this feature, there are x number of steps you can make to disable it", is counterproductive.  

Instead of a blitz of changes that the user must go and make to bring it back to an interface they are comfortable with, the more rational course is to let them make the changes one at a time, for the features they want, instead of forcing them to spend hours trying to get comfortable with the 'new and improved' windows, and remove the clutter they don't want.

Worse still is telling companies that they can remove social networking, go back to the kb/mouse their employees are comfortable with and trained at with just a few 'easy' changes. Expecting businesses or individuals to go out and invest in the latest touch screen technology not only burdens them, either to reset the program or financially to do the upgrades.

(Please don't bother telling me I should have read the info and watched the videos, I did. I bought this computer to evaluate the new windows, not to tag along merrily with the programmers dreams of a radical new society. In my evaluation, Microsoft should stick as close as possible to earlier versions of windows, allowing me and my customers, private and business, to add features one at a time if we desire, not be forced to spend hours per computer removing features we don't want.)

deadkenny say:

Just to explain, the software that's available in NSU is published via a different route to that which is available FOTA (flashing over the air).

 

It's not always the case that both are made available at the same time, or even at all (e.g. there may be a decision to just make an update available FOTA if NSU is proving unreliable).

 

Note also that FOTA updates may not include the whole content. To reduce the download it may just be the core firmware and not the content pack. You may not want the content anyway, but it may include various updates to applications, themes, other bits and pieces that are not critical to the basic operation of the phone. NSU can deliver the whole lot.

 

 

And yes, you should in theory be able to jump direct to 031.023. However, firmware updates are often tricky and I'd strongly advise using the content copier feature in PC/Ovi Suite to backup the phone (don't bother with backing up the memory card though, as it will take forever!), then update the firmware, and after that if all is not well do a factory reset and restore the backup.

 

Edit: Further on this, I've noticed some oddities with FOTA though. The original software for upgrading UK CV variants (for example) to 031.023 seems to be 022.007, and yet all are on 021.024. My phone doesn't show any updates yet it should be there, so maybe FOTA is tied to a base SW. How on earth you update from a SW version that was never available for you phone, I'm not sure. Will try NSU later as supposedly 031.023 is available for UK CV now via that.
