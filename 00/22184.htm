 There has to be an answer or an update coming that will fix this issue.

Add-ons aren't the only source of interference.  The point of booting in safe mode is to take the next step at removing possible causes.  It is a diagnostic, not a fix.

FWIW lately I have only seen issues about interference with Edge.  There, users are finding it necessary to uninstall their third-party security packages to avoid their symptom.  Again, that is something that neither running with no add-ons or even starting in safe mode would uncover.  It would just be the next phase of a clean-boot troubleshooting procedure.

An alternative would be to get a dump of a crash and try to puzzle out a cause with that.  E.g. you may spot some modules which are always loaded that could be removed.  Etc.

a. On the newly converted Local account, click Start and then PC Settings.

b. Click Users and accounts and under Your Profile click Connect to a Microsoft account.
