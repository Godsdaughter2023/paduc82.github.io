To do so, follow the steps given below:

Step 1:

Convert the Microsoft account to a Local account:

a. Sign in to your Microsoft account on Windows 10 Insider Preview. 

b. Click Start and then PC settings.

c. Click Users and accounts and under Your Profile click Disconnect on the right of the screen.

e. Type in a Username, Password, and Password Hint that you want set for your Local account and click Next. 

f. Click on Sign out and Finish button.

Step 2:

Switch back Local account to Microsoft account:

a. On the newly converted Local account, click Start and then PC Settings.

b. Click Users and accounts and under Your Profile click Connect to a Microsoft account.

Mailboxes - How to use Meeting Analyzer to collect calendar logs - Microsoft Community

alex rizeanu msft say:

Mailboxes - How to use Meeting Analyzer to collect calendar logs

Technical Level : Intermediate

Summary

The Meeting Analyzer takes advantage of a new Exchange PowerShell command that is available in the Exchange Online service:

  • Get-CalendarDiagnosticLog

The admin will need the Office365 Tenant Admin credentials, SMTP address of the user who reproduced the issue, and Meeting Subject.


Details

Use these steps to collect Exchange Calendar logs using Meeting Analyzer:

  1. Download Meeting Analyzer from https://meetinganalyzer.codeplex.com/
  2. Extract the files then run MeetingAnalyzer.exe.
  3. You will be prompted for the following:
  • Tenant Admin Name and password

  • SMTP address of user mailbox

  • Meeting subject

  • There will be 2 output files, <SMTP>_<Meeting Subject>.log and .csv file. The location of these files is where the Meeting Analyzer Application is saved.
  • You will be prompted if you want to analyze data for another meeting. Type yes or no then press Enter.
Reviewing the logs Reviewing the logs
  • <SMTP>_<Meeting Subject>.log contains the same information you would normally see in CalDiagViewer under Timeline .
  • .csv file contains the same information you would see in CalDiagViewer under TableView.

Note: You will notice a small discrepancy in terms of events numbers visible in .log when compared .csv file because in the CSV line 1 is the property name headers (e.g: row 65 in the CSV is #64 in the .log file).

Sometimes you can’t access some specific websites on Internet Explorer whereas other websites work fine, this mostly happens with secure websites. When you connect to a secure website, Internet Explorer uses an encrypted channel that uses Secure Sockets Layer (SSL) technology to encrypt transactions. Corrupted information in the SSL can cause websites not to load correctly. Clearing the SLL state may resolve this problem.

Method 1: Clear Secure Socket Layer (SSL)

To do this, follow these steps for your version of Windows.

  1. Swipe in from the right edge of the screen (if you're using a mouse, press Windows logo key+C keys), and then tap or click Search. Enter Internet options in the search box, and then tap or click Settings.

  2. In the search results, tap or click Internet Options. Tap or click the Content tab, and then tap or click ClearSSL state.

Method 2: Date and time settings

Incorrect date and time settings on your PC can also cause problems accessing web servers. Make sure that the date and time settings are correct on your PC. To check the date and time, follow these steps for your version of Windows.

  1. Swipe in from the right edge of the screen (if you're using a mouse, press the Windows + C keys), and then tap or click Search. Enter date and time in the search box, and then tap or click Settings.

  2. In the search results, tap or click Date and Time, and then tap or click Change date and time.

  3. In the Date and Time Setting window, set the current date and time.

  4. Click OK, click Apply, and then click OK.

Method 3: Manage add-ons and Reset Internet Explorer

If the date and time settings on your PC were correct, some incompatible or defective Internet Explorer add-ons might be interfering with the website.
