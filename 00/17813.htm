Is it JUST the internet?  Or, does the slow speed also happen when moving data across your internal network?  I don't have any kind of sure-fire solution, but when I was having slow speeds across my network, these are the things I did (one of which solved the problem):

  • Shut down, turn off, and physically unplug from the power every device on your network (i.e., computer, router, and cable modem).  Let it all sit for a while, then plug it all back in.
  • Check with whoever manufactured your computer/motherboard/network adapter and see if there's a new driver.  If so, install it.
  • I don't know if it applies to your system (Intel stuff), but see if there's new Chipset Device Software (INF Update Utility) and Management Engine Interface (MEI) software.  If so, run those.
  • If none of that helps, you could also try going to Device Manager | Network Adapters, right-clicking on your adapter and selecting Update Driver Software.  You could also try the Uninstall option and then right-click on the root of the Device Manager tree and telling it to Scan for Hardware Changes.

Hopefully, one of those will fix the problem.

Sub KillSomeTime()
