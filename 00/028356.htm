robert long [msft] say:

Hi Thierry - Sorry this is confusing.  This is something that comes up with customers a fair amount and we are looking at making improvements here.

 

I see you had this or a similar question in another forum here: 

http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/office/forum/webapps-excel/excel-web-app-function-language/b1f56848-056f-4ca2-b9e1-641a6b1798f9

 

I would refer you to the post provided by David M on Jan 14 that provides a good explanation of this issue.   I am going to copy/paste his response here:

 

Basically, your formula language in Excel derives from your 'regional settings' and these are derived from the "Account Settings" section of your Microsoft Account.  From SkyDrive, you can click your name in the top right, then click Account Settings.  Clicking "Edit Personal Info" takes you to a page that lists your Country/Region.  By changing your Country/Region you can change your regional settings. Your UI language settings (buttons, dialogues, strings, etc.) derive from the 'UI settings' which are different from your 'regional settings'. This is the same way Excel desktop client works, there is the concept of UI settings and regional settings. Regional settings control number and date formatting and formula language, among other data specific items. Formulas happen to be part of the data, and so they are rendering according to your regional settings.


If you want different regional settings today, you must change this Country/Region setting for your Microsoft account.  We understand this is not the best solution and we are working to improve this experience. 

For most people in most scenarios, this setting does provide the locale information they expect based on their country or region in which they live.  However, there are some scenarios such as your's where this isn't the case. 

While we can't comment on future features, Microsoft appreciates and is interested in your feedback.  Hopefully changing your Country/Region will work for you.  Would you expect another configuration option to manage your regional settings?  Or would more formatting options in the web app solve your problem?
 
Thanks
 
Rob
