Original Xbox One does 1080P.  The Xbox One S can do 4K, but only for Streamed 4K video content and 4K UHD movie discs.  You can upscale games to 4K or play them at 1080P without up-scaling on the One S.

Notes:

USB 3.0 is a considerably faster interface than USB 2.0.  USB 3.0 connectors almost always have blue plastic inserts compared to the black inserts on USB 2.0 connectors.  Although you have a good router, it doesn't support the current highest speed wi-fi protocol -- 802.11-ac -- and my understanding is that you won't see much difference between a USB. 2.0 and a USB 3.0 adapter.  I don't know when this article was written:  http://bestwirelessroutersnow.com/usb-2-0-vs-usb-3-0-wireless-adapters/

The three most common wi-fi connection protocols today are 802.11-g, 802.11n, and 802.11-ac.  Your router supports the first two.  For the best performance, you should buy a wi-fi adapter that is at least capable of using 802.11-n.  See https://www.flashrouters.com/blog/2012/11/29/the-difference-between-wireless-g-wireless-n-wireless-ac/

Finally, try not to connect a USB wi-fi adapter to a USB port that is in the narrow space between the back of your computer and the wall, especially one of the "mini" or "micro" units.  Depending on the construction of your walls, this could significantly reduce the quality/range of the wi-fi connection you'll get.  If you have to use one of the connectors in the back, get an adapter with an external antenna.  You can even get one with an antenna on the end of a cable so you can put the antenna in a better location, e.g.: https://www.newegg.com/Product/Product.aspx?Item=N82E16833166056&cm_re=wifi_antenna-_-33-166-056-_-Product
