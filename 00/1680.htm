What we want is a connector like the following
We will create a custom one by modifying a standard rectangle.

Create two copies of a standard rectangle and then edit point on one of the copies (right click to get the context menu)
Once in edit point mode select the top segment of the rectangle and delete it! You will be left with something that looks like the bottom of the desired connector.
Strangely PP now creates a curved segment (see left side of 'rectangle') that you will need to straighten out. Select the segment and select the 'Straight Segment' right click  menu option.
I then use the remaining of the two original rectangle as a guide to straighten the new shape. I like to drag the new shape over the original one and then edit the point until it overlaps exactly the original point

Now we can take the new shape and add line connectors (shown in light orange below) to the two unconnected corners. Group all three objects and now you have a custom connector with 5 segments that stay connected and can be individually controlled. 
You can further enhance it by turning one of the line connectors to an elbow connector. Parts of the group can be individually colored and customized
You only have to do this once. Save your shape and copy paste it every time you need this type of connector
