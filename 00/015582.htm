Assuming you have modified the Footnote Reference and Footnote Text styles, you can use two Replace operations; just reverse the order you have been using. Applying Footnote Text, a paragraph style, won't remove Footnote Reference, which is a character style. If you want to be extra careful, apply Footnote Text first, via the footnote pane, and then use Find & Replace to (re-)apply Footnote Reference. Just try it.

hansv mvp say:

WindowTop and WindowLeft give the coordinates of the top left corner of the form relative to the top left corner of the usable area of the Access application window.

If you have maximized the form, its title bar and border have moved outside this usable area, so WindowTop and WindowLeft are negative (90 is about the width of the border, and 480 the height of the title bar in the units used in VBA: twips, where 1440 twips = 1 inch)
