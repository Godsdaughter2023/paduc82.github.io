Hi AndreeLaurin,

I would like to know which Office 365 plan you are using. If a hold isn't placed on a mailbox before it's deleted, the contents of the mailbox won't be preserved or discoverable. The deleted mailbox can be recovered within 30 days of deletion, but the mailbox and its contents will be permanently deleted after 30 days if it isn't recovered.

If you are using Exchange Online Plan 2 (Enterprise E3/E4) then you can use this feature is called inactive mailboxes. Inactive mailboxes allow you to preserve former employees’ email after they leave your organization. A mailbox becomes inactive when a Litigation Hold or an In-Place Hold is placed on the mailbox before the corresponding Office 365 user account is deleted. The contents of an inactive mailbox are preserved for the duration of the hold that was placed on the mailbox before it was made inactive.

After the mailbox is deleted, the license that was associated with it will be available to assign to a new user. Inactive mailboxes don’t require ongoing licenses.

For more detailed information: Manage inactive mailboxes in Exchange Online

Note: In-Place Hold and Litigation Hold are available only Office 365 Enterprise E3/E4 plan.

Moreover, if you are not using Office 365 plan above please take a backup data of the former employee, next step removes the Office 365 license and then delete the former employee's user account. For your reference: How to block employee access to Office 365 data

stefan blom say:
If all editors are being replaced with a single one, called "Author," then someone has run the Document Inspector on the document and selected the option to remove personal information. To stop that from happening in the affected document, click File | Options | Trust Center | Trust Center Settings | Privacy Options and clear the check mark next to "Remove personal information from file properties on save." Click OK to confirm the settings and save the document.
