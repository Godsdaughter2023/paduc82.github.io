graham mayor say:

Most Word fields do not update automatically. Notable exceptions are Time and Date fields and the StyleRef field.

Rohn007 has suggested a macro that updates fields, however the version as shown will only update fields in the body of the document. Documents comprise of various story ranges (imagine layers of transparencies overlaid to give the final combined version) which must be handled separately. How you would handle updating of the filename field would depend on where it was located in the document. The update macro code used as an example athttp://www.gmayor.com/installing_macro.htm called from an autoopen macro would work for most field locations, but even that is not an ultimate solution for complex documents.

Personally I wouldn't use a field to display the filename in a document. I prefer to use a macro to add it at the end as text. The following macro will do that and can be run again to change the filename and path if this should change.

Sub InsertFileNameAtEnd()
With ActiveDocument
    If Len(.Path) = 0 Then .Save
    If InStr(1, .Range.Paragraphs.Last.Range.Text, _
        ".doc") = False Then
        .Range.InsertAfter vbCr & .FullName
        With .Range.Paragraphs.Last.Range
            .Paragraphs.Alignment = _
                wdAlignParagraphRight
            .Font.Name = "Arial"
            .Font.Size = 8
        End With
    Else
        .Range.Paragraphs.Last.Range.Text = _
        .FullName
    End If
End With
End Sub

funjeleki say:

I was finally able to do this. I have a Nokia E5 and have the JoikuSpot lite version installed. My ISP is Straightalk. My laptop has Windows 7 and I had an old router from back when I had broadband on a shelf. The only thing I had to buy was a crossover ethernet cable from Staples for around $13.00.

 

Set up your router to make a wireless network (you should have instructions on how to do this, if not you can usually google it). Turn your laptop on. Turn on your hotspot on your phone. Connect your laptop to your hotspot. Run your crossover cable from your Router to your laptop and then turn on the router.

 

From the Start Menu - Select Control Panel - Select Network and Sharing Center - Select Change Adapter Settings on the right hand side. Click on the Local Area Connection, hold down the CTRL key and select the Wireless Network Connection that is currently connected to your wifi. With both items highlighted, right click and select "Create a Bridge". Once the bridge is created, you will right click on the newly created Network Bridge and select "Status". Click on the "Details" Button and make note of the following:

 

IP Address

Subnet Mask

Default Gateway

Primary DNS

Secondary DNS

 

All of the above information will be entered into the Manual Settings of the Playstation except for the IP Address. Whatever the last number is, add a 1 to it.

 

Turn on your playstation. Go to Settings, scroll to Network Settings, select Internet Connection Settings. Select "Custom" settings, then wireless, then manual settings. Using the information you recorded earlier fill in the information (Remember to add a 1 to your last number in the IP address only. If your IP address was 180.124.4.2 then you would enter 180.124.4.3).

 

Enter all of the other information exactly as you recorded. Do not make any changes to the other prompts such as the Proxy Server or UPnP. If they default to "Do Not Use" or "Auto" then allow them to stay that way. Now test your connection. You should now have internet on your PS3.

 

For those who say only certain routers will do this, I would have to say that isn`t true, because I know my router is at least 4 years old and I haven`t used it in more than a year. It is worth a try if you have an old router laying around or if you want to invest in a cheap one. Like I said earlier, I bought a crossover ethernet cable which I could only find at Staples. It has to be the crossover ethernet cable. Best Buy and RadioShak do not carry them (at least my stores didn`t). The package will specifically say "Crossover". You can call your stores ahead of time and talk to the Computer Gurus of the stores and not the Game Guys. If they don`t know what you are talking about then you are either talking to the wrong person or the store doesn`t carry them. It took me over a month of research and trial and error before I finally got this to work. I hope this post is helpful to someone. Good luck!

techbuzzireland say: How do I make a conference call on my Nokia device? - FAQ
Conference call is a network service, make sure that the conference call is supported by your network operator or service provider before following the steps below.
To make a conference call on your device:
Make a call to the first participant.
To make a call to another participant, select Options > New call. The first call is automatically put on hold.
When the new call is answered, to join the first participant in the conference call, select Options > Conference.
To add a new person to the call, repeat step 2, and select Options > Conference > Add to conference.
To have a private conversation with one of the participants, select Options > Conference > Private (or in some devices select the Participants option shown on the screen). Select a participant and Private. The conference call is put on hold on your device. The other participants can still continue the conference call.
After you finish the private conversation, select Options > Add to conference to return to the conference call (or in some devices select the Conference call option shown on the screen).
To drop a participant, select Options > Conference > Drop participant, scroll to the participant, and select Drop (or in some devices select Participants option on the screen, scroll to the participant and select Drop participant) .
To end the active conference call, press the end key.
Note: to mute the microphone of your device during the conference call, in the active call mode, select Options > Mute.
