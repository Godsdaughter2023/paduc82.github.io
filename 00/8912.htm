Office365 subscriptions via CSP - Microsoft Community

vincentchoy say:

Office365 subscriptions via CSP

Technical Level : Intermediate

Summary

There are various ways one can obtain a subscription for Office365. One of them is via CSP.

This is a new licensing model announced by Microsoft in July 2015 and has become available towards early 2016.

The contents of this article is written based on CSP model in July 2016. Microsoft may have tweaked it after this date.

This wiki discusses some differences between the CSP model that a potential subscriber need to take note of.


Details

CSP Licensing

What is a CSP license?

CSP is a new licensing scheme by Microsoft introduced in July 2015. It is progressively being offered by Service Vendors in 2016. 

Other O365 licenses include Online Purchase, Open License and Enterprise Agreement.

How do I know if a license offered to me is CSP?

CSP is often offered by large service vendors like Godaddy, telco & ISPs. They can also be bundled in certain PC lease or service plans (e.g. A company may offer a Business One plan that includes a notebook plus subscription to Office365).

To be sure it is advisable to ask what Office365 license type they are offering.

1. CSP is offered in 2 tiers. Either by a tier one partner (like godaddy or a telco), or a tier 2 partner (who buys from a distributor).

2. CSP model allows the selling vendor to own the entire Office365 relationship with the customer, including billing and support.

CSP Support

1. Unlike other Office365 licensing models, the CSP model requires the selling partner to own the support relationship

2. Other licensing models allow customer to raise support tickets direct with Microsoft or contact Microsoft Support direct. In CSP model this function is disabled in the O365 admin portal.It will ask the customer to contact the CSP partner for support.

3. In the CSP model, the CSP Vendor has to login to the customer's admin portal and raise a support ticket on behalf of the customer. Microsoft may choose to reply the CSP Vendor and let the CSP Vendor reply to the customer.

4. Customer may want to have a detailed conversation with the CSP vendor towards the support workflow and reply path, and the number of hops the reply travels (and typical response time) compared to the other licensing models where a customer can raise a support ticket direct with Microsoft 24x7 and gets a reply within the next few hours.

5. In a O365 tenant with mixed licenses purchased via multiple licensing models (e.g. CSP + Open Lic + Online Purchase), the customer is supposed to be able to raise support issues with Microsoft direct for the non-CSP licenses. However at the time of writing this feature has technical issues.

Delegated Admin

1. In the CSP model, during first setup, the customer is required to approve the CSP vendor as a delegated admin. This delegated admin has far reaching powers and is almost equivalent to a global admin. This includes being able to do e-discovery and reset passwords, and may mean a potential security risk that is unacceptable to some customers.

2. The delegated admin may be removed after setup, but may have potential support issues since the customer is reliant on the CSP to support the tenant and cannot raise his own support ticket.

Is CSP licensing for you?

1. CSP offers flexible licensing. You can add or remove licenses anytime (subject to your contractual term with the CSP vendors). This may prove more flexible and cost less than other Microsoft licensing plans.

2. Do note the support and delegated admin requirements for CSP and whether you have concerns in this area.

andreas killer say:
Okay I got it. You misunderstood the term "data validation".

A data validation can only be used in a cell where the user enters the value into this cell. You can not use a data validation to check if the result of a formula is correct (in your sense).

I suggest that you remove the data validation in column E and apply a conditional formatting instead. Do as follows

Select column E
click tab Data\Data Validation
confirm the error msg with Ok
click "Clear All" at the bottom left, then Ok
click tab Home\Conditional Formatting\Manage Rules
click New Rule\Use a formula to determine with cells to format
apply this formula =COUNTIF(E:E,E1)>1
choose an appropriate format you like (e.g. red interior)
click Ok, Ok

Now you can see if and where you have a duplicate in column E.
