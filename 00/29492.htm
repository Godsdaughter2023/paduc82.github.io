You Can select Hibernate if you don't want your laptop to lose work,when your battery runs out of charge.

Sleep vs hibernate...

Sleep is a power saving state.when we use sleep your pc will consume power ( but very less) to keep itself awake.it may be useful for desktops(where power is plugged in always) but for laptops using sleep will not yield better result,as it consumes our battery power when we are not plugged in,resulting in some loss of charge.

Coming to Hibernate...

Hibernate is an option specially designed for laptops.Your laptop will never consume any power,when you put your laptop to hibernate mode,as your laptop will look like it was shut downed,but when you power on again you can see your work being resumed.

You Can use sleep as well as hibernate  while choosing critical battery action(see previous answer)

when you select sleep your laptop will go sleep and shutdowns(you will lose unsaved work)when the battery loses 

its full charge..(but don't worry modern Windows OS like W10,8.1,etc. will automatically enter into hibernate mode from sleep,when the battery charge reaches critical point.)

On the other hand, if you select hibernate your laptop will enter into hibernate and you will be resumed to where you left when you power on again.

So,use 

1).Sleep -if you are not going to use your laptop for particular period of time like 10 to 15 minutes.

2) Hibernate-if you are not going to use laptops for extended period of time (like night hours) and you won't have an opportunity to charge the battery during that time.
