Junk mail belongs in your Junk folder. If messages arrive there, the junk filter is working correctly. Rules operate only on messages delivered to the Inbox. They cannot work messages delivered to Junk.

Since the messages in the Junk folder are junk, simply ignore them. They'll go away after 10 days.

You cannot prevent the delivery of junk messages without being able to control the message transport. Only Microsoft has that type of control. For messages arriving in your Inbox, what you can do is create a rule that says "If sender contains xxx and recipient contains xxx then delete and mark as read", where "xxx" is the string preceeding @outlook.com or @hotmail.com in your address. You could use your entire address, if you wish.

stefan blom say:
After looking into this further, I think you'll find that the alignment works correctly if you unlink the header in the landscape section. On the Header & Footer Tools Design tab, click to deselect Link to Previous (in the Navigation group).
 
As long as the sections remain linked, Word has to recalculate the position for the center alignment of the table as you move the focus from a portrait to a landscape section (or vice versa), which is causing the problem you are seeing. Unlinking fixes things.
