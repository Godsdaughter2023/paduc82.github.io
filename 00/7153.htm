jim gordon mvp say:

Hi

You replied to an old Q&A thread, so before we get too involved, there is a specific order of troubleshooting to follow.

First and foremost is to make sure both Mac OS and Office have current updates installed.

Make sure your Mac has a working internet connection before checking for updates. Install Mac OS X updates followed by Office updates.

Install Mac OS X updates first:

To get Mac OS updates, go to the App store on your dock, and then click the Updates button to reveal available updates.

Then Install Office Updates

To get the update:

1. Open any Office application:

3. Make sure your Mac has a working internet connection

4. Go to the Help menu and choose Check for Updates

5. Let AutoUpdate check for and install updates

6. Restart your Mac

Still crashing? The next thing to do is font management. Duplicate and bad fonts are the next most likely culprits for crashes.

There are several font utilities you can use. Apple provides Font Book in your Applications folder. Whichever utility you use, your first goal is trash fonts that are duplicates, keeping only the newest version. Your second goal is the verify the integrity of each and every font on your system. Trash any and all fonts that are imperfect in any way. Then restart your Mac and then immediately empty the trash to get rid of all those drive-space hogging useless fonts.

If you are certain that your fonts are perfect, and you are still having crashes, then it's time to fix the operating system itself by reinstalling system fonts, doing a Safe Boot, repairing the volume and directory, and in extreme cases reinstalling Mac OS. But these are measures that you take AFTER you've made sure everything is up to date and all duplicate and bad fonts have been removed. Having to take these steps is pretty rare.
