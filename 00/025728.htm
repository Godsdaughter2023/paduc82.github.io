Hi Pranav,

A faulty USB charging cable may cause your Microsoft Band 2 not to charge. However, here are some few things that you can check:

  • Make sure you connected the charging cable to a USB port (USB 2.0 or greater) that can supply power. Powered USB ports include those on your computer and those on USB hubs or adapters connected to a power source.
  • Most USB hubs don’t supply power and won’t charge your Band.
  • On desktop PCs, powered USB ports are often on the back of the PC.
  • AC adapters, like those that power your phone, are generally sufficient to power your Band.
  • Try charging another device, such as your phone, from the USB port to make sure the port is working properly.

Also, try restart your band.

  1. Press and hold the power button and the action button on your Band at the same time.
  2. After three seconds, your Band will show Turn Power off?
    You can power down your Band and restart it by swiping left and tapping Yes, or you can continue to hold down both buttons for an additional seven to eight seconds.
  3. After an additional seven to eight seconds, your Band’s screen will flash red. You can release both buttons and your Band will perform a quick restart, or you can continue to hold down both buttons for a few more seconds.
  4. Release both buttons. You Band will now be turned off.
  5. Press the power button to restart your Band.

If still your Band won't charge, contact our Answer Desk.

Let us know if you need further assistance.

netsh winsock reset catalog

netsh int ip reset

c. Reboot your computer and check internet connection

ipconfig /flushdns

ipconfig /registerdns

ipconfig /release

ipconfig /renew

c. Reboot your computer and check internet connection
