canadian tech say:

Disk drives in laptops are the most likely point of failure.  They are actually pretty delicate

Download and run your hard drive manufacturer's drive testing software to determine if the hard drive is failing.  If you are getting that message from Windows, it is highly likely that it is indeed about to fail.  If you need a link to the correct software, tell us the model number of the drive.  You can find it in Device Manager.

Get your data backed up very soon before the drive becomes unusable.

You will likely be replacing the drive.  Replace it with a rotating 7200rpm one.  I just did this for a client yesterday.  Cost for a 500G 7200rpm drive was about $65.  I stress 7200.  Most are 5400 and for a few extra dollars, you will see a noticeably improved performance.

Those unpackaged hard drives you find in computer stores are usually the best buy.

You will need a Windows 7 install disk that matches your product key in windows version (home/pro) and bitness (32/64).  If you do not have one, you can borrow one from a friend.
