dan at it associates say:

If you purchased Office H&S 2010 with the licensed designation FPP, then Yes, the license permits you to move  licensed activations from one computer to another.

 

If you purchased Office H&S 2010 with the license designation PKC or OEM, then No, the license does not permit you to move it to any other computer.***

 

***Loophole:  Depending upon where and how you purchased a PKC license, if you are in North America Microsoft gives you either a 30-day or 45-day return window.  Since you are within that return window, you have two options:

1.  Return the product to Microsoft and buy a new one to install on the new computer.

2.  Try a telephone activation.  Install the product and attempt to activate.  If the activation is refused, rerun the Activation Wizard and choose Telephone Activation.  Call the phone number displayed by the Activation Wizard.  When the automated system picks up, do not enter or say anything into the system which will eventually transfer you to a live activation representative.  Explain your situation and emphasize that you are within the return period.  The rep or their supervisor should recognize that the path of least resistance is to allow an exception and a re-activation of your product key card license.
