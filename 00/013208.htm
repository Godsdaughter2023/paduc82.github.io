Hi IntralinkgroupWebmaster,

 

I understand that some legit emails lands to your Junk folder instead of your Inbox.

 

This happens because Outlook.com may treat the emails as Junk if the sender does not belong to your contacts list.

 

There are two things that we can do about this:

 

Check and adjust Junk filter settings by:

  1. Ensuring that the sender's email address are added to your contacts list.
  2. Check their emails that landed in the Junk folder and untag them as Junk. This brings their email address under your whitelist thus further avoiding the chance of having their emails land on your Junk folder in the future.
  3. You may have to do this process for a few times before Outlook.com starts putting those emails to your Inbox.

Choose IMAP protocol if your mail client supports it. This way, you can see emails even those that lands in your Junk folder through your mail client.

jack cook - aka help_line say:

So sometimes it is difficult to diagnose an issue without holding the device in my hands.

When you install an app on SD, it still installs something (very little) in main memory. Possibly you have too much on your device.

What I would do is try to free up as much memory on your device as you can. Then try to install one app on the SD card and see if it works.

The other option is to hard reset and set it up again by installing as many apps on the SD card as you can. Apps like Facebook I would install in main memory
