jonrichco1 say:

Five ways to access Word 365/2016 templates

I have about 10 personal Word templates. I have been researching the best ways to create documents based on them and thought I would post what I have found in case others face this issue. I have so far identified five approaches, ranked out of 10.

1. Use File Explorer to locate your dotm or dotx template file and double click it (1 out of 10 about 5 clicks)

2. In a Word file, click File/ Options/ Save and set "Default personal templates location" to your templates file folder. Then when you click File/ New/ Custom, your templates folder will be shown. Click this and your templates will be shown (5 out of 10 - 4 clicks)

3. In a Word file, click File/ Options/ Advanced/ General/ File locations, set Workgroup templates to your templates file folder. When you click File/ New/ Shared, all your templates will be shown. You can access this easily from the keyboard using Alt FNE, which brings all your templates to the screen but only lists the first 8 letters of each filename making it difficult to decide between templates of similar names (7 out of 10 - 3 clicks).

4. Place a shortcut to each template in your Quicklaunch folder (create the folder first if needed). Then pin the folder to your taskbar (9 out of 10 - 2 clicks)

5. Add "New Document or Template" (in Commands not in the ribbon) to the Quick Access Toolbar. It will show all the templates in your templates folder (10 out of 10 - 2 clicks). Thanks to Jay for this. The top part of the window which opens on my system is:

Note: Ctrl N gives you a new document based on your Normal template.
