john w. vinson say:

Hans and theDBGuy are both right - different solutions that both work. The developers of Access made things confusing by using the same word - "Currency" - for two different purposes!

The Currency datatype (in table design, it's a choice along with Number, Text, Date/Time and so on) is a scaled huge integer; it accepts numeric values with four decimal places of accuracy (so you can enter 50.54 or 27.3129). I'd use it in your case. The Double type of Number will also work but may have unexpected side effects - it can have up to fourteen digits of accuracy, but may have "roundoff error" out in the fourteenth digit and may not display exactly as expected.

The Currency format is not the same as the Currency datatype, though! The Format can be applied to any sort of number - Currency, Long Integer, Double, etc.; it just puts in commas and the computer's regional currency symbol (dollar sign, Euro symbol, etc.). But you are not limited to that symbol. You can store your data in a Currency field and then use a Format such as

"RS #,##0.00"

to display a stored value of 2015.5000 as

RS 2,015.50
