neha singh msft say:

Hi Michael,

When we click on + Create Site in SharePoint, it automatically creates a team site with an Office 365 group, as shown in the following screenshot. This newly created site will appear among the sites you’re following. Your site will not inherit the permission settings or navigation of other sites.

Once the site is created, it will take some time to appear under Office 365 groups in Outlook. However, the group will immediately reflect under All Plans in Planner. To verify this, go to Planner and click the newly created group under All Plans, then click the ellipses > Calendar, it will give you the following message:

Once the Calendar is set up, this group will start appearing with other groups in Outlook.

Note: Group created in Planner appears in Outlook and vice versa, all these groups have their own team sites.

The following are the answers of your questions which you asked in your earlier post:

  1. However, I just created a demo site using "+ Create Site" then opened Outlook> Groups and did not see the demo site. Wouldn't all Office 365 groups be populated under Groups in Outlook? As mentioned above, you have to wait for few minutes before it starts appearing under Groups in Outlook, group will start appearing in Planner immediately.
  2. Logging into microsoftonline.com and selecting SharePoint in the app launcher takes me to SharePoint Online, correct? Correct
  3. SharePoint Online is different than Office 365 Groups, correct? We do use Office 365, and I keep reading articles about Office 365 team sites. How are Office 365 team sites different from SharePoint Online.  Every Office 365 Group you create will get a new modern Team Site provisioned that shows a clear linkage to the Group. The integration of groups and SharePoint team sites means that any time a new team site is created, a new group membership will be created as well. You can easily see the members of the site if the site is listed as public or private within your organization and how it has been classified. Here’s an article with more information: New capabilities in SharePoint Online team sites including integration with Office 365 Groups
  4. Can you have multiple team sites? Yes, you can.
  5. Under featured links I see Public Site and Team Site. Can I delete this Team Site - it has no relevant information? Yes, you can delete the link. It will not delete the actual site, only the link will be delated. To delete the link, click Edit under Featured Links and click Cancel >Done
  6. Am I creating an Office 365 Group or a SharePoint Site? You are creating an Office 365 group with SharePoint site.
  7. Are the other SharePoint sites I create using the "+ create site" button nested under this Team Site or are these "+ create sites" stand alone SharePoint Online sites. The site created by clicking “+ create site “are same as the standalone SharePoint Online team site, the only difference is that these sites are created with an Office 365 group. These sites will be independent sites and will not inherit any permissions from another site.

Public Site and Team Site are different. Public site is an external-facing website where as team site is internal. If you want to create an internal site for HR department, I’ll suggest you create a site under the SharePoint option in the App Launcher. This will create an Office 365 group along with a team site. And yes, the site inside of O365 Groups will function the same as a standalone SharePoint Online Site.

Let me know if you have any doubt.

    try using "w" for wait.

    usually the p for pause is a 3 second "soft pause" that automatically does the next set of numbers while the "hard pause" a.k.a. wait is the one where you must hit a button to continue with the next set.  I use a motorola Q and I use the center button for that functionality.
