Does this object look the same, or might it have a strikethrough line through the text?  The normal behavior for email deletion in an IMAP account is that the message header gets a strikethrough line through it and it may or may not also turn grey.  This is a "Message Marked for Deletion" and Outlook normally hides these from view - "View > Change View > Hide Messages Marked for Deletion"; Outlook also has an "IMAP Messages" view which shows the strikethrough header.  In addition, the normal behavior for deleted messages is to remain in whatever folder until they are Purged (Folder tab, in the Cleanup section), and some IMAP services have no knowledge of a Deleted or Trash folder. 

So, with the "correct" settings, Outlook attempts to compensate to avoid confusion.  If you go to "File > Account Settings", select the IMAP account, click "Change", click the "More Settings" button and go to the "Advanced" tab, you'll see a "Deleted Items" section.  If the first checkbox is checked, messages are marked, but not moved; this, in conjunction with the "IMAP Messages" view selection, could account for what you are seeing including the popup, which is normal for a delete request to a message already marked foe deletion.  Please see this page for a detailed explanation:

Setting the Sent Items folder for IMAP accounts in Outlook 2013
