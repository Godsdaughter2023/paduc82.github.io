grschinon say:

As with any lithium (ion or polymer) battery:

 

- Charge and discharge the battery fully for the first 3-4 cycles in order to let it reach its optimal capacity.

 

- Thereafter, avoid discharging it fully as this damages it, unlike nickel (Ni-Cd and NiMH) cells which do like being discharged and charged.

 

- Whenever possible, top up the charge.

 

- Only once a month or so, do perform a full charge/discharge cycle in order to "recalibrate" the battery. Without this occasional full discharge its capacity will drop over time more than is normal.
