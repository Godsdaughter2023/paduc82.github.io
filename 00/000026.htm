Of course it's a scam,

Microsoft has nothing to do with IP addresses other than they're used by computers running their operating systems, along with all others including phones, tablets and other devices on the Internet.

There's no such thing as a "compromised IP address", it's a computer or other device that becomes compromised or the accounts that an individual uses to access certain information.

john mcghie say:
The post he sent you to by Daniel Grau is the answer to your question.

You are getting an interaction between the Default Tab Settings in the Normal Template and the List Format applied when you apply bullets.

I suggest you work with the List Bullet set of styles (there are nine, List Bullet through List Bullet 9).  You need to define one of those styles to each level of your bullets outline.

daniel g. grau say:
To apply direct bullet formatting to a paragraph:
  1. Place your cursor in the paragraph
  2. In the main menu bar, select Format > Bullets and numbering and choose a bullet type. (If you don't see the bullet you need, click on a bullet type you won't be using.)
  3. Click on Customize
  4. Change the indents based on the illustration to the right
  5. Change the bullet, type if needed (to do this, choose one from the predefined list shown or use the three buttons Font, Bullet, Picture)
  6. Click on OK

That's it. The next time you need that bullet, repeat step 2.

However, as John pointed out, you'll get easier and consistent results if you use Styles, particularly in long documents.
