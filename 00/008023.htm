iris9290 say:

Hi Cbeppe,

 

Thanks for your post and welcome to the forum. 

 

Let's hope this works, instructions below on how to blindly copy your contacts to your Sim card on your E66.

 

Press the contact shortcut key to open your contact list (wait a few seconds for the phone to open the list before going to the next step as it won't open straight away). 

Press the top left select button - this will bring you to the Options list. 

Press the select key down seven times - This will bring you to Mark/Unmark

Press the middle select key to select this option

Press the down select key once - this will bring you to 'Mark all'

Now press the middle select key to select this option

Now press the top left select key again and press the select key down five times - this will bring you to 'Copy'

Press the middle select key to select. Press again to select the 'Copy to SIM directory'.

 

The contacts will now be copied to your SIM card. Please keep in mind that the SIM card might not be able to store all 200 of your contacts, but it should at least save a big part of it.

The answer is you don't. On Hand quantities should be calculated based on transactions and should NOT be stored. The On Hand field in your table should only be updated when a physical inventory is done and then there should be an effective date. You then include all transactions in your query after that effected date. 

As a general rule calculated value should not be stored. This is a common mistake made by novice designers. 

You can do an Update query, but to do so would violate normalization rules, the integrity of your data and established business practices.

qa guy commelec say:

You don't store.

Have a table of transaction with product, date, quantity, and type transaction.    Based upon the type of transaction it will be plus or minus.  Receive actions are plus and ship are minus.   You might also have transaction of 'broken' which would be a minus.

You start with a transaction of Inventory where you do a physical count and make a record.    Then whenever you need to know how much is on hand you sum all transactions starting with the last inventory and every transaction since that date.    

To know the level on last Monday you sum from last inventory through last Monday.

scottgem say:

As Karl and I said, you maintain a table of transactions. EVERY transaction that involves a movement of stock is recorded in a table (or tables) with a date. To avoid keeping every transaction from the dawn of time, you update the stored on hand when you do a reconciliation of physical inventory. That physical inventory value then becomes a baseline and you only look at transactions AFTER the effective date.

This preserves your accuracy, because you can update and correct individual transaction and have your On Hand calculation immediately updated.
