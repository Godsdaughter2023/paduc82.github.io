So, the login script functionally does exactly what the GPO does, such as removing all the existing then reconnecting based on computer and user. In summary, I ditched connecting network printers through a group policy object in favor of a  logon script.

What is the logical relationship between Prospects and Clients? I would expect it to be "Each record in Prospects will relate to either zero or one Clients; each Client will relate to one and only one Prospect" - a longwinded way of saying it's a one to one relationship. scottgem say:

First, I disagree with having both a Prospects and Clients table. Second, I don't see that there SHOULD be a relationship between the Client and prospects tables if you have to keep them separate.. 

What I would have is single People table with a filed that indicates Prospect or client. When a Prospect buys a policy you change the value of that field to indicate they are now a Client. This will make your job much easier as you don't have to create a mechanism to move records from one table to the other. In addition, your Dependents table can then link to the one People table. So you don't have to change them either.
