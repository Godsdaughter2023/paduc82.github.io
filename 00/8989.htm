Well, I'll just point out that "Print background colors and images" is totally irrelevant. Although the Watermark button is in the Page Background group on the Page Layout tab, this is misleading because the watermark is a wrapped graphic anchored to the header and consequently is in the drawing layer and not actually a background.

 

What could be relevant (especially if this is the only wrapped graphic in the document) is the setting for "Print drawings created in Word." This setting applies to all "drawing objects," which by definition include all wrapped graphics, including text boxes, WordArt (text watermarks), etc. Since a watermark is by definition "Behind Text," it is wrapped and consequently in the drawing layer.

Form fields and content controls were not meant to be mixed. Legacy form fields, ActiveX controls, and content controls are all meant to be used independently and exclusively.

greg maxey say:

That article says the formfields and content controls cannot work together.  Why would anyone want to I don't know, but the article is wrong.  They can.  The only issue is that tabbing between content controls is fouled up (i.e., without some trickery you can't tab from the last content control to the first).  The article says that you can't navigate from a content control to a formfield. Private Sub Document_ContentControlOnExit(ByVal ContentControl As ContentControl, Cancel As Boolean)
If ContentControl.Title = "I'm the CC before the Formfield" Then
  ActiveDocument.Bookmarks(""I'M_THE_FORMFIELD_BM").Range.Fields(1).Result.Select
End If
End Sub
