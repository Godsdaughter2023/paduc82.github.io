jay freedman say:

When you choose the Fancy format for a table of contents in the Custom Table of Contents dialog, that changes the definitions of the styles named TOC 1, TOC 2, etc., which are applied to the entries in the table (ignoring whatever is in the styles applied to the headings in the text).

The TOC 1 style includes the All Caps font property, and TOC 2 and TOC 3 include the Small Caps font property. You can change this (after the TOC is created) by modifying these style definitions.

  • Press Ctrl+Alt+Shift+S to open the Styles pane.
  • Scroll the pane almost to the bottom to find the TOC 2 style's name. If you don't see it in the list, click the Options button at the bottom of the pane; in that dialog, set the "styles to show" dropdown to "All styles" and set the sorting dropdown to "Alphabetical"; and click OK.
  • Right-click the name of the TOC 2 style and click Modify.
  • In the Modify Style dialog, click the Format button at the bottom right, and click Font in the menu.
  • In the Font dialog, clear the "Small caps" check box and click OK.
  • In the Modify Style dialog, click OK.

The Heading 2 entries in the TOC should now display the same capitalization as in the headings they represent.

If necessary, repeat the procedure for the TOC 3 style.

Why I am no longer using Release Preview on my primary devices. - Microsoft Community

cbarnhorst say:

Why I am no longer using Release Preview on my primary devices.

1.  MSFT isn't using it, why should I?  There have been no cumulative updates released for testing ahead of release to the public in nearly two years.  Also, the last feature update was not released to Insiders in the RP ahead of release to the public either.

2.  The past two cycles have revealed the dangers to data on primary devices by updating too soon.  It pays to read the forums, reviews, and tech media reports before updating a primary device.

3.  The downside of the RP is that RP devices are not eligible for MSFT and OEM support until the devices are opted out of even the RP ring.   Even though both Release and Release Preview devices are on the same exact build (major and minor strings), an RP device is still detected by support personnel as an "Insider" device and policy prevents agents from assisting in anything but opting out of the WIP.

4.  I don't "cut the line."  As a result of the current situation with the 1809 feature update, it should be clear to all that the only sane thing to do with primary devices is to patiently wait for the device to be updated when MSFT stages the update to you.  Staging, in this case, means MSFT withholds the update until known issues have been resolved on its end. 

Feel free to add your reasons for leaving the Release Preview ring on your primary devices (or never having opted-in in the first place).
