Solution 1: Check Your Achievement Progress On Your Console

When you unlock an achievement, you may not see a notification or an updated gamerscore right away. The Xbox Live service validates your achievement, which may cause a short delay.

If your gamerscore is not updated online, verify that it's updated locally on your Xbox One console. After your gamerscore is updated on your console, verify that the changes are synchronized on Xbox Live.

  2. Select Achievements > See my achievements.

Solution 2: Check Your Achievement Progress Online

You can check the progress of your achievement online to verify that your console has synchronized with Xbox Live.

To view your achievements online:

  1. Sign in at xbox.com.
  2. Select your profile icon in the upper-right corner, and then select Profile.
  3. Tap or select Achievements.
  4. Select the title of the achievements you're trying to unlock.
  5. If the achievements appear under Achievements, it's already unlocked.

a. user say:

This could also be an issue with what encryption your wireless signal is using, AES or TKIP. I have noticed that by changing it from AES to TKIP, or vice versa depending on what it is currently set to, can make a huge difference in the wireless adapters ability to connect to the specific wireless signal you're attempting to log-in to.

 

To determine what encryption type your router's wireless signal is currently set to, you'll need to log-in to the router (logging in to the router would either need to be done on the current computer via Ethernet or on another computer which is already connected to it). Logging in to the router also depends on the type of router. If it were supplied by your ISP, you'll most likely need to contact them if you do not have the credentials to log in to it and determining what the encryption type is set to. If you purchased your own router (Linksys, D-Link, Netgear etc...), and you do not already know the credentials for logging into your router, you'll need to contact the router manufacturer.

 

Once in the router under wireless (usually advanced), you'll be able to determine what the encryption is set to. Most newer routers have the option to use both AES and TKIP simultaneously, if that option is available use it. If that option is NOT available, I suggest using TKIP, it is commonly more compatible.

 

You should also change the wireless connection's encryption on the computer you are trying to connect with to TKIP.

Method 1: Troubleshoot BSOD.

Method 2: System File Checker.

I suggest you to follow the steps mentioned in Using System File Checker in Windows 10 and check for any system file corruption.

Method 3: Check Disk.

Click on the Start Menu and search for Command Prompt > Right-click on Command Prompt > Click on Run As Administrator > Type the following command and hit Enter.

chkdsk /f C:

C: is the driver, where the Windows operation system is installed.

Note: While performing chkdsk on the hard drive, if any bad sectors are found on the hard drive then chkdsk tries to repair that sector, if any data available on that might be lost.

Method 4: Disable Fast Startup.

Step 1: Click on the Start Menu and search for Control Panel and hit Enter.
Step 2: In Control Panel, click on Large icons beside to View by option and go to Power Options > Choose what the power button does on the left pane and click on Change settings that are currently unavailable.
Step 3: Uncheck Turn on fast startup (recommended) > Click on Save changes > Restart the computer once and check for the issue.

Method 5: Update Graphics Card.

I suggest you to update the Display adapater by following Update drivers in Windows 10, restart the computer once updated, and check for the issue.
