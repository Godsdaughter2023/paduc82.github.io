myall_blues say:

Apologies that's my fault - I didn't think through the options enough.

Excel does know about leap years and 28/30/31 day months. To prove it, put in a cell =DATE(2019,3,0) which translates as Day zero of month 3, which Excel then interprets as the last day of month 2 . you will see it returns the value 28/2/2019.

On the question of 'subtract E4 from G5 it gives me the total number of days', it's important to understand how dates work in Excel. To Excel all dates are just a number. If you take a cell containing =TODAY() and format it as general or number then today (March 5, 2019) it will appear as 43529. The reason for this is Excel calculates dates as the number of elapsed (or partial) days since January 1, 1900. So today is 43529 and midday today is 43529.5 for example.

Thus if you do G5-E4, as 05-Mar-2019 - 06-July-2009, that is 43529-40000=3529=29-Aug-1909.

Try this formula:

=DATEDIF(E4,G5,"y") &" y,"&DATEDIF(E4,G5,"ym") &" m," &DATEDIF(E4,G5,"md") &" d"
