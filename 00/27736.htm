davidf2 say:

When I was talking about your printer I was trying to say that every printer has a limited 'printable area'. Even if you can print full bleed pictures, that does not necessarily mean that you can print to the edge on regular docs. If your printer is cropping off some of the content and not printing it, then that suggests that content is outside the printable area for that printer.

 

The article I referenced before gives one method for determining the printable area. Here is another article: http://ed.mvps.org/Static.aspx?=Publisher/horidiag . Determine the printable area on your page and see if that answers your questions.

 

The other thing I have noticed over the years is that not only is the printable area different on each printer, but also the settings can affect how centered on the page the content is printed. Your original question was how to print your pages with 'perfect' .5 inch borders top, bottom and sides. I would again suggest that you appy those margine guidelines on your page for .5 inch and keep your content within those borders and see if the output is what you want. If the contents seems offcenter then try this experiment.

 

Drag all the content on a page off into the scratch area temporarily. Create a new text box that is 7.5X10 and align it exactly with the margin guides on your page. Then add a border to that text box (right click, format text box, color and lines). Now you should have an empty text box that is perfectly centered on your page...print it. If the print page shows the text box borders and is centered then you know to just keep your content within those .5 inch margin guides and your output should be fine.

 

If you find that your output has shifted to one side or the other, then look in your printer settings for the options to print on the 'long side' or the 'short side', or some sort of language. This is a setting for booklets that automatically shifts the content to accommodate the gutter between pages. If you cannot change that setting then you can move your guide margins to compensate for that shift. For some reason I have one printer that always prints postcards right of center. I have not been able to find the printer setting that causes that so I have to shift all the content on the publisher page about .25 inches to the left in order for the content to be centered when I print it.

 

Enough said...you will just have to experiment a bit and learn the capabilities and limitations of your printer. And for what it is worth the bigger the document the more Word is a better choice than Publisher. Perhaps Word is a better choice for this project...

don varnau say:

Hi,

1. Internet Options> Privacy> try a cookie setting of low or accept all. Click the advanced button and verify that nothing is checked.

2. Verify that the computer's date, time and time zone are correct. These settings can affect cookie handling.

3. An existing cookie may be corrupted. From Internet Options> General > Browsing History> Delete (next screen) uncheck Preserve Favorites website data then check and delete Temporary Internet Files and Cookies.
Note: After clearing cookies, sites which usually remember you won't recognize you on your next visit.

4. Is a security/privacy program "managing" your cookies? CCleaner and CA Internet Security have this option, as do some other programs. Find this feature in the program's options and disable it.

5. See if a browser add-on is causing the problem. (Probably not- but easy to check.)
Start Internet Explorer without add-ons by right-clicking the IE icon on the desktop. Choose Start without add-ons.
or
from Start> Programs> Accessories> System tools> Internet Explorer (no add-ons)
If the problem goes away, an add-on is causing the problem.

http://blogs.msdn.com/ie/archive/2006/07/25/678113.aspx

diane poremsky mvp say:
Hi Harlan,
The usual fix is to open the application, right click on the icon and choose unpin. Then right click and pin.

But if the icons are pinned to the taskbar and when you right click to unpin and its not an option, try deleting the icons and repinning.

Go to %APPDATA%\Microsoft\Internet Explorer\Quick Launch\User Pinned\TaskBar (copy and paste it into the address bar of File Explorer and press enter) and delete the office icons. Reopen the apps and repin.
