There's a lot to consider when designing your report. You must have some pretty complex queries behind your report (or a VERY slow computer/network) for it to take that long to generate. What we've checked:

  • checked Open Files to make sure the fires are definitely not in use, which they aren't.
  • Checked DFS health and replication status, all ok. The files replicate to a second DC, however all users are directed to a single DFS reference computer.
  • Installed latest Office and Windows Updates. Currently version of Microsoft Office is 16.0.7766.2099 and Windows is up to date.

What we've tried:

  • Tried to have users access the files from Excel on their local desktops (not the terminal server) but still get the error
  • Tried checking for file errors and repairing Excel files - no change
  • Tried doing a "Save As" and saving as the same document name to force the saving process - didn't work

Workaround
