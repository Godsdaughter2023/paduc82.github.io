daniel pineault say:

Have you explored Hybrid Databases?  Do note that Access over the Net, regardless of Hybrid, Access/GoDaddy SQL Server, ... will be quite slow.

When moving to the Web, you need to use proper Web Technologies (PHP/MySQL, .Net, ...).  Access was simply never intended for such application.  Yes, you can twist its arms, but ...

IMHO, the true issue here is your company's No Access philosophy.  There is simply no reason for it and it demonstrates true ignorance.  Using technology such as CITRIX, Terminal Services,  et al. you could easily deploy your database very easily at relatively low cost.  Instead, you will either take significant performance hits (so waste users time, thus incur cost to your company) or incur significant cost because you'll need to restart the entire project in a new language when it could be deploy, as is, very easily.  If deployed using say CITRIX, the entire db can located on the server, thus never impacting the Network at all!

I've faced such steadfaced opinions in the past at a couple clients and once you breakdown timelines and cost, IT's objections quickly get "dealt" with and common sense reign.

All I can say to you is, Good luck!
