john korchok say:

If you don't need to edit anything, you can pop in a jpeg on each slide and show it, most people probably wouldn't notice the difference.

You can also use them as guides to recreate the presentation. Place the jpegs on the slides, right-click on them and choose Send to Back>Send to Back and use them as a graphic guide to reconstruct the original.

david marcovitz say:

In addition to what John said, the short answer to your question is NO. A JPEG contains all the pixels but not the shapes. That is, for looking purposes, it should look the same as the original slide, but for editing purposes, you don't have anything to edit. Of course, you can use photo editing software to edit, but you aren't going to be able to easily edit text or move objects around or change font sizes because none of that information is stored as part of the JPEG.
