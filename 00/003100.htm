Problem solved. Try to download free apps or games and everything will work.

1. Go to settings > region and language > change the phones region to a supported country > restart the phone (in my case I switched back to Serbia)

2. Go on store. Find some game or application for payment.

3. Add payment option which you wish (I added my Visa based in Serbia)


Enjoy

bitflung say:

on phone, open music player.  click options -> refresh library.  wait a long, long time.  3.8GB will take a while.  during this process you'll see a count of songs found.

 

you can't close music player while this happens, and it is a much longer wait than you might expect - so be sure you don't need to do anything with your phone for at least 30 minutes.  put it on a charger if you can, it's a lot of sdcard access which is a power hog.

 

once this is done, the phone has an internal database of music - much like the Library.xml file in iTunes.  just like itunes, copying music into the music folder doesn't make it show up in the music player - you must tell itunes to import the music into the library database.  but after this painful period of waiting... the music player works very nicely and you only have to do this again if you add or remove music.

 

goodluck,

talking moose say:
You're correct that this is an encoding issue.

Your full stops (the periods in your subject) are getting converted via AutoCorrect to the ellipsis character ( … ) when you type the third period followed by another character. Some servers handle the ellipsis encoding in different ways.

As a test I created one message and copied your example above and pasted it into the Subject line. As I expected, everything pasted as periods. However, when I appended my own "sSSs... " the three trailing periods were automatically converted to an ellipsis.

Next, I sent the message to three different types of accounts: Comcast, MobileMe and Exchange 2007. The Comcast and MobileMe accounts received the subjects correctly but my Exchange account displayed the problem.

Looking at the source of the sent message I can see the subject has been converted from: 

Test SS..ssSS....SS...SS...sSSS…sSSs…

to:

Test SS..ssSS....SS...SS...=?ISO-8859-1?B?c1NTU4pzU1Nzig==?=

That's really no big deal. That's correct. However, if I view the Content-type and Content-transfer-encoding of my sent message:

Content-type: text/plain; charset="US-ASCII"
Content-transfer-encoding: 7bit

and compare that to what I received, I see that both Comcast and MobileMe receive the message with the same Content-Type and Content-transfer-encoding but Exchange has altered the encoding:

Content-Type: text/plain; charset="Windows-1252"
Content-Transfer-Encoding: quoted-printable

This tells me that the issue lies with the server and not Outlook. I would assume this is a setting that can be adjusted on your server but can you get your service provider to do it? That may be difficult since most Exchange shops tend to cater to Windows users over Mac users.

My suggestion is that you avoid special characters, such as the ellipsis, in your subject lines.

Hope this helps!
