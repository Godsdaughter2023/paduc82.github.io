tftd guru say:

Windows 10 Facebook App no audio on videos - working fine through all browsers

Using the Windows 10 Facebook App from the Store, all Facebook videos have no sound on my Windows 10 laptop when played through the USB to DAC playback device. No, they are not muted. They have sound if I play them through the default Realtek speaker driver playback device only.

All of the Facebook videos do have sound through the USB to DAC playback device when accessing Facebook through a browser, whether Edge, IE11, Chrome, or Firefox, on the same system. Any ideas?

tftd guru say:

I figured out the problem. The app appears incompatible with the 384Khz sampling rate that my DAC driver was set to. Once I dropped the rate to 192Khz the audio was heard correctly. Odd that Facebook in the browsers did not experience this but the Facebook app did.

For those experiencing a similar problem, the bit depth setting on the DAC driver didn't matter to this issue, 16, 24, 32 bit made no difference. The 384Khz sampling rate did not work at any of them. The 192Khz rate works with all of them. Remember to restart the app when you are testing.

muniyappank say:

Change Sleep settings in windows 10 from CMD prompt

How to change Sleep settings dropdown value in windows 10 from CMD prompt?
nathant_06 say:
Hi MuniyappanK,

You can enter the below commands to do so, the equivalent of setting them to never: 
powercfg /change standby-timeout-ac 0
powercfg /change standby-timeout-dc 0
powercfg /change monitor-timeout-ac 0
powercfg /change monitor-timeout-dc 0
powercfg /change hibernate-timeout-ac 0
powercfg /change hibernate-timeout-dc 0


AC and DC determine 'on battery' and 'plugged in', while 0 disables these options.

Hope this helps,
Nathan
