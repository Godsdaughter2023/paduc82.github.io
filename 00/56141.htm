To answer those questions: 

An account can only share to one single "Home Console". That with the ability of the "owner" signing in to any console and being able to download/play his games on any console as long as he is connected online, allows you to use the games and Gold membership on two consoles (the account's "Home Console" and the console the "owner" is actually signed in on, no matter what console that is). 

So with a third console coming into play, it won't work to share to two consoles.

As for the GTA V question: The money your kid spent is tied to his Gamertag and Microsoft Account. If the kid uses the same Microsoft Account email he used on the original Xbox, your kid can play on ANY Xbox console in the world and have access to his money in GTA V.

Gaming progress is tied to the Gamertag Account, and will roam with the account to any console the kid signs in and connects online. The savegame will then downloaded from the Xbox Live cloud, and the kid will be able to continue playing on whatever console they like. 

This is true for disc and downloaded games (as long as the kid's account also owns the license to play the game).

As for moving games from any console to the X with an external drive, be aware that if a game is Xbox One X enhanced, the Xbox One X will have to download the additional assets before the game can be played. 

You can prepare for that by going to the "Settings" of the "source" console, and under "System" go to "Backup and Transfer".

There you can enable the checkmark to download 4K game content, and the non-X console will then download the X enhanced game parts. 

After that is done, you can really just swap the external drive to the Xbox One X, and the family can immediately play on the X right after unwrapping the console. 

By the way: Make sure you already connected the Xbox One X online before putting it under the tree, as the console will have to download an initial update, which might take quite some time depending on your internet speed, as it will download up to 3 GB of update data. 

So to avoid that pesky update time, update the console in advance, and then wrap it up and put it under the tree.
