You can enable Wifi and cable connected at a time, but you can only use the Internet from one of them. The computer will prefer the cable if cable is connected.

Okay, for completeness, I have now resolved this, so am sharing for the benefit of future generations ;)

neilpzz you were absolutely right. Although the error message that comes up halfway through trying to install MS Digital Image software says 'can't install this software on a 64-bit platform', that's not the real issue. I worked this out when I went back and rebooted my previous (nearly dead) PC, which this software ran on perfectly well for the last 6 or 7 years, and which was, yes, also a 64-bit system!

It turns out the problem is that the installer is looking for IE6, and of course IE6 is long dead and gone. When it can't find it, it gums up the install. So the solution is to bypass the installer looking for IE6 and getting hung up on the fact that IE6 can't be found on the PC. My son found the solution online, bless him. In summary, when that dialogue box pops up saying 'can't be installed on a64-bit platform' you close that dialogue box, then wait for it to open a follow-up 'Finish' dialogue box. DO NOT CLOSE THAT BOX.

Instead, go to the Windows home button, and click on the little account icon (your name and person icon). Then click sign out. Then sign in again. Then ignore the dialogue box and carry on with the installation. Because of the order in which things close down when you sign out, this process of interruption basically fools the installer into bypassing its need to find IE6, and the software proceeds to install perfectly.

What I can also say, is that having - in the meantime - downloaded and tested at about 6 or 7 different free photo editing programmes from GIMP to PaintPad, NONE of them are half as good, versatile or easy to use as the 13 year old MS Digital Image. They're either a lot more sophisticated and complicated, or they're a lot more basic and slow. And none of them have the same clean, intuitive interface coupled with such a comprehensive set of tools and capabilities. I'm amazed Microsoft discontinued this product, it's soooo good. They say a lot of the same capabilities were taken into MS Photos, but they really weren't, and the interface is nothing like as well thought out.
