bernie deitrick, excel mvp 2000-2010 say:

You need to have two identically configured lists - I will assume that they are on two different sheets: one named "Dec" and the other named "Jan"  and that your lists start in column A, with headers in row 1.

Since Jan is the newer list, the formulas will go into columns on that sheet.

To determine if an employee is new or transfered, enter in a cell in row 2 on Jan:

=IF(ISERROR(MATCH(C2,Dec!C:C,FALSE)),"New!",IF(INDEX(Dec!B:B,MATCH(C2,Dec!C:C,FALSE))<>B2,"Transfered!","No Change"))

To find out if an employee left, you need to use the formulas on the Dec list, looking for employees in that list that are not on the Jan list: somewhere in row 2 on Dec:

=IF(ISERROR(MATCH(C2,Jan!C:C,FALSE)),"Left!","")

Of couse, each of these formulas need to be copied down to match the list on their sheet.

Bernie

bernie deitrick, excel mvp 2000-2010 say:

This part of my formula does that:

IF(INDEX(Dec!B:B,MATCH(C2,Dec!C:C,FALSE))<>B2,"Transfered!","No Change"))

and this compares the current department to Dec's:

So I'm not sure why you are having a problem.

Create a new list-style, and make the numbering in your own order.

In the Format-dropdown menu, go to Numbering.

Here you can se;ect your level (under "Click level to modify), select where you want the previous level number to be shown (onder Enter formatting for number) and select the previous heading level (under Include level number from).
