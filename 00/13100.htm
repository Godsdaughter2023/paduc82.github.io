Hi Chelsea,

You can protect items in OneNote from being modified by putting password on it. Password protection in OneNote is designed to help keep your notes safe from prying eyes. When a section is password-protected, all of its pages are locked until you enter the correct password. Though, passwords can be applied only to notebook sections, not to entire notebooks. In order to set the password, please follow the steps below:

  1. Right-click the section tab you want to protect and then choose Password Protect this Section.

  2. In the Password Protection task pane, choose Set Password.

  3. In the Password Protection dialog box, type the password you want into the Enter Password box.

  4. Confirm the password by typing it again into the Confirm Password box, and then click OK. If you forget your password, no one will be able to unlock your notes for you

If you’ve applied passwords to multiple sections in your notebook, you can lock them all at the same time:

  1. Right-click any of the protected (but currently unlocked) section tabs and then choose Password Protect this Section.

  2. In the Password Protection task pane, choose Lock All.

For more information on how to password-protect your OneNote, please access this link.

  2. In the OneNote Options dialog box, choose Save & Backup.
  3. On the right, under Save, choose Back Up All Notebooks Now.
  4. When you see the notice that the backup is successfully completed, click OK.

For most people, the most current set of notes will probably always be the most important.
