galahad72 say: Besides Cambria Math, Word will recognize these math fonts in equation mode:

XITS Math [like Times]
Asana [like Palatino]
Asana Math [more glyphs than Asana]
Latin Modern [based on Computer Modern]

There are more math fonts at http://www.gust.org.pl/projects/e-foundry/tg-math, but I haven't explored them all yet.

If additional math fonts are installed in Windows, they come up as options in Equation Options | Default font for math regions.

One of the GUST math fonts is called Termes Math. It is extremely similar to Times New Roman. Termes Math has no alternative set of old style numerals -- only the lining ones. This would provide a solution to mixing old style in the text and new style in equations without having to run Jay's macro. But Termes Math and possibly other GUST fonts may lack alternatives that are present in Cambria Math.

In a new workbook, set up a Sheet as you wish including formatting rules.

Delete all other sheets from that workbook.

Save the workbook as a Template named SHEET.xltx

Store that workbook in folder. . . . .

C:\Users\User\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Templates

To insert a new sheet in Excel, right-click on any sheetname and Insert. . . then select SHEET.xltx from the list.
