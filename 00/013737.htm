John McGhie's Advice on using email while traveling...

There *is* a simpler way -- use an SMTP server that permits "Authenticated Connection". Such a server will enable you to connect no matter where you are. I use the one at www (dot) fastmail (dot) com (dot) au. That permits me to connect and send from anywhere in the world (I also move around a lot) and even provides alternative ports for use when connected to an ISP that blocks email ports.

In "Outgoing Server" specify "Use SSL to connect". It will slow you down a little, but prevent creeps from intercepting your mail. Then click "More Options" and specify your (fully qualified) login details. For example "Alex(at)Swinburne(dot)edu(dot)au" instead of "AlexF", because when you are connecting externally, the email server cannot use your ISP domain.

Sadly, some ISPs prevent all external access to their SMTP servers. You will need to try with both to see which one works. Then just leave that one set, regardless of where you connect from.

If you decide to try FastMail, you will need a paid-for account to get the high-end features, one of which enables you to cause the FastMail server to drop a copy of everything you send into your Sent Mail folder, so you have a record of what you send, no matter where you sent it from. Another one I use all the time is "Reflect" which forwards any mail received by your FastMail account to the Work email address you specify, but codes it so that if you Reply, the reply goes through the FastMail server and is re-addressed so it looks and behaves like it came from "Your Account", not your "Work Account". I find that extremely valuable one place I work, which prevents access to webmail :-)

Do you mean gradient? Or are you referring to a different [i.e., lighter] shade of a particular color?

How are you going about it?

There's a distinction between Shading & Fills. Shading applies to paragraphs of body text & Table Cells whereas Fills are properties of Shapes, including Text Boxes.

Paragraph Shading using gradients is not an option in any version of Word, Mac or Windows. Shading can only be applied as a solid color, but the Mac version offers more options in that regard than what the Windows versions provide. If you go to Format> Borders & Shading - Shading, then click on the More Colors button in Mac Word you have your choice of 5 separate sets of Color Pickers (represented by buttons at the top of the pane) which offer a virtually unlimited range. You can choose from an extensive array of formulated colors or blend your own custom colors of any intensity you wish. Patterns can be selected as well. Shading is applied as a formatting attribute of the paragraph or cell.

Fills, OTOH, are a property of a Shape. They can be any Solid shade, as Gradients [blends from one color or shade to another], as patterns, Textures or even Pictures. In order to use Fills to accent body text, though, they have to be applied to a Shape such as a rectangle. The Shape also needs to have Text Wrapping applied to it so it can be sent behind the text or, if sufficiently transparent, allowed to overlay the text.
