    waseemulla shariff say:

    Hi glasstumbler,

    1. Do you have multiple partitions on your XP computer?
    2. Are you transferring the data only from C drive or from multiple drives?

    Windows Easy Transfer maps the old computer drives to the new computer drives while transferring data, i.e If you choose to transfer files and settings from the C and D drives on your old computer, but your new computer only has a C drive, then you will be asked to select which drive on your new computer you want to transfer the information to, however if both computers have the same drive letters, you will not see this page. Instead, information on each drive on your old computer will be transferred to the respective drives on your new computer. For example, if your old computer has C and D drives, then information on those drives will be transferred to the C and D drives on your new computer.

    If the size on the other partition doesn't have enough disk space then we come across the error: "Not enough hard drive space available"

    We would recommend:
    a. Click on Advanced when transferring files from the old computer and select the only files and settings that you want to transfer, if other partitions are selected make sure to uncheck them if it’s not required for you to transfer.
    b. If you would like to create new drives or increase drive space on your new computer, do it before starting the transfer.
    c. Try to transfer one partition data at a time and check.

    Important: Once the transfer process is successful or failure, you can anytime view Windows Easy Transfer Reports to know the details about the transfer.
    See Transfer files and settings from another computer

    2. See Not enough hard drive space available
