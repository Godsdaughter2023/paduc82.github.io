Fomula to calculate the area of a regular hexagon - Microsoft Community

jeffjensen say:

Fomula to calculate the area of a regular hexagon

Say I have a 3 inch hex (measured across the short diameter) in A1. What is the formula in B1 to determine the surface area?

Thanks,

Jeff

mike middleton say:

In cell A1 enter the short diameter of a regular hexagon, where "short diameter" is the distance from the midpoint of a side to the midpoint of the opposite side.

For the area, in cell B1 enter

=A1*A1*SQRT(3)/2

or

=A1^2*SQRT(3)/2

For a short diameter = 3 inches, the area is approximately 7.794 square inches.

(The "short diameter" described above is twice the length of an apothem.)

Windows Hello quit working - how to fix it - Microsoft Community

dcjoshua say:

Windows Hello quit working - how to fix it

Hi, a number of people, including me, have reported that Windows Hello quit working in the midst of all the false low storage notification / 10586.29 stuck update / overheating drama.  I've seen the complaints, but I'm the only one who mentioned a solution in that 30-page thread, and more people subsequently appear to have reported the problem and may not have seen what I wrote.  So I am making a new post in hopes they will find it.

Power cycle and soft reset do not fix the problem.  Hard reset is not necessary, though.

Just do the Improve recognition step one time.  As soon as you do it, Windows Hello starts working again and can recognize you.

This worked for me, several times, while I was still experiencing the other issues like overheating and low storage notifications, app updates failing, etc.

(Until it stops again.  Hopefully, everyone gets the 10586.29 update or later to load successfully and we can move past this early adopter growing pain.)

Settings > Your Accounts > Sign-in Options > Improve Recognition.

For the record, my phone eventually updated without me having to do a hard reset.  Some people in the 30-page thread have decided that hard reset is the only solution, and while it may be true that there is a way to force the update in through a hard reset, it is not the only way.  Going on memory, I think I was at a point where the Phone Update was failed, and offering me "Retry" button.  I stopped there, went to the store, got Field Medic.  Also hit retry as many times as necessary to update all my apps, Check for Updates (in the App store) again for good measure, then launched Field Medic and started logging.  Then I went back to the Phone update and hit Retry.  About 15 minutes later the phone asked to restart, another 15 minutes of spinning gears, then another 10 minutes of "migrating data", and then I was on 10586.29 and no more problems.  Battery life (and temperature) are fine now.

This may all be moot since the .29 update was pulled from the servers, but I thought I would share my experience.

Unlocked 950 XL Dual SIM.
