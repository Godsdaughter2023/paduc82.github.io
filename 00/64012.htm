If you're unable to connect your Surface Pro3 to a wireless display, please try these solutions:

Restart your Surface and your TV, monitor, or projector

Sometimes, a simple restart can resolve the issue. On your Surface, go to Start  > Power > Restart. Turn the TV or other display off and on, then try connecting them again.

Install the latest updates

In many cases, downloading and installing the latest updates for Windows and your Surface can help fix the issue. On how to install the latest updates, click here.

Turn off Bluetooth on your Surface

Bluetooth can occasionally interfere with Miracast. Go to Action center in the taskbar and make sure Bluetooth is off.

cmdrkeene say:

To remove your account, you first need to create an account that someone else can use. This will only take about 1 minute.  Here's how:

  1. Login to the computer and open the Settings apps
  2. Go to Accounts, and choose "Family & Other Users"
  3. Click "Add Someone Else to this PC"
  4. Ignore the request for an email address, and click "I don't have this person's sign-in info"
  5. Ignore the form to create a new account and click "Sign in without a Microsoft Account"
  6. Provide a username and password that they want to use
  7. After creating it, click the new account and choose "Change Type" and make sure it is set as an administrator (not standard) user.  They will need this so they can use the computer.

Now that you've created the new account, log out and sign into the new account. Go back to settings and now you can delete yours.
