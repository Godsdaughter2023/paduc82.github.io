Login Information

  • Gateway - 192.168.2.1
  • Username - N/A (None)
  • Password - N/A (None)

DMZ

  1. Open a web browser.
  2. In the address bar at the top, type in 192.168.2.1
  3. Click Login in the upper right.
  4. Input the password and click Login (by default, the password should be left blank).
  5. Select DMZ from the menu on the left under the Firewall section.
  6. Check the box below Enable.
  7. Enter the IP Address of the Xbox.
  8. Select Apply Changes at the bottom of the page.

Disable DMZ

  1. Open a web browser.
  2. In the address bar at the top, type in 192.168.2.1
  3. Click Login in the upper right.
  4. Input the password and click Login (by default, the password should be left blank).
  5. Select DMZ from the menu on the left under the Firewall section.
  6. Uncheck the box below Enable.
  7. Select Apply Changes at the bottom of the page.

Resolved for me (Issues and solutions)

1. Xbox One says that it has lost signal.

  Connect HDMI to Xbox first then to Laptop. One of KryptoNite 420's Ideas was to play an audio file on loop in VLC while connecting it (Which is a great idea, at the time though XBMC was playing so I's say an audio steam of any sort will work as a keep alive signal while connecting it)

2. Screen Lag

  Their is a noticeable lag between your actions on the computer and them being displayed to your tv via passthru. To fix this I set the laptop to display only to second screen (Xbox One) at 1080 and the lag is gone ( Or extremely slight ) by default the laptop tried to display as clones (same image on laptop and tv and i believe the scaling is where the issue came from). You can adjust this setting in Display properties.

3. TV app appears to be froze

   Some reason Xbox has the HDMI input suspend when their is no activity i.e. no fullscreen motion or audio stream. The way I get around this is just that I have a wireless keyboard and press the play button (Start streaming music) and the activity wakes the input back up. (Xbox if your reading this can you please turn off the suspend for the HDMI input) (In other words if their isn't big changes acress your screen and no sound the input goes to sleep, not an issue if it where actual tv input because the tv constantly refreshes with now images and never shuts up, but a laptop could sit idle at the desktop with no sound allowing the HDMI to suspend creating the impression that the TV app froze)

Some suggestions from Kryptonite 420 that I think would be good for anyone who wants this setup:

Set laptop to do nothing when you close the lid (If you have an external keyboard and mouse you wont need access anyway)

Disable sleep mode

and set to never shut off or put to sleep the monitor
