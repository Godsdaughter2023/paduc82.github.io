Correct Function to Calculate Compound Interest - Microsoft Community

jim simpson99 say:

Correct Function to Calculate Compound Interest

I'm having trouble determining and making work the correct financial function in excel to calculate monthly compound interest today.

$10,000 is the deposit on March 25-2011

24% is the annual interest rate compounded at 2% monthly.

Interest is compounded monthly on the monthly anniversary date of the deposit.  For this deposit it would be 25 April, 25, May, etc.

What would be the total interest due on today's date?  (Using TODAY() function.

Thanks

JIM
joeu2004 say: Jim wrote:
I'm having trouble determining and making work the correct financial function in excel to calculate monthly compound interest today.
$10,000 is the deposit on March 25-2011
24% is the annual interest rate compounded at 2% monthly.
Interest is compounded monthly on the monthly anniversary date of the deposit.  For this deposit it would be 25 April, 25, May, etc.

What would be the total interest due on today's date?  (Using TODAY() function.

The key question is:  how is the daily interest rate computed?  That is really up to the bank.  I will use 24%/365.

If the account terms are literally "compounded monthly", not simply "paid monthly", I presume daily interest does not compound [1].

Based on those assumptions, one way to compute "total interest due today" is (ERRATA in bold)

A1, start date:                        =DATE(2011,3,25)
A2, end date:                         =TODAY()
A3, whole months:               =DATEDIF(A1,A2,"m")
A4, additional days:             =A2-EDATE(A1,A3)
A5, last monthly balance:   =FV(2%,A3,0,-10000)
A6, partial-month interest:  =ROUND(A5*A4*24%/365,2)
A7, total interest paid:          =ROUND(A5-10000,2)+A6

If the bank compounds interest in the last partial-month [1], the formula in A6 would be:

=ROUND(A5*(1+24%/365)^A4-A5,2)

Note:  The amount in A5 is really an approximation.  If the bank is truly paying interest to the account each month, that amount is rounded to the penny each month.   The FV function does not do that.

Caveat:  DATEDIF(...,"m") and EDATE do not compute whole months the same way.  There should be no problem with the days 1 through 28, even through 30 except when the last month is February.

-----
[1] For some securities like CDs, interest usually compounds daily even if the terms say monthly.  And some banks will compute compounded daily interest for the last partial month of an account, regardless of how the terms are stated.
