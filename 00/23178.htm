jeeped say:

It is actually pretty easy to fools the Office programs into doing something that was programmed out of them, e.g. putting a period (full stop) after etc. The trick is to get Word to put in a period while it thinks it is putting something else in.

Open a blank document and type a few words then etc <without a period> and leave the cursor at the end of the word. Now use the ribbon's Insert tab, Text group, Quick Parts, Field. Choose =(Formula) from the list at the left, then click the Formula... button to the right. Formula: should be =0, and the Number Format: should be . (just a period/decimal point/full stop, nothing else).

The "etc" you typed should be "etc.". Highlight (e.g. select) the three letters and the field and hit CTRL+C.

Go to Office button, Word Options, Proofing, Autocorrect Options... and create a new entry. Replace etc With <paste the copied etc. here>. Click OK, OK and test it out.

On a side note, I noticed that with the regular full stop Autocorrect was changing the text if the E in Etc was capitolized (beginning of a sentance), but not if it was lower case.

I've found answer to this.

You need to modify the registry.

1. Open regedit

2. HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE -> SOFTWARE -> Microsoft -> Windows -> CurrentVersion -> Policies -> System

3. On the right, you will see ConsentPromptBehaviorAdmin, ConsentPromptBehaviorUser ... etc.

4. click on empty space, ADD DWORD(32bit), then name it "EnableLinkedConnections". Double click it and set the data to 1.

5. Reboot and you will see the mapped drives showing.

This has worked for me.
