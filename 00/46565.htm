steve winograd say:

The volume label is the string that appears before the drive letter when you view the drive in My Computer .  For example, if it says External Drive (E:) , the volume label is External Drive .

The convert command will preserve the files on the external hard drive.

If you don't need to preserve the files on the external hard drive, you can right-click and format the drive as NTFS (which erases everything on it) and specify a new volume label.  That's much more efficient than converting the drive from FAT to NTFS.
