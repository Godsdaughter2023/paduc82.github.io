The best course of action would be to study the current search box. How does it work? What properties and events are involved in making it do its thing. Paragraph Marks - Microsoft Community

snoozeforsuz say:

Paragraph Marks

I'm an editor and work on a PC with manuscripts that were generated outside of my office, possibly on other word processing programs. In some docs, especially when poetry is involved, the paragraph marks at the end of the line look fine. However, they are not true Word paragraph marks. This came up when I was electronically style tagging a manuscript. I tagged the first line of poetry and all the following lines changed to the new style. Naturally, I checked to make sure that my style tag wasn't based on another style tag. It was not. Then I discovered that if I place my cursor at the end of the line, it shows up to the right of the paragraph mark. So, these breaks have the paragraph mark as their symbol but act like soft returns. Has anyone encountered this and is there a name for these marks and a way to search for them?

Thanks!

suzanne s. barnhill say:
It could be that the breaks are line breaks masquerading as paragraph breaks. This is sometimes seen when text is pasted from an email or the Web. You can find and replace them by searching for ^013.

snoozeforsuz say: Thanks, Suzanne. When I search using ^013, it finds all breaks, paragraph marks included. I have searched using ^l for line breaks, but that only finds the soft returns. I've also tried copying and pasting the offending paragraph mark into the find-and-replace box, but it pastes as a blank space.

stefan blom say:

If you have manual line breaks that you don't want to get rid of, you can do the find and replace in three steps:

1. Search for ^l and replace with, say, %%%.

2. Replace ^13 with ^p.

3. Finally, replace %%% with ^l.
