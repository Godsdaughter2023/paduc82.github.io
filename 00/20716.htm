  2. Click settings (looks like a little gear icon), and then more mail settings.
  3. Click sending/receiving email from other accounts.
  4. Click add an email account and follow the steps to input your POP3 and SMTP server info.

Note: for additional details, see this article: http://windows.microsoft.com/en-US/hotmail/other-accounts-mail-ui?woldogcb=0.

 

  1. Once that is successfully setup – that is, Outlook.com is now syncing your POP3 mail to your Microsoft account – you can enable “mail” for this Microsoft account on Windows Phone. To do this: 
    1. On Windows Phone, go to Settings > Email+Accounts, tap Microsoft account and tap “email” under content to sync. 
    2. There will now be an Outlook icon on your start screen that represents the inbox at Outlook.com. If you get mail in this way, then you should remove the broken POP3 mail account from his phone.

You can modify the point size of the Line Number character style.
