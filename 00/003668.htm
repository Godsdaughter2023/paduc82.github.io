acellis say: Hey John,

I believe I found a workaround, though it is sure to leave your Inbox a disorganized mess.

After I deleted all the "custom" subfolders that I created in Outlook, my inboxes seem to be synchronizing properly.  Send/Receive All Folders (whether it performs as scheduled or I execute it manually) now actually synchs and updates each Inbox with unread mail counts for new mail.  

You can change the frequency of the S/R All schedule under File --> Options --> Advanced --> Send/Receive.  Additionally (based on a suggestion in another thread), I also edited the Send/Receive Groups settings for each of my accounts to select "Use the custom behavior defined below" and checked only the Inbox for each account.  I do not think this is a necessary step/setting, but I don't plan to reset it to "Download complete items...for subscribed folders" unless synchs begin acting up again.

I think the issue is that Outlook is unable to process the hierarchy of user-created subfolders, as they don't exist at the other end (in the actual mailbox), so Outlook can't actually synch the folder, gets an error, and doesn't finish updating the Inbox.  Ideally, Outlook would recognize that subfolders are really just an extension of the Inbox and "ignore" the hierarchy synch "error" (basically, Outlook would "know" that emails in subfolders are Inbox items but have been "flagged" by the user to show up in a subfolder), but apparently this is too much to ask of MS programmers.

Larry Ruehl's post in the linked thread is the original source for this workaround:  http://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/office/forum/office_2013_release-outlook/outlook-2013-sync-issues-8004010f-0-0-720/b3da93e8-39df-48a6-bf61-c1adb4f7f138?page=2

I didn't go through all the steps he mentioned as my accounts were pretty new.  I just moved all of the emails that I had placed in subfolders back into their Inbox and deleted the subfolders entirely.

I believe that if I were to log into my mailbox and first create subfolders there, Outlook should see that the subfolders then exist and allow me to subscribe to them, at which point they would be visible and usable in Outlook.  That's a complete guess though.  I may get around to testing it out, but I'm hopeful that Microsoft will get their sh*t together and fix this mess.

Another suggestion was to open several instances of Outlook and have each instance set to activate a different Inbox, so all Inboxes are activated/selected at once and all update immediately.

SWITCH FROM WORKING OFFLINE TO ONLINE

Applies To: Outlook 2016Outlook 2013Outlook 2010Outlook 2007

In Outlook, you have the flexibility to make Outlook work online or offline with your mail server.

2016, 2013 2010 2007

How do you know when you're working offline or online in Outlook? There are visual cues, starting with the status bar at the bottom of your Outlook window. If either of the following status indicators are shown, it means Outlook isn’t connected to your mail server:

or

You can’t send or receive email until you reconnect.

If the Status is WORKING OFFLINE

Returning to an online work mode is a simple process.

  • In Outlook, on the Send/Receive tab, choose Work Offline to reconnect.


Notes: 

  • If the background of the Work Offline button is shaded (as shown in the image above), you're not connected. But if the background is clear, you are connected.

  • If you don’t see the Work Offline button and your email account is a Microsoft Exchange Server account, Cached Exchange Mode might be turned off. Follow the steps in Turn on Cached Exchange Mode to change that setting. Or contact the person who manages the Exchange server for your organization.

There can be different reasons why you're disconnected from Outlook. What follows are just a few examples of some of the possible reasons.

  • If your Internet connection is working, there might be a problem with the mail server.

  • If you can log onto your email from a website—for example, http://outlook.com—check to see whether you can receive and send email that way. If email isn’t working on the website, call your technical support for your email service provider for help.

  • If you can send and receive mail on the website, then your mail server is fine. But your computer might need updates or there might be a problem with your account settings.

Tips for reconnecting to Outlook

Here are some suggested ways to reconnect to Outlook.

Reset the Work Offline status

  • On the Send/Receive tab, choose Work Offline, and check your status bar. If the status shows Working Offline, repeat the action until the status changes to connected.
