graham r seach say:
Hi Greg,
 
Firstly, you're relying on the fields' ordinal positions when you refer to them as rec.Fields(1) and so on. It would be much better for you to to refer to them by their indivudual field names, by using rec![My field name].Value.  That way, if you move the fields around in the table, your code won't break.
 
Secondly, you don't need to use their Value property (rec.Fields(1).Value) because the Value property is the default. Just refer to them by name: rec![My field name]. Just a side note; you only need to use square brackets  [ ]  if the object name contains spaces or special characters, or is a reserved keyword.
 
Thirdly, when you setup strCriteria, why are you formatting the date just so? If you are using US date format, you don't need to format the date at all, in which case strCriteria becomes as follows:
    strCriteria = "[Bill Date] = #" & Forms![FRM-Wireless Email Managers Report]!Text1 "# AND [DepartmentName = """ & Department & """"
 
If you're using UK date format, use this construct:
    strCriteria = "[Bill Date] = #" & Format(Forms![FRM-Wireless Email Managers Report]!Text1, 'mm-dd-yyyy') & "# AND [DepartmentName = """ & Department & """"
 
If, however, this code is being executed from Forms![FRM-Wireless Email Managers Report], then you can shorten the whole thing like so:
    strCriteria = "[Bill Date] = #" & Me!Text1 "# AND [DepartmentName = """ & Department & """"
...or...
    strCriteria = "[Bill Date] = #" & Me!Text1, 'mm-dd-yyyy') & "# AND [DepartmentName = """ & Department &
 
Then, as Nadia suggests, you can call the report like so:
    DoCmd.OpenReport ReportName, acViewPreview, , , strCriteria
