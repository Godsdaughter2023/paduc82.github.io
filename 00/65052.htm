Since Vista, the "Timeout Detection and Recovery" (TDR) components of the OS video subsystem have been capable of doing some truly impressive things to try to recover from issues which would have caused earlier OS's like XP to crash.

arminatorx say:

I understand your Ping measurements differ between your Xbox and your "internet". 

First, you probably need to understand what exactly Ping is, and how it is measured. 

In order to get a ping measurement, you choose a server that is supposed to return your ping measurement, then you send a ping packet through the internet to that server, the server returns it as soon as it arrives on it's network and the time it takes for your data packet to leave your console, reach the server and come back to your console is the ping. 

To make a real world analogy: 

If you have a race car driver at your home, you can ask him to drive to the store down your street, and come back while you clock the time. It might take him a few minutes to drive down the street, get a punchcard stamped to prove he was at the store and then return home. 

Now you decide you want to Ping the Xbox campus in Redmond. Let's assume you live in Europe, so your racecar driver leaves your garage, you start the stopwatch, and the driver speeds out of town, towards the next airport, gets into the plane, flies around the globe to reach the USA, get into a rental car, drive across the country to finally reach Redmond, get his punchcard stamped at the Xbox Campus, then get back into the car, drive back across the USA to the airport, get into the plane, fly back to Europe, into the sports car, and race back to your house. 

As soon as he reaches your home, this trip took several days. 

So in that case you wouldn't complain about the trip to Redmond taking several days compared to the trip down the road to your supermarket being slower. 

So to compare only ping numbers is of no use, as you don't tell us which target you pinged. 

If you ping a server that is on the other end of the world, the signal natually takes longer than pinging a server near your Internet Provider's location. 

So if by your "internet" ping you mean you used Speedtest to ping the nearest location, try to manually set the speedtest target to be near Seattle or Redmond in Washington USA. 

Then compare that ping with the ping measurements you get from your Xbox console's network test.
