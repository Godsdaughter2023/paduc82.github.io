bill manville say:

Do you mean delete cells (moving cells below up or moving cells left from the right)?

Or do you mean clear the cells of their content but leave the cells where they are?

By after 180 days I assume you mean 180 days after that particular cell data was updated, rather than 180 days after the last update of any such cell?

How would you know when the "x" or "o" was entered into the cell?

You could use a companion cell for each candidate cell in which to record the date/time of last entry.  You might choose to use a hidden worksheet for this. A Worksheet_Change event procedure on the entry sheet would record the information. 
If you never add or delete cells or sort the content of the entry worksheet then the date/time could be recorded together with the address of the changed cell, but that would be invalidated by adding, deleting or sorting.  A formula linking to the entry cell would survive adding and deleting but not sorting.  So that's a non-starter if adding, deleting or sorting are possible.

The other option that comes to mind is to automatically attach a comment to the cell giving the date/time of entry.  That will travel with the cell as additions, deletions and sorting take place.

If you need help to proceed, please clarify the requirement by answering my questions above and advising whether adding, deleting and sorting would be possible on the entry sheet.
