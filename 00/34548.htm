vijin v say:

Hi Kan,



How may drive are there on your computer?


We need to have an “unallocated disk space” to the right of the Partition\Volume you wish to extend on the same drive. Only when “unallocated disk space” is available “extend” option is highlighted or available. (As it is likely your D: partition is right next to it - you CAN NOT add the D: partition or part of it to your C: drive).


(Press Windows + R and type diskmgmt.msc to open Disk Management.)



Note: Please take a complete backup or move all your important files from D: driver before deleting D drive.



We have to DELETE your D: partition completely (this means you will need to copy/save any files on your D: partition first). Right click on the D: partition and delete it. Then we will be able to add or extent C: driver.



Once you delete the D: drive, This will free up the entire  GB of space on your drive and will create and place the "unallocated free space" immediately to the right of your C: drive.



You will now see that when you right click on the C: drive that the option to "extend" your C: drive is now available. Click "Extend..." and then option windows will open. At that point you may choose to allocate all remaining free space to your C: drive OR you may allocate whatever amount you'd like to your C: drive. You are then allowed to recreate your D: drive with the remaining unallocated free space.
