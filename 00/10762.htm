jay freedman say:
There is a way to get an editable printout of the styles, if you have OneNote as part of your Office installation. Select "Send to OneNote" as the printer, and print the style information to that.

Initially OneNote gets a picture of each page of output. Right-click each page in turn and choose "Copy Text from This Page of the Printout", and then paste the text into a blank Word document or another OneNote notebook.

The other way to go about this is to just make a list of all the fields in the Font and Paragraph dialogs plus the few additional ones in the Modify Style dialog (e.g., "Based On" and "Following Style"). The only other information that's in the printout is whether a style is a Quick Style (i.e., whether it's shown in the gallery on the Home tab) and the style's Priority and Hide Until Used values (set in the Manage Styles dialog, which you open from the third button at the bottom of the Styles pane).
