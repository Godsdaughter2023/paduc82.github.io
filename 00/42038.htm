mikereardon_texas say:

Onedrive moving files.

Onedrive has moved my documents, desktop and pictures from my PC to Onedrive. I prefer not doing this. Is there a way to change the option where I can use Onedrive as a back-up only and keep my files on my PC?

davem121 say:
Hi Mike

Right-click the OneDrive icon in the notification area, and select Settings

Click on the Settings tab. under "Files On-Demand," see if the 'Save space and download files as you use them' is ticked

That option would cause your files to be only stored on OneDrive


Otherwise:

Click your Start Button, type regedit and hit Enter to open the Registry Editor

Click View and make sure 'Address Bar' is turned on

Paste this into the Address Bar at the top and hit Enter

HKEY_CURRENT_USER\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\Explorer\User Shell Folders


Please provide a screenshot of that Registry Editor window

sarahkong say:
HI MikeReardon_Texas ... The way onedrive works is it syncs your files from PC to the onedrive cloud. Backing up would do essentially the same thing.
Let me explain further.
Currently your files are all on your PC as well as in Onedrive.
Every few minutes they sync any changes.
They have not moved your files they are just syncing.
So the files are stored in both locations. Therefore not requiring a backup.
If you were to disable this capability then you would have to setup backups and use a 3rd party utility to do that as the built in backup in Windows works sometimes.
If there are any folders that you do NOT want in onedrive then all you need to do is go to onedrive settings click account tab and select choose folders.
Does this help clarify things?
