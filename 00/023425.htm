Try the below..which I have tried with the sample data in Sheet1. The resultant table will be created in Sheet2

Sub MyMacro()
Dim lngRow As Long, lngCol As Long
Dim lngTRow As Long, wsDest As Worksheet

Set wsDest = Sheets("Sheet2")

For lngRow = 2 To Cells(Rows.Count, "A").End(xlUp).Row
For lngCol = 4 To Cells(1, Columns.Count).End(xlToLeft).Column
lngTRow = lngTRow + 1
wsDest.Range("A" & lngTRow) = Range("A" & lngRow)
wsDest.Range("B" & lngTRow) = Range("B" & lngRow)
wsDest.Range("C" & lngTRow) = Range("C" & lngRow)
wsDest.Range("D" & lngTRow) = Cells(1, lngCol)
wsDest.Range("E" & lngTRow) = Cells(lngRow, lngCol)
Next
Next

mattrome say:

When migrating to new workstation, do i need to manually transfer OST , PST files?

Hello tech gods.

Question: When migrating to a new workstation, if I want Outlook to work exactly as it did on my old workstation, do i need to manually transfer the Outlook OST , and PST files?

If so, what is the procedure to do this?

Thank you!

-Matt

diane poremsky mvp (slipstick.com) say:
You'll move your pst files, but will need to let outlook recreate the ost files - they work only in the account and profile that created them.  You'll need to add the accounts to the profile yourself.  See move outlook to a new computer - finishing touches for other files you might want to move.

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:
One other thing to keep in mind with OSTs.  If any of the folders in the account's data file say "This computer only" after them, then save the contents of those folders in a PST or you will lose the data when you recreate the account.

mattrome say: Thank you for your reply.

How do I save the contents in a "PST"?

brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:
Create the PST: New Items>Outlook Data File.  Then copy the data (items or folders) to that data file.  Take the dta file to the new PC and add it to Outlook there.
