hal hostetler - mvp-outlook say:

Your smartphone and tablet do not now, nor have they ever had any knowledge of Outlook 2007.  All three are email clients and clients do not talk to, work with, or know about each other.  All they know about and are able to work with is your email SERVER (Comcast, in this case). What you see on each device depends on what kind of email account you have and how its setup. 

If you have an IMAP account, all three should show the same folder structure and have the same email content; IMAP is designed to work with multiple devices and to keep folders and email on all clients synchronized.  If you have a POP account, none of the three clients will be in sync unless you "fudge" the account settings by instructing all three clients to "Leave a copy of emails on the server" and instructing ONE of the three to "Remove emails from the server after (xx) days".  POP is designed so that when a client connects, it downloads all available email, then deletes it from the server.  In such a case, if you connect with, say, your smartphone, it will get everything on the server at the time.  If you then connect with your tablet, the only email you'll get on it is what arrived between the time you closed the smartphone and opened the tablet. POP accounts were never designed for multiple device sync.  The SSL and TSL settings only deal with getting the client connected to the server and have NO effect on synchronization what so ever.

So, how are the three setup, POP or IMAP?  If they're setup as POP, are all three configured to leave a copy of email on the server and is one of them setup to remove the email from the server after (xx) days?

Unfortunately, there is no overlapping font support for Tamil between OS X and Windows.
