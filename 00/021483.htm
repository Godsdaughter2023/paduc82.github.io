suzanne s. barnhill say:

Backgrounds are intended for Web pages and do not print. Word 2003 has a Print Options setting that allows you to print it, but I believe that Word 2002 does not have this setting. In that version you would have to save the document as an HTML file, view it in a browser, and print from the browser with the Internet Options set to print background colors and images.

Instead of using a background, you can insert a full-page-size texture-filled rectangle anchored to the header and formatted as Behind Text (see http://sbarnhill.mvps.org/WordFAQs/AnchorToHeader.htm). Be aware, however, that your printer very likely won't be able to print clear to the edge of the page, so there will be a white border.

After lots of trial and error I found a link online that finally fixed my problem and frustrations. I hope it will help fix your issue. Please respond to me to let me know if it helped you. 

  1. At the stop  of the screen in Outlook go to Send/Receive, then Send/receive groups, and define send/receive.
  2. On the right side of the send/receive groups window click on the Edit button.
  3. Uncheck the "Get folder unread count for subscribed folders".

  5. In the next window below select INBOX and leave the other folders unchecked.
  6. Click OK and then exit out of your options.
  7. Do a send/receive to verify your Outlook is faster now.
