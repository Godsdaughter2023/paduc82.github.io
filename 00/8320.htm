rob koch say:

As cyber has mentioned, you have this backwards, it is malicious actors who try to use often not so well known Microsoft Windows operating system file names or something quite similar in an attempt to fool less knowledgeable people into believing something invalid..

In the case of the osrss.dll and related files, these are portions of the OS Remediation System Service which is sometimes loaded in order to deal with problems occurring in the Windows Updates on a specific PC system.

The following thread in these forums goes into some detailed discussions of these files and their purpose, as well as how to tell whether they're really the versions distributed by Microsoft.

https://answers.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/forum/windows_10-other_settings/os-remediation-system-service/671c62b2-705a-44c1-870d-e1ed6555be37

Malicious actors like to use such lesser known and relatively hidden file names in the fake articles or other scams they create, since most people don't have the ability to tell whether these are fake or real.  So you'll often find such invalid information relating to exactly the same file names, even though the files themselves may be the real Microsoft ones and totally benign.

You have to use your own brain to decide whether those displaying the information are reputable like Microsoft or simply someone you don't know trying to scam you.

In other words, use some common sense.

Rob
