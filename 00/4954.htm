I solved this by updating my Android System WebView via Google Play

https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.google.android.webview

On Android 7.0 and above Android System WebView is now a part of Google Chrome. Updating and enabling WebView as described above therefore isn't possible anymore. In my case I got the blank screen by some issue related to this...

I got Cortana and my other apps (with the same issue) to work again by following this metode:

1. Go to phone settings > apps > Chrome, press disable

2. Update and enable Android System WebView (see link above)

3. Your apps with blank screen will now likely work again. :)

Dilemma, now you don't have Google Chrome, but your other apps likely works, 

In Cortana I only had to sign into the app using the WebView, and then I could enable Google Chrome again making both apps work side by side. In apps that uses WebView more frequently, disabling and enabling Chrome regularly might be a downside, I belive this could be solved by just downloading another brownser like Dolphine, Firefox etc.

vincentchoy say:

Yammer information is organized into 2 levels. 

1. Network

2. Groups

Network -

Each Office365 tenant typically has one corporate internal network. This network is restricted to (a) employees with the company who have employee email (b) individuals invited by Yammer admin who may not be employees of the company, also known as guests.  So information found on the corporate internal network would be restricted to the above mentioned.

Yammer also allows external networks. These are additional networks that users/admin can create, that allow collaboration with people outside your organization. Information put into a particular external network would be viewable by members of that external network.

As a rule of thumb, information on each network can only be viewed by members of that particular network.

Group

Within each network, for better contextualization, would be groups. There are two types of groups. Private groups and non-private groups. Information in private groups are restricted to only members of that particular group. Information in other groups are visible to people on the network where the group resides.

So to answer your question

can people outside my network access my data / information / communication etc 

Answer : No

 

can people outside my group access my data / information / communication etc

Answer : Yes. If your group is public, and they are members of your network.

Answer : No, if your group is private and they are not members of the group.
