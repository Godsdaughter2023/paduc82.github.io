john mcghie say:
The simplest way out of this is simply to hit three page breaks on the one-page section.  Fix the headers and footers, then delete the page breaks.  (I think it's the ONLY way...)

It's a very good idea to get your headers and footers correct before you add the section-breaks.  That way, all your "Same as Previous" and headers and footers will be correct on every section, because each section break you add copies the header and footer specs from the one below it.

Add two section breaks at the front to isolate the cover page and front matter.  Then get the headers and footers to be correct automatically using the StyleRef fields as required.

You may then not need to add any further section breaks.  But if you do (to force a Chapter to start on a right page) then its headers and footers will automatically be correct.

Hope this helps
