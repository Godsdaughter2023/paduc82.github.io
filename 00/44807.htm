The issue is somehow related to disk manager and the windows service initiating the device. (CompMgmtLauncher.exe and svchost.exe)

Using SysInternal's Procmon, I was able to track the registry access when the external USB device was plugged-in.

I noticed that the USB drive entry was not present under:

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Enum\USBSTOR

Upon detecting the drive, the entry is now present:

HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Enum\USBSTOR\Disk&Ven_WD&Prod_5000BEV_External&Rev_1.75

and also:


HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Control\DeviceClasses\{53f56307-b6bf-11d0-94f2-00a0c91efb8b}\##?#USBSTOR#Disk&Ven_WD&Prod_5000BEV_External&Rev_1.75#57442D575852304134395535363938&0#{53f56307-b6bf-11d0-94f2-00a0c91efb8b}

The process responsible for this was: C:\Windows\system32\svchost.exe

Earlier it called the following command:

C:\Windows\system32\svchost.exe -k DcomLaunch

It seems to be there was a security privilege issue which maybe related to DCOM.

What resolved my issue permanently was I had disk management open (mmc.exe snap-in) and then plugged in my external USB HDD while I observed the

2 processes via procmon.

Anyway, I at least hope the above registry keys gives a clue between the interaction between svchost.exe and CompMgmtLauncher.exe in the external USB problems.

marilyn o say:

If you cannot view the Japanese characters properly in software programs even with the Japanese language pack installed, your software may not support Unicode.

Try making a change to the software by setting to non-Unicode. 
Go to Control Panel. 
Click on "Clock, Language, and Region". (If you view by Category)
Click on Region and Language. (If you view by Large Icons)
Click on "Change keyboards or other input methods" of "Regional and Language".
Go to Administrative tab.
In the section for Language for non-Unicode programs, click on the Change system locale button.
Click on drop down and select Japanese.
Restart your computer for the changes to be made.

Now try your program again and see if the Japanese characters are now correctly displayed.

Hope this helps.
