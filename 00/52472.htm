Need to replace/remove video and keep audio... HOW??? - Microsoft Community

ceemee1201 say:

Need to replace/remove video and keep audio... HOW???

I am trying to remove or replace video while keeping the audio.  I am looking for some sort of 'time-line' view ... but ...

Using Windows 7 and have had no other problems with this program.

PLEASE help!!!!! ( and thank you very much .... )
considerate_guy say:

In order to separate the audio from the original video (so you can put other video in its place), you need the music to be on a separate track.

This is most easily done in Movie Maker 2012 since it now allows you to load a video file as the added narration.  Start by adding your original video and then muting its volume (Video Tools | Edit, set Video Volume to the far left to mute it).

Now using the Add Narration button, choose that same video file a second time.  You'll see a copy of its audio added below and you'll see its waveform.

At this point the video and the audio are treated separately.  So if you wanted to insert a picture, you could trim out a portion of video, add in the photo, but the audio would be playing from the narration "track".

The caveat is you have to manually keep the two tracks in synch.  It is very important that what ever you remove from the video is then replaced by the exact same amount of video or photos.  If you don't do that, when the video returns to playing, the audio may be out of synch.
