Hi GiloEA,

I was also experiencing this strange issue for the last few days which started bugging me from last week.

If nobody is snooping around your WiFi to crack it, then you may try the fix which worked for me. You would have to change the authentication method for wireless on your router to WEP. 

Presuming that you are not on WEP already (Well nobody is nowadays :P), follow the below steps to change the wireless authentication method.

  1. Connect a computer via LAN to your Wireless router.
  2. Enter Router Login Page, should be mentioned on your router label as 'Default Gateway'. If you are unable to find it, use any Search Engine to find it.
  3. Find the Wireless Security settings under router settings and change it to WEP (Either 64 or 128 Bit).
  4. You would probably need to change your wireless password on the same page.
  5. Once you have changed the authentication method and saved changes, reboot your Router.
  6. If you have changed the password too, change it on your phone and other devices.

*The WEP authentication method is less secure as compared to WPA/WPA2 against snooping attempts, hence DO NOT use this method if you are concerned about your WiFi being misused.

If you find any issues while changing authentication method, it's better to call your tech. support than messing the whole configuration. Do let me know if this works for you.
