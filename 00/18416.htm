365 Business Premium - How to create an Outlook Archive - Microsoft Community

susanfox- [o365] say:

365 Business Premium - How to create an Outlook Archive

We are small business currently running installed Outlook and managing PST file for archiving. We like to ability to open PST files using Outlook on the desktop so we can search and find old emails.

We are migrating to Outlook 365 Premium and will continue to use synched Outlook clients on the desktop. However, we’d prefer not to import large PST files into Outlook 365 as we want to reduce the size of the synched files for reasons of speed etc.

Is there a way of providing access to older emails with Outlook 365 without having to have a large OST file? For example, can we either:

1 Open (NOT Import) old PST files using Outlook 2016

2 Import and archive (to the cloud) old emails from PST file, retaining the ability to search them online but not synch them to the client Outlook application?

Thanks. Any alternative ideas appreciated.

samwang_msft say:

Hi SusanFox,

The short answer is yes.

1. You may refer to this article to open .pst files in the Outlook 2016 client.
https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Open-Outlook-Data-Files-pst-381b776d-7511-45a0-953a-0935c79d24f2

2. Office 365 Business Premium subscription contains the In-Place Archive feature. The archived emails are restored online which doesn’t consume the desktop memory. If it is enabled for a certain mailbox, the In-Place Archive folder will appear automatically under your primary mailbox in OWA.

However, the In-Place Archive feature in the Outlook client requires the Outlook Proplus version which is included in Office 365 Enterprise E3/E4 subscription. If you decide to migrate to Business Premium plan, you may consider importing the old .pst files to your primary mailbox when you set up your account via Exchange in the Outlook client. Then after the emails are synced to OWA, you can manually drag those emails from the .pst files to the In-Place Archive folder in OWA. After that, the data can be searched in OWA as you want. Note that the storage of an archive mailbox is 50 GB in Business Premium plan.

About how to enable archive for Office 365 users:
https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/library/jj984357(v=exchg.150).aspx

Regarding importing .pst files in the Outlook client:
https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Import-Outlook-items-from-an-Outlook-Data-File-pst-345ad0da-7bc0-4a02-9cb7-2d1cf48ba8e3

Let us know if anything is unclear.

Sam
