syed n say:

Hi stuckfree,

 

Magnets are used in computers for various purposes, for instance in hard drives. Many parts of a computer/laptop can be affected by a magnet. The most commonly referred to piece of hardware affected by magnets is the hard drive.

This is not exactly true. Hard drives are well protected against most magnetism, and most even use a magnet to spin, but many strong magnets and electromagnets can and will damage a hard drive. More likely to be affected is your computer's BIOS (Basic Input\Output System) ROM, which tells your motherboard how to use basic components and boot into the Operating System.

So it is better to avoid placing computers\laptops close to any such appliances.

erdeslawe say:

You can try the HP Support Site - http://support.hp.com/gb-en/document/c04773236/

A Code 43 error is telling you that Windows 10 can't recognise the identity of the Graphics Card.  There is no solution to that unless the Graphics manufacturer provides one, which they won't do if they no longer support that card, i.e. it was never intended to work in Windows 10 (which was unknown when it was produced) and the manufacturer has moved on to newer things.

Windows Update will always try to find the best Driver match it can, but only has what the manufacturers' have supplied or basic/generic Drivers to choose from.  The Drivers are basically the controlling software for the hardware components in your computer and, if you don't have fully compatible drivers for your graphics card/chip then you may experience some display problems or some display controls/capabilities may not work as they should.
