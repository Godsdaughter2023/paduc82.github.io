jdkingocc say:

Cover Page before Section Break issue

I think I've found a bug.

 

To demonstrate:

 

  1. Start a new blank Word document
  2. Insert a cover page
  3. Insert a new page at the end of the document
  4. Insert a section break (on next page) at the end of the document
  5. Turn off link to previous on the header of this new section and type something in the footer
  6. Insert a new page at the bottom of the document, notice that the new page did not receive the footer
Now do the same steps above but swap steps 2 and 4, you'll get the expected behavior of new pages maintaining the footer of their section.

As long as you add at least one section break before inserting a cover page all section breaks work as expected.

rdrewd2 say:

Hi,

Sounds like you just missed the cutoff for renewal, which should only change the process slightly.  Unless they've changed something recently, they don't start uninstalling any software immediately upon expiration.  If you'd like to keep Office365 and use the new subscription you purchased, navigate back to the same accounts web page shown in the screenshot I posted earlier.  Instead of clicking "Services & Subscriptions" in the blue bar, click right next to that on "Payment & Billing".  From that drop-down menu, choose "Payment options", then click on "Redeem a code or gift card" and enter your new 25-digit subscription code there and follow the prompts to reactivate.  If for some reason you're having trouble getting to that page, you can also access it by clicking this link and signing in with your Microsoft account:  http://commerce.microsoft.com

Your problems connecting to help may be coming from their end, not yours, since you don't seem to have any connectivity or account issues.  You also mentioned the matter of limited funds (believe me, I understand); I would of course recommend staying with Office 365, but if it's an expense that you simply can't justify right now and decide to return your un-activated key, know that there are several free options available.  These are just examples, but: programs like LibreOffice or Apache OpenOffice can sit in for Word, Excel, Powerpoint, and Access, Thunderbird mail can sit in for Outlook, Dropbox can sit in for OneDrive, etc.; just keep your MS account active and when you strike it rich six months from now, switch back to 365.  Again, I'd stick with what you've got, but if you need some free alternatives to use for now, there are plenty of options out there.  Hope that helps.
