To restart, press and hold the Power button until a Slide down to power off message appears on-screen, then swipe down. (It usually takes about three seconds for the message to appear.) To turn your phone back on, press the Power button.

If that doesn't work, press and hold the Volume down and Power buttons at the same time until you feel a vibration (about 10 to 15 seconds), then release the buttons. Your phone will restart automatically.


Please be reminded that:

Your phone may vibrate as soon as you press the Volume down and Power buttons because it's changing your ringtone to vibrate. Wait to release the buttons until you feel the second vibration 10 to 15 seconds later.

If you don't let go of the buttons immediately after the vibration at 10 to 15 seconds, your phone may show a black startup screen instead of restarting normally. If that happens, try pressing the button combo again.

Not all phones restart this way, so check with your phone's manufacturer to find out how to force a restart.


If your phone has a replaceable battery (it's often under your phone's back panel), you can also restart your phone by removing and reinserting the battery, and then pressing the Power button to turn on your phone. The date and time may be off after you restart this way, but you can fix that by swiping over to the All apps list from Start, then selecting Settings > Time & language > Date & time.

I now have contacts and calendar duly in good working order on the new PC.

However, I have two sets of folders for the incoming email account (I have only one email account).

New emails go into a set of folders consisting of Input, Sent and Trash. The imported folders are more extensive, e.g. Purchases, Finance and so on, and are arranged so that I can sort emails using rules.

How can I get rid of the first set of folders and direct new emails into the second set of folders?

palcouk say:

Apparently your original OL2010 was using a pop mail account, and as such your method of transferring the data was incorrect.

With Ol2010 closed you should have copied the pst data files to a usb stick and thence to \Documents\Outlook Files\ on the new PC.

Then when you started OL2010 and commenced the config you should have manually added your mail account as a pop account, using the settings provided by your ISP, and then in data files tab set your copied pst data file as the default store.

Everything would then have been as it was before.

As it is you added your mail account as an IMAP account, hence the other set of folders (Trash etc)

karl timmermans [outlook mvp 2012-2018] say:

Unfortunately, the account I want to delete is the default account.

I can't set the account I want to retain as the default account, because the "set default" is greyed out when I select the account I want to retain.

Any further advice on resolving this problem would be appreciated.


Confirm the <type> (POP3, Imap etc) of account that you have configured.

From OL menu --> File --> Account Settings --Email tab

(shows email account(s) along with the type)

Under Data Files tab - shows the number of data files configured in the profile. Just for accuracy, the Outlook Nav Pane does not show <email accounts> but rather the data files configured for the profile

If it's IMAP, you will always have 2 sets of folders contained in 2 different PST files. One for the email data and the other for the <default> contact/calendar folders contained in the <default data file> for the account. You cannot set the PST file for your email folders as the default nor can you delete either of the PST files.

twilkin17 say:

UPDATE:

The user I am supporting updated his OneNote to the latest version. This changed the error code to "We're sorry, something went wrong during sync. You might not have permissions. (Error code: 0xE0190193)".

We discovered that at some point the original OneNote file he was synching was created from a previous Office 365 account that needed to be deactivated. Since the OneNote file was looking for an older account to synch it could no longer sync to the current account he is using.

Only way to get around this was to have the user make a new OneNote file, manually create the Tabs and copy the contents to the newly created tabs into the new OneNote file.
