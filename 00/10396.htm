jack cook - aka help_line say:

OK, so we have it.  It was something running in the background and in fact it is the Today screen that is used for the Home Screen display you are using.

 

It was not what I was using and unfortunately I had not tried all the possibilities that are there.

 

So first of all, lets see just what that message is.  It is part of a Today Screen setup that will enable you to access a variety of features.  If you go to START/SETTINGS then scroll down to item 3, Display, and click on Home Screen, you will see at the top HOME SCREEN LAYOUT.

 

My guess is that you will probably see the name displayed as "SAMSUNG SIMPLE" or something that uses Samsung as part of the name.  This is one of several SAMSUNG Today screens.  You can change it to a variety of others but the Samung ones will display that "grey bar" or combinations of it with some other features.  For example one is SAMSUNG Calendar.  That one shows a calendar on the Today Screen as well as that "grey bar"

 

So, OK, just what is the Gey bar doing when it says NO MEDIA. 

 

When you turn on the screen, the Today Screen appears.  If you press the DOWN toggle on the 5 way button, it should change to Messages and Email, Missed calls, Appointments, My Documents and Storage Card, Settings and Task manager.  You can scroll up or down to view different items.

 

So, now lets look at the "No Media"

Press the OK button ... what did you get?  Ah, the ability to play media that you have stored on your device.  Hopefully you may have alrady stored a few music files on your device so pick a song from My Music and tell it to play.

 

Now the Media Player will show with your song playing.  Now hit the HOME button ... what do you see where it used to say No Media?  It should be the song that is playing.

 

Now if you have loaded several music files, pressing thge right toggle will change the song being played.  Cool huh?  To stop it from playing press the OK and it will pause.

 

Play around with it ... it is cool.  Wantto clear it and get back to No Media?  Press START/APPLICATIONS/Task Manager and STOP Media Player.  Go back to the Today Screen and you will see No Media is back.

 

So play with it ... experiment .. it is cool

 

Try the different Today screens ... some are very cool (others are not).  I never tried it because I use a program called Facade and use their Today Screen because it displays what I want to see when I turn on my device.

 

Good luck and thanks again for posting the picture ... it made the difference in finding the solution!!

 

Enjoy your device!!!

I solved by deleting this section 'http://schemas.microsoft.com/office/infopath/2007/PartnerControls:Person' from xsd header, then i merge the content of BuiltInActiveXControls.xsd in this section below into myschema.xsd :

<xsd:element name="Person">
  <xsd:complexType>
   <xsd:sequence>
    <xsd:element ref="my:DisplayName" minOccurs="0"/>
    <xsd:element ref="my:AccountId" minOccurs="0"/>
    <xsd:element ref="my:AccountType" minOccurs="0"/>
   </xsd:sequence>
  </xsd:complexType>
 </xsd:element>
 <xsd:element name="DisplayName" type="xsd:string"/>
 <xsd:element name="AccountId" type="xsd:string"/>
 <xsd:element name="AccountType" type="xsd:string"/>

But change tag "<xs>" into "<xsd>" and also change tag "pc:" into "my:", like above.

Then I put above code below the section :

<xsd:element name="group_1">
  <xsd:complexType>
   <xsd:sequence>
    <xsd:element ref="my:Person" minOccurs="0" maxOccurs="unbounded"/>
   </xsd:sequence>
  </xsd:complexType>
 </xsd:element>

So it will becoming like this :

<xsd:element name="group_1">
  <xsd:complexType>
   <xsd:sequence>
    <xsd:element ref="my:Person" minOccurs="0" maxOccurs="unbounded"/>
   </xsd:sequence>
  </xsd:complexType>
 </xsd:element>
 <xsd:element name="Person">
  <xsd:complexType>
   <xsd:sequence>
    <xsd:element ref="my:DisplayName" minOccurs="0"/>
    <xsd:element ref="my:AccountId" minOccurs="0"/>

When you activate your Office license, it gets stored in digital format corresponding to your hardware on Microsoft activation servers. The server also maintains the count of your license.
