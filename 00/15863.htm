A couple quick notes of things I noticed within your installation package and your own comments to Kosh and above.

First, the download I received from your website didin't appear to be digitally signed, which is a huge issue in terms of modern antimalware monitoring, since this doesn't allow your package to be identified with your organization.  Microsoft has been giving reputable organizations a 'pass' when they distribute software that contains digital signatures for several years now, since they can also use these to block or quickly identify organizations  and thus code with a bad reputation.

I note your own comments about the possible lack of support and future development of the "Ghost Installer" and from the several bad reviews I found in a search for this software, I think this may be partially the reason.  I suspect that the not so great reputation this installer has combined with a lack of support from the creator is likely to make Microsoft disinterested in spending much time attempting to resolve any issues it might be causing.

As to what MSE is doing to 'cause ' this delay, it is simply treating your installation package as the unidentified, scripted installer package that it is and the behavior monitoring is performing emulation of the code in an attempt to verify that the program isn't going to perform anything malicious within the operating system.  Since as is typical an installer will absolutely perform activities that might appear suspicious, the lack of a digital signature means that there's no way to 'whitelist' the package, so the emulation delay is the penalty incurred as a result.

joannacarlson say:

How do I make a column with the same thing in each row?

I am trying to assign each row with the number 11. How can I do this without having to manually type a number 11 in each row? 

chip pearson say:

Enter 11 in the first cell. Then, select the cells into which you want the 11 to appear, and then press CTRL D (short for Fill Down).

 

trip_to_tokyo say:

You don't say how many rows you want to do it for but assuming there are 10 rows highlight those 10 rows then type in:-

 

11

 

 - followed by:-

 

CTRL + ENTER

 

All 10 rows will be filled with the number 11.

 

There are other ways to do this depending upon your requirement(s).
