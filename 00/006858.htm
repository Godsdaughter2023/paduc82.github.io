    suzanne s. barnhill say:

    A practical way to lay out such a roster is to use a table. You can put a picture in a table cell and the text in a cell below, or you can put the picture and text together in a cell. If you need multiple columns, you can use table columns, but a better approach is to format the page into two columns before inserting a one-column table; then the table will flow naturally from the bottom of the left column to the top of the right column.

    If you want the listings to be aligned symmetrically, you can set an Exact row height for the cells so that each one is the same height regardless of content (and rows in the two columns will be aligned). If you want to use the space as efficiently as possible, leave the row height undefined, and they will adjust according to the amount of content.

    Even if you don't use a table, be sure to make your pictures In Line With Text. Don't try to wrap the pictures or put text in text boxes. Whenever you have many "floating" objects on a page, you're just asking for trouble.

    You could either have 

     a. unwanted space defined below the footnote separator or

     b. unwanted  space defined above the footnote (either defined in the Style, or the Paragraph) or

     c. unwanted paragraphs or lines above the footnote (unlikely, IMO, as I think you would have managed to delete that)

    I would check (b) first, e.g.

     1. click in the footnote paragraph

     2. click the small arrow at the bottom right of the Home tab, Paragraph group

     3. If the "Space above" is set to anything other than 0, you may need to set the space above to 0.     ActiveWindow.View.SplitSpecial = wdPaneFootnoteSeparator

    then press enter 

    Then 

    1. click in the footnote separator paragraph

    2. click the small arrow at the bottom right of the Home tab, Paragraph group

    If unwanted space below the paragraph is defined in there, set it to zero and see if that fixes the problem.
