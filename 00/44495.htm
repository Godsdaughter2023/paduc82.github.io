mike pepper say:

Found the cause on my machine, and a solution.  After a reinstall ("restore") of Windows 10 the problem temporarily went away, but then reappeared as I rebuilt my environment.

It turns out that the utility for managing a StarTech USB-to-VGA adapter is/was causing the problem.  Uninstalled that utility and the problem went away.  Apps open normally, and no more "remote procedure call" error.

The actual adapter and driver for it are still in the computer, but the management utility is not.  Clearly a StarTech problem -- for them and for me.

Found a solution (for me): Windows was reporting that I had the correct, up to date driver for the StarTech USB2VGA device, but also reported that the manufacturer is called "MCT" Magic.  Found them online and downloaded a slightly newer driver & utility combo, and all is good.  All Windows apps work and no RPC error!

Hope this is helpful for someone. It certainly has been frustrating here.

m3tal daz3 say:

By using the magic of google I found this out: 

"Unless the console is actively updating games on your external drive, there is no code that periodically wakes it up while the console is asleep. 

It sounds like you may have a drive that isn't happy about powering down.  The USB ports remain powered in standby and it is up to the drive to listen to USB power requests and power down.  Your drive may not want to do this.  You are correct that constant cycling of the drive will eventually wear it out faster.  You can reach a middle ground by changing the option in settings to leave the drive powered all the time.  The drive will last longer constantly rotating than it will starting and stopping all the time"
