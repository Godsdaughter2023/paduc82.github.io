Assume that the list in column A of Sheet1 is named myList. In cell A2 of Sheet2, enter the formula:

     =IFERROR(INDEX(myList,MATCH(0,COUNTIF($A$1:A3,myList),0)),"")

roady [mvp] say:

You cannot manually configure an Exchange account in Outlook 2016. It sounds like tried to configure it as an Exchange ActiveSync account instead.

Due to changes in the connectivity of Exchange, Outlook 2016 completely relies on the Autodiscover service of Exchange to properly configure Outlook. This includes having the proper DNS CNAME record for autodiscover in your public DNS zone. Whether it should point to Office 365 or your own environment depends on your deployment type (online or hybrid).

Once that is configured, all you have to do in Outlook is providing your name, email address and password to configure your Exchange account.

Note that a properly functioning Autodiscover service has always been a hard requirement for any supported Exchange environment since Exchange 2007. Outlook 2010 and Outlook 2013 also can't be configured manually when connecting via the MAPI over HTTP protocol.
