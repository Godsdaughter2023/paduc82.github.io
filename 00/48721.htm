Hi House,

 

Change the Hotmail password online as well as the password reset security information.  Then Outlook should then be configured wrong to access the account and they shouldn't be able to get into Hotmail to change things - but everything already on the computer will be available (same with other e-mail accounts). 

 

Plus you need to consider that they may have access to more than that - so more than just Hotmail email may be at risk if they can get enough access to get that far (and I could in under 10 minutes,  so I've little doubt others could as well).  All passwords, account information, personal information, stored information to other online accounts if setup for automatic login and remembering you, access to your router (from the computer in their car parked nearby) and thus to your home network and other computers, and pretty much anything on the computer. 

 

Don't forget to report it - police may want you to allow some access before you shut down or change everything so they can possibly trace it if the robbers are not smart enough to disguise the computer and spoof the TCP/IP addresses.  I don't suppose it has a GPS?  If they use your ISP account or use stored info to access other accounts, it may be traceable if they aren't computer-savvy.

 

But for Hotmail itself, it's best to check with Hotmail to be sure as they've no doubt had this happen before and can perhaps do more (like maybe Sync Outlook with another blank email account and possibly wipe clean what's in Outlook's normal files if they try to gain access but before you prevent it from happening - but I'm not certain about that and especially not if you block access first - so best to talk to them and the authorities about it first).  There may be choices to make to balance your security about what's on the computer and what can be done with it with catching the thieves and possibly tracing the computer (this isn't like remote hacking where tracing is almost impossible if done right and if they got that far, it probably was) - but they'll know more about that than we do and can give you more appropriate advice.
