In Windows 10, you can now set DPI (Dots per Inch) scaling to make text and other items, such as icons, fonts, and windows, to display larger or  smaller for all displays or per-display.


If you are running a screen resolution of 1366x768 on a tablet, chances are that UI will look good at 100% DPI settings. In Windows 8 you can choose either 100% to optimize the UI on the tablet display or up to 150% to optimize the UI on the external display. You have to compromise.

This a method that I just did myself for Windows 10, and worked for me.

Before you begin, you must be connected to the internet and also you must not have any pins previously setup prior to this process.  Check your sign-in options on your account and remove all pins, if any. 

1.  Open "File Explorer" on the task bar.:

2.  On your left, locate and right click on "This PC" select "Manage"  

3.  A new window/utility will pop up called "Computer Management"

4.  Click on "Device Manager", then click on "System Devices"

5   Scroll through the drop down, right click on "Surface Camera Windows Hello" and select "Uninstall Device".

5.  A pop will appear asking to confirm the uninstall and will have a check box labled "delete the driver software for this device".  Make sure that the box IS checked and proceed to uninstall.

6.  After it's done, in the "Computer Management"  tabs, click "Action" and select "Scan for Hardware Changes"

7.  It will do a quick scan, refresh, and should center on a tab called "Other Devices" and there should be a device named "Unknown Device" with a yellow harzard sign..

8.  Wait for about 5-10 seconds and WIndows, (even though with its software just deleted), should recognize the device and automatically re-install the device with its up to date drivers.

9.  Press the power button once to "Sleep" your device. 

10 Press it again and at the sign-in screen, Your lockscreen should ask you if you want to setup windows hello.

11. Sign in with password and proceed with the windows hello setup.
