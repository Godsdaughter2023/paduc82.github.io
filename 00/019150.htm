charles kenyon say:

You cannot use the normal.dot file from Word 2003 as the normal.dotm file for Word 2016 even if Word will recognize it. At best you will disable much of Word by doing this.

You can use the Organizer to move macros from your old normal.dot file to the new normal.dotm file.

Where were your images stored? They cannot be stored in macros but might have been in AutoText entries in your normal.dot file.

An alternative to moving the macros would be to rename a copy of your normal.dot file something like normal2003.dot and placing it in your Word Startup Folder to make it a Global Template. That would give access in Word 2016 to your macros and any AutoText contained in the old normal.dot file.

roady [mvp] say:

Outlook 2013 switched to the ost-format since that is the "caching" equivalent of the pst-file which is intended for "storage".

For additional information also see:

What is stored in the ost-file and why is it larger or smaller than my mailbox?

and

Pst-files vs. pst-archives vs. ost-files

Note that the ost-file can also contain "storage" folders. These folders are marked with "This computer only". You'll need to create backups of these folders in pst-files when you deem their contents important. It is best to reduce the amount of folders marked with "This computer only" as much as possible.
