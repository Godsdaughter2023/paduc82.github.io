gren say:

A nice guy or girl from the MS tech support answered me : The Recipient cache is a file that is needed for auto complete. Auto complete is the one that is showing when you are creating a new email. For example, you will sending an email on an account and you have typed the email account on the "to:" it will create a recipient cache and it will be saved on autocomplete. When you will send an email again, you no longer need to add the whole email account. It will be saved on auto complete and it will show you the account that you have sent email previously.

So it is not related to contacts linking, for example : link an Exchange-stored contact with the Facebook page of the same person and the Linked In page of the same person. It's not for that.
