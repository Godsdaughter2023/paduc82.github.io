Shehzad

rena yu msft say:

Hi Shehzad,

 

There is no kind of “sub-administrator” concept in Office 365. As a global admin, he will be able to manage everything in the tenant.

In your scenario, there are two options:

  • For information security protection aspect, you can register 4 tenant for a group and 3 companies, and separate these domains into them. So global admin in company A won’t see anything in company B, C. However, the global admin in group 1 will need to have global admin accounts in all 3 tenants to be able to manage all 3 companies.
  • For operation convenience aspect, you can choose to keep these domains into one Office 365 tenant. But giving global admin permission to an admin in company A will grant him access to everything in the tenant (i.e., in your group 1 and all companies).
