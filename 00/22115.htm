ron coderre say:

I don't recall Excel's formula-drag feature behaving the way you want.  To verify, I tried your proces in XL2007 and XL2003. Neither incremented the referenced range by 12.

Perhaps one of these options will suffice:
A1: =SUM(OFFSET($B$11:$B$22,(ROW()-1)*12,))
or
A1: =SUM(INDEX(B:B,11+(ROW()-1)*12):INDEX(B:B,22+(ROW()-1)*12)),br />

Dragging either formula down causes the sum range to increment by 12's: $B$11:$B$22, $B$23:$B$34, etc

Note: if the formula will not begin on Row_1, the multiplier must be adjusted to zero out the first row reference.

Example for a formula beginning on Row_3:
A3: =SUM(OFFSET($B$11:$B$22,(ROW()-3)*12,))

The self adjusting version would be this:
=SUM(OFFSET($B$11:$B$22,(ROW()-ROW($A$3))*12,))

One more comment: The OFFSET function is volatile, so it can make the workbook sluggish if you intend to use thousands of them.

Is that something you can work with?
