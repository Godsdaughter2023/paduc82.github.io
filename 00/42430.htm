lemp say:

That doesn't appear to be a Microsoft-generated error message (which is why I thought you had incompletely quoted it; my apology for that assumption).

Looking at the background of your screen shot, my guess is that you received that error message when you attempted to run the application named GCE8525B105.  Is that correct?

I couldn't find much online about the GCE-8525B, but I did find downloads for the GCE-8520B and the GCE-8527B.  Both of those downloads look just like the file folder in your screen shot, with the exception that the name of the "ReadMe" file in your folder is in German (LiesMich). 

Given that the executable files ("applications") that I downloaded for similar model optical drives are both firmware updates -- NOT drivers -- I expect that what you have is also a firmware update.

As you will see from the content of the ReadMe file, updating firmware is potentially hazardous.  I don't know if the error message means that the firmware update was unable to run at all or if it ran partially and thus -- potentially -- "bricked" your optical drive.

In any event, as you have seen, modern versions of Windows generally do not require third-party drivers for optical drives.  The driver -- cdrom.sys -- is built into Windows.

One thing I neglected to ask you:  when you double-click on the entry for the problem drive, what is the message under "device status"?

I suspect that your problem drive is bad, although I suppose it could be a bad controller on your motherboard or a bad cable.  The only way to know for sure is to swap in a known working optical drive.
