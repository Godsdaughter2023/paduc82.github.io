averagejoeny say:

Thanks, Cal54!  I forgot about disabling the secure boot within the UEFI on the Surface Pro 3.  That did the trick once I set "Secure Boot Control" to "Disabled" in the UEFI.

The Surface Pro 3 still would not boot from the officially downloaded Microsoft Surface Pro 3 recovery image USB, but it would at least boot to the Windows 10 Creators Update (1703) USB I created.  From here, I was able to boot, wipe out all the partitions, and start to install Windows 10.  From there, I was then finally able to boot from the recovery USB.

The UEFI/BIOS in the Surface Pro 3 is made by American Megatrends, however, the Surface Pro 4 and the Surface Book appear to be Surface proprietary.  Similar configurations on these two devices would be under Security>Secure Boot>Change configuration.  The default is "Microsoft & 3rd party CA" with "Microsoft only" and "None" as the other two choices.  There are also many other Boot configuration options within the Surface Pro 4 and Surface Book UEFI.

Regardless of the configuration settings, I still was unsuccessful booting from a USB drive on the Surface Pro 4.  Hopefully, RayoV will have better luck with his Surface Book.

Thanks again!

Joe
