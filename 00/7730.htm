doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

To be able to stop the page numbering from appearing, you will have to insert a Section Break to separate that page from the preceding pages in the document.

Then, when you are in the Header or Footer pane of the Section for which you do not want page numbers, click on the Link to Previous button in the Header & Footer Tools>Design tab of the ribbon.

Note, that button is a toggle so that each time you click on it, the Header or Footer in that Section is either linked or unlinked from that in the previous Section.

With the header or footer unlinked, you can then modify them without affecting that in the previous Section.

a. user say:

Hi,

 

It is a pleasure to help you with your issue in adjusting your junk mail settings. Let's find out what are the possible options that we can do to resolve it. To prevent receiving junk emails, we suggest that you set your junk mail filter set to exclusive. If you are using Hotmail.com interface, kindly follow these steps:

 

1. In the Inbox, click the Gear icon located at the upper right corner of your screen and select More mail settings

2. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.

3. Under Choose a junk e-mail filter, choose the level of protection that you want, and then click Save.

 

Note: If the Exclusive level was set, messages from senders not on your Contacts or Safe senders lists will have been sent to the Junk e-mail folder.

 

Also, have you tried creating a message rule to block unwanted emails? If not, I suggest that you create one to prevent unwanted messages. Please follow these steps:

 

1. Go to www.outlook.com and sign in with your Microsoft account

2. Click on the Gear Icon, and then select More options

3. Under Customizing Outlook category, click Rules for sorting new messages

4. Click New

5. Under Step 1, click the first box then select Sender's address

6. The next box should be contains word then in the last box type a word associated with the spam message you received or any specific word that most likely used in the spam emails that you always receive

7. Under Step 2, tick on Delete these messages then click Save

Note: By doing this rule, email that has the content of any word that you inserted in the rule will prevent entering your inbox.

 

Let us know if you have any questions.
