karl timmermans [outlook mvp 2012-2018] say:

For the record, contacts/calendar items have always been stored locally regardless of which version of Outlook used (or any other <email client> from any other vendor for that matter). Been that way since the introduction of the IMAP protocol (an industry <not a Microsoft> standard).

The difference between 'OL'2013/'2016 and earlier Outlook versions is that a .pst file was used in earlier versions whereas an .ost file is used in OL'2013+ which has one very important side effect. Delete the IMAP account and you lose all your "This Computer Only" data. Also, backing up an .ost file is pointless since it can only be opened by the Outlook profile/email account originally used to create it.

Each protocol (POP, IMAP, Exchange etc) comes with its own capabilities. Some background articles in case the info is of any interest

Understanding Email Protocols (POP, Imap, MAPI, EAS)

Technically, a control's DefaultValue property contais an expression that is evaluated at the first keystroke in a new record.  To make your VBA statment do the same thing Access does with the property, use:

   Me.cboSite.Text = Eval(Me.cboSite.DefaultValue)

BUT, you need to make sure the stuff you type into the property corresponds to the combo box's BoundColumn property, which if often different from the first visible column (specified in the ColumnWidths property) that is displayed in the combo box..

dean bailes say:

I too have had this exact same problem with my connected account since the "Outlook.com upgrade".

This is the only way I've found to work around it although it seems to work quite well for me.  The only caveat is that you have to be able to set up email forwarding from your connected email account provider and you must also have a live.com or outlook.com email address associated with your Outlook.com account...

So:

1. If you don't already have an email alias for your connected account set up, do the following:  From the "Connected Accounts" page in Outlook.com choose "Manage or choose a primary alias"

Then you need to go through the steps to create an alias for the email address you want to use, i.e. the one from your connected account (we are going to use this for sending emails).  Ensure you verify your email address.

2. Come back to the "Connected Accounts" page again and this time *DELETE* your connected account, so that you don't have one any more.

3. Click "Change your From address" and set it to whatever you want your default from address to be.  Because you have created an Email alias, your old connected account email address should still be an available option for you.

4. Go to your connected account email provider and set up email forwarding to forward all emails automatically to your outlook.com/live.com email address.

At the end of this process, the email forwarding should mean that you still receive all your emails into your Outlook.com mailbox and the Email Alias that you've set up should mean that you are also still able to send as that.

Hopefully that helps a few people out as this was driving me crazy for days trying to find a solution.
