In Windows 10 you may create up to two types of Accounts. A Local Account, which is a standalone account not tied to anything, and of course a Microsoft Account (which is what you have that ties everything together and allows you to manage, setup and control from your account dashboard).

Anyways Lets get on to resolving the problem. Please follow the below steps to remove the MSN account and add a Local account instead.

1 - Go to Start (a windows logo on the bottom left or on your keyboard), click settings, or you can simply type it in the cortana search box and hit enter.

2 -  Once you reach Settings, click Accounts.

3 - Under Accounts screen, the first tab you'll see is the one that says "Your info".

4 - Click sign in with a local account instead. Once you proceed with this it will sign you out and you will need to log back in with your new Local account.

5- Once logged into your Local account, go back to Settings using the Start menu the same way as before. This time you have to select the Email & app accounts tab. 

6 - Once you reached the corresponding tab, select your Microsoft Account and click remove. Do the same thing, under Email, Calender and contacts under the same tab. 

7- Restart the computer and you are completely done.

Hi,

At this point, I would suggest manually resetting the Windows Update related folders in your computer and see if that lets you install this update.

Note: Performing these steps would erase the Windows Update history from the computer, but will not uninstall any updates.

1. Press Windows and R keys together on your keyboard,then type in Services.msc in the Run box and hit Enter. This should open up the Services windows for you.

2. Then find the following three services in the Services list and Stop them (by right clicking on them and clicking on Stop) one by one:

Windows Update

Background Intelligent Transfer service (BITS)

Cryptographic Service

3. Open Windows Explorer and go to the following location:

C:\Windows\

and rename the SoftwareDistribution folder to SoftwareDistribution.old

4. Go to the following location:

C:\Windows\System32\

and rename the Catroot2 folder to Catroot2.old

(be careful here, do not rename the Catroot folder, you need to rename only the Catroot2 folder)

5. Now, go back to the Services window and Start the three services that you had stopped earlier.

Now, close all open windows, restart the computer and try checking for updates and installing them. If it fails again, please reply here so that we could assist you further.
