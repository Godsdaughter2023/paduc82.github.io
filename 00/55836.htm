jbellec say:

This question is asked over and over, even though it's been answered many times.

Canon - and every other hardware manufacturer - decides which of its products it will support on Windows 10. Microsoft can't force Canon to support its hardware on Windows 10.

Microsoft publishes the information that hardware manufacturers need in order to make their hardware compatible with Windows 10. Then, it's up to each hardware manufacturer to decide which of its products it will support on Windows 10.

Only Canon can write drivers for their hardware, for two reasons: 1) Only Canon has the proprietary knowledge of their hardware needed to assure the proper functioning of its driver; and 2) Only Canon has the legal right to create drivers for their hardware because it's their intellectual property.

No third-party organization is going to write drivers for someone else's hardware. There's a cost to writing the code, testing the code, debugging the code and providing technical support, to say nothing of insuring against legal liability.

None of this information is secret or private.

If you'd like to explore the technological and business reasons why a hardware manufacturer drops support for its own hardware, we can talk about that. But for now, your solution is to buy a new printer.
