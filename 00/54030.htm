•There’s a problem with your network setup, and therefore you can’t connect to Xbox Live.

•Your account may be blocked.

Solutions

To resolve this issue, try the following solutions:

Solution 1: Clear the system cache

To clear the system cache, follow these steps:

1.Press the Guide button on your controller.

2.Select settings, and then select System Settings.

3.Select Storage or Memory.  

4.Highlight any storage device, and then press Y on your controller. (It doesn't matter which storage device you select; the cache will be cleared for all storage devices.)

5.Select Clear System Cache.

6.When prompted to confirm storage device maintenance, select Yes.

Solution 2: Reset your system

To reset your system, follow these steps:

1.Press the Guide button, and then select Turn Off Console.

2.Unplug the power cable from the back of your router, modem, or gateway for 5 minutes. If you have both a router and a modem, remove the power cable from both devices.

3.After 5 minutes, plug the modem or gateway in first and wait for all the lights to return to their normal states.

4.Plug in the router and wait for all the lights to return to their normal states.

5.Turn the Xbox console on again and run the Xbox Live Connection Test. To run the test, follow these steps.

Note If you are using an Xbox 360 Wireless Controller, you can turn on the console by using the Guide button on the controller.

1.Select settings.

2.Select System.

3.Select Network Settings.

4.Select your network.
