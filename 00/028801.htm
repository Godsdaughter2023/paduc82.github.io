teylyn say:
Hello,

yes, I think you are missing something. With your data sample:

Product Id      Rate      Price

1                    3            98

1                    4            99

1                    5            100

2                    3.5          99

2                    4             100

2                    4.5          101

3                    2.5          100

3                    3             101

3                    3.5          102


If you enter a ProductID in H1 and a Rate in H2, then the price will be returned with this formula. It does not matter how many rows there are for product 1, or for product 2, because the formula looks at the whole data set, not just a few rows. You do not have to change any range names.


=INDEX(C1:C50,MATCH(H1&H2,INDEX(A1:A50&B1:B50,0),0))


The same goes for the Vlookup technique with the helper column. 


You can create dynamic range names for the data set as a whole, so that the range names catch all the rows in the data set. For example these three range names:

Products =Sheet1!$A$1:INDEX(Sheet1!$A:$A,COUNTA(Sheet1!$A:$A))
Rates =OFFSET(Products,0,1)
Price =OFFSET(Products,0,2)

Then use 

=INDEX(Price,MATCH(H1&H2,INDEX(Products&Rates,0),0))


cheers, teylyn
