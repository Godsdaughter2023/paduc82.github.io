Facebook Messenger - Microsoft Community

michaels---01 say:

Facebook Messenger

Technical Level : Intermediate

Summary

Details

There have recently been a number of articles in news sites and blogs concerning what is claimed to be a serious invasion of privacy by the Facebook Messenger app, specifically these articles suggest that the app's terms and conditions allow the it to access your personal data without your knowledge or consent, which they go on to suggest is malicious behaviour on the part of Facebook. One such article that was recently brought to our attention by a worried customer which mentions these seemingly alarming possibilities:

 

 

In truth, such articles are either just misinformed, or perhaps in some cases deliberately scaremongering; they claim that these "permissions" are part of Facebook's terms and conditions for using the app, but they are not. What they are is APIs (Application Programming Interfaces), the wording of the texts shown above comes from Google, not from Facebook (these articles focus on the Android version of the app, but Windows Phone, iOS and others have similar APIs); and they don't refer to what the company making the app is doing with your data, only to what features of the phone and data the app has access to in order to do its job.

Any chat app, whether it be Facebook Messenger, Skype, Whatsapp, WeChat, Viber, Line, KIK, et cetera, needs access to a variety of phone features and data in order to work, for example:

 

  1. If an app has a feature to call or message a mobile number outside of the internet, then it needs permission to access that part of the phone's OS, it does not mean that your phone is chatting with its friends at your expense whilst you are not looking.
  2. If an app allows you to send an audio or video message, it would need to have permission to use your mic and/or camera. It does not mean James Bond or Sydney Bristow are watching you.
  3. If an app allows you to find your friends who also use the same service, then in order to identify who are your friends from its own customer database, it needs to see your contact data, it does not mean that your data is being used for any malicious purpose or being relayed to other parties without your consent.

 

If the app did not have these permissions in advance, then every time you select to record a voice message, for example, the phone might have to have to pop up a box saying "Do you want to give this app permission to access your microphone? Yes/No".

The Google Play store has used very strong and emotive language in its permission requests (we understand that the texts have been amended since these concerns were highlighted in public), which is probably why some less-knowledgeable journalists and conspiracy theorists have seized on this information to make you think that Mark Zuckerberg's horns have been sharpened; but in truth whenever you install any app in Android or Windows Phone devices, you are shown the permissions it requests at the point of download and you have the option to refuse - if you refuse, then the app will not be installed because it will not work properly. I understand that iOS works slightly differently and does give you the YES/NO box the first time any particular API is called, personally I would consider that less convenient!

When you install an app in Windows Phone (or Android, for those of you who are bi-phonal!) you can check the permissions it wants before the download begins.

 

Don't be scared, the truth is out there, and it's not usually anywhere near as bad as some would have you believe.

 

Happy messaging!
