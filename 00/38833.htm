Hey cclloyd,

While there is no Microsoft brand adapters, you can find HDMI to VGA adapters online or at a local tech store. Best Buy might have them too. But that would only give you video. For audio, I know there are HDMI splitters that split HDMI cords into the Audio and Video. But then you would need another adapter to go from HDMI to your standard aux sound port. I do not know if there is such a thing. Basically, it can be done, but you would need a bunch of adapters.

What you could do is you could do the HDMI to VGA for the video. For the audio, get an Xbox audio adapter. That will give you the Red and White Audio ports, and a Digital Optical Audio port. Then get an adapter that will turn those Red and White ports into a Standard Aux headphone plug (which is the 3.5mm connector you are talking about I think). You can find those at a radio shack or Best Buy. That will get you the audio and the 1080p video if you get the adapter that allows it and your monitor supports it.

So it will be something like this.

_________________________________________________________

Video

Xbox> HDMI cable> HDMI - VGA/DVI (whichever one you prefer)> Monitor

_________________________________________________________

Audio

Xbox> Xbox audio adapter> Red and White Audio Cables > Red and White audio cables - Standard Auxillary (headphone plug basically)> Monitor.

________________________________________________________

If I confused you or anything you need clarity on, just ask.

When I say Red and White audio cables, I am referring to Composite cables with the red, white, and yellow plugs which used to be standard before HDMI and Componet.

Monitor size

How can I find out the size of one of my monitors?

a. user say:

Use a measuring tape and measure the diagonal length of your monitor. Don't worry about the length taken up by the bezel (the plastic edge around the screen) which is minimal. The size of a monitor is measured as the diagonal length of the screen, rounded to the nearest whole number. Common popular monitor sizes today are (in inches) 22, 24, 27, 30 or more.
