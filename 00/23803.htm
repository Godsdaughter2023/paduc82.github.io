joeu2004 say:

[Corrected]

Kevin wrote:

I need a formula to show what my estimated finish time would be if I had a target of £240.

I have never heard of estimating finish time based on a target amount of earnings.  I dare say that would be unethical.  A job takes a certain amount of time. independent of what you might want to earn.  The amount for labor is the hourly rate times the estimated time.

That said, I'll play along ....

Kevin wrote:

For example

start 1pm

current time 2:30pm

Earned £36 so far...

£36 divide by one and a half hours = £24 per hour

Estimated finish time is Target £240 divide by £24 = 10 hours from start time

1pm +10 hours = 11pm or 23:00

The critical factor that you had omitted previously is what "current time" is or what the hourly rate is.

If your cell assignments are as follows (some of which you provided previously):

A1, start time: 13:00 or 1:00 PM

A3, current time:  14:30 or 2:30 PM

A5, hours worked:  =(A3-A1)*24    (formatted as Number)

A7, current earnings:  36.00    (formatted as Currency)

A9, hourly rate:  =A7/A5    (formatted as Currency)

A11, target earnings:  240.00    (formatted as Currency)

A13, target hours:  =A11/A9    (formatted as Number)

A15, target finish time:  =A1 + A13/24    (formatted as Time or Custom [h]:mm)

Caveat:  This mostly assumes that the target finish time is on the same day.  I do not see any guarantee of that, since you determine the finish time based on target earnings(!).

It would behoove you to enter date as well as time into A1 and A3, and to format A15 to display date and time.

[EDIT] You can still format them to show only time of day, although A15 might be difficult to understand that way.

Alternatively, if A1 and A3 contain only time of day, the format [h] will display more than 23 if the finish time is another day.

[EDIT] Aha!  In fact, A3 does include the date, if A3 has the formula =NOW(), as you indicated originally.  In that case, A1, must also include the date.  But if A1 contains only time of day, the formula in A3 should probably be =MOD(NOW(),1), or more reliably =--TEXT(NOW(),"h:m"), so that A3 also contains only time of day (not recommended).

ronrosenfeld say: After raising this issue in a different forum, and despite looking at that page a bunch of times, I seem to have missed this comment by Charles Williams:

http://www.decisionmodels.com/calcsecretsc.htm

Circular Reference Cell Calculation Sequence

When your workbook contains circular references and Iteration is switched on (Application.Iteration is True) Excel has to use a slightly different calculation algorithm:

The first step is the same as a recalculation (or full calculation, depending how the calculation was invoked) that does not involve circular references. This enables all the cells that come before the circular reference to be properly calculated and the list of cells in the circular reference to be identified together with their dependents. During this first step Application.Iteration is False.

The second step occurs once on each iteration: The list of cells in the circular reference is calculated sheet by sheet, in alphabetic sheet name sequence (even in Excel 2002 & 2003). Within each sheet the list of cells and array formulae in the circular reference, and all cells that are dependent on them, is calculated from left to right and top to bottom. (all the columns in the first row from left to right, then the next row ...). Each array formula in the list is calculated as a single block of cells from left to right and top to bottom, so if you want to use calculation by columns rather than by rows you have to use a multi-cell column array formula. This calculation ignores the dependency tree and relies on the iterative calculation process to resolve dependencies. Volatile cells that are not dependent on the cells in the circular list are NOT recalculated on each iteration.

This step is repeated until the iteration limits (maximum number of iterations and maximum cell change per iteration) are reached. During this second step Application.Iteration is True.

a. user say:

Hi Basia62,

 

Let me assist you in finding your lost emails or getting them back.

 

We would like to verify if you had set up Email forwarding to receive emails from another account or if you had added a send and receive email account in your Microsoft account.

 

Meanwhile, here are the possible reasons why you are unable to find your emails:

 

1. Emails got deleted due to Inactivity.

When users don't sign in for more than 365 days or within the first 10 days after signing up for an account, the system will flag it as Inactive. After an account becomes inactive, all messages, folders, and contacts are deleted. Incoming messages will be sent back to the sender as undeliverable.

 

Also, if you were accessing your emails on a mail client such as Windows Live Mail or through your mobile device, but didn’t log into your web account for over 365 days, you may lose emails due to inactivity in web account.

 

2. Account has been hacked or compromised.

You may lose emails if your account was hacked and the hacker had already deleted emails in your account.

 

3. Conversation threading is enabled.

If the feature Conversation threading is turned on wherein all emails with the same subject line are grouped together. You can click here to know more about this feature.

 

4. Schedule Cleanup is set.

This feature can be set to automatically delete emails. You may refer to this link for more information about it.

 

5. Messages can be deleted if you are using a mail client or a third party spam filtering software to access your account and the option Always leave a copy of the message on the server is not selected. This often happens when using the Pop3 protocol to retrieve your mail.

 

You can use the recover deleted or lost emails feature in Outlook.com to bring back your lost emails. However, when Outlook.com recovers missing or deleted emails, our system restores as many emails as it could regardless of its dates.

 

Please be reminded that there is no guarantee that the messages that you are looking for will all be recovered. You can refer to this Solution Article for the steps to retrieve your lost or deleted emails.

 

Note: The system can recover messages but not the folder itself. Recovered messages will be presented in your Deleted folder. Also, emails will not be recovered via recovery link if they were deleted from email clients like Windows Live Mail, Microsoft Office Outlook, etc.

 

If you have other questions or clarifications, please let us know.
