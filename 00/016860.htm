On the printer I use, which is a Hewlett Packard, in order to print two sheets to a page I click on print, then click on Properties, click the Finishing Tab, then under that tab click Pages per sheet and choose how many pages per sheet I need. In your case it would be two. Then click on ok, and click ok again to print.

 

The other way you can do it is to print back to back, which will only use 9 pages in total as you want, again this would depend on your printer as to whether this would be possible. As above click on print, then properties, the the printing shortcuts tab, and select Yes under Print on Both sides. Click ok then ok again to print. Some printers automatically take the sheet of paper back in to print on the other side when doing back to back, on others it will print out either all the odd or all the even pages, than you'd get a prompt on screen tell you to put the sheets back in a certain way so it can do the back to back printing.

 

N.B. The tab names may differ on your computer, depending on what printer you have, just have a look around the printing properties after you've clicked print and you should find what you need.

a. user say:

This is how Microsoft processes email.

  1. Analyses email for spam and malware. If they are 100% sure it is deleted.
  2. If the analyses thinks it is spam, but isn't certain, it will send the inbound email into your Junk folder and once there all processing stops.
  3. If the analyses sends the email to your Inbox then your Rules can be run and delete emails.

Likely what is happening in your situation is #2, so the Rules aren't being run if an email goes strait to the Junk folder.
