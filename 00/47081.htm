lamiari59 say: Hi,

I had the same issue with WiFi on Windows 8.1 & Lenovo laptop.
I've downloaded the Win 8.1 driver setup utility from Lenovo site. It reported that the driver has been updated successfully, however WiFi kept dropping connection every 20 minutes.
For some reason the utility didn't install the right version of the driver.

Installing the driver manually helped in my case.

1. Lenovo driver setup utility unpacks the drivers into a folder something like
C:\drivers\WLAN Driver (Atheros, Broadcom)\Broadcom\6.30.223.143\Driver\Win8.1
(in your case instead of Broadcom might be something else)

2. Open Device Manager and find your wireless adapter under "Network adapters"

3. Open Properties. Switch to "Drivers" tab. Click "Update driver" -> "Browse my computer.." ->
navigate to the above folder where the driver was unpacked -> "Next"

That actually installed the latest Windows 8.1 driver.

Hope that helps.

Notes
1) I also have an external USB WiFi antenna and it worked fine with just plug-and-play.
2) My other laptop (not Lenovo) with Windows 8 has exactly the same problem with internal WiFi. In different threads here people with other hardware but with Windows 8/8.1 complained about WiFi connection being dropped. So, I don't think it is Lenovo specific. davidsf4 say:

Solution:  Don't use a "USB recovery drive", use a "USB Windows media recovery".

When searching the Internet, you'll find two methods to restoring your system when, for example, removing a hard-drive and replacing it with an SSD (solid state drive).  Manufacturers like HP have you create recovery media on a USB drive.  When attempting to use it, you'll likely get the above "system drive too small" error prompt.

The solution is to just replace your Windows 10 operating system onto the new SSD drive and all will go well.  Follow these two YouTube videos:

Putting Windows 10 onto a USB Drive:

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gLfnuE1unS8



Windows 10 from USB to Computer:

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6uRT-bWE2EQ

This assumes you do not wish to clone your old hard-drive to the new SSD.  I highly recommend against cloning when moving to an SSD because the whole purpose of the new drive is to speed things up.  You don't want all the excess  bloat that your former drive holds.

The recovery drive option recommended by HP (which I do NOT recommend) assumes there's some specific HP software/drivers necessary to run certain parts of your computer and these are on the recovery disk as well.  In fact, the Windows 10 operating system files have everything you need to properly run your computer (be it HP or any other brand).
