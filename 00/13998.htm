doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:

By default, inked characters are inserted in front of text and doing anything with them can be a bit of a pain.

 

You can select each of them in turn (using the Select Objects facility in the Write section of the Pens>InkTools tab of the ribbon, and then click on the layout options button that appears and change the text wrapping so that they are in-line with text.  However as you do that to each object, its position with respect to the others will change.

 

A simpler method might be to click on the Show\Hide (¶) button to show the paragraph marks  and press the Enter key to insert a new paragraph.  That will almost certainly move the characters down, and then by selecting each one in turn, you can click on the anchor that will appear attached to the last paragraph mark and drag it to the previous paragraph mark.  After having re-anchored the objects to the first paragraph mark, you can then select the second paragraph mark and press Enter or insert text without affecting the position of the inked objects.

 

OK, another way, if you click on the Select Objects button, you should be able to hold down the Ctrl key and select each of the characters and then from the Drawing Tools Format tab of the ribbon, click on the Wrap Text item and then uncheck the Move with text item.  You can then use the Enter key without causing the objects to move.
