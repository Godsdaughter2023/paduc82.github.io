josh-h say: Hi, 

If you're not seeing any other issues in performance or maintenance of the PC, then this very well could be an issue with SFC Scan itself. Sometimes an OEM will update or alter files that the SFC Scan stumbles over like this. 

Have you done any manufacturer updates, or Windows updates recently? Or perhaps downloaded something, then removed it? The fact that there is nothing listed in the reports is strange, but it also makes me think that yeah, this might just be SFC seeing something, or something that used to be installed and is not able to reason it out. 

There are options to resolve this, one being transferring the affected files from another PC over to this one, but since there's no telling what exactly those files are, that would be difficult. There's also the option to do a repair install, and just redo the entire install of Windows without getting rid of your personal file, however that's a fairly large task and if you're not actually seeing performance issues, it's probably not worth the trouble.
