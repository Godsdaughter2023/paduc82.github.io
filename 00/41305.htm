no.compromise say:

For Internet Explorer, the process name is iexplore.exe.  iexplorer.exe (note the last 'r') is not Internet Explorer, and may be malware or some software trying to masquerade as IE.

IE8 uses process isolation, in which IE uses multiple processes.  More details at: IE8 and Loosely-Coupled IE (LCIE)

Previous versions of IE ran one process per instance.  You could have multiple processes running, but for most people it was just one iexplore.exe process.

jess bor say:

Hi,

In order to temporarily stop automatic updates in your Windows 10 computer, you may follow the steps provided below:

  1. Press the Windows key.
  2. Type Settings and click it.
  3. Click Update & security.
  4. Click Advanced options.
  5. Put a check on Defer feature updates.

This will temporarily stop automatic updates in your computer. For more information about this, you may click this link:

Defer upgrades in Windows 10.

Let us know the result for us to assist you further.
