ali golabkesh say:

use logical test in SUMIFS

How I can put logical test as CRETERIA in SUMIFS formula.

for example Creteria Range=A5:A10 and Creteria <12 AND >3.

rick rothstein, mvp - excel say:

It's working when I'm comparing with numbers (12 & 3) in above example but when put a cell reference or cell name instead numbers the comparing is doing by the names of cell not quantity of it.

assume 12 is in cell F1 and 3 is in cell F2 and I want to compare with quantity of these cells.

I tried "<F1" and ">F2", didn't work. Also I dont know how can use both conditions (AND) or one of them (OR) in SUMIFS formula.

Anything inside of quote marks is considered text and has no meaning outside of the characters that make it up. In order for F1 and F2 to be considered as cell references (and not simply individual characters), you must move them outside of the quote marks. You do this by concatenation. Try this modification to Hans' formula and see if it does what you want...

=SUMIFS(B5:B10,A5:A10,"<"&F1,A5:A10,">"&F2)

NOTE: Please mark the message or messages (yes, you can mark more than one) that answer your question as the "Answer" so that others will know your question has been resolved.

hansv mvp say:

To include a cell reference, use expressions such as "<"&F1 and ">"&F2

SUMIFS always uses AND conditions. If you want to use an OR condition, you can use an array formula (to be confirmed with Ctrl+Shift+Enter):

=SUM(IF((A5:A10<F1)+(A5:A10>F2),B5:B10))

This will sum the cells in B5:B10 for which the corresponding cell in A5:A10 is either smaller than the value of F1 or larger than the value in F2.

To combine AND and OR:

=SUM(IF((A5:A10>F1)*(A5:A10<F2)+(A5:A10>F3),B5:B10))

* acts as AND, and + as OR.
