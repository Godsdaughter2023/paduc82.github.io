Verify that Windows Defender is uninstalled (if XP), or Disabled (if Vista/W7).

Verify that you are up-to-date with SERVICE PACKS and updates (including .NET and IE). Yes, it can take some time to do this.

Verify that the Windows Firewall is on, and set to defaults. 

Verify that you are using version 2.0.657.0 of MSE (as of 2010-12-16).

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

What needs to be realized is that there is no such thing as a page in the structure of a Word document. The program is designed to present the content on pages because that's what users demanded to see, but Word documents are a flow of text from start to finish. The real structure consists of 1 or more Sections comprised of Paragraphs.

Pagination is a matter of layout specifications which indicate how many sheets of paper will be required to print the document. Actual page composition is handled at the time the document is printed.

Long story short :-) as you continue to type on page 1 the overflow will not "jump" to page 2. Instead, a new page 2 is generated & what was page 2 now becomes page 3. If you display the nonprinting characters [¶] you'll see that what appears to be a blank second page actually contains an empty paragraph.

As much as you would prefer not to, the template at least will have to be modified or possibly a new one made. Please note that I am referring to a template, not an individual document created from a template. It sounds like you may be dealing with a Letterhead template or something similar. Have a look at the section pertaining to 

Letterhead for a multi-page letter in this tutorial published by MVP Suzanne Barnhill for constructing a template of this nature. [It uses Word for Windows as a model, but the procedure is the same in Mac Word.]
