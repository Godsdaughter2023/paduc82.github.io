dana.d say:

Hi.  Here's my guess.

I believe it has to do with "ROW(INDIRECT("1:" & A4 ...".    This is a string to Solver, so it doesn't really see a "Formula" per say.  The "string" is causing integer jumps in size of the background cells.

Another 'problem' with this model is that A4 usually won't start off as an integer.  If it's not an exact integer, then the formula will return an error, and Solver will abort abruptly.

For example, this array formula will return 10.

=SUM(ROW(INDIRECT("1:" & "4")))

However, this array formula will return an error:

=SUM(ROW(INDIRECT("1:" & "4.00001")))

Therefore, I used the Evolutionary method.  I started with 1, and added the constraint that A4 <= 50.

I'm not sure why 'Evolutionary'  didn't work for you earlier.  You may have been missing an upper bound on A4.

I got an integer solution of 13.

Hope this helps.  :>)

As a side note for GRE

Make A1 the integer cell.

The equation in A2 would be:

=((1.07)^A1*(100000 + (0.03-0.07)*1000000) - (1.03)^A1*100000)/(0.03-0.07)

Now, using GRE, minimize A2, with constraints:

A1:  Integer

A2 >= 0

I get the integer solution of 13 right away.

And just for curiosity, if we solve the equation = 0 for A1 (ie time), I get...

=(LOG(1000000*(0.03-0.07)+100000)-LOG(100000)) / (LOG(1.03)-LOG(1.07))

13.40755

The integer solution is 13   :>)

If both values grow at the same rate, say 10%, then the equation will give a Div/0  error.

As a limit thou, it all reduces to:

=1.1*1000000/100000

11

if both grow at the same rate of 10%, then we arrive at 0 in 11 periods.

jim gordon mvp say:

I see.

The information you have is generally correct. The Office for Mac forum is a special case with some different rules. Now that the forums have merged (Sept 10), I should qualify that my remarks apply ONLY to Office for Mac.

  1. Office for Mac is sometimes used as a testing ground for ideas before they are rolled out to Office for Windows.
  2. Early on, Microsoft support people answering forum questions were providing answers for Windows Office to Mac users, and those answers were often blatantly wrong when applied to the Mac version of Office. 

Hence, the forum and reporting rules are a little different for Mac.

Microsoft | Community was previously known as Microsoft | Answers and had a slightly different organization. Mac stuff was isolated. Shortly after the beginning as a result of user and moderator feedback, it was agreed that the Answers/Community forum for Mac would be strictly user-to-user with only rare visits from Microsoft employees. UserVoice was intended primarily as a site to request new features, but it quickly morphed into a site to also request restoration of missing features and features that Windows Office has but are omitted on the Mac. Microsoft would also accept bug reports in UserVoice, but it was not set up for that purpose. The way Microsoft set up bug report tracking was to gather bug reporting information from the Send A Smile button in the apps. Support incidents can be purchased and those are also tracked in the bug reporting system. MVPs voluntarily watch the Mac forums and alert Microsoft when there is a sudden rash of new reports about a particular problem. 

Then came along the Insider concept. I think Windows Insider came first, then Office for Mac Insider, which is the prototype for Office for Windows Insider which I think just rolled out or is now rolling out. In the Mac forums, the Insider category is closely watched by the Office for Mac team. Microsoft employees do participate and engage directly with users in the Mac Insider forum. Moderators were told to encourage Insiders to post both to the Insider forum (so other Insiders could share in the conversation), as well as using Send A Smile.

Even with Send A Smile, Microsoft is experimenting on the Mac. Insider builds of Outlook for Mac have replaced Send A Smile with Help > Contact Support. Again, Insiders are urged to post to the Outlook Insider for Mac forum as well as use the new Contact Support feature.

Bug reports are allowed in Answers/Community for Mac, but are not tracked by Microsoft. Most crashes are the result of not updating, duplicate or bad fonts. I spend most of my time getting people to update and fix their fonts and that almost always fixes what's wrong. There are some other crashes, and when we ("we" meaning MVPs and other volunteers who regularly provide answers) find those, we tell people to send their crash reports in using the Microsoft Error Reporting dialog, and also to Send a Smile describing the crash. 

To summarize how things are set up for Mac forums and feedback:

Uservoice: New features. Missing features. Wishes. Suggestions

Community:

Insider: Any and all types of comments, bug reports, suggestions, work-arounds regarding the current Insider builds of Office for Mac

Word, Excel & PowerPoint: Primarily user-to-user questions and comments

Outlook: Mostly user-to-user but recently having a robust Microsoft presence

OneNote: Mostly user-to-user but recently having a robust Microsoft presence

Send a Smile (or Help > Contact Support in Outlook)

Bugs and compliments.

The MVPs will be meeting with Microsoft about this organization in November. It's worked well for us so far.
