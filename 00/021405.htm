Follow the steps mentioned and check if it helps:

Step 1: Open Outlook in safe mode to check is add-ins are causing the conflict:

·         Close Outlook.

·         Press the windows key+R on the keyboard to open the  Run window.

·         In Run window and type outlook /safe

Note: There is a space between 'outlook' and '/'.

 

If you are able to work with Outlook without any problem then the issue might be due to add-ins. In order to disable the add-ins follow the steps given below:

·         In Outlook, click on File< Options< Add-ins.

·         Under Manage select COM Add-ins.

·         Click on GO options beside Manage, uncheck all the add-ins and click Ok.

·         Try to open Outlook and check if it works fine.

 

Note: You may also refer the link mentioned which explains how to open the Office applications in safe mode and also explains how to disable the add-ins:

http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/support/why-cant-i-start-my-office-2013-application-HA104011864.aspx

 

Step 2: Repair the PST file:

Scan the PST file opened in Outlook until it states no-errors are found. Refer to the link mentioned which explains how to repair PST file:

http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/outlook-help/repair-outlook-data-files-pst-and-ost-HA102749454.aspx

Note: Ensure to run the scan PST till you get the message No errors found.

 

Step 3: Repair Office 365 installation:

·         Close all the Office applications.

·         Click on Start< Control Panel< Programs and Features.

·         In the programs list, right click on Microsoft Office 365 program and click on Repair. Choose Online Repair option to repair the Office installation.
