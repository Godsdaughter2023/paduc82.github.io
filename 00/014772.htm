jay freedman say:

Short of finding the missing USB stick, there is probably no electronic copy of the document to be had. It's possible that Word was making autorecovery (.asd) files while you were editing the document, but those files would be deleted automatically if you closed the file normally -- they remain on the drive only if Word crashes, which is their purpose.

Since you have a paper copy, you can scan it and use optical character recognition (OCR) to recreate the electronic file. OCR software is sometimes included with scanner hardware, or you can use OneNote that's included with Office. (In OneNote, you scan into the program or paste in the graphic files that come from the scanner; then right-click the image and choose "Copy text from picture". That puts the text on the clipboard, and you can paste that into a Word document.)
