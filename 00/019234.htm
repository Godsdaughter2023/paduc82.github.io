This was the aproach I finally reach tonight, hope somebody else helps: from the Delaunay triangulation you've got the borders of triangle(s), and with basic maths you can get the intersection of this border lines with a given plane (Z) -check if the cut is inside the border limits [triangle]-, so you've got two points if cuts the triangle, need a third point inside the triangle with the same Z... remember is easier to get the Z for any random (X,Y), so evaluating a point in the line that joins those two cut points gets you near the Z you're looking for, and can get the nearest given Z by linear interpolation with one of the vertex of the triangle- then you can interpolate a polynomialof degree 2, so can get all the points you want inside this triangle with the given Z.

To get a Z for a random X,Y inside a triangle (or three given x,y,z points), just follow the formulae in this article, it's in spanish but I think it's clear understandable the math part (they get continuity 0 in the interpolation, but it's very fast): <cite>http://ropdigital.ciccp.es/pdf/publico/2002/2002_enero_3417_03.pdf</cite>
