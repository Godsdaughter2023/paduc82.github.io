joe men say:

Hello Theresa,

To find the lost device, it must be running Windows 10 and be signed in to your Microsoft account. You can also use Find my phone to find, locate, lock, and even erase your Windows Phone.

Here are the steps on how you can find/track a Windows device:

  1. Go to account.microsoft.com/devices. If you're asked to sign in, use the same Microsoft account you used to sign in to the lost device.
  2. Choose your device from the list, then select Find my device.
  3. You'll see a map with a highlighted location. However, this may not be your device's current location. When a Microsoft device is turned on, we periodically save your device location, so check the Last seen at time-stamp.
  4. In the meantime, we'll automatically start a new search. Wait a bit and the new device location should appear.

Notes:

  • You must be signed in to the lost device with a Microsoft account for which you're the administrator. If you're using a work or school account, Find my device won't work.
  • To send its location, your device must be connected to the Internet, have enough battery power, and use GPS.

thundertiger say: When you upgrade the firmware on your phone it is required to remove any encryption and passwords on the device and the memory card so you won't accidentally lock yourself out of your phone or memory card. The firmware upgrade should only effect the phone, but it is a good idea to remove the memory card before you upgrade the firmware. You WILL lose any programs, contacts, ring tones, images, etc.. that is stored on the phone itself when you go to install a new firmware. ALWAYS back everything up BEFORE performing a firmware update. Then after the firmware update is finished you can go back and install your backups. One suggestion would be to not backup any settings. As those generally cause problems when you restore them from backup. NOTE: You may have to reinstall all your programs from scratch as they may not get backed up.

When you enable "By Author" for tracking colors, you give up all control over the colors used. As long as the color used for your changes is different from those used by other editors, Word is working as designed.

If you accept all the changes (and delete all the comments) made in the names of the added reviewers, you may be able to remove those reviewers.
