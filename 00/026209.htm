Hi Gary,

OneNote for iOS and Mac can not directly open .one files, they can only sync down files from OneDrive.

You will need to use OneNote on Windows to open up that .one file and move it to a notebook on OneDrive.

Once that file is integrated into a Notebook on OneDrive, you can confirm by using a browser and logging into OneNote.com to see all notebooks.

Then you can log into that account with iOS OneNote and sync that notebook down.

Here is an article that helps explain the process.

https://support.office.com/en-us/article/How-to-move-my-local-notebooks-to-OneDrive-or-SharePoint-7343C424-CDEE-4BBF-9245-312549E81FC0

Just so you don't overeat as a result, the calorie expenditure for the Band is fairly inaccurate (overestimates). This is a problem with every fitness tracker, treadmill, and exercise bike (that doesn't have a power meter). I would just use it to track total daily activity and ignore the calories burned for the most part.

A hard hour cycling for me (160-170 bpm heart rate) is about 650 calories. That's really going hard. It's possible that you burned 1000 calories in an hour gardening but unless it was spent carrying mulch bags up a hill, I would guess you're probably closer to 1/3 of that total.

But to answer your question overall, you need to track an activity to see caloric burn. Otherwise you're seeing just a slightly elevated basal metabolic rate in essence.
