robert__p say:

Hi Jennifer,

Generally, if one user doesn’t have the “Send on Behalf” permission for one mailbox and he will receive the error “You do not have the permission to send the message on behalf of the specified user.” when trying to send emails on behalf of the user.

So I would like to confirm whether the affected users are trying to send email on behalf of other mailboxes by changing the “From” address?

If yes, you need to grant the “Send on Behalf” permission for the user according to the above article first. Please note that it applies to user mailbox too. Here are the steps:

1. Sign in to Exchange admin center (https://outlook.office365.com/ecp) with your admin account.

2. Navigate to recipients—mailboxes, double click the mailbox that you want to assign permissions for.

3. On the mailbox properties page, click Mailbox Delegation. To assign permissions to delegates, click + to add the “Send on Behalf” permission. Click save at last.

If you already assign the permission to the user and you encounter the issue, I would like to confirm the following information for troubleshooting:

1. Where do the affected users try to send emails on behalf of other users, in the Outlook client or Outlook Web App (https://outlook.office365.com/owa)?

2. If in the Outlook client, what’s the detailed version of the Outlook client?

3. If you remove the permission and re-add it, does the issue persist?

Thanks,

Robert
