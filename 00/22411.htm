Unable to 'send mail as' through Outlook SMTP servers: - Microsoft Community

jojo& say:

Unable to 'send mail as' through Outlook SMTP servers: Error 535 on Gmail.

Hello,

I realise this problem has been around for a long time. There used to be a workaround thanks to a flaw in the Linked Accounts feature, but as you can no longer link your Live/Outlook accounts in the same fashion since Spring 2013, there is now no workaround published on the forums. I would like to create an opportunity for the Microsoft staff to offer a clear and universal solution which is up to date as per August 2013.

PROBLEM DESCRIPTION
When you want to use your Gmail account to 'send mail as' your Outlook/Live username, you need to setup a special feature in the Gmail settings. Gmail offers you to choose between sending an email as youroutlookaddress through Gmail servers or through Outlook's SMTP servers. If you wish to proceed through Gmail servers, things work fine, but if you wish to go through Outlook's SMTP, they don't.
Namely, the setup process requires you to supply:
  • The SMTP Server address: smtp.live.com 
  • The connection port 587, 465, or 25
  • Your Outlook/Live account username
  • Your Outlook/Live account password
  • The encryption type: TLS, SSL, or unsecured (port 25 only)
And no matter the combination you use, there is no way you can set this up for your Outlook/Live account. The errors displayed span as follow:
  • With port 587 and TLS selected: "Authentication failed. Please check your username/password. [Server response: 535 5.2.1 Login to your account via a web browser to verify your account. code(535)
  • With port 587 and SSL selected: "Couldn't reach server. Please double-check the server and port number.
  • With port 465 and SSL selected: "Your other email provider is responding too slowly. Please try again later, or contact the administrator of your other domain for further information.
  • With port 465 and TLS selected: "Your other email provider is responding too slowly. Please try again later, or contact the administrator of your other domain for further information.
  • With port 25 and TLS selected: "Authentication failed. Please check your username/password.
