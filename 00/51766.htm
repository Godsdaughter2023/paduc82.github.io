tobytime say: One of those posts pointed me in the correct general direction, THE REGISTRY.  I suspected this was the problem, but had had no success finding the entries by searching because they were missing!

For anyone wanting to fix this problem:

Backup the registry!!  (I didn’t, but have regretted it in the past.)

Run Regedit

Go to HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\services\Tcpip\Parameters\Interfaces

I compared my working computer to the non-working one.

The working computer had a number of entries (separate keys), which I suspect I could delete, but didn’t want to risk it. 

When I compared working to non-working, I found that four entries were missing:

DefaultGateway                  REG_MULTL_SZ          192.168.1.1
DefaultGatewayMetric       REG_MULTL_SZ          0
IPAddress                           REG_MULTL_SZ          192.168.1.xxx
SubnetMask                       REG_MULTL_SZ          255.255.255.0

These were visible under [Control Panel - Network and Internet - Network Connections - RgtClk Adapter - Properties] IPv4 (except the Default Gateway); they weren’t being saved in the registry.  No idea why, but this fixed the problem.
