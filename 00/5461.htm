monirg say:
Hello;
 
Problem solved.
Here’s a summary in a point format of each of the two (2) working procedures:
 
Procedure 1: Charles Kenyon et al
==========
a) Copy the old (Word 2003) Normal.dot template (~ 2 MB) from:
... C:\Documents and Settings\Monir\Application Data\Microsoft\Templates
b) Rename the template as myOldmacros.dot and transfer it to the new laptop (Word 2010) at:
... C:\Users\Monir\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Word\STARTUP
c) Start Word 2010, and under: Developer::Macros, ALL old macros would be displayed in the Macros window, but not all the options would be available. Edit and Delete would be grayed out.
d) To Edit or Delete an old macro from the displayed list:
> close Word
> if you expect to access the template very often, as in my situation, create a desktop shortcut for:
... C:\Users\Monir\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Word\STARTUP\myOldmacros.dot
> right-click the icon and select Open
> select the Developer::Macros code tool in the ribbon sub-section
> now ALL the macro options in the Macros window are available, including Edit & Delete.
e) Procedure works fine.
f) Note: By putting the old Normal.dot template (which functions as Add-In) in the ...\Word\STARTUP folder, the Word 2010 application may considerably slow down, depending on the contents of the old template.
In my situation, the delay is ~ 60 sec to open or close a new blank Word 2010 document. (See also item ix below).
 
Procedure 2: Graham Mayor et al
==========
i) Copy the old (Word 2003) Normal.dot template (~ 2 MB) from:
... C:\Documents and Settings\Monir\Application Data\Microsoft\Templates
ii) Transfer the copy to the new laptop (Word 2010) at:
... C:\Users\Monir\AppData\Roaming\Microsoft\Templates
iii) Rename the existing Normal.dotm in the Templates folder as normal.old
iv) Start Word 2010. Make sure that the option ‘Prompt before saving the normal template’ is checked:
... (File :: Options :: Advances :: Save)
v) Close Word, and Save the normal template at the prompt.
vi) Restart Word and check Developer::Macros accessibility, options, etc..
vii) Remove the Normal.dot and normal.old from the Templates folder (i above), leaving only Normal.dotm.
viii) Procedure works fine.
ix) Note: Opening and closing a new blank Word 2010 becomes a bit slower. But I believe this maybe attributed to the fact that the majority of the ‘old’ macros (as I’ve just discovered) were ‘recorded’ 8 – 9 years ago, and although they work fine and as desired, each contains many redundant statements and very inefficient.
But, it isn’t a top priority for me right now!
 
Thank you ALL for your generous time and tremendous help in resolving the issue.
