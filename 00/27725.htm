abi99 (2) say:

My Lumia is not listed anywhere on my PC.

There is only one place to look for the Windows Device Manager. The one I mentioned. If your phone is not listed there, you face an issue on the USB hardware layer. In that case, either the

A) USB cable is broken, please, try another one

B) USB Host-Controller in your computer is incompatible, please, mention its brand and model

C) USB port on your Lumia is dirty or broken, please, try another Lumia 435

Case B might sound confusing to you, because other devices work. However, you cannot conclude from 1, 2, 3, onto N. Your USB Host-Controller might face an interoperability issue with Lumia 435 phones. One workaround is to test every USB port on your computer, and double-check while the Device Manager is open. Anyway, if you were able to rule out A and C, please, mention the brand and model of your USB Host-Controller(s).

First, your phone has to appear in the Device Manager. If it is not listed there, neither the USB MTP driver of the Windows Media Player nor the Windows Phone Desktop app are able to kick in, because they do not see your phone either. By the way, Windows XP should be of no issue when the Windows Media Player is installed. However, even there, the Lumia has to pop-up in the Windows Device Manager first.
