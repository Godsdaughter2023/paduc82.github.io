rohn007 say:

Pro Plus is a limited distribution bundle.  Getting the installation file is tricky. It is not provided through the normal distribution modes. Exactly how / where did you buy it?

For consumers there are basically 3 ways to get it.

The easy way is through the Home Use Program (HUP) that gives employers right to grant employees option to buy a discounted copy of Office Pro Plus for approx US$10-16.   If you got it that way, click on the "Home Use Program" link in the error message. Their help desk should be able to help you with the download.

The other legitimate way is through a Volume license. Volume agreements require you to buy a minimum of 5 licenses (does not have to be all Office), and it tends to be more expensive than other ways.

Finally, Pro Plus is often "bootlegged" on various no MS approved web sites.  They buy a volume license, resell the product keys for ridiculously low prices, like $40-100.  The problem is volume licenses explicitly prohibit sale of the key outside of the original purchasing company.  Unwitting buyers get caught 2 a few ways:

  • The key type may have a limited number activations
  • the key may be tied to a corporate activation server (your problem)
  • MS may recognize that the key has been bootlegged and simply block it
