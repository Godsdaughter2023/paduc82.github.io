e.j. gun say:

Let's say in cell A1 you have the text "B7".

In cell B7 you have the value 15.

In cell B2, place the formula "=INDIRECT(A1)"

What do you get?  You get the value 15, because cell A1 is "pointing to" cell B7.

 

That's how INDIRECT works - the argument is a reference to another cell, not just a number.  In your case, you are telling Excel to put the value of cell "5" into A2, which does not make sense.  You can put something like "A5" in cell A1 and then cell A2 would have whatever value is in cell A5.

 

HTH,

Eric

 

P.S. - you might be thinking of the way the OFFSET function works - take a look at both functions in Excel help.
