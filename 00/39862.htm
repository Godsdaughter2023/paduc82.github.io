canadian tech say:

The Microsoft Store employee gave you BAD advice.  It simply is not true.

I do this often for my clients.  Many of my clients' computers are 7,  8 or even 10 years old -- still running Windows 7 quite well.

In most cases, drivers are still available on OEM web sites.  And, in most cases, there have been no or very few legitimate driver updates.  There are more "driver update" sites on the web than you can count.  They are downright dangerous.  The vast majority of their updates are either bogus, don't work or are the wrong ones, AND, they come with much malware.  If your system is Intel-based, Intel has a good driver update utility that may be worthwhile trying. 

If your PC is more than 5 years old, as a matter of good maintenance practice, it you are doing a clean, new install, replace the hard drive and do a full install instead of a Factory restore.  The Win7 install process will in itself install the vast majority of the drivers it needs.
