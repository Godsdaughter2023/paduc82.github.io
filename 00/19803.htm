How to delete header sections? - Microsoft Community

bluesoda212 say:

How to delete header sections?

Hi there,

I am working on a document created by others. In this Word document, it has numerous headers and sections, which created a lot of blank pages I cannot even delete. I tried all the methods suggested online -- didn't work. I found the fundamental problem of these blank pages is they are sections of the headers. So I am just wondering how can I reverse/delete these sections created by the headers? I want to have a completely clean doc without headers and sections. I appreciate your contribution.

Thanks,

Pat

john korchok say:

Headers don't create sections.

Sections are created by inserting Section Breaks in documents. Using Word>Preferences>View and checking All in the Show Non-Printing Characters section will display them. Then you can select and delete them. This will hide the headers associated with the section. It may also change formatting for the pages before the break, since section formatting is contained in the section breaks. Do this work on a copy of the file, not the opriginal.

To get a clean version, you'll probably find it easier to create a new blank document, then paste in content from the other file, exclusing the section breaks.

bob jones aka: cybertaz say:

Another way to explain it is that Headers & Footers are properties of Sections, not the other way around. One purpose of inserting Section Breaks is to enable different Headers and/or Footers for the Section. However, there are various types of Section Breaks including Odd Page & Even Page Section Breaks. Use of those very well may cause the "blank" pages you're referring to because there cannot be 2 consecutive Odd or 2 consecutive Even pages. Word automatically generates the necessary filler page when an Odd Page Section Break is inserted on an Odd page or an Even Page Section Break is inserted on an even page.

If you truly want a "completely clean doc..." use Command+A to Select All, then copy the content. In a new blank document use Edit> Paste Special & select either Unformatted Text or Unicode Unformatted Text. The clean content will be pasted as plain text with no Section Breaks & no Headers or Footers. However, it also will break any fields including page numbering, Table of Contents, Index, etc. & will exclude any graphic content. You then can edit & format to suit your purpose & later copy/paste graphics if necessary.

cornhuskr4life say:

Protecting a form within a Word document, but not the whole document.

I have a Mentoring document that has multiple forms within the document. To activate the fields in the forms I had to protect the document, and used the "fill in forms" permission. The problem is that while in this protect mode, the remainder of the document cannot be used functionally, like the hyperlinks in the Table of contents and throughout the document are disabled in this mode. Is there another way I can protect and activate the forms within this document, while retaining the functionality of a read only document in the remainder of the text?
jay freedman say:
First, while the document is not protected, insert continuous section breaks before and after each part of the document
that constitutes a "form". Then place the cursor in each "form" in turn and write down the section number shown in the
status bar at the bottom of the window.
 
Now open the Restrict Formatting & Editing pane. When the editing restrictions dropdown is set to "Filling in forms", a
blue link to "Select sections" will appear below the dropdown. Click the link, and uncheck boxes until only the "forms"
sections are still checked. Then finish protecting the document as before. The sections you unchecked will be
unprotected.
 
Even in unprotected sections there will still be some features that don't work. Test your form to see whether you can do
everything you need, and post back if there are problems.
 
Jay Freedman
