jmh3143 say:

To report spam or unsolicited emails:

 

In Outlook, select the offending message and click the Junk button to report the message in one action; or you may click the arrow next to the Junk button and select:

  • Junk for routine junk email.
  • Phishing scam for email that is trying to trick you into giving out sensitive personal information like passwords, bank accounts, or your Social Security number.
  • My friend's been hacked! if you suddenly start getting junk email or phishing from a sender you would normally trust.

 

For non- Outlook or Hotmail customers should address an email to report_spam@outlook.com. Attach a copy of the spam email to this email and send it. If we do not receive a copy of the email as an attachment our ability to take action against the spamming account is severely impacted.

 

Note: We ask for a full copy of the email so that we have conclusive evidence showing from which Outlook/Hotmail account the SPAM was sent. It is possible that the email address was spoofed, and we don't want to mistakenly close a non-spamming account.

 

To report abuse:

Customers who wish to report abuse about harassment, impersonation, child exploitation, child pornography etc., received from an Outlook/Hotmail account should send their complaint to abuse@outlook.com with all the pertaining information.

 

If you are being threatened please call your local law enforcement.

 

If you wish to report abuse against a non-Outlook/Hotmail account, please send the complaint to abuse@xxxxxx.com , where xxxxxx.com is the text that appears after the "@" symbol in the sender's e-mail address. For example, if the unwanted email came from someone@mymailmessages.com then report the email message to abuse@mymailmessages.com. You can also use the site http://abuse.net  to identify the correct abuse reporting address.

In addition to these ways of reporting Spam you can also click the "Report Spam," button in your email program.  In fact, Outlook and Hotmail have setup systems where we automatically receive these reports from the following ISPs and anti-spam providers: Yahoo, Gmail, AOL, Comcast, Roadrunner, Outblaze, SpamCop, Signal-Spam, Verizon and Nortel.

Several other ISPs have setup their "Report Spam" button to point directly to abuse@outlook.com.

jason (office engineer) say:

Hi Nelly,

So there are couple of problems that you have described and I will do my best to explain how to work around each of them.

First of all, the issue with the Sways failing to load and slowing down is a known bug that we are working to improve.  The problem happens when a Sway is duplicated and edited many times like this:  it becomes very slow and hits the types of errors that you described.

The recommended workaround is to use the template feature instead of the progressive duplicate strategy that you have been using.  Merely create a new Sway from scratch that you want to act as a template and only include the generic content that you want to show up in future newsletters.  Next, choose Save as Template from the ... menu.  That template will now appear on the My Sways page and you can create a new Sway from it each week.

Sway also supports copying and pasting content from one Sway to another to make template creation easier.  You can select and copy multiple cards at a time from another Sway (with Ctrl+c) and paste them into your new Sway (with Ctrl+v) to save you some time.

The second issue you mentioned has to do with some of your images showing an error.  This can happen any time you copy an image from a web page that is not publicly available.  One way to work around this is to use the card copy/paste method from the storyline I described above.  Another way to accomplish this is to save the image locally first and then upload it into the new Sway.

I hope this helps clear things ups and I apologize that this is such a complicated process.  Do let us know if you run into any other issues or have further questions.

Jason
