john - project say:
PMP849,
Wow, that wasn't much fun but I did finally stumble on the trick to make it work. This type of thing was a whole lot easier before the "ribbon", in my humble opinion.

Apparently Excel doesn't like the type of data contained in the % Complete field from Project. It won't reformat it until you do a couple of things. First of all, I assume you have the little alert indicator triangle in the upper left corner of each cell that has the % complete data. If you do not, you need to set the Options/Formulas group/error checking rules to show "numbers formatted as text or preceded by an apostrophe". Second, click on the little alert indicator to bring up the alert options. Select the one to convert the text to a number. That gets the data into a form that Excel can recognize as numeric. Third, select the whole column where the % complete data is shown. Then go to Home/Styles group and click on the Cell Styles icon. That will bring up the cell styles window. Click on the Percent style for the number format. Wha Laa! You're there.

Hope this helps.
