Well, we have to disregard one thing straight away and that's the idea you've incorrectly been given about replacing the video card.

Your computer which is a laptop has two video 'cards' namely and Intel 4600 and an nVidia GT 640. Both of these are chips on the motherboard rather than dedicated video cards of the type you find in PCs. So I'm afraid changing either of them is out of the question since they're both soldered on to the motherboard.

The Intel graphics are perfectly capable of running lightweight tasks like playing simple games, browsing the web, watching youtube videos etc., while the nVidia chip can handle much more demanding tasks such as playing graphic intensive games. The computer is programmed to switch between the two as and when it proves necessary.

However, it's a gamers machine, not one designed to run Autodesk. If you check the recommended graphics solution on this page you'll see they're all nVidia Quadros and all on Workstations, not laptops. Here's a screenshot of it.

Dropping down to the 2014 release and the OS has to be Windows 8.0 or less. It can run on the Intel 4600 according to the specs which is why it probably worked just fine before.

The problem here is that support for Windows 8.0 expires on January 12 next year since Microsoft made it a prerequisite to upgrade to the 8.1 Update in order to receive support until the year 2023.

What I would suggest you do is to download the free trial for the current version and see if it will run on your laptop.  http://www.autodesk.com/free-trials

That I would imagine will be a lot cheaper than replacing the laptop.
