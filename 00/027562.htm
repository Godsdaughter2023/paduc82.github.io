periwinkle_28 say:

Hi Lollylou,

 

Teletypewriter (TTY), also known as a text telephone or a Telecommunication Device for the Deaf (TDD) allows people who are speech impaired, hearing impaired or hard of hearing to type messages to anyone else who has a TTY using a telephone line.

 

If you want to use your phone with a TTY/TDD to make a phone call: 

 

  1. Plug your phone into the TTY using the appropriate cable and connector for the headset jack. 
  2. On Start, flick left to the App list, and then tap Settings. 
  3. Tap Ease of access, and then tap Full mode. 
    Note: Depending on your mobile operator, you might have additional modes available. If so, tap the mode that you want to use.
  4. Turn on the TTY.
    That's all you need to do - now you are ready to make your call. See the documentation for your TTY for more info on how to use it.

Note: This feature is a network or region dependent, so you have to verify from your network provider if they support this feature.

philip bannor say: If you can connect your phone to your PC so that you can browse your memory card, etc - but Nokia PC Suite (or OVI Suite) will not recognise your phone, make sure you have the Windows Service called 'Service Layer' (it doesn't seem to have a description, but if you look in the Properties of this service you will see that the executable is "C:\Program Files (x86)\PC Connectivity Solution\ServiceLayer.exe") - make sure this service is 1) started and 2) set to start automatically. This MAY require a restart of your PC. Make sure the service is running ('started'); restart PC Suite and reconnect your phone. This solved the problem I was having with PC Suite.
