steve rindsberg say:
When you see "not responding" in the title bar of a program, it can mean that the program has gone into the weeds, but can also mean that it's busy doing something complex that eats a lot of processor cycles and consumes so much of its "attention" that it's not paying attention to e.g. your mouseclicks etc.  

If you have very complex slides, that could easily happen when resizing or zooming.  The fact that the program goes non-responsive then comes back after a while reinforces this theory.  Still ... theory, not assertion. ;-)

So .. possible fixes/workarounds.

For starters, you may not actually need to resize the presentation.  PowerPoint will automatically resize your slides to fit, as large as possible, on whatever paper you select in the printer driver at print time.  You just need to click the down arrow next to Full Page Slides and make sure there's a check next to "Scale to fit paper"

You may also want to try selecting complex portions of your slide, copying them, moving them out of the way or deleting them, then Paste Special, as PNG.  I'd do that on a COPY of your presentation, not the original.  Please.

If you have lots of small shapes with gradient fills or lines, consider removing the gradients.
