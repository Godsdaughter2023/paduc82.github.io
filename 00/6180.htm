m h g say:

You're going from old (work) to new (home) and back to old (work). If the old version at work is Office 2007, and you're using .XLSX, .XLSM or .XLSB file formats, file-open password handling changed between Office 2007 and Office 2010. File-open passwords in Office 2007 were easily defeated. File-open passwords in Office 2010 and later are much more robust. Unfortunately, Office 2010 and later are entirely silent about converting Office 2007 file-open password-protected files to Office 2010 and later ones.

If you're using Office 2007 at work, that's your problem. And that's why you can open the file on your phone and Macs running more recent versions of Office.

If you have to use this file at work, don't use a file-open password. If you're concerned about protecting it, move it into a password-protected .ZIP file to e-mail it between work and home.

In place of
