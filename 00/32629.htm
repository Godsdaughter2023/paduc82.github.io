nsilmike say: First, don't do that... using the laptop until the battery dies is a good way to shorten the battery life (as in it will need to be replaced.)  You can set your power settings on battery to warn you when you get too low... turn that on.  You will get the best battery performance and life if you use it gently and don't discharge it completely unless absolutely necessary.
Second, if the computer loses power and shuts down suddenly, then programs and applications cannot shut themselves down properly.  That is very likely the cause of your sound issue.  Rebooting probably fixes this, right?
Third, if the computer shuts down suddenly, it cannot 'clean' the swap file.  The swap file is a location on your hard drive that windows temporarily stores data that can't fit in memory when you run multiple tasks.  If the swap file isn't handled properly, your performance is impacted... slowly you will use up more and more hard drive space for useless tmp files, and your PC performance will be impacted as well.

If you are being harassed by a person and have issues being DDoSed, mute and block that player, and then disconnect your Router/Gateway/Modem for about a minute. After you reconnect to the internet, you'll have a new IP address. 

Unless that person manages to get into the same party or multiplayer match, they don't have your new IP. Therefore I recommend to set your Parties to "invite only", and to try and only play with people you know and trust, instead of public matches (if possible).
