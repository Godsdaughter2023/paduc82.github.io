bill manville say:

In spreadsheet B, A2:
=IF(MOD(ROW(),2)=0,"",OFFSET('Spreadsheet A'!$B$1,INT(ROW()/2),0))

In B2 the same formula but with $A instead of $B
In C2 the same with $C and in D2 the same with $D

Then you can fill the formulas down as far as necessary.

If you go too far you will find rows of 0 appearing.
That could be fixed by making the formula more complicated:

=IF(MOD(ROW(),2)=0,"",IF(INT(ROW()/2)>=COUNTA('Spreadsheet A'!$A:$A),"",OFFSET('Spreadsheet A'!$B$1,INT(ROW()/2),0)))
