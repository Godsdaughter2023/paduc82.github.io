johna5 say:

Turned out in my case to be a subtly incorrect registry key in [HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows NT\CurrentVersion\Ports].  Specifically, the (Default) entry existed as blank.  It should read (value not set).  One solution is to export the key to a file... and look for a line reading: @="", open the file and remove that line, rename the registry Ports to Ports.bad (for backup) and then import the file.  That's what I did and it worked fine after restarting the spooler.  Another solution might more easily be to delete the (Default) entry and then restart the spooler ... In any case, I now have all my local ports that survive a reboot.
