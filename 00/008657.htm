doug robbins - mvp office apps & services (word) say:
To save me having to create a 32 or more page document with linked textboxes on each page, if you can send me a copy of the document, referencing this thread in the covering email message, I will investigate the issue.

suzanne s. barnhill say:
The reason for this is that there is a fixed limit of 32 linked text boxes in Word. To write flowing text in two columns, use a two-column table instead. Make sure to start a new row at least once a page.

wyrel79 say:

Thank you Suzanne,

That would indeed explain the problem.  Out of interest, have you any idea why there is such a limit?

suzanne s. barnhill say:
It dates back to the introduction of text boxes, I think, and probably reflects a time when computer processing power was considerably less. It is not stated in “OperatingParameter Limitations and Specifications” or even in “WD97:Operating Parameter Limitations and Specifications,” which lists more specs (some obsolete). The only reason I happen to know is that years ago someone came up with a macro for booklet printing just to get around this limitation. Once Word 2002 introduced the "Book fold" option, that became unnecessary.

Viewing Tasks and To-Do List at same time in Outlook 2016 - Microsoft Community

gordon-kidz say:

Viewing Tasks and To-Do List at same time in Outlook 2016

I am a heavy user of the combined task/to-do list format in Office 2010. Just upgraded to 2016 and can't seem to find a setting to see these in one list. What am I missing?

gordon-kidz say:

Well, I finally discovered the answer on my own. In case someone else comes along with this same question, here is the solution.

First of all, let me clarify that what I was asking was never addressed. In Outlook 2010, one could view a "to do" list (alone) with all flagged emails and tasks in ONE merged list. I am not looking for an abbreviated list viewed from calendar view or email window, but a fully dedicated view in TASKS.

This is possible after all, but with some exceptions:

* Emails from previous versions do NOT migrate into 2016 well if flagged. 

* Emails that are flagged and not in the "in" box are moved into the "in" box in 2016, often creating duplicates.

* Categories in 2010 are not applied to email flags in 2016, but must be recreated (in a different list) and re-posted to emails.

* Combined list does finally appear after all the above work is recreated, but if the new email flags are not identical to the old/migrated task categories, of course they will not merge together.

* Finally, the various views of the task list are not quite the same, but there are enough options that a user can find something somewhat similar to the version used preivously.
