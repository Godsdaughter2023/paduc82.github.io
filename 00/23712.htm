mccbleue say:

What exactly do you mean by "problem with the power"?

 

It is normal for the phone to shut down and restart several times during the update, in rare cases it occurs that the phone cannot restart afterwards, in which case with a phone like the 7210 all you can do is take it to a Nokia service point. If the phone is still under warranty, damage done to your phone during the process of a software update can be fixed free of charge.

 

If you mean that you actually lost electricity in the building, or some other problem causing the PC to shut down during the process, then it is probable the process of installing the software has been interrupted at a critical phase and left the phone without a full set of software - this means that the phone cannot start at all and again would have to be referred to Nokia service.This is always a risk when updating the software of any phone, this is also why there are warnings in the updater not to disconnect the phone from the PC during the process - it is also why the update will not run unless the phone has almost a full charge, so that it can be sure that the phone is always able to power up itself.

 

It is worth noting that a laptop or a PC with connected to the power via a transformer with an emergency pwoer reserve is often the safer option, because if the mains electricity shuts down the computer will continue to run for a period of time on its own power, and therefore you lessen the risk of aborting the update process unexpectedly.

Yes, you can do this.


You need to be able to:

  1. Log in to MS Domain management with an MS account (that is not on your new domain). It is at http://www.domains.live.com/. Add the new domain.
  2. Add an MX record into your domain with whoever hosts it for you (GoDaddy, 1and1 etc). This points your domain's email to Outlook.com. The text you need to put in the MX record is given to you when you add the domain in step 1.
  3. Add your new email addresses (up to 50)
