I cannot send or receive SMS, what can I do?

Confirm with your network operator that the text messaging (SMS) is included in your service contract and that the message center number is correctly defined in your device. Most of times, the SMS sending or receiving fails after you change the operator since the message center number may not be updated. Contact your current network operator for the correct SMS settings. More and more operators can send you the settings straight to your device. If you choose to change the message center number in your device manually, do the following:

  • For S60 device, select Menu > Messaging > Options > Settings > Text message > Message center in use.
  • For S40 device, select Menu > Messaging > Message settings > Text message > Msg. center in use. 
  • For S30 device, select Menu > Messages > Message settings > Sending profile > Profile 1 > Message center number.

If the previous action does not help, check the following:

  • If your device supports dual mode, change to GSM mode and retry.
  • Insert another SIM card in your device and try. If it is working, network problem is eliminated.
  • Insert your SIM card in another device and try. If it is working, you may restore the factory settings on your device.
     

If the problem still remains, visit your local Nokia Care Point.

"Select Members" is the same view as the address book view. The address book view contains no data. It only displays the electonic addresses that reside in your Contacts Folder(s). So if you think you have a contact that has a valid electronic address but does not appear in that view, by definition, that Contact must reside in a folder other than the one you are displaying. Do you have more than one Contacts Folder? Use Folder List view to examine your entire foler hierarchy to see if you've created more than one Contacts Folder.

I dont understand how using Categories is any easier?  Why is the "distribution" list thing unwieldly?

For example, "Friends", I have lots of friends, but do not forward email to all of them.  Some are only interested in certain kinds of emails.  With a distribution list I can send email only to those friends with that particular interest.

OK. A Contact can have many categories - not just one.

For example. Joe Bloggs can have Categories of Friends, Cricket Club, Lunch Club. Fred Gubbins can have Categories of Mechanic, Bowling Club, Friends.

Didtribution lists ONLY contain email addresses - because Categories are associated with a Contact, you have the FULL Contact detail available to you.

Categories are FAR easier to maintain then Distribution lists, particularly so if the DL contains more than a few members.

If you add a new contact, you THEN have to do a separate operation to add them to a DL, and similarly if you remove the contact, you then have to a) remember what DL they are in and b) edit the DL, because the Contact doesn't carry the information about what DL it is in. If you use Categories, you add the Category to the contact while you are creating it, and if you remove the contact at a later date, then they are just err, gone! Nothing else to do.

Also if and when you transfer your Outlook data to a new machine, Distribution lists are reknowned for being flakey in the transfer whereas Categories are not.

Give them a try - many people don't use anything else these days.
