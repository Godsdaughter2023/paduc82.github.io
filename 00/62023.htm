arminatorx say:

First of all, make sure the console is connected online properly, as the 360 game discs are there "only" as a proof of purchase. The actual backwards compatible game has to be downloaded from the Xbox Live Marketplace by the console automatically, as soon as it detects the disc. 

In order for the console to detect the disc, the security sector of the disc has to be readable. The actual data of the game can be scratched or otherwise unreadable, the only important thing for the Xbox One has to be the security sector. 

So please try the following: 

Eject your Xbox 360 game disc, and then hold the power button on the console for about 10 seconds. 

After the console has shut down, press the power button again to turn the console back on. 

When the console starts, check that it shows a green boot screen with the Xbox One logo. If the green boot screen does not appear, hold the power button for 20 seconds again, and restart the console. 

While the green boot screen shows, it can take some time for the console to start up. So take your Xbox 360 game disc to the kitchen in the mean time, and apply a small drop of mild dish washer soap to the silver side of the disc. 

With wet fingers, "massage" the soap around the silver surface of the disc, until the whole silver side is covered in soap. 

From now on, hold the disc with your index finger in the center hole, and with your thumb on the edge of the disc only!

Rinse the disc under running water to get the soap off. Then shake the disc several times, and wipe the remaining water drops with a soft, lint-free cloth. If you have someone in the family wearing glasses, the cleaning cloth for such glasses would be ideal. 

Now, with the clean disc, return to your console, make sure the console is connected online, and updated, and then insert the disc. 

Check that the game tile appears with a disc icon in the corner. The console should then start to download the game from the Xbox Marketplace. 

If the console does not detect the game disc, check if the console can read a movie DVD (not a BluRay disc). If the console continues to fail reading movie DVDs or all Xbox 360 game discs, but works with BluRay movies and Xbox One game discs, it might be that your console's disc drive is faulty. 

In that case you would have to sign up the console for repair at https://devicesupport.microsoft.com

To open a command prompt as Admin,

Start button,

scroll down to the Windows system entry,

right click on the Command prompt entry,

hover over More,

click on the Run as Administrator entry,

give yourself Admin permission to proceed by clicking Yes in the "Consent UI" dialog that appears,

Hey there Techn9cian 816 this could be too do with your network address translation otherwise known as NAT, Sometimes the reason why you cannot connect to other people on Xbox live is because they have a Moderate to strict NAT Setting, have you checked all of your settings? On Xbox and your router configuration page? otherwise i suggest you open up some ports on your router these being  

* TCP 80
* UDP 88
* UDP 3074
* TCP 3074
* UDP 53
* TCP 53

Thats what the ports are.

In the address bar type in your routers IP address. It should be default at 192.168.1.1 or 192.168.2.1. When you're prompted for a password you have found it =P. If you haven't changed it the username and password should both be admin, and admin.

Step 3: USING DMZ When you enter into your router config window, you need to find the Tab DMZ, it should be under applications and gaming if you are using Linksys. Once you find the DMZ tab, click enable and type in the last digits of your XBOX into the box below. Yes when you do this you are opening all the ports on your Xbox, which is insecure, but show me someone that has hacked into an xbox 360!

MAKE SURE YOU SAVE SETTINGS!

Step 4:Using Port Forwarding After you have enabled DMZ, find the Port Forwarding tab, which should be under advanced routing. For the name type in XBL or whatever you want, and for port range put 87 to 89, choose UDP, then your XBOX last digits and enable. Then do 3073 to 3075 choose TCP, then your XBOX last digits and enable. Then again do 3073 to 3075 choose UDP, then your XBOX last digits, and enable.

MAKE SURE YOU SAVE SETTINGS

Step 5: Using UPnP This is just in case. Go into UPnP forwarding and enter the same stuff you did for Step 4.
MAKE SURE YOU SAVE SETTINGS

Step 6: Now logout of your router config. Unplug your router and modem from the power, then plug in the router 1st, wait 30 seconds. Then plug in the modem and wait at least 2 minutes. If you don't do these hard restarts it might not work!

Step 7: Restart your Xbox and go to the system blade, choose network settings, then test connection. This may take a bit, but when it gets down to NAT type it should say OPEN. If not, sorry. vibroplex say:

Some NAT problems are subtle and don't appear in the Test Xbox Live Connection test. Most likely there's a port blocked... ports can be blocked in your router, modem, or at your ISP. Since the Xbox 360 uses different ports than PCs & Macs use, you can't see the effect of a port blockage except on the Xbox.

 

A sequential power cycle is the first step in clearing a port blockage:

1) turn off the Xbox then

2) unplug power from the router (Some folks have a combination modem-router AKA 'gateway' or only use a modem with no router. If either, skip steps 3 & 6).

3) unplug power from the modem.

4) Wait at least 30 secs then replug the modem (or gateway) power.

5) Wait 'til the light on the front of the modem (or gateway) which says ONLINE, READY, CABLE or DSL is on solid (not flashing), then plug in the router. If you have a gateway instead of a modem and router or a modem with no router, then when that light says it's ready, skip step 6.

6) Wait until the WiFi light on the router is on; you may not use WiFi, but it's still a good indicator your router is finished booting up.

7) Turn on the Xbox.  For more background on Ports, see whatismyipaddress.com/port

 

If the problem persists, please send us the info we need from forums.xbox.com/.../155724.aspx so we may work on this further.

hassanb say:

Hello,

You can't link an offline account with an already online account. If you wish to make an offline account an online account you'd need to login to your console with the offline account and push the middle button on your Xbox 360 controller and go to Join Xbox LIVE. You'll need to enter a Microsoft Account e-mail that does not have an e-mail associated with it. When that process is complete you'll end up with a randomly generated gamertag with all of the offline achievements transferred to an online account.

You can then change that gamertag one time for free within the first 30 days of the account creation if you wish to do so. However, it's not possible to merge accounts at all which is what I believe you wish to do.

Translation:

Olá,

Você não pode vincular uma conta off-line com uma conta já online. Se você deseja fazer uma conta off-line uma conta on-line você precisa fazer o login para o seu console com a conta off-line e apertar o botão do meio no seu controlador Xbox 360 e vá para Aderir Xbox LIVE. Você precisa inserir uma conta de e-mail Microsoft que não tem um e-mail associado a ela. Quando esse processo for concluído, você vai acabar com um gamertag gerado aleatoriamente com todas as conquistas off-line transferidos para uma conta online.

Você pode então mudar essa gamertag uma vez de graça nos primeiros 30 dias da criação da conta, se desejar fazê-lo. No entanto, não é possível mesclar contas em tudo que é o que eu acredito que você deseja fazer.
