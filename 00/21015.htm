brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:
Every mailbox is autonomous. No mail client can (or should) sync them with each other. If you want to have a combined view, some devices allow that (although they're not really combined, just presented as though they are), but real synching involves you having a process that moves messages between the mailboxes. Mail systems allow this by forwarding or, for many, but not all, mail systems, by connected accounts. This allows you to gather your mail into a single mailbox and manipulate it there. Personally, I don't like combined mailboxes. It's too easy to respond with the wrong address as the sender.
