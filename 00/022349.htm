Numbering applied to Cover Pages is a bit complex and Doug's formula will not work for everyone. The answer depends on which version of Word created the document and how the Cover Page was inserted. If you use the Cover Page on the Insert tab, you will get different results depending on the version of Word. If you have a typed page that is followed by a next-page section break that restarts numbering, Doug's formula will work in any version of Word.

If you use a Cover Page inserted by Word in Word 2010 & 2016, the cover page will not show up in the NumPages field and will automatically be numbered as page 0. In Word 2013, it will show up in the NumPages field even though numbered 0. In Word 2007, the cover page is page 1 and shows up in the numpages field.

Whether a page number will show up on the cover page or not depends both on the structure of the cover page (some of them have graphics that cover the number) and on whether the Different First Page option is chosen.

See Cover Page Anomalies for more.

Here are screenshots from a document, with and without a cover page. Note the numbers in the status bar and in the Document Property Pages. The Document Property "Pages" will always include the cover page.

Results in Word 2007 and 2013 will be different. I suspect but do not know that documents in compatibility mode will act like those of the original version.

Note in these screen shots the Page in the status bar reflects the page preceding that shown in the headers. Note also the different page fields in the status bar. The one at left is the formatted page number as calculated by Word; the one in the x of y format is the physical page number before Word tweaks it.

So, in a document in native Word 2010 or 2016 structure, with a Word Cover Page, the computation field Doug gave will give the wrong result. If the cover page is not one inserted by Word but is rather a typed page followed by a next-page section break that restarts numbering, it will work in every version of Word.

Even when you choose to delete your out-of-date security info, MS don't fully remove it for 30 days. This waiting period helps to deter hackers from updating your security info and gaining access to your account without your knowledge.
