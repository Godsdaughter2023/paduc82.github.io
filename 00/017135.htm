john korchok say:

In your original post, you only had this experience with one file, so it's plausible to assume that the one file contained the problem. Since this has started with a different file, it makes it more likely that your copy of PowerPoint is misconfigured. Below are the 2 repairs most likely to fix this issue. Start by Repairing Permissions:

  1. Close PowerPoint.
  2. In the Finder, open Applications>Utilities>Disk Utility
  3. Select your disk in the left column.
  4. Select the First Aid tab, then click on Repair Disk Permissions.
  5. After it finishes, restart PowerPoint and test.

If the problem still exists, try Deleting Prefs:

  1. Close PowerPoint.
  2. In the Finder, while holding down the Option key, click on Go>Library
  3. Open the Preferences folder.
  4. Drag com.microsoft.powerpoint.plist to the desktop.
  5. Restart PowerPoint and test. If it runs normally, trash the preferences file.

diane poremsky mvp say:

It wouldn't be in Rules but under Forwarding (in outlook on the web options).

Did you try powershell yet?
Get-InboxRule -Mailbox user-alias |fl Name,Identity,ForwardTo,ForwardAsAttachmentTo
Then use this cmdlet to delete the rule:
Remove-InboxRule -Mailbox *** Email address is removed for privacy *** -Identity "RuleName"

More information at Prevent Users from Forwarding Mail to Internet Addresses
