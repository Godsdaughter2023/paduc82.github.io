Issues with the Auto-Hide taskbar setting and Cortana/Search - Microsoft Community

jason d [msft] say:

Issues with the Auto-Hide taskbar setting and Cortana/Search

We have been getting reports that our users are having problems with the Cortana/Search program when the taskbar has been set to Auto-Hide.

These problems can surface in several ways:

  • Most of the Cortana/Search pane may appear off-screen

  • The Cortana/Search pane may stop appearing

  • The Search input box may stop accepting input

  • In Tablet mode, the Search input box may block the “All Apps” button.

The workaround for these issues is to disable the Auto-Hide setting on build 9926.

  1. Right-click on the taskbar and select properties

  2. On the Taskbar tab, uncheck “Auto-hide the taskbar”

How do I put a colored frame on a power point slide? - Microsoft Community

silviaruw say:

How do I put a colored frame on a power point slide?

How do I put a "colored frame" on a power point slide?
bob jones aka: cybertaz say:
Your brief request leaves a lot of room for interpretation :-) It isn't even clear whether you want to apply the "border" to only 1 slide or have it appear on all.

Adding to what Cody suggested...

If you go to the Home tab> Insert> Shape - Basic Shapes you'll find a Frame tool. Draw the frame then slide the yellow diamond left/right to adjust the weight of the frame. You can also format it in any number of ways to change the color, use a gradient, etc. Once it's formatted as you width use the Arrange command to Send to Back.

As an alternative -- IMO preferable to creating 2 shapes as Cody suggested -- use any of the Shape tools then format the Fill to None & format the Line to the weight/color/effect you prefer.

Another option: Once you draw the Shape, go into the Format dialog to check the box for Slide Background Fill in the Fill category.

cody c. say: Hey Silvia,

There's not a template or setting built into PPT that will automatically accomplish what you're looking for. However, it's pretty easy to manually create what you're looking for.

You can edit the Slide Master and use shapes to create your own colored frame. When you edit the Slide Master, the frame will appear on all slides in the presentation. 

To access the Slide Master, choose the View menu from the top of your screen, point to Master, and then click Slide Master.

Once you're there, you can add shapes by clicking the Shape icon on the ribbon:


I'd probably accomplish this by adding a square that encompassed the entire page. This shape would represent the frame. Then I'd add a slightly smaller white shape on top of the first square to give space for content. 

To learn more about inserting shapes in PowerPoint, have a look at the following link:
Add or change a shape

Hope this helps!
