andre da costa say:

What type of license is this?

Is it a retail license that came preinstalled on the computer or is it a store bought license?

If it came preinstalled on the computer it is an OEM license which means, you should have options to reinstall Windows 7 without needing a product key. A certificate of authenticity is normally provided with a new computer and the product key for your license can be found at the bottom (if its a laptop) or top or side if its a desktop.

-------------------------------

If its a retail license, the product key can be found in the packaging that came with your purchase of Windows 7, you can also find the product key in your Store Account if you bought it from the Microsoft Store;

Find your Product Key if your purchased from Microsoft Store:
http://store.microsoft.com/Help/FAQ?err=t2#at2

 

New computers that come preinstalled with Windows 7 often have what is called a recovery partition. This is used to reinstall the operating system in the event of a system crash. To access it, you will need to boot into when you start your computer by pressing a function key. This can be either F1, F2, F9, F10, F11, F12 or even DEL or Tab key. Consult the owners manual that came with your PC for instructions about how to reinstall Windows.

　

- Samsung uses ESC on most of its systems

- HP uses F9 on most of its systems

- Fujitsu uses F12 on most of its systems

- Acer uses F12 on most of its systems, but you often need to go into the BIOS to enable it

- Dell uses F12

　

In general, look at the screen during POST for a hint about what is possible.

　

If your recovery partition is not available or damaged, you should contact the manufacturer of your computer and request a recovery disc set you can use to reinstall Windows 7. They might charge a small shipping and handling fee.

I'm not quite sure what you mean.
Installing an Upgrade removes effectively all evidence that the previous OS existed. If the previous license was an OEM one, it remains valid for that machine, in the event that you wish to revert back to the original OS.
 
If the previous OS was a Retail one, it is subsumed into the Upgrade, and cannot be used either in another install on that machine, or on another machine, unless the Upgrade is removed (i.e. formatted), at which time the license is freed and available again.
