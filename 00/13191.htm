rob koch say:

No, this is a new feature of Internet Explorer 10, which is why you received the "welcome to your new browser" page.

 

Since the only time that the password is displayed is when you are currently typing it into a dialog box, just don't click the symbol if you don't wish to see it, it's that simple.

 

These characters were always available to any spy program on your PC, since only the screen display itself isn't displaying the characters, they are still being entered and collected in the background to be sent to the remote web site once you press the submit, login or whatever button they provide.  This was just a visual feature to avoid someone easily reading what you are typing from the screen itself.

 

There's only a security risk if you actually click on the symbol while someone else is standing there, so don't.

    If you want to just exclude a folder (and all its contents), then exclude that folder and that's that.  The drive letter is really just a special type of folder in a way - the primary folder for the drive.  If you exclude a drive letter, then you will exclude that drive (or more accurately, that partition) from scans (including real-time scans).  Remember that removable devices are assigned drive letters when attached, so while excluding an external drive may work by excluding that drive letter now, if removed and reattached later (perhaps with something else as well), then it may have a different drive letter.  The best way to avoid scanning removable drives is to either detach them before the scan or go to settings and set it not to scan removable drives.  If you have many and want to exclude one for a specific scan, then confirm that drive's current drive letter is the same as what you have excluded (or what you add to the exclusion list).

     

    Actually, exclusions should be done at the lowest level possible.  If you NEED to exclude a file (typically because it is reading as a false positive or causing scans to freeze), then just exclude the file.  Every exclusion is a hole in your security and so, the fewer the better and the least excluded, the better.

     

    I might be able to help more if you explain exactly how you are setup and exactly what you are trying to do (or not do).  Why do you want to exclude a folder or a drive (is it to save time or because of a problem with the scan)?  What type of scan are you running?  What operating system?

     

    I hope this helps.  If not, please explain your situation in as much detail as possible (the more I know, the better my response will be) including the above information and anything else you think may help and we'll go from there.

     

    Good luck!
