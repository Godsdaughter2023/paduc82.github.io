vincentchoy say:

Yammer Networks are corporate networks and they are thus "company"dependent. Meaning company A and company B have different Yammer networks The verification Yammer does on who can access the company network is their company email domain (assuming that you are with company A then you will get a company A email address). So a employee of company B should not have access to company A.

In situations where Company A, B and C belong to the same corporation, then Yammer allows multiple email domains to be registered within a Yammer network and will be recognized as belonging to the same corporation under one corporate Yammer network. (ie Company A, B and C will not be having their own Yammer networks).

You have to see if your current situation is similar, eg Public works and Telecommunications have independent Yammer networks.

If that is the case, Yammer has a Guest Access feature that allow people who do not belong to Company A to be admitted as guests. This requires Company A Yammer admin to do create an access for you.

Hi Dawn Scranton,

The solution would be -

Scenario A - Employee Resigns from Company A and moves to Company B

A Yammer network is a corporate network meant for Company A and its allowed partners (via external networks). As such once Employee is out of the employment of Company A, he can only be admitted if he retains a company A email account, or is allowed in as a Guest (this can be done via Yammer Admin Console).

Scenario B - Employee moves from Subsidiary A to Subsidiary B, both having the same parent company

If Subsidiary A and Subsidiary B maintain their own independent Yammer networks, the Scenario A applies.

The way that Subsidiary A and Subsidiary B employees can share the same Yammer network is when the Parent Company has One Single Yammer network where Subsidiary A and Subsidiary B employees are members of. This can be achieved via adding Subsidiary A and Subsidiary B email domains into the Parent Yammer Network. As far as I'm aware there is no such feature in Word (any version).

The closest I can suggest is to create a userform (http://gregmaxey.mvps.org/Create_and_employ_a_UserForm.htm) containing an image control, and a macro to display it. For each word or phrase that you
want to show a picture for, you then need to create a MacroButton field (http://www.word.mvps.org/FAQs/TblsFldsFms/UsingMacroButton.htm) that calls the macro. The macro has to be written so that it

separate files on disk.
