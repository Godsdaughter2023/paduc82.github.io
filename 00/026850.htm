chippy media say:

Much Sought Solution to "Custom Domains" that can't access Outlook.com (Browser AND App), or use the SMTP Server. (X-JIT-Error: ADDriverStoreAccessNonLocalException)

This solution is ONLY for people with ALL THREE of the following conditions:

  • Your email address DOES NOT END WITH @msn.com, @hotmail.com, @live.com, @outlook.com etc (or any other country code like @hotmail.ca). This is only for people who have their OWN custom owned domain like *** Email address is removed for privacy ***. (This point was added recently to add clarification, and was not included in the first version of the post.)
  • Custom Domain Email Addresses for Outlook Account Users (Not office 365) whose email cannot be accessed (Browser Inbox, Outlook App, SMTP Server), however all other Microsoft Services tied to this account work properly.
  • Users who had a custom domain from a historical service (Not Office 365), and then created a new Office365 account with the same Email Address and custom domain.

Problem: 

Duplicate Email Addresses exist to Microsoft on an internal level, albeit they are both from separate services (Free Outlook Service, and Premium Office 365 Service).


How this Problem Occurs:

Outlook.com used to offer support for custom domain management for free (which operates a custom domain email under the FREE Outlook service, not Office 365). New customers looking to manage custom domains must use Office 365, Microsoft's premium online service, which also includes enterprise-class email. If you previously used custom domains with Outlook.com, you may still log in to Outlook.com with your existing custom domain email address, but admins can no longer create new accounts in the domain. These accounts are still able to send and receive email, however, if you create a new email account with the same address/alias, under the same domain, with Office 365 [premium service], your previous CUSTOM DOMAIN Outlook.com email address inbox [historic free service] will no longer be accessible , and will display the error code below.


X-JIT-Error: ADDriverStoreAccessNonLocalException

Manual Solution:
You must change the email address of your Free Outlook Account. You can create a new "alias" that using a free, non custom domain to send and receive email from. Once you have added the new "alias" (new email address), you will be able to set that as your default alias as the new, non custom domain address. Once this is done, your Microsoft Outlook email services will available immediately. You will be able to access all the old emails, contacts, saved documents, etc that were in the old Outlook Account, under your new Free Alias (non custom domain).

To set a new alias, visit Manage your account aliases

(Microsoft Guide Here)

Microsoft Must Fix: 

Microsoft should prompt such users to create a new alias for their free Outlook account automatically, instead of seeing the error below. Microsoft has in the past used an automated way to detect when DNS is no longer working with a free custom domain email service. When this happened you would be promoted to either fix the DNS, or change the alias of the account. This would prevent the dead end error code below.
