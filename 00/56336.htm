Hi,

Could be drivers, could be the program which is playing the sound, could be the audio codec,
could be some other program that is running in the background, and it could be hardware. And
to add even more complexity it could be the interaction of two or more of those. I have even
seen another hardware item that when installed caused similar issues and that hardware had
nothing directly to do with the sound.

What program is it that triggers the crackling? Have you checked it settings for any sound
shaping functions which might be the cause? Have you tried another program? If codec are
involved have you tried updating them.

When you troubleshoot issues such as these it is important to keep an open mind as there
is nothing yet that is pointed to as the sole direct cause.
