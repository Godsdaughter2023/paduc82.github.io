suzanne s. barnhill say:

Office 2007 (which is Office 12) can save as PDF but only if you have the Save As PDF add-in installed.  The history on that is that the original plan was to ship Office 2007 with native PDF authoring capability. At the very last minute, there was resistance from Adobe, and Microsoft had to offer the feature as an add-in. The add-in was free but had to be downloaded from http://www.microsoft.com/en-us/download/details.aspx?id=9943 or http://www.microsoft.com/en-us/download/details.aspx?id=7.

I believe the add-in was eventually incorporated in a Service Pack, so if your copy of Office is patched up to date, you should have it. The fact that you have Save As PDF as an option is a pretty good indication that you do, but I suppose you could try downloading the add-in and trying to install it.

A better solution, however, would probably be to repair the installation. Go to Office Button | Word Options | Resources and click on Diagnose. Office will find and repair any problems.
