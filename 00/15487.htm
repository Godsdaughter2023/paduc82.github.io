john mcghie say:

Hi Whatever Your Name is :-)

This can get VERY convoluted.  I would maintain that it's a bug in the design of the mechanism, Microsoft would claim that it's working the way they designed it!

The issue is "The Lawyers" yet again.  In this case, it's lawyers' requirement to be able to produce text in which the numbering and the formatting are independent.  In a legal document, the numbering expresses the logical structure of the text, the formatting may create a different structure unrelated.

Anyway...  To do this, outline numbering can be applied three ways: as Direct (List) Formatting, using a List Style, or using Paragraph styles.

Similarly, the Tab command can toggle between inserting a tab character, or functioning as a Promote key, or an Indent key.  In the same way as the Indent/Outdent buttons on the Ribbon can switch to Promote/Demote.  This change is governed by whether list formatting is detected in the text.

Without using VBA it is not possible to see whether a given paragraph is a member of a list or not, other than by hitting Tab to see what happens.

But if there IS a list applied to the text when you hit Tab, the tab key will turn into a Demote button. This will change the outline level of the paragraph: and when that happens the appropriate Heading style and numbering will be applied if you are in fact in a Heading list.

If you create List Styles, and define a paragraph style to each level, you will then be able to "predict" he behaviour: if you have a list style applied, and you change the outline level of a paragraph, Word will bring in the style defined to that level of the outline.

You and I will still get very confused, but at least we can now understand that we are supposed to be confused because they designed this mechanism for Lawyers...

Hope this has has helped, even though I suspect it has just added to the confusion :-)

ynnhoj gnahz say:

Hi Ian,

Yes, if your Office 365 account is configured automatically (Exchange protocol) in Outlook, deleting the emails in the client will remove the email from Office 365 server, which means it will delete the email in your main mac.

As a workaround, please add your Office 365 account in your MacBook as POP account. Go to Preferences -- Account, click Add new, then type your address and then choose not Office 365 on the top. Select POP, incoming server is Outlook.Office365.com, outgoing is smtp.office365.com.

After it is added, Outlook will download a copy to your computer. Click Advanced in Accounts, set Delete copies from the server to Never.
