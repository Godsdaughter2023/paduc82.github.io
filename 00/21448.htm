Pasting jumps to next column - Microsoft Community

solkeys say:

Pasting jumps to next column

I am trying to paste something in column D and in pastes in column E instead. Why and how do I correct it?
jeeped say:

Your description was a little vague regarding 'something' but this behavior is usually caused by trying to paste where the first character is a tab. The tab is being interpreted by Excel as a delimiter so pasting <tab>123 into D1 will put 123 into E1.

This occurs because the Text-to-Columns default delimiter is a <tab>. If you absolutely have to paste in data that uses a <tab> as a prefix then you will have to reset the Text-to-Columns default delimiter to nothing.

Select any cell with something in it and choose Data, Text to Columns. Choose Delimited from the first screen and click Next. Clear all of the check boxes from the second screen and click Finish in the lower right. Excel will not use the <tab> character as a delimiter after you have done this. You can paste your <tab> prefixed data into cells without having them shift right.

Caution: tabs don't belong in Excel data. Check your data after pasting to make sure there are no rogue characters prefixing or suffixing them.

Alternately, you can observe the GIGO principle and fix the data before pasting it or paste it into column C so it end up in column D.

myall_blues say: What you have run into is what is commonly known as the 'floating point error' where small errors in the way Excel handles numbers result in apparent errors in calculations. This is not restricted to Excel but is a function of how computers store floating point numbers.
If you click on the cell containing -0, then click in the formula bar and press F9, you will find that the actual number stored in that cell is -3.41060513164848E-12, in other words a very small negative number close to but not exactly zero.
See here for more information:
https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/help/214118...
https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/microsoft-365/b...

To fix it, change the formula in the -0 cell (which I assume is =SUM(cell1:cell2) to
=ROUND(SUM(cell1:cell2),2)
