If you create put the following macro in the Normal.dotm template or in another template that is saved in the Word Startup folder, it will do what you want:
 
Sub FileOpen()
    Dim fd As FileDialog
    Dim LogoFile As String
    Set fd = Application.FileDialog(msoFileDialogFilePicker)
    fd.Title = "Select the file that you want to open"
    fd.InitialFileName = "C:\Test\" 'Change to your desired folder
    fd.Filters.Add "All files", "*.*", 1
    With fd
        If ..Show = -1 Then
            Documents.Open ..SelectedItems(1)
        End If
    End With
    Set fd = Nothing
End Sub

Word 2016 added a bug related to image resizing but not only; also general responsiveness become much more luggish.

john - project say:
blawson1999,
In Project milestones are a special type of task. By definition they have zero duration. It is not unusual for one or more milestones to be subtasks under a summary line and the completion or non-completion of those milestones can give interesting results when looking at the summary line metrics. For example, Project calculates the summary line percent complete value using the following formula:
% complete [summary line]=(sum of subtask Actual durations)/(sum of subtask Durations)*100%

Now throw a milestone into the group of subtasks. Give all performance subtasks a completion of 100% but not on the milestone. You will see that the above formula doesn't quite hold since even though the formula says the summary line will be 100%, the zero duration milestone still must be shown as complete. What that actually says is the formula has an additional criteria for milestones. The summary line will only be shown as 100% complete IF the above formula is 100% AND all milestones are also 100%.

As I recall there is also a condition wherein all subtasks are shown as 100% complete but the summary line still shows less than 100% complete. Unfortunately I can't remember exactly what that condition is. Maybe somebody else will jump in and help me out with that one.

How all this impacts the Status and Status Indicator fields I don't know. I haven't worked with them enough to give you a synopsis. But, based on the above info about milestones I thought there might be a connection to what you were seeing.

Hope this helps.

John
