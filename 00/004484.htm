sam with a plan say:

Fixed it!

I took a leap of faith thinking that if I uninstalled and then reinstalled My Phone on my device, then synced, that it would restore my contacts back to my device.  It worked.  However, it was a shot in the dark as there is no way of telling which way it was going to sync, i.e "from device to online" or "from online to device".  Had it been the former, my contacts would have been erased from the online My Phone account and gone forever.  Phew!

Maybe a little future suggestion for MS:  My problem (and other people's problem, I'm guessing) could have been avoided if MS would make it abundantly clear which direction things are being synced before the device automatically does so.  A handy message showing the summary of what is going to happen during the sync process would be awesome...like how many contacts will be deleted/added if the sync is allowed to occur.

Sam.

j meister mixmaster 7 say: That's very good to know.  I've been thinking about restoring my factory settings due to some software problems I've been encountering, but I don't want to lose all my information, especially contacts.  Did you have to send some sort of a link from MyPhone to your phone to initiate the sync, or did you do it all from the program on your phone?

sam with a plan say:

Before Windows Mobile Device Center deleted my contacts, I had them all backed up in the MyPhone service.  Here was the process I used to restore them:

  1. First, since I wasn't sure what was going to happen, I archived all my contacts in the online MyPhone service, except for one contact (a "dummy" contact).
  2. Then I selected "Stop Service" in the MyPhone app on my device.
  3. Next, I re-launched the MyPhone app on my device and had to go through the setup process again (i.e. enter Live account, password, blah blah).
  4. With setup completed, I simply chose to manually sync my contacts in the MyPhone app on my device.  This is where I wasn't sure what was going to happen.  What was going to be the direction of syncing..."device to MyPhone account" or "MyPhone account to device"???
  5. After it finished syncing, the "dummy" contact had been restored to my device!
  6. Now that I was confident in the restore method, I un-archived all my contacts in the online MyPhone service, then selected sync on my device.  Voila! All my contacts had been restored.

So, no link was needed to initiate the sync.  I just had to manually stop and restart the service on my device.

Microsoft should really address #4 above in all of its syncing processes...let the user know the direction of syncing and what will result from said syncing...before it actually occurs.

Just my $.02

Hello,

Vlookup only ever returns one result, not many for a sum. But you can do this with a SUMIF function.

=SUMIF(Sheet2!$A$1:$E$4,A1,Sheet2!$B$1:$F$4)
