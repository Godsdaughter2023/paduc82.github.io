It is not true that you cannot add the styles dropdown to the QAT (Quick Access Toolbar) .
You will find the Styles dropdown in the All Commands group - called "Styles..."
You can also make use of the QuickStyles group on the ribbon - though I guess you will prefer the former.
You could also use the Styles Task Pane CTRL+ALT+SHIFT+S
or the Apply Styles task pane - CTRL+SHIFT+S both of which can be detached from the side of the Word Window to behave like floating toolbars either together or separately.
Modifying the ribbon is not that difficult - but if you don't see the incentive to do so, in time saved in future, then I am not going to persuade you of the benefits of doing so. manta---01 say: Phone models are different, some phones support multiple "modes" to connect to PC via cable.
Your 6230i supports 2 modes: PC Suite mode and so called mass storage mode.

With PC Suite mode you should be able to just unplug the cable from phone or PC and the connection get's shut down and you can reconnect when ever you like without a need to restart PC.

The mass storage mode is different, it is same like a USB memory stck or similar devices, you should get an icon to windows tray area (bottom right in your PC screen normally) and by right clicking that icon you can "stop" the connection to your phone's memorycard and then remove the cable connection. That is the recommended way, but it is required only with mass storage mode.

In 6230i, every time you connect the cable there should come a pop up question in your phone screen about the mode, something like "do you allow data transfer?". If you answer YES = you are connected in mass storage mode

if you answer "no"(or reject or similar), then the connection will load PC Suite USB drivers and you are able to use Pc Suite features. And then you can disconnect cable without any "stopping".
