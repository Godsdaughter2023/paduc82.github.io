dan at it associates say:

That's the critical info.

 

It tells us that you purchased your Office 2010 license as a direct purchase and downlaod from Microsoft, which makes it a full retail license, and full retail licenses are permitted to be moved from computer to computer, as you want to do.

 

So the answer to your question is Yes, you are permitted to uninstall it from the old computer then install it on your new computer.

 

Your full retail license for Office 2010 allows you to concurrently activate it on two of your computers, your "Main" computer and your "portable" computer.  If you happen to have Office Home and Student 2010, that license allows you to concurrently activate it on up to three computers if they are all used by members of your household (but their usage is not permitted for commercial, nonprofit, or other revenue generating activity).

 

You mentioned you are selling your old computer. In order to help protect your Office license from being misused by the new owner, and to help obscure the personal information that you have entered into your computer as you used it over the last few years, at a bare minimum you should run the computer manufacturer's factory recovery program to return the computer to "out-of-the-box" condition.  If you don't know what that is, visit the support pages at your computer manufacturer's support site for your exact model to learn how to do a factory recovery.

 

If this is a "clone" or "whitebox" computer (assembled by a local assember and not a big-name company), use a disk-wiping program (search for DBan boot and nuke) to erase the contents of the hard disk drive.

 

When selling be sure to give the new owner all of the discs and materials that came with the computer.
