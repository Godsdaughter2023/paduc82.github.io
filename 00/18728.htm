Hi Simon,

Based on my test about creating a Preservation Policy on the Security and Compliance Admin Center portal, you need decide whether you want to turn on the Preservation Lock or not. Based on your screenshot, the policy Is not on Preservation Lock, therefore, this policy can be deleted. In my test, I waited for about 5 minutes and then closed/refreshed Security & Compliance Admin site. Now that policy which had  (Deleting…) status before is removed from the list.

To check if the policy is on Preservation lock, go to Security & Compliance Admin > Data Governance > Retention > D. Click the Policy > Preservation Lock. Notice that both Preservation Lock and Status Tabs cannot be modified. After a policy’s been locked, no one— including the administrator—can turn off, disable, or remove content from the policy. And it’s not possible to modify or delete content that’s subject to the policy during the preservation period. After the policy’s been locked, the only ways you can modify the preservation policy are adding content to it or extending its duration. A locked policy can be increased or extended, but it can’t be reduced, disabled, or turned off.

As your policy is not on “Preservation Lock”, please check if you can remove the policy by connecting to Office 365 Compliance Center Powershell, and running the cmdlet:

1.) Get-RetentionCompliancePolicy

- This is to check the Enabled and Mode status of all the policies.

2.) Set-RetentionCompliancePolicy "All Public Folders" -Enabled:$false

- This is to turn-off the policy.

3.) Remove-RetentionCompliancePolicy "All Public Folder"

- This is to remove or delete the policy.

Now you need to wait for some time and check again if the policy is deleted.

About creating a “New” preservation policy, you can run the cmdlet:

New-RetentionCompliancePolicy “All Public Folders” –Enabled:$true -PublicFolderLocation <public folder path> –RestrictiveRetention:$false

Hi Troy,

I have checked the file you provided, I find the  Stacked Line with Markers type is applied to the charts.

Please note that a stacked line chart is a line chart in which lines do not overlap because they are cumulative at each point. The stacked line chart displays series as a set of points connected by a line. Values are represented on the y-axis and categories are displayed on the x-axis. When the second line is rendered, its values will be added to the points of the previous line. Therefore, each point going upwards on the chart is a cumulative sum of the values at that point.  For your scenario, the first line start at 3.4, the second line should start at 3.4+16.5=19.9, the third line should start at 3.4+16.5+20.6=40.5. If you limit the y-axis to 80, some parts of the second and third line whose values are above 80 will not show up.
