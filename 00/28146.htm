Combining Word documents can be a challenge in any version of Word. I do not have a Mac and am writing from my experiences with the Windows version. 1. Work with copies!
2. Create a new document and copy the content from your first document, or open the copy you want to be at the beginning of your combined document.
3. Go to the end of the new document and insert a next-page section break.
4. Copy the content of the next document and paste after the section break.
5. Repeat steps 3 and 4 as needed.

To delete all section breaks in a document use the Replace function and replace ^b with ^p.

To delete all manual page breaks in a document, use the Replace function and replace ^m with ^p.

Every document must contain one section break, it at the last paragraph mark in the document.

brad c. [msft] say:

Hi everyone-

This message appears only in the free edition of OneNote 2013.  We made OneNote 2013 free last year for use with notebooks on OneDrive.  The free edition will still show your local notebooks created in previous versions of OneNote, but with this prompt to help you move them to OneDrive, where you can access them from any device.

If you're seeing this message, you have a couple of options:

1) Click the message and follow the prompts to move the notebook to OneDrive.  This is the recommended option as your notebook will be backed up and available on your other devices.  Notebooks on OneDrive are private, secure, and protected against PC failure.  For sensitive content, you can password protect sections, which prevents others (and even Microsoft) from accessing the content without the password.

2) If you need to keep the notebook local, you can purchase a paid edition of OneNote via Office 365.  If you're already an Office 365 subscriber, make sure you've installed one of your available desktop licenses on this PC.  For educational use, check with your school to see if a free or discounted Office 365 subscription is available.

Hope that clears things up!

One of the major rules for SUMPRODUCT() is, it can only return numbers. If your column contains "text" just remove it. It will display the result correctly 31.

By the way if you really want to keep that text, then for error handling you can use the below array formula. After pasting this formula press Ctrl+Shift+Enter to get the result 31.

=SUMPRODUCT((B3:B24="Apple")*(A3:A24>TODAY()-10)*(C3:C24="ABC")*($D$3:$D$24<>"="""))*(D3:D24)
