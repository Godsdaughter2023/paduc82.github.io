gary's student say:

Say Sheet1 has employees in col A and years of service in col B.  For example:

Larry 3
Moe 1
Curly 7
Shepp 5

 

Run the following macro:

Sub Allocater()
Dim s1 As Worksheet, s2 As Worksheet, k As Integer
Set s1 = Sheets("Sheet1")
Set s2 = Sheets("Sheet2")
k = 1
s1.Activate
n = Cells(Rows.Count, "A").End(xlUp).Row
For i = 1 To n
    nm = Cells(i, 1).Value
    cnt = Cells(i, 2).Value
    For j = 1 To cnt
        s2.Cells(k, 1).Value = nm
        s2.Cells(k, 2) = k
        k = k + 1
    Next
Next
End Sub

Sheet2 will display:

Larry 1
Larry 2
Larry 3
Moe 4
Curly 5
Curly 6
Curly 7
Curly 8
Curly 9
Curly 10
Curly 11
Shepp 12
Shepp 13
Shepp 14
Shepp 15
Shepp 16

So Larry will have three tickets numbered 1 thru 3

Moe will have ticket number 4, etc.

 

To pick a winner:

=RANDBETWEEN(1,SUM(Sheet1!B:B)) for the winning ticket number

neo wu msft say:

Hi Rodney,

 

Thanks for your posting.

 

The sites will be locked and have the home page removed while the process wait to confirm that the Group is cleaned up. The site will typically be removed anywhere from 5 min to a few hours.

If 24 hours have passed then the site will be orphaned. Based on the lock state, a 404 would be expected if the Group was deleted and site locked.

 

That's the reason why deleted site not disappeared from SharePoint homepage immediately.

 

Here is a workaround:

  1. Run Set-SPOSite on your site to set the lockstate to "unlock". ( set-sposite -identity "site URL" -lockstate "unlock")
  2. Run Remove-SPOsite to delete the site.

 

For your reference: Connect to SharePoint Online PowerShell
