If you'll always be entering the 1st of the month date in A2, enter this formula in A3 and copy down to A3:

=EDATE(A2,1)

Format in the Date format of your choice.

Note that the EDATE function requires the Analysis ToolPak add-in be
installed if you're using a version of Excel prior to Excel 2007. If you
enter the formula and get a #NAME? error look in Excel help for the EDATE
function. It'll tell you how to fix the problem.

a. user say:

Hello,

 

Once you’ve created a new Outlook.com account, there's no way to rename your Hotmail account with that name. If you delete the new account, it will take 365 calendar days for that account name to become available again, and at that point, anyone would be able to get that name.

 

Instead, you can automatically forward your Hotmail messages to your new Outlook.com account. You’ll have all of your email in one place, and can reply to it using your new address. To auto-forward your mail:

 

1.     Sign in to your Hotmail account.

2.     Click Options, and then click More options.

3.     Under Managing your account, click Email forwarding. You might have to sign in again.

4.     Select Forward your mail to another email account.

5.     Enter the email address for your new Outlook.com account.

6.     If you want, select the Keep a copy of forwarded messages in your Hotmail inbox check box.

7.     Click Save.

 

To know more about Alias and renaming accounts, please visit this article.
