Hiding "Default Paragraph Font" and "Normal" - Microsoft Community

rhetoric101 say:

Hiding "Default Paragraph Font" and "Normal" Styles?

In Word 2010, is it possible for me to hide the styles "Normal" and "Default Paragraph Font" in the Styles pane?

I don't see either of these styles listed in the Formatting Restrictions list.

pamcaswell say: It all has to do with the styles hierarchy--what supersedes what. " The general hierarchy of style types is [from lowest to highest priority]:
    • Document Defaults
    • Table Styles
    • Paragraph Styles | Paragraph Part of Linked Styles
    • Numbering Styles
    • Character Style | Character Part of Linked Styles
    • Direct Formatting"
    Quoted from Order of Operations, a Word Team Blog.

So, table styles supersede document defaults and paragraph styles supersede table styles.  For the  special case of the normal paragraph style: When the normal paragraph style is not changed from the default settings, its settings are effectively  the document defaults, which table styles override.  But when normal has different settings from the defaults, its paragraph style settings override the table style.  


Insertions, whether by pasting, inserting, or typing, take on the formatting, including the style, in effect at the cursor.  Tables are no different.  As you found,  insertion of a table into a heading 3 paragraph results in all the cells having the heading 3 style.  To get back to the table style settings,  select the table and clear it by clicking the Clear Formatting button on the Home tab.

Using table styles to format table text only works reliably in W2007-13.  Because we can't change the system defaults in W2003, normal and the defaults only match if the normal paragraph style settings are TNR 12, no paragraph spacing, and single line spacing, and automatic font color.  And when they don't match, the table text will be formatted in the normal paragraph style (overriding the table style settings).  To avoid this, do not make any font settings in the table style; use paragraph style to format table text instead.

Pam

Hi there --

here's the official answer from the Zune team.

Your Zune account has a country (go to “account” on zune.net to see which country). This cannot be changed. Your PC settings have to match your Zune account settings – and that will dictate which Marketplace you get. If a user wants the German Marketplace, his Zune account and PC settings must say “Germany.” The Live ID country (which may or may not be the same as the Zune country) needs to match the credit card country in order to make purchases.

So, basically, in order to purchase from the Zune Marketplace, all of your settings - Zune account, Live ID, PC, and credit card - need to be from the same country.

We understand people move countries sometimes. Unfortunately, we have licensing restrictions that cause us to, at this time, not allow users to change their Zune country. If you find yourself in the situation where your Live ID has a different country from your Zune account, if you can't switch your Live ID to the same country as your Zune account, you'll have to create a new Zune account with the correct country.

The team understands this may be painful for some, and we're thinking about how to improve this process in the future.
