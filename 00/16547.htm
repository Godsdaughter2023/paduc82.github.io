We found that the Excel insert worksheet was from a very old Excel template.  We found a workaround that fixes the issue permanently and this only affects two users.  When a user comes across one of these Word docs with the Excel insert, they click in the upper left corner of the old current worksheet and Ctrl-C to "copy all".  Then they insert a new updated Excel sheet into the Word doc and Ctrl-V to "paste".  This keeps all of the formatting in place and they only have to adjust a couple of lines down and from the left to make it the same size as the old one.  Very easy process.

jay freedman say:

No, you can't delete the braces (they aren't really characters in the internal representation of the file; they're just for display).

What you can do is this:

  • Display the field code. Turn on nonprinting characters (click the ¶ button on the Home tab).
  • Click to the left of the first real character inside the field code.

  • Use the vertical scroll bar and/or the scroll wheel (if your mouse has one) to bring the end of the document into view -- DO NOT use the arrow keys or the Page Up/Down keys, which move the cursor.
  • Hold the Shift key while you click to the right of the last real character in the field code. That selects all of the code but not the braces.

  • Cut the selected text to the clipboard.
  • Delete the field (which is now just two braces with a couple of spaces inside them).
  • Paste from the clipboard.
