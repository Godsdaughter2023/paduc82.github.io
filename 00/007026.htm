brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:
IMAP accounts don't use their PSTs as the primary storage.  They're cached copies of the messages that are kept on the server.  You do not restore IMAP accounts by reusing their PSTs.  In fact you cannot reuse an IMAP PST.  You simply re-add the account and allow Outlook to create a new PST, populating it from the master data on the IMAP server.  There's no way you can have messages in an IMAP PST that are not on the server because any change you make in IMAP gets reflected both in the PST cache and on the server.  If you do have messages in the PST that are not on the server, it must be because you created your own PST independent of the IMAP PST and moved or copied messages from the IMAP account to that PST.  If that's the case, put the PST in some folder other than the folder where Outlook stores the IMAP PSTs and then add those PSTs to your mail profile with File>Open>Outlook Data File.
