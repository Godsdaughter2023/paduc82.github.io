Setting Up A Child Account - Guide For Parents

Summary
The windowsphone OS is linked to Xbox. Xbox has some strict parental controls in place, and that applies to windowsphone as well. Thanks to this, parents who buy windowsphone to their children will be unable to set up the phones. This short article explains what needs to be done to get things done.
Details

This short guide is meant for parents who are setting up a windowsphone for their under age - less than 18 years - kids. This article is more of a succinct summary. You will find in depth information at the following link - My Family FAQ


Before You Start


The parent must have a Microsoft Account. This could be a @live.com, @msn.com or @outlook.com. If the parent does not have one, they can create one by visiting outlook.com. Please note. If the parent is creating a new account, they will need to send at least one email to anyone before they can set up the child account. This email can be sent to anybody, even to themselves.


If you already tried to setup the phone without the child account properly set up, then you need to do a phone reset. Do this by going to settings > about > reset. When the reset is complete, start doing the things that are described below.


Setting Up Child's Account


  • Visit outlook.com. Create an account for your child.
  • Once her account is created, send an email from her account to yours. Some kind of verification thing will pop up. Do this first. Then, check if the email from her account has reached yours.
  • Now, visit xbox.com. Login using her account. You should get a prompt to verify from a adult account. Continue with that and you will be prompted to enter your details. This is where you get to authorize her account.
  • Once the Xbox authorization is completed, come to the phone.
  • Turn on the phone. It should prompt you for Microsoft account details of your child. IT will go through without a hitch.

cortig say:
Every time Outlook needs to check for e-mails on your account, it needs
to access the Keychain where the password is stored to use it to
connect to the Mail server.
If you choose Deny, it doesn't get the password and cannot check for e-mail.
It will ask you again the next time it needs to check for e-mails obviously.
 
You can either not use the keychain at all and re-enter the password
every time Outlook checks the e-mails (in which case you should delete
the entry for your account in /Applications/Utilities/Keychain Access),
or allow Outlook to access the keychain for auto-checking.
 
You probably got that prompt after an update: The keychain sees the
update as a new application and wants to confirm that Outlook is
allowed to use the password for your e-mails.
