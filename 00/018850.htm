jay freedman say:
Hi Samir,

Yes, you can fix the equation without retyping it.

First let's take the question of squaring the last expression. Instead of putting the cursor after the closing parenthesis and then choosing the superscript, select the expression (Δy) and then choose the superscript. You'll then get just the superscript box, and you can type the 2 in it.

An alternative method is to put the cursor after the closing parenthesis and type the characters  ^2 and then press the space bar. The ^ character is a code saying that the next item will be superscript, and the space tells the editor that you've completed the expression that will be superscripted. This is what the editor calls "linear" as opposed to "professional" display.

For the radical, it's easiest to convert the equation to linear display, edit it, and then change back to professional display. Click the down-arrow on the right side of the equation box and choose Linear in the menu. You'll see this:

d = √((Δx)^2 ) + (Δy)^2

Delete the closing parenthesis after the first ^2 and insert a closing parenthesis after the second ^2, giving you this:

d = √((Δx)^2 + (Δy)^2)

Click the down-arrow of the equation box again and choose Professional. Your equation will look as you want it.

found the answer!! say:

As explained in  http://support.microsoft.com/kb/897290 the POSTNET barcode that Word can create is now obsolete, so it was removed from the Envelope dialog starting in Word 2007.

If you really want to put in a POSTNET barcode, you can go to Insert > Quick Parts > Field and insert a BarCode field (which is how the option in the Envelopes dialog used to do its work). The syntax
of the field code is explained here:
http://office.microsoft.com/en-in/word-help/field-codes-barcode-field-HP005186205.aspx

Jay Freedman
MS Word MVP  FAQ: http://word.mvps.org


Hello everyone!   I too was very frustrated by this.  When I used the "add field" under the quick parts menu in Word 2010 and added "barcode", I would always get "Invalid Zip" as the response.  After some investigating, I found the fix:  if you go to the USPS website at https://ribbs.usps.gov/onecodesolution/download.cfm and download uspsEncoderMsOffice-1.3.0.zip, you can install it in seconds.

 

Once you install, all you have to do is the following: I went back to the "mailings" menu, selected "envelopes", then went to the address field and clicked "insert", then clicked "quick parts", selected "bar code" and clicked "is US address" and entered the zip code.  This worked perfectly for me in Word 2010. 

 

Hope this helps!!

Try this

Sub insertrows()
MyColumn = "A"
For x = Cells(Rows.Count, MyColumn).End(xlUp).Row To 1 Step -1
       Rows(x).Copy
       Rows(x).Resize(11).Insert
Next x
End Sub
