    Sleep mode is a quick way to power down your computer without losing your work, and without a lengthy restart procedure the next time you need to access your applications. Every now and then, however, a computer may have issues waking up from sleep mode. There are several solutions you can try to resolve PC sleep problems.

    Restart

    First restart the computer to see if the issue persists in a normal startup sequence. Sometimes computers can lag after waking due to confusion with various software; a quick restart is often all it takes to resolve the problem. If a restart does not resolve the issue, you may be experiencing more complicated problems.

    Sleep Test

    Often freezes after sleep are caused by memory-intensive programs that are unable to resume their operations upon waking. A simple way to test for this problem is turning on your computer, letting it load all of its background processes, and then putting it to sleep without opening any other applications. If the computer wakes without issue, the freezing issue could be caused by a specific application.

    Malicious Software

    Malicious software can often slow system performance to a crawl, especially upon startup or waking. Run a deep system scan with a malware, virus and spyware detector to ensure your computer has not picked up an errant infection. Removing harmful software from the computer could resolve not only the sleep and wake issue, but other performance issues as well.

    Frozen Applications

    It is entirely possible that one program is frozen, causing the rest of your applications to experience trouble upon waking. To remedy this situation, press “Ctrl-Shift-Esc” to open the Task Manager, click "More details," select the "Processes" tab, look through the list of running applications and processes, and then close anything marked with “Not Responding.”

    You need to clear the Printer Spooler cache.
    To do so, you need to follow the steps mentioned below –

     

    a) Click “Start” and type “services.msc” (without the quotations) and hit “Enter”.
    b) Double Click “Print Spooler” in the services list.
    c) Click “Stop” and then “Ok”.
    d) Click the “Start” button (icon) and copy and paste the path mentioned below –


    %WINDIR%\system32\spool\printers

     

    e) Now delete everything in the folder that comes up.
    f) Now perform step a) once again and Right Click on “Print Spooler” and select “Properties”.
    g) On the “General Tab”, make sure that the “Startup type” is set to “Automatic”.
    h) Click on “Apply” and “Ok”.
    i) Restart your computer and check.
