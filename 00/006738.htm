The trouble with VBA subs is that they are generally unaware of changes to the worksheet

So when you extent your data, the sub still looks at the old range

You could use a WorkSheet_Change sub but this would seem to be overkill

A VBA user-defined-function can be made to respond to a worksheet change

THe formual in G2 of the image is =mytest()

The code foe this formula is

Function mytest()
Application.Volatile
  LastRow = Cells(Rows.Count, "C").End(xlUp).Row
  temp = Application.Sum(Range(Cells(2, "C"), Cells(LastRow, "E")))
  mytest = temp / Cells(LastRow, "E")
End Function

If the data is extended down, the formula is aware of the change

You indicated that you installed activesync and I assume you are using a USB cable to connect your device to the PC.  When you plug in the device to the PC, does the PC make a sound indicating that a device was connected?
