rich michaels - mvp (greatcirclelearning.com) say:
Hi Jaracic,

Back in the binary format days (doc & ppt) you could copy/paste PowerPoint images and not have issues. However, now with docx and pptx formats it really has changed. I found quality problems with the transferred image, dropped pixels, and even total failures of the copy/paste function itself. 

And BTW, these are not just Mac issues; my initial problem findings in 2011 & 2012 were PC based. I had two major US companies asking me for help with the image quality they were getting from PowerPoint slide images inserted into Word documents.

Apps I write now that do this job stay off the clip board and instead rely on creating image files from the source PPT file and inserting those images programmatically into Word.

How can you do this yourself and not have to write your own app?

PowerPoint and Word for that manner have a built in function to Save as Picture. If you do this and save the Slide as an image file you can then insert the image file into a Word document and I'm sure you'll be much happier with the outcome.

Of course if you want to save yourself some time you could look at a Word 2011 plug-in app from my company that does this. It's called zAPPs-doc-Tools.

However, my bottom line advice is... Whether you do it manually or with a productivity app to help you, for this type of image work, stay off the clip board.
