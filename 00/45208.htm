How do I get rid of the Black Spaces on the sides of video pictures in - Microsoft Community

carolesl say:

How do I get rid of the Black Spaces on the sides of video pictures in Live Movie Maker?

Most of my pictures but not all have large black spaces on the sides. Is there any way to eliminate those spaces? I am using Windows 7- Windows Live Movie Maker.
considerate_guy say:

The answer has to do with aspect ratios.  Most cameras take pictures in a nearly square aspect ratio (e.g. 4 units wide 3 units high).  If you are creating a 4:3 project (check on the Project tab), you should see them fit perfectly without any black bars.  The exception would then be if you had pictures taken vertically (in a portrait orientation).  In order to fit the picture into the 4:3 rectangle of your project, Movie Maker has to add black bars.  It's not really adding bars, it's just putting your picture on a black background.  Whatever isn't covered by a picture, is black.


This gets worse if you are making a widescreen (16:9) aspect ratio project.  Here you are going to have space left over even if your picture is horizontal (landscape orientation).  That's because the rectangle for your movie is wider than your picture.  And here if you add a vertical picture you see even more black.


Beyond changing the aspect ratio of your project to 4:3, there isn't much more you can do within Movie Maker.  The next option would be to use your favorite image editor and crop your pictures so they are exactly the same ratio as your movie.  So if that's 16:9, you'd need to trim all your photos to that ratio.  That means even vertical photos will have to be cropped so they are wider than they are tall, which is probably not what you want.


So the short answer, Movie Maker does this so it can show all of your picture without cropping.  You can adjust the aspect ratio of your project (Project tab) to be closer to the aspect ratio of your pictures, but after that, you only other choice is to manually crop your photos outside Movie Maker.


P.S.  One thing you can do to make the black bars less noticeable is to add some panning and zooming animations (Animation tab).  It may not eliminate the bars, but since the picture is zoomed in and moving you may not notice the black as much.


P.P.S.  One good option for cropping is to do it with Photo Gallery.  Double-click on a photo to bring up the Edit tab, find Crop in the ribbon area (part of the Adjustments tools).  Below the button you'll see a down-arrow.  Click that and choose 'Proportion'.  Depending on your project settings, choose either 16 x 9 (widescreen) or 4 x 3 (standard).
