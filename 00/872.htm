vincentchoy say:

There is currently no way to "backup for the purpose of restore" a Yammer network, regardless whether you are basic or enterprise.

You can do a data export once you have upgraded to Yammer Enterprise. However the export is essentially a dump of the contents of your Yammer data (not a backup file), split into conversations, users, files...etc. There is no function for you to bulk load this dump back into a new or alternative Yammer network.

Your fear of activation is generally unfounded. Yammer is provided for every Office365 bundled plan sold by Microsoft (Business, Business Premium, E1, E3,E4, E5, Education). You will not be the first person to upgrade a Yammer Free account to Enterprise, nor will you be the last.

Whether that upgrade is safe, or fraught with risk, all you have to do is to spend some time surfing he net. You can search this community, you can use google and search the web in general. See how many complaints there are of mess ups, data losses..etc. I doubt you will find many. To compare, try searching "One Drive for business sync issues" and see how many you find.

The one possible problem that you would encounter, and this sometimes crop up in  this community, is that your global admin for Office365 doesn't end up as a Verified Admin in your Yammer Enterprise network. That can easily be solved with raising a support ticket.

So I say, go ahead, just do it, and enjoy your Yammer Enterprise

Ok, first note that I have edited my answer because I have some new information from my last discussion with the Microsoft support.

There is huge mistake not to do for managing to upgrade your Yammer basic plan over to Office 365.

That huge mistake is to not activate Yammer on Office 365 if you're not ready to migrate your domain into Office 365.

Why ?

Because if you activate Yammer on Office 365 on the default domain (*.onmicrosoft.com) or on the test domain, then you will never be able to upgrade your Yammer basic plan.

The "only moment" where you can upgrade your Yammer basic plan to Office 365 is while doing the first activation of Yammer into Office 365.

If you have activated Yammer like me for testing features before hand, then you are screwed.

Your only solution is to use the "Network migration" feature : but this feature only migrate users informations. Not the content.

Also, you cannot export data for a Yammer basic plan (no administration panel). And to be fair, this would be pointless since there is no "import" feature on Yammer.

The Microsoft support told me they cannot "unactivate/delete/unsubscribe" the yammer network that was activated on Office 365.
