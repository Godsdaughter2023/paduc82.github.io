Welcome to Microsoft Community. I apologize for the delayed in response.

 

From what I understand, you already tried following the workaround given in this article but you are concerned because you tend to easily forget your password of your other Microsoft account. Currently, the option to merge two or more Microsofts accounts is not available on Outlook.com.

 

Here’s an alternative method that I can offer :

 

I suggest you to rename your 2nd Microsoft account (primary email address that you want to become an alias).

 

Note: I strongly recommend you to back up your emails before proceeding with this process to prevent data loss. You can use Windows Live Mail to do this. Simply add your account and from there, back up your messages.

 

Once you have renamed this account it will become an alias (old email address) of the newly renamed account. You can also use this alias to sign in again using the password of the main account.

 

While renaming the account, you will be prompted to choose a folder where you want to move your email messages from your old account. You can choose to create a new folder or direct it to your Inbox folder. All emails sent to your old email address will be forwarded either in your Inbox or the created folder for the account.

 

After that, remove your alias (old email address) from the newly renamed account so you can add it to your main account as an alias. After 30 days, you can now add the email address as an alias of your 1st Microsoft account. Please refer to the same article I gave previously and follow the steps under “Add an alias to your account”.

 

Windows Live Mail is a great tool to transfer the emails from (old account) the deleted alias from your 2nd Microsoft account to your 1st Microsoft account. All you have to do is configure the two accounts and follow these steps:

 

1.     Open the folder (2nd Microsoft account) where you want to transfer the emails from.

2.     Press Ctrl+A to select all the images.

3.     Drag and drop the email messages in each folder from the 2nd Microsoft account (old email address) to a preferred folder on your other account (1st Microsoft account).

 

As for your contacts, you can easily export it from the 2nd Microsoft account (old email address) and import it to your main (1st) account. Here’s how:

 

To export contacts:

1. Go to https://people.live.com

2. Click Manage from command bar then select Export.

3. A pop-up message will appear if you want to save or open WLMContacts.csv, select Save. (Note: The WLMContacts.csv file will be saved in your computer Downloads folder.)

 

To import CSV file in your contact list (People page):

1. Go to https://people.live.com.

2. Click Import from file in People page.

3. In Step 2, choose Microsoft Outlook (CSV).

4. In Step 3, click browse and choose the file you want to import.

5. Click Import contacts.

 

Or if you cannot see the Import from file link in when you go to People page, follow the steps below:

 

1. In your People page, click Manage.

2. Click Add people.

3. Click Import from file.

4. In Step 2, choose Microsoft Outlook (CSV).

5. In Step 3, click browse and choose the file you want to import.

6. Click Import contacts.

 

Once done, you will be prompted that you have successfully imported your contacts to your Outlook.com account.

 

Good luck and let me know how it turns out for you.

 

Thank you.

Hello

1st field/column = person name

2nd field/column = skill (each person has 20 skills)

3rd field/column = skill level (number field that is 1-5)

4th field/column = modified date

how do I filter to get the last modified date of each persons particular skill. 

It tried to max in the criteria field of the modified date column but it still returns all the records.

Any help is greatly appreciated.

john w. vinson say:

There are a few ways to do it, but one is to use a Subquery as a criterion on the date field. Create a query based on your table and on the criteria under the Modified Date field put

=(SELECT Max([Modified Date]) FROM tablename AS X WHERE X.[person name] = tablename.[person name])

Of course if there are ties for the maximum date you'll get all rows. Note also that a person's name is not unique (I know three guys named Fred Brown), and people can change their names; you really should have a People table with a unique PersonID and use that ID in your skills table.
