john sr wilson say:
As steve says it's possible with a macro BUT the whole footer / slide number thing from version 2007 is not that well thought out (IMHO) and doesn't auto update.

I just wouldn;'t use the slide number at all but instead delete the number placeholder from all layouts and ADD to the large master a standard text box. Put the cursor in the box and INSERT > Slide number. Now type afterthe <#> " of 12" (whatever). Format font etc as required. Name the box in the selection pane "myNumber" (use any name you want but adapt the code)

Changing the number either manually or with a very simple macro should update all slides. If there were ALREASDY numbers on the slide you may need to unselect the slide number box and apply to all.

Sub update_NUM()
With ActivePresentation.Designs(1).SlideMaster.Shapes(myNumber").TextFrame
.DeleteText
.TextRange.InsertSlideNumber
.TextRange.InsertAfter " of " & CStr(ActivePresentation.Slides.Count)
End With
End Sub

graham mayor say:

Or, as macros are permissible in your document, you could create a userform to hold the names of the cities in a list box and display that listbox on entry to the field. List boxes can be configured to select by letter (or the full value).

Assuming a legacy form field create a simple userform in the document e.g.

The code behind the form would be as simple as

Option Explicit

Private Sub CommandButton1_Click()
ActiveDocument.FormFields("Text2").Result = Me.ListBox1.Text
Unload Me
End Sub

Private Sub UserForm_Initialize()
    Me.Caption = "Select City"
    With Me.ListBox1
        .TabIndex = 0
        .AddItem "Albany"
        .AddItem "Birmingham"
        .AddItem "Colchester"
        .AddItem "Doncaster"
        .ListIndex = -1
    End With
End Sub

Include your list of cities in place of those shown and you would call it from a macro, in an ordinary module in the document, run on entry to the form field. I have left the default names, for the elements and have assumed a text form field named 'Text2' You can change the names as appropriate.

Option Explicit

Sub GetCity()
UserForm1.Show
End Sub

When you tab into the field the userform displays and you can make your selection. You are also not limited to the 25 selections in a dropdown form field.

[SOLUTION! mid-2019] OS X/macOS Mail.app Office 365 - Microsoft Community

jimthing say:

[SOLUTION! mid-2019] OS X/macOS Mail.app Office 365 (Home/Personal) subscribers CAN set-up as Exchange!

(todays date: Wed.10.Apr.2019)

WHAT:

After fiddling around for ages setting up my "@outlook.com" email address as a plain IMAP account on my Apple's Mail.app on macOS, I have finally been able to set it up instead as an "Exchange" account! This is after multiple attempts to find a solution on these forums or the web in general over the last couple of years; all to no avail until now. 

WHY: 

This is necessary to avoid getting duplicate sent messages inside your sent folder on macOS, that then sync to any other device, thus showing these duplicates in their sent folder as well. (Highly annoying!)

-------------- 

HOW: 

The way i did this was to set-up as Exchange inside the native MS Outlook app for macOS first, then set-up in macOS Mail.app afterwards, then delete account from the Outlook app afterwards (as I don't use that email app client). 

(A): Outlook app for macOS correct setup: 

(A1) IMPORTANT: go to https://account.microsoft.com sign-in > then go to Security then small-print at bottom "more security options" > then "Create a new app password" and so ahead and make one (I can't remember the process exactly, but it should be obvious enough!). Close the tab. 

(A2) Then, as a O365 Home user, download the Outlook app for MacOS from the Mac App Store: https://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/microsoft-outlook/id985367838?mt=12 

(A3) On opening the Outlook app, do not allow it to auto-set your outlook.com email address. Cancel out of this if it offers it, then go in to: Outlook menu > Prefs > Accounts. 

(A4) Tap the "Add Email Account" button, enter your outlook.com email address. It will then say "Outlook.com" and ask for password: enter the AP (app password. Tip: in other apps/services these things are often called "app-specific passwords" or ASP's]) that you set-up previously in step (A1). And your account should setup inside the Outlook app. 

(A5) Open the Outlook preferences, tap "Advanced" where you will see under the "Server" tab, "Server: https://outlook.office365.com/EWS/Exchange...". This tells us that your account is Exchange enabled, as it has both "https://outlook.office365.com" and "EWS/Exchange" in the text there!

(A6) [optional] After doing all of (B) below, you can click the minus button in Outlook app Preferences for this account to delete it from this app, and delete the app if you don't need it. 

------- 

(B) Setup this outlook.com email inside Apple mail.app on macOS. 

(B1) In mail.app, open Prefs > Accounts, then select the "+" button. Select "Exchange", and enter name and your outlook.com email. 

(B2) It'll open a window with Exchange and "Sign in to your Exchange account using Microsoft", press the Sign In button. 

(B3) Next window, enter the AP (app password) setup in step (A1). 

(B4) Next window, "Select the apps..." tick the options wanted (I just use Mail, but you may use all or some of the other options). 

(B5) Bingo! Your outlook.com email should appear as an "Exchange" account in the "Accounts" sidebar of Mail.app's Prefs, and appaer in the main email window. Feel free to label it whatever you choose under the "Description" and choose the sync settings you want (leave them all as "...on the server"!). Under "Advanced Exchange Settings" should be "TLS Certificate: None"). 

-------------- 

Notes: 

(i) Whether this works for free outlook.com email addresses that have not signed-up for any O365 accounts, I haven't checked. 

(ii) Steps in (A) may or may not be necessary. So try doing the app password thing in step (A1) first, then jump straight to (B). If it doesn't work, then try the rest of the steps in (A), before doing (B) afterwards. 

Hope this helps someone previously annoyed!
