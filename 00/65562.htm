a. user say:

Hi Jakeh,

Thank you for posting your query on Microsoft Community. Let me assist you.

"Extend Volume" option under Disk Management tool is only available when there is contiguous unallocated space behind the volume you want to extend. As here is no unallocated space after the C partition drive, so extend volume greyed out.

Therefore we need an unallocated place on the right of the goal partition (drive: C), theoretically we should shrink volume D for unallocated space, but you will find you could only create an unallocated space behind drive: D, not drive: C, unless you could move the unallocated space or you could only make drive: D become unallocated place (delete drive: D) but this way may lead all your data in drive: D get loss, of course you could create a new partition and copy drive: D in it.

Data loss Warning:

Converting a hard disk partition or volume from NTFS to FAT32 requires that you reformat the partition, which deletes any data on it. Be sure to back up any data you want to keep before you begin.

I can understand you want to add the unallocated space to the (C:) drive on the computer. For doing so, you have to first add the Unallocated space (31.51 GB) to the drive (J:) and then try adding it to the (C:) drive. Try the below steps and check if it helps.

Steps:-

1. Right click on the System Reseved (J:) drive and click on Extend Volume.

2. Keep clicking Next and follow the on screen instructions.

3. The Unallocated space will be added to (J:) drive.

4. Now, right click on (J:) drive and click on Format.

5. Right click on the (J:) drive again and select Delete Volume.

6. Now (J:) drive shall be shown as Unallocated space.

7. Finally, right click on the (C:) drive and select Extend Volume.

8. Keep clicking on Next and follow the on screen instructions.

Hope this information is helpful. Please do let us know if you need further assistance, we’ll be glad to assist you.
