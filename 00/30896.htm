raiannon say:

Heeeey.  Good news!

There is a new feature on your Xbox One.  You can now do a factory reset BUT only wipe the system partition.

That's right, if you go to Settings -> All Settings -> System -> Console Info -> Reset

You will now have 2 options (besides cancel)

1. Reset and remove everything

2. Reset and keep my games and apps

When you choose the second option, your system will wipe the system partition and reinstall it.  When it boots back up, you'll be asked the normal things, what's your time zone, what's your favorite color, what's your name and password.  But then you get in, and your profile and games and apps are still there.

This can fix a TON of lag issues, because it can clear up all kinds of nasty partial files and overflowing cache files.  Personally, I think I'm going to make it part of my semi-annual upkeep. 

In your case, I would recommend uninstalling netflix, do a reset keeping your games and apps, get back in and reinstall it.  Once you do that, I bet you'll be just gravy.  But let me know.  :)

    dave n9 say:
    The messagestore is a type of index that keeps track of  the messages (where
    they are stored, are they deleted, etc.).
    It does not contain any messages.  Messages are stored as individual .eml
    files.
    When it is compacted, it removes the stored data for messages that have been
    deleted, as there is no need to retain that information.  The compaction
    will reduce the size of the index.
    It is not likely to slow down your computer, but could speed up Windows Mail
    following the compaction.
    Yes, you need it.

    bruce hagen ~ mvp say:

    'Compress' in the UK translates to 'compact' in the US, including Redmond. When you move or delete a message in WinMail, the space it took up is not reclaimed until you compact, (compress), your folders. It is not the same as compressing Windows files or folders.

    When you say there are three or four years of compacted messages, what exactly do you mean by that? If you save messages, they will be there. If you delete them they won't be there.

    If anything, not compacting will slow down WinMail and compacting will speed it up.
