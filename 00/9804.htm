Short answer, you can't have what you want. Wide spacing is now "it".

Long answer.

About a year ago MS responded to complaints about QAT spacing by "improving" it to look like the spacing on the "Old install" machine. When you update the "new install" machine to be current, it will look the same. There is no way to get back to the better, "old" spacing. "MS knows best" <sigh>

Somehow you managed to get 2 different versions installed from the same disk. A neat trick.

On the machine with the "old install", the version information translates to:

Version 1706 Build 8229.2108. 

That was the "current version" that was released July 27, 2017

On the machine with the "new install" the version information translates to

Version  ???? Build 4266.1003

That version was released between Oct 5, 2015 and Oct 21, 2015

You can find some build and version information on this site:

https://support.office.com/en-us/article/Version-and-build-numbers-of-update-channel-releases-ae942449-1fca-4484-898b-a933ea23def7

You can get more build and version information from this site:

https://technet.microsoft.com/en-us/office/mt465751

Bottom line

Both machines need to be manually updated. On the same screen you captured, click on the "Update Options" button then click on "Update Now" to force the update. You may have to do it more than once to get all of the updates.

In theory that should bring both machines up to Version 1707, Build 8326.2070 (in practice it may not come all the way to current).  And both machines will have the wide spacing on the QAT (sorry).

robert-jan mahieu say:

Does the MyPhone migration overwrite SkyDrive?

I used to have a phone running Windows Mobile 6.5, from where I synced my contacts to MyPhone. A few months ago I switched to a phone running Windows Phone 7 and have been using SkyDrive for my contacts instead.

Both the MyPhone and SkyDrive account are linked to the same Live-account, but needless to say, my contacts in MyPhone aren't up to date anymore.

So how is this handled with the migration? Will my (new) contacts in SkyDrive be overwritten with my (old) contacts in MyPhone? Or will the old contacts be placed in a seperate folder of some sort?


- Robert-Jan

julie fka profjulie say: Your active contacts that you sync between your windows live account and your windows phone 7 are stored in the Contacts module of Windows Live...they are not stored in your SkyDrive account.  There won't be any mix of your old My Phone Contacts with your active Windows Live contacts.

According to this knowledgebase article, the data in My Phone that will be migrated to your Windows Live SkyDrive account will be migrated as separate files:

Contacts will be migrated over as a csv file
Calendar items will be migrated over as ics files (this data will not appear in your Windows Live Calendar)
Text messages will be migrated over as txt files
Photos will be migrated over as jpg, jpeg, gif, png, tif, fit, wbmp, bmp, opl, n3a, or ota files
