Hi Cactusbythewindow,

First of the first, the activation is different from sign in. You need to activate the Office with a valid licensed account. You can sign in with multiple accounts. Please see the detailed descriptions below:

The activation is to use a valid licensed account to make your desktop Office apps available to use. That is, for you now, the student account. The reason why you can use the Office desktop apps is that your student account is assigned the valid license from your school. The license is paid by your school.

The sign in purpose is for connecting the related services of the related account. For example, if you sign in with your school account, you can save or open files stored in your school OneDrive for Business directly within from the Office apps. If you sign in with your personal account, you then can save or open files stored in your personal OneDrive account directly within from the Office apps. You also can sign in with multiple accounts.

After the descriptions above, please see the answers for your questions:

- Will I lose access to OneNote, despite the fact it's freeware because it's subject to whatever licence agreement I got it through? (From what I can tell, it looks like the answer to this is yes.)

A:  After your graduation, your school account will be invalid. That means, you will lose the access to your school OneDrive for Business. Therefore, you can’t access to the files stored in your school OneDrive for Business. So, you need to migrate all the files stored in your school OneDrive for Business to your personal OneDrive.

- Would I avoid the issue of losing access to my notes when I graduate, if I were to uninstall the student version of OneNote, install the freeware version instead and use that with my personal Microsoft account?

A: Migrate all the files stored in your school OneDrive for Business to your personal OneDrive (free) is the way to avoid the data lose. As the activation is different from sign in, you can’t use the desktop Office apps when your school account is invalid. If you want to activate Office with your personal account, you need to purchase a subscription using your personal account. If you want to use free Office, you can use Office Online but not Office desktop client apps.

- What happens if I sign in using my Personal account to Office 365 programs licenced to my Student Account?

A: You can use the related services connected with your personal account. Please remember that the sign in is different from activation.

A: The “read only” you mentioned should be the reduced functionality mode. If the Office desktop client apps are not activated with a valid account, this mode will happen. But the Office Online is free for you, you can edit files anytime you want.
