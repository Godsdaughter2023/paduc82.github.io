OK I figured it out myself and will post this for other Mac users with the same dilemma.

 

Turns out it's as simple as can be. After you've set up your bluetooth on the phone to connect with the Mac, make sure the phone is on and the bluetooth is ON both on the phone and the Mac (should have a bluetooth icon in the upper right of your menu bar (on any Mac w BT). Up pops a window with your media folders that are on your phone. Photos are in the images folder, videos in the video folder etc. Just highlight the ones you want to transfer to your Mac,  hit Get, and in the Mac finder window that opens, navigate to the folder you want to drop these photos into. (I made a new folder in my Pictures folder called Phone Pix). When you've finished downloading the photos, you can then select any or all of them in the phone window and conveniently delete them from your phone memory, saving space for future pix.

dennis wi - msft say:

Hello Helen,

 

You have a couple options to populate your family group field for new records in your guest form.

 

The first option is as William suggested in using a form/subform design. I don't believe you can use multiple split forms when using a form/subform design so you may need to change the form view to single form. To setup form/subform design, you would open the family group form in design view and then drad the guest form to into the family group form. It sounds like you have defined the relationship for these tables so the subform should automatically set what are called the linkmastfields/linkchildfields properties. When you open the family group form in form view you will only see the related records of the current family group in the guest form. When you add a new guest it will be added for the currently selected family group.

 

The second option would be to continue using the 2 separate forms. Since there's no formal relationship having two forms, you can specify the default value of the family group field in your guest form to pull the current value from the family group form when a new record is created.

 

To do this, follow these steps:

1. Open the guest form in design view

2. Locate the family group field and select it

3. Open the properties window if it's not open

4. Under the data tab for this field you should see a default value property

5. Enter the form reference to your family group field from the family group form.

It would look something like this Forms![family group]![family group id]

6. Save and close the guest form

 

Set default values for fields or controls

http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/access-help/set-default-values-for-fields-or-controls-HA010096451.aspx

Dennis

a. user say:

If it's still helpful for anyone, here's an alternate solution I found that involves adding an environment variable rather than editing the registry.

  1. Click on Windows Start menu
  2. In the search box, search for “environment variables”
  3. Click on “Edit environment variables for your account” (you could create it as a system variable instead; it should work either way)
  4. Click the “New” button under the user variables box
  5. Enter variable name “PQ_EnableTls12ForOAuth” and variable value “true” (without quotes)
  6. Hit OK to save the new variable and then re-open Excel
