reneyu_msft say:

Hi Joesatch,

As I mentioned above, in Office 365 for business plan, the deleted user is not licensed, as the licenses have already been released when we delete the user.

The designed cmdlet we use for removing licenses would only apply to active users instead of the user whose license has been released.

However, since, due to the design, Office 365 would re-assign the released licenses to the user who we deteled but try restoring, we are able to restore the user first, then remove the licenses and delete the user again, which is doable by PowerShell cmdlets.

After you connect to msolservice, run the following cmdlets:

Restore-MsolUser -UserPrincipalName *** Email address is removed for privacy ***
Set-MsolUserLicense -UserPrincipalName *** Email address is removed for privacy *** -RemoveLicenses "organization:ENTERPRISEPACK"
Remove-MsolUser -UserPrincipalName *** Email address is removed for privacy *** -Force

You may run the below cmdlet first to see which licenses are assigned to the user before being deleted:

Get-MsolUser -UserPrincipalName *** Email address is removed for privacy *** -ReturnDeletedUsers | Select UserPrincipalName,Licenses

In addition, if you intend to reduce the license from your Office 365 subscription after the user is deleted, PowShell is not what we use in this case. Please find the information at Remove licenses from your Office 365 for business subscription.

Best Regards,
Rene

john mcghie say:
The bibliography at the end of the document is entirely "generated" text.  The "actual" text that is placed into the bibliography is contained in the Reference Character in the body of the document.

The Reference Character contains a link to text in your Sources file (on your computer) or in the document itself.

We can hope that the Sources are still present on your computer (although if the document did not start out as a .docx, that's a bit of a large "hope).  To find out: 

First, follow the instructions in the Word help topic "Add or change sources, citations, and bibliographies", Step 3-2.

You will either get a perfectly collated and numbered bibliography, or a prompt telling you that no Source was found.

If you get the latter, you will need to follow the rest of the instructions in the help topic to re-create the sources file.  Given that you converted out to Google Docs, there is a risk that the references themselves have been stripped.  If that's the case, then I am afraid you will also need to re-add all of the citations.

Hope this helps
