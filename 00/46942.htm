red264 say:

You can buy HDMI ARC converter boxes on Amazon that will enable HDMI-ARC on old sound systems, they are essentially dummy sound systems that pose as an ARC receiver then pass the audio to the sound system plugged in at the other end. 


Be aware that HDMI-ARC is no different from optical, it only supports PCM 2.0 (stereo) Dolby 5.1 and DTS 5.1, thats all you get. If you desire HD audio formats or PCM 7.1 you need a 4K/HDR compatible sound system.


Dolby Atmos is buggy on Xbox because of the way the Xbox audio engine works, MS have an audio upmixer that merges the system UI sounds with audio streams but this does not play well when bitstreaming Atmos. Which on a technical level is handing off the audio from Xbox to the sound system to decode so you can see why their upmixer causes all sorts of problems. There really should be a toggle in the audio settings to disable the upmixer (thereby muting system audio) when using Atmos or bitstreaming Blu-ray/Movie/T

  2. Clean the mouse area around the button/wheel/touchpad.

  3. Verify the mouse is paired to the host.

    Go to Settings > Devices > Bluetooth & Other Devices. Verify that the mouse is listed, and shows as “Connected.”

  4. Remove and re-pair the mouse to the host.

    Remove and replace the same batteries. This will ensure the mouse is getting full power from the batteries.

  6. Restart the host.

    You may need to re-pair the device after you restart the host. Check Settings > Devices > Bluetooth & Other Devices to verify paired state.
