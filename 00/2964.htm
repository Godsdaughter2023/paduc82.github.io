macropod say:

In answer to your questions:

1) what to write where u have written "Entire content related to Borrower_Add"

This means exactly what it says and what the further instructions told you you would need to do, namely:

Where the above field code contains the expressions 'Entire content related to Borrower_Add', etc., you need to insert all of the content, including mergefields, text, images, tables, etc. related to that item.

2) in this you have used four addresses, but our addresses varies from 2 to 15 addresses

That's all very well but first you said:

We have in Excel one Name with 2 different addresses of one person

then you said:

some names have upto 5 address

after which you provided a screenshot with only four addresses with different field names to those you originally provided. If you don't provide the appropriate specifications and details, you can't expect to get advice that exactly matches your needs.

Regardless, the principle is the same - simply keep adding more fields like:

{IF{MERGEFIELD Borrower_Add1}<> "" "{IF{Key \# 0}= 1 {QUOTE 12}}Entire content related to Borrower_Add1 {SET Key 1}"}

before the final one, which lacks the {SET Key 1}, modifying the mergefield referred to and the output content, until all the potential addresses are accounted for. With as many as 15 addresses to cater for, your final field coding will be exceptionally complex and difficult to maintain. This has nothing to do with the complexity of your MIS and everything to do with a failure to extract the data in a form that is more suitable for use in a mailmerge.

3) What does {IF{Key \# 0}= 1 {QUOTE 12} this do?

You don't have to understand what all the field codes do - all you need to do is to create and use them as described. That said:

• {QUOTE 12} inserts a page break.

• {IF{Key \# 0}= 1 {QUOTE 12} tests whether a bookmark named Key (initially created by {SET Key 0}) has a value of 1 and, if so, uses the {QUOTE 12} field to insert a page break.
