jim gordon mvp say:
The additional controls you see in the Windows version are called Active-X controls. Active-X is an open source technology that has not been ported to the Mac, so they simply don't exist on the Mac.

Some people rejoice that these controls have not been ported, as there is a feeling that Active-X has some unsurmountable security concerns that the open source community could never rectify. 

To my knowledge there is no project in place in the open source community to port Active-X to the Mac. Since Active-X is open source, any party that wants to could port it to the Mac, but so far there's been no movement in that direction by Apple, Microsoft, Oracle, or any other open source supporters.

john w. vinson say: Well, you certainly need a third table, and you may need some redesign of your existing tables. Why is the Employee Number a "dummy"? What are the Primary Keys of your tables? A table without a primary key is a bit like a car with no steering gear... not much use! A training database models a many to many relationship: each Employee may take zero, one, or many Courses, and each Course will include zero, one, or many Employees. The proper table structure would be:

tblEmployees
  EmployeeNumber (Primary Key, required)
  LastName
  FirstName
  <other biographical data as needed>

tblCourses
  CourseNumber (Primary Key, required)
  CourseName
  Topic
  <other information about the course as a thing in itself>

tblEnrollment
  EnrollmentID (autonumber Primary Key)
  EmployeeNumber (link to tblEmployees, who's enrolling in the course)
  CourseNumber (link to tblCourses, what are they getting into)
  <other information about THIS employee in THIS course, e.g. date enrolled, date completed, status, comments>
