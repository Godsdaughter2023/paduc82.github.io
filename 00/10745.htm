OK, I understand (/remember) that startFromScratch clears all the tabs too.  I'd never be brave enough to take away all of the built-in tabs from users, but I guess you know what you're doing.  What I did do is build a utility that lets my users choose between seeing the built-in "dynamic" tabs (that switch automatically depending on what the users does), or a custom tab that replicates the built-in tab (but don't switch dynamically) or hiding a built-in tab all together.

So if you want to hide everything and "start from scratch", then I think you need to hide all the tabs and contextual tabs manually.  You can get a list of tab idMso's from here: http://www.microsoft.com/en-us/download/details.aspx?id=3582 for 2007 and here: http://www.microsoft.com/en-us/download/details.aspx?id=6627 for 2010 (which apparently might have a few that are missing from the 2007 one).  But just for you, here's some cut-down XML to do what you need (I think):

 

 <contextualTabs>
  <tabSet idMso="TabSetSmartArtTools">
   <tab idMso="TabSmartArtToolsDesign"  visible="false"/>
   <tab idMso="TabSmartArtToolsFormat"  visible="false"/>
  </tabSet>
 
  <tabSet idMso="TabSetChartTools">
   <tab idMso="TabChartToolsDesign"  visible="false"/>
   <tab idMso="TabChartToolsLayout"  visible="false"/>
   <tab idMso="TabChartToolsFormat"  visible="false"/>
  </tabSet>
 
  <tabSet idMso="TabSetDrawingTools">
   <tab idMso="TabDrawingToolsFormat"  visible="false"/>
  </tabSet>
 
  <tabSet idMso="TabSetPictureTools">
   <tab idMso="TabPictureToolsFormat"  visible="false"/>
  </tabSet>
 
  <tabSet idMso="TabSetTableTools">
   <tab idMso="TabTableToolsDesign"  visible="false"/>
   <tab idMso="TabTableToolsLayout"  visible="false"/>
  </tabSet>
 
  <tabSet idMso="TabSetSoundTools">
   <tab idMso="TabSoundToolsOptions"  visible="false"/>
  </tabSet>
 
  <tabSet idMso="TabSetMovieTools">
   <tab idMso="TabMovieToolsOptions"  visible="false"/>
  </tabSet>
 
  <tabSet idMso="TabSetCDAudioTools">
   <tab idMso="TabCDAudioToolsOptions"  visible="false"/>
  </tabSet>
 
<!--  <tabSet idMso="TabSetInkTools">
   <tab idMso="TabInkToolsPens"   visible="false"/>
  </tabSet>-->
 </contextualTabs>
 
 <tabs >
  <tab idMso="TabSlideMasterHome"  visible="false"/>
  <tab idMso="TabHome"    visible="false"/>
  <tab idMso="TabInsert"    visible="false"/>
  <tab idMso="TabDesign"    visible="false"/>
  <tab idMso="TabAnimations"   visible="false"/>
  <tab idMso="TabSlideShow"   visible="false"/>
  <tab idMso="TabReview"    visible="false"/>
  <tab idMso="TabView"    visible="false"/>
  <tab idMso="TabSlideMaster"   visible="false"/>
  <tab idMso="TabHandoutMaster"   visible="false"/>
  <tab idMso="TabNotesMaster"   visible="false"/>
 </tabs>

 

There is also tabDeveloper and tabAddIns, but I would leave those in place.  I understand the TabSetInkTools contextual tabs are only available on tablet PCs.

I would be sure to put a custom tab of your own in there as I would expect it to be unhappy if there are no tabs at all.

You can then leave out startFromScratch and thus leave in the user's QAT.

Hope that helps.

The keyboard layout has probably been set to US International.

Open the regional and language options control panel.

Activate the Keyboards and Languages tab.

If you see something like English (Australia) - US International, you have to select/change it to English (Australia) - United States.
