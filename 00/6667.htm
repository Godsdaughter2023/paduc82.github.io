john w. vinson say:

What is the Datatype of Amount? If it's Integer, this error will happen if the sum is over 65536. Currency would be a better choice; its range is almost big enough to handle the US national debt, much less any reasonable amount.

Your criteria will NOT do what you want, though; as written it will retrieve all records. If the field contains "Symbol" then it does NOT contain "", and vice versa, so the criterion will always be TRUE. Also, the (default for totals queries) HAVING clause is applied AFTER all the summing is done. Try instead:

SELECT [Frip Transactions].Symbol, Sum(nz([Amount])) AS Dividends

GROUP BY [Frip Transactions].Symbol;

gord dibben say:

Routine to create what you asked for.

Prolly more than you need but I'm bored<g>

Sub Add_Checkboxes()
   'assume series of questions to A2:Ax
   'create checkboxes in B2:Bx
   'assign true/false results to C2:Cx
   'create formula in F2:Fx to return wingdings checkmark or blank
   'hide column C
Dim myCBX As CheckBox
Dim myCell As Range
Dim RAN As Range
Dim numrows As Long
Application.ScreenUpdating = False

numrows = InputBox("How Many Rows?")

Set RAN = ActiveSheet.Range("B2:B" & numrows + 1)

With ActiveSheet
      .CheckBoxes.Delete
      .Range("C:C,F:F").ClearContents
    For Each myCell In RAN

    With myCell
        Set myCBX = .Parent.CheckBoxes.Add _
            (Top:=.Top, _
             Width:=12, _
             Left:=.Left + ((.Width - 12) / 2), _
             Height:=.Height)
             .Offset(0, 1).ColumnWidth = 0
             .RowHeight = 19
        With myCBX
            .LinkedCell = myCell.Offset(0, 1).Address(external:=True)
            .Caption = ""
            .Value = xlOff
            '.OnAction = "'" & ThisWorkbook.Name & "'!dothework"
        End With
    
    End With
    
    Next myCell
    
    With Range("F2")
      .Value = "=IF(C2=TRUE,CHAR(252),"""")"  'bernie's formula
      .AutoFill Destination:=Range("F2:F" & numrows + 1), Type:=xlFillDefault
    End With
    
    Range("A1").Value = "Question"
    Range("B1").Value = "Check for Yes"
    With Range("F:F")
        .ColumnWidth = 4
        .HorizontalAlignment = xlCenter
        .VerticalAlignment = xlCenter
    End With
End With
Application.ScreenUpdating = True
End Sub

Those are not languages present in phones sold in this area so they go to an NCP to have the language added to the phone's pre-existing language set.

robertwoods5109 say:

Junk email settings changing during upgrade

 we found that when upgrading from Office 2010 to Office 2016 that the user’s Junk Mail settings are changed.  For example, a user had their Junk Mail setting set as “Low” before the upgrade and afterwards it was changed to “No Automatic Filtering”. 

 

My concern is that the Junk Mail settings for all of our users will change once I upgrade them from Office 2010 to Office 2016.  Is there a way to stop this from happening so that their Junk Mail settings stay as they are after the upgrade?

 

Thanks for your help.

 

moranyan say:

Hi Robert,

Regarding “Is there a way to stop this from happening so that their Junk Mail settings stay as they are after the upgrade”, the answer is no, we are not able to change the Junk Mail setting from “No Automatic Filtering” to “Low” after the upgrade from Outlook 2010 to Outlook 2016 because the Junk Mail setting of the Outlook 2016 is set as “No Automatic Filtering” by default. While the Junk Mail setting of the Outlook 2010 is set as “Low”.

For more information, please refer to:

Outlook 2010:

support.office.com/.../Change-the-level-of-protection-in-the-Junk-Email-Filter-e89c12d8-9d61-4320-8c57-d982c8d52f6b

Outlook 2013 & 2016:
