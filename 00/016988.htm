dmg313 say:

Conditional Formatting of Columns Based On Column Headers

Hello

I have a sheet with a large amount of data and there are many columns that have the same header name, anywhere from 5 to 45 times repeated, which is the nature of the work. Therefore, I wanted to know how I could format columns of data based on the column header. For an example, if I have the word "Date" as ten different column headers (eg. G1, H1, AA1, AS1, AR1, etc.) how could I quickly format each column with the Column header (Row 1) with the word "Date" so that it will automatically format the subsequent rows within that specific column, for example it would change the values to format into the Short Date format.

Thank you for any assistance.

hansv mvp say:

Select the entire range. I'll assume that A1 is the top left cell in the selection.

On the Home tab of the ribbon, click Conditional Formatting > New Rule...

Select 'Use a formula to determine which cells to format'.

Enter the formula   =A$1="Date"    where A1 is the top left cell of the selection; note the $ before the row number 1.

Click Format...

Activate the Number tab.

Select Date, then your preferred date format.

Click OK, then click OK again.

As I understood your issue, your new email messages are going in the junk folder instead of your inbox even if the sender is in your safe sender's list. Is that correct? If yes, you may create a rule that directs the emails from specific senders to your inbox. Kindly follow the steps below on how to make a rule.

 

  1. Log in to your account.
  2. Click the gear icon located at the right upper side of your screen.
  3. Click More mail settings.
  4. Under Customizing Outlook, click Rules for sorting new messages.
  5. Click New.
  6. There you can create the rule, click Save when you are done.

If the issue still persist after doing the steps above, kindly get back to us and let us know.
