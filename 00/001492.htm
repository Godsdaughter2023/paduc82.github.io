By default, when you press Alt in Ribbon versions of Word, you get popups showing the accelerators for the Ribbon, but you can still use many of the accelerator keys that you used in Word 2003 and earlier. cmdrkeene say:

Greetings!  Thanks for sharing the post.

I think I can help.  From the looks of it, the dialog window doesn't understand high-DPI scaling for super HD monitors.  On these monitors, there's so many pixels in the same space that a typical 10point font looks miniscule. So the system scales everything up by a factor to make it appear a normal size.  Older software, especially something that old, doesn't work with scaling, but there's ways to make up for it.

I can think of two things I would try.  Now I don't have my hands on the computer, but if I did, this is what I'd try:

  1. First, I'd try to enable a compatibility option on the setup program. Here's how:  find and right-click the Office 2007 installer/setup file, and choose "Properties".  Then on the compatibility tab, check the option to 'disable DPI scaling.".  Save your setting by clicking OK, then double-click that setup program again.  Hopefully it works well now.

    or


  2. If that doesn't work, you can temporarily turn off ALL dpi scaling on your computer.  To do this, open "Settings" app on your Windows 10 computer, and click the Devices section. Then you'll see a slider that lets you pick how much to scale (enlarge) the screen.  Put that down to 100% and apply it.  Install Office, then go back and move the slider back how you want it again.

I believe one of these will help, hopefully the first one.  Please let me know if it does.
