TRY THIS OVERHEATING FIX:

To all who are suffering from this problem, this is what you must do to fix it:

1. Press down and hold the Windows logo key on your keyboard while pressing the letter "i" simultaneously to bring up your settings window (this is a shortcut route).

2. Now that you are in your settings window, click on "Accounts".

3. Next, click on "Verify" which appears just under your account name in light blue.

4. Finally, enter your password to verify your account.

5. Coolness and order are restored to your Windows 10 Operating System!

*Diagnosis*

If you're logged in with a local account without using a password, you may never run into this issue because you're less likely to be prompted to "Verify" your account.

However, anyone else who has synced an email account to Windows 10 (or even Windows 8.1) will be asked to "Verify" your account whenever Windows notices that you've made some notable changes by their standards.  Using Windows 10 without entering your password to verify your user account can kick Windows 10 security parameters into overdrive as seen by the sustained high disk and CPU usage via Task Manager.

If you cut your internet off (disable wi-fi switch), your computer might freeze because you've interrupted the Wi-Fi Background Task (wifitask.exe) which can go berzerk when you haven't verified your user account.  However, if you're able to disconnect from the internet safely without your computer freezing, then your computer won't run hot and kick on the fan into overdrive.  Therefore, the main culprit is CLEARLY A NETWORKING BUG/GLITCH.  The absence of an internet connection will immediately stabilize your computer but it also renders your PC virtually useless in this day and age.

Thus Microsoft needs to rectify this issue with the Wi-Fi Background Task going wild on any temporarily "unverified" account because it can make a computer run ridiculously hot while it tries to ramp up security measures.  This overheating issue is not caused by dust in your vents, or some wacky virus/spyware/malware/adware attack.  This problem is a network security user account verification BUG/GLITCH.

Hopefully, this fix solves your problem so that you don't have to install or reinstall a recent Windows 10 Insider Preview Build.

With Windows 10 slated for a late July release, let's hope that Microsoft keeps on listening to our valuable feedback so that they can build a rock solid operating system for the people, by the people.
