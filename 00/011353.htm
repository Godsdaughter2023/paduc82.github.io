joeu2004 say:

[EDIT: On second thought, I think the use of the terms "gross" and "net" in this context is debatable.  I think it would be clearer if we just talk about about "purchase price" and "total cost".]

It is clear that you what to derive the purchase price of 470,000, given the total cost of 483,500, which is the purchase price plus the tax of 13,500.

Also, note that the UK SDLT is a "progressive" (marginal) tax schedule, which is easy to confirm by a google search.  See https://www.gov.uk/stamp-duty-land-tax/residential-property-rates .

So the correct presentation of the tax rates is the yellow-highlighted portion of the table below.

As demonstrated by your example, for a purchase price of 470,000, the tax is 13,500 = 0% of the first 125,000 plus 2% of the next 125,00 plus 5% of the next 220,000, where 220,000 = 470,000 - 250,000.

The following demonstrates one solution in order to derive the purchase price (470,000) from the total cost (483,500).  See row 10.

(Download "sdlt gross answers.ms.xls" from https://app.box.com/s/v0dvml5g870wu3z7tczg9dmapm8x53f9 .  Ignore any preview errors.)

Formulas:

A2: =ROUND(C2+D2, 2)

B2: =ROUND(C3 + D2 + E2*(C3-C2), 2)

H2: =ROUND(C2*E2 - D2, 2)

D3: =ROUND(D2 + E2*(C3-C2), 2)

G3: =E3-E2

B10: =ROUND(SUMPRODUCT((A10>$C$2:$C$6)*(A10-$C$2:$C$6), $G$2:$G$6), 2)

C10: =ROUND(A10+B10, 2)

E10: =ROUND((C10 +VLOOKUP(C10, $A$2:$H$6, 8)) / (1+VLOOKUP(C10, $A$2:$H$6, 5)), 2)

F10: =E10=A10

Rows 11-15 demonstrate the correctness for random prices in each tax backet (tier).  The formulas are:

A11: =ROUND(RANDBETWEEN(C2*100, C3*100)/100, 2)

A15: =ROUND(RANDBETWEEN(C6*100, C6*200)/100, 2)

Note that B1:B6 and F1:F6 are not really needed for the solution.  They are provided for presentation purposes only.

B10 demonstrates one way to calculate the total progressive tax using SUMPRODUCT.  It depends on "differential" tax rates in G2:G6.

Normally, the tax rate in E2:E6 is a marginal rate, which is applied to the price minus the low bracket amount ("over...").

However, mathematically, we can treat the tax rate as a flat rate which is applied to the entire price, then reduced by a "flat offset", which is calculated in H2:H6.

That simplifies the derivation of the purchase price from the total cost, using the formula in E10.

[EDIT:  Once we accept the "flat offset" method, the formula in B10 can be simply:

=ROUND(A10*VLOOKUP(A10, $C$2:$H$6, 3) - VLOOKUP(A10, $C$2:$H$6, 6), 2)

That is almost certainly more efficient than the "diff% method", despite the two VLOOKUPs.  Then we no longer need the differential tax rates in G2:G6.]

To get the right percent values we have to calculate the gross price in the lookup table too:
E2: =D2+(D2-0.01)*F2

To make the example easier to understand how to calculate that let use calculate the gross price first:
B2: =VLOOKUP(B1,D2:F6,3)
B3: =B1*B2
B4: =B1+B3

B6: =VLOOKUP(B4,E2:F6,2)

B7: =1+B6

B8: =B4/B7

Function MakeFolderTree(myPath As String)

    Dim newPath As String

    Dim strRet As String

    Dim v As Variant

    Dim i As Integer

    

    v = Split(myPath, "\")

    newPath = v(0)

    ChDrive newPath

    For i = 1 To UBound(v)

        newPath = newPath & "\" & v(i)

        strRet = Dir(newPath, vbDirectory)

        If strRet = "" Then

            MsgBox "I am now making the folder " & v(i)

            MkDir newPath

        End If

    Next i

End Function

claud wang support moderator say:

hi manjunatha, 

please follow the steps below to restrict them not to delete migration batch:
1. connect to exchange online using powershell.
2. create a new management role. for example, here is an example management rol “migrationbatch”.
new-managementrole -parent “migration” -name migrationbatch
3. remove the “remove-migrationbatch”, “remove-migrationendpoint”, “remove-migrationuser” role entry:
remove-managementroleentry migrationbatch\remove-migrationbatch
remove-managementroleentry migrationbatch\remove-migrationendpoint
remove-managementroleentry migrationbatch\remove-migrationuser

4. login to office 365 exchange admin center (eac) (https://outlook.office365.com/ecp/), click permission > admin role, click the plus symbol to create a new admin role group, click the plus symbol under “roles”, select the "migrationbatch" role. click save.

5. click the plus symbol under “members” to assign the migrationbatch role to the users. click ok to finish.
after that, the user shouldn’t delete the migration batch. here is a screenshot of the user’s eac page (the delete button doesn't exist):
