Thank you for posting to the forums regarding the troubles you are having signing into the Zune software.  I have seen various issues signing in caused by a multitude of sources.  With that said, you will find a list of things I suggest anyone with a sign in issue try.  As you did not mention what was tried to this point, I am going to include all, including some things that may seem simple or silly.  I do encourage you to try each one.

  •  Launch the Zune software and go directly to the marketplace without trying to sign in.  Once you see the top plays and suggested artists, click settings in the upper right corner > go to account > summary > sign in.
  • Verify the time, date and time zone on your computer.   You can do this by going to your desktop and then clicking on the time.  After you select the "change date and time" option set all to match where you are currently located.
  • If the issue continues the next thing to do is to open an instance of Internet Explorer.  Once open click the gear icon in the upper right corner and go to Internet Options.  Go all the way to the advanced tab and click both the restore and reset buttons.  Once you have done this and clicked on okay, reboot your computer and try signing into the software again. 
  • If you are still unable to sign in please go to http://account.microsoft.com and sign into your account.  Once you are signed in, go to the security & privacy section.  Here you will click on "check recent activity".  Go through the activity that is shown.  If you see any activity that does not look to be yours, select the option that this was not you.  When reviewing this, if you have the option to confirm that a specific activity WAS you, please mark those accordingly.
  • Finally, try uninstalling and reinstalling the Zune software.  Before doing this please clear your computer temporary files by pressing and holding the Windows key + R > typing %temp% > pressing enter > highlighting all the files and folders > selecting delete > skip any that refuse to delete.  Once this is done, run the following tool to uninstall anything with Zune in it, http://support.microsoft.com/mats/Program_Install_and_Uninstall?wa=wsignin1.0 .  Once everything is gone, reboot the computer and install back to the computer from here, http://www.microsoft.com/en-us/download/details.aspx?id=27163 .

If you find that you still cannot sign in this may be related to the account or the network.  That would leave me with three suggestions to try and figure this out.

  • Sign into the Zune software with another Microsoft account.  Any Microsoft account tied to either a Xbox Live or Xbox Music account will be able to successfully sign in.  If you have another account or a family member has an account, try signing into Zune with this.
  • Attempt to sign into your account from another computer with the Zune software.
  •  If you are able to connect this computer to another network, it may be worth verifying that you get this error while connected to various networks.

Please let us know if you have any other questions or concerns we can assist you with.
