    nicholas_w_ say:

    Workaround for Connected POP Account issues.

    Please follow the steps below to fix issues with your POP account. If the steps below do not resolve your issue, please reach out to Outlook community for more support.

    Step 1: Update your POP account settings.

    1. Got to https://outlook.live.com/owa/?path=/options/connections
    2. Sign in with your Microsoft account.
    3. You should be able to see the status of the account as “Update failed”.



    1. Select the account and go to “Edit connected accounts” page by either double clicking on the account or clicking on the pencil icon.



    1. Update the password of the account and save the settings.
    2. Please wait for some time for account status to turn from “pending update” to “Up to date”

    Step 2: Only necessary if Step 1 did not resolve your issue.

    In some cases, updating the password (using steps above) might not work and status of the message would show “pending update” or show “update failed”. Sometimes major mail providers block the connection on POP and requires the users to explicitly enable it on their accounts via mail provider settings.

    For Gmail POP account follow steps available at https://support.google.com/accounts/answer/6010255?hl=en

    For Yahoo POP account follow the steps available at https://help.yahoo.com/kb/account/SLN27791.html?impressions=true

    For all other email providers, please contact them for steps on how to get unblocked.

    Step 3:  If Steps 1 and 2 did not resolve the issue you will need to remove your POP account and reconfigure it as IMAP connection.

    If the above steps are not helping and your email server supports getting emails through IMAP. You should consider switching to use IMAP instead. IMAP provide lots of advantages over using POP. IMAP provides ability to sync changes to all of the folders and not just inbox. The changes are synced across all of your devices.  

    To remove the failing POP account, select the account and remove the same by clicking on ‘trash’ icon.



    Once the account is removed, try adding it again. Remember to select “Create a new folder for imported email, with subfolders like in the account you connect” .


    Conclusion 

    If any of the above steps still did not resolve your issue, please contact support for further assistance.  

    contact support for further assistance.  

    For more information, please visit Fixes or workarounds for recent issues on Outlook.com

    Solution: How to Left Align Entries and Right-Align Page Numbers - Microsoft Community

    martin rosoff say:

    Solution: How to Left Align Entries and Right-Align Page Numbers in Your Table of Contents (Word 2016)

    This solution worked for me. Give it a try and see if it will work for you. 

    Formatting Your Table of Contents

    Pre-requisite to formatting the left and right alignment of your TOC:

    Highlight your entire TOC and clear formatting by selecting the Styles pane. Under Apply a Style, click “Clear Formatting.”

    Apply the TOC style level, E.g. TOC 1, TOC 2, TOC 3 and so on to the respective lines in your TOC.

    Next, without highlighting any entries of your TOC, select Insert | Index and Tables. The Index and Tables window displays.

    Select the number of levels you wish to show in your TOC under “Show levels”.  

    In the Index and Tables window, put check marks in all three boxes:
    - Show page numbers
    - Right align page numbers
    - Use hyperlinks instead of page numbers

    Tab Leader: Select “………” 

    Select OK.


    Formatting the Lines of/Headings in Your TOC

    Step 1: Highlight your entire TOC

    Step 2: Highlight the entire TOC and select the Styles Pane. 

    Step 3: At the bottom of the Style pane, under in the List drop down, select All Styles

    Step 3: Under Styles on the right hand side of your screen, select TOC 1 under the “Apply a style:” heading. Click the down arrow in the TOC 1 field. The Modify Style window displays.

    Step 4: Select the drop down arrow to the immediate right of Format in the lower left hand corner of the window. 

    Step 5: Select Paragraph ...  .  The Paragraph window displays. Set the Indentation: Left: field to a number of your choosing. Click OK to close the Paragraph window.  

    Step 6: Click OK to close the Modify Style window. 

    Step 7:  Repeat Steps 3 - 6 for each TOC level.



    Page Numbering Alignment in TOC

    Step 1: Highlight the entire TOC, go to Format | Tabs. Tabs window displays. Select one at a time the Alignment: Left button, Tab Stops field should be empty. Leader: None. Default stops: 0”. Do this again, this time selecting Alignment: Right. Click OK. Close the Tabs window.

    Step 2: Highlight the entire TOC and select Insert … Index and Tables. Select Table of Contents tab. Formats: From template. Click the Modify button.

    Step 3: Select TOC1 and then click the Modify … button

    Step 4: Click Format in the lower left hand corner of the Modify Style window

    Step 5: Click Tabs

    Step 6: Set the Tab stops to a number of your choosing. Alignment: Select “Right”. Leader: Select “ …….”, then click the “+” button. Click OK. 

    Step 7: Repeat Steps 3 - 6 for each TOC level.

    Step 8: Click OK to close the Modify Style window.

    Step 9: Click OK to close the Style window.

    Step 10: Click OK to close the Index and Tables window.

    Step 11: Click OK in the “Microsoft Word: Replace this table of contents?” Window.

    Step 12: You’re done.
