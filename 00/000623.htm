john korchok say:

Here's a potential fix, BUT don't try this if your headings use multilevel list numbering, this will mess that up. They should be plain unnumbered headings.

The principle behind this is that heading styles that are set to a Body Text outline level do not display in the Nav pane. But Word 2013 won't let you change the outline level of styles that are already in use in the document, so this change is easier to implement in the template that is attached to the document. This technique also assumes your document styles are based on your template. Since you will have to update your document styles, your document appearance could change if your styles or local formatting are different than your template.

You can find which template is attached:

  1. In Word, click on FILE, then on Options.
  2. Click on Add-ins.
  3. Change the Manage dropdown to Templates, then click on Go...
  4. The Document template field displays the attached template. Make a note of the name and path.
  5. Check Automatically update document styles (This is important so the template style changes ripple through to the document automatically).

If this is the Normal template, then customizing the outline levels will affect all future documents, not just this one. If it's a dedicated template that's just for this document, no problem.

  1. Close your document and open the template.
  2. If the template is Normal, open it by pressing Alt + F11 to open the VBA editor.
  3. Press Gtrl + G to open the Immediate window at the bottom.
  4. Paste in application.NormalTemplate.OpenAsDocument in the Immediate window and press Enter. Word opens the template.
  5. Close the VBA editor and return to the Word window.
  6. Press Alt + Ctrl + Shift + S to open the Styles list.
  7. Beside Heading 4, click on the down-pointing arrowhead and choose Modify.
  8. Click on the Format button, then on Paragraph.
  9. Change the Outline level dropdown from Level 4 to Body Text. OK out.
  10. Repeat steps 7-10 for Headings 5 and 6.
  11. OK out, save the template and close it.
  12. Open your document, then the Nav Pane. Only Headings 1-3 should display.

stefan blom say:
Note that Word won't let you change the Outline level property for the built-in heading styles.

john korchok say:

If you set the level to Body Text, close, then reopen the style paragraph settings, I believe Word resets it back to the preset level. However, if you set it to Body Text, close it and then follow the procedure, only Headings 1-3 display by default. You can still force the display of 4-6 by clicking the white arrowhead beside Heading 3.

Whatever the explanation, it's different than the stock behavior of Word in a useful way. Setting the outline level is having a noticeable effect on the Navigation Pane display.

If using built-in styles is out, you can create duplicates of 4-6 with slightly different names and set to Body Text level, then use Find and Replace to change the styles attached to those headings. This also displays only Headings 1-3 in the Nav Pane, but you lose the option to force the display of 4-6.
