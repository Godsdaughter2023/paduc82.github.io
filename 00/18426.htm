The spreadsheet was probably originally created in a "reduced functionality" version of Excel....

(like the online version, or the "Docs" version, etc... )

It is VERY SIMPLE to "un-grey" the dimmed buttons in these spreadsheets.

With the spreadsheet open, click ALT + F11 to bring up the VBA editor.

On the left side, you'll should see the Project Explorer window... it looks like an outline with the top level entries in bold.  (If it's not visible, press CTRL + F4 to make it appear).

Among the bold lines, find the name of your spreadsheet. 

It will be preceded by "VBAProject" and enclosed in parenthesis.

Click the "+" sign to the left of your filename to expand the child entries - do this until you see "This Workbook."

Click on "This Workbook" and you should see a large list appear in the properties box below.

(If the properties box isn't visible, click F4)

Find "DisplayDrawingObjects" and click on the right side column to activate the drop down for it.

Change this entry to "-4104 - xlDisplayShapes".

Save your change (CTRL+S), close the VBA Editor window (ALT+F4) and whallah!  You're back in bid'nizz!
