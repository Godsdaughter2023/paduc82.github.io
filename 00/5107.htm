cliffgu_msft5984 say:

Hi Jameel,

I’d like to explain that the Office Document Cache is different from the OfficeFileCache.

The former is included in the latter, and the latter consists of the following elements:

Document library files synced, file attachments in SharePoint lists synced, files opened directly from SharePoint Online by Office applications.

Once we sync files to the local folder from OneDrive for Business, it will also sync to the Local OfficeFileCache folder in order to support the features such as co-authoring. That’s why the file cache is growing big. Deleting the cache from the Upload Center settings can only delete the Office Document Cache but not the OfficeFileCache.So you can still find the OfficeFileCache in C:\Users\yourusername\AppData\Local\Microsoft\Office\15.0 (16.0 for Office 2016).

The high storage in the OfficeFileCache may be caused by the big volume of OneDrive for Business caches, so I suggest you clear the OfficeFileCache manually (It's ok if one or two items cannot be deleted).

When you use OneDrive for Business to resync files, the cache files will be reproduced in the OfficeFileCache folder again, so generally deleting the files in the OfficeFileCache folder will not affect your computer.

Thanks,
Cliff

cliffgu_msft5984 say:

Hi Jameel,

Thanks for your updates.

In answer to your questions:
1. No. As I mentioned above, when you use OneDrive for Business to resync files, the cache files will be reproduced in the OfficeFileCache folder, so the files of high volume caches will reappear in OfficeFileCache after you sync again.
2. After you sync the files from your OneDrive for Business library, the OneDrive – company name folder which stores the synced files will be generated under your user folder by default if you don’t change the saving location before syncing. If you click Stop syncing a folder and sync files from your OneDrive for Business library, the OneDrive – company name 1 folder will be generated under your user folder. You can remove the previous folder OneDrive – company name or remember to remove the folders before re-syncing to avoid the duplicated folders which might cause confusion. The Unsynced Changes folder is generated in case you have updates that cannot be synced to the server. If you see no files in the folder, you can delete it.
3. If you upload a new file to your OneDrive for Business library, the changes will not take place in the local OneDrive – company name folder after you turn off syncing. In other words, the new file will not appear in the local OneDrive – company name folder. The synced files and folder will remain locally but if you modify the file in the local folder, the changes will not take place in your OneDrive for Business library likewise.
