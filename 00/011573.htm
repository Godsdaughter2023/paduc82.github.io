rohn007 say:
Same message as Sri's, just different words.

The Office activation system does not include any way of "deactivating" an installation.

If you have a "retail" / FPP / "2-computer" license, the MS License Terms allow you to transfer your installation(s) to new computers. Part of the terms is that you disable the current installation, typically by uninstalling Office. However, a "dead" computer or HD also meets that requirement.

If you have "used" all of your allowed concurrent installations (3 for Home and Student, 2 of Home and Business or Pro), you still can do the transfer. 

Install Office on the new computer and Activate it.  Try internet activation first. If you get an error to the effect of "too many activations", then you can do a Phone activation.  When you phone in, don't answer any of the prompts until you are given the option of talking to a "service" rep. Explain that you are transferring an installation from a "dead" computer. They should allow the activation to complete.

Note: if you bought Office installed and activated at the factory, an OEM license, or a "Medialess License Kit" license it is only good for install on the first computer. Those types of licenses do NOT allow transfers.

1. Messages were manually deleted either due to accidental deletion or were moved or deleted due to your account filter settings.

3. You are using an e-mail client or a third party spam filtering software to access your account.
This often happens when using the Pop3 protocol to retrieve your mail and the option “Always leave a copy of the message on the server” is not selected.

4. The account was marked as “Inactive." Accounts become inactive if you don’t sign in via web browser at least once a year.

5. If the feature Conversation Threading feature is turned on.

6. If you use Sweep to schedule a cleanup.

7. If you rename your account to @Outlook.com a prompt would have asked you – ‘Where do you want to save your emails?’ You will have the option to create a new folder for your old account or save the emails in the Inbox of your renamed account.
