Your following lines of code sets both customerRng and itemRng to exactly the same range.

Set customerRng = wsCustomerList.Range("A1:A" & LastRow(wsCustomerList))

For Each itemRow In customerRng.Rows

You need to determine the start and end of the range that is to be assigned to itemRng immediately before starting the nested loop.

stunn. say:

Are you looking for formulas for polar co-ordinate conversions for calculating bearings?

Assuming X in A1 and Y in B1:

Distance:  =SQRT(A1^2+B1^2)

Bearing (radians):  =ATAN(B1/A1)

Bearing (degrees):  =DEGREES(ATAN(B1/A1))

If you need to find X and Y from the distance (in A2) and radian bearing (in B2):

X:  =A2*COS(B2)

Y:  =A2*SIN(B2)

and if the bearing is in degrees:

X:  =A2*COS(RADIANS(B2))

Y:  =A2*COS(RADIANS(B2))

HTH

sockatume---01 say: This behavior is by design. The autofocus is activated by touching the optical navi key, and the photo is taken by clicking the key. It could, in principle, scroll through the "sidebar" as well as focus, but it would mean that:

1) It scrolls through the sidebar options when you try to focus
2) It tries to focus every time you scroll through the sidebar options

Neither of which is desirable.

There's no "half-press" switch in the Navi key, so there's no way they can do traditional half-press autofocus.
