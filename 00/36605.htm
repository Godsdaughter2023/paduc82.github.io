    lemp say:

    1.  Save the files in the "Public" folders.  Files saved in "Public" folders are accessible to all users on a Windows 7 computer, even if they are not "Administrator" users (users who are "Administrators" have access to all files on the computer).

    2.  You can also explicitly share files or folders between users on the same computer (or on a local network).

    To share a file or a folder (which will share all files in the folder), right-click on the item and select "Share with."  You can also click on a file or folder to select it and then click the "Share with" button on the tool bar at the top of the window. 


    In either case, select "Specific people."  On the next screen, click the down-arrow and select a name from the list of users on your computer (or select "Everyone") and then click the "Add" button.

    Click the arrow next to the name in the "Permission Level" column and select "Read", "Read/Write", or "Remove" as desired.  Then click the "Share" button.

    newawryzen say:

    Just in case someone stumbles across this thread and tries these suggestions, let me say that all suggestions were valid and valuable.  I bricked my system because I failed to read the instructions when using the "driver verifier" utility - and neglected to setup my system to boot into safe mode before starting.  I saw where it might be necessary to boot into safe mode - but I "assumed" entering safe mode on a Win10 system would be like every Win OS before it.  Not so.

    In the end, ALL my problems were the direct result of a failure of the 8GB DIMM in bank 0.  After bricking my system, I booted to my Win10 clone drive.  Everything seemed fine, except that periodically I would get a message saying that the drive contained errors.  At that point, I decided to run the "memtest86" test as suggested earlier.  It returned errors immediately - and reported over 1200 errors in the first 15 minutes.  I have 2 x 8GB DIMMs on my system.  I removed the DIMM from bank 1 and ran the test again.  Same errors.  I swapped the two DIMMs and rant the test again without errors for 30 minutes.  Problem solved.  I should have guessed that it was memory-related issues all along (system crashing sporadically; can't restore images because they are being reported as corrupt; drives now reporting errors, etc.).  Once running on just the single 8GB DIMM - all these problems disappeared.

    In hindsight, it was a blessing in disguise.  My initial Win10 Pro build was an upgrade to an existing Win7 Pro system - that was a few years old, and had seen its share of program installs/deinstalls, and everything else that tends to leave crumbs all over the place.  After fully rebuilding my system, I reclaimed 31 GB of space on the system volume.  That equates to 31 GB of useless junk (and yes, I did delete windows.old and all but the most current restore point on that old volume).  Maybe most of it was error logs! :)   In any event, the rebuild is working flawlessly.  This machine has NEVER run this well.  Soon I will get back the replacement 8GB DIMM from Crucial, and I can pretend that this never happened.  I've had PC's since 1990, and this is my first-ever encounter with a memory failure.  I've seen everything else, and this one caught me by surprise.  Everything makes sense now.

    Thank you ZigZag for all your help!
