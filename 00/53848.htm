2. Restart the console

Steps for unreadable disk:

Make sure the disks are clean from dust and dirt. If they appear dirty or spotty clean them with a soft cloth.

Clear your system Cache and try to load the game again - support.xbox.com/.../clear-system-cache

Try removing your hard drive while the console is off then start it again and try to play - if this works let me know more steps are needed.

Check for tray errors-Press the open tray button and watch the tray if it opens about half way then retracts or acts weird then this may be a hardware issue.

Check to see if the console is overheating. Feel the top of the console as you play and see if it gets hot to the touch. Over heating does not always result in a red light error and has been know to cause disk read errors.

If you get the open tray error let us know there are many "fixes" but the most reliable resolution is replacement.

Hope this helps.

paulo reichert say:

The first thing to accept is that your battery has a useful life that is ticking, no matter what you do. There are things you can do that will accelerate the deterioration of your battery capacity but generally, you should just use the computer as normal as per your needs.

The main recommendations I have are:

  1. Use the device as normal and do not worry about fully discharging or re-charging the battery regularly. Modern Li-Ion batteries have smart controllers that will normalize the use of the cells so to not use always the same and burn them quicker.
  2. The sweet spot is as you mention, to discharge the battery down to something in between 50% - 80% and recharge it from there.
  3. Keeping your device on charger all the time, without allowing it to discharge, will eventually burn your battery. I had this problem with a MacBook Pro that I seldom used unplugged and after a couple of years, having done 60 charge cycles, the battery was bust.
  4. Also do not let your device run out of charge and leave it for too long as the battery may go on a deep discharge that you can't recover from. If you're storing the device for a prolonged period of time you should charge the battery to roughly 50% and store it like that.

You can keep an eye on the state of your battery by running battery reports regularly. To do that open an admin command prompt and type:

powercfg /batteryreport
