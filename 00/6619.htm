fetaa say:

Hi Kannan,

 

This message is telling you that there may be both secure and non-secure content on the page. Secure and non-secure content, or mixed content, means that a webpage is trying to display elements using both secure (HTTPS/SSL) and non-secure (HTTP) web server connections. This often happens with online stores or financial sites that display images, banners, or scripts that are coming from a server that is not secured. The risk of displaying mixed content is that a non-secure webpage or script might be able to access information from the secure content.

 

To disable it:

Start Internet Explorer.>On the Tool menu, click Internet Options.>Click the Security tab, and then click Custom level.>In the Settings box, scroll down to the Miscellaneous section, and under Display mixed content choose Enable.

 

Note: Internet Explorer blocks non-secure content to keep your information safe and is set to Prompt by default. When this setting is set to Enable, Internet Explorer does not prompt you with the "Only secure content is displayed" message even if the webpage is using non-secure elements.

As Gordon pointed out, chances are you are using the wrong product key. Here is the standard MS response to your problem:

Information about Office 2010 Product Keys -http://support.microsoft.com/kb/2002262 "This is not a valid Office product key. Verify that you have the correct key and retype it."

This behaviour occurs for one or more of the following reasons.:

  • Check the numbers and letters on the product key. Sometimes the number eight (8) can resemble a capitalized B, and a number zero (0) can resemble a capitalized O. Copy the product key from an email message to avoid typing errors.
  • Make sure that you are not using a product key from an older version of Office. These product keys will not work with Office 2010.
  • Do not use a product key that might be attached to the bottom of your laptop, or on the back of your desktop computer. These are usually the Microsoft Windows product keys and will not work with Office 2010.
  • If a Product Key Card was purchased at a retail store and activated at the register, do not use the key that is inside the card to unlock Office 2010. You must logon to the website listed on the card and follow the instructions to obtain the actual product key for the version of Office that you purchased.
  • A product key for an Office 2010 stand-alone product (such as Microsoft Visio Professional 2010) will not be accepted as a product key for an Office 2010 suite. It is important to match the product key to the product for which it is specified.
  • Volume License product keys will not be accepted for a retail Office 2010 product.

 

Office “Came Installed” on computer

If “Office” came installed on a new computer it is either the "Starter" version which consists of feature limited Word and Excel, or a time limited trial of one of the Office versions with more of the Office suite of applications.

Starter is yours to “keep” as long as you have this computer. It may NOT be transferred to a new computer.

Trial versions may require Product Keys to activate them. The sticker on the bottom of the laptop/computer is rarely for Office. If you look closely, you will see that it only mentions a Windows version, nothing about Office. You can get a trial version and Product Key by going to this site:https://office.microsoft.com/en-us/try/try-office-2010-FX101868838.aspx

- which version of office you want:https://secure.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/wiki/Microsoft_Office_2010

- how you will buy the Product Key used to activate the license. 

There are two types of product key you can buy:

Product Key Card vs “Traditional” Retail disk -http://www.microsoft.com/oem/en/products/office/Pages/office_2010_sell.aspx
