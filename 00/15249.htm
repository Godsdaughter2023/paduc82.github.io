How do I fix my audio notes on Microsoft Word 2011? - Microsoft Community

geeeenaa say:

How do I fix my audio notes on Microsoft Word 2011?

I record notes on microsoft word 2011 on my macbook pro. However, when I play back the recordings, it plays a static I record notes on microsoft word 2011 on my macbook pro. However, when I play back the recordings, it plays a static noise throughout the whole audio recording. I think this started happening when I changed the audio recording settings to custom. But, this problem still occurs after I switched back the settings to high quality.

Does anyone know how to fix this? 

john mcghie say:
Basically, you can't fix an existing recording.

To fix new recordings, plug in a higher quality microphone, or get closer to your subject.  Keep your mobile phone or any other mobile device at least a metre away from the computer and microphone.

Then again, it may be that you are trying to play AIFF/sowt back on a device that does not recognise it correctly?  AIFF/sowt is a new version of AIFF quietly slipped in by Apple in OS X.  It has a reversed byte order (little-endian) and some older devices will play it back as static.

It may also be that your sampling rate is too low for what you are trying to record.  I would set the output to MP4 and use at least 16 khz (telephone quality) for speech, 32 khz (AM radio quality) for pop music, or 48 khz (CD quality) for live performances.

I would stay clear of AIFF: It will produce a file of about 10 MB per 60 seconds: a bit greedy!

Hope this helps

stefan blom say:
Usually, when the \t switch doesn't work, it is because the list separator is something other than you expect (you define the list separator in the regional settings in Control Panel).
