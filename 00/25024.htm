Drop Cap causing a break in the paragraph - Microsoft Community

evangelinecolbert say:

Drop Cap causing a break in the paragraph

When I apply a drop cap, why is half the text in that paragraph moving to the next page ?
This happen only on certain pages within my document. On other pages, the paragraph stays intact.
Thanks for any suggestions!
john mcghie say:
Hi Evangeline:

Because when you added the drop-cap, you increased the amount of vertical space the paragraph occupied. Word moved part of it to the next page to make room.

By default, Word permits paragraphs to break across pages.  You can disable this, by setting the "Keep Lines Together" property in the paragraph properties.  For keep lines together to work, you must turn "Widow/Orphan" off on the same dialog: they are mutually exclusive.

You can (and I always do...) set "Keep Lines Together" in your style, so none of the paragraphs are split at the bottom of a page, drop-cap or no.  Readers hate chasing paragraphs to the next page, and paper is not that expensive these days.

Word is not actually "breaking" the paragraph, it is simply "flowing" the lines that won't fit to the next page.

Hope this helps
