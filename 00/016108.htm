daniel g. grau say:

Got your file, thanks. It is a Unicode UTF-8-encoded file with Unix paragraph breaks.

Word can read it, but messes up the smart single quotes, whether you open it as Text Only or Unicode 6.1 UTF-8. In both instances, you'll need to replace the ‚Äô sequences with an apostrophe. Or you can download TextWrangler (a free text-only editor), which I've yet to see fail to interpret a file's encoding.

From TextWrangler, you can:

  • Select all text and paste into Word, or
  • Do a Save As after changing the options to "Western Mac OS (Roman)" and "Classic Mac (CR)" at the bottom of the document's window

Back to Word. Paragraph breaks are, as mentioned, Unix style, meaning ASCII 10 (line feed). Thus, they cannot be found if you look for ASCII 13 or ^13 as we were doing. Neither can they be found with ^10, as Word (in its wisdom) won't allow it.

Solution 1

I did a global replacement (86 instances) using no wildcards, from ^p to ^p (this effective changes the paragraph breaks from LFs to CRs, which is what we need on the Mac). After that, the replacement with wildcards I gave before:

([!.^13])^13([!^13])

to:

\1 \2

worked fine. It eliminated all CRs except after a period. A final touch would be to restore the single quotations and probably add Space Before/After as a paragraph attribute. Or replace ^p by ^p^p.

Solution 2

Word won't let you look for line feeds, but (in its wisdom) it will find them if they are included within a range of values. So we can look for the range ASCII 9 to ASCII 13 instead.

Search for:

([!.^9-^13])[^9-^13]([!^9-^13])

Replace by:

\1 \2

Try that, it works here.

Windows 10 says that the selected language will be the display language after the next sign-in. Sign out from Windows 10, and sign in again with your user account. Windows 10 is now in the language that you have chosen.

3.

Click on Accounts.
Click on Sync your settings.
Turn off the Language preferences toggle switch.

In OneDrive for business, to remove the permission and to see with who you’ve shared the folder, please follow the steps below:

  3. Click the  Information panel icon.
