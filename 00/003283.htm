daniel g. grau say:
Word offers Sections, which among other things let you specify different headers/footers and number of columns within the same document. By default, a document has only one section.

Sections are inserted thorough the main menu bar's Insert > Break option and they can cause a page break or be continuous. The latter is what you need.

Once you have inserted your continuous section breaks, you can protect your document at the section level. In other words, the protection can be applied to just one of those sections.

It will be easier to work if you enable the display of non-printing characters by clicking on the ¶ button in the Standard toolbar.

Sub DeleteRows_With_Word()
Dim lRow As Long
lRow = Cells(Rows.Count, "A").End(xlUp).Row
    FindString = "word"
    Set b = Range("1:" & lRow).Find(What:=FindString, lookat:=xlWhole)
    While Not (b Is Nothing)
        b.EntireRow.Delete
        Set b = Range("1:" & lRow).Find(What:=FindString, lookat:=xlWhole)
    Wend
End Sub
