john - project say:
FairytaleGardens,
The duration of a summary task has nothing to do with the duration of the subtasks (i.e. it is NOT additive). The duration of any task is the difference in working time between the start of the task and the finish of the task. For a summary task, the duration is the difference in working time between the start of the earliest subtask and the finish of the latest subtask.

In your example although each subtask is one full day duration, the predecessor tasks apparently are pushing the start dates of one or more subtasks out and furthermore, at least one of those predecessors must finish somewhere in the middle of the day (e.g. 2:02 PM maybe).

However, just for reference, the Work field is additive. Work at summary level is equal to the sum of work of all subtasks. Is that perhaps what you are thinking?
