scottiem say:

I have managed to (finally) solve the the problem myself.

The issue was related to the Network and sharing centre not  properly reporting that I was connected to a network, the permissions were wrong in the Network List hierarchy.

To fix,

Press Windows and R keys and run 'regedit'

find 'HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SOFTWARE\Microsoft\Windows NT\CurrentVersion\NetworkList'

Right Click on NetworkList and choose Permission. Check  that Full control is ticked for my user account (it wasn't). Administrator should also have full control.

Click on Advanced and check the box 'replace all child object permissions with inheritable permissions from this object',  and Apply and then OK.

Worked fine after that but took a couple of minutes, a restart probably wouldn't have hurt.
