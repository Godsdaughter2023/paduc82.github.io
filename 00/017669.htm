I agree with Alex.  Let me add some points though.   Use a belts and suspenders approach:
  • Find a source of antivirus reviews that you trust, and read those.  Be sure you compare like versions though (not Avast 2012 vs. MSE 2011 and so on.)
  • Back up your chosen real time antivirus with an on demand scanner.  My personal favorite is the free edition (only!  the paid version is real-time) of Malwarebytes Antimalware.  Run the on demand scanner every 2 or 3 or 4 weeks at full scan mode.  Never install more than one real time antivirus at a time though. NEVER.
  • Make sure you have an effective firewall (windows firewall in vista or 7 is excellent.)  In XP the firewall is pretty good... see bullet point one, and do the same for firewalls if you feel the need.
  • If you have a home router, use it. At the very least, your PC is protected by being behind NAT (network address translation... so your PC IP address is not public... your router's is, and this is pretty durn good protection.  If your router is moderately modern, it also has SPI - a kind of router firewall that is very very good.  But, continue to use the software firewall on your PC as well.)
  • Use safe surfing.  Avoid P2P sites (notorious for malware distribution.)  Do not click on any links in spam.  Avoid suspicious sites (many search engines will provide this type of warning.)  Be suspicious of 'strange' links in email even from friends... email is easy to spoof, so don't get fooled.
  • Consider Secunia PSI (free for home use) which scans your PC on demand for out of date software that is vulnerable to attacks and can update it, or at least tell you that it needs an update.  
  • Consider spywareblaster, which blocks registry attacks.  also free for home use.
  • Enough?  Remember... only the paranoid survive!

We all know Windows looks pretty good right out of the box, with its clean system tray and all, but once you install a few 3rd party programs, along comes their system tray apps which eventually clutters up the menu box.
