Lock vlookup in Excel 2010

I have set up a sheet with a vlookup in which a seniority number is entered in a cell and the name shows in the cell next to it. I have found that often someone enters the wrong number, hits tab then realizes the mistake. Once in the next cell they hit delete thereby deleting the vlookup formula rather than shift tab and entering the correct number. This then takes a ctrl z and a shift tab to correct the error and seems to confuse the person entering the data.

Can I somehow lock the cells that contain the vlookup formula so the looked up value shows but if the delete key is hit the formula is not deleted so I can avoid the ctrl z step?

vijay a. verma say:
1. CTRL+A 3 times to select entire worksheet or hit the triangle between column A and Row 1
2. Right Click > Format Cells > Protection tab > Uncheck Locked
3. Select the cell which you want to lock and Right Click > Format Cells > Protection tab > Check Locked
4. Review tab > Protect sheet > Protect with Password (you can also uncheck "Select Locked Cells" so that user will not be able to go to this cell, at all)

steve earp say: Hi Toto,

I spent a few days recently going through the same issues, but managed to work it out with the IT guys that look after the small business I work for. Steps of success were:

  • Use Internet Explorer to navigate to the address of the site (mail.xxxx.com.au rather than the IP address, as I tried both options);
  • Export Certificate in .p7b format using the Certificate export wizard (.p7b format is native to IE, and is compatible with the WP7 software system;
  • Email it as per previous;
At this point my issue changed from the 80072F0D to the 80072F06, which was a good sign.


I set the phone up with the following information and it worked:

  • Username: Tthis was NOT the username I used to sign in to my desktop, rather the name of my account (for example, for Joe Bloggs: I would sign in with "jbloggs", but the account name on the exchange server is "joe.bloggs").
  • Password: same for your account.
  • Domain: this is the only tricky part you'll need to sort out with your admin - for me it was a shortened domain name three letters long (e.g. "xyz"), however with my limited knowledge, you may need to check this out, as it was the last hurdle.
  • Server: mail.XXXXXXX.com.au (where XXXX is your company name on your mail exchange, and .au only applies to my case because it's an Australian company.
  • Server requires encrypted SSL connection?: Yes
  • Logging: Off (Recommended)
I hope this helped, because the discussions in this thread have helped me in solving my issue.

Also - realising that you solved it in your November 27 post, this is more for other people - I didn't need to instally all free certificates from other providers, this worked with the certificate from my small business exchange only.

Cheers,

Steve
