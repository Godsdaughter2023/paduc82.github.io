The discounted payback period uses WACC or weighted average cost of capital as the rate for discounting the cash flows.

WACC has three or sometimes four components

  1. Cost of Debt
  2. Cost of Preferred Stock
  3. Cost of Common Stock
  4. Cost of Retained Earnings
WACC is the sum of these costs times the percent contribution of each component as the weight

I do have a spreadsheet that finds discounted payback period in excel but it would require knowing the WACC as the discount rate

roady [mvp] say:

File-> Options-> Trust Center-> Attachment Handling-> disable: Add properties to attachments to enable Reply with Changes (disabled by default)

All other functionality of tracking changes for Word documents is controlled by Word itself and not Outlook. The same goes for any formatting changes that you encounter.
