scottgem say:

You need to understand how Access stores Date/Time values. They are stored as a Double Precision number where the Integer portion is the number of days since 12/30/1899 and the decimal portion is a fraction of a day (so .25=6AM)

So when you total time values that exceed 24 hours the time spills into the integer portion. So if you get a total of 30 hours you get 1.25. If you format that to display a time value it will only show 6 AM.

So you either need to display the time as a decimal or you need to convert the result.

If you use DateDiff("n",[StartTime],[EndTime]) you will get the difference in Minutes. You can then parse out the total hours by dividing by 60 and the remaining Minutes using the MOD operator. You can then display this result as a text string using an expression like hours & ":" & Minutes. If you search you can probably find a function that will do it for you.

Welcome to Microsoft Community! Let me assist you regarding your concern not being able to receive emails.

 

Please try to check the following settings if you are having issues receiving emails.

 

A. Email Forwarding

  1. On your inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.
  2. Under Managing your account, click Email forwarding.

Note: If it's set to Forward your mail to another email account, tick on Don't forward then click Save.

 

B.  Blocked Sender's List

  1. On your inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.
  2. under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.
  3. Click Blocked senders.
  4. In the Blocked senders list, select the name or domain (the part of an e-mail address that follows the @ sign, such as hotmail.com) that you now want to receive mail from, and then click<< Remove from list.

 

C. Add the email address to your Safe senders list

  1. On your inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.
  2. Under Preventing junk email, click Safe and blocked senders.
  3. Click Safe Senders.
  4. In the text box, type in the email address or domain you’d like to always receive email from, and then clickAdd to list >>.

 

D. Check your Email Rules

  1. On your inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.
  2. Under Customizing Outlook, click Rules for sorting new messages.
  3. Perform one of the actions as per your requirement:
    Click     Edit next to the rule that you want to edit.
    Or
    Choose the rule that you want to delete and click     Delete.
    Note: Not all international characters are supported as filters.

 

E. Check your Junk Mail Settings

  1. On your inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.
  2. Under Preventing junk email, click Filters and reporting.
  3. Under Choose a junk e-mail filter, choose the level of protection that you want, and then click Save. If the Exclusive level was set, messages from senders not on your Contacts or Safe senders lists will have been sent to the Junk e-mail folder.

 

F. Check your Reply-to address

  1. On your inbox, click the gear icon and select More mail settings on the dropdown list.
  2. Under Writing email, click Reply-to address.
  3. Select Current e-mail address radio button.
  4. Click Save.

 

Hope this resolved your concern. Please do not hesitate to contact us again if you have other concern.

That's by design. Has been like that for several versions. You can think of it as "field names are case insensitive", so a change from "FieldName" to "fieldname" is not seen as a change. This also explains why two field names have to be more different than by case alone.

Fix as follows: "FieldName" > "ffieldname" > save > "fieldname" > save.
