Per the Technical Information tab of Win32/Emotet:

http://www.microsoft.com/security/portal/threat/encyclopedia/entry.aspx?Name=Win32/Emotet#tab=2

"Depending on the platform it's running on, it will inject a DLL (x86 or x64) from the original dropper into explorer.exe."

"It also injects the payload DLL (x86 or x64) in all running processes. It create two mutexes that are called <PID>M and <PID>I, for example 6A0M or 6A0I, to signal this."

Though other specifics of the Trojan:Win32/Emotet.G variant are likely different, the above 2 processes are likely to both still exist with potentially different values for the mutexes, since these methods are commonly used by malware to re-infect.
