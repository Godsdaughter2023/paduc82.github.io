Anastasia,

Just to add to Bill's advice & hopefully to clarify matters -

- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -

There is no download of Windows 7 available for you. 

The message you got [When I'm trying to get Windows ...] was because you tried to download from a webpage that is only intended for those holding "Retail" licences for Windows 7.  It does not mean that your product key "doesn't work" but merely that it was not a qualifying product key for what you were trying to do on that webpage.  So, please forget that webpage and its message as they might lead to further misunderstandings during your attempts to sort things out.

Your call to Dell also suffered from the same misunderstanding.  You told them that your product key "doesn't work" and they quite correctly said that was a Microsoft issue not a Dell issue.  But it was not correct of you to say that your product key "doesn't work".  So, please forget that call & Dell's response as they might lead to further misunderstandings during your attempts to sort things out.

There is nothing wrong with your product key and hence no need for you to investigate ways "to update the product key".

- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -

You have lost the most convenient method of reinstalling Windows as there was a "factory restore partition" on your defective hard drive that was specifically put there to reinstall Windows.  There was a means of backing up that partition to a USB using Dell DataSafe Backup Local but you obviously did not do so or you'd be asking different questions.

- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -

Your only options are:

1  Pay Dell for a Dell-branded Windows 7 Reinstallation DVD for your Edition [Home Premium etc] and your Type [32 / 64 bit].  You might also be able to specify that you want it for your particular model of computer so it has all the drivers you need but I don't think Dell get that specific.  You can check the Edition as it's on the label that shows your product key.  Your Type [32 / 64 bit] depends on your computer model so you can ask Dell or check on their support website for this information if necessary.  Dell will charge a fee for this DVD. 

2  Borrow a Dell-branded Windows 7 Installation DVD for your Edition [Home Premium etc] and your Type [32 / 64 bit] from somebody.  It will probably "self-activate" and you will therefore not even need the product key after all - so just skip that dialog during the installation and let it try to activate itself.  You can always add the product key in later on through Control panel, [View by - Small icons, if necessary], System - see the Activation status area at the bottom of the window. 

3  Borrow a standard Windows 7 Installation DVD for your Edition [Home Premium etc] and your Type [32 / 64 bit].  You will need to put in the product key to activate it.  You can do that during installation or after installation has finished.

- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -

Afterwards -

When you have completed installation and activation, you will probably find that not all hardware devices are as good as they used to be.  You will need to use your Dell Service Tag on their website's support area to identify & download the appropriate drivers for your hardware.  Doing so is straightforward but will probably take some time - you can make it less of a burden by not getting drivers for hardware that seems to be installed correctly & seems to be behaving correctly - you can always go back later for more as long as you take due notice of their advice about which sequence to use when installing drivers as some of them need to be installed before others [they have a help page explaining all this but I thought it was important to point it out to you now]. 

You will also need to run through all Windows updates issued since the [re]installation DVD was made.  Control panel, [View by - Small icons, if necessary], Windows update.  This can take a long time.

Best of luck,
