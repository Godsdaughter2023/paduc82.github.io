john mcghie say:
Certainly "try" the Reset Toolbars routine.  However, usually when the Normal template corrupts badly enough to crash Word, the Normal template is cactus and needs to be deleted.

Rich's point is very important:  Create your OWN toolbar, and customise that.  Customising the built-in toolbars is asking for trouble, and you've got it.

Word can have "several" toolbar stores in play (usually has...).  Unless you understand "Current Context" it is easy to make a change to the Formatting toolbar in the normal template, a different change in the Attached template, and a third change in the document itself.  When Word comes to try to merge the three conflicting templates, it goes "bang".

When you attempt to use Organiser to copy toolbars from one place to another, you will find Word won't allow you to copy the built-ins.  That's a protective mechanism to attempt to avoid this very result.  But it means that whenever a template corrupts (and if you do a lot of customisation, that will be "quite often") you lose all your customisations.

If you create your own toolbar, you can re-name the old template out of the way, allow Word to create a new one, then use Organiser to copy the custom toolbar from the old to the new.  Generally you will get back all your customisations and macros undamaged.

I format documents only with styles, so I do a lot of this :-)  You're on the right track.  Just keep your eye on the customisation context, in other words, take care that you know WHICH file you are saving the latest customisation to.

Hope this helps

notfinished say:

I would just like to expand on point number 5 on "Changing the amount of silence between songs"

With Windows Media Player open,  click on "Tools" then click on "options" and then click on the "Burn" tab.

The drop down pane will show "Burn CD without gaps" with a check mark.  This is Windows Media Player 12 default position. If you chose to remove the check mark,  Windows Media Player 12 will add a 2 second gap between songs burned.

From my experience, if you are burning music that already have gaps built into the song files, Windows Media Player 12 will add an additional 2 seconds to the silence gaps you already have on your songs.

These appear to be the only two options relating to the amount of silence between songs that Windows Media Player 12 offers when burning music CDs.

As an aside, this drop down pane also contains the adjustment to burn speed option.  Windows Media Player 12, defaults to the "Fastest" speed but you can choose other burn speeds down to the "Slowest".

formula to convert cms to ft & Inches in excel - Microsoft Community

michael qc say:

formula to convert cms to ft & Inches in excel

I have colums of measurements in centimetres and want to convert them to feet and inches to the nearest 1/8 of an inch. 
michael qc say:
If you want FEET AND INCHES (which neither of the other solutions address), one way:

Assume that your cm values are in column A, cells A1...Axxx.

In B1, enter

    =INT(ROUND(A1 / 2.54 *8, 0) / 96)

to get feet. In an adjacent column, enter

    =ROUND((A1 / 2.54 - B1 * 12) * 8,0) / 8

to get inches to the nearest 1/8". Copy B1:C1 down as far as required.

Thank you this has worked part of the way. However it leaves me with a decimal part of an inch where I was hoping to end up with the nearest 1/8.

j.e. mcgimpsey say:

It absolutely leaves you with the nearest 1/8" (but as you say, in decimal format). To see fractions, format your values to show fractions (Format/Cells/Number...)

You could combine the two calculations as Text, but you wouldn't be able to do calculations on them, i.e.:

   =INT(ROUND(A1 / 2.54 * 8,0) / 96) & "' " & TEXT(ROUND((A1 / 2.54 -INT(ROUND(A1 / 2.54 * 8, 0) / 96) * 12) * 8, 0) / 8, "0 0/8\""")

which can obviously be simplified, but I'll leave that as an exercise...
