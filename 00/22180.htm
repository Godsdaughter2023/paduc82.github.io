How can I use Out of Office Assistant and not reply to the listservs I - Microsoft Community

romain walker say:

How can I use Out of Office Assistant and not reply to the listservs I belong to?

Does anyone have an easy way to do this?
brian tillman [outlook mvp 2007-2019] say:
Use a rule to the Out of Office Assistant that sends replies only if the sender does not match the address of your mailing list. Open the OOA. Select Send automatic replies and compose your reply message. At the bottom should be a button that says Rules. Click it, then click Add Rule. In the From field, put the listserv sender addresses separated by semicolons. Click Advanced. Check the Only items that do not match these conditions. Click OK. Now select one of the actions. (You must have an action.) I usually choose Alert with and specify an action of Play, chosing some sound file on my PC. You could also choose the Move to action and specify Inbox as the destination folder. Click OK, then OK again, then OK once more. Now the OOA will send the reply to any sender except for the listserv addresses.

Excel formula, remove QTY , from another sheet - Microsoft Community

armspecial say:

Excel formula, remove QTY , from another sheet

I have two Sheets.

Sheet 1 name is 01.09.2017. 
Sheet 2 name is rest

In sheet 1 i have 3 columns

name   QTY     price    together
asd          2      1000      2000   

in rest i have 2 columns

name   QTY
asd        50

i want to remove from rest QTY as many QTY as are in sheet 1. How can i write the formula ?

hansv mvp say:

You cannot use a formula in the QTY column on rest to subtract a value from QTY itself, it would lead to a circular reference. You could use a formula in a third column, for example in C2

=B2-SUMIF('01.09.2017'!$A$2:$A$1000,A2,'01.09.2017'!$B$2:$B$1000)
