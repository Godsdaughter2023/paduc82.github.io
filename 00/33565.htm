andre da costa say:

Try the following first:

1. Put the Windows 7 installation disc into the disc drive, and then start the computer.

2. Press a key when the message indicating "Press any key to boot from CD or DVD …". appears.

3. Select a language, a time, a currency, and a keyboard or another input method, and then click Next.

4. Click Repair your computer.

5. Click the operating system that you want to repair, and then click Next.

6. In the System Recovery Options dialog box, click Command Prompt.

7. Type sfc /scannow, and then press ENTER.

Boot to the Windows 7 Home Premium desktop

Insert your Windows 7 Home Premium upgrade DVD

Click Install Now

When the setup menu appears, click Upgrade

a. user say:

Hello Juan,

Thank you for posting your concern in Microsoft Community.

Here are some suggestions that may help you remember a forgotten Microsoft account email address.

  • If you used the account to send email, ask someone you sent an email to what address the message came from.
  • If you used the account with Windows Live Messenger, launch Messenger to see if it automatically logs in with your account.  If it does, you can log out and the resulting login screen will display your Microsoft account.
  • You can also ask your contacts to check their Messenger contacts for your email address.
  • If you used another computer in the past to log into your account, on that computer go here, click into the account name box, and push the down arrow on your keyboard.  This may display a list of previous logins and reveal your Microsoft account name.
  • If you have any other paid services associated with your Microsoft account, you can contact that service’s support for assistance in identifying the Microsoft account.

Also, In Windows 7, you can check to see if your email is saved in the Windows credential vault.

To do so, go to Control Panel, User Accounts and click Manage your credentials.  This credential vault can store user names and passwords.  Your email may be listed here if it was ever saved

Another important link to refer to:

Recover your Microsoft account

Hope the above information is helpful. If you need further assistance, we would be happy to help you.
