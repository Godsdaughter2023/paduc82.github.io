aigeec say:

Transcoding with Windows Media Player

diana d say:

Hi Aigeec,

Welcome to Microsoft Answers.

Yes, you will be able to view AVI files on the TV depending upon the compatibility of the TV with Windows 7. It uses a technology called Digital Living Network Alliance (DLNA). Using the TV with DLNA logo, you can browse the media library available on the Windows 7 PC. You can use the TV to watch the video and pictures, and listen to music stored on the PC. Let’s say for instance you have a DivX movie you want to watch on your new DLNA certified television which only supports WMV and MPEG2. Windows 7 will determine the capability of the TV (codec, bit rate, etc.) and dynamically convert the DivX video to a format the TV can play.

More information check the link: http://blogs.msdn.com/e7/archive/2009/05/12/media-streaming-with-windows-7.aspx

Here are the steps to setup streaming:
