considerate_guy say:

I'm assuming these are photos (rather than videos) you are adding to your project? Do you know the exact dimensions of the pictures?

You should choose the aspect ratio of your project to match the aspect ratio of the photos. For example, if the photos were 4000 x 3000, that's an aspect ratio of 4:3. If the photos were 1920 x 1080, that's an aspect ratio of 16:9.

In addition, there is a "feature" in Movie Maker. If the aspect ratio of a photo is close to 3:2, then Movie Maker will perform a small auto-crop to make it fit your project dimensions. This may be what you are seeing, so it's important to know the dimensions of the photos having problems.

One final thing to consider is the playback device. I'm not sure if you are viewing the final movie on a TV monitor or on a computer screen, but video devices (such a TV) will often have an "overscan" region. Overscan is the situation in which not all of a televised image is present on a viewing screen. It exists because television sets from the 1930s through the 1990s were highly variable in how the video image was positioned within the borders of the cathode ray tube (CRT) screen.

You'll still see this overscan situation on older display devices to be compatible with older material that didn't expect the edges of the content to be visible.

So the questions:

  1. What are the exact dimensions of the source photos having issues?
  2. What device are you playing the final movie on where you see the cropping?
