dianeoforegon say: First the .olm file is nothing like the .pst file. It's a one time snapshot/copy of your data. It can only be used my Outlook for Mac 2011 by importing back into Outlook.

Outlook for Mac can import a .pst file and it will show up as a folder with .pst in the name, but it's nothing other than a folder with pst in the name.

The .olm file does not delete the emails from Outlook unless you specifically select to export after deleting.

Your data is stored in your Identity.

You can export your messages as .mbox, .olm and .eml files. These are nothing like a .pst file. You can convert using third party software.

If you delete your Identity, you will delete all data including contacts, calendars, tasks, notes and account. Your Identity is located in your Documents folder.

Documents
   Microsoft User Data folder
     Office 2011 Identities
        Main Identity
          Data Records (categories, contacts, messages, etc.)
          Database (index of your files)


You did not mention what type of account(s)? (POP, IMAP, Exchange) you have but it sounds like you have POP. If you had IMAP or Exchange all messages in all folders would be syncing between your Mac and your PC. IMAP only syncs messages but Exchange syncs all data.

Office365 is a subscription service where you 'rent' Office monthly rather than purchase and own outright a retail version. What I think might be confusing you, is Microsoft's Exchange services that are now available for a monthly fee. You can use this option with either the subscription or retail version.

http://office.microsoft.com/en-us/business/compare-office-365-for-business-plans-FX102918419.aspx

You can use your own domain name with Office 365 Exchange. If you have your own domain name, you can setup as either POP or IMAP. I highly suggest you switch to IMAP. Log into your host to get the info needed to setup your account as IMAP.

See this chart "Compare Sync options for POP, IMAP & Exchange Accounts"

Outlook.com is the new Hotmail. Microsoft just rebranded their old email service.

Outlook is an email client available on both the Mac and PC but other than the name they are very different.

Hope this answers your concerns.
