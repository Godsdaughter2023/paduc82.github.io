You will not be able to find an international version from here! Here's where you need to go beyond the steps of the other two answers.

Now that you have your language as English, return to the Language Preferences page.

Next to English, there should be a little link in blue to the far right titled Options. Click it.

Under Input Method, click Add an input method.

Scroll through the list until you find United States-International (it should be right above the normal US one and below United States DVORAK R if your computer is like mine)

That should activate the international keyboard.

sally777 say:

I didn't need to post onto the technet forum, since I solved the problem. I decided to bite the bullet and just give my idea a try and hope for the best (created a system restore point first). The solution was so freakin' easy...wonder why I couldn't find any details about how to do this...??

Anyway; On the Ultimate box (the one I was trying to remote desktop into), I opened up the management console (mmc.exe) and added the snap-in for certificates, for the computer account, local computer. Double-clicked Certificates, double-clicked the folder for Remote Desktop, double-clicked the folder for Certificates, and highlighted the certificate (there was only one, with the name of the computer as the title), and deleted it (first I copied it and put it into another folder, just in case).

This totally solved the entire issue, and the workaround is not necessary anymore.

I hope this helps someone else.

Sally
