rocco99 say:

After some tweaking, the steps recommended in Article 959625 worked.
1. Article 959625 is described as applying to the following situation: "On a client computer that is running Office Communicator 2007, you may be unable to make a TAPI call from Office Outlook 2007."
2. If I have Office Communicator 2007, I don't know it.
3. This fix also apparently applies to Outlook 2010.
4. The scenario described in Article 959625 is not the only one for which this registry hack applies.  See a description of my problem at the start of the
thread.
5. I started with backing up my registry by creating a restore point as recommended in the Article.  The recommended Article for doing this wasn't for Vista, but the page had a link for Vista.  The Article for Vista listed several methods of backing up registry.
6. Article 959625 discusses installation of hotfix rollup 959642.  I tried this and it would not install. I think it is only is for Outlook 2007. Therefore, this seems to be an unnecessary step for Outlook 2010.
7. Article 959625 then discusses a hack to registry subkey HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\Software\Microsoft\Office\Outlook\Call Integration.  This is the locus of the solution.  However, my system  did not have the "Call Integration" subkey.  Therefore, I created one as Sue suggested -- extremely prescient and a very good point!
8. From there, I followed the instructions of Article 959625:
  "b. Locate and then click the following registry subkey: HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\Software\Microsoft\Office\Outlook\Call Integration
   c. On the Edit menu, point to New, and then click DWORD Value.
   d. Type TAPIOVERRIDE, and then press ENTER:
   e. Right-click TAPIOVERRIDE, and then click Modify.
   f. In the Value data box, type 1, and then click OK.
   g. On the File menu, click Exit to exit Registry Editor."

This was simple to understand and do. Restarted the system, opened Outlook, went to a Contact, clicked "Call" icon, same (or very similar) screen as in Outlook 2007 came up, and I was able to call out.

At last!!!!!!!
