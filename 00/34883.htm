david ziffer say:
So I figured this weird "Sign In" name in the Microsoft game collections (such as my wife's Microsoft Solitaire Collection) and possibly other Microsoft apps. It's a new world folks, and you might as well roll with it because fighting it will just wear you out. Let me tell you in advance that your objective here is NOT to get rid of this login name entirely, but rather to change it to something you want.

When you registered your Windows 8 (including Windows 8 on any version of the Surface tablets) you effectively created a set of accounts on a bunch of Microsoft web sites, including microsoft.com, outlook.com (formerly hotmail), msdn.com (for developers) and xbox.com. You own these accounts whether you realize it or not. They all have the same login ID and password that you specified when you set up Windows 8. Usually your login ID is some email address, such as *** Email address is removed for privacy ***.

When you created your Microsoft account credentials, Microsoft created a full-blown xbox.com account for you, complete with an uninitialized avatar (similar to Wii avatars) and a "gamertag", which is the unfamiliar name you are seeing in your "Signed In" section. Probably for some very good reasons, Micosoft does not use your login (e.g. "JohnDoe") as your "gamertag". One of those good reasons is that your login name might already be in use by someone else. So instead what Microsoft does is create an artificial "gamertag" for you using a phrase generator. The name my wife got was "TwangyMirror76".

When you launch your game collection you are not just opening up a localized game that runs privately on your machine like in the old days. You are instead entering an Xbox portal. When the portal tells you it is "Signing In" it is not using the unfamiliar name that you are complaining about ... rather it is using your real account credentials (e.g. *** Email address is removed for privacy ***) to access xbox.com. Then xbox.com RETURNS the bogus name that you are seeing as your sign-in name, which is just your "gamertag". The gamertag has nothing to do with your real account credentials, which will remain unchanged (and which will allow your game app to continue to log in even if you change the "gamertag").

You cannot get rid of your Microsoft game collection's dependence on xbox.com. All you can do is customize it so that it doesn't irritate you. In this case you want to choose your own "gamertag" that will be displayed as your "Sign In" whenever you are using an xbox-connected application. There are two ways to do this:

1. FROM WITHIN THE GAME ITSELF
Click on the "gamertag" in the "Signed In" area, which opens a new window that takes you to the Xbox site.
On the Xbox site click on the "Signed In' words in the upper right.
Select "Change gamertag" from among the options it presents, which opens a new page.
On this page you have the option to change your "gamertag" once for free.

2. FROM THE XBOX WEB SITE
Go to xbox.com and log in using your real account credentials (not the "gamertag").
Click on your annoying gamertag at the upper right (e.g. "TwangyMirror76")
Click "Edit My Profile"
On this page you have the option to change your "gamertag" once for free.

Using both methods you will need to log out of your app (or on Surface possibly out of the machine) and restart the app before you'll see your new "gamertag" in the "Signed In" section of your games.

It's a brave new world, that has such apps in it. Relax. You'll get through this.

akute say: Figured it out myself!!!

It is possible to sort an artist's albums in release date order in Windows Media Player 11.

Right-click the sorting columns near the top of the screen.

Select "Choose Columns..."

At the bottom of the pop-up option box, click the check box "Include release date in default sort order."

Which leads us to our next problem!

Now the albums are sorted from newest to oldest, as opposed to the desired oldest to newest chronological order.

You may click the "Album" column heading to resort, but Windows Media Player 11 does not put that sorting preference to memory.

Will there be or is there a fix for this?

Edit:

Found my answer for this as well:

http://www.microsoft.com/windows/windowsmedia/player/faq/library.mspx#q1_20

It appears not having WMP11 remember your sorting preference was done intentionally.

This concludes my questions, and in all honesty, feels like a poor choice that I would very much like to see fixed.

Hope this helps someone else in the future.
