install Office 2007 on new laptop?

I purchase Office 2007 online and my laptop has since died.  Have product key (15 digits).  Can I install on my new laptop?  I do not have an installation disc though.  Can this be done or do I have to re-purchase Microsoft Office?
rohn007 say:

Short answer: Yes and No.

Yes, depending on how you bought Office 2007 in the first place, you may have the right to transfer the installation to the new computer.

No, not with a 15 digit "product key". All office product keys are 25 characters long, typically written by MS as 5 groups of 5 char separated by hyphens.

When you bought Office 2007 online, it is most likely that you were given a "Medialess Licesne Kit", MLK type license. This license was sold online without the installation disk.  It was cheaper than the "tradional" boxed CD retail version.  As well as saving money by not including the installation disk, MS saved money by making the license terms more restrictive. This type of license can only be installed on one computer and the installation is permanently tied to that hardware. No transfer allowed.

Bottom line, I don't think you will be able to install on the new machine because you do not have the correct product key and because you probably have a license type that does not allow transfers.

There are many ways a computer can "die". If the old HD is still good, you could get a technician to connect it to another computer, and then run a (free) tool to extract the Office product key from the old HD so you can try to use it to install on the new machine.

PS:

Office 2007 is going "Off support" / "end of life" in October this year. That means MS will no longer provide ANY updates for it. That will make it an ongoing security risk on your computer. This is probably a good time to look for a new version of Office.
