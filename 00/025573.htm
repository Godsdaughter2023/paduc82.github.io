a. user say:

Hi,

Thank you for posting your query on Microsoft Community.

Since the blocking of email from a blocked email account through Sweep doesn't seem to work on your end, you can use the following options:

A. Check you Junk mail filter and make sure that it is in its exclusive state. Here's how:

1. Sign in to your Outlook.com account.

2. You’ll be directed to your Inbox.

3. In the upper right corner, please click the Gear icon and then chooseMore mail settings.

4. You’ll be directed to the Outlook Options page, under Preventing junk email please choose Filters and

Reporting.

5. Under Choose a junk email filter please select Exclusive - Everything is sent to the junk email folder except messages from your contacts and safe senders, Hotmail service announcements, and alerts that you signed up for.

6. Click Save.

B. Create message rules which will apply for incoming emails. Please follow these steps:

1. Sign in to your Outlook.com account.

2. You’ll be directed to your Inbox.

3. In the upper right corner, please click the Gear icon and then chooseMore mail settings.

4. You’ll be directed to the Outlook Options page, under Customizing Hotmail, please choose Rules for sorting new messages.

5. Click New.
6. Choose the condition that you want to apply to the rule inStep 1.
7. Choose the action in Step 2 that you want to take on the emails that meet the condition specified in Step 1.
8. Click Save.

Note: You can use Subject for Step 1 and  Delete these messages—don't even put them in my Deleted folder for Step 2.

C. Adding email addresses to your Blocked sender's list. Aside from using Sweep for blocking emails from a particular sender, you can directly add email address to this list and domains that you want to block. Here are the steps to follow:

1. Sign in to your Outlook.com account.

2. You’ll be directed to your Inbox.

3. In the upper right corner, please click the Gear icon and then chooseMore mail settings.

4. You’ll be directed to the Outlook Options page, under Preventing junk email please choose Safe and Blocked senders list.

5. Click the Blocked senders link.
6. On the box under the Blocked e-mail address or domain: option, type in the e-mail address or domain of the sender.
7. Click the Add to list>> button.

Hope this information was helpful and do let us know if you need further assistance. We will be glad to assist.
