vincentchoy say:

I believed you purchased your Office365 licenses from MetroCSG? 

If my guess is correct, then MetroCSG who is a license reseller probably purchased the licenses from Synnex who is a license distributor.

Microsoft's Office365 subscription licenses can be purchased in a few ways, each way has its own characteristics.

1. Online - This is where you go to Microsoft website and purchase the licenses with a credit card or Microsoft invoices you and you transfer payment to Microsoft. Microsoft supports you directly as a online customer. You contact Microsoft via raising support tickets. You can also pay extra for a reseller to support you. 

2. Reseller - Open License. You purchase license via a reseller. Open licenses are pre-paid 12 months in advance. Adding extra licenses (12 month licenses) mid term results in the end date of the subscription shifting forward. You cannot get a refund of you cancel mid term. Microsoft supports you directly as a online customer. You contact Microsoft via raising support tickets. The reseller makes a margin between your purchase price and his cost. He may also support you for a fee depending on your arrangement.

3. Reseller - Cloud Solution Provider (CSP) License.  This is a syndication type license which in the past is sold via internet and telecommunication companies like Godaddy, where Microsoft OEMs the Office365 service to them. The CSP partner then bundles the Office365 together with his other services and sells it a bundle. CSP Reseller is then responsible forbilling and support to his customers. In the CSP model, you cannot raise support tickets directly to Microsoft and you have to contact your CSP Reseller.

There are other licensing models for large enterprises, education and government which I believe might not apply to you.

As such I believe you are in situation #3, CSP license where MetroCSG is a CSP tier2 reseller and he purchases the Office365 via Synnex as a CSP Distributor. In such a case MetroCSG is responsible to support you and they currently appear as a advisor in your Office365 Partner relationship. Synnex appears as a cloud solution provider. 

You can remove your advisors. However, they are supposed to support you and removing them will prevent them from being able to access your system to help you with system administrative tasks.  For security reasons you can remove them, and allow them access when you need their help, but the trade off is until you are free to grant them access, they cant support you.

You cannot remove your Cloud Solution Provider unless you stop using this particular license type. If you change to a different provider, then their name will also show up in your Partner Relationship Page.

daniel g. grau say:
When you specify "format document" in the Save As statement, you are telling Word 2011 to save into its native format, that is, .docx. This is actually a ZIP file, which explains why Bash sees it as such. In fact, if you change the .docx extension to .zip, then right-click to select "Open with" and choose "Archive Utility," it will expand into a new folder.

To get a file in .doc format, you need to use "format document97" instead of "format document."

As to why some files do not work, this could happen if you are trying to open a .docx file with an older version of Word (like v.X or 2004), which would use an external Microsoft-supplied file converter. The converter does not work well with "fancy" files that use Word 2011's features not available in older versions.
