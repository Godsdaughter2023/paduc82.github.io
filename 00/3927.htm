Hi,

 

Sorry for the late reply. We are currently experiencing high volume of inquiries. By the way, let's find out what are the possible options that we can do to resolve your issue.

 

By upgrading to Outlook.com, you are only upgrading the user interface of Hotmail to the new user interface. All your emails should still stay in your Inbox.

 

Kindly answer these questions so that we can isolate your issue:

  • When was the account switched to Outlook.com?
  • Did the data loss occur right after Outlook.com upgrade or was the data still there for a brief time?
  • Did you have issues logging in before noticing data loss – did you have to reset password?
  • Did you click on any link before the issue occurred?
  • Did you try to close or downgrade to Hotmail.com before the issue occurred?

If your emails were over 365 days old, you may have lost them due to inactivity in account even before you upgraded to Outlook.com. You can try to recover some of the deleted messages yourself. Click here for more information.

 

If you access your emails on a mail client such as Windows Live Mail or through your mobile device, but didn’t log into your web account for over 365 days, you may lose emails due to inactivity in web account.

 

If you renamed your account, you got another option to save your Hotmail emails differently when renaming your account to @Outlook.com account: If you chose to rename your account to @Outlook.com, a prompt would have asked you – ‘Where do you want to save your emails?’ You will have the option to create a new folder for your old account or save the emails in the Inbox of your renamed account. If you chose to create a new folder, the default name of the folder will be the name of your old account and the folder will appear in the left pane under ‘Folders’. All your old emails will go in this folder.

 

It is also possible that someone hacked the account and the hacker had already deleted emails in your account before you renamed your account or before your account is automatically upgraded.
