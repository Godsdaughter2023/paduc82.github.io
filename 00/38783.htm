considerate_guy say:

In order for Movie Maker to have the "Burn to DVD" option, you must have Windows DVD Maker installed on your version of Windows.

Windows DVD Maker came with Windows Vista and Windows 7, but support for it has been dropped in Windows 8 and 8.1. I suspect you are on the most recent version of Windows and that's why you don't have the option.

Even Apple has dropped support for the legacy video DVD standard by getting rid of their iDVD program in the iLife suite. The video DVD is an MPEG-2 video format and can only handle standard-definition (480p NTSC) video, not HD video.

I would instead be looking into other options for archiving your footage rather than creating video DVDs, but if you still need to, there are 3rd party options as noted by skier-hughes, like Freemake Video Converter (which can save to DVD), DVD Flick and DVD Styler.

Options for archiving HD footage (and not relying on the legacy SD video DVD format) are:

  • Burn the .mp4 file directly onto a DVD disc as a data file, and then play it in a Blu-ray player (not a DVD player).
  • Store your HD footage on your computer, a network drive or an external hard drive. And don't forget to have a backup copy stored elsewhere on another drive/external drive or a cloud backup.
  • Store a copy of your video on a video sharing site like YouTube, Vimeo or OneDrive. Video takes up a lot of space so make sure you have the room to do this and a way to retrieve the file later.
