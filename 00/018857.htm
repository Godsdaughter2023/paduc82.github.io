\\Public Folders - michael\All Public Folders\Master Calendar1

I ASSUME this is the correct "path." So, from the previously mentioned page, I assume the correct method  to open this path would be:

"c:\Program Files\Microsoft Office\Office14\Outlook.exe" /select "\\Public Folders\All Public Folders\SDRI Master Calendar1"

But, as you may have guessed because I'm here, it AIN'T WORKING!

If anyone can help me out on this, that would be lovely!

Anyone got some WORKING syntax for this?

----------

Ohh wait... Eureka! I figured it out after much trial and tribulation. I had tried the correct solution, but incorrectly... Let me explain. I figured that maybe the outlook:calendar syntax might work. But I had been trying (after initial failure) a variety of options including just "\\Public Folders\All Public Folders\Master Calendar1" and I accidentally tried putting outlook: in front of that and it failed so I assumed that was the wrong approach.

In fact it was the RIGHT approach, but the wrong path, since I took out the " - michael".

----------

Here's the successful solution in case anyone needs it:

"c:\Program Files\Microsoft Office\Office14\Outlook.exe" /select "outlook:\\Public Folders - michael\All Public Folders\Master Calendar1"

Wow, that was a pain in the hindquarters. They really need to bring back the simple drag/drop functionality...

Aha! It works!

Today, I am full of WIN!

So, the first path is the path to your copy of Outlook. The second path with outlook: in front of it is the path listed by right clicking your public calendar + the name of your calendar. Both need to be in quotes if there are any spaces in your path(s).

So, one might say:

"path to Outlook.exe" /select "outlook:path to calendar + calendar name"

More or less.

WIN! Go me!

With the Office 365 subscription, what you get is the "actual Word program." Actually, you get all the parts of Office you want, including language packs. r porter say:

I now have a workaround.  Buried deep within the printer setup there is a setting for Pure Black and Pure Gray and what they are applied to.  I now have grayscale images with full color logos on the same page.

If anyone has a similar issue here is how to access the settings (this is for Toshiba 3550 printer but others may be similar):

In Publisher

Printer Settings

Image Quality

Image Quality Type

Presentation (Also works with Photo and General)

Check both Pure Balck and Pure Gray

Apply to - Text, Graphics and Image (default is Apply to - Auto and this is the problem).
